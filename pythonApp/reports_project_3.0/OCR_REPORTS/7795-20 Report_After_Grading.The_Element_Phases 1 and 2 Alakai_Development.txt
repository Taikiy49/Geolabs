Dear Mr. Wallenstrom: 
We are pleased to submit our earthwork observation and compaction testing report 
for work performed during the grading operation of The Element, Phases 1 and 2, 
Ho`opili Development, Parcel 13A, in general accordance with our fee proposal dated 
September 20, 2018 and subsequent additional budget request. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Element, Phases 1 and 2 project is located within Parcel 13A of the Ho`opili 
Development in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. Parcel 13A is situated in the 
middle and western portions of the Ho`opili Development and encompasses 
approximately 11.3 acres of land area. Phase 1 of The Element project entails developing 
7.3 acres of the 11.3-acre parcel and consists of eight multi-family dwelling structures 
with associated roadways, parking, and a community center. Phase 2 entails developing 
the remaining 4 acres of the project and consists of four multi-family dwelling structures 
with associated roadways and parking areas. 
We understand mass grading of the project site was performed previously by the 
Master Developer (D.R. Horton); therefore, The Element project site was constructed 
upon a mass graded parcel to near the building pad grades. As a result, the additional 
site grading work consisted of only minor cuts and fills to achieve the design finished 
grades for the project. Goodfellow Bros., Inc. performed the earthwork operations for the 
project as a subcontractor to Moss & Associates LLC, the General Contractor. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits generally were 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from

the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a sheepsfoot and/or vibratory smooth drum roller. It should be noted that the upper 2 feet 
of the subgrade was previously moisture-conditioned to about 2 to 4 percent above 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 85 to 90 percent of the 
maximum dry density, forming a 2-foot-thick reworked zone, during the mass grading 
operation.  
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing generally were 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was 
backfilled with well-compacted fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the subgrade was 
capped with 18 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent 
of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations, including compaction of building 
pads, garages, walkways, and driveways.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.42. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering from January 21, 2020 to 
July 6, 2021.
