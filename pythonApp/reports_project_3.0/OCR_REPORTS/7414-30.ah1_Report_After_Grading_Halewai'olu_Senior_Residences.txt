This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services

Senior Residences project situated at 1331 River Street in Honolulu on the Island of

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at 1331 River Street (formerly 1331-1347 River Street) 
in the Chinatown area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves 
the development of an affordable senior rental housing project of up to 17 stories in 
height atop a podium parking structure. The housing development provides over 
150 affordable one or two-bedroom housing units. In addition, a new one-story 
community center with a roof-top garden abuts the new 17-story senior housing 
development to provide recreational activities for the residents. The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.

The high-rise housing development and the community center structures are 
supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 7.625-inch diameter micropiles 
with allowable compressive load capacities of 80 and 250 kips per micropile, 
respectively. The layout of the new senior housing development project is shown on the 
Site Plan (Plate 2). 
Site grading work for the project generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order 
of less than about 2 feet thick. The project also included the installation of new utility 
connections, backfilling around pile caps, elevator pit, and stairwells, and pavement 
construction, among others. The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes 
observation and compaction testing performed during the earthwork and infrastructure 
installation for the project only. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Unlimited Construction Services, Inc. was the General Contractor for the project. 
Jayar Construction, Inc. performed the site grading construction, utility line installation, 
and pavement installation. The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, 
placement and compaction of fills and backfills, utility trench excavation and backfill, 
and pavement construction. 
Following the demolition, clearing, and foundation installation operations, fill and 
backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, 
brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of 
the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified 
Proctor) under the building slab areas. The compaction requirement for the finished 
subgrades under the pavement areas was increased to 95 percent (or greater) of the 
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
New utility lines, such as water, sewer, and drain lines, and utility line 
connections adjacent to the project site were installed as part of the development. To 
reduce the potential for appreciable settlement of the pipelines, the bottom of the utility 
lines and below-grade structures were over-excavated for installation of a stabilization 
layer below the bedding layer, consisting of about 24 inches of No. 2 Rock (ASTM C33, 
No. 4 gradation) or No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 gradation) wrapped in a 
non-woven filter fabric (Mirafi 180N), where the soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits were 
encountered. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized 
on Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations with variations in construction
