INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our vibration monitoring services performed
during the production pile driving operations at the Kekaulike Revitalization Diamond
Head Block Project in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes the findings resulting from our
field monitoring work and presents our discussions derived from our engineering
analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a 3 story commercial and residential
building complex on the site as a public housing development The proposed
development will also include one level on the Maunakea Street side and two levels
on the Kekaulike Street side of below ground parking which will extend to an
elevation of approximately 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL or deeper
The project site is currently bounded by low rise buildings along King and
Kekaulike Streets and across Maunakea and Hotel Streets The adjacent buildings are
constructed of brick masonry rock masonry as well as concrete masonry units
Existing structural distresses such as crack openings were observed at various
locations of the building faces Due to the relatively poor condition and proximity of the

PRODUCTION PILE PROGRAM
The field vibration monitoring work was conducted between May 9th through
August 3rd during the production pile driving operations performed by Hawaiian
Dredging Construction Company A diesel hammer with a rated
maximum driving energy of approximately 69 898 foot pounds was used to drive the
16 inch octagonal precast and prestressed concrete piles
A total of 234 production piles were driven to depths of about 48 to 152 feet
below the existing ground surface at the site Detailed observations of the production
pile driving operations were presented under a separate report prepared for the City
and County of Honolulu The production pile locations were generally predrilled to a
depth of approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface prior to the pile driving
operations The locations of the production piles for which monitoring was performed
during the pile driving operations are shown on the Pile Location Plans Plates 2 and 3

METHODOLOGY
An was used to monitor the vibration generated during the
production pile driving operations A sensor geophone was placed in the vicinity of
the existing structures during the course of pile driving operations in order to obtain
vibration data representative of that experienced by the existing structures
The consists of a 3 component geophone and a signal
conditioner recorder unit The geophone senses ground motion along three mutually
perpendicular orthogonal axes thus providing detection of ground motion in the
vertical transverse and longitudinal directions The signal conditioner recorder unit
receives and processes these signals to provide a graphic record of vibrations The
Blastmate II permits scanning of vibration levels over extended periods when operated
in the Monitor Mode Resultant peak particle velocities RPPV in units of inches per
second ips are automatically computed by vector summation of the motions along the
orthogonal axes
The Blastmate Il was set at a scanning interval of every minute during the
vibration monitoring process During the 1 minute scanning interval the Blastmate Il
samples the vibration data at a rate of 1 024 data points per second and stores the
resultant peak level of vibration which occurs during that 1 minute interval At the end
of every scanning interval a stripchart record is produced on the recording paper by
the built in plotter The height of each bar on the stripchart record represents the
highest vibration level observed during each scanning interval The magnitude of the
highest vibration level observed and its corresponding time are shown on the stripchart
graph and data printout generated by the built in plotter at the completion of the driving
operation of each production pile A 1 page summary of each production pile driving
event can be produced by connecting the Blastmate II to a computer

During the pile driving operations the times corresponding to every foot of driven
pile length were recorded in our field monitoring log This information was used to
evaluate the approximate depth which produced the maximum vibrations during the
driving of each production pile when used in conjunction with the stripchart graph and
data printout The horizontal surface distance between the geophone and the
production pile was also measured and entered into the field log
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Since vibration amplitudes for a given energy source attenuate decrease with
increasing distance in exponential form graphic representation of vibration amplitude
versus distance data will appear linear when plotted on a log log graph form
The threshold for persons to perceive vibrations occurs at a relatively low level
of motion Depending on character duration and repetition of vibrations perception
may occur at levels between 0 01 and These levels are well below those
required to cause damage to typical structures
Vibration damage thresholds for pile driving induced motions have not been
specifically established A number of researchers have presented a variety of proposed
criteria The criteria that we normally use are related to the type of construction age
and general condition of structures and are adapted from a variety of studies of actual
damage thresholds observed for blasting operations and other vibration inducing
sources

Based on Chae 1978 the following recommended threshold of damage criteria
should provide reasonable indicators for reducing the potential for direct vibration
damage
Class 4 inches per second
for structures of substantial construction
Class Il 2 inches per second
for relatively new residential structures in sound condition
Class Ill 1 inch per second
for relatively old residential structures in poor condition
Class inch per second
for old residential structures in very poor condition
The structures adjacent to the Kekaulike Revitalization project site may be
classified as Class III to Class IV structures Because of the repetitive nature of pile
driving vibrations it has been recommended that the above categories be lowered by
one class Thus a vibration damage threshold criteria of 0 5 inch per second was
recommended for use during the production pile driving operations for the subject
project
The intersection of the regression line with the Class Ill Reduced criteria line
suggests that damages may occur to structures that are located within this distance of
the vibration source Based on our vibration monitoring results obtained during the test
pile driving operations damages may occur to structures that are located within 30 feet
of the vibration source Due to the proximity of some of the production piles to the
existing structures efforts were made to reduce the potential adverse effects of

These efforts included increasing the predrilling depths and using a lower hammer

Vibration monitoring of the production pile driving operations was conducted
between May 9th and August 3rd A numerically ordered summary of the resultant
peak vibration levels observed during the production pile driving operations is presented
on Plates 4 through 12 Attenuation plots with different hammer settings for the
resultant peak vibration data obtained for the production pile driving operations are
presented on Plates 13 through 15 A regression analyses power curve was
performed on the vibration data to determine representative rates of vibration
attenuation with increasing distance from the source and these lines are also shown
on the plots
Results obtained from the vibration monitoring performed during the production
pile driving operation appeared to indicate that the majority of the vibration levels
recorded have exceeded the assumed threshold limit of 0 5 inch per second However
we understand that no noticeable damage to the existing structures have been
observed or reported by the contractor during the production pile driving operations
Therefore it is our opinion that the vibration damage threshold criteria may be
increased to 1 0 inch per second for the subject project based on a Class Ill
classification for the existing structure Even with a higher vibration damage threshold
criteria of 1 0 inch per second some of the production piles driven generated vibrations
exceeding the 1 0 inch per second threshold criteria However none of the production
piles monitored generated vibrations in excess of 2 0 inches per second Therefore
we recommend that a formal visual crack survey be conducted as soon as possibile to
confirm the above findings and observations and to provide for documentation
