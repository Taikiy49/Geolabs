The following contains our geotechnical recommendations for design of the 
proposed Outfall Improvements and Rehabilitation project at the Waianae Wastewater 
Treatment Plant (WWTP) in Waianae on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The outfall improvements and rehabilitation project site is located across 
Farrington Highway from the existing Waianae WWTP. Based on the information 
provided, we understand consultation and geotechnical design parameters will be 
required to design the new manhole structures and outfall pipe encasement in the area. 
The project area extends south from Leihoku Street by about 350 linear feet in the area 
fronting the beach across Farrington Highway from the Waianae WWTP. 
In order to provide information to assist in the design of the sewer line outfall 
encasement at selected locations, three on-land borings extending to a depth of about 
36.5 feet below the existing ground surface were drilled along the existing sewer line 
outfall alignment. To supplement the subsurface data obtained from the on-land borings, 
Dynamic Cone Penetration (DCP) testing was conducted at eight selected locations in 
the vicinity of the existing sewer line outfall pipe alignment. 
Based on our field observations, the project site slopes down gradually from the 
east to the west and is covered predominately by beach sand and coral reef closer to the 
shoreline. Based on the information provided, we estimate the existing ground surface 
elevation ranges between about +4 and +10 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
SUBSURACE CONDITIONS 
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three exploratory borings in 
support of the proposed outfall improvements and rehabilitation project. Three borings, 
designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 3, were drilled and sampled to a depth of about 
36.5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of about 109.5 linear feet of 
exploration.

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings, the project site 
generally is underlain by beach sands and coral formation over coralline deposits. The 
beach sand is generally loose at the ground surface and grades to medium dense 
extending to depths of about 3.5 to 5.5 feet below the ground surface. Underlying the 
beach sand, our borings encountered medium hard coral formation extending to depths 
between about 8 and 12 feet below the ground surface. It should be noted that the coral 
formation is exposed at the ground surface closer to the ocean.  
Below the coral formation, our borings encountered coralline detritus consisting of 
medium dense to dense silty sands and gravel with zones of loose sands extending to 
depths between about 27 and 28 feet below the ground surface. Boring No. 2 
encountered alluvium consisting of soft sandy silts within the coralline detritus at depths 
between about 20 and 23 feet below the ground surface. Our borings encountered soft to 
hard coral formation below the coralline detritus extending to the maximum depth 
explored of about 36.5 feet below the ground surface. 
We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at depths ranging from about 
6.8 to 7.8 feet below the ground surface. The groundwater levels encountered generally 
correspond to about Elevations +0.3 and +1.7 feet MSL at the time of our field exploration. 
Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean, groundwater levels are 
expected to vary with tidal fluctuations. In addition, groundwater levels may change due 
to seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, storm surge, and other factors. 
In addition to exploratory boreholes, our field exploration program also included 
performance of DCP testing at eight selected locations along the proposed outfall 
improvements and rehabilitation project alignment to provide additional data and 
information for our evaluation and analyses. 
The DCP test provides a measure of a material’s in-situ resistance to penetration. 
The test is performed by driving a metal cone into the ground by repeatedly striking it with 
a 17.6-lb weight dropped from a distance of 22.6 inches. The penetration of the cone is 
measured after each blow and is recorded to provide a continuous measure of shear 
resistance up to 10 feet below the ground surface. Test results can be correlated to 
California Bearing Ratio, in-situ density, resilient modulus, and bearing capacity. 
The DCP tests, designated as P-1 through P-8, generally were conducted at 
discrete depth intervals between depths of about 8.5 to 44.3 inches below the ground 
surface. Based on the field logs of the DCP tests, it appears the top of the medium hard 
coral formation ranges from the ground surface to approximately 3.7 feet below the 
ground surface.

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site, we believe the 
proposed manhole structure and outfall pipe encasement proposed likely is founded on 
the coral formation encountered near the surface. Based on the design plans provided, 
the bottom of the pipe encasement is ranges from about Elevation 0 feet MSL to 
approximately Elevation -9 feet MSL. At these elevations, we believe the concrete 
encasement for the outfall pipe will be embedded in the coral formation encountered in 
the borings and should provide adequate resistance to scour from the ocean waves. It 
should be noted that the concrete for the new pipe encasement should be placed neat 
against the coral formation at the sides and at the bottom to provide adequate resistance 
to scour of the sediments above the coral formation.  
As for the new manhole structure cover for MH-2, MH-3, and MH-4, we believe the 
subgrade below the outfall pipe and manhole structure consist of coral formation and 
underlying coralline deposits. As long as the added weight imposed by the new precast 
concrete manhole cover does not impose a weight more than 800 pounds per square foot 
(psf) more than the existing condition, we believe that would be acceptable as this amount 
added pressure should not result in appreciable additional settlement of the outfall pipe 
at the manhole structure locations.
