Probing and Grouting Operations
Structural Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in general
conformance with the recommendations presented in our report dated
for the project The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression
of loose zones below the building structures
Constructors Hawaii Inc surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole
locations prior to the probing operation In general the probe holes were located at the
column footings and spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals along the wall footings The
approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 1 In
general the probe holes at the new building structures were drilled to a depth ranging from
about 11 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to approximately
10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
Structural Systems Inc drilled the probe holes about 4 inches in diameter using a
the probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe
holes to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior
to the grouting operation A total of 101 probe holes with an aggregate length of about
lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled for the project
The probe holes were backfilled with sand cement grout batched by
Structural Systems Inc on site and injected at low pressures Grout quantities were
estimated from the number of hopper loads used to fill each probe hole one hopper
contains approximately 2 cubic feet of grout The grouting operation generally proceeded
in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the grouting hose was
connected to the top of the temporary PVC pipe using a clamp lock Grout
injection then commenced at the direction of our field representative who
provided direction as to when the injection was complete or whether
additional injection was required
e Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low
pressure injection of grout through the PVC pipe which was retracted from
the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Our field representative observed
the grouting operation and recorded the approximate grout take
In general we did not observe significant voids and or cavities during the probing
operation except for some easy drilling at certain probe holes Relatively high grout intake
was observed at Probe Hole Nos 2 and 20 with grout intake of approximately 21 and
17 cubic feet 0 8 and 0 6 cubic yards respectively during the grouting operation
Approximately 268 5 cubic feet 9 95 cubic yards of sand cement grout was utilized
