INSTRUMENTATION MONITORING
As part of the landslide study we drilled four borings each to a depth of
approximately 100 feet in November 2001 and September 2003 Four borings were
converted to inclinometer monitoring stations designated as Inclinometer Nos 1
through 4 to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths Inclinometer
locations I 1 through I 4 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
As part of the landslide remediation design we drilled an additional nine borings
each to a depth of approximately 100 feet in 2008 Five of the borings were converted to
inclinometer monitoring stations designated as Inclinometer Nos 5 through 9
Inclinometer locations I 5 through I 9 are also shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the inclinometers were read at approximately two month to
three month intervals to measure the rate of ground movement and to monitor the
effects of heavy rain in relation to movement The cumulative displacements measured

Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street from
January 2003 to August 2003 A majority of the cracks previously recorded was patched
with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development was recorded
from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping resumed in 2005 During this period a
majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement resurfacing was again observed
on the resurfaced pavement with some extended new cracks as indicated in
Progress Report No 20 dated
It should be noted that some wide cracks developed previously in the front of
1198 Mona Street were patched by strips of hot mix asphaltic material last year However
some of the original cracks have once again reflected on the surface of the patches as
shown on the photo below placed less than 12 months ago It appears that the hot mix
asphaltic patching is not efficient to patch the wider and deeper cracks
The crack observations indicate that the lateral earth movement and or creep at the
project site may still be active To reduce the potential for significant surface water
infiltration into the subsurface the cracks offset and gaps should be sealed with crack filler
or sealer providing more durable temporary repairs We will continue to monitor the
pavement deformation The revised Crack Location Plan will be available in the next
progress report at the end of the 2012 monitoring period

SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos 1 and 2 indicated some increased movement within the last
18 months with average lateral movement rate of about 0 27 to 0 4 inches per year
Inclinometer Nos 3 through 6 and 9 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement and or creep at their respective instrument locations with average lateral
movement rate of about 0 15 to 0 4 inches per year However Inclinometer Nos 7 and
8 currently do not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral movement
Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the
project site Based on our pavement deformation observation we believe that lateral
near surface earth movement and or creep at the site is still active
Based on inclinometer Nos 1 through 3 and 6 it appears that the sliding zone
may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground surface
Inclinometer Nos 4 and 5 are located between Inclinometer Nos 2 and 3 It appears
that the slide zone may be located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos 1 through 3 at
a depth of approximately 20 feet below the ground surface
We believe that the ground movement slip circle may not extend to the existing
slope toe as evident by no significant ground movement measured by Inclinometer
Nos 7 and 8 This may be partially due to the buttress installed at the slope toe in the
previous landslide remediation project in the 1970 s
Inclinometer No 9 indicated lateral creep movement at shallow depths We
believe that this creep movement may be related to traffic surcharge laterally acting on
the surface fill materials and medium stiff clayey soils encountered in the boring The
existing leaning electrical utility pole with leaning angle of up to about 7 degrees from
vertical axial adjacent to Inclinometer No 9 may by caused by the creeping ground
movement
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
