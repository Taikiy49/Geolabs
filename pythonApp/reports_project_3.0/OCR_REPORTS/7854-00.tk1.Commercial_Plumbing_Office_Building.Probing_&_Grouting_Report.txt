Dear Ms. Ueno: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Commercial Plumbing Office 
Building project between January 10 and 16, 2019. The probing and grouting observation 
work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated March 26, 2019. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is located on a vacant parcel consisting of three properties at 1804 
and 1812 Colburn Street and 1803 Dillingham Boulevard in the Kalihi areas of Honolulu 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project location and general vicinity are shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The project consists of the construction of a three-story precast reinforced 
concrete office building structure with a mezzanine level, stairway and elevator, and an 
elevated ramp.  We understand that the new office building will be supported by spread 
column and perimeter wall footings. 
Probing and Grouting Program 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the new office 
building structure in general accordance with the recommendations presented in the 
above-referenced geotechnical consultation letter report dated August 21, 2018, prepared 
by Yogi Kwong Engineers, LLC. The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was

to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities 
or compression of loose zones below the building structure. 
Jayar Construction, Inc. surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole 
locations prior to the probing and grouting operations. In general, the probe holes were 
located at the center of the column footings and at 10-foot spacing along the combined 
footings, all extending to a minimum depth of 20 feet below the footing subgrade 
elevations.  In addition, probe holes were also located along the wall footings at 10-foot 
spacing extending to a minimum depth of 10 feet below the footing subgrade elevations.  
The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan, 
Plate 2. 
Geolabs, Inc. performed the probing work for the Commercial Plumbing Office 
Building project using a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers.  The 
probe holes were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 10 to 
27 feet below the existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 10 or 20 feet 
below the design footing bottom elevations.   
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted 
of fill materials over tannish white to tan coralline materials.  After completion of the probe 
drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with flowable fill with a compressive strength 
of 800 – 1,000 pounds per square inch (psi).  The flowable fill used to fill the probe holes 
was supplied by HC&D, LLC and the boom pump was operated by American Standard 
Concrete Pumping Hawaii, Inc. 
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and 
recorded the probe time, grout time, and approximate grout take.  A total of 89 probe 
holes with an aggregate length of about 1,645 lineal feet below the existing ground 
surface were drilled for the new office building as summarized in the following table.  The 
probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.3.
