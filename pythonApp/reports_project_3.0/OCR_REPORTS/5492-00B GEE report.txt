PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Keelikolani Complex within the
Kamehameha Schools premises in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1

planned from the existing Keelikolani Complex to nearby structures as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 Due to the sloping terrain at the walkway location we envision that
retaining walls will be required for the proposed walkway construction Detailed
information for the project was not available at the time this letter report was prepared

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the new walkway project consisted of drilling and
sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 20 7 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the drilled boring is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring generally encountered stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays
extending to a depth of approximately 13 feet below the existing ground surface Hard
basalt rock formation was then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 20 7 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that
boulders may be present in the subsurface as encountered in our drilled boring at a depth
of about 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Static groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our field
exploration However subterranean seepage was encountered at a depth of about
2 5 feet below the existing ground surface in the boring drilled It should be noted that
subterranean seepage is common in the area due to the sloping terrain and wet
environment at the project site In addition groundwater levels are also subject to change

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration encountered stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty
clays overlying hard basalt rock formation extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 20 7 feet below the existing ground surface Subterranean seepage was
encountered in the drilled boring at a depth of about 2 5 feet below the ground surface at
the time of our field exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
shallow foundations may be used to support the proposed new retaining walls planned
at the site An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
stiff soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
New concrete walkways planned for the project should be supported on 6 inches
of non expansive select granular fill or aggregate subbase The select granular fill or
aggregate subbase should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction In
addition crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections The subgrade soils beneath
the walkways should be scarified to a minimum depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Detailed discussions of
these items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the
following sections of this letter report
Retaining Wall Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration we
envision new retaining structures planned for the project may be supported on shallow
foundations such as continuous footings An allowable bearing pressure of up to
may be used for design of shallow foundations bearing on the in situ stiff to very stiff
clayey soils as encountered in our boring drilled at the walkway location This bearing
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Retaining structure footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches and the
foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade For sloping ground conditions the footing
should be extended deeper to obtain a minimum 8 foot setback distance measured
horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope Wall footings
oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped footings

Foundations located next to other foundations utility trenches or easements
should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or they should be extended to a depth as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
appreciable foundation settlement
For retaining wall footings bearing on the in situ materials the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation may be backfilled with the on site soils compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Lateral loads acting on the retaining wall may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes the soil around footings is
well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations located on
slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately around a perforated pipe perforations facing down placed at the base
of the wall The perforated pipe should be daylighted or discharge to an
appropriate outlet

Walkway Slabs
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey soils with a low to slight expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in
the moisture content we recommend that the subgrade soils below the concrete walkway
be scarified to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
The slab subgrades should be kept moist until the final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
For design of the concrete walkway we recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick
layer of non expansive select granular fill or aggregate subbase be placed below the
concrete walkway for uniform support The select granular fill or aggregate subbase
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In addition crack
control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkway Thickened
edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade It should be noted that areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soil and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs
Site Preparation
In general site grading and earthwork operations should be observed by a Geolabs
representative It is important that a representative from our office be present to observe
the grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during
the excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions at the foundation
subgrade levels are similar to those encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other deleterious and organic
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site After clearing and
grubbing existing structures to be demolished should be completely removed
Over excavation resulting from demolition should be backfilled with compacted select
granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in

8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition areas at grade or areas designated to receive fill
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Imported fill materials including the 6 inch thick non expansive select granular fills
required underneath the concrete walkways should consist of non expansive select
granular fill materials such as crushed coral and basalt gravel The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 In addition the material
should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
Fill and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon

The location of the field boring indicated in this report is approximate having been

and elevation of the field boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used

transition Water level data from the boring was measured at the times shown on the
