PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Shriners Hospital for Children in the
Punahou area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is located at the southwest
corner of the intersection between Punahou Street and the Punahou Street Off Ramp
for the Interstate Route H 1 Highway We understand that it is proposed to demolish the
existing hospital building and construct a new Main Hospital In addition it is proposed
to construct several smaller buildings The smaller buildings include a Family Quarters
building and an Office Education building
As part of the project two new bridge structures will span over the existing box
culvert at the new parking lot to reduce additional loading imposed on the existing box
culvert A total of 22 drilled shafts will be installed for the bridge crossings The south
culvert crossing will be supported by 12 drilled shafts and the north culvert crossing will
be supported by 10 drilled shafts

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Foundation International Inc performed the drilled shaft installation A total of
22 drilled shafts with a minimum diameter of 24 inches were installed at the site from
to The shafts were designated as Shaft Nos 1
through 22 Shaft Nos 1 through 12 were installed for the South Culvert Crossing and
Shaft Nos 13 through 22 were installed for the North Culvert Crossing The approximate
drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2 A Geolabs
representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel reinforcements and
concrete placement operations during the installation of the drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a track mounted drill rig equipped with
24 inch augers and drilling buckets 24 and 30 inch diameter core barrels and 24 inch
cleanout buckets Temporary casings with diameters of 30 inches were utilized to case the
upper 10 feet of the drilled shafts for drilled holes that encountered loose cinders The
production shafts were excavated to depths ranging from about 20 to 21 5 feet below the
ground surface which corresponds to elevations ranging from about 16 2 to 22 2 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover between the sidewalls of
the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage The shaft concrete was placed after
placement of the rebar cage using tremie or by free fall methods Since the drilled holes
were relatively dry free fall methods were allowed for concrete placement The maximum
free fall height was about 8 feet
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the South and
North Culvert Crossings have been installed in general conformance with the project plans
and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to verify that the
shaft position is within the tolerance of the project specifications Based on our
observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
