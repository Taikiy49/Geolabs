INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Seberg Residence in the
Punahou Manoa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering reconmendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed new
residence will be a two story structure approximately 75 by 55 feet in
plan dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage and
driveway areas
The grading plan of the project was not available at the time of our
report preparation however site grading involving cuts northern
portion and fills southern portion is anticipated Retaining walls may
be planned for grade separation at various portions of the lot
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Ms Dana
Seberg dated The scope of work consisted of the
following
1 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface and obtaining surface samples
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading and foundation design
recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The general area consisting of three lots is located at the end of
Judd Hillside Street in Manoa The subject lot Lot 2 is on the eastern
corner of the area and bounded by a wall to the southeast and a fence to
the northeast This approximately rectangular shaped 14 913 square feet
lot is largely barren of vegetation
The topography of the site slopes downward from north to south with a
gradient of approximately 4H 1V to 5H 1V The current ground elevations
range from about 316 feet southern portion to 342 feet northern
portion above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the
surface It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed residence
Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered
at fill areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and
then replaced with compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Fill Materials
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site soils may be used as fill material If required
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than
20 passing the 200 sieve All imported fill material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
transportation to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78
Fill on areas where the existing surface exceeds a slope ratio of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope for
details see Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of
the fills particularly to reduce the potential sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers
The site should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundation
A slope ratio of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2H 1V may be used for
permanent fill slopes
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill The bearing value
is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2174 00 Page 6
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements
are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of
an inch differential
Individual footings should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
removed prior to concrete placement
lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor of the residence will be concrete
Slab on grade A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel should be provided below
the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended below the slabs
Retaining Walls
It is anticipated that retaining walls may be used for grade
separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
2 A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
3 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade If located on a
sloping area the footing should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain an 8 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope

face An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil and
compacted fill
4 If wall foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements
they should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines
5 Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of

6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls to protect against build up of
hydrostatic pressure Subdrains should be properly constructed
to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2174 00 Page 9
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Concrete Driveway
Concrete driveway is planned for the residence The subgrade soils
should be compacted maintained in a moist condition and prevented from
drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from objects indicated on the plan provided by Wayson W C Chong
Architect A I A Elevations of the data points were approximately
estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the plan The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface and
obtaining surface sample at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the
boring
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
