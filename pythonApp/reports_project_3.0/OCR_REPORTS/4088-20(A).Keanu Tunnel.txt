PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that a new embankment was
constructed to stabilize the near vertical slope at the Keanu Tunnel West Portal site The
new embankment measures approximately 380 feet wide and up to 22 feet high in
dimensions The earthwork operations for the embankment construction were performed
by Royal Contracting Company from July 30 to August 21 2003 A layout of the slope
stabilization project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information provided the embankment was constructed using fill
materials consisting of grayish brown clayey gravel with sand imported from the Colony
residential development at Hawaii Kai Peninsula The filling operations started at the
lowest point and continued up in level horizontal compacted layers of about 10 inches thick
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In addition the new fills
were benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The
fill slopes were constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to
obtain a well compacted slope face
We understand that compaction testing of the fill materials was not performed
during the earthwork operations of the embankment construction In order to evaluate the
above embankment for conformance with the project plans and specifications test
