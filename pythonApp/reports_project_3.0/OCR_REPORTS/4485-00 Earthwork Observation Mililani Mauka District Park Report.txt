EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
MILILANI MAUKA DISTRICT PARK
Dear Mr Gaston
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading and water main jacket construction for the Mililani
Mauka District Park development from to in accordance with
our fee proposal dated May 4 2000
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between Ukuwai Street and the Interstate Route H 2
freeway in the Mililani Mauka Residential Development Area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
project involves grading 14 2 acres for future recreational areas which include
softball soccer fields a baseball field tennis courts basketball courts gymnasium play
area comfort station and a parking area The site grading work generally consisted of fills
up to 4 feet thick Thicker fills of up to about 12 feet were placed to fill an existing silt basin
at the western portion of the site The project also included the installation of a reinforced
concrete jacket for a portion of an existing 20 Inch water main The grading plan for the
project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and water main jacket installation for the project
site
SITE GRADING AND WATER MAIN JACKET INSTALLATION
The site grading and water main jacket installation work for the Mililani Mauka
District Park project was observed by Geolabs Inc from to
The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction
of fills up to about 4 feet thick in order to achieve the design finished grades for the project

Fills of up to about 12 feet were placed to fill an existing silt basin The clearing and
grubbing and site grading work was performed by RHS Lee Inc The reinforced concrete
jacket installation and backfilling work for the 20 Inch water main was performed by
Yoshimura Contracting During the site grading operations samples of the on site and
imported materials that were utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture
content was established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the site grading and water main backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 8 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade
soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and water main backfilling work for
the Mililani Mauka District Park project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references

grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
