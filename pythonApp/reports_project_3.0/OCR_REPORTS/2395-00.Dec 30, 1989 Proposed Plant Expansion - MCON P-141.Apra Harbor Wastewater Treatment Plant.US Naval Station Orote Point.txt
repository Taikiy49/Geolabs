INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed expansion of the existing Apra
Harbor Wastewater Treatment Plant in Guam Mariana Islands The general
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or
bidding

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the existing Apra Harbor Wastewater
Treatment Plant in Naval Station Orote Point on the island of Guan
Mariana Islands The treatment plant is situated on Orote Peninsula on
the eastern side of Marine Drive midway between Inner Apra Harbor and the
Pacific Ocean
The existing Plant has various structures including a chlorination
building a chlorine contact chamber a headworks a primary clarifier and
pump house two secondary digestors and a digestor control building four
drying beds a control building with an attached aerated grit chamber an
aeration chamber and influent effluent pumping
The Plant site is generally flat with ground elevations varying from
about 11 feet MSL at the eastern end to about 13 feet MSL at the
entrance of the plant site Some of the existing buildings and structures
are on raised ground at approximately 15 to 16 feet MSL
The proposed plant expansion will be on vacant lands among the
existing structures in the central portion of the Plant site
Open areas at the Plant site are well grassed or paved with asphaltic
concrete driveways

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the western central portion of the
island of Guam the largest and southernmost of the Mariana Islands The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The foundation of the island is composed of volcanic rocks including
pyroclastic materials which were extruded during early to mid Tertiary
time Volcanic activity ended during the Oligocene epoch
Following the cessation of the volcanic activity extensive
coral algal reef deposits were laid down on the island from the Miocene
through Pleistocene epochs
Tectonic uplift has raised these old reef deposits and subsequent
marine activity created lagoonal environments where fine grained sediments
accumulated During the recent century development of the military
facility has shaped the existing land by grading and filling
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the proposed plant expansion site
were explored by drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 61 5
to 66 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The exploration encountered a thin fill crust of four 4 to five 5
feet of medium dense to dense coral sand and gravel which is underlain by
soft to medium stiff clays and loose to medium dense sand and gravel
deposits predominantly of lagoonal origin Coral reef detritus and
formation were encountered below the lagoonal deposits at approximately 15
feet below the existing ground

The lagoonal deposits particularly the clay layer are highly
compressible under structure loads Generally the lagoonal layer is
about seven 7 to ten 10 feet thick below the surface fill crust The
clay layer has a variable thickness and appears to be thickest at Boring
No 4 the location of the proposed biotower
The reef detritus gravel underlying the lagoonal deposits has
generally medium to high density The reef formation below the detritus
layer also has generally medium to high density It appears that the
formation grades locally denser at approximately 55 to 60 feet below the
existing ground indicating possible cementation in the coral growth
Groundwater Level
During our field exploration the highest groundwater level was
measured in Boring No 2 at the approximate depth of 8 5 feet below the
existing ground surface or at the approximate elevation of 2 3 feet MSL
The lowest groundwater level was measured also in Boring No 1 at
the approximate depth of 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface or
at the approximate elevation of 1 6 feet MSL
Due to the proximity to Apra Harbor and the Pacific Ocean it is
likely that the groundwater levels at the project site will be influenced
by tidal fluctuations and storm surge conditions Therefore the
groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration
may not reflect the highest possible levels

Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The sheeting bracing system used must comply with all applicable
safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical
engineer be retained to design evaluate the excavation support system
used However the adequacy and safety of the sheeting and bracing
installation should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor His representative who is continuously present on site
will have the best opportunity to cbserve any changing conditions
during construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpected high
groundwater level unsuitable construction sequence or techniques
etc which might affect the excavation support stability
Even with good construction procedure some movement of the sheetpile
walls may occur because of changes in earth stresses during
excavation This depends greatly on workmanship e g how quickly
and tightly the sheetpiles and the bracing support are installed It
also depends on the types of subsoils the depth and width of
excavation and the type of support

accurately predict movement of a retaining system and the adjacent
ground Therefore it is important that the excavation support

possible sloughing of the excavation faces Such monitoring is

Dewatering
Dewatering of the deep excavations will be necessary where the
existing groundwater level is above the excavation bottom
The granular subsoils are relatively permeable and may be susceptible
to piping and quick conditions Therefore dewatering operations should
be done without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and
should be coordinated with the excavation support so that the excavation
is not jeopardized The dewatering operations should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening at the bottom of the
excavation
Dewatering Method
The method and equipment used for the dewatering operations should be
the choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
considerations It should be realized that the dewatering system
chosen will have an effect on the penetration depth of sheeting used

considered in determining a suitable method of dewatering
a The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to the
existing site improvements
b The method should maintain adequate excavation stability and
provide safe working conditions
_c The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modification
to accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering by means of a well point or deep well system along the
outside of the excavations could be used This method could reduce

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of bearing stresses against the bottom of a sewer pipe
has a great impact upon the load carrying capacity of the pipe
itself Pipe bedding is therefore an important design
consideration
Due to the prevailing high groundwater level and the soft loose
subsoil conditions we recommend that a two foot thick crushed rock
cradle be used to stabilize the bottom of the utility trenches and
provide a working platform for the pipe installation The rock
cradle material should consist of clean granular material such as
ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel and should be brought up to within six 6
inches of the invert grade or to the bottom of any concrete jacket or
cradle
We also recommend that clean granular material such as ASIM C 33 No
67 gravel be used as the bedding material for the new utility A
minimum of six 6 inches of compacted or clean granular bedding
material such as ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel should be placed below the
new pipes The granular bedding should extend beyond the sides of
the pipes for a minimum of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and
also half way up both sides of the pipes The recommended pipe
bedding detail is also illustrated on Plate 5
Where dense sand and gravel subsoils are encountered at the bedding
subgrade above groundwater level the two foot rock cradle cushion
below the pipe bedding may be deleted
