DISCUSSION
1 All deleterious material such as lumber logs brush
roots and rubbish were disposed of offsite prior to
placing fill
2 All loose and compressible materials were removed to
solid natural ground or bedrock in areas to receive fill
3 Surfaces to receive fill were scarified and bladed to a
depth of 6 inches watered and mixed as required to achieve

Fill consisting of on site material was placed in horizontal

optimum moisture conditions and compacted to a minimum of
85 of maximum density
Field density tests were taken at vertical intervals of
approximately 2 feet Areas of fi11 which were found to
contain insufficient moisture and or failing tests were
reworked until passing test results were obtained
Fill placed on surfaces having a slope gradient steeper
than 5 1 were keyed and benched into firm material in
accordance with recommendations of the above referenced
preliminary report
Compaction on the face of fill slopes was achieved by
over filling and cutting back
The transition zone of fill over cut slopes was stripped
of all topsoil prior to placing fill
Transition lots were overcut four feet and refilled with
compacted fill to provide a uniform pad bearing surface
and to prevent differential settling or heaving between

This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations
made to the slopes or pads on the subject tract subsequent
to the issuance of this report without our knowledge and
written approval All ramps made through slopes and pads
and other areas of disturbance which require the placement
of compacted fill to restore them to the original condition
will not be certified unless such backfilling operations
are performed under our inspection and supervision and tested
for required compaction Loose material cast over certified
compacted slopes shall negate our certification of slope
face compaction unless material is removed in accordance
with our instructions
If this office is to certify backfill placed in utility
trenches and behind retaining walls then such backfilling
operations must be performed under our inspection and
supervision and tested for required compaction Of major
concern are utility trenches beneath or adjacent to structures
Trenches in excess of three feet in depth will require a
density test for every two feet of compacted fill placed
All trenches will not be tested but rather a random sampling
will be made to insure that the compaction methods employed
