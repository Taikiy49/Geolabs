Dear Mr Lee
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation design recommendations for the proposed residence
The study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory
testing of the swelling potential of the soils reviewing of the
subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary house foundation
plans This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden structure approxi
mately 40 by 66 feet in plan dimensions A basement level with CMU
retaining walls are also planned

Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the basement level garage
driveway and the open lanai
Rock masonry CRM walls are planned along the perimeter of the lot
and vary from 2 to 12 feet high Continuous wall for the residence
will range from 2 to 10 feet
The layout plan for the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the eastern side of Kuini Street in the
Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Lot 91 slopes upward from Kuini Street at about a 5 1 H V ratio to a
relatively level pad which then continues on a 2 1 H V bank about 5
feet high along the eastern boundary of the lot
A concrete lined drainage ditch is located along the top of the bank
at the rear of the site
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 0 to 11 0 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt will generally be suitable for
foundation support of the proposed house provided that some care is
exercised during construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that its swelling potential may increase to moderate if
allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize the swelling potential
the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and protected
from drying during the period of time required for preparation prior
to final concrete placement

Minimum footing embedment should be 12 inches below the outside
finished grade for on grade slab thickened edges and 18 inches for
spread footings measured from the lowest adjacent finished grade to
the bottom of the footing
An allowable soil bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for these
footings bearing on undisturbed stiff soils
The bottom of all footing excavations should be examined by a soils
engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been reached and that
the soil conditions are consistent with those encountered during the
field exploration
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the site will have cuts up to 7 feet at
the basement level and small fills of generally less than one foot
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1279 00 Page 5
Fills and backfills should consist of hon expansive soil such as
select borrow and be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and then
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill it should
first be approved by a qualified soils engineer and should generally
be placed wet of optimum moisture content 30 to reduce the
swelling potential
Slab On Grade
For the on grade slabs planned for portions of the house and footing
excavations additional special procedures are recommended to further
reduce the swelling potential of the on site soil
After excavation and prior to final concrete placement special lime
saturation is recommended The general procedure is listed below
1 Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
We believe that about ten to fifteen 50 1b bags of hydrated lime
would be adequate to cover these areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
The subgrade soils should be water saturated for at least 2 days
with perforated hoses prior to concrete placement to reduce the
swelling potential of the underlying subsoil It is important that
the soils engineer should be notified to inspect the above subgrade
preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying soils
prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course and should be separated from wall foundations

A vapor barrier membrane should be placed over the base course layer
if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor
covering
House Foundation
Continuous spread footings may be used for the proposed residence An
allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used on the stiff
on site soil
The bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade cleaned of loose material
and bear on firm soil It should also be examined by a soils engineer
to see that adequate bearing soil has been reached and that the loose
soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that CMU basement walls and CRM retaining
walls are planned for the house foundation The guidelines listed
below for wall design should be followed
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 45 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth

2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff soil
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure does not apply for walls on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can also be used against
sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations should maintain a minimum embedment of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls Any subdrain should be
properly constructed and drain freely by gravity onto lower
open space
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
