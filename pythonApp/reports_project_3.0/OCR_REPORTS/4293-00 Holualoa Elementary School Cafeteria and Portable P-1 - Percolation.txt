Dear Mr Taylor
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed
percolation testing capacity testing for the leaching field at Holualoa Elementary School
within the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii This letter report summarizes
our observations during the percolation testing at the subject site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The subject project involves the construction of a cafetorium multi purpose
building and relocation of an existing portable building within Holualoa Elementary
School In addition a wastewater septic system consisting of sewer lines septic tanks
soil absorption bed approximately 58 feet x 77 feet in dimension and appurtenances
will be constructed to service the above mentioned buildings The soil absorption bed
leaching field will be excavated to a depth where a permeable sub stratum is
encountered
SUMMARY OF PERCOLATION TESTING
The percolation rate testing capacity testing for the leaching field at Holualoa
Elementary School was performed on A representative from
Geolabs Hawaii performed and observed the percolation testing of the leaching field
Based on our field observations a 10 foot by 77 foot strip at the center of the
leaching field was left unexcavated leaving two separate pits The depths of the
leaching field range from about 9 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface In

general the subsurface conditions at the leaching field consisted of closely fractured
basalt rock to depths of about 8 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface The basalt
rock was underlain by clinker materials extending to the maximum depths excavated of
about 9 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface Several lava tubes were also
observed within the leaching field
The project specifications state that the percolation testing shall be approximately
100 to 150 gallons per minute gpm until the daily flow volume of approximately
10 000 gallons is reached The percolation test consisted of injecting approximately
112 to of water for a total of about 12 280 gallons Water was injected into
the two separate leaching field pits within the southwest corner of each pit
Approximately 7 650 gallons of water was injected into the western side of the leaching
field and approximately 4 630 gallons of water was injected into the eastern side of the
leaching field
Based on our field observations no significant head build up was attained during
the injection of water into the leaching field A head build up of about 2 to 3 inches was
measured within an area of about 5 foot by 5 foot at the southwest corner of each pit
The results of the percolation tests are presented on Plates 1 through 3 It is our
opinion that the percolation rate testing capacity testing was performed in accordance
with the general intent of the project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
Our services were performed in accordance with the project plans specification
and generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice The findings
and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from field observations short term injection testing and verbal communications
with personnel at the site This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of
Keauhou Kona Construction Company for specific application to the Holualoa
