Dear Mr. Curran: 
In accordance with the request from Medline Industries, Inc., we have been 
providing earthwork observation and compaction testing services for the above project.  
Our field observations indicated that coralline materials mixed with clayey soils were 
encountered below the building slab and pavement areas at the above project.  Based on 
our laboratory test results performed on the materials, it appears that the clayey soils are 
slightly to highly expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations.  As stated in our 
geotechnical engineering report dated February 2, 2016 prepared for the development: 
Building Slab Area 
Where the clayey soils are encountered at the slab subgrade level (i.e. bottom of 
the slab cushion), we recommend over-excavating the clayey soils a minimum of 
12 inches and replacing with non-expansive, select granular fill material 
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
Pavement Area 
Where clayey soils are encountered at the pavement subgrade level, the clayey 
soils should be over-excavated a minimum of 6 inches and replaced with 
aggregate subbase material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
compaction. 
Based on our evaluation of the field conditions and laboratory test results, we 
recommend implementing the following measures during the subgrade preparation work.  
The different areas described below are referenced in the attached map.

Area A
Clean coral exposed at existing grade. No further action required.
Area B
Coral mixed with slightly expansive clayey soils. We recommend providing
6 inches of non-expansive select granular material (such as the on-site coral or
Grace Pacific 3-inch minus material) below the 6-inch aggregate subbase layer.
Area C
Coral mixed with highly expansive clayey soils. We recommend providing
12 inches of non-expansive select granular material (such as the on-site coral or
Grace Pacific 3-incus minus material) below the 6-inch aggregate subbase layer.
Area D (Driveway, Parking, and Loading Dock
Coral mixed with highly expansive clayey soils.
AC Pavements: Add 6 inches of select borrow below the base course layer
PCC Pavements: Increase the 6-inch aggregate subbase to 12 inches.
