PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing BRAVO Docks B20 and B21 project site is the easternmost facility
along the waterfront at the Naval Shipyard facility at Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii It provides two berths along the east side of the east repair basin The dock is a
reinforced concrete structure mostly supported by precast concrete bearing piles except
for the in board edge of B20 which is supported by a continuous spread footing There
are approximately 844 concrete bearing piles and most of them are 16 inch square
precast and conventionally reinforced as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
At dock B21 both out board and in board edges of the dock are supported on
longitudinal pile rows capped with cast in place longitudinal girders Transverse beams
span between the out board girder and the in board girder or footing to add support to the
8 inch thick concrete deck The deck slabs include longitudinal duct and transverse
beams The standard gauge tracks are no longer used and have been paved over with
asphalt Several utility vault structures are also included under the dock Along most of
the in board edge of the dock at B21 the fill behind the structure is retained by a
SSP steel sheetpile retaining wall
Based on the study conducted by another firm we understand that the existing
bearing piles are generally in good condition however cracking and spalling of surface

METHODOLOGY OF EVALUATION
We understand that the purpose of the project is to evaluate the capability of the
existing BRAVO docks B20 B21 to resist the potential large loading from a combination of
Naval vessels Our approach to achieve this evaluation is based on the following
assumptions
1 The conditions of the existing foundation are similar to the as built plan
drawings
2 The existing piles are in good condition
3 The existing dock superstructure is considered as a rigid reinforced concrete
diaphragm because the piles are flexible relative to the deck structure
stiffness
4 The tie rods behind the continuous spread footing in the area of dock B20
are ignored due to the possibility of corrosion
5 Our analysis is based on loading information provided by Sato
Associates Inc dated on
Our analyses consisted of lateral analysis and estimation of uplift resistance for pile
foundation system and sliding friction and passive earth pressure estimation for
continuous spread footing The computer programs Group 4 0 and Lpile Plus 3 0 were
used for pile foundation lateral analysis
In general lateral load resistance of piles is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of the pile and
induced moment in the pile Program Group 4 0 and Lpile Plus 3 0 were developed by
adaptation of a finite difference method to analyze the behavior of piles in a group or a pile
subjected to axial lateral loadings and overturning moment from the pile cap to piles For
Group 4 0 the program requires a symmetrical groups arrangement Both programs
compute deflection shear bending moment and soil response with respect to depth in
nonlinear soils for each individual pile The analysis was carried out with the use of
internally generated non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli A solution requires
iteration to accommodate the nonlinear response of each of the piles The equations of
equilibrium are satisfied and compatibility is achieved between pile movement and soil
response and between the movement of the cap and the pile head movement
The programs can model various pile head boundary conditions and various pile
properties as a function of depth The group geometry pile properties soil properties and
loading conditions were input into the programs based on available information loading
information provided by Sato Associates Inc and our previous experience in the
general area The individual concrete pile was assumed to be cracked and an
appropriately reduced 50 percent modulus of elasticity was used

Recommendations
Our analyses indicated that higher lateral deflection occurs in the beginning and
end of Area 1 due to relatively higher lateral loads and less piles Higher bending moment
is also induced in the piles within these areas In addition maximum tension force induced
in the piles exceeds the estimated uplift resistance of piles
Based on our analyses we believe that the existing piles foundation in the areas
near Station 0 04 and Station 3 22 may not be able to resist the lateral loads from
mooring lines alone As indicated previously due to the unknown existing conditions of
the continuous spread footing caution should be used when the sliding friction of the
footing will be mobilized to resist the lateral loads We believe that the sliding friction of the
continuous footing should not be used to resist the outboard directed lateral forces without
further investigation We believe that deep foundation system may be installed on the
landside of the continuous spread footing for additional resistance
The deep foundation system may consist of 36 inch in diameter cast in place
concrete drilled shafts to resist the lateral mooring loads The drilled shafts should be
structurally connected to the existing deck to integrate into the existing transverse beams
of the decks
Lateral load resistance of drilled shafts is a function of the stiffness of the shaft
stiffness of the surrounding soils allowable deflection at the top of the shaft and induced
moment in the shaft Based on the subsurface soil conditions indicated on the drawings
provided we have performed preliminary engineering analyses to estimate the shaft
length and lateral load resistance Based on our analyses we believe that the drilled shaft
length of 50 feet may be adequate to resist about 20 kips lateral loads acting on the top
the shaft associated with a lateral deflection on the order of 2 inches
