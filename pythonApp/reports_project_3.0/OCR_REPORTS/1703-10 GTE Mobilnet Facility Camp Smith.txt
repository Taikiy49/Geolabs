PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The GIE Mobilnet network for the island of Oahu includes several
cell sites and a switch which together will create a mobile communication
network
The facility at CINCPAC Headquarters Camp H M Smith includes a new
12 by 20 feet by 10 feet high prefabricated fiberglass building ona
permanent foundation
Two 13 foot fiberglass omni antennas will be mounted on the top of
the existing penthouse on Building No 1 These antennas are not included
in our scope of work
SITE DESCRIPTION
The new building will be located behind Building No 1 CINCPAC
Headquarters The area is presently landscaped and surrounded by access
roads The topography is relatively flat to level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled at the site to depths of 12 and 15 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The two borings encountered residual soil consisting of stiff
reddish brown clayey silt to the depths drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed relatively competent subsoils at the
new building site
Shallow spread footing foundation may be used for the building
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the stiff
in situ soil Minimal settlements are anticipated for the structure
loads
Design lateral earth pressures and unit weight are as follows
a Active soil pressure 45 p c f
b Passive soil pressure 270 p c f
c Soil moist unit weight 110 p c f
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing soil have
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropirate
sample depths

APPENDIX _ B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One one inch ring swell test was performed on a natural undisturbed
sample to evaluate the swelling potential of the soil The test results
are summarized on Plate B l
One direct shear test was performed to evaluate the shear strength
parameters of the natural soil The test results are plotted on Plate
B 2
