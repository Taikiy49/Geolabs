Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Keahole Power Plant Unit ST 7 at
73 4249 Queen Kaahumanu Highway in the Kailua Kona area on the Island of Hawaii
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is near the southeast corner of the intersection of Queen Kaahumanu
Highway and Keahole Airport Road as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided it is desired to perform resistivity surveys at the
proposed locations using the Wenner 4 Pin Method The Wenner 4 Pin Method provides
an average value of the subsurface resistivity over the tested depth The resistivity survey
was conducted in accessible areas at the specified locations requested Detailed
discussions and the field resistivity test results are presented in the following sections of
this letter report
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our survey was to obtain in situ resistivity data in general
accordance with the Standard Test Method for Field Measurement of Soil Resistivity Using
the Wenner Four Electrode Method ASTM G 57 The scope of our work consisted of the

Field Survey
Geophysical survey equipment i e ground resistance testing equipment was
mobilized to the project site on August 7 and 8 2007 for the field resistivity survey
Seventy nine resistivity tests were conducted at six selected locations designated
Wenner 4 Pin Method and an AEMC Model 4610 ground resistance testing equipment
manufactured by AEMC Instruments The method utilizes four surface probes or pins
electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the underlying mass The average
resistivity values of the subsurface mass extending from the existing ground surface to the
test depth was assessed by using this method along the ten test reference lines shown on
the Resistivity Survey Plan Plate 3 Results of the resistivity surveys are presented on
Plates 4 1 through 4 6
The in situ resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along
the reference lines shown on the Resistivity Survey Plan The average soil resistance
along the proposed alignments was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings as shown on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 The depth of penetration of
a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing
The ground resistance testing equipment AEMC Model 4610 provides a voltage
between the outer electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The
voltage drop between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in

On the basis of the field resistivity test results the majority of the test results for the
subsurface soils within the project site have in situ resistivity values ranging from about
12 600 to 528 500 ohm cm
Limitations
The analyses and findings submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from our field resistivity surveys The field resistivity surveys are
based upon information obtained from field data points by means of geophysical surveys
Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the field data points may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings
provided in this report
Hawaii Electric Light Company Inc staked out the field resistivity test locations
indicated herein The physical locations of the resistivity survey locations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
