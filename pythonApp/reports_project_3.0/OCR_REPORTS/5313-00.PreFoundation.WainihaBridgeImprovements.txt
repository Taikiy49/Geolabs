As requested this letter provides preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the bridge foundations for the Wainiha Bridge Improvements project
located on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our preliminary recommendations are
based on subsurface information from the completed borings for the project and
information provided by the structural and hydraulic engineers
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves three bridges crossing three branches of the Wainiha River
along Kuhio Highway Route 560 in the Hanalei District on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The bridges are designated as Wainiha Bridge No 1 at Mile Marker Bridge
Mile Marker 6 73
The existing bridges are structurally deficient and in considerable disrepair due to
their age and the corrosive environment All three bridges have deteriorated to the point
where it is not feasible to conduct temporary repairs due to loss of section to the main
structural members caused by corrosion The existing bridges are ali one iane wide
Except for the short 3 C span each span consist of six parallel steel trusses supporting
timber joist and topped with a wood plank wearing surface Each truss is an inverted king
or queen truss with short center posts and two steel tension rods Span 3 C has simple
steel beams instead of truss elements
Kuhio Highway is the only road serving the north shore of Kauai and there is no
means of detouring around a road closure for the proposed work Any construction plan
must be implemented with minimal road closures A major concern is the low load
capacity of the bridges at 8 tons maximum The 8 ton limits are serious limitation for
businesses and government agencies servicing the community along the highway as well
as major safety issue for emergency vehicles

alluvial deposit generally consisted of loose to medium dense silty sand A very soft to soft
swamp deposit consisting of organic silty clay was encountered within the alluvial deposit

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration the preliminary foundation loads provided
and the preliminary scour depths we recommend that deep foundations consisting of
drilled shafts be used for foundation support of the new bridge structures The drilled
shafts will extend through the loose to very soft alluvial and swamp deposits and into the
underlying medium stiff to hard and medium dense to very dense alluvial deposits
