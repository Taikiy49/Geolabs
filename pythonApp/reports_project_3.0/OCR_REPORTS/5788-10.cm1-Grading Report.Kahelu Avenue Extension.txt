PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the extension of the existing Kahelu Avenue by the
construction of approximately 800 lineal feet of new roadway and infrastructure within the
right of way The project site is in Mililani on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Site Plans Plates 1 1 and 1 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work from November 2007 to
October 2008 The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation
placement and compaction of fills up to about 4 feet in thickness to achieve the design
finished grades Paradigm Construction performed the site grading and utility backfilling
work During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 13 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared over excavated surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion the site grading and utility installation work for the Kahelu
Street Extension Project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references
1 Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended
2 Report by Geolabs Inc entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
