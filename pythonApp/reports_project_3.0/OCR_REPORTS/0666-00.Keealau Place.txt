PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a total of six single family residential
units on the northern 31 000 square foot portion of the approximately 180
foot wide by 450 foot long parcel A recreation center will be adjacent to
the dwelling units
The dwelling will be of post and beam construction Some grading will be
required at the driveway area with the retaining wall
A ten foot wide roadway will connect the homes and recreation center to

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Ulumawao peak and associated ridge is part of the rim of the caldera
of the old Koolau shield volcano The job site is on this ridge Just
east of the property there is an excellent outcrop where several dikes
can be seen in the decomposed basalt formation The material in the
subject area is a weathered partly decomposed gray green basalt In
the area where the houses are to be built the basalt is a multicolored
weathered partly decomposed basalt of gray green blue hue but upon
breaking down it appears brown in color There are many angular cobbles
in the silty matrix qualifying the material as a breccia
The surface soil is a light brown silty clay with large angular cobbles of
the underlying parent basalt The soil layer is very thin about 12 inches
thick and grades into the underlying weathered partly decomposed
fractured basalt formation
Surface
Presently an approximately 10 foot wide road easement runs along the
western portion of the property It has a two to three inch layer of
crushed coral surfacing The makai end of this roadway is overgrown with
tall grass Much of the fine material in the crushed coral has been eroded

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A LT
General
Based on the results of our limited geologic reconnaissance it is our
opinion that the site can be developed for the proposed residential
development
Erosion Control
During heavy rains erosion of the upper foot of the slope will occur
Site grading should provide drainage away from the slope surface and
should run off the front area toward the road pavement The existing
trees and vegetation on the slope surface provide ground cover for
erosion control and slope stability protection Site clearance and
grubbing on the existing slope should be minimized for building construction
Foundation
It is our opinion that the in place decomposed and weathered basalt rocks
below the steep slope surface can support the proposed residential structures
Spread and line footing foundation can be utilized to support the post and
retaining wall footings The footing should bear a minimum of 18 below
final adjacent grade into stiff or decomposed materials
For estimate purposes 3 000 p s f bearing capacity may be utilized

W
Footing On Sloping Portion
A minimum set back distance of 3 feet should be maintained from the
outer edge of footing measured horizontally out to the slope surface
If fractured rock formation is encountered at the individual footing
excavations the highly weathered and fractured surface portion should
be removed prior to footing concreting
Post footing should be a minimum of 18 inch square and its concrete
poured in place After the posts have been attached to the footings
concrete should be used to backfill the excavation up to the top of
the individual excavations
Earthwork Site Grading
Filling on the slope surface is generally not recommended to establish
additional space for driveway construction Keying and benching would
be required prior to additional fill being placed on this slope surface
Roadway
The existing cut roadway which is about 10 feet wide has been covered
with approximately 2 to 3 inches of crushed coral Much of the fine
material in this coral surface has been washed away
