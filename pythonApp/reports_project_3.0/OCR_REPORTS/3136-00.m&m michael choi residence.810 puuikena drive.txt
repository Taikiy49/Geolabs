INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Choi residence at 810 Puuikena Drive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 2
within the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single family residence at 810 Puuikena Drive within
the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on the information provided to us the subject lot is approximately
rectangular in shape with an area of approximately 9 965 square feet The proposed
residence will be a two story structure with slab on grade construction We understand
that a swimming pool will also be included in the proposed project construction
Based on the information provided to us maximum column loads are estimated
to be on the order of 20 kips per column with wall loads of approximately 1 kip per lineal
foot Construction of new utility lines and asphalt pavement for the driveway are also
anticipated for the project
The subject property is presently vacant with a sparse cover of vegetation The
existing ground elevations range from about 915 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the
southwestern portion to 928 feet MSL at the northeastern portion of the site Based on
the proposed finish floor elevations of about 918 to 925 feet MSL grading consisting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 3
of minor cuts and fills up to three feet 3 is anticipated to achieve the design finish floor
elevations for the proposed construction
Our previous experience in the area suggest that the project site may be underlain
by residual soils over decomposed basalt material Special attention will be given to the
potential swelling characteristics of the near surface soils on the future floor slabs
supported on grade

conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations
and site grading for the project The work was done in general accordance with our
proposal to Mr Kenneth C C Chang of Innovative Architectural Designs Inc dated

site and its vicinity

field geologist of our firm

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by very stiff residual soils
consisting of silty clays at shallow depths Based on the competent subsoil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 6
encountered at the project site it is our opinion that conventional shallow foundations
consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed single family residence
Laboratory tests performed on the on site silty clay soils indicate that the material
generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a layer of
capping fill consisting of non expansive select borrow to reduce the potential effects of
the moderately expansive near surface soils Detailed recommendations are presented
in the following subsections
Foundations
We understand from conversations with the project structural engineer that light
structural column loads on the order of 20 kips per column are anticipated for the
proposed construction Because of the generally competent subsoil conditions
encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may
be used for support of the proposed new residence
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on the stiff in situ soils encountered at
shallow depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 7
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Building foundations located alongside the swimming pool should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom of the retaining walls or as deep as the retaining wall footings
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the undisturbed stiff
clayey soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative Compaction or the footing bottom be extended down to the
undisturbed firm in situ soil Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 8
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing
conditions and the embedment depths
Slab On Grade
The project site is underlain by a deposit of silty clay soils which are considered
to be moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs
supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to
lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these
soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately expansive soils we
recommend that the concrete slabs be supported on 18 inches of non expansive select
fill material The non expansive select fill should extend at least two feet 2 beyond the
perimeter of the proposed structure
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The prepared capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report The select fill would serve as a
capping fill layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils
and this capping fill would also reduce the potential loss of moisture from the prepared
moist subgrade soils The reduction in future subgrade saturation should reduce the
potential swelling of the underlying moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction
requirements for the subgrade soils and select fill are discussed in the Site Grading
section of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 9
We recommend that a minimum four inch 4 thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be installed below the slab to provide
uniform support for the slab
To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches 2 of moist fine sand may
be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid
in curing of the slab concrete
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are anticipated for the swimming pool construction and at other
locations of the project site for grade separation General guidelines for the design of the
retaining wall structures are as follows
1 Wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum
depth of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade For sloping
ground the footing should extend deeper to obtain a minimum eight foot
set back distance measured horizontally from the outisde edge of the
footing to the face of the slope
2 An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the
design of wall footing foundations bearing on the in situ stiff soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 10
3 For conventional retaining wall an active earth pressure of 40 p s f
equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth is recommended For at rest
earth pressure a value of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of
depth may be used
The surcharge effect from loads adjacent to retaining structures should also
be included in the design of the wall A rectangular distribution over the
height of the wall with a pressure equal to 35 and 50 percent of the
surcharge load is normally used for active and at rest conditions
respectively
4 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used
to evaluate the resistance against sliding of the walls
5 Backfill directly behind the retaining walls should consist of select well
draining self compacting granular material with sizes generally between
three fourths inch and one fourth inch 4 such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to reduce the compaction effort required
The upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious
material to reduce water infiltration behind the wails
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be utilized
behind the walls to protect against build up of hydrostatic pressure
Subdrains should be properly constructed to drain water away from the
wall

Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading for the proposed project will consist of minor cuts
and fills up to about three feet 3 to achieve the design finish floor elevations for the
project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 18 inches below the
future slab or pavement subgrade level such as bottom of No 3B Fine gravel cushion
layer or bottom of base course layer If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are
encountered at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be
required to expose firm material The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
compacted select granular fill material The clayey material resulting from the over
excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site
Areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of eight inches 8 moisture
conditioned to at least two percent 2 above optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 85 percent relative compaction Select granular fill material should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such as
crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than three inches 8 in largest dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 12
and a swell potential of not more than one percent 1 when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No
200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site
Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavement for the access driveway is anticipated for the project
construction We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement
designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will consist of at least 18 inches of
imported select granular fill material The imported select granular fill material should be
compacted to 95 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils below the select
granular fill material snould be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement sections be used for preliminary
design purposes
2 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Base Course 95 percent compaction
8 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on 18 Inches of
Compacted Select Granular Fill
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density tests should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy
of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 13
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping
in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of two
inches 2 into the subgrade soil below the base course aggregate to reduce migration
of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a _ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up
to about one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in maximum eight inch 8 level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper two feet
2 of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95
percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3136 00 Page 14
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from features shown on the Topographic Survey Map dated
March 11 1993 prepared by Technical Field Data Service Inc Elevations of the borings
were estimated by interpolation from the elevation contours on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition

representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and

evaluate the swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plate
B 1

evaluate their shear strength characteristics The test results are presented on Plate B 2
