the results of the field permeability testing performed at the wash down area at the Hana

The findings are intended to assist in the design of the drywells for the disposal of wash
down run off only The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the
end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves repair improvements to the damaged rock revetment in the
vicinity of the boat launching ramp at Hana Bay on the Island of Maui Hawaii The
revetment was apparently damaged by high waves and is undermined thus causing
roadway collapse
Based on the updated information provided we understand that it is proposed to
include a wash down area as part of the boat ramp improvement project Therefore field
permeability testing is desired at the proposed wash down area to evaluate the infiltration
characteristics of the subsoils in the shallow subsurface The information will be used to
formulate geotechnical recommendations for the design of drywells for the disposal of
wash down run off
PURPOSE AND SCOPE

information will be used to assist in the design of the drywells for the disposal of wash
down run off Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the drywell site were explored by drilling and sampling
one boring designated as P 1 to a depth of about 23 5 feet below the existing pavement
surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring generally encountered cinder materials consisting of medium dense
sandy gravel extending to a depth of approximately 8 5 feet below the existing pavement
surface Below the upper cinder materials medium dense beach sand deposit overlying
basalt boulders and dense sandy gravel were generally encountered and extended to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 23 5 feet below the existing pavement surface
We encountered water in the boring at a depth of about 7 9 feet below the existing
pavement surface at the time of our field exploration The measured water level
corresponded to about 1 6 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW It should be noted that
water levels are expected to vary with tidal fluctuations storm surge conditions seasonal
rainfall and time of year Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the boring

FIELD PERMEABILITY TESTING
Two field permeability tests were conducted at the proposed wash down area to
evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered at the
boring location The permeability tests were performed in Boring No P 1 as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the planned drywells will be installed at a depth of about 4 feet
below the ground surface Therefore the field permeability tests were performed at about
4 and 8 feet below the existing pavement surface Both falling and constant head
permeability tests were performed to determine the average hydraulic conductivity of the
underlying subsurface materials Based on our field exploration the subsurface materials
encountered in the upper 8 5 feet generally consist of medium dense sandy gravel soils
The permeability test results are presented in Appendix C
The hydraulic conductivity k value is calculated as 2 0 x 10 cm s and
cm s at a depth of about 4 and 8 feet based on the falling and constant head
field permeability test results respectively Based on our evaluation we believe that the
higher k value of 2 9 x 10 cm s is more representative of the permeability of the sandy
gravel material encountered in our drilled boring Therefore it is our opinion that this
k value may be adopted in the design of the drywells planned for the project

APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Testing
APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Testing
The permeability of the in situ materials at the wash down area was evaluated by
performing both falling and constant head tests in Boring No P 1 The falling and constant
head tests were performed at depths of about 4 and 8 feet respectively below the existing
pavement surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Casing was used for drilling and testing
During the falling head testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to the
top The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which to
base calculation of the permeability value of the subsoils The test results of the falling
head permeability test are presented on Plate C 1
The constant head test was performed by pumping water into the borehole until the
water surface has stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a sufficient period of time The
water height and the rate of pumping were then measured to provide data upon which to
base calculation of the subsoil permeability The test results of the constant head
permeability test are presented on Plate C 2
