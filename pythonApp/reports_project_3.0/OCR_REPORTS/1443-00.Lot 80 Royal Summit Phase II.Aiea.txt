Dear Mr Prevo
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story structure approximately 45 by 69 feet in plan dimensions
The general layout of the house foundation is shown on the Site Plan
Plate l

The house will be of wood frame construction with concrete
slab on grade for the garage driveway and the entire house
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls up to 6 feet high are
planned extending along the northern and half of the eastern property
line
Cuts generally less than 7 feet deep are anticipated for the lower
level of the house
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northeastern corner of Kaahele and
Naalii Streets in the Royal Summit Phase II Subdivision in Aiea on
the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad about 20 feet wide is parallel to Naalii Street
at the front portion of the site The ground surface then slopes
upward at about 5 1 H V to a second level pad at the rear of the
lot The ground surface then slopes upward to the adjacent lot at a
2 1 H V slope for a vertical height of about 6 feet
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 80 to depths ranging from 5 7 to
10 9 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate l

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site silty clay soil
indicates that this material generally has low swelling potential if
maintained at or above the optimum moisture content 30 more or
less However previous experience in the area indicates that some
of the on site soils can exhibit medium to high swelling potential if
allowed to dry out first and then subsequently wetted Therefore to
minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site soil below the on grade slabs be capped
with at least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1443 00 Page 4
subgrade soils underneath the above select fill also be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until the select
fill and slab concrete placement are completed
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive imported soil such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral The on site soil under the
garage driveway and lower level of the house areas should also be
over excavated to provide the 12 inch minimum non expansive select
fill capping The underlying remaining fill beneath the non expansive
fill cap and other areas may consist of either off site borrow or the
on site soils in the Royal Summit area that generally have low to
moderate swelling potential
Imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The moisture content of the
on site fill should be kept within 5 percentage points wet of the
optimum during compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1443 00 Page 5
Fills placed on existing slopes having gradients steeper than 5 1
H V should be keyed and benched into the existing ground to bond the
new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the garage driveway and the entire house will
be of slab on grade construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future outside surface moisture infiltration
under the slab
Post and wall footings located on the existing slope should be further
embedded down a sufficient depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing to the
Slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1443 00 Page 6
resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the required
footing confinement An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be
used for footings on stiff on site soil The footing excavation
should be examined and approved by the soils engineer
Swales should be provided to divert surface water away from
foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and the
entire house It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay
soils are moderately expansive and therefore should be
over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom
wetted and then replaced with non expansive imported soils as
previously discussed The over excavation and backfilling should
extend a minimum of one foot beyond the edge of the slabs The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Similar over excavation and replacement of the moderately expansive
heaving silty clay soils are also recommended for the lanai slabs
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the soil below the capping fill pad
be kept moist continuously

A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided below
the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that CMU retaining walls up to 9 feet high may
be required for grade separation between the garage and lower levels
of the house and portions of the lot perimeter The guidelines for
the retaining wall designs are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade If
located on a sloping area the foundation should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a minimum 6 feet horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing to
the slope face
3 An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff on site
soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1443 00 Page 8
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
6 Backfill behind the house retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel
7 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
