Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Area F 8
Ewa by Gentry
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project
from March through November 1993 The subject site is located to the west of Fort
Weaver Road and is bounded by Area 6 to the north and west Fort Weaver Road to
the east and Areas 8A and 9 to the south within the Ewa by Gentry Development in
the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The mass grading operations at the above referenced site was performed by
RHS Lee Inc and the infrastructure construction work was performed by Oahu
Construction The mass grading work generally consisted of scarifying and compacting
the existing subgrade and placing up to about 4 to 5 feet of new fills The available on
site fill materials generally consisted of sandy coral gravel with silt and reddish brown
silty clay
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The fill material was then placed
in near level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry
density Road subgrade capping fill material consisting of sandy coral gravel and
base course were compacted to a minimum 95 percent of its maximum dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method

Results of the compaction tests for the mass grading operation and infrastructure

Our services consisted of periodic field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures and observation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above grading operations
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to
their use in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this
report
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Field observations by our field technician during the mass grading operation and
infrastructure construction for the project indicate that this work was performed in
accordance with the general intent of the recommendations presented in our
geotechnical engineering report entitled Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Ewa by Gentry Areas dated
Site Grading

Lot owners should be advised not to block the surface drainage and to obtain
professional consultation in the planning design and construction of any future
improvements
