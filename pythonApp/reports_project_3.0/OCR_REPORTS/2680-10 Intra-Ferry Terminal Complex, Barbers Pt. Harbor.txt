Indicator Pile Driving Report
Intra Ferry Terminal Complex
Barbers Point Harbor Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated we have observed the
indicator pile driving operations conducted between and January 8
1993 at the above referenced project site Three 3 test piles were driven for the
indicator pile program
A Delmag D80 diesel pile hammer was used to drive the test piles at the
approximate locations shown on Plate 1 The piles were 20 inch square precast and
prestressed concrete piles which were casted to 90 feet length Based on information
provided by the pile driving contractor we understand the pile hammer has a rated
energy of about 54 200 foot pounds A copy of the field indicator pile driving records is
attached for your reference
Indicator Pile Predrilling and Driving
Generally all three 3 indicator piles were predrilled to depths of about 15 feet
below the approximate top of coral formation The predrilling procedure consisted of
initially vibrating a 34 inch diameter temporary steel casing to between 10 to 18 feet below
the top of coral formation A steel H beam with a cutting tool was used to spud or
predrill down to at least 15 feet below the approximate top of the coral formation
Cuttings from the spudding operation were removed by airlift to clean the hole The test
pile was then inserted into the hole and the pile was driven to at least the minimum
driving criteria recommended in our report
The three 3 test piles were driven to approximate tip elevations of 41 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL at Bent 3 62 feet MSL at Bent 5 and 61 feet MSL at Bent 7 The
pile tip elevations are about 17 to 18 feet below the approximate top of the coral

ATTACHMENT A
Revised Predrilling Pile Driving and Grouting Procedures
Set temporary platform
Survey in and set templates for 34 inch steel pipe casing
Vibrate casing approximately 15 feet into the coral formation on correct batter
Vibrate H beam with cutting tool to a depth of at least 15 feet below the
approximate top of the coral formation Confirmation of the depth of predrilling in
the field by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii is strongly recommended
Remove H beam with cutting tool and insert an 8 inch airlift
Remove cuttings and clean hole with airlift
Remove airlift and insert 20 inch square concrete pile
Drive pile to required pile driving criteria Driving criteria is a minimum of 40 blows
per foot for the last one foot of penetration or at least 8 blows per inch for the last
2 inches of penetration These driving criteria is based on the Delmag 30 hammer
with a rated energy of 54 200 foot pounds used in the indicator pile program
Driving criteria would have to be re established if different driving equipment are
used
Following pile driving operations insert one solid tremie pipe to the bottom of the
predrilled hole and place grout in casing to fill the void between driven pile and
predrilled hole Each pile should be grouted with at least 3 to 4 cubic yards of
grout to maintain at least a 5 foot positive head and to account for possible grout
loss Top of the grout should be sounded and should be at least 8 feet above the
top of the estimated top of coral formation before removal of the casing
The grout mix should consist of a sand cement slurry mix minimum three parts
sand to one part cement should have a minimum 28 day compressive strength
of not less than 4 000 psi and should be flowable
The steel casing should be extracted slowly by about 15 feet such that the bottom
of the casing is below the approximate top of coral formation The steel casing
should be maintained at this location for at least 10 minutes After the 10 minute
waiting period the casing may be completely extracted
The procedure should be repeated for each production pile
