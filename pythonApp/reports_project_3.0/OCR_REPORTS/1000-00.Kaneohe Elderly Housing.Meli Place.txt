SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff
to very stiff soils upon which spread
footing could be used to support the
proposed 6 story structure
Portions of the structure are situated
on or near sloping areas and therefore
deeper footing embedment is required
to resist downslope creep movement and
to develop the full bearing capacity of
the soil Alternatively the building
should be set back away from the top
slope
The near surface soils are low to
moderately expansive and will require
some special procedures for slab on
grade and pavement construction

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
performed at the site of the proposed Kaneohe Elderly
Housing located on Meli Street in Kaneohe The location
of the site with respect to the surrounding area is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed Kaneohe Elderly Housing will consist of an
L shaped 6 story high structure on the flat portion of the
Site located adjacent to the existing steep slopes along
the eastern side of the site Asphaltic concrete parking
areas are planned over the relatively flat western portion
of the site Access to the project would be from Meli Place
Maximum column loads are anticipated to be about 250 kips
with shear wall spacing of about 22 feet on center
Site grading will require minor cut and fill Finish floor
elevation for the L shaped structure is planned to be about
72 5 feet
It is our present understanding that the existing slopes

along the northern and eastern side of the property will
generally remain the same and no major construction on
the existing slopes is planned
Geology
The site of the development is located roughly midway be
tween the Pali and the coast on the windward slope of the
Koolau Range This area is part of a deeply eroded and
weathered section typified by thick alluvial deposits
overlying volcanic dike complex These areas are basically
mapped as consolidated older alluvium The extended
period of erosion has carved out smaller drainageways and
resulted in some deposition of more recent alluvium
Surface Conditions
As shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 the development site
is located north of Meli Place off Kamehameha Highway in
Kaneohe The site of the proposed development is situated
on a relatively level terrace at about the same eleva
tion as Kamehameha Highway and Meli Place Along the
northern and eastern boundaries the ground surface slopes
for a vertical height of 8 to 15 feet The site is bor
dered on the west by a relatively level commercial area
located between Kamehameha Highway and the subject property
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P 4
The ground elevations at the site vary generally from about
76 to 78 feet at the upper level development area to about
60 feet in the lower drainageways Heavy vegetation covers
most of the site and slopes Water was not noted in the
drainageway at the time of our field exploration
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were investigated by
drilling eight 8 exploratory borings at the locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The depths of borings
ranged from 15 0 to 46 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
Based on the information revealed from the drilled borings
the site is generally underlain with stiff to very stiff
clayey silt with some highly decomposed gravel consoli
dated older alluvium
Groundwater level was encountered in boring and
8 at 28 to 31 foot depths during our field exploration
Some wet zones were noticed in some borings at various
depths probably due to rainfall seepage
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed generally stiff to very
stiff foundation bearing soils at the proposed project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P 5
site From a soil engineering point of view the site is
considered suitable for the proposed development
Shallow foundation support could be used for the propo
sed structures except where they are situated on or near
the steeply sloping areas On sloping areas deeper
footing embedment is required to develop sufficient
foundation support
Is is anticipated that site grading for this project
would be minimum However a few feet of fill would be
required to raise the building site up to the design
elevation of 72 5 feet At the present building location
portions of the southern building wing are situated over
the existing steep slope and would require retaining
walls to retain this fill along the slopes Due to the
difficulty of constructing a retaining wall on sloping
areas an alternate grading scheme utilizing cut in this
area instead of filling in order to create a level area
could be considered Alternatively if practicable the
buildings should be moved back a minimum distance of 6 feet
away from the top of slope to eliminate the need for re
taining walls and deeper footing embedment
Laboratory swell tests obtained on the on site soil sam
ples indicated low to moderate expansion potential

Therefore conventional slab on grade with special construc
tion procedures described under sub heading Slab on
Grade should be used
Specific recommendations for the foundation design and
construction are presented in the subsequent sections of
this report
Foundation
Spread footing foundations are recommended for the pro
posed 6 story structure An allowable bearing pressure
of 4 000 p s f could be used for footings bearing on the
very stiff undisturbed clayey silt material underlying
the site The above value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for temporary
loads such as wind and seismic
On level areas the bottom of the footings should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the outside finish
grade However footings located on or near the top of
Slope should be embedded an additional amount in order to
minimize the possibility of loss of ground support for the
structure due to erosion and slope creep and to develop
the full bearing capacity of the soil Therefore footings
on or near sloping areas should be embedded a sufficient
depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back dis
tance which is measured from the outer edge of the footing
out horizontally to the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P 7
Anticipated total and differential settlements for founda
tions bearing on the natural ground are estimated to be on
the order of less than 4 inch and 4 inch respectively
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 350
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead load times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be
used provided that passive pressure is reduced by one
third
Site Preparation and Earthwork
Minor cuts and fill generally up to about three feet are
anticipated for the general site grading
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry den
Sity established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density
Retaining walls and backfill would be required for portions
of the building situated near or on the existing slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P 8
Backfill on sloping ground must be properly keyed and
benched into the natural ground to bond the fill to the
natural ground and prevent excess pressure on the walls
The reddish brown on site soils have low to moderate ex
pansion potential and are acceptable fill and footing
backfill material if they are compacted on the wet side
of optimum moisture content Any additional off site
borrow required should be tested and approved by the soil
engineer prior to transportation to the site The borrow
material should be non expansive and should not contain
boulders organic or deleterious material
Slab on Grade
The laboratory swell tests indicate that the on site soils
have low to moderate swell potential therefore it is
recommended that slab on grade should have deeper peri
meter footings extending down a minimum of 12 inches below
the finish grade to reduce ground movement due to drying
and wetting around the slab All slab subgrade should
be kept adequately moist prior to concrete placement to
minimize future heaving
It is recommended that slab on grade should be placed over
a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maxi
mum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P G
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring mater
ial a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls would be required for the portions of
the building situated over the existing slope Well
graded granular material such as base course rock should
be used as backfill material behind the walls
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations Parti
cular attention should be focused on the footing set
back requirement where foundations are situated on sloping
areas If practicable these walls on steeply sloping
ground should be avoided by relocating the structure away
from the slope or cutting down the area to generate addi
tional level area
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pres
sures as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equiva
lent fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equiva
lent fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge should be
included where applicable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1000 00 P 10
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 350 p c f can be utilized However
the passive resistance should be reduced by one third
when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Driveway and Parking Area Pavement
Asphaltic concrete driveway and parking areas are planned
adjacent to the proposed structure on fairly level areas
The laboratory CBR tests indicate that the near surface
on site soils have moderate expansion potential especially
when it is used as a compacted fill This swelling charac
teristic would require thicker pavement sections on the
subgrade of fill area
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the remolded on site
reddish brown clayey silt surface soil the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness

W O 1000 00 P Il
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of construction and the
above design sections can be revised accordingly
The select borrow should be crusher run quarry waste
coral sand or cinder compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density The subgrade should be shaped to drain water
away and avoid ponding below the pavement section
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading footing excava
tions and backfill operations be observed by a soils engi
neer during construction to see that adequate bearing and
compaction have been obtained and that the subsurface con
ditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
