Dear Mr. Breeden: 
This revised letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations 
performed during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the new tank 
foundations for the Replace JPTS Fuel Tanks project located at the Joint Base Pearl Harbor-
Hickam between August 2 and 17, 2017. This revision reflects updating design bottom of 
footing elevation and drilling equipment on the probing and grouting record summary and 
individual logs. The probing and grouting observation work was performed in general 
accordance with our scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated November 29, 
2016, and subcontract change order from Reliable Contracting Group, LLC dated August 
11, 2017. 
Project Considerations 
The proposed Replace JPTS Fuel Tanks project is located at the Joint Base Pearl 
Harbor-Hickam on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. It is generally bounded by Moffet Street to 
the northeast, McClelland Street to the south and existing facilities to the west. As part of 
the site work package for this project, probing and grouting at the foundation locations was 
conducted during construction due to the potential presence of cavities and/or voids within 
the coralline formation underlying the project site.

Probing and Grouting Program 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the foundations 
supporting the new fuel tanks in general accordance with Specification Section 31 23 00.00 
20 Paragraph 3.9 Probing and Grouting. The purpose of the probing and grouting 
operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the 
collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the new structure. 
The general contractor, Reliable Contracting Group LLC, surveyed the footing layout 
and marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations. In general, 
the probe holes were spaced at approximately 5 to 15-foot intervals along the wall footings 
and every 50 square feet for the spread footings. The approximate probe hole locations and 
identification are shown on the Floor Plan, Sheet S-101. 
Geolabs, Inc. performed the probing and grouting work for the project using a 
CME-45C truck-mounted drill rig and a CME-45C track-mounted drill rig, both equipped with 
4-inch diameter, solid-stem augers.  In general, the probe holes were about 4 inches in 
diameter and extended to a depth of about 15 feet below the bottom of footing.  The 
materials encountered during the probe drilling operation generally consisted of tan and 
brown sandy fill materials and brownish gray to dark gray clayey lagoonal deposits overlying 
coralline formation. A 12-inch thick concrete slab was encountered at approximately 7 to 8 
feet below ground surface in the northern portion of the site. After completion of the probe 
drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a sand-cement pumpable Controlled Low 
Strength Material (CLSM) grout.  The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by 
Hawaiian Cement and pumped into the probed locations via tremie method by a boom pump 
truck operated by Ohana Concrete Pumping Co., Inc. 
It should be noted that the grout volume stroke counts were estimate and provided 
by the boom pump truck operator. Aggregated grout volume per stroke was calculated 
based on total volume of the grout placing in the probe holes and total of accumulated stroke 
counts.  
During the drilling operation, 13 probe holes encountered a concrete slab at 
approximately 7 to 8 feet below the ground surface. These 13 probe holes were re-drilled 
on August 16 and 17, 2017 to a depth of about 15 feet below the ground surface. 
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe depths and approximate grout take. A total of 41 probe holes, including the 13 re-
drilled probe holes, with an aggregate length of about 520.5 lineal feet below the ground 
surface was drilled for the new fuel tank foundations. Approximately 5.5 cubic yards of sand-
cement CLSM grout was used to fill the probe holes drilled for the project.  A summary of 
our foundation probing and grouting observation is provided on the Summary of Probing 
and Grouting Record, Plates 1 through 3. Details of drilling and grouting (Field Probing and 
Grouting Record) are provided in Appendix. 
It should be noted that the ground surface was prepared by the contractor prior to 
our probing and grouting operation, to near the bottom of the footing or below bottom of the
