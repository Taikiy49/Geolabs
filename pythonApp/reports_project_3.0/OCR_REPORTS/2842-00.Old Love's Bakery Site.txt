Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report
Old Love s Bakery Site
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed at the old Love s Bakery site in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions near an area at the northeast boundary of the site for the determination of the
Stability of the adjacent building during the pumping out of groundwater from a pit in the
central portion of the site
Project Considerations
The project site is located in the Kapahulu area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
During the site s former use as Love s Bakery there were several underground fuel
storage tanks at various locations throughout the site
As part of the site cleanup process it is necessary to pump groundwater out of
a pit located at the central portion of the lot for treatment Concern has been expressed
about the stability of the adjacent two story commercial building along the northeastern
boundary of the site during the pumping out of groundwater from the pit
It is our understanding that two pumps with the combined capacity of 20 gallons
per minute will be utilized to pump the groundwater out of the pit for treatment

The scope of our work consisted of determining the stability of the adjacent

Subsurface Conditions
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 borings to a
depth of 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled along the northeast boundary of the site
The boring drilled in the northwest portion of Area B generally encountered a
surface layer approximately 4 feet in thickness consisting of very dense to dense tannish
white sandy coralline gravel underlain by tannish white coral formation to a depth of 8 5
feet Below the coral formation a basalt formation was encountered to 26 5 feet depth
the maximum depth drilled
The boring drilled in the southeast portion of Area B generally encountered a
surface fill layer approximately 8 feet in thickness consisting of medium stiff to hard gray
and brown silty clay with traces of basalt and coral gravel underlain by a 6 inch thick layer
of soft gray brown clay with basalt sand and gravel Below the clay layer a basalt
formation was encountered to 26 5 feet depth the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the borings from 5 to 8 feet below the existing
ground surface at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered and laboratory test results are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Findings and Recommendations
Dewatering of a site can cause ground settlement in adjacent areas in certain soil
conditions During the lowering of the groundwater level the unit weight of the previously
saturated soils increases due to the loss of buoyancy and thereby increases the vertical
stress on the underlying materials This increase in stress to the underlying materials may
cause detrimental settlements and instability of adjacent structures if the underlying
materials are soft or loose

From our exploration a hard basalt formation was encountered at a depth of 8 5
feet below the existing ground surface while groundwater was encountered about 5 to 8
feet below the ground surface
Because the hard basalt formation was encountered at a relatively shallow depth
and the existing groundwater level was approximately 1 to 3 5 feet above the top of the
basalt it is our opinion that pumping out of groundwater from the pit will not cause
excessive settlement and instability to the adjacent building within the limits of our
exploration
Furthermore since a coral deposit which is relatively pervious was noted at the
sides of the pit area it is our opinion that a significant drawdown of the groundwater will
probably not occur from pumping out at a capacity of 20 gallons per minute
