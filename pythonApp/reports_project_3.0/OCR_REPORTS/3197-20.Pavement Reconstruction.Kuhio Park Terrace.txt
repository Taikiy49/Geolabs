SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally
underlain by medium stiff to stiff silty clays over basalt
formation for the depths explored
Laboratory testing indicated that the surficial silty clay soils
encountered at the project site exhibit moderately high
shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Unless new pavements supported by these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress resulting from
the shrinkage and swelling of these underlying clayey soils
due to changes in the soil moisture content
We recommend that the clayey subsoils beneath the new
asphaltic concrete pavements at the Family Center Parking
Lot be over excavated to a minimum depth of 18 inches
below the bottom of the base course layer and replaced with
select granular fill For the new portland cement concrete
pavements at the Apartment C Parking Lot the over
excavation may be reduced to a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the bottom of the base course layer The over
excavation process may be terminated if hard basalt formation
is encountered
For temporary cosmetic repair purposes a minimum asphalt
concrete overlay thickness of 1 5 inches may be considered
for the subject project However it is our opinion that a
subbase layer consisting of 18 inches of select granular
material would be required below the base course cushion to
establish long term satisfactory performance of the pavement
sections
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and special design recommendations

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the pavement reconstruction project at Kuhio Park Terrace in Kalihi on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located in the Kalihi area on the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to reconstruct the existing pavements at the Apartment C Parking
Lot and the Family Center Parking Lot
Our previous project experience in the area suggested that the project site may be
underlain by adobe clay soils over basalt formation at shallow depths Particular
attention was given to the swelling potential and strength properties of the near surface
adobe clay soils

conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design of the new
pavements for the subject project The work was done in general accordance with our

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the Kuhio Park Terrace in the Kalihi area on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The Apartment C Parking Lot is bounded by Apartments C 6
C 7 C 8 to the north and Linapuni Street to the south The Family Center Parking Lot
is located to the southeast of Apartment C Parking Lot and across Linapuni Street
Our field reconnaissance indicated that the entrance areas of the existing A C
pavements at both parking lots have deteriorated and signs of significant pavement
depressions were observed

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by medium stiff to stiff silty
clays over basalt formation for the depths explored Laboratory tests performed on the
surficial silty clay soils indicate that the clayey soils exhibit moderately high shrink swell
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations New pavements supported by these
subgrade soils need to be designed for future distress resulting from the shrinkage and
swelling of these underlying clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content Details
of our pavement design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Pavement Design
Based on the information provided to us the pavement reconstruction work at the
subject site will involve the following scope of work
1 Removal and replacement of the existing asphaltic concrete pavement at
part of the Family Center Parking Lot with new asphaltic concrete
pavement and placement of asphalt concrete overlay for the remaining
parking areas
2 Removal and replacement of the existing asphaltic concrete pavement at the
entrance of the Apartment C Parking Lot with new portland cement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3197 20 Page 6
concrete pavement and placement of asphalt concrete overlay for the
remaining parking areas
New Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
hk Sf
Due to the presence of the expansive subgrade soils encountered at the Family
Center Parking Lot we recommend that the clayey subsoils beneath the new
asphaltic concrete pavements be over excavated to a depth of at least 18 inches
below the bottom of the base course layer and replaced with select granular fill
The over excavation process may be terminated if hard basalt formation is
encountered If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the
over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be required to remove
these soils to the underlying firm material The resulting over excavation should
also be backfilled with compacted non expansive select fill material The clayey
material resulting from the over excavation should be removed and disposed of off
site
The over excavated subgrade surface should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above optimum moisture content and lightly compacted to about 85 to
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
Select granular fill material needed for the 18 inch replacement capping layer
should be placed as soon as possible after the over excavation and recompaction
of the existing clayey subgrade soils The prepared new select granular fill and the
underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of asphaltic concrete Select granular fill snould be placed in level lifts not
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3197 20 Page 7
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
Select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such as
crusher waste crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should
be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20
percent passing the No 200 sieve
The base course cushion between the asphalt concrete and the underlying select
granular fill should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Our pavement section recommendations are as
follow

Based on our field exploration results hard basalt formation may be anticipated at
a depth of about 18 inches below the existing ground surface If hard basalt
formation is encountered during the over excavation process it is our opinion that
the expansive subgrade clay over excavation process may be terminated and the
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular fill material
Where rigid pavements will be used we recommend that pavement joints and joint
sealants be provided Joint sealants should be used to provide a seal to reduce
potential infiltration of water and to provide a compressible joint We believe
sufficient joints should be provided in rigid pavements for the following reasons
1 To reduce potential curling and warping stresses in the pavement caused
by temperature and moisture gradients across the pavement slabs
2 To reduce and control cracking due to volume changes in the concrete
3 To facilitate construction

Asphalt Concrete Overiay
2 a
We understand that asphalt concrete overlay is planned for part of the existing
A C pavements at the subject project Since our field exploration disclosed that
the project site is underlain by expansive adobe clay soils at shallow depths it
is our opinion that the asphalt concrete overlay could only serve as a temporary
cosmetic measure and potential pavement distresses may still occur in the future
It should be understood that a subbase layer consisting of 18 inches of select
granular material as recommended above would be needed below the base course
cushion for long term satisfactory performance of the pavement sections To
accomplish this pavement section it would involve re construction of the existing
parking pavement areas
For asphalt concrete overlay a minimum overlay thickness of 1 5 inches is
recommended Prior to asphalt concrete overlay the existing asphalt concrete
surface should be thoroughly swept to remove all loose materials A tack coat
should be placed on the existing asphalt concrete to bond the new asphalt
concrete overlay to the existing pavements
As previously indicated some portions of the pavement have deteriorated and
potholes were evident Where the pavement has deteriorated the portion of the
deteriorated pavement should be repaired by removing the deteriorated pavement
section with loose to soft subgrade soils The resulting excavation should be
backfilled with aggregate base course compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3197 20 Page 10
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs be extended a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade soils below the base course aggregate to reduce migration
of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system is
recommended to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we believe that a subdrain system should be considered adjacent to
paved landscape areas
We recommend that the project geotechnical engineer Geolabs Hawaii be
retained to provide the field observation and monitoring services during the over
excavation and backfilling operations at the subject project in order to observe the actual
exposed subgrade soils compliance with our recommendations and to provide additional
consultation if necessary

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from features shown on the Site Plan provided by Paul Louie
and Associates Inc on March 30th The physical locations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
GEOLABS HAWAI

interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
