This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnais
sance performed for the residence of Mr Mrs Tom Nance at Lot 59 of the Waialae
Iki V Subdivision in the district of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field reconnaissance laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design input and may
not be appropriate to form a basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor
for construction bidding purposes

Site Grading
Based on the available site plan the proposed finish floor elevation for the
residence is at 890 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Grading for the proposed residence
will consist of minor cuts and fills to provide a level building pad for the new residence
Additional cuts are also anticipated in the concrete driveway and garage area on the west
side of the new residence The following recommendations are presented to provide
general guidelines for the site grading work
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the areas within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
2 In areas to receive fill because of the high moisture content of the on site
soils the subgrade should be proof rolled to provide a firm and unyielding
subgrade surface if the subgrade soils have a moisture content in excess
of 5 percent above the optimum moisture content
If the subgrade soils have a moisture content less than 5 percent above the
optimum moisture content then the subgrade soils should be scarified to
a depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the

optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
3 Generally the on site clayey soils should not be reused as fill material If
embankment fill is desired the fill material should be either imported select
granular fill or non expansive select on site rocky material possibly located
below the highly expansive silty clays The fill material should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to 90
percent relative compaction
If imported fill is required the material should meet the requirements for the
select granular fill material
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability of its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii before delivering to the site Only select granular fill
should be used for slope embankment and within the upper 24 inches
below the finish subgrade in building and slab areas The select granular
fill should extend at least 2 feet beyond the edges of the building and slabs
The select granular fill material should be non expansive granular material
such as select borrow crusher waste crushed coral mudrock or cinder
The select granular fill material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
should contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve On site
soils are not suitable for select fill due to their high expansion potential

5 All fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in

6 In order to properly bond new fills to the existing ground for the filling on the
slope we recommend that the existing slope be benched Fill slopes

Slab On Grade
The laboratory tests indicated that the subsurface soils at the project site have high
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content
GEOLABS HAWAII

Therefore to reduce the potential for distress to the new residence due to the
shrink swell effects of the near surface soils we recommend that the concrete
slabs be supported on 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill material The
non expansive select granular fill material should extend at least 2 feet laterally
beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
The select granular fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The select granular
fill would serve as a layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive
subgrade soils and would reduce the loss of moisture from the subgrade soils
The reduction in drying should reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the
underlying expansive clayey soils Compaction requirements for the subgrade
soils and select granular fill are discussed in the Site Grading section of this
report
Slab on grade construction may then by utilized for the proposed residential
structures constructed on the above prepared capping granular fill layer
However it should be noted that minor cracks in the slab can be expected
Therefore we recommend that generally a flexible floor covering be used on the
slab We also recommend that interior walls be designed to incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate some possible movements
The prepared capping fill should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of concrete Concrete floor slabs should be supported on a minimum 4 inch layer
of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 Gradation above the capping fill to
serve as a slab cushion and capillary break

The thickened edge around the slab on grade should be embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade An allowable bearing
pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design The
allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces The slab subgrade
should be kept moist prior to concrete placement The backfill against the slabs
should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
Slab thickness and reinforcing should be designed by a Structural Engineer For
slab on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the No 3B Fine gravel cushion to prevent
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab A 2 inch layer of moist fine
sand should be placed over the vapor barrier membrane to aid in curing of slab
concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placement of concrete
Spread and Continuous Footings
Other structures at the new residence may be supported by a foundation system
consisting of spread or continuous wall footings An allowable bearing capacity
of up to 2 500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of 24 inches is
recommended The allowable soil bearing pressures is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for transient applications such as
temporary wind and or seismic loads

If footings are located near the top of slopes or on slopes the footing should be
embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance from the outside edge
at the base of the footing to the edge of the slope is not less than 8 feet
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry prior to placement of concrete
if shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
The individual footing bottom preparation should be monitored during construction
by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to evaluate if adequate bearing capacity is
obtained prior to placement of reinforcement and concrete

Concrete Pavement
We anticipate the garage area and driveway to be of concrete pavement
construction Our preliminary pavement design assumes the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the near surface silty clay soils encountered during our field reconnaissance
If site grading exposes soil other than those assumed additional tests should be
performed to confirm or revise the recommended pavement sections for actual field
conditions If imported fills are used for the proposed roadways the subgrade on which
the pavement structural section is to be placed will need to have a minimum California
Bearing Ratio CBR of 6 Based on the above assumptions the following pavement
section may be used for preliminary design purposes
Rigid Pavement

12 0 Inch Select Borrow 95 Percent Relative Compaction

concrete slab on 12 inches of select borrow

APPENDIX A
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory tests were performed on soil samples obtained during our site
reconnaissance A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate
tests to aid in evaluating pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials retrieved
Classification Tests
Frequency the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content is used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in evaluating
certain properties such as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a
percentage of the dry weight of the samples tested are presented on Plate 1
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of the soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining
to the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests were performed to evaluate the swelling potential of the soil
These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support and fill Two
1 inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded samples of the clay soils to evaluate
the swelling potential of the soils in remolded conditions The test results are summarized
on Plate 1
