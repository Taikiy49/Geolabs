PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project area is located in lower Palolo Valley approximately mile to the
north of the H 1 Freeway The site is bounded by Pukele Avenue to the west and
Mokuna Place to the east as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on the pre
liminary construction plan and profile of the project ground surface elevations range
from about 130 to 180 feet Mean Sea Level

main and appurtenances beneath Pakui and Hinahina Streets It is our understanding
that either polyvinyl chloride PVC or ductile iron pipe is planned for use as the new
8 inch water main at the subject site In general the average invert of the proposed
water main is anticipated to be between 4 and 6 feet below the existing paved ground
surface with some localized dips to about 8 to 12 feet below the existing paved ground

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume Ill dated 1991
and subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Performance of 47 in situ soil resistivity tests at 16 locations along Pakui
and Hinahina Streets These locations generally correspond to the
300 foot spacing maximum required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume Ill 1991
2 Coordination of the field work by an Engineer Geologist from our firm
3 Reduction and analysis of the field resistivity data pertaining to the
corrosion characteristics of the soils at the site

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e electrical resistance meter was mobilized
to the site on January 10th and our field work was performed in sunny and generally
dry surface conditions
Forty seven separate soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 16 locations as
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 4 using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a
resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance
meter has a rated output of 120 volts and 24 watts
The Wenner 4 Pin Method utilizes four copper surface pins electrodes driven
into the ground surface at calculated spacings An electrical current is applied to the
electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the underlying soils beneath the pin
layout The average electrical resistivity of the soil mass extending from the ground
surface to the planned invert elevations of the water main were assessed by using this
method at the test locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 4
The resistance meter R 40C induces a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be read directly from the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and electrode spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistance the computed resistivities and the
corresponding corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 5 1 through 5 4
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion

The soil resistivity tests were conducted at the existing ground surface elevation
on the exposed vegetated shoulder area located in between the concrete curb and
sidewalk at each of the test locations as shown on the Site Plans These test locations
were selected to be as close to the new water main alignment as possible in open
areas where sufficient space would allow the placement of the electrode pins
The four electrodes were placed at equal spacings of generally 3 to 9 feet
between pins Among the variable electrode spacings used for each survey one
spacing may have more significance than the other two allowing greater contribution
to the measured soil resistivity from the soil mass nearer to the proposed pipeline invert
elevation The more significant spacing and therefore more representative resistivity
data for the soil located near the pipeline invert is marked with an asterisk on the
resistivity test results provided on Plates 5 1 through 5 4
that Test 4 6 and 7 were not significantly influenced by the close proximity
of potential current conductors such as water line laterals or other buried utilities and
structures The results of these control tests indicate that minimal interference was
encountered
Test Nos 2 and 11 were conducted to evaluate the electrical resistivity of the
soils located at the depths of the localized dips in the invert elevation of the pipeline
alignment Due to the depth of penetration required to obtain the deeper resistivity
values much wider electrode spacings were needed Therefore the tests were
conducted in areas where the wider electrode spacing could be accomplished Based
on the test results it appears that the deeper soils do not vary significantly in electrical
resistivity from the soils nearer to the ground surface
LIMITATIONS

from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report

having been estimated from features and reference points shown on the undated
