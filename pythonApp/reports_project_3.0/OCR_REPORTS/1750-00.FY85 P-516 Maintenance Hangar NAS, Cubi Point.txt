In accordance with your request we have performed laboratory testing
for soil cement mixtures for the above referenced project

This report summarizes our laboratory soil cement testing results
se_and Sco of Work
The purpose of our laboratory testing was to provide a soil cement
mixture utilizing SP SW and SM ML soils which could develop an unconfined
compressive strength of 500 psi after 28 days of curing
Testing Procedure
The proposed soil cement construction procedure to be utilized for
the P 516 Maintenance Hangar project consists of injecting a cement
slurry through a drilled hole into the underlying sandy soils then
mixing the in situ sand and cement slurry within the drill hole
Although this field procedure cannot be exactly duplicated in the
laboratory the laboratory testing procedure attempted to maintain the key
elements in the soil cement mixture

The various components of the tests and their effect on the mixture
Soil
The existing soil below the Dungaree Beach site consists of coralline
sand with generally less than 20 percent fines The Unified Soil
Classification of the material is predominantly SP SW to
Two types of soil were used in the laboratory testing Both
consisted of coralline sand one with about 2 percent fines USC SP and
one with about 24 percent fines USC SM The gradation curves for both
soils are presented on Plate 1
We believe that the sandy soils used in the laboratory tests are
similar to the sandy soil below Dungaree Beach
Moisture Content of Soil
The sandy soils were tested ina saturated condition to similate the
field condition
Mixtures using various moisture contents were tested It was found
that the maximum sand moisture content which could produce a uniform
mixture was about 20
Cement
Type I Portland cement was used in the soil cement mixture Cement
contents of 10 20 and 30 of the dry weight of the sand were used in the
testing
The cement was mixed with water 1 1 to form a slurry then added to
the saturated sand
Density
In order to evaluate the effect of the soil cement density on the
unconfined strength after curing the specimens were subjected to varying
compaction efforts during placement However it was found that due to
the saturated condition of the sand the resulting densification que to
the varying compaction efforts was minimal and all of the specimens ended
up with similar densities

Laboratory Testing Procedure
In general the testing procedure was as follows
1 The saturated sand was mixed with the cement slurry
2 The mixture was placed into plastic containers 3 inches in
diameter by 6 inches high One set 3 specimens was placed into
the containers with minimal compaction effort A second set 3
specimens was placed with a high compaction effort A third set
was placed with a medium compaction effort
3 The samples were cured for 3 7 or 28 days prior to compression
tests
4 The samples were tested in a concrete compression machine
The test results for the various soil cement mixtures are shown on
