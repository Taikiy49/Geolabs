INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed road development project from the town of Koloa to Poipu
on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Koloa District on the island of Kauai Hawaii It
is proposed to construct a bypass road from the vicinity of the town of Koloa to the Poipu
resort area
The proposed bypass road will consist of approximately three 3 miles of road with
a paved travelled way of two 2 lanes totaling twenty four 24 feet in width with ten 10
foot unpaved shoulders on each side
The proposed roadway alignment will start from the north at a point on Maluhia
Road approximately five hundred 500 feet south of Wailaau Road and proceed
southeast crossing Waihohonu Stream approximately five hundred 500 feet north of
Wailani Road The route will then bend around the eastern edge of the town of Koloa and
proceed south to the northern boundary of the McBryde Sugar Company fields at Weliweli
Road continue southward along the western border of the fields traversing the western

Pee Street
Grading plans were not available at the time of preparation of this report It is
anticipated however that site grading will require localized cuts and fills up to ten 10
feet in vertical dimension

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Koloa District on the southern coast of the island
of Kauai and extends from a point on Maluhia Road approximately five hundred 500 feet
south of Wailaau Road to the Poipu resort area
The proposed roadway alignment crosses Waihohonu Stream approximately 1 500
feet from the northerly terminus at the town of Koloa

The terrain is in general gently rolling and slopes towards the south Near the
southern terminus the proposed alignment traverses the western flank of a low hill Puu
Wanawana
The area traversed by most of the alignment is predominately agricultural with
residential development in the northern sector and resort development just beyond the
southern terminus
The vegetation of the area is predominately sugar cane and pasture grasses in the
agricultural areas In underdeveloped areas the vegetation varies from dry land cover
such as haole koa in the south to sub tropical forest such as java plum mango and
guava in the north
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Kauai was built up by a single large shield volcano During the
Tertiary period basaltic lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series built a roughly
circular island to a height over 17 000 feet above the surrounding sea floor Near the end
of the Tertiary Period this main shield building volcanic phase came to a halt
Following a long period of erosion and subsidence vulcanism was renewed during
the Pleistocene epoch with the Koloa Volcanic Series This post erosional volcanic activity
laid a veneer of fresh lava over the older Waimea Canyon Series across most of the
western half of the island
After the cessation of this period of renewed vulcanism the geologic forces of
weathering erosion and deposition during the late Pleistocene and the Recent epochs
have shaped the island to its present form

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed bypass road alignment is
underlain by competent subsoils
It is anticipated that medium dense to dense clinker and saprolite deposits and
hard basalt formations will be encountered at shallow depths less than one 1 foot to
approximately eight 8 feet below much of the southerly portion of the road alignment
Hard excavation and or ripping may be anticipated for cuts into the hard basalt
formations encountered
Stiff to medium stiff alluvial and residual soils are anticipated along the proposed
alignment north of Koloa

Site Grading
Although no grading plans are available at this time it is anticipated that cuts and
fills will be generally limited to less than ten 10 feet
Site Preparation
Prior to initiation of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth
of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and replaced with compacted non
expansive select material
Fill Placement
The on site clayey silt and silty clay soils are suitable for use in the compacted fill
Rocky material may be generated if site grading excavation extends deeper
than about one 1 to three 3 feet It is anticipated that only a small percentage
of the excavated rocky material will be less than eight 8 inches in size and could
be incorporated in the fill Since deep fills are not anticipated at this project any
excavated boulders larger than eight 8 inches in size should be disposed of off
site
GEOLABS HAWAIT
W O 2726 00 Page 9
After site preparation the fill should be placed and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 in 10 inch compacted lifts The fill within two 2 feet of the subgrade
level should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Where the existing ground is steeper than five 5 horizontal to one 1 vertical
5H 1V keying and benching are required to properly bond the new fill to the
slope The filing operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above structural fill require
ments Details of the keying and benching requirements are shown on Plate 3
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is required it should
be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
to see that it is suitable for its intended purpose The imported fill material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than three 3 inches in
maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Although grading plans are not available at the time of preparation of this report
it is anticipated that cut and fill heights will generally not exceed ten 10 feet
Based on this assumption it is recommended that fill slopes of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V be used for preliminary design
It is recommended that preliminary design plans profiles and earthwork sections
be provided to the project geotechnical engineer as early as possible for review
and evaluation This will allow confirmation of the recommendations and or

Earthwork Quantities
Based on our laboratory test results and previous experience it is anticipated that
the soil materials encountered during the course of construction will experience a
decrease in volume when excavated processed and used as fill material In general the
net change will depend upon the actual in situ density of the material the density to which
the material is compacted and the amount of handling losses or waste Based on our
laboratory test results a shrinkage factor of about 18 may be anticipated for the soil
materials This does not include any factor for handling losses or waste
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2726 00 Page 11
It is our further anticipation that the rock materials encountered along the project
site will experience a volume change when excavated processed and used as fill material
In general the net change will depend upon whether the material is clinker saprolite or
dense basalt the average particle size of the material as placed in the embankment and
the amount of void space in the compacted fill
Discussions with contractors who have worked with similar materials indicate that
the volume change can range from a factor of about 0 75 decrease in volume for loose
clinker to as high as 1 5 increase in volume for massive dense basalt
Pavement
The proposed road will be located both in cut and on fill The road subgrade
material may consist of alluvium residual soil or rock
Based on the results of laboratory CBR tests on the site materials the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if necessary The
recommended section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided
for areas adjacent to paved areas Since the shoulder areas will not be paved it is
essential that they be graded to drain water away from the pavement section and not
allow water to pond next to and soften the subgrade and base course layers

Culvert Structure
A box culvert structure is planned at the Waihohonu Stream crossing
Several smaller pipe culvert structures are also planned along the alignment The
foundation design of these pipe culvert structures was not in the scope of our work
Drilling of additional borings may be found to be warranted when their actual locations
and sites are established
General guidelines for the culvert construction and fill placement for the box culvert
structure at Waihohonu Stream are as follows
Trench Backfill
The backfill up to about one 1 foot above the culvert should consist of
select well draining self compacting granular materials with sizes generally
between three fourths 3 4 inch and one fourth 1 4 inch such as 3 Fine gravel
or ASTM C 33 No 67 The backfill snould be lightly tamped to reduce the
possibility of damaging the culverts
The upper portion of the backfill from the level one 1 foot above the
culvert to the finish grade should consist of general fill material as recommended
under the section entitled Site Grading The fill should be placed generally in
maximum ten 10 inch thick loose level lifts and mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 to minimize possible future ground subsidence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2726 00 Page 13
Cushion
A twelve 12 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67
is recommended below the culvert structure to provide drainage and uniform
bearing
Headwaill Structure
Headwall structures are anticipated at the inlet and outlet of the culvert An
allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f may be used for walls resting on the
stiff to medium stiff natural ground or compacted fill material
Well graded granular materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 or sand should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This is to reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the walls
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended for design of the wails
1 Level Backfill K 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Sloping Backfill K 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
K 20p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 4 may be used to determine the sliding resistance
of the walls A passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may be used
Additional loads due to traffic should be included where applicable

A drainage line is planned along the proposed roadway alignment
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples to evaluate the corrosivity

Based on the laboratory test results the upper soil materials encountered along

to the start of construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our fieid
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of the soils The Plasticity Index of the material is
used to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these
tests are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs and are
summarized on Plates C 1 and C 2
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of the soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining
to the performance or suitability of a soil
Proctor ASTM D 1557 78 tests and California Bearing Ratio ASTM D 1883 tests
were performed on selected samples as an aid to evaluating their embankment fill and
pavement support characteristics The results of these tests are summarized on Plate C 1
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate C 3
Corrosion Property Tests
Soil resistivity tests ASTM G 57 78 and pH tests ASTM G 51 77 were performed
on selected samples as an aid to evaluate their corrosion potential The results of these
tests are summarized on Plate C 4
