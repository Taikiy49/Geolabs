PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Ikuwai Place Ikuwai Way Punalau Place Pohaku
Loa Way Kamehameha Highway and adjacent streets in the Haleiwa area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided we understand that it
is proposed to construct new 8 inch and 4 inch water mains and appurtenances along
these streets and Kamehameha Highway for water system improvement The streets
and highway are shown on the Site Plans 1 through 3 Plates 2 1 through 2 3
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known
at this time but they are expected to be about three feet below ground surface In order
to evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was
conducted along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions
and results of our corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter
report

characteristics of the subsurface soils along the proposed 8 inch and 4 inch water mains
planned at the project site Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM
Test Designation G 57 95 and the requirements provided in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey Our field work was performed under sunny and
shower conditions with generally dry to wet surface conditions
One hundred forty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of
300 foot intervais at 37 locations designated as R 1 through R 37 along the shoulder
and grass areas of the streets and the highway using the in Pin Method
and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory
Inc The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivity
values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth was
assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on the Site
Plans 1 through 3 Plates 2 1 through 2 3 Results of the resistivity surveys are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 8
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder and grass areas of the existing roads of the proposed 8 inch and 4 inch water

mains The average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each
survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 9 feet at the
existing ground surface The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of
the electrode spacing Since the depth of the water main invert was not available at the
time of our field exploration a spacing of 3 to 9 feet was used for the electrode spacing
for each soil resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 of this report Field Resistivity
Testing Summaries are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 6 It should be noted that the soil
resistivity may be influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground
facilities and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test locations
indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed water main of Ikuwai Place Ikuwai Way
Punalau Place and Pohaku Loa Way within the project site have corrosion ratings of
2to3 This indicates the subsurface soils to have very corrosive to corrosive potential
Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 4 and 5 were noted
Also a majority of the test locations indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed
water main of Kamehameha Highway and the side street within the project site have
corrosion ratings of 3 to 5 This indicates the subsurface soils to have corrosive to mildly
corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion rating of 2 were
noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
