PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is along Kuhio Highway near the Princeville development in the
District of Hanalei on the Island of Kauai We understand that an approximately 350 to
400 foot long section of the steep slope adjacent to the highway has experienced
near surface sloughing of earth material and localized slope failure The slope section is
about 160 to 185 feet high and is bounded between switchback segments of Kuhio
Highway below Princeville The asphaltic concrete highway in this area has two lanes with
minimal shoulders and the existing slope is heavily forested The project site is in a high
rainfall region with relatively high subsurface soil moisture content
It is desired to stabilize the existing slope failures and reduce sloughing of
near surface earth materials onto the highway using anchored wire mesh It appears that
the slope may consist of moderately to extremely weathered rock and residual and
saprolitic soils with some talus deposits near the slope toe Due to the overall slope
steepness approximately one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V and limited access test
borings were not drilled Therefore we conducted a limited subsurface exploration
consisting of soil sampling from shallow hand excavated test pits at selected locations

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series Following the cessation of this
main shield building phase renewed volcanic activity occurred with the extrusion of
basaltic lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of
thick alluvial sediments of the Palikea Formation
The Island of Kauai is the oldest of the main Hawaiian Islands and today
encompasses approximately 555 square miles with a maximum elevation of about
5 170 feet above Mean Sea Level at Mount Waialeale near the center of the island It is
believed that during the Tertiary Period about 5 to 6 million years ago in the Pliocene
Epoch basaltic lavas belonging to the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series built a roughly
circular island volcano from the ocean floor which is estimated to have been about
20 000 feet deep at the time Near the end of the Pliocene Epoch this main shield
building volcanic phase came to a halt
Following a long period of erosion and island mass subsidence volcanism was
renewed during the early Pleistocene Epoch about 1 5 million years ago with the eruption
of the Koloa Volcanic Series from multiple vents throughout the island This post erosional
volcanic activity laid a veneer of fresh lava and interbedded volcanic sediments over the
older deposits of the Waimea Canyon Series Because of the long period of erosion that
occurred between the eruption of the Waimea Volcanic Series and the Koloa Volcanic
Series the deposits of the Koloa Volcanic Series typically filled the erosional valleys and
depressions that existed at that time Consequently the basaltic rocks of the Koloa
Volcanic Series are commonly found to overlie thick residual soil and alluvial deposits
The dominant geologic processes of weathering landform erosion and sediment
re deposition followed during the late Pleistocene and Recent Epochs Combined with the
erosional and depositional effects of the Pleistocene sea level fluctuations which occurred
in response to the worldwide advance and retreat of the continental ice sheets the Island
of Kauai has evolved to its present form
The project site resides on an erosional escarpment of the broad upland plateau
generally encompassing the Princeville area The escarpment is believed to have formed
by down cutting and erosion as a result of the Hanalei River which borders the project site
towards the south
Based on a review of available geological map information the plateau is underlain
by a thick sequence of extremely weathered basaltic rock saprolite with some volcanic
cinder and ash interbeds belonging to the Koloa Volcanic Series The soil materials
generally consist of in situ basalt rock and cinder that has been deeply weathered to form
silty and clayey saprolitic soils with embedded decomposed rock material These saprolitic
soil deposits extend for considerable depth below the upland ground surfaces Within the

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
As previously mentioned the Kuhio Highway project site is between sections of
switchback roadway in the Hanalei area of the Island of Kauai The top of the south facing
slope is across the highway southerly from the existing Princeville fire station The
bottom of the slope is at the highway elevation of the lower switchback Additional steep
southerly facing slopes extend from the lower roadway down towards the Hanalei River
The slope is heavily vegetated with a complete canopy of brush and trees with some
localized areas of grass vine ground surface cover
A reconnaissance was conducted on under generally fair weather
conditions with no rainfall Ground surface elevations in the vicinity of the existing slope
failures range between about 75 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the lower section of
highway to about 245 feet MSL at the upper section of highway as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The overall general slope inclination is approximately 1H 1V The flatter upper half
of the slope has inclinations averaging about and the lower half of the slope have
inclinations averaging about Near the break in slope inclination approximately
mid slope a steep cliff face composed of hard moderately weathered basaltic rock is
exposed
Two areas of recent slope failure adjacent to the lower section of the highway were
observed designated as Slope Stabilization Areas 1 and 2 as shown on Plate 2 The
larger western slope failure area Slope Stabilization Area 1 as shown on
Photograph No 1 Plate 4 is approximately 100 feet wide and 30 feet high and is bounded
by temporary concrete barriers which were installed to limit debris encroachment on the
roadway The slide area forms a concave erosional feature and contains disturbed
vegetation growth and growth of tall grass The exposed slide scar encompasses a sharp
geologic contact between highly weathered highly fractured medium hard basaltic rock
above and stiff saprolitic consisting of tan to brown colored strongly laminated friable
clayey silt representing a layer of volcanic ash below Typical slope cross section is
presented on Plate 3 1

The smaller eastern slope failure area Slope Stabilization Area 2 as shown on
Photograph No 2 Plate 4 is approximately 60 feet wide by about 20 feet high and forms
an obscure erosional scar among a growth of tall grass It appears to consist of a relatively
thin skin release of near surface materials on the order of about 2 to 3 feet thick Typical
slope cross section is presented on Plate 3 2
Both slides are below a natural concave drainage feature and a prominent hard
basalt rock cliff face The upper slope drainage feature has the potential to concentrate
storm water runoff towards the axis where the drainage feature is truncated by a rock cliff
face During times of heavy surface runoff storm water sheet flow may converge into a
relatively concentrated stream that cascades over the rock cliff to impact the extremely
weathered materials stiff volcanic ash encompassing the existing landslide area It
should be noted that the ash material containing a close micro fractured structure is friable
and susceptible to raveling erosion
The basaltic rock cliffs observed in both areas have a potential to produce rockfall
conditions due to the presence of fractured rock outcrops localized erosional remnant
boulders and other boulder pile accumulations The near vertical rock cliffs extended
down to the highway elevation in the middle of the project site between Station 2 70 and
Station 3 40 designated as Rockfall Remediation Area as shown on Plate 2 Boulders
and larger rock fragments dislodged from the hillside have a very high potential to
encroach on the roadway below During our site reconnaissance many loose boulders
that may have fallen from the slope were observed along the south side of the roadway
beyond the guardrail Because of the narrow roadway condition with limited shoulders we
believe the rockfall hazard potential is high within the project area and adjacent slopes
Although groundwater seepage was not observed during our site reconnaissance it
should be noted that groundwater seepage was reportedly observed emanating from the
slide area in the past

EVALUATION OF ANCHOR WIRE MESH
We understand that an anchor wire mesh system is planned on the lower portion
of the failure slopes The anchored wire mesh system is a slope treatment that involves
the installation of steel wire mesh panels anchored onto the slope face with grouted
soil rock nails in an effort to prevent the soils or rocks from moving falling under the
permanent system The primary intent of this stabilization system is to closely pin the wire
mesh to the slope face as a retention system
Based on the field exploration results we believe that material encountered near
Station 1 65 and Station 4 17 may be colluvium talus deposit while layering of ash and
a a with pockets of clinker are exposed near Station and 2 57 within existing
concave areas Based on our field observation and reconnaissance the near vertical
basalt rock cliff may extend down to the highway elevation level between Station 2 70 and
3 40 Therefore the project site may be divided into the following three areas for
implementation of the proposed anchored wire mesh system
Slope Stabilization Area 1 from Station 1 15 to Station 2 70
Rockfall Remediation Area from Station 2 70 to Station 3 40
Slope Stabilization Area 2 from Station 3 40 to Station 4 20
A computer program SnailWin from CALTRANS was used to evaluate the slope
stability for the lower slopes reinforced by the grouted soil rock anchors and to determine
minimum length of the anchors in order to achieve the factor of safety up to an accepted
level usually at least 1 3 The program uses a bi linear wedge analysis for failure planes
exiting at the toe of the slope

25 and 15 kips for 35 and 20 feet long grouted anchors respectively In addition the
grouted anchors should have a corrosion allowance of mils zinc galvanization
included in their diameter
