This letter report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services in
support of the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES construction
dewatering permit application for the project Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated The analytical laboratory
testing of the groundwater sample was performed by Oceanic Analytical Laboratory Inc
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
Waikikian Tower project will be situated to the north of the existing parking garage within
the Hilton Hawaiian Village complex and adjacent to Kalia Tower
We understand that it is desired to develop a high rise building structure with
350 time share units at the project site In general the project will consist of a main
high rise building and several smaller structures As part of the Waikikian Tower project
we understand that there will be a need for some off site utility improvements to service
the development
Based on available information relatively deep trench excavations of up to about
10 to 12 feet deep may be required for the installation of the various utility lines planned
at the project site Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean and the
relatively shallow groundwater elevations at the project site dewatering of the proposed
excavations will be necessary during construction of the utility lines Because excavation
dewatering may involve the discharge of groundwater into an adjacent storm drainage
system a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES permit may be
required

In order to obtain general baseline groundwater quality data for permit application
preparation it was desired to sample and test the groundwater underlying the project
site For this purpose a temporary monitoring well was installed approximately 20 feet
below the existing ground surface to collect water samples for the NPDES permit
application
The groundwater samples were collected from a temporary groundwater monitoring
well which was installed on March 16 2005 by Geolabs during our geotechnical
engineering exploration
SITE CONDITIONS
The Waikikian Tower development project site is located within the existing Hilton
Hawaiian Village complex in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The off site
utility improvements portion of the project will generally be located along Ala Moana
Boulevard and Dewey Lane as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The topography at the project site is generally level with existing ground surface
elevations at about 4 to 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The existing six lane Ala Moana
Boulevard is one of the main thoroughfares leading into the Waikiki area Therefore
traffic along Ala Moana Boulevard is generally very heavy especially during rush hour
traffic Dewey Lane is an undivided roadway that provides beach access from Ala Moana
Boulevard Dewey Lane also services delivery trucks for the adjacent Ilikai Hotel In
general traffic along Dewey Lane may be considered light to moderate
TEMPORARY MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted on
March 16 2005 The temporary groundwater monitoring well consisted of 2 inch diameter
Schedule 40 PVC pipe installed to about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The
well generally consisted of about 15 feet of screen with 0 020 inch slot opening and 5 feet
of solid casing The surface completion consisted of an 8 inch well cover that was
cemented flush with the existing ground surface
Drilling of the temporary monitoring well was accomplished by using a
truck mounted drill rig advancing 6 inch outside diameter OD casing and coring tools
The coring tools and associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned prior to
drilling the borehole
The temporary monitoring well is identified as Boring No 7 and the approximate
location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A detail of the temporary monitoring well
installation is provided as Plate 3 of this report

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general the boring designated as Boring No 7 encountered about 6 feet of
surface fill materials consisting of loose to medium dense sands The surface fills were
generally underlain by lagoonal deposits extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface for Boring No 7 The lagoonal
deposits generally consisted of loose to dense silty gravel and gravelly silts
The groundwater level in Boring No 7 was measured at a depth of about 4 feet
below ground level on March 16 2005 It should be noted that groundwater levels are
expected to vary with seasonal rainfall tidal fluctuations and time of year
A detailed description of the soil materials encountered in the drilled boring is
presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
GROUNDWATER CONDITIONS
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured at the project site
Due to the relatively close proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean it is likely that
groundwater levels measured in the temporary monitoring well will be affected by tidal
fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels may be influenced by
seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Based on our review of the analytical laboratory results it appears that no
significant adverse water quality conditions were detected in the analyses of the water
samples tested The Department of Health NPDES Notice of Intent NOI Form G
Parts 14 through 16 are provided in Appendix B The laboratory test report and chain of
custody forms are provided on Appendix C
The test results for the NOI Form G Part 15 Water Quality Parameters appear to
be below the specific numeric criteria specified under Hawaii Administrative Rules HAR
Chapter 11 54 For the NOI Form G Part 16 Toxic Parameters arsenic cadmium
chromium lead mercury selenium silver benzene toluene and ethyl benzene were
tested The test results except arsenic chromium and lead were reported as
not detected which means the sample results were below the reporting limit for the
particular test method and analysis The test results indicate that the arsenic chromium
and lead levels are below the specific numeric criteria specified under Hawaii
Administrative Rules HAR Chapter 11 54
The reporting limit is dependent on the testing equipment and the statistical control
applied by the laboratory to the test results Therefore laboratories adopting the same
standard test method may have different reporting limits

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The temporary monitoring well identified as Boring No 7 extends to about 20 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the drilled boring is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with coring tools
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled boring in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
