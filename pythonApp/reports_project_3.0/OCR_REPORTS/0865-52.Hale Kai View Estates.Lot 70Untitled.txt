As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The investigation consisted of drilling one
boring and excavating two 2 test pits within the proposed building
site performing laboratory soil tests and reviewing of the grading
and compaction test records on the building site This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a two story wood
structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site the garage level
will be cut slightly into the slope with the living areas on the upper
level

development near the end of Kulani Street The lot is bounded by

varies from about 164 to 205 feet The existing grades of the lot are
flatter than those shown on the grading plan since some amount of

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
a surface layer about 1 to 6 feet thick of highly expansive gray
brown adobe clay over weathered vesicular basalt Therefore the
slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
measures to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction would generally result in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should such a need occur in the future
For post and beam construction the individual post footings should
extend a minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground surface In the
rear portion of the house the footing embedment may be reduced if the
gray weathered rock material is encountered and if the footing is
embedded at least 12 inches into this type of material All footings
should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled with
compacted fill to provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff undisturbed soil Design of perimeter thickened
section of this report

If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This
is especially important in the more level portion of the house to
reduce ponding of surface run off water that may affect expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and patio It
appears that the rear portion of the garage and patio area would
require cuts of up to about 2 feet and 4 feet respectively It is
probable that the more competent gray weathered vesicular basalt rock
may be encountered in the rear portion of the garage and patio
excavation The front portion of the garage will have less cut and
would likely to terminate in the expansive gray adobe clay which is
generally unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay where
encountered they should be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the garage slab bottom and then replaced with non expansive
borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral The over
excavation and backfilling should extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond
the edge of the slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 52 Page 6
The non expansive borrow soil should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78 Similar over
excavation and replacement of the expansive adobe clay soils are
recommended for the on grade lanai slabs
To further reduce future post construction swelling of underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the adobe clay below the capping
fill pad be moistened thoroughly This moistening is to pre induce
swelling of the underlying clay before concrete slab placement We
suggest that wetting be done at the end of each day
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
After the above removal and replacement the perimeter thickened edge
footings of the on grade slabs could then be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
It appears that a layer of expansive soils more than 5 feet thick
was also encountered in the driveway area Since the driveway can
tolerate some amount of heaving the adobe clay over excavation and
replacement may be reduced to 2 feet minimum below the bottom of the
driveway slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
After moisture conditioning of the exposed adobe clay it should
then be immediately backfilled with a select non expansive borrow
material which would act as a capping over the underlying adobe clay
This 2 foot minimum capping under the driveway or walkway should be
used even if new fill is used to raise the grade Where less than 2
feet of new fill is planned over excavation and replacement of the
adobe clay with select non expansive material is recommended to
provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot beyond the
driveway edges
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil Over excavation beyond the edge of
the walkway may not be necessary
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 20 to
25 percent gradient Up to 4 feet of cut is anticipated for the patio
area

The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
adobe clay soil should definitely not be used for fill material
below structures and behind retaining walls
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures
Retaining Walls
CRM retaining walls are planned around the garage level and the patio
to retain the proposed cuts of up to about 4 feet
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on
the wall we recommend that the expansive adobe clays if encoun
tered be over excavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom
of the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such
as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The over excavation
and replacement should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond
the toe of the footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 52 Page 9
The retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Therefore the total
depth of the above over excavation and replacement would be 3 5 feet
below the lowest adjacent finished grade where adobe clay is
encountered
In the rear half of the garage the footing embedment may be reduced
to 12 inches below the finished grade if the gray weathered rock is
encountered In such a case over excavation below the footing will
not be necessary The soil engineer should inspect the footing
excavation verify the suitability of foundation soils in the
excavation as well as to confirm the over excavation requirements
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the stiff natural soil
A base friction factor of 0 4 for soil can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall In addition a passive soil
resistance of 250 p c f can be utilized if the soils are confined by
slab or pavement
To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted laterally
on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay should be over
excavated a distance equal to the height of the wall Over excavation
is not necessary in the weathered rock material for this purpose

Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials such as
select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill would improve
drainage reduce the compactive effort required for the wall backfill
and reduce lateral pressure on the walls Impervious soil such as
on site clay soils should be used only for the top one foot of
backfill to reduce seepage of surface run off behind the walls
A properly designed subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes
should be utilized behind the walls Subdrains should discharge
freely by gravity onto lower ground
For the above retaining wall design the following lateral earth
pressures can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
granular backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earth
work construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
