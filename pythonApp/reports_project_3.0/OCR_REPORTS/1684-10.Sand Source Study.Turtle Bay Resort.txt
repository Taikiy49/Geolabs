INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil reconnaissance
performed for the sand source study at Turtle Bay Resort
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the geotechnical exploration was to delineate the
areas where suitable sand materials can be excavated for use for on site
or other proposed construction Since the Turtle Bay Resort is both Fee
and Lease land the study attempted to differentiate the sand quality and
locations for each type of ownership Also a preliminary cost
comparison for removing sand and backfilling at the Turtle Bay Areas
verses hauling sand from a commercial source nearby was performed
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were
used in the engineering study that formulated our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1684 10 Page 4
Dk No 25
The main objectives of the exploration were
1 To map the locations and extent of both clean and dirty sand
materials in both Fee and Lease Land Areas by utilizing visual
and boring information from this investigation and information
derived from previous investigations see Appendix C
2 To evaluate the excavation difficulties and depths of water at
the sand source areas
3 To evaluate cost comparisons of utilizing the existing sand
sources at the Turtle Bay Resort and the cost of hauling from
another source
4 To perform laboratory tests to evaluate the general
classification of the soils in accordance to the Unified Soil
Classification System From these results and from previous
test data the sand was differentiated as clean or dirty sand
The clean sand may be used as drainage layers in the golf course
construction while the dirty sand could be used as a source of structural
fill utility line cushion and backfill materials
The scope of our study included field reconnaissance a review of
previous data and reports the drilling of nine exploratory borings and
laboratory testing of selected soil samples The locations of the 9
exploratory borings are indicated on Plate 2 of the Site Plan
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between Kawela Stream near Kawela Bay
west side and Kahuku Point by Marconi Road east side The ocean
shoreline forms the northern boundary and Kamehameha Highway the
southern boundary of the site The total area involved in this study
covers about 800 acres

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the northern flank of the Koolau
Volcano This volcanic shield formed a salient point for the growth of
coral reefs on the flatlying fringes along the coast of northern Oahu
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1684 10 Page 6
Dk No 25
During the Pleistocene the sea level fluctuated widely as a result
of glacio eustatic changes When the sea stood at higher levels than the
present the coral reefs grew to elevations above present sea level The
emerged coral reef formations encountered at the project site have been
assigned to the Waimanalo stand of the sea at elevation 25 Stearns
1978 In the project areas the reef material has been mantled by
unconsolidated beach sands or man made fill to develop the area
Some amount of fill was encountered on the easterly portion of the
project site which was probably placed during the site s former use as a
military installation
Subsurface Soil Conditions
Nine 9 borings were drilled primarily in Areas 1 and 3 at the
site to depths of 3 5 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 In general the borings encountered a surface layer approximately 8
to 15 feet thick of tannish brown fine to medium grained coral sand
with some gravel Unified Soil Classification SM SP Below this was
tannish white coarse grained coral sand with some gravel SP to 11 5 to
16 5 feet depths drilled
At Boring 8A a pocket of soft dark gray clayey silt ML was

coral reef was encountered at 4 feet below a cobble and boulderly layer
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 3 8 to
8 3 feet below the existing ground surface except for the borings drilled
in Area 3 where the groundwater was ata depth of 16 feet or deeper

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
From the field investigation and a study of previous reports done in
the area we have delineated four 4 areas at the Turtle Bay Resort site
that are generally underlain by 8 to 15 feet of coral sand above the
groundwater
These four areas designated as possible sand borrow source cover
about 15 123 acres of the 800 acre Turtle Bay Resort land area
The remainder of the site was either presently developed or the
soils are not suitable for a sand borrow source
Iwo areas Nos 1 and 4 are Fee Land that make up about 107 acres
while the other two areas Nos 2 and 3 are Lease Land that cover about
17 acres
The four areas are located outside of the primary dune sand limits
as shown on the Sand Dune Map by Group 70 The quality of the sand at
the four areas was classified as clean or dirty sand depending on the
percentage of fines passing the No 200 Sieve For this study we
defined clean sand as having less than 10 fines and dirty sand with more
than 10 fines
The golf course designer or engineer architect should review the
boring logs grading analysis forms and sieve analysis plots from this
report and previous reports to determine if these soils meet the desired
specifications for the golf course construction or other uses see
Appendix C

Excavation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated sandy material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1684 10 Page 11
Dk No 25
Excavated on site sandy material generally free of organic and other
expansive components may be used as a source of fill material for the
golf course or other construction purposes
It appears that the sand can be excavated without difficulty by
conventional earth moving equipment
The practical depth of excavation for Areas 1 2 and 4 is limited to
about 3 5 to 8 foot depths where the water level was encountered In
Area 3 the water level was about 16 feet deep Excavation below the
water table would require special equipment and would be much more
costly Additionally granular material would be needed to backfill the
underwater excavation Therefore we recommend that the maximm depth of
excavation be kept about 1 foot above the high water level
Backfilling
Except for landscape and non structural areas all imported fill
material to replace the excavated sand areas should generally consist of
low expansive clayey silt or granular materials less than 6 inches in
size All backfill materials should be placed in 8 inch maximm
horizontal loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the excavation and backfilling operations be
observed by a soils technician during construction to see that adequate
compaction been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation

approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different fron
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling nine
9 borings to depths of 3 5 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were calssified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 9
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
