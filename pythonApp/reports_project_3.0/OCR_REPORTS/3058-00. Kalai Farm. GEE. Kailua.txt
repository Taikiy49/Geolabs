performed for the proposed site grading at Kalalai Farm of Kailua on the island of Oahu

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Maunawili area of Kailua on the windward side of
the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is presently used for agricultural purposes It is
proposed to grade the site to provide a level area and to improve the drainage for future
agricultural use The planned site work also includes construction of a retaining wall
adjacent to and parallel to the existing CRM wall The two 2 CRM walls are to serve as
a channel to intercept and divert surface water flows through the site
Based on the grading plans provided to us we understand that the site grading
will involve fills of up to about 15 feet thick We also understand that the site will continue
to be used for agricultural purposes and structural improvements are not planned above
the fill with the exception of the proposed CRM retaining wall Further details of the
proposed construction were not available at the time this report was prepared

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Maunawili area of Kailua on the windward side of
the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by existing residential lots to the
east and southwest The site is located in a small valley with slopes to the east and west
An existing CRM wall is located at the northwest portion of the site as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The existing CRM wall is about 500 feet long and some portions of the wall
have ravelled due to erosion
The proposed project site is presently heavily vegetated with grass and trees
Based on observations during our field exploration and a topographic map Site and
Grading Plan dated provided by Gray Hong Bills Associates Inc
the existing topography of the site varies from about 55 to 105 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our geotechnical exploration clayey silt soils with gravel were
encountered at the proposed project site from the surface to the maximum depths
explored The soils generally grade from very stiff to stiff with increasing depth Soils
below the water table were generally softer than overlying soils The soils encountered
at the site are generally competent and should provide adequate bearing for the fills
planned
Recommendations addressing the above considerations and other geotechnical
aspects of the project are discussed in the following sections of this report

Site Grading
In general based on the results of our exploration it appears that conventional
earthwork methods may be used for construction of the grading work The following
recommendations are general guidelines for the design of the mass grading and for the
development of general specifications
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the areas within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of the existing cover of shrubs
and other vegetation The material should be disposed of properly off site or the
material should be shredded chipped and stockpiled for use in landscaping It is
our opinion that in the areas which have five feet 5 of fill or less existing trees
having a diameter of four inches 4 or larger may be saved However the banana
trees should be removed
Fill Placement
Following clearing and grubbing of the site areas designated to receive non
structural fill should generally be scarified to a depth of eight inches 8 brought
to near optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 85 percent of
the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for overexcavation and engineered fill
construction to account for variations in the overexcavation quantities
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3058 00 Page 7
In general excavated soil materials if any should be suitable for use as fill
material Any boulders or cobbles larger than 12 inches in maximum dimension
should be separated from the excavated material and either disposed of off site or
be placed in the deeper fills as discussed below
Imported material may be required to construct the planned fill Imported fill for
non structural areas should consist of clayey or silty soils containing no rocks or
clods greater than six inches 6 in maximum dimension that will form a stable
mass when compacted Import fill in structural fill areas should consist of non
expansive select material containing no more than 20 percent by weight passing
the No 200 Sieve without rocks or clods greater than six inches 6 in maximum
dimension It is recommended that off site borrow be tested by Geolabs Hawaii
in order to evaluate the suitability of the material for its intended use prior to
delivery to the job site
Boulders or rock fragments exceeding six inches 6 in maximum dimension
should be buried in the deeper portions of the fills provided they are not nested
and fine grained soil is properly compacted between the boulders to reduce the
potential for loss of fines from the upper portion of the fill No boulder larger than
three feet 8 in its largest dimension should be placed in the fill ft may be
necessary to reduce the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site Care
must be exercised to avoid placement of any boulders in locations which would
conflict with future utility lines In general boulder or rock fragments larger than
12 inches in size should be placed at depths greater than four feet 4 below finish
subgrade level No boulders or rocks may be used in the structural fill area and
no boulders or rock fragments greater than six inches 6 in size may be placed
within two feet 2 of finished grade in mass grading areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3058 00 Page 8
Structural fills or backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches
8 in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with procedures in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 91 The new CRM wall will be constructed roughly parallel to the existing
wall Within the limits of a zone extending a minimum of four feet 4 laterally in
each direction from the proposed new wall alignment a bench should be
excavated to create a nearly level surface upon which the structural fill will be
placed The bench excavation should be extended to a depth equal to the lowest
existing surface elevation within the limits described above All fill placed above the
bench within these limits should be engineered structural fill
Fills for non structural areas may be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten inches
10 in loose thickness and should be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
Slopes
Fills to be placed on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
should be benched into firm soil to lessen potential instability of the new fill The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill placement
Silty soils are susceptible to erosion when exposed particularly in slope areas
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be
utilized to reduce the potential of erosion and or instability of the finished slopes

Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the vicinity of the site indicate
that the upper portion of the site is most likely underlain by competent soils at relatively
shallow depths It is our opinion that conventional excavation methods may be used for
site grading
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This
review is necessary to evaluate conformance with the intent of the earthwork
recommendations provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot
assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations presented
Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the grading operations
This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications
and or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated during design The recommendations presented
in this report are contingent upon the above observations
Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with the project specifications prior to hauling to the project site
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings and test pits Variations of conditions between the
field borings and test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations and elevations of the field borings and test pits are approximate
having been estimated from the Site and Grading Plan prepared by Gray Hong Bills
Associates Inc date The locations and elevations of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Kalalai Farm Inc and other
project consultants for specific application to this project in accordance with generally
accepted geotechnical engineering practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the preparation of the foundation design for the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information
for use to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or contract
bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a competent
geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or performance of
site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four
4 borings to depths of about 16 feet below the existing ground surface and seven 7
test pits to depths of about 8 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown
on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings and test pits were visually classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification
of Soils Visual manual Procedure by an engineer from our firm Graphic presentations
of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
