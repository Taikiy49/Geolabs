INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
investigation performed for the proposed relocation of a portion of the
Tripler Army Medical Center sewer line adjacent to Fort Shafter Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report also incorporates the findings of our previous
investigation entitled Off Site Gravity Sewer Line B Fort Shafter
Oahu Hawaii performed in July 1977
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and water conditions along the proposed
sewer line alignment to provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the
design of the sewer line manhole foundation and the required excavation
bracing system for the proposed sewer line construction
The scope of work performed in this investigation was in general
accordance with our proposals dated March 27 and It
included drilling and sampling ten 10 borings to depths varying from
19 to 66 5 feet three 3 Dutch cone penetration tests to depths of 54
to 70 feet below the surface laboratory testing engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory findings and report preparation
In addition to the above scope of work we have also performed four
4 field vane shear and four 4 field permeability tests to evaluate
the in situ strength and seepage characteristics of the soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1528 00 Page 3
At the time of our field exploration several possible routes were
being considered for the eastern portion of the alignment Initially a
route extending through the hillside area to the east of Building G
was considered to minimize disturbance to the baseyard area However
due to the steep ground and very deep excavation which would be
required this route was eliminated in favor of the present route to the
west of Building G
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
locations of the borings and cone penetration tests are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The existing sewer line which originates at Tripler Amny Medical
Center and Ahuimanu Military Housing crosses the existing Hawaiian
Telephone Company baseyard which is underlain by soft marsh deposit
Due to filling and other construction the sewer line has subsequently
deflected in this area causing back up of sewage and infiltration at the
joints of the sewer and therefore a replacement is necessary
Two alignments for the new sewer replacement had been proposed
However the southern approach which runs southward between the General
Cable Corporation and Hawaiian Telephone Company baseyard and then
eastward along the Military Reservation towards the Fort Shafter Sewage
Pump Station was found to be unsatisfactory The main drawback with
this alignment is the anticipated very poor subsoil condition close to
the Moanalua Stream Also the alignment will cross a large number of
utilities underlying the paved area between the General Cable
Corporation and Hawaiian Telephone Company baseyard
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1528 00 Page 4
The proposed new sewer replacement will be 30 inches in diameter
and about 2000 feet long The current recommended alignment will start
from the General Cable Corporation yard which is located adjacent to the
Hawaiian Telephone Company baseyard in Mapunapuna The new line will
traverse from the existing sewer manhole No 6A and the existing 27 inch
gravity line at this point and run along the northerly edge of the paved
access road next to Building A of the Hawaiian Telephone Company
baseyard From the northeastern corner of Building A it continues
southerly along the paved area west of Building G to the flat A C
paved lot of the Military Reservation area Then along this paved area
for about 560 feet it will be connected to a new box structure at the
existing Fort Shafter Pump Station
According to the preliminary design plan developed thusfar the new
sewer line will be located away from the existing sewer line for most of
its length The last 500 to 600 feet southeast portion will probably
be parallel to and about 10 to 15 feet from the existing line
It is our understanding that the invert elevations of the new line
will begin at elevation 6 feet near the existing Sewer Manhole No 6A
to about elevation 9 feet at the Fort Shafter Sewage Pump Station

side

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is geologically situated on the southwest flank of
the Koolau Volcanics These volcanics are composed of basaltic lava
flows of early Pleistocene Age The basalt is overlain by a complex
interlayered system of terrestrial and marine sedimentary deposits
During the late Pleistocene Age the sea level rose and fell several
times as a result of glacioeustatic fluctuations resulting in local
swamp conditions which was later superimposed by more recent deposits of
terrestrial detritus of sand gravel silt and clay eroded from the
older rock of the area
In more Recent time the efforts of man have shaped the lowlying
land to its present form by placement of fill in low areas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
We believe that the proposed sewer replacement can be constructed
within the area covered by our soil investigation provided that the
recommendations contained herein are closely followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1528 00 Page 9
A large portion of the current proposed sewer line will be
constructed amid existing development namely to the south and west
the Hawaiian Telephone Company baseyard and to the north the Moanalua
Highway structure The selection of appropriate construction procedure
good workmanship and a proper construction monitoring program are
important for the successful execution of this project All the above
are vital to reduce the risk of distress or damage to the adjacent
structure and ensure the future performance of the sewer line
The soil conditions appear to be complex below the project site
However for design purpose the soil condition at the site is divided
into two groups Group 1 generally categorized as loose sand
condition extends along the mauka side of the Hawaiian Telephone
Company Group 2 generally categorized as dense sand condition
covers the remaining alignment of the sewer line Separate design
recommendation based on these two soil groups will be presented
Under the current design concept the sewer structure will be
bearing on and hence supported by the underlying soil The estimated
settlement for the sewer structure under soil Group 1 condition is about
1 75 inches occurring within a period of 6 to 12 months Additional
long term settlement due to secondary consolidation on the order of
10 of the total settlement may occur over the life of the structure
Generally sewer lines can be designed to provide sufficient flexibility
to accommodate this amount of settlement However if the anticipated
settlements are determined to be intolerable pile foundation support
may be used
Future development of the area adjoining the sewer line could
produce differential settlements if additional loads are applied on the
area underlain by compressible materials In this case if there is any
anticipated plan for future development which will cause the pipe to
settle over its allowable tolerance the section of the pipe should be
designed to be supported on pile foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
All excavations should be protected by sheeting with adequate
bracing support system
As the invert of the sewer line is below the water table
dewatering will be required during the excavation and trenching
operation
Settlement of the Sewer Structure
Settlement of the sewer structure is normally caused by the
following two events
1 When excavation for the sewer line is carried out heaving of the
underlying soil will occur because of stress relief Subsequent
backfilling will cause recompression of the underlying soil and
hence settlement The amount of heave is generally more
significant when the underlying soil is stiff For the loose or
soft subsoil condition at the site the anticipated heave will be
small
2 Because of the difference in unit weight between the lighter
excavated material and heavier backfill there will be a net
increase of loading acting on the underlying soil which will cause
consolidation and settlement
Our laboratory consolidation tests indicated that soils underlying
the proposed sewer alignment generally have high moisture contents and
are normally consolidated to slightly overconsolidated The
consolidation characteristic of the soil at the site indicate that the
settlement due to the previous site filling for development has
practically ceased The slight overconsolidation measured in the tests
may be a result of lowering of the water table due to the leakage of the
existing sewer

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
friction resistance between the backfill material and the trench wall
To calculate the vertical loads on the pipe an average unit weight for
backfill material of 125 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25
are recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the
width of the trench and therefore the width of the trench should be
kept to minimum

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe Pipe
bedding the contact between the foundation and the pipe is therefore
an important design consideration
Due to the high groundwater level close to or above the proposed
sewer invert and the very soft soil condition throughout the section
under Group 1 soil condition it is recommended that a two foot thick
crushed rock cradle be utilized to stabilize the bottom of the trench
and provide a working platform along this portion of the sewer line
The rock cradle material should be brought up to within six inches of
the invert grade or to the bottom of any concrete jacket or cradle The
rock cradle material should consist of gravel less than three fourths
inch and larger than one fourth inch such as No 3B Fine ASTM C 33
67
We recommend Class B First Class Bedding for the proposed sewer
The sewer pipe should be bedded in a minimum of 6 inches of compacted
granular material placed on a flat trench bottom The granular bedding
should extend beyond the sides of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth
the outside pipe diameter and should extend half way up the sides of the
pipe The recommended bedding detail is illustrated on the enclosed
Plate 4
In the section underlain by Group 2 soil the same pipe bedding
should be utilized But in this area a firm trench bottom is expected
and the two foot thick rock cradle can be eliminated

The method and equipment used for excavation should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits Depths of
excavations in this operation will vary from approximately 15

Hard ripping or special excavating methods for hard materials

Sheeting and Bracing
Due to the low strength and high moisture content of the
predominant soils at the site sheeting and bracing of the
trench wall is recommended throughout the project to prevent
cave in of the trench walls which may cause subsidence of
adjacent areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1528 00 Page 14
For Group 1 soil condition special attention is required to
prevent upheaval of the trench bottom due to the particularly
loose and low strength characteristics of the underlying
material For Group 2 soil condition care should be exercised
to prevent piping that may occur at the bottom of the trench
during dewatering
The required depths of penetration of the sheeting to minimize
the occurrence of upheaval and piping are presented on Plates 5
and 6 Minimum factors of safety of 2 and 1 5 are recommended
respectively for Groups 1 and 2 soil condition
The required depths of penetration are also affected by the
method of dewatering Plates 5 and 6 have both assumed no
drawdown on the outside of the sheeting If drawdown by the
means of deep well system outside the sheeting is used the
depths of penetration can be reduced However care must be
taken to prevent upheaval or piping when pumping is stopped
Sheeting and bracing must comply with all applicable safety
requirements The adequacy and safety of the sheeting and
bracing operations should be made the responsibility of the
contractor since his representative is continuously present at
the site to observe any changing conditions which might affect
stability

Even with good construction procedure some movement of the

Dewatering
As previously stated dewatering is necessary because
groundwater will normally be encountered above the proposed
invert level throughout the site
The method and equipment used for the dewatering should be the
choice of the contractor It should be realized that the
dewatering system chosen will have an effect on the depth of
sheeting used
In the determination of the preferred method of dewatering
three basic selection criteria are suggested
a The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to
adjacent development
b The method should maintained adequate excavation stability
and provide a safe working condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
c The method should be flexible with easy modification to
accommodate various conditions
Dewatering by the means of a well point system along the
outside of the excavation could be used Although this method
could require a shallower sheet penetration the drawdown is
likely to affect the groundwater condition underneath Buildings
A and G of the Hawaiian Telephone Company Hence
recharging injecting the pumped water back into the soil to
maintain the water level and minimize drawdown in areas near
the existing structures may be required to prevent drawdown and
minimize the risk of settlement to these buildings
It appears that sump pumping from inside the trench may also be
used if sufficient penetration of the sheeting to prevent
piping and upheaval at the bottom of the trench is achieved
Backfilling
Backfilling should proceed as soon as possible after the
structures gain sufficient strength to withstand earth loads
without damage
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to
at least 12 inches above the top of the pipe The first zone
should consist of selected free draining well graded granular
material placed by hand or by suitable equipment so that the
backfill material is in contact with the entire periphery of
the pipe and in such a manner as not to disturb the pipe The
granular material is recommended to insure high density
backfill with minimum compactive effort Compaction of this
portion of the backfill by ponding and jetting may be permitted
provided the backfill material is self draining The material
should be well graded less than 3 inches in size and with no
more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling ten 10 borings to depths of 19 0 to 66 5 feet and three
3 cone penetration tests to depths of 54 to 70 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings In
addition four 4 field vane shear tests were performed in Boring Nos
1 and 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 1
through A 10 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or by
hydraulically pushing a 3 inch diameter thin wall tube The blow counts
or the hydraulic pressure to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
The cone penetration test was performed by mechanically
pushing an electric cone into the ground The tip resistance and the
shaft friction were recorded at regular depth of penetration Inter
pretation of soil profile was carried out based on the magnitude of the
recorded forces and the friction ratio Interpretation of undrained
shear strength of soil based on empirical equations was also carried
cut Graphic presentations of the results are shown on Plates A 11
through A 15
The field vane shear tests were performed by using a vane
with 2 5 inch diameter and 5 inch length The vane was first pushed into
the soil and then rotated The change of the measured torque with
angular rotation was monitored Maximum torques for both the undisturbed
and remolded soil condition were recorded These results were summarized
and are presented on Plate A 16

Four permeability tests were performed in the drill
holes during the course of our field exploration in order to
evaluate the in situ permeability of the on site soil Both
pump out and falling head test methods were utilized
Pump out tests were performed at Borings 1 and 5
Three additional holes at distances of 5 10 and 20 feet from the
test well were drilled to monitor the drawdown effect due to the
pumping A 2 inch centrifugal pump was used for both tests The
pump out test data are presented on Plates C l1 and C 2
At Borings 2 and 8 falling head tests were per
formed first by filling the holes with water to saturate the
soil and then the percolation data were recorded The test data
are presented on Plates C 3 and C 4
