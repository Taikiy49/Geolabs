This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the grading work for the Warabeya USA Warehouse Improvements project from 
September 23, 2019 through August 21, 2020. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
We understand that improvements are planned for the former Fujifilm Building at 
94-468 Akoki Street in Waipahu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The planned improvements for Warabeya USA and Mitsui Bussan Logistics, Inc. 
(MBLI) consist of constructing a depressed loading dock abutting the west side of the 
existing building.  A ramp will also be constructed to allow access into the warehouse.  A 
smaller on-grade loading area and truck turnaround are planned on the east side of the 
existing building.  Retaining structures less than 10 feet away from the perimeter of the 
existing building will be required.  In addition, a mezzanine level is planned on the MBLI’s 
side and “goal posts” will be installed to protect the freezer door.  A layout of the planned 
improvements is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company is the general contractor for the project.  
C.J. Peterson Services, Inc. performed the site grading operations. The site grading work 
consisted of subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, and 
pavement construction.  
Following the clearing operation, the over-excavated subgrade was recompacted to 
a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative compaction, as appropriate.  Fill and backfill 
materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, brought to above 
the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 or 95 percent of the maximum 
dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 187 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.8 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading 
work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical recommendations presented in the above-referenced 
geotechnical engineering report (August 1, 2017) and subsequent recommendations.
