INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the site grading and foundation design for a single
family residential development
A grading plan was not available during our investigation

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located north mauka of Farrington
Highway along Maipalaoa Road in
The 7 5 acre site is bounded on the west by Maile Channel
a concrete drainage channel The site is generally flat
with surveyed elevations ranging from about 4 to 6 feet
above sea level except for a small swale crossing the
makai third of the site During the field exploration
the site was covered with sparce trees and low shrubs
and scattered piles of dirt and debris The site has
been previously graded and the ground is covered predomin
antly by coral clayey sand and gravel fill
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a
single family residential development consisting of 49
lots with a 44 foot wide access roadway throught the site
Two to three feet of fill is planned to raise the entire
site to about elevation 6 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology and Soil Conditions
The project site is located on the Waianae coastal plain
which consists of a thick sequence of alluvial sediments
of basically marine origin near the coast
W O 872 00 P 3
A complex series of sea level changes has resulted in the
deposition of layers of beach sands marine clays strati
fied with the growth of coral reefs The site lies appro
ximately 500 feet from the existing shoreline
Based on the test borings the site has been graded and
covered with a 1 to 4 foot layer of man made fill consisting
of coral sand and gravel and brown clay Underlying the
fill is a 1 to 7 foot thick layer of a stiff olive brown
marine clay The clay layer is generally thickest toward
the makai portion of the site and along the channel boundary
Below the clay to the extent of the borings a dense clayey
coral sand and gravel was encountered In addition Boring
No 7 encountered very dense ledge coral beneath the clayey
layer at 3 5 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively stiff subsoils
at the site The existing surface fill material consists
of a mixture of coral sand and gravel with brown clay This
material has low to moderate expansion potential depending
on the amount of clay present in the mixture
Two to three feet of fill are planned to raise the grade at
the site Non expansive soil should be used to fill the
site then conventional foundations can be used for the
residential structures
Major settlement problems are not anticipated for fill
heights less than 10 feet
Site Grading
Cuts are not planned at the site Imported fill will be
required to raise the site grade The imported fill should
be non expansive with swell less than 2 and should not
contain organic and other deleterious material or rocks
in excess of 8 inches in diameter Off site borrow
material should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to transportation to the site to assure that it is
suitable for the proposed construction
W O 872 00 P 6
The site should first be cleared of vegetation and debris
then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch loose horizontal lifts
compacted to the above specified density
Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical fill slopes are recommended
All fill slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and
cut back to obtain a well compacted slope
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable and surface water should be diverted away
from the slopes
Foundations
After filling the site with two to three feet of select
non expansive soil conventional spread footing foundation
or slab on grade construction can be utilized
Slabs should be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum
of 5 feet
Post footings located close to the tops of slopes should have
a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured
from the outer edge of footing to the slope face
W O 872 00 P 7
For the light residential structures an allowable bearing
value of 2000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing
on compacted fill
Surface water should be diverted away from building foun
dations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be used on level areas where the slab
subgrade soil is non expansive structural fill material
It should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the access road
Imported fill material will be required for the site grading
If at least 10 inches of select material CBR greater than
12 is used in the roadway embankment areas the pavement
design may be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

W O 872 00 P 8
For roadway subgrade bearing on the existing tan sand and
brown clay fill CBR less than 8 based on City and County
Design Standard the pavement design will be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 inch Select Borrow
18 inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the proposed fill material
and at subgrade soils during construction and the design
sections can be revised based on these tests
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications

depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
