INTRODUCTION

performed for the new site of the Proposed 0 3 M G Reservoir in Pahoa on the island of
Hawaii Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

The reservoir structure was originally planned and designed on the adjacent parcel
However prior to construction it was discovered that the site was underlain bya large
lava tube with possible archeological value Therefore a new site was selected for the
reservoir structure
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration at the new site and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data
obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new 300 000 gallon capacity water reservoir in Pahoa
Puna District on the island of Hawaii to supplement the existing Pahoa water system
It is our understanding that the new reservoir will be a reinforced concrete tank
structure approximately 18 feet in height and 61 feet in outer diameter An asphaltic
concrete A C paved access road will connect the reservoir to the existing Pahoa Road
An A C driveway is also planned around the reservoir
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Pahoa Puna District on the island of Hawaii It is
situated approximately 2 150 feet southwest from the intersection of Keaau Pahoa Road
and Pahoa By Pass Road
The site slopes gently to the east at a gradient of about 8 The existing ground
elevations range from about 714 feet western portion to 705 eastern portion above
Mean Sea Level MSL
The overall site is undeveloped land covered with ohia trees and uluhe ferns on a
thin discontinuous layer of humus Fresh glassy pahoehoe was noted outcropping in
some places The reservoir site had been cleared of vegetation during our field
exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to a depth of approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a very thin layer of organic silt humus
less than one foot thick overlying in situ basaltic lava rock formation The volcanic rock
formation extended to the maximum depth explored of 20 5 feet below the existing ground
surface

The basaltic rock formation encountered at the project site is in the pahoehoe form
as characterized by the ropy hummocky surface of the rock Pahoehoe lava is prone
to having subsurface voids or cavities which were created when the lava flow was
deposited Small voids were encountered at shallow depths in all three 8 borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Reservoir Foundation
Based on the latest site grading plan the reservoir foundation will bear on basaltic
rock formation It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous footing foundations
may be used to support the proposed reservoir structure
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 5 000 p s f may be utilized for the design
of footing foundations bearing on in situ rock formation This bearing value is for total
dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short
duration wind and seismic load considerations
Foundations bearing on in situ rock should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed reservoir structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend that an
equivalent fluid pressure of 400 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive

Foundation Probing
Our field exploration encountered voids or cavities in the borings The basaltic
rock encountered at the site is pahoehoe which is the form of lava flow most prone to
contain tubes voids and cavities
In order to reduce the potential of loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of a void below a foundation we recommend that a program of cavity probing
be implemented The probes should be drilled at each individual footing location and on
five 5 foot centers around the circumference of the exterior ring footing foundation The
probes should be a maximum of three 3 inches in diameter and should be drilled to a
depth of 10 feet below the bottom of footing
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of cavities the probes should be
filled with neat cement grout injected at low to moderate pump pressures i e the
pressure available from a grout pump If the volume of grout injected exceeds four 4
times the calculated volume of the probe hole the grout mix should be adjusted to a one
1 part sand to one 1 part cement 1 1 mixture
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
the project geotechnical engineer to monitor the presence of voids and to allow additional
recommendations to be made if changed conditions occur or if large voids are
encountered

Site Gradin
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the gently sloping project terrain Based on
the existing topography the earthwork for the project may involve up to five feet
excavation in rock to create a level pad for the reservoir foundation Up to two feet of fill
will be required for the access road to the reservoir area
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
It is our opinion that excavation in the hard rock formation encountered at the site
may require hard ripping or hoerams Blasting may be considered to expedite the
progress of the earthwork Care must be exercised to avoid over ripping or over shooting
which would disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting in a potential loss of
bearing strength
The excavated rock material if reduced to less than 6 inches in size will be
suitable as fill material If imported fill material is required the material should be a select
granular material less than 6 inches in maximum size with less than 15 passing the No
200 sieve
Fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and then compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the
reservoir foundation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommenda

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the earthwork and foundation construction
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifica
tions or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by scaling from the
Revised Reservoir Location Plan undated provided by D A G S and also by measuring
on site with reference to the project site corners staked out by the project surveyor
Elevations of the borings were determined by interpolation from elevation contours on the
plan The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross section are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three 3 borings
to depths of 18 0 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill
the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination if the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in our laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Rock samples were obtained from the borings by using NQ wireline core
barrels advancing diamond bits The percentage of sample recovered from each
core run is shown at the appropriate depth on the Boring logs REC the rock
quality determination RQD is also shown on the Boring Logs
