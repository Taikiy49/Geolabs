PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Kapolei West Mass Grading – Phase 1 project site is in the Kapolei area in the 
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The site is situated south of the Oahu 
Railway and Land (OR&L) railroad tracks and east of Ko’olina Golf Course. A Project 
Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
The project involves the mass grading of Tax Map Key (TMK): 9-1-014: 041.  The 
total area to be mass graded is approximately 13.6 acres.  Detailed information for the 
planned development was not available at the time this report was prepared.  Based on 
the grading plan, fills on the order of about 20 feet thick are planned for the site.   
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the site grading operations for the project site.

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Royal Contracting Company performed the site grading operations. The site 
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills on 
the order of up to about 20 feet thick to achieve the design finished mass grades.  During 
the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill 
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. 
The fill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture content, 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557.   
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.13 of this report.  
These tests were performed on the constructed fills at the site.  Test locations and 
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.  These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used.  Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on 
visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions 
exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading 
operations for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by 
this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced report dated July 30, 2013 and subsequent recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
