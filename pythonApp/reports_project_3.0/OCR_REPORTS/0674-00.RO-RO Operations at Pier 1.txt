INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation for
the proposed container yard expansion at Pier I Kahului Maui
Hawaii The purpose of our study was to define existing on site
soil conditions and to make recommendations for planned pavement
design and construction
W 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed container yard expanston ts at Pter I of the Kahului
Harbor north of the extsttng warehouse and Matson Container Yard
areas The ocean ts along the northern boundary A man made jetty
ts located at the western tip of the project The area is fairly
level and presently the site elevations range from 7 0 to 9 0 feet
The western half of the stte ts unpaved and is currently being
used as a temporary storage yard for equipment and breakwater
armored concrete structures for the Army Corps of Engineer s
Breakwater Construction project on the Pier 1 jetty
The east half is in use as part of the existing container yard
for container stacking and storage South of the project area
ts the existing pavement area for loading and unloading of con
tainer and truck traffic
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is anticipated that the proposed container yard expansion fin
ish grade will be close to the existing elevations Minor site
grading will be required to obtain a uniform surface and to provide
surface drainage The improvements will include an asphalt pave
ment light poles and storm drains

The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing indicated that
the site area consists of relatively competent sand and coral fill

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The sotls encountered tn our tnvestigatton will generally provide
excellent support for the planned container yard expansion Based
on the results of our laboratory tests the on site sandy soils
can be considered a competent select borrow subgrade material
The existing subsurface sotl has tn place dry densities below
95 relative compaction However with additional proof rolling
effort the on site soils will provide subgrade support for the
anticipated container traffic loads
Site Preparation
The test borings indicated that the upper fill materials are
generally competent It was probably placed without sufficient
compaction control to assure uniform density For the proposed
pavement construction it is recommended that the existing sub
grade soil of the proposed paving site be proof rolled to obtain
a more uniform and denser state within a 3 foot thick prism This
proof rolling would also delineate areas of unsuitable pavement
support Prior to proof rolling the exposed surface at subgrade
elevation should be scarified down to a depth of 6 inches Water
should be used to bring the underlying sandy soil to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum
density as determined by the ASTM D1557
The proof rolling should be monttored by a soils engineer who
would delineate the areas of unsuitable support
W 5
These areas tf encountered should be undercut to remove the
unsuitable materials to a maxtmum depth of three feet and re
placed with compacted select on site borrow as prevtously speci
fied
Fill matertal up to 3 feet below fintsh grade shall be placed
tn 8 inch thick loose layers and compacted to 90 of its maximum
density Above this and up to the base course elevation the
subbase course of the pavement section should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of the above specified density
The excavated on site sandy soils can be considered satisfactory
for use tn the controlled fills All select import fill should
be examined and approved by the soils engineer prior to use in
controlled fill area In general the subbase course within the
3 foot pavement prism should be of granular material having a
laboratory CBR value of 45 or more
If any unsuitable subgrade materials such as silt and clay poc
kets are encountered during the site grading the subgrade should
be undercut to remove the unsuitable materials down to a maximum
depth of three feet below the subgrade and replaced with compacted
on site sandy soil

Following a review of the above referenced letter dated March 9
1977 prepared by the State Harbor Division the following is our
reply to the comment It does not appear logical to scarify the
6 of the exposed surface at subgrade before proof rolling if the
Facdate is presently compacted well enough to support straddle
oads
In the above comment we assume that the phrase SURFACE IS PRE
SENTLY COMPACTED means that the area is compacted by the truck
traffic In general the hard surface crust compacted by present
truck traffic does not usually provide uniform densification since
certain highly concentrated traffic area will receive more compac
tion than the less heavily used area
Also since the final grade of this container yard area has not been
developed we anticipate that part of the site will receive some cut
and therefore this grading operation might remove part of the com
pacted crust
Scarifying the extsting hard surface crust would be necessary not
only to provide a uniform surface for compaction but also act in a
site preparation aspect by allowing the water to penetrate down to
the lower so il layers much easier during the watering process for
compaction This additional moisture would be required for the
underlying sandy subgrade soils in order to further densify the
