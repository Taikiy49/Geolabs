SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration relatively competent soil
conditions such as stiff alluvium and fill over residual soils
were encountered along most of the proposed alignment Boulders
should be anticipated in the trench excavations
Spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the stiff
natural soils may be used for the pump station building
The text of this report should be referred to for special and
detailed foundation design
Introduction
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions along the proposed force main alignment and wastewater pump
station site to provide soil data for use in the design and construction
of the proposed project The approximate location of the alignment is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

W Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
GENERAL PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Wastewater Pump Station WWPS will be approximately
25 feet by 33 feet in plan and constructed of reinforced concrete and
concrete masonry units The structure will be constructed in two
levels The first level will be an underground compartment with a
finished floor elevation of about 15 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL and
will house the wet well and sewage pumps The second level has a planned
finished floor elevation of 29 feet above MSL which is approximately at
the existing ground elevation and it will contain the operator
facilities pump motors and stand by generator
A 10 inch diameter force main about 1 100 feet long is planned
starting at the wastewater pump station near the southern corner of the
existing Kukanono STP and proceeding for about 100 feet parallel to an
existing 8 inch gravity sewer up toward the access road to the sewage
GEOLABS HAWAII

treatment plant The alignment will then follow Manu Oo Street cross
through the existing Kailua YMCA parking lot and connect to the Kailua
Road Interceptor Sewer on Kailua Road
SITE DESCRIPTION
Force Main Alignment
The proposed force main sewer system will be connected to the
existing Kukanono Sewage Treatment Plant located at the end of Manu Oo
Street on the northwestern side of Kailua Road as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 From the southern corner of the treatment plant the force main
alignment will extend parallel to an existing 8 inch gravity sewer to the
plant access road The field observation indicates that the ground
surface slopes downward from the access road toward the treatment plant
at gradients varying from 12 to 25 Numerous surface boulders and thick
haole koa trees were also observed
From the access road the alignment continues in a southeasterly
direction parallel with Manu Oo Road and then crosses through the
existing Kailua YMCA parking lot and under Kailua Road connecting to the
Kailua Road Interceptor Sewer a total distance of about 1 100 feet
The ground surface elevation along the proposed alignment varies
from 32 feet above MSL at the treatment plant up to 105 feet above MSL at
the end of the alignment

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generally relatively competent soils were encountered along the
proposed force main alignment and at the proposed wastewater pump station
site Medium stiff to stiff alluvial and residual soils were encountered
within the excavation depth for the force main and pump station
Moderate amounts of basalt boulders should be anticipated for trenches in
these soils
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Ioads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
friction resistance between the backfill material and the trench wall
To compute the vertical loads on the pipes a unit weight of 110 p c f
and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are recommended Earth forces upon
the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the trench Therefore the
width of the trench Bd should be kept to a minimum The enclosed Plate
4 indicates three alternative types of trenches
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The stiff older fill and alluvial soils encountered along the
alignment have good bearing capacity and therefore minimal settlements
are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
The sewer line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface The
resulting net loading between the weight of the excavated material and
backfill will be essentially small and therefore significant
settlements are not anticipated if proper construction techniques are
utilized
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and therefore is
an important design consideration
A minimum of six inches 6 of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend
beyond the pipe sides a minimm width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding material
should consist of well graded granular material such as 3 Fine gravel
refer to Plate 5 for other details

Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction May 1975

possible damage to the pipe We believe water tamping can be used
to compact the select granular backfill materials

Wastewater Pump Station
General
The proposed finished floor level is planned at about 10 to 14 feet
below the existing ground surface Borings 5 and 6 encountered
medium stiff to stiff silty clay alluvial deposits which we believe
can generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
2 story pump station structure provided our recommendations are
followed
Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may bear on the
stiff alluvial silty clay The laboratory ring swell tests
indicate that the clay soils may exhibit moderate swelling
potential if allowed to dry and then rewetted later Therefore
the finished subgrade below the proposed pump room should be kept
moist and protected from drying from the start of construction
until the slab concrete placement is completed

Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for
the proposed pump station structure An allowable bearing value of
2500 p s f may be used on the stiff undisturbed silty clay
Footing should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
finished grade
Estimated settlements are on the order of one half of an inch total
and one quarter of an inch differential
Excavation up to about 12 feet deep are planned for the pump

available the excavation may be cut back at a temporary slope of
1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall supports

structures

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the pump room floor
level It should be noted that the silty clay encountered at the
proposed finished floor level are moderately expansive and
therefore should be over excavated down to a depth of 18 inches
below the slab bottom and then replaced with non expansive imported
soils such as select borrow coral or crusher waste
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the soil below the
capping fill pad be kept moist from the start of construction until
the select fill and slab concrete placement are completed
An additional 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be
provided below the slab In order to protect against future

incorporated below the slab

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations
subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for slabs and
pavements be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface

those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
