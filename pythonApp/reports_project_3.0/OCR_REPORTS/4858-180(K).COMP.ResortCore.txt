PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kukuiula Resort Community development area encompasses approximately
1 020 acres of land in the Koloa District on the Island of Kauai Hawaii located north of
Lawai Beach Road generally between Poipu Road and the Spouting Horn The area was
formerly used for sugar cane cultivation but is currently vacant Portions of the site were
mass graded in the early 1990 s for a previous development plan
The Kukui ula Resort Core project site is situated in the south central portion of the
development area It is proposed to grade the site and construct a one story Main
House a two level Golf Clubhouse a two level Spa facility and two level Club Villas The
site improvements will also include access roads and parking lots
The existing grade at the Resort Core site was on the order of about 10 to 20 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL The mass grading raised the site to about 24 feet MSL in
the lower areas and to about 34 feet MSL in the upper areas Therefore a significant
amount of fill was placed to raise the site to the proposed finished grades
The Resort Core mass grading was included in the Mass Grading and Major
Drainage Plans Phase1 which also included mass grading for the major roads drainage
structures and golf courses The Resort Core grading is generally shown identified as
Parcel EE on the plans
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations for the project site

SITE GRADING
Fills up to about 16 feet high were placed at the site Prior to filling the existing
ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious material then
proof rolled with a compactor Kiewit Pacific performed the mass grading operations
Boulder fill with material up to about 3 foot size was utilized in the deeper fill
areas The boulders were placed and seated with a large bulldozer to create a stable
mass Choker material consisting primarily of granular material less than 6 inches in size
was then spread over the boulders and worked into the voids by a large bulldozer and
heavily watered by a water truck to further fill the voids between the boulders A lift of
choker fill 1 to 2 feet thick was then compacted over the boulder layer The boulders
were kept a minimum of 5 feet below the finished grade and 8 feet from slope faces With
Geolabs assistance Kukui ula Development Company Hawaii Inc documented the
areal extent of the boulder fill
The fill material above and beyond the boulder fill consisted primarily of crushed
rock from the on site crusher Silty clay soils from the golf course excavation were used
to construct portions of the fill slopes adjacent to the boulder fill Finally an approximately
one foot thick layer of silty clay from the golf course was placed at the finished grade to
promote grassing and reduce erosion
Conventional compaction testing generally is not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of
passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction was
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Eight passes of a compactor was established as the
criterion for adequate compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the fill materials utilized as fill
except boulders were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 2
The fill materials excluding boulder fill were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 These tests were performed on the
constructed fills with 3 inch minus materials Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
