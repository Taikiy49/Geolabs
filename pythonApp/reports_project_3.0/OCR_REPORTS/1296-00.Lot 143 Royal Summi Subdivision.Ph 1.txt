PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story residential structure approximately 36 by 56 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage
driveway and the entire house The house main floor level will be of
wood frame construction Concrete Masonry Unit CMU retaining walls
up to 9 feet high are planned for the ground floor level
Cuts up to 7 0 feet high are anticipated at the front portion of the
lot to accommodate the ground floor level
The general layout of the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northeastern side of Aloali i Street
in the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Lot 143 has two relatively level pad areas separated by a transitional
Slope of about 5 1 H V with the lower pad located at the front of
the site A 2 1 H V bank sloping upward and about 5 feet high is at
the rear of the site

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 0 to 7 0 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings The mass grading plan for
Royal Summit Phase 1 dated prepared by Community
Planning Inc indicated that the lot received up to 7 feet of
compacted fill over the natural grade along its western boundary and
up to 14 feet along the eastern boundary
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the grading records and our laboratory soil tests on the
near surface soil samples indicate that Lot 143 was filled with
reddish brown clayey silt the same type of soil that exist as natural
soil at the site Laboratory swell tests on these surface materials
indicate that these soils have low to moderate swelling potential if
they are maintained at or above optimum moisture content Therefore
some special foundation design and subgrade preparation are
recommended

Generally cuts up to about 8 feet are planned for the basement level
It is anticipated that minimal fill will be required for the house
construction
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill within 2 feet below the cushion
material under the slab This would provide a competent bearing layer
for the foundations in this portion of the house
The stockpile of reddish brown clayey silt material in the Royal
Summit Subdivision Phase II area may be available for fill at lower
costs Although this material generally has a low to moderate
swelling potential it could have been contaminated with other
expansive soil in stockpiling operation Therefore it is recommended
that these soils be used only up to 2 feet below the slab subgrade and
the remaining upper 2 feet should consist of non expansive granular
soil select borrow

Laboratory test results of the stiff existing soils indicated that

The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the SLAB ON GRADE section is also required
We further recommend that a deeper footing embedment of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade for individual footings and
perimeter slab footings be used for the stiff on site soils An
allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
Footings located on steep 2H 1V sloping areas should be embedded an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the closest edge of the footing to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help minimize
the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended
bearing capacity of the soil
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be observed by
a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been obtained

Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and
then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximun dry density as
determined by the ASTM D 1557 78 test
If the reddish brown clayey silt from Royal Summit is used for fill

Slabs On Grade
Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the garage driveway and house
Since the concrete slab will bear on the existing low to moderately
expansive soil a deeper 18 inch perimeter footing embedment and
special subgrade preparation with lime saturation are recommended
The general procedures listed hereunder for lime saturation should be
followed

1
Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations at a rate of about one pound per square foot
prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
The subgrade soils should be water saturated with perforated hoses
by frequent sprinkling for at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying
subsoil It is important that the soils engineer should be notified
to inspect the above subgrade preparation and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
slabs

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter
systems should be installed around the house and that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the foundations and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slab should also be avoided

Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against slab
footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and
recommendations made in accordance with generally accepted soil
and foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or
implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring
data and laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen
conditions such as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of site grading and foundation plans for this
project
