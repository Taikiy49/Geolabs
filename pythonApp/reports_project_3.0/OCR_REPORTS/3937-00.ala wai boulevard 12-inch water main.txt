ENVIRONMENTAL WATER QUALITY ASSESSMENT
NPDES CONSTRUCTION DEWATERING PERMIT APPLICATION
ALA WAI BOULEVARD 12 INCH WATER MAIN
This report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services in support
of the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES construction dewatering
permit application for the subject project Our work was performed in general accordance
with our fee proposal dated August 15 1997 The analytical laboratory testing of the
groundwater sample was performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waikiki area of the District of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is desired to construct approximately
2 000 lineal feet of new 12 inch water main and appurtenances along Ala Wai Boulevard
extending from Ala Moana Boulevard to Kalakaua Avenue
It is anticipated that some dewatering of excavations may be necessary during
construction of the proposed new water main Because the excavation dewatering may
involve the discharge of groundwater effluent into an adjacent storm drainage system an
NPDES permit may be required In order to obtain general baseline groundwater quality
data for the preparation of the permit application it is desired to sample and test the
groundwater underlying the subject site
The groundwater samples were collected from a groundwater monitoring well
which was installed on March 6 by Geolabs Hawaii

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this project was to provide the monitoring well
installation and groundwater sampling and testing services necessary to obtain baseline
water quality data in support of the preparation of an NPDES permit application for
construction dewatering activities Our scope of work generally consisted of the following
tasks and work efforts
1 Filing of permit applications for street usage excavation of the public
right of way and clearance from underground utilities In addition traffic
control safety measures were implemented during the course of the field
work
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of a boring to a depth of about 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring was drilled using environmental drilling
and sampling protocol
4 Conversion of the boring to an environmental groundwater monitoring well
of approximately 15 feet in depth
5 Collection of groundwater samples from the monitoring well by a Geologist
from our firm The chain of custody documentation special handling and
transportation of the water samples to the laboratory were also performed
by our Geologist
6 Analytical laboratory testing of the groundwater samples for the following
constituents NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents and selected
NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents including testing for the presence
of BTEX products
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on this phase of the project
and presenting our findings and laboratory test results
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Geologist from
our firm
9 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
10 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed new water main will be located along Ala Wai Boulevard within the
Waikiki area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project limits consist of
Ala Moana Boulevard to the southwest and the Kalakaua Avenue intersection to the
northeast The project site is within a residential area
The ground surface along the proposed Ala Wai Boulevard alignment consists of
asphalt concrete pavement and is generally level with elevations on the order of about
4 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A slight crown in the roadway appears to shed runoff to
the perimeter gutters leading to nearby storm drains
Monitoring Well Installation
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted on
March 6 The groundwater monitoring well consists of 2 inch diameter PVC pipes
installed to a depth of about 15 feet below the pavement surface The well generally
consists of about 10 feet of perforated casing and about 5 feet of solid casing extending
to just below the pavement ground surface where a locking plug caps the PVC pipe The
surface completion consists of a flush mount traffic rated cover that is cemented flush
with the pavement surface
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck mounted drill rig
and associated drilling and sampling tools were washed by steam cleaning prior to the
drilling of the borehole
The monitoring well is identified as B 1 and the approximate location is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A detail of the monitoring well installation is provided as Plate 3 of
this report
Subsurface Conditions
The monitoring well was installed adjacent to the proposed water main alignment
in the paved traffic parking right of way Subsurface materials were examined and
classified by a Geologist from our firm Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring
in accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
free falling 30 inches
In general the boring encountered a thin surficial crust of dense silty gravel fill
material with some cobbles extending to depths of about 2 to 3 feet below the ground
surface Beneath the fill a horizon of medium dense silty sand was encountered to a
depth of about 5 feet below the ground level Below the sandy materials generally loose
to very loose lagoonal deposits consisting of silty and clayey coral gravels were

Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured during the well
installation and groundwater sampling effort Due to the relatively close proximity of the
project site to Ala Wai Canal and its hydrogeologic relationship with the nearby coastline
it is likely that groundwater levels measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal
fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at the project site may be
influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Based on our review of the analytical laboratory results it appears that no
significant adverse water quality conditions were detected in the analyses of the water
samples that were tested The water sample test results are provided in Appendix B and
include the official laboratory report and the Department of Health NPDES NOI Form A
Most of the test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents
appear to be within normal values for typical brackish to saline groundwater However
the total suspended solids and turbidity values appear to be higher than expected The
higher than expected values may be partially due to the groundwater sampling operation
despite normal care excercised during the sampling event
For the NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents BTEX products were selected for
screening based on the location of the project site within an urban community All test
results were reported as non detect which means the sample results were below the
reporting limit for the particular test method and analyte
The reporting limit is dependent on the testing equipment and the statistical control
applied by the laboratory to the test results Therefore laboratories adopting the same
standard test method may have different reporting limits

LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from the field monitoring well Variations of conditions beyond the monitoring
well may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the variations
The location of the field monitoring well is approximate having been estimated by
taping from references indicated on the preliminary undated topographic survey of a
portion of Ala Wai Boulevard provided by Okahara Associates The elevation of the
monitoring well is also approximate having been estimated by interpolation from
elevation points on the available drawing The physical location and elevation of the
monitoring well should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations on the log of boring depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring was collected at the time shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in tides rainfall temperature and other factors
