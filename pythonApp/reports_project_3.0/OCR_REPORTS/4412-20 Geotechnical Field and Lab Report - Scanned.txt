This data report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering explorations
conducted for the Wanaao Road Replacement Sewer project located in Kailua on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This data report presents the results of our field explorations and laboratory testing
of selected soil samples for the project The findings presented herein are intended to
provide geotechnical input in support of the feasibility study pertaining to the replacement
sewer line installation using microtunneling and jet grouting The findings presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Wanaao Road Replacement Sewer Project is located in Kailua on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The original project limits were defined by the upstream
manhole 30541441 KKO6XA2600 located at the intersection of Keolu Drive and Nanialii
Street and the downstream manhole 29541377 KKO6XA0100 located at the Kailua
Heights Wastewater Pump Station WWPS fronting Wanaao Road It is proposed to
replace the existing sewer line which consists of approximately 5 000 lineal feet of 21 inch
and 24 inch unlined reinforced concrete pipes We understand that it is subsequently
desired to extend the sewer line replacement project on the upstream end by another
1 600 lineal feet
Three field exploration programs consisting of a total of 22 borings extending to
depths of about 19 to 116 feet below the existing pavement surface were conducted in
March and April2004 to evaluate the subsurface and groundwater
conditions along the replacement sewer line alignment Based on our field explorations
the majority of the replacement sewer line alignment generally is underlain by medium
dense to loose silty sands and gravel at about the pipe invert elevations

EXPLORATION PROGRAM
The exploration program for the project generally consisted of test boring
exploration and laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained from the test borings
The following subsections provide a description of the exploration program for the project
Test Boring Explorations
The subsurface conditions along the proposed Wanaao Road Replacement Sewer
alignment were explored by advancing 22 borings to depths ranging from about 19 to
116 feet below the existing pavement surface The borings are identified as Boring Nos 1
through 11 101 through 107 and 201 through 204 The approximate locations of the
borings drilled are shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2 and the Site Plans Plates 3 1
through 3 4
In general the boring locations were distributed evenly along the replacement
sewer line alignment in order to obtain a general overview of the subsurface conditions
throughout the length of the alignment Based on input from URS Corporation some of
the borings were sited within proximity to the anticipated jacking and receiving pits for the
microtunneling alternative
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil
samples collected from the borings are presented in Appendix B
Laboratory Testing
Selected soil samples retrieved from the field borings were tested in the laboratory
with an emphasis on evaluating the gradation and strength characteristics of the materials
Consolidation tests with time rates of consolidation ASTM D 2435 were performed on
selected samples of the potentially compressible deposits to evaluate the compressibility
characteristics of the materials encountered Unconsolidated undrained triaxial
compression tests ASTM D 2850 were performed on selected in situ soil samples to
evaluate the undrained shear strengths of the alluvial deposits Results of the laboratory
tests performed on the selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

LIMITATIONS
The geotechnical field and laboratory data submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between and
beyond the exploration points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway
The locations of the field exploration points indicated in this report are approximate
having been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Plan and Profile
Drawings provided by Sato Associates Inc on March 4 2002 and the Final
Topographic Survey Maps provided by Sato Associates Inc on
Elevations of the field borings were estimated by interpolation between the spot elevations
and contour lines shown on the same plans The physical locations and elevations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented in the logs of borings of this report were
based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic
representations It should be noted that fluctuation of the water level may occur due to
variation in rainfall temperature and other factors

sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates

attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions along the proposed Wanaao Road Replacement Sewer
project were explored by drilling and sampling 22 borings designated as Boring Nos 1
through 11 101 through 107 and 201 through 204 The borings extended to depths
ranging from about 19 to 116 feet below the existing pavement surface The approximate
locations of the test borings drilled are shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2 and the
Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 4 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drilling
rig equipped with continuous flight augers and coring tools as appropriate
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encouniered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 22
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the hollow
core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The resuits of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Four Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selecied soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits of the soil and for soil classification The
test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Fifteen Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 including three Hydrometer
tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation
characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentations of the
grain size distribution are provided on Plates B 2 through B 4
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested The results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate B 5
Four Consolidation tests with time rates of consolidation ASTM D 2435 were
performed on selected samples of the potentially compressible soils to evaluate the
compressibility characteristics of the materials encountered Results of the consolidation
tests are presented on Plates B 6 through B 9
Four Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression tests ASTM D 2850 were
performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the undrained shear strengths of the soils
The approximate in situ effective overburden pressures were used as the applied
confining pressures for the relatively undisturbed soil samples The test results and the
stress strain curves are presented on Plates B 10 through B 13
