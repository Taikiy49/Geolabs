PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involved the construction of two story multi family residential
buildings in the vicinity of the intersection of Eleventh Street and Lanai Avenue on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii The lot area is approximately 90 000 square feet The housing
units consist of studios one bedroom and two bedroom units A total of 72 parking
stalls were also provided
The project involved site grading and infrastructure installation including
driveway parking areas sidewalks and utilities Since the site was relatively level the
mass grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills less than 2 feet in height and
placement of structural fill under the buildings
The project location and general vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The grading plan for the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the site grading and utility installation work for The Courts at
Lanai project from October 2005 to May 2007 The site grading work consisted of clearing
and grubbing over excavation placement and compaction of fills up to about 2 feet in
thickness to achieve the design finished grades Lanai Company performed the site
grading and utility backfilling work During the site grading operations samples of the
on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture

During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests

be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

BUILDING CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the house structures and the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
