INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the pavement design and related earthwork
construction of the proposed driveway repairs at Malolo Beverages in
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2289 00 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to obtain soils data for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the pavement
design and related earthwork for the proposed repairs to the existing
driveway at the project site
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of March
15 1989
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of six 6
borings to depths ranging between 5 0 and 11 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected
samples obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of
the field and laboratory data the development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Airport Industrial Area between
Koapaka and Ualena Streets in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The site is situated to the east of the Malolo Beverages
office warehouse building The existing driveway is paved with asphaltic
concrete which is deeply rutted and cracked The site is nearly level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2289 00 Page 3
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to remove and replace the existing asphaltic concrete
pavement section It is proposed to repave this area with Portland
cement concrete to provide a rigid pavement section Based upon the
information provided to us by Malolo Beverages personnel most of the
traffic on the driveway consists of trucks having gross vehicle weights
of 40 000 pounds or less Traffic frequency was not provided
Plans of the proposed repairs were not available at the time of our
exploration however it is anticipated that the existing grades will
essentially remain unchanged
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain which
consists of a thick horizontal series of interbedded marine and nonmarine
sediments capping Koolau Basalts The near surface structure at the site
consists of elevated Pleistocene coral reef deposits of variable
consistency and composition
At the project site these reef deposits are overlain by a thin
layer of man made fill from grading during the development of the area
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging between 5 0 and
11 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface layer of asphaltic
concrete varying from one 1 to two 2 inches in thickness underlain by
base course of varying thickness and quality Below the pavement
GEOLABS HAWAII

section our borings encountered man made fill ranging from one 1 to
about five 5 feet in thickness and consisting of clayey silts with
basalt and coral gravel
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time
of our exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the existing pavement section and its
subgrade has failed and should be replaced rather than rehabilitated by
an overlay
Pavement Sections
It is our understanding that the new pavement will be a rigid
Portland cement concrete pavement Based on the vehicle load data
provided to us our field exploration and laboratory testing we
recommend the following pavement section
Rigid Pavement
mt TT a in me
7 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
11 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The above recommended section assumes adequate drainage and a
subgrade compacted to 95 relative density or higher The base course
layer should also be compacted to 95 relative density or higher The
section also assumes the use of concrete with a minimm 28 day flexural
strength of 650 pounds per square inch If any of these assumptions are
invalid the pavement section should be revised as applicable

Contraction joints are recommended along the centerline of the
driveway and transversely at about twenty 20 foot centers Expansion
joints with thickened edges should be installed where the new pavement
abuts the existing structure and the street sidewalk The filler in
expansion joints should be an impervious material and should be
maintained to minimize the infiltration of surface water through the
joints
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that site
grading will be minimal and will involve only the removal of the existing
pavement section
The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading and earthwork
1 The existing pavement section and fill soils within the grading
limits should be stripped to the finish subgrade level The
resulting demolition debris and any deleterious materials should
be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding
areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled to subgrade level with well compacted
engineered fill
2 Subsequent to removal of the old pavement the area within the
grading limits should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches or to
coral formation whichever is less The scarified area should
then be moisture conditioned to near optimm moisture content
proof rolled and recompacted to 95 maximum dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78 Any rubble or boulders encountered
during the scarification which exceeds six 6 inches in maximm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2289 00 Page 6
dimension should be removed and discarded Any soft weak or
yielding areas encountered during the proof rolling and
recompaction operations should be over excavated and backfilled
with select material placed in 8 inch thick lifts compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density as described above
3 Imported fill material if required should be non expansive
granular soils generally less than three 3 inches in size and
conform to the specifications given in Section 30 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Select
Borrow for Subbase Course
4 The subgrade should be shaped to provide drain towards the outer
edges of the pavement i e to the eastern property line and
towards Koapaka and Ualena Streets and provisions should be
made to daylight the drainage from below the pavement
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written conment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and pavement recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and pavement phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points found in the field Elevations of the
data points were not available at the time of our exploration The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data if encountered and noted in the field data points
was collected at the times given on the graphic representations and or in
the text of this report This data has been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors
