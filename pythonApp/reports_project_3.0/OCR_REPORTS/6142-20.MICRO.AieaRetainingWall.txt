GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING OBSERVATIONS 
MICROPILE, TIEBACK, AND GROUND ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
AIEA HEIGHTS DRIVE EMERGENCY ROADWAY IMPROVEMENTS 
AIEA, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Chinen: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the micropile, tieback, and ground anchor installation and testing for the 
Aiea Heights Drive Roadway Improvements project located in Aiea on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.   
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and the results of testing 
performed for the micropiles, tiebacks, and ground anchors from February 3, 2011 through 
June 14, 2011.   
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is along Aiea Heights Drive between Kaupili Place and Analio Place 
in the Aiea area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The Aiea Heights Drive roadway consists 
of an asphaltic concrete paved roadway with one traffic lane in each direction. The 
roadway was constructed along a hillside with steep slopes rising on one side of the 
roadway and steep slopes descending on the other side of the roadway. Existing 
guardrails and overhead utility lines were observed along the top of the down slope side of 
the roadway. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. 
As part of the improvement for the distressed roadway, a hybrid retaining wall with 
micropiles, tiebacks, and ground anchors was designed by Schnabel Foundation 
Company Design-Build team to mitigate the existing damaged pavement section and to 
prevent further undermining and loss of the roadway pavement.

The specified micropile anchor length varied along different portions of the retaining 
wall with lengths ranging between 38 and 45 feet.  The micropiles consisted of 18 to 
25 feet of upper cased section and a lower bonded length of 20 feet.  A total of 52, 8-inch 
diameter micropiles were specified for the project. The micropile was composed of No. 10, 
Grade 75 epoxy coated threaded bar and 4,000 pounds per square inch (psi) cement 
grout.  In addition, three sacrificial micropiles were installed and tested prior to the 
production micropile installation to verify the micropile capacity. 
A total of 26, 6-inch diameter tiebacks were specified with lengths between 29 and 
35 feet.  The tiebacks consisted of an upper unbounded length of 10 feet and a lower 
bonded length between 19 and 25 feet.  The tieback was composed of No. 8, Grade 150 
threaded bar with encapsulated grout and 4,000 psi cement grout.  Performance and proof 
testing was specified for the tiebacks. 
A total of 22, 6-inch diameter ground anchors with anchor length of 15 feet were 
specified for the project.  The ground anchor was composed of No. 8, Grade 75 epoxy 
coated threaded bar. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the 
installation and testing of the micropiles, tieback and ground anchors for the City and 
County of Honolulu for general conformance with the project plans developed by Schnabel 
Foundation Company Design-Build team for the project. 
MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Micropile Installation 
Pre-production and production micropile installation was performed by Schnabel 
Foundation Company. A total of 3 pre-production micropiles and 52 production micropiles 
were installed for the project.  The micropiles were installed using a Davey Drill DK325 
mounted on a CASE excavator.  The Davey Drill DK325 rig was equipped with a downhole 
hammer and an 8-inch diameter drill bit. The approximate locations of the pre-production 
and production micropiles are shown on the Micropile, Tieback, and Ground Anchor 
Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
The installation of the pre-production micropiles (Test Nos. 1 and 2) was performed 
on February 3, 4, and 8, 2011.  The production micropiles (Nos. 1 through 52) were 
installed between April 14 and 27, 2011.  Since basalt rock was encountered at about 
12 and 14 feet below the existing ground surface instead of the anticipated saprolite for the 
two specified pre-production micropiles, an additional pre-production micropile (Test No. 3) 
was installed further up-station within the saprolite. 
The pre-production micropiles were drilled to depths of about 45 feet (Test Nos. 1 
and 2) and about 35 feet (Test No. 3) below the existing ground surface.  The depth of the 
production micropiles ranged from about 36 to 42.5 feet.  After the hole was drilled to the 
specified depth, the hole was cleaned by blowing out the cuttings and loose material in the 
drill hole.  A 1-inch diameter Grade 150 epoxy coated bar and a 1.25-inch diameter

Grade 75 epoxy coated bar were installed in the drilled hole for the pre-production and 
production micropiles, respectively.  Permanent steel casing (65/8-inch OD) was also 
installed into the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along the micropile at 10-foot 
intervals. 
A ½-inch diameter high density plastic grouting tube was installed along the 
reinforcing bar for grouting purposes. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top 
through the grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting 
tube was removed after the completion of the grouting operation. A summary of the 
micropile installation is provided in the Summary of Micropile Installation and Testing, 
Plates 3.1 through 3.3.  The micropile installation records are provided in Appendix A. 
Micropile Testing 
In accordance with the project documents, a compression load test was performed 
on Micropile Test No. 1, and tension load tests were conducted on Micropile Test Nos. 2 
and 3 for the pre-production micropiles.  Micropile Test No. 1 was loaded in compression 
to a maximum test load of 113 kips, while Micropile Test Nos. 2 and 3 were loaded in 
tension to maximum test loads of 54 and 64 kips, respectively.  The micropile testing was 
performed by Schnabel Foundation Company. 
For the production micropiles, a total of 7 proof tests were performed.  The proof 
tests were performed on Micropile Nos. 6, 9, 15, 19, 25, 37, and 47 to maximum test loads 
from about 53.6 to 56.6 kips.  Because of soft ground conditions encountered below the 
reaction blocks for Micropile No. 6, a replacement proof test was performed for 
Micropile No. 6. 
The micropile head movements at each load increment up to the maximum test 
load were measured and recorded.  The results of the pre-production and production 
micropile testing are provided in the Summary of Micropile Installation and Testing, 
Plates 3.1 through 3.3.  The micropile test results are provided in Appendix B. 
TIEBACK AND GROUND ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Tieback and Ground Anchor Installation 
Production tieback and ground anchor installation was performed by Schnabel 
Foundation Company.  A total of 29 tiebacks and 25 ground anchors were installed for the 
project.  The drilling equipment used for installing the micropiles was used for the tieback 
and ground anchor installation.  A 6-inch diameter drill bit was used.  The approximate 
locations of the production tieback and ground anchors are shown on the Micropile, 
Tieback, and Ground Anchor Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
The tiebacks and ground anchors were installed from May 18, 2011 to 
June 9, 2011.  The tiebacks and ground anchors were drilled and grouted in the same 
manner as the micropiles.  The tiebacks were drilled to lengths of about 29 to 35 feet at 
inclinations of 40 and 54 degrees from horizontal.  The ground anchors were drilled to a

length of about 15 feet at inclinations of about 30 to 40 degrees from horizontal.  A No. 8,

Tieback and Ground Anchor Testing 
In accordance with the project plans, a minimum of the first two and two percent of 
the remaining tiebacks installed should be performance tested. In addition, a minimum of 
ten percent of the ground anchors should be proof tested. Therefore, a total of three 
performance tests were conducted among the installed production tieback anchors. 
Production tieback anchors (T1-U, T2-U, and T14-U) were subjected to performance tests.  
The remaining production tieback anchors (total of 26 tieback anchors) were proof tested.  
In addition, seven of the ground anchors were proof tested. 
In general, the production tieback anchors were loaded to maximum test loads of 
about 36 to 68 kips.  The ground anchors were loaded to maximum test loads of about 
10 to 17 kips.  The anchor head movements for the tieback and ground anchors at each 
load increment up to the maximum test load were measured and recorded.  The results of 
the tieback and ground anchor testing are provided in the Summary of Tieback and 
Ground Anchor Installation and Testing, Plates 4.1 through 4.3.  The tieback and ground 
anchor test results are provided in Appendix D.   
Three of the tieback anchors (T15-U, T16-U, and T26-U) and one of the ground 
anchors (T8-L) could not hold the maximum test load without excessive displacement of 
the anchor head.  Three additional tiebacks and four additional ground anchors were 
installed and tested.  In addition, one of the ground anchors (T26-L) was deleted since the 
anchor was not accessible to the drilling equipment. 
Selected photographs taken during micropile, tieback, and ground anchor 
installation and testing are presented in Appendix E. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some of the work for the 
micropile, tieback, and ground anchor installation and testing may have occurred without 
our direct observation.
