INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kekaulike Revitalization Phase II project located
inthe Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The location
of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated March 26
1992 to Mitsunaga and Associates Inc
This report summarizes our findings from the preliminary field exploration and our
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses
of the data obtained during the exploration The recommendations are intended for
preliminary design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
During our preliminary exploration access for our drilling equipment to a portion
of the site was blocked by structures to be demolished Additional field exploration
should be accomplished after removal of the structures to confirm our preliminary findings
or modify our recommendations if necessary
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Kekaulike Revitalization Phase II project involves the construction
of a 4 story commercial and residential building complex on the site as a public housing
development A basement for vehicular parking is being considered and may also be
included as part of the proposed project development if found to be feasible

Groundwater Level
The groundwater levels in the borings were checked several times during our field
exploration and were encountered at depths varying from about 9 5 to 12 2 feet below the
existing ground surface
It is our experience that relatively low groundwater levels were observed in the
vicinity of the project site and this could have been caused by subsurface pumping in the
neighboring areas For planning and design of the project we believe that the
groundwater level should be assumed to occur at an elevation of approximately two 2
feet based on Mean Sea Level
Due to the proximity of the site to the ocean and to Nuuanu Stream it is
anticipated that the in situ groundwater level will fluctuate with tidal changes rainfall and
other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view assuming that further exploration generally confirms the general
soils conditions described in this report It appears that the underlying loose sandy and
coralline deposits cannot provide adequate support for shallow foundations without
inducing excessive settlements Therefore deep pile foundations embedded in the
underlying bouldery alluvium or basalt formation are recommended to provide foundation
support of the proposed structure
It is Our understanding that construction of a basement for vehicular parking may
also be included if found to be feasible in the proposed project Adequately designed
and properly implemented shoring and dewatering systems will be required for the
basement construction An adequate and properly implemented dewatering system is
particularly critical in the event the depth of the excavation exceeds approximately ten 10
feet The design and installation of the shoring and dewatering systems will generally be
governed by the contractor s construction methods and available equipment and these
should therefore be made the sole responsibility of the contractor within legal regulations
and also safety requirements It will also be necessary to provide for permanent control
of the groundwater encountered at the site

preliminary design It is recommended that additional borings be drilled and sampled to
confirm the conditions upon which the foundation design recommendations contained
herein are based or to form the basis for revisions to the recommendation if found to be
required The additional exploration should be deferred until after the existing structures
are demolished and removed

Site Grading
Minimal site grading involving cuts and fills is anticipated for the proposed
construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation rubbish old footings and pavements and other deleterious
materials The resulting grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the scarifying operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Generally select on site sandy soils may be used as fill If additional imported fill
is required the fill material should be non expansive select material less than three 3
inches in maximum dimension with less than 25 percent passing the No 200 Sieve and
a Plasticity Index less than 20 The material should be free of organic matter debris and
other deleterious material
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Pile Foundations
Based on the currently obtained preliminary exploration data and our previous
findings in connection with the Phase Kekaulike Revitalization project it appears that the
upper loose sandy to coralline deposits cannot provide adequate support for shallow
foundations such as mat or spread footings without inducing excessive settlements
under the proposed structure Therefore deep pile foundations embedded in the
underlying bouldery alluvium or basalt formation are recommended for foundation support
of the project The following are preliminary recommendations pertaining to pile
foundations which should be verified modified by additional exploration data
Pile Capacities
Sixteen and one half inch octagonal or 12 inch square prestressed concrete piles
may be used to support the proposed structure Our preliminary analysis indicates
that allowable vertical pile capacities ranging from about 55 to 75 tons per pile may
be used for 16 inch octagonal piles with lengths of 70 to 100 feet respectively
Allowable vertical pile capacities ranging from about 40 to 60 tons per pile may be
used for 12 inch square piles again with lengths of 70 to 100 feet respectively
It is Our Opinion that the piles will develop their bearing capacity by a combination
of end bearing on the underlying bouldery alluvium or basalt formation and of
frictional contribution from the pile shafts embedded in the surrounding coralline
deposits and alluvium A minimum pile spacing of three 3 feet on centers is
recommended

Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of about 30 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control so that pile driving within the loose deposits may be performed
at a reduced energy level to minimize the development of tensile stresses within
the piles which could cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose sandy soil the
hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced energy so that
excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach five 5 blows per inch for the last six 6 inches or
when aggregate blow counts are 60 blows for the last foot These are
preliminary estimates subject to modification pending the findings of the
recommended additional exploration program and or the results of the test
pile program described below
Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following objectives

1 To examine the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
2 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths and to further
verify delineate different areas with different production pile lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program should
consist of a minimum of 10 percent of the total number of piles as test piles for the
project The test piles may be incorporated into the actual foundation system later
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical engineer
should be consulted for selection of the test pile locations The test piles should be cast
ten 10 feet longer than the estimated production pile lengths Also the test pile program
should provide flexibility by allowing for addition of or relocation of the test piles as may
be deemed necessary to suit the conditions encountered during the test pile driving
A number of splice cans should be made available at the site during the test pile
driving period Splice cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least five 5 feet long
As a part of the test pile program a minimum of one 1 pile load test should be
budgeted The selected pile should be load tested to twice the allowable load capacity
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for
production pile driving The test pile program should be conducted under close and
continuous field observation by the project geotechnical engineer who will determine when

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete on grade slabs will be utilized for the ground floor or
basement floor parking facility These slabs will support vehicular traffic and therefore
function as rigid concrete pavements
For preliminary design of these rigid pavements we recommend that a minimum
of 6 inch thick Portland cement concrete on top of a minimum of 4 inch thick compacted
base course should be used If the rigid pavements are utilized in basement parking a
Suitable waterproofing mud slab in lieu of the compacted base course may be
considered

Utility Trenches
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and
foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot be considered to be responsible for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and test pile and production pile
driving for the project This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design
concepts or specifications and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
