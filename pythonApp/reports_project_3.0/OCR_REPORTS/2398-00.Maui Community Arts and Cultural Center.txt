This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed Maui Community Arts and
Cultural Center at Wailuku on the island of Maui Hawaii
location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation and pavement
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated July
24 1989 The scope of work consisted of the following
1 The drilling and sampling of twelve 12 borings to depths
between 6 5 and 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surface soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation and pavement recommendations

It is proposed to develop the site as a cultural center for the
performing and fine arts to serve the people of Maui County

following construction

more than 10 feet deep
2 A 1150 seat three 3 level Main Theater with underground trap
room seat storage and orchestra pit having tentative finished
floor elevations at 12 8 and 4 feet respectively
3 A Gallery Wing
4 A Black Box Wing

It is our understanding that the finished ground floor elevations of
the proposed theaters and buildings range from 23 to 25 5 feet

STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Wailuku about a mile inland from
Kahului Harbor and north of Maui Community College on the island of Maui
Hawaii
A 4 inch thick huge concrete slab approximately 120 feet wide was
noted along the eastern boundary of the site and extended in the northerly
direction with a length greater than 1 000 feet It appears that the
military had built a storage facility consisting of Quonset huts on the
concrete slab sometime between World War II and the Korean War The last
known use of these buildings was by small businesses such as a fresh
produce store and a trucking company
In the period up until demolition numerous abandoned vehicles
ranging from motorcycles to full sized military trucks had accumulated on
the site The vehicles have since been pushed to an area approximately in
the middle of the site
An old gas pump as well as the remains of restroom facilities were
also observed at the site Potential underground hazards due to previous
land use activities and hydrocarbon contamination if any should be
evaluated to consider potential health and safety impacts during site
grading and excavation work This environmental hazard site assessment is
not within our current scope of work
Most of the western half of the site is vegetated by keawe trees and
tall grasses Sand dune structures were observed in some areas Piles of
boulders mixed with silty clay were also observed An archery range is
located near the western end of the project site The areas adjacent to
the northern and southern boundaries of the site are undeveloped A
smaller concrete slab of 185 by 40 feet in plan dimension was also
observed in the southwestern quadrant of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of twelve 12 borings to depths ranging between 6 5
to 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a 4 to 8 foot thick
surface layer of medium dense fine sand overlying 10 to 15 feet of
residual soil consisting of medium stiff to stiff Clayey silt In the
eastern half of the site hard basalt formation was encountered at depths
of approximately 15 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface Thin
lenses of medium stiff ash materials generally less than 3 feet thick
were also encountered in Borings 5 and 6 at approximately depths of 2 5
feet and 1 foot respectively
In the western half of the site where the ground surface elevations
increased from an elevation below 20 to above 30 and 40 feet our field
exploration encountered a thicker surface layer of medium dense fine sand
extending to depths of at least 16 5 feet the maximm depth drilled
below the existing ground surface
During our field exploration groundwater was encountered at
approximate depths of 14 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
corresponding to elevations of 2 to 1 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Higher groundwater levels were noted in Borings 6 and 8 at elevations of
5 5 and 3 5 feet MSL respectively indicating possible perched seepage

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed relatively competent subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed Community Art Center site The proposed
development is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view
provided that our reconmendations are followed
In general the proposed structures may be supported by shallow
spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on in situ soils or
compacted structural fill
Special design construction considerations are required for
underground structures such as the trap room seat storage and orchestra
pit of the main theater due to anticipated deep excavation and
depending upon the final grades selected partial construction under
groundwater level
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
report preparation However it appears that the major site grading work
would consist of
A cuts up to 10 feet deep or more in the western half of the site
to lower existing site grades to accommodate for the proposed
finished floor elevations of 24 5 and 25 5 feet and

B fills up to 5 and 7 feet thick on the eastern half of the site to
raise the existing ground to finished floor elevations of 21 0 to

Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials such as abandoned vehicles and
miscellaneous items The area to receive fill or foundations should
then be scarified to a depth of 8 inches and recompacted to 95 of
its maximum density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any soft loose areas oil contaminated soil abandoned cesspools or
other deleterious materials disclosed during site preparation should
be replaced by removing the unsuitable materials and backfilling with
compacted suitable on site soil or imported material In particular
the high moisture content ash where encountered under the new fills
or structures should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site soils Contaminated soils should be removed to off site and
disposed of in accordance with the appropriate environmental laws and
regulations
It is our opinion that the existing concrete slab may be left in
place where the overlying fill is more than four 4 feet thick and
if the future foundation or utility excavation is not anticipated to
extend below the slab elevation
Fill Material
Generally the on site sandy material will be suitable for use as
structural fill if free of vegetation and over sized particles The
excavated on site ash material if any should not be re used within
the structural fill areas It may be stockpiled for subsequent use
as topsoil for planting if applicable

Fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to ten 10
inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dary

Fills placed against slopes steeper than five horizontal for one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched to properly bond the new
fill into firm existing ground See Plate 3
To provide adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back to
the design grade The amount of slope overfilling required will
depend on the type of compactor used but generally should be a
minimim of two 2 feet

horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V Intermediate benches may not be
required for fill slopes up to 20 feet in vertical height For fill
slopes higher than 20 feet but less than 30 feet an intermediate
bench about 8 feet wide should be placed at the mid height of the
slope

Foundations
Our field exploration generally encountered relatively competent
subsurface conditions Spread or continuous footing foundations may be
used for the proposed structures
The bottom of all footing excavations should be recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
prior to concrete placement Should soft loose soils be encountered at
the bottom of the footing excavations it should be removed and replaced
with granular fill compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimm of thirty 30
inches below the lowest adjacent outside finished grade The minimm
embedment for interior footings may be reduced to twenty 20 inches below
the finished grade
For footings bearing on recompacted in situ soil or compacted fill
an allowable net soil bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be used This
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads
are considered
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface run off away
from building foundations and slab
Anticipated footing settlements would be on the order of less than
one inch total and one half of an inch differential provided that the
above recommendations are followed

On Grade Slab and Exterior Walkways
Based on the non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at
this site on grade slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick layer of
base course materials To protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier between the slab and cushion
should be considered
The thickened edges of on grade slabs should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom of all
thickened edge excavations should be moistened and kept moist until
concrete placement The backfill against the concrete slab should be
adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
Underground Structures
It is our understanding that underground structures such as a trap
room seat storage and an orchestra pit are planned for the Main
Theater The anticipated depth of excavation is 9 to 17 feet Relatively
easy excavation is anticipated for the soils encountered
Due to the depth of excavation anticipated properly designed and
installed interlocking sheet piling support or braced shoring of the
excavations are recommended to minimize caving in of the trench walls
Sheeting and bracing installation must comply with all applicable
safety requirements The method and equipment used for excavation
sheeting and or bracing installation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits The adequacy and safety of the
sheeting and or bracing installation should be made the responsibility of
the contractor since his representative is continuously present on site to
observe any changing conditions which might affect stability
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2398 00 Page 11
For calculating preliminary earth loads in struts of braced
excavation an uniform lateral earth pressure of 26 p s f per foot of
depth may be used This recommended pressure does not contain a factor of
safety Additional loads due to surcharge traffic perched groundwater
or sloping ground conditions close to the edge of the excavation should be
added to the recommended pressure
An alternative to the sheeting and bracing operations would be to
slope back the open cut where sufficient area adjacent to the proposed
excavations is available for the over excavation The temporary slope of
the excavated faces may be as steep as the angle of internal friction of
the excavated materials which may be taken as 30 degrees from
horizontal for compacted fill and in situ medium dense fine sand
However if this overexcavation method is utilized backfill for the
overexcavation should be properly installed and compacted in 10 inch loose
lifts to obtain a minimm of 95 of its maximum dry density Proper
monitoring of the backfilling is necessary to assure that the proper level
of compaction is obtained to minimize probable settlement of future
footings to be located on or near the compacted backfill and to minimize
overloading of the buried retaining walls due to excessive compactive
effort
Dewatering measures may be required if excavation extends below the
high groundwater levels or if perched groundwater seepage is encountered
in the proposed underground excavation
The same allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be used for
footings supporting the underground structures However should high
moisture content ash materials or soft loose soils be encountered at the
bottom of footing excavation it should be over excavated to stiff in situ
soil and replaced with granular select borrow material compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
In order to provide more uniform support of the footing which is below the
groundwater level a minimum 18 inch thick layer of clean granular
material should be provided below the footing bottom

Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the automobile parking
area In general the compacted on site sandy materials can provide an
excellent subgrade support Assuming that either the on site soils or
imported select granular fills with CBR greater than 12 are used in the
pavement subgrade we recommend that the following preliminary pavement
design be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at
least 95 of the ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 maximum dry density to a depth
of 6 inches below the bottom of the base course
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
during construction and the above design section can be revised to
incorporate additional select borrow if necessary The recommended
section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided
for adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the proposed
construction should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review
and written comment prior to construction This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earth work and foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume
no responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction to monitor
the site mass grading footing excavation and foundation construction
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling
from the topographic map Elevations of the data points were determined
from the topographic map The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
GEOLABS HAWAII

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
and water seepage may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use by the project
consultants working on the Maui Community Arts and Cultural Center and for
specific application to the site grading and foundation design in
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and
practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the

project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the

engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the

purposes

attain a properly constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
