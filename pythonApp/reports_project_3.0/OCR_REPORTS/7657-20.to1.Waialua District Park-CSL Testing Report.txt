Dear Mr. Kunishige: 
In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated 
January 11, 2019, we performed non-destructive integrity testing of the drilled shaft 
foundations for the Baseball Field Lighting System Replacement project at Waialua District 
Park. This letter report summarizes our findings and recommendations obtained from 
Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing on the drilled shaft foundations installed for the 
new lighting system at Waialua District Park in the Waialua area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation, drilled shaft foundation 
installation observation service, and non-destructive integrity testing service for the 
Baseball Field Lighting System Replacement project. The purpose of our non-destructive 
integrity testing service was to evaluate the concrete integrity of the eight drilled shafts 
installed to support the light pole structures for the above project. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is within the existing Waialua District Park in the Waialua area on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The park is bounded generally by Goodale Avenue to the 
north and west, Waialua Beach Road to the east, and Waialua Elementary School to 
the south. In general, the project involves the reconstruction of the existing baseball field 
lighting system. We understand that a total of eight new light poles are planned for the 
project. The new light poles are supported on deep foundations consisting of 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with a diameter of about 60 inches 
and lengths ranging from about 25.7 to 27.3 feet were installed to support the new light 
poles.

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using 
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest. Crosshole Sonic Logging 
tests were performed between all the access tube combinations in the drilled shaft (four 
tubes resulting in six combinations). The northernmost access tube was designated as 
Tube No. 1, and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction.  
The steel access tubes were extended approximately 2.7 to 3.1 feet above the top 
of the pedestal concrete for access considerations for Shaft Nos. P-1 through P-8. We 
understand that potable water was used to fill the access tubes after concrete placement. 
Based on our observation in the field, the water levels within the access tubes were 
generally above the top of the drilled shaft elevation (approximately 3 feet below the top of 
the pedestal concrete) at the time of testing in all the shafts. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the 
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. The 
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, is presented on 
Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.8. The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination 
(profile) and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 5.1 through 5.16. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10 percent, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of 
less than 10 percent, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for 
Shaft Nos. P-1 through P-8 with the exception of a FAT increase of up to 19.2 percent in 
Shaft No. P-2 from about 15.1 to 16.2 feet below the top of the shaft. It should be noted 
that changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the 
degree of the reduction and the associated increase in FAT. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for all the drilled shafts ranged from 
approximately 12,200 to 16,800 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL tests 
and FAT increases of less than 10 percent, we believe that concrete integrity of all the 
drilled shafts, except for Drilled Shaft No. P-2, within the testing depths meets a Concrete 
Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the above CCRC.  
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-2 ranged from 
approximately 12,400 to 14,100 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases no greater than 10 percent (for almost the entire drilled shaft), we believe 
the concrete integrity of Drilled Shaft No. P-2 within the tested depth may be classified 
within a Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” except for the one small anomaly with a 
Concrete Condition Rating of “Questionable” in accordance with the above CCRC. It 
should be noted that the one small anomaly is minor in nature with velocity reductions (or 
FAT increases) of about 10 percent to 19.2 percent. 
Based on the relatively small portion of Drilled Shaft No. P-2 with a minor anomaly 
and the anomaly was not identified in more than one of the four access tube combinations, 
we believe the anomaly to be small in extent and does not warrant an exhaustive 
investigation. Therefore, we recommend the acceptance of Drilled Shaft Nos. P-1 through 
P-8 installed for the project.
