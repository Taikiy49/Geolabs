INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface explorations
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design
and construction of the foundations to support the proposed 20
story structure
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of a 20 story reinforced

concrete high rise structure approximately 50 feet by 110 feet in
plan dimensions A surrounding parking structure of 3 to 4 decks
will essentially occupy the entire property The lower deck will

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The rectangular site is approximately 119 feet by 182 feet in
plan and is located makai of Kinau Street near its intersection
with Piikoi Street in the Punchbowl section of Honolulu Accord
ing to the topography map provided by VTIN Pacific Inc the ground
surface at the site varies from a high point of elevation 35 2
feet at the Ewa mauka corner of the property to a low point of
elevation 25 5 feet at the Diamond Head makai corner Presently
the site is utilized for stock piling of soil and broken concrete
piles
It is our understanding that a swimming pool previously existed
at the Diamond Head mauka corner of the property and has been
backfilled
Geology
The proposed building site is underlain by alluvium water depos
ited soils and cinders derived from the nearby Punchbowl Volcano

W O 538 00
August 15 1974
The cinders were deposited upon coral formed when the sea level
was at a higher stage than the present It appears that the area
has experienced several sea level changes as well as numerous
periods of active volcanic eruptions
Following the formation of the coral during periods of high sea
levél the cinders were deposited during periods of active volcanic
eruptions The cinders commonly called black sand were reworked
by wave action to form zones of rounded black sand The black
sand was subsequently overlain by a brown clayey silt or silt
alluvium eroded from the nearby mountains
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing
seven test borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan
and Boring Locations Plate 1 A detailed description of the
field exploration methods and the logs of soils encountered are
included in the appendix A description of the accompanying
laboratory testing and the test results are also included in the
appendix
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
by an upper alluvium layer varying from 5 to 8 feet in thickness
This upper alluvium consists of loose to medium dense silty sand
and medium to stiff clayey silt

August 15 1974
The upper alluvium is underlain by black sand encountered between
elevations 21 at Boring 3 and elevation 28 at Boring 6 The black
sand varies in thickness from 9 to 12 feet and is predominantly
medium dense to dense
The black sand is underlain by hard brown silty clay or silty sand
with coral fragments This material grades into a light tan to
gray medium dense to dense coral sand extending to the limit of
the explorations at 50 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 3 5 and 6 at elevations of
2 1 and 2 respectively The remaining borings did not
encounter groundwater
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed lower level of the
finished floor will vary between elevations 22 5 and 25 5
Allowing approximately two feet for the minimum depth of penetra
tion for the foundation beneath the finish floor level it there
fore appears that the foundations will be bearing upon the surface
of the black sand
The black sand is medium dense and can provide adequate support
for the proposed structure Foundations bearing upon this material
can be designed for maximum contact bearing pressures of 5000 psf
for combined dead and live loadings
Page 5
W O 538 00
August 15 1974
The bearing value can be increased to 8000 psf providing that the
site is proof rolled at the foundation bearing level to eliminate
localized zones of loose material
The proof rolling requires a minimum of 12 passes with a ten ton
vibratory roller capable of delivering a total impact force in
excess of 45 000 foot pounds This proof rolling should be per
formed upon the proposed bearing surface for the foundations
designed for 8000 psf
The proof rolling and compaction operations should be closely
supervised by a soils technician to assure proper implementation
of the construction recommendations The recommendations given in
this report are contingent on such inspections
The foundations bearing within the upper alluvium can be designed
for maximum contact bearing pressures of 5000 psf providing that
the foundation excavations are inspected for localized soft zones
which must be removed and replaced with compacted fill
Based upon the above bearing pressures the estimated settlements
are one inch total and one half inch differential
Retaining Walls
It is recommended that the proposed retaining walls be designed
for an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pcf if the on site soils

are utilized as backfill The backfill should be compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T A frictional factor of 0 55
should be utilized for sliding resistance of the retaining wall
foundations
It is recommended that drains be placed outside the retaining
walls to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressures Ground
water was not encountered within the proposed building depths
during the subsurface explorations but due to the granular nature
of the materials accumalations of water can be expected during
periods of high percipitation
Floor Slabs
Expansive soils were not encountered during the subsurface explora
tions and slab on grade construction can therefore be utilized for
the floors The floor slabs should be divorced from load bearing
members to prevent cracking due to differential movements It is
recommended that the subgrade be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 72T

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings ranged from 18 5 to 50 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
appended Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drill
ing operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphical representation of the soils encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
diameter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

a total impact force in excess of 45 000 foot pounds

floor slabs should be divorced from load bearing members to
prevent cracking due to differential movements A potential
of one inch differential settlement could occur between the
