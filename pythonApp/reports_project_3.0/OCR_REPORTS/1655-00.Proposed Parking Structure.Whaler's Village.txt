Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
Proposed Parking Structure
Whaler s Village
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering investigation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Project Consideration
It is proposed to construct a new 3 level concrete parking
structure over the existing parking lot at Whaler s Village in Kaanapali
on the island of Maui Hawaii
W O 1655 00 I D 1820 Page 2
The proposed structure will be approximately 210 by 240 feet in
plan dimensions Relatively high column loads on the order of 400
kips and minimal site grading are anticipated
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed structure by determining the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the underlying soil
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling five 5 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in Appendix A The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
General Site Conditions
The proposed site is located to the west of Kaanapali Parkway in
the existing parking lot located to the north of Building A of
Whaler s Village at Kaanapali
The 50 000 square foot site is currently used as a parking lot for
patrons of the shopping village The ground slopes slightly from the
southwest towards the northeast of the site
Ground cover consists primarily of deteriorating asphaltic concrete
pavement with extensive cracks

Subsoil Condition
Five borings were drilled to depths of 15 0 to 26 5 feet below the
ground surface All borings encountered a top soil layer of medium
dense to dense dark brown silty sand with basalt gravel underlain by
medium dense to dense light brown beach sand at a depth of about 1 5
feet to the depth drilled The beach sand was found to have a cemented
gone at about 10 to 15 foot depth
In Borings 2 and 5 the beach sand graded to loose at a depth of
about 5 feet but became denser again at a depth of about 8 5 feet in
Boring 5 This may indicate the presence of a localized loose
layer lens of the beach sand at depths of about 6 to 8 5 feet in the
area between these two borings
Groundwater was encountered at 7 4 to 9 feet below the ground
surface at the time of our field exploration
Discussion and Recommendations
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and column loads we
believe that shallow spread footing foundation bearing on the
recompacted in situ medium dense to dense sand may be used for the
parking structure
We recommend that the foundation be placed a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration
wind seismic loading Settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one inch total and one half
of an inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1655 00 I D 1820 Page 4
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure
of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used against the buried footing
of the structure to determine the passive soil resistance
Additional Probing
Due to the probable presence of localized loose layer lens of the
beach sand we recommend that at the time of construction the soils at
the bottom of the footings near Borings 2 and 5 areas be probed to a
depth of at least 7 feet below the footing bottom level Should such
layer lens be encountered the loose soil would have to be
over excavated completely and replaced with select on site sandy soil or
other suitable imported material compacted in 8 inch compacted lifts to
ASTM D 1557 78 maximum densities
Alternatively the extent and thickness of the suspected loose
layer lens of sand may be delineated now by additional probings at the
anticipated columm footing locations around Borings 2 and 5 to further
evaluate if other special footing preparations or considerations be
needed prior to the final foundation design stage
Site Grading
Due to the relatively flat ground minimal cut is anticipated for
the site development The finished grade level is not available at this
time but it is anticipated to remain approximately the same as the
existing one
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
pavements debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material

W O 1655 00 I D 1820 Page 5
The areas which are to receive the foundation should be kept moist
and tamped prior to placement of the footing reinforcement steel if they
are loosened during excavation The areas which are to receive pavement
should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the cleared
ground surface moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density Any loose or soft spots encountered at the
footing locations or subgrade level should be recompacted if it is
sandy or removed if it is clayey or organic and then replaced with
select on site sandy soil or other suitable imported material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
If required new imported fill material should consist generally of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Pavement
The asphalt concrete pavement of the existing parking lot is
deteriorated with extensive cracks This pavement distress may be due
to aging and the use of an inadequate base material and may be
aggravated by the high volume automobile and truck traffic
We recommend that the existing pavement should either be replaced
with new sections or repaired with overlay during the construction of
the proposed parking structure

In order to ensure good bonding between the old pavement and the
overlay the existing asphaltic concrete pavement should be kept free of
debris rubbish or other unsuitable materials prior to and during the
course of overlaying operation
It should be noted that the service life of the overlaid pavement
would probably be less than that of a completely replaced section
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading pavement and foundation
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading subgrade preparation footing
excavations additional probing over excavation and backfill of loose
layer lens be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of site grading pavement and foundation design of the
proposed construction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration Laboratory Testing
NG SS ea Ne
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
five 5 borings to depths of 15 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A l through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
