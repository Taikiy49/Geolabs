PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the eastern portion of the Ewa by Gentry Residential
Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
located east of Fort Weaver Road south of Area 19 and north of the Hawaii Prince Golf
Course The project involved utility and roadway construction and fine grading of an area
of about 9 34 acres for a residential development A Project Location Map and a Site Plan
are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the utility and roadway construction and fine grading operations for the
project site
UTILITY ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION AND FINE GRADING
We observed the utility and roadway and fine grading work for the Area 39 project
from May 1 2006 to The utility and roadway construction and fine
grading work consisted of placement and compaction of fills and backfills on the order of
up to about 12 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades Albert C Kobayashi Inc
performed the grading work During the grading operations we obtained samples of the
on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to
our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the utility and roadway construction and fine grading operations periodic
field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on the
attached Plates 4 1 through 4 22 These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the

work for the above referenced project that we observed and tested in the period covered

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
