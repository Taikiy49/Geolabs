SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Our field exploration along the proposed Baldwin Avenue Resurfacing project 
generally encountered very stiff to hard clayey silts and silty clays extending to the 
maximum depth explored of approximately 6.5 feet below the existing pavement surface. 
We did not encounter groundwater in the shallow borings at the time of our field 
exploration. 
In general, the type and severity of the distresses exhibited in the pavement areas 
would determine whether rehabilitation or reconstruction of the existing pavements should 
be performed. Existing pavements showing minor distresses and severity are generally 
rehabilitated with cold-planing and an AC overlay. Reconstruction of the existing 
pavements is usually performed for those areas where the observed distresses are likely 
caused by failures in the underlying base, subbase, and/or subgrade materials. 
Our pavement condition survey at the project site indicated that the majority of the 
pavements under consideration for resurfacing appear to exist in fair to good condition. 
Based on our engineering evaluation and construction considerations, we recommend 
rehabilitating these areas with a regular AC overlay consisting of cold-planing 2.5 inches of 
the existing AC surface and replacing with 2.5 inches of new AC overlay. 
Since the majority of the pavement areas in poor condition are confined to a certain 
stretch of Baldwin Avenue, it is our opinion that the pavement reconstruction should be 
designed based on a pavement life of 20 years in accordance with the guidelines 
presented in the pavement design manual prepared by the State of Hawaii - Department 
of Transportation, Highways Division, Material Testing and Research Branch. In general, 
the existing pavements may be reconstructed with a new pavement structural section 
consisting of 2.5 inches of AC on 4 inches of ACB over 6 inches of AB.  It should be noted 
that CBR tests and/or field observations should be performed on the actual subgrade soils 
during construction to confirm that the above pavement design section is adequate. 
A layer of reinforcing geogrid (such as Tensar TriAx Grid, TX7 or equivalent) on a 
layer of permeable separator (Mirafi 180N or equivalent) should be provided below the 
new pavement structural section.  The reinforcing geogrid would serve to enhance the 
R-value of the subgrade materials by providing stabilization and reinforcement to the 
underlying base/subbase/subgrade materials.

SECTION 1.  GENERAL 
1.1 
Introduction 
This pavement justification report presents the results of our geotechnical 
engineering analyses performed for the Baldwin Avenue Resurfacing, Phase 2, 
(Haliimaile Road to Hana Highway) project in the Makawao area on the Island of Maui, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory 
testing, and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for 
the rehabilitation and reconstruction of the existing pavements only. The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
report. 
1.2 
Project Considerations 
The roadway resurfacing project is along Baldwin Avenue between about 
Haliimaile Road and Hana Highway in the Makawao area on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. 
The approximate limits of the roadway resurfacing project are shown on the General 
Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The existing roadway within the project limits is a two-lane undivided roadway 
measuring approximately 5 miles.  Based on the information provided, we understand 
that it is desired to resurface the existing pavements with a new asphalt concrete 
overlay. However, a pavement condition survey conducted by our office indicated that 
some locations along the roadway alignment are in poor condition. These areas are 
experiencing pavement distresses consisting of rutting, depressions, and cracking. 
Therefore, it is our opinion that these areas will need to be reconstructed with a new 
pavement structural section. 
Special attention was given to the potential presence of near-surface soft and/or 
loose soils, which may be a contributing factor to the pavement failures observed at 
some locations of the roadway under consideration. The findings obtained from our field 
exploration and laboratory testing programs will be used as a basis to formulate

SECTION 2.  SITE CHARACTERIZATION 
2.1 
Regional Geology 
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes; the older West Maui 
(Tertiary Epoch) and the more recent East Maui, also known as Haleakala (Pleistocene 
Epoch).  The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow, gently sloping plain located between these 
two volcanoes.  The project site is situated on the isthmus connecting the two volcanic 
domes. 
The isthmus was built by the deposition of sediments over the underlying basalts 
of the two volcanoes.  During the Pleistocene Epoch, sea levels fell to stands lower than 
present sea level exposing large areas of coralline sand on what was the ocean bottom. 
Wind action transported these coralline sands inland forming extensive areas of sand 
dunes.  In some areas, these dunes have been mantled by man-made fill which was 
apparently placed during the period that the area was used for the cultivation of sugar 
cane. 
2.2 
Existing Site/Pavement Conditions 
The existing Baldwin Avenue within the project limits is generally a two-lane 
undivided roadway between Haliimaile Road and Hana Highway. The approximate 
limits of the roadway under consideration are shown on the General Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The shoulder areas beyond the edge of pavement generally consist of trimmed 
grass, sidewalks at some locations in Paia Town, traffic signs and utility posts. The 
surface soils along the shoulder areas below the vegetation generally consist of brown 
clayey silts with some sand as well as weathered rock formation in some areas. The 
existing Baldwin Avenue roadway alignment generally slopes down from south to north 
throughout the project limits.  
Our office conducted a site reconnaissance in September 2013 to collect general 
information for the existing pavement conditions along the portion of the roadway under 
consideration. Detailed descriptions of our field surveys are presented in the following 
subsections. It should be noted that the term “Structural Defects” refers to areas where 
the pavement deterioration are believed to be caused by failures in the underlying base,

2.21 Areas with Minor to Moderate Distresses and Severity 
Based on our site reconnaissance, the portion of Baldwin Avenue extending from 
Haliimaile Road to Ulumau Place within the existing pavements under 
consideration generally falls into this category and appear to be in fair condition with 
localized low to moderate signs of structural defects observed. Overall pavement 
deterioration is restricted to light alligator cracking and of low severity, localized 
subgrade fatigue of low to moderate severity, as well as a few rough AC patches 
along the outside edge tire lanes. The ride characteristic of the pavement was 
generally fair with the exception of the AC patches.  
2.2.1 Areas with Base/Subgrade Failures 
In general, the remaining portion of Baldwin Avenue extending from Ulumau Place 
to Hana Highway, as well as various isolated locations from Haliimaile Road to 
Ulumau Place, within the existing pavements under consideration, falls under this 
category and was observed to be in poor condition with moderately to highly 
distressed areas. Alligator and alligator block cracking were observed to be 
widespread and generally moderate to high in severity. The poor areas consist of 
elongated, severely distressed zones of alligator block cracking with depressions of 
up to 1.5 inches, rutting and some shoving of the pavement within the tire lanes. 
Potholes patched with AC material within the fatigued tire lanes and utility trenches 
were observed at various locations along the alignment. The ride characteristic of 
the roadway was observed to be generally fair to irregular and bumpy in the areas 
of poor pavements. The approximate limits of these areas requiring pavement 
reconstruction are shown on the Site Plans, Plates 3.1 through 3.19.

3.1 
Methodology of Pavement Design 
In general, the type and severity of the distresses exhibited in the pavement 
areas would determine whether rehabilitation or reconstruction of the existing 
pavements should be performed. Existing pavements with minor distresses are 
generally rehabilitated with an AC overlay with cold-planing.  The procedures used in 
determining the new pavement section were generally determined based on the 
methodology described in Chapter 3 of the Pavement Design Manual dated 
March 2002. The Pavement Design Manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii - 
Department of Transportation, Highways Division, Materials Testing and Research 
Branch. The pavement design methodology is based on the Hveem Stabilometer 
method developed and used by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans).

In general, the type and severity of the distresses exhibited in the pavement 
areas would determine whether rehabilitation or reconstruction of the existing 
pavements should be performed.  Existing pavements with minor distresses are 
generally rehabilitated with an AC overlay with cold-planing.  Based on our evaluation, it

AC overlay consisting of cold-planing 2.5 inches of the existing AC surface and 
replacing with 2.5 inches of new AC overlay.

locations are shown on the Site Plans, Plates 3.1 through 3.19. The borings were drilled 
using a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers. 
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and 
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488, Standard 
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils, and monitored the drilling operations on 
a near-continuous (full-time) basis. These classifications were further reviewed visually 
and by testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general accordance with 
ASTM D2487, Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes 
(Unified Soil Classification System), as shown on the Soil Log Legend, Plate A-0.1. 
Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with ASTM D2487 are described 
in the Soil Classification Log Key, Plate A-0.2. Rock materials were described in general 
accordance with the Rock Description System, as shown on the Rock Log Legend, 
Plate A-0.3. The Rock Description System is based on the publication “Suggested 
Methods for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in Rock Masses” by the 
International Society for Rock Mechanics (March 1977).  Graphic representations of the 
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings, Plates A-1 through A-30. 
Relatively “undisturbed” soil samples were obtained in general accordance with 
ASTM D3550-01(2007), Ring-Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving a 3-inch 
OD Modified California sampler with a 140-pound hammer falling 30 inches. In addition, 
some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with 
ASTM D1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving a 2-inch OD 
standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop. The blow counts needed 
to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18-inch drive are shown as the 
“Penetration Resistance” on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths. The 
penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the number of blows 
required for the specific sampler type used. The blow counts may need to be factored to 
obtain the Standard Penetration Test (SPT) blow counts. 
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in 
the field.  The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined 
compressive strength of the sample.  Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are 
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths.

Moisture Content (ASTM D2216) and Unit Weight (ASTM D2937) determinations 
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of 
soil properties. The test results are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate 
sample depths. 
Ten Atterberg Limits tests (ASTM D4318) were performed on selected soil 
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits of the near-surface fine-grained soils. 
The test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample 
depth. Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B-1. 
Six laboratory Resistance (R) Value tests (ASTM D2844) were performed on bulk 
samples of the near-surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of 
the soils. The test results are presented on Plates B-2 through B-7.
