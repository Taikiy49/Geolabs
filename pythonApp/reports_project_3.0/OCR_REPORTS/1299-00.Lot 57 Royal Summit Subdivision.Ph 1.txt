Dear Mr Shoehigh
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
According to the preliminary house plan prepared by Systems Dynamics
Ltd dated the proposed residence will be a two story
wooden structure approximately 42 by 68 feet in plan dimensions with 3
wooden decks and a 3 car garage Concrete on grade slabs are planned
for the driveway and garage area Both slab on grade and post
and beam construction is planned for the house
Low retaining walls are anticipated to provide grade separation
between various portions of the house
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northern side of Naali i Street in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
The lot has two generally level pads with a transitional slope
measuring about 5 1 H V between them The lower pad is located at
the rear of the site The ground surface then continues to slope
downward at 2 1 H V and is bisected by two 8 foot wide benches at
every 15 feet of vertical drop for a distance of about 35 feet The
compacted fill slope then daylights to the natural slope and continues
to the bottom of an existing gulch
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS

depth of 6 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered will generally be
suitable for foundation support of the proposed house provided that
some Care is exercised during construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that its swelling potential may increase to moderate if
allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize the swelling potential
the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and protected
from drying during the period of time required for preparation prior
to final concrete placement

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the residual soil
Post footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the Slab On Grade section is also required
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway kitchen
dining and living room areas It is recommended that a minimum
12 inch perimeter footing embedment be utilized to provide a barrier
against future water intrusion under the slab area

Site Grading
It is our understanding that the site will have small cuts and fills
of generally less than two feet

Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil
such as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum
8 inch loose lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site soils
in the Royal Summit area generally has low to moderate swelling
potential and would require special preparation prior to use as
structural fill The on site soil can be used for yard fill
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundation
Two horizontal to one vertical cut and fill slopes are recommended

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
