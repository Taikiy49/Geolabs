PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site and to provide data for the use in design and con
struction of the site grading and building foundations for
the Brookcourt Phase III project site
The subsurface soil and groundwater conditions were
explored by field reconnaissance of the site as well as
test borings and probings conducted in significant areas
of the project site
Laboratory tests were performed to evaluate the representative
physical properties of the surface and subsurface soils that
would influence the grading and foundation design and
construction
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on Site Plan
Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Information regarding the field explorations and laboratory
testing are presented in the Appendices

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located about 9 000 feet east of
Emepela Place between Haiku Road and Kahuhipa Street
in Kaneohe The site is adjacent to and part of the south
eastern boundary of the Brookcourt project
The site is located on the southeastern edge of the large
marshy drainageway which crosses through the area
The lower ground areas consist of gently sloping marshy
wet ground conditions including heavy vegetation and high
water conditions Running water was noted in the small
stream through the central portion of the drainageway
The gently sloping southeastern portion of the site is
about 10 to 20 feet higher than the lower drainageway
area and it is covered with grass and trees
The elevations at the site generally vary from about
103 to 112 in the lower drainageway area and about 112 to 130
in the upper southeastern portion
ae mm
W O 873 10 P 4
Medium and high rise apartment buildings and warehouses
are located along the southern and southeastern boundaries
of the site The remaining areas were vacant at the time
of our field exploration
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of three 3 story
wood framed townhouse structures about 50 by 80 feet in
plan The units will be situated mostly over the higher
portions of the area but partially over some of the lower
marshy areas
A driveway and parking area will be located along the
upper southeastern boundary of the site
A tennis court is planned in the southwestern portion of
the area
Access to the site will be from the proposed Phase II of
the Brookcourt project A bridge structure in lieu of a
culvert is now planned to provide access across the
drainageway into the site
A retaining wall about 10 feet high is planned along the
southeastern boundary of the project

According to the preliminary finished grades available to
us at this time most of the site will be filled Fills
up to about 20 feet are planned in the low marshy areas
near the proposed buildings Up to about six 6 feet
of fill is planned for the tennis court area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The development site is located roughly midway between
the Pali and the coast on the windward slope of the Koolau
Range This area is part of a deeply eroded and weathered
section typified by alluvial deposits overlying a volcanic
dike complex The extended period of erosion has also
resulted in deposition of quiet water recent alluvium
in valleys and drainageways including localized deposits
of organic silt
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings probings and site reconnaissance
the following major soil conditions were encountered at
the site
1 Higher southeastern area Stiff to very stiff clayey
silt with weathered gravel Consolidated Older Alluvium
were encountered in these areas
ee ee
W O 873 10 P 6
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings
2 Marshy drainageway Soft marshy subsoils Recent
Alluvium about 8 to 10 feet deep near the center
of the drainageway and thinning out near the edges
over stiff clayey silt were encountered in this area
Below these upper soils is a rock layer at approximately
95 feet elevation
Fluctuating water levels generally from 0 to 3 feet
deep were noted during the field exploration The
water levels in the drainageway will fluctuate with
rainfall conditions at the upper valley area

Foundations
For the proposed wood framed residential structures spread
or continuous footing foundations can be used if the under
lying soft subsoils below the fill and building areas are
removed prior to site filling
The footings should extend a minimum of 18 inches below
the finish grades Foundations located close to the top
of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain
a six foot minimum horizontal set back distance from the
fill slope face
The foundations can be designed using the following
allowable bearing values
Very stiff natural soil up to 4000 p s f
Compacted fill over
stiff ground 2500 p s f
The bearing values for compacted fill are based on the
assumption that 90 percent relative compaction can be
achieved
The above bearing capacity values are for total dead plus
live loads and may be increased by 33 percent when tem
porary lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
W O 873 10 P 18
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to
300 p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead load
times a coefficient of friction equal to 0 45
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
If the soft subsoils behind the buttress fill are not
removed prior to site filling then some of the units
will be located partly over stiff in situ ground and
partly over fill underlain by soft to medium subsoils
In order to reduce differential settlement between the
portion of the buildings over cut and the portions over
fill drilled caisson foundation extending to the weather
ed rock formation at about 15 to 20 foot depths should be
used for the portion of the building located over fill
underlain by soft marshy soils The caissons should be

The remaining portions of the building on stiff in situ
ground can be supported on spread footings designed as
