FIELD EXPLORATION
For our limited study the field exploration consited of one
shallow hand auger hole to 4 feet between the garage and
house Disturbed samples were obtained from the auger
cutting Laboratory tests were performed on the auger
cuttings and surface samples obtained around the house
LABORATORY TESTS
Laboratory soil tests performed on the near surface samples
consisted of Atterberg Limits one inch thick ring swell
test and natural moisture content determination
Our laboratory test results indicate that the existing soils
can be classified as a clay CH soil with high swell poten
tial 10

Based on our visual observations and laboratory soil tests
it appears that much of the damage noted may be due to the
following causes

Basic remedial work would consist of underpinning the affected
foundation of walls and individual shallow post footings to
obtain competent bearing further down and minimize future
settlement

The low spots near slabs and foundations should be filled
to drain water away from the structures and avoid ponding
The garage retaining wall appears to be an under designed
structure with no grouting of the cells We anticipate
eventual recontruction after a few years Removal and
replacement of improper backfill as well as a new wall
might be required
It is our opinion that deeper footing embedment down to a
depth of a minimum of 24 inches would be required for the
individual post footings bearing on the existing expansive
soils The footing excavation should be backfilled with
concrete up to above the ground surface and the 4x4 posts
should be bolted to a footing strap
The extent of the remedial work required would have to be
determined after more detailed field exploration Additional
borings would have to be drilled near the affected footings
and slabs to actually determine the existing underlying soil
condition and to evaluate the depth of underpinning and if
the backfill removal may be required
The existing CMU wall should be evaluated by a structural
engineer to see if they are competent to support the existing
backfill loads

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and
foundation engineering principles and practices This war
ranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied
The preliminary recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from
shallow hand probing and laboratory test results obtained
during this investigation and are intended for the remedial
planning of this project
Additional explorations would be required to obtain more
detailed information of the subsoil conditions prior to
design of the remedial work
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by hand probing There unfore

pockets may occur in localized areas Additional expendi

repairs
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accomodate
