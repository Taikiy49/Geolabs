INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation
for the proposed Corrigan residence a
2 story structure located on a down slope lot on Mawaena
Street
The purpose of the subsurface soil investigation is to identify
the existing soil conditions for the design and construction
of the building foundation
W 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site of the proposed Corrigan residence is
located on a hillside in Kahaluu on the windward side
of the tsland of Oahu It lies on the east makai side
of Mawaena Street and overlooks the center portion of
Kaneohe Bay
The total area of the site lot is 11 368 square feet
and is approximately 152 feet deep and 86 feet wide The
rear 50 feet of the site is of a triangular configuration
with its apex at about the rear center
The site is bounded on the west by Mawaena Street and on
the north south and east sides by 5 adjacent residential
lots A lava rock wall runs along the north and south side
boundary lines and separates the site from the adjacent lower
elevation lots
There is no constructed means of access into the site from
Mawaena Street
At the time of our soil investigation there were no existing
structures present except for the lava rock walls along most
of the property line A portion of this lava rock wall also
runs across the site about 50 feet from the rear
The site slopes in a makai direction and is basically divided
into three sections The front one third section of the site
W O
has a slope of about 6 percent the center section has a
slope of about 5 percent and the rear 35 feet has a slope
of about 22 percent The highest elevation of the site
is 116 feet along Mawaena Street and its lowest elevation
of 71 feet is at the rear The average elevation of the
site is higher than the elevations of the adjacent lots on
the north and east sides There were no visible signs of
drainage ways present
The site vegetation consists of a dense matted down layer
of California grass about 3 to 4 feet high throughout
the site and Haole Koa trees growing along the north side
of the site
During the investigation a portion of the front one third
of the site was grubbed for working convenience
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The present plan is to construct a 2 story high building
about 1800 square feet in plan area The main floor will
be at elevation 110 feet and the lower floor will be at
elevation 100 feet
Based on the planned floor elevations site grading could
involve a maximum of 6 feet of fill at the north side of
the building area and about 12 feet of cut at the west
side of the proposed building
W 4
Retaining walls are anticipated for the proposed structure
which will be constructed over the slope area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated near the northwest side of
the once formed but now eroded Koolau volcanic caldera
a sunken in crater or depression at the summit of a
volcano over a mile in diameter and its underlying
naturally occurring soil formation consists of a soil
weathered from the rock surrounding the caldera
During the formation of the Koolau caldera numerous dikes
magma that solidified in near vertical fractures most
common in the surrounding caldera areas were developed in
the surrounding caldera rock ranging from a few inches to
about 12 feet thick averaging 2 to 3 feet These dikes
represented a relatively denser rock more resistant to
weathering and erosion because of the slower rate of soli
dification of the magma within the fractures that contained
them High temperatures volcanic gases and hot water from
the volcano altered and weathered the rocks in the summit
and surrounding areas while stream and wave erosion along
with the changing sea levels over millions of years eroded
the caldera and its eastern flank away
W 5
What we see today at the project site and in the areas
of Kaneohe and Kailua are the remnants of this continuous
weathering and eroding process The present topography
of these areas in and around the ancient caldera is of
a mature to old age landscape of relatively low rolling
hills and localized swampy areas The ground is generally
comprised of a thick residual soil with localized areas of
weathered dike rock within the soil matrix
The U S Soil Conservation Services Soil Survey of Islands
of Molokai and Lanai State of Hawaii
August 1972 shows that the project site s soil lies on
the border between soilttypes AeE and ALF and describes
them as follows
AeE Alaeloa Silty Clay 15 30 slopes This
soil occurs on smooth side slopes and toe
slopes in the uplands
MH Unified Soil Classification
ALF Alaeloa Silty Clay 40 70 slopes
MH Unified Soil Classification
Subsurface Exploration
Two test borings were drilled to depths of 13 and 22 feet
in order to explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain
samples for laboratory testing In additional three test
W 6
pits were dug by a backhoe while one shallow test pit was
dug by hand to further identify soil conditions over the
slope area Detailed information on field explorations
and laboratory testing are covered in the appendix along
with the boring and test pit logs and laboratory test
results The locations of borings and test pits are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
Soil Condition
The site is blanketed by about 1 foot of dark brown clayey
sand with some gravel Beneath this surface soil is a stiff
mottled brown and light brown clayey sand soil layer with
some decomposed rock At Boring Nos 1 and 2 locations
this fill layer is about 12 feet thick with traces of roots
The fill material is quite uniform throughout the entire
layer except for a piece of wire which was encountered in
Test Pit No 3 at a depth of 4 feet The fill layer could
possibly be placed on the slope during the Mawaena Street
roadway construction No distinguishable debris branches
or foreign material noted in the boring and test pit holes
The fill layer is underlain by a stiff mottled reddish
brown silty clay to the full depth of the borings
No groundwater was noted in the drilled holes and test pits
at the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface condition encountered during this
soil investigation and the analysis of the site s topo
graphy the proposed building area is underlain by a
relatively thick layer of fill The fill layer is about
12 feet thick along the Mawaena Street side and estimated
about 7 to 10 feet thick on the down slope area A gene
ralized subsurface cross section A A as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 is presented on Plate 2
During the test pit excavations at the project site no
distinguishable compacted layer can be identified in the
existing fill and traces of roots were noted uniformly
throughout the entire layer It is our opinion that the
fill was placed in at one mass grading a long time ago
and can be classified as an uncontrolled fill
For slope stability and uniform support of the proposed
building construction we recommend that the entire
building should be supported on the in situ reddish
brown silty clay residual soil below the existing
fill
Based on the present scheme it is estimated that the
footing at down slope side will be required to go down
about 5 to 7 feet below the lower floor elevation in
order that the footing can bear on the residual soil
W 8
If the final building location could be moved back 10
feet toward Mawaena Street the footing depth could then
be reduced to 3 to 5 feet below the lower floor elevation
As mentioned above the thickness of the fill over the
slope area is an estimate only therefore final footing
bottom elevations should be determined during site grading
and footing excavation under a soils engineer s inspection
The on site soil is highly expansive upon wetting from
the existing moisture content Slab on grade construction
should be designed for the expansive soil condition If
slab on grade is used over the existing fill continuous
retaining wall structure should be provided at the down
slope portion of the building as perimeter footing foun
dation This will avoid having to remove the existing
fill under the slab area and then backfill with proper
keying and benching procedures into the original slope
Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundation bearing below the
existing fill on the underlying stiff mottled reddish
brown clay soil can be designed for an allowable bearing
pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot The bearing
value can be increased to 4 000 pounds per square foot
when considering seismic and wind loadings Due to the
W 9
expansive soil condition wall and spread footing foundation
should bear a minimum of 2 feet below the adjacent final
grade
Footings on slope should extend below slope surface such
that the outer edge of footing should have a minimum hori
zontal set back distance of 5 feet out to the slope surface
For footing bearing on the in situ residual soil it is
estimated that the total settlement will be less than one
inch with differential settlement of less than one half of
an inch
Slab On Grade
For slab on grade over the on site expansive soil one of
the following two alternatives may be incorporated One
alternative is to construct a 2 feet capping of non expansive
compacted select borrow material beneath the slab The other
is to utilize deeper perimeter footing designed for high
expansive soils For construction details of footing and
slab on expansive soil condition refer to Plates3 and 4
Prior to placing the granular base course the subgrade
soil in the undercut area should be wetted to above its
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 compaction
W 10
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
polyethylene moisture barrier should be placed below the
concrete slab
Retaining Wall
Retaining wall should be designed to receive active lateral
pressure equivalent to a fluid density of 55 pounds per
cubic foot if granular material is used as backfill material
behind the wall A friction factor of 0 35 can be utilized
to determine sliding resistance of the retaining wall foun
dation
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be incor
porated to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the wall
Earthwork and Grading
The proposed site grading will consist of mainly cut into
the existing slope bank
New fill up to 6 feet will be required behind the retaining
wall at the northeast corner of the building area The on
site soils are acceptable as general fill material only
Select borrow should be off site non expansive fill materials
such as coral or quarry waste
W ll
All construction areas to receive fill should be undercut
to remove the surface vegetation and topsoil Prior to
the placement of new fill the exposed surface should be
wetted to above its optimum content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density Additional fill should
then be placed in 9 inch horizontal lifts and compacted to
the above specified density
Where the fill to be placed over the slope area of the site
the fill material should be keyed and benched into the firm
soil below the existing fill layer as approved by the soils
engineer
Slope
Fill and cut slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2 1
ratio or flatter may be used The on site clayey soils can
be cut at essentially vertical slope for short term construc
tion condition up to 8 feet deep Beyond that depth unsup
ported excavation should be sloped back at one horizontal
to one vertical slope ratio 1 1
Drainage
The on site soil is highly expansive Water should not be
allowed to pond adjacent to foundations and on top of the
slopes The site should be graded to provide positive
drainage away from all structures and prevent any runoff
over the slopes
W 12
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the fill placement inspection of
cut slopes and foundation excavations should be carried
out by the soils engineer to permit correlation between
the preliminary soil data obtained during this investi
gation and the actual soil conditions encountered during
construction in order to minimize misunderstanding of the
field forces by both the letter and spirit of our report
as well as to note any subsurface conditions different
from those forming the basis of our recommendations

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 2 borings B 1 and B 2 which were terminated
at depths of 13 and 22 feet below the existing ground sur
face Field exploration was supplemented by digging 3 test
pits with a backhoe and one shallow hand dug test pit over
the slope area The locations of the borings and test pits
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2 In the
borings samples were obtained by driving either a standard
penetration split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depth
