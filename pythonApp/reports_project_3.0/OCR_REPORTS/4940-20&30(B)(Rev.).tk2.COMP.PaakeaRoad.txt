This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the roadway construction for the Paakea Road Hakimo Road Lualualei Naval Road
Connector project from through in general
accordance with our fee proposals dated and May 6 2008
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project will involve the construction of new roadway extending from
Hakimo Road to Lualualei Naval Road for a total length of approximately 1 700 linear feet
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The new roadway generally consists of two 12 foot
wide travelways with a 10 foot wide grassed area on both sides
Kaikor Construction Associates Inc
Page 2

The new road will traverse along an existing narrow concrete paved road from
Hakimo Road Paakea Road to Ulehawa Stream and a gravel paved road from
Ulehawa Stream to Paakea Road Lualualei Naval Road intersection As part of the project
construction a new 100 foot long single span concrete bridge will replace the existing
bridge over the Ulehawa Stream crossing Geotechnical engineering observations
performed during the load test and production shaft installation program for the new bridge
structure are presented in our report entitled Drilled Shaft Installation Report Waianae
Coast Emergency Alternate Route Paakea Road Hakimo Road Lualualei Naval Road
Connector Lualualei Waianae Oahu Hawaii dated
Based on the plan and profile drawings provided we envision that new fills on the
order of up to about 10 feet thick will be required to raise the existing ground surface to the
design finished grades along the areas leading up to the new bridge abutments In
general the roadway improvement project involved the subgrade preparation and
placement of the pavement structural section
Kaikor Construction Associates Inc and Site Engineering Inc are the general
contractors during the initial and final phases of the project with C J Peterson Services
Inc performing the earthwork operation for the entire project The earthwork operation
consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation of the surface expansive clays
preparation of pavement subgrades and placement and compaction of select borrow and
base course
Following the clearing operation the areas within the new pavement limits were
generally over excavated where necessary to the bottom of the select borrow layer
The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
The select borrow subbase and base course were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The maximum dry density of the materials referred to
in this report were determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 143 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 The results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on the attached Plates 3 1 through 3 8
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
