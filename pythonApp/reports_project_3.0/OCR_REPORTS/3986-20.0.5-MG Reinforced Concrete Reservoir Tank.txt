in accordance with our proposal dated we have completed the
foundation probing and grouting services for the reinforced concrete reservoir tank
located at an elevation of 618 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the above referenced project
This report summarizes our field observations during the probing and grouting operations
performed at the subject site
The project site is located in the vicinity of the Mauna Kea Beach Hotel in the Ouli
area of the District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The project involves the construction of a reinforced concrete reservoir
tank at an elevation of 618 feet MSL a reinforced concrete reservoir tank at an
elevation of 780 feet MSL several control buildings and booster pump pads an access
roadway and related water lines and transmission lines
Our scope of work discussed in this letter report included probing and grouting
services for the reinforced concrete reservoir tank at an elevation of 618 feet MSL
only The reservoir tank has a diameter of approximately 74 feet with a finished floor
elevation of approximately 600 feet MSL as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The reservoir

tank will be supported by a perimeter wall footing and four interior column footings As
recommended in our geotechnical engineering report the tank footprint will need to be
probed and grouted as part of the foundation preparation The foundation probing and
grouting operation is an attempt to locate and grout potential subsurface cavities and or voids
beneath the tank foundations
The reinforced concrete reservoir tank foundation locations were surveyed
by the contractor and the probe hole locations were marked by our
field engineer prior to the probing operations The probe holes were marked along the
perimeter footings spaced at about 10 foot on center and at the center of the four interior
column locations Five additional probe holes were marked within the interior reservoir tank
footprint At the time of our probing and grouting operations the existing ground surface was
at the level of the top of the structural fill material for the reservoir tank foundations The
approximate locations of the probe holes for the reservoir tank are shown on the Probe Hole
Location Layout Plate 3
The probing and grouting operations were performed by Isemoto Contracting
Company The probe holes were drilled with a track mounted drill rig equipped with an
air track hammer The probe holes drilled were about 3 inches in diameter and extended to
about 11 feet below the existing ground surface or at least 10 feet below the bottom of the
foundation elevation A total of 33 probe holes were drilled for the reservoir tank as shown
on Plate 3
Materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of gray
medium hard to hard basalt formations interbedded with layers of reddish brown clinker
material Based on our observations of the probing operations small cavities and or voids
were encountered in 11 of the 33 probe holes drilled A summary of our foundation probe
drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plate 4
Following the probing operations each probe hole was fitted with a 2 5 inch diameter
PVC pipe to maintain the integrity of the open hole After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled with grout by injecting grout into the probe holes at
an average rate of about 0 0035 cubic yards per minute The grout used to fill the probe
holes consisted of 2540D grout mix which has a minimum unconfined compressive strength
of 2 500 psi The 2540D grout was supplied by West Hawaii Concrete A total of 1 2 cubic
yards of grout was used to fill the 33 probe holes and minor cavities encountered The
maximum grout intake was located at Probe Hole No 29 where voids were encountered
between about 4 and 6 feet below the surface
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical engineering
exploration report Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the cavities
encountered at the reservoir tank location have been grouted and that the subgrade area
should provide the recommended foundation support for the reservoir tank structure
