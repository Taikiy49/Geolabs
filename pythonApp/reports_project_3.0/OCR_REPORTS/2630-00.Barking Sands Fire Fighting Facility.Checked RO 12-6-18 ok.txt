PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to explore the subsurface soil conditions at the
proposed site to provide soil data as to the suitability of the site for the disposal of wash
water
The scope of our exploration which was performed in accordance with our revised
proposal of included logging and sampling of two 2 backhoe
excavated test pits laboratory soil testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and
laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of the field exploration our field and laboratory testing and
test pit logs are presented in the appendices The approximate locations of the test pits
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Fire Fighting Facility will be located on the grass covered open field bounded
by Palai Olani and Kukui roads This area is north of the existing airfield and east of the
Tank Farm at the Pacific Missile Range Facility in Barking Sands on the island of Kauai
Hawaii
The site is an open and unoccupied rectangular field which is large enough to
house the approximately 360 by 360 foot proposed facility The ground surface at the site
slopes very gently from the southwest to the northeast with a mean elevation of about
eight 8 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Ground cover consists of low cut grass
overlying fine to medium beach sand with a thin topsoil mantle of sandy silt

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Percolation Tests
It is our understanding that the runoff and wash water from training will be diverted
to a central drain and transported to a shallow leaching field for percolation into the
subsurface Two 2 percolation tests were performed in the low area where the leaching
field will be located to determine the permeability of the subsurface soil Detailed
information on percolation test set up and results are presented in Appendix C
Based on the test results it appears that the near surface sandy soil at the low
area has a permeability of about 5 2 x 10 feet per minute and may dissipate clean water
at an average rate of 1 5 cubic feet per day per square foot drainage area under an initial
4 5 foot head It should be noted that siltation of the surface soils will greatly reduce

these percolation rates therefore periodic maintenance is essential to maintain good
percolation rates in the soils
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the leaching field excavations be observed by the soil
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soil engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If the conditions encountered during construction are different from those
presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating two 2 test pits
to depths of 3 5 and 7 5 feet below the existing ground surface with a backhoe The test
pits were logged and representative samples taken before performing two 2 percolation
tests utilizing the test pits The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the test pits were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the excavation
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the United Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented in the Test
Pit Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
