INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Connector Road from the proposed Kauai Wal Mart store to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 2
Ahukini Road located in Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design input of
pavement and site grading and may not be appropriate to form the basis for the
preparation of construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed connector road project is adjacent to the proposed Wal Mart store
located on the north side of the proposed project site It is proposed to construct an
access road from the southern edge of the proposed Wal Mart store to the existing
Ahukini Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We understand that the proposed
connector road will be approximately 360 feet long and about 30 feet wide
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report was
prepared However we understand that the finished elevation for the proposed connector
road will be near the existing ground surface of approximately 220 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL From the site topographic map provided to us minor cuts and fills on the order
of 3 feet is expected relative to the existing ground surface to construct the proposed
connector road
Construction of the connector road will require that the existing irrigation ditch
where it crosses the connector road be replaced by a siphon pipe for drainage purposes
The siphon pipe will need to be embedded deep enough to facilitate construction of the
new pavement section of the proposed connector road

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the location of the proposed connector road The subsurface information
obtained was used to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to pavement design and site grading for the
proposed connector road
Our work was done in general accordance with our fee proposal dated September
17 1993 The scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment and
operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings at the proposed project site to a
depth of approximately 11 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
geologist of our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the
materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop geotech
nical engineering recommendations pertinent to pavement design and site
grading for the proposed connector road project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 4
6 Coordination of our work on the project by a Project Manager of our firm
7 Quality assurance of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing clerical
support and reproduction
9 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and logs of boring are presented in
Appendix A of this report Results of the laboratory tests are presented in Appendix B
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed connector road project is located to the west of Lihue Airport on the
northwest side of Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii The site for the proposed
connector road is bounded to the north by the proposed Wal Mart store to the east by
open fields to the south by Ahukini Road and to the west by Kuhio Highway
An existing irrigation ditch which is constructed of an earth berm of about 3 to
4 feet high traverses the site from the east to the west as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The irrigation ditch is about 5 feet wide and about 3 feet deep Since the top
of the irrigation ditch is built up above the existing ground the invert of the irrigation ditch
is near the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 5
Based on our field exploration and the topographic map entitled Topographic
Survey of KCOM WAL MART Development by Esaki Surveying and Mapping Inc dated
the topography of the site is relatively level with existing ground
surface elevations of approximately 216 to 223 feet MSL The approximate limits of
the proposed connector road project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration consisting of drilling and
sampling three borings to a depth of about 11 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered very stiff to hard silty and clayey residual
soils to a depth of about 11 feet below the existing ground surface We encountered
a near surface layer of loose to medium dense gravel fill in Boring No 2
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater depths are subject to change due to
rainfall time of year and tidal fluctuations
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the three borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 3 are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
of Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are
presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain by
a layer of very stiff to hard clayey and silty residual soils to the maximum depth drilled of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 6
about 111 feet below the ground surface Laboratory tests performed on the near
surface clayey soils indicate that the material generally has a moderate shrink swell
potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the pavement structures to
reduce the swelling potential of the near surface soils
We anticipate that the irrigation ditch and the associated earth berm where it
traverses the connector road will be removed to facilitate construction of the proposed
road Soft and or wet soils at the bottom of the existing irrigation ditch should be over
excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled with compacted engineered fill Proper
backfilling of the sides of the siphon pipe will be necessary to provide a firm subgrade on
which to construct the pavement section A minimum cover of at least 6 inches should
be provided between the top of the new siphon pipe and the bottom of the base course
layer of the pavement section
Detailed discussions and recommendations for pavement site grading and other
geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of this report
Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete and or portland cement concrete
pavements may be used for the proposed connector road construction We anticipate
that the vehicle loading for the proposed connector will consist primarily of passenger
vehicles and delivery trucks similar to the proposed Wal Mart store requirements
We have made our preliminary pavement designs assuming the pavement
subgrade soil will consist of the near surface clayey and silty residual soils as described
in the logs of borings If site grading exposes soil other than those assumed we should
perform additional tests to confirm or revise the recommended pavement sections for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 7
actual field conditions If imported fills are used for the proposed road construction the
subgrade on which the pavement structural section is to be placed should have a
minimum California Bearing Ratio CBR of 12 Based on the above assumptions we
recommend the following Asphalt Concrete and Portland Cement Concrete pavement
sections be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavements
3 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
8 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
11 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on moist compacted subgrade
Rigid Pavements
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on moist compacted subgrade
The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere during
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 8
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches
into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the recommended design sections The
recommended pavement sections assume that good drainage will be provided for areas
adjacent to the pavements
If rigid pavements will be used we recommend that pavement joints and joint
sealants be provided Joint sealants should be used to provide a seal to reduce potential
infiltration of water and to provide a compressible joint
Site Gradin
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 3 feet relative to the
existing ground surface would be required to construct the proposed connector road
Deeper excavations may be needed to install the siphon pipe that is planned to replace
the portion of the irrigation ditch traversing the proposed connector road
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub spoil material and
demolition debris should be disposed of off site The near surface gravel fill layer of about
8 inches thick encountered in Boring No 2 should be reworked as engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 9
If zones of soft or wet soils are encountered at the excavated subgrade levels
over excavation may be required to expose firm soils This should be evaluated by
Geolabs Hawaii in the field Contract documents should include additive and deductive
unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill construction to account for variations
in the over excavation quantities
The exposed surface following stripping and or over excavation should be scarified
to a depth of at least 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The on site materials may be reused as fill materials provided that they contain no
lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in greatest dimension It should be noted that
because of the moderate expansion potential of the on site soils the on site soils should
be thoroughly moisture conditioned prior to use as fill materials Fills and backfills below
pavement areas should be moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in thin lifts normally 6 to 8 inches in loose lift
thickness and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Any imported soil should be select fill Select fill should contain no lumps
fragments or rocks greater than 3 inches in largest dimension with less than 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve In addition select fill should also have a CBR value of at least
12 Fill materials should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to
the job site Imported soils having low expansion potential should be brought to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 10
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth unyielding subgrades and
should be kept moist until covered by concrete slabs or pavements
Siphon Pipe
We understand that the portion of the existing irrigation ditch and associated earth
berm located across the proposed connector road will be removed We anticipate that
a siphon pipe on the order of 48 inches in diameter will be installed across the proposed
connector road for drainage purposes Therefore after removal of the irrigation ditch any
soft and or wet soils at the sides and bottom of the ditch should be over excavated to
expose firm soils The over excavated materials should not be used as on site fill
materials and should be disposed of off site The resulting excavation should be properly
backfilled with engineered fill
For the siphon bedding we recommend a minimum of 6 inches of compacted
granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel or select borrow be provided below the
siphon pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond the sides of the siphon pipe
a minimum width of one fourth 4 the outside diameter We recommend that the
granular bedding material extend to at least 12 inches above the top of the siphon We
understand that the top of the siphon pipe may be very close to the bottom of the base
course layer of the new pavement section Therefore we recommend that a minimum
cover of at least 6 inches of the granular bedding material be provided between the top
of the new siphon pipe and the bottom of the base course layer of the pavement section
Due to the large diameter of the siphon pipe proper compaction of the backfill
above and around the siphon pipe is necessary to provide a firm subgrade on which to
construct the pavement section The backfill above and around the siphon pipe should
consist of select granular fill material The backfill materials should be placed in thin lifts
and moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 10 Page 11
less than 95 percent relative compaction The on site clayey and silty soils are not
recommended for backfill around and above the siphon pipe
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This
review is necessary to evaluate adherence to the intent of the foundation and earthwork
recommendations provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the subgrade preparation
and excavation and backfill of the existing irrigation ditch This is to observe compliance
with the intent of the design concepts specifications and or recommendations and to
allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated
during design The recommendations presented in this report are contingent upon the
above observations
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
Any imported material required should be non expansive and should be tested and
approved by our office prior to delivery to the project site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been estimated by taping
from visible features shown on the Conceptual Site Plan prepared by Austin Tsutsumi
Associates Inc dated Elevations of the borings were interpolated
between the contours shown on the topographic map entitled Topographic Survey of
KCOM Wal Mart Development prepared by Esaki Surveying Mapping Inc The
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used

APENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling three
borings to a depth of approximately 111 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted rig advancing continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the
borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
