PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A potential borrow site for fill material to be used for the Leihano and Kapolei BMX
Projects is between Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kalaeloa Harbor in Kapolei on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The borrow site is about 300 feet by 800 feet in plan dimension and is in a
future drain channel site We understand that the bottom of the future channel will be
about 30 to 40 feet from the existing ground surface In addition we understand that a
portion of the borrow site will be used by the new COSTCO development

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late
Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau Volcano reached the
end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
that were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and further north these alluvial
sediments were interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef formations to form
a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from zero feet in the area of H 1 freeway to
over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths of about 40 5 to 42 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer about 0 to 4 5 feet
thick The surface fill layer generally consisted of soft to medium stiff sandy silt and very
stiff clayey silt underlain by coralline deposits to about 29 0 to 40 8 foot depths The
coralline deposits generally consisted of very soft to stiff sandy clay very loose to dense
sands and gravels soft clayey silt with sand and gravel soft to hard coral formation and
hard sandstone formation In two of the borings an alluvial deposit was encountered
below the coralline deposits extending to the maximum depth drilled of about 42 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The alluvium was comprised of medium stiff clay and
silty clay
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate depending on
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1
through 4 2 in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil
samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed borrow excavation site is underlain by surface fill coralline deposits
and alluvial deposits We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our
field exploration Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the majority of the
materials within the borrow excavation site is suitable for use as non expansive capping fill
material
Surface fill material consisting of sandy silt may be considered suitable for
non expansive capping fill material In addition coralline deposits consisting of sands and
gravels sandy clay coral and sandstone formation may be used as capping fill material
The coral and sandstone fragments should be reduced to less than 3 inches in largest
dimension It is estimated that about 75 percent of the excavated material from the
proposed borrow site may be used as non expansive capping fill material

Based on the test results it is our opinion that the clayey silt soils within the surface
fill and coralline deposits are suitable for general fill material but are not suitable for
capping fill material It is estimated that about 15 percent of the excavated material from
the proposed borrow site may be used as general fill material
Two of the borings encountered highly expansive alluvial soils from about 29 0 to
37 0 foot depths The alluvial deposits consisted of clay and silty clay soils The highly
expansive alluvial deposits are unsuitable for use as general fill material Therefore these
highly expansive soils should not be use as fill for the two projects
We performed laboratory testing on the recovered core samples from the coral and
sandstone formations encountered in the borings The unconfined compression strength of
the coral and sandstone samples ranged from about 280 to 1 880 pounds per square inch
psi It is anticipated that the hard coral and sandstone formations may be excavated with
normal heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the
material is near the existing ground surface

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths of about 40 5 to 42 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with coring and auger
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Boring Plates A 1 1 to 4 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
O D standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive
are shown on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of coral materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a
hollow bit to cut into the coralline formation The coralline material left in the hollow core of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from
a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50 It
should be noted that RQD does not apply to coral material However the RQD values of
the coral material are included in the boring logs for rough characterization of the coral
material

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 was performed on selected soil samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were run on
remolded samples Swell test results are presented on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are presented on the Log
of Boring at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test results is
provided on Plate B 2
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soil and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the engineering
characteristics of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture
content of the soils Compaction of the samples was performed by dropping a 10 pound
hammer from a height of 18 inches The samples were compacted in five equal layers with
each layer receiving 56 blows of energy The samples were soaked in water prior to the
penetration test The test results are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
Four unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on coral and
sandstone core samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the
underlying coral and sandstone formation The test results are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depth
