Preliminary Evaluation and Recommendations
Temporary Bypass Crossing
Rehabilitation of 24 Inch Sewer Force Main
From Fort Shafter to Sand Island WWTP
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have recently
completed the portion of field exploration work pertaining to the temporary bypass
crossing on the Fort Shafter side of Moanalua Stream for the above referenced project
This letter summarizes our work performed thus far on the project and presents our
preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation and recommendations derived from our
analyses for the subject project
Project Considerations
Based on the information provided the existing 24 inch cast iron force main starts
at the Fort Shafter Sewage Pump Station Bldg 1605 and connects to the City and
County of Honolulu s Sand Island Wastewater Treatment Plant WWTP on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The force main is approximately 13 850 feet long between the sewage
pump station at Fort Shafter and the Hart Street junction box at Sand Island WWTP
The 24 inch force main was originally constructed in 1945 by the U S Army and
was subsequently modified by relocation and or replacement of the original line due to
line breakage Due to the deteriorated condition as the force main approached the end of
its useful life expectancy studies were conducted in 1975 1984 and 1996 to evaluate
the existing condition of the force main and to recommend various remedial alternatives
for implementation

Because of the various construction constraints associated with open trench
construction methods such as subsurface utility conflicts relocation surface disturbance
to affected property owners and traveled roadways shoring support dewatering
requirements and handling of potentially contaminated soils etc we understand that
two trenchless alternatives are being considered for the rehabilitation of the existing force
main including Cured In Place Pipe CIPP and Insertion Sliplining method
Both methods will require full bypass of the existing system during the rehabilitation
process of the force main therefore a temporary bypass crossing over Moanalua Stream
will need to be constructed Based on the information provided by the project Structural
Engineer each end of the bypass crossing will be subjected to a vertical load of 90 kips
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions on the Fort Shafter side of the temporary bypass
crossing were explored by drilling one boring extending to a depth of about 95 feet below
the existing ground surface Our boring generally encountered a thin surficial fill crust of
approximately 5 feet thick The surficial fill crust was underlain by soft and highly
compressible lagoonal deposit consisting of gray clayey silts and silty clays extending to
a depth of approximately 90 feet below the existing ground surface High penetration
resistance was then encountered and extended to a depth of about 95 feet below the
ground surface the maximum depth explored Groundwater level was encountered at a
depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Discussions and Preliminary Recommendations
Based on our field exploration at the Fort Shafter side of the bypass crossing thick
compressible deposits consisting of soft lagoonal deposits were encountered to a depth
of about 90 feet below the surficial fill crust Because of the moderate to high structural
loads anticipated for the bypass crossing a shallow foundation system for support of the
proposed bypass crossing would be subjected to appreciable settlement on the order of
about 3 to 5 inches As a result driven piles are recommended for the foundation support
of the proposed bypass crossing on the Fort Shafter side of Moanalua Stream
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses we recommend that
12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles end bearing on the very stiff dense
materials encountered at greater depths be used to support the proposed new bypass
crossing An allowable compressive load capacity of about 45 tons with a preliminary pile
length of about 100 feet below the ground surface may be used for the recommended pile
foundation design
We believe that the piles may be installed without predrilling Because of the
potential variation of subsurface conditions such as the presence of boulders we

Pile Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the anticipated foundation
loads of about 90 kips we believe that driven pile foundations may be used for support of
the proposed bypass crossing Based on our engineering analyses we recommend that
12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles be used for the pile foundation design
The piles would derive support primarily from end bearing on the very stiff dense
materials underlying the site at a depth of about 90 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site an allowable
compressive load capacity of 45 tons based on a preliminary pile length of 100 feet below
the ground surface may be used for the design of the pile foundations The load capacity
is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads
such as wind or seismic forces Piles should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet
center to center to avoid reduction in capacity due to group action
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead load of the driven pile
and by shear along the pile surface and the adjacent soils The allowable uplift capacity
for the 12 inch square pile is 12 kips The uplift load capacity already includes the dead
weight of the piles
Due to the relatively long preliminary pile length of about 100 feet we anticipate
that splicing of two shorter pile sections would be required in order to facilitate the
handling and pile driving operations The upper pile section should consist of a minimum
50 foot section in order to provide the recommended uplift load capacity Splice cans
may be fabricated with inch thick high grade steel and should be at least 5 feet long
Where piles will be subjected to high tensile forces consideration should be given to
mechanical splicing of the piles to take advantage of the bending capacity and the full
uplift load capacity of the pile
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads imposed on the bypass crossing may be resisted by a combination of
the lateral load capacity of the driven piles and the passive pressure against the vertical
faces of the pile caps Passive earth pressure against the vertical faces of the pile cap
may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per
foot of depth pcf
