PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions
The scope of our work consisted of the following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site

locations along the Villages 5 and 6 Collector Roads and Utility Corridor A
These locations generally corresponded to the 300 foot spacing required in
the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1997

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
Our field work was performed in sunny conditions with high
temperature of approximately 87 F with generally dry surface condition
Twelve soil resistivity surveys were conducted at five locations at the above
referenced area using the Wenner four pin method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts voltage output and 24 watts power
output into the ground This method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to
evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of 3 to 6 feet depending on the depth of the water line from
the existing ground surface Among these four spacings used one spacing or two may
have more significance than the others for allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil
resistivity from the closest mass of soil to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings are
flagged with one asterisk on the attached plate
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them and the voltage drop between the

Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristic

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this report
Elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate having been
estimated from features and reference points shown on the construction plans prepared
by R M Towill Corporation dated All elevations were rounded to the
nearest foot to emphasize that they may not be accurate enough for other purposes
Thus elevations and locations of the presented data should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
