SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The current and previous field explorations disclosed competent
subsoils in the upper ridges of the site variable subsoils
consisting of soft to medium stiff recent alluvium in the smaller
central drainageway and marshy conditions consisting of soft
subsoils and high water level in the larger drainageway or stream
area Some ground settlements are anticipated due to the weight of
the new fill in the drainageways
Conventional shallow foundations are recommended for all buildings
at the site Building construction in the lower drainageway areas
should be delayed until the settlement gauges and piezometers
indicate that the settlement rates have reduced sufficiently
Special stabilization procedures are required in the lower
southeastern portion of the site where marshy soil conditions were
encountered
The text of this report should be referred to for special detailed
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of twenty five 25 two story wood
framed townhouse structures approximately 45 by 70 feet in plan The
proposed structures will be situated over the whole site along both sides
of a central access road and parking It is our understanding that the
townhouse adjacent to the southeastern property limit may be eliminated
and replaced by a recreational park
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1685 00 Page 2
The site grading for the proposed development scheme involves up to
about 20 feet of cut and fill Most of the site cutting will be in the
upper ridge area located in the southwestern and southern portions of the
site while most of the fill will be placed in the lowlying central
drainageway and the southeastern stream area
A new sewer line will be constructed to replace the existing 8 inch
sewer This new sewer will be connected to the existing 12 inch sewer
which runs along the stream and will be maintained
The existing larger stream area will be filled and the running
water will be re routed through a buried culvert crossing the southeastern
portion of the site
A retaining wall up to about 10 feet high is planned along the
southeastern boundary of the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and groundwater conditions at the site to provide recommendations for the
design of the site grading building foundations the culvert structure
and the retaining wall foundations An investigation was performed by our
firm for a previous development scheme at the site However the previous
investigation concentrated on the upper ridge areas and stream where the
units were originally planned The current investigation was concentrated
on part of the two drainageway areas and the southeastern boundary of the
site where our previous explorations did not fully explore

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development is located adjacent to Haiku Road and
Emepela Place in Kaneohe The present topography at the site consists of
two gently sloping ridges higher areas and two valleys lower
drainageways
The first higher ridge is located at the northwestern corner of the
site next to Haiku Road and is heavily vegetated with grass and trees
Ground elevations in this area vary from about 150 to 160 feet above MSL
Some surface rubbish fill was also noted in this area
Adjacent to the south is a smaller drainageway running in a west to
east direction through the central portion of the site The bottom of the
drainageway is gently sloping with elevations ranging from about 130 to
112 feet The side slopes of the drainageway are relatively steep with an
GEOLABS HAWAII
average slope of about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical The bottom of the
drainageway is covered with trees An area of loose cast over fill and
rubbish was noted along the northwestern portion of the drainageway side
slopes This fill included concrete and asphalt rubble abandoned cars
and miscellaneous junk The approximate location of this rubbish fill is
outlined on the Site Plan Plate 2
The second higher ridge area is located adjacent to the central
drainageway in the southern portion of the site This area slopes down
gently towards the east with elevations ranging from about 160 to 135
feet
A larger drainageway stream is located along the southeastern
portion of the site and joins the central drainageway Marshy wet ground
conditions including heavy vegetation and high water condition were noted
in this drainageway Elevations in this area varied from about 105 to 110
feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site of the development is located roughly midway between the
Pali and the coast on the windward slope of the Koolau Range This area
is part of a deeply eroded and weathered section typified by thick
alluvial deposits overlying volcanic dike complex The extended period of
erosion has also resulted in deposition of quiet water recent alluvium in
valleys and drainageways including localized deposits of organic silt
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings from the previous and current field
explorations and site reconnaissance the following three major soil
conditions were encountered at the sites

conditions at the upper valley area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The upper ridge areas are generally underlain by competent subsoils
therefore normal site grading methods and conventional spread or
continuous footing foundations are recommended for buildings in these
areas
The drainageway in the central portion of the site is underlain by
variable medium stiff to soft subsoils Settlement gauges are
recommended in this area to monitor the anticipated ground settlement due
to the weight of the proposed fill up to 20 feet

Building and utility construction should be delayed until tolerable
settlement rates are achieved Therefore the existing sewer needs to be
maintained until the new sewer is installed Surcharging can be used to
decrease the waiting period in some building areas if required
During site grading drainage from Emepela Place needs to be
diverted from the central drainageway to facilitate earthwork operations
Soft subsoils and high water table were encountered in the large
marshy drainageway in the southeastern portion of the site Special
stabilization methods will be required for filling this lowlying portion

is recommended to decrease the settlement waiting time and reduce future
differential settlements of the proposed structures

Ground Preparation of Lower Marshy Area Stream
The presence of the soft subsoils and existing stream makes the
stabilization and filling of this area complex Several
alternatives including complete removal and replacement of the soft
soils with gravel and the use of a surcharge fill over the existing
soft soils were considered Following discussion with the design
team it was decided that a scheme involving partial removal of the
soft subsoils only under the new pipe culverts and future
buildings would be a desireable alternative With this scheme the
settlements under the pipe culverts and buildings would be minimized
while the soft soil removal cost reduced and the need to temporarily
relocated the stream away from the marsh area during filling and
surcharging could be eliminated Conceptualy the procedure would
be as follows
a Divert as much of the stream flow as possible to the southern
edge of the marsh area
b Start stabilization and fill 2 3 as described below from
the northern edge of marsh over the proposed culvert location
c Exeavate the soft soil under the culverts and replace them with
gravel see Plate 3 This should be done in small sections to
minimize instability
d Install culverts and headwalls then divert stream into
culverts
Stabilize and fill the remaining marshy area
GEOLABS HAWAI
Page 9
It is estimated that the undercutting of the surface vegetation and
organic material and scrubbing will average six inches over the fill
portion of the site
The existing surface soil in the low areas is marshy and wet and
does not have sufficient strength to support heavy construction
equipment Therefore a working platform or drainage blanket should
be established prior to the major filling of the swamp area The
working platform should consist of a combination of a synthetic
filter fabric and a 12 inch gravel layer The filter fabric reduces
the required gravel thickness and eliminates the strict gradation
requirements of the drainage blanket since it acts as a filter
medium to prevent gravel loss into the soft ground and prevent the
mud and fine soil particles from contaminating the granular blanket
material The fabric also provides tensile reinforcement to the
gravel layer and permits earlier use of heavy equipment on a minimum
thickness of compacted aggregate see Plate 4
Fill Placement
After ground preparation fill over the drainageways should be
placed as soon as possible to allow the fills as much time as
practicable to settle during the site grading operations and
thereby reduce the post site grading waiting period for building
construction
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch lifts compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78
In the soft stream area the fills should be placed at a slow rate
of about l foot per day to minimize the possibility of slope
failure

Settlement
Significant ground settlements are anticipated from the proposed
site filling at the following site areas
Central Drainageway Based on our findings the estimated
settlements along the central drainageway area are generally on
the order of about 5 to 7 inches from west to east In the
Boring 6 area where a very soft pocket layer of clayey silt
with organic matter exists the settlements may increase up to
one foot The estimated settlements under the existing sewer
due to the site filling range from about 3 inches to 6 inches in
the same direction Smaller settlements are anticipated for the
remaining thinner fill areas in the eastern end of the site
The time required for the estimated settlements to occur is very
difficult to predict and may vary due to the various
consistencies of the subsoils It is estimated that without a
surcharge fill a 3 to 6é month period may be required for 90 of
the total settlement to occur in this general area and a
lé month period for the Boring 6 area in particular

Lower Marshy Area The soft marshy soils along the larger
stream area are swampy and highly compressible Where the
soft soils are not removed and the area filled with the
proposed 9 feet high fill the estimated settlements are on
the order of about 15 to 18 inches
The time required for the estimate settlements to occur is
again very difficult to predict However it is estimated
that without a surcharge fill a 7 month period may be
required for 90 of the total settlements to occur

Building Foundations
The proposed wood framed townhouse structures may be supported by
conventional spread or continuous footing foundations
The footings should extend a minimum of 18 inches below the finish
grades Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition may further
increase the embedment of the footing foundation Foundations located
close to the top of slopes should be embedded an additional depth to
obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the out
edge of footing out to the slope face
The foundations can be designed using the following allowable
bearing values
Very stiff natural soil upper ridges up to 3500 p s f
Compacted fill 2500 p s f
The above values of bearing capacity are for total dead plus live
loads only and may be increased by one third when temporary lateral loads
wind and seismic are considered

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling ten
10 borings to depths of 15 0 to 31 0 feet below the existing ground
surface A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A 1 thru A 10
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or a 2 5 inch diameter thin wall
tube hydraulically pushed The blow counts or the hydraulic pressure to
drive the sampler the last 12 or 24 inches are shown on the Log of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths

Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on select samples as an aid in the evaluation and
classification of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Two direct shear were performed on selected undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsoils These test results
are plotted on Plates B 1 and B 2 Two unconsolidated undrained triaxial
tests were performed on selected undisturbed samples to evaluate their
undrained shear strength The test results are plotted on Plates B 3 and
Bm4
Three consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the consolidation characteristics of the soft recent alluvium
materials The consolidation curves are presented on Plates B 5 thru B 7
