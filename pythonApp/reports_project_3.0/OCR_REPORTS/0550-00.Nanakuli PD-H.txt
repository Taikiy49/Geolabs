Subject Progress Report on Percolation Tests

Gentlemen
This progress report covers the drilling and testing of a new injection
well to date Four previously drilled injection wells were located
re drilled to their previous bottom levels and tested to measure the
present percolation rates
A new injection well referred to as Boring 2 was drilled to a depth
of 55 feet with 6 diameter auger The upper 40 feet was cased with a
4 PVC pipe The uncased section extended from 40 to 55 feet below the
ground surface A percolation test indicated a rate of approximately 60
GPM with the water level raising to 2 feet below the existing ground
surface
After the four existing wells referred to as Boring and 6 were
located a new 5 feet long PVC pipe was used to extend the pipes up
to the present graded surfaces Because of the caving materials in
the wells they required re drilling and cleaning to their original
drilled depths Percolation tests indicated rates in excess of 60 GPM
for Boring 4 5 and 6 Boring 1 reached its maximum intake at 60 GPM
Based on the results of the present drilling and percolation testings
we would recommend extending Boring 2 to 100 feet The purpose is to
increase the percolation rate by increasing the surface area and to
possibly locate a more pervious zones below The additional subsur
face information will enable us to determine if deeper injection wells
would be required for the anticipated sewage volumes

This report covers the preliminary test data of the six injection
wells to date Four previously drilled injection wells were located
and re drilled Two new injection wells referred to as Boring 2
and Boring 3 Site Plan Plate 1 were drilled to a depth of 100
feet and 52 feet respectively with 6 diameter auger The upper
40 feet of Boring 2 was cased with a 4 PVC pipe Boring 3 is not
cased at this time
The first injection test was performed on and
thereafter All borings were tested by pumping water in excess
of 60 gpm for 30 minutes recovery of water was instantaneous in
each case The test on October 2nd was performed with a 3 Gorman
Rupp Centrifugal Pump The pump is certified from 18MT Contractor
Pump Bureau To measure the flow rate a Trident Crest 3 high
duty meter serial number 3844119 was rented from Board of Water
Supply The time of the test was 7 30 a m and the static water
level was about 9 5 feet which is about 1 5 feet above the sea
level Dillingham Tide Chart Pump rate of 140 gpm was registered
by the meter All borings were tested individually for 30 minutes
with no overflowing and recovery of water level was instantaneous
Based on the results of these injection tests we would like to
schedule final testing with you in accordance with the State Health
Department regulations Tentatively we have set December 4th as
