In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for
completion of site grading for the proposed Konohiki Estates Subdivision
development The location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The scope of our investigation included the excavation of ten test
pits visual observations of the existing site conditions Laboratory
testing and engineering analysis
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests which
were used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed development consists of 21 residential lots with the
necessary access roads It is our understanding that the site was
graded about two years ago generally according to the proposed grading
plan but without engineering inspection and compaction testing The
Site grading involved cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet high
It appears that the lot grading in the southerly and easterly portions
of the site had not been completed at the time of our field exploration
Therefore additional grading is planned for these areas
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located north of Kaunualii Highway in Lawai
on the Island of Kauai
The ground surface at the site slopes gently with drainage
generally towards the south Kaumualii Highway The ground surface
elevations vary from approximately 630 to 540 feet The area is
presently vacant and sparsely covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The island of Kauai was built up by a single large shield volcano
during the Tertiary Period Near the end of this Period this main
shield building volcanic phase came to a halt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1408 00 Page 3
Following a long period of erosion and subsidence volcanism was
renewed during the Pleistocene epoch producing the Koloa Volcanic
Series These post erosional volcanics laid a veneer of fresh lavas
over the older volcanics
After the cessation of this renewed volcanism the geologic forces
of weathering erosion and deposition during the late Pleistocene and
the recent epochs have shaped the island to its present form
At the site the lava rocks of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic
Series have been deeply weathered to form a mantle of stiff reddish
brown residual silty clay soils
SOIL CONDITIONS
The soils at the site can generally be grouped into three major
catagories fairly loose fills moderately compacted fills and stiff
natural soils
Approximately 4 to 9 feet of loose fill materials were encountered
in Test Pits 1 2 and 3 consisting of on site clayey silt clays in the
north central portion Lots 5 and 6 of the site The soil samples and
field density tests obtained from these test pits indicated a very low
percent of compaction in the range of 61 to 83
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1408 00 Page 4
Moderately compacted fills up to 10 feet thick were encountered
in Test Pits 6 and 10 The density tests indicate 85 to 93 compaction
in these areas
Underlying the surface fills and in the cut areas the dominant
natural soil consisted of very stiff mottled brown to reddish brown
silty clay saprolite after basalt
Some loose soils were stockpiled in the vicinity of Lots 20 and 21
during our site investigation
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the fill placed in the gully
area along the eastern portion of the site was poorly compacted This
fill area should be reworked and recompacted
The fill areas encountered outside this area may generally provide
adequate support for the proposed improvements with minor site
preparation
Site Preparation Gully Area
The 111 in the north central gully area was apparently placed
upon the underlying soil without the necessary engineering controls to
assure a Stable fill Also it appears that no provision has been made
for surface drainage in this gully Ideally the loose fill should be
entirely removed and recompacted to the recommended densities However
in order to reduce costs it is our opinion that if this filled area is
undercut a minimum of three feet below the existing surface and
backfilled with compacted on site fill satisfactory support for the
proposed residences could be obtained
The undercut surface should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density The backfill should be placed in 9 inch
maximum loose horizontal lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum of 853
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
A subdrain should be placed along the bottom of the gullies
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1408 00 Page 6
General Site Preparation
While the test pits indicated that the other filled areas are
generally competent they were apparently placed without sufficient
engineering control to assure uniformity It is therefore recommended
that the proposed lots and roadway areas be proof rolled with a heavy
roller to delineate areas of uncompacted or unsuitable support
The proof rolling should be monitored by a soils engineer who would
delineate the areas of unsuitable subgrade support These areas if
encountered should be undercut to remove the unsuitable materials to a
maximum depth of two feet and replaced with compacted on site fill as
previously specified
Periodic in place density determinations should be made to assure
that the subgrade has been uniformly compacted to a minimum of 85 of its
maximum density
Cut areas should be proof rolled and compacted to 85 dry density
as specified above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1408 00 Page 7
In addition the existing piles of loose fills should be removed
and the underlying original ground stripped of organic or loose soil down
to firm soil It was noted during our recent site visit that grass and
bushes were located in some of the proposed fill areas The limits of
these proposed fill areas should be cleared and grubbed
Any other localized soft or loose fill area encountered during
clearing and grubbing operations should be excavated and backfilled with
compacted select material
Filling and Compaction Requirements
Aan thg and Mompaction Requirements
After the above site preparation additional fill should be
moistened to optimum moisture content placed in 9 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 853 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have low to
moderate swelling potential and therefore the material may be utilized
for the fill
Where it is necessary to place fill over existing slopes steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 keying and benching operation is
critical and required to properly bond the new fill to the existing slope
surface The filling operation should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above
filling requirements

Slopes
Fill or cut slopes should not be steeper than 2 1 H V The
on site soil is primarily silty with some sand or clay and easily
erodable Therefore the slope should be protected from run off water
to reduce soil erosion Interceptor ditches or swales should be
provided at the top of the slope to direct water away from the slope
To assure adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back to
the design grade

The recommended section also considers medium traffic and that

House Foundations
For the light residential structures spread footing foundations
or slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects of Slope creep
and erosion slabs should be set back a minimum distance of 6 feet away
from the tops of cut or fill slopes Post footings located close to the
tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 6 feet away from the slope face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
2500 p s f may be used for footings resting on stiff in situ soil or
compacted Fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
settlement of the backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1408 00 Page 10
Slabs on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted
granular cushion and capillary break such as 3 Fine rock The
thickened edge of the slabs should extend a minimum of 10 inches below
the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
waterproof membrane should be incorporated beneath the slab A one inch
layer of well graded sand should be placed over the membrane to protect
it and to aid in the curing of the concrete
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not allowed to
dry out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to
minimize ground settlement along the utility alignments On site
material generally less than 6 inches in size may be utilized as
backfill All backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of the
on site material is fine grained and would not be suitable for
compaction by water jetting or ponding alone and therefore would
require mechanical compaction

Cesspools
A cesspool was marked on the grading plan between Lots 18 and 19
This and other cesspools noted on the site should be identified and
checked to see if the cesspool was backfilled properly after sludge and
other organic material was removed
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the previous site grading and the
undercutting proof rolling and backfilling of the fill area be
inspected to assure adequate compaction of the in place materials and to
assure that the recommended compaction level is consistently achieved
It is recommended that the backfilling compaction be inspected to
assure that the recommendations are correctly implemented
The approximate extent of the loose fill area which was outlined
on the Site Plan is based upon the information obtained from the
grading plan and our subsurface explorations This estimated extent
should be utilized for estimating purposes the actual extent must be
established during the construction operations
