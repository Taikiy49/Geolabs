The present grading scheme is to level off the split level

portion of the lots and cutting down about 3 feet of the

anticipated

The elevations of the upper portion of the lots range from
79 to 86 and the elevations of the lower portion of the lots
range from 74 to 77 Between the upper and the lower areas
a two horizontal to one vertical slope bank runs parallel
to the street through the middle of all the lots
At the time of this subsurface exploration shrinkage cracks
were noted on the slope bank especially over the exposed
Adobe soil areas near Boring Nos 2 and 6
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Explorations
A total of nine short test borings were drilled at the upper
level portion of the lots varying from depths of 4 to 6 feet
below existing grades The purpose of this study is to explore
the near surface soil conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing Detailed information on field exploration
and laboratory testing is covered in the appendix along with
the boring logs and laboratory test results Locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 and 2
Soil Conditions
In general the upper portion of the 12 lot project site is
blanketed by a 3 foot thick capping fill layer consisting
of medium dense brown silty and gravelly sand over medium
stiff to stiff dark brown Adobe clay soil The thickness
of the non expansive capping material tapers off at both
ends of the site to about 1 foot thick near Boring Nos 1
6 7 locations

W 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The present scheme to re grade these 12 lots by cutting
2 to 3 feet from the upper area and filling up the lower
area will reduce the thickness of the existing capping
layer which is used to stabilize the underlying highly
expansive Adobe clay soil
To retain a 3 foot capping layer at the upper area over
excavation and backfilling with low expansive soil is
recommended in this area If expansive soil such as
Adobe soil tis used for filling the lower lot area
a minimum of 3 feet of capping material should be placed
above the expansive soil as in the upper lot area
Site Preparation
The site shall be cleared of all deleterious and organic
material
In area to receive fill the ground should be scarified a
minimum depth of 12 inches In area where desiccation
cracks extend to further depths scarification will have
to extend to the depth of the desiccation crack The
scarified surface should then be moistened to the wet
side of its optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum density of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 5
Fill Placement
After the above surface treatment fill should then be
placed in 9 inch maximum loose lifts and compacted to
90 of maximum density
Due to the expansive nature of the site clay soils they
should be compacted as wet of optimum moisture as is prac
tical for the equipment in use The moisture content of
the fill soils should be at least 2 above the optimum
moisture content during compaction
Where new fill are to be placed over the existing slope
bank area the fill material should be keyed and benched
into the firm soil as approved by the soils engineer
Fill slope of two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 ratio
may be used No expansive soil should be placed within
2 feet from the slope surface
It is our understanding that the new fill material will
come from Marina s Ridge The material up in the ridge
is generally rocky and has many boulders Rock over
8 inch in diameter should be broken down or disposed off
site All imported fill should be approved by the soils
engineer prior to be used in fill areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 6
If practical during excavation at the upper area the
on site soils should be stockpiled separately by soil
types for later use The sandy soil can be reused as
capping material over expansive soil
A minimum thickness of 3 feet of low expansive capping
material over expansive soil should be maintained at
the upper area The depth of over excavation for the
capping layer will be based on the final grading plan
and determined during grading operation
All grading operations should be under the direct and
continuous observation of the soils engineer
