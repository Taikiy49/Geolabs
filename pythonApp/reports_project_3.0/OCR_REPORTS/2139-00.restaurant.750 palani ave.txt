INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading and foundations
of the proposed restaurant building at 750 Palani Avenue in the Kapahulu
area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to obtain soils data for the formulation
of engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed building
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of August
30 1988
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of four 4
borings to depths ranging between 2 0 and 12 0 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were engineering evaluation of the field
data the development of design recommendations and report preparation
A detailed description of our field exploration is presented in the
appendix of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 750 Palani Avenue in the Kapahulu area
of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated on the northwestern side of Palani Avenue and is
currently occupied by three 3 residential type wooden frame structures
The site is nearly level with a slight slope towards the west
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a restaurant on the site The proposed
restaurant will be a single story structure with slab on grade floors and
a basement kitchen It is anticipated that floor and column loads will be

The current site grading scheme is to raise the grade of the building

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain which
consists of a thick horizontal series of interbedded marine sediments and
volcanic deposits capping Koolau Basalts The near surface structure at
the site consists of elevated Pleistocene coral reef deposits of variable
consistency and composition At relatively shallow depths below the reef
deposits the Kaimuki member basalt of the Honolulu Volcanic Series was
encountered
At the project site these reef deposits are overlain by a thin layer
of man made fill from grading during the urbanization of the area
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 2 0 and
12 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered thin surface layers of fill
composed of stiff brown clayey silts with gravel varying between 0 5 and
1 5 feet in thickness Below the fill the borings encountered medium
hard coral algal limestone coral formation Boring 1 which extended
through the coral formation did encounter the dense Kaimuki Basalt at a
depth of about 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface

Groundwater was encountered in Boring 1 at the depth of 8 0 feet
below the existing ground surface in an uncased drill hole Due to the
nature of the formation encountered and the relative proximity to the
ocean it is anticipated that the water levels may fluctuate in response
to periods of heavy precipitation tidal influences and storm surge
conditions
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical point of view provided that our recommendations are
followed We recommend that spread or strip footings bear on the near
surface coral formation or on engineered fill compacted in accordance with
the recommendations given below The kitchen basement wall footings are
anticipated to bear in the underlying dense basalt rock encountered at
about 6 5 feet below ground surface
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that
approximately 2 5 to 3 feet of fill will be required in the rear portion
of the lot to raise the grade around the proposed building
The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading
1 Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The
resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be over excavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2139 00 Page 5
2 Imported fill material if required should consist of
non expansive select granular borrow fill such as crusher waste
select borrow coral or equivalent
3 All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding eight 8 to nine 9 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry density
as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Basement Excavation
Based on our field exploration it is our opinion that the basement
may generally be excavated by conventional methods However some hard
excavation may be encountered within the coral limestone or basalt rock
and these may require the use of special excavating equipment such as
hoerams
Shoring for the basement excavation should be at the discretion of the
contractor and should conform to the appropriate safety and grading
ordinances and regulations
Foundation Support
In order to provide adequate embedment and support for the proposed
perimeter retaining walls we recommend that the strip footings bear on
in situ coral formation For the one story structure spread and line
footings may bear on either the compacted engineered fill or the
underlying coral formation The basement wall footings are antcipated to
bear on the underlying basalt
For the design of foundations bearing on either the in situ coral or
underlying basalt rock an allowable bearing capacity up to 8000 p s f
may be utilized

For the design of foundations bearing on structural fill compacted in
accordance with the recommendations presented herein an allowable bearing
capacity of 2500 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Perimeter boundary retaining wall foundations should be embedded a
minimum of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent outside grade Interior
footings should be embedded a minimm of eighteen 18 inches below the
slab finish grade

Settlements
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on engineered fill
constructed in accordance with the above recommendations are believed to
be less than one half inch total and one quarter of an inch differential
For foundations bearing on the underlying dense coral or basalt formation
minimal settlements are anticipated
Cavity Probi
Due to the variable nature of coral formations and the common
occurrence of voids and or cavities in such formations we recommend that
a foundation subgrade probing program be implemented Each foundation
column location and on 10 foot centers in strip footings should be
probed to a minimum depth of five 5 feet or to basalt whichever is
less with drilled probes at least three 3 inches in diameter The
resulting probe holes should be backfilled by the injection of neat cement
grout under moderate pressure to aid in the detection of any cavities
which were not directly noted in the probe drilling The probing and
grouting should be performed prior to site filling and footing excavation

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor s of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be
kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to floor coverings or restaurant equipment an impervious moisture
membrane is recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist
fine sand is provided above the membrane to help concrete curing
Pavement Section
It is anticipated that the front portion of the lot will be paved for
use aS a parking area and that asphaltic concrete will be used for the
surface course of the pavement Based on our field exploration and
laboratory testing we recommend the following pavement section
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The above recommended section assumes medium traffic adequate
drainage and a subgrade of select granular borrow material or equivalent
If any of these assumptions are invalid the pavement section should be
revised as applicable
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work In addition the
geotechnical engineer should be retained to provide continuous observation
and consultation services during the cavity probing phases of the work
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction
are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in
part upon information obtained from four 4 borings Variations of
soil rock conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

taping from reference points shown on the Topographic Map prepared by

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to 2 0 to 12 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Portable
hand carried drilling equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the exploration operations The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split spoon
falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock samples were obtained by using an NX rotary core barrel
advancing drill bits to cut and recover inch diameter core samples
The percent recovery REC and the rock quality determination RQD for
each core run is shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depth
