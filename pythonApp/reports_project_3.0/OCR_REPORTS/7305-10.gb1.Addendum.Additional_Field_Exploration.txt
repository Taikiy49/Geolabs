Dear Mr. Bokides: 
This addendum presents the results of our additional field exploration work and 
laboratory testing conducted for the Na Pua Makani Wind Project in Kahuku on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
Our additional field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling 
eight borings at the proposed wind turbine locations. In addition, four seismic shear 
wave velocity profiling using seismic cone penetration testing equipment was conducted 
at the project site.  
The borings were designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 4, and 6 through 9, 
extending to depths of about 58 to 98.5 feet below the existing ground surface. The 
approximate locations of these drilled borings are shown on the Site Plans, Plates 3.3 
and 3.4 herein. 
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology are presented in 
Appendix A of the above-referenced report prepared for the project. Graphic 
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings, 
Plates A-23 through A-30 herein. Photographs of the core runs are presented on 
Plates E-1 through E-9 in this addendum. 
Shear wave velocity testing was performed in Boring Nos. 2, 4, 6, and 8 to 
depths of about 61.3 to 80 feet below the existing ground surface using seismic cone

Additional laboratory testing consisted of nine Unconfined Compression tests

unconfined compressive strength of the basalt rock and coral encountered. Unconfined
