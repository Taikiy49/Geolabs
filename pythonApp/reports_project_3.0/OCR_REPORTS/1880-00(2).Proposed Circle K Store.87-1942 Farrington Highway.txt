INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the design of site grading slabs on grade and foundations
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at
this site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 72 foot
store and a 34 by 52 foot pump island with four gasoline dispenser
units To supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area
consisting of three 15 000 gallon tanks is planned for a 28 by 31 foot
area adjacent to the Circle K store The convenience facility will be
surrounded by an asphaltic concrete parking lot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern corner of the junction
of Farrington Highway and Maaloa Street in Nanakuli Oahu Hawaii
The site is bounded to the east by a Seven Eleven convenience store
and to the east by a vacant lot
The site measures 270 by 110 feet in plan dimension and is
generally flat with ground elevations varying between 7 4 and 9 7 feet
above Mean Sea Level
An abandoned Texaco Service Station exists at the site and is
presently occupied by R C Auto Center

Two small concrete islands and a small one story building occupy the
site and most of the site area is covered with A C pavement
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Two 2 borings were drilled at the project site to a depth of 16 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered about 4 5 feet of fill
consisting of very stiff to hard dark brown to brown clayey and gravelly
silts underlain by medium dense to dense tan to dark brown silty sands
and gravels Below this the drilling encountered a 3 0 to 3 5 foot
thick layer of hard tannish white coral underlain by dense to very dense
tannish white silty coral gravel and sand to a depth of 16 5 feet the
maximum depth drilled
Groundwater levels were encountered at the time of our exploration
from 7 9 to 8 2 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the close
proximity of the project site to the coastline it is anticipated that
groundwater levels will fluctuate in response to tidal influences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
It is anticipated that site grading will require fills of
approximately 2 to 4 feet in thickness Imported fill material should be
non expansive free of organic and other deleterious matter and form a
dense stable soil mass when compacted to the required density
Generally the soil particles should be well graded in size with no
individual particle to exceed 6 inches in maximum dimension
Additionally the imported fill material should have a minimum CBR value
of 15

Prior to the commencement of fill placement the areas within the
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed including any
required demolition The existing A C pavement should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
fill material
The existing surface soils should be scarified to a depth of 12
inches moisture conditioned to near optimum content and compacted to 95
of their maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft
yielding or pumping areas noted during the compacting process should be
excavated to firm material and backfilled with suitable material placed
in 6 inch compacted lifts and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as
recommended above
Backfill
It is anticipated that it may be necessary to remove the existing
underground storage tanks and other buried utilities The resulting
excavations should be backfilled to a level two feet below finish
subgrade with non expansive select borrow material placed in 6 inch thick
compacted level lifts and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Between two feet below finish subgrade to
finish subgrade the excavation should be backfilled with non expansive
select borrow placed as recommended above and compacted to 95 maximum
dry density
If groundwater is encountered in the excavations the excavation
should be backfilled with 3 Fine rock to a level at least 2 feet above
the groundwater surface This layer of 3 Fine rock should then be
capped with an 8 inch lift of base course compacted to 90 maximum dry
density Backfilling operations may then proceed as recommended above

Store Building and Pump Island
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered competent upper
fill crust underlain by hard coral reef formation which will provide
adequate support for the proposed lightly loaded Circle K store
building and pump island Shallow spread or continuous wall footings
bearing directly on the recompacted existing silty sand and gravel fill
or on select imported fill compacted as recommended in Site Grading
above may be used
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimm
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable
bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used for foundations bearing on
fill either existing or compacted as specified herein The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may he increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
slabs
The bottom of all thickened edge excavations should be wetted and
kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the poured
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water
away from the slab perimeter
Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three approximately 15 000 gallon tanks
underground in a 28 by 31 foot area adjacent to the Circle K Store

Utilities
Existing utilities and previously buried cesspools should be located
and verified in the field It may be necessary to move an existing line
or adjust structural details to accommodate the utility Should an
unanticipated condition or buried structure be encountered during
construction each condition should be evaluated by the soils engineer
and the design modified if necessary

Plans and specifications for foundations and pavement design should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that all subgrade preparation site filling footing
excavations and excavation backfill be observed by the soils engineer to
check that adequaté bearing and compaction have been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of weak organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of the foundation design of this project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
