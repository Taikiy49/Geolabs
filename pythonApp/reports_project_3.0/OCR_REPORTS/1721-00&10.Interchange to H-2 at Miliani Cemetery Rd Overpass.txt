PROJECT CONSIDERATION
A new interchange consisting of four 4 on off ramps is planned
for the H 2 Freeway at the Mililani Cemetery Road overpass As a result
of the construction of Ramp WG 1 and WG 4 both located on the Mililani
bound side of the freeway two retaining walls are required to provide the
necessary grade seperation

The retaining wall for Ramp WG 1 wall have a maximum height of about
10 feet and is anticipated to be embedded in an existing fill slope
Backfill behind the Ramp WG 1 wall is anticipated to slope up at a
gradient of about 2 1 H V The maximum height of Ramp WG 4 wall which
retains a level backfill is anticipated to be on the order of 14 feet
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
It is our understanding that the geotechnical investigation for the
other interchange structures will be performed by the State Highway
Department Therefore the scope of this investigation will be limited to
only the foundation design for the two above referenced retaining walls
Therefore our geotechnical investigation was designed with the
purpose to explore the subsurface soil conditions at the two wall
locations in order to provide design recommendations for the two 2
retaining walls
The scope of our investigation which was performed in accordance
with our proposal dated and included
drilling and sampling three 3 borings at the Ramp WG 1 wall location and
three 3 borings at the Ramp WG 4 wall location Also included in the
scope were laboratory testing of select soil samples obtained engineering
analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of
design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory
testings are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along the eastern side of the H 2
Freeway near the Mililani Cemetery Road overpass on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The existing fill slope on which the Ramp WG 1 retaining wall will
be constructed has an average gradient of about 2 1 H V Along the
wall alignment the slope flattens to a 5 to 15 foot wide bench Beyond
the bench the fill slope continues with gradients of 2 1 H V or steeper
until it meets the existing Panakauahi Gulch Our visual inspection at
the time of field exploration indicated that the fill slope is densely
covered with vegetation which consists mainly of tall California grass and
small Koa trees
The southern section of the Ramp WG 4 retaining wall will be
constructed along the existing Mililani Cemetery Road while the northern
section will extend onto an existing slope between the H 2 Freeway and the
Mililani Cemetery Road The A C paved Mililani Cemetery Road appears to
be in good condition with only minor cracks on the pavement Most of the
slope area are covered with dense vegetation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Due to the limited scope of work the discussion and recommendation
presented herewith are only for the foundation design of the two proposed
retaining walls Other issues which we believe to be critical to the
interchange project such as site grading and long term stability of
slopes after the wall construction will not be studied in details in this
report
In general our borings encountered competent subsoil conditions
underlying the two proposed retaining walls Based on our currently
available subsoil data and the subsequently interpretated soil strength
parameters a preliminary slope stability was performed at the Ramp WG
wall location The results of the analysis led to our belief that the
construction of the wall and the placement of the additional fill would
not substantially reduce the factor of safety of the slope to a concerned
level Therefore we believe that both of the proposed Ramp WG 1 with
2 1 sloping backfill and WG 4 with level backfill retaining walls can
be designed and constructed provided our recommendations are followed

Backfill Material
The above recommended earth pressures assume that well graded
granular materials such as Select Borrow Aggregates coral sand or
equivalent are used as backfill material directly behind the retaining
walls The use of these granular materials would minimize the compactive
effort required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional
lateral pressure which would be imposed on the newly constructed walls
In general the granular backfill should be placed in an area within
a line drawn at a 45 degree angle from the heel of the retaining wall
structure as shown on Plate 3 for the Ramp WG 4 wall
For the Ramp WG 1 wall which retains a sloping backfill slight
modification to the above requirement can be used For details of the
recommended limits for granular backfill for the Ramp WG 1 wall refer to
Plate 4

residucal soil can be used Notwithstanding the recommended use of

Drainage Provision
To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls
sufficient drainage should be provided We would recommend that the
drainage provision consist of the followings
il Weepholes Sufficient levels of weepholes should be installed
at regular spacings to relieve water pressure behind the
retaining wall

2 Drainage Ditch Drainage ditches should be provided immediately
behind the top and at the ground level in front of the wall
These ditches will direct the surface run off away from the
wall
3 Subdrain A 4 inch perforated pipe should be placed on the heel
directly behind the wall At a distance of no more than 50 feet
along the alingment of the wall the subdrain should daylight
into the drainage ditch in front of the wall for Ramp WG 1 and
WG 4 walls or onto the face of slope for Ramp WG 1 wall
4 Drainage Blanket for wall with sloping backfill only A
6 inch thick drainage blanket is recommended between the
compacted on site backfill material and the existing slope
The drainage water should be intercepted by a longitudinal
perforated pipe which would daylight at regular spacing onto the
face of the finished slope
Details of the above mentioned drainage provisions are also shown on
Plates 3 and 4
All the backfill material should be placed in S inch maximum lifts
and be compacted to at least 90 for cohesive soil or 95 for granular
material of its maximum dry density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78
method The above compaction requirements are based on the assumption
that small compaction equipment is available
For the Ramp WG 1 wall we would recommend that keying and benching
as shown on Plate 5 be performed prior to the backfilling operation

Bearing Capacities
The footing of the Ramp WG 1 retaining walls would be placed on the
stiff existing fill while that for the Ramp WG 4 wall would be on the very
stiff natural soil Assuming that the fill is well compacted and also
that the natural soil is not disturbed we would recommend the use of the
following bearing capacity
Bearing Material Allowable Bearing Pressure
Ramp WG 1 Wail Compacted Fill 3000 P S F
Ramp WG 4 Wall Natural Residual Soil 4000 P S F
All wall footings along the slope should be embedded a sufficient
depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 10 feet as
measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope face For walls
located on level ground the footings should be embedded a minimum of 2
feet below the lowest adjacent grade
Settlement of the walls under the above recommended pressures is
estimated to be on the order of about one half of an inch
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the wall footing may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil

Slope Protection
Based on all of our currently available information on the
subsurface condition near the Ramp WG 1 retaining wall location we
believe that the slope in that particular area may have sufficient factor
of safety against failure after the construction of the wall However we
also believe that despite this finding the slope should still be
carefully protected from erosion
Slope planting and grassing should be carried out as soon as the
slope is backfilled If required slope benching is recommended at 20
feet vertical slope height intervals in order to limit surface runoff on
the slopes Also if required special surface and subsurface drainage
measures may be considered
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation of the retaining walls
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the foundation excavation and backfilling be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been cbtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as

to accommodate these possible costs
