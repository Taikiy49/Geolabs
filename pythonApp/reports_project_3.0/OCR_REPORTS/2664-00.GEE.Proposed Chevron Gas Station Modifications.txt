INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Chevron Gas Station Modifications in Waianae
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve an existing Chevron gas station by performing the
following major modifications
Three 3 existing underground fuel tanks will be removed from the central portion
of the gas station area To replace the old fuel tanks three 3 new underground tanks
will be installed in the central eastern area of the property near a supermarket building
located in the neighboring lot
The new fuel tanks will measure approximately thirty 30 feet long and eight 8
feet in diameter and will be installed below the existing ground surface It is anticipated
that an excavation approximately twenty 20 feet deep will be required for the tank
installation
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2664 00 Page 3
Existing gas pump islands covered by a canopy in the gas station area will also
be demolished and replaced Two 2 new gas pump islands are proposed to be built
on the south and north sides of a new sales building to be located at the approximate
center of the gas station area A new car wash facility is also included as a part of the
project
The scope of this report is limited to consideration of the installation of three 3
new underground tanks and the southerly proposed gas pump island at the locations
proposed at the time of our exploration as shown on The Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing Chevron gas station is located at the junction of Farrington Highway
and Puhano Street in Waianae on the island of Oahu Hawaii
A one story masonry supermarket building is located on an adjacent lot within a
few feet of the eastern boundary of the project site
Topography of the site and surface elevations were not available at the time of
preparation of this report Ground level at the gas station is generally flat and paved with
both asphaltic and portland cement concrete
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
The subsoil conditions at the new fuel tanks and the gas pump island in the
southeastern area of the project site were explored on March 26 1991 by drilling and
sampling two 2 borings to depths of about 20 6 to 24 1 feet below the existing ground
surface The two 2 borings were drilled at locations pre determined by the client The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
A dense coral formation was encountered in both borings but at widely different
depths This formation is overlain by coralline fill and detritus of widely varying densities
The underlying coral subsoils are overlain by a thin surficial layer of soft to medium stiff
silty fill materials ana aiso in Boring No 1 by stiff to very stiff clays

To minimize the shoring support required one viable option is to relocate the new
fuel tanks to along the eastern boundary of the gas station where a dense coral formation

feet away from the adjacent supermarket building
If this suggested relocation is considered additional borings are also
recommended prior to completion of shoring and dewatering design to determine the
depth to which shoring support will be required

construction To accomodate this gravel cushion an excavation depth of up to 20 feet
is anticipated to be required

Excavation Method
Based on the limited subsurface data available we believe that excavation of the
material utilizing conventional mechanical earthmoving equipment should not be
difficult Occasional hard ripping however may be required when excavating the
dense coral formation
The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be at the option of
the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
To reduce the potential of ground movement or subsidence which may damage
the existing adjacent structures we recommend that the excavated soils be
stockpiled at least fifteen 15 feet away from the excavation
Shoring Support
if future borings verify the occurrence of the competent dense coral formation at
shallow depth within the proposed tank site it appears that the shoring support
may be minimized or eliminated in the lower portion of the excavation below the
surface of the dense coral formation We do not recommend however that
shoring be eliminated above the dense coral formation
it is imperative that adequate shoring be provided in order to reduce the potential
of adjacent ground loss or movement We recommend that continuous
interlocking steel sheetpile shoring properly braced with horizontal wales and or
struts be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2664 00 Page 10
It is essential that interlocking steel sheetpiles be properly installed prior to
excavation and maintained tight against the excavation faces by horizontal bracing
progressively installed during excavation The shoring support should not be
removed until the tank excavation is properly backfilled
The steel sheetpiling should be driven properly with a suitable sheetpile hammer
to refusal in the dense coral formation or to a sufficient depth below the bottom of
the excavation where only loose subsoils are encountered in order to assure the
stability of the excavation bottom and to reduce the possibility of bottom heaving
or piping quick conditions which may be created by upward groundwater
seepage during dewatering
Assuming no groundwater drawdown outside of the excavation we estimate that
the sheeting should be driven to refusal in the dense coral formation or a minimum
of 11 feet below the excavation bottom in areas where the dense coral formation
may be absent it is not recommended to use a deep weil system outside the
excavations to draw down the natural groundwater level in order to reduce the
sheeting embedment required
We recommend that qualified geotechnical and structural consultants be retained
by the contractor to design the shoring system required based on the available
boring data augmented by information obtained by a future additional boring
program However for preliminary construction cost estimation purposes we
suggest that the apparent earth pressure diagram presented on Plate 3 be
considered for development of the initial shoring design

It is our opinion that the adequacy and safety of the shoring design and installation
should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor His representative who
should be required to be continuously present on site will have the best
opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during construction such
as unforseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table unsuitable
construction sequence or techniques etc which might affect the stability of the
shoring
However it must be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground might still occur because of changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately predict due to the
complexity of the stress changes It depends greatly on workmanship e g how
quickly and tightly the sheetpiles and the bracing support are installed on the
subsoil conditions and also on the size and rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed
properly and as early as practicable and that the adjacent ground should be
continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of movements
with instruments such as settlement markers and inclinometers Such monitoring

the contractor s full time on site representative should have the authority to stop
excavation if conditions in his opinion so warrant

Dewatering
Dewatering of large quantities of groundwater from the tank excavation is
anticipated to be required since the natural groundwater level may be as high as ten 10
feet above the bottom of the excavation and the underlying subsoils consist of porous
coral formations and or coral sand and gravel detritus which are normally highly
permeable
Dewatering Method
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation dewatering should be
at the option of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should mean any works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table
and to exclude it from the excavation to permit construction of the proposed
underground structures under safe and dry conditions These works or systems
should therefore include but not be limited to items such as pumping inlet and
outlet lines siltation pits etc
We suggest that the following three 3 basic criteria be considered in selecting a
suitable method of dewatering
a The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to the existing
adjacent structures
b The method should maintain the stability of and also provide safe and dry
working conditions in the excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2664 00 Page 14
Cc The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications to
accommodate various ground conditions
Based on these criteria it appears that sump pumping inside the excavation may
be used if the steel sheetpile shoring is driven to refusal or sufficiently deep to
prevent piping and or heaving at the excavation bottom
Dewatering by means of a well point system along the outside of the excavation
is not recommended The resultant areal depression of the natural groundwater
table could induce consolidation of subsoils and subsequent ground settlements
which may damage the existing adjacent structures such as the supermarket
building
Construction Precaution
The potential for the dewatering system selected causing areai depression of the
natural groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
operation It is also recommended that qualified geotechnical and structural
engineers be retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering
system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site will have the best Opportunity to
promptly observe the effects of dewatering during construction and to implement
as Soon as possible necessary precautionary or remedial measures including but
not limited to stopping or slowing down the dewatering operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2664 00 Page 15
Where encountered at the excavation bottom permeable granular subsoils may
be susceptible to piping and quick conditions Therefore the project dewatering
Operations should be done without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water
only and should be coordinated with the shoring installation so that the
excavation stability is not jeopardized The dewatering operations should be
carried out without creating a quick condition or softening of the excavation
bottom
Extra caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches that connect
to the excavation If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in the utility
trenches could act as subdrains and cause significant areal groundwater
drawdown resulting in a potential for causing settlements and damages to the
utility lines and or other existing adjacent structures
Gas Pump Island Foundation
The proposed gas pump island in the southeastern portion of the project site is
underlain by generally competent silty and clayey subsoils to approximately 13 feet depth
We believe that the proposed light weight structure may be supported on shallow spread
footing foundations
If the subsoil conditions below the other proposed gas pump island in the northern
portion of the project site are found by additional boring to be similar to those
encountered in Boring No 1 it is our opinion that the same footing foundation
recommendations may be applied
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2000 p s f may be used for the footing
foundation design The bearing capacity may be increased by up to one third for wind
seismic and other short duration loading conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2664 00 Page 16
We recommend that a minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches be
used and that the footing subgrade be compacted to a minimum 95 of the maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Up to one 1 inch of total settlement and one half 1 2 of an inch of differential
settlement are anticipated for the gas pump island footings under the allowable desian
loadings
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the gas pump islands may be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance developed against the footing foundations
We recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used
to determine the passive earth pressure developed against the embedded footings and
a frictional coefficient of 0 4 be used to compute the frictional resistance developed
between the footings and the recompacted subgrade
