INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at 1328 Claudine Street
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed in
general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two story structure approximately 110 by 30 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage and driveway areas
Site grading involving minor cuts and fills are anticipated
Retaining walls are planned for grade separation at various portions of
the lot
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of soil engineering
recommendations pertinent to the site grading and foundation design of the
project
The scope of work included
1 excavating three 3 test pits to depths ranging from 6 0 to 8 5
feet below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil rock conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered

5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing pavement and foundation design
recommendations and
6 preparation of this report presenting the design recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appedices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located at the eastern end of Claudine Street in
the district of Kaimuki on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The lot is approximately rectangular shaped and includes about 7 500
square feet The site is bounded by existing residences on the north
east and west
The topography of the site slopes gently downward from north to south
with ground elevations ranging from about 53 feet southern portion to 65
feet northern portion above Mean Sea Level
At the time of our exploration the site had been cleared and some
amount of preliminary grading had been performed Cuts were observed
along the property boundaries and in the northern portion of the site at
locations where retaining walls are planned

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed a surface layer of highly expansive
adobe clay underlain by weathered basalt formation at the proposed
residential site Since only minimal cuts and fills are planned and
because light structure loads are anticipated the main concer in
designing the on grade slabs and building footing foundations would be to
adopt special procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the
subsoils
Foundation Support
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed residence
provided special procedures are utilized to reduce the shrinkage swelling
effects of the clay soils
The adobe clay soils below the foundations have high volume change
potential and can shrink when dried or heave considerably if saturated in
the future Therefore we recommend that the expansive clays below the
foundations should be over excavated down to a minimm depth of three 3
feet below the bottom of the footings The over excavation should also
extend a minimum of three 3 feet beyond the edge of the footings
The over excavation should be backfilled with non expansive borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding six 6 inches thick and compacted to a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2236 00 Page 5
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The top one foot adjacent to the footings should be backfilled with
impervious non expansive soil compacted to the above density for
details refer to Plate 2 For the interior footings in lieu of the
over excavation procedure the footings may be embedded a minimm of
thirty 30 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may
be used for spread footings bearing on compacted structural fill or stiff
in situ soil
If a footing is situated next to a utility line it should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill must
be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from all foundations This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
The expansive on site clay soil under on grade slab areas should be
over excavated to a depth of at least three 3 feet below the slab or to
less expansive soil and replaced with non expansive borrow soils in order
to reduce the effects of soil heaving on the slabs see Plate 3
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils
are recommended for driveways walkways and other slabs if required
To reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils below the
over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to concrete
placement

To prevent ponding condition the foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against the footing immediately after setting
of the concrete
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
A 4 inch thick cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway over excavation and replacement of the
expansive clay is recommended However since the driveway can tolerate
some movement the depth of over excavation may be reduced to two 2 feet
provided some small slab cracking or movement is tolerable
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
To reduce post construction swelling of the expansive clay the
subgrade soils should be kept continuously moist prior to concrete
placement
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that a retaining wall approximately eight
8 feet high is planned for grade separation between the garage and
northern portion of the residence In addition retaining walls generally
less than six 6 feet high are planned along the property lines adjacent
to the existing residences The guidelines for retaining wall design are
listed below
1 In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive adobe clays
if encountered be over excavated down for a depth of two 2

A base friction factor of 0 4 for soil can be utilized to

To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay

or equal to the height of the wall whichever is smaller
Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
such as select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill
would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required for
the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure on the walls
Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be used only
for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of surface

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendation is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the geotechnical engineer during construction to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site for
use
If conditions are different from those presented in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
