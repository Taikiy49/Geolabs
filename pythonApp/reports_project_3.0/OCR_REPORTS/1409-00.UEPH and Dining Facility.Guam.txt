INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation performed for the proposed UEPH Unaccompanied Enlisted
Personnel Housing and Dining Facility Addition at the Naval Magazine in
Guan

The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil data such as shear
strength compressibility and classification for the foundation design
of the proposed structures
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated March 27 1984 included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of 8 borings laboratory soil
testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data
and report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of the report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The project includes the construction of the following
1 One 3 story concrete UEPH building approximately 60 by 120
feet in plan dimension Anticipated wall loads are about 7 to
il kips per foot Retaining walls up to 8 feet high are
planned around the eastern end of the building

2 One 2 story concrete general purpose building approximately 65
by 30 feet in plan dimension with a basement level on the
eastern end of the building Maximum anticipated column loads
are 160 kips or about 10 kips per foot for bearing walls
3 One 1 story ft concrete building addition to the
existing Enlisted Dining Facility Anticipated wall loads are
3 kips per foot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project sites are located within the U S Naval Magazine Fena
Valley Watershed in Guam Federated States of Micronesia
UEBPH and General Purpose Building
The proposed site for the UEPH and General Purpose Building is
bordered by Building 406 to the west parking lot access roadway to the
north and open landscape areas to the east and south
This site is relatively flat to the southwest of Building 406 To
the east of Building 406 the site is relatively flat for a distance of
about 90 feet and the ground surface then continues downward with a slope
ratio of generally less than 5 1 H V The ground surface elevation
generally varied from 340 to 370 feet

southwest of Building 406
Overhead electrical lines cross through the middle portion of the
site in a generally west to east direction
A i2 inch drainline about 60 feet long and 2 5 to 4 0 feet below
the existing ground surface and extending from the southeastern corner of
Building 406 were also observed
A dirt roadway crosses the site from Bona Springs Road on the west
to the eastern side of Building 406
Ground cover in this area consisted mainly of well maintained lawn

The proposed site for the Dining Facility Extension is bordered by

The site is relatively flat and slopes gently towards the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a
layer of medium stiff residual soil with soft and wet layers overlying a
very stiff to hard saprolite layer
Pile foundations are recommended for the 2 story general purpose
building and the 3 story UEPH building Spread footing foundation is
recommended for the l story dining facility addition
The upper residual soil layer is generally medium stiff to stiff
However soft and wet layers about 5 feet thick were encountered within
the stratum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1409 00 Page 7
Calculated footing settlements for the anticipated foundation loads
are on the order of 1 to 3 inches depending on the actual thickness and
depth of the soft layer below the footing Additional differential
settlements would occur due to differences in pressure caused by the
proposed cut and fill on the western and eastern sides of the building
respectively
Due to the anticipated settlements for footing foundations pile
foundations are recommended for the structure
The piles would develop their load capacity primarily from
frictional resistance or adhesion between the piles and the stiff to very
stiff residual and saprolite soil
fwelve inch square pre stressed concrete piles are recommended
The piles should extend into the very stiff to hard soil encountered at
about 20 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface and be driven to a
total depth of 50 feet
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 3 blows per
inch for the last 6 inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 40
tons per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order of less than
one half of an inch are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1409 00 Page 8
Depending on the total number of piles required it may be
desirable to have a number of test piles installed at various pile groups
to evaluate the actual pile driving behavior and to determine the length
of the production piles to be ordered
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth The backfill
against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with
select materials compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of the
maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation
Any site filling including retaining wall construction and
backfilling should be placed as early as practicable before the building
foundation construction to allow the ground settlements to occur prior to
construction of the superstructure
Recommendations for the retaining wall design are presented in a
subsequent section

Dining Facility Addition Foundation
Foundation drawings for the existing structure indicate that the
foundations may consist of spread footings bearing on about 5 feet of
compacted coral fill overlying short approximately 8 feet pier
foundations Based on the information from the borings and the
anticipated light building loads it is our opinion that the pier
foundations are not required and that spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used to support the addition structure
A 3 foot thick layer of granular fill is recommended below the
addition to provide uniform support for the slab and foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1409 00 Page 10
Laboratory swell tests on the upper soils indicate that they may
have moderate swelling potential if they are first allowed to dry and
then saturated The 3 foot thick granular fill would help to minimize
the swelling of the underlying soils
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
foundations on compacted granular fill The bearing capacity value is
for total dead plus live loads and may be increased to one third for
short duration wind and or seismic loading
A minimum footing width of 18 inches is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 70 and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to compression of the
trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII

Estimated settlements will be on the order of less than one half of
an inch total and one quarter of an inch differential
Concrete On Grade Slab
For the anticipated static floor loads at the proposed buildings a
4 inch slab over 4 inches of well graded aggregate base course is
recommended
A plastic vapor barrier over 4 inches of 3 fine gravel may be
substituted for the base course should a capillary break be deemed
necessary to protect the floor covering A thin layer of sand should be
placed over the plastic barrier to aid in concrete curing and minimize
shrinkage cracks
Retaining and basement walls are planned for the UEPH and General
Purpose Building respectively The following guidelines are recommended

An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for wall footing on level area However the passive
resistance should be reduced by one third when combined with
frictional resistance
A base friction factor of 0 4 may be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
Well graded granular material such as 3 Fine aggregate should be
used as a granular drainage filter backfill material minimum 12
inches wide behind the wall Granular non expansive material
such as select borrow coral or sand should be used as backfill
material behind the filter layer The backfill should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78 designation
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Site Grading
Cuts and fills up to 6 feet may be required at the UEPH Building
Cuts up to 8 feet deep are planned for the General Purpose Building
Site Preparation
Existing concrete slabs foundations and asphaltic concrete
pavements below the structures should be removed
Vegetation organic or other deleterious material should be removed
The existing ground should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fill Material
The on site soils have high moisture content and would require
substantial drying out prior to being used as fill Therefore
imported material may be required for the fill The material to be
imported should be non expansive free of organic matter and form
a dense stable mass when compacted to the required densities
In general the structural fill material should be well graded
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing a 200 sieve

Fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its

The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away trom all foundations
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading retaining and basement walls
and utility constructions should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included
It is recommended that the test pile program and the production
pile driving operations be monitored on a full time basis by a qualified
soils technician under the direction of a soils engineer to verify that
the piles have been driven in accordance with the recommendations and to
modify our recommendations should the soil conditions encountered during
pile driving be different from our field exploration

Field Exploration
ee ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
8 borings to depths of 30 to 80 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Pacific
Drilling Inc of Guam was contracted to drill the borings and
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment was used
The soil encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers from Honolulu who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System Graphic presentations of the soils
encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs Plates
A 1 1 through 8 2
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler or a 2 4 inch diameter ring sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ie a a an
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional tests were
performed These include Atterberg Limit and specific gravity tests
The results of these tests are presented on Plate B 1
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
remolded samples of the near surface soils to evaluate their swelling
potential The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsurface materials The
test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Seven consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the alluvial
sediments under the site The test results are presented on Plates B 5
through B 11
One unconsolidated undrained triaxial test was performed on an
undisturbed sample of the residual soil to evaluate the undrained shear
strength of the soil The test result is presented on Plate B 12
