Dear Mr Kato
At your request and as required by the City and County of Honolulu Geolabs Inc
has provided geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services during the
drilled shaft foundation installation for the above project This letter report summarizes our
geotechnical engineering observations during the drilled shaft foundation installation for
the McKinley High School Softball Stadium project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the
construction of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special Inspection
services by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation for general conformance with
the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The project site is within the McKinley High School campus in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that it is planned to construct a new softball
stadium on campus
The new softball stadium will be between the existing softball field and track and
field The softball stadium will include a backstop scoreboard field lighting and fencing
around the stadium In addition restrooms and storage buildings are planned at the north
side of the softball stadium An emergency access road is planned along the west side of
the softball stadium

The field lighting system will consist of six 60 foot high flood light poles supported
on 42 inch diameter drilled shaft foundations extending to a depth of about 21 feet below
the finish grade In addition the scoreboard will be supported on two 36 inch diameter
drilled shaft foundations and the backstop will be supported on seven 24 inch diameter
drilled shaft foundations extending to depths of about 9 5 and 10 feet below the finish
grade respectively The existing speaker pole will be relocated and supported on a
48 inch diameter drilled shaft foundation extending to a depth of about 12 feet below the
ground surface
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the above project only PER Inc is the general
contractor for the project and Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company is the drilled
shaft installation subcontractor
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
The drilled shaft contractor installed a total of 16 shafts from April 2 through 9
2012 The as built diameters and the lengths of the drilled shafts installed for the project
are summarized on Plate 3 Geolabs representatives observed the drilled shaft
excavation reinforcing cage placement and concrete placement during the drilled shaft
installation
In general the drilled shafts were excavated using an drill rig equipped
with augers and clean out buckets Due to the available equipment of the drilled shaft
contractor and to facilitate installation of the drilled shafts the drilled shaft diameters were
drilled to larger diameters than the plan diameters A 48 inch auger was used to drill the
floodlight pole drilled shafts a 28 inch auger was used to drill the backstop post drilled
shafts a 42 inch diameter auger was used to drill the scoreboard post drilled shafts and a
54 inch auger was used to drill the speaker pole drilled shaft
The as built diameter of the drilled shafts supporting the floodlight poles is
48 inches and the as built lengths range between 21 and 22 feet The as built diameter of
the drilled shafts supporting the backstop posts is 28 inches and the as built lengths range
between 10 and 13 feet The as built diameters of the drilled shafts supporting the
scoreboard posts and the speaker pole are 42 and 54 inches respectively The as built
lengths of the drilled shaft supporting the scoreboard posts and speaker pole range
between 11 and 12 feet
After excavating the shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing cages
were lowered into the drilled holes followed by the anchor bolts for the light pole
foundations The reinforcing cages and anchor bolts were then adjusted to the appropriate
positions
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled shaft holes using the tremie method A 4 inch diameter steel tremie
pipe was lowered down near the bottom of the drilled hole During concrete placement we
