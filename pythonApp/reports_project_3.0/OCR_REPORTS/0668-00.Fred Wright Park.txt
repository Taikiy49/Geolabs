Gentlemen
This report summarizes the findings of the subsurface exploration
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design
and construction of the foundations to support the proposed flood
lighting poles at Fred Wright Park Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct eight 90 foot height poles
with floodlighting fixtures mounted near the top
The dead load of each pole is about 5 kips Based on a 4 kip wind
loading an overturning moment of 250 foot kips is anticipated for
each pole
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project is located at Fred Wright Park in Wahiawa
Wright Park is situated between California and
Kilani Avenues just south of Koa Street and adjacent to the Wahiawa
Ho
Recreation Center and gym complex
At present the project area facilitates a baseball diamond and
field area with bleachers and ball stop fence The entire pro
ject area is level and grassed
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site four test borings
were drilled at the proposed site utilizing truck mounted rotary
auger equipment Depths of borings ranged from 18 5 to 26 5 feet
below surface elevations The locations of the borings are shown
on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The field classifications were supplemented by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A des
cription of the soils encountered and a graphical representation are
presented on the boring logs plates A 1 through A 4
LABORATORY TESTING
Motsture and density determination and Atterberg Limits were performed
on selected samples to aid in the classification of the soils The
results of these tests are presented on the boring logs at the appro
priate sample location
Ho
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of
the encountered soils one unconfined compression test and two
direct shear tests were performed on the near surface sample
The test results are shown on Plate B 1 through B 3
One swell test was conducted on an undisturbed l inch thick ring
sample to evaluate the swell potential of the on site soil Plate
B 4 summarizes the swell test results
SOIL CONDITIONS
The test borings indicated that the site is underlain by about a
14 feet layer of very stiff to hard reddish brown residual clay
soil Under this layer is the medium stiff to very stiff silty
soils with some decomposed rock fragments extending to the depth
of the borings
About one foot of surface fill material was encountered at boring
no 4
No ground water was encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is suitable for the proposed floodlighting poles construc
tion The on site residual clay soil is hard and will provide ade
quate support for the pole spread footing foundation
Spread or mat footing may be used
Allowable bearing pressure up to 8 000 pounds per square foot may
be used The bearing value can be increased by 1 3 for wind and
Ho
seismic loadings The total settlements are estimated to be
3 4 inch The bottom of the foundation should be placed a mini
mum of 3 feet into the undisturbed on site soil
It is recommended that the spread or mat foundation be excavated
carefully allowing no loose soil left inside the excavation and
poured without forming to assure firm lateral contact with the
natural soils For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid
density of 300 pounds per cubic foot may be used in the design
for lateral resistance The coefficient of friction can be taken
as 0 4 for the total shearing resistance between the base of foun
dations and the underlying soils
All the footing excavations backfill should be in 6 inch compacted
layers compacted to 90 of maximum density

We are pleased to submit this revised proposal for a soil and foundation
investigation for the construction of the proposed floodlighting poles
It is our understanding that the scope of work involved will be divided
into two parts
Part 1
The first phase is to conduct a sub surface soil investigation and to
prepare a soil report providing recommendations for the floodlight pole
foundation construction
The work will include boring investigation laboratory testing
engineering analysis and recommendations for design purposes We will
also include some consultation time with the structual engineers and
other designers prior to the finalizations of our soil report
We propose to drill four 25 foot deep borings one boring at every other
pole light locations
An engineer or geologist from our firm will continuously monitor the
drilling operations log the test borings obtain the necessary soil
samples and make detail observations of the site conditions
Selected soil samples will be tested in our laboratory to measure the
bearing capacity settlement and expansion potential of the on site soils

The formal report will summarize our work on the project and presentation
of our findings and recommendations pertinent to site and foundation
For this phase of work the cost will be including 4 state tax
Phase II
During construction we will provide two construction field inspections
of pole light foundation inspect footing excavations to be sure that they
are down to competent soil layer
The backfill footing and compactions will be tested by the city testing

occur during the construction
