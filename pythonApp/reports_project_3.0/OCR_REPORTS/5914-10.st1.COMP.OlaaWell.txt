This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the construction for the Olaa Well No 6 and Tank project in the Puna area on the
Island of Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is within the District of Puna on the Island of Hawaii The project site is
involved outfitting a new well and constructing a tank at the Olaa Well No 6 site in
the vicinity of North Kulani Road The new reservoir structure has a capacity of about
one million gallons The tank structure has a diameter of about 100 feet with a finish floor
elevation of about feet Mean Sea Level MSL A control building and booster
stations was also constructed at the site In addition access roadway associated water
lines mechanical equipment pads and other appurtenances were constructed as part of
the project
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Isemoto Contracting Company Ltd performed the earthwork for the project Our
observation and testing services during the construction of the project occurred between
April 2010 and May 2011

After clearing and grubbing finished subgrades in cut areas and areas to be filled
were generally proof rolled with a Dynapac 362 roller compactor minimum 20 tons for
about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
As required by the project plans and specifications the footprint of the new
reservoir was over excavated by a minimum 3 feet below the 12 inch thick layer of
compacted base course or select borrow subbase and re compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction
Fill materials used for the project consisted of structural fills select borrow subbase
and base course materials Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials
generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension Structural fills required to raise the grades
were generally placed in level lifts of about 12 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Select borrow subbase and base course materials were generally placed in level lifts of
about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 59 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Compaction test results are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction
of the Olaa Well No 6 and Tank project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated March 19 2008 and subsequent
recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
