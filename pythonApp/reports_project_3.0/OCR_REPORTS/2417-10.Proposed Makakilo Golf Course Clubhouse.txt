GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the eastern slopes of Puu Makakilo in the Ewa
District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is steep rugged and vegetated with a thin cover of grasses and
sparse kiawe haole koa and other semitropical trees Throughout the site there are
scattered outcrops of basalt and welded cinder formations These outcrops create
localized areas of sharp vertical relief ranging from about two 2 to ten 10 feet in height
In general the topography of the project site slopes down in an easterly direction
with existing elevations ranging from about 730 to 610 feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs
Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of Waianae along the southern rift
zone
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events which produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into weak to strong rock
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally Puu Makakilo
is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles most of the site This residual soil grades rapidly to
the underlying volcanic materials

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and geotechnical engineering analyses it is our
opinion that development of the proposed golf course clubhouse is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view The residual soils cinder and basalt formation
encountered at the site should provide adequate foundation supports for the proposed
golf course clubhouse development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 10 Page 7
The excavated granular materials if reduced to less than 12 inches in size should
provide a suitable source of fill material Fills in existing sloping terrain should be properly
keyed and benched to bond the new fills to the existing slopes
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Grading
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site or chipped shredded and stockpiled for
use in landscaping either in the clubhouse complex or for the larger golf course areas
Any soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any
areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm natural material and the
resulting depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
In general the excavated soils and rock materials if reduced to less than 12 inches
in size are suitable as fill material However our laboratory testing indicated that the
surface residual soil of reddish brown silty clay has low to moderate swelling potential
Therefore the residual clayey soils should not be used as fill materials for the upper two
2 feet of the proposed building finished subgrade to reduce shrink swell effect of the
subsoils on the foundations and floor slabs
The excavated granular materials such as basalt and welded cinder if reduced to
less than six 6 inches in size should be adequate as a source of trench and structural
backfill if needed The stockpiled quarry waste from the existing adjacent quarry should
also be suitable as fill and backfill material if properly processed Imported material if
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 10 Page 8
required should be non expansive with no particles greater than six 6 inches in size and
no more than fifteen 15 percent passing the No 200 sieve It is recommended that if
additional off site borrow is required it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to
determine if it is suitable for its intended use prior to its delivery to the job site
The excavated cinder and basalt materials may not have sufficient fines to produce
a well graded fill material Therefore some mixing and handling of the excavated
materials may be required
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fil
material will be necessary It is also advisable to institute a dust control program during
grading operations
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density
determined in accordance with the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and structural
backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 80 maximum dry density determined in accordance
with the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any fills to be placed on existing slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal
to one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm residual soil or in situ
volcanic formations to lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill placement

Subdrains may be needed at existing natural drainageways and at locations where
seepage is encountered during site grading see Plate 4 If required subdrains should
be installed prior to fill placement in order to minimize potential seepage accumulation and
subsequent weakening of the new embankment fill materials
Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in its vicinity
indicate that the site is most likely underlain by a thin surface layer of residual soil over
dense cinder and ledges of dense basalt at relatively shallow depths
It is anticipated that the residual soil and most of the cinder materials may be
excavated with large bulldozers such as the Caterpillar D 9 series Some hard excavation
and or ripping may be required in localized well cemented areas or when ledges of dense
basalt are encountered
The underlying basalt formation may require some hard excavation such as heavy
ripping hoerams or possibly blasting
Generally the excavation method should within safety considerations and practical
limits be left to the contractor Blasting may be considered to expedite the excavation
process However care should be exercised to avoid overshooting which could disrupt
the formation resulting in a loss of strength
Slopes
Provided that granular materials 12 inch minus are utilized design fill slopes of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are recommended Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and subsequent cutting to final grades in order to secure a
GEOLABS HAWAII

stable fill slope Fills to be placed on existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be properly keyed and benched into the existing slopes
For cut slopes the following slope ratios or flatter are recommended
a Residual Soil 2H 1V
b Cinder
c Dense Basalt 1H 1V
Water should be diverted away from the top of the slopes Slope planting should
also be utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the finished slope
surfaces
In general slope benches should be provided for slopes steeper than three
horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V and having vertical heights greater than 30 feet
Foundations
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the proposed
Structures It is anticipated that spread footings will be bearing on various kinds of soil
conditions
In general an allowable bearing capacity of about 3 000 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on compacted structural fills in situ dense cinder or rock formation A
minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is
also recommended The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third when temporary loads winds and seismic are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 10 Page 11
The bottom of footing excavations on fills should be recompacted to obtain a
minimum of 95 maximum dry density determined in accordance with the ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
If loose soft soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to dense in situ soils or basalt and replaced with granular
select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated footing settlements
should be less than 3 8 of an inch for footings bearing on compacted structural fills and
should be negligible for footings bearing on dense in situ cinder or rock formation
Slab On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the ground floor of the proposed
clubhouse main building and basement cart storage structure A 6 inch thick layer of
compacted base course is recommended below the slabs to provide uniform support
It is anticipated that for the proposed main clubhouse building the slab on grade
will be supported partially by fills and partially by cuts The slab on grade for the cart
Storage building will be supported by different materials due to different cut depths of
about 1 to 10 feet below the existing grade in order to reduce the potential of differential
settlement and to prevent potential cracking on slabs supported by different materials it
is Our recommendation that the slab subgrade below the 6 inch thick base course layer
should be over excavated by a minimum of 12 inches and backfilled with select granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 10 Page 12
fill compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The exposed subgrade below the 12 inch over excavation
should be kept moist prior to backfill
In order to protect against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier on
top of the cushion layer is recommended for the slabs One 1 inch of moist fine sand
may be placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing

Infrastructure Installation
It is anticipated that underground utilities will be installed as part of the develop
ment work Based on the results of our field exploration it is anticipated that excavation
for utility line trenching in cinder and the above specified structural fill should be relatively
easy
In general the subsurface materials encountered at the site are relatively
competent and would require minimal shoring However areas of loose soil or cinder
pockets may be encountered and proper shoring may be required Trench shoring
design and installation should be left to the contractor However trench shoring and
bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
Trench backiills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Water Features
It is our understanding that the proposed golf course clubhouse will include water
falls and other water features Due to the dry site conditions and the anticipated rocky
nature of the material at the site we recommend that the ponds of the proposed water
falls and water features be lined with synthetic flexible membranes to reduce the
possibility of seepage To reduce the potential of punctures in the flexible membranes
a minimum six 6 inch thick cushion layer of sand is recommended for the bottoms of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 10 Page 15
the ponds The cushion layer should be installed prior to the placement of the flexible
membrane and as per liner manufacturer s installation procedures
Although the flexible membranes are considered to be impervious there is a
possibility of seepage due to punctures in the liner or breaks in the seams Therefore
we recommend that a subdrain system be installed below the sand cushion This
subdrain should be in a herringbone pattern and be connected to main drains

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determination was performed on selected samples as an aid in
the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 to A 7 at the appropriate sample depths
Three 3 one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
Three 3 sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
gradation distribution characteristics of the in situ granular soil The test results are
summarized on Plate B 2
One 1 direct shear test was performed on remolded residual clayey soil samples
to evaluate the shear strength characteristics The test results are presented on Plate
B 3
