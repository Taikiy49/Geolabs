SITE GRADING
The project site was graded for the development of 20 individual residential lots
with associated construction of an access road and infrastructure Site grading
consisting of cuts of about 30 feet for the eastern portion of the site and fills on the
order of up to about 20 feet for the western portion of the site were needed to attain the
design grades The mass grading and infrastructure work were performed by Royal
Contracting Company Ltd A general layout of the project site is presented on the Site
Plans Plates 1 and 2

Geotechnical Engineering Report dated March 9 1990 Detailed observations and

Subdrain Installation
During the grading operation subsurface seepage was noted at various locations
across the project site Subdrains were installed and daylighted into drainage
structures or outside the grading limits for relief of seepage water The approximate
locations of the subdrains installed are shown on Plates 1 and 2
Fill Placement
During the mass grading operations representative samples of the on site and
imported soils utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of
our laboratory tests are presented on Plate 3
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by repre
sentatives from our office to observe that the grading was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the subgrades
were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
general accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Method
D 1557 The fill material was then placed in near level lifts compacted to a minimum
of 85 to 90 percent of its maximum dry density In place density tests of the fills were
performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922
Nuclear Method The test results are presented on the attached Summary of Field
Density Test Results Plates 4 1 through 4 15
HOUSE FOUNDATION AND CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
We strongly recommend that a competent geotechnical engineer be retained by
each individual lot buyer to review the house foundation plans and specifications prior
to construction to determine adherence to the general foundation recommendations
provided in our Geotechnical Engineering Report Home owners should be advised to
extend foundation sufficiently down into competent in situ stable soil and not to block
or alter surface drainage It is important that a properly designed and installed drainage

system be incorporated into the house design and construction at each individual lot
for relief of surface runoff and subterranean seepage in order to ensure proper
performance of the house foundation Professional geotechnical engineering
consultation and observation should be obtained in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements
The services of a geotechnical engineer should also be utilized during the
foundation construction and regrading of future lots to observe that the soil conditions
are consistent with our previous findings and that the required footing set back and
embedment criteria are followed The geotechnical engineer should test and approve
all additional imported fill material observe the footing excavation soft soil removal
backfill placement and recompaction and footing preparation prior to concrete
placement Our recommendations are contingent upon the above field monitoring and
confirmation
We strongly recommend that the aforementioned recommendations and
restrictions be made available to all future lot and home purchasers of this subdivision
LIMITATIONS
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or
finished grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period
without our knowledge and written acknowledgement
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of earthwork observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
the earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance
with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
