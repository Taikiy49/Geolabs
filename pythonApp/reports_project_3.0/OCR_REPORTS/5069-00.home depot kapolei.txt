INFRASTRUCTURE INSTALLATION
The earthwork operations for the driveway improvements and offsite utilities were
performed by Jayar Construction Inc from to After
clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades within the driveway improvements were
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Where necessary on site excavated coralline materials were used to raise
the existing ground surface to the design finished subgrades i e bottom of the base
course layer The coralline materials generally were placed in approximately 6 inch lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The 9 inch base course layer was placed in two equal
lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
On site excavated coralline materials were used to backfill the utility trench
excavations The backfills were placed in approximately 6 inch lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 239 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 15 of this letter report
The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site In general test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing
and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
