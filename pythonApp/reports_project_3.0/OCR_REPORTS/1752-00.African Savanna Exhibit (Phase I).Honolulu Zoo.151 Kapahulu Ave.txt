DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATION
General
In general our ten encountered borings drilled at the proposed
redevelopment site drilled a 3 to 4 foot thick layer of highly
compressible lagoonal silty clay with organic lenses below the upper 1 to
4 foot surface fill crust Beneath this upper highly compressible layer
is another 7 to 16 feet thick of very soft to very loose moderately
compressible coralline silty sands and sandy silts
Due to the underlying soft ground conditions and the high ground
water levels near the ground surface significant settlements are
anticipated particularly due to the weight of the large site grading fill
which will be required to develop the existing level for the proposed
African Savanna Exhibit
However we believe that the majority of the fill settlements would
occur fairly rapidly because of the limited thickness of the highly
compressible upper lagoonal silty clay and the granular nature of the
underlying compressible lagoonal coralline silty sand and sandy silt
To induce the majority of the ground settlements to occur quickly
prior to the initiation of foundation construction we recommend that the
required site improvement fill plus additional surcharge fill for the
proposed structure locations be placed as soon as possible The placement
of both site improvement and surcharge fillings and the subsequent
removal of surcharge fills at the structure locations should be
co ordinated and carried out with the construction sequence of new surface
improvement and surcharge fillings removal of the surcharge fill at the
proposed structure locations and then construction of the proposed
structures and the on grade slabs
We recommend construction of the structures should be delayed until
most of the ground settlement due to site improvement and surcharge fill
loads have occurred first

Foundation Recommendations
Since the design of the proposed buildings and retaining walls have
not yet been finalized details of the structures and their loadings are
unavailable However it is our understanding that the individual colum
load for the proposed structures will be small less than 5 kips
Our subsurface exploration encountered a surficial crust of fill and
volcanic deposits which is generally limited in thickness 2 5 to 4
feet Shallow spread or continuous footing bearing on the upper crust
may be used for foundation support of the lightly loaded structures
However estimated footing settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be moderately high probably on the order of about 3 inch
total and 1 5 inch differential Therefore we believe that a surcharge
program should be considered for the project to induce maximum ground
settlement to occur prior to the construction of building foundations
retaining walls and on grade slabs
In general spread footings should also be placed at a level as high
as possible to reduce the footing load applied to the underlying
compressible deposits It is recommended however that the footing size
be restricted to maximum width of 4 feet and the footing bottom be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside finished
grade Foundations exceeding the above criteria and or extending below
the firm fill crust may require special design due to close proximity of
the soft subsoils and high water level Special footing design may
consist of 2 foot over excavation below the footing bottom and replaced
with compacted gravel confined by geotextile fabric over soft loose
lagoonal deposit

Site Filling
Based on our findings we believe that significant ground
settlements will occur due to the weight of the new fill and the building
loads
Because of the limited thickness of the highly compressible silty
clay and the granular nature of the very loose gravelly silty sand sandy
Silt deposits we anticipate that fill settlements in most areas would
develop fairly quickly It is our opinion that probably the majority of
ground settlements would occur within the first two to three months after
the full load is applied
The maximum height of fill proposed for site grading is about 9
feet The estimated total ground settlements due to the weight of the new
fill is on the order of about 6 to 8 inches We recommend that an average
additional half a foot of site grading fill be incorporated in the fill
quantity estimate to compensate for ground settlement effects For the
surcharge program recommended the surcharge fill height over each of the
proposed structure areas should be about 10 feet

The placement of the required site fill and surcharge fill should be
carried out in two 2 increments of about 5 feet each with a one week
rest period in between This will allow the underlying soft soils to gain
strength to reduce the possibility of bearing or slope failure
We recommend that a minimum 3 months surcharge period be provided
for each site in the construction schedule A minimum of two 2
settlement gauges should be installed in each building and embankment fill
area prior to site filling Semi monthly periodic optical survey readings
of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual fill settlement rates
The readings could be taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to the
soils engineer who will evaluate the actual ground settlement conditions
and determine when construction of the proposed structures can be started
Foundation settlements have been discussed earlier at the
Foundation section
Because of the anticipated significant ground settlements we
recommend a minimum 10 foot set back of all proposed site filling
exceeding 4 feet height away from the existing on site buried structures
or underground utility such as concrete box culvert and sewer pipes which
will remain Hence it appears that the proposed fill mound water
filtration and holding structures located just northeast of the existing
culvert should be relocated per above set back recommendation
Iateral Earth Pressures
At the present time the details of the proposed retaining walls
have not been firmly established The lateral earth pressure acting on
the retaining walls will depend on the type of backfill used the extent
of the backfill material and the compactive effort required on the
material behind the structure walls Granular material such as select
sand is recommended for the backfill adjacent to the structures in order
to reduce the compactive effort required and to minimize the build up of
additional lateral earth pressures

Hippopotamus and Croccodile Viewing Structure
It is our understanding that a hippopotamus and croccodile viewing
structure with surrounding ponds will be constructed at the southwestern
portion of the site near Boring B 7 but the design concept of this
structure has not yet been firmly established

Based on our boring data and slope stability analysis we recommend
that the excavation for this structure should not be carried out below
elevation 2 feet Unsupported excavation in the soft to medium stiff
silty clay and loose saturated silty sands below water will result in
slope instability
Ponding areas should be lined with an impervious membrane to
minimize the ingress of water into the toe of the constructed slopes
It is anticipated that the foundation of this structure will be
placed in the highly compressible deposits and very close to the
groundwater table To minimize potential excessive settlements and the
possibility of ground bearing failure we recommend that 2 feet of
over excavation be carried out and the excavation should be first lined by
a geotextile fabric and then backfilled using 3B Fine gravel prior to
foundation construction Lateral support for the excavation walls and
dewatering measures may be required during construction
Utility line excavation close to the footing foundation should be
carefully coordinated to avoid potential undermining of poured footing due
to trench movements
Site Preparation
The placement of site grading and surcharge fill should be carried
out at about the same time and prior to construction of the proposed
structures Final placement of fill in an area along the eastern edge of
the project site may be delayed in order to accommodate the excess amount
of fill generated upon completion of the surcharge program
It is recommended that the existing building foundations and
pavements be removed in order to provide a uniform subgrade support for
new on grade slabs and A C pavements We recommend that the upper 6
inches of the subgrade soil beneath the new foundation concrete
on grade slabs footings and pavement be scarified moisture

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation retaining
structures and pavement design should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and written comments prior to construction to see that the

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX B
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were perfomnned on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Atterberg Limits test was performed on four selected samples of the
soils to evaluate its liquid and plastic limits The test result is shown
on Plates B 1 and B 2
Sieve analysis were performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate their grain size distribution The test results are presented on
Plates B 1 and B 2
Three consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of subsoils The test
results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 5
