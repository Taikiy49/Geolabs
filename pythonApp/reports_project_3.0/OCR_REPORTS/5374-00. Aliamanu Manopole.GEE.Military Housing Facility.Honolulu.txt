herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of a 120 foot high monopole antenna to
replace an existing monopole antenna at the Aliamanu Military Housing Facility in
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the available information the
replacement antenna will be supporting six antennas and seven dishes The proposed
120 foot monopole is also expected to resist wind at 110 MPH for link operability and
wind at 150 MPH for structural survivability

Monopole Shaft Foundations
We understand that the proposed monopole will be subjected to ground line
overturning moment and lateral forces at the foundation level Based on the
loading conditions provided and the subsurface conditions encountered we
recommend that the monopole be supported by drilled shaft foundations
Based on the preliminary design of the monopole foundation a 5 foot diameter
drilled shaft was considered in our analyses The major considerations in our
foundation analyses included the lateral loads and ground line overturning
moments induced in the shaft foundations
In general lateral load resistance for shafts is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the
shaft and induced moment in the shaft Based on the information provided by
Ohana Telcom Construction Inc we evaluated the required shaft length using
the computer software program LPILE Plus for Windows This program is a
microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded pile program
originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program solves for
deflection and bending moment along a shaft under lateral loads as a function of
depth The analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then
computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths The analyses
were performed for laterally loaded 5 foot diameter cast in place concrete shaft
installed in hard rock formation The results of our engineering analyses are

Monopole Construction Considerations
The performance of the shaft will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures As a result we recommend
that a representative from Geolabs be present to observe the installation of the
drilled shaft during construction In our opinion the following may have a
significant impact on the effectiveness and cost of the shaft foundations
The load carrying capacity of shaft depends to a large extent on the contact
between the shaft and the surrounding rock as well as the hardness of the rock
Therefore proper construction techniques are important The contractor should
exercise care in drilling or excavating the shaft hole and in placing concrete in the
hole The bottom of shaft should be cleaned of loose or disturbed material prior
to constructing the foundation The bottom should also be fairly level The
concrete should be poured neat against the excavation sidewalls Backfill
against the shaft foundation should not be allowed
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 7 to 9 inch slump range should be
used to provide close contact between the shaft and the surrounding soils The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce segregation of the
aggregates from the concrete mix and to displace disturbed material from the

Site Grading
Based on the existing topography we envision that the site grading work for the
proposed project will generally consist of minor cuts and fills
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered
fil The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
The materials generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of general
fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock
fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension If required import fill should
consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed basalt or coral The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No
200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should
have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site
for its intended use
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not
be allowed to compact the on site soils
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is
important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operation to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered and whether the exposed soil rock
conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
