INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed applied instruction building addition for Mobile Technical Unit
One MOTU 1 at Naval Station in Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to provide a one story building addition to the existing Building 1377
at the Naval Station in Pearl Harbor
The proposed building will be a concrete or CMU structure with a slab on grade
floor measuring approximately 85 by 105 feet in plan
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed building construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located adjacent to the northwest side of Building 1377 between
Club Road and Avenue A in the Naval Station in Pearl Harbor
The site is currently used as an open parking lot for Building 1377
The site is generally flat and paved with asphalt concrete pavement
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the building site were explored by drilling and
sampling four 4 borings to the depth of 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed that the site is mantled by a fill crust of medium dense to
very dense sand below an approximately 4 inch thick asphalt concrete pavement The
thickness of the fill crust was found to be less than about 5 feet in Boring and
4 but increased to about 13 feet in Boring No 2

gravel were encountered below the tuff layer
Groundwater levels were measured at about 10 0 to 17 0 feet below the existing
ground surface in the borings during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation Support
Based on the subsoils encountered it is our opinion that the medium dense
sand gravel fill crust may provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building
addition
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated due to the generally flat existing grades
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of debris rubbish old pavements and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The cleared grade should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated
and backfilled with select granular materials compacted to a minimum of 95 compaction
as recommended above
Based on the field exploration results the near surface soils may be used as a
source of fill backfill material If required imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than
20 passing the 200 sieve
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to hauling into the site
W O 1977 00 P Page 6
If required fill backfill should be placed in maximum Q inch thick loose level lifts
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The site should be graded to divert surface drainage away from the proposed
building addition
Foundations
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the existing fill crust may
be used to support the proposed building addition The footing excavations in the fill
crust should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for design of the footings
bearing on the compacted foundation subgrade The footing bearing value is for total
dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one third for seismic wind
and or other short duration loading considerations
The new footing foundations should be placed at the same level as the existing
adjacent footings of Building 1377 In general a minimum footing embedment of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
If a footing foundation is located next to a sewer drainage easement the footing
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1977 00 P Page 7
Lateral loads acting on the new building may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the footings and the compacted footing subgrade
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation system is
well compacted The passive earth pressure in the upper 12 inches of soil below the
finished grade should be neglected unless the ground surface is paved A coefficient of
0 4 may be used to calculate the frictional resistance
Slab on Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade floor will be used for the new building
A 4 inch layer of base course gravel compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 should be provided below the slabs as cushion
fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture
barrier is recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
New utility lines may be needed for the new building A granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine is recommended for
placement under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The backfill within one
foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the
pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill above the one foot thick lightly tamped
zone should be placed in maximum 9 inch loose level lifts and mechanically compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 If
the ground surface above the trench will be paved the pavement subgrade or the final
g inch loose level lift should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given
herein if this review is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
it is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the foundation construction of the project This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by scaling from a site
plan provided by the Department of the Navy The site plan dated is
marked with an alternate layout of the proposed building The physical locations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the Department of the Navy
and its design consultants for specific application to the site grading and foundation
design of the new building in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineers in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
in contract bid estimation A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain
a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in
the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimation purposes
