COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
REMOVAL ACTION AT QUARRY LOCH SITE
PEARL HARBOR OAHU HAWAII
Dear Mr Alsop
This report summarizes our compaction testing services performed during the
earthwork operation of the Removal Action at Quarry Loch Site project from
through May 2 2002 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
August 28 2001
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The Quarry Loch Site is located in the Pearl Harbor Naval Complex on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project site is located along the eastern portion of the Quarry Loch
within a paved parking area makai of North Road and south of Millican Field The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map and Site
Plan Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The backfilling and compaction of the collection sump trenches were performed
at the project site The backfill for the collection sump trenches were placed in loose
lifts of about 6 to 8 inch and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the backfilling and compaction operation of the collection sump trenches
samples of the imported material utilized as backfill were obtained and transported to our
office for testing The maximum dry densities optimum moisture contents and grain size
distribution were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 1557 Modified Proctor C 117 and C 136 Grain size distribution The
test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

Periodic field density tests were performed during the backfilling operation A total
of 30 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and sand cone
method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1556 The results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 2 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
using a tape measure from features shown on the construction plans These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Our services consisted of on call compaction testing and laboratory testing services
during the earthwork construction for the above project
