EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
AREA 19B PHASE 2
EWA BY GENTRY EAST
Dear Mr Brant
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading and roadway and utility construction for the Ewa by
Gentry Area 19B Phase 2 development from August 3 2001 to March 1 2002 in
accordance with our fee proposal dated August 20 2001
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the eastern portion of the Ewa by Gentry
Residential Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located east of Fort Weaver Road south of Area 19A Phases 2 and 3 and
west of Area 19B Phase 1 and includes a total of 25 residential lots and related access
roads A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for the Area 19B Phase 2 project were
observed by Geolabs Inc from August 3 2001 to March 1 2002 The site grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of
about 2 to 5 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the project The site
grading was performed by RHS Lee Inc and the utility installation work was performed by
Albert C Kobayashi Inc During the site grading operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 14 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references
