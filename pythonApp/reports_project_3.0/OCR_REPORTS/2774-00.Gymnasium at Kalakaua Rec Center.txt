INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of an up dated review of the soil boring data
presented in our previous soils report dated This up dated
geotechnical engineering report contains recommendations reflecting current geotechnical
engineering principles and practices and current development plan The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for final construction cost estimates or for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing gymnasium and to construct a new
gymnasium at Kalakaua Recreation Center for the Department of Parks and Recreation
City and County of Honolulu
It is our understanding that the proposed gymnasium will have a metal roofing and
a partial second floor storage area Asphalt concrete pavement for a new parking area
is also planned for the proposed development
Construction drawings for the proposed project are transmitted to us dated

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site in the Kalakaua Recreation Center is located at 720 McNeill Street
near the intersection with Kaumualii Street in the Kalihi district of Honolulu on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The Kalakaua Recreation Center is located south of the Kalihi Kai
Elementary School
The project site is presently occupied by an existing gymnasium situated next to
existing Boxing gymnasium Recreation building and locker rooms A courtyard area and
a basketball court are located northerly of the project site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The emerged reefs of Oahu are more extensive than those of the other Hawaiian
Islands and constitute an important aspect of the geology The site is on an old Honolulu
Plain which like much of the rest of southern edge of Oahu is underlain by a broad
elevated coral reef partly covered by alluvium carried from the Koolau Range Lava flows
of the Honolulu Series may be found interbedded with the reef deposits since they were
formed about the same time
The reefs at the site area appear to have been formed during the 25 foot ocean
level and were later covered by river deposits from the Kalihi stream systems The site
designated a change in energy level of the stream Part of the site is underlain by
bouldery deposits which may be boundaries of ancient river banks
Field Exploration
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling eight 8 borings to
depths ranging from 3 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The exploration program
was performed on February 1975 for the previously proposed development scheme
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by 2 to 6 feet of stiff to
very stiff clayey silt except in the vicinity of Borings 3A to 3C where boulders were
encountered at depths of about 1 5 to 7 0 feet
The upper clayey silt layer is underlain by dense to hard tan white cemented coral
sand and gravel generally extending to a depth of 25 feet which is the maximum depth

of the drilled borings Basalt rock was encountered beneath the cemented coral sand
and gravel at a depth of 22 feet in Boring 4
Groundwater level was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about 21 to
22 feet at the time of the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value up to 3 500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
the in situ stiff to very stiff clayey silt This value may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered A minimum footing embedment of
eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
If loose soft soil lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to in situ stiff clayey silt or dense cemented
coral sand and gravel The resulting depression should then be backfilled with select
granular material with each 6 inch lift compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2774 00 Page 6
Due to the heterogeneous nature of the bouldery deposits they will not provide
uniform foundation support Foundations in the vicinity of Borings 3A to 3C or in any area
where boulders are encountered footing excavations should extend below the boulder
layer so that the footings can either bear upon the underlying cemented coral sand and
gravel or on a minimum 12 inch of compacted backfill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance with the above
recommendations will have negligible settlements probably less than 1 4 inch total
footing settlement
Foundation Probing
Due to time constraint and existing building location additional exploratory borings
within the proposed gymnasium footprint could not be implemented in the present scope
of work However it is recommended that after demolition of the existing gymnasium
probe borings of about 15 feet deep or two 2 times the footing dimension should be
drilled at major footing locations as part of construction monitoring The probe borings
will confirm the competency of the upper foundation bearing layer and detect potential
voids cavities in the underlying cemented coral sand and gravel layer
Probes should be drilled at each major individual footing location and every ten
10 feet on centers along the wall footing location The probes should be a minimum of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2774 00 Page 7
three 3 inches in diameter and should be drilled to a minimum depth of fifteen 15 feet
or equal to two 2 times the footing width below the footing bottom
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of cavities the probe holes
should then be filled with neat cement grout injected at low to moderate pump pressures
from a grout pump If the volume of grout injected exceeds four 4 times the calculated
volume of the probe hole the grout mix should be adjusted to a one 1 part of sand to
one 1 part of cement mixture
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
the project geotechnical engineer to monitor the presence of voids and to allow additional
recommendations to be made if changed ground conditions occur and or if large voids
are encountered
Slab on Grade
Laboratory testing in the previous exploration program indicated that the near
surface soils were non expansive Therefore slab on grade construction can be utilized
without special precaution such as subgrade over excavation
It is recommended that all subgrades to receive slabs on grade be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches then the scarified surface brought to slightly wet of optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any boulders or hard spots
encountered during scarifying operations should be removed and backfilled with
compacted granular material
It is recommended that all floor slabs with moisture sensitive floor coverings be
protected by a minimum of six 6 inch thick cushion and capillary break layer of No 3B
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2774 00 Page 8
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel A plastic vapor barrier on top of the cushion
and capillary break layer is also recommended One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be
placed above the plastic membrane to help curing of the concrete
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Pavements
It is our understanding that asphalt concrete pavements will be utilized for the new
parking area
Based on our previous experience in the project area and the assumption that
anticipated traffic will be composed of light vehicles the following preliminary pavement
section is recommended
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
6 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Compaction
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
It is recommended laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade during construction to verify the above design and the above preliminary design
section can be revised if necessary
Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris old footings construction rubbles and organic
matters encountered during grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly
disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2774 00 Page 9
The proposed gymnasium and parking areas should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches after site clearing and grubbing The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly
moisture conditioned to slightly above the optimum moisture content and then
recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Drainage
The finished grades outside the footings slabs and pavements should be shaped
to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that
a gutter system be installed around the building and that the gutter run off water be
diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Pianters next to the foundations snouid be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made in
accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering principles and
practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or implied
This report has been prepared in order to assist the architect and engineer in the
preparation of the foundation design of this project Therefore this report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information for use to form the basis for preparation
of construction cost estimates or for contract bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this
project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation
of this report and or performance of site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings performed on February 1975 for the previously
proposed development Variations of conditions between the field borings may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs

subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that foundation probing footing excavations and backfilling be
observed by a competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to being transported to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
