Project Consideration
The project site is situated in the District of North Kona northeasterly of Keahole
Airport on the island of Hawaii It is a portion of the Ka u Ahupuaa It is proposed to
subdivide the site into agricultural residence lots
To aid in the collection and diversion of stormwater run off it is proposed to
construct seepage pits and other drainage features at the project site Therefore in situ
percolation tests are needed for the design of these structures
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our service was to perform shallow subsurface percolation tests
at various locations within the project site to obtain permeability data pertinent to the
design of drainage structures In order to accomplish this we have performed the
following tasks and work efforts

Percolation Testing
Three 3 shallow pits were excavated to depths of about 2 to 3 feet below the
existing ground surface with plan dimensions of about 3 to 6 5 feet by using a bulldozer
at the project site Test pit and 3 are located at Station 150 40
and 122 20 respectively on road A Clinker gravel and cobbles with a little silt were
generally encountered near the surface during the pit excavation A 3 700 gallon water
truck was used to inject clear fresh water into the excavated pits for percolation testing
A paddle wheel flow sensor was used to monitor the flow rate Flow rates for the
percolation tests ranged from about 185 to 150 gallons per minute gpm The
percolation test duration was about 14 to 30 minutes During the injection of water the
changes in water level in the excavated pits were recorded with respect to time and rate
of injection
The calculated coefficient of permeability of the shallow subsurface soils at the
project site was in the range of 7 to 20 feet per minute corresponding to between 50 and
150 gallons per square feet per minute The detailed results of these tests are presented
on Plates 1 through 3

Based on the percolation test results it is our opinion that a permeability coefficient
of 15 gallons per square feet per minute may be used for the design of drainage
structures at shallow depths within 2 to 3 feet of the ground surface at the project site
Limitations
The findings and analyses submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as test pits Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until operation of the constructed drainage structures if
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
