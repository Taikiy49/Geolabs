INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation for the proposed clubhouse at the Ala Wai Golf Course The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new clubhouse as a part of the
improvements to the existing Ala Wai Golf Course The proposed building
will be a two story concrete structure with approximate plan dimensions of
120 by 360 feet The design column loads from the structure have been
determined by the project structural engineer to be about 140 to 166 kips
It is our understanding that the site will be raised about 18 inches
due to flood considerations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the foundation design
recommendations of the proposed clubhouse
Our scope of work included but was not limited to Grilling and
sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging from 24 5 to 38 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The results of our field exploration and
laboratory tests are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is within the Ala Wai Golf Course The golf course
is located in the Kaimuki district of Honolulu Hawaii and is bounded by
Date Street to the north Kapahulu Avenue to the east Manoa Palolo
Drainage Channel to the west and Ala Wai Canal to the south The proposed
clubhouse site is in the southeastern portion of the golf course as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site is generally level
with a ground surface elevation of about 4 5 feet

The site is currently occupied by an existing clubhouse It is our
understanding that the existing clubhouse will be demolished for the
construction of the new building

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered a 2 foot thick layer of
soft clayey silt at shallow depth 2 feet below the existing ground
surface underlain by silty sand and gravel lagoonal deposits of about 20
to 25 feet in thickness The consistency of the lagoonal deposits graded
from medium dense to very loose between 10 and 25 feet below the ground
surface Coral algal reef formation was encountered at depths of 25 to 30
feet
Both spread footings and pile foundations have been evaluated for the
proposed structure
Foundation Support
Spread Footing
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed clubhouse if
some settlements are tolerable
Due to the close proximity about 2 feet below the ground surface of
the organic soil to the proposed new footing we recommend that this
layer be over excavated below each footing and replaced with select
granular material to reduce potential long term settlement and
increase the bearing capacity of the footing subgrade The required
depth of over excavation varies with local thickness of the organic
material but is generally expected to be about 4 feet below the
existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1917 00 Page 5
Dk No 41
The excavation should be backfilled with non expansive select borrow
material placed in 12 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted to
90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
If groundwater is encountered in the excavation the excavation
should first be backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to a level at least
12 inches above the groundwater level This layer of 3B Fine gravel
should then be capped with select borrow compacted as described
above
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade is recommended Spread footings of relatively large size may
be used to reduce the contact pressure For a footing size of 10 by
10 feet assuming an allowable bearing pressure of 1500 p s f the
estimated total and differential settlements are on the order of 1 0
and 0 5 inches respectively For a footing size of 15 by 15 feet
the anticipated total and differential settlements are on the order
of 0 75 and 0 25 inch respectively
If the estimated amount of settlements are not acceptable for the
proposed structure then we recommend that pile foundations be
utilized
Pile Foundation
n i mE
Foundations bearing on the hard coral algal reef may be utilized for
the structure
The piles should penetrate the compressible upper soils and loose
lagoonal deposits and bear on the hard coral algal reef formation
Sixteen and one half inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles are
recommended The piles should extend to the hard coral formation

W O 1917 00 Page 6
Dk No 41
anticipated at a depth of about 25 to 30 feet below the existing
ground surface However greater depths may be required locally
since coral algal formations usually contain weak zones and cavities
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimm of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 100 blows
per foot for the last foot or 10 blows per inch for the last 2
inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 100 tons
per pile may be used Pile settlements are expected on the order of
less than one half of an inch
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven to competent bearing formation
To resist lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the pile
caps and tie beams may be used We recommend that an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth be
used for passive soil resistance The backfill against the pile caps
or tie beams should be properly backfilled with select materials
compacted to a dry density of at least 95 maximm density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Site Grading
Fills of about 18 inches are planned due to flood considerations
These fills may initiate ground settlements on the order of about 1 to 2
inches Therefore we recommend that the fills be placed as soon as
possible and the foundation construction be delayed until most of the
settlements have occurred We estimate that a 2 to 3 month waiting
period may be required for the settlements to occur Settlement gauges
should be installed at the commencement of earthwork to monitor the
settlement rate and determine when foundation construction may begin

It is our understanding that the existing old clubhouse will be
demolished for construction of the new building At the on set of
earthwork the existing A C pavement areas within the proposed building
and new grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any
unsuitable materials such as old footings vegetation and rubbish should
be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 95 maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any
loose or wet spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with suitable imported non expansive material
compacted to the above mentioned standard
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from all foundations
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3B Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
slabs
The bottom of the slab area including the thickened edges should be
kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away
from the slab perimeter
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our exploration the preliminary pavement
design is as follows

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written coment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can not be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
May occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of project
designers for specific application to the site grading and foundation
design of the project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or
implied is made
