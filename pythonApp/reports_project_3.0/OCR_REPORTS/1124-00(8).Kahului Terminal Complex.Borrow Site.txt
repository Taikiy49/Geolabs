PRELIMINARY BORROW SITE EVALUATION
KAHULUI TERMINAL COMPLEX EVALUATION
This report presents the results of our preliminary borrow site
evaluation for the future construction of the Kahului Airport Terminal
Complex expansion which will require a substantial amount of new fill
material The investigation was performed in accordance with our
proposal dated and consisted of excavating and sampling
six test pits at the proposed borrow site visual observations and
laboratory field testing
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed borrow site is located to the southwest of the
existing Kahului Airport Terminal Complex and is roughly bisected by
the centerline extension of Runway 2 20 The area is between Hemaloa
Street and Hana Highway and is composed of a low knoll which falls
gently toward the northeast and southwest

The northern area of the proposed borrow area is an abandoned
Sugar canefield and the southwestern portion is a canefield which was
recently harvested

Geology
The geological setting of the site has been previously described in
our reports prepared for Airside and Landside improvements of
the Kahului Aiport Terminal Complex Expansion projects dated June
24 1982 and March 11 1983 respectively
In general thick andesitic basalt rocks of Kula volcanic series
lava overlies the older alluvial soils at the site The surface of
this andesitic basalt has been weathered and decomposed into
residual soil of reddish brown silts that mantle the area

Soil Conditions
In general our test pits encountered the following materials
Topsoil Very stiff brown silt with some Clay and organic
matter ranging from 0 5 feet to 1 5 feet in thickness
Residual Soil Very stiff to hard reddish brown silt with some
clay ranging in thickness from 1 0 feet to 4 5 feet in
thickness except in Test Pit 4 where it was not encountered
Clinker Saprolite Very stiff to hard mottled dark brown silt
with some sand and weathered gravel ranging in thickness between
1 5 feet to 4 5 feet
Basalt Hard gray andesitic basalt at depths between 2 5 feet
and 9 5 feet with an average depth of 5 5 feet

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings the soil and weathered clinker materials
underlying the site are only 3 to 5 5 feet thick Dense hard
andesitic basalt occurs below the surface soil and or weathered
clinker layer The andesitic basalt will require blasting to excavate
and have to be processed through a crusher in order to be acceptable
for fill
The following recommendations only apply to the upper soil
portion which can be readily excavated for use as fill material
In general our study indicated that the soil materials are of
variable and probably not a very high quality for use as borrow
materials and require special handling before being used in
construction The in place densities of the materials encountered
p c f The four California Bearing Ratio tests performed indicated a
range of bearing ratios between 6 5 and 14
Stripping
If the area investigated is to be used as a borrow site it is
recommended that the 10 to 15 inches of topsoil be stripped from the
site and not be used as fill material This topsoil should be
stockpiled for use in landscaping or disposed of off site

Roadway and Apron Fill
It appears at this time that the soil from the borrow site would
not meet the minimum CBR value of 15 required for roadway subgrade
Some of the material may not meet the minimum CBR value of 8 required
under apron area In order to utilize the pavement recommendations
presented in our previous reports the materials from the proposed
borrow site should not be used any closer than 2 feet from finished
pavement grade or shoulders including those in the apron area A
higher grade material with a minimum CBR of 15 which may consist of
other imported borrow may be required to prepare the remainder of the
fill to the subgrade level of the roadway and parking area pavements
On the other hand it is possible that as the excavation
progresses the quality of soils such as the saprolitic clinker just
above the andesitic basalt encountered in Test Pits 4 and 6 might
improve with depth Provision should be made to segregate higher
quality material for subgrade use if significant quantities are
encountered
Some boulders and rock fragments may also result from the borrow
operations Rock fragments up to 8 inches in size may be incorporated
in the fill provided that the rock particles are well surrounded in
the soil matrix Oversized rock should not be used in fill

Foundation Support
No specific restriction is required for the borrow site material
as fill under the building area We believe that the proposed fill
from this borrow source will provide an allowable soil bearing
pressure of 3 000 p s f when placed and compacted according to the
recommendations presented previously in our report dated March 11
1983 prepared for the Landside improvement portion of the new
Kahului airport terminal complex project
Slab On Grade Support
The laboratory CBR tests generally indicate that the soils fron
this borrow site generally have a low swelling potential It is noted
at the time of exploration that the in place moisture contents were on
the dry side of the optimum moisture content It appears that borrow
fill soil should be moisture conditioned prior to compaction during
the grading operation
Slab on grade may be constructed on this borrow fill material
However the subgrade fill material should be pre moistened prior to
concrete placement to minimize the swelling potential due to excessive
drying In addition on grade slabs should be provided with thickened
edges to reduce soil moisture fluctuation The thickened edges
abutting an open unpaved area should extend down to at least 18
inches below the lowest adjacent grade or 8 inches if abutting a
paved area

Drainage
To minimize excessive moisture entering into the exposed ground
from rain good site drainage should be maintained during the borrow
operation
Since the soils at this borrow site is Silty and erosive the
ground cover should be stripped only as necessary according to the
immediate borrow requirements This will minimize loss of materials
due to excessive erosion Such a limitation would also improve dust
control
Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing stripping and grubbing
operations be monitored carefully to avoid contamination excessive
stripping and to control erosion
Our tests indicate that the quality of the materials at the
borrow site varies To achieve maximum utilization of the borrow fill
for roadway or slab on grade construction follow up inspection and
observation of the excavated soils would be required by a qualified
personnel

Since the extent of our field exploration is limited to six test
pits the quality of overburden soils above the andesitic basalt may
vary significantly from those tested during the actual borrow
operation Periodic monitoring and testing of the encountered
materials should be made by qualified personnel in the field If
lower quality materials are encountered these should be placed in the
lower portion of the general fill such as in the parking areas If
higher quality materials are encountered these may be placed in the
subgrade zone to obtain a better support and therefore more economical
pavement sections Such flexibility would be desirable in scheduling
fill placement
