Dear Mr Shigetomi
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
the swelling potential of the soils reviewing of the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story split level wooden
structure approximately 46 by 80 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car
garage Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire structure

The front half of the house will require up to 4 5 feet of fill in
order to match with the present grade at the front of the lot
MU retaining walls are planned to provide grade separation between
the front and the rear portion of the house and also along the entire
lot perimeter
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Aloali i Street
in the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii The
lot has two generally level pads with a transitional slope measuring
about 5 1 H V between them the lower pad is located at the rear of
the site There is an existing concrete lined drainage ditch located
at the rear of the site and running parallel to the western boundary

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
SAR EEN ANY RGCUMMENDAT LONS
The field exploration disclosed that in general the stiff reddish
brown clayey silt will be suitable for foundation support of the
proposed residence provided some precautions are taken during
construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that swelling potential will increase if this material is
allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize future swelling potential
the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and protected
from drying during the period of time required for preparation prior
to final concrete placement
The bottom of all footing excavations should be examined by a soils
engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been reached

Foundations
It is anticipated that up to 4 5 feet of new fill will be required
under the front half of the house foundation We feel that the key
element regarding the foundation design for this area will be the type
and amount of new fill material to be used
We recommend that select non expansive soils such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral should be used for fill within the upper two
2 feet of the house finished grade This would provide a competent
bearing layer for the house foundations in this portion of the house
The garage and the rear half of the house will be close to the
existing lot grade and therefore will bear on the existing soils

the following sections

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and driveway
Where a minimum of 2 feet of imported non expansive soil such as
crusher waste is used for the fill conventional slab on grade design
with an 8 inch deep perimeter may be used
Where on site fill soil is used a deeper 18 inch footing embedment
and special subgrade preparation with lime saturation is recommended
The general procedure listed hereunder should be followed
1 The slab subgrade soils should be saturated with perforated hoses
by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying
subsoil

should also be followed for the Sidewalk driveway and patio

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the Slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system should be
installed around the house and that the gutter water should be
diverted far away from the footings and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 11
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be hon expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
