Gentlemen
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed three story office building and warehouse conm
plex in Kakaako Honolulu Hawaii
The purposes of our investigation were to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site and present recommendations for design
and construction of foundation
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed commercial complex covers an area approximately
52 000 square feet on the Ewa portion of the block bounded by
Queen Cooke Kawaiahao and Emily Streets in Kakaako Honolulu
Hawaii The site location is shown on Plate l

The proposed structure consists of a three story office building
fronting on Queen Street with one and two decks of parking over
the warehouse portion of the complex The column loads will be
about 300 kips for the warehouse and 800 kips for the office
building
FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of seven borings ranging in depth
from 13 to 26 feet The drilling was accomplished using truck
mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight hollow
stem augers or weight driven A rods The borings were super
vised by a geologist from our firm The approximate boring
locations are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented
in Appendix A
In six borings either the Standard Penetration Test or 245 inch
O D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals The
Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a two inch O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling
a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of the
relative density or consistency of a soil The number of blows
required to drive the ring sampler for 12 inches was converted to

testing Boring No 4 consisted of continuous penetration with
an A rod of a two inch O D washer attached to the tip driven
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
GEOLOGY
The proposed building site is located within the Honolulu Harbor
area Situated on the southwest flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range forming the southeast portion of Oahu Hawaii
Honolulu Harbor borders on and participated in much of the
Pearl Harbor embayment s geologic history During the Late
Pleistocene Epoch coral algal reefs grew in the shallow probably
less than 100 feet deep slowly submerging embayment Influxes
of clastic non marine alluvial debris clays and silts appar
ently smothered most of the reef allowing only a few patch reefs
to grow Fluctuations of sea level accompanied by pyroclastic
vulcanism from Mt Tantalus about 5 000 years ago finally gave
the harbour its latest natural configuration Recent fill
_operations have given Honolulu Harbor its present outline

SITE CONDITIONS
The commercial building complex site is presently occupied by
a cluster of structures and sheds The interior of the existing
warehouse is covered with concrete slabs The site is fairly
level
The majority of the site is immediately underlain by a four inch
thick concrete pavement In some areas the concrete has a
six inch thick gravel base but generally is directly on fill
This fill consists of gravel sand and clay with some small
amounts of debris The density of this fill does not appear to
be uniform throughout its four foot thickness
Below the fill soft wet sandy clays containing coral and shell
fragments were encountered These old bay muds average about
four feet in thickness Underlying the bay muds are dense
black sands composed of tuff and cinder fragments It varies
from three and one half to six feet in thickness and was
encountered in most borings at about eight foot depths This
black sand layer rests on coral reef debris which consists of
light colored hard cemented shell and coral sands The coral
reef was found at depths of 11 to 14 feet in all borings and
extended to the limits of our borings Groundwater was found
from 3 5 to 7 5 feet below the existing surface and may be sub
ject to tidal fluctuations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
GENERAL
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the planned
construction with columns supported on pile foundations bearing
on the shallow coral reef Consideration was given to other
types of foundations including spread footings supported on the
dense sand formation found at about 8 foot depths or a mat
foundation above the present grade Dewatering would be required
for spread footings due to the high water table and therefore
may be uneconomical compared to a short pile foundation system
The variation of density in the upper fill will preclude the use
of a mat foundation
The proposed slab on grade may be supported on fill bearing
directly on the present concrete slab
PILE FOUNDATIONS
We recommend that the proposed structure be supported on pre
stressed concrete piles driven to dense coral material which
was encountered at about 11 to 14 feet below the present ground
surface The depth of this hard coral may vary depending on
the profile of the coral formation
For twelve inch square prestressed concrete used allowable
Page 6
W O 485 00
bearing capacity of 80 tons each shall be used Piles should
be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum rate of energy of
15 000 foot pounds per blow The final driving resistance
using this rate of energy should be on the order of 180 blows
for the last foot of penetration or no more than 25 blows per
inch for the last two inches Our borings indicated that the
coral ledge is hard However in view of the uncertainties
involved in pile driving in coral some allowance should be
made for the presence of pukas or soft pockets in the coral
material
We recommend that a pile load test be conducted prior to the
commencement of pile driving operations It is also recommended
that some 8 to 12 test piles be driven prior to actual produc
tion driving in order to verify the pile lengths We will be
glad to assist you in determining the test pile locations
We anticipate that total settlements will be less than one half
inch with differential settlements on the order of one quarter
inch
LATERAL CAPACITIES
The lateral capacity of the recommended piles will be six kips
per pile Resistance to lateral loads may also be provided by
friction of pile caps and grade beams A coefficient of friction
of 0 4 may be used between concrete and site soils

SLAB ON GRADE
The upper fill will support concrete slab on grade provided
the upper foot is compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum
density
However as an alternate the existing concrete slab may be
left in place with a minimum of 18 inches of granular material
placed between the proposed and existing slabs This granular
material should be compacted to a minimum of 90 density
Drainage should be provided for this granular material
If the alternate recommendation is followed this will require
breaking through the slab only for pile penetration Care
should be taken so that the concrete is not damaged beyond the
pile caps
All slab on grade should be structurally separated from
columns

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained fron
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70

Subject Supplementary Foundation Recommendations
Proposed 3 Story Office Building Warehouse Complex

Gentlemen
This is to confirm our recent telephone conversation with Mr S
Kuniyoshi of Inc pertaining to the allowable
foundation bearing capacities for the subject structures
It is presently proposed to utilize shallow spread footings bear
ing within the fill material 1 to 2 feet below the existing con
crete slab Our analysis indicates that foundations at this level
Should be designed for maximum bearing pressures of 1 500 pounds
per square foot The estimated settlements are 1 inch total and
4s inch differential
The foundation excavations should be inspected by a soil engineer
prior to concreting Soft or loose soils disclosed at this time
should be removed or compacted in place
Due to the magnitude of the anticipated settlements the grouting
of the column base plates should be delayed as long as possible to
permit the initial settlements to occur The column base plates
can then be raised to the correct level and grouted in place which
will compensate for the initial settlements
Should you have any questions concerning our observations or con
clusions please do not hesitate to contact us

This letter is to verify the telephone conversation of February 5
1974 between Mr Kuniyoshi and the writer regarding lateral pressures
for the project structure
Resistance to lateral loading may be resisted by passive pressures
friction along base of grade beams or by the piles Passive pressures
can be considered equal to the pressure developed by a fluid with
a density of 250 pounds per cubic foot On exterior footings the
upper foot of soil should not be considered in developing passive
pressures
All backfill against grade beams and pile caps should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density as per ASTM D 1557 72T
