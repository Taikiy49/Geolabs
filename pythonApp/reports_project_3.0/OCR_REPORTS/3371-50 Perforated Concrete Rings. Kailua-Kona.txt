Injection Test Results
6 Foot Diameter Perforated Concrete Rings
Kailua Kona Hawaii
In general accordance with our proposal dated June 7th we have performed an
injection test for the 6 foot diameter perforated concrete rings on the island of Hawaii
The following is a summary of the injection test results performed at the site
The purpose of the injection test on the perforated concrete ring is to evaluate its
infiltration capacity The concrete ring tested is 6 feet in diameter by 4 feet high The
concrete ring has 3 to 4 inch diameter conical openings with a total outflow area of about
424 7 square inches The total outflow area was based on information provided by your
office A plan view and cross section of the concrete ring tested are provided on Plate 1
The injection test was conducted using the constant head method Prior to the
injection test the concrete ring was seated on an impermeable surface and sealed at the
bottom During the injection test water was pumped into the concrete ring through a flow
meter which measured the rate of water flow The depth to the water surface from the
top of the concrete ring the flow rate and time elapsed were recorded during the test
The results of our injection test are presented on Plate 2
Based on the injection test results the concrete ring tested has a calculated
coefficient of permeability of about 0 0327 feet per second Based on the calculated
coefficient of permeability of about 0 0327 feet per second we extrapolated the results of
the injection test to a 20 foot high column of concrete rings with no infiltration through the
bottom of the concrete ring column Based on the calculated coefficient of permeability
