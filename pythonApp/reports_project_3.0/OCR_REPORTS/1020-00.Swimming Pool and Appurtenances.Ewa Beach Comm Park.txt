As requested we have performed a soil engineering inves
tigation at the referenced site to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions in order to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the proposed improvements
Our soils investigation which consisted of drilling four 4
short borings with a rotary hydraulic truck mounted drill
rig was performed in accordance with our proposal dated
One boring was drilled in each location for the swimming
pool new shower facility future training school and in the
existing parking lot Visual observation of the site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is flat and grassy Basket ball volley
ball and tennis courts including a building exist on the
proposed site
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed improvements consists of a swimming pool
shower facility and future training school Cesspool or
leaching fields would be required for the new shower faci
lities and swimming pool
SOIL CONDITION
The soil encountered in the borings consisted basically of
brown sandy silt and corals
Laboratory tests indicate that the brown sandy and coral
soils have none to very low swell potential Groundwater
was encountered in the borings at various depths More
detailed description of the soils encountered in the borings
are presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent soils at the pro
ject site From a soils engineering point of view the pro
posed improvements are feasible at the site
Shallow spread footing foundations can be used for the
proposed structures
Site Grading
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris and
other deleterious material The ground should be scarified
to a minimum depth of six 6 inches brought to near opti
mum moisture content and then compacted to a minimum of 90
density per ASTM D 1557 test method
Fill and backfill materials should consist of select borrow
or broken up on site coral with less than 6 inch maximum
size They should be placed in 8 inch horizontal loose
lifts and then compacted to 90 maximum density per ASTM
D 1557 test method

Foundations
For the light loadings anticipated an allowable bearing
value of 4500 p s f is recommended for spread footing foun
dation bearing on the white coral formation
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
adjacent ground surface
The site should also be graded to drain water away from the
building foundations
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the foot
ing should extend to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce Footing settle
ment due to settlement of the trench backfill
Settlements are anticipated to be minimum for footings bear
ing on the dense coral layers
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
thickened edge around the Slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface

The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be
allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement
Parking Lot Pavement
Based on laboratory CBR test results on the on site coral
material the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade soil
Additional CBR tests may be required on the actual subgrade
soil during construction and the above design section may
have to be revised if necessary
Swimming Pool
It is anticipated that the swimming pool excavation will be
in dense white coral formation which will provide excellent
Support for the pool structure The swimming pool wall may
be designed to resist lateral earth pressures equal to an
equivalent fluid pressure of 32 p ca f

Since water level exists at about 7 foot depth below the
existing ground surface the pool structure would be sub
jected to uplift pressure when the pool is drained There
fore the pool should be designed to resist such pressures
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recom
mendations made in accordance with generally accepted soil
and foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied
The recommendations contained in this report are our con
sidered opinions based on careful observation of the soil
revealed at the soil test boring locations and on results of
laboratory tests performed on soil samples obtained from the
borings
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by borings The unforeseen con
ditions such as cavities expansive soil pockets and dis
continuity of underlying coral may occur in localized
areas Additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed structure
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible costs
