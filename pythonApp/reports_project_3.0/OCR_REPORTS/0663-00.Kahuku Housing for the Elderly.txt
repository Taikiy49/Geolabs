INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation and
recommendations for design and construction of the proposed one story
family housing development Also presented are the results and discussion
of the percolation testing for use in design of a shallow depth cesspool
system for the project
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed Kahuku Housing for The Elderly project is located
southwest of Kahuku Golf Course makai of Kamehameha Highway in Kahuku
Oahu Hawaii
Page 2
W
The site is presently occupied by Kahuku Housing Corporation residential
units located on the north and west areas of the site There is an abandoned
bunker with 2 large gun placements and underground facilities located in
the southeast half of the project area
The site is generally flat along the north and west edges and slopes
up to the bunker area in the south Existing slope ratios are about 3
horizontal to 1 vertical
Existing roadways outline the project area Vegetation is dense along
the eastern roadway with ironwood trees in the center and Keawe growth
toward the south The central portion of the site is presently cultivated
as a fenced community garden located between and over the abandoned
structures

GEOLOGY
The Kahuku Housing for The Elderly site is underlain by calcareous
soil of uniform grained sand and sea shell fragments deposited by wind
and wave action of the Jancas series The soil may be associated with
soils of the geographical regions of Pulehu Mokuleia Kaloko and Lualualei
According to the U S Soil Conservation Service s Soil Survey of
Islands of Molokai and Lanai State of Hawaii
August 1972 the site soil type is described as follows
JaC Jaucas Sand SP low shrink swell potential
SOIL CONDITIONS
Our exploration disclosed uniform soil conditions over the entire
site Generally a calcareous sand covers the site ranging from a loose
silty sand at the surface to a medium dense sand from depths 1 to 2 feet
below the surface The surface soils contain some organic material
and are light brown in color

PERCOLATION TESTS
Percolation tests were performed in and 2 Due to
caving of the wells the 4 inch diameter boring holes had a 4 inch diameter

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The proposed housing development is feasible from a soils and foundation
engineering standpoint The on site sand can support the proposed single
story residential housing development
Site Grading
The calcareous on site sand is non expansive and therefore it can be
used as fill material for the lowlying area
Before the start of grading work the site should be stripped of
vegetation and existing surface structures which include the existing housing
and abandoned bunkers All debris accumulated from grubbing should be
removed from the site
If any abandoned cesspools are located during construction all the
loose sludge material should be removed and replaced with on site sand
Water tamping may be used to compact the backfill The underground structures
in the bunker area should have positive drainage established at the bottom
before placing any compacted fill This is a precautionary step to prevent
the structures from acting as underground reservoirs for rain or groundwater
activity in the area
In areas where filling is required the existing ground surface should
be scarified and water added if necessary to achieve near optimum moisture
content prior to compaction This existing ground surface layer should then
Page 6
W
be rolled to achieve compaction at a minimum of 90 of its maximum density
as established by the test method Fill layers can then
be placed moistened and compactéd in 9 inch lifts at 90 compaction
Fill to be placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched to provide an adequate bond
between the existing ground and new material
Our general specifications for fill placement compaction and earth
work supervision are contained in the grading specifications included with
this report
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes can be designed for slope ratios up to 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical 2 1 slope To prevent future wind and drainage erosion slopes
should be planted immediately after construction
Foundations
The light single story wood framed structures will be of post and
beam construction Allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square
foot may be used for the footing bearing on the on site medium dense sand or
compacted fill Perimeter footings should bottom a minimum of 24 inches
below the surface to be protected from undermining by surface wind and water
erosion
Trench Excavation
Because of the loose nature of the dry on site sand deep trench walls

APPENDIX C
GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
GEOLABS HAWAII
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
III
IV
2
Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
g in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
4
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
V Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
VI Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
