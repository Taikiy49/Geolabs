In accordance with the Agreement for Professional Services entered into on 
June 8, 2016 with Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. (HDCC), Geolabs 
observed the probing and grouting operations for the shallow foundations for the 
Consolidated Car Rental (ConRAC) Facility project at the Kahului Airport in Kahului on the 
Island of Maui, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during 
the probing and grouting operations for the project. 
We understand that a portion of the new ConRAC Facility will be supported on 
shallow foundations consisting of spread and continuous strip footings. To reduce the 
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities or 
compression of loose zones below the building structure, a probing and grouting program 
consisting of 205 4-inch diameter probe holes was implemented for the shallow 
foundation portion of the new car rental structure. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the ConRAC Facility 
project, and the findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced report. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the probing and grouting operations for conformance with the intent

PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
In general, the Consolidated Car Rental (ConRAC) Facility project involves the 
construction of a reinforced concrete structure consisting of a Customer Service Area, 
Ready/Return Area, Quick Turn-Around (QTA) Area, Employee Parking and Storage Area. 
The structure will have three-floor levels, plus a basement on the southwestern portion 
with overall plan dimensions of about 445 feet by 1,330 feet. The site is generally bounded 
by Airport Road to the east, Aalele Street to the south, Keolani Place to the west, and the 
Kahului Airport parking lot to the north. The site is traversed by a large box culvert oriented 
in a southeast-northwest direction. The general project vicinity is shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, we 
recommended supporting the northeastern portion of the new car rental facility on a 
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and continuous strip footings. An 
allowable bearing pressure of up to 6,000 pounds per square foot (psf) was recommended 
for footings bearing on the dense weathered basalt or hard basalt rock formation 
anticipated at relatively shallow depths. A probing and grouting program was implemented 
to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities 
or compression of loose zones below the building structure. The general contractor for the 
project and the probing and grouting subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction 
Company, Inc. (HDCC). 
PROBING AND GROUTING OPERATIONS 
The probing and grouting operation was conducted over a time span from 
December 12, 2016 through March 30, 2017 under the near-continuous (full-time) 
observation by our field engineer. In general, the probe holes were located along the 
continuous strip footings (one probe hole at 10 feet on-center) and at each isolated spread 
footing location (one probe hole per 50 square feet). The approximate probe hole locations 
are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2.  In general, the probe 
holes at the new building structure were drilled to a depth of about 18 to 24 feet below the 
existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 15 feet below the design footing 
bottom elevations. 
The probing operation was accomplished with a 4-inch diameter Downhole 
hammer.  Each probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 50 Drill 
mounted on a Komatsu PC228USLC excavator.  Pressurized air continuously flowed 
through the hollow rods while advancing to the target probe depth.  Evidence of 
cavities/voids was observed by advancement rate and drilling intensity during the probing 
operation.  After completion of the probe drilling operation, the probe hole was tremie

During grouting at Probe Nos. 70 and 73, a substantial grout take of approximately 
44.2 and 93.0 cubic feet of grout, respectively, was required to fill the holes indicating 
potential voids. Additional probe hole locations (Probe Nos. 70A, 70B, 73A and 73B) were 
added adjacent to Probe Nos. 70 and 73 along the wall footing alignments to remediate 
potential subsurface voids. 
Based on information provided by the Contractor, Probe Nos. 114 and 115 were 
located on a previously excavated utility trench.  Therefore, hard drilling conditions would 
not be anticipated until a depth of about 21 and 15 feet below ground surface, respectively. 
As a result, these two probe holes were eliminated from the project.  The Contractor plans 
to span the strip footing across the trench backfill by bearing the strip footing on each side 
of the utility trench on basalt rock formation. 
Probe No. 131 was located too close to an existing utility line and therefore was 
omitted from the probe and grout operations. Based on the information provided by the 
Contractor, Probe No. 131 is located at the base of the parking structure ramp with 
negligible loading. 
Based on our field observations, we believe that all the required probe holes were 
drilled to the minimum specified depth of 15 feet below the bottom of footing into the 
underlying basalt rock formation. In general, we did not observe significant voids and/or 
cavities during the probing and grouting operations, with the exception of Probe Nos. 70 
and 73 as discussed above.
