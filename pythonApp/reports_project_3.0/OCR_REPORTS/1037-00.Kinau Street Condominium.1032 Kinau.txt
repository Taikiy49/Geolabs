PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A 9 story high apartment building including two levels of
parking is planned for this development The lower base
ment parking level will be at about elevation 32 feet
Maximum vertical shear wall load of 24 kips per foot and col
umn loads up to 235 kips are anticipated for the reinforced
concrete structures
Basement retaining walls would be required for the lower
parking level and it will be 6 to 8 feet below the existing
ground surface
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTIONS
The proposed project site is located on the mauka side of
Kinau Street near its intersection with Piikoi Street in the
Punchbowl section of Honolulu The existing site is an as
phaltic conrete paved parking lot approximately 130 by 62
feet in plan dimension
The ground surface varies from a low point of about elevation
39 feet near Kinau Street up to a high point of 39 feet in
the rear
Presently there is a highrise structure with a half basement
parking area located on the east side and a single family
wooden dwelling occuping the adjacent western parcel

SOIL CONDITIONS
The site is underlain by alluvium water deposited soils
and cinders derived from the nearby Punchbowl Volcano The
cinders were depostied upon coral formed when the sea level
was at a higher stage than the present It appears that the
area has experienced several sea level changes as well as
numerous periods of active volcanic eruptions
Following the formation of the coral during periods of high
sea level the cinders were deposited during periods of active
volcanic eruptions The cinders commonly called black sand
were reworked by wave action to form zones of rounded black
sand The black sand was subsequently overlain by a brown
clayey silt or silt alluvium eroded from the nearby mountains
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing
five test borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site
Plan and Boring Locations Plate 1 A detailed description
of the field exploration methods and the logs of soils en
countered are included in the appendix A description of the
accompanying laboratory testing and the test results are also
included in the appendix
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration laboratory
testing and the results of our engineering analysis the site
could be developed as planned
Fairly competent materials consisting of black sand and coral
were encountered at the site

For the 9 story structure shallow spread and continuous foot
ings bearing on the upper black sand formation could be used
Additional recommendations for the design of foundations and
construction are presented in the following paragraphs
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations are
recommended All footings should be founded on the dense black
sand layer underlying the site below the basement level
An allowable bearing pressure of 5000 p s f could be used to
design the footings found on the dense black sand material
A one third increase may be used to allow for infrequently
applied wind and seismic loads
For the above bearing pressure the estimated settlements are
on the order of one inch total and one half inch differential
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by the
soils engineer to comfirm the conditions upon which our recon
mendations are based Any loose areas encountered during foot
ing excavation should be removed and replaced with compacted
granular fill
Design of Basement Walls
Basement walls below grade will be subject to horizontal earth
pressure imposed by granular backfill material It is

recommended that the lateral earth pressures induced on base
ment walls that are restrained from deflection be designed
for walls that are free to deflect
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 350 p c f
Friction may be taken as normal dead load times a coefficient
of friction factor equal to 0 50 A combination of friction
and passive pressure may be used provided that passive pressure
is reduced by one third
Basement Parking
Slab on grade may be used for basement parking since expansive
subgrade soil was not encountered during the field explorations
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel material such as 3B Fine rock to act as a capillary
break Proof rolling of the subgrade material is recommended
prior to gravel placement
The slab on grade should be separated from walls and foundations
Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include excavation back
filling and compaction inspection during construction

The proposed basement excavation would extend to depths of
4 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface
It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving equipment
could be used to conduct all the excavations for the base
ment and foundations
Excavation should be protected from surface runoff which may
enter the excavations All loose soils or mud should be
removed from the bottom of all footing excavation prior to
concrete placement
Due to the silty nature of the subsurface material and the
presence of volcanic cinders within the upper 8 feet the
basement excavation walls may unravel with time when cut at
a near vertical slope or it may collapse if the large ex
cavation is left unprotected for a long period of time
Therefore the vertical excavation required along the east
ern Diamond Head property boundary should be handled with
extreme care due to the proximity of adjacent highrise and
parking structures It is our estimate that the basement
elevation of the adjacent highrise structure is at about ele
feet To minimize disturbance of the adjacent
footings underpinning or construction of the basement wall
in small sections may be required

Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings and behind the basement walls The on site
sandy soils could be used for backfilling around footings
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 10 inch
lifts and compacted to about 90 of maximum density per
ASTM D 1557 70 Modified Proctor standard
Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and fill
ing operations be inspected by the soils engineer to verify
adquate compaction and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Additionally all the imported fill material if required
should be tested and inspected by a soil engineer before bring
ing it to the job site
Our present recommendations are contingent upon the above
inspection and testing
The plans for foundations and site grading should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and comments prior to con
struction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
