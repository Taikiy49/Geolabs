INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our subsurface investigation for
the design of the terminal roadways parking area sewer water and
drain systems at the new Lihue Airport Complex
State Project No K 1074 The general site location is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This project is a part of the proposed New Lihue Airport Complex which
also includes construction of major drainlines and channels a new
terminal building aircraft parking aprons and realignment of cane
haul roads and irrigation systems Soils investigations have been
performed for the other related projects and soils reports will be
submitted under separate covers The general layout of the New Lihue
Airport Complex is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

W O 1106 20 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to explore the subsurface condi
tions at the site to provide soil parameters for the design of the
proposed improvements The following scope of services was performed
in general accordance with our proposals dated and

Preparing a written report for design use

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface Conditions
The project site is located on the outskirts of Lihue Town on the
eastern coastline of Kauai Hawaii The site for the new terminal
complex is situated to the southwest of the existing airport and is
bounded by Kapule Highway on the west Ahukini road to the north the
present Taxiway A to the east and southeast and sugar cane fields
to the southwest
The area is presently cultivated with sugar cane by the Lihue
Plantation Company Ltd There are two cane haul roads extending
into the area from Kapule Highway Irrigation ditches cross the site
in many directions leaving soft wet areas in the vicinity of the
ditches Many weathered basalt bounders were exposed in the existing
irrigation ditch cuts
The ground surface elevation at the proposed access roadways and
parking area generally vary from 168 feet near Kapule Highway to 140
feet near Ahukini Road The ground surface slopes generally down
toward the northeast at gradients of about 1
The new 12 inch effluent disposal force main alignment will extend
around the southwestern portion of the new airport complex which is
presently under sugar cane cultivation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1106 20 Page 6
The new 21 inch sewer line along Kapule Highway will extend through a
grassy area southwest of the airport complex
The planned water line alignment that will extend along Ahukini Road
presently has ground cover consisting mainly of grass hibiscus and
oleander trees and some old asphalt pavements
The island of Kauai was built up by a single large shield volcano
During the Tertiary period basaltic lavas of the Waimea Canyon
Volcanic Series built a roughly circular island to a height over
17 000 feet above the surrounding sea floor Near the end of the
Tertiary Period this main shield building volcanic phase came to a
halt
Following a long period of erosion and subsidence volcanism renewed
during the Pleistocene epoch producing the Koloa Volcanic Series
These post erosional volcanics laid a veneer of fresh lavas over the
older Waimea Canyon Series across most of the eastern half of the
island
After the cessation of late volcanism the geologic forces of
weathering erosion and deposition during the late Pleistocene and
the Recent epochs have shaped the island to its present form
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Q 1106 20 Page 7
At the site the lava rocks of this late volcanism had been deeply
weathered to form a mantle of reddish brown residual clayey silt soil
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the new airport complex and related
off site utilities were explored by drilling a total of 37 borings to
depths ranging from 15 0 to 31 5 at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Selected soil samples recovered during our field investigation were
further tested in our laboratory to evaluate their geotechnical
engineering characteristics
Detailed information of our field investigation the log of borings
the laboratory testing and results are presented in the appendices of
this report
Subsurface Conditions
in general most of the project area is presently under cultivation
and the subsurface conditions consist of a thin layer of topsoil over
a relatively thick mantle of stiff residual soil consisting of reddish
brown clayey silt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1106 20 Page 8
The topsoil at the surface consists of dark brown Slightly organic
clayey silt The topsoil has been filled and plowed over and
intermixed with some decayed and burnt vegetation and other organic
matter from cultivation activities The topsoil layer is generally
about 1 foot thick
The reddish brown clayey silt below the topsoil layer is very stiff
to hard at shallow depth however it grades somewhat less stiff at
depths of about 10 to 15 feet It was noted that the moisture
contents generally tend to increase and densities decrease at 10 to
1S foot depth In general the in place clayey silt soil exhibits low
swelling potential However some of the laboratory test results
indicated that if the soil is recompacted at moisture contents below
the optimum moisture content the compacted soil may have moderate
swelling potential
Boulders were encountered in some of the borings Based on visual
observations of the existing drainage ditch cuts it appears that a
significant amount of boulders could be generated from the site
cutting and utility excavation
Old deteriorated asphalt and concrete pavements were encountered near
the surface in Borings 67 and 68 along Ahukini Road

Groundwater Level
Groundwater was generaly encountered at about elevations ranging from
123 to 139 feet in and 49 drilled near the existing
STP Boring 65 near Kapule Highway and Halau Street and Boring 22A at
the sewage pump station location
The remaining borings did not encounter groundwater although many of
them were drilled to depths below elevation 139 feet It appears that
the observed groundwater level may reflect localized seepage condi
tions possibly originating from irrigation ditches reservoir and
other sources The borings indicate that the seepage may be carried
by a weathered clinker or cinder layer underlying the area
The area near the existing sewage treatment plant is generally low
and may be subjected to relatively high water level fluctuations
particularly after heavy rain

Site Grading
The rough grading for the parking area and access roads will be per
formed first under a separate contract K 1072 which includes with
the rough grading work for the new terminal building and aircraft
apron areas The rough grading for the parking area and terminal
access roads will consist mainly of site filling up to about 5 feet
high
Subsequent site grading under this contract is anticipated to be
minimum
It is our understanding that the construction of the remaining improve
ments at the parking areas and access roadways will begin immediately
after the completion of the rough grading
We recommend that the road subgrade level be compacted to obtain 95
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Roadway and Parking Area Pavements
The pavement thickness design for the roadways and parking areas is
governed by the subgrade soil quality CBR traffic volume and dis
tribution estimated traffic growth factor and design service life

It is anticipated that the subgrade material below the road pavement
section will consist of compacted reddish brown clayey silt fill
material excavated from the proposed new aircraft apron area
Laboratory CBR test results on soil samples obtained for this
investigation and at the new aircraft apron area indicate that the
on site material can provide a relatively high CBR value when it is
properly compacted For the road pavement design it is our opinion
that a design CBR value of 15 can be assigned for the on site material

Excavation
Generally the depth of excavation will vary from about 4 to 15
feet below the existing ground surface The borings indicated
that residual soil should be encountered ranging from very stiff
to hard and grading to stiff below 10 to 15 foot depth Relatively
easy digging is anticipated for the trenches although some amount
of boulders should be anticipated in the residual soil formation
Due to the depth of the excavation shoring of the trench walls
are recommended to minimize caving in of the trench walls The
excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with the applicable safety requirements The design and safety of
the trench supports should be the responsibility of the contractor

w Page 15

The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should

monitoring is particularly critical in the areas where existing
structures are located adjacent to the trench excavation

Lateral Earth Pressure
The lateral earth pressure acting on the drain structures will
depend on the type of backfill used the extent of the backfill
material and the compactive effort required on the material behind
the structure walls Granular material is recommended for the
backfill adjacent to the structures to reduce the compactive
effort required and minimize the build up of additional lateral
earth pressure

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
lll en
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifica
tion and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths The Atterberg Limits are also summarized on
Plates B 1 through B 4 and plotted on Plate B 5
One sieve analysis was performed on a sample to evaluate its
grain size distribution The test result is presented on Plate
B 3
Three laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples
obtained from the auger cuttings near the proposed finished
pavement elevation to evaluate the subgrade Support capacity of
the compacted soil The test results are summarized on Plates
B 1 and B 2
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
their natural condition The test results are summarized on
Plate B 6
Ten direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural soil The
test results are presented on Plates B 7 through B 16
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed sample
to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the natural
soils The test result is presented on Plate B 17
Five pH and resistivity tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the corrosion potential of the soils along
the utility alignments The test results are summarized on
Plate B 18
