INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsurface
conditions and provide preliminary recommendations for the
design and construction of the proposed Kamalu Road Realignment
Route 581 at Three alternate alignment
sites are included in this study
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
Kamalu Road is located in the Wailua Homestead area of Kauai
The general project site is located along Kamalu Road about
1 600 feet northeast of Kuamoo Road
The present road alignment generally runs in a southwest to
northeast direction and included two near 90 degree turns
The present road is covered with asphaltic concrete
The terrain at the site is gently rolling and is covered with
trees and grass A drainageway about 10 feet deep and generally
running in a north south direction crosses the southwestern
portion of the roadway alignment A culvert is located below
the existing roadway at the drainageway crossing Most of
the drainageway is covered with grass and trees A few inches
of water was noted in some areas of the drainageway
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALUY ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 3
Kalama stream crosses the northeastern portion of the align
ment About 1 to 5 feet of water was noted in the stream
at the time of the boring exploration The stream crosses
below the existing Kalama Road through a culvert structure
Several houses are located in the area
The elevation at the site varies from about 255 to 300 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed realignment would straighten the existing road
by eliminating the 2 sharp turns in the present alignment
Three alternate alignments are being considered The alter
nate alignments vary from about 2 600 to 3 100 feet in length
Cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet are planned
Proposed major structures include a concrete box culvert at
the drainageway crossing and a bridge structure over Kalama
Stream
Foundation recommendations for the proposed bridge structure
are not included in this report Additional borings and
investigations for the bridge will be performed at a later
date when the final location is determined

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated in the Lihue basin or Lihue
depression area which generally extends from Kapaa to
Nawiliwili on the north south latitudes respectively and
from Kalepa Nonou ridge to the major mountain range on the
east west longitudes respectively
One geologic theory about the formation of the Lihue basin
area is that a collapse on the eastern flank of the Kauai
shield volcano occurred resulting in a subcircular caldera
7 to 10 miles wide that eventually became the basin area
Another theory is that the basin primarily resulted from
streams eroding and ultimately destroying the amphitheater
4
headed valleys which they once formed on the eastern flank
leaving the basin or depression areas which were once the
valley floors
In either case a relatively flat lower topographic area
was created upon which weathering and erosion developed
alluvial and colluvial conglomerates during a volcanic
quiescent period more than 1 5 million years in duration
Volcanic activity then resumed in this area and the new set
of eruptions were named the Koloa Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 5
Presently it is the lavas from the Koloa Volcanic Series
interbedded with sediments which developed during the periods
between successive Koloa eruptions that exists in the upper
depths of the Lihue basin Surface soil layers of various
thicknesses throughout the basin area lastly covered the
Koloa Volcanic Series lavas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicated that most of
the site is underlain by residual soils Some soft soils
and high water levels were noted in the lowlying drainageway
area Some settlements should be anticipated when filling
over the drainageway
The moisture contents of the in situ soils are generally
quite high greater than 50 Some drying out of the
excavated material may be required to attain the required
compaction criteria
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
Proposed cuts and fills are generally less than 10 feet
Due to the high moisture content of the in situ soils generally
10 to 20 percent above optimum moisture content air drying
of the excavated material may be required before using it as
fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 8
Surface vegetation and topsoil should be removed prior to
filling
To develop a working platform in the lowlying drainageway
area 2 feet of granular material such as 3B Fine is recom
mended over the soft soils prior to filling The granular
layer will also serve as drainage blanket to facilitate
pore water dissipation resulting in accelerated new fill
settlement
Select on site material can be used for fill The fill
should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 The top 6 inches should be compacted
to 95 of its maximum dry density
A shrinkage factor of about 20 is estimated for the in situ
soil The underlying soils should be allowed to adjust to
the new fill load for as long as practicable prior to asphalt
paving
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical may be
used
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and slope planting should be utilized to reduce slope
erosion
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 9
Slope protection should be considered for the upstream fill
slope in the drainageway
Culvert Structure
A concrete box culvert about 6 feet high by 13 5 feet wide js
planned for the drainageway crossing
Soft subsoils and high water levels were noted in the lowlying
drainageway area where the culvert and most of the fill will be
placed
For Alignments 1 and 2 the boring indicated about 9 feet of soft
soil below the bottom of the drainageway For 10 feet of fill
it is estimated that 3 to 6 inches of settlements may occur in
these soft subsoils If the culvert structure cannot tolerate
these settlements it is recommended that the site be surcharged
with a minimum of 10 feet of loose material A 1 foot layer of
gravel should be placed below the surcharge to facilitate drain
age during consolidation Another alternate would be to construct
about 5 feet of the fill then place an additional 5 feet of sur
charge over the fill When the settlement rates have decreased to
a tolerable rate the surcharge may be removed and the culvert con
struction may begin Based on the consolidation test data and pre
vious experience in similar type of soil conditions it is estimated
that 6 to 8 months may be required for the major portion of the fill
settlements to occur Settlement gauges should be used to monitor
the settlement rates and determine when culvert construction can
begin If practicable surcharging with about 20 feet of material
may be utilized to accelerate the settlements and reduce future
settlements It is estimated that about 3 months would produce
the desired settlements with 20 feet of surcharge

PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 10
For Alignment 3 the boring indicated that rocky materials
may be located below a few feet of soft material in the
drainageway In this case the soft material may be removed
down to the rocky material and backfilled Granular material
such as 3B Fine should be used as backfill material below the
water level Little settlement would be anticipated in this
Situation
To provide uniform support and a working platform a 2 foot
layer of granular material should be provided under the
culvert structure
An allowable bearing value of 1 500 p s f may be used for
the culvert bearing on the above granular layer
Holes should be incorporated throughout the slab to help
release the hydrostatic pressure build up under the structural
slab
Wing Walls
Wing walls are planned for the box culvert
Assuming a well graded granular backfill material an equi
valent fluid pressure of 40 p c f can be used for lateral
earth pressures against the walls Additional load due to
traffic or sloping surcharge should be included where appli
cable
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 11
To provide a uniform support under the footing a two foot
layer of granular material should also be provided under
the wing wall retaining structure An allowable bearing
value of 2 000 p s f may be used on the granular layer
This value may be increased by one third at the toe of the
wall to account for the triangular pressure distribution
below the footing and for temporary loads
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Roadway Pavements
For the relatively light traffic conditions on the proposed
roadway the pavement design may be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow over a
prepared subgrade
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
This pavement design is based on an average of the CBR test
results of the on site soil at the anticipated moisture contents
that may be used during construction The CBR values of the
on site subgrade materials will vary with the moisture content
of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED KAMALU ROAD REALIGNMENT ROUTE 581 P 12
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils at the time of roadway construction and the above design
sections can be revised based on the additional tests
The roadway subgrades should be compacted and shaped to drain
Design Review
The plans for site grading and box culvert foundations should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommen
dations is included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
To a degree the performance of any structure is dependent upon
construction procedures and quality Hence observations of
site clearing and fill placement should be carried out by a
soils engineer to permit correlation between the preliminary
soil data obtained during this investigation and the actual
soil conditions encountered during construction
