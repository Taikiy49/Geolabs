In accordance with your request we have conducted an investiga
tion of the floor slab distresses to the first floor level of the
apartment building and parking garage at the subject Heritage
House Development project located on Hawaii Kai Drive just south
of Hahaione Street This letter summarizes our findings and con
clusions
The area under investigation includes the first floor level of
the apartment building between Column Lines 13 to 24 and Column
Rows A to G In the parking garage the investigation was limited
to the slab on grade area between Column Lines 11 and 20 and
Column Rows A through D on the first level

The investigation entailed seven borings three in the apartment
structure and four in the parking garage to investigate condi
tions beneath the distressed concrete floor slabs A survey of
the location of the floor slab cracks was conducted The crack
patterns boring locations and plan extent of the investigation
is shown on the enclosed Plates i and 2
The test borings were made utilizing portable auger equipment and
continuous undisturbed 2 5 inch 0 D ring samplers The samples
were classified in the field and returned to the laboratory for
additional classification and testing A detailed description of
the soils encountered and the number of blows required to drive
the sampler a distance of 12 inches with a 140 pound hammer is
shown on the enclosed boring logs
Natural moisture content density and Atterberg Limit tests were
performed on the soil samples to provide additional classifica
tion information The results of the laboratory testing is shown
on each boring log at the appropriate sample depth In addition
to the classification tests swell tests were also performed on
selected samples at their in situ moisture contents to identify
the expansion potential of the soils A summary of the swell
test results is also attached

APARTMENT BUILDING
Plate 1 shows the locations of the three test borings drilled on
the first floor level of the apartment building and depicts the
crack locations established by a field survey Floor Slab cross
sections at selected locations are also included to illustrate
the relative position of the floor slabs on each side of the
cracks os
As can be seen in the cross sections the floor slabs have expe
rienced differential movements generally settling adjacent to
the load bearing walls and areas of deepest fill It is our
understanding that the entire area was undercut several feet and
backfilled with an off site borrow material Additional excava
tions were made to place the foundations at the specified bearing
levels Following the backfilling operation and the construction
of the bearing walls the floor slabs were poured on the prepared
subgrade
Boring encountered 4 to 4 inches of concrete underlain
by 111 composed of a mixture of sandy silt clay silt and silty
clay with traces of gravel The fill had in place densities
varying from 69 to 98 pcef and an average moisture content of
approximately 20 percent The fill extended to a depth of approxi
mately seven feet in Borings 1 and 2 and to a depth of two feet
Page 4

in Boring 3 The fill was generally underlain by the natural
adobe soil
The swell tests showed the fill encountered in Borings 1 and 2
is non expansive The material encountered in Boring 3 was
slightly expansive with a one percent swell
The crack patterns and cross sections indicated that the floor
slabs have settled in the areas of the deepest fill adjacent to
the load bearing walls The in place densities are generally well
below the estimated maximum density of 115 pcf for the fill
material The swell tests indicate that the material is generally
non expansive It is our opinion that the distresses to the floor
slabs in the apartment building are the result of settlement
within the insufficiently compacted backfill
Additional distresses may be noted with subsequent consolidation
of the fill These settlements should be of a similar magnitude
as the existing distresses and no major movements are anticipated
The investigation into the materials beneath the floor slabs does
not eliminate the possibility of expansive soils being present
within the backfill If the expansive soils were concentrated
in one area additional distresses could occur due to subsequent
expansion of this material

PARKING GARAGE
Our investigation of the distresses to the concrete floor slabs
in the parking garage included the area between Column Lines 12
and 20 and Column Rows A through G on the first level Plate 2
shows the location of the floor slab distresses as measured by
our field survey and the locations of Borings 4 through 7
drilled to determine the nature of the subgrade soils Four
eross sections which indicate the relative movement of the floor
slab at the crack locations are also shown on Plate 2
Borings 5 through 7 encountered 3 to 4 inches of concrete under
lain by the expansive adobe soil Boring 4 penetrated 4 inches
of concrete underlain by 1 5 feet of gray fine silt 111 under
lain by an adobe fill of grayish brown silty clay
The adobe material had densities varying from 17 to 84 pcf and
an average moisture content of approximately 30 percent The
swell test indicate that the upper fill in Boring 4 is non 7
expansive The adobe soils encountered in and 7
were moderately to highly expansive Swells of 0 6 to 3 7 percent
were measured on the natural soils This swell potential does
not include the swell that occurred prior to sampling of the
soils

The four borings drilled within the parking garage did not
encounter the recommended 6 inch cushion of free draining granular
material beneath the floor slabs It appears the concrete floors
were placed directly upon the adobe soil with only a moisture
membrane Unless these slabs have been designed to resist the
expansion pressures due to the swelling of the subgrade soils
additional distresses at the rear of the structure can develop
It appears that the edge of the floor slabs adjacent to Column
Row A has been loaded by equipment and material storage in
addition to the scaffolding necessary to support the concrete on 7
the second level These loads were placed near the wall at the
edge of the slab which apparently overstressed the concrete
The cracking at the front of the structure between Column Lines
A and B therefore appears to be the result of overstressing of
the concrete slabs due to the imposed construction loadings
possibly aggravated by the expansive subgrade soils
Due to the difficulties in controlling the moisture content and
groundwater supply available to the subgrade soils it is expected
that the adobe will continue to expand producing additional
distresses to the floor slab It will be necessary to remove the
expansive materials to eliminate the potential for additional
distresses in the floor slabs within the parking structure
