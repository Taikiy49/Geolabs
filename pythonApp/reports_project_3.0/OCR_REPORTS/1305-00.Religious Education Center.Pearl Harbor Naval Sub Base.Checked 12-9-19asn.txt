This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation performed at the above referenced site The general
location of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate l
The purpose of our study is to explore the subsurface conditions at
the site to provide data for use in the design of the site grading
foundation and pavements of the proposed Religious Educational Center
Our work was performed in general accordance with the scope of services
outlined in our proposal of
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the U S Naval Submarine Base at Pearl
Harbor Oahu Hawaii It is located within the block bounded by Bole
Edge Pierce Streets and North Road The western side of the site is
occupied by the existing Building 999
The eastern half of the site is currently being used for automobile
parking The ground surface is slightly raised and covered by Portland
cement concrete slabs
The ground surface elevation is at approximately 114 feet based on
U S Navy Datum
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building will consist of one story classroom type
structures in an L shaped arrangement CMU block walls and concrete
Slab on grade are planned The proposed layout for the
Religious Educational Center is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Building loads are anticipated to be very light
A parking lot area is also planned to the south of the buildings
The final floor elevation was not available to us at the time of our
investigation however only minor grading is anticipated for the
proposed building and parking area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Three borings were drilled in the proposed building area to depths
of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring logs are
presented on Plates A 1 through A 3 and the laboratory test data are
presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
Soil Conditions
In general our borings encountered a 3 5 inch concrete slab over
4 5 to 5 feet layer of loose to medium dense sandy gravel fill In
Boring 2 a volcanic tuff boulder was encountered at a depth of 4 5 to
6 5 feet
Below the upper fill layer all our borings encountered relatively
competent natural soils In Borings 1 and 3 the natural soil under the
ill consisted of dense brown to gray brown silty sand to a depth of
about 12 feet underlain by a thin layer of stiff brown Silty clay of
less than 2 feet thick further underlain by highly weathered volcanic
tuff at depths ranging from about 12 to 15 feet The weathered tuff
extended to a depth of about 20 feet the depths drilled at Borings 1
and 3
In Boring 2 the natural soil under the tuff boulder at the bottom
of the fill consisted of stiff to very stiff brown sandy clayey silt to
20 feet the maximum depth drilled

Groundwater was encountered in all three borings at 12 3 and 13 3
feet below the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
aS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is mantled by about
4 5 to 6 5 feet of loose sandy gravel fill underlain by relatively
competent natural soils
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed building on the existing fill provided that the fill is further
densified to minimize potential settlements
Site Grading
Relatively minor site grading is anticipated for the proposed
construction The maximum fill or cut will probably be about 2 feet
One of the main considerations for site grading work is to densify
the existing loose sandy gravel fill layer to minimize potential
settlements
In view of the granular nature of the fill layer and the light
building loading we believe that compacting the fill from the stripped
surface with a heavy vibratory roller would be adequate for this project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1305 00 Page 5
After the existing structures and concrete slabs are removed the
existing upper fill material below the proposed building subgrade should
be moisture conditioned prior to densification The fill surface should
then be compacted with a heavy vibratory roller to obtain a minimum 953
compaction at the subgrade level It is anticipated that 8 to 10 passes
may be required to densify the upper portion of the fill down to a depth
of 1 to 2 feet to a density of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78 The moistening
and compaction of the existing fill from the ground surface should be
monitored by a soils engineer
Any soft or weak pockets disclosed during site grading should be
repaired by excavating and backfilling with compacted on site granular
Fill and as approved by the soils engincer
If the future grade of the site is to be raised we recommend that
the existing A C pavements be broken up and the existing concrete
slabs removed Broken up A C pavements less than 3 inches in size
may be reused in the fill After the A C and slabs are removed the
Fill area should be compacted by a heavy vibratory roller as discussed
earlier
All building and pavement areas should be compacted to at least 90
of the maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to
the placement of any new fill All new fills should also be compacted
to 90 of the maximum dry density In addition the top 6 inches of the
subgrade under the A C pavement in the parking area should be compacted
to 95 of the maximum density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1305 00 Page 6
If additional off site borrow is required it should be tested and
approved by a soils engineer prior to hauling to the site The borrow
or imported material should generally be non expansive and not contain
large percentages of cobbles organics or other deleterious matter
Foundation
Shallow spread footings or continuous wall foundations may be used
for the proposed one story structure
We believe that foundations may either bear on the compacted upper
fill layer densified as discussed under the SITE GRADING section or
on the underlying natural soils
In general the bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade Thickened edge slab
footing should have a minimum embedment of 12 inches If a footing is
located next to a utility trench it should be extended to the bottom
level of the utility trench or be designed to span over the trench to
reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
For foundations bearing on the existing compacted sandy gravel fill
9
a bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1305 00 Page 7
Estimated settlements of the foundations placed in the upper fill
layer are anticipated to be less than one half inch total and
one quarter inch differential We believe the majority of the footing
settlements would occur prior to the completion of the project
construction
The site should be graded to drain water away from the structure and
foundations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction may be utilized after the site is
prepared as recommended in the SITE GRADING section
The on grade slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
such as 3 Fine crushed rock to minimize capillary rise through the
cushion fill The cushion material should be tamped by 4 passes of a
hand pushed vibratory plate compactor
Pavements
For the A C parking area planned we recommend the following
pavement design section
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade Soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1305 00 Page 8
The base course and subgrade should be compacted to 95 of the
respective maximum dry densities The recommended section considers
medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided for adjacent to
paved areas
If the grade is to be raised the pavement design will depend upon
the CBR value of the imported materials To maintain the above pavement
section the imported subgrade fill material should be capable of
developing a minimum CBR value of 12
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 3
borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the borings which ranged in
depth from 20 0 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were Classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
Sampler for 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the boring

Limit test and one sieve analysis were performed The results of these
tests are presented on Plate B 1l

fill The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed sample to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsurface soil The test
results are presented on Plate B 3
