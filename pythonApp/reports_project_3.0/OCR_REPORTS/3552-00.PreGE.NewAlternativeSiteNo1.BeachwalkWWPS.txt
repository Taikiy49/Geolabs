Preliminary Findings and Evaluations
Our review of available in house soils and geologic data in the vicinity of the
proposed site suggest that the area may be underlain by a surficial fill crust of about 3 to
5 feet thick The thin surficial fill crust is generally underlain by loose lagoonal deposits
varying from about 10 to 25 feet in thickness Relatively competent coral reef formation

underlying the loose lagoonal deposits is anticipated at an elevation of about 10 to

should be noted that the vertical extent of the loose lagoonal deposits at the proposed

Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions described above it appears that
excavations for the below grade level of the pump station building will likely encounter
competent coral reef formation at the design bottom elevation of 30 feet MSL
Therefore we believe that the below grade level of the pump station building may be
supported by mat foundation bearing on the competent coral reef formation anticipated at
the proposed site
Depending on the thickness of the surficial fill crust and the structural loads the
on grade portion of the pump station building may be supported by shallow foundations
without significant footing settlements Otherwise deep foundations consisting of driven
piles end bearing on the underlying coral reef formation may be required to support the
on grade portion of the pump station building
Construction of the below grade level for the new pump station building will likely
entail relatively deep excavation and dewatering of the excavation Due to the relatively
shallow groundwater table and permeable coral formation anticipated at the proposed
site large quantities of water will need to be removed or displaced from the excavation
Because of the loose lagoonal deposits anticipated at the proposed site shoring of the
deep excavations will be a major consideration during project construction The shoring
system will serve to reduce the potential for cave ins of the loose lagoonal deposits as
well as a cut off wall for the dewatering operations The use of conventional sheet pile
shoring may or may not be feasible due to the potential presence of hard coral ledges
that may be encountered at shallow depths which may result in penetration refusal
during installation Sheetpile cut off walls may be feasible with pre drilling through the
hard coral ledges Therefore other types of shoring methods such as mixed in place soil
wall would need to be considered and evaluated based on the actual field conditions
encountered at the proposed site
As discussed above the on grade portion of the new pump station building may
need to be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of driven piles end bearing
on the coral reef formation anticipated at the proposed alternate site If a driven pile
foundation system is selected predrilling for the driven piles may be required due to the
potential presence of hard coral ledges Downdrag effects of the soft lagoonal deposits
on the deep foundation system will also need to be evaluated and addressed
