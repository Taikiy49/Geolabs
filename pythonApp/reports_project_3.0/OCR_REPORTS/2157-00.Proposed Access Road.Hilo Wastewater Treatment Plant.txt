INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the grading and utility line
installation for the proposed access road portion of the proposed Hilo
Wastewater Treatment Plant project near the town of Hilo in the District
of South Hilo on the island of Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions along the proposed access road alignment in order to
develop a generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering
design recommendations pertinent to the proposed road and infrastructure
development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 00 Page 2
The work was done generally in accordance with our fee proposal of
The scope of our work included the excavation and logging of eight
8 test pits to depths ranging between 6 0 and 14 0 feet below the
existing ground surface and the performance of six 6 seismic refraction
geophysical surveys along the proposed road alignment Also included were
reduction and interpretation of the geophysical survey data engineering
evaluation of the field and geophysical data the development of design
recomendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in the
appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the east of the existing Hilo
International Airport General Lyman Field in the South Hilo District of
the island of Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located in raw undeveloped land which is vegetated by a
subtropical forest with a dense understory of ferns and shrubs The
topography of the site is gently rolling with a slight slope towards the
north northwest Access along the proposed road alignment is by an access
trail blazed through the forest from the vicinity of the existing
airport Extensive clearing and some grading was required to make the
access trail and project site accessible to four wheel drive trucks and
exploration equipment
The existing ground surface elevations of the site were not available
during our exploration but are believed to range from approximately 30 to
40 feet above Mean Sea Level

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the alignment for the construction
of an access road approximately 6 500 feet in length to serve a
municipal wastewater treatment plant facility The site improvements will
involve the construction of a two lane paved roadway in an approximately
sixty foot wide right of way Asphaltic concrete pavements are
anticipated
It is anticipated that the site grading will require cuts and fills
on the order of six to eight feet
Included in the access road project will be the installation of
underground water communications and electrical lines
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the eastern flank of Mauna Loa a
large basaltic shield volcano which makes up a large portion of the mass
of the island of Hawaii The site and its vicinity has been mapped by
Stearns and Macdonald 1946 as rocks of the Kau Volcanic Series of Mauna
Loa which are Recent in age These rocks are generally thinly bedded
primitive tholeitic basalts The Kau Volcanics are frequently underlain
at relatively shallow depths by lava flow of the Kahuku Volcanic Series
The Kahuku rocks are also primitive basalts which may be capped with a
thin layer of Pahala Ash
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of
ash Cinder and Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and when
the moisture content is high enough it becomes thixotropic i e it
loses strength when remolded
Observations at the project site and in its inmediate vicinity
indicate that rock is usually exposed at the ground surface below the
forest understory and that there is little or no ash soil over the rock

Subsurface Exploration
In general our exploration encountered two sets of subsurface
conditions on the project site In the western part of the site the
surface and subsurface conditions were dominated by a a lava flows
consisting of loose to medium dense clinker overlying hard dense basalt
In the eastern part of the site conditions were dominated by pahoehoe
lava flows consisting of fractured vesicular basalt The general
demarcation between these areas is in the vicinity of Station 32 50 as
indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by the
excavation of eight 8 test pits to depths ranging between 6 0 and 14 0
feet below the existing ground surface in areas where excavation was
feasible and by the performance of six 6 geophysical surveys seismic
refraction to obtain data pertaining to the characteristics of the rock
materials in areas where excavation was not possible The approximate
locations of the test pits and seismic surveys are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The logs of the test pits and the results of the seismic
refraction surveys are presented in Appendix A of this report along with
detailed descriptions of the field exploration
The test pits encountered a surface layer of loose to medium dense
gravel and cobbles with some sand silt and boulders representing
weathered volcanic clinker This stratum extended from the ground surface
to depths ranging from 6 0 feet below the ground surface to the maximm

had been contaminated by black organic silt from the decay of vegetation
from the forest overlying the site
Below the clinker layer our exploration encountered relatively hard

backhoe used for the exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered at
shallow depths below the surface of the project site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed access road utilities and related site
work However due to the potential existence of cavities within the
volcanic rock we recommend a program of cavity detection be conducted
during construction This is discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
roadway and right of way grading limits should be cleared of all
vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in
landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits designated
to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing unengineered rockfill
along the access roads be removed to natural rock formation within the
project grading limits If the stripped rockfill material does not
contain organics rubbish ash soil or other deleterious materials and if
it is generally less than 12 inches in maximum size it may be re used as
fill material Otherwise it should be wasted off site
The on site volcanic material if reduced to generally less than 12
inches in size should be a source of adequate fill material Imported
fill material if required should conform to the requirements stipulated
in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works
Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 00 Page 7
The on site volcanic material if reduced to less than six 6 inches
in size should be a source of adequate trench and structural backfill
material Imported material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated
September 1986
If the upper two to three feet of clinker material contains more than
ten 10 percent organic material it should be stripped from below the
roadway and shoulder areas This organically contaminated material should
not be used as embankment fill or backfill unless the organics can be
separated from the clinkery material The material may be utilized as
general fill
Any fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum
dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two
2 feet of fills and backfills below road subgrade level should be
compacted to 95 maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills
consisting of excavated volcanic material Therefore we recommend a
testing program under the observation of the geotechnical engineer to
determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction This program should be conducted at the
start of grading to establish the compaction criterion for the remainder
of the grading work
Slopes in dense rock formation may be cut to a ratio of 0 5H to 1V or
flatter Slopes in clinker may be cut to a ratio of 2H to 1V or flatter
Care should be exercised to avoid overcutting of the slopes

Fill slopes should be placed at ratio of 2H to 1V or flatter Fill
slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design
slope
Excavation
It is our opinion that the clinker formation encountered in the
westerly portion of the proposed road alignment may be excavated by
conventional methods Due to the nature of the basalt rock formation
encountered in the easterly portion of the site it is anticipated that
some hard excavation e g hoerams chipping and or blasting will be
required for cuts foundation structure excavations or trenches that
encounter dense rock formation
We anticipate that the clinker material may have a volume decrease on
the order of 10 to 15 percent and that the on site basalt rock formation
may have a volume increase on the order of 25 to 35 percent when excavated
and reused as fill material The excavated material may require some
processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater than 12 inches
in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill material The material will
require further processing to remove or reduce the fragments to less than
6 inches in size prior to its being used as trench and structural
backfill
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing should
be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work However
excavations in dense rock formation will most likely stand unsupported for
temporary construction purposes
Cavities
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at shallow
depths below the project site Although our exploration did not encounter
any voids or cavities during our exploration previous experience
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 00 Page 9
indicates that such cavities lava tubes are commonly found in volcanic
formations Several substantial tubes have been found in the South Hilo
District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void situated
directly below the road subgrade causing settlement of the pavement we
recommend that the subgrade be proof rolled a minimm of 10 passes with a
heavy bulldozer or heavy vibratory compactor prior to the placement of
fill in fill areas or base course in cut areas to detect and collapse
potential voids which could exist in the subgrade Voids which are
detected should be exposed and backfilled with on site granular fill
material compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented herein
or with neat concrete
Alternately geophysical surveys such as ground penetrating radar
could be used when the road is brought to subgrade level to detect
anomalies which could be potential voids The anomalies could then be
probed and if they are actual voids be backfilled with Portland cement
and sand grout injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage
fashion i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the intent of the earthwork recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as visual
observations test pits and geophysical surveys Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping and estimation from reference points located in the field and
shown on the proposed alignment plan prepared by Inaba Engineering
Incorporated Elevations of the data points were not available at the
time of our exploration The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors

V
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around then
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 00 Page 8
Appendix B Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of
visual observations and test strips to determine the lift
thickness and number of passes of a compactor required to
achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start
of the project The number of passes can then be used as the
criteria for compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill

The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and

Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
