PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Villages at Aina Lea development is on the mauka side of Queen Kaahumanu
Highway in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The development is a
master planned community with multiple residential development parcels a golf course
and infrastructure improvements Phase 1 of the project generally consists of an affordable
housing development In addition various infrastructure improvements such as
roadways wastewater treatment plant water reservoirs storm water disposal systems
and underground utility lines are planned
Mass grading for Phase encompasses an area of about 49 7 acres Based on
the preliminary grading plan approximately 250 000 cubic yards of excavations and
140 000 cubic yards of embankments are planned The majority of the cuts and fills will
be on the order of about 10 feet high or less to achieve the design grades However
cuts and fills of up to about 20 to 30 feet may be required at some locations
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general we envision that the project site is underlain by interbedded layers of a a
basalt and dense pahoehoe basalt formation Various amounts of silty volcanic ash
materials may be also anticipated at the project site In general the dense basalt
formations at the project site may be hard to very hard and relatively unweathered to
moderately weathered It should be noted that cavities and or voids are commonly
present in the basaltic lava flows in the vicinity Therefore the presence of cavities and or
voids should be anticipated during the project construction

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials to be used as embankment fill
materials We wish to point out that the contractor should exercise while clearing and
grading near collapsed lava tube features and or cavities which may represent potential
lava tubes or cavities that may further collapse under the load of heavy equipment
In general we recommend ripping the fill areas of the project site to a depth of
about 3 feet below the existing ground surface at the commencement of grading The
purpose of ripping the fill subgrades is to assist in detecting and collapsing cavities and or
voids contained in these porous near surface materials As a minimum ripping of the
ground surface should extend at least 3 feet laterally beyond the fill areas

Fills and Backfills
In general we recommend capping the fill areas of the project site with a minimum
2 foot thick layer of structural fill material to serve as a choking layer for the larger sized
general fill materials below Structural fill materials should consist of well graded
granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient
fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass In general the
excavated materials may be re used as a source of structural fill materials provided that
they are processed to meet the maximum particle size and gradation requirements
presented above Where appropriate such as within utility corridors the thickness of the
structural fill layer may be increased to a minimum of 1 foot below the invert elevation of
the utilities to facilitate future construction of the utilities

Fill and backfill materials below the minimum 2 foot thick structural fill layer may
consist of general fill materials General fill and backfill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in size with
an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches Materials between 12 and 18 inches in

If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material

considered to provide the required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded

above fill material recommendations should be applied to the over cut areas and the fill

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction General fills and backfills should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 18 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers
vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Rock fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum dimension of the
majority of the rocks or 5 feet whichever is less Placement should be by haul trucks
traversing previously placed lifts and dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift
Spreading of the rock fill should be by a large bulldozer to facilitate seating of the rocks
The rock fill should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks traversing in front
of the current rock lift face and spraying water continuously during placement
Compaction equipment such as a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar heavy compaction
equipment providing suitable energy to achieve the required compaction should be
utilized The number of passes usually six to eight passes to be made to achieve proper
compaction should be determined at the on set of site grading by the performance of a trial
compaction test or by plate bearing tests Subsequent layers of rock fill above the
previously placed rock fill should provide a choking layer of smaller sized boulder materials
to fill in the voids between the boulders of the preceding rock fill layer
Conventional compaction testing generally is not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of
passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
Geolabs representative Based on this testing program the number of passes may then

Excavation
The project site is generally underlain by a a basalt and dense basalt formations
pahoehoe lavas ranging from medium hard to very hard It is anticipated that the a a
basalt and basalt formation near the ground surface may be readily excavated with normal
heavy excavation equipment such as excavators and also by ripping with large
bulldozers However some of the deeper excavations in the dense pahoehoe lava may
require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and field data from the borings
performed at the project site Contractors should be encouraged to examine the site
conditions and the subsurface data to make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
In general we envision that most of the cut slopes would expose a a basalt with
volcanic ash and fractured pahoehoe basalt formation In general we believe that the cut
slopes planned at the site may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Where dense basalt formations are exposed at the cut slope
faces the cut slopes may be steepened to 1H 1V or flatter A Geolabs representative
should observe and map the cut slopes during construction to evaluate whether the
exposed cut slope materials are consistent with our assumptions
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the general fill and structural fill materials
recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter In
addition fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be benched A keyway should
be provided for fill slopes greater than 10 feet in vertical height placed on existing ground
steeper than 5H 1V The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and
well compacted surface The fill slope face should be free of large boulders greater than
18 inches in largest dimension and should be free of voids which would allow erosion
and migration of fines to occur
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above ffill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the
design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face The fill slope face should be
finished to a relatively smooth and well compacted surface The fill slope face should be
