PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of the stairway and sidewalk site improvements
To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a track mounted drill rig and two operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings extending to depths of approximately
10 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a field
engineer geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the project
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 to 3 extending to depths of approximately 10 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a track mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled
at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The on site soils have high expansion potential Subgrade soil preparation and
moisture conditioning will be a critical factor in the performance of the foundations and
slabs Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
structure foundations and site grading are presented in the following sections of this letter
_ report
Structure Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used
to support the new stairway structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to
2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on
the compacted structural fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces The bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent ground level
In order to reduce the expansion potential of the on site soils the expansive
materials should be over excavated to a minimum depth of approximately 2 feet below the
bottom of the footing and slab The bottom of the footing excavations should be
moistened to a minimum of 3 above optimum moisture content and compacted to at
least 85 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of structural fill The resulting
excavation should be backfilled with structural fill material compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of the soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench Alternatively the footing should be extended to a depth as deep as the inverts of

passive pressure resistance should be further reduced for foundations located on slopes
Concrete Sidewalks
We understand that the existing walkways will be improved and new walkways will
also be constructed In order to reduce the expansion potential of the clay soil we
recommend that a minimum 24 inch thick layer of non expansive structural fill be placed
below the concrete walkways The subgrade soils below the walkway should be
moisture conditioned to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction The select granular fill should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints
should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle
intersections Areas adjacent to slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding next to
the slab

We anticipate that grading work for the project construction will consist of minor

be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important that a representative from
our office observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials
are encountered during excavation and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those encountered in our exploration

At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be

Structural fill material should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed coral basaltic gravel or cinder sand Imported materials should be well
graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
imported fill material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported materials should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for the intended use
In general fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Site Plan transmitted
by Bryce E Uyehara A I A Inc on The physical location of the
boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As previously mentioned groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the
time of our exploration However it must be noted that groundwater levels are subject to
change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors
