Dear Mr. DeMuesy: 
In accordance with the project specifications, we conducted a compressive pile load 
test on a selected test pile (Pile No. P108) for the MV-22 Hangar project within the Marine 
Corps Base Hawaii (MCBH) at Kaneohe Bay on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  This letter 
report presents the results of our field observations and the test data collected during the 
compressive pile load test conducted on June 20 and 21, 2016. 
The compressive pile load test was conducted on a 16.5-inch octagonal precast, 
prestressed concrete pile. The test pile was driven on June 8, 2016, using a 
Delmag D19-32 diesel hammer capable of delivering a maximum rated energy of about 
42,800 foot-pounds per blow of the hammer.  Pump setting No. 4 (rated energy of about 
42,800 foot-pounds per blow of the hammer) was used throughout the majority of driving 
to install the pile to the tip elevation of about -75.0 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL).  During the 
test pile driving operations, a log of the blow counts needed to advance the test pile was 
maintained by our engineer.  The approximate load test pile location is shown on the Test 
Pile Location Plan, Plate 1.  The test pile driving record is provided on Plate 2 for 
reference. 
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load 
Test Method, modified for longer hold times.  The maximum test load on the test pile was 
planned at about 280 kips.  In general, the load test was loaded to a test load of 280 kips 
(200 percent of the design load of 140 kips) in 17.5-kip load increments.  
In general, each load increment of the load test was held for at least 15 minutes. In 
addition, 2-hour holds were maintained at 140, 210, and 280 kips to evaluate the creep

pile is provided on Plate 4. Based on the shape of the load test curve and the project load 
test acceptance criteria, the 16.5-inch octagonal concrete test pile (Pile No. P108) met the 
test acceptance criteria at a test load of about 280 kips. After 2 hours of maintaining a load 
of 280 kips, the total gross settlement of the pile butt was less than the elastic pile 
compression, plus 0.15 inch, plus one percent of the pile tip diameter.
