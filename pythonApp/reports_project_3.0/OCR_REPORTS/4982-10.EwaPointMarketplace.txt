EARTHWORK AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
The Ewa Point Marketplace site is a rectangular shaped parcel of approximately
2 4 acres in area located at the intersection of Fort Weaver Road and Kaimalie Street
in the Ewa Beach area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is bounded on the
east by Fort Weaver Road on the north by Kaimalie Street on the west by the Ocean
Pointe development and on the south by a vacant parcel and the Ewa Beach Shopping
Center
The proposed retail development includes a two story L shaped main retail
building and two individual building pads for restaurant development The new building
will be surrounded by driveways and parking areas to accommodate approximately
142 passenger vehicles The main retail building will be constructed of Concrete
Masonry Unit CMU walls around the perimeter with steel columns concrete
slab on grade and metal roofing
The design finished floor elevations of the new buildings range from about
18 95 to 21 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Fills on the order of about 2 to 4 feet thick
were placed to achieve the building pads elevation

The site grading operations foundation excavation pavement and _ utility
construction for the Ewa Pointe Marketplace project were performed by
Jayar Construction Inc The site grading work consisted of footing excavation subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the excavated subgrades
underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The bottom of the footing excavations were recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density as described above
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 145 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 8 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
