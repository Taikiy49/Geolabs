PROGRESS REPORT NO 1
EARTH MOVEMENTS IN THE VICINITY OF THE
END OF WOOLSEY PLACE
In general accordance with our scope of work dated
we have recently performed our field exploration and instrumentation
program in the vicinity of the end of Woolsey Place for the proposed study
of earth movements in the area Presented below are the summary of our
work progress our preliminary findings and preliminary recommendations
for remedial work pertaining to the proposed study
Study Area
The subject study area considered in this progress report is
basically confined to a section of the slope between the eastern end of
Woolsey Place and Lanikaula Street below Four 4 private house lots
located on the slope are involved which include 3004 and 3008 Woolsey
Place and 3017 and 3021 Lanikaula Street
Landslide and or erosional damages also exist in adjacent house lots
on both the eastern and western sides of this slide area However the
observed damage patterns indicate that these areas should be considered
separately and their study needs to be supplemented with additional field
exploration and or instrumentation

Field Exploration and Instrumentation
The field exploration and instrumentation program included the
drilling and sampling of six 6 borings and the installation of three
3 piezometers and one 1 slope indicator in selected borings

Subsoil Conditions
Based on the borings the subsoil conditions along the slope from the
end of Woolsey Place to the lower Lanikaula Street may be generalized as
follows
1 The stable hillside portion at the eastern end of Woolsey Place
is primarily underlain by hard boulder colluvium at shallow
depth as indicated by Boring 103
The downslope portion of the cul de sac which has experienced
movement is underlain by cavities below the slab pavement loose
and wet fill and a cobble and boulder layer over hard colluvium
at deeper depths as indicated by Borings 101 and 102
2 About 6 to 7 feet of soft and wet adobe clay alluvium was
encountered over the hard colluvium in Borings 111 and 112 which
were drilled along the sliding slope
3 Very thick 45 feet adobe clay was encountered in Boring 113
which was drilled downslope along Lanikaula Street where there
was no visually observable landslide damage

Instrumentation Results
Piezometer Readings
Groundwater level was monitored in the three piezometers installed in
and 112 in the subject study area Since it is
suspected that shallow groundwater seepage or perched groundwater may
have played an important role in the landslide mechanism two of the
piezometers were designed to intercept water seepage between the
depths of 4 to 7 feet in Borings 102 and 112
Generally speaking a higher groundwater level about 4 to 8 feet
below ground was observed at the southern or upper side of the
slide in Borings 102 and 112 The water level measured fluctuated
with time and probably with rainfall in the study area Recently
the water level has been falling gradually
A much lower initial groundwater level maximum 35 feet below ground
was observed in Boring 101 The measured water level had risen
significantly since then and fluctuated with time and rainfall in the
study area Recently the water level has also been falling
gradually
No groundwater was observed in both Boring 103 at the southern
stable portion of Woolsey Place and Boring 113 on Lanikaula Street
Slope Indicator Readings
The slope indicator was installed in Boring 111 located mid slope
between Woolsey Place and Lanikaula Street near the apparent toe of
slide

No meaningful readings could be taken since the electronic measuring
device could not be lowered beyond 4 feet into the PVC pipe of the

It was visually estimated that the ground surface at the slope

Mechanism of Landslide
The site is underlain by adobe clay soils which are known for their
low strengths particularly when saturated Therefore the role of the
adobe clay in the slide movements was carefully studied Theoretical
analyses indicate that if the clay soils are saturated and weakened the
factor of safety against slope movement could be relatively low A review
of the soil profile Plate 2 indicates that due to the much deeper
thickness of clay along the lower portion of the slope near Lanikaula
Street this area should be much more susceptible to slides However
the opposite has occurred at the site i e the large movements have
occurred in the area where the clay soils are relatively thin This leads
to the conclusion that although the presence of the adobe clays contribute
to the observed movements they are probably not the primary cause in this
case
Based on the subsoil conditions and the instrumentation results we
believe that the subject landslide may be a shallow translational type
which may have been initiated by water seepage which weakened the strength
of the upper alluvium layer and also increased the driving forces on it
Visual observations indicate that the movements are continuing
As indicated by the excessive bending of the installed slope
indicator at shallow depth it appears that the majority of ground
movement is confined to the upper 4 to 6 feet of soft wet adobe clay

alluvium along a major portion of the slide It is our opinion that
this downslope movement in turn induced the severe settlement on Woolsey
Place where the soft wet clay layer is basically absent
Based on the groundwater conditions it appears that the water
seepage may come from several possible sources which include natural
hillside seepage and or leakage from broken utilities However the
absence of groundwater in the highest and lowest and
113 respectively indicates that the natural hillside seepage may not
have played a critical role in triggering the landslide Instead the
presence of a consistently higher but gradually dissipating water level in
the piezometers installed in Borings 102 and 112 at the west side of the
Slide seems to indicate local leakage from previous broken utilities
This probable water leakage might have created a perched water table at
shallow depth of about 6 5 feet and the water drained into the piezometer
in Boring 102 causing an initial significant rise in water level of about
10 feet overnight Since then the water level in the boring has been
generally dissipating indicating that the perched water may not come from
a continuous source such as a still leaking utility pipe
Since it would be extremely difficult to isolate natural hillside
seepage from utility leakages for examination at this stage it is our
opinion that the water seepage which probably initiated the landslide
should be considered as a combination of both natural hillside seepage and
broken utility leakages for remedial work design
Remedial Work
The remedial work should be considered separately for the moving
portion of Woolsey Place and the sliding portion of the slope in the upper
two 2 private house lots between Woolsey Place and Lanikaula Street
A schematic section Plate 2 illustrating the recommended remedial
work is attached

Woolsey Place
We recommend that the damaged sliding area of Woolsey Place be
removed down to hard colluvium at a depth of about 10 feet below the
existing ground and replaced with a buttress or gabion structure
along with a trench drain on the upslope side of the excavation
The purpose of the buttress fill or gabion structure is to stabilize
the road section from further sliding while the trench drain is to
intercept natural hillside seepage and or future utility leakage if
any
It should be noted that the buttress fill or gabion structure may
need to be extended into the private properties to achieve a stable
road section on Woolsey Place
The concept of using reinforced soil was considered however it may
not be applicable in this case since the reinforcing strips would
interfere with the nearby existing relocated utilities
Private House
The two private house lots 3004 and 3008 Woolsey Place located
downslope of Woolsey Place had translated with the landslide and
suffered extensive damages including wide cracks footing settlement
tilted retaining walls and stairways etc
Since the slide is predominantly a shallow translational type it is
our opinion that the two upper houses if considered structurally
salvable may be underpinned down to the very stiff to hard colluvium
at about 8 to 10 feet below the existing ground The underpinning
foundations or structures such as caissons piers columms should be

designed for resisting the lateral pressures from the sliding soils
which may continue to move downslope for a considerable period of
time before the moisture contents of the soils are reduced
sufficiently
Due to the present condition of the existing structures and difficult
access for equipment it is obvious that the remedial construction
may be very costly Therefore an alternative may be to remove the
houses then remove the sliding adobe clay and then
reconstruct replace the houses
The other two private house lots downslope 3017 and 3021 Lanikaula
Street suffered relatively minor damages which are generally
concentrated in the backyards near the apparent toe of slide It is
our opinion that the present foundation safety of these two lower
houses may not be threatened provided the above recommended remedial
work will be performed for the upper sliding road and houses
Additional Studies
Additional studies are in progress at the two adjacent lots to this
area 3002 and 3014 Woolsey Place Preliminary evaluations indicate that
the mechanism of movement and remedial work may be similar to that on this
report
Topographic survey data is required prior to final design of the
remedial work along Woolsey Place This work is also in progress
Further studies will also be performed to evaluate other possible
buttress structures for use at the site
LIMITATIONS
The general site is underlain by adobe clay soils which are subject
to long term creep movements The recommendations presented in this
report should help to reduce drastic movements however creep movements
may continue

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
slope indicator and observation wells Variations of conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by measuring from the attached Site Plan Elevations of the data points
were not available at this stage of report preparation The physical
locations of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuations
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature utility
leakages and other factors
