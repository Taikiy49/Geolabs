This is a summary of production pile driving for the above
project
The total number of piles driven were 203 of 16 octagonal
and 48 of 12 Square concrete piles The enclosed Table l
2 summarize the driving record for each of the above two pile
types and any irregularities which may have occurred
All of the piles were driven under our inspection and in accor
dance with the recommendations of the referenced September 22
1975 report The Pile No reference system shown in the summary
conforms to locations indicated on the attached Foundation Plan
of this report

Because of localized loose pockets some of the recommended
85 feet long piles did not achieve the 100 ton bearing capacity
at this length Two alternate solutions are proposed
1 Add a splice can and continue driving a minimum
of another 30 feet long pile addition in order
to avoid the splice connection being located
within the upper 30 feet of soft mud formation
The purpose is to prevent potential buckling
of splicing within the soft formation
z Accepted the reduced pile capacity of the already
driven in place piles and a short length low
bearing capacity pile was driven to the first
coral layer to obtain the extra additional
bearing capacity
To speed up driving time and saving on splice can connection
the second solution was used whenever possible This was con
Sidered necessary under the stairwell locations where the re
quired pile up lifts prevent splicing
Because of excessive deviation of pile nos 200 and 201 from
their correct positions in the pile group an additional pile
no 200A was driven in accordance with the structural engineer s
new location to compensate for the above deviations
