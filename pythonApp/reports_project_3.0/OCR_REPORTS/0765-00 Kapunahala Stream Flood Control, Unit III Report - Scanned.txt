INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface soil conditions at the site to develop pertinent
soil data and engineering recommendations for the design
and construction cost estimates of the proposed flood
control structure
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed flood control project consists of constructing
a new drainage structure about 1200 feet long mostly on
the western side of Kahekili Highway
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
The new drain will cross under Kahekili Highway and empty
into an existing open channel on the eastern side of the
Highway The existing drainage channel will be improved
as part of the flood control project
Preliminary plans indicate that the new drain structure
will be a 10 by 6 feet concrete box culvert and the exist
ing drainage channel will be enlarged to an 18 feet rec
tangular section
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii just
north of Likelike Highway near the intersection of Likelike
and Kahekili Highways as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The upper western section of the alignment runs in an east
west direction toward Kahekili Highway and is in an unde
veloped field of dense tall grass banana and hau trees
and adjacent to residences to the north This section
is wet and swampy with several small streams draining near
the beginning of the alignment The ground is perpetually
wet in the upper section and small spring may exist
At Kahekili Highway the alignment runs northward paralell
to the highway until it reaches an existing drainage channel
running through the nearby residential subdivision This
area is about 8 to 10 feet lower than Kahekili Highway
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 3
The proposed alignment crosses Kahekili Highway adjacent
to the existing drain culverts and intersects an existing
open channel structure on the eastern side of the highway
Between Kahekili Highway and Keneke Road the channel is
a partially lined trapezoidal structure approximately 8
feet deep and 20 feet wide East of Kaneke Road the
_channel is a rectangular concrete structure approximately
8 feet high and 18 feet wide
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Subsurface conditions were explored by drilling six test
borings to depths of 16 5 to 31 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the test
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Soil samples
were obtained at selected depths as shown on the boring logs
Plates A 1 through A 6
A detailed description of the field exploration methods
and laboratory test results are given in the Appendix
Geology
The project site is situated on the lower windward slope
of the Koolau Range at the base of the Pali This area
is part of the deeply eroded section of the Koolau Range
typified by detrital deposits overlying highly weathered
basalts
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
An extended period of weathering and erosion has produced
alluvial and colluvial deposits of clay boulders sand
and silt at the foot of the range
Soil Conditions
Based on the test borings and field observations the sub
surface conditions can be divided into 2 sections east
and west of Kahekili Highway
The upper western section generally consists of a soft
peat and highly organic clay 4 to 8 feet thick grading
into a loose to very loose silty sand to a depth of 15
to 20 feet
The lower eastern section except the area immediately
beneath Kahekili Highway is generally underlain by a
5 foot layer of stiff brown silty clay underlain by a
soft gray brown alluvial silty clay layer 10 to 15 feet
thick
The area under Kahekili Highway as reflected in test
boring B 2 did not encounter any soft layers under the
highway fill However previous borings below the high
way have encountered soft alluvial deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Poor subsurface soil conditions were encountered along the
project alignment particularly in the western half of the
site where soft clays and peat over loose silty sand allu
vial deposits were encountered from near the ground surface
to below the proposed invert level In the western portion
the borings encountered soft clays at the invert level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
High water levels were encountered in the western portion
of the proposed alignment and therefore dewatering will
be required
Due to the proximity of residences along portions of the
alignment sheeting and bracing should be used in the
trench excavations to reduce loss of ground and the
resulting settlements to the adjacent residences
Dewatering operations should also be done with care
to prevent loss of ground
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The subsoils at the proposed invert elevation have very
low bearing capacities However the proposed construc
tion will not materially increase the vertical loading
upon these subsoils if no additional fills are placed
in the future above the existing ground elevation
To provide uniform support and a working platform a
2 foot layer of granular material should be provided
under the structure
Since the net increase in load on the subsoils caused by
the construction of the culvert structure will be small
the resulting settlements should also be relatively small
if the culvert is properly constructed The excavating
and dewatering of the trench should be done with care to
prevent loss of ground and large settlements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
Lateral Earth Pressures
The lateral earth pressures acting on the drain structures
will depend on the type of backfill used and the extent
of the backfill material behind the structure walls
For the design of the box culvert structure the following
lateral earth pressures are recommended
Select Granular Material 55 p c f equivalent
above water fluid pressure
Select Granular Material 25 p c f equivalent
below water fluid pressure
For the open channel sections an equivalent fluid pressure
of 50 p c f can be used to calculate the lateral loads on
the walls Granular backfill material should be used and
weepholes provided through the channel walls to reduce the
buildup of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Culvert Construction
Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should
be the choice of the contractor within practical
limits The estimated depths of excavation will
be about 9 to 22 feet Easy digging is anticipated
through the soft subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
Sheeting and bracing is recommended for the project
to prevent a cave in of the trench walls Due to
the low strengths and high water contents of the
soils and the proximity to existing residences
special care must be exercised to prevent a failure
of the trench walls or upheaval of the trench bottom
due to the excavating and dewatering methods
Sheet pile supports may be required for the trench
stability
Where the alignment is not near existing residences
or structures and sufficient space is available
the excavation may be cut back at slopes of 3 hori
zontal to 1 vertical or flatter in lieu of the wall
supports
If practical the excavation should be made in short
sections and backfilled as soon as possible
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away
from the tops of the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of excavation
should be continuously monitored for cracks or dips
and other indications of movements and possible
sloughing of the trench walls This monitoring is
particularly critical in the area where existing
retaining walls and residential structures are con
structed adjacent to the trench excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
Dewatering
High groundwater levels were noted in the upper western
portion of the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the
contractor provided adequate stability of the exca
vation is monitored The alluvial deposits encountered
in the borings along the alignment consisted mostly of
organic clays and peat over a silty sand with about
20 to 40 per cent fines
The project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out the soil fines pumping clear
water only
The dewatering should be coordinated with the exca
vation support system so that the stability of
the excavation is not jeopardized The operations
should be carried out without creating a quick
condition or softening the bottom of excavation
An alternative to dewatering would be to place
the pipe in a wet trench condition
Backfill
Select granular material should be used as backfill
for the culvert and channel structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
Due to the high moisture and organic contents
much of the on site material will not be suitable
as backfill material Some of the alluvial silty
sands can be used as backfill provided the moisture
contents can be reduced
The backfill should be compacted to a minimum of
90 per cent of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 The space left by the removal of
the sheeting should also be backfilled Proper
compaction of the trench backfill material will
reduce future settlements of the backfill and the
adjacent ground surface
If the area away from the existing residences and
streets will not be utilized in the future for
dwellings or other structures a cost savings
could be obtained by permitting the contractor
to use the on site sandy soils as backfill with
minimal compactive effort However settlements
will occur within the trench due to the consoli
dation of these materials and the lateral earth
pressure acting on the culvert will increase

by drilling 6 borings to depths of 16 5 to 31 5 feet below

geologist who continuously monitored the drilling opera

in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6

split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
