PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil conditions at the
proposed reservoir sites in order to provide recommendations for use in the design of the
site grading and foundations of the proposed construction The general location of the
project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The work was performed in general accordance with our revised proposal of
to Tom Nance Water Resources Engineering and modified by our
Memorandum of Understanding dated August 2 1991 The scope of work included the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tions of the surface soil conditions and to select locations for the borings
3 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
4 Drilling and sampling ten 10 borings to depths of 11 1 to 90 0 feet below
the existing ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered

6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations

in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed construction consists of two 2 four 4 million gallon M G
reinforced concrete reservoirs each approximately one hundred fifty 150 feet in diameter
and twenty 20 feet high It is our understanding that the bottoms of the reservoirs will
be at an elevation of one hundred eighty five 185 feet above Mean Sea Level
Extensive excavation entailing cuts on the order of fifty 50 to sixty 60 feet
measured vertically will be required to develop a level area on which to found the
reservoirs
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the north of Lunalilo Freeway Interstate Route H 1
at the base of Puu Makakilo in Ewa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2738 00 Page 4
The reservoirs will be sited on an area encompassing approximately five 5 acres
which slopes in a generally southerly direction with ground surface elevations ranging
from approximately one hundred eighty 180 to two hundred sixty 260 feet above Mean
Sea Level The site is located somewhat less than a mile along an existing dirt road
from Makakilo Drive which is on the eastern edge of Makakilo City The site is currently
overgrown with thick grass and scrub keawe trees and boulders distributed randomly on
the surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Reservoir
Borings B 1 through B 4 disclosed that based on the proposed finish floor
elevation the reservoir foundation would bear within two 2 feet of or directly on a thick
layer of saprolitic sandy silt with gravel and boulders which should provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed reservoir
Either a mat foundation or a continuous ring footing with interior columns resting
on spread footings may be used for this reservoir An allowable bearing capacity up to

3000 p s f may be used for the design The allowable bearing capacity value may be
increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Our borings did not encounter hard rock formations under the site of this reservoir
at or near the proposed elevation of the bottom of the foundation If however an
unanticipated intrusion should be encountered during construction the area should be
overexcavated by eighteen 18 inches to provide for a cushion layer of properly
compacted granular material to provide uniform bearing
Based on the borings laboratory test data and estimated reservoir loads the
anticipated settlements are on the order of less than one half inch total and one fourth
inch differential
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p s f per foot of
depth can be used A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the structure and foundations

Special Considerations
As noted hereinbefore Boring B 4 located between the two reservoirs indicates
a strata of dense saprolitic silty sand with gravel and clinker with a thickness of
approximately fifteen 15 feet between their proposed bearing elevations and the
basaltic rock This has been taken into consideration in selection of the
recommended bearing capacity for Reservoir I For Reservoir Il consideration
must be given for two possible conditions
1 The rock may be encountered at the elevation of the footing bottom
throughout
2 The rock may be found to exist two 2 to three 3 feet below the
elevation of the footing bottom in a limited areal extent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2738 00 Page 8
In the event Condition No 1 pertains the foundation design and construction may
be considered to be straightforward
In the event Condition No 2 is encountered the saprolitic material may be
removed and replaced with concrete placed monolithically with the foundation
Cavity Probing
Due to the possible occurrence of cavities and lava tubes in the underlying lava
flows we recommend that the footing construction portion of the project include a
program of probing and grouting to detect potential voids below the reservoir foundations
The wall footing excavation should be probe at approximately 10 feet on centers One
probe should be drilled at each individual spread footing The probe holes should have
a minimum diameter of 2 inches and extend below the bottom of the footing a depth of
10 feet The probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe holes should be
made to delineate the horizontal extend of the cavity The probing and grouting
operations should be monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommendations
are carried out properly and to locate potential voids near the footing excavations
Slope
Based on our interpretation of the boring logs we feel that two differing cut slope
designs should be adopted for this project one for the western portion of the excavation
and the other for the eastern portion
The western portion of the slope should be at a ratio of one and one half horizontal
to one vertical for the lower twenty five 25 vertical feet of height then an eight
8 foot wide bench and a V slope up to daylight
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2738 00 Page 9
For the eastern portion of the excavation the cut slope may be one horizontal to
one vertical 1H 1V for the first twenty feet followed by another slope of one horizontal
to one vertical 1H 1V and a final slope of one and one half horizontal to one vertical
to daylight The lower slopes in this area should have eight 8 foot wide
benches at their tops
The two cut slope designs are shown schematically on Plate 3
The requisite transition between the two cut slope designs should be centered on
the Slope Transition Line shown on Plate 3
A diversion ditch should be provided around the upper crest line of the excavation
and erosion protection by means of grassing or other appropriate means should be
provided

Site Preparation
The topography indicates some minimal development exists at the site consisting
of a concrete lined ditch and an underground six 6 inch PVC pipe exposed at
three locations Disposition of these features should be as determined by the
project civil engineer
Disposal of Waste
The project entails excavation in its entirety There is no embankment construction
contemplated at the time of preparation of this report Excavated material will

consist of topsoil and other materials if any resulting from clearing and grubbing
residual and saprolitic soils and basaltic rock Off site wastes areas will be

governed by the nature of the areas and requirements of their owners
Methods of Excavation
It is anticipated that the residual and saprolitic soils may be excavated with
conventional equipment with no untoward difficulty Some ripping may be required
at deeper elevations
Hard ripping and a requirement for the use of hoe rams should be anticipated for
excavation of the rock materials
The above comments regarding excavation requirements are based on our
evaluation of cores recovered from our borings and examination of outcrops and
cut faces along Interstate H 1 It is recommended that contractors wishing to bid
on this project make their own independent evaluation of conditions to be
encountered to form the basis of the bids
Trenching
Trenches and backfill for influent and effluent pipes outside the reservoir perimeters
should be in accordance with the requirements of the Board of Water Supply Any
pipes that may be located below the reservoirs should be provided with concrete
jacketing placed integrally and concomitantly with the foundations

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommenda
tions given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the excavation and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifica
tions or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made This is particularly important for this project with respect to the
foundation of Reservoir Il and possible modifications that may be deemed advisable
depending on the depth of overburden actually occurring over the basaltic formation

evaluate the recommendations given in this report
