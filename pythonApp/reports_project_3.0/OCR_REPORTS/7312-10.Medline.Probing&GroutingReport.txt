Dear Ms. Slattery: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Medline project from 
August 1 through 5, 2016. The probing and grouting observation work was performed in 
general accordance with our fee proposal dated July 5, 2016. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is located at Lot 14 within the Kapolei Business Park, Phase 2 
development in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. Based on the information 
provided, we understand that the project will involve the construction of a warehouse 
building along the east side of the property and sixteen sunken loading docks that can 
accommodate semi-trailer trucks with 45-foot long containers along the west side of the 
property. An asphalt concrete paved parking lot is also planned along the southeastern 
corner of the property to serve the new warehouse facility. 
Probing and Grouting Program 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the new building 
structure planned for the Medline facility in accordance with the recommendations

presented in our geotechnical engineering exploration report dated February 2, 2016. The 
purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of 
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below 
the building structure. 
Swinerton Builders surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations 
prior to the probing and grouting operations. In general, the probe holes were located at 
the column footings and spaced at approximately 10-foot intervals along the wall footings. 
It should be noted that probe holes were not required for non-bearing walls, such as 
partition walls, loading dock retaining walls, and trash enclosure walls. The approximate 
probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
Geolabs, Inc. performed the probing work for the Medline project using 
two truck-mounted drill rigs equipped with continuous flight augers.  The probe holes were 
about 4 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 11 to 18 feet below the 
existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing 
bottom elevations. 
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted 
of surface fill materials over tannish white to tan coral formation and coralline detritus 
materials.  After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with 
flowable fill with a compressive strength of 500 to 1,000 pounds per square inch (psi).  The 
grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Island Ready-Mix Concrete, Inc. and the 
boom pump was operated by Ohana Concrete Pumping Company, Inc. 
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and 
recorded the probe depth and approximate grout take.  A total of 202 probe holes 
(including 15 additional probe holes as discussed below) with an aggregate length of 
about 2,505.75 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled for the 
warehouse structure as summarized in the following table.  The probing and grouting 
summary is provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.7.
