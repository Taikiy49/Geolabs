In accordance with your request a geotechnical engineering exploration was
conducted at portions of the proposed golf course to evaluate the suitability of the soils for
borrow material This letter report presents the results of the test pit exploration
Project Considerations
The entire project encompasses about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of
Kauai and includes hotel vacation ownership commercial park golf course and
residential uses Portions of the project s infrastructure have been constructed including
the mass grading on site regional wastewater treatment plant portions of the on site
drainage system non potable source and transmission line for the golf course and
potential potable water sources
Significant mass grading is anticipated for the development Portions of the site are
underlain by hard basalt rock at relatively shallow depths Therefore it is desired to
delineate areas where significant thickness of soil may be present for use as borrow soils
for the mass grading Portions of the future golf course Hole 7 and 13 were
identified as potential borrow sites Test pit excavation was used to explore the thickness
of soil available at these selected sites
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the potential borrow sites To
accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts

Findings and Recommendations
The test pit exploration disclosed that the future golf course Hole 7 and
13 have significant thickness of soils that may be used for borrow material It appears
that hard rock formation is generally 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface
The surface clayey silt soils are generally competent and suitable for embankment
fill However the clay content often increases with depth resulting in soils with moderately
high expansion potential Therefore these soils should be not be used within about 2 feet
below structures or should be carefully checked on an individual basis prior to use as
structural fill
Guidelines for placement and compaction of the soils presented in our individual
geotechnical reports for the development should be followed

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field test pits Variations of conditions between and
beyond the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the test pits depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the test pits were measured at the times
shown on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal
rainfall and other factors
