during the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of the new East Kapolei Fire Station within a
2 2 acre lot at the East Kapolei I Development in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project site is bounded by Kapolei Parkway to the south Kinoiki Street to the
west Kamokuiki Street to the north and another 2 2 acre open lot along the east
Based on the information provided the proposed fire station will have a plan area of
about 189 feet by 146 feet The fire station will consist of a 4 507 square foot apparatus
room office space living quarters storage rooms equipment rooms dormitory rooms
bathrooms and toilets kitchen and dining room exercise room library and conference
room communication room fueling area with above ground diesel fuel storage tanks
paved access driveways and parking areas We understand that the fire station will be a
single story concrete structure with an open bay of approximately 54 feet wide for the

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Okada Trucking Co Ltd performed the site grading operations foundation
excavation pavement construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of
clearing and grubbing footing excavation subgrade preparation placement and
compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and
backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the subgrades were scarified to a
minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The prepared subgrades were then over excavated where necessary to
approximately 18 inches below the building slabs below the cushion fill layer sidewalk
slabs and pavement sections to provide the recommended 18 inch thick non expansive
select granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent
of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The base course and subbase course
under the AC and PCC pavement were generally placed in near level lifts brought to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the foundation excavation and preparation the excavated footing bottom was
generally recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Fills and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor Compaction requirement for subgrades under slab and pavement
areas subjected to vehicular traffic was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and

During the site grading operations sample of the on site soils and imported
materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 248 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 16 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated March 11 2011
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
