INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface conditions
and to provide recommendations for the design and construction of the
proposed gymnasium facilities at Kalaheo High School
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

Site Description
Kalaheo High School is located on the east face slope of the
ridge separating Kaneohe Bay and Kawainui Swamp in Kailua
Oahu Hawaii The school campus is at the base of the slope
adjacent to Mokapu Saddle Road
The proposed gymnasium facilities will be located immediately
south of the parking lot under construction The original
ground of the proposed building area is covered by dense Koa
trees burned off by a recent fire
The slope behind the gymnasium is stable and no evidence of
slide was noted during this investigation
The runoff in the proposed building area generally drains toward
the existing gully and then collected by a storm drain located
at the southern end of the proposed gymnasium site A new con
crete ditch is presently under construction along the north end
of the proposed office storage structure
Proposed Constructions
The proposed gymnasium site is approximately 230 by 100 feet in
plan dimensions
The central gymnasium structure is about 33 feet high and 140 by
110 feet in plan The 16 foot high classroom and office storage
structures will be located at the south and north ends of the gym
nasium

The exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil conditions
within the proposed building areas
Generally the site is blanketed by a surface layer of dark brown
expansive when wetted Below the dark brown clay very stiff to

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface soil conditions encountered during the explora
tory program were relatively good from a soils and foundation
engineering viewpoint Spread and wall footings may be utilized
to support the proposed structures The surface soil layer is
expansive and it should not be utilized as backfill materials
under the proposed building structures
Foundation
Column and bearing wall footing foundations should extend below
the uark brown surface soil and into the stiff tannish brown
clayey silt soils Allowable bearing pressure of 5 000 pounds
per square foot may be used for the on site in situ formation
To minimize differential settlements between cut and fill areas
all footings should bear on similar in situ clayey silt soil
In the southern corner of the classroom wing structure new fill
up to 10 feet high will be placed upon the existing gully Footing
foundation there should extend through the new fill and bear upon
the underlying tannish brown clayey silt formation
It is estimated that total settlements for the structures should
be less than 3 4 inch with a differential settlement of less than
3 8 inch in each separated structures

For the portions of the footing foundation located next to the
existing gully at the southwest corner of the building the
outer edge of footing should have a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 4 feet out to the slope surface
Slab On Grade Floor Slab
Slab on grade beared on the light brown clayey silt soil can be
utilized without special provisions for expansive soil Slab
on grade located on the dark brown surface soil mantle near the
existing ground elevation should be designed for expansive soil
conditions A minimum of 2 feet of non expansive backfill should
be used if conventional concrete slab or walkways are utilized
The underlying on site tannish brown clayey silt is siightly
expansive and it may be used as the select backfill
Slab on grade with floor covering should be protected by a plastic
vapor barrier
Groundwater was not encountered for a depth of 35 feet below exis
ting ground surface However under the building floor slab a
4 inch layer of 3 4 inch minus crushed rock base course is recom
mended to obtain uniform slab support and for drainage purpose
It is recommended that the subgrade be compacted wet of optimum
moisture content to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM Designation D 1557 72T
To reduce slab distress by differential settlements between the
slab on cut and fill areas joints should be utilized between

Retaining Wall
Retaining wall should be designed to resist active lateral
loads equivalent to a fluid pressure of 40 pef if on site
light brown silty soils are utilized as backfill The back
fill should be compacted between 90 to 95 maximum dry density
Over compaction behind the wall can increase the lateral earth
pressure above the design value The on site dark brown surface
clayey materials should not be utilized for backfill behind re
taining wall
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized for determining sliding
resistance of the retaining wall foundations
Weep holes or drains should be provided behind the wall with one
foot minimum free draining granular materials to prevent the build
up of hydrostatic pressures
Earthwork and Grading
The surface layer of dark brown clayey soil has a potential of high
Swelling and low shear value when wetted Therefore it should
not be used as a source of structural fill However the under
lying brown clayey silt below the surface mantle is a suitable
fill material

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavationsbe inspected by a
soil engineer prior to the placement of concrete or steel reinforcing
to assure that adequate foundation bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
preliminary subsurface investigations

As previously discussed the backfilling and earthwork operations
should be inspected on a full time basis This inspection should
include the necessary field density testing to assure that the
proper degree of compaction is consistently achieved Excessive
compaction of the backfill behind the retaining wall can increase
the lateral earth pressures above the design values
To minimize differential settlement concrete slab on fill areas
should be delayed as much as possible and preferably after the
upper structure is completed

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
5 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 19 5 feet to 35 0 feet below the exis
ting ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plate A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
samplers or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples as an aid in the soil classification

A Modified Proctor Compaction test was performed on the tannish
brown clayey silt of the on site soil to evaluate the suitability
of the soil as fill The results are given on Plate A 6
Six swell tests were conducted on near surface samples to measure
the swell potential of the soil which will be used for fill material
The swell test results are summarized on Plate A 7
A total of three direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics OMecnemenGOUn
