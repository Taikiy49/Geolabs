Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Areas F and G of Ewa by Gentry Makai West
Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading operations for Areas F and G of Ewa by Gentry Makai West from
January 1998 through September 1998
The subject site is located on the western side of Kapolei Parkway and south of Geiger
Road in Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is also bounded by the Sun Terra
single family development to the east and the Coral Creek Golf Course to the west
The mass grading work for Areas F and G was performed by RHS Lee Inc The mass
grading work consisted of fills on the order of 3 to 5 feet in height Cuts were only made at
high areas to allow placement of 24 inches of coralline material from the finished grade The
fill materials were generally generated from Area G3 and the western end of Area G2 of the
adjacent Coral Creek Golf Course The available fill materials generally consisted of
tannish white silty coral sand
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed the exposed subgrades
were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 The fill material was then placed in level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and
the test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are
summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 9 of this report Note that the test numbers are not
consecutive as the adjacent golf course grading tests were also included in the numbering
