Dear Mr Okada
In accordance with your facsimile request of May 27th we are presenting hereby the
following recommendations pertaining to the proposed retaining structures located at the above
referenced project site near the corner of Hualalai and Kapiolani Streets
It is our understanding that retaining structures will be required to provide grading
separation at the project site Based on Boring No 3 of our soils report we recommend that
an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot be used for the design
of the short 3 to 4 foot high CRM retaining wall bearing in the near surface basalt clinder
formation The allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loadings such as wind or seismic forces For footings located near
the top of slopes or on slopes the footings should be embedded deep enough such that the
horizontal distance from the outside edge at the base of the footing to the edge of the slope
is not less than 8 feet
Assuming that compacted granular material such as select borrow material or
equivalent is used for backfill a lateral earth pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot equivalent
fluid pressure may be used to design walls with level backfill in active condition Lateral load

Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure
A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of permeable material such
as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a filter fabric and
immediately adjacent to the wail with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the
wall discharging to a discharge facility A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent should
be placed between the drainage material and the compacted backfill material As an
alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
instead of the drainage material with filter fabric The prefabricated drainage product should
also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the height of the wail should be considered in the design For
uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wail a rectangular distribution
with uniform pressure equal to 30 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting on the
entire height of the wall which is unrestrained at the top may be used in design Additional
analyses during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and line
loads
