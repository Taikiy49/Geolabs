INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed upgrade of Kaunakakai Wharf at Kaunakakai Harbor near the
town of Kaunakakai on the island of Molokai Hawaii The general location of the project
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2451 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and may
not be sufficient for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to upgrade the existing Kaunakakai Wharf by reconstructing part of
the wharf deck to provide additional load carrying capacity This increased capacity is
to support heavy forklift operations Hyster Challenger Model H620B for loading and
unloading containers The wharf area scheduled to be upgraded in the current project
is located between Bay Nos 37 and 42 and between gridlines K through M as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing wharf is currently supported by piles with vertical capacities estimated
to be on the order of about 80 tons It is our understanding that higher capacity piles will
be needed to provide adequate support for the upgraded wharf area
Based on the details provided to us no site grading is anticipated for the proposed
wharf upgrade

conditions at the project site The information obtained will be used to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommen
dations pertinent to the design of the foundation support system and for the installation
of new piles at the subject wharf area

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Kaunakakai Wharf in Kaunakakai Harbor near
the town of Kaunakakai on the island of Molokai Hawaii
The existing wharf is situated approximately 550 yards southerly of the town
of Kaunakakai and is accessible by an asphaltic concrete paved road
The wharf has reinforced concrete decks and extensions built between the
late 1920 s and the early 1950 s In 1969 to 1970 the decks were strengthened
to provide greater load carrying capacity
The existing concrete wharf deck is approximately 687 feet in length and
fronts an existing quay which is paved with asphaltic concrete The portion
currently proposed for upgrading is between gridlines K and M A part of this
portion is also paved with asphaltic concrete probably over the original concrete
deck
The wharf surface is generally level with elevations averaging about seven
7 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Since suitable bearing strata were not encountered until depths of about 115 feet
below the existing wharf deck surface long piles will be required to develop sufficient
vertical load carrying capacity to support the new wharf deck with its increased load
support requirement

Pile Foundation
prestressed concrete piles with a minimum total length of 130 feet per pile be used
The piles would develop their load capacity under axial compression partially from
adhesion between the piles and the subsoils and primarily from end bearing on the
medium dense to dense sandy silt layer encountered at about 115 feet below the wharf
The final tip elevation should be about 130 feet below the existing wharf deck elevation
unless sufficient penetration resistance is encountered at shallower depths
An allowable vertical capacity of up to 100 tons per pile may be used for the pile
foundation design
Pile settlements on the order of about One half of an inch are estimated due to the
anticipated loadings and the elastic shortening of the pile under the loadings
_Two shorter pile sections with lengths of 60 and 70 feet may be spliced together
to form an integral pile of 130 feet long The splice can should be at least six 6 feet in
length and fabricated of high grade steel at least one fourth of an inch in thickness

Pile Driving
The piles should be driven until one of the following is satisfied
1 The pile tip reaches the recommended tip elevation
2 The driving encounters a resistance of 72 blows per foot for the last
foot of penetration or
3 The driving encounters a resistance of 6 blows per inch for the last
three 3 inches of penetration
The pile driving hammer used should be capable of delivering a minimum of about
Zé
30 000 foot pounds of energy
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be
rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving process is
interrupted for more than four 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the
required depth
During soft driving i e when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the hammer
stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so that excessive tensile stress
is not developed in the pile
The pile driving should be monitored by a qualified geotechnical engineer to see
that the piles are driven to the required depth or penetration resistance

Test Pile Program
The pile capacity and driving criteria recommended herein were developed from
our analyses of the data obtained from an exploration consisting of two 2
borings A test pile program is recommended to provide field data to verify some
of the design assumptions and to develop an economical pile foundation system
for the new wharf deck
The test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To more accurately determine the production pile lengths
2 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the pile driving criteria and
_ predrilling depths
We recommend that about ten 10 percent of the total piles or a minimum of five
5 piles be test driven at the site These test piles may be incorporated into the
actual foundation system
As a part of the test pile program a pile load test should be budgeted The
selected test pile should be load tested to two 2 times the design pile capacity
or 200 tons in accordance with the standard loading procedure of ASTM Test
Method D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for _
production pile driving later The test pile program should be conducted under the

Predrilling
We recommend that predrilling be used to attempt to penetrate the cobble and
boulder fills encountered in Boring No 1 prior to pile driving It is our opinion that a
minimum 10 feet of predrilling may be required at all proposed pile locations within the
narrow upgrade area between gridlines K and M
However it should be noted that the interlocking cobbles and boulders may cave
in after the initial predrilling Therefore repeated predrilling or the use of casing or drilling
mud may be required at any given pile location We recommend that sufficient
contingency fund be allowed for additional predrilling and or for the use of other feasible
construction method s to allow pile driving through the cobble and boulder fill layer

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by taping from the
existing structures as indicated on the General Site Plan provided by Arnold Okubo
Associates
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross section are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Field Exploration

depths of 128 5 to 148 5 feet below the existing wharf deck at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard
split spoon sampler for 18 to 24 inches with a 140 pound hammmer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last or the middle 12 inches are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples and some soil samples were obtained by coring with a HQ
wireline core barrel The Core Recovery REC and Rock Quality Designation RQD of
the core samples are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
