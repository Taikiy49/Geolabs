geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Expansion of Lanai Quarry on the

above project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to expand the existing Lanai Quarry to provide aggregate and fill
material for continued development on the Island of Lanai The project site is roughly
triangular in shape encompasses about 52 acres of land area and is located to the south
of Lanai City near the Manele Golf Course The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
At present there is an existing quarry operation in the southwestern corner of the
site The remainder of the site is vacant undeveloped land that slopes generally down to
the south and is vegetated with weeds shrubs and bushes typical of dryland areas in
Hawaii

DISCUSSION
As described above our field exploration encountered a relatively shallow
surface layer of overburden material to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface In situ basaltic rock formation was encountered under the overburden
material and extended to the maximum depths explored The basalt formation when
first encountered was highly weathered and graded to slightly weathered with
increasing depth In Boring No 2 the basalt formation encountered directly below the
overburden material consisted of volcanic clinker and extended to a depth of about
20 feet below the ground surface
The borings encountered material similar to that exposed in the existing quarry
excavation There is a significant amount of clinker and scoriaceous basalt which will
generate B grade rock with lesser amounts of potential A grade rock Substantial
processing may be required to segregate the two grades of rock material
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a
hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt If 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and

LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from two field borings within a 52 acre site Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until quarrying is underway
The boring locations were detetmined by taping from reference points shown on
the Proposed Quarry Site Plan prepared by R M Towill Corporation dated
The physical location of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representation of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation
variation in rainfall temperatures and other factors
