INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new addition at 2625 Anuenue Street in the Manoa area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 2625 Anuenue Street in the Manoa area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to expand the existing living
quarters with the construction of a new addition at the existing carport area
Based on the information provided by the project architect the proposed
expansion will consist of a one story wood framed structure with post footings and slab
on grade construction Detailed information pertaining to the structural loads of the
proposed expansion was not available at the time this report was prepared In general
we anticipate the proposed addition to be lightly loaded with column loads of less than
20 kips Minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2625 Anuenue Street in the Manoa area of Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is rectangular in shape covering an area
of approximately 9 940 square feet It is currently occupied by a one story wood
framed structure at the center with a smaller house and a carport at the south side of
the subject lot The remaining lot area is occupied by grass lawn with a concrete
driveway located along the southeastern property line The subject property is bounded
by tile and rock walls wood and chain link fences along the east south and west
property lines as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the topographic survey map provided the site is sloping gently with
existing ground elevations ranging from about 184 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the
east to about 191 feet MSL at the west

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring to a depth
of approximately 21 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location
of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring encountered an 8 inch thick concrete slab underlain by a very thin
layer of sandy gravel cushion material This was underlain by stiff silty clays clayey
silts and sandy silts to a depth of approximately 8 feet below the existing slab
surface Medium dense sandy soils were then encountered to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 21 feet below the existing slab surface A boulder was
encountered in our drilled boring from a depth of about 2 to 4 feet below the slab
surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our field
exploration Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time
of year
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered from our field exploration are
presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1 of Appendix A Laboratory testing was
performed on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented in
Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is underlain by stiff in situ soils
near the existing ground surface Based on the competent subsoil conditions
encountered in our boring and the anticipated light structural loads it is our opinion that
shallow foundations bearing on the near surface stiff soils may be used for support of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3343 00 Page 6
the proposed expansion New footings to be located adjacent to the existing residential
structure should be lowered to the same elevation as the existing building footings to
avoid surcharging the existing foundations Detailed recommendations are presented
in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Detailed information pertaining to the structural loads were not available at the
time this report was prepared In general we anticipate the proposed expansion to be
lightly loaded with column loads on the order of less than 20 kips Because of the
generally competent subsoil conditions encountered in our boring we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed
expansion
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the near surface stiff soils This bearing value is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces
Individual footings and continuous wall footings bearing on the near surface stiff
soils should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish
grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
New footings to be located adjacent to the existing house should be lowered to
the same depth as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the existing
foundations The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed in the field
during construction by Geolabs Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3343 00 Page 7
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the near surface stiff
soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended
down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Boulders encountered during the footing excavation should be
removed and the depression should be properly backfilled with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the near surface stiff soils to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for foundations bearing on the near surface stiff soils

We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm
adequate foundation bearing conditions and the required footing embedment depths
Slab On Grade
A concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the project construction of the
proposed expansion To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the
swelling of the on site clayey subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils
be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation section of this report
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more
uniform support for the slab and to serve as a capillary moisture break To reduce
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel
cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic
membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab and
driveway area a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction is recommended in lieu of the No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer The
2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted for the garage and driveway
slabs Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures

Site Preparation
We understand that minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project
construction At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Ail vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted Utilities that
are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2
percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density If
zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the subgrade level deeper
over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting
over excavation should be backfilled with compacted select granular fill material
Select granular material for fills and backfills under the slab and driveway areas
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Select granular material should consist
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3343 00 Page 10
of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than
3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent
when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less
than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested
and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the project A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such
as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the
trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the site preparation and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this
report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for con

If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifica
tions to the design should be made

upon information obtained from our field boring Variations of subsoil conditions in the

become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field boring indicated in this report is approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Topographic Survey

depths
