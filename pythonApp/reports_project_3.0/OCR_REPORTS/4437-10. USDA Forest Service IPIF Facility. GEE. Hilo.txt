Project Considerations
The project site is located west of Komohana Street in the vicinity of the University
of Hawaii Hilo campus on the Island of Hawaii Based on the information provided the
proposed facility will consist of four building structures two shade houses and other
improvements
We understand that the four buildings will be one story structures and will be
connected with covered pedestrian breezeways The building structures will consist of
steel wood and concrete masonry units CMU The finished floor elevations range
between 358 5 to 361 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In addition we understand that a
parking area and driveways will be located at the east end of the project site
Based on the existing ground surface elevations and the proposed finished floor
elevations we envision cuts on the order of about 2 feet will be required at the south side
of the project site and fills on the order of about 10 feet will be required at the north side of
the project site
Subsurface Conditions

vicinity of the project site the project site is generally underlain by medium hard to very
hard dense to vesicular basalt formation Clinker layers of various thicknesses and

Discussion and Preliminary Recommendations
Discussion of our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations for
design of site grading building foundations and rock anchors are presented in the
following sections of this preliminary letter report
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing finished subgrades in cut areas and areas designated
to receive fills should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory drum roller or similar
heavy equipment a minimum of 8 passes to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during the proof rolling
operations should be backfilled with well compacted fill materials
In general we recommend that the fill areas be capped with a minimum 2 foot thick
layer of structural fill material to serve as a choking layer for the larger sized general fill
materials Provision of the 2 foot thick layer of structural fill would facilitate construction of
the future improvements above the fills Structural fill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient
fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass In general the
excavated materials may be re used as a source of structural fill materials provided that
the materials are processed to meet the maximum size and gradation requirements
presented Where appropriate such as within utility corridors the thickness of the
structural fill layer may be increased to a minimum of 1 foot below the invert elevation of
the utilities to facilitate future construction of the utilities
Fill and backfill materials below the minimum 2 foot thick structural fill layer may
consist of general fill materials General fill and backfill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in size with
an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches The excavated on site materials if less
than 18 inches in maximum dimension may be used as a source of general fill or backfill
materials other than for trench backfill provided that they are processed to meet the
above gradation requirements If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material off site borrow or on site rock crushing of larger rock

Permanent fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the
design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face In the event that over cutting of
a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be implemented instead of
backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills The fill slope face should be free
of large boulders greater than 18 inches in largest dimension and also should be free of
voids which would allow erosion and migration of fines to occur Water should be diverted
away from the top of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to
reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Building Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the project site we believe that a
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed building structures Based on the finished floor elevations and the
existing ground surface elevations we anticipate that the footings for the building
structures will bear on both the basalt rock encountered at the project site and compacted
fill materials Allowable bearing pressures of up to 3 000 and 6 000 pounds per square
foot psf may be used for footings bearing on the structural fill materials and the basalt
rock formation respectively These bearing values may be increased by 50 percent for
transient loads such as those caused by wind and or seismic forces

As previously mentioned the potential for presence of lava tubes blister and or
cavities exists at the project site To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of the cavities below foundations we recommend that a program of
cavity probing and grouting at the foundation locations be conducted during construction
For preliminary estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each
spread footing location and at maximum 10 foot intervals along continuous footings The
probeholes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet
below the planned bottom of footing elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or
suspected during the probing operation additional probes should be drilled at closer
spacing to help delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The
probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected
at low to moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout should have a slump of about
6 to 9 inches
Due to the low structural loads we believe that the potential for loss of foundation
support resulting from collapse of the cavities below the foundations is relatively small in
areas where the fill thickness is greater than 5 feet Therefore probing and grouting at
footing locations where fills are greater than 5 feet in thickness may be omitted
Rock Anchors
Due to the open nature of the covered pedestrian breezeways these structures will
be subjected to high uplift forces and overturning moments caused by wind The covered
pedestrian breezeways may resist the uplift forces by rock anchors embedded in the
medium hard to hard basalt rock formation
A rock anchor is constructed by drilling a borehole installing a steel tendon or
reinforcing bar into the borehole and grouting the borehole The basic components of a
grouted rock anchor consist of the anchorage and the bonded and unbonded lengths of
the steel tendon or reinforcing bar In general the unbonded length of the rock anchor is
the portion of the steel tendon or reinforcing bar that does not have direct contact with
the grout allowing the tendon or reinforcing bar to elongate elastically and transferring
the resisting force from the structure to the bonded length The bonded length of the rock
