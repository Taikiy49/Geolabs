As requested a preliminary geotechnical engineering assessment was conducted
at the existing four stockpiles owned by the State to evaluate the suitability of the soils for
general and capping material This letter report presents the results of the preliminary
assessment
Project Considerations
The stockpiles are located on the northern western eastern and southeastern
sides of Kalaeloa Barbers Point Harbor The project location and general vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Test pits were excavated at the four stockpile
locations to evaluate the suitability of the soil for general and or capping material
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering assessment was to obtain an
overview of the stockpile conditions to evaluate the suitability of the soils for capping
material Our work consisted of the following work efforts
1 Mobilization demobilization of a backhoe and operator to the project site and
back
2 Excavation of 12 test pits to depths of approximately 18 to 35 feet below the
existing surface of each stockpile
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the test pits by our field
engineer

Subsurface Conditions
The stockpiles were explored by excavating 12 test pits with a track backhoe 345B
Generally test pits were excavated at three different locations for a large stockpile in the
center and one on each end of the stockpile For the smaller stockpiles an average of
about 2 test pits were excavated
The test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The Logs of Test Pits are
presented on Plates A 1 and A 2 Stockpile Nos 1 and 2 consisted of a mixture of clay
and coral gravel About one half of Stockpile No 3 consisted of a mixture of clay and coral
gravel from about the center of the stockpile toward the southern end of the stockpile The
northern half of the stockpile from the center consisted of a tannish sand and silt with coral
gravel and some cobbles and boulders Stockpile No 4 was fairly uniform with tannish
brown silty sand with coral gravel and some cobbles and boulders
Findings and Recommendations
The field exploration results indicate that Stockpile No 4 area and most of the
northern half of Stockpile No 3 area consist primarily of coralline sand and gravel and
could be used as capping fill structural fill
The remaining stockpiles consists of a mixture of clay and coralline sand gravel
and would not qualify as capping fill unless they are selectively excavated to avoid
contaminating the coral layers However this material may be used as a general fill if
properly moisture conditioned and covered with sufficient capping fill

One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits A graphic presentation of the test result is provided
on Plate B 1
Three Sieve Analyses ASTM C 117 ASTM C 136 were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
selected soil sample of the potential borrow materials to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils Results of the CBR test were also used to evaluate the quality
of the material for use as fills The test results are presented on Plate B 3
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field test pits Variations of conditions between and
beyond the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the contractor
in determining if the stockpiles that were sampled and tested are suitable for general
and capping fill material Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use to form the basis for preparation of construction cost
estimates or contract bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a
competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or
performance of site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unsuitable soils may not have been
encountered during the excavation process Unforeseen soils such as adobe clay
organic materials or any type of unsuitable soils may not have been encountered during
the test pit excavation Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
This geotechnical exploration conducted was not intended to investigate the
potential presence of hazardous materials existing at the site The equipment
techniques and personnel used to conduct a geo environmental exploration differ
substantially from those applied in geotechnical engineering
