GEOLOGY AND GENERAL SOIL CONDITION
In our opinion the soils at the site could be derived from
weathering of lava flows of Nepheline basalt deposited by the
Koloa Volcanic Series during the middle Pleistocene Epoch
According to the Soil Conservation mapping the most prevalent
soil at the site is the Kalapa Series which ranges from dark
red to reddish brown silty clay and clay with a subangular
structure The clayey soil is underlain by highly weathered
rock
The soil is strongly acidic and has a severe to very severe
erosion potential It varies in permeability from moderate
to rapid and has a moderate to low shrink swell potential with
high shear strengths
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on site inspection it is our opinion that the site is
suitable for the proposed Mausoleum construction Vertical cut
up to 20 feet high into the existing slope will be required for
the proposed construction The on site soils are anticipated to
be highly erosive Continuous retaining wall should be constructed
in sections in order to limit the exposure of cut slope to potential
land slide during heavy rain condition
Kauai Memorial Gardens 76
Spread footing foundation can be utilized to support the pro
posed retaining wall and slab on grade and walkway construction
For estimate purposes 4 000 p s f bearing capacity may be
utilized The footing should bear a minimum of 18 below final
adjacent grade into stiff or decomposed materials
Footing On Sloping Portion
A minimum set back distance of 5 feet should be maintained from
the outer edge of footing measured horizontally out to the slope
surface Because of the deep excavations boulders and rock for
mation could be encountered during the site grading and footing
excavations If rock formation is encountered at footing level
considerably high bearing capacity can be utilized
Slab On Grade
Based on our experience with the reddish brown soil in this area
the clayey soil are generally of low expansion potential type
therefore slab on grade construction can be utilized without
special provisions It is recommended that the concrete slab
be provided with a minimum of 4 inch base course consisting of
well graded gravel less than 3 4 inch maximum size The subgrade
should be compacted and shaped to drain
Plastic barrier should be placed under concrete slab where moisture
sensitive floor covering are utilized
Kauai Memorial Gardens 76
Retaining Wall
Lower lateral pressure for the retaining wall design can be
used provided granular material is utilized as backfill along
with positive drainage systems behind and below the wall footing
to relieve any excessive hydrostatic pressure Equivalent fluid
pressure of 40 p c f could be used for preliminary cost com
putation A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized for deter
mining sliding resistance of the retaining wall foundations The
retaining wall excavations into the clayey soil can stand verti
cally with temporary shoring However due to the erosion poten
tial of the on site material retaining wall excavation and con
struction should be constructed in sections not exceeding 25 feet
in width It should also be arranged that retaining wall construc
tion be performed as soon as possible after excavation
