SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is suitable for the proposed construction The struc
tures can be supported upon spread or line foundations designed
for maximm bearing pressures of 3 000 pounds per square foot
Expansive soils were not encountered and slab on grade construc
tion can be utilized without special site preparation
Due to the limited nature of the subsurface explorations it is
recommended that the foundation excavations and the building
areas be inspected during the construction operations for
undisclosed subsurface conditions
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface investigation
and presents recommendations for the design and construction of the
foundations to support the proposed structures

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development includes 12 two story structures to be
located on a four acre parcel between Ohana Nui Circle and the Ohana
Nui Drainage Channel The anticipated site grading will be limited
to the foundation excavations and approximately two feet of fill
placed in the vicinity of the proposed structures for proper site
drainage
The soil loadings for the proposed development will consist of
lightly loaded two story structures and shallow fills
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed four acre site is a long strip of land adjoining Ohana
Nui Circle on the south It has an average width of approximately
95 feet and a total length approaching 3 300 feet It is bound on
the north by Ohana Nui Circle and on the south by a metal fence
separating the site from Ohana Nui Drainage Canal
The grass covered ground surface is essentially level and is occupied
by several scattered small utility buildings and concrete pads At
the rear of the site the ground surface drops vertically approxi
mately six feet to the draining channel

Geology
The Hickam Air Force Base is within the proximity of the Makalapa
and Aliamanu Craters which supplied volcanic deposits throughout
this area In addition to volcanic activity stream deposits were
washed into Pearl Harbor from the nearby eroding mountains forming
an extensive alluvial fan extending a considerable distance into
the ocean Coral reefs developed within this areas as the basin
sank and during periods of higher ocean levels
Geologically the soils within this area have been derived from
alluvial deposits bay clays reef coral and some volcanic cinders
and tuff
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing four test
borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and the
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix along with
the boring logs and laboratory test results
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by
an upper layer of 6 feet to 11 feet of clayey silt with coral frag
ments and occasional thin coral layers This appears to be a mixture
of the coral reef deposits and intermittent alluvial deposits
Page 4
W O 537 00
Beneath this upper layer alluvial soils composed of sand and cinder
and coral gravels with silty sand or clayey silt were generally
encountered In Borings 1 and 4 the soil beneath the upper layer of
clayey silt contained seams of hard mudrock which is a cemented vol
canic tuff
The upper layer of soil varied from very stiff to hard in consistency
The underlying materials varied from medium dense to dense where
they were granular and from medium to hard in consistency where they
were cohesive
Groundwater was encountered between Elevations 101 and 103 which
approximately corresponds to the water level in the adjoining channel
The elevations utilized in this report were obtained from an untitled
site plan provided to facilitate our investigation The elevations
are based upon an assumed datum and do not correspond to the
U S G S Elevations
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
At the proposed bearing levels the foundations would bear upon
the upper clayey silt soil mantle This material varies from very
stiff to hard in consistency and will provide adequate support for
the proposed structures The consolidation and direct shear tests
performed on samples from Boring 4 indicate that the material is only
slightly compressible and has a high shear strength

The foundations should be designed to bear a minimum of 12 inches
below the final ground surface where they may be designed
from maximum contact bearing pressures of 3 000 pounds per square
foot For this bearing pressure the estimated settlements are 1
inch total and inch differential The foundation may bear upon
compacted fill or natural soils
Due to the limited site explorations which consisted of four borings
spaced over a distance of approximately 3 300 feet it is recom
mended that the proposed building locations be proof rolled with a
heavy compactor prior to the foundation excavations Each founda
tion excavation should be inspected to determine if the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of the subsurface
explorations
The areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted prior
to the placement of any new fills All deleterious and organic
materials should be removed prior to the compaction operations
Fills should then be placed in the 6 inch maximum lifts and com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density determined by
American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 72T
Three swell potential tests were conducted on samples obtained from
the subsurface explorations These tests resulted in swell poten

Slab on grade construction to be provided with floor coverings

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings varied from 10 3 feet to 15 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan included in the text of this report
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drill
ing operations The classifications were verified by visual in
spection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphical representation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of
the encountered soils a Direct Shear Test was performed on a
selected sample from Boring 4 The test results are shown on
Plate A 5
A consolidation test was performed on a sample from Boring 4
to measure the soil response under various vertical loadings
The consolidation test results are graphically presented on
