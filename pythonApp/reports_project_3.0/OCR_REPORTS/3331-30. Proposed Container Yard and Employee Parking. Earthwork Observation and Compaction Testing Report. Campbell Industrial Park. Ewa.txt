This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the site grading for the proposed Container Yard and Employee Parking project at the

Site Grading and Fill Placement
The project site is located at the Distribution Center of Fleming Companies Inc
within the Campbell Industrial Park in the Ewa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It was proposed to convert the project site into a parking area for containers and
employee vehicles with asphalt concrete pavement A general layout of the project site
is provided on the Site Pian Plate 1
The site grading work was performed by Oahu Construction Company with our
firm providing the grading and compaction monitoring services Based on the original
site topography minimal cuts and fills were required to attain the design finish
subgrades for the new pavement areas The site grading consisted of clearing and

grubbing placement and compaction of engineered fills and base course material
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were proof
rolled with a vibratory drum roller prior to placement of fill materials During the site
grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill placement operations the fill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Periodic field density tests were taken during
the fill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted A total of 79 field density tests including
retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests taken are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 of this report These tests were taken on the
prepared ground surfaces on constructed engineered fills and on the base course at
the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved
by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
for the above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the above referenced report dated
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
