INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide soil data for
the design and construction of the proposed reservoir and
service roadway
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed reservoir is located about 400 feet southeast
of the end of Ragsdale Place in Nuuanu Valley Oahu Hawaii
The proposed site is generally situated on the lower portion
of the eastern valley side slope The area is steep with
existing ground slopes of about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
and is heavily vegetated
The reservoir elevation is proposed at 850 feet The existing
ground elevation at the reservoir site generally varies from
850 to 880 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed structures consist of a concrete reservoir
tank about 40 feet in diameter and a narrow winding access
road leading from Ragsdale Place to the reservoir site
Retaining walls generally less than 10 feet high are
planned along most of the access road

A perimeter service road will also be constructed around the
reservoir Up to about 30 feet of cut is anticipated to
create a level pad and roadway
The proposed access road will have a 12 foot pavement width
Cuts generally less than 10 feet and fills less than 5 feet
are anticipated for the reservoir access road
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
One boring and one hand auger probing were drilled at the
reservoir site to explore the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing The boring was
drilled to a depth of 10 4 feet and the probe to a depth
of 11 5 feet Due to the very steep terrain and heavy vege
tation which limited access to the site portable drilling
equipment were utilized The portable equipment could not
penetrate into the rocky material encountered at about 10
foot depth therefore seismic refraction surveys were con
ducted to supplement the boring data The approximate
locations of the boring probing and seismic surveys are
shown on the Site Plan Plate l
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
probing seismic test and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
The boring and hand augered probe encountered a surface layer
about 1 5 to 3 feet thick of medium stiff brown clayey silt
recent hillwash deposits over stiff light brown clayey
Silt to about 9 to 10 foot depths Dense decomposed
rock was encountered at 9 to 10 foot depths and could not
be penetrated withthe hand auger or standard split spoon
sampler
Three seismic surveys conducted at the site generally indi
cated a surface soil layer about 10 to 16 5 feet deep over
a relatively high velocity material believed to be a weathered
basalt formation It should be noted that the interpretation
of the seismic data is subject to verification and could

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the reservoir site is
underlain by a stiff clayey silt soil layer about 10 foot
thick over a weathered rock formation
It is our opinion that the subsoils can provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed tank structure
Foundations
Spread and continuous footing foundation can be used for the
reservoir structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 20 P 5
Due to the steep terrain at the site cuts varying from 5
feet on the lower side to 25 feet on the higher side are
anticipated to create a level area for the proposed reser
voir tank Therefore it is anticipated that stiff soil may
be encountered at foundation level where the cuts are gene
rally less than about 10 feet while rock formation may be
encountered in the deeper cut areas
In order to reduce differential settlement due to foundations
bearing on the different materials rock and soil a lower
bearing capactiy is recommended for the footings on the
stiff soil
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f is recom
mended for footings bearing on dense weathered rock and
2000 p s f on stiff clayey silt
Based on the field exploration and estimated reservoir loads
very little settlement is anticipated for footings on dense
weathered rock and settlements generally less than of
an inch are anticipated for the footings on the stiff soil
The footings should bear a minimum of 12 inches below the
adjacent finish grade The ground surface around the
footings should not be excavated or allowed to erode
Surface water should be diverted away from the reservoir
foundations

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls generally less than 10 feet in height
are planned along most of the access roadway and around the
tank structure
In order to help resist downhill slope creep and to develop
the bearing capacity of the soil the wall footings situated
on sloping areas should be embedded a sufficient depth to
obtain a minimum 6 foot horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope
face Walls on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
wall footings bearing on the stiff natural ground

Site Grading
Cuts up to 35 feet vertical height are anticipated at
the reservoir site Cut slope ratio in the upper stiff
brown clayey silt can be constructed at 14 horizontal to
1 vertical In the lower weathered rock formation slope
ratio could be steepened up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
A drainage bench should be constructed at a minimum of 20
feet vertical heights interval
Moderate to hard excavation is anticipated in the deeper
cuts where rock formation is anticipated
Fill slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are recom
mended Slope planting would be required on all slopes
The site should be cleared of all vegetation and loose top
soil prior to filling or concrete placement
Select on site material can be used for fill The fill should
be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 10 inches and compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into the existing natural ground
Sliver fills should be avoided if practical where required
they should be properly keyed and benched into competent soil

Access Roadway Pavement
Based on our field observations and laboratory testing of the
near surface soils at the site and light traffic conditions
the following pavement design is recommended for the service
roadways
Z inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

above design sections can be revised based on the additional
tests
The roadway subgrades should be compacted and shaped to drain

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the subgrade prepara
tion for the reservoir sites
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspection

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling one boring to a depth of 10 4 feet deep utilizing
portable hand carried drilling equipment and by probing
to a depth of 11 5 feet with a hand auger The approximate
locations of the boring and probing are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 Seismic refraction surveys were also
conducted to supplement the drilling data and to obtain
general information on the underlying rocky material which
could not be penetrated with the portable drilling equipment
The soils were classified by visual and textural exa
mination in the field by our engineer who continously moni
tored the drilling operations The classifications were
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
soils encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring
Log Plate A 1 and Probing Log Plate A 2
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetra
tion sampler and ring sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Log
at the appropriate sample depth
