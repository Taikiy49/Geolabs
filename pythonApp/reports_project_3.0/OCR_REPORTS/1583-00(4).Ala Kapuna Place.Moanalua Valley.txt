As requested we have made a preliminary geologic reconnaissance of
the near surface soil conditions below the above referenced site The
purpose of the reconnaissance was to evaluate the general suitability of
the near surface soils for development in order to aid in the appraisal
of the land value
The reconnaissance consisted of a review of available soils and
geologic maps and literature concerning the area b a general geologic
reconnaissance of the project site and c reviewing the previous report
at the site performed by Dames Moore in 1973
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The site is a long narrow strip of land located northeast of
Moanalua Freeway and between the Red Hill Coast Guard residential area
and Moanalua Golf Course It slopes down towards the southeast at about
10 to 20 gradients According to the Dames Moore report thick fills
were placed in the middle section of the site which may be unstable
Since their mapping was done 12 years ago additional materials may have
been dumped at the site
Several drainage paths cross through the site
The site covers an area of about 21 7 acres and is mostly overgrown
with bushes and trees
Geology
The geologic conditions of the project site are fairly complex
The project site is located on the southern flank of the elongated
Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu
Hawaii The range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe
and aa basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the
existing mountain trends
Also the project site is located near the northerly flanks of the
Makalapa Aliamanu Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series These vents deposited both younger pyroclastic materials and
lava flows over portions of the project site
In situ weathering has reduced the upper portions of these deposits
to residual soils and saprolites Continued erosion and deposition by
the intermittent streams has resulted in the burial of these volcanics
by alluvial materials

Soil Conditions
The information on the soils likely to be encountered in the
project area was obtained from the report by Dames Moore From their
investigation and visual observations of the ground surface it appears
that the northeast corner of the site is underlain by Rockland where
the exposed rock covers up to 25 to 90 of the area The remainder is
covered by alluvial and residual soils weathered from the igneous rocks
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The Dames Moore Report entitled Foundation Investigation
Proposed Apartment Development Moanalua Oahu Hawaii dated August
17 1973 was referred to in our study Based on their report and a
field reconnaissance of the area our general observations and
recommendations are as follows
General
1 We believe that the site is suitable for development of midrise to
lowrise structures Conventional foundations may be utilized for
most of the areas
2 The drainage paths should be accurately located and if possible
building construction should avoid these where possible
3 It appears that the soils which will be encountered at the site are
Suitable for construction except for large boulders and
miscellaneous fills and rubbish that may be encountered
4 It is anticipated that excavations at the site can be done with
dozers and ripping equipment In many areas the soil layer or
soft rock is fairly deep 20 to 25 feet or more

Additional Field Exploration
The recommendations in this report are presented as a guide for

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The recommendations submitted in this report are general and not
intended to be used for final design purposes since they are based on
brief and limited preliminary geologic site reconnaissance of the site
and a literature review of available soil geologic information and a
previous foundation investigation
Unanticipated soil and rock conditions such as gully deposit or
pockets of clays ash fill cavities etc are commonly encountered
and may occur in localized areas Therefore additional field
exploration and studies should be made after plans are further developed
before design and construction of the project
This report has been prepared in order to assist the developer in
determining the suitability of this site for development
