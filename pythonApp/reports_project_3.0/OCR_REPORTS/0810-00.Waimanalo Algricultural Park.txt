INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary soil
investigation and feasibility study at the above referenced
Waimanalo Reservoir The work was performed according to
the subcontract agreement dated
A forty feet high above the base earthen embankment or
reservoir is planned at the site of the existing Waimanalo
diversion structure Borrow materials will likely be obtained
from mauka the existing reservoir Typical embankment slopes
will be 2 horizontal to 1 vertical both upstream and down
stream A ten feet wide crest is planned at elevation 300
the bottom of the reservoir will be at elevation 260 feet
It is understood that water source is diversion from other
series of reservoirs for irrigation purposes and surface
runoff
PURPOSE SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation and feasibility study is to
determine the general subsurface soil and groundwater condi
tions at the existing dike and vicinity to determine the
feasibility of the enlargement of the reservoir as presently
planned We were also to provide preliminary design details
for the planned embankment for estimating purposes and
recommendations for additional soil investigation and study
if the next phase of construction is authorized
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 810 00
P 3
The scope of our subsurface exploration and preliminary inves
tigation for this initial phase consisted of four borings
spread throughout the reservoir area with sampling standard
penetration tests water level measurements and a reconnaissance
of the existing Waimanalo diversion structure and vicinity
Laboratory testing was performed on representative samples to
help provide the basis for our study and analyses This pre
liminary report summarizes our findings and recommendations
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A Information
regarding the field explorations and laboratory testing are
also presented in the Appendices
SITE CONDITIONS
The existing Waimanalo Reservoir or diversion structure is
located approximately 0 5 mile makai of the Pali Koolau
mountains The drainage basin is thus relatively limited
in extent The site elevations range from 260 to over 340
feet The existing dike is approximately 10 to 14 feet high
earthfill structure currently operated by Waimanalo Irri
gation System Heavy vegetation obscures much of the north
west portion of the existing dike but walls channels pipe
lines and other miscellaneous facilities were observed to
be built into the existing structure There are electric
power line and pipeline easement mauka from the present
reservoir
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 810 00
P 4
The elevation of the top of the existing dike varies from
280 feet in the northwest to 282 feet in the southeast
Assuming the initial dike elevation was roughly level the
patterns of apparent settlement are in agreement with sub
surface conditions encountered in the borings The ele
vation of the existing reservoir bottom is 268 feet
Detailed topography and existing site conditions are
shown on Site Plan Plate l
Water seepages of approximately five gallons per minute were
observed at two locations near the toe of the dike A down
stream resident has constructed a diversion ditch at the toe
of the dike in order to keep water off his pasture This
resident has also filled the low swampy area near the north
west corner of the dike to improve the activity within pasture
A water seepage was also observed in this pasture area makai
the gate and fence The water seepages are relatively constant
reportedly slightly increasing during periods of heavy rainfall
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Waimanalo Reservoir site is generally underlain by recent
alluvium consisting of brown sandy and clayey silt soils
The upper 20 to 25 feet of soil materials beneath the north
west portion of the dike are likely recent or quiet water
deposits contaminated with organic matter and layers of peat
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 810 00
P 5
Although relatively close to Pali the depth of basalt in
the general area is extrapolated on the order of several
hundred feet The soft organic soils are presently satu
rated the older alluvium was previously submerged when
the Pleistocene sea level was approximately 560 feet above
present sea level The probability of soluble materials
which could be leached from the subgrade remaining is
low
Soils
Approximately 10 feet of heterogeneous or non uniform fill
soils were encountered in 2 borings drilled through the
crest of the existing dike The upper five feet of fill
stratum is generally medium stiff dense moderately well
compacted the deeper fill stratum encountered below
5 feet grades to soft loose or poorly compacted up to
the base of the existing embankment natural foundation
subgrade
Based on the information developed from 2 borings within
the existing embankment and the northwest portion of the
proposed enlargement area the underlying natural soils
generally consist of relatively impermeable sandy and
clayey silt soils which are saturated and soft loose below
a depth of about 5 feet A few permeable layers of coarse
gravel and cobbles together with an occasional boulder
Boring 3 were also encountered

The permeability of these coarse layers depends upon the
degree of weathering the clay content etc Lenses and
layers of very soft peat fibrous organic materials were
encountered at about 15 feet below the existing ground in
Borings 3 and 4 located near the northwest portion of the
existing dike The underlying strata encountered below
25 to 30 foot depths in this area are medium stiff to
stiff with varying amount of gravels and occasional
boulders
Within the south and east portions of the site the upper
10 to 30 feet of natural soils are generally soft to medium
stiff and brownish gray to dark gray Borings 1 and 2

Groundwater
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging
from 5 to 14 feet corresponding approximately to depths of
fill soils Piezometers have been installed in the makai

levels due to reservoir and climatic variations if this

CONCLUSIONS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on the subsurface information developed in the 4 borings
drilled at various locations of the reservoir area it appears
the underlying soils are non uniform and will not be suitable
for foundation support without extensive overexcavating and
recompaction of the underlying soft soils in the northwest
portion of the proposed embankment area The lateral extent
of the underlying soft soils is unknown due to the spacing
of the borings drilled and lack of subsurface information
within the reservoir However it can be concluded that
1 The existing embankment for the Waimanalo
Reservoir will not be adequate for support
of the new proposed embankment due to the
non uniform compaction soft and loose con
dition of the fill soils within the existing
embankment area Complete removal of the
existing dike is recommended

RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and preliminary recommendations are presented Since these
recommendations are based upon limited initial work they
are only suitable for preliminary design and cost estimating
purposes
Due to non uniform embankment fill heavy existing vegetation
water seepage and organic subgrade beneath part of the struc
ture it is recommended that the existing dike be completely
removed This will enable construction of a properly engin
eered structure throughout The possibility of scraping
vegetation and topsoil off the present embankment and adding
new fill was considered Due to many populated areas makai
marginal conditions of the existing structure and organic
soils beneath portions of the existing dike we believe
that it is in the ultimate best interests of the project
to construct completely new reservoir embankment
Another alternative that of placing fill over soft organic
subgrade was also considered Due to large anticipated
differential settlements on the order of several feet
and likely cracking and spreading of the embankment possibly
leading to piping and endangering downstream slope stability
we further recommend construction of a deep cut off or key to
reduce differential settlement and cracking of the proposed

embankment This key should be located beneath the center
of the embankment and should be extended below water level
through the organic subgrade up to roughly 25 feet below
natural ground surface The construction of this key will
not be an easy matter However the alternatives preloading
berms sand drains etc are expensive and equally difficult
Due to the potential difficulties anticipated during excava
ting and dewatering a deep key or cut off and compaction
beneath the water level and upper organic strata a com
pletely new site for the reservoir should be given serious
consideration
Grading
Prior to construction the existing reservoir should be
drained of water the site should be cleared of existing
vegetation deleterious materials and existing fill soils
should be removed prior to earthwork Specifically all of
the existing dike should be removed fill soils from this
dike may be stockpiled for future use The soft wet organic
topsoils and soils contaminated by deleterious materials
should be excavated and removed to designated spoil areas
A cut off or key should be excavated below the center of
the embankment The depth of key will depend upon depths
of soft organic soils roughly 5 feet in Boring 1 and

25 feet in Boring 4 Shallow excavation should be made by
backhoe operating from the sides of the excavation deep
excavation may be performed using track loaders Flat side
slopes on the order of 4 horizontal to 1 vertical and
continuous dewatering of sumps will be required Well
points probably would not dewater the excavation for the
key due to the generally fine grained soils wet excavation
using clam shell would not permit the compaction of a fine
grained impervious backfill to cut off under seepage
It is our recommendation that the proposed embankment also
consist of homogeneous section of impermeable silty soils
compacted at relatively high water content
The on site silty soils available from required excavations
may be used both for compacted embankment and for key or
cut off any large cobbles and boulders which would inter
fere with compaction should be removed The existing fill
materials from the present dike may also be reused if free
from organic materials debris such as pieces of concrete
etc Existing fill that contains deleterious materials
should not be buried within new embankment Any imported
fill materials obtained from excavations off site or imported
from borrow areas should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to importation

Sandy and clayey silt soils should be placed in maximum
nine inch thick loose lifts brought near optimum moisture
content and compacted to 100 percent relative compaction
using ASTM Designation D 698 70 Standard Proctor as the
laboratory standard The field moisture content during
compaction should be greater than equal to or within
minus five percent of the laboratory optimum moisture
content All lifts must be placed horizontally In
order to improve uniformity of the embankment and blanket
the surface of each compacted fill should be lightly scari
fied prior to placing the next lift
Slopes
The proposed embankment slopes may be sloped at 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical gradient Due to the relatively small size
of the proposed reservoir rock rip rap will not be required
The surfaces of all embankment slopes above water levels
should be sodded with grass to minimize erosion due to
rainfall and wave action
The mauka excavation slope upstream from the reservoir _will be
over 60 to 70 feet high The maximum recommended excava
tion slope is 3 horizontal to 1 vertical gradient This is
to provide a similar factor of safety against shear failure
corresponding to the embankment slopes It will also minimize
erosion of the cut mauka slope due to groundwater seepage out

surfaces of this slope above water levels should also
be sodded with grass
Settlements
If the embankment is constructed as previously outlined
the settlements are expected to vary with height of em
bankment and consistency of the foundation subgrade
materials Relatively small settlements are generally
anticipated the camber or surcharge normally required
to maintain design free board or safety from overtopping
after settlements are estimated to be on the order of
one foot
Additional Investigation
Additional closely spaced boring exploration laboratory
testing and engineering analyses will be required in the
event that final design and construction of the project
is contemplated These additional explorations should
include drilling sampling and testing for slope stability
calculations and analyses of probable embankment material
mauka of the reservoir at the existing dike to delineate
zones of soft organic subgrade and makai of the reservoir
in order to test permeability and downstream slope stability

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four borings to depths of 50 to 80 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment
with continuous flight solid stem 4 inch auger An NX core barrel
was also used to extend Boring 3 through a large boulder Undisturbed
and disturbed samples were obtained and standard penetration tests
were performed
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineering geologist who con
tinuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 9
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either a
The ring sampler and standard penetration tests were driven using 140 pound
hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths
Field permeability tests were also attempted in Borings 1 and 3
These tests failed to develop quantitative data due to relatively permeable
fill soils encountered in Boring 1 and relatively impermeable natural soils
in Boring 3 Additional and more detailed permeability test should be planned
for design phase of the soil investigation
