Soil Engineering Investigation
West Beach Resort Off Site
Waste Water Pump Stations
Hawaii
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions at the two proposed Waste Water Pump Stations WWPS to
provide soil data for use in the design and construction of the proposed
project The approximate locations of the WWPS s are shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

GENERAL PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Waste Water Pump Stations WWPS Numbers 1 and 2
will be approximately 30 by 42 feet and 35 by 42 feet respectively in
plan Both WWPS s will be constructed of reinforced concrete and
concrete masonry units The structures will be constructed in two
levels The first level will be an underground compartment which houses
the wet well and sewage pumps The second level will contain the
operator facilities pump motors and stand by generator The finished
floor elevations of the two pump stations are shown as follows

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
In general relatively competent hard cemented sand and coral
formation were encountered at the foundation level of both WwPS s
Spread footing foundations bearing on these soils are recommended
Because the proposed finished floor elevation is below the water
table dewatering is required
Foundations
Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for
the proposed pump station structure An allowable bearing value up to
4000 p s f may be used on the cemented sand or coral formation
Footing should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
finished grade
Estimated settlements are on the order of one quarter of an inch
total and one eighth of an inch differential

Dewatering
Water level was encountered at elevations of about 2 0 to 4 0 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL Therefore dewatering is anticipated for the
excavation which will extend to elevations of 6 to 9 feet below MSL In
general cemented sand and coral formation were encountered below the
water level
The cemented sand deposit encountered below the water level is
relatively permeable and may be loosened if improper pumping methods are
adopted

Additionally cavities were encountered below the water table at
WWPS No 2 and other areas in the development Although the vertical
dimension of the cavities were found to be about 1 to 2 feet its extent
in the horizontal direction cannot be fully determined Large quantities
of flow are anticipated particularly if cavities are encountered in the
excavation
In order to control the expected large quantity of flow into the
excavation we would recommend that the cavities be grouted with
soil cement grout before excavation work begins Prior to the grouting
operation a probing program may be carried out in order to determine the
size and the extent of the cavities The probing program would help to
estimate the amount of grout required and hence provide information
regarding the cost of the operation The project dewatering operation
should be done without pumping out the soil fines pumping clear water
only
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the pump roon floor
level It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations and
cushion layer constructions for slabs and pavements be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil seepage and soft spots may occur in localized areas
and may require additional probing or corrections in the field There
fore additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain
a properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recom
mended to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of site grading and foundation plans of the proposed
construction
