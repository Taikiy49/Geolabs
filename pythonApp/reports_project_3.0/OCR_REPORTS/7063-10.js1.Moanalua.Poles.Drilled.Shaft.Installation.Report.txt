PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located on the mauka side of Moanalua Road in the Waimalu 
area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves replacement of two existing 
transmission line monopoles, designated as Pole Nos. 9 and 10. The two existing poles 
are being replaced due to corrosion of the conductor support arms.  
The new monopoles will be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 
60-inch diameter cast-in-place drilled shafts. Each new transmission line monopole will 
be mounted to a base plate with anchor bolts extending approximately 15 feet into the 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. The drilled shafts were designed to derive vertical 
support primarily from skin friction between the drilled shaft and the surrounding soils. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Replace Poles Along Moanalua Road 
project only. Hawaiian Electric Company, Inc. was the general contractor for the project 
and Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company (HDCC) was the subcontractor that 
installed the drilled shaft foundation support system. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
The drilled shaft subcontractor installed two drilled shafts from November 13, 
2017 through November 22, 2017 at the locations shown on the Drilled Shaft Location 
Plan, Plate 2. Representatives from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft excavation, 
placement of reinforcing cage and concrete operations during the installation of the 
drilled shafts. 
In general, the drilled shafts were excavated using a Soilmec R-625 track 
mounted drill rig. Excavation tools used for the 60-inch diameter shafts included 60 and 
64-inch diameter augers, a 60-inch diameter digging bucket, 60 and 64-inch diameter 
core barrels, and a 60-inch diameter clean-out bucket. As part of the drilling 
subcontractor’s means and methods, HDCC elected to install permanent steel casing at 
both monopole locations in lieu of temporary steel casing for hole stability during the 
drilled shaft construction.  Permanent steel casing with an outside diameter of 64 inches 
was installed to depths of about 38 and 28 feet below the existing ground surface at 
Pole Nos. P9 and P10, respectively. 
It should be noted that basaltic cobbles and boulders were encountered at an 
elevation higher than originally anticipated at Pole No. P9. Due to the very hard and slow 
drilling conditions in the basaltic cobbles and boulders, the drilled shaft design length was 
re-analyzed and reduced from 42 to 38 feet below the existing ground surface by our 
office.

After excavation of the drilled shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with 
the use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, we 
observed that concrete was placed within an acceptable time frame for substantial 
completion.  
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes, concrete was 
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method. Concrete was placed through a 
4-inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom of the excavation. During 
concrete placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained 
above the bottom of the tremie pipe. Concrete with slump ranging from 7 to 9 inches was 
used for the drilled shafts. Slump tests of concrete were performed by others. A 
summary of the drilled shaft installation is provided below. Details of the drilled shaft 
construction are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.4 for Pole No. P9 and Plates 4.1 
through 4.4 for Pole No. P10.

Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts 
supporting the new replacement monopoles for the project were installed in general 
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications, the geotechnical 
recommendations presented in the above-referenced geotechnical engineering report 
(dated April 17, 2015) for the project, and subsequent recommendations. In addition, it is 
our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to 
provide the allowable design load capacities with a safety factor of 2.0.
