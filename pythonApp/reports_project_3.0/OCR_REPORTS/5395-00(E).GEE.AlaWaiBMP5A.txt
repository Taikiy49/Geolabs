1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Ala of the Intersection of University Avenue and H 1
Freeway project in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration percolation testing and engineering
analyses for the storm water runoff drainage project The recommendations presented
herein are intended for the design of an infiltration trench for the subsurface disposal of
storm water runoff The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The project site is located
on the southern flank of the Koolau Volcano
The Koolau Volcano was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the
northwest to southeast trending rift zones These lavas consist of interbedded
pahoehoe and a a type flows with pahoehoe lava being the dominant form A a is the
Hawaiian term for basaltic lava flows typified by a rough jagged spinose clinkery
surface Pahoehoe is the Hawaiian term for basaltic lava flows typified by smooth
billowy or ropy surfaces After the cessation of the main shield building phase of
Koolau Volcano there was a long period of erosion
During the Pleistocene Epoch there was a renewal of volcanic activity with the
eruptions of vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series Along the crest of the ridge along the
westerly side of Manoa Valley there are the Tantalus Roundtop and Sugarloaf vents
When these vents were active one or more lava flows from these vents spilled down
the westerly wall of the valley and moved across the valley floor These lavas ponded
and filled the lower portion of Manoa Valley The ponding allowed the lava to densify as
it cooled forming dense massive rock The new lava flows from these vents blocked
Manoa Stream and forced the stream to the east between the East West Center and
Waahila Rise These flows generally terminated in the Moiliili District where the basalt
rocks were quarried in the past Reef limestone related to the Waimanalo stand of the
sea underlies the basalt at the former Moliliili Quarry

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the

material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets
the requirements for fill material

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of

We anticipate that basic utility installation for the proposed project will consist of

ROCK FRACTURE CHARACTERISTICS
The following terms describe general fracture spacing of a rock
Massive Greater than 24 inches apart
Slightly Fractured 42 to 24 inches apart
Moderately Fractured 6 to 12 inches apart
Closely Fractured 3 to 6 inches apart
Severely Fractured Less than 3 inches apart
DEGREE OF WEATHERING
The following terms describe the chemical weathering of a rock
Unweathered Rock shows no sign of discoloration or loss of strength
Slightly Weathered Slight discoloration inwards from open fractures
Moderately Weathered Discoloration throughout and noticeably weakened though not able to break by hand
Highly Weathered Most minerals decomposed with some corestones present in residual soil mass Can be broken by hand
Extremely Weathered Saprolite Mineral residue completely decomposed to soil but fabric and structure preserved

APPENDIX C
APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Tests
The permeability of the in situ soils was evaluated by performing three constant
head tests in Boring and 3 The boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The tests were performed at depths ranging from about 8 to 9 feet below the
existing ground surface
The constant head tests were performed by pumping clear water into the
boreholes until the water surface had stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a
sufficient period of time The water height and the rate of pumping were then measured
to provide data upon which to base calculation of the subsoil permeability The field
permeability test results are presented on Plates C 1 thru c 3
