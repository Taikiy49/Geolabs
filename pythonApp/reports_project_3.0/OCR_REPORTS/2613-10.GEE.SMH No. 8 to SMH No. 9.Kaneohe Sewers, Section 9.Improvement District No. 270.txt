INTRODUCTION
S of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed sewer reconstruction project between SMH No 8 and SMH
No 9 under Section 9 of the Kaneohe Sewers located in Kaneohe on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the
data obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These findings and
recommendations are intended for design input Therefore they may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Under the current master design plan it is planned to reconstruct the existing
sewer lines for Kaneohe Sewers Section 9 in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The scope of work performed under this project is limited to the alignment section
between SMH No 8 and SMH No 9 of Section 9 The subject alignment section is
located alongside an existing drainage ditch at the backyard of a single family
residence situated at Place in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The new sewer line will consist of 8 inch diameter vitrified clay pipes with a total length
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 3
of approximately 160 feet Based on the information provided to us the new sewer line
is generally 7 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface with invert elevations ranging
from about 111 to 118 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the available geologic data and previous experience in the vicinity of
the project site the proposed sewer line alignment may be underlain by soft subsoils
at the invert elevations with a relatively shallow groundwater table Due to the
inaccessibility of the drainage ditch to a truck mounted drill rig portable drilling
equipment was used to perform the field exploration work
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations for the design of the
proposed sewer line

11th The scope of our work consisted of the following

operators to and from the project site

below the existing ground surface

a Field Engineer from our firm

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject alignment section is located alongside an existing drainage ditch at
the backyard of a single family residence situated at the cul de sac of Awanene Place
in Kaneche on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Most of the new sewer alignment will run alongside the slope bank to the east
of the existing drainage ditch The existing ditch is approximately 3 to 4 feet wide and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 10 Page 5
is relatively shallow Cobbles and boulders were observed along the floor of the entire
drainage ditch within the project limits Running water was observed along the
drainage ditch at the time of our field exploration possibly surface runoff from overnight
showers of the previous evening
The slope bank adjacent to the drainage ditch on the east side is slightly sloping
with dense vegetation such as avacado mango and banana trees on the upper
portion A relatively steep slope separates the drainage ditch from the existing
residential structures on the west side of the ditch
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions along the proposed sewer line alignment were
explored by drilling and sampling two borings to depths of 19 and 21 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration disclosed that the proposed sewer line alignment is
generally underlain by a deposit of silty clays with consistency ranging from very soft
to stiff extending to the approximate elevations of the pipe inverts This was underlain
by loose clayey sands and gravels to a depth of approximately 14 feet below the
existing ground surface In Boring No 1 medium stiff sandy clay was then encoun
tered and extended to a drilled depth of 21 feet below the ground surface In Boring
No 2 the loose deposit was underlain by medium dense silty sands and medium stiff
silty clays to a depth of 19 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings during our field exploration
at depths of approximately 1 and 4 feet below the existing ground surface These
depths generally correspond to elevations of approximately 115 and 117 feet MSL

W O 2613 10 Page 6
Due to the presence of the existing ditch it appears that these observed water levels
in the drilled borings may not indicate the actual groundwater but a reflection of the
existing drainage water conditions at the project site Groundwater levels are expected
to vary with seasonal rainfall run off and time of year
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the boring

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the proposed sewer line alignment is
generally underlain by relatively soft and loose deposits extending to a depth of
approximately 14 feet below the existing ground surface Relatively shallow
groundwater levels were also encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration Due to the anticipated depths of excavation and the presence of soft loose
subsoils temporary shoring of the excavated trenches will be necessary to reduce the
potential for subsidence of the adjacent ground and nearby structures during the project
construction Dewatering may be required prior to installation and backfilling of sewer
line due to the presence of a relatively shallow groundwater table Due to the presence
of soft loose soils anticipated at the invert elevations we recommend that a bed
ding cushion layer consisting of 24 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped around by a
layer of filter fabric be provided below the sewer pipes and manhole structures to
provide more uniform bearing support In addition we recommend that the sewer pipes
be concrete jacketed due to the potential for flooding during construction which may
cause erosion of the backfill around the sewer pipes Detailed discussion and
recommendations are presented in the following sections

Excavation
Based on the information provided to us we anticipate that excavations on the
order of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface will be required for the project
construction
Excavation Method
The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations Generally
the clayey and sandy soils encountered during our exploration could be
excavated by a hoptoe backhoe or other conventional construction equipment
Excavation Support
It is our opinion that excavations in the soft silty clay and loose clayey
sand gravel deposits will require temporary shoring support for the proper
installation of the sewer line Due to the physical limitations of equipment
accessibility to the site area installation of shoring support system involving
interlocking steel sheet piling may not be practical for the subject project In
addition the potential presence of boulders along the sewer line alignment may
hinder the sheet piling driving and installation Therefore an alternative shoring
support system should be considered if sheet pile shoring is deemed to be not
feasible for the subject project We recommend that the sewer trench be
excavated in short sections to reduce the potential for hillside instability In
addition the excavated section should be backfilled as soon as possible after
placement of the sewer pipes and prior to excavation of the next section The
temporary excavation should conform to the requirements of local state and
federal safety regulations We wish to point out that provisions for temporary
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 8
shoring of the planned excavations should be incorporated into the construction
phase of this project
Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The selected excavation shoring system must comply with all applicable safety
requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system proposed
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changed conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpected high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequences and or techniques all of which may affect
shoring stability
However it should be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately estimate due
to the complexity of the stress changes The magnitude depends greatly upon
workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports
are installed and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and
the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be
installed properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent
ground be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 9
movements with instruments such as settlement markers and inclinometers
Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing structures and
utilities
Dewatering
Dewatering of excavations for the proposed sewer line may be necessary
depending on the exposed site conditions during construction Since dewatering will
involve discharge of groundwater a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System
NPDES permit may be necessary The contractor should consult their independent
consultant or the State of Hawaii Department of Health for the latest regulations and
information pertaining to the groundwater disposal and permit application
Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the contractor The selection
of equipment and methods of dewatering should be decided by the contractor who
should be aware that modifications to the dewatering system may be required during
construction depending on the conditions encountered The dewatering method
selected should have minimal impact on the groundwater level surrounding the
proposed excavation
It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural
groundwater table and or to exclude water from the excavation to allow construction of
the proposed sewer line under safe and relatively dry conditions
Dewatering Considerations
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selecting a
suitable method of dewatering
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 10
1 The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance or damage
to existing structures and environment
2 The dewatering method should maintain stability of and also provide safe
and dry working conditions in the excavation
3 The dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifica
tions to accommodate various ground conditions
Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the
natural groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during
construction and to implement as soon as possible necessary precautionary or
remedial measures including but not limited to stopping or slowing down the
dewatering operations
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be
done without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 11
coordinated with shoring installation such that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected
Any groundwater draw down outside the excavation may cause settlement due
to compression and consolidation of potentially compressible soils Therefore
the use of a deep well system outside the excavation to draw down the
groundwater level should be avoided only allowed if the draw down of the
groundwater table would not affect the surrounding existing structures
Special caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches
connected to excavations If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in
the utility trenches could act as subdrains and cause significant areal groundwa
ter draw down resulting in settlements and potential damage to utility lines and or
other existing adjacent structures
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a significant effect on the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important
design consideration
For pipes bearing on the soft silty clay or loose clayey sand gravel deposits we
recommend that a minimum of 24 inches of granular bedding material such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped around in a layer of filter fabric
Mirafi 140N or equivalent be placed below the pipe to provide more uniform support
of the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum
width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and it should also extend half way up the
sides of the pipe In general minimum compaction effort is required for the No 3B
Fine gravel as a cushion in the sewer trenches due to the self compacting free draining
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 12
nature of the gravel material However a criterion of 3 passes of a plate compactor
may be used to attain uniformity Due to the potential for flooding during construction
which may cause erosion of the backfill around the sewer pipes we recommend that
the sewer pipes be concrete jacketed A typical cross section of the sewer trench is
shown on Plate 3
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be placed in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
The trench backfill from the top of the concrete jacket to the finish grades may
consist of the excavated on site soils provided they are aerated to the proper moisture
contents for compaction To minimize future soil erosion of trench backfill it is critical
that the sewer trench backfill be placed in thin lifts less than 8 inches in loose thickness
with each lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D
1557 91 test procedures
Precautions should be taken against the occurrence of pipeline floatation during
periods of heavy rainfall before and during trench backfilling due to possible influx of
large quantities of surface run off water into the trenches The uplift buoyant forces due
to groundwater influx may lift empty or even partially filled pipes
If practicable the trenches should be kept relatively free of water during
backfilling Should backfilling under water be necessary clean granular material such
as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be utilized to minimize
the potential of excessive settlement of the trench backfill to occur in the future
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 10 Page 13
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench the size of
the pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between the
backfill material and the trench walls To calculate the vertical loads on the buried
sewer pipes an average unit weight of 120 pounds per cubic foot p c f for backfill
material and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 between the backfill and trench sidewall are
recommended Earth pressure forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with increased
width of the trench Therefore the width of the sewer line trench should be kept to a
minimum
Settlements
Primary settlement of the new sewer line construction is normally caused by the
difference in the unit weight of the lighter excavated original earth and the heavier
compacted backfill material placed over the sewer pipes The net increase in loading
will cause settlement of the underlying subsoils below the trench invert Based on our
calculations primary settlement on the order of inch is anticipated for the project
The above estimate assumes that proper construction procedures and good
workmanship will be engaged during construction Additional settlement could occur
if improper trench support or dewatering procedures are used Therefore we believe
that the proposed sewer lines should be designed with sufficient flexibility to
accommodate possible settlements
New fills along the sewer line alignment are not anticipated for the subject
project Should significant additional fills be required for the project construction
Geolabs Hawaii should be informed to re evaluate the potential impacts on the future
settlement behavior of the new sewer line

Joints and Connections
Due to the soft subsoil conditions encountered along the proposed sewer line
alignment we recommend that flexible pipe joints be used to connect the sewer pipes
to accommodate some amount of differential settlement
Manhole Structures
Based on our field exploration we anticipated that the manhole structures will
be placed on the soft silty clays or loose clayey sands gravels as encountered in our
drilled borings We recommend that an 24 inch layer of gravel such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped around by a layer of filter fabric Mirafi
140N or equivalent be provided below the structures to provide uniform bearing
support An allowable bearing pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot p s f may
be used for the design of manhole structures bearing on the 24 inch gravel cushion
layer The increase in load on the subsoils due to the manhole structure should be
small Therefore large settlements are not anticipated if proper shoring and dewatering
techniques are utilized in construction
The lateral earth pressures acting on the proposed underground manhole
structures will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of backfill and the
compactive effort on the backfill material around the structures Therefore granular
material with less than 10 percent passing the No 200 sieve is recommended for use
as backfill around the structures to reduce the compactive effort required which may
cause additional lateral earth pressures We recommend that the new manholes be
designed to resist the following lateral earth pressures provided granular backfill is used
around the structures
1 Above water level 60 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Below water level 90 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 10 Page 15
The above values assume that the backfill around the manhole structures is
properly placed and compacted Surcharge loading due to traffic and other applicable
sources should be added to the above values for design
Construction Monitoring
We understand that the subject alignment is part of a master plan to reconstruct
the sewer system in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii Since the entire project
will involve relatively deep trenching and excavation in a well developed urban area
we believe that the importance of construction monitoring including field observation
and engineering supervision should be emphasized It is our understanding that the
extent of construction monitoring required may vary with individual contractors
However we believe that the following items of work should be considered for
applicability to the subject alignment
Prior to Trench Excavation
1 A damage survey of all existing buildings and structures adjacent to the
proposed sewer alignment should be conducted including photographs
and crack surveys prior to the commencement of construction
2 Inclinometer casings should be installed at about 75 foot intervals along
the proposed alignments in front of critical buildings or structures The
inclinometer casings should penetrate through soft loose subsoil layers
into underlying stiff material for at least 10 feet to establish a reference for
measurement of potential movements
3 Settlement markers should also be installed as much as possible in a
straight line pattern adjacent to the existing structures on both sides of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2613 10 Page 16
the proposed sewer line We recommend that one marker be installed at
approximately 30 to 50 foot intervals along the proposed sewer
alignment
4 Sets of two observation wells should be installed perpendicular to and on
each side of the proposed sewer alignment to allow observation of the
effects of groundwater draw down due to dewatering One of the two
wells should be placed as close to the existing adjacent structure as
possible The observation well sets should be spaced no more than 75
feet apart in front of existing adjacent structures which are considered as
critical

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of the test
are summarized on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depth A graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
