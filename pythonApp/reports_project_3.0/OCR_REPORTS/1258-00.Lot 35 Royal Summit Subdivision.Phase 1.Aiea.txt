Dear Mr Imai
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in
order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed
residence The study consisted of drilling two shallow borings
laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the soils a
review of the subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story split level structure
approximately 48 by 80 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car
garage and a covered lanai Concrete slab on grade is planned
for the entire structure

A retaining wall is planned to provide the grade separation

The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the
Plot Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Leke Place in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
The lot has two generally level pads with a transitional slope
measuring about 5 1 H V between them the lower pad is to the
rear of the lot The ground then slopes down toward the west at
a 2 1 H V ratio
During our site investigation a waste soil Stockpile was noted
in the proposed driveway entrance area
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 35 required cuts up to 5 feet deep at the front portion
and small fills up to 2 feet deep along the rear portion of the
lot

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings which were drilled at the approximate location
shown on the Plot Plan encountered 2 to S feet of reddish brown
silty clay residual soil underlain by saprolite and weathered
rock
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface material
indicated that the underlying residual soil ranges from dry to
moist 15 to 35 percent moisture contents and exhibited low to
moderate expansion potential in its natural State However
previous experience indicates that this type of material could
have high swelling potential if allowed to dry out Therefore
measures should be taken to prevent excessive drying of the soil
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils have low to moderate swelling potential therefore some
precautionary measures are recommended for foundations and
on grade slabs placed on the existing soil
It is anticipated that approximately 4 5 feet of new fill will
be required under the front half of the house foundation We
feel that the key element regarding the foundation design for
this area will depend on the type and amount of new fill
material to be used

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and
driveway
Where a minimum of 2 feet of imported non expansive soil such
as crusher waste is used for the fill conventional slab on
grade design with an 8 inch deep perimeter may be used
Where on site fill soil is used a deeper 18 inch footing
embedment and special subgrade preparation with lime saturation
is recommended The general procedure listed hereunder should
be followed
1 The slab subgrade soils should be saturated with hoses with
small holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to
concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil
2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation and thickened edge of the
driveway prior to the above water saturating of the subgrade
soil We believe that about ten to fifteen 50 1b bags of
hydrated lime would be adequate to cover the slab subgrade
It is important that the soils engineer should inspect the
above subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of
the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install a gutter system around the house and that the gutter
water should be diverted far away from the footings and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against slab
footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
