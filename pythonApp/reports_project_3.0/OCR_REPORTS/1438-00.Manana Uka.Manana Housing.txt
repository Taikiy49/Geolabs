In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of July 18th
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of four 3 story apartment
structures with two paved parking areas The buildings will be
constructed of CMU walls and range in size from about 30 by 85 feet to 30
by 173 feet
It is anticipated that most of the site will be filled from 1 to 4
feet
The general layout of the proposed development is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The anticipated foundation wall loads are up to 4 5 kips per foot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of Luehu Street
between the State Library Building and the existing subdivision in Pearl
City Home Road is located about 200 feet east of
the site
The topography of this site is fairly flat sloping towards Luehu
Street at a slope gradient of about 2 to 3 percent The elevations at
the site range from approximately 88 to 80 feet
During the time of our exploration the site was mostly barren with
some low dirt mounds about 2 to 3 feet high Remnants of an old A C
road were noted along the east side of the lot An abandoned sewer line
appears to run along this roadway
A few large boulders are stockpiled near the northeast portion of
the lot

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes the Waianae
and Koolau Shield The project site is situated on the southern flank of
the Koolau Range
The shallow surface conditions at the site basically consists of
residual soil formed by the in place weathering and decomposition of the
lava rock formation
Soil Conditions
To explore the subsurface conditions four 4 borings were drilled
and sampled to depths of 20 to 21 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The borings encountered very stiff reddish brown silty clay and
clayey silt residual soil to depths of 10 to 21 feet
Highly weathered rock was encountered in Borings 1 and 2 at 14 and
10 feet respectively
Laboratory tests indicate that the residual soil has low to
moderate swelling potential
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings encountered very stiff residual soil which can provide
suitable bearing for the proposed structures However the laboratory
tests disclosed that some of the residual soil may have moderate swelling
potential Therefore slab on grade should be placed over a minimum of
12 inches of select non expansive soil to minimize the swelling potential
of the soil
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils should
be removed and disposed of properly off site Following the
clearing the ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned to optimum moisture and recompacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with
select on site material or non expansive import fill material
compacted to the above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement
It is anticipated that imported fill material will be required to
raise the site to the proposed finished grade Imported fill
material should be non expansive free of organics and other
deleterious materials and generally less than 6 inches in size
GEOLABS HAWAI
Manana Associates
W O 1438 00 Page 5
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density as specified above
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Foundations
Spread footing foundations may be used to support the proposed
structure
Footings for the 3 story structures should be placed a minimum of
18 inches below the outside lowest adjacent grade and should bear on the
very stiff natural soil or on the compacted non expansive fill The
structural fill should be placed during mass grading and completed prior
to footing construction
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f on the very stiff natural
soil and 2500 p s f on the compacted fill may be used The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
If a footing is situated adjacent to a utility trench the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used between the foundation and recompacted fill Additionally a
passive soil pressure of 300 p c f may be used to calculate lateral
resistance

Manana Associates

Estimated settlements for footings bearing on the natural ground or
compacted fill are anticipated to be less than one half of an inch total
and one fourth of an inch differential
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay soils are
moderately expansive and therefore should either be filled over or
over excavated down below the slab bottom to provide a minimum 12 inch
capping layer of non expansive imported soils
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underiying soils we recommend that the soil below the capping fill pad
be kept moist continuously
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to act as a
capillary break and to provide uniform support
Should an impervious membrane be incorporated below the slab to act
aS a vapor barrier one inch of moist sand should be placed on top of the
membrane to aid in concrete curing

Design Review
Final plans and specifications for site grading foundations and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

Field Exploration
he ee ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four 4 borings to depths of 20 to 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ke ee
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural soil The test
results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Nine one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B 3
One CBR and one moisture density determination tests were performed
on a bulk sample to evaluate the compaction characteristics of the
on site soils The test results are presented on Plate B 4
