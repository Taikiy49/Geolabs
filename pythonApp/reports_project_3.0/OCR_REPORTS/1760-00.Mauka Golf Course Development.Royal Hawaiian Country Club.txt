INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the subject project

The proposed Royal Hawaiian Country Club in Maunawili includes a
private access road two golf courses clubhouse structures and a roadway
bridge This preliminary study covers only the general clearing and site
grading for specific cut and fill areas within the proposed Mauka Golf
Course Subsurface exploration reports for the other development areas
will be issued separately
It is our understanding that railroad tie retaining walls are
planned in the Mauka Golf Course development to support selected fill
embankments Also a major retaining structure may be required to
support site grading fill for a parking area next to the Mauka
Clubhouse We understand that a geotechnical evaluation of these
retaining structures will be carried out at a later date and under a
separate report when the design criteria for these structures have been
established
The purpose of this study was to carry out surface geologic
reconnaissance and to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions at selected sites within the Mauka Golf Course development to
provide general subsurface soil data for site grading design utility
line excavation and recommendations for fill embankment
The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling seven
7 borings and eight 8 probes to depths of approximately 5 to 54 5
feet below the existing ground surface The results of our preliminary
field exploration and laboratory tests which we used in the engineering
analyses to formulate our recommendations are presented in the
appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of an area of land about 5000 feet long
2000 feet wide and located from just north of Olomana Stream to about
1000 feet south of Makawao Stream
In general the topography slopes down towards the stream channels
about 120 to 410 feet above Mean Sea Level
The area is generally covered with thick vegetation including Hao
trees Some large surface boulders were visible particularly along
stream courses The project site is located in a high rainfall area and
the ground was frequently wet during the field exploration in December
1986 and January 1987

Field Exploration
The preliminary field exploration consisted of surface geologic
reconnaissance seven 7 drilled borings to depths of 6 to 54 5 feet
below the existing ground surface and eight 8 hand auger probes to
depths of 3 to 11 feet at the approximate locations shown on the Site

Soil Conditions
Based on the preliminary field exploration the geologic soil types
in the project site may be generalized into the following three groups
Recent Alluvium Recent alluvial deposits were encountered mainly
in lower areas along existing streams This alluvium primarily
consisted of medium stiff to very soft brown clayey silt with many
boulders Localized soft soil conditions were encountered at Boring
12 and Probing 11 locations near Makawao Stream
Older _Alluvium Weathered Colluvium In general weathered older
alluvial and colluvial deposits were encountered mainly in areas
adjacent to existing stream courses and also west and south of
Makawao Stream The alluvium generally consisted of medium stiff to
stiff clayey silt to silty clay soils with some cobbles and
boulders
Colluvium is slope wash or previous landslide debris and is
locally inter mixed with the older alluvium It generally consisted
of unweathered to extremely weathered basalt cobbles and boulders in
a medium stiff to stiff clayey silt matrix Locally soft and wet
zones were encountered within the deposits
Residual Soil In general residual soil to extremely weathered
rock derived mainly from basaltic rocks were encountered mainly east
of Makawao Stream The weathered materials were primarily stiff
clayey silt with some gravel

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed indicate that
from a soil engineering point of view the site in general is suitable
for the proposed golf course development However some areas may
require special design and construction procedures
The preliminary subsurface exploration generally revealed that the
explored proposed site grading areas are underlain primarily by clayey
silts which are medium stiff to stiff alluviun colluvium and residual
soil Tocally some soft and wet zones were encountered within the
deposits
Based on our field exploration the most important aspect of this
preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation is the stability of the
proposed high cuts and fill embankments which are necessary to provide
the needed site grading for the golf course construction

Slope Stability
Some major cut slopes about 15 to 90 feet high are proposed for
the proposed golf course construction Based on our present boring and
probing data it is anticipated that most of the proposed cut slopes will
be excavated primarily in weathered colluvium and extremely weathered
basalt
Based on the laboratory determined shear strengths which were
measured after saturation and slope stability analysis performed it is
our opinion that cut slopes less than 40 feet high may be designed with a
slope ratio of up to 1 5 H V Cuts higher than 40 feet should utilize
a slope ratio of 2 1 H V or less in order for the proposed cuts to
have a minimum overall factor of safety of 1 5 see Plate 3 The
analysis assumes 8 foot wide benches every 20 feet of vertical slope
height and that excess water pressure will not be encountered in the
slope
In order to provide drainage of wet or seepage zones behind a
proposed cut slope along the 8th Fairway Boring 9 area trench drains
are recommended to intercept seepage and minimize potential build up of
excess water pressure at this site
Some soft soils and high water levels 0 5 foot below existing
ground were noted in a lowlying area adjacent to Makawao Strean A
granular stabilization layer and a multi phase fill placement program is
recommended in this area to minimize potential excessive settlement and
to stabilize the soft ground for short and long term embankment
stability
Part of the golf course development will be constructed by the
placement of fill embankments on the existing hillsides and also filling
existing valleys In order to reduce the destabilizing influence of
excessive build up of pore water pressure a trench drain system should

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have in place moisture contents well above its
optimum moisture content about 40 to 60 Therefore drying of
the excavated material may be required before using it as fill
material
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in
other drier areas should be modified to 85 for this project
Laboratory shear strength tests on compacted samples indicate that
the 85 compaction can provide adequate soil strength for the
proposed embankment fill The fills should be placed in 10 inch
maximum compacted lifts
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted
fill
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm
ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope For details see
Plate 5 This detail is essential for the stability of the fills
particularly to reduce the potential of sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers

Upon placement of the proposed approximately 10 to 20 foot thick
embankment fill adjacent to the stream course significant ground
settlement may occur due to compression of the soft deposits caused
by the new fill load The estimated total primary settlement due to
the new fill is about 1 to 2 feet
Since structures are not planned and large settlement in the fill
areas are tolerable along the fairway the main concern would be
bearing failure and the short and long term stability of the fill
embankment
We recommend that the placement of the required embankment fill
should be carried out in several increments of about 5 feet each
with at least one week rest period in between This will allow the
underlying soft soils to gain strength incrementally prior to
additional loading in order to reduce the possibility of bearing or
Slope failure

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon foundation obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by scaling from the topographic map Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined from the topographic map The physical location
and elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven 7 borings to depths of 6 to 54 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Eight 8 auger probes were hand driven to depths of 5 to 13 feet

the Site Plan Plate 2

visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings and Probings Plates A 1 through A 15

wall tube hand pushed The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

out at specific depth intervals inside selected borings The blow counts
to drive the cone at 12 inch intervals are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate probing depths

Community Planning Inc dated

techniques were used to obtain additional subsurface data

south of Makawao Stream Boring 8 The survey was performed using a
BISON model 1570C Refraction Seismograph receiving seismic signals
generated by the impacts of a 10 pound sledge hammer The approximate
location of the transect is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and the
results are presented on Plate A 16

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two 2 Atterberg Limit tests were performed to aid in the
Classification of the soils The test results are summarized on Plate
Bel
Two 2 California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed on
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate their suitability for road
subgrade support The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Proctor tests were performed to determine the moisture content
verses dry density relationship of the compacted soil The test results
are summarized on Plate B 1
Eight 8 direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the
soil The test results are plotted on Plates B 2 through B 10
Eleven 11 one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plates B 11 and 12

1
Horizontal slotted drains shall be installed where directed by the
engineer or owner The drains shall be sloped at 5 percent or as

pointed extension which shall extend not more than 0 5 foot beyond the

pipe PVC 2110 Slotted pipe shall be furnished in 10 foot lengths
ends of the pipe shall be machined to provide for a smooth wall slip
coupling Average configuration shall be 42 slots per row per foot
using 0 02 inch slots The slots shall consist of three 3 rows cut
circumferentially on the pipe on 120 degree centers commercially cut
at the factory
