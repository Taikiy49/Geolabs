PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is bounded by Kaiulani Avenue to the west, Koa Avenue to the south, 
and Prince Edward Street to the north on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site was 
previously occupied by the King’s Village complex. The general contractor for the project 
was Nordic PCL Construction, Inc. and the ACIP pile installation subcontractor was Kelikai 
Pacific Foundation, JV. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, we 
recommended supporting the new high-rise condominium and parking structure on a deep 
foundation system consisting of 24-inch ACIP piles with an allowable compressive load 
capacity of up to 1,200 kips per pile. Most of the ACIP piles would extend through the soft 
and/or loose lagoonal deposits (near the surface) and derive support primarily from skin 
friction and end-bearing within coral ledges and/or coralline detritus materials encountered 
below the lagoonal deposit. The ACIP pile length of up to about 100 feet was utilized for the 
1,200-kip capacity piles. The following table summarizes the ACIP pile foundation lengths 
utilized for the project.

BI-DIRECTIONAL METHOD LOAD TEST 
As part of the project specifications, two sacrificial 24-inch diameter load test piles, 
designated as LT-1 and LT-2, were installed at the approximate locations indicated on the 
ACIP Pile Identification Plan, Plate 1. The load test piles were installed by Kelikai Pacific 
Foundation, JV between January 8 and 10, 2020. The load test piles were installed with a 
bi-directional load cell and embedment strain gage instrumentation. 
A Bauer BG28 drill rig with a continuous hollow stem auger was utilized to install the 
ACIP test piles. A Schwing SP1250 hydraulic concrete pump was used to pump the grout 
into the pile system. The 24-inch diameter load test piles were extended to a depth of about 
110 feet below the existing ground surface.  
After the load test piles were installed and cured for about 7 days, GRL Engineers, 
Inc. performed the load tests on January 15 and 17, 2020. The 24-inch diameter load test 
piles were loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load Test Method to a

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of installing 185 ACIP piles 
(including 5 tower crane piles), was conducted from March 2, 2020 to April 20, 2020 under 
the near-continuous (full-time) observations by field engineers from our office. Kelikai Pacific 
Foundation, JV utilized Bauer BG28 drill rig with continuous hollow stem augers to install 
the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling/pumping 
equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, grout pressure, etc., on 
a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, which 
included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout volume 
used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed are shown 
on the ACIP Pile Identification Plans, Plate 1. The production ACIP pile installation summary 
is presented on Plates 2.1 through 2.7 of this letter report. 
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction.  The associated problems and the corresponding resolutions are presented in 
the “Remarks” column of the ACIP Pile Installation Summary. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been installed 
to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the allowable design load capacities.
