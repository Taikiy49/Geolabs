REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and the Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano ceased lava
flows from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad

plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the
southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project
site and to the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of
the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys
in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous
reef materials Placement of man made fills and commercial development of the area
within the last 20 years have brought the project area to its present form
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site designated as Lot is located in the
Kalaeloa area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is situated

and lows across the project limits

Coral Outcrop CR
Coral outcrop consists of coral or cemented calcareous sand formed in shallow
ocean water during the time the ocean stand was at a higher level It is exposed
on the ocean shore on the coastal plains and at the foot of the uplands Coral
outcrop makes up about 80 to 90 percent of the acreage The remaining 10 to
20 percent consists of a thin layer of friable red soil material in cracks crevices
and depressions within the coral outcrop
The coral formation in the project area are anticipated to be moderately weathered
and medium hard to hard in nature It should be noted that the strength characteristics of
coral formation are highly variable In addition the depositional nature of the coral
formation may create localized areas of hard crystalline coral intermixed with soft
severely fractured detritus debris It should be noted that coral formation is frequently
cavernous or contain pockets of voids Therefore there exists the potential for the
presence of cavities and or voids within the coral formation across the project site In
addition cavities in the coral formation could be filled with soft and or loose coralline
detritus and alluvial deposits
Groundwater is not anticipated at shallow depths below the existing ground
surface In general the project site is at an elevation several tens of feet above the
highest basal groundwater table in the area However groundwater levels can vary
significantly depending on seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection
and other factors In addition it should be noted that water seepage is encountered
commonly in coral formations due to infiltration of surface water

Undocumented Fills Alluvium
Undocumented fills and or alluvium may be present across the project site and may
not be exposed until additional exploration and or construction is underway In general
we recommend that undocumented fills and or clayey alluvium disclosed during additional
exploration and or site grading be over excavated where necessary to expose the
underlying firm ground The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with
compacted fill The areal extent and depth of over excavation should be determined in the
field during construction by the project geotechnical engineer The bottom of the
over excavated level should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of fill
materials
Cavities and Voids
As mentioned previously coral formations generally are highly variable in terms of
hardness weathering and fracturing In addition cavities and or voids are encountered
commonly in coral formations in the vicinity of the project site In many cases cavities and
voids in the coral formation may be filled with loose coralline deposits In areas where
coralline formation is anticipated near the ground surface special attention should be
given to the subgrade preparation to reduce the potential for distress to the future
improvements such as floor slabs and pavements
The excavated subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches where
possible moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled
with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to detect the presence of potential
near surface cavities sinkholes or loose zones The vibratory drum roller should be
operated at speeds of about 200 to 300 feet per minute If the proof rolling operations
detect a near surface cavity or loose zones the area should be ripped open cleaned of
the debris and then backfilled with compacted fill materials or lean concrete

Coral Excavation
As mentioned previously the development site likely is underlain by coral formation
at or near the existing ground surface The coral formation in the project area are
anticipated to be moderately weathered and medium hard to hard in nature It is
envisioned that the majority of the coral formation may be excavated with heavy
excavation equipment such as a large backhoe excavator However coral formations
typically contain localized hard and crystallized zones Therefore we anticipate that
difficult excavation conditions may arise in localized areas during construction It is
possible that the excavation for a portion of the utility line trenches will require the use of
hoerams or chipping Care must be exercised by the contractor to avoid over ripping

Lot Drainage
It should be emphasized that proper surface drainage especially within the lots is
critical to the performance of foundations and slabs Therefore the finished grades
outside the building structure should be sloped to shed water away from the foundations
and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems be installed
around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and
pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs also should be
avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsurface soils
Drainage swales with positive drainage should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation systems because ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
against the footings and slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the
potential for appreciable waier infiltration into the subsurface
Additional Exploration
A field exploration program should be conducted for the final design of the mass
grading and infrastructure installation for the proposed development In addition a
detailed field exploration and geotechnical engineering analysis should be conducted for
the design of the fine grading and structure foundations during the individual lot
development
