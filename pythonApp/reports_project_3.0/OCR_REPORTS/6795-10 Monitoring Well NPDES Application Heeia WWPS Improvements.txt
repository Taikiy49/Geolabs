This letter report summarizes our ground water sampling and testing services in

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Heeia WWPS is at 46-195 Yacht Club Road, in an existing residential 
subdivision. The pump station lot is between two residential lots and extends a relatively 
short distance from Yacht Club Road to the Kaneohe Bay shoreline. The existing 
structure consists of a one-story above-grade building over a two-level below ground 
structure. A paved asphaltic concrete driveway and underground utilities are present at 
the site.  
The proposed improvements primarily encompass a new building addition 
consisting of a one-story above-grade Generator Room over a one-level basement.  We 
anticipate that construction for the new building foundation will extend below the 
groundwater level; therefore, dewatering of the excavation may be required.  A National 
Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permit approved by the Hawaii State 
Department of Environmental Services will be necessary for the discharge of trench 
water (construction dewatering) into the storm sewer and sanitary sewer system if 
groundwater is encountered during the improvement works.   
Boring No. 1, drilled as part of the geotechnical investigation for the proposed plant 
improvement, was converted to a 2-inch diameter groundwater monitoring well consisting 
of about 40 feet of well screen with 0.020-inch slotted openings and about 10 feet of

SITE CONDITIONS 
The Heeia WWPS is at 46-195 Yacht Club Road, in an existing residential 
subdivision. The existing structure consists of a one-story, above-grade building over a 
two-level underground building. An asphaltic paved parking area and underground 
utilities are also present at the site. Based on the topographic survey map provided, the 
existing pump station site is relatively level to gently sloping towards the shoreline with 
existing ground surface elevations on the order of about +9 to +10 feet MSL. 
The project site is bounded by two adjacent residential lots and extends between 
Yacht Club Road towards the Kaneohe Bay shoreline in the Heeia area of Kaneohe on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The approximate project site location is shown on the 
Project location Map, Plate 1. The approximate limits of the Heeia WWPS project site

MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION 
We conducted the drilling, well installation, and well development tasks on 
January 15, 2013. Boring No. 1 was drilled to a depth of about 51.5 feet below the 
existing ground surface.  Monitoring well MW-1 was installed to a depth of about 49.5 feet 
below the existing ground surface.

PVC pipe installed to a depth of about 49.5 feet below the existing ground surface. The 
well consists of about 40 feet of well screen with 0.020-inch slot openings and about 
10 feet of solid casing.  The surface completion consisted of a 6-inch well cover that was 
installed flush with the existing ground surface. 
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck-mounted drill rig 
advancing PQ wash boring and coring tools.  The drilling and sampling tools were

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Boring No. 1 (converted to monitoring well MW-1) was drilled and sampled to a 
depth of about 51.5 feet below the existing ground surface. The approximate boring 
location (MW-1) is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Our field exploration generally encountered about 5 to 10 feet of medium dense/ 
medium stiff surface fills overlying loose sand and alluvial cobbles and boulders 
extending to approximately 17 feet below the existing ground surface. These materials 
were further underlain by stiff to very stiff alluvial deposits extending to about 40 feet 
below the existing ground surface.  Finally, medium dense to dense granular alluvial 
deposits were encountered extending to the maximum depth explored of about 51.5 feet 
below the existing ground surface.   
We encountered groundwater at a depth of about 11.3 feet below the existing 
ground surface in the drilled boring at the time of our field exploration.  The groundwater 
level encountered during drilling of the boring generally corresponds to an Elevation of 
about -0.85 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL).  Due to the close proximity of the project site to 
the shoreline, water levels are expected to vary with tidal fluctuations and storm surge 
conditions. It should be noted that water levels also may vary with seasonal rainfall, time 
of the year, and other factors. 
A detailed description of the soil materials encountered in the boring is presented 
in Appendix A. 
GROUNDWATER CONDITIONS 
A relatively shallow groundwater level was encountered at the project site. Due to 
the close proximity of the project site to the shoreline, it is likely that the groundwater level 
measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal fluctuations. In addition, the 
groundwater level may be influenced by seasonal precipitation, storm surge conditions, or 
other effects. 
At the time of groundwater sampling, the static water level in the monitoring well 
was measured at a depth of about 6.2 feet below the existing ground surface. This 
corresponds to an Elevation of about +3.4 feet MSL. 
Our geologist purged the monitoring well and collected the groundwater sample 
using a low-flow peristaltic pump. The sample was collected into containers provided by 
TestAmerica as being appropriate for the requested analyses. The ground water sample 
was kept in a cooler on ice and submitted to TestAmerica within about 2 hours of 
collection. The laboratory test report and chain of custody form are provided in 
Appendix B.

BEST MANAGEMENT PRACTICES (BMP) 
For this project, BMP would generally involve developing a non-discharge protocol.  
The most practical protocol for eliminating discharge of dewatering effluent is ground 
disposal by infiltration. If space on the site permits, a temporary dewatering discharge pit 
may be excavated on the site and the effluent discharged to the pit. The pit should be 
sized to accommodate a full day’s discharge with allowances for freeboard, decreased 
infiltration rates with clogging and excessive dewatering rates. 
If space for a temporary discharge pit is not available, consideration should be 
given to disposal of the effluent by the “back trench” method, in which the effluent is 
discharged to the granular bedding of an existing trench or previously constructed trench. 
ABANDONMENT OF MONITORING WELL 
Abandonment of the monitoring well was not part of our current scope of work; 
therefore, we recommend including abandonment of the monitoring well in the 
construction documents. The monitoring well should be abandoned properly and sealed 
with cement grout.

Field Exploration 
The monitoring well, identified as MW 1, extends to a depth of about 50 feet below 
the existing ground surface. The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 2. The boring was drilled using a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem 
augers. 
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural 
examination in the field by our geologist, who observed the drilling operations on a 
near-continuous basis. These classifications were further reviewed by visual 
observation and testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general conformance 
with the Unified Soil Classification System, as shown on Plate A. Graphic 
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Boring, 
Plates A-1.1 and A-1.2. 
Relatively “undisturbed” soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in 
general accordance with ASTM D 3550, Ring-Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving 
a 3-inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140-pound hammer falling 30 inches. In 
addition, some samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance 
with ASTM D 1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving a 
2-inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop. The blow 
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18-inch drive 
are shown as the “Penetration Resistance” on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate 
sample depths.
