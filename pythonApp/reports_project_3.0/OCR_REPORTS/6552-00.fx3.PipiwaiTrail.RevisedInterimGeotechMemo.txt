This memorandum serves as our revised Interim Geotechnical Memorandum IGM
for the Pipiwai Trail Improvements project based on our preliminary field investigation This
revised IGM summarizes the project setting existing conditions and potential geotechnical
hazards for the project and provides preliminary geotechnical recommendations for design
of the proposed improvements
Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui Tertiary
Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene Epoch
The project site is located in the Kipahulu area on the southwestern flank of the Haleakala
Volcano
The Kipahulu region of Maui was created by lava flows from the Kula Volcanic
Series and Hana Volcanic Series A long period of volcanic dormancy followed in this
region of Maui after cessation of the Kula Volcanic Series about 150 000 years ago
During this period the Kipahulu Valley generally was carved by stream erosion
Subsequently new volcanic activity about 13 000 to 30 000 years ago subjected the
valley to two series of lava flows the early and late Hana Volcanic Series
The Pipiwai Trail generally extends from Hana Highway to Waimoku Falls The
lower portion of the trail generally is underlain by lava flows from the Hana Volcanic
Series Lava flows from the Kula Volcanic Series are predominant along the upper portion
of the trail to Waimoku Falls Typically the basalt formation consists of thinly to thickly
bedded a a and pahoehoe type lava flows
The basalt formation of the lava flows generally are underlain by a veneer of stream
deposited alluvial soils The alluvial soils may consist of a poorly sorted mixture of
boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines

The existing boardwalk is generally in fair to good condition and is used by many
visitors daily However the existing boardwalk could be improved for increased safety and
durability and from an aesthetic point of view Some of the common issues observed
include the following
1
In many areas the concrete masonry block foundations have settled tilted
and or twisted off center The longitudinal stringers are no longer supported
on the concrete masonry blocks in some locations Therefore some
bouncing and tilting could be felt when walking on the boardwalk
2
Soils adjacent to the concrete masonry blocks have eroded in many areas
along the down slope side of the boardwalk Therefore bottoms of the
concrete masonry blocks are exposed and or shifting of the blocks has
occurred in many areas
3
The outside stringers appear to be placed too far in at many locations with
edges of the planks overhanging the stringers by over 5 to 6 inches This
significant overhang results in bending of the planks when walking along the
edges of the boardwalk
4
The boardwalk is generally not straight longitudinally wavy and vertically
sagging and tilting Some amount of waviness sagging and tilting of the
boardwalk could be seen at most locations
5
Gaps between the planks are not consistent In some areas large gaps of
up to 1 5 to 3 inches were observed especially around the outside curves

Surface and Subsurface Conditions
The following subsections describe the conditions of the existing surface and
anticipated subsurface materials along the Boardwalk and at the Pedestrian Bridge and
Trail Terminus areas
Boardwalk
The majority of the boardwalk segments are located in a bamboo forest The
topography of the areas around the boardwalk is generally level to slightly sloping Based
on the topographic survey map the ground elevations vary only by about 30 feet along the
approximately 1 800 foot stretch of Segments 1 through 12 Drainage gullies create
localized areas of steeper slopes such as at Segments 6 and 13
The surface soils generally consist of very soft and wet clays Based on the heavily
vegetated condition of the site we envision the surface soils generally would remain wet
throughout the year It appears that boulders and cobbles are embedded in the clayey
surface soils at relatively shallow depths less than 1 to 3 feet in most areas Further
investigation would be required to evaluate the thickness of the clayey soils
Because the surface materials generally consist of wet clayey soils significant
sources of fill materials are not present along the existing boardwalk There are some
scattered cobbles and boulders throughout the trail but significant efforts would be
required to breakdown the material into usable aggregates

Pedestrian Bridge and Trail Terminus
The proposed pedestrian bridge and overlook platform is located at the end of the
Pipiwai Trail near Waimoku Falls At the base of Waimoku Falls two branches of the
stream converge into a single stream
The pedestrian bridge will traverse the southern branch of the stream The existing
trail has stone steps leading down to the stream on both sides The existing stream bed
between the stone steps is about 35 feet in width The span from the tops of the stone
steps is about 65 feet The stream was about 6 to 12 inches deep at the time of the
preliminary field investigation We envision that the water depth and flow rate of the stream
would vary significantly based on precipitation
Proposed pedestrian bridge crossing
The proposed overlook platform is located between the northern and southern
branches of the stream The proposed overlook platform will be about 60 feet past the top
of the stone steps

Water and Groundwater
Due to the presence of Pipiwai Stream shallow groundwater conditions should be
anticipated at the pedestrian bridge and overlook platform areas In addition groundwater
and stream water levels are expected to fluctuate with precipitation Significant rise in
stream water levels may be a potential hazard to the pedestrian bridge and overlook
platform Therefore design of the pedestrian bridge and overlook platform should take into
consideration the rise in water levels due to storm events
The surface soils are generally very wet along the boardwalk areas The trail
traverses several drainage gullies in these areas Although standing water generally was
not observed at the time of the initial field investigation ponding of surface water runoff
should be anticipated after periods of heavy rain in areas of local topographic depressions
In addition shallow groundwater conditions and zones of perched water may be present at
the site In general very high moisture contents of the near surface soils along the
boardwalk should be anticipated year round
Ground Settlement
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions the boardwalk area is underlain by
clayey soils with embedded cobbles and boulders Potential ground settlement due to
consolidation would depend on the thickness of clayey soils and amount of embedded
cobbles boulders present in the soil In general ground settlement due to consolidation is
not expected to be a significant hazard considering the relatively light structures planned
However settlement of foundations due to erosion of the underlying materials and or
inadequate preparation of the subgrades should be addressed in the design of new
structures
Based on the presence of cobbles and boulders at the proposed pedestrian bridge
and overlook platform areas ground settlement is not anticipated to be a significant hazard
at these locations
Slope Stability
In general the topography of the site is generally level to slightly sloping In
general slopes steeper than about 1H 1V are limited to localized drainage gully crossings
The depths of these drainage gullies are generally less than about 6 feet therefore
hazards associated with slope stability may be considered low for this project

Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused by shifts in the
tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to volcanic
activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or during volcanic
eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground movement of magma
that comes close to the surface but does not erupt
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on the
Island of Hawaii Earthquakes directly associated with the movement of magma are
concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on the Island of
Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over 90 percent are related to
volcanic activity the risk of seismic activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes with
increased distance from the Island of Hawaii
The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes greater than
Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the Island of Hawaii
To a lesser degree the Island of Maui also has experienced earthquakes greater than
M5 Therefore moderate to strong earthquakes have occurred in the County of Maui

Foundation Details The boardwalk foundations should be constructed with
consistent foundation details The following paragraphs summarize the
preliminary conceptual design of the boardwalk foundations
Special considerations in regards to the design of boardwalk foundation include the
remoteness of the project site lack of aggregate sources on site and wet subgrade soil
conditions Due to the remote location of the project site importing of materials would
need to be limited as much as practicable Therefore we envision that the majority of the
existing boardwalk materials such as planks stringers and concrete masonry blocks will
be re used for the boardwalk reconstruction The following subsections present alternative
foundation systems for the boardwalk
Shallow Foundations
In general the boardwalk may be supported on shallow spread footings consisting
of concrete masonry blocks In general the concrete masonry blocks should be
embedded a minimum 12 inches below lowest adjacent grade We envision that
the footings will be excavated manually Where hard materials are encountered
during footing excavations the embedment depth may be reduced to 6 inches In
the process of excavating to provide a level footing subgrade with the
recommended embedment we anticipate the footing excavation will be larger than
the concrete masonry block foundations Due to the wet conditions and the lack of
decent backfill materials we recommend backfilling the footing excavations with
mortar sand cement grout around the concrete masonry block foundations to
reduce the potential for ponding water to accumulate around the poorly backfilled
footing excavations
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 1 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of footings embedded in mortar and bearing on the on site
materials for the service limit state Foundation bearing values of 2 250 and

Stabilization of Subgrade Soils
For boardwalk footings supported on a shallow foundation system we believe the
footing subgrade soils may be stabilized to reduce the anticipated amount of
settlement In general subgrade stabilization would consist of over excavating by a
minimum of 2 feet below the bottom of footing and replacing with granular backfill
wrapped in a woven geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X or equivalent The over
excavation should extend at least 12 inches laterally beyond the footprint of the
footings on all sides Backfill materials should be moisture conditioned and
re compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
If the shallow foundations supported on stabilized subgrades in accordance with
our recommendations we estimate the total settlements of the foundations to be
less than about 1 inch static loading conditions Differential settlements between
adjacent footings supported on similar materials may be on the order of about
0 5 inches
It should be noted that sufficient sources of granular fill materials are not present
along the boardwalk There are some scattered cobbles and boulders throughout
the trail but significant efforts would be required to breakdown the material into
usable aggregates In addition the compaction requirement likely will be difficult to
achieve for backfill materials placed over the soft clayey soils Therefore cost
implications and constructability issues should be evaluated if implementation of the
foundation subgrade stabilization is desired
