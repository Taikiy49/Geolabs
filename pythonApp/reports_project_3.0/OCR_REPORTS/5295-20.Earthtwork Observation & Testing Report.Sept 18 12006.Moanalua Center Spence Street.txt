PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located at the existing Moanalua Shopping Center in the vicinity of
Spence Street on the makai side of Bougainville Boulevard in the Pearl Harbor area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided the existing Moanalua
Shopping Center will be demolished and replaced by a two story office building for the
Navy and two one story buildings for a retail strip mall As part of the project development
a new asphaltic concrete paved parking lot will be constructed in the vicinity of
Buildings 2602 and 2613 In addition minor repairs and resurfacing of the existing
pavements around the new development may be required for the project construction
The layout of the new development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The new office and retail buildings will have floor areas of approximately
55 000 and 25 000 square feet respectively Based on the information provided we
envision that the new buildings will be constructed of tilt up concrete wall panels with

EARTHWORK AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
The general contractor for the Moanalua Center project is Nordic Construction Ltd
The site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and _ utility
installation for the project were performed by Koga Engineering The site grading
consisted of the demolition of existing structures and pavements footing excavation
subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 8 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 8 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and
preparation the surface soils were removed until hard volcanic tuff formation was exposed
at the bottom of the footing excavations The resulting over excavation was backfilled with
flowable fill minimum compressive strength of 150 psi up to the design footing bottom
elevations
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 397 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 21 of this report These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on our field representatives observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading foundation excavation
pavement and utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated and subsequent
recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
