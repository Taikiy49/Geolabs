PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project entails the construction of a new student center at the southwestern
side of the Sacred Hearts Academy premises in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with the project specifications a probing and grouting program was
implemented to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse
of cavities or compression of loose zones below the new building structure The probing
and grouting observation work were observed by our office and summarized in our report
entitled Foundation Probing and Grouting Report Sacred Hearts Academy Student
Center 3253 Waialae Avenue Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
We understand that it is desired to proceed with the building footing excavation
slab subgrade preparation and utility line installation work for the new Student Center A
layout of the new facility is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Based on the existing
topography we envision minimal cuts and fills will be required to achieve the design finish
grades for the project construction

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The general contractor for the project is Okada Trucking Co Ltd who also
performed the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation work The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing footing
excavation subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations and completion of the foundation
probing and grouting program the surface soils at the footing foundations were generally
over excavated to expose the underlying basalt rock formation Our field representatives
observed and approved the footing subgrade condition prior to backfilling the
over excavation with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM up to the design footing
bottom elevation
During the earthwork operations non expansive select granular fill materials were
used to raise the existing ground to the bottom of the building slab cushion and pavement
section The non expansive select granular fill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The aggregate subbase under the AC pavement was
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New utility connections were installed to serve the new development In general a
bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping
and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 139 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 9 of

in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
