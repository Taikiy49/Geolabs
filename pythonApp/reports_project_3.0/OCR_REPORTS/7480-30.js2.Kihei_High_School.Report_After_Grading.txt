PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site encompasses approximately 70 acres of undeveloped land east of 
Piilani Highway, between Kulanihakoi and Waipuilani Gulches, as shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1, and General Site Plan, Plate 2. Phase 1 of the project included 
construction of the new school infrastructure, including on-site and off-site infrastructure 
improvements described below. 
On-Site Infrastructure 
Site grading including excavation, crushing and stockpiling of excavated 
basalt rock. 
Preparation of subgrade for the building pads, parking areas, and 
driveways.

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The site grading and utility installation work for the Kihei High School, Phase 1 
Infrastructure (On-Site & Off-Site) project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from August 6, 
2018 to January 14, 2020.  The site grading and utility installation consisted of clearing 
and grubbing, cuts up to about 20 feet deep, fills up to about 16 feet thick, and placement 
and compaction of fills and backfills to achieve the design finished grades for the project. 
Goodfellow Bros., Inc. performed the site grading and utility installation work.  During the 
site grading and backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for 
testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results for materials used at the on-site and off-site locations are summarized in the 
attached Appendices A (Plate A) and B (Plate B). 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content and compacted in accordance with project specifications to a minimum of 90 or 
95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of

the compaction tests performed at the on-site and off-site locations are summarized in the

tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and

approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
“Boulder” fills with a maximum aggregate dimension of 3 feet were used in portions 
of the embankment fills, and structural fill consisting of materials particles no larger than 
6 inches in largest dimension and containing between 10 and 30 percent particles passing 
the No. 200 sieve.  Since conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in 
these “boulder” or “structural” fills, a testing program to evaluate the number of passes by 
a specific compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction was performed. 
Hand probe and visual observations were used during compaction of the “boulder” and 
“structural” fills to confirm a firm unyielding compacted material. 
Our services consisted of full-time and part-time field observation of the contractor’s 
fill and backfill placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the 
above grading operations.
