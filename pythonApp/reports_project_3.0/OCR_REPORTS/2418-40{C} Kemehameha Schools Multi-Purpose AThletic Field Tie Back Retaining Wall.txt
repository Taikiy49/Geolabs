PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Kamehameha Schools in the Kapalama
Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A geotechnical engineering exploration of the project site was performed by
Geolabs Inc and our recommendations were presented in the report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Multi Purpose Athletic Field Kamehameha
Schools Bishop Estate Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated The purpose of
our geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project was to observe
the construction of the specialty tieback retaining wall for general conformance with the
project plans and specifications
Based on the project plans a specialty retaining wall system consisting of a tieback
retaining wall was constructed behind to the east of the bleachers for the multi purpose
athletic field as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In general construction of the permanent

tieback retaining wall involved installing soldier piles consisting of wide flange steel beams
embedded in drilled holes along the retaining wall alignment The annular space between
the steel beam and the drilled hole was then filled with structural concrete up to the bottom
of the finished excavation elevation Above the finished excavation level the annular
space between the steel beam and the drilled hole was filled with lean concrete or
controlled low strength material CLSM to facilitate excavation of the materials to install
the retaining wall facing
When excavation for the cut was made the first level of ttebacks was then installed
Welded wire fabric was placed on the excavated face and a flashcoat of shotcrete was
applied to the face for temporary shoring of the excavation As the permanent tiebacks
were installed each of the tiebacks was stressed and proof loaded Excavation for the
retaining wall then proceeded to the next level and a subsequent level of tiebacks was
then installed The temporary facing of shotcrete with welded wire fabric was applied a and
extended downward as the excavation progressed
DIFFERING SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the field explorations performed in 1990 1992 and 1999 the project site
was underlain by stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays extending to depths of about
5 to 60 feet below the ground surface The surface soil layer was underlain by weathered
basalt formation extending to the maximum depth explored of about 83 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the
time of our field exploration
As shown on the project plans a tieback retaining wall system consisting of soldier
piles and permanent tiebacks was specified at the north and northeastern portions of the
multi purpose athletic field During installation of the soldier piles for the tieback retaining
wall a differing subsurface condition was encountered along the tieback retaining wall
alignment at the northeastern portion of the multi purpose athletic field In general hard
basalt formation was encountered within the planned depths of the soldier piles making
drilling for the soldier piles difficult Therefore a soil nail retaining wall system was
adopted for the retaining wall at the northeastern portion of the multi purpose athletic field
The tieback retaining wall system was installed only at the northern portion of the
multi purpose athletic field

soldier Pile Instailation
In general an engineer from Geolabs was present at the site to observe the drilling
steel beam installation and concrete placement during installation of the soldier piles The
soldier piles were drilled to depths of about 20 to 42 feet below the ground level The steel
beams were then installed in the drilled holes and the drilled holes were filled with
structural concrete up to the bottom of the finished excavation elevation The upper
portions of the drilled holes above the finished excavation level were filled with CLSM to
the top of the holes
Based on our observations we believe that the 37 soldier piles for the retaining wall
have been installed in substantial conformance with the project plans and specifications
and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices A summary of the
soldier pile installation is provided on the Soldier Pile Installation Summary Plates 4 1 and
4 2 The individual soldier pile installation records of the 37 soldier piles installed for the
project are provided in Appendix A for reference
Tieback Installation
An engineer from Geolabs generally observed the drilling of the tieback holes
installation of the tieback tendons and grouting of the tiebacks during the tieback
installation for the retaining wall Based on our records the tiebacks were installed to
depths of about 51 to 67 feet behind the face of the retaining wall The drilled holes for the
tiebacks were inclined between about 18 and 20 degrees from the horizontal plane The
tendons for the tiebacks were then installed with 3 foot tail lengths and the drilled holes for
the tiebacks were grouted to the design bond lengths
It should be noted that two of the tiebacks Tieback Nos T 6 and T 49 were
abandoned during the installation due to difficulties in getting the tiebacks installed in
accordance with the project specifications Therefore the two abandoned tiebacks were
Based on the observations of our field representatives we believe that the tiebacks
for the permanent retaining wall have been installed in general conformance with the
project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction
practices A summary of the tieback installation is provided on the Tieback Installation
Summary Plates 5 1 through 5 3 The individual tieback installation records for the
66 tiebacks and the two abandoned tiebacks are provided in Appendix B
Tieback Testing
In accordance with the project specifications all the tiebacks for the project were
subjected to proof loading or performance testing In general ninety percent or
60 tiebacks for the project were proof tested In addition the remaining six tiebacks were
subjected to performance testing which included 2 hour creep tests In general all the

tiebacks were loaded to the maximum test load of 150 percent of the design load The
associated movements for the tiebacks at each load increment up to the maximum test
load were measured and recorded by an engineer from Geolabs After loading the
tiebacks to the maximum test load the load on each tieback was subsequently lowered to
the design load and the tieback was locked off at the design load The proof tests and the
performance tests conducted on the tiebacks were performed under the observation of an
engineer from Geolabs A summary of the tieback testing is provided on Plates 6 1

the specified criteria and are acceptable for incorporation into the permanent retaining
wall Based on our observations of the tieback installation and the tests performed it is
our professional opinion that the tiebacks have been installed to provide the design load
with tolerable movements The test results on the individual tiebacks tested are presented
on the Tieback Test Data Sheets in Appendix C
Shotcrete Application for Permanent Retaining Wall
The tieback retaining wall system consisted of a temporary and a permanent
concrete wall facing that was constructed by applying shotcrete to the face of the
excavation As part of our special inspection services on the project an engineer from
Geolabs was present to observe the application of shotcrete for the permanent retaining
wail facing
In general our field representatives observed the placement of the reinforcing bars
for the permanent retaining wall facing and the application of shotcrete for the permanent
retaining wall facing Based on our observations during the tieback retaining wall
installation it is our professional opinion that the shotcrete for the permanent retaining wall
facing have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices
