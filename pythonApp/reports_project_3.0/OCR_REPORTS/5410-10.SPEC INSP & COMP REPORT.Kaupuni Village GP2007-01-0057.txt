Due to the presence of soft and or loose soil deposits underlying the project site
ground settlements on the order of about 3 to 5 inches may occur when the new structural
fills are placed over the compressible soil deposits Therefore a settlement monitoring
program will be implemented during the mass grading phase of the project to monitor the
filled ground settlement
SETTLEMENT MONITORING PROGRAM
As previously mentioned new structural fills on the order of about 4 feet thick will
be placed across the entire project site for the planned development As indicated in our
geotechnical engineering report prepared for the project loose sandy soils and soft silty
clays may be anticipated at shallow depths and extend to depths of over 25 feet below the
ground surface across the project site Therefore some ground settlements would occur
when the new structural fills of about 4 feet thick were placed over the potentially
compressible soils
A settlement monitoring program was established to evaluate the magnitude and
rate of the estimated settlements during the settlement waiting period prior to the
construction of the building structures and infrastructure installation To monitor the actual
settlement rate six settlement gauges were installed at selected locations across the
project site prior to the fill placement operations Alcon Associates Inc optically read
the settlement gauges on a periodic basis and transmitted the readings to our office for
review
In general non expansive select granular fill material was used as a source of
structural fill materials to raise the existing ground to the finish grade of the mass grading
operations The settlement monitoring period commenced on May 23 2007 and
terminated on Filled ground settlements up to about 3 8 inches were
recorded based on the settlement gauge readings provided by Alcon Associates Inc
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company Ltd performed the mass grading operations for the
Kaupuni Village project The mass grading work consisted of subgrade preparation
placement and compaction of structural fills CRM site retaining wall construction and
subsurface drain excavation and installation

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was
over excavated where necessary to provide the 4 foot thick structural fill capping layer as
indicated above The over excavation requirement was terminated earlier when hard coral
formation was encountered during the excavation operation The over excavated
subgrades were then scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content and recompacted with a large sheepsfoot compactor to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor
Structural fill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 and 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
For the CRM site retaining wall along the property line the in situ soils were
over excavated to a depth of about 2 feet below the footing bottom and replaced with
structural fills compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The 2 foot
structural fill requirement was omitted where hard coral formation was encountered at the
footing subgrade elevation
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 58 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 4 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading
operations for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the
referenced report dated May 12 2005 and subsequent recommendations
