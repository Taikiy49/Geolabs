Dear Mr. Maulit: 
The pavement structural section for the Kaupakalua Road Reconstruction, 
Phase 2 project was based on the recommendations presented in the above-referenced 
report prepared by our office. The recommendations for the pavement structural section 
consisted of 2.5 inches of Asphaltic Concrete (AC) over 4.0 inches of Asphalt Concrete 
Base (ACB) on 6.0 inches of Aggregate Base (AB) over a layer of reinforcing geogrid 
(Tensar TriAx Grid, TX7) on a layer of permeable separator (Mirafi 180N) over compacted 
subgrade. Results of the pavement design analyses are contained in the 
above-referenced Pavement Justification Report, dated January 21, 2019, for the project 
prepared by Geolabs, Inc. 
We understand that a shallow waterline traverses the eastbound lane of the 
Kaupakalua Road from the edge of pavement to approximately 3 feet into the travel 
lane and extends approximately from Stations 173+00 to 178+00 for a total length of 
500 linear feet. The depth of the waterline varies from about 18 inches at 
Station 173+00 to about 7 inches at Station 174+00 and remains at 7 inches deep to

Station 176+00, where it begins to progress back to about 18 inches below the top of 
the pavement by Station 178+00. Because the water main traversing part of the travel 
lane at shallow depths of about 7 to 18 inches below the existing grades, it is not 
practical to install the 12.5-inch thick pavement structural section without encountering 
and/or potentially damaging the shallow utility line during construction.  Given the above 
considerations for the shallow utility line, a thinner pavement structural section will be 
required to reduce the potential for encountering the shallow utility line that may require 
relocation and/or repair during construction. 
Based on our discussions with the County of Maui and the Contractor, we concur 
with the modified pavement reconstruction section extending approximately 5 feet in 
from the edge of pavement of the eastbound travel lane from Stations 173+00 to 
174+00, and from 176+00 to 178+00 (approximately a total of 300 linear feet) that a 
pavement structural section consisting of about 6.5 inches of asphaltic concrete 
pavement (2.5 inches of Mix V AC sandwiched with a layer of GlasGrid in the middle 
placed over 4.0 inches of ACB) would be a reasonable compromise given the shallow 
utility line. The width of the reduced pavement structural section is on the order of about 
4 to 5 feet from the edge of pavements.  This reduced pavement structural section (from 
12.5 inches down to 6.5 inches of total pavement thickness) is a significant reduction in 
the pavement structural section and the corresponding pavement design life. 
For the eastbound lane of roadway from Stations 174+00 to 176+00, we also 
concur with changing the design to resurfacing of the roadway with 2.5 inches Mix V AC 
sandwiched with a layer of GlasGrid in the middle placed over the cold planed area. 
Compaction of the asphalt is recommended to be closely monitored to just achieve the 
compaction requirements and not over compact the area. 
Based on the reduced pavement structural section, the pavement design life was 
reduced from 20 years to about ten years when calculated based on the State of Hawaii 
Department of Transportation method of asphaltic concrete pavement design.  The 
State of Hawaii Department of Transportation method of asphaltic concrete pavement 
design is the same method used in the Pavement Justification Report for the project.  
Due to the reduced pavement structural section, we recommend that a more diligent 
inspection program should be implemented to review the conditions of the reduced 
pavement structural section overtime to maintain the integrity of the pavement.  If 
needed, a more vigorous maintenance program would be implemented based on the 
results of the inspection program. 
An installation guide of the GlasGrid Pavement Reinforcement System is 
provided with this letter for information purposes only.  The Contractor should follow the 
installation procedures and requirements from the manufacturer of the specific 
pavement reinforcement system used for the project.

ROAD PREPARATION 
Complete all crack sealing, pothole filling, base repairs, leveling

ROAD SURFACE CONDITION 
Surface must be: 
˴ Dry 
˴ Clean and dust-free 
˴ Between 5°C (40°F) and 60°C (140°F)

PRODUCT STORAGE 
In order to preserve adhesive properties GlasGrid products must

TWO EASY-TO-PLACE METHODS 
Fabric Tractor Installation
The first, and more common approach, involves mechanical 
placement, typically with a fabric tractor that has been modified 
so that the GlasGrid material can be front-mounted (Figure 1). 
The tractor is typically used for full-width installations but can 
also be used for detail repairs that are sufficiently large. 
Fabric Tractor Procedure:
1.	 Load roll onto front of tractor. Always load the red-colored
end of the core on the same end each time to allow place‑ 
ment of GlasGrid product with the self-adhesive side down. 
2.	Roll with rubber tired roller or with specially designed fabric
tractor to activate the pressure sensitive adhesive. 
3.	If a tack coat is specified, apply tack coat and allow to cure.
4.	Pave.
Manual Placement Installation
An alternative installation method involves manual placement 
of the GlasGrid product (Figure 2). Although the product is 
physically placed by hand, it is highly recommended that the 
GlasGrid roll be mounted on the back of a truck or other vehicle 
to help maintain tension during placement. Manual installation 
is more commonly used for localized areas of road.
Manual Placement Procedure 
1.	 Load roll on an appropriate dispensing device on the back
of a pick-up truck. To ensure that the GlasGrid product  
will always unroll with the sticky side face down, load  
so that the red-colored end of the core is on the same  
side each time.
2.	Secure the roll on the dispensing device with locking
device. 
3.	Secure start of roll on pavement as the truck slowly moves
away. 
4.	For shorter pieces pull GlasGrid product off roller to desired
length. Cut with utility knife and lay by hand. 
5.	Roll only with rubber tired roller. Roller must be kept clean.
6.	If a tack coat is specified, apply tack coat and allow to cure.
7.	 Pave.

TEST FOR PROPER ADHESION 
The following procedure can be used to determine whether 
sufficient adhesion exists between the GlasGrid and the 
underlying asphalt:
˴ Cut a square-shaped sample of the 
GlasGrid material approximately  
1m² (1 sq yd) in size.
˴ Place the sample on the road surface to 
be paved and under the same conditions.
˴ Apply adequate vertical pressure to fully activate the 
pressure sensitive adhesive e.g., by use of a rubber tired 
roller or by other means.
˴ Insert the hook of a spring balance under the center of the 
GlasGrid sample (Figure 5).
˴ Pull the spring balance upward until the sample starts to 
pull loose, and record the gauge reading.
˴ In the event that 20 lbs (19 kg) or more force is required  
to pull the sample up from the road surface, sufficient 
adhesion has been achieved to support typical paving 
operations. Therefore the placement of GlasGrid may 
proceed. 
˴ In the event that the sample does not have sufficient 
adherence, identify the cleanliness or moisture issues 
present and resolve them before installing the rest of  
the GlasGrid material.
