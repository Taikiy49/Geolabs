INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed Keaau Shopping Center to be
located on Pahoa Road in The general
area of the site with relation to the surroundings is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development for the shopping center will include
four 4 single story commercial structures The structures
will be used for a restaurant variety and garden store
retail shop and a supermarket The actual structural loading
was not available at this time However it is assumed
that the proposed structural loading will be wood construc
tion and relatively light
Asphalt concrete parking area will be provided on the north
eastern portion of the site
Minor cut and fill up to about 2 0 feet is planned for the
required site grading
SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The lava rock encountered at the project site belongs to the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 976 00 P 4
present Mauna Loa shield volcano and represents the latest
lava flows of the project area which came down the slopes
in successive flows of various thicknesses and time intervals
This relatively young rock formation encountered close to
the ground surface generally consists of the clinker portion
of the Aa basalt
No lava tubes were encountered within the limits of our test
pit exploration although lava tubes may be encountered in
this type of lava flow A relatively small cavity was
noted during the excavation of Test Pit No 1 but it
appeared to be a cavity formed in a highly weathered and
fractured section of rock rather than a lava tube The
fractured porous and weathered nature of this rock makes
the formation permeable in those specific areas but the
entire area may not necessarily be permeable since seepage
water was noted to be ponding on the rock surface at the
bottom about 3 0 feet down of Test Pit No 1
Surface Conditions
Presently most of the site is covered with grass and brush
up to about 4 0 feet high Presently there are no struc
tures remaining at the site The site slopes down gently
from north to south with ground surface elevations ranging
from 344 to 360 feet

Presently the site is being used as a horse pasture
The proposed development site was used previously as a
theatre building along with a few single story structures
existing at one time on the northeastern portion of the
property Old foundation slab was encountered in one of
the test pits Pit No 2 about 2 feet below the existing
ground surface However old foundation debris as well
as abandoned cesspools may exist elsewhere on the site
The surface fill materials on the northeast portion of the
Site consist of clayey silt with gravel and some boulders
Aa clinker was noted near the surface in the remaining
southern half of the site
Subsurface Conditions
A total of seventeen 17 test pits were excavated at the
proposed development site to explore the general subsurface
conditions The test pits ranged in depth from 3 0 to 10 0
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on Site Plan Plate l
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendices along with test pit
logs and laboratory test results

Based on the information obtained from the dug test pits
and field reconnaissance the northeastern portion of the
Site is generally underlain with about 1 to 4 feet of sur
face fill material The fill material consists of clayey
Silt gravel boulders vegetation and some old concrete
debris
Below the surface fill Aa basalt flow was encountered up
to the depths of all the test pits The Aa basalt lava
flow generally consisted of a loose to dense clinker layer
about 1 to 7 feet thick over dense weathered basalt
During the field exploration cavities were encountered only
at Test Pit No 1 near the restaurant site at a depth of
approximately 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Some groundwater seepage was noted during the excavation of
Test Pit No 1 Generally the materials encountered at
the other test pits were also noted to be relatively moist
The moist conditions are probably due to the recent heavy
rainwater Seepage coming from the upper areas adjacent to
the site
Groundwater was not encountered in the other test pits at
the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration laboratory test results
and engineering analysis the proposed light load commercial
structures could be supported on shallow spread and conti
nuous wall footings founded on the in situ Aa clinker
layers or compacted fill The bottom of the dug footing
should be compacted by mechanical tamping to obtain a uni
form dense bearing surface
In the general area of the proposed restaurant structure
it appears that hard weathered basalt could be encountered
at a depth of about 3 0 feet below the existing ground sur
face If higher bearing capacity is needed foundations
for the restaurant structure could extend down through the
upper Aa clinkers founded on underlying hard basalt
Bearing values up to 5000 p s f may be used for footing
bearing on this basalt formation Subsurface cavities
could be encountered below the shallow foundation level
To reduce the possibility of foundation settlements due to
the presence of cavities near the bottom of footing excava
tions located in the restaurant structure a cavity probe
should be performed at each major column location with an
air drill for a depth of 3 feet below the footing bottom

The northeastern portion of the site general location of
the proposed variety and garden store encountered surface
fill about 1 to 4 feet This fill material is mixed
with debris and old foundation pieces This fill layer
should not be used for foundation support However this
surface layer can be re cycled after the concrete rubble
and boulders are removed
In general site preparation and proof rolling should be
performed prior to any footing excavation
Specific recommendations for site grading preparation design
and construction of foundation are presented in the subsequent
sections of this report
Site Preparation
Prior to foundation excavation and filling the site should
be grubbed and cleared of all surface vegetation debris and
old foundations if present
Based on the test pits it appears that portions of the site
could be underlain with previous demolition debris and
abandoned cesspools below the existing ground surface If
these are encountered within the proposed structural and
parking areas they should be excavated the debris and
rubble removed and then recompacted to obtain a minimum 90
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 976 00 P 9
density Old cesspools encountered should be backfilled
with compacted granular fill
After the above site clearing and preparation the remaining
exposed subgrade within the structural slab and parking areas
should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and then proof
rolled to detect soft pockets The proof rolling should be
observed by a soils engnieer and continue until a minimum
90 density is obtained
The preliminary site grading plan indicates only minor
less than five feet cut and fill Other and additional
earthwork operations at the site will generally include footing
excavations and backfill
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings On site excavated Aa clinker materials
broken up to less than 6 inch sizes or non expansive
imported granular material could be used for backfilling
around footings under slabs and parking areas if required
No existing on site fill clayey silt material mixed with
debris or large rock organic or deleterious matter should
be used as structural fill under the footing foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 976 00 P 10
It is recommended that the off site borrow if required
should be tested and approved by the soil engineer prior
to transportation to the site to assure that it is suitable
for the proposed construction The granular material should
not generally contain large rock 6 inches or more organic
or deleterious matter
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 8 inch
lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maximum
dry density within the limits of structures and 90 percent
outside the structure areas as obtained using ASTM D 1557
70 Modified Proctor as a standard
Foundations
For the proposed structures generally shallow spread
footing foundations and or continuous wall footings placed
on the near surface Aa clinker formation or compacted
fill can be used to support the proposed light structural
loads All disturbed loose material at the bottom of
footing excavations should be recompacted to obtain a
uniform bearing surface
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 p s f can be used
for the footings bearing directly on the compacted Aa
basalt clinkers formation or compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 976 00 P 11
Tor the proposed restaurant structure higher bearing
pressure up to 5 000 p s f could be used if the footings
extend down to a depth of 3 feet and bear on the underlying
hard weathered basalt
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
possible cavities or lava tubes in the underlying rock
formation we recommend major column footing excavation
be probed with an air drill in the proposed restaurant
structure location The probe holes should have a minimum
diameter of about 2 inches and should extend below the
bottom of the footing for a depth of about 3 feet The
probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the
hole using low pressure masonry grout If a cavity is
suspected below a footing additional probe holes should
be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the cavity
We recommend that footing excavations cavity probing and
grouting operations be monitored by the soils engineer
to see that their recommendations are carried out properly
and to locate any potential voids near the footing excavations
The allowable bearing values may be increased up to one third
for temporary loading conditions such as wind and seismic
loads

The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and
be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade and on the above recommended Aa basalt or compacted
fill
Pootings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trench
Footing settlements are anticipated to be minimal less than
4 inch provided our recommendations are followed during
the design and foundation construction
Surface water should be diverted away from building founda
tions
Excavations should be protected from surface runoff which
may enter the excavations Loose soils or mud should be
removed from the bottom of all footing excavations prior
to concrete placement
Slab On Grade
All slab on grade subgrade should be prepared per above
site preparation recommendations All slab subgrade should
be compacted to obtain 90 maximum dry density established
per ASTM D 1557 70 specifications

In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be un
desirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are
to be used a minimum four inches of free draining granular
gravel type material should be placed to act as a capillary
break and provide uniform slab support If moisture sensitive
flooring material is used a plastic membrane could be used
over the granular material to act as a vapor barrier The
membrane should be covered by one inch of sand to protect it
during construction and to aid in curing of concrete

The subgrade should be shaped to drain toward the drainage
well locations Weepholes should be provided at the sides of
the drain box to relieve water seepage at the subgrade level
Cesspools
Based on the Aa clinker material encountered at the proposed
cesspool locations it is our opinion that medium to high
permeability could be anticipated
Percolation tests were not performed during our field test
pit exploration since it is not included in our scope of work
for this project However the State Department of Health
inspector did come down to the site to observe the exposed
test pits located near the proposed cesspool locations
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and speci
fications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading

tion of the site grubbing removal of debris and rubble from
existing fill material prior to re use filling fill place

It is also recommended that the footing excavations and
cavity probing be observed by a soils engineer during con

FIELD EXPLORATION

excavating seventeen 17 test pits to depths of about 3 0 to

by visual and textural examination in the field by our engi
neers who continuously monitored the excavation operation

testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification SyS
tem A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the
Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 through A 17
In the test pits disturbed bulk soil samples were ob
tained by hand at various depths for laboratory testing
