We understand that it is planned to develop a high rise residential condominium
building consisting of 26 stories over a five level base parking structure 31 stories total
The new high rise building tower will have a footprint of approximately 76 by 100 feet
superimposed over a five level base parking structure with plan dimensions of about
160 feet by 120 feet In general we understand that the high rise building will
accommodate about 100 residential units
A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted at the project site between
October 15 and A report of our findings and recommendations was
issued on May 28 2003 Based on our geotechnical engineering exploration and the
design concept we recommended the use of a pile foundation system to support high
structural loads of the proposed high rise building and parking structure The pile
foundation system for support of the high rise building and parking structure consisted of
16 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable design load
capacity of 250 kips 125 tons per pile in compression
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
driven pile foundation installation for the high rise building structure only The general
contractor for the project is Swinerton Pacific Joint Venture and the pile installation
subcontractor is Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 21 piles including one instrumented
load test pile at various locations across the site was conducted from
through The purpose of the test pile program was to establish the
driveability of the piles given the subsurface conditions at the project site the selected
driving equipment and methods of pile installation In addition the test pile program also
provided information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing strata
across the project site which was used to estimate the length of the production piles
A Junttan HHS9 hydraulic impact hammer was used to drive the 16 inch
octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles test piles The Junttan HHS9 hydraulic
impact hammer has a maximum rated energy of 79 2 kip feet Based on our field
observations the pile hammer generally was delivering driving energies between
of the pile driving operations

embedment strain gages for load testing purposes The location of the instrumented load
test pile was selected by our office based on the 20 test piles driven The instrumented
load test pile was driven on A static pile load test was conducted on the

Production Pile Driving
Based on the test pile driving program and the static pile load test we
recommended production piles of about 130 and 140 feet in length In addition we
recommended that the production pile locations be predrilled to a depth of about 50 feet
below the existing ground surface to clear obstructions and to break down the medium
hard coral ledge that may be present at the project site prior to pile installation During the
production pile installation some of the production pile locations were predrilled to depths
of about 60 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate installation of the piles
because hard materials appeared to extend below 50 feet in depth Based on our
evaluation we believe that piles with predrilled holes deeper than 50 feet but not more
than 60 feet should not affect the load bearing capacity of the piles
The production piles for the project were driven with the same hammer
Junttan HHS9 hydraulic impact hammer used to drive the test piles A total of
200 production piles excluding the 21 test piles consisting of 16 inch octagonal
precast pre stressed concrete piles were driven between and March 19
2004 for the project Out of the 221 total piles four piles were additional piles driven to
replace and or supplement the driven piles due to problems with the initially driven pile
