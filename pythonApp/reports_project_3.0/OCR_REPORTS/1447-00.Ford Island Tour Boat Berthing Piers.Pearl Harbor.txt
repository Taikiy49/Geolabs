INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation performed for the proposed berthing piers on Ford Island
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 00 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent data such as shear strength
and classification for the foundation design of the proposed structure
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated May 1 1984 included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of two 2 borings laboratory soil
testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data
and report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of the report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is our understanding that two 7 feet wide by 80 feet long piers
will be constructed to provide berthing facilities for four Arizona
Memorial tour boats The piers will consist of concrete piles and pile
caps with timber beams and wood decking Also included is a fender
system consisting of timber piles and timber walers and chocks and
planking with rubber dock fenders
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the southeastern part of Ford
Island Oahu Hawaii East of the site is the existing Pier S 375 with
four 50 foot long piers and to the northeast is Building 442 Located
along the shore is a 10 foot wide concrete landing platform A small
crane frame believed to be used for loading and unloading equipment was
found at the western end of the platform

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 61 5 at B 1 and 105 feet at B 2 below the
landing platform and water surface respectively Boring 1 was drilled
onshore over the existing concrete platform while Boring 2 was drilled 78
feet offshore on board a watercraft Approximate locations of these
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil Conditions
The two borings encountered Significantly different subsoil
conditions
In Boring 1 the mudline was encountered at 8 5 feet below the
concrete platform Loose to medium dense alluvial deposit consisting
mainly of gray clayey sand with some coral gravel was then encountered
down to a depth of 40 feet Below the alluvial deposit lies the stiff to
very stiff silty clay which extends to the full depth of this boring
In Boring 2 however the mudline was not encountered until
elevation 36 5 feet MLLW was reached The soft to very soft alluvial
deposit consisting mainly of loose clayey sand and some coral gravel was
encountered down to elevation 80 feet Medium dense silty coral sand
then extended to elevation 90 feet where the material became more
gravelly Stiff clayey silt was encountered at elevation 98 feet and
extended to the end of the boring at 105 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings indicate that the subsoil condition varies by a
tremendous amount over the proposed pier length The soil conditions
encountered in our borings are also very different from previous borings
drilled for the nearby existing tour boat piers It appears that the
proposed piers may be situated over an old stream or drainageway Also
a steep average sea floor gradient of H V was encountered
Therefore those piles which are installed close to the seaward end of
the proposed pier will have a large unsupported length Furthermore
Boring 2 disclosed a very poor ground condition which consisted of a
thick layer of soft and weak material below the mudline
Due to the unfavorable soil conditions encountered we believe that
the original design concept will need to be modified A few options and
recommendations are discussed under OPTIONS TO ORIGINAL DESIGN
Foundation Analysis
It is our understanding that the proposed pier will experience both
lateral and vertical loadings with magnitude of 2 25 and 17 tons
respectively on 2 pile bent configuration
Lateral Pile Capacity
An analysis of the laterally loaded pile using the COM622 computer
program was performed This program utilized finite difference
method of analysis where non linear stress strain relationship of
soil can be used Based on the soil parameters developed and the
laboratory stress strain curves representative p y curves were
developed for each major soil category for input into the program
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 00 Page 5
Since the lateral soil modulus varies with the deflection
experienced the computerized analysis first assumes a modulus
value then calculates the pile deflection based on the assumed
modulus The calculated deflection is then used to determine the
modulus from the p y curves The calculated modulus and the
assumed modulus are then compared The procedure is repeated until
the new assumed and calculated moduli are essentially the same
The ultimate lateral soil bearing values are also presented to aid
in determining the limiting conditions in lateral resistance The
results indicate that with the use of inch octagonal precast
concrete piles and under the subsoil condition as indicated by
Boring 2 lateral deflection at the top of the pile is in excess of
20 inches when a lateral load of 2 25 tons is applied to the bent
Under subsoil condition as indicated by Boring 1 the lateral
deflection is about 1 inch The above analysis assumes a free head
pile condition It is our opinion that the free head condition is
appropriate for the seaward end of the pier However the
structural rigidity of the pier closer to shore May produce some
fixity on the pile head and thereby reduce the calculated lateral
deflections

modulus values for the design loads are presented below

Vertical Pile Capacity
Piles penetrating 110 feet from the water surface into soil
conditions indicated by Boring 2 may develop an allowable vertical
capacity of 17 tons per pile For the soil conditions indicated by
Boring 1 similar vertical capacity may be developed when they are
driven to elevation 60 feet
Options to Original Design
It appears that the original design concept of two vertical piles
per bent cannot provide sufficient lateral resistance against the design
lateral forces A few options considered are presented below
Option 1 Batter Piles
Hm am te came os ome me cae cam ae Some me See
Batter piles may be utilized in the Boring 1 area to provide increased
lateral resistance However due to the long piles required in the
Boring 2 area the effectiveness of batter piles may be limited
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 00 Page 7
Option 2 Use of More Piles
The use of more piles per bent will reduce the lateral load acting
upon each individual pile Our analysis shows that under the
subsoil condition as indicated in Boring 2 the deflection can be
reduced significantly when the lateral load is reduced to 1 kip per
pile
Option 3 Use of Larger Diameter Piles
The lateral resistance of a pile increases with the pile diameter
Analysis shows that with the use of 54 inch diameter cylindrical
pipe pile the lateral deflection at the top of the pile under the
design load and subsoil condition indicated by Boring 2 can be
reduced to a level of less than 1 inch However the use of large
piles are not economically practical for the project
Option 4 Shorter Piers
Shorter piers like Piers S 375 and S 376 had been built We
believe their constructions were possible mainly because of the
competent subsoil condition that they encountered
Our borings indicate that the subsoil conditions improve as we move
closer to the shore Therefore it may be advantageous to utilize
shorter piers to avoid the adverse condition as experienced in the
Boring 2 area However the practicality of this option depends on
the actual variation of the soil profile in particular the depth
of soft soil along the pier location Hence in order to further
evaluate this option additional borings probings would be required
at the site to establish a more detailed soil profile

We believe that the options mentioned above or some combinations of
the options may still be able to provide an adequate engineering solution
to the proposed project Therefore we would be happy to provide you
with further consultations if decisions regarding the choice of these
options is made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
