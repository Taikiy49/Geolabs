Four shallow test borings were drilled at the locations shown

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In the vicinity of Building C the existing ground surface varies
between elevation 73 feet and elevation 77 feet with the pro
posed finished floor at elevation 79 Fills of 2 to 6 feet will
be required within the building area In the vicinity of
Building D where the existing ground surface varies from eleva
tion 63 to approximately elevation 73 fill thicknesses of 6 to
16 feet will be required to obtain the proposed finished floor
levels
The proposed fill thicknesses will create significant loads on
the underlying materials which in turn will consolidate due to
these new loadings The differences in the fill thickness
between Buildings C D can result in large differential settle
ments It is therefore recommended that the maximum fill in

the vicinity of the buildings be limited to approximately 8
feet utilizing retaining walls and column construction where
necessary to obtain the desired floor levels Implementation
of this recommendation necessitate the use of structural floor
slabs in the eastern end of Building D
Site Preparation and Earthwork
The onsite soil can be utilized as fill providing that they are
properly compacted Fill beneath floor slab should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum density established by ASTM
test designation D1557 70 The fill placement should be moni
tored by a soils engineer to assure that adequate compaction
has been achieved
The existing top soil and deleterious material should be removed
from the areas to receive fill and the existing surface scari
fied and compacted to a minimum of 90 of a maximum density per
The existing well and septic tank located in
the vicinity of Building D should be undercut a minimum of three
feet below foundation level and backfilled with granular material
in accordance with the applicable health department standards
Concrete rubble or other essentially granular materials may be
utilized in the low lying areas as fill provided the material

is placed outside of the building area in thin lifts not exceed

Our field observations indicate that the onsite soils are
presently above optimum moisture content and must be dryed prior
to compaction Frequent rains will delay the drying operations
and possibly result in delays in the construction of these
buildings Selected granular material can be utilized within
the building area in lieu of the onsite soils This material
should be essentially granular and cohesionless and should not
exceed three inches in size The select material should be com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum density which can be
more readily accomplished considering the high rainfall condi
tions of the construction area This fill placement should also
be monitored by a soils engineer to assure adequate compaction
The 111 placement should be initiated promptly in the vicinity
of Building D so that the maximum advantage can be realized by

Foundation Design
The foundations in the vicinity of Building C should extend
through the surface fill and be founded upon undisturbed soil
where an allowable bearing value of 3500 psf maybe utilized for
both spread and line foundations
This value may also be used for foundations within Building D
which bear upon the natural soils Foundations bearing upon
engineering control fill may be designed for contact bearing
pressures of 2000 psf The total settlements under these load
ings is estimated not to exceed 1 inch total with differential
settlements estimated at i inch All footings under the
building should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
finished adjacent grade
Lateral Earth Pressures
Due to the slightly expansive nature of the existing on site
soils it is recommended that the retaining walls within the
proposed building area be designed for lateral pressures equiva
lent to an equivalent fluid pressure of 30 pounds per cubic foot
This pressure may also be utilized where granular backfill has
been employed providing the material is not overcompacted

Floor Slabs
Slab on grade construction can be utilized for the floor slabs
over the fill areas providing that additional reinforcing is
included to span localized areas of differential settlement
It is our understanding that a five inch reinforced concrete
slab will be utilized in all areas where the fill exceeds three
feet in thickness
Construction Inspection
To assure that adequate compaction is consistently achieved
and that the foundations have reached competent bearing material
it is recommended that the earth placement and foundation con
struction be inspected by a soils technician under the direction
of a registered professional soils engineer The scope of the
proposed construction inspections and the estimated costs were
included as Phase II of our proposal of May 20 1974

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
4 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the
borings ranged from 5 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan and Boring Locations Plate 1 included in the text of this
report The soils were classified by visual and textural exami
nations in the field by our geologist who continuously supervised
the drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphical representation of the soils encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 4 Soil samples
were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D diameter or a
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
