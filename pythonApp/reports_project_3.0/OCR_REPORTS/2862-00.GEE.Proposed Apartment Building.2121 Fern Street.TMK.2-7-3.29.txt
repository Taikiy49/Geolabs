INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed three 3 story apartment building at 2121 Fern Street in the
Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates
or for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing two 2 single story houses and construct
a new three 3 story apartment building at the project site It is our understanding that
concrete columns with CMU walls and concrete floor construction will be utilized for the
proposed building On grade parking with concrete pavement is also anticipated
Based on the structural loading information provided to us by Michael K
Kasamoto the Consulting Structural Engineer on the estimated maximum
column load will probably be approximately two hundred 200 kips

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at 2121 Fern Street in the Moiliili District of
Oahu Hawaii Presently the site is occupied by two 2 single story residences and an
enclosed garage
The project site is generally level and measures approximately 120 by 50 feet in
overall plan dimensions It is separated from other residential units to the east by a 6 foot
high CMU wall toward the front and by a 4 foot high chain link fence along the rear
section A 4 inch wide concrete header is used to separate the subject lot from the
adjacent property to the west The lot is also bounded by a 15 inch high CMU wall at the
réar
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 17 and 25 feet below the existing ground surface A fourth boring was
advanced by continuous penetration without sampling to a maximum depth of 17 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a very thin surficial layer of fill material
approximately one 1 to two 2 feet thick consisting of medium stiff clayey silt This was
immediately underlain by loose compressible layers of clayey sand and coral gravel with
silty sand down to approximately nine 9 feet below the existing ground surface followed

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the exploration results and the anticipated maximum column loads of
approximately two hundred 200 kips we believe that the upper thin fill layer and the
underlying compressible deposit cannot provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed 3 story concrete building Unacceptably large footing settlements up to several
inches may occur under the new building loads
Surcharging of the site to accelerate the anticipated ground settlement is
considered to be not feasible due to the proximity of neighboring structures There is a
very real probability that the area of induced settlement would extend to encompass these
structures and cause structural distress
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2862 00 Page 6
Moreover the high groundwater table and porous nature of the sandy subsoil
conditions encountered may pose a dewatering problems during excavation required for
construction of even shallow spread footings
Finally attempts to preclude the problems of settlement associated with shallow
spread footings by deeper footings would entail exorbitantly expensive shoring and
dewatering to assure the stability of the requisite excavations and to protect the
neighboring structures during construction
Pile Foundations
In consideration of the infeasibility of the use of spread footings as discussed
above and of the anticipated high column loads we recommend that short pile
foundations ending in the underlying coral formation be used to support the proposed
building
Based on the data obtained from our field exploration and laboratory testing
foundations of piles driven through the upper loose deposits of clayey sand and silty sand
occurring to depths of approximately 16 feet are recommended to support the proposed
apartment building It is anticipated that properly designed and constructed pile
foundations bearing in the underlying hard coral formation can develop sufficient support
for the proposed 3 story structure
Pile Capacities
Twelve inch square precast prestressed concrete piles embedded in the coral
formation with an allowable vertical pile capacity of 40 tons per pile may be used
The piles may develop the allowable vertical pile capacity mainly by end bearing
GEOLASS HAWAI
W O 2862 00 Page 7
on the coraline bearing stratum and partially by frictional resistance of the pile
shafts embedded in the surrounding soils A minimum pile spacing of three 3
feet on centers is recommended
An allowable lateral pile capacity of up to five 5 kips per pile and an allowable
uplift capacity of ten 10 kips per pile may be used in the design
Pile Lengths
Due to the erratic nature of coral formations in general it may be anticipated that
some piles may penetrate or punch through less competent portions of the coral
layer without meeting the driving criteria to indicate that the recommended pile
capacity of 40 tons has been attained It is our opinion that if the piles punch
through the upper portion of the coral layer they should be spliced and driven until
the driving criteria is satisfied
For the initial design and construction cost estimating purposes an average
pile length of about 30 feet may be used
The actual production pile lengths should be determined after completion of a test
pile program
Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of about 24 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be performed at
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2862 00 Page 8
a reduced energy level to minimize the development of tensile stresses within the
piles which could cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose sandy soil the
hammer stroke should be carefully controlled with reduced energy levels so
that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach three 3 blows per inch for the last three inches or
when aggregated blow counts reach 40 blows for the last foot
For piles that punch through the upper coral layer without meeting the
above driving criteria the piles should be spliced and the pile driving
continued until the driving criteria is meet
Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to satisfy the following objectives
1
To examine the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2862 00 Page 9
2 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths and to further
verify delineate potential areas requiring different production pile lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program should
comprise a minimum of ten 10 percent of the total number of piles as test piles for the
project Test piles that meet the specified driving criteria may be incorporated into the
actual foundation system on approval by the engineer
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical engineer
should be consulted for selection of the test pile locations The initial test piles should be
cast ten 10 feet longer than the 30 foot estimated pile length or to about forty 40 feet
long Also the test pile program should provide flexibility by allowing for addition or
relocation of the test piles as may be deemed to be necessary by the project geotechnical
engineer to suit the conditions encountered during the test pile driving
A number of splice cans should be made available at the site during the test pile
driving program Splice cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least five 5 feet long
A minimum of one 1 pile load test should be included in the test pile program
The selected pile should be load tested to twice the allowable load capacity in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for
production pile driving The test pile program should be conducted under close and
GECGLABS HAWAI
W O 2862 00 Page 10
continuous field observation by the project geotechnical engineer who will determine when
pile driving may be stopped and provide guidance should revised or additional
procedures be required due to unforeseen driving conditions
During the test pile driving period the geotechnical engineer may select to redrive
retap certain piles to evaluate the soil set up freeze effect on pile capacity
The geotechnical engineer should be consulted to evaluate the final pile bearing
capacity and production pile lengths based on the results of the test pile driving and the
pile load test
Perimeter Wails
A new six foot high CMU wall is anticipated along the perimeter of the project site
It is our opinion that continuous footings may be used to support the proposed perimeter
wall provided that the bottom of the footing excavations is adequately compacted and the
footing embedment is limited to a depth of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent finish
grade
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 1 000 p s f may be used for the design of
the wall footing foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils
Site Grading
The weight of new fills will induce ground settlement due to compression of the
upper loose subsoils Therefore any site filling that may be required should be limited
to a maximum height of less than one foot above the existing ground surface including
the thickness of pavement and base course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2862 00 Page 11
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
We recommend that the areas below pavements slabs on grade or embankments
should be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be removed and replaced with compacted select non expansive
material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of select
non expansive granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than
20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the building
foundations

concrete pavement is planned for on grade parking at the ground floor level

Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials such as No 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel should be used
for the trench backfill up to about one 1 foot above the pipes to reduce the possibility
of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from the level one 1 foot
above the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select
granular material and placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future
ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be shaped to shed water away
from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2862 00 Page 14
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or slab footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bidding This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of
preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been determined by taping from features shown on the Topographic Survey Worksheet
dated prepared by Engineers Surveyors Hawaii Inc Elevations of the
field borings were estimated by interpolation from the contours shown on the same map
The physical locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the time given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
