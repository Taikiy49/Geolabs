Laboratory Test Results
Makakilo Quarry Basalt Select Fill
In accordance with your request we have performed laboratory tests on the
Makakilo Quarry basalt select fill material Laboratory test results including California
Bearing Ratio CBR Sieve Analysis Modified Proctor tests are summarized on
Plate 1
Based on our laboratory test results the material tested contains no particles
greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension and the amount of material passing the
No 200 sieve is approximately 6 percent In addition using the portion of the sample
passing the 2 inch sieve our laboratory CBR test indicate a CBR value of 70 when
compacted to 90 percent relative compaction Swelling of the CBR sample was not
noted
Based on the laboratory test results it is our opinion that the Makakilo Quarry
select fill material tested meets the requirements of select borrow material specified in
Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction City and
County of Honolulu provided that the sample tested is representative of the stockpile
materials

STATEMENT OF WORK AND SCHEDULE
GENERAL DESCRIPTION
The SUBCONTRACTOR will provide all labor materials not specified as being
supplied by the CONTRACTOR and equipment necessary to bore into and
sample soil and to abandon boreholes at and at the
Waikakalaua Fuel Storage Annex adjacent to Wheeler Army Airfield Hawaii
The SUBCONTRACTOR will provide all supplies labor and equipment
necessary for decontamination All personnel from the SUBCONTRACTOR will
be experienced in the performance of the work described in this exhibit
DRILLING SOIL SAMPLING AND BOREHOLE
ABANDONMENT
A 2 1 Drilling and Soil Sampling
A total of nine soil boreholes will be drilled during this effort Drilling
will be performed at three sites designated as Area H Area K and Site
2 Areas H and K are located at Hickam AFB and Site 2 is located at
the Waikakalaua Fuel Storage Annex Three boreholes will be drilled at
each site as shown on Figures A 1 A 2 and A 3
The boreholes at Area H Area K and Site 2 will be advanced using
auger drilling methods At Area H the boreholes will be drilled to a
depth of 18 feet below ground surface At Area K borehole depths will
range from 6 to 8 feet below ground surface At Site 2 borehole depths
will range from 40 to 60 feet below ground surface Boreholes shail be
drilled with a 7 inch outside diameter hollow stem auger Soil samples
for laboratory analysis will be collected at the total depth of each
borehole using a split spoon sampler An additional soil sample will be
collected at 40 feet below ground surface from borehole HI
Figure A 3 The SUBCONTRACTOR shall provide a minimum of
two steel 2 inch inside diameter split spoon samplers The split spoon
samplers shall be capable of accepting standard 2 inch diameter brass
liners which will be supplied by the SUBCONTRACTOR along with a
supply of compatible plastic end caps Brass liners that are damaged or
used to collect laboratory samples will become the property of the
CONTRACTOR Undamaged liners that are not sent to the laboratory
will remain the property of the SUBCONTRACTOR Augers and other
heavy drilling equipment will be decontaminated at areas specified by the
CONTRACTOR

A 3 EQUIPMENT DECONTAMINATION
Drilling Soil Sampling and Monitoring Well Construction
Prior to during and after the drilling of soil borings and installation of wells
care shall be exercised to prevent cross contamination between locations Before
beginning drilling operations the drilling rig augers and drilling rods shall be
cleaned using high pressure steam All well casing screen and downhole well
construction tools shall be decontaminated in the same manner
Sampling equipment consisting of split spoon samplers mixing bowls and hand
tools shall be decontaminated between sampling events Decontamination
procedures for sampling equipment are as follows
Rinse with high pressure steam
Wash with a non phosphate biodegradable detergent and stiff brush
Rinse with high pressure water
Rinse with distilled water
Air dry
Wrap with plastic sheeting or aluminum foil
A decontamination area will be designated by the CONTRACTOR
The SUBCONTRACTOR shall be required to provide all equipment and supplies
necessary for decontamination Steam cleaning and washing equipment shall be
