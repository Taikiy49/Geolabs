SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Tracker System project located on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed project
These recommendations are intended for the design of concrete slab and site
preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a Tracker System for Power Light Solar
Electrical System The Tracker System will consist of the installation of solar panels and
the construction of a concrete pad with a canopy for the equipment
Based on our experience in the project vicinity we anticipate the subsurface
conditions may be underlain by residual soils overlying saprolite at relatively shallow
depths
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a soil and or rock data set to formulate geotechnical
recommendations for the design of foundations and site preparation for the proposed
facility In order to accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of
the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a Case 580 backhoe and an operator to
the project site and back by the owner
2 Excavation of three test pits to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is located near the intersection of Miki Road and Kaupili Road
within the Maui County Power Plant on the Island of Lanai Hawaii At the time of our
field exploration the property was vacant Based on our field observations the project
site is generally overlain by reddish brown clayey silt materials
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of the excavation of three test pits designated as
Test Pit Nos 1 through 3 extending to a depth of about 5 below the existing ground
surface The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our test pits generally encountered residual soils consisting of very
stiff clayey silts on the order of about 3 to 4 5 feet thick The residual soils were
underlain by hard saprolite consisting of clayey silts with sand and gravel The saprolite
extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
Groundwater was not observed in the test pits at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels can vary significantly depending on rainfall temperature
surface runoff groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered from our field exploration are
presented in Appendix A
END OF SITE CHARACTERIZATION

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered a layer of residual soils consisting of
very stiff clayey silts on the order of about 3 to 4 5 feet thick Below the layer of residual
soils hard clayey silts with sand and gravel generally were encountered and extended
to the maximum depth explored of approximately 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration
Based on the subsurface materials encountered we recommend supporting the
new equipment on a concrete mat bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new
compacted fills In order to achieve uniform support for the concrete mat we
recommend providing a minimum of 6 inches of aggregate subbase materials below the
slab area In addition we recommend properly preparing the subgrade soils prior to fill
placement
We envision that the new construction will involve minor cut and fill operation
Detailed discussions and recommendations pertaining to these items are further
presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Concrete Slab
In general we believe that the new equipment may be supported on a concrete
mat bearing on the recompacted on site soils and or new compacted fills placed to
achieve the design finished grades at the site Based on the generally stiff subsurface
conditions we believe that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the
recompacted on site soils and or new compacted fills needed to achieve the finished
grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Based on the existing site condition we envision that the concrete mat may be
underlain by the near surface clayey soils as encountered in the test pits We
recommend properly preparing the subgrade soils prior to fill placement Soft and or

3 2 Uplift Load Resistance of Pile Foundation
Based on the plans provided a short pier foundation may be used to support the
canopy for the equipment We understand that the standard size of the pier is 18 inches in
diameter and 7 feet deep Based on the information we believe that the uplift load governs
the pier foundation design
In general uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead weight of the
drilled shaft and by shear along the pile surface and the adjacent soils
Based on the subsurface conditions we believe that an uplift load capacity of
25 kips may be achieved for the pier diameter of 18 inches and a pile length of 7 feet The
uplift load capacity provided includes the weight of the pier

3 3 Site Grading
Based on the existing topography we anticipate that site grading work for the
proposed construction will consist of minor cuts and fills The following grading items
are addressed in the subsequent subsections

Fill Materials
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
It should be noted that moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey
subgrade soils and new fill materials are critical elements of the earthwork Therefore a
Geolabs representative should observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether
undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification process
and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Soft
and or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed off site and or used in
landscape areas where appropriate
The exposed subgrade then should be over excavated where necessary to
provide the 6 inch capping layer subbase below the concrete slab The bottom of

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The aggregate subbase materials recommended below the concrete slab should
be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts of
about 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of the earthwork Geolabs should conduct observations and soil
density tests during the grading operations to assist the contractor in obtaining the
required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content on the subgrade
soils and each fill lift Where compaction is less than required additional
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment in moisture content as
necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture
content requirement of the general fills and subgrades at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture is an important requirement considering the expansive nature of
the clayey soils
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new concrete slab should be sloped to shed
water away from the slab and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the slab should also be avoided
