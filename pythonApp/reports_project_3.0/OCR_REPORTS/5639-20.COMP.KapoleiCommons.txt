Dear Mr. Pang:
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations, foundation excavation, pavement construction, and
utility installation for the Kapolei Commons project from December 10, 2007 through
August 27, 2009 in accordance with our fee proposal dated September 24, 2007.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Kapolei area within the District of Ewa on the Island of
Oahu, Hawaii. It is approximately bounded by the Interstate Route H-1 Highway to the
north, Kalaeloa Boulevard to the east, Kapolei Parkway Extension (future) and the
existing Honolulu Advertiser Production and Distribution Facility along the south, and
open land to the west.
The project involved the development of the site into a commercial retail center
providing approximately 652,900 gross square feet of retail/commercial space. The new
buildings will generally consist of one and two-story structures. In addition, about
3,000 new parking spaces will be provided. New driveways and underground utility lines
will also be constructed. Site grading work for the construction generally consisted of cuts
and fills less than about 5 feet thick relative to the existing grades. The layout of the
commercial retail center development is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1.

excavation, pavement construction, and utility installation. The site grading consisted of
clearing and grubbing, footing excavation, subgrade preparation, placement and

Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the exposed subgrades were
proof-rolled with a 10-ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in
detecting and collapsing near-surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage
cracks. The exposed subgrades were proof-rolled without moisture-conditioning the
subgrade soils (at the pre-existing moisture contents). Subsequent to the proof-rolling
operations, the subgrades were scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches,
moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content, and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.
The prepared subgrades were then over-excavated (where necessary) to
approximately 18 inches below the building slabs (below the cushion fill layer), sidewalk
slabs, and pavement sections to provide the recommended 18-inch thick non-expansive
select granular fill layer. The over-excavated subgrades were moisture-conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent
of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
(Modified Proctor). Compaction requirement for subgrades under pavement areas was
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.
During placement of the non-expansive select granular fill layer, the fill materials
were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture content,
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor). The base course and aggregate
subbase under the AC pavement were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to
above the optimum moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor).
During the foundation excavation and preparation, the excavated footing bottom was
generally recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.
New utility connections were installed to serve the new development. In general, a
bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No. 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping
and manholes for uniform support. Free-draining granular materials consisting of
No. 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill. The trench backfill was placed in 8-inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction. Where trenches
are below pavement areas, the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.

utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an

failed areas, were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM

3.71 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained.
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading,
foundation excavation, pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
December 18, 2006 and subsequent recommendations.
LIMITATIONS

of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials.
