INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the design of site grading slab on grade and foundation
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at
this site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 67 foot
store and a 24 by 40 foot pump island with two gasoline dispenser
units To supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area
consisting of three 10 000 gallon tanks and tie down concrete dead man
anchors is planned for a 28 by 32 foot area adjacent to the Circle kK
store The convenience facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic
concrete parking lot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of Farrington
Highway immediately east of Times Supermarket in the town of Waipahu
Oahu Hawaii
A Job Iot building and parking area lie along the eastern boundary
of the site
The site measures 230 by 160 feet in plan dimension and is
generally flat with ground elevations varying between 7 2 and 9 5 feet
above Mean Sea Level
An abandoned Texaco Service Station exists at the site and is
presently occupied by a truck rental business

SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Two 2 borings were drilled at the project site to depths of 16 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
The present poor condition of the existing asphaltic concrete
pavement requires that it be broken and disposed of properly off site or
if necessary reused in the fill
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Should unsuitable
materials such as black organic clays and silts vegetation and rubbish
be encountered they should be separated and disposed of properly
off site in order to prevent contamination of the other excavation
material

After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive slab on grade
footing and new pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches below the bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximm density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the
footing or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
select on site soil or with other suitable imported material compacted to
the above mentioned standard
The site should be graded to provide drainage for surface run off
away from all foundations
Store Building and Pump Island
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered soft compressible
soils underlying a 4 5 to 7 5 foot thick crust of dense fill We believe
the upper compacted clayey silt with sand and gravel fill crust can
provide adequate foundation bearing support for the proposed lightly
loaded Circle K Store building and pump island utilizing shallow
spread or continuous wall footings Footing settlement due to the soft
compressible organic clays and silt should be less than one half of an
inch total due to light structural loads
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 18 inches An allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f may
be used The bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
slabs

The bottom of all thickened edge excavations extending down to depth
of 18 inches below outside the finish grade should be wetted and kept
moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the poured
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water
away from the slab perimeter
Underground Storage Tank Area
It is planned to place three approximately 10 000 gallon tanks
underground in a 28 by 32 foot area adjacent to the Circle K Store
There is presently only a 4 5 to 7 5 foot thick fill crust and
total tank and concrete dead anchors per typical Section A G 1 details
provided indicated a total excavation of at least 16 to 17 feet below
the ground surface The tanks and concrete deadman excavation will
extend into the soft material and below the water level Also the
concrete deadman anchors would likely bear on the underlying soft soils
which have low bearing capacity Therefore a stabilization pad may be
required below the tank excavations We recommend the bottom elevation
of the four 4 4 by 4 by 35 foot long concrete deadman anchors be
evaluated in light of the need of sheeting shoring and dewatering
required to provide a stable deep 16 foot plus excavation within the
existing shopping center We believe the braced excavation should be
properly constructed utilizing continuous sheetpiles and extending
sufficiently deep into the underlying soils below the bottom of
excavation to develop required lateral resistance
The stabilization pad may consist of a 15 inch thick layer of 3B
Fine gravel wrapped in geotextile or equivalent
The separating geotextile will retain the gravel and prevent it from
pushing into the soft soil

Utilities
Existing utilities and previously buried cesspools should be located
and verified It may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust
structural details to accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated
condition or buried structure be encountered during construction each
condition should be evaluated by the soils engineer and the design
modified if necessary
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations and pavement design should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
We recommend that all subgrade preparation footing excavations
underground storage tank excavations and excavation backfill be observed
by the soils engineer to check that adequate bearing and compaction have
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of weak organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
