This letter report presents the findings from our site reconnaissance performed in
support of the concept study for the Kahe Wind Generation Facility at Kahe Point The
general site location and vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes our findings and discussions based on our site

findings and discussions presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The site for the proposed wind generation facility is located on the west facing
hillside above Kahe Point in the Makakilo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In the
current concept the components of the wind generators will be trucked to the site and
assembled Therefore a critical component of the project is the feasibility of
construction of the access road
Access to the wind generation facility site will be through the existing Makakilo
Subdivision namely Makakilo Drive Kikaha Drive and Umena Place The alignment
will then follow an existing asphalt paved private road Palehua Road to the site At the
site existing dirt roads will be paved and new roads will be constructed The new
access roads will be paved with asphalt or concrete and have a minimum width of
15 feet with 5 foot stabilized shoulders

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southwest facing hillside above Kahe Point and
Kapolei west of the Makakilo development Based on our field reconnaissance the
project site presently consists of vacant land with a sparse cover of dry vegetation The
ground surface generally slopes down toward the southwest at grades varying from about
seven horizontal to one vertical 7H 1V along the tops of the ridges to 1H 1V along the
drainageways gulches The ground surface elevations range from about 1 000 to
1 600 feet Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A field exploration consisting of drilling and sampling borings was not conducted
for this preliminary study Instead research and review of available soil and boring data
in the vicinity of the project site was conducted in order to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the project site

Based on our field reconnaissance and the available information it appears that
the majority of the project site is covered by a deposit of reddish brown to brownish red
silty clay to clayey silt soils near the existing ground surface Rock outcrops were
noted particularly in the steeper areas near the drainageways and gulches
Groundwater is not anticipated at shallow depths below the existing ground
surface In general the site is at an elevation several hundreds of feet above the
highest basal water table in the area However seepage could be encountered
particularly after heavy rainfall
SOIL CLASSIFICATION
The primary surface soils at the project site are classified in the Soil Survey of
Islands of Molokai and Lanai State of Hawaii issued August 1972
by the U S Department of Agriculture Soil Conservation Service as follows
a Stony steep land rSY consists of a mass of boulders and stones
deposited by water and gravity on side slopes of drainageways
b Mahana silty clay loam McC2 6 to 12 slopes eroded This series
consists of well drained soils on uplands on the Islands of Kauai and
Oahu These soils developed in volcanic ash
Cc Mahana silty clay loam McD2 12 to 20 slopes eroded This series
consists of well drained soils on uplands on the Islands of Kauai and
Oahu These soils developed in volcanic ash
d Helemano silty clay HLMG 30 to 90 slopes This series consists of
well drained soils on alluvial fans and colluvial slopes on the sides of
gulches
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance and review of the available information we
believe that the access road construction is feasible from a geotechnical engineering
point of view
Road Pavement
We understand that flexible pavements will be used for most of the access roads
We anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of semi trailer
trucks to transport the wind generator components Although the vehicle axle loads are
relatively high we anticipate that the number of trips will be low The preliminary
pavement designs are based on the assumption that competent pavement subgrade soil
similar to the reddish brown silty clay residual soil prevalent in the area is encountered

Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following pavement sections be
used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
Rigid Pavement For grades over 12
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and re compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered
during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to
carry the surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the
site during or after construction
Cut and Fill Slope Design
Generally competent soil conditions are anticipated along the road alignment In
general we believe that the planned cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Steeper cut slopes up to 1H 1V may be utilized where rock
formation is encountered
New fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of
the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill
placement recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Additional Exploration
Additional field exploration and geotechnical engineering analysis should be
conducted for the final design of the site grading and structure foundations
