INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of soils and foundation
investigation for above referenced cooperative ownership
housing project The work was performed according to our
proposal dated
W O P 2
The proposed cooperative ownership housing will include
three 5 story apartment buildings approximately 42 by
156 feet in plan dimension The pad finish grade will
be elevation 50 to 54 feet Based on the preliminary
grading plan from 14 feet cut to 5 feet of additional
fill are planned within some of the building areas
Detailed structural loadings are presently not available
but are expected to be moderate and typical for these
type of structures
The soils and foundation recommendations for the adjacent
elderly housing project have been provided in our report
dated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to determine subsurface
soil conditions beneath the proposed apartment buildings
and parking areas We were also to provide recommendations
for foundations pavement design slope stability and
grading recommendations for general site development
The scope of the investigation included ten borings from
8 to 30 feet deep within the proposed building areas and
two 6 foot borings in the vicinity of the proposed parking
Soil sampling standard penetration tests and a reconnaissance
of the cooperative ownership housing portion of the site was
performed under the observation of our engineer

W O P 3
Additional laboratory testing was performed on selected
samples to help provide the basis for our analyses The
boring information from the adjacent elderly housing
development was also utilized in this investigation
This report summaries our conclusions and recommendations
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate l
Site Plan and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Information regarding the field explorations and laboratory
testing are also presented in the Appendices
SITE CONDITIONS
As shown on Site Plan Plate 1 the site was formerly
occupied by residential structures There are existing
shrubs trees driveways there is a large existing
retaining wall loosely backfilled At the time of our
explorations loose fill materials were also encountered
adjacent to the adjoining paved streets Piles of trash
and organic matter have been dumped in several localized
areas throughout the site
A small valley or former stream channel divides the north
east and the southwest portions of the site The surface
elevation of the valley bottom is roughly 50 feet the
surface elevation at the top of the large existing retaining
wall is approximately 68 feet The elevation of the Paiwa
Hapapa Streets intersection is about 70 feet

W O P 4
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Approximately 20 feet of loose backfill materials coral
and basalt cobbles gravel sand and silt were encountered
adjacent to the retaining wall The depths of fill decrease
with increasing distance from the wall generally becoming
three to four feet deep adjacent to the paved streets
The site is generally covered by shallow existing fill
The dominant underlying natural soil consists of stiff silt
The silt is alternately sandy and clayey the clayey silt is
slightly expansive changes in volume with changes in moisture
content Some gravel cobbles and boulders were encountered
generally below 8 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the present borings 30
feet maximum depth explored
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
The loose fill is not recommended for supporting foundations
The underlying natural soils or properly compacted engineered
fills are suitable for providing support for spread type
footings Since the upper fill will generally be excavated
beneath building areas we have provided recommendations for
the compaction of the existing fill and additional fill in
GEOLARS HAWAI
W O P 5
order to provide uniform support for foundations floor
slabs and paving on grade
Grading
The existing wall backfill loose piles of fill all
trash and debris must be removed Existing vegetation
should also be stripped The presence of debris pieces
of bricks coral and sand may be used to establish whether
particular excavated materials are of backfill material
After stripping and excavating the loose fill the ground
surface should be scarified brought to optimum moisture
content and compacted A balanced cut and fill is anti
Cipated The on site natural soils and fill not contaminated
with deleterious materials are suitable for use in required
fill Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the soil engineer prior to importation
Imported fill should be of a granular non expansive nature
After compacting the surface materials required additional
fill soils should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick lifts
and each layer compacted to 90 relative compaction using
ASTM D 1557 70 modified Proctor as a standard All lifts
must be placed horizontally

Shallow excavations and trenches excavated into the natural
soil formations less than three feet deep may be left
open for short term construction periods with minor sloughing
of the vertical walls Permanent natural slopes more than
three feet high should be sloped at 1 horizontal to 1
vertical Permanent fill slopes should be 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical
Foundations
Footings on Natural Soils
Spread footing foundations established in the natural
soil may be used to support the proposed apartment
buildings
At a minimum depth of nine inches into stiff natural
materials the spread footing foundations may be
designed using a net allowable bearing capacity of
4000 pounds per square foot Above value of bearing
capacity is for total of dead plus live loads and
may be increased by 33 when temporary lateral loads
wind and seismic are considered The bottom of
all footings should be at least 24 inches below the
finished floor pavement or lowest exterior grade
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively
small up to three fourths inch

Footings on Partial Cut and Fill Soils
Based on the preliminary grading plan as shown on
Site Plan Plate 1 the buildings located on the
north and east of the site will have partial fills
ranging from 5 to 7 feet deep at some locations
Footings for these two buildings will be deep if
established in the underlying natural soils It
may be more economical to support the footings
partially in cut and partially in engineered fills
An allowable bearing value of 2500 pounds per square
foot may be used for these footings The above
bearing value is for total of dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary
lateral loads are considered
The footings should be at least 12 inches wide
and should be established at least 24 inches
below the finished floor or lowest adjacent grade
Anticipated settlements are expected to be on the
order of 3 4 inch
Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead loads times

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils and foundation
investigation for the above referenced elderly housing project
The work was performed according to our proposal dated April
19 1979 and was performed concurrently with the adjacent
co op ownership development of the Jack Hall Housing 2 project
The proposed elderly housing development will consist of four
3 story apartment buildings approximately 43 by 60 feet and
43 by 120 feet in plan dimension Based on the site plan
Plate 1 the finish pad grade will be elevation 46 feet up
to four feet cut and three feet of fill are planned within
some of the building areas
W O P 2
Detailed structural loadings are presently not available
but are expected to be moderate light and are typical
for these type of structures
Preliminary soils and foundation recommendations have
been provided in our letter dated
PURPOSE SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to determine subsurface
soil conditions beneath the proposed apartment buildings
and parking area We were also to provide recommendations
for foundations pavement design and grading recommendations
for general site development

CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
The use of localized loose fill is not recommended for supporting
foundations or floor slabs The underlying natural soils are
suitable for supporting conventional spread type footings We
have provided recommendations for the excavation of the existing
fill and the degree of compaction required for new fill inorder
to provide adequate floor slab support
Grading
Loose piles of fill all trash and debris should be removed
Existing vegetation should also be stripped The exposed
natural subgrade may then be excavated and or compacted A
balanced cut and fill is anticipated The on site natural
soils and fill not contaminated with deleterious materials
are suitable for use in required fill Any imported fill
material should be tested and approved by the soil engineer
prior to importation Imported fill should be of a non
expansive nature

After proof rolling the natural surface by about eight
passes of a roller required fill soils should be placed
in maximum 9 inch thick lifts and each layer compacted
to 90 relative compaction using ASTM D 1557 70 modified
Proctor as a standard All lifts must be placed horizon
tally
Shallow excavations and trenches excavated into the soil
formations less than three feet deep may be left open
for short term construction periods with minor sloughing
of the vertical walls
Permanent cut slopes more than three feet high should be
sloped at 1 horizontal to 1 vertical Permanent fill
slopes should be 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Foundations
Spread footing foundations established in the natural soils
may be used to support the proposed apartment buildings
The spread footing foundations may be designed using a
net allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per square
foot Above value of bearing capacity is for total of dead
plus live loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary
lateral loads wind and seismic are considered

natural soils the bottom of all footings should be at least
18 inches below the finished floor pavement or lowest exterior
grade
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
up to one half inch
Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead loads times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4 four tenth
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third

In order to protect against moisture infiltration a poly
ethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated within
floor slabs Floor slabs should also be underlain by a
