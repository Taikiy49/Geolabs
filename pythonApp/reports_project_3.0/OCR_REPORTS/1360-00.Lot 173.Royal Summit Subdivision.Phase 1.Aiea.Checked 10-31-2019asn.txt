DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
For a post and beam house construction deep footing embedment is
recommended for posts situated on slope to minimize slope creep and to
gain lateral confinement Such a construction will generally result
in a lower foundation cost on a sloping terrain and that the
individual footings can be adjusted should minor movements occur
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to medium swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 18 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected
from drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete
placement

Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 18 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs including the swimming pool slabs should consist of
non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
The on site soil under the garage and driveway areas should be
over excavated to provide the 18 inch minimum non expansive fill
capping The remainder of fill beneath the non expansive fill cap and
other areas may consist of either off site borrow or the on site soils
in the Royal Summit area that generally have low to moderate swelling
potential
Imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The moisture content of the
on site fill should be kept within 5 percentage points wet of the
optimum during compaction

H V should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing
slope refer to Plate 2 for details
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations

Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage the
swimming pool and its decking It should be noted that the on site
clayey silt soils are moderately expansive and unsuitable to provide
direct slab support Therefore the on site soil should be
over excavated down to a depth of 18 inches below the slab bottom
wetted and then replaced with non expansive imported soils as
previously discussed The over excavation and backfilling should
extend a minimum of one foot beyond the edge of the slabs The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 8 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade and bear on the above select
borrow backfill
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clayey silt
soils are recommended for the lanai slabs if constructed on grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the clayey silt below the capping
fill pad be kept moist
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations
if any

Swimming Pool
For the swimming pool planned at the rear of the residence a lateral
earth pressure of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth
may be used for walls restrained at the top at rest condition with level
backfill

As discussed earlier the swelling potential of the on site soil
increases with decreasing moisture contents Therefore the swimming
pool excavation should be kept moist all the time
The bottom and sides of the swimming pool should be supported on
non expansive imported fill of at least 18 inches thick over the on site
soils as recommended under the SLAB ON GRADE section
For the construction of the swimming pool the excavations should not
fall below a plane 45 degrees down from the closest edge of the house
footings If interference is projected the house foundation should be
deepened
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
