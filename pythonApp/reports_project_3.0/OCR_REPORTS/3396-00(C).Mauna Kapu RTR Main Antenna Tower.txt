INTRODUCTION
This report presenis the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Mauna Kapu Remote Transmitter Receiver RTR Main Antenna
Tower located between Schofield Barracks and Makakilo on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These geotechnical engineering recommendations are intended for the
design of foundations only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed main antenna tower site is located on a ridge
between Schofield Barracks and Makakilo on the westerly side of the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new 140 foot high self supporting tower for the
main antenna on the northeast side of the parking lot opposite the equipment building
door We understand that a specific model for the proposed antenna tower has not
been selected at the time this report was prepared Based on the information provided
we anticipate that the antenna tower will be moderately heavy in weight and will be
subjected to significant uplift forces and moment at the foundation level Minimal site
grading work is anticipated for the project construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed of two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene
epochs Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of Waianae along the
southern rift zone
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events which produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter
and ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented
the pyrociastics into weak to strong rock
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally the ridges are
heterogeneous mixtures of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a
surface layer of saprolite soil which mantles most of the site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The Mauna Kapu Remote Transmitter Receiver RTR site is located within the
Waianae Mountain Range in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
RTR site is bounded to the north and east by the Palehua Military Reservation and to
the west by the ridgeline crest of the Waianae Mountains The ground surface
elevations at the site are on the order of about 2 510 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Access to the site is via a winding paved roadway named Palehua Road
The RTR site is situated on what appears to be a cut area of decomposed basalt
rock and saprolite soil near the crest of the Waianae Mountain ridgeline Based on
spot elevations shown on the site plan the ground surface elevation at the proposed
tower location is estimated to be about 2 507 feet MSL The centerline location of the
tower is situated on a gently sloping oil base coated gravel parking area about 23 feet
to the northeast of the existing concrete building at the site The area surrounding the
RTR site is densely vegetated with tail trees and steep slopes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
On the basis of our geotechnical engineering exploration we believe that the
proposed antenna tower may be supported on drilled pier foundations embedded in the
upper dense silty sands and underlying stiff clayey silt soils The drilled piers would
derive support principally from skin friction between the pier shafts and the surrounding
soils Due to the presence of granular subsoils in the upper 8 feet temporary casing
for the upper 10 feet may be required during the drilled pier construction to reduce the
potential for caving in of the drilled holes The performance of the drilled piers will
depend significantly upon the contractor s method of construction construction
procedures and workmanship The recommendations and general guidelines
presented in this report should be properly followed by the contractor during foundation
construction work Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Drilled Pier Foundations
In order to develop the anticipated vertical and lateral load capacities the
proposed new antenna tower may be supported by a foundation system consisting of
cast in place concrete drilled piers embedded in the upper dense silty sand and
underlying stiff clayey silt deposits The cast in place concrete drilled piers would
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3396 00 C Page 7
derive vertical support principally from skin friction between the pier shafts and the
surrounding soils
_ For the design of the drilled pier foundations an allowable skin friction value of
up to 200 and 800 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the upper dense
silty sand and underlying stiff clayey silt materials respectively The allowable skin
friction value may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces Load bearing contribution from the upper 5 feet of subsoils
below the finish pad grade should be neglected The end bearing componeni of the
drilled piers should also be discounted due to the potential cave in of the upper
granular soils that would preclude obtaining a clean bottom during drilled pier
installation The drilled piers should have a minimum diameter of 18 inches Drilled
piers should have a minimum center to center spacing of three times the diameter to
avoid capacity reduction due to group action
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the face of piers Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth p c f down to a depth of 10 feet below the ground surface Below the depth
of 10 feet the passive resistance may be estimated based on a uniform pressure
distribution equal to 3000 p s f These values are ultimate values therefore a safety
factor should be included by the designer Unless covered by slabs or pavements the
passive resistance in the upper one foot of soil should be neglected The passive
resistance may be assumed to act over two times the diameter of the piers
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead weight of the structure
the weight of the pier and pier cap and by side shear along the shaft surface and the
adjacent soils Contribution of uplift resistance from the upper silty sand layer should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3396 00 C Page 8
be neglected A shaft resistance of 600 p s f may be used for the stiff clayey silts to
resist intermittent uplift forces
Pier settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch We
believe that a significant portion of the settlements is elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied
Construction Considerations
Drilled piers are desirable to the subject project because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled piers will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures In our opinion the
following conditions would have a significant effect on the effectiveness and cost of the
drilled pier foundations
Based on our field exploration the proposed new antenna tower location is
underlain by sandy material in the upper 8 feet To reduce the potential for caving in
of the drilled holes temporary casing for the upper 10 feet may be needed during
construction of the drilled piers Care should be exercised during removal of the
temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking of the concrete pier Therefore
a minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be maintained above the bottom of the
casing during removal of the casing
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling
the pier holes and in placing concrete into the holes

Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is not recommended A lfow shrink
concrete mix with high slump should be used to provide close contact between the pier
shafts and the surrounding soil The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner
to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix
Arepresentative of Geolabs Hawaii should be present to observe the installation

GUIDE SPECIFICATIONS FOR DRILLED PIERS
I LOCATIONS AND DIMENSIONS
Drilled pier foundations shall be constructed at the locations to the lengths and in
accordance with the details and notes shown on the plans As built pier lengths shall
extend to the depths shown on the plans and shall not be deeper by more than 5 feet
without prior written permission from the Geotechnical Engineer Pier diameters shall
be as shown on the plans for each individual pier The diameter shown shall be the
diameter of the drilling bit or bucket
ll ALLOWABLE TOLERANCES ON LOCATION STRAIGHTNESS AND
PLUMBNESS
The center of the top of the pier and reinforcing cage and built as the bottom
of the column cap wall or grade beam shall not deviate from the location shown on
the plans by more than 2 inches in any direction
The drilled pier shall not deviate from a plumb line referenced to the center of
the drilled pier as described above by more than 2 percent of the drilled pier length
from the top to the point of interest
The clear distance between reinforcing steel and the drilled pier hole sidewall
shall not be less than 3 inches
lil EQUIPMENT AND OPERATION
Drilling casing setting of reinforcing cages pumping bailing concrete
placement pulling casing and all other phases of the pier construction operation shall
be performed with equipment and tools capable of meeting the specifications
All loose material shall be removed from the hole prior to placing concrete
Cleaning shall be by machine or hand operated equipment Drilling and placement of
concrete shall be scheduled such that concrete is placed in each pier hole on the same
day in which drilling is completed Holes spaced closer than three pier diameters
center to center shall not be drilled on the same day unless approved by the
Geotechnical Engineer
IV CASING
Casing shall be used where needed to maintain the integrity of the pier hole
during drilling installation of reinforcing steel and placement of concrete

Where casing is used the hole shall not be drilled ahead of the casing any
further than the hole will stand without sloughing At any time sloughing occurs the

Inside casing diameter shall not be less than the required drilled pier diameter

V CONCRETE
Concrete shall comply with all provisions of the project plans At the time of
placement the concrete shall have a slump necessary to produce the specified strength

compressive strength of 3 000 p s i at 28 days as determined by the concrete cylinder
compression tests ASTM C 39 9
VI REINFORCING
The reinforcing cages shall be braced and tied in such a way that the cages are
not deformed or distorted during fabrication placement of the cage in hole and
concrete placement
Any reinforcing cage which is bent collapsed or distorted or which varies from
a straight line by more than 3 inches at any point shall be rejected
Reinforcing cages shall be set in the hole in such a way that they do not rake
the sidewall and cause sloughing Reinforcing cages shall not be spliced in the drilled
pier holes instead they should be fabricated to the full length prior to placement
The reinforcing cage shall be hung from a solid support at the top of the hole
so that it does not bear on the bottom of the hole and hangs free in the hole The
reinforcing cage shall be set at the required location and elevation prior to beginning
placing concrete and shall be held and supported in such a way that it does not move
during concrete placement
Reinforcing dowels which are to be set in the concrete after completion of
placement if required are to be installed immediately following placement of concrete

Vil PLACEMENT OF CONCRETE
f the hole is filled in the dry concrete shall be placed through a hopper and
elephant trunk positioned in the center of the reinforcing cage The elephant trunk

shall be of sufficient size to prevent spilling of concrete outside of the elephant trunk
For placing concrete in a cased hole a minimum head of 5 feet of concrete shall
be maintained in the casing at all times Where staged casing is used the casing shall
be pulled and removed in units in such a way that after pulling one unit of casing there
is at least 5 feet of concrete in the remaining casing until the hole is topped out at the
cut off grade
Unless otherwise noted vibration of the concrete is not required until the
concrete reaches an elevation of approximately 10 feet below the top of the drilled pier
After this elevation is reached the concrete shall be vibrated continuously to the top of
the pier and until good densification of the concrete has been achieved Concrete shall
be added as required to compensate for consolidation and expansion into the hole
The concrete vibrator and the vibration operation shall conform to all requirements for
internal concrete vibrators and vibration of concrete given in the job specifications
The elapsed time from beginning the placement of concrete until completion shall
not exceed 1 hour Placement of concrete shall not commence until sufficient concrete
to complete the pier is on the job site waiting to be placed
Vill DISPOSAL OF SOIL FROM DRILLING
Soil obtained from drilling shall not be used as fill unless otherwise approved by
the Geotechnical Engineer and it shall be disposed of off the site or used in
landscaped areas as appropriate as designated by the Architect or Civil Engineer

IX OBSERVATION
The drilling of holes setting of reinforcing setting and pulling of casing
dewatering placing concrete and any other operation involved in the construction of
the drilled piers must be performed under continuous observation of the Geotechnical
Engineer The contractor shall give notice at least 48 hours in advance of his
performing such work He shall do no such work in the absence of the Geotechnical
Engineer
