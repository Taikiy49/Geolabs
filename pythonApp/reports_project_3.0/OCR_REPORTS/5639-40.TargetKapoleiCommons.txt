PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new Target Store T-2411 is at the north central area of the Kapolei Commons
development in the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The
Kapolei Commons development is approximately bounded by the Interstate Route H-1
Highway to the north, Kalaeloa Boulevard to the east, Kapolei Parkway Extension and the
existing Honolulu Advertiser Production and Distribution Facility to the south, and open
land to the west.
The site for the retail store is approximately rectangular in shape and provides
approximately 159,430 square feet of retail space. In addition, a parking lot for over

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The mass grading work and building pad construction for the Target Store at
Kapolei Commons were performed by Delta Construction Corporation under a separate
project. Jayar Construction, Inc. performed the footing and plumbing excavations,
construction of the elevator pit, loading dock, and truck apron slab during the store building
construction described in this report.
The footing bottoms for the store building, loading dock and elevator pit walls, etc.
were generally compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor). Base course
underneath the building slab and loading dock approach slab were generally compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. Free-draining granular materials
consisting of No. 3B Fine gravel was used for the plumbing trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill. The trench backfill was placed in 8-inch
level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative
compaction. Where trenches are below pavement areas, the upper 3 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction.
During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing.
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2.
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 220 field density tests were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017. Compaction
test results are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.11. These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills. Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
