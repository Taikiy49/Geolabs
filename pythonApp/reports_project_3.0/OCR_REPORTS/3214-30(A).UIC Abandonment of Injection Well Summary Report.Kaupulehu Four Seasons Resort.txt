7 Description of injection well abandonment procedures
Geolabs Hawaii arrived on site at about 0830 and met
with all parties involved to review the injection well abandonment specifications
prepared by the Department of Health Safe Drinking Water Branch for UIC Application
UH 1594A
Cover Removal and Cleaning
At 0930 on January 23rd the site work contractor began
removing the concrete covers from the drywells Work progressed one drywell at a
time by exposing the entire cover from the existing gravel subgrade and lifting it from
the top of the drywell During the cover removal any loose gravels that had fallen into
the well bottom were removed In addition any debris or other material all ready in the
well bottom was removed Geolabs Hawaii maintained a log of events during the
opening and cleaning of the drywells including photographs of the drywells prior to
backfilling
During the removal of the concrete cover of Drywell No 7 the perforated drywell
ring was tilted and a portion of the ring rim was damaged Refer to Photo Nos 18 and
19 showing the orientation of the ring damage and subsequent repair of the rim prior
to grouting
All of the drywells with the exception of and 9 did not require
cleaning Drywell No 7 required some cleaning of concrete debris and gravel derived
from the cover removal event Drywell No 9 had an accumulation of about 1 foot of
excess gravels and cobbles which required removal prior to backfilling
Once cleaned all of the drywell depths were measured again with the covers
removed The drywell depth was measured from the top of the ring to the bottom of
the well The concrete cover thickness measured about 1 8 feet This thickness was
added to the measured drywell depth to correlate with the measured drywell depth of
record All depths were confirmed to match within inches an acceptable margin of
error considering the means of the depth measurements
The rings of Drywell No 11 were removed by the site work contractor during
excavation and removal of a 30 inch diameter drain line intersecting the subject drywell
The open uncased excavation observed at the Drywell No 11 location measured
about 10 feet deep and about 10 to 12 feet in diameter The perforated rings were
removed in order to remove the drain line located at the bottom of the well Geolabs
noting the changed condition of the removed rings made a telephone cail to Mr Norris
Uehara on January 23rd requesting approval to utilize structural fill as backfill in place
of grout for the subject drywell only

Backfilling of the Drywells
The grouting of the drywells commenced at 10 00 on January 24th proceeding
one drywell at a time Geolabs Hawaii maintained a log record of the events during the
backfilling including photographs taken during and after the grouting procedure
A total of 90 cubic yards of the specified grout mix 3 1 sand cement was
delivered to the site by West Hawaii Concrete in 8 loads Refer to Appendix B for
copies of the grout mix design and grout delivery slips Grout backfilling of 12 drywells

The drywells located in proposed cut areas were grouted up to the planned future

The excavated location of Drywell No 11 was cleaned to expose dense in situ
basalt formation Structural fill consisting of well graded 6 inch minus granular
material was placed and compacted in 1 foot lifts extending to the existing ground
