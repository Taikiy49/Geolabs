Project Considerations 
The Disney Aulani project is in the process of installing two new disposal wells.  
In accordance with the Hawaii Department of Health administrative rule for 
Underground Injection Control (UIC), it is required to have a geologist maintain logs for 
this work. 
Logging Procedure and Scope of Work 
Geolabs, Inc. has provided geological logging services for the wells in a manner 
appropriate to the well construction method and in general accordance with good 
professional practice and principles.  The logging of the wells was based on visual and 
textural examination of samples of cuttings collected at about 5-foot intervals during the 
drilling.  The samples were delivered to our laboratory for examination and logging.  The 
procedure was generally as follows: 
Cuttings samples were removed from the sample bags and spread on a 
white absorbent paper (paper towel).  Initial visual observation was 
conducted to determine the origin of the material, e.g., alluvium, basalt 
formation or coralline formation.  Closer examination was made to attempt 
to differentiate specifics about the material, e.g., a`a or pahoehoe form of 
basalt.  If possible, further description of the formation was provided.  A 
verbal description was made for each cuttings sample and the 
descriptions were compiled for longer depth intervals.

Report
The well log is presented as verbal descriptions on the attached Injection Well
Logs, Plates 1.1 through 2.2. In general, the well at the site penetrated basalts of the
Waianae Volcanic Series.
Geolabs, Inc.’ observations of the samples indicated that the basalt formation
generally consisted of alternating layers of aa and pahoehoe basalts as are typically
found in the Waianae flank. These basalts appear to be thickly bedded and may have
ponded when deposited. No unusual geological or hydrological conditions appeared to
be associated with the samples examined.
