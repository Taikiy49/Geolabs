PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation is to explore the subsurface soil
and water level conditions and to provide preliminary design parameters
for the foundation support of the structure particularly to evaluate if
there is an adequate surface crust to support a spread footing type of
foundation system
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two
2 borings to 60 feet below the existing ground surface Also included
were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained engineering analysis
and evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of the
preliminary design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the Diamond Head side of Kalakaua
Avenue between McCully and Pumehana Streets approximately 77 feet north
of the Ala Wai Canal It is flanked by the Makiki Stream to the east and
two 2 story concrete buildings to the north
Presently the site is occupied by a light wood framed structure
being used as the Veterans of Foreign War Meeting Hall and an on grade
parking lot
The area is generally flat and partially paved with asphaltic
concrete

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our drilled borings indicate that about 30 foot thick highly
compressible layers of lagoonal sands and clay deposits exist below the
upper 5 foot surface fill crust at the proposed building site Hence the
main concern will be the tolerable magnitude of total and differential
foundation settlements The foundation system utilized would depend on
the magnitude of the column loads and the ability of the structure to
tolerate the resulting settlements
Detailed discussion and recommendations are presented as follows
Foundation Recommendations
Our borings encountered about 30 foot thick layers of loose and
highly compressible lagoonal deposits underlying a thin surface crust at
the proposed building location
Since the proposed building has not yet been designed details of
the structure and loading are unavailable The main consideration for the
foundation design would be ground settlements caused by the new site
filling and building loads For very light wood framed structures one to
possibly two story high shallow lightly loaded spread footing foundation
may be utilized As the loading increases the settlements would also
increase and therefore for heavier structures a surcharge program or
even pile foundations may be required to reduce the total and differential
foundation settlements

Because of the soft subsoil conditions the structure should be
capable to withstand large settlements which may occur
Our préliminary analysis indicated that for 5 foot square footings
20 feet apart with loading of about 2000 pounds per square foot
calculated settlements are on the order of about 6 inches total and
about 3 inches differential
To reduce the above amount of settlement to tolerable limits light
wood framed structures should be considered to reduce the structural
loading Also the use of individual spread footing may require
special provisions to be incorporated under the column for
periodic adjustment of the structural columns as the footings
settle
Alternatively a surcharge fill program may be used to induce the
anticipated ground settlements prior to the building construction
For a surcharge fill height of about 5 feet the anticipated
surcharge period would be about 3 to 6 months Settlement gauges
should be installed at various appropriate locations to monitor the
actual ground settlements and to determine when the surcharge fill
can be removed
If spread footings are used they should be embedded at least 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Any soft or
expansive soils encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
should be removed to firm soils and backfilled with compacted fill

All footings should be tied together with grade beams to improve the
rigidity of the foundation hence reducing the differential
settlement An allowable bearing value of about 1500 p s f with a
maximum footing size of 5 foot square can be considered
Pile Foundation
We recommend that a pile foundation system be seriously considered
at this site
The piles would develop their load carrying capacity primarily from
frictional resistance between the piles surface and the dense sandy to
gravelly coral materials and some additional end bearing resistance could
be obtained from the dense coral sand located below the pile tip
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles could be used for the
pile capacity recommended The piles should extend through the soft
layers and driven into the dense coral sand encountered fron about 35 feet
below the existing ground surface We estimate the average pile length is
about 45 feet below ground surface elevation
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 40 tons
per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order of less than one half
of an inch are anticipated
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 3 blows per inch
for the last 6 inches and or a minimum total penetration depth of 45 feet
below the existing ground surface
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation

To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth The backfill
against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with
select on site sandy materials compacted to a dry density of at least 90
of the maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
It should be noted that the friction plus end bearing piles could
lose some of their load carrying capacity if the adjacent areas are filled
and thereby causing ground settlements and downdrag loads on the piles
Site Grading
Site filling should be avoided or at least minimized to less than
one foot since the weight of the new fill would initiate long term ground
settlements for spread footing and additional downdrag loading on the
driven piles If site filling is required it should be done as soon as
practicable and pile driving operation should be delayed until the fill
settlement has occurred first
The ground surface below on grade slab should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft pockets
encountered in the initial site preparation should be removed and
backfilled with select backfill material
Backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The upper sandy fill materials may be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

It is recommended that the site grading footing excavation

the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained competent bearing soil has been reached and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation

approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
