PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the eastern portion of the Ewa by Gentry Residential
Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
east of Fort Weaver Road south of Iroquois Point Road and west of Keaunui Drive The
borrow site is within the future park site A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on
Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
backfilling of the borrow pit at the project site
BACKFILLING OF THE BORROW PIT
Geolabs Inc observed the backfilling of the Borrow Pit at Area 19 project from
to At the writing of this report the backfilling of the
borrow pit area to the design finish grades shown the drawing have not been completed We
understand that the remaining backfill for the borrow pit area will be performed at a later time
In addition we understand that the borrow pit area will be fenced and not used

The backfilling work performed consisted of the placement and compaction of fills on
the order of up to about 25 feet thick RHS Lee Inc and Pineridge Farms Inc performed
the grading work During the grading operations samples of the imported materials utilized
as backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 15 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed backfills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the backfilling work for the above project observed and
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance with
the general intent of the recommendations in the following references

2 Report by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Ltd entitled Subsurface
