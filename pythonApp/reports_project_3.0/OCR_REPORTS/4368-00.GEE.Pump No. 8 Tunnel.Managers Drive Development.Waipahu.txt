INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the Pump No 8 Tunnel at the proposed Managers Drive Development located in the
District of Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is located southwest of
Managers Drive southeast of Interstate Route H 1 Highway northwest of the Waipahu
Sugar Mill and northeast of an old cane haul road and Waikele Stream The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
regarding the existing old water transmission underground tunnel resulting from our field
exploration and engineering analyses This report presents our assessment of the risk that
the tunnel may pose to the future development and of the feasibility of sealing the tunnel
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the 40 acre parcel into a single family residential subdivision
with 226 lots and 11 roads It is anticipated that the proposed residences will consist of light
one and two story structures The 11 roads will have an aggregate length of about 6 200
lineal feet Infrastructures including sidewalks and utilities are also planned for the proposed
project
An existing old water transmission underground tunnel extends approximately 400 feet
into the development site from the existing gulch along the southwest boundary The tunnel
invert is believed to be at an elevation of about 8 feet MSL which is about 50 to 100 feet
below the existing ground surface

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
GEOLABS INC
W O 4368 00 Page 4
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau
The lava flows on the plateau have undergone in situ weathering to depths of 50 to
100 feet and are characterized by the red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such
as Waikakalua Kipapa and Kaukonahua are the result of this in situ weathering
This in situ chemical weathering of the lava has formed a mantle of residual soils that
grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and
is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks Spheroidally weathered boulders are
typical in the Schofield Plateau The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

H 1 Highway northwest of the Waipahu Sugar Mill and northeast of an old cane haul road

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the existing old water
transmission tunnel poses minimal risk to the proposed development at the ground surface
This determination is based on the following factors
relatively small size of the tunnel about 6 to 8 feet in height and width
relatively deep invert of tunnel and therefore thick rock layer thickness over
tunnel about 50 to 100 feet below the existing ground surface
generally medium hard to hard consistency of the basalt rock formation above
the tunnel and
relatively light loads from anticipated vehicular traffic and single family houses
The loads from the proposed development should diminish with depth such that the
tunnel should be unaffected by these loads We also believe that in the event of a collapse
of the tunnel based on the thickness of competent basalt rock formation arching over the
tunnel should take effect and therefore distress at the ground surface should be minimal
We believe that sealing the tunnel by grouting may be problematic due to the
a uncertainty of the alignment b depth of the tunnel and c flowing water in the tunnel

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 to depths of about 87 to 115 5 feet
below the existing ground elevation at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers and rock coring equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 and D 3550 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to
drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a
hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of the bit is
mechanically recovered for examination and description The core barrel was advanced
using water as a drilling fluid
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from
a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be shown
on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material in
excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
