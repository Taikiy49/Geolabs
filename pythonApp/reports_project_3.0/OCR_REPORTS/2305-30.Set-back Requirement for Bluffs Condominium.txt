INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the determination of set back requirements for the proposed Bluffs
Condominium project at The general location of the project site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2305 30 Page 2
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A portion of the Bluffs development is situated adjacent to a very steep natural
slope Previously we had recommended that the structures be set back a minimum of
40 feet away from the top of slope Based on the latest building layout provided to us
dated three 3 buildings are situated relatively close to the top of
slope
It is proposed to construct two story condominium units near the existing cliff at
the eastern portion of the Bluffs development It is our understanding that column loads
up to 31 kips are anticipated for the interior columns and up to 11 2 kips for the outer
columns for the columns located near the cliff
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil and rock data set for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the
proposed structures located near the cliff
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of January 16
1992 The scope of work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

to and from the Princeville area

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Princeville area on the northern coast of the island
of Kauai Hawaii The project site is located to the east of the existing Princeville
development area on top of the bluffs overlooking Anini Beach The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site appeared to have been cleared at one time since only small shrubs and
tall grass exists The natural forest growth resumes at the project boundaries where the
slope begins to drop down into the adjacent valley
The site is located on a near level to gently sloping hilltop overlooking a deeply
eroded gulch The slopes surrounding the hilltop vary but are generally two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V and one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V with some steeper
segments all of which are heavily vegetated No scars from recent slope failures or
slides were observed during the time of our exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling six 6 borings
to depths of thirty 33 to one hundred three 103 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a stiff to very stiff residual soil layer at the
surface approximately nine 9 to eleven 11 feet in thickness consisting of reddish
brown to brown ciayey silts underlain by a stiff to very stiff saprolite deposit consisting of
mottled brown and gray clayey silt with fine sand to a depth ranging from 69 to 75 feet
Below the saprolite layer an extremely weathered basalt deposit consisting of medium

drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the borings from 87 2 to 96 1 feet below the
existing ground surface in borings during the time of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by stiff to
very stiff residual and saprolite deposits overlying a medium dense to dense extremely
weathered basalt deposit
The slope stability analysis indicates that the general hillside is stable against a
massive landslide failure However shallow surface debris flows may occur on the
steeper portions of the slope face As is common to hillsides the slopes may experience
downhill surficial shallow slope creep movements on a long term basis
Based on our slope stability analysis of the cross sections of the natural hillsides
obtained from the topographic maps transmitted to us we recommend that drilled pier
foundations be utilized for three 3 of the buildings situated close to the steep portions
of the slope The locations of these three buildings which will be labeled Building A B
and C are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2305 30 Page 6
We recommend that the drilled piers have a minimum diameter of eighteen 18
inches with four 4 inch steel pipes concreted within the holes The piers should be
installed in pairs to allow the rear pier to act as an anchor to the front pier
Based on information provided to us exterior pier loads up to 11 2 kips and interior
pier loads up to 31 kips are anticipated for the columns near the top of the slope The
outer row or exterior piers should be placed at about 14 foot center to center spacing and
should extend to a minimum depth of 30 feet The rear or interior piers should be placed
about 11 feet behind from the front piers and should extend to a minimum depth of 30
feet The front and rear piers should be tied together by grade beams
Based on the boring data estimated total footing settlements under the design
loads are anticipated to be on the order of less than one 1 inch total and one half 4
of an inch differential
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this review

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the pier installation and other
spread footing foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior to the start of

LIMITATIONS
The recommendations submitted in this report are based upon information
obtained from the field borings Variations of conditions between the borings may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from reference points indicated on the grading plan by Richardson Nagy and
Martin dated Elevations of the borings are also approximate having
been estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same plan The physical
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to identify appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 90 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate locations on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Direct Shear tests ASTM Test Method D 3080 72 are used to determine the angle
of internal friction and the coefficient of cohesion in a soil sample These data are used
in various engineering calculations The results of the direct shear tests are presented
on Plates B 1 through B 10
