This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Emergency Replacement of
Paauau Stream Bridge project between July 8 and
The project site is located along Mamalahoa Highway Route 11 at the eastern
boundary of the Town of Pahala in the District of Kau on the Island of Hawaii as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consists of constructing a replacement
bridge of approximately 43 feet in width and 124 feet in length The replacement bridge
abutments and three intermediate piers are supported on a shallow foundation system
The intent of the foundation probing and grouting operation is an attempt to locate and
grout potential subsurface cavities and or voids beneath the bridge abutment and pier
foundations
The bridge abutment and pier foundation locations and the probe hole locations
were surveyed and marked in the field by the general contractor Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company prior to the probing operations The probe holes were spaced at
about 10 foot on center along the abutment and pier foundations A total of 49 scheduled
probe holes were located at the bridge abutment and pier foundation locations The

The probing and grouting work for the replacement bridge was performed prior to
excavation for the bridge abutment and pier foundations The probing work was
performed by Isemoto Contracting Company The probe holes were drilled with two
track mounted drill rigs equipped with air percussion hammers The probe holes drilled
were about 3 inches in diameter and extended to about 22 to 36 feet below the existing
ground surface The probe holes that were drilled generally extend to a depth of at least
10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations A summary of our foundation probe drilling
and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of fill materials underlain by gray and brown basalt formations interbedded with layers of
reddish brown clinker materials After completion of the probe drilling operations the
contractor filled the probe holes with a fine aggregate masonry grout specified in the
project specifications The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by
Jas W Glover Ltd The field probing and grouting records are provided in Appendix A of
this letter report
Additional probe holes were drilled adjacent to the scheduled probe holes that
generally had grout intakes of 0 4 cubic yards or greater Of the 49 scheduled probe
holes five probe holes one probe hole at Abutment No 1 two probe holes at Pier No 1
one probe hole at Pier No 3 and one probe hole at Abutment No 2 had grout intakes of
0 4 cubic yards or greater Therefore twenty additional probe holes were drilled for
Abutment No 1 Pier No 1 Pier No 3 and Abutment No 2 The approximate locations of
the additional probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan Plate 2
Approximately 12 8 cubic yards of fine aggregate masonry grout was used to fill both the
scheduled and additional probe holes drilled for the bridge foundations
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications and
our geotechnical engineering exploration report Based on our observations it is our
professional opinion that the relatively small voids and or cavities encountered at the
bridge abutment and pier foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade
area should provide the recommended foundation support for the bridge abutment and
pier foundation
