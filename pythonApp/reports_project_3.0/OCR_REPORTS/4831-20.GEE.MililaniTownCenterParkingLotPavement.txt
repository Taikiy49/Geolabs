PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located in the Mililani Town Center in Mililani on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Several pavement distress areas
were noted within the parking lot area We understand that it is desired to evaluate the
existing pavement conditions and provide pavement repair recommendations at the
following locations
1 Roadway from Consolidated Theaters and in front of Chilis to the end of
Taco Bell
2 Roadway in front of Kaiser Permanente building
3 Roadway between Location No 2 above and the center mall buildings
4 Roadway between the center mall buildings Kaiser Permanente building
and the Rehabilitation Hospital of the Pacific building
The existing roadway pavement at the above locations consists of an asphalt
concrete pavement The main roadway within the shopping center consists of two lane
roads with one lane in each direction One way roadways are located within the adjacent
parking areas
The two lane roadway in front of the Consolidated Theaters building was recently
re constructed because of numerous pavement distresses The pavement distresses
consisted of potholes alligator pattern cracks and depressions of the pavement Our

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
eleven borings to depths of about 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface asphalt concrete layer with
thickness ranging from 1 25 to 5 inches The surface asphalt layer was underlain by a
medium dense to dense and very stiff to hard fill layer with thickness ranging from 0 to
2 2 feet The fill layer consisted of silty sand with gravel and clayey silt with gravel Below
the fill layer stiff to hard reddish brown silty clay was encountered to the maximum boring
depth of 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface At the majority of the boring sites it
appears that an asphalt concrete overlay had been placed over the original asphalt
concrete Groundwater was not encountered at the time of the field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 11 Results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soils samples are presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
OBSERVED PAVEMENT DISTRESS
Numerous pavement distresses were noted within the four pavement evaluation
areas These pavement distresses are shown on the Observed Pavement Distress Plans
Plates 3 1 and 3 2 Description of the observed pavement distresses in these areas are
presented as follows
Location No 1 Roadway From Consolidated Theaters and in Front of Chili s to
the End of Taco Bell
The two lane roadway section in this area is sloped towards the centerline of the
roadway for drainage purposes Surface runoff water drains along the roadway towards a
drainage inlet located near Chil s Pronounced stripping and raveling were noted along
the roadway centerline Stripping is the loss of adhesive bond between the asphalt
concrete and the aggregate in the asphalt concrete and is most often caused by the
presence of water Raveling is the progressive dislodgement of aggregate particles and
binder from the surface of the asphalt concrete pavement Stripping usually precedes
raveling
Alligator pattern cracks were noted in several pavement areas near Taco Bell In
addition brown coloring was noted within the cracks indicating that the underlying
subgrade material may have been ejected from the cracks Also near Taco Bell a water
valve box and the adjacent pavement area were depressed In one area near the
Consolidated Theaters building a portion of the asphalt concrete overlay was removed

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field observations and the results of our field exploration we believe
that the pavement distresses may have been caused by an inadequate base course
support layer below the surface asphalt layer poor surface drainage and surface water
erosion
Pavement that utilizes a surface asphalt layer is called a flexible pavement For
flexible pavements the material layers within the pavement section are designed to
reduce the surface loading with depth Therefore the better load resistant materials are
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
Castle Cooke Commercial Properties LLC Page 4
W O 4831 20
placed over materials that have lower load resistant properties Typically the surface
asphalt layer is underlain by a granular base course layer Based on our field exploration
the surface asphalt layer is underlain by clayey residual soils or fill materials consisting of
silty sand and clayey silt The laboratory test results indicate that the silty sand fill material
has a larger amount of fines content or material passing the No 200 sieve 14 percent
compared to the maximum recommended fines content of 9 percent for base course
material
The pavement distress was aggravated by poor surface drainage within the
roadway area As cracks developed within the surface asphalt layer surface water was
allowed to permeate within the underlying fill and subgrade materials As vehicles travel
over the distressed area the wet foundation materials are ejected through the cracks that
worsens and extends the distress During our site visit traces of soil materials were
observed at the top of the pavement surface within several areas where pavement cracks
were present
In several distress areas the roadway slopes towards the centerline for drainage
purposes Stripping and raveling of the surface of the asphalt layer caused by surface
water were observed
Because of the above findings we recommend that the existing pavement within
the distress areas be replaced with a new asphaltic pavement section described below
To reduce the potential for stripping and raveling of roadways with centerline drainage the
installation of a 6 inch thick concrete center swale is recommended for these roadways
The concrete swale should be underlain by at least 6 inches of compacted base course
material
The anticipated traffic along the driveway consists of mainly automobile traffic with
light to moderate truck traffic Based on the above we recommend that a new asphaltic
concrete pavement section be constructed within the project area The pavement section
should consist of the following
Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 5 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
A woven filter fabric consisting of Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be used between
the silty clay subgrade and the base course layer Prior to placement of the filter fabric
soft areas encountered at the finished subgrade level should be over excavated to expose
firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with base course material
that is compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Base course material
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate that is compacted to not less than 95 percent

Hawaii California

relative compaction Aggregate base course material should meet the material
requirements for Base Course as specified in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Department of Public Works City and County of Honolulu
September 1986
Should the above recommended repair be cost prohibitive the following alternative
repair may be considered although the pavement life will be significantly reduced The
alternative repair includes placing an asphalt concrete overlay over some of the repair
areas It should be noted that future distress will occur within the asphalt concrete overlay
areas The alternative repair consist of the reconstruction of only the main roadway from
Consolidated Theaters and in front of Chili s to the end of Taco Bell and the main
roadway circling the center mall with the above pavement section The other distress
areas are parking areas that are off the main roadway In these areas the existing
pavement may be cold planed and re surfaced with an asphalt concrete overlay at least
1 inch in thickness
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to
carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site
during or after construction
Construction Observation
Construction observation of the pavement repair during construction is an integral
part of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include the removal
of soft subgrade soils fill placement and compaction and pavement construction Other
aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those considered in this
report The recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent upon such
observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
