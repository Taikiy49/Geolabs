Dear Mr. Jacobson: 
In accordance with the contract entered with Hilton Resorts Corporation, Geolabs 
observed the installation of the jet grout test section and production columns for the off-site 
sewer improvements for the Ka Haku project in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during the jet 
grout column installation for the project. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the jet grout column installation for conformance with the intent of 
the project plans and specifications. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
A new high-rise building is planned at the former King’s Village complex in the Waikiki 
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. As part of the new high-rise building project, 
off-site sewer improvements are required. 
A new 12-inch diameter sewer line is planned from a new manhole at the high-rise 
building site to the existing manhole at Kaiulani Avenue.  A total of about 80 feet of new 
sewer line is planned for the project.  The invert of the new sewer line will range from about 
+0 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) at the new manhole at the high-rise building site to
about -3.4 feet MSL at the existing manhole on Kaiulani Street.
The off-site gravity sewer line was designed to be supported by jet-grouted columns 
following the strict guidelines by the City and County of Honolulu, where appreciable 
settlement of the sewer line is not tolerable.  The project required primary jet-grouted support

JET GROUT TEST SECTION 
As part of the project requirements, one sacrificial jet grout test section was installed 
adjacent to the planned sewer manhole SMH-1. The test section was installed by CMZ of 
Hawaii, Inc. (CMZ), on December 19, 2019.  
The test section consisted of installing three jet grout columns, designated as T-1 
through T-3, consisting of 5-foot, 7-foot, and 2.5-foot diameter jet grout columns, 
respectively.  T-1 and T-2, intended as secondary cradle columns, were installed to a depth 
of about 10 feet below the ground surface.  The primary jet grout column, T-3, was installed 
to about 51 feet below the ground surface. 
After 7 days, the test section was excavated for physical inspection of the installed 
test columns.  Based on our observations, test columns T-1 and T-3 had diameters of at 
least 5 and 2.5 feet, respectively.  However, test column T-2 was only about 64 inches in 
diameter and did not meet the intended size requirement of 7 feet.  Following the test section 
installation, the contractor decided not to utilize 7-foot diameter jet grout columns for the 
production jet grout columns. 
On December 27, 2019, continuous coring of test column T-3 was conducted using 
a drill rig equipped with triple-tube core barrel.  Unconfined compression tests were 
conducted on the samples of soil-crete retrieved from the test column.  The results of the 
compression tests are provided on Plate 1. 
PRODUCTION JET GROUT COLUMN INSTALLATION 
The production jet grout column installation program consisted of installing nine 
primary jet grout columns and 24 secondary cradle columns. In addition, the contractor 
installed 53 jet grout columns along the sides of the planned trench for dewatering and 
shoring support.  The production jet grout column installation, including shoring support 
columns, was conducted from January 15, 2020 to February 11, 2020 under the 
near-continuous (full-time) observations by a field representative from our office. CMZ used 
a Casagrande C8 crawler drill rig equipped with a Jet-Vision jet grout measuring system to 
install the jet grout columns. 
In general, the 2.5-foot diameter primary jet grout columns were installed along the 
centerline of the new sewer line at a maximum center-to-center spacing of 10 feet.  Five-
foot diameter jet grout columns were utilized for the secondary cradle columns.  The cradle 
columns were also installed along the centerline of the new sewer line at a center-to-center
