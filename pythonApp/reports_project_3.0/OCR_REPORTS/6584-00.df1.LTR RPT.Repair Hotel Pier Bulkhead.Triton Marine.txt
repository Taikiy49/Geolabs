Dear Mr. Myers: 
This letter report presents the results of our limited geotechnical engineering 
exploration performed in support of the above project.  Our services were generally limited 
to the drilling and sampling of one boring, performance of a constant head percolation test 
in the boring, laboratory testing of selected samples, and preparation of this letter report. 
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our findings resulting from our field exploration, 
laboratory testing, and engineering analyses. The findings presented herein are subject to 
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project involves repairs and improvements to the existing Hotel Fuel Pier at the 
junction between Wharf H5 and Wharf K12.  Specifics of the work were not provided nor 
were they considered to be relevant to our work on the project. 
A single boring was drilled and sampled to a depth of about 36.5 feet below the 
existing ground surface. Drilling and sampling was performed using a truck-mounted 
drilling rig. A constant head infiltration test was conducted in the boring to provide 
information on the permeability of the subsurface materials at the site.  The borehole was 
backfilled with grout after drilling and sampling. 
Purpose and Scope 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration is to obtain an overview of 
the subsurface conditions. In order to accomplish this, we conducted an exploration 
program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts:

Site Description 
The project site is within the Pearl Harbor portion of what is now identified as Joint 
Base Pearl Harbor Hickam on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The “H,” or Hotel, Wharves 
were developed between the Kilo Wharves at the base of what was formerly Kuahua 
Island and the mouth of Halawa Stream.  The project site is about +5 feet Mean Sea Level 
(MSL).  Based on our observations, the existing site is generally level. 
Subsurface Conditions 
Our subsurface exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring, 
designated as Boring No. 1, extending to a depth of about 36.5 feet below ground surface.  
The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural 
examination in the field by our geologist, who monitored the drilling operation on a 
near-continuous basis. Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified 
Soil Classification System, Plate 3.  Graphic representations of the materials 
encountered are presented on the Logs of Boring, Plates 4.1 and 4.2. 
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with 
ASTM D 1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving a 2-inch OD 
standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop. The blow counts needed 
to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18-inch drive are shown as the 
“Penetration Resistance” on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths. 
In general, the upper 5 feet below the existing ground surface consisted of very stiff 
brown clayey silt with some sand, gravel and cobbles.  This surface horizon represents 
man-made fill which was placed in dry conditions.  Below this upper fill layer, our boring 
encountered loose to medium dense brown sandy gravel which appeared to be 
man-made fill placed in wet conditions.  This fill extended to the maximum depth explored 
of 36.5 feet below the existing ground surface. 
At the time of our field exploration, groundwater was encounter in the boring at 
4.7 feet below the existing ground surface.  It should be noted that the groundwater level is 
expected to fluctuate in response to the tide in the adjacent harbor and with variation in 
rainfall, surface water runoff, groundwater withdrawal and other factors. 
Laboratory Testing 
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil and rock samples 
obtained from our field exploration. 
Moisture Content (ASTM D 2216) and Unit Weight (ASTM D 2937) determinations 
were performed on selected soil and rock samples as an aid in the classification and 
evaluation of their properties. Results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Boring at 
the appropriate sample depths. 
Three Grain Size Distribution tests (ASTM C 117 & C 136) were performed on 
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties. 
Graphic test results are presented on Plate 5. 
Percolation Testing 
A constant head test was conducted to assess the average permeability within an 
open interval of soil/rock material. The boring was drilled using PQ wireline coring tools 
advanced to the scheduled test depth.  Upon reaching the nominal test depth, the wireline 
rods were pulled back approximately 10 feet for the test.

The constant head test was conducted using the pump mounted on the drill rig, 
which provided approximately 50 gallons per minute (gpm).  The test was conducted for 
about 30 minutes of continuous pumping.

Limitations 
The findings and analyses submitted herein are based in part upon information 
obtained from the field boring.  Variations of subsoil conditions may occur, and the nature 
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway. If 
variations then appear evident, Geolabs should be contacted to re-evaluate the findings 
presented herein. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the boring depict 
the approximate boundaries between soil and, as such, may denote a gradual transition. 
Water level data from the boring was measured at the time shown on the graphic 
representations. These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the 
formulation of this report. However, it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to 
variation in rainfall, temperatures, tide, and other factors. 
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Triton Marine Construction 
Corp. for specific application to the “Design-Build RFP, Repair Hotel Pier Bulkhead, Pearl 
Harbor, Oahu, Hawaii” project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical 
engineering principles and practices. No warranty is expressed or implied. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered. Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or 
corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to attain a properly 
constructed project. Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs.
