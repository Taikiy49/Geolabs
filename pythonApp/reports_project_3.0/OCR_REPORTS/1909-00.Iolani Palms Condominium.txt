SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the southwestern slope of Punchbowl
Puowaina between Prospect and Iolani Streets The location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site measures approximately 144 by 170 feet in plan dimensions
and slopes down towards the southwest Ground surface elevations vary
from about 200 to 162 feet above Mean Sea Level
A 2 story dwelling and garage structure presently occupy the
northwest Ewa mauka corner of the site A well trinmed grass lawn with
mango plumeria and other trees covers the remaining area around the
dwelling Retaining walls currently provide grade separation between the
site Prospect and Iolani Streets Retaining walls are also located
Diamond Head and south makai of the existing dwelling
Underground utilities are believed to exist within the site

PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
It is proposed to construct a four story building approximately 98

SUBSURFACE_CONDI TIONS
Geology
Punchbowl Puowaina is a result of a violent phreatomagmatic
eruption of ash cinder and spatter similar to those which formed Diamond
Head Leahi and Salt Lake cementation and consolidation of the as
forms volcanic tuff a brown hard often glassy rock known as
mudrock Our exploration of the site and its vicinity indicates that
the area is underlain by ashy silty sand cinder sand and volcanic tuff
derived from Punchbowl eruptions
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 19 5 and
30 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The borings encountered two sets of soil conditions dividing the site
into two areas These areas are designated as the Ewa area in the
northwestern half of the site and the Diamond Head area in the
southeastern half of the site
In the Ewa area the borings encountered a surface layer of medium
dense tannish brown silty sand ash and cinder ranging in thickness
from 3 5 to 4 5 feet
Below this ash and cinder stratum the borings encountered mediun
dense to dense slightly cemented to uncemented black cinder sand
approximately 15 0 to 27 0 feet in thickness
Underlying the cinder sand medium hard volcanic tuff was encountered
at 20 5 to 30 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
Diamond Head Area
In the Diamond Head area the borings encountered a surficial layer
of fill consisting of loose tannish brown to brown silty sand
approximately 6 0 to 7 5 feet in thickness
Below this surficial fill layer the borings encountered medium dense
to dense tan mottled silty sand with some gravel extremely weathered
volcanic tuff ranging in thickness from about 1 5 to 2 3 feet
Underlying the extremely weathered volcanic tuff medium hard
moderately weathered tannish brown volcanic tuff was encountered to
28 3 feet the maximm depth drilled

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed We recommend spread or strip
footings bearing on the dense cinder sand or the underlying volcanic
tuff mudrock
The bottom of all footing excavations should be inspected by the
geotechnical engineer prior to the placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils
are removed prior to concrete placement
It is our opinion that for footings designed and constructed in
accordance with the criteria given herein the potential total and
differential settlements should be less than one half inch
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading plans are not currently
available However based on a finish grade of 170 5 feet at the lower
basement parking level excavation up to 20 feet high will be required
along the northern boundary
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill

With the exception of large pieces of broken volcanic tuff the
on site sandy soils should be suitable for use as a source of backfill
and fill materials If imported material is subsequently required
after on site sandy is hauled off site the materials should be similar
to the excavated silty sands or they should conform to the criteria
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public
Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 Test Method
Cut areas underlying structures or pavements should be excavated
to rough finish subgrade and compacted to 90 of its maximm dry
density as specified above if soil material is encountered at that
elevation
Excavation Support
The excavation for the basement parking areas may require two
10 foot high excavations at the eastern mauka end while cuts of up to
20 feet deep will occur along the northern boundary
The upper silty sand fill and part of the cinder sand soils
encountered within the excavation face may tend to unravel or slough
when exposed in vertical or near vertical cuts Therefore
consideration should be given to adequate shoring and bracing the
excavation to prevent damages resulting from movement of the excavation
walls In general the design of the shoring bracing should be left to
the contractor Therefore the prospective contractor bidding on this
project should be advised of the potential need for adequate support of
the excavation walls and adjacent utilities lines if any

Foundation Support
For the design of spread or strip footings theoretically two
different bearing values apply for the two anticipated bearing layers
For footings bearing on the dense cinder sand an allowable bearing
capacity of 3000 p s f may be utilized A higher allowable bearing
capacity of 6000 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on the
underlying volcanic tuff Accurate delineation of these two different
bearing layers under the lower parking level is not possible due to
insufficient number of borings drilled However based on the four
boring data our rough estimate is presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
Volcanic tuff or mudrock formation may likely be encountered at or
below the footing bottoms located on the eastern or Diamond Head
portion while cinder sand would be located on the western Ewa area
Deviation from the above should be anticipated
These bearing values may be increased by one third when temporary
wind and seismic loads are considered The footings should be embedded
a minimum of two feet 2 below the lowest adjacent grade and bear on
either cinder sand or volcanic tuff
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the
soft and or weak materials to either cinder sand or hard mudrock and
then backfilled the resultant void s with lean concrete

Retaining Walls
It is anticipated that retaining walls will be used for the
parking levels and to provide grade separation between the proposed
building other portions of the lot and adjacent properties The
guidelines for design of retaining walls are as follows
For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls unrestrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
active condition 2H 1V fluid pressure
sloping backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
A bearing capacity of up to 4000 p s f is recommended for
foundations extending to bear on either the cinder sand or
underlying volcanic tuff
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing in level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls located on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade For sloping ground the footing should extend to
obtain a minimum 8 foot set back from the outside edge of the
footing measured horizontally to the slope face

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the basement parking area floors of the
proposed building will be concrete slabs on grade The slab and its
cushion may be supported by the underlying basement subgrade soils
The cinder sand subgrade should first be moisture conditioned to the
wet side of optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 relative
dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method A 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 relative dry density should be
provided below the slabs
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements may be desired for the driveways and

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different fron
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

5 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the

laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of
the materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 4
