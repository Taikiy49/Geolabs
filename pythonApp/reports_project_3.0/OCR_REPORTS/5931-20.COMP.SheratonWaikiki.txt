This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the earthwork operations and pavement construction for the Hotels Master Plan
Renovation at Sheraton Waikiki project from through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing Sheraton Waikiki Hotel along the makai side of
Kalakaua Avenue in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
bounded approximately by the Royal Hawaiian Shopping Center and Kalakaua Avenue to
the north an existing development to the east Waikiki Beach to the south and existing
development and Lewers Street to the west A layout of the project site is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
We understand it is desired to improve the public areas of the existing hotel facility
under a master renovation plan Based on the information provided and subsequent
discussions the main improvements and construction will include the reconfiguration and
expansion of the existing swimming pools including new pool decks

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Nordic PCL Construction Inc is the general contractor for the Hotels Master Plan
Renovation at Sheraton Waikiki project Jayar Construction Company Inc performed the
earthwork operations and pavement construction The earthwork operations consisted of
demolition and clearing subgrade proof rolling placement and compaction of fills and
backfills and pavement construction
Following the demolition and clearing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to the subgrade elevations within the swimming pool
and pavement limits The over excavated subgrades was then scarified to a minimum
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor under the swimming pool area The
compaction requirement was increased to a minimum of 95 percent under the pavement
areas Base course underneath the sidewalk and driveways were brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent
respectively of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
During the earthwork operations sample of the on site soils and imported materials
utilized as fill materials was obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 204 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM 6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on the constructed fills and backfills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations and pavement construction
for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the
referenced report dated and subsequent recommendations

LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
