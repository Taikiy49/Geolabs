GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
ASING COMMUNITY PARK SITE IMPROVEMENTS
PARKING LOT WALKWAYS AND BACKSTOP
Dear Mr Muranaka
In accordance with our proposal dated May 2 2001 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Asing Community Park Site Improvements
project in the District of Ewa on the Istand of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes
the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents our findings and
recommendations for the proposed project These findings and discussions are intended
for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Asing Community Park in the District
of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a 40 stall parking lot an
accessible concrete walkway from the parking lot to the existing walkway along Fort
Weaver Road and a backstop for a softball field as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In
addition we understand that it is planned to install a lighting system
Construction of the proposed concrete walkways and asphalt concrete paved
parking lot will involve new slab on grade and pavement sections Our experience in the
area suggests that the project site may be underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays with
varying amounts of basaltic and coralline gravel near the ground surface Special
attention was given to the swelling potential of the near surface soils as they might affect
the performance of the new concrete walkways and pavements at the parking lot
The new softball backstop will be a standard Type C backstop with a maximum
post spacing of 8 feet and a height of 23 feet In addition the site improvement will also
include installation of a lighting system in the vicinity of the parking lot We anticipate that
ihe proposed light poles will be the typical standard size light poles of galvanized steel
and or aluminum construction of approximately 25 feet in height Special attention was
given to the lateral load supporting characteristics of the subsurface materials for the light
pole and backstop foundation at the project site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was used
to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining io the design of the proposed Asing Community Park Site
Improvements project Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated May 2 2001 Our scope of work included the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Research and review of in house soils and other available boring data in the
vicinity of the project site
2 Reconnaissance of the project site to observe the surrounding site
conditions by a project engineer from our firm
3 Collection of bulk soil samples for CBR analyses
A Laboratory testing of samples obtained during the field reconnaissance as
an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed park
improvements
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and the results of the laboratory tests
performed on the bulk soil samples are presented on Plates 3 and 4 of this report
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the northern side of the existing West Loch Elderly
Housing Complex which is at the northeastern corner of Renton Road and Fort Weaver
Road The site is bounded by a parking lot to the south a driveway to the east a fence
along Fort Weaver Road to the west and an open softball field to the north The new
backstop will be located at the northern end of the softball field as shown on the Site Plan

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by observation of the
surrounding site conditions by a project engineer from our firm and the collection of bulk
soil samples for CBR analyses The bulk soil samples were collected from the proposed
parking area from the surface to a depth of approximately 2 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate location where the bulk samples were collected is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The bulk soil materials collected were classified by visual and textural examination
by our engineer and were further reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as
shown on Plate 3
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 on the near surface clayey bulk soil samples to evaluate the pavement
support characteristics of the soils The test result is presented on Plate 4
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by stiff to
very stiff silty clays with varying amounts of coralline gravel near the ground surface The
silty clays grade from very stiff to hard with depth extending to the maximum depths
explored of about 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered during our field exploration However
groundwater levels may be influenced by other factors such as seasonal precipitation
groundwater withdrawal and or injection

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The project site is underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays with varying amounts of
coral gravel near the existing ground surface which grades to hard with depth We
understand that a driveway and a parking lot with asphaltic concrete pavements are
planned for the project In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for the parking lot will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light to medium sized trucks Based on our
field exploration and laboratory testing the on site silty clays appear to have a CBR value
of 9 0 with an expansion value of about 1 9 percent
Based on the subsurface conditions in the general vicinity of the project site and the
anticipated loading conditions lateral load and ground line overturning moment we
believe that the new backstop may be constructed in accordance with footing dimensions
provided in the Parks and Recreation s Standard Detail for a Type C Backstop The
backstop foundations will generally consist of a 24 inch diameter 48 inch deep shaft
coupled with a 18 inch diameter 36 inch deep smaller shaft at some post locations The
performance of the shaft foundations will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction construction procedures and workmanship The
recommendations and general guidelines presented in this report should be followed
closely by the contractor during foundation construction work
In consideration of the lateral load and moment demands acting on the proposed
light pole foundations we recommend that drilled shafis embedded in the very stiff to hard
silty clays be used to support the proposed lighting system We recommend that the
drilled shafts consist of 30 inch diameter shafts embedded a minimum of 5 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grades for the proposed light pole foundations located on level
ground conditions Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this report
Pavements
In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for the driveways and parking lot of this
project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light to medium sized trucks
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing the on site silty clays appear to have
a CBR value of 9 0 with an expansion value of about 1 9 percent Based on the above
assumptions we recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for
preliminary design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade

The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base and select borrow
subbase course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above sections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into
the subgrade soil below the aggregate base and select borrow subbase course layers to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain
system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For
long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscaped areas
Concrete Walkways
We understand that it is planned to consiruct a concrete walkway at the northern
side of the existing parking The new concrete walkway will connect ihe existing walkway
from Fort Weaver Road to the new parking area We recommend that a minimum 12 inch
thick layer of non expansive select granular fill be placed below the concrete walkways
The subgrade soils below the walkway should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The select granular fill should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections Areas
adjacent to slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding next to the slab
Backstop Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the general vicinity of the
project site we believe that the new backstop may be construcied in accordance with
footing dimensions provided in the Parks Recreation s Standard Detail for a Type C
Backstop The backstop foundations will generally consist of a 24 inch diameter 48 inch
deep shaft coupled with a 18 inch diameter 36 inch deep smaller shaft at some post
locations The cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support from both
skin friction and end bearing between the shafts and the surrounding soils An allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 5 kips per shaft may be used for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces

The anticipated settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than inch We
believe that a significant portion of the settlemenis is elastic and should occur as the loads

The performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures To reduce the potential for caving in
of the drilled holes temporary casing may be required during the foundation construction
Appropriate drilling equipment should be utilized by the drilled shaft contractor to install the
drilled shafts to the depths and dimensions recommended herein The load bearing
capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the contact between the drilled
shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction techniques are important
The contractor should exercise care in drilling the shaft holes and in placing concrete into
the holes
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soil The concrete
should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the
concrete mix If pre cast concrete pole bases are used the gap between the pole bases
and the surrounding soils should be backfilled with lean concrete A representative from
Geolabs should be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts during construction
Light Pole Foundations
In order to develop the required resistance to the lateral load and moment
demands the proposed light pole structures should be supported by a foundation system
consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts As previously indicated we envision
that the proposed light poles will be of galvanized steel and or aluminum construction of
approximately 25 feet in height Based on these typical conditions we anticipate that the
structural loading at the base of the light pole may be as follows
Vertical Load _ 500 pounds
Lateral Load 1 000 pounds
Overturning Moment 25 000 foot pounds
Based on the anticipated structural loads for the new light poles we recommend
that the light pole foundations consist of cast in place concrete drilled shafts with a
minimum diameter of 30 inches Based on the anticipated structural loads and the
subsurface conditions encountered at the site we recommend that drilled shaft
foundations with an embedment length of not less than 5 feet below the design finished
grades be used for the new light pole structures located on level ground conditions If the

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished grades we anticipate
that minimal site grading work will be required for construction of the new improvements at
the subject site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill

Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill
In general we envision that the excavated on site materials may be re used as fill
or backfill materials provided that deleterious materials are removed and that the
excavated on site materials are processed to a maximum particle size of about 3 inches
Fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts of 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
Imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular fill material such as
crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less
when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site
for the intended use
Cut and fill slopes planned at the site may be designed based on a slope inclination
of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Finished slopes should be protected as
soon as they are graded to reduce the potential for significant erosion of the finished
slopes Slope planting or other means of slope protection should be provided to reduce
the potential for significant erosion of the finished slopes
Drainage
The finished grades outside the proposed parking area and concrete walkways
should be sloped to shed water away from the slabs and foundations and to reduce the
potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should be avoided Planters next to foundations and slabs should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce waier infiltration into the subsoils

foundation and slab recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil

water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the light pole
foundation consiruction subgrade preparation and fill and backfill placement Other
aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report
was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such
observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
