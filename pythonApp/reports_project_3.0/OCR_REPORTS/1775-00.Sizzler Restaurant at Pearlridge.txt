In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering study for the subject project The approximate location of
the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of the
foundation and site grading for the proposed structure

The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to depths of 21 0 and 31 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Bulk samples of possible import fill material from the nearby
excavation site for Pali Momi Medical Center were obtained and tested in
our laboratory to determine the soil engineering properties for site
grading
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed building will be a
one story structure with concrete slab on grade approximately 80 by 90
feet in plan A future retaining wall about 10 feet high is proposed
along the northern portion of the site The entire building site will be
filled from about 4 to 8 feet in height
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is situated on the makai side of Moanalua Road and at the
northeast corner of Pearlridge Shopping Center The site is bounded by
First Hawaiian Bank and Kalauao Spring Drainage Channel at the Diamond
Head and Ewa sides respectively

The area is presently paved with asphaltic concrete and serves as a
parking lot The site slopes gently towards the makai side with
approximate gradients of 10 1 H V or flatter The present elevation of
the site varies from about 40 to 50 feet above Mean Sea Level which is
about 6 to 10 feet lower than the adjacent Moanalua Road and parking lot
driveway
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 21 0 and 31 0 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings encountered very stiff to hard alluvium consisting of
clayey silt with gravel and boulders to the full depths drilled
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings at the time of
our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff to hard clayey silt soil encountered can provide suitable
foundation support for the proposed new fill and building
About 4 to 8 feet of fill is planned in order to provide a finished
grade elevation of 50 5 feet above Mean Sea Level
Select non expansive import fill material should be used to provide
adequate foundation support of the new building
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished floor of the proposed
building will be at elevation 50 5 feet Therefore about 4 to 8 feet of
fill is planned for site grading Minimal settlements are anticipated due
to the weight of the new fill over the stiff alluvial soils

Site Preparation
Prior to filling the existing trees and vegetation should be removed
and the existing asphaltic concrete pavement should be broken and
reused in the fill
Fill Material and Placement
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive soils
and submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory testing
and approval prior to its intended delivery to the project site
One possible source of fill material is from the nearby excavation
site for Pali Momi Medical Center Bulk samples from the above
mentioned excavation site were obtained and tested for soil
engineering properties The laboratory test results are summarized
in Appendix B The results of our laboratory CBR and swell tests
indicate that these excavated soils have moderate compacted strength
and are moderately expansive Therefore we recommended that these
soils be used as fill material only below three 3 feet of the
finished grade The upper three 3 feet of fill material should
consist of selected non expansive granular material with maximm size
less than 3 inches
Fills should be placed in maximum 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for the
anticipated light foundation loads on the compacted fill The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for short duration wind and seismic loading
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used
between the foundation and the compacted fill
Minimal footing settlement is anticipated due to the light building
loads
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire structure To
minimize the swelling potential of the soil beneath the slab it is
recommended that the upper three 3 feet of fill consist of select
material as specified under the Site Grading section of this report
A moisture barrier membrane should be placed below the slab to
protect the flooring covering
The slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion layer of 3F
gravel

The concreting of the slab should be coordinated in such a way that
the time period between the initial excavation and the final concrete
placement is kept to a minimum in order to prevent excessive drying of the
subgrade
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that future retaining walls about 10 feet
high are proposed to provide grade separation around the building area
The following recommendations for retaining wall design may be used
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfil1 per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes if any
3 A base friction factor of 0 40 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
5 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Pavements
New A C pavements around the proposed building and for parking
facilities are anticipated
The following pavement section may be used for preliminary design
purposes
Flexible Pavement Driveway and Parking Area
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The recommended section considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
