PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a septic tank and seepage pit for the proposed residence
at Menehune Fishpond in the Niumalu area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii Access to the
site is from a dirt road off of Hulemalu Road The ground elevations at the residence site
generally varies from about 8 to 20 feet MSL The site is currently covered with grass and
small trees The site and its vicinity are shown on the attached Project Location Map and
Site Plan Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The scope of our work did not include the foundation design of the proposed
house One borehole was drilled to a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
using a truck mounted drill rig After drilling and sampling percolation test was performed
to evaluate the permeability of the underlying ground formation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the project consisted of drilling and sampling one
boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the leaching field area is generally
underlain by stiff brown clayey silt to a depth of about 10 5 feet underlain by gray to white
silty fine sand to the maximum depth explored of approximately 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled boring at a depth of about 10 feet
below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater
level encountered correspond to approximately Elevation 3 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to fluctuation due to seasonal
precipitation tidal fluctuation and other factors occurring off site Detailed descriptions of
the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration are presented on the Logs
of Boring Plates 3 and 4 of this report

PERCOLATION TEST RESULTS
Based on the information provided we understand that on site sewage treatment
and disposal system using septic tank and leach field is planned for the new residence
Therefore it was desired to conduct field percolation tests at the future leach field area
in order to obtain data for preliminary design of the sewage disposal system
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of
the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by
inch diameter boreholes to depths of about 3 and 4 feet below the existing
ground surface
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the borehole to
protect the bottom of the hole from scouring and sediments Based on the results of the
trial runs the hole was presoaked with 12 inches of water for at least 4 hours and then
permitted to swell for at least 12 hours before testing During testing the hole was
carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the
calculation of the percolation tests was based
Based on the percolation tests conducted we believe that a percolation rate
ranging from 5 to 7 5 minutes per inch may be used for the project site Based on Table
I in the Manual of Septic Tank Practice by the US Department of Health Education
and Welfare this corresponds to a maximum rate of sewage application of about 2 2
gallons per square foot per day Due to the highly variable nature of the alluvial
deposits underlying the project site the absorption capacity of the disposal system
should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction Results of the
individual percolation tests are presented on Plate 5 of this report
