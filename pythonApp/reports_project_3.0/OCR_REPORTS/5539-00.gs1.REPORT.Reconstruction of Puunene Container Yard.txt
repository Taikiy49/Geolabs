PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between Wharf Street and Puunene Avenue within the
State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Kahului Harbor facility on the Island of
Maui Hawaii The project includes the construction of a new container yard with high
capacity and lower capacity pavements Based on the project drawings the high capacity
pavement section consists of 6 inches of Portland Cement Concrete PCC pavement
reinforced with No 4 rebars at 12 inches on center in each direction The PCC pavement
is underlain by 18 inches of lean concrete base with a minimum 28 day concrete strength
of 2 000 pounds per square inch psi A polyethylene bond breaker is specified between
the PCC pavement and the lean concrete base
We understand that replacement of the above reinforced concrete pavement
section with an un reinforced PCC pavement section as part of the VECP is desired The
pavement design will be based on the existing subsurface information provided in the
geotechnical report for the project by Masa Fujioka Associates MFA Based on the
available information the design vehicle for the pavement design was a Hyster 920
Container Handler Design traffic information was not available in the contract documents

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 AASHTO T 180 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The Portland Cement Concrete should have a minimum flexural strength of
650 pounds per square inch psi when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation C 78 Aggregate subbase materials required for the pavement sections
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to Section 703 17 of the
State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 HSS The aggregate subbase should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the aggregate subbase course and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding
should not be allowed on the site during or after construction Where landscaping is

Pavement Joints
Due to the rigidity of the Portland cement concrete pavements significant stresses
may develop in the concrete from variations in temperature and moisture content In order
to relieve the high level of stress and to reduce the potential for significant cracking
adequate joints should be provided in the Portland cement concrete pavement alternative
In addition to the jointing requirements the perimeter joint around the edge of the rigid
pavements should be doweled to reduce the potential for slab separation along the joint
due to stop and go traffic The following describes the various types of joints and general
guidelines that should be implemented in the design of concrete pavements
Crack Control Joints Contraction Joints
In general decreases in temperature or moisture content such as that during
concrete curing will cause the concrete to contract and possibly crack Crack
control joints are used to provide controlled cracking due to volume changes and
relieve the stresses caused by curling or warping Crack control joints such as saw
cut joints or formed grooves are provided to a depth of generally one fourth 14 the
Slab thickness These saw cut joints or formed grooves crack control joints
provide a weakened plane which will likely crack through the full depth as the
concrete shrinks during the curing process
in general the spacing of crack control joints should be limited to a maximum of
20 feet Smaller joint spacing should be used if the concrete pavement will be
subjected to extreme temperature gradients during the placement and or life span
of the pavement The crack control joints should be provided in both longitudinal
and transverse directions The transverse joint spacing should not vary from the
longitudinal joint spacing by more than 25 percent
Expansion Joints Isolation Joints
Expansion joints are typically used to relieve compressive stresses developed at
critical locations due to fluctuations in temperature Expansion joints are cut to the
full depth of the concrete slab and compressible filler materials are placed in the
cut These joints allow for isolated lateral movement of the slabs and reduce the
potential for damage to the adjoining concrete spalling of the edges of the concrete
slabs
Expansion joints should be used at pavement intersections and at intersections of
pavements with fixed structures such as the light poles and mooring bollard
Because expansion joints generally do not provide for load transfer across the joint

Construction Joints
Construction joints are generally required when two abutting slabs are placed at
different times Therefore construction joints usually form the edges of each day s
work For the relatively thick concrete slab 16 inches planned for this project the
construction joints should consist of a keyed or butted joint In general construction
joints should be aligned with the crack control or expansion joints to reduce the
number of pavement joints required
