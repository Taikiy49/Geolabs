PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project consisted of constructing an off-site retention basin for the Makena 
Residences & Beach Club.  The retention basin has a bottom elevation of about +200 feet 
Mean Sea Level (MSL).  A 36-inch diameter outlet drain, cement rubble masonry (CRM) 
lined inflow ditch, and overflow spillway were provided for the retention basin. 
The construction of the retention basin required cuts of up to about 40 feet in depth 
to achieve design grades.  Minor fills of less than about 5 feet in height were also required 
in some areas.  The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1.

EARTHWORK OPERATION 
Goodfellow Bros. LLC was the general contractor for the project covered by this 
report.  Our observation and testing services during the sitework performed by 
Goodfellow Bros. LLC for the project occurred in April 2020. 
In general, areas to be filled and subgrades in cut areas were proof-rolled with a 
vibratory drum roller for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near-surface cavities 
and/or voids. 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the project 
site and some imported materials. General fills were placed in the majority of fill areas of the 
project site. General fills consisted of well-graded granular materials generally smaller than 
3 inches in dimension.  Relatively impermeable fill materials were also placed at the surface 
of the fill embankment.  Fills and backfills were generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches 
in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction. 
During the earthwork operations, a sample of the imported materials utilized as fill 
materials was obtained and transported to our office for testing. The maximum dry density 
and optimum moisture content were established in our laboratory in general accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on the attached 
Plate 2. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill operations on an as-need basis 
to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil 
conditions were noted. A total of four field density tests were performed using the nuclear 
probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Compaction test results are summarized 
on Plate 3. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating 
from on-site survey stakes and topographic features. These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas 
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction 
was obtained. 
Based on the observations by our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations performed by 
Goodfellow Bros. LLC for the project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project 
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented 
in the referenced report dated June 2, 2016 and subsequent recommendations.
