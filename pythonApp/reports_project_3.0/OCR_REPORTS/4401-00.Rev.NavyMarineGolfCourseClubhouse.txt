INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the new Navy Marine Golf Course Clubhouse project in the Moanalua area
of Naval Station NAVSTA Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of
building foundations site preparation and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new Navy Marine Golf Course Clubhouse project will be constructed on a
design build basis on the Diamond Head side of Valkenburgh Street between Warden
Avenue and Bougainville Street in the Moanalua area of NAVSTA Pearl Harbor on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided it is proposed to demolish the
existing clubhouse building and construct a new clubhouse facility in the same vicinity We
understand that the new clubhouse will be a two story building with a total floor area of
approximately 22 480 square feet As part of the project development new asphalt
concrete paved parking lots and entry driveway are also planned at the site to serve the
new facility
Based on the information provided we envision that the new building will be
constructed of a structural steel gravity load carrying space frame and reinforced concrete
shear walls with slab on grade floor system at the ground level The typical second floor
framing system will consist of 3 inch thick concrete topping over composite metal decking
supported by composite steel beams The second floor columns and roof system will
consist of structural steel rafters supporting structural metal decking without topping

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau
The project site is located near the southwestern flank of the Aliamanu vent and
Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series As the molten magma
ascended through these vents it reacted with the groundwater generating steam This
steam caused explosive eruptions which deposited low cones of ash material that have
consolidated to form tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid down when the
sea level was higher than the present level The Aliamanu eruptive event deposited its
material over alluvium originating from Halawa Stream
After this phase of volcanic activity ceased sedimentary deposits accumulated
over the Aliamanu tuff before the next phase of volcanic activity began Then the
Salt Lake eruptions began when the sea level was at about 40 to 60 feet below its
GEOLABS INC
W O 4401 00 Rev Page 5
present level The Salt Lake eruptive event deposited its material over earlier tuffs from
the older Aliamanu events

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration and laboratory test results indicate that the new building site is
generally underlain by expansive clayey soils at shallow depths Unless slabs on grade
supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to the slabs on grade resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of the
expansive clayey soils To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the
swelling of the on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select granular
capping fill material be provided for a depth of 3 feet below the slab cushion or to the
underlying volcanic tuff surface whichever is less As mentioned previously the
recommended capping fill layer should also extend to the outside edge of the building
foundations
Prior to placement of the select granular capping fill the clayey subgrades if
exposed at the over excavated subgrade level should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The select granular capping
fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially
expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade
soils In our opinion the reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils
should reduce the impact of shrink swell effects on the slab due to potential heaving of the
underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted

We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be incorporated below the building slabs as a cushion fill
layer To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier membrane is recommended on top of

Exterior sidewalks and patio slabs are also anticipated for the proposed project

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections will be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the

pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper backfill material
and placement could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes

material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and

potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the

from the foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be setback a minimum
distance of 3 feet from the building or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the
potential for water infiltration into the subsoils

Corrosion Protection
One set of pH and minimum resistivity tests was performed to evaluate the
corrosivity of the upper clayey soils encountered at the subject site The results of
these tests are presented on Plate B 2 of Appendix B The minimum resistivity value of
the near surface soil sample tested is on the order of about 1 800 ohm cm which
corresponds to a Corrosion Rating of 2 very corrosive based on the guidelines
provided by the City County of Honolulu Board of Water Supply Therefore we
recommend that all metallic substructures such as ductile iron water lines in contact
with the upper clayey soils be properly designed to protect against potential corrosion

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about
10 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the test
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
