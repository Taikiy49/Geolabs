Review of Alternate Design for MSERW No 5 Foundation
Kahekili Highway Widening
Likelike Highway to Vicinity of Haiku Road
In accordance with your request we have reviewed the submittal proposing an
alternate design for the foundation of MSERW No 5 at Vailey No 1 of the subject
project The calculations and plan for the alternate design were prepared by Nishimura
Inc Based on our review of the geotechnical engineering
aspects of the submittal we have the following comments
1 The horizontal loads used in the calculations to determine the permanent
piles sheetpile wall and steel pile system are based on a pressure
diagram using active earth pressures Based on this design concept
excavation of the soils on the lower side of the permanent piles are not
planned Therefore movement of the permanent pile system to develop
an active condition does not appear to be possible Therefore we believe
that the pressure diagram used in the design may need to be revised to
indicate a restrained condition using at rest pressures for the surcharge
load and the lateral soil pressure This condition will increase the
horizontal loading on the permanent pile system significantly
2 Since the MSERW backfill materials will consist of select granular
materials we believe that the average unit weight of 120 p c f used in the
calculations to determine the surcharge load from the MSERW appears
to be low The typical unit weight of the MSERW backfill materials used

3 The subsoils at the location of the permanent pile system may be
classified as corrosive to buried metallic structures with minimum
resistivity values in the range of 1 300 to 5 200 ohm cm Since the steel
sheetpiles and steel piles will be permanent structures retaining the
highway embankment the alternate design system should take into
consideration corrosion potential It should be noted that the MSERW
design for corrosion potential was based on a 100 year life
4 The steel piles and battered concrete piles are shown to be embedded
into the basalt formation underlying the soft subsoils Based on the
borings conducted in the area the basalt formation directly below the soft
subsoils ranges from medium hard to hard The contractor should
indicate how these piles are to be installed to the required embedment
depths into the medium hard to hard basalt formation It appears that
predrilling the pile locations and backfilling with concrete may be needed
5 Based on the plan submitted the deadman anchor still appears to be
located within the zone of influence of the stee sheetpile wall Therefore
passive resistance of the deadman anchor would impose additional
loading on the permanent wall The contractor s designer should verify
that the deadman anchor is located beyond the influence zone of the
permanent sheetpile wail
6 In our geotechnical engineering exploration report for the project we
indicated that the MSERW No 5 will impose a load on the subsoils
resulting in potential settlements on the order of 4 to 6 inches We
believe that the anticipated settlement of 4 to 6 inches would affect the
integrity of the tie beam connection to the rigid pile cap Therefore the
design should consider including some flexibility in the tie beam to pile
cap connection to accommodate the anticipated settlements
7 Because of anticipated settlements on the order of 4 to 6 inches the
effect of the settlements on the sheetpiles should also be considered in
the alternate design One of the potential effects of settlements on the
sheetpiles would be downdrag forces on the sheetpile
