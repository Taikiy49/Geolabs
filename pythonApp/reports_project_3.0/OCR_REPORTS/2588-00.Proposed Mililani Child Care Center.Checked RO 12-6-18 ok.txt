Based on the plans provided to us the proposed child care center will consist of
three 8 single story buildings Buildings A B and C Each of the buildings will utilize

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located northerly of the Mililani Interchange of the H 2 Freeway
on the easterly side of the freeway The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography of the site is essentially level The ground elevations range from
about 761 to 765 feet above mean sea level
At the time of our field exploration the site was occupied by abandoned pineapple
fields and narrow dirt roads
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of the two
extinct shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the
bulk of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority
of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its
eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the
Schofield Plateau
In situ chemical weathering of the basaltic lava rocks in the plateau has formed a
mantle of residual soils which grade with depth to saprolite which is a form of residual
soil which retains the original structure of the parent rock The saprolite grades with
depth to basaltic rock formation

Agricultural land development within the last century has brought the project area
to its present form
Field Exploration
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 14 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by residual soils
consisting of stiff reddish brown clayey silt which grades to saprolite a form of residual
soils which retains relict structure of the parent rock from depths of about 10 feet to the
maximum depth of the drilled borings
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings during field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration are presented in Appendix A
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained The test results are
presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed buildings provided that care is exercised
during construction to limit the swelling potential of the soil as recommended herein
The laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt indicate that this
material generally has low to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2588 00 Page 6
content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation The on site clayey silt soils tends to
be moderately expansive if it has been compacted to dense conditions air dried to low
moisture contents and subsequently saturated Therefore to reduce the potentially
damaging effects of soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the subgrade
soils below the concrete slab and gravel cushion layer be moistened and protected from
drying out from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots
encountered at fill areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then
replaced with compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The on site clayey silt can be used as fill or backfill materials If required imported
fill should consist of non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts moisture
conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Footing Foundations
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed buildings
of the child care center
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on
the natural soil or compacted fill with a minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade In addition the minimum dimension of the shallow
footing foundations should not be less than 16 inches
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to minimize footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid ponding of
surface run off water that may affect the expansive potential of the soils around the
foundations
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be moderately expansive
if compacted to dense conditions and allowed to dry Therefore the subgrade soils
especially the upper 12 inches below the concrete slab and gravel cushion layer should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2588 00 Page 8
not be over compacted The subgrade soils should be thoroughly moistened and
prevented from drying out prior to the concrete placement of the slab The thickened
edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
A minimum 4 inch thick layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
cushion fill should be provided below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier should be incorporated below the slab
Covered Walkway
Covered pedestrian walkways approximately eight 8 feet wide are indicated
connecting the three 3 buildings A thickness of four 4 inches of plain portland cement
concrete is typical for pedestrian walkways and is recommended for this project
The thickened edge of the concrete slab may be eliminated if some amount of
small slab heaving and cracking is acceptable The subgrade soils should be maintained
in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement A
minimum 4 inch thick layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel cushion fill
should be provided below the slab
Contraction joints consisting of grooves formed in the surface prior to initial set of
the concrete should be provided along the centerlines of the walkways and perpendicular
ly at four 4 foot intervals Expansion joints should be provided at walkway intersections
at locations where the walkways abut structures and around the posts supporting the roof
of the walkways

section may be revised if necessary The recommended section is based on the
assumption that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas and the
anticipated traffic will be light
Drainage
The finish grade outside the slab footings and pavement should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Excessive landscape
watering near foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2588 00 Page 10
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall and slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions

this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

recommendations given in this report
