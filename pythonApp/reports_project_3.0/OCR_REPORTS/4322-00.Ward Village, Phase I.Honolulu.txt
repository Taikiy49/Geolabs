INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the Ward Village Phase project to be located to the west of the intersection of Queen
and Kamakee Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing
and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site grading and pavements only and are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Range
and its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the genesis of the
GEOLABS INC
W O 4322 00 Page 5
Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana area are dominated by
fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are intercalated
with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The base of the stratigraphic
section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments along the coast
and in areas that were formerly valleys and drainage ways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site generally
consist of near surface medium dense to dense fills ranging from about 2 to 3 feet thick The
fills were underlain by loose to medium dense lagoonal deposits extending to a depth of
about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits were underlain by
silty coral sands and gravel extending to the maximum depth explored of about 26 feet below
the existing ground surface Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 1 3 to 4 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our borings and the anticipated
structural loads we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings may be used to support the proposed new one story structure The new
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grades New footings to be located adjacent to the existing structure foundations should
be embedded to the same depths as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the
existing foundations The new footings should not be embedded by more than about
6 inches below the bottom of the existing footings to avoid undermining the existing
footings The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed in the field during
construction
It should be noted that the existing medium dense to dense fill crust at the project site
is relatively thin on the order of about 2 to 3 feet thick only Therefore footing excavations
and other excavations should be kept as shallow as possible to reduce the potential for
encountering the underlying soft loose lagoonal deposits in the excavations If the
excavations expose the soft loose lagoonal deposits the soft loose soils should be over
excavated by a minimum depth of 24 inches and should be replaced with No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a non woven filter fabric

Foundations
Based on the relatively dense near surface soil conditions encountered at the project
site we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may
be used to support the proposed new one story building An allowable bearing pressure of
up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing
on the recompacted on site soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces The soils at the footing subgrade level should be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete In
addition the footing subgrade should be firm and unyielding Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades However it should be noted that the existing medium dense to
dense fill crust at the project site is relatively thin on the order of about 2 to 3 feet thick only
Therefore footings should be kept as shallow as possible to reduce the potential for
encountering the underlying soft loose lagoonal deposits in the footing excavations If the
footing excavations expose the soft loose lagoonal deposits during construction the
soft loose soils should be over excavated by a minimum depth of 24 inches below the bottom
of footing elevation The resulting excavation should be backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a non woven filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or
equivalent

New footings to be located adjacent to the existing structure footings should be
embedded to the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper to avoid
surcharging the existing foundations If the new footings are to be extended deeper than the
existing footings they should not extend by more than about 6 inches below the existing
footings to avoid undermining the existing footings The actual depths of the existing footings

trenches or easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential
for appreciable foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings supported on the
recompacted fills may be on the order of about 1 inch We estimate that the differential
settlements between adjacent foundations supported on the recompacted fills to be on the
order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted granular fill soils
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf above the groundwater table
and 150 pcf below the groundwater table This assumes that the soils around the footings
are relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed one story building and will
be supported by new fills needed to raise the existing ground to the finished subgrade
elevation We anticipate fills on the order of about 2 feet or less will be required to elevate
the existing ground to the finished subgrade of the slab In general the asphalt concrete
pavement on which the new fills will be placed should be removed Following removal of
the asphalt concrete the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Fills should be placed in general
accordance with the recommendations provided in the Site Grading section of this report
We recommend that a 4 inch thick cushion fill layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more uniform support
and to serve as a capillary break The slab cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible
after the scarification and compaction of the slab subgrade soils For slabs on grade with
moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over
the gravel cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration of moisture vapor through the
concrete slab Two inches of moist sand may be considered above the plastic membrane
to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision
of the 2 inches of fine sand above the vapor barrier will be left up to the discretion of the
project structural engineer Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be
considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking

After preparation of the slab subgrades they should be kept moist prior to placement
of cushion fill and or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be moistened to close the cracks or scarified
moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of aggregate subbase
material The aggregate subbase should conform to the material requirements as specified
in Section 703 17 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 HSS The aggregate subbase material below concrete walkways
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils
underneath the sidewalk slabs should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report Contraction joints should be provided
at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
relatively impervious soils and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the slab and foundation
Site Grading
Based on the existing ground elevations and the proposed finished grades fills on
the order of less than about 2 feet will be required to achieve the design finished subgrade
elevations for the proposed one story building In general site grading operations should
be observed by a representative of Geolabs It is important that a representative from our
office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide
guidelines for the site grading work
GEOLABS INC

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site In addition the existing asphalt concrete pavement within the new
building footprint and other improvements should be removed to expose the underlying base
material in order to facilitate the placement of new fills Slabs and foundations of the existing
structures that are to be demolished should be completely removed
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and demolition should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material The over excavation resulting from the
clearing and demolition should be backfilled with well compacted fill material Existing utilities
were observed to traverse the proposed new building footprint Existing utilities to be
abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled
Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled
by pumping lean concrete Controlled Low Strength Material under low pressure
After clearing and demolition finished subgrades or areas to receive fills should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In areas subjected
to vehicular traffic finished subgrades below pavement areas the compaction requirement
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
It should be noted that the medium dense to dense fill crust present at the site is
relatively thin on the order of about 2 to 3 feet thick only Therefore excavations below the
existing ground surface should be kept to a minimum to reduce the potential for encountering
the underlying soft loose lagoonal deposits If soft loose materials are encountered at the
excavated subgrade elevations they should be over excavated by a minimum depth of
24 inches below the subgrade elevation The resulting excavation should be backfilled with

Pavements
New flexible pavements are planned for the driveways and parking areas for the
proposed project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for these areas of the project will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks In
addition rigid pavements are planned for the loading area where vehicle loading will consist
mainly of heavy trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the
pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the silty sands and gravelly soils encountered in our
borings Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following pavement
sections be used for preliminary design purposes

Rigid Pavement
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade

moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course
materials should meet the requirements of Subsection 703 17 and 703 06 respectively of
the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994
HSS The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings extending to depths ranging from about 11 5 to 26 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
