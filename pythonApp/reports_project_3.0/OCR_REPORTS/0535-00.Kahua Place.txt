Geology
The geologic history of the project area is characterized by
typically leeward soil weathering and erosion Lava flows from
the Koolau Shield Volcanoe during the late Pliocene Period
initially created the slopes Weathering and subsequent lava
flows resulted in interbedded weathered residual soils and un
weathered lava flows
Subsurface Explorations
Three test borings were drilled at the locations shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 to delineate subsurface conditions
in the vicinity of the proposed roadway A detailed description
of the field explorations is given in the Appendix along with the
boring logs and laboratory test results
Subsurface explorations indicate that the site is immediately
underlain by stiff to hard reddish brown clayey silt or stiff to
hard gray brown clayey silt with red streaks The soil extends
to a depth of approximately 10 feet where refusal was encountered
upon a light tan weathered basalt The soil above the basalt is
a residual soil derived from the in place decomposition of lava
and contains layers of hard basalt varying from a few inches up
to one foot in thickness The hard rock layers were encountered

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The residual soils encountered in the vicinity of the roadway
expansion are competent soils which can provide adequate support
for the proposed structures and roadway
The following comments are presented to aid in the preliminary
design of the project
Foundation Design
The residual soils can provide adequate support for lightly
loaded structures with essentially no settlements It is recom
mended that a preliminary value of 3000 psf be utilized as the
maximum allowable bearing pressure This value can be increased
by one third when considering seismic and wind effects
Normal foundation construction procedures can be utilized The
foundations should be a minimum of 12 inches below the surrounding
surface and should maintain a minimum setback distance of 4 feet
between the outer edge of the foundation and the face of any slope

The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to the place
ment of the concrete for soft spots or other deleterious material
which must be removed
Site Grading
The existing soils are stable at the present slopes and will pro
vide a competent base for any additional fills Fill placed on
slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5 1 should
be properly keyed and benched to assure adequate bond between the
two materials
Any fill placed at the site should be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum density established by ASTM Test D 1557
72T
The on site soils can be utilized as fill providing the in place
moisture content is reduced to near the optimum moisture content
approximately 24 to facilitate proper compaction
Retaining Wall
On site soil utilized as fill behind retaining walls should be
compacted to 90 percent of the maximum density as determined
above It is recommended that the retaining walls be designed
for an equivalent fluid pressure equal to a fluid density of 35

Roadway Pavement
The CBR value was determined for a recompacted soil sample taken
from the vicinity of Boring 3 and gave a CBR value of three for
the saturated soil This illustrates that the on site soils are
sensitive to moisture changes and will have a low strength when
saturated It does appear that a select subgrade will be
required for the roadway design
Based on our experience with similar soils the following pavement
section is recommended for your preliminary evaluation
Asphaltic Concrete 2 Inches
Crushed Rock Base 6 Inches
Subbase Course 18 Inches
Slab On Grade
The laboratory testing indicates the on site soils are non expan
sive Slab on grade construction can therefore be utilized
without detrimental expansive affects It is recommended that
the slab on grade areas be inspected prior to the placement of

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 10 foot borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan and Boring
Locations Plate 1 included in the text of this report
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drill
ing operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphical representation of the soils encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 3
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
diameter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture and density determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification of the soils
The results of these test are presented on the Log of Borings at
the appropriate sample location
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a sample
from Boring 3 to measure the soil response under vertical pavement
loadings The CBR test resulted in a CBR value of 3
