Dear Ms Yanagawa
As part of our on going construction monitoring works at the Sea Country Area 7
project in Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii we have determined the presence of
expansive materials at the finished grade within Phase I Increment 3 of the subdivision
Reference 2
The laboratory ring swell tests performed on surface soil samples taken at the
above subject lots indicated expansion potentials ranging from medium 5 10 to very
high 15 Based on these test results we recommend the stripping of these expansive
soils where they are encountered to at least 3 feet below finish grade or until dense
competent coralline materials are encountered whichever comes first
Over excavation may be limited within the footprint of the proposed residential
structures extending at least 3 feet laterally from all sides of the building line Driveways
located within these expansive materials should also be over excavated by at least 1 5 feet
below finish grade
Structural fill should be used to replace the over excavated expansive soils and
should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt or
cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles no
larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of
20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in
