In general the Ewa Mall Expansion project involves expansion of the western
Ewa end of the Ala Moana Center in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
area was formerly occupied by Sears department store and mall parking and will be
converted to four levels of retail development In accordance with the project design
requirements a static compressive pile load test was conducted on a dedicated augered
cast in place ACIP pile on October 4 and 5 2013 This letter report presents the results
of our engineering analysis field observations and test data obtained from the static
compressive pile load test The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Previously we provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the ACIP
foundation design and our recommendations were presented in the letter report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Ewa Mall Expansion Ala Moana Center Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii dated
The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of
approximately 86 1 feet relative to the existing ground surface The load test pile was
installed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company HDCC using a crane mounted
drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the drilling depth drilling
rate auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis HDCC used continuous
flight augers to drill the pile As the augers were removed the hole was grouted from the
bottom upwards After sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the
top of the pile a reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole
To provide support for the load test two 18 inch ACIP reaction piles approximately
85 feet long were installed in similar fashion as the load test pile with 3 inch diameter
center bars in lieu of a rebar cage The reaction piles and load test pile were completed

To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load
test pile was instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain gages Model
No VCE 4200 manufactured by Geokon The strain gauges were connected using a
multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 data acquisition system to record the strains during the
compressive load test The embedment strain gages were installed in the load test pile at
the following depths below the existing ground surface

The piling subcontractor HDCC provided the load testing equipment including a
hydraulic jack hydraulic pump reaction beam reference beams and other accessories
Based on the calibration data provided by HDCC an equation relating the applied load
kips to the hydraulic pump pressure psi was generated and used during the load
testing
As requested Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a
multiplexer a data logger and data acquisition program to collect the strain gage readings
during the load testing The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses and
evaluation of the static pile load test In addition displacements at the top of the load test
pile were measured and recorded by Geolabs
The load test pile was loaded and reloaded in general accordance with the Quick
Load Test Method ASTM D1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface
conditions at the project site In general the test pile was loaded to about 1 060 kips in
approximately 55 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least
15 minutes or until the rate of the pile head movement generally was stable In addition
the static load test was held for approximately 1 5 hours at the 440 kip load increment and
4 hours at the 700 kip and 930 kip load increments to evaluate the effects of creep After
