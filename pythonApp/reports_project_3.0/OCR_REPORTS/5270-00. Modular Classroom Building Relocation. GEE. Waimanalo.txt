In accordance with our proposal dated May 27 2004 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Modular Classroom Building
Relocation for Parents and Children Together PACT project in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the
project site and presents our findings and recommendations for the proposed project
These findings and recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that the project involves
relocating the existing modular building for Parents and Children Together PACT to the
proposed new location The project site is located on the southern side of Waakea Street
behind the 7 Eleven Facility in Waimanalo on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed
new building location is within the same property lot as the existing building location and
approximately 85 feet to the northwest of the existing building site The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information
obtained was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations slabs on grade
retaining walls site grading and other geotechnical aspects only To accomplish this we
conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled
at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density plasticity and strength characteristics
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the
field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 including one hydrometer test
ASTM D 422 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation
characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the test
results are provided on Plate 7
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate 8

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we recommend
that shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and or continuous footings be used
to support the relocated building planned for the project An allowable bearing pressure of
up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations of the
structure bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills The bottom of
the footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grades Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations
for design of building foundations site grading and other geotechnical engineering aspects
are presented in the following sections of this letter report
Building Foundations
As previously mentioned we understand that it is planned to relocate a modular
building to the proposed location at the project site Based on the relatively competent
subsurface soil condition encountered on the project site we recommend that the
proposed structure be supported on shallow foundations consisting of spread and or
continuous footings An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for design of footings bearing on well compacted structural fill or in situ soils
The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for
transient loads such as those caused by wind and seismic forces The bottom of the
footing should be embedded a minimum of 6 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm or dense materials The resulting
excavations should be backfilled with granular fill material compacted to a minimum of

90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
the soils as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Foundations located adjacent to slopes or on sloping ground should be embedded
a sufficient depth to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 6 feet measured
from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope If foundations are located
next to utility trenches and easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal
to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench Alternatively the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
appreciable foundation settlements
If the foundation is designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements of the foundations bearing on the
well compacted structural fill or in situ soils to be less than 1 inch Differential settlements
between footings are estimated to be on the order of 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and bearing materials and by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on well compacted structural
fill or in situ soils Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf for footings
embedded in the well compacted structural fills or in situ soils This value assumes that
the concrete for footings is placed directly against the well compacted structural fill
materials or in situ soils Unless covered by pavement or slab the passive resistance in
the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the construction of
the modular building Based on our field and laboratory test results we believe that the
building slab may be constructed on grade provided that the slab subgrade is properly
prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented below and in the
Site Grading section of this report

In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill layer consisting
of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to
provide uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary moisture break To reduce
the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel cushion fill layer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered on the floor
slab because they can better mask minor slab cracking
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds
per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted aggregate
subbase Where slabs are intended to function as rigid pavements a minimum slab
thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design purposes In addition provisions
should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints that will be subjected to
vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
In general we recommend that concrete slabs on grade for exterior concrete
walkways be supported on at least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill or
aggregate subbase Furthermore crack control joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections The area adjacent to
the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with low expansion relatively
impervious soils and graded to divert water away from the slabs to reduce the potential for
water ponding around the slab edges
Slab subgrades should be prepared by scarifying the subgrade soils to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioning the soils to above the optimum moisture content
and proof rolling the subgrade with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Slab subgrades should be kept moist until
covered by concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks and
recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete placement Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage
may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
Site Grading
In general areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch
loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete or Controlled Low
Strength Material CLSM under low pressure
The areas to receive fill and finished subgrades should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site sandy soils may be re used as a source of
general fill or backfill materials Imported fill materials may be used at the project site
Imported general fill materials needed for the site filling should consist of materials with a
low expansion potential Imported general fill materials should consist of materials with
particle sizes of 3 inches or less in maximum dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR greater than 12 and a swell potential of less
than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Select borrow subbase and aggregate base course materials should meet the
material requirements in Sections 30 and 31 respectively as specified in the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986 Imported fill materials should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for the intended use
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The general
fills and backfills within the upper 3 feet of the pavement subgrade should be compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Aggregate base course and select borrow
subbase materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a

Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavements are planned to serve the
relocated building In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the project
primarily will consist of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks Based on the
near surface soil conditions encountered in our field exploration we believe that the
following flexible pavement section may be used for preliminary design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrades below pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled
with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to achieve a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction In addition the proof rolling operations also would serve to assist in detecting
the presence of potential near surface cavities or loose zones The aggregate base
course should consist of crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above pavement sections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to
carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site
during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade soil below the aggregate base course layer to reduce the
potential for migration of excess landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively
a subdrain system could be constructed to collect the excess water from the landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system be
constructed adjacent to the paved landscaped areas

Underground Utility Lines
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below new utility pipes planned at the
project site Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation
of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and placement could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils with a
maximum particle size of 6 inches The trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below building
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement surface should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
