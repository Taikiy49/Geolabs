DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain
by a thin surface layer of gray highly to critically expan
sive Adobe clay about 1 5 to 2 5 feet thick over dense
weathered rock These Adobe clay soils are subject to
large shrink swell movements and therefore foundations
should not be placed directly on these soils Overexca
vation of the Adobe clay is recommended for this lot
Foundations
Slab on grade construction is planned for the proposed
residence
The thin surface Adobe clay layer should be removed down
to dense weathered rock below the foundations slabs
walkways and driveways This Adobe clay overexcavation
should extend for a distance of 5 feet beyond the proposed
structures After Adobe clay removal the foundations
and slabs could bear on the underlying dense weathered
rock Where required select non expansive borrow fill
material could be used to raise the finished grade as
desired
The slab perimeter footing should be embedded a minimum
of 8 inches below the adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 24 P 5
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used on the
dense weathered rock and 2500 p s f on compacted fill
The backfill of utility line trenches close to the foundations
should be well compacted to reduce settlement due to settle
ment of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the
house to reduce ponding of surface run off water and
possible saturation and settlement or heaving of the
foundation soils
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the
slab and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a poly
ethylene moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
slab The membrane should be protected by a thin layer
of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Site Grading
The existing terrain near the proposed house location
A low cut slope may be required for the backyard develop
ment
Based on the field exploration we anticipate that the
upper 1 to 2 feet of the cut would be in Adobe clay
while the lower portion would encounter weathered rock

The thin Adobe clay layer should be stripped off or
flattened as much as possible to reduce later sloughing
or downhill slope creep Cuts in dense weathered rock
could be made at slope ratios of 1 1 H V
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material Adobe clay or material contaminated
with Adobe clay should not be used for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557

recommendations is included in the project plans and speci
