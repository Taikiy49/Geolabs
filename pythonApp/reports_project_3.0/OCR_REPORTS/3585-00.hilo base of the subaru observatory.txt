Foundation
Based on our review of the boring logs prepared by Harding Lawson Associates
and our site reconnaissance the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site
consist generally of basalt rock formation with occasional cavities and or voids of
approximately 0 5 to 2 feet in vertical height We believe that the on site basalt rock
formation would provide suitable foundation support for the proposed building structures
Therefore we recommend that shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on
basalt rock formation be used to support the proposed buildings An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 6 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design of footings
bearing on the basalt rock formation This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Because some portions of the building are located over fill areas deeper
footing embedments may be necessary for the footings to bear on the basalt rock
formation A representative from Geolabs Hawaii should observe the footing excavations
prior to placement of reinforcements and or concrete to confirm the bearing materials and
embedment depths
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward
from the bottom of the below grade structure If the footings are designed in accordance
with the recommendations presented herein total settlement of footings is estimated to
be on the order of inch with differential settlements on the order of inch
In order to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of cavities and or voids below footings we recommend that a program of probing
and grouting at the footing locations be implemented during construction Special
attention should be given to the Foundation Probing section of this report In addition
special attention should also be given to the site preparation recommendations presented
in the Site Grading section of this letter report

Lateral loads acting on the buildings may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 5 may be used for footings bearing on the basalt rock
formation Resistance to lateral loads due to passive pressure may be calculated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 400 pounds per cubic foot p c f for footings embedded in
structural fill material and or basalt formation These values assume that the concrete for
footings is cast neat against the footing excavation or the soil around the footing is well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches below finished grade should be neglected

In order to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of cavities and or voids below a footing we recommend that a program of cavity
probing and grouting the foundation locations be conducted during construction As a
minimum we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each spread footing location up
to 100 square feet in bearing area and 10 foot on center for continuous footings The
probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet
below the planned bottom of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or
suspected during the probing operation additional probes should be drilled at closer
spacing to help delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void
Additional probes should also be drilled for spread footings with over 100 square feet of
bearing area
The probes are generally backfilled to aid in the detection of cavities and or voids
Probes and any cavities voids discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout
injected at low to moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of
1 part sand 1 part cement and 0 7 part water As an alternative a sand cement mixture
with a slump range between 6 and 9 inches may also used
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of
cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take and or
changed conditions are observed
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a longer

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed structures
For slabs on grade located entirely over basalt rock cuts or entirely on structural fill we
recommend that the concrete slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion
fill The cushion fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of
moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the
slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a
2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab
concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
Based on the grading plan provided the proposed two story building will straddle
the cut and fill transition To reduce the potential for differential settlement between the
portion of the slab located above the basalt rock cut and the portion above the fill area
we recommend that the slab on grade bear on a more uniform material to reduce the
potential for differential movements between dissimilar subgrade materials To provide
more uniform support of the slab located above the cut and fill transition these slabs on
grade should bear on 8 inches of cushion fill in lieu of the typical 4 inch cushion fill The
vapor membrane and 2 inch sand is also recommended for slabs with moisture sensitive
surfacing

Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 2 foot zone of permeable
material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately around
a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall discharging to an
appropriate outlet As an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as
MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used instead of the drainage material The
prefabricated drainage product should also be hydraulically connected to a
perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the
design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a
rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 30 percent of the vertical
surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall which is free to deflect
cantilever may be used in design For walls that are restrained a rectangular
distribution equal to 45 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting over the
entire height of the wall may be used for design Additional analyses during design
may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and line loads
Dynamic Lateral Earth Pressures
Since the project site is located in Seismic Zone 3 in accordance with the Uniform
Building Code dynamic lateral earth pressures will need to be considered in the
design of retaining walls Dynamic lateral earth pressures due to seismic loading
may be estimated by 10H p s f rectangular pressure distribution for
level backfill where H is the height of the wall in feet For a 2H 1V sloping backfill
condition a dynamic lateral earth pressure of 15H p s f should be used in design
The dynamic lateral earth pressures presented above are in addition to the static
lateral earth pressures An appropriately reduced factor of safety may be used
when dynamic lateral earth pressures are accounted for in the design of the
retaining wall
Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate that the proposed site grading will
consist of minor cuts and fills on the order of 2 to 6 feet relative to the existing ground
surface The following guidelines are provided for design of site grading and site
preparation

Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to evaluate
whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and proof
rolling process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in the field exploration
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish surface ash soils and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce
potential contamination of the excavated materials
In areas to receive fill or finished subgrades in cuts the subgrade should be proof
rolled with heavy equipment such as a 10 ton vibratory drum roller a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or similar heavy equipment a minimum of eight passes to assist in detecting
and possibly collapsing near surface cavities The heavy equipment used for proof rolling
the subgrades should be traveling not faster than 100 feet per minute If cavities are
detected and or suspected during the proof rolling process the cavity should be ripped
open and backfilled with structural fill material
The fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill material should consist of
well graded granular materials in which the majority of the material has a maximum size
of less than 12 inches with an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches The on site
cut and or stockpiled material if less than 18 inches in maximum dimension may be used
as general fill or backfill materials To facilitate construction of utilities boulders should
not be placed within the limits of the proposed utility corridors Fill slopes may be
designed with an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
All fill areas should be capped with a 12 inch thick layer of structural fill material
The structural fill material should consist of well graded granular material of 6 inches in
maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a
or boulders should be considered to provide the required gradation and particle sizes to
develop a well graded material Any imported material if necessary should be observed
and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being transported to the site

General fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 24 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Structural fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Where fills are composed of rock boulders and or cobbles conventional
compaction testing is not practicable Therefore a testing program to evaluate the
number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction
Based on the field exploration the site is underlain by slightly to severely fractured
volcanic rock ranging from medium hard to hard It is anticipated that the volcanic rock
may be excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with large
bulldozers where the rock is near the existing ground surface However excavations into
the deeper formations may require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing rock formations and field data from the borings
performed at the site We recommend that all contractors proposing to bid on this project
be encouraged to examine the site conditions and the boring data to make their own
interpretation
Based on our field exploration we believe that the cut slopes exposing dense
basalt formation may be designed with an inclination of or flatter If clinker
zones and or cavities are encountered at the cut slope face the designed cut slopes may
need to be flattened Large cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should be
backfilled and or grouted if necessary Geolabs Hawaii should be present during
construction to evaluate if the exposed materials are consistent with our assumptions or if
the cut slopes need to be flattened

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical

different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
