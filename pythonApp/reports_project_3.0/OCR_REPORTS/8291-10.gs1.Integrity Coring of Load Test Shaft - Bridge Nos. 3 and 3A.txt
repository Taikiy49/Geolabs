This memorandum provides the results of the integrity coring performed on the
Load Test Shaft for Bridge No. 3 for the above project. Installation of the Load Test
Shaft for Bridge No. 3 was completed on March 29, 2022. Based on the results of the
Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing, integrity coring of the Load Test Shaft
extending to a depth of 50 feet at two locations was recommended. The design 28-day
concrete compressive strength for the drilled shaft is 5,000 pounds per square inch
(psi).
The Load Test Shaft was cored to a depth of about 50 feet below the top of the
shaft concrete surface on April 20 and 21, 2022. The coring was performed at two
locations. The first coring location was between CSL tubes 2 and 3 [Core 3 (2-3)], and the
second location was between CSL tubes 3 and 4 [Core 3 (3-4)].
Based on visual observations of the recovered cores, low RQD values and a void
were encountered when the core hole was drilled through the abandoned CSL tube in the
shaft for Core 3 (2-3). For Core 3 (3-4), steel rebar was encountered about 37 feet below
the top of the shaft. It appears that the diagonal bracing steel within the rebar cage was
encountered in the coring. The coring logs are presented on Plates A-1.1 through A-2.2. In
addition, photographs of the recovered concrete core samples are presented on Plates 3.1
through 3.6.
We performed uniaxial compressive strength tests on 15 recovered core samples
from the shaft as presented on Plate 4. Six compressive strength tests were performed
within the upper 5 feet of the shaft. The compressive strength tests were conducted on
May 3, 2022, corresponding to 35 days after shaft concrete placement.
Based on the compression test results, concrete compressive strength at the top
5 feet of Core 2-3 and Core 3-4 ranged from 1,290 to 6,200 psi. The average compressive

The average compressive strength of the core samples from 10 feet and below was 
5,768 psi. 
Based on ACI 318, the average compressive strength of the concrete cores should 
be greater than or equal to 85 percent of the design compressive strength. In addition, no 
compressive strength of the cores should be less than 75 percent of the design 
compressive strength. For the design compressive strength of 5,000 psi, 85 and 
75 percent of the design compressive strength is 4,250 psi and 3,750 psi, respectively. 
Based on the test results, the average compressive strength of the cores in the 
upper 5 feet does not meet the minimum 4,250 psi requirement. In addition, the lowest 
break cores in the upper 5 feet does not meet the minimum 3,750 psi requirement. 
Therefore, the shaft concrete in the upper portion of the Load Test Shaft for Bridge No. 3 
does not meet the project requirements. 
It should be noted that the specified overpour and cut down method in the project 
specifications was not performed on the Load Test Shaft. Instead, the contractor 
overpoured the concrete and removed the concrete soon after. Therefore, the overpour 
and cut down method specified in the project specifications shall be performed for the 
production shafts or the production shafts will be rejected.
