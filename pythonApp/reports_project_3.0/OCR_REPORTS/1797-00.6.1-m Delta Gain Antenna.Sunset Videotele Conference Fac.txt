PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 16 5 by 16 5 square foot reinforced
concrete mat foundation to mount a 6 1l meter dish antenna
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on a ridge between Paumalu Gulch and
Kaunala Gulch above Sunset Beach on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The proposed antenna site is located in the existing COMSAT site on
the ridge and at about 3 to 13 feet east of the existing Administration
and Control Building Further east at about 18 feet of the proposed
antenna location an approximately 8 foot wide open drainage ditch extends
along the toe of an upward slope with a slope ratio of about H V
The topography at the site is flat but the ground elevations are
high at about 445 feet above Mean Sea Level
The ground presently unoccupied is heavily vegetated with wild
grasses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two 2 borings were drilled at the site at the approximate
locations indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2 Both borings were drilled
at the antenna mat foundation locations and extended to about 15 feet
below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent subsoil conditions for
support of the proposed antenna using the planned 16 5 by 16 5
square foot mat foundation
Mat Foundation
A maximum allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used for
the mat foundation on the stiff to hard residual soil Minimal
settlements are anticipated for the structure loads
lateral loads acting on the proposed antenna may be resisted by
passive earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
mat foundation and by frictional resistance developed between the bottom
of the mat foundation and the bearing soil
Design lateral earth pressures unit weights and coefficient of
friction are as follows
a Active Soil Pressure 35 p c f
b Passive Soil Pressure 300 p c f
c Soil Moist Unit Weight 105 p c f
d Coefficient of Friction 0 4

Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the mat foundation of the proposed dish antenna The bottom of the
excavation should be thoroughly tamped prior to the placement of the mat
foundation
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of backfill material All imported
fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or
granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve All backfill materials should be placed in maximum of
8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 95 of the maximum density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation support of the antenna should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the excavations for the antenna foundation be
observed by the soils engineer prior to placement of concrete or
reinforcing steel to check that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface evaluation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ee say ym en me wie ets
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were futher
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
