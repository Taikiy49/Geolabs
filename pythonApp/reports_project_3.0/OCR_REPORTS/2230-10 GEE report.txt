INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed renovation and expansion of Hookipa Cottage facility at the
Women s Community Correctional Center WCCC within the Koolau Correctional
Complex in Kailua Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents Our
geotechnical engineering recommendations based on the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended to provide design input for the site
grading and foundation design and may not be adequate for construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed site improvements consist of several irregular shaped buildings to
the south of the existing Hookipa Cottage in a roughly rectangular shaped area about
900 feet in length and about 500 feet wide
Based on the plans provided to us housing units consisting of 1 to 2 story
reinforced concrete buildings are planned and will be constructed on fill embankment
across drainageways or on relatively level ground
Access roads are planned in the northern portion of the site in the future for the
second phase of the construction Also planned for the second phase of the construction
are a water storage tank and a pump station to the east of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2230 10 Page 3
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soil conditions at the site to provide geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the site grading infrastructure improvements and foundation design
for this project
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated November
3 1989
The scope of work was limited to the following tasks and work efforts
A Logging and monitoring of the drilling and sampling of twenty 20 borings
by a drilling company under separate contract to the Department of
Accounting and General Services to depths ranging between 10 0 and 41 0
feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the site and its surroundings to observe the general
topographical and surface conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its Surroundings
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the exploration
to aid in determining their engineering properties and classification

5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
geotechnical recommendations for site grading pavement and foundation
design of this project
6 Preparation of this report to present our findings and design
recommendations
Details of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in the
appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located about 3 000 feet southeast of the intersection of Kailua Road
and Kalanianaole Highway in Kailua Oahu Hawaii Mount Olomana lies about one mile
south of this 9 5 acre site
The site generally slopes down in a northerly direction The western and eastern
portions slope down at an approximate gradient of 4 from about elevation 210 to 190
feet The middle portion of the site is characterized by a drainageway with a maximum
elevation difference of about 14 feet The side slopes of this drainageway have a slope
ratio varying from ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V to three horizontal to one vertical
3H 1V This drainageway slopes down towards the north at an average gradient of 4
The bottom elevation varies from elevation 210 feet on the upper southern end to
elevation 174 feet at the lower northern section
It appears that the proposed buildings will be sited in the southern two thirds of the
site which is currently open area covered with grass

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The soil conditions encountered by the twenty 20 borings generally indicate that
the site is underlain by residual soils developed from the in situ weathering of Koolau
Basalts Borings drilled in the high ground at the western and the eastern portions of the
site encountered stiff to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soils with high in situ water
contents ranging from 30 to 70 percent Similar clayey silt strata were encountered in the
drainageway area in Boring 13 and 15 However our exploration and
laboratory testing indicate that the soils encountered in the drainageway have lower
strength and higher compressibility than the soils in the upper areas
Groundwater was encountered at elevations ranging from 134 5 to 165 0 feet MSL
in Boring and 16 during our exploration
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
samples obtained during the exploration The results of the laboratory testing are
presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
point of view provided that the recommendations presented in this report are closely
followed in design and during construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2230 10 Page 6
Our exploration encountered moderately compressible medium stiff to stiff deposits
of residual clayey silt with moderate swelling potential It appears that these soils are
generally competent and will provide adequate bearing to support the proposed 1 to
2 story buildings on spread or continuous footing foundations provided that our
recommendations are followed
Ground Settlement
Significant fill ground settlements are anticipated in the drainageway area due to
the loads which will be imposed by the new fill placed over the relatively thick moderately
compressible soils The estimated settlements due to the placement of up to 14 feet of
fill are onthe order of 2 to 4 inches The estimated time required to achieve 95 of the
estimated settlement is roughly four 4 to six 6 months
It is therefore recommended that site grading within the drainageway area be
completed at least four 4 months prior to construction of the structures and utilities
Otherwise a preloading or surcharge program will be required to shorten the waiting
period These recommendations are intended to reduce the amount of settlement which
the proposed structures and utilities located within this area may experience
Settlement Monitoring
It is recommended that an adequate number of settlement gauges be installed prior
to and after completion of site filling within the drainageway area to monitor the rate and
magnitude of the fill ground settlements Construction of buildings utilities retaining walls
and pavements should not commence until readings obtained from the installed
settlement gauges indicate that the remaining settlements will be tolerable to the proposed
facilities Based on the soil data obtained from the exploration it is estimated that the
waiting period of fill ground settlement would be roughly about four 4 to six 6 months

Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and
spoil material should be properly disposed off site or stockpiled for use in
landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas exposed during the clearing
operations should be over excavated to firm ground and then backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Fill Material
Areas requiring fill should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered during re compaction
should be over excavated down to firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
engineered fill material
Generally the on site soils appear suitable for use as engineered fill Since most
of the on site soils are fine grained they should be compacted by mechanical
compaction as compaction by water jetting or ponding is not suitable
If additional imported material is required it should be select non expansive
material which contains no fragments larger than three 3 inches in maximum
dimension with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index less
than 20 as determined by ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 Import material should
be free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material and should be
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Fill Placement
Our exploration and laboratory testing indicate that the on site soils have in situ
moisture contents on the order of 20 to 30 higher than optimum moisture content
for compaction These high in situ moisture contents reduce the workability of
soils and frequently necessitate double or triple handling of the soils to reduce the
moisture to a level where the standard 90 compaction may be achieved
Previous experience in similar areas with relatively high rainfall and high in situ soil
moisture contents indicates that a reduction of the level of compaction to 85 can
still provide adequate bearing strength to support relatively light structures
Therefore to facilitate earthwork construction for this project we are
recommending a reduced 85 compaction criteria
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Fill placement should commence at the lowest
point and continue upward in level compacted layers
Any fill placed on existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing subsoils This
is graphically illustrated on Plate 3 Sketch of Keying and Benching
Cut areas designated as structural or pavement areas should be excavated to
subgrade level then scarified and recompacted a minimum of 85 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
To intercept potential groundwater seepage at the base of the drainageway fill and
to reduce the potential of piping failures provisions should be made to install

Building Foundations
It is Our opinion that spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed buildings An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on the residual soil and on fill placed as recommended above
These bearing values are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated footing settlements
are on the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch differential assuming
the initial site filling is completed first and that the building construction is delayed until the
fill settlements have reached tolerable limits For more details refer to the section under
Ground Settlement
Individual spread footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded to a
minimum depth of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade It is
recommended that the building foundations be located away from the top of fill cut slopes
with a minimum set back of about ten 10 feet from the outer edge of the footing to the
outside face of the slope Additional footing embedment would be required for the
footings located closer than ten 10 feet from the slope
lf foundations are located next to utility easements they should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are feasible for the floors of the proposed buildings A
4 inch cushion layer consisting of 3 Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
Where slabs are situated partly on cut and partly on fill there is a potential for
some slab cracking due to the difference in stiffness between the two materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2230 10 Page 14
In order to reduce the differential settlement between portions of the slab on cut
and fill we recommend that the cut portion be over excavated for a minimum depth of
one 1 foot below the slab bottom and replaced with properly compacted select material
This assumes that a 3 foot thick layer of select material is present below the finish grade
at the fill area as recommenced in the Site Grading section
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended to be installed below the
slabs
The slabs on grade should have thickened edges which extend down a minimum
of 12 inches below the outside finish ground surface elevations
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is recommended that gutter
systems be installed on the proposed buildings and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided
Planters or landscaping next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are important for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting of

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from a limited number of field data points such as
borings Significant variations of conditions between the field data points may exist
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until further
boring exploration or during construction If these variations are encountered it will
be necessary to update the recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined by taping
from objects indicated on the Topographic Survey Map provided by Hawaii State
Department of Accounting and General Services Division of Public Works on June
23 1989 Elevations of the data points were estimated from the same plan Their
accuracy should be considered only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between different soil strata and may also
denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are
based upon interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual
subsurface conditions
