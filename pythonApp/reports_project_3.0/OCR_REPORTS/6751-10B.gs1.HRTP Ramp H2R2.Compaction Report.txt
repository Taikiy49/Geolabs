This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading construction for the Honolulu Rail Transit Project 
(HRTP) Ramp H2R2, Kamehameha Highway Station Group project from March 28, 2016 
to June 1, 2017 in accordance with our fee proposal dated December 8, 2015. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located near Pearl Highlands in the Pearl City area on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The new ramp is about 1,425 linear feet long and will provide 
access to the Pearl Highlands Station from Kamehameha Highway. The Project 
Location Map and Site Plans are provided as Plates 1, 2.1 and 2.2, respectively. 
Cuts of up to about 30 feet and fills up to about 12 feet will be required at the 
ramp site. In addition, a fill surcharge monitoring program was specified for the project. 
The results of the settlement monitoring were presented in our memorandum entitled 
“Settlement Gauge Readings, Ramp H2R2, HRTP – Kamehameha Highway Station 
Group, Island of Oahu, Hawaii,” dated October 5, 2016. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the HRTP Ramp H2R2 project from 
March 28, 2016 to June 1, 2017.  The site grading work consisted of excavation up to 
about 30 feet in height, and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 
12 feet thick to achieve the finished grades for the project. Royal Contracting Company 
Ltd. performed the site grading work. During the site grading operations, we obtained 
samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and 
transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum 
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM 
D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the pavement areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plate 4 of this report. These tests were performed on the 
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. 
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied 
by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on 
visual observation and hand probing. 
The contaminated materials on-site were removed prior to fill placement. Wet 
subgrade conditions were encountered along Interstate Route H-2 and near the Farrington 
Highway overpass.  The wet/soft subgrade was removed and replaced with compacted fill 
materials. Soft subgrade soil conditions were encountered along the southern side of 
Ramp H2R2 from approximately Sta. 21+15 to Sta. 21+50. The installation of a working 
platform consisting of aggregate subbase material underlain by geogrid and geotextile 
fabric was recommended over the existing subgrade soils.  
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, compaction procedures, and confirmation of subgrade 
soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed 
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report was performed in conformance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
