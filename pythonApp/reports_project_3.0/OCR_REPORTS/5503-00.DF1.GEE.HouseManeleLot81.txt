Project Considerations
The proposed project involves the construction of a luxury garden single family
residence on Manele Lot 81 on the Island of Lanai Hawaii Based on the available
conceptual site plan provided for the preparation of this report the residence will be a
three bedroom home with a detached garage and detached guest quarters It is
anticipated that the home will enclose about 4 000 square feet of floor space The
conceptual site plans also indicate the use of low retaining walls at various locations to
provide grade separation
The project will involve site grading and infrastructure installation including a
short driveway sidewalks and utilities Since the site is relatively level we anticipate
that the site grading work will generally consist of shallow cuts and fills less than 4 to
5 feet in height to provide level pads for the home the garage and the guest quarters

Regional Geology
The Island of Lanai was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from a
single shield volcano The volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
in age As the volcanic activity ceased the central portion subsided forming a low
basin now known as the Palawai Basin Block faulting on the southern flank of the
island formed a broad graben leading from the Palawai Basin to the south to Manele
Bay The project site is located within the Manele Graben
2 2
Site Description
The project site is located at Lot 81 in Manele on the Island of Lanai Hawaii
The site consists of a single parcel of land with an irregular polygonal shape It appears
that the site was previously graded by the placement of rock fill materials At present
the site is an open area surrounded by low grasses and dryland vegetation such as
keawe trees A shallow ditch separates the site from the adjacent road
In general the site slopes down gently towards the southeast Based on the
topographic information of the conceptual site plan the existing ground surface
elevation at the site ranges from approximately 324 to 340 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
2 3
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths of about
20 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface layer of rock fill material
ranging in thickness from about 2 5 to 3 5 feet overlying slightly weathered basalt rock
formation which was penetrated to the maximum depth explored of about 20 feet below
the existing ground surface The basalt rock formation was moderately to severely
fractured Occasional outcroppings of basalt formation through the rock fill were
observed at several locations across the site

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
The occasional outcroppings of rock formation observed in the existing surface of
the site have the potential of inducing adverse differential loading on the proposed
residential structures i e point loading on slabs on grade or strip footing To
reduce the potential for distresses to the structures from such differential loading
we recommend that cut portions of the building site be scarified down to a minimum
of 18 inches below subgrade level and then recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction This scarification and recompaction should also
apply to the transition area between the cut and fill portions of the building pads
Based on the site conditions encountered we recommend that prior to the
placement of fill in areas designated to be filled the existing ground surface be
proof rolled with vibratory sheepsfoot compactor or similar heavy equipment for a
minimum of six passes to help verify that adequate support for the fill has been
achieved The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a
representative from Geolabs Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed
during clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with fill materials
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft
soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material

Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to the City and County of Honolulu Department of
Public Works Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated
September 1986
Structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and structural fill materials should be moisture conditioned to optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that cut
slopes would expose rock fill materials or in situ basalt formation In general we
believe that cut slopes into the rock fill material may be cut at an inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Cut slopes into the underlying
basalt formation may be cut at a steeper inclination of 1H 1V or flatter

Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
We recommend that fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed

requirements should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design
grade with sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for
erosion of the finished slopes

Building Foundations
Shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed residential structures provided they are embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of residential building foundations bearing on properly compacted fill
on site rock fill or basalt formation This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade For footings
constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground the footings should be embedded
deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured from
the outside edge of the footings to the slope face
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fill on site
rock fill or in situ basalt formation to be less than 1 inch total Differential settlement is
estimated to be less than ½ inch

If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be deepened to the
underlying firm materials
If in situ basalt rock formation protrudes into the foundation excavation the rock
formation should be removed to foundation subgrade level to reduce the potential of
inducing point loading stresses on the foundation
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 psf This assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively
undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected For footings bearing on
properly compacted fill or on site stiff silty clay a coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be
used
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc prior to placement of reinforcement steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Retaining Structures
Retaining walls may be required to provide grade separation in the proposed
residential development In general the following guidelines may be used for
preliminary design of the retaining structures
Wall Foundations
In general we believe that retaining wall foundations may be designed in
accordance with the Foundations section presented in this report The
foundations should have a minimum width of 18 inches and should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade on relatively flat
areas For sloping ground conditions the footings should extend deeper to obtain a
minimum 8 foot setback distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of
the footing to the face of the slope The horizontal setback distance may be
reduced to a minimum of 3 feet if the footings are embedded at least 12 inches into
in situ basalt rock formation Wall footings oriented parallel to the direction of the
slope should be constructed in stepped footings

Drainage
Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
placed immediately around a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the
retaining structure discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an
alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may
be used in lieu of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should
also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Backfill behind the permeable drainage zone may consist of granular fill less than
3 inches in maximum dimension Unless covered by concrete slabs the upper
12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively impervious materials to reduce the
potential for water infiltration behind the retaining structures

Utilities
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular
material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site
soils as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty clay soils or select
granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of
the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to
compact the materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not
be used to compact the backfill material

A P P E N D I X A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of three borings to depths of approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger equipment at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the field exploration operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System Logs of the materials encountered are
presented on Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designations D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket
penetrometer tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
