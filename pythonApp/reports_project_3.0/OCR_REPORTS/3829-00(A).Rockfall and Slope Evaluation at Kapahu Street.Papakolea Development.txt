SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Observations made at the site indicate that the potential risk
for future rockfall hazard is significant The major hazard is
from boulders rolling from the slope and impacting the
buildings at the site
Modification or stabilization of the slope would be extremely
difficult and costly due to the limited accessibility and existing
structures above and below the slope Therefore several
options related to various barricade structures and relocation
of the buildings on the lot are presented for further
consideration
It is our opinion that the safest and potentially least costly
option may be to set the houses back from the slope and
install a rigid concrete barrier near the toe of the existing
slope Other options include the installation of a chain link
fence and provision for a maintenance easement for the fence
and perimeter or the installation of a specialized flexible
fence barrier The flexible fence option would likely increase
construction costs while lowering maintenance costs
Additionally to reduce the possibility of experiencing more
frequent or larger rock falls or debris slides we recommend
implementing measures to reduce the surface storm water
from flowing over the top of the slope Furthermore we
recommend the removal of accumulated debris and rubbish
from the top edge of the slope
The text of this report should be referred to for more detailed
discussions and specific design recommendations

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the rockfall and slope evaluation project at Kapahu Street within the
Department of Hawaiian Home Lands Papakolea Development in Honolulu on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field reconnaissance and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended to assist in the review and selection of possible
remedial measures for slope stabilization and the protection of homes located below the
subject slope The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kapahu Street project site is located within the existing Department of
Hawaiian Home Lands Papakolea Subdivision development in the Papakolea Punchbowl
area of Honolulu on the island of Street runs approximately west
to east parallel to the base of a steep rocky hillside
Along the base of the hillside on the northerly side of the street six existing
residential lots have been affected by rockfall from the steep slope contained within their
backyard property limits The rockfall may be described as repeated occurrence of falling
and rolling boulders with some soil material from the steep slope The boulder and
cobble materials have rolled within the backyard areas and at times have struck the
building structures located on some the properties
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3829 00 A Page 3
Our reconnaissance of the site included visual observation of the six land parcels
identified as 42 12 through 17 This corresponds to Lot Nos 27 through 32 at
the site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Our site reconnaissance confirmed that
rockfall has occurred at the site and the potential exists for continued rockfall hazard
Large boulders were observed deposited on the generally flat ground surface of the
backyards located at the base of the steep slope The boulders reportedly had dislodged
from the rock slope during heavy rains and tumbled to the backyard area of some of the
subject properties
Based on our field observations we believe a hazard exists pertaining to the
occurrence of future rockfall events at this site The homes located at the base of the
slope reside in an area that could conceivably be directly impacted by rolling boulders
Based on the site geometry and geologic conditions it is our opinion that slope
modification or stabilization would be extremely difficult and costly however barrier
structures could be constructed to reduce the possibility of rockfall damage to property

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned to the island of
Oahu with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These most
recent volcanic series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series The Punchbowl
crater located to the west of the project site is one such extinct tuff and cinder cone
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 5
The project area is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Range and is
composed of basalt rock formation built by the extrusion of lavas of the Koolau Volcanic
Series These rocks are generally characterized by layered flows of jointed dense
vesicular basalt with interbedded clinker layers For the past 1 2 million years weathering
of the Koolau lava flows has occurred creating the valley and ridge topography visible
today on the southern side of the Koolau mountain range
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
mass wasting which is essentially the detachment of soil and rock masses from the
valley walls and transportation downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity
and deposited as colluvium Once these colluvial materials reach a stream typically in
the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the sediments are
transported and deposited as alluvium
The project site is located on the southern lower hillside flank of a prominent
ridgeline situated to the northwest of the Tantalus Makiki area The ridgeline is bounded
by Kanaha Stream to the east and Pauoa Valley to the west The Kapahu Street site is
located at the boundary of a gently sloping alluvial valley floor and a steep lower hillside
flank of the ridge mentioned previously The rockfall hazard is primarily due to the
existence of highly fractured basalt rock composing the steep hillside The subject slope
appears to have been formed by natural erosion of the area and may have been modified
steepened somewhat during development of the subdivision
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the break in slope between a steep hillside and a
gently sloping plain located adjacent to Kapahu Street and a portion of Anianiku Street
in the Papakolea area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii Six residential lots
have been affected by repeated rockfall emanating from the steep rock slope located
on the northern half of the properties as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 6
The existing homes were constructed on the level portion of the lots with the rear
of the homes approximately 10 to 20 feet away from the toe of the slope One of the
homes is situated only about 5 feet from the slope toe The close proximity of the
homes to the slope combined with the absence of a level cleared ground area
bordering the slope increases the risk that rolling boulders may impact the buildings
At the rear of the homes and fronting the toe of the slope an irregular loosely
stacked low rock wall exists which continues to act as the sole retaining structure for
debris and boulders rolling from the slope behind Behind the wall the ground slopes
upward moderately and is composed chiefly of colluvium consisting of some clayey soil
sand and boulder and cobble rock fragments The colluvial slope merges with the
base of the steep rock slope Based on the available regional topographic plan of the
area the rock slope resides at an inclination of about 4H 1V The rock slope extends
up to the rear of the subject property boundaries where there exists another break in
slope at the backyards of the hilltop homes The overall vertical height of the slope is
estimated to be about 50 feet
The rock formation composing the steep slope consists of layered basalt rock
with interbedded clinker seams The rock formation appears extremely fractured with
open joints and some overhanging blocks of dense rock material Much of the mid to
upper levels of the rock slope were obscured by dense vegetation making observation
difficult Based on our observations the rock slope shows the potential for future
dislodgment of boulders and rockfall hazard to the buildings at the base of the slope
Several boulders reported to be a result of recent rockfall were observed located
at the backyard areas The basalt boulders generally ranged in size from about 3 to
5 feet in diameter A single boulder located at 42 12 was observed resting
against the side of the home as a result of a recent rockfall The boulder measured
approximately 6 feet in diameter and was generally round in shape The boulder
reportedly had hit the side of the home but did not break the wall The boulders
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3829 00 A Page 7
observed at the site were round in shape and generally composed of dense rock
material Based on our observation of the highly fractured character of the rock
formation many of the larger boulders that dislodge and roll downslope are believed to
break into smaller boulders and cobbles to form the colluvial talus apron located at the
toe of the slope However some of the denser rock blocks that dislodge from the slope
do not break into smaller fragments and are more likely to reach the backyard area of
the homes
Our observations indicate that one of the residents has begun to excavate and
terrace the moderately sloping colluvial embankment at the rear of their lot The effort
is being made to reduce the potential for loose boulders to strike their home by allowing
the boulder an area to dissipate energy after falling from the steep slope The
observations were made at 42 16
Due to the geometry of the slope and location of the homes access to the slope
is difficult especially at 42 15 where the home is located within 5 feet from
the toe of the colluvial slope
Based on our observations of the fallen boulders at the site it is our opinion that
the main hazard associated with the rockfall condition is the potential for fallen boulders
to roll towards and impact the buildings located near the toe of the slope Direct impact
from falling boulders does not appear to be a significant hazard due to the geometry of
the slope Therefore our focus has been directed towards evaluating potential
solutions to reduce the hazard of rolling boulders at the site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The rockfall hazard at the site does not appear to be related to a particular
drainage problem but instead appears to be the result of natural weathering and mass
wasting processes associated with the evolution of the slope Storm water runoff appears
to be the mechanism which initiates many of the rockfall events however signs of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 8
concentrated water flow erosion from the top of the slope were not observed during our
site visit Therefore we believe the rockfall occurring at the site is a naturally occurring
hazard induced as a result of the strongly fractured rock condition and steepness of the
slope Because the rockfall events are known to be related to heavy rainfall episodes it
may be inferred that storm water runoff contributes to the rock dislodgment and
movement of materials downslope
Based on our observations and communication with area residents it is our
opinion that the major rockfall events tend to occur more frequently during periods of
prolonged rainfall of moderate to high intensity Once the ground surface at the top of the
slope becomes saturated it is conceivable that the surface flow of storm water runoff may
increase and begin to flow over the top of the slope which in turn may contribute to the
dislodgment of boulders from the slope It should be noted that observations of this
occurrence have not been made directly therefore it is not presently known the role that
the potentially significant storm water flow may play in contributing to the rock
dislodgment
Furthermore our observations of the top of the slope have revealed that a
significant amount of greenwaste and rubbish has accumulated at portions of the top of
the slope These materials should be removed and the area kept clear of debris
accumulation The accumulation of said debris is of concern in that should extraordinarily
heavy storm water flows be experienced the debris could be mobilized by storm water
flow and cascade over the slope thereby significantly increasing the potential for the
dislodgment of boulders
In summary of the above there is no direct evidence that storm water flows from
the top of the slope are the primary driving force behind the erosion and rockfall
experienced at the site However the storm water flows associated with high intensity
rainfall events may be contributing to the acceleration of erosion therefore it may be
prudent to reduce the potential for storm water runoff to flow over the top of the slope In
addition to implementing measures to reduce the storm water flow at the top of the slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 9
we recommend that the accumulations of debris and rubbish be removed These top of
slope remedial measures in combination with the measures presented below should aid
in reducing the potential for rockfall damage
Based on our observation of the site conditions we believe there are several
options available to reduce the hazard to property associated with the rockfall at this site
Each option contains advantages and disadvantages and various cost factors to
consider before an ultimate method can be selected for implementation Based on the
existing site conditions there appears to be no single method that is feasible for a
complete reduction of risk from the rockfall hazard However implementing one or more
of the following mitigative measures at the site may reduce the risk The following options
are provided to reduce the potential risk of rockfall on the buildings at the site
sweep loose rock outcroppings from the entire slope
e install a wire mesh netting to retain the slope
e install a rigid barrier such as portable concrete barricades
e relocate buildings further away from the slope
e relocate buildings and install portable concrete barricades
e install a semi rigid barrier such as a chain link fence barrier
e install a flexible barrier fence at the toe of the slope
Boulder Sweep
This option involves mechanically removing loose boulders or rock outcroppings
from the slope to reduce the amount of material available as rockfall source The major
constraint for this option is the limited access to the site from the bottom of the slope The
close proximity of the houses to the slope generally precludes access to the slope for
heavy equipment to perform the sweep Additionally the sweep is not advisable due to
the close proximity of the houses and danger associated with falling rock during the
sweep Finally the slope would need to be stripped of all vegetation to expose the slope
face and once the sweep is performed other unstable conditions may be created by the
work For these reasons we do not recommend this option for the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 10
Slope Netting
This option involves the installation of a wire mesh netting to cover the entire slope
exposure The installation would involve the removal of most of the slope vegetation and
the installation of an elaborate system of wire mesh anchored into the rock slope This
option is very expensive and problems with site accessibility and safety of the existing
homes during the work are major concerns For these reasons we believe this option is
not viable for this site
Rigid Barrier
For this option we envision the placement and anchorage of a line of precast
concrete barriers along the level backyard areas of the subject lots The concrete barriers
could be similar to those utilized for highway construction Prior to placement of the
barrier portions of the colluvial slope should be removed or graded at the bottom of the
rock slope face in order to provide sufficient room for placement of the barriers
Furthermore the removal or grading of the colluvial material would provide additional
space for development of a level ground terrace to aid in the dissipation of rolling rock
energy in front of the barrier line Ideally a level area about 10 feet wide should be
cleared in front of the barrier line to provide for equipment access to remove debris or
boulders which are envisioned to accumulate behind the barrier In addition the concrete
barriers should be located at the same elevation as the level terrace to reduce the
possibility of boulders overtopping the barrier structure
This option would require periodic maintenance of the cleared area behind the
barrier line to allow for dissipation of rolling boulder energy In addition the clearing
maintenance would allow for heavy equipment access to replace any of the barriers
should they become damaged Because the concrete barrier is a static barrier impact
from rolling boulders would likely cause some damage to the structure Damage to and
replacement of sections of the barricade should be anticipated with implementation of this
option However this option may be one of the least expensive in terms of cost
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3829 00 A Page 11
Building Relocation
This option would involve the relocation of the buildings to locations further toward
Kapahu Street to increase the open space between the building and the toe of the slope
The level open backyard space would be necessary to dissipate rolling boulder energy
without the use of a barricade at the bottom of the slope An open backyard space of
about 40 feet from the toe of the colluvial slope to the rear of the house would probably
be sufficient to allow for natural rolling energy dissipation of large boulders to occur The
existing homes appear to be constructed of post and pier foundation which makes this
option viable from a house relocation standpoint
However the condition of the existing buildings are unknown therefore the level
of effort required to relocate the buildings is unknown Such factors as the existing
structural integrity availability of space on the lot for relocation and utility reconnections
must be considered This may be another relatively low cost viable option provided the
buildings could be moved
Based on our observations the building at 42 17 can not be relocated
because adequate level space for re siting of the building is not available on the parcel
lot Should the building relocation option be considered further this parcel may require
the installation of a fence structure because of the limited space available for other barrier
options and site access for barrier maintenance The other lots appear to have adequate
space for some building relocation
Building Relocation Rigid Barrier
It is our opinion that it would be more effective and offer a greater degree of
safety to implement a combination of relocating the buildings and installing a rigid
barrier By relocating the buildings additional space would be gained for the
installation and maintenance of the barrier In addition the colluvial slope may not
need to be removed or graded as a result of the extra space gained If the buildings
cannot be relocated then removal or grading of the colluvial deposits would likely be
required to facilitate the installation maintenance and provision for an energy
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 12
dissipation area on the slope side of the barrier We highly recommend this option over
implementing only building relocation or only rigid barrier installation
Semi Rigid Fence Barrier
This option involves the installation of a chain link fence barrier The fence would
be installed at the toe of the colluvial slope and should be designed with deepened
anchor posts and heavier than normal gauge framing pipes In addition some of the
colluvial slopes would need to be removed or graded to provide for a level energy
dissipation area on the slope side of the fence line
One disadvantage to this method is that the fence could be subject to damage
from boulder impacts and would require periodic maintenance or replacement of highly
damaged sections deemed beyond repair Also the area on the slope side of the chain
link fence would need to be maintained and cleared of debris or boulders which will
accumulate and reduce the effectiveness of the barrier system
The semi rigid barrier fence may have a relatively low initial cost however
maintenance of the fence line and periodic debris clearing would be required on a long
term basis It is our opinion the chain link fence would offer a higher degree of safety for
stopping rolling boulders than a rigid concrete barrier although maintenance costs will
likely be higher
Flexible Barrier Fence
This option involves the construction of a specialized flexible barrier fence
constructed of woven wire rope nets The term flexible implies a dynamic system that
absorbs energy with greater flexibility than a static system such as those mentioned
previously It is this flexibility which increases the time for an impacting object to
decelerate to zero velocity that enables these barriers to dissipate high energies while
undergoing less damage thus reducing some of the need for barrier maintenance The
net fences are specially designed to dissipate various energy loadings using various
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3829 00 A Page 13
brake systems and net types all of which are tailored by the manufacturer for specific site
conditions
The flexible fence is more expensive than the other barrier types mentioned
however flexible fencing may offer greater factors of safety in that they have been
designed and tested to withstand various loads Because the fence is flexible
maintenance or repair work may not need to be performed as often A manufacturer such
as Brugg Cable Products should be consulted for specific application of the product to the
site
We anticipate that a safe relatively low cost and low maintenance system is
desired for this site As a result we recommend the consideration of the following
methods ranked in order of most suitable to least suitable in terms of cost and the overall
needs for this project
1 Building relocation and installation of a rigid concrete barrier
2 Installation of a chain link fence barrier
3 Installation of a flexible fence barrier
Due to the absence of subsurface data and detailed topographic survey of the site
it is difficult to provide cost estimates for the various options provided We have ranked
the options in accordance with our understanding of the site conditions and needs of the
Owner however once a suitable option is selected additional exploration and survey
should be conducted to obtain additional information required to finalize the design
