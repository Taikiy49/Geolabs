PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is enclosed in a parking area located at 727 Waiakamilo Road in
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The existing parking area serves for a flooring
supplier and retail shops We understand that an isolated sinkhole was experienced
previously within the parking area and was temporarily repaired by concrete patching It
was desired to find probable causes of the sinkhole evaluate potential risk and
recommend a permanent repair solution
Based on the information provided we understand that the existing pavement area
was designed to support traffic consisting of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and
occasional moderately loaded trucks We envision that the existing pavement section may
consist of 2 inch asphaitic concrete over a minimum 6 inch base course

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When

The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of

correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials The project site is situated in an area where
the subsurface soil deposits are dominated by calcareous reef deposits Land
development and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project
area to its present form
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 727 Waiakamilo Road in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The majority of the project site is covered by asphaltic concrete pavement
with some planter areas and low retaining walls The existing parking area is bounded by
Waiakamilo Road to the northwest retail shops to the northeast and a flooring supplier to
the southeast as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A low retaining wall was located on the
northern property boundary and provided grade separation
The project site is generally sloping down from the northwest parallel to
Waiakamilo Road to the southeast The topographic map was not available at the time
this report was prepared The existing pavement was generally in good to fair condition
with some longitudinal cracks attributed to the age of the pavement and traffic loads on the
sloping ground
A concrete patch approximately 7 to 8 feet in diameter was located near the south
end of the parking area and is believed to be the sinkhole that was experienced previously

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
As mentioned previously the subsurface conditions at the parking area renovation
site were explored by drilling and sampling six test borings extending to depths of about
10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface Boring No 1 was drilled near the center
of the concrete patched area and the remaining five borings were spread within the
parking area The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general the project site was underlain by a pavement section consisting of
2 inches of asphaltic concrete and a minimum of 6 inches of thick base course material
over coral formation extending to the maximum depth drilled of about 15 feet below the
ground surface However approximately 9 feet of loose soft fill material was encountered
in the sinkhole area

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered in the borings drilled the
sinkhole area may be an old cesspool or previously filled cavity The sinkhole area or
cavity was backfilled with unsuitable material without compaction effect We believe that
the compression of the unsuitable backfill under its own weight and repetitious traffic loads
is a primary cause for the settlement
Based on the field observation the existing pavement is generally in good or fair
condition with some longitudinal cracks attributed to the age of the pavement and sloping
ground conditions subjected to traffic loads No major over stress was observed on the
existing pavement surface except the sinkhole that was patched with 5 inches of thick
concrete Therefore it is our opinion that the sinkhole area may be relatively small and
isolated The actual size of the sinkhole area is unknown
Repair of Sinkhole Area
Based on our field observation cracks were observed along the perimeter of the
concrete patching We believe that on going settlement is still experienced in the sinkhole
area Because of the loose soft consistency of the fill material we envision that additional
settlement on the order of 6 inches may occur in the future
To avoid continuing distress at the sinkhole area we recommend that the
unsuitable backfill within the sinkhole area be completely removed and replaced with
compacted select borrow material The excavation should completely open the sinkhole
area and exposing the solid coral formation that were encountered in our borings
In general the select borrow material should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction for the backfill 3 feet below the finished grade The
compaction requirement for the backfill within 3 feet below the finished grade should be
raised to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum

moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
To omit the compaction effect due to difficulty of compaction operation in the
relative small confined area Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM may be used in lieu
of the select borrow material
If the size of the sinkhole is determined too small to remove loose soft fill
completely we recommend that on grade structural slab be designed straddling the
support on the adjacent coral formation over the sinkhole An allowable bearing pressure
of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations
bearing on the coral formation encountered in our borings In general the concrete slab
should have a thickened edge embedded into coral formation no less than 6 inches to
ensure a firm support A minimum 12 inch thick cushion layer consisting of select borrow
material should be provided below the concrete slab for uniform support during concrete
curing We recommend that a structural engineer be consulted for the details of the
on grade structural slab design
Pavement Design
As mentioned previously the existing pavement is generally in good to fair
condition with some longitudinal cracks attributed to the age of the pavement and sloping
ground subjected to traffic loads We understand that the existing pavement may be
reconstructed
In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading will consist primarily of passenger
vehicles pick up trucks and occasional moderately loaded trucks Our laboratory tests
performed on the near surface soils encountered beneath the existing pavement appears
to have a CBR value of at least 12 with a swell value of less than 1 percent Considering
the generally light to medium traffic anticipated for the parking area renovation project we
recommend that the following pavement design be used for preliminary budgeting
purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 inch Aggregate Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils for the new pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the parking area renovation project The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation
of the excavation in the sinkhole area and subgrade preparation Other aspects of
earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs This is
to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared
The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
