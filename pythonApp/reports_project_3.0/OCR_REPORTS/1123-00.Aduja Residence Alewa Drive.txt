Single family residence

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dices of this report

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the end of a long concrete
driveway originating at the 1300 block of Alewa Drive and is
Situated on the eastern side of Alewa Heights overlooking
Nuuanu Valley It is currently vacant and is very densely
vegetated by haole koa
The site slopes down steeply to the east at an average slope of
about H V
Basalt rock outcrops were noted particularly in the lower half
of the lot
The ground elevations at the proposed house location varies
from about 540 to 570 feet above sea level
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a tri level structure with the
upper floor at the driveway level and two floor levels below
Wood floors are planned for all the levels
A retaining wall is planned around the western edge of the
house to support the new concrete driveway A short retaining
wall is planned along the western side of the driveway

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled at the northern half of the site with
small portable hand carried drilling equipment
The borings encountered about 5 feet of stiff Silty clay soil
mostly residual with about 2 feet of fill or slope wash in
Boring No 2 over highly weathered rock formation extending
down to a depth of about 10 Boring No 2 to 12 feet Boring
No 1 Boring No 1 encountered dense basalt from 12 to 17
feet the total depth of the boring
Reconnaissance of the general lot area indicated surface rock
outcrops at various locations particularly at the lower half
of the lot
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicates that rock formation should be
encountered at shallow depths below the proposed house loca
tion The rock and weathered rock can provide adequate support
for the proposed house and retaining walls

Foundations
The individual house post footings and retaining wall footing
can be supported on the underlying weathered rock formation
The borings and site reconnaissance indicates that the wea
thered rock formation may be encountered from 0 to 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Cuts of several feet deep are
anticipated in portions of the two lower house levels There
fore it appears that after cutting the underlying rock
formation should be encountered close to the finish site
surface at most of the footing locations
The footings should extend to the weathered rock formation and
have a minimum embedment depth of 12 inches below the outside
finish grade on level areas On sloping areas the footings
should be embedded an additional amount to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 3 feet measured from the outer
edge of the footing to the slope face
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used on the
weathered rock formation This value is for total dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary
wind or seismic loads are considered

The underlying rock formation appears to be predominantly a
pahoehoe flow These types of lava flows may contain lava tube
or voids Some small tubes and blisters were noted in the out
crops at the site Therefore there is a possibility that
settlements could occur if a footing is placed over an unde
tected lava tube To minimize this possibility small diameter
probe holes could be drilled with a jack hammer below the
footing excavations in order to detect voids or tubes The
probe holes should penetrate a minimum depth of 4 feet below
the bottom of the footing and then grouted under low pressure
from the bottom of the hole If cavities are detected
additional probing and grouting may be required to fill the
cavity
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleared of
loose material and bear on firm material We recommend that
the footing excavations be inspected by a soils engineer to
check that adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose
soils are removed prior to concrete placement
The lot should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations

Retaining Wall
A retaining wall approximately 10 feet high is planned along
the western side of the house A shorter retaining wall
generally less than 5 feet high is planned along the western
side of the driveway
The footing for the 10 foot high retaining wall should extend
into the weathered rock formation and have a minimum embedment
depth of 18 inches below the ground surface An allowable
bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used on the dense weathered
rock formation
The shorter 5 foot high wall can be supported by the stiff
residual soil or rock formation A minimum footing embedment
of 18 inches is recommended An allowable bearing value of
3000 p s f can be used on the stiff residual soil
Granular material such as 3B Fine aggregate should be used as
a granular drainage blanket for a minimum of 18 inches behind
the walls Granular material such as select borrow should be
used for the remaining backfill behind the walls The backfill
should be keyed and benched into the natural sloping ground to
prevent the build up of additional earth pressure against the
walls

A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
The walls should be designed to resist the lateral earth pres
sures as follows
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 45 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge or traffic should be
included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 5 between the footing and rock and 0 4
between stiff soil and the footing may be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the walls Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 400 p c f can be utilized

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the backfilling and excavation opera
tions be observed by the soils engineer during construc
tion This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation It is also
recommended that the footing excavation be observed by the
soils engineer to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 17 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Boring Loca
tion Plan Plate 1 Portable drilling equipment was used to
drill the borings due to the poor accessibility of the site
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The clas
sifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in gen
eral conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 92
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths Rock samples were obtained by diamond coring
with an NX double tube core barrel The percentage of core
recovery and RQD Rock Quality Designation are presented on
the boring logs
