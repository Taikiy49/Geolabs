Dear Ms Mizuno
We observed the grading and house foundation excavations for four of the houses
Lot 50 and 52 at the site on an on call basis Based on our observations
the grading and house foundation excavations were generally constructed in accordance
with our report dated August 3 2009
Recently two additional lots Lot Nos 49 and 51 were graded However due to
miscommunication misunderstanding of our recommendations these two lots were not
graded in accordance with our recommendations The miscommunication misunderstanding
occurred due to a change in personnel and subcontractor at the project Since our field
technician who observed the previous lots was unavailable we also had a field technician who
was not familiar with the project specifications and was only at the site to perform compaction
tests
The primary issue is the material used to backfill under the house and driveway
We recommended placing non expansive structural fill consisting of crushed coral or
basalt under the house and driveway for a depth of 3 feet below finished grade These
two new lots were backfilled with borrow from the Kaumualii Highway widening project
This borrow soil consists of brown clayey silt soil with low to moderate swelling potential
depending on the moisture content This borrow material is of a lower quality than the
coral basalt structural fill however it is better than the expansive clay on the lots

In order to make use of the borrow material placed on Lot Nos 49 and 51 and
avoid the expense to remove and replace it we provide revised recommendations for
these two lots
Individual House Footings
The house footings are designed to be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the finished grade The spread footings should be placed on a minimum of 12 inches of
structural fill i e crushed coral basalt We understand that the contractor will excavate the
spread footing locations to 3 feet below finished grade place 1 foot of structural fill then
pour the footing on the structural fill The borrow soil will be used to backfill above the
footing to finished grade To facilitate the footing construction the excavation may be cut
neat to allow footing concrete placement without forms However loose material that falls
into the excavation must be removed prior to concrete placement
Garage Foundations
The perimeter thickened edge footings for the garage slab are designed with a
minimum 12 inch embedment below finished grade The perimeter footings should be
over excavated to allow placement of an additional 24 inches of structural fill under the
thickened edges This would help to provide a barrier around the garage to prevent water
infiltration under the garage concrete slab
Driveways
We recommend placing a minimum of 12 inches of structural fill below the
pavement sections recommended in our report
The remaining recommendations presented in our report are still applicable with the
above modifications
It is our opinion that with the above changes the potential for swelling of the soils
under the garage slabs and driveways increases slightly due to the reduced thickness of
non expansive soil However our tests indicate that the garage slabs and driveways
should perform adequately if the subgrade soil moisture content is maintained above
optimum moisture until the concrete placement
