Geotechnical Exploration
On August 30th the subsoil conditions below the concrete garage slab were
explored using a hand auger A jack hammer was used to enlarge the 2 inch diameter
hole near the center of the garage slab to accommodate the four inch 4 diameter hand
auger Soil samples were retrieved continuously after about six inches 6 of augering
Based on data from the hand augered hole HA 1 the concrete slab is underlain by an
approximately one inch 1 thick yellowish brown sand overlying a moisture barrier
membrane Below the membrane dense brown silty sand with some gravel was
encountered to approximately 1 feet below the top of the concrete slab Below the silty
sand dense gravelly sand with clay was encountered to the maximum augered depth of
about three feet 3 The stiffness of the materials were probed using a inch diameter
steel rod The materials encountered in the hole were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a staff engineer from our firm The log of probe hole
HA 1 is shown on Plate 1 and its location is shown on Plate 2
Laboratory Tests
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in evaluating pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampled Results
of the laboratory tests are presented on Plates 3 and 4
In general the subsoils tested have a relatively consistent moisture content of
about 12 to 15 percent Ring swell tests performed on the sandy capping fill indicate that
the materials do not exhibit significant swelling potential The Atterberg Limits test
performed on the fine materials of the capping fill indicates that the fine grained soils may
be classified as low plasticity clay which should not exhibit significant swelling potential

was performed by Walter P Thompson Inc and the data was transmitted to our office
and is presented on Plate 2

Discussions
Based on the field and surveying data and laboratory results the following is a
summary of our findings
1 The compaction of the near surface soils appear to be adequate based on
probing of the soil layers in the hand augered hole using a steel rod
2 The sandy capping fill material exhibits low swelling potential based on swell
tests performed on the soil samples retrieved
3 The Atterberg Limits test on the capping fill indicates that the fine grained
soils should not exhibit significant swelling potential
4 The concrete garage slab does not appear to have been heaved lifted
based on the survey data
5 A moisture barrier membrane and fine sand were encountered below the
garage slab The absence of base course appears to indicate that the
garage slab was constructed as a typical concrete slab In general where
a concrete slab is subjected to vehicular traffic loads the concrete slab
should be designed as a rigid pavement
6 In general we do not believe that cracks on the concrete garage slab are
associated with the subgrade soils
Recommendations
Based on our evaluation the cracks on the concrete garage slab do not appear
to be related to the heaving and or settlement of the subgrade soils as supported by our
laboratory tests Therefore the garage slab may be repaired cosmetically however we
are uncertain about the long term performance of the garage slab We understand from
conversations with your office that there appears to be hollow spots below the slab
These hollow spots appear to be confined to the center of the slab Where a hollow spot
exists below the slab the hollow spot should be backfilled to provide adequate bearing
support for the slab

To backfill the hollow spots we recommend that a high slump about 8 inch
sand cement mix be pumped into the hollow by a grout pump The sand cement mix
should have a low shrinkage factor less than 0 1 and should have a minimum 28 day
compressive strength of 3 000 p s i Several holes should be drilled through the slab to
serve as a conduit from which the grout is to be pumped Care should be taken in
pumping the grout to avoid lifting of the slab during pumping The cracks on the
concrete slab may then be repaired cosmetically
It should be noted that we are uncertain of the long term performance of the slab
by repairing the garage slab cosmetically Therefore it is recommended that periodic
survey of the slab be conducted to evaluate if the cracks have arrested or is continuing
