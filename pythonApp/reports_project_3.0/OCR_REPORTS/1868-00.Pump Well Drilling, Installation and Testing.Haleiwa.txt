Well Drilling and Installation
The well was drilled to a depth of 38 5 feet first using 4 inch
diameter continuous flight augers Then the boring was reamed to
approximately 6 inch diameter using 6 inch diameter hollow stem augers
A 265 CFM air compressor and peripherals were used to clean out loose
cuttings inside the well prior to installation of the well screen and
casing

Blank non perforated 4 inch I D PVC casing was installed in the
upper 18 5 feet of the well The lower 20 feet of the well was cased with
factory slotted 4 inch I D PVC screen Gravel 3B Fine filter pack was
placed at approximately 15 to 38 5 feet depth The remaining annular
space was backfilled and grouted to the top of the well
The completed well was developed by air lifting for approximately 6
hours using an air compressor
Soil Rock Conditions
The test well drilled at the site encountered highly weathered
basaltic rock formations below the upper approximately 15 feet layer of
alluvial brown clayey silt Hard basaltic rock was encountered at
approximately 38 5 feet below existing ground level
Groundwater was encountered at about 25 0 feet below existing ground
level at the time of well drilling and installation
A summary log of the subsurface conditions encountered in the
drilling is presented on Plate A 1
Pump Test
A Berkeley Model 5HP single phase submersible pump was used
for the pump test The pump intake was placed at approximately 37 feet
depth Well yield was approximately 55 GPM during the first three minutes
of the test and remained at approximately throughout the
approximately 3 hours pump out test
