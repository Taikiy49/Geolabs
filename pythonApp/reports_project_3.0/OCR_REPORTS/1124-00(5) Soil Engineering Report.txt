Soil Engineering Report
Proposed Temporary Employee Parking Lot
Kahului Airport Terminal Complex Expansion
In accordance with our proposal dated March 17 1983 we have
conducted a study of the near surface soil conditions at the above
referenced site
The study consisted of field reconnaissance shallow test pit
excavation laboratory testing of the near surface soils for subgrade
Support and a review of the parking lot site grading plans This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed parking lot will be approximately 320 by 320 feet in
plan dimension Asphaltic concrete is planned for the entire parking
area

The grading plan for the parking lot is shown on the Site Plan

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject site is on the northwest side makai of Keolani Place
opposite the existing employee parking lot located in Kahului Maui
Hawaii
The site is relatively flat with a shallow drainage swale located
about midway through the site The swale trends roughly northwest to
southeast and is perpendicular to Keolani Place The existing ground
toward the rear portion of the site
Presently two abandoned concrete building slabs are located along
the south and west corners of the site fronting Keolani Place
Currently the site is used for temporary vehicular storage by
various automobile rental agencies
Ground cover consisted of dense overgrown grass with some bushes
Surface elevations ranges from 32 0 to 38 0 feet
SURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The area reconnaissance and shallow test pits encountered a stiff
reddish brown clayey silt which appears to be a residual soil
W O Page 3
The stiff residual soil in general is derived from in place
weathering of basalt rock and usually overlies less weathered basalt
formations However no outcrops of basalt were noted during our
reconnaissance or in the shallow test pits
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the grading plans for the proposed parking lot
indicates small cuts and fills generally ranging from 0 5 to 2 0 feet
Based on the site reconnaissance and laboratory testing of the near
surface soils we believe that the site is suitable for the proposed
development
Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the onset of earthwork the areas to receive fill should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation
structures debris rubbish and any other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any soft pockets encountered at the subgrade level during the
clearing operations should also be removed and replaced by
backfilling with well compacted select fill
W O Page 4
Following the clearing and grubbing all fill areas should be
scarified to a depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASIM D 1557 78
The site should be shaped to allow free drainage of surface runoff
away from paved areas
Fill Material and Placement

The on site residual soil is suitable for fill material
All fill material should be moisture conditioned prior to spreading
and compaction The moisture content of the soil should be within
3 percentage points of the optimum
All fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding six
6 inches in compacted thickness Fills should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Pavement Design
Based on the laboratory CBR test results on the on site reddish
brown clayey silt the recommended design pavement section is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
W O Page 5
The recommended section considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans for the site grading should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from shallow test pits and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and shallow test pits These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or
expansive soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
