This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during the roadway and utility construction for the Mokulele Highway

proposal dated March 19 2007
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mokulele Highway Widening project site is in the Wailuku District on the Island
of Maui Hawaii In general the project site is in the northern portion of Kihei Town from
the intersection of Kaiwahine Road and Piilani Highway to the driveway of Kealia Pond as
well as portion of North and South Kihei Roads
The Mokulele Highway Widening Phase 1A project is the conclusion of a
five phased project from Kahului Town to Kihei Town The project consisted of the
construction of a new four lane highway from the vicinity of the Kealia Pond driveway
through the intersection of North and South Kihei Roads and Piilani Highway and ended at
the tie in to the existing Piilani Highway at Kaiwahine Road In addition a new two lane
bridge Waiakoa Uka Bridge was constructed adjacent to the _ existing
Waiakoa Uka Bridge The bridge consisted of a drilled shaft deep foundation system with
one pier and a span of about 160 feet along the north bound lanes of Piilani Highway
Also a new four lane wide 7 cell box culvert was constructed at the intersection of
Mokulele Highway Piilani Highway and North and South Kihei Roads The roadway was
elevated up to 10 feet above the existing ground due to previous flooding problems
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the site grading and utility installation work from
May 7 to 14 2007 and to May 2 2008 The overall process of site grading
consisted of the general mass grading fill of the roadway alignment as a boulder fill
operation followed by the utility line installation construction of the box culvert and
improvements to North and South Kihei Roads
Although our observations were limited due to contractural issues between the
construction manager and the State Highways division we did observe portions of the fill
operation The remaining observations of the boulder fill construction activities were made
by the State D O T inspector
The boulder fill operation consisted of initial proof rolling of the existing ground
surface with a sheepsfoot vibratory roller in preparation for the boulder fill placement
Then import trucking of rock fragments up to 2 feet in size which was mixed with finer
aggregates and soil materials were conducted for the fill placement The trucking travel
path was routed over the previously placed boulder fill with overlapping tire paths for
additional densification of the finer materials between the boulders Next a Cat D9 dozer
was used to spread out the boulder fill into a 2 to 2 5 foot lift During the placement of the
boulder fill water was added to the boulder fill with a water truck and pressurized water
hose Then a 10 ton sheepsfoot vibratory drum roller followed behind the dozer
operations The boulder fill placement extended along the four lane roadway alignment
from about Station 318 25 to Waiakoa Uka Bridge in the vicinity of Station 15 75
The mass grading operations were carried out using heavy machinery equipment
such as semi trailers Caterpillar dozers sheepsfoot compactors graders and a water
truck for dust control and moisture conditioning of the fill materials In addition the utility
line installation and retaining wall backfill operations were performed with large excavators
rubber tire backhoes front end loaders sheepsfoot trench compactors and jumping jacks
These types of equipment are typical and considered suitable for the import placement
and compaction of the fill materials
During the site work operations some samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with AASHTO T1180 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with AASHTO T180 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent

Additional Recommendations During Site Construction
During the excavation for a revised step down footing of the retaining wall section
12C of the box culvert very soft wet soils were encountered which was just above the
water table at an elevation of about 3 5 to A site meeting took place with the

project engineer State DOT highways Division Maui District and the contractor to discuss
a recommendation to over excavate 2 feet below the bottom of footing place crushed
gravel wrapped in a permeable separator such as Mirafi 180N fabric which was folded
over the top of the gravel at the bottom of footing elevation Then the concrete for the
retaining wall footing section could be placed An agreement was reached from the site
meeting and the work was carried out following the recommendations
More soft wet soils were encountered in the same general vicinity as the retaining
wall during the installation of the 18 inch waterline The soft soils would pose a potential
settlement problem for the waterline Therefore to stabilize the invert ground conditions it
was recommended to over excavate about 1 foot below the invert and place crushed
gravel wrapped in the Mirafi fabric up to the original pipeline invert
During the subgrade preparation for the Mokulele highway in the month of January
heavy rains flooded the shoulder and right lane of the south bound alignment from the
vicinity of Stations 318 50 to 323 00 The area became very soft due to saturation of the
subgrade soils The contractor scarified the saturated area and air dried the soils for a few
days However during recompaction soil pumping was observed and water became
prevalent at the surface Since more rains were expected it was decided to remove the
saturated soil and replace it with a granular base type of material from the existing
Mokulele Highway The excavation extended from 12 inches to about 18 inches below the
subgrade elevation to the top surface of the boulder fill material The select granular fill
material was placed in level 6 to 12 inch lifts and compacted with a vibratory drum roller
Finally during the construction of the realigned South Kihei Road more soft wet
soils were encountered near the very shallow water table at the subgrade elevation from
the vicinity of Stations 4 95 to 6 20 It was recommended again to over excavate to a
depth of about 15 inches below the subgrade elevation place crushed gravel wrapped in
Mirafi fabric and static roll the surface of the gravel and fold the fabric over the top of the
gravel Once the subbase fill was placed over the reworked subgrade area compaction
tests indicate that greater than 95 compaction was achieved
