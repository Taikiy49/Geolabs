SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the New Swimming Pool and Shower Facilities project at Lanakila District
Park located at the corner of Lanakila Avenue and Kuakini Street in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations site
preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site Lanakila District Park is located at the corner of Lanakila Avenue
and Kuakini Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information
provided the subject lot is approximately triangular in shape Our field reconnaissance
indicated that the project site slopes downward from about Elevation 82 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL on the northeast to about Elevation 64 feet MSL on the southwest as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the proposed improvements consist of a new swimming pool
of approximately 25 meters by 50 meters with concrete bleachers a shower facility and a
small office which will be constructed in the vicinity of the existing gymnasium building
Structural details were not available at the time this report was prepared

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield volcano Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southern flank of elongated Koolau Volcanic
Range which forms the southern portion of the Island of Oahu Hawaii The range was
formed from basalt lava flows originated from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing
mountain trends During the late Tertiary and Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age deep valleys
including the Nuuanu Valley were eroded by streams During higher stands of the sea
level in the Pleistocene Epoch the valleys were infilled with alluvial sediments to the high
sea level stands Underlying these alluvial sediments are the older volcanic lava rock
formation

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a fill layer
of stiff silty clay with sand extending to depths of approximately 4 to 9 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The fill layer was underlain by weathered basalt rock formation
or boulders extending to the maximum depths explored of approximately 14 to 15 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on the competent subsoil conditions
encountered at the project site we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread
and or continuous footings bearing on 12 inches of compacted select granular fill material
may be used to support the proposed new shower facility and office building
Based on our laboratory tests the in situ clayey soils exhibit moderately high
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To
reduce the potential for distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
the swelling of the near surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils be
capped with a minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill
material below slab areas In general the subgrade soils should be compacted to
provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of fill
material Use of the vibratory action of a compactor should be carefully controlled to
avoid inducing a pumping subgrade condition The slab subgrade should be kept moist
and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of capping fill cushion fill and or slab
concrete Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
foundations retaining walls site preparation and pavements are further discussed in the
following sections of this report

3 2 Swimming Pool
We understand that a new swimming pool is proposed at the subject site In
general we believe that the swimming pool wall foundations may be designed in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Building Foundations sections
of this report Since the swimming pool will be a water retention structure we envision
that the foundation for the pool will consist of a mat slab foundation To reduce the
potential for structural distress of the mat slab resulting from the swelling of the
expansive soils and for more uniform support of the slab we recommend that the mat
slab foundation be supported on at least 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill
material compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The 24 inch
non expansive select granular fill material should also be provided under the swimming
pool slab deck to reduce the potential for future slab distresses resulting from the
swelling of the on site expansive soils

3 3 Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by clayey soils
with moderately high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least
24 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a
minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and
the underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final placement
of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to serve as a capillary break and to provide
more uniform support of the slabs To reduce moisture infiltration and subsequent

should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface
run off away from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey
soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths of about 14 to 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using portable drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight
augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of

APPENDIX C
Permeability Testing
APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Testing
The permeability of the in situ soils were evaluated by performing three
falling head tests in a selected boring Boring No 3 at depths ranging from
approximately 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface
During the falling head testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to
the top of the borehole The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to
provide data upon which to base calculation of the permeability values of the
subsoils The test results of the field permeability tests are presented on
Plates C 1 and C 2
