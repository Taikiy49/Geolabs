As requested, Geolabs, Inc. observed the installation of the production drilled shafts 
for the above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during 
the drilled shaft foundation installation for the Pali Highway Landslide Mitigation, Federal-
Aid Project No. ER-22(002) project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the 
project. The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the drilled shaft foundation installation for conformance with the 
plans and to provide subsequent recommendations as appropriate. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project includes the installation of a new rock shed roof structure over the 
existing inbound Pali Highway tunnel. The rock shed structure is supported on a 
foundation system consisting of both shallow foundations and drilled shaft foundations. 
A total of three drilled shafts for the new rock shed structure were installed.  
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Pali Highway Landslide Mitigation, 
Federal-Aid Project No. ER-22(002) project only. Goodfellow Bros., Inc. (GBI) was the 
general contractor for the project, and CMZ of Hawaii was the drilled shaft subcontractor.

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Three production shafts were installed for the Pali Highway Emergency Repair, 
Rock Shed Structure project. The following subsection provides discussion on the 
production drilled shaft installation. 
Production Drilled Shafts 
The drilled shaft contractor installed a total of three production shafts at the 
locations shown on the Drilled Shaft Foundation Location Plan, Plate 2. The contractor 
installed Drilled Shaft No.1 from May 15 to May 16, 2019, followed by concrete 
placement on May 16, 2019. Shaft No. 2 was drilled from May 17 to 21, 2019, followed 
by concrete placement on May 21, 2019. Shaft No. 3 was drilled from May 22 to 29, 
2019, followed by concrete placement on May 29, 2019. Details of the drilled shaft 
installations are presented on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary, Plate 3. 
Representatives from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft excavations, placement of 
reinforcing cages, and concrete placement operations during the installation of the 
production drilled shafts. 
In general, the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Casagrande 180 
HD drill rig equipped with a 48-inch diameter auger, 48-inch core barrel, and 46-inch 
clean-out bucket.  
After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with 
the use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete 
was placed in the drilled shafts within the same day as the substantial completion of the 
excavation. Concrete was placed through a 4-inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting 
from the bottom of the shaft excavation. During concrete placement, we observed that 
at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe. 
Concrete placement continued until a minimum of 3 feet of overpour was achieved. 
Concrete with slump ranging from 7.5 to 10.5 inches was used for the drilled shafts. 
Slump tests and compression tests of concrete were performed by others. The 
individual Drilled Shaft Installation Records are presented on Plates 4.1.1 through 4.3.4. 
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the production 
drilled shafts supporting the rock shed structure for the Pali Highway Landslide 
Mitigation Project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project 
plans. In addition, it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed 
to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design compressive load capacities.
