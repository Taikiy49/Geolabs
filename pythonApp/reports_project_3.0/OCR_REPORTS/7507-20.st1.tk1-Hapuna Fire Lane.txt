Dear Mr. Hayes: 
In accordance with your request, we conducted a geotechnical engineering 
exploration in support of the design of the new fire lane project at Hapuna Beach Prince 
Hotel on the Island of Hawaii. This letter report presents our geotechnical engineering 
recommendations pertaining to the site grading and pavement design for the project. It 
should be noted that our recommendations provided are subject to the limitations noted 
at the end of this letter report. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Renovations are planned for the existing Villas and Towers G, H, and I at Hapuna 
Beach Prince Hotel in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii.  As part of the 
renovation, a new fire lane will be constructed to provide emergency access to the makai 
side of the property. 
The new fire lane alignment will be constructed between the existing Villas and 
Tower I and extend near the end of Tower H. Based on the preliminary plans provided, 
cuts and fills of up to about 2 to 4 feet in height may be required at portions of the fire 
lane alignment. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
A total of eight Dynamic Cone Penetration (DCP) tests, designated as DCP-1 
through DCP-8, were conducted to estimate the California Bearing Ratio (CBR) value of 
the in-situ subgrade soils.  The approximate DCP test locations are shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2.

DCP tests are typically used to assess material properties down to a depth of about 
48 inches below the surface.  The 17.6-pound Dynamic Cone Penetrometer (DCP) can 
be used to estimate the strength characteristics of fine and coarse-grained soils, granular 
construction materials and weakly stabilized or modified materials. The 17.6-pound DCP 
generally cannot be used in highly stabilized or cemented materials or for granular 
materials containing a large percentage of aggregates greater than 2 inches. 
We understand that the subsurface materials generally consist of top soil over 
beach sands and rock fill.  Based on our DCP testing, the top 6 to 9 inches of materials 
at the site generally has CBR value of about 2 to 8 or greater.  The subsurface materials 
below the top 6 to 9 inches in depth generally have CBR value of about 17 or greater.  It 
should be noted that DCP Test No. 6 encountered loose to medium dense soils with CBR 
value of 2 to 8 to a depth of about 28 inches below the ground surface.  The results of the 
DCP testing are presented in the Appendix A. 
Groundwater is not anticipated during construction of the project. However, 
groundwater levels likely will change due to tidal fluctuations, seasonal precipitation, 
surface water runoff, and other factors.

height may be required to achieve the design finished grades. Items of site grading that

A Geolabs representative should monitor the site grading operations to observe 
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification 
process, and to confirm whether the exposed subsurface conditions are similar to those 
anticipated at the site. 
Site Preparation 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits should be 
thoroughly cleared. Vegetation, debris, deleterious materials, and other unsuitable 
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off-site to reduce the potential for 
contamination of the excavated materials. The existing lawn and top soil should be 
removed prior to placement of engineered base material and Geoblock units. 
Areas to be filled and finished subgrades in cut areas should be scarified to a depth 
of at least 8 inches (where possible), moisture-conditioned, and re-compacted to at least 
95% relative compaction. Where scarification of the subgrades is not practical, subgrade 
materials should be proof-rolled with a minimum 10-ton (static weight) vibratory drum

Fills and Backfills 
In general, fill materials required to raise the existing ground to design grades 
should consist of structural fill materials. Structural fill materials should consist of 
well-graded granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with 
sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass. In addition, 
structural fill materials should contain less than 30% particles passing the No. 200 sieve 
by weight. In general, the excavated on-site materials, except for top soil, may be reused 
as a source of structural fill materials, provided that they are processed to meet the 
maximum particle size and gradation requirements presented above. 
Aggregate base course materials required for the fire lane section should consist 
of crushed basaltic aggregates and should conform to Section 31 of the County of Hawaii 
Standard Specifications (September 1986). 
Imported fill materials (where required) should be free of organics and deleterious 
materials and should be suitable for the intended use. In addition, imported fill materials 
should be observed and/or tested by Geolabs prior to being transported to the site for the 
intended use. 
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in 
loose thickness, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and 
compacted to at least 95% relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place 
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same 
soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content 
(percentage by weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of 
rocks, boulders, and/or cobbles. Instead, a testing program should be conducted at the 
start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a Geolabs representative 
to evaluate the number of passes needed to achieve the desired level of compaction. 
Based on this testing program, the number of passes may then be used as the field 
criterion for adequate compaction. 
For preliminary estimating purposes, compaction may be accomplished by using 
a large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 10 tons traveling at a speed

NEW FIRE LANE 
We understand that it is desired to use a vegetated porous pavement system, such 
as Geoblock 5150 or equivalent, for the new fire lane construction.  Geoblock 5150 is a 
polyethylene open-cell block which is infilled with top soil to provide a medium for the 
vegetation to grow.  The Geoblock unit, together with engineered base material, top soil 
infill, and vegetation provides support for the intended vehicular loading. 
Subgrade preparation and fill placement under the new fire lane should follow the 
recommendations provided in the “Site Grading” section herein.  As mentioned above, 
one of our DCP tests encountered loose to medium dense soils with CBR value of 2 to 8 
to a depth of about 28 inches below the ground surface.  Therefore, pockets of loose 
materials may be encountered during construction.  Where soft and/or loose soils are 
encountered at the fire lane subgrades, the soft and/or loose soils should be over-
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials.  The resulting over-excavation should 
be backfilled with select granular fill, placed in 8-inch level loose lifts, and compacted to 
at least 95% relative compaction. 
We recommend providing a minimum of 4 inches of ¾-inch base course below the 
Geoblock units.  The base course should be moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to at least 95% relative compaction.  The placement 
and installation of the Geoblock units should follow the manufacturer’s guidelines and 
recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
The findings and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based on 
DCP test results only. Variations of subsurface conditions from those assumed herein 
may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until 
construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, Geolabs should be contacted 
to re-evaluate the recommendations presented in this letter report. 
The DCP test locations indicated herein are approximate, having been taped from 
visible features at the project site shown on the Site Plan transmitted by West Point 
Investment Corporation on June 29, 2017.
