PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Manana Community Park and Youth Facility site is located at the intersection
of Waimano Home Road and Spine Road in the Pearl City area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project involved the demolition of the existing warehouses and development
of the site into a community park and youth facility with the following construction A layout
of the new facilities is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
e A recreation center at the eastern corner of the project site
e A soccer field with practice area
e A baseball field and a softball field

The new recreation center is a single story building measuring up to about
140 feet long and 60 feet wide in plan dimensions with a total floor area of approximately
8 325 square feet The new building was constructed of CMU walls with metal framing
and slab on grade construction The finish floor elevation of the building was set at about
143 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the information provided by the project
structural engineer maximum column loads are estimated to be on the order of about
20 kips per column with wall loads up to approximately 3 kips per lineal foot
Based on the grading plan provided and our field observations site grading work
for the project construction generally consisted of cuts on the order of about 9 to 11 feet
deep and fills on the order of about 4 to 6 feet thick New retaining walls up to about 7 feet
high were constructed to provide grade separation at various locations across the project
site In addition new asphalt concrete paved access road and parking lots were
constructed to serve the new facility
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS AND BUILDING CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork operations for the Manana Community Park Youth Facility site
were performed by Jayar Construction Inc In general the mass grading operations
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills Embankments
and excavations on the order of about 4 to 6 feet thick and 9 to 11 feet deep respectively
were required to achieve the design finished grades for the project In general the on site
soils generated from the cut areas were used in fill areas to raise the existing ground to the
design finished grades
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Following clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 6 inches below the finished
subgrades in the building areas to provide the recommended 6 inch thick non expansive
select granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent
of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor During the

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 119 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on
site survey stakes and visible features These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
with a pointed steel rod
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations during the site
grading foundation excavation utility line installation and pavement construction for the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report
dated and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
it should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
