Dear Mr Mrs Young
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations

Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story structure approximately 55 by 67 feet in plan dimensions
with a partial basement at the back of the house A swimming pool
approximately 14 by 37 feet in plan dimension is also planned at the
back of the residence The general layout of the house foundation and
the swimming pool is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 158 to depths ranging from 5 5
to 10 5 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass grading
plan for Royal Summit Phase 1 dated prepared by
Community Planning Inc indicated that lot 158 received up to 15
feet of compacted fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1339 00 Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low swelling potential if the
moisture content of the soil is maintained over 30 Based on the
findings from our field exploration the present moisture content is
quite dry with natural moisture contents ranging from 24 to 32
Thus the swelling potential could increase to a moderate range
Therefore to minimize the swelling potential the subgrade soil
should be moistened and protected from drying from the start of
construction until final concrete placement
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill below the slab and backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural
soil
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1339 00 Page 5
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
The southwestern corner of the house partial basement is located
about 7 feet from the existing sewer easement To minimize the
possibility of footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill or due to sewer trench excavation at a later date for
repair purposes we recommend that the footings in this area be
the easement
It is our understanding that concrete slab on grade is planned for the
garage driveway and the basement level of the house It is
recommended that a minimum 18 inch perimeter footing embedment be
utilized to provide a barrier against future water intrusion under the
slab area
Where on site soil is encountered at subgrade level garage driveway
and basement level special subgrade preparation with lime saturation
are recommended to minimize the swelling potential of the soils The
general procedures listed hereunder for lime saturation should be
followed

Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations at a rate of about one pound per square foot
prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
The subgrade soils should be water saturated by constant sprinkling
for at least 2 days prior to concrete placement to reduce the
swelling potential of the underlying subsoil It is important that
the soils engineer be notified to inspect the above subgrade
preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying soils
prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils The very soft silt and lime mud
deposited at the footing after saturation should be removed just
prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
slabs

structurally separated from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

For the swimming pool planned at the rear of the residence a lateral

The on site soils have low swelling potential if maintained at a moisture

potential increases with decreasing moisture contents therefore the
swimming pool excavation should be kept moist all the time
Lot Grading
Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such
as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum 8 inch loose
lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximun dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site soils in the Royal Summit
area generally have low to moderate swelling potential and would require
special preparation prior to being used as structural fill Therefore
the on site soil should only be used for yard fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1339 00 Page 9
Prior to construction the area to receive any 111 should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximun dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
