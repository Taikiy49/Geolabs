INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our updated geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed Lot 2 Subdivision
Princeville at Hanalei on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general
location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our updated exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations
are intended for design input and are not intended for construction cost
estimation or bidding purposes

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located on the extreme northern shore of the island of
Kauai Hawaii Most of the area is bounded on the south by Kuhio
Highway on the north by residential developments Lot 1 Unit X and
Woods 7 Subdivision and the Princeville Golf Course on the east by the
existing Princeville Shopping Center and on the west by Hanalei
Plantation Road A small separate portion of the site is located north
of the existing shopping center and adjacent to the junction of Emmalani
and Ka Haku Roads
In general the terrain is a rolling plateau which slopes down to
the west at less than 5 grades The ground surface elevation ranges
from about 230 to 270 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL at the western and
central portions of the site where most of the construction is planned
The ground elevation ranges from about 280 to 290 feet above MSL at the
small separate northeastern portion where the two story building is
planned
The plateau at the western and central portions is traversed by
several east west trending dirt and gravel roads There is an A C paved
road in the adjacent Woods 7 Subdivision to the north which was graded
by filling up a major portion of a natural drainway in the area A
steep near vertical cut bank up to 20 feet high is located behind an
existing maintenance warehouse and other minor structures in the
neighboring lot adjacent to the northwestern boundary of the site
In the small separate northeastern portion an existing 30 inch
drain pipe under Ka Haku Road empties into the area The drainage water
is then carried through an Open ditch along the northern portion of Woods
7 Subdivision and eventually routes into the existing golf course

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau along
the north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built during
the late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from
the Pooku Kapaka and other now obscure vents of the Koloa Volcanic
Series Intercalated with the lava flows are ash beds and related
pyroclastics
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which
retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
The residual soils were subsequently covered with thin surface fills
of similar materials by human activities
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling eleven 11 borings to depth of 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface

borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of the drilled borings encountered
a surface fill layer between 0 5 and 3 feet thick of stiff clayey silt
Below the fill layer the borings generally encountered medium stiff
to stiff mottled brown and light brown clayey silt varying in thickness
between 2 and 6 5 feet These are residual soils that derived from the
in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally structureless
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered generally
medium stiff saprolite soils consisting of clayey silts having
predominantly brown color mottled with black spots or streaks
Occasionally the saprolite soils were found to have soft consistency at
depths more than 15 feet below the existing ground
Highly weathered basalt in the form of very dense to hard sand and
gravel was encountered at the depth of about 20 feet below the existing
ground surface in Boring No 11
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our current
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The updated field exploration encountered generally competent
subsoils for the proposed development in the project area However
modified grading techniques may be required for the earthwork due to the
relatively high in situ moisture content of the subsoils and the
relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that shallow footing foundations be used to Support the
proposed structures of the development

Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and the grading
plans were not available However minor cuts and some fills are
anticipated to grade the area for the proposed construction In the
vicinity of the proposed gas station site filling over existing slopes
may be required
Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a relatively high rainfall
area most of the subsoils had in situ moisture contents of about 40
to 60 within the upper 10 feet of soils explored These moisture
contents are considerably higher than the ASTM D 1557 78 optimum
moisture contents which range from about 24 to 33 for the upper
soils see Plates B 5 and B 6 Therefore considerable drying of
the excavated materials will be required prior to their use as fill
materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally
occurs in the area throughout the year drying of large quantities
of soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading
operations To facilitate construction it is our opinion that the
normal 90 ASTM D 1557 78 compaction standard for general site fills
used in drier areas may be reduced to 85 for this project Our
previous experience in Princeville and similar areas indicates that
85 compaction can provide adequate support for general site fills
under roadway and light building loads
Fills within 12 inches below roadway or footing subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum ary density established
by ASTM D 1557 78

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The tree holes and any loose soft or yielding areas spots
encountered at the pavement or footing subgrade level should be
over excavated down to in situ firm ground and replaced with select
on site soil or imported backfill compacted to a minimum of 85 of
its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6
inches below the cleared grade level air dried if necessary and
recompacted to the above recommended minimum of 85 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The ground surface below pavements or footings should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 6 inches below subgrade level air dried if
necessary and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Placement

off site borrow is required the material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
to see that it is suitable for the proposed construction The
material should generally be non expansive and should not contain
large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious matters

Foundation Support
Based on the soil conditions and anticipated light foundation loads
we believe that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations bearing
on the recompacted in situ surface fills or residual subsoils may be used
to support the proposed structures
GEOLABS HAWAII

An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f with a minimum
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic loading
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Where the footing foundation is located on or near the top ofa
slope the footing embedment should be increased to provide a minimum
10 foot horizontal set back distance between the outer edge of footing to
the slope face
Based on the boring data estimated total footing settlements under
the design loads are anticipated to be on the order of less than one 1
inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structures may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the recompacted
in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used against the buried vertical faces of the
footings to determine the passive soil pressure

It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavement will be used for
most of the project parking areas and driveways

preliminary pavement sections may be used

2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete

The above pavement sections assume a subgrade of on site materials
having a relative compaction of at least 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78
maximum dry density within 12 inches below the subgrade
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils during construction and the above design sections may be revised
if necessary The recommended sections consider medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas Weepholes should
be provided in the parking area drainage inlet structures in order to
relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate in the base course
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction with 4 inch concrete slabs placed over a
4 inch layer of compacted base course may be used for the proposed
structures The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a
minimum of 8 inches below the lowest adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
moisture barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
Our laboratory test results indicate that the on site soils in
general have low swelling potential Therefore the slab subgrade
should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out prior to concrete
placement

Underground utilities are anticipated for this development It is
anticipated that most of the trenching will be in medium stiff residual
or saprolite soils

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping
from reference points indicated on the topographic map dated
by Wes Thomas Associates and revised by Esaki Surveying and Mapping
Inc on Elevations of the data points were
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic
map The physical locations and elevations of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs

Eight 8 one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected
near surface soil samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils
The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two 2 laboratory CBR tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of the soils The test
results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 laboratory compaction tests modified Proctor were performed
on selected bulk samples of the near surface soils to determine their
compaction characteristics The test results are also summarized on Plate
B 2 and plotted on Plates B 5 and B 6
Two 2 direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsoils The
test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
