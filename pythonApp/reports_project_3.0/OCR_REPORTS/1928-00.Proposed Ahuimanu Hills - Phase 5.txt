INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the the project site grading
infrastructure installation and residential structure foundations of
the proposed Ahuimanu Hills Phase 5 residential development at
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed residential development

existing ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of
selected samples obtained during the field exploration engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the south and west of Hui Kelu
Street in the Ahuimanu area of The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The site is located mauka of the existing residential developments
in an area which is densely covered with tropical and subtropical
vegetation such as ferns hao guava and mango
The topography of the site is characterized by two 2 ridges with
relatively plane crests separated by drainageways serving as channels
for intermittent and perennial streams that originate near the nearby
Koolau Pali In several areas the side slopes of the drainageways are
quite steep ranging from about 1H 1V to near vertical along the
stream courses

The existing ground surface elevations of the site range from
approximately 220 to 400 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to subdivide the area into a residential
development of 57 single family residences The site improvements will
involve the construction of three 3 access roads infrastructure
installation and site grading to provide building pads for the
residential structures
It is anticipated that the road and lot grading will require cuts
and fills of up to fifteen feet Fills up to 20 feet high are planned
at the stream crossing
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated at the base of the Koolau Pali a
high cliff which extends along most of the windward side of the Koolau
Range The climate along the Pali is typified by a high to very high
average annual rainfall
Following the main shield building stage of Koolau volcano in the
late Pliocene early Pleistocene stream erosion became the dominant
agent affecting the morphology of the area
The project site is located in a major amphitheater headed valley
along the two ridges of a drainageway Along these ridges in situ
chemical weathering of the parent Koolau Basalt has formed deep
residual soils and saprolites On the side slopes of the ridges and in
the floor of the drainageway mass transfer materials such as alluviun
colluvium and landslide debris have accumulated Some of these
transported materials are sufficiently old enough to have been
subjected to in situ chemical weathering

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling eleven 11 borings to depths ranging between
24 5 and 26 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
In general the borings encountered stiff clayey silts and silty
clays to the maximum depths drilled Colors of the soils ranged from
browns to reds with frequent mottled areas resulting from the deep
chemical weathering which has occurred at the project site
Boring 5 which was drilled in the central drainageway
encountered a thin surface layer of clayey silt underlain by boulders
and cobbles interbedded with stiff to very stiff clayey silt
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of
this report
Groundwater levels were encountered in and 7 during
the time of our exploration at the times and depths indicated on the
appropriate boring logs in Appendix A
Previous experience in the area indicates that seepage is
frequently encountered in cut areas
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed However the wet soil
conditions and high to very high average annual rainfall at the project
site will require special earthwork design and special construction
methodology
W O 1928 00 Page 5
dk duke
Steep Natural Slopes
The existing slopes along the natural drainageways are generally
steep In some areas the slopes were found to be as steep as
H V to near vertical and about 15 to 25 feet high see Plate 4
These steep slopes are common in the general area and are an indication
of the stable structure of the in place colluvium material Previous
experience combined with visual observations and hand probing along the
slopes indicate that the slopes at the site are generally stable
However these slopes along drainageways are ina dynamic environment
subject to erosion during heavy rainfall and scouring from stream
flows Therefore it is possible that in isolated areas some
sloughing of the slopes may occur in the future
In order to reduce possible damage to new fills and structures we
recommend that they be set back beyond a 1 1 H V plane extended
upward from the toe of the slope This recommendation is illustrated
on Plate 5 If appears that this set back requirement would primarily
affect Lots 54 and 56
The future homeowners should be made aware of this set back
recommendation and be advised of the potential risk for slope changes
along the drainage ways
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations within
the portions of the grading limits designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
W O 1928 00 Page 6
ak duke
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as
engineered fill if their high moisture contents can be substantially
lowered Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site materials have very high in situ moisture contents of about
15 to 35 percent above optimum Therefore significant drying of
excavated materials will be required prior to their use as fill and or
backfill
Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the on site soils and
the high amounts of rainfall occurring in the area it is our opinion
that the compaction requirement for this residential project may be
reduced from the standard of 90 to a minimum of 85 maximum dary
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Even with this reduced 85
minimum compaction requirement we believe substantial moisture
reduction would still be required along with the use of light
compaction construction equipments
If additional or drier imported materials are required the
material should generally be non expansive able to be compacted ina
dense stable mass and have a minimum California Bearing Ratio of 15
when tested in the laboratory in accordance with ASTM D 1883
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness Fill placement should commence at the
lowest point and continue upward in level compacted layers
In areas to receive fill where the existing ground surface is
steeper than 5H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in the proper bonding of the new fill in to the
in situ soils see Plate 6
Subdrains
To reduce piping and seepage pressure buildup subdrains should be
placed in the bottom of drainage ways prior to filling

The subdrains should be placed ina herringbone pattern For
preliminary design purposes the laterals may be spaced at 50 foot
intervals The actual locations of the subdrains will be determined in

Drainageway Crossing
About 15 to 20 feet of fill is planned in the central drainageway
for a roadway crossing A box culvert is planned through this high
roadway fill
The field exploration encountered alluviun consisting mainly of
river gravel and cobbles in the drainageway Settlement predictions in
this type of subsoil are very difficult Based on the field exploration
and previous experience in the area it is estimated that about
one half of an inch of settlement may take place at the center of the
structure Less settlements should occur at the ends of the
structure Due to the granular nature of the deposit it is our
opinion that most of the settlement should occur within one month
If these settlements are not tolerable surcharging may be used to
reduce the settlements
Previous experience in the area indicates in the event the
contractor does not exercise construction methology to allow for drying
of the soils there is a tendency for soils in such thick fills to
begin pumping after the placement of a few lifts due to the high
natural moisture content of the soils In order to reduce the tendency
to pump consideration may be given to embankment construction with
some lifts of granular material reinforced with a geotextile such as
Tensar or Geogrids Test areas should be placed under the observation
of the geotechnical engineer to determine the optimum thickness of soil
versus gravel

Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are
recommended for the site
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design
slope then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having a vertical
height greater than 20 feet
Seepage is occasionally encountered through gravelly layers
exposed in cut slopes For small intermittent seepage the water may
be diverted into swales or subdrains If large seepage flows are
encountered other methods such as horizontal drains may be required
Foundation Support
For the design of residential structure footings an allowable
bearing capacity of 2000 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on
in situ soils or on fills compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations This value may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered The footings should
be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the
soft and or weak materials down to very stiff natural alluvial soils
and backfilling of the resultant void with fill material compacted in
accordance with the above recommendations

In areas where residential structures are to be constructed near
the steep side slopes of the drainageways the foundations should be
set back sufficiently to bear outside an imaginary line of 1H 1V
projected upward from the base of the slope
Culvert Structure
Wing walls will be constructed at both ends of the culvert An
allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended for the wall
footings resting on alluvium To provide uniform support a minimum of
12 inches of granular bedding material should be placed under the
structure
For lateral earth pressure design the following equivalent fluid
pressures are recommended
a Unrestrained condition level backfill 45 p c f
b Unrestrained condition 2 1 sloping backfill 60 p s f
c Restrained condition 60 p s f
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are proposed in conjunction with the site grading
structures and roadways These walls will Support existing hillside
slopes
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill or
natural soil may be used for the design of the retaining wall
foundations
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls

Design Review

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping and estimation from survey points placed in the field by the
surveyors Elevations of the data points were approximately determined
by hand levelling and estimation from the same points The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors

VI Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within each
layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill
layer which has not been properly compacted Subsequent
removal of any unacceptable layer shall be done at the
Contractor s expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches 8 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated
in any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained
material to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for
placement in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical
Engineer s Report
GEOLABS HAWAI
Standard Earthwork and Page 8
Grading Specifications Continued
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable
of producing the specified results in an expedient and
efficient manner The equipment shall be in good working
condition fully ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a
uniformly compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above
specified levels of compaction out to the finish slope of
fill slopes This may be achieved by either overbuilding
the slope and cutting back to the compacted core or by
direct compaction of the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes
shall be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope
material into firm material and the transition stripped of
loose material prior to fill placement Fill shall be
placed and compacted from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not
in accordance with these specifications shall not be
approved and shall be removed or reworked until the required
density is obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests

water
