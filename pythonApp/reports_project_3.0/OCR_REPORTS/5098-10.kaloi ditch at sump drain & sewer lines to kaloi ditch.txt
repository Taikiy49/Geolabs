Subsurface Conditions

Drainage and Sewer Structures
We understand that two large drain lines are planned from the Kapolei Parkway
Extension to the water quality sump The northern drain line system will consist of 78 inch
diameter reinforced concrete pipe RCP sections three manhole structures and an outlet
structure The invert elevations for the manhole and outlet structures range from about
4 to 12 feet MSL For the southern drain line system the system will consist of 8 feet by
5 feet box culvert or 84 inch diameter RCP sections four manhole structures and an
outlet structure The manhole and outlet structures for the southern drain line system will
have invert elevations ranging from 4 to 13 8 feet MSL
A new 12 inch diameter sewer line is planned along the southern portion of the
project site Sewer manhole structures will be constructed for the new sewer line with
invert elevations ranging from about 2 07 to 7 31 feet MSL
Based on the above invert elevation of the manhole structures excavation depths
of up to about 20 feet below the existing ground surface will be required for the project
construction Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we believe
that shoring of the sides of the excavations may be necessary to protect personnel
working in the excavations The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be
made the sole responsibility of the contractor

Box Culvert Structure
We understand that a reinforced concrete box culvert may be used for the southern
drain line In general we anticipate that shallow foundations bearing on the coralline
deposits encountered at the project site may be utilized for support of the planned
reinforced concrete box culvert structure Based on the field exploration results we
believe that an allowable bearing value of up to 3 000 pounds per cubic feet may be used
for the culvert structure bearing on the coralline deposits
An 8 inch gravel cushion layer should be provided between the bottom of the box
culvert and the underlying foundation soils to provide more uniform bearing support The

Mass Grading for Area 47 School
Based on the preliminary grading plans for the Area 47 School site fills up to about
3 feet and cuts of up to about 1 foot are planned Based on our previous field exploration
moderately expansive silty clay soils were encountered near the existing ground surface
To reduce the swelling potential of the near surface clay soil we recommend that the
Area 47 school site be capped with a minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select
granular material If the location of the future building areas is known the extent of the
non expansive capping fill may be reduced to below the building areas The
non expansive select granular fill material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 to
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The fill material should also be
free of vegetation and deleterious materials In general the on site coralline materials
should be suitable for this purpose
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in
a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement subgrades the minimum
relative compaction should be 95 percent
In areas where cut subgrades expose coral rock formations the subgrades should
be proof rolled with a minimum 20 ton roller or a Caterpillar D 9 dozer or similar heavy
equipment for a minimum of six passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities

Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should be

The on site cut materials generated from excavations into the underlying coral rock
formation may be used as general fill below 2 foot structural fill materials provided that
they are screened of the over sized materials greater than 8 inches in largest dimension
and processed to contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material This
requirement is necessary to facilitate future utility trench excavations The on site silty clay
should not be used as structural fill within the top 2 feet of finish grade However these
materials may be used as general fill Imported fill should consist of low expansive
granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand meeting the requirements
of structural fill
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optinum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in lose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Base course and select borrow
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum 95 percent
relative compaction
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that
most of the cut slopes would expose soils consisting of medium stiff to very stiff silty clay
or medium hard to hard coral formation In general we believe that cut slopes into these
material may be cut at an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fill
slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding Filling operations should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill
placement recommendations
