Addendum
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Proposed Building at 2112 Bannister Street
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This addendum presents the results of our additional geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the currently proposed warehouse building at 2112 and 2112A
Bannister Place in Kalihi Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of our additional exploration was to better define the depth of the
underlying pile bearing stratum and the length of piles required and if predrilling is
required for the recommended pile foundation in the northern portion of the site Also the
central portion of the site was explored to better define the boundary between pile and
spread footing foundations Since the column loads have been increased a significant
amount a deeper boring was drilled in the southern portion of the site to explore the
increased depth of influence
Project Considerations
Currently the proposed project involves the construction of a two story warehouse
building named Laumaka Center at the project site It is our understanding that the
ground floor will be of concrete construction and the second floor of steel frame
construction
Column loads up to 280 kips are anticipated

It is proposed to place the building slabs on grade across the site by excavating

An abandoned VCP sewerline is located along the western boundary in the

The scope of this additional work included drilling and sampling of four 4 borings
at the project site to depths from 31 5 to 149 5 feet below the existing ground surface
laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration and analyses
of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of the foundation and site grading of the project
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling four 4 borings
to depths of 31 5 to 149 5 feet below the existing ground surface One of the four borings
was drilled in the northern portion of the site another boring was drilled in the southern
portion of the site and two borings were drilled in the central portion of the site The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The boring drilled in the northern lower portion of the site generally encountered
a surface layer about 5 5 feet in thickness consisting of medium stiff brown clayey silt
underlain by loose to very stiff alluvial deposits to about 129 5 feet depths These
deposits were underlain by very dense clayey coralline sand and gravel to 149 5 feet the
maximum depth drilled
The boring drilled in the southern higher portion of the site generally encountered
a surface fill layer about 6 feet in thickness consisting of very stiff dark brown silty clay
underlain by very dense to medium dense sandy coral gravel and silty sand to a depth
of 16 feet Below the coralline deposit a stiff to loose alluvial deposit was encountered
to 31 5 feet depths
The borings drilled at the central portion of the site generally encountered a surface
fill layer about 1 5 to 8 feet in thickness consisting of very stiff brown silty clay underlain
by stiff to loose alluvial deposits to about 27 to 33 feet depths These deposits were
underlain by a hard basalt formation to 43 5 feet depths

Findings and Recommendations
Site Grading
It is planned to level the site by excavating the higher southern portion to about 2
to 3 feet below the existing ground surface and to fill the lower northern portion with about
1 foot of fill
Based on our laboratory testing the subsoils encountered near the finished
subgrade level in the southern portion of the site have a moderate expansion potential
Therefore special grading design as previously recommended will be required to reduce
the heaving of these moderately expansive subsoils
Our laboratory testing also indicates that the excavated material from the southern
portion of the site have a moderately expansive potential These expansive soils should
not be used as fill below the building in the lower northern portion of the site
Foundations
For the proposed building a combination of conventional spread and continuous
footing foundations and pile foundations are planned for the southern and northern
portions of the site respectively
Spread Footing Foundations
In the southern portion of the site spread and continuous footing
foundation bearing on the stiff in situ soil may be used An allowable bearing value
of 4 000 p s f may be used The bearing value is for total dead load plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered

Pile Foundations
In the northern portion of the site pile foundations are planned to reduce
the excessive settlements estimated for conventional spread footing design
Based on our field exploration the depth to an underlying competent
bearing stratum increased rapidly towards Kalihi Stream Near the central portion
of the site a hard basalt formation was encountered at about 27 feet below the
existing ground surface The depth to the basalt formation increased towards
Kalihi Stream to about 60 feet below the existing ground surface at Boring No 4
and extended beyond the maximum depth of 149 5 feet below the existing ground
surface drilled in Boring No 104 which was located near Kalihi Stream A Cross
sectional Sketch is presented on Plate 2
Because of the deep depth to an underlying competent bearing stratum
near Kalihi Stream a combination of end bearing and friction piles are
recommended for the northern portion of the site
The piles near the central portion of the site would develop their load
capacity primarily from end bearing on the basalt and very dense alluvial layers
encountered at depths of about 30 to 60 feet below the existing ground surface
Our analysis indicates that with the use of a inch octagonal prestressed
concrete pile an allowable design capacity of 100 tons may be used
The piles near Kalihi Stream would develop their load capacity primarily
from friction in the alluvial deposits Based on the boring data laboratory testing
and analysis it appears that for a inch octagonal prestressed concrete pile
driven down to an embedment depth of 60 feet an allowable design capacity of
50 tons may be used
A minimum pile spacing of about three 3 feet on centers is recommended
An allowable lateral pile capacity of up to 10 kips per pile may be used in the
design

To reduce the effects of pile driving on the existing footings for the
adjacent warehouse building six 6 feet of predrill is recommended for the pile
locations along the adjacent wall footing The predrilled holes should have a
minimum diameter of 20 inches
The predrilled holes may be filled with the cuttings from the drilling
after placement of the piles
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site surface soils in the southern portion of the site
have a moderately high expansion potential Therefore the existing surface soils in the
southern portion of the site should be over excavated below the slab bottom to a depth
sufficient to provide a minimum twelve 12 inch thick capping layer of imported non expansive
soil between the slab and the natural ground
To reduce the amount of cracking near the slab on grade and wall connection the
slabs on grade should be tied into the wall reinforcement
