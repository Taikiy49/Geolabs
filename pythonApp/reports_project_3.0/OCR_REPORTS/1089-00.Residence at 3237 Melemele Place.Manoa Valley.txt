In accordance with our proposal letter dated August 31
1981 we have performed a soil engineering investigation at
the above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions in order to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the foundation for the proposed
Single family residence
The investigation consisted of two borings two hydrauli
cally drilled probes five hand auger probes visual obser
vations and laboratory soils tests

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on the south side of Melemele
Place in the Woodlawn area of Manoa Valley The site is
approximately 13 000 Square feet in area and roughly trape
zoidal in plan view The site generally slopes and drains
to the west
Previous grading operations have cut a relatively level
bench through the center of the site During this grading
a layer of overcast fill was Placed over the natural slope
immediately below the bench Above the bench is a cut slope
Standing at an average of 1 1 H V This cut slope was
formerly steeper but has been flattened somewhat by the
deposit of loose material at its base as a result of minor
erosion and unravelling Below this bench and overcast
fill the natural slope is standing at a slope of
H V

The site is currently vacant and is vegetated with a thick
cover of vines and small haole koa except for some areas of
the cut slope and bench which are relatively bare
Access to the site will be the northeastern property line
where the bench daylights at Melemele Place
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story structure appro
Ximately 36 by 56 feet in plan with wood decks extending
out on the western and southern sides of the house The
house will be situated on the existing bench area and
extending over the natural downhill slope on the western
side of the site
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway and
garage Slab on grade is also contemplated for the portion
of the house on the relatively flat bench area while post
and beam construction is planned for the remaining portions
of the house over sloping ground
It appears that retaining walls would be required along the
downhill side of the garage and along the rear portion of
the house

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by relatively complex soil conditions A buried drainageway
with soft soil condition and high water level was encoun
tered in the southern portion of the site Special founda
tion design will be required for the portion of the house
over this area
There is a very steep 1 1 slope along the eastern boundary
of the lot Some ravelling of this slope is occurring due
to erosion Ideally this slope should be flattened to
about H V ratio However this is not practicable
Since the regrading would daylight outside of the property
In order to intercept the erosion materials from the slope
which may otherwise be washed down toward the proposed
house we recommend that a toe wall be constructed

Foundation
Poor soil conditions were encountered in the buried drain
ageway in the southern side of the proposed house Exces
Sive settlements are anticipated for slab on grade or shal
low post footing foundations over the drainageway area Due
to the depth approximately 10 feet of the soft soils and
high water table encountered it would be difficult and
expensive to remove and replace the soft subsoils to allow
slab on grade or shallow foundations to be used
Therefore it is recommended that drilled pier foundation be
used for the portion of the house over the buried drain
ageway The piers should extend a minimum of 2 feet into
the underlying decomposed rock layer which was encountered
in the test borings at about 10 foot depth An allowable
bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used for the piers The
approximate limits of the buried drainageway is indicated on
the Boring Location Sketch Plate 1
Relatively high water level is anticipated therefore the
concrete will have to be tremied or pumped from the bottom
of the hole Care should be taken to avoid segregation of
the concrete and to see that soft soils are not trapped
below the concrete

For the remaining portions of the house on stiff ground
conventional shallow foundations can be used The general
recommendations for these shallow footings are as follows
1 The footings should be embedded a minimum depth
of 18 inches and should bear on the stiff colluvium
2 On sloping areas the footings should be embedded
an additional amount in order to develop a minimum
setback distance of 5 feet measured from the outer
edge of the footing to the Slope face
3 An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be
used
The bottoms of all foundation excavations should be cleared
of loose material and bear on firm soil We recommend that
the footing excavations and pier construction be inspected
by a qualified soils engineer to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained
Major settlement problems are not anticipated if the founda
tions are constructed properly However some amount of
differential settlement may occur due to the varying load
and type of foundations used for the house

Site Preparation and Fill Placement
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vege
tation debris and other loose overcast soil
Fills and backfills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 They should also be keyed and
benched into firm natural soil in order to properly bond the
new fill to the slope
It appears that additional off site borrow material will be
required This material should be tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site to
assure that it is suitable for the proposed construction
The material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain boulders organic or other deleterious matter
Garage Slab On Grade
The garage slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected
from drying prior to concrete placement The subgrade soils
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1089 00 Page 9
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density
Slab on grade should be separated from the foundations
When a slab is located below the outside ground surface a
subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is
recommended to relieve seepage water
Drainage
The site is situated in a relatively steep area which is
subject to high rainfall Additionally there is a very
steep slope along the eastern side of the lot The general
drainage pattern of the surrounding adjacent area should be
evaluated to see that concentrated runoff is not directed
toward the proposed structures Also runoff from the steep
Slope should be diverted away from the proposed residence
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls generally less than 6 feet high are planned
for the garage and possibly along the toe of the steep
natural slope

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
two test borings and two probings utilizing truck mounted hydraulic
drilling equipment and by digging five probe holes utilizing hand auger
drills in the sloping portions of the site
The depths of the borings extended to 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface and the probings terminated at depths of 3 0 to 10 0 feet
The approximate locations of the borings and probings are shown on
the enclosed Boring Location Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineering geologist who continuously monitored the
probing and drilling operations The classifications were made in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils is presented in the Boring and Probing Logs
Plates A 1 through A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either a
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches
The blow count used to drive the Sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
In the probings disturbed samples were obtained from the auger
cuttings
