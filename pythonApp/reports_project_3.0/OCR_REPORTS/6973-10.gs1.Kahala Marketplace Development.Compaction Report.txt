This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the 
Kahala Marketplace Development project from June 6, 2019 to August 26, 2020 in 
accordance with our fee proposal dated July 9, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located on the mauka side of Waialae Avenue, east of Hunakai 
Street, and south of Keanu Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project 
generally involves the construction of one to two-story structures, retail spaces, parking stalls, 
and driveways.  The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1, 
and on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the project site. 
SITE GRADING, UTILITY INSTALLATION & FOUNDATION PREPARATION 
The site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation work for the Kahala 
Marketplace Development project were observed by Geolabs, Inc. from June 6, 2019 to 
August 26, 2020.  The site grading, utility installation and foundation preparation consisted of 
clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills up to about 2 feet to achieve the 
design finished grades for the project.  The site grading, utility installation and foundation 
preparation work were performed by Royal Contracting Company Ltd. who was the 
subcontractor for the prime contractor, Allied Builders System.  During the site grading and

backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing.  The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with 
ASTM Test Designation D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D2922 (Nuclear Gauge 
Method).  Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 
4.6 of this report.  These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.  These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas assessed to 
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or 
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Contaminated subsoil materials were encountered at the project site.  The 
contaminated materials were excavated and removed from the project site.  The excavations 
were backfilled.  A separate report was prepared for the backfilling of the excavations. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill and backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced 
tests, it is our opinion that the site grading, backfill installation, and foundation preparation 
work for the Kahala Marketplace Development project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the 
period covered by this report, were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
