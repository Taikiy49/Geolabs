Soils Engineering Investigation
Proposed Kaluanui Prawn Ponds
Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced site in order to
provide soil information to aid in the site grading design of the
proposed development The study consisted of excavating eight shallow
test pits performing laboratory testing reviewing the preliminary
grading plan and engineering analysis This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary grading plan prepared by Park Engineering
Inc the proposed site is approximately 2200 by 2500 feet in plan
dimension It is proposed to develop 65 prawn ponds of various sizes
with sloping bottoms ranging from 3 to 4 feet deep

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in Kaluanui along the northeastern
coastline of Oahu Hawaii Access to the site is off of Kamehameha
Highway on Puhuli Street which is presently under construction as
shown on Plate 1
The area is trapezoidal in shape and slopes downward from the
foothills of the Koolau Mountain Range at the rear of the site to a
depressed marshy area along Kamehameha Highway Ground surface
elevations range from 2 feet in the lowlying areas up to 50 feet at the
rear portion of the site Ponding of water was noted in the lowly ing
soft ground area while firm soil exists in the drier higher ground
area

In the past the site was used for sugar cane cultivation with
some abandoned fields noted along the front and northwest corner of the
Site The remaining areas have ground cover consisting of tall grass
and weeds with some haole koa trees and are currently used as pasture
grazing land
Irrigation ditches crisscross the site in various directions with
some amount of water flow visible possibly from natural springs or
surface runoff from the foothill areas
To the south southwest of the Puhuli Street cul de sac is an
existing wooden pole frame structure and some abandoned vehicles

gravel to 8 feet the maximum depth excavated
In the upper portion of the site the soil conditions consist of

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent stiff alluvial soils in
the upper two thirds of the site Conventional grading operations may
be used in this area
Soft ground conditions with high water level were encountered in
the lower portion of the site Some special site preparation and use
of light construction equipment are recommended for this area The
approximate limits of the soft ground area are indicated on the Site
Plan Plate 1

Site Preparation
Upper Area Stiff Ground

The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and other
deleterious material In areas to receive fill the ground should
be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 density
Lower Area Soft Ground
Soft soil and high water conditions were encountered in this
area Conventional grubbing and undercutting of the vegetation
would cause significant remolding and loss of strength of the
soils and decrease the depth to the water level This would then
require the use of special gravel and filter fabric stabilization
prior to site filling Therefore conventional grubbing and
undercutting are not recommended for the fill areas
For this project we recommend that only the vegetation above
ground be removed The existing ground and vegetation root system
should be left in place to help to form a crust over the soft
soils

Fill Placement
After site preparation the fill should be placed in maximum
10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximun dry density
established by ASTM test designation D 1557 78
The berms should be filled prior to excavation of the ponds to
allow proper compaction of the fill slopes
The on site cut material from the upper areas are suitable for use
in compacted fill and backfill
To minimize ground pumping light equipment should be used over
the lower soft ground area
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to properly bond the
new fill to the slope The filling operation should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance
with the above structural fill requirements For construction details
refer to Plate 2
Slopes
Maximum two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes
are recommended for the upper areas of the site

W O 1317 00 Page 7
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Settlements
Site filling over the lowlying soft ground area will initiate
ground settlements in the soft subsoils Anticipated settlements are
on the order of 2 to 4 inches Utility construction should be delayed
until most of the settlements have occurred which we estimated would
occur within about 3 to 4 months
Settlement gauges are recommended to monitor the rates of
settlements to determine when utility construction can begin
Excavation
Conventional excavation methods may be utilized in the upper
areas The excavation in the soft ground areas would extend into the
soft soils and below the water level Therefore backhoe excavation
may be required
Roadways
All weather roads consisting of a 4 inch thick coral surface are
planned The coral gravel should be well graded and compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
