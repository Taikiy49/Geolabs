PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ko Olina Beach Villas project site designated as Tax Map Key
Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project entails the development of two residential
tower structures Beach Tower and Ocean Tower consisting of 11 to 15 story high rise
building structures with a one to two level below grade parking structure The layout of the
residential development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company as a _ subcontractor to Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company performed the mass grading work for the Ko Olina Beach Villas
project Generally the mass grading work consisted of excavation to the basement
subgrade elevation subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the excavated
subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative compaction under the building slab and pavement
areas respectively Relative compaction was based on the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the mass grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 337 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 26 of this report
The field density tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used

LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
