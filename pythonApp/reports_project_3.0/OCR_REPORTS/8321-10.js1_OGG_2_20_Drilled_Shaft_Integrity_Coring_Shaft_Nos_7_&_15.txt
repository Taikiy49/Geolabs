This technical memorandum presents the concrete integrity coring results for 
Drilled Shaft Nos. 7 and 15 at the Holdroom and Gate Improvements project at Kahului 
Airport on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. In accordance with the project specifications, 
concrete integrity coring on a minimum of one production drilled shaft at each of the three 
passenger boarding bridge (PBB) locations is required. In addition, the core hole should 
be a minimum of 3 inches in diameter, cored vertically, centrally located, and extend 
throughout the full depth of the drilled shaft. 
Drilled Shaft Installation 
Drilled Shaft Nos. 7 and 15 were installed at Gate Nos. 1 and 3, respectively, at 
the fixed columns for the PBBs. The location of drilled shaft Nos. 7 and 15 are shown on 
the Integrity Coring Location Plan (Plate 1). Generally, we observed the installation of 
Drilled Shaft No. 7 on September 15 and 16, and Drilled Shaft No. 15 on September 28 
and 29, 2022. The following provides a summary of the drilled shaft installation details for

Concrete Integrity Coring 
The concrete integrity coring was performed by Geolabs, Inc. using a Central Mine 
Equipment Model CME-45 truck-mounted drill equipped with PQ coring tools. The PQ 
coring tool cuts a hole measuring 4.8 inches (Outside Diameter) and extracts a 3.3-inch 
diameter core (Inside Diameter). All drilling fluid generated during the coring operations 
was contained within the pile cap excavations. 
After extraction of the concrete cores from the core barrel, measurements and 
photographs of the cores were taken. The cores were then wrapped in plastic wrap to 
preserve the natural moisture per ASTM C42. All coring depths were measured with 
reference to the existing top of drilled shaft concrete (Approximate Top of Drilled Shaft 
Concrete Elevation: +38.5 feet MSL).  
Detailed logs of the concrete coring are presented on the Log of Coring (Plates 2 
and 3). It should be noted that coring was terminated at 18 feet below the top of concrete 
for both Drilled Shaft Nos. 7 and 15 (approximately 2.5 to 2.8 feet less than the total drilled 
shaft length) due to interference with the steel reinforcing cross-bracing present near the 
bottom of the drilled shafts. Photographs of the core samples recovered from the drilled 
shaft coring operation is presented on Plates 4 and 5. Fractures observed in the core 
photographs are mechanical breaks due to the drilling operations. 
In general, based on the concrete cores retrieved during the integrity coring 
operation and our field observations during the concrete placement, we believe the 
concrete within Drilled Shaft Nos. 7 and 15 should be considered of “good” quality.
