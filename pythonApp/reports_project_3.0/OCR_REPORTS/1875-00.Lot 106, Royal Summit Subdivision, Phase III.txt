In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing of the
swelling potential of the soils and reviewing the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
The proposed residence will be a two story wood frame structure
approximately 52 by 56 feet in plan dimensions A semi basement level
with CMU walls up to 8 feet high is planned for the garage level
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the front of the house
including the garage and driveway areas The rear portion of the house
will utilize post and beam construction

The subject lot is located on the north eastern end of Leihulu Place

Discussion and Recommendations
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the moisture content of the
soil Therefore to reduce effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to reduce the swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the house areas the on site soil should
be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive
select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site

Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the swelling
potential of the natural soil

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported
soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete
placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and sprinkled
with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration An impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
small slab cracking are tolerable The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operation
be observed by the soil engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our evaluation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations
