This report presents the results of percolation tests performed
at the subject site adjacent to the existing King Kamehameha
Hotel in Kailua Kona Hawaii The location of our test borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The purpose of the percolation tests was to obtain onsite sub
surface drainage characteristics and obtain percolation data for
well design It is planned to use percolation holes for draining
surface run off water
FIELD INVESTIGATION
A total of four 3 5 inch diameter borings were drilled with a
skid mounted drilling machine with rotary rock bit to depths of
18 feet below the existing ground surface

SOIL CONDITIONS
The material encountered in all four borings was lava Puka puka
rock with soil cover varying from one to four feet in thickness
Voids and pukas were noted in the rock formation
WATER LEVELS
Water level was noted in all borings and it varied from one foot
in Boring 1 at the low lying area to eleven feet below the exist
ing ground surface in Boring 2 This groundwater level is
subjected to tidal influences
PERCOLATION TESTS
The percolation tests were performed by filling the boreholes
with water measuring the flow rate and water level in each hole
No casing was used in any of the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The open structure of the lava rock allowed large quantities of
water to be introduced in each hole without any substantial rise
in the water level The upper four feet of sand and clay in
Boring 1 collapsed in the hole and did not allow for complete
high flow testing The upper two feet of soil and rock in
Boring 4 collapsed from the water pressure when the flow reach
200 gpm
Page 3
W O 50600
Borings 2 and 3 took flow of over two hundred gpm without any
appreciable rise in water level A peak flow of 350 gpm for
two minutes was reached in these two borings There was no
collapse of these borings as they encountered rock at the
surface
Based on the results of our borings and percolation tests it
is our opinion that a 3 inch diameter hole will be capable
of percolating 300 gpm Proper maintenance should be provided
to prevent siltation of the holes Provision should be made
to case the loose material encountered above the water level
It is our recommendation that each percolation hole should
extend a minimum of 10 feet below the water level at each hole
location
Each percolation hole should be tested to insure the peak
design flow can be leached out at each location
