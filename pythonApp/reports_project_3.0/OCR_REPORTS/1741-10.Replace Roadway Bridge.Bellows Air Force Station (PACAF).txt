PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the replacement of an existing wooden bridge
over Waimanalo Stream on the eastern side of the station A new concrete
bridge is planned over the 90 foot distance across the stream
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
Originally the bridge was designed to span over the stream without a
center pier Therefore the previous exploration was conducted only for
the foundation design of the bridge abutments Subsequently it was
decided to evaluate possible cost savings by the use of a center pier to
reduce the structural loads
The purpose of this evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions in the center of the stream to provide design parameters for
the foundation support of the proposed middle pier for the bridge
structure
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling one 1
boring to 57 5 feet below the existing bridge surface Also included were
laboratory testing of selected samples obtained engineering analysis and

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The boring drilled at the bridge center indicated that in general
the near surface soils below the stream consists of very loose sandy silts
which are unsuitable for foundation support Therefore a pile foundation
is recommended to support the center pier of the proposed bridge
structure
Detailed discussion and recommendations are presented below
Pile Foundation
A hard coral reef formation was encountered at a depth of about 48
feet below the existing bridge surface This formation was penetrated for
approximately 10 feet and appears to be a suitable bearing stratum
Therefore end bearing piles into the coral formation are recommended for
the bridge center pier
It should be noted that coral formations are often erratic
particularly near stream areas since the presence of fresh water will
inhibit the growth of coral We recommend that additional probes be
drilled or test piles driven at the bridge center to verify the existence
of the coral layer

Discussions with the structural engineer indicate that the larger
pile section may be required due to the large unsupported height
of the pile
Due to the variable soil conditions we recommend that a minimum
of two 2 test piles for the bridge center be first installed to
determine the drivability of the piles and to estimate the final
production pile lengths for the project The lengths of these
test piles should be about 55 feet
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 30 000 foot pounds of energy The type of hammer to be
used for production pile driving should be the same one used for
test pile installation
After the test piles are installed one pile should be selected
by the soils engineer for load testing to two times the design
load of 106 tons in order to further verify the competency of the
bearing stratum
The 100 ton piles should be driven to a final driving resistance
of about 15 blows per inch for the last inch or to about 100
blows for the last foot
The final pile driving criteria may be modified after the
completion of the test pile installation and the load test
program
Both the test and production pile driving should be monitored by
a geotechnical engineer to see that they are driven into
competent bearing formation

Design Review
The plans for the bridge foundation should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The pile driving operation should be monitored on a full time basis
by a qualified soils engineer or a soils technician under the direction of
a soils engineer to verify that the piles have been driven in accordance
with our recommendations
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one
boring to a depth of 57 5 feet below the bridge surface The approximate
locations of this boring and as well as the two previous borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A versa drill was used to drill the boring
through the bridge center
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
