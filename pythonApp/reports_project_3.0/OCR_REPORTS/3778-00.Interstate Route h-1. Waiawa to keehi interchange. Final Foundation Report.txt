INTRODUCTION
The Keehi Field Testing Program KFTP is a project
oriented research program which was undertaken to confirm
or to modify in accordance with its findings preliminary
foundation design assumptions and thereby establish the final
design recommendations necessary for the completion of con
struction contract documents for the Keehi Interchange The
purpose of this report is to present those findings and field
experiences and to demonstrate how these were used in for
mulating the final foundation recommendations
This report should not be viewed as an exhaustive study
or as the final interpretation of all the data we have recorded
Such an endeavor would go beyond our present scope of work and
our client s needs It is however in our opinion a thorough
review sufficient to derive reliable cost effective solutions
to the design questions which we set out to answer
The report will address the area beginning with Pier ll
and continuing east of Ahua Street to Middle Street and Kalihi
Stream Final foundation recommendations for Piers 1 through
10 were presented in our previous Foundation Report of May
1976 entitled Final Report Foundation Design Puuloa Road
to Ahua Street

The ability of soil to adhere to a pile surface is termed
adhesion When adhesion acts in upward support of the pile
shaft it is termed positive skin friction When it acts in
the opposite direction so as to add to the service load on
the pile it is called negative skin friction This latter
Phenomenon will occur whenever there is settlement of soils
in the general area of a pile with respect to the pile itself
The adhesion at any time is a function of the soils
_shear strength at that time Because the underlying soils
are soft and are presently undergoing consolication they
will be increasing in shear strength ana thus adhesion with
time
As settlement is occuring over the project area the
soil immediately surrounding the pile will instead of com
pletely settling adhere to the pile perimeter ane a volume
of deformed soil in the near vicinity will hang from the pile
The weight of this hanging soil plus any Shear forces develowss
along the periphery of the deformed soil volume is termed
rag force or downdrag

COST EFFECTIVENESS DISCUSSION
The KFTP and other Keehi geotechnical investigations and
studies were undertaken because of the need to find engineer
ing answers to difficult questions In so doing solutions
have evolved which we believe are not oniy technically sound
but which have paved the way step by step for very sizable
construction cost savings Because of the expenditures
appropriated for the geotechnical investigations it may be
instructive to trace their cost effectiveness
At the inception of our participation in the foundation
design of Keehi Interchange the State DOT supplied us with
boring logs laboratory tests and pile load test results which
the State hac conducted Based on this information we were
to interpret the data and provide foundation recommendations
Our review and interpretation of that data pointed out
the need for additional and more extensive soils investiga
tions Thus new boring and laboratory testing programs were
undertaken In addition in situ vane shear testing was con
ducted borehole piezometers were installed in the soft clav
and a Dutch Cone Penetrometer exploration program was accom
plished
There was a marked difference between the previous labora
tory information and the new For instance the percent con
solidation of the soft clay was found to be greater than the
initial tests had indicated It was determined that the soft
clays were approximately 80 consolidated instead of 55 The
Bo
water content was found to be less than the liquid limit
whereas before the reverse was thought to be true
The preconsolidation pressure On the deep stiff clay
was reasonably determined which enabled us to make calcula
tions of the long term settlement of pile groups
The Dutch Cone penetrometer was primarily used in the
spread footing area between Puuloa Road and Ahua Street A
high capacity bearing layer was found and delineated at a
Shallow depth This gave rise to a 120 ton Pile recommen
dation in that area The friction jacket values correlated
very well with the vane shear values of the soft Clays Thi
type of corroborative information increased the confidence
in the data that was gathered
In the northeast corner of the project up along Middle
Street deposits of soft Clay thinned out and were more
accurately located and clearly defined py the new borings
and Dutch Cone Penetrometer Excavation and replacement of
this material with compacted granular backfill could now be
economically feasible and the extent of the viaduct structur
was reduced Significantly
The new boring and laboratory information made it possi
ble for us to define the limits of areas to be sand drained
versus structures This new information also enabled us to
increase the allowable bearing Capacities of the pil
in the deep areas from 45 tons to 75 tons This increase

gations many times over

SECTION 660 BITUMEN COATING
660 01 Description This work shall consist of furnishing and
applying bituminous coating and primer to prestressed concrete
pile surfaces as required in the plans and as specified herein
660 02 Materials
A Bituminous Coating Bituminous coating shall be an
asphalt type bitumen conforming to ASTM Designation D 946
with a minimum pentration grade 50 at the time of pile driving
Bituminous coating shall be applied uniformly over an asphalt
primer
B Primer Primer shall conform to the requirements of
ASTM Designation D 41
660 03 Construction Requirements All surfaces to be coated with
bitumen shall be dry and thoroughly cleaned of dust and loose
materials No primer or bitumen shall be applied in wet weather
nor when the temperature is below 65 F
The primer shall be applied to the surfaces and allowed to
completely dry before the bituminous coating is applied Primer
Shall be applied uniformly at the quantity of 1 gallon per 100
square feet of surface
Bitumen shall be applied uniformly at a temperature of not
less than 300 F nor more than 350 F and shall be applied either
by mopping brushing or spraying either at the plant or in the
field All holes or depressions in the concrete surface shall
be completely filled with bitumen The bituminous coating shall
be applied to a minimum dry thickness of 1 8 inch but in no
case shall the quantity of application be less than 8 gallons
per 100 square feet
Bitumen coated piles shall be stored before driving pro
tected from sunlight and heat Pile coatings shall not be exposed
to damage during storage hauling or handling The Contractor
shall take appropriate measures to preserve and maintain the
bitumen coating At the time of pile driving the bitumen coating
shall have a minimum dry thickness of 1 8 inch and a minimum
penetration value of 50 If necessary the Contractor shall re
coat the piles at his expense to comply with these requirements
660 04 Method of Measurement Bitumen coating will be measured
by the square yard of coating in place on concrete pile surfaces
No separate payment will be made for primer
