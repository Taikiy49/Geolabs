REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and the Koolau volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These late series eruptions are referred
to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series

During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stand When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above its present level
Glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations affected the processes of erosion and
deposition When the sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly
lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level
was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher
elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and transport downslope toward
the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials reach the
stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream
flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this the
transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists
of a poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the
erosional base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes begin
to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the material
Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse grained portion
of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or conglomerate
Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors and upstream in
headwaters This continued erosion generates material that is transported downslope
covering the older alluvial deposits Depending on the local base level and rate of
transport these newer sediments are generally transient in terms of geologic time In
addition their consistency and density are generally less than those of the older partially
consolidated deposits
The project site is located at the northeastern margin of a broad alluvial basin
encompassed by multiple stream channels and underlain by thick deposits of older
alluvium Some highly weathered basaltic rock materials representing younger volcanic
deposits of the Honolulu Volcanic Series may be encountered in close proximity of the
project site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located below the rear parking area for the Burger King
restaurant located in the Kaneohe area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The project site is bounded by the restaurant parking lot toward the
east Likelike Highway toward the north and residential development toward the west The
hillside project site encompasses three properties identified as
and 033
Based on our understanding the slope located at the rear of the Burger King
Restaurant experienced a slope failure and severe slope erosion caused by extended
heavy rainfall in March 2006 The slope is approximately 40 feet in overall height with sub
vertical and over steepened inclinations located near the top of the slope and flatter slope
inclinations of about one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V located at the middle to lower
portions of the slope The flatter middle and lower slopes appear to consist of loose soft
soils deposited as a result of colluvial accumulation derived from upslope erosion
Based on the available topographic survey of the site the ground surface
elevations range from about 85 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the toe of the slope to
about 125 feet MSL at the top of the slope
Based on our observation of the accessible and visible portions of the slope the
slope appears to be composed of semi consolidated older alluvium consisting of stiff clays
and silts with embedded and decomposed rock fragments and a few weathered cobbles
and boulders Some large trees with heavy brush type vegetation cover much of the
project hill slope surface Steepened hill slopes approximately inclination are
located at the margins of the recent slope erosion failure zone which were observed
covered by dense vegetation
Several homes with associated backyard wall structures were observed located
along the toe of the hill slope located on generally level ground surfaces An existing rear
parking lot for the restaurant is located approximately 10 to 20 feet from the top edge of
the hill slope An existing 4 foot high chain link fence is located along the top edge of the
slope separating the steep eroded hill slope from the adjacent level grades at the rear of
the restaurant
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
It appears that the slope failure was a result of saturation of the existing over
steepened slopes The existing slopes in the vicinity are steeper than 1H 1V which are
generally considered marginal for long term stability The recent heavy and prolonged
rains saturated and weakened the soils causing the instability

Stabilization of the failed slope would require either construction of a retaining
structure to support the slope or flattening the slope to a more stable ratio Several
alternatives have been considered and are described below
SHORT TERM STABILIZATION MEASURES
Since the slope failure has already encroached into private property and design
and construction of a permanent structure may take many months we evaluated
whether short term measures may be installed sooner
Temporary Sheetpile Wall
Steel sheetpiles may be installed along the upper boundary of the slope to
support the slope face There appears to be sufficient space to allow access for
equipment to install the sheetpiles from the Burger King parking lot The sheetpiles may
be left in a cantilever condition or a lateral support system Raker supports may be
installed to limit lateral deflection of the ground at the top A schematic section to
illustrate this alternative is presented on Plate 3
The advantage of this system is the relative simplicity and rapid installation
Disadvantages include the cost for the sheetpiles if left in place for a long period of time
and the possible damage to the existing pavements caused by vibrations during
installation The order of magnitude cost for this alternative is 400 000
Shotcrete Facing
The failed slope may be protected by a shotcrete facing to prevent water
infiltration and erosion of the slope face Due to the near vertical slope present the
failed slope must first be filled in order to place the shotcrete We anticipate that a filled
slope of 1H 1V would be sufficient to install the shotcrete facing and could be
constructed within the City property A shotcrete facing of about 4 inches with provisions
for weepholes is anticipated The backfill should consist of granular structural fill
compacted to a minimum of 90 relative density and keyed and benched into the
existing slope A schematic section to illustrate this alternative is presented on Plate 4
The advantage of this system is that it can be constructed entirely within the City
property and could be considered a short to moderate term solution with a design life of
a couple of years The disadvantage is the need for equipment access to the lower
portion of the site in order to construct the fill The order of magnitude cost for this
alternative is 200 000

LONG TERM STABILIZATION MEASURES
Slope Flattening
The existing steep slope may be flattened to a stable slope to achieve stability
For the material observed a maximum slope ratio of about is recommended
The slope grading would require provisions to prevent runoff over the slope face and
slope protection to prevent erosion This alternative would require grading about 10 to
30 feet into the Burger King property Since this alternative requires acquisition of
private property estimated costs were not evaluated
High Concrete Retaining Wall
A conventional concrete retaining wall may be constructed to support the slope
However due to the required height and footing width of wall it may extend outside of
the City property and into the State owned property A schematic section to illustrate
this alternative is presented on Plate 5 The order of magnitude costs for this alternative
are Due to the high cost and extensive grading required this alternative is
not recommended
Lower CRM Concrete Retaining Wall
A CRM or concrete retaining wall on the order of 20 feet high with a 2H 1V slope
backfill may be constructed to support the slope In order to reduce the wall height the
wall must be situated partially within the State property A schematic section to illustrate
this alternative is presented on Plate 6 Special construction equipment is not required
however equipment access will be required either through the Burger King parking lot
or residential properties
The advantage of this alternative is that conventional design and construction is
used The disadvantages include extensive grading equipment access required use of
State property and high cost The order of magnitude cost for this alternative is
750 000
Seqmental Retaining Wall
Segmental retaining walls consist of modular block face units with a reinforced fill
mass consisting of geogrids and granular fill materials Similar to the above wall
alternative the wall must be situated within the State property in order to reduce the
wall height and have sufficient space to install the reinforcing geogrids A schematic
section to illustrate this alternative is presented on Plate 7 The order of magnitude cost
for this alternative is 450 000

Drilled Shaft Retaining Wall
The drilled shaft retaining wall consists of a series of closely spaced drilled shafts
with a diameter of about 3 to 4 feet about 40 to 50 feet deep The drilled shafts would
be constructed from the Burger King parking lot but would be within the City property
Permanent casing may be required for a portion of the drilled shafts where the slide has
removed the soil support The space between the drilled shafts may be covered with
shotcrete or a decorative facing A schematic section to illustrate this alternative is
presented on Plate 8
The advantages of this alternative are that the wall may be constructed within the
City property and from the existing paved parking lot with minimal grading work on the
slope The disadvantage of this alternative is the relatively high cost The order of
magnitude cost for this alternative is 900 000
