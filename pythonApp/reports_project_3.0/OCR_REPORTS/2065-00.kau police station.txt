INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the the project site grading
and structure foundations of the proposed Kau Police Station near
Naalehu in the District of Kau on the island of Hawaii

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed development
The work was done generally in accordance with our fee proposal of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of five
5 borings to depths ranging between 10 0 and 15 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of
selected samples obtained during the field exploration engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the southeast of the Hawaii Belt
Road in the Kau District of the island of Hawaii The project is
situated next to the existing State Department of Transportation
Highways Baseyard approximately one 1 mile northeast toward Pahala
of the town of Naalehu The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is currently used as dairy pasture and is vegetated by a
thin cover of dry grass The topography of the site is nearly level
with a slight slope towards the southeast Access to the site is
through a gate from Hawaii Belt Road

The existing ground surface elevations of the site range from

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of
a police station facility The site improvements will involve the
construction of a single story metal framed building with slab on grade
floors two 2 parking areas and driveways Asphaltic concrete
pavements are anticipated
It is anticipated that the site grading will require cuts up to
about five 5 feet deep and placement of fills on the order of two to
three feet thick
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the southeast flank of Mauna Loa
a large basaltic shield volcano which makes up a large portion of the
mass of the island of Hawaii The site and its vicinity has been
mapped by Stearns and Macdonald 1946 as rocks of the Kahuku and Kau
Volcanic Series of Mauna Loa which are late Pleistocene to Recent in
age These rocks are generally thinly bedded primitive tholeitic
basalts may be capped with a thin layer of Pahala Ash
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of
ash cinder and Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and
when the moisture content is high enough it becomes thixotropic i e
it loses strength when remolded
Discussions with local residents indicate that the site was
formerly exposed rock and that fill was imported to provide a thin soil
cover This fill material may have been derived from sugar mill
washings and is most likely a combination of ash and organic matter

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging between 10 0
and 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a thin surface layer of stiff
brown silt Pahala Ash or Fill ranging from 0 2 to 1 5 feet in
thickness overlying a pahoehoe lava flow about 3 to 5 feet thick This
flow is underlain by 1 to 6 feet of clinkery material which is
underlain by another pahoehoe flow to the maximum depths explored
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of
this report
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings
during the time of our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered
at shallow depths below the surface of the project site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed police station and related site
work However due to the potential existence of cavities within the
volcanic rock we recommend a program of probing and grouting at
foundation locations be conducted during construction This is
discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of

Foundation Support
We recommend that the structure foundations bear on in situ basalt
or fill placed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein For the design of foundations bearing on in situ basalt an
allowable bearing capacity of 5000 p s f may be utilized For
foundations bearing on compacted fill an allowable bearing capacity of
3 000 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the
soft and or weak materials down to in situ basalt and backfilling of
the resultant void with fill material compacted in accordance with the
above recommendations

Settlements
Minimal settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the

Cavity Probing
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at
shallow depths below the project site Although the drilled borings
did not encounter any voids or cavities during our exploration
previous experience indicates that such cavities lava tubes are
commonly found in volcanic formations Several substantial tubes have
been found in the Kau District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void situated
below a building foundation causing foundation settlement we recommend
that the foundation subgrades be probed The probes should consist of
a 3 inch minimum diameter hole drilled to a depth of 10 feet below the
bottom of the footing Probes should be drilled at each major footing
location and on 10 foot centers along continuous or strip foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout 1
sand to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage
fashion i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions may be supported
by the basalt clinker or compacted fill A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine
rock should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab
subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report

by taping and estimation from reference points shown on the Site Plan
prepared by Matsumoto Suzuki and Associates Incorporated undated

levelling and estimation from the same plan The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used

VI
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within each
layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill
layer which has not been properly compacted Subsequent
removal of any unacceptable layer shall be done at the
Contractor s expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated
in any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained
material to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for
placement in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical
Engineer s Report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable
of producing the specified results in an expedient and
efficient manner The equipment shall be in good working
condition fully ballasted and self cleaning

The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a
uniformly compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above
specified levels of compaction out to the finish slope of
fill slopes This may be achieved by either overbuilding
the slope and cutting back to the compacted core or by
direct compaction of the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes
shall be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope
material into firm material and the transition stripped of
loose material prior to fill placement Fill shall be
placed and compacted from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not
in accordance with these specifications shall not be
approved and shall be removed or reworked until the required
density is obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform compaction tests and to make
field observations
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to heavy rain
the previous fill surface shall be approved before
resumption of earthmoving operations
