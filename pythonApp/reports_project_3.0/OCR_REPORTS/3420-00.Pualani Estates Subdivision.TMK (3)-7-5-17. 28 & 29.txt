REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is situated on the southern flank of Hualalai mountain which has
formed the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
site appears to be of pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the
ocean Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an
internal structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in
pahoehoe lavas These cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state
and represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and inter flow cavities such as
blisters and pockets Lava tubes intra flow cavities are formed when molten lava
drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for
a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller
in horizontal extent
Basalts are considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water
quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions Water is transmitted through
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3420 00 Page 6
the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the major water transmission features

Based on our review of the Probe Hole Logs and other available information it

ground surface It should be emphasized that the vertical and lateral extent of these

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials The ash soils
encountered near the ground surface should be removed to expose firm
materials
In areas to receive fill the subgrades should be proof rolled with a minimum 20
ton vibratory roller or a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment for
a minimum of 6 passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities Any
loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations
should be backfilled with compacted structural fill materials The proof rolling
operation should also be performed at the finish subgrades in cut areas
Due to the potential presence of cavities contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and _ structural fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction General fills and backfills
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 24 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Backfills behind retaining walls
should be compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction Over
compaction of the retaining wall backfill would exert additional lateral earth
pressures on the walls and should be avoided Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91 Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory
rollers multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable
compaction equipment
Rock fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum dimension of the
rocks or 5 feet whichever is less Placement should be by haul trucks traversing
previously placed lifts and dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3420 00 Page 14
Spreading of the rock fill should be by dozer to facilitate seating of the rock
The rock fill should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks
traversing in front of the current rock lift face and spraying water continuously
during placement Compaction equipment such as a 20 ton compactor or other
compaction equipment providing suitable energy to achieve the required
compaction should be utilized The number of passes to be made to achieve
proper compaction should be determined at the on set of site grading by the
performance of a trial compaction test or by plate bearing tests
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rock boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the
number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of
compaction should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis
of this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field
criterion for adequate compaction

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on subsurface information available it appears that most of the cut slope
would expose relatively dense basalt rock with frequent fractures In general we
believe that the cut slopes planned at the site may be designed with a slope
inclination of 1H 1V or flatter However based on the subsurface conditions

exposed on the cut slope face should be backfilled and or grouted if necessary

if the exposed materials are consistent with our assumptions
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 1 H 1V or
flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched

House Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that the in situ hard basalt formation and compacted structural fill generated from the
site grading would provide adequate foundation support for the proposed residential
structures Shallow isolated spread and or continuous footings may be used to support
the proposed structures
For foundations bearing on compacted structural fill and or basalt rock formation
we recommend that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f be used for
foundation design This assumes that the subgrades for all footings bearing on
compacted fill will be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
These bearing pressures are for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Where footings are located adjacent to slopes or on slopes
the footings should be embedded to a depth to provide a minimum horizontal set back
distance of at least 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footing to the face of
the slope Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or
utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane
projected upward from the bottom of the below grade structure

considered in the design of retaining walls Dynamic lateral earth pressures due
to seismic loading a 0 30g may be estimated by 10H p s f rectangular
pressure distribution for level backfill where H is the height of the wall in feet
For a 2H 1V sloping backfill condition a dynamic lateral earth pressure of

dynamic lateral earth pressures presented above are in addition to the static
lateral earth pressures An appropriately reduced factor of safety may be used
when dynamic lateral earth pressures are accounted for in the design of the

In general the purpose of the subbase course required in the County of Hawaii
Standard is to serve as a foundation or stabilizing course such that the subsequent
base and surfacing may be constructed on top of an inferior subgrade soil The
standard pavement section does not take into consideration good quality subgrade
conditions Since the subgrade soils along the proposed roadway alignment at the
proposed Pualani Estates Subdivision will consist of compacted granular fill material or
basalt rock formation it is our opinion that a subbase course for stabilization foundation
purpose is not necessary due to the high quality of the subgrade materials anticipated
at the subject site If the roadway subgrade exposes the near surface ash soil at the
site we recommend that the ash soils be removed and be replaced with structural fill
material 6 inch minus material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
If a subbase course is mandatory we believe that the compacted rock fill or
basalt rock formation below the base course layer can be considered as a subbase
material since its pavement support characteristics with CBR value over 60 would be
equal to or better than that of the select borrow subbase course required by the County
of Hawaii Standard On this basis we recommend the following preliminary pavement
designs be used for the subject site
