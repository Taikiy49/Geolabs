As requested on August 25 1984 we made a visual inspection of
the near surface soil conditions below the above referenced lot
house addition Tne purpose of the study was to provide con

Our involvement consisted of visual inspection of two 2 test

the encountered soils within the shallow pits and rendering of
our opinion on the foundation design at the bedroom and living
room additions This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed house addition will consist of two one story wood
structures dining and bedroom additions located near the up
slope rear portion of the existing post and beam wood frame
house Some heaving was noted on the brick pavement outside the
laundry area The existing rock wall located at the rear yard
area indicated that the wall is inadequately designed against the
on site adobe slope wash deposit Visual observations of the
garage retaining wall and the wood frame structures indicated it
does not appear there is instability in this and the neighboring
lots
Based on the above limited data it was agreed by the owner that
the soil engineer will have to assume that general site stability
of the lot is adequate and that our involvement is only to
provide him guidance in the design of footing foundation to
minimize the detrimental effect of the on site swelling adobe
clay soil conditions Post and beam construction with wood
floors are planned for the additions to the existing house
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site reconnaissance indicate that the proposed bedroom and
living room additions are located in the talus slope formation
underlain by clays and boulders

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration confirm that the dining and bedroom area
additions are underlain by medium stiff grayish brown clay with
gravel and boulders The laboratory test results on this
material generally indicated that adove clay has very high
swelling expansion potential and also it will be subjected to
shrinkage due to drying Therefore the foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of these subsoils
Foundation
For this house addition post and beam construction is highly
desirable and recommended due to the sloping terrain drainage

Post Footings
For post footing construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow
or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternately the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground surface The
footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled
and compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing
Footings on sloping areas should be embeded an additional depth
in order to provide a minimum 5 foot horizontal setback distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope face
Schematic sections for footings on expansive soil are shown on

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This
is especially important in the rear portion of the house to
reduce ponding of surface run off water possible saturation
settlement and or heaving of the foundation soils
On Grade Slab
We do not recommend the use of on grade slab in this area
However if they are absolutely needed they should be designed
to minimize the swelling and shrinkage characteristics of the
adobe soil We believe the slab should be on a minimum of 2 foot
select borrow fill Quarry Material over a moist subgrade
saturated with a 1 2 inch layer of hydrated lime
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
