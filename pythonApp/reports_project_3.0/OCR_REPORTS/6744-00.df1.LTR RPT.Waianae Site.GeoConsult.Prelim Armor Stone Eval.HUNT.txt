Geotechnical Engineering Consultation 
Preliminary Armor Stone Evaluation 
Waianae Sites, Oahu, Hawaii 
Dear Mr. Waters: 
As requested, we submit this letter report presenting the findings of our consultation 
services with respect to the use of rock materials from two Waianae area sites as armor 
stone for improvements at Iroquois Point. 
Project Considerations 
Armor stones are required for a beach restoration project at Iroquois Point.  Two 
potential sources of armor stone material have been located in Waianae area on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
It was desired to conduct a preliminary evaluation of the material to determine 
whether it might be suitable for the intended use. 
Purpose and Scope 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services is to provide consultation on 
the potential use of the rock material as armor stones.  Our scope of work generally 
consisted of the following tasks and work efforts: 
1. 
Visual and manual examination of stockpiled rock material by our project 
engineer/geologist. 
2. 
Collection of hand samples from randomly selected stones at the site for 
laboratory testing consisting of specific gravity and absorption. 
3. 
Preparation of this brief letter report summarizing our work on the project 
and presenting our findings.

Field Exploration 
On September 10, 2012, a site visit was made by our project engineer/geologist 
to make observations of rock materials at two sites in the Waianae District of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  These sites are identified as the “Malulani” site and the “Bully” site.   
Field testing was conducted at each site on a small stockpile of stones ranging 
from about 4 to 6 feet in diameter.  The field testing consisted of striking selected stones 
with a 3-pound sledge hammer, and drop testing of selected stones at the site.  Hand 
samples were collected for laboratory testing. 
Findings and Conclusions 
“Malulani” Site 
The stone material is generally blocky and sub-angular in shape.  The material 
appears to be residual corestones originating from the in-situ chemical weathering of 
basaltic lava flows.  These corestones are generally from the dense centers of a`a flows 
and represent the most resistant part of the old flows.  
Subjective testing was conducted by striking selected stones with a 3-pound 
sledge hammer.  This test indicated that most of the stones tested had very little or no 
rind of weathered material on their exteriors.  Most of the stones produced a ringing 
sound indicating very sound rock in their interiors.   
Drop testing was conducted on four selected stones in general accordance with 
the specifications by placing one large stone on the ground surface as an anvil stone and 
dropping another stone on to the anvil from a height of roughly 10 feet.  All of the stones 
tested survived at least two or three drops without breaking.   
Laboratory testing indicates that the samples collected conform to the specification 
for specific gravity and absorption.  The following table summarizes the laboratory test

Our laboratory testing indicates that the material generally meets the project 
specifications for armor stone.  However, there is a possibility of variability in the material 
as different areas are worked for the material.  Therefore, we recommend implementing a 
program of periodic testing as a quality control measure to assure that the material 
continues to meet specifications.
