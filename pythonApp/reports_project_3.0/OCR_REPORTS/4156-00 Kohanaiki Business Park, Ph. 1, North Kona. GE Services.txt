New Pavement Section
Kohanaiki Industrial Park Phase 1
Kaloko North Kona Island of Hawaii
Dear Mr Yamamoto
We understand that it is desired to re evaluate the pavement section for the roadway
planned at the Kohanaiki Industrial Park Phase 1 project Based on the information
provided asphalt concrete pavements will be used for the proposed roadways and parking
areas planned In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the proposed roadways
and parking areas for this project would consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light
trucks Therefore we have assumed light to medium traffic loading conditions for pavement
design purposes We understand that the following pavement section is generally required
by the County of Hawaii Department of Public Works
County of Hawaii Standard Detail R 34
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Moist Compacted Subgrade
In general the purpose of the subbase course required in the County of Hawaii
Standard section is to serve as a foundation or stabilizing course such that the subsequent
base and surfacing layers may be constructed on top of an inferior subgrade soil The
standard pavement section does not take into consideration good quality subgrade
conditions
Based on our on going construction observation services at the site we observed
that the roadway subgrade consists primarily of basalt rock or compacted granular fill
material Because of the generally good subgrade conditions along the proposed roadway
alignment at the Kohanaiki Industrial Park Phase 1 project it is our opinion that a subbase
course for stabilization foundation purposes is not necessary due to the high quality of the
subgrade materials anticipated at the site If the roadway subgrade exposes poorer

KOHANAIKI INDUSTRIAL PARK PHASE 1
KALOKO NORTH KONA HAWAII
Dear Mr Yamamoto
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing services
performed during the grading operations for the Kohanaiki Industrial Park Phase 1 project
Our services were conducted from through August 17 1999 in general
accordance with our revised fee proposal dated and additional budget
request of
SITE GRADING
The project site is located mauka of Queen Kaahumanu Highway in Kaloko in the
District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that it is proposed to
construct 26 individual industrial lots encompassing areas varying from about 1 01 to
1 63 acres per lot for the industrial park The total area of the industrial park grading is
approximately 30 4 acres
The site grading operations were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company In general
the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing and grubbing preparation of subgrades
placement and compaction of fills and trench excavation and backfill The depths of
excavations and thickness of embankment fills at the site were generally up to about 10 feet
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials that
were utilized as fill materials 3 inch minus were obtained and transported to our laboratory
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed basalt rock subgrades
were generally proof rolled with a bulldozer Caterpillar D 9 size or larger a minimum of
six passes Fills consisting of boulders rock fragments larger than 12 inches in dimension
up to about 5 feet in diameter were used to raise the prepared ground surfaces to the
finished mass grading grades shown on the plans The boulder fills were placed in generally
single boulder lifts moisture conditioned seated in place and compacted Compaction of
the boulder fills was achieved with a bulldozer Caterpillar D 9 size or larger passing over
the top of the boulder fill layer about four to six passes to facilitate seating of the boulders
In general fill areas in the roadways were capped with about 2 feet of 6 inch minus
granular materials Compaction of the 6 inch minus granular materials was accomplished
using a bulldozer Caterpillar D 9 size or larger passing over the fill surface a minimum of
four to six passes Backfills for the underground utility trenches generally consisted of 6 inch
minus granular materials After grading to the subgrade elevations an approximately 6 inch
thick layer of aggregate base material was placed over the finished subgrades of the
roadways followed by 2 inches of asphalt concrete as part of the roadway pavement
construction
Fill materials consisting of boulders were generally placed for grading of the industrial
lots Boulders up to about 3 feet in diameter were placed as fill materials up to near the
finished grades shown on the plans for most of the fill lots In some cases boulders as large
as about 5 feet in diameter were utilized as boulder fills In addition a thin surface layer of
well graded granular material was placed above the boulder fills to dress up the surface of
the lots
A portion of the Queen Kaahumanu Highway fronting the project area was widened
as part of this project Widening of the highway entailed construction of new pavements for
the new lane and shoulder areas of the highway Compaction tests were taken on the
untreated aggregate base course materials for the highway pavements
Due to the nature of the fill materials boulder fills and 6 inch minus fill materials the
fills and backfills were generally evaluated based on field observations during the fill
placement Where applicable such as the aggregate base materials and 3 inch minus fill
materials field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 and D 3017 Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill
operations where applicable to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of 291 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 In addition some sand cone density tests were also
performed on the highway pavement materials in general accordance with ASTM Test
