In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering evaluation at the above referenced site Our work was done in
general accordance with our proposal of
The purpose of the evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed antenna
The results of our field exploration which we used in the engineering
analysis to formulate our conclusion and recommendations are presented in
the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the 1907 lava flow approximately 1 to
1 5 miles south of Manuka Bay in the Kau District on the island of
Hawaii The site measures 20 by 20 feet in plan Ground surface
conditions at the site consist of loose boulders and cobbles reflecting
the original clinker lava flow surface Vegetation at the site is
non existent
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
From visual observations of the surface exposures and a seismic
refraction survey it appears that the general area is underlain by loose
to medium dense clinker at depths up to about 30 feet

selected for the antenna support Based on the preliminary design
drawings supplied to us 7 by 7 foot footings embedded 7 feet below the

Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the tower foundations The bottom of the excavations should be thoroughly
tamped prior to the placement of concrete
Based on our seismic velocity data and visual observations of the
on site conditions it is our opinion that the excavation into the
underlying clinker formation may be made with a large dozer or backhoe
However the actual rippability of the clinker formation should be
determined by the contractor on site
Excavated on site clinker broken down to 6 inch minus size may be
used aS a source of fill material If required all imported fill
material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or granular
materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve All backfill materials should be placed in maximum of 12 inch
horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 90 of the maximum density as
established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive granular
material tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from a seismic refraction survey
Variations of conditions away from the survey alignment points may occur

The location of the field data points were determined by tape
measurement on the site with reference to the provided preliminary site
plan The physical location of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
subsoils along the surveyed alignment depict the interpreted boundaries
between clinker zones with varying degree of density and as such may
denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or
cross section are based upon interpolation between field data points and
may not represent actual subsurface conditions
