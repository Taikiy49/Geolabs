I INTRODUCTION
A GENERAL _
This report presents the results of a soils investigation
for the proposed project The study consisted of subsurface
borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses as outlined 7
in our proposal dated and in accordance with
our contract Based on the results obtained engineering recom
mendations concerning design and construction are presented
t
The site of the proposed sewer force main alignment is in
the Nuupia Pond area of the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station
The project starts with a pump station at the existing KNCAS
sewage treatment facility and extends approximately 5000 feet
south and east to a proposed pump station at the Kailua Sewage
Treatment Plant
In general the first segment approximately Station 0 00 to
Station 18 00 proceeds southeast across a sanitary landfill
and the new H 3 highway right of way This segment was planned
as pile supported The second segment approximately Station 18 00
to Station 40 00 continues east and then south along an existing
coral fill road crossing Nuupia Pond This segment was proposed
1
to be supported above ground on piers The third segment
approximately Station 40 00 to Station 52 00 continues east
oe
terminating at the existing Kailua treatment plant
II FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of seven 7 borings
ranging in depth from 11 5 to 46 5 feet The total drilling
footage of 193 feet was accomplished using a truck mounted
rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight hollow stem
auger The borings were continuously inspected by an engineer
or geologist from our firm The approximate boring locations
are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch
O D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not
exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of
driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of
a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field and
representative material was placed in air tight containers and
returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing

III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Geologically most of this project will be constructed over
very loose coral sand and soft bay muds which extend 6 to more
than 15 feet below sea level The sand protion of this material
will be subjected to water movement caused by rise and fall of
tides This could produce a quick action which will reduce
the bearing capacity of the material
Overlying the very loose sand is road fill 1 to 4 feet
thick composed of imported coral fragments to provide a narrow
road across Nuupia Pond This road shows signs of settlement
and very likely coral material has been added from time to time
to keep the road in service over a period of many years Boring
Nos 3 and 4 show medium dense to dense sand below 6 feet but
hydrostatic water pressure pushed material up inside casing at
10 foot depth
Boring Nos 5 and 6 were drilled through trash dump fill
which is about 10 feet thick composed of wood and cardboard
boxes cans tires bed springs clothes trees cuttings and
other objects covered with thin layers of dirt
Boring No 7 was drilled in the new H 3 roadbed where 3 feet
or more of crushed rock has been provided for the road base
Water in all the borings stood at or near sea level

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
A GENERAL
The exploration program disclosed relatively poor subsurface
soil conditions along the proposed alignment The design and
construction of the proposed force main will require special
considerations in order to prevent serious problems The
following sections present our conclusions and recommendations
based on the limited investigation laboratory testing and
engineering analyses
B PUMP STATION at KMCAS SEWAGE TREATMENT PLANT _
Based on information obtained from previous studies see
Appendix A Boring Logs C the bottom of the pump station
slab elevation 97 5 U S N Datum will be in loose grey silty
coral fragments with sand and shells and will be below the
ground water table
The pump station may be supported on a mat foundation using
a maximum allowable net soil bearing pressure of 1000 psf
Settlement of the pump station should not exceed 14 inches
Pile support may also be used for foundation support to
reduce anticipated settlements
Since the pump station is below ground water level resistance
to hydrostatic uplift pressure must be provided If the dead
6
weight of the structure is not sufficient a friction factor of
0 25 can be applied to the lateral earth pressure for additional
resistance to uplift
The pump station walls should be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of pcf for submerged soil 62 pef for
water below the water table and 35 pcf above the water table
During construction large quantities of water are anticipated
Therefore a sheet piling cofferdam may be required for bracing
the pump station excavation and decreasing dewatering requirements
i
Flexible connections between the pump station and the force
main are recommended to prevent rupture of the line
The control house for the pump station may be designed for
spread or continuous footings with an allowable bearing capacity
of 500 psf The footings should have a minimum width of 14 inches
and should be at least 18 inches below the adjacent ground surface
C FIRST SEGMENT STATION 0 00 to 18 00
The area is underlain by very loose sand with zones of very
soft clay and silt covered by a heterogeneous trash fill Pile
support for this segment of the force main will therefore be
necessary to prevent excessive differential settlements
A 10 inch square prestressed concrete pile driven to a minimum
penetration of 30 feet should be capable of supporting design loads
of 20 tons each
7
C
All piles should be designed and driven in accordance with
governing building codes and should be driven under the super
vision of a qualified soils engineer
Settlements for piles are difficult to predict without a
static load test However we anticipate settlements of less
than 1 5 inches
Higher capacity piles and or smaller settlements could be
achieved by driving the piles to refusal in rock
Excavations of trenches deeper than 4 feet should be braced
per safety regulations
Backfill of the trenches should be relatively free draining
granular material properly compacted to 90 of the maximum density
based on ASTM D 1557 70
D SECOND SEGMENT STATION 18 00 to 40 00
This section of the alignment which is to be supported above
ground on piers crosses Nuupia Pond and is underlain by very
loose to medium dense SAND and soft to stiff CLAY Due to the
Significant difference in subsurface conditions observed between
boring B2 and 4 we recommend that additional
borings and or probes be made to more accurately define the sub
i
surface conditions along this portion of the alignment
Based on the available information the alignment in the
8
nae
vicinity of boring B2 cannot be supported on piers on spread
footings without remedial work The alternates available are
pile support or a preload fill and spread footings
To use spread footing in this area would require overfilling
the area to at least 5 feet above finish grade and allowing the
surcharge to remain in place for at least 3 months The excess
material could then be removed and spread footing could be
utilized More specific recommendations could be made after
additional investigation has been completed
Excluding the compressible areas e g boring B2 spread
footings may be utilized Footings may be designed for an
allowable bearing pressure of 500 psf The footings should have
a minimum width of 12 inches and should be at least 18 inches
below the adjacent ground surface
Settlements for the spread footings should not exceed 1 inch
Flexible connections are recommended at the transition points
H
between different types of pipe support
E THIRD SEGMENT STATION 40 00 to 52 00
This portion of the alignmént was underlain by sand and silt
and in trench type construction is planned
Excavation of the trenches in this area should be relatively
easy Excavation deeper than 4 feet should be braced to comply
ies
9
with safety regulations Dewatering the excavation should not
be necessary for the major portion of this segment however local
areas might require dewatering
We recommend Class B bedding consisting of compacted granular
bedding with compacted backfill The granular bedding should be
at least 6 inches thick and extend half way up the pipe at the
sides The remainder of the backfill should be compacted to
90 of the maximum density based on ASTM D 1557 70
Settlement of the pipe should be negligible if good construc
tion practices are followed
A friction factor of 0 27 between soil and pipe may be utilized
for design where necessary
As mentioned previously flexible connections are recommended
at transition points between different types of pipe support
