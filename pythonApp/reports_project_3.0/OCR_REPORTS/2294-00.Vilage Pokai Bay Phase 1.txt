INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary
geotechnical engineering exploration for the design of the mass
grading of the proposed Village Pokai Bay Phase I residential
development The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

Our work on the project was performed in general accordance
with our proposal to RYM Inc dated March 31 1989 This report
summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of clearing and grading the
approximately 32 acre site to construct 198 single family
residential lots
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our preliminary exploration was to obtain
soil rock data for the formulation of preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the mass
grading for the proposed Phase I residential development
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill
rig and operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of one 1 boring at the project
site to a depth of 14 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the
boring by a Field Engineer Geologist from our firm
4 Reconnaissance of the existing slope banks in the
proposed cut areas to observe and sample the soil profile
exposed

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the base of Puu Mailiili in the
Waianae district on the leeward coast of the island of Oahu
Hawaii Most of the site was formerly used as a coral limestone
quarry The remainder of the site is raw land that may have been
used as pasture but is now covered with dryland vegetation such as
grasses kiawe trees and haole koa

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
General
The project site is situated in a former limestone quarry
therefore there is an abundance of exposed cut slopes within the
area to allow visual observation and sampling of the subsoils
The visual observations were supplemented by the drilling of
one 1 boring to a depth of 14 5 feet below the lower portion of
the former quarry
Soil Conditions
The visual observations and boring indicated that the site is
underlain by cemented coralline sand and gravel with some hard
coral ledges Stiff alluvial deposits may be encountered below
about elevation 25 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is
underlain by competent coralline soils
Off site borrow soils will be required to supplement the on
site cut materials to attain the proposed final grades

Fill Material and Placement
It is anticipated that off site borrow from a designated site
near the project will be used for fill materials Preliminary
information indicates that the borrow soils may have moderate
to high swelling potential depending on the initial
compaction density and molding moisture content prior to
saturation Therefore in order to reduce the swelling
potential of the soil fill materials should be placed at
moisture contents above the optimum moisture content
Expansive soils should be placed a minimum of three 3 feet
below finished grade
Materials used for fills placed within the top three 3 feet
of finished grade should be non expansive generally less than
three 3 inches in maximum dimension and a plasticity index
PI per ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 not exceeding 20 The
on site coralline soils would be suitable for fill within the
upper three 3 feet

The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Each layer should be spread

uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of the material

Additional fill material should not be placed on any fill

Cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting
back to the design slope to obtain a well compacted slope
face
Slopes greater than 20 feet in height should have benches at
their mid points or at about 20 foot intervals
House Foundation Support
It is our opinion that shallow spread or strip footing
foundations may be used to support the houses Alternately
thickened edge slabs on grade may be used to support the lightly
loaded single family residential structures
For the design of the foundations of single family residential
structures an allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be
used for footings bearing either on the in situ soils or on
properly compacted fill consisting of on site materials The
bottom of footing foundations should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should
be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written
comment prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineer services during site grading and
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with
the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the
site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from visual observations
and geophysical surveys Variations of conditions may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were
approximately determined from reference points shown on the Grading
Plan prepared by Community Planning Inc The physical location
and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
