INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
investigation performed at the site of the proposed State
Judiciary Complex on Punchbowl Street east of the new Federal
Building The location of the site with respect to the sur
rounding area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 891 00 P 2
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed State Judiciary Complex will consist of a
cross shaped structure The main building or bar of the
cross will be four stories high with a basement parking and
a mechanical penthouse The northeast portion of the pro
posed structure will be only two stories high with a base
ment parking provided
The basement floor slab elevation is planned at 2 5 feet
MSL Column loads are not known at this time but they
are believed to be relatively heavy
SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is generally underlain by a calcareous
soil formation several feet below the upper surface fill
material consisting of coral fragments coral sand silt
and some seashell fragments This type of calcareous for
mation is common throughout the relatively lower level areas
of Honolulu and constitutes a major part of the cap rock for
the island s groundwater system A volcanic cinder layer
several feet thick underlies the upper calcareous soils
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 891 00 P 3
These calcareous formations were developed during a higher
stand of the sea which drowned the lower coastal land areas
and created upon them these shallow water coral reefs and
sand silt deposits The subsequent lowering of sea level
then exposed these shallow water reefs and the coastal land
areas became comprised of emerged coral reef material
Swampy surface conditions can be associated with these
emerged coastal lands when there is drainage from higher
elevations running into and collecting upon them because
of its flatness and closeness to base level The swampy
surface material would generally consist of organic silts
and clays and cinder deposits overlying the calcareous
emerged reef and sand formation
Surface Conditions
The site of the proposed State Judiciary Complex is
located on the intersection of Punchbowl Street and Pohukaina
Street For the northeast portion of the structure the
site extends towards Halekauwila Street across from Reed
Lane
Presently the site is being occupied by one story concrete
block and steel framed structures The space between the
existing structures is generally covered with asphaltic con
crete pavement Underground utilities are believed to
exist throughout the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 891 00 P 4
The site is relatively flat with the surface elevations
ranging from 6 5 to 5 2 feet mean sea level datum
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were investigated by
drilling ten exploratory borings at the locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 The depths of borings ranged from
25 0 to 100 0 feet below the existing ground surface
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring
logs and the laboratory test results
One piezometer was installed in Boring No 4 to monitor the
long range water level fluctuations Groundwater levels
observed in the piezometer and other borings during our in
vestigation is presented on the Log of Borings It is anti
cipated that the groundwater level will undergo minor sea
sonal fluctuations
Based on the information revealed from drilled borings the
site is underlain by a variable depth of loose to medium
dense fill composed predominantly of brown silty coral sand
and gravel The depth of fill ranges from approximately
2 0 to 4 0 feet below the existing grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 891 00 P 5
The variable fill is underlain by a loose to medium dense
tan coral sand and gray silty sand layer which varies from
a depth ranging form 2 0 to 7 5 feet below the existing
grade
Dense volcanic cinders was encountered underlying the above
materials and ranging in depth from 7 5 to 16 0 feet below
the existing grade
Generally dense to very dense silty coral formation under
lies the cinder formation throughout the site The depth
of the coral formation ranges from 12 to 18 feet below the
existing grade The coral formation is generally cemented
at the top and becomes less cemented with sand pockets at
deeper depths Boring No 3 encountered substantial amounts
of partially cemented sand pockets in this coral formation
Although the individual borings indicated the depths thick
nesses and trends of the coral it must be kept in mind
that coral reef formation are usually very variable and
conditions may change abruptly over short distances
Underlying the coral formation coral debris consisting of
medium dense to very dense Silty coral sand and gravel was
encountered ranging in depth from 38 0 feet to the depths
of most of the borings drilled Two deep borings Boring
Nos 5 and 6 encountered gray massive basalt at a depth of
approximately 88 0 feet and down to the 100 0 foot depths
drilled

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our field investigation shallow
pile foundation system appears the most feasible means to
transmit the heavy structural loads to the underlying coral
formation
Spread footing foundation System was also considered for
this project Footing excavations up to 14 feet would be
required to reach the volcanic cinders overlaying the coral
formation Dewatering required is anticipated to be diffi
cult along with shoring and bracing required to protect the
deep footing excavations Moreover the thickness of the
partially cemented cinder layer varies from 2 to 6 feet
and this would cause differential settlements between the
adjacent footings if it is used for foundation support
The above alternative and problems were discussed with
of Project Management
Structural Engineer and Mr Harry Miyagi Project Archi
tect in a meeting held on During this
meeting it was decided that shallow pile foundation system
Should be used for this project We would be happy to pro
vide additional recommendations for the design of spread
footings if required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 891 00 P 7
Specific recommendations for the design and construction
of the shallow pile foundation system are presented in the
subsequent sections of this report
Pile Foundation
Short end bearing pile foundation System consisting of
16 inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles should be
used to transfer the column and wall loads to the under
lying coral formation Based on the present subsurface
boring data the pile lengths are estimated to vary from
about 15 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface
Piles installed into the competent coral formation could
be designed to carry loads up to 100 tons each dead plus
live loads
The following guidelines should be used for the design and
construction of the pile foundation
1 Based on the present subsurface information we anticipate
that the pile lengths would vary from about 15 to 25 feet
for most of the piles However cavities and sand poc
kets are known to exist within the coral formation as
was disclosed in Boring No 3 and some piles may pene
trate deeper before reaching the required resistance
Based on our past experience 10 to 15 percent of the
piles may require deeper penetration to achieve the
above desired load carrying capacity

The western corner on Punchbowl and Pohukaina Street
intersection of the site was occupied by a warehouse
at the time of our field exploration and the information
on the subsurface conditions could not be obtained The
estimated pile lengths will be provided upon completion
of drilling additional borings in this area
Coral formation usually has an uneven surface and short
piles if installed without pre drilling will tend
to move away from their original location during driving
In order to avoid this situation it is important that
pile locations be pre drilled a minimum of one foot into
the coral formation prior to pile installation More
over the existing volcanic cinders overlying the coral
formation would provide obstruction to the pile installa
tion to the desired bearing stratum Therefore pre
drilling at the pile locations will be required through
the upper volcanic cinder layer and into the coral
The depths of predrilling would vary from 15 to 20 feet
below the existing ground surface
We recommend that a minimum of about 25 test piles be
installed to estimate the final production pile casting
lengths for this project The lengths of these test
piles should be about 25 feet long
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O
891 00 P 9
Pile should be driven with a hammer delivering about
30 000 foot pounds of energy The same type of hammer
should be used for the production pile driving as used
for the test pile installation
After the test piles are installed two to three piles
should be load tested to two times the design loads of
100 tons each in order to further verify the competency
of the bearing stratum
The 100 ton test piles should be driven to a final
driving resistance of about 25 blows per inch for the
last two inches and about 150 blows for the last foot
The final pile driving criteria may be modified after
the completion of the test pile installation and the
load test program
The piles should be spaced no closer than 3 times the
pile widths
Most of the foundation piles for this project will be
relatively short and they will be installed through
pre drilled holes The lateral pile capacity for these
short piles would be small about 1 kip each For
resisting the large lateral loads batter piles are
recommended

Exploration for Cavities
Cavities and loose sand pockets are known to exist in the
underlying shallow coral formation in this area as dis
closed at the adjacent State Credit Union job site and
the Federal Office Building To reduce the possibility
of later foundation settlement due to possible cavities under
the pile foundation in the shallow depth coral formation
it is our opinion that probe holes should be drilled in each
major column location prior to test pile installation The
probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
should extend a minimum of 10 feet into the underlying pile
Supporting coral formation The probe holes should be pressure
grouted from the bottom of the drilled holes prior to the
basement excavation During the probing operation if cavities
are encountered additional borings may be required to further
explore the size and nature of the underlying cavity We
recommend that the above probe drilling and backfill opera
tions be monitored by the project soils engineer
Settlement
Our preliminary estimates indicate that the total settlement
on the order of about half an inch and differential settle
ment equal to about half the total settlement should be
anticipated Most of the settlement should occur during
the building construction Settlement predictions may be
revised upon the review of the pile load test data

Basement Wall
Basement walls up to 4 feet high are planned for the proposed
structure Pile foundation would be utilized to support the
basement wall and structural loads
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
on site sand should be used as backfill material behind the
walls Impervious material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended for
the basement wall design
Backfill above water 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Backfill below water 25 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure plus
water pressure
Dewatering
Some groundwater dewatering may be required during the
excavations for the basement parking and other excavations
below the groundwater level Some seepage water may also
occur due to percolation of rainfall through the upper fill
and cinder layers Dewatering of seepage water and surface
run off may be accomplished by sump pumps placed at the bot
tom of the excavation
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 891 00 P 12
Basement Parking Slab
It is our present understanding that the proposed basement
parking floor slab would be at elevation 2 5 feet MSL
It is anticipated that seasonal high tides may cause occasional
infiltration of groundwater close to the bottom of the slab
Concrete slabs should be designed to sustain these uplift
pressures caused during high tides Basement slabs may be
Supported either on pile caps or on grade If slabs are
supported on grade then they should be Structurally dis
connected from the adjacent pile caps and walls in order
to minimize the effect of differential settlement between
the on grade floor slabs and pile foundations
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of granular rock gravel material such as 3B Fine rock
to act as a capillary break
Earthwork
The site should be cleared of all debris and old foundations
prior to any filling
Select on site sandy material should be stockpiled and reused
as fill and footing backfill material The fill and back
fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches
maximum and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557

Additional Field Exploration
The western corner on Punchbowl and Pohukaina Street
intersection was occupied by an existing warehouse at the
time of our field exploration No borings were made to
explore the subsurface soil conditions in this part of the
building area We recommend that after the warehouse
structure has been demolished a minimum of three additional
borings about 50 feet deep each should be drilled to pro
vide the required subsurface information
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 891 00 P 14
If the structure is to be supported on piles the pile
driving operations along with the predrilling of the pile
locations should be monitored on a full time basis by a
qualified soils technician under the direction of a soils
engineer to verify that the piles have been driven in
accordance with the recommendations
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 10 borings ranging in depth from 25 0 to 100 0
feet below the existing ground surface The locations of
the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 19 In
the borings samples were obtained by driving either a stan
dard penetration split spoon sampler or 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance
of 30 inches Four inch core samples of rock were obtained
by using a 4 inch core barrel The blow counts to drive the
sampler for the last 12 inches along with percent core recovery
and RQD are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depth

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
made on selected samples as an aid in the soil classification
and in evaluating soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
location
Seven Sieve Analyses were performed on selected coral
sand samples to evaluate the percentages of different grain
sizes The results are presented on Plate B and B 2
One Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial test and Direct
Shear test were performed to evaluate the strength character
istics of the encountered soils Boring No 3 at 5 0 to 6 5
feet samples were remolded before testing because of the
loose state of the silty coral sand encountered The test
results are shown on Plates B 3 and B 4
The strength of the coral formation was evaluated by
subjecting representive rock core specimens to unconfined
compression tests The results of the strength tests are
presented on Plate B S
