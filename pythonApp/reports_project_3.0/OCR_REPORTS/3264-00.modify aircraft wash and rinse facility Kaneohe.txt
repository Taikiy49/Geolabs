INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed modifications at the existing wash rack and rinse facilities
within the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station KMCAS in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general locations of the project sites and their vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommen
dations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These geotechnical engineering recommendations are intended for the design of
pavements and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of the
middile to fate Pliocene in age and Koolatris estimated to be of the tate Pliocene to early
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3264 00 Page 7
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion cut
valleys deep into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
The site of the KMCAS at Kaneohe Bay is located on Mokapu Peninsula which was
built by three volcanic vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series These vents are believed
to be among the oldest of the series The pyroclastic deposits and lava flows from vents
formed salients permitting the growth of a coral reef that merged with the main body of
the island of Oahu
During the Pleistocene ice Age lowering of the sea level in response to
continental glaciation sands from emerged coral reefs were blown inland by the prevailing
winds to form sand dunes along the northern portion of the airfield
An extensive deposit of unconsolidated calcareous marine sediments primarily
consisting of beach sand made up of water worn coral shells and organisms covers the
western tip of Mokapu Peninsula Man made fills consisting of marine sediments
dredged from the ocean floor were used to build up the area around the southern
and western shorelines of Mokapu Peninsula

Subgrade Preparation
Since different subgrade conditions were encountered at the wash and rinse
facilities different subgrade preparation requirements will be necessary The following
presents our recommendations for subgrade preparation
Wash Rack Facility
For the wash rack facility we recommend that the subgrade be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction based on 100 percent design gross weight The subgrade
compaction requirement should be increased to 100 percent relative compaction
for pavement design based on 75 percent design gross weight Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
To reduce the potential for subgrade pumping heavy or rubber tired equipment
are not recommended In addition vibratory action of the compaction equipment
should not be used to reduce potential pumping of the subgrade If soft clayey

Base Preparation
The new flexible and rigid pavement sections planned for this project will consist
of asphalt concrete and Portland Cement Concrete pavements overlying an aggregate
base course The primary functions of the aggregate base course consist of the
following
1 To provide uniform support for the pavement surface course throughout the
design life
2 To improve load transfer across joints
3 To reduce potential pumping by providing drainage through the use of a
material with little or no fines
4 To provide a construction platform_to expedite construction

5 To reduce the potential for volume change in the subgrade soils by

potential

Portland Cement Concrete Overlay
Strengthening of a pavement will increase its load bearing capacity and is
accomplished with the use of an overlay of either asphalt concrete or Portland Cement
Concrete Fully bonded concrete overlays are used only where the base slab is in
relatively good condition and no structural distress is evident The new concrete overlay
would be placed directly on top of the base slab Prior to placing the overlay the surface
of the base slab must be properly prepared to ensure a full bond with the overlay By
bonding the overlay to the existing base pavement the new section should behave as a
monolithic slab Joints in the overlay must be matched by type expansion matched with
expansion contraction with contraction and by location to those in the base slab
The overlay thickness design equation is an empirical equation originally based on
field test sections and subsequently modified according to in service airfield pavements

Material Specifications
Engineering fabric consisting of pavement reinforcing fabric shall conform to the
requirements in this section The fabric shall be placed in accordance with the
requirements in this section The engineering fabric shall be furnished in protective
covers capable of protecting the fabric from ultraviolet rays abrasion and water
The pavement reinforcing fabric shall be manufactured from high modulus woven
polypropylene material The fabric shall also conform in all respects to the property
requirements listed below
Average Grab Tensile Strength ASTM D pounds
Maximum Grab Tensile Elongation ASTM D percent
Average Burst Strength ASTM D psi
Trapezoid Tear Strength ASTM D pounds
Puncture Resistance ASTM D pounds
Roll Size and Weight Approximately 500 Square Yards weighing
approximately 210 pounds

Placement Specifications
The fabric should be laid in the direction of construction traffic Fabric rolls should
be laid out such that they overlap by a minimum of 24 inches at the ends and sided of
rolls Aggregate should be back dumped on the fabric after it has been pulled taut in
both directions over the subgrade Wheeled or tracked vehicles should not be operated
directly on the fabric

Guideline Specifications for Geogrids
Biaxial 1100 or equivalent
Material Specifications
The geogrid shall be an integrally formed grid structure with high tensile modulus
in relation to the material being reinforced with large apertures and thick ribs and
junctions to permit significant mechanical interlock with the material being reinforced and
with high continuity of tensile strength through all the ribs and junctions of the structure
The geogrid shall maintain its reinforcement and interlock capabilities under repeated
dynamic loads and shall be resistant to both ultraviolet degradation and all forms of
biological or chemical degradation normally encountered in the material being reinforced
The geogrid shall also conform in all respects to the property requirements listed
below
