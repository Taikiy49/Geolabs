Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C103 85
Range User Facility
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Range User Facility at the Pacific Missile
Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
general location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Range User Facility includes an air conditioned pre engineered
steel building approximately 3 000 square feet in area Total weight of
the proposed building is approximately 20 kips Fill up to about 1 0
foot is anticipated for the construction of the building
W O Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the soil underlying the proposed
facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located along Lii Road in the adjacent lot
located to the northwest of Building 105 at the Pacific Missile Range
Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 3
The site is presently confined by chain link fence and used as a
temporary parking yard for big trailers As a result one of our borings
was drilled outside the perimeter fence while the second boring was
located inside the adjacent open lot to the south of the proposed
pre engineered building site
Ground level at the site is sloping gently down from the north
toward the south Ground cover consists mainly of fine beach sand with
silt and coral gravel
Subsoil Condition
Two borings were drilled to depth of 20 feet below the ground
surface Both borings encountered dense to very dense dark brown to
brown fine beach sand with silt underlaid by dense to very dense beach
sand with medium grain size at a depth of about 8 feet
Groundwater was encountered at a depth of 15 to 16 feet below
ground surface at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light building load
we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing on the
upper in situ dense fine beach sand may be used
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O Page 4
For footings bearing on sand we recommend they should be placed a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift
wind blast or other lateral loading condition may further increase the
embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used to determine the passive resistance
Site Grading
Due to the gently sloping ground small fill up to about one 1
foot is anticipated for the site development
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be kept
moist and tamped prior to placement of the footing if they are loosened
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O Page 5
during the excavation The areas which are to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the cleared ground
surface moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density Any loose or soft spots encountered at the footing or
subgrade level should be recompacted if it is sandy or removed if clayey
or organic and replaced with select on site sandy soil or with suitable
imported material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density If
the on site fine sandy soil is used the compaction requirement can be
modified to a range of 90 to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density for
the top 6 inches below the subgrade elevation for the areas which are to
receive pavement

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

proposed construction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
LS OM a me ete
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
