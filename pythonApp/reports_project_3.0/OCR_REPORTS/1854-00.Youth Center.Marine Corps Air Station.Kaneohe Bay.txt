PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering study was to explore
the subsurface soil conditions for the design of the foundation for the
proposed building
The main objectives of our work were to explore the thickness and
competency of the underlying soil for spread footing foundation support
and to investigate the swelling or expansion potential of the surface
soil and its effect upon the the new building slab and parking areas
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling four 4
borings 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface Also included
were laboratory testing of the selected samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station on
the eastern shore of the island of Oahu The site is approximately 220
by 120 feet in plan dimension and bordered to the northwest by Lawrence
Road The elementary school is about 150 feet southwest of the site
The ground surface elevations vary from about 105 to about 108 feet
The site slopes down gently to the northwest and is covered with grass
lawn and Kiawe trees A covered pavilion with a concrete bench and
table as well as several other concrete slabs with tables presently
exist at the site
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
four 4 borings to depths of 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings encountered a crust of dense fill about 2 to 3 feet
thick overlying deposits of stiff clayey silt Borings 2 and 3
encountered a 2 to 4 feet thick layer of loose silty sand at about 3 feet
below the existing ground surface near the water level
Groundwater level was encountered in Borings 1 to 3 at the time of
our field exploration and varied from 3 8 to 5 4 feet below the existing
ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface stiff clayey silt
soil indicate that this material generally has medium swelling potential
depending on the in situ moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent
subgrade saturation Therefore to reduce the effects due to possible
soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the subgrade soils
underneath the slab be kept moist and protected from drying from the
start of construction until fill placement and or slab concrete
placement
About 2 to 3 5 feet of fill is planned in order to provide a
finished floor for the proposed building at elevation 108 5 feet
Select non expansive import fill material should be used to provide
adequate foundation support of the new building and to reduce possible
soil swelling
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished floor of the proposed
building will be at elevation 108 5 feet Therefore 2 to 3 5 feet of
fill is planned for site grading Settlement due to 3 5 feet of fill is
estimated to be on the order of less than one inch However the
settlement is expected to occur rapidly
Prior to site filling the existing vegetation trees broken
structures and any concrete slabs encountered should be removed The
exposed subgrade soil should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches Rubbish deleterious materials such as large roots should be
removed The scarified subgrade should be proof rolled moisture
conditioned and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft materials encountered during
proof rolling should be removed

Laboratory tests performed on the near surface soil samples indicate
that this material generally has medium swelling potential Therefore
to minimize effects due to soil swelling we recommend that the subgrade
soils underneath the structural fill and pavement be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until placement of
structural fill and or pavement
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive
granular soils and submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory
testing and approval prior to its intended delivery to the project site
Selected non expansive granular material with maximm size less than 3
inches are recommended for the structural fill material
Fills should be placed in maximum 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78 test method
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for the
anticipated light foundation loads on the compacted structural fill The
bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for short duration wind and seismic loading
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of

Five one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the undisturbed
and recompacted soils respectively The test results are presented on
Plate 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on select undisturbed samples
to evaluate the shear strength parameter of the soil The test results
are plotted on Plates B 3 and B 4
