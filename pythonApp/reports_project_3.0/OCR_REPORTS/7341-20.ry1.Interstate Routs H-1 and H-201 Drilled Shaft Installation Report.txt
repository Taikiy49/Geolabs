recommendations as appropriate.

The first project site, identified as Destination Sign H1EBR-253, is located north of 
Nimitz Highway at Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam Eastbound Exit east of the main gates 
of Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam. Based on the project plans, we understand that the 
objective is to replace the existing two-post sign structure with another two-post sign 
structure approximately 10 feet west of the existing structure. Based on the project plans 
provided, we understand that the new posts would be supported by deep foundations 
consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts with a diameter of 60 inches and an 
embedment depth of approximately 14 feet. The approximate location of the first site is 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.1. 
The second project site, identified as Destination Sign 78EBR-830, is located at 
Interstate Route H-1 Eastbound Off-Ramp (Exit 3) to Tripler Hospital and the Airport. Based 
on the project plans, we understand the objective is to replace the existing two-post sign 
structure with another two-post sign structure approximately 5 feet east of the existing 
structure. The new posts would be supported by deep foundations consisting of 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts with a diameter of 60 inches and an embedment depth 
of approximately 20 feet. The approximate location of the second destination sign site is 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.2. 
The third project site, identified as Destination Sign H1EB-305, is located at Interstate 
Route H-1 Eastbound Off-Ramp (Exit 20B) to Vineyard Boulevard. Based on the project 
plans, we understand the objective is to replace the existing one post double cantilever sign 
structure with another one-post double cantilever sign structure approximately 10 feet east 
of the existing structure. The new post would be supported by a deep foundation consisting 
of cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts with a diameter of 60 inches and an embedment 
depth of approximately 30 feet. The approximate location of the third destination sign site is 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.3. 
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the drilled 
shaft foundation construction for the new destination signs for Interstate Routes H-1 and 
H-201, Destination Sign Upgrade/Replacement, Phase 3 project at the locations mentioned 
above only. Global Specialty Contractors, Inc. was the general contractor responsible for 
the project’s installation of the reinforcing cage and concrete placement. Kelikai, Inc. was 
the subcontractor responsible for the drilled shaft excavation. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
underlying volcanic tuff and alluvial deposits was utilized for foundation support of the new 
destination sign structures.

at various locations on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Site Plans, Plates 2.1

Representatives from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement 
of reinforcing cage, and concrete placement operation on a near-continuous (full-time) basis 
during the installation of the production drilled shafts. The diameter and as-built lengths of 
the drilled shafts installed for the project are provided in the Drilled Shaft Installation 
Summary, Plates 3.1 through 3.3. The drilled shaft records are provided in the Appendix 
(Plates A-1.1 through A-5.4) of this report. 
In general, the drilled shaft foundations were excavated utilizing a Bauer BG-33H 
Track Mounted drill rig equipped with excavation tools. Excavation tools used for the drilled 
shaft installation included a 60-inch diameter rock auger, core barrel, digging bucket, and 
clean-out bucket. It should be noted that a temporary casing was used for Destination Sign 
H1EB-305 due to groundwater encountered. Groundwater was only encountered at 
Destination Sign H1EB-305 at a depth of approximately 6 feet below the existing grade at 
the time of shaft installation. The groundwater levels measured generally corresponded to 
about Elevation +4.6 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
After excavating the drilled shafts to the designated tip elevations and cleaning out 
the bottom of the shaft hole, the reinforcing cage was lowered into the drilled hole to the 
appropriate elevations using the cable line of the Bauer BG-33 Track Mounted drill rig or the 
CAT TL1255D telescopic forklift. For Destination Signs H1EBR-253 and 78EBR-830 sites, 
concrete was dropped through a chute that extended down the hole. Concrete with a 
measured slump of 8 inches and a temperature of 80 degrees was used for the drilled shafts 
at the Destination Sign H1EBR-253 site. Concrete with a measured slump ranging from 6.75 
to 7 inches and a temperature of 87 degrees was used for the drilled shafts at the 
Destination Sign 78EBR-830 site. 
For the Destination Sign H1EB-305 site, concrete was placed through a tremie pipe 
due to the presence of groundwater. During concrete placement, we observed that at least 
5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe. Concrete with 
a slump of 5 inches and a temperature of 84 degrees was used for the drilled shaft

that the drilled shafts supporting the new destination signs for Interstate Routes H-1 and 
H-201, Destination Sign Upgrade/Replacement, Phase 3 project were installed in general
