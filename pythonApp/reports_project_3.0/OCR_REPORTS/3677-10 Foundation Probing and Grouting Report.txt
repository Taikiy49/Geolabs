Foundation Probing and Grouting Report
Proposed New Office Building
512 Kalihi Street
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated January 7th we have completed the
foundation probing and grouting services for the subject project This report summarizes
our field observation of the probing and grouting performed at the site
The project involves the construction of a new two story office building The project
site is located at the corner of Kalihi and Colburn Streets in the Kalihi area in Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The intent of the foundation probing and grouting is to attempt to
locate and fill subsurface cavities beneath the foundation of the proposed building The
foundation probing and grouting operations were conducted on January 31st
Foundation probe holes were drilled at proposed column footing locations and or
spaced at approximately 15 foot centers for continuous footings A total of 28 probe
holes 4 inch diameter were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig for a total of 336 lineal
feet Probe holes were drilled to a depth of 12 feet which corresponds to the minimum
depth of 10 feet below the bottom of the footing elevation level Approximate locations of
the foundation probe holes are shown on the attached Foundation Plan Plate 1
Materials encountered during the probe drilling consisted typically of tan coral sand
and gravel which typically is the result of the cuttings from coral formation Based on our
observation of the probing operations it appeared that cavities and or voids were not
encountered at the site A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting

Upon drilling of the probe holes the holes were fitted with 2 inch PVC pipes to
maintain the integrity of the open hole During grouting operations each probe hole was
grouted from the desired depth upwards with low to moderate injection pressures A
sand cement mixture was used to grout the probe holes A total of approximately
1 3 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 28 probe holes Our observations of the
grouting operations and the estimated grout volume are summarized on Plate 2
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations were conducted in
general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical
engineering report Based on our observations cavities were not encountered in the
probe holes therefore we anticipate that the footing subgrade should provide suitable
foundation support for the proposed building
