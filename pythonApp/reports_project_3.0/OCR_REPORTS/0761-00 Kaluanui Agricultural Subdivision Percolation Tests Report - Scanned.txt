This report presents the results of our percolation tests
performed at about the center of the subject proposed sub
division development mauka of Kamehameha Highway in Kaluanui
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site and in situ seepage
characteristics for the design of sewage disposal system
Field Exploration
Two 4 inch diameter auger borings were drilled down to
25 feet below the existing ground surface at the location
shown on Plate 1 The drilling operation was monitored by
our geologist who also logged the borings The boring logs
are presented on Plates A 1 A 2

P 2
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclose that the area immediately
Surrounding the boring area is blanketed by a thin 1 to 2 feet
layer of soft brown clay in the higher areas and a layer of
organic clays of undetermined thickness in the depressions
swamp These conditions are underlain by loose gray and
brown sand and gravel with some brown clay lenses
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 1 at 14 inches and in
Boring 2 at 2 feet 8 inches below the existing ground surface
Percolation Test
A constant head percolation test was performed in lach of the
two drilled holes The tests were completed by vides filling
the drilled hole up with water then adjusting the flow until
a constant head of water was maintained at the ground surface
The rates of water dropped into the boring holes were recorded
ranging from 3 2 to 27 3 gallons per minute see Plate A 3
Additional seepage rate was obtained at B 2 location by the
falling head test For details of this test refer to Pilate
A 4 Falling Head Test Boring No 2 Falling head test was
not performed at B 1 location since it took only 15 seconds
for the drilled hole to recover from overflowing

Based on the test results for the above bore holes it is
our opinion that the seepage rates through the clayey sand
and gravel layers below the existing high ground water level
will be variable However it should provide adequate per
colation rates for the proposed private cesspool sewage
disposal system
Due to the high water level the cesspool excavation should
be performed with care and speed It should also be back
filled immediately with free dratning granular material as
soon as the pre cast concrete manhole units are installed
Additional layer of granular filter rock is also recommended
to be placed at the bottom of each cesspool
Based on the percolation test data obtained at the drilled
holes a 6 foot diameter cesspool extending a minimum depth
of 20 feet below the ground water level into the underlying
sandy clay and gravel layers would be required
