Dear Mr Yung
In accordance with your request we have observed the test pile driving operations
for the above referenced project on May 21 and 22 1998 A diesel
hammer was used to drive the 16 5 inch octagonal precast and prestressed concrete
indicator piles Our field observation indicated that setting number 4 was used for the pile
hammer during the test pile driving operation with a rated energy of approximately
39 400 foot pounds
Test Pile Driving Observations
The test pile driving operations were performed by Lahaina Pier Pile Company
Inc LPPC A total of 25 test piles consisting of Pile 83 89
221 227 and
233 were driven to depths of about 25 6 to 56 feet below the existing ground surface at
the site Pile Nos 68 and 208 were damaged and or broken during the test pile driving
operations Therefore replacement piles will need to be driven for these two piles during
the remaining production pile program Individual pile driving records have been
transmitted to your office during the course of the test pile driving operation The
foundation and predrilling plans summary and pile driving records are attached for your
reference

Pile Nos 41 and 192 were subsequently selected to be load tested with the pile
load test being performed by Ernest K Hirata Associates Inc EKHA A copy of the
Pile Load Test Results prepared by EKHA was transmitted to our office for our analyses
Based on our test pile driving observations and the pile load test results provided it is our
opinion that the test piles excluding Pile Nos 68 and 208 have been driven to sufficient
depths and or resistance to provide the design vertical compressive load capacity of
65 tons per pile and may be incorporated into the actual foundation system for the subject
project
Predrilling Depths
Based on our field observations during the test pile driving operations we
recommend that predrilling be performed for the remaining production piles to facilitate
the pile driving operations through the hard coral ledges encountered at the project site
A predrilling depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface may be used for the
majority of the remaining production piles as shown on the Predrilling Plan Plate 1
Based on the test pile driving results a deeper predrilling depth of 30 feet is
recommended for the piles located within the clouded area to reduce the possibility of
supporting a large number of piles on shallow coral ledges
Production Pile Lengths
The remaining project production piles excluding the clouded area may be cast
with the estimated pile lengths as shown on the attached Foundation Plan Plate 2
Based on the available subsoil information and our test pile driving observations we
recommend that Pile 160 152 and 218 located within the clouded area
shown in the attached Foundation Plan be driven first to determine the actual pile lengths
required in this area A cast length of 60 feet may be used for these five indicator piles
Production Pile Driving Criteria
The project production piles should be driven with the same hammer and setting
and the pile driving operation may be terminated when one of the following two driving
criteria are met
e The pile driving may be terminated when the aggregated blow counts are at
least 70 blows per foot for the last foot
The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts reach 15 blows
per inch for the last 3 inches
