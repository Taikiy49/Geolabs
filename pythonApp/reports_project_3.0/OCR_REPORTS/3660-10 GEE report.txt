INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed banana packing plant at the relocated site in the Mokuleia
area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses of the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed banana packing plant site was originally located at a junction of the
cane haul roads between Farrington Highway and Kawaihapai Reservoir in the Mokuleia
area on the island of Oahu Hawaii A geotechnical engineering exploration was
conducted for the proposed banana packing plant and our findings and
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Proposed Banana Packing Plant Mokuleia Oahu Hawaii dated
Based on the updated information provided to us we understand that the
proposed banana packing plant was subsequently relocated from its original planned
location to about mile toward Kaena Point In addition the relocated banana packing
plant will be smaller in size measuring approximately 100 by 100 square feet in area We
anticipate that the proposed packing plant will be an open steel framed structure with
concrete slab on grade and steel columns supporting the roof load Detailed information
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 3
pertaining to the structural loads and finish floor elevation of the proposed structure were
not available at the time this report was prepared Based on the existing topography and
current design concept we understand that minor site filling is planned to attain the
design finished grades for the project construction We understand that on site sewage
disposal system will be utilized for the subject project Therefore field percolation tests
were conducted to obtain data for preliminary design of the sewage disposal system
Results of the field percolation tests will be submitted under a separate report
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site the subsurface condition
will likely consist of alluvial deposits consisting of sandy and clayey soils Special
attention was given to the swelling potential of the near surface soils and its effects on the
slab on grade construction for the project Because of budgetary constraint test pit
excavations in lieu of borehole drilling were performed during our field exploration The
backhoe excavator and operator were provided for our field exploration use by the
Owner Client

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and forms
the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lava banking
against its eastern flank
The project site is located on the north flank of the Waianae Shield Volcano After
the cessation of volcanic activity in the later Pliocene early Pleistocene Epoch erosional
dissection of the original dome had created a thick alluvial apron along the seaward
margins of the Waianae Range This alluvial plain is composed of interbedded sediments
of both terrigenous and marine origin

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration consisted of excavating three test pits designated as Test Pit
Nos 1 through 3 at the proposed relocated project site to depths ranging from about 5 to
7 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the test pits are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration in Test Pit Nos 1 and 3 encountered near surface
alluvial soils consisting of very stiff to hard sandy silts and clays to depths ranging from
about 1 5 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface This was underlain by a thin layer
of loose gravelly sands over very stiff silty clays extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 7 feet below the existing ground surface Medium dense clayey sands
overlying loose gravelly sands were generally encountered in Test Pit No 2 extending to
a depth of about 6 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the shallow test pits at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change
due to seasonal precipitation tidal fluctuations and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered during our exploration are
presented on the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 and A 2 of Appendix A Laboratory
testing was performed on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented in
Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered alluvial deposits of sandy and clayey
soils in the excavated test pits at the subject site Based on the existing topography and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 7
current design concept minor site filling is anticipated to attain the design finished grades
for the proposed construction Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions
encountered in our test pits and the anticipated light structural loads it is our opinion that
shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils and or engineered fill
materials may be used for support of the proposed banana packing plant Our laboratory
swell test results indicate that the near surface soils exhibit a moderately high swelling
and shrinkage potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore we
recommend that the building slab be supported on at least 12 inches of non expansive
select granular fill material for more uniform support Detailed discussion of these items
and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in the following

Shallow Foundations
Based on the current design concept and the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the relocated site we believe that shallow spread footings may
be used for support of the proposed banana packing plant structure An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the
design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils and or engineered
fill materials This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel and
concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 8
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or engineered fill materials Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Slab On Grade
We understand that concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed banana
packing plant Based on the information provided to us the concrete slab on grade will
be subjected to vehicular traffic such as forklifts Therefore a 6 inch layer of base course
below the concrete slab is recommended for more uniform support The base course
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction We recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural
engineer for the intended loads and be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate
some amount of differential movement For design of structural slabs a modulus of
subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted
base course A minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be
backfilled tightly against the slab with non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
foundations
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select capping fill material be
provided for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the base course cushion The capping
fill should also extend beyond the slab perimeter a minimum of 12 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 10
Prior to placement of the select capping fill the subgrades should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The select
capping fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying
potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the
subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential drying and wetting of the
subgrade soils should reduce the impact of shrink swell effects on the slab due to
potential heaving of the underlying expansive soils when wetted
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of base course
cushion and concrete To reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to
reduce the costs of form construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat
against the soil excavations Graphic illustration of the slab on grade is presented on the
Schematic Section Slab on Grade on Expansive Soil Plate 3
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and current design concept we anticipate that
site preparation work for the proposed construction will consist of minor fills over the
existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 11
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities that
are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition finished subgrades and areas designated
to receive fills should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of
the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
Imported fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished subgrades should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less
than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 12
Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Site grading operations should be observed by a _ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operation to evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during
the excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required to serve the new facility A granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential of formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material and placement could result in settlement of the backfill
and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3660 10 Page 13
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for water ponding Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations
