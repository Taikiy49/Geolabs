In accordance with your request we have performed a soils and
foundation investigation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of May 12 1986
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
design of the foundations for the proposed structures
W O 1705 00 Page 2
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling six
6 borings to about 15 feet below the existing ground surface Five
additional short borings were drilled to further define the shallow rock
conditions near Kamehameha Highway and to evaluate the existing asphaltic
conerete pavement conditions Also included were laboratory testing of
the samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
Project Consideration
The proposed commercial development will consist of two one story
structures including a 12 500 square foot commercial building and a 3 000
square foot restaurant
The existing parking lot will be reconstructed and extended to serve
both the proposed structures and the existing buildings
About 2 5 to 5 0 feet of fill are anticipated for the construction
of the proposed structures Up to about 2 0 feet of fill and minimal cut
are anticipated for the proposed parking lot
A new retaining wall is planned along the southern property line of
the site at a distance of about 5 feet behind the proposed commercial
building

Site Description
The proposed site is located on the makai side of Kamehameha Highway
in Pearl City on the island of Oahu Hawaii
There are two existing buildings on the approximately 76 000
square foot triangular parcel which include a one story warehouse and a
two story building Both buildings are now served by an existing paved
parking area and driveway
The proposed commercial building will be located adjacent to the
southern property line of the site The proposed restaurant will be
located at the eastern corner of the site fronting Kamehameha Highway and
adjacent to the proposed commercial building
The ground surface at the site slopes down gently from the
Kamehameha Highway towards the rear or southwest direction There is an
existing short retaining wall at the eastern corner of the site to provide
grade separation between the subject parcel and the lower adjacent
property
Ground cover varies from unpaved ground with coral and basalt gravel
surface concrete strips and pads to asphaltic concrete pavements The
asphaltic conerete pavements were generally deteriorated with extensive
cracking in some areas

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
eleven 11 borings to depths of 1 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
26
In general the borings encountered a surface layer of stiff to hard
silty clay residual soil underlain by weathered bedrock
The depth to rock varied sharply from about 2 to 5 feet near
Kamehameha Highway to 10 to 15 feet below the proposed commercial
building
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
Discussion and Recommendations
General
The borings encountered competent subsoils at the proposed building
areas Therefore spread or continuous footing foundations bearing
directly on the stiff on site soil or compacted structural fill may be
used to support the proposed structures
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and site grading we
recommend that the proposed building be supported by spread or continuous
footing foundations An allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be
used for spread or continuous footings bearing on stiff on site soil or
compacted structural fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1705 00 Page 5
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading Estimated
settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The rear wall of the proposed commercial building will be situated
close to the proposed retaining wall along the southeastern boundary of
the site In order to minimize settlement of the building footing due to
lateral wall movement and to minimize additional lateral load on the
retaining wall we recommend that the building wall footing be placed
below a 1 1 plane extending upward from the heel of the lower retainin
wall
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structures can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
stiff on site soil or compacted structural fill In addition an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to
determine the passive soil resistance
Site Grading
About 2 5 to 5 0 feet of fill are anticipated for raising the
existing ground at the proposed building areas to the proposed finished
grades Up to about 2 0 feet of fill and minimal cut are also anticipated
for the proposed parking lot
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1705 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable materials
such as debris rubbish vegetation old pavements and slabs should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
subgrade elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Any loose or wet spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to the above mentioned
standard
Imported structural fill should consist of non expansive material
less than 3 inches in maximm size with a laboratory CBR value higher than
12
The structural fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The existing short retaining wall within the proposed building site
should be removed and the resulting excavation backfilled with imported
material in maximum 10 inch loose lifts to a minimm of 90 of its ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from the buildings

Retaining Walls
To retain the raised ground a new retaining wall will be
constructed along the southern property line of the site at a distance of
about 5 feet behind the proposed commercial building The following
guidelines may be used for the retaining wall design
1
The following lateral earth pressure may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on stiff on site soil
The wall foundation on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for the wall footing on level areas
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed buildings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1705 00 Page 3
We recommend that the concrete slab be placed on a 4 inch layer of
3B Fine gravel cushion fill
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slab should be structurally separated from the wall foundation
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavement may be used for the new parking area
Based on our laboratory CBR tests we recommend the use of the
following pavement section for preliminary design purpose
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above pavement section assumes light traffic conditions and a
compacted subgrade shaped to drain
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
during construction to verify the adequacy of the above section
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation retaining
wall and pavement design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included
GEOLABS HAWAI

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Five one inch ring swell tests were performed on undistrubed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B l
Two Atterberg Limits were performed on selected samples of the soils
to evaluate liquid and plastic limits The test results are shown on
Plate B 2
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample to evaluate
the subgrade support characteristics of the soil for pavement design The
test result is summarized on Plate B 2
One direct shear test was performed on a select undistrubed sample
to evaluate the shear strength parameter of the soil The test result is
plotted on Plate B 3
