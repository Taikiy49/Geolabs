INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface condi
tions and to provide recommendations for the design and construc
tion of the proposed library and band room extensions at Niu Valley
Intermediate School Niu Valley Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Niu Valley Intermediate School 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
Niu Valley Intermediate School is located at the toe of the west
slope of Pia Valley in Niu Valley Oahu Hawaii The school site
is northwest of the intersection between Kanau and Halemaumau
Streets The two building areas covered by this investigation
are
1 the extension of the present library located
at the mauka portion of the school campus and
2 the extension of the existing band room at
the western end of the school campus
The existing library next to Halemaumau Street is on a relatively
flat area
The proposed library extension will occupy a portion of the exis
ting lawn area at both the north and the south sides of the library
building
The proposed band room extension will be constructed immediately
west of the existing band room at the west end of the school cam
pus The main portion of the proposed building addition is ona
level area The western portion of this new building area is pre
sently occupied by a slope bank This bank has a slope ratio of
about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 for the first 6 feet in
height and then it reduces to a slope ratio of 6 horizontal to
1 vertical 6 1 to about 120 feet west of the existing band room
Niu Valley Intermediate School 3
On the upper slope bank surface boulders up to more than 5 feet
in diameter were noted mainly about 60 feet away upward in the
south west direction from the existing band room
At the time of this investigation our geologic reconnaissance
did not indicate visible sign of slope movement or instability
at the existing slope bank
As indicated on the old construction drawing of the existing band
room building the area around the building and including the rear
slope area had previously been graded Shallow fill was also placed
under and around the building area
GEOLOGY
The geology of the Niu Valley area can be best described as the
end results of the period of erosion of the Koolau Volcano which
was followed by alluvium accumulation in the valleys as the island
slowly sank at least 1 200 feet Macdonald The Honolulu volcanic
series poured lava flows over the deeply eroded topography and had
some influence over the Niu Valley area also The sea advanced to
the Waimanalo high sea stand about 120 000 years ago and deposited
a transgressional sequence of sand and coralline debris A long
pause at roughly elevation 50 feet caused cementation of these
deposits Another extensive coral growth began at about elevation
25 feet which resulted in the coral exposed in the test borings
The project area in Niu Valley is at the hinge line between the
basalt and coral with alluvium deposits on top of both

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the building sites were explored
by drilling two borings for the library extension site and two
borings with two test pits for the band room extension site
In addition two hand dug test pits were made at the upper
slope bank of the band room area during the site slope recon
naissance The locations of the test borings and test pits are
shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description
of the field exploration methods and accompanying laboratory
testing is given in the appendix along with the boring logs
test pit logs and laboratory test results
Our findings and recommendations for the proposed library and
band room extensions are presented in the following two sections
of this report

Niu Valley Intermediate School 5
PROPOSED LIBRARY EXTENSION CONSTRUCTION
The proposed improvements consist of two additions One portion
is 24 by 76 feet in plan dimension makai and the other is 36 by
76 feet mauka of the existing library building
The proposed extensions will be a one story structure connected
to the existing library building Column and bearing walls are
utilized to support a metal deck and open web joist roof system
A 10 kip column load and a 1 5 kip per liner foot of wall load
are anticipated
Soil Condition
The test boring data indicates a uniform layer of very stiff
gravelly clay soils located immediately below the recent fill
and topsoil surface layer of about 12 inches thick The gravelly
clay soils graded into clayey gravel at about 4 feet at boring
no 3 and 8 feet at boring no 4 extending down to the full
depth of both borings Both borings were terminated at about
14 feet below ground surface
No groundwater was encountered in the borings during the subsur
face exploration in this area
Discussion And Recommendations
General The subsurface exploration indicates that generally
competent foundation bearing soils were encountered beneath the
proposed foundations
Niu Valley Intermediate School 6
The stiff clayey soils will provide adequate support without
excessive settlements The in situ clayey soils are of low
expansion potential Slab on grade may be designed without
special provision for expansive soil condition
Foundation It is recommended that spread and wall footings
be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 4 000 pounds
per square foot bearing upon the undisturbed in situ stiff clayey
soil The bearing pressure should be reduced to 2 000 pounds per
square foot if footings are to bear on compacted structural fill
provided that the fill material is of low to non expansive soil
For the above recommended bearing pressure it is estimated that
settlements will not exceed inch total and inch differential
New footings next to the existing building foundation should be
lowered to match the existing adjacent footing elevation When
the proposed footing is located higher than the existing footing
it should be kept a set back distance out of the 45 degree stress
zone under the footing foundation in order to avoid interference
between the old and the new footings
Continuous footing should be designed to step down to avoid utility
lines under the foundation or it should be designed to bridge over
the utility lines
for expansive soil conditions Slab subgrades close to the existing
Niu Valley Intermediate School 7
grade should be free of topsoil The subgrade should be kept
moist and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum density
established by ASTM D1557 A 4 inch layer of base course should
be provided above the subgrade and compacted to 95 of its maxi
mum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be placed below the concrete
slab
Earthwork And Compaction Requirements Footing excavation back
fills should be compacted in 8 inch loose lifts compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum density within the slab areas
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the surface
vegetation and any previously placed topsoil fills about 12 inches
thick Prior to the placement of any new select backfill and fill
the ground surface should be brought back to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D1557 Additional fill should be
non expansive select fill placed in horizontal loose lifts of
8 inches and compacted to the above specified density The on site
clay soil should not be reused under the slab area since it is highly
expansive when remolded
PROPOSED BAND ROOM EXTENSION CONSTRUCTION
Niu Valley Intermediate School 8
PROPOSED BAND ROOM EXTENSION CONSTRUCTION
The proposed band room extension consists of a 44 by 36 feet
one story building The proposed building will be constructed
immediately west of the existing band room building
Concrete columns and C M U wall will be used to support a metal
deck and an open web joist roof system The anticipated column
load is 15 kips per column and the wall load is 1 kip per liner
foot
The grading operation for this area involves cutting back the
existing slope bank which presently occupies the western end
of the proposed extension area to a 15 feet clear distance
The maximum cut for the proposed graded slope will be Jess than
10 feet below the existing slope surface At the eastern portion
of the proposed band room shallow fill would be required to raise
the ground to match the existing band room floor elevation
Soil Condition
In general the eastern portion of the proposed building is located
at the level lawn area which is blanketed by a layer of about 12
inches of dark brown Adobe clay topsoil This surface soil has
a lot of cracks when it is in a dry condition The topsoil layer
grades into a more boulderly and gravelly reddish brown clay soil
In Boring No 2 an orange tan coral sand layer was encountered
4 feet below the ground surface This coral layer was also found
Ntu Valley Intermediate School 9
in Test Pit No 2 about 7 feet below the existing ground A
hard ledge coral layer of about 4 5 feet thick was encountered
at Boring No 2 below the coral sand layer
The slope bank jn the western part of the proposed band room
area has a 12 inch thick of dark brown surface clay layer which
grades to a reddish brown gravelly clay soil down to a depth of
5 to 9 feet below the surface
Boring No 1 and Test Pit No 1 disclosed an orange tan coral
sand deposit at depths of 10 5 to 15 feet below the surface
Below this is the hard coral ledge layer which extends to the
end of the test hole
The upper mauka slope area was explored by two additional hand
dug shallow test pits At Test Pit No 3 location there is a
6 inch dark brown surface clay layer underlain by a 1 5 feet thick
of boulderly clay Below this is the stiff reddish brown clay layer
At Test Pit No 4 area many surface boulders were noted The pit
was terminated in the boulderly clay layer 2 5 feet below the
ground surface
Discussions And Recommendations
General The surface soils under the proposed band room building
may vary a great deal due to the area had previously been filled
with various types of soil
The laboratory test results indicate the surface soil encountered
at this site is highly expansive Therefore it is recommended
that the surface layer be undercut a minimum of 12 inches before
Niu Valley Intermediate School 10
the fill operation proceeds at the proposed band room building
area
Foundation Spread and line footing foundation bearing on the
in situ clayey soils may be designed for an allowable bearing
capacity of 3 000 pounds per square foot For the footing bearing
On compacted fill layer the bearing capacity should be reduced
to 2 000 pounds per square foot
The estimated total settlement for the proposed structure should
be less than inch with a differential settlement of less than
ke inch
The new footings next to the existing band room should be lowered
to match the existing footing level which is about elevation 20
feet If the proposed footing bottom is located at an elevation
higher than the adjacent footing level the new footing should be
located at a set back distance outside the 45 degree foundation
stress zone This is to avoid stress interference between the
old and the new footings
Slab On Grade The surface soil layer at the new building exten
sion area is highly expansive and should be undercut a minimum
of 12 inches and replaced with non expansive select borrow under
the slab on grade
Slab subgrade close to the existing grade or at the cut area should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum density established
by ASTM D1557 and should be kept moist prior to base course and slab
placement
Niu Valley Intermediate School 11
Slab on grade should be provided with 4 inches of granular base
course layer and a vapor barrier
Cut Slope Based on the results of the laboratory testing and
stability analysts the two horizontal to one vertical 2 1
cut slope as proposed in the grading plan is adequate The
slope stability analysis indicates a factor of safety against
sliding failure greater than 1 5 However during the grading
operation the cut slope should be inspected by the soils engineer
to permit correlation between the soil data obtained during the
investigation and the actual soil conditions encountered during
construction Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encoun
tered at the talus slope area and it cannot be fully determined
by taking soil samples and digging test pits These unforeseen
soil conditions may require flattening of the recommended slope
ratio
To minimize erosion the slope planting should be placed as soon
as practicable
Earthwork And Compaction Requirements Footing excavation back
fills within the slab area should be compacted in layers to 90
maximum density
Prior to receiving fill the existing lawn area under the slab on
grade should be undercut a minimum of 12 inches in order to remove
the surface vegetation and the highly expansive topsoil Prior
to the placement of any new fill the ground surface should be
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
Niu Valley Intermediate School 12
of 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM D1557
Additional fill should then be placed in 8 inch horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to the above specified density
During the grading operation if any soft spot is encountered
within the proposed building area the loose materials should
be removed down to stiff undisturbed soil and backfilled to
the above specified compacted layers
The on site clay soils when it is remolded as fill material
have high expansion potential Therefore the cut soil from
the project should not be used as a source of fill material
under the slab on grade
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavation and the cut
slope construction be inspected during the construction to
assure that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
The earthwork operations for the fill under the slab area should
be tested to assure that the proper degree of compaction is con
sistently achieved
To minimize differential settlement concrete slab construction
should be delayed as long as possible
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by drilling 4 borings
utilizing truck mounted drilling equipment The depth of the borings ter
minated at 13 8 to 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface Two test
pits were excavated by utilizing a backhoe at the proposed band room building
area In addition two more shallow hand excavated test pits were made at
the upper slope area during site reconnaissance The locations of the borings
and test pits are shown on the enclose Site Plan Plate 1
During the subsurface exploration one bulk bag sample of near surface soil
was taken at Test Pit No 1 location for use in the evaluation of their
characteristics as a soil and more importantly as a source of fill
The soil were classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings samples were obtained by driving either a standard split
spoon or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depth
A description of soils encountered in the test pits are presented on the Test
Pit Logs Plate A 5

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING

as an aid in the soil classification and in evaluating soil
properties Atterberg Limits and unit weight determinations
were performed on selected samples as additional aid in classi

on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample location

on undisturbed and remolded surface samples for measuring the
swell potential The swell test results are summarized on
Plate B l

sample of the on site soil to evaluate the suitability of the
soil as fill and pavement support The results are given on
Plate B 2
Direct Shear tests were performed on selected clay samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the encountered soils
The test results are shown on PlatesB 3 through B 5
