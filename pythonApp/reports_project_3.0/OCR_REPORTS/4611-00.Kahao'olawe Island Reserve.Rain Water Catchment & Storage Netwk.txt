REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kaho olawe is located about 7 miles toward the southwest of the Island
of Maui and about 17 miles toward the southeast of the Island of Lana i The Island of
Kaho olawe is approximately 45 square miles in land area with a maximum elevation of about
1 480 feet above sea level located on the Lua Makika summit at the eastern portion of the
island
The Island of Kaho olawe is believed to be composed of a single shield volcano formed
by the extrusion of successive layered thinly bedded pahoehoe and a a basalt lava flows that
emanated from the rift zones and caldera at Lua Makika The primary eruptive rift zones of
the former volcano extend westerly from the summit and to a lesser degree to the north and
east from the summit
The area at Lua Makika is believed to represent the eroded crater of a lava shield that
covers an area of about 10 square miles and constitutes the highest portion of the island
Some of the basalt rock composing the upper caldera filling layers of the western portion of
the island have been age dated at about 1 million years old Naughton et al 1980 The
scouring effect of the wind is the most prominent erosional process observed on the Island of
Kaho olawe especially at the summit of Lua Makika where surface top soils have been
stripped to expose the underlying hardpan soil horizon
SITE DESCRIPTION
The rain water catchment project site is located in the vicinity of Lua Makika which is
situated at the east central portion of the Island of Kaho olawe as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate comprises the crest of the central upland backbone of the
island of Kaho olawe The project site generally consists of gently to moderately sloping
terrain underlain by tan and reddish brown silts and clays with fine sand and some gravel
representing both residual and saprolite soil products derived from the complete weathering
of basalt rock
Based on the available topographic plan the existing terrain at the project site consists
of rolling hills separated by a broad gently sloping basin The existing ground surface
elevation ranges from about 1 440 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the western end of the site
to about 1 400 feet MSL near the central portion of the site and to about 1 470 feet MSL
at the eastern end of the site

NEAR SURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The soils observed at the project site appear to be relatively uniform in appearance and
may be classified as low plasticity silts and clays with fine sand and some gravel representing
the in situ residual and saprolitic soil derived from the complete weathering of basalt rock
Based on our observation of the site the tan and reddish brown colored soils appear to be
very stiff though friable due in part to the arid environment The soils appear to have a
generally low shrink swell potential
Most of the gently sloping terrain has developed a hardpan crust of soil which is
relatively resistant to the initiation of erosion Abrasion and scouring by wind appears to be
responsible for the formation of the surficial hardpan crust However once the surficial crust
is exposed or broken through resistance to erosion from stormwater runoff is generally
lowered forming gullies
Significant topsoil development and occurrence of loosely deposited alluvial and or
windblown materials were not observed likely due to exposure to the stripping action of wind
When excavated the on site soils were observed to have a blocky and friable texture that
promotes the formation of soil clumps The soil clumps may be broken down to fine particles
of clay silt sand and some friable gravel
Two near surface soil samples identified as S 1 and S 2 were collected from selected
locations at the project site The approximate location of Soil Samples S 1 and S 2 with
respect to the project site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The soil samples were tested
in our laboratory to characterize the soil materials The laboratory test results are presented
on Plates 3 and 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance and the laboratory test results the near surface
conditions at the project site generally consist stiff to very stiff low plasticity clayey silt with
some fine sand and gravel Based on the near surface ground conditions encountered at the
project site we believe that shallow foundations consisting of a concrete pad with
thickened edge footings or a perimeter ring footing bearing on the stiff natural ground may be
used for support of the proposed tank structure
In addition to the tank structures we also envision that concrete slabs on grade will be
used for support of the other proposed structures such as the well pump pad and booster
pump pad To provide for uniform support of the concrete pads we recommend that the

Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of a concrete pad with thickened edge footings or a perimeter
ring footing bearing on stiff natural ground may be used for support of the proposed tank
structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of the thickened edge footings or perimeter ring footings This
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
if soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing elevation
the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm material The
over excavation should be backfilled with on site soils compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements would be on the order of about 1 inch
We anticipate the differential settlements across the concrete pads to be on the order of
about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used for foundations bearing on the compacted select borrow
subbase Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed tank floor
and various equipment pads To provide uniform support of the concrete slab we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the slab concrete be properly prepared and
capped with at least 6 inches of select borrow subbase The select borrow subbase should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural
slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used for the compacted select borrow subbase
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Thickened edges that are used
as footings to support structural loads should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade as recommended in the Foundations section of this report
Areas adjacent to the slab edges should be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively
impervious soils on site soils and graded to divert water away from the siab to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the foundations

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted general fill The excavated soft soils should be
properly disposed of off site and or stockpiled in a designated area where
appropriate
Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to within 3 percent of optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or that the soil
subgrade be prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with weli compacted
general fill or controlled fill
Fills and Backfills
in general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
controlled fill materials provided that they are free of vegetation and deleterious
materials and the maximum particle size should be less than 6 inches in maximum
dimension It should be noted that the on site soils appear to be relatively dry
between 10 and 20 percent below the optimum moisture content Therefore an
ample water supply will be necessary during the grading operations to provide
adequate water to achieve the specified moisture and compaction requirements

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials and or controlled fill should be moisture conditioned to within
3 percent of the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where required aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should
be moisture conditioned placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction based
on ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to and beyond the structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the pad and finished surfaces to reduce the potential for ponding
Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after construction
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the storage tank
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the structures
Utility Trench
We understand that new water transmission lines and utility connections may be
required for the proposed project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes for
support Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of
the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
