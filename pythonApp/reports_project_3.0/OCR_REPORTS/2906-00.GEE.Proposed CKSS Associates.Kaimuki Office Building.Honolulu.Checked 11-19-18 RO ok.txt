INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed CKSS Associates Kaimuki Office Building in the district of
Kaimuki on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
The recommendations presented herein are intended for foundation design input and are

purposes
This report supplements our design memorandum dated which
presented our preliminary recommendations regarding the design concepts for the
project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an office building at the project site Based on the
information provided to us the development will consist of an office building of four
stories and a roof story above four split levels of subsurface basement parking It is
anticipated that the building will be of reinforced concrete construction Up to about 25
feet of cut will be required to construct the basement parking structure pads

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Northeast quadrant of the block bounded by 9th
10th Waialae and Harding Avenues in the district of Kaimuki on the island of Oahu The
site is bounded by Waialae Avenue on the north and 10th Avenue on the east
The site is currently occupied by Central Pacific Bank to the west and King s
Garden Chinese Restaurant at its northeastern corner An existing asphaltic concrete
parking lot lies in between these two structures
Based on the available topographic survey it appears that the existing ground
surface slopes gently from approximately 144 feet elevation above Mean Sea Level MSL
at the southeastern corner down to approximately 134 feet above MSL at the
northwestern corner of the site a slope of approximately four percent
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling three
3 borings to depths of about 36 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a four to seven foot thick surface layer of
reddish brown silty clay overlying a basaltic rock formation to the maximum depths drilled
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2906 00 Page 5
The basaltic rock formation encountered at the project site is generally in the
pahoehoe form as characterized by the ropy hummocky surface of the rock Pahoehoe
lava is prone to having subsurface voids or cavities which were created when the lava
flow was deposited A void was encountered at about 19 to 22 feet below the existing
ground surface in Boring 2
Groundwater
With the exception of Boring No 3 groundwater was not encountered at the time
of our field exploration At Boring No 3 groundwater appeared to exist between 24 and
30 feet below the existing ground surface or at approximately 105 5 to 111 0 feet above
MSL However we also noted that the water level was dropping throughout our field
exploration The final measurement made three 3 days after the demobilization of our
drill rig indicated that no water level was measured down to the maximum depth drilled
The higher groundwater elevations measured were much higher than anticipated
for the geologic and hydrologic conditions at the site and in its vicinity We believed that
the water levels measured are probably transient perched water levels or the result of
drilling water from our drilling operations and therefore it may not reflect the static
groundwater conditions on a permanent basis
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface conditions encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs in Appendix A

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
Based on preliminary drawings available for our review dated May 11 1992 it
appears that construction of the basement parking levels will entail excavation as deep
as 25 feet below the existing ground
It is anticipated that construction of the footings and slabs and the recommended
cushion layer below the slabs will entail excavation of approximately 21 feet into the
basaltic rock formation The underlying basalt rock formation is anticipated to be very
dense and hard
Spread and continuous footing foundations on rock are recommended to support
the proposed structure
Footing Foundations
All spread and wall footings should be designed to bear on the in situ rock
formation All footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent exterior grade
An allowable bearing capacity of 15 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footing foundations bearing on the in situ rock formation The bearing value indicated is
for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for loads
of short duration such as those caused by wind and seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be free of all rock debris and loose
material All such loose material should be scaled off and removed The cushion layer
below the concrete should be placed against firm and sound rock surfaces
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2906 00 Page 7
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be provided by
passive pressure and frictional resistance We recommended that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 400 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive soil pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundations and a frictional
coefficient of 0 45 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the
footing bottoms and the rock formation
Retaining Walls
We recommend that the following design guidelines be used for the proposed
retaining walls
1 Lateral Earth Pressure assuming granular backfill material is used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill

at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill
Live vehicular loads and or other surcharge loads should be added to the
above recommended pressures for the wall design where appropriate
2 An allowable bearing capacity of 15 000 p s f may be used for the design
of the retaining wall footings bearing on in situ rock formation
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2906 00 Page 8
3 A passive earth resistance of 400 p s f equivalent fluid pressure may be
used for design of retaining walls with level ground in front of the wall The
passive earth pressure should be reduced for walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 45 may be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the walls
5 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls Subdrains should be constructed properly to drain water
away from behind the walls
6 For the backfill of exterior retaining walls not adjacent to paved areas the
granular backfill should extend to a height of two 2 feet below finish grade
The remaining backfill should consist of select impervious soil For the
backfill of interior walls or walls adjacent to paved areas the granular backfill
should extend to the bottom of the slab or the pavement subgrade
Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed building Concrete slabs subject
to vehicular traffic should be considered as rigid pavements which are discussed in a
subsequent section
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a four inch layer of
aggregate cushion fill such as No 3 Fine or ASTM C 233 No 67 and separated from
wall foundations by expansion joints The cushion layer should be compacted to a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2906 00 Page 9
minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Settlement
Footing settlements under the design loadings are anticipated to be minimum on
the order of less than one half inch and one quarter inch differential
Probing and Grouting
In order to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to collapse of loose
pockets or cavities such as those encountered in Boring No 2 in the underlying rock
we recommend all of the footing excavations should be probed at approximately ten 10
feet on centers for continuous footings and with a minimum of one probe drilled at each
column location for spread footing location If a cavity is located below a footing
additional probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the cavity
The probes should have a minimum diameter of three 3 inches and extend below the
bottom of the footing to a depth of at least ten 10 feet The probe holes should be
grouted from the bottom of the hole using low pressures The probing and grouting
operations should be monitored by the project geotechnical engineer to see that these
recommendations are carried out properly and to locate potential voids near the footing
excavations as well as to recommend modifications to the grouting procedures which
may be needed to minimize grout loss in cavities and to expedite the above construction
program
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish concrete rubble and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2906 00 Page 10
The surface layer of silty clay in the area to be occupied by the proposed
construction should also be completely removed and disposed of properly off site
Excavated rock material if reduced to less than three 3 inches in size will be
suitable as fill material Filler shall be added to the excavated material if required to
obtain a well graded mixture If imported fill and backfill materials are required the
material should be generally a granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 15 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Fill and backfill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2906 00 Page 11
Excavation and Shoring Support
It is our opinion that in general hard excavations may be required in the basaltic
rock formation from depths below about four 4 feet from the existing ground surface to
the bottom of excavation Excavation by means of ripping or hoe ram equipment might
be slow and costly to accomplish It is our recommendation that a qualified blasting
specialist should be consulted to look into the possibility of using alternate blasting
method However extreme care must be exercised to avoid over ripping which would
disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting in a potential loss of bearing strength
The contractor should be given as much leeway as possible in the choice of
equipment and methods to be used for excavation The adequacy and safety of the
excavation support that may be required should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety consideration including all applicable
regulations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of three 3 borings to depths of about 36 feet below the existing ground surface The
borings were drilled by a truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers
or rotary core drilling tools as appropriate The logs of the borings are shown on Plate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
The soil materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
Identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist or an engineer from our
firm
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination description and
evaluation
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks e g if 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown
on the Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
