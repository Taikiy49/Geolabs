PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints on
Kilauea Avenue in Hilo on the Island of Hawaii Based on the information provided we
understand that it is proposed to expand the existing parking lot within the church
premises Percolation tests were required for this expansion to design stormwater
disposal retention structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain information
pertaining to the permeability of the subsurface materials to formulate a summary of the
soil rock conditions for the proposed additional parking lot Our scope of work included the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators from Honolulu to the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of two borings to depths of about 11 and 16 feet below
the existing ground surface
3 Performance of two percolation tests to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface materials

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints
premises on Kilauea Avenue in Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The project site is located
south of the existing church building and parking lot as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The project site is currently a lawn with several large trees The site gently slopes
down from west to east Based on the topographic survey map provided the elevations of
the site range from 27 5 to 33 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 11 and 16 feet below the
existing ground surface We drilled the borings using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a layer of volcanic ash of about 0 5 to
1 5 foot thick overlying basalt formation extending to the maximum depths drilled of
approximately 11 and 16 feet below the existing ground surface We encountered clinker
materials consisting of loose to dense gravel and sand embedded within the basalt rock
formation
We did not encounter groundwater in our borings at the time of our exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors
such as seasonal rainfall storm surge conditions and other factors Detailed descriptions
of the materials encountered in our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5

LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained
from the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials and to evaluate the engineering
properties
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussions of the findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
are presented in the following sections of this report
Percolation Testing
Field percolation tests were conducted in Boring Nos 1 and 2 in the proposed
parking lot site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 to evaluate the infiltration
characteristics of the subsurface materials
We performed percolation tests in Boring Nos 1 and 2 at depths of about 11 and
16 feet respectively We performed constant head percolation tests to determine the
average hydraulic conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field
exploration the subsurface materials encountered consist of moderately fractured basalt
rock formation and clinker materials The percolation test results are presented on Plate 6
Based on the constant head field permeability test results the calculated hydraulic
conductivity k value at each test location is summarized in the following table In general
the calculated hydraulic conductivity k value for each of the tests indicated that the basalt
rock formation and the clinker materials are moderately to highly permeable materials

LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and recommendations provided in this letter report
The field boring locations indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Topographic Survey Map provided by
Edmond Akiona Architect on The boring elevations were estimated from
the contour lines and spot elevations shown on the same plan The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or indicated in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of The Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter Day Saints on Kilauea Avenue in Hilo on the Island of Hawaii and their project
consultants for specific application to the proposed parking lot expansion project in
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in
the design of the proposed project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data
or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation of construction cost
estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent
geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the performance
of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen subsurface conditions such as _ perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
