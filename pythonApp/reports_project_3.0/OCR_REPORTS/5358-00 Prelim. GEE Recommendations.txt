This letter provides our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations for
the above project Our preliminary recommendations are based on subsurface information
from recently completed borings drilled at the project area In order to expedite the
progress and design of the project we are submitting herein our preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
of eight borings to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface Based on
the borings the project site is generally underlain by a surface fill layer with thickness
ranging from about 0 to 2 feet The fill layer generally consisted of stiff to very stiff silty
clay with some gravel The fill layer was underlain by an residual soil deposit to a depth of
20 feet the maximum depth explored The residual soil deposit generally consisted of
medium stiff to hard silty clay Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at
the time of our field exploration
Shallow House Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions we believe that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of
the proposed housing structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on properly
compacted fill or stiff on site silty clay The bearing pressure provided above is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces In general the bottom of the footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on properly compacted fill or
on site stiff silty clay Resistance to lateral loads due to passive earth pressure may be
calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth pcf This value assumes that the concrete for footings is cast neat against the
in situ soils or that the soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade
should be neglected
Slabs On Grade
Based on the field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface clayey
soils with moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting
from shrinkage and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the housing units be properly prepared and
capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material capping fill
below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade
footprint a minimum of 24 inches
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be incorporated below the
slab to serve as a capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slab To
reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel cushion fill
layer
For slabs on grade that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as residential
driveways the 4 inch gravel cushion layer and the moisture barrier may be omitted For
the design of the structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per
square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted non expansive
granular fill
A minimum slab thickness of 4 5 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes provided that rebar reinforcement is provided For house and garage slabs we
recommend that rebar reinforcement be utilized For driveway slabs we recommend that
as a minimum heavy duty welded wire fabric be utilized Provisions should be made for
proper load transfer across the slab joints that are subjected to vehicular traffic The
thickened edges of the slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be
backfilled with on site silty clay soil tightly against the slab and graded to divert water away
from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around the foundation

Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the driveways and parking areas
Based on the CBR value of the on site natural clayey subgrade material the following
pavement section may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes until the finish
subgrade material is verified and laboratory testing of this material is conducted
Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Paved areas should be sloped at 2 to 5 percent and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction
Site Grading
We anticipate that the mass grading work will consist of cuts and fills of up to 2 feet
in the majority of the site Our laboratory test results indicated a moderate to high swell
potential of the on site silty clay material Therefore the on site silty clay material
generally will not be suitable for fill material within 1 feet of finish grade below structures
unless special foundation design is utilized The non expansive select granular fill material
capping fill should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in largest
dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should also be free of
vegetation and deleterious materials
In general we believe that cut slopes into the on site silty clay material may be cut
at an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fill slopes should be
designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
