INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed sand processing
plant in Campbell Industrial Park at Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the locations of major structures of the proposed sand
processing plant site in order to provide recommendations for use in
the design of the site grading and foundations of the proposed
facility

The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of five

selected samples obtained during the field exploration engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation

testing are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Campbell Industrial Park at
Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located to the north of Malakole Road and adjacent to
the Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor The site is nearly level and is
currently vacant The existing ground surface elevation at the boring
locations varied from approximately 22 to 26 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a sand processing plant on the site
The plant will be used to manufacture aggregate sand from crushed coral
limestone The plant will consist of a series of crushing screening
and processing equipment mounted on concrete pads Anticipated colum
loads range from 10 to 44 kips It is anticipated that the equipment
will generate moderate vibratory loads

It appears that the sand processing plant site had been previously

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located west of Honolulu on the edge of the
Ewa coastal plain which consists of a thick horizontal series of
interbedded marine and non marine sediments capping Waianae Basalts
The near surface structure at the site consists of elevated Pleistocene
coral reef deposits of highly variable consistency and composition
At the project site these reef deposits are overlain by man made
fill which is believed to be related to the construction and dredging
of the nearby Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging between 21 0
and 26 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered surface layers of fill
composed of tan to white silty coral sands with gravel varying between
approximately 6 5 and approximately 17 5 feet in thickness This fill
appears to exhibit varying degrees of compaction and the soils range in
consistency from loose to medium dense Below the fill the borings
encountered medium hard coral algal limestone formation and or dense

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed However the variable nature of
the upper fill encountered on the site makes its capability to provide
direct spread footing foundation support questionable We recommend
that spread or strip footings bear on recompacted f1i11 in accordance
with the recommendations given below
Foundation Support
The originally proposed design for the plant structure foundations
called for a thin mat foundation supporting each structure In our
opinion it is more economical to utilize spread or strip footings to
support the proposed sand processing plant structures If additional
rigidity and or vibration dampening is needed then the footings for
each structure should be connected by grade beams or a similar design
In order to provide more uniform support for the proposed
foundations we recommend that the footing areas be over excavated see
Plate 3 to a depth of three 3 feet below footing subgrade or to the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1925 00 Page 5
Dk No 42
in situ coral reef surface Prior to backfilling the bottom of the
resulting excavation should first be flooded by water tamping allowed
to drain and then the subgrade be recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 If any soft areas weak
areas or boulders are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation they should be remedied by over excavation to competent
materials and backfilling of the resultant void with select on site
sandy coral fill material compacted to 95 maximum dry density The
backfilling of the originally over excavated footing subgrades should
then proceed with the placement of select on site sandy coral material
less than 3 inches in maximm size in about 9 inch loose level lifts
and compacted to 95 maximm dry density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78
For the design of spread or strip footings an allowable bearing
capacity of 3000 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on
subgrade prepared in accordance with the above recommendation This
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic
loads are considered The footings should be embedded a minimm of
three feet 3 below the lowest adjacent outside grade
The bottom of all footing over excavations should be inspected by
the geotechnical engineer prior to the placement of the compacted
backfill and reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has been
obtained and that loose soils or boulders are removed and properly
backfilled prior to placement of the compacted backfill
Estimated settlements for foundations constructed in accordance
with the above recommendations are believed to be less than one inch
total and one half of an inch differential We recommend provisions be
made under the colums of process plant equipment to accommodate these
and additional footing settlements which might occur in the future

Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading are not currently
available However it is assumed that some minor amounts of grading
work will be required
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site sandy soils should be suitable for use as
engineered fill If additional imported material is required the
material should conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of
the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Select
Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding nine inches 9 in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78 Test Method
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed scale house and
other buildings will be concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their
cushions may be supported by the upper fill provided the subgrade is
first scarified to a depth of ten inches 10 moisture conditioned to
optimum moisture content and recompacted to 95 relative dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine
gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill

In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be
considered below the slabs provided an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or reconmendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
