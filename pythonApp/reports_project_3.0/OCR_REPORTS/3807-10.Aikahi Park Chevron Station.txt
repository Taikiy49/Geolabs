INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Aikahi Park Chevron Station renovation project located in the
Kailua District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading retaining
walls and pavement sections only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located in the Aikahi Park Shopping Center in the
District of Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that the project will
involve converting the existing service bay into a FoodMart only and a new structure is
not planned at this time However construction of a side wall and several footings may
be required to convert the existing service bay into a FoodMart In addition we anticipate
that the hydraulic lifts currently used in the service station may have to be removed The
removal of the jacks may require backfill of up to about 5 feet We understand that a
detached canopy parking areas and minor site grading work are also planned
Because the area is currently operating a gas station and automotive service
station environmental measures should be taken into consideration This report covers
recommendations pertaining to geotechnical engineering aspects only and
environmental study should be done by others

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and forms
the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
growth and retreat of the great continental glaciers As the glaciers advanced less water
was available to fill the ocean basins and sea levels fell below the current sea level As
the glaciers melted and retreated more water became available and sea level rose to and
often above the current level The glacio eustatic changes during this period resulted in
the accumulation of several hundred feet of marine and non marine sediments
The project site is underlain by interbedded lagoonal and beach deposits resulting
from the glacio eustatic sea level changes Land development in this century and
placement of man made fills have brought the project site to its present form

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings to depths of about 10 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Generally our field exploration encountered fills to about 4 5 to 6 5 feet below the
existing ground surface consisting of gravels in a stiff silty clay matrix Below the fills
lagoonal deposits consisting of medium stiff to stiff silty clay with organic materials were
encountered to about 10 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal
deposits were underlain by a layer of beach deposit consisting of medium dense coral
sands to about 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface The beach deposits were
underlain by another lagoonal deposit consisting of soft clayey silts with organic materials
to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in both Boring Nos 1 and 2 at 7 8 and 8 6 feet
below existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration respectively It should
be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall time of year tidal
fluctuations and other factors

Foundations
The proposed project will involve converting the existing service bay into a
FoodMart We understand that a new structure is not planned and the existing structure
will be used for the FoodMart if possible In the event that new foundations are required
we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous
footings may be used to support the new structure Our recommendation is based on the
subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footings bearing on the surficial fills This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind
and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that total footing settlements under the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3807 10 Page 7
anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements
of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the fills These values assume that the concrete for footings is
placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings are
to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should extend to
a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the
below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the conversion of the
service bay into a FoodMart The near surface fills encountered at the proposed site
consist of gravels in a silty clay matrix which exhibit moderate expansion potential when
subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore to reduce the potential of structural distress
to lightly loaded slabs we recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive
select granular fill material be provided below the concrete slab The non expansive fill
should extend a minimum of 3 feet laterally beyond the edge of the slab The subgrades
for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select fill recommended below the floor slabs should consist of
imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve All select granular fill materials should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrade should be kept moist until covered by
the cushion fill or base course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3807 10 Page 9
To provide more uniform support we recommend that the concrete slab on grade
be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill The cushion fill layer should consist
of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill should be placed
as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils
as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we
recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane
placement of a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the membrane prior to placing the
slab concrete may be considered If the sand layer is desired the sand should be
moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per
cubic inch p c i may be used for the select granular fill The thickened edges of slabs
adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade

Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minor grading with cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 foot
and backfilling of up to about 5 feet relative to the existing ground surface may be
expected for the proposed project
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the parking and driveway areas the
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or the
on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface silty and
clayey soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the
footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of about 10 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Field Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One one inch ring swell test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the test are summarized on
Plate B 1
One unconfined compression test ASTM D 2166 was performed on a selected
sample to evaluate the compression strength characteristics The results of the test are
summarized on Plate B 2
