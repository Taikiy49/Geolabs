INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 0 1 Million Gallon M G Reservoirs and Waterline System
within the Kawaihae Residence Lots project in the area of Kawaihae in the District of
North Kohala on the island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hawi and Pulolu
Volcanic Series of Kohala which were deposited during the middle to late Pleistocene
Epoch Stearns and Macdonald 1946
These series are typically composed of basaltic and andesitic lava flows which
are mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ashy soil similar to the ash soils
locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ashy soil at the project site differs from Pahala
Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the in situ moisture content
is generally lower and the soil exists in a dry friable state The soil encountered at the
project site may also be a product of in situ weathering of the lavas rather than an air
laid ash
The soils at the site have been subjected to wind erosion which has resulted in
the formation of a desert pavement on the surface of the site This desert pavement
is a surface layer of cobbles and gravel which were left behind as the finer ashy soil
was blown away
At and in the area of the project site the majority of the lava flows are in the a a
form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of fragmental clinker
underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which frequently has a thinner less
continuous iayer of clinker below the massive core Some of the lavas observed at the
site appeared to be pahoehoe or transitional between a a and pahoehoe These flows
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3310 00 Page 7
do not have the clinkery surface and generally form ledges of fractured basalt The
surface of the lavas at the site have been mantled by the ashy soil as discussed above
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The Kawaihae Residence Lots project site is located approximately 1 miles
north northwest of Kawaihae Harbor in the District of North Kohala on the island of
Hawaii and encompasses approximately 150 acres
The terrain in the region of the site is generally rolling with some steep slopes
locally approaching an inclination of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V The terrain
typically reflects the original surface of basaltic lava flows in both the a a and pahoehoe
forms
The site is situated on a ridge between two gulches and the ground along the
axis of the ridge generally slopes in a westerly direction at a ratio of about five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1 V or flatter The ground surface elevations range from
about 100 to approximately 650 feet MSL with drainage trending towards the west
The site is vegetated with a thin cover of dry grasses with kiawe algaroba trees
Most of the ground surface is covered with basaltic cobbles and gravel mantling an
ashy soil There are localized exposures of basalt clinker and rock formation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
consist generally of a surficial layer of ashy soils over weathered clinkers and hard
basalt formation at shallow depths Based on our evaluation of the subsurface
conditions with respect to the proposed development the principal geotechnical
engineering considerations for the proposed water reservoir development include the
following
1 foundation support for the proposed reservoirs
2 probing and grouting for potential cavities and clinker zones beneath the
foundations and
3 site grading and pavement design for the access driveways
The proposed water reservoirs generally are expected to be located entirely in
cut areas In general reservoir foundations are sensitive to differential movements
Because we anticipate that foundations for the reservoirs will straddle clinker zones and
dense basalt formation we recommend that a shallow foundation bearing on at least
12 inches of compacted base course be used to support the reservoir The base
course layer would provide more uniform support to the footings
Due to the potential presence of cavities as commonly encountered in basaltic
lava flows we recommend that a program of probing and grouting at the footing
locations be implemented during construction A representative of Geolabs Hawaii
should observe the probing and grouting operation to evaluate the extent of cavities
Special attention should be given to the Foundation Probing section of this report

Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed water
reservoir structures Since differing subgrade materials are anticipated at the design
slab subgrade elevations a 12 inch layer of base course compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction is recommended below the concrete slabs on grade to
provide more uniform bearing support For design of structural slabs a modulus of
subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be
used for the compacted base course
Ashy soils encountered at the bottom of the base course layer should be over
excavated to expose the underlying clinkers or basalt formation with the over
excavation backfilled with compacted base course material as described above Prior
to the placement of the base course layer the excavated subgrade should be proof
rolled at least 4 passes with heavy equipment such as a D 9 bulldozer to assist in
detecting and possibly collapsing near surface cavities
Foundation Probing
Based on our previous experience in the area voids and or cavities may be
present in the basaltic lava flows encountered at the project site To reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3310 00 Page 13
footing we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting be conducted
during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probes be drilled at each spread
footing location and 10 foot on centers for continuous footings The probe holes should
be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned
bottom of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the
probing operation additional probes should be drilled at closer spacings to help
delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and
any cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to
moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of 1 part sand 1 part
cement and 0 7 part water
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site
we recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a
longer lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction may
likely be required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the
presence of cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess
grout take and or changed conditions are observed

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Ash soils generally have relatively low strength and if the in situ moisture
contents are high enough they exhibit thixotropic properties i e they lose
significant strength when remolded Therefore itis our recommendation that the
ash soils be removed below structural areas
After clearing and grubbing the reservoir areas should be over excavated to a
minimum depth of 12 inches below the future footing and slab subgrade levels
to provide the base course layer The over excavation should extend a minimum
of 12 inches laterally beyond the limits of the structure Any loose or soft spots
encountered at the over excavated subgrade levels should be removed and
replaced with compacted fill

loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing operations should be backfilled

Fills and Backfills
Fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill material up to 12 inches
below the footing and slab subgrades or the bottom of the base course layer
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 6 inches in largest
dimension The on site cut materials may be used as fill or backfill materials
provided they are processed and reduced to a relatively well graded select
granular material with an average maximum size of about 6 inches The base
course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Any imported material if necessary should be
observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being
transported to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum

moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the

Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of

Corrosion Testing
Chemical analyses including pH value and minimum resistivity were performed
on selected soil samples obtained from the excavated test pits to provide additional
data for corrosivity evaluation The soil samples were retrieved at depths ranging from
2 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface
The pH value of the soil samples was obtained in general accordance with the
test procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 2976 The resistivity test was
