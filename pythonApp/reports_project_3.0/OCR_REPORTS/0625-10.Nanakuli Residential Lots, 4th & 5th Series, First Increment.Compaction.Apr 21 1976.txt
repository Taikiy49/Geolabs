From July 1977 to September 1977 periodic inspections and
density tests were made at the subject site This report
summarizes our inspections and testings Except for the
electrical trench backfill along the Nanakuli Avenue lot
property lines the site has been constructed to final
grade and all lots had been cut or filled and compacted
adequately up to about final grade Compaction test
results for in place fills are enclosed in the enclosed
Compaction Test Summary
In general the site grading operation consisted of cut
and fill The source of the lot fill materials consisted
of existing site stockpiles and borrow material from the
cut areas
The Department of Hawaiian Home Lands
c o Wilson Okamoto Associates Inc P22
As indicated in our soils report dated for this
increment the mauka site was covered with a 2 foot layer of
moderately to highly expansive clayey soils Some of these
expansive soils exist near the final lot grade either as a
compacted fill or in shallow cut lot areas No special non
expansive capping fill was used at this project site At the
makai side reddish brown surface soils are slightly expansive
and was also used as lot fill up to the finish grade Since
post and beam house construction will be utilized at this pro
ject non expansive capping fill was not provided
Prior to receiving fill the existing ground surface was
grubbed to remove the surface vegetation topsoil and pre
viously placed loose stockpile fills The exposed scarified
ground surface was then moisture conditioned and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum density established by ASTM
Test Method D 1557
Keying and benching methods were incorporated where filling
was required on natural slopes greater than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical slope ratio
The fill was then placed in essentially horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the above referenced maximum
density

RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundation
Post and beam construction should be utilized for this project
and as planned
Post footing foundation should bottom at a minimum depth of
12 inches below the adjacent ground surface and bear on stiff
in situ soil or compacted fill materials
For other foundation design details set back slab on grade
and others please refer to our soils report dated April 21
1976
