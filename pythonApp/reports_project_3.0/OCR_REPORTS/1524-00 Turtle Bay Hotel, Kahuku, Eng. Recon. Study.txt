At the request of Mr Paul Low of we have made a
brief reconnaissance study at the above referenced site
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The golf course driving range under construction is located about
500 to 600 feet northwest of the existing golf clubhouse at the Kuilima
Resort project grounds The ground surface generally consists of brown
silty clay that is underlain by a weathered emerged coral reef
PURPOSE OF INVESTIGATION
The purpose of this reconnaissance is to explore a sunken area that
was discovered when heavy equipment was grading the site A small
W Page 2
opening was exposed at the surface and by flashing a light into the
opening it appeared that the void may be sizable A backhoe at the site
was utilized to enlarge the opening so we could enter the void and
visually examine its extent Some measurements and photographs of the
solution cavity were made
OBSERVATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS
Based on visual observations it appears that the solution cavity
resulting from the dissolving of the coral reef by fresh water streams
begins at the opening we entered and traverses in a general direction
northwesterly towards the ocean A small stream channel about 3 feet
wide runs along the bottom of the cavity The water was not running at
the time the reconnaissance was made An estimate of the dimensions of
the cavity are as follows
Width Approximately 15 to 20 feet
Height Approximately 4 to 5 feet
Length Unknown but visually with a light source it appears the
cavity runs for about 30 to 40 feet before bending to
the right From there it is not known how far it
extends towards the ocean
The coral formation above the ceiling of the cavity probably varies
from 4 to 5 feet thick at the location observed
