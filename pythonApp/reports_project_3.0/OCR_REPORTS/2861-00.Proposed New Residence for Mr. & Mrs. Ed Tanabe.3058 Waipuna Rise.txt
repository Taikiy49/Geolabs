INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new residence located at 3058 Waipuna Rise in the east side
of Manoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2861 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations
resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be appropriate
to form the basis for preparation of construction estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 3058 Waipuna Rise in Manoa Valley A residential
structure previously located at the site has been demolished and the lot is currently
empty It is proposed to construct a new split level single family residence at the project
site
It is our understanding that the upper portion of the proposed two 2 story
residence is to be of wood framed post and beam construction The proposed lower
section of the new residence and garage are to be of concrete slab on grade construction
with wood framed structure on top Therefore cut and fill operations for site grading will
be minimal
New CMU retaining walls are also planned near the center of the lot to provide
grade separations between lower on grade portion and upper post and beam
construction

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering evaluation of the site stability and foundation
recommendations pertinent to the proposed structure

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 3058 Waipuna Rise in the Manoa residential district
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is located on the eastern hilly terrain
of Manoa Valley
The project site is fronted by Waipuna Rise on the south and Alani Drive on the
east Existing CRM walls bound the site along the western property limit The northern
property limit is bounded by existing CMU walls
In general the topography of the project site is sloping to the west with a slope
ratio of about eight horizontal to one vertical GH 1V The lot is fronted by Waipuna Rise
which slopes downwards to the west with a gradient of slightly less than 12 percent At
the time of our field exploration an existing house at the site was undergoing demolition
An existing CMU wall remains near the center of the site with a vertical height of about
four and one half 4 5 feet
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epochs by the activities of two 2 basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
Koolau is the younger of the two and reached the end of its main shield building phase
about 2 million years ago The project site is located on the southern flank of Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2861 00 Page 5
Koolau was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the northwest to the
southeast trending rift zones These lava flows are interbedded pahoehoe and a a flows
with pahoehoe being the dominant form The project is situated at the base of the
easterly wall of Manoa Valley in a drainageway cut into the valley wall
Continued erosion in the drainageway upslope of the site has resulted in the
deposition of talus and alluvial materials in the lower reaches of the drainageway The
site is underlain by soils derived from these talus and alluvial materials

Subsoii Conditions
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by an upper layer
approximately 35 to 40 feet thick of medium stiff to very stiff silty clay with cobbles and

silt to a depth of about 56 feet the maximum depth drilled
Soft zones were not encountered in the drilled borings
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings during our field exploration at
about 11 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by medium stiff to very stiff
hillside talus and alluvial materials The laboratory test results and previous experience
indicate that the surface clay material generally has moderate to high swell shrink
potential Also this clay soils will lose its shear strength when it is saturated by water
infiltration and seepage Therefore the foundation will require special design
consideration site and foundation construction procedures in order to reduce potentially
damaging effects that may be caused by the swell shrink characteristics of the subsoils
Slope Stability
The project site is located near the base of the eastern slope of Manoa Valley
which has a history of some slope movements and instability The property s eastern
boundary is fronted by Alani Drive and slopes downward to the west In general the
project site and the adjacent lots are at a relative gentle slope ratio of approximately eight
horizontal to one vertical 8H 1V
Rotational stability analyses of the existing subsurface soil conditions were
performed by using a computer program STABR developed by the University of
California at Berkeley The program provides determination of the factors of safety of
circular slip surfaces by using both the Ordinary Method of Slices and the Bishop s
Modified Method
With assumed parameters of 50 p s f for cohesion and an angle of internal friction
of seven 7 degrees for the silty clay below the groundwater water level our long term
slope stability analysis effective stress analyses indicates that slip circles passing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2861 00 Page 7
through the interface between the alluvium and colluvium at about 40 feet below the
existing ground will possess factors of safety larger than 2 60 Therefore it is our
opinion that the proposed residential construction is located within an area which should
not affect nor be effected by the stability of the sloping site
It is our understanding that the project site and the neighboring lots in the
Subdivision have been developed for several decades Visual observation in the vicinity
of the project site did not reveal any indications of major distress due to slope stability
In our opinion the proposed construction should not pose any potential threat to the
stability of the proposed residential structure located on the subject lot nor to the adjacent
lots provided the following subsurface drainage interception devise is implemented
Trench Drain
High groundwater was encountered in both drilled borings It is possible that
zones of steady or intermittent seepage may be indicated by this high groundwater
encountered Excessive or increases in groundwater flow or seepage could eventually
weaken the strength of the subsurface clay soil
We recommend the installation of a deep trench drain continuously along the
eastern perimeter of the upper portion of the building site to intercept the groundwater
flow or seepage The high point of the system should be located near the corner of the
lot near the intersection of Alani Drive and Waipuna Rise and be approximately 16 feet
below the existing ground surface The trench should extend along Alani Drive the
northern side of the lot across the western side of the proposed car garage and
daylight along Waipuna Rise The approximate location of the trench drain is also shown
in the Site Plan Plate 2 A typical sketch of the trench drain is shown in Plate 5 The
upper two 2 feet of the trench drain should be backfilled and compacted with
impermeable clayey material to prevent surface run off water from infiltrating into the
trench

Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the proposed
structure
In order to reduce the detrimental effects of the moderately to highly expansive soil
we recommend that the expansive clays under the spread continuous footings be over
excavated to a minimum depth of three 3 feet below the finished grade and then
replaced with non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The non expansive materials should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted non
expansive material
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of three 3 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade
Both of these design concepts are shown schematically on Plate 3 Schematic
Sections Footings on Expansive Soil
An allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
compacted non expansive material or on the in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should be extended to an elevation
below the bottom of the trench to reduce the potential for footing settlement due to the
possibility of settlement of trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2861 00 Page 9
Footings located on sloping ground should be embedded deeper to provide a
minimum horizontal set back distance of eight 8 feet measured from the toe of the
footing to the face of the slope
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface runoff that may affect the expansive soils around the foundations
All footing backfill should be properly compacted in six 6 inch lifts to avoid future surface
water infiltration On site clayey soil if it is properly moisture conditioned to about
optimum moisture content could be utilized to provide a relatively impermeable backfill
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that on site silty clay soils are moderately to highly expansive
if allowed to dry and subsequently wetted In order to reduce the expansive effects of the
soils we recommend that a minimum three 3 foot capping layer of non expansive
imported select borrow material be placed below the slabs on grade
The moderately to highly expansive clay below the slab should be removed to a
minimum depth of three 3 feet below the nearest adjacent finished grade and replaced
with non expansive off site borrow material as crusher waste select borrow or coral The
underlying clay soil should be moistened by water sprinkling and maintained in a moist
condition prior to backfilling with the non expansive material The non expansive material
should then be placed in layers not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A minimum four 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided directly below the slab In order to protect against
moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slab

The thickened edges of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade The three 3 foot over excavation and
replacement should extend a minimum distance of three 3 feet horizontally outside the
edges of the slab Ideally the replacement material in this zone outside the slab should
be impervious as well as non expansive If granular material is used to replace the
underlying expansive clay we recommend that a minimum 12 inch capping of non expansive
and impervious material be utilized above the granular backfill

Post and Beam Construction
It is our opinion that post and beam construction with an elevated wood framed
floor may provide a more economical foundation system than an on grade concrete
structural slab
Since the upper portion of the proposed new residence is situated in sloping
terrain we recommend that post and beam construction be used for the upper structure
We recommend that individual post and beam footings should be embedded on an
excavated trench consisting of a minimum of three 3 feet of select backfill extending
below the on site silty clay The subsequent individual post footings should then be
embedded and constructed on this over excavated select borrow backfill trench The
above footing trench should extend a minimum of about one 1 foot beyond the footing
width
Concrete Driveway and Garage Siab
For the concrete driveway and garage slab recommendations regarding the three
should be followed However since these vehicular slab can accommodate some
amount of relative movement the non expansive capping layer may be reduced to two
2 feet in thickness to reduce construction costs if some slab cracking is tolerable

A thickness of six 6 inches is recommended for the vehicular concrete pavements
for the driveway and garage slab
The thickened edges of the garage slab should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the adjacent grade Recommendations for the ground floor slab on grade
construction should be followed
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 feet intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the concrete slabs
Lot Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble old footings and organic matter
encountered during scarifying and grubbing should be removed and properly disposed
of off site The lot grading operations should be performed with care so as not to disturb
the adjacent perimeter walls along the northern and western property boundaries
We recommend that the lower portion of the lot where the driveway and on grade slab
are located should be built with select borrow import fill to provide additional slope stability
improvement Also the import fill required will provide the necessary non expansive capping
fill for the on grade concrete slab construction
The fills and backfills within the top three 3 feet below the on grade slabs should
consist of non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where
embankment for slab construction is required the on site clay soil should be over excavated
to provide a three 3 foot minimum thickness of non expansive select capping fill underneath
the on grade slabs
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches prior to
placement of fills The scarified subgrade should then be thoroughly moisture conditioned
and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten

dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provided drainage of surface runoff away from all

Drainage
The finished lot grades outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter runoff
water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2861 00 Page 14
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for site grading utility and
foundation construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
lf this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be
responsible for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
