INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Mr Mark Teruya Residence at 705 Kaulana Place within the
Hawaii Loa Ridge Development on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single family residence at 705 Kaulana Place within
the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on the information provided to us the subject lot is approximately
rectangular in shape with an area of approximately 14 000 square feet Detailed design
information pertaining to the architectural and structural aspects of the proposed
residence are not available to us at the time of preparation of this report Based on our
discussion with Mr Mark Teruya we understand that a swimming pool may be included
in the proposed project construction Construction of new utility lines and pavement for
the driveway are also anticipated for the project
Our previous experience in the area suggest that the project site may be underlain
by residual soils over decomposed basalt material Special attention should be given to

Foundations
Detailed information pertaining to the structural loadings are not available to us at
the time of preparation of this report Because of the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the proposed new residence
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on the stiff in situ soils encountered at
shallow depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive clay soil we recommend that the
footings be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finish grade Due
to the sloping terrain at the subject site we recommend that the foundations be
embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 8 foot set back distance measured
horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the slope face Continuous
foundations running parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped
footings

should be set back inside the sewer easement for slope stability and ease of future
sewerline maintenance work
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines

0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils

Slab On Grade
The project site is underlain by a deposit of silty clay soils which are considered
to be moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs
supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to
lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these
soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately expansive subgrade soils
we recommend that the concrete slab on grade be supported on 18 inches of non
expansive select fill material The non expansive select fill should extend at least 2 feet
beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The prepared capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report The select fill would serve as a
capping fill layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils
and this capping fill would also reduce the potential loss of moisture from the prepared
moist subgrade soils The reduction in future subgrade saturation should reduce the
potential swelling of the underlying moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3183 00 Page 9
requirements for the subgrade soils and select fill are discussed in the Site Grading
section of this report
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C
33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be installed below the slab to provide uniform support
for the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be
placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid
in curing of the slab concrete The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab a 6 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative compaction is recommended in lieu
of the No 3B Fine gravel capillary break layer
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be provided
below the concrete slab The 18 inch capping layer recommended under the slab and
pavement areas may be reduced to 12 inches if some amount of slab cracking can be
tolerated Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Cavity Probing
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by basalt formation at
shallow depths The basaltic rock encountered at the site is pahoehoe which is the form
of lava flow most prone to contain tubes voids and cavities Based on our previous
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3183 00 Page 10
experience in the vicinity of the project site large cavities or caverns have been
encountered during the foundation construction on some of the house lots
in order to reduce the possibility of potential large voids situated below a
foundation causing future footing settlement and or foundation failure we recommend
that the foundation subgrades be probed The probes should consist of 3 inch minimum
diameter probe holes drilled to a depth of 10 feet below the bottom of the footing Probe
holes should be drilled at each column location and at 10 foot centers along continuous
foundations
The probe holes should be subsequently backfilled with a neat cement grout
injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage fashion i e from the bottom of the
probe hole up to the top If large cavities greater than one foot in height are
encountered during the probe drilling or if grout takes exceeds four times the theoretical
volume of the probe hole a mortar like grout one part sand to one part cement should
be injected instead of the neat cement grout If the grout intake exceeds one half cubic
yard it should be stopped and the grout allowed to set prior to continuation of the
remaining grouting backfilling operations
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment it is most likely preferable
to perform the probing and grouting operations prior to structure and footing excavation
The probing and grouting operations should be conducted under the full time
observation of a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to allow additional evaluation field
consultation and possibly field adjustment of the above recommendations if necessary

After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 18 inches below the

or bottom of base course layer If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are
encountered at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be

basalt formation to lessen potential instability of the new fill The filling operations should
Start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above recommendations for fill placement A 2H 1V cut and fill
slopes are recommended
Select granular fill material for fills and backfills under the slab and pavement areas
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Select granular fill material should consist
of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches
in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio
CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20

approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use The

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
3 borings to depths ranging from 12 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
portable drilling equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist wno monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
