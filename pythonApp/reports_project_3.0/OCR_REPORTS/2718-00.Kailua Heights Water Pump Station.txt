INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
regarding the causes of the settled sidewalk at the existing Kailua Heights Water booster
Pump Station in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii This report confirms and
supplements the material contained in our letter report dated The
general project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the information
obtained These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
to form the basis of preparation for construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
An unreinforced concrete slab in front of the existing water pump station has
settled and cracked A concrete walkway leading to the southeastern entrance of the
pump station also appears to have settled It is proposed to determine the causes of
these pavement distresses in order to determine possible measures for their repair
It is understood that correction of these settled slabs will be included in a project
for general renovation of the facility
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to obtain an overview of the shallow subsurface
conditions immediately around the existing pump station The subsoil data obtained was
used to determine the probable causes of the slab distresses and to formulate our
geotechnical engineering recommendations for repair of the damage

SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing Kailua Heights Water Pump Station is located at the corner of Aunauna
Street and Aupupu Street in the Kailua Heights district of Kailua on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
It was observed that an unreinforced concrete sidewalk in front of the pump station
has sheared off apparently as a result of settlement at about one third 1 3 of its length
from its northwesterly end A small cavity below the slab could be observed through the
crack opening It appeared that the subgrade immediately below the broken slab had
eroded locally
A concrete walkway leading to the southeastern entrance of the pump station also
appeared to have undergone localized settlement This settled walkway area was
covered with eroded soil apparently washed down from the slope behind the building
The slope behind the pump station appeared to have been eroded by surface
water runoff Deposition of soil probably originating from slope erosion was noted on
both sides of the pump station Moist subgrade conditions were also noted below the
transported soil which has accumulated in the unpaved area immediately behind the
building

Subsoil Conditions
The subsoil conditions around the pump station were explored by drilling and
sampling a total of six 6 borings to depths of about five and one half 5 5 to thirteen
13 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Three 3 of the borings Boring A and 5 were drilled at the location of
the sheared off area at the front sidewalk to explore the subsoil conditions in that area
Boring No 1 A was drilled after Boring No 1 encountered a metal pipe at five and one
half 5 5 feet and was terminated The other three 3 borings Boring and 4
were drilled around the other sides of the building which did not indicate settlement for
comparison
In Boring A and 5 soft and very moist subsoils including existing clay
fill and native clayey silt residual soil were encountered below the damaged slab in front
of the pump station Competent basalt saprolite was encountered below the soft subsoils
at approximately ten 10 feet below the broken slab in Borings No 1 A and 5 Our
laboratory ring swell tests indicate that the existing clay fill has high expansion potential
when allowed to dry and subsequently re wetted
In Boring and 4 medium stiff to stiff clayey silt residual soil was
encountered above medium dense to very dense sandy basalt saprolite at approximately
eight 8 0 to nine and one half 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings the broken slab in front of the pump station and the soft
subgrade soils should be removed and replaced with a new reinforced concrete slab and
properly compacted select granular backfill A subdrain system around the building
should be considered to prevent or reduce build up of hydrostatic pressure around the
building basement and below the front sidewalk
The surface drainage system at the pump station should be improved to divert
future surface runoff away from the building The slope behind the building should be
flattened as space allows to slow down future surface runoff over the slope in order to
reduce further slope erosion
A program should be initiated for periodic inspection and maintenance of the
surface drainage and the face of the slope
Probable Causes of Pavement Distresses
Front Sidewalk
As mentioned previously soft and very moist subsoils were encountered below the
front sidewalk which was cracked and settled while generally competent subsoils
were encountered on all other sides of the building
Therefore it is our opinion that the pavement distress were probably caused by
the loss of ground support due to the combined actions of the following two 2
probable factors
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2718 00 Page 7
1 Inadequate compaction of the existing clay fill which apparently was placed
more than twenty 20 years ago based on the date 1967 of the available
plans
2 Softening of the clay fill and the underlying cohesive residual soils by
undetected sources of water such as utility leakages transient ground
seepages and or surface runoff infiltration etc
Southeasterly Walkway
Boring No 3 was drilled next to the concrete walkway on the southeasterly side
of the pump station that appeared to have settled Generally competent subsoils
were noted in this boring as previously mentioned
Based on the competent subsoil conditions encountered in this boring it appears
that the concrete walkway has probably not settled as previously suspected but
rather at a low elevation which has resulted in an accumulation of eroded soil
Although no repair per se is considered to be required for this condition it may
be considered desirable during installation of the subdrian system described
below to raise the elevation of this sidewalk to preclude recurrence of the
accumulation of soil materials which created an unsightly condition
Slab Repair
Based on the aforementioned probable causes the following repair program is
recommended for the settled sidewalk in front of the pump station
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2718 00 Page 8
1 The existing ground utilities located below the settled sidewalk should be
examined to detect probable leakages and any which are found should be
repaired
2 Remove and replace the broken sidewalk slab for a minimum of twenty 20
feet from its northwestern end The replacement concrete slab should be
adequately reinforced and supported by a minimum four 4 inch thick base
course cushion compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The new slab
should be separated from the existing sidewalk slab to remain in order to
avoid differential slab settlement due to different subgrade materials
3 Remove the subgrade to a minimum depth of ten 10 feet below the twenty
20 foot long slab section removed and also remove any soft subsoils
encountered during excavation The excavation below the new slab and the
four 4 inch thick base course cushion should be backfilled with select non
expansive granular material such as select borrow or base course which
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 in maximum nine 9 inch
thick horizontal loose lifts
The excavated on site subsoils are not suitable for backfilling the excavation
due to their high expansion potential upon drying and re wetting However
the on site materials may be used to backfill excavations in unpaved areas
adjacent to the slab if needed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2718 00 Page 9
It appears that there is no subdrain system to relieve possible build up of
hydrostatic pressure around the basement walls of the pump station Groundwater
seepages infiltration utility leakages or any other sources of water may have a potential
to accumulate around the pump station basement and also below the front sidewalk This
would tend to result in saturation and loss of strength of the subsoils We therefore
recommend that the installation of a subdrain system around the basement be considered
in conjunction with this repair program to mitigate or reduce these potential problems
The excavation for subdrain installation should be backfilled with clean granular
material such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel to a minimum horizontal
distance of two 2 feet behind the basement walls The gravel should be compacted with
a sled compactor for a minimum of three 3 passes in maximum one 1 foot horizontal
loose lifts
The upper two 2 feet of backfill for the subdrain excavation in areas not covered
by sidewalks may consist of the excavated on site cohesive soils compacted to a
minimum of 85 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Select borrow or base course compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density should be used for the upper two 2 feet of backfill below
sidewalks or pavements
Excavation
About ten 10 feet of excavation is anticipated for the slab repair and subdrain
installation We recommend that the excavations be properly supported with adequate
shoring due to the excavation depth and soft subsoil conditions that may be encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2718 00 Page 10
The method of excavation and shoring support should be left to the choice of the
contractor within practical limits and safety considerations as required by applicable
governing regulations The contractor should be given the sole responsibility of properly
designing constructing and monitoring the performance of the required shoring system
by retaining the services of qualified professional geotechnical engineer and structural
engineer for this purpose
Slope Erosion
During our field exploration it was observed that the slope behind the pump station
apparently had been eroded and that transported soil depositions were widespread on
the existing ground surface behind and on both sides of the building
Therefore we recommend that the surface drainage system of the pump station
be improved to divert future surface runoff away from the building
As aminimum we feel that an adequately sized concrete ditch should be provided
at the toe of the slope In addition we recommend that the slope be flattened as much
as possible such as to a ratio of 2 horizontal 2 H to one vertical 1 V within limitations
imposed by space constraints to slow down future surface runoff over the slope in order
to reduce further slope erosion Based on our curvory visual observation of the scope
during our field exploration it appears that stiff residual soil and or weathered basalt may
be encountered on the slope when it is flattened If a more accurate soil profile of the
slope is desired we recommend that additional borings be drilled on the slope prior to
contract bidding
A suitable vegetative ground cover should also be established and maintained on
the slope to further reduce erosion

Inspection and Maintenance
We recommend that a program of periodic inspection of the area be initiated and
maintained to detect and remove any soil or other debris which may collect in the
concrete ditch and ground adjacent to the building thus preclude free drainage of
rainwater
Construction Precautions
As noted a metal pipe was encountered in Boring No 1 at a depth of five and one
half 5 5 feet below the ground surface This pipe is not shown on the drawings made
available to us Therefore it is considered necessary to emphasize that the contractor
must exercise proper care during excavation to preclude damage to this and other
underground features which may not be known to exist or shown on the available
drawings
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the repair work should be forwarded to the project
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bid
This review is needed to determine proper interpretation of the recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project to see that critical
items of work such as excavation backfilling and compaction are properly constructed
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six 6 borings to
depths of five and one half 5 5 to thirteen 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Due to limited headroom
at the desired boring locations portable drilling equipment was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
Operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Graphic presentations of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches or a lesser height where dictated by
the headroom available The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches or a
shorter penetration when refusal was encountered are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths The reduced falling heights of the hammer are also indicated
with the blow counts at the appropriate sample depths
