Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Kapolei Business Park Phase 1
development off Kalaeloa Boulevard in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site designated as Lot No 24 is a rectangular shaped parcel of
approximately 2 15 acres in area It is situated to the northwest of the intersection
between Opule Street and Lauia Street
The development consists of a one story rectangular light industrial
warehouse office structure The new building will be surrounded by driveways parking
areas and loading zones along the perimeter We understand that the warehouse
building will be constructed of tilt up walls on all sides with steel columns at the side walls
The remaining building will be supported by steel columns with metal roofing and a
concrete slab on grade floor

Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the warehouse building
The footing foundations have already been excavated at the time of our probing
operation Therefore the probe holes were located at or close to the center of the column
and panel footings depending on the accessibility by our drilling equipment The
approximate locations of the probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan
Plate 1 In general the probe holes at the new warehouse building were drilled to depths
of about 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled by Geolabs using a
truck mounted drill rig Mobile B 80 equipped with continuous flight augers Following the
probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes
to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the
grouting operation A total of 57 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 570 lineal
feet below the design footing bottom elevations were drilled for the project
The probe holes were backfilled with flowable fill 150 psi supplied by Ameron
injected at low pressures The grouting work was performed by American Standard
Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc and observed by a field representative from Geolabs
Grout quantities were estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole
compared to the number of strokes to empty the concrete truck The grouting operation
generally proceeded in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter steel grout pipe attached to the end of the
grouting hose was then inserted into the open hole and lowered to
approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout injection then
commenced at the direction of our field representative who provided
direction as to when the injection was complete or whether additional
injection was required
