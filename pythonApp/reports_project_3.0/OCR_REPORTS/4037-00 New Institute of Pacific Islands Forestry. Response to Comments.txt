Fill Material and Compaction
Fill and backfill materials for general fill should consist of well graded granular
materials in which the majority of the material has a maximum size of less than 12 inches
with an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches Below structures the fill should be
capped with a 12 inch thick layer of structural fill material consisting of well graded
granular material of 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
General fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 24 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent relative
compaction Structural fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction
Where fills are composed of rock boulders and or cobbles conventional
compaction testing is not practicable Therefore a testing program to evaluate the
number of passed of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction
