PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located south of Interstate Route H 1 highway and west of
Manager s Drive in the District of Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The 40 acre
parcel is divided into two phases Phases 1 and 2 The location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The Phase 2 project site includes a total of
104 residential lots and roadways The site grading work generally consisted of cuts and
fills of up to 13 feet The grading plan is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for The Renaissance Phase 2 project
was observed by Geolabs Inc from to The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placing and compacting fills up to about
13 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project The site grading
and utility backfilling work was performed by Royal Contracting Company During the site
grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 63 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for The
Renaissance Phase 2 project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
within 3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to
slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to constructing a 3 foot wide sidewalk adjacent to
and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
