Dear Ms. Loui: 
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration in 
support of the Evaluation of Existing Soil Stockpile project at the Sand Island Wastewater 
Treatment Plant (WWTP) in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location 
and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical recommendations resulting 
from our field exploration and laboratory testing for the project. These findings and 
geotechnical recommendations are intended to evaluate the suitability of the existing soil 
stockpile for use as general fill and/or non-expansive granular fill material at the 
Maintenance Building Septage and Site Improvements (MBSSI) project.  The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the information provided, the existing soil stockpile is located at the 
southeastern portion of the Sand Island WWTP in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The soil stockpile is roughly 500 feet by 400 feet and about 8 feet in height. We 
understand it is desired to evaluate the suitability of the existing soil stockpile for use as 
general fill and/or non-expansive granular fill material at the MBSSI project.  
PURPOSE AND SCOPE 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview 
of the subsurface conditions at the existing soil stockpile to formulate geotechnical 
engineering recommendations pertaining to the suitability of the existing soil stockpile as a 
potential source of fill materials for the MBSSI project. To accomplish this, we conducted 
an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts:

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
We explored the subsurface conditions of the existing soil stockpile by excavating 
and sampling eight test pits, designated as TP-1 through TP-8, extending to a depth of 
about 8 feet below the existing ground surface.  The approximate test pit locations are 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. In addition, six bulk samples, designated as Bulk-1 
through Bulk-6, were collected at selected depths of the excavated test pits for laboratory 
testing to evaluate the engineering properties of the existing soil stockpile.

Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing program, the existing soil 
stockpile generally consisted of tannish brown to brownish tan silty sands with some 
gravel.  The majority of the soil materials generally exhibited fines content ranging 
between about 24 and 30 percent.  In addition, the majority of the soil materials generally 
exhibited a California Bearing Ratio (CBR) value of over 65 and swell of less than 0.5 
percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883. 
Based on our evaluation and assessment, it is our opinion that the existing soil 
stockpile may be used as a source of general fill and/or non-expansive granular fill 
material for the MBSSI project.  Due to the potential for variations in subsurface conditions 
across the soil stockpile, we recommend that additional laboratory be performed during 
construction to confirm and/or modify the recommendations presented herein. 
LIMITATIONS 
The findings and recommendations presented herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from the test pits excavated and bulk samples collected. Variations of 
the subsurface conditions between and beyond the field data points may occur, and the 
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is 
underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the 
recommendations provided herein. 
The test pit locations indicated herein are approximate, having been staked out in 
the field using a hand-held Global Positioning System (GPS) gadget. Elevations of the test 
pits were interpolated from spot elevations shown on the Grading and Drainage Plans 
dated May 7, 2019, prepared by AECOM. The physical location and elevation of the test 
pits should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the test pits depict 
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition. Water level data from the test pits were measured at the times shown on the 
graphic representations and/or presented in the text of this report. These data have been 
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report. It should be noted that 
groundwater fluctuations may occur due to variation in rainfall, seepage, temperature, and 
other factors.

Field Exploration 
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling eight test pits, 
designated as Test Pit Nos. 1 through 8, extending to a depth of about 8 feet below the 
existing ground surface. The test pits were excavated using a track excavator.

textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488, Standard 
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils, and monitored the excavation 
operations on a near-continuous (full-time) basis. These classifications were further 
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general 
accordance with ASTM D2487, Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for 
Engineering Purposes (Unified Soil Classification System), as shown on the Soil Log 
Legend, Plate A-0.1. Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with

Laboratory Tests 
Five Sieve Analysis tests (ASTM C117 & C136) were performed on selected bulk 
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil 
classification. Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B-1. 
Two Direct Shear tests (ASTM D3080) were performed on selected bulk soil 
samples to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the sandy materials tested.  The 
test results are presented on Plates B-2 and B-3. 
Five laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests (ASTM D1883) were performed on 
bulk soil samples obtained from the existing stockpile to evaluate the suitability of the 
stockpiled materials for fill material.  The test results are presented on Plates B-4 through 
B-8. 
Three Modified Proctor compaction tests (ASTM D1557) were performed on bulk 
soil samples to evaluate the relationship between the moisture content and the dry density 
of the stockpiled materials. The test results are presented on Plates B-9 through B-11.
