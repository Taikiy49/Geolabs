PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The highway realignment project is east of the existing Honoapiilani Highway in
Lahaina on the Island of Maui Hawaii The proposed Honoapiilani Highway Realignment
Phase 1A project will involve construction of a new highway between the future Keawe
Street Extension to the north and Lahainaluna Road to the south The location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 to
2 11
The ultimate highway will be a divided four lane roadway with two lanes in each
direction The Phase 1A project consists of the construction of one side of the ultimate
roadway with two 12 foot wide travel lanes The roadway will be about 3 200 linear feet long
and have 4 to 10 foot wide shoulders Concrete drainage swales will be utilized on the side of
the roadway The project consisted of the construction of the following elements

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for the Honoapiilani Highway Realignment
Phase 1A project was observed by Geolabs Inc from to
The site grading and utility installation consisted of clearing and grubbing fills of up to about 28
feet in depth and placement and compaction of fills and backfills to achieve the design
finished grades for the project Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company performed the site
grading and utility installation work During the site grading and backfilling operations samples
of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the pavement areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
During the site grading and backfilling operations periodic field density tests were
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 114 of this report These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at
the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Rock fills with maximum aggregate dimension of 3 feet were used in portions of the
embankment fills Since conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in these
rock fills a testing program to evaluate the number of passes by a specific compactor
needed to achieve the desired level of compaction was performed
