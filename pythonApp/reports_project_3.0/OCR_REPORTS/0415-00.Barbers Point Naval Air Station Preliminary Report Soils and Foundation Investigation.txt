o II FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of a preliminary geologic recon
naissance and a subsurface exploration program of 7 borings
each approximately 45 feet deep The drilling was accomplished
using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous
flight hollow stem augers and a NX core barrel with diamond bits
The borings were continuously inspected by an engineer or geologist
from our firm The approximate boring locations are shown on
Plate 2 and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test 2 5 inch O D
ring samples or rock cores were taken at selected depth intervals
The core barrel was used when the drilling became too hard for
the conventional flight auger
2
W O 415
The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a 2 inch O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of
the relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field and
representative material was placed in containers and returned
to our laboratory for inspection and testing

A GEOLOGY
The Ewa Plain encompassing Barbers Point Naval Air Station
was formed during a late stage Pleistocene Epoch of Oahu s
Geologic history The plain was formed by the combined
constructional sedimentary processes of coral algal reef
and alluvial plain formation Stream sediments were washed
into the Pearl Harbor embayment from the Waianae and Koolau
Volcanic Ranges as the region sank The embayment may never
have been very deep and it probably filled as it sank The
Shallow water origin of the area is evidenced by the presence
of shallow water coral algal reefs similar to those forming
in Kaneohe Bay now These reefs apparently grew during
the warmer interglacial stages of the Ice Age when eustatic
3
W O 415
sea level rose above its present height During the glacial
stages sea level declined due to the accumulation of water
in the glaciers
A late stage relative lowering of sea level has left the
reef exposed The exposure of the reef to rainwater mixed
with rotting vegetation formed slightly acid groundwater
This acid groundwater is probably responsible for puka
and openwork a lattice like appearance of the reef
formation by enlargement of natural reef cavities
SOILS
Geologic reconnaissance and subsurface drilling indicates
that most of the area apparently is underlain by at least
45 feet of moderately hard to soft coral algal reef and
some stiff clay However because of the variability of
reef environments over distances of a few feet the possi
bility of encountering soils with characteristics different
than those found in the boreholes cannot be discounted This
variance other than pukas would occur on the surface as
a result of cavities or channels being filled with soft soils
Much of the near surface reef material is relatively hard
because of solution and redeposition of calcium carbonate
by evaporating capillary water Although individual portions
of the reef may be very hard the underlying reef may be full
of pukas or lattice coral

A Site Preparation
Because of the highly variable nature of the coral and the
presence of pukas the following pre construction pro
cedures are recommended
1 Existing vegetation should be cleared and wasted from
the site All surface soils coral debris and soft
coral should be stripped and wasted from the site
This item should include the clayey silt located in
Borings 4 and 5
2 The exposed coral surface should then be thoroughly
ripped with a D 9 cat or equivalent with rippers in
an attempt to uncover the near surface pukas
