INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new residence at 3570 Kalihi Street in the upper part of Kalihi
Valley of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses of the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

The project site is located at 3570 Kalihi Street in the upper part of Kalihi Valley on
the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided the subject lot is
approximately rectangular in shape with an area of approximately 15 200 square feet
We understand that it is proposed to construct a new one story residential
structure near the front of the subject lot A second residential structure to the rear of the
subject lot may also be planned in the future It should be noted that the
recommendations presented in this report are solely for the design input of the currently
proposed new residence near the front of the subject lot These recommendations may
need to be revised and or modified depending on the actual design concepts for other
planned improvements in the future at the subject lot

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and forms
the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank
The project site is located in the upper part of Kalihi Valley which was formed by
stream erosion of Tertiary Age Koolau Range basalt flows This initial period of erosion
and valley carving was followed by several periods of relative rise and fall of sea level
which permitted valley filling by alluvium followed by subsequent periods of erosion
During the Quaternary Age Honolulu Volcanic Series lava flowed down the valley and
was subsequently partially covered by Quaternary Age alluvium Continued recent
alluvial processes have covered part of the site with black to dark gray boulder clay

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 3570 Kalihi Street in the upper part of Kalihi Valley of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is approximately rectangular in
shape with an area of approximately 15 200 square feet The project site is currently

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the current design and grading concept new fills on the order of about
4 to 5 feet over the existing ground surface are anticipated in order to attain the design
finished grades for the proposed construction It is our opinion that shallow post and
beam foundations bearing on the new imported fill materials may be used for support of
the proposed new residence Our laboratory swell test results indicate that the near
surface in situ soils exhibit moderate swelling and shrinkage potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore we recommend that slabs on grade required for the
project be supported on at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material
for more uniform support Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and current grading concept we anticipate that
site preparation work for the proposed construction will consist of minor cuts on the order
of up to 3 feet at the north side of the project site and new fills on the order of up to about
5 feet over the existing ground surface at the proposed building footprint
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3662 00 Page 8
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if
appropriate
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities that
are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and grubbing the areas within the building slabs on grade i e the
carport storage and laundry areas should be over excavated where necessary to
provide the minimum 12 inch thick capping layer of non expansive select granular fill
material below the slab subgrade level i e bottom of base course The select granular
fill should extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond the perimeter of the proposed building
slabs on grade
The over excavated subgrades or areas designated to receive fills should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3662 00 Page 9
compacted engineered fill at no additional cost to the owner Contract documents should
include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
The on site expansive clayey soils generated from the cut areas should not be
used as fill materials below the building footprint and pavement areas They should be
disposed of off site properly or used in landscaping areas if appropriate General fill
materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the bottom of the 12 inch non
expansive capping fill and to the finished grades under the post and beam foundations
should consist of imported materials with a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent The 12 inch non expansive
capping fill should consist of non expansive imported granular material such as crushed
coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less
than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density

Shallow Foundations
Based on the current design concept we understand that post and beam
foundations will be utilized for support of the proposed new residence In addition new
fills on the order of about 4 to 5 feet will be required under the building footprint to raise
the existing ground surface to the design finished grades Therefore it is our opinion that
post and beam foundations bearing on the new engineered fills may be used for support
of the proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per
the new engineered fills placed in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Site Preparation section of this report This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel and
concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3662 00 Page 11
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings
bearing on the new engineered fill materials Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade If foundations are located next to utility tenches or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slab On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the carport
storage and laundry areas of the proposed new residence To reduce the potential for
structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site expansive soils we
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3662 00 Page 12
recommend that non expansive select capping fill material be provided for a minimum
depth of 12 inches below the base course cushion The capping fill should also extend
beyond the slab perimeter a minimum of 12 inches
Prior to placement of the select capping fill the clayey subgrades should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The select capping fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential drying
and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of shrink swell effects on the
slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of base course
cushion and concrete To reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to
reduce the costs of form construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat
against the soil excavations
A 6 inch layer of base course below the concrete slab is recommended for more
uniform support The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction We recommend that the concrete slab
at the carport be designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and be
reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some amount of differential movement
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic
inch p c i may be used for the compacted base course A minimum slab thickness of
GEOLABS HAWAII

4 inches for the carport may be used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should
be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular
traffic

backfilled tightly against the slab with non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately around a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall
discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should also
be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Backfill behind the permeable drainage zone may consist of the on site clayey
soils or select granular fill material Unless covered by concrete slabs the upper
12 inches of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce
water infiltration behind the walls

is currently planned for the proposed new residence We anticipate that the vehicle

Utility Trench
New utility connections will be required for the proposed new residence A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential
of formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for
the sides of the pipes Improper backfill material and placement could result in settlement
of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select fill material The backfill
should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
