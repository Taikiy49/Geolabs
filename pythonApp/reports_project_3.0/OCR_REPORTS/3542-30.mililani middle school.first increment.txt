SITE GRADING
The project site is located within the existing Mililani Mauka development in the
Mililani area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site encompasses approximately
15 acres in area and is bounded approximately by Kuaoa Street to the north a sewerline
easement to the west Lehiwa Drive to the south and open undeveloped land to the east
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The mass grading operations footing excavations roadway and utility construction
for The Mililani Middle School First Increment project were performed by Oahu
Construction Company The site grading operation consisted of clearing and grubbing
over excavation of expansive clays and unsuitable materials placement and compaction
of engineered fills and utility trench excavation and backfill
During the site grading operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 12 inches below the finish subgrades in the building
areas to provide the recommended 12 inch non expansive select capping fill The
non expansive select capping fill were also placed approximately 2 feet beyond the
building perimeters The over excavated subgrades were generally moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to about
90 and 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular capping fill layer the fill
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The
base course and select borrow underneath the AC pavements were also compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 587 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 16 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading
footing excavations and roadway and utility construction for the above referenced project
observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report
dated May 16 1996 and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have occured without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appear to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
