INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil investigation is to evaluate the
nature of the soils underlying the project site and to
provide recommendations for the design of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed residential development
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located three miles west of the town of
Waimea and approximately one mile west of the intersection
of Kawaihae and Kohala Waimea Roads The site is situated
just below a break in the lower slope of the Kohala Mountains
on what is presently open range land It is bordered by
Kawaihae Road to the south
The entire development site covers 57 5 acres The proposed
Units 1A and 1B occupy the eastern 19 acres of the total deve
lopment
The project site is generally covered by low grass and sparse
cactus The surface of the ground is also heavily covered
with loose gravel and boulders some very large
A stream channel crosses the eastern portion of the site from
northeast to southwest and is about 15 20 feet deep The
stream was dry at the time of the field exploration of the
site
The elevation at the site ranged from about 2050 to 2220 feet
The site generally slopes down toward the south at about 5 to
10 percent gradients

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
Units TA and 1B of the Kamuela View Estates Subdivision will
consist of 43 single family residential units Units 1A and
1B are located on the eastern third of the entire development
site
Cuts of generally less than 3 feet and fills up to about 10
feet are planned Most of the fill will be in the existing
stream A new stream channel will be constructed to replace
the existing stream Pipe arch sections will be utilized
where the proposed alignment crosses the subdivision streets
The rest of the channel will be unlined The channel will
be about 7 to 9 feet deep

Geology
The site although located on the south flank of the Kohala
mountain is underlain by Pleistocene lava flows originating
from Mauna Kea The rock known as the Hamakua Volcanic Series
is a dense gray olivine basalt The northern extent of this
flow series marked by a break in the slope just north of the
site altered the drainage pattern in this area The steep
guiches in the older Kohala Slope were partially filled and
the streams diverted sharply to the southwest This area of
the island is also known to be capped by a layer of Pahala ash
of variable thickness This well known wind deposited material
is a vitric volcanic ash believed to be derived from Mauna Kea
eruptions
Based on the test pits and field observations the site appears
to be underlain by weathered to highly weathered olivine basalt
with localized deposits of alluvial silt and boulders and small
deposits of Pahala ash The near surface conditions in the
proposed Units 1A and 1B can be divided into 3 subsurface soil
conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general competent bearing soils were encountered at the
site Spread footing foundation or slab on grade may be
used
Variable soils were encountered along the proposed drainage
channel alignment The field exploration indicates that
hard rock should be encountered near the invert along most
of the proposed channel alignment
Earthwork
The preliminary grading plan for the project indicates that
cuts generally less than 1 to 3 feet are planned for the
site Fills up to 10 feet will be required to cover the
existing stream area
Prior to filling in the stream loose material and vegetation
should be cleared from the bottom and sides of the stream
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMUELA VIEW ESTATES P 7
Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into the exist
ing firm ground
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests no major
settlement problems are anticipated for fills up to 10 feet
Select on site materials or borrow materials from the future
subdivision areas may be used in the filling operations In
general the materials should be less than 8 inch maximum size
and non expansive
Slopes
Except along the proposed stream channel cut and fill heights
less than 4 feet are planned In the proposed channel slope
heights up to about 11 feet are planned
For fill slopes ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1
should be used
For cut slopes the following slope ratios can be used
Unweathered rock 3 1 H V
Weathered rock 1 1 H V
Highly Weathered rock _1 1 H V
Alluvium ash 2 1 H V
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMUELA VIEW ESTATES P 8
Slope protection should be considered to reduce erosion
except where weathered or unweathered rock is encountered
Foundations
For the proposed light residential structures spread
footing foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs
should be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum dis
tance of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops
of slopes should have a minimum horizontal set back distance
of 5 feet from the slope face Post footings away from the
tops of slopes should be placed 8 inches below the finished
grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pre
sure of 3000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the
stiff natural soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from house foundations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Where the subgrade is granular the base course layer may be
omitted

To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethelene moisture barrier should be considered

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
