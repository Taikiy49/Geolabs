Dear Mr. Tagal: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the earthwork construction of the Bridge No. 7E Project.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located on Kaumualii Highway, Route 50 approximately 7.4 miles 
west of Lihue on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii.  The project generally consisted of 
removing and replacing the existing culvert bridge crossing located about 1,500 feet 
west of the intersection of Maluhia Road.  A Project Location Map is shown on Plate 1. 
Construction of the new bridge involved installing a temporary bypass crossing 
prior to demolition of the existing culvert bridge.  The temporary bypass crossing 
consisted of a Mirafi wire mesh wall structure, supported on a surge rock stabilization 
layer installed in the stream.  The permanent replacement bridge consisted of a box 
culvert crossing structure, supported on a 16.5-inch octagonal precast prestressed 
concrete pile foundation system.  Locations of the bypass crossing and the replacement 
bridge are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services 
during the site grading work for the period between May 8, 2017 and May 8, 2018. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 15 feet 
deep for removal of the existing culvert bridge, construction of the new culvert structure, 
head walls, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, and pavement construction.

At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials.  During the site grading operations, samples of 
the on-site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our 
laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The on-site fill and backfill materials generally consisted of on-site soils 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a 
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  Due to the high natural moisture content of 
the subgrade, the compaction efforts of the bottom of culvert slab did not meet the 
specified requirement of 95 percent relative compaction (see Test Nos. 23 and 24); 
however, exceeding 90 percent relative compaction.  The representative from Central 
Federal Lands approved the lower relative compaction with the addition of geogrid 
placed below the bedding and backfill aggregate, and above the required geotextile 
fabric. Impervious backfill material below the weepholes required by the project special 
provisions were moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and 
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. 
Import borrow material from Kauai Aggregates was used as general fill 
throughout the site. The imported borrow material was moisture-conditioned to near the 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction. Base Course Aggregate material imported from Kauai Aggregates used 
below the culvert, wingwalls, approach slabs, and roadways was moisture-conditioned 
to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent 
relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil 
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by 
ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) 
corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test 
results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.13.  Test locations and elevations were 
estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.  These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas 
that did not meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the 
required minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill 
and backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the 
above grading operations.
