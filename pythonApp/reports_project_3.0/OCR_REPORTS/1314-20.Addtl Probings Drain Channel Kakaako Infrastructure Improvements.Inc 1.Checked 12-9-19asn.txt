INCREMENT 1
The following presents the results of our additional
probings for the design of the proposed new rectangular open
channel section located downstream of Kelikoi Street in Kakaako
Oahu Hawaii This report supplements our previous soil
engineering investigation reports of February 3 and May 21
1984 The approximate location of the open channel section and
its vicinity is shown on tne Project Location Map Plate l
Our work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal of
W O 1314 20 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the planned rectangular
drainage channel will be 30 feet wide 15 feet deep and about
650 feet long located between the intersection of Kelikoi and
Keawe Streets and the ocean Its alignment will follow the
existing unlined open drainage channel The new channel
construction consists of driving vertical sheet piles which will
act as side wall members and placing a riprap layer for the
channel bottom lining
The inverts of the open channel range from 8 to 9 feet
below the Mean Sea Level
The eastern portion of the open channel will be adjacent
to a large stockpile consisting of various types of soil
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The three previous borings drilled for the drain channel
design encountered varying soil conditions The two lower
borings B 47 B 48 encountered coral formation near the
proposed invert level while the borings in the upper area B 44
and B 14 did not encounter the coral formation
The design embedment depth of the sheetpile and the
required construction procedure differs substantially in the
coral and non coral areas

The purpose of the additional probings is to delineate the
limits of the coral formation to determine the boundary between
the two systems Also it is desired to evaluate the elevation
of the top of coral formation in the lower areas for embedment
design of the sheet piles
Description of the materials encountered in the probings
are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
FIELD EXPLORATION
Eleven probings were drilled to depths of 20 to 33 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The probes were drilled using
auger and rotary drilling methods Samples were taken in some
of the probes to verify the material encountered
The Logs of the Probes are presented on Plates A 1 through
A ll
RESULTS OF PROBINGS
The probings indicate that the coral formation is
generally absent mauka northeast of Station 5 40 The
approximate delineation of the limit of the coral formation is
indicated on the Site Plan Plate 1

Based on the field exploration the top of the coral
formation in the area from Sta 0 to 4 50 varies from about 12 5
to 18 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The lateral earth pressures for the upper sand layer as
presented in the previous Geolabs Hawaii soil reports may be
used for a cantilever or tie back sheetpile wall design
It is our understanding that the channel walls will be
braced therefore a rectangular earth pressure distribution as
indicated on Plate 3 is recommended
For the hard coral formation a design lateral bearing
value of 2 000 p s f may be used

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report
are based on our site reconnaissance and soil information from
probing data obtained from this supplementary and the previous
soil investigations
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by probing Unforeseen conditions
such as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized
areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some
contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these
possible costs
