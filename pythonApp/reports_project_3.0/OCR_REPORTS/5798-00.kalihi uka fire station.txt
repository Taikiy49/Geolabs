SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our analyses for the design of new pavements
for the Kalihi Uka Fire Station project along Kamehameha IV Road located in Honolulu
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the design of
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The existing driveway and parking lot consist of asphaltic concrete pavement
which have experienced moderate to severe pavement distress consisting of raveling
and weathering alligator cracks depression and rutting The project involves removing
the existing asphaltic concrete pavements and replacing with new concrete pavements
to improve pavement performance We anticipate that the traffic at the project site
mainly consists of passenger vehicles with light pick up trucks and fire trucks
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a soil and or rock data set to formulate geotechnical
recommendations for the design of pavement section and site preparation In order to
accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and an
operator to the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings each extending to a depth of about
11 5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of approximately
34 5 lineal feet of exploration

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is located at Road within Kalihi Uka Fire
Station premises in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site served as
driveway and parking lot for the facility at the time of our field exploration
The project site is generally flat and paved with asphalt concrete Based on our
field observations moderate to severe pavement distress was present along the
existing driveway and parking lot
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of the drilling and sampling of three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 each extending to a depth of about 11 5 below
the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings generally encountered asphalt pavement sections
overlying fill consisting of soft to very stiff clay extending to about 4 5 to 7 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Below the fill soft gray expansive clay also known as
adobe clay was encountered extending to the maximum depth drilled of about
11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 3 at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater was observed in Boring No 2 at depths of about 5 0
and 5 75 feet below the existing ground surface It is our opinion that the groundwater
encountered might be perched groundwater It should be noted that groundwater levels
can vary significantly depending on rainfall temperature surface runoff groundwater
withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered from our field exploration are
presented in Appendix A Laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples
encountered and the results are presented in Appendix B

Design Subgrade Conditions
We envision that the new pavements will be constructed near the existing grades
Based on our field exploration we anticipate the majority of the pavement subgrade
soils will generally consist of soft to very stiff clays for the new driveway and parking
lot
We have performed our pavement designs assuming that the pavement subgrade
soil will consist of the near surface clay soils encountered in our borings Based on
our previous engineering experience with such materials we assume that the
subgrade soils had a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 2 with an average
laboratory Resistance R Value of about 5
Based on our engineering experience with adobe clay we believe that the on site
clay soils may exhibit a moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore we recommend providing a minimum 18 inches of
aggregate subbase materials below the concrete slab Based on Figure 1 2 Effect
of Various Thicknesses of Aggregate Subbase and Untreated Permeable Base on
K Value presented in the revised Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002
prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division
Material Testing and Research Branch the K value of the subgrade and the
subbase combination is determined to be 130 pounds per cubic inch pci for this
project
If site grading exposes soils other than those assumed additional tests should be
performed to confirm and or revise the recommended pavement section for actual
field conditions
Design Pavement Section
As previously mentioned the on site clay soils generally exhibit a moderate to
high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore a minimum
18 inches of aggregate subbase material should be provided below the concrete slab

Preparation Below Pavement Section
We anticipate the majority of the new pavements will be constructed near the
existing grade The existing asphaltic concrete pavements will be demolished After
demolition the area within the contract grading limits should be cleared and grubbed
thoroughly at the on set of earthwork Vegetation construction debris deleterious
materials and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly
off site or disposed in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
below areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated
to expose stiff materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with aggregate
subbase materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed off site
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated
where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate
subbase course layer The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with AASHTO Test Method T 180
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation a layer of high
strength geotextile such as should be placed over the pumping
subgrade Geolabs should be contacted to evaluate the pumping subgrade conditions

3 5 Fill Materials and Compaction Requirements
lf required fill materials should consist of non expansive structural fill such as
crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10
and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or
less Imported fill materials if required should be tested by Geolabs for conformance
with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Aggregate subbase materials required for the new pavements should meet the
requirements of Section 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications
September 1986 The aggregate subbase should be moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to no
less than 95 percent of the maximum density

3 6 Pavement Drainage
The pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after construction To
reduce the potential for water infiltration into the pavement structural section we
recommend providing a pavement edge drain system along the sides of the new
pavement to intercept subterranean seepage from the upper hillside
The edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations
facing down surrounded by No 3B Fine gravel and wrapped around by a layer of
non woven filter fabric to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage
material Care should be exercised when placing the subbase and or construction of the
surface course to avoid damaging the edge drain collector pipe A typical edge drain
detail is provided on Plate 3 The edge drains should be daylighted into appropriate
drainage structures for proper discharge

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 each extending to a depth of about 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Log Legend Plate A Graphic representations of
the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test results are summarized
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
