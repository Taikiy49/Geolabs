INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary soils investigation
for the design of the proposed office building to be located at Wong
Ho Lane and South Beretania Street Honolulu Oahu Hawaii Tax Map
Key 22 The general vicinity of the project site is shown on
Plate 1 Project Location Map
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed building will be a 1ll story high reinforced concrete
structure consisting of 7 level of office on 5 levels of parking
structure including a basement level parking The office tower will
be approximately 49 by 95 feet in plan dimensions and parking structure
about 85 by 106 feet

SCOPE OF SERVICES
For the preliminary foundation investigation of the proposed develop
ment three 3 borings were drilled in truck accessible areas outside
the existing 2 story structure Boring locations were toned to avoid
damaging the existing underlying utilities Several underground
structures were detected and necessitated relocating Borings 2 and 3
more toward Wong Ho Lane The approximate locations of borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled 36 0 to 45 5 feet below the existing ground
surface to examine preliminary characteristics of the subsurface
materials for foundation support The drilling operation was directed
by one of our engineers at the site Disturbed and undisturbed soil
samples as well as cores of coral ledge were obtained for further
examination and testing in our laboratory Descriptions of our field
exploration log of borings and laboratory testing are presented in
the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located south of Beretania and west of Wong Ho
Lane It is presently occupied by 1 and 2 story concrete masonry
structures and a parking lot that fronts Wong Ho Lane
In general the site is flat and slopes down toward the south

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Soil Conditions
Our preliminary findings indicate that the site is mantled by stiff
brown clayey silt over medium dense volcanic cinder sand deposit
underlain by a cemented coral ledge to the depths explored
The surface mantle of stiff brown silt is 3 to 5 feet thick and
contains some weathered cinder sand particles Tree roots may be
encountered near the original ground surface although the ground
surface is now covered by the existing building and asphalt concrete
pavement Several underground utilities and probably other structure
may be encountered
The volcanic cinder sand under the surface clayey silt is silty in top
2 to 3 feet zone that has been weathered and become brownish in color
The lower portion cinder is black and relatively free of silt and
fresh The cinder sand is only medium dense
The underlying coral ledge consists of moderately hard algal coral
with its cavities infilled with cemented calcareous silt and sand
The coral ledge is moderately hard near the top but cementation
decreases somewhat with depth and becomes softer A pocket of brown
cemented siltstone containing coral fragment was encountered near the
bottom of Boring 2 This may indicate an ancient erosion channel or
gully within the coral ledge

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicated competent bearing strata of coral
ledge just below the basement level at the site Shallow foundations
can also be considered for lightly loaded footings
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread footings for columns and continuous
footings for wall are recommended It is recommended that the pro
posed office tower be founded in coral ledge The footings should be
set at least 6 inches into the coral ledge An allowable bearing
value of 10 000 p s f may be used for main footings resting on the
coral ledge at a depth of about 13 to 16 feet below existing ground
Lightly loaded footings may be founded in the upper stiff silt or
medium dense brown silty sand and designed to an allowable bearing
pressure of 2500 p s f The footing should be embedded at least 2
feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade
It has been known that the underlying coral ledge had been incised by
old stream channels in the geological past throughout the Honolulu
areas Judging from an excavation across from Beretania Street to the
GEOLABS HAWAII

northwest of the project site it is our opinion that the coral ledge
will likely extend underneath the western half of the site where the
office tower is to be located However this condition should be
examined further by drilling additional borings under the tower area
Furthermore the coral ledge contains occasional large cavities
although they were not encountered in our borings Such a potential
condition should be examined before footing is constructed If any
cavity is detected it should be grouted It is recommended at least
two probes be made at each heavy column footings and one probe every
15 feet along major wall footing
The estimated maximum footing settlements would be about inches
total and 3 4 inch differential over a distance of 20 feet for the
office tower structure founded in coral ledge Settlements are also
estimated to be about inches total and 3 4 inch differential
over a distance of 20 feet for the garage footings founded in the
upper brown sandy silt and or silty sand
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the bottom of
the trenches

Basement Ret aining Walls
It is our understanding that the proposed structure with basement will
be built essentially on the property lines For basement excavation
it will be necessary to underpin the existing adjacent structures
The basement walls along the western boundary will be a primary
bearing wall and presumed to be rigid and non yielding Thus a
relatively high earth pressure will be imposed on non yielding walls
Other walls may also be restrained if concrete decking is poured
before backfilling Design earth pressures of 50 p s per foot of
depth may be used provided that the on site black cinder sand is used
as backfill A vertical drainage curtain would not be required in
such a case
Active earth pressures of 35 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
free standing unrestrained retaining walls These pressures are all
based on the assumption that the on site black cinder sand is used as
backfill Therefore it is recommended that the black cinder sand be
properly segregated without contamination and stockpiled for backfill
use
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be
used at the base of the foundations Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may also be used

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course on a
subgrade that consist of the onsite brown sandy silt or silty sand
The materials within the top 12 inches below the slab should be com
pacted to 95 of their respective maximum dry densities as determined
in accordance with the ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure The subgrade
should be kept moist and recompacted prior to placing the base course
For main driveway ramp and loading dock the concrete slab should be
at least 6 inches thick and for passenger car parking areas the con
crete slabs should be at least 5 inches thick The flexural strength
of concrete is assumed to be at least 650 p s i at 28 days
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
For the basement and footing construction a substantial amount of
excavation will be required All excavation should be properly
shored especially those along property boundary
The site should be cleared of all roots debris cesspools if any
and old foundations Select on site black cinder sand material should
be carefully segregated from other materials and stockpiled for use as
retaining wall backfill material The on site brown Silty sand may be
used as general fill and backfill above footings if required

The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations proof rolling and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing strata have been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation Modification may be needed if the actual conditions
are substantially different from those anticipated
Although not anticipated at this time any imported material required
should be non expansive tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
we om a
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 45 5 feet below the existing pier
deck surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the
borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer and or geologist who continously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed
by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classi fication System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Borings
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler 12 inches are shown on the log of
borings at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NWD4 series double tube core barrel The core recovery and
rock quality designation RQD are presented on the logs of
borings
