In accordance with your request we have performed additional
probings at the above referenced project in order to evaluate if the
previous underground storage tank excavation areas were properly
backfilled The exploration consisted of drilling three shallow borings
with close interval sampling at the previous underground storage tank
areas obtaining representative soil samples for visual evaluation and
the recording of blow counts for use to determine the consistency of the
backfill soils
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
SOTL CONDITIONS
The borings drilled at the locations of the previous underground
storage tank excavations primarily encountered medium stiff to stiff
clayey silt with gravel and sands with loose zones encountered locally
The backfill soil is probably an off site borrow

SUMMARY OF FINDINGS
The borings indicate that the backfill was moderately compacted but
should not significantly impact the proposed new A C pavements planned
During construction this area should be proofrolled with a heavy roller
to provide additional compaction of the upper portion of the fill
Based on the above findings and anticipated construction we believe
that the project sites can be developed as planned
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that subgrade preparations footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by the soil engineer during
construction to see that adequate compactions have been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our boring
exploration
