_ INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil
investigation for the proposed off site Sewage Pump
Station for the Nawiliwili Coral Fill Industrial
Subdivision located in A
previous soil investigation Soil Investigation Off
Site Sewer and Pump Station A Nawiliwili Kauai
Hawaii W by Geolabs
Hawaii disclosed difficulties in placing sheeting
and excavating the existing boulder fill and rock
To reduce construction difficulty it was recommended
that the sewer invert be raised necessitating a relo
cation of the sewage pumping station The purpose of
this investigation was to provide soil data for the
design and construction cost estimate of the relocated
Sewage Pump Station
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of a complete sewage
pump station facility within the project site The
facility consists of a wet well and pumping station
approximately 14 by 24 feet in plan and extending about
25 feet below the ground surface a generator building
12 by 14 feet in plan at ground level and an A C parking
area
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located near the northwest
corner of Nawiliwili Harbor adjacent to Waapa Road
The site is bounded to the south by Waapa Road to the
north by a steep basaltic rock cliff and to the east
and west by undeveloped land similar in character to the
project site
The site generally covered by dense trees and
shrubs presently has a cleared 10 to 15 foot wide trail
from the southeast corner at Waapa Road to the location
of the pump station structure Numerous basalt boulders
cover the mauka north half of the site up to the toe
of the cliff Except for the mauka cliff the site is
generally of low relief rising gently from an elevation
of 7 feet at Waapa Road to 12 feet at the toe of the
cliff Presently there is a utility anchor pole and
guy wire located near the center of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site sits at the foot of a near vertical
sea cliff the face of which has been partially covered by
alluvium and talus The sea cliff was formed by wave ero
sion during a period when the sea level was as much as 100
feet below the existing sea level Subsequently the area
Surrounding the cliff was covered by a combination of marine
sands and non marine clays and silts as a result of sea
level flucuations
The rocky cliff consists of a series of lava flows
from the most recent volcanic activity on Kauai As a
result the basaltic rock is still fairly fresh and
unweathered Because the basalt was fractured and jointed
When it originally cooled the dense rock face of the cliff
breaks easily into small boulder size fragments which accu
mulate at the foot of the cliff singly or sometimes as
small rock slides Presently at the project site the
older alluvium is covered by approximately 20 feet of
talus boulders The boulders tend to be dense and
unweathered at the surface becoming more weathered
and decomposed at depth as descriped in the Soil Con
dition section

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Two test borings were drilled at the site to a
depth of 26 5 feet to explore the subsurface soil con
ditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration
and laboratory testing is presented in the Appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
During the clearing of a trail into the site
numerous 10 to 12 inch loose boulders were encountered
on the surface and down to a depth of about 2 feet The
test borings encountered nearly continuous dense boulders
to a depth of 5 feet Boring No 1 encountered occasional
boulders and cobbles in a clayey silt matrix to a depth
of about 12 feet and a single dense 8 to 10 inch boulder
at 7 5 feet Below the loose cobbles and boulders is a
dense layer of highly weathered and decomposed boulders to
a depth of about 18 feet Below this to the extent of
the borings a very stiff to hard brown clayey silt was
encountered
Groundwater levels were noted to be at approximately
elevation 2 feet in the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The pump chamber and wet well will extend about
25 feet below the existing ground surface probably
bearing on the very stiff clayey silt
Sheeting and bracing and dewatering will be
required for the construction of the pumping station
Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should
be left to the contractor Based on the borings most
of the excavation will be in boulderly material and
partially in very stiff clayey silt near the bottom
It will be difficult to drive sheeting through
the boulder material Trenching through the boulders
may be required to place the sheeting Bracing of the
sheets will be required to prevent cave in of the excava
tion walls
Another alternative would be to slope back the
excavation side walls where there is sufficient area
For the temporary excavation conditions a side slope
of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used in the compact
boulder material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Pie
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept
away from the tops of the excavation
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered above the proposed
invert level throughout the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to
the contractor provided adequate stability of the
excavation is maintained
The deposits encountered in the borings consisted
mostly of fill with a soil matrix
The project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out the soil fines pumping clear water
only
The dewatering should be coordinated with the
excavation support system so that the stability of the
excavation is not jeopardized The operations should
be carried out without creating a quick condition
or softening the bottom of excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P i7
Backfill
Select granular material should be used as back
fi11 for the sewer line trench The on site and boulder
fill material generally less than 6 inches in size can
be utilized for the backfill
The backfill should be compacted to a minimum of
90 per cent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 The space left by the removal of the
sheeting should also be backfilled Proper compaction
of the trench backfill material will reduce future settle
ments of the backfill and the adjacent ground surface
Pumping Station Foundation
For the proposed structure an allowable bearing
value of 3000 p s f is recommended for foundations
bearing on the very stiff clayey silt layer
Minor settlements are anticipated due to the
pump station structure
Generator Building
For the light one story building spread footing
foundations or slab on grade can be used
The vegetation should be removed and the existing
subsoils recompacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of its
maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f can be
used on the recompacted surface soils
The perimeter footings should extend a minimum of
2 feet below the finish grade
Pavement
Based on the laboratory CBR test and the very light
anticipated traffic the following pavement section is
recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of
90 per cent of its maximum dry density
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Field inspection and engineering observation during
the pump station construction are important to permit
correlation between the preliminary soil data obtained
during this investigation and the actual soil conditions
encountered during construction
Backfilling operations should be inspected by a soils
engineer and density tests should be taken to verify that
adequate compaction density has been obtained

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 2 borings using a portable trailer mounted
hydraulic drill with 4 inch continuous flight auger
Coring was done with an NX core barrel and diamond
DT The borings were drilled to a depth of 26 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were reviewed by visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified
in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
