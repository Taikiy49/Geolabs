PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the Matson Terminal along Piers 51 through 53 on 
Sand Island in the Honolulu Harbor.  We understand that the proposed infrastructure 
upgrades will include modifications to the existing crane girders, installing tie-downs to 
secure the new and existing cranes for storm wind loads, replacing the crane electrical 
power distribution system at Piers 52 and 53, installing a crane electrical power 
distribution system at Pier 51C, and installing a new/replacement terminal power supply 
equipment in the yard. A total of 22 micropiles with an allowable tensile load capacity of 
150 kips were designed to provide the necessary uplift load resistance at the tie-downs 
of the proposed crane stowage locations and support of the new cable anchor vaults 
planned for the project. As part of the construction requirements, one pre-production 
micropile was installed and tested to evaluate the adequacy of the foundation support 
system. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the design of the 
micropile foundation, and our recommendations are presented in the referenced reports 
and subsequent modifications during the project construction. The purpose of our 
geotechnical engineering observation during construction is to provide engineering 
consultation during the construction of the micropile foundations and to provide “Special 
Inspection” services (as required by the City and County of Honolulu) by observing the 
micropile foundation installation and testing at the project site for general conformance 
with the project plans, specifications, and our recommendations. 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. (HDCC) is the general contractor 
and also conducted the micropile installation and testing for the project. 
MICROPILE INSTALLATION 
In general, the contractor installed a total of twenty-two (22) production 
micropiles, designated as MP-1 through MP-22, from April 25 through July 12, 2018 at 
the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Location Plans, Plates 2.1 
through 2.5. Details of the micropile installation are presented in the Micropile 
Installation Records in Appendix A and also summarized on Plate 3. 
In general, the micropiles were installed using either a KLEMM KR-806D 
track-mounted drilling rig equipped with a 6-inch diameter down the hole hammer 
advancing a 7⅝-inch Outside Diameter (OD) steel casing to the specified depth of the 
micropile to effectively create a drilled hole of at least 7⅝ inches in diameter. Advancing 
steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth was required in order to facilitate the 
micropile drilling and ensure the quality of the grouting. Air pressure was utilized during 
the drilling operation to drive down the hole hammer drilling bit and advance the steel 
casing.

After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out, a 
2.25-inch nominal diameter (No. 18), Grade 75 all threaded reinforcing rebar equipped 
with 7-inch PVC centralizers every 10 feet, were lowered into the cased hole. 
Grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal high-shear and high-speed 
ChemGrout CG-500 mixer equipped with a grout pump. The grout mix consisted of 
one sack of 94-pound Portland Cement Types I and/or II and 5 gallons of water with a 
28-day design compressive strength of 4,000 pounds per square inch (psi). 
The grout was introduced into the cased hole starting from the bottom through a 
1-inch diameter PVC tremie pipe. After sound grout appeared at the top of the cased 
hole, the upper 6 to 9 sections (6.5-foot per section) of temporary steel casings were 
withdrawn one at a time to create a grout bulb of about 39 to 61 feet long within the 
competent coralline materials. The grout drawdown was monitored and filled to the top 
of the casing with grout maintaining a positive grout intake throughout the entire 
grouting operation. Upon removal of the upper steel casings, the remaining casings 
were generally lifted up about 3 feet and plunged back about 3 feet into the competent 
coralline materials and left in-place as permanent casings.  
It should be noted that two micropiles, designated as MP-5 and MP-6, were 
abandoned due to obstruction encountered during the drilling operation. These 
micropiles were replaced by micropiles designated as MP-5R1 and MP-6R1 as 
illustrated on Plate 2.2. 
MICROPILE TESTING 
To evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles, proof testing 
was conducted on five selected production micropiles (MP-2, MP-9, MP-14, MP-20 and 
MP-21) from July 26 through August 14, 2018 at the approximate locations shown on 
Plates 2.1 through 2.5. In general, the micropiles were gradually loaded to the maximum 
test load of about 225 kips (150 percent of the design load) in about 38-kip load 
increments. The micropiles were held at each load increment for 4 minutes and held at 
the maximum test load for 10 minutes to evaluate creep effects. At the maximum test 
load, the creep movement during the holding period between 1 and 10 minutes was in 
general, less than 0.04 inches (typically allowed for the ground anchor system) for the 
tested micropiles. Details of the proof tests are presented on Plates 4.1 through 8.2 and 
are summarized in the table below.
