The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site to provide data for use in design and construction
of the building foundations The work was performed gener
ally in accordance with our proposal of
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
apppendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A

testing are presented in the Appendices
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the eastern corner of Kilauea
Avenue and Aala Lane in downtown Hilo Hawaii
The lot is trapezoidal in shape and has a frontage of about
190 feet along the southern boundary The lot is about 430
feet deep from Kilauea Avenue
The site is bounded by Kilauea Avenue on the north Aala Lane
on the west an existing parking lot on the south and resi
dences on the east

The existing Tire Warehouse structure is located at the nor

structure the site had been cleared of the previous wooden
residences situated around the property
Two gravel roads cross the project The first one extends
in a north south direction from Kilauea Avenue The second

SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a sur
face layer from 0 to 11 feet thick of clayey silt gravel
and cobbles over a relatively thin lava flow approximately
0 to 10 feet thick Based on the borings it appears that
the thickness of this thin upper lava flow decreases toward
the north This upper flow was not encountered in the nor
thern corner of the lot near the existing Tire Warehouse
Instead a surface fill layer 4 to 6 5 feet thick was encoun
tered here A more definite delineation of the fill area
could not be made since the existing warehouse structure re
stricted access for the drilling equipment
Below the thin upper lava flow a layer of volcanic ash
approximately 7 to 11 feet thick was encountered beginning
at about elevation 20 feet
Another lava flow was encountered below the volcanic ash
layer Weathered rock and clinker was encountered at the
top portion of this rock formation Dense basalt rock was
encountered at lower depths down to 44 feet the maximum
depth drilled

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed relatively complex soil
rock conditions below the project site In general a lava
rock formation was encountered at depths of 0 to 11 feet be
low most of the site However this upper rock formation
thins out toward the north and was not encountered in the
northern corner of the site where the existing Tire Ware
house operation is located This upper rock formation is
underlain by a 7 to 11 foot thick layer of volcanic ash which
is further underlain by another lava rock formation
For the proposed 2 story parking structure shallow spread
footing foundation can be used bearing on the near surface
rock formation
At the southern half of the proposed 9 story office building
the upper rock formation was found to be about 5 to 10 feet
thick therefore spread footing foundation can also be used
At the northern half of the office building the upper rock
formation was not encountered To reduce differential settle
ments the foundation for this portion of the building should
extend through the volcanic ash layer down to the lower lava
rock formation encountered at 15 to 17 foot depth To avoid
deep spread footing excavation caisson foundation are recom
mended for this half of the building

Spread Footing Foundation
Spread footing foundation bearing on a minimum of 5 feet
of rock can be designed using an allowable bearing pressure

of 10 000 p s f The bearing values are for total of dead

porary lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
The footings should extend a minimum of 24 inches below

Settlements are anticipated to be minimum for the above

Caisson Foundation
Where the upper rock formation is too thin less than 5 feet
thick or not encountered near the ground surface caisson
foundation extending down to the lower rock formation en
countered at about 15 to 17 foot depth are recommended
The caissons should be designed for an allowable bearing
value of 10 000 p s f Normally a higher bearing value
could be used on dense rock formation however the borings
indicate that the upper portion of the lower rock layer con
sists of weathered rock and clinker therefore a somewhat
lower bearing value is recommended
In order to maximize the bearing area and capacity of each
caisson a belled bottom could be utilized
The bottom of the caisson should be kept relatively clear
of loose material The concrete should be placed as soon

as practicable to reduce the possibility of the upper mater
ial caving in the hole prior to concrete placement Should
excessive cave in occur a temporary casing may be required
Although large cavities were not noted in the borings a
few small cavities were encountered
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to pos
sible large cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava
rock every major footing location should be probed The
probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
should extend below the bottom of footing a depth equal
to the footing width or a minimum of 5 feet The probe
holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using
low pressures
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly and to locate any poten
tial voids near the footing excavations

Retaining and Basement Walls
Retaining and basement walls up to 10 feet high are planned
for the parking structure
The wall foundations should bear on the near surface rock
formation An allowable bearing value of 10 000 p s f is
recommended for dense rock formation
Well graded granular material such as black sand cinder
or clinker should be used as backfill material behind the
walls Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet
of the backfill
Por lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations The necessary inspections include the observa
tion of the site grubbing fill placement thickness quality
of the excavated fill materials and the verification that
sufficient passes have been performed to obtain the required
density on each fill lift
It is also recommended that the footing and caisson excava
tions be observed by a soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by dril
to depths 6 5 to 44 feet deep utilizing truck
mounted drilling equipment The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan and Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the soils encount
ered in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 15
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
50 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash NX
core barrel The core recovery and rock quality designation
RQD are presented on the boring logs
