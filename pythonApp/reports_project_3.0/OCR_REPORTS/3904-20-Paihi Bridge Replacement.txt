Project Considerations
The Paihi Bridge project site is at Milepost 44 95 in the Kipahulu area in the District
of Hana on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project generally involves the demolition of the
existing bridge and the construction of a longer single span bridge with two abutments
The foundation system for each abutment consisted of two 36 inch drilled shafts
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Paihi Bridge Replacement project only
Global Specialty Contractors Inc was the general and Delima s Drilling LLC was the
drilled shaft installation contractor for the project
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of shallow fills
overlying a weathered basalt rock formation with intermittent clinker layers The surface
material consisted of about 4 to 8 inches of asphalt pavement over a 6 inch thick base
coarse material
At Abutment 1 from a depth of about 1 to 4 feet below the existing ground
surface shallow fill material consisting of silty gravel and sand with cobbles and boulders
was encountered From 4 feet extending to 38 feet maximum depth of the drilled shafts
the material generally consisted of clinker material silty gravel and basaltic fragments
with two rock layers at 4 to 6 feet and 14 to 18 feet
At Abutment 2 from a depth of about 1 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface shallow fill material consisting of silty gravel and sand with cobbles and boulders
was encountered From 6 feet weathered rock formation material was encountered that
graded to fresh basalt formation with increase in depth down to the redesigned shaft tip
elevation
Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements a sacrificial load test shaft was constructed and
tested Following completion of the load test the drilled shaft lengths for the project were
verified based on the favorable results of the instrumented load tests The following
subsections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and discussions on the
production drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shaft
As part of the project specifications a sacrificial 36 inch diameter load test shaft
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the Site Plan Plate 1 The drilled
shaft contractor excavated the load test shaft on July 7 and The excavation
process involved a Texoma 600 drill rig with the use of a 36 inch core barrel with
J 3 carbide teeth The load test shaft was excavated from the asphalt surface down to the

Comments Observations
During the excavation process for Shaft and DS 4 at Abutment 2 very
hard competent rock was encountered from about 10 feet below the existing ground
surface This very hard rock material became nearly uncutable as the depth progressed
with an average excavation progress of 5 inches per day Due to the competency of the
very hard rock formation the design capacity of the drilled shafts was achieved within
shaft lengths of 10 5 to 11 feet Therefore the shaft lengths for Shaft and DS 4
were reduced from 30 feet to 11 0 feet for Shaft No DS 3 and from 30 feet to 10 5 feet for
Shaft No DS 4 Since the rebar cage was already fabricated prior to Shaft and
DS 4 excavation the rebar cages were cut with the use of a Target Saw to the appropriate
length within the reduced length of the drilled shaft
Based on our field representative s observations it is our professional opinion that
the production drilled shafts supporting the project were installed in general conformance
with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical
recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report dated
In addition it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have
been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design load capacities
