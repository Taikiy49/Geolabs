Due to the accessibility difficulties involved in drilling in the heavily vegetated area
to the east of the comfort station parking lot our field crew drilled at the east end of the
parking lot near the proposed construction site The proposed construction site is
located on the other side of the chain link fence at the east end of the parking lot The
monitoring well was installed near the centerline of the stall as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 and the Site Plan Plate 2
After drilling of the boring for the monitoring well we were notified that this area
was actually under the jurisdiction of the County of Maui as the land was dedicated to the
County of Maui by Makena Resort Corp
Typically the monitoring well used to take the initial water sample for the NPDES
Construction Dewatering Permit Application is kept in working condition until the
construction requiring dewatering is complete During construction water samples are
taken to monitor the water quality of the discharge We are therefore requesting that this
monitoring well be allowed to remain in place until construction requiring dewatering is
complete
Details showing the construction and proposed abandonment of the monitoring
well are shown on the Monitoring Well Detail Plate 3 and on the Abandonment of
Monitoring Well Detail Plate 4

In general the construction of the monitoring well involved
1
Drilling of one boring to a depth of about 26 feet below the existing grade
The diameter of the hole ranges from about 9 to 12 inches from the surface
to about 1 foot below the existing grade to about 6 inches from about
1 foot to 5 feet below the existing grade to about 3 inches from about 5 to
26 feet below the existing grade
Installation of a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe to the final depth of the boring
The upper 5 feet consists of a solid PVC pipe while the lower PVC pipe is
perforated
Filling around the PVC pipe annulus made between ground and PVC with
No 3 silica filter sand followed by hydrated bentonite seal on site material
then concrete
Prior to placement of concrete a flush mount metal cover with a tamper
resistant locking cap was placed flush with the existing A C pavement
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work however Geolabs Hawaii will offer the following procedure in the specifications for
the contractor to properly abandon the monitoring well The specifications will also
require the contractor to coordinate with the proper agencies of Maui County so that they
may monitor the abandonment process For the abandonment of the monitoring well we
propose the following procedure
1
4
5
Remove the flush mounted well cover this may be accomplished by cutting
around the cover with a jack hammer
Remove the general backfill and hydrated bentonite seal to 3 feet below the
existing grade
Break and remove the upper 3 feet of PVC pipe
Grout within the PVC pipe
Grout the remaining 3 foot deep excavation
Due to the centerline location of the monitoring well we do not anticipate that
significant wheel loads will be imposed on the well Also due to the small size of the hole
we do not anticipate that significant settlement will occur However upon notice from
