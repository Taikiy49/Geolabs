Dear Mr Smith
In accordance with our proposal dated May 16 2014 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program for the
proposed agricultural subdivision at Dillingham Ranch in the Mokuleia area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and
percolation testing at the proposed leach field site and presents our discussion and
evaluation for the proposed project These findings and discussions are intended for
preliminary planning only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed agricultural subdivision project is at the foot of the north facing
slopes of the Waianae Mountain Range in Mokuleia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We
understand the project would involve the development of about 92 large agricultural lots
on gently sloping terrain along the base of the mountains
As part of the development the proposed sewage disposal system for the
agricultural lots will consist of septic tanks at each individual lot The sewage effluent at
each lot will be conveyed to a large leach field located down slope of the lots Based on
the preliminary information provided we understand that the leach field will be located in
the area northwest of the intersection between Cane Haul Road and the main ranch road
This area is bounded by Cane Haul Road on the south side the main ranch road on the
east side and the drainage ditch on the west side

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating 24 test pits designated as
Test Pit Nos 1 through 24 to depths ranging from about 2 5 to 13 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the test pits excavated in the central and west areas of the study area
encountered dark grayish brown clay extending to depths of about 8 to 13 feet below the
existing ground surface We did not encounter coralline deposit at the west and central
areas of the study area within the maximum excavated depths of the test pits
The test pits Test Pit 8 through 12 14 through 16 and 18 through 20
excavated at the east end of the study area generally encountered dark grayish brown
clay extending to depths of about 1 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface Below
the clay coralline deposit was encountered extending to the maximum depths excavated
of about 2 5 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the information obtained from the test pit excavations the coralline
deposit is concentrated at the east end of the study area The area where coralline
deposit was encountered appears to be triangular in shape The area where coralline
deposit was encountered is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change
due to rainfall time of year seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Test Pits in Appendix A

Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by hand
drilling 4 0 inch diameter boreholes to depths ranging from about 4 to 5 8 feet below the
existing ground surface
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel The time
intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the
percolation tests were based
