PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It was desired to further investigate Class A slopes with shared ownership that
have not been evaluated nor are currently being evaluated by a City rockfall mitigation
design contract Sixteen 16 Class A slope sites were selected for the field staking
survey to determine the actual property boundary location and where the potential
falling rock hazard resides with respect to the actual property boundary location on the
slope face
The intent is to provide supplemental survey and geological hazard evaluation
information to assist in the evaluation for future City rockfall mitigation projects where the
potential rockfall hazard is documented to reside with City property Among sixteen
selected slopes five Class A slope sites were designated as high priority for field
investigation as Stage 1 as listed below

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our additional geotechnical engineering evaluation is to provide
updated property boundary and potential rockfall hazard information for five selected
Class A slopes In order to accomplish the objective an evaluation program generally
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts was performed
1
Research and field staking of the City property boundary on the slope face
by ControlPoint Surveying
2
Site visits by our geologist and project manager following field staking to
evaluate the existing potential rockfall hazard conditions with respect to
the field property boundary designated on the slope
3
Coordination of our overall work by our project manager
4
Preparation of this report to present our findings
5
Quality assurance of our work and client consultation by our principal
engineer
6
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SITE RECONNAISSANCE
Field reconnaissance was conducted by our geologist and engineer to review the
recent property boundary staking with respect to potential rockfall hazard conditions The
reconnaissance consisted of visual observation of the surveyor s field staking at the five
selected Class A slope sites and general evaluation of the existing potential rockfall
hazard condition both above and below the staked property boundary The Stage 1
reconnaissance was performed on March 7 through 11 2014
The surveyor placed field stakes at approximately 20 foot intervals across the
slope The boundary survey staking also included the setting of nails or iron pipes as
appropriate at each of the staked locations Site Plans reflecting the staking for the five
slope sites were provided by the surveyor and are presented in Appendix A

LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from visual site observations only Variation in the surface and
subsurface conditions between our observation points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until actual construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings and
recommendations provided herein
It should be noted that slopes composed of rock materials rock slopes deteriorate
with the passage of time due to natural weathering processes wet dry and hot cold
cycles and erosion conditions Due to the inherent deterioration of rock slopes resulting
from natural processes weathering wet dry and hot cold cycles and erosion conditions
the potential for rockfall hazard at any site changes with the passage of time Therefore
the findings and recommendations contained in this letter report may be used only within a
reasonable time from the date of issuance of this letter report Land use site conditions
and or other factors may change with the passage of time Therefore additional work to
evaluate the applicability of the findings and preliminary recommendations contained in
this letter report due to changes with the passage of time will be required There are no
guarantees in the professional engineering and architectural design fields with respect to
potential rockfall hazards due in large part to the unpredictable nature of rockfall activity
which is affected by many external variables including natural and man induced causes
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of City and County of Honolulu
Department of Design and Construction in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
Any party other than the client who wishes to use this report shall notify Geolabs Inc in
writing of their intended use
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of evaluating and assisting the
client owner in the understanding of potential rockfall hazards located within the project
study area Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper
information to serve as the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates A
contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional
site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated surface and subsurface
conditions are encountered commonly Unforeseen subsurface conditions such as
perched groundwater soft deposits hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas
and may require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in
construction delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient
contingency fund is recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
