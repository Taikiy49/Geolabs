INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Inn at Schofield Barracks project in the Wahiawa District of
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained through field exploration and laboratory
testing
These recommendations are intended for design input only and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located inside the Schofield Barracks Military Reservation and
northwest of Wheeler Air Force Base at the central portion of the island of Oahu The
proposed project site is situated on the northwest corner of the intersection of Kolekole
Avenue and Humphreys Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the proposed development at the site will consist of
construction of several one story and two story structures with associated parking areas
The proposed development will consist of a lodging facility a restaurant and a
maintenance building The proposed lodging facility will consist of approximately six 6
long rectangular structures wrapped around a central courtyard or parking area as
shown on Plate 2 We understand the structures will be of masonry and or concrete
construction supported by thickened edge slab on grade foundations We anticipate the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3078 00 Page 3
structural loads to be relatively light with wall loads on the order of 3 to 4 kips per lineal
foot
The finish floor elevations of the proposed structures range from approximately
877 feet to 882 feet Mean Sea Level MSL We anticipate the site grading to consist
of minor cuts and fills on the order of up to three feet 3 to provide level building pads
for construction of the structures We understand that asphalt concrete paved parking
areas and access driveways will be used for the development Further details regarding
the proposed construction were not available at the time this report was prepared

subsurface conditions at the project site and to develop an idealized subsurface data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation
design and site grading for the proposed development

the existing ground surface

Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths of 21 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the near surface clayey
silt soils encountered in our borings generally exhibit low to moderate expansion potential
when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content As a result of the moderate
expansion potential special attention should be given to the site preparation and grading
of the site to reduce the moderate swelling potential of the near surface soils
The near surface materials encountered were very stiff to hard and are generally
suitable for foundation support of the proposed development Slab on grade foundations
may be used to support the proposed structures Where the proposed structures will be
located over the cut fill transition over excavation of the cut areas and compaction of the
soils to the requirements of structural fill are recommended to provide more uniform
support for the slab on grade foundations
Recommendations addressing the above geotechnical considerations and other
geotechnical aspects of the proposed development are discussed in the following
sections of this report
Site Grading
The finish floor elevations of the proposed structures vary between 877 feet and
882 feet MSL Based on these finish floor elevations only minor cuts and fills of up to
three feet 3 will be required to provide level building pads for construction of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3078 00 Page 7
proposed structures The following recommendations are presented to provide general
guidelines for the site grading work
1 The area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
2 In areas to receive fill the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of
eight 8 inches moisture conditioned to between two percent 2 and
five percent 6 above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures
3 In the cut areas in order to provide more uniform support for the slab on
grade foundations the cut areas should be over excavated by 12 inches
below the design subgrade level and replaced with structural fill The
requirement for over excavation in the cut areas is also to reduce the
potential for differential settlements of the slab on grade foundations due to
bearing on the very stiff to hard in situ soils and the compacted fills
4 Generally the on site clayey silt soils may be reused as fill material provided
they are properly moisture conditioned and compacted as recommended
herein All fill and structural fill materials should be placed in level loose lifts
not exceeding eight inches 8 in thickness moisture conditioned to
between two percent 2 and five percent 5 above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3078 00 Page 8
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures
described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
If needed imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular material less than three inches 8 in maximum size with less than
20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be
tested and approved for its intended use by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site
5 Measures should be incorporated into the grading of the project site to
reduce the potential for erosion and to prevent concentration of water
around structures The ground surface around structures should be sloped
away from structures
Slab on Grade Foundations
In consideration of the generally very stiff to hard subsoil conditions encountered
at the site we believe that slab on grade foundations may be used to support the
proposed structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500 pounds per square
foot p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the in situ materials
encountered near the surface or on structural fills Tne bearing pressure provided is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by up to one third 1 for transient loads such
as wind or seismic forces The thickened edge footings should be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade A minimum footing wicth of 12 inches is also
recommended
The bottom of the footing excavations should be firm and unyielding If soft and or
loose soils are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they should be over
excavated to expose competent in situ material and replaced with select granular material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3078 00 Page 9
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Compaction of the bottom
of footing excavations 90 percent relative compaction may be necessary if the bearing
materials have been disturbed
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressures exerted
against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the base of
foundations slabs and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure equal to an
equivalent fluid weighing 400 pounds per cubic foot p c f and a base friction factor of
0 35 may be used for design Unless covered by slabs or pavements passive soil
resistance in the upper 12 inches should be neglected
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate
total settlements to be less than one inch 1 with differential settlements of less than
Y inch
It is critical that footing excavations not be allowed to dry before placing concrete
If shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the footing excavations should be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks prior to placement of concrete A representative
of Geolabs Hawaii should observe the finished footing excavations prior to placement of
reinforcement and concrete to evaluate the bearing soils and the embedment depths
Concrete Floor Slabs
As previously indicated the on site materials exhibit low to moderate expansion
potential when subjected to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for
slab distress due to the moderately expansive materials we recommend that the slab
subgrade be compacted to 90 percent relative compaction between two percent 2 and
five percent 5 above the optimum moisture content
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3078 00 Page 10
Subgrade soils should be kept moist and should not be allowed to dry prior to
placement of cushion fill and slab concrete If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrade
the subgrade should be scarified moisture conditioned to between two percent 2 and
five percent 5 above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 percent
relative compaction
A minimum six inch 6 thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should be placed over the prepared subgrade soils to serve as a slab cushion
and capillary break A moisture barrier overlying the cushion fill should be incorporated
below the concrete slab to reduce the potential of damage to moisture sensitive floor
coverings by infiltration of moisture It is advisable to place two inches 2 of moist fine
sand over the moisture barrier membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in curing
of concrete
Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements for the parking areas and access driveways are
anticipated for this development We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles and delivery trucks Therefore we have assumed
medium traffic loading conditions for design purposes We have made our preliminary
pavement designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will consist of the near surface
soils encountered in our borings with a California Bearing Ratio CBR of 24 Based on
the above assumptions we recommend the following pavement sections be used for
preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist
Compacted Structural Fill

The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of eight inches 8 moisture
conditioned to between two percent 2 and five percent 5 above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The
base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95
percent relative compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping
in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of two
inches 2 into the subgrade soil below the baserock to reduce migration of landscape
water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed
to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to
about one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site clayey silt soils
The backfill should be placed in maximum eight inch 8 level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction between two percent 2 and
GEOLABS HAWAII

five percent 5 above the optimum moisture content to reduce the potential for future
ground subsidence

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration
Moisture content and unit weight determinations are used as an aid to soil
Classification and to aid in evaluating engineering properties The moisture content
expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the tested sample and unit weight are
shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Atterberg Limits test ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to evaluate the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The plasticity index of the material is used
to classify the soil in accordance with the Unified Classification System The results of the
Atterberg Limits test are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths and
are summarized on Plate B 1
An unconfined compression test was performed to evaluate the unconfined
compressive strength of the near surface soils The results of the test are summarized
on Plate B 1
Expansion tests are usually performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils
These tests aid in evaluating the swell potential of the near surface materials for support
of foundation and slabs on grade Three 3 one inch 1 ring swell tests were performed
on relatively undisturbed and remolded samples of the materials encountered Results
of the swell tests are summarized on Plate B 2
