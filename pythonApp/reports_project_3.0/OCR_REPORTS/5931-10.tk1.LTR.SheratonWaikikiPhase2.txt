As requested Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the micropile foundation installation for the Sheraton Waikiki
Phase 2 Hotels Master Plan Renovation project in the Waikiki area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations
performed during the micropile installation for the above project
Geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the micropile foundations were
presented in our geotechnical engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Hotels Master Plan Renovation Sheraton Waikiki and Royal Hawaiian
Waikiki Oahu Hawaii dated The purpose of our geotechnical
engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the micropile foundation
installation for general conformance with our recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing Sheraton Waikiki SW along the makai side of
Kalakaua Avenue in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
bounded approximately by the Royal Hawaiian Shopping Center and Kalakaua Avenue to
the north an existing development to the east Waikiki Beach to the south and existing
development and Lewers Street to the west The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Due to the anticipated poor subsurface conditions at the site we recommended
supporting new structures planned for the hotel renovation project such as the grand
staircase on a micropile foundation system In general the cased micropile foundation
system consists of a small diameter usually less than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings drilled we believe

evaluate the adequacy of the foundation support system
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
As previously indicated the original proposed micropiles consisted of a 7 5 inch OD
micropile extending to a depth of about 79 feet below the ground surface Because of
material availability and scheduling issues a smaller 5 5 inch OD micropile extending to
95 feet deep was proposed to achieve the same design load capacity Due to the
favorable results from the subsequent micropile load test as discussed below the smaller
diameter inch OD was reduced in length from the previously suggested
95 feet to about 80 to 90 feet
The production micropiles installed at the site consisted of the cased micropile
system The components of the cased micropile system include an unbonded length
permanent steel casing a bonded length at the bottom to provide vertical load resistance
primarily from friction between the grout and surrounding soils and a steel reinforcement
bar extending the full depth of the drilled hole
The micropiles were installed by drilling 5 5 inch OD steel casing with a Beretta T59
track mounted drill rig equipped with a down the hole hammer drill bit The steel casing
was extended down to provide a minimum bond length of 60 to 70 feet in the coralline
detritus deposit and or coral formation The steel casing was left in the drilled hole and
cleaned out by blowing air through the drill rods After the drilled hole was cleaned out a
single galvanized all thread steel reinforcement bar Grade 75 with a diameter of 1 25
inches was inserted into the drilled hole down to near the bottom of the hole The
micropile was grouted by tremie methods until relatively clean grout was observed at the
top of the steel casing The grout consisted of a sand cement mixture The steel casing
was extracted to provide a minimum bond length of 60 to 70 feet

Micropile Proof Testing
In accordance with the project documents two production micropiles were
subjected to proof tests In general the proof tests consisted of loading the micropiles in
tension to a maximum load of 90 kips which is 150 percent of the allowable compressive
load capacity of 60 kips The associated movements of the micropile head at each load
increment up to the maximum load were measured and recorded by Geolabs Inc
and 22 were subjected to proof testing In general the maximum
load was held for a minimum of 10 minutes and the creep movement between 1 and
10 minutes were less than 0 04 inches In addition the maximum micropile head
movements were approximately 0 118 and 0 236 inches Test data and results from the
two mircopile proof tests are presented on Plates 6 1 through 6 4 for reference
Based on our field observations and the proof load tests performed we believe that
the micropiles for the project have been installed in general conformance with the project
documents and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on
our observations of the micropile installation it is our opinion that the micropiles have been
installed to the sufficient diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity
