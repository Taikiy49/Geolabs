INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration for the subject portion of the South Kohala Resort
project This report presents our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soils and rock conditions along the proposed access road
alignments in order to provide data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of proposed road
grading and infrastructure installation

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The South Kohala Resort project site is located in the Ouli section
of the District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii and encompasses
approximately 485 acres The site is divided into two areas by the Queen
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 D Page 3
Kaahumanu Highway The mauka area is bordered to the north by Waiulaula
Gulch to the south and east by raw undeveloped land and to west by Queen
Kaahumanu Highway The makai area is bordered to north by the golf course
of the existing Mauna Kea Beach Hotel to the south by Hapuna State Beach
Park to the west by the northern part of Hapuna Bay and to the east by
Queen Kaahumanu Highway
The terrain is generally rolling with some steep slopes locally
approaching a ratio of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V The
terrain typically reflects the original surface of thick basaltic lava
flows in the A a form The ground surface elevations range from about sea
level to approximately 600 feet above sea level with drainage trending
towards the west
The site is vegetated with a thin cover of dry grasses with kiawe
algaroba trees occurring in the lower areas near Hapuna Bay and in
drainageways
Most of the ground surface is covered with basaltic cobbles and
gravel mantling an ashy soil There are localized exposures of basalt
clinker and rock formation
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the South Kohala Resort project site into a
planned resort residential and recreational development The resort
portion of the development is currently planned to be a hotel facility in
the makai portion of the site on a low bluff overlooking Hapuna Bay The
remainder of the makai area is planned as golf course
It is proposed to develop the mauka area primarily as residential and
golf course with other recreational facilities and maintenance support
within the area

As part of the development it is proposed to grade and construct
approximately three and a half miles of access road through the site It
is anticipated that the road construction will also involve the
installation of underground utilities along the road alignments
Based upon the topography of the site we estimate that road grading
will involve cuts of up to about twenty 20 feet deep and fills of up to
about twenty 20 feet thick
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua
Volcanic Series of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene
Stearns and Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which
are mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil at the project site differs from
Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the
in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists ina dry
friable state
The soils at the site have been subjected to wind erosion which has
resulted in the formation of a desert pavement on the surface of the
site This desert pavement is a surficial layer of cobbles and gravel
which were left behind as the finer ash was blown away
At and in the area of the project site the lava flows are in the Ala
form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which
frequently has a thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the
massive core The clinker surface of the lavas at the site have been
mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 D Page 5
Visual observations of exposures of existing road cuts indicate that
the clinker layers may be as much as twenty feet thick with discontinuous
ledges of hard basalt interbedded within the clinker
In parts of the main drainageways of the site the ash soil and
clinker have been stripped by erosion exposing the underlying dense rock

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analyses the development of the
proposed access roads is feasible from a geotechnical engineering
point of view The dense clinker and competent basalt formation
encountered at the site should provide adequate bearing support for the
proposed roads site excavations fill embankment and infrastructure
construction With the exception of the ash soil which should be
stockpiled for use as topsoil excavated volcanic materials should provide
a suitable source of fill material if reduced to less than 12 inches in
size
Road Grading
In general it is our opinion that conventional rock grading methods
may be used The customary grading practices on the western side of the
island of Hawaii are usually modified somewhat due to the rocky nature of
the material
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the roadway and
right of way grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be
disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
Ash soils generally have relatively low strength and if the in situ
moisture contents are high enough they are thixotropic i e they lose
strength when remolded Therefore it is our recommendation that the ash
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 D Page 7
soil be removed from fill roadway and structural areas The ash soil may
be either stockpiled for use as topsoil or thoroughly blended into
rockfill material as discussed below
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture
conditioning of fill material will be necessary It is also advisable to
institute a dust control program during grading operations
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below
any areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm
natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with well
compacted engineered fill consisting of well graded 12 inch minus size
rocky materials
In general the excavated soil and rock materials less than 12
inches in size may be suitable as fill material The material if
reduced to less than six 6 inches in size should be adequate as a
source of trench and structural backfill if needed Imported material
if required should conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 30
of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Select
Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
Consideration should be given to mobilizing a crusher to the site to
aid in reducing the size of large boulders and rock fragments It is
possible that portions of the dense basalt rock ledges could yield
aggregate quality material if properly segregated and crushed
Alternately large boulders or fragments ranging from 18 to 24
inches in size could be buried in the deepest portions of the roadway
embankment fills provided that they are not nested and or an adequate
choking layer of at least eighteen 18 inches of 6 inch minus material is
used to cap the rockfill to reduce the potential of loss of fines from the
upper portion of the fill Care must be exercised to avoid placement of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 D Page 8
boulders in locations which would conflict with future utility lines In
general boulder or rock fragments larger than 12 inches in size should be
placed at depths greater that four 4 feet below finish subgrade level
The excavated rock material may not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material therefore the surficial ash soil can be
mixed into the rocky excavated material to increase the percentage of
fines and to create a better graded fill material
Generally embankment fills required should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimm of 90 maximm dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Trench and structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding nine 9 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two 2
feet of fills and backfills below road subgrade level should be compacted
to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills
composed of excavated volcanic material Instead a testing program to
determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction should be conducted at the start of the
grading phase of the project On the basis of this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate
compaction Generally five 5 to seven 7 passes of a heavy compactor
running over the surface of each loose rockfill lift should considered
adequate
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1
horizontal to vertical should be properly keyed and benched into the
underlying consolidated clinker or basalt formation to lessen potential
instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The keys and benches should
generally not be based in the upper ash soil

Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in
its vicinity indicate that the site is most likely underlain by dense
clinker with ledges of massive dense basalt at relatively shallow depths
It is anticipated that most of the ash and clinker materials may be
excavated by conventional dozer ripping Some hard excavation and or
ripping may be encountered in well cemented areas or when large lava balls
boulders or ledges are encountered
The underlying basalt formation will most likely require hard
excavation such as heavy ripping hoerams or possibly blasting
Generally the excavation method should be left to the contractor
Blasting should be considered to expedite the excavation process
However care should be exercised to avoid overshooting which could
disrupt the clinker or rock formation resulting in a loss of strength

embankment fill slopes of two horizontal to ome vertical 2H 1V are

Infrastructure Installation
It is anticipated that underground utilities will be installed as
part of the road development work Based on the results of our test pit
excavations it is anticipated that relatively hard excavation will be
required for utility line trenching
In general the subsurface materials encountered at the site are
relatively competent and would require minimal shoring However areas of
loose soil or clinker may be encountered Trench shoring design and
installation should be left to the contractor However trench shoring
and bracing should conform to the appropriate OSHA or other health and
safety requirements
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding about
nine 9 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two 2
feet of backfills below road subgrade level should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 maximum dry density
Foundation Support
It is anticipated that some form of drainage structures may be needed
as part of the road development e g bridge culverts It is our opinion
that relatively shallow spread or strip footing foundations may be used to
support the structures It is anticipated that foundations will bear on
either in situ dense basalt rock formation on rockfill or on compacted
clinker or rock surface
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 5 000 p s f may be used for
the design of foundations bearing on in situ basalt rock formation or
dense rockfill clinker in level areas Lower allowable bearing capacity
of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for the design of foundations located in
sloping areas with the footings to be set back away a minimum of ten 10
feet away from the finish slope faces These values may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 D Page 11
In order to provide uniform support for the design drainage structure
foundations and to minimize the potential of foundation distresses
resulting from voids which may exist near the footing subgrades we
recommend that footing excavations be probed with a track drill to a
minimm of four 4 foot below subgrade level The cavity probe hole
drilling should be monitored by the project geotechnical engineer Where
cavities are suspected in certain footing locations and depending on the
findings by additional probe hole drilling within the area cement sand
grout may be used to backfill the suspected cavity or void zones
Provision for such grouting backfill should be provided in the contract
bid documents for use if necessary
Foundations should be embedded a minimm of two 2 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Foundations located on sloping areas
should have a minimum set back of ten 10 feet from the outside edge of
the footing to the adjacent slope face This may require additional
footing embedment to achieve the above specified proper set back distance
lateral Pressures
Lateral loads on the proposed structures may be resisted by a passive
resistance of up to 300 p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical
foundation faces A frictional coefficient of 0 45 may be used to
calculate resistance to sliding

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during grading construction
and the excavation of foundation phases of the work This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
geophysical surveys and test pits Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by triangulation and measurement from reference points shown on the South
Kohala Resort Master Plan prepared by Belt Collins and Associates
Incorporated The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used

Contractor s and Geotechnical Engineer s Responsibilities
1
The Geotechnical Engineer s services shall be provided by
the Owner For the purpose of these specifications
observation by Geotechnical Engineer includes that
monitoring and testing performed by any person or persons
employed by and responsible to the licensed Civil Engineer
retained as the Geotechnical Engineer
The words monitoring or observing shall mean periodic
observation of the work and the taking of soil tests as
deemed necessary by the Geotechnical Engineer for
substantial compliance with plans specifications and
design concepts
The presence of the Geotechnical Engineer will be for the
purpose of providing observation and field testing to
provide the Owner a greater degree of confidence that the
earthwork conforms to the contract documents The service
does not include supervision or direction of the actual work
of the Contractor his employees or agents
The Contractor should also be informed that neither the
presence of the Geotechnical Engineer nor the observation
and testing shall excuse him in any way for defects
discovered in his work The Geotechnical Engineer will not
be responsible for job or site safety on this project Job
and the site safety will be the sole responsibility of the
Contractor

II Special Considerations
A
Cut slopes shall be excavated to the lines and grades indicated
on the contract drawings and where necessary adjusted in
accordance with the recommendations of the Geotechnical Engineer
during construction
All trench backfill placed above 12 inches of the top of the
pipes shall be compacted in maximm lift thickness of 9 inches
unless specifically authorized by the Geotechnical Engineer
The Geotechnical Engineer may require thinner lifts should
compaction operations consistently fail to produce the specified
compaction requirements In any event the required levels of
compaction shall be obtained throughout the full depth and width
of each layer of backfill
Jetting and ponding of the backfill or fill materials shall
not be permitted on this project unless specifically authorized
by the Geotechnical Engineer in writing
Upon completion of grading or temporary interruption of grading
and the subsequent termination of observation by the
Geotechnical Engineer no further filling or excavating shall be
performed without notifying the Geotechnical Engineer in
advance and ascertaining his requirements

III Site Preparation
A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be
disposed of in accordance with Section 10 CLEARING AND
GRUBBING of the Standard Public Works Specifications
All vegetation and deleterious materials encountered within the
Grading Limits shall be cleared and disposed of off site This
removal shall be completed prior to excavating and filling
Organically contaminated topsoil and ash soil shall be stripped
prior to actual grading Materials determined as being
unsuitable for placement in compacted fill shall be removed and
wasted off site Any material incorporated as a part of the
compacted fill must be approved by the Geotechnical Engineer
Any abandoned underground structures such as cesspools
cisterns tunnels septic tanks wells pipelines or others not
located prior to grading are to be removed and the resulting
depression backfilled and compacted in accordance with these
specifications
Loose surface soils encountered in areas to receive fill shall
be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be observed and approved by the Geotechnical
Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

IV Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks or
lumps in excess of twelve inches 12 in diameter
Representative samples of the materials to be used as compacted fill
shall be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the
proposed materials shall be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least
ten working days prior to its intended jobsite delivery

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed eighteen inches 18 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
Rock fragments or boulders larger than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Boulders ranging in size from 18 to 24 inches encountered in
excavations may be used in the deeper fills provided that the
following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
GEOLABS HAWAII
Appendix B Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 4 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and
number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
Manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and

cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by

Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in

obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted access
to the fill to perform the compaction tests
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous

operations
The Contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it
will be free draining and will not pond surface water

VI Unforeseen Subsurface Conditions
A If any conditions not described in the contract documents such
as perched water seepage lenticular or confined strata of a
potentially adverse nature are encountered during grading these
conditions shall be immediately brought to the attention of the
Project Engineers so that supplemental recommendations may he
made to treat these problems
B Should excavations encounter loose or unstable conditions the
Contractor shall notify the Geotechnical Engineer and Project
Civil Engineer immediately so that supplemental recommendations
can be given
