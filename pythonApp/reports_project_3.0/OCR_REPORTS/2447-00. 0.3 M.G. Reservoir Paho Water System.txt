PROJECT CONSTIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new capacity water
reservoir in Pahoa Puna District on the island of Hawaii to supplement
the existing Pahoa water system
It is our understanding that the new reservoir will be a reinforced
concrete tank structure approximately 18 feet in height and about 60 feet
in diameter An asphaltic concrete A C paved access road will connect
the reservoir to the existing Pahoa Road An A C driveway is also
planned around the reservoir
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Pahoa Puna District on the island of
Hawaii It is situated approximately 2 300 feet southwest from the
intersection of Keaau Pahoa Road and Pahoa By Pass Road
The site slopes gently to the east at a gradient of about 8 The
existing ground elevations range from about 723 feet western portion to
714 feet eastern portion above Mean Sea Level MSL
The overall site is undeveloped land covered with trees and bushes on
a thin layer of humus The reservoir site had been cleared of vegetation
during our field exploration

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
Arilling and sampling of three 3 borings to a depth of 11 0 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a thin layer of organic silt
humus less than two 2 foot thick overlying in situ basaltic lava
rock formation The volcanic rock formation extended to the maximum depth
explored of 11 0 feet below the existing ground surface
The basaltic rock formation encountered at the project site is in the
pahoehoe form as characterized by the ropy hummocky surface of the rock
Pahoehoe lava is prone to having subsurface voids or cavities which were
created when the lava flow was deposited Voids were encountered at
shallow depths in Boring Nos 1 and 3
Groundwater was not encountered during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Reservoir Foundation
Based on the site grading plan minimal grading is anticipated
therefore the finished reservoir pad level will be on or close to the
excavated basaltic rock formation It is our opinion that shallow spread
or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the proposed
reservoir structure
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 8 000 p s f may be utilized
for the design of footing foundations bearing on in situ rock formation
This bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration wind and seismic
load considerations

Foundations bearing on in situ rock should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are
estimated to be less than one quarter 1 4 of an inch total and
one eighth 1 8 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed reservoir
structure may be provided by passive soil pressure and frictional
resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 500 p s f
per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive soil pressure developed
against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundations and a
frictional coefficient of 0 5 be used to calculate the frictional
resistance developed between the footing bottoms and the rock formation
Foundation Probing
Our field exploration encountered voids or cavities in Boring Nos 1
and 3 The basaltic rock encountered at the site is pahoehoe which is the
form of lava flow most prone to contain tubes voids and cavities
In order to reduce the potential of loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of a void below a foundation we recommend that a
program of cavity probing be implemented The probes should be drilled at
each individual footing location and on ten 10 foot centers around the
circumference of the exterior ring footing foundation The probes should
be a maximum of three 3 inches in diameter and should be drilled toa
depth of 10 feet below the bottom of footing
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of cavities the
probes should be filled with neat cement grout injected at low to moderate
pump pressures i e the pressure available from a grout pump If the
volume of grout injected exceeds four 4 times the calculated volume of
the probe hole the grout mix should be adjusted to a one 1 part sand to
one 1 part cement 1 1 mixture

Site Grading
Minimal site yrading is anticipated for the gently sloping project
terrain Based on the latest site grading plan the earthwork for the
project will involve up to three feet of excavation and one 1 foot of
fill to create a level pad for the reservoir foundation Up to two feet of
fill will be required for the access road to the reservoir area
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
The thin surface organic soil layer should be removed below the tank
and roadway areas
It is our opinion that excavation in the hard rock formation
encountered at the site may require hard ripping or hoerams Care must be
exercised to avoid over ripping which would disrupt the structure of the
rock formation resulting in a potential loss of bearing strength
The excavated rock material if reduced to less than 6 inches in
size will be suitable as fill material If imported fill material is
required the material should be a select granular material less than 6
inches in maximum size with less than 15 passing the No 200 sieve
Fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding 12
inches in loose thickness and then compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report _
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by scaling
from the site and grading plan dated provided by Okahara
Associates Inc and also by taping from the project site comers staked
out by the project surveyor Elevations of the borings were determined by
interpolation from elevation contours on the plan The physical locations
and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or
cross section are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to a depth of 11 0 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification Systen A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Rock samples were obtained from the borings by using NQ wireline
core barrels advancing diamond bits The percentage of sample recovered
from each core run is shown at the appropriate depth on the Boring Logs
REC The rock quality determination RQD is also on the Boring Logs
