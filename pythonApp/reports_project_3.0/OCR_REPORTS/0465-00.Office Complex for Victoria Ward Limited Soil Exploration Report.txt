SCOPE OF EXPLORATION
The purpose of this exploration was to determine general soil conditions
for foundation design purposes for the proposed Office Complex for
Victoria Ward Ltd Ala Moana Boulevard Ward Avenue and Auahi Street
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report includes field explorations laboratory tests general
recommendations for building foundation design and limitations
FIELD EXPLORATION AND LABORATORY TESTS
Five borings and nine continuous penetration tests probings were made
at the site at locations shown on the Boring Location Sketch
Borings were made with 6 in diameter augers using finger type bits and
carbide bits Soil samples were recovered with 2 in diameter split
spoon samplers driven with a 140 lb hammer falling 30 inches
Continuous penetration tests probings were made by using a 2 in
diameter blunt point attached to A rods and driven with a 140 lb hammer
falling 30 inches
LJ
Laboratory tests included natural water caqntent Atterberg Limit and
grain size analysis
A summary of the laboratory test results is given in Tables IA thru IC
SOIL CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
Soil samples were visually observed and subjected to appropriate tests
in the laboratory Based on visual observations and laboratory tests
the soil descriptions given on the boring logs are generally made in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System

Foundations
The boring logs indicate that underlying the surface crust
is soft clayey sand with coral fragments from about 5 ft to
a depth of about 25 to 38 ft where a dense sand and coral
stratum was noted to depths of about 40 to 65 ft
Because the semi basement will cut thru the surface crust
and rest on the soft layer of clayey sand and coral pile
foundations are recommended
The piles should extend thru the soft layer and bear in
the dense sand and coral deposit which is about 25 to
38 t from the surface
With pile foundations the building willbe supported
on structural members that are relatively stiff while
the surrounding ground is relatively compressible
Differential settlements will occur where structural
members are partly supported on piles and partly on
the ground
Pile foundations will usually minimize or reduce
structural settlements However even with pile
foundations some differential settlements may occur
should soft deposits underlie the tips of the piles
The differential gettlements anticipated for pile
supported elements will probably be minor
LJ

l

penetrate about 2 to 5 ft into the bearing
stratum of dense sand and coral
The piles should be driven with a hammer

Uplift or Buoyancy at
Because the semi basement floor will be at elevation 2
and the ground water table will vary with rainfall and tidal
fluctuations to about elevation 3 the semi basement structure
should be designed to resist uplift The dead load of the
structure should be of sufficient weight to resist hydrostatic
pressure Friction from the soils acting on the basement walls
and the piles may be considered as an added factor of safety
_ The estimated uplift capacity of the piles due to friction
is about 5000 ibs for a 30 ft pile The estimated friction
of the soil around the perimeter of the basement wall is
about 200 p s f
A pressure relief system should ve considered to keep the
ground water level from rising above a selected elevation
say 2 or 3 ft around the perimeter of the building The
pressure relief system may be a perforated pipe drain
surrounded by ped gravel and drained by gravity into a sump
ox manhole Check valves or sump pits may be used as part
of the pressure relief system

Driveway and Parking Areas

Lateral Pressures Against Basement Walls
Subdrains should be placed behind basement walls that extend
below the outside ground elevation
Fairly well graded granular material or select granular
material should be used for backfilling against the wall

below the ground water table should be used for the wall
design

LIMITATIONS
In general soil formations are commonly erratic and rarely uniform or
regular The boring logs indicate the approximate subsurface soil
conditions encountered only at the drill holes where the borings were
made at the times designated on the logs and may not represent conditions
at other locations or at other dates Soil conditions and water levels
tay change with the passage of time and construction methods or improve
ments at the site
During construction should subsurface conditions much different from
those in the borings be observed encountered or otherwise indicated
we should be advised immediately to review or reconsider our recommen
dations in light of the new developments
If there is a substantial lapse of time between the submission of this
report and the start of work at the site or if conditions have changed
due to natural causes plan changes or construction operations at or
adjacent to the site it is recommended that this report be reviewed to
determine the applicability of the recommendations considering the time
lapse and the changed conditions
