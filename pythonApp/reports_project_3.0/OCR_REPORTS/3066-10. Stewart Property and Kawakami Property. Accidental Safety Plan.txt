Introduction and Scope
This Accident Prevention Plan is designed for the specific requirements and peculiarities
of subsurface drilling and sampling for exploration purposes The plan establishes a
safety program applicable to the activities of Geo Services Inc during the
performance of Contract Number DACA83 93 P 0263 Subsurface Drilling and Sampling
for PME Complex Project Hickam Air Force Base Oahu Hawaii
Purpose
This plan establishes a recognized uniform set of rules and procedure to prevent injury
or death to employees and visitors and to prevent damage to equipment and existing
facilities The information contained in this safety program will be used by Geo Services
Inc to insure that all personnel on site are aware of and comply with the safety precaution
as related to this project
Policy
It is the policy of Geo Services Inc that it will not allow or require an employee to work
in surrounding or under conditions which are unsanitary hazardous or otherwise
dangerous to health or safety unsafe tools or equipment and machinery Geo Services
will have continual on site enforcement of its safety program é and regular safety instruction
for all job site personnel

SAFETY TRAINING
Geo Services Inc s supervisors and drillers will constantly acquaint and instruct their
personnel regarding job safety It is a firm policy that a portion of each Project Safety
Meeting will be devoted to safety indoctrination This will insure that the personnel
involved will be familiar with the Accident Prevention Plan safety requirements and the
contribution they are to make for an accident free job
Supervisors will hold tool box safety meetings at starting time every Monday to instruct
workmen and to emphasize safety consciousness Meeting records will be maintained
at the company s office
All field employees and supervisors will be trained in first aid and CPR
Supervisors shall provide each employee with initial indoctrination on safe drilling
techniques and continued training as will enable him to perform his work in a safe
manner Indoctrination shall be accomplished prior to any employee beginning work on
the jobsite

SAFETY GUIDELINES FOR DRILLING INTO SOIL AND ROCK
Purpose The purpose of this operating procedure is to provide guidelines for the safe
conduct of drilling operations with truck mounted and other engine powered drill rigs The
procedure addresses off road movement of drill rigs overhead and buried utilities use
of augers rotary and core drilling and other drilling operations and activities The
procedures and guidelines established herein meet the U S Army Corps of Engineers
safety and health requirements as required in the Safety and Health Requirements
Manual EM dated October 1992
Application These guidelines shall be applied for all Geo Services Inc projects in which
truck mounted or other engine powered drill rigs are used The guidelines are applicable
to all Geo Services Inc employees as well as employees of firms subcontracted by Geo
Services Inc to operate drill rigs
Responsibility and Authority Drill rig safety and maintenance is the responsibility of the
drill rig operator Drill rig safety and maintenance shall be in accordance with EM
SAFETY GUIDELINES
Off Road Movement of Drill Rigs Before moving the rig the operator must do the
following
To the extent practical walk the planned route and inspect it for depressions
gullies ruts and other obstacles
Ensure that reverse signal alarms are functioning
Check the brakes of the truck carrier especially if the terrain along the route of
travel is rough or sloped
Discharge all passengers before moving onto steep or rough terrain
Engage the front axle on vehicles before travelling rough or steep
terrain
ACCIDENT PREVENTION PLAN Page 6
GEO SERVICES INC
Driving drill rigs along the sides of hills should be avoided however if side hill travel
becomes necessary the operator must conservatively evaluate the ability of the rig to
remain upright while on the hill Rigs will be equipped with seatbelts The possibility that
the presence of drilling tools on the rig may reduce the ability if the rigs to remain upright
by raising the center of gravity of the rig must be considered
Logs ditches road curbs and other long horizontal obstacles should normally be
approached and driven over squarely not at an angle
When close lateral or overhead clearance is encountered the driver of the rig should be
guided by another person on the ground
Loads on the drill rig and truck must be tied down while the truck is moving and the mast
must be fully in the lowered position
After the rig has been positioned to begin drilling all brakes and or locks must be set
before drilling begins If the rig is positioned on a steep grade and leveling of the ground
is impossible or impractical the wheel of the transport vehicle should be blocked and
other means of preventing the rig from moving or topping over employee
Buried and Overhead Utilities
The location of overhead and buried utility lines particularly electric must be determined
before drilling begins and their locations must be noted on all boring plans and
assignment sheets Although the Government s representative normally clears all utilities
it is the drillers responsibility to review utility plans and observe field locations of all utilities
with the Government s representative prior to beginning work This is always done during
the initial site safety meeting
When overhead power lines are close by the drill rig mast should not be raised unless
the distance between the rig and the nearest power line is in accordance with Corps
requirements or the power lines have been properly shielded or the power has been shut
off and precaution has been taken to prevent the lines from being energized The drill rig
operator or assistant should walk completely around the rig to make sure that proper
distance exists in accordance with the following criteria

When the rig is positioned near an overhead line the rig operator should be aware that
hoist lines and power lines can be moved toward each other by wind
Although gas lines natural gas or POL are relatively rare in Hawaii all gas lines will be
properly located by drawings and electronic toning and avoided during drilling
Clearing the Work Area
Before a drill rig is positioned to drill the area on which the rig is to be positioned should
be cleared of removable obstacles and leveled if sloped The cleared leveled area should
be large enough to accommodate the rig and supplies Clearance shall be in accordance
with Chapter 11 of EM
Safe Use of Augers
Never place hands or fingers under the bottom of an auger flight when hoisting the flight
over the top of another flight in the ground or other hard surfaces such as the drill rig
platform
Never allow feet to get under the auger flight while the flight is being hoisted
When an auger is rotating stay clear of the auger and other rotating components of the
drill rig Never reach behind or around a rotating auger for any reason
Move auger cuttings away from the auger with a long handled shovel or spade never use
hands or feet
Never clean an auger attached to the drill rig unless the engine is turned off and the
auger has stopped rotating
Safe Use of Drive Sample Hammer
Never hold hammer stem to support drill tools while drive sampling Use rope if
necessary
Never lift hammer without rope and cathead or power winch and wireline
Safe Use of Cathead
Never use cathead and rope when either or both are wet Suspend work during heavy
or moderate rain

Inspect cathead for nicks chipped areas cracks or other burrs which could snag the
rope Repair or replace defective cathead
Never use thin or frayed rope
Safe Use of Hand Tools
Each tool should be used only to perform task for which it was originally designed
Damaged tools should be repaired before using or replaced
Safety goggles or glasses should be worn when using a hammer and chisel Nearby
worker and bystanders should be required to wear safety goggles or glasses also
Tools should be kept clean and stored in an orderly manner when not in use
Safe Use of Wire Line Hoists Wire Rope and Hoisting Hardware
Safety rules and guidelines contained in the Wire Rope User s Manual published by the
American Iron and Steel Institute shall be used whenever wire line hoists wire rope or
hoisting hardware are used
