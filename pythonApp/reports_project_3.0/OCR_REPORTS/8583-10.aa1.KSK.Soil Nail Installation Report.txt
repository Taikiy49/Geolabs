SOIL-NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING

Dear Ms. Hayashi: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the pre-production sacrificial and production soil 
nails for the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Erosion Control Site 11 in the Vicinity of 
Kaha’opulani and Nae’ole Buildings project located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the soil 
nail installation and testing performed for the project from May 19 through July 12, 2023. 
Hi-Tech Rockfall Construction, Inc. was the specialty subcontractor responsible for the 
installation and testing of the soil nails for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located on the south side of Kaha’opulani and Nae’ole 
Buildings at the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama campus on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. Erosion and distress were observed near the top of the slope. The slope grades 
downwards to the existing homes along Makanani Drive.  
Based on our geotechnical exploration and engineering analysis, a soil-nailed 
slope system consisting of a series of individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled 
holes and covered with shotcrete to stabilize the slope was recommended for the 
project. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the 
construction and testing of the soil nails to confirm our design assumptions and that the 
contractor installed the soil nails in general conformance with the project plans and 
specifications.

SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
The soil-nailed slope system consisted of 180 individual reinforcing bars grouted 
into drilled holes to stabilize the existing slope. One additional sacrificial soil nail was 
installed for pre-production verification testing. 
The soil-nail installations, a verification test, and proof tests were performed by 
Hi-Tech Rockfall Construction, Inc. The approximate locations of the grouted soil nails are 
shown on the Soil Nail Location Plan, Plate 2. Installation and testing of the nails were 
conducted from May 19 through July 12, 2023. A summary of the soil nail installations is 
presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.6. 
Soil Nail Installation 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during the 
construction of the soil nails. The nails were drilled using an excavator equipped with a 
6-inch diameter pneumatic downhole hammer drill attachment. 
The nail holes were drilled to depths of 15 to 25 feet from the slope face with an 
inclination of about 15 degrees. After the hole was drilled to the specified depth, an epoxy 
coated Grade 75 No. 9 bar encapsulated within a corrugated plastic sheathing was 
inserted into the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along the nail at 5-foot intervals. 
The nails were grouted using a 1-inch diameter PVC tremie tube that was fully 
inserted into the drilled hole. The grouting of the anchors was terminated when fresh grout 
appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting tube was removed after the completion of the 
grouting operation. The grout was designed with a minimum 28-day compressive strength 
of 4,000 psi. 
Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, the pre-production verification soil nail 
was loaded to 150 percent of the design load (Design Load = 15 kips) and held for 
60 minutes. The nail was then loaded to 200 percent of the design load. Based on the 
project specifications, ten percent of the production nails (18 nails) were selected for proof 
testing. The production test nails had at least 4 feet of unbonded length for testing. Proof 
test nails were loaded to 150 percent of the design load, and the load was maintained for 
10 minutes to measure the creep movements. The criteria for acceptance of the soil nails 
consist of the following: 
For the verification test, a total creep movement of less than 0.08 inches 
per log cycle of the time between 6 and 60-minute readings is measured 
during creep testing, and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
For the proof tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.04 inches is 
measured between 1 and 10-minute readings or a total creep movement

In accordance with the project specifications, a verification test was performed on 
the pre-production sacrificial soil nail, designated as T-1, on May 22, 2023. Nail T-1 was 
loaded to the maximum test load of approximately 30 kips (200 percent of the design 
load). The head movement for the soil nail at each load increment up to the maximum 
test load was measured and recorded. After testing the soil nail to 200 percent of the 
design load, the test nail was unloaded in eight load decrements. The total measured 
head movement at the maximum test load was about 0.210 inches. Results of the 
verification test, including Load Versus Displacement and the Creep Movement Versus

Based on our observations, we believe that the soil nails observed by Geolabs 
have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and

LIMITATIONS

nail installation and testing may have occurred without our direct observation.  However, 
we believe that the level of geotechnical observation provided is reasonable in reducing 
the geotechnical risks to the project.
