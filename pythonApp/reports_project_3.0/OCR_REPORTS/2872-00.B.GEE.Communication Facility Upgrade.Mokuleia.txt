This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Communication Facility Upgrade in Mokuleia on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project Location Map

geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of data obtained during
the exploration These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input and

SITE AND SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on top of the Waianae Ridge in the Mokuleia Forest
Reserve in the Peacock Flats area above the Old Dillingham Ranch Mokuleia Homestead
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The associated property bears Tax Map Key 001
The site was covered with trees and bushes except in the communication tower
area at the time of our field exploration There were three 3 existing towers adjacent to
an existing equipment room at the project site Part of the project area was covered with
an asphaltic pavement
The subsurface materials at the site consisted of residual soil deposits of silty sand
in the top four 4 to eight 8 feet in Boring and 3 Underlying the silty sand
and from the surface in Boring No 4 saprolitic soils were encountered to a depth of 26 5
feet the maximum depth drilled The saprolitic soils originated from extremely weathered
basalt and retained the structure of the parent rock The material of which the saprolite
was composed of may be described as a plastic clayey silt with various amounts of sand
and gravel The silt was in a loose to medium dense condition according to the field
standard penetration tests However because of the high clay content the silt behaved
like a medium stiff to stiff cohesive soil

Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our exploration
However a temporary rise of ground water level is possible especially during periods of
heavy rain
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Tower Foundations
Based on the available subsurface information we recommend that a mat
foundation be used for the support of the proposed communication tower if a new one
is to be constructed The communication tower is relatively light in weight but will be
subjected to significant moment and tension force at the foundation level as indicated
above For the given loading conditions a fifteen 15 by fifteen 15 feet mat may be
used for the support of the tower The bottom of the mat foundation should be a
minimum of six 6 feet below the finished grade The design relies on the weight of soil
on top of the mat to offset the moment and uplifting forces
If it is decided that the fifty foot 50 high tower is to be obtained by extension of
an existing tower tie down anchors may be used to provide additional capacity against
uplift and moment reactions at the base of the tower
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling will be required for the construction of the tower
foundation The bottom of the excavations should be cleared of loose material prior to
the placement of concrete
The material resulting from excavation for the mat foundation may be used as
backfill The backfill snould be placed in eight inch 8 loose lifts and compacted to 95

Retaining Walls
A retaining wall approximately six 6 to eight 8 feet in height is proposed around
the existing radio equipment building The guidelines for design of the retaining wall are
as follows
1 The following lateral earth pressure values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 45 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 Allowable bearing pressure of 2000 p s f may be used for retaining wall
foundations bearing on the undisturbed in situ material
3 A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used for the
wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be reduced for walls
located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the wall on the undisturbed in situ material
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen inches 18 below the lowest adjacent finish grade

For sloping ground the footing should be lowered as required to obtain a
minimum eight foot 8 set back distance from the outside edge of the
footing measured horizontally out to the face of the slope
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls Subdrains should be constructed properly to drain water
away from the wall
7 Select granular backfill material such as 3 B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 aggregate is recommended behind the retaining wall to minimize
excessive compaction required and to aid in the drainage of the area behind
the wall The above granular backfill should have a minimum horizontal
thickness of twelve inches 12 from the back of the wall
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed facility upgrade should be forwarded to
the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
This review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be
responsible for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe that critical
items of work such as excavation and backfilling are properly performed This is to
identify possible deviations from the design concepts specifications or recommendations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different

modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from existing structures and or objects in the field The physical locations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
Data upon which to base determination of the surface elevation at the boring
locations was not available at the time of preparation of this report

Field Exploration

of four 4 borings to depths ranging between 16 5 and 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled by a truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing hollow stem continuous flight
augers or rotary core drilling tools as appropriate

ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
Identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs
of the brings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 4

Classification Tests

engineering properties of the material
