INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate subsurface
conditions to provide recommendations for the design and construction of
the proposed bowling center at Wheeler Air Force Base Wahiawa Oahu
Hawaii The approximate location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and reconmendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed bowling center measured approximately 132 by 72 feet
is a single story structure with CMU wall construction Maximum column
load is anticipated to be 30 to 40 kips
An asphalt concrete parking area is planned for the northern
portion of the site
Minor grading is anticipated for the site development
SCOPE OF SERVICE
The scope of work includes drilling and sampling five 5 borings
laboratory tests of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field
and laboratory data development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration log of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE CONDITION
The site is located at the eastern corner of the intersection of
Wright Avenue and Curtis Loop inside Wheeler Air Force Base

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface soil condition at the site was explored by drilling
and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging from 10 to 15 feet below
the existing ground surface The location of these borings are shown on
Site Plan Plate 2
The borings indicate that the site is underlain by stiff to very
stiff clayey silt down to at least the full depth of the borings

of Molokai and Lanai State of Hawaii August 1972

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicates that generally canpetent
foundation bearing soils are located beneath the proposed structure
The stiff residual soils below the foundation levels should provide
adequate support without excessive settlement The laboratory swell
tests indicated undisturbed in situ near surface soils appear to have low
expansion potential Slab on grade may be designed without special
provisions for expansive soil conditions
Foundation
We believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing
foundation bearing on the in situ stiff silty material can be used to
support the proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f may be used for the
spread or continuous wall footing bearing on stiff natural soil All
footings should be embedded a mininum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1547 00 Page 5
Settlement under the designed loading is expected to be small and
it is estimated that total and differential settlements would be less
than 1 2 and 1 4 of an inch respectively
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the spread or wall footing may be resisted
by passive earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of
the foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 250 p s f for level backfill his assumes
that the soil around the foundation is well campacted The frictional
resistance may be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying prior
to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1547 00 Page 6
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated within
floor slabs When the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is recommended to
relieve seepage water
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the driveway and
parking areas
Based on the results of laboratory CER tests on the compacted site
material and assuming a light traffic and drained subgrade conditions
the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation and pavement should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our reconmendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to assure that the subsurface

investigations
The structural fill under slabs and pavements should be tested to
assure that the proper degree of compaction has been achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
