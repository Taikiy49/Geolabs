PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Mililani district on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
bounded by Ukuwai Street to the northwest Ainamakau Drive to the northeast and
Meheula Parkway to the south and east The location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The development consists of the construction of Longs Drugs and three building
pads for the project site In addition asphaltic concrete parking areas and driveways will
be constructed The project also includes the widening of Ainamakua Drive New utility
lines and structures will be constructed The new utilties include water sewer and drain
lines The layout of the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Cuts and fills on the order of about 4 feet or less are required to achieve the design
grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading parking area and roadway construction and utility installation at the
project site

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The earthwork construction at the Mililani Mauka Commercial B Longs Drugs
Buildings A2 thru A4 and Ainamakua Drive Widening project consisted of site grading
parking area and roadway construction and utility installation The site grading parking
area and roadway construction and utility backfilling work were performed by Paradigm
Construction LLC During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The
compaction test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the parking and roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density In addition the base course materials below the pavement
areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 34 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Loose soft backfill conditions were encountered along Sewer Line SL A between
material was removed and replaced with compacted fill and Controlled Low Strength
Material CLSM
In addition loose soft subgrade and aggregate base course materials were paved
over in portions of the driveway areas at the western portion of the project site These
driveway areas were cut open and the subgrade and aggregate base course materials
were re compacted Subsequent to re compacting the subgrade and aggregate base
course the cut open areas were re paved
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
