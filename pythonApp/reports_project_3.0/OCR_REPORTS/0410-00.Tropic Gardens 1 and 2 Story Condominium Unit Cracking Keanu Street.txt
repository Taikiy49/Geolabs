_ This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
performed on the subject property The purpose of the investi
gation was to determine the nature of the soils underlying the
site to ascertain their engineering properties and to provide
recommendations for rectifying the existing cracking and reducing
the possibility of future additional cracking

EXPLORATION
The site wag explored on August 29 and 30 1972 by drilling
5 exploratory test borings with a mobile minuteman drilling
equipped with a rock bit and a hollow stem flight auger Several
of the borings as indicated on the logs required multiple
redrilling to advance the holes past cobbles and boulders The
borings varied in depth from 3 0 to 10 5 feet The boring loca
tions are shown on Plate 1 and the soils encountered are logged
on Plates Al through AS
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depth intervals not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration
Test consists of driving a 2 inch O D split spoon sampler
18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance
N and is an approximate measure of the relative density or

ENGINEERING DISCUSSION
The site problems appear to be caused by three major items
The underlying soils are highly expansive exhibit large volume
change with moisture change and with alternate wetting and
drying literally will move vertically To compound this problen
_ the site has very poor drainage around the buildings Drainage
should be away from the buildings but in many cases on the site
water literally drains toward the building and ponds
The third factor seriously affecting the site is the lack of
foundations The foundation appears to be simply an extension
of the slab with no effective footing For the site soils the
footings should be at least eighteen 18 inches below outside
grade and should be a minimum of 12 inches wide The existing
_ footings slab are at best several inches below exterior
grade and thus are extremely subject to volume change
vay
The only factor that can be modified economically is the drainage
A drainage plan should be prepared by a consulting firm which
specializes in that field This would involve diverting all
surface drainage away from the buildings and carrying this water
to the street
Roof gutters should be cleaned out and all water carried directly

clay Further investigation would be required for this method

Based upon an analysis of our field investigation and laboratory
test results the following recommendations and conclusions are
presented

mainly by the underlying expansive soils This problem has been
amplified by very poor drainage on the site and the lack of
