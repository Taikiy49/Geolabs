Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
In general the Halawa Corporation Yard site is generally underlain by a
near surface layer of fill materials overlying alluvial deposits and saprolitic materials at
greater depths The majority of the materials encountered consisted of stiff to very stiff
clayey silts and silty clays with varying amounts of cobbles and boulders throughout the
boring depths and in localized areas It should be noted that a significant amount of
cobbles and boulders were encountered in the borings drilled across the yard site
Groundwater was encountered in some of the drilled borings at depths ranging from
about 5 5 to 49 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our previous field
exploration
Boring No 19 which was drilled at the proposed location of the material testing
laboratory encountered highly expansive silty clays with cobbles and boulders extending
to a depth of approximately 11 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was
not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our previous field exploration
Descriptions and graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the drilled boring
are provided on the Boring Log Legend and Log of Boring Plates 3 and 4 Laboratory
ring swell test results conducted on a near surface sample of the clayey soils are
presented on Plate 5 of this report for reference
Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions anticipated at the Material Testing
Laboratory relocation site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footing
foundations may be used to support the proposed new building Due to the substantial
amount of cobbles and boulders encountered during our previous field exploration we
recommend that a 12 inch thick layer of compacted structural fill material be provided
below the bottom of footings for uniform support
Based on our previous field exploration and laboratory test data we anticipate
that the project site is generally underlain by near surface clayey soils with very high
expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling
of the highly expansive clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least
3 feet of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion

Building Foundations
Based on the soil boring data obtained from our previous field exploration
conducted at the Halawa Corporation Yard we anticipate that the proposed building site is
generally underlain by very stiff silty clays with varying amounts of embedded cobbles and
boulders below the existing pavement section In general we believe that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of
the proposed building planned at the project site An allowable bearing pressure of up to
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations of the new
building bearing on a minimum of 12 inches of structural fill This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
Based on our previous soil boring data at the proposed building site cobbles and
boulders may be encountered at or near the design footing subgrade elevations
Therefore we recommend that a 12 inch thick layer of compacted structural fill material be
provided below the bottom of footings for uniform support The 12 inch thick structural fill
generally would serve to improve the foundation bearing conditions and to reduce the
amount of differential settlement due to differing subgrade materials Over excavation for
the compacted structural fill should extend beyond the edges of the footings a minimum of
12 inches Prior to placement of the structural fill the bottom of the footing
over excavations should be compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of the over excavated subgrade
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
should be backfilled with structural fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
The compacted structural fill may consist of the excavated base course and
subbase material provided that they are not contaminated with the on site expansive
clayey soils The structural fill may also consist of imported select granular fill material
such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should also
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping

If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our

about 1 inch We believe that the differential settlements between adjacent foundations to
be on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected In addition the
passive pressure should also be reduced for foundations located on slopes
Based on the above discussion on foundation subgrade conditions we strongly
recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from Geolabs prior
to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on our previous field exploration and laboratory test data we anticipate that
the project site is generally underlain by near surface clayey soils with very high expansion
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the
highly expansive clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we recommend that
the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 3 feet of non expansive
select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion The capping fill should
also extend beyond the new building footprint a minimum of 2 feet Since cobbles and
boulders were encountered at various depths below the existing ground surface across the
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California

project site the 3 foot capping fill would also serve to provide more uniform support for the
building slabs on grade that might otherwise bear directly on the cobbles and boulders
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are properly prepared The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of slab concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be prepared again as recommended Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be
placed below the interior slabs to provide the slabs with more uniform support The
cushion fill should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer Flexible floor coverings
such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab
cracking
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation we
recommend that a 6 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction be provided in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer The
aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of
subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be
used for the compacted aggregate subbase Where slabs are intended to function as rigid
pavements a minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes In addition provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab
joints that will be subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to
unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab with the
on site soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water
ponding around the slabs

Utility Trench
We anticipate that new utility connections for the project will consist primarily of
water and sewer lines In general good construction practices should be utilized for the
installation and backfilling of the trench for the proposed utilities The method and
equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations should
comply with the applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring
design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor
Based on the results of our previous field exploration it appears that conventional
excavation methods may generally be utilized for utility line excavation at the site The
clayey soils encountered at the site are generally stiff to very stiff However some of the
utility trench excavations may encounter dense to very dense cobbles and boulders as
described in the Logs of Borings which may pose some excavation difficulties during the
trench excavation
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial trench backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to
reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical
that a free draining granular material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids
below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Use of the on site clayey soils as initial trench backfill immediately around the utility pipes
is not recommended
In general we recommend that a minimum cover of 3 feet be provided between the
top of the utility pipes and the finished grade to facilitate construction of the pavement

Drainage
Proper surface drainage is critical to the performance of foundations constructed on
expansive soil conditions Therefore the finished grades outside the structure should be
sloped to shed water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is
also advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should
also be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water
infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away
from slabs and foundations
