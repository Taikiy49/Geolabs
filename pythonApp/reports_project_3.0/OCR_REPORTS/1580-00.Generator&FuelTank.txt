PROJECT CONSIDERATION
Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard
Building 177 Switching Station D
The proposed location for the four 4 gas turbine generator units
is on the existing paved area west of Building 177 The two
KVA transformers will be constructed on pads located northwest of the
generators An above ground vertical steel fuel tank is also planned
adjacent to the generator site The tank will have an 80 000 gallon
holding capacity and it is anticipated to have a vertical height of 16
feet and a diameter of about 29 feet New reinforced concrete slab and
walls will be provided around the storage tank to contain future fuel
spillage Site preparation will consist of excavation and subgrade
preparation to receive new A C pavements a raised level platform for
skid mounted generator units and fuel tank slab foundations
Barbers Point Naval Air Station
Building 91 Switching Station P
One skid mounted 2 000 KW gas turbine generator unit will be
installed adjacent to Building 91 at the southwest corner of the
intersection of Midway and Mission Streets in Barbers Point Naval Air
Station The new fuel tank planned will be 21 feet in diameter about 8

SITE DESCRIPTION
Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard
The project site is located to the west of Building 177 at Pearl
Harbor Naval Shipyard on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The site was relatively level at the time of our field
exploration The proposed site for the installation of the fuel tank
was not paved However the surrounding areas are paved with asphalt
concrete The whole site appears to be vacant
Barbers Point Naval Air Station
The project site is located to the south of Building 91 across
Mission Street at Barbers Point Naval Air Station on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The site is triangular in shape with perimeter dimensions of
approximately 250 200 and 180 feet The site is level and at the time
ef our field exploration was used as a parking area Ground cover
consisted of coral sand and gravel with very little vegetation

The subsoil condition disclosed at B 1 in Pearl Harbor Naval

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Despite a variety cf construction considerations mentioned
previously under the section PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS this report
only covers the foundation design of the proposed fuel tanks
In general our field exploration disclosed competent subsoil for
the proposed construction The use of spread footing foundation is
recommended Settlement is expected to be minimal
Foundation Design
Very dense to hard sand and gravel were encountered at both
proposed locations at Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard and Barbers Point Naval
Air Station Therefore it is our opinion that spread footing foundation
bearing on the dense in situ sandy material can be used to support the
fuel tanks
An allowable bearing value of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for
the dense sand and gravel material The footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Under the design loading settlement is estimated to be on the
order of one half of an inch total and one fourth inch differential

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the bottom
of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety
Site Preparation for Footing Foundation
Minimum grading work is anticipated for the proposed construction
of the fuel tanks
Prior to the placement of the footing foundation the subgrade soil
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches below the finished
grade moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of the maximum
density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any soft
spots encountered should be removed to firm material and replaced with
on site granular soil and compacted to the standard mentioned above If
required import fill material should be non expansive free of organic
matter generally less than 3 inches in size and form a dense stable soil
mass when compacted

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the spread footing excavations subgrade
preparations and installation of tank be observed by the soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
these presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
A RES
The subsurface conditions at both sites were explored by
drilling one 1 boring to a depth of 20 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 3
and 4 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates 1 and 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
