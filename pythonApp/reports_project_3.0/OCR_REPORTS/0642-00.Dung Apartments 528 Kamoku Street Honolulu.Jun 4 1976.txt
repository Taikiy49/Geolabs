SUMMARY
The proposed 3 story apartment structure can
be supported on pile foundation A 12 inch
square concrete pile bearing into the lower
stiff clay stratum will provide an estimated
allowable capacity of 40 tons
Because of the very soft underlying mud deposit
at this site spread footing may be considered
if low bearing capacity of 1 000 p s f is used
This report presents the results of a foundation
investigation at the proposed 3 story high Dung
Apartments site located on Kamoku Street near
the Ala Wai Canal of Honolulu The purpose of
the subsurface soil investigation was to identify
existing soil conditions and to obtain soil data
for design and construction of foundations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is approximately 60 feet wide along the Kamoku Street
frontage and 120 feet deep mauka The site is relatively fiat
with elevation varing from 5 2 to 5 8 feet
Presently the site is occupied by three 1 story rental cottages
The Ala Wai Canal is located makai of the site

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The present plan is to construct a 3 story high apartment building
40 by 100 feet in plan dimension Columns and bearing walls will
be used to support the building Maximum column loads up to 130
kips are anticipated Bearing wall loading is about 28 kips per
lineal foot A C parking will be located at grade
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Two test borings were drilled to depths of 28 0 and 34 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Soil samples were obtained from the
bore holes for selected laboratory testing Detailed subsurface
information is presented in the two boring logs in the Appendix
Locations of borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Soil Condition
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
with approximately five feet of medium density man made fills con
sisting of silty clay and coral sand layers
Beneath the fill lagoonal deposits of very loose and soft sand mud
with coral fragments was encountered This loose material extends
to depths of 25 feet
Below the lagoonal deposits or about 25 feet a very stiff silty
clay layer was encountered to the full depth of the borings
The groundwater level is influenced by tidal flucturations and was
generally encountered 4 feet below the existing ground surface at
approximately elevation 1

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
The upper land fill and the underlying loose lagoonal deposits
can provide a very low bearing capacity without excessive buil
ding settlements For a building structure with heavy column
and wall loadings large size footings upon the site soil con
ditions might be uneconomical and difficult to design for the
large settlements Therefore pile foundations are highly re
commended for the proposed apartment structure The pile foun
dation will be supported by both friction and end bearing upon
the lower stiff clayey stratum
Foundation
spread Footing Foundation Based on the borings and laboratory
testing results spread and wall footing foundation placed at
minimum depth upon the surface fill layer can be designed for an
allowable bearing pressure of 1 000 pounds per square foot
Based on the above bearing pressure and loading the calculated
total settlement would be about one inch for the columns and 1
inches for the bearing walls
The estimated differential settlements would approximately be one
half of the above settlements
Pile Foundation It is estimated that a 12 inch square concrete
pile extending 15 feet into the lower very stiff clayey layer will
develop an allowable bearing capacity of 40 tons per pile

This pile should be driven to a final driving resistance of
65 blows per foot for the last two feet or 8 blows per inch
with a hammer delivering a rated energy of 15 000 foot pounds
Prior to production pile driving 3 test piles of 45 feet long
should be driven to establish more accurately the order lengths
and to establish final driving resistance criteria
The piles should be spaced no closer than three pile diameters
otherwise group action may Fediice the pile capacity They may
be considered to be fixed at 5 foot depths from present grade
The driving criteria may be modified during construction if ex
cessive resistance is encountered
Pavement
A surface sample of on site clayey soil was tested for swelling
potential The laboratory result indicated low expansion of less
than one percent Based on the present data the following pave
ment section may be used for designing the parking area
2 Asphalt Concrete
6 Base Course
8 Total Pavement Thickness
The base edurse should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR of 85 and confirming with section 31 2 Standard Speci
fication of Public Work Construction or equivalent The subgrade
soil should be scarified down to a depth of 6 inches moisted and
compacted to 95 compaction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations for the spread
footing construction be inspected by the soil engineer prior to
the placement of concrete or reinforcing to insure that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
If the structure is to be supported on piles the pile dring opera
tions should be monitored on a full time basis by a qualified tech
nician under the direction of a soil engineer to verify that the
piles have been driven in accordance with the recommendations
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
2 borings utilizing truck mounted and portable drilling equipment
The depths of the borings terminated at 28 0 and 34 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
Operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classific eion System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 and A 2
Soi samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth

as an aid in the soil classification and in evaluating soil
properties Atterberg Limits and unit weight tests were run

soil conditions The results of these tests are presented on
the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample location
One swell test of one inch thick ring sample was conducted on
a surface sample for measuring the swell potential The swell
test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Direct Shear tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the strength characteristics of the encountered soils The test
results are shown on Plate B 2
To determine the consolidation characteristic of the on site soil
one consolidation test was performed The consolidation curve is
