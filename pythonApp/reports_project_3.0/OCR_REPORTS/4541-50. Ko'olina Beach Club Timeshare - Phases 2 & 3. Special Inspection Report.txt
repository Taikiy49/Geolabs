At your request and as required by the City and County of Honolulu Geolabs Inc
has provided geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services during the
drilled shaft foundation installation for the above project The following letter report
summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the installation of the drilled
shaft foundations for the Hale Moana Tower structure of the Ko Olina Beach Club
Timeshare Phases 2 3 project
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Ko Olina Beach
Club Timeshare Phases 2 3 project and our recommendations are presented in the
above referenced report The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during
construction was to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions
during the construction of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special
Inspection services by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation at the Ko Olina
Beach Club Timeshare Phases 2 3 project for general conformance with the project
plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The Ko Olina Beach Club Timeshare Phases 2 3 project site is located in the
Ko Olina area on the southwestern portion of the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project generally involves the construction of a new
high rise building Hale Moana Tower and a parking structure

The Hale Moana Tower will be stepped up to 15 stories measuring approximately
100 to 140 feet high and will be supported on drilled shaft foundations with an allowable
design load capacity of 1 500 kips in compression The parking structure will be two levels
with column loads of about 125 to 200 kips per column The parking garage and other
minor structures will be supported on a shallow foundation system consisting of spread
footings and or continuous wall footings
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Hale Moana Tower of the Ko Olina Beach
Club Timeshare Phases 2 3 project only The general contractor for the project is
Nordic PCL A Hawaiian Joint Venture and the drilled shaft installation subcontractor is
Malcolm Drilling Co Inc
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of surface fills
overlying inter bedded layers of coralline detritus and coral formations The surface fills at
the project site generally were less than about 2 feet in thickness and consisted of very
stiff to hard silty clays and medium dense silty sands The surface fills generally were
underlain by medium hard to hard coral formation extending down to depths of about 9 to
25 feet below the ground surface Below the upper coral formation the subsurface
conditions consisted of inter bedded layers of coralline detritus and coral formations
extending down to the maximum depth explored of about 121 5 feet below the ground
surface
Due to the inter bedded nature of the coralline detritus and coral formations at the
project site the subsurface conditions may be considered highly variable across the
project site The coral formations encountered in the borings generally consist of
algal coral reef and cemented sands The cementation of the coral formations appears
to vary considerably with materials ranging from competent algal coral reef formation to
weak coral and medium dense silty sands with coralline debris The coralline detritus
encountered at the project site generally consists of loose to medium dense sands silt
and coralline debris with weak to virtually no cementation
In addition layers of alluvium ranging from about 2 to 8 feet in thickness also
were encountered in the borings at depths ranging from about 45 to 63 feet below the
ground surface The alluvium encountered generally consisted of stiff to very stiff silty
clays Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 15 2 to 18 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of the field exploration in 2003 The water levels
measured generally correspond to between about Elevations 0 1 and 2 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL

Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements a dedicated load test shaft was constructed and
tested to failure Following completion of the load test the drilled shaft lengths for the
project were revised based on the favorable results of the instrumented load test The
following subsections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and
discussions on the production drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shaft
As part of the project specifications a dedicated 36 inch diameter load test shaft
was installed at the project site at the approximate location indicated on the Drilled Shaft
Location Plans Plates 2 1 thru 2 4 The load test shaft was excavated by the drilled shaft
contractor on April 6 and 7 2004 The load test shaft extended to a depth of about 75 feet
below the existing ground surface The drilled shaft contractor lowered the reinforcing
cage with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage instrumentation into the
drilled hole and placed concrete in the load test shaft on
The drilled shaft contractor used polymer slurry to keep the drilled hole open during
excavation of the load test shaft hole Temporary casing was not installed for the load test
shaft however the top 12 5 feet of the drilled shaft excavation was drilled with a 42 inch
diameter auger A log of the materials encountered during load test shaft excavation and
details of the load test shaft construction are presented on the attached Drilled Shaft
Records
After the load test shaft was installed and cured for at least 3 days a load test was
performed by Load Test Inc The load test shaft was loaded to plunging failure of the
bottom plug of the load test shaft Complete details of the load test program are presented
in the report prepared by Load Test Inc entitled Report on Drilled Shaft Load Testing
Osterberg Method Test Shaft Olina Resort Oahu HI dated
Based on the results of the load test we recommended that the drilled shaft lengths
be reduced from the initially specified 60 feet to 50 feet on the majority of the production
drilled shafts with a bottom of foundation cap elevation of 13 feet MSL For the drilled
shafts with a bottom of foundation cap elevation of 22 75 feet MSL a drilled shaft length
of 60 feet below the bottom of foundation cap elevation was maintained
Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 71 production shafts from
through May 14 2004 The as built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled
shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this letter

report Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of
reinforcing cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled
shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a SoilMec R 622 drill
rig equipped with 36 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar to the
load test shaft Because the drilled holes stayed open without significant caving in of the
sidewalls polymer slurry and temporary casing were not used during excavation of the
production drilled shafts After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip
elevations the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate
elevations with the use of a crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled
hole concrete was placed through a 4 inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom
During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was
maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled shaft holes using the tremie method Concrete with slump ranging
from 7 to 9 inches were used for the drilled shafts It should be noted that Pacific Testing
Services Inc monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance
of slump tests and compression tests Logs of the individual drilled shafts installed and the
materials encountered in each of the drilled shafts during shaft excavation are presented
on the attached Drilled Shaft Records
It should be noted that Drilled Shaft Nos 69 and 70 were installed from the existing
ground surface elevation of about 20 6 and 17 feet MSL respectively instead of the
bottom of foundation cap Based on the project plans the bottom of foundation cap
elevations for these two drilled shafts are 21 3 feet MSL Therefore these drilled shafts
will need to be extended to the bottom of foundation cap elevation The Structural
Engineer of Record was notified of the installed conditions of Drilled Shaft Nos 69 and 70
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the production drilled shafts supporting the Hale Moana Tower were installed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the
geotechnical recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report for the
project dated In addition it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design load
capacities
