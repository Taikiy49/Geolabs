This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the above proposed Operational Trainer Facility Addition
at the Barbers Point Naval Air Station Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project
Location map Plate l
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions by drilling and sampling borings Data obtained from
borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were utilized to
provide recommendations for the design of the foundation of the
subject project Our work was performed in general accordance with
the scope of services outlined in our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct an addition to the existing Building
1874 at Barbers Point NAS It will be a one story structure of
concrete masonry on concrete slab with metal roof deck
It is our understanding that a major portion of the new building
will have concrete floor slabs depressed 18 inches below the finished
floor grade to provide space for electronic cables A metal flooring
system will be utilized to support the electronic equipment at
finished floor level
The maximum column loads are anticipated to be on the order of 52
kips and the maximum wall loads on the order of 2 5 kips per linear
foot
Site improvements will include sidewalks and a new asphalt
concrete paved service road and landscaped areas
The proposed layout of the building is shown on the Site Plan

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located about 350 feet east of the
intersection of Saratoga Street and Nassau Avenue inside the Naval Air
Station About one half of the proposed building addition site is now
occupied by an uncovered concrete slab adjacent to the existing
Building 1874 while the other half of the site is in a landscaped
area The proposed service road will be located partially ina
relatively level landscaped area and partially in natural land
overgrown with haole koa bushes Coral outcrops consisting of loose
coral fragments with filled and unfilled surface cavities are noted in
the overgrown area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the extensive Ewa coastal plain which
consists of emerged reef limestone deposits with very little soil
overburden These reef deposits are most likely the result of the
Kaena 95 stand of the sea and have been modified by chemical
weathering

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by a thin veneer of
brown clayey silt topsoil in the landscaped area a thin layer of
dense coral sand and gravel fill of about 0 5 to 1 foot underlain by
cemented ledge coral to at least 30 feet the maximum depth of our
borings The coral consisted mainly of cemented coral sand and gravel
which is believed to be coral algal reef detritus There was a pocket
of stiff brown clayey silt from 7 to 11 feet deep in Boring 1 which is
believed to be an infilled solution Cavity in the coral formation
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that a competent coral formation
exists at a shallow depth within the proposed site and will be
suitable for spread footing foundation support
The footing should be embedded at least 6 inches into cemented
coral or at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade whichever
is the deepest
An allowable soil bearing of 6000 p s f may be used It may be
increased by one third when considering temporary wind or seismic
loads A minimuin footing size of 16 inches should be used

Cavity Probing and Grouting
It should be noted that the coral formation frequently contains
filled and unfilled voids or cavities After the site is cleared for
building construction the foundation area especially below the
footings should be probed for possible cavity or cavities It is
recommended that a 3 inch diameter probe hole be drilled at each
column location or on 8 feet centers along continuous wall footings
as appropriate The probe holes should be drilled to a minimum of 5
feet below the bottom of the footing The resulting probe holes
should be backfilled with neat cement grout mix injected at low
pressures
If cavities are encountered they should be grouted with cement or
concrete grout with a compressive strength of at least 2000 p S i
For small cavities with height of less than 6 inches measured at the
probe location cement grout should be used For cavities with height
exceeding 6 inches concrete grout may be used In such a case the
probe hole may need to be enlarged
If excessive grout take is noted filling should be temporary
suspended to allow the grout to set Thereafter the filling should
then be resumed The temporary suspension of filling is to limit the
excessive grout take
We recommend that the probing and grouting operations be monitored
by a soils engineer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1304 00 Page 6
It is possible that cavities may still be encountered during
footing excavations even after probing When encountered these
cavities should be cleaned of soils or other debris and backfilled
with lean concrete
We also recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by a
soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our design
recommendations were based
It is anticipated that the total and differential foundation
settlements if constructed according to the above design criteria
should be negligible
Site Preparation
All vegetation debris and other deleterious materials should be
removed from the proposed building addition and roadway sites The
brown clayey silt topsoil should be stripped and stockpiled for future
landscaping use The existing concrete slab at the proposed building
addition site should be removed
The existing haole koa bushes within the project area should be
cleared and the ground surface levelled The coral fragments on the
surface may be crushed by earth moving equipment Any ground cavity
encountered at the surface within the roadway area should be cleaned
to its bottom or at least 3 feet below the finished roadway surface
whichever is the shallowest and then backfilled with compacted
on site coral sand and gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII

The stripped surface should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches moisture conditioned and then recompacted The on site coral
material may be used as engineered fill provided that the fragments
are crushed down to 3 inches or smaller in sizes Oversized fragments
should not be used as fill
All 111 and backfill material should be placed in horizontal
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
On Grade Slab
On grade slab may be used for the building addition after the site
has been excavated or scarified and recompacted The on grade slab
should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed
rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to provide wiform support A
moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs
The slabs may be of conventional design The thickened edges
exposed to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 8 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade
Pavement
The coral sand and gravel surface would provide good subgrade
support for the pavements when compacted to a dry density of at least
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum For flexible pavements the
section should consist of 2 inches of asphalt concrete and 6 inches of
base course

CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications prepared for site grading cavity probing
and grouting foundation and pavement should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing cavity probing
and grouting engineered fill placement and compaction foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data
and laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils cavities
trash fill or expansive soils may occur in localized areas
Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during construction
to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency fund is
thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs

the preparation of site grading plans and foundation design of this
project

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depths

Limits test was performed and the results are presented on Plates B 1
Two laboratory compaction and two CBR tests were performed on
bulk samples of the on site near surface soils to evaluate their
pavement subgrade support and compaction characteristics The test
results are summarized on Plate B 1l
One set of direct shear test was performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of near surface
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2
