PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil conditions at the site in order
to provide data for use in the design of the site grading
for the proposed subdivision development
The results of our field exploratory and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analyses that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendices of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 2 and the logs of the borings are pre
sented in Appendix A Detailed information regarding the
field exploration and laboratory testing are also presented
in the appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern edge of Waipahu
town in Waipio Ewa Oahu The project site has a triangular
shape becoming narrow to the south It is bounded to the
north by H 1 Interstate Highway Waipahu Street to the south
east and the existing cane haul road to the southwest
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1073 00
P 3
The site is relatively level with ground elevations ranging
from 102 feet at the site entrance along Waipahu Street up
to elevation 119 feet for the soil berm along the northern
boundary A drainageway enters at the northeastern corner
running southwest along Waipahu Street having a depth of
approximately 10 feet In addition an old irrigation flume
parallels the northern property line
Ground cover consists primarily of California grass some
weeds and haole koa trees There are several piles of end
dumped debris and soil on the site along with the hulk of an
abandoned vehicle
Access to the site at the present time is limited through the
existing cane haul road since direct access from Waipahu
Street is blocked by numerous large boulders approximately
4 feet in diameter
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development consisting of 13 house lots
and one lot left as the existing cane haul road It is antici
pated that the haul road lot will be subdivided at a later
date to form additional house lots There will be two access
roads in the development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1073 00
P 4
It is anticipated that site grading will involve cuts up to
4 feet deep in the northern portion of the site and fills
generally 4 or less feet thick in the south portion of the
Site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the southwestern portion of
the Koolau Shield Volcano at the mouths of Panakuahi and Wai
awa Gulches Alluvium deposited by these two streams during
the Pleistocene period formed an alluvial fan The site is
underlain by these fan deposits
Field Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of 11 0 to
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface in order to explore
the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by very
stiffto hard silty clays derived from the weathering of alluv
ial materials
A thin layer of fill approximately 1 5 feet thick was encoun
tered in Boring No 1 This existing fill appears to be stiff

and well compacted
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very stiff to hard alluvial
material consisting of reddish brown and brown Silty clays
Laboratory tests indicate that these soils generally have low
swelling potential
Site Grading
There are existing piles of rubbish and boulders on the
Site Due to the relatively small fills anticipated
boulder disposal in the proposed new fill is not recon
mended Therefore prior to filling the site should be
cleared of boulders vegetation rubbish and other dele
terious material
In areas to receive fill the ground should first be
proof rolled with a heavy roller to detect soft spots in
the existing fill If soft spots are encountered they
Should be over excavated down to firm ground and recom

pacted The ground should then be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture con
tent and then compated to a minimum of 90 of its maxi
mum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 70 test method
After the above site preparation the fill should be
moistened to above optimum moisture content placed in
10 inch lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of
its maximum dry density established by the above ASTM
test designation
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have low swelling potential therefore the material may
be used for the fill
Cut and fill slopes generally less than 10 feet in ver
tical height are anticipated
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then
cutting back to the design slope to obtain a well compac
ted slope face

Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the
Slope and slope planting utilized as soon as practicable
to reduce erosion
Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of all the trenches is important to mini
mize surface ground settlement along the utility alignments
On site material generally less than 6 inches in size may
be utilized as backfill All backfill should be compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Most of the on site material is fine grained and would not be
Suitable for compaction by water jetting or ponding and there
fore this would require mechanical compaction
House Foundations
For the light residential structures spread footing founda
tions or slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects
of slope creep and erosion slabs should be set back a mini
mum distance of 5 feet away from the tops of slopes Also
post footings located close to the tops of slepes should be
embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure
of 2500 p s f can be used for footings resting on very stiff

in situ soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench
backfill should be well compacted to reduce the footing set
tlement due to settlement of the backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compac
ted base course The thickened edge around the slab on grade
should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent ground
surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed
to dry out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
Based on the present laboratory CBR test results of the mater
ial near the roadway area the preliminary pavement design is
as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 inch Select Borrow
18 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade Soil

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by the soil engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 11 0 to 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface utilizing a truck mounted
rotary hydraulic drilling rig The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the enclosed Boring Location
Plan Plate 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural exami
nation in the field by our engineer who continuously moni
tored the drilling operations The classifications were
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in our laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
soils encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch O D
Standard penetration sampler or a 3 inch O D ring sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
GEOLABS HAWAII
Iv
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 1
H V the ground surface shall be keyed and benched
into competent soil to bond the fill to the exist
ing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by
the soils engineer where springs or zones of seep
age are encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
submitted to the soils engineer for testing and approval
a minimum of 5 working days prior to its proposed deliv
ery to the site Fill material shall contain less than
5 organic matter and other deleterious material and
shall not contain rocks or lumps in excess of 8 inches
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not exceeding 10 inches in thickness Each layer
shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within
each layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soi
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi

Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
