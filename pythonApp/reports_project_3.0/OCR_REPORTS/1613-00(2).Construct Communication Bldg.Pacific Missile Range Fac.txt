Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C3 87
Construct Communication Building
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Kokee State Park Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Communication Building at the Pacific Missile
Range Facility at Kokee State Park on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
general location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
W O Page 2
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the soil underlying the proposed
facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling three 3 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed Communication Building consists of an air conditioned
pre engineered steel structure approximately 1200 square feet in area
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
building However the finished grade of the building was not available
at this time

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located at an approximate distance of 125 feet
to the north of an existing 195 foot microwave relay tower in the Pacific
Missile Range Facility at Kokee on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
final location of the building is approximately 40 feet north of the
preliminary one which was used as a reference for locating the borings
during our drilling operations
The site is an open area measured at about 43 by 28 feet A
natural slope with a low gradient exists close to its west boundary and
appears to be in a generally stable condition
The project site topography is sloping gently down from the
northeast toward the southwest Ground cover consists mainly of some
grass and brown to reddish brown clayey silt
Subsoil Condition
Three borings were drilled to a depth of 15 feet below the ground
surface All borings encountered medium stiff to stiff brown to reddish
brown clayey silt derived fron decomposed rock to the drilled depth of
the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time
of our field exploration but the soils appeared to have high in situ
moisture contents

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Subsequent to the field exploration the proposed building was
relocated about 40 feet northeast of the original site Based on the
topography and general geology of the area it is our opinion that the
drilled borings are probably representative of the new location
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light building load
we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing on the
upper in situ medium stiff to stiff clayey silt may be used It is our
opinion that footings at the western side of the proposed building near
the existing slope should be located at a minimum horizontal setback
distance as recommended on Plate 3
For footings bearing on the stiff in situ clayey silt we recommend
they should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
an inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O Page 5
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
medium stiff soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive resistance
Site Grading
Due to the gently sloping ground minimal site grading is
anticipated for the site development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be kept
moist and recompacted prior to placement of the footing if they are
loosened during the excavation The areas which are to receive pavement
should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the cleared
ground surface moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density Any loose or soft spots encountered at the
footing or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with suitable
imported material compacted to the above mentioned standard
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
If fill is required the area to receive fill after clearing and
grubbing should be scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots encountered
should be removed and replaced with suitable imported material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
The on site clayey silt soil generally has low expansion potential
if maintained at its natural high moisture content However the
expansion potential would increase if the soil is allowed to dry out
Therefore extreme care should be used to keep the subgrade and fill
soils moist at all times If required new imported fill material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in
maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Structural fill
should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted horizontal lifts and compacted
to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Pavement
Depending on the traffic conditions the following pavement section
recommendation may be used for preliminary design purpose
Flexible Pavement Light Traffic and Parking Area
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Coricrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

W O Page 7
The base and subbase course should consist of crushed rock
compacted to a dry density of at least 953 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade will be used for the
proposed building The on grade floor slab should be placed over a
4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3
Fine rock to provide uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should
be placed under the slabs
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 3 borings to depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface
A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A l thru A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
