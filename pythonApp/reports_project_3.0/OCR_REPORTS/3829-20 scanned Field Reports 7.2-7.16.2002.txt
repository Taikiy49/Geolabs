Based on today s discussion at the project site and in accordance with Mr Patrick
Young s request it is desired to document some issues that pertain to the current slope
mass excavation and finish slope grading work The following items pertaining to the
existing condition of the cut slopes were noted
Cut Slope at 42 12
We understand that the existing toe of the cut slope is to remain at the present
location The cut slope presently stands at about one horizontal to two vertical 1H 2V
and flatter and consists of weathered basalt rock and clinker with surface soil deposits
located on the upper flatter slope segment We understand that the cut slope will not be
faced with Grouted Rip Rap as alternatively proposed Geolabs Facsimile Memo dated
May 22 Based on our observations the present cut slope condition without the GRP
appears stable however it should be noted that the un faced slope condition will be
affected by some erosion and raveling of the exposed loose rock and soil materials The
erosion may require more frequent clearing of the buffer zone to maintain the area free of
accumulated soil Aweathered rock debris Furthermore we recommend that the eastern
half of the existing cut slope exposure be manually swept of loose surface rocks to
reduce the potential for future rockfall
Cut Slope at 42 13
Based on our observations the cut slope presently stands at about 1H 4V lower
segment to 1H 2V upper segment Based on the present exposure of the loose soil
and rock fragments with some boulders composing the cut slope we recommend that the
colluvial talus slope be flattened further to 1H 1V to intersect with the old rock cut slope
located in the background The construction plans show the cut slope to be excavated at
1H 2V which is not appropriate for the loose colluvial materials that were exposed during
slope excavation The existing toe of the cut slope may remain at the present location If
hard rock materials are exposed during excavation the cut slope may be locally
steepened We recommend that our geologist be present to observe the slope flattening
in the event that unanticipated conditions are encountered during the slope excavation

Cut Slope at 42 17
The construction plans call for the construction of GRP slope facing as necessary
for slope stabilization at this lot Based on our observations of the exposed slope
condition and limited probing of the subject slope it appears that most of the slope is
composed of weathered in situ basalt rock and does not require GRP facing for slope
stabilization However based on our observations the lower part of the slope
highlighted on attached drawing appears to have surface colluvial soils that may mantle
the underlying basalt rock The surface soils are generally soft loose and may range up to
about 3 feet in thickness based on hand probing of the area The surface soil deposits
may be subject to erosion by rainfall run off Therefore we recommend that the GRP
slope facing may be reduced in area coverage from the area coverage shown on the
construction plans to include only that portion of the slope that appears to be covered by
the surface soil materials The approximate location of the soil materials that should be
faced with GRP is shown on the attached drawing
It should be noted that our recommendation is based on observation and limited
probing of the slope surface prior to the setting of the finish grade at the buffer zone and
prior to clearing the slope face of all accumulated surface debris Therefore if hard rock
material is encountered on the slope face at finish grade where the soil deposits were
noted to occur our recommendation for reduced GRP facing should be re evaluated
