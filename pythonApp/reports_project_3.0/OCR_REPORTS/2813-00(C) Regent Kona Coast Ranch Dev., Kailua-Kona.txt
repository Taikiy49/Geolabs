Ground Penetrating Radar Operation
Ground penetrating radar utilizes impulse radar technology to obtain real time
profiles of the subsurface materials A radar frequency pulse is transmitted into the
subsurface and when it encounters an interface between two materials with
differing electrical properties it is reflected back to a receiver on the surface In
a relatively homogeneous medium this reflection is noted as an anomaly
The reflected pulse is intercepted by the receiver and then electronically processed
and printed on a graphic recorder producing a record similar to a marine depth
recorder chart As the transmitter and receiver antenna array is moved across the
ground surface a real time graphic record of the subsurface is generated
Markers can be inserted into the data print to reference distances as the array is
moved providing a means to physically locate anomalies
Ground Penetrating Radar is generally considered most applicable to the location
of subsurface voids and buried structures Depth of penetration and data
resolution are dependent upon soil type moisture content electrical properties
antenna frequency and power Under optimum conditions penetrations of about
15 and 50 feet are possible for our 500 and 100 Megahertz antennae respectively
The recommendations and findings presented in this memorandum are a result of
a review of the geophysical data obtained with the ground penetrating radar and of our
findings during the visual reconnaissance and probing accomplished by ripping with a D 9
bulldozer The work was performed during the week of March 23 1992

Road Grading Recommendations
Prior to the placement of structural roadway fills the existing roadway surface
should be proofrolled with a vibratory drum roller to expose and collapse any near surface
voids which were not indicated exposed in the geophysical survey Any exposed voids
should be backfilled with compacted structural fill
Fills should be placed in lifts with a maximum depth of one foot and compacted
with a sheepsfoot and or smooth drum vibratory roller with a static weight of not less than
twenty tons The compaction should be observed by the geotechnical engineer so that
the number of passes needed to attain proper compaction can be determined The fills
in the upper three 3 feet below the finish subgrade should consist of well graded
granular materials with a maximum particle dimension of twelve I2 inches The fills
below finish subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of their
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described by ASTM
Test Method D 1557
The base course should be placed and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
of its maximum dry density
The backfill for the utility trenches and the placement procedures should conform
to Department of Water Supply standards
