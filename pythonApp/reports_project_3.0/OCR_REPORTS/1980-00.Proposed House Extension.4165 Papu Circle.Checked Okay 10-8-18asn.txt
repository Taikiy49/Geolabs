FOUNDATION ENGINEERING EVALUATION
PROPOSED HOUSE EXTENSION
4165 PAPU CIRCLE
In accordance with your request we have performed a foundation
engineering evaluation at the above referenced site The purpose of our
study was to examine the surface and near surface soil conditions at the
site of the proposed house extension to provide soil data for the new
footing design
The study consisted of a site reconnaissance the drilling of one
1 boring laboratory testing and a review of the preliminary house
plan This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the southwestern corner of the
intersection of Papu Circle and Kaikoo Place in Honolulu on the island of
Oahu

It is currently planned to add a second floor to the southern end of
the existing one story residence New footings to support the extension
will be located on the existing level grassed lawn area
Detailed plans of the proposed extension were not available at the
time of our evaluation However it is anticipated that the proposed
extension will utilize a CMU wall footing supporting wood floors
FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling one 1
boring to a depth of 9 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring
encountered very stiff brown sandy silt with gravel and cobbles to a depth
of 5 feet and clayey silt with cobbles and boulders from 5 feet to the
full depth of the boring
No groundwater was encountered in the boring at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The very stiff brown sandy or clayey soil encountered generally can
provide suitable formation support for the proposed residential structure
Site Grading
Prior to additional filling or foundation construction the site
should be cleared of vegetation and debris then scarified moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78

The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive granular soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
We recommend that an experienced soils technician be utilized to
observe the filling to verify that the proper type of fill material is
used and that the fill is compacted to the above specified density
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed house
extension construction The footings should bear on firm natural ground
or compacted soil An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used
with a one third increase allowed for wind or seismic loading
Interior spread footings on level areas should be poured in place and
be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the finished grade
Perimeter wall footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Slab On Grade
It it is desired to use slab on grade for the floor of the house and
lanai It is recommended that a minimum 12 inch deep thickened edge
embedment be utilized to provide a protective barrier against future water
migration under the house and covered slab area
It is recommended that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel be provided
as cushion fill below the slab
A vapor barrier membrane may be considered over the 3B Fine gravel
layer if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor
covering
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling from
the Plan by Urban Works The physical location of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
May occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Om Sat See em me Sm eS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one
1 boring to a depth of 9 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A portable drill
rig was used to drill the boring
The soils encountered in the boring were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Log Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Log at the appropriate
sample depth
