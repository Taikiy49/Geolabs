Foundation Probing and Grouting Report
MG Reservoir
Hilo Island of Hawaii
Dear Mr Fukumoto
In accordance with the project specifications we have completed the probing and
grouting observations for the MG Reservoir project This letter report
summarizes our field observations made during the probing and grouting operations
performed at the subject site
The MG Reservoir project site is located along Kawailani Street in
the Hilo area on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project involves the construction of a new Reservoir and connecting pipeline
consisting of ductile iron pipe
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
probing and grouting operations for the MG Reservoir only The Kawailani
Reservoir is approximately 90 feet in diameter and 20 feet in height The storage
tank is to be supported by a perimeter wall footing and twelve interior column footings As
part of project requirements the tank foundation was to be probed and grouted as part of
the foundation preparation The intent of the foundation probing and grouting operations is
an attempt to locate and grout potential subsurface cavities and or voids beneath the tank
foundations
The MG Reservoir foundation locations and probe hole locations
were surveyed and staked out by the sitework contractor Jas W Glover Ltd The probe
holes were marked along the perimeter footings spaced at about 10 foot on center and at
the center of the twelve interior column footing locations At the time of the probing and
grouting operations the existing ground surface was about 1 to 3 feet below the footing
subgrade level The approximate locations of the probe holes for the storage tank are
shown on the Probe Hole Location Layout Plate 2
The probe holes were drilled with a track mounted drill rig equipped with an
air track hammer The probe holes drilled were about 3 5 inches in diameter and
extended to depths of about 7 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface or at least
10 feet below the bottom of the foundation elevation A total of 52 probe holes were drilled
for the storage tank as shown on Plate 2

Materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of
reddish gray to gray medium hard to hard basalt formations interbedded with layers of
clinker materials Based on our observations of the probing operations small cavities
and or voids were encountered in 15 of the 52 probe holes drilled A summary of our
foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
Following the probing operations each probe hole was fitted with a 2 inch diameter
PVC pipe to maintain the integrity of the open hole After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled by injecting grout into the probe holes at an
average rate of about 38 gallons per minute A total of about 29 5 cubic yards of grout
was used to fill the 52 probe holes and cavities encountered The maximum grout intake
was located at Probe Hole No 38 where a void was encountered from about 6 to 8 feet
below the surface
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the specifications and the geotechnical
engineering exploration report Based on our observations it is our professional opinion
that the cavities encountered at the storage tank location have been grouted and that the
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the water tank
structure
We appreciate the opportunity to be of continued service to you on this project If
you have questions or need additional information please contact our office
