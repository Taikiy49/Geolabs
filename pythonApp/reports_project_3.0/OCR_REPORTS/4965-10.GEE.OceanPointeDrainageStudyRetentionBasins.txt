In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration in support of the Ocean Pointe Drainage Study
Retention Basins in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report

noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Ocean Pointe development area in Ewa Beach
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided it
is planned to perform a drainage study at the project site The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that it is proposed to upgrade portions of the existing retention
basins at the project site for storm water disposal The existing retention basins are
located on the east side of the existing Kaloi Emergency Overflow Channel The retention
basin upgrades may consist of widening the narrow portions of the existing retention
basins We understand the retention basins will have invert elevations ranging from
approximately 2 75 to 6 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL We understand that the top of
the weir structure located at the southwestern portion of the retention basins will be at
about Elevation 8 5 feet MSL Therefore we have assumed that the top of the highest
water surface elevation within the retention basin will be at the top elevation of the weir
structure

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of retention basins for storm water runoff
disposal To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house soils and geologic information
pertaining to the project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig a water truck
and two operators to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of six borings extending to depths of about 26 8 to
27 5 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a field
geologist from our firm
5 Performance of two permeability tests in each boring drilled at depths of
about 3 to 5 feet and 24 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface to
evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials
encountered at the site
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
7 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of the retention
basins for storm water runoff disposal at the project site
8 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
9 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the Ocean Pointe development at Ewa Beach in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Ocean Pointe development is
located to the west of Fort Weaver Road near Hanakahi Street The project site is located
to the east of the Kaloi Emergency Overflow Channel and to the northeast of Oneula
Beach Park The location of the project site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the available topographic map the existing ground surface elevation
ranges from about 2 to 7 feet MSL At the time of our field exploration the surface of
the existing basins consisted mainly of coralline detritus materials Clays and silts were
observed at the southern portion of the existing basins Some construction activities were
observed at the central portion of the wider basin The construction activities consisted of
excavation boulder crushing hauling and dust control Stockpiles of coralline materials
were observed in the area with construction activities Basaltic boulders were laid along
the ground surface at the southern portion of the wider basin
Shrubs and grass were observed at the southern portion of the wider basin In
addition wood debris from trees were observed along the banks of the narrower basin
Standing water was also observed at localized areas of the narrower basin
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 4 through 9 extending to depths of about 26 8 to
27 5 feet below the existing ground surface Boring Nos 1 through 3 were drilled during
our previous field exploration extending to depths of about 48 to 58 feet below the existing
ground surface The Logs of Borings for Boring Nos 1 through 3 are included as part of
this letter report in Appendix A for ease of reference The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers and rotary coring
tools The approximate locations of the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture of the soils encountered Moisture content
ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the
classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Boring Nos 4 through 9 drilled at the existing retention basins located adjacent to
the Kaloi Emergency Overflow Channel generally encountered coralline detritus materials
from the ground surface extending to the maximum depths explored of about 26 8 to
27 5 feet below the existing ground surface The coralline detritus materials encountered
consisted of medium dense to dense gravel and sands with moderate cementation of the
grains Dense coral formation was encountered at various depths in several of the borings
drilled for this study
Groundwater was encountered at depths ranging from about 1 7 to 6 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that the
groundwater levels encountered at the project site are subject to change due to tidal
fluctuation rainfall seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors The
Logs of Borings are presented on Plates A 1 through A 9 in Appendix A of this letter
report

Permeability Testing
Field permeability tests were conducted along the existing retention basins adjacent
to the existing Kaloi Emergency Overflow Channel Two permeability tests were
performed at each boring location Boring Nos 4 through 9 to evaluate the infiltration
characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered at the project site The locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The permeability tests at each boring location were typically performed at depths of
about 3 to 5 feet and 24 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface Both falling head
and constant head permeability tests were performed to evaluate the average hydraulic
conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field exploration the
subsurface materials encountered at the project site consist of medium dense to dense
coralline detritus consisting of gravel and sands with moderate cementation of the grains
and dense coral formation
Based on both the falling head and constant head field permeability test results the
calculated hydraulic conductivity k value at each test location is summarized in the
following table In general the calculated hydraulic conductivity k value for each of the
tests indicated that the subsurface materials consist of moderately to highly permeable

Estimated Retention Basin Capacities
We understand that the retention basins will be excavated to invert elevations
ranging from about 2 75 to 6 5 feet MSL Based on the top elevation of the weir
structure we anticipate that the top of the highest water surface elevation will be at about
8 5 feet MSL Based on the anticipated ponded water depths of about 2 to 5 75 feet and
the calculated hydraulic conductivities for the subsurface materials encountered at the
project site the estimated infiltration capacities of the retention basin range from about
0 004 to 5 0 gallons per minute per square foot of surface area gpm square foot Based
on the above and the distribution of permeability values we believe that an average
infiltration capacity of about square foot may be used for the design of the
retention basins
It should be emphasized that the estimated infiltration capacity provided assumes
that coralline detritus and coral formation is exposed at the bottom of the retention basins
and that the bottom of the retention basins are free of sediments It should be noted that
build up of sediments at the bottom of the retention basins is highly likely The build up of
sediments at the bottom of the retention basins will greatly affect the hydraulic
characteristics of the materials at the bottom of the retention basins and likely will reduce
the infiltration capacity of the retention basins Therefore counter measures to reduce the
potential for build up of sediments at the bottom of the retention basins or periodic
maintenance of the retention basins to remove the sediments should be implemented
In addition fills should not be placed on the bottom of the existing retention basins
to raise the grades of the retention basins to the design invert elevations Placement of
fils on the bottom of the retention basins could adversely impact the hydraulic
characteristics of the materials at the bottom of the retention basins
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical services during
construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring that are critical
include observation of the bottom of the retention basins Other aspects of the retention

different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the site plan provided by
Gray Hong Bills Nojima Associates Inc Elevations noted on the logs of borings were
obtained by interpolation between the spot elevations shown on the Retention Basin Plan
prepared by Gray Hong Bills Nojima Associates Inc on May 5 2003 The physical
locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition As mentioned previously groundwater was encountered in the borings at the
time of our exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may
occur due to tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors

their client for specific application to the Ocean Pointe Drainage Study

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in
the design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this report may not contain
sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a

performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are

may require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
