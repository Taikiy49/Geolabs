PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering investigation is to
explore the subsurface soil and water conditions at the proposed
building site to provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the design
of the building foundations and pavement thickness for parking lot
construction The general project location is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated Originally preliminary information indicated
that a portion of this area may be underlain by soft soil conditions
Therefore deeper borings were planned to evaluate possible deeper
foundations for the building Subsequently during the course of our
investigation better soil conditions were encountered and spread
footing foundation appeared feasible Therefore the boring depths were
shortened and the number of borings increased to evaluate the
variability of the competent near surface soils
At the time of our report preparation information such as
structural loading on the foundations and finished grades of the
proposed building etc were not available for detailed analysis
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 2 story concrete and or CMU
structure The building will be irregular shape approximately 135 by
150 feet in plan dimension
It is our understanding that a ground level parking area and
entrance road is planned on the north and east sides of the building
next to Iwilei Road and Jarrett Street
Due to the sloping ground at the site some cut and or fill are
anticipated over the building site for drainage purposes

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the makai side at the intersection
of Iwilei Road and Jarrett Street in Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The facility will cover an area of approximately 150 by 160 feet
with access from Iwilei Road and Jarrett Street
The topography generally slopes down towards Iwilei Road with
elevations ranging from 11 to 13 feet The area is presently occupied
by the City and County Medical Examiners
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site was originally located in an area where an emerged
coral reef formation was located near the surface while the surrounding
area was lower and used for rice fields Later it appears that the
site was filled for development
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions were explored by drilling seven 7 borings at
the building site to depths of 19 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface
The soil conditions encountered appear to be relatively uniform and
consisted of a thin surface layer of medium stiff to very stiff brown
sandy silt 1 to 3 feet thick over dense silty coral sand and gravel
weathered coral formation to the full depth of the borings

borings at the time of our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent soil conditions below the
proposed construction From a soil engineering point of view the site
is suitable for the proposed new building
Site Grading
Due to the gently sloping ground relatively small cuts and or
fills are anticipated for the site development
Fill Construction
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation debris
and deleterious material The grubbing work should be such that
all roots one inch or larger should be removed
After clearing and grubbing the area to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade and recompacted
to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density Any soft or weak
areas disclosed during this recompaction should be overexcavated
removed and then backfilled with engineered fill
The on site silty soil or excavated coral may be used for fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist
of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in
maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve The
minimum laboratory CBR value should be 15 at 95 relative
compaction of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density with CBR
expansion value not greater than 1
GEOLABS HAWAIi
I D 1723 Page 5
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density For fill placed outside of the proposed building area
the compaction can be reduced to 90 minimum relative compaction
per above ASTM Standard
Building Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the dense coral
formation weathered reef deposit or on the new structural fill is
recommended for the structure An allowable bearing value of 2500
p s may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fill
material and for the coral sand formation the allowable bearing value
may be increased to 5000 p s f A one third increase of the above
bearing value may be used for temporary wind and seismic loads
All footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
adjacent finished grade For footings bearing on coral formation the
embedment may be reduced to 12 inch to minimize cutting into the
underlying weathered coral layer
The bottom of all footing excavations should be free of soft
material
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to minimize building settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
Site drainage should be designed to divert surface water away from
the building foundation

Settlement
Based on the boring and laboratory test data it is our opinion
that for moderate building loads minimal settlements generally less
than one fourth of an inch may be anticipated for footings bearing on
coral or compacted structural fill layer
Lateral Earth Pressures
If retaining wall are required it should be designed to resist
lateral loads of 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 4 can be
utilized to determine the resistance of the structure and the foundation
On Grade Slabs
Depending on the proposed finished grades the subgrade of the
slabs may be in compacted fill or the dense coral formation
The thickness of the concrete slab may be of a conventionai
thickness of 4 inches A minimum of 4 inch base course should be
provided under the slab A moisture proofing membrane should be
considered below the slab to protect the floor covering from moisture
infiltration
Driveway and Parking Pavement Thickness
The pavement section required for the site will depend on the
actual subgrade soil conditions The near surface silty soil is
variable and would require a thicker pavement section than the
underlying coral or imported structural fill Where minimal cut or fill
is anticipated and the subgrade conditions will consist of the brown
sandy silt the following preliminary pavement design may be used for
cost estimating purposes

The pavement subgrades should be compacted and sloped to drain
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for
slabs and pavements be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation

If conditions encountered during construction are different from

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven 7 borings to depths of 19 5 to 20 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
