Dear Mr. Okada: 
In accordance with the project design requirements, a static compressive pile load 
test was conducted on a driven test pile on November 11, 2020. This letter report presents 
the results of our field observations and the test data collected during the static pile load 
test. 
The proposed project involves the design and construction of a Waterfront 
Recreational Facility project to the north and northwest of the existing Wai Kai Lagoon at 
the Ocean Pointe Development by Haseko in Ewa Beach on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
Based on the information provided, we understand several structures and boardwalks will 
be constructed along and within the Lagoon. 
The static pile load test was conducted on a 16.5-inch octagonal precast, 
prestressed concrete pile. The test pile was cast with a length of 74 feet. The test pile was 
driven to a depth of about 44.3 feet below the existing ground surface, which corresponds 
to a pile tip elevation of about -35.5 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The approximate location 
of the load test pile is shown on the Test Pile Location Plan, Plate 1.  
The test pile was driven on November 10, 2020 using a Junttan HHK9a hydraulic 
impact 
hammer 
capable of 
delivering 
a maximum 
rated 
energy 
of about 
79,200 foot-pounds at the maximum stroke height of 4 feet. During the test pile driving, a 
record of the blow counts and field observations of the driving behavior was recorded by 
our engineer. The test pile driving log is provided on Plate 2 for reference. 
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143 Procedure A 
with modifications. The maximum test load on the test pile was planned at about 175 kips. 
In general, the test pile was loaded to a maximum test load of 175 kips (250 percent of the 
design load of 70 kips) in increment of approximately 8.75 kips.  Each load increment was
