Dear Mr Honma
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project
from April 1994 through September 1994 The subject site is located in the Villages of
Kapolei development It is bounded by Kama aha Avenue to the north a proposed
community park to the east the proposed Kapolei Collector Road C to the west and
the proposed Village 6 Single Family Subdivision to the south as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The project site was previously mass graded by others The earthwork
operations and utility installation work at the above referenced site were performed by
Oahu Construction Company The grading work generally consisted of scarifying and
compacting the existing subgrade and placing up to about 3 feet of new fills The
available on site fill materials generally consisted of brown silty clay
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The fill material was then placed
in near level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry
density Road subbase and base course material were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of its maximum dry density

During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests for the earthwork and grading operations and infra
structure construction are summarized on the attachments of this report Plates 3 1
through 3 14
Our services consisted of periodic field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures and observation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above grading operations During the grading period various on
site and borrow soils were tested prior to their use in the field The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Field observations by our field technician during the earthwork operation and
infrastructure construction for the project indicate that this work was performed in
general accordance with the intent of the recommendations presented in the above
referenced report
ADDITIONAL RECOMMENDATIONS
Any additional fills should be properly compacted under the observation of a
geotechnical engineer
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finish
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgement
Owners should be advised not to block the surface drainage and to obtain
professional consultation in the planning design and construction of any future
improvements

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe ail activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of earthwork observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project It should also be recognized that there are
variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to
monitor the earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision
of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless
of when they are found
