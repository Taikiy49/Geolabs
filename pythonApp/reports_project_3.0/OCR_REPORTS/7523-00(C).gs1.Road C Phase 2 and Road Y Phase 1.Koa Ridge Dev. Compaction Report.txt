This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading, and utility and roadway construction for Road “C” 
Phase 2 and Road “Y” Phase 1, Koa Ridge Development project from June 2, 2021 to 
May 23, 2022 in accordance with our revised fee proposal dated February 26, 2021. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Koa Ridge is a master-planned development project located in Central Oahu 
between Waipio and Mililani Town on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We understand the 
development will encompass roughly 770 acres and is planned for residential, commercial, 
educational, and recreational uses. The Road “C” Phase 2 and Road “Y” Phase 1 project is 
a roadway infrastructure consisting of a total of approximately 2,500 linear feet of roadway 
planned in the southcentral portion of the development. A Project Location Map and Site 
Plans are shown on Plates 1 and 2.1 and 2.2, respectively. 
The roadway improvements will include the installation of new roadway pavements, 
concrete walkways, underground utilities, and other related site improvements for this 
roadway infrastructure project. Cuts and fills on the order of 5 feet thick or less will be 
required to achieve the design finished grades of the project. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading and utility and roadway construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY & ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading and utility and roadway construction work for the 
Road “C” Phase 2 and Road “Y” Phase 1, Koa Ridge Development project from 
June 2, 2021 to May 23, 2022.  The site grading work consisted of cuts and fills on the 
order of 5 feet thick or less to achieve the finished grades for the project. Royal 
Contracting Company Ltd. performed the site grading and utility and roadway construction 
work. During the site grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and utility and roadway construction operations, periodic 
field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.24 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations 
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and utility and roadway construction 
work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.
