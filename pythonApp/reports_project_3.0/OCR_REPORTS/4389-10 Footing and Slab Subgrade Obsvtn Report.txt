Footing and Slab Subgrade Observation Report
Truck Wash Facility
Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant
Dear Mr Tamayo
This letter summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the footing and slab subgrade preparation for the Truck Wash Facility of the Honouliuli
Wastewater Treatment Plant on and The footing and
slab preparation work was performed by JAS W Glover Ltd
The subject site is situated within the existing Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The treatment plant is bounded by North
Hanson Road to the west Geiger Road to the south and the Coral Creek Golf Course to the
east and north
_ The subject site is located at the southeastern corner of the plant site The subject site
is bounded by the influent pump station to the west influent screens and an activated carbon
unit to the south and the Coral Creek Golf Course to the east Based on the grading plans
provided the topography at the subject site is relatively level with ground elevations generally
ranging between 33 and 35 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
On a site visit was made by a licensed project engineer to observe
the nine 9 isolated spread footing subgrades The footing subgrades were observed to be
adequately firm The footing subgrade at the northeast corner of the sump pit structure
exposed a shallow fill layer about 6 inches thick The remaining footings exposed natural soil
material Only the northern isolated spread footings were observed to have adequate
embedment at the time of the site visit however we understand that fill will be placed to raise
the grade around the structure by about 6 to 12 inches
On a site visit was made by a field engineer to observe the slab and
thickened edge subgrades Field density tests were performed on the base course fill material
below the slab and thickened edge subgrades in general accordance with the American
Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
