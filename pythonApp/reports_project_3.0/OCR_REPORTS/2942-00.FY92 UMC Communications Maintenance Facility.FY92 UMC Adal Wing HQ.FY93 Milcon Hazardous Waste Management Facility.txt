INTRODUCTION
We have conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration in support of the
design of the subject projects This report summarizes our work on the projects and
presents our findings pertinent to the design of the site preparation and foundations for
the three 8 projects
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The work addressed in this report pertains to three 3 projects situated on
Andersen Air Force Base on the island of Guam in the Mariana Islands The following are
brief descriptions of the individual projects
FY92 UMC Communications Maintenance Facility
To provide adequate operational space and to consolidate the communications
maintenance operations at Andersen Air Force Base it is proposed to construct
an addition or extension to the existing Building 20011 and renovate space inside
of the building for communications maintenance use
The new addition or extension would consist of a single story reinforced concrete
structure providing approximately 4 200 square feet of new building space In
addition the project would provide additional paved parking areas and access for
vehicles used in conjunction with the operations
Wing Headquarters
To provide adequate operational space and to consolidate administrative and
command operations for the 633rd Air Base Wing it is proposed to construct an
addition to the existing Building 23028 and renovate space inside of the building
for command and administrative use
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2942 00 Page 3
The new addition would consist of a single story reinforced concrete structure
providing approximately 3 400 square feet of ground floor area t is our
understanding that portions of the on grade floor slab will be subject to forklift
traffic for the bulk handling of documents and records
FY93 MILCON Hazardous Waste Management Facility
The hazardous waste management program on Andersen Air Force Base consists
of the consolidation and accumulation of hazardous materials for shipment off
base Currently these activities are conducted in a converted pump house
building which does not conform to hazardous waste regulations
It is proposed to construct a new building which would be in conformance with the
appropriate standards Based on the information provided to us it is anticipated
that this building would be a single story reinforced concrete structure with slab on
grade floors It is understood that this building would be a warehouse type
structure and that the floor slab will be subject to forklift traffic
The new construction will provide approximately 2 300 square feet of storage area
and will include paved parking and traffic areas around the building

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
Guam is the southernmost of the Mariana Island archipelago which is an island arc
parallelling the trend of the subduction zone forming the Mariana Trench
The base of the island was formed during the Eocene Epoch by the eruption of
basaltic rhyolite lavas forming a volcano which is now the southern portion of the island
On the northern flank of this volcanic massif during the Oligocene and Miocene Epochs
a complex of coral and algal reefs built an elongated platform of limestones and related
deposits Tectonic activity has lifted these coral algal limestones above sea level to form
the northern Guam Plateau
Weathering and solution working of the limestones has partially recrystallized the
calcium carbonate of the limestones forming massive limestones in some areas and
poorly to well cemented detrital limestones in others Typically the detrital limestones are
highly variable in strength characteristics and are frequently karstic or cavernous in
nature
The project site are situated in a relatively level portion of the northern plateau
which forms the main portion of Andersen Air Force Base Our exploration at the three
3 sites generally encountered relatively massive reef limestone overlain by a thin layer
of detrital coral formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2942 00 Page 7
Typically the surface of the underlying limestone is irregular due to solution
working Frequently formations such as those encountered at the project sites will have
buried pinnacles of massive limestone with spaces between the pinnacies filled with
weathered material or detrital material
As previously discussed Guam is situated adjacent to the subduction zone which
forms the Mariana Trench The island is tectonically active and has experienced
approximately 20 earthquakes exceeding 7 0 since 1902 Of these events 15 occurred
prior to 1950 and five have occurred subsequent to that date
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the three 3 sites were explored by the visual and
textural examination of the materials encountered during the excavation of fourteen 14
shallow test pits
In general the test pits encountered a thin surface layer of topsoil fill usually less
than 0 5 foot followed by detrital coral formation consisting of weakly to moderately
cemented coarse sandy gravel with some silt This material has a porous structure and
is friable i e it breaks down to a sandy gravel when excavated
Underlying the detrital coral formation the test pits encountered coral algal reef
limestone formation This material is strong and presented difficult excavation for the
backhoe Refusal to excavation was encountered at depths ranging from about two 2
to five 5 feet below the ground surface at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in Appendix A of this
report

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings development of the proposed project sites is feasible
provided that our recommendations are followed The limestone formations encountered
at the project sites should provide adequate bearing support for the proposed structures
Spread or strip footings may be used to support the proposed structures and
should bear on the upper detrital limestone after the subgrade has been scarified and
recompacted in accordance with the recommendations presented herein As an alternate
the foundations may bear on new structural fill provided that the fill is select granular
material placed and compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein
site Preparation
Detailed grading plans for the proposed projects were not available prior to our
field exploration However it is anticipated that site preparation at each site will consist
of minor grading as follows
The Communications Maintenance Facility will require shallow cuts of up to about
three 3 feet in depth to lower the grade to match the grade of the existing
Building 20011 and parking lot
The Wing Headquarters will require little or no site grading work to match the
existing building area
The Hazardous Waste Management Facility will require fill ranging from less than
one 1 foot to about four 4 feet in thickness to create a level building area
matching the grade of the adjacent road

The following recommendations are general guidelines for the design of the
grading and for the development of general specifications
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of existing structures foundations stockpiled materials all
vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material snould
be disposed of properly off site
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
overexcavated to firm natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Following the clearing of the site and backfilling of any overexcavated areas areas
to receive fill should be scarified brought to near optinum moisture content and
proofrolled with a heavy vibratory roller for a minimum of four 4 passes Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during proofrolling operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted engineered fill Upon completion of the proofrolling
operations the area should be further compacted if necessary to obtain a
minimum of 95 percent maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
In general excavated on site soil materials should be suitable as fill material
Generally the on site coral materials reduced to less then three 3 inches in
maximum size should be suitable for use as engineered fill
Imported material if required should generally consist of well graded coral gravel
having no particles or fragments greater than three 3 inches in maximum
dimension with no more than 10 percent passing the 200 sieve Imported

material should be free of decomposable organic matter and other deleterious
materials
Boulders or cobbles larger than three 3 inches in maximum dimension should be
screened or removed from the excavated material and either disposed of off site
or reduced in size to less than three 8 inches
The excavated coral material should be adequate as a source of trench and
structural backfill if needed Trench and structural backfill should not contain any
rocks or lumps larger than three 3 inches in maximum dimension
Generally fills required for site grading at any of the three 3 sites should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding nine 9 to ten 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one

formation to lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The filling
operations should always start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for
fill placement
Trench and structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Slopes
Design cut slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are
recommended
Any fill slopes required should be designed at ratios of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V or flatter and should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to
the design slope ratio Care should be exercised to avoid overcutting the slopes in order
to avoid the placement of sliver fills on the slope faces in an attempt to repair the overcut
area
It is anticipated that materials used to construct fill slopes or encountered in cut
slopes will generally consist of granular materials It is recommended that whenever
possible slope faces be redressed with a vibratory drum roller to densify any material
disturbed when the slopes are cut back
The project sites are located in an area with a relatively high rainfall during the rainy
season It is recommended that the slopes be protected by diverted drainage away from
slopes and by seeding or planting as soon as practicable

Foundation Design
Based on our field exploration it is anticipated that some of the foundations will
span from cut to fill or from detrital coral formation to the reef limestone In order to
provide uniform support for the foundations we recommend that the foundations
subgrades be scarified to a depth of twelve 12 inches below the bottom of the footings
and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on the coral materials which have been recompacted or on new fill
which has placed and compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein This value may be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
The footings should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent
grade
For the Wing Headquarters building an allowable bearing capacity of 4 000 p s f
may be utilized for existing foundations provided that these foundations were embedded
into the detrital coral formation underlying the site
A coefficient of 0 4 may be used to calculate the frictional resistance between the
concrete of the foundations and the underlying subgrade materials
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2942 00 Page 13
Lateral loads may be resisted by a passive pressure of 300 pounds per square foot
of depth of the buried foundation faces Except in paved areas or slab areas the upper
one foot of embedment should not be included for development of passive resistance
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of proposed buildings on the project site will be
concrete slabs on grade It is also anticipated that some of the slabs will be subject to
vehicular traffic e g forklifts and therefore will function as rigid pavements Pavements
will be discussed in a subsequent section
Building slabs and their cushions may be supported by the upper fill provided the
subgrade is proofrolled and recompacted as recommended above A four inch layer of
clean coral gravel generally conforming to ASTM Standard C 33 No 67 gradation should
be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be considered below the slabs
provided an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing

Cavity Detection
As discussed above the site is situated in a karstic coral algal limestone terrain
Subsurface cavities and other voids are commonly encountered in coral formations To
reduce the possibility of foundation or structural settlements resulting from the collapse
of such voids we recommend that a program of probing and grouting the footing
locations be considered for the project
The probes should be drilled at each column location and on ten foot centers for
line footings The probe holes should be a minimum of three 8 inches in diameter and
should extend a minimum of ten 10 feet below the footing subgrade The holes should
be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low pressures
If a cavity or void is suspected at a footing location additional probes should be
drilled to attempt to delineate the extent of the suspected void
In order to avoid having to drill the probes inside the footing excavations which
would increase drilling costs it is suggested that the probing and grouting work be
performed after either site clearing or site grading but prior to the commencement of
foundation excavation work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2942 00 Page 16
The probing and grouting operations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer to see that the above recommendations are properly implemented and to
evaluate other solutions to backfill potential voids

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the sitework excavation and
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Sieve Analysis tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are performed to determine the
grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for classification in
the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other engineering
properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 2 through B 4
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B 1
