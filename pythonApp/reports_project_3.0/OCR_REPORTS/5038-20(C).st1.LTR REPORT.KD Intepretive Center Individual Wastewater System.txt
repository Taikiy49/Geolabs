This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed for the
Individual Wastewater System at the Interpretive Center for Kaupulehu Development
project
Project Considerations
The Kaupulehu Development project site is located about 9 miles northeast of
Keahole Airport in Kaupulehu in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii As
part of the development we understand that an individual wastewater system will be
required for the restroom facilities for the Interpretive Center
We understand that the State of Hawaii Department of Health requires
performance of a percolation test to evaluate the suitability of the site location for use of
an individual wastewater system Therefore it was desired to conduct field percolation
tests at the leaching field location
Percolation Test
Two field percolation tests were performed at the proposed absorption bed area
for the Interpretive Center The field percolation tests were performed in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of
wastewater effluent
The percolation tests were performed in a 4 inch diameter hole of about 8 8 to
10 8 feet in depth Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the
boreholes to protect the bottom of the boreholes from scouring and sediments During
