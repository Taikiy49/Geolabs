SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the Koa Ridge Off Site Sewer extending from Kamehameha Highway in
Waipio to the Waipahu Waste Water Pump Station WWPS on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations based
on our review and analyses of historical information the site conditions and the site
reconnaissance conducted for this project The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the proposed construction of an off site sewer line to serve
the Koa Ridge development in the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
proposed sewer line will extend from Kamehameha Highway near its intersection with
the Mililani Cemetery Road to the existing Waipahu WWPS on Waipahu Depot Road
as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The proposed alignment is
approximately 18 950 feet in length and traverses from Kamehameha Highway across
the Central Oahu Regional Park along Kipapa Gulch to the current northern terminus of
Paiwa Street The proposed sewer line then goes down Paiwa Street to Koaki Street
turning down Koaki Street to Kopake Street and then turning down Puko Street to
Mokuola Street lt then follows Mokuola Street to its intersection with
Farrington Highway and proceeds down Farrington Highway to Waipahu Depot Road
and down Waipahu Depot Road to the Waipahu WWPS
Further details of the proposed sewerline such as profile and structures were
not available at the time this report was prepared

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the
weathered remnants of two extinct shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Koolau is
the younger of the two and forms the eastern two thirds of the island
After the cessation of Waianae s shield building activity lava flows from Koolau
continued to bank against Waianae s eastern flank forming a broad plateau between the
two 2 volcanoes This plateau is referred to as the Schofield Plateau which consists
of a thick sequence of residual soils and saprolite over the parent rock After the
cessation of the main shield building phase of Koolau erosion deposited alluvial
materials on the lower flanks of the volcano
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciations in the continental areas of the world The higher sea
level stands caused the accumulation of sediments in the heads of the old bays
accumulation of alluvial sediments and reef deposits at corresponding higher elevations
and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The
lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits
In the Waipahu area this series of glacial fluctuations was partly responsible for the
creation of the West Loch of Pearl Harbor When sea level stabilized at its current
stand there was rapid accumulation of soft sediments in the head of the Loch
Agricultural development and urbanization of the Waipahu area during the
20 Century resulted in the placement of man made fills in low and marshy areas The
base of the stratigraphic section along the proposed alignment is the Koolau Basalts
The soils along the proposed alignment have been classified by the
U S Department of Agriculture as Molokai silty clay loam 3 to 7 percent slopes MuB
Waipahu silty clay 6 to 12 percent slopes WzC Waipahu silty clay 0 to 2 percent

2 2 Site Description
The project site is located in the Waipio Waipahu area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii As described above the proposed alignment traverses from Kamehameha
Highway across the Central Oahu Regional Park along Kipapa Gulch to the current
northern terminus of Paiwa Street The proposed sewer line then goes down Paiwa Street
to Koaki Street turning down Koaki Street to Kopake Street and then turning down
Puko Street to Mokuola Street It then follows Mokuola Street to its intersection with
Farrington Highway and proceeds down Farrington Highway to Waipahu Depot Road and
down Waipahu Depot Road to the Waipahu WWPS The site and its immediate
surroundings are shown on the Site Plans Plate 2 1 and 2 2
At the time of our reconnaissance the portion of the alignment between
Kamehameha Highway and the end of Paiwa Street was either vacant with no structures
or developed infrastructure or was being developed as part of the Central Oahu Regional
Park In the vacant portion the surface of the site was vegetated with haole koa grasses
and other vegetation typical to abandoned pineapple and sugar cane fields The
remainder of the alignment is along existing streets in suburban residential and
commercial areas It was noted that there are two golf course cart path culvert crossings
under Paiwa Street for the Waikele Golf Course In addition it was noted that the
unnamed street or driveway from Koaki Street running parallel to Paiwa Street has a large
retaining wall structure supporting Paiwa Street Details of the wall construction are not
known

Residual Soils
Relatively competent reddish brown to brownish red clayey silts and silty clays
derived from the in situ weathering of basaltic rock These soils are generally 20 or
more feet in thickness and grade with depth to saprolitic soils Isolated remnant
boulders may be encountered in deeper excavations
These residual soils generally have low shrink swell potential and have good
engineering properties They extend along the proposed alignment from
Kamehameha Highway to about Koaki Street
Older Alluvial Soils
Relatively competent light brown to dark brown silty clays with occasional sand and
gravel interbeds These soils are generally 20 or more feet in thickness and overlie
residual soils or basalt bedrock There is a potential for encountering indurated
zones during excavation
These older alluvial soils have moderate to high shrink swell potential and have
good to fair engineering properties In their undisturbed state they have good
bearing capacity but the shrink swell potential limits their use as backfill and for
pavement support They generally extend along the alignment from Koaki Street to
about Kauolu Place
Recent Alluvial Soils
The soils are found in the lower portions of the proposed alignment between
Kauolu Place and the existing Waipahu WWPS They range from medium stiff dark
brown silty clays to very soft black organic silts In the upper part of this portion of
the alignment these recently deposited soils are generally mantled by a surface
crust of man made fill ranging in thickness from about 5 to 10 feet which was
placed to stabilize these soils for agricultural and suburban development As the
alignment proceeds down Waipahu Depot Road to the WWPS this surface crust
thins to about a foot or less

These soft alluvial soils have very poor engineering properties and are not suitable
for structural support or for use as fill or backfill In the lowest portion of the
alignment it is anticipated that these soft soils may be under consolidated and that

The proposed sewer alignment overlies the Waipahu sector of the Pearl Harbor

WWPS groundwater is estimated to occur at very shallow depths on the order of less
than 5 feet below ground surface

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our reconnaissance the project site is underlain by three distinct sets
of subsurface conditions Each of these sets presents unique considerations for the
design and construction of the proposed sewer line
Based on the type of project being proposed i e a sewer line the principal
geotechnical engineering considerations include excavation excavation support pipe
installation pipe bedding or support backfill and dewatering where applicable The
following provide discussions of those considerations for each of the sets of subsurface
conditions
3 1 Residuals Soils
As described above the soils in the portion of the proposed alignment between
Kamehameha Highway and the area of Koaki Street consist of relatively competent
soils It is anticipated that conventional cut and cover trench excavation and pipe
installation may be used in this portion of the alignment While excavation support is
the responsibility of the contractor and must comply with applicable safety requirements
itis anticipated that no special trench support efforts will be required
For the portion of the alignment between the terminus of Paiwa Street and
Koaki Street consideration could be given to the use of trenchless construction
techniques such as horizontal directional drilling micro tunneling or pipe jacking due
to the extensive suburban development and congested traffic in this area Trenchless
methods may be required for the golf course cart path culvert crossings under Paiwa
Street at Waikele Golf Course If trenchless methods are used for this project
consideration should be given to the potential of occasionally encountering residual
boulders particularly if the pipe is installed at depths of more than about 10 feet below
the existing ground surface

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
For this portion of the project a conventional pipe bedding or cushion of about
6 inches of clean granular material should provide adequate support of the new sewer
line
In general the excavated material should be adequate for use as backfill
material Backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction Backfill material above 2 feet from finished
subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture
conditioning of the fill materials is an integral part of the recommendations provided
herein Compaction of the fill should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory
rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
It should be noted that even with proper moisture conditioning and compaction
there is a tendency for utility trenches to settle after construction Therefore
consideration should be given to the use of controlled low strength material CLSM as
backfill in high traffic areas or deep trenches The use of CLSM would reduce the
potential for future settlements and expedite construction work by reducing the need to
leave the trench open for backfilling and compaction
In general it is anticipated that dewatering will not be required in this portion of
the alignment

3 3 Recent Alluvial Soils
As described above the portion of the proposed alignment from about
Kauolu Place to the existing Waipahu WWPS is dominated by soft soils and a high
groundwater table Conventional cut and cover trench excavation and pipe installation
could be used in this portion of the alignment however the soft soils and high
groundwater will make for difficult working conditions Excavation support is the
responsibility of the contractor and must comply with applicable safety requirements It
is anticipated that special trench support efforts will be required to stabilize the trench
walls and to reduce the potential of ground subsidence adjacent to the trench
For this portion of the project due to the soft soil conditions and high water table
special pipe bedding such as over excavation and replacement will most likely be
required for support of the new sewer line In the very soft area along Waipahu Depot
Road ground stabilization or structural pile supports may be required Flexible pipe or
special pipe connections may be required due to the possible on going areal ground
settlements
For this portion of the alignment consideration could be given to the use of
trenchless construction techniques such as_ horizontal directional drilling
micro tunneling or pipe jacking due to the extensive suburban development and
congested traffic as well as the poor subsurface conditions in this area However due
to the very soft soils present ground stabilization by jet grouting will probably be
required to stabilize the soft soils below the pipe Jet grouting would be costly since the
thickness of the soft soils may be on the order of 100 feet below Farrington Highway
The presence of artesian conditions would be problematic for jet grouting operations
Therefore it is critical to determine the presence or absence of artesian conditions
