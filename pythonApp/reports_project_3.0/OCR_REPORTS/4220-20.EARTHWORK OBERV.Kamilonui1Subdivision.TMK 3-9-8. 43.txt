BACKGROUND INFORMATION
The project site is located at the base of the eastern flank of Kaluanui Ridge near
the mouth of Kamilonui Valley in the Hawaii Kai area of east Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The subdivision development is located along the western side of the
planned Hawaii Kai Drive Extension at the northern end of the existing Hawaii Kai Drive
The parcel encompasses approximately 17 4 acres in land area and is situated at the
base of the western hillside The site is generally covered with a relatively heavy growth
of trees and grass
The residential project involved the development of the property into a
subdivision with sixty 60 single family house lots The buildings are two stories high
and constructed of light gauge metal framing with concrete slab on grade In addition
paved access roadways and infrastructure were installed to serve the new residential
development A schematic site plan of the residential development is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1

Based on the existing topography and grading design fills generally up to about
10 feet thick along the eastern portion of the site and cuts of over 20 feet deep into the
existing western hillside were performed to create a relatively level area for the residential
development In addition CRM and Keystone retaining walls were constructed along the
Hawaii Kai Drive Extension and within the project site to provide the necessary grade
separation for the development
Since the project site is located along the lower eastern flank of Kaluanui Ridge
it may be subjected to falling rocks boulders similar to other sites bordering ridge slope

EARTHWORK OPERATONS
The site grading work and infrastructure installation were performed by
Royal Contracting Company and RHS Lee Inc The earthwork operations consisted of
clearing and grubbing of the existing ground cutting of the western hillside
over excavation of the expansive adobe clays preparation of slab and pavement
subgrades placement and compaction of non expansive capping fill and base course
excavation and placement of non expansive select granular fill behind retaining structures
and new fill slopes and utility trench excavation and backfill It should be noted that field
observations of the building foundation excavation and preparation work were performed
by others and were specifically excluded from our scope of work for this project
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to provide 3 feet of non expansive select granular fill
material capping fill at the building pad footprints and extending 2 feet beyond The
3 foot non expansive select granular capping fill was reduced to 12 inches in areas where
basalt rock formation was exposed at the slab subgrade level In general the
over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to about 85 to 90 percent of the maximum dry
density During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The maximum
dry density of the materials referred to in this report were determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
The existing ground at the access roads were generally over excavated where
necessary to provide 6 inches of base course over 36 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material above the in situ adobe clay soils In areas where basalt rock

was provided between the Asphalt Concrete AC paving and the underlying basalt rock

Based on our field observations and laboratory test results performed on the
coralline materials we have eliminated the 36 inch non expansive select granular fill below
the base course layer along this section of the roadway
The proposed cut slope along the western hillside was originally designed with a
slope ratio of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V Based on the generally competent
condition of the basalt rock formation exposed at the cut face we have recommended
that the majority of the cut slope be steepened from 1H 1V to The top 5 to
10 feet of soil cover above the rock excavation was flattened to 2H 1V to reduce the
potential for future erosion of the soil cover In addition the cut slope to the south of Lot
No 1 was revised from 1H 1V to 2H 1V based on the exposed soil conditions at the
slope face In addition adobe clays encountered behind new fill slopes were removed
to a minimum of 10 feet from the finish slope face and replaced with non expansive
select granular material
In accordance with our recommendations non expansive select granular fill was
provided a minimum of 10 feet behind the new retaining walls constructed at the site In
addition a 2 foot thick non expansive select granular fill was provided below the new
retaining wall footings and the underlying in situ adobe clay soils
New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new residential development In general a bedding layer
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B
Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on
the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Where

During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
It should be noted that the 3 foot non expansive select granular layer underneath the
building pad and pavement areas was generally constructed of the 3 inch minus crushed
rock materials generated from the hillside excavation through on site crushing operations
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 1174 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the Nuclear Probe Method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 63 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and
construction plans These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated March 15 2002
and subsequent recommendations
HOUSE LOT IMPROVEMENT CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
We strongly recommend that a competent geotechnical and or structural engineer
be retained by the individual lot owner to provide professional consultation services in the
planning design and construction of any future lot grading and house improvements
such as extension of the dwelling addition of gazebo or pavilion retaining wall and
swimming pool etc The geotechnical engineering exploration and slope stability
assessment reports prepared for the development should be made available to the lot
owner for his her consultants use in the planning and design of the lot grading and
planned improvements The retained geotechnical engineer should review our reports to
ensure his her design of the planned improvements or lot grading would not jeopardize the
stability of the existing fill slopes and Keystone retaining walls

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
