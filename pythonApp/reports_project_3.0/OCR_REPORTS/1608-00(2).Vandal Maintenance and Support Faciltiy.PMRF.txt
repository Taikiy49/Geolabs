Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C102 85
Vandal Maintenance and Support Facility
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Vandal Maintenance and Support Facility at the
Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai
Hawaii The general location of the project site and vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Vandal Maintenance and Support Facility includes an insulated
40 by 100 foot pre engineered building Total weight of the building is
anticipated to be approximately 12 kips Minimal site grading is
anticipated for the construction of the building
W O Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the soil underlying the proposed
facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located approximately 70 feet to the east of
Building 412 at the Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on
the island of Kauai Hawaii

The site is open and unoccupied in an area measured at about 40 by
100 feet The area to the east of the site is currently used as
temporary storage yard Ground level at the site is generally flat
However the surface elevation within the site is not available at this
stage
Ground cover consists of some dry grass and silty fine sand A
section of an abandoned A C paved area was encountered near the south
side of the site
Subsoil Condition
Two borings to depth of 20 feet below the ground surface were
drilled Both borings encountered a thin top soil of dark brown silty
fine sand underlaid by medium dense to dense beach sand with medium grain
size Groundwater was encountered at 12 to 14 feet below ground surface
at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and the light building
load we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing
on the recompacted in situ medium dense to dense sand may be used
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O Page 4
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the maintenance and support
building can be obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance
A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the medium dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive resistance
Site Grading
Due to the relatively flat ground minimal cut and or fill are
anticipated for the site development The finished grade level is not
available at this stage
GEOLABS HAWAIli
W O Page 5
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
vegetation structures debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
If cut is required excavation in sand should be easy
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be kept
moist and tamped prior to placement of the footing if they are loosened
during the excavation The areas which are to receive pavement should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation
moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to
obtain a firm subgrade Conventional density testing with the Sand Cone
Method may not be practical for the beach sand deposit
Loose spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with compacted on site sandy soil
If fill is required the area to receive fill after clearing and
grubbing should be scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots encountered
should be removed and replaced with select on site soil or with suitable
imported material compacted to the above mentioned standard

The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density If
the on site sandy soil is used the compaction requirement should be
increased to 100 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density for the top 6 inches
below the subgrade elevation for the areas which are to receive pavement
Pavement
Depending on the traffic conditions the following pavement section
recommendation may be used for preliminary design purpose
Rigid Pavement No Vehicular Traffic
4 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Flexible Pavement Light Traffic and Parking Area
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such

require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore

properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
