INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the existing residence located at Highway
in the Nanakuli district of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the current findings from our preliminary field exploration
and laboratory testing It presents our geotechnical engineering observations and
preliminary recommendations which were derived from our analyses of the data obtained
from the limited field exploration and laboratory testing of a few selected soil samples
These preliminary recommendations are presented in order to provide the client with
remedial solution options for his consideration and cost comparison They may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction repair plans nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Highway in the district of
Nanakuli on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided to us various
structural distresses have been observed at the subject residence It was proposed to
conduct a geotechnical engineering exploration in an attempt to evaluate the underlying
cause s for the observed phenomena and to consider remedial recommendations
pertaining to the existing problems at the subject residence
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the existing
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop a soils rock data set for
structural distress problems at the subject residence

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Highway in the district of
Nanakuli of Oahu Hawaii The subject residence is located on the mauka side of
Farrington Highway and is the innermost structure of a group of three with similar type
of construction and appearance The subject residence consisted of a wood framed
single story structure with slab on grade construction and a detached carport The terrain
at the subject lot is relatively level with most of the yard area covered with a blanket of
basalt gravel It is our understanding that the rear unit area has been filled up several feet
above the surrounding backyard area
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance of the project site the following structural
distresses could be observed at the subject residence
1 Large cracks could be observed in the masonry block at the top of the front
column supporting the garage roof In addition the garage columns
appeared to be tilting and are out of plumb

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicated that the subject slab on grade residence is underlain
by approximately 3 to 4 feet of highly plastic clay soils at shallow depths Further
discussion with the property owner indicated that these highly plastic clay soils were
probably fill material imported to the project site to raise the previous lowlying ground
surface to its present grades
These clay soils were classified as CH according to the Unified Soil Classification
System locally referred to as Adobe Clay Laboratory tests performed on these abode
soils indicate that the material exhibits a very high shrink swell potential when subjected
to moisture fluctuations and will lose considerable shear strength when saturated We
believe that the structural distresses exhibited at the subject residence could be a result
of this shrink swell behavior of the adobe soils encountered at shallow depths below the
on grade slab and foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3145 00 Page 7
Long Term Solution
Since the structural distresses exhibited at the project site appear very likely to be
attributed by the high shrink swell behavior of the underlying adobe soils The moisture
contents of the underlying expansive adobe soil are in the range below the saturated soil
water contents It is believed that additional swelling and shrinkage could still occur in the
future if subjected to more water infiltration or soil wetting It is our opinion that a more
assured long term solution to the problem would be to demolish the existing structures
complete removal of the underlying expansive adobe soils below the slab and
replacement with compacted non expansive fill materials An alternative to slab on grade
design would be the use of individual post and beam construction with individual post
footings embedded in the underlying coral formation located below the imported adobe
clay fill layer This is discussed in more detail in the following paragraphs
New Slab On Grade Reconstruction
All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials resulting from the
demolition of the existing residence should be removed and disposed of properly
off site Slabs and foundations of the existing structures should also be completely
removed to expose the underlying adobe soils The adobe soils encountered
within the new building and garage limits and at least 2 feet beyond should be
excavated to expose the underlying coralline deposits Based on our field
exploration results we anticipate that approximately 4 feet of over excavation of the
surficial expansive soils would be required The bottom of the excavation should
be proof rolled using a smooth drum roller and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material The excavated adobe soils
should be properly disposed of off site prior to the hauling of imported fill material
to reduce potential contamination of the imported fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3145 00 Page 8
Imported fill material needed to backfill the above over excavation and to raise the
ground surface to the proposed finish grades should consist of granular material
such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill material should contain a
maximum of 20 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
Fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
New Post And Beam Construction
An alternate solution to reconstruction of the demolished residence with slab on
grade design is the use of post and beam construction with individual spread
post footings bearing on the underlying coral formation However the garage
slab may still require adobe subgrade soil removal and replacement with select
borrow fill

Restoration of Structural Appearance
It should be realized that due to the presence of existing house structure and utility
systems located in the subject lot major remedial work would be difficult costly and
would create considerable inconvenience for the tenants It is our opinion that existing
structural distresses such as settlement of the floor slab may be restored to its structural
appearance by a process termed slab jacking
grout is pumped beneath the slab under pressure to produce a lifting force which literally
floats the slab to the desired position The grout is introduced through small holes drilled
through the concrete Slab jacking has the attendant feature of some chemical
stabilization of the soil which helps preclude future differential movement produced by
moisture variations Since it is not practical to remove the underlying adobe clay soils
it is desirable to alter the in situ soil s expansive behavior by the use of soil stabilizing
chemicals through the slab jacking operation The use of hydrated lime to reduce the
swelling potential of the expansive soils is common Its shortcomings lies in the fact that __
or saturation This stems largely from the facts that lime is sparsely soluble in water and

the clay soil needing stabilization is both impermeable and heterogeneous Therefore the
use of soil stabilizing chemicals may help to abate the swelling potential of the adobe soils
but the limitations in its practical application may significantly reduce its effectiveness
Since the amount of upheaval settlement and the load distribution is not uniform
across the entire site particular care and a well prepared approach must be exercised
to avoid shearing or unevenly raising the house structure We recommend that a detailed
topographic survey should be performed for the existing floor slab to obtain additional
data for further evaluation prior to making a decision Other cosmetic repairs may then
be taken to restore the residence to its architectural appearance
Cut Off Curtain Wall and Other Considerations
It should be noted that the foundation and cosmetic repairs discussed above are
to restore the structural and architectural appearance If the initial cause of the problem
is not identified and eliminated the problem is likely to recur
Since the structural distresses observed at the subject residence are a result of the
shrinkage and or swelling behavior of the underlying adobe soils when subjected to
moisture fluctuations it is important that additional remedial measures aiming at stabilizing
the moisture content of the adobe soils thus reducing the potential for further shrinkage
and or swelling should be implemented It should be understood that as long as the
underlying expansive clay is not removed a potential for future structural distress still
exists although the following measures would minimize any external source of moisture
change For such a purpose we recommend that the following steps be taken
1 A buried cut off curtain wall should be provided along the perimeter of the
subject residence to reduce the entry and escape of moisture into and out

wide and extend to a depth of at least 6 inches into the underlying coralline
deposits The adobe clay surface exposed during the wall construction

The existing perimeter sidewalk as well as the patio slab at the backyard
should be demolished for the curtain wall construction Where the subject
residence is abutted against the adjacent structure the curtain wall should
be extended a minimum of 5 feet along the adjacent building outline All
joints between the exterior wall and the curtain wall and concrete sidewalks
should be sealed water tight
A high density impervious plastic membrane could be considered over the
above installed cut off concrete curtain wall and it should extend out several
feet away from the slab perimeter The membrane will serve to reduce the
infiltration of surface runoff into the underlying adobe soils and should be
sloped away from the house foundation for proper drainage
Landscape planting should be avoided around or near the repaired curtain
wall slab area to minimize water infiltration toward the adobe clay subgrade
soils
A gutter system should be installed around the residence and the discharge
be diverted away from the foundation and slab areas These drainage
requirements are essential for the proper performance of the foundations
and slabs since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of four
borings to depths ranging from 4 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
portable drilling equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered
are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
