INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kunia Medical Office Complex project located in the District of
Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation and site grading only
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located between Kupuna Loop Kupuohi Street and
Kunia Road in the District of Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project will
include three two story steel framed office structures and a two story concrete parking
structure with one level below grade A retaining wall is planned adjacent to Kupuna
Loop with on grade parking and landscaping covering the remainder of the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed office complex To accomplish
this our exploration program consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and two
operators to and from the site
2 Drilling and sampling of six borings to depths of approximately 15 to
16 3 feet below the existing ground surface for the three office buildings
three borings to depths of approximately 20 feet for the parking structure
and one boring to a depth of 10 feet for the retaining wall

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located Both the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3835 00 Page 4
Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their original shield outlines and are now
long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the
Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In
general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed Kunia Medical Office Complex project is located in the District of
Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Kupuna Loop
Kupuohi Street and Kunia Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Presently the lot is vacant and bounded by trees shrubs and chain linked fences

Based on our field observations and a review of the Topographic Survey Map

from north to south with existing ground surface elevations of approximately 247 to

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain by
very stiff to hard silty and clayey saprolite overlaying highly weathered basalt formation
extending to the maximum depths drilled of about 10 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the very stiff to hard near surface soils may be used to
support the proposed structures
Our laboratory test results indicate that the near surface soils have a low potential
for shrink swell when subjected to changes in the moisture content Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements presented in the
Slabs On Grade section of this report to reduce the potential shrink swell effects on floor
slabs

We anticipate minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less
than 3 feet will be needed to provide level areas for buildings Also excavations of up to
about 15 feet may be necessary for the below grade parking structure

and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of

Foundations
The proposed facility will consist of three two story steel framed office structures
and a two story concrete parking structure with one level below grade Based on the
subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed structures
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footings bearing on the stiff near surface saprolite This allowable bearing pressure is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the total footing
settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with
differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3835 00 Page 7
footings embedded in the saprolite These values assume that the concrete for footings
is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below the finished grade should be
neglected
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead weight of
the structure 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable and
3 side shear between the sides of the footing and surrounding soil Contribution to uplift
resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be estimated using a
unit weight of 105 p c f For side shear between the sides of the footings and the
adjacent soils a shear value of 150 p s f may be used to resist uplift loads
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings are
to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should extend to
a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the
below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the
GEOLABS HAWAII

footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed office
buildings The near surface soils encountered at the proposed site consist of silty and
clayey saprolitic soils which exhibit a low expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore we recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared and kept
moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete The subgrades for slabs on
grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
To provide a more uniform support under slabs we recommend that the concrete
slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer
should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill
should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the
existing subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture
sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the
cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In
order to promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the
vapor membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above
the membrane and moistened slightly prior to placing the slab concrete

Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minor grading with fills on the order of less than 3 feet and
excavations of up to about 15 feet relative to the existing ground surface for the proposed
development
Site grading operations should be observed by a _ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3835 00 Page 11
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the on grade parking and driveway
areas the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to
placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The on site silty and clayey soils generated from the excavation may be reused as
a source of fill and backfill materials The on site silty and clayey soils should be properly
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3835 00 Page 12
Permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched
If imported fill materials are required it should consist of well graded granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more and should have a
maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing
the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use Imported materials should be brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Excavation
For construction of the two story parking structure with one level below grade we
anticipate that excavations on the order of about 15 feet below the existing ground
surface will be required In general the method and equipment to be used for excavation
should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety consider
ations In general our field exploration encountered very stiff to hard clayey silt saprolite
grading to highly weathered basalt with depth We anticipate that conventional
excavation techniques may be used for the proposed excavation However it should be
noted that hard layers and localized hard zones may be encountered occasionally in the
proposed excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site was underlain by very stiff to hard
saprolitic soils Therefore if construction space is available it is our opinion that an open
cut may be used for the excavation of the proposed structure A slope inclination of
1H 1V or flatter may be used for the temporary cut

If the open cut method is not practical for the proposed excavation we
recommend that a temporary shoring system be used for the proposed excavation The
temporary shoring should conform to the requirements of local state and federal regula

Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment traffic or sloping ground conditions
adjacent to the excavations should be added to the design lateral earth pressures
Stockpiling of excavated on site and or imported materials within a horizontal distance
equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the excavation should be avoided
Pavements
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the driveway and parking areas for the
proposed project We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We have made
our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to
the clayey silt soils encountered in the borings with a minimum CBR of 15 and swell of
less than 2 percent Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction If pumping of the subgrade
occurs during construction the subgrade should be proof rolled to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction After proof rolling a layer of filter fabric such as
Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement of the
GEOLABS HAWAII

subbase and base course The subbase and base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design section
The recommended pavement section is based on the assumption that good drainage will
be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in
or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade below the base and or subbase course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a_ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or the
on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII

content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey silt
soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

REFERENCE SITE PLAN SCHEME 3 UNDATED 51 GRAPHIC SCALE IN FEET
BY KUNIA ROAD
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling ten
borings to depths of about 10 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 10
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils Three of the four samples tested were
undisturbed while the remaining sample was remolded prior to saturation The results
are summarized on Plate B 1
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the shear strength of the material Results of the test are presented on
Plate B 2
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade support
Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 3
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the
near surface soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
