INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering expioration performed
for the Ward Village Shop project to be located to the northeast of the intersection of Auahi
and Kamakee Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 7
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing
and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only and are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Range
and its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the genesis of the
Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana area are dominated by
fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are intercalated
with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The base of the stratigraphic
section in this area is composed of Koolau Basalt
Over the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments along the coast
and in areas that were formerly valleys and drainage ways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our borings and the generally light
structural loads anticipated we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread
and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed new one to two story
building structure The new footings should bear on the recompacted granular fill materials
and should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
New footings to be located adjacent to the existing structure foundations should be
embedded to the same depths as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the
existing foundations The new footings should not be embedded by more than about
6 inches below the bottom of the existing footings to avoid undermining the existing footings
The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed in the field during
construction
It should be noted that pockets of loose fill materials may be encountered at the
footing subgrade level during construction Therefore some of the footing subgrades may
need to be over excavated by about 18 to 24 inches and replaced with compacted select
granular fill materials minimum 90 percent relative compaction depending on the subgrade
conditions exposed at the footing location If the excavations expose soft loose materials

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed building and
be supported on the re compacted on site materials In general the exposed subgrade
should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Fills
should be placed in general accordance with the recommendations provided in the Site
Preparation section of this report
We recommend that a 4 inch thick cushion fill layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more uniform support
and to serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill should be placed as soon
as possible after the scarification and compaction of the slab subgrade soils For slabs on
grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be
placed over the gravel cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration of moisture vapor
through the concrete slab Two inches of fine moist sand may be considered above the
plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete Provision of the 2 inches of moist fine sand above the vapor barrier will be left up
to the discretion of the project structural engineer Flexible floor coverings such as carpet
or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
After preparation of the slab subgrades they should be kept moist prior to placement
of cushion fill and or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be moistened to close the cracks or scarified
moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner

Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site grading on the order of 2 feet or less of new fills would
be required to achieve the design finished floor elevation for the proposed building In
general site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is
important that a representative from our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions
during excavation are similar to those encountered in our field exploration The following
recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site In addition the existing asphaltic concrete pavement within the new
building footprint and other improvements should be removed to expose the underlying base
material in order to facilitate the placement of new fills
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and demolition should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material The over excavation resulting from the

clearing and demolition should be backfilled with well compacted fill material Existing utilities
were observed to traverse the proposed new building footprint Existing utilities to be
abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled
Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled
by pumping lean concrete or Controlled Low Strength Material under low pressure
After clearing and demolition finished subgrades or areas to receive fills should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In areas subjected
to vehicular traffic finished subgrades below pavement areas the compaction requirement
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density

additional cost to the owner

The on site sandy and gravelly soils generated from the on site excavations may be
re used as a source of fill and backfill materials provided that they are well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the
materials should be free of deleterious materials Clayey and silty soils resulting from the
on site excavation should not be re used as a source of fill material
It should be noted that the excavated on site soils might require aeration prior to being
re used as fill in order to achieve the required compaction due to the relatively shallow
groundwater table encountered at the project site In general on site materials to be used
as fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction In areas subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
if imported fill materials are needed the imported material should consist of
non expansive select granular fill such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand

accordance with ASTM D 1883 test procedures Imported fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for

Utility Line Trenches
We envision that some new utility lines and connections will be required for the
proposed project In addition some of the utility line trenches may extend below the
groundwater table encountered within 5 feet of the existing ground surface In general
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended below the pipes If soft loose soils are encountered at or near the invert of
the pipes an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a non woven filter fabric
Mirafi 180N or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer for more uniform
support
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for
the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes or about 12 inches above the
groundwater level to provide adequate support around the pipes Itis critical that the
free draining materials be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around the sides of the pipes Improper
backfill materials around the pipes and improper placement could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes or
groundwater level to the top of the subgrade may consist of the excavated on site soils
provided that they are free of deleterious materials Due to the relatively shallow
groundwater table the excavated on site soils may require aeration to reduce the moisture
content of the soils prior to being re used as backfill materials The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose
lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches will be located
below areas subjected to vehicular traffic the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings extending to depths of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to evaluate
the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were performed in
accordance with ASTM Test Designations C 136 and D 1140 Graphic presentation of the
grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 1
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR Test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
