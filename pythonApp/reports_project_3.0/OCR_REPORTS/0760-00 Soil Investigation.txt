The subject project site is located on Mokuhano Street about
100 feet north of its intersection with Hawai Kai Drive in
The site is bounded on the east by Mokuhano Street and on the
north by a military access road Undeveloped land is located

toward the east at less than 12 percent gradient
At the time of our field exploration the site is overgrown
with moderate to heavy growth of grass and bushes Some

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface conditions indicated that the proposed develop
ment by means of cut and fill is feasible from a soils engin
eering viewpoint
The reddish brown silty surface soil has low to moderate
expansion potential Slab on grade bearing on this silty
material should be designed for medium expansion potential
condition The underlying brown to gray sandy and gravelly
soil has low expansion potential and high CBR values which
makes it an excellent source of select capping fill layer
or to be used as a select borrow material in the roadway
construction
Site Grading
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation
Prior to the placement of compacted fill the existing ground
surface should be cleared of debris vegetation boulders
and the upper six inches of the ground surface should be
scarified brought to near optimum moisture content and then

Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundations may be utilized at the
lower level area and post footing foundation should be
utilized at the upper slope area
For the light structural loading anticipated at this project
an allowable bearing pressure of 2000 p s f may be used for
the footing bearing on the stiff on site soil or upon the
compacted fill
For slab on grade construction deep perimeter footings are
recommended around the house slabs The perimeter footings
should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the outside ground
elevation and have a minimum thickness of 9 inches A 5
bar should be placed at the top and bottom of this cut off
wall footing The subgrade soil should be compacted wet of
its optimum moisture content to a minimum of 90 of its dry
density A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided
above the moist subgrade soil and compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
For post footing foundation on slope area the bottom of the
footing should penetrate a minimum of 2 feet below the finish
grade into the stiff on site soil At steeper slope area
the footing should extend down to a level such that the
outer edge of the footing should have a minimum horizontal
set back distance of at least 5 feet out to the slope face
