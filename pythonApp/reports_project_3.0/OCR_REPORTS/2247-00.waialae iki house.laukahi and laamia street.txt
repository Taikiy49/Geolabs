In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration of the subject site The work was conducted in

recommendations
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated at the intersection of Laukahi and
Iaamia Streets on Waialae Iki Ridge Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the project site and its vicinity is shown in the Project Location Map
Plate 1
At the time of our exploration the subject lot was occupied by an
abandoned split level dwelling It is our understanding that this
abandoned structure is to be demolished Existing ground elevations on
the lot range from 222 feet above Mean Sea Level in the NE corner to 208
feet in the SW corner The lot encompasses approximately 8248 square
feet
W O 2247 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The current plan is to demolish the existing abandoned residential
structure and to construct a new two story home with a lower level garage
and an attached swimming pool The available plans indicate that the
proposed home will be of wood frame construction Concrete slabs on grade
are anticipated for the Japanese Room and the garage area
Post and beam construction with spread footing foundations supporting wood
framing and floors is anticipated for the rest of the structure A
retaining wall is anticipated to provide grade separation from the house
Retaining walls are also planned along the south and east property lines
Cuts and fills on the order of less than five feet are also anticipated
primarily in the swimming pool area
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the above referenced site to develop geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the foundations and
site grading for the proposed house and associated structures
The subsurface conditions in the lot were explored by performing
three borings to depths ranging from 8 0 to 19 8 feet below the existing
ground surface The boring logs are presented in Appendix A of this
report The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
Also included in our work on the project were laboratory testing of
selected samples obtained in the field exploration to aid in classifying
the materials encountered and in determining their engineering
properties The laboratory test results are presented in Appendix B of
this report
Engineering analyses were performed on the field and laboratory data
to formulate design recommendations pertinent to the proposed residential
development This report presents our findings and recommendations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploratory borings indicated that the site is covered with two
to two and one half feet of silty clay adobe with a moderate to high
swell potential which is underlain by approximately three to four feet of
stiff clayey silt with gravel The borings encountered rock formation at
approximately five feet below the existing ground surface No groundwater
was encountered in the borings during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
From a geotechnical engineering point of view the site is suitable
for the proposed development
The field exploration indicates that the site is underlain at shallow
depths of dense competent basalt rock formation
The underlying silt with gravel and rock materials below the surface
adobe clay layer should provide adequate bearing for the proposed house
retaining structures pavement and swimming pool
Site Grading
It is our understanding that site grading will be minimal involving
a three to four foot thick fill in the vicinity of the swimming pool and
a cut of approximately two feet below the existing subgrade to the bottom
of the pool depth of pool about six feet
Site Preparation
Prior to lot grading surface vegetation and organic material should
be grubbed and wasted off site The existing adobe surface soils below
the proposed footings slab on grade and concrete walkway or pavements
should be over excavated to expose a non expansive subgrade and competent
bearing surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2247 00 Page 4
In fairly level areas after adobe soil removal and prior to
receiving new structural backfill or fill the existing ground should be
scarified to a depth of twelve inches moistened and compacted to 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material
In general selective excavated silty gravel broken up rock and
other fragments and non expansive soils may be used as fill For fills
within three feet of finished grade below the structure and for structural
backfill the maximum size of particles in the fill material should be
three inches to facilitate compaction and decrease the potential for
forming voids
It appears imported borrow material is required and it should
consist of non expansive CBR Swell per ASTM Test Method D1883 73 less
than 1 select granular materials less than three inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Foundations
It is proposed that slabs on grade with continuous perimeter footings
be constructed for the garage and Japanese Room Post and beam spread
footings are anticipated for the remainder of the house
Slab areas should be over excavated to remove existing expansive
soils to three feet below the bottom of the slab and to two feet outside
the edges of the slab and backfilled with non expansive select soils in
six inch lifts compacted to 95 of its maximm dry density A minimm
thickened edge width of 18 inches should be utilized and the bottom of all
thickened edges should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade or bear on the underlying competent basalt rock formation
The outside edges of the slabs should be set back a minimum of 10 feet
away from the nearest adjacent slope face

We recommend that spread footing foundations should bear directly on
the underlying clinker rock formation or on non expansive borrow fill soil
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density We recommend a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches Also a set back of about eight feet
away from the nearest adjacent slope face should be observed
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated post spread footing settlements are expected to be
relatively small on the order of less than one eighth to
structural fill
Concrete Slabs On Grade
Based on the expansive subgrade soils encountered at the site
over excavation of clay soils to more competent underlying silty rocky
materials or to three feet below the bottom of the slab is recommended
The over excavated areas should be backfilled with select non expansive
borrow soils The select backfill should be placed in 8 inch lifts and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
To reduce the potential of differential settlement where the slab may
bear partly on cut in rock and partly on fill the cut portions in rock
should have a minimum 6 inch of base course cushion fill under the slab
bottom
Retaining Wall
Retaining walls with a maximm height of five feet along the south
and east property lines and with a maximum height of 10 feet in the garage
to provide grade separation from the house are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2247 00 Page 6
It is recommended that the foundations for the retaining walls extend
through the surface expansive soils to bear on the more competent
underlying clinker dense basalt or rock formation
An allowable bearing capacity up to 6 000 p s f may be utilized for
wall footings on hard basalt formations For footings bearing on
compacted select backfill a lower bearing value of 3 000 p s f should be
considered
Clean granular materials such as 3B Fine gravel should be used as
backfill directly behind the walls and the limit of excavation to reduce
the amount of compactive effort required and to facilitate drainage of the
backfill behind the wall The backfill behind the walls should be capped
with one foot of non expansive imperious silty material to reduce the
potential of water infiltration behind the wall
We recommend that these walls be designed to resist a lateral earth
pressure of 40 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depths
Weepholes or a subdrain system should be incorporated into the
retaining wall designs to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls

Swinming Pool Design
Swimming pool design and construction are the responsibility of the
swimming pool builder This contractor is also generally responsible for
quality control and performance of the completed pool If necessary the
lateral earth pressure given in the Retaining Walls sections of this
report may be given consideration in the pool design
Since expansive soil was encountered the pool and the adjacent
concrete slab walkways should be designed for potential swelling shrinkage
of the expansive clay soil conditions Our recommendations above should
be considered and incorporated in the pool construction
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork foundation recommendations given herein If this review is
not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and

to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes and or visual observation Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in
this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from property corners Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by topographic survey Lot 77 of the Waialae Iki
View Lots Unit II File Plan 749 dated The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to depths ranging between 8 0 to 19 8 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System The materials encountered in the borings are
presented on Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches and by rock coring The blow counts
used to drive the samplers for 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
