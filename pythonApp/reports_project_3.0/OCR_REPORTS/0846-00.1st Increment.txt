SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent
sandy subsoils at the site A thin layer
of adobe clay was noted in portions of
the site
The adobe soil should be removed below
foundations and pavements
Shallow foundations bearing on the in situ
dense sandy coral soils are recommended for
the classroom buildings An allowable bearing
value of 4000 p s f is recommended
The text of the report should be referred
to for detailed recommendations and special
design consideration
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed structures
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed school site is located off Lualualei Homestead
Road about 5000 feet east of Farrington Highway in Waianae
The site is bordered by Lualualei Homestead Road on the
north Waianae Kai Subdivision on the west and undeveloped
land on the south and east
The area was vacant at the time of our field exploration
and covered with Kiawe trees grass and boulders
The topography is generally a little higher near the central
portion and slopes down toward the east and southwest at
less than 5 percent gradients
An existing unlined drainage ditch about 3 to 8 feet deep
generally running in a east southwest direction was noted
near the future parking area
The surveyed elevations at the site generally varied from
about 68 to 78 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structures include two 2 story classroom
buildings approximately 32 by 120 feet in plan and a
parking area
Maximum column loads of about 70 kips are anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
_ Subsurface Exploration
Seven borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15 to
25 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendices along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the area appears to be underlain by
a coral sand formation consisting mostly of dense cemented
sand and sandy silt A thin surface layer of adobe clay
about 2 feet thick was encountered mostly in the southern
portion of the site
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent sandy subsoils
at the site A thin surface layer of adobe soil was
noted mostly in the southern portion of the area see
Site Plan
The adobe soil layer should be stripped below the
building area and for a distance of 5 feet beyond the
building perimeter and below sidewalk and pavement areas
Foundations
After removal of the adobe soil spread or continuots
footing foundations are recommended for the structures
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the dense coral sand formation
The bottoms of footings should be placed about 2 feet
below the finish grade and on dense coral formation
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be tamped
prior to concrete pouring
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Minor settlements are anticipated for the foundations
recommended above

Site Grading
Extensive grading is not planned for the project
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of all
vegetation and debris
The stripped adobe soil should not be used within
3 feet of the finish grade The adobe may be used
to fill the lower portions of the existing drainage
ditch or for landscaping
Many boulders were noted on the ground surface Due to
the low fills planned the boulders should not be used
in the fill
The on site silty sand material can be used for structural
fill The fill should be placed in 10 inch maximum hori
zontal loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM test method
D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of gravel
generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than 1 4 inch in size
The above slab on grade design requires that no adobe soil
should be used within 3 feet below the slab finish grade
For additional protection against moisture damage of the
flooring material a polyethylene moisture barrier can be
used

Parking Area
The proposed asphaltic concrete parking area will be
located next to the existing Leihoku Street
Some filling of the southern portion of the parking
area will be required
Adobe soils should not be used within the top 3 feet
of the finish parking lot grade
Based on the results of laboratory CBR test on the site
coral material the preliminary pavement design is as
follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Coral Subgrade
Soil
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised accordingly

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 7 borings to depths of about 15 to 25 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were
drilled by MAS Drilling Company at the approximate loca
tions shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content Atterberg Limit and unit weight
determinations were performed on selected samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Four sieve analyses were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the grain size characteristics of the subsoils
The test results are presented on Table 1 Plate B l
Two direct shear tests were performed to evaluate the
shear strength of the underlying soils The test results
are presented on Plates B 2 B 3
One moisture density test Modified Proctor was
performed on a bulk surface sample to evaluate the material
as a potential source of fill The moisture density curve
is plotted on Plate B 4
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed
on a bulk surface sample in the proposed parking area to
evaluate the suitability of the soil as pavement support
The test results are presented on Table 1 Plate B l
Two swell tests on one inch ring samples were conducted
on the near surface soil to measure its swell potential The
test results are summarized on Plate B 5
