PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kulani Correctional Facility which is
about 20 miles southwest of Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that the current wastewater treatment and disposal system at the
Kulani Correctional Facility includes several cesspools and septic tanks The current
wastewater treatment and disposal system does not comply with federal and state
regulations therefore a new wastewater treatment and disposal system must be
implemented at the Kulani Correctional Facility by April 2005
We understand that three types of wastewater treatment and disposal methods
were considered The three methods include a mechanical type treatment plant
non aerated type lagoon and aerated type lagoon Based on the information provided we
understand that it is proposed to construct an aerated lagoon and leach field to treat and
dispose of the wastewater generated at the project site The project site intended for the
wastewater treatment and disposal system is located about 2 000 feet to the south of the
main camp as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the proposed leach field locations and to perform field percolation
tests The subsurface information obtained was used to evaluate the subsurface
infiltration characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertaining to
the design of the wastewater disposal components of the on site wastewater disposal
system The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
and our additional fee request via facsimile dated August 31 2001
Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available in house soils and geologic information pertaining to the
project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of ten borings extending to depths of approximately
6 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface
profile at the leach field locations As part of the field exploration ten
percolation tests were performed in the borings to evaluate the subsurface
infiltration characteristics for the formulation of recommendations pertaining
to the design of the disposal components of the on site wastewater disposal
system
4 Additional field exploration including the excavation of five test pits extending
to depths of about 5 8 to 10 7 feet below the existing ground surface to
re evaluate the subsurface profile at the leach field locations and an
alternate leach field location Performance of five percolation tests to
evaluate the subsurface infiltration characteristics
5 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings and test pits
by a geologist from our firm
6 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating
their engineering properties

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the Kulani Correctional Facility which is situated
about 20 miles southwest of Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The project site is located about
2 000 feet to the south of the main camp of the correctional facility as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Presently the project site may be accessed via an existing dirt road which begins
at the main camp of the correctional facility Both the aerated lagoon and the leach field
sites are located to the east of the existing dirt road Based on the topographic map
provided and our observations at the project site the site slopes slightly down from west to
east The existing ground surface at the aerated lagoon site ranges from about Elevations
5 131 to 5 140 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The existing ground surface at the leach
field site ranges from about Elevations 5 122 to 5 138 feet MSL
We understand that the aerated lagoon and leach field sites were previously used
as a pig farm and pastureland respectively At the time of our field exploration the sites
were relatively open and bordered by fencing The aerated lagoon site was covered with
some wild grasses and the surface soils consisted of silty sands and sandy silts Two
abandoned structures were located to the north of the aerated lagoon site The leach field
site was covered with some wild grasses and the surface soils consisted of sandy silts
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling ten borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 10 extending to depths of about 6 5 to 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Subsurface exploration was concentrated at the
proposed leach field site only because our exploration was directed at evaluating the
subsurface infiltration characteristics of the near surface materials The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers In addition
five test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 were excavated to depths of about
5 8 to 10 7 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Bering Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site leach field site generally encountered a
surface layer of soft to stiff sandy silts extending to depths of about 0 5 to 2 feet below the
existing ground surface The sandy silts were underlain by very loose to loose sandy and
gravelly soils with varying amounts of silt extending to the maximum depths explored of
about 6 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our exploration
However due to the high rainfall environment at the site groundwater levels especially
perched groundwater conditions can fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal
rainfall storm surge conditions and other factors Detailed descriptions of the materials
encountered in our field exploration are presented on the attached Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 13 and on the Logs of Test Pits Plates 14 and 15
LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained
from the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered and to evaluate
the engineering properties
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 117 and C 136 were performed on selected
materials encountered in the borings to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils
and to aid in soil classification Graphic representation of the test results is provided on
Plate 16
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that the current wastewater treatment and disposal system at the
Kulani Correctional Facility does not comply with federal and state regulations Therefore
an aerated lagoon and leach field are planned to treat and dispose of the wastewater that
GEOLABS INC
DMT Consultant Engineers Page 5
W O 4300 20
is generated at the correctional facility We have conducted field percolation tests to
obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of the disposal components of the on site
wastewater disposal system A percolation test was performed at each of the ten borings
drilled The approximate locations of the field percolation tests are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 3 In addition selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration were
tested in the laboratory to evaluate the moisture and density as an aid in classifying the
materials encountered
The field percolation tests conducted in the borings were performed in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards
To evaluate the subsurface profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater
disposal the percolation tests were performed by drilling 4 inch diameter boreholes
extending to depths ranging from about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately 1 to 2 inches of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the
boreholes to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During the test
the holes were carefully filled with water to a minimum height of 6 inches above the gravel
pack The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which
calculation of the percolation rates were based The results of the individual percolation
tests are presented on Plates 17 through 31 of this report
Based on the percolation tests conducted in the borings depths of about 5 to
10 feet below the ground surface the percolation rates at the leach field site range from
about 7 5 to 333 minutes per inch with the majority of the percolation rates exceeding
60 minutes per inch The percolation tests were performed in the loose sandy and
gravelly soils with varying amounts of silt According to the Manual of Septic Tank
Practice prepared by the U S Department of Health Education and Welfare proposed
leach field locations with percolation rates exceeding 60 minutes per inch would not be
suitable for the siting of the absorption system
As mentioned previously the subsurface conditions at the leach field site generally
consisted of a surface layer of sandy silts of about 0 5 to 2 feet thick The sandy silts were
underlain by sandy and gravelly soils with varying amounts of silt Based on the
subsurface profile at the site it appears that the poor percolation rates were inconsistent
with the generally granular materials encountered in the borings It is possible that the
poor percolation rates obtained from the percolation tests in the borings resulted from
smearing of the boring sidewalls from the upper silt materials We believe that the upper
soft silty materials may have smeared sidewalls of the borings inhibiting the water to
infiltrate the pore spaces within the sandy and gravelly soils
Additional Leach Field Exploration
To confirm whether boring sidewalls were smeared causing the poor percolation
rates three test pits were excavated at the leach field site In addition two test pits were

excavated at two alternate leach field site locations The test pits were excavated with a
backhoe to extending depths of about 5 8 to 10 7 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on
our field observations during the additional field exploration the test pit sidewalls were
relatively clean and smearing of the test pits walls was not noted
After excavating the test pits a percolation test was performed at each test pit
location to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of the disposal components of
the on site wastewater disposal system The test was performed by excavating an 18 to
20 inch square pit of about 1 foot high terminating at depths ranging from about 5 8 to
10 7 feet below the existing ground surface Water was injected into the pits at with an
average flow rate ranging from about 40 to 200 gallons per minute gom Based on our
observations no head build up was observed in Test Pit Nos 1 through 4 during the
pumping duration A head build up of 2 to 7 inches was observed in Test Pit No 5
during the pumping duration The time for the 7 inches of head to seep away was about
2 3 minutes
Based on the percolation test conducted at depths of 5 8 to 10 7 feet below the
existing ground surface the percolation rates at the proposed and alternate leach field
sites are generally less than 1 minute per inch Due to the variability of the subsurface
conditions across the leach field site a percolation rate of 5 minutes per inch may be used
for design of the proposed leach field set at depths of about 7 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface Due to the variability of the subsurface conditions the absorption capacity
of the disposal system should be further confirmed by additional percolation tests during
construction
In addition based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department
of the absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high
groundwater level As mentioned previously groundwater was not encountered in our
borings or test pits at the time of our exploration However it should be noted that the
project site is located in a high rainfall environment Therefore groundwater levels can
fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or
injection and other factors

portions of the specifications Items of grading that are addressed in the succeeding

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft and or loose organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills and finished
subgrades in cut areas should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Fill and Backfill Materials
Fills and backfills to be used as general fills should consist of well graded
granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with
sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass In
general the excavated materials may be re used as a source of general fill
materials provided that the materials are processed to meet the maximum size
and gradation requirements presented above It should be noted that the surface
clayey silt soils should not be used as general fill or backfill materials
We understand that an on site stockpile materials consisting of cinder sands and
gravel may be used for fill materials We recommend that the stockpiled cinder
sands and gravel materials be used only as a source of trench backfill material

where the cinder sands and gravel will be mechanically compacted provided that
the material meets the above requirements We believe that compaction of the
cinder sands and gravel by heavy construction equipment may crush the cinder
sands and gravel reducing the particle sizes and altering the gradation of the
materials
Imported fill materials should be free of organics and deleterious materials and
should be suitable for the intended use based on the recommendations for fill
materials provided in this report In addition imported fill materials should be
observed and or tested by Geolabs prior to being transported to the site for the
intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of rock
and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of passes of a
compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be conducted
at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of passes
may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For preliminary
estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a vibratory drum
roller having a static weight of about 10 tons traveling at a speed of about 300 feet
per minute The roller should pass over the fill surface at least six times for general
fill
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered during our field exploration we
believe that the planned cut slopes will expose the loose to medium dense sands
and gravel at the site In general cut slopes for the project planned at the site may
be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter
Permanent fill slopes may also be designed with a maximum slope inclination of
2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and
benched into firm and stable materials to provide stability of the new embankment

to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Underground Utility Lines
We understand that underground utility lines will be required for development of the
project For utility lines planned at the project site granular bedding consisting of 6 inches
of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as the open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper placement could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of well graded granular materials
less than 6 inches in maximum size We understand that the on site stockpile materials
consisting of cinder sands and gravel may be used for trench backfill The on site cinder
sands and gravel may be used as a source of trench backfill provided that deleterious
materials and particles greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension are removed
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed
in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Where trenches are located in areas subjected to vehicular traffic
the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below areas subjected to vehicular traffic should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
