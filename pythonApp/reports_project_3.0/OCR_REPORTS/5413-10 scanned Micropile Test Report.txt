As part of our special inspection services for the above project we have observed
the pile load testing on one micropile including recording the load test data at a selected
location within the building at the project site This letter report presents the results of our
field observations during the pile load test program and our findings and recommendations
for the proposed micropile foundation support system for the development
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
We understand that it is desired to provide foundation supporting capacity for
columns and walls of the new building structure using a micropile foundation support
system Installation of the micropile foundation support system will be conducted by the
micropile installation contractor Pacific Ground Systems to provide a micropile capable of
supporting an allowable compressive load of 35 tons 70 kips with a factor of safety of 2 0
Based on the design package submitted by Pacific Ground Systems we
understand that Titan with an approximate grout bulb diameter of about
6 inches and an embedment depth of approximately 55 feet below the existing ground
level will be used to achieve the allowable compressive load capacity of 35 tons The
Titan micropile system generally consists of a continuously threaded hollow reinforcing
bar as a load carrying steel member together with a grout body of neat Portland cement
mix which allows transfer of tension and compression forces mainly through friction of the
threaded bar via the grout body into the soil
The Titan micropiles were installed using a rotary and percussion rock drill mounted
on a backhoe to advance the steel rods Neat cement grout was applied through the
threaded hollow core reinforcing bar and injected into the surrounding soils through the
drill bit tio under pressure During the micropile installation the drilling time required to
advance each section of the threaded bar and the total number of sacks of cement used

The load test was performed using a donut jack operated by a hand hydraulic
pump equipped with a pressure gauge Calibration of the donut jack and hydraulic
pump was provided by the micropile installation contractor Pacific Ground Systems
The reaction for the hydraulic jack was provided by a steel reaction beam supported by
micropiles at each end of the beam The reaction piles were the same diameter as the
load test pile with an embedment depth of about 39 feet from the existing ground
surface
The load test piles were loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the load test
micropile was planned at about 140 kips which is 2 0 times the allowable compressive
load of 35 tons 70 kips In general the load test was loaded to the maximum test load
of 140 kips in approximately 8 75 kip load increments Each load increment was held
until pile movement stabilized except for the maximum load of 140 kips which was held
for 480 minutes After loading the test pile to the maximum test load the test load was
unloaded in approximately four equal decrements The results of the load test are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 and are summarized as follows
