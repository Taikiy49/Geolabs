In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Reconstruction of Walkways at

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Aina Moana Magic Island within Ala Moana Regional
Park in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is planned to upgrade the existing perimeter
walkway at Magic Island to accommodate emergency type vehicles We understand that
it is planned to replace the existing asphaltic concrete AC walkway with portland cement
concrete PCC In addition the interior walkways within the perimeter walkways will be
reconstructed with new AC pavements The interior walkways will be subjected to light
vehicular traffic consisting of maintenance cars and trucks
Based on the available information we understand that the existing walkways
consist of AC which is about 1 inch thick and is underlain by about 4 inches of base
course material The base course consists of on site select granular material

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
shallow subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained
was used to evaluate the subsurface characteristics for the formulation of
recommendations pertaining to the design of pavements and site preparation for the
project The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available subsurface information of the site
2 Excavation of shallow bulk samples at seven locations along the access
road alignment
3 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
pavement support characteristics
4 Analyses of field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of pavement
thickness and site preparation
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
6 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
7 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
pavements and site preparation are presented in the following sections of this letter report
Pavements
As previously mentioned the existing perimeter AC walkway will be replaced with
PCC pavements to accommodate emergency vehicles and the interior AC walkways will
be reconstructed with new AC pavements We have made our preliminary pavement
designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will consist of the clay gravel and sand
observed during our field exploration with CBR values ranging between 17 9 and 26 1 and
expansion values ranging between 0 6 and 1 3 percent It should be noted that silty clay
without sand and gravel having low CBR values were encountered at the southwest and
northeast areas of the project site If silty clay soils without sand and gravel are
encountered at the pavement subgrade we recommend that the silty clay soils be
over excavated to the underlying sandy and gravelly soils and replaced with
well compacted select granular fill material
If site grading exposes soil other than those assumed additional tests should be
performed to confirm or revise the recommended pavement sections for actual field
conditions Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following pavement
sections be used for preliminary design purposes
Rigid Pavements
5 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
9 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Flexible Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade

We understand that the existing walkway consists of a 1 inch thick layer of
asphaltic concrete underlain by a 4 inch thick layer of base course The new PCC and
AC pavements may be placed directly over the existing base course layer provided that
the existing base course layer is moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose areas
that are encountered should be over excavated to the underlying firm or dense soils and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with aggregate base course compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
In addition we believe that the AC layer may be re used as a source of general fill
material provided that the AC is free of deleterious material and it is reduced to fragments
that are less than 3 inches in largest dimension The AC may be used in the existing base
course layer provided that the AC fragments 3 inches in largest dimension are well mixed
with the existing base course
Due to the absence of proper load transfer at the PCC pavement edges there will
be an increase in stress at the pavement edges Therefore there is a greater potential for
cracking at the pavement edges To reduce the potential for cracking at the pavement
edges thickened edges should be considered for the PCC pavements The edge of the
PCC pavement should have a minimum thickness of 7 inches The thickened edge of the
pavement should taper to the design pavement thickness over a distance of 24 inches A
typical edge detail for the PCC pavement is shown on Plate 3
To reduce the potential infiltration of water into the pavement structural section the
pavement surface and the subgrade should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained
to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures This is critical
to the long term performance of the pavement structure considering the nature of the
on site soils Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Ponding of water adjacent to the edge of the pavement structural section will
allow infiltration and accumulation of subsurface water which will likely soften the
subgrade soils and cause premature failure of the pavement structure
Extensive landscaping should be avoided adjacent to the pavement areas
otherwise we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed to collect the excess
water from landscaping irrigation and to reduce the potential for migration of landscape
water into the pavement section The recommended pavement section assumes that
good drainage will be provided for the paved areas

removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
material The excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of
off site Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities
Existing asphaltic concrete should be completely removed Over excavation
resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with compacted select granular fill
material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and demolition the pavement subgrade should be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction If the existing base course
will be used as part of the new pavement section we recommend that the base course be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
backfill material provided that they are free of deleterious materials vegetation and are
screened of particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The asphaltic concrete
may be re used as general fill or backfill material provided that they are crushed to
particle sizes less than 3 inches in largest dimension The asphaltic concrete may also
be used in the base course layer provided that they are reduced to particle sizes less than
3 inches in largest dimension and well mixed with the existing base course to meet the
required specifications Imported fill material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
