INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Punahou Off Ramp Improvements project located alongside the
existing Punahou off ramp in the Makiki area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations such as Punchbowl These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of Punchbowl Hill The
Punchbowl Hill Puowaina is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the end
of a spur of the Koolau Range The tuff consists mostly of brown palagonitized vitric ash
and lapilli with scattered fragments of coral limestone and Koolau basalt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3837 00 Rev Page 5
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic
sea level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration along the Punahou off ramp site generally encountered stiff to
very stiff clayey silts and silty clays overlying medium dense to dense cinder sands
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit poor
pavement support characteristics with a relatively low California Bearing Ratio CBR of
about 5 Therefore it is our opinion that a pavement structural section consisting of
5S inches of asphalt concrete AC on 9 inches of asphalt concrete base ACB over
7 inches of untreated permeable base UTPB on 12 inches of aggregate subbase
ASB may be used for the preliminary design of the new pavements for the off ramp
lane In preparing the pavement justification for this project we also considered an
alternative pavement structural section consisting of 5 inches of AC on 8 inches of
asphalt concrete ACB over 7 inches of asphalt treated permeable base ATPB on
12 inches of aggregate subbase ASB However based on our cost comparison
between the two flexible pavement sections we believe that the recommended
pavement structural section Option No 1 above appears to be the more economical
of the two flexible pavement sections
In lieu of the flexible pavement section we also evaluated a rigid pavement
structural section consisting of 14 inches of Portland Cement Concrete PCC on
6 inches of untreated permeable base UTPB over 6 inches of aggregate subbase
ASB Reconstruction of the entire off ramp structure is beyond the scope of this
current project Therefore since the scope of this project is confined to the addition of
GEOLABS HAWAII

only one lane for the off ramp it would not be practical to construct a one lane addition
using concrete
We understand that part of the new off ramp will traverse an existing underground
box culvert near the property line between Shriners Hospital for Crippled Children and the
Jodo Mission of Hawaii For the design of new pavement over the existing box culvert
we understand that a concrete slab bridge spanning over the box culvert will be used for
the subject project The concrete slab bridge should be designed as a simply supported
beam with shallow foundations supporting the structural slab In order to avoid
surcharging the walls of the existing box culvert excavations to depths at or below the

New retaining walls will be required to provide grade separation between the new

we understand that two existing maintenance utility structures will need to be relocated for

encountered in our borings and the anticipated light structural loads we believe that a
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the

the relocated site
Laboratory tests indicate that the near surface clayey soils exhibit a slight

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all surface water off the
pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction To collect infiltrated water into
the pavement structural section we recommend that a pavement edge drain system be
provided along the side of the new travelway
We envision that an edge drain will be needed for the untreated permeable base
layer to collect and daylight the infiltrated water from the pavement structural section
The edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations facing
down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable separator non woven
filter fabric conforming to Section 648 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road
Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 should be placed around the untreated
permeable base to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage material

as shown on Plate 3 In addition a permeable separator should also be placed between
the untreated permeable base and aggregate subbase Care should be exercised when
placing the subbase base and or construction of the surface course to avoid damaging

Existing Box Culvert
We understand that a portion of the new lane will traverse an existing underground
box culvert along the property line at Shriners Hospital for Crippled Children adjacent to
the Jodo Mission of Hawaii as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing box culvert
is about 12 feet wide by 13 feet deep with a top slab thickness of about 7 inches Based
on our discussions with the project structural engineer we understand that a concrete
slab bridge spanning over the existing box culvert will be used for the pavement design at
this location The concrete slab bridge may be designed as a simply supported beam
with shallow foundations supporting the structural slab The concrete slab should be
reinforced as a structural slab and should be detailed by the project structural engineer
In order to avoid surcharging the existing box culvert with additional loads
structural loads from the bridge foundations should be transferred to the underlying soils
at or below the bottom of the box culvert Therefore we recommend that the foundations
of the concrete slab bridge extend to a depth at or below the bottom of the existing box
culvert As an alternative the excavation may be backfilled with Controlled Low Strength
Material CLSM up to the bottom elevations of the concrete slab bridge footings A
flexible material such as neoprene should be provided between the CLSM or concrete
backfill and the adjacent box culvert walls such that loads from the new structural slab
bridge are not transferred onto the box culvert A schematic design of the concrete slab
bridge at the box culvert location is provided on Plate 4 The parameters for foundation
design presented in the following Retaining Walls section may be used to design the

support the retaining wall structure An ultimate bearing pressure of up to
6 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used to evaluate the extreme event
limit state of the footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils To evaluate the
strength limit state of the foundations a resistance factor of 0 6 or a bearing
pressure of up to 3 600 p s f may be used
Wall footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches Wall footings oriented
parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped footings In
general footings should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the lowest
adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or
easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration and laboratory testing disclosed that the proposed new chiller
site is generally underlain by near surface clayey soils with a slight shrink swell potential
when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural distress to
future slabs on grade resulting from the swelling of the near surface clayey soils we
recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with AASHTO Test Method T 180
ASTM D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and
concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
We envision that the slabs on grade will be subjected to equipment loads and
vibration Therefore a 6 inch layer of aggregate base course compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction is recommended for support of the concrete slabs For
design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch
p c i may be used for the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs
adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest

slab with non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to

Site Preparation
We envision that excavations up to about 6 feet below the existing ground surface
will be required for construction of the new off ramp lane and retaining wall In addition
excavations on the order of about 15 feet will be needed to construct the concrete slab
bridge foundations at the existing box culvert location Minimal site grading work is
anticipated for the construction at the new chiller site In general all grading work should
conform to Section 200 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and
Public Works Construction 1994 and the site specific recommendations contained in
this report
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing below any areas designated to receive fill or future
improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or stiff material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Existing structures and pavements that are to be demolished should be completely
removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with
compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch loose lifts

abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping lean
concrete under low pressure

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project In general a
