PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located along the southeast end of the existing Rainbow Wahine 
Softball Stadium at the University of Hawaii at Manoa Campus on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. We understand that the Phase IIB project will include the following: concourse area 
adjacent to the first base grandstand where the area is to be converted into landscape 
concourse with pedestrian paving, seating, and future ticket office; new plaza adjacent to 
Kahele Road and a new sidewalk connecting to the existing sidewalk to the north; and 
three new building structures. The Project Location Map and Site Plan are provided as 
Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
Based on the grading plans, we anticipate site grading for the project will involve 
minor cuts and fills up to about 3 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the Rainbow Wahine Softball Stadium 
Phase IIB project from September 8, 2020 to September 13, 2021.  The site grading work 
consisted of cuts and fills up to about 3 feet in height to achieve the finished grades for the 
project. Jayar Construction Inc. performed the site grading work. During the site grading 
operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and 
backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the pavement areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.8 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field 
representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed 
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report was performed in conformance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
