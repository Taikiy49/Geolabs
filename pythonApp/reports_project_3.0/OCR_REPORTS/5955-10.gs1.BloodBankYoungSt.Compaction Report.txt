Dear Mr Yoza
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and compaction
testing services performed during foundation preparation pavement and utility installation
for the Blood Bank Young Street project from to in
accordance with our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at 1907 Young Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The site is bounded by Young Street and Artesian Street The location of the project site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves the construction of a
three story concrete building and parking area The layout of the project is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the foundation preparation parking area and utility construction at the project site
FOUNDATION PREPARATION AND PARKING AREA UTILITY CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork construction at the Blood Bank Young Street project consisted of
foundation preparation and parking area and utility construction The footing construction
and pavement and utility backfilling work were performed by CE S Corporation During
the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The
compaction test results are summarized on Plate 3

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the parking areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density In addition the select borrow and base course materials
below the pavement areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the footing preparation and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 9 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Voids within loose debris fill material were encountered at the side and bottom of
the grade beam and wall footing excavation near the intersection of Grid Lines B and 5 It
appears that an abandoned cesspool was present at this location The loose fill material
was removed down to stiff natural material about 10 feet below the bottom of footing level
Groundwater was encountered in the excavation about 8 1 feet below the bottom of the
footing level at the time of our site visit on March 18 2011 The excavation was backfilled
from the bottom of the excavation to about Elevation 3 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with
No 3B Fine gravel A filter fabric was placed on top of the No 3B Fine gravel prior to
placing additional backfill material Above the filter fabric the excavation was backfilled
with compacted on site material
Soft subgrade conditions were encountered at the Artesian Street entrance of the
project It appears that soft subgrade conditions were caused by water entering the
subgrade from the new building downspouts A soft subgrade area of about 5 4 feet long
by 2 2 feet wide was removed and replaced with compacted fill material
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the footing preparation and pavement and utility
construction work for the above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references
