SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the Ewa District of Oahu and is located
between the Makakilo Community and Barbers Point Naval Air Station It
is bounded by Farrington Highway on the north side Barbers Point Access
Road on the west side the railroad right of way on the south side and
Oahu Sugar Company s fields on the east side The land is owned by the
Estate of James Campbell and is currently leased to Oahu Sugar Company
The topography is gently sloping towards the ocean The ground
elevations generally vary from about 110 feet near Farrington Highway to
55 feet along the railroad right of way at the makai end of the site
Most of the area is used for growing sugarcane with many haul roads
drainage ditches and irrigation trenches extending throughout the site
The Makakilo and Makalapa Gulches cut through the site and carry
storm runoff from the mauka areas Plate 2 The gulches merge at the
southwestern corner of the site and the storm water is led to a coral
pit which is located inside the Barbers Point Naval Air Station via a
box culvert The gulches are usually dry and carry water only during
rainstorms

Alluvial deposits were encountered mainly in the northern half and
southwestern quarter of the site The alluvium sampled primarily
consisted of medium stiff to stiff brown to reddish brown silty clay
to clay
In general most of the clays can be classified as low to moderately
expansive Local soft zones in the clayey deposit were encountered
beneath drainage ditches irrigation trenches and in areas where
water leaked from irrigation hoses
Coral Formation
The southeastern quarter of the site is underlain by a dense coral
formation and cemented calcareous sand In general the coral
formation is capped by thin 0 5 to 1 0 foot top soil primarily
consisting of brown silt with coral fragments
Cavities were observed in coral exposures at the southwestern end of
the site and also were encountered in borings by previous
investigations drilled in West Beach and Barbers Point Naval Air
Station areas

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The near surface soils over about three quarters of the site consist
of silty clay having low to moderate swelling potential Easy excavation
and conventional site grading procedures are anticipated for earthwork in
these areas Some of these soils may be moderately expansive and could
require special procedures for the house foundation design such as deep
footings subgrade saturation or capping with non expansive soil
Coral formation is present at shallow depths in the southeastern
quarter of the site The coral is generally hard and may require
moderately hard ripping for excavation The excavated coral material can
provide a good source of low expansive structural fill Cavities of
varying sizes are often found in the coral formation If encountered
backfilling of the cavities with grout or compacted fill may be required
Basalt rock formation may be present at shallow depths in isolated
areas in the northern portion of the site The basalt is generally hard
and would require hard ripping to excavate The excavated rock may
produce many boulders which would have to be placed in the deeper fill
areas
