PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for site
grading building foundations and driveway pavement design for the
subject development The recommendations are based on the soil
information obtained from borings drilled for this exploration
The study consisted of drilling 5 borings laboratory testing
engineering analyses and report preparation The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
were utilized in the engineering analysis to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development in
the Aina Haina District of Honolulu This approximately pie shaped
9 000 square foot lot is fronted by Maono Loop to the east and is just
south of Maono Place

At the time of the exploration the site was undeveloped and
covered with waist to shoulder deep grass weeds and bushes
The site slopes down to the south at a gradient of approximately
5 1 H V The ground elevations of the property vary from 463 feet to
481 feet above MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled to explore the subsurface soil conditions
and to obtain soil samples
In general the site is underlain by a variable surface layer
consisting of expansive silty clay to silty basalt gravel and boulders
over decomposed basalt
The exploration program consisted of drilling five borings to
depths of 5 to 11 feet Borings 1 and 2 were terminated at 11 feet
after encountering decomposed basalt Borings 3 through 5 were
terminated at 5 to 6 feet after obtaining near surface soil samples and
penetration data The near surface data was used to evaluate the
conditions under which the surface soil layer was deposited The
borings were drilled with a truck mounted drilling rig The locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Groundwater
At the time of the drilling groundwater was not encountered in any
of the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a
variable surface soil layer Grading plans by Gray Hong Assoc Inc
for the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development indicate that 4 to 10 feet of fill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1870 00 Page 4
Dk No 34
was placed over most of Lot 54 The variable surface soils encountered
from 4 to 8 feet depths during the soil investigation appear to be these
on site clayey bouldery fill materials The laboratory test results and
previous experience indicate that much of the clay material is
expansive Therefore the foundations will require special design and
construction procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of
the subsoils
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed structures
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive soils be over excavated down to a depth of 2 feet
below the bottom of the individual footings or to the decomposed rock
formation and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste select borrow or coral The replaced borrow material
should be compacted in about 8 inch lifts to obtain a minimm of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive backfill soil
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade or to rock formation for details refer
to the Schematic Section Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil We recommend the bottoms of
footings should be placed a minimm of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill

Dk No 34
Surface water should be diverted away from all foundations This
is to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the
expansive soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 2 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The expansive clay below the slabs should be removed for a minimm
depth of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade and
replaced with non expansive off site borrow materials such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral compacted in 8 inch lifts to a minimm
of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 Prior to backfill
with the upper select fill the underlying expansive soil remaining
should be wetted by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
Slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to
Schematic Section Plate 4
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway we believe the above specified
recommendations to provide a non expansive capping layer for the
concrete slab on grade should also be used However since the driveway
could tolerate some amount of swelling the non expansive capping
thickness could be reduced to 1 foot to reduce costs if some cracking
is tolerable For the reduced capping layer we recommend the driveway
subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete placement

A minimum 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete
Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that a 6 to 8 foot high retaining wall may
be used for grade separation between the up hill lot and the level
surface created for the residence s main floor and driveway The
guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 200 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
5 The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 8 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the toe of the wall out to the existing sloping
ground surface

6 The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a H V plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines
7 Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
8 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Lot Grading
The required site fills and backfills within the top 24 inches
minimum below the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill
is anticipated such as the garage driveway and the residence areas it
is necessary that the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a
24 inch minimm thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath
the on grade slabs
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts from the bottom up
properly keyed and benched into firm ground Each lift should not exceed
8 inches in compacted thickness and be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 Field compaction tests should
be made at each lift to verify the above degree of compaction

Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement footings and
retaining wall should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away
from the foundation and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoil
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for house foundation retaining wall site
grading and utility construction should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included

CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations
LIMITATIONS

made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied

Field Exploration
es
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
5 test borings 5 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
a rill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger was used to
drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
