GEOLOGY
The subsurface material encountered during the investigation is
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W
Page 3
recorded to be a formation created during a relatively recent
segment of geologic time A period where sea levels were higher
and where the present coastal plains of Honolulu were submerged
The location of the site lies nearly between two types of forma
tions An older consolidated calcareous marine deposit repre
sented by emerged coral reefs towards the northwest and a
younger unconsolidated non calcareous deposit represented by
younger alluvium deposited from higher elevations toward the
southeast Geologic changes after the emergence of the coral
reefs probably involved the formation of low lying stream channels
or drainageways through the old reefs which collected silty
clayey lagoonal mud deposits generally grayish in color and
sometimes organic
The borings themselves revealed calcareous sands and gravels
coral and shell fragments silty and clayey fines mixed through
out the investigated depth that varied in color from a white
and tan to various hues of gray Interbedded layers or pockets
of clay were also revealed in certain areas
Generally the subsurface material indicates an emerged calcareous
formation which is locally variable due to nearby younger shallow
water deposits of silts and clays

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed improvements consist of a new relocated base
ment wall which is required for the widening of the access
truck ramp to the loading dock area and a new retaining wall
along the southern boundary of the site to replace a portion
of the existing 1 1 H V gunite slope located behing the exis

anticipated for the basement wall The new retaining wall would
be about 10 feet high

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the borings it appears that the site is underlain by
a layer of dense coral extending from near the ground surface to

Shallow spread footing foundations bearing on the dense coral
formation can be used for the proposed retaining and basement
walls
Based on a geologic reconnaissance of the area and previous
boring data from the soil investigation by others performed
for the existing warehouse building it appears that the
project site is situated at the transition between dense coral
formation and soft to medium alluvial deposits Therefore al
though dense coral was encountered in our drilled borings there
is a possibility that some softer alluvial deposits may be en
countered in the footing excavations and may require overexcava
tion or redesign of the wall footings
For this reason it is important that the foundation excavation
should be inspected by the soil engineer during construction
to evaluate the competency of the footing subgrade soils It may

New Basement Wall
As previously mentioned access for drilling equipment was not
possible at the location of the proposed wall Therefore the
two borings were drilled adjacent to the existing wall along
the access truck ramp The borings are located about 15 to 20
feet away from the new wall location Assuming that the soils
under the new wall are similar to that encountered in the borings
it appears that dense coral formation would be encountered at
the footing level
General guidelines for the wall foundation design are as follows
1 The wall foundations should bear into the dense coral
formation An allowable bearing value up to 4000 psf
is recommended for footings bearing on dense coral
2 Significant backfilling would be required behind the
wall Well graded granular material such as coral
or base course rock should be used to backfill behind
the walls

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling three 3 borings and one drilled probing utilizing
a truck mounted drill rig The depths of all the borings
and probing terminated at 15 to 32 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soil and
rock encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1l
through A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs
of borings at the appropriate sample depth
