INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed three story high residential
structure at 1823 Lime Street in the Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2461 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing buildings at the site and
construct a new three story high structure The proposed apartment
building will have CMU walls on the ground floor with wood construction on
the upper levels Anticipated wall and column loads are on the order of 6
kips per foot length and 80 kips respectively
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of the
foundations of the structure In particular the soil exploration is to
evaluate if there would be an adequate fill crust with sufficient
thickness to provide a spread footing type of foundation support
The scope of our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling
four 4 borings to depths of 8 0 to 36 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is approximately 50 by 115 feet in plan dimension
Currently the site is occupied by two 2 2 story apartment buildings A
driveway runs through the eastern portion of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2461 00 Page 3
The site is fronted by a small open area between the City and
Electric Company s McCully substation is located east of the site
The site is generally level with elevations ranging from 3 8 to 4 5
feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4
borings to depths of 8 0 to 36 5 feet with a truck mounted drilling rig
The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the borings the subsoils consist primarily of an upper
3 foot thick silty sand fill layer overlying up to about 1 to 3 feet of
highly compressible organic clayey silt Below the thin organic clay
layer the borings encountered loose lagoonal deposits Relatively stiff
alluvial soils were generally encountered below the 25 foot depth
Groundwater level was encountered at about 4 0 to 4 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our drilling Due to the close
proximity of the project site to the Ala Wai Canal it is anticipated that
groundwater level will fluctuate in response to tidal influences and flood
conditions in the canal
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally indicated that a 1 to 2 foot thick
highly compressible layer of soft clayey silt located below the upper
3 foot surface fill crust at the subject site Loose lagoonal silty
gravel materials was then encountered to depths in excess of 20 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2461 00 Page 4
We believe that the thin upper fill layer and the underlying highly
compressible silty deposit cannot provide adequate foundation support for
the proposed building without inducing significant settlements under the
new building loads In order to provide adequate bearing for the proposed
building structure it is our opinion that either the soft clayey silt and
lagoonal deposit at the footing foundation locations be over excavated and
replaced with select compacted structural fill or pile foundations
should be considered More detailed discussions about pile support type
of foundation system are included in the subsequent Pile Foundation
section
Shallow Spread or Continuous Footing Foundations
For the proposed 3 story structure it appears that spread or
continuous footing foundations may be used provided that the structure can
tolerate some amount of footing settlements To minimize potential
footing settlements and the possibility of footing bearing failure we
recommend that the footing excavations extend down to a minimum depth of
about five 5 feet below the footing bottom to remove the soft clayey
silt soil and the upper portion of the loose lagoonal deposits The base
of the over excavation should also extend a minimm of 1 5 feet beyond the
footing edges See Plate 3 The bottom and side of the footing
over excavation should first be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric
and then backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to raise the backfill a minimm
18 inches above the groundwater level Then non expansive select borrow
materials such as base course or coral should be placed in horizontal
loose lifts not exceeding 10 inches thick on top of the lower 3B Fine
gravel and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASIM D 1557 78 prior to footing construction The top 1 foot below
finish subgrade level should be backfilled with impervious non expansive
soil compacted to the above density See Plate 3
For square footings the geotextile fabric should be lined in both
N S and E W directions to retain the gravel on four 4 sides of the above
footing over excavations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2461 00 Page 5
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on the above specified and prepared
backfill
Footings should be placed a minimm of fourteen 14 inches below the
lowest outside adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral
loading conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing
foundation
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements are
on the order of one inch total and one half of an inch differential
All footings should be adequately tied together with grade beams to
improve the rigidity of the foundation hence reducing the effects from
differential settlements
Pile Foundations
It should be noted that if potential future development construction
at the adjacent properties should involve an inadequately braced basement
excavation or dewatering then the above designed shallow footing and
foundations bearing on 3B Fine gravel fill at the project site could be
jeopardized Should the above possibility become a major concern then
pile foundations are recommended for the project
If pile foundations are to be considered for this project or should
the current column load or structural loading be increased we recommend
that deeper borings be drilled to locate the suitable pile bearing layer
Our current soil exploration budget limited the borings to depth of about
36 feet We will be available to further discuss the required scope of
work for additional explorations at the site

Site Grading
Site filling should be avoided or at least minimized to less than one
foot since the weight of the new fill would initiate long term ground
settlements for spread footing
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as old footings old slabs and pavement vegetation and
rubbish should be removed and disposed of property off site
The ground surface below the slabs should be scarified to a minimm
depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft pockets encountered in the
initial site preparation should be removed and backfilled with select
backfill material
The upper sandy fill materials may be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size
with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during foundation bottom
excavation and backfilling of the project This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may

occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from objects indicated on the Topographic Survey Map dated
provided by Roy K Yamamoto Architect AIA Inc
Elevations of the data points were approximately determined by
interpolation from elevation points on the plan The physical location
and elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
