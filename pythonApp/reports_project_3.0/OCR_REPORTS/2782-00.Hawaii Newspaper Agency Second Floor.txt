INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed second floor addition and new press units at The Hawaii
Newspaper Agency Building on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at The Hawaii Newspaper Agency Building at 605
Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The proposed project involves the addition of a mezzanine floor and ten 10 new
press units on the first floor level within the existing press room
The project site is covered with an existing concrete slab approximately seven 7
to eight 8 inches in thickness and consists of a lower floor area and an upper floor area
approximately three and one half 3 5 feet higher than the lower floor area It is our
understanding that an old concrete foundation structure about three 3 feet in thickness
exists beneath the upper floor area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2782 00 Page 3
From preliminary plans provided to us the mezzanine floor addition will be of steel
column and beam construction with reinforced concrete footings Estimated column
loads up to 105 kips are anticipated
Also planned is an extension of the upper floor area by approximately thirty 30
feet to accommodate the new press units The fill for this extension will be contained by
a reinforced concrete retaining wall The weight of each press unit will range from
seventeen 17 to twenty four 24 kips

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the existing press room at the Hawaii Newspaper
Agency Building at 605 Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu on the island of Oahu
The project site is covered with existing concrete slabs and consists of two floor
levels at different elevations The upper floor area located in the eastern portion of the
site is approximately three and one half 3 5 feet higher in elevation than the lower floor
area located in the western portion of the site An existing concrete ramp connects the
upper and lower floor areas
Presently the site is being used as a temporary storage area for newsprint and
other related products

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered a relatively thin compressible lagoonal
deposit at shallow depths in the lower floor area of the site
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed mezzanine floor addition
bearing on the medium dense to dense on site soils and on the existing hoe press
foundation respectively
For the estimated loads of up to 105 kips on the footings supporting the mezzanine
floor estimated settlements are on the order of about three quarter of an inch
If the settlements indicated for spread footing design are not tolerable then micro
pile foundations may be considered
For the combined column footings bearing partially on the existing hoe press
foundation and partially on the existing subgrade special foundation preparation is
recommended
Foundations
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed mezzanine floor addition
An ailowabie soil bearing vaiue of 3000 p s f may be used for the footings bearing on the
medium dense to dense on site sandy soils For footings bearing on the existing hoe
press foundation an allowable bearing value of 5000 p s f may be used The bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2782 00 Page 8
value is for total dead load plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
For the estimated column loads of 105 kips estimated settlements are on the order
of about three quarters of an inch total and one half of an inch differential
For the combined column footings between grid lines B and C and along grid lines
C D and E the footings will be partially bearing on the existing hoe press foundation and
partially bearing on the existing soils subgrade Due to the possibility of excessive
differential settlement of the combined footings the following foundation preparation is
strongly recommended
Below the combined footings bearing on the soil subgrade the existing subgrade
should be excavated to a depth of twelve 12 inches below the bottoms of the footings
Then the subgrade at the bottom of the excavation should be compacted to a dense
state and the excavation backfilled with compacted select fill material compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Individual footings not bearing on the hoe press foundation should be embedded
a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that soft loose soils are removed and properly replaced prior to
concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2782 00 Page 9
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used
Micro Pile Foundation
If the settlements indicated above for spread footing design are not tolerable then
small diameter micro pile foundations consisting of pipe piles are recommended to
support the new mezzanine floor addition
The pipe piles should consist of six 6 inch diameter minimum drilled holes within
which four 4 inch diameter steel pipes are to be placed The estimated minimum depths
of the drilled holes are 12 and 17 feet below the existing concrete surface for the lower
and upper floor areas respectively After placing the steel pipes the holes should be
grouted both inside and outside the steel pipes with a cement sand water mixture to
increase the stiffness of the micro piles A non shrinking type of cement grout should be
used
A design capacity of fifteen 15 kips per pile with a minimum embedded length of
five 5 feet into the coral formation may be used A minimum pile spacing of one and
one half 1 5 feet on centers is recommended
Since the groundwater level is at about a seven 7 foot depth in the lower floor
area tremie placement of the cement grout from the bottom of the drilled hole upwards
is recommended

The installation of the pipe piles should be observed by the project geotechnical
engineer to determine if the drilled holes and steel pipes are properly grouted
Because of the height limitation in some areas within the existing building for the
caisson drilling rigs a drilled pier foundation is not considered to be feasible
Structural Fill and Foundation Preparation
Structural fill up to three 3 feet thick is planned for the upper floor extension area
The fill will be placed on the existing lower floor slab
Prior to placement of the structural fill drain holes approximately one half 14 inch
in diameter should be drilled through the existing lower floor slab A triangular pattern
with 5 foot spacing center to center should be used
Any loose or soft spots encountered below the column footings should be over
excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted non expansive
select material
Imported fill material for structural fill and material for replacement of any over
excavated material should consist of non expansive select granular materials less than
three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior
to delivery to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum ten 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2782 00 Page 11
If the use of compaction equipment is not desirable for the structural fill placed
above the lower floor slab a self compacting granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel
or ASTM C 33 No 67 may be used

Slab On Grade
A six 6 inch thick reinforced concrete slab on grade is planned for the new press
foundation in the upper floor extension area which extends about thirty 30 feet into the
lower floor area
In the existing upper floor area it is our understanding that the two 2 foot thick
concrete pad for the new press foundation will not be used and the top existing 8 5 inch
thick concrete slab and the coral gravel and sand layer above the hoe foundation will not
be removed prior to placing the new press foundation pad as indicated on the preliminary
plans The new press units will be resting on the existing floor surface
Due to the uncertainty of the relative density of the coral fill below the existing
thick concrete siab it is recommended that the underlying corai fill material be
removed as previous planned

In the upper floor extension area the new pad will rest on compacted select fill
placed on the existing lower floor concrete slab A four 4 inch cushion layer composed
of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided below the slab In order
to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the siab
In the eastern end of the building on the upper floor area a new 4 inch concrete
slab on grade over a 4 inch cushion fill is planned to be placed over an existing slab to
fill an 8 inch depression area Also in the northern upper floor area a new 6 inch
concrete slab on grade over a 6 inch cushion fill is planned
Because of the possibility of moisture accumulating in the cushion fill layer it is
recommended that the cushion fill layer be replaced with concrete
Existing Utility Lines
Since the project site is located within an existing building it is anticipated that
existing utility lines may be encountered during construction of the proposed project
Therefore we recommend that all existing utility lines be located accurately prior
to construction and that an adequate program be devised to protect the existing utility
lines during construction

geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of the

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain engineering
properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the
tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Unconfined Compression tests ASTM Test Method C 39 86 were performed on
selected concrete core samples The ends of the concrete core samples were cut parallel
to each other and at right angles to the longitudinal axis of the core The axial lengths
of the samples were 2 0 to 2 5 times their diameters The stresses at failure are
presented on Plate B 1
Consolidation tests ASTM Test Method D 2435 80 are used to evaluate the
consolidation characteristics of the soil The results serve as the basis for determining
the estimated settlement to be anticipated under applied loadings The result of the test
is presented on Plate B 2
