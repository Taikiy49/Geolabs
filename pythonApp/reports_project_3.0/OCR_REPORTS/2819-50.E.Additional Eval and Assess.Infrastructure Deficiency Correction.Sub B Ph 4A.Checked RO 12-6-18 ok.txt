An additional geotechnical engineering evaluation was
undertaken to assess the existing subsurface conditions at the
Ewa Villages Subdivision B Phase 4A area during the
Emergency Sewer and Drain Line Reconstruction project to
supplement the findings and recommendations contained in our
report dated Fourareas requiring
reconstruction work have been selected as the Special
Excavation Areas for additional field observation and testing
In situ density tests using the sand cone and nuclear probe
methods were performed to evaluate the degree of compaction
of the base subbase fills and trench backfills and subgrade
materials exposed in the excavated trenches Selected soil
samples of the excavated materials were obtained for laboratory
testing to determine their engineering properties and proctor
values
Based on our field observations and laboratory test results it
appears that there are no significant differences in the
appearance and engineering properties between the upper base
course and lower subbase materials used in the pavement
structural section Our gradation tests indicated that both
materials contained a large amount of fines with moderate CBR
values In general it appears that the base and subbase
materials tested did not meet the requirements of base and
subbase materials as specified in the construction documents
which could result in premature pavement failure when these
pavements are subjected to normal vehicular traffic
In general our field density test results indicated that the upper
base course layer was compacted to or exceeded the specified
requirement of 95 percent relative compaction However the
underlying subbase material appeared to be inadequately

90 percent relative compaction In situ density test results

inadequately compacted with the results generally indicating

appreciably less than the requirement of 90 percent relative
compaction We believe that inadequate compaction of the

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed during the Emergency Sewer and Drain Line Reconstruction project at the Ewa
Villages Subdivision B Phase 4A area of the Ewa Villages Development in the District of Ewa
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS INC
W O 2819 50 E Page 3
This report summarizes the observations and test results from our additional field
exploration and laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering findings derived
from our evaluation for the subject project The findings presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT BACKGROUND
The project site is located at the Ewa Villages Subdivision B Phase 4A area of the
Ewa Villages Development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
grading plans provided Subdivision B is divided into 126 lots covering an area of
approximately 19 65 acres We understand that the area has been previously mass graded
by the placement of approximately 5 to 8 feet of fill materials The roadway construction and
utility improvements for the subdivision were completed about 4 to 5 years ago however the
individual properties are currently undeveloped The mass grading and infrastructure
installation work were performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd with Pacific Rim
Consulting and Inspection Corporation performing the field compaction testing
We understand that appreciable ground movements have occurred in the project area
after completion of the infrastructure installation Various types of distresses were observed
in the field and documented including sagging of utility lines settlement of the ground surface
and cracking in the asphalt concrete pavements In several locations the pavements have
sunk around the utility structures such as the manholes In addition large shrinkage cracks
were observed in the soil surfaces of the undeveloped lots
A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted to evaluate the surface and
subsurface conditions within the roadways in an attempt to determine the probable causes or
factors contributing to the observed distresses from a geotechnical engineering point of view
Detailed discussions and findings were presented in our report entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Evaluation Infrastructure Deficiency Correction Project Subdivision B Phase
4A Ewa Villages Development dated Based on the
results obtained from the field exploration we believe that the existence of the following
conditions may have contributed to the distresses observed at the subject site
GEOLABS INC
W O 2819 50 E Page 4
Presence of base and subbase courses with varying thicknesses and generally
inadequate compaction in terms of base and subbase courses supporting a
roadway pavement
Presence of inadequately compacted fills and trench backfills consisting of
medium stiff clayey soils
Rainy weather during the infrastructure installation work per the inspection
reports
As recommended in the above referenced geotechnical engineering evaluation report
dated additional field observations and testing should be conducted on
the exposed pavement sections and utility trenches during the repair work to confirm and or
modify the findings and recommendations presented in the report
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering evaluation was to perform additional field
observations and testing on the exposed pavement sections and utility trenches at selected
locations during the sewer and drain line reconstruction work at the project site The
subsurface information obtained was used to further assess confirm and or modify the
findings and recommendations presented in our previous report prepared for the subject
project The scope of work for this evaluation included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Coordination and scheduling of our field work with personnel or representatives
from the City and County of Honolulu and Rons Construction Corporation
2 Performance of 78 in situ density tests using the sand cone and nuclear probe
methods to evaluate the degree of compaction of the base subbase fill trench
backfill and subgrade materials exposed in the excavated trenches
3 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties Results of the laboratory tests are presented in
Appendix A

4 Field observation of the exposed soil conditions during the utility trench
excavation to assess the potential presence of soft sediments and or
inadequately compacted trench backfills resulting from the rainy weather during
construction and or previously existing irrigation ditches
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering findings pertinent to the probable causes of the
existing distresses at the subject site
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and presenting
our geotechnical engineering findings
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a senior project engineer from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and consultation by a principal engineer from our
firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Quality of the Base and Subbase Materials
Based on the construction drawings we understand that the following pavement
structural section was required for the existing roadways in Subdivision B
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
7 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course
10 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase Course
19 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade Soils
The base and subbase courses should have been compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Based on the subsurface soil profiles exposed in the trench walls it appears that the
thickness of the AC is generally on the order of about 3 inches which is thicker than the 2 inch
minimum requirement The base course encountered in the excavated trenches is generally
on the order of about 6 to 9 inches which is within the 7 inch requirement shown on the
GEOLABS INC
W O 2819 50 E Page 9
construction drawings However the thickness of the subbase course encountered is
significantly more than the 10 inch requirement shown on the same drawings Therefore it
appears that the in place pavement section has either met or exceeded the design thickness
requirements
Based on the laboratory test results performed on the base and subbase materials the
base course material may be described as tannish white silty gravel with sand The
percentage of fines passing through the No 200 sieve is on the order of about 20 percent
which is substantially higher than the 12 percent maximum limit for coralline bases stipulated
in Section 31 of the Standard Specifications City and County of Honolulu The material also
has a laboratory CBR value of 20 which is considerably less than the field CBR value of 85
required for base course The subbase material may be described as brownish tan silty sand
with gravel Again the percentage of fines passing through the No 200 sieve is on the order
of about 24 percent which is also higher than the 15 percent maximum limit specified in the
Standard Specifications In general it appears that the base and subbase materials tested
did not meet the requirements of base and subbase materials as specified in the construction
documents which could result in premature pavement failure when these pavements are
subjected to normal vehicular traffic in the future
Compaction of Trench Materials
Our in situ density tests performed on the base course material indicated that the
degree of compaction varies from about 89 to 100 percent with the field density test results
generally meeting the specified requirement of 95 percent relative compaction The in place
density test results on the subbase material generally range from about 85 to 98 percent with
the majority of the field density test results in the high 80s and low 90s The degree of
compaction for the on site fill and trench backfill material encountered in the special excavation
areas varies from about 72 to 98 percent with the test results generally in the high 70s and low
80s The median values of these test results are calculated and summarized in the following

Based on our field observations and the compaction test results obtained we believe
that the base course layer has generally been adequately compacted However it appears
that the compaction of the subbase course and the underlying fill trench backfill materials are
appreciably less than the specified requirement of 95 and 90 percent relative compaction
respectively We believe that the inadequately compacted subbase course and on site
fill trench backfills as evidenced by the low degrees of compaction are likely the significant
contributing factors to the pavement distresses observed across the project site
Existing Subgrade Conditions Below Bedding Layer
We understand that some utility trenches may have been inundated due to the rainy
weather experienced during the infrastructure installation The rainy weather could have
softened the exposed soils at the bottom of the trenches resulting in a deposit of soft soils in
the trenches If these soft soils were not properly removed and replaced with compacted fills
they may be a factor contributing to the cause of the utility distresses observed outside the
irrigation ditch areas at the site Therefore additional evaluation was conducted during the
utility repair work to assess the potential presence of these soft soils and or inadequately
compacted backfills when the trench backfills were exposed for the portions of the lines that
were replaced
GEOLABS INC
W O 2819 50 E Page 11
Field observations and testing including a combination of hand probing pocket
penetrometer and field density tests were performed to evaluate the exposed subgrade soil
conditions below the bedding layer underneath the sewer pipes at the special excavation
areas Based on our field observations and testing it appears that the afore mentioned
condition may exist at the special excavation area along of Road A The subgrade
soils encountered in this area exhibit higher moisture contents 30 to 35 percent versus 24 to
26 percent and lower degree of compaction 78 to 83 percent versus 85 to 91 percent when
compared to the other special excavation areas It appears that some of the bedding material
under the overburden weight of the trench backfill might have migrated into the underlying soft
subgrade soils caused by the rainy weather This could result in settlement of the bedding
layer and potentially induce some of the distresses exhibited by the existing sewer pipes In
addition during the course of removing the sewer pipe along Road A it was reported that
one segment between about and 4 55 of the sewer pipe both ends of the pipe
did not have the shear rings

APPENDIX B
Summary of Field Density Test Results

backfill and subgrade materials in each of the four special excavation areas to evaluate the
degree of compaction of the existing materials The tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method and D 2922 Nuclear
Probe Method The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 6
Representative samples of the tested materials were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and
the test results are summarized below
