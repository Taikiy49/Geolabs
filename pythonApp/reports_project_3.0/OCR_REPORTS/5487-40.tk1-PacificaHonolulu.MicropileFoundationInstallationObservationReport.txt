SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consisted of fill materials at
the ground surface underlain by lagoonal deposits The fill materials encountered generally
consisted of medium dense silty sands and very stiff sandy silts extending to depths of
about 3 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits underlying the
fill materials generally consisted of loose to medium dense compressible silty sands and
sandy coralline gravel extending to depths of about 12 to 17 feet below the existing ground
surface
Below the compressible lagoonal deposits our field exploration generally
encountered an upper coral ledge on the order of about 2 to 8 feet thick within depths
ranging from about 12 to 22 feet below the existing ground surface The borings then
encountered coralline detritus materials consisting of medium dense coralline sands and
gravel with pockets of loose sandy soils and dense ledges of coral formation extending to
the maximum depth explored of about 125 feet below the existing ground surface
It should be noted that a lower coral ledge varying between about 3 and 17 feet
thick was generally encountered across the project site between depths of about 65 and
120 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that the coral ledges both
upper and lower ledges encountered at the project site are highly variable erratic in
terms of thickness and quality and likely is absent at some locations

MICROPILE INSTALLATION
Micropile foundations were used to provide the necessary compressive and or uplift
load capacities during construction of the low rise portion of the Pacifica Honolulu project
The micropile foundation system was adopted in this situation generally due to the low
capacity required as well as accessibility considerations due to construction sequencing
that precluded the use of heavy construction equipment for other foundation support
systems Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in
our Micropile Foundation specifications dated The purpose of our
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the
micropile foundation installation for general conformance with our recommendations
The reinforcing bars for the micropiles generally consisted of the Titan 52 26 bars
with a minimum drill bit diameter of 5 inches for the project The Titan micropile system
consists of hollow steel rods that are continuously advanced into the ground These drill
rods are abandoned in the hole and serve as the reinforcing steel for the micropile The
micropiles were drilled using a forklift mounted rotary percussion drill rig Drilling fluids
consisting of a neat cement grout mixture flushed soil cuttings from the hole and created a
grout bulb around the drill rod Drilling of the hole installation of the reinforcing member
and grouting of the micropiles generally occur in a single operation
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the project vicinity we believe
the micropiles would need to extend to a minimum tip elevation of 20 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL and embedded a minimum of 10 feet into the coral formation to develop an
allowable compressive and uplift load capacity of 100 kips 50 tons per micropile
respectively As part of the construction requirements and subsequent field
observations one static load test and seven proof tests were conducted on selected
micropiles to evaluate the adequacy of the foundation support system
Static Load Test
In accordance with the project requirements a compressive load test was
conducted on a sacrificial micropile on August 7 2007 The approximate location of the
load test micropile designated as LT MP is shown on the Micropile Foundation Plan
Plate 2 1 The load test micropile was loaded to a test load of 200 kips 200 percent of the
design load of 100 kips in 20 kip load increments in general accordance with the Quick
Load Test Method ASTM D 1143 The static load test results were presented in the
micropile load test report dated August 8 2007 prepared by Meta Engineering
Based on our field observations and the static load test results we believe the
equipment and method of installation generally are acceptable for installing the specified
micropiles in general accordance with the project requirements In addition it is our

by Pacific Ground Systems Inc Sixty eight 68 micropiles were installed during various

In order to further confirm the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles
proof tests were conducted on seven micropiles including
46 50 and 58 The proof tests were conducted by loading the micropiles in tension to
about 133 kips about 133 percent of the design load The micropile movement was
measured for each load increment using two dial gauges and a surveyor s level In
addition the maximum load was held for 10 minutes to evaluate the potential for creep
effects Based on the proof tests conducted 46 50 and
58 met the proof test acceptance requirement
