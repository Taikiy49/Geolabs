SUMMARY OF EXPLORATION
At the proposed cable depot site the field
exploration generally indicated a surficial crust
of about 5 feet thick consisting primarily of
silty sands with gravel Loose silty sands and
soft clayey sandy silts were encountered below
the crust in two 2 of the borings Very
dense hard coral formation was encountered at
20 foot depths in all four 4 borings
At the proposed Berthing Facilities site the
field exploration generally indicated about 10
feet of soft clay with mudline at elevation 30
feet overlying 5 feet of silty coral gravel
Below this the drilling encountered about 5 feet
of hard coral reef over about 35 feet of medium
dense silty sandy coral gravel Very dense coral
sand was encountered at elevation 85 feet
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil engineering exploration
performed for the proposed AT T Cable Ship Berth and Cable Depot at Sand
Island Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed to provide boring and
laboratory data for use in analyses to be performed by
of STV Lyon Associates The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes the
findings of our subsurface exploration
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a berthing facility consisting of an
apron pier dolphins and bollards for the Cable Ship Charles L Brown
It is anticipated that the apron pier will be constructed as either a pile
supported concrete wharf or a bulkheaded quay

In addition to the berthing facilities it is proposed to construct a
cable pan storage depot which will include a cable pan yard
office accessory shed and a paved parking area The anticipated maximum
load for a single cable pan is about 224 tons
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface exploration was to obtain the subsoil
information required by your geotechnical engineer for
his analyses and development of engineering recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed project
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling seven 7
borings and one 1 probing to depths ranging from 20 1 to 91 5 feet below
the existing ground or water surface obtaining surface samples from
different locations at the proposed Cable Depot site performing
laboratory tests on selected samples obtained to classify and identify the
engineering characteristics of the subsoils and preparing this
exploration report
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is located on the seaward fringe of the Honolulu
Coastal Plain This coastal plain is composed of interbedded alluvial
marine and post erosional volcanic deposits Extensive development of the
coastal plain and reclamation of lowlying areas has given the land its
present shape

During the Pleistocene as sea level rose and fell backreef deposits
of sands and gravels with ledges of coral algal formation were laid down
as a fringing reef grew on the seaward margin of the island In some
areas patch reefs and sand bars built up small islets on the reef table
and in the backreef lagoon The original core of Sand Island was one of
these islets
Land reclamation projects during this century have built Sand Island
to its current topography Harbor dredging to minus 40 feet MLLW
completed in 1981 has given the bottom of Honolulu Harbor its present
configuration
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in Honolulu Harbor on the north side of
Sand Island and at the boundary between the Anuenue Fisheries Research
Center and Sand Island State Park
Currently the site is an empty lot and is partially covered with
vegetation An existing chain link fence separates the fisheries and the
park The site is generally level with elevations ranging from 6 to 7
feet above Mean Sea Level
There is a rock sea wall riprap along the shoreline facing into the
harbor

LIMITATIONS
The exploration results submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field data points such as borings probes
and or surface sampling Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it
may be necessary to perform additional exploration
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey prepared by M E Pacific
Inc dated Elevations of the data points were
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic
survey map The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of providing the
geotechnical engineer of STV Lyon Associates with boring and laboratory
test data for use to develop design recommendations for the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
