PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within a portion of the Village 6 Parcel 2 of the existing
Villages of Kapolei development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is bounded by Kamaaha Avenue to the north Fort Barrette Road to the west
and Kaiau Avenue to the east We understand that 18 housing units with four to eight
dwelling units per structure will be constructed on the project site The project includes site
grading and residential structures development and installation of building utilities A
Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
building grading and utility installation for the project
BUILDING GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the building grading and utility installation work for the Kealakai at
Kapolei project from to The building grading work
consisted of footing excavation and footing and slab on grade subgrade preparation for the
project The utility installation work consisted of trench backfill of plumbing and sewer lines
within the buildings Reef Construction Company performed the building grading and utility

installation work During the building grading and utility installation operations samples of
the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture
content were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the pavement areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 5 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Soft subgrade conditions were encountered below the footings for Buildings 8 9
and 10 The footing subgrade soil may have been softened by the heavy rains The soft
subgrade soils below the footings were removed to stiff natural ground and replaced with
compacted fill materials
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures during the above grading
operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the building grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following

LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of
the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
