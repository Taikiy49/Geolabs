INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
investigation performed for the proposed small craft berthing in Pearl
Harbor
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil data such as lateral
subgrade moduli and soil classification for the foundation design of the
proposed improvements
It was felt that the following items would be of critical concern to
the foundation design
1 Depth and characteristics of a suitable bearing layer for the
proposed pile supported pier and shed covering
2 Lateral subgrade moduli of the upper soils for determining the
lateral capacity of the piles
3 Anticipated pile driving characteristics
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated May 27 1986 included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of six 6 borings laboratory soil
testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data and
report preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project includes the demolition of three existing
finger piers Nos 1193 through 1195 which will be replaced by the
construction of a new larger concrete pier about 16 feet wide by 150 feet
long at the location between the existing Finger Pier Nos 1193 and 1194
The existing boat shed above the existing Finger Pier Nos 1195
through 1197 will also be demolished and replaced by a larger new metal
framed shed approximately 50 by 110 feet in plan which will extend fron
the new pier to Finger Pier No 1197
A new fender system and utilities will be provided for the new pier
and a 5 ton monorail overhead crane will be provided for the new shed
It is our understanding that the design lateral loads on both the
piles supporting the proposed pier and the proposed boat shed are not
available at this time However the range of the lateral loads is
anticipated to be about 1 to 4 kips

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau
Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or estuarine
environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps about 300
feet below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
During the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits These
deposits were subsequently covered by man made fills placed during the
middle portion of this century for land reclamation projects during the
construction of the existing Naval Submarine Base

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
5 borings to depths of 81 to 106 feet below the water surface MLLW and
one 1 boring to 106 5 feet below the existing ground surface Boring

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Based on our borings along the proposed new pier we belive that the
proposed new pier may be supported by end bearing piles on the highly
weathered but hard and continuous tuff formation at the depths of about
61 to 72 feet below water surface MLIW To resist the design lateral
loads batter piles may be required for the seaward side of the pier
where the lagoonal deposits are thicker and weaker
The hard highly weathered tuff formation was not encountered in
Boring No 5 at the northwest corner of the proposed new boat shed We
believe that in this area the proposed shed should be supported by
friction piles
The tuff bearing stratum was also not encountered in Boring No 6 at
Wharf S 1A Therefore we recommend that the proposed monorail overhead
crane column at the wharf area be supported by friction piles
New Concrete Pier
The proposed new concrete pier may be supported by both vertical and
batter piles end bearing on the hard highly weathered tuff formation at
depths of about 61 to 72 feet below water surface MLW
Vertical Pile Capacity
The piles would develop their vertical load capacity primarily from
end bearing on the hard highly weathered tuff formation Since the
hard continuous bearing stratum varies in depth along the pier the
total vertical pile length required is estimated to vary from about
65 feet below water surface MLIW near the shore to about 75 feet
below water surface MLIW at the seaward end of the pier We
believe a better estimate of the total pile length required may be
obtained after the test pile program

Lateral Pile Capacity
Lateral resistance of the driven end bearing pile can be analyzed by
using the appropriate soil modulus values of the subsoils at various
depths below the mudline in Boring Nos 1 to 4 The analysis was
carried out by using a finite difference program PILEDG which was
derived from the program COM624
The program adopts the P Y Curve approach and has the capability to
generate internally P Y curves given the soil data Since the
lateral soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates
the pile deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated
deflection is then used to determine the modulus from the P Y
curves The calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then
compared The procedure is repeated until the new assumed and
calculated moduli are essentially the same
The above analysis assumes a free head pile condition It is our
opinion that the free head condition is appropriate for the seaward
end of the pier However the structural rigidity of the pier
closer to shore may produce some fixity on the pile head and
thereby reduce the calculated lateral deflections

pile a laterally loaded pile analysis was performed for Boring Nos 1 to

PITEOG which was derived from the nrogram COM624
PILEDG which was derived from the program COM624

lateral deflection at the top of the pile and the applied lateral force
under a free head condition are summarized on Plate C 2 A summary of
maximum moment along the pile under different boundary forces is shown on
Plate C 1

methods the lateral subgrade modulus K values of the subsoils for
Boring Nos 1 to 5 are presented on Plate C 3
