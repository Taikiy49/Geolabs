INSTALLATION OF PAVER TEST SECTIONS
KALAELOA PARCEL 1 BACKBONE ROADWAY IMPROVEMENTS
Dear Mr Corpuz
This report summarizes our field observations for the Installation of Paver Test
Sections Kalaeloa Parcel 1 Backbone Roadway Improvements project in Kapolei on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii
This report provides information on the installation work performed by Goodfellow
Bros GBI for five test sections along the south side of Franklin D Rosevelt Avenue in
accordance with the FDR Pavedrain Rework Work Plan that was reviewed by the
design engineers of record and the manufacturer Additional Paver infiltration tests were
performed at each test section after completion of the installation Installation work and
infiltration testing were conducted between September 13 and 26 2023
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The project site is along Franklin D Roosevelt Avenue between Kamokila
Boulevard and Copahee Avenue in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
Paver shoulder strips were installed along the southern edge of Franklin D Roosevelt
Avenue between Station 0 00 intersection of Kamokila Boulevard and Franklin D
Roosevelt Avenue and Station 15 00 intersection of Copahee Avenue and Franklin D
Roosevelt Avenue as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and Site Plan
Plate 2
After unexpected settlement observed on the recently completed Paver shoulder
strips it is desired to determine an effective repair scheme To examine the actual
performance of the proposed Paver strip repair schemes under the current heavy traffic
loading five test sections were selected and installed between Station 7 81 and
Station 14 69 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

INFILTRATION TEST
Infiltration tests were performed on September 25 and 26 2023 in each of the test
sections generally in accordance with ASTM C1781 A total of 17 tests were performed
three in each test section and two outside the test sections The infiltration testing was
performed using an infiltration ring from a 5 gallon bucket plumbers putty to seal the
outside of the ring a stopwatch and potable water
Hunt Communities Hawaii LLC
Page 16
W O 6248 30

Hawaii California
The infiltration ring was placed on top of the Paver blocks and sealed with
plumbers putty to ensure water drains through the Paver blocks In general the
pre wetting test was initially conducted using 8 pounds of water pouring into the infiltration
ring at an approximate 0 5 inch constant head above the Paver blocks Time elapses from
each beginning to end of the water drained were recorded The final recorded test was
then performed using 40 pounds of water with the same procedures In the event the
pre wetting test takes greater than 30 seconds to drain the quantity of water used for the
final recorded test will be reduced from 40 to 8 pounds The photo below Image No 17
illustrates the setup of the infiltration testing
Image No 17 GBI setting up infiltration ring
Due to the high infiltration rate at each of the test sections and the difficulty of
maintaining a 0 5 inch constant head water was poured at a rate that would allow water to
drain without splashing and minimize the turbulent water conditions A stopwatch was
used to record the elapsed times from each beginning and end of the water drained The
results of the infiltration testing are summarized in the table below

appears the calculated infiltration rate ranges between 1 561 and 2 587 inches per hour
which exceeds the design infiltration rate of 1 500 inches per hour However two
infiltration tests performed outside the test sections achieve relatively low to minimal
infiltration which may be caused by excessive soil debris accumulated from the runoff In

SUMMARY
The baseline readings are established within five Test Section Options A through
E for sloping degrees from the pavement to the concrete header To examine the
performance of each test section under the current heavy traffic loading weekly or
bi weekly measurements may be performed at each established station for monitoring
Results of the infiltration testing performed indicate the infiltration rates at each
test section exceed the design requirement of 1 500 inches per hour The proposed
method of releveling No 57 rock with No 3A gravel may be feasible without impacting
the design infiltration capacity
