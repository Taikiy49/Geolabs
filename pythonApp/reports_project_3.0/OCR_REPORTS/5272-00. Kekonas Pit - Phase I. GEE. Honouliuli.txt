Our field observations indicate that the existing pit was excavated into dense
coral formation and cemented coralline deposits Our test pit exploration generally
encountered 3 to 4 feet of soft sediments at the bottom of the submerged portions of the
site underlain by dense coral formation
Significant fills up to about 30 to 40 feet thick will be placed in order to achieve
the design grades for the proposed project With proper keying and benching and
compaction effort the existing soils in this area should be able to support the high fill
Significant settlement may be anticipated from the compression of the fills and
in situ soils Sufficient time should be allowed for the high fills to settle and come to
equilibrium prior to construction of permanent structural elements on the site It is
anticipated that the fill will be placed incrementally over a relatively long period of time
therefore a significant settlement waiting time may not be required An assessment of
the rate of settlement should be made at the completion of filling and prior to
construction of permanent structures
Specific recommendations for structural design elements such as house
foundations and road pavements will depend on the type of material used to fill the site

recommendations for the design of this project

SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Kekona s Pit in the Honouliuli area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the mass grading construction only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located on the western side of Fort Barrette Road at the
intersection with Franklin D Roosevelt Avenue Kapolei High School is located to the
east of the site across Fort Barrette Road
The project site is a deep borrow pit approximately 300 feet wide by 700 feet long
and 40 feet deep The exposed side walls are near vertical and consist of dense
coralline formation The bottom of the pit is relatively level It appears that trenches
were excavated along the bottom of the slope in the southern portion of the pit Water
was present in the trenches
We understand that it is desired to fill the pit on an intermittent basis as suitable
fill material becomes available The site will be developed when the pit is filled to near
the existing ground surface It is anticipated that the filling process may occur over a
period of about 10 years
Geotechnical consultation is desired to provide recommendations for suitable fill
material and placement procedures It is important that the fill material and placement
be documented properly to provide an accurate record of the fill construction

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau The site is located on the southwestern flank of the
Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project
site and to the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness
of the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1
Highway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves
as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern
Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the
present Agricultural development within the last 100 years has brought the area to its
present form
2 2 Site Description
The project site is located on the western side of Fort Barrette Road at the
intersection with Franklin D Roosevelt Avenue Kapolei High School is located to the
east of the site across Fort Barrette Road
The project site is a deep borrow pit approximately 300 feet wide by 700 feet long
and 40 feet deep Access into the site is through an existing unpaved road off of
Fort Barrette Road The exposed side walls of the pit are near vertical and consist of
dense coralline formation varying from cemented sand to cemented beach rock and
coral The bottom of the pit is relatively level with a gentle slope down toward the south

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the general geology of the area and previous explorations indicate
that the project site is located within a coastal plain of deep calcareous marine deposits
primarily coral formation Field observations of the existing excavated pit walls and the
test pit exploration confirmed the presence of coral formation within and below the site
Several feet of soft loose recent deposits are present within the submerged areas in the
southern portion of the site Special filling procedures are recommended to reduce
future settlements due to compression of these soft loose deposits
In general the site is suitable for the proposed deep fills Settlements are
anticipated due to compression of the underlying coral material from the new fill weight
and compression of the fill due to its self weight Due to the incremental filling planned
the settlements are not anticipated to pose a significant problem to the project schedule
We recommend that a settlement potential assessment be made when the fill level
approaches the finished grade to determine if a settlement waiting period or special
foundation design is required for the planned structures
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Ground Settlement
Fills up to 30 to 40 feet deep will be placed for the proposed development
Significant ground settlements are anticipated due to the thickness of the fills The
anticipated ground settlements are primarily the result of the following two processes
compression of the fill material under its own weight
compression of the in situ soils induced by the new fill loads
Due to the incremental filling planned the settlements are not anticipated to pose
a significant problem to the project schedule We recommend that a settlement
potential assessment be made when the fill level approaches the finished grade to
determine if a settlement waiting period or special foundation design is required for the
planned structures

At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should

be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
After clearing and grubbing the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of
10 inches and moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1551 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared
again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation
of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill The need
for over excavation should be evaluated by a representative of Geolabs in the field
The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements
for fill material
Site Preparation Submerged Areas
Several feet of soft loose sediments were encountered in the submerged areas
Ideally these soft sediments should be excavated and the sediments disposed of
or allowed to dry and reused within the fill to reduce future settlements
However to reduce costs an alternative filling procedure may be used to attempt to
squeeze the soft deposits out Clean gravel material should be used for
underwater fills The fills should start at one end and progress toward the opposite
end of the submerged area The granular fills should be pushed in with a bulldozer
or backhoe to force the soft deposits to accumulate in front of the fill When the
opposite end of the submerged area is reached the soft soils that have been
squeezed out should be removed and allowed to dry or hauled off site

Another alternative would be to use boulders generally less than 3 feet in size
combined with clean gravel to fill the submerged areas One layer of boulders
should be placed in the bottom of the pits and pushed down with a backhoe to seat
them into the soft deposits Clean gravel should be placed over the boulder layer to
attempt to fill the void spaces between the boulders Additional layers of boulders
and clean gravel should be placed until the fill extends above the water level
Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of materials less than 6 inches in largest
dimension and be free of vegetation and deleterious materials The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 5 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 3percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The fill material within the top 5 feet of finished grade should
have a low expansion potential
Fill material for the top 5 feet should consist of low expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
Fill placed underwater should consist of clean gravel generally less than 8 inches
in maximum size and with less than 5 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction This higher

Excavation of Upper Bench
The existing pit sidewalls are near vertical Therefore there is a potential for
significant differential settlement due to the differences between the compressibility
of the deep fills and the existing coral formation In order to reduce this potential for
differential settlement we recommend that when the fills reach 10 feet from the
finished grade a 10 foot wide bench be excavated to enlarge the fill area to remove
the abrupt change in fill thickness
