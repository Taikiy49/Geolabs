Dear Mr Chu
In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed
geotechnical engineering exploration for the High Street Miscellaneous Drainage
Improvements in the vicinity of Main Street to Keanu Street on the island of Maui Hawaii
This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the project site
These findings are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report Work on site was performed on 2001
Field Exploration
The project site is located along High Street between Kolo Place and Kahookele
Street in Wailuku on the Island of Maui Hawaii The location of the project site is shown
on the attached Project Location Map Plate 1
Two boreholes were drilled on High Street at the approximate locations designated
by DOT personnel Borehole No B 1 was drilled at approximately Station 295 45 Offset
right Offset were reckoned from the existing solid double yellow center lines on the street
The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan 2
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling the boreholes
utilizing truck mounted drilling equipment The boreholes were drilled to depths of

penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling feely from a distance of 30 inches

Plate 8 The results of the moisture content tests are shown on the boring logs at the

Limitations
The purpose of our substrata investigation was to obtain a general overview of
the subsurface conditions at the High Street Miscellaneous Drainage Improvements in
the vicinity of Main Street to Keanu Street project site
It should be noted that variations of subsoil conditions between and beyond the
field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been designated by DOT personnel The stratification lines shown on graphic
representations of the borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition Water level data presented in the text of
this report were based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on
the graphic representations However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors
