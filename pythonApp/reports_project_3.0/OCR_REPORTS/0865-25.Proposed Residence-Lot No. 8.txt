DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lots are under
lain by grayish brown clay fill The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they
have moderate to high expansion potential Therefore
for this lot the slab on grade and foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
The laboratory tests indicate that the on site soils have
moderately high expansion potential To reduce the expansive
effects of the clay soils we recommend that a minimum 3 foot
capping layer of non expansive material should be placed
below the concrete slabs and driveways
In the garage areas where minimum site fill is required for
lot grading the expansive clay below and for a distance
of 3 feet around the slabs should be removed for a minimum
depth of 3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced
with compacted non expansive off site borrow material such
as quarry waste The underlying remaining clay soil should
be scarified moistened to above optimum moisture content
and recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to back
filling with the 3 foot capping select fill

W O 865 25 P 5
Normal slab on grade construction with a 12 inch thickened
edge can then be utilized for the garage
For the proposed residence 1 to 4 feet of fill is planned
In the portion of the residence where less than 3 feet of
fill is required the existing ground should be overexcavated
for a depth of 3 feet below the finished floor elevation and
replaced with select non expansive material such as quarry
waste The underlying expansive clay subgrade should be
scarified moistened to above optimum moisture content then
recompacted to 85 density prior to backfilling Where more
than 3 feet of fill is planned additional overexcavation
below the existing ground is not required The clay subgrade
soils must be kept saturated prior to fill or footing concrete
placement
The perimeter house retaining wall footing should be embedded
a minimum of 24 inches below the outside finished grade to
provide a cut off wall to reduce future infiltration of out
side moisture under the slab area
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of 3B
Fine gravel base course
In order to reduce the above specified overexcavation and
replacement costs for slab on grade foundations we recommend
that post and beam type of house construction be considered
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should extend to a minimum depth of 24 inches below the ground
surface The footings should be poured in place

The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and
free of loose soils or debris
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and
to avoid ponding condition near the slab area Also
the owner should be made aware of gutter water which
should be diverted far away from the perimeter footings
and walkways
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used
for the above recommended footings on compacted fill
Driveway and Sidewalk
For concrete driveway and sidewalk recommendations
provided for concrete slab on grade should be followed
However since the driveway and sidewalk could tolerate
some amount of swelling the non expansive capping thick
ness could be reduced to 2 feet All subgrade should be
kept moist prior to concreting
Site Grading
If required fills and backfills should be placed in
8 inch maximum loose lifts and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation
and debris

The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as a
source of structural fill
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
