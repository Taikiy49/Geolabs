CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing engineering analyses and office research the following
conclusions and recommendations are presented
Due to non uniform materials including large boulders and
relatively loose condition of fill it is our opinion that it
would not be prudent to build on Lots 137 through 139 as
presently graded We further recommended that existing surface
materials beneath the lots be partially excavated and removed
This will enable construction of a shallow raft or layer of
compacted fill immediately beneath building areas The
possibility of scraping vegetation off the present fill and
adding fill without any stabilization was considered However
due to the marginal condition of existing fill materials we
believe that it is in the ultimate best interests of the project
to partially remove and place surface compacted layers for more
uniform support On the other hand deep excavations and
complete removal of fill would be slow and very difficult and
are believed to be unwarranted
The on site boulders are so large that they should generally be
wasted below the toe of the slope boulders less than 18 inches

Corrective Grading
The upper fill materials makai of Kamookoa Place should be
excavated and removed The depth of excavation should be from
three toe five feet or sufficient to place at least three feet of
compacted granular fill under the bottom of all foundations and
bottom of floor slabs No rock larger than 6 inches should be
used in the upper 3 to 5 feet of fills
Where additional fill is required beyond the existing toe of
slope a key should be excavated to basalt rock at the toe of
existing fill slopes in order to provide additional stability
In areas where limited new fill is placed on the existing fill
slope keying through the fill materials to basalt will be
necessary Where extensive slope fill is not planned excavation
and backfill of the upper 6 to feet of the boulder slope is
recommended

Additional fill and backfill should be compacted in horizontal
layers keyed and benched into the existing slope The boulders
bedded in sand may be used to backfill the key
After compacting the ground surface by at least eight passes of a
roller required backfill and fill soils should be placed in
Maximum nine inch thick lifts and compacted to 90 percent
relative compaction using ASTM Designation D 1557 70 Modified
Proctor as the standard
Fill may be sloped at an overall gradient of 2 horizontal to 1
vertical
For this project continuous observation and frequent field
density testing where applicable during grading should be
performed under technical control of the soils engineer This is
to ensure that recommendations of this report are followed and
that subsurface conditions between test pits do not vary
substantially from those encountered during explorations
