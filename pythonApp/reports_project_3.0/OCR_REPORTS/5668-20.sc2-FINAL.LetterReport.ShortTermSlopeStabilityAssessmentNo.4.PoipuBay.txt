Project Considerations
The project site is at the existing Poipu Bay Golf Course which is in the coastal
Poipu area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii A large multiple block type slope failure
occurred in March 2006 along a section of steep sea cliffs bordering the 15th and
16th Holes of the golf course The cliff failure occurred following an extended period of very
heavy rainfall in March 2006 The collapsed portion of the cliff is within the existing
shoreline conservation zone The collapse area involved the full height of the sea cliff and
extended approximately 300 feet long by about 25 feet wide

Geologic Conditions
The project site is along a section of the southeast facing coastline in the Poipu
area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The coastline fronting the golf course is comprised of
tall sea cliffs composed of lithified dune sand sandstone overlying weathered basaltic
rock formation of the Koloa Volcanic Series The geologic contact between the weathered
basalt rock formation and overlying coralline sandstone formation is sharp and is near the
elevation of the present day Mean Sea Level The coastline is exposed to strong ocean
swells that focus on the exposed weathered basalt sandstone geologic unit contact The
ocean wave action has created erosional notches undercuts by eroding the weaker
highly weathered basaltic rock material underlying the sandstone cliffs
Evidence of continual notch undercutting and resulting large block type rockfall
failures along the sandstone cliff is real as evidenced by accumulations of fallen blocks of
sandstone lining the length of the shoreline In general it appears that where the fallen
blocks of rock are present they should provide some natural armoring of the cliff base The
natural armoring should help reduce ocean wave attack and material erosion In addition it
is our opinion that the cliff stability may improve locally following the release of
overhanging blocks and the resultant decrease in cantilevered load
Based on our observations the shoreline cliffs adjacent to the 15th and 16th Holes
represent a dynamic and evolving geological condition The steep cliffs and boulder strewn
shoreline exhibit a history of large block type rockfall slope failures The erosion and
retreat of the exposed cliff face is anticipated to continue with the passage of time
Background of 2006 Slope Avulsion
On March 27 2006 after an extended period of heavy flooding a large slope failure
involving a substantial span of sea cliff approximately 300 lineal feet long and about 20 to
30 feet wide fell into the ocean The slope failure released several very large blocks of
coralline sandstone rock that were deposited along the toe of the cliff As a result of the
slope failure avulsion the top edge of the cliff is currently about 10 to 30 feet inland from
the pre existing cliff face The approximate location of the 2006 slope avulsion area is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 1
The slope failure resulted in the loss of a portion of the existing top of cliff shoreline
access trail within the existing shoreline conservation zone The slope failure occurred
adjacent to the existing 15th Hole Green and 16th Hole Tees A portion of the existing cart
path traverses within approximately 18 lineal feet inland from the new top of cliff After the

Site Reconnaissance
We conducted our site reconnaissance on to observe the
existing site conditions with respect to potential shoreline slope instability The purpose of
our reconnaissance update was to review the existing ground surface conditions along the
top of the slope for visible signs of slope instability The top of slope reconnaissance
spanned between the 16th Hole Tees and the 15th Hole Fairway along a distance of about
1 400 linear feet The reconnaissance was conducted by our geologist under generally dry
fair weather conditions
Existing Condition Observations
Based on our reconnaissance conducted along the top of the cliff visible signs of
imminent large scale slope failure such as ground subsidence or extensive ground surface
cracking were not observed
We observed that most vegetation had been trimmed to better expose the ground
surface adjacent to the 16th Hole Tees In addition the temporary safety fencing installed
to restrict public access along the top of the cliff remains in place adjacent to the 16th Hole
Gold Tees as shown by Photograph No 13 on Plate 9
We observed an existing jagged surface crack within the coralline sandstone rock
comprising a prominent cliff line protrusion adjacent to existing Monitoring Point No 3 The
crack measured about 3 feet in length and trends roughly parallel to the overall cliff face
orientation at a distance of about 10 feet inland from the adjacent cliff face The irregular
surface aperture along the visible crack alignment ranges between about 2 and 5 inches
wide narrowing substantially with depth The approximate depth of the crack was
observed to be about 2 feet as evidenced by probing from the ground surface The

Cliff Undercut Measurement
Esaki Surveying and Mapping Inc conducted a rough baseline measurement of
the existing cliff undercuts on The depths of cliff undercutting were
approximately measured and plotted with respect to the top edge of the slope The
undercut measurements were recorded adjacent to the 16th Hole Tees and at a shorter
length of slope below existing Monitoring Point Nos 9 and 10 The measurements were
plotted and are reflected by the cliff undercut line presented on the Site Plan Plates 2 1
and 2 2 The measurement of cliff undercut depth is useful in estimating where potential
future cliff avulsion s may occur and the potential magnitude of future cliff avulsion
Based on our review of the 2011 cliff undercut line with respect to the top of cliff line
adjacent to the 16th Hole Tees it appears the existing cliff undercut is generally irregular
with some localized undercuts of about 10 to 15 feet deep separated by adjacent cliff
areas showing minimal undercut depth However two prominent and very substantial cliff
undercuts measuring about 25 to 30 feet in maximum depth and another measuring about
30 to 45 feet in maximum depth were mapped below the existing Red Tee and the
existing active Gold Tee respectively Geolabs previously measured the undercut depth
near the existing Red Tee at about 22 feet relative to the top of cliff The deeper cliff
undercut below the active Gold Tee was not accessible to Geolabs and no undercut
depth estimate had been provided The larger undercut below the active Gold Tee is
discernable as a shadowed area in the available aerial photographs taken of the site in
2006
Based on the cliff undercuts adjacent to the 16th Hole Tees the undercut below the
active Gold Tee is more substantial in depth than previously indicated by indirect
top of slope visual observation and may be of concern for long term slope stability
Based on the mapping the cliff undercut is triangular in plan view shape coming to a point
at the deepest portion of the measured undercut Based on our review of the mapped
undercut information the deep but pie shaped undercut beneath the cliff does not appear
to represent an imminent slope failure due to the geometric shape of the void However
the affected area should be monitored using the existing survey monitoring points along
the top of the cliff
The map plot of the cliff undercuts measured below existing Monitoring Point
Nos 9 and 10 appear to show that the undercut does not extend beneath the current

Public Access at Top of Slope
We observed some existing temporary safety fencing spanning between the
March 2006 slope avulsion zone westerly to the existing active 16th Hole Gold Tee The
fencing is intended to deter public access seaward of the fence where potentially
unstable ground conditions exist
Based on our review of the undercut mapping we recommend extending the
existing temporary safety fencing approximately 60 linear feet further towards the west
to fully encompass the cliff area adjacent to and seaward beyond the existing active
16th Hole Gold Tee The reason for the fence extension is to limit public access directly
above the documented deep cliff undercut and associated potential ground instability

Public Access at Toe of Slope
Based on our observations the shoreline along the toe of the cliff alignments are
highly susceptible to falling rock hazards Natural weathering and erosion contribute to
periodic toppling failure of existing loose rock blocks and boulders comprising the top
edge of the cliffs The public should be discouraged from accessing the shoreline
directly below the coastal cliffs
Survey personnel should approach from the ocean take safety precautions and
be aware of the potential for falling rock hazards while performing any survey work
along the sea level shoreline
Long Term Setback Recommendations
We recommend maintaining the temporary fencing and warning signage such
that public access makai of the fencing is restricted until a permanent mitigation scheme
is implemented It is our opinion the permanent mitigation could consist of a setback
and realignment of the affected portion of the golf course further inland and away from
the zone of potential coastal sea cliff instability
For preliminary planning purposes a safe use setback may be approximated by
extrapolating a hypothetical failure surface at about a inclination extending
from the maximum undercut demarcation to the top of slope elevation This assumes
that a future large block type slope failure would initiate at the assumed undercut depth
and the new cliff face produced would stand at an inclination of about or
steeper An extrapolated new top of slope boundary assuming a large block type failure
encompassing the existing undercut depths is presented on the Site Plans Plates 2 1
and 2 2 The estimated new top of slope boundary may be used for preliminary
planning purposes provided some additional buffer space beyond the extrapolated
boundary is included to estimate the preliminary setback boundary
A formal geotechnical engineering exploration is recommended to formulate
appropriate long term setbacks from the cliff face for portions of the golf course located
in close proximity to the steep potentially unstable cliffs
