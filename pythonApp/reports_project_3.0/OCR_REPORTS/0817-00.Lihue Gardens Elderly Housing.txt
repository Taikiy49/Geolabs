SUMMARY
The subsurface exploration disclosed
competent bearing soils at or near
the existing ground surface
Shallow foundations are recommended
for relatively light residential
structures An allowable bearing
value of 3500 p s f is recommended
The surface soils appear to have
low expansion potential and slab
on grade construction can be used
The text of this report should be
referred to for detailed recommen
dations and special design considera
tions
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the site grading and foundation design for the proposed
development
A grading plan was not available during our investigation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located west of Kuhio Highway in Lihue
Kauai covering an area of approximately 4 3 acres
The site is bordered by undeveloped land similar in character
to the project site Approximately two thirds of the site
is flat sloping to the south at about a 2 percent gradient
A 20 to 30 foot deep gully with a 2 1 horizontal vertical
slope bounds the eastern perimeter of the site Surveyed
elevations vary from 172 to 212 feet During the field
exploration the site was covered with dense trees shrubs
and grass except for several dirt roads and trails through
out the site Also scattered throughout the site was mis
cellaneous rubbish consisting of abandoned cars broken
concrete and metal debris
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed development consists of ninety 1 and 2 story
wood framed housing units around the perimeter of the site
and an A C parking area and driveway near the central portion
Future access to the site will be by an access road from
Kuhio Highway through the proposed Lihue Gardens develop
ment site
Minor grading is anticipated for this development
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 3
GEOLOGY
The project site is located in the Lihue Basin on the
southeastern corner of the single shield volcano that
forms the island of Kauai The Lihue Basin consists of
an ancient collapsed caldera which has been filled with
post erosional volcanics of the Koloa Series The Koloa
Series volcanics consisted of both pahoehoe and aa lava
flows which cover about half the surface of the eastern
part of the island and the entire Lihue Basin These
flows were originally flat and have been moderately
eroded and extensive residual soils have developed in
the upper sections grading down to basaltic rock at an
unknown depth at this site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Four borings were drilled to depths of about 15 feet below
the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface con
ditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring logs
and laboratory test results

PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 4
Soil Conditions
Based on the test borings the site is underlain by stiff
to hard residual soils consisting of reddish brown clayey
silt to depths of 4 to 10 feet yellowish brown clayey silt
to depths of 7 to 11 feet and red to gray and black mottled
Clayey silt to the extent of the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration disclosed competent bearing
soils at the site Shallow foundations bearing on the
stiff in situ soil will provide adequate support for the
proposed one and two story structures
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low
expansion potential therefore slab on grade can be
designed without special provisions for expansive soil
conditions
To minimize deep footing design the building should be
set back about 5 feet away from the top of existing slopes
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 5
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soil
On level areas the bottom of footings should be placed
about 18 inches below the finish grade Where the footings
are located near the top of slope they should be embedded
an additional amount such that there is a minimum 5 foot
horizontal set back distance between the outer edge of
footing and the slope face
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foun
dations
Site Grading
Minor grading is planned for the site
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 8 inch lifts compacted to the
above specified density
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 6
Slab On Grade
To resist the downhill creep movement of the near surface
soil and to develop the full bearing capacity of the sub
soil the slab perimeter footing close to the top of slopes
should extend down a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back of 5 feet from the slope face
Conventional slab on grade can be used on level areas
located 5 feet or more away from the tops of slopes
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR test on the on site soil the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 7
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of the roadway construc
tion and the above design section may be revised accordingly
if other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located throughout the site If
encountered the cesspool locations should be flagged in
the field Where existing cesspool are located below or
adjacent to footings and slabs the sludge should be removed
and the cesspool filled with compacted granular backfill
The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should
be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soil engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED LIHUE GARDENS DEV P 8
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 4 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture contents and Atterberg Limits were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and eva
luation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
One direct shear test was performed on a selected
sample to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
subsoils The test results are plotted on Plate B 1
One ASTM D 1557 density test Modified Proctor was
performed on the on site soil to evaluate the suitability
as a potential source of borrow fill The maximum density
and moisture curve is plotted on Plate B 2
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a sample of
the near surface soil in the proposed parking area to eva
luate its suitability as pavement support One swell test
on l inch thick ring samples with 55 p s f surcharge load
was performed on the near surface soil to evaluate its
swelling potential The test results are summarized on
Plate B 3
