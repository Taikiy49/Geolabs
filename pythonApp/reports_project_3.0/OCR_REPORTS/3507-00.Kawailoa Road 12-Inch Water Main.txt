SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
KAWAILOA ROAD 12 INCH WATER MAIN
In accordance with your request a soil resistivity survey was conducted for the
above referenced project in the Lanikai area of the District of Koolaupoko on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the Kawailoa Road project is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our fee proposal dated May 12 1995 This report presents the results of our soil
resistivity survey and summarizes our work on this portion of the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Kawailoa Road in the Lanikai area of the District
of Koolaupoko on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 it is proposed to install roughly 1 400 lineal feet of new 12 inch water main
and appurtenances along Kawailoa Road between Kaelepulu Stream and Alala Road
The new 12 inch water main will be installed on the makai side of Kawailoa Road
adjacent to the existing 8 inch water main which will be abandoned It is our
understanding that ductile iron or polyvinyl chloride pipe would be used for the water
main construction
Based on the preliminary project plans and profiles we understand that the new
water main will be installed at approximately 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground level
The ground surface elevations in the project area range from about 5 to 11 feet

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and Field Geologists to and from
the project site
2 Performance of 27 in situ soil resistivity tests at nine separate locations
Test Nos 1 through 9 along the makai shoulder area of the proposed
water main alignment These test locations generally correspond to the
300 foot interval maximum required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
3 Coordination of the field work by a Project Geologist from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the electrical resistivity data pertinent to the
corrosion characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of six copies of this letter report summarizing our work on this
portion of the project and presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on January 3rd and 8th for the field survey Our field work was performed under sunny
weather and generally dry ground surface conditions Surface soil conditions were
observed to consist of mainly sandy material
Twenty seven soil resistivity tests were conducted with intervals ranging from
about 100 to 250 feet at nine locations at the project site using the in Pin
Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc
Test Nos 1 through 5 were completed on January 3rd Test Nos 6 through 9 were

The resistance meter is capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output
power into the ground The method utilizes four surface electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The
average resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the
planned invert elevations of the water main were assessed by using this method at the
approximate test locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
survey are presented on Plates 3 and 4
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface
elevations on the exposed shoulder area along the proposed water main alignment
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal horizontal spacings of generally 3 to 6 feet between electrodes
The resistance meter impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes causing
an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 3 and 4 of this report
It should be noted that soil resistivity may be influenced by the close proximity
of existing utility lines temperature variations weather conditions and other factors
Attempts were made to locate the testing points away from existing buried utilities to
reduce the potential for interference of the electrical transmission
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In
general the lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried
metallic structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will
contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
