1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Borrow Site at Kukui ula Development project in Koloa on
the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our review of available geological and geotechnical
engineering information previous field explorations laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The entire Kukui ula Resort Community project encompasses about 1 045 acres
in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes hotel vacation ownership
commercial park golf course and residential uses Significant mass grading is
anticipated for the development

Resort Community development area to the west of Poipu Road The site is currently an
abandoned landing strip encompassing about 23 acres and based on the current
grading scheme will provide about 260 000 cubic yards of borrow material

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single dissected basaltic shield volcano
built by the extrusion of lavas from the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series beginning
about 5 to 6 million years ago The eruption of the Waimea Volcanic Series ended about
2 million years ago followed by a long period of erosion Following the cessation of
this main volcano shield building phase about 1 million years ago renewed volcanic
activity occurred with the extrusion of basaltic lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic
Series and the concurrent deposition of thick alluvial sediments of the Palikea
Formation
Rock formations of the Koloa Volcanic Series are generally characterized as
thick lava flows composed of dense basalt extruded from groups of vents aligned in
north south trends at various locales Associated with the Koloa Volcanic Series lava
flows are some deposits of pyroclastic materials volcanic ash and cinders which
usually form cinder cones surrounding the vent Rock formations of the Koloa Volcanic
Series cover most of the eastern half of the Island of Kauai including the project site
During the Pleistocene Epoch many sea level changes occurred as a result of
widespread continental glaciation As the great continental glaciers accumulated the
ocean level fell since less water was available to fill the oceanic basins Conversely as
the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose because more water was
available The landmass of Kauai remained essentially stable during these changes
and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted
in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower relative to the present sea level
of the Island of Kauai
The Kukuiula Resort Community development area is situated on ground
underlain by basaltic rock lava flows of the Koloa Volcanic Series In some places
generally thin surficial soils consisting of silty and clayey residual and alluvial materials
may mantle the underlying rock formation The basalt rock formation observed in the
Koloa area appear to be representative of the pahoehoe lava flow type which spread

The borrow site is situated in the northern portion of the development area where
low hills have been mapped as cinder cone vents of the Koloa Volcanic Series The
cinder in these cones has weathered to form a relatively deep residual type soil
Buried cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lava flows especially in
pahoehoe lavas Cavities are formed when the lava is in a molten state and represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and localized blisters and pockets inter flow
cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains quickly from the feeding
conduit of a cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for large
longitudinal distances along the flow Lava tubes may also consist of a network of
branching and merging segments that may change directional course over relatively
short distances Lava tubes may extend from far inland all the way to the ocean since
they serve as the primary lava conduit for advancing flows Blisters and pockets are
more isolated elliptically shaped features which are generally smaller in horizontal and
vertical extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water
quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is
transmitted through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and _inter flow
contacts cavities and along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as the primary
water transmission features because of the high permeability

has brought the area to its present form

2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The borrow site is situated in the northern portion of the Kukuiula Resort
Community development near the town of Koloa on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The
project site is bounded to the north by vacant land and to the east west and south by
future residential developments The project site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
At the time of our exploration most of the project site was vacant and covered
with low grasses and various trees The topography of the project site generally slopes
from north to south with the existing ground surface elevations ranging from 400 to
440 feet above Mean Sea Level The central portion of the site is relatively flat and
was previously graded as an aircraft landing strip

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
3 1 General Soil Conditions
Based on our exploration the site is generally underlain by competent residual
type soils consisting of silty clays and clayey silts The soils encountered generally have
good engineering properties and are capable of supporting moderate to high foundation
and pavement loads when properly compacted These soils generally have moderate to
high in situ moisture contents that may require aeration to improve their workability
when used as fill material The residual soils generally exhibit low expansion potential
but contain zones with high expansion potential
3 2 Site Grading
Detailed grading plans for the proposed borrow were not available at the time this
report was prepared however we anticipate that the grading will generally consist of
cuts up to about 15 feet deep Items of grading are addressed in the following
subsections 1 Site Preparation 2 Excavation 3 Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements 4 Cut Slopes and 5 Grading Estimation
A Geolabs representative should observe site grading operations It is important
that our office representative observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether
undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to confirm
whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should
thoroughly be cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials

Excavation
Based on the anticipated grading and on our field exploration excavation will
involve cuts into relatively competent residual soils It is anticipated that these soils
may be excavated with normal heavy excavation and earthmoving equipment It is
anticipated that some remnant boulders may be encountered during the
excavation These boulders should be segregated from the borrow material and
either used in rock fill or disposed properly at an off site location Some hard
excavation may be encountered in isolated areas of the borrow site
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on field data obtained from the field borings Contractors bidding on this project
should be encouraged to examine the site conditions and soil data to make their
own interpretation
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Material excavated from the borrow site when used as fill and backfill should be
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished with sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork therefore Geolabs should conduct observations and soil
density tests during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required
degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less

be given to our laboratory test results indicating the subsurface soils have an

compaction level of 90 percent relative density there would be a loss of about
10 percent by volume This estimated loss is highly preliminary in nature and
does not consider losses associated with site preparation handling
transportation and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches
of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths In addition relatively undisturbed soil samples were
obtained in general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches
