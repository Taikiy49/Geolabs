INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residential development along Booth Road in the Pacific
Heights area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of site grading house
foundations and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the intersection of Booth and Pacific Heights Roads
in the Pacific Heights area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
current development plan it is proposed to develop the project site into twenty detached
single family residences ranging from about 3 750 to 9 100 square feet in lot size for a
total of approximately 2 42 acres in area
We anticipate that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will be
utilized to support the lightly loaded one to two story residential structures The project
will also involve the installation of new utility connections such as sewer water and
drainage as well as construction of new driveways and an access road Based on the
current grading concept cuts and fills generally on the order of up to about 5 feet thick
are planned to achieve the design finish pad elevations
We understand that a total of six borings and twenty two test pits had previously
been drilled excavated within the development site as part of an exploration program

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the majority of the project site is generally
blanketed by a relatively thin layer of miscellaneous fill materials overlying highly
expansive clayey soils at shallow depths Based on the subsoil conditions encountered
at the project site it is our opinion that a shallow foundation system may be used to
provide support for the proposed residential structures However laboratory test results
indicate that the near surface clayey soils generally exhibit high shrink swell potential
when subjected to fluctuations in moisture content Therefore we recommend that the
proposed slab on grade foundation be supported on a minimum of 3 feet of
non expansive capping fill to reduce the potential for distress resulting from shrinking and
swelling of the near surface expansive soils Special attention should be given to the
recommended procedures for subgrade preparation and slab on grade design to reduce
the potential shrink swell effects of the near surface clayey soils on the future house
performance

reused as a source of general fill material provided that they are free of vegetation

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Non expansive select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Select
borrow and base course materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the subgrade soils at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important requirement considering
the highly expansive nature of the on site clayey soils Because moisture
conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are critical elements of

Slab On Grade Foundations
In general we anticipate that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge
footings will be used to support the proposed one to two story residential structures
planned for the subject development Our exploration indicates that the site is generally
underlain by highly expansive clayey soils at shallow depths Unless slab on grade
foundations supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is potential
for future distress to the residential structures resulting from wetting and subsequent
swelling of the expansive clayey soils To reduce the potential for structural distress
resulting from the swelling of the on site expansive soils we recommend that
non expansive select capping fill material be provided for a minimum depth of 3 feet
below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the slab perimeter a
minimum of 2 feet
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may
be utilized for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the non expansive select
capping fill materials This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for
footings bearing on the new engineered fill materials Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
The thickened edge footings along the perimeter of the slabs should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The embedment of the

If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending

of inch
Prior to placement of the select capping fill the clayey subgrades should be
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and lightly
compacted to about 85 to 90 percent relative compaction The select capping fill material
should provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive
subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In
our opinion the reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should
reduce the impact of shrink swell effects on the slab due to potential heaving of the
underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and
concrete To reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to reduce the costs
of form construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat against the soil
excavations The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom
longitudinal stee reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should be
connected by stir ups The ends of the stir ups should extend into the floor slab area and

should be tied to the welded wire mesh The welded wire mesh in the slab on grade
should be embedded at the proper height in the concrete to provide adequate slab

details of this thickened edge which is intended to function both as a moisture barrier and
as a perimeter bearing wall footing
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
