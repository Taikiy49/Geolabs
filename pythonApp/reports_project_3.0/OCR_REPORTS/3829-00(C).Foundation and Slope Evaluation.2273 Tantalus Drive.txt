SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance disclosed that
the subject house foundations have experienced significant
settlement and movement The distress may be attributed to
some of the existing footings bearing directly on the surficial
expansive clay soil with minimal embedment
In addition surface water flowing beneath the house from an
abandoned storm water drain line located near the roadway
has undermined some of the footings and has likely
contributed to increased soil moisture fluctuations thereby
adversely affecting the expansive clay soil condition
We recommend embedding the existing house foundations
through the surficial expansive clay soil and a minimum of
6 inches into the underlying more competent basalt rock
formation Furthermore we recommend that the leaking drain
line be removed or properly abandoned to stop the surface
storm water flow beneath the house As additional measures
we recommend that rain gutters and downspouts be installed
on the building to divert runoff away from the foundation of the
home Also the perimeter ground surface around the building
should be graded to provide for positive drainage away from
the home foundation

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the existing residence at 2273 Tantalus Drive of the Papakolea
Development in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for foundation
repair and drainage design input only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands
Papakolea Development on Tantalus Drive in the District of Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The site is situated in the residential district constructed on the mountain
slope facing southeast and located above Kanaha Stream as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
An existing single story residence of wood framing and post and pier construction
is situated on a sloping lot The 15 year old building is situated well below the existing
road level and according to the occupants has had a history of foundation movement
Recently the home has experienced additional significant distress concentrated at two
down slope corners of the building due to the failure of footings supporting the foundation
posts Some of the footings have collapsed and others have settled resulting in
suspended foundation posts with no bearing capacity As a result of the loss of bearing
capacity portions of the home have settled differentially leaving the structure poorly
supported and at risk for further damage

In addition we understand that there are problems with surface water entering the
property from an abandoned storm water drain line located upslope of the residence and
adjacent to the roadway It has been reported that storm water from the partially plugged
drain line flows beneath the house during heavy rainfall and the concentrated flow of
water has collapsed some of the footings

The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the foundation
distress and the surface and shallow subsurface conditions at the project site The
information was used to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to possible methods to stabilize the
house foundation The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated May 8 1997 Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts

obtain background information related to the foundation distress

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield volcano Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southern flank of Koolau and is composed of
basalt rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks
are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded
thin clinker layers For the last 1 to 2 million years weathering of the Koolau lava flows
has occurred forming a thin mantle of alluvial and residual soils which grade to basalt
rock formation with depth In the vicinity of the project site rock formation is located at or
very close to the existing ground surface However the soils mantling the rock formation
are generally composed of a dark brown adobe clay material This clay is known to be
responsible for a variety of foundation problems resulting from its high shrink swell
characteristic during cyclic wetting and drying of the soil

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
At the time of our field evaluation the ground surface at the project site was
generally dry The subject lot is a steeply sloping rectangular parcel that drops abruptly
from the road elevation at the front of the lot northern property boundary and continues
down to the lower rear boundary of the property Exposed basalt rock formation was
observed sporadically at the ground surface exhibiting a natural terraced topography
consisting of near vertical rock faces separated by moderately to steeply sloping ground
surface with a thin soil covering
At the front of the lot a set of concrete stairs provides access to the central portion
of the lot where the existing home is located Based on information obtained from the
occupant a pre existing home had been located at the front of the lot closer to the street
however it had been demolished and the existing house was constructed further
down slope
Toward the central and lower portion of the lot the ground surface was observed
to be moderately sloping at about two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V the exception
being directly beneath the existing home where some grading had been performed to
provide suitable foundation support of the pier foundations The grading beneath the
house appeared to consist of some leveling of the surficial soil and the construction of a
rock retaining wall for grade separation A topographic map of the site was not available
for our use during our reconnaissance
As mentioned previously the ground surface at the front of the lot appears to
consist of rock formation with some soil covering of the flatter slope portions although the
flatter slopes were masked by growth of grass and other vegetation In the vicinity of the
house and the lower slope the ground surface appears to consist of adobe clay soil with
scattered outcroppings of basalt rock formation The basalt rock consists of a ledge of
rock approximately 2 to 3 feet in height located beneath the northern edge of the house
footprint Below the ledge and under the central portion of the house adobe clay soil

possibly fill material was observed to be retained by an approximately 3 foot high rock
wall Below the rock wall in the vicinity of the southern edge of the house scattered

The majority of the existing footings were observed to bear directly on the surficial
adobe clay soils Some of the footings were observed bearing on what appeared to be

3 inches above the footing pad In addition two of the footings at the northeast corner of
the building which were constructed of cemented basalt boulders were completely
collapsed leaving the posts suspended Because of this failure the home occupant
reported that the entire northeast corner of the house was subsiding
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating five shallow borings and test
pits to variable depths extending to about 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Samples were taken at selected depths from the excavations The approximate locations
of the excavations are shown on the Foundation and Test Excavation Pian Plate 2
The field exploration generally encountered stiff dry to damp dark brown adobe
clay soil materials extending from the ground surface to variable depths of 1 7 to about
3 feet below ground level the maximum depth penetrated by the excavations The
excavations were terminated when refusal was met by what appeared to be basalt rock
formation consisting of either very dense rock or dense clinker material Some cobbles
and boulder fragments were encountered during the hand auger drilling which required
converting some of the test borings to test pits to evaluate the condition of the rock
encountered

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our geotechnical reconnaissance indicates that the existing house has
experienced significant settlement distress resulting from collapsed and or settled spread
footing pads and piers Based on our observations the cause of the foundation distress
may be attributed to the following
e existing footings bear directly on the expansive clay surface soils with minimal
embedment and
e surface water reportedly flowing beneath the house from an abandoned drain
line
Our field exploration generally encountered near surface clay soils overlying
weathered basalt rock formation at relatively shallow depths of about 1 to 3 feet below
ground level Based on the subsurface conditions encountered it is our opinion that all
the existing footings should be reconstructed and deepened to bear within the underlying
more competent basalt rock formation rather than the surface expansive clay soils
Laboratory tests indicate that the near surface clay soils exhibit high shrink swell potential
when subjected to moisture fluctuations which in turn enhance the potential for
down slope creep movement of the soil mass In order to reduce the effects of the highly
expansive soils due to moisture fluctuations we recommend that the foundations be
embedded to bear a minimum of 6 inches below the top of the rock formation surface

Based on conversation with the home occupant and a representative of the State
of Hawaii Department of Hawaiian Home Lands an abandoned storm water drain line
may be responsible for the periodic discharge of concentrated flow of storm water into the
subject property The drain line emanates from beneath Tantalus Drive and discharges
to the ground surface at the top of the steep hillslope adjacent to an existing crown flower
tree Itis our understanding the drain line had been previously abandoned however
runoff continues to enter the pipeline from an unknown source
Based on conversation with the home occupants the drain line discharge flows
directly down the steep upper slope The flow then curves slightly towards the southwest
to flow beneath and along the northeastern perimeter of the house Based on our
observations evidence of a flowing water condition was visible on the steep slope
highlighted as washed and accumulated debris and toppled vegetation This flow was
reported by the occupant to be directly responsible for collapsing two of the footings at the
northeastern corner of the house It is our opinion the flow may also be contributing to the
wetting of the expansive clay soils beneath the house thus accelerating settlement and
movement of the remaining footings
In summary we recommend that the footings be reconstructed to bear a minimum
of 6 inches below the top of the basalt rock formation at the site In addition measures
should be taken to stop surface water from flowing beneath the house including the
complete abandonment of the leaking drain line the addition of roof gutters to the home
and the grading of the ground surface surrounding the home to shed water away from the
foundation
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from borings and test pits Variations of subsoil conditions
between the borings or test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
