PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This report presents the results of our soil engineering and
geologic reconnaissance performed for the subject project The purpose
of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions to provide recommendations for the design of the foundations
of the 0 15 M G reservoir a one story Control Building and design of
the proposed cut slope at the site The approximate location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

field reconnaissance excavation and sampling of three 3 test pits a
series of seismic refraction survey laboratory testing engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory findings and report preparation
The results of our field reconnaissance and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The site is situated on the lower slopes of the mountain range about
3000 feet west upslope of Hana Highway in the Hamoa District
An existing drainageway is located about 200 feet south of the
southern edge of the tank site
The ground surface generally slopes down towards the east at a slope
ratio of about 5 1 H V The ground elevations generally vary from
about 350 feet to 380 feet above Mean Sea Level
loose boulders were noted at several locations around the site
Some rock outcrops were noted south and west of the site The overall
site is presently covered with a few trees and grass
An existing dirt road crosses near the northern portion of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by utilizing geophysical
equipment Refraction Seismograph along with excavating and sampling
three 3 test pits to depths of 2 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The three test pits generally encountered 3 to 7 feet of brown
clayey silt volcanic ash mixed with gravel cobbles and boulders over
hard weathered basalt rock formation In Test Pit No 1 a loose clinker
layer was encountered from 3 to 7 foot depths
The seismic survey generally indicated low velocity material to
depths of about 10 to 12 feet and medium velocity material to about 25 to
30 feet the depths surveyed

ENGINEERING GEOLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicated that the site is
underlain at shallow depths by weathered lava rock formations which could
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed structures
From a soils engineering point of view the site is feasible for the
proposed reservoir construction provided our recommendations are
followed
0 15 M G Reservoir
The study disclosed that the proposed site is underlain by brown
clayey silt volcanic ash and weathered lava rock clinker which could
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed reservoir The
revised grading plan indicates that the tank may be partly on soil and
partly on rock

Foundation
Because the reservoir may be located partly on rock and partly on
soil the subgrade should be overexcavated and backfilled with a
12 inch cushion layer of base course below the structure to provide
uniform support This cushion layer may be eliminated if the tank
subgrade is found to be entirely in rock formation

Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted fill
are recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value up to
3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on compacted fill The
allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for temporary
wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 16 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
rocky materials or the trench be backfilled with concrete to
minimize footing settlement
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of all footing excavations
and trench backfill should be compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundation
Probing and Grouting
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
cavities or loose clinkers in the underlying lava rock the footing
excavations should be probed at approximately 10 feet on centers
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 6 feet The probe
holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the
cavity The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by
the soils engineer or the project inspector to see that these
recommendations are carried out properly to locate potential voids
near the footing excavations

Excavation
The field reconnaissance consisting of seismic refraction surveys
and shallow test pits indicates that the site may be underlain at
fairly shallow depths by medium dense Aa basalt with weathered
clinker layers mantled with a thin layer of volcanic ash soil mixed
with cobbles and boulders
We believe that the excavation into rock for the proposed reservoir
may be done by bulldozer and ripping equipment
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the seismic survey and
test pit data

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly backfill operations and slope cuts
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
