Foundation Probing and Grouting Report
Hilo Base of The Subaru Observatory
In accordance with our proposal dated March 27th we have completed the
observation of the foundation probing and grouting of the two structures designated as
Phase and Phase Il The purpose of our work is to provide field observation and the
necessary geotechnical engineering consultation services for the probing and grouting
operations
The project involves the construction of a new facility for the Hilo base of the
National Astronomicai Observatory of Japan at the University Park University of Hawaii
The proposed project consists of construction of a two story building Phase a one
story building Phase II and parking lots We understand that the buildings will be of
reinforced concrete construction Foundation probing and grouting operations were
conducted by Sonomura M Contracting Company between April 2nd and 4th for the
Phase building and between May 6th and 8th for the Phase II building The intent of the
foundation probing and grouting is to attempt to locate and fill subsurface cavities
beneath the foundation of the proposed buildings
Foundation probe holes were drilled at footing locations and or spaced at
approximately 10 foot intervals along continuous footings A total of 248 probe holes
were drilled with 124 holes at each building The Phase building has Probe Hole Nos

The foundation probe holes were drilled using an Ingersoll Rand track drill Probe
holes were drilled to a depth of about 13 feet for a total of 1 612 lineal feet for each
building The probe hole depth corresponds to the minimum depth of 10 feet below the
bottom of the foundation subgrade level Materials encountered during the probe drilling
consisted typically of dense gray basalt At Probe Hole and 93 of
the Phase building a moderately dense gray basalt was encountered A cavity of
approximately 1 foot in vertical height between 4 and 5 feet deep was encountered in
Probe Hole No 122 at the Phase II building Based on our observation of the probing
operations the other probe holes did not appear to encounter any voids and or cavities
A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on
Plates 3 and 4 for the Phase and II buildings respectively
After the probing operations the probe holes were fitted with PVC pipes to
maintain the integrity of the open hole During grouting operations the PVC pipe was
removed and the holes were filled with grout The grout used to fill the probe holes
consisted of Class 1 A 2500 grout mix which has a minimum unconfined compressive
strength of 2500 p s i with a 6 to 8 inch slump range The grout was supplied by Jas W
Glover Ltd Grouting was performed by Sonomura M Contracting Company using a
piston pump and a 2 inch hose to inject grout into the probe holes The hose was
lowered down to the bottom of the hole and retracted as the grout was applied For the
Phase building each probe hole took about 11 gallons of grout which corresponds to
approximately 1 gallon per lineal foot We estimate approximately 6 8 cubic yards of
grout was used to fill the 124 probe holes For the Phase II building we estimate that a
total of 6 7 cubic yards of grout was used based on injection times needed to fill the probe
holes and cavities encountered Our observations of the grouting operations and the
estimated grout volume are also summarized on Plates 3 1 through 4 3
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations Based on our observations significant cavities were not
encountered and the subgrades should provide adequate foundation support for the two
buildings
