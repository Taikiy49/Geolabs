SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is underlain by very stiff residual soils
Shallow spread footings are recommended for foun
dation support A maximum allowable soil bearing
pressure of 3000 p c f with one third allowance
for wind or seismic forces is recommended The
bottom of footings should be at least 12 inches
in the very stiff reddish brown residual soil
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation conducted for the proposed Sperry Warehouse
Kauai to be located at Lihue Industrial Park Unit 2 Lot 26
the site is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions to develop design recommendations for the proposed
warehouse

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed warehouse will be a concrete masonry structure
with high bays housing mezzanine areas The proposed building
layout is shown on Plate 2 Plot Plan The anticipated founda
tion loads will be a maximum of 3 kips per linear foot for wall
footings and 57 kips maximum for column footings It is anti
cipated that no cold storage will be considered at this time
Some filling was being done at the time of our investigation
It is our understanding that this was for raising the site
grades toward the rear of the property However we did not
inspect the fill operation

SITE CONDITIONS
The site is generally trapezoidal in shape and bounded on the
east by Aukele Street The property is about 207 0 feet deep
from Aukele Street toward Rice Street The north and south
sides presently abut vacant parcels A low masonry wall
separates the site from Rice Street at the rear
The ground surface is relatively flat and ranges in surface
elevations of about 142 feet along the Aukele Street and rise
Slowly to elevation 147 towards the back of the lot near Rice
Street
At the time of our exploration the site had only limited
ground cover consisting of some grass There is a line of high
tension wires running through the property along Aukele Street
According to a preliminary grading plan it appears that the
site was previously cut 2 to 3 feet Thus there was no
indication of tilling or plowing for agricultural use at the
Site

The subsurface conditions consist mainly of very stiff to hard
reddish brown clayey silt to the depth explored This silt is
believed to be residual soils derived from decomposition of
basalt flows of Koloa Volcanic Series Thus it is anticipated
that boulders would be expected at depth although not encoun
tered in the borings
Groundwater was not encountered at the site
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
We believe that the site is suitable for the proposed project
Care must be exercised on the filling over the site Shallow
spread footings in the natural stiff soils are recommended
Although it is possible to place footings in an engineered fill
of suitable quality and proper construction unless the new
fill is judged to be satisfactory all footings should be
placed in the natural soil Additional handling may be re
quired if new fill is found to be unsuitable for the pavement
subgrade or for the floor slabs
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1122 00
Page 5
Foundation Support
For the design of spread footings a maximum soils bearing
pressure of 3000 p s f may be used on the stiff natural soil
A one third increase in the bearing pressure may be allowed for
wind or seismic loads The footings should be placed at least
18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade or 12 inches in an
undisturbed stiff reddish brown natural clayey silt whichever
is lower
If any soft or weak spots are encountered at the bottom of
footing excavation they should be repaired by over excavating
soft and or weak materials to stiff natural soils and
backfilled with lean concrete
The bottom of footing excavation should be inspected by a
qualified soils engineer
We estimate that for footings designed and constructed to the
above criteria the potential settlements would be less than
one half of an inch and differential settlement will be less
than one third of an inch

Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that there will be small retaining
walls that will support different levels of floors For the
design of low walls we recommend that active earth pressures
of 40 p c f foot of depth be used with appropriate surcharge
loads Surcharge loads if required may be assumed to be
equal to 1 foot of soil backfill for each 100 p s f of
surcharge weight
The lateral force may be resisted by passive earth pressure of
It is very important that the backfills in front of the
retaining wall footings conform to the recommendations under
SITE GRADING presented in a subsequent section
Slab On Grade
We believe that the floor slabs can be constructed on grade
For office floor slabs a conventional 4 inch concrete
thickness may be used
If it is anticipated that the floor slabs may need to support
heavy forklifts and trucks a thicker concrete floor would be

required We recommend that where such loadings are consi
dered slab thickness should be at least 6 inches with edges
thickened to 8 inches for load transfer
The concrete slabs should be placed over well compacted base of
at least 4 inches The base material should meet the require
ments set forth in Section 31 Aggregate Base Course in the
Standard Specification for Public Works Construction May 1975
County of Kauai
The cushion and the fills beneath the slabs should be compacted
to at least 95 of the maximum density determined in accordance

It should be noted that the above recommendations for slab
design will not be applicable for cold storage purpose A
special design study should be made for cold storage purpose

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate soil bearing strata have been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Plot Plan
Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to drill
the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The clas
sifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in gen
eral conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
