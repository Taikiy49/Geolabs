INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface
conditions at the proposed job site and to provide recommen
dations for the design and construction of the proposed Library
Building at the Hawaii School for the Deaf and Blind

Site Description
The Hawaii School for the Deaf and Blind is located on the
Diamond Head side of Waikiki in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The proposed library building is situated on a relatively
flat area at the school entrance on Leahi Avenue The site
elevation varies from 8 0 to 8 5 feet
Presently the proposed library building location is occupied
by a l story wood framed structure supported by stone wall
combined with post and beam construction
Proposed Construction
The proposed library building is a 1 story slab on grade
structure of masonry and wood construction Perimeter
bearing walls will support the laminated wood beams and
roof decking
Wall loading of 2 to 3 kips per foot is anticipated
The proposed finish floor elevation is 9 5 feet Generally
Site grading will include cutting down of the existing surface
layer within the building area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Diamond Head area known as Leahi is one of several vents
of the Honolulu Series lying along a north northeast rift zone
Diamond Head itself is a tuff cone formed by hydromagnatic ex
plosion From the surface the sequence of rocks are as follows

Tuff alluvium clay lagoonal or swamp type reef limestone
clay and gravel clay and coral and the Basalt of Koolau
volcanic series
The sequence has shown several changes in estatic levels but
most pronounced of all is the limestone of the Waimanalo stand
of the sea Most foundation work will be confined in this
formation since it exceeds a thickness of over 500 feet At
this site alluvial clay zone exists above the reef limestone
near surface elevation
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building site con
sisted of drilling four test borings The borings were drilled
at the locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 A
detailed description of the field exploration methods and ac
companying laboratory testing is given in the appendix along
with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Condition
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
with approximately three to four feet of dark brown clay varying
from very stiff to hard Tan sandy silt coral formation was
found beneath the dark brown clay soil
In boring no 1 below the dark brown clay there is a one foot
layer of brown sand overlying another four feet of very stiff
brown clay layer Below these is the coral formation At
boring no 2 the sand deposit increases to a 8 foot layer

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicated the site is underlain
by about four feet of very stiff to hard clay soils
Continuous wall footing bearing on the stiff clay soil will
provide adequate support for the proposed l story structure
without excessive settlement
Foundation
Line and spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed
library building Foundation bearing within the stiff in situ
clay soils can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure
of 2 000 pounds per square foot The bottom of footings should
be a minimum of 18 inches below the finish adjacent grade
Where existing or abandoned utility lines are located under
the foundation the footing should either step down or be
designed to bridge over the loose backfill areas
During construction if any abandoned cesspool or loose utility
backfill is located below the footing foundation the sludge
and soft materials should be removed and replaced with lean
concrete up to its design footing bottom elevation

Slab On Grade
The on site surface soil is moderately expansive For
slab on grade the surface clay soil encountered below
the base course layer should be undercut and replaced
by a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive compacted
select backfill The select borrow can be of coral sand
or quarry waste materials The backfill should be placed
in two 6 inch layers and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM Designation
Did557 727 Prior to placing this granular backfill the
subgrade soil in the undercut area should be scarified to
a minimum of 6 inches wetted to its optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum 90 maximum density
Groundwater was noted at a depth about 7 feet below the
existing ground surface and it is necessary to install a
4 inch layer of capillary break base course under the slab
Slab on grade with floor coverings should be protected by a
plastic vapor barrier The plastic sheet should be covered
by a l inch layer of sand to help concrete slab curing and
to protect the plastic membrane from puncturing
Earthwork
Site grading will involve cutting down about one foot of the
surface clay soils and replaced with a non expansive backfill
under the slab area Disturbed soils due to removal of existing
trees and structure should be replaced with compacted layers

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
4 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 8 3 to 20 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the enclosed Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch 1 D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on all the samples as an aid in the soil classification
and in evaluating soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample
location
Three swell tests of one inch thick ring sample were conducted
on near surface samples for measuring the swell potential The
swell test results are summarized on Plate B 3
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test and a modified proctor
compaction test were performed on a surface sample of the on
site soil to evaluate the suitability of the soil as fill and
pavement support The results are given on Plate B 4
Direct shear tests were performed on selected clay samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the encountered
soils The test results are shown on Plate B 5
In addition Torvane Shear tests were conducted on undisturbed
ring samples The test results are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample location
