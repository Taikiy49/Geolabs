Site Conditions
The project site is located adjacent to the ocean in Waimea
Kauai It is bordered by Laau Street on the north and Moana
Street on the east The site is fairly level and covered
with sparse vegetation
The first floor of the proposed condominium building had

Soil Conditions
The soil conditions encountered in the test pits consisted
of about 1 to 2 feet of variable fill soil consisting mostly
of reddish brown clayey silt and Silty clay over a loose
to medium density dune sand deposit
Groundwater was encountered in one of the test pits at
8 5 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The construction plans and one test pit excavated adjacent
to a footing indicate that the wall footings were embedded
about 28 inches below the finished grade and the footings
were 32 inches wide
Based on our field exploration it appears that the footings
for the proposed building are bearing on the natural dune
sand deposit The dune sand is loose to medium density with
very little cementation
A laboratory direct shear test performed on a remolded sample
of the dune sand yielded an angle of internal friction of
34 degrees Based on this friction angle and the embedment
and width of the footings a net ultimate bearing capacity
of 10 900 p s f is derived
It 1s our understanding that an allowable bearing value of
about 3500 p s f was utilized for the footing design
yielding a calculated factor of safety of about 3 1
Based on the laboratory test results and foundation design
it appears that adequate bearing can be derived from the
dune sand deposit

Estimated settlements of less than 1 inch total are anti
cipated for the design footing loads Since the subsoils
are granular these settlements should occur immediately
upon load application therefore large long term settle
ments are not anticipated
Since the ground floor slab has already been poured we
were not able to ascertain the condition of the 1 to 2
feet of new fill which was placed over the dune sand
deposit Laboratory ring swell test performed on samples
at this fill material encountered outside the building
area indicated low to moderate expansion potential
depending on the molding or compaction moisture content
of the soil The ground surface adjacent and around the
building should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations to avoid ponding and to reduce water seepage
and possible heaving under the building slab
