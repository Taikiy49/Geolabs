SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three test pits designated as TP 1 through TP 3 were excavated within the
proposed City park site to explore the subsurface conditions and to provide foundation
design parameters In addition three grab samples designated as A 1 through A 3
were collected near the finished subgrade of the proposed facility structures The
approximate test pit and grab sample locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
An excavator was used to extend the test pits to depths of about 7 to 8 feet
below the existing ground surface The subsurface conditions encountered in the
excavation was observed and recorded by a Geolabs representative Bulk samples

LABORATORY TESTING
Laboratory testing was performed on the bulk and grab samples obtained during
the field exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties The tests performed are described in the following
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate 4
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 and C 136 was performed on a selected
soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution of the soils are
provided on Plate 5
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on samples of the remolded soils
to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils The test results are presented on Plate 6
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a soil sample to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the near surface
soils The test results are presented on Plate 7
RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the competent soil conditions we believe that slab on grade
foundations with thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed comfort
station structures planned for the park development
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of foundations bearing on the compacted on site clayey soils

transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory testing the near surface
soil exhibits a swell value of about 4 to 5 percent slight to moderate expansion potential
Therefore we recommend embedding the thickened edge footing 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade The foundation subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to no less than 90 percent relative compaction
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by friction developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for foundations bearing on the re compacted low expansion
soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes the
soils around the foundations are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
We recommend incorporating a minimum 6 inch layer of No 3 Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation below the concrete slab as a cushion layer It should be
noted the slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
Foundations next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We anticipate that vehicle loading for the parking lots will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and pick up trucks with occasional heavy service trucks Based on
the laboratory test results and anticipated traffic conditions we recommend the following
pavement section
Asphalt Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soils below pavement areas should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to at least 95 percent relative compaction CBR tests and or field observations should
be performed on the actual subgrade soils during construction to confirm whether the

LIMITATIONS
The test pit and grab sample locations indicated herein are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan transmitted by
Gray Hong Nojima Associates Inc on and revised in April 2009
The boring elevations were estimated based on interpolation between the spot
elevations of field stakes at the time of the field exploration The physical locations and
elevations of the test pits and grab samples should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification shown on the logs of test pits depict the approximate
boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition Water
level data presented in the text of this letter report were based on measurements
recorded in the borings at the times of the field exploration These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this letter report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and
other factors
This letter report has been prepared for the exclusive use of D R Horton
Schuler Division for specific application to the design of the proposed Makakilo C D
Park in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and
practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This letter report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers in the design of the proposed project Therefore this letter report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil and or rock conditions
are encountered commonly during construction Unforeseen subsurface conditions
such as soft deposits hard layers cavities or perched groundwater may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the field which may
result in construction delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore we
recommend a sufficient contingency fund to accommodate these possible extra costs
The geotechnical engineering exploration conducted was not intended to
investigate the potential presence of hazardous materials existing at the project site It
should be noted that the equipment techniques and personnel used to conduct a
geo environmental exploration differ substantially from those applied in geotechnical
engineering
