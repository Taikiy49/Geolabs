Dear Mr Snyder
In accordance with your request Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering
services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new Maunawili Road Bridge
No 2 over Kamakalepo Stream This letter report summarizes our geotechnical
observations performed during the drilled shaft installation for the above bridge structure
In general the purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled
shaft foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and
specifications
Geolabs Inc performed a geotechnical engineering exploration and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Maunawili Road Bridge No 2 Over Kamakalepo Stream Kailua Oahu
Hawaii dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves improvements to the existing two lane Maunawili Road Bridge
No 2 over Kamakalepo Stream in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
included demolition of the existing bridge and construction of a replacement bridge that
has about the same bridge width behind the existing bridge abutments In addition the
replacement bridge was constructed in phases to provide continuous access across the
bridge site
Based on the information provided we understand that the new bridge structure will
have a span of about 44 feet The new bridge will be of concrete construction and will be a
single span bridge supported by abutments at both ends

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loading a deep
drilled shafts was utilized for foundation support of the bridge structure Based on the
drilled shaft analysis results we recommend shaft tip elevations of 38 to 44 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL for the bridge foundation shafts The approximate drilled shaft locations
are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2
The drilled shafts were designed to derive vertical support primarily from skin
friction The end bearing component of the drilled shafts was discounted in the design due
to difficulties associated with obtaining a clean bottom below the groundwater level during
construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The borings at the bridge site generally encountered a surface fill layer ranging in
thickness from about 4 to 6 feet The fill layer consisted of sandy gravel and soft to stiff
silty clay with sand gravel and cobbles The fill layer was underlain by an alluvial
deposit to the maximum depth explored of 52 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The alluvial deposit generally consisted of very soft to very stiff clayey silt with highly
weathered gravels and cobbles and dense silty gravel with sand and cobbles
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging from about 7 4 to
13 2 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The
groundwater levels encountered generally correspond to elevations ranging from about
74 8 to 79 6 feet MSL
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Foundation International Inc performed the installation of the production shafts
Four 42 inch diameter and one 18 inch diameter production shafts were installed at the
Maunawili Road Bridge No 2 site from to May 22 2007 The shafts were
designated as Shaft 2 3 and 4 Shaft and 2 were installed for the
West Abutment structure and Shaft Nos 3 and 4 were installed for the East Abutment
structure The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft
Identification Plan Plate 2 A Geolabs representative observed the shaft excavation
placement of the steel reinforcements and concrete placement operations during the
installation of the production drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a track mounted drill rig equipped with
18 and 42 inch drilling buckets 42 and 48 inch diameter core barrels and
18 inch and 42 inch cleanout buckets Temporary casings with diameters of 48 and
24 inches were utilized to case the upper portions of the drilled shafts during the shaft
construction The production shafts were excavated to elevations ranging from about
37 to 40 feet MSL

After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 5 inches between
the sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a
3 5 inch OD steel pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the
tremie pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the specifications
In the shaft excavation where temporary casing was installed a minimum of 5 feet of
concrete was maintained above the bottom of the casing during casing removal and
concrete placement
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the Maunawili
Road Bridge No 2 over Kamakalepo Stream have been installed in general conformance
with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted
construction practices The top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey
equipment to verify that the shaft position is within the tolerance of the project
specifications Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our
professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to
provide the design load capacity
