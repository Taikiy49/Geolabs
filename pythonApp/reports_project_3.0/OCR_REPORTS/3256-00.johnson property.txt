INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Johnson Property Lot No 33 at Aleka Place of Kailua on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for design input of site grading
foundation and pavement and may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation
of final construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Olomana area of Kailua on the windward side of
the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to grade the site and to construct a new
residence at the subject site Based on the preliminary grading plan provided to our
office we understand that the site grading will involve fill slopes up to about 40 feet high
with fills of about 45 feet thick Details of the proposed construction were not available
at the time this report was prepared However based on conversations with the owner
we understand that the construction of the subject lot will be in conjunction with the
neighboring lot to the northwest In areas along the northwest property line between the
subject lot and the neighboring lot fills up to about 20 feet are planned to raise the
existing grade In addition a drain line is also planned in this area

Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three borings to depths of
about 26 to 31 feet below the surface In addition we also excavated four test pits to
depths of about 6 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field borings encountered stiff to very stiff clayey silt residual soil
from the surface to depths of about 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below
the residual soils saprolite consisting of stiff to very stiff clayey silts were encountered to
the maximum depth explored of approximately 31 feet below the existing ground
surface

existing slope and on the neighboring lot The stiff to very stiff clayey silts residual soils
were encountered in Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 Fill soils consisting of a mixture of clayey silt
cobbles boulders rebar concrete debris and bricks were encountered in Test Pit Nos

surface respectively
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings and test pits at the time of our
field exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate depending on
rainfall time of year and other factors

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
On the basis of our geotechnical exploration stiff to very stiff residual soils and
saprolite consisting of clayey silts were encountered at the subject site from the surface
to the maximum depths explored The soils encountered at the site should provide
adequate bearing for the substantial fills planned at the site Because of the presence of
the debris fills encountered near the toe of the proposed fill slope removal of the debris
fill and replacement with compacted engineered fill will be required to support the 40 foot
fill slope planned
Recommendations addressing the above considerations and other geotechnical
aspects of the subject project are discussed in the following sections of this report
Existing Fill
Based on information obtained from our test pits the fill materials appear to consist
of a mixture of clayey silts cobbles boulders rebar concrete debris and brick These
fill soils are considered unsuitable for use as fill material for the proposed development
based on the significant amount of debris observed In addition the debris fill
encountered is not suitable for support of the substantial fills planned in this area As a
result we recommend that the existing debris fill be completely removed to expose firm
native materials and the excavation should be backfilled with compacted engineered fill
Removal of the debris fill should extend a horizontal distance equal to the depth of over
excavation beyond the toe of the proposed fill slope or a minimum lateral distance of 5
feet beyond the toe whichever is greater The toe of the proposed fill slope should be
properly keyed into the stiff native materials which is further discussed in the Site
Grading section of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII

Fill Slopes and Settlements
As previously indicated a fill slope on the order of 30 to 40 feet high is planned at
the site Substantial fills on the order of about 45 feet thick will be needed to construct
the proposed fill slope For permanent fill slopes a slope ratio of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V or flatter is recommended Where the existing ground is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V keying and benching will be required to properly bond
the new fill to the existing slope A typical sketch of keying and benching is presented
on Plate 3 Intermediate benches should be provided on the fill slope and should be
spaced every 20 vertical feet
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling beyond the design slope limits and
cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate compaction at the edge of the
slope The slopes should be planted as soon as possible to reduce the potential for
erosion
The consistency of the residual soils encountered in our borings ranged from stiff
to very stiff Settlement of the stiff in situ soils due to the placement of the proposed fills
are estimated to be small and should occur as the fills are placed Due to the substantial
thickness of fills planned settlement of the fills due to their own weight are expected As
a result to reduce the magnitude of this settlement we recommend that the fills be
placed at a moisture content of at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction We estimate that the
settlements of the fills up to 45 feet thick to be on the order of less than 3 inches if they
are compacted as recommended above A portion of the estimated settlement should
occur during the construction period

Site Grading
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
It is important that a representative from our office observe the site preparation and fill
operations to evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
encountered in our exploration
The following recommendations for site grading are general guidelines for the
earthwork anticipated and for the development of general specifications
1 Prior to the commencement of earthwork the areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of the existing
cover of shrubs and other vegetation The thin cover of loose topsoil on
the ground surface should be removed These materials should be
disposed of properly off site
2 The existing debris fills encountered in our test pits near the toe of the
proposed fill slope should be completely removed within the limits of the
proposed fill Removal of the debris fill should extend beyond the edge of
the toe a minimum of 5 feet or a horizontal distance equal to the depth of
the excavation whichever is greater
The resulting excavation which may serve as a key should be backfilled
with compacted engineered fill All engineered fill materials should be
placed in thin lifts normally 8 inch loose lift thickness moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optinum moisture content and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction __

Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should
be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with compacted engineered fill Keyways should be
excavated at the toe of the proposed fill slope to the minimum widths and
depths recommended on Plate 3 Deeper excavations may be needed
based on the exposed ground conditions This should be evaluated by a
representative of Geolabs Hawaii in the field during construction Keyways
should be sloped toward the subdrain upslope side to provide positive
drainage
Following clearing and grubbing of the site areas designated to receive fill
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches brought to at least 2
percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction

should consist of granular soils containing no rocks or clods greater than
6 inches in maximum dimension Any fine grained soils to be used in the
_ fill should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery tothe

Subdrainage
Seepage is generally expected to occur at the bottom of slopes We recommend
that subdrainage be provided in the following areas
1 At all springs and seepage areas
2 Where fill abuts natural uphill slope
3 On the uphill side of all keyways
4 In other areas of the site where seepage is observed during and after
grading or as recommended by Geolabs Hawaii during construction
Subdrainage should consist of perforated pipes with perforations placed down and
should be at least 4 inches in diameter All subdrains should be surrounded and
underlain by at least 6 inches of drainage material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation or equivalent A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent
should wrap around the drainage material as shown on Plate 4 In general subdrain
trenches should be at least 12 inches wide and at least 2 feet deep Subdrain trenches
should be capped with engineered fill Subdrains should be positioned along the upside
of all keyway excavations and should discharge into storm drain structures where
possible or other outlet structures A typical subdrain detail is presented on Plate 4

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed structure
Concrete floor slabs will be supported on the fill materials needed to construct the fill
slope or on the near surface residual soils encountered at the site The near surface
residual soils or the proposed fill materials may be potentially expansive Therefore we
recommend that swell tests of the near surface subgrade soils of the proposed structure
be performed when the location of the structure has been finalized to provide additional
data on the need for non expansive capping fill for support of slabs on grade

Drainage
The finished grades should be sloped to shed water away from foundations and
to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and
pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
