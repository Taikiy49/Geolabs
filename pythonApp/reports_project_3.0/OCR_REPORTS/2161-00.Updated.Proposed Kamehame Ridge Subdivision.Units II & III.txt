Material and Compaction Requirements
Ihe upper one to two foot surface soil is generally expansive and
should be utilized in the lower portion of the new fills lcx ated a
minimum of three 3 feet below the finished grade
Where the existing grade is at or near the design finish grade the
expansive soil should be over excavated to a minimum depth of three
3 feet below the finish grade or to the underlying competent rock
fonnation whichever is less and replaced with non expansive select
on site material compacted to 90 of its maximum density
Most of the fill material will be generated from excavation in the
rock fonnation
In general the excavated rock material less than
twelve 12 inches in size can be used as a source of general fill
material
Much of the fills will be along sloping areas which will
require these smaller size materials that can be well compacted to
provide the necessary stability of the fills
Ihe above specified rock fill should be placed in eighteen 18 inch
maximum loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
dry density established by AS IM Test Method D 1557

compaction testing is generally not practicable in excavated lava rock
material

observations and test pits to detennine the number of passes of a
compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project
Ihe number of passes can then
be used as the criteria for compaction
