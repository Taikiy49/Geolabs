INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation inves
tigation for the proposed buildings located at the Kalakaua
Intermediate School in Kalihi Oahu Hawaii
The purposes of this investigation were to investigate the subject
site to evaluate the subsurface conditions determine foundation
requirements and probable behavior of foundation for the proposed
buildings
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed buildings will consist of a library and administration
building of one story masonry wall construction The classroom
building will be two stories and of masonry wall construction

Maximum vertical depth plus live loads will be on the order of
60 kips for columns and on the order of 5 kips per lineal foot
for wall loads Minimal grading operations will be required
to prepare the site for construction
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed building sites are located on the Kalakaua Inter
mediate School campus in the Kalihi area of Honolulu The
Campus is on the Diamond Head side of Kalihi Street between
Dillingham Boulevard and King Street
The project site was previously occupied by temporary class
rooms and an administration building The area is presently
covered with sparse vegetation and some concrete rubble The
site is relatively level with a slight slope in a southerly
direction
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by the flows of the Waianae Shield
volcano and the Koolau Shield volcano The project site lies
on the Honolulu plain which in turn forms the southern toe of
the Koolau Shield volcano The geologic history of the area is
characterized by thin lava flows from the Koolau Shield volcano
during the late Pliocene Period and subsequent weathering of the

basalt lava Intermittent eustatic changes in sea level sub
merged the Honolulu plain forming a shallow marine basin where
coral growth was abundant Later the Honolulu eries volcanics
covered the area with a thin veneer of volcanic tuff and thin
lava flows
Subsurface Conditions
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site eight borings
were drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan Plate l
A more detailed description of the field exploration and labora
tory testing is contained in the Appendix which also includes a
log of each boring and the results of our laboratory tests
Our borings revealed that the site is immediately underlain by
medium stiff to hard dark brown clayey silts that vary in depth
from 5 to 10 feet In and 7 the upper three feet
were medium stiff and this may be due to over excavation during
the removal of the temporary buildings The deposition of this
clayey silt probably occurred when drainage is poor as is the
present condition Underlying the clayey silt is a stratum of
decomposed coral which was encountered from 5 to 15 feet The
coral stratum indicates a marine environment in the past during
a sea level that was at least 25 feet higher that it is today
7 and 8 all encountered a stratum of basalt that
was found from 9 to 15 feet This basalt appears to be an

early lava flow from the Honolulu Series volcanics This basalt
flow is about 4 to 6 feet thick and grades into coral as the
depth increases
No groundwater was encountered in any of the deep borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is suitable for the proposed buildings supported on
conventional shallow footings Slabs on grade may be used
Foundation
The upper onsite clayey silts are moderately stiff and can support
foundations at shallow depths A bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds
per square foot may be used for dead plus real live loads Foot
ings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below final adjacent
grade Pressures may be increased by one third when considering
wind or seismic loads Continuous footings should have a minimum
width of 12 inches
Some softer materials were found in the area of and
7 Footing excavations in these areas should be inspected prior
to placement of steel or concrete If the bearing stratum is
found to be soft the material should be overexcavated to firm
soil and the overexcavated material replaced with lean concrete
a well graded compacted granular material or S4C materials

Due to the moderate expansiveness of the clayey silts it is
our opinion that continuous footings should be designed with
a minimum of two No 4 reinforcing bars one top and bottom
We anticipate that maximum total settlements from the recom
mended building loads will be on the order of three fourths of an
inch Differential settlements between columns and walls will
be less than one half inch
Slabs on Grade
Our laboratory tests indicate the clayey silt to be moderately
expansive We therefore recommend that a minimum of 6 x 6
10 welded wire mesh be used in slabs on grade A six inch
free draining granular base should be provided beneath slabs
to act as a capillary break In areas where moisture sensitive
flooring is to be used we recommend that a plastic membrane
be placed above the granular layer A one inch cushion of sand
should be placed on the membrane All slabs on grade should be
structurally separated from column and wall footings
Lateral Loads
Lateral loads on the buildings may be resisted by either passive
pressures or by friction along the base of footings or a combin
ation of both Passive pressures may be assumed to be equal to
the pressure developed by a fluid with a density of 300 pounds

Site Preparation
After all unsuitable materials have been removed the ground
surface should be stripped to remove all vegetation All
areas to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth
of six inches brought to near optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density as determined by
ASTM D 1551 70T
Local soft areas should be removed to competent material and
replaced with structural fill
Fill may then be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density
The onsite clayey silt may be suitable as structural fill
Import fill material should be non expansive and approved
by a qualified soils engineer prior to bringing on the site

Field Exploration
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling eight test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary
auger equipment with rock coring attachments The depths of
the borings ranged from 15 to 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate l
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our field engineer who continu
ously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System The description of rocks is
based on local geologic terminology Graphical representa
tions of the soils and rocks encountered are presented on the
Log of Borings Plates A 1A through A 1H Soil samples were
obtained by driving a 2 4 inch I D sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to driving the sampler and the percent of recovery of rock
cores are also shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate
sample depth
