SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated within the Pacific Missile Range
Facility in Barking Sands Kauai
The topography at the site is gently sloping down toward the west at
gradients of about 2 to 5 The ground elevation at the site generally
varies from about 6 to 12 feet above MSI
There are several existing one story buildings in the area

Soil Conditions
In general the borings encountered medium dense to very dense fine
sand to the depths drilled The soils appear to be derived from dune sand
with a slight amount of cementation
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of 7 5 to 9 0
feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings encountered competent sandy subsoils at the
site which can provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
one story structures
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense in situ
sandy soils may be used to support the buildings
An allowable bearing value of up to 3500 p s f may be used on the
dense sandy material The bearing value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic
loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1485 00 Page 5
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade Where footings are adjacent to the existing
building foundation the new footings should extend down to the same
level as the existing footing
Any loose pockets encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 If a footing is situated adjacent to a
utility trench the trench backfill must be well compacted to prevent
potential footing settlement due to settlement of loose trench backfill
Minimal footing settlements are anticipated for the design column
loads if the above recommendations are followed
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using
a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above resistance values are
maximum values and do not contain a factor of safety

following slab sections are recommended for the proposed new building and

The subgrade soils should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Following the clearing the
ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 The use
of heavy vibratory compaction equipments to compact the subgrade should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 7
be avoided adjacent to the existing buildings to minimize footing and
ground settlements due to excessive vibrations
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with select
on site material or non expansive import fill material compacted to the
above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement_
The on site sandy soils are suitable for structural fill material
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density
The site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete driveways and parking areas are planned at the
site The following pavement section is recommended over the in situ
sandy soils
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
over sandy subgrade soil compacted
to 95 of its maximum dry density

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the site grading foundation and
pavement design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is reconmended that the site clearing and grubbing fill place
ment and compaction foundation excavation backfilling and subgrade
preparation be observed by the soils engineer during construction This
is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation If
conditions encountered are different from those presented in this report
then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
