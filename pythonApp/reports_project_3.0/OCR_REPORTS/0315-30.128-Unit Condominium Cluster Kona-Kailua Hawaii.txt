This report presents the findings and conclusions of a prelimi
nary soils investigation performed on the subject property
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the soils
underlying the site with respect to the planned development
to define their engineering characteristics and to provide
preliminary recommendations for grading foundations and floor
Slab support
The investigation included a geologic reconnaissance of the area
drilling test holes identification logging and sampling of the
soil and rock encountered laboratory testing and engineering
analysis

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject site is located about one mile south of the Kona
Kailua shopping center and lies between Alii Drive and the ocean
immediately south of the Surf and Sand Hotel See Vicinity Map
Plate 1 The area is about 700 to 800 feet long and 240 feet
wide and rises from sea level to 54 feet at Alii Drive The
area has been cleared of trees which have been stockpiled at
various places on and adjacent to the site Several prominent
rock ridges run in a general east west direction making the limits
of the most recent lava flows These ridges are composed of
jagged blocks of basalt and locally represent caved in lava tubes
One such ridge indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2 is fairly
continuous At the highest point on this ridge there is an

FIELD EXPLORATION
Nine 9 test holes were drilled on the site at locations
shown on the attached Plate 2 Site Plan The drilling was
done with a rotary auger drill capable of rock coring The
holes varied in depth from 9 to 14 feet and were drilled mostly
in basaltic rock Samples and standard penetration tests were
made of the fill material near the ocean Surface samples were
taken of the upper 6 to 12 inches of soil scattered throughout
the site The soils and rocks encountered in the nine test holes
do not necessarily represent subsurface conditions at other points
on the site however sampling procedures are believed to be
representative

GEOLOGY AND SOILS DESCRIPTION
The area of the proposed development is located on the lower
Slopes of Puu Hualalei on the northwest flank of Mauna Loa
The U S Geological Survey has mapped these formations as
basalt with pahoehoe and aa flows many of which are less than
several hundred years old
A thin cover of brown silt 6 to 12 inches deep lies in small
areas scattered across the site A man made fill 0 to 5 feet
high has been constructed across the lava flows next to the
ocean just above high tide The fill material consists mostly
of Silty SAND and moderately well graded rocky material
Standard penetration tests in 2 locations indicate the fill
Material has a medium dense to dense consistency
The cores of the underlying basaltic formation indicate mostly
puka puka rock varying from moderately hard to moderately soft
One small cavity was encountered in Boring No 2 and other
cavities do exist on the site as noted from visual observation
The largest cavity noted was the caved in lava tube previously
mentioned under Site Description and shown on Site Plan Plate 2

The information obtained during our investigation indicates
that the subject site is suited to the proposed construction
insofar as the following recommendations are incorporated in
the design considerations project plans and job specifications
Cut_and Fill Slopes
Cut slopes in rock should be designed at a slope ratio of
1 l horizontal to l vertical Fill slopes should be designed
at a slope ratio of 2 horizontal to l vertical
