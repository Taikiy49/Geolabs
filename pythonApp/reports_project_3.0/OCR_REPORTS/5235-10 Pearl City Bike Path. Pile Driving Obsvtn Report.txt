PILE DRIVING OBSERVATION REPORT
HECO 8-INCH PIPELINE
PEARL CITY BIKE PATH
WAIAU, OAHU, HAWAII
Dear Mr. Luke:
In accordance with your request, we provided pile driving observation and load
testing services for the HECO 8-Inch Pipeline at the Pearl City Bike Path between
April 21 and 29, 2004. Our work generally included field observations during the test pile
driving operations, performing two proof tests on two selected test piles, and field
observations during the production pile driving operations. This report summarizes our
field observations made and the results of the proof testing performed for the
above-mentioned project.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along a portion of the Pearl City Bike Path near the
Waiau Power Plant on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.
Based on the information provided, it is planned to install an 8-inch diameter
pipeline along the bike path. In general, the majority of the pipeline will be installed below
grade. However, a portion of the pipeline will be installed above grade. The portion of the
pipeline to be installed above grade is located between Station 671+60 and
Station 674+00.
The above grade pipeline will be located across a watercress farm area near the
bottom of the embankment for the bike path. Originally, it was planned to install 22 H-piles
with a center-to-center spacing of approximately 20 feet to support the above grade
portion of the pipeline. However, we understand that one of the H-piles was eliminated
(Pile No. 7). The piles for the project will consist of HP10 x 42 H-piles in-lieu of the
originally proposed HP8 x 36 H-piles due to the availability of materials. The locations of
the piles and pile numbers are shown on the Pile Identification Plan, Plates 2.1 and 2.2.

TEST PILE AND PROOF TESTING PROGRAM
The test pile driving program was conducted on April 21 and 22, 2004 under the
near-continuous observation by a field representative from our office. The purpose of the
test pile driving program was to establish the driveability of the piles, given the subsurface
conditions at the project site, selected driving equipment, and proposed methods of pile
installation. In addition, the program also provided information on the driving resistance
and contours of the load-bearing stratum across the project site, which was used to
estimate the lengths of the production piles.
Healy Tibbitts Builders, Inc. used an MKT 9B3 double-acting steam/air hammer for
the test pile driving operations. The maximum rated energy of the double-acting stear/air
hammer is approximately 8,750 foot-pounds. The test piles consisted of HP10 X 42, with
lengths ranging from 30 to 50 feet. The test piles with lengths of 50 feet generally
consisted of two 25-foot long sections that were welded together during pile installation.
A total of six test piles, including Pile Nos. 2, 5, 10, 14, 18, and 21, were driven to
tip elevations ranging between about —9 and -—29 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The
following table provides a summary of the penetration resistance (blows/foot) for the final
foot of penetration for the six test piles driven.

Based on our field observations and the above table, the lowest penetration

Proof Testing
Proof tests were conducted on two selected test piles, designated as Pile Nos. 2
and 10, on April 26 and 27, 2004. Pile Nos. 2 and 10 were loaded to the maximum test
loads of 20 and 19 kips, respectively, in 1 to 1.5-kip load increments in general accordance
with the Quick Load Test method of ASTM Test Designation D 1143. After loading the pile
to the maximum test load, the maximum test load was held for at least 1 hour.

The total pile displacements of the two proof tested piles at the 10-kip load were
observed to be 0.045 and 0.047 inches, which is appreciably less than the 1 inch total pile
settlement estimated and normally accepted for similar projects. Therefore, we believe
that the test piles are capable of supporting a compressive load of 10 kips with acceptable
settlement characteristics. Detailed results of the proof tests for Pile Nos. 2 and 10 are
presented on Plates 3.1 through 4.3 of this letter report.
Pile Driving Criteria
Based on our observations of the test pile program and the proof tests performed
on Pile Nos. 2 and 10, we believe that a termination criteria based on a penetration
resistance of 20 blows for the final foot may be used for the project to provide the design
vertical compressive load capacity for each production pile. Based on the project plans
and specifications, we understand that the piles should have a minimum embedment
depth of 15 feet into the ground surface.
