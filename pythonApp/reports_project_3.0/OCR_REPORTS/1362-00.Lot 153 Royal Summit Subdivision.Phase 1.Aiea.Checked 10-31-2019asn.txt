SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Kuini Street in the
Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Lot 153 is terraced and slopes downward from the front towards the
rear of the lot A relatively level pad about 20 feet wide is
located adjacent to the roadway The site then slopes downward at
about a 5 1 H V ratio to a lower second level pad at the rear of the
lot The ground surface then continues down on a 2 1 H V slope
below the western boundary

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low swelling potential if
maintained at or above the optimum moisture content 30 more or
less However previous experience in the area indicates that some
of the on site soil can exhibit medium to high swelling if allowed to
dry out then wetted Therefore to minimize effects due to soil
swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil
below the on grade slabs be capped with at least 6 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the
select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the
start of construction until the slab concrete placement

Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed

Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
Post and wall footings located on the existing slope should be
embedded down a sufficient depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing to the
slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help
resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the required
confinement An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used
for footings on stiff on site soil The footing excavation should be
examined and approved by the soils engineer
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
