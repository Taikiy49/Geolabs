I INTRODUCTION
General
Submitted herein are the results of our geologic and soils
investigation for the Ward Avenue Relief Sewer alignment as
requested and authorized by your company May 8 1972
This report summarizes our work and presents our recommenda
tions for design and construction of the sewer project
Project Description
The proposed sewer line extends along the center of Ward
Avenue for a distance of approximately 3500 feet between
Auahi and King Streets Honolulu Hawaii
The average depth to invert is approximately 15 feet
Five subsurface borings ranging from 15 to 26 feet deep were
drilled on June 14 and 15 under the supervision of an engineer
or geologist from our firm The borings were done using truck
mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight hollow
stem augers
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate 1 The
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
ool
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depth intervals usually not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Pene
tration Test consists of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler
18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance
N and is an approximate measure of the relative density or
consistency of a soil These resistance values are plotted on
the boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified in
the field and representative material was placed in air tight
containers and returned to our laboratory for inspection and
testing

Pearl Harbor embayment s geologic history Late Pleistocene

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration program disclosed relatively soft
and loose soil conditions along the proposed alignment Design
and construction of the sewer system and related appurtenances
may therefore encounter some minor difficulties
Based on the field investigation laboratory testing and
engineering analyses our recommendations for design and
construction of the proposed system are presented in the
following sections
Loads on Pipe
Earth pressures on buried conduits tend to increase with
increasing trench width We therefore recommend that the
trench width be kept to a minimum or utilize sloping trench
sides or a subditch see Plate 2
Frictional resistance between the trench walls and backfill
and the unit weight of the backfill material directly in
fluence the earth pressures on the conduit
6
The applicable unit weight and friction parameters for the
various materials are presented in the previous section III C
These values should be used for the conduit load calculations
Superimposed loads on the conduit due to concentrated or
distributed loads should also be included

Pipe Bedding
Stress distribution against the bottom of the pipe has a
Significant influence on its load carrying capacity The
contact between the pipe and the foundation material is
therefore an important design consideration
We recommend that Class B bedding be used which can be
7
achieved by two methods
1
2
Shaped Bottom with Tamped Backfill The bottom of the
trench excavation should be shaped to a cylindrical
surface with a radius 2 inches greater than the radius
to the outside of the pipe and sufficient to allow
60 percent of the width of the pipe barrel to be bedded
in fine granular fill placed in the shaped excavation
Carefully compacted backfill shall be placed at the sides
of the pipe to a thickness of at least 12 inches above
the top of the pipe
Compacted Granular Bedding with Tamped Backfill The
pipe should be bedded in compacted granular material
placed on a flat trench bottom The granular bedding
should have a minimum thickness of one fourth the outside
pipe diameter and should extend halfway up the pipe
barrel at the sides The remainder of the side fills
and a minimum depth of 12 inches over the top of the
pipe shall be filled with carefully compacted material
see Plate 3
For reducing the differential settlement we recommend
overexcavating at least 12 inches and backfilling with
granular material compacted to 90 percent relative
compaction according to ASTM D 1557 70
8
Construction of Sewer
Within practical limits the method and equipment used for
construction of the sewer should be left to the contractor
The depth of excavation for construction will probably be
less than 16 feet and excavation below groundwater level
will be necessary
The relatively loose condition of the subsurface soils could
cause some excavation problems due to caving and sloughing
of the trench wall Relatively flat trench slopes or sheet
ing and bracing will be necessary
The CORAL limestone encountered in some of the borings near
the proposed invert will also present some excavation problems
_ Hard ripping or special equipment should be anticipated
Material should not be stockpiled within 10 feet of the
edge of the excavation
Sheeting and Bracing
Sheeting and bracing will probably be required where SAND
and soft Clay encountered to prevent cave in of the trench
wall and subsidence of areas adjacent to the trench
Sheeting and bracing should comply with all applicable safety
requirements The design and safety of the sheeting and
bracing should be the responsibility of the contractor since
his representatives are at the construction site to observe
any changing conditions which might affect stability

Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered in all of the borings and was
approximately 6 feet to 9 5 feet above presently proposed
invert elevation
Dewatering of the trench should be continuous for concrete
placement pipe laying and as long as necessary to ensure
a safe and satisfactory installation The method of dewater
ing should be left open to the contractor provided excavation
stability and satisfactory drawdown are maintained
Precaution should be taken to avoid floating the pipe out
of the partially filled trench if dewatering is discontinued
too early
Backfill
Backfill should be placed so that the pipe will not be dis
placed or damaged Backfill material to a depth of one foot
over the crown of the pipe should consist of selected free
draining well graded material and be placed by hand or suitable
equipment and compacted to a density consistent with design
assumptions We recommend 90 to 95 compaction

90 compaction is recommended Some of the native materials is
suitable for backfilling provided that compressible organic
materials rubbish debris and other objectionable material
are first removed Rock greater than 12 inches should not
be permitted in the backfill
The upper zone of backfill should be constructed in a manner
corresponding the design assumptions for the pavement We
recommend 95 compaction to keep settlements within tolerable
limits
Before and during the backfilling operation precautions
should be taken to prevent floatation of pipelines due to
the entry of the large quantities of water into the trench
Settlements
Settlements of the pipe should be minimal provided good
construction practices are followed
Settlements of ground adjacent to trenches can be avoided by
preventing loss of ground due to oversteepened slopes or
insufficient shoring of vertical trenches
Additional precautions should be taken where the alignment
passes larger structure as excessive loss of ground in the
excavation could cause settlements to the adjacent structures
