PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling eleven borings
designated as Boring 14 16 and 17 extending to depths
ranging from about 11 5 to 102 5 feet below the existing pavement surface Two of the
borings Boring Nos 2 and 5 were drilled inside the existing Don Quijote Store three of
the borings Boring and 9 were drilled around the perimeter of the future
Target Store building site while the other six borings were drilled in the future parking
areas
In addition six shallow borings designated as SB 1 through SB 6 were drilled to a
depth of about 2 feet below the existing pavement surface for collection of bulk samples
of the near surface soils to evaluate their pavement support characteristics and potential
use as fill materials for this project The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration program conducted for the project the project site
appears to be underlain by highly variable subsurface conditions across the footprint of
the future store building Weak and compressible soil deposits were encountered in the
upper 16 feet of the subsurface in Boring No 3 and between about 16 and 53 feet of the
subsurface in Boring No 7 However these soft and or loose compressible soil deposits
were generally absent in the upper subsurface within Boring and 9 drilled
across the future store building footprint as described below
In Boring No 2 drilled at the east side of the building site very stiff sandy clays
and medium dense beach sands were encountered to a depth of approximately 13 feet
below the existing slab surface Coralline detritus materials consisting of medium dense
sands with gravel were then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 53 3 feet below the existing pavement surface
In Boring No 3 drilled at the southeastern corner of the building site soft sandy
clays and loose beach sands were encountered at depths between about 2 5 and 16 feet
below the existing pavement surface Coralline detritus materials consisting of loose to
medium dense sands were then encountered and extended to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 53 feet below the existing pavement surface
In Boring No 5 drilled at the center of the building site very stiff sandy clays and
medium dense silty sands were encountered to a depth of approximately 20 feet below

SITE GRADING
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated finished grades for the new
development we anticipate that minimal site grading work will be required for the
proposed construction The following grading items are addressed in the subsequent
subsections
Site Preparation
Fill and Backfills
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
A Geolabs representative should monitor the site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification
process and to confirm whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris deleterious materials existing structures to
be demolished and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill and or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm
natural materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft soils should not be re used as fill materials
and should be properly disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures and pavements to be demolished
should be completely removed Over excavations resulting from the demolition
operations should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing
utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should
be properly backfilled with granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete or Controlled Low Strength Material under low pressure
In general the over excavated subgrades and areas designated to receive fills
should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to
the maximum dry density
Fills and Backfills
Laboratory gradation and CBR tests were performed on the bulk samples of the
near surface soils upper 2 feet collected at selected locations across the existing
parking lot Based on the test results obtained it is our opinion that the
near surface sandy and gravelly soils should be suitable for use as select granular
fill materials within the building footprint provided that the maximum particle size is
less than 3 inches in largest dimension
Imported materials to be used as select granular fill should be non expansive
granular material such as crushed coral or basalt The select granular fill should be
well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of
20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM D 1883 The material also should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use
Where groundwater is encountered within the excavations backfill materials
should consist of free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped on all sides with non woven filter fabric
The free draining granular materials should be used up to a level about 12 inches
above the groundwater level to facilitate compaction of the fill materials

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Fills and backfills within
3 feet of the pavement finished grade elevation should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction Aggregate base course and subbase materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction

LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between
and beyond the boring locations may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
Geolabs should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
