Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Ko Olina Beach
Club Timeshare Phases 4 5 project and our recommendations are presented in the

Project Considerations
The Ko Olina Beach Club Timeshare Phases 4 5 project site is located in the
Ko Olina area on the southwestern portion of the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project generally involves construction of a new
high rise building Hale Naia Tower and a parking structure

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions generally consisted of surface fills overlying
inter bedded layers of coralline detritus and coral formations The surface fills generally
consist of stiff to very stiff sandy and silty clays and medium dense clayey sands and the
surface fills extended down to depths of about 13 5 to 20 feet below the ground surface
The coralline detritus encountered below the surface fills consisted of medium dense to
dense coralline sands and gravel with pockets and or zones of loose sands and gravel In
some cases the coralline detritus consisted of slightly cemented zones of sands and
gravel
The surface fills were generally underlain by medium hard to hard coral formation
and coralline detritus extending down to the maximum depth explored of about 100 feet
below the ground surface It should be noted that the inter bedded nature of the coralline
detritus and coral formations may be considered highly variable across the project site
The coral formations encountered in the borings drilled generally consisted of algal coral
reef and cemented sands The cementation of the coral formations appeared to vary
considerably with materials ranging from competent algal coral reef formation to weak
coral and medium dense silty sands with coralline debris detritus The coralline detritus
encountered in our borings generally consisted of loose to dense sands silt and coralline
debris with weak to virtually no cementation
In addition a lens or layer of alluvium of about 5 feet thick was also encountered
in Boring No 302 at a depth of about 87 5 feet below the ground surface The alluvium
encountered generally consisted of very stiff clayey silts Groundwater was encountered
at depths of about 8 8 to 18 9 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of the field
exploration in 2005

Load Test Shaft
As part of the project specifications a dedicated 36 inch diameter load test shaft
was installed at the project site The load test shaft was excavated by the drilled shaft
subcontractor on Temporary casing was not installed for the load test shaft
however the top 6 8 feet of the drilled shaft excavation was drilled with a 40 inch diameter
auger The load test shaft extended to a depth of about 90 6 feet below the existing
ground surface The drilled shaft subcontractor lowered the reinforcing cage with an
Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage instrumentation into the drilled hole and
placed concrete in the load test shaft on
After the load test shaft was installed and cured a load test was performed by Load
Test Inc on The load test shaft was loaded to a maximum load of about
3 231 kips Complete details of the load test program are presented in the report prepared
by Load Test Inc entitled Report on Drilled Shaft Load Testing Osterberg Method
Olina Beach Club Phase IV Kapolei HI LT 9218 dated August 3 2006
Based on the results of the load test we recommended reducing the drilled shaft
lengths from the initially specified 55 feet to 50 feet below the bottom of foundation cap
elevation for the majority of the production drilled shafts with an allowable axial
compressive load capacity of 1 500 kips For the drilled shafts with an allowable axial
compressive capacity of 550 kips a drilled shaft length of 20 feet below the bottom of
foundation cap elevation was maintained
Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft subcontractor installed 147 production drilled shafts from
August 1 2006 through The as built diameters and lengths of the
production drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 7
of this letter report In general Geolabs representatives observed the drilled shaft
excavation placement of reinforcing cage and concrete operations during installation of
the production drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a SoilMec R 825 drill
rig equipped with 36 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar to the
load test shaft Because the drilled holes stayed open without significant caving in of the
sidewalls polymer slurry and temporary casing were not used during excavation of the
production drilled shafts After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip
elevations the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate
elevations with the use of a crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled
hole concrete was placed through a 4 inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom

During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was

Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was

monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance of slump

and subsequent amendments In addition it is our professional

allowable design load capacities
