In accordance with our fee proposal dated December 22, 2016, this letter report 
presents the results of our geotechnical engineering consultation for the Runway 8L 
Widening Phase 1 at Honolulu International Airport on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  
This letter report summarizes our findings and presents our geotechnical 
engineering recommendations resulting from our review of available boring information at 
the site and engineering analyses for the project.  These recommendations include the 
allowable bearing pressure for the design of the electric handhole and Land and Hold 
Short Operations (LAHSO) light foundations only.   
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located at the Honolulu International Airport on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  We understand that two electric handholes and six LAHSO lights are planned for 
the runway widening project.  The electric handholes and LAHSO lights are located near 
Station 97+00.  Based on the information provided, the electric handholes are about 4-foot 
wide by 4-foot long.  The electric handholes will generally extend about 5 feet below the 
pavement surface.  The LAHSO light foundations will generally extend about 3.5 feet 
below the pavement surface. 
We understand that it is desired to determine the allowable foundation bearing 
capacity for LAHSO light structures and electric handholes planned for the runway 
widening project. We will use the boring information included in the project drawings on 
Sheets 150 through 155 to determine the foundation bearing capacity.  The boring logs 
were prepared by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering.

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Based on the borings prepared by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering, the 
subsurface conditions at the electric handhole and LAHSO lights generally consist of 
16 to 17.5 inches of asphaltic concrete over silty coralline sand extending to depths of 
about 7 to 8 feet below the existing pavement surface.  The silty coralline sand was 
generally medium dense near the existing pavement surface.  The silty coralline sand 
graded to loose at a depth of about 5.5 feet below the existing pavement surface.  The silty 
coralline sand was underlain by soft clay extending to the maximum depth explored of 
about 10 feet below the existing pavement surface. 
Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 6.6 to 7.1 feet below the existing 
pavement surface.  It should be noted that groundwater levels may change due to tidal 
fluctuations, rainfall, seepage, time of year, seasonal precipitation, and other factors.  
ELECTRIC HANDHOLES AND LAHSO LIGHT FOUNDATIONS 
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the electric handholes and 
LAHSO light structures, we believe that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 
2,500 pounds per square foot (psf) may be used for the design of the shallow foundations 
supporting the electric handholes and LAHSO light structures.  This bearing value is for 
sizing the foundations based on dead-plus-live loads and may be increased by one-third 
(1/3) for transient loads, such as those caused by wind or seismic forces. 
The bottom of footing excavations should be prepared to firm and unyielding 
condition for a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placing reinforcing steel 
and/or concrete.  A Geolabs representative should observe the foundation excavations 
prior to placing the reinforcing steel and/or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing 
conditions.
