Compaction Report Mass Grading
Peacock Flats Roadway Project
Mokuleia Oahu Hawaii
From to we observed the earthwork and
grading operations performed for the above roadway project The roadway project
consisted of realignment of a section of Mokuleia Road and improvements to the Peacock
_ Flats Access Road at the washout area and at three 3 turnout areas This report
summarizes our observations and compaction tests performed at the above referenced
site
The above project site is located in the area formerly known as the Dillingham
Ranch in Mokuleia on the north shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii
Site Grading

grading operations consisted of cuts up to 25 feet and fills up to 12 feet The material on
the site was generally brown silty clay

being authorized to be on site to observe the roadway grading work Subsequent to
three 3 test pits were made into the previously compacted subbase

Fill Placement
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the existing ground
was scarified and recompacted The fills were then placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density for the compacted subgrade
material and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density for the select
borrow and base course materials

Since the on site clayey material generally has a higher potential to swell when
over compacted and the new roadway will not be heavily travelled a minimum 90
compaction was used for the compacted subgrade materials
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
accordance with the American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation
D 1556 Sand Cone Method and D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested in our
laboratory prior to use in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in accordance with ASTM test designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plates 1 and 2
The results of the compaction test are summarized and presented with this report
Based on the compaction tests and observations it is our opinion that the project
roadway grading has adequately been completed
