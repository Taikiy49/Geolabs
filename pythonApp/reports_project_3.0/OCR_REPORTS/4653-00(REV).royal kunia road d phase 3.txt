PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the eastern portion of the Royal Kunia Development Area in
the District of Kunia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Royal Kunia Road D Phase 3
project originally started construction in 1995 but was not completed due to a construction
conflict with an existing Board of Water Supply force main meter near the intersection of
Puia Street and Road D The grading work for the original construction was monitored by
Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc HGGI and is described in the grading memorandum by
HGGI entitled Royal Kunia Increment E Road D Phase Oahu Hawaii
Tax Map Key 51 dated The above grading memorandum by HGGI
is provided in Appendix A
The project includes the construction of the intersection at Puia Street and Road D to
complete the Road D Phase 3 project The location of the project site is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 We understand that the select borrow course for the roadway area at the
intersection of Puia Street and Road D was placed during the original construction
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading during roadway and sidewalk construction for the project site

ROADWAY AND SIDEWALK CONSTRUCTION
Geolabs Inc observed the site grading during roadway and sidewalk construction
work at the intersection of Road D and Puia Street for the Royal Kunia Road D Phase 3
project from March 1 2001 to March 7 2001 The site grading work consisted of
scarification and re compaction of the existing select borrow layer and placement and
compaction of base course within the roadway and sidewalk areas to achieve the design
finished grades for the project Delta Construction Corporation performed the roadway and
sidewalk construction work The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content of the
base course material from Makakilo Quarry were obtained from Grace Pacific Corporation
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the base course
material was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction
tests performed are summarized on Plate 3 of this report These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the referenced
tests it is our opinion that the site grading work at the intersection of Puia Street and
Road D for the Royal Kunia Road D Phase 3 project observed and tested by Geolabs in
the period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of
the recommendations in the following references

Grading Memorandum
The mass grading work for the above project was generally constructed with on site and
off site soils The fills were placed and compacted in thin layers A soil technician from
our office was present at the site on an intermittent basis to observe grading progress and
to take density tests Whenever fill operations were on a continuous basis a soil
technician usually visited the site daily
Grading Plans dated made by ParEn Inc were used as a guide for soil
testing purposes
A tabulation of the field density test results is attached Where low tests were noted the
area was rerolled and in most cases retested The density test results at the time and at
the locations taken were in our opinion in substantial conformance with the density
requirements of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended and with the
recommendations in our Preliminary Soil Report Royal Kunia Road Db Phase 3 dated
as applicable
Even though in our opinion the field density tests by our office conform to the density
requirements of the City s Ordinance the passage of time natural causes or construction
at or adjacent to the site may result in changes in soil conditions and the following
precaution is suggested
Regrading the site in the future by cutting filling or altering the drainage pattern
may cause ground instability in some situations For this reason site regrading
should be avoided or made under the guidance of a Soils Engineer
