Dear Mr Wright
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations for the pad construction of the single family residential
project located at Highway along Kawailoa Drive in the North
Shore area on the Island Oahu Hawaii The grading operations took place from July 6
through August 6 2010
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
agricultural parcel consisting of about 4 6 acres along Kawailoa Drive between Haleiwa
and Waimea on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Development of the above parcel includes
construction of a single family residence Construction of the building pad required
general fills of up to about 4 feet thick and non expansive capping fill of up to about
2 feet to achieve the finished grades
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Bob Cherry Construction performed the site grading operations which involved
clearing and grubbing subgrade preparation placement and compaction of the general fill
and the capping fill Geolabs started the site observations and field testing after the
clearing and grubbing operations and initial layers of fill were already in place
During placement of the general fill and subsequent non expansive capping fill
layers the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent of the
maximum dry density for the general fill and capping fill respectively The maximum dry

During the site grading operations samples of the imported materials utilized as

Field density tests were performed during the fill placement operations to confirm
our field observations A total of 30 field density tests were performed using the nuclear
probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Results of the compaction tests

implied by the methods used Capping fill assessed as unsatisfactory were removed from
the site and replaced with new materials until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading of
the above residential lot observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the proposed structure should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
to install gutter systems around the house structure and to divert discharge away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations because ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from foundations
Landscaping of the house lot by the homeowner is recommended to reduce the potential
for erosion and for altering the drainage patterns

recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the
tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
