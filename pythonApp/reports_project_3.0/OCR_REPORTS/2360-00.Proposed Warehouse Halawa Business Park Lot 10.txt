INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the two 2 proposed warehouse buildings at Lot
10 of the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley on the island of oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct two 2 warehouse buildings on Lot 10 of
the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley Oahu Hawaii The proposed
buildings are rectangular in shape approximately 60 feet and 190 feet in
plan dimension A mezzanine level is planned for both buildings Based
on the information provided to us the office warehouse buildings will he
constructed with exterior CMU walls and interior concrete colums
Concrete slab on grade will be utilized for the building floor
Construction of new utility lines and flexible asphalt pavements for the
access roadway and parking areas are anticipated

generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical

foundation design of the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Halawa Business Park in
Halawa Valley Oahu Hawaii This area was developed and graded in the
recent past The site development and grading consisted primarily of fill
placement
Currently the site is vacant The topography of the site is
generally level Along the property boundaries the lot is separated from
other commercial lots by slopes standing at a ratio of about 2H 1V The
lot is located at the end of a cul de sac

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the
surface It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide
adequate bearing support for the proposed structure and pavement
Our laboratory testing indicates that these soils have variable
expansion potential ranging from low to very high Therefore special
grading will be required to reduce potential distresses to the proposed
structure caused by heaving subsoils
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is planned
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2360 00 Page 5
Due to the variable expansion potential of the subsoils encountered
near the anticipated finish subgrade level we recommend that the areas
below the buildings be over excavated to a cept of at least thirty 30
inches below the slab subgrade level
When the cut reaches this over excavation level all structural and
pavement areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than three 3
inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
The replacement fill should be placed in maximm 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure The subgrades of all footings should be scarified and
recompacted to 95 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method
footings bearing on the select fill material which has been recompacted
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2360 00 Page 6
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimm of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the
foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected

Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine
is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in
maximum 10 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimm of
90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce
future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also he
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
GEOLABS HAWAI

strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recomendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from the Site Plan by Architects 2 Inc Elevations of the data
points were approximately estimated by interpolation from elevation
points on the Grading Plan by Community Planning Incorporated dated
The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
5 borings to depths ranging between 10 0 and 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface and obtaining surface samples at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rotary drill
rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
