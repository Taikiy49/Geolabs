PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site known as Parcel 2 is at Kapolei Village 6 within the existing Villages of
Kapolei development in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The planned
development will consist of residential buildings generally up to four stories in height We
envision that slabs on grade foundations with thickened edge footings are planned to support
the proposed residential structures The project will also include the installation of new utilities
including sewer water and drainage lines as well as construction of new roadways and
parking areas to serve the residential development A Project Location Map and a Site Plan
are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
Based on the topographic survey map the project site generally slopes down towards
the south at grades of about 2 percent Cuts and fills on the order of less than 5 feet thick are
anticipated for the grading work to achieve the final design grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
mass grading and utility installation operations

MASS GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the mass grading and utility installation work for the Kapolei Village 6
project from to The mass grading and utility installation
consisted of clearing and grubbing fills of up to about 5 feet in depth and placement and
compaction of fills and backfills to achieve the design finished grades Royal Contracting
Company performed the mass grading and utility installation work During the mass grading
and backfilling operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the pavement areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
During the mass grading and backfilling operations periodic field density tests were
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 46 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references
