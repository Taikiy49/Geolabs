DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicated that the two
reservoir sites are underlain at shallow depths by lava rock formations
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1568 10 Page 7
which could provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
structures
From a soils engineering point of view the two sites are feasible
for the proposed reservoir construction provided our recommendations are
followed
Reservoir Foundation
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that at the
proposed finished grade elevation 185 for the potable site and 194 for
the non potable site the reservoirs should be underlain by lava rock
formations
Foundations
mi
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the lava rock is
recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value up to
5000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the rock
formations The allowable bearing value may be increased by one
third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 16 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAIi
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to the
bottom of the utility trench and bear on rocky materials or the
trench should be backfilled with concrete to minimize footing
settlement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundation
All trench backfill away from the reservoir foundation should be
properly compacted to obtain a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock the footing
excavation should be probed at approximately 10 feet on centers
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 6 feet The probe
holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer or the project inspector to see
that these recommendations are carried out properly and to locate
any potential voids near the footing excavations

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the two sites are underlain at
fairly shallow depths by dense Aa basalt with weathered clinker
layers mantled with a thin layer of alluvial soil mixed with
cobbles and boulders
The more massive basalt in the cut slope will likely require
special excavating procedures such as hoe ram equipment or
blasting to make the proposed cut Blasting should be avoided at
the potable site due to the proximity of the existing reservoirs
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the boring core data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine the rock outcrops and the boring data and to make
their own interpretation

Construction Monitoring

monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
