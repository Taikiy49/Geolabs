performed for Building Nos 2 to 5 and the Manager s Building of the proposed Pali Ke
Kua Development in Princeville on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A previous report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Proposed Two
Story Building dated was performed
for Building No 1 located at the northern boundary of the site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Princeville area on the northern coast of the island
of Kauai Hawaii The existing condominiums border the site to the west a
golf course is to the east and Ka Haku Road forms the southern boundary of the site
The area slopes gently downward from east to west with existing ground elevations
ranging from 165 to 173 feet above Mean Sea Level The area was formerly used as a
pasture for the grazing of cattle and horses Grading of the area was being performed
at the time of our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau along the north shore
of the island of Kauai This plateau was built during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene
Epochs by the extrusion of basaltic lavas and related pyroclastics from the Pooku vent
of the Koloa Volcanic Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived from the in situ
weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual soils grade with depth to saprolite
a form of residual soil which retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent
rock
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling five 5 borings
to a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2839 20 Page 5
In general the soil conditions encountered in the borings appear relatively uniform
The borings generally encountered a surface fill layer between two and one half 2 5 and
three and one half 3 5 feet thick of very stiff to hard reddish brown clayey silt
Below the fill layer stiff to very stiff residual soils consisting of reddish brown to
brown clayey silts and silty clays varying in thickness between four and one half 4 5 to
five and one half 5 5 feet were encountered and were underlain by a saprolite deposit
consisting of stiff to very stiff brown clayey silts to the maximum drilled depth of the
borings
Groundwater was not encountered during the time of our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils within the project
site However modified grading techniques are required for the earthwork portion of the
proposed site improvements due to the relatively high in situ moisture content of the
subsoils and the relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed lightly loaded residential structures
Site Grading
Cuts and fills up to about three 3 feet are planned for the new building areas
At the time of our field exploration the grading work for the project was being
performed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2839 20 Page 6
All areas within the proposed grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off
site
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm natural material and the
resulting depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall areas most of the subsoils within
the top 15 feet had in situ moisture contents of about 40 to 60 percent at the time of our
exploration Therefore considerable drying of the excavated materials would normally be
required prior to their use as fill materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally occurs in the area
throughout the year complete drying of large quantities of soils might not be readily
achieved during mass grading operations To facilitate construction it is our opinion that
the normal 90 percent ASTM D 1557 78 compaction standard for general site fills as used
in dryer areas may be reduced to 85 percent for this project Our previous experience
in Princeville and other similar areas indicates that 85 percent compaction is generally
adequate for site fills under roadway and light building loads
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is our opinion that the
on site soils have low expansion potential and except for the high moisture content they
may be used as a source of fill and backfill materials if additional off site borrow is
required the material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior
to hauling to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed construction The material
should be non expansive with no particles greater than six 6 inches in size
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2839 20 Page 7
Fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of their maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench backfill and structural fill should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 85 percent maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills to be placed on slopes having ratio steeper than five horizontal to one vertical
5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm soil to properly bond the new fill to the
existing ground
Mechanical compaction equipment is highly suited to the materials encountered
at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used as a means to
attempt to compact the on site soils
Foundations
Based on the soil conditions encountered we believe that spread and continuous
footing foundations may be used to support the proposed residential structures
For the structure bearing on the in situ soils or fill compacted in accordance with
the recommendations presented in this report an allowable bearing value of up to 2 500
p s f may be utilized The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for short duration wind or seismic loading
individual spread footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen inches
below the nearest adjacent subgrade For interior post footings located under post and
beam house design due to potential drying up of the moist in situ soil under the house
will result in surface soil shrinkage we recommend that individual post footings should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2839 20 Page 8
be extended below this upper potential shrinkage zone which could be as much as four
four 4 to five 5 foot deep Some footing adjustment might be required if the post
footings are embedded above this potential shrinkage layer
The bottom of footing excavation should be compacted to a minimum of 85 of
their maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If loose or soft pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be over excavated to dense in situ soils or placed engineered fills and
replaced with granular material compacted to 95 maximum dry density
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the footing should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span across the trench to
reduce footing settlement which may otherwise result from potential settiement of the
trench backfill
Based on the boring data estimated footing settlements under the design loads
are anticipated to be on the order of less than one half inch total
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structures may be provided by passive
soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to
compute the frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the compacted
foot of depth may be used against the buried vertical faces of the footings to determine
the passive soil pressure

Slabs On Grade
Slab on grade construction is a more preferable foundation support system for
lightly loaded single family structures compared with a post and beam design due to soil
shrinkage under a raised structure supported on individual post footings We recommend
that a minimum four inch concrete slabs be placed over a four inch layer of No 3B Fine
gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 for the proposed residential structures The thickened edges
of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 16 inches below the lowest adjacent
subgrade
To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material a moisture
barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
Our laboratory test results indicate the on site soils in general have low swelling
potential at their natural moisture contents and exhibit a tendency to shrink when allowed
to dry Therefore the slab subgrade should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out
using perimeter footing embedment Trees and landscape vegetation should be placed
away from the footing slab areas to reduce excessive soil moisture suction and root
growth

The 12 inch select borrow along with the rest of pavement in Option 1 provide
sufficient stiffness to sustain the traffic load In the absence of select borrow or when its
thickness is reduced as in Option 2 it is important to maintain the integrity of the base
course The stiffness of the base course will be severely reduced when mixed with the
finer materials from below This mixture could occur if water is allowed to stay in the base
course and soften the underlying material Because of the high rainfall in the area it is
likely that water could be trapped in the base course unless some precautions are
undertaken The function of geotextile is to provide a separation between the base
course and the underlying material The separation maintains the free draining nature of
the base course by preventing mixture of base course with the underlying fine grained
materials The geotextile should be a Polyfelt TS 600 or equivalent Care should be
undertaken not to puncture the geotextile when placing the base course on geotextile
We understand that part of the pavement at the project site has been constructed
The construction was for a design of 6 inch base course and 2 inch asphaltic concrete
No select borrow was used We believe that this design may not be suitable for the
anticipated subgrade condition and the local climate Option 2 is provided as a remedy
if the total pavement thickness has to be kept at 8 inches In addition to geotextile we
recommend that the pavement be reinforced with geogrid The geogrid should be
embedded in the lower one half to three fourths of the asphaltic concrete A Tensar SS2
geogrid or equivalent should be used
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction and the above design sections may be revised if necessary The
recommended sections consider medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided
adjacent to paved areas Due to the high rainfall in the area it is essential that the road
subgrades and shoulders be shaped to drain water away form the pavement sections

Weepholes should be provided in the parking area drainage inlet structures in order to
relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated to be required for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine Gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials such as the above No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
should also be used for the trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to reduce
the compaction effort required thus minimizing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper potion of the subsequent trench backfill from a level one foot above the
pipes to the top of subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 8 inch
horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 85 of the maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to minimize future ground subsidence
Below pavement areas within one foot of the finished subgrade the subgrade level
compaction criteria should be increased to 90

Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be

construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the construction phases of the work This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction in underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were determined by taping from reference points
shown on a site grading plan prepared by Wilson Okamoto and Associates dated April
3 1992 Elevations of the field borings were determined by interpolation from contours
and elevation points on the same plan The physical locations and elevations of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
samples
Classification Tesis
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or stability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluations the materials for use as foundation
support and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate C 1
