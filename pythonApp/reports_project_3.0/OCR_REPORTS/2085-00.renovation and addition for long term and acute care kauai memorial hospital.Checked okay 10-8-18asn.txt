INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Renovation and Addition for Long
Term and Acute Care at the Kauai Veterans Memorial Hospital in Waimea on
the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an addition for long Term and Acute Care
to the existing Kauai Veterans Memorial Hospital in Waimea Kauai The
proposed building addition will measure by about 63 by 137 feet in plan
It is our understanding that the proposed hospital addition will bea
one story structure with steel trusses and exterior concrete walls
imposing a design loading of about 3500 pounds per linear foot on the wall
footing foundations
Minimal on site grading is anticipated for the proposed building
construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading
foundations and pavement of the proposed building addition project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2085 00 Page 3
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated June
13 1988 to the State of Hawaii Department of Accounting and General
Services Division of Public Works Management Branch The scope of work
was limited to
1 logging of six 6 borings by MAS Drilling Co to depths
ranging from about 13 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observation of the surficial soil conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading foundation and pavement
design recommendations and
6 preparation of this report presenting the design recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
Kauai Veterans Memorial Hospital is located to the west of Waimea
Canyon Drive in Waimea on the island of Kauai Hawaii The proposed
hospital addition is to be built in an empty grass lawn between the east
side of the existing hospital facilities and Waimea Canyon Drive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2085 00 Page 4
The project site is generally level with ground elevations ranging
from approximately 33 to 35 feet above Mean Sea Level
It was reported that a large crack was noted about six months ago in
a wall of the existing hospital structure In addition our site
reconnaissance indicates that local areas of the existing building appears
to have experienced heaving which resulted in cracking of the concrete
slabs on grade asphalt pavements and a column base
SUBSOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed building site were explored
by drilling six 6 borings to depths of about 13 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of these borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is mantled by about 2 5 to 4 0 feet of very
stiff to hard reddish brown to brown clay over hard vesicular basalt to
the maximm drilled depth of 20 feet in the borings
Laboratory testing indicates that the surface layer is generally
moderately to highly expansive when saturated from a dry condition
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed a thin mantle of moderately to highly
expansive soil with low in situ moistures over hard basalt formation at
the project site We believe that the expansive soil layer is also
present below the existing hospital and it may have contributed to the
soil heaving problems noted
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2085 00 Page 5
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the exterior wall footing foundations be placed directly on the hard
basalt to support the proposed structure The embedded portions of the
exterior walls may then form a buried cut off wall to reduce moisture
infiltration into the expansive soil layer below the new building
The embedment of the interior footing foundations may be reduced toa
minimum of 1 5 feet Where the new footings are adjacent to the footings
of the existing hospital building the new footings should be placed ata
level equal to the existing footings
The expansive subgrade soil at the proposed slab areas should be
over excavated and backfilled to reduce the potential of soil heaving
under the slabs
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated subsoil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 5000 p s f or
2500 p s f may be used for footings bearing directly on the hard basalt
or the very stiff to hard on site surface soil respectively
Based on the boring data the exterior wall footings should be
embedded about 2 5 to 4 feet below the existing grade to bear on the rock
formation The interior footings should be placed a minimm of 1 5 feet
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The allowable footing bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Estimated settlements under the design loading are anticipated
to be minimal for both types of footing foundations directly bearing on
hard rock or stiff to hard clay
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2085 00 Page 6
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be obtained
by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional coefficient
of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance between the bottom
of the footings and the hard rock or compacted on site soils In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to determine the passive pressure against the vertical buried
faces of the footings
Concrete Slab On Grade
Based on our laboratory swell test results the clayey subgrade soils
below the proposed slabs are expansive and may heave considerably if
saturated in the future Therefore we recommend that the expansive soil
below the proposed slab areas be over excavated down to minimm depth of
2 0 feet below the cushion layer in order to reduce the effects of soil
heaving on the slabs
The over excavation should be backfilled with non expansive borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral which should be
placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches thick and compacted to a
minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Where exposed or not confined by the exterior wall footings the
thickened edge slabs should be embedded a minimm of 3 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade to form a cut off wall see Plate 3
To reduce future post construction swelling of the wumderlying
expansive soil we recommend that the on site soil remaining below the
over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slabs and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect against
moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slabs

Site Grading
At the onset of earthwork the area within contract grading limit
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable materials such
as rubbish and vegetation should be removed and disposed of properly
off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated material
Prior to backfilling of the over excavation the expansive clay soil
below the non expansive backfill should be scarified to a depth of at
least 6 inches below the cleared grade elevation moisture conditioned to
above optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimm of 85 and no
more than 90 of its ASTM D dry density
Ioose spots encountered at the cleared subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimm of
90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from the proposed and existing building
Drainage
The finished grade outside the proposed building should be shaped to
shed water away from the new foundations and slabs and to avoid ponding
condition
It is also advised that gutter systems be installed around the new
structure to collect and divert run off water far away from the
foundations and slabs Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or provided with
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from permeating into the
expansive subsoil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2085 00 Page 8
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the proposed foundation and slab system since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the footings immediately after setting of the footing concrete to
reduce the potential of creating a ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the proposed slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the earthwork and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

If exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping from
the existing structures as indicated on the topographic survey map dated
for the project site prepared by Esaki Surveying and
Mapping Inc Elevations of the data points were determined by
interpolation from elevation points on the plan The physical locations
and elevations of the field data points should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
any profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formilation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation in
water level may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six
6 borings to depths of 13 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 MAS
Drilling Company drilled the borings by using a truck mounted drill rig
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 0 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by coring with an NX core barrel
The Core Recovery REC and Rock Quality Designation RQD of the core
samples are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
