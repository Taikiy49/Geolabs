Dear Mr. Kaneshiro: 
This addendum to our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, Hawaii 
Baptist Academy Elementary School Improvements, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated 
May 5, 2022, provides an alternative pavement section for the project. 
We understand that an alternative pavement section is desired to reduce the 
amount of imported material needed for the pavements.  Based on our analysis, the 
following alternative pavement section utilizing geogrids may be considered. 
Alternative Flexible Pavement 
  2.5-Inch Asphaltic Concrete 
  8.0-Inch Aggregate Base Course (95 Percent Relative Conpaction) 
11.0-Inch Aggregate Subbase Course (95 Percent Relative Compaction) 
21.5-Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Geogrid (Tensar NX850 or Equal) on 
Filter Fabric (Mirafi 180N or Equal) on Moist Compacted Subgrade 
The aggregate subbase material should meet the requirements stipulated 
in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction, City and 
County of Honolulu (September 1986). Aggregate subbase material should be 
moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture content, placed in level lifts not
