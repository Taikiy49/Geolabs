Dear Ms Murano
As part of our on going geotechnical engineering observation services at the project
site we have retrieved three bulk samples of the subgrade soils from the proposed access
road at the site As requested we have performed laboratory tests on the subgrade
materials for the pavement design of the Parker Wells Phase II project The following is a
summary of the laboratory tests performed on the bulk samples retrieved from the site by our
field representative
Modified Proctor ASTM D 1557 and California Bearing Ratio CBR ASTM D 1883
tests were performed on the three samples retrieved to evaluate the material for pavement
design purposes The results of the modified Proctor and CBR tests are summarized on the
attached Summary of Laboratory Test Results Plate 1
The proposed pavement section shown on the plans consist of 2 inches of asphalt
concrete AC over 6 inches of base course on 6 inches of select borrow subbase course
Sheet C 30 of the plans Based on the results of the CBR tests we believe that the select
borrow subbase course currently shown on the plans may be omitted from the pavement
section Therefore the revised pavement section may consist of 2 inches of AC over
6 inches of base course
It should be emphasized that the subgrade materials for the access road should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction based on
ASTM D 1557 as stipulated in the project specifications
