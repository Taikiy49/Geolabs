This report presents the results of our soil investigation
at the above referenced project The purpose of the study
was to investigate possible causes of the walkway damages
and to recommend remedial measures
The soil investigation consisted of a review of the founda
tion drawings for the affected structures a review of
selected pile driving records visual observations at
the site test borings near the damaged structures hand
dug test pits around the structure foundations and laboratory
testing of selected subsurface soil samples

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the eastern quadrangle
Building B of the Bachelor Officer Quarters Mess
Complex in the Pearl Harbor Naval Station
Building B consists of a quadrangle of four 2 story
concrete buildings surrounding an open courtyard area
The area around Building B is flat To the south of
the building complex the site slopes down 6 to 8 feet to
another level area
SITE GRADING
Grading plans for the project dated March 9 1973 indicated
that about 3 to 4 feet of fill was placed in the northern
half of the quadrangle and up to 10 feet of fill in the
southern half
STRUCTURE FOUNDATIONS
Construction plans for the project indicate that the 2 story
structures and second floor walkways were designed to be
supported by pile foundations The piles specified were
16 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles
designed for 40 ton load capacity

FIELD OBSERVATIONS
The following visual observations were noted at the project
site
Cracks were noted in the interior closer to courtyard
area column supporting the walkway between the northern
and western buildings The cracking pattern indicates
that the interior column has moved downward with respect
to the exterior column and has caused the second floor
walkway to twist and crack
Small cracks were noted in the interior column supporting
the second floor walkway between the western and southern
buildings The cracks indicate that the interior column
has moved downward in relation to the exterior stairway
wall
Sinkholes some more than a foot deep were noted around
the southwestern portion of the quadrangle The sink
holes appear to be caused by the washing out of the
fines in the upper fill into the lower rubble fill
The sinkholes were encountered around the sprinkler
heads and gutter down spouts
Evidence of ground subsidence was visible around the
building complex Some tilting and differential
settlement were noted on some of the sidewalks

FIELD EXPLORATION
Test Borings
Two borings were drilled to about 65 foot depths near the
damaged columns Ten other borings were attempted but
could not penetrate the rubble fill These borings were
abandoned at 7 to 17 foot depths after encountering concrete
rubble with steel rebars The location of these borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the borings the subsoils at the site can be
generalized as follows
0 to 3 feet Reddish Brown Clayey Silt
Recent Fill
3 to 20 feet Rubble fill consisting of cobbles
boulders concrete slabs steel etc
20 to 50 feet Gray Clay soft swamp deposit
50 to 65 feet Brown Clayey Silt over carbonate
sandy clay stiff alluvium
Water was encountered at about 14 foot depths in the borings
Test Pits
Three test pits were excavated by hand at the two damaged
column areas to expose the grade beams and piles

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Walkway Column between Northern Western Buildings
eee i ad
The damage here is a result of differential settlement
between the interior column which is settling with the
surrounding ground and the adjacent column which is
pile supported and has virtually no settlement
Pile driving records for the piles under the two walkway
columns indicated that these piles were driven to 65 foot
depths into the underlying stiff alluvium These piles
should be capable of supporting the walkway column loads
A new grade beam connecting the two existing piles can be
constructed and used to support the walkway columns
Walkway Column between Southern Western Buildings
The damage here is a result of differential settlement
between the interior column and the adjacent columns
The pile driving records for the piles under the walkway
columns indicated that the pile under the stairway wall
was driven down to 61 feet into the stiff underlying
alluvium However the pile under the interior column
was only driven to 23 feet

Based on the borings this pile is apparently resting
on some rubble fill just above the soft clay deposit
The soft clay deposit is probably experiencing settle
ment due to the previous rubble fill and later additional
fill placed for the BOQ project site grading Since the
pile did not penetrate the soft layer it was probably
being pulled down by this general ground settlement
Generally this type of consolidation settlement can be
expected to decrease with time Since the project was
completed several years ago additional major settlement
is not anticipated however some long term settlement
should be expected The additional anticipated settle
ment is difficult to predict because of the unknown load
history of the rubble fill and unknown settlement to date
If the additional long term settlement is not tolerable
a new pile will be required for the foundation The
pile should be driven down into the alluvium layer to
depths of about 65 feet Predrilling or spudding will be
required to penetrate the 20 foot rubble fill
Spread footing is not recommended here due to additional
settlement anticipated from the long term consolidation
of the soft clay material Additional uneven settlement
could also occur due to piping action of the upper rubble
fill

Sinkholes and Sidewalk Settlements
As mentioned the sinkholes and much of the sidewalk
settlement is caused by the piping action of the upper
fine material being washed into the voids of the rubble
fill Grout should be pumped into the sinkholes to fill
the underlying voids It is impossible to predict where
the sinkholes will occur therefore future sinkholes will
have to be grouted as they occur To help to reduce future
sinkholes the site grading should be improved to rapidly
drain surface water particularly at the gutter down spouts
away from the site and prevent ponding
Some of the sidewalks are being affected by the areal settle
ments To reduce the tilting of the sidewalks and resulting
damage to the building plaster finish the sidewalks should
be separated from the structures on piles

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
ey
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling two borings to depths of 63 to 66 feet below
the existing ground surface Ten other borings were
attempted but could not penetrate the rubble These
borings were stopped at 7 to 17 foot depths The locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer or geologist who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were reviewed
by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 9
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
