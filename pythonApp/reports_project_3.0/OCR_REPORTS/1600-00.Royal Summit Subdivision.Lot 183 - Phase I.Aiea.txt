Soil Engineering Report
Royal Summit Subdivision
Lot 183 Phase I
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will
be a two story structure approximately 50 by 80 feet in plan dimensions
The general layout of the house foundation is shown on the Site Plan
Plate l

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage the
entire basement level and portions of the main floor level of the house
The house will be of wood frame construction
To accommodate the basement level CMU retaining walls generally
less than 6 feet high are planned under the front portion of the main
floor level for grade separation
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southern side of Kaahele Street
in the Royal Summit Phase I Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad is situated along the front portion of the
site The ground surface then slopes downward at about a 5 1 H V ratio
to a second level pad at the rear of the lot
A 5 foot wide drainage easement with a concrete lined drainage
ditch and a 6 foot wide sewer easement are situated along the rear
property line

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 183 to depths ranging from
6 5 to 10 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate l
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass grading plan
prepared by Community Planning Inc dated for Royal
Summit Phase I indicated that Lot 183 was cut up to 4 feet below the
natural grade at the front portion of the lot and received up to 5 feet
of compacted fills over the natural grade at the back portion of the lot
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1600 00 Page 4
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural
soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral The on site soil under the garage
driveway and house areas should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch
minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris

W O 1600 00 Page 5
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
where the slope is steeper than 5 1 H V the fills should be keyed and
benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Garage and Driveway On Grade Slabs
The laboratory tests indicate that the near surface soils have low
swelling potential if maintained near the optimum moisture content
However if allowed to dry out the soils may exhibit moderate swelling
potential Therefore the slab subgrade must be kept moist after
excavation and protected from drying during the following days required
for preparation prior to final concrete placement
In order to minimize the swelling potential of the soil we
recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive soil such as
crusher waste be placed below the slab and cushion layer

It is important that the soils engineer should inspect the above
subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying
soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in sucha
way that the time period between the initial excavation and final
concrete placement at each lot should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
We recommend that 12 inch deep perimeter footings be used measured
from the lowest adjacent grade to act as a cut off wall against the
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting around the
slab
We further recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion
fill be provided below the slab In order to protect against moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations

Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possibile and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations

Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
