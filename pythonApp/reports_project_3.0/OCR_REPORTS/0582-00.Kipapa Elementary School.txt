INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed library and classroom build
ings at the Kipapa Elementary School Mililani Town Oahu
Hawaii
_The purpose of this investigation was to explore the site s
subsurface conditions and to determine foundation require
ments for the proposed buildings
W 2 May 6 1975
Proposed Construction
It is presently planned to construct the proposed one story
structures of concrete and masonry with timber roof trusses
on block bearing walls It is anticipated that concrete
slabs on grade will be utilized for the floors
Site Description
The existing Kipapa Elementary School is located in Mililani
_ Town of Central Oahu Hawaii The present facility consists
of twelve buildings The proposed library building will
adjoin the existing administration building on the south in
the area southwest of the existing two story classroom
Geology
The site is situated in the Schofield Plateau formed by the
basaltic flows of the Koolau Volcanic Series during the
Pliocene Age The flows reach a thickness of 3 100 feet and
are composed of Pahoehoe and Aa lavas ranging from 10 to 80
feet in thickness The site soils belong to the Wahiawa
Series which is derived from weathered igneous rock

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
library and classroom buildings Conventional spread and line
foundations can be utilized upon the underlying very stiff to
extremely stiff silty clay
Foundations
The proposed buildings may be supported on conventional spread
or line foundations bearing in the very stiff silty clay
and designed for allowable bearing pressures of
The foundations should bear a minimum of twelve inches below
the final adjacent grade
Oo
W O May 6 1975
The estimated settlements for this loading is inch total
and inch differential
The foundation bearing pressures may be increased to 4 000
psf when considering short term wind and seismic loadings
Slabs On Grade
Qur laboratory testing indicates the on site surface soils
are not expansive Slabs on grade construction can therefore
be utilized without special undercutting or compaction pro
cedures It is recommended that a minimum of four inches of
granular material be utilized beneath the floor slabs This
material should be well graded gravel less than 3 4 inch
maximum dimension and should be compacted and graded to pro
vide a level surface and drainage beneath the floor slab
Where moisture sensitive floor coverings will be utilized or
dampness in the floor slab would be undesirable a plastic
vapor barrier should be utilized between the concrete floor
slabs and the granular base material If separate spread or
line foundations are utilized it is recommended that the
floor slabs be divorced from the load bearing members to pre
vent distresses due to slight differential settlements
