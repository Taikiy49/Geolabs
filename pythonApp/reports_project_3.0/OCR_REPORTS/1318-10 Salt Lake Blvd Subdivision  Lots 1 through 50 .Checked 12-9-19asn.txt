earthwork and grading operations at the above referenced site

performed at the above lots

The grading operations at the above referenced lots consisted of
cut areas up to 8 feet thick and fills up to 25 feet thick The
fill materials used consisted of on site grayish brown silty sand
with some volcanic tuff gravel mudrock and grayish brown clayey
silt with volcanic tuff gravel Also various fill materials
were imported The fill material used in the upper 5 feet
generally has a low swelling potential
During the fill placement operations periodic field density
tests were done in accordance with the American Society for
Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone
Method Based on the field observations and compaction tests
we believe that the fill lots have been compacted adequately

soils were tested prior to being used in the fill The maximum
soil density and optimum moisture content was established in our
laboratory in accordance with the ASTM D 1557 test designation

Recommendations
Foundations
For the proposed light residential structures spread footing
foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs should be
set back from the tops of the slopes a minimum distance of 5
feet Post footings located close to the tops of slopes should
be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face Post footings
away from the tops of slopes should be placed a minimum of 12
inches below the finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The thickened edge
around the slab on grade should extend a minimum of 8 inches
below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Subdrain
Following heavy rains water seepage was noted at the rear of Lot
15 A subdrain was installed to intercept the seepage below the
surface and drain it away from the lot The subdrain should not
be removed

We recommend that the soils engineer should test and approve all
imported fill material inspect the backfill placement and
recompaction and footing excavations prior to concrete
placement

recommendations need to be revised for specific house and lot
conditions
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within
the lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the
observation of a soils engineer
