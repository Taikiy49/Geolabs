PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the intersection of Ft Weaver Road and Keaunui Drive in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The planned improvements involved widening
of approximately 900 feet along Ft Weaver Road including improvements to the sidewalk
and shoulder area A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1 and
2 1 through 2 4 respectively
The new roadway along Ft Weaver Road was widened to accommodate a total of
seven traffic lanes a 16 foot wide center median two 4 foot wide median shoulders
two 10 foot wide outside shoulders and 10 and 6 foot wide sidewalks The traffic lanes are
about 12 feet wide The new roadway along Keaunui Drive is comprised of a total of six traffic
lanes The traffic lanes are 10 and 12 feet wide In addition a bike path was constructed near
the shoulder area The site grading work involved cuts and fills generally up to about 2 feet
thick In addition electrical utility lines were installed
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading operations and infrastructure installation

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Ft Weaver Road and
Keaunui Drive Intersection project from to The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order
of up to about 2 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades Albert C Kobayashi Inc
performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site grading operations
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 12
These tests were performed at the site on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references
