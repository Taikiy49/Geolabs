As requested we are providing the following preliminary recommendations for design of
the retaining walls at the subject project We understand that approximately 1 000 lineal feet of
retaining walls between 4 to 10 feet high are planned at the site The retaining walls will be
located at approximately Stations 25 00 through 28 50 both sides of the road Stations 30 00

We believe that shallow foundations may be used to support the walls An allowable
bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design of footings
This is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 14 when considering transient
loads such as wind or seismic forces The footings should be embedded a minimum of two feet
2 below the lowest adjacent grade
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive pressure acting against the
near vertical faces of foundations and friction between the base of the footing and the bearing
soil Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches from finish grade should be neglected
A friction coefficient of 0 3 may be used between the footing and the supporting subgrade
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due to
adjacent soils and surcharge effects The recommended lateral earth pressures for design of
cantilever wails expressed in equivalent fluid pressures are presented below

These lateral earth pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused
by groundwater trapped behind the walls Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the
build up of hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a one 1 to two
foot 2 wide zone of permeabie material No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
equivalent immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the
base of the wall discharging to a discharge facility As an alternative a prefabricated drainage
board may be used instead of permeable material The prefabricated drainage board should also
contain a perforated pipe at the base
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a horizontal
distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the design For uniform
surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a rectangular distribution with uniform
pressure equal to 30 percent of the surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall
may be used in design of cantilever wails
