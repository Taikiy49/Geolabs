Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covering an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the following
five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent geologic
time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the
project site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe
type lava flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava is
typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is characterized
by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous
rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when the lava is in a molten state and they represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as primary water transmission features
because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
The Maniniowali Development project site is in the Kaupulehu area in the
District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the original site topography
prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular or hummocky lava flow terrain with
frequent topographic depressions separated by adjacent higher relief mounds and low
ridge features
Based on our observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation and clinker materials It
should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly encountered in
basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube features were
observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should be noted that
near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass grading
operations were generally collapsed and the loose materials from within the collapsed

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan the original ground surface elevations within the limits
of grading ranged from about 164 to 174 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the majority of
Lot No 31 Lot No 31 was generally graded to a finished pad elevation of about
169 feet MSL Mass grading at Lot No 31 generally consisted of excavations of up to
about 5 feet deep and placement of up to about 4 feet of new fills to achieve the design
grades
At the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade
preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential
for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures is to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or
voids contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of
practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast
on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground was
generally leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 bulldozer working the surface for about
four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with
the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally
smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within the top 2 feet of the
lots within the fill areas of the pad consisted of structural fill materials
It should be noted that general fills consisting of boulders of up to about 24 inches
in dimension generally were placed about 2 feet below the finished grades within fill areas
of the lots and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders generally were
placed about 6 feet or more horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that the mauka portions of
