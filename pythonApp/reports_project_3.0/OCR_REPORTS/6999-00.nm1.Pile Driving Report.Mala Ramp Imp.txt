In accordance with our fee proposal dated August 1, 2013, we have observed the 
pile driving operations for the Mala Boat Launching Ramp Loading Dock Improvements 
project on March 27, 2014 and March 28, 2014. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Mala Boat Launching Ramp Loading Dock project is located in the northern 
area of Lahaina Town, on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The existing loading dock and boat 
launch slab-on-grade has experienced significant deterioration over the years and it is now 
planned for reconstruction including the loading dock concrete decks, walkways, boat 
launch slab-on-grade, asphalt concrete pavement section and restoration of the foundation 
support piles. 
In addition, there are four existing concrete piles that were found to be structurally 
unfit for reconstruction; therefore, replacement of each pile is required. The DLNR has 
designed in-house, 24-inch diameter composite “HarborPile” 50 feet long to be driven by 
conventional hammers to end bearing refusal on the underlying rock formation. We 
understand that the DLNR desires geotechnical engineering observations by one of our 
staff engineer/geologists to log the installation of the replacement piles and provide the pile 
logs upon completion. Please note that Geolabs is not the design engineer of record for 
this foundation design.
