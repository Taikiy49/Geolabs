PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the Hilo Harbor Pier 4 Container Yard area in Hilo on 
the Island of Hawaii.  We understand that the existing asphaltic concrete (AC) surfacing 
on the concrete pier structure is raveling creating depressions in the AC surfacing.  In 
addition, we understand that the pier structure was recently constructed. 
Based on the as-built drawings, the pier structure includes a 7-inch concrete 
topping with a 1-inch asphaltic concrete surfacing.  We understand that the purpose of 
the 1-inch AC surfacing was to protect the underlying concrete topping.  The AC surfacing 
extends the entire length of the pier and is about 36 feet wide. 
We understand that a more durable material should be considered for the new pier 
surfacing replacement.

SITE RECONNAISSANCE 
A site reconnaissance was conducted on December 11, 2018.  The available as-built 
drawings indicate that the AC surfacing layer over the concrete pier topping is about 1-inch 
thick.  Confirmation of the thickness of the AC surface layer, about 1-inch thick, was made 
during our site reconnaissance. Based on our site reconnaissance, raveled and broken up 
fragments of AC, and depressions were observed at localized areas along the concrete pier 
structure.  Portland cement concrete was exposed at the bottom of the depressions in the 
AC.  The AC fragments and depressions were observed in the areas where steel ramps are 
temporarily placed when a barge is docked at Pier 4.  The locations where the steel ramps 
are placed are between Mooring Bitt Nos. 3 and 4 and between Mooring Bitt Nos. 5 and 6. 
Based on conversations with the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation – 
Harbors Division representative and our observations, it appears that the AC surfacing layer 
is being damaged due to the routine operations of Young Brothers Hawaii.  When a barge 
is docked at Pier 4, steel ramps are placed from the concrete hardstand to the barge to allow 
container handlers to access the barge to load/unload containers.   The steel ramps 
generally span over the concrete pier.  However, during tidal fluctuations, the barge drops 
during low tide.  This causes the steel ramps to drop and make contact with the AC surfacing 
layer.  When the container handlers travel on the steel ramps, the steel ramps scrape the 
AC surfacing layer causing the AC to ravel and break up resulting in depressions.  
Photographs of the AC surfacing distress and the steel ramp being used are shown on 
Plates 3 through 5. 
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the probable cause of the observed damage to the AC surfacing layer, we 
recommend removing the distress AC and replacing it with a more durable surface layer 
in lieu of repaving the damaged areas with new AC.  Therefore, the installation of a bonded 
concrete overlay on top of the existing concrete topping may be considered in the areas 
where the steel ramps are used to access the docked barge.  It should be noted that using 
a bonded concrete overlay may not be a long-term solution; however, the bonded concrete 
overlay should last longer than a new AC overlay.  We believe that the following items may 
be critical to the performance of the bonded concrete overlay. 
Condition of the Existing Concrete Topping 
Preparation of Existing Concrete Topping Surface 
Jointing of the Bonded Concrete Overlay 
The condition of the existing concrete topping should be considered when designing 
a bonded concrete overlay.  In general, the existing concrete should be in good condition 
with minimal surface distress.  Significant cracks in the existing concrete topping could 
reflect through the new bonded concrete overlay.  Since the concrete pier is relatively new, 
we anticipate that the condition of the existing concrete topping is in relatively good 
condition.

Due to the relatively thin overlay thickness, a good bond between the existing 
concrete topping and the new concrete overlay is critical to the performance of the concrete 
overlay.  Therefore, proper surface preparation of the existing concrete topping surface 
should be conducted prior to placing the concrete overlay.  The existing AC surfacing layer 
should be thoroughly removed to expose the existing concrete topping layer.  After removal 
of the existing AC surfacing layer, the existing concrete topping surface should be 
roughened by using the sand blasting method or other method recommended by the 
structural engineer.  After roughening, the existing concrete topping surface should be 
thoroughly cleaned to remove loose material.  Cleaning may consist of vacuum-sweep or 
sweeping the surface followed by blowing compressed air. 
Jointing of the bonded concrete overlay is another item that should be considered in 
the design.  Joints should match the location and width of the existing concrete topping in 
order to create a monolithic structure and to reduce reflective cracking.   To reduce curling 
and warping stresses, intermediate sawcut joints between the matched joints should be 
considered.  
Due to the specialized nature of the thin concrete overlay, we recommend that an 
experienced structural engineer be retained to provide guidance and recommendations on 
the mix design, detailed design and installation of the thin concrete overlay.
