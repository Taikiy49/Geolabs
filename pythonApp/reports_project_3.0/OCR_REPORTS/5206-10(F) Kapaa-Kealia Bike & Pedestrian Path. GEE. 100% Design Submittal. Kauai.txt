PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

bike and pedestrian path along the shoreline from Lihi Park to Ahihi Point A 10 to 12 foot

Beach Park to Ahihi Point The project will also include bridge improvements at Waikaea
and Moikeha Canal Bridges and Kapaa Stream Bridge a new bridge structure at
Kumukumu Stream crossing new comfort stations new pavements at Kealia Lookout and
Kealia Beach Park areas and site grading The overall project location is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

wide from Lihi Park to just beyond the Kapaa swimming pool gravel parking area and
10 feet wide from the swimming pool area to the end of the project near Ahihi Point The

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene Epoch
more than million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of basaltic lavas of
the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation
The majority of the Island of Kauai is covered by lavas of the Waimea Canyon
Volcanic Series These lavas consist of four distinct formations
and Makaweli These formations are comprised of thin bedded a a and pahoehoe flows to
massive basalt flows that ponded in calderas and graben
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the island of
Kauai These rocks are generally characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded
from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various locales Associated with the
vents are pyroclastic materials which usually form low cinder cones at the vent
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great
continental glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water
available to fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global
sea levels rose because more water was available The land mass of Kauai remained
essentially stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature
These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea that were both higher and
lower relative to present sea level of Kauai
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenious sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs
The basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the Koloa Volcanic Series are
generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin
clinker layers The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade
to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed of mainly silty material and is
typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock
formation with depth

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
twelve borings designated as Boring Nos 5 through 16 to depths of about 5 to 6 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 5
At the southern portion of the path Boring Nos 5 through 8 the borings generally
encountered a surface fill layer of about 1 to 5 feet in thickness The surface fill layer
generally consisted of dense sands and gravels and very stiff clayey silt and silty clay
The surface fill layer was underlain by a beach deposit to depths of about 5 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The beach deposit generally consisted of medium dense
sand
At the northern portion of the path Boring Nos 9 through 16 the borings generally
encountered a surface fill layer comprised of dense silty sand gravel and cobbles and stiff
to very stiff silty clay and clayey silt The surface fill layer was about 0 5 to 3 feet thick
The surface fill layer was underlain by a residual saprolite deposit to a depth of about
6 feet below the existing ground surface the maximum depth explored The
residual saprolite deposit consisted of very stiff clayey silt and silty clay In one of the
borings a basalt formation was encountered below the fill layer Also the surface fill layer
was underlain by a beach sand deposit in one of the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of the field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 12 in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed
on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally encountered a dense and stiff surface fill layer
underlain by medium dense beach sand and very stiff residual saprolite soils We believe
that path pavement structure may be supported by the dense and stiff on site soils
encountered in the drilled borings provided our recommendations in this report are
followed
The site grading along the path will involve relatively small cuts and fills In general
the excavated beach sand and residual saprolite soils may be used as a source of general
fill material The subgrade soils under the pavement should be_ properly
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

Path Pavements
Rigid and flexible pavements will be used for the new bike and pedestrian path for
this project We understand the rigid pavement for the new bike and pedestrian path will
be subjected to vehicular traffic in some areas To reduce the potential for cracking at the
edge of pavement we recommend that a thickened edge pavement slab be utilized in the
areas subjected to vehicular traffic
Based on the field exploration the path pavement will be underlain by dense to stiff
fills medium dense beach sand and very stiff residual saprolite deposit consisting of
clayey silt and silty clay Based on the laboratory test results the following pavement
sections may be used

The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be moisture conditioned to
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction The subgrade should be kept moist prior to placement of the
concrete pavement and base course Base course material should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and
density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections

Site Grading
We anticipate the site grading work will generally consist of minor cuts and fills of
up to about 2 feet for the project site Site grading operations should be observed by a
representative from Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office
observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are
encountered during the excavation process and to confirm whether the exposed soil rock
conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as
fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend the soil be thoroughly moistened to close
cracks or prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill
material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557
Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill materials

In general the on site sands and gravels clayey silt and silty clay materials may

an inclination of 3H 1V should be used

twelve borings designated as Boring Nos 5 through 16 to depths of about 5 to 6 feet

Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 12

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Five California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on bulk
samples of near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement
subgrade support Results of these CBR tests are presented on Plates B 1 and B 5
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 A were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 6 and B 7
