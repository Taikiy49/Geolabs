PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on Leihoku Street across from the Waianae Mall in the Leeward
coast area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is situated approximately between the
existing Pokai Bay Subdivision and the Waianae Wastewater Treatment Plant The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that a new YMCA facility is
planned at the above site The existing mountainside to the south side of the proposed
YMCA facility is on the order of about 400 to 600 feet high which is considered average
to moderate for the slopes on the Island of Oahu The lower portion of the hillside
extending from the existing concrete drainage interceptor ditch to the toe of the hillside
consist of terraced slopes with an overall gradient of approximately one and one half
horizontal to one vertical The upper portion of the hillside above the concrete
drainage interceptor ditch is composed of multiple sub vertical scarps and rock ledges
separated by flatter inter slope regions with an overall slope inclination of about 1H 1V
As required by the City and County of Honolulu a slope assessment and rockfall
hazard evaluation was conducted for the proposed Waianae Coast YMCA development
in an effort to study the potential rockfall hazards present at the property and to provide
recommendations for mitigation of the potential rockfall hazards for the proposed
development The findings and recommendations are presented in our report entitled
Slope Assessment and Rockfall Evaluation Waianae Coast YMCA Facility Waianae
Oahu Hawaii dated
As a result of the slope assessment and rockfall hazard evaluation rockfall
protection improvements have been constructed at the project site to reduce the potential
for rockfall activity to affect the downslope development In general the rockfall mitigation

ROCKFALL IMPACT BARRIER INSTALLATION
The rockfall protection improvement work covered by this report generally consisted
of the installation of approximately 570 lineal feet of low energy 30 foot ton capacity
rockfall impact barrier manufactured by Chama Valley Production Co Prometheus
Construction preformed the installation work between about June 25 and
The major work tasks observed by Geolabs Inc included the following activities
Construction of the rockfall barrier column footings
Construction of the wire rope lateral ground anchors
Installation of the rockfall barrier support ropes nets and chain link mesh
Proof testing of the rockfall barrier lateral ground anchors
Our services during construction consisted of part time field observation of the
contractor s work performed at the project site However the ground anchor proof testing
performed by Prometheus Construction was observed on a full time basis The
approximate locations of the twelve 12 wire rope ground anchors identified as Anchor
Nos 1 through 12 constructed at the project site are shown on the Lateral Anchor
Designation Plan Plate 3 The results of the anchor proof testing observed by our field
representatives are summarized and presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 24

LIMITATIONS
Geolabs Inc assumes no responsibility for alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment In addition Geolabs Inc assumes no
responsibility for the accumulation of various earth deposits and debris which may be
intercepted and retained by the rockfall barrier system
Rockfall activity may be generally classified as a natural event which cannot be
calculated or predicted Therefore it is impossible for Geolabs Inc to guarantee absolute
safety for persons and property with scientific engineering methods Thus it is imperative
to maintain and service the rockfall barrier system on a regular appropriate basis
Furthermore the degree of rockfall protection may be diminished by rockfall events that
exceed the absorption capacity of the system as calculated by good engineering practice
failure to use original parts or by environmental corrosion
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of construction observation
services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the
tests and methods used to monitor the construction and materials Our services were

practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an
engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor
shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and
specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
