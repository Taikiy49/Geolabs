INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface soil conditions at each of the sites to provide soil
data for the design and construction of the Site grading and
foundation for the proposed one story residential structures
The study consisted of subsurface borings laboratory soil
testing and engineering analysis as outlined in our proposal
dated March 13 1980
Based on the results of our engineering studies conclusions
and recommendations pertinent to each site development are
presented in this report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed construction consists of a Single one story resi
dential structure at each of the three individual sites Con
crete slab on grade construction is planned
Grading plans were unavailable at the time of our investigation
however it is anticipated that only minor site grading is
required at each site

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling 2 borings
at each site utilizing truck mounted auger drilling equip
ment The depths of all the borings were terminated at 9
to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the locations
shown on the enclosed Site Plans Plates 1 2 and 3
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
is presented on the Boring Logs
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs
of borings at the approporiate sample depths

SITE 1 LANAKILA AVENUE
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located off Lanakila Avenue east of Kuakini Street
in Honolulu
Access to the site is by a narrow 10 foot wide dirt road
The lot is rectangular in shape and approximately 12 500 square
feet in area
The site is generally level with ground elevations ranging
from 89 to 92 feet The lot was vacant at the time of our
field exploration Vegetation at the site consists mostly of
heavy grass about 4 to 5 feet high and a few small trees
Some small piles of rubbish were noted in the northern portion
of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration encountered a surface layer about 5 5

formation to the depths drilled
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
adobe clay These soils are highly expansive and will ex
perience tremendous volume change or heaving with changes in
moisture content
Special foundation and pavement design is required for these
soils
Foundation
Structures placed on the expansive adobe clay soils will be
subject to the tremendous shrink swell potential of these soils
In order to reduce the shrink swell effects the adobe clay
Should be removed below the bottom of the footings and slabs

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be ob
served by a soil engineer during construction to see that ade
quate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling to
the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
