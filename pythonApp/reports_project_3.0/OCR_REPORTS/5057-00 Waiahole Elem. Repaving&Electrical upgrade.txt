SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Severe pavement deterioration including alligator cracking depressions and
potholes were observed along the existing asphalt concrete pavements Our field
exploration indicated that the existing pavements are generally underlain by clayey
subsoils with fair pavement support characteristics at shallow depths Thin layers of
compacted base course were encountered underlying the existing asphaltic concrete
pavement which is inadequate to support the traffic loads
We believe that the structural defects observed were the result of traffic loading
over an inadequate pavement section causing failures in the underlying subbase
and or subgrade materials Therefore instead of resurfacing of the existing pavement
sections complete removal to expose the subgrade areas and replacement with
compacted base and subbase materials are recommended for proper reconstruction of
the deteriorated pavements
For the design of new flexible pavements we recommend that a pavement
structural section consisting of 2 inches of asphalt concrete over 6 inches of aggregate
base course over 6 inches of select borrow subbase be used

specific design recommendations

1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed repaving within the premises of the existing Waiahole
Elementary School in the Waiahole area of Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The

The project site is located within the existing Waiahole Elementary School in the
Waiahole area of Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The parking lots are on the

failures from age and deterioration such as alligator pattern cracking exposed

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two separate
submarine shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age 2 7 to 3 4 million years ago and Koolau
is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age 2 2 to 2 5 million years
ago After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep
valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These more recent volcanic eruptions are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the worldwide glaciers grew and advanced less
water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present
stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded a larger volume of water
became available such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level The
larger glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations ranged from about 250 feet above the
present sea level to about 400 feet below the present sea level Thus the island
shorelines have transgressed and regressed in response to the fluctuations
The processes of terrestrial erosion and the associated depositional
environments were affected by these glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations When the
sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly lower and valleys were
carved to depths below the present sea level As a result terrestrial sediments were
carried farther seaward and deposited in what had been marine environments When
the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations In addition the higher sea level allowed for the
deposition of marine deposits such as coral and limestone in environments that had
been covered with terrestrial soils and rock

In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock mass from the slopes and movement of the material
downslope toward the axis of the valley by gravity to form colluvial soil deposits Once
the colluvium reaches streams in the valley alluvial processes become dominant and
the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium Large areas of alluvium can
be found in the lowland areas and bordering streams The project site is situated in the
lower portions of Waihole Valley over these alluvial colluvial deposits

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Severe pavement distresses and failures including alligator cracks depressions
exposed aggregates and potholes were observed on the existing asphalt concrete
pavements Based on our field exploration the existing pavements are generally
underlain by insufficient thickness of compacted base course and subbase overlying
clayey subgrade soils at shallow depths We believe that the pavement distresses and
failures observed at the project site were caused by traffic loads over an inadequate
pavement section Therefore it is our opinion that the existing deteriorated pavements
should be removed and replaced with a new pavement structural section constructed on
a properly prepared subgrade Special attention should be given to the drainage
requirements since water infiltration into the underlying clayey subsoils may result in
subsoil softening and loss of bearing support Detailed discussion of these items and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
3 1 Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration performed for the subject project the existing
pavement structure is generally underlain by clayey subsoils with fair pavement support
characteristics at shallow depths Therefore complete removal of the existing pavement
sections to expose the subgrade areas and replacement with compacted base and
subbase materials are recommended for reconstruction of the severely deteriorated
pavement
Based on our laboratory tests the on site subgrade course materials from our
field exploration have a CBR value of 13 2 with an expansion value of about
0 9 percent Considering normal traffic anticipated for the school it is our opinion that
reconstruction with the following new pavement section may be used for the subject
project

3 2 Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and current scope of work we anticipate that
minor site grading work will be required for the proposed pavement reconstruction project
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All asphalt concrete paving vegetation debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and yielding areas encountered below pavement areas should be over excavated to
expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing grubbing and demolition the future pavement areas should be
excavated where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the select
borrow layer The pavement subgrades should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil

3 3 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement
surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be

It is our understanding that the current project scope includes reconstruction of
concrete sidewalk adjacent to the project pavement repair area Based on our field

concrete slab over 6 inches of select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction is recommended for the new concrete sidewalk section Subgrade
preparation should follow recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of
this report

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed ona
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 2
