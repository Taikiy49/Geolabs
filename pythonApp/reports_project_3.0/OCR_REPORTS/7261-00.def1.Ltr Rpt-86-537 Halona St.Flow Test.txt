In accordance with our fee proposal dated September 18, 2015, we conducted flow 
testing of the existing seepage pits at 86-537 Halona Road. This letter report summarizes 
the findings from our flow testing and presents our discussion and evaluation. The findings 
and discussion are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted 
at the end of this letter report.   
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is the former location of a group home that the City and County 
of Honolulu wishes to redevelop as housing for indigent persons.  It is our 
understanding that the former group home was served by two cesspools that were 
installed in the 1960’s and the system was upgraded in or about 1986 with the 
installation of a septic system using two seepage pits. 
It is estimated that the indigent housing project will generate about 1,200 gallons 
per day of wastewater.  It was desired to test the existing seepage pits to evaluate their 
capacities.  This required exposure of the pits, opening of their ports and emplacing 
water into the pits.  The testing included measurement of the water level in the pits to 
assess the rate of percolation into the surrounding subsoils. 
PURPOSE AND SCOPE 
The purpose of our exploration is to obtain an overview of the subsurface 
conditions at the existing cesspool. To accomplish this, we propose an exploration 
program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts:

INSPECTION AND FLOW TESTING 
Using the 1986 as-built plans provided, the seepage pit lids were exposed using 
the hopto.  Field measurements indicated that their actual location varied slightly from 
the as-built plans.  The access ports in the lids were located and opened.  Visual 
inspection indicated that the port covers were in poor condition and should be replaced.  
It was also noted that one of the covers for the existing septic tanks was missing and 
there was an open hole.  This hole was given a temporary cover and flagged for safety 
but a new cover should be provided as soon as possible. 
We conducted sounding in the seepage pits to determine the depth to bottom 
and the depth to fluid, if present. Following this inspection, we conducted further testing 
by placing approximately 4,000 gallons of clean fresh water into each seepage pit, 
using the water truck, and then by recording the time required for the water to dissipate, 
(i.e., a falling head percolation test). Since the actual geometries of the wells were not 
available and the actual characteristics of the receiving aquifer were not known at the 
time this report was prepared, the performance test data was not subjected to rigorous 
analysis to estimate hydraulic conductivities or maximum capabilities. The test data was 
reviewed on a subjective basis to estimate the seepage pit performance.   
Testing indicated that the approximately 4,000 gallons of water placed into 
Seepage Pit No. 1 (the mauka well) dissipated in approximately 8 minutes. In Seepage 
Pit No. 2 (the makai well), approximately 4,000 gallons of water dissipated in about 
30 minutes. We understand that the design capacity for this wastewater system is 1,200 
gallons per day. Based on the results of the flow testing, we believe that the

LIMITATIONS 
The analysis and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from visual observations and short-term flow test. Variations of 
subsurface conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not 
become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be 
necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein.  
Locations of cesspool and buildings indicated herein are based on the topographic 
survey plans transmitted by Leaps O Boundaries, Inc. on or about September 17, 2015.
