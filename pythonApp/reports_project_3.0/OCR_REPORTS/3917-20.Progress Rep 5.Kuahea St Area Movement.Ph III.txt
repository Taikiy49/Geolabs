PROGRESS REPORT NO 5
PHASE Ill ADDITIONAL INSTRUMENT MONITORING
KUAHEA STREET AREA MOVEMENT
PROJECT NO 97504
Dear Mr Steinberger
This fifth progress report is presented to summarize our work efforts and the results
of the additional field instrumentation monitoring conducted for the Kuahea Street Area
Movement project located in Palolo Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report provides summary information pertaining to the recorded earth
movements and subsurface water levels detected to date during the Phase ili instrument
monitoring effort Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated and Amendment No 2 for Engineering Services for the Kuahea
Street Area Movement Project Contract No F 60578 CIP No 97504
This report is the first semi annual progress report covering the additional 12 month
instrument monitoring period which commenced in August 2002 The end of the
additional monitoring period is anticipated to expire in August 2003 at which time a second
semi annual progress report will be prepared A draft of this progress report was issued in
March 2003 for review and comment and this progress report is the finalized version of
the report based on review comments from City personnel

Based on our review of the rainfall information and monthly precipitation summaries
provided by the NWS the months of October 2002 and January 2003 experienced the
highest rainfall totals for the current monitoring period associated with significant storm
events The November monitoring of Inclinometer Nos I 2 3 and I 8 indicate that the
recorded subsurface movements generally increase slightly in magnitude following periods
of greater rainfall such as that which was experienced in the month of October None of
the inclinometers recorded an increased rate of movement following the higher
precipitation experienced in the month of January
Our interpretation of earth movement in response to increased rainfall is that if a
period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged period of
normal or lower than normal rainfall earth movement rates at some of the inclinometers
tend to increase abruptly following the period of higher precipitation and then settle back to
normal levels The effect has been noted in the past with Inclinometer Nos I 1 I
4 and I 8 Other inclinometers at the site do not appear to record the earth movement
rate response such as described
Subsurface Water
The location of the piezometers installed at the project site is shown on the
Instrument Location Plan Plate 2 Based on our monitoring of the subsurface water levels
recorded from Piezometer Nos P 1 through P 7 between January 31 and
the record shows that the piezometers identified as P 1C P 2A P 3C
P 4A P 4B and P 6B have encountered appreciable measurable volumes of subsurface
water seepage at their respective screened well points In general the measurable
volumes of water that were recorded are within the normal range of water levels
recorded during previous monitoring however the recorded water levels were noted to be
at the higher end of the normal ranges previously identified for the respective instrument
locations In general the piezometers that encounter significant measurable subsurface
water typically indicate the water presence on a year round basis with some elevated
spikes in the water levels occurring following periods of above average rainfall
P 7 continue to have encountered only trace amounts of subsurface water seepage
during the past monitoring period These piezometers have remained generally dry or
have indicated a static accumulation of about 0 7 foot or less of water in the standpipes

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
Based on our observation of the existing ground surface conditions within the
Kuahea Street project area there does not appear to be visible signs of extraordinary
deformation to pavements and surface structures The longitudinal pavement cracks
previously noted along the northern side of Waiomao Road in the vicinity of Area B
appeared to have been sealed with a surface coating to reduce the potential for significant
surface water infiltration into the pavement subgrade The arc shaped pavement cracks
that cross Waiomao Road along the western boundary of the Waiomao Landslide also
appear to have been sealed to reduce the potential for significant infiltration of surface
water into the subsurface
A portion of the driveway and sidewalk located at 2333 Waiomao Road appears to
have been repaired with the on grade placement of concrete It should be noted that
continued settlement of the roadway pavement immediately surrounding the existing
manhole fronting 2340 Waiomao Road was observed In addition the eastern boundary
of the Waiomao Landslide that crosses Waiomao Road was observed to exhibit a
noticeable bump when crossing with a vehicle The existing pavement patch at this
location appears to be in fair condition however the pavement offset across the boundary
appears to be growing slightly in magnitude
Observation of the area along Kuahea Street at the head of the Waiomao Landslide
indicates that the road pavement and northern road shoulder continue to experience some
settlement of the ground surfaces Large cracks in the pavement at Kuahea Street were
not observed and the existing asphaltic concrete patches were observed to be in
generally fair condition

Observations of the area surrounding the intersection of Kuahea Place and Kuahea
Street show signs of continued pavement and structure deformation The residence at
2221 Kuahea Place appears to be vacant and appreciable distress to the retaining walls
and dwelling is visible from the street area In addition the dwelling located at
2331 Kuahea Street appears to be experiencing signs of continued distress evidenced by
the large cracks in the concrete driveway and retaining wall fronting Kuahea Street
In addition we observed some small gaps about 0 5 inches in width within the
concrete pavement of Kuahea Street fronting 2334 and 2340 Kuahea Street that appear to
have some void space located below the pavement The gaps were observed to be
located within the roadway pavement surrounding some repair patches and along the
edge of the concrete pavement at the curbs Based on the location of the pavement gaps
near pavement depressions and along the shoulder curbing it appears that the potential
exists for surface water runoff to enter through the gaps and percolate below the concrete
pavement It should be noted that Piezometer No P 2 has continuously recorded high
water levels in the shallow subsurface at this location Piezometer No P 2 is located
immediately downslope of the observed concrete pavement gaps We recommend that
the gaps be sealed to reduce the potential for appreciable infiltration of surface water
runoff beneath the pavement which may be causing some erosion of the pavement base
layer
It should be noted that the vacant privately owned lot located at 2318 Waiomao
Road was recently cleared of the thick vegetation allowing observation of the existing
ground surfaces Based on our observation of the exposed ground surfaces and the old
asphaltic concrete pavements located at the site there does not appear to be visible signs
of ground surface deformation within the vacant parcel This observation may support our
belief that the longitudinal pavement cracking and settlement experienced along Waiomao
Road upslope of this location may be related to some movement of the existing retaining
wall which parallels the roadway
The graded hillslopes located within the footprint of the Waiomao Landslide as well
as the area of Kipona Place at the toe of the landslide do not exhibit overt signs of ground
surface deformation The vegetation on the graded slopes downslope of Waiomao Road
were observed to be well maintained offering fair exposure of the surface terrain beneath
the grass No new earth slumps were observed at the site
