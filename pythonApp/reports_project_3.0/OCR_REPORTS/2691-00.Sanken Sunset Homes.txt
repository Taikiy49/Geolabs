SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Considerable grading had been performed at the site prior to
our reconnaissance and field exploration Our findings
indicate that the soils at the project site consist of previously
placed materials composed of expansive clays intermixed with
basalt boulders overlying in situ colluvium to the maximum
depths explored
Mass grading at the project site would require special
procedures to accommodate the site topography and nature
of soils encountered in our exploration The previous grading
was performed without sufficient consideration given to
geotechnical factors and as such may be considered as
uncontrolled Therefore it is our opinion that this fill material
should be removed to expose the original subgrade and the
grading for the proposed development be performed in
accordance with the recommendations presented in this
report to lessen the potential for failure of the site
improvements
The in situ adobe clay soils encountered at the project site are
highly expansive requiring special subgrade preparation for
building foundations The adobe clay soils at the project site
have low shear strength values and are subject to downslope
creep movement if they become saturated or have surcharge
loads placed upon them
The soils have very low permeability characteristics making
on site dispersal of sewerage impractical

to the proposed construction of two 2 residential structures at the subject project site

OVERALL OBSERVATIONS
General
Deposits of expansive clay soils known locally as adobe clays are frequently
found at the bases of ridges and steep slopes in Hawaii These adobe clay soils have
low shear strength values and are often subject to downslope creep movement if they
become saturated or have surcharge loads placed upon them Such downslope creep
movements have been cited as a primary cause of significant property damage at several
subdivisions located in eastern Oahu The subdivision developments were reportedly
underlain by thick deposits of adobe clay soils on sloping terrain and portions of these
soils were left in place during the initial development of the sites Over time the effects
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2691 00 Page 8
of grading and wetting of the adobe clay soils gave rise to downslope creep movement
of the soils Generally in sloping terrain all adobe clays should be removed during new
site grading to support surface construction
In addition to the potential for downslope creep movements the adobe clays have
a high expansion potential wnen wetted which poses a substantial risk to structures
constructed upon them Unless special provisions for grading and foundation design are
implemented significant structural damage may occur
Generally the potential risks of development on expansive clay soils on level
ground may be forestalled by appropriate grading requirements by stabilization of the
soils and or by proper foundation and pavement design
At the project site our field exploration indicated that the deposits of adobe clay
soils are generally relatively thick and are overlying a sloping grade Based on the
findings from our analyses of the data obtained from our field exploration and laboratory
testing complete removal and replacement of the adobe clay soils would be required to
lessen the potential of downslope creep movement This removal would need to extend
to the areas below and upslope of the intended site improvements to expose the native
non expansive competent soils
To provide a stable platform for the southerly building pad the previously placed
embankment material and the underlying adobe clay would need to be removed in their
entirety to expose native competent materials up slope and below the proposed pad
Construction of the pad upon which to place the building would require the importation
and controlled placement of engineered embankment with select materials Keying and
benching into competent native materials would be necessary
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2691 00 Page 9
As stated above the problems presented by the nature of the on site soils may be
addressed to some degree by proper grading design However the location of the
existing northernmost building pad in conjunction with the upslope Comsat Road and
entrance driveway negate the implementation of grading design recommendations which
would provide for a stable platform as it presently exists This is because the existing cut
slope in the rear upslope portion of the northernmost pad would need to be cut back
to provide a maximum steepness of 6 horizontal to 1 vertical 6H 1V or would need a
retention structure designed to support the existing slope and upper roadway The
construction of such a retention structure could not be performed without the extreme risk
of undermining the above roadway
In addition to the construction of a retention structure the underlying placed fills
and in situ adobe clays would need to be completely removed and replaced with select
engineered fills
The results of two percolation tests performed at the site indicate that the
soils at the project site are not suitable for the disposal of on site sewerage via the
installation of a leaching field due to their very poor percolation characteristics It is our
understanding that the project site is located in an area where installation of new
cesspools or seepage pits is not permitted
Our site reconnaissance revealed indications of severe erosion in an area along
the north portion of the northernmost building site as well as along the southwest property
line due to water run off from up slope areas during heavy rains Proper control of the
groundwater run off would be a necessary part of design and construction of site grading
to protect site improvements and the adjacent lower properties from damage due to
erosion

Conclusion
The presentation of design recommendations for the subject site are influenced by
the following observations which are based upon our site reconnaissance field
exploration laboratory testing and the findings obtained there from
1 The site has been previously graded by uncontrolled deposition of the
unsuitable material The resulting uncontrolled embankments and disturbed
soils must be completely removed from the site Subsequent requisite
grading should be accomplished utilizing specialized techniques developed
with consideration given to the nature of the subsurface materials
2 The underlying in situ soils consist in part of highly expansive adobe clays
These materials exhibit a high potential for downslope creep when
surcharged or saturated Due to the sloping nature of the site these
materials would need to be removed completely and replaced with selected
imported fill material to form a competent subgrade
3 The adobe clay soils up slope of the site improvements would need to be
regraded to provide a stable slope ratio It should be anticipated that
structurally adequate retention structures will be found to be required
Construction of such structures would require the removal of the existing
materials on the face of the existing slopes to expose competent underlying
materials

RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Gradin
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of trees vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
As discussed above all previously placed embankment material disturbed soils
and the in situ adobe clay soils should be removed completely from proposed
building sites and the areas below and up slope of the intended improvements and
disposed of off site The removal of the in situ adobe clays in these areas should
be extended to depths sufficient to expose competent underlying colluvium
Boulders in excess of two feet in diameter are not suitable as fill in the upper five
feet below subgrade and should be removed or stockpiled for use in the deeper
fills
In areas to receive fill the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to the wet side of optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Soft or yielding areas should be
over excavated and stabilized by backfilling with select imported material placed
in ten 10 inch thick loose lifts compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material and Placement
Off site borrow soil required for embankment construction should be tested by the
geotechnical engineer to evaluate its suitability for use for select fill prior to its
delivery to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2691 00 Page 12
Off site borrow soils should be non expansive with maximum particle size not
exceeding 3 inches with a laboratory CBR swell value not exceeding 1 and with
a CBR value of 20 or more It should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or other deleterious materials and should have sufficient fines so that there
is a minimum of 15 passing the No 200 sieve and should be capable of forming
a competent stable mass when compacted
Fill material should be placed in level lifts with a maximum loose thickness of ten
10 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Each layer should
be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of the material and water
content
Additional fill material should not be placed on any fill layer which has not been
properly compacted
Boulder Fills
Many surface boulders were noted over most of the sloping hillside Additional
large quantities of boulders will be generated during the removal of the adobe clay
soils _ Boulders may be used in deep fills provided that the following
recommendations are followed
1 Boulders must not be nested together They should be placed so that
compaction equipment may be able to effect adequate compaction around
them Boulders in excess of three feet in diameter should not be placed in
the fills but should be reduced to acceptably smaller sizes or removed from
the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2691 90 Page 13
2 Boulders should not be allowed within five 5 feet of the finish grade
vertically or horizontally
3 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in proposed utility
alignments to prevent difficulty in the later excavation of the utility trenches
Boulders may also be used for upper slope buttress fills that may be found to be
required For any remaining boulders provision should be made to properly
dispose of them off site
Based on the assumption that the surface adobe clay soils will be removed
completely from cut slope areas and that properly engineered fill slopes will be
constructed of select imported material cut and fill slope ratios of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V may be used for the project grading design
Steeper cut slope ratios of up to may be used in very stiff competent
colluvium or weathered basalt formulations Adobe clay soils encountered at the
top of the cut slopes should either be removed or sloped back to a gentle cut
slope of about 5H 1V
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting back to the design
slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
keying and benching are required to properly bond the new fill to the slope The
filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level

Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread or strip footing foundations bearing on select
imported fill materials placed and compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations may be used to support the proposed houses Thickened edge slabs
on grade may also be used to support the lightly loaded single family residential
structures
For the design of residential structure footings an allowable bearing capacity of
native soils or on fills placed and compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and
seismic loads are considered In general the footings should be embedded a minimum
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be treated by over excavation of the soft and or weak materials down to very
stiff natural colluvial soils and backfilling of the resultant depression with select imported
fill material compacted in accordance with the above recommendations
In areas where residential structures are to be constructed near fill slopes the
foundations should be set back sufficiently so that the bottom of the footing is a minimum
of six 6 feet from the face of the slope

The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the foundations
Drainage and Erosion Control
All cut and fill slopes must be protected from excessive quantities of high velocity
storm run off Interceptor ditches should be provided at the top of all cuts and both cut
and fill slopes should be grassed
Substantial erosion was noted to have occurred along the southwest property line
and in the northern portion of the site It appears as though the erosion is caused by
water run off from up slope areas crossing Comsat Road and flowing onto and across
the project site Properly designed water interception and diversion systems are
necessary to control and dispose of this storm run off water and thus lessen the potential
for damage to the proposed site improvements and adjacent lower properties
Groundwater seepage originating from springs or seepage in areas above the site
may occur It is important that when the cuts are performed at the site a geotechnical
engineer observe the exposed soils for indications of seepage which if not controlled
may undermine or de stabilize any fills placed
Sewerage
As discussed above the permeability tests performed as a part of our field
exploration indicate that the near surface soils have low infiltration percolation capacities
Areal limitations of the site preclude construction of a leaching field of sufficient extent to
adequately handle the quantities of sewage generated by the proposed residential
development In the event that future site grading is performed which exposes the
underlying native materials further testing may be performed to determine the percolation
characteristics of the in situ soils at lower elevations However it is unlikely given the
GEOLABS HAWAII

geological morphology of the area that materials with the desired characteristics would
be encountered
Pavements
Assuming that the recommendations for site grading presented in this report are
followed pavement sections would bear upon non expansive in situ materials or on
embankments constructed of select imported materials For preliminary design
purposes we recommend that the pavement section consist of one of the following
For Flexible Pavements
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Compacted Moist Subgrade
For Rigid Pavements
4 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Compacted Moist Subgrade

as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual
subgrade encountered during construction to verify the adequacy of the above section
The recommended section assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved
areas

We recommend that additional geotechnical exploration consisting of the

Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the final design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed geotechnical conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
