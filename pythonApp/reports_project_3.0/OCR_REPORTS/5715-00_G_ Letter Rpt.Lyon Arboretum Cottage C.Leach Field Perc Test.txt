In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program at the proposed
leach field area at Cottage C of the UHM Lyon Arboretum in the Manoa Valley area of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from
our field exploration and percolation testing at the proposed leach field site and presents
our discussion and evaluation for the proposed project These findings and discussions
are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves renovation and replacement of some cottages for
University of Hawaii at Manoa in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site
is above Paradise Park Cottage C a two story building will be demolished and
replaced with a new laboratory facility
It is our understanding that a leachfield is now being required for the disposal of
waste water effluent generated at the site Our previous scope of work did not include
testing for the design of a leachfield Therefore it is it is necessary to conduct percolation
tests in general accordance with the Hawaii Department of Health requirements
At present there are two optional locations for the leachfield Based on the
available topographic map these locations were not accessible to our truck mounted
equipment We had proposed to drill sample and conduct percolation tests in
two boreholes to depths of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface using portable
drill equipment

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration is to measure the permeability of the
near surface soils at the proposed leach field location to formulate geotechnical
recommendations for design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal
system The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1
Mobilization and demobilization of field personnel and a geologist to and
from the project site

between about 5 5 and 7 0 feet below the existing ground surface

areas to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics
5

exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the proposed leach field sites consisted of drilling
and sampling of four hand augered borings ranging between about 5 5 and 7 0 feet below
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our drilling encountered a surface horizon of brown silty clay Alluvium
underlain at a depth of about 5 feet by brown clayey sand Weathered Volcanic Cinder
We did not encounter groundwater in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change as a result of tidal
fluctuation time of year precipitation surface runoff and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Borings in Appendix A
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and
leach field method are proposed for the replacement building development Therefore we
have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the design of the
proposed sewage disposal system
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
3 0 inch diameter boreholes each to a depth of 5 5 feet or 7 feet below the existing ground
surface
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was placed at the bottom of the boreholes to protect
the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes were
carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel pack The time
intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the
percolation tests were based

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted herein are based in part upon information
obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil conditions
between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided herein
The field boring locations indicated herein are approximate having been estimated
by taping from the features shown on the Erosion Control Plan provided by Architects
Pacific Inc on or about The physical locations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
