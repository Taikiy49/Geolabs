This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for Target Store T2660 at the
Puunene Shopping Center project from February 26 to March 5 2014 The probing and
grouting observation work was performed in general accordance with our scope of
services outlined in our fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The proposed Puunene Shopping Center development is in the Kahului area on the
Island of Maui Hawaii It is approximately bounded by Hookele Street to the north
Pakaula Street to the east open land to the south and Mokulele Highway to the west As
part of the sitework package for this project we understand that a program of probing and
grouting at the foundation locations of the Target Store building will be implemented during
construction due to the potential for presence of cavities and or voids within the basalt
formations underlying the project site
Probing and Grouting Program
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the Target Store

5 2013 The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential

for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose
zones below the new building structure
The general contractor Swinerton Builders Inc surveyed the footing layout and
marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In general
the probe holes were spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals along the wall footings and
every 50 square feet for the column and mat footings The approximate probe hole
locations are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan Plate 2 Due to the relatively
light load and or non bearing nature of the entrance canopy planters foundations the
probe holes originally planned for these structures were eliminated from the probing and
grouting program for the project
Goodfellow Brothers Inc performed the probing work for the project using a
track mounted drill rig equipped with air pneumatic percussion hammers The probe holes
were about 4 inches in diameter drilled with a 3 inch diameter bit and extended to depths
of about 11 to 15 feet below the existing building pad surface corresponding to
approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations The materials
encountered during the probe drilling operation generally consisted of select granular fill
materials underlain by reddish brown clayey silt residual soils overlying basalt rock
formation materials After completion of the probe drilling operations the probe holes
were filled with a sand cement pumpable Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM grout
The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Hawaiian Cement Puunene Quarry
and the 32 42 meter boom pump trucks were operated by American Standard Concrete
Pumping Hawaii Inc
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and
recorded the probe depths and approximate grout take A total of 257 probe holes with an
aggregate length of about 3 174 lineal feet below the existing building pad surface were
drilled for the Target Store building Approximately 27 4 cubic yards of sand cement
CLSM grout was used to fill the scheduled probe holes drilled for the project A summary
of our foundation probing and grouting observation is provided on Plates 3 1 through 3 9
During the grouting operation excessive grout intake was observed at Probe Hole
No 48 Subsequent investigation disclosed that the probe hole was located over a newly
installed 12 inch diameter drain line As a result approximately 2 77 cubic yards of CLSM
grout was pumped into the inactive drain line system before the grouting operation was
stopped by our field representative
It should be noted that approximately 0 45 cubic yard of CLSM grout was used in
filling Probe Hole No F3 We believe that relatively high grout intake could be attributed to
the very fluid nature of the CLSM grout with a slump of slightly over 9 inches as well as
some overflowing at the top Subsequent monitoring indicated that the top of the grout
backfill did not drop more than one inch by the end of the day
