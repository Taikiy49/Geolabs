PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Resort Community development encompasses about
1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The project is part of the
planned improvements for the Kukui ula Resort Community The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The Parcel FF is located in the south central portion of the Kukui ula Development
in the Koloa area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project involves subdividing
Parcel FF into 10 individual home sites
The area was formerly used for sugar cane cultivation but is currently vacant
The current development plan calls for grading and installation of related infrastructure
such as roads drainage sewer and other utilities Lot fine grading and house
construction will be by the individual lot buyers or other developers It is anticipated that
the residential structures will be one to two stories high The site grading plans indicate
that the grading for the development involved placement of fills up to about 6 feet in
height
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations for the project

SITE GRADING
Earthwork at the subdivision site generally consisted of fills up to 6 feet high Prior
to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation and
deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor Earthworks Pacific performed the
grading work The applicable mass grading plan sheet is attached
The fill material used at the site consisted of brown to reddish brown silty clay and
clayey silt excavated from the golf course Laboratory swell testing on samples hauled to
the site disclosed that the soils generally have low swell potential however variations in
natural formations should be anticipated
During the site grading operations samples of the fill materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion the site grading work for Parcel FF observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs
and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation including a licensed geotechnical engineer should be
obtained in the planning design and construction of houses and improvements
