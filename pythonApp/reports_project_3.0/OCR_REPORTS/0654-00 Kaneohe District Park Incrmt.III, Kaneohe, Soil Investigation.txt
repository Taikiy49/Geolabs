SUMMARY
The existing volleyball courts are located over an old
drainage way High groundwater conditions exist Spring
water is anticipated below pavement subgrade elevation
For the new pavement reconstruction a blanket of clean
3B fine crush rock base course with less than 5 of fines
should be used to provide capillary break and subsurface
drainage In addition a drainage system consisting of
a 4 inch perforated pipe should be constructed at a minimum
of 3 feet below the pavement finish grade to lower and
divert the subsurface seepage water toward the stream bank
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of new pavement at the volleyball courts in
Kaneohe District Park This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The Kaneohe District Park is located approximately half a
mile west of the intersection of Likelike Highway and Kahekili
Highway and is on the Keaahala Road The volleyball court
next to the basket ball court is situated at the southwest
portion of the Kaneohe District Park complex
A 4 foot high retaining wall runs along the west side of the
volleyball courts The ground surface behind the retaining
wall slopes gently upward toward Hawaii State Hospital
An existing stream runs in the west to east direction and
separates the courts from the Park Office building and the
Swimming pool
The existing asphalt cement pavement had severe heaving
damage in a strip that runs east to west across the volley
ball courts The approximate location of the damaged area
is outlined on the attached Site Plan Plate l
The remaining areas of the volleyball courts and the basket
ball courts are in good condition
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed construction for the volleyball court consists of
repaving the existing A C pavement and providing improvement
of the subsurface drainage and subgrade condition under the
affected pavement area

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our subsurface exploration and laboratory testing
results in general the subsurface soils under the volleyball
court pavement are silty clayey and have a high organic content
The swell tests on the one inch thick ring samples taken on
the clayey portion of the bouldery subgrade layer show that the
percent of swell is low The low percentage of swell in the
ring samples may be due to the presence of the gravel and sand
W Page 5
in the sample However a CBR swell test using a 6 diameter
mold indicated that the same clayey soil below the bouldery
layer at Boring No 1 is highly expansive
The groundwater is relatively high and fluctuates about 1 to
4 feet below existing pavement The unstable groundwater
condition may be due to high perched water in the subgrade
material Spring water is possible in this area
The gray organic silty clay encountered beneath the bouldery
layer is a old stream bed deposit This means that the existing
volleyball court is located over a former stream bed or drainage
ditch
Under the present pavement both the silty coral base course
and the clayey and silty boulder subgrade provide poor drainage
for the high groundwater seepage The fines in the base course
layer also could not provide capillary water breakage
It is our opinion that the above conditions cause the pavement
to heave and to deteriorate Therefore any new paving on the
volleyball courts should also include adequate subdrainage system
below the A C pavement
Drainage System Under The Pavement
To prevent any excessive hydrostatic pressure of seepage water
coming from the upper area and behind the retaining wall at the
west side a 4 inch diameter perforated pipe should be placed
at a minimum of 3 feet below the pavement surface along the wall
footing
W Page 6
The pipe should be provided with a minimum of 2 nch bedding
material such as No 3B fine rock and then use this same material
as backfill up to the bottom of the select borrow under the pave
ment
In addition to stabilize the groundwater over the volleyball
courts it is recommended that four more 4 inch diameter per
forated pipe be placed in the same manner as specified above
across the volleyball courts perpendicular to the retaining
wall The pipes should not be more than 20 feet apart
All the perforated pipe should be connected to a positive drain
age system with outlets to the existing stream at the south of
the court
Pavement Design
The following pavement section may be used for designing the new
volleyball court at this site
3 Asphalt Concrete
9 Base Course
12 Select Borrow
24 Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be clean crushed rock such as the No 3B
fine rock material or equivalent to the quarry with a CBR of
85 and conforming with section 31 2 Standard Specification of
Public Work Construction
The select borrow should be crushed rock with less than 5 of
fine material passing No 200 sieve such as the 2 coarse or
equivalent
W Y 054 00 Page 7
Commercial base course such as the S4C or coral material is
not recommended to be used as base course material The
clayey boulder subgrade should be removed down to 2 feet
below the pavement surface The new specified base course
and select borrow should then be backfilled and compacted in
6 inch layers to 95 maximum density in accordance with ASTM
test method D 1557 72T
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the excavation for removing the on site
silty coral base course and clayey boulder subgrade be inspected
by the Soils Engineer prior to the placement of subdrain system
and specified backfill to assure that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Earthwork operations should be monitored by the Soils Engineer
Construction inspection should include the necessary testing to
assure that the proper degree of compaction is consistently a
chieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
Should you have any questions concerning our findings or recom
mendations please do not hesitate to contact us

A site visit was made to the above project on March 16 1977
In general the existing silty coral base course and the clayey
boulder subgrade had been removed down to the recommended sub
grade elevations At the center portion of the volleyball court
an area about 30 x 35 feet in plan dimension had been over exca
vated two feet below the proposed select borrow layer
The over excavation was initiated by your field inspector when
soft gray organic clay soil was encountered At the time of this
visit water was seeping out from the bottom of this over cut area
A ditch was dug toward the stream direction in order to drain the
ponding water
These soft clay and high water level conditions are consistent with
the findings of our previous boring investigation
The following are our recommendations for the present site condition
1 Backfill over excavation with quarry waste granular material
The granular backfill should be placed over the wet spot in
order to develop working platform for the upper select borrow
and the base course construction
