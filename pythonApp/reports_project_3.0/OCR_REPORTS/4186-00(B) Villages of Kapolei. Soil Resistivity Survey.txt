This report only presents the results of our soil resistivity survey

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Village 6 project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by Kapolei Parkway
to the south and by Fort Barrette Road to the west It is situated on the south side of the
existing Village 6 and is partially separated by the existing Kaiau Avenue partially
constructed Based on the current development plans it is proposed to develop the site for
residential use by the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands The entire project site
encompasses an area of approximately 35 7 acres The proposed development will also
include the installation of new utilities including water sewer and drain lines as well as
construction of new roadways
The site has been mass graded as part of the Villages of Kapolei mass grading work
The mass grading work was performed by Oahu Construction Company and involved the
placement of fill materials generally consisting of dark brown silty clays with some coral
gravel The fill materials generally consisted of soils from various sources made available
to the grading contractor to raise the site up to the current elevations
Several new roadways such as Kaiau Avenue Extension and Roads A through P are
olanned at the site We understand that 12 inch diameter water lines will be installed along
Kaiau Avenue Extension and that 8 inch diameter water lines will generally be installed along

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity values
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in the
Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The scope
of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of resistivity equipment and field engineers to and
from the project site
2 Performance of 93 in situ soil resistivity surveys at 30 locations along the
proposed water line alignment corresponding approximately to the 300 foot
maximum spacing required in the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The resistivity surveys were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78
3 Coordination of the work by a field engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion characteristics
of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

A total of 93 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 30 locations of the project site
using the in Pin Method and a resistance meter Nilsson Model 400
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity of the soil mass extending from the existing
ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the water lines was assessed by using this
method at the approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the
soil resistivity survey with plots of the soil resistivity versus pipeline stationing are presented
on Plates 3 1 through 14 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed roadways water line alignment The average soil resistance along the proposed
roadway was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings
of generally 2 to 8 feet apart at the existing ground surface The depth of penetration of a
resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The electrode spacing for each
soil resistivity survey location was generally determined by the depth of the water line invert
from the existing ground surface It should be noted that some underground utility lines were
observed within the Road A area near some of our soil resistivity survey locations
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 14 2 of this letter report However it should be noted that
the soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the survey locations
temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the lesser the potential for corrosion
Based on the resistivity values and the corrosion ratings of the subsurface materials
at the site the subsurface materials at the site may be classified as very corrosive to
moderately corrosive for buried metallic structures In general it appears that the proposed
water line may need to be designed based on Corrosion Rating of 2
