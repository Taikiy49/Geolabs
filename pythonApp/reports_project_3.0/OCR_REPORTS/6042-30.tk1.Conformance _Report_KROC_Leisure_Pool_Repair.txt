EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC and Jayar Construction
Company Inc performed the site grading operations pavement construction and utility
line installation for the leisure pool repair work at the Ray Joan Kroc Corps Community
Center The repair work generally consisted of subgrade preparation trench excavation
and backfill and pavement construction
During
the
leisure
pool
repair
work
the
exposed
soils
have
been
moisture conditioned on a periodic basis to reduce the potential for shrinkage and or
swelling due to fluctuations of the moisture content In general the bottom of the
excavated trenches was recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
prior to the placement of new piping and backfill material No 3B Fine gravel was used as
the bedding material and initial backfill around the piping Subsequent backfills generally
consisted of the on site clayey soils up to the bottom of the 18 inch non expansive capping
layer The clayey soils were placed in near level lifts moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Non expansive select granular material for the 18 inch
capping layer were placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Base course
material underneath the AC paving were brought to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the backfilling operations on an as need
basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil
conditions were noted A total of 115 field density tests including retests of failed areas
were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
