This report presents our geotechnical engineering design and construction
management services performed for the Emergency Landslide Remediation Adjacent to
Burger King Kaneohe project in the Kaneohe District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes the geotechnical engineering design in support of the
landslide remediation and the quality assurance quality control program during
construction This report also presents the concerns challenges and alternate solutions
during the design and construction process
Project Considerations
The project site encompasses a steep slope at the rear parking area of the
Kaneohe Burger King restaurant on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The slope resides within
the City and County of Honolulu and the State of Hawaii properties between the Burger
King restaurant and a subdivision located at the bottom of the slope as shown in the
Site Plan Plate 2 The existing steep slope is approximately 40 feet high and had
experienced some severe erosion as a result of heavy spring season rainfall as shown in
the following photograph taken during site investigation Some existing residential
homes including wall structures and swimming pool are located near the toe of the steep
slope as also shown in the following photograph
It was desired to repair the failed eroded slope to protect the public from the rear
parking of the Burger King restaurant at the top of the slope and the residential homes
located near the toe of the slope Investigation and design for the landslide remediation
was tasked to a design team consisting of Geolabs Inc M E Pacific currently AECOM
and KSF Inc

The complicated property configuration ownerships by Burger King City and
County of Honolulu and State of Hawaii as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and the
steep slope presented challenges to the landslide remedial design These challenges
included difficult construction access and easement acquisition as well as hidden steep
sloping ground conditions public safety during construction and cooperation with the
State of Hawaii and private owners
The site investigation was expedited to address emergency request after the slope
failure due to heavy seasonal rainfalls and to provide recommendations for a cost efficient
remedial solution meeting the long term and quick construction criteria The design team
provided extensive design efforts addressing various construction considerations and
owner s requests concerns as well as extensive coordination between the owners State
of Hawaii City and County of Honolulu Burger King restaurant and private home
owners consultants regulatory agencies and contractors Final construction drawings
were delivered after several revisions
The following design concerns were addressed during the process of the project
design
There was concern that an old spring shown on the subdivision construction
drawings may cause soft ground conditions near toe of the slope creating
potential settlement to the segmental retaining wall
Solution Design a segmental retaining wall system utilizing high density
polyethylene geo grid to reinforce the backfill zone and improving the shear
strength of the reinforced zone This composite system generally forms a
gravity wall structure with an ability to tolerate significant total and differential
settlements
Abandoned old drainage ditch shown on the subdivision construction
drawings was hydraulically connected to an old drainage culvert under
Likelike Highway The ditch may be backfilled with undocumented and
uncompacted fill which may cause soft and yielding subgrade conditions at
the bottom of the segmental retaining wall
Solution Design of the segmental retaining wall with a minimum
embedment of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade as
shown in the following photographs

construction access road was removed and backfilled for slope restoration as required by
the contract The contractor proposed to restore the original one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V slope using a reinforced soil slope Slope stability analysis was conducted and
submitted by the contractor for the proposed slope restoration using geo grid reinforcing
layer placed every 24 inch lift referring to Submittal No 11 Based on the submittal a
factor of safety for the 1H 1V slope was at least 1 5

Conclusions
With limited budget and time constraint a thorough site investigation would be
impractical due to the extremely difficult access for drilling exploration equipment
However the design team was able to complete the conceptual evaluation field
exploration and preliminary remediation design in about seven 7 months delivering an
innovative and cost effective design alternative to mitigate the failed eroded steep slope
adjacent to the busy public restaurant and the residential homes
The construction drawings were executed in a timely manner meeting the criteria of
the emergency remediation The construction drawings were prepared and coordinated
through the owners consultants and contractors to facilitate problem solving and
teamwork resulting in a practical construction solution
The design team also provided quality construction support services with timely
responses to the construction submittals and RFIs The periodic site visits conducted by
the engineers effectively provided the construction progress update raised construction
concerns and provided quick recommendations solutions as appropriate
In conjunction with the project completion we provided the construction quality
assurance and quality control services for the slope restoration upon the contractor s
request and the City s approval We believe that quick initiation and action are the key
elements for the successful project
With the team s effort and support the construction for this emergency landslide
remediation project was completed approximately one month ahead of schedule and
under budget No change order was claimed by the contractor Upon completion of
construction Royal Contracting Company Ltd received a merit award from GCA of
Hawaii for the Emergency Landslide Remediation adjacent to Burger King Kaneohe
project as shown in the following photographs
