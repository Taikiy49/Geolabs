This report presents the results of our percolation test for
the proposed subdivision development on Kamehameha Highway
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary sub
surface soil conditions at the site and the in situ seepage charac
teristics for the design of sewage disposal systems
Field Exploration
A total of three 4 inch diameter auger borings with depths ranging
from 23 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface were drilled
at the southern portion of the site
The borings were drilled with a truck mounted rotary rig in the
open area cleared by Mr John Cassell The locations are shown
on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 The drilling operation was
monitored by our field engineer who also logged the borings The
logs of the borings are presented on Plates A 1 through A 3
W 2
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclose that the southern portion
of the site is blanketed by a 17 foot layer of reddish brown
silty clay underlain by light brown to brown silty clay with
gravel boulder and sand matrix which extended to the limits
of the exploration
Although two of the borings were located slightly outside the
eastern project boundary the subsurface soil data should be
the representative of the southern site subsurface soil condi
tions The northern portion of the site may encounter more
sandy coral soils due to its close proximity toward the beach
area
Groundwater levels were encountered in all drilled borings at
about 16 to 17 feet below the existing ground surfaces
Percolation Tests
Falling head percolation tests were performed initially in the
drilled holes Due to intrusion of percolation water bore holes
nos 2 and 3 caved in before the completion of the percolation
testing Subsequently additional new test holes were drilled
in the vicinity of the abandoned cave in holes
After the new borings were drilled clean gravel was used to fill
up the lower portion of the hole below the groundwater level to
prevent potential cave in condition The portion of the drilled
W 3
hole above the groundwater level was cased with a 3 inch diameter
drilling casing Water was then used to fill up the casing first
and then it was adjusted until a constant head of water developed
at the top of the casing The percolation rates are summarized
in Plate A 4 Summary of Constant Head Percolation Tests
Falling head percolation tests were also performed in the drilled
holes at different drilled lengths above groundwater level How
ever the resulting percolation rates were too low to be considered
as acceptable condition Therefore percolation tests for this
project site were mainly on the sandy and gravelly soil layer below
the groundwater level
Based on the test results from the above bore holes it is our
opinion that the gravelly soils below the groundwater level will
provide adequate percolation rates for the proposed private cess
pool sewage disposal system Based on the percolation test data
obtained at the drilled holes a 6 foot diameter cesspool extending
a minimum depth of 5 feet into the soil layer below the groundwater
level about 23 feet from existing ground surface would be required
for each single family residence
The space between the block lining and the undisturbed soil side
wall of 8 foot minimum diameter excavation should be immediately
backfilled with free drainage granular materials Additional layer
of granular filter material is also recommended to be placed at the
bottom of each cesspool
