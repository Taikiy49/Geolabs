INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at 5687 Kalani
anaole Highway on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed project will consist
of a two 2 story single family residence
A concrete slab on grade is planned for the residence
Building foundation loadings are anticipated to be moderate to
light and typical for the proposed wood framed residential struc
tures
Minimal site grading involving up to 1 5 feet of fills is
anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide building foundation
recommendations for the subject house The recommendations are
based on the soil information obtained from the borings drilled for
this exploration
Our work consisted of drilling three 3 borings to depths of
11 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The ap
proximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The
results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were
utilized in the engineering analysis to formulate our recommenda
tions are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

EXISTING CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Niu Valley area of Honolulu
in the island of Oahu This approximately rectangular shaped
16 217 square foot lot is located at 5687 Kalanianaole Highway
opposite to the Niu Valley Shopping Center
The existing lot is generally flat with elevations at
approximately 4 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL The lot is lined
with coconut trees Stockpiles of sand dirt and boulders were
also observed on the east and west sides of the site during our
field exploration
A CRM seawall is located approximately 33 feet south of the
southern rear property line of the subject lot
At the time of our field exploration the site was vacant and
covered with grasses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled with
a truck mounted drilling rig The locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered approximately a 4 foot
thick surface layer of medium dense moist silty sand overlying
medium dense gravelly sand to silty sandy gravel to the maximum
depth of the borings drilled which was 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface In Boring B 3 loose to very loose silty

the ocean it is anticipated that groundwater levels may fluctuate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
medium dense silty sand to gravelly sand Based on the findings
at Boring B 3 it appears very loose and soft pockets zones of
silty gravel to gravelly silt were also encountered below the upper
crusts at depths of about 9 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface High groundwater levels were also encountered at
approximate depth of 4 feet below the existing ground surface The
proposed 1 5 feet of new fill would help to provide a thicker
bearing layer to support the slabs on grade and footings It is
our understanding that the finish slab elevations will be at an
elevation of 6 feet
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following
sections
Foundations
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to
support the proposed structure
Due to the high groundwater levels and subsoil conditions
encountered it is our opinion that an allowable bearing capacity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2471 00 Page 5
of up to 1 500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the new
compacted structural fill or recompacted in situ sandy soil The
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
If loose soft soil pockets were encountered at the bottom of
the footing excavation it should be over excavated to dense in
situ soil and replaced with select on site sandy soils or granular
select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation
This is to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect
the performance of the footing foundations
Slab On Grade
A minimum 6 inch thick layer of 3B Fine cushion fill is
recommended below the slab The above poorly graded coarse
aggregates will also act as a capillary moisture break to minimize
moisture infiltration of the slab bottom In order to further
protect against possible moisture infiltration a vapor barrier may
also be incorporated below the slab in addition to the above 3B
Fine gravel capillary break and cushion layer
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive structural fill or in situ silty sand subgrade soil
The perimeter thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2471 00 Page 6
For the concrete slab on grade driveway the perimeter
thickened edge may be reduced to 8 inch embedment depth
Erosion Protection
The above recommendations for spread footing foundations and
slabs on grade consider submerged soil conditions but do not
consider the potential of undermining due to erosion resulting from
possible periodic flooding or tsunami
It is our understanding that the site development plan will
provide a set back of about 80 feet between the existing CRM
seawall at the water s edge and the proposed house Due to the
relatively close proximity of potential sources of inundation
i e the ocean and the Niu Valley Drainage Channel it is our
opinion that the final design of the proposed site improvements
should include provisions for flood erosion control It is our
understanding that the proposed site grading and the finish floor
Slab elevation include consideration for the design flood levels
currently required by code and regulation
Lot Grading
Minimal site grading up to 1 5 feet of fill is anticipated
for the proposed residence
Due to the loose sandy subsurface soil layers it is
anticipated that some fill ground settlements would occur It is
our opinion that due to the sandy nature of the underlying loose
deposits fill ground settlements should occur in a relatively
short time period probably within the first two 2 to three 3
weeks after the completion of fill placements Therefore we
recommend the fill be placed first at least two 2 to three 3
weeks prior to the foundation construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2471 00 Page 7
The fills and backfills within the top 18 inches minimum
below the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such
as select on site silty sand select borrow crusher waste or
coral All imported fill materials should be tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction all areas within the grading limits and
the proposed house area should be cleared and grubbed of all
rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site
The area to receive the new fill material should first be
scarified to a depth of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to
above optimum moisture content and then compacted to obtain 90 of
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal loose
lifts not exceeding 10 inch thick and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per the above ASTM Test Method D 1557
78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run
off away from all foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should
be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommen
dations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during the site
grading and construction of the fill placement and foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifica
tion or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the
site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

based in part upon information obtained from field borings

evident until construction If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling three 3 borings ranging between 11 5 to 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch
diameter continuous flight auger was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory A graphic presentation of the materials encountered
in the borings are presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through
A 3 All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2
inch standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow
counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
