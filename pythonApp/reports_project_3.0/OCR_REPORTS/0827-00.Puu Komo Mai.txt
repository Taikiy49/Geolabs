INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for
the design and construction of the proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on the northwestern side of
Lilipuna Road about 650 feet northeast of its intersection
with Kamehameha Highway
The site is bordered by Lilipuna Road on the southeast
a vacant lot on the north and the access road for the
Puu Alii Townhouse Development on the west
The site slopes upward from Lilipuna Road at an overall
gradient of about 6 horizontal to 1 vertical
The site was vacant during our field exploration and covered
with trees and grass
The ground elevation at the site varies from about 106 to
140 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed development consists of two 4 story townhouse
structures approximately 54 by 158 feet and 56 by 132 feet
in plan The first floor levels will incorporate basement
walls
In general the site grading will consist of cutting 2 to 10
feet for the first floor level and filling up to 10 feet
behind the basement walls

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Six borings were drilled at the site to depths of about 21 feet
below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface
conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by stiff residual
soils consisting of reddish brown clayey silt The upper
5 feet is very stiff and grades to medium to stiff with
depth
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed a very stiff surface layer
over stiff to medium soils at the site Shallow foundations
bearing on the undisturbed soil or compacted fill will provide
adequate support for the proposed structure

P 4
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low
expansion potential therefore slab on grade can be
designed without special provisions for expansive soil
conditions
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for the proposed structures
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the very stiff in situ soil within 5
feet of the existing ground Below about 5 feet the
moisture content of the in situ soil increases and the
shear strength decreases Therefore a lower allowable
bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended where the
footings are located in cut greater than 5 feet For
footings bearing on compacted fill an allowable bearing
value of 2500 p s f is recommended
The bottom of the footing should be placed about 2 feet
below the finish grade To avoid additional load on the
basement walls footings located behind the wall should be
placed below a 1 horizontal to 1 vertical plane drawn upward
from the bottom of the basement wall
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trench

Estimated settlements for the structures are anticipated to
be less than one inch total and one half inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Site Grading
Cuts and fills up to about 10 feet are planned for the
site development
The site to be graded should be cleared of vegetation
debris and other deleterious material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified
to a minimum of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
test method
Fills should be placed in 8 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to the above specified density
The reddish brown on site soils are acceptable fill and
footing backfill material Any additional off site borrow
required should be tested and approved by the soil engineer
prior to transportation to the site The borrow material
should be non expansive and should not contain boulders
organic or deleterious material

Most of the fills behind the basement walls should be placed
during the mass grading to reduce the amount of backfill
required behind the walls during the foundation construc
tion
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that Sslab on grade should be placed over
a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum
dry density
The subgrade should be protected from drying and kept moist
during construction It should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls will
help to prevent seepage water
Basement and Retaining Walls
Basement walls up to about 10 feet high are planned for
both buildings Retaining walls about 5 feet high will
be used in the parking area

Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet
of the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge
should be included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

An A C parking area is planned between the two buildings

brown silty surface soil the preliminary pavement design
is as follows

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by a soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate bearing and com
paction have been obtained and that the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
