INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed liquid sugar plant within the existing California Hawaiian
C H refinery site at Aiea on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3026 00 Page 2
A design memorandum dated was issued by our office
presenting our preliminary recommendations for the subject project
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new liquid sugar plant within the existing Aiea C H
refinery site on the island of Oahu Hawaii The new facilities will be located at the
northeastern portion of the existing refinery site and will consist of the following structures
1 A Main Building which will be a two story structure of approximately 60 by
100 feet in plan dimension
2 A Boiler Lean To Building which will be approximately 20 by 40 feet in plan
dimension and located immediately to the north of the Main Building
3 A Yard Building which will be a one story open structure with a floor area
of about 1800 square feet
4 Three 3 water tanks each of which will be approximately 13 feet in
diameter and 16 feet high
5 A Pipe Bridge connecting the Main Building and the Yard Building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3026 00 Page 3
It is our understanding that the Main Building will consist of a steel frame structure
with metal sliding and concrete floors Concrete slab on grade with metal roofing will be
utilized for the Yard Building We also understand that the other proposed structures
would be of steel construction As part of this project construction of new utility lines and
asphalt pavements for the access roadway are planned We anticipate that site grading
will consist of minor cuts and fills on the order of two feet 2 to provide a level building
pad for construction
Based on the information provided by GMP Associates Inc structural loads
varying from 5 to 76 kips per column are anticipated for the proposed construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located off Aiea Heights Drive within the existing Aiea C H
refinery site on the island of Oahu Hawaii The new facilities will be located at the
northeastern portion of the refinery site Most of the proposed project site is presently
an existing parking area with concrete pavement
The project area is bounded by a huge existing warehouse to the east and a series
of tank structures to the north A rockwall and an asphalt driveway are present along the
southern and western sides of the project area
The majority of the subject site is relatively level with the existing ground surface
elevations ranging from approximately 114 to 119 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven 7 borings
to depths ranging between 15 and 20 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our exploration the site generally appears to be underlain by surficial
clayey soils overlying a saprolite deposit consisting of stiff silty clay with gravels extending
to depths of approximately four 4 to eight feet 8 below the existing ground surface
Below the saprolite the borings encountered a moderately weathered basalt formation
extending to the maximum depths explored The surface of the future Main Building site
is currently covered with approximately seven inches 7 of concrete pavement
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time of
year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a
deposit of silty clay which is considered to be highly expansive based on the laboratory
test results Therefore special provisions and construction procedures should be
observed and implemented during site grading to reduce the effects of potentially
expansive soils on the proposed structures
Conventional shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings
may be used for support of the Main Building Equipment Yard Boiler Lean To and
Water tank structures Because of the presence of highly expansive subgrade soils we
recommend that all shallow footing foundations bear on a minimum of two feet 2 of
compacted select granular fill Alternatively a drilled pier foundation system may be used
for support of all structures including the proposed pipe bridge to reduce over
excavation quantities and import of select granular fill material If a drilled pier foundation
system is used select fill beneath the shallow foundations may be eliminated We
recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on at least two feet 2 of
compacted select granular fill due to the highly expansive nature of the anticipated slab
subgrade Detail recommendations are presented in the following subsections
Shallow Foundations
We understand from conversations with the project structural engineer that
structural column loads varying from 5 to 76 kips per column are anticipated for the
proposed construction Because of the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3026 00 Page 7
the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for
support of the Main Building Equipment Yard Boiler Lean To and Water Tank structures
Due to the significant variation of column loads and the presence of highly
expansive subsoils we recommend that shallow footings be supported on two feet 2
of select granular fill material The fill should extend horizontally beyond the sides of the
footing a distance of at least one half 2 the footing width or two feet 2 whichever is
greater The select granular fill material should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 test procedures The over excavated surface and the
backfilling operations should be performed in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report
Alternately to reduce the import of select granular fill footing locations may be
over excavated down to the underlying basalt formation and the footings may bear
directly on the weathered basalt The resulting over excavation may also be backfilled
with lean concrete up to the design footing subgrade elevations
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on compacted select granular fill material
for footings bearing on the basalt formation These bearing values are for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3026 00 Page 8
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on compacted select granular fill to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential For footings bearing directly on the basalt rock formation total and differential
settlements are estimated to be less than inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 and 0 45 may be used for
footings bearing on the compacted fill or basalt formation respectively Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing
conditions and the embedment depths

In consideration of the uplift forces and lateral loads acting on the foundation of the
pipe bridge we recommend that drilled pier foundations be used to support the proposed
pipe bridge connecting the Main Building and the Equipment Yard Because of the

The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the

Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading will consist of placement of one 1 to two feet 2
of fill to raise the existing ground to the finished subgrade elevations
Areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed
All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed These should include but are not limited to the tank structure
at the future Boiler Lean To location the old foundation slab of a previous structure at the
proposed water tank areas and the concrete pavement slab at the proposed Main
Building site Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with
compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with compacted select
granular fill material Utilities that are to be abandoned in place should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath slabs and pavement
areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 24 inches below the future slab or
pavement subgrade level such as bottom of base course If zones of soft loose or
saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over
excavation may be required to expose firm material The resulting excavation should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material The clayey material resulting from
the over excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site Based on the
existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevations of the structures we
anticipate that a relatively small amount of over excavation of the surficial expansive soils
would be required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3026 00 Page 13
The over excavated surface should be moisture conditioned to at least two percent
2 above optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent
relative Compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 test procedures Select granular fill material should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in loose lift thickness and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
The select granular fill material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than three inches 3 in largest dimension The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than one percent
1 when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain
less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be
tested and approved for its intended use by our firm prior to delivery to the project site

Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the
pipes Granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should be used for the trench
backfill up to about one foot 1 above the pipes to reduce the compaction effort and the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
placed in maximum ten inch 10 horizontal loose lifts mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper two feet 2 of the
trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3026 00 Page 15
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from slabs and footings

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of
seven 7 borings to depths ranging between 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer failing 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of four inches 4 in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0
foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
