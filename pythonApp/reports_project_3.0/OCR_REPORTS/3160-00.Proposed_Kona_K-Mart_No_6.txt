REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes
up the west central part of the island The lava formation encountered at the site appears
to be of pahoehoe flow which spreaded and ponded as it approached the ocean
Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal
structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe
lavas These cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and inter flow cavities such as blisters and pockets
Lava tubes intra flow cavities are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow
leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally
along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalts are considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3160 00 a Page 7
porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along
clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they serve
as the major water transmission features
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Increment A 1 of the Liliuokalani Trust Subdivision development is located on
the north side of Kailua Kona in the District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii The
subject site is located to the west of the existing Palani Road and to the north of Queen
Kaahumanu Highway as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The site is
bounded by the proposed Queen Liliuokalani Boulevard to the northwest and by the
proposed Keohokalole Street to the southwest as shown on Plate 3
The subject site is located in a relatively undeveloped area of Kailua Kona Lava
flow and clinker formations from previous volcanic activity in the area were observed on
the existing ground surface creating a very rugged ground surface Fine grained soil and
ash were noted on the ground surface in some areas near the existing Palani Road The
project site is vegetated with Keawe trees and grasses The project site slopes down
from the northeast to southwest with elevations ranging from 170 feet MSL along the
northeast limits to about 110 feet MSL along the southwest limits

portion of Increment A 1 for the commercial development One boring was drilled in the

In general our field exploration encountered volcanic rock consisting of hard to
very hard basalt with occasional clinker layers from the surface to depths ranging from
10 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface However Boring Nos 4 and 12
encountered a surficial layer of ash of about 1 foot in thickness followed by basaltic rock
extending to the maximum depth drilled The basalt encountered ranged from very
closely to widely fractured and ranged from virtually unweathered to moderately
weathered The basalt formation encountered included dense and vesicular porous
basalt The clinker layers encountered ranged from 2 to 5 feet thick and consisted of
rock fragments with variable amounts of soil matrix The clinker layers encountered were
generally dense
Cavities were encountered at different depths within the basalt formation in 11 of
the 13 borings drilled at the site As previously mentioned cavities are commonly
encountered in the pahoehoe flows In general the cavities encountered ranged from
about 2 to 1 feet in the borings and were encountered at various depths A relatively
large cavity was encountered in Boring No 9 from a depth of about 17 to 29 feet below
the ground surface It should be emphasized that the vertical and lateral extent of these
cavities are highly variable
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors

Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site consist
generally of basalt rock formation with frequent cavities and some clinker layers Based
on our evaluation of the subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed development
the principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed commercial
development include the following
1 foundation support of the proposed structure located across a cut and fill
transition area
2 probing and grouting of the cavities and clinker zones beneath foundations
for buildings and the retaining wall and
3 grading of the proposed project site and pavement design for the roads
and parking areas

store would be located on fill with the remainder located on cut in basalt rock To reduce
the potential for differential settlement between the portion of the building located on cut
and the portion on fill we recommend that shallow spread footings for the entire K Mart
store bear on compacted fill To provide the compacted fill in the cut areas the footing

the fill thickness is less than 5 feet below the bottom of footing

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rock boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the
number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of
compaction should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of
this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion
for adequate compaction

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling 13
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 13 at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 The depths of our borings range between about 10 and
35 feet below the existing ground surface They were drilled using truck mounted drill
rigs equipped with rotary coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A of Appendix A Graphic presentation of the
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD

Poor 25 50

Good 75 90

Percolation Testing
Percolation tests were performed by using the constant head method in 3 drilled
holes adjacent to Boring and 12 A 4 inch hole was drilled adjacent to the
borings to a depth of about 5 feet for each test A water truck was used to pump water
into the test holes The flow rate was monitored by a flowmeter until a relatively constant
flow rate was achieved The results of the percolation tests are presented on Plates B 1
through B 3
