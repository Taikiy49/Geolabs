PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located along Hikimoe Street between Waipahu Depot Road and 
Mokuola Street in the Waipahu area of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. The existing roadway was paved with Portland Cement Concrete (PCC). 
Sections of the PCC pavement that experienced settlement and failure had been overlaid 
by asphaltic concrete. 
The project consisted of reconstruction of the existing PCC pavement on 
Hikimoe Street for the bus operation improvements, as part of the rail station connectivity 
for transit-oriented-development. The design-build contract requires design and 
construction for the improvement of bus operations at Waipahu Transit Center, including 
pavement reconstruction of Hikimoe Street. The general reconstruction area is shown on 
the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The site grading consisted of removal of 24 inches of existing subgrade soils and 
replacing with select granular fills below 8.5-inch of concrete pavement between 
Station 0+16.79 and Station 5+50.10 where the existing pavement experienced 
settlement and/or failure. Remaining reconstruction areas (between Station 5+50.10 and 
Station 12+37.63) included removal of the existing concrete pavement and replacing with 
8.5-inch of concrete pavement overlying 6-inch of aggregate select borrow subbase

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the roadway reconstruction limits were 
demolished by removal of concrete pavement and sidewalk. Concrete debris generated 
from the demolition operations were disposed of off-site. Following the demolition 
operations, the soft and/or yielding subgrade soils were over-excavated for installation of 
24-inch thick stabilization layer. The stabilization layer consisted of 24 inches of select 
granular materials reinforced with two layers of Tensar TX140 geogrid and one layer of 
Geotex 801 geotextile permanent separator. 
In general, bottom of the stabilization was moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent 
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to about 85 percent of the maximum 
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. The select granular fills were 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to about 
90 to 95 percent of the maximum dry density. Compaction effort was established using a 
smooth vibratory drum roller.  
It should be noted that the reinforcing geogrid was installed to stabilize the 
soft/yielded subgrade. To reduce the risk of disturbing soft/yielded subgrade condition, the 
compaction for the first lift of the select granular fills was reduced from 95 to 90 percent. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic density tests were conducted in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of fills and backfills 
during the site grading operation.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.11. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering April 23 through 
July 16, 2019.
