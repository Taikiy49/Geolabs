1 1 Introduction
performed for the Maile Way Repaving University of Hawaii Manoa project located in
the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity

This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our

1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located on the University of Hawaii Manoa campus in the
Honolulu District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves repaving of
Maile Way from University Avenue to East West Road for a distance of approximately
2 000 linear feet as shown on the Site Plan Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Maile Way consists of one traffic lane in each direction a center median and
parking on both sides of the roadway There are several roadways and driveways that
intersect with Maile Way and connect Maile Way to the campus buildings
Based on information provided we understand that the pavement section for the
western portion of Maile Way consists of 2 inches of asphaltic concrete on 6 inches of
aggregate base course over 18 inches of aggregate subbase material In addition we
understand that this area has been overlaid with 2 inches of asphaltic concrete
Campus shuttle buses run along Maile Way during the day and evening hours
In addition a city bus route goes through Maile Way

and subsurface soil conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained was
utilized to develop an idealized subsurface data set for the formulation of geotechnical

performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated The scope of work for this exploration included the following
tasks and work efforts

engineer from our firm

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with
a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea level rose to above the present sea level
The process of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are then transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the
stream flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this
transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load

2 2 Site Description
The project site is located along Maile Way on the University of Hawaii Manoa
campus The existing roadway along Maile Way consists of one lane of traffic in each
direction a center median and parking on both sides of the roadway The existing
roadway is covered with an asphaltic concrete surface
Based on our observation several types of pavement distresses were noted on
the existing pavement surface The pavement distresses consisted of alligator cracking
depression longitudinal cracking and rutting It appears that the pavement distresses
were more prevalent on the western portion of Maile Way
Based on available drawings there are numerous utility lines along Maile Way
These utility lines include sewer water drain gas and chill water lines
Based on the topographic survey map provided the area generally slopes down
at a gradient of approximately 3 to 8 percent toward the east The ground surface
elevations along the roadway range from about 86 feet Mean Sea Level MSL to
about 143 feet MSL

3 4 Pavement Structural Section
Based on the relatively high traffic volume anticipated for Maile Way we believe
that base materials for flexible pavements should consist of asphalt concrete base
ACB Therefore flexible pavement sections utilizing untreated aggregate base
courses also were not considered in our pavement design analyses

3 5 Site Preparation Below Pavement Areas
We anticipate that the majority of the new roadway pavements will be
constructed near the existing grade The future pavement areas should be excavated
where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate
subbase course layer The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with AASHTO Test Method T 180
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide stabilization by cement treatment may consist of one

3 6 Compaction Requirements
In general fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Aggregate
subbase materials required for the new pavements should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Subsection 703 16 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for
Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 The aggregate subbase should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to not less than 95 percent of the maximum density
determined in accordance with AASHTO T 180 ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basaltic
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to at least 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity determined
in accordance with AASHTO T 209 ASTM D 2041 test procedures
Asphaltic concrete AC material should be constructed in general accordance
with Section 401 Asphalt Concrete Pavement of the State of Hawaii Standard
Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 and subsequent
amendments

3 7 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils It should be noted that the
clayey subgrade soils are susceptible to softening and loss of strength upon saturation
Therefore special attention should be given to the surface and subsurface drainage of
the pavements As a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped and drainage
gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate
drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or
after construction To reduce the potential for subsurface water from entering into the
pavement structural section we recommend that a pavement subdrain system be
provided along the sides of the roadway In addition the installation of transverse
subdrains across low elevation areas is recommended
The subdrain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations
facing down surrounded by permeable material such as AASHTO M 43 No 67
gradation material A permeable separator non woven filter fabric conforming to
Section 648 of the HSS should be placed around the permeable backfill material to
reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage material The subdrains
should be daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for proper discharge

preparation to reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the surface clayey soils

critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed
by Geolabs or a qualified geotechnical engineering during site grading to assist the
contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture
content on each fill lift It is highly recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the Maile Way Repaving
University of Hawaii Manoa project The items of construction monitoring that are
critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the subgrade preparation
and fill placement and compaction
Other aspects of earthwork construction also should be observed by a
representative from Geolabs or a qualified geotechnical engineer This is to observe
compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared

construction experience in similar subsurface conditions The field observations should
be performed by the geotechnical engineer or a representative from his her firm under

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 The borings were
advanced to depths of approximately 8 5 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled for the project are shown on
the Site Plan Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer or geologist who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
