INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residence located at 3239 Huelani Drive in the west side of
Manoa Vailey on the isiand of Oahu Hawaii The location of the site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 2
This report summaries our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 3239 Huelani Drive in Manoa Valley A residential
structure previously located at the site has been demolished and the lot is currently
empty It is proposed to construct a new single family residence at the project site
It is our understanding that the proposed two 2 story residence is to be of wood
framed post and beam construction The proposed garage and driveway are also to be
wood framed elevated structures supported by post and beam construction Therefore
cut and fill operations for site grading will be minimal
New CMU retaining walls to be tied to the existing CRM wall are also planned
near the southwestern corner of the lot to provide grade separations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the
proposed residence

The work was done in accordance with our fee proposal dated December 16
1991 to Sueda and Associates Inc The scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tion of the surface soil conditions and to select locations for the borings to
maximize the value of the information obtained
3 Mobilization and demobilization of hand carried portable drilling equipment
and operators to and from the project site
4 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths of 12 0 to 46 5 feet
below the existing ground surface using auger and core drilling techniques
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and the engineering properties of the
materials encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 3239 Huelani Drive in the Manoa residential district
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is located on the western hilly terrain
of Manoa Valley
The project site is fronted by Huelani Drive on the westerly property boundary
Existing CRM walls bound the subject lot along both the northerly and southerly property
limits The easterly property limit is bounded by a sewerline easement
In general the topography of the project site is sloping easterly with slope ratios
of about three horizontal to one vertical H 1V to four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V
The lot is fronted by Huelani Drive sloping downwards southerly with a gradient of slightly
more than ten 10 percent At the time of our field exploration the previously existing
house had been demolished and the site had been grubbed and cleared of surface
boulders A short CRM retaining wall remains near the southwestern corner of the site
providing a grade change of about three and one half 3 5 feet high
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epochs by the activities of two 2 basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
Koolau is the younger of the two and reached the end of its main shield building phase
about 2 million years ago The project site is located on the southern flank of Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2826 00 Page 5
Koolau was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from northwest to southeast
trending rift zones These lava flows are interbedded pahoehoe and a a flows with
pahoehoe being the dominant form The project site is situated at the base of the
westerly wall of Manoa Valley in a drainageway cut into the valley wall
Continued erosion in the drainageway upslope of the site has resulted in the
deposition of talus and alluvial materials in the lower reaches of the drainageway The
site is underlain by soils derived from these talus and alluvial materials
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 12 0 to 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a surface layer
approximately one 1 to five 5 feet thick of medium stiff silty clay overlying talus
deposits consisting of cobbles and boulders interbedded with clayey silt to a depth of
46 5 feet the maximum drilled depth
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generai
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a surface layer about
one 1 to five 5 feet thick of medium stiff clay overlying cobbles and boulders
interbedded with clayey silt The laboratory test results and previous experience indicate
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2826 00 Page 6
that the surface clay material has moderately high expansion potential Therefore the
foundation will require special design and construction procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Slope Stability
The project site is located within Manoa Valley which has a history of instability
Our scope of work however did not include exploratory borings sampling or
testing of the materials upslope and downslope of the immediate project site nor
analytical evaluations of the stability of the overall hillside on which the project site is
located
It is our understanding the project site and the neighboring lots in the subdivision
have been developed for several decades Visual observation in the vicinity of the project
site did not reveal any indications of major distress due to slope instability Our field
exploration did not encounter thick deposits of adobe clay which has been identified as
one of the main causes of reported hillside instability in the Manoa Valley Therefore it
is our opinion that the proposed construction will not be inherently unstable nor will it
jeopardize the stability of the subject site or of the adjacent lots
Foundation
The proposed residence is located at an existing hillside lot Therefore deep
footing foundations are recommended to support the proposed structure We
recommend that the footing foundations be embedded a minimum of three 3 feet below
the lowest adjacent finish grade The design concept is shown schematically on Plate 3
Schematic Section Deep Footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 7
For footings located in the existing sloping ground the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum eight 8 foot horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer toe of the footing to the face of the slope Our field exploration
indicated that the site is underlain by bouidery talus therefore hard chipping into on site
boulders during footing excavation may be required
The recommended deep footing embedment also reduces the potentially
detrimental effects of soil swelling which may be caused by the moderately to highly
expansive soil encountered at the site
An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
in situ soil with gravel and boulders
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate footing
embedment and bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to
placement of concrete
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed building may be provided by
passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A frictional coefficient of 0 3 may be used to
estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings and the in situ soils
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 8
If soft or loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular
select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Footing settlements under design loadings are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one 1 inch total and one half 2 inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils around the
foundation
Lot Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed residence
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubbles and organic material
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be removed and properly disposed of
off site
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches thoroughly
moisture conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM
D 1557 78 to reduce surface infiltration
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations and to prevent any ponding situation within the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 9
Imported fill and backfill materials that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are anticipated to provide grade separation for the proposed
project The following design guidelines for the retaining walls are recommended
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill and above groundwater
Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill and above groundwater
2 A bearing value up to 3 000 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of
three 3 feet may be used for design For footings located within a slope
the footing embedment should be further increased to provide a minimum
eight 8 feet horizontal set back distance measured from the outer toe of
the footing to the slope surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 10
3 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f may
be used A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the walls
4 Backfill behind the retaining walls should consist of granular non expansive
material such as select borrow or 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
to minimize excessive compactive effort required The upper 12 inches of
backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the walls
5 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to protect
against build up of hydrostatic pressure
Drainage
A trench drain with a minimum depth of four 4 feet below the finish ground is
recommended near the front of the lot to intercept possible seepage from the upper slope
area The approximate location of the recommended trench drain is shown on Plate 2
Site Plan The details of the recommended trench drain is shown on Plate 4 Typical
Trench Drain
The finished grade outside the footings should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that a gutter system be
installed around the residence and that the gutter run off water be diverted as far away
from the foundatioris as feasible Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2826 00 Page 11
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and the lot
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for site grading utility and
foundation construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations

practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or implied

of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report

Field Exploration

spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by a NX core barrel The core recovery REC
