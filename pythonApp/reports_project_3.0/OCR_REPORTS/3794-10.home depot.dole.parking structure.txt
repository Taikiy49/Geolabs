In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated March 17 1998 we have
recently completed our field exploration for the proposed parking structure at the
future Home Depot site located in the Iwilei area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu

during the current field exploration to depths ranging from about 21 5 to 80 5 feet below
the existing ground surface These borings were drilled to supplement the two borings
drilled previously at the parking structure site and the four borings drilled previously at the
retail building site to provide a better delineation of the subsurface materials encountered
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the attached Site Plan
Plate 1
We understand that the retail building will be supported by a spread footing
foundation in conjunction with a surcharge program The current subsurface exploration
was performed to provide information to optimize the design of the parking structure
foundation Therefore we are providing the following letter report for design of the pile
foundation system of the parking structure at the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Variable soil conditions were encountered throughout the parking structure site
from a relatively thick layer of compressible soft loose soils as much as 20 feet thick in the
northwestern portion of the site with decreasing thickness to almost no soft loose soil
layer toward the southeast These compressible soils have a major impact on the site
grading and foundation design of the project Additionally some of the near surface soils
were found to be contaminated and may require special treatment
Old Foundations
It is our understanding that the site for the parking structure appears to be situated
over footing foundations from a previous structure as well as a concrete box drain
currently in use We understand that the parking structure will straddle the concrete box
drain however some of the existing foundations are obstructions to future pile
foundations In addition there is a potential that the existing footings if removed would
be considered contaminated material
In order to facilitate the pile driving operations through these potential obstructions
we recommend that the existing foundations at proposed pile cap locations be located

existing foundations located in contaminated soil disposal should be in accordance

Ground Settlement
We anticipate the site will be raised approximately 3 feet above the existing grade
to achieve the finish grade Ground settlements are anticipated from the weight of the
new site fill due to compression of the soft clay and loose sand layers below the
site Estimated settlements due to the proposed fill are anticipated to be up to 3 inches
Due to the limited thickness of the clayey portions of the compressible layer and the
relatively high permeability of the loose sand it is anticipated that the settlements will
occur relatively rapidly We estimate that 90 percent of the settlements may occur within
about 1 to 2 months
Pile Foundations
Due to the variability of the subsurface soil conditions and required loads we
recommend that the parking structure be supported by a pile foundation system with a
test pile program to optimize pile design
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the anticipated column load
of 250 kips we recommend that driven pile foundations be used to support the proposed
2 deck parking structure We believe that 16 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete piles may be used to support the proposed parking structure The piles would
derive support from frictional resistance of the stiff clayey and silty soils underlying the site
at greater depths as encountered in our borings drilled for the subject project
An allowable compressive load capacity of up to 100 tons may be used for the
recommended pile foundation design The allowable compressive load capacity for the
piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as wind or seismic forces Piles should be spaced a minimum of 4 feet
center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity due to group action and to facilitate
pile driving

Predrilling
Our borings indicated that the project site is partially underlain by a surficial layer of
contaminated soils Therefore in order to minimize contamination to the
underlying relatively clean soils predrilling may be required at the proposed pile
locations to the depth of the water table about 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The contaminated cuttings should be properly disposed of
We wish to emphasize that the diameter of the predrilled holes should be limited to
between approximately 16 to 20 inches in order to provide the 1672 inch octagonal
pile with sufficient soil contact for lateral load resistance If an over sized predrilled
hole is encountered during construction the annular space between the piles and
predrilled holes should be backfilled with pea gravel or grouted with tremie
concrete to provide the recommended lateral load capacity
Uplift Load Resistance
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead load of the driven pile
and by shear along the pile surface and the adjacent soils An uplift load capacity
of 25 kips based on a 45 foot long pile section may be used in design of the pile
foundation

Downdrag
Downdrag is the downward shear drag acting on piles due to downward
movement of surrounding soil strata relative to the piles Downdrag may occur by
placement of fill over a compressible soil layer causing the soil to move downward
relative to the pile inducing a drag load that could be a substantial increase to the
anticipated loads In order to reduce downdrag loads we recommend that the fills
be placed as soon as practicable and ground settlements completed prior to pile
installation
Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of pile foundations will result primarily from the elastic compression of
the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total settlement of the
pile supported foundation to be less than inch with differential settlements
between columns supported on piles not exceeding about one half that amount
We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied

Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum energy
of 42 000 foot pounds in the field The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control such that driving within the loose soft soil may be controlled at
a lower energy to reduce tensile stress development within the piles which could
damage or cause cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be
driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy
to the top of the pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs Hawaii
should review the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate if the equipment is
capable of installing the piles to the recommended capacity The contractor
should forward pile and driving equipment data forms to Geolabs Hawaii prior to
construction for evaluation Geolabs Hawaii should review the submittal and
assess the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
Pile Driving Criteria
For preliminary design purposes pile driving may be terminated when aggregated
blow counts are at least 100 blows per foot for the last foot or 12 blows per inch for
the last 4 inches This preliminary driving criteria should be confirmed after the
selection of the pile driving equipment and during the test pile driving operation to
accommodate variations of subsurface conditions in the field
In general each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may
be rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is
interrupted for more than four hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required
depth and or meeting the pile driving criteria During soft driving i e when the pile
tip is within soft loose soils the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully
controlled such that significant tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
Because variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the bearing
soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field by a
representative from our office during driving It should be noted that the pile
design was developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data This
criteria is based on the use of a diesel driving hammer with a rated energy of
42 000 foot pounds to drive a 16 inch octagonal concrete pile with a design of up
to 100 tons A test pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary
pile design and to establish pile driving criteria The production pile driving criteria
may be revised based on the test pile program

Test Pile Program
We recommend that a test pile program which includes at least one load test be
undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To examine the driveability of piles with the use of the selected
hammer
2 To estimate the production pile lengths
3 To assess the test pile driving criteria which may be modified for use
as the production pile driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program consist of a
minimum of 10 percent of the total piles The test piles may be incorporated into
the actual foundation system upon the evaluation of the load test results When
the final foundation plan is available Geolabs Hawaii should be consulted for
selection of the test pile locations Test piles should be casted at least 10 feet
longer than the estimated pile lengths to account for potential variation In addition
the test pile program should provide flexibility to allow for addition or relocation of
the test piles as may be deemed necessary by our office to suit the conditions
encountered during the test pile driving Restriking of all the test piles may be
required as part of the test pile program The pile load test should be performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1143 loading the test pile to
200 percent of the design load
We recommend that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii be present during pile
driving operations to observe the actual driving behavior and to further evaluate
the field performance It should be noted that the pile design and driving criteria
recommended were developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration
data Therefore observation of the pile driving operations by Geolabs Hawaii is
necessary to confirm our design assumptions

Geolabs Hawaii This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
