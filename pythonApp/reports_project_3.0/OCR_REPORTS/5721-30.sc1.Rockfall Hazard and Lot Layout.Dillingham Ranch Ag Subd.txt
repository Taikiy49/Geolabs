POTENTIAL ROCKFALL HAZARD CONDITIONS AND UPDATED LOT LAYOUT
DILLINGHAM RANCH AGRICULATURAL SUBDIVISION
Dear Mr Smith
We recently received a Request For Proposal RFP for engineering services
related to the performance of an updated potential rockfall hazard evaluation for the above
agricultural subdivision project The project has been dormant for a number of years
Previously we conducted a rockfall hazard evaluation based on the 2008 lot layout In
addition based on our preliminary evaluation we prepared a design for rockfall hazard
mitigation utilizing a system of rockfall impact barriers to intercept potential falling rock
hazards During our previous rockfall hazard evaluation dwelling setbacks and the use of
rock catchment ditches were found to be of limited use due to the existing steep terrain
and lack of suitable space to develop the catchment ditches and appropriate structure
dwelling setbacks away from the potential rockfall source areas
As part of the RFP a preliminary map was provided showing the old 2008
subdivision layout superimposed on the updated subdivision layout Based on the
information provided and with respect to the requested rockfall hazard evaluation update
we understand that the number of lots has increased and the location of some lots has
changed affecting the potential exposure of the subdivision to potential falling rock
hazards
Based on our preliminary review of the updated lot layout with respect to rockfall
hazard mitigation we noted the following
Updated Lot
It appears the subdivision roadway and lots have been shifted in the mauka
direction into steeper and higher elevation rocky terrain Ideally with respect to rockfall
hazards the lot boundaries should be shifted in the makai opposite downslope direction
away from the potential rockfall source slopes Based on our preliminary review dwelling

Summary
We understand the developer desires to utilize dwelling setbacks in lieu of fixed
rockfall protection structures such as rockfall impact barriers to mitigate the potential
rockfall hazard conditions Dwelling setbacks can be effective to mitigate potential
rockfall hazards on larger size lots that have appropriate open space and slope gradients
below the rock source to serve as natural rockfall buffer boulder energy dissipation
zone However the lot layout should be defined with the setback concept in mind to take
advantage of natural terrain barriers longer gentle slopes Between lots natural
