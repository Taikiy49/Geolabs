inclinometer was installed in this boring to better delineate slide limits Locations for

Based on the data interpretation and review the water level in Boring No 2
Piezometer Nos P 2a and P 2b experienced severe excessive seepage water in the

monitoring period The dropping and raising water level may be related to the additional
ground lateral movement
PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations were recorded in our previous reports
Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway were
observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to September 2002
No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from September 2002 to
January 2003
Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 A majority of the cracks previously recorded was patched
with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development was recorded
from January 2004 to August 2004

SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface
ground movement and or creep at their respective instrument locations However
Inclinometer No I 2 currently does not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral
movement Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at
the project site Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe that lateral
near surface earth movement and or creep at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface in the area near Boring Nos 1 and 3 Inclinometer No 4 is located between
Inclinometer Nos 2 and I 3 It appears that the slide zone near Boring No 4 may be
located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos 1 and I 3 at a depth of approximately
20 feet below the ground surface
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
