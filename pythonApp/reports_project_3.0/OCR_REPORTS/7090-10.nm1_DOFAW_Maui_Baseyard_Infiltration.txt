In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated 
September 1, 2020, we performed infiltration testing services in support of the DOFAW Maui 
Baseyard Phase 1 Renovation in Kahului on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. This letter report 
summarizes work performed and presents our findings and results for the field infiltration 
testing performed at the project site. The findings and recommendations presented herein 
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is located within the existing Division of Forestry and Wildlife 
(DOFAW) Baseyard in the Kahului area on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The existing 
baseyard is located along Haleakala Highway between Kuleana and Aalele Streets, near 
the Kahului Airport. Based on the information provided, we understand it is desired to 
evaluate the infiltration characteristics at the proposed stormwater retention chamber area 
located at the northeast corner of the DOFAW Baseyard. The project location and general 
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The purpose of our exploration was to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the 
underlying subsurface materials at the proposed stormwater retention chamber area at the 
site by performing two Low Impact Development (LID) tests, designated as I-1 and I-2, at 
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. These tests were performed in 
general accordance with the procedures in Appendix D of the State of Maryland, 
Department of the Environment “Stormwater Design Manual, Volumes I and II” (Rev. 2009). 
These procedures are consistent with other state’s procedures and may generally be 
considered an industry standard.

The purpose of our field exploration was to perform field infiltration testing for the 
design of the stormwater runoff disposal structure planned at the site. To accomplish this, 
we conducted the following tasks and work efforts:

Subsurface Conditions 
Our field exploration consisted of drilling two borings, designated as Boring Nos. I-1 
and I-2, extending to depths of about 4.3 and 3 feet below the existing ground surface, 
respectively. The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Our borings generally encountered alluvial deposits consisting of soft clayey silts 
extending to the maximum depth explored of 4.3 feet below the existing ground surface. It 
should be noted that a surface fill stockpile consisting of sandy gravel extending to a depth 
of about 1.5 feet below the existing ground surface was encountered in Boring No. I-1. 
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration. 
However, groundwater levels can vary significantly depending on rainfall, temperature, 
surface runoff, groundwater withdrawal, and other factors. 
Field Infiltration Testing 
The field infiltration tests were performed by auguring two borings, designated as I-1 
and I-2, to the selected test depth generally near the proposed LID locations provided by 
Bowers + Kubota. The approximate infiltration test locations are shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 2. Upon reaching the test depth and cleaning out the borehole of soil cuttings, a 4-inch 
diameter PVC solid casing was set to the bottom of the drilled hole to allow infiltration only 
through the soil exposed at the bottom of the boring. The annular space between the 
borehole and outside of the PVC casing was filled with bentonite to ensure a seal between 
the borehole and PVC casing. The cased boreholes were then pre-soaked prior to 
performing the infiltration test. 
Falling head infiltration tests were performed to determine the average infiltration 
rates of the underlying subsurface materials. Each test consisted of four trials of filling the 
casing with 24 inches of water and taking periodic readings over a 1-hour trial period. The 
infiltration rates are then calculated based on the results of the fourth and last trial for each 
test location. The calculated infiltration rates at each test location are summarized in the 
following table. The field infiltration test results are presented on Plates 6.1 and 6.2.

It should be noted that the infiltration values presented above are the rates of

lateral spreading of the water in the subsurface materials. Based on the results of the field 
infiltration testing, the average infiltration rates at these locations indicate that an 
infiltration/detention system and similar methods of stormwater management would be 
feasible for the project. Due to the variability of the subsurface conditions and infiltration 
rates obtained, the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed by 
conducting additional infiltration tests during construction. 
Limitations 
The geotechnical field data submitted in this letter report are based in part upon 
information obtained from the field borings and infiltration tests. Variations of the subsurface 
conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident 
until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to 
re-evaluate the findings provided in this letter report. 
The field boring and infiltration test locations indicated herein are approximate, 
having been estimated using Google Earth™. Elevations noted on the borings were 
estimated from the Site Plan transmitted by Bowers + Kubota in August 2020. The physical 
locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree 
implied by the methods used. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict the 
approximate boundaries between soil/rock types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition. We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field 
exploration. However, it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall, 
temperature, and other factors.
