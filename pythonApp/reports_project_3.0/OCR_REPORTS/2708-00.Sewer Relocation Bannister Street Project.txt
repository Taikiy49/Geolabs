INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed sewer relocation at 2112A Bannister Place in
Kalihi on the island of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed in accordance with
our proposal dated May 22 1991
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for development of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Kalihi area of the city of Honolulu on the island
of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to relocate the sewer easement at the property and to
install a new 8 inch sewer line
The proposed sewer line runs along the northern boundary of the property near
Kalihi Stream and along the eastern boundary of the property
The proposed sewer line invert elevation ranges from 16 48 to 17 62 feet above
Mean Sea Level

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the installation of the proposed
sewer line
The work was done in accordance with our proposal of May 22 1991 The scope
of work was limited to
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths of sixteen and one half
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by
a Field Geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and pertinent engineering properties of the
materials encountered
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed sewer line
installation

on the project and presenting our findings and recommendations

8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and
clerical support
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed sewer line is located within the property at 2112A Bannister Place
in Kalihi on the island of Oahu
Presently the site is occupied with two 2 apartment buildings one 1 house
asphaltic concrete paved parking areas and an asphaltic concrete paved driveway
In general the site slopes upwards gently from north to south with existing ground
elevations ranging from about 20 to 21 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions along the proposed sewer line were explored by drilling
and sampling three 3 borings to depths of sixteen and one half 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our Opinion that the proposed sewer line may be constructed along the
proposed alignment provided our geotechnical engineering recommendations as
presented in this report are followed
Fair to good soil conditions were encountered at the project site The proposed
sewer line invert elevation is about 7 0 to 8 5 feet above the groundwater elevations
encountered during the time of our exploration
Due to the absence of ground water and the competence of the materials
encountered within the anticipated depth of the trench excavation it is anticipated that the

governing regulations
The final determination of the requirement for shoring and the design there of if
found to be required should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor who should
retain the services of qualified professional geotechnical and structural engineers for this
purpose
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between the backfill
material and the trench side walls To compute the vertical loads on the pipes an
average unit weight for backfill material of 120 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are
recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the trench
Therefore the width of the trench should be kept to a minimum
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The stiff fill layer and the stiff to medium dense alluvial deposit encountered at the
proposed invert elevations generally have good bearing capacities
The new sewer line construction in general will not materially increase the vertical
loading upon these underlying soils The net difference in loading between the weights
of the excavated material and the sewer pipe and backfill as constructed will be small
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great effect upon the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important design
consideration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2708 00 Page 7
For pipes bearing on the stiff to medium dense fill or alluvial deposits a minimum
of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material should be placed below the
pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of
one fourth the outside pipe diameter and it also should extend at least half way up the
pipe sides The bedding material should consist of clean granular material such as 3B
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least twelve 12
inches above the top of the pipe The first zone should consist of select free draining
well graded granular material placed by hand or by suitable equipment so that the backfill
material is in contact with the entire periphery of the pipe and in such manner as not to
disturb the pipe Granular material is recommended to provide a relatively high density
backfill with minimum compactive effort The material should be well graded less than
one 1 inch in size and with no more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve
In unpaved areas the excavated on site soils less than ten 10 inches in size can
be re used as the upper zone of the trench backfill from above one foot over the pipe to
the finished grade Backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Below paved areas the backfill within two 2 feet of the finished grade including
the thickness of the pavement section should consist of select granular material less than
three 3 inches in size and with less than 15 passing the No 200 sieve and be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2708 00 Page 8
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Pavements removed or disturbed during trenching operations should be replaced
by materials equivalent to or better than those currently existing with respect to both
quality and thickness
A typical section of the pipe trench is presented on Plate 3
Joints and Connections
Due to the various soil conditions which will be crossed by the pipe flexible pipe
joints are recommended to allow for some amount of differential settlement
Manhole Structures
Structures supported on the stiff to medium dense fill or alluvial deposits may be
designed using an allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f Minimal settlements are
anticipated for these structures
The lateral earth pressure acting on the proposed underground manhole structures
will depend on the type of backfill to be used the extent of backfill and the compactive
effort on the backfill material around the structures Therefore granular material with no
more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve is recommended for use as backfill around the
structures to reduce the compactive effort required and thus the build up of additional
lateral earth pressures We recommend that the new manholes be designed to resist the
following lateral earth pressure provided granular backfill is used
Above water level 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure

The above value assume that the backfill around the manhole structures is properly
placed and compacted Surcharge loading due to traffic or other loadings should be
added to the above values for design
Existing Utility Lines
Since the general project site is in a developed urban area it is anticipated that
many existing utility lines may be encountered during construction of the sewer line
Therefore we recommend that all existing utility lines along the proposed sewer
alignment be located accurately prior to construction and that an adequate program be
devised to protect the existing utility lines during construction
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for
bid This review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during trench excavation and backfill placement to see
that adequate support and compaction have been obtained This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior
to the start of construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from structures indicated on the 8 Sewer Relocation Plan undated prepared by
R M Towill Corporation Elevations of the borings are also approximate having been
estimated by interpolation from elevation information provided on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2708 00 Page 11
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of The Mark A Robinson
Trust and their consultants for specific application to the project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty
expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in
the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information for use in preparation of construction
contract estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a
competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
