Dear Mr Matsushita
In accordance with your request and the project specifications a static load test
was conducted on a sacrificial micropile for the Kaiser Moanalua Ambulatory Clinic
Phase 1B project The project site is located at 2828 Paa Street in the Mapunapuna area
of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report presents the results of our
field observations and the test data collected during the micropile static load test
conducted on for the above project This letter report presents the
results of our field observations and measurements taken during the micropile static load
test program
The static load test was conducted on a sacrificial micropile designated as
Micropile No No LT 1 is located at the eastern corner of the existing
Ceridian Building The micropile was installed by Structural Systems Inc on October
28 2005 utilizing a Beretta T59 track mounted drill rig The micropile extended to a depth
of about 99 5 feet below the existing pavement surface with a 5 5 inch outside diameter
OD steel casing A record of the micropile installation is provided on Plate 1 of this letter
report for reference
Based on our field observations during the micropile installation a basaltic boulder
and cobble layer was encountered at a depth of about 87 feet below the existing pavement
surface Overlying the basaltic boulder and cobble layer it appears that alluvial deposits
consisting of very soft silts and clays were encountered After reaching the micropile tip
installed to the full depth of the drilled and cased hole A sand cement grout was used to
backfill the drilled hole The bottom 15 feet of the micropile was uncased to provide a
bond length within the basaltic boulder and cobble layer As part of the bond length the
permanent steel casing was extended by about 3 feet into the basaltic boulder and cobble
layer
The sacrificial micropile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The design load of the micropile is

The micropile settlement was measured with two dial gauges attached to a single
reference beam The dial gauges were referred to as left and right of the load test
micropile The measuring needle of each dial gauge was set vertically on a
fixed horizontal plate on the top of the load test micropile As the load test micropile
moved downward the dial gauge readings decreased The secondary method to
measure the micropile settlement consisted of a 1 100 inch division ruler set on the
micropile head with a surveyor s level to observe the micropile settlement
In addition two 1 100 inch division rulers were set on each side of the load frame
and a remote location on the existing building wall The remote location was set outside
the immediate test area and area of potential setttement The surveyor s level was used
to observe movements of the above mentioned locations Photographs of the static
load test setup are presented on Plate 2 of this report
In general the load test micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of 240 kips
in 10 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least 5 minutes The
test load was held for 210 minutes 3 5 hours at 90 kips The rate of settlement at the
90 kip test load was less than 0 01 inches per hour at the end of the holding period
The loading was increased to the maximum test load of 240 kips in 10 kip load
increments The top of the reaction frame above the load test micropile was observed
to be rotating vertically toward the silt fence at the maximum test load Additional
loading of the load test micropile caused more rotation and pushed the hydraulic jack off
the load test micropile Therefore the load test was terminated at the maximum test
load of 240 kips The results of the micropile load test are summarized in the following
table The load test readings and pertinent information are presented on the Micropile

Based on the micropile load test results we believe the 5 5 inch OD micropile
having a bond length of about 15 feet into the boulder cobble layer is capable of
supporting an allowable compressive load capacity of 90 kips with an adequate factor of
safety and with reasonable settlement characteristics compatible with the project
design
