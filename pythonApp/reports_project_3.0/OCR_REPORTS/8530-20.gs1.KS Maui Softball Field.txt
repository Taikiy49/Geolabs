Dear Mr. Muraoka: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during earthwork construction for the Kamehameha Schools Maui 
Softball Field project from June 7, 2023 to September 26, 2023 in accordance with our fee 
proposal dated May 4, 2023. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the athletic field within the Kamehameha Schools 
Maui Campus in Makawao on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. We understand that the 
existing grass softball field will be replaced with a new synthetic turf softball field. In 
addition, a new concrete slab for the dugouts, sidewalks, a storage container, and 
backstop area will be constructed. A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on 
Plates 1 and 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the earthwork observed at the project site.

EARTHWORK 
We observed the earthwork for the Kamehameha Schools Maui Softball Field 
project from June 7, 2023 to September 26, 2023.  The grading work consisted of footing 
excavation, drain line backfill, and footing and slabs-on-grade subgrade preparation for the 
project.  Maryl Group, Inc. performed the earthwork. During the grading operations, we 
obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials 
and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with 
ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent of the maximum dry 
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
During the grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.3 of this report. 
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and 
backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as 
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or 
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the grading and utility installation work for the 
above-referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.

LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the slopes or
finished grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period
without our knowledge and written acknowledgment.
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some work may have occurred
without our observation. However, we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project. It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials. Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type.
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to the
plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
