This report presents the results of our soil investigation for
the proposed residencial construction at 1534 Ala Lani Street
Honolulu Hawaii The purpose of our study was to investigate
the general subsurface soil condition at the site and to provide
foundation and site grading recommendations for the construction
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface condition at the site one boring was
drilled utilizing portable rotary wash equipment The location
of the boring is shown on the site plan Plate 1 Our field
engineer was present throughout the field investigation to log
the boring and to observe site conditions The boring log is
presented on Plate 2 All materials have been described according
to the Unified Soil Classification system and local geologic ter
minology

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is a 60 x 180 feet parcel located on the
Ewa upper slope in Moanalua Valley Honolulu The lot slopes
down from the upper fence toward Ala Aolani Street with about
1 horizontal to 1 vertical grade in the longitudinal direction
The sloping terrain is presently covered with grass Large
boulders up to 4 feet in diameter were noted on the existing
talus slope surface At the adjacent makai parcel in place
weathered rock formation was exposed at the upper slope sur
faces above elevation 310 However this type of formation
disappeared when it reaches this parcel At the upper makai
corner of this lot erosion ditch was noted Evidence of earth
movement was also noted at the upper mauka boundary
SOILS
The site is immediately underlain by about 7 feet of talus
deposits consisting of stiff brown clayey silts and weathered
basalts rock matrix The matrix then graded into a weathered
vesicular basalt layer which extended to the 19 feet depth
explored
Water was used in the drilling and it remained in the drill
hole Because of the cave in at the upper layer groundwater
level could not be accurately measured after the drilling casing
was pulled out from the bore hole However during the exploration
no signs of groundwater seepage was observed on the existing slope

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general the project site is located on a talus slope which
has a potential for down slope earth movements New construction
should be designed to avoid imbalance of the existing slope sta
bility Therefore 38 feet high cut as shown on the present plan
should be avoided Cutting of high slopes into the existing boulder
clay talus would initiate eventual sliding down of upper talus deposits
To provide the necessary minimum site grading for the garage construc
tion retaining wall should be utilized to retain earth movements
at the toe of talus formation The residence should be constructed
on top of garage at the front and supported on post beam at the
rear portion Drainage ditch and boulder catching fence should be
provided at the upper back portion of the residential construction
Other retaining wall units on slope should be avoided now or in the
future site improvements
Foundation
In general spread and post foundations should bear into the stiff
clayey silt soils and bottomed approximately four feet below the
existing ground surface A bearing value of 2000 pounds per square
foot may be used for dead plus live loads Pressure may be increased
by one third when considering wind or seismic loads

Individual post footings on existing slope should extend below
the slope surface and into firm clayey silt soils such that the
outer edge of footing should have a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 4 feet out to the slope surface For erosion pro
tection the footing excavation on slope should be backfilled
with concrete to match the upper Slope surface
Garage Retaining Wall
For the garage construction the cut into the Slope and the height
of retaining wall should be no more than seven feet The width of the
cut should be limited to the full length of a 2 car garage only
Based on the one boring information it appears that full depth
of the retaining wall cut will penetrate through talus formation
and the retaining wall footing should be embedded in the underlying
decomposed basalt layer
For the garage retaining wall design a lateral earth pressure
equivalent to that developed by a fluid with a density of 80
pounds per cubic foot may be used For footing bearing on de
composed rock allowable bearing pressure of 4000 psf may be used
The coefficient of friction between concrete and on site material
for resistence to horizontal sliding may be taken at 0 4 During
retaining wall construction the cut slope behind retaining wall
should be made no steeper than one horizontal to one vertical and
backfilled as soon as the wall is constructed The backfill im
mediately against the wall to the Sloped surface should be compacted
open grade granular materials such as black sand cinder or quarry
material

Adequate drainage system should be provided to prevent the
build up of excessive pore pressure behind the retaining wall
Slab On Grade
Slab On Grade should be avoided for the house construction For
slab on grade at the garage it is recommended that a 4 inch layer
of 3 4 inch gravel be placed above the subgrade and compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density
Boulder Catching Fence
Boulder chain link fence should be provided at uphill side of the
building area for protection of any loose boulders which might roll
down in the future
Erosion Control
Erosion control measures such as planting grassing and rip rap
should also be provided to minimize surface erosion due to water
running over exposed slopes
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that all foundation excavations should be
inspected by a soil engineer to verify that no geologic planes
of weakness or groundwater seepage exist below the present slope
and subsurface conditions as anticipated
The recommendation given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
