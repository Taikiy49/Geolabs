SITE CONDITIONS
The subject property is located at the end of Kalohelani
Street in the Queens Gate Subdivision in Hawaii Kai
The lot is relatively flat with a Slight slope down toward
the western Road G and front streets

south and the tennis court to the east

system is located close to the house

such that the water run off from the sprinkler system and
rain drains toward the house foundations

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
a EE RERUN DAL LOND
The observed cracking pattern and damage at the residence
indicates that foundation movement has occurred in portions
of the residence
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests it
appears that most of the foundation movement at the resi
dence is caused by saturation and subsequent heaving of the
underlying clayey soils The saturation of the clayey soils
also softens and weakens the soil and could also result in
settlement of portions of the foundations
These clayey subsoils have a high affinity for water and
therefore generally exhibit a high shrink swell potential
characteristic The total swelling experienced by the
underlying clayey soil depends largely on the initial moisture
content of the soil and the amount of water available for
eventual saturation
Laboratory swell tests were performed on the underlying
clayey soil to simulate conditions of previous dry subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII

soil condition and subsequent saturation of the subgrade
soils by water ponding adjacent to the house foundation
Our laboratory test indicated a swell of over 13 from the
air dried state to the saturated state The poor site
drainage around the house also contributed to the heaving
problems by allowing water to pond next to the foundations
causing the surface water to infiltrate under the interior
portion of the house slab and thereby causing the underlying
expansive clay to swell
One of the main objects of any future remedial work under
taken at the site should be to prevent or minimize water
from entering under the slabs and foundations Also flexi
ble joints and floor coverings can be used to allow for some
future ground movements It is our opinion that the following
steps could be adopted to reduce additional future movements
to the residence
1 Construct a concrete cut off wall around the perimeter
of the slab The purpose of this cut off wall is to
create a barrier to minimize outside water from freely
entering under the shallow thicken edge and migrating

toward the interior slab area It is our opinion
that the cut off wall should extend a minimum of 24
inches below the ground surface and have a minimum
width of 12 inches This cut off wall should be
placed as close to the slab as practicable
About 2 pounds of hydrated lime per lineal foot should
be placed in the bottom of the new cut off wall exca
vation prior to concrete pouring The moist lime
Slurry could help to chemically reduce the clayey
characteristics of the surrounding soils and also
help to create an additional barrier to water infiltra
tion
The horizontal extent or limit of the cut off wall would
depend on the extent of the underlying clayey soil
Additional probings of the presently unaffected areas
arond the house would be required to determine the extent
of this cut off wall
2 Underpin the southeastern corner of the living room
foundation wall Field observations indicate that the
subsoils in this area are very soft and saturated and

Underpinning of this portion of the house foundation is

Underpinning should be done with care and in small sec

Regrade area around the house to drain water far away

The planting area and the sprinkler system existing around

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING DURING REPAIRS
EEN DURING REPATRS
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
remedial construction to monitor the contractor s operations
It is also recommended that the cut off wall excavation and
construction be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that proper installation and grade have been
obtained to drain potential surface and seepage flow away
from the foundation and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface exploration
The lime stabilization operations should also be inspected
by a soils engineer
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
