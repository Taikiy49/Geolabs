In accordance with our proposal dated May 11 2000 we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration at the Service Center project on Malakal Island in the
Republic of Palau This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the
proposed project site and presents our findings and recommendations for the proposed
project These findings and recommendations are intended for design input only and are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Service Center project site is located on Malakal Island in the Republic of Palau
The project site is located on the north side of Ngerungesiil Road as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Based on the information provided the project will involve the construction of a
two story building structure with a finished floor elevation of about 6 5 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL We understand that the Service Center will be used as an industrial type facility The
first floor of the Service Center will be used as a warehouse and storage space and the
second floor will be used as office space The maximum column loads for the proposed
structure is estimated to be on the order of about 30 to 40 kips
We understand that the existing ground surface across the site ranges from about
Elevation 3 to 5 feet MSL Based on the proposed finished floor elevation and the existing
ground surface elevations we envision that fills on the order of about 1 to 3 feet thick will be
needed to raise the existing ground to the proposed finished floor elevation

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on Malakal Island in the Republic of Palau The project site
is located on the north side of Ngerungesiil Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based
on our site reconnaissance the project site is relatively flat with ground surface elevations
ranging from about 3 to 5 feet MSL The project site is bordered on the north side by the
ocean
At the time of our exploration two one story buildings were observed at the northwest
portion of the project site In addition waste stockpiles were noted at the northwest to west
and northeast portions of the project site Vegetation at the project site appeared to be
minimai consisting of scattered grass in localized areas
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 49 to 52 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered during our field exploration we
recommend that a shallow grid foundation system consisting of inter connected grids of
reinforced continuous footings be used to transmit the service center building loads to the
supporting soils Due to the heterogeneous nature of the undocumented fills at the project
site we anticipate that differential settlement may occur for foundations supported directly
on the undocumented fills at the site To reduce the potential for structural distress due to
differential settlements we recommend that the undocumented fills within the footprint of the
proposed building be over excavated where necessary to provide a minimum of 2 feet of
compacted structural fill below the bottom of footing elevations Detailed discussion of these
items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design are presented in the
following sections of this letter report
Building Foundations
In general we recommend that the proposed building be supported on a shallow grid
foundation system consisting of inter connected grids of reinforced continuous footings Due
to the heterogeneous nature of the undocumented fills at the project site we anticipate the
potential for differential settlement to occur if the shallow foundations were to be supported
directly on the undocumented fills To reduce the potential for structural distress due to
differential settlements we recommend that the undocumented fills within the footprint of the
proposed building be over excavated where necessary to provide a minimum of 2 feet of
compacted structural fill below the bottom of footing elevations Therefore the shallow grid

foundation system should be supported on at least 2 feet of compacted structural fill The
structural fill below the building footprint should be prepared and compacted as discussed
in the Site Grading section of this letter report
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of the shallow grid foundation system bearing on the 2 feet of compacted
structural fill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Where foundations are located next to utility ttenches and or easements
they should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or embedded as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fill Resistance
due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the
footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive pressure
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected It should be emphasized that
surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
In anticipation of the heterogeneous nature of the undocumented fill materials some
amount of settlement and differential settlement should be expected under the new fills
planned at the project site Therefore construction of the slabs on grade and other
improvements above the fill should be delayed as long as practicable in order to permit the
ground settlement to occur In order to reduce the potential for distress due to the
anticipated differential movements between the slabs and foundations we recommend that
free floating slabs on grade with no structural connections to the wall and column
foundations be used Joint filler and sealant may be used to fill openings between the edge
of slab and other structural elements In addition we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grades be supported on the 2 foot thick structural fill material recommended below
the footprint of the proposed building In addition the structural fill should extend a minimum
of 3 feet laterally beyond the building footprint

In general concrete slabs on grade should be supported on a 4 inch cushion layer
of open graded gravel such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation placed
above the 2 feet of structural fill This to is to provide for more uniform support of the slab

Exterior concrete sidewalks walkways around the building will likely be required for the

recommended below the building footprint Contraction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the sidewalk walkway with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The subgrade soils beneath the sidewalks walkways should be prepared in accordance with
the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this letter report
Ground Settlement
Due to the relatively thick layer of very loose to medium dense lagoonal deposits
underlying the site placement of the 1 to 3 feet of new fills to raise the site will induce some
ground settlements We estimate that ground settlements on the order of about 1 to 2 inches
would occur ds a result of the 1 to 3 feet of new fills placed at the site To reduce the effects
of the ground settlements on the proposed improvements we recommend that site filling be
initiated as early as possible to allow the existing ground to adjust to the new fill loads prior
to construction of foundations slabs on grade and other improvements in and above the fill
Based on our analyses we believe that a settlement waiting period on the order of
about 2 to 4 weeks after completion of the filling operations should be allocated in the
construction schedule to allow the filled ground to achieve equilibrium We believe that the
construction of foundations and improvements above and in the fills may commence after
about 2 weeks following completion of the fill placement
To confirm some of the assumptions used in our analyses the actual settlement rate
resulting from the new fill placement should be monitored It should be noted that the
estimated settlement period could vary by as much as 50 to 100 percent from the actual
settlement period required for the compressible undocumented fills and lagoonal deposits
at the project site Therefore provisions should be made for potential delays in the project
construction schedule if a longer period is required A proper contingency plan should be
devised to accommodate the potential delay in construction schedule and the possible extra
costs that may be involved

Sheet Pile Bulkhead
Based on the available information a cantilever sheet pile bulkhead is proposed for
the northeast corner of the project site Based on the design finished grades and the existing
grades we envision the sheet pile bulkhead will retain a soil mass on the order of about
10 to 15 feet high The sheet pile bulkheads along the waterfront will serve two purposes
1 to provide lateral support for the undocumented and new fills and 2 to maintain stability
of the soil mass including the lagoonal deposits Design of the sheet pile bulkhead will need
to consider overtuming stability Geotechnical design parameters to evaluate the overturning
stability condition are presented in the following sections The overturning stability will need
to be evaluated by the design engineer
Lateral Earth Pressures

be designed with lateral earth pressures due to the in situ soils which include the new
fill the undocumented fills and the lagoonal deposits Based on these conditions the
recommended lateral earth pressures for design of the bulkheads expressed as an

Site Grading
Based on the proposed finished floor elevation and the existing ground surface
elevations we anticipate that fills on the order of about 1 to 3 feet will be required The site
grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important that
a representative from our office observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether
undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification process and
whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared at
the on set of earthwork Unsuitable materials within the grading limits should be removed
and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing should
be over excavated to expose firm or dense material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of existing structures to be demolished should be completely
removed Existing underground utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with compacted on site fill materials The
fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed
in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed building should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
Depending on the existing ground elevations and the bottom of footing elevations the
existing ground surface may need to be over excavated to provide the recommended 2 feet
of compacted structural fill below the bottom of footings within the building footprint Where
applicable the over excavation should extend a minimum of 3 feet beyond the limits of the
proposed building
If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the over excavated level the soft and or
loose soils should be over excavated by about 24 inches below the over excavated subgrade
level The over excavation should be lined with a non woven filter fabric Mirafi 180N or
equivalent and should be backfilled with clean granular materials such as drain rock

ASTM C 33 No 4 or No 67 gradation The non woven filter fabric should be pulled taut
to remove any slack The clean granular materials should be wrapped all around with the
non woven filter fabric Due to the potential for presence of soft and or loose soils at the
over excavated subgrade level contract documents should include additive and deductive
unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation and backfill quantities
After clearing grubbing and over excavation the exposed subgrades should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The compaction requirement for areas subjected to vehicular traffic such as the parking lot
and driveway should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Fill and backfill materials up to the foundation slab on grade or pavement subgrade
elevations should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in largest
dimension In general we envision that the excavated on site materials may be re used as
a source of general fill or backfill materials provided that deleterious materials organics are
removed and that they are processed to a relatively well graded select granular material with
a maximum size of about 6 inches The excavated clayey soils should not be re used as fill
or backfill materials
It should be noted that the excavated soils may have to be aerated prior to being
re used as fill to achieve the required compaction requirement Structural fill material
required beneath the proposed buildings and sidewalks walkways should consist of crushed
coral volcanic breccia limestone or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the fill
material should contain between 10 to 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a CBR value of 20 or more and a swell value of 1 percent or less when
tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Fills or backfills below the water level including the fills behind the proposed sheet pile
bulkhead should consist of clean granular material such as drain rocks ASTM C 33 No 4

Pavement Design

Detailed information pertaining to the traffic loads was not available at the time this letter
report was prepared For preliminary design purposes we have assumed that the new
pavements at the parking lot will generally be subjected to passenger vehicles and light
pick up trucks However we anticipate that heavier vehicles such as maintenance and
delivery trucks may also be expected at the site

LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and recommendations provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the site plan provide by Morita
Hotel Corporation Inc The elevations of the borings were estimated from the spot
elevations shown on the topographic survey map provided by Morita Hotel Corporation Inc
The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data presented in the text of this report were based on measurements from the
borings at the times shown on the graphic representations These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
groundwater level changes may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and
other factors
