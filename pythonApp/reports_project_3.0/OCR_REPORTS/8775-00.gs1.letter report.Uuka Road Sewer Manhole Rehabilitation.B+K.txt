GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING STUDY 
ʻUʻUKU ROAD SEWER MANHOLE REHABILITATION 
WAIMEA, KAUAʻI, HAWAIʻI 
Dear Mr. Nishimura: 
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering study performed 
for the ʻUʻuku Road Sewer Manhole Rehabilitation project in Waimea on the Island of Kauaʻi, 
Hawaiʻi. The general location and project vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
This report summarizes our findings and presents our preliminary geotechnical 
engineering recommendations resulting from our site visit and desktop study performed for 
the proposed project. It should be noted that the findings and recommendations provided are 
preliminary, and a design-level geotechnical engineering exploration should be performed 
for the final design of the project.  The preliminary findings and recommendations presented 
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATION 
The project site is located along ʻUʻuku Road in Waimea on the Island of Kauaʻi, 
Hawaiʻi.  We understand that the existing Sewer Manhole (SMH) No. 1 has settled, and 
the sewer line/lateral connections to the manhole may have also settled. We understand 
that it is desired to restore/rehabilitate the existing SMH structure and connections. 
We understand that the project has been separated into two phases. Phase 1 will 
consist of the assessment and scoping for the restoration. Phase 2 will consist of design 
and permitting for the restoration. This report is for Phase 1 work of the project. We 
understand that Phase 2 work will be performed under the contract modification.

ESTIMATED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
A review of available in-house subsurface information and geologic maps was 
performed for the project.  The geologic maps indicate that the project site is underlain by 
recent alluvial deposits.  These recent alluvial deposits are generally very soft and very loose. 
Based on our review of available in-house subsurface information, we anticipate that 
the project site may be underlain by a surface fill layer about 4 to 5 feet thick that may consist 
of silty gravel and silty clay.  Below the surface fill layer, very soft and very loose alluvial 
deposits are anticipated to extend to depths greater than 50 feet below the existing ground 
surface.  These alluvial deposits may consist of sand, sandy silt, and silty sand. 
Based on the as-built drawings, the existing ground surface at the SMH is about 
Elevation +6 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). Therefore, we anticipated that shallow groundwater 
conditions would be encountered with estimated groundwater levels at about 4 to 6 feet 
below the existing ground surface or higher. 
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS 
A site visit was performed on October 17, 2023 to observe the existing conditions at 
the SMH location.  The SMH No. 1 is located within the cul-de-sac area of ʻUʻuku Road.  
Numerous cracks were observed on the top of the asphaltic concrete pavement near the 
manhole structure.  In addition, surface water was noted at the pavement surface near the 
manhole structure.  Furthermore, an existing waterway was observed in the cul-de-sac area.  
The top of the water in the waterway was within several feet of the top of the existing roadway 
surface.  Photographs taken during our site visit to the project site are presented on Plate 2.  
PRELIMINARY RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the results of our desktop study and our site visit, the following preliminary 
recommendations are presented. A design-level geotechnical engineering exploration 
should be performed for the final design of the project. 
Sewer Manhole Structure 
Based on the as-built drawings provided, the invert elevation for SMH No. 1 is -0.47 
feet MSL with a top of the manhole elevation of about +6 feet MSL.  Therefore, the manhole 
structure extends below the existing groundwater level.  We anticipate that the bottom of the 
manhole structure is bearing on the soft/loose alluvial deposits.  We understand that the 
manhole structure has settled.  Therefore, the top of the manhole elevation may be lower 
than +6 feet MSL. 
We recommend the bottom of the new manhole structure bear on a bedding layer 
consisting of 6 inches of No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 gradation). To reduce the 
potential for excessive settlement due to the soft/loose bearing soils, the installation of a

Excavation 
In order to install the new manhole structure and sewer lines, we anticipate that 
excavations below the existing ground surface will be required for the project construction. 
Based on the bottom of the manhole structure elevation, we envision that temporary shoring 
of the excavation will be required for the project. 
In general, we believe that interlocking steel sheet piling or other similar methods 
should be used for temporary shoring purposes, especially where dewatering of the 
excavations will be necessary.  The selection of the proper shoring system to be used is the 
responsibility of the contractor. 
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole 
responsibility of the contractor. The contractor’s representative should be required to be 
continuously present on-site during excavation and construction work. They will have the 
best opportunity to promptly observe changing conditions during construction, such as 
unforeseen subsurface soil conditions, unexpectedly high groundwater table, inappropriate 
construction sequence or techniques, etc., which may adversely affect shoring stability. 
Therefore, it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed 
properly and as early as practical, if necessary, and that the adjacent ground should be 
continuously monitored for cracks, dips and/or other indications of movements with 
instruments. 
Dewatering 
Dewatering of excavations will be necessary where the existing groundwater level is 
above the bottom of the proposed excavation. Groundwater is anticipated at depths of about 
4 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface or higher. Based on the groundwater levels 
anticipated, dewatering will be required for the new manhole structure. Therefore, dewatering 
provisions will need to be included in the contract documents for the proposed construction. 
Since the excavation may involve the discharge of groundwater, a National Pollutant 
Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permit may be required for this discharge. The 
contractor should consult their independent consultant for the latest regulations and 
information pertaining to the permit application.

Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the contractor. The selection of

Underground Sewer Lines 
We understand that new underground sewer lines may be constructed for the 
proposed project.  The methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be 
determined by the contractor, subject to practical limits and safety requirements.  The 
excavation should comply with all applicable local, state, and federal safety requirements.  
Trench shoring design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor.  Trench 
shoring and bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements. 
In general, for the support of the underground sewer lines, we recommend that a 
granular bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 
gradation) be used under the pipes. To reduce the potential for excessive settlement due to 
the soft/loose bearing soils, the installation of a stabilization layer is recommended below the 
bedding layer.  The stabilization layer should consist of 24 inches of No. 2 Coarse aggregate 
wrapped with a filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equal. 
The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above pipes should consist of free-draining 
backfills, such as No. 3B Fine gravel, to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from 
compaction of the backfill. It is critical that a free-draining granular material be used to reduce 
the potential for formation of void below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate 
support for the sides of the pipes. The use of on-site soils as backfill immediately around 
utility pipes is not recommended.  
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the 
finished subgrade should consist of the approved on-site granular soils or select granular 
material. The backfill material should be moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content, placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness, and compacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground 
subsidence. The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be 
compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction. Mechanical compaction

Site Grading 
We anticipate that majority of the site grading work for the depressed cul-de-sac area 
will be relatively minor, involving fills up to about 1 to 2 feet in height or less to achieve the 
finished grades. Deeper cuts and fills will be required for the new manhole structure and 
sewer lines.  
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits should be cleared 
and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, deleterious materials, existing structures to be 
demolished, and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly 
off-site to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas 
designated to receive fill and/or future improvements should be over-excavated to expose 
firm natural material, and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well-compacted 
fill. The excavated soft soils should not be reused as fill materials and should be properly 
disposed of off-site. 
In general, the over-excavated subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should 
be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content, and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. For 
roadway areas, the recompaction should be increased to a minimum 95 percent relative 
compaction. 
If soft subgrade conditions are encountered, the soft subgrade should be removed to 
a depth of about 18 inches and replaced with a stabilization layer.  The stabilization layer 
should consist of 18 inches of No. 2 Coarse aggregate wrapped with a filter fabric such as 
Mirafi 180N or equal.  In addition, a layer of Tensar InterAx 850 or equal should be installed 
between the No. 2 Coarse aggregate and the bottom filter fabric. 
Imported materials should consist of non-expansive select granular material, such as 
crushed coral or basalt. The select granular fill should be well-graded from coarse to fine with 
no particles larger than 3 inches in the largest dimension. The material should have a 
California Bearing Ratio (CBR) value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less 
when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883. The material should also contain between 
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No. 200 sieve. Imported fill materials should be 
tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for 
the intended use. 
Where groundwater is encountered (within the excavations), backfill materials should 
consist of free-draining granular materials, such as No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 
gradation), wrapped on all sides with non-woven filter fabric (Mirafi 180N or equal). The 
free-draining granular materials should be used up to a level of about 12 inches above the

In general, fills and backfills should be moisture-conditioned to above the optimum

types of acceptable compaction equipment. Water tamping, jetting, or ponding should not be 
allowed to compact the fills. 
Pavement Design 
We anticipate that the repaving of the cul-de-sac using a flexible pavement may be 
required for the project. In general, we anticipate the vehicle loading for the cul-de-sac will 
primarily consist of passenger vehicles and occasional light trucks. We have assumed the 
pavement subgrade soils will have a CBR value between 8 and 12 with CBR swell value 
between 1 to 3 percent. On this basis, we recommend using the following pavement design 
for the roadway of this project.

LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to observe all the site conditions based on a 
site visit and review of literature only. Variations of subsurface conditions from those 
described in this letter report may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may 
not become evident until further geotechnical engineering exploration is conducted at the site 
or construction is underway. A design-level geotechnical engineering exploration should be 
conducted to confirm and/or modify the preliminary recommendations provided herein. Our 
services on this project were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of 
geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. 
Our geotechnical consultation services provided for this project were not intended to 
investigate the potential presence of hazardous materials existing at the site. It should be 
noted that the personnel used to conduct a geo-environmental exploration differ substantially 
from those applied in geotechnical engineering. 
This letter report has been prepared solely for the purpose of providing preliminary 
recommendations for the project only. Therefore, this report may not contain sufficient data 
or the proper information to serve as the basis for detailed design and preparation of 
construction drawings. A design-level geotechnical exploration should be conducted for a 
detailed design of the project.
