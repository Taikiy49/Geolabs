PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soll conditions at the proposed guard tower
retaining wall and the altered berm at the igloo storage area in Johnston
Island and to provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the design of
the tower foundations retaining wall and berm alteration construction

In particular the settlement and bearing characteristics of the
underlying fill layers were of primary concern
The general project location is shown on the Project Location Plan

The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
and included four borings field reconnaiscance
laboratory soil testing engineering analysis and report preparation
The result of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The guard tower will be a steel framed structure approximately 35
feet high and supported on four reinforced concrete columns The base
dimension will be about 16 feet by 16 feet The structure will have an
outside spiral stairway with a conerete slab and an office on the ground
floor Maximum column loads are estimated at 35 kips
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1629 00 Page 3
The maximum lateral column forces are estimated to be about 5 kips
with maximum overturning moments of 35 foot kips Two potential
locations for the guard tower were investigated
A double security fence system will be installed northwest of Igloos
Nos 746 to 761 In order to provide the required 30 foot clear distance
away from the fence a portion of the earth berm against the existing
igloos will be cut back A new retaining wall approximately 8 feet
high is planned to support the cut back slope
A portion of the existing berm north of Igloo No 761 will be
regraded to provide the required set back distance from the new fence
The locations of the proposed improvements are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated within a storage igloo facility on the
southern perimeter of Johnston Island west of the aircraft parking area
The igloo facility covers an area of approximately 600 by 1050 feet
and is accessible from two existing roads
GEOLABS HAWAII
WO 1629 00 Page 4
The topography is generally level with elevations ranging from 6 6 to
9 6 feet above Main Sea Level The area is presently used for storing
toxic chemical munitions in concrete igloos
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site was originally an atoll which has been enlarged by dredging
and filling operations over the years
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions were explored by drilling one 1 boring at each
of the two proposed guard tower sites to depths up to 40 5 feet below the
existing ground surface and two 2 short borings along the retaining
wall alignment to depths of 10 to 11 5 feet A hand dug probing was
conducted on that portion of the existing berm which will be excavated to
develop a wider level clear area
The soll conditions at the tower site consist of a medium density
upper coral dredged fill crust 3 to 5 feet thick over loose sandy
gravel dredged fill underlain by coral reef formation at variable depths
of 14 to 29 feet
The borings along the retaining wall location indicated a dense
dredged coral fill layer up to 8 foot thick over loose coral sand and
gravel
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1629 00 Page 5
Hand probing in the proposed berm relocation area along the northern
project boundary indicated that berm fill near the surface was relatively
dry and it appeared to be compacted coral dredged material
Groundwater Conditions
Groundwater was encountered in all of the borings drilled at depths
of 6 to 8 5 feet at the time of our field exploration
Because of the close proximity to the ocean the groundwater levels
will be influenced by tidal fluctuations
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by dredged
fill consisting of coral sand and gravel The dredged fill is generally
dense near the surface but grades to loose with depth particularly near
the water level
At the two guard tower sites the dense surface layer was found to be
only about 3 to 5 feet thick Therefore recompaction of the soils below
the footings are recommended to provide a uniform compacted layer below
the footing
At the retaining wall site the dense surface layer was found to be
about 8 feet thick and therefore special foundation preparation is not
required

Guard Tower Foundation
The test boring disclosed that the original proposed site Boring 1
location is underlain by 5 feet of dense dredged fill underlain by loose
coral gravel to a depth of 14 5 feet where a coral ledge is encountered
At the alternative location for the security tower Boring 2
location the test boring disclosed that the dense surface layer was
only 3 feet thick The loose sandy soils were thicker and extended from
3 feet depth to 30 feet where coral was encountered
The thickness of the dense surface layer and depth to coral formation
is expected to vary considerably from point to point in the area
Spread footing foundation may be used to support the tower structure
provided recompaction of the subgrade soils is performed
In order to provide uniform support for the structure foundation we
recommenda that the sandy soils for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing be overexcavated and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM
D 1557 prior to footing placement
Spread footing foundation bearing on the compacted on site sandy
material may be designed with an allowable bearing capacity of 1500
p S f

The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footing located next to utility trenches should
extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on dense dredged
fill or structurally designed to span over the well compacted backfill to
minimize footing settlement

Backfilling
Backfilling should proceed as soon as possible after excavation The
granular on site material may be used for the backfill which should
be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Retaining Wall
A 900 feet long by 7 5 feet high retaining wall is planned to retain
the cut slope into the berm north of the existing igloo structures
To develop its bearing capacity the wall foundations should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the finished grade

An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f can be used on the medium
to dense dredged fill
We recommend that weil graded granular material such as the on site
dredged sand should be used as backfill material behind the wall to the
entire limit of the required wall excavation The backfill should be
moderately compacted to about 85 of ASTM D 1557 to minimize excess
lateral pressure in the wall
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist the following
lateral earth pressures
Active Condition with 2 1 H V sloping backfill

Active Condition with 4 1 H V sloping backfill

of the wall
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Berm Relocation
For the proposed new fence area the existing berm embankment about
45 feet beyond the northeast corner of the proposed retaining wall area
will be cut about 30 feet by 180 feet We recommend that a cut slope of
2 1 H V may be used with a 6 foot wide slope bench provided at the
mid point of the slope
Near the finished grade the slope should be cut with extreme care by
the contractor We believe a backhoe should be used to provide the final
finishing of the cut slope surface to minimize over cutting by a dozer
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away and
prevented fron flowing over the slope face
Utilities
Utilities and other underground structures or obstructions are
anticipated The locations of underground utility lines should be
verified It may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust
structural details to accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated
condition or buried structure be encountered during construction each
condition should be evaluated and the design modified if necessary
