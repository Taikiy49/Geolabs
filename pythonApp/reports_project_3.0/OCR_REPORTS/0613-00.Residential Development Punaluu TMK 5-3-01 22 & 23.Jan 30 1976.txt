This report presents the results of our geologic reconnaissance
and test pits at the subject development site located makai of
Kamehameha Highway in Punaluu Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is presently planned to construct a total of 8 single family
residential units on the ft parcel The project is
in preliminary design stage and minimum cuts and fills are anti
cipated The present development scheme is to cluster the proposed
houses around the central landscaped open space A perimeter road
way will be constructed outside of the cluster development and along
the three sides of the parcel

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geology of the area consists of a coastal plain approximately
half a mile mauka It extends upward to an elevation of 20 feet
then it is tumcated by the Koolau Basalt formation near Kahana
Bay
The subsurface materials in this plain are mostly thin alluvium
and deep deposits of calcareous sand The sand deposits can
extend down to a depth of about elevation 50 feet in this area
Beyond that depth basalt and coral may be encountered
Surface
The proposed development site consists of 2 beach front parcels
located in Punaluu Oahu It can be reached by Kamehameha Highway
The combined site is 205 feet wide by 240 feet deep totalling
70 000 square feet
Presently each single family dwelling occupies one half of the
site and separated by a panax hedge in the middle The area is
generally level and its yards are well kept
A 6 foot high rubble wall fronts the ocean and steps from top
of wall leads down to the beach

A 20 foot wide State right of way to the beach runs along
the south boundary
Numerous coconut rubber and bread fruit trees are located
along front and at rear areas of the two residences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our test pits and site reconnaissance
the site can be developed for the proposed residential develop
ment
Proposed residential structures will have to be constructed
behind the new shoreline setback The on site soil is fine
beach sand and it is not expansive
Cesspool
It is anticipated a minimum of 20 feet of the subsurface soil
consists of well drain beach sand formation and cesspool is
feasible at this parcel
The on site beach sand generally is quite pervious and the new
cesspool construction dimensions would be of minimum requirements
To obtain the necessary permit from State Health Department per
colation field testing at a later date will be required to obtain

more accurate drainage characteristics and to verify the
minimum depth and size of the future cesspool construction
Foundations
It is our opinion that the on site sand can support the light
loads from the proposed residential structures
The type of foundations will depend on the building design and
foundation loads Based on present test pit and reconnaissance
information we anticipate the use of slab on grade and or shallow
footings are feasible The site soils are generally non expansive
and well drained therefore footing and slab on grade can be
designed without considering expansive forces Since this is a
beach front development foundations should extend below the
finish ground sufficient for wave and erosion protection
FUTURE STUDIES
To provide percolation testings required for cesspool construction
permits two test borings of about 20 foot depth each would be re
quired The borings will be located at the building set back line
toward the beach and at the back near Kamehameha Highway Addi
tional informations such as water level permeability and cave in
characteristic of the subsurface soils for cesspool design and
construction would also be obtained during this future boring
exploration
