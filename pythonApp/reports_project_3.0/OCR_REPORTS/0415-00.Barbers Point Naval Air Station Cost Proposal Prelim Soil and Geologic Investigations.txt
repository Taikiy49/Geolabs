GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
We have visited the site and have reviewed previous boring logs
of the area and previous reports performed by this office in
the Barbers Point Pearl Harbor area It is expected that the
major soil type that will be encountered on the site will be
calcareous algae coral reef containing primary calcareous sand
and gravel filled cavities and secondarily formed solution
cavities pukas Also due to the complex eustatic sea level
changes and geologic history of the Pearl Harbor Embayment Ewa
Coral Plain it is expected that the reef and reef debris will
be intercalated with bay muds and deltaic land derived clays
silts sands and possibly gravels However calcareous reef
materials are expected to dominate the subsurface lithology
The solution cavities pukas are formed by the action of
rainwater mixing with rotting vegetation to produce weak acids
which corrode the subaerially exposed reef material forming
Karst topography These pukas are most abundant above the
water table approximately sea level in this area
