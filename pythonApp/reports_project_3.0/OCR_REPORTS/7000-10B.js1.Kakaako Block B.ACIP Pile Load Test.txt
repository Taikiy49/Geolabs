Dear Mr. Char: 
In accordance with the project design requirements, a static compressive pile load 
test was conducted on an augered cast-in-place (ACIP) Test Pile No. 57 (located within 
Parcel B-1) on March 2 and 3, 2015.  This letter report presents the results of our 
engineering analysis, field observations, test data, conclusions, and our recommendations. 
In general, the Kakaako Block B project involves construction of two new multi-story 
buildings at 400 and 440 Keawe Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The 
project site is bounded by Auahi Street to the south, Keawe Street to the east, 
Pohukaina Street to the north, and Waterfront Towers to the west.  The project location 
and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
We provided geotechnical engineering exploration services previously and 
recommended using ACIP foundation to support the new building structures. Our 
recommendations were presented in the report entitled, “Geotechnical Engineering 
Exploration, Kakaako Block B, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii, dated June 13, 2014.  
The pile load test was conducted on an 18-inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of 
approximately 60.7 feet below to the existing ground surface.  The ACIP pile was installed 
by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company (HDCC) using a crane-mounted drill rig 
equipped with automatic monitoring equipment providing the drilling information, such as 
drilling depth, drilling rate, auger torque, stroke count, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
HDCC used continuous flight augers to drill the pile.  As the augers were removed, the 
hole was grouted from the bottom upwards.  After sound quality grout (based on visual 
observation) appeared at the top of the pile, a reinforcing cage was inserted into the 
grouted hole.  A 27-inch steel casing was then set around the top of the rebar cage 
providing a stable platform for the load testing apparatus, including hydraulic jack, load 
cell, spherical bearing and steel spacer plates.  The approximate location of the load test

To provide support for the load test, two 18-inch ACIP reaction piles, approximately 
60.0 and 60.5 feet long, were installed in similar fashion except using 3-inch diameter 
center bars in lieu of rebar cages.  The reaction piles and load test pile were completed on

Test Pile Location Plan, Plate 2. 
To better understand and predict the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil 
interface, the load test pile was instrumented with five vibrating wire embedment strain 
gages (Model No. 4200A-2) manufactured by Geokon, Inc.  The strain gages were 
connected using a multiplexer to a Geokon Micro-10 data acquisition system to record the 
strains during the compressive load test.  The embedment strain gages were installed in 
the load test pile at the following depths below the existing ground surface.

The ACIP piling subcontractor, HDCC, provided load testing equipment, including a 
reaction beam, hydraulic jack, hydraulic pump, reference beams, and other accessories. 
Geolabs, Inc., provided the load cell, digital dial gauges, survey level equipment, and a 
spherical bearing. The testing loads were measured by the load cell.  
Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a multiplexer, data-logger, 
and data acquisition program to collect the strain gage readings during the load testing. 
The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses and evaluation of the static 
compressive load transfer. Displacements at the top of the load test pile were measured 
and recorded by the digital dial gauges. 
The pile load test was conducted in general accordance with the Quick Load Test 
Method of ASTM D1143 and modified by our office to suit the design intents.  In general, 
the test pile was loaded to about 675 kips in approximately 37.5-kip load increments. Each 
load increment was held for at least 15 minutes or until the rate of the pile head movement 
generally was stable.  In addition, the static load test was held for approximately 2 hours at 
the 300-kip, 450-kip, and 600-kip load increments to evaluate the effects of creep. After 
reaching the maximum test load of 675 kips, the load test pile was unloaded in

confirms the ACIP pile design parameters.
