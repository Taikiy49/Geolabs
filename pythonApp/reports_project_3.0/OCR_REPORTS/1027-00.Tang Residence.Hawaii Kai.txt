As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced property to evaluate the shallow subsur
face soil conditions and possible causes of the observed
foundation movement
It is our understanding that the subject house was constructed
several years ago Movement of the floor slab in the rear
portion of the house has occured Subsequently a concrete
patio slab was constructed extending from the rear of the
house to near the rear property line
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject property is located on the mauka side of Kahului
Street on the western side of Kalama Valley in Hawaii Kai
The lot slopes up toward the mauka direction There is an
elevation difference of approximately 10 feet from the side
walk level up to the backyard
The existing residence is a tri level structure consisting of
a garage at street level with a second and third floor level
above the garage
The garage level has CMU basement walls The portion of the
house directly over the garage has a wood floor system while
the remainder of the house has a concrete slab on grade
CRM retaining walls are located along the front and both sides
of the house to create terrace levels

FIELD EXPLORATION
Since the residence utilities and landscaping were already
constructed at the site access for drilling equipment was
very limited Therefore hand operated auger holes were
utilized for the soil investigation Four holes were drilled
adjacent to the rear exterior wall of the structure
Disturbed samples were obtained from the auger cuttings and
taken back to our laboratory for testing The hand auger
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
SOIL CONDITIONS
The hand auger holes encountered dark brown clay to about
3 5 to 4 0 depths the depths drilled This clay has high
shrink swell potential as indicated by the results of labor
atory one inch ring swell test performed on a remolded sample
of the clay encountered under the patio slab

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The cracking pattern noted at the residence indicates that
there has been differential movement between the heavier
load bearing walls and the lighter partition walls and slabs
Assuming that the soil conditions below the Slab in the
interior of the house are similar to those encountered below
the patio slab the field exploration and laboratory tests
indicate that the foundation movement at the residence is
caused by heaving of the underlying clay soils These clay
soils typically have high expansion potential particularly
when it has low in place water contents The relatively poor
site drainage around the house also contributed to the heav
ing problems by allowing water to pond next to the foundations
causing the surface water to infiltrate under the interior
portion of the house slab and thereby causing the underlying
expansive clay to swell
Laboratory swell tests on remolded samples of the Clay soil
encountered below the patio slab indicated that the clay still
has a potential for further swelling if more water is allowed
to penetrate the soil
The main object of any future remedial work undertaken at the
Site should be to prevent or minimize water from entering under
the slabs and foundations Also flexible joints and floor
coverings can be used to allow for some future ground move
ments It is our opinion that the following steps could be
adopted to reduce additional future movement to the residence
1 The ground surface around the house should
be graded to drain water far away from the
foundations and to prevent water ponding
2 Roof gutters should be utilized to catch
runoff The downspouts for the gutter
system should be designed to carry the
water far away from the foundations
3 A 3 feet deep concrete cutoff wall could
be utilized around the perimeter of the
house and patio slab The purpose of cut
off wall is to create a barrier to pre
vent or minimize outside water from enter
ing under the interior slab area The cut
off wall should have a minimum width of
12 inches
