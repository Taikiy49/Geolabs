In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration at the above referenced site Our work was
performed in general accordance with our proposal of
The scope of our evaluation included the drilling of seven 7
borings geologic observations of the existing site conditions
laboratory testing engineering analysis and report preparation
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The purpose of the exploration was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to
evaluate the borrow material quality and to aid in the grading design of
the proposed borrow site

I D 2083 Page 2
The main objectives of this evaluation were
1 To evaluate the suitability of the borrow soils for use in the
planned site grading for the proposed new golf course
2 To evaluate potential excavation difficulties and explore the
depths to groundwater at the proposed borrow site
3 To explore the occurrence of basaltic bedrock at the proposed
borrow site
4 To recommend side slope ratios for cut slope design
A preliminary grading plan for the golf course construction was not
available at the time of this study
SITE DESCRIPTION
The borrow site is located about one half mile mauka or south of
Kamehameha Highway and is immediately south of Borrow Site B which was
previously studied The Turtle Bay Resort Hotel is located about 1 5
miles northwest of the site
Access to the site is from Kamehameha Highway along the paved Gate
Cc access road then along a dirt road
The site is fairly steep with slope gradients that vary from 5 to
locally 20 or more Ground elevations vary from about 180 to 300 feet
above MSL
At the time of our exploration the site was mostly occupied by
abandoned sugar cane fields A trail was cleared by a trackloader
through the sugar cane and bushes to some of the boring locations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower gentle
slope of the Koolau Volcano Weathering has altered the basalt rocks to
an iron rich mass of reddish brown colored soils Several drainageways
have cut gullies with fairly steep side slopes into the residual soils
The site soils consist of stiff silts and clays with varying amounts of
sand gravel and boulders
Soil Conditions
In general the natural soils at the site consist primarily of stiff
to hard low to moderately expansive clayey silt and clays with some
boulders

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the on site soils generally consist of
approximately 23 to 84 feet of very stiff to hard clay and clayey silt
residual soils and extremely weathered basalt and some boulders It
appears that the soils at the site may be suitable for use in the mass
grading of the proposed golf course grading and for use in the lower
portion of the structural fill areas for the Turtle Bay Resort project
Due to the low to moderately high swelling potential the borrow
soils should be kept a minimum of 3 feet below structures
It is anticipated that the excavated clayey soils from the site can
be sandwiched between sandy borrow soils from the Turtle Bay Resort area
to provide better drainage
Basaltic rock may occur at shallow 23 feet depth at the
northwestern portion of the site The basalt will be difficult to
excavate

The stripped surface soil containing organic matter may be reused as
topsoil in the planting areas but should not be used as fill
Entrapped deleterious materials such as mats of grasses or piles of
wood sugar cane etc under the fill should be avoided
Boulders were encountered in some of the borings and a significant

The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials
Slope Construction

Based on our findings it appears that the required cut slope at the
borrow site would be made largely in soil Basaltic rock may occur
at shallow 23 feet depth at the northwestern portion of the site
and some rock may be encountered in cuts deeper than about 50 feet
in the remaining areas of the site A cut slope ratio of 2 1 H V
is recommended for slope cuts in soil Where firm weathered to
fresh rock is encountered slope cut ratios up to 1 1 H V may be
used
Easy excavation and conventional site grading procedures are
anticipated for earthwork in the on site residual soil and extremely
weathered basalt
The moderately to fresh basalt is generally hard and would require
either hard ripping or blasting to excavate The excavated rock may
produce many boulders which would have to be placed in the deeper
fill areas

Golf Course Site Grading
Special grading design is contemplated for the golf course fills due
to landscaping concerns Sewage effluent water will be used for
landscape watering and therefore there is some concern for adequate
drainage in the surface soils which could cause ponding problems and
hinder the growth of the vegetation In order to provide good drainage
in the near surface soils the soils should be moderately compacted to
allow proper drainage of the landscape water Since the soils in the
golf course will not be required to support structural load it is our
opinion that a lower compaction effort may be used
Preliminary golf course grading recommendations are as follows

Site Grading for Remaining Development Area
Plans for the remaining development areas are not available at this
time Preliminary site grading recommendations are presented below for
use as a guide Specific recommendations for the individual development
areas should be provided on an individual basis after additional study
Ground Preparation
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and existing
structures
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
Following the clearing and grubbing all areas designated to receive
fill should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 The moisture content of the soil should be above the
optimum moisture content
Fill Material and Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site materials are suitable for use in compacted
fill and backfill The laboratory swell tests indicated that most
of the on site soils could have low to moderately high swelling
potential depending on the initial compaction density and molding
moisture content prior to saturation At or below the optimum
moisture content the soil exhibits moderate or slightly higher
swell potential However it was noted at a molding moisture
content slightly above the optimum moisture content the wet side of
the optimum moisture the swell potential is substantially
reduced Therefore in order to reduce the swelling potential of
GEOLABS HAWAII

I D 2083 Page 8
the soil fill materials should be placed at moisture contents
slightly above the optimum moisture content Fills placed within
the top 3 feet of finished grade should be carefully checked to
verify that they have low swelling potential
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in the fill
provided they are not allowed to nest together and the voids between
boulders are filled
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are recommended
for the borrow soils
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design
fill slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Slope benches are generally recommended for slope height in excess
of 20 feet
