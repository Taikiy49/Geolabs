Project Considerations
The project involves repair and or reconstruction work for the existing seawall
along Moanakai Road in the Kapaa area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
existing seawall is approximately 1 050 feet along Moanakai Road between its
intersection with Keaka Road and Panihi Road The existing seawall was constructed
as a result of the damage from Hurricane Iniki in 1992 Damages to the existing
structure and surrounding areas due to erosion are present along portions of the
seawall Therefore an engineering study was desired to evaluate the existing
conditions and to formulate potential remediation measures
Existing Site Conditions

the Kapaa area of the Island of Kauai The project site is shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The existing seawall is about 1 050 feet in length and generally
ranges from about 7 to 9 feet in height

ranges from about 9 to 11 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW Elevations along

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
The anticipated subsurface conditions were evaluated based on literature research
and our experience in the project vicinity Based on the geologic setting we anticipate
that the project site is generally underlain by fills and beach deposits over interbedded
layers of coral formation and coralline detritus Near surface materials behind the existing
seawall will likely consist of beach sands and fills placed for the construction of the
seawall and roadway Based on the presence of shallow coral ledge off of the sandy
beach in front of the seawall we believe that medium hard to hard coral formation should
be anticipated near sea level In addition based on the as built drawings the existing
seawall was likely placed on the coral formation In general we anticipate that coralline
detritus consisting of medium dense to dense silty sands and gravel are interbedded with
coral formation at greater depths
Based on the proximity of the site to the Pacific Ocean groundwater levels are
anticipated near sea level We envision that groundwater may be encountered from
elevations ranging from about 2 to 4 feet MLLW It should be noted that groundwater
levels are subject to change due to tidal fluctuations time of year seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors
Because a field exploration program consisting of drilling and sampling borings
was not conducted for the project we recommend retaining Geolabs during construction
to evaluate the actual subsurface materials encountered

Repair Reconstruction of Existing Seawall
Based on the existing conditions our recommendations for repair and
maintenance for the southern and northern portions of the seawall are provided in the
following subsections
Southern Portion to
Based on our observations the existing seawall in this area generally appears to
be in a functional condition Because of the relatively good conditions of the existing
seawall we understand that only minor repairs and routine maintenance will be
conducted to keep the seawall in a functional condition
We envision that maintenance of the existing seawall at the southern portion may
consist of removal of dead tree stumps and backfilling of eroded areas Prior to
backfilling bottoms of eroded areas should be exposed and cleared of debris and other
deleterious materials In general backfill should consist of select granular fills
moisture conditioned and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction in areas subjected to vehicular traffic Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density In order
to reduce the potential for future migration and erosion of the backfill materials a filter
fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent should be provided on bottoms and sides of the select
granular fill
We understand that voids that extend inshore from the seawall are present in
some areas In areas where placement of compacted fill is not practicable a
consideration may be given to grouting the void with Controlled Low Strength Material
CLSM with a slump of about 6 to 9 inches The CLSM should be injected at low to
moderate pressures As an alternate to CLSM sand cement grout with a slump of about
6 to 9 inches may also be used for the grouting operations
It should be noted that the existing seawall consisting of dry stacked rocks
contains large void spaces Therefore potential for the voids to take in excessive CLSM
or sand cement grout should be anticipated We recommend obtaining unit prices for
additional grouting during bidding The contractor should also be made aware that the
grouting operations may need to be conducted in several phases to allow the CLSM or
sand cement grout to set prior to injection of additional CLSM or sand cement grout

Site Grading
Based on the existing conditions at the site we envision that site grading for the
project may consist of excavation and backfilling of up to about 6 to 8 feet in height
Geolabs should monitor the site grading operations to observe whether undesirable
materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification process and to confirm
whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those anticipated
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Soft and yielding
areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill
Areas receiving fill should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in areas subjected to vehicular
traffic Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

Fill Materials
Fill and backfills required except armor and underlayer stones for the new
revetment should consist of select granular fill materials Select granular fill should
consist of non expansive crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and
a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 It
should be noted that the fill requirements presented herein are intended as guidelines only
and may be modified based on additional laboratory testing and field observations on the
available fill materials conducted by Geolabs
Aggregate base course materials required for the flexible pavement section should
consist of crushed basaltic aggregates and should conform to Section 31 of the County of
Kauai Standard Specifications should test the imported fill
materials for conformance with these recommendations

Excavations
We envision that the project site is generally underlain by beach sands coral
formation and coralline detritus The reconstruction of the seawall may require excavation
to an elevation of about 4 feet MLLW Therefore encountering of groundwater in the
excavations should be anticipated In addition near surface materials generally consist of
beach deposits which is susceptible to surface raveling due to the cohesionless nature of
the materials
It is anticipated that the beach sands and soft coral formation near the ground
surface may be readily excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as
excavators However some excavations in the hard coral formation may require the use
of hoerams or chipping

Pavement Design
We envision that reconstruction of portions of the existing Moanakai Road will be
required due to the proximity of the repair and or reconstruction work of the adjacent
seawall The existing Moanakai Road is an undivided two lane roadway consisting of
flexible pavement The vehicular traffic is generally considered light consisting primarily
of passenger vehicles with some light trucks accessing the beach and residences
Therefore we have assumed generally light to medium traffic loading conditions for
pavement design purposes
We envision that the pavement subgrades will generally consist of medium dense
to dense beach sands On this basis we recommend using the following pavement
design for reconstruction of Moanakai Road where required
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to no less
than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should also be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
A Geolabs representative should observe and evaluate the condition of the
pavement subgrades prior to placement of base course CBR tests and or field
observations should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during construction to
confirm the above design section is adequate Paved areas should be sloped and
drainage gradients should be maintained to carry the surface water off site Surface water
ponding should not be allowed on site during or after construction
