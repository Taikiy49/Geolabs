As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The investigation consisted of excavating
three 3 test pits within the building location performing
laboratory soil tests and reviewing the grading and compaction test
record on the building site This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a one story wood
structure approximately 36 by 40 feet in plan dimension Based on the
preliminary plans provided to us the garage will be set back about 20
feet from Kahena Street

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 26 is located on Kahena Street in the northeastern portion of the
subdivision development about 200 feet west of Pukoo Street It is
bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest subdivision lots on the
southeast and northwest and a concrete drainage channel on the
northeast The lot slopes downward from Kahena Street towards the
east at about 7 5 percent gradient The elevation of Lot 26 generally
varies from 241 to 262 feet The existing rectangular concrete
drainage channel is about 10 feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending across the
rear portion of the lot
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent subdivision site grading operation Lot 26 was
covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from
previous grading operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 3 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil con
sisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been
mixed with some adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicate that Lot 26 is underlain by a mixture of
moderately to highly expansive brown clay and gravel
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the test pits are
presented on the attached logs Plates A 1 through A 3 The
approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the attached Site
Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
soil can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System One inch ring swell tests indicate that the
soil has a moderate to high shrink swell potential The test results
are summarized on Plate B 1l
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown clay and gravel fill The laboratory test results on this
material generally indicated that they have moderate to high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 26 the slab on grade and foundation
construction would require special design procedures in order to
minimize the detrimental shrink swell effect of the subsoils

House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions
This would generally result in lower foundation costs and the
individual footings could be readily adjusted should the need occur
Where utilized concrete slab on grade should also be properly
designed for expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the above compacted non expansive
backfill soil
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the above compacted select backfill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls up to 5 feet high are planned in the middle portion of
the house
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on
the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated down
for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of the footing and then
replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste or
coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
The over excavation and replacement should extend a minimum distance
of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of the above
over excavation and replacement would be 3 0 feet below the outside
finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
Off site non expansive soil such as 3B Fine or crusher waste
should be used as backfill behind the wall
The wall should be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of 35
p c f equivalent fluid pressure

A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the footing and
compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral resistance of the
wall
A subsurface drainage system should be incorporated behind the wall to
prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and the rear portion
of the house It appears that the garage area would require fill up
to two feet in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage
Select non expansive borrow soils should be used for fills The
on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the expansive
clays should be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such
as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method This
over excavation and replacement should also be extended for a
horizontal distance of 1 foot beyond the perimeter of the slab
W Page 7
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slab The membrane should be protected by a thin layer of
sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
area Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the
Clay over excavation and replacement can be reduced to a minimum of 2
feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill
is planned select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap
over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select
hon expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the edge of the
driveway
Joints are recommended for the driveway at about 8 to 10 foot intervals
in order to allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay with over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 7 5
gradient
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and con
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
