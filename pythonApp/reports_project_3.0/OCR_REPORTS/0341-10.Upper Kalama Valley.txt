LOCATION AND SITE DESCRIPTION
The property as shown on Plate 1 covers approximately 40 acres
of sloping ground in the upper end of Kalama Valley Bhukai Street
a dirt road provides site access from Kalanianaole Highway near
Sandy Beach Park
The site covers the lower slopes of the head of Kalama Valley
Slopes average 4H to 1V on the sides of the valley and 3H to 1V

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The attached site plan Plate 1 indicates that approximately
22 acres will be developed The planned development will consist
of approximately 200 units of townhouses and garden units The
structures will be one and two stories and loadings are expected
to be relatively light These structures will surround a series
of small man made ponds in the central and lower portion of the
valley to create open space for scenic purposes
Cut and fill grading is projected for road and building site
preparation

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A General
The exploration program disclosed somewhat varying conditions
across the proposed site From an engineering and geologic
standpoint the proposed development is feasible However
construction including grading foundation and utility
installations will be difficult time consuming and expensive
due to the numerous large boulders present throughout the
property

Grading
In general site grading will be very difficult due to the
large boulders present on the site The degree of difficulty
for grading operations will increase toward the head of the
valley Due to the construction difficulties and the geologic
conditions observed we recommend that grading in particular
cuts be kept to a minimum The upper portion of the valley
which consists of boulders without a clay matrix should not
have cut slopes Fill material for the upper sections of the
site will be necessary to fill the void space between the
boulders Imported granular material will be required because
the on site clay will not penetrate into the voids
Utilities
Excavation for underground utilities will be very difficult
and costly due to the large boulders
Bedding and backfill for utility lines will have to be imported
granular material
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings can be utilized on the site

Slope Stability
In general slope stability will not be a problem on this
site
1 Ridges
The ridges forming the valley walls are composed of lava
flows and tuffs within the property These slopes rise
about 2H 1V and are considered to be stable
4 Talus and Alluvium
This material makes up the major portion but not all
of the valley fill on the lower slopes It consists of
variable proportion of cobbles and boulders in a clay
binder This material may be subject to creep where the
clay fraction is sufficient to prevent point to point

Cohesionless Talus Debris
Numerous loose cobbles and boulders from the valley walls
are channelized by the topography into boulder trains
and rock piles
Downslope movement of the boulders is aided by gravity
and rainwater runoff There is a rough downslope increase
in block size to the base of the talus This material
could present slope stability problems if cut steeper
than the natural slopes
Rockfall
There is a danger of individual large boulders rolling
down onto the property from above Some means of safe
guarding the houses along the project boundary should
be provided As a natural safeguard the trees along
the project boundary should not be removed Also a
barrier i e chain link fencing similar to that used
on freeway medians should be considered

F Proposed Man made Ponds
The proposed man made ponds will be relatively shallow
These ponds are planned in the central valley bed area to
collect drainage from the project boundaries
The proposed ponds should be lined either by impermeable
clays or a combination of vinyl lining to prevent percola
tion and loss of water In addition outlets should be
provided We shall provide additional recommendations when
layout of the development is finalized
CONSTRUCTION CONTROL
We recommend continuous inspection of the grading operation under
the supervision of a soils engineer
All footing and slab areas must be inspected by a soils engineer
prior to placing forms reinforcing steel or concrete The exca
vations should be level and free of slough at the time of inspection
