2 1 Regional Geclogy
The Island of Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older Tertiary Epoch
West Maui and the more recent Pieistocene Epoch East Maui also known as
Haleakala Pleistocene Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sioping plain
located between these two volcanoes The Isthmus of Maui was created by lava flows
from Haleakala Volcano ponding against West Maui Volcano The gently sloping plains
were subsequently filled with alluvium washed down from the slopes of West Maui and
East Maui Haleakala Volcanoes The erosional processes are dominated by the
detachment of soil and rock masses from the mountain walis and the soil materials are
transported downslope toward the isthmus primarily by gravity as colluvium Once
these materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley ailuvial processes
become dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
The project site is located on the western flank of the West Maui Volcano which
farms ihe west side of the Isiand of Maui The West Maui Mountains have been deeply
incised by erosion depositing large quantities of unconsolidated recent sediments along
the coastal regicns At the project site the unconsolidated recent sediments are likely
absent therefore it appears that the surface fills for the resort development may be
placed directly over the underlying basalt rock formation or weathered components of
the basalt rock formation which constitute the mass of the West Maui Mountains The
basalt formations at the project site may be part of the Wailuku Voicanic Series which
consist of a thick series of thinly bedded a a and pahoehoe lava fiows dipping seaward

Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout ithe worid are caused solety by shitts
in the tectonic plates in contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before
or during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the
underground movement of magrna that comes close to the surface but does not
erupt The Island of Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but
most of the earthquakes are so smal that only sensitive instruments could detect
the smail tremors However some of the earthquakes are strong enough to be felt
and a few cause minor to moderate damage
in generai earthquakes associated with voicanic activity are most common on ihe
island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kiavea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on
the island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawai over
90 percent of earinquakes are related to volcanic activity ihe risk of seismic
activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes with increased distance from the
Island of Hawaii The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the
Island of Hawaii
it shoud be noted that some earthquakes in Hawaii also are caused by the burden
of the isiand masses overlying the lthosphere which cause the lithosphere to sag
beneath the immense weight The isostatic adjustment of the Pacific lithosphere
beneath the islands burden causes some moderate to high intensity earthquakes
The last earthquake to occur as a result of the lithosphere adjustment was the
1973 Honomu Earthquake Magnitude 2 beneath the Hamakua Coast of
the Island of Hawaii This earthquake was widely felt throughout the State

determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification

Ten one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples retrieved to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests
conducted are summarized on Plate B 1
Six Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils fo evaluate the liquid and plastic iimits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sampie
depths Graphic presentations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
Five Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on
Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils fo evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples were remoided io near the optimum moisture content ana
saturated Resulis af the tests performed are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 6 and B 7
