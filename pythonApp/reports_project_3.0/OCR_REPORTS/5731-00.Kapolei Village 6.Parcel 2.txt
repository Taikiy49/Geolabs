2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late
Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island As volcanic activity in the
Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded
eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The project
site is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site
and to the south these sediments have accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations forming a sedimentary wedge The thickness of
the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the Village 6 Parcel 2 project site is generally
underlain by stiff to very hard silty clay and clayey silt extending to the maximum depth
explored of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface We encountered weathered
sandstone mudrock and coral formation within the silty clay and clayey silt soils in some
of the borings Our laboratory tests indicated that the near surface clayey soils exhibit
moderate shrink swell characteristics when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture
contents Therefore special attention should be given to the site preparation
recommendations for the site grading and foundation design to account for the moderately
expansive soil conditions
Based on the generally stiff subsurface soil conditions encountered we believe that
a shallow foundation system slabs on grade with thickened edge footings and spread
footings may be used to support the proposed building structures Due to the moderately
expansive soil conditions we recommend capping the expansive clayey soils with a
minimum of 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill material below the building
slab areas We also recommend providing 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill
material below the walkway and lanai slabs In addition special attention should be given
to the recommended procedures for subgrade soil preparation below slabs and
pavements to reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the near surface clayey soils
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections herein

3 2 Slabs On Grade
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that slabs on grade
with thickened edge footings may be used to support the new one to two story residential
structures Our exploration indicates that the site is generally underlain by moderately
expansive clayey soils at or near the existing ground surface Unless slab on grade
foundations supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential
for future distress to the residential structures resulting from wetting and subsequent
swelling of the expansive clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend providing non expansive select granular fill
material capping fill for a minimum depth of 18 inches below the building slab cushion
The capping fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the house slab a minimum of 2
feet The perimeter thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The embedment of the interior thickened
edge footings may be reduced to a minimum depth of 12 inches below the adjacent
finished grade The base of the thickened edge should have a minimum width of 12
inches
The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom
longitudinal steel reinforcement In addition the concrete slab should be reinforced with
reinforcing steel bars The project structural engineer should be consulted for design

3 6 Utilities
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend using granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation under the
pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for pipe
damage from compaction of the backfill It is critical to use a free draining granular
material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and
to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes We do not recommend the use of
on site soils as backfill directly around utility pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to
the finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty clay soils or select
granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to no less than
95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to
compact the materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not
be used to compact the backfill material

advised to install gutter systems around the residential structures and to divert the
