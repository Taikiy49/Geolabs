Dear Mr. Creps: 
This letter report presents the results of the field percolation testing performed in 
support of the design of an individual wastewater system (IWS) for the Lower Quarry area 
at the Grace Pacific Makakilo Quarry in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The findings presented herein are intended to assist in the design of the septic 
disposal system only. The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at 
the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves the reconstruction of a small IWS at the Lower Quarry.  It is 
understood that this system will serve less than five persons per day.  As part of the 
renovation, it is proposed to install a new leachfield for the disposal of wastewater effluent 
from the septic tank. 
To evaluate the subsurface percolation characteristics, we drilled two boreholes to 
depths of about 9.8 and 10.1 feet below the existing ground surface.  Subsequently, we 
drilled two additional boreholes immediately adjacent to the first borings and conducted 
falling head infiltration tests in the supplemental borings which were tested in accordance 
with Hawaii Department of Health procedures.  The test results are presented in 
Appendix B.

PURPOSE AND SCOPE 
The purpose of our exploration was to perform field percolation testing at selected 
locations within the site to obtain infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials. 
This information may be used to assist in the design of the septic disposal system. Our 
work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated July 29, 2014. The 
scope of our work included the following tasks and work efforts: 
1. 
Mobilization and demobilization of a truck-mounted drill rig, water truck, and 
two operators to and from the project site. 
2. 
Drilling and sampling of two boreholes at selected locations within the 
project site to depths of about 9.8 and 10.1 feet below the existing ground 
surface to obtain soils information and drilling of two supplemental boreholes 
to a depth of about 5 feet for percolation testing. 
3. 
Performance of falling head infiltration tests in the drilled borings in 
accordance with Hawaii Department of Health requirements at the depth of 
about 5 feet below the existing ground surface. 
4. 
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings and field 
infiltration testing by our field engineer/geologist. 
5. 
Analyses of the field data to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the 
near-surface materials for preliminary design of the septic disposal system. 
6. 
Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and 
presenting our findings and recommendations. 
7. 
Coordination of our work on the project by our senior engineer. 
8. 
Quality assurance of our work and client/design team consultation by our 
principal engineer. 
9. 
Miscellaneous work efforts, such as drafting, word processing, and clerical 
support. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The subsurface infiltration conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and 
testing of two borings, designated as Boring Nos. 1 and 2, to depths of about 9.8 and 
10.1 feet below the existing ground surface.  The approximate boring locations are shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
In general, the borings generally encountered a surface layer of dense to very 
dense sandy gravel/gravelly sand fill to the depth of about 3 feet below the existing ground 
surface.  Below the surface fill, our borings penetrated hard weathered basalt formation to

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based upon the results of our field testing, it is our opinion that the subsurface 
materials encountered at the site are sufficiently permeable for the disposal of wastewater 
effluent.  The falling head tests conducted for the septic systems indicated a moderate rate 
of infiltration, on the order of about 40 minutes per inch in Boring No. 1; and a rate of over 
60 minutes per inch in Boring No. 2.  Based on our observations of the drilling process, it 
appears that the percolation in Boring No. 1 is infiltrating through fractures in the 
underlying rock formation and that Boring No. 2 encountered denser and less fractured 
rock. 
LIMITATIONS 
The findings submitted herein are based upon information obtained from the field 
percolation tests. Variations of subsurface conditions and infiltration characteristics may 
occur between and beyond the field percolation tests. 
The boring locations indicated herein are approximate, having been indicated in 
the field by Tapestry Partners, Ltd. and shown on a topographic plan transmitted by 
them on or about August 11, 2014. The physical location of the field percolation tests 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used.
