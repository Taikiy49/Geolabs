SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our findings development of the golf course
clubhouse replacement and new halfway house is feasible
from a geotechnical engineering point of view provided that
our recommendations are followed
Our exploration encountered competent volcanic rock formation
underlying the clubhouse and halfway house sites at relatively
shallow depths The surface topsoil and ash soils generally
have poor foundation support characteristics
It is our opinion that shallow spread or strip footing founda
tions may be used to support the replacement clubhouse and
new halfway house
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed and
specific design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed golf course clubhouse replacement and new
halfway house at Mauna Kea Beach Hotel in Ouli District of South Kohala on the island
of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project sites are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for use in project planning
and to provide engineering design input The recommendations and findings presented
herein are not intended for other uses nor are they intended for final construction
estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soils
and rock conditions at the sites of the proposed golf course clubhouse replacement and
new halfway house in order to provide data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading and building founda
tions for the proposed projects The scope of our exploration consisted of the following
tasks and work efforts

test pits by a Senior Engineering Geologist from our firm The test pits were
excavated using a backhoe provided by the Mauna Kea Beach Hotel
maintenance department
2 Analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
considerations and recommendations pertinent to the site grading and
foundation design for the proposed clubhouse replacement and the new
halfway house
3 Preparation of six 6 copies of this report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing Mauna Kea Beach Hotel golf course
clubhouse and to construct a new clubhouse structure at the same location It is our
understanding that the new clubhouse will be a one story structure of composite wood
and concrete construction with concrete slab on grade floors We understand that
retaining walls may be required for grade separation
In addition it is proposed to construct a halfway house between the ninth green
and tenth tee of the golf course located to the east of the clubhouse site This new
halfway house will consist of a one story concrete and wood structure about 18 by 18 feet
in plan dimension The new structure will provide restroom facilities and space for
refreshment sales The proposed structures are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is anticipated that earthwork for the proposed construction will be minimal
consisting generally of excavation and backfill for structural elements such as foundations
and retaining walls

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project sites are situated at the existing Mauna Kea Beach Hotel in Ouli
District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
sites are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The existing clubhouse site is generally level except for grade separation walls
between the clubhouse and the fronting street The area around the existing building is
landscaped
The site of the proposed halfway house is about 200 feet east of the existing
clubhouse on a low knoll between the ninth green and tenth tee At the time of our field
exploration there was a portable kiosk at the site for the sale of refreshments Generally
the area is nearly level with a vegetative cover of thin grasses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project sites are underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic
Series of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and
Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which are mantled
by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil
at the project sites differs from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii
in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a friable state
Based on previous experience in the area the lava flows underlying the site are in
the A a form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of fragmental
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3085 00 Page 5
clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which frequently has a thinner less
continuous layer of clinker below the massive core The clinker surface of the lavas at
the site have been mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above
Our experience in the vicinity of the project site indicates that the upper clinker
layers are generally cemented and have good strength characteristics
Field Exploration
The scope of our field exploration consisted of the logging and sampling of four
4 test pits excavated at the project sites using a backhoe provided by the Mauna Kea
Beach Hotel maintenance department Logging and sampling was conducted by a Senior
Engineering Geologist from our firm
In general the test pits encountered competent volcanic rock or cemented clinker
formation at shallow depths ranging between one half 2 and three feet 3 below the
existing ground surface At the clubhouse site the basalt formation was generally
overlain by topsoil and ash soils At the new halfway house site there was a thin surface
layer of ash soil over the basalt formation
Groundwater was not observed in the excavated test pits at the time of our
exploration
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings the proposed development at the project sites may be
supported on a shallow foundation system bearing on the basalt rock formation or
cemented clinker encountered near the ground surface As an alternate the foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3085 00 Page 6
should bear on fill placed and compacted in accordance with the recommendations
presented herein Detailed discussions and recommendations for site grading and
foundation design are presented in the following sections of this report
Site Preparation
All areas within the grading limits should be cleared of all deleterious materials
including surface soils and demolition debris This material should be disposed of
properly at an off site location Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
The topsoil and ash soils have poor strength characteristics Therefore these soils
should not be used for foundation support for fill nor for backfill
Imported structural fill and backfill materials should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986 General and yard fill
materials should generally consist of non expansive well drained soils free of boulders and
other deleterious materials
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 91 Trench and structural backfills should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3085 00 Page 7
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 65H 1V should be keyed and benched into the underlying soil or basalt formation
to reduce potential instability of the new fill
Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the sites and in their vicinity
indicate that the sites are most likely underlain by cemented clinker and dense basalt at
relatively shallow depths It is anticipated that hard excavation and or ripping may be
encountered in cemented areas of the clinker horizons Hard excavation may also be
required in the basalt rock formation Generally hard excavation is considered to be
techniques such as heavy ripping hoerams or splitting Generally the excavation method
is the responsibility of the contractor
Design slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter may be used
for cut and fill slopes
Foundation Support
The upper topsoil and ash soils encountered at the sites have poor strength
characteristics These soils should not be used for foundation support Foundations for
the proposed buildings should bear on the competent rock and cemented clinker
formations encountered at relatively shallow depths below the existing ground surface
The rock or cemented clinker formation should be trimmed to provide a plane
bearing surface for the foundations i e the foundations should not be placed on sloping
rock or cemented clinker faces Care should be taken in excavation and trimming of the
rock to avoid creating high spots which could induce point loading on the foundations
High spots should be broken out of the foundation bearing area Any overbreak in rock
excavation should be backfilled with lean concrete to the footing subgrade elevation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3085 00 Page 8
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot p s f may
be utilized for the design of foundations bearing on the clinker or basalt rock formation
This value may be increased by one third for temporary wind and or seismic loads The
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on granular fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein This value may also be increased by one third for
temporary wind and or seismic loads Footings bearing on fill should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Foundations should only bear on one type of material i e footings should not
span from fill to rock If a foundation will soan between the two materials the portion of
the subgrade in rock should be over excavated a minimum of 12 inches The over
excavated area should be backfilled with select granular material placed in six inch 6
lifts and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as established by ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 91 This will provide uniform support for the foundation In this
case the foundation may be designed with an allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 p s f
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of any footing excavation
they should be over excavated to expose competent materials and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with select granular material placed in six inch 6 lifts and
compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91

Slabs On Grade
it is anticipated that the floors of the clubhouse replacement and the new halfway
house will be concrete slabs on grade Building slabs and their cushions may be
supported by the in situ volcanic clinker and rock formation or by fill placed and
compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented herein Slabs on grade
should not be supported on the Pahala Ash material encountered in our test pits Where
the slabs are located directly over the Pahala Ash material the slabs should be supported
on 12 inches of select fill material or the Panala Ash may be removed
A Four inch 4 layer of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel No 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill and to serve as a capillary break As an alternate
to the No 3B Fine gravel cushion an equal or greater thickness of basaltic termite barrier
sand may be used
Slab perimeter thickened edges should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent exterior grade or be embedded a minimum of six inches 6 into rock
formation To reduce the potential of future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to the floor coverings a moisture membrane should be considered below the slabs above
the cushion fill A one inch layer of moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic
membrane to protect the plastic membrane and to aid in concrete curing

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to
Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comment prior to construction This review is
needed to evaluate adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the earthwork and foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications and or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations provided
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
Geolabs Hawaii prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as visual observations and test pits
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur the nature and extent
