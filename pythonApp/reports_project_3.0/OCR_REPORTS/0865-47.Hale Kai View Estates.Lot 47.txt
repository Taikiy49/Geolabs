As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of excavating three 3
test pits within the proposed building site laboratory soil tests
and a review of the grading and compaction test record on the building
site The report summarizes our observations and recommendations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicates that the front portion of the site from Kulani
Street is underlain by about two feet of on site fill material while
the remaining portion of the lot is underlain by a thin surface layer
of dark gray expansive adobe clay about 0 5 to 2 0 feet thick over
dense weathered rock Rock outcrops were noted in the existing
surface on the northern portion of the lot

laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1 The underlying

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a thin
layer approximately 0 5 to 2 0 feet thick of grayish brown clay The
laboratory test results on this material indicated that they have high
expansion potential Therefore for Lot 67 the slab on grade and
foundation construction should extend below the surface expansive clay
and bear on the weathered rock formation
Foundation
For post and beam construction the individual post footings should
extend below the upper expansive clay and bear on the weathered rock
formation All footings should have a minimum embedment of 12 inches
below the finished grade They should be poured in place and the
excavation backfilled and compacted to provide an impervious layer
over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the dense weathered rock formation
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is
especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water and possible saturation and
settlement or heaving of the foundation soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
It is our understanding that slab on grade is planned for the garage
and the rear portion of the house It appears that the rear portion
of the garage area would require cut up to about 4 feet in order to
obtain sufficient level area for the garage Therefore better less
expansive material may be encountered in the rear portion of the
garage excavation The front portion of the garage will have little
or no cut and would encounter the expansive clay
The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils the
expansive clays encountered below the slab subgrade level should be
over excavated down to the weathered rock or a maximum depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 Similar
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils are
recommended for the rear portion of the house
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the compacted
fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 2 feet thick was encountered in
the front portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveways can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay over excavation and
replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
or to weathered rock whichever is less Where 2 or more feet of fill
is planned select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap
over the expansive clay
Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over excavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive material
is required The above requirements should be followed for a minimun
distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recommended
at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
The condition of the subgrade below the existing 30 foot long driveway
is uncertain Its future performance will depend on the type of soil
underneath
GEOLABS HAWAII
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 13 to
17 gradient
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structure
Retaining Walls
CMU retaining walls are planned around the garage and the rear portion
of the house to retain the proposed cuts and fills of up to about 4
feet The following procedures should be noted

The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum depth of 18
inches below the outside finished grade and into weathered rock
formation
Granular backfill such as select borrow or crusher waste
should be used for a distance behind the wall equal to the
height of the wall This would improve drainage reduce the
compactive effort required for the wall backfill and reduce
lateral pressure behind the walls
Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be used only
for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of surface
run off behind the garage wall Clay backfill should not be
used under the house
A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes should be
utilized behind the wall to prevent the build up of hydrostatic
pressure
