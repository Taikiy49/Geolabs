Probing and Grouting Report
Doutor Coffee Farm Improvements Phase 1
Kailua Kona Island of Hawaii
Dear Mr Yamamoto
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the probing and grouting operations for the pedestrian and vehicular bridge foundations for the
Doutor Coffee Farm Improvements Phase 1 project
The subject site is located to the west of Mamalahoa Highway and approximately
2 miles to the north of the town of Holualoa in the Kailua Kona area on the Island of Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
vehicular bridge will be constructed Each bridge will be supported on two concrete abutment
structures The abutment structures are to be founded on a spread footing foundation
Previously a field exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc for the project Based
on the results of the field exploration a probing and grouting program for the bridge abutment
structures was recommended The purpose of the probing and grouting work was to reduce
the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression
of loose zones below the abutment structures
The probing and grouting work was performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc A
track mounted drill rig ECM 370 with an air driven cutting bit was used for the probing work
The 3 inch diameter probe holes extended about 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevation
and were backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures A
2 000 pounds per square inch psi sand cement grout mix was used A field engineer from
Geolabs Inc observed the probing and grouting operation
The probe holes were drilled on August 18 2000 and grouted on August 21 2000
A total of 22 probe holes each 10 feet deep with an aggregate length of about 220 lineal
feet were drilled A total of 8 probe holes were drilled for the pedestrian bridge abutments
and a total of 14 probe holes were drilled for the vehicular bridge abutments
