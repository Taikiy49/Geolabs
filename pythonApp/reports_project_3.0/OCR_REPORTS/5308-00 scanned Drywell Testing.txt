INJECTION TESTING
On September 27 and 28 2004 injection well testing was conducted at the project
site The injection testing consisted of placing water from an on site source e g pumping
from a nearby water feature on site irrigation line or garden hose into the wells and
measuring the build up of the water level in response to the injection of water Injection
flow rates were generally monitored using a flow meter When possible constant head
conditions were established in the wells being tested
The original scope of work called for the testing of four 4 individual wells
designated as DL 1 through DL 4 inclusive However during the field work it was found
connected by overflow to an adjacent culvert Therefore during the test for DL 4 the
water levels were also monitored in wells DL 5 and DL 6 The results of the field testing
are presented on the attached Plates 1 through 6 inclusive
DISCUSSION AND FINDINGS
Injection Well DL 1
This well was tested by injecting water pumped from an adjacent water feature
Water was injected at rates varying between about 30 and 70 gallons per minute gpm
The test started with the higher flow rate of about 70 gpm and it took about one hour to
raise the water level in the well to about 3 feet from the lower lip of the well manhole or
vault Flow rate was then reduced to about 50 gpm and the water level maintained at this
level for about 3 hours Therefore it appears that the maximum rate that this well is
capable of accepting is on the order of about 50 gpm At the conclusion of the test the
water level in the well dropped about one foot over a period of about 3 hours indicating
slow recovery and possible formation saturation
Injection Well DL 2
This well was tested by injecting water from a garden hose Water was injected at
a relatively constant rate of about 5 gom Over a period of about 3 hours the well filled at
this low flow rate Falling head testing indicated that the well recovered about 0 5 foot over
a period of about 6 hours These low infiltration rates indicate that the capacity of the well
seems to be less than 5 gpm

Injection Well DL 3
This well was tested by injecting water from an on site irrigation line Water was
injected at rates varying between about 35 and 100 gallons per minute gpm The test
started with the higher flow rate of about 100 gpm which was reduced to an average of
about 90 gpm At the average injection rate of about 90 gpm it took about 2 hours to raise
the water level in the well to about 2 feet from the lower lip of the well manhole or vault
Flow was then stopped At the conclusion of the test the water level in the well dropped
about 2 feet over a period of about 12 hours indicating moderately good recovery
Injection Wells 5 and DL 6
Field testing indicated that these three wells were interconnected and that they
were also connected to an overflow It was not possible to increase the head level in the
three wells more than about 0 5 foot without overflowing to the adjacent culvert The field
testing indicated that the system of three wells was capable of accepting about 15 gom
without bypassing to the overflow The system showed relatively good recovery in about
30 minutes
FINDINGS
In general our testing showed that wells DL 1 and DL 3 have relatively good
capacity Injection capacity in well DL 2 is marginal and testing of well DL 4 was
inconclusive due to its connection to 6 and the culvert overflow
It should be noted that these tests were relatively short term and do not account for
tidal influences and seasonal variations in the water table at the project site It should also
be noted that these tests in general were conducted using clear fresh water without the
sediment loading that is typically associated with storm water runoff and irrigation return
water It should be anticipated that the capacity of the wells will decrease over time as a
result of formation clogging due to sediment and other influences such as biological
action
