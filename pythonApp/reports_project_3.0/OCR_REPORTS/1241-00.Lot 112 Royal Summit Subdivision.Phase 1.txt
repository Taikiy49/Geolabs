In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in
order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed
residence The study consisted of drilling three shallow
borings laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the
soils a review of the subdivision site grading plans and the
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 30 by 97 feet in plan dimension Concrete Slab on grade
is planned for the garage house and lanai
CRM retaining walls up to 13 feet high are also planned along
the rear and southern portion of the lot
Fills of up to about 13 feet are planned for the southern
portion of the site
The layout plan for the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the southeastern side of Kaahele Street in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu
Hawaii Lot 112 is terraced and generally slopes down from the
north toward the south There is a 2 1 H V bank about 10
feet high along the northern boundary sloping downward to a
level pad A transitional slope located about midway through
the site and having a slope ratio of about 4 1 H V slopes
toward the south to a second level pad along the southern
boundary
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that this lot required cuts from 2 to 10 feet However this
area may have been filled previously for the extension of
Kaahele Street prior to subdivision grading

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered stiff to hard reddish Silty clay to the
depth of the borings drilled A review of the subdivision
grading plans and the field borings indicate that this material
may be an older fill placed during the initial development of
the area
Laboratory swell tests performed on the silty clay soil
indicates that this material generally has moderate to high
swelling potential depending on the moisture content of the
soil Previous experience indicates that this type of material
could have high swelling potential if allowed to dry out
Therefore measures should be taken to prevent excessive drying
of the soil
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils have moderate to high swelling potential therefore
precautionary measures would be required for foundations placed
on the existing soil
It is anticipated that 4 to 10 feet of fill will be required
under the southern portion of the house foundation We feel
that the key element regarding the foundation design will be the
type and amount of fill material used
GEOLABS HAWAII

Ideally select non expansive soils such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral should be used for fill within 2 feet of
the house finished grade
The on site stockpile of excess material from the Royal Summit
Subdivision grading work may be available at a lower cost
However this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential and also could be contaminated with expansive soil
during handling Therefore special grading preparation and
foundation procedures would be required In addition there
would be a higher risk of foundation movement in the future For
these reasons the on site Royal Summit soils are not recommended
for fill within 2 feet of the slab subgrade
Areas requiring small fill such as the northern portion of the
house should be excavated to a minimum depth of 24 inches below
the slab subgrade and replaced with the abovementioned select
non expansive soil The existing reddish brown soil removed
during the excavation may be used as fill along the southern
portion of the site below the non expansive Capping layer
Foundations
Foundations bearing on the minimum 2 foot cap of compacted
non expansive select material may use an allowable bearing value
of 3000 p s f
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1241 00 Page 5
Slab perimeter footings and individual footings could be
designed with a minimum footing embedment of 8 inches below the
outside finished grade
To reduce cost the remaining fill below the 2 foot cap of
non expansive fill could consist of the on site select fill from
the Royal Summit Subdivision grading The material should be
approved by a qualified soils engineer prior to its being used
The bottom of all footing excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm soil The excavation should be
observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil
has been reached
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
Site Grading
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris

If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill it
should be placed wet of optimum moisture content 30 to
reduce the swelling potential
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical fills placed should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and
driveway
Where a minimum of 2 feet of off site non expansive soil such
as crusher waste is used for the fill conventional
slab on grade design with an 8 inch deep perimeter may be used
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course and should be separated from foundations
A plastic vapor barrier placed over the base course layer may be
used should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect the
floor covering

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that CRM retaining walls are planned
along the rear and the southern portion of the lot in order to
retain the proposed fill of up to about 13 feet The guidelines
listed below should be followed
1 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished
grade
Wall situated on slopes should been embedded an additional
amount to maintain a minimum 8 foot horizontal setback
distance measured from the outside slope face to the edge of
the wall footing
2 A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls
3 An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff soil
4 A friction factor of 0 4 for soil can be utilized to
determine the sliding resistance of the wall In addition a
passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized for
walls on level areas This passive pressure does not apply
for walls on 2 1 slope H V

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install gutter around the house and they should also be made
aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
perimeter footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper perfor
mance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength or heaving The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
