INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions at the proposed site and to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the pavement and foundations
of the proposed structure
Site Description
The proposed Hilo Rehabilitation Complex is situated near the
Hilo Hospital in the Puhonua region of Hilo The project site
is located southeast of the intersection between Waianuenue
Avenue and Henderson Road
Page 2
W O 623 00
Geographically the area is in the vicinity of the Wailuku
River banks about the same elevation as the Rainbow Falls
Kaumana springs of the Kupa pan Hills are near by and pos
sibily the entire region is of similar origin
Presently the site was once cleared of sugar cane At the
time of field exploratory canes have grown back The site
slopes towards the ocean and lava rock ledges are exposed
like steps Between these surface ledges are ponds of soft
mud probably washed down by the torrential rain well known
in the Hilo area
The southern edge of the site is a steep gully which runs
down to a tributary of the Wailuku river
The site where the sheltered workshop will be constructed is
presently occupied by piles of rubbish and overgrown with
vegetation There are several other mounts like this within
the project site These mounts measured anywhere from 70 to
80 feet in width and reach a height of more than 15 feet On
rainy days low spots of the site is quite muddy and inaccessible
The approximate location of the rubbish stockpile was outlined
onthe Site Plan Plate 1

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing
four test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1
At the time of boring exploration the ground surface was wet
and swampy The original boring locations were not accessible
by a truck mounted drill rig Drilling was subsequently per
formed by a portable equipment
Competent rock was encountered near surface and subsequently
the borings were terminated at shallow depths A detailed
description of the field exploration methods and accompanying
laboratory testing is given in the appendix along with the
boring logs and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed essentially uniforn soil and
rock conditions within the proposed building area Medium
hard gray black Pahoehoe Vesicular Lava was encountered in
all borings about two feet below existing ground surface
The surface soil was gray brown volcanic ash with in place
moisture content of about 150 to 250 percent Groundwater
was not noted in the bore holes at the time of field explora
tion
Geology
The Hilo geology is divided between the volcanoes of Mauna Loa
and that of Mauna Kea The proposed site 1S situated at the
border line between these two giant volcanic series The
upper member of Hamakua volcanic series and Pahala ash deposit
which consist largely of olivine basalts are not encountered
during drilling Therefore the site area is considered to be
within the Mauna Loa series probably the prehistoric member of
Kau volcanic series
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our investigation the proposed structures may be
supported on spread and continuous footings founded on the
dark gray lava rock or upon compacted granular fill
Page 5
5522716
Foundation
Spread and line footing foundations may be used for the
proposed building Footings should either all bear upon
the in situ lava rock or be supported on compacted fill
layers Allowable bearing pressure up to 3 000 pounds
per square foot may be used The bearing value may be
increased by one third when considering wind and seismic
loads For footings on rock settlements will be minimal
For the building spanning over cut and fill where the
footing location is in rock the rock should be over exca
vated 6 inches and replaced with Aa base course Generally the
bottom of exterior footings should be a minimum of 18 inches
below the finish adjacent grade
For the portions of the footing foundation located near the
top of the slope at the south and east sides of the building
location a minimum 5 foot setback should be provided from
the outer edge of footing out to the slope surface
Slab On Grade
The silty ash surface soil is sensitive to liquefaction due to
compaction equipment movements It can not provide adequate
subgrade support In the structural and parking areas all the
wet silty surface soils should be removed and replaced by a com
pacted granular backfill such as Aa materials which are readily
available on the Islands of Hawaii

Compacted structural fill should be placed in 8 inch loose
layers and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the laboratory
maximum density using ASTM D 1557 70 as a standard Slabs
should have a minimum 4 inch layer of Aa base course layer
Pavements
Parking and driveway pavement sections should be placed on
well compacted granular backfill

with a CBR of 85 and conforming with section 31 2 Standard Speci

Earthwork and Compaction
The on site thin surface soil mantle has moisture content exceeding
150 Hilo area is generally too rainy to dry the surface ash
material Area to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and any previously placed rubbish stockpiles The
existing sloping ground surface should be benched to properly key
the newly placed fill to the underlying rock formation Prior
to the placement of any new fill the upper layers of mud and ash
fill on the existing ground surface should be removed and hauled
away from building and parking areas The fill material should be
placed in 8 inch loose layers and compacted to a minimum of 90

It is recommended that the foundation excavations be inspected

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four borings utilizing skid mounted rotary drilling rig with auger
and rock coring attachments The borings were terminated after
obtaining 5 feet of coring into rock The locations of the borings
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate l
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural examina
tions in the field by our geologist who continuously supervised
the drilling operation The classifications were verified by
further visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A
graphic presentation of the soils and rock encountered is pre
sented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
