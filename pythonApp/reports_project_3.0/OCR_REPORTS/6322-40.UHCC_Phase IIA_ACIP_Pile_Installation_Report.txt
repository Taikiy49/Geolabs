Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the University of
Hawaii Cancer Center project and the findings and recommendations are presented in the
referenced report The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the
geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation installation for conformance with
the intent of the recommendations presented in our report In addition we provided
Special Inspection services as required by the City and County of Honolulu by
observing the ACIP pile foundation installation for general conformance with the project
plans and specifications

Based on the referenced report for the project we recommended supporting the
new extension wing on a deep foundation system consisting of 18 inch augered

load capacity of up to 300 kips 150 tons per pile The ACIP piles would extend through
the soft and or loose lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support primarily
from skin friction and end bearing within the coral ledges and or coralline detritus materials
encountered below the lagoonal deposit

ACIP TRIAL AND TEST PILE INSTALLATION
A test pile or indicator pile program which consisted of installing two 2 ACIP trial
piles and one 1 ACIP test pile was conducted for the project on A
Manitowoc 12000 series crane with crane mounted hollow stem auger powered by an
APE 75C Hydraulic pump was used to install the trial and test piles A hydraulic concrete
pump with an average stroke volume of 0 063 cubic yard per stroke was used to pump the
grout into the system The trial piles were installed to a depth of about 76 feet below the
existing ground surface with no reinforcement The test pile Pile No 322 was selected
from the future production pile locations The approximate locations of the trial and test
piles are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the trial and test
pile program was to verify the subsurface conditions at the project site the selected ACIP
equipment and methods of ACIP pile installation
A summary of the test pile driving conditions and observations is included in the
ACIP Pile Installation Summary Plate 3 of this report for reference Based on the results of
the test pile program it appears that the recommended design length is sufficient to carry
the design loads
