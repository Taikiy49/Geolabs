Soil Conditions
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the test pit
locations the material could generally be described as follows
1 A four inch thick concrete slab over 2 inches of gray sandy
gravel cushion was encountered No thickened edge was noted on
the driveway slab
2 The near surface material generally consisted of clayey silt
ranging from about 0 5 feet to 3 0 feet or more below the
existing ground surface This material appears relatively moist
to saturated and medium stiff to soft
Comparing the exposed subsurface conditions and qgosuits of moisture
content determinations it was noted that generally the clayey materials
in the vicinity of the damaged areas are relatively soft and quite moist
saturated
Laboratory swell test results on remolded one inch thick 2 4 inch
diameter ring samples of these clayey soils indicate that this soil has
low to moderate swelling potential when saturated from a dry condition
CONCIUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests it appears that
most of the driveway slab movement at the residence was probably caused by
saturation and subsequent heaving of the underlying clayey soils The
saturation of the clayey soils also softens and weakens the soil and
could also result in settlement of portions of the driveway slab
Laboratory swell tests were performed on the underlying soil to
simulate conditions of a dry subgrade soil condition with subsequent

saturation of the subgrade soils by water ponding adjacent to the driveway
slab foundation Our laboratory test indicated a swell of up to 8 from
the air dried state to the saturated state Therefore if the subgrade
soils were very dry prior to concrete placement and then were subsequently
saturated heaving of the slab could occur
The poor site drainage around the house also contributed to the
heaving problems by allowing water to pond next to the driveway slab
causing the surface water to infiltrate under the interior portion of the
driveway slab and thereby causing the underlying expansive clay to
swell
Additional laboratory tests on saturated samples at the in situ
moisture content indicated that minimal heaving would occur if the soils
are maintained at their present moisture content
One of the main objectives of any future remedial work undertaken at
the site should be to prevent or minimize water from entering or leaving
under the slabs It is our opinion that the following steps could be
adopted to reduce additional future movements to the driveway slab
1 Construct a concrete thickened edge around the perimeter of the
driveway slab The purpose of this thickened edge is to create a
barrier to minimize outside water from freely entering and
migrating towards the interior slab area and or drying out of
the soils under the slab It is our opinion that the thickened
edge should extend a minimum of 8 inches below the ground surface
and have a minimm width of 6 inches
About 2 pounds of hydrated lime per lineal foot should be placed
in the bottom of the thickened edge The moist lime slurry could
help to chemically reduce the clayey characteristics of the
surrounding soils and also help to create an additional barrier
to water infiltration

2 Increase thickness of subbase material between concrete slab and

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING DURING REPATRS
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during remedial
construction to monitor the contractor s operations
It is also recommended that the thickened edge excavation and
construction be observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that proper installation and grade have been obtained to drain potential
surface and seepage flow away from the driveway and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
exploration The lime stabilization operations should also be inspected

the above inspection services should you desire in the future
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
