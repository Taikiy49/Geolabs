REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is located on the northwestern flank of Hualalai mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island and consists partly of the prehistoric
basaltic lava flows of the Hualalai Volcanic Series which were most likely deposited
during the late Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and MacDonald 1946
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 40 A Page 6
Hawaiian volcanic activity is typified by an abundance of flank eruptions along
rift zones or zones of weakness in the earth s crust The native basalt formation
observed at the site appear to be of pahoehoe lava which spread and ponded as it
approached the ocean Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy
surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly
encountered within pahoehoe lavas Most cavities are formed when the lava remains
in a molten state and may represent both lava tubes intra flow Cavities and blisters
and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes intra flow cavities are formed when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are
generally localized and smaller in horizontal extent
In general basalt are considered to be relatively permeable rock and can
transmit water quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions Water is
transmitted through the porous matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts
Cavities and along clinker layers

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our reconnaissance pahoehoe lava flows are present near the ground
surface at the project site The pahoehoe encountered at the site is characterized by
a relatively smooth and hard surface with an internal structure of vesicular porous
rock Excavation into this material may be very difficult using conventional construction
equipment therefore use of a jack hammer hoe ram and or blasting may be required
We anticipate that excavation into the basalt formation will result in a very jagged
and rough surface which will adversely affect the performance of the synthetic liner
planned for the A E Basin Therefore we recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick
cushion layer consisting of compacted base course materials be provided below the
entire A E Basin bottom and side slopes of the basin An underlining consisting of a
geotextile fabric should be placed on the cushion layer below the impervious liner to
reduce the potential for puncture of the impervious liner from edges of the rocky
excavation
The material type performance installation and protection details of the
impervious liner should be designed in accordance with the requirements of the liner
manufacturer Particular attention should be given to proper anchoring of the liner at
the top of the basin to reduce the potential for liner failure
Loose clinkers were observed near the existing ground surface at localized areas
across the project site The loose clinkers should be removed to expose dense basalt
formation and replaced with compacted fill where necessary within the liner and
structural areas Shallow foundations bearing on the in situ dense basalt formation
and or structural fill may be used for support of the proposed structures An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for the design of foundations

Impervious Liner
We understand that a permanent synthetic liner will be provided for the proposed
A E Basin It is our opinion that the material type performance installation and
protection details of the impervious liner should be designed in accordance with the
recommendations of the liner manufacturer
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicate that there is a
tendency for the liners to become detached at the top of the water impoundment It
appears that this may be the result of inadequate anchoring of the top of the liner and
insufficient flexibility of the liner material It is our recommendation that the liner
manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction detailing and installation of
the liner with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the potential of liner
failure Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the impervious liner
near the top anchor should also be considered Excavation for the anchor trench may
encounter difficulties in maintaining a neat and stable trench since a large portion of the
A E Basin banks will be in cuts into basalt formation The anchor trench should be
backfilled with base course material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Technical representatives from the manufacturers of the impervious liner should
be required to be on site at all times during their installation to assure that proper
construction procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative from the
supplier s may not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function
A geotextile should be required beneath the impervious liner to increase the
puncture resistance of the liner Specifications for the geotextile fabric underlining
should conform with the recommendations provided by the liner manufacturer As a
minimum requirement the geotextile fabric should have a minimum weight of 8 ounces
per square yard when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3776

Foundations
Based on our field reconnaissance the hard pahoehoe lava flows encountered
at the project site would provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
structures Because of the small lava tubes that are present and the potential for
cavities below the proposed structures a foundation probing and grouting program is
recommended for the project construction Requirements for probing and grouting are
further discussed in the Foundation Probing section of this report
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for design of
foundations bearing on the basalt rock formation and or structural fill This bearing
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The footings should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade We estimate the
footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing on basalt
rock and or structural fill to be less than inch total and inch differential
For the proposed Clarifier and Chlorine Contact Chamber we recommend that
mat foundations bearing on 12 inches of compacted base course overlying the in situ
basalt rock formation be used for foundation design of the embedded structures The
compacted base course layer is to provide more uniform support for the concrete slab
and to reduce the potential for point loading of the mat
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of the foundation system

Foundation Probing
In general the basaltic rock formation in Hawaii consists of lava flows prone to
contain tubes voids and or cavities In order to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of voids below foundations we recommend
that a cavity probing program be carried out for the proposed structures Probes are
generally drilled at each individual spread footing location and on 10 foot intervals along
wall footing locations and mats The probes are generally 3 inches in diameter and
should be drilled to a minimum depth of 10 feet below the planned bottom of footing
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing or grouting
operations additional probes should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate the
vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and any cavities
discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate
pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of 1 part sand 1 part cement
and 0 7 parts water

recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete Since the potential exists for encountering cavities
and or voids at this site the contractor should be made aware that a longer lag time

The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation

Pavement Design
We understand that an access road will be constructed to serve the proposed
sewage treatment plant Based on our field reconnaissance the roadway alignment
will be underlain by pahoehoe lava flows We recommend that the roadway subgrades
be proof rolled by a vibratory drum roller weighing at least 15 tons for a minimum of
6 passes prior to the placement of the base course layer
We anticipate the vehicle loading for this access road will consist primarily of
utility trucks for maintenance purposes We have made our preliminary pavement
design assuming the pavement subgrade will consist of compacted granular fill or hard
GEOLABS HAWAI

basalt formation Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not

the site during or after construction
Utility and Main Effluent Lines
We envision that new utility lines including a main effluent line connecting the
Clarifier and the A E Basin will be installed for development of this project In general
a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular material less
