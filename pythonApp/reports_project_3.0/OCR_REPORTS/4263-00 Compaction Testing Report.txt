EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located to the southwest of the existing Kapaa Central Landfill
Site closed between H 3 Freeway and Kapaa Quarry Road in the Kailua area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The Kapaa Landfill Site No 2 Closure Improvements were to
provide for the containment of disposed solid wastes
The earthwork operation consisted of clearing and preparation of the existing
ground placement and compaction of embankment fill and cover soil construction of
flare facility pad and central area ditch Following the clearing operation the existing
ground was generally compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Embankment fills and cover soils
were generally brought to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 85 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as embankment fill and cover soil materials were obtained and transported to our
office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill placement operation A
total of 151 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 5 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the results of the referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork
operation during construction for the above referenced project tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report was performed in general conformance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
