INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed residence for Mr and Mrs Moo
Young Sung at 797 Moaniala Street Hawaii Loa Ridge in Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated

This report summarizes our findings and presents our recommendations
derived from analyses based on data obtained during our exploration
These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two story masonry building with slab on grade floors a swimming pool
and concrete deck and walkways Concrete slabs on grade will also be used
for the garage slab and driveway
It is anticipated that site grading will consist of cut up to about
two 2 to eleven 11 feet in depth Retaining walls will be used for
grade separation
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done is general accordance with our proposal to Mr Moo
Young Sung c o Collaborative Seven dated The scope of
work was limited to
1 drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between
11 5 and 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surface soil conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the upper portion of the Hawaii Loa
Ridge development area in east Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The subject lot is on the southerly side of Moaniala Street The
site has been graded previously into a terraced wedge shaped parcel The
upper terrace believed to be fill is relatively level and is about 36
feet deep along the Moaniala Street frontage
At the back of this upper terrace the ground surface slopes down at
about two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V for about four 4 to five
5 feet to the lower terrace The lower terrace believed to be the
original ground surface slopes gently down in a southerly direction at
about five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
At the time of our exploration there was a mound of soil stockpiled
near the eastern property corner and the site was vegetated with a thin
cover of dry bermuda grass

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4
borings to depths ranging between 11 5 to 16 5 feet below below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by very
stiff silty clay fill and residual soil overlying decomposed basalt
saprolite Weathered basalt rock formation was encountered at depths

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement amd footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations and
driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
