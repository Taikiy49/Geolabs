PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new residence for Mr Mrs W
Tamura Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence
will be a two story structure approximately 56 feet by 86 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire first floor

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium stiff on site soil encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the near
surface on site stiff clayey silt to clay indicate that this material
generally has moderate to high swelling potential depending on the initial
moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore
to reduce the effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 18 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the
select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the start
of construction until the slab concrete placement

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 18 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where minimal fill or cut is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide an 18 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade
slabs
It is our understanding that fills up to about 3 5 feet is
anticipated for the rear portion of the house lot to provide level ground
around the proposed swimming pool Due to the encountering loose subsoil
conditions we recommend that any required fills more than 1 foot thick
should be placed immediately at the beginning of earthwork to initiate
potential fill ground settlements due to the weights of new fills We
recommend that two 2 settlement gauges should be installed at this fill
area and the swimming pool excavation Pool construction should he
delayed toward the later part of the residential construction this would
allow the mojority of fill ground settlement to occur first This could
be verified by the survey data from the settlement gauge monitoring It
is our opinion that the settlement rate should be relatively fast and
majority of the fill ground settlement should be completed in about 3
months
All imported fill material should be non expansive select fill such
as quarry waste coral or cinder tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material

The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches

ary density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
The individual spread footings should be embedded a minimm of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt to clay soils have
moderate to high swelling potential if they are allowed to dry and then
get saturated Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down
to a minimum depth of eighteen 18 inches below the slab bottom the
exposed soils thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade refer to Plate 3 for details

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the remaining in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist prior to capping fill and
concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Walkway Driveway
Since the walkway and driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving
the 18 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be reduced to
twelve 12 inches if some amount of small slab cracking is tolerable
Again the subgrade soils should be thoroughly wetted to a moist condition
and prevented from drying out prior to capping fill and concrete
placement
Joints are also recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete

required fill should first be filled up to its finish grade and slope down
beyond the property limit before excavation and construction of the
retaining wall However if this overfill provision is not
feasible desirable and the perimeter retaining wall has to be constructed
before fill placement we recommend that structural details of the

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from property
pins indicated on the advance print of site plan transmitted on October 9
1989 Elevations of the borings were approximately determined by
interpolation from elevation points on the same plan based on an assumed
zero at the property pin on the western corner of the lot The physical
location and elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines showm in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
