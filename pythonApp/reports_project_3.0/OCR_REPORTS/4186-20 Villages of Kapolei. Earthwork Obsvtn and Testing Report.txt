This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the mass grading utility installation and roadway construction for the Kapolei Village 6

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kapolei Village 6 project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei
Development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
approximately bounded to the north and east by existing residential developments to the
south by Kapolei Parkway and to the west by Fort Barrette Road as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
Based on the development plans the proposed housing development has been
broken down into three phases designated as Phase 1 through 3 As part of the overall
development the project included the installation of new utilities such as sewer water and
drain lines as well as construction of new roadways We understand that Phases 1 and 3
of the project site will be developed for residential use by the Department of Hawaiian Home
Lands
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the mass grading operations and infrastructure installation for the entire site

SITE GRADING
The project site has been previously mass graded as part of the Villages of Kapolei
mass grading work by Oahu Construction Company Ltd Subsequent to the mass grading
operations additional site grading and roadway and utility construction for the Village 6
project were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company The current site grading work consisted
of placement and compaction of fills on the order of less than about 3 feet thick to achieve
the design finished grades for the project It should be noted that small cavities were
encountered at two locations during the site grading operations A cavity was encountered
below one of the light pole foundations located at of Road G Another cavity was
encountered during the excavation of the drain line located at of Road G These
cavities were subsequently cleaned of the over sized and loose materials and backfilled with
Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM
During the site grading operations the existing ground was generally over excavated
where necessary to approximately 18 inches below the building slab cushion to provide the
recommended 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill material capping fill In
addition the capping fill also extended beyond the perimeter of the house slab by a minimum
of 2 feet In general the over excavated subgrades were then moisture conditioned to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the
maximum dry density During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the
fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The
maximum dry density of the materials referred to in this report were determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
The majority of the sidewalk slabs were generally underlain by 6 inches of select
borrow subbase material as required in the plans Where expansive clayey soils were
exposed at the subgrade level the select borrow subbase material was increased to at least
12 inches The additional over excavation and select borrow subbase placement was
generally implemented in Road C1 Road G to and Road k of the
Phase 2 development and Roads L M N and P of the Phase 3 development The
over excavated subgrades at the sidewalk areas were moisture conditioned to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative

compaction The select borrow subbase at the sidewalk areas were generally placed in near
level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of

Laboratory CBR tests were performed on the exposed subgrade materials at selected
locations along the roadways to confirm the design pavement sections The results of the
laboratory CBR tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report Based
on the test results the primary and secondary roads were generally over excavated where
necessary to approximately 18 and 12 inches respectively below the base course layer for
placement of the select borrow subbase material in accordance with the plans The
over excavated subgrades at the pavement areas were moisture conditioned to above the
optinum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The
select borrow subbase and base course at the pavement areas were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new residential development In general a bedding layer consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and manholes for uniform
support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around
the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill
was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 1 887 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 87 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations performed during the site
grading utility installation and roadway construction for the subject project observed and
tested by Geolabs Inc in the period covered by this report were performed in general

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was performed
without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor earthwork construction and materials Our services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
