This report summarizes our probing and grouting observation performed for the new 
Beach Club project at Makena Golf & Beach Club on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located within the existing Makena Golf & Beach Club 
development. The project generally consists of constructing a new Beach Club.  The new 
Beach Club will generally consist of three levels, with partial basement condition at the 
lower garage level. 
PROBING AND GROUTING 
The probe hole locations for the Beach Club were surveyed and staked out by 
Goodfellow Brothers, Inc. (Contractor).  The probe holes were generally marked along 
the wall footings spaced at a maximum spacing of 10 feet or less on center.  Based on 
the project plans, a total of 186 probe hole locations, designated as Probe Hole Nos. 1 
through 185 and 136A, were marked out for the probing and grouting operations.  At the 
time of the probing and grouting operations, the existing ground surface was generally 
about 3 feet above the bottom of footings at the majority of the footing locations.  The 
approximate locations of the probe holes drilled for the project are shown on the Makena

The probe holes were drilled with an air-track drill rig.  The probe holes drilled were 
about 3 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 13 to 18 feet below the 
existing ground surface.  The depths of the probe holes drilled corresponded to at least 
10 feet below the bottom of the footing elevation. 
In general, the materials encountered during the probe hole drilling operations 
consisted of fill materials and interbedded layers of a’a clinker and basalt formation.  After 
completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled by injecting grout 
into the probe holes at low to moderate pumping pressures (at the pumping pressures 
from a grout pump).  A total of about 24 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 186 probe 
holes and cavities encountered.  
Based on our observations during the probing and grouting operations, relatively 
small cavities and/or voids were encountered in some of the probe holes drilled.  
Significant cavities and/or voids were not encountered in the probe holes drilled.  A 
summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations for the project is 
provided on Plates 1.1 through 1.5, Appendix A of this letter report. 
The foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted in general 
conformance with the intent of the project specifications and the recommendations 
contained in the geotechnical engineering exploration report. Based on our field 
observations, it is our professional opinion that the cavities encountered at the foundation 
locations probed have been grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the 
recommended foundation support for the new Beach Club. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all the cavities 
and/or voids present beneath the new building.  Therefore, some of the cavities and/or 
voids may not have been detected even with the probing and grouting program 
undertaken for the project.  However, the level of foundation preparation undertaken for 
the new building foundations bearing on the on-site a’a clinker and basalt formation 
appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project.  
Performing probing and grouting of the foundations bearing on the on-site materials is to 
provide the owner with a higher degree of assurance that substantial cavities and/or voids 
are not located directly below the new building foundations. 
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of 
geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied.  It should be 
noted that provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance, nor does 
it guarantee construction of any type.
