INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed desalinization plant It is our understanding that the

desalinization plant will be used to provide irrigation water for the Makakilo Golf Course

on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in accordance with

This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on
the data obtained during the exploration The recommendations are intended for

construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a desalinization plant to provide irrigation water for the
Makakilo Golf Course It is our understanding that the proposed desalinization plant will
consist of a single story rigid steel framed building with clear well control room and acid
storage Other facilities of the proposed plant include Well Nos 1 and 2 a transformer
pad a carbon dioxide storage and a neutralization tank
The proposed building will be approximately 60 by 65 feet in plan dimension It is
our understanding that the clear well located at the northern corner of the building will
be approximately 10 by 14 feet in plan dimension and 15 feet deep
Concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the proposed desalinization building
Asphaltic concrete pavements will be provided for a 12 foot wide vehicular access
roadway and paved areas surrounding the proposed desalinization building
Based on the anticipated building finish floor elevation at 53 0 feet above Mean Sea
Level MSL and the existing topography minimal site grading is anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the westerly side of Fort Barrette Road
approximately one half of a mile south of Puu Kapolei The site is approximately square
170 feet by 160 feet in plan dimension and is located in active sugar cane Cultivation
land It appeared that the sugar cane had been recently harvested at the time of our field
exploration
The project site is generally level with average current ground elevation at
approximately fifty one 51 feet above MSL
SUBSOIL CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four 4
borings Boring Nos 1 to 4 to depths of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered stiff to very stiff brown Clay from the ground
surface to depths of 9 to 19 feet Stiff brownish tan clayey sandy silt with coral gravel
were encountered below the clay layer to the maximum drilled depth of Boring Nos 1 and
2 Medium dense to loose white coralline sand with gravel were encountered in Boring
Nos 3 and 4 from depths of about 12 0 to 21 5 feet
Groundwater level was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the encountered soil conditions it is our opinion that the proposed
project is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view The following foundation
design parameters considerations are recommended
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations can be used to support the
proposed desalinization building An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f can be
used for footing foundations bearing on the in situ stiff clay A minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade is also recommended
If loose soft soil pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing excavations
they should be over excavated to the stiff in situ clay The resulting depression should
then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce potential of footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils around the
foundation
Estimated footing settlement under a column load of 48 kips is on the order of one
half 0 5 inch total

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the floors of the proposed desalinization
plant
Laboratory testing indicated that the surface clay soils encountered in our field
exploration are moderately to highly expansive To reduce the detrimental swelling effects
of the clay subgrade on the concrete slabs it is recommended that the subgrade
underneath the slab cushion material should be over excavated by a minimum of two 2
feet The resulting depression should be backfilled with non expansive select granular
material such as select borrow crusher waste or coral in loose lifts less than 10 inches
thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
It is recommended that the placement and compaction of the two 2 feet thick
non expansive capping material be performed after completion of backfilling behind the
clear well perimeter walls located inside the desalinization building With this construction
procedure the compacted capping layer would also provide uniform foundation support
underneath the concrete slabs and would reduce potential of differential settlement or slab
cracking in areas abutting the clear well
A minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel is
recommended below the concrete slabs on grade One 1 inch of moist fine sand may
be placed above the cushion layer to help concrete curing
Clear Well
It is Our understanding that the clear well will be approximately 10 by 14 feet in plan
dimension and 15 feet deep The perimeter walls of the clear well will be subjected to
lateral earth loads The basement perimeter walls will be non yielding members and

2 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per

to determine the resistance against sliding of the walls
3 Backfill directly behind the retaining walls should consist of granular
non expansive material such as select on site excavated coral material
select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 to reduce the
compactive effort required The upper 12 inches of backfill should consist
of non expansive impervious material to reduce water infiltration behind the
walls
4 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to protect
against build up of hydrostatic pressure Alternatively the walls should be
adequately designed to resist build up of hydrostatic pressure
5 An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f can be used for the wall

It is recommended that the excavation for the clear well construction should be
supported by sheet piling in areas within the desalinization building to limit excavation and
backfilling below the building which could result in differential support settlement
conditions underneath the building foundations
The construction including backfilling of the clear well structure should be
completed prior to placement of two 2 feet of non expansive granular capping material
underneath the concrete slabs on grade of the desalinization building It is our opinion
that the capping material would also function as a cushion layer to provide uniform
foundation support especially in areas abutting the clear well
Excavation in open areas and outside the desalinization building footprint can be
performed with sloping excavation of one and one half horizontal to one vertical
The excavation method and excavation support system should be the choice of
the contractor and must comply with all applicable safety requirements The adequacy
and safety of the excavation support should be made the sole responsibility of the

best opportunity to observe any changing conditions during excavation and construction
such as unforeseen subsoils unsuitable construction sequences or techniques etc
which might affect the excavation stability

Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble and organic matter encountered
during grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The proposed concrete slab on grade areas should be excavated to the planned
subgrade level to receive the recommended two 2 feet of non expansive capping
material The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches The
scarified subgrade should then be thoroughly moisture conditioned and recompacted to
obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 78 If loose soft pockets are encountered they should be over excavated
to stiff in situ clay and backfilled with compacted granular fill
The placement and compaction of the recommended 2 feet of non expansive
Capping fill material should be scheduled after completion of the backfilling operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2640 00 Page 10
behind the clear well perimeter walls This would reduce differential supporting strength
resulting from fill and backfill conditions and hence reduce potential of slab cracking in
areas adjacent to the clear well
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Utilities
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67B is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement of
bidding The review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
concepts and recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to delivery to the site
lf actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modification to the
design should be made
