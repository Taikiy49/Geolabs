As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil condi
tions to provide recommendations for the design and construc
tion of the proposed warehouse building
Our investigation which was performed in accordance with our
proposal of March 23 1981 consisted of five borings drilled
in the proposed building location and visual observation of
the site
The results of our field exploration which were used in the
engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and recom
mendations are presented in the appendix of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed warehouse will be a one story steel framed
structure approximately 40 by 60 feet in plan dimension
The warehouse floor will be about 5 feet above the exist
ing ground A ramp and loading dock is planned along the
western side of the structure
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated
About 5 feet of fill will be required to raise the warehouse
floor
Anticipated column loading are on the order of 20 kips
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern side of Kuhio
Street in the Hilo Harbor area
The proposed building will be situated about 45 feet north
east of the existing Harbor office
The general area is clear and relatively flat and covered with
gravel
GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the information obtained from borings the site is
generally underlain with fill material from existing ground

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration and the results
of our engineering analysis the site is suitable for the
proposed structure
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the under
lying rock formation are recommended for the warehouse struc
ture
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is
underlain by a bouldery fill layer over basalt rock formation
at shallow depths
The surface fill was found to be quite heterogeneous and
varied from soft to dense In order to minimize differen
tial settlement of footings on loose fill the spread or
continuous footings should extend through the upper surface
fill and down to bear on the rock formation at about a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1030 00
P 4
2 to 5 foot depth
An allowable bearing value of 8 000 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the dense rock formation This
bearing capacity value is for total of dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary lateral
loads wind and seismic are considered
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundation
Minor settlements are anticipated for foundations on the
dense rock formation
Although large cavities were not noted in the borings a
few small cavities were encountered
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
possible large cavities or lava tubes in the underlying
lava rock every major footing location should be probed
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches
and should extend below the bottom of footing a depth
equal to the footing width or a minimum of 5 feet The
probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole
using low pressures

If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly and to locate any poten
tial voids near the footing excavations
Site Filling
About 5 feet of fill is planned inside the warehouse to raise
the floor slab
Prior to filling the existing upper fill material should be
scarified for a depth of 6 inches proofrolled and recompacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density
Imported fill should generally be granular non expansive
material less than about 3 inches in size Fill and back
fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Slab on Grade
cushion layer of gravel generally less than 3 4 inch and
larger than 1 4 inch in size
Floor slab should be structurally separated from the founda
tions

Lateral Earth Pressures
The perimeter warehouse walls will support about 5 feet of
fill The following design lateral earth pressures are
recommended
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure active case
Walls restrained at top 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure at rest condition
Additional load to traffic or surcharge should
be included where applicable
Passive earth pressure 300 p c f

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned around the warehouse
The preliminary pavement design is as follows
Z inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Due to the high concentrated loading 6 inch concrete pave
ment is recommended for the loading dock areas

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
the backfilling operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be ob
served by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation
Imported material should be non expansive tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
to depths of 5 to 14 feet deep utilizing truck
mounted drilling equipment The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were Classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs Plate A 1
through A 5S
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash NX
core barrel The core recovery and rock quality designation
RQD are presented on the boring logs
