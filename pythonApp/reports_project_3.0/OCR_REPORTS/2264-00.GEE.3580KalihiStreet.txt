PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the site and to construct a new residence for
Roshi and Mrs Tanouye The proposed residence will be a split level one
be used for the garage and utility areas The remainder of the structure
will utilize post and beam spread footing foundations to support the
structural framing
Retaining walls will be used to provide grade separation along the
project property lines and between the garage and the lower house level
Concrete driveways are planned to provide access to the garage areas

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a soils data set for
the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to
the proposed residential development
Our work on the project consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Excavation and sampling of four 4 test pits at the project site
utilizing equipment provided by the sitework contractor
2 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the test
pits by a Field Engineer from our firn
3 A site visit by a Principal Engineer of our firm to make visual
observations of the existing soil conditions
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered and
in determining their engineering properties particularly their
shrink swell potential
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
proposed residential development
6 Preparation of a design memorandum presenting our findings and
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations for the use
of the project design team

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the upper part of Kalihi Valley on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 At the time of our field
exploration sitework for the proposed residential development had already
begun and the contractor s excavation work was near finish subgrade
The project plans indicates that the subject lot previously sloped
gently towards Kalihi Street and was formerly occupied by a house and
wooden sheds These structures had been demolished and the site was
cleared prior to our involvement in the project
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by visual
observation of the cut already made by the sitework contractor and by the
excavation and sampling of four 4 test pits at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our visual observations of the existing cuts indicate that the site
was originally underlain by a surface layer consisting of soft to medium
stiff dark grayish brown to black clay Adobe about two 2 to three
3 feet in thickness Medium stiff to stiff medium brown clayey silt
with much sand gravel and some cobbles Older Alluvium was observed
GEOLABS HAWAII

underlying this surface adobe layer This alluvial soil extended to the
maximum depth explored in our test pits of about nine 9 feet below the
building subgrade or about 17 feet below the original ground surface
The adobe soils are highly to critically expansive and the alluvial
soils are moderately expansive The project site is located in a high
rainfall area therefore the on site soils have very high in situ
moisture contents and are prone to shrinkage if allowed to dry out
A minor amount of seepage was noted in the cut face of the retaining
wall excavation at the rear of the lot A small pocket of debris fill was
also observed in this cut face
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the subject site is suitable for the proposed
residential development providing the recommendations presented herein are
Closely followed
The project plans should be re reviewed by the designers for
structural and elevation conflicts Any such conflicts should be
resolved
Site Grading
Site grading work had already commenced prior to our involvement in
the project The following recommendations are intended for the remainder
of the earthwork on the project
Any adobe clay remaining under structural or pavement areas should be
over excavated to the underlying alluvial soils and the resulting
excavation backfilled with imported non expansive select material
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2264 00 Page 5
As discussed above the project site is located in a high rainfall
area The on site soils have very high moisture contents and are prone to
pumping failure under repeated wheel loading by heavy equipment care
should be taken to minimize heavy equipment traffic through the site to
reduce the potential for pumping failure of the subgrade soils Any areas
which do show pumping under equipment loads should be over excavated to
firm natural material and the resulting excavation backfilled with
imported non expansive select material compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
We believe non expansive select borrow such as 3B Fine gravel would
be ideal for use since little or no compactive effort would be required
if it is used
The on site soils should be protected from drying during sitework and
construction to reduce the potential of shrinkage from drying and
subsequent swelling when the soils become wet after construction
House Foundations
Our exploration encountered moderately expansive soils at the footing
subgrade elevations on the project site These soils are in a naturally
moist state and should be protected from drying to reduce the potential of
dehydrating during the construction phase of the project and then
subsequently swelling after construction
An allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f may be used for the
design of the house foundations on either select fill or the underlying
older alluvial layer
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
Slabs on grade should have thickened edges extending a minimm of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade A minimm of six 6 inches of 3
Fine gravel cushion should be used under the slabs A vapor barrier

Retaining Wall and House Footings
Our review of the available project plans indicates a conflict
between the foundation of the rear retaining wall and the rear garage
slab house foundations i e the garage slab will extend about two 2
feet over the toe of the wall foundation We do not recommend using
retaining wall foundations to support the house foundations since
retaining walls will yield to mobilize the active lateral earth
pressures This could cause distresses to the slab and result in cracking
or movement
Our review of the available plans also indicates that there will be
an interior retaining wall located between one of the garages and the
lower portion of the _ house Drainage should be provided for this
retaining wall Since the outside of the wall faces the crawlspace area
normally weepholes would most likely provide adequate drainage However
if this seepage disposal into the crawlspace area is not acceptable then
the subdrain outlets from the back of the retaining wall should be
connected to a closed conduit in order to properly drain the seepage water
away from the residential areas

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as visual
observations and test pits Variations of conditions between the field
data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report

The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from property corners Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by elevation given by the sitework contractor
The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
