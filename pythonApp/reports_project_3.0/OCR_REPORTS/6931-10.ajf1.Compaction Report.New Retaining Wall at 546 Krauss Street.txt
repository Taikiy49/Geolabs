Dear Mr. Teixeira: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during a portion of the site grading of the New Retaining Wall at 
546 Krauss Street project from October 14, 2015 through January 26, 2016 in accordance 
with our revised fee proposal dated October 1, 2015.  
On January 26, 2016, our services were terminated by the addressee. At that time, 
fills up to about 3 to 4 feet thick were not yet placed to achieve the design finished grades. 
Therefore, certification of these fills not observed by our office should be provided by 
others. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is at the intersection of Krauss Street and Iaukea Street in the 
Papakolea area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. An existing distressed wall 
was situated on the upslope side of Krauss Street and was constructed of stone masonry. 
The project consists of restoring the retaining conditions along the property line by 
demolishing the existing wall and installing a new CMU retaining wall, approximately 
121 linear feet in length and with a height ranging from about 3 to 7 feet. A Project 
Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for a 
portion of the site grading at the project site.  
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from October 14, 2015 through 
January 26, 2016. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavation for the

new retaining wall foundations and placement and compaction of fills and backfills up to 
about 7 feet thick behind the new retaining wall to achieve the finished design grades.  
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Close Construction, Inc. performed the site 
grading for the project. During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site soils and 
imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory 
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are 
summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. 
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of 
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water 
content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 and 4.2. Test locations and elevations were estimated using 
on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be considered 
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not meet the 
required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required minimum 
compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s grading 
activity which includes: foundation subgrade preparation, fill and backfill placement, 
compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the 
above grading operations.  
It should be noted that our earthwork observation and testing services were 
terminated on January 26, 2016 at about 2:15 pm at the request of Buddy Teixeira of 
Close Construction during the placement and compaction of fill materials for the planned 
slope above the new retaining wall. Based on our field observations, the contractor still 
had fills up to about 3 to 4 feet thick left to be placed for this slope to achieve the design 
finished grades. The approximate elevations of our last field density tests before our 
services were terminated were about +302.5 and +304.5 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) at 
approximate Stations 1+10 and 0+70, respectively. Certification of fills placed beyond that 
point should be provided by others. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the portion of the site grading work for the New Retaining Wall at 
546 Krauss Street project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report, were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.

Geolabs assumes no responsibility for alterations made to grades on the project 
subsequent to our observation and testing period without our knowledge and written 
acknowledgment.

occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some work may have occurred 
without our observation. However, we believe the level of earthwork observation and 
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project. It
