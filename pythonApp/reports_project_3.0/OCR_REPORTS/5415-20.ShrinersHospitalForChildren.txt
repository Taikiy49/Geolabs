PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing Shriners Hospital For Children in the Punahou
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is at the southwest corner
of the intersection between Punahou Street and the Punahou Street Off Ramp for the
Interstate Route H 1 Highway A Project Location Map is shown on Plate 1
We understand that a new Main Hospital is planned to replace the existing
hospital Phase 1 of the project will consist of demolishing Buildings B C and D of the
existing hospital and constructing the western half of the new Main Hospital This will
allow the existing hospital to remain in operation during construction Based on
the information provided the Main Hospital will have a plan area of about 134 feet
wide by 273 feet long The Main Hospital will have a total of two levels above grade and a
single level basement We understand the basement level will beset
at 31 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The main Hospital foundation construction requires a

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
JE Dunn Construction Company and Ralph S were the general
contractors for the Shriners Hospital For Children Honolulu Hospital project Jayar
Construction Inc performed the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement
construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of clearing footing
excavation subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 For the hospital building foundations the
original compaction requirement for the foundation and slab subgrades was a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density During compaction of the foundation subgrades
we observed crushing of the in situ cinder sand To reduce the potential for crushing of
the cinder sand to finer materials we revised the compaction requirement to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density Based on our observations the over excavated
building subgrades compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
appeared to be firm and unyielding The select borrow below the foundations and slabs
was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density For pavement
the subgrades select borrow subbase and base course fill materials were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 40 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
