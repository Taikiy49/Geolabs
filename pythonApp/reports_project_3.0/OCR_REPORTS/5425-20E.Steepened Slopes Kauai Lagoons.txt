Project Considerations
The Kauai Lagoons project site is in the Nawiliwili area of Lihue on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii As part of the redevelopment master plan the Mass Grading phase will
include site grading at the future Marriott Vacation Club International
Club RCC and Ritz Carlton Residences Townhomes developments
Based on the grading plans cuts of up to about 10 to 12 feet deep will be required
to achieve design grades along Road C In addition fills of up to about 12 to 14 feet high
will be placed at the MVCI building pad areas Therefore we understand grade
differences of up to about 25 feet in height will be required to achieve the design grades
between the makai side of the MVCI development and Road C
In general fill slopes exposing the on site soils and fill slopes constructed of the
re compacted on site materials should be designed with a slope inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter However due to the limited space in this
area we understand it is desired to construct some steepened cut and fill slopes Based

Steepened Slopes
Based on the information provided the finished grade at Road C is at about
78 feet Mean Sea Level MSL We envision grade differences of up to about 25 feet high
will be required between the MVCI development and Road C Based on the available
subsurface information borings drilled by our office for the design of the various
developments the on site materials generally consist of stiff to hard clayey silts We also
envision the excavated on site materials will be used as a source of fill materials
In general we believe a cut and fill slope inclination of up to may be
achieved with the provision of some ground reinforcements and special erosion control
measures Based on the available systems in Hawaii we envision the anchored wire
mesh system consisting of a wire mesh cover reinforced with grouted soil anchors should
provide the erosion control measure and ground reinforcements for the steepened cut
and fill slopes In addition a layer of erosion control matting should be provided below
the anchored wire mesh system for temporary erosion control measure while the ground
cover establishes for the permanent erosion control measure
Based on our experience with the system we believe the anchored wire mesh
should provide positive erosion control measures for the steepened slopes For the
steepened slope system with an anchored wire mesh system slope benches may be
omitted for slopes up to 30 feet in vertical height A typical detail showing the anchored
wire mesh system is provided on the Typical Steepened Slope Detail Plate 2
The anchored wire mesh system is an active slope stabilization system where an
alloyed high tensile steel wire mesh is anchored onto the slope face by applying a defined
torque force in the grouted soil anchors and spike plates The anchored wire mesh
system should be designed to withstand the gravity forces from the near surface soil
horizon to reduce the potential for the soils to move under the anchored wire mesh
system Based on our evaluation we recommend using an 8 foot by 8 foot anchor
spacing arranged in a triangular grid pattern with 10 foot deep nails for design of the
anchored wire mesh system at the project site

affects the performance of the steepened slope system The following summarizes the
general construction sequence and construction considerations

3 Excavate dells Excavate dells a small depression at each grouted soil

4 Install grouted soil anchors Anchor heads should not project above the
slope line The first row of grouted soil anchors should be installed at the top
of slope about 4 feet beyond the crest of the slope The grouted soil anchor
should be installed at maximum 8 feet on centers along the slope crest The
grouted anchor bars should be spaced at maximum 8 feet on center both
horizontally and vertically along the slope face
The grouted soil anchors should consist of minimum 1 25 inch diameter
solid steel reinforcing bars installed in a minimum 4 inch diameter drilled
hole Based on our preliminary analysis grouted soil anchors should be at
least 10 feet deep After installation of the reinforcing bars the annular
space should be grouted The grout should be non shrink and should have
a minimum unconfined compressive strength of 4 000 pounds per square
inch psi or greater at 28 days The Grouted Soil Anchor Details are

a Proof test grouted soil anchors The grouted soil anchors are designed
based on an allowable pullout capacity of 10 kips Therefore the proof
tests of the grouted soil anchors should be tested to at least 150 percent
of the allowable pullout capacity
6 Hydromulch slopes Groundcover seedlings may be applied to the erosion
control matting to create an aesthetically green appearance
7 Install erosion control matting Erosion control matting should be North
American Green C350 or equivalent material
8 Install wire mesh The wire mesh is draped over the prepared slope with the
completed grouted soil anchors Then a spike plate is placed over the wire
mesh at the grouted soil anchors Once the mesh is fastened to the slope by

Due to the potentially corrosive environment the anchored wire mesh and
hardware should be corrosion resistant Therefore we propose the
anchored wire mesh and hardware be galvanized as a minimum or the
hardware be made of stainless steel We also recommend the anchored
wire mesh system and all associated hardware such as the high strength
steel wire mesh compression claws and spike plates be powder coated
with a black pigment to blend into the environment
It should be noted that installation of the grouted soil anchors and wire mesh should
proceed as soon as practical after grading of the slopes to reduce the potential for
surface raveling and erosion during construction It should be noted that the contractor
should be solely responsible for the maintenance of the steepened slopes during
construction until stabilized by the anchored wire mesh
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from literature research field borings drilled and bulk samples
collected Variations of the subsurface conditions between and beyond the borings and
bulk samples may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided herein
