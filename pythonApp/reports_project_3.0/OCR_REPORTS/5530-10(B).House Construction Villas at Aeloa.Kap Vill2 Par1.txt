PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site known as Parcel 1 is at Kapolei Village 2 within the existing
Villages of Kapolei development in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
planned development consists of nine residential buildings generally up to two stories in
height Slabs on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will support the
proposed residential structures The project also includes the installation of new utilities
including sewer water and drainage lines as well as construction of new roadways to
serve the residential development A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown
on Plates 1 and 2 respectively

HOUSE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the house grading and utility installation work for the
Kapolei Village 2 Parcel 1 The Villas at A eloa project from to
The house grading work consisted of footing excavation and footing
and slab on grade subgrade preparation for the project The utility installation work
consisted of trench backfill of plumbing lines within the house pad areas Reef
Development of Hawaii Inc performed the house grading and utility backfilling work
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry density and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests were
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The
compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plate 4 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the house grading and utility installation work for
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs
and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface runoff away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation including a licensed geotechnical engineer should be
obtained in the planning design and construction of future houses and improvements
