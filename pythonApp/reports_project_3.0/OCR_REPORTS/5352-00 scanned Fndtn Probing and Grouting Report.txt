This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing and grouting operations for the Hawaii Army National Guard 93 Civil Support

35 feet in dimension The probing and grouting work was performed by Geolabs Inc in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated
Probing and Grouting Operations
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to evaluate the presence of
cavities and or voids and to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting
from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the main building
Probing and grouting along the wall column footing locations were performed on
10 and 15 2004 A representative from Pacific Geotechnical Engineers
geotechnical engineering consultant was present on site during the probing and grouting
operation The locations of the probeholes designated in the project structural plans were
determined and marked in the field by a representative from Kaikor Construction building
subcontractor The approximate locations of the probeholes are shown on the Probe
Hole Location Plan Plate 1
A total of 34 probeholes approximately 5 inches in diameter were drilled along the
wall column footing using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Two of the probeholes Probe Hole Nos 5A and 9A were additional probes drilled
between Probe Hole and 9 10 respectively The probeholes were drilled to
depths of approximately 15 feet below the proposed bottom of footing elevation The total
