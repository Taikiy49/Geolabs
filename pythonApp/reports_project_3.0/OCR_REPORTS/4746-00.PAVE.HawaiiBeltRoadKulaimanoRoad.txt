SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be required for the project at Hawaii Belt Road and Kulaimano
Road Due to the high rainfall the shallow perched groundwater conditions encountered
during our field exploration and the silty and clayey nature of the surface soils
encountered at the project site we believe that a permeable drainage layer should be
incorporated into the pavement design of the new roadway Therefore we have only
considered pavement structural sections with a permeable drainage layer in our analyses
for the project
Our field exploration encountered volcanic ash soils at the ground surface along a
portion of the roadway alignment We anticipate that the volcanic ash soils may be
encountered at or near the ground surface between Station 145 05 and Station 155 00
In general we recommend that the volcanic ash soils encountered at the pavement
subgrade be over excavated to remove the volcanic ash soils or up to 36 inches below the
pavement structural section The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with
structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fill material compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Based on the traffic data provided the R Value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses performed we recommend that a flexible pavement structural section
be used for the new pavements Based on our analyses we recommend that a flexible
pavement section consisting of 4 inches of AC placed on 8 inches of ACB over 6 inches of
UTPB over 6 inches of ASB be used for the design of pavements for the proposed project
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures In addition we recommend that a
pavement edge drain system hydraulically connected to the untreated permeable base
be provided along the edges of the new pavement areas to collect infiltrated water into the
pavement structural section The text of this report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and specific design recommendations

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes including the following Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea some activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and is considered to be dormant and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both are still active The project site is situated on the southeast
flank of the Mauna Kea Shield Volcano
The majority of the surface rock exposures at Mauna Loa consist of lavas and
volcanic deposits belonging to the Laupahoehoe Volcanic Series and the older Hamakua
Volcanic Series Pleistocene age In addition a regional ash deposit identified as Pahala
Ash Pleistocene age forms a soil mantle overlying and interbedded within the rocks of
the Hamakua Volcanic Series The ash deposits typically occur as kipukas isolated
deposits with some significant accumulations ranging up to about 30 feet in thickness
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of ash cinder and
Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and when the moisture content is high
enough it becomes thixotrophic i e it loses significant strength when remolded or
disturbed Therefore the typical Pahala Ash may be potentially liquefiable during seismic
events
2 2 Climatic Conditions
The Island of Hawaii is located in the tropics in the Pacific Ocean Hawaii is
characterized by mild temperatures throughout the year moderate humidity persistent
northeasterly trade winds significant differences in rainfall within short distances and
infrequent severe storms
The temperatures on the Island of Hawaii typically range between 65 degrees
Fahrenheit and 85 degrees Fahrenheit The maximum temperature recorded on the
Island of Hawaii was approximately 100 degrees Fahrenheit at Pahala Elevation
870 feet Mean Sea Level MSL on and the lowest temperature

The annual rainfall on the Island of Hawaii averages about 25 to 30 inches The
rainfall may be as much as fifteen times the average annual rainfall over the upper
mountain slopes and crests on the windward side and may be less than one third the
average annual rainfall in the leeward lowland areas The leeward areas obtain their
rainfall mainly from a few winter storms Therefore the rainfall is usually seasonal and
the summers are dry In the wetter regions the rainfall comes from both winter storms
and trade wind showers
The project site is located in the District of South Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The
average annual rainfall near the project site is about 150 inches The precipitation at the
project site may be considered high

3 3 Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on our field exploration experience in the area and the current design
concept we anticipate that the pavement subgrade soils will consist of new compacted fill
materials the existing fill materials and the volcanic ash soils encountered during our field
exploration We anticipate that the near surface volcanic ash soils would be encountered
between Station 145 05 and Station 155 00 In general if the volcanic ash soils are
encountered at the pavement subgrades we recommend that the volcanic ash soils
should be over excavated to remove the volcanic ash soils or to a depth of up to 36 inches
below the pavement structural section
The resulting excavation should be replaced with structural fill material compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil determined in accordance with AASHTO Test Method T 180 ASTM D 1557
The structural fill material should consist of well graded select granular material with a
maximum particle size of about 6 inches
Based on our experience with similar subgrade materials we believe that the
structural fill materials and the existing granular fill materials would exhibit a Resistance
R value equivalent to that of aggregate subbase material Therefore a design R value of
60 was adopted in our pavement analyses for the subgrade material If site grading
exposes materials other than those assumed in our analyses additional tests should be
performed to confirm and or revise the recommended pavement sections for actual field
conditions

Preparation
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or disposed of in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials Soft
and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to
receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose dense materials
The resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted structural fill materials
The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

After clearing and grubbing the pavement subgrade areas exposing the existing
granular fills should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory drum roller for a
minimum of eight passes to provide a firm and unyielding surface prior to the placement
of the aggregate subbase layer
It should be noted that volcanic ash soils may be encountered at or near the
pavement subgrade elevation between about Station 145 05 and Station 155 00
Where volcanic ash soils are exposed at the pavement subgrade level the volcanic ash
soils should be over excavated to remove the volcanic ash or up to 36 inches below the
pavement structural section The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted structural fill material The soils at the bottom of the structural fill layer
should be proof rolled to provide a firm and unyielding base and should be lined with a
permeable separator geotextile fabric before placement of the structural fill materials
Therefore provisions should be made in the contract documents to remove the volcanic
ash soils and replace the resulting excavation with structural fill materials with geotextile
fabric to be determined in the field during construction
Due to the potentially thixotropic nature of the near surface volcanic ash soils use
of the vibratory action of the compactor should be carefully controlled to avoid disturbing
the volcanic ash soils In addition moisture conditioning of the volcanic ash soils should
be carefully controlled to avoid over wetting of the soils for the condition where deep
volcanic ash soils are encountered below the subgrade level It should be noted that the
near surface volcanic ash soils will likely be sensitive to the moisture content and difficult
to achieve a firm and unyielding subgrade condition Therefore additional compaction
effort with proper moisture control may be necessary to meet the specified compaction
criteria

3 6 Fill Materials
In general the excavated on site granular materials may be re used as a source
of general fill and backfill materials up to the bottom of the aggregate subbase layer
provided that they are free of organic materials and contain no lumps or rock fragments
greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The excavated volcanic soils should not be
used as fill material and should be disposed of properly off site The on site granular fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We anticipate that imported structural fill material will be required for the project
Imported structural fill material should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The material should have a CBR
value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less In
addition the material should have a minimum R value of 60 or more mported fill
materials should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery
to the project site for the intended use

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off the
pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction To collect infiltrated water into the
pavement structural section we recommend that a pavement edge drain system be
provided along the sides of the new travel way
We envision that an edge drain will be needed to collect and daylight the infiltrated
water into the pavement structural section from the untreated permeable base layer
The edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations facing
down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable separator non woven filter
fabric conforming to Section 648 of the HSS should be placed around the untreated
permeable base to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage material as
shown on Plate 3 In addition a permeable separator should also be placed between the
untreated permeable base and aggregate subbase Care should be exercised when
placing the subbase base and or construction of the surface course to avoid damaging
the edge drain collector pipe A typical edge drain detail is provided on Plate 3 The edge
drains should be daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for proper discharge

Laboratory Testing

were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soil The
results of the test are presented on Plate A 1
