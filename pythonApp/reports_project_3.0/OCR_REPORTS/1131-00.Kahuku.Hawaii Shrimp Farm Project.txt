As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the existing subsurface con
ditions in order to provide recommendations for site grading
design and construction of the foundations for the proposed
structures
Our investigation consisted of ten 10 borings drilled in the
proposed locations of the four 4 support facility buildings
and parking area seven 7 borings drilled in the proposed
three 2 hectare production ponds and visual observations of the
Site
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appendices
of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in Kahuku on the northern coastal
tip of Oahu Hawaii Access to the site is off the existing
Nudist Camp Road leading to the now abandoned Kahuku Airstrip
as shown on Plate 1
The area is trapezoidal in shape and generally level with
ground surface elevation averaging about 9 feet in the eastern
half of the site sloping to a depressed area in the western
half with a surface elevation of about 4 feet to as low as 2
feet in the lowlying areas
In the upper eastern portion of the site there exists two 2
dwellings one of which is currently occupied Old concrete
building foundations are still present along the southern
boundary with numerous abandoned vehicles and debris A
cultivated watermelon farm which we believe will be abandoned
soon is located in the northeastern corner of the site

There are two asphalt paved roads running in an east west
direction along the southern proposed shrimp farm development
area In the western portion of the site is the lowlying
depressed area which is approximately 1000 feet long and 570
feet wide The ground surface elevation in this lowlying area
ranges from 2 to 4 feet Ponding of water was noted in the
softer wet ground area while firm soil crust exists in the
drier higher ground areas
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The current plan indicates the construction of four 4 struc
tures for broodstock maturation and larval rearing utility
finishing operation and feed mill In addition there will be
three 2 hectare production units with C P E liner resting on
soil cement subgrade Asphaltic concrete paved roadways and
parking area are planned near the entry at the southeastern
boundary of the project site
Broodstock Building
The broodstock building will be approximately 204 by 288
feet in plan dimension with half of the structure having
Shallow footings and a concrete slab resting on compacted
fill The other half will contain lined concrete raceways

GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions at the project site are the result of a
series of glacioeustatic sea level changes during the Quater
nary Period Stearns 1978
Most of the site is underlain by coral algal reef materials
deposited during the Waimanalo 25 feet high stand of the sea
In some areas of the site this reef material is overlain by
somewhat cemented dunes of calcareous sands deposited during
the Mamals 350 feet low stand of the sea
In the low areas of the site the Waimanalo reef material is
generally mantled by a thin layer of recent alluvium or recent
organic marsh soils
Some amount of fill were encountered on the project site which
were probably placed during the site s former use as a military
installation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
Building Area
es
Minimal site grading is recommended in the eastern portion
of the site where the proposed Support facilities will be
constructed Due to the loose nature of an underlying sand
stratum at relatively shallow depths of about 4 5 to 6
feet it is recommended that only grubbing leveling and
removal of the topsoil for this area be performed In this
fashion building foundations would be Supported by a
minimum 3 feet of dense coral sand
Well graded granular material such as on site coral sand
should be used for fill Fill should be placed in 9 inch
loose lifts compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum
dry density as established by ASTM D 1557
Production Units
It is anticipated that three pads of descending elevation
will be utilized for each two hectare production unit It
is for this reason we have divided the area into two sec
tions utilizing the limits of the lowlying area as the
imaginary dividing line

West Portion
In the lowlying area we believe it would be advan
tageous to remove any soft overlying organic silts
during the clearing and grubbing of the site The
material could be stockpiled for any future use in
landscaping or topsoil Although this would require
construction equipment to operate close to or at the
existing sea level we do not foresee any major
problem due to medium dense underlying coral sand
layers
Removal of 1 to 1 5 feet of organic silts would
reduce any anticipated large settlements or the need
to surcharge the area before actual building con
struction could start
Well graded granular material such as on site coral
sand should be used for fill They should be placed
in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557

East Portion
No specific grading plan was available during our
investigation However it is anticipated that this
area will be a borrow site for the lowlying sections

therefore it will be necessary to scarify the upper

maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557
There will be enough cementation due to the recon
paction of the surface coral sand to minimize
unravelling during lining of the production race
ways Depending on the degree of unravelling that
could be withstood due to normal maintenance and
harvesting it may be advantageous to permanantly
stabilize the surface coral sand with a minimum 6
inches of soil cement cap for long term use

Foundations
For the relatively light building loads anticipated shallow
continuous footing foundations bearing on the upper coral sand
layer may be used An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f
may be utilized in the dense sand The bearing capacity given
is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary lateral loads wind or seismic are
considered A minimum footing width of 24 inches is recon
mended
Footings should have a minimum embedment of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade It is recommended that the foot
ings be placed as high as practicable to reduce the footing
load imposed in the relatively loose layer encountered at about
4 5 to 6 feet
Probing of all major footings should be done to insure a mini
mum three foot of dense coral sand at intervals of 10 feet on
center for continuous footings Should loose material be
encountered within this 3 foot minimum the footing must extend
beyond this loose layer into dense coral sand where practical
Otherwise removal of loose material is recommended and back
filling with a compacted well graded coral sand should be
utilized

W O 1131 00 Page 13
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of the footing excava
tion should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557
Estimated settlements are on the order of about one half of an
inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Pavement
Gravelly coral sand is anticipated at the road subgrade level
which should be compacted at a minimum of 95 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 The preliminary pave
ment design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness over
a Prepared Subgrade
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers
medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided for
adjacent to paved areas

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading probe drilling foot
ing excavations and backfill operations be monitored and
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing and compaction have been obtained and the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our sub
surface investigation The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon the above inspections
Any imported f i1l1 material should be a well graded granular
material and meet the approval of the soils engineer A
sample of any proposed import material should be submitted
to the soils engineer for testing and approval a minimum of
10 working days prior to its intended delivery to the pro
ject site

APPENDIX B
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are
presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Six sieve analysis tests were performed on selected
samples to determine the grain size distribution The summary
of test results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed
on a sample of coral sand to evaluate its pavement support
characteristics The test result is presented on Plate B 2
One consolidation test and five one inch ring swell
test were performed on the soft clayey silt soils to evaluate
its shrinkage and swelling characteristics The consolidation
curve is presented on Plate B 3 with the summary of swell tests
on B 4
