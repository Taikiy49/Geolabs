Dear Mr Masumoto
In accordance with the project specifications we have been retained as the Quality
Control Specialist pertaining to related geotechnical work activities for the above referenced
project Our responsibilities include the attendance of the Coordination and Mutual
Understanding Meeting Quality Control Meeting and performance of the three phases of
control Preparatory Initial and Follow Up for the project construction We have fulfilled the
requirements of the Preparatory Phase with the attendance of the site meeting held on
August 18 1999 This letter summarizes our field observations and presents the field
records documented during the Initial Phase of the predrilling and pile driving operations for
the subject project
Based on our field observations conducted on August 23 and 24 for Pile Nos 8
through 12 the proposed fender pile locations were generally predrilled to an elevation of
39 feet US Navy Datum 100 feet is equivalent to Mean Lower Low Water using a
heavyweight H pile beam with a 20 inch square cutter head advanced by a vibratory hammer
manufactured by ICE Plumbness of the H pile beam was checked and maintained
throughout the predrilling operations Based on the timber debris stuck at the cutter head
and floating on the water it appears that the cutter head was successful in cutting through
the timber pile obstructions at the fender pile locations
Subsequent to the predrilling operation the concrete fender piles were stabbed
seated and then driven by a Delmag D 30 single action diesel hammer Plumbness and
alignment of the concrete fender piles were checked throughout the pile driving operations
The pile driving operation was terminated when the pile tip achieved Elevation 39 feet The
individual pile driving records for Pile Nos 8 through 12 are attached for reference
