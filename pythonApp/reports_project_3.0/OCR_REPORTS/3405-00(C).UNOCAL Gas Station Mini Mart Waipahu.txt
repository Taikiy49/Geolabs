INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed UNOCAL Gas Station Mini Mart project located at the Royal
Kunia Road at Times Supermarket on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading and
excavation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report

We understand that it is proposed to construct a new gas station mini mart
located at the Times Supermarket Shopping Center on Royal Kunia Road in the
Waipahu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided the

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 5
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed
a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is
located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with depth

Foundations
The proposed gas station mini mart facility will consist of a mini mart several
pump islands a car wash facility and two underground fuel storage tanks Based on
the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 8
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the stiff near surface fills This allowable bearing pressure is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as
temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the total footing
settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch
with differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the fills and or residual soils These values assume that the
concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished
grade should be neglected
Uplift loads especially for the canopy of the pump islands may be resisted by
a combination of the following 1 dead weight of the structure 2 weight of backfill
material above the footing where applicable and 3 and side shear between the sides
of the footing and surrounding soil Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight
of the backfill above the footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 105 p c f For
side shear between the sides of the footings and the adjacent soils a shear value of
150 p s f may be used to resist uplift loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 9
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings
are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should
extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the
bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
structures such as the mini mart car wash and pump islands The near surface soils
encountered at the proposed site consist of silty and clayey fill soils which exhibit a
slight expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore we
recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared and kept moist prior to placement
of cushion fill and slab concrete The subgrades for slabs on grades should be
scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 10
above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs mini mart we recommend that the
concrete slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion
fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab
cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and
recompaction of the existing subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on
grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable
membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture
vapor through the concrete slab in order to promote more uniform curing of the slab
concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a
2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab
concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the car wash
facility and pump islands we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer
of base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of
the 4 inch cushion fill layer and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth
of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction For design
of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i
may be used for the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 11
to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate grading with cuts and fills on the order of 1 to 2 feet and
excavation and backfill of up to about 15 feet relative to the existing ground surface
may be expected for the proposed development
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe wheiher the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly mois
ture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pump islands car wash facility
and pavements the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 C Page 12
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior
to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The on site silty and clayey soils generated from the excavation may be reused
as a source of fill and backfill materials The on site silty and clayey soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V
or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched
lf imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain
between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should
be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use Imported materials should be brought
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction

Excavation
In order to install the underground fuel storage tanks we anticipate that
excavations on the order of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface will be
required for the project construction In general the method and equipment to be used
for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and
safety considerations In general our field exploration encountered stiff to very stiff silty
and clayey fill materials underlain by stiff to hard residual saprolite soils We anticipate
that conventional excavation techniques may be used for the proposed excavation
However it should be noted that hard layers and localized hard zones may be
encountered occasionally in the proposed excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site was underlain by stiff fills and
residual saprolite soils Therefore if construction space is available it is our opinion
that an open cut may be used for the excavation of the proposed underground fuel
storage tanks A slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter may be used for the temporary
cut
If the open cut method is not practical for the proposed excavation we
recommend that a temporary shoring system be used for the proposed excavation
The temporary shoring should conform to the requirements of local state and federal
regulations
Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment traffic or sloping ground
conditions adjacent to the excavations should be added to the design lateral earth
pressures Stockpiling of excavated on site and or imported materials within a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the excavation
should be avoided

Pavements
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the driveway and parking areas for
the proposed project We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We
have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the silty and clayey soils encountered in the borings with a minimum
California Bearing Ratio CBR of 1 5 and swell of less than 1 percent Based on the
above assumptions we recommend the following pavement section be used for
preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
12 0 Inch Basalt Subbase Course 95 percent relative compaction
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction If pumping
of the subgrade occurs during construction the subgrade should be proof rolled to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction After proof rolling a layer of filter fabric
such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement
of the subbase and base course The subbase and base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design
section The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage will be
provided for areas adjacent to the pavements

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs
extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below the base and or subbase course

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the
pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface silty and
clayey soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the
footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Four one inch ring sweil tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results are summarized
on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade
support Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 3
