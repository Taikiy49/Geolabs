Sheetpile Design Memorandum
Torson Ala Moana Tower
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
Subsequent to our meeting on the following is a summary of our
recommendations pertaining to sheetpile retaining wall design
1 We believe passive resistance will be increased if the steel sheetpile can
penetrate into the relatively competent and hard coral ledge encountered
at depths of about 25 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface on the
eastern half Diamond Head side of the site A typical lateral earth
pressure diagram with equivalent fluid pressure is shown on the attached
Plate 1 However it should be noted that steel sheetpiles of adequate
thickness and a heavy vibratory hammer will be required to penetrate the
sheetpiles into the hard coral ledge by 6 to 12 inches In addition hard
sheetpile driving is anticipated Provision of more lateral braces and raker
supports when alternate sections or several sections of the steel sheetpile
cannot penetrate into the hard coral ledge should be considered in the
sheetpile wall design
2 On the western half Ewa side of the site medium dense coral sand and
gravel were encountered at depths of about 25 to 45 feet instead of the
hard coral ledge It is our opinion that longer sheetpiles can be driven
around the western half perimeter of the site A typical lateral earth
pressure diagram with equivalent fluid pressures is shown on the attached
Plate 2
