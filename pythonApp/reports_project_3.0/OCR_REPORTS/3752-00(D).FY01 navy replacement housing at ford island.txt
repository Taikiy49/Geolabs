INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed FY01 Navy Replacement Housing at Ford Island project located
within the Pearl Harbor area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the preliminary design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and preliminary
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is located on the northeastern side of Ford Island in Pearl Harbor on
the south central coastal plain of Oahu Hawaii A complex series of erosional and
depositional events resulting from glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional
volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch resulted in the accumulation of alluvial
deposits in a lagoonal deltaic environment in Pearl Harbor during higher stands of the sea
Ford Island is a remnant of the hard reef rock that once covered a larger portion of the
embayment and subsequently was carved away in many places by streams during lower
stands of the sea During the Recent Epoch the sea has adjusted to its present level and
additional sediments have continued to accumulate along the lochs and channels of the
harbor Land development in the past 40 to 60 years have added man made fills along the
shoreline
SITE DESCRIPTION
Ford Island is located in the East Loch of Pearl Harbor within the core of the Pearl
Harbor Naval Complex on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The island approximately 450 acres
in area is separated from the Pearl City Peninsula to the north Naval Supply Center to the
east Navy Shipyard to the south and Waipio Peninsula to the west by a ship channel ranging
between about 300 to over 500 meters wide The island is dominated by an old airfield
several military training facilities a data processing office two bachelor quarters facilities and
approximately 50 family housing units Access to Ford Island is provided via a low profile
bridge with a floating retractable pontoon connecting the main side to the north of Bowfin Park
and the east side of Ford Island

House Foundations
We understand that slab on grade foundations will be utilized during the future house
construction Based on the competent subsurface conditions encountered at the project site
we believe that the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills would provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed residential structures over the majority of the project site
For foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills allowable
bearing pressures of about 120 to may be used for preliminary design
of the foundations The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
GEOLABS INC
W O 3752 00 D Page 10
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft
and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations the material
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
should be backfilled with select granular fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM
D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The perimeter thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of
300 mm below the lowest adjacent exterior grade In general the base of the thickened edge
for slab on grade foundations should have a minimum width of 300 mm Footings located
adjacent to slopes or on slopes should be embedded to a depth to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of at least 2 5 meters measured from the outside edge of the
footing to the face of the slope Footings located adjacent to planned retaining walls should
be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations Foundations
located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a 45 degree
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or should be
extended to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated light design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills as recommended
herein to be less than 25 mm Differential settlements between adjacent footings supported
on similar materials should be on the order of about 15 mm

0 30 to 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils or compacted

Slabs On Grade
We envision that concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the floor slabs garage and
carport slabs driveways and sidewalk walkway areas As previously mentioned some of the
near surface clayey soils may exhibit moderate to high potential for shrink swell when
subjected to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress
to the future slabs on grade resulting from the potential swelling of the surface clayey soils and
to provide for uniform support we believe that non expansive select granular fill materials
should be provided for a minimum depth of 150 to 300 mm below the slab cushion
The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprints a minimum
of 600 mm
Prior to placement of the select granular fill the clayey subgrades should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 200 mm moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The select
granular fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying
potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey
GEOLABS INC
W O 3752 00 D Page 12
subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction of drying and subsequent wetting of the subgrade
soils should reduce the potential impact of shrink swell effects on the slab caused by potential
heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted The select granular fill should
be moisture conditioned placed in level loose lifts not exceeding 200 mm and compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction
To reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils the perimeter footing
excavations should be backfilled as soon as practicable after concrete placement
The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom longitudinal
steel reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should be connected by
stir ups A structural engineer should be consulted for design details of this thickened edge
which is intended to function both as a moisture barrier and as a perimeter wall footing
In general we recommend that a minimum 100 mm thick cushion layer consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to provide
uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary moisture break To reduce future
moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier
is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Approximately 50 mm of moist fine sand
may be considered above the moisture barrier to provide protection of the moisture barrier and
to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the 50 mm of moist fine sand above the
moisture barrier is optional and will be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can
better mask minor slab cracking
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage and carport
slabs and driveway areas a 150 mm layer of aggregate subbase is recommended in lieu of
the 100 mm cushion fill layer vapor barrier and 50 mm sand layer The aggregate subbase
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative

compaction The 150 to 300 mm select granular fill layer recommended for slab on grade
construction should also be provided for the garage and carport slabs and driveway areas
Exterior concrete sidewalk walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 150 to 300 mm cushion layer consisting of the select granular fill
material be provided below the concrete slab The select granular fill cushion layer should be
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography we envision that minimal site grading work will be
required for construction of the new improvements at the subject site In general the area
within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of
earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures and pavements to be demolished
should be completely removed Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 150 to 200 mm level loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping lean
concrete under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition areas beneath future new slabs should be
over excavated to provide at least 150 to 300 mm of compacted non expansive select
granular fill material below the bottom of the slab cushion If zones of soft loose or saturated
soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level further over excavation may be
required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting over excavation should be
backfilled with compacted non expansive select granular fill material
The over excavated subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth of
200 mm moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
GEOLABS INC
W O 3752 00 D Page 18
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction If the subgrade soils are pumping
during subgrade preparation the pumping subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment
Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi
500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement of subsequent lifts
of fill
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by slab
cushion fill or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade
we recommend that the soil be re scarified and prepared as recommended above Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
fill
Non expansive select granular fill material required under the slab areas should consist
of imported granular material such as select borrow crushed coral or basalt gravel The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 75 mm in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent passing
the No 200 sieve Structural fills required underneath the footing foundations
along the shoreline area should also meet the above requirements Aggregate base course
and aggregate subbase required for the project should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
and should conform to Sections 703 06 and 703 17 of the State of Hawaii Standard
Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994
Non expansive select granular fill material required for the capping layer below the new
sidewalks and floor slabs should be moisture conditioned placed in level lifts not exceeding
200 mm in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 200 mm in loose thickness and

in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Pavement Design
New asphaltic concrete pavements will be required for the proposed replacement
housing project As previously mentioned some of the subgrade soils at the site may exhibit
shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore we recommend that
the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with 150 to 300 mm of aggregate
subbase to reduce the potential for distress resulting from the swelling of the potentially
expansive subgrade soils Considering the light to medium traffic anticipated for the new
pavements we believe the following preliminary pavement section may be used for budgeting
purposes

compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Paved areas should be sloped

Utility Trenches
We envision that new utility lines such as sewer and water lines and _ utility
connections will be required for the project In general granular bedding consisting of 150 mm
of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 300 mm above the pipes It is critical
to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper
backfill material around the pipes and placement could result in settlement of the backfill and
damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 300 mm above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular fill materials The
on site clayey soils may be used as trench backfill in landscape areas only The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum
200 mm level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 1 meter of the trench backfill below the pavement
finished grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Existing Underground Utilities
Existing underground utility lines may traverse the subject housing development
Therefore we recommend that all existing utilities within the project limits be accurately
located by the contractor prior to construction The contractor has the sole responsibility to
devise and implement an effective program to protect the utilities during construction
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with select granular fill materials The select granular fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in 200 mm
level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided In addition planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration into the subsurface
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of our
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for significant water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations
and slabs

Field Exploration

total of 30 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 30 The borings were extended to

the near surface soils The approximate locations of the borings drilled and bulk samples
collected are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers

examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered in the
borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 30

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Eleven 25 mm ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of these tests are
summarized on Plates B 1 and B 2
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on
Plate B 3
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 ASTM D 1140 were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 4
Two direct shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct shear
tests are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
Two consolidation tests with time rates ASTM D 2435 were performed on samples
of the potentially compressible soils to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the
materials encountered Results of the consolidation test are presented on Plates B 7 and B 8
Four laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
The test results are presented on Plates B 9 through B 12
Four Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 13 through B 16
