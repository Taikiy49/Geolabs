SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be constructed as part of the Extension of Kapolei Parkway
Phase 8B project Based on our field exploration we anticipate that the majority of
pavement subgrade soils will generally consist of clayey and silty soils The clayey and
silty subgrade soils are very dry and friable with relatively deep shrinkage cracks To
reduce the potential for future pavement distress due to the presence of shrinkage cracks
in the subgrade soils we recommend that special site preparation requirements for the
soils below the existing ground surface be implemented during construction In general
the special site preparation consists of over excavation or removal of the existing soils to
a minimum depth of 3 feet below the existing ground surface to remove the zone of soil
disturbance due to shrinkage cracks
We envision that the over excavated materials near the existing ground surface
contain appreciable amounts of organic materials Therefore materials containing
appreciable amounts of organic materials should be removed as part of the clearing and
grubbing operations and the resulting soil spoils should be disposed properly off site
or used in landscaped areas if appropriate We estimate that the thickness of the
materials containing appreciable amounts of organic materials may be up to about 12 to
18 inches from the existing ground surface
The over excavated soils below the organic laden materials may be re used as a
source of general fill materials provided they are free of deleterious materials and are
moisture conditioned and compacted as recommended herein After over excavation of
the 36 inches of soil below the existing ground surface the over excavated subgrade
areas should be scarified to a depth of at least 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction
Based on the traffic data provided the R value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses performed we recommend that a flexible pavement structural section
be used for the new pavements for the widened highway The recommended pavement
structural section for new roadway consists of 4 0 inches of asphaltic concrete AC on
9 0 inches of asphalt concrete base ACB over 6 0 inches of untreated permeable base
UTPB and 12 0 inches of aggregate subbase ASB course If the incorporation of a
drainage layer is not practical the pavement design option consisting of 4 inches of AC on
8 0 inches of ACB over 21 inches of ASB may be considered for the project

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was formed by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two main
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau beginning in the mid Pliocene Epoch The older
Waianae Volcano was the first to develop closely followed by the development of the
younger Koolau Volcano For a period of time Oahu was comprised of two separate
islands formed by each of the volcanic centers The volcanoes continued to erupt and
bank against the slopes of each other with the Waianae Volcano terminating its eruptive
activity prior to the cessation of the Koolau Volcano
Following extrusion of the lavas in the early Pleistocene Epoch the island
underwent a long cycle of erosion and weathering thus forming the prominent
ridgelines and summits as we know today During the erosion period Oahu began to
slowly subside more than 350 meters 1 200 feet in elevation resulting in the drowning
and sedimentation of the valleys and the formation of the steep Koolau Pali Coral reefs
continued to grow in the surrounding shallow waters of the island
From the mid to late Pleistocene Epoch the sea level repeatedly rose and fell in
response to global glaciation and the availability of surface waters to sustain the
oceans The various sea level elevations and their representative deposits are known
as stands and include from the oldest to the youngest respectively Kahuku Kahipa
Kaena Laie Waialae Waipio and Waimanalo stands Geologic deposits associated
with the various sea level stands including marine sediments and coral reefs were
deposited and subsequently altered or removed by later sea level fluctuation Therefore
depositional records reflecting the changes in sea level and the occurrence of emerged
coral reef deposits are often incomplete
The project site is situated on the Ewa Plain located to the southeast of the
Waianae Mountain Range The Ewa Plain is a gently sloping alluvial plain formed by the
deposition of alluvial clays and silts derived from the weathering of basalt rock further
up slope The alluvial deposits accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments

considered in the pavement analyses The flexible pavement sections presented in this
report were generally determined based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of

Material Testing and Research Branch The pavement design methodology is based on
the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the California Department of
Transportation Caltrans
The design procedures for the rigid pavement sections generally were based on
the design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design
for Concrete Highway and Street Pavements

3 3 Design Subgrade Conditions
We envision that the majority of the new pavements for the highway widening will
generally be constructed on the compacted fill consisting of the excavated on site
materials or in a cut condition exposing the on site clayey and silty soils
We performed our pavement designs assuming that the pavement subgrade soil
will generally consist of the near surface clayey and silty soils with an average
laboratory Resistance R value of about 9 based on laboratory test results Therefore
an R value of 9 has been adopted for the subgrade soils in our pavement design
analyses The results of the laboratory R value tests are presented on Plates A 1
through A 8 of Appendix A If site grading exposes soils other than those assumed in
the pavement design additional tests should be performed to confirm and or revise the
recommended pavement structural section for actual field conditions
Based on our laboratory tests the on site clayey and silty soils generally have
relatively low CBR values and may exhibit slight expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore we also recommend that a minimum of 18 inches of
subbase materials also may include permeable base materials be provided below the
concrete pavements for the rigid pavement sections

with relatively deep shrinkage cracks Based on field observations we noted that
substantial shrinkage cracks on the order of about 2 to 3 feet deep are present at the

resulting in large fluctuations of the soil moisture content Because of these conditions
at the existing ground surface we recommend that special site preparation procedures
be implemented during the earthwork construction of the project

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils It should be noted that the
clayey and silty subgrade soils are susceptible to softening and loss of strength upon
saturation Therefore special attention should be given to the surface and subsurface
drainage of the pavements As a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped and
drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to
appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the
site during or after construction To collect infiltrated water into the pavement structural
section we recommend that a pavement edge drain system hydraulically connected to
the untreated permeable base be provided along the sides of the new roadway
We envision that an edge drain will be needed to collect and discharge infiltrated
water into the pavement structural section from the untreated permeable base layer In
general the edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with
perforations facing down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable
separator non woven filter fabric conforming to Section 648 of the HSS should be
placed around the untreated permeable base to reduce the potential for migration of
fines into the drainage material as shown on the Typical Edge Drain Detail Plate 3
