In accordance with our service agreement we observed the static load test test
pile installation and production pile installation for the augered cast in place ACIP pile
foundations for the NICU PICU Building project In addition we also observed installation
of production drilled shaft foundations This letter report summarizes our geotechnical
observations during the ACIP pile and drilled shaft foundation installation for the
NICU PICU Building project
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the
NICU PICU Building project The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor
the geotechnical engineering aspects of the ACIP pile and drilled shaft foundation
installation for conformance with the plans and specifications and to provide subsequent
recommendations as appropriate In addition we provided Special Inspection services
as required by the City and County of Honolulu by observing the foundation installation
for general conformance with the project plans and specifications and subsequent
recommendations and amendments

Punahou Street and Bingham Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The

ACIP PILE LOAD TEST PROGRAM
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design length of the ACIP pile foundation
a load test pile program was implemented The load test pile program included the
installation and observation of four reaction piles and one instrumented load test pile
LT 1 A static pile load test was conducted on the instrumented ACIP pile from April 11 to
The pile load test was conducted on a 18 inch diameter ACIP pile with a
length of approximately 101 feet relative to the existing ground surface
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the concrete pile soil
interface the load test pile was instrumented with ten vibrating wire embedment strain
gages installed at various depths along the length of the load test pile The load test and
reaction piles were sacrificial piles and were not incorporated into the foundation system of
the parking structure
A Manitowac M12000 crane with crane mounted hollow stem auger powered by a
APE75C top drive drill was used to install the load test and reaction piles Multiple
hydraulic concrete pumps were used to pump the grout into the pile system The load test
pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143 Quick Load Test Method to a
maximum test load of about 1 011 kips
Based on the static pile load test results it appears the load test pile behaved like a
friction pile with the majority of the test load being resisted by adhesion between the ACIP
pile surface and the surrounding soils Based on our field observations and the results of
the static pile load test we confirmed the design embedment lengths for the 18 inch
diameter piles could be shortened to 70 and 80 feet below the existing ground surface to
achieve the design compressive load capacity of 300 and 400 kips respectively per ACIP
pile with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

DRILLED SHAFT FOUNDATION INSTALLATION
The drilled shaft foundation installation was conducted from April 25 to May 15
2014 under the near continuous full time observations by a Geolabs field representative
A total of thirteen 36 inch diameter drilled shafts were installed at the project including
two replacement drilled shafts No 330A and 330B installed for one rejected drilled shaft
No 330 The design length of the drilled shafts was 65 feet for the 36 inch diameter
drilled shafts with design compressive load capacity of 400 kips The locations of the
drilled shafts installed are shown on the Foundation Numbering Plan Plate 1 3
During the drilled shaft installation operation logs were maintained for each drilled
shaft which included information pertaining to drilled shaft number date installed depth of
drilled shaft concrete volume used etc The drilled shaft installation summary is presented
