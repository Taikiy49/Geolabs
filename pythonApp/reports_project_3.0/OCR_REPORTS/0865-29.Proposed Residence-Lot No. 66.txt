conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of

grading and compaction test record on the building site
This letter summarizes our observations and recommendations

Foundation
A post and beam foundation system is recommended for this lot
for the following reasons
1 The on site soils have high expansion potential there
fore slab on grade would require special foundation
design including overexcavation of the clay and replace
ment with off site borrow material to reduce the effects
of the underlying expansive soils
2 The lot slopes upward toward the west and it would
require additional slope cutting possibly into rocky
material if a flat area is required for slab on grade
3 Due to the lot slope retaining walls may also be required
on the lower eastern portion of the lot to create a
level area for slab on grade Imported non expansive

Post and Beam
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should be extended through the surface clay soil a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the ground surface or a minimum
of 6 inches into the weathered rock formation The footing
should consist of poured in place concrete An allowable
bearing value of 4 000 p s f may be used on the dense
weathered rock formation and 2 500 p s f on the upper
Silty clay soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the house
to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible sat
uration and settlement or heaving of the foundation soils
Concrete Slab on Grade
If slab on grade is highly desired for this lot special
foundation design is required to reduce the effects of the
on site expansive soil conditions

The expansive clay below and for a distance of 3 feet
around the slab should be removed for a minimum depth of
3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced with
compacted non expansive off site borrow material such as
quarry waste The underlying remaining clay soil should
be scarified moistened to above optimum moisture content
and recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to back
filling with the 3 foot capping of select fill
The excavated clay soil is expansive and therefore it is
not suitable for re use as fill below structures pavement
or as backfill behind retaining walls
The slab on grade thickened edge should extend a minimum
of 8 inches below the outside finished grade
The finished grade outside the slab and pavements should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding conditions near the slab area Also the
owner should be made aware of gutter water which should
be diverted far away from the perimeter footings and walk
ways
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils more than 3 feet thick were encountered in the
eastern portion of the lot driveway area In order to reduce
GEOLABS HAWAII

the expansive effects of the clay it should be kept a minimum
of 2 feet below the dirveway subgrade level Where 2 or more
feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material
should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less
than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement
of the expansive clay with select non expansive material is
required The above requirements should be followed for a
minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
If a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation
and replacement similar to the driveway is recomended to
reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at
about 10 1 to 5 1 H V ratios A cut slope may be required
behind the proposed house
Based on the field observations we anticipate that cuts deeper
than about 2 feet may encounter weathered rock formation in the
western portion of the proposed house while clay soil would be
anticipated in cuts along the eastern portion

A cut slope ratio of 1 1 H V can be utilized in weathered
rock A cut slope ratio of 3 1 H V is recommended for the
upper on site clayey soil
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material If available some of the weathered rock
excavated material smaller than 8 inches could be used in
the fill if it is not contaminated with the clay The clay
soil should not be used for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557

comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi
cations
