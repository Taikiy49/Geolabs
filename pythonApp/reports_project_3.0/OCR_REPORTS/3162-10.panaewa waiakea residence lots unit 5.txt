This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Panaewa Waiakea Residence Lots Unit 5 project located
in South Hilo on the island of Hawaii
PROJECT BACKGROUND
The project site is located in the area of Waiakea and is bounded by Noeau
treet to the north Ohuohu Street to the east Kahaopea Street to the south and
Pililpaa Street to the west as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
We understand that the project site was mass graded in 1992 for the develop
ment of 20 residential lots within the approximately 4 acre site Based on information
provided the mass grading work was performed without the observation of a geo
technical engineer In general the 20 residential lots consist of Lot Nos 1 through 10
and 17 through 20 on the upper section and Lot Nos 11 through 16 on the lower
section Based on the Grading Plan Sheet No 3 of the construction plans excavation
volume of about 14 116 cubic yards and embankment volume of about 14 524 cubic
yards were involved n general grading consisted of cuts on the order of up to 10 feet
and fills on the order of up to 8 feet
Based on the exploratory test pit logs by Ernest K Hirata Associates Inc
shown on Sheet No 8 of the construction plans prior to the mass grading the site was
generally underlain by sandy gravel over basalt formation Based on the grading notes

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to evaluate the quality of the existing
embankment fills at the project site by performing test pits at selected locations and
present our observations and recommendations pertaining to the house pad
construction Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Observation of 13 test pit excavations at the project site to depths ranging
from about 1 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface by a project
engineer from our firm
2 Review of the construction plans and evaluation of the test pit data
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our subsurface exploration consisted of the excavation logging and backfilling
of 13 test pits on March 14th at the project site The test pits were excavated by a
rubber tired Case 580C hopto to depths of about 1 to 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The test pit locations were measured from the property pins which were

At the lower section lots Lot Nos 11 through 16 our test pits generally
encountered a surficial layer of fill material consisting of loose and poorly graded bony
basalt cobbles and gravels with occasional boulders varying in thickness from 1 to
2 feet Below the loose surficial fill material dense and well graded basalt gravels and
cobbles and or basalt formation were encountered to the maximum excavated depth
of 4 feet
At the remaining lots on the upper section our test pits generally encountered
a surficial layer of fill material consisting of loose and poorly graded bony basalt
cobbles and gravels overlying dense compacted fill material and or dense basalt
formation extending to the maximum excavated depth of 5 feet The loose surficial fill
layer generally ranges between to 1 feet thick At Lot No 17 the loose surficial fill
layer was approximately 3 feet thick
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pit excavations at the time of our
field exploration For detailed descriptions of the subsurface condition encountered
please refer to the Test Pit Logs presented on Plates 2 1 through 2 7
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our subsurface exploration the lower section lots Lot Nos 11 through
16 generally consist of approximately 1 to 2 feet thick of loose surficial fill material
consisting of basalt cobbles and gravels Significant void spaces were noted in the
loose surficial fill materials
At the upper section lots Lot Nos 1 through 10 and 17 through 20 the loose
surficial fill materials generally vary from to 1 feet thick At Lot No 17 the loose
surficial fill layer was about 3 feet thick Significant void spaces were also noted in the
loose fill layer
Based on our field observation of the void spaces it is our opinion that the
above mentioned loose surficial fill materials do not contain adequate fines and were
not compacted to 95 percent of the maximum dry density as required in the project
specifications

Since compaction of fill material has a major impact on the foundations of the
future improvements on the pads and the existing grades are not certified to grade we
recommend that the loose surficial fill materials be excavated processed and
recompacted In general the following guidelines may be implemented in the final
grading of the site
1 Prior to commencement of final grading work all areas within the grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the excavation
materials
2 The loose surficial fill material should be removed to expose firm ground
The exposed firm subgrade should be proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or similar heavy equipment such as a 20 ton vibratory roller or
equivalent a minimum of four passes The excavated loose material may
be reused as fill in the upper 3 feet below the finish grade provided that
it contains sufficient fines to produce a well graded mixture with no rock
fragments greater than 6 inches in largest dimension
3 Rock fragments greater than 6 inches in largest dimension may be used
provided that the following are followed
a they are placed 3 feet below the finish grade
b they are not placed in concentrated pockets and
Cc they are blended with finer materials to produce a well graded
mixture
4 If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a well
graded mixture off site borrow should be considered to provide the
required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded material
5 In general we recommend that the loose surficial fill material at the lower
section lots Lot Nos 11 through 16 be removed down to approximately
1 to 2 feet below the existing grades As discussed previously topo
graphic survey indicated that the existing grades of the lower section lots
are generally 1 to 2 feet higher than the proposed finish grade
Therefore during the final grading the cuts required to attain design

LIMITATION
The analyses and recommendations presented in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from excavated test pits Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented
herein
