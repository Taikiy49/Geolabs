CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of limited field explorations including
the geophysical survey classification testing engineering analyses
and office research the following conclusions and recommenda
tions are presented
It is our opinion that the lots are generally suitable for
development from a geotechnical engineering standpoint the
design and construction should incorporate the recommendations
presented herein
Based upon office review of daily field inspection reports
for Lots 95 through 102 and 108 there were no adverse comments
concerning these lots therefore it does not appear that
corrective grading will be necessary
Based on the results of field explorations at Lots 93 94 it
appears that foundations should extend through existing fill
soils to underlying basalt The floor slabs may be supported
on the heterogeneous existing fill Alternately corrective
grading removal and compaction of backfill may be considered
for the mauka one third of Lots 93 94 underlain by fill
Corrective grading however does not appear to be as econo
mical and practical as using deep spread type footings

In order to provide increased stability for foundations
adjacent to sloping ground the footings should be deepened
so that a set back distance of at least 5 feet could be
maintained The set back is the distance from outside
edge of the footing at the bottom of the footing to the
slope face
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
on the order of one half inch
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 250
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead loads times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4 four tenth
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure component is reduced by one
third
Grading
Currently final grading plans are not available but it is
anticipated that minor additional fill will be used
All areas to receive fill and backfill should be stripped
of vegetation Benching and keying are required to properly
bond new fill to slopes
Diamond West Development of Hawaii

P 10
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 9 inch
lifts and compacted to at least 90 percent of maximum
dry density obtained using ASTM Designation D 1557 70
modified Proctor standard
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be used with both compacted subgrade
and heterogeneous fill subgrade requirements for compaction
and base course will depend upon subgrade material and usage
Existing fill is coarse and contains many cobbles Excava
tion which loosens the surface of the fill should be followed
by compaction of the subgrade to 90 percent modified Proctor
The slab for parking basement utility room may be supported
directly on grade
The slab for living areas should be underlain by a four inch
layer of 3 4 inch gravel or other select layer of base To
further protect against moisture and dampness an impermeable
moisture barrier may be incorporated within the base course
layer
