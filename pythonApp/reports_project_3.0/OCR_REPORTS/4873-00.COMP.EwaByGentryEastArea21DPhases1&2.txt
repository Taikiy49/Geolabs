PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the eastern portion of the L wa by Gentry
Residential Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located east of Fort Weaver Road and adjacent to Iroquois Point Road and
the HECO easement The project includes a total of 31 residential lots and related access
roads A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for the Area 21D Phases 1 and 2
project were observed by Geolabs nc from to
The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction
of fills on the order of about 1 to 4 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the
project The site grading was performed by RHS Lee Inc and the utility installation work
was performed by Albert C Kobayashi Inc During the site grading operations samples
of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained
and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are suminarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 7 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hanc probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
During site grading soil samples of the fill material generated by RHS Lee Inc s
on site crusher were retrieved and transported to our laboratory for testing Laboratory
test results generally indicated that the placed subgrade materials generated from the
on site crusher have a moderate expansion potential It was recommended that the
placed fill material within the top 2 feet from finished subgrade be removed and replaced
with non expansive to low expansive fill materials or modifications to the concrete
slab on grade design be made The modification consists of extending the 12 inch deep
thickened edge slab an additional 6 inches into the compacted soil subgrade for a total
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground This deeper
embedment will help reduce the potential for moisture changes beneath the concrete
selected

referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to siied
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
within 3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to
slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales shou d be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
