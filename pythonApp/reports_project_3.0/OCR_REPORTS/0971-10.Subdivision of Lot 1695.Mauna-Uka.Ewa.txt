PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site in order to provide data for use in the design
of the site grading for the proposed subdivision develop
ment
A preliminary soil and geologic investigation was performed
at the site in November of 1980 It consisted of field
observations and geologic reconnaissance at the site Since
the site was inaccessible for the conventional boring rigs
shallow hand augered probings were used to obtain near sur
face samples of the underlying soil for laboratory soil
testing A preliminary soil engineering report was issued
in November of 1980
Subsequently it was determined that the project grading design
would require retaining walls along the eastern boundary of
the lower half of the site and on the sloping ground near the
western boundary above lots 10 to 14 and 26 through 28
Additional borings utilizing portable hand carried drilling
equipment were made at the locations of the proposed retain
ing walls to obtain soil parameters for the design of the
proposed walls

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Pacific Palisades district
of Pearl City Oahu
The configuration of the site is basically an elongated strip
of land beginning at Auhuhu Street on the west side boundary
and extending roughly 3 700 feet eastward The north and east
ern half of the south property line is bordered by existing
residential lots The western half of the south property line
is bordered by Palisades Park a church and Auhuhu Street
The site is basically situated along the side slopes of a
former relatively steep sided drainageway

W O 971 10
The site descriptions can be generalized by dividing it into
two half areas
Western Half
The western half of the site extends from Auhuhu Street to
the eastern edge of Palisades Park It represents the nor
thern side slope of the above mentioned drainageway of which
the southern side slope was cut away to create Palisades Park
Eight of the proposed lots at the western end of the site
have frontages on Auhuhu Street This area is relatively
steep with slope ratios of 1 1 to 2 1 H V This area
appears to have been cut during the construction of Auhuhu
Street Most of this area was exposed and some existing
slope erosion was noted
The overall slope gradients throughout the rest of this
section of the site ranged from 1 1 to 24 1 The slope
surface throughout the site undulates laterally creating
intervals of rises and depressions when seen from a distance
Small erosion gullies were noted on the exposed slope sur
faces An existing concrete drainage ditch runs along the
south boundary and extends up to the eastern edge of the park
Utility poles were located midway up the slopes Existing
minor structures consisted of a flume on the Slope across
from the park and an over extended concrete wall from an
adjacent upper residence

The vegetation consisted of a moderate growth of grass and
shrubs with a few trees
Eastern Half
The eastern half of the site extends from the eastern edge
of Palisades Park to the upper end of the site This section
represents both of the side slopes of the drainageway
Overall slopes throughout this section of the Site ranged
from 1 1 to 2 1 H V The side slopes as in the western
half also undulate creating rises and depressions
The narrow floor of the drainageway was dry during the inves

Utility poles were located midway up on the north slope A
garden was encountered on the north Slope extending down to
the drainage floor from the adjacent upper residence near the
east property line
The vegetation consisted of a dense growth of grass shrubs
and trees
GEOLOGY
The location of the Palisades area is situated on a ridge
extending down from the Koolau mountain range separating
Walawa Valley and Waimano Valley The small steep sided
drainageway which comprises the site represents a tributary
valley developed on this ridge

W O 971 10
The site and surrounding areas on the ridge are geologically
mapped to consist of a mottle colored dense to vesicular
effusive basalt that is deeply weathered in most places
Within the limits of the site the exposed surfaces and hand
dug shallow depth probings revealed mainly a deeply weathered
in place rock The original rock structures are partially
visible in the residual soils developed in the exposed sur
faces resulting from spheroidal weathering of the rock mass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading plans for the existing Pacific Pali
sades Elementary School adjacent to the Site indicates that
the lower southwestern portion of the site was cut in order
to install the existing concrete lined drainage ditch It
appears that the remaining portions of the Site except along
Auhuhu Street have not been previously graded
Due to steep terrain relatively heavy vegetation and limited
frontage at the site the site was inaccessible for conven
tional drilling equipment and therefore portable hand auger
equipment was utilized for most of our field exploration
Portable hand carried drilling equipment were utilized for
borings at the proposed retaining wall locations The hand
auger probings and borings encountered stiff residual soil
consisting of clayey silt and silty chay with some clay portions

PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed development scheme consists of the construction
of a 3000 foot access roadway from Auhuhu Street generally
following the existing drainage ditch to the end of the
project site and the creation of 80 Single family residential
lots The lots would slope upward from the access road
In the lower western half of the site the general grading
concept consists of cutting into the hillside to generate
the required level area for roadway construction
Retaining walls are anticipated along portions of the access
roadway
No grading is presently planned along Auhuhu Street
In the higher eastern half of the site filling over the
low central portion of the valley is anticipated to create
the level area required for road construction
A drainline is planned under the new roadway to replace the
existing concrete drainage channel

Site Grading
In the lower western half of the site the general grading
concept consists of cutting into the hillside to generate
the required level area for roadway construction Cuts up
to about 20 feet may be required Fills generally less than
8 feet are planned near the existing concrete drainage ditch
In the upper eastern half of the site fills up to about
20 feet high are planned over the lowlying center portion
of the drainageway to create sufficient level area for the
proposed construction Most of the upper slope areas will
not be graded During our field exploration soft subsoils
were not encountered in the bottom of the drainageway
therefore it is our opinion that large settlements of the
fill are not anticipated
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation
debris and soft material Prior to filling the existing

ground should be scarified to a minimum of 6 inches and com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per above
referenced ASTM test method
The on site excavated material generally is suitable as a
source of fill Laboratory soil tests indicate that the near
surface soils have low to moderate expansion potential
These soils should be placed a minimum of about 2 feet below
the finished grade and capped over with better on site decom
posed rock material from the underlying deeper cuts if
available
If additional off site borrow is required the material should
be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to trans
portation to the site The material should generally be non
expansive and should not contain large percentages of
boulders organic or deleterious material
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5
horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into
the existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to
the slope For details see Plate 4

Relatively high cut slopes are planned in the western por
tion of the site For cuts into the existing stiff residual
material slope ratios up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical can
be used However due to the difficulty of constructing the
future houses on this steep slope a slope ratio of 2 hori
zontal to 1 vertical may be preferable
Fill slopes should be constructed at maximum slope ratios of
2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having a ver

Subdrains
To reduce piping and seepage pressure build up subdrains
should be placed in the bottom of the drainageway prior to
filling
The subdrains should be placed in a herringbone pattern
For preliminary design purposes the laterals can be spaced
at 100 foot intervals The actual locations of the subdrain

House Foundations
The proposed house designs were not available for review at
this time during our soil investigation study We anticipate
that the basic house design would consist of excavating into
the existing slope for the garage level and constructing the
remainder of the house over the garage level It is anti
cipated that basement walls would be required for most of
the houses and some post footings may be situated on sloping
ground
Due to the sloping terrain at this site deeper pier type
foundations will be necessary to minimize erosion and sub
sequent loss of bearing support for the post footing founda
tion
General guidelines for house foundation design are as follows
1 Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
resist erosion and the moderately expansive effects
of the near surface soils
2 Footings on sloping areas should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizon
tal set back distance The set back distance is the
horizontal distance measured from the outer edge of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 971 10
the footing out to the slope face For a 2 1 slope
ratio a minimum of 3 foot embedment at the lower
side would be required This additional embedment
depth is required to help resist downhill slope
creep and to develop the recommended bearing capacity
of the soil To eliminate difficulty of backfilling
the excavation we recommend that concrete be used
to construct the 3 foot deep pier footing with the
column strap buried in the concrete backfilled
excavation
3 An allowable bearing value of 3000 psf on the stiff
natural soil and 2500 psf on compacted fill is recom
mended for the anticipated light loads
4 The bottom of all foundation excavations should be
cleaned of loose material and bear on firm soil The
excavations should be observed by a soils engineer to
see that adequate bearing soil has been obtained
5 The ground surface should be shaped to drain water
away from the foundations
In order to reduce foundation cost due to deeper foundations
required on sloping terrain where practicable the site
grading should be designed to generate sufficient level lot
areas For example in the upper eastern half of the site

basement wall heights
Slab on Grade
The laboratory tests indicate that the near surface soils are
low to moderately expansive therefore we recommend that the
subgrade soils should not be allowed to dry out and it should
be thoroughly saturated prior to concrete placement It addi
tion a deeper perimeter footing a minimum of 12 inches below
the outside finish ground surface should be used around the Slab
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

to relieve seepage water
Basement and Retaining Walls
Basement walls up to 10 feet high may be required for the
garage level of the houses Retaining walls up to about
14 feet high are planned along the eastern side of the pro
posed access road next to the existing Palisades Park
Retaining walls generally less than 10 feet high are anti
cipated along the rear upper portions of Lots 10 through 14
and 26 through 28
On level areas the wall foundations should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade Where
the walls are situated on or near Sloping ground the foot
ings should be embedded an additional amount to obtain a min
imum 6 foot set back distance The set back distance is the
horizontal distance measured from the outer edge of the foot
ing out to the slope face This additional embedment depth
is required to help resist downhill slope creep and to develop
the bearing capacity of the soil
An allowable bearing value of 4 000 p s f on the very stiff
natural ground and 2 500 p s f on compacted fill can be used

Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the on site soils
the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 inch Select Borrow
18 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section 31 2
Standard Specification for Public Work Construction or equi
valent Non expansive select borrow should have a minimum
CBR value of 25 and consist of crushed rock mudrock coral
sand or cinder material
Base course and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of
its maximum density
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual sub
grade soil samples at the time of roadway construction and
the above design section can be revised accordingly

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classi
fication and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Probing and Boring Logs
Plates A l through A 16
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on selec
ted remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of
the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk surface samples
of the near surface soils to evaluate its suitability as pave
ment support The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two ASTM D 1557 70 moisture density tests modified
proctor were performed on bulk surface samples of the near
surface soil to evaluate its compaction characteristics
The test results are summarized on Plate B 2 and the curves
are plotted on Plates B 3 and B 4
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the residual soil
The test results are plotted on Plates B 5 and B 6
