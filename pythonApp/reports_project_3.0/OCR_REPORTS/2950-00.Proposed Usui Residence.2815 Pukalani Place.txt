INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the Proposed Usui Residence at 3815 Pukalani Place in the
Kaimuki area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for use in project planning

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soils
and rock conditions at the site of the proposed residence in order to provide data for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site
grading retaining walls and building foundations for the proposed single family residence
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Attendance of site meetings coordination of the field exploration and
logging of seven 7 test pits by an Engineering Geologist from our firm
The test pits were excavated by the building contractor
2 Laboratory testing of the selected samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and evaluating
their engineering properties
3 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering considerations and recommendations pertinent to the site
grading retaining walls and foundation design of the proposed residence
4 Preparation of six 6 copies of this report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the southeasterly portion of the Kaimuki area of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site slopes gently down in a southeasterly direction with ground surface
elevations ranging from about 294 feet Mean Sea Level MSL along Pukalani Place to
about 264 feet MSL along the rear of the lot
At the time of our field exploration the previously existing residential structure at
the site had been demolished and site preparation was starting
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a split level residence at the site Generally the new
residence would be primarily of wood framed construction with concrete and masonry
elements Retaining walls would be used to provide grade separation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The eastern half of the island of Oahu is composed of a single basalt shield
volcano built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series during the late
Pliocene and early Pleistocene
The project site is situated on the lower portion of Mauumae Ridge which is an
erosional remnant of the original Koolau shield In the lower portions of this ridge the
surface soils consist of a thin layer of montmorillonitic clays underlain by interbedded a a
and pahoehoe lava fiows
The surface montmorillonitic clay soils generally have a high shrink swell potential
and poor strength characteristics

The scope of our field exploration consisted of the logging and sampling of seven
7 test pits excavated at the project site by the home building contractor Logging and
sampling was conducted by an Engineering Geologist from our firm
In general the test pits encountered competent volcanic rock or cemented clinker
formation at shallow depths ranging between 0 2 and 3 0 feet below the existing ground
surface Throughout most of the site the rock formation or cemented clinker is overlain
by highly expansive silty clay soils Adobe

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings development of the proposed project site is feasible
provided that our recommendations are followed Shallow spread or strip footings should
bear on the basalt rock formation or cemented clinker As an alternate the foundations
should bear on fill placed and compacted in accordance with the recommendations
presented herein
Site Preparation
All areas within the gracing limits should be cleared of all deleterious materials
including the surface boulders and demolition debris This material should be disposed
of properly at an off site location Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally we anticipate that earthwork will involve two main types of material e g
soils and excavated volcanic rock materials Based on our exploration the majority of the
cut and materials generated from these cuts will be volcanic rock materials including the
cemented clinker material
The upper clay soils are highly expansive and have poor strength characteristics
These soils should not be used for foundation support for fill nor for backfill
Imported structural fill and backfill materials should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction

Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986 General and yard fill
materials should generally consist of non expansive well drained soils free of boulders and
other deleterious materials
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve inches
12 in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 91 Trench and structural backfills should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the underlying soil or basalt formation
to reduce potential instability of the new fill
Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in its vicinity
indicate that the upper portion of the site is most likely underlain by cemented clinker and
dense basalt at relatively shallow depths It is anticipated that hard excavation and or
ripping may be encountered in cemented areas of the clinker horizons Hard excavation
may be required in the basalt rock formation Generally hard excavation is considered
to be techniques such as heavy ripping hoerams or splitting Generally the excavation
method is the responsibility of the contractor
Design slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter may be used
for cut and fill slopes

Foundation Support
The upper clay soils encountered at the site are moderately to highly expansive
and have poor sirength characteristics These soils should not be used for foundation
support Foundations for the proposed residence should bear on the competent rock
and cemented clinker formations encountered at relatively shallow depths below the
existing ground surface
The rock or cemented clinker formation should be trimmed to provide a plane
bearing surface for the foundations i e the foundations should not be placed on sloping
rock or cemented clinker faces Care should be taken in excavation and trimming of the
rock to avoid creating high spots which could induce point loading on the foundations
High spots should be broken out of the foundation bearing area Any overbreak in rock
excavation should be backfilled with lean concrete to footing subgrade
An allowable bearing capacity of 4 000 p s f may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on the clinker or basalt rock formation This value may be increased
by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads The footings should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade If the rock or clinker formation
is encountered at a depth of less than 18 inches the embedment may be reduced if the
foundation is bonded to the underlying formation by dowelling with reinforcing steel
Based on the plans and information provided to us it is believed that one line of
footings for the new home may need to bear on structural fill This structural fill should
consist of select granular fill placed and compacted as recommended herein
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on granular fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein This value may be increased by one third for

temporary wind and seismic loads Footings bearing on fill should be embedded a
minimum of two feet 2 below the lowest adjacent grade
Foundations should only bear on one type of material i e footings should not
span from fill to rock If a foundation will soan between the two materials the portion of
the subgrade in rock should be over excavated a minimum of twelve inches 12 The
over excavated area should be backfilled with select granular material placed in six inch

placed in six inch 6 lifts and compacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as

2 Allowable bearing capacities of 4 500 p s f may be used for retaining wall

retaining walls should not bear on fill
3 A passive earth resistance of 450 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be reduced for
walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 55 may also be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the wall bearing on rock or clinker formation
5 Retaining wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
If the rock or clinker formation is encountered at a depth of less than 24
inches the embedment may be reduced if the foundation is bonded to the
underlying formation by dowelling with reinforcing steel For 6 dowels an
allowable capacity of 2 5 kips per dowel may be used to resist sliding

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that some of the floors of the proposed new home at the project
site will consist of concrete slabs on grade Building slabs and their cushions may be
supported by the in situ volcanic clinker and rock formation or by fill placed and
compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented herein A four inch 4
layer of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine should be provided below the slabs as
GEOLABS HAWAII

cushion fill As an alternate to the 3B Fine cushion an equal or greater thickness of
basaltic termite barrier sand may be used
Siab perimeter thickened edges should extend a minimum of twelve inches 12

In order to reduce the potential of future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be considered

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as test pits Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximated by measurement from
points shown on the site plan by Lacayo Architects dated Elevations
of the data points were estimated from the same plan The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report This data has been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors

engineering properties of the material

Physical Property Tests

of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tesis are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
