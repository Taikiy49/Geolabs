Project Considerations
The Kakela Drive and Mohala Way Water System Improvements project is located
west of McKinley Street and east of Manoa Road in the Manoa area in the Honolulu
District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project is part of the Metro 405’ system. The
project consists of the installation of approximately 2,180 linear feet (LF) of waterline
replacement with pipe diameters of 2, 4, 6, 8, and 14 inches along the following roadways:
Kakela Drive, Kakela Place, Kakela Iki Place, and Mohala Way. The scope of work
requires that the system be designed in accordance with the Honolulu Board of Water
Supply (BWS) standards, including corrosion control.
Based on the Board of Water Supply standards, corrosion testing will be performed
at every 300-foot interval. For this project, a minimum of about ten tests would be required.
We anticipate that the new waterline pipe invert will be about 4 feet from the existing
ground surface.
For the design of the project, we recommend drilling a total of ten boreholes to
depths of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of 100 lineal feet of
drilling and sampling. The boreholes will be drilled using a truck-mounted drill rig. Soil
samples will be collected from each borehole using standard split-spoon samplers. We
assume that the drilling work will be performed during daylight hours and that traffic control
will be required. We anticipate the project site is underlain by fill, alluvium, residual and
saprolite soils, and basalt rock formation.

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration is to obtain an overview of
the subsurface conditions along the new waterline alignments to develop a generalized
soil/rock data set to formulate geotechnical engineering recommendations for the final
design. In order to accomplish these objectives, we propose the following tasks and work
efforts during the design and construction phases of the project:
Task A - Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
1. Review of available in-house soil and geologic information for areas around
the waterline project location.
2. Filing of application for permits to drill from the City and County of Honolulu
prior to drill crew mobilization. It is assumed that the Right-of-Entry permits
for the site, if required, will be obtained by others at no cost to us.
3. Coordination of the utility toning with the various utility companies and
clearance of the proposed boring locations by our field engineer/geologist.
4. Traffic control at the proposed boring locations during our field exploration
program.
5. Mobilization and demobilization of a truck-mounted drill rig, water truck, and
two operators to and from the project site.
6. Drilling and sampling of ten boreholes to a depth of about 10 feet below the
existing ground surface for a total of 100 lineal feet (LF) of drilling and
sampling.
7. Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our field
engineer/geologist.
8. Laboratory testing of selected soil/rock samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties. Laboratory tests will include Moisture and Density,
Atterberg Limits, Sieve Analysis, pH, Resistivity, Sulfates, and Chlorides, as
appropriate.
9. Analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the design of the waterline project.
10. Preparation of a formal report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations.
11. Coordination of our work on the project by our project engineer.
