We have completed our geotechnical engineering consultation services pertaining
to the design of the new fender pile system for the existing Bravo Wharves B15 to B19 at
the above project site This letter report summarizes the results of our analysis performed
and presents our geotechnical engineering findings and recommendations derived from
our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The existing Bravo Docks B15 to B19 is located in the center portion of the Naval
Shipyard facility between the east B20 B21 and the west B12 B13 quay walls at Pearl
Harbor on the Island of B16 and B17 B18 are on the west and east
sides of the pier respectively B19 is the head quay wall connecting Piers B18 and B20
The general project location is shown on Plate 1 We understand that it is desired to
replace the existing timber fender piles by precast prestressed concrete piles
Based on as built information provided we understand that a geotechnical
exploration program was conducted for a previous Bravo Docks B15 to B18 fender
improvements project by another firm about ten year ago No boring information is
available within the B19 area It is desired to review the available boring logs drilled
previously in the adjacent areas to re evaluate the new fender pile design provided by
Moffatt Nichol Engineers
Based on the information provided we understand that the new fender pile system
proposed for the repair of the Bravo Docks B15 B19 consists of 24 inch and 18 inch
square precast prestressed concrete piles We also understand that maximum lateral

PREVIOUS INFORMATION AND ANALYSIS ASSUMPTIONS
As mentioned earlier the geotechnical exploration program was conducted for the
fender pile improvements project previously Two borings B 1 and B 3 were drilled at
Bi6 and B17 respectively In addition one boring B 1 was drilled at B20 for the
Recapitalized B20 project and located adjacent to the B19 Details of the boring location
of the Appendix
Based on the boring information shown on the drawings the project site is
underlain by soft harbor sediment over weathered to extremely weathered volcanic tuff
formation The coral formation is located at greater depth In general the mudline at the
site is at approximately 32 to 36 feet MLLW The top of the mudrock varies from
about 53 to 100 feet MLLW In addition the results of sounding conducted recently
indicated that the mudline elevation in the areas of B15 to B18 is located at an elevation of
approximately 35 feet MLLW We understand that potential over dredging may occur in
the future Therefore our analysis has considered 5 feet of potential over dredging
mudline elevation of approximately 40 feet MLLW
Based on the above information our analyses were conducted based on following
assumptions
