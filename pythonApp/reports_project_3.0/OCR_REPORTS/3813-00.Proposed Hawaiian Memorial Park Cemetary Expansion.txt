Preliminary Geotechnical Recommendations
Proposed Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery Expansion
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
We have recently completed our field exploration for the proposed Hawaiian
Memorial Park Cemetery Expansion located on Kamehameha Highway in Kaneohe on
the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter serves to present our preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the site grading of the proposed project
Project Considerations
The project site is located on the northern perimeter of the existing Hawaiian
Memorial Park Cemetery straddling Kawa Stream Based on the information provided to
us we understand that it is proposed to expand the cemetery to include several burial
gardens and a paved access road at the site We also understand that an embankment
will be required across Kawa Stream for the new access road and a box culvert will be
provided through the embankment to carry the stream flow
Based on the topographic map submitted to our office we anticipate that the
earthwork needed to achieve the proposed grades will generally consist of cuts and fills of
less than about 10 to 15 feet thick and substantial fills up to about 35 to 40 feet thick for
the access road embankment In addition special consideration has been given to the
stability of the proposed slopes and disturbance of natural drainage paths due to the
proposed site grading

Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling six test pits
designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through through TP 6 to depths up to about
9 5 feet below the existing ground surface In general our field exploration encountered
layers of silty clay Alluvium overlying clayey sand with gravel and cobbles adjacent to
Kawa Stream TP 1 at the new box culvert location In the vicinity east of Kawa Stream
TP 2 and TP 6 our field exploration generally encountered layers of silty clay Older
Alluvium However in TP 6 the material graded with cobbles and boulders at a depth of
approximately 4 feet below the existing ground surface In the central and eastern
portions of the project site TP 3 through TP 5 interbedded layers of clayey silt and silty
clay saprolite were encountered grading with gravel cobbles and boulders at
approximately 3 feet below the existing ground surface Boulders encountered were
generally less than 2 5 feet in diameter Detailed findings are presented on the Logs of
Test Pits Plates A 1 through A 3 Approximate locations of the test pits are presented on
the Site Plan Plate 2
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was encountered only in TP 1
which was located adjacent to Kawa Stream However groundwater levels can fluctuate
depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection
and other factors
Site Grading
Based on the existing terrain at the subject site and the preliminary grading plans
provided we anticipate that site grading work for the proposed project will generally
consist of cuts and fills of less than about 10 to 15 feet thick However for the access
road embankment we anticipate substantial fills up to about 35 to 40 feet thick may be
required around the proposed box culvert at the Kawa Stream crossing to attain the
design finish grades Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections
include 1 Site Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills 3 Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements 4 Excavation 5 Cut and Fill Slopes and 6 Subdrainage
Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation process and to confirm that the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to
those encountered in our field exploration

Site Preparation
pt mm
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered
fill
After clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill should be scarified
to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to between 2 and
S percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened
to close all cracks or the subgrade should be re scarified moisture conditioned
and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade
due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require special provisions
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the subgrade soils
should be proof rolled using a minimum 10 ton smooth drum roller and a woven
geotextile such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade
prior to placement of fill material Alternately the pumping areas may be
over excavated a minimum of 6 inches below the finish subgrade to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation geotextile and engineered fill placement
to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Fills and Backfills
The on site silty clay and clayey silt materials generated from excavations may be
used as a source of fill or backfill materials provided that they are screened of the
over sized materials greater than 6 inches in largest dimension depending on the
usage
If imported material is required the material should be non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 6 inches in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or
more and should have a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less Imported fill

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Select fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Areas subject to vehicle traffic should be compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with AASHTO T 180 ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content corresponding to the maximum dry density
Excavation
Our field exploration disclosed that in the areas to be excavated the site is
underlain by either clayey silt saprolite or silty clay Older Alluvium ranging from
stiff to very stiff and grading with cobbles and boulders in localized areas The
method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations Generally the
near surface silty clays and clayey silts may be excavated with normal heavy
excavation equipment however deeper excavations into some localized areas
containing boulders may require ripping with large bulldozers

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the materials encountered from our field exploration cut and fill slopes
should be designed with an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter for the proposed project Fills placed on existing slopes having slope ratios
steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the firm and stable
materials to enhance the stability of the new embankment fill against sliding The
filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design limits
of the slope and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate compaction
at the edges of the slope In the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying
and benching requirements should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope
to the design grade with sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops
of slopes or channelized and slope planting should be provided as soon as
possible to reduce potential erosion of the finished slopes
For fill slopes greater than 10 feet we recommend that a key be provided at the
base of the fill slope to provide stability of the new embankment fill against sliding
The key should be embedded at least 2 feet below the lowest adjacent grade and
should have a minimum base width of 10 feet
For cut and fill slopes steeper than 3H 1V with vertical heights greater than 35 feet
we recommend that a minimum 8 foot wide bench be provided at the mid point of
the slope to reduce the potential for erosion due to surface water run off
Subdrainage
Considering the wet climate of the area seepage conditions may be present at
localized areas at the site and along the proposed access road alignment
Therefore we recommend that provisions be incorporated into the construction
documents to provide for subdrains in the following areas
1 Where new fills are placed against existing slopes having slope
ratios steeper than 5H 1V
2 In other areas of the site where seepage is observed during and
after grading or as recommended by the Engineer during

Box Culvert
Based on the information provided the new Hawaiian Memorial Park Expansion
will include an access road that will traverse the existing Kawa Stream Therefore we
understand that a new embankment for the access road will be constructed with a box
culvert installed to carry the stream flow through the embankment as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Based on the information provided the new box culvert will be a reinforced
concrete box culvert measuring approximately 180 feet long 10 feet wide and 8 feet
high Temporary diversion of Kawa Stream will be required for the construction of the
new drainage structure The new box culvert should be designed to resist lateral and
vertical earth pressures due to the adjacent soils Items that are addressed in the
following subsections include 1 Site Preparation 2 Foundations 3 Lateral Earth
Pressures 4 Drainage 5 Settlement and 6 Other Considerations

recommended in the Site Grading section of this report with special consideration

of the box culvert A trench should then be cut for the installation of the box
culvert The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for the design
of the box culvert bearing on the silty clay with gravel and cobbles This allowable
bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
In general the box culvert subgrades should be compacted to achieve a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction However due to the location of the
foundation of the box culvert in the streambed we recommend that a minimum
12 inch thick gravel cushion layer be provided between the new box culvert
structure and the underlying alluvium The gravel cushion layer is intended to
provide more uniform bearing support and should consist of free draining material
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation or similar material A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the box culvert and
the supporting materials

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations facing down
at the base of the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an
alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain
may be used instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product
should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Backfill behind the permeable drainage material may consist of the on site soils or
select fill material compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction
Over compaction of the retaining wall backfill would exert additional lateral earth
pressures on the walls and should be avoided
Settlement
Fills on the order of about 35 to 40 feet will be required for construction of access
road embankment at the Kawa Stream crossing at the project site Because of
the large quantity of material required to raise the existing ground to the
proposed finish grades we believe that the new fills planned would induce some
ground settlements as a result of consolidation of the underlying soils We
estimate settlements on the order of about 8 inches may occur due to the
anticipated 40 feet of new fills Due to the granular nature of the sandy soils and
the relatively stiff clay layer we believe that the majority of the settlements would
occur within about 4 to 6 weeks after fill placement Therefore construction of
the pavement section across the access road embankment and other
improvements on the embankment should be delayed by at least 4 weeks after
completion of the access road embankment to allow the anticipated settlements
to occur prior to construction of improvements on or within the access road
embankment In addition we recommend that settlement gauges be installed on
the access road embankment to monitor the progress or the settlement

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery Expansion project located
on the northeast perimeter of the existing Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery in
Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located on the northeast perimeter of the existing
Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery straddling Kawa Stream Based on the information
provided to us we understand that it is proposed to expand the cemetery to include
several burial gardens and a paved access road at the site We also understand that an
embankment will be required across Kawa Stream for the new access road and a box
culvert will be provided through the embankment to carry the stream flow
Based on the topographic map and preliminary site grading plan submitted to our
office we anticipate that the earthwork needed to achieve the proposed grades will
generally consist of cuts and fills of less than about 15 to 25 feet thick and substantial fills
up to about 35 to 40 feet thick for the access road embankment

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield volcano Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in
age Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae
became extinct while Koolau was still active After a long period of volcanic inactivity
during which erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned
with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level The processes of erosion and
deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations When the sea
level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly lower and valleys were
carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level was high the erosional
base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls which are then transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3813 00 Page 5
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium In general
stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream flows transmit
large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this transport of sediments is
intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a poorly sorted mixture
of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the erosional base levels change
these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes begin
to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the material
Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse grained portion
of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or conglomerate
Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors and upstream in
headwaters This continued erosion generates material which is transported downslope
covering the older alluvial deposits Depending on the local base level and rate of
transport these new sediments are generally transient in terms of geologic time In
addition their consistency and density are generally less than those of older partially
consolidated deposits The project site is situated on alluvial colluvial deposits near the
base of the Koolau range

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling six test pits
designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through through TP 6 to depths up to about
9 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition two borings were drilled to depths
of 21 5 and 26 5 feet during our second phase exploration to evaluate the compressibility
of the deeper deposits under the proposed box culvert and also the hardness of the
material where deeper cuts are planned In general our field exploration encountered
layers of silty clay alluvium overlying clayey sand with gravel and cobbles adjacent to
Kawa Stream TP 1 at the new box culvert location In Boring No 1 alluvium underlain
by older alluvium at a depth of approximately 17 5 feet below the existing ground surface
was encountered In the vicinity east of Kawa Stream TP 2 and TP 6 our field
exploration generally encountered layers of silty clay older alluvium However in TP 6
the material graded with cobbles and boulders at a depth of approximately 4 feet below
the existing ground surface In the central and eastern portions of the project site TP 3
through TP 5 interbedded layers of clayey silt and silty clay saprolite were encountered
grading with gravel cobbles and boulders at approximately 3 feet below the existing
ground surface Boulders encountered were generally less than 2 5 feet in diameter
Near TP 3 Boring No 2 encountered very stiff saprolite to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was encountered only in TP 1 and
B 1 which were located adjacent to Kawa Stream However groundwater levels can
fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In the vicinity of Kawa Stream our field exploration generally encountered alluvial
materials consisting of silty clay grading with sand gravel and cobbles with depth
Because of the large quantity of material required to raise the existing ground to the
proposed finish grades and the construction of a box culvert in the vicinity we believe
that the new fills planned would induce some ground settlements as a result of
consolidation of the underlying soils Therefore special attention should be given to the
recommendations provided in the Settlement section of this report
In the central and eastern portions of the project site our field exploration generally
encountered interbedded layers of clayey silt and silty clay saprolite grading with gravel
cobbles and boulders at approximately 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Boulders encountered were generally less than 2 5 feet in diameter and test pits were
excavated relatively easily with no rock formation encountered during our field
exploration Therefore we anticipate the near surface silty clays and clayey silts may be
excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment
The on site silty clay and clayey silt materials generated from excavations may
be used as a source of fill or backfill materials provided that they are screened of the
over sized materials greater than 6 inches in largest dimension depending on the
usage Special attention should be given to the recommendations provided in the Site
Grading section of this report

provided we anticipate that site grading work for the proposed project will generally
consist of cuts less than 15 feet and fills less than about 25 feet thick However for the
access road embankment we anticipate substantial fills up to about 35 to 40 feet thick
may be required around the proposed box culvert at the Kawa Stream crossing to attain
the design finish grades Items of grading that are addressed in the following

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3813 00 Page 9
After clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill should be
scarified to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to between
2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks or the subgrade should be re scarified moisture
conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
special provisions
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the subgrade
soils should be proof rolled using a minimum 10 ton smooth drum roller and a
woven geotextile such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the
subgrade prior to placement of fill material Alternately the pumping areas may
be over excavated a minimum of 6 inches below the finish subgrade to expose
firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation geotextile and engineered fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Fills and Backfills
The on site silty clay and clayey silt materials generated from excavations may
be used as a source of fill or backfill materials provided that they are screened of
the over sized materials greater than 6 inches in largest dimension depending
on the usage
If imported material is required the material should be non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 6 inches

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Select fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Areas subject to vehicle traffic should be compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with AASHTO T 180 ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content corresponding to the maximum dry
density

Excavation
Our field exploration disclosed that in the areas to be excavated the site is
underlain by either clayey silt saprolite or silty clay older alluvium ranging from
stiff to very stiff and grading with cobbles and boulders in localized areas The
method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the materials encountered in our field exploration cut and fill slopes
should be designed with an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter for the proposed project Fills placed on existing slopes having slope
ratios steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the firm and stable
materials to enhance the stability of the new embankment fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for
fill placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design
limits of the slope and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate
compaction at the edges of the slope In the event that over cutting of a slope
occurs keying and benching requirements should be implemented instead of
backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills Water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes or channelized and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce potential erosion of the finished
slopes
For fill slopes greater than 10 feet we recommend that a key be provided at the
base of the fill slope to provide stability of the new embankment fill against

grade and should have a minimum base width of 6 feet as shown on the Sketch

For cut and fill slopes steeper than 3H 1V with vertical heights greater than
35 feet we recommend that a minimum 8 foot wide bench be provided at the

Subdrainage
Considering the wet climate of the area seepage conditions may be present at
localized areas at the site and along the proposed access road alignment
Therefore we recommend that provisions be incorporated into the construction
documents to provide for subdrains in the following areas
1 where new fills are placed against existing slopes having slope ratios
steeper than 5H 1V and
2 in other areas of the site where seepage is observed during and after
grading or as recommended by the Engineer during construction
In general subdrains should consist of 4 inch diameter perforated pipes with
perforations facing down as shown on Plate 4 Schematic Section Typical
Trench Drain All subdrains should be surrounded and underlain by at least 6
inches of drainage material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation or equivalent A non woven filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or
equivalent should wrap around the drainage material In general subdrain
trenches should be at least 12 inches wide and at least 2 feet deep

Box Culvert
Based on the information provided the new Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery
Expansion will include an access road that will traverse the existing Kawa Stream
Therefore we understand that a new embankment for the access road will be
constructed with a box culvert installed to carry the stream flow through the
embankment as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on the information provided
the new box culvert will be a reinforced concrete box culvert measuring approximately
180 feet long 10 feet wide and 8 feet high Temporary diversion of Kawa Stream will
be required for the construction of the new drainage structure The new box culvert
should be designed to resist lateral and vertical earth pressures due to the adjacent
soils Items that are addressed in the following subsections include 1 Site
Preparation 2 Foundations 3 Lateral Earth Pressures 4 Drainage 5 Settlement
and 6 Other Considerations
Site Preparation
In general site grading operations for the embankment fill should be performed
as recommended in the Site Grading section of this report with special
consideration to the Cut and Fill Slopes subsection of this report The area
within the limits of grading for the box culvert should be filled to at least the finish
elevation of the top of the box culvert A trench should then be cut for the
installation of the box culvert The excavation should comply with all applicable
local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and
installation should be the responsibility of the contractor
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for the
design of the box culvert bearing on the silty clay with gravel and cobbles This
GEOLABS HAWAI

allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
Due to the location of the box culvert in the stream bed we recommend that a
minimum 12 inch thick gravel cushion layer be provided between the new box
culvert structure and the underlying alluvium The gravel cushion layer is
intended to provide more uniform bearing support and should consist of
free draining material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation
or similar material A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the
base of the box culvert and the supporting materials
Lateral Earth Pressures
The box culvert and adjacent wing walls if required should be designed to resist
the lateral earth pressures due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects In
general an at rest condition should be used for the box culvert since the walls
are not free to deflect or are restrained An active condition may be used for
walls that are free to deflect cantilever by as much as 0 5 percent of the wall
height If the tops of the walls are not free to deflect beyond this degree or are
restrained the walls should be designed for the at rest condition The

These lateral earth pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that might be
caused by groundwater trapped behind the walls above the groundwater level
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads should be
considered in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded

45 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations facing down
at the base of the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an
alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain
may be used instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product
should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Backfill behind the permeable drainage material may consist of the on site soils or
select fill material compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction
Over compaction of the retaining wall backfill would exert additional lateral earth
pressures on the walls and should be avoided
Settlement
Fills on the order of up to about 35 to 40 feet will be required for construction of
the access road embankment at the Kawa Stream crossing at the project site
Because of the large quantity of material required to raise the existing ground to
GEOLABS HAWAII

the proposed finish grades we believe that the new fills planned would induce
some ground settlements as a result of consolidation of the underlying soils We
estimate settlements on the order of about 8 to 12 inches may occur due to the
anticipated 35 to 40 feet of new fills Due to the granular nature of the sandy
soils and the relatively stiff clay layer we believe that the majority of the
settlements would occur within about 4 to 6 weeks after fill placement
Construction of the pavement section across the access road embankment and
other improvements on the embankment should be delayed until the settlements
have reduced to tolerable levels We recommend that settlement gauges be
installed on the access road embankment to monitor the progress of the
settlement in order to determine when construction of permanent improvements
may commence

construction operations

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating and sampling
six test pits to depths up to about 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface using a track
backhoe In addition two borings were drilled and sampled to depths of about 21 5 and
26 5 feet below the existing ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers The approximate locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the test pits and borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the excavation and
drilling operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representation of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected soil layers in the field The
pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive strength
of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the Logs of
Borings and Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths

Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on
the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 1
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate its liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of the test are presented on Plate B 2
One consolidation test ASTM D 2435 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate compressibility characteristics of the on site soils Results of the
consolidation test are presented on Plate B 3
