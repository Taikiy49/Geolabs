DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The results of our field measurements and tests reveal that
there is little degree of uniformity in the thickness of struc _
tural section and degree of compaction in the subgrade The
subgrade is wet and in an over optimum moisture condition The
thickness of the A C is between 14 and 2 inches The base
course generally varies from 3 to 6 inches Test Pit No 2
revealed 12 inches of base course In patched areas it appears
that cinder material was used as a base course and mixed with
the existing coral base course
The condition of the A C course shows that a fair amount of
raveling has ocaurred This would be normal for older pavements
that contained coarse mixtures lacked sufficient asphalt or
were not compacted to proper density
The compaction of the subgrade varied from a low of 74 to a
high of 91 of maximum density It appears that the subgrade
was not compacted sufficiently at the time of construction The
upper six inches of subgrade should normally be compacted to at
least 90 of maximum density to have good pavement performance

There was no correlation of compaction to the existing condition
of pavement Badly cracked pavement had densities as high as
89 This may be due to the densification of the subgrade causing
the pavement failure In areas where the pavement is in fair
condition the compaction varied from 79 to 91 of maximum
density The lower densities were generally in parking stall
areas which may have been subjected to lower traffic volumes
These areas may fail in the future if subjected to heavier
traffic volumes
Pavement Structure
Based on the results of our investigation it is our opinion that
the parking area pavements are under designed The subgrade
soils are of very low strength The estimated C B R values
will be in the range of 1 to 5 However some parking lots have
performed rather well and this may be due to adequate drainage
and lighter traffic Areas I and II revealed generally lower
moisture contents in the subgrade and are located on the higher
elevations of the site

Drainage
The accumulation of excess water in the subgrade underlying
any pavement structure may cause weakening and failure of the
pavement Good drainage of the subgrade plays an important
role in providing trouble free service over long periods of
time The most severe failures occurring in the present
pavement were located along the surface drainage swales
It is therefore recommended that a subsurface drain be pro
vided beneath the pavement structure located beneath the
surface swale The detail of this drain is shown on Plate 2
The perforated pipe should be connected to the storm drain
system or some other positive drainage system The coral
base material or equal base material will be adequate as a
filter material around the pipe
Construction Methods
Overlay thickness is designed to improve a deficient pavement
condition but not to provide extra strength needed for
localized weak areas Therefore these weaker areas must be
corrected to provide a uniform foundation for the overlay
especially in the parking areas that are subjected to garbage

The following methods are recommended for repairing
and overlaying the parking areas
A Patching of Distress Areas
1
The surfacing and base should be removed to at least
one foot outside of the perimeter of the cracked
area The cut faces should be straight and vertical
When soft unstable subgrade soils are found they
should also be removed to firm stable ground
Fill the hole with a granular compacted material If
the hole is over 6 inches in depth the backfill should
be placed in layers and compacted The patch should be
built up to the level of the adjacent pavement
All large tree roots should be severed from tree and
removed from beneath the pavement structure The
resulting hole should be treated as a patched area

1

Apply a light tack coat

Overlay
After all patches and depressions have been filled the
old surfacing should be broomed clean In order to
insure a good bond between the old and new pavement it
is essential that all dust and small particles are re
moved from the old surface Washing or flushing may be
necessary
Vertical faces of existing surfaces should be sprayed
with a uniform coating of asphalt emulsion or liquid
asphalt
A tack coat of rapid curing liquid asphalt should be
applied to the existing surface to insure a bond
between the old surface and new overlay
The overlay should then be placed and compacted in accor
dance with acceptable construction procedures that will
insure a level and compacted asphalt pavement
