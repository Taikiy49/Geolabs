INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation performed
for the proposed wharf area repairs U S Navy Special Project No R5 82
at the pilot boat pier side of Pier S 21 in the Pearl Harbor Naval
Submarine Base Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of
the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that a new tieback sheet pile bulkhead wall
is to be constructed for the repair of the wharf area that has settled
The wharf area to be repaired is shown in more detail on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The new tieback wall will have independent tieback anchor
system and will be structurally separated from the existing wharf deck
structures The design also calls for a uniform live load of 750 p s f
over the wharf area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 2
Additional dredging in front of the new sheet pile wall is not
planned
SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions and to provide design recommendations for the new tieback
sheet pile bulkhead at the site The scope of our investigation
consisted of the following
1 Drilling of four 4 borings at the wharf area behind Pier
S 21 The depth of the borings ranged from 56 5 to 70 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
2 Performing laboratory testing
3 Analyzing and evaluating subsurface conditions to develop design
recommendations for sheet pile design
4 Preparing a written report summarizing the above work
Detailed description of our field exploration and boring logs along
with the laboratory testing data are presented in the appendices of this
report

SITE CONDITIONS
The existing facilities in the project area are generally shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 These include a pile supported concrete wharf
deck at Elevation 108 8 U S Navy Datum backed by a line of sheet
Pile a tieback sheet pile facing the pilot boat pier S 400 a paved
roadway a fenced yard and several underground lines which traverse the
project site These buried lines and structures may have substantial
impact on the cost and time for installing the proposed buried anchor
structures
The asphalt paved areas behind the wharf deck are relatively uneven
and have settled in the past
The harbor bottom had been dredged at various times and the
existing contours indicate that relatively uneven slopes generally
bottom from Elevation 70 to 75 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying both sites are the result of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by
glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene
MLLW 100 feet U S Navy Datum all elevations mentioned in
this report will be referring to this datum plane
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 4
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene eTa
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash
which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
layers at or near the present surface Subsequent erosion dissected and
thinned those tuff deposits
The project site is located in the old buried stream channel that
separates old Kuahua Island volcanic tuff island from the main tuff
body in the Pearl Harbor area
During the recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the
area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits which were
subsequently covered by man made fill for the construction of the
existing piers in the Naval Submarine Base
Subsurf ace Exploration
The subsurface conditions in the new sheet pile bulkhead area were
explored by drilling 4 borings to depths varying from 56 5 to 70 feet
below the existing ground surface
In general the subsurface conditions along the sheet pile bulkhead
area consisted of thin asphalt concrete surfacing 1 5 to 4 feet of
dense silty sand and gravel fill and about 20 feet of loose Silty sand
and gravel fill In particular a loose boulderly layer was encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 5
in Boring 1 from a depth of about 14 feet to about 23 feet The
boulders consisted primarily of weathered volcanic tuff with occasional
basalt rock fragments The loose fill was underlain by 20 to 30 feet of
loose to medium dense lagoonal deposits of tan and brown coral sand and
gravel containing silty layers
Below the loose lagoonal deposits Borings 1 and 4 encountered
various mixtures of weak coral deposits and weathered volcanic tuff at
about 40 to 50 feet below the ground surface Borings 2 and 3
encountered stiff to very stiff alluvial silt and clay deposits at about
52 feet below the ground surface
A generalized soil profile along the proposed bulkhead sheet pile as
interpreted from the borings is presented on Plate 3
Groundwater Levels
The groundwater levels were observed during our field exploration
and are presented in the appropriate borings logs In general the
groundwater levels appear to fluctuate with tide and varied from about
Elevation 100 to 101 5 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings we believe a special construction procedure
may be required to install the sheet piles through the boulderly layer
encountered from 14 to 28 foot depth in Boring 1 These boulders should
be removed to avoid damaging the sheet piles during driving The cost
of boulder removal may be significant Therefore a more accurate
delineation of the limits of boulder removal should be developed by
drilling 2 to 3 additional borings
The project area is found to be underlain by loose fill placed under
water over loose channel coral deposits Therefore it is likely that
small additional settlements can be expected under the heavy wharf live
loading
Sheet Pile Design Pressures
The recommended active earth pressure diagram is shown on Plate 4
These active pressures include the effect of a uniform surcharge live
load of 750 p s f on the ground surface The groundwater level behind
the sheet pile should be assumed at Elevation 102 0 feet The water
level in front of the sheet piles will be determined by others The
differences in hydrostatic pressures should be added to the design
lateral pressure diagram

Usually over dredging could be expected even for the purpose of
maintaining harbor depth Therefore the design mudline elevations
should be lower than the actual sounding bottom by an appropriate depth
It is estimated that the required depth of new sheet piles will be
longer than the existing sheet piles In addition rust holes are
reported to exist in the existing sheet piles Therefore we believe
that possible lateral support from the existing sheet piles may be
ignored
The new bulkhead wall is designed to be separated from the existing
wharf deck and independent tieback anchors will be used Thus no
additional new force will be assumed to act laterally to the deck from
the new bulkheads
Additional Considerations for Sheet Pile Construction
The possible existence of very soft sediment in front of the
existing sheetpile bulkhead facing the Pilot Boat Pier should be
probed We recommend that if the soft sediment is found it should be
removed to avoid entrapping the mud between the new and the old sheet
piles The entrapped soft sediment will exert excessive lateral
pressure and also cause future settlement of backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 9
To avoid entrapping mud behind the sheet piles backfilling against
sheet piles should be made from the back of the sheet piles and not from
the landside toward the back of the sheet piles In addition the
backfilling should start at the deepest corner at the end of S 21
closest to S 400 and work toward the shallower ends
It is recommended that the backfilling procedures be monitored by a
soils engineer to verify that no entrapment of mud occurs behind the
sheet pile and to verify that soils conditions are consistent with our
findings in this investigation
Deadman Anchor Tieback System
For sheet pile tieback we recommend that the buried deadman anchors
be located at least a horizontal distance of 52 feet behind the
sheetpile and thread tie rods through the existing utilities
Generally this horizontal distance will place the passive earth
pressure wedge of the deadman anchor outside the active wedge zone of
the sheet pile
Our recommended design pressure diagram for deadman anchor design is
shown on Plate 5
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 10
We recommend that the top of the deadman be embedded at least 2 feet
below the finished grade To avoid loss of passive resisting earth
pressures provisions should be made to avoid or to limit future
trenching operation in front of the deadman anchor
It is anticipated that the bottom of the deadman anchor excavation
will be below the prevailing groundwater level and that dewatering may
be necessary Any soft or loose soil encountered at the bottom of the
excavations should be removed to a minimum depth of 18 inches and the
over excavation backfilled with coarse crushed rock such as No 3B
Coarse material
Settlement
This investigation disclosed that the site would generally be
underlain by 20 to 25 feet of loose silty sand and gravel fill over 20
to 30 feet of loose to medium dense lagoonal sand and gravel deposits
Therefore some ground subsidence can be expected if heavy load is
applied
Generally the design uniforn surcharge live load of 750 p s f will
be equivalent to about 6 5 feet of fill Therefore 3 to 4 inches of
settlement can be expected if this magnitude of loading is applied for a
prolonged period of time We recommend that the impact of such
settlement be evaluated The future settlements may be minimized by
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 10 Page 11
placing controlled surcharge installing compaction sand piles or
compaction grouting or a combination of these methods If surcharg ing
is considered the adequacy of the existing sheet pile wall along the
remainder of S 21 should be evaluated
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the new tieback sheet pile bulkhead
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all sheet pile installation and deadman
anchor installation and backfilling operations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
penetration and preparation have been made and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the new sheetpile bulkhead site were
explored by drilling 4 borings at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the
borings which ranged in depth from 56 5 to 70 0 feet below the existing
ground surface
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were Classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
Shelby tube sampler was used The blow counts used to drive the sampler
for 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths The rock core samples were obtained by using double tube NWX
size core barrel
