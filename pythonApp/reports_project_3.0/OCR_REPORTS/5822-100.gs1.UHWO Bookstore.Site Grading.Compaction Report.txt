PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the University of Hawaii West Oahu Campus in
the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Project Location Map and the Site
Plan are provided as Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The project consists of the construction of a new bookstore addition at the
existing Campus Center Building We understand that the new bookstore addition will
be a single story structure with a footprint of approximately 3 176 square feet The
bookstore addition will be located along the mauka side of the upper Campus Center
Building adjacent to the existing dining area In addition a private dining room will be
constructed within the existing dining area
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading construction at the project site

SITE GRADING
We observed the site grading work for the University of Hawaii West Oahu
Bookstore project from to May 20 2021 The site grading work
consisted of placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 2 foot thick to
achieve the finished grades for the project Summit Construction s grading subcontractor
performed the site grading work Dur6ing the site grading operations we obtained
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and
transported them to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM
D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557
During the site grading periodic field density tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 3 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade
soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
