This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading for the Resort Core Parking Area and Pads B and
C of the Kukui ula Development
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kukui ula Resort Community development area encompasses approximately
1 020 acres in the Koloa District on the Island of Kauai Hawaii north of Lawai Beach
Road generally between Poipu Road and the Spouting Horn The area was formerly
used for sugar cane cultivation but is currently vacant Portions of the site were mass
graded in the early 1990 s for a previous development plan
The Kukui ula Resort Core project site is situated in the south central portion of the
development area It is proposed to grade the site and construct a one story Main
House a two level Golf Clubhouse a two level Spa facility and two level Club Villas The
site improvements will also include access roads and parking lots
Mass grading for the area was done in 2008 by Kiewit Pacific A compaction report
for this work was issued on Subsequently additional fine grading was done
for the Parking Area and Pads B and C area The fine grading was limited to less than
3 feet of cuts and fills
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations for the project site
SITE GRADING
Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation
and deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor Jennings Pacific Inc
performed the mass grading operations

The fill material consisted primarily of crushed rock 3 inch minus from the on site
crusher Silty clay soils from the golf course excavation were used for some of the
parking lot fills and to cover the slopes for aesthetics and landscaping purposes
During the site grading operations samples of the fill materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Under the building areas the compaction requirement
was increased to 95
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 08 These tests were performed on the
constructed fills with 3 inch minus materials Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representatives observations and the test results it is our
opinion the fine grading work for the Parking Area and Pads B and C portion of the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
geotechnical report
