INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes our geophysical survey ground penetrating
radar work performed for the proposed FY90 Barbers Point Family Housing
Project at the U S Naval Air Station in Barbers Point Ewa Oahu
Hawaii The work was performed in general accordance with the scope and
provisions of Contract No M 0068 Foundation Investigation and
Analysis FY90 Barbers Point Family Housing
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed FY90 Barbers Point Family Housing Project site
encompasses an area of approximately 18 acres in Naval Air Station Barbers
Point The majority of Naval Air Station Barbers Point
is underlain at shallow depths by coral algal limestone Previous
projects within the area have indicated that this limestone is frequently
karstic in nature i e solution cavities and voids are commonly
encountered
To economically provide preliminary subsurface data pertaining to the
potential existence of cavities or voids within the bounds of the project
site the design agency U S Army Corps of Engineers Pacific Ocean
Division contracted to have the site explored by geophysical methods
i e ground penetrating radar GPR This geophysical exploration was
supplemented by borings to calibrate and confirm the interpretation of the
GPR surveys

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the foundation investigation and analysis was to
economically provide preliminary subsurface soil data for input into the
preliminary design of the proposed family housing project Particular
emphasis was placed on the location of potential cavities and voids within
the project site
Our scope of work on the project was limited to the following
1 conducting geophysical surveys in the accessible portions of the
project site using the ground penetrating radar GPR method and
interpretation of the geophysical data
2 providing drilling and sampling services for subsurface
investigation by borings The borings were interpreted and
logged by representatives of the U S Army Corps of Engineers
Pacific Ocean Division Therefore analyses of the boring data
are not included in this report
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
Naval Air Station Barbers Point is located at the southwestern end of
the island of Oahu The project site is located along the northern
boundary of the base and is roughly bounded by Franklin D Roosevelt
Avenue to the north Bougainville Place to the east a gravel road to the
south and Nassau Avenue to the west The site encompasses approximately
18 acres of vacant land
The topography of the site is nearly level The site is currently an
open field and the surface is vegetated with a thick cover of grasses It
is believed that the site was formerly used for the operations of
lighter than air aircraft

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes
Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end
of its activity before Koolau
As volcanic activity on the island of Oahu drew to a close the
island began to subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing
coral algal reefs began to grow on the seaward margins of the island
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell
in response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
locally the fringing coral algal reefs continued to grow during the
high stands of the sea leaving fossil reefs which are now exposed above
sea level At the project site and in its vicinity there are exposures
of such fossil reefs which most likely grew during the Kaena 95 feet
stand of the sea Interbedded with these coral algal materials are
alluvial sediments which were transported from the nearby Waianae Range
Geophysical Exploration
Ground penetrating radar surveys were conducted at the site in order
to attempt to detect and delineate potential subsurface voids
The ground penetrating radar system utilizes impulse radar technology
to obtain a continuous high resolution profile of the subsurface A
pulse is transmitted by the transceiver antenna to the subsurface
materials This pulse or signal propagates through the subsurface until
it encounters a soil interface void or embedded object Depending upon
the dielectric characteristics of the interface void or object a portion
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2406 00 Page 4
of the signal is reflected back to the transceiver The received signal
is then processed and recorded
The ground penetrating radar geophysical technique does not detect
cavities or voids per se Rather it detects interfaces between materials
of different dielectric constants These interfaces act as reflectors to
the radar impulses The reflected impulses are received by the antennae
and recorded on an electrostatic printer in a continuous strip format
The data is received and recorded on a real time basis which may be marked
for linear scale and calibrated for approximate vertical scale
Strong reflections within the data field show up as anomalies
Anomalies may be interpreted as interfaces between materials of different
consistency Anomalies within a data field of hard or consolidated
material may be interpreted as voids or soft areas Conversely anomalies
within a data field of soft or unconsolidated material may be interpreted
as hard zones or objects
The radar profiling system used on this project consisted of two
separate 500 MHz two nanosecond 2 ns pulse rate transceiver antennae
mounted five feet apart on a wooden platform The antenna array is
connected by an electrical cable to the processing and display equipment
and is towed along the ground at speeds of about three 3 to five 5
feet per second Photo A Therefore data was acquired for every inch of
travel
The dual antenna system allows running two parallel lines
simultaneously thus reducing the number of transects required and
reducing the possibility of missing anomalies of large areal extent Photo
B
The location of anomalies was referenced to a surveyed grid pattern
established by the U S Army Corps of Engineers prior to the start of the
geophysical exploration Each transect was assigned a row number

W O 2406 00 Page 5
corresponding to the grid As the transects were run the horizontal
distance from the origin of the transect was continuously measured and
reference marks were inserted into the data at 10 foot intervals
DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
Previous experience indicated that the proposed family housing
project site is underlain by coral algal limestone formation at relatively
shallow depths
Coral and coral algal limestones are the result of the growth of
marine organisms which secrete calcium carbonate The formations
constructed by these organisms are frequently discontinuous in nature and
have voids where the organisms did not grow Often these voids are
filled with detritus However the voids also frequently act as lines of
weakness for the formation of solution cavities caves and sinkholes
The proposed family housing site was geophysically explored using the
ground penetrating radar method in an attempt to locate and delineate
potential voids below the surface of the site A total of 83 transects
were run to scan the subsurface in the accessible portions of the site
The geophysical exploration indicated a large number of anomalies
throughout the site The approximate location and approximate limits of
these anomalies are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Following the geophysical exploration the subsurface conditions at
the site were explored by the drilling and sampling of thirty eight 38
shallow depth borings in areas where anomalies in the radar data were
located These borings were supervised and logged by the U S Amny Corps
of Engineers Based on the information provided to us about the borings
possible voids were encountered in only three 3 of the drilled borings
It appears that a large majority of the anomalies detected by the radar
survey were loose zones within the hard coral algal limestone formation
