INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of soil and foundation
investigation at the above referenced development The
work was performed according to proposal number 8620 78
dated August 3 1978
The proposed recreation facility will consist of an one
story building with reinforced concrete columns wood beams
unit masonry walls with cement plaster finish
The finished floor elevation will be 7 5 feet the high roof
elevation will be 22 5 feet This structure replaces the
originally planned comfort station and seating area

PURPOSE SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to determine soil and
water conditions and to provide data for use in design of
foundations floor slab support grading and general site
development
The scope of work included two borings with sampling deep
penetration tests and a reconnaissance of the area Labora
tory testing consolidation strength moisture density
liquid and plastic limits was performed on representative
upper samples to help provide the basis for our analysis
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 Site
Plan and the logs are presented in Appendix A Information
regarding the field exploration and laboratory testing are
also presented in the Appendices

general area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based upon the borings the soils consist of shallow silty
sand materials underlain by clayey silt with some sand and
gravel The upper strata are dense stiff to medium brown
to tan in color The upper sandy materials and overconsoli
dated or firm dessicated silt crust may be the results of
leveling the area behind the retaining wall and natural
drying above water level The sandy materials could be
either artificial fill or natural dune sand The under
lying gray silt strata are soft and normally consolidated
brownish gray to dark gray in color
The strength or consistency of the soils is at a minimum
roughly at present ground water level Below water level
the soils become gradually firmer with increasing depths
the consistency of the soil grades medium to soft approxi
mately 30 feet deep the soils become stiff to medium near
the bottom of the penetration tests 53 feet deep
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging
from 7 to 9 feet below present ground surface Water levels
may fluctuate slightly with climatic or tidal variations

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the soils to the depths
influenced by the proposed construction approximately
15 feet deep are generally medium to soft
The proposed building may be floated on the crust of
moderately firm upper soils however some consolidation
or settlements of the soft underlying silt will occur
In our opinion the estimated settlements are not so
large as to warrant the use of deep pile foundations
piles would be relatively expensive at this site due to
the large required depth or penetration The expected
differential settlements using individual footings are
also less than amounts ordinarily considered detrimental
to the building s structural frame Anticipated differential
settlements are of such magnitude that some cracking of the
plaster finish on unit masonry walls should be expected
unless either continuous strip type or combined mat type
foundations are provided
Foundations
Due to soft underlying soils we recommend that foundations
consist either of spread type footings tied together by
rigid grade beams continuous foundations or mat type
raft foundations such as post tensioned concrete slab

The type of foundation used should be chosen based upon
the rigidity of the foundation system and the anticipated
settlements
A new allowable bearing value of up to 1000 pounds per
square foot may be used for design of shallow footings
Above value of bearing capacity is for total of dead plus
live loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary lateral
loads wind and seismic are considered The bottom of all
footings should be at least 18 inches below the finished
floor pavement or lowest exterior grade Comments regarding
estimated settlements are presented below
Settlements
Because of soft underlying soils from two to three inches
total settlements of separate footings would be expected
Actual settlements of continuous or mat foundations would
be less depending upon the imposed pressure versus 1000
p s f used in calculations Estimated differential settle
ments using isolated footings are approximately 0 5 inch
Thus maximum distortion of roughly 5 inch divided
by 16 feet column spacing may be anticipated Although the
building frame could safely tolerate this amount of distortion
normally allowable distortion for plaster finished walls is
only about 5 times less than the maximum calculated
distortion

The actual differential settlements using either continuous
or mat type foundations will depend upon the structural loads
and the rigidity of the grade beams or the raft Pending
detailed structural analysis we tentatively recommend using
a mat type foundation
The estimated settlements will occur relatively slowly
Estimates of time rate of consolidation which depend upon
other variables such as length of porewater drainage path
are relatively imprecise The settlements are expected to
be complete roughly one year following construction
Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 250
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead loads time a
coefficient of friction equal to 0 4 four tenth
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Grading
The existing ground surface should be scarified and
compacted any additional fill should be compacted in
layers

After removal of existing vegetation the ground surface
should be compacted by at least eight passes of a roller
Any required additional fill soils should be placed in
maximum nine inch thick lifts and compacted to 90 relative
compaction using ASTM Designation D 1557 70 Modified
Proctor as the standard
Shallow excavations less than 3 feet deep for any under
ground utilities may be excavated at horizontal to l
vertical Permanent fill slopes more than two feet high
should be sloped at 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Slab On Grade
In order to protect against moisture infiltration a poly
ethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated within
the concrete mat type foundation A four inch thick layer
of gravel base course should also be placed beneath the
concrete mat
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that stripping and foundation subgrades
be inspected by the soils engineer prior to the placement
of compacted fill or foundation concrete to insure that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation Density tests should also
be performed by the soils engineer to verify compaction
of fill soils

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
in October 1978 by drilling and sampling 2 borings to depths
of 22 5 feet and by performing continuous penetration tests
below the bottom of boring The penetration tests were from
50 to 55 feet below existing ground surface and were performed
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The borings were drilled using rotary drilling equipment with
continuous flight solid stem 4 inch auger
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general con
formance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler Split barrel sampler standard and
continuous split spoon samplers were driven using an 140
pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate depths
