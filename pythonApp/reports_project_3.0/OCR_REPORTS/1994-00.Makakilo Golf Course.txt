INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering reconnaissance for the design and construction of the site
grading of the proposed Makakilo Golf Course The project site is located
on the eastern slopes of Puu Makakilo in the Ewa District of leeward Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Makakilo Golf Course development consists of an 18 hole
golf course and related facilities The golf course grading plans
prepared by Belt Collins and Associates indicate cuts and fills of up to
about 20 feet deep In some areas fills will be placed on steep existing
slopes

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the eastern slopes of Puu Makakilo in
the Ewa District on the leeward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The project site is fallow rural land that has previously been used
for the cultivation of sugar cane and for pasture Most of the site is
densely vegetated by kiawe haole koa and other semitropical trees shrubs
and grasses
In general the topography of Puu Makakilo s eastern slope is rolling
with locally steep relief where drainageways cross the site The overall
slope and drainage is towards the east
Elevations at the site vary from about 300 to 800 feet above Mean Sea
Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs Puu Makakilo is situated on the
southeastern flank of Waianae along the southern rift zone
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 1994 00 Page 4
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or
more eruptive events that produced large quantities of pyroclastic
material such as cinder spatter and ash In some areas welding and or
hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the pyroclastics into weak to
strong rock e g tuff
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of
very dense basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials
Structurally Puu Makakilo is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded
cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are discontinuous and difficult
to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has
produced a surface layer of residual soil which mantles most of the site
This residual soil grades rapidly to the underlying volcanic materials
Below and to the east of the proposed golf course site there is an
existing rock quarry During the operation of this quarry stockpiles of
quarry waste have been placed in some areas along the boundary between the
quarry and the golf course site These stockpiles generally consist of a
mixture of residual soil cinder and rock fragments The approximate
locations of these stockpiles are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
development of the proposed golf course is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view The residual soils cinder and basalt
formation encountered at the site should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed golf course development The excavated materials should provide
a suitable source of fill material if reduced to less than 12 inches in
size

Grading
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or chipped shredded and stockpiled for use in
landscaping
The stockpiled quarry waste material should be stripped to firm
natural ground
The excavated basalt material may not have sufficient fines to forma
smooth surface Therefore the surface residual soils may be stockpiled
for use at finished grade to provide a smooth surface and topsoil for
landscaping concerns
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture
conditioning of fill material will be necessary It is also advisable to
institute a dust control program during grading operations
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below
any areas designated to receive fill should be overexcavated to firm
natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with well
compacted engineered fill
In general the excavated soil and rock materials less than 12 inches
in size may be suitable as fill material The material if reduced to
less than six 6 inches in size should be adequate as a source of trench
and structural backfill if needed The stockpiled quarry waste should
also be suitable as fill and backfill material if properly processed
Imported material if required should be non expansive with no particles
greater than six 6 inches in size and no more than fifteen 15 percent
passing the 200 sieve It is recommended that if additional off site
borrow is required it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1994 00 Page 7
determine if it is suitable for its intended use prior to its delivery to
the jobsite
Large boulders or rock fragments should be buried in the deepest
portions of the fills provided that they are not nested and an adequate
choking layer is used to cap the rockfill to reduce the potential of loss
of fines from the upper portion of the fill Care must be exercised to
avoid placement of any boulders in locations which would conflict with
future utility lines In general boulder or rock fragments larger than
24 inches in size should be placed at depths greater than four 4 feet
below finish subgrade level
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 90 maximum dry density as established by ASIM D 1557 78 Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills
composed of excavated volcanic material Instead a testing program to
determine the mumber of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction should be conducted at the start of the
grading phase of the project On the basis of this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate
compaction
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1
horizontal to vertical should be keyed and benched into firm residual
soil or in situ volcanic formation to lessen potential instability of the
new fill see Plate 3 The filling operations should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance
with the above recommendations for fill placement

Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in
its vicinity indicate that the site is most likely underlain by a thin
surface layer of residual soil over dense cinder and ledges of dense
basalt at relatively shallow depths
It is anticipated that most of the residual soil and cinder materials
may be excavated with D 9 bulldozers Some hard excavation and or ripping
may be encountered in localized well cemented areas or when ledges of
dense basalt are encountered
The underlying basalt formation will most likely require some hard
excavation such as heavy ripping hoerams or possibly blasting
Generally the excavation method should within safety considerations
and practical limits be left to the contractor Blasting should be
considered to expedite the excavation process However care should be
exercised to avoid overshooting which could disrupt the formation
resulting in a loss of strength
Slopes
Provided that granular materials 12 inch minus are utilized design
fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are
recommended Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio

Infrastructure Installation
It is anticipated that underground utilities such as drainlines and
irrigation lines will be installed as part of the development work
Rased on the results of our field exploration it is anticipated that
relatively hard excavation will be required for utility line trenching
In general the subsurface materials encountered at the site are
relatively competent and would require minimal shoring However areas of
loose soil or cinder may be encountered Trench shoring design and
installation should be left to the contractor However trench shoring
and bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety
requirements
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Ponds and Water Features
It is our understanding that the proposed golf course development will
involve ponds and water features Due to the dry site conditions and the
anticipated rocky nature of the material at the site we recommend that
the ponds be lined with synthetic flexible membranes to reduce the
possibility of seepage To reduce the potential of punctures in the
flexible membranes a cushion layer of sand is recommended for the bottoms
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1994 00 Page 10
and sides of the ponds and water features and should be installed prior to
the placement of the flexible membrane The cushion should be a minimum
of six 6 inches thick
Although the flexible membranes are considered to be impervious there
is a possibility of seepage due to punctures in the liner or breaks in the
seams Therefore we recommend that a subdrain system be installed below
the sand cushion This subdrain should be in a herringbone pattern and be
daylighted to drain outside the pond embankment
Foundation Support
It is anticipated that some form of structures may be needed as part
of the golf course development e g the clubhouse and maintenance
buildings It is our opinion that relatively shallow spread or strip
footing foundations may be used to support the structures
Site specific exploration to provide data for foundation design
recommendations should be conducted for the structures when their final
location and configuration have been established

Additional Exploration
This report is based upon a preliminary reconnaissance and
exploration for the subject project The majority of the project site
is densely vegetated and not accessible to truck mounted exploration
equipment without extensive trail clearing Therefore we recommend
that additional exploration work be performed in critical areas which

the assumptions upon which our preliminary recommendations are based

construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from visual observations and
geophysical surveys Variations of conditions may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
from reference points shown on the Grading and Drainage Plans prepared
by Belt Collins and Associates Inc The physical location of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
geophysical surveys depict approximate boundaries between soil rock
types and as such may denote a gradual transition

Vv Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Rock fragments or boulders larger than twelve 12 in diameter
may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in any one
area and that there is sufficient fine grained material to fill
the voids between the rocks
Boulders ranging in size from 18 to 24 inches encountered in
excavations may be used in the deeper fills provided that the
following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them

2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to

3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension

Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and
number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
