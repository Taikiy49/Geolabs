INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration for the proposed Kaneshige Residence at 1948 Ala Mahamoe Place
in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed
in general accordance with our proposal dated May 20 1989
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2334 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and presents
our geotechnical engineering design recommendations These findings and
recommendations are based on analyses derived from the data obtained
during our exploration The recommendations are intended for design input
and as such may not be adequate for construction estimation or for
bidding purposes
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Moanalua area of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The site is situated on the eastern side of Ala
Mahamoe Place and is a downhill sloping lot The approximate location of
the site is shown the Project Location Map Plate 1
There is an existing wood framed residence on the lot This
structure shows evidence of minor distresses caused by differential
settlements During our field exploration it was noted that the spread
footing foundations at the rear of the house are bearing at grade rather
than being properly embedded into the underlying formation and have been
The lot slopes down from the street boundary in a easterly direction
at a ratio of about three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V The rear
yard flattens out and is supported by an existing CRM retaining wall
During our exploration it was noted that there had been some soil loss
through the retaining wall by piping action
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing residence and to construct a
new residence at the site The proposed new residence will be a two story
split level wood framed structure A post and beam flooring system
supported by spread footing foundations is planned An on grade slab is
proposed for the lower level storage area

A new CMW retaining wall will be used to support the front side of
the proposed house

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five 5
borings to depths ranging between 2 0 and 16 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a
thin about 2 foot thick layer of silty clay fill based on our drilled
borings overlying what appears to be basalt rock formation to maximm
depths drilled
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It appears that the bouldery and rocky formation was encountered at
relatively shallow depths and it can provide adequate foundation support
for the proposed residence
Foundation Support
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used to support the
proposed residence For the light residential loads an allowable bearing
capacity of 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing
on the underlying basaltic rock formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2334 00 Page 5
It is believed that the rock formation has a downslope dip
Therefore the spread line footing foundations should be embedded a
minimum of six 6 to eight 8 inches into the rocky formation depending
on the hardness of the rock surface to properly embed the footing to
overcome the effects of this potentially adverse dip
In general for foundations located on slopes or adjacent to slopes
the footings should either be embedded on rock per above recommendation or
they be further embedded to obtain a minimm 10 foot horizontal set back
from the outside edge of the footing to the nearest slope face This
additional embedment is recommended to help resist the potential effects
ef erosion and down hill slope creep which could impact the footings
Foundations which might be located very near to the existing
retaining walls should also be embedded sufficiently deep so that their
bearing surfaces are below the active wedge of the walls i e an
imaginary line extending upward from the base of the wall at a 45 degree
slope
We recommend the foundation excavations should either be backfilled
with concrete as concrete pier structures or they should be properly
backfilled with select non expansive material placed in eight 8 inch
loose lifts and compacted to 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 if they are placed at the bottom of the excavation
Slab On Grade
The on site surface silty clay soils have a moderately high expansion
potential upon resaturation if allowed to dry Therefore the on site
soil should be over excavated down to a depth of twelve 12 inches
minimum below the four 4 inch minimm gravel slab cushion fill
thoroughly moistened and then replaced with select non expansive imported
material such as coral cinder or select borrow The thickened edge of
the slab should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2334 00 Page 6
To further reduce future swelling of the underlying expansive soils
we recommend that the in place soils below the upper capping fill pad be
kept moist prior to concrete and cushion fill placement
A four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be
provided below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
Slab
Proposed Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that upper retaining walls may be used for
grade separation along thle upper house perimeter The guidelines for
retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressure the following may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p s f equivalent
active condition 4H 1V sloping fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
2 A base friction factor of 0 40 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall

3
The wall foundations should be embedded a minimm of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade or twelve 12 inches
into rock Generally if located on a sloping area the footing
should be embedded down an additional amount to obtain a 10 foot
minimm horizontal set back distance measured from the outer
edge of the footing out to the slope face unless it is embedded
on competent rock formation An allowable bearing value of up
to 5 000 p s f is recommended for foundations bearing on rock
formation
Unless the footing is on rock for wall foundation located next
to sewer drainage easements it should be embedded below a one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as
the inverts of the utility lines Re compaction of the adjacent
previous utility trench backfill may be required prior to
retaining wall construction along the easement area if they are
found to be improperly backfilled by the project geotechnical
engineer
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as 3B Fine gravel The
top twelve 12 inches of backfill should consist of
non expansive impervious material compacted to about 90
maximim dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce
water infiltration behind the wall
Existing Retaining Walls
There are existing retaining walls on the site which will probably
remain and be incorporated into the proposed site improvements Visual
observations of these walls indicate that they are in relatively good
condition
However ascertaining that these existing walls have adequate
foundations and are structurally sound in terms of seismic loading or long
term stability is beyond the scope of this exploration

W Page 8
As previously discussed there is evidence that soil has been lost
from the rear yard by piping action through the existing CRM retaining
wall Continued soil loss of this type could potentially impact the
foundations which may be bearing on non rock layer of the new
residence Therefore we recommend the following to minimize future
piping losses through the wall
1 The existing wall backfill for a width of about eighteen 18
inches should be removed for the full depth of the wall or to
five 5 feet below the existing surface whichever is less
2 The bottom and upslope side of the resulting excavation should
be lined with a layer of geotextile filter fabric such as
Mirafi 140N or similar see Plate 3 for details
3 The excavation should be backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to a
height of 12 inches below finished grade and the geotextile
placed over the top of the gravel
4 The remaining backfill should consist of impervious
non expansive material placed in eight 8 inch loose lifts and
compacted to 90 maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that the water be diverted far away from the foundations
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also be
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or should have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineering for review and written comments
prior the construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to cbserve compliance with the
design concepts specification or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

W O 2334 00 Page 10
The location of the borings were approximately determined by taping
from reference points indicated on the Ground Level Floor Plan Scheme 3
by Gordon H Ogata Associates Inc dated March 23 1987 Elevations of
the data points were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the same plan The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
5 borings to depths of 2 0 to 16 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
summary of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the boring logs by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using an NWD4 double tube core
barrel with diamond bit The recovery REC and rock quality
determination RQD of each core run are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
