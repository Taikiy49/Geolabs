SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The study is being conducted for the City and County of Honolulu to address the
potential rockfall hazards adjacent to City streets located on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
This report is based on the National Highway Institute NHI Rockfall Hazard Rating
System RHRS program
The multi phase RHRS program consists of six stages 1 Rock Slope Inventory
2 Preliminary Slope Rating 3 Detailed Slope Rating 4 Preliminary Conceptual Design
and Cost Estimate 5 Project Identification and Development and 6 Review and
Database Update Stages 1 and 2 Rock Slope Inventory and Preliminary Slope Rating
were addressed in the Rock Slope Inventory and Preliminary Rating report
Volumes 1 through 4 which were prepared in March 2002 for this City and County of
Honolulu RHRS study Stage 3 Detailed Slope Rating was addressed in the Detailed
Slope Rating report Volume 5 which was prepared in November 2003 for this City and
County of Honolulu RHRS study This report identified as Volume 6 covers the findings
and recommendations derived from the performance of Stage 4 Preliminary Conceptual
Design and Cost Estimate of the RHRS study
This Volume 6 report presents the preliminary conceptual designs and construction
cost estimates for the 56 Class A hillslopes contained in the City and County of Honolulu
rock slope inventory in general accordance with the established procedures for the RHRS
program as outlined in the FHWA Publication No dated November 1993
The text and appendix of this report should be referred to for detailed discussion
and presentation of the slope inventory and preliminary slope classification
END OF SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed by the application of the Federal Highway Administration FHWA Rockfall
Hazard Rating System RHRS program to the City and County of Honolulu Rock Slide
Potential Inspections project Our current work carries forward with the RHRS Stages 1
and 2 Rock Slope Inventory and Preliminary Slope Rating previously established in
Volumes 1 through 4 and the RHRS Stage 3 Detailed Slope Ratings previously
established in Volume 5 The current work in Volume 6 addresses the conceptual
remedial design alternatives and preliminary cost estimates for the Class A hillslopes
identified in the Rock Slope Inventory for City streets located on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii
This report summarizes our work efforts and findings associated with the
performance of the RHRS Stage 4 Preliminary Conceptual Designs and Cost Estimates
for the Class A hill slopes Our work was performed in general accordance with the
Federal Highways Administration RHRS program which was initially developed and
implemented by the Oregon Department of Transportation ODOT and the National
Highway Institute NHI The RHRS program continues to evolve since its inception
which occurred over 10 years ago The findings presented in this report are based on
our research field evaluation and engineering analyses conducted for the City and
County of Honolulu Rock Slide Potential Inspections project The Stage 4 Preliminary
Conceptual Designs and Cost Estimates is an integral component that supports the
prioritization of remedial construction to reduce the potential hazards associated with
potential rockfall activity The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
Many of the public streets owned and maintained by the City and County of
Honolulu traverse steep hillside terrain located throughout the Island of Oahu Due to
W O INC Page 1
Hawaii California
SECTION 1 GENERAL
the steep terrain many of the City streets are located below man made cut slopes and
natural hillsides that are composed of basaltic rock altered by varying degrees of
weathering and erosion Over time erosion of the slopes by exposure to weathering
construction activity and ground vibration from heavy traffic may result in rockfall that
has the potential to produce hazards to public safety
It is desired to proactively construct remedial stabilization and or protective
systems to reduce the hazards associated with the potential rockfall activity However
because of the large number of potential rockfall sites and limited funds it is necessary
to systematically classify the potential rockfall hazard along these roads in order to
establish a rational way to categorize and prioritize remedial work Thus the Rockfall
Hazard Rating System program is being utilized to facilitate the categorization and
prioritization of the work The information obtained from the field studies including the
conceptual remedial designs and cost estimates is best managed by a computer
database developed specifically for the project
The RHRS is a comprehensive process that allows responsible agencies to
actively manage the rock slopes located adjacent to their roadway system In general
the RHRS process consists of six stages that build upon each other to achieve the
ultimate objective of assisting the prioritization and planning of remedial construction
work The six stages of the program are outlined as follows
1 Rock Slope Inventory Specific information on rockfall sites are gathered
Information required includes an inventory of all City owned streets from
City GIS maps and street index book delineation of streets with rock
slopes from geologic and soil maps and records of past rockfall activity
from City maintenance records and police reports
2 Preliminary Slope Rating Potential rockfall sections are grouped into
three broad classes A B and C This step will eliminate sites with
minimal or no rockfall potential Class C and separate sites with higher
potential Class A from those with less potential Class B in order to
focus the effort on the sites with higher risk
W O INC Page 2
Hawaii California

3 Detailed Slope Rating A numerical rating is given to selected sites
typically on Class A slopes in order to categorize risk and prioritize
remedial work The rating system incorporates factors such as slope
height ditch effectiveness traffic volume sight distance roadway width
geologic characteristics climate and rockfall history A numerical score is
assigned based on these factors in order to quantify the rating The
numerical scores can then be used to prioritize site remedial work
4 Preliminary Conceptual Design and Cost Estimate Appropriate
concept level remedial designs are proposed along with preliminary cost
estimates in order to assist in the prioritization of the remedial repair
projects
5 Project Identification and Development The information gathered in the
previous steps are used to develop a priority list for remediation projects
Several different approaches may be used based on cost rockfall risk
cost benefit locations and other factors However prioritization of
remedial projects should normally be based on the numerical classification
rating to reduce selection bias
6 Review _and Database Update All of the slopes contained within the
slope inventory are reviewed periodically to evaluate whether changes
have occurred which affect the scoring Numerical ratings should be
reviewed and updated if necessary at intervals of about 5 years
Stages 1 and 2 of the Rockfall Hazard Rating System which include the rock
slope inventory and preliminary slope classifications Classes A B or C were
completed and presented in the Volume 1 through Volume 4 reports Stage 3 of the
RHRS which includes the detailed numerical slope ratings for the Class A slopes
were presented in the Volume 5 report Presently it is desired to advance the RHRS
program with the Class A rated slopes by presenting conceptual design alternatives
and preliminary construction cost estimates to facilitate the prioritization of remedial
work for the Class A slopes This report identified as Volume 6 presents the
preliminary conceptual designs and cost estimates for the 56 Class A slopes
contained in the project slope inventory

SECTION 2 0 DISCUSSION
2 1 Objective
The objective for the RHRS Stage 4 as applied to the City and County of
Honolulu Rock Slide Potential Inspections study is to develop preliminary concept level
remedial design alternatives and construction cost estimates for selected hillslopes
contained in the project slope inventory For this study the selected slopes consist of
the 56 Class A slopes contained in the project slope inventory
The preliminary conceptual designs and cost estimates are intended to provide
an additional basis for the prioritization of remedial construction projects The
completion of Stage 4 concludes the information collection process for the Class A
slopes and the beginning of the project development stage Stage 5
2 2 Methods
The development of the preliminary conceptual designs and cost estimates for
this project is based on the general approach documented in the FHWA Rockfall
Hazard Rating System RHRS technical manual Publication No 057
dated November 1993
Prior to the performance of the numerical slope ratings research was conducted
to identify possible remedial schemes that could be used for potential rockfall hazard
mitigation on the hillslopes contained in the project slope inventory During the detailed
slope ratings conducted in the field the rating personnel recorded additional
site specific information to assist in the selection of appropriate remedial schemes The
site specific information consisted of the standardized information addressed by the
twelve detailed hillslope rating categories and other information pertaining to hillslope
vegetation the general location of existing improvements and the estimated location of
property boundaries which may assist in determining ownership of the hillslope
Based on the compiled field and research information each slope site was
reviewed in detail in order to designate appropriate conceptual level remedial
alternatives which could be implemented to reduce the potential hazard associated with

potential rockfall activity In general the selection of the site specific conceptual
remedial alternatives was based on our consideration of the existing hillslope
conditions geological conditions constructability and general construction costs
Therefore two or three potentially viable conceptual design alternatives are proposed
for each Class A slope site Of the proposed alternatives one alternative was
designated as the preferred alternative based on our research and preliminary
understanding of the existing site conditions The Site and Conceptual Alternative
Summary sheets presented in Appendix A provide a summarized description of the

proposed remedial improvements The drawings schematically illustrate the main
components comprising the proposed remedial alternatives The twenty nine Remedial
Alternative Concept Drawings are presented in Appendix B
Based on our proposed conceptual designs the basic construction and material
components were itemized to assist in the development of preliminary construction cost
estimates The unit costs were compiled from our experience and research of local
costs for materials and labor The Cost Estimate Breakdown sheets presented in
Appendix C provide a breakdown of the estimated construction costs for each of the 56
Class A hillslopes
The remedial conceptual alternatives and preliminary construction cost estimates
for the 56 Class A hillslope sites are summarized on Plates 1 1 and 1 2
It should be emphasized that the conceptual remedial alternatives presented in
this study focus on long term engineered solutions designed to reduce the potential
hazard associated with potential rockfall activity Other short term remedial options
which may consist of the removal of loose rocks and talus accumulations from the
slope or the installation of standard chain link type fencing are considered to be interim
remedial measures that may temporarily lower the risk associated with potential rockfall

2 6 Considerations for Project Development
Stage 5 of the RHRS program consists of remedial project identification and
development Projects may be advanced to the development stage by prioritizing the
remedial work using the information collected in the preceding stages of the RHRS
program

Hawaii California

The Stage 5 Project Identification and Development stage is essentially the final
phase of the prioritization of remedial work The Rockfall Database Management
Program computer database is an integral tool to be used by trained personnel to sort
and query the information to assist in the identification and development of projects for
construction Projects may be advanced by various methods which may include one or
more of the following criteria using the information collected during the previous stages
of the RHRS program
Projects may be advanced on the basis of their detailed rating
numerical score
e Projects may be advanced based on their numerical scores relative
to their estimated construction cost
Projects may be advanced on the basis of having similar remedial
designs
e Projects may be advanced based on the site location
It should be emphasized that some of the remedial projects may be affected by
hillslope property ownership considerations Based on our estimation of the possible
location of the City and County of Honolulu roadway Right of Way from available Tax
Maps in relation to adjacent private or governmental property boundaries it is
anticipated that some of the hillslopes may be located entirely within private property or
may be located only partially within the City Right of Way In addition a few hillslopes
may be located mostly within State of Hawaii or Federal property Therefore we
believe that the initial development of prospective remedial projects should begin with
the field identification of the existing property boundaries in order to identify the hillslope
ownership This step is necessary to assist in the selection of a viable remedial
alternative since some alternatives may require the procurement of construction
easements or additional Right of Way acquisition
In addition to the identification and staking of existing property boundaries in
relation to the hillslope limits potential rockfall sections a topographic survey of the
hillslope will likely be necessary to support additional detailed site study design work
and the preparation of construction plans for the proposed rockfall mitigation projects
Archaeological assessments of some slope sites may also be necessary to identify

Finally it is recommended that the site numerical ratings and related information

SECTION 3 0 LIMITATIONS
The findings interpretations and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from research the use of available public
information and site observations Variations of hillslope conditions between and
beyond our preliminary observations may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until additional site evaluation or construction is
underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs should be contacted to
re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The location of the hillslope sites rockfall sections are approximate having
been estimated by taping from property corner reference points and visible features
shown on the available Tax Key Maps obtained from the City s GIS information
database The physical location of the hillslope sites should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The site observations which contribute to the basis of the development of the
preliminary conceptual remedial alternatives and cost estimates depict the general
surface conditions observed at the time of our site visit thus some difference in site
observations may be anticipated during site visits made at different times Experienced
personnel implemented the field evaluations and professional judgments were
exercised to evaluate the existing site conditions with respect to the presence of
adverse structural geology and possible remedial measures that may be appropriate on
a site specific basis The field data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report
The preliminary cost estimates provided in this report are based on anticipated
Year 2004 local costs for labor and materials for assumed quantities based on
estimation performed exclusive of construction plans As such the preliminary cost
estimates contained herein are intended solely for comparison use to support the
selection of viable remedial alternatives and the prioritization of the remedial projects

General Description of Site Potential Hazard
The rock slope segment is located along a main roadway in a residential community on
the western slope of Punchbowl Crater The slope segment consists of highly fractured
rock cut slopes and upper natural hillslopes composed of generally low relief rock
material covered by thin soils The cut slopes are composed of adversely bedded
open fractured volcanic tuff which may produce individual large blocks of broken rock or
localized regions of rockslide activity Evidence of past rockfall and rockslide activity on
the cut slopes was observed In general the natural slopes that extend upward toward
the crater rim appear to have few rock outcrop exposures with the exception of a portion
of the slope located about midway along the slope segment There is no sidewalk or
curb and gutter improvement at the site Some underground utilities including hydrants
and utility poles are located along portions of the base of the slope A widened and
unpaved shoulder area used for parking is located along the base of the slope at the
northern two thirds of the slope segment The southern third of the slope segment has
existing cut slopes located closely adjacent to the roadway with minimal existing
shoulder area The cut slopes appear to be located mostly within the adjacent Federal
property The potential hazard consists of potential rock cut slope instability that may
produce possible large blocks of falling rock material that may encroach upon the
shoulder parking and roadway areas
