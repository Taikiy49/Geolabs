PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to provide subsurface scil
conditions at the end of the existing pier where the new pier extension
will be connected and to evaluate the general pile driving characteris
tics at that location
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance
Grilling and sampling one 1 boring
The boring log is presented on Plate A 1 of this report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is our understanding that a 12 fcot wide by 200 foot long pier
will be connected to the northern end of the existing T Pier The pier
will consist of concrete piles pile caps and beams
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the northwestern part of the Marine
Air Station at Kaneohe Hawaii The site is located about 150 feet west
of the shoreline of Kaneche Bay and the NOSC Facility The existing
wooden beam and wooden decked T Pier was constructed approximately 15
years ago
Field Exploration
The subsurface condition at the northern end of the existing pier
was explored by drilling one 1 boring to a depth of 91 5 feet below the
deck of the existing pier The boring was done with portable wash boring
equipment that was hand carried to the site on the existing pier The
approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil Conditions
The mudline was encountered at 30 5 feet below the wooden deck
platform Very soft light gray clayey bay mud was encountered to a
depth of 43 feet Belew the bay mud was a loose to soft gray alluvial
deposit consisting of clayey sand and coral gravel that extended to about
64 feet and was underlain by a stiff brown clay layer that extended to
about 83 feet A medium dense silty sand and coral gravel zone was noted
from about 83 to 87 feet and followed by medium density brown clayey sand
which extends to the full depth of the boring

DISCUSSION AND OBSERVATIONS
General
The one boring indicated that the subsoil condition consists of a
thick layer of soft and weak material below the mudline
Based on previous borings in the general area the proposed pier
about 70 feet below the existing deck elevation and penetrating about 40
feet below the mudline
Based on the boring data it appears that the 70 foot design pile
length design may not provide sufficient vertical capacity for the 50 ton
design load capacity Assuming that the medium dense sandy scils
encountered at the bottom of the boring continue preliminary evaluation
indicates that a pile length of about 115 to 125 feet below the deck
may be required to provide a 50 ton capacity with a factor of safety of
3
We recommend that additional borings be drilled along the proposed
new pier addition to provide a better estimate of the necessary pile
lengths
