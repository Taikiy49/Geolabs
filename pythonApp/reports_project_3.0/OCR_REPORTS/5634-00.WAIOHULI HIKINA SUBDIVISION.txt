Dear Mr. Au:
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading, and roadway and utility construction for the
Waiohuli Hikina Subdivision project from March 17, 2006 to November 8, 2007 in
accordance with our fee proposal.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site, located in the Kula District on the Island of Maui, is an extension of
the existing Waiohuli Subdivision along Lau’ie Drive for the Department of Hawaiian Home
Lands (DHHL). The project site is bordered by Lau’ie Drive to the north, the existing
subdivision to the east and west, and the future Waiohuli/Keokea Subdivision to the south
as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.
The project consisted of the installation of infrastructure for the subdivision. Three
new roadways with the infrastructure of water, drainage, and electrical were constructed
throughout the subdivision. Also, some of the lots were graded to meet the drainage
requirements.
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation.
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the site grading and utility installation work from March 17, 2006
to November 8, 2007. The project work was done in two phases. The first phase consisted
of construction based on the original project plans and documents and was completed in
March of 2007. Then, a problem was discovered when the topographic contours in the
plans did not match the untouched lots of the subdivision. In some areas, there was up to
6 feet elevation discrepancy. Therefore, DHHL retained the contractor to revise the lot
grading to promote proper drainage of the areas in question. Geolabs observed this
additional work from April to November 2007.

The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing of the roadways, as well
as some lots, and the placement and compaction of fills on the order of about 1 to 12 feet
thick to achieve the design finished grades. Royal Contracting Co., Inc. performed the site
grading and utility installation work. During the site grading operations, samples of the
on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
(Modified Proctor) and the test results are summarized on Plate 2.
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to +2% of the optimum
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density.
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 (Nuclear Gauge Method).
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.46 of
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site. The test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes and various features illustrated in
the project plans. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing.
Noteworthy Observations During Site Work:
During the clearing and grubbing along the north side of Lots 23 through 27, buried
vegetation, consisting of whole trees, grass and other deleterious materials were
encountered. The extent of the vegetation extended to a depth of about 3.5 feet below the
existing ground surface. We recommended that the contractor immediately notify the
DHHL project engineer. A discussion took place on-site with the contractor,
DHHL inspector, and Geolabs engineer. We recommended removal of the unsuitable
materials and replacement with select granular fill material. The remedial work was
approved by DHHL and completed by the contractor.
During construction of Road 46, a boulder fill method of fill placement was done for
the deep areas along the roadway alignment. We observed the placement of
approximately 12 to 18-inch boulders nestled together. Then, a fine aggregate fill was
placed within the voids between the boulders. Water was then added with the use of a
water truck to assist with the migration of the fines to the underlying voids within the
boulder layer. Finally, a vibratory drum roller and sheepsfoot compactor were both used to

After the original project contract was completed, additional work was added. Once
the discovery of the topographic discrepancy of several lots were discussed, the contractor
was instructed by DHHL project engineer to fill certain lots that had significant drainage
problems from May through August 2007. These were Lot Nos. 1, 4, 30, 31-36, 41 and 42.
Lot Nos. 1, 4, 30-34 and 41 consisted of 1 to 3 feet of fills. Lot Nos. 36 and 42 consisted of
a portion of the lot filled to 5-foot depth. Lot No. 35 consisted of 0.3 to 0.5 feet of fill.
Finally, additional work resumed during November 2007, to improve the drainage in
an area that was located off the project plans. The work area was situated between
Lot Nos. 36 and 37 and was bordered to the north by Lot No. 315 of the existing
subdivision. The drainage improvements consisted of an extension of approximately
128 linear feet of the existing trapezoidal ditch to the south of Lot No. 314. Then, clearing
of about 90 feet past the trapezoidal ditch was done and a slope and berm was
constructed to divert the flow of water to within the beginning of the trapezoidal ditch area.
The depth of fill varied from 0.5 to 4 feet.
Our services for this project consisted of part-time to full-time field observation of
the contractor's fill placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and
confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations.
