INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed warehouse located at Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
The scope of this study was to investigate the subsurface soil
conditions and develop recommendations for foundation design and
site development
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a warehouse and office
building covering the entire site The building will be U shaped
and approximately 190 by 115 feet in planned dimension The

W
front center portion of the property will be a parking lot The
rear portions of the warehouse will contain a basement and second
floor office space Maximum column loads of 150 kips are anti
cipated for the two story structure and basement area and 20
kips on the front on grade portion of the warehouse Maximum
wall loads will be on the order of 2 kips per lineal foot The
warehouse slab will be designed for loadings of 250 pounds per
square foot Minimal site grading operations will be required to
prepare the site for construction
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed warehouse site approximately 188 feet by 130 feet
is located on Kahuipa Street in the Heeia Industrial Park in
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii Streets gutters and sidewalks have been
constructed on the front of the property The front 100 feet of
the site is fairly level then slopes down to a stream located
along the rear of the property The site is covered by a heavy
growth of weeds
Geologically the site was at one time a lowland sedimentation
basin between the ocean and Koolau Range Recent fills have
covered the silty organic marsh deposits Based on our study of
the original Industrial Park grading plans it appears that from

W
2 to 8 feet of fill has been placed on the site The thinner
fill is along the front of the property
Subsurface
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site six borings
were drilled at the location shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A more detailed description of the field investigation and
laboratory testing is contained in the Appendix which also
includes the log of each boring and the results of our labora
tory tests
Our borings revealed that the site is immediately underlain from
2 to 9 feet of medium stiff clayey silt that appears to be a
recent fill The in place density of this fill does not appear
to be too uniform Beneath this fill is a 3 to 5 foot thick
layer of organic clayey silts that appears to be the original
ground level prior to filling operations
Underlying this organic material is a stiff clayey silt that
appears to be residual highly decomposed basalt rock This
residual soil becomes slightly coarser with an increase of depth
and was encountered in all our borings to the depths explored
The groundwater level in our borings was not uniform throughout
the site The level was encountered at depths of 12 to 22 feet
below the existing ground surface The lower water table of

Elevation 84 was encountered in Boring 3 which is located in the
northeast corner of the site
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the planned con
struction with columns and bearing walls being supported on
shallow conventional footings However due to the erratic
nature of the upper bearing materials it is recommended that
for the on grade portion a minimum of two feet below the footings
be over excavated and replaced with a granular compacted material
The basement footings will have to be carried through the organic
silt into firm bearing materials
Foundations
The front portion of structure on grade may be supported on
spread and continuous footings bottomed a minimum of two feet
below final adjacent grade Due to the relatively soft and
erratic condition of the upper soils and to the fact the foot
ings will pass through older alluvium and recent fill it is
our recommendation that the footing excavations be over excavated
and replaced with compacted material The over excavated soil
could be suitable for this material However due to the high
in situ moisture content it may prove difficult to dry out and
compact Hence a granular material is recommended The over

perimeters A safe soil bearing of 2500 pounds per square

The basement portion of the structure may also be supported on
spread and continuous footings bearing on the stiff clayey silt
underlying the organic silt found from 6 to 14 feet below the
existing ground surface The basement footings may be designed

Retaining Walls
The active lateral pressure developed against a wall will depend
upon the unit weight of the retained soil the inherent strength
of the soil the angle of friction between wall and soil the
presence of groundwater and whether the wall will be allowed to
yteld Basement walls should be designed to resist the active
lateral pressure developed by the soil behind the wall
Laterally restrained walls that will be backfilled after the
wall is an integral part of the structure should be designed to
resist a fluid weighing 50 pounds per cubic foot for the portion
of wall above the highest anticipated groundwater level which
is Elevation 92 The portion below the water table should be
designed for an equivalent fluid density of 85 pounds per cubic
foot
For a yielding wall where the backfill is placed prior to con
necting the wall to the structure the wall should be designed
to resist lateral pressures developed by fluid densities of 35
and 80 pounds per cubic foot respectively for above and below
the water table
Resistance to horizontal forces may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction along the bottom of footings
Passive pressures may be assumed equal to the equivalent fluid

Slabs On Grade
The site soils are slightly expansive It is therefore recom
mended that slabs on grade be placed on a minimum of four inches
of granular type material In areas where dampness in the floor
slab would be undesirable or where moisture susceptible floor
coverings are to be used a plastic membrane should be placed
over the granular material A minimum reinforcement of 6 x 6
10 gauge welded wire mesh should be placed in the concrete
separated from column and wall footings The upper 12 inches
of subgrade soil should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
maximum density

Pavement
Due to the variability of the upper site soils it is our recom
mendation that the upper 12 inches of pavement subgrade be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum density as determined
by ASTM D 1557 72T
Based on our experience with similar site materials and antici
pated loadings the following pavement sections may be considered
for preliminary planning

operations by sampling subgrade materials for E BR tests
Site Preparation
The site shall be cleared of all deleterious and organic matter
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches The scarified area shall then be
brought to near optimum moisture thence compacted to a minimum
density of 90 percent Fill shall then be placed in horizontal
6 inch maximum lifts and also compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
In cut areas the upper 12 inches of slab on grade and pavement

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soils at the site were explored by drilling six
test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary auger equipment
The depths of the borings ranged from 20 to 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were later verified
by inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System Graphical representations of the encountered soils
are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1A to A 1F Undis
turbed soil samples were obtained by driving a sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blow counts required to drive the sampler the final 12 inches
are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depth
LABORATORY TESTING

the site soils direct shear tests were performed on 5 selected

undisturbed samples Atterberg Limits were performed on selected
representative samples to aid in the classification and compressi
