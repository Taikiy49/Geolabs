INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the site grading drainage foundation and pavement design for the
proposed 1 5 M G water reservoir for the Princeville Water System Improvements The
general project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction estimates or for bidding
purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to obtain data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of grading drainage
reservoir foundations and roadway pavements for proposed facility
The scope of our preliminary exploration consisted of the following tasks and work
effort
1 Review of available soils and geoiogic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site to make visual observations of the
surface soil conditions and review of the grading plans to select locations
for the borings
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drilling rig and operators
to and from the project site
4 Drilling and sampling a total of three 3 borings at the proposed reservoir
site to depths ranging between twenty six and one half 26 5 and fifty one
and one half 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a one and one half 1 5 million gallon M G concrete
reservoir for potable water approximately 110 feet in diameter with a finish floor elevation
approximately 411 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Cuts into the adjacent hillside with
a maximum height of approximately twenty five 25 feet in vertical height will be required
at this site
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located approximately 5 000 feet south of Kuhio Highway in the
vicinity of the Princeville airport on the north shore of the island of Kauai Hawaii The
proposed site for the 1 5 M G Water Reservoir is located to the south and adjacent to an
existing water reservoir of the same size The site for the proposed reservoir ranges in
elevation from a high point approximately 438 feet above MSL in the eastern portion of
the site to an elevation of approximately 409 feet above MSL in the northern portion of the
site This lower area comprises the pad upon which the existing reservoir is sited The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The location of the proposed reservoir and our borings is shown on the attached Site
Plan Plate 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on a broad gently sloping plateau along the northern
shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built during the late Pliocene and
Pleistocene Epochs by the extrusion of basaltic lavas and related pyroclastics from the
Pooku vent of the Koloa Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 5
In general the soils in the project area are residual soils derived from the in situ
weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual soils grade with depth to saprolite
a form of residual soils which retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent
rock
Subsurface Exploration
The site was explored by the drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths
existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface conditions below the proposed
reservoir structure and within the cut slope areas The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the soils conditions encountered throughout the project site appear to
consist of a thin layer of surface alluvium underlain by soils ranging from very soft and
moist saprolitic soils to relatively hard clayey silts with varying degrees of sand and
weathered basalt gravel
In Boring No 1 located in the eastern portion of the planned cut slope
groundwater seepage was encountered at the depth of approximately four and one half
4 5 to five 5 feet below the existing ground surface In this boring and in Boring No 2
very soft and moist soils were encountered at the approximate elevation of the foundation
of the proposed 1 5 M G Water Reservoir Although these borings were performed within
the cut slope area and outside of the perimeter of the tank itself it is considered possible
that similar conditions may be encountered at subgrade elevation within the tank
perimeter
Groundwater was encountered in the borings during the time of our exploration at
elevations ranging from approximately 395 0 to 398 5 feet above MSL This groundwater
GEOLABS HAWAI

is probably in a perched condition and is generally the result of low permeability ash beds
or lenses within the Koloa Volcanic Series
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the borings are shown on the
Boring Logs Appendix A
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils within the project
site However modified grading techniques are recommended for the earthwork portion
of the proposed site improvements due to the relatively high moisture content of the in
situ soils and the extent of rainfall which occurs in the area In addition if the soils
encountered at the subgrade elevations are similar to those encountered in Boring Nos
1 and 2 at the same elevation stabilization of the subgrade for foundation and pavement
construction is recommended
Our review of the grading plans indicates that cuts of up to a maximum height of
approximately twenty five 25 feet are anticipated Plans currently available indicate that
no embankment construction is anticipated to be required It is possible however that
in the course of the grading soft soils may be encountered within the reservoir foundation
and perimeter roadway areas which will require over excavation at finish subgrade
elevations Due to the anticipated high moisture content and soft consistency of the in
situ soils at the proposed subgrade level it is our opinion that over excavation and
backfilling with granular material will be required to stabilize the area and facilitate
construction of the proposed reservoir and pavement Compaction of the backfill over
the soft and wet subgrade soils may prove to be difficult Therefore the use of a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 7
geotextile at subgrade elevations should be considered to lessen the difficulty of
compaction of the backfill material The geotechnical engineer can determine if the
geotextile fabric is needed at the subgrade level after the subgrade is exposed
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The deleterious materials
should be removed from and disposed of properly of site
Construction traffic performing the site excavation near and at finish subgrade
elevation should be carefully controlled and kept to a minimum to commensurate with
construction requirements Many passes of heavy construction equipment over the same
area may induce heaving and pumping of the subgrade soils resulting in instability and
loss of strength particularly during periods of heavy rainfall Due to the nature of the soils
likely to be encountered at subgrade elevations it is recommended that the finish
subgrade be probed and tested by a geotechnical engineer to determine the competency
of the exposed materials
The fill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the dry density as determined
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Design slope ratios of one and one half horizontal to one vertical may
be used for cut slopes Drainage benches eight 8 feet wide are recommended at the
mid height of all cuts exceeding twenty 20 feet in vertical height
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 8
Vegetation should be established on cut slopes as soon as possible after
construction to lessen the potential for damage due to erosion Surface water should be
diverted away from the top of the cut slope and prevented from flowing across the slope
face
Subdrains
Due to the wet climate conditions typical of the project site and giving consideration
to the seepage encountered in Boring No 1 it is recommended that provisions be made
in the construction budget for subdrains to allow for proper drainage of seepage water
as excessive water run off may weaken the clayey silt soils encountered within the cut
slope area
A representative of the geotechnical engineer should be present at the project site
when the slope is being excavated to make a determination as to whether the horizontal
drains are necessary or not Horizontal drains should be installed if it is required at the
appropriate cut slope level and generally prior to the completion of the slope bank
excavation
If the amount of seepage is determined to be excessive it is recommended that
subsurface horizontal drains be installed to enhance the stability of the cut slope A
schematic representation of typical details of a horizontal drain installation is shown on
Plate 3 Suggested specifications for horizontal drains is presented on Plate 4
Reservoir Foundations
Based on the data obtained during our field exploration the reservoir foundations
will bear on in situ saprolitic soil formations As stated above it is considered to be
probable given the nature of the area that the bearing soils may be soft and wet
Therefore special consideration should be given to subgrade preparation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 9
The material to a depth of 1 5 feet below and 1 5 feet outside of the reservoir
foundation elevation should be over excavated and replaced with No 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 Generally this material is considered to be self compacting Due to
the anticipated soft and wet subgrade consistency we recommend that lightly loaded
equipment such as a D 3 Bulldozer or smaller should be utilized to spread the material
and tracked to knit the material together to form a stable mass The initial lift of No
3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be placed to a minimum depth of one foot
prior to loading with equipment to aid in bridging the underlying soils
These recommendations will necessitate the deepening of the perimeter subdrains
below the bottom 1 5 feet over excavation to adequately drain the foundation subgrade
In the event that the over excavated subgrade is found to be excessively soft
consideration should be given to lining of the soft soils at this elevation with a non woven
geotextile such as Mirafi 140 fabric or equivalent to prevent the migration of the
recommended gravel backfill into the underlying soft soils
Providing that these recommendations are followed an allowable bearing pressure
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
Footings located next to utility trenches should be extended down to an elevation
equal to or deeper than that of the trench bottom The trench backfill should consist of
select granular material such as base course or select borrow The trench backfill which
should be completed prior to foundation construction should be compacted to a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 10
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 or filled with concrete
All footing excavations should be properly backfilled and compacted to 85 density
as indicated above
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the
reservoir foundations
Based upon the boring data and the projected reservoir loads the estimated
settlements would generally be on the order of less than three quarter 3 4 inch total and
three inch differential
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of
depth may be used A friction factor of 0 3 may be utilized to determine the sliding
resistance of the structure
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete flexible pavements are anticipated for the proposed perimeter
roadway to serve the reservoir Based on the observations from our field exploration and
the anticipated traffic loads we recommend that the following pavement section be used
for preliminary design purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on a compacted subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2810 00 Page 11
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils encountered during construction to verify the adequacy of the above
sections The recommended section assumes that the subgrade will be compacted to
a minimum of 85 of its dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 and also assumes
light traffic and positive drainage of the paved areas
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for site grading utility and
foundation construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be
responsible for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
indicated during our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations

Suggested Specifications for Horizontal Drains
Horizontal Slotted Drains
1 Horizontal slotted drains shall be installed where directed by the engineer or owner
The drains shall be sloped at 5 percent or as directed by the engineer
2 The entrance to the pipe shall be tightly plugged with a rounded or pointed
extension which shall extend not more than 0 5 foot beyond the end of the pipe
3 The horizontal holes shall be drilled with rotary drilling equipment
4 The drains shall be slotted 1 5 inch schedule 80 polyvinyl chloride pipe PVC 2110
Slotted pipe shall be furnished in 10 foot lengths with a nominal O D of 1 9 inches
and nominal D of 1 5 inches The ends of the pipe shall be machined to provide
for a smooth wall slip coupling Average configuration shall be 42 slots per row
per foot using 0 02 inch slots The slots shall consist of three 3 rows cut
