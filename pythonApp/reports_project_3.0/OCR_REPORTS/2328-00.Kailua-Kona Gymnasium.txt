INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading pavement and
building foundation of the proposed Kailua Kona Gymnasium in the town of
Kailua Kona on the island of Hawaii
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
May 16 1989 with the exception that our exploration was limited to
performing seismic refraction surveys test pits and a surface geologic
reconnaissance
The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The proposed layout of the proposed gymnasium and parking
area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized soils
data set for the formulation of engineering design recommendations
pertinent to the proposed Kailua Kona Gymnasium complex
The scope of our work included a surface geologic reconnaissance the
excavation and logging of two 2 test pits to depths ranging between 1 5
and 2 0 feet below the existing ground surface and the performance of two
2 seismic refraction geophysical surveys engineering evaluation of the
field and geophysical data the development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in the
appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated along the northern edge of the existing
airfield runway at the old Kona Airport on the island of Hawaii The
existing pavilion and parking area are located approximately 80 to 100
feet to the east of the site
The southern portion of the site is relatively flat as a result of
previous grading of the site for the construction of the airfield runway
The northern portion however is generally hummocky representing the
original lava flow surface Elevations at the site range from
approximately 8 to 16 feet above Mean Sea Level
At present the site is vacant and is sparsely vegetated with grass
and small shrubs Several stockpiles of cobbles and boulders were noted
in the southern portion of the proposed gymnasium building location

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the current preliminary plans the proposed gymasiun
building will be a 30 foot high single story steel framed structure with
perimeter wall construction measuring 120 by 170 feet in plan dimensions
The floors will be slab on grade with thickened edge foundations In
addition parking stalls are planned along the perimeter of the proposed
gymnasium building as well as the strip of land to the south along the
existing runway Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated
A site grading plan was not available at the time of our
exploration However minimal cuts and fills are anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the
prehistoric member of the Hualalai Volcanic Series These flows are
generally of Holocene age and were extruded by the now dormant Hualalai
Volcano
Observations at the project site and in its immediate vicinity
indicate that pahoehoe lava flows are usually exposed at the ground
surface Pahoehoe lava is generally characterized by a smooth ropy or
billowy surface and frequently contains voids or lava tubes
In the southern portion of the site the lava flows have been mantled
by some amount of fill which was probably placed during the construction
of the existing airfield runway
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by the
excavation of two 2 test pits to depths ranging between 1 5 and 2 0 feet
below the existing ground surface in areas where excavation was feasible
and by the performance of two 2 geophysical surveys seismic refraction

to obtain data pertaining to the characteristics of the rock materials in
areas where excavation was not possible The approximate locations of the
test pits and the results of the seismic refraction surveys are presented
in Appendix A of this report along with detailed descriptions of the
field exploration
In general our exploration encountered two 2 sets of subsurface
conditions at the project site In the southern portion of the site
adjacent to the existing rumway the surface and subsurface conditions
consist of an upper fill layer of loose to medium dense silty gravel with
cobbles and boulders over fractured vesicular basalt In the northern
portion of the site conditions were dominated by pahoehoce lava flows
consisting of fractured vesicular basalt exposed at the ground surface
The approximate delineation between these two 2 areas is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the seismic refraction surveys the typical seismic
velocities of the fractured vesicular basalt range between 4 500 to 5 000
feet per second Generally basalt formations with seismic velocities
less than 7 800 feet per second are considered to be rippable by a large
bulldozer such as a caterpillar D 9
Subsurface cavities lava tubes were not encountered in the
excavated pits However voids which were open to the ground surface were
noted in the northern portion of the site
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the test pits during the
time of our exploration

Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
roadway and right of way grading limits should be cleared of all surface
vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
materials should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use
in landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits designated
to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing airfield fill and cobble
and boulder stockpiles located in the southern portion of the site be
removed to natural rock formation within the project grading limits If
the stripped fill material does not contain organics rubbish ash soil or
other deleterious materials and if it is generally less than eight 8
inches in maximm size it may be re used as fill material Otherewise
it should be wasted off site
The well graded on site volcanic material if reduced to generally
less than eight 8 inches in size should be a source of adequate fill
material Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated
September 1986

density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and

Excavation
It is our opinion that the upper fill layer encountered in the
southern portion of the site may be excavated by conventional methods
Due to the variable nature of basalt rock formation encountered in the
northern portion of the site it is anticipated that some hard excavation
e g hoe rams chipping and or blasting may be required for foundation
and trench excavations in the dense underlying rock formation
It appears that the excavated basalt rock material will require some
processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater than eight 8
inches in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill material The basalt
lava material will require further processing to remove or reduce the rock
sizes to less than three 3 to eight 8 inches in size prior to being
used as trench and structural backfill respectively

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
proposed gymnasium structure
The bottom of all footing excavations in loose clinker lava rock
should be scarified to develop a choking layer and thenrecompacted to 95
maximum dry density prior to the placement of reinforcing steel except
when the footing will bear directly on in situ rock
An allowable bearing value up to 10 000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the dense in situ basalt rock formation However for
footings bearing on recompacted structural rock fill a bearing value of
3 000 p s f may be used to design the footings The above values are for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
A minimm footing width of eighteen 18 inches should be utilized
and the bottom of all footings should be placed a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent grade or a minimum of four 4 inches
into rock formation In addition the above footing excavation should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that the aforementioned
recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any potential voids
or cavities near the footing excavations For details of cavity probing
refer to the following section under Cavity Probing
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations

Cavity Probi
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at shallow
depths below the project site In order to reduce the possibility of a
potential void situated below a foundation causing settlement and or
foundation failure we recommend that the foundation subgrades be probed
The probes should consist of a 3 inch minimum diameter hole drilled to a
depth of 10 feet below the bottom of the footing Probes should be
drilled at each major footing location and on 10 foot centers along
continuous or strip foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout 1 sand
to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage fashion
i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment it is feasible
to perform the probing and grouting operations prior to structure and
footing excavation
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 5 between the
foundations and underlying rock materials is recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 400 p c f may be used
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than three fourths of an inch and larger than one fourth of
an inch in size

Slab on grade should have perimeter footings extending down a minimm
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade or four 4 inches
into rock formation
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement in order to minimize slab cracking due to
dehydration The scarified and choking layer below the slab subgrade
should be compacted to a minimm of 95 of the maximm dry density
established by ASIM D 1557
Pavement
Based on observations of the on site materials and our previous
experience with these types of materials the preliminary pavement design
is as follows

6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade materials as
they are encountered in the field during construction particularly if
imported material is used The above design section may be revised on the
basis of the tests

misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on

and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times

These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the fommlation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
