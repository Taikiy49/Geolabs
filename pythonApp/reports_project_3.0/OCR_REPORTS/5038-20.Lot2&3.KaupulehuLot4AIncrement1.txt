Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the
project site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe
type lava flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean Ava lava
typically is characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a
jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Aia lava is
generally present at the ground surface of the project site Pahoehoe lava is characterized
by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous
rock
Cavities are encountered commonly in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava
tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and
pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features

As mentioned previously the Kaupulehu Lot 4A Increment 1 project site is located
in the Kaupulehu area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general

hard basalt formation at greater depths It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations
ranged from about 29 to 40 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at Lot Nos 2 and 3
Lot Nos 2 and 3 generally were graded to a finished pad elevation of about 32 5 feet
MSL Mass grading at Lot Nos 2 and 3 generally consisted of excavations of up to about
6 5 feet deep and placement of up to about 3 5 feet of new fills to achieve the design
grades
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading
operations for the project to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities
and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist
in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures
employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type
projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 and or Komatsu 475 bulldozer
working the surface for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After
leveling of the rugged surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were
proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825 sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about
eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of processed on site materials including a a basalt and
dense pahoehoe basalt were placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills
consisted of well graded granular materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension
In general the fill materials within the top 2 feet of the lots within the fill areas of the pad
consisted of structural fill materials It should be noted that general fills consisting of
boulders of up to about 18 inches in dimension generally were placed about 2 feet below
the finished grades within the pad areas of the lots and choked off with finer sized
materials
Subgrades in the cut areas generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that some portions of
