PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the design and construction of a new dugout at the existing
softball field at Castle High School in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site
and its vicinity are shown on the attached Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is located on the eastern corner of the existing softball field Based
on our field reconnaissance the softball field is bounded by retaining walls to the north and
south a covered resting area to the east and a parking lot to the west A ditch was
observed behind the resting area Currently the proposed dugout site is partly a lawn and
partly serves as the covered rest area The project site is generally flat
Subsurface conditions were assumed based on our experience in the area anda
review of available in house soil reports relevant to the project site A subsurface
exploration was not performed for this project

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain information
pertaining to the subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information was
used to formulate a summary of the soil rock conditions for the design of the dugout Our
scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house soil reports and documents
relevant to the project site
2 Reconnaissance of the project site to observe the surrounding site
conditions and obtain subsurface data by a project engineer geologist and
an assistant from our firm
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
4 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
No subsurface exploration was performed for the Softball Field Dugouts at
Castle High School However based on our research and experience in the adjacent
areas we believe that the project site may be blanketed by a layer of fill material underlain
by stiff residual soils We also believe that groundwater may not be encountered at
shallow depths It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
seasonal precipitation tidal fluctuation and other factors occurring off site
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENATIONS
Based on our experience and a review of the available subsurface information we
believe that the proposed softball field dugout project is generally feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view Based on typical dugout details the structure will
be supported on concrete slab on grade with thickened edges along the sides of the slab
We anticipate that cuts on the order of 3 feet will be required to achieve design grades
We envision that retaining structures may be required to retain soil resulting from the cuts
These items are further discussed in the following sections
Site Preparation
Based on the information available for the project we envision that minimal site
grading work will be required for the construction In general site preparation should be
observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important that a representative from
our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to
those assumed in our analyses The following recommendations are intended to
provide guidelines for the preparation of the earthwork design and specifications

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation construction debris and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or yielding
areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose
firm and or dense materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fills The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site
The subgrade materials should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the on site materials generated from the excavations may be re used
as a source of fill and backfill materials provided that the materials are screened of
over sized materials greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension and are free of
deleterious or hazardous materials Where used as fill and backfill materials the on site
soils should be properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Imported fill materials if required for site filling should consist of select granular
fill materials such as crushed coral and or basaltic gravel The materials should be well
graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension
In addition the materials also should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The materials should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or higher and a swell value of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Fill and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase required for the project should
consist of crushed basaltic aggregates and should conform to Sections 30 and 31 of the
City and County Standard Specifications Aggregate base course and aggregate
subbase materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Imported fill materials should be tested
for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use

Slab On Grade
We anticipate that the slab for the new dugout construction will consist of concrete
supported on the compacted in situ soils provided that the slab subgrade is properly
prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented below and in the Site
Preparation section of this letter report
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of base
course be provided below the slab The aggregate base course should consist of crushed
basaltic aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The base
course materials should conform to the requirements described in the Site Preparation
section of this letter report
The thickened edges of slab should be embedded at least 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade It should be emphasized that the areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the edges of the slab with relatively impervious soils These
areas should also be graded to divert water away from the slab and to reduce the potential
for water ponding around the slab
Retaining Structures
Based on the information provided we understand that retaining structures may
be required for the proposed dugout project In general the following guidelines may be
used for design of the retaining structures at the project site
Retaining Structure Foundations
Based on the material at the site we believe that shallow continuous footings
may be used for support of retaining walls planned for the project An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted
in situ soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
or seismic forces
Wall footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches In addition wall
footings on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete

If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the soft and or loose materials should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm dense materials The over excavation may be
backfilled with non expansive select granular fill compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent materials The select granular fill should conform
to the description in the Site Preparation section of this letter report
Lateral loads acting on the retaining structure may be resisted by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
and by passive earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the
foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used for footings
bearing on the compacted granular fill soils Resistance due to passive earth
pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted The passive
pressure should be reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected

Drainage
Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the potential for
build up of hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system for site retaining
walls would consist of 1 cubic foot of permeable material such as open
graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped with non woven filter
fabric placed at each of the weephole locations The weepholes should be
spaced not more than 6 feet apart
The backfill from the bottom of the wall to the bottom of the weephole should
consist of relatively impervious material to reduce the potential for significant
water infiltration into the subsurface In addition the upper 12 inches of the
retaining wall backfill should consist of relatively impervious material to reduce
the potential for significant water infiltration behind the retaining structure unless
covered by concrete slabs at the surface
Drainage
The finished grades outside the dugout should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to
foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the
potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately
after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into
the subsurface Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed new dugout structure The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include
observation of footing excavation and subgrade preparation and the placement and
compaction of fill materials
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes
that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this letter report was prepared The recommendations provided
in this letter report are contingent upon such observations If the actual exposed
subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from those
assumed or considered in this letter report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
