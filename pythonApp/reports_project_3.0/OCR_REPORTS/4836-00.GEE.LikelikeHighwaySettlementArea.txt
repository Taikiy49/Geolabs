2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of
lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as
the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The caldera of the Koolau Volcano extends from Kaneohe to Waimanalo and from
the base of the Pali to Lanikai The project site is located within the Koolau caldera area
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and transport downslope toward
the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials reach the
stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium

Hawaii California

In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream
flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this the
transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists
of a poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the
erosional base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes begin
to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the material
Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse grained portion
of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or conglomerate
Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors and upstream in
headwaters This continued erosion generates material that is transported downslope
covering the older alluvial deposits
Depending on the local base level and rate of transport these newer sediments are
generally transient in terms of geologic time In addition their consistency and density are
generally less than those of the older partially consolidated deposits The deposits of
low permeability soil horizons at the base of the Koolau Range have created a confined
aquifer condition At the Kaneohe area these confined aquifers are supplied by
groundwater from dikes within the Koolau Range Since water within a confined aquifer is
under pressure boreholes that penetrate these aquifers will encounter a piezometric or
artesian head of groundwater

3 2 Probable Cause of Settlement
Based on the review of available information and the results of the field exploration
we believe that the probable cause of the distressed pavement area along Likelike
Highway is due to the consolidation settlement of the underlying very soft alluvial deposit
with thickness of about 37 feet The results of the laboratory consolidation tests indicate
that the very soft alluvial deposit is underconsolidated Based on our analysis it appears
that about 60 percent consolidation has occurred within the very soft alluvial deposit A
range of the probable ultimate primary consolidation settlement due to the overlying fill
layer was calculated The estimated ultimate primary consolidation settlement range from
22 to 26 inches Based on our analysis the estimated time to achieve 90 percent
consolidation is about 27 years Since the very soft alluvial deposit contains organic
materials settlement due to secondary consolidation will also occur
3 3 Short Term Remedial Measures
Based on the above findings short and long term remedial measures were
developed In addition an intermediate remedial measure was developed Alternatives
for short term remedial measures consist of asphalt concrete overlay placement and
shallow compaction grouting The recommended repair area for these short term remedial
measures was developed from the recent optical survey readings taken by the State of
Hawaii Department of Transportation personnel and is presented on Plate 8
Asphalt Concrete Overlay
This short term remedial measure consists of placing an asphalt concrete overlay
within the distressed area to raise the roadway back to near the as built roadway
elevations To provide proper bonding of the new and existing asphalt surface the
existing asphalt concrete surface should be cold planed to a depth of about
2 inches prior to placing the asphalt concrete overlay
Since additional loading is introduced with this alternative the total probable
ultimate settlement and settlement time will increase Based on our analysis we
estimate that about 1 inch of settlement will occur in about 1 year after the

Hawaii California

asphalt concrete overlay is placed For 3 years after overlay placement about
3 inches of settlement is estimated
The estimated cost for this remedial measure is about 280 000 with an estimated
construction duration of about 2 weeks This alternative provides the least cost and
shortest construction duration for the short term remedial measures
Shallow Compaction Grouting
The proposed shallow compaction grouting consist of the injection of light weight
high density polymer grout into the existing fill layer to lift the pavement slab back to
near the as built roadway elevations Typically 5 8 inch diameter holes for grout
pipes are drilled through the existing asphalt surface and into the underlying fill
layer to a depth of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface The holes are
drilled at about 3 to 4 foot spacings The bottom of the hole is over reamed and the
polymer grout is injected in the hole at controlled pressure The lower portion of the
fill layer is used to resist the force to lift the upper pavement slab
The estimated cost for the shallow compaction grouting alternative is about
400 000 The estimated construction duration is about 3 weeks This method is
more costly and has a slightly longer duration compared to the overlay measure
If this alternative is selected it is recommended that a test section be constructed to
evaluate this method prior to production work Because the existing pavement
section consists of flexible asphalt instead of a rigid concrete slab the injected
grout may lift the pavement surface higher at the injection points compared to the
pavement surface between the injection points Should this occur cold planing of
the asphalt surface and placing an asphalt overlay may be required One
advantage of this remedial measure is that there would be less additional
settlements due to use of light weight grout with a unit weight of about 4 pounds per
cubic foot

concrete slab The concrete slab will be supported on 16 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles at 10 foot spacing The reconstructed pavement section
will be placed on the concrete slab Since the surface fill material will be excavated
and supported on the concrete slab downdrag loading was not considered in the
pile design A preliminary pile length of 75 feet is recommended A sketch of this
remedial measure is presented on Continuous Subsurface Concrete Slab On
Concrete Piles Plate 12
This alternative has a relatively low risk potential Since the surface fill layer will be
supported by piles and reduce the loading on the underlying compressible layer
less differential settlements will occur compared to the above measures This
alternative is less expensive than the bridge structure with an estimated
construction cost of about 6 9 Million The estimated construction duration is
about 12 months Other considerations consist of noise generated from the pile
driving work

platform from the underlying subgrade material The geosynthetic reinforced

Stabilization columns consisting of stone columns jet grouted columns and driven
concrete piles were evaluated Because of the long length of the columns required
and the potential bulging of the columns into the thick very soft organic deposit
stone column type of stabilization was determined to be risky and unsuitable for the
project An evaluation of utilizing jet grouted columns was also made The
jet grouted columns created less impact to traffic since the columns could be
constructed with minor lane closures In addition the construction noise during the
installation of the columns is not as loud as the noise created during pile driving
Unfortunately the construction cost of the jet grouted columns is very costly with
estimated construction cost of about 9 6 Million Also since the surface crust is
relatively thin full soil arching may not occur between the columns Therefore
differential settlements may occur between the locations above the columns and
the locations between columns The use of driven concrete piles was also
considered and is recommended with this remedial measure The platform will be
supported by 16 2 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles The piles
will be spaced at 5 feet on centers with pile length of about 75 feet A 3 foot wide
concrete pile cap will be constructed for each pile to provide support for the
overlying granular platform layer
This alternative is a relatively new technique but has been tried on several projects
in the U S and other parts of the world Because of this a risk factor of low to
moderate was assigned for this alternative Since the upper fill material will be
supported on piles less differential settlements will occur The alternative will
create noise during the pile driving operation In addition more piles will be
required because of the closer spacing and therefore will require a longer
construction duration The estimated construction cost is about 6 8 Million with a
construction duration of about 14 months

Intermediate Remedial Measure
Because of the large differences in construction costs between the short term and
long term solutions an evaluation of an intermediate remedial measure was
requested An intermediate remedial measure utilizing lightweight backfill
consisting of geofoam was developed The lightweight backfill will reduce the
amount of settlement that will occur
Excavation of about 7 feet from the existing ground surface will be required for the
installation of a 3 foot layer of lightweight fill consisting of Expanded Polystyrene
EPS blocks or geofoam The geofoam has a unit weight of about 2 pounds per
cubic foot A filter fabric and a thin sand layer will be placed at the bottom of the
geofoam blocks to assist in block alignment and provide a more uniform bearing
surface Since the geofoam blocks are susceptible to damage from petroleum
products a protective covering consisting of a 3 inch thick concrete slab will be
constructed above the geofoam blocks The concrete slab will be overlain by the
reconstructed pavement section The amount of fill above the geofoam blocks are
designed to counteract the buoyant force imposed on the blocks when the water
level is at the ground surface level A sketch of the intermediate remedial measure
is presented on Geofoam Backfill Plate 15 As with the long term solutions
construction of a detour road will be required for the intermediate remedial
measure

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths
between about 11 5 and 76 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition the
bottom of Boring No 2 was probed to about 100 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using truck mounted drill equipment equipped with continuous
flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Four Specific Gravity tests ASTM D 854 were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the specific gravity of the soil solids for the selected samples The test
results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits Test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of the test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
Four Consolidation tests ASTM D 2435 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the soils compressibility characteristics for settlement analysis
Results of the consolidation tests are presented on Plates B 3 through B 6

APPENDIX C
Groundwater Monitoring
APPENDIX C
Groundwater Monitoring
The groundwater level at the site was monitored by piezometers installed in
Boring Nos 1 and 2 The piezometers were installed in a 2 inch slotted PVC pipe at a
depth of about 30 feet from the existing ground Below the piezometer level the
borehole was backfilled with bentonite From the piezometer level up to about 5 5 feet
below the existing ground surface the annular space between the PVC pipe and the
borehole wall was backfilled with clean sand A bentonite cap seal ranging in thickness
from 3 to 3 5 feet was installed above the sand backfill The piezometers consisted of
vibrating wire piezometers Model No 4500B from Geokon Inc The groundwater level
was continuous monitored and periodic readings were taken and recorded by a
Datalogger Model No 8001 from Geokon Inc The piezometer readings are presented
on Plates C 1 and C 2
