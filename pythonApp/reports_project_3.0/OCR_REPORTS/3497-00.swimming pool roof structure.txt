Foundation Excavation Observations
Swimming Pool Roof Structure
3180 Pacific Heights Road

This letter summarizes our field observations during the foundation excavation at
the above referenced project Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated August 3 1995
Project Considerations
The project site is located at 3180 Pacific Heights Road in the District of Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a roof structure for the existing
swimming pool within the property A shallow foundation system consisting of 12 spread
footings was used for the support of the proposed roof structure Based on the plans
provided the footings have plan dimensions of about 4 by 4 feet with a minimum of 3 feet
of embedment below the deck of the swimming pool
Based on our observations soft soils and seepage water were encountered during
the excavation for the footings of the roof structure Based on our field observations and
our engineering analyses we recommended that the soft soils at the footing locations be
over excavated to expose firm ground or a maximum of 2 to 3 feet below the bottom of the
footing and the resulting excavation be replaced with a mud slab Details of our
recommendations for the foundation excavation were presented in our letter dated August
14 1995

Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Foundation for Swimming Pool Roof Structure
3180 Pacific Heights Road

This letter report presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations for the
foundation design of the swimming pool roof structure at the above referenced project
Our work consisted of a field visit engineering analyses and preparation of this letter
report in general accordance with our fee proposal dated August 3rd
Project Considerations
The project site is located at 3180 Pacific Heights Road in the District of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a roof structure for
the existing swimming pool within the property Based on the information provided soft
soils and seepage water were encountered during the excavation for footings of the
roof structure
As requested we visited the site on July 26th to observe the exposed soil
conditions of the foundation excavation Based on our field observation we noted that
a total of 11 footing excavations were made to depths of about 1 to 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Generally the footings excavated have plan dimensions of
about 4 by 4 feet Based on our field observations dark brown silty clay with gravels
were noted within the footing excavations Probing with a inch steel rod indicated
that the bottom of the excavated footings were soft with penetrations of about 4 to 18
inches deep In addition seepage water was observed in nine of the footing
excavations ranging from about 2 to 8 inches in depth at the time of our field visit

Foundations
Based on our field observation soft soils and seepage water were encountered
near the bottom of footing levels We believe that the soft soils encountered at the _
bottom of footing level are the result of saturation of the material by seepage water
during and after the excavation Based on the soft soil conditions encountered near the
footing level we recommend that the soft soils be removed to reduce the potential for
significant settlement of the footings
In order for the contractor to place the steel reinforcements for the footings we
recommend that the soft soils below the bottom of footing be removed and be replaced
with concrete The soft soils at the footing locations should be over excavated to
expose firm ground or a maximum of 2 to 3 feet below the bottom of the footing
whichever is less The depth of thé over excavation should be evaluated in the field
by a representative from Geolabs Hawaii The resulting over excavation should be
backfilled with concrete to serve as a mud slab The concrete should be cured for at
least 24 hours before footing construction The footings may then bear directly on the
concrete mud slab We wish to emphasize that the over excavation of the soft soils
should be done followed closely by the concrete placement for the mud slab
Otherwise the seepage water would soften the exposed firm soils requiring further
over excavation
Based on the above recommended foundation preparation we believe that an
allowable bearing pressure of up to 1 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used
for the design of the spread footings bearing on the concrete mud slab If our
recommendations for foundation design and preparation presented in this letter report
are followed we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be on the order of less than inch with differential settlements of about one
half of that amount
The footing excavations should be kept dry after the footing over excavation and
backfill with concrete We believe that the irrigation for the adjacent landscape area
is the probable source of seepage water Therefore we recommend that the landscape
irrigation within proximity to this area be limited to reduce the amount of seepage water
infiltrating from the ground surface
The footing excavations should be backfilled above the footings to the ground
surface The on site soils or imported materials may be used as a source of backfill
material provided that it is compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
