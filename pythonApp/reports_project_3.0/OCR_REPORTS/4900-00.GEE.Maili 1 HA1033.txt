PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the vicinity of Maliona Street in the Maili area in
Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves the renovation of
20 single family dwelling structures and related site improvements Renovation of the
dwelling units will involve modernization of the existing seven 2 bedroom and thirteen
3 bedroom single family house structures to current building standards Site improvements
may involve replacing existing sewer and water lines site and discharge improvement to
alleviate the existing drainage problems new driveways and parking stalls and concrete
walkways and roadway curbs
Construction of the new driveways parking stalls and new roadway curbs will
involve replacing the existing asphaltic concrete pavement which is badly deteriorated
with spalls and cracks The existing driveways and parking stalls at each cluster of
residential units are approximately 15 feet wide and 150 feet in length The existing
pavement will be removed and replaced together with the rest of the proposed site
improvement
Our scope of work was limited to the evaluation of the pavement thickness design
for the new driveways Our experience in the area suggests that the project site may be
underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays with varying amounts of basaltic and coralline
gravel near the ground surface Special attention was given to the swelling potential of the

subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was used
to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of the proposed new driveway pavements Our
work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site consisting of two sites designated as Site A and Site B is
located approximately 100 feet on the makai side of the intersection at Maliona Street and
Kulaaupuni Street in the Maili area of Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Site A
consisting of two 7 unit residential clusters is located on the northern side of Maliona
Street and extends towards Hila Street Site B consisting of a six unit residential cluster
is located on the southern side of Maliona Street An asphalt paved driveway
approximately 10 feet wide exists at each cluster of dwelling units The site is relatively
flat At the time of our site visit the asphalt pavement was observed to be in very poor
deteriorated condition with alligator cracks ruts exposed aggregates and some potholes
in several areas
FIELD EXPLORATION
The project site was explored by visual observation of the surrounding site
conditions and the collection of near surface bulk soil samples for CBR analyses The
bulk soil samples were collected by excavating a test pit on the roadway to a depth of
approximately 2 feet below the existing pavement surface The approximate locations of
the test pit and bulk samples are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The bulk soil materials collected were classified by visual and textural examination
by our engineer and were further reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory
LABORATORY TESTING
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 on the silty sandy gravel base course samples to evaluate the pavement
support characteristics The test result is presented on Plate 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration disclosed that the existing 17 inch thick asphaltic
concrete pavement on the existing driveways is underlain by dense silty sandy basaltic
_ gravel base course extending to a depth of approximately 18 inches from top of the
asphaltic concrete AC pavement The base course materials were underlain by reddish
brown silty clay The silty clay material was observed to be stiff to hard to the maximum
depths explored of about 24 inches below the top of existing pavement
Groundwater was not encountered during our field exploration However
groundwater levels may be influenced by other factors such as seasonal precipitation
groundwater withdrawal and or injection

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The project site was observed to be underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays with
varying amounts of coral gravel near the existing ground surface We understand that
new asphaltic concrete parking areas and concrete cubs and walkways are part of the
proposed site improvement Based on the laboratory test results the on site base course
materials have a CBR value of 62 6 with an expansion value of about O percent It
appears that the pavement deterioration is primarily due to the advanced age of the
pavement and partly due to poor drainage in localized areas
Pavements
In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for the driveways and parking lot of this
project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light to medium sized trucks
Based on the above assumption and our field exploration and laboratory testing we
recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary design
purposes
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optinum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base and select borrow
subbase course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above sections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into
the subgrade soil below the aggregate base and select borrow subbase course layers to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain
system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For
long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscaped areas

Concrete Walkways
We understand that concrete walkways are also planned for the entrances to each
accessible unit In addition concrete curbs may be installed also along the new roadway
and edge of parking area We recommend that a minimum 12 inch thick layer of
non expansive select granular fill be placed below the concrete walkways The select
granular fill should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The
underlying subgrade soils below the select granular fill should be moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal
to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections To reduce the potential for
water ponding next to the slab adjacent areas should be backfilled tightly against the slab
and graded to divert water away from the slab
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished grades we anticipate
that minimal site grading work will be required for the proposed improvements at the
subject site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill
In general we envision that the excavated on site materials may be re used as fill
or backfill materials provided that deleterious materials are removed and that the
excavated on site materials are processed to a maximum particle size of about 3 inches
Fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts of 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction

Imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular fill material such as
crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less
when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for the intended use
Drainage
The finished grades outside the proposed parking area and concrete walkways
should be sloped to shed water away from the slabs and foundations and to reduce the
potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should be avoided Planters next to foundations and slabs should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation and slab recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled against the slabs immediately after setting the concrete to reduce
water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface run off away from slabs and foundations
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed project should be
forwarded to Geolabs Inc for review and written comments prior to advertisement for
bids This review is necessary to evaluate conformance of the plans and specifications
with the intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations provided herein If this
review is not made Geolabs Inc cannot be responsible for misinterpretation of our
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Inc be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during earthwork construction of the proposed project This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared
The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations If
the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field sampling Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the sampling may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this
report
The locations of the field bulk samples indicated in this report are approximate
having been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan transmitted by
Architects Pacific Inc on The physical locations of the source of the bulk
samples should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
