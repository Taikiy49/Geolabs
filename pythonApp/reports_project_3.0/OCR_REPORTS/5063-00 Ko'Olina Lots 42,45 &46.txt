Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to
early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows
from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau
now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank
of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project

sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of
the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys
in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous
reef materials Placement of man made fills and commercial development of the area
within the last 20 years have brought the Ko Olina area to its present form
Site Description
As mentioned previously the project site is located within the Ko Olina Resort in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site Lots 44 and 45 is
bounded by Aliinui Drive to the south and the Ko Olina Golf Course to the east west and
north A railroad track traversing in an east west direction bisects the project site as
shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our site reconnaissance the southern portion of the project site is
generally graded to a near level condition Mounds and stockpiles of soil were present at
the northern portion of the project site In some areas the mounds of soil appear to be
native alluvium interbedded with thin layers of coralline materials Stockpiles of coralline
gravel cobbles and boulders were also present at the northern portion of the project site
The vegetation at the project site is relatively light with the majority of the vegetation
consisting of wild grasses A recent topographic survey map was not available at the time
this letter report was prepared However based on the available maps and our
observations the ground surface elevations at the project site may range from about
20 to 30 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 104 The borings were advanced to
depths of about 35 5 to 40 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition research
and review of available borings and test pits in house information in the vicinity of the
proposed project site was conducted to supplement the data from the limited field
exploration conducted for this due diligence study
Based on the limited field exploration and a review of available in house
information the native soils at the project site generally consist of coralline deposits and
alluvium The coralline deposits at the project site generally consist of medium dense to
dense silty sands and gravel The alluvium at the project site generally consist of medium
Stiff to stiff silts and clays The limits of the alluvium and coralline deposits are difficult to
define due to the irregular depositional nature of the near surface materials However
based on available geologic maps the approximate original limits prior to mass grading of
the site of the alluvium and coralline materials are shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
Our in house information and site observations indicate that portions of the project
site were graded in a fill condition during the original mass grading operations of the
Ko Olina Resort We estimate that fills of up to about 10 feet in thickness may have been
placed at the southern portions of the project site In general the fill materials placed
during the initial mass grading operations consisted of on site materials generated from
excavations at other areas of the Ko Olina Resort Therefore we anticipate that the near
surface materials may consist of mixture of alluvium and coralline deposits In areas with
deeper fills rock fills consisting of coralline boulders and cobbles were also placed
Coral formation is anticipated below the surface fill materials It should be noted
that based on our past experience in the vicinity of the project site the coral formation at
the project site is highly variable in terms of hardness and quality Pockets of voids and
cavities are commonly encountered in the coralline deposits in this area In addition
cavities in the coral formation could be filled with soft and or loose coralline detritus and
alluvial deposits
Shallow groundwater levels should be expected due to the low elevations at the
project site In general we envision that the groundwater levels will range from about 1 to
1 foot MSL However due to the close proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean
groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation
groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors

Expansive Soils
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site the on site alluvial soils
may exhibit moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations As
previously mentioned the native alluvial soils were observed at the project site in some
areas In addition alluvial soils may have been used as fill materials during the initial
mass grading operations for the Ko Olina Resort development
The lateral extent and thickness of alluvial soils should be delineated during the
design phase of the proposed project by conducting a detailed field exploration program
Furthermore the expansive potential of the alluvial soils should also be evaluated during
the design phase Where expansive soils are encountered over excavation of the alluvial
soils may be necessary under foundations slabs on grade and pavements We estimate
that over excavation depth on the order of about 2 to 3 feet may be required in areas
underlain by the expansive soils The resulting over excavation would need to be
backfilled with non expansive select granular fill
Cobbles and Boulders
As mentioned previously rock fills consisting of coralline cobbles and boulders
were placed during the initial mass grading operations In general we believe that rock
fills at the project site were limited to deep fill areas only Therefore the presence of
cobbles and boulders should be anticipated at portions of the project site Excavations for
building foundations and or utility lines may encounter some difficulties due to the
presence of these potential obstructions In addition if deep foundations such as driven
pile foundations will be required for the proposed development the presence of these
potential obstructions would require predrilling prior to pile driving
Cavities and Voids
Coral formations are generally highly variable in terms of hardness weathering
and fracturing In addition cavities and voids are commonly encountered in coral
formation in the vicinity of the project site In many cases cavities and voids in the coral
formation may be filled with loose coralline deposits In areas where coralline formation is

Site Preparation
Detailed grading plans were not available at the time this letter report was
prepared In general the excavated on site materials may be used as a source of fill
materials provided that they are processed to meet the gradation requirements of the
various fill types Due to the potential for presence of expansive soils special attention
should be given to the site preparation aspect of the proposed construction Over
excavation of the subgrades under structures and roadways may be necessary depending
on the expansive nature of on site soils Fill areas may require placement of non
expansive select granular material below future improvements such as building slabs
sidewalks and or pavements
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size but less than
2 feet in largest dimension encountered during site grading may be used in fills provided
that several provisions are followed In general boulders should be placed at least 4 feet
below the finished subgrades and at least 6 feet laterally away from slope faces In
addition fills consisting of cobbles and boulders should be properly choked off at the
surface to reduce the potential for migration of fines through the voids between the
boulders However we do not recommend the placement of boulders at the future
building areas if driven pile foundations are anticipated for the new structures
Foundations
We understand that the details of the proposed structures have not been
determined at the time this letter report was prepared Based on the subsurface
conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that the proposed structures may be
supported on a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings
provided that several provisions are followed
As mentioned previously voids and cavities may be present in the coralline
deposits at the project site To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting
from collapse of the cavities below foundations a program of cavity probing and grouting
of the foundation locations may be necessary during construction In addition due to the
variable nature of the fill materials and potentially expansive nature of the alluvial soils
over excavation of these materials may be required Furthermore column loads for
structures supported on shallow foundations should be limited to about 300 to 400 kips
Structures with column loads greater than 300 to 400 kips should be supported on
a deep foundation system consisting of driven piles or drilled shafts It should be noted
that drilled shaft foundations may be more desirable than driven pile foundations due to
noise considerations at the resort Based on our review of the available subsurface

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 104 extending to depths of about
35 5 to 40 5 feet below the existing ground surface for our current field exploration The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket Penetrometer and Torvane Shear tests were performed on selected
cohesive soil samples retrieved in the field The Pocket Penetrometer test provided an
indication of the unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample The Torvane
Shear test provided a quick estimate of the undrained shear strength of the soil sample
Results of the Pocket Penetrometer tests and the Torvane Shear tests are presented on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized on
Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on
Plate B 3
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 was
performed on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The result of the test is presented on Plate B 4
One Modified Proctor Compaction tests ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The result of the test is presented on Plate B 5
