EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
DRAINAGE SUMP
SLOPE RECONSTRUCTION AT THE ARBORS
EWA BY GENTRY
This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services performed
during the earthwork and grading operations during reconstruction of the drainage sump slope
adjacent to The Arbors from August 19th through November 11th The area of slope
reconstruction is located south of The Arbors development between Fort Weaver Road and the
proposed North South Road within the Ewa by Gentry Development in the district of Ewa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii
SITE GRADING
Plans for the slope reconstruction were prepared by Belt Collins Associates and are
dated Geotechnical guidelines for the project were presented in our reports titled
Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report dated and
Overcast Fill Slope Between Arbors and Sump Area C 1 and C 2 dated
The grading work was performed by RHS Lee Inc Grading in the slope buttress areas
generally consisted of the following

1 Excavation of a keyway to the approximate grades shown on the grading plans
2 Construction of a subdrain along the upslope side of the excavation
3 Placement of granular fill material in roughly horizontal lifts
4 Moisture conditioning of the fill to near optimum moisture content and compaction
of the fill material to at least 90 percent of its maximum dry density The maximum
dry density was determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Grading of the slope outside buttress areas generally consisted of the following
1 Removal of loose existing fill to expose dense coralline material or to a temporary
slope gradient no steeper than 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
2 Where removal of loose fill resulted in slopes lower than planned finish grade
granular fill material was placed in roughly horizontal lifts up to about 3 feet below
the adjacent pad grades of the Arbors development Fill within the upper 3 feet
was contour graded
3 The fill was moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent of its maximum dry density
OBSERVATION AND TESTING
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement and
compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above
grading operations During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were taken

in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The results
of the compaction tests are summarized in the attachments of this report
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to their use
in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the
test results are summarized in the attachments of this report
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as variations in
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Field observations made by our field technician during the mass grading work for the
project indicate that this work was performed in general accordance with the intent of the
recommendations in our geotechnical engineering reports
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that some work may have
occurred without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation
and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project
It should also be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
