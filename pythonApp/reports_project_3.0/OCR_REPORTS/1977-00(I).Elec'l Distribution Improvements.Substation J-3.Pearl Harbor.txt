INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Electrical Distribution
Improvements Substation J 3 at Naval Submarine Base in Pearl Harbor on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated March 16
1989
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve and upgrade the electrical distribution
system in the SUBASE area of Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii As part of the
system improvements it is proposed to construct an addition to the
existing electrical substation J 3 located adjacent to Piers S 11 and
S 12
The proposed addition will be a single story concrete structure with
a slab on grade floor to house a transformer and switch
gear
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to the
Department of the Navy Pacific Division PACDIV dated March 16 1989
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths of 31 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading and foundation design
recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Naval Submarine Base area
of Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The existing Substation J 3 Building
1280 is located adjacent to Piers S 11 and S 12 and surrounded by
Building and 790 The general area is level and covered
with asphaltic concrete pavement
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2

overlying very loose lagoonal silty sand and gravel deposits Stiff

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered medium dense granular
fill material near the ground surface It is our opinion that the upper
medium dense silty sand and gravel fill may provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed addition
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old pavements
demolished walls and other deleterious materials These materials should
be removed and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated and then recompacted to a minimum of
90 compaction as recommended above
Based on the field exploration results most of the on site near
surface soils may be used as fill material If required imported fill
material should consist of non expansive select granular materials less
than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1977 00 I Page 5
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
addition An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the upper medium dense silty sand and gravel fill
layer The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and or seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
New footings should be placed at the same level as the adjacent
existing footings of Substation J 3 In general a minimm footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is
recommended
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be scarified to a depth
of six 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 prior to placement of the
reinforcement The footing excavation should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been obtained
prior to concrete placement

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may
be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor of
the structure A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs provided that
an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be planned for the project A granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3 Fine is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in
maximum 9 inch horizontal lift and mechanically compacted to a minimum of
W O 1977 00 I Page 7
90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect
against potential future ground subsidence
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling two 2 borings to depths of 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the boring
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
