INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our subsurface investigation
performed for the subject project
W O 1568 00 Page 2
The West Beach Resort Development includes golf courses hotels and
apartment structures marina construction and related infrastructure
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and groundwater conditions at the site to provide soil data for use
in the design and construction of the mass grading for the development
site
Other areas of the development such as the marina excavation
building foundations and off site utilities were not included in the
scope of work for this report and will be covered in separate reports
The approximate location of the project and its vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The general mass grading concept consists of filling the site to
provide a gentle overall slope from Farrington Highway down to the
ocean Generally less than about 10 feet of fill is planned for most of
the site except along the proposed lagoon and marina areas where fills
up to 20 feet are planned
Almost all of the embankment material will be generated from
excavation along the shoreline to form the lagoons and marina Some fill
material will be generated from the excavation for the drainage channel
along the eastern portion of the site
Also included in the mass grading work are the access roads and the
lined and unlined drainage channel along the eastern portion of the site
The general layout of the development is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated makai or south of Farrington Highway
between Kahe Point and Honokai Hale Subdivision and extends to the
shoreline
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1568 00 Page 4
The topography is gently sloping down towards the ocean at an
overall gradient of about 3 per cent The ground elevations generally
vary from about 45 to 70 feet along Farrington Highway to 10 feet above
MSL near the shoreline
Most of the area is vacant with many old cane haul roads extending
through the site The area is covered with trees grass and abandoned
sugar cane Many surface boulders are visible particularly along the
northern portion of the site
There is an existing 40 foot wide railroad right of way which
crosses through the middle of the site generally in a northwest to
southeast direction A major fuel oil line is also located within the
railroad easement
The recently constructed Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor is located
on the southeastern side of the area A large stockpile of dredged
material from the harbor construction was noted in the southeastern
portion of the development site The stockpile was about 30 to 50 feet
high and consisted of coral sand and gravel

Soil Conditions
Based on the field exploration the soil conditions at the site may
be generalized into three distinct groups as follows
Rock Formation Basalt rock outcrops are visible along the mauka
side of Farrington Highway and appear to be present at shallow depths at
the northern boundary of the site along Farrington Highway and the
existing subdivision The basalt rock is generally hard and massive
Alluvium Alluvial deposits were encountered in the northern
portions of the site generally north of the railroad easement In the
northwestern portion of the site the alluvium generally consisted of
basalt boulders in a clay matrix over basalt rock formation The clay
matrix material was generally classified as moderately expansive These
GEOLABS HAWAIi
fe boulder deposits can generally be classified as more recent alluvium In
the northeastern portion of the site the alluvium consisted primarily of
fine grained clayey silt to silty clay soils
Coral Formation The southern two thirds of the site is underlain
by a dense near surface coral formation and cemented sand The coral
and cemented sand were found to be generally hard however cavities were
encountered in some of the borings near the water level
The approximate limits of the above soil groups are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Groundwater was encountered in the borings generally at about
elevation 1 to 5 feet above MSL
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration and laboratory tests indicate that from a
soil engineering point of view the site is suitable for the proposed
development
The subsoils are generally stiff and can support the weight of the
proposed fills The cavities encountered in the coral appear to be
filled with soft silts and therefore the overlying coral formation may

be able to bridge over the cavities An attempt could be made to grout
the cavities however due to the extremely large development site this
does not appear to be economically practical
The majority of the fi11 material will be generated from the
excavation of the lagoons and marina and are expected to consist of
suitable sandy soils
Special grading design is planned for the golf course fills due to
landscaping concerns Non potable water will be used for landscape
watering and therefore there is a serious concern for the potential
build up of salts in the surface soils which would be detrimental to the
growth of the vegetation In order to minimize the salt build up in the
near surface soils the soils must be only moderately compacted to allow
proper drainage of the landscape water Since the soils in the golf
courses will not be required to support structural load it is our
opinion that a lower compaction effort may be used
Site Grading
Ground Preparation
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris headwalls and
other structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
wW 0
1568 00 Page 8
The surface soils containing organic matter from previous sugar
cane cultivation should be removed We estimate that this layer
would be about 6 to 12 inches thick
Any local soft spots and soft material in irrigation ditches
encountered within the construction area should be removed and
replaced with compacted fill
Following the clearing and grubbing all areas designated to
receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 The moisture content of the soil
should be above the optimum moisture content
Fill Material and Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site soils are suitable for use in the fill The
soils should be compacted on the wet side of optimum moisture
content to minimize the swelling potential
Granular soil from the lagoon and marina excavations generally
less than 12 inches in size may be used for fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1568 00 Page 9
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in the fill
to provided they are not allowed to nest together and the voids
CO between boulders are filled
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density
In the golf course areas the top four feet of fill should be
compacted to a minimum of 75 but not greater than 85 of ASTM
D 1557 due to landscape watering requirements
Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended for the on site soils
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design
fill slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Slope benches are generally reconmended for slope heights in excess
of 20 feet

Drainage Culvert Structures
It is our understanding that box culverts and pipe culvert
CS structures of varying diameters are planned below the proposed road
alignments

In order to provide sufficient compaction around the culvert and to
minimize possible damage to the culvert from the compaction operations
C the general embankment fill should first be placed in the drainageway up
to 2 feet above the top of the culvert A trench should then be
excavated through the compacted fill to the culvert invert and the pipe
placed in the trench
Trench Backfill
Select on site granular material generally less than 6 inch in
size may be used as backfill to 2 feet above the top of culvert
and could be compacted by water tamping Above this level general
embankment fill of 12 inch minus may be used in accordance with our
4 recommendations for fill
Cushion
A six inch cushion layer of gravel such as S4C or 3 Fine is
recommended below the culvert structures to provide uniform
support

drainage channel at the eastern portion of the site

silts and gravel may be encountered at the invert level An allowable

dense deposit

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill and

specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by

soils report
Te Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite

Preparation of Fill Areas
Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose com
petent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall
be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface

III Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be tested
and approved prior to transportation to the site Fill
material shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter
and other deleterious material and shall not contain
L rocks or lumps in excess of twelve inches 12 in
diameter
IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
which will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed
to attain uniformity of material and water content
within each layer

Appendix C Continued Page 4
Rock fragments or boulders less than two feet 2
in diameter may be utilized provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than two feet 2 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved
for placement in the fill by the soils engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot
vibratory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the
soil type and capable of producing the specified
results in an expedient and efficient manner The
equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
