As requested we conducted a static compression pile load test on a selected test
pile Test Pile No 4 for the Consolidated Training Facility within Hickam Air Force Base
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report presents the results of our field
observations and the test data collected during the static pile load test conducted on
February 27 and 28 2007
Project Considerations
The project site is at the intersection of McChord Street and Moffet Street in
Hickam Air Force Base on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the available plans a
two story training facility is planned at the site The training facility will be supported on
deep foundations consisting of 16 5 inch precast prestressed concrete piles
We understand that 45 foot long piles were estimated in the Contract Documents
Three test piles 45 foot long were driven to a depth of about 43 feet below the existing
ground surface However low penetration resistance was observed during the pile driving
operations Based on the low penetration resistance observed an adequate bearing layer
was not encountered during the driving operations at the full depth of the pile
about 45 feet and the Contracting Officer was notified of the situation
Because an adequate bearing layer was not encountered the Contracting Officer
concurred with our suggestion and directed the pile driving subcontractor to splice the test
piles with a second pile section The test piles were spliced with a second 45 foot long
section Test Pile and 5 were driven to depths of about 49 6 feet 88 feet and
87 2 feet below the existing grade respectively Based on the driving records the
Contracting Officer selected Test Pile No 4 on which to perform a static pile load test

Static Pile Load Test
The static pile load test was conducted on a 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete pile Test Pile No 4 The test pile was driven on February 13 and
15 2007 using a hydraulic hammer delivering a maximum rated energy
of up to about 44 000 foot pounds per blow of the hammer The load test pile was driven
to a depth of about 88 feet below the existing ground surface using two 45 foot long
sections During the driving operations of the load test pile a log of the blow counts
needed to advance the pile was maintained by an engineer from our office The pile
driving record is provided on the attached Plate 1 for ease of reference
The pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D 1143 Standard Loading
Procedure The maximum test load on the pile was 360 kips which was 120 kips more
than stated in the Contract Documents of 240 kips We understand that 120 kips with a
factor of safety of 2 of downdrag was required as part of the load test The load test was
loaded in 30 kip load increments In general each load increment of the load test was held
until the rate of settlement was less than or equal to 0 01 inch per hour At the maximum
test load of 360 kips the load was held for 12 hours to evaluate creep effects After
loading the pile to the maximum test load the test load was unloaded in approximately
four equal decrements and held for at least 1 hour at each decrement The load test
results are presented on the attached Load Test Data Sheets Plates 2 1 through 2 9 and
are summarized as follows
