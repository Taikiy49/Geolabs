PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed reservoir site in order to provide
recommendations for use in the site grading and foundation design of the
proposed facility
The work was generally done in accordance with our proposal of
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling two 2 borings
to depths of 24 5 to 39 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory tests
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kapaa Homesteads district in the
eastern portion of the Island of Kauai The general location of the site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The reservoir site is approximately 1200 feet southeast of the
junction of Waipouli Road and Olohena Road and approximately 100 feet
southwest of the Olohena Road

In general the geomorphology of the area is dominated by broad
gently sloping weathered lava aprons slightly dissected by narrow stream
valleys The proposed reservoir site is situated on a broad gently
sloping surface near the head of a small gully The existing ground
surface varies from approximate elevation of 540 to 550 feet above Mean
Sea Level
The area is presently used as grazing lands for cattle and covered
with short grasses and shrubs
PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
The proposed project consists of a 0 5 M G reinforced concrete
reservoir structure approximately 74 feet in diameter and 16 feet high
Cuts into the hillside up to about 8 feet are planned to create a
level pad area for the reservoir Site filling for sections of the access
roadway is planned
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths of 24 5 to 39 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings revealed that the reservoir site is generally underlain
to the maximum depth drilled by wet stiff to soft orange brown clayey
silt with gravel residual soils from highly weathered basalt A soft
stratum was encountered at approximately 10 feet below the existing ground
surface This soft layer varied in thickness from about 8 feet in Boring
2 to 20 feet in Boring 1 The soil conditions encountered below the very
soft stratum grade from medium stiff to hard

No groundwater was encountered in the borings during the time of our
field exploration but the soils have high in situ moisture contents
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration at the reservoir site generally
encountered compressible underlying soils with high in situ moisture
contents which would experience excessive settlements under the reservoir
loads at maximum water storage conditions
Based on the proposed reservoir slab finished grade our borings and
laboratory data the calculated total settlement of the center of the
reservoir is on the order of 3 inches under the maximm water loads
Estimated footing settlement under an assumed column load of 120 kips is
on the order of 1 inch Therefore it is our opinion that the reservoir
should not be constructed as presently planned unless special alternative
design and construction procedures are followed to reduce the anticipated
settlements We believe a surcharge program or removal of overburden to
balance the weight of the reservoir can be used to reduce settlements due
to the proposed reservoir loads to tolerable limits
Detailed discussions of the recommended alternatives are presented in
Surcharge of Reservoir Site
One alternative to reduce excessive ground settlement due to the
reservoir loads would be to preload or surcharge the site to induce
ground settlements prior to reservoir construction
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1808 00 Page 5
Dk No 28
The total weight of the surcharge should be about 10 feet over the
proposed reservoir finished grade It should be placed in two increments
of about 5 feet each with a one week rest period between intervals to
allow the soft ground to gain strength and to reduce the possibility of
bearing or slope failure
The length of the surcharge period will depend on the amount of
surcharge used If this 10 foot high surcharge is used our estimated
surcharge time would be on the order of 6 months Estimated ground
settlement due to the 10 foot surcharge is on the order of 3 to 6 inches
depending on the thickness of the very soft compressible soil stratum
Settlement gauges should be installed in the area and periodic survey
readings of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual settlement
The readings should be taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to
the soils engineer to determine the actual surcharge time required
After completion of the surcharge program construction of the
proposed reservoir may commence
Lowering of Finished Grade
If the delay in construction time associated with the recommended
surcharge program is not desirable another alternative would be to lower
the finished grade of the proposed reservoir in order to remove sufficient
overburden soil to balance most of the reservoir loads It is our opinion
that by lowering the finished grade 8 feet below the proposed finished
floor elevation most of the reservoir loads would be compensated by the
removal of the existing overburden and thereby the ground settlements
would be reduced

Lowering the finished grade by 8 feet would require a larger cut
volume and a higher cut slope than originally planned Therefore the
reservoir site would require a larger area and subsequent expansion of
property lines
Reservoir Foundations
The borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a very soft
clayey silt soil stratum at approximate depth of 10 feet below the
existing ground surface At the proposed finished grade for both
alternatives described above the subgrade would be underlain by this very
soft soil stratum
In order to provide adequate bearing support for the proposed
reservoir it is our opinion that a gravel mat of at least 3 feet thick
and 4 feet greater than the width of the footing be provided underneath
the bottom of spread and wall footings The gravel mat thickness may be
reduced to 12 inches below the reservoir slab The backfill materials for
the gravel mat should consist of non expansive granular select borrow and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78
Provided that the gravel mat is properly constructed as described
above we believe that an allowable net bearing capacity of 2000 p s f
can be used
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Exterior wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to
utility trenches should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench or
the trench backfill be well compacted to minimize footing settlement

The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from
the reservoir foundations
Settlement
Provided the recommended surcharge program or overburden removal have
been followed and the gravel mat underneath the footing properly
constructed the estimated settlements would generally be on the
order of less than one half inch total and one quarter of an inch
differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 3 can
be utilized to determine sliding resistance of the structure and
foundation
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the ridge about
0 to 16 feet at the reservoir site and a small amount of fill and cut for
the access roadway
Due to the high annual rainfall the natural moisture content of the
on site soils are generally high 40 to 75 Therefore significant
drying out of the excavated material would be required to attain the
recommended compaction criteria Alternatively drier off site material
may be used for the fill

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no filling excluding gravel mat under footing
and slab will be required at the tank site Some filling for the
access roadway may be necessary
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
fill placed should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground
to properly bond the new fill to the slope for details refer to
Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of the fills
The filling operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have high in place moisture contents Therefore it
may not be practicable to dry the excavated soil to near the optimum
moisture content Due to the high in situ moisture content and since
the proposed fills are low it is our opinion that the normal 90
compaction standard used in other drier areas could be modified to
85 for this project
It is anticipated that the existing ridge to the northwest of the 0 5
M G reservoir will be cut about 8 to 16 feet A cut slope ratio up
to 2 1 H V may be used Slope benches are recommended at 20 foot
maximum vertical height intervals

Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with extreme care
by the contractor A backhoe should be used to provide the final
finishing of the cut slope surface Overcutting beyond the design
slope should be avoided since backfilling and recompacting would
create a very loose surface layer which could easily erode
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away and
prevented from flowing over the slope face The slope should be
planted as soon as practical to prevent unravelling due to erosion
Due to the high rainfall in the area it is not uncommon to encounter
seepage zones through gravelly layers exposed in the cut slopes If
encountered special provisions such as horizontal drains or toe
drains may be required to stabilize the cut slopes
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the project roads
Based on the results of our soil exploration program the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas

Relocation of Reservoir Site
If both the surcharge program and lowering the reservoir finished
grade alternatives are not desirable the reservoir site may be relocated
to where the soil is less compressible or where substantial amount of site
cutting to compensate for the weight of the reservoir structure can be
provided Additional borings would be required for the relocated site
Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent for our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to monitor
the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our geotechnical engineering study
Any imported material required should generally be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above evaluation

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied

fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils seepage trash fill or
