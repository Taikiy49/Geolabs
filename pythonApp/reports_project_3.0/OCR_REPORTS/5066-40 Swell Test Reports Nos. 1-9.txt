This letter report provides supplementary recommendations for house foundations
based on laboratory swell testing on samples of the soils at finished grade This letter
report reflects the swell tests performed for additional lots
Project Considerations
The project site is at the end of Makakilo Drive in the Makakilo area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project involves site grading for single family units The site
development also includes the construction of a public roadway system within the
residential zoned portion of the project site and installation of sewer water supply and
lighting facilities
Relatively deep fills were placed for much of the area Most of the fills were
imported to the site Originally structural fill coral was planned to fill the top 3 feet of the
lots However due to unavailability of the structural material import from Mililani and
selected on site soil were used in the top 3 feet of the fill lots
It was desired to conduct laboratory swell testing on the soil samples obtained in
the lot areas that were mass graded near the finished grade to evaluate the swell potential
of the soils
Based on our laboratory swell test results we categorized the on site soils into
four general types as described below
Type 1 Low Expansion Soil with 0 to 4 0 Swell
Type 2 Moderate Expansion Soil with 4 1 to 8 0 Swell
Type 3 High Expansion Soil with 8 1 to 12 0 Swell
Type 4 Very High Expansion Soil with over 12 0 Swell
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California

The house foundation recommendations for concrete slab on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows
Type 1 4 Inch thick minimum concrete slab with wire mesh reinforcement over
4 inches of gravel cushion with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter
footing
Type 2 5 Inch thick reinforced concrete slab over 3 inches of gravel cushion
with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 3 Remove 2 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
Type 4 Remove 3 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of house foundations bearing on the compacted on site clayey
soils or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades This bearing
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of foundations
should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to
placement of reinforcing steel and concrete A Geolabs representative should be
present to evaluate the bearing materials and embedment depths of the foundations
The slab subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition prior to
concrete placement The slab perimeter footing excavation should be backfilled tightly
as soon as practical after the setting of the concrete and sloped to drain water away
from the foundations
Laboratory Swell Testing
As mentioned above the house foundations were divided into four general types
based on the swell potential laboratory testing conducted on the soil samples obtained
near the finished grade of the lots during mass grading operation
In general the soil samples were remolded and tested in 2 4 inch diameter by
one inch high rings air dried overnight and then saturated for 24 hours under a surcharge
pressure of 55 psf Details of the swell test results and recommended foundation and slab
types are summarized on the Summary of Swell Test and Foundation Type
Plates 1 through 7

This letter report provides supplementary recommendations for house foundations
based on laboratory swell testing on samples of the soils at finished grade This letter
report reflects the swell tests performed for additional lots
Project Considerations
The project site is at the end of Makakilo Drive in the Makakilo area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project involves site grading for single family units The site
development also includes the construction of a public roadway system within the
residential zoned portion of the project site and installation of sewer water supply and
lighting facilities
Relatively deep fills were placed for much of the area Most of the fills were
imported to the site Originally structural fill coral was planned to fill the top 3 feet of the
lots However due to unavailability of the structural material import from Mililani and
selected on site soil were used in the top 3 feet of the fill lots
lt was desired to conduct laboratory swell testing on the soil samples obtained in
the lot areas that were mass graded near the finished grade to evaluate the swell potential
of the soils
Based on our laboratory swell test results we categorized the on site soils into
four general types as described below
Type 1 Low Expansion Soil with 0 to 4 0 Swell
Type 2 Moderate Expansion Soil with 4 1 to 8 0 Swell
Type 3 High Expansion Soil with 8 1 to 12 0 Swell
Type 4 Very High Expansion Soil with over 12 0 Swell

The house foundation recommendations for concrete slab on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows
Type 1 4 Inch thick minimum concrete slab with wire mesh reinforcement over
4 inches of gravel cushion with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter
footing
Type 2 5 Inch thick reinforced concrete slab over 3 inches of gravel cushion
with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 3 Remove 2 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
Type 4 Remove 3 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of house foundations bearing on the compacted on site clayey
soils or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades This bearing
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of foundations
should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to
placement of reinforcing steel and concrete A Geolabs representative should be
present to evaluate the bearing materials and embedment depths of the foundations
The slab subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition prior to
concrete placement The slab perimeter footing excavation should be backfilled tightly
as soon as practical after the setting of the concrete and sloped to drain water away
from the foundations
Laboratory Swell Testing
As mentioned above the house foundations were divided into four general types
based on the swell potential laboratory testing conducted on the soil samples obtained
near the finished grade of the lots during mass grading operation
In general the soil samples were remolded and tested in 2 4 inch diameter by
one inch high rings air dried overnight and then saturated for 24 hours under a surcharge
pressure of 55 psf Details of the swell test results and recommended foundation and slab
types are summarized on the Summary of Swell Test and Foundation Type
Plates 1 through 5

This letter report provides supplementary recommendations for house foundation
based on laboratory swell testing on samples of the soils at finished grade
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the end of Makakilo Drive in the Makakilo area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves site grading for single family units The site
development also includes the construction of a public roadway system within the
residential zoned portion of the project site and installation of sewer water supply and
lighting facilities
Relatively deep fills were placed for most of the Increment 1 area Most of the fills
were imported to the site Originally structural fill coral was planned to fill the top 3 feet
of the lots However due to unavailability of the structural material import from Mililani
was used in the top 3 feet of the fill lots
It was desired to conduct laboratory swell testing on the soil samples obtained in
the lot areas that were mass graded near the finished grade to evaluate the swell potential
of the soils
Based on our laboratory swell test results we categorized the on site soils into
four general types as described below
Type 1 Low Expansion Soil with 0 to 4 0 Swell
Type 2 Moderate Expansion Soil with 4 1 to 8 0 Swell
Type 3 High Expansion Soil with 8 0 to 12 0 Swell
Type 4 Very High Expansion Soil with over 12 0 Swell
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California
Castle Cooke Homes Hawaii Inc Page 2

May 25 2006
The house foundation recommendations for concrete slab on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows
Type 1 4 Inch thick minimum concrete slab with wire mesh reinforcement over
4 inches of gravel cushion with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter
footing
Type 2 5 Inch thick reinforced concrete slab over 3 inches of gravel cushion
with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 3 Remove 2 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
Type 4 Remove 3 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of house foundations bearing on the compacted on site clayey
soils or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades This bearing
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of foundations
should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to
placement of reinforcing steel and concrete A Geolabs representative should be
present to evaluate the bearing materials and embedment depths of the foundations
The slab subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition prior to
concrete placement The slab perimeter footing excavation should be backfilled tightly
as soon as practical after the setting of the concrete and sloped to drain water away
from the foundations
Laboratory Swell Testing
As mentioned above the house foundations were divided into four general types
based on the swell potential laboratory testing conducted on the soil samples obtained
near the finished grade of the lots during mass grading operation
In general the soil samples were remolded and tested in 2 4 inch diameter by
one inch high rings air dried overnight and then saturated for 24 hours under a surcharge
pressure of 55 psf Details of the swell testing results and recommended foundation and
slab types are summarized on the Summary of Swell Test and Foundation Type Plate 1
