As requested, Geolabs performed on-site observations of the chemical grouting
operations performed as part of the Liholani Twin Culvert Repairs in Pukalani, Maui, Hawaii.
The twin culverts consist of two 132-inch diameter metal corrugated drainage pipe culverts
that convey water from Kaakakai Gulch across Liholani Street at approximately Station
32+65. The culverts are identified as the North and South Culverts.
The use of chemical grouting was specified to strengthen the existing fill materials
around the culverts. This memorandum provides a summary of the chemical grouting
observations made for the project.
Chemical Grouting Observations
The chemical grouting work was performed from April 5, 2022 to June 2, 2022. The
chemical grouting was performed by Kelikai Inc. The chemical grout was provided by Uretek
USA and consisted of a polyurethane-foaming mixture. The grouting performed for the North
and South Culverts are summarized below.
North Culvert
The grouting work on the Twin Culverts commenced on April 5, 2022, with the drilling
of the grout locations in the North Culvert. The grout locations were noted by letter for the
vertical row location and number for longitudinal hole location (Example A1) and were
spaced at 18 inches in a triangular layout pattern. The distribution of the vertical grout rows
for both North and South Culverts is provided on the Liholani Twin Culverts Probe Sketch,
Plate 1.
The North Culvert contained Rows A through U with 14 longitudinal hole locations for
each row. The grout holes were drilled to a depth of 12 inches. The drilling of the grout
locations for the North Culvert was completed on April 5, 2022, at which time the grouting
started. Probe Holes Ai through D5 were injected with grout, at which point it was
determined that the probe holes were required to be water jetted (flushed) prior to grout
injection. Therefore, the remaining holes of the North Culvert were water jetted on
April 7, 2022. The chemical grout injection resumed on April 8, 2022 up to Probe Hole E14,
at which time the grouting work was shifted to the South Culvert.

The chemical grout injection resumed at the North Culvert on May 24, 2022 and was 
completed on June 2, 2022. Due to the excessive amount of grout intake at the South 
Culvert, an adjustment to the grouting procedure was made by pumping up to 20 clicks into 
the hole, and if not filled, move on to the next hole. At a later time, these probe holes that 
were not filled in the initial grouting were redrilled and grouted. If the amount of grout 
exceeded 20 clicks, the procedure was repeated again until the probe holes were filled.  The 
clicks were used to measure the volume of chemical grout pumped into the probe holes.  
A total of 266 probe holes were grouted with 4,734 clicks (pumps) for a total volume 
of approximately 490 cubic feet (based on the manufacturer's submittal as 9.665 clicks per 
cubic foot). It should be noted that during the grouting operations, it was found that the 
depths to competent material in Rows T and U ranged from 16 to 20 inches, which exceeded 
the depth of the grout holes. Per Structural Sheet S-2.3, the floor of the culvert would be 
excavated to a depth of competent material and backfilled with concrete. Therefore, after 
confirming with the structural engineer, the grouting of Rows T and U were eliminated since 
the grout would be removed for the placement of the concrete. A summary of the grouting 
for the North Culvert is presented on Plates 5 through Plate 11. 
South Culvert 
Work commenced on the South Culvert on April 11, 2022 with the drilling of the probe 
holes and were completed on April 12, 2022. The vertical rows for the South Culvert ranged 
from A to T, and an average of 14 probe holes were drilled per row with the exceptions 
where some probe hole locations for the Number 14 probe would fall on the joint seam of 
culvert sections. At these locations, the Number 14 probe hole was not performed since it 
would be located outside of the grouting limits for the project. The probe hole locations were 
water jetted on April 18, 2022 and were completed in one day. The chemical grout injection 
work began on April 19, 2022 and continued until May 2, 2022, when the project shut down 
due to a low supply of chemical grout fluid as well as an issue with the expansion rate of the 
grout. The contractor obtained more chemical grout fluid and made adjustments to the 
grouting procedure and resumed grouting on May 23, 2022. The grouting procedure 
adjustment described above for the North Culvert was used. 
The grouting of the South Culvert was completed on May 23, 2022. A total of 
272 probe holes were grouted with 7,755 clicks (pumps) for a total volume of approximately 
802 cubic feet (based on the manufacturer's submittal as 9.665 clicks per cubic foot). A 
summary of the grouting for the South Culvert is presented on Plates 12 through 17. 
Results/Remarks 
The grout volumes provided above are based on the manufacturer’s provided 
quantity of 9.665 clicks per cubic foot based on an expansion rate of 17. In addition, the 
finished density of the grout is 4.3 pounds per cubic foot (pcf). Based on the manufacturer, 
these values are based on an unconfined condition and will change for a confined condition

that is similar to the conditions at the project site. Therefore, the actual volume of grout used 
is less than the volume provided above.  
In-situ testing of the grout was performed to try and determine the density of the grout 
in an unconfined condition and in a confined condition. Samples of the chemical grout used 
were tested for density prior to the start of the day from May 23, 2022 to June 2, 2022. The 
unit weights were determined in the laboratory and are presented on Plate 2. Based on the 
results, the density of the grout in an unconfined condition ranged from 3.8 to 9.1 pcf with 
the majority of the grout density from 3.9 to 4.5 pcf. This generally correlates with the 4.3 pcf 
density provided by the manufacturer for the unconfined condition. 
Coring at 26 different locations at the North and South Culverts were performed on 
June 6, 2022 to try and verify the in-place grout density and to evaluate the stabilization of 
the fill materials. The core holes were 2 inches in diameter and extended about 12 inches 
behind the culvert walls. Hard core drilling was encountered during the coring indicating that 
the fill materials around the culvert have been stabilized. Most of the recovered core samples 
had the chemical grout mixed with soil and gravel materials. However, two coring holes 
recovered chemical grout that was free of foreign material for density testing. The in-place 
densities were 5.6 and 17.2 pounds per cubic foot (pcf) with an average in-place density of 
11.4 pcf. The average in-place density in a confined condition is higher than the 4.3 pcf 
density provided by the manufacturer. Results of the density tests are presented on Plate 2, 
and the recovered core photographs are presented on Plates 3 and 4.  
A total grout volume of 1,292 cubic foot was calculated using the manufacturer’s 
conversion for an unconfined condition. Based on the in-situ testing performed, we believe 
that the actual volume of grout used should be less than the volume determined using the 
conversion from clicks to volume provided by the manufacturer.
