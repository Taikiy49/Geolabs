Dear Ms Yanagawa
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and infrastructure installation work for the Makakilo C D Park project
from August 28 2013 through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Makakilo C D Park site is located between Interstate Highway H 1 and
Kulihi Street on the southeastern side of the new Makakilo C D subdivision in the
Makakilo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is also known as Kahiwelo Park The
project site was previously graded during the subdivision mass grading operation
In general the overall project involves the construction of a new park site consisting
of a comfort station a basketball court a baseball yard asphaltic concrete paved parking
lots and driveway other associated infrastructure sewer water and irrigation lines and
landscape areas The layout of the new park site is shown on the Grading Erosion
Control Plan Plan Sheet C 3 as attached for reference
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of less than
about 2 feet thick The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation
and compaction testing during the earthwork and utility trench backfill for the Kahiwelo
Park only

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company Ltd is the general contractor for the project and
performed the site grading operations pavement construction and utility line installation
The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of
fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following clearing and grubbing of the previously mass graded site the excavated
subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted
with a vibratory sheepfoot roller to obtain a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative
compaction as specified on the plans and specifications of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building basketball slab and pavement areas respectively of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor It
should be noted that the materials placed in the landscape areas were also compacted to
a minimum of 80 percent of the maximum dry density as requested by the Department of
Park and Recreation DPR to reduce potential of soil erosion
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 1
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 23 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations

LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and

geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
