INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Fujita Residence Addition in the area of Dowsett Highlands
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
GEOLABS HAWAII

from our analyses based on the data obtained through the field exploration and laboratory
testing
These recommendations are intended for design input only and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Fujita residence is located at 3331 Kamaaina Drive in the Dowsett
Highland area on the island of Oahu We understand that an addition with plan
dimensions of 36 by 20 feet is planned on the northwest side of the existing residence
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
On the basis of information provided to our office the planned addition will be two
2 stories in height and will be located on a relatively flat area on the northwest side of
the existing house The proposed addition is approximately 600 square feet in area with
a finish floor elevation of about 594 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
We anticipate that site grading would consist of minor cuts and fills on the order
of three feet 3 Further details regarding the proposed construction were not available
at the time this report was prepared

for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation
design and site grading for the proposed addition

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 3331 Kamaaina Drive in the Dowsett Highlands area
on the island of Oahu The approximately 4 acre lot is currently occupied by the existing
Fujita residence which is located near the center of the lot The existing ground surface
of the lot slopes down toward Kamaaina Drive with ground elevations varying from about
615 feet MSL at the southeast corner to about 580 feet MSL at the northwest corner
of the lot

The existing residence is situated on a relatively flat area A slope of about eight feet 8
high slopes down toward Kamaaina Drive Several trees were observed on the lot

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our laboratory test results the on site clayey silt soils generally
encountered at shallow depths have a high in situ moisture content with moderate
shrink swell potential The high moisture content of the near surface soils may pose
difficulties for compaction during construction In addition the high moisture content also
indicates that the soil is susceptible to significant future surface soil shrinkage due to
drying under a post and beam raised structural floor design Therefore special attention
should be given to the subgrade preparation and foundation embedment design to
reduce the potential for shrink swell effects We recommend that slab on grade
foundations bearing on a layer of non expansive granular capping fill be used for the
foundation support of the new addition
Site Grading
The proposed finish floor elevation for the new addition is at 594 feet MSL Minor
cuts and fills of up to three feet 3 will be required to provide a level building pad for the
proposed addition Additional cuts and fills are also anticipated to achieve the design
slope on the northwest side of the addition with the required inclination The following
recommendations are presented to provide general guidelines for the site grading work
1 The area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3029 00 Page 6
In areas to receive fill because of the high moisture content of the on site
soils the subgrade should be proof rolled to provide a firm and unyielding
subgrade surface if the subgrade soils have a moisture content in excess
of five percent 5 above the optimum moisture content
If the subgrade soils have a moisture content less than five percent 5
above the optimum moisture content then the subgrade soils should be
scarified to a depth of ten inches 10 moisture conditioned to at least two
percent 2 above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Methods D1557 78
Generally the on site soils may be reused as fill material however
compaction of the materials to the requirement for structural fill may be
difficult due to the high moisture content If the use of on site material for
engineered fill is desired the fill material should be moisture conditioned to
at least two percent 2 above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to between 85 and 90 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
lf imported fill is required the material should consist of non expansive
select granular material less than three inches 3 in maximum size with
less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material
should be tested and approved for its use by Geolabs Hawaii prior to
delivery to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3029 00 Page 7
4 Structural and embankment fills should be placed in loose lift not exceeding
ten inches 10 in thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent of
its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
5 The subgrade for areas to receive slabs and pavements should be scarified
to a depth of ten inches 10 moisture conditioned to at least two percent
2 above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
6 In order to properly bond new fills to the existing ground for the filling on the
slope we recommend that the existing slope be benched Fill slopes
should be constructed by overfilling and then cutting back to the design
slope to provide a well compacted edge on the slope Fill slopes should be
inclined at two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
7 Measures should be incorporated into the grading of the addition to reduce
the potential for erosion and to prevent concentration of water around
structures The ground surface around structures should be sloped away
from structures
Slab On Grade Foundations
The laboratory tests showed that the subsurface soils at the project site have
moderate to high shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture
content Therefore to reduce the potential for distress to the proposed addition due to
the shrink swell effects of the near surface soils we have presented two 2 alternatives
for design of slab on grade foundations
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3029 00 Page 8
Alternative No 1
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the shrink swell of the
on site soils we recommend that non expansive off site select granular material be
used for a minimum depth of 18 inches below the slab subgrade The non
expansive capping materials should extend at least two feet 2 laterally beyond
the perimeter of the proposed structure
The select material should be non expansive granular material such as select
borrow crusher wasie crushed coral mudrock or cinder The material should be
well graded with no particles greater than three inches 3 in greatest dimension
It should not contain boulders or organic and other deleterious material The
material should be tested and approved for its use by Geolabs Hawaii prior to
delivering to the project site
Prior to placement of the capping material if the moisture content of the subgrade
soils is at least five percent 6 above the optimum moisture content the
underlying subgrade should be proof rolled to provide a firm and unyielding
surface Otherwise the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of ten inches
10 moisture conditioned to at least two percent 2 above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to between 85 and 90 percent of its maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The select capping
fill should be placed in maximum of twelve inch 12 thick loose horizontal lifts
moisture conditioned to slightly above optimum moisture content and compacted
to not less than 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3029 00 Page 9
The select capping material should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils have been prepared as recommended The capping fill would
serve as a layer of moist soil to reduce the potential loss of moisture from the
underlying in situ moist subgrade soils The reduction in drying will reduce the
potential shrink swell effects of the underlying saprolite soils
Conventional slab on grade construction may then be utilized for proposed
structures constructed on the above prepared capping granular fill layer However
it should be noted that minor cracks in the slab can be expected Therefore we
recommend that a flexible floor covering be used on the slab We also
recommend that interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to
accommodate some possible movements
The above prepared capping fill slab subgrade areas should be wetted and kept
moist prior to final placement of concrete A minimum six inch 6 thick layer of
3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be placed over the
capping fill to serve as a slab cushion and capillary break A moisture barrier
overlying the cushion fill should be incorporated below the concrete siab to reduce
the potential of damage to the floor coverings by infiltration of moisture One inch
1 of moist fine sand may be placed over the moisture barrier membrane to aid
curing of the concrete
The thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of eight inches
8 below the lowest adjacent exterior grade An allowable bearing pressure of
2000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design The allowable
bearing pressure above is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces

Alternative No 2
The requirement for imported select capping fill material may be reduced by
utilizing modified subgrade preparation and site grading procedures in conjunction
with a deepened thickened edge slab
The thickness of the select capping material may be reduced to six inches 6 if
the thickened edges of the slabs are embedded a minimum of twenty four inches
24 below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The base of the thickened edge
should be a minimum of eighteen inches 18 in width An allowable bearing
pressure of 2000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design The
allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads
The subgrade soils should be prepared as recommended in Alternative No 1
above In order to reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to
reduce the costs of form construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast
neat against the soils
The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom
longitudinal reinforcements The top and bottom bars should be connected by
stirrups The ends of the stirrups should extend into the floor slab area and be tied
to the welded wire mesh A structural engineer should be consulted for design
details of this thickened edge which is intended to function both as a moisture
barrier and as a perimeter wall footing
We recommend that a six inch 6 layer of 3B Fine gravel be used below the
four inch 4 minimum thickness concrete slab as a cushion layer The cushion
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3029 00 Page 11
layer should be lightly tamped to densify it The cushion and slab subgrade
should be thoroughly wetted prior to concrete placement to allow the select
capping layer to remain in a moist condition In order to prevent moisture
infiltration which could damage floor coverings a plastic vapor barrier may be
considered below the slab One inch 1 of moist fine sand should be placed
above the plastic sheet to aid in the curing of concrete
Isolated Footings
Isolated spread footings may be necessary for support of the wood deck in front
of the addition In consideration of the shrink swell potential of the subsurface soils the
spread footings should bear on the stiff saprolite soils and should have a minimum
embedment of thirty inches 30 below the lowest adjacent grade The minimum width
of footings should be not less than 18 inches and the footings may be designed using
an allowable bearing pressure of 2000 pounds per square foot p s f The base of the
foundation excavation should be firm and unyielding Also if spread footings are located
near the top of slopes or on slopes the footings should be embedded deep enough such
that the horizontal distance from the outside edge at the base of the footing to edge of
slope is not less than eight feet 8
All footing excavations should be observed by a representative from our office prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to evaluate the bearing materials and the
embedment depths
Resistance to Lateral Loads and Settlements
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressures exerted
against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the base of footings slabs
and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure equivalent to 300 pounds per cubic
foot p s f and a base friction factor of 0 3 may be used for design

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from visible features shown on the topography map prepared
by KOP Hawaii dated Elevations of the field borings were estimated
by interpolation from the elevations on the same plan The physical locations and
elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Ground waiter levels from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuations may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
