SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is
underlain by a thick layer of highly compressible
lagoonal deposits underlying a thin surface
crust
The proposed structure will be heavily loaded and
we believe it may experience significant
foundation differential settlements if it is
supported by spread footing or mat foundation
bearing on the thin surface crust Therefore we
believe pile foundation should be used to support
the proposed structure
Since a suitable hard bearing stratum was not
encountered to the drilled depths of the borings
up to about 180 feet we recommend that friction
piles driven to a minimum depth of 135 feet below
the existing ground surface be used
An allowable vertical pile capacity of 80 tons
may be used for the friction pile provided no
additional downdrag load is imposed on the pile
shaft
In order to minimize downdrag forces on the
friction piles we recommend that site filling be
avoided or at least minimized to less than one
foot

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation performed for the proposed Hazardous Flammable Storehouse in
Pearl Harbor
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent data for the foundation design
of the proposed structure
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated May 14 1986 included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of four 4 borings laboratory soil
testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data and
report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of this report The approximate location of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of a one story approximately 35 000
square foot storehouse for hazardous flammable goods The structure will
be a steel framed building with 25 foot stacking height tilt up concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII

walls metal roofing and concrete structural slab Maximum wall loads of
about 7 kips per foot are anticipated The proposed building is
anticipated to have heavy design floor loads of about 250 p s f Also
included and adjacent to the main storehouse are a POL storage shed to the
north and a Compressed Gas Cylinder shed to the south Sealed asphaltic
concrete floor is planned for these two storage sheds
Minor site grading including minimal cut and fill is planned for
the proposed storehouse building and sheds along with supporting
utilities sidewalk and parking areas
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located in an existing wood pallet
yard to the east of Building 406 on Neches Avenue in Pearl Harbor Oahu
Hawaii
Building 596 an existing warehouse for the pallet yard is located
on the western corner of the site This building will be demolished
together with several other existing small buildings nearby to provide
space for the proposed construction
The ground level at the site is generally flat and the ground cover
consists primarily of compacted coral gravel fill material The ground
around Building 596 is partially paved with asphaltic concrete and
concrete pavements The yard is partially occupied by stacks of wood
pallets
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by drilling
and sampling four 4 borings to depths of about 151 to 176 5 feet below
the existing ground surface After the completion of our field boring
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1720 00 Page 4
Dk No 15
exploration the preliminary building location was shifted Thus Boring
No B 4 is located outside of the present building limit as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 However it is our opinion that these borings should
still be representative of the anticipated soil conditions below the site
The drilled borings encountered a relatively thin surface fill crust
of only 6 5 to 7 foot thick underlain by a thick layer of soft lagoonal
deposits to depths of about 133 to 139 feet below the existing ground
surface The lagoonal deposits consisted of soft clayey to sandy silts
and loose silty fine sand to sand which have high compressibilities and
low shear strengths
Underlying the thick lagoonal deposits was a medium dense layer of
cobbles and boulders about 10 to 14 feet thick The subsoils then changed
to stiff to hard older alluvium consisting of silty clay and clayey silt
to the drilled depths of the borings A generalized subsurface
cross section is illustrated on Plate 3
The groundwater level was monitored during our field exploration and
was found to fluctuate with tidal conditions A high of 6 7 feet to a low
of 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface were recorded
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the field exploration and engineering analyses it is our
opinion that excessive settlements would occur if the proposed building is
supported by spread footing type foundations
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1720 00 Page 5
Dk No 15
Therefore a pile foundation with structural slab is recommended to
support the proposed structure Since no suitable continuous hard bearing
stratum was encountered to the drilled depths of the borings a friction
pile foundation is recommended Site filling should be avoided or
minimized to less than one foot to minimize downdrag loads on the friction
piles
Hazardous Flammable Storehouse
Several foundation alternatives as described below have been
considered for the proposed structure
Shallow Foundations
Spread or continuous type of footings bearing on the surface crust
were evaluated however the calculated total settlements under the
building load and the heavy floor loads are on the order of 2 and 6
inches respectively and the total settlements under both building
and floor loads would be in excess of 6 inches These settlements
were considered excessive for the new structure
The feasibility of a mat foundation bearing on the thin surface
crust was also analyzed However we believe that the settlements
for a mat would be even larger since the zone of stress influence
below the bottom of the mat would extend much deeper into the
underlying thick and soft lagoonal deposits

The use of a surcharge program to preload the site prior to building
construction to allow the use of footing foundation was also
considered Since the proposed building will be used as a permanent
storage facility for hazardous flammable goods the structural

integrity of the building is of critical importance It is our
opinion that most of the anticipated footing settlements at least
90 to 95 should be induced prior to building construction with
the use of a surcharge program However due to the large
thickness of the soft compressible soils and the high clay content
low permeability in portions of the site particularly near
Boring No 4 a very large quantity of surcharge would be required
over the site to attain sufficient pressure to induce the required
ground settlements within a reasonable construction period
Based on the laboratory test results we estimate that with a
surcharge height of ten feet 10 the required waiting time for
the primary settlements to occur would be in excess of 1 year It
should be noted that it is very difficult to accurately predict the
time required for the surcharge fill settlement to take place since
it is affected by minute details of the soil structure and
deposition For very thick deposits we believe time estimates
could vary by as much as 50 to even 100 Therefore ifa
surcharge fill is used provisions must be made for potential delay
in building construction schedule should a longer settlement
waiting time be required
It is also our opinion that even with a surcharge program some
secondary long term settlements could still occur and cause some
cracking of the building slab In addition to the above concern
the loading distribution from the high surcharge would spread out
to the surrounding areas and therefore the corresponding ground
settlements may affect the existing structures utilities and
roadways situated within the vicinity of the proposed storehouse

Pile Foundation
Based on the above limitations with the use of shallow spread
footing foundations anticipated soft soil conditions and heavy
design loading we recommend that the proposed hazardous flammable
storehouse be supported by a friction pile foundation
The piles should be used to support the proposed structure in
conjunction with a structural slab
A very high pile capacity up to the structural limit of the pile
could be developed if sufficient embedment could be achieved
However our borings disclosed that the site is underlain by an old
stream bed deposit consisting of cobbles boulders and river gravel
at a depth of about 135 to 150 feet It is our opinion that most of
the piles will not be able to penetrate through the cobble and
boulder layer and therefore the pile penetration and corresponding
pile capacity will be limited The cobble and boulder river deposit
was found to be highly variable in composition and thickness and
therefore should not be counted on for high bearing support
The piles would develop their load carrying capacity primarily from
frictional resistance or adhesion between the pile shafts and the
soft lagoonal soil and partially from end bearing on the medium
dense boulder layer
recommended Based on present boring data the piles should be
driven to a minimum total depth of about 135 feet below the existing
ground surface in order to develop sufficient frictional resistance
or adhesion between the piles and the soft soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1720 00 Page 8
Dk No 15
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 32 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 5 blows
per inch for the last 6 inches or 15 blows for the last inch
For the above recommendations an allowable net pile capacity of 80
tons per pile may be used This allowable capacity does not include
any downdrag forces Pile settlements on the order of less than
one half of an inch are anticipated
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven to the required depths and that sufficient
frictional resistance or adhesion has been developed
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
main objectives
1 To determine if pre drilling through the upper fill layer is
required
2 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the driving
criteria
3 To more accurately determine the pile order lengths
To achieve these objectives we recommend that about 10 percent of
the total piles be test driven at the site These test piles can
later be incorporated into the actual foundation
As a part of the test pile program two 2 individual pile load
tests should be budgeted The test piles should be load tested to
two times the design capacity or 160 tons in accordance with the
ASTM D 1143 test procedures

Dk No 15
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot of depth The backfill
against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled
with select materials compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of
the maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Site filling should be avoided or minimized to less than one foot in
order to avoid ground settlements and thus eliminate any downdrag
loads on the piles
POL Storage Shed
A POL storage shed is planned next to the northern side of the new
building The shed will be used to store POL storage drums which may be
stacked as much as four drums high The average pressure from these
storage drums stacked four high is about 500 to 600 p s f
The weight of these drums would cause ground settlements on the
order of 6 to 12 inches These ground settlements would cause
irregularities in the pavement surface which could adversely affect the
forklift operations More importantly the ground settlements would
induce downdrag loads on the adjacent new building piles and cause
building settlements
Ideally the shed should be supported by a pile supported slab
system similar to the new building in order to eliminate the ground
settlements However due to high expense of the pile foundations and
structural slab other alternatives were evaluated

The downdrag forces on adjacent building piles may be minimized by
relocating the proposed shed a sufficient distance away from the
proposed building to minimize the influence of ground settlement

Based on an estimated H V stress distribution the shed
should be moved a minimum horizontal distance of about 70 feet from
the proposed building since the underlying soft subsoils extend to
about 148 feet below the existing ground surface The relocated
shed area should then either be preloaded prior to pavement
construction to induce the anticipated ground settlements or
provisions should be included in future maintenance programs to
resurface the pavement periodically whenever ground settlements
affect the serviceability of the pavements
A surcharge program may be used for the proposed shed area to
minimize these detrimental effects of the settlements
The borings in this portion of the site indicate that the
compressible subsoils in this area may contain more sand than the
other portions of the building site therefore a surcharge fill may
be feasible since the ground settlements are anticipated to occur
somewhat faster in this area Additionally the storage area can
tolerate some long term settlements therefore the settlement
waiting time may be reduced
The average loads from the four levels of POL storage drums are
anticipated to be about 500 to 600 p s f If a surcharge program is
utilized we recommend that the storage drums be limited to two
levels to reduce the load to 200 to 300 p s f If ten feet of
surcharge is placed over the area the estimated surcharge period is
about 6 months The surcharge may consist of loose fill or
concerete blocks with sufficient weight to preload the subsoils
Prior to surcharging all underground utility lines at the surcharge
area and its immediate vicinity should be cleared or relocated to
minimize the influence of ground settlements which may break the
lines and interrupt services

Six 6 settlement gauges and three 3 piezometers should be
installed prior to placing the surcharge fill The gauges should be
read by a surveyor on a weekly basis and the readings transmitted to
the soil engineer for review and to determine when the surcharge
may be removed The piezometers should be monitored by the soil
engineer to supplement the survey data and to further verify the
surcharge period
Compressed Gas Cylinder Shed
The average loads from the compressed gas cylinders are anticipated
to be about 75 p s f which is relatively light We believe that the
induced ground settlements at the proposed shed area and downdrag forces
on adjacent building piles due to this load would be minimal
However to avoid any further significant ground settlements it is
our opinion that the operational stacking arrangement of the compressed
gas cylinders should be limited to 1 stack so that the anticipated floor
loads of about 75 p s f would not be exceeded
Site Grading
It is our understanding that minimal site grading is planned for the
proposed building
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish old foundations and pavement should
be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination cf the excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the area to receive minimal fill and
cut for the proposed building should be scarified at least 6 inches below
the cleared grade moisture conditicned and recompacted to 95 of its
maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
GEOLABS HAWAII

Any loose spots encountered after initial site clearing should be
removed and replaced with select on site soil or with suitable imported
fill material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The on site sandy soil of the surface crust may be used for fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 of its maximum density based on ASTM
D 1557 78

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the surcharging site grading subgrade
preparation and pile driving be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction and bearing have been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
The moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of the tests are presented on the Log of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Nineteen 19 sets of torvane tests were performed on undisturbed
samples The results are presented on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample of the
underlying soils to further identify its physical characteristics The
test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One 1 sieve analysis test was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate its grain size distribution The test result is summarized on
Plate B 1
One 1 direct shear test was performed on a selected sample of the
underlying soil to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the soil
The test result is presented on Plate B 2
Four 4 consolidation tests were performed on the natural soils to
evaluate their settlement characteristics The curves are presented on
Plates B 3 through B 6
Six 6 unconsolidated undrained triaxial tests and one 1
consolidated undrained triaxial test were performed on undisturbed shelby
tube samples to evaluate their undrained strength The test results are
presented on Plates B 7 through B 13
