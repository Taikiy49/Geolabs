INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Hickam Golf Course Irrigation System Upgrade project
located within Hickam Air Force Base in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Mamala Bay Golf Course within Hickam Air Force
Base in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to upgrade
the existing irrigation system at the Mamala Golf Course Based on the information
provided the proposed project will consist of construction of a 1 0 million gallon M G
open reservoir of approximately 9 feet deep a pump station and approximately 750 feet
of water transmission line extension
We understand that the proposed 1 0 M G irrigation reservoir will have plan
dimensions of approximately 100 by 350 feet At the time this report was prepared the
top elevation of the reservoir berm and the bottom elevation of the reservoir are set at
about 11 5 and 2 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL respectively The maximum elevation
of the water surface for the irrigation reservoir will be at approximately 10 5 feet MSL
Based on the existing ground elevations of about 6 to 8 feet MSL we envision that the

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3466 00 Page 5
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes came to an
end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
During the recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with soft and loose lagoonal deposits These deposits were subsequently
covered by man made fills placed during the middle portion of this century for land
reclamation projects during construction of the existing Hickam Air Force Base

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven borings to
depths of about 11 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings encountered fills consisting of medium dense sands to
depths of about 3 to 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below the near surface
fills a layer of soft to medium stiff silty clay ranging from about 0 5 to 2 feet in thickness
was encountered in Boring 4 and 6 This silty clay layer was then underlain by
silty sands and sands with gravel to the maximum depth explored of about 16 5 feet

underlain by loose to medium dense sands to the maximum depths explored of about
11 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface with the exception of Boring No 5
where a layer of clayey silt of about 2 5 feet in thickness was encountered between the
sandy soil layers
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 4 5 to 7 5 feet
below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater levels
encountered generally correspond to an elevation of about 1 foot Mean Sea Level
MSL Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean it is likely that the
groundwater level would be affected by tidal fluctuations In addition groundwater levels
are also subject to change due to seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and
other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by sands and
gravels ranging from loose to medium dense in relative density extending to the
maximum depth drilled of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Thin layers
of soft to medium stiff silty clays of up to 2 5 feet in thickness were interbedded within the
sandy soil deposits
Groundwater levels encountered in our borings were measured at an elevation of
about 1 foot MSL at the time of our field exploration Due to the proximity of the site to
the Pacific Ocean we anticipate that the groundwater levels would be influenced by tidal
fluctuations To reduce the potential for damage to the impervious liner system due to the
buoyant uplift forces and to reduce the potential for difficulties during subgrade
preparation the bottom of the proposed irrigation reservoir should be designed to be
about 1 to 2 feet above the measured groundwater level
Based on the proposed reservoir bottom elevation at 2 5 feet MSL and the
schematic reservoir sections we anticipate that the bottom of the reservoir will be near
the groundwater level Therefore special attention should be given to the earthwork
recommendations presented herein to reduce the potential for pumping subgrade
conditions when performing earthwork operations near the groundwater level In addition
soft soils were encountered near the bottom of the reservoir in Boring and 6
Therefore provisions should be made in the contract documents to remove and replace
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3466 00 Page 8
the soft soils encountered during construction For support of the proposed pump station
we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the in situ sandy soils may be

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris generated from demolition
of the existing structures rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the
excavated materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3466 00 Page 9
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing and grubbing
operations should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with approved on site fill material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the
over excavated subgrade should be proof rolled to obtain a firm and unyielding
subgrade It should be noted that soft soils were encountered near the bottom
elevation of the reservoir in Boring and 6 Therefore loose and or soft
soils may be encountered in localized areas at the bottom of the reservoir Where
soft soils are encountered the soft soils should be removed or over excavated to a
maximum depth of 2 feet below the finish subgrade The resulting over excavation
should be lined with a filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent and backfilled
with No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation We recommend that the
contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Following clearing and grubbing subgrades to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of
fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Based on our field exploration the proposed bottom of the reservoir will be near
the groundwater table Therefore in lieu of scarifying and compacting the bottom
of the reservoir subgrade we recommend that the finish subgrade of the reservoir
bottom approximately 2 5 feet MSL be proof rolled with a smooth drum roller
10 ton minimum to reduce the potential for pumping conditions of the subsoils

Fill and Backfill Materials
Generally the on site granular materials may be reused as a source of fill and
backfill materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension However soft and wet silty and clayey soils
were encountered in our borings therefore silty and clayey soils generated from
the excavation should not be used as fill and backfill materials and should be
disposed of off site The fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
The fill and backfill material within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade in
roadway areas should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction The finish grade of the reservoir should be proof rolled with a
smooth drum roller 10 ton minimum in order to obtain a relatively smooth surface
for placement of the impervious liner system
Observations and soil density tests should be performed during grading operation
to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the
proper moisture content on each fill lift Where compaction is less than required
additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content
as necessary to obtain the specified compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3466 00 Page 11
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
If imported fill materials are needed for fill and backfill materials the imported fill
and or backfill materials should consist of non expansive select granular materials
In general the imported material should consist of non expansive material such
as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well
graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more
and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill material
should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the materials encountered from our field exploration cut and fill slopes
should be designed with an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter for the proposed irrigation reservoir Fills placed on existing slopes having
slope ratios steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the firm and
stable materials to enhance the stability of the new embankment fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design limits
of the slope and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate compaction
at the edges of the slope The finished fill slopes should consist of a smooth
surface for placement of the impervious liner system

Settlement
Fills on the order of about 4 to 6 feet will be required for construction of the
reservoir embankment at the project site Because soft clays up to 2 feet thick and
loose sands up to 5 feet thick were encountered in our borings we believe that the new
fills planned would induce some ground settlements as a result of consolidation of the
underlying soft and loose soils We estimate settlements on the order of about 2 inches
may occur due to the anticipated 4 to 6 feet of new fills Due to the granular nature of
the sandy soils and the relatively thin soft clay layer we believe that the majority of the
settlements would occur within about 4 to 6 weeks after the fill placement
Pump Station Foundation
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system may be used to support the proposed pump station
structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the medium dense in situ soils This allowable bearing pressure is
for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such
as temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the total footing
settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with
differential settlements of approximately inch
Footing subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction If loose and or soft soils are observed at the footing subgrade level they
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting over excavation
should be backfilled with open graded material such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or lean concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3466 00 Page 13
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward
from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
foundations A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the
foundation and the supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be
calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
groundwater table An equivalent fluid pressure of 150 p c f may be used for foundations
below the groundwater table These values assume that the concrete for footings is
placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below the finished grade should be
neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths

Impervious Liner and Underlining
We understand that a synthetic liner will be provided for the open irrigation
reservoir to reduce water infiltration through the underlying relatively permeable material
It is our opinion that the material type performance installation and protection details of
the impervious liner should be designed in accordance with the recommendations by the
liner manufacturer It is critical that the liner maintain its integrity to prevent saturation and
build up of seepage water pressures within the fill embankment
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicates that there is
sometimes a tendency for the liners to become detached at the top of the water
impoundment embankment It appears that this may be the result of inadequate
anchoring of the top of the liner and insufficient flexibility of the liner material It is our
recommendation that the liner manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction
detailing and installation of the liner with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce
the potential of liner failure Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the
impervious liner near the top anchoring should also be considered
Technical representatives of the manufacturers of the impervious liner should be
required to be on site at all times during installation to assure that proper construction
procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative of the supplier s may
not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function

of the project In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes Where soft loose soils
are encountered at the pipe inverts a stabilization layer consisting of at least 18 inches of

No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a filter fabric such as

For the trench backfill from the bedding material up to about 12 inches above the

subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction

indicative of a highly corrosive environment to steel and metals in contact with the soils

LIMITATIONS

reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report

also approximate having been estimated by interpolation from the same map The
physical locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times shown on
the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall seepage infiltration variable

APPENDIX A
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling seven borings to
depths of about 11 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck
mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the test are presented on Plate B 1
Four sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plates B 2 and
B 3
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the drained shear strength characteristics Results of the test are
presented on Plate B 4
Three sets of pH ASTM D 2976 and minimum resistivity tests ASTM G 57 were
performed on selected samples of the soils to evaluate their corrosion potential of the
materials Results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 5
