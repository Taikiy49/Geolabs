INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the foundations site
preparation and earthwork for the proposed expansion of the Honouliuli
Wastewater Treatment Plant project in Ewa on the island of Oahu
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions within the proposed plant expansion area in order
to develop a generalized soils rock data set for the formulation of
engineering design recommendations pertinent to the proposed wastewater
treatment plant expansion

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site except for the proposed odor control building
is situated to the north of the existing Honouliuli Wastewater
Treatment Plant facilities in Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Most of the proposed expansion site is relatively level open
grassed area which appears to have been previously graded Within the
project site there are also an asphaltic concrete roadway and areas
covered with gravel
The existing ground surface elevations of the site range from
approximately 30 to 40 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of
plant facility expansion at the existing Honouliuli Wastewater
Treatment Plant in Ewa on the island of Oahu The major improvements
consist of the following construction
1 Two 2 new primary clarifier tanks approximately 145 feet in
diameter
2 A new preaeration tank
3 A new effluent channel discharge line
4 An odor control building
It is anticipated that general site grading will be minimal
However the installation of the primary clarifiers and preaeration
tanks will require excavation to depths on the order of ten 10 to
twenty 20 feet below the existing ground surface

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain on the
southern coast of the island of Oahu This coastal plain is composed
of a thick wedge of interbedded marine deposits and terrestrial
sediments which have accumulated on the southern flanks of Waianae and
Koolau the two extinct volcanoes which built the island
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels throughout the
world fluctuated in response to the growth and retreat of the great
continental glaciers As the glaciers grew less water was available
to fill the ocean basins and sea levels fell to stands below current
sea level As the glaciers melted and retreated more water became
available and sea levels rose to stands often above the current level
When the stand of the sea was high and prolonged marine deposits
accumulated at elevations above the current sea level
At the project site the near surface materials are composed of
coral algal reef deposits which are primarily a medium hardness
limestone with a open lattice like structure It is believed that
these coral deposits were lain during the Kaena 95 feet MSL stand
of the sea and subsequently planed to near their current elevation
during the Waimanalo 25 feet MSL stand The coral formation has
also been subject to subaerial weathering
Coral algal reef materials are chemically composed primarily of
calcium carbonate During subaerial weathering slightly acidic rain
and groundwater slowly attack the carbonate dissolving the weaker
portions of the formation This creates solution cavities ranging from
small pores to large caverns

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of thirteen 13 borings to depths ranging
between 15 0 and 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface using
auger and core drilling techniques The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The logs of the borings
are presented in Appendix A of this report along with detailed
descriptions of the field exploration
In general the borings encountered a surface layer of very stiff
to hard brown clayey silt which is also slightly expansive when
wetted with some sand representing a surface soil layer or imported
topsoil placed during the previous site grading This stratum extended
from the ground surface to a maximum depth of about 5 5 feet below the
ground surface at Boring No 1 location with an average thickness on
the order of about one 1 to two 2 feet In some borings the
surface fill was underlain by dense sand and gravel fill material to
about two 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the slightly expansive surface fill our exploration
encountered moderately hard tan to white coral algal reef formation
This coral was generally hard very competent and had few apparent
voids to a depth of about fifteen 15 feet below the existing ground
surface At greater depths in most of the borings the coral became
softer more friable and tended to have more voids Most of the voids
encountered during our exploration appeared to be infilled at least
partially with medium dense sand and gravel __
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings
during the time of our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The underlying coral formation
encountered at shallow depths below the surface of the project site
should provide adequate bearing for the proposed wastewater treatment
plant expansion facilities However due to the potential existence of
cavities within the coral material we recommend a program of cavity
detection at foundation locations be conducted during construction
This will be discussed in detail at a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
project grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation deleterious
materials and the dark brown clayey silty top soil The resulting grub
and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site the dark
brown clayey silt topsoil could be stockpiled for use in landscaping
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing
operations within the portions of the grading limits designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
The on site coral material if reduced to generally less than
twelve 12 inches in size should be a source of adequate fill
material Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
dated September 1986
The on site coral material if reduced to less than three 3
inches in size should also be a source of adequate trench and
structural backfill material Imported material if required should

Excavation
Generally the in situ coral formation should be rippable by a
bulldozer or other heavy equipment Due to the nature of the coral
formation encountered at the site it is anticipated that some hard
excavation e g hoerams and or chipping may occur in isolated
areas Therefore allowance should be made for the additional expense
and time to perform the hard excavation
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing
should be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the
work However excavations in competent coral formation normally can
stand up unsupported at relatively steep angles for temporary
construction purposes

Foundation Support
We recommend that the major structural foundations bear on the
underlying in situ coral formation or on compacted structural fill
placed in accordance with the recommendations presented herein For
the design of foundations bearing on in situ coral a higher allowable
bearing capacity of up to 8 000 p s f may be utilized For
foundations bearing on structural fill consisting of on site excavated
coral material less than 12 inches in maximum size compacted to 90
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method
utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the
soft and or weak materials down to in situ dense coral formation and
backfilling of the resultant void with fill material compacted in
accordance with the above compaction recommendations

Cavity Probi
The field exploration encountered coral reef formation at shallow
depths below the project site Cavities or voids were encountered in
Boring Nos B 7 and B 10 during our exploration Previous experience
indicates that coral formations are discontinuous and that soft pockets
and or cavities are commonly found in coral formations
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void situated
below a foundation causing settlement and or foundation failure we
recommend that the foundation subgrades be probed The probes should
consist of 3 inch minimm diameter probe holes drilled to a depth of 10
feet below the bottom of the footing Probe holes should be drilled at
each colum location and at 10 foot centers along continuous mat or
strip footing foundations
The probe holes should be subsequently backfilled with a neat
cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage
fashion i e from the bottom of the probe hole up to the top If
large cavities greater than one foot in height are encountered during
the probe drilling or if grout takes exceed four 4 times the
theoretical volume of the probe hole a mortar like grout 1 sand to 1
cement should be injected instead of the neat cement grout If the
grout intake exceeds one half cubic yard it should be stopped and let
set first prior to continuation of the remaining grouting backfilling
operations

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of some of the proposed
structures will have interior concrete slabs on grade It is
anticipated that the slabs and their cushions may be supported by dense
coral or compacted fill A 4 inch layer of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel
should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade
should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane should he
considered below the slabs

provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork phase of the work including the probing and grouting

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information cbtained from field data points such as visual
observations and drilled borings Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping and estimation from reference points located in the field by
and shown on the Honouliuli WWIP Existing Topo plan provided by P
Associates Inc Elevations of the data points were determined by
interpolation from the contours and elevation points shown on the same
plan The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors
