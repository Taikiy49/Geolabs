Existing Pavement Surface Condition
The 1970 Dames Moore report indicated that the then existing
concrete pavement surface was seriously cracked and the asphaltic
concrete surface was also oxidized and cracked at random locations
In 1982 approximately 8 years since the apron rehabilitation
project was carried out in 1974 a survey of the then existing crack was
carried out by Geolabs Hawaii The observations indicated that cracking
existed in most parts of the concrete apron while the asphalt concrete
surface was in generally good condition The most severe cracking
occurred at the central section of the apron area especially on location
nearest to the terminal building Very little to no cracking occurred at
both ends of the apron
The current surface crack condition follows similar pattern as
observed in 1982 with the exception that new cracks was found in the
asphaltic apron area The current observations also indicated that most
of these cracks had been backfilled

Existing Subgrade Condition
It appeared that most of the borings indicated that the subgrade was
formed by a residual soil consisting mainly of clayey silt with some
highly decomposed basalt gravel Under the FAA classification system
the material belonged to the Soil Group E 7 and hence together with
poor drainage condition was classified as F 5 subgrade The CBR FAA
Subgrade Class Comparison Chart indicated that the corresponding CBR
value for F 5 class subgrade was between 8 and 9
Field and laboratory CBR tests had been performed by both Dames and
moore and Geolabs Hawaii The results indicated a large variation
Laboratory values varied from 6 5 to 41 0 with a mean of 21 and a
standard deviation of 13 In accordance with the FAA Advisory Circular
design criteria the recommended design CBR value is
generally selected as the value which is one standard deviation below the
mean of the test values Therefore based on the laboratory test
results the design CBR value for the residual silt subgrade soil would
be 8

Discussion and Conclusion
General
The pavement analysis carried out earlier in this report indicated
that the existing pavement thickness is Significantly less than that
390 000 and 460 000 pounds respectively Therefore unless further
improvement can be carried out to the existing pavement the latter
should be considered inadequate to serve the abovementioned aircrafts
Methods of Improvement
The methods of improvement can in general be divided into the
following main catagories
1 Improvement of the subgrade so that a higher subgrade CBR value
or modulus value can be achieved
2 improvement to the base course material so that a reduction in
the design pavement thickness can be achieved and
5 overlay the existing pavement so that the required adequate
pavement thickness is achieved
These three methods will be discussed individually and presented as
follows
Improvement of Subgrade
Improvement of the subgrade within the study indeed can only be
considered hypothetical First of all it will be unlikely to
perform grouting to the existing subgrade soil because of its fine
grain nature Secondly it would take a major effect also almost
impossible to convince the FAA to reclassify the existing subgrade
so that a higher subgrade CBR value can be used in the design
Furthermore the deteriorating condition at the airport simply
suggests that such an attempt will meet with refusal
W O 1446 00 Page 8
Improvement of Base and Subbase
Successful improvement to the pavement of Keahole Airport at the
island of Hawaii by injecting grout into the base and subbase
material have been carried out
The structural benefit imparted to a pavement section by such
improved base and subbase is reflected in the modulus of subgrade
reaction assigned to the foundation Workable as it is the
improvement method by grouting will face some significant drawbacks
when applied to the study area in the Kahului Airport The major
reason will be the already existing poor drainage condition of the
subgrade soil Grouting of the base course layer will further
deplete the chance of proper drainage Subsequently the subgrade
soil will be saturated and weakened from moisture that migrated
through cracks and construction joints in the pavement surface
during rainy season causing deterioration to the pavement surface
Besides the drainage problem it is anticipated that the possible
amount of improvement for the base and subbase will be insufficient
to achieve a significant increase in strength of the pavement for
the design aircraft types with their maximum loadings

Conclusion
The study concluded that the existing pavement thickness at the
apron and taxiway A area of Kahului Airport is inadequate to serve the
weight
Different methods of improvement to the existing pavement had been
considered and it appeared that pavement overlay will provide the most
assuring solution For the concrete apron depending on different method
of overlay 6 to 10 inches of concrete overlay would be required For
the flexible pavement the bituminous overlay required would be 7 inches
