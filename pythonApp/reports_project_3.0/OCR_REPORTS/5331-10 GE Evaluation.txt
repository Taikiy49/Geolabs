GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
PAVEMENT SECTION FOR RTG VEHICLES
RECONSTRUCTION OF PIER 51B CONTAINER YARD
Dear Mr Luke
In accordance with our fee proposal dated this letter report
provides pavement design recommendations for Rubber Tired Gantry RTG crane
vehicles for the Reconstruction of Pier 51B Container Yard project located within Honolulu
Harbor in the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the parameters used in our pavement analysis and
presents our recommended pavement design section for the RTG crane vehicles for the
proposed project The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Our office issued a previous report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Reconstruction of Pier 51B Container Yard Honolulu Harbor Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
dated The project involves replacing the pavement system at the
container yard approximately 16 acres in area and the drainage and water system within
the Pier 51B facility at Honolulu Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The new pavement in the previous report was designed for loading from current
vehicles transporting containers We understand that Horizon Lines the current tenant of
the parcel will utilize a different vehicle for container operations Therefore the pavement
thickness design will need to be checked for this new vehicle
Existing subsurface data was used in the revised pavement design analysis
therefore new borings were not drilled

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our previous subsurface exploration at the project site generally encountered an
existing pavement structure comprised of about 4 to 6 inches of asphaltic concrete
underlain by about 7 to 22 inches of aggregate base course followed by about 12 to
38 inches of aggregate subbase material The existing pavement structure was underlain
by a fill layer to depths of about 5 8 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface The fill
layer generally consisted of loose to medium dense sand and clayey sand and medium
stiff silty clay Below the fill layer a lagoonal deposit was encountered to about 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled The lagoonal deposit
generally consisted of very loose to medium dense silty sands and gravels and very soft
clayey silt with fine sand Groundwater levels were encountered from depths of about 6 to
6 9 feet below the existing ground surface
Since the project is in close proximity to the ocean the groundwater levels at the
project site is anticipated to be relatively close to the nearby tidal levels and fluctuations It
should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
PAVEMENT DESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that a rigid pavement will be utilized to support the RTG crane
vehicle traffic for the project In addition we understand that the RTG crane vehicle
consists of a rubber tired moveable gantry crane vehicle with four tires in line on each side
of the crane The distance between the set of tires is 77 feet and the distance between
each tire is about 8 feet 2 5 inches Based on design traffic information provided by
others we understand the design number of load repetitions is 540 passes of the
RTG crane vehicle with four wheels per side each week The design procedures used in
determining the new Portland concrete pavement section is based on the Design of
Heavy Industrial Concrete Pavements developed by the Portland Cement Association
Based on design vehicle and traffic information provided by others the following
parameters were used for our pavement analyses

Subgrade Modulus of Reaction
We anticipate the pavement subgrade soils will consist of the recompacted granular
fill materials encountered near the surface of the site Based on the above assumptions
and a properly prepared subgrade we recommend that the following rigid pavement
section be used for preliminary design and budgeting purposes
Heavy Industrial Concrete Pavement Section
16 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete Minimum Flexural Strength of 650 psi
16 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Compacted Granular Fill
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 AASHTO T 180 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The Portland cement concrete should have a minimum flexural strength of
650 pounds per square inch psi at 28 days when tested in accordance with ASTM C 78
We recommend that CBR and field density tests be performed on the actual pavement
subgrade materials encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
recommended pavement sections
