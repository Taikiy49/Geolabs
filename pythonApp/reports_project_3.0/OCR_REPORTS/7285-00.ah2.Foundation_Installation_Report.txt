Dear Mr. Agapay: 
Geolabs observed the testing and installation of the auger cast-in-place (ACIP) 
piles and the drilled shafts for the Waipahu Station – Farrington Highway Station Group 
project on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical 
observations during the ACIP pile and drilled shaft foundation installation. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering services for the Waipahu Station – 
Farrington Highway Station Group project in general accordance with the requirements 
outlined in the project technical specifications (Section 31 63 30 – Drilled Concrete Shaft 
Foundations and Section 31 63 16 – Auger Cast Grout Piles). The purpose of our 
geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the 
ACIP pile and drilled shaft foundation installation in conformance with the plans and 
specifications, as part of the “Special Inspection” in general accordance with Item 1704 of 
the 2006 International Building Code.

Geotechnical recommendations for the ACIP pile foundations were presented in the 
Geotechnical Calculations Report dated February 28, 2017. The purpose of our 
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the ACIP 
pile and drilled shaft foundation installation for general conformance with our 
recommendations and project specifications. 
ACIP Test Pile Program 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the 
ACIP pile foundation, bi-directional axial pile load tests were conducted on two sacrificial 
ACIP piles, designated as TP-1 (mauka side) and TP-2 (makai side), on April 7 and 
June 19, 2017, respectively.

The pile load tests were conducted on two 24-inch diameter ACIP piles with lengths 
of approximately 118.8 and 118.2 feet below the existing ground surface for TP-1 and 
TP-2, respectively. To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil 
interface, the load test piles were instrumented with multiple levels of sister bar strain 
gages installed at various depths for each load test pile. The locations of the two test 
ACIP piles (TP-1 and TP-2) installed are shown on the Foundation Location and 
Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
A Manitowoc 4100W Crawler Crane equipped with a Bauer/Berminghammer ACIP 
BG46 drill, automatic monitoring equipment and a continuous flight hollow-stem auger was 
used to install the test piles. A hydraulic concrete pump was used to pump the grout into 
the pile system. The test piles were load tested in general accordance with ASTM D1143, 
Procedure A - Quick Test Method. The maximum applied test loads on the piles were 
1,523 and 2,017 kips for TP-1 and TP-2, respectively. 
The bi-directional load test results were summarized in the following reports by 
Applied Foundation Testing: 
“Final Report of AFT Cell Bi-Directional Load Testing, Waipahu Transit 
Center Station, Mauka Entrance Test Pile – Waipahu, HI (AFT Project 
No. 516034),” dated April 17, 2017.  
“Final Report of AFT Cell Bi-Directional Load Testing, Waipahu Transit 
Center Station, Makai Entrance Test Pile - Waipahu, HI (AFT Project No. 
516034),” dated July 7, 2017. 
Based on our field observations and the results of the static pile load tests, it is our 
opinion that the ACIP pile load tests were performed exceeding the specified maximum 
test load without failure, meeting the project specification. 
 Production ACIP Pile Installation 
The production ACIP pile installation, consisting of installing 66 ACIP piles (24-inch 
diameter), was conducted from March 24 through June 30, 2017 under the 
near-continuous (full-time) observation of a Geolabs field representative. Automatic 
monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of 
providing auger depth, grout volume, grout pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis.  
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed 
are shown on the Foundation Location and Identification Plan, Plate 2. A summary of 
pertinent information for the production ACIP piles is presented in the ACIP Pile 
Installation Summary, Plates 3.1 through 3.4.

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
One method shaft and 4 production 72-inch diameter drilled shafts were drilled to 
depths of about 151.5 feet and 171.9 feet below ground surface for the mauka and makai 
sides, respectively. The purpose of the method shaft was to demonstrate the adequacy of 
the proposed construction methods and equipment planned for use on the production 
shafts. The method shaft was installed between April 14 and May 4, 2017. The production 
shafts were installed between May 10 and June 22, 2017 for the project. The approximate 
drilled shaft locations and identification numbers are shown on the Foundation Location 
and Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
The drilling and concrete placement operations were conducted under the 
near-continuous observation of a Geolabs field representative.  During the drilled shaft 
construction, logs were maintained for each drilled shaft, which included information 
pertaining to shaft number, size and length of the shaft, soil conditions encountered during 
excavation and concrete placement.  A summary of the drilled shaft installation is 
presented on the attached Drilled Shaft Installation Summary, Plate 4. 
The production drilled shafts for the project were drilled with a Soilmec SR100 drill 
rig equipped with a 66-inch diameter digging bucket and a 72-inch diameter auger, digging 
bucket and clean-out bucket. Permanent steel casing with a diameter of 78 inches was 
installed to elevations ranging from -60.0 to -75.3 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) in the 
method and production drilled shafts. The production drilled shafts were excavated down 
to tip elevations ranging from -141.5 to -162.0 feet MSL. Upon drilling to the tip elevations, 
all loose/soft spoils were removed from the excavation with the 72-inch diameter clean-out 
bucket. 
After each shaft was excavated to the tip elevation and cleaned out, the drilling 
contractor placed the rebar cage in the excavated shaft to the design elevation. Concrete 
was placed into the excavated shafts by using tremie method. The shaft concrete was 
provided by HC&D, LLC with a design compressive strength of 4,500 psi. Each shaft was 
inspected by a Geolabs representative prior to rebar and concrete placement.
