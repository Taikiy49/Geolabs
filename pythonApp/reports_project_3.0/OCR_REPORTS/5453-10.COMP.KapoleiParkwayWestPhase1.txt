This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the roadway pavement and utility construction for the Kapolei Parkway West Phase 1
project from through in general accordance with the
scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Based on the information provided the project involved the construction of eight
lanes of the new Kapolei Parkway Extension west of Kalaeloa Boulevard and the
intersection improvements and modification upgrades for the Kalaeloa Boulevard and
Kapolei Parkway intersection in the City of Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In
addition the project also included the construction of a private drainage basin just to the
west of the existing Honolulu Advertiser Production and Distribution Building
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California
Engineering Concepts Inc Page 2
Based on the information provided the project involved clearing and grubbing of
approximately 22 acres of project site mass excavation of approximately 110 000 cubic
yards of materials installation of various underground utilities including sewer drain
water electrical telephone cable gas and irrigation construction of concrete curb gutter
and box culvert and installation of the roadway pavement section It should be noted that
testing for concrete asphalt paving and asphalt concrete base were specifically excluded
from our scope of work for this project The layout of the new roadway alignment is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
Delta Construction Company is the general contractor who also performed the
earthwork for the project The earthwork operation consisted of clearing and grubbing of
the existing ground over excavation and fill placement preparation of pavement
subgrade placement and compaction of aggregate subbase and cushion fill and utility
trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operation the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 32 and 38 inches below the finished pavement grades
to provide the recommended pavement structural sections for the Kapolei Parkway
West and Kalaeloa Boulevard respectively Prior to the placement of the aggregate
subbase the pavement subgrade was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Imported coralline material used as a source of aggregate subbase in the
pavement structural sections was moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utility trench
backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Where trenches were below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement finished grade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Pumping subgrade condition was encountered at the bottom of the subbase layer
along the east side of Kalaeloa Boulevard from about to about
The existing pumping subgrade was over excavated by approximately 12 inches and
replaced with compacted select granular material
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor anc the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 471 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 The results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 26 of this
report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation
and hand probing
Based on our field representatives observations and the referenced test results it is
our opinion that the earthwork operations during the project construction observed and
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent addendums and recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality
of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects
regardless of when they are found
