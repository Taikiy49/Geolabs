INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface soil conditions at the site to develop pertinent
soil data and soil engineering recommendations for the design
and construction cost estimates of the proposed off site drain
line
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project will consist of a 60 inch diameter RCP
drain line extending about 224 feet and a new CRM outlet
structure The drain line will extend from the proposed
Paalaa Kai Subdivision project north across Waialua Beach
Road and empty into an existing drainage ditch which flows
into Paukauila Stream The new drain line will replace an
existing 24 inch diameter drain line and CRM outlet structure
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed drain line generally extends in a north south
direction from the proposed Paalaa Kai Subdivision project
to an existing unlined drainage ditch The Paalaa Kai
Housing project is located along Waialua Beach Road about
2000 feet west of Weed Junction
After crossing Waialua Beach Road the drain alignment runs
along residential property and a dirt road and terminates
at an existing CRM outlet structure which empties into an
existing unlined drainage ditch The drainage ditch empties
into Paukauila Stream about 220 feet to the north
There is an existing 24 inch diameter drain line extending
close to the new alignment

Bearing Capacity Settlement
Fairly competent subsoils were encountered along the align
ment
The net increase in load on the subsoils caused by the
drain line construction will be small therefore the
resulting settlements should also be small if the sewer
line is properly constructed and no additional fills are
placed
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has
a great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the
pipe therefore the pipe bedding is an important design
consideration
The trench should be excavated to a depth of 6 inches below
the pipe barrel The bottom of the trench should then be
compacted Six inches of compacted granular bedding ma
terial should be placed and shaped to provide full bearing
for the pipe The pipe should be placed and the granular
bedding extended beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of
one fourth the outside pipe diameter and halfway up the
pipe sides The remainder of the trench backfill should
be in accordance with the Standard Specifications for Public
Works Construction May 1975 The recommended bedding sec
tion is illustrated on Plate 2

W P
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the open trenches
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered a few feet above the proposed
invert level throughout the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the con
tractor provided adequate stability of the excavation
is maintained
The soils encountered in the borings below the water level
consisted of silty sand with about 10 to 30 per cent fines
The dewatering operations should be done without pumping
out the soil fines pumping clear water only
The dewatering should be coordinated with the excavation
Support system so that the stability of the excavation is
not jeopardized The operations should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening the
bottom of excavation
An alternative to dewatering would be to place the pipe
in a wet trench condition

Backfill
The on site silty sand encountered near the invert level
can be used as backfill around the pipe Except under the
roadway area the upper brown silty clay soil can be used
as backfill from 12 inches above the pipe to the ground
surface
In the roadway area select borrow or sandy soil material
should be used for the top 2 feet of the backfill
The backfill above the pipe should be compacted to a mini
mum of 90 per cent of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 test method Proper compaction of the trench
backfill will reduce future settlements of the backfill and
the adjacent ground surface
Outlet Structure
The proposed outlet structure will consist of a rectangular
concrete channel about 10 feet long and a CRM trapezoidal
transition structure to the existing unlined drainage channel
The invert of the outlet structure will be at about elevation
1 0 feet below the encountered ground water level The
dewatering operations should follow the guidelines presented
under the drain line construction recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
The structure foundations should rest on the silty sand
deposits encountered below the water level in the borings
After dewatering the bottom of the excavation should be
tamped prior to footing construction An allowable bearing
value of 2500 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on
the recompacted silty sand
The structure walls should be designed to resist lateral
earth pressures as follows
Above water level 45 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Below water level 20 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Weepholes should be provided through the channel walls to
reduce the buildup of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Field inspection and engineering observation during the drain
line construction are important to permit correlation between
the preliminary soil data obtained during this investigation
and the actual soil conditions encountered during construction
Backfilling operations should be inspected by a soils engin
eer and density tests should be taken to verify that adequate
compaction density has been obtained
