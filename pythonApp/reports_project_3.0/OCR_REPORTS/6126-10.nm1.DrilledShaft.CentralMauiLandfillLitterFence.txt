Location Map Plate 1
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration and provided a report
entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Central Maui Landfill Improvements
The purpose of our work was to provide engineering consultation
and assist in problem resolutions during the construction of the drilled shaft foundation In
addition we provided Special Inspection services Item No 11 by observing the drilled
shaft foundation installation at the Central Maui Landfill Litter Fence project site for general
conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The project generally involves the construction of a 1 200 foot long by 30 foot high
litter fence to collect the airborne litter debris generated by the landfill operations The new
litter fence consists of 23 fence posts which extends 11 2 to 13 5 feet into the subsurface
strata This embedment of the posts was installed as a drilled shaft foundation system

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of shallow surface
fills and residual soils overlying highly weathered to fresh basalt rock formation The
surface soil materials were previously placed by the general contractor Goodfellow
Brothers Inc and generally consisted of imported quarry 1½ inch minus select granular
fill The residual material generally consisted of medium stiff to hard clayey silt soils with
traces of sand and rock fragments The surface fills and residual soils extend to depths of
about 1 to 3 feet below the existing ground surface Underlying the residual soils is
extremely weathered rock formation also known as Saprolite The Saprolite generally
consisted of hard dense clayey silts and silty sand material with highly weathered basalt
rock fragments The Saprolite extended to 5 feet below the ground surface on the
southern end of the project site and to 16 3 feet at the northern end of the site The
Saprolite soil material progressively decreases in weathering with depth and the rock
formation progressively increases in competency until a very dense very hard fresh
basaltic rock formation was encountered In addition the basalt formation consists of a
series of a a type lava flows with inter bedded clinker layers which generally consists of
red brown gravel with sand and silt The depths to rock formation varied from 5 to 12 feet
from the existing ground surface at Shaft through DS 20 and no rock was
encountered in Shaft through DS 23
Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
In December 2009 the subcontractor started to drill the production shafts prior to
receiving the Notice to Proceed for the drilled shaft installation A 36 inch diameter auger
was used to drill to rock formation in Shaft through Excavation
used a truck mounted drill rig to accomplish the drilling operation Shaft
as no rock formation was encountered Later as drilling continued on the remaining shafts
auger refusal was met on the rock formation The contractor shut down operations and
notified the construction manager they had commenced with the installation operations
and then shut down due to drilling difficulties
In March 2010 the contractor returned with a new drill rig and drilling tools
consisting of air pneumatic percussion hammers to advance through the hard rock
formation The following provides a description of the production drilled shaft installation

Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed a total of 23 production drilled shafts for the
Litter Fence alignment The installation of the production drilled shafts was observed by a
Geolabs representative starting on March 17 2010 to completion on The
approximate locations and the drilled shaft number designations of the drilled shafts are
shown on Site Layout Plan Plate 2 The as built drilled shaft logs of the production drilled
shafts installed for the project are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 23 A Geolabs
representative observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of fence posts and
concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts with the
exception of the drilling of Shaft through DS 23
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Lo Drill by Bay
Shore Industries Inc drill rig equipped with 24 and 36 inch diameter pneumatic
percussion air hammers and two large 1600 series air compressors During the drilling
operation very hard rock formation was encountered which reduced the drilling
production rate down to 1 to 2 inches per hour Therefore the depths of the drilled shafts
were reduced to a minimum of 6 feet deep with a minimum of 4 feet of rock formation
embedment This design revision was provided by the design team of CH2M Hill The
contractor later chose to drill to a target depth of 13 8 feet such that they would not have to
make adjustments to the fence post connecting segments above grade The shafts were
later cleaned out to full depth with a vacuum truck just prior to the fence post installation
In addition due to the extended period of time between the drilling and concrete
placement of Shaft through DS 23 it was recommended to over ream the
sidewalls of these shafts to provide a fresh soil face for bonding with the concrete after
placement The over reaming was accomplished with a 38 inch auger and drilled for the
full depth of the shaft with a truck mounted drill rig and was observed by a Geolabs
representative
After excavation and cleanout of the production shafts were completed the fence
posts were lowered into the drilled holes with the use of a boom truck to the appropriate
depth of 13 5 feet below the existing ground surface After the fence posts were installed in
the drilled hole concrete was placed by the drop chute method and guided around the
perimeter of the post During concrete placement we observed that the shaft hole
remained clear of sidewall material cave in or debris Concrete with slump ranging from
about 5 5 to 9 25 inches was used for the drilled shafts The concrete arrived on the
project site with a slump of approximately 4 inches Then the slump was increased by the
addition of Gelinium a super plasticizer which increases the flowability of the concrete
without the addition of water The addition of the plasticizer into the concrete truck was
monitoring by the project s special inspector Richard Miyahira It should be noted that the
concrete placed in the drilled shafts was tested in accordance with the project
specifications as well as standard industry practices for the performance of slump
temperature and compressive strength tests
