SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located adjacent to Kamehameha Highway
at the foot of Kaonohi Street in Aiea of the island of Oahu Hawaii The
site is occupied by the existing BJ Center showroom and the area is
covered with asphaltic concrete pavement for parking usage
The general area is level with elevations ranging from about 14 feet
MSL Ewa side to 11 feet MSL Diamond Head side

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered medium stiff to very soft
clayey soils near the surface It is our opinion that the upper near
surface subsoils are not able to support the anticipated structural loads
without causing excess settlements
Based on the available information it appears the the showroom is
supported on pile foundations with an allowable capacity of 40 tons per
pile We have attempted to locate the old foundation data of the
showroom however the actual production pile lengths and pile driving
records of the foundation construction are not available now
Because the previous pile data is not available to evaluate their
maxima potential pile load carrying capacities and the anticipated heavy
column loads of the project we recommend separate deep foundation system
be provided to support the structural loads
Micro Pile Foundations
Since it is not cost effective or feasible to mobilize the
conventional pile driving rig to the new project area small diameter
micro pile foundations consisting of pipe piles are recommended to support
the escalator and new structures of the proposed renovations
The pipe piles should consist of minimm 6 inch diameter drilled
holes with 4 inch diameter steel pipes inserted first After inserting
the steel pipes the hole should be grouted both inside and outside the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2363 00 Page 5
steel pipes with cement sand water mixtures to increase the stiffness of
the micro piles Non shrinking type of cement grout should be used This
would also help reduce the potential corrosion of the pipes The new
foundation system may be connected to the existing pile caps through grade
beams
A design capacity of 10 kips per pile with a minimum embedded length
of 40 feet below the existing ground surface may be used A minimm pile
spacing of one foot and half on centers is recommended
Since the groundwater level is at about 6 foot depth tremie pour of
the cement grout or pumping grout from the bottom of the drilled holes is
recommended
The installation of the pipe piles should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if the steel pipes and drilled holes
are properly grouted
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction may be used for the renovations
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as
cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs provided that
an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from the existing objects indicated on the topographic survey map
dated by Controlpoint Surveying and Engineering The
elevations of the field borings were determined from the same map The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
