PROPOSED 650 LINEAL FOOT RETAINING WALL
KAILUA WWTP MODIFICATIONS PHASE III
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the proposed 650 lineal foot retaining wall at Kailua Wastewater Treatment
Plant WWTP Modifications Phase III in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 650 lineal foot CMU retaining wall with an average
width of 4 feet along the proposed new service road which is located to the south of
the biotower buildings within the Kailua WWTP in the district of Kailua on the island of
Oahu Hawaii We understand that the retaining wall will have a maximum height of six
feet 6 with slope backfill inclinations of approximately two horizontal to one vertical

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our reconnaissance is to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soils data set to evaluate and confirm the initial soil
design parameters pertinent to the proposed retaining wall
Our scope of work for this geotechnical engineering reconnaissance included
excavation and logging of four 4 test pits along the proposed retaining wail to depths
of about 3 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface laboratory testing of selected
samples obtained during the field exploration and analyses of the field and laboratory
data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering design parameters pertinent to the
design of the retaining wall Our scope of work does not include the analysis of the
stability of the slope behind the proposed retaining wall

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance the following
are our findings and recommendations for the design of the retaining wall
1 The stiff in situ subgrade soils below the proposed retaining wall footings
should provide an allowable bearing capacity of at least 1 500 pounds per
square foot p s f This bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only

pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation
system and by frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the
