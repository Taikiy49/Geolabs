GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development sites were a part of the old Salt Lake
bank which had been filled to create the present sites During
the lake filling and grading operations a substantial amount

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our field exploration and preliminary
engineering analysis the site could be developed
Due to the presence of compressible materials under the
variable surface fill shallow spread footing foundation sys
tem is generally not recommended for high rise type of con
struction
However if the high rise structures could be located
selectively occupying only the eastern portion of the site
along Ala Napunani Street then spread footing foundation
system could be used Boring and 7 drilled in
this general area encountered volcanic tuff at shallow depth
ranging from 4 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface
Selective building site planning could therefore eliminate
the use of pile foundation thereby providing potential
foundation cost saving
In Boring and 6 volcanic tuff formation was
encountered at depths ranging from 18 0 to 30 0 feet below
the existing ground surface If the proposed high rise
structures are located in the general area of these boring
locations pile foundation should be used to transmit the
anticipated heavy loads to the underlying tuff formation

Additional recommedations for the preliminary design of
foundations grading and construction are presented in the

Spread footing foundation could be used to transmit
column loads to the competent underlying volcanic
tuff formation in the general areas of Boring
and 7 where dense tuff formation was
encountered near the ground surface An allowable
bearing capacity up to 8 000 pounds per square foot
may be considered
The above estimation of allowable bearing capacity
for volcanic tuff formation is based on the materials
encountered in our drilled borings and our past
experience with similar material in the general area
of Salt Lake However cinder layers are known to
exist in this volcanic tuff formation and some
adjustment of the above allowable bearing capacity
may be required subsequent to our final soil
investigation
Additional field exploration consisting of borings
and samplings will be required after the tentative
building types locations and structural loads

In the general areas of Boring 4 and 6

towards the Golf Course The piles installed into
the underlying volcanic tuff formation could be
designed to carry loads up to 100 tons each live
and dead loads
