This report presents the results of our infiltration tests performed for the Mehana
Parcel 9 project in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Location Map and Site Plan Plate A1
This report summarizes the results of our infiltration testing and engineering
analyses for the storm water detention infiltration system The recommendations
presented herein are intended for the design of the detention infiltration system for the
subsurface disposal of storm water runoff The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
Project Considerations
The project involves the development of drainage improvements using a
subsurface storm water detention infiltration system for the Mehana Parcel 9 development
in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on preliminary information provided we
understand that underground storm water chambers are planned near the corner of the
future Kama aha Boulevard and the new regional drainage corridor as shown on the
Location Map and Site Plan Plate A1 The detention infiltration system will be designed to
impound surface runoff and allow storm water runoff to infiltrate into the ground The
proposed detention infiltration field encompasses approximately 10 000 square feet with
an invert of 42 feet
In order to provide information for the design of the detention filtration system we
conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration by excavating four test pits for
double ring infiltrometer testing In accordance with the Storm Water BMP Guide dated
June 2012 by the City and County of Honolulu the proposed detention infiltration field
requires a minimum of four permeability tests and two test pits for using an infiltration rate
factor of safety of 2 during BMP sizing calculations The test pits were initially extended to

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to

early Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows
from Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau
now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank
of the Waianae Volcano
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials Agricultural development within the last 100 years and
recent mass grading work brought the Kapolei area to its present form
Site Description
The project site is located on the southern outlying area of the City of Kapolei
Parcel 9 is situated on the southwest corner of the Mehana at Kapolei Development and is
bounded by the proposed future Kakala Street to the north Kama aha Boulevard to the
east and the new makai drainage channel Regional Drainage Corridor to the south
At the time of our field exploration the project site was largely occupied by
stockpiles from excavation of the new channel construction Four test pits were located
along the eastern and southern boundary of the proposed infiltration field with ground
elevation of approximately 55 feet

Infiltration Testing
The water infiltration rates into the subsurface soils were tested by performing
double ring infiltrometer tests at the bottom of each test pits in accordance with ASTM
D 3385 Standard Test Method The tests were performed by driving two open concentric
cylinders into the ground partially filling the rings with water and maintaining a constant
water level
The double ring infiltrometer test estimates the vertical movement of water through
the bottom of the test pit while the outer ring helps to reduce the lateral movement of
water in the soil The volume of water added to the inner ring is measured as the infiltrated
water volume The tests were carried out in several increments until achieving a steady
state with a relatively constant water infiltration rate Detailed testing data and results are
presented in Appendix B
