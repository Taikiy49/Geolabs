This report presents the results of our soil engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Booster Pump Building in Waiahole Valley on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our soil engineering exploration was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the proposed Booster Pump Building and the
driveway in order to provide recommendations for use in the design of
the site grading and foundations for the proposed structure
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATTONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A one story Booster Pump Building approximately 12 by 14 feet
in plan dimension The building will be constructed of cMu
walls with a concrete slab on grade Fills generally less
than 2 feet thick are planned below the building
2 A 12 foot wide A C paved driveway about 45 feet long that
will connect with the existing Waiahole Valley Road north
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southwest side of Waiahole
Valley Road north
The topography is fairly flat at the proposed building site The
proposed building location is approximately 3 feet higher than the
existing Waiahole Valley Road north
The site is currently heavily vegetated with planted ginger
plumeria and other small plants and trees

Subsurface Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the project site were explored by drilling
and sampling two 2 borings to depths of 16 5 and 21 5 feet
The site is generally underlain by relatively uniform medium to
very stiff brown to mottled brow clayey silt alluvium with some
weathered gravel
No groundwater was encountered in the borings during the time of
our investigation but the soils have high in situ moisture contents
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings disclosed that at the proposed finished grade the site
is underlain by a stiff brown to mottled brown clayey silt which could
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building
Building Foundation
For the proposed one story structure spread and continuous wall
footings bearing on the stiff alluvial soil or compacted fill are
recommended An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on these soils
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on stiff
undisturbed soil or the trench backfill should be well compacted to
minimize footing settlement

The bottom of all footing excavations and trench backfill should be
compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximm dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to concrete placement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundations
Based on the borings laboratory test data and estimated building
loads minimal settlements are anticipated
Iateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 3 can
be utilized to determine Sliding resistance of the structure and
foundation
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade is planned for the building
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer
of open graded gravel such as 3 Fine aggregate as a cushion layer
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of fills less than 2 feet
at the building location and minor cuts for the driveway

Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material
Fill Placement Compaction Requirements
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have in place moisture contents Therefore it
may not be practicable to dry the excavated soil near the optimum
moisture content Due to the high in situ moisture content and
since the proposed fills are low it is our opinion that the normal
90 compaction standard used in other drier areas should be
modified to 85 for this project
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for the driveway
Based on the results of our soil exploration program the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic
and that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement driveway cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should generally be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and

Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 2
borings to 16 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted
drilling equipment was utilized to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are show on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths
