This progress report presents the findings of our additional field
exploration program in the vicinity of the end of Woolsey Place for the
study of earth movements in the area Presented below are the summary of
our work progress our preliminary findings and preliminary
recommendations for the remedial work
Study Areas
This progress report covers two 2 separate areas involving four 4
private house lots which are located adjacent to the study area covered
in our previous Progress Report No 1 These four 4 private house lots
are 3014 and 3002 Woolsey Place and 3029 and 3015 Lanikaula Street
The location of the subject study areas are shown on the attached
Site Plan

Subsoil and Groundwater Conditions
A review of the old subdivision grading plans indicated that the
front portion of the house lot at 3014 Woolsey Place may be underlain by
fill materials
The drilled borings at the rear portion indicated that the typical
alluvial clay subsoils may not be present at the house lot Instead only
a surface fill layer over hard colluvium was encountered in the borings to
the drilled depth of about 20 to 25 3 feet
The fill materials varied from about 10 feet of basalt boulders in
Boring No 122 which was drilled at the eastern rear lot corner to about
16 feet of stiff clayey silt in Boring No 104 which was drilled at the
western rear lot corner The colluvium generally consisted of hard clayey
silt to cobbles and boulders in a clay matrix
Soft and wet zones were not encountered in the drilled borings
The grading plans also indicate that a major portion of the one story
house at 3002 Woolsey Place may be underlain by a surface fill layer as
thick as 10 feet

Mechanisms of Earth Movements
Based on the observed damage patterns the probable mechanisms of
earth movements for the two study areas appear different
3002 Woolsey Place and 3015 Lanikaula Street
It appears that the lost ground support at the northeastern corner of
the one story house at 3002 Woolsey Place was caused by surface
erosion of about 10 feet of soil below the original ground It also
appears that the three different levels of damaged retaining walls
downslope of the house were washed open partially or totally Debris
of the water transported soils and boulders were observed scattered
along the slope down to an apparent toe of slide in back of the house
at 3015 Lanikaula Street
Based on these damage patterns and the subsoil conditions we believe
that the basic mechanism of the upper earth movements may be the
erosion of the surface fill layer and subsoils by excessive amount of
surface runoff from the adjacent southern hillside during the New
Year s Eve storm As the water flowed downslope the subsoils and
the additional hydraulic force exerted excessive lateral pressures on
the downslope retaining walls which eventually failed
The apparent toe of slide further downslope below the retaining
walls appears to be related to the shallow translational landslide
which originated in the neighboring lot at 3004 Woolsey Place refer
to our Progress Report No 1 The excessive surface runoff might
have aggravated or accelerated the toe of this slide

3014 Woolsey Place and 3029 Lanikaula Street
Extensive ground movements including long wide cracks severe
settlements and retaining wall tilting were observed at the front
portion of the two story house at 3014 Woolsey Place and along the
street
It appears that the upper portion of the house had translated
downslope laterally and vertically as the result of a shallow
landslide
The apparent toe of slide observed at the rear of the upper lot 3014
Woolsey then pushed the rear retaining walls of the lower house at
3029 Lanikaula Street causing severe distress to the lower house
It is our understanding that this lower house structure has been
abandoned
The differential movements of the upper house is indicated by the
tilting of the basement columns at the rear portion of the house A
discussion with the foundation builder of the house indicates that
the columns may be bearing on footings placed directly on the hard
colluvium subsoils at about 5 to 10 feet depth which appear to be
stable
Based on our communication with the resident at 3014 Woolsey Place
it appears that the earth movement process had started gradually
about one to two years before until November 1987 then significantly
accelerated possibly due to the leakage of a water line in front of
the lot The movements were then further accelerated by the New
Year s Eve storm
Based on these observations and information we believe that the
earth movements may have been triggered by influx or increase in
groundwater flow or seepage possible due to utility leakage from
Woolsey Place and then accelerated by the winter storm

The presence of utility leakage may be indicated by the still
existing high groundwater table encountered in Boring 122
The utility leakage and excessive groundwater seepage may have
weakened the surface fill layer at the front portion of 3014 Woolsey
Place The downslope movements of the fill layer then pushed the
upper portion of the building laterally while the lower basement
columns which support the rear portion of the building on the deep
footings generally remained stable Eventually the supporting
columns were broken and then tilted as the earth movements propagated
downslope
Remedial Work
Due to the different mechanisms of earth movements the remedial work
for the two study areas as well as Woolsey Place should be considered
separately
The basic concepts of the remedial work should conform with the
previous concepts adopted in the Progress Report No 1
Woolsey Place
We recommend that the damaged area within the road right of way be
removed down to hard colluvium presumably at a depth of about 10
feet below the existing ground and replaced with a crib wall
retaining structure along with a trench drain on the upslope side of
the excavation refer to Progress Report No 1
The purpose of the crib wall is to attempt to stabilize the road
section from further sliding while the trench drain is to intercept
natural hillside seepage and or future utility leakage if any
A crib wall is recommended due to the anticipated ease of
construction and aesthetic value

3002 Woolsey Place and 3015 Lanikaula Street
Since surface erosion may be the basic mechanism of the upper earth
movements under the house at 3002 Woolsey Place we recommend that an
adequate storm drain system be provided at the end of Woolsey Place
to collect and divert the surface runoff away during future heavy
rain storms It is our understanding that this drain system upgrade
is in progress
We also recommend that this upper house be evaluated for structural
adequacy by a qualified consultant prior to the construction of any
long term remedial work
To stabilize the structure it is our opinion that foundation
underpinning such as a perimeter retaining wall may be used below the
undermined northeastern corner The retaining wall should extend
into the stable colluvium subsoils presumably located at the depths
of about 6 to 7 feet or less below the present ground surface after
erosion
The structural scheme to support the building corner on the retaining
wall and the method to backfill behind the wall under the house
should be carefully evaluated We suggest that the back of the
retaining wall may be partially backfilled with light weight
materials to reduce the load on the slope and the gap between the
backfill and the bottom of the house may be grouted subsequently
After the above recommended remedial work is performed it is our
opinion that the downslope retaining walls may be repaired or rebuilt
accordingly However due to the saturated condition the subsoils
may continue to creep downslope for a considerable period of time
before the moisture contents of the soils are reduce sufficiently
Therefore it may be desirable to delay the repair of the retaining
walls

Qualified professional structural and geotechnical engineers should

The lower private house lot at 3015 Lanikaula Street suffered
relatively minor damages which are generally concentrated in the
backyard near the apparent toe of slide We believe that the
present foundation safety of this lower house may not be jeopardized
provided the above recommended upslope remedial work is performed
3014 Woolsey Place and 3029 Lanikaula Street
Based on the observed damage patterns of both houses we recommend
that the acquisition of the upper private house lot at 3014 Woolsey
Place be considered for the following reasons
1 The structure appears to be in an advanced state of structural
distress It is our opinion that foundation underpinning will be
very difficult to construct because of the existing grade
separations and also very limited space at the back and under the
house It will also be extremely difficult to install the
foundation underpinning through subsoils such as the boulder
fill encountered
2 The stability of the upper house will very likely be jeopardized
when deep excavation is performed along the adjacent western
boundary of the lot to repair the downslope utility line The
removal of the upper house would provide better access to perform
the the repair and or realignment of the utility lines in the
upper house lot
3 The existing house at 3029 Ianikaula Street has been damaged by
the toe of slide from this upper lot Attempts to salvage the
lower house would first require the removal of the toe of slide
material However this would probably initiate additional

LIMITATIONS
The general site is underlain by adobe clay soils which are subject
to long term creep movements The recommendations presented in this
report should help to reduce drastic movements however creep movements
may continue
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and observation wells Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

The location of the field data points were approximately determined

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
