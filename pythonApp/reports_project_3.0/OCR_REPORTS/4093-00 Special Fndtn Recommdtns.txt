Pipe Pile Recommendations
The spacing of the pipe piles should be based on the allowable bearing
pressure house loading and the ability of the footing grade beam to span
unsupported between pipe piles The pipe piles should be spaced no closer than 2 feet
from center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity due to group action
The pipe piles should have a minimum outside diameter of 3 inches and a
minimum thickness of 0 16 inches We anticipate the maximum length to be about

50 feet The pipe piles should consist of galvanized steel Three 3 inch diameter
Schedule 40 pipe 3 inch outside diameter and 0 216 inch thickness may be used
The length of the pipe piles may be extended with welded or threaded connections
Rebar reinforcement should be utilized to connect the pipe piles and the footing The
pipe pile should be installed in a minimum 12 inch diameter hole The annulus between
the hole and the pipe pile should be filled with concrete
An allowable bearing load of 15 kips may be utilized for pipe pile foundations
embedded a minimum of 5 feet into the underlying undisturbed stiff to very stiff clayey
soils The pipe pile foundations should also have a minimum depth of 20 feet below the
ground surface This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Settlements of the pipe pile foundations will result primarily from the elastic
compression of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total
settlement of the pipe pile supported foundation to be less than about one half inch
with differential settlements between other pipe pile supported foundations to be less
than about one fourth 7 inch We believe that these settlements are primarily elastic
and should occur as the loads are applied
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by a combination of the
lateral load capacity of the pipe piles and the passive pressure against the vertical
faces of the grade beams Passive earth pressure against the vertical faces of the
grade beam may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pcf
The performance of the pipe pile foundations will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures As a result we
recommend that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii be present to observe the
drilling and installation of the pipe piles during construction In our opinion the
following may have a significant impact on the effectiveness and cost of the pipe pile
foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in some of the borings drilled
for the project we anticipate that drilling through boulders may be required The
contractor will need to have the appropriate equipment and or coring tools to drill
through the boulders Appropriate measures by the contractor will also be needed to
avoid dislodging boulders into the hole prior to placement of the pipe pile and concrete
The load carrying capacity of the pipe piles depends to a large extent on the
contact between the pipe piles and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling
the holes installing the pipe piles and placing concrete in the holes The bottom of
holes should be cleaned of soft and or disturbed material prior to installing the pipe pile

Slab On Grade Recommendations
Due to the potential for residual areal settlement from the fill placed we
anticipate that about inch possibly up to 1 inch of settlement may occur over the
thick fill areas on a long term basis To reduce the amount of distress caused by
differential settlement between the footings and slabs we recommend the following
1 Selected interior slabs should be designed as structural slabs that can
span unsupported between grade beams
2 Selected garage lanai and walkway slabs should be designed as floating
slabs We define floating slabs as slabs constructed in a separate
concrete pour with no structural connections to other members A
pre molded joint filler should be included as part of the design Exterior
slabs should be constructed at a lower elevation than interior slabs so
that movements are less detrimental and less noticeable
a Selected driveways should be doweled into the garage slabs The dowels
should be fixed on both sides Since differential areal settlement caused
by varying amounts of fill is expected the role of the dowels is to help
reduce the occurrence of separations or uneven slabs at the joints Note
however that minor cracking within the slab may occur if the differential
settlements are large
A subgrade modulus of 60 pci may be utilized for the design of the garage and
driveway concrete slabs A minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for
preliminary design purposes Provisions should be made for proper load transfer
across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of
slabs adjacent to unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade We recommend that rebar reinforcement be utilized for those
slabs on grade listed above
The subgrade soils below concrete slabs interior garage and driveways

to close all cracks or prepared again as recommended above Saturation and

Settlement Monitoring
It is our understanding that it is desired to construct homes on Lot 20
21 and 22 within the first phase of house construction To accomplish this we
recommend that surface settlement monuments be established soon after the large fill
area is brought to finish grade After the settlement rate has subsided construction of
the homes may commence We believe that the settlement will take approximately 2 to
3 months to subside The 4 to 9 months mentioned in the geotechnical engineering
exploration report represents the worst case scenario
It should be noted that the reason for the waiting period is to reduce settlement
of slabs and downdrag forces on the pipe pile foundations We anticipate settlements
to be on the order of 1 to 3 inches
We recommend that two surface monuments be established at each of the
aforementioned lots These surface monuments should be monitored on a weekly
basis by a qualified professional surveyor until the rate of settlement of the fill has
subsided The readings should be transmitted to Geolabs Hawaii for review

Special Foundation Recommendations
For Lot and 6
Royal Kunia Site 4
Dear Mr Arakawa
Based on the proposed house locations at Lot and 6 residing over or
adjacent to the perimeter retaining wall footings we recommend special foundations for
those footings within the influence zone The influence zone is defined as the area
above a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V plane projected upwards from the heel of
the perimeter retaining wall footings House footings within the influence zone may
impart a surcharge load onto the retaining wall Although the vertical loading can
sometimes act as a stabilizing force its horizontal loading may act as a destabilizing
force unless the house footing is located below a 1H 1V plane projected upwards from
the heel of the retaining wall footing This destabilizing force may cause a failure in the
retaining wall in a number of modes and may lead to movement of the house structure
The problem is further aggravated when house footings are located above a
1H 1V plane projected upwards from the bottom of the retaining wall stem In this case
deflections of the wall stem may lead to significant movement of the house structure
Pipe Pile Recommendations
Based on the relatively fixed locations for the house and retaining walls and
other constraints we recommend that pipe piles be provided for those house footings
within the influence zone of the retaining wall We also recommend that the retaining
wall footing within the house structure limits of Lot No 5 be modified to include rebar
reinforcement at the lower portion of the footing The lower rebar reinforcement should
increase the tension capacity of the lower portion of the footing and support the pipe
pile loads

Revised Special Foundation Recommendations
For Lot 20 21 and 22
Royal Kunia Site 4
Dear Mr Gaston
Based on the decision to further delay the construction of houses at Lot Nos 7
and 22 we believe that the pipe pile foundation solution with a structural slab
with grade beams previously recommended for these lots may be substituted with a
structural mat foundation Initially the construction of the houses at these lots was
planned for the first phase therefore there would not be sufficient time to allow the fills
to settle However it is our understanding that the construction of the houses at these
lots will be constructed about 9 months following the completion of the mass grading
work
During this 9 month period settlement monitoring will be conducted by others
with surveying equipment This data should be transmitted to our office for review The
data will likely reveal one of the following scenarios
e The settlement rate has subsided and construction of the houses as planned
may proceed
e The settlement rate has not subsided OR has subsided to an insufficient
degree Therefore the construction of the houses should be further delayed
while additional settlement monitoring is conducted OR the pipe pile
foundation solution should be implemented
The structural mat foundation may be designed based on the following design
parameters
