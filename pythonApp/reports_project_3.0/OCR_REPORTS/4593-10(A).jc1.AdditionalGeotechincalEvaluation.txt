PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site encompasses an area of hillside earth movement between
Mona Street upper limit and Hind Iuka Drive lower limit in the Aina Haina area of the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The landslide displaced several houses in the late 1960 s The
homes were demolished and stabilization measures such as slope flattening and
placing buttress fill near the toe of the slope along Hind Iuka Drive were installed in
1975 The land was left as a passive park
Geolabs was retained to perform field exploration and instrumentation monitoring
for design of a permanent landslide remediation scheme along Mona Street A drilled
shaft wall was designed as the remediation scheme extending to a depth of
approximately 50 feet with a subsurface drainage system intercepting subsurface
seepage

primarily stabilize the lateral movement of Hind Iuka Drive The mitigation will reduce
the lateral earth movement between Hind Iuka Drive and Wailupe Stream in Lot 1142
but not eliminate the movement

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration conducted previously the project site is generally
underlain by surface fill younger and older colluvium deposits over weathered basalt rock
formation at greater depth The surface fill consists of stiff clayey soil with cobbles
boulders and gravel The younger colluvium is primarily soft to medium stiff clay with
cobbles and boulders while the older colluvium varied from very stiff or hard silty clayey
or weakly cemented sandy soils with cobbles and boulders to dense or hard cemented
cobbles and boulders in a clay matrix
The thickness of the younger colluvium deposit varies from 9 to 27 feet with
dramatic changes in consistency No definite slip failure plane was recognized in the soil
recovered from the borings The borings encountered groundwater at depths from 12 to 53
feet below the ground surface It should be noted that groundwater was not present in
some of the borings The water levels recorded may be affected by the drilling water and or
seepage
Details of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in the referenced
report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Services Mona Street Landslide Remediation
Hawaii dated as well as results of the laboratory
tests performed on selected soil samples
A total of nine inclinometers converted from the borings drilled are read on a
bi month basis to monitor the ground movement under on going monitoring program
Locations of the inclinometers are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Details of the
monitoring results are presented in the referenced monitoring reports
Previous slope stability analysis indicated that the slide zone along Mona Street
may be at shallow depths of about 10 to 15 feet below the ground surface Based on the
significant variation of groundwater encountered in the borings and measured by the
instrumentation excessive pore water pressure from heavy rain was believed to exert
destabilizing forces on the sliding wedge

To validate the inclinometer monitoring results slope stability failure mechanism
was simulated using the computer program Phase 2 version 6 from RocScience Inc
The model for the slope stability analyses is established using the two dimensional 2D
finite element stress analysis method to compute the factor of safety of the critical slip
circle and estimate ground displacement in 2D plane strain state based on
Mohr Coulomb failure criterion
The soil profile using subsurface conditions encountered was used in the model
The slope stability analysis is initiated by computing the ground displacement at the
locations of existing inclinometers I 7 I 8 and I 9 Then the computation is repeated for
back analysis by estimating the Young s modulus of the soil materials until the computed
ground displacements at the locations of I 7 I 8 and I 9 achieve the same magnitude of the
actual measurements
The driving forces of the ground movement creep consist of the weight of soil mass
traffic surcharge estimated as 150 pounds per square foot and excessive groundwater
pressure The shear strength reduction method is used to repeatedly calculate the critical
slip circle factor of safety until the limit equilibrium state is achieved
After the computed lateral ground displacements reach the approximate magnitudes
of actual measurements from the existing inclinometers the drilled shaft wall on the edge
of Hind Iuka Drive in front of Lot 1142 is added into the slope stability analysis model The
lateral displacements are computed at each existing inclinometer location for slope
remedial evaluation as well as the critical slip circle factor of safety The results of the
slope stabilization analysis are summarized in the following table and further illustrated on

To provide sufficient lateral resistance we recommend installing the drilled
shafts to a depth of about 50 feet below the ground surface Similar to the evaluation
conducted previously we estimate that approximately 80 percent of the lateral pressure
on the soil mass between the gaps may be transferred onto the drilled shafts due to soil
arching
The drilled shaft lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE
Plus Version 6 0 which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally
loaded drilled shaft program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin
The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a drilled shaft under
lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis was carried out with the use of
non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then
computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths
Due to cap beam interaction the drilled shaft was modeled with rotational
restraint and free head lateral displacement boundary conditions The soil arching was
considered for about 80 percent of the lateral pressure transferred onto the drilled shaft
To be conservative the drilled shaft was also modeled to resist 100 percent of lateral
pressure to compare the results of the soil arching effect Additional traffic surcharge of
150 psf was included in the analysis Excessive groundwater pressure was not applied
on the shaft because the bottom of the trench drain at the depth of 15 feet is above
the slip surface depth at about 8 feet below the ground

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field measurements Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the measurement points may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway
lf variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field measurement points indicated in this report are
approximate having been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan
transmitted by M E Pacific Inc on Elevations of the measurement
points were only based on the water level at the time of the field measurement The
physical locations and elevations of the measurement points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the measurement
plots depict the approximate mudline depth and as such may denote a gradual
transition These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in
rainfall tides temperature and other factors
