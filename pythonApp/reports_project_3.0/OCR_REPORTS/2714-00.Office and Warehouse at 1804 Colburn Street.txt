on the island of Oahu The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a three 3 story office and warehouse structure on the
site Based on the plans provided to us the new structure will be of steel frame and
masonry construction with on grade slabs for the first story floor and parking area
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal to Pacific Mechanical
Company Inc dated The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity

to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 1804 Colburn Street in the Kalihi Kai area of Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is adjacent to the Island Recycling Inc to the
north and west and to the Island Slipper Factory Ltd to the east
At the time of our exploration the site was vacant and surrounded by chain link
fencing The existing terrain is generally level with reddish brown silty clay at the surface
A topographic map of the site was not available at the time of our report preparation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 16 2 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The exploration results indicate that the subsoils consist of about one 1 to three
3 feet of fill overlying coral formations with sand and gravel Stiff alluvium was
encountered at about 20 foot depth in Boring No 2
Based on our laboratory test results the existing near surface fill materials
comprise reddish brown to brown silty clay with moderate swelling characteristics when
saturated from a dry condition
Groundwater was encountered at about nineteen 19 feet below the ground
surface in Boring No 2 at the time of our field exploration It is anticipated that the
groundwater level may vary with tidal fluctuation rainfall and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained to assist in determination of their classification and engineering properties The
test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered moderately expansive surface fill
materials overlying coral formation with sand and gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2714 00 Page 5
Our previous experience with coral formations and in this general area indicates
that cavities and lenses of loose material are very common in these formations The
cavities and loose materials may be the cause of excessive future foundation settlements
if they are present at shallow depths below the bottom of the footings
Therefore we believe that footing locations should be probed during construction
to delineate potential cavities or loose lenses The probe holes and cavities or loose
lenses should be backfilled with grout under low pressure to ensure sufficient support to
the foundations above
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and pavements and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to firm natural ground and the resulting depression backfilled with
fill material properly compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended
above
If required imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve All imported fill material snould be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2714 00 Page 6
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations Design
Spread or continuous footings may be used for the proposed office and
warehouse The near surface fill materials encountered are relatively thin only one 1
to three 3 feet thick and it is therefore anticipated that both the fill and the coral
formations will be encountered at the bearing surfaces of the footings
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade is recommended An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
footing design The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered It is estimated
settlements will be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage lines they should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines The
existing fill materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should be scarified
to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 The above six 6 inches of scarification and recompaction may be waived
if competent coral formation is encountered at the bottom of footing excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2714 00 Page 7
Alternatively the footings may be embedded deeper to bear on the underlying
coral formation For footings bearing on the coral formation an allowable bearing value
of 6000 p s f may be used In the southern portion of the site it is estimated that
excavations up to three 3 feet below the existing ground may be required to provide
bearing for footings on the coral formation In the northern portion of the site the footing
excavations may be anticipated to be only approximately one 1 foot deep
The properly prepared footing bearing surfaces should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of reinforcement and concrete to determine if
adequate bearing has been obtained
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of the materials should be
neglected
Cavity Probing and Grouting
Our previous experience in coral formations and the area of this project site
indicated that the formations are prone to contain cavities and lenses of loose material
Cavities are formed in coral formations due to the presence during active growth of the
coral of fresh water streams which prevent the growth of the coral
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2714 00 Page 8
To reduce the potential of footing settlements due to possible occurrence of
cavities or lenses of loose material near the footing bottom elevations in the coral
formation probe holes are recommended below each column footing and on fifteen 15
foot centers for continuous wall footings The probe holes should be minimum of three
3 inches in diameter and extend below the bottom of the footings to a depth equal to
two 2 times the footing width or a minimum of ten 10 feet The probe holes should be
grouted with a sand water cement mixture under low pressure
If a cavity or loose material is encountered below a footing additional probe holes
should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and thickness of the cavity or loose
lens and properly grouted The probing and grouting operations should be observed by
the project geotechnical engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are
carried out properly and to evaluate other field solutions which may be required to
properly backfill voids and densify loose lenses near the footing bottom elevations
Slabs On Grade
For the design of slab on grade floors for the proposed employee s lunch and
locker room the use of four 4 inch thick concrete slabs placed over a four 4 inch thick
layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C33 No 67 cushion is recommended To protect
against moisture damage of floor covering materials a moisture barrier such as
polyethylene membrane should be placed below the slabs A one inch layer of sand
should also be placed over the membrane prior to placement of concrete to aid in Curing
of the concrete
Based on our laboratory tests the on site existing fill material appears to be
moderately expansive when saturated from a dry condition Therefore it is recommended
that the on site soils within twelve 12 inches of the bottom of the slab cushion be
replaced with non expansive imported select materials compacted to a minimum of 90
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2714 00 Page 9
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to compaction of the imported select materials
The above 12 inch select materials may be reduced accordingly if coral formations are
encountered at shallow depths within 12 inches below the proposed slab cushion
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades
Slabs subjected to vehicular traffic should be considered to be rigid pavements
rather than floor slabs The above slabs on grade recommendations are not applicable
to rigid concrete pavements

Utilities
New underground utility lines may be anticipated for the project It is anticipated
that most trenching will be in the relatively competent coral formation and relatively difficult
excavation may be encountered The to be excavated coral materials are generally
suitable for use as trench backfill The backfill should be properly compacted as
recommended in the section entitled Site Grading to reduce potential ground
subsidence It is considered to be the contractor s responsibility to locate and protect all
existing utility lines prior to and during construction
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for
bidding This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
concepts and recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading cavity probing and grouting and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from fieid borings Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from the project boundary as defined by the existing chain link fence The physical
locations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
Information regarding the surface elevations at the boring locations was not
available at the time of preparation of this report The stratification lines shown in graphic
representations of the field borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report This data has been reviewed
