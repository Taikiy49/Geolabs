Soil Engineering Reconnaissance
Proposed Lot Grading
Streets
Waimanalo
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions in order to provide preliminary data for use in
the design of the grading work for the proposed five lots
The scope of the reconnaissance included five hand auger probings
visual observation of the existing site conditions by one of our
geologists laboratory testing and engineering analysis
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Site Description
The proposed 5 lots cover an area of about 16 acres located south
easterly of the intersection of Mahailua and Waikupanaha Streets in
Waimanalo on the island of Oahu Hawaii
W O 1504 00 Page 2
Tne easterly portion of the lots are located adjacent to a grassy
and brushy drainageway that runs down towards the north Water was
running along the drainageway at the time of our exploration The
easterly lots are located along a ridgeline with sideslopes that vary
from 5 1 to about 2 1 H V The ground surface of the ridge also slopes
down in a northerly direction with gradients of about 7 to 10 percent
The westerly lots are generally located on fairly flat ground that
slopes down in a northerly direction with a gradient of about 5 percent
The southern portion of one of the lots will cross the toe of a ridgeline
below the Pohakunui Heiau site
The westerly lots are generally used for nursery plantings and have
some irrigation piping along the ground surface
A boulder stockpile is located near the northeast corner of the site
Project Considerations
The proposed grading plan is to excavate the higher ground into
fairly level pads and utilize the material for filling the lower areas
for shade house construction
Cuts of up to about 25 feet and fills of about 12 feet are
contemplated at the site
Roadways are planned around the lots Drainage ditches are proposed
along the roadways for the westerly lots Two new pipe culverts and
headwalls are proposed at the roadway crossings in the northern end of
the site

Subsurface Soil Conditions
To exailine the areas to be excavated and filled five hand auger
probings were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The probings drilled along the drainageway generally
encountered 1 5 to 2 7 feet of stiff silty clay at the surface underlain
by gravelly and cobbly material refusal
The probings in the higher ridge areas generally encountered 4 to 5
feet of stiff brown silty clay underlain by a stiff mottled reddish brown
silty clay with decomposed rock to about 10 foot depth the maximum
augered depth in Probing No 5
Water was not encountered in the probings during our exploration
For more detailed information regarding the soils encountered in the
probings refer to the Log of Probings Plates A 1 through A 5
Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration and reconnaissance along the drainageway
generally encountered stiff surface materials consisting of brown silty
clay overlying gravel and cobble stream deposits
Site Grading
Fills up to about 12 feet thick are anticipated near the top banks
of the drainageway
Prior to commencement of grading the site should be thoroughly
cleared of vegetations boulders rubbish and other deleterious materials
Any other localized soft or loose fill area encountered during
clearing and grubbing operations should be excavated and backfilled with
compacted select material
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1504 00 Page 4
Following the site preparation fill should be placed in 9 inch
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method At the time of
compaction the fill material should be at a proper moisture content to
achieve the above recommended compaction If the material is too wet or
too dry it should be dried or wetted as necessary
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have low to
moderate swelling potential and therefore may be utilized for the fill
For filling over existing slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5 1 keying and benching operation is critical and required to
properly bond the new fill to the existing slope refer to Plate 3 for
details The filling operation should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above fill
requirements
Slopes
Fill or cut slopes should not be steeper than 2 1 H V The
on site soil is primarily silty with some sand or clay and may be easily
erodable Therefore the slope should be protected from run off water to
reduce soil erosion Interceptor ditches or swales should be provided at
the top of the slope to direct water away from the slope
A six foot wide bench should be provided at the mid point of the
slope where slope heights exceed 20 feet
To assure adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back to the
design grade

New Culvert Installation
The new pipe culverts can be installed after the embankment is
filled up to at least 2 feet above the crown of the culvert structure A
six inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel is recommended below the pipe
culvert to provide uniform bedding support For the initial portion of
backfilling around and up to the mid height of the pipe culvert granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel or sand should be utilized The
remaining backfill above the 3B Fine gravel should consist of select
on site soils and be mechanically compacted in horizontal lifts
Headwall and Outlet Structure
Two headwall and outlet structures are required at the outlet end of
the culverts buried under the new roadway We understand that the
boulder stockpile located at the northeast corner of the site will be
used for construction of these structures The basalt rock appears
suitable for this use
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended for the
structures resting on granular backfill compacted to 95 of its maximum
dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
For backfill material directly behind the structures we recommend
the use of granular material such as 3B Fine gravel sand or
equivalent up to 2 feet below the top of the structure On site silty
soil should be used for the remaining portion of the backfill up to the
finished grade This would reduce the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls
We recommend that the headwalls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows

Additional loads due to traffic should be included where applicable
A passive soil resistance of 250 p c f and a friction factor of 0 4
between concrete and compacted fill can be used The passive resistance
should be reduced by one third when both friction and passive resistances
are considered
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to
minimize ground settlement along the utility alignments On site
material generally less than 6 inches in size may be utilized as
backfill All backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of the on site
material is fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction by
water jetting or ponding alone and therefore would require mechanical
compaction
Cesspools
If cesspools are noted on the site they should be identified on the
plans and checked to see that they are backfilled properly after the
sludge and other organic material have been removed
Service Road Pavements
For the light truck and automobile traffic anticipated for the
service access roadways the following pavement thickness may be used
based on the results of our laboratory CBR tests on the on site soils
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section can be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic and that
good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the embankment fills and to monitor
the contractor s grading operations It is also recommended that the
headwall and outlet excavations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material backfill and base course required should be
non expansive tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
hand augering The approximate locations of the probes are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

visual and textural examination in the field by our Geologist
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented on
the Log of Probings Plates A 1l through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the probing by collecting
auger cuttings or by driving a 2 inch barrel sampler with a
10 pound hammer The blow counts to drive the sampler are shown
on the probing logs at the appropriate sample depths
