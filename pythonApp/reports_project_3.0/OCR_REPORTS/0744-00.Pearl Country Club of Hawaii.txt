This report presents the results of a soil investigation at
the subject development site The soil investigation con

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the 5th fairway of the Pearl
Country Club golf course near Aiea Oahu It is bordered
on the northwest by uncleared land and on the southeast by
a nearby stream channel
Urban Engineering Consultants Inc P 2
The topography of the fairway within the limits of the site
consists of two types of relief which tends to divide the
fairway longitudinally into two halves The northwestern
half of the fairway slopes down towards the southeast at a
grade of about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical while the south
eastern half is relatively level up to the fairway boundary
Boring No 1 is located on the northwestern side of the
fairway on the top of the fairway slope overlooking the
5th hole green Boring No 2 is located on the southeastern
side of the fairway near the top edge of an almost vertical
cut overlooking the 7th hole green
Geology
The project site is located on a ridge or spur near the
Aiea area that separates Waimalu Valley from Kalauao
Valley The rock encountered at the site is from the
Koolau shield volcano and the soil there is principally
derived from this rock by the subsequent weathering of it
over millions of years after its formation
A discontinuous outcrop of rock is located on the upper areas
of the slope above Boring No 1 and displays itself more as
a boulderly collection of rock probably due to the weathering
and erosion upon its once more massive formation In the
lower area down slope and closer to the stream channel
the site becomes more typical of an area that has been under
deposition

Urban Engineering Consultants Inc P
A near vertical cut in this lower area exposes colluvium and
alluvium which was probably deposited by both gravity and
flowing water from the upper slopes
Soil Conditions
Boring No 1 indicated that there is a soil mantle about
6 feet thick consisting mainly of gray brown clayey ma
terials Below the clayey soil layer are various layers
of basalt lava rock that extend down to the full depth of
the boring
Boring No 2 located on the other side of the fairway did
not encounter any basalt rock The underlying materials
in this area consist mainly of various layers of silty
clay with rock fragments boulders and tannish brown
decomposed rocky material interbedded with mottled brown
clayey silt soils
For details see the individual boring logs
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed plan is to excavate into the western sloping
bank along Fairway No 5 area in order to widen this fair
way Anticipated grading will consist mainly of cutting
into the existing uncleared slope bank The maximum ver
tical slope height will be approximately 35 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
Urban Engineering Consultants Inc P 4
No fill is anticipated at this time
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the boring investigation site recon
naissance and laboratory testing it is our opinion that the
proposed site grading can be developed for the subject fair
way area
Stable cut slopes can be maintained where the underlying soils
are residual soil decomposed rock and basalt rock layers
Fill should be avoided on the existing slope steeper than
2 horizontal to 1 vertical unless retaining wall is utilized
to retain the required fill material
Earthwork and Site Grading
The present grading plan prepared by your company indicated
the proposed cut located on the northwestern side of the
fairway will be in stiff clayey soils and basalt rock for
mations Maximum vertical height of cut will be less than
35 feet The following cut slope ratios may be used
Clayey Soils 1 H V
Decomposed Rock 135 1 H V
Gray Basalt with Clay Seams 1 1 H V
If the overall slope ratio is flatter than 2 1 H V for the
entire cut bank drainage bench may be eliminated provided
that slope planting is provided on the upper clayey soil
layers
