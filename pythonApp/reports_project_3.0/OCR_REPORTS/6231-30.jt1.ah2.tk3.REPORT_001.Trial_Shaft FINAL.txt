Geolabs Inc also known as Geolabs observed the installation and testing of the
trial shaft for the Mauka Extension project at the Daniel K Inouye
International Airport on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes our
geotechnical observations and test results during the trial shaft installation performance of
Crosshole Sonic Logging CSL testing and concrete integrity coring
Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering services for the
Mauka Extension project in general accordance with the requirements outlined in the
project technical specifications Section 02372 Drilled Shafts The purpose of our
geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the trial
shaft installation and testing in general conformance with the plans specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new IIT Mauka Terminal is located at the northern portion of the existing Daniel
K Inouye International Airport on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is generally
bounded by the existing Daniel K Inouye International Airport structures to the west and
south North Nimitz Highway to the north and Aolele Street to the east The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The new terminal will be a two story L shaped building that will extend across part
of the existing commuter parking lot with a portion extending into the existing commuter
terminal building and apron The new building will be connected to the north end of the
existing interisland terminal building and extend about 535 feet north This portion of the
new building will be about 50 feet long and 150 feet wide The L portion of the building
will be about 440 feet long by about 180 feet wide and will extend from the parking lot pass
the existing commuter terminal building and into the aircraft apron area

TRIAL SHAFT INSTALLATION
The 36 inch diameter trial shaft was drilled to a depth of about 52 8 feet below the
pavement surface The purpose of the trial shaft was to demonstrate the adequacy of the
proposed construction methods and equipment planned for use on the production shafts
The trial shaft was installed between April 9 and 10 2018 The approximate trial shaft
location is shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan Plate 2
The drilling and concrete placement operations were conducted under the
near continuous observation of Geolabs Inc s field representative During the trial shaft
construction logs were maintained for the drilled shaft which included information
pertaining to shaft number size and length of the shaft soil conditions encountered during
excavation and concrete placement Record of the trial shaft installation is provided in
this letter report as the attached Trial Shaft Installation Logs Plates 3 1 through 3 4
The trial shaft for the project was drilled with a Watson 3110 drill rig equipped with a
36 inch diameter auger digging bucket and core barrel and a 34 inch diameter clean out
bucket The trial shaft was extended to approximately 52 8 feet below the existing
pavement surface corresponding to a tip elevation of about 50 3 feet below finished
ground floor elevation It should be noted that our field representative did not direct the
Contractor to extend the trial shaft length to more than 50 feet below the existing ground
surface Details of the trial shaft as built conditions are summarized in the following table

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING
The project specification Section 02372 Drilled Shafts requires performing CSL
testing on all drilled shafts with the exception of the load test shaft installed for the
project in accordance with ASTM D6760 for non destructive testing We performed the
CSL testing on for the trial shaft installed for the subject project
The CSL testing was conducted in general accordance with ASTM D6760 using
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest Crosshole Sonic Logging
tests were performed between all the possible access tube combinations in the trial
shaft 3 tubes resulting in 3 profiles The northern most access tube was designated as
Tube No 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction
The steel access tubes were extended approximately 2 6 feet above the top of the
trial shaft concrete for access considerations We understand that potable water was used
to fill the access tubes within one hour after concrete placement Based on our observation
in the field the water level within the access tubes was below the top of the shaft concrete
in access Tube and 3 at the time of testing The tubes were topped off with
potable water prior to testing
In general the CSL data is recorded in 20 mm intervals in accordance with
ASTM D6760 The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria CCRC Deviations from the
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating The
CCRC is summarized in the table below

CONCRETE INTEGRITY CORING
The project specification Section 02372 Drilled Shafts requires performing
concrete integrity coring on the trial shaft installed for the project The trial shaft concrete
integrity was evaluated by coring the upper 40 feet of the trial shaft at two locations
designated as B1 and B2 The purpose of the concrete coring integrity testing was to
evaluate the effectiveness of the concrete placement method planned for use on the
production shafts The concrete coring was performed by Hirata Associates Inc on April
18 and 19 2018 Eight unconfined compression tests were performed on selected
concrete cores and the results are summarized on Plate 7
Based on our observations of the core samples retrieved and the laboratory
unconfined compression test results it is our opinion that the trial shaft has been properly
concreted and the design concrete mix has achieved the minimum compressive strength
of 4 500 psi specified for the drilled shaft foundations
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the CSL test results the increased first arrival times FATs were
generally less than 10 corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less
than 10 and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for the trial shaft
It should be noted that changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects
depending on the degree of the reduction and the associated increase in FAT
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the trial shaft ranged from
approximately 13 900 to 14 800 feet per second Based on the results of the CSL test and
FAT increases no greater than 10 we believe that concrete integrity of the trial shaft
within the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of Good in accordance with
