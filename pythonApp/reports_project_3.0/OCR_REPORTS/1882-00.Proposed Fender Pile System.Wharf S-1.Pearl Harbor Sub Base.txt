INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed fender pile system
at Wharf S 1 Submarine Base Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to study the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide pertinent soil parameters for the
design of the proposed new fender pile system at Wharf S 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to install a new fender pile system to replace
portions of the existing one at Stations 2 75 and 4 15 of the subject
Wharf S 1 The proposed fender pile system will consist of vertical
18 inch square prestressed concrete piles
Tt is our understanding that the tentative scheme for each portion
of the proposed fender pile system will consist of five vertical piles
at about 5 feet center to center spacing with a vertical cylindrical
rubber energy absorbing system designed to dissipate the berthing and
The design docking force applied to two 2 concrete fender piles
is 33 kips with point of application at 7 feet below the water level
It is our understanding that the concrete fender piles would be
pile casting to increase the bending flexibility of the piles As a
result the fender piles could deflect more and thus could absorb more
of the berthing and mooring impact energy

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located to the south of Wharf S 1 and at the
eastern inland end of Quarry Ioch in the subbase area of Pearl
Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The existing wharf is a concrete deck structure supported on long
concrete or composite piles with the top of the wharf deck at elevation
of about 108 feet U S Navy Datum MIIW Elevation 100 feet
Timber fender piles were observed along the ocean side of the wharf
During our field exploration measurements indicated that the
existing mudline at the proposed fender pile locations along the wharf
Was generally sloping towards the sea with a slope ratio of about
H V The depths to mudline measured in Borings B 1 and B 2 were 36
and 37 feet below the top of the wharf deck respectively
SUBSURFACE CONDI TTONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in an estuarine and
deltaic environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace
with the subsidence

Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series mantled the coral reefs and alluvium with deposits of volcanic
ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
layers Subsequent erosion has dissected and thinned the mantles of
tuff at the site
During the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current
stand the area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits These deposits were subsequently covered by man made fills
placed during the middle portion of this century for land reclamation
projects during the construction of the existing Naval Submarine Base
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling and sampling 2 borings close to the proposed fender pile
locations along the wharf to depths from about 81 to 86 feet below the
existing top of the wharf deck level The approximate locations of

The drilled borings generally encountered loose and soft lagoonal

gravel were encountered beneath the lagoonal deposits to the depths
drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and engineering analysis it
appears that a critical consideration in the proposed fender pile
design and construction is the embedment depth of the piles below the
existing mudline
We believe that the proposed 18 inch square prestressed concrete
fender piles should be embedded to a minimm depth of about 60 feet
below the existing top of deck level of the wharves equivalent to a
depth of about 24 feet below the existing mudline
Vertical Pile Capacity
The fender piles would develop their vertical load capacity both
from end bearing and frictional resistance adhesion between the piles
and soils
The vertical loading on the pile is minimal since the ship forces
are primarily horizontal Therefore the length of pile required will
be governed by lateral pile load considerations as described below
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that the pile is driven to the required depth and that sufficient
resistance have been developed
Lateral Pile Capacity
The loading on the fender piles will be primarily lateral loads
due to the berthing and mooring forces from the ship The ship loads
will be transmitted to the fender piles through a cylindrical rubber
energy absorber

Since the fender piles would be subjected to repeated loads a
critical consideration would be to limit the permanent lateral
deflections of the piles This may be achieved by sufficient embedment
of the fender piles However the energy absorbing capacity of the
pile is determined by the amount of temporary deflection experienced
therefore excessive pile embedment must be avoided
Lateral resistance of the fender piles can be analyzed by using
the appropriate soil modulus values of the subsoils at various depths
below the existing mudline at the proposed fender pile locations A
lateral pile analysis using the program BMCOIG was carried out and
supplemented by the use of non linear P Y curves generated using the
program PILEDG to represent the soil modulus values
Since the lateral soil modulus varies with the deflection
experienced the computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value
then calculates the pile deflection based on the assumed modulus The
calculated deflection is then used to determine the modulus from the
non linear P Y curves The calculated modulus and the assumed modulus
are then compared The procedure is repeated until the new assumed and
calculated moduli are essentially the same
Based on the analysis the point of fixity zero movement of the
pile under the design load was calculated to be at about 55 to 57 feet
below the existing top of wharf deck about 20 feet below the existing
mudline Therefore we believe that the fender piles should be driven
to a total minimum depth of about 60 feet below the existing top of the
wharf deck in order to develop sufficient resistance to the design
lateral load Splices should be avoided
The fender piles should be driven to the design depth with a light
hammer delivering about 18 000 foot pounds of energy Due to the soft
soil condition care must be exercised during the pile installation to
avoid overdriving the pile below the design top elevation

Test Piles
Ideally test piles should be driven along the proposed locations
at Wharf S 1 prior to production pile driving to confirm the soil
conditions along the proposed fender pile locations and to evaluate the
pile driving characteristics in the field However due to the limited
number of piles involved test piles may not be practical
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed fender pile system
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all fender pile installation be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate penetration
and preparation have been made and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our exploration
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
