INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Reconstructed Sewer at University Avenue on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical investigation is to explore the
subsurface soil and water conditions at the proposed sewer line
alignment in order to provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the
design of the proposed sewer line construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 2
The scope of work performed in this investigation is in general
accordance with our proposal dated It included
drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths varying from 19 5 to 26
feet laboratory testing engineering evaluation of the field and
laboratory findings and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and log of borings are presented in the appendices The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The existing sewer line generally extends along the center of
University Avenue and Date Street and is 18 inches in diameter The
sewer line invert is located about 12 to 18 feet below the existing
ground surface
The proposed new sewer structure will be 21 to 24 inches in
diameter and about 2300 feet long The starting point will be from the
intersection of Coyne Street and University Avenue in Moiliili The new
line will traverse about 1900 feet along University Avenue to Date
Street then about 400 feet along Date Street to its ending point at
Kapiolani Boulevard
According to the preliminary design plan developed thusfar the new
gravity sewer line will probably be located near and parallel to the
existing line and along the median strip of University Avenue for most
of the route except along Date Street where it will be on the mauka side
of the street and about 15 to 20 feet from the existing line

It is our understanding that the invert elevations of the new line
will be similar to those of the existing 18 inch gravity line which
range from Elevation 4 3 feet at the intersection of Coyne Street and
University Avenue to about 12 7 feet at the intersection of Date Street
and Kapiolani Boulevard
GENERAL SITE CONDITION
The project site is located in the Moiliili area of Honolulu It
is bordered by Coyne Street on the north Date Street on the south and
Kapiolani Boulevard on the east
In general the new sewer line will be located along relatively
flat A C paved roadways and a median strip where the ground elevation
varies from about 5 to 9 feet The new line will be placed below
numerous buried lines and structures and parallel to the existing line
that generally follows the centerline of University Avenue and Date
Street
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is geologically situated in an area that borders
between a Quaternary limestone and recent alluvium Within the
surrounding Moiliili area the subsurface formations are typically
variable and discontinuous as experienced from previous project sites in
the nearby vicinity Some areas are underlain by emerged coral reef
formations relatively near the surface while other areas are underlain
by fairly thick lagoonal deposits of soft and compressible clays and
silts These variable formations are reflective of the different
depositional conditions
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 1513 00 Page 4
During the late Pleistocene Age the sea level rose and fell
several times as a result of glacioeustatic fluctuations resulting in
local swamp conditions which was later superimposed by more recent
deposits of terrestrial detritus of sand gravel silt and clay eroded
from the older rock of the area
In more Recent time the efforts of man have shaped the lowlying
land to its present form by placement of fill in low areas
Locally the northern end of the project site appears to be
underlain by soft compressible clays and silts peaty soils that
originated from a former swamp The majority of the site appears to be
situated in an area of a former coral reef formation The reef
formation may contain numerous cavernous openings in part caused by
solution of the coral by fresh water since emergence and in part from
natural openings in the formation that were never filled
These cavernous features sometimes make a reef formation very
permeable The hardness soundness and density of coral may vary
greatly in a particular reef structure

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Three general soil conditions were encountered along the proposed
sewer alignment The northern or mauka portion above King Street
encountered soft compressible lagoonal deposits from near the ground
surface to below the pipe invert Sheeting and bracing as well as
special pipe bedding will be required for this section of the alignment
The central portion along University Avenue generally encountered
competent coral formation to the invert depths Cavities were
encountered in Boring 4 and may be present throughout the coral
formation Less extensive trench support may be used in this portion
depending on the actual hardness of the coral formation
The southern or makai portion along Date Street encountered a
thin upper layer of soft lagoonal deposits over coral sand Sheeting
and bracing will be required for this section of the alignment
As the invert of the sewer line is below the water table
dewatering will be required during the excavation and trenching
operation Alternatively the pipe may be placed in the wet if the
quantity of water through the coral formation is excessive
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
friction resistance between the backfill material and the trench wall
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 7
To calculate the vertical loads on the pipe an average unit weight for
backfill material of 125 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25
are recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the
width of the trench and therefore the width Bd of the trench should
be kept to minimum The attached Plate 4 indicates three alternative
types of trenches
Additional traffic load on the sewer pipe located along Date Street
and northern portion of University Avenue where the alignment is within
the roadway area should be considered
Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe Pipe
bedding the contact between the foundation and the pipe is therefore
an important design consideration
Since the groundwater level is above the proposed sewer invert and
due to the very soft condition in the northern section above Beretania
Street it is recommended that a 2 foot thick crushed rock cradle be
utilized to stabilize the bottom of the trench along this portion of the
sewer line The rock cradle material should be brought up to the bottom
of the bedding or any concrete jacket under the pipe
We recommend Class B First Class Bedding for the proposed sewer
The sewer pipe should be bedded in a minimum of 8 inches of compacted
granular material placed on a flat trench bottom The granular bedding
should extend beyond the sides of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth
the outside pipe diameter and should extend half way up the side of the
pipe The recommended bedding detail is illustrated on the enclosed
Plate 5

Settlements
In the central and southern portions of the alignment where dense
sand and coral soils were encountered the net increase in load on the
subsoils caused by the sewer construction will be small and therefore
the resulting settlements should also be small if the sewer line is
properly constructed
In the northern soft soil area some amount of surface settlements
may occur due to disturbance of the subsoils dewatering operations and
increase in unit weight of the trench backfill The amount of
settlement should be small if proper construction methods are used
Cavity Probing
The project site is situated within an area where cavities in the
coral formation have been encountered in many previous projects
Although it appears that the existing sewer line has not been
adversely affected by collapse of any underlying cavity the potential
for damage to the line due to cavity collapse still remains
In order to determine if large cavities exist under the alignment
probe holes may be drilled at intervals of about 15 to 20 feet and to
depths of about 5 feet below the invert level of the sewer line If
large cavities are encountered they may be grouted through the probe
holes or broken open and backfilled depending on the size and depth of
the cavity
If cavities are encountered below the pipe there are several
methods that could be used to stabilize the cavity or to provide
structural support for the sewer pipe depending on the size and depth
of the cavity Some of the alternatives are as follows
a Smaller cavities may be grouted through the probe holes
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 9
b Cavities near the invert level may be broke open and backfilled
with granular material
c For large cavities at some depth below the pipe invert
cast in place piles may be used to support the pipe The
actual size and type of reinforcement required will depend on
structural considerations

The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice
of the contractor within practical limits Depths of excavations
in this operation will vary from approximately 13 feet to in excess

Hard ripping or special excavating methods for hard materials are
not anticipated except at the central area where a dense coral
formation is located The remainder of the alignment should be
through relatively easy to excavate medium to soft sandy and
lagoonal soils
Due to load considerations discussed in the preceding sections the
width of the sewer trench excavation should be kept to a minimum
for proper sewer installation and backfill
The excavated material should not be stockpiled within 15 feet of
the trench or next to the northern and eastern ends of the site
that is underlain by compressible layers

Trench Wall Supports
om eet eke nO ce com on Sa
The trench excavation should be provided with proper bracing in
accordance with the anticipated loading conditions traffic and
construction materials stockpile and equipment The adequacy and
safety of the bracing should be the responsibility of the
contractor
Sheeting and bracing is required for the northern portion to
prevent a cave in of the trench walls Due to the low strengths
and high water contents of the soils and the proximity to existing
structures special care must be exercised to prevent a failure of
the trench walls or upheaval of the trench bottom due to the
excavation and dewatering operations
Sheet pile or other support system may be required to obtain trench
stability
Even with good construction procedure movement of the excavation
bracing system occurs because of changes in stress during
excavation Movements depend greatly on workmanship for example
how quickly and tightly the supports are installed It also
depends on the type of soil the depth and width of excavation and
the type of support In view of the complexity it is difficult to
predict movement of the bracing system and the adjacent ground
Therefore it is important to realize that the support should be
installed as early as practicable and that measurements of vertical
and horizontal movements are useful especially in area where they
may cause damage

Dewatering
ne Sm om se
Dewatering of the trench excavation is necessary because
groundwater will be encountered above the proposed invert level
throughout the site
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained
Generally low volumes of water are anticipated from the soft
lagoonal soils in the northern portion of the alignment
Significant flows may be experienced in the coral formation through
the central portion particularly if cavities are encountered
Moderate flows are anticipated in the sandy soils along the
southern portion
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with the excavation shoring support so that the
stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or
softening the bottom of the excavation
Portions of the sewer excavation through the coral formation may
encounter a very large quantity of water flow As an alternative
to complete dewatering of the excavation to a dry condition the
sewer pipe may be installed in a wet condition However this
would require careful inspection of the trench bedding thickness
and accurate control of the invert elevation of the sewer pipe

In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction May 1975
Granular material such as sand 3B Fine gravel or crusher waste
should be used for the backfill up to about one foot above the pipe
to reduce the compactive effort required and possible damage to the
pipe
Except for the organic clays silt and boulders most of the
excavated on site soils less than 6 inches in diameter can be
re used as the upper portion of the trench backfill from above one
foot over the pipe to within two feet of the finished grade
Backfill from 1 foot above the pipe to within 2 feet of finished
roadway grade or to finished grade in non paved areas should be
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The backfill within 2 feet
of the finished roadway grade should consist of select borrow and
be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during
backfilling Should backfill under the water level be necessary
granular material such as base course rock should be utilized to
minimize excessive ground settlement of the trench backfill in the
future

Manhole
For manhole structures bearing on coral formation or dense sand an
allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used Minimal settlements
are anticipated For manhole structures on the soft lagoonal deposit a
2 foot layer of gravel 3B Fine should be provided below the structure
to provide uniform support An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f
may be used on the gravel layer
The lateral earth pressure acting on the underground structures
will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of the backfill
material and the compactive effort required on the material behind the
structure walls Granular material such as the select on site sand is
recommended for the backfill adjacent to the structures to reduce the
compactive effort required and to minimize the build up of additional
lateral earth pressures
For the design of the box culvert and manhole structures the
following lateral earth pressures are recommended
Backfill Above Water 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Backfill Below Water 25 p c equivalent fluid pressure
plus water pressure
Surcharge loading due to existing structures construction
equipment etc must be added to the above values
Joints and Connections
Due to the various soil conditions that will be crossed by the
pipe flexible pipe joints are recommended to allow for some amount of
differential settlements These flexible connections are particularly
important in the soft ground area where the new pipe line will be
connected to the existing 18 inch sewer line
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1513 00 Page 14
Roadway Pavement
The borings indicate that the existing pavement section consist of
2 to 4 inches of asphalt concrete over 4 to 10 inches of base course or
granular fill Generally the on site silty sand and crushed coral can
provide good pavement subgrade support For backfilling in the existing
roadway the new pavement sections should match those of the existing
ones provided that the backfill material for subgrade soil within 2 feet
of the finished grade consists of compacted select borrow or suitable
granular material approved by the soils engineer and be compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the trench excavation and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Quantity of seepage into the excavated trench should be closely
monitored in order to determine the pumping capacity required The
groundwater level adjacent to the construction should also be monitored
in order to see if a drawdown effect has been created
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report the engineer should immediately be
informed and appropriate modifications to the design made
