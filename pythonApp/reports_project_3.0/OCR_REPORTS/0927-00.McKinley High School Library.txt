INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our subsurface inves
tigation performed at the site of the proposed McKinley High
School Library Building to be located adjacent to the Multi
Purpose Classroom Building at McKinley High School The
location of the proposed building site with respect to the
surrounding area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed library building will consist of a single
story reinforced concrete structure with possible future
expansion to a two story structure The plan dimensions
of the structure will be about 70 x 170 feet Finished
floor elevations are not known at this time
The exterior column loads are anticipated to be 300 kips
dead and live loads About 2 feet of fill is planned for
the site grading
No basement is planned for the proposed structure

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is generally underlain by a calcareous sand
and emerged coral reef formation several feet below the upper
surface material consisting of clayey silt coral sand silt
and some seashell fragments This type of calcareous for
mation is common throughout the relatively lower level areas
of Honolulu and constitutes a major part of the cap rock for
the island s groundwater system
These calcareous formations were developed during a higher
stand of the sea which drowned the lower coastal land areas
and created upon them these shallow water coral reefs and
clay sand silt deposits The subsequent lowering of sea
level then exposed these shallow water reefs and the coastal
land areas became comprised of emerged coral reef material
Swampy surface conditions can be associated with these
emerged coastal lands when there is drainage from higher
elevations running into and collecting upon them because
of its flatness and closeness to base level The swampy
surface material would generally consist of organic silts
and clays and sand deposits overlying the calcareous
emerged reef and sand formation

Surface Conditions
The site of the proposed library building is located adja
cent to the multi purpose classroom structure at McKinley
High School Presently the entire site is covered with
asphalt concrete pavement and some lawn areas Underground
utilities are believed to exist throughout the site
The site is relatively flat with surface elevations ranging
from about 6 5 to 8 5 feet
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were investigated by
drilling five 5 exploratory borings at the locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1 The depths of borings ranged
from 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring
logs and the laboratory test results
Based on the information revealed from the drilled borings
the site is underlain by a variable depth of soft to medium
stiff layer which is composed predominantly of brown clayey
silt The depth of this upper layer ranges from approxi
to 6 0 feet below the existing grade

The upper variable layer is underlain by a soft gray silty
clay and loose coral sand layer which extended down to a
depth ranging from 8 to as much as 10 5 feet below the exis
ting grade
Generally very dense coral formation underlies the above
formations throughout the site The depth of the coral
formation ranges from 8 to 10 5 feet below the existing
grade The coral formation is generally less cemented at the
top and becomes more cemented with occasional sand pockets at
deeper depths
Although the individual borings indicated the depths thick
nesses and trends of the coral it must be kept in mind that
coral reef formation are usually very variable and conditions
may change abruptly over short distances
High groundwater level was encountered in the borings during
our field exploration The measured groundwater levels are
presented on the log of borings Minor seasonal groundwater
fluctuations should be anticipated
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration disclosed generally soft deposits over
a dense coral formation at the site The thickness and

characteristics of the upper soft deposits vary and are not
considered suitable for the proposed building foundation sup
port
The underlying dense coral formation which was encountered
generally at about 8 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface would provide the required foundation support to the
proposed structure However building loads could be trans
mitted to the underlying coral formation using various types
of foundation systems
1 A foundation system consisting of cassions or cast in
place piles installed into the coral formation could provide
the desired foundation support to the proposed structure
However the installation of cassions or cast in place piles
may be difficult and uneconomical due to the high water level
and the limited number of capable local cassion subcontractors
Moreover it would be very difficult to ascertain that all the
loose material had been removed from the bottom of the pre
drilled cassion or cast in place holes underwater The
presence of loose material below the tip of the cassion or
cast in place pile hole could cause excessive and detrimental
settlement of the foundation

2 Spread footings bearing on the underlying competent
coral formation could be used to provide foundation support
for the proposed structure However footing excavations
up to 10 feet down will be required in relatively soft
deposits The lower 4 to 6 feet of the footing excavations
will be below the groundwater level Large amounts of
dewatering should be anticipated requiring bracing and
shoring of the deep excavations
The eastern portion of the proposed building will be
located only a few feet away from the adjacent multi purpose
classroom building The building is supported on shallow
pile foundation and bears on the underlying coral formation
The dewatering in the footing excavations next to the exis
ting structure may cause damage to the slab and sidewalks
of the structure due to the possibility of the sandy soil
and fines being removed during dewatering Special and
expensive construction methods would be required to over
come the above problems caused by the dewatering in soft
clay and loose sand deposits
3 It is our opinion that the above problems associated with
dewatering shoring and bracing of the deep foundation exca
vation could be avoided if an alternate of short pile foun
dation system is used We realize that the amount of noise
generated from the pile hammers may not be very desirable
if classes are being conducted near the construction acti
vities

However the noise problem associated with pile driving
could be minimized if the pile locations are predrilled
first and driving of piles into the predrilled holes could
take place when classes are dismissed Therefore proper

The pile installation activities could also be performed

Spread Footing Foundation
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations
on dense coral formation could be used
An allowable bearing pressure of 6 000 p s f can be
used for the footings bearing directly on the dense
coral The allowable bearing value may be increased up
to one third for temporary loading conditions such as
wind loads Footing settlements are anticipated to be
relatively minor if the footing bottoms are free of
loose mud or cave in soils fallen from the excavation

Excavations
Excavations up to about 10 feet will be required for the
footings to be placed into the underlying dense coral
The material above the coral should be easy to excavate
Unprotected deep excavations up to about 10 feet into
soft underlying soils may collapse Therefore adequate
and properly designed sheeting and bracing of the exca
vations would be required Sheeting and bracing should
also be used for the excavations close to the existing
multi purpose building to avoid possible damage to the
existing structure The excavation sheeting should
extend down into the dense coral formation for addi
tional lateral support
Heavy equipments and material stockpiles should be kept
away from the excavations
Settlement of the adjacent ground due to deep excavation
operations may occur unless the excavations are properly
protected They can be minimized by preventing loss of
ground and by introducing points of support for the side
walls as soon as practical and at appropriate vertical
spacings

Dewatering
High groundwater was encountered in the borings at about
4 0 feet below the existing ground surface Therefore
large amount of dewatering should be anticipated for
excavations as deep as 10 feet Although the method of
dewatering should be left open to the contractor adequate
stability of the excavation should be maintained The
dewatering operations should also be done without pumping
out the soil fines pumping clear water only and without
creating a quick condition or softening the bottom of
the excavation
Careful dewatering is particularly essential near the
existing buildings to reduce settlements of the slabs
pavements and other structures supported on grade
Pile Foundation
Short end bearing pile foundation system consisting of
12 x 12 inch square prestressed concrete piles could be
used to transfer the column and wall loads to the under
lying coral formation Based on the present subsurface
boring data the pile lengths are estimated to vary from
about 10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
Piles installed into the competent coral formation could
be designed to carry loads up to 50 tons each dead plus
live loads

The following guidelines should be used for the design
and construction of the pile foundation
1 Based on the present subsurface information we
anticipate that the pile lengths would vary from
about 10 to 15 feet for most of the piles However
cavities and sand pockets are know to exist within
the coral formation and some piles may penetrate
deeper before reaching the required resistance
2 The pile installation close to the multi purpose
building would require predrilling to avoid
possible damage to the existing structure due to
vibration Predrilling of remaining pile locations
could reduce pile hammer noise significantly during
school hours Predrilling may also be required to
socket the short pile 1 to 2 feet into the dense
coral formation This need for predrilling and
socketing will be determined during the test pile
driving Piles could be installed into the pre
drilled locations during off school hours
3 We recommend that a minimum of about 10 test piles be
installed to estimate the final production pile casting
lengths for this project The lengths of these test
piles should be about 15 feet long

Pile should be driven with a hammer delivering about
30 000 foot pounds of energy The same type of
hammer should be used for the production pile
driving as used for the test pile installation
After the test piles are installed one should be
load tested to two times the design loads of 50
tons in order to further verify the competency
of the bearing stratum
The 50 ton test piles should be driven to a final
driving resistance of about 9 blows per inch for
the last two inches and about 50 blows for the
last foot The final pile driving criteria may
be modified after the completion of the test
pile installation and the load test program
The piles should be spaced no closer than 3 times
the pile widths
Most of the foundation piles for this project will
be relatively short and some of them adjacent to
the existing structure will be installed through
pre drilled holes The lateral pile capacity for
these short piles would be small about 1 kip each

Floor Siab
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of granular rock gravel material such as 3B Fine rock to
act as a capillary break
The slab on grade should be separated from walls and foun
dations
Some settlements are anticipated due to the weight of the
new fill over the site These settlements should occur
relatively rapidly However to minimize future differ
ential slab settlement the fill should be placed as soon
as practicable and the slab construction delayed as long
as practicable

Earthwork
Fill up to two feet are planned during site grading The
site should be cleared of all debris old foundations
and asphalt concrete pavements prior to any filling
Select on site material should be stockpiled and reused
as fill and footing backfill material The fill and
backfill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of
8 inches maximum and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 All fill material should be approved by a soils
engineer prior to its use
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation

drilling five 5 borings ranging in depth from 16 5 to 21 5

rock were obtained by using a 4 inch core barrel The blow counts
