EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 45 at this site is generally flat and covers about 0 2 acre
while most of Lot 46 is on steeply sloping ground that rises 70 to 80
feet above the street level The northerly and easterly sides of Lot 46
has been cut and benched for roadway construction This lot covers
about 1 5 acres
Geology
The project site is located on the northern flank of the Salt Lake
vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with
the groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions
which deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form
tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Salt Lake tuff is a younger air laid ash deposit that was laid
down when the sea level was receding below its present level The Salt
Lake eruptive event deposited its material over earlier tuffs probably
from the older Aliamanu crater eruption nearby The Salt Lake tuff is a
fine grained compact rock which breaks into chunky blocks
Soil Conditions
The information on the soils likely to be encountered in the
project area was obtained from the Soil Survey of the Island of Kauai
and Lanai State of Hawaii by USDA Soil
Conservation Service August 1972 From their survey and visual
observations of the ground surface it appears that the area is
underlain by rockland where the exposed rock covers up to 90 of the
area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that Lot 46 is designated for P 1 zoning and will not
be developed Therefore our reconnaissance will focus on Lot 45 which
is zoned R 5
Based on limited data and a field reconnaissance of the area our
general observations and recommendations are presented below
General
The field engineering and geological reconnaissance indicate that
Lot 45 may be suitable for the single family residential construction
The natural rock formation or properly compacted fill could provide
adequate bearing for light residential structures and related utilities
The following guidelines may be used for preliminary site
development concept purposes
1 Stockpiles of loose soils and boulders below the building areas
should be removed
2 Volcanic tuff rock is exposed at the surface in several locations
We believe that excavation into the rock can be accomplished by
dozer and ripping equipment
3 If deposits of clayey soils are encountered they should be removed
below building slabs and driveways
