INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Market Building and Parking Area in
the Laie Village Shopping Center on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated

This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Zion
Security Corporation dated The scope of work consisted
of the following
1 Drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths of 5 0 to 21 0
feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading pavement and foundation
design recommendations and

site

central portion of the site for the existing Laie Village Shopping Center

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered loose granular soils
underlying a 4 5 to 7 5 foot thick crust of stiff fill and medium dense
beach dune sand deposits
Fills up to about 4 feet is planned for the site Ground settlements
on the order of one half of an inch are anticipated for the fills placed
over the underlying loose sands
Construction of the building floor slabs and permanent facilities
such as sewer and water lines drainage structures and pavement should be
delayed until the settlement gauges indicate that the majority of fill
settlements has occurred or within the facility s tolerance We estimate
that the fill settlement waiting period would be brief probably on the
order of about one month
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the new
structural fill may then be used to support the proposed market building
and retail store
An existing sewer easement is located beneath the proposed market
building It is our understanding that this sewer line will be removed
Since utility backfill is generally not well compacted we recommend the
loose backfill should be over excavated and then replaced with on site
soils or select fill materials compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Site Grading
Fills up to about 4 feet high are planned below the new building
structure and parking area
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old pavements
demolished footings and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill should then be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated and then replaced with select fill materials compacted to a
minimum of 95 compaction as recommended above
Imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials generally less than 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as quarry waste cinder sand coral
and other non expansive soils All imported fill material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation
to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximm ary density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations

Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
project An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches may be used for footings bearing on the
above compacted fill material
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements for the anticipated colum load of 30
kips are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one quarter of an inch differential
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines For the western
perimeter portion of the new market structure located adjacent to the
existing drainage canal we suggest that the footing be lowered to a
suitable level to avoid future construction disturbance to the new footing
located along the drainage canal We will be happy to provide additional
consultation on this when the plans are being finalized
The bottom of all footing excavations should be compacted to obtain
minimum 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
prior to placement of the reinforcement The footing excavation should
first be observed by the project geotechnical engineer to determine if
adequate bearing has been obtained and just prior to concrete placement

Cold Storage Units
Cold storage units are planned for the market The freezing
temperatures developed by the cold storage units could initiate frost
action in the subsoils
Frost action occurs when there is a combination of freezing
temperature high water level or available water source and subgrade
soils which have high capillary rise potential When the surface of the
soil is below freezing temperature upward moisture migration from the
high perched water level can occur due to capillary action and
condensation The water will then collect and freeze to form lenses of
ice in the upper part of the ground which will displace the soil and cause
heaving The formation of the ice lenses can continue indefinitely as
long as suitable conditions exist
GEOLABS HAWAII
2427 00 Page 10
The on site soils consists of silts and clays which generally have
limited potential for frost action due to its low permeability However
the on site clayey soils have high water contents which could provide a
source of water and be susceptible to freezing and subsequent heaving
below the cold storage unit
In order to reduce the possibility of frost action below the new cold
storage unit a capillary break layer is recommended below the unit to
prevent upward migration of water and to act as a drain for accumulated
condensation from the freezer unit
A clean gravel such as 3B Fine gravel is recommended for the
capillary break material The thickness of the capillary break layer
would depend on the effectiveness of the slab insulation and depth of
frost penetration Generally a minimum thickness of two 2 feet is
recommended under the insulated slab In addition the capillary break
layer should extend beyond the market freezer floor slab for a minimum of
eighteen 18 inches beyond the limits of the cold storage unit

Utility Trench
New utility lines may be planned for the project A granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67B gravel 3B Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill to reduce
the level of effort needed to achieve compaction The backfill up to
about 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 9 inch horizontal
lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimm of 90 of maximum dry
density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce potential future ground
subsidence
Existing utilities and cesspools should be located and verified It
may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust structural details to
accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated condition or buried
structure be encountered during construction each condition should be
evaluated by the soils engineer and the design modified if necessary

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from references on the topographic survey map by Towill
Shigeoka Associates Inc dated August 30 1985 Elevations of the
data points were interpreted from the same map The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on

of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
