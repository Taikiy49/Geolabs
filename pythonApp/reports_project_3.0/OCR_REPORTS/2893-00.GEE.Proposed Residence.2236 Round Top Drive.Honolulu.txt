INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residential development at 2236 Round Top Drive in the
Makiki District of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of data obtained during
the exploration These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input and
are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimate or for
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located upslope of the northern side of Round Top Drive in the
Makiki District of Oahu Hawaii An existing residence is situated at the center of the site
and occupies about one half of the lot An existing garage is situated at the south end
of the lot We understand that a three story residential building will be constructed west
of the existing building
The ground floor of the new residence will be used as a garage to replace the
existing garage which will be demolished The ground floor will be located at elevation
320 above Mean Sea Level MSL A new driveway will be constructed leading from
Round Top Drive up to the new garage Site grading and retaining walls are planned for
the new driveway
The elevations of the proposed driveway in front of the existing building vary from
approximately 310 to 320 feet above MSL

Based on the topographic survey map dated and provided by
Control Point Surveying and Engineering Inc the project lot slopes from about elevation
331 above Mean Sea Level MSL along the northern perimeter down to approximately
310 above MSL along the southern end of the lot We understand that the existing
garage and part of the perimeter wall at the south end of the lot will be demolished and
replaced by the new driveway
At the time of our field exploration an existing concrete rubble masonry CRM
retaining wall constructed of boulder sized rock material was located at the northern side
of the lot The retaining wall was approximately five 5 feet high Cracking on the CRM
wall was noticed at the time of our field exploration
The area to the rear of the existing building slopes upward with an approximate
gradient of 1 5 horizontal to 1 vertical

Subsurface Conditions
The site was explored by drilling and sampling a total of six 6 borings to depths
of approximately 15 8 to 25 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Boring Nos 1 through 3 were drilled at or in the vicinity of the proposed building

Below a generally thin layer of loose brown and gray sand the borings generally

appeared to be loose backfill material behind the existing garage
Groundwater Conditions
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
exploration However a temporary rise of ground water level is possible and should be
anticipated especially during rainy seasons
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundations
Based on the boring data obtained we recommend that shallow spread and or
continuous footing foundations be used to support the proposed building The
foundations should be located in the in situ dense cinder sand below the proposed
ground floor level A net allowable bearing capacity of up to 3000 p s f may be used for
the foundation design This bearing value may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for
considerations of seismic loads wind loads and other short term loadings The footings
should be embedded at a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade and extend a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum ten foot set back distance from
the outside edge of the footing measured horizontally to the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2893 00 Page 6
Footing settlements are estimated to be on the order of up to one half 1 2 inch
total and one quarter 1 4 inch differential under the footing design bearing pressure
It is anticipated that the in situ cinder deposit at the bottom of the footing
foundations could be disturbed by the footing excavation We recommend that the
excavated footing subgrade be properly recompacted to obtain a minimum of 95 percent
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 in order to provide a more uniform subgrade support
Resistance to lateral loadings on the proposed structure may be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance developed on the footing foundations
We recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used
to determine the passive earth pressure developed against the footing foundations The
passive pressure must be reduced for footings located on slopes A frictional coefficient
of 0 4 may be used to determine the frictional resistance developed between the footings
and the recompacted footing subgrade
Site Grading
Considerable amounts of cut and fill are anticipated for the proposed development
at the site Up to about 17 feet of excavation is anticipated at the north side of the
proposed building area where an existing retaining wall will be removed A portion of the
existing perimeter wall at the front of the lot will be demolished to form an entrance for the
new driveway The existing garage will be demolished and backfilled with about six 6
feet of fill to form the driveway
At the on set of earthwork the area within the project grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable material should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2893 00 Page 7
After clearing demolition of existing structures and completion of any excavation
that may be required the resulting surface should be scarified to a depth of at least six
6 inches below finish grade and recompacted to receive backfills fills or the new garage
slabs The scarified subgrade in the fill and backfill areas should be recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The scarified slab subgrade
should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density
Soft or loose spots encountered at the scarified subgrade level should be removed
and replaced with fill material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent maximum dry
density in accordance with the ASTM standard specified above
The excavated on site cinder soils may be used as backfill and fill material If
required imported fill and or backfill materials should consist of non expansive granular
materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 percent passing
the number 200 sieve
Fill and backfill materials should be compacted in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Retaining Wallis
The proposed project will involve the design and construction of several retaining
walls The ground floor at the north side of the proposed building will be embedded at
approximately 17 feet below the outside grade Thus the building wail will act as a
retaining wall in this area Lower retaining walls are also anticipated for the new driveway
We understand that the existing CRM retaining wall at the northern portion of the site
which was noted to have some cracking at the time of our field exploration will be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2893 00 Page 8
reconstructed Your telephone conversations with U Kuong Ung of our office indicated
that the perimeter retaining wall at the south end of the lot had been reconstructed due
to serious cracking damages It is suspected that the hydrostatic pressure due to a
temporary rise of groundwater level could be one of the important factors conducive to
the previous damages of the retaining walls It appears that these retaining walls were
built without a proper drainage system behind the wail Also proper retaining wall footing
embedment is critical to develop the proper bearing support and lateral earth pressure
resistance
We recommend that the following design guidelines be used for the proposed
retaining wall designs
1 Lateral Earth Pressure assuming granular backfill material is used
Level Backfill
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Sloping Backfill
Walls unrestrained at the top 72 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Live vehicular loads and or other surcharge loads should be added to the
above recommended pressures for the wall design where appropriate
2 An allowable bearing capacity of 3 500 p s f may be used for the design of
the retaining wall footings bearing on recompacted in situ cinder
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2893 00 Page 9
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f equivalent fluid pressure may be
used for design of retaining walls with level ground in front of the wall The
passive earth pressure should be reduced for walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 45 may be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the walls
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For sloping
ground the footing should extend a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
ten foot set back distance from the outside edge of the footing measured
horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes should be
provided behind the walls to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls Subdrains should be constructed properly to drain water
away from behind the walls
7 The excavated on site cinder material and or imported select granular
materials may be used as wall backfill to the bottom of pavement or slab
or to a depth of two 2 feet below finish grade in open areas The two foot
thick capping backfill in open areas should consist of select impervious soil
to reduce the potential of surface water infiltration to backfill behind the
wails
Pavement
It is our understanding that concrete pavement is planned at the ground floor level
of the new garage and the new driveway The following pavement section may be used
for preliminary design purposes

6 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

The slab thickened edges adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subject to vehicular traffic
The above recommended pavement section is based on the assumptions that the
traffic will be light and that adequate drainage will be provided Laboratory CBR and
density tests should be performed on the exposed subgrade during construction to verify
the subgrade strength and the above pavement section may be revised if necessary
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe that critical
items of works such as retaining wall and fill slope construction subgrade over excavation
and backfill and footing excavations are properly performed This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by

design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
