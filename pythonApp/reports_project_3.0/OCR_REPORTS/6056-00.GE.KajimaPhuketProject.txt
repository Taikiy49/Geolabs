This letter report presents the findings from our geotechnical engineering
consultation services for the Kajima Phuket project in Phuket Thailand This letter report
summarizes our findings based on our review of readily available geological information
related to the site and its vicinity and our field reconnaissance of the property The
information provided is intended to inform the owner about the anticipated geological
hazards and subsurface conditions at the property to assist in the evaluation of the project
feasibility The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this letter report
Project Considerations
The project site is along the western coast of Phuket Island Thailand The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
approximate project limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The preliminary master
plan includes a Resort Hotel Core complex surrounded by luxury resort villas with canal
and lagoon features throughout the site The northwestern portion of the site includes
hillside villas on steep terrain overlooking the site
Significant earthwork will be required to develop the site including embankment fills
and excavation for the water features
It is desired to evaluate the feasibility of the planned development from a civil
engineering construction standpoint Our portion of the study focused on potential
problems and costly construction items related to the soil and rock conditions such as
hard excavation slope instability difficult compaction due to high moisture content
settlement due to soft soil conditions construction delays settlement waiting periods
special ground stabilization due to soft soil conditions near the ground surface and
suitability of on site soil rock for embankment fills

Regional Geology
The Phuket Island Province is part of the southern geographic region of Thailand
on the Thai Peninsula The southern peninsula climate is rain forest tropical typified by
generally year round rainfall with monsoons occurring from May to June and September to
October There are three main landforms typified by 1 highlands 2 steep hills and
ridges and 3 the lowlands The highlands are generally composed of granitic rock and
sediments of the Paleozoic Phuket Group and the Formation The steep
hills and ridges are generally composed of Permian limestone and the lowlands are
generally underlain by alluvial and colluvial sediments of the Tertiary Krabi Formation

Geologic faulting has had an influence on the surface topography by producing
local scarps fault valleys and directional river courses The present landscape is defined
as mature with generally unpreserved erosional and depositional surfaces The western
shoreline at Phuket is classified as submergent as evidenced by existing offshore drowned
valley structures The region is believed to be stable but slowly subsiding Earthquakes are
unknown in the peninsula and Cenozoic age rocks are rare In general the geology may
be characterized as a north south trending mountain range aligned with the predominant
geological structural trends In the project vicinity hills are composed of a belt of granitic
rock that traverses north to south along the western coast of Phuket with younger alluvial
and colluvial blanket deposits along the hill margins Beach deposits consisting of quartz
rich sand with shell and coral fragments are close to the existing shoreline
The earliest stratigraphy belongs to the Paleozoic Cambrian through
Carboniferous Phuket Group The Phuket Group is subdivided into the upper and lower
formations and generally contains and laminated
sandstone mudstone The younger Rat Buri Formation is generally composed of
Permian Age limestone and is typically limited to the eastern and central peninsula region
in linear aligned belts The post Paleozoic stratigraphy encompasses the Ko Yao
Formation which generally consists of Triassic Jurassic Age arenaceous sediments and
sandstone followed by the Tertiary Age Krabi Formation generally consisting of sediments
and shale Finally the youngest surficial sediments generally consist of beach deposits
river gravels alluvial and colluvial deposits estuarine and mangrove deposits mudflats
and travertine The beaches are best developed along the western coast and contain
some embedded beachrock material and limited coral reef material The alluvial and
colluvial deposits have historically been worked and re worked for the local mining of tin
As mentioned previously Paleozoic Age granitic intrusive bodies are encountered
in a north to south trending belt along the west coast of Phuket The regional granitic rock
batholithic intrusives span over 2 500 kilometers from Indonesia to Burma The coarse
grained biotite granites in the Phuket area have been age dated at approximately the
Carboniferous Age
Existing Site Conditions
The site is along the western coast of Phuket Island south of the
Phuket International Airport It is adjacent to Layan Beach and Katha Island The project
site encompasses approximately 105 hectares and extends from the beach approximately
1 600 meters inland The site is bisected by Road and is accessed via
several public paved roads and private dirt roads
The topography along most of the site is relatively flat to gently sloping with ground
elevations varying from 0 to 5 meters There are many ponds of varying sizes in the lower
areas with depths ranging from a couple meters to 30 meters in the large pond next to the
beach The topography and ponds in the area reflect the tin mining activities that were

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
Based on our site observations and a review of available geologic information we
anticipate that the project site may be underlain by four general soil conditions
Granite
The surrounding hillside areas including the northwestern portion of the site
planned for hillside villas and the offsite area northeast of the site that is currently being
used for borrow soil mining is generally underlain by granite The granite is highly
weathered and easily excavated by mechanized equipment Laboratory soil tests indicate
that the material may be readily broken down to a silty sand This material is generally
competent and suitable for embankment fills The depth to hard granite rock is unknown at
this time and should be explored by borings during the next phase of geotechnical
exploration Hard granite outcrops were not noticed during our site reconnaissance
Photograph Nos 1 and 2 illustrate the typical granite found at the site
Alluvium Colluvium
Much of the site is covered with alluviurn colluvium derived from weathering of the
hillside granite and deposited by water and gravity onto the lower portions of the site
Undoubtedly the condition and composition of some of the material is a result of the
previous mining activities The laboratory testing indicates that the material can be
classified as fine to medium grained sand with some gravel This material is generally
competent when placed properly and is suitable for embankment fills
Beach Deposits
The beach deposits are primarily along Layan Beach but are also mixed with the
alluvium colluvium further inland The laboratory testing indicates that the beach deposits
can be classified as fine to medium grained sand This material is generally competent but
quantities are very limited on the site

Pond Muds
The pond muds are located in the bottoms of the ponds and are a result of
sedimentation of material washed into the ponds by runoff These materials are very soft in
place and contain high percentages of clay The laboratory testing indicates that the pond
muds can be classified as plastic clayey fine sand This material is not suitable for
embankment fills and should only be used for landscape topsoil purposes or wasted off
site

Laboratory Testing
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained
from our field reconnaissance
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
Eight Sieve Analysis tests C 136 D 1140 were performed on selected soil

mud retrieved from the bottom of the ponds

Embankment Fills
Most of the site is relatively low and must be raised above the flood elevation
Although the on site materials are suitable for embankment fill there is a limited supply
available due to the overall low ground elevation at the site Therefore off site borrow sites
will be required to meet the grading needs Identification and exploration of potential
borrow sites are important in the early stages of the project design to assure that suitable
material is available within close proximity of the project site
Soft Ground Stabilization
The lowlying areas are soft and close to or below the groundwater level see
Photograph Nos 3 and 4 Therefore a stabilization layer may be required prior to
placement of the fill The stabilization layer will facilitate equipment traffic and help to
prevent equipment bogging down during fill placement The stabilization layer consists of a
geotextile overlain by gravel or coarse sand A typical stabilization layer is illustrated on
Plate 4
An additional benefit of a stabilization layer will be the reduction of loss of fill into the
soft deposits When fill is placed on soft wet soils a significant amount of the fill is pushed
into the soft mud until there is a sufficient amount to bridge over the soft soils A geotextile
will act as a separator between the fill and soft soils thereby reducing the amount of fill
lost
Due to the high groundwater level and seasonally heavy rainfall installation of a
subdrainage system within the new fill will help to efficiently drain subsurface water
through the fill The subdrain should consist of perforated 100 millimeter pipes within
gravel bedding see Plate 5
It is important to obtain accurate long term measurements of the tide water level at
the site to determine appropriate finished grade levels and optimum locations of the
subdrains

Ground Settlement
Much of the site will be filled 1 to 4 meters to achieve the desired finished grade
elevation The soft and wet soils within the low areas will compress due to the weight of
the new fill The total settlement will depend on the height of new fill and the thickness of
soft compressible soils below the fill The settlements may range from several centimeters
to tens of centimeters or more Additional fill will be required to compensate for this
settlement
A waiting period may be required in the construction schedule to accommodate the
ground settlement The waiting period will depend on the thickness of the compressible
material as well as the composition i e amounts of sand and clay Loose sandy material
generally compresses quickly with waiting periods on the order of weeks Conversely soft
clay soils generally take a long time to compress due to the low internal permeability of the
soil structure Therefore waiting periods would be on the order of many months
It is very important to obtain boring data in the lowlying portions of the site to
determine the composition and thickness of the compressible soils below the water table
Structure Foundations
The proposed residential structures except for the hotel should have relatively light
structural loading Therefore shallow spread or mat type foundations may be used on
properly compacted fill provided the consolidation settlements are completed prior to
construction
The adjacent Shangri La development is utilizing short 200 to 250 mm square
precast concrete piles to support their resort structures Photograph Nos 5 and 6 are of
the pile installation at the adjacent property The use of the pile foundations may allow
shortening the settlement waiting period and the use of less stringent compaction
requirements They would also provide a higher level of assurance that the structures
would be adequately supported and minimize the chance of movements particularly
during and after storms Additionally the use of pile foundations could reduce the amount
of fill required if the building floors are structurally supported and raised off the ground The
cost effectiveness of pile foundations should be evaluated in the preliminary design phase
of the project
Retaining Structures
Significant retaining walls will be required to develop the steep northwestern portion
of the site where the Hillside Villas are planned Various types of retaining walls
including conventional concrete cantilever gravity soil nailed tie back and mechanically
stabilized earth retaining walls may be used depending on the actual site conditions and

Retaining structures will also be needed along the future lagoon canal edges to

Summary
Based on our reconnaissance study it is our opinion that the project site is
developable from a geotechnical engineering point of view There are several challenges
posed by the site topography and geological conditions however there are technical
solutions available to mitigate these conditions
We recommend that the next phase of the design include borings strategically
placed to gather actual subsurface data to allow more definitive design The boring
program should be performed in phases for more cost effectiveness A recommended
boring location plan for the next phase of study is shown on Plate 16
