INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Townhouse Project at 707 Palani Avenue in the Kapahulu
area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These findings and recommendations are subject
to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk
of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority
of the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in
central Oahu
The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau shield Its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic
fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and the genesis of the
Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Waikiki area are dominated
by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are
intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The base
of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
The Koolau volcanic shield was built during the early Pleistocene by thinly
bedded basaltic lava flows The main shield building activities ceased approximately
2 million years ago Evidence from drilled wells indicate that the island of Oahu has
subsided as much as 1200 feet since the cessation of this early volcanic activity
Macdonald and Abbott 1970
During that period of subsidence coral algal reefs began to grow on the southern
coast of Oahu forming broad bays with barrier reefs across its mouth The growth of
the reefs essentially kept pace with the rate of subsidence A series of lagoons formed
behind the barrier reefs and both terrigenous and marine sediments accumulated in the
lagoons Macdonald and Abbott 1970

Land development and reclamation projects within the last 100 years have
brought the Ala Wai area to its present form Many of the resulting fills are of poor
quality in terms of supporting heavy structural loads

Based on the field exploration results and the current grading concept we
believe that the thin upper fill crust and the underlying compressible deposits would not
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed buildings without appreciable
settlements under the new building loads Therefore we recommend that drilled pier
foundations deriving support from skin friction and or end bearing in the underlying coral
formation below the soft soils be used to support the proposed buildings The drilled
piers should have a minimum diameter of 18 inches and embedded length of not less
than 15 feet Due to the presence of loose soft subsoils with shallow groundwater
table temporary casing during the drilled pier construction should be used to reduce
the potential for caving in of the drilled holes In addition placement of concrete by wet
construction methods will be necessary
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
material generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore for support of future slabs on grade we recommend that the
expansive clayey soils be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select
granular material to reduce the potential for distress due to swelling of the moderately
expansive near surface soils when subjected to moisture fluctuations Detailed
discussions and recommendations pertaining to the design of foundation slab on
grade pavement site preparation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
presented in the following sections of this report
Drilled Pier Foundations
In order to develop the required bearing and lateral load capacities the proposed
new buildings may be supported by a foundation system consisting of cast in place
concrete drilled piers Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsoil
minimum diameter of 18 inches and a length not less than 15 feet be used to support
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 9
the proposed structures The cast in place concrete drilled piers would derive support
from skin friction and or end bearing on the underlying coral formation An allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 15 kips per pier may be used for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic
forces
Lateral loads imposed on the foundations can be resisted by a combination of
the following 1 resistance of the pier soil system to deflect and 2 passive resistance
on the vertical faces of pier caps where applicable Lateral load resistance of the pier
is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the pier shaft
allowable deflection at the top of pier and induced moment in the pier Our lateral load
analyses indicate that each pier may be subjected to a lateral load of up to 8 kips with
an estimated deflection at the top of the pier of less than inch for a free head
condition For passive soil resistance an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth may be used
Pier settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch We
believe that a significant portion of the settlements is elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied

Loose Soft Subsoils
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by soft silty clays with
shallow groundwater table To reduce the potential for caving in of the drilled
holes temporary casing of the holes should be used Care should be exercised
during removal of the temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking of
the concrete pier Therefore a minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be
maintained above the bottom of the casing during removal of the casing The
anticipated high groundwater level may also pose other construction difficulties
since observation of the sides and bottoms of the drilled pier may not be
possible
Workmanship
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in
drilling the pier holes and in placing concrete into the holes
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is not recommended Placement of
concrete by wet construction methods using a tremie pipe will be required due
to the high groundwater table anticipated at the site The concrete should be
placed in a suitable manner by displacing the water in an upward fashion from
the bottom of the drilled hole A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be
present to observe the installation of drilled piers during construction
_ A low shrink concrete mix with high slump should be used to provide close _
contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soil In addition the

Slab On Grade
We envision that concrete slabs on grade may be required for the proposed
project Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by near surface
silty clays which are considered to be moderately expansive when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs supported by these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these clayey soils due to changes in the
moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the
swelling of the moderately expansive subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade
soils be properly prepared and capped with a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive
capping fill material for support of slabs on grade The non expansive capping fill
should extend laterally at least 2 feet beyond the edges of the slabs on grade The
upper 6 inches of capping fill around the house structures should consist of non
expansive impervious material to reduce water infiltration into the underlying capping
fill material
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after
preparation of the existing clayey subgrade soils Subgrade soils should be prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section
of this report The capping fill and the underlying subgrade soils should be kept moist
prior to final placement of concrete The capping fill would reduce the potential for loss
of moisture from the prepared moist subgrade soils This reduction in loss of moisture
should reduce the potential for swelling of the moderately expansive clayey soils

In addition we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67

Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We

Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavement will be required for the ground
floor level parking at the proposed townhouse buildings We anticipate the vehicle
loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up
trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement
subgrade soil will consist of the clayey fill soils encountered in our borings The
subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Based on the above assumptions we
recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings to depths ranging from 12 to 22 feet below the existing ground surface After
demolition of the existing structures an additional nine probe holes were drilled by
continuous penetration without sampling to depths of about 12 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface in order to evaluate the extent and thicknesses of the surface
crust and underlying soft deposits at the proposed building locations The approximate
locations of the borings and probe holes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings and probe holes were drilled using both truck mounted and portable drilling
equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 and
Logs of Probe Holes Plates A 5 through A 13
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths The blow counts needed to drive the penetration cone in the probe holes are
shown on the Logs of Probe Holes
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals

Excellent 90 100
The rippability of a rock mass is a function of the relative hardness of the rock
its relative quality brittleness and fissile characteristics A dense rock with a high RQD
would be very difficult to rip and would probably require more arduous methods of
excavation

APPENDIX C
Guide Specifications for Drilled Piers
I LOCATIONS AND DIMENSIONS
Drilled pier foundations shall be constructed at the locations to the lengths and
in accordance with the details and notes shown on the plans As built pier lengths
shall extend to the depths shown on the plans and shall not be deeper by more than
5 feet without prior written permission from Geolabs Hawaii Pier diameters shall be
as shown on the plans for each individual pier The diameter shown shall be the
diameter of the drilling bit or bucket
ll ALLOWABLE TOLERANCES ON LOCATION STRAIGHTNESS AND
PLUMBNESS
The center of the top of the pier and reinforcing cage the bottom of the column
cap wall or grade beam shall not deviate from the location shown on the plans by
more than 2 inches in any direction
The drilled pier shall not deviate from a plumb line referenced to the center of
the drilled pier as described above by more than 2 percent of the drilled pier length
from the top to the point of interest
The clear distance between reinforcing steel and the drilled pier hole sidewall
shall not be less than 3 inches
il EQUIPMENT AND OPERATION
Drilling casing setting of reinforcing cages pumping bailing concrete
placement pulling casing and all other phases of the pier construction operation shall
be performed with equipment and tools capable of meeting the specifications
All loose material shall be removed from the hole prior to placing concrete
Cleaning shall be by machine or hand operated equipment Drilling and placement of
concrete shall be scheduled such that concrete is placed in each pier hole on the same
day in which drilling is completed Holes spaced closer than three pier diameters
center to center shall not be drilled on the same day unless approved by Geolabs
Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
Appendix C Page 2
Guide Specifications for Drilled Piers
IV CASING
Casing shall be used where needed to maintain the integrity of the pier hole
during drilling installation of reinforcing steel and placement of concrete
Where casing is used the hole shall not be drilled ahead of the casing any
further than the hole will stand without sloughing At any time sloughing occurs the
hole shall be cased to the bottom of the sloughing area immediately before proceeding
to drill the hole any deeper
Inside casing diameter shall not be less than the required drilled pier diameter
The hole shall be filled with concrete and the casing removed as specified hereafter
All casing must be removed Failure to remove all casing will result in rejection of the
drilled pier
V CONCRETE
Concrete shall comply with all provisions of the project plans At the time of
placement the concrete shall have a slump necessary to produce the specified strength
and should be determined by the cone slump test ASTM D 143 Unless a higher
compressive strength is specified elsewhere the concrete shall have a minimum
compressive strength of 3 000 p s i at 28 days as determined by the concrete cylinder
compression tests ASTM C 39 9
Vi REINFORCING
The reinforcing cages shall be braced and tied in such a way that the cages are
not deformed or distorted during fabrication placement of the cage in hole and
concrete placement
Any reinforcing cage which is bent collapsed or distorted or which varies from
a straight line by more than 3 inches at any point shall be rejected
Reinforcing cages shall be set in the hole in such a way that they do not rake
the sidewall and cause sloughing Reinforcing cages shall not be spliced in the drilled
pier holes instead they should be fabricated to the full length prior to placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
Appendix C Page 3
Guide Specifications for Drilled Piers
The reinforcing cage shall be hung from a solid support at the top of the hole
so that it does not bear on the bottom of the hole and hangs free in the hole The
reinforcing cage shall be set at the required location and elevation prior to beginning
placing concrete and shall be held and supported in such a way that it does not move
during concrete placement
Reinforcing dowels which are to be set in the concrete after completion of
placement if required are to be installed immediately following placement of concrete
and prior to the initial set of the concrete The concrete shall be vibrated after setting
the dowels The dowels shall be securely fixed with templates or by other means in
order that they are not displaced during vibration of the concrete Driving dowels into
the concrete after the initial set is forbidden In the event dowels are not placed prior
to the initial set of the concrete they shall be placed by drilling and grouting as directed
by the Structural Engineer
Vil PLACEMENT OF CONCRETE
If the hole is filled in the dry concrete shall be placed through a hopper and
elephant trunk positioned in the center of the reinforcing cage The elephant trunk
shall be long enough and be of sufficient diameter to prevent segregation The hopper
shall be of sufficient size to prevent spilling of concrete outside of the elephant trunk
For placing concrete in a cased hole a minimum head of 5 feet of concrete shall
be maintained in the casing at all times Where staged casing is used the casing shall
be pulled and removed in units in such a way that after pulling one unit of casing there
is at least 5 feet of concrete in the remaining casing until the hole is topped out at the
cut off grade
Unless otherwise noted vibration of the concrete is not required until the
concrete reaches an elevation of approximately 10 feet below the top of the drilled pier
After this elevation is reached the concrete shall be vibrated continuously to the top of
the pier and until good densification of the concrete has been achieved Concrete shall
be added as required to compensate for consolidation and expansion into the hole
The concrete vibrator and the vibration operation shall conform to all requirements for
internal concrete vibrators and vibration of concrete given in the job specifications
