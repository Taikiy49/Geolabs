This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during the
mass grading foundation excavation roadway and utility construction for Kapolei
Intermediate School from through March 19 1999 in accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site encompasses approximately
20 acres in area and is bounded approximately by Kapolei Parkway to the north the future
Makai Village 8 to the south and west and Kapolei Lower Channel to the east as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1
The mass grading operations foundation excavation roadway and utility construction
for Kapolei Intermediate School were performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd The
site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation of unsuitable materials
placement and compaction of engineered fills pavement construction and utility trench
excavation and backfill
During the site grading operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Makai Village Partnership Page 2
W O 3801 10
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 1 foot below the finish subgrades in the building areas to
provide the recommended 1 foot non expansive select capping fill layer The over excavated
subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular capping fill layer the fill
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation
excavation and preparation the footing subgrades were compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction prior to the placement of steel reinforcement and concrete
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 1 015 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests taken are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 34 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading
foundation excavation roadway and utility construction for the above referenced project
observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were performed
in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS

simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were performed
without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
