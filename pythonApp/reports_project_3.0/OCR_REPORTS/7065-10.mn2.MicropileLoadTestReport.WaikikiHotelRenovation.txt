MICROPILE COMPRESSIVE LOAD TEST REPORT 
WAIKIKI HOTEL RENOVATION 
2452 KALAKAUA AVENUE 
HONOLULU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Hirai: 
In accordance with the project specifications, a static compressive load test was 
conducted on a sacrificial micropile for the Waikiki Hotel Renovation project.  The project 
site is located at 2452 Kalakaua Avenue in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  This 
letter report presents the results of our field observations, the test data collected during the 
testing conducted on March 27, 2017, and our analyses and recommendations. 
The sacrificial micropile located within the central portion of the basement at the 
existing Waikiki Hotel site was installed by Structural Systems, Inc. on March 15, 2017, 
utilizing a TEI-TD75 track-mounted drill rig.  The micropile extended to a depth of about 
64.1 feet below the existing ground surface with a 5.5-inch outside diameter (OD) steel 
casing.  A record of the micropile installation is provided in this letter report for reference, 
as shown on Plates 1 and 2. 
Based on our field observations, the sacrificial testing micropile was installed by 
advancing the steel casing to the design depth of about 64 feet below the ground surface. 
The micropile generally encountered loose to medium dense beach sand/lagoonal 
deposits overlying coralline reef formation occurring at a depth of about 44 feet below the 
ground surface.  Upon reaching the tip elevation, the cement grout was pumped via a 
tremie pipe from the bottom to the top of casing, displacing the groundwater inside the 
steel casing. After the initial grout application, a #11 GR-150 all-thread center bar with 
centralizers, spaced at every 10 feet, was installed to the full-depth of the borehole. 
The steel casing was extracted from the bottom 20 feet of the micropile, providing a 
bond length within the coralline reef formation while the remaining 44 feet of the steel 
casing was left in-place at the top of the micropile (unbonded zone) as permanent casing. 
The permanent steel casing was, then, further extended 3 feet in the coralline reef

formation. It should be noted that the sacrificial micropile was extended about 3 feet above

The sacrificial micropile was tested in general accordance with ASTM D1143. The 
allowable compressive load capacity was specified as 80 kips for the micropiles. A 
hydraulic jack system reacting against a load frame supported by four reaction micropiles 
(one at each corner of the load frame) was used to apply the testing load to the micropile.  
In general, the reaction micropiles extended to depths of about 65 feet below the existing 
ground surface.  A load cell was used to measure the loads applied to the micropile. 
The micropile settlement was measured with two dial gages attached to a single 
reference beam.  The dial gages were referred to as “left” and “right” of the load test 
micropile. In addition, two 1/100-inch division rulers were set on each end of the load 
frame and a remote reference was mounted on an independent beam that holds the dial 
gages in place, to verify potential ground settlement that could affect the dial gages 
readings. The surveyor’s level was used to observe the rulers and the remote reference 
for movement. 
In general, the micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of 200 kips in a 25-kip 
load increment.  Each load increment was held for at least 5 minutes.  The test load was 
held for 60 minutes (1 hours) at 160-kip load increment, about two times of the design 
allowable compressive load capacity.  The gross rate of settlement at the 160-kip load 
increment was less than 0.01 inches per hour. The test load was, then, further increased 
to the maximum test load of 200 kips in 15-kip and 25-kip load increments. Subsequently, 
the micropile was unloaded in approximately four equal decrements and held for 
15 minutes as the rebound readings were recorded in 1,2,4,8 and 15 minute intervals. The 
results of the micropile load test are summarized in the following table.  The results of the 
load test are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.3 and are also summarized in the table 
below.

Total micropile head displacements at 160-kip load increment (two times of the
design allowable compressive load capacity) and at the maximum test load 200-kip were
about 0.376 and 0.447 inches, less than the specified 0.5 inches, as illustrated on Plate 4.
Based on the above load test results, we believe the 5.5-inch O.D. micropile
installed at the project site with a bond length of about 20 feet in the coralline reef
formation is capable of supporting the specified allowable compressive load capacity of
80 kips with an adequate factor of safety and with reasonable settlement characteristics
compatible with the project design.
Based on the review of the load testing results, we recommend the production
micropile length to be remained as 60 feet below the concrete cap, consisting of 40-foot
unbonded length and 20-foot boned length. We also recommend increasing the micropile
allowable compressive load capacity from the original 80 kips to 100 kips.
