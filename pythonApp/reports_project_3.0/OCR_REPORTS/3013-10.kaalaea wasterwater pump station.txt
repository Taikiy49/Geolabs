SUMMARY REPORT
GROUNDWATER SAMPLING SERVICES
PROPOSED KAALAEA WASTEWATER PUMP STATION
This report summarizes our groundwater sampling services on the subject project
Our work was performed in general accordance with our revised fee proposal dated
May 26 1993
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in Kahaluu on the windward side of the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new wastewater pump station and force main at the
site The construction will involve dewatering of the excavations at the proposed pump
station site and along the Kamehameha Highway force main alignment
It is intended that the water from the dewatering operations will be discharged to
the City and County storm drain system Therefore a permit under the National Pollution
Discharge Elimination System NPDES will be required for this discharge

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the drilling
monitoring well installation and groundwater sampling services necessary to obtain
groundwater samples for the preparation of the NPDES permit application In order to
accomplish these tasks our scope of work generally consisted of the following work
efforts
1 Preparation of excavation permits and utility clearances as well as field
toning for underground utilities
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
3 Drilling and installation of three groundwater monitoring wells to depths of
about 15 feet below the existing ground surface at each well location
4 Logging of the borings by a geologist from our firm
5 Well development and purging of each monitoring well
6 Collection of groundwater samples from each of the wells by a geologist
from our firm approximately 2 days following completion of the monitoring
wells

DISCUSSION
Site Description
The project site is situated in the Kahaluu area in the Koolaupoko District on the
windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii Land use in the area of the project is
primarily rural residential The proposed wastewater pump station site is presently a
vacant lot adjacent to Wailehua Road Ground surface elevations at the site range from
about 7 to about 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The proposed sewer force main alignment runs from the pump station site in an
easterly direction along Wailehua Road to its intersection with Kamehameha Highway
At this intersection the alignment turns in a southerly direction and follows Kamehameha
Highway to the existing Kahaluu Wastewater Pump Station near the intersection of
Kamehameha and Kahekili Highways Please refer to Plate 1 for the location of the
project site and force main alignment Existing ground surface elevations along the force
main alignment range from about 4 to 8 feet MSL

trench in the area Below the fill layer the boring encountered the native dark gray clayey
silt and sand to the maximum depth drilled of 15 feet below ground level The boring logs
of MW 2 and MW 3 are presented on Plates 6 and 7 respectively
Groundwater Conditions
Shallow groundwater conditions were encountered in all of the drilled borings
Groundwater levels observed in MW 1 and MW 2 indicate a slight artesian environment
as evidenced by groundwater levels in excess of sea level These artesian head levels
may vary in response to precipitation in the recharge area and in response to tidal
fluctuations in the nearby ocean

Subsurface Field Investigation
Our field investigation was conducted on October 5th and 6th Drilling of the
boreholes was accomplished using a truck mount drilling rig advancing 8 inch outside
diameter O D continuous flight hollow stem augers Subsurface materials were
examined and classified in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84
by a geologist from our firm Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch O D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling 30 inches
After drilling to a depth of about 15 feet a monitoring well casing was lowered into
each borehole The casing consisted of 2 inch O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC
end cap made up the lower portion of the well Five feet of blank 2 inch O D Schedule
40 flush threaded PVC pipes composed the upper portion of the well The blank PVC
casing extended upward to the ground surface The wells were enclosed in both traffic
rated hole covers and standpipes which were padiocked The wells were backfilled with
a No 3 filter sand pack and sealed with layers of bentonite and grout The well housings
were then cemented in place
Development of the wells was accomplished by surging with a 2 inch surge block
and bailing to remove stagnant water and sediment present in the wells Sampling
occurred approximately 48 hours after development at which time the wells were again
bailed and a minimum of three well casing volumes were removed for purging prior to
sample collection
The hollow stem augers and associated drilling tools that had contact with the soil
and or groundwater were steam cleaned between the drilling and sampling of each
borehole

LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the borings may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
variations
The locations of the field monitoring wells are approximate having been estimated
by taping from references indicated on the topographic survey maps by R M Towill
Corporation dated October 1992 Elevations of the monitoring wells are also approximate
having been estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same plans The
physical locations and elevations of the monitoring wells should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
