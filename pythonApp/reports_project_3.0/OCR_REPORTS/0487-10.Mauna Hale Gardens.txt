SITE LOCATIONS AND PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed 3 3 acre development is located immediately east of
Makapu Saddle Road near its intersection with Kahinani Place in
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii According to the preliminary site plan
provided by Village Architects and Associates Corp the site is
located on a north facing hillside with the ground surface varying
from approximately Elevation 70 feet at Kahinani Place to a high
point of approximately Elevation 200 at the southeastern corner
of the property The existing site slopes are fairly steep with
a total relief of approximately 130 feet A steep sided gully
traverses the western portion of the property and is the principle
surface drainage channel for the site
The ground surface is grass covered with some moderately heavy to
heavy growths of scrub keawe and guava Some larger trees grow
in the vicinity of the gully Ornamental plants and trees are
located around the partially razed structure near the entrance to
the property
Previous sitework consisted of the construction of a concrete
paved access road leading to the frame dwelling and limited exca
vations and cuts in the upper portion of the site near Elevation
120 This earthwork may have been in connection with the adjoining
highway construction which produced a high road cut approximately
Page 3
W O 487 10
200 feet bordering the eastern edge of the property
It is our understanding that the proposed site development will
entail five clusters of townhouse structures composed of two to
five units in each cluster Separate parking areas will also be
provided for each cluster
Based on the preliminary site plan it appears that the earthwork
will entail maximum cuts on the uphill side of the proposed
clusters approaching 14 feet in height Shallow fills of 4 to 6
feet will be required for the parking structures It is our
understanding that the townhouse units will be of post and beam
construction which will reduce the fills on the low side of the
structures Should slab on grade construction be utilized fills
approaching 15 feet in height would be required to support the
lower downhill portion of the dwellings
The entrance road and access driveway will entail site grading
varying from 4 feet of fill on the low side to 4 feet of cut on
the high side
GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes Waianae and
Koolau Shield Volcanoes The subject site is located on the wind
ward side of the Koolau Volcano Range Following the volcanic
formation the warm climate and high rainfall has chemically

weathered the basalt rock leaving residual soils derived from the
in place decomposition of the bedrock Some exposed basalt was
noted in the upper end of the steep gully in areas where erosion
has removed the surface soils
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATIONS
General
Six test borings were drilled at the locations shown on Plate 1 to
provide detailed subsurface information and to supplement the
information obtained in our preliminary geologic reconnaissance
Soil samples both disturbed and undisturbed were obtained from
the test borings and visually classified in the field by our geol
Ogist The samples were returned to the laboratory for additional
classification and measurement of the physical properties A
detailed description of the field investigations and laboratory
testing is contained in the Appendix The log of each boring and
the results of the laboratory tests are also appended
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface explorations revealed that the site is generally
blanketed by up to 6 inches of topsoil in the undisturbed natural
areas Underlying the topsoil and at the surface in the exposed
cut areas the borings encountered a very stiff to hard silty clay
or clayey silt residual soil with thicknesses varying from 5 feet
Page 5
W O 487 10
to 12 feet at the boring locations Where the test borings
extended through the residual soil a weathered basalt or clayey
Silt with numerous basalt fragments was encountered
Groundwater was not encountered at any of the borings during the
period of the subsurface explorations This does not preclude
the existence of localized groundwater during the rainy seasons
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the subsurface findings it is our opinion that the site
is suitable for the proposed townhouse cluster development
Moderately expansive soils were encountered throughout the upper
portion of the site and the foundation must be designed to resist
these expansive pressures
From the estimated maximum cuts it appears that the excavations
will penetrate into the weathered basalt and may encounter limited
areas of hard unweathered rock where the excavations exceed ten
feet in depth
Foundations
Spread or line foundations bearing upon the natural soil or prop
erly compacted fill can be designed for contact bearing pressures
of 3000 psf for combined dead and live loads This value may be
Page 6
W O 487 10
increased by one third for consideration of wind and seismic
loadings A minimum horizontal setback distance of four feet
should be maintained between the outer edge of the foundations and
the slope face
Higher bearing values of 6000 psf can be utilized where the upper
four feet or more of the weathered soils are removed and the
foundations will be bearing on the underlying hard residual soil
or decomposed rock It appears from the proposed site plan that
this condition will exist along the uphill foundation areas
To minimize differential settlements the building pad bearing
elevations should be varied where possible so that the foundations
are resting upon similar materials either entirely on fill or
entirely on cut Where this is not feasible and the foundations
will be bearing on cut and fill the foundation bearing upon cut
should be over excavated to 12 inches below the foundation sub
grade and backfilled with compacted soil
The anticipated maximum settlement for foundations bearing in
natural ground or upon properly compacted fill is estimated to be
4 inch total and inch differential well within the structural
tolerances for this type of construction
With the exception of the materials encountered in the vicinity
of Boring 1 the remaining soils located within seven feet of the
Page 7
W O 487 10
existing ground surface have a moderate expansion potential
Swell tests performed on these soils resulted in swells of 0 4
percent to 2 1 percent on natural soils and 4 3 percent on a
remolded sample compacted to simulate the proposed fill conditions
This expansion potential is sufficient to distress slab on grade
construction or lightly loaded foundations placed directly upon
the expansive materials Due to the limited sampling the actual
in situ expansion potential should be established during construction
If the field testing confirms the laboratory tests special slab
on grade construction procedures should be avoided where possible
Where required a minimum of two feet of non expansive material
would be required beneath the proposed slab subgrade
The utilization of post and beam construction with spread foun
dations will minimize the detrimental effects of expansive soils
Where the spread foundations will be placed within the soil
mantié the foundations should maintain a minimum bearing pressure
of 1000 psf to offset the expansive soil pressures If interior
partition walls with bearing pressures less than 1000 psf are
utilized they should be placed upon a minimum of two feet of non
expansive compacted soil
RETAINING WALLS
Retaining walls with the on site soils as backfill and a level
Page 8
W O 487 10
backfill slope should be designed to resist active loads equiva
lent to a fluid pressure of 40 pcf The backfill immediately
adjacent to the retaining wall should be open graded compacted
granular material Weep holes or a subsurface drainage system
should be provided to prevent the build up of excessive hydro
static pressures behind the wall A friction factor of 0 4 can
be utilized for determining the sliding resistance of the retain
ing wall foundations
If sloping backfills are utilized or if building foundations are
placed within 10 feet of the top of the retaining wall the earth
pressures should be revised accordingly If these conditions
occur they should be evaluated on an individual basis to establish
the design earth pressures
Slopes
Generally the slopes at the site are stable in their existing
condition Limited areas of colluvial materials and or previously
placed backfill may be encountered The extent of these unsuitable
materials should be established during the construction operations
If these materials occur within the proposed cut or fill areas
they should be removed to provide a stable slope
The proposed cut slopes should be constructed no steeper than 1 horizon
tal to 1 vertical 1 1 the proposed fill slopes should not
Page 9
W O 487 10
exceed 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1 The cut slopes should
be inspected during the grading operations to determine if undis
closed geologic planes of weakness exist
Site Preparation and Earthwork
Within the grading limits the site should be cleared of all
deleterious and organic matter which should be removed from the
Site In areas to receive fill the ground surface should be scari
fied to a minimum depth of six inches The scarified surface
should then be brought to near optimum moisture content and com
pacted to a minimum density of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 72
The fill should be placed in horizontal six inch maximum lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the above density
Subsurface drainage should be incorporated into the earthwork if
excessive seepage is encountered to prevent the build up of hydro
static pressures
Where the natural ground surface is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 the fill should be keyed into the competent
underlying soil The keying is a critical operation which should
be performed under the direct monitoring of a Soils Engineer to
assure the proper construction of the proposed fills on relatively
steep sloping ground

It is anticipated that the on site excavated soils can be utilized
as fill They have a moderate expansion potential and should not
be used within two feet of slab on grade areas If the expan
Sive soils are utilized as backfill behind retaining walls they
should be compacted to between 85 and 90 percent of the maximum
density as determined by the above tests
Pavement Design
Based on experience with similar residual soils the following
pavement section should be considered for the preliminary design
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
6 Inches Selected Subase
The final pavement section should be established during the
grading operations by sampling the actual subgrade materials and
determining their CBR values
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the cut slopes fill placement and founda
tion excavations be inspected by an engineer from our firm to
verify that the subsurface conditions are consistent with those
encountered in the test borings The compaction operations should
also be monitored on a full time basis to assure that the

recommended compaction densities are consistently achieved The
special construction procedures recommended for the slab on grade
and spread foundation construction in the expansive soil areas
are especially critical and should also be inspected The recom
mendations given in this report are contingent upon such inspec
tions

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
six borings utilizing truck mounted equipment for Boring 1 and
portable rotary auger equipment for the remaining borings The
depths of the borings varied from six to fifteen feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings are shown
on the enclosed Site Plan and Boring Locations Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
diameter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depth
