INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation
performed for the proposed Ewa Expandable Housing Subdivision The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the 14 8 acre site for 161
Single family residential lots with 44 foot wide roadways to provide
access through the subdivision
The preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
investigation but it is believed that most of the site grading will
consist of filling lowlying areas and cutting along the existing cane
haul road at the site Fills up to 1 to 2 feet in thickness and cuts
up to 3 feet deep are anticipated
House foundation loadings are anticipated to be light and typical
for single family structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the project site in order to provide soil and
groundwater data for the design and construction of the site grading of
the subdivision lots roadways utility lines and foundations of the
proposed single family residential development
This study consisted of a review of available soils and geologic
maps and literature concerning the area geologic reconnaissance
borings test pits laboratory testing and engineering analysis
The information obtained from our field exploration and laboratory
tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this
report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development site is situated on the south side of
Renton Road and west of Fort Weaver Road It is bordered by Fernandez
Village on the north old railroad track and road on the south Fort
Weaver Road on the east and a cane field on the west
The site is a recently cleared cane field The area is fairly
flat with ground elevations that vary from about 37 to 45 feet above
Mean Sea Level
An old cane haul road that generally parallels Renton Road crosses
through the northerly portion of the site
Some concrete irrigation structures were noted at various
locations within the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a detrital alluvial formation of the
Quaternary Period identified as a valley flat that abuts the lower
Slope of the Koolau Volcano This older alluvium consists mainly of
stiff clayey silts and silty clays In the Ewa area remnants of
emerged coral reefs may underlie the older alluvium Some man made
fills were made at the site to construct an access road
Subsurface Exploration
Six borings were drilled and ten test pits excavated at the
proposed development site to explore the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing The borings were drilled to
depths of 16 0 to 16 5 feet and the test pits were excavated to depths
of 7 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 4
The approximate locations of the borings and test pits are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the log of borings test
pits and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings drilled and the test pits excavated the
subsurface conditions were generally fairly consistent over the site
The alluvial surface soils were stiff brown clayey silt and silty clays
down to about 13 to 14 feet where dense decomposed coral sand and gravel
were encountered in most of the borings The top of the weathered coral
surface was penetrated only a few feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings and test pits
during the time of our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclose that the on site soils are generally low to
moderately expansive with occasional highly expansive pockets or layers
of soils occurring at various locations and depths In general we
believe that if the on site expansive soils are capped with at least one
foot of imported low expansion potential borrow fill material the
potential for future swelling can be reduced significantly
To minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site soil below the on grade slabs be capped with
at least 12 inches of select i1l which will exhibit low swelling
potential when wetted and that the subgrade soils underneath the above
select fill also be moistened and protected from drying from the start
of construction until the select fill and slab concrete placement are
completed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 5
As an alternative special foundation design could be utilized for
Slab on grade on the on site soil condition
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
investigation but it is contemplated that the grading will consist of
excavating the existing cane road and minor cutting of the existing
higher ground to fill up portions of the lower areas
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the loose cane stalks and concrete irrigation
structures should be removed from the site Then the area to
receive fill should first be scarified moisture conditioned to
optimum moisture content and then recompacted to 85 but not more
than 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
test method
Inspection for Expansive Soils
We recommend that lots in cut and with less than 1 foot of fill
also be identified in the field and inspected by the soils
engineer during mass grading to see if highly expansive soil exist
within the one foot of the finished rough grade Such inspection
may also include near surface soil sampling and performing
laboratory swell tests If soils are found to be expansive then
they would have to be over excavated Generally for preliminary
cost estimate purpose about one foot of capping or over excavation
and backfill with low expansion potential soil is recommended at
this time
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 6
Fill Material
Excavated on site material generally less than 6 inches in size
may be used as a source of fill material
The site will require some imported fill to raise the site grade
backfill of trenches and over excavation of soil pockets
Imported fill especially when used as capping fill should in
general be of low expansion potential with maximum particle size
not exceeding 3 inches The CBR expansion value should be less
than 1 percent and the CBR value should be 12 or greater We
recommend imported fill materials be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being hauled to the site
Fill Placement
The fill materials within the top 2 feet of the finished grade
should be placed in horizontal lifts of about 10 inches in
compacted thickness The moisture content of the on site fill
should be kept within 3 percentage points wet of the optimum
during compaction
For the light residential construction we believe each fill layer
should be compacted to between 85 to 90 relative compaction
according to ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The top 6 inches of
the roadway subgrade should be increased to 95 relative
compaction

Utility Trenches
A dense weathered coral formation was encountered at about 12 feet
in some of the borings at the site If utility trenches are
required to extend through these materials moderate excavation
may be encountered
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize
subsequent ground surface settlement along the utility
alignments All backfill should be compacted to 90 of its
maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
In general cut and fill slopes of 2 1 H V can be used for the
proposed development
Surface runoff water should be diverted away from the top of the
Slope Slope planting should be incorporated as soon as
practicable to reduce erosion on fill slope as well as alluvial
soil exposed on the cut slope surface
Settlement
Stiff alluvial soils underlain by weathered coral formations were
encountered at the site Therefore ground settlement is not
anticipated for fills placed over this area
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that most of the houses will be of slab on grade
construction
GEOLABS HAWAIi
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
2500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on alluvium or compacted
fill This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
To reduce the effects of soil heaving for concrete on grade slab
the expansive on site soil could be over excavated down to a depth of
one foot below the slab bottom and then replaced with low expansion
potential borrow soils The over excavation and backfilling should
extend a minimum of 1 5 feet beyond the edge of the slabs
To control potential slab heaving from the swelling of expansive
clays after slab placement the clayey soils should be maintained in a
moist condition at the time when the above capping fill and slab cushion
fill are being placed We believe that this condition could be achieved
by adequately wetting the subgrade surface prior to the placement of
moisture proof membrane or slab concrete to prevent drying of the
underlying clay layer The amount of watering required will depend on
the weather and site condition A relatively heavy wetting may be made
at the end of each day during this construction period Excessive
wettings which would muddy and soften the site should be avoided
The moistening work may be done concurrently with other
construction activities and should be monitored by a soils engineer It
should be done sufficiently in advance of covering the slab area
If select fill is used to cap the on site soils the perimeter
thickened edge wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 6 inches
below the adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 9
If select fill is not available special foundation design
utilizing deeper footings and or lime saturation may be incorporated in
the slab on grade to minimize the expansion potential of the soil The
details of the special foundation design required may be determined when
potential borrow soil are identified
We recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel or S4C cushion
fill be provided below the slab In order to protect the floor against
future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface water away
from building foundations as well as under the structures
Recommendations discussed under the DRAINAGE section should be
followed
Pavements
Several asphaltic concrete paved roads are planned for the
project Some cut and fill are planned
Based on the results of our preliminary laboratory CBR tests on
the site materials the preliminary pavement design for cost budget
purpose is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Low Expansion Potential Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Subgrade Soil Compacted to 95
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised accordingly
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 10
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around each house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape and its watering near the
house foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1463 00 Page 11
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should have low expansion
potential tested and approved by the soils engineer prior its delivery
to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be imade
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring test pit
and laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance soil borings and test pits

expansive soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifications
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Log of Borings and Test Pit Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Torvane and Penetrometer shear strength tests were
performed on selected samples The test results are presented on
the Test Pit Logs
One moisture density relationship test modified Proctor
ASTM D 1557 78 was performed on a surface soil sample to
determine its compaction characteristics The test results are
summarized on Plate B l
Three laboratory CBR tests were performed on surface
soil samples to evaluate their suitability for pavement support
The test results are summarized on Plates B 1l and B 2
To further identify soil characteristics four Atterberg
Limit tests were performed The results are presented on Plates
B l and B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
Samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
subsoils The test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Thirteen one inch ring swell tests were performed on
remolded and undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling
potential of the soils in both natural and compacted conditions
The test results are summarized on Plates B 5 and B 6

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of irrigation structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by
specific requirements or recommendations within the soils
report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of irrigation structures debris organic matter and
all other deleterious materials These materials shall
be removed and disposed off site
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be recompacted to match
the density of the natural soil Localized soft pockets
Shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials

Til Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved a minimum of seven 7 working days
prior to transportation to the site Fill material
Shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks
or lumps in excess of three inches 3 in diameter
In addition the imported borrow fill material shall be
of low expansion potential less than 1 swell per ASTM
D 18835 Test Method and have CBR values of 12 or greater
when compacted to 95 of the maximum dry density
according to the ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure

IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not to exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within
each layer
B Rock fragments less than three inches 3 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is suffi
cient fine grained material to fill the voids between
the rocks
C Rocks greater than three inches 3 in diameter
Shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils engineer
D All 111 shall be compacted to a minimum of 85 but
not more than 90 of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 78 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be
demolished and removed as directed by the soils
engineer Backfilling of the excavations shall be in
accordance with these specifications
Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to heavy
rain the previous fill surface shall be approved before
resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the
Fill so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
