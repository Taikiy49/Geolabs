PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at 615 Keawe Street between Queen Street and 
Halekauwila Street in the Kaka`ako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
project involves the development of one high-rise tower of up to 42-stories in height. In 
addition, the project also involves the development of an eight-story parking structure 
with a recreation deck on the top floor adjacent to the high-rise tower. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The high-rise residential development and the parking structure are supported on 
a deep foundation system consisting of 24-inch diameter augered-cast-in-place (ACIP) 
piles with varying lengths and allowable compressive load capacities ranging from

Street located at the eastern end of the project site and the limitations of drilling 
equipment for drilling adjacent to existing structures, seven 24-inch diameter ACIP piles 
were converted into 30-inch diameter single drilled shafts. The layout of the new 
high-rise residential development project is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Site grading work for the project generally consisted of minor cuts and fills on the

in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing performed during the 
earthwork and infrastructure installation for the project only. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The General Contractor for the project was Hawaiian Dredging Construction 
Company. Jayar Construction, Inc. performed the site grading, utility line installation, 
and pavement construction. The earthwork components consisted of subgrade 
preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, utility trench excavation 
and backfill, and pavement construction. 
Following the demolition, clearing, and foundation installation operations, fill and 
backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, 
brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of 
the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified 
Proctor) under the building slab areas. The compaction requirement for the finished 
subgrades under the pavement areas was increased to 95 percent (or greater) of the 
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
New utility lines, such as water, sewer, and drain lines, and utility line 
connections adjacent to the project site were installed as part of the development. To 
reduce the potential for appreciable settlement of the pipelines, the bottom of some of 
the utility lines and below-grade structures were over-excavated for installation of a 
stabilization layer below the bedding layer, consisting of about 24 inches of No. 2 Rock 
(ASTM C33, No. 4 gradation) or No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 gradation) 
wrapped in a non-woven filter fabric (Mirafi 180N), where the soft and/or loose lagoonal 
deposits were encountered. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized 
on Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations with variations in construction
