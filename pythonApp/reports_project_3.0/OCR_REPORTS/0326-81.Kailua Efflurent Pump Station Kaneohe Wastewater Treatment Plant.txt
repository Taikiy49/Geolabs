I INTRODUCTION
A GENERAL
This report presents the results of our subsurface soil
investigation for the proposed Kaneohe Effluent Pump Station
The investigation consisted of soil borings laboratory testing
and engineering analyses as outlined in our proposal letter
dated and in general agreement with our contract
Engineering recommendations concerning design and con
struction are presented in this report
B PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The site of the proposed pump station is in the southeast
corner of existing Kaneohe Wastewater Treatment Plant which
is adjacent to Kawa and Kaneohe Streams
The pump station will cover an area approximately 100 ft
x 75 ft with the invert of the wet well at approximate ele
vations of 13 ft Ground elevation is approximately 5 ft
_ ITI FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of two
and deep and a continuous penetration sounding 40 ft
deep The drilling and sounding was accomplished using a
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem auger The borings were continuously inspected by
an engineer or geologist from our firm
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1
oy
and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5
inch 0 D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals
not exceeding 5 ft The Standard Penetration Test consists of
driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches The
_ number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 in
is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of
a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring
logs Samples obtained were visually classified in the field
and representative material was placed in air tight containers
and returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing
III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A GEOLOGY and SOIL CONDITIONS
The site for the Kaneohe Effluent Pump Station is located
near the mouth of Kawa Stream where it empties into Waikalua
_ Loha Fish Pond next to Kaneohe Bay Kaneohe Stream also
empties into Kaneohe Bay just north of the Kaneohe Sewage Plant
The site is level with some filled ground
The proposed effluent pump station lies near the edge of
deep bay muds deposited many years ago when this area was
_ much lower in elevation Geologically this portion of Oahu
was eroded into deep canyons at the time when the sea level
was several hundred feet lower than it is now As the sea
level rose these deep canyons were filled with alluvial
deposits eroded from the mountains Still later coral reefs
formed off shore developing lagoonal areas in which thick
layers of mud were deposited _
The boring logs for the two test holes indicate a con
tinuous layer of soft muds extends from near the surface to at
least 65 ft in depth These muds consist of dark gray silty
CLAY clayey SILT and layers of organic SILT with a scattering
of coal fragments and sea shells The muds are very soft in
the upper 40 ft becoming somewhat stiffer below that depth
The blow counts with the Standard Split Spoon Sampler were
generally less than 2 per foot increasing to 4 per foot below
40 ft Very firm to hard formation was encountered at 65 ft
depth See individual boring logs for a more complete des
cription of the soil layers

liv ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field Investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions and
recommendations are presented
J
A GENERAL
The subsurface soil conditions encountered at the site
preclude the use of conventional spread or continuous footings
for foundation support The structure should be founded on
driven end bearing piles or friction
B PILE FOUNDATIONS
Our analyses indicate that a 12 inch square reinforced
concrete pile driven into the bearing stratum at approximate
elevation 59 ft approximately 45 ft of penetration below
the proposed wet well invert should be capable of supporting
a design load of 45 kips per pile Higher capacities may be
realized however a static load test would be required to
confirm higher loading
A pull out capacity of 10 kips per pile may be utilized
for design uplift pressures
We recommend that a test pile be driven near one of the
borings to establish pile driving criteria
All piles should be designed and driven in accordance
with governing building codes and should be driven under the
supervision of a qualified soils engineer
An alternate solution would be to use shorter friction
piles oe
An allowable design Capacity of 650 lbs per ft of
embedment for a 12 inch square pile could be utilized
Pullout capacity for the friction piles would be approxi
per foot of embedment
C SETTLEMENT
Settlements of pile foundations are complex and difficult
to predict without a static pile load test We anticipate
total settlements of less than 3 4 inch with differential
settlements less than 1 2 inch for the end bearing piles
Settlements for the friction piles are expected to be on the
order of 1 to 2 inches
D CONNECTIONS
Flexible connections between the pump station and the force
main are recommended to prevent rupture of the lines due to
differential settlements

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
LABORATORY TESTING
1
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish
the engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered
at the project site
Classification Tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
Classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg Limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard Procedures ASTM D424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D423 for the liquid Limit were used in the perform
ance of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of the grain
sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
4y
were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were
tested for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear
box Each set consisted of at least two tests each test
run under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance ff
