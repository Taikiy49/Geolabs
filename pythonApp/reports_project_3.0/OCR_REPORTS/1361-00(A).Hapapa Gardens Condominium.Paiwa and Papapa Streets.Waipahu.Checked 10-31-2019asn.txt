INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation performed
for the proposed Hapapa Gardens Condominium project in Waipahu Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The condominium project site is about 4 acres in size and bounded
by Hapapa Street to the north general directions Paiwa Street to the
west the existing Waipahu Hall Elderly Housing to the south and a
proposed cluster subdivision undeveloped site to the east A
substantial amount of cuts and fills is required to grade the site for
the condominium lot preparation and related access roadway construction
A general layout of the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the preliminary grading plan cuts of up to 10 feet and
fills of up to 4 feet are planned A new retaining wall up to about 6
feet high and 180 feet long is planned along a portion of the boundary
adjacent to the existing Waipahu Hall Eiderly Housing site near Paiwa
Street
The foundation loadings for the one story residential structures are
anticipated to be light
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to provide recommendations for
Site grading foundation and pavement design of the project based on a
recent visit made by one of our engineers to the site and the soil
information obtained from the 12 borings drilled in 1979 for a previously
planned development scheme The depth of the borings varied from 6 to
30 7 feet The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

The drilling soil sampling and standard penetration tests were
performed under the observation of our engineer Laboratory testing was
performed on selected samples Detailed information regarding the field
exploration and laboratory test results are presented in the appendices
This report summarizes our conclusions and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
The site was previously occupied by plantation homes with shrubs
large trees driveways a large loosely backfilled retaining wall and
piles of loose fill materials in various locations of the site as well as
low soil berms adjacent to the adjoining streets Piles of trash and
organic matter were dumped in several localized areas throughout the site
Of particular concern would be the lowlying feature in the
southeastern portion of the site It appears that this is the remnant of
a small former drainage gully that ran through the parcel ina
northwest southeast direction This gully was then blocked by the
construction of the existing rubble masonry wall in the middle of the
site and its upstream side filled
At the time of our exploration in 1979 the surface elevation of the
gully bottom was roughly 50 feet and the surface elevation at the top of
the existing rubble masonry wall was approximately 68 feet Comparison
of the previous topographic survey with the recent survey indicates that

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geologically speaking the site is located in a detrital alluvial
formation of Quaternary period identified as a valley flat that abuts
the lower slope of the Koolau Volcano This older alluvium is generally
weathered to an iron rich mass of various colored soils with spheroidally
weathered cobbles and boulders The soils generally consist of stiff
silts with varying amount of clays and sands These valley flats are
frequently cut by numerous streams and gullies of various sizes Recent
man made cuts and fills were made for various activities
Soil Conditions
The soils at the site can be grouped into two major catagories
loose fills and stiff natural soils The fills can be further divided
into old fills placed before 1979 and recent fills placed since 1979
The natural soils are generally underneath the fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 A Page 5
Approximately 20 feet of loose old fill materials consisting of
loose bricks coral basalt cobbles gravel sand and silt were
encountered adjacent to the retaining wall in the 1979 exploration
Boring 12 The depths of the old fill varied Significantly in our
borings with thicker fill located towards the middle of the suspected
former gully and thin fill over some of the remaining areas Some of
these old fills exhibited sudden settlement collapse when submerged
below water A volumetric consolidation settlement of about 43 was
noted when the soil sample was loaded to about 950 p s f and submerged
in the water This is an indication of the loose nature of the old fill
material
Since 1979 up to 5 feet of loose recent fills consisting mostly of
brown sandy and clayey silt with weathered basalt rock fragments were
placed in the condominium parcel Isolated pockets of expansive clay
were noted at the surface of the site
Underlying the surface fills the dominant natural soil consisted of
very stiff brown silt which are alternately sandy and clayey The clayey
Silt is slightly expansive and swells upon absorbing additional
moisture Weathered and less weathered basalt gravel cobbles and
boulders were encountered generally below 8 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the boring data and results of the 1979 field
investigation laboratory testing data and engineering analysis we
believe the underlying natural soils and properly compacted fills will be
suitable for spread type footing support The new cut Slope planned in
the suspected old gully area would require over excavation and
recompaction of the loose old fill encountered at finished grade to
provide a well compacted slope face
Site Grading
Clearing Grubbing and Stripping
According to the present grading plan it appears that the existing
wall and some of its backfill loose piles of fills and existing
footings along with the trash debris and the vegetation would be
removed during site grading Tree and roots should be grubbed and
any roots in excess of 1 5 inches in diameter should be removed
Cesspools may be encountered near the previous plantation homes
They should be identified prior to grading operation if
practicable Sludge and other organic matter should be removed and
the cesspool backfilled with compacted select material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 A Page 7
Any other localized soft or loose fill area encountered during
clearing and grubbing operations should be excavated and backfilled
with compacted select material
After clearing and grubbing the existing overgrown area should be
stripped of topsoils that contain vegetation and organic matter
The stripping may average 12 inches The stripped material may be
reused as topsoil in the planting areas but should not be used as
engineered fill
In addition piles of loose fills placed since 1979 should be
removed and the underlying original ground stripped of organic or
loose soil down to firm soil It was noted during our recent site
visit that stems of smaller trees and bushes protruded through the
new fills which indicated that the area was not cleared before the
recent fills were placed The limits of these recent fill areas can
be identified by comparing the present topographic map against
previous ones
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify complete removal of any unsuitable
materials

Surface Preparation
lela et
After stripping the area to receive fill should be scarified at
least 6 inches below the stripped grade moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of the maximum density as determined by the ASTM
D 1557 78 test Modified Proctor Any soft loose or weak areas
disclosed during this recompaction should be over excavated removed
and then backfilled with engineered fill
Monitoring of the surface preparation by a soils engineer is
particularly important especially to identify soft or loose areas
or unsuitable materials
Slope Construction
In the suspected buried gully area it is recommended that the old
fill containing loose bricks coral sand and rock fragments exposed
in the cut slope and under the toe portion of the slope should be
removed and replaced with compacted fill The excavated material
may be suitable for fill provided that over sized particles are
removed A sketch presenting a typical section and recommended
construction is shown on Plate 3
The bottom of the key excavation should be at least 3 feet below the
Finished grade of the toe if situated in the old fill or at least 2
feet into the stiff natural silt whichever is shallower The width
of the key at the bottom should be at least 15 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 A Page 9
Fill or cut slopes should not be steeper than 2 1 H V The
on site soil is primarily silt with some sand or clay and easily
erodable Therefore the slope should be protected from run off
water to reduce soil erosion Interceptor ditches or swales should
be provided at the top of the slope to direct water away from the
slope
To assure adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back
to the design grade
The construction of fill on slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched properly Keying and benching
should consist of excavating a horizontal bench into the slope A
typical section showing keying and benching into an existing slope
for this project is shown on Plate 4

Decomposed and hard cobbles and boulders were encountered in some
borings and also noted in the loose piles of fill at the site
Therefore boulders would probably be found interspersed over the
site
The engineered fill material within the top 3 feet of the finished
grade should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness For the engineered i11 below 3 feet of the
finished grade the thickness of the lift may be increased to 10
inches Boulders or rock fragments in excess of 6 inches should not
be placed within the top 3 feet of the rough finished grade
Occasional over sized rocks may be placed in the lower portion of
the fill provided they are not more than 2 feet in maximum diameter
and can be embedded in compacted soil mass
If the material is too wet or too dry it should be dried or
moistened so that the moisture content at the time of compaction
will be within 3 percentage points of the optimum moisture content
Imported fill if required should be a granular non expansive
material with maximum particle size not exceeding 6 inches

Foundations
Spread Footings
he A le me mk lm le ae SOL le et
Spread footing foundations bearing on the natural soil or compacted
fill may be used to support the proposed structures
All footings should be placed at a minimum depth of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade The spread footing may be designed using
a net allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f for total of dead
plus live loads The bearing capacity may be increased by one third
for wind or seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that a retaining wall generally less than 6
feet high is planned along the boundary between the site and the Waipahu
Hall Elderly Housing to provide grade separation The guidelines listed
hereunder for wall design should be followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 A Page 12
1 For lateral earth pressures the planned retaining wall may be
considered as unrestrained at the top Active earth pressures of 35
p s f per foot of depth may be used
Z An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundation bearing on the stiff soil provided the wall foundation is
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjoining ground
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 can be used against Sliding of the wall
4 Generally a passive earth resistance from the adjacent land should
be ignored for the wall footing design because the lateral support
may be lost if excavation is made in an area adjacent to the toe of
the wall If lateral confinement can be assumed the passive earth
resistance may be assumed to be 300 p s f per foot of depth below
the lowest adjoining ground surface
5 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly constructed
to drain freely by gravity into suitable outlets
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house constructions It is
recommended that a minimum 12 inch thickened edge embedment be utilized
to provide a protective barrier against future water intrusion under the
house and covered lanai slab area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 A Page 13
To minimize the swelling potential of the soil the subgrade should
be kept moist from the start of construction to the concrete placement
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer
of 3B Fine aggregate and separated from wall foundations
A vapor barrier membrane may be considered over the base course layer
if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Pavements
A short road to provide access will be extended from Paiwa Street to
the southern end of the property Some cut and fill are planned
Based on the results of the previous laboratory CBR tests on the
site material the preliminary pavement design is as follows

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the surface preparation excavation and
placement of fi11 and foundation excavation for retaining walls be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
