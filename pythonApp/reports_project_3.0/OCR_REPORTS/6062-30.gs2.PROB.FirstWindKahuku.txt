Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

Kamehameha Highway The project construction will consist of twelve wind turbine
generators WTG designated as through 12 an operations and

improvements

Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in the above referenced report The purpose of the
probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below WTG No 8
marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing
operation In general the probe holes were located using a 10 foot on center grid pattern
for the wind turbine foundations The probe holes were also placed at the perimeter of the
beveled edge of the foundation The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the
Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the probe holes at WTG No 8 were drilled
to a depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to
approximately 10 feet below the bottom of the foundation
Drill Pro Company drilled the probe holes about 6 inches in diameter using a
jeep mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Prior to the grouting
operations a vacuum truck was used to remove cuttings encountered at the bottom of the
probe holes A total of 32 probe holes were drilled at WTG No 8 with an aggregate length
of about 320 lineal feet below the existing ground surface prior to the initial grouting
operation Three additional probe holes with an aggregate length of about 30 lineal feet
below the existing ground surface were drilled after the initial grouting operation
The probe holes were backfilled with sand cement grout supplied by Ameron
American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc performed the grouting work and a
Geolabs representative observed the grouting operations A 4 inch diameter rubber hose
attached to the end of the concrete boom pump trunk was inserted into the open hole and
lowered to near the bottom of the hole Each probe hole was grouted from the desired
depth upward by low pressure injection of grout through the rubber hose which was
retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Our field representative observed
the grouting operation and recorded the approximate grout take Grout quantities were
estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole
In general we did not observe significant voids and or cavities during the probing
operation We did observe significant grout intake for Probe Hole No 28 during the
grouting operation Approximately 2 cubic yards of sand cement grout was placed into
Probe Hole No 28 Three additional probe holes designated as Probe Hole Nos 28A
28B and 28C were drilled around Probe Hole No 28 We did not observe significant
grout intake for the three additional holes The probing and grouting summary is provided
on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the recommendations contained in the
geotechnical engineering exploration report Based on our field observations it is our
professional opinion that the cavities encountered at WTG No 8 location have been

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all the cavities
and or voids present beneath WTG No 8 Therefore some of the cavities and or voids
may not have been detected even with the probing and grouting program undertaken for
the project However the level of foundation preparation undertaken for WTG No 8
bearing on the on site coralline sandstone and coralline deposits appears to be
reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project Performing probing
and grouting of the foundations bearing on the on site materials is to provide the owner
with a higher degree of assurance that substantial cavities and or voids are not located
directly below the wind turbine generator foundation
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of

that provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type
