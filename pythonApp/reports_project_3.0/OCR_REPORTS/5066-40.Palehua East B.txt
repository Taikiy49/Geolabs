Dear Mr Miyazaki
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction
operations from June 2004 to February 2008 for the above project in accordance with
our fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The Phase 1 portion of the project consists of a singe family residential
subdivision with 143 lots and three roads It is anticipated that the residences will
consist of light one and two story structures To accommodate grade differences
retaining walls up to 10 feet high were necessary for some of the lots Infrastructure
including sidewalks and utilities were also constructed for the project
The mass grading work involved about 63 300 cubic yards of fill and about
174 000 cubic yards of cuts The mass grading work generally consisted of cuts up to
15 feet and fills up to 30 feet
Site Grading
Royal Contracting Company performed the earthwork operations At the on set
of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally cleared and
grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from the clearing
operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing operations
the areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were compacted using a
sheeps foot and or smooth drum vibratory roller

In general on site soil materials generated from the excavation were primarily used
in the deeper fill area The on site fill materials generally consisted of silty and clayey soils
with some gravel The expansion of the on site fill material ranged from low to high Import
material with lower expansion potential from Mililani was used in the upper portions of
some of the lots Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were
obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 3
Field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density tests
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to confirm
our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
noted
On site soil material from the excavation was primarily used in the deep fill areas
in general the fills were compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction where the
thickness of the fills was less than 15 feet When the thickness of the fills were greater
than 15 feet the upper 15 feet of the fills were compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction and the remaining fills 15 feet below the finished grade were compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction
Keying and benching were used for fills on sloping ground steeper than
five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V Boulder fill was used in the deep fill areas at
Lot Nos 279 through 284 In general boulder less than 18 inches in diameter generated
during site grading was used below 5 feet of the finished grade The boulders were
spread out with finer material placed and compacted to fill the spaces between boulders
A total of 3 699 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and
D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the above
project are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 199 Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the
Palehua East B Subdivision Phase 1 project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of
the plans and specifications and our recommendations In general areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were re worked to achieve the specified minimum compaction

Laboratory Swell Tests
Variable soil conditions were encountered at the lot finished grade Therefore
laboratory swell testing was conducted on soil samples obtained at the lot pad finished
grade to evaluate the swell potential of the soils to aid in the foundation design of the
houses
Based on our laboratory swell test results we categorized the on site soils into
four general types as described below
Type 1 Low Expansion Soil with 0 to 4 0 Swell
Type 2 Moderate Expansion Soil with 4 1 to 8 0 Swell
Type 3 High Expansion Soil with 8 1 to 12 0 Swell
Type 4 Very High Expansion Soil with over 12 0 Swell
The house foundation recommendations for concrete slab on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows
Type 1 4 Inch thick minimum concrete slab with wire mesh reinforcement over
4 inches of gravel cushion with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter
footing
Type 2 5 Inch thick reinforced concrete slab over 3 inches of gravel cushion
with 18 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 3 Remove 2 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
Type 4 Remove 3 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
The laboratory swell test results and slab on grade foundation recommendations
were presented in our interim Report Nos 1 through 8 and finalized in Report No 9
dated
Settlement Monitoring
Settlement gauges were installed in areas with more than 20 feet of fill to monitor
the rate and amount of settlement due to compression of the new fill Settlement gauges
were installed in Lot 125 and 127 and surveyed by a
licensed surveyor for a minimum of four months to verify that the settlements had
stopped

Water Seepage
Water seepage was encountered during the mass grading excavation of the hillside
at the rear of Lot Nos 1 through 9 The water seemed to originate from higher upslope
beyond the project limits Following field reconnaissance the seepage locations were
identified and subdrains were installed to intercept and drain the water into the drainage
system
Additional Recommendations for Homeowner
Homeowners should immediately verify whether their swales have been
compromised or altered from the original condition of initial move in If these swales have
been altered consult your plot plan and reinstall the swales Swales should also be
grassed as leaving them bare will result in soil erosion during heavy rains
If the swales can not be completely reinstalled because of constructed wall
obstructions installation of drain pipe along the rear and sides of the property can help
drain excess water away from the sidewalks and building slabs
lf a homeowner constructs improvements or expansions beyond the original floor
area for example expanding the lanai adding another 1 floor bedroom or family room
etc the services of a qualified geotechnical engineer should be retained to evaluate the
soil conditions at their respective lot for site specific building recommendations
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the
surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements Lot owners should also be advised not to water
excessively adjacent to the house structure

of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
