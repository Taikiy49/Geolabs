REMARKS Further Review of Hot Asphalt Treated Base Course HATB
We understand that the Contractor proposed to substitute the Cement
Treated Base Course CTB with Hot Asphalt Treated Base Course HATB
for the above referenced project However we were not aware of the fact
that the Contractor did not intend to meet the specification requirements of
Plant Mix Bituminous Pavements Section P 401 provided in the Federal
Aviation Administration FAA Advisory Circular No Airport
Pavement Design and Evaluation
As clearly stated in our facsimile transmittal dated August 21 2002 we
believe that HATB may be substituted for CTB as long as the HATB meets
all the specification requirements of Section P 401 as required in the FAA
Advisory Circular Based on further communication with FAA we
understand that this FAA requirement has to be satisfied because this is a
FAA funded project In addition we have no latitude with respect to a
variance which can only be approved by FAA Headquarters
In general we would expect the AC surface course that the Contractor
proposes to supply for the project will meet all the P 401 requirements
Because the AC that the Contractor intends to submit must meet the P 401
requirements it is our opinion that the HATB proposed for the base course
layer can also meet the P 401 requirements Therefore the Contractor may
elect to resubmit their design by changing the aggregates to make the Job
Mix Formula of the HATB meeting the requirements of P 401

REMARKS Evaluation of Proposed Hot Asphalt Treated Base Course Design
In accordance with your request we have reviewed the test results of a
HATB sample obtained and tested by Fulton Hogan Ltd for the above
referenced project
Based on Table 1 of the P 401 Specifications the Plant Mix Bituminous
Pavement or its substitution should meet the three Marshall Design Criteria
including Stability Flow and Air Voids Based on the test results provided it
appears that the sample meets the stability and flow requirements but it
does not meet the air voids requirement The air voids in the tested sample
is about 10 1 percent which is significantly higher than the range of 2 8 to
4 2 percent stipulated in Table 1 of the P 401 Specifications
As noted previously it is a requirement by the Federal Aviation
Administration that all the requirements of the P 401 specifications be met
including the HATB We all acknowledge that the P 401 specification
requirements are very stringent however all the requirements must be met
Therefore it is our opinion that the sample tested did not meet the
specification requirements of P 401 and may not be used as the HATB for
the project
