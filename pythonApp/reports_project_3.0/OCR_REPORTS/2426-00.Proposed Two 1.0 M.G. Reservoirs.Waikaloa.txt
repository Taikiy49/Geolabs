SITE DESCRIPTION
The two M G reservoir sites are located about 1 5
miles upslope east of the intersection of Paniola Road and
Waikoloa Road on the Big Island of Hawaii The site slopes down
towards the north at a ratio of about 10 to 30 gradient 1H 1V

reservoirs vary from approximately 1195 to 1225 feet above Mean
Sea Level
An existing newly constructed water well is located about 150
feet north of the proposed reservoirs
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers
an area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed
by the activity of 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which
is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent
geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa
and Kilauea both still active
The site is located on the western flank of Mauna Kea
mountain which makes up the northern part of the island and
consists partly of the older Hamakua Volcanic Series The lava
flows at the sites are Aa basalt types with dense zones of hard
rock and loose zones of clinker The clinker phase is highly
permeable Lava tubes are generally not encountered in Aa flows
A dry desiccated and erodible layer of volcanic ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash covers the surface of the lava flows

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations disclosed that the two 2 tanks are
underlain by a thick generally more than 3 feet thick layer of
volcanic ash mixed with cobbles and boulders over clinker and lava
rock formations From a geotechnical engineering point of view
the sites are feasible for the proposed construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2426 00 Page 5
The underlying volcanic rock can provide adequate bearing for
the proposed structures
Reservoir Foundation
The borings and test pits disclosed a thick layer of volcanic
ash and clinker extending about 3 to 15 feet below the existing
ground elevations Below this was a dense hard basalt flow that
was more than 10 feet thick Based on the proposed finish floor
elevation for the reservoirs at 1192 0 feet the tanks would bear
on the dense clinker and hard rock which could provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed reservoirs
Test data from similar soils in other areas indicate that
the ash soil structure may be subject to collapse when completely
saturated Therefore in order to minimize the possibility of
settlement in the event thick ash is encountered below a tank site
we recommend that the ash deposit be removed and stockpiled and
the excavation backfilled and compacted with on site select
granular materials Some of the ash soils can be mixed with the
excavated rocky materials to improve the gradation of the soil for
backfilling
Depending on the structure of the underlying rock formation
a choking layer consisting of well graded rock materials may be
required between the underlying rugged rock surface and the upper
cushion fill or concrete slab
For the footings bearing on the compacted clinker rock fill
or natural dense clinker an allowable net soil bearing pressure
of 4000 p s f may be used This value may be increased by one
third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered In
order to provide uniform bearing support the reservoir structures
should bear entirely on clinker or entirely on dense rock Since
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2426 00 Page 6
rock Since the two reservoirs will span over both of these rock
types we recommend that the tank pads in the dense rock areas be
over excavated to a depth of 12 inches below the footing subgrade
and this area should be backfilled with structural fill to match
the remaining foundations in the clinker areas
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility
trenches should extend down to a grade equal to or deeper than the
trench Alternatively the trench backfill which should be
completed first prior to foundation construction could be
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 or filled with concrete
All footing excavations should be properly backfilled and
compacted to 95 density per the above specifications
The site grading should also be designed to divert surface
water away from the tank foundations
Settlement
Based upon the boring data and the anticipated reservoir
loads the estimated settlements would generally be on the
order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth
of an inch differential

Due to the possible occurrence of loose pockets or cavities
in clinker and lava tubes in the underlying lava flows we
recommend that the footing construction portion of the project
include a program of probing and grouting to detect potential
voids below the reservoir foundations The wall footing
excavation should be probed at approximately 10 feet on
centers One probe should be drilled at each individual
spread footing The probe holes should have a minimum
diameter of 3 inches and extend below the bottom of footing
a depth of 10 feet
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of
cavities the probes should be filled with a cement grout mix
consisting of a one 1 part sand to one 1 part cement 1 1
mixture injected at low to moderate pump pressures i e the
pressure available from a grout pump If the volume of grout
injected exceeds four 4 times the calculated volume of the
probe hole the grouting should be temporarily stopped to
allow the grout to set up prior to resumption of the grouting
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the
observation of the project geotechnical engineer to monitor
the presence of voids and to allow additional recommendations
to be made if changed conditions occur and or if large voids
are encountered

Fill Material
In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches
in size may be used as general fill material If the
excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material the surficial ash soil may be
mixed into the excavated rocky material to increase the
percentage of fines to provide a better graded material for
compaction and increase the stability of the fills For fills
within 2 feet of finished grade below the structures and for
subsequent structural backfill within the reservoir limits
the maximum size of particle in the fill should be reduced to
6 inches to facilitate compaction
Fill Placement
Embankment fills if required should be placed in horizontal
loose lifts up to 18 inches thick and compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable
in excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and tests strips to determine the lift thickness
and number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the
desired compaction should be conducted at the start of the

The field exploration indicates that the 1 0 M G reservoir
sites are underlain at fairly deep depths by dense Aa basalt
mantled with a thick layer of clinker rock mantled with
volcanic ash
It is anticipated that the ash and clinker may be excavated
with normal rock excavating methods It is our opinion that
excavation in the dense rock formation encountered at the site
will require hard ripping hoerams and or blasting Care must
be exercised to avoid over ripping or over shooting which
would disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting
in a potential loss of bearing strength
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the
subsurface materials are based on interpretation of the boring
core data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this
site should examine the boring data and to make their own
interpretation
At the 1 0 M G reservoir sites it appears that most of the
cut slopes will be in varying materials such as ash clinker
and dense rock A compound slope with slope ratios of one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V for cuts in the dense
GEOLABS HAWAII

basalt formation and two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
for cuts in the upper clinker and ashy soils may be used
Soil slopes should be protected by grass as soon as possible
after construction to minimize the erosion potential
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted
away from the cut and prevented from flowing across the slope
face

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during construction
of the foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based upon information obtained from our site reconnaissance and
soil information from the borings and test pits Variations of
conditions between the borings and test pits may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings and test pits were determined by
taping from data points or structures indicated on the topographic
survey Elevations of the borings and test pits were approximately
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the
topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of
the boring and test pits depict the approximate boundaries between
soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition The
strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between borings and test pits and may not represent
actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of
this report These data have been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted
that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
