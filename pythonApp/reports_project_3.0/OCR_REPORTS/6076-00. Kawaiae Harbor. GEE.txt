SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Pier 2A Shed Demolition project H C 50038 within the Kawaihae
Harbor in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes
the findings from our field exploration and presents our geotechnical recommendations
for design of the Pier 2A Shed Demolition project only
The geotechnical recommendations provided herein are intended for the design
of pavements light pole foundation site preparation and underground utilities only The
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
We understand it is desired to demolish the existing Overseas Terminal Shed
OTS in the Pier 2A area of Kawaihae Harbor and to repave the area with rigid
pavements We understand the OTS structure encompasses an area of approximately
13 000 square feet Following demolition of the OTS structure it is desired to repave the
footprint of the OTS and the surrounding area with concrete pavements The total area

container handler lift trucks

inlets and manholes and installation of a new light pole

Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads imposed on the light pole foundation may be resisted by the lateral
load capacity of the cast in place concrete shaft In general lateral load resistance
of the shaft is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the
shaft allowable deflection at the top of shaft and induced moment in the shaft
The lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for
Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally
loaded cast in place concrete shaft program originally developed at the University
of Texas at Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a
cast in place concrete shaft under lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis
was carried out with the use of non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The
lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various
depths

3 4 Underground Utility Lines
We envision new underground utility lines will be required as part of the project
Generally we anticipate most of the utility line trenches will be excavated in the on site
fills encountered at the project site
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of open graded gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be provided below the pipes for uniform bearing
support As mentioned previously soft lagoonal and loose coralline deposits may be
present below the surface fill materials Where deeper utility lines encounter the soft
and or loose materials a subgrade stabilization layer should be provided below the
granular bedding In general we recommend the subgrade stabilization layer where
required consist of 24 inches of No 2 Rock ASTM C 33 No 4 gradation wrapped in a
non woven filter fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent
Free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper placement
of the backfill could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage

Summary of Findings and Remarks
We have reviewed the geotechnical exploration report by PSI Inc revised on
entitled Report of Geotechnical Exploration Westin Kaanapali Ocean
Resort Villas Lot Hawaii PSI Project No 888 65048 and compared it
with the settlement data from the surcharge activity for Wings 7 8
The subsurface conditions described in the PSI geotechnical report
showed the site was underlain by loose to medium dense clayey sand and silty sand and
very loose to medium dense coral gravel with interbedded soft silty clay layers The PSI
report estimated that structures supported on shallow foundations could experience
settlements of 4 to 5 inches If the site is preloaded PSI estimated the mat foundations
would experience total settlements of 1 to 2 inches based on a maximum allowable
bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot psf The report also indicated that the
majority of the pre loading settlements should occur within 2 to 3 weeks after placement of
the surcharge fill
There were no laboratory test results included in the PSI report Therefore it is
very difficult to estimate the expected settlements Most of the settlements appeared to be
immediate elastic compression Assuming a surcharge load width of 80 feet and a
surcharge pressure of the calculated modulus of subgrade reaction k ranged
from about 1 3 to 2 5 tons per cubic foot tcf for the measured settlements These
modulus values are very low The PSI report recommended k of tcf for design
of the mat foundations
In summary most of the compression settlement resulting from pre loading at the
site appeared to have occurred within 30 to 40 days from surcharge placement
Therefore it could be concluded that the settlements from pre loading on Wings 7
