This letter presents the results of our geotechnical engineering consultation for the Ala
Moana Boulevard Beautification project as a part of the Hilton Hawaiian Village off site
improvements The project site is located along Ala Moana Boulevard in the Waikiki area in
the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This letter summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our engineering analyses based on nearby field explorations conducted for the
off site improvements These recommendations are intended for the design of street light pole
foundations only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter
Project Considerations
The project site is located along Ala Moana Boulevard in the Waikiki area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii We understand that a new high rise hotel building is in construction at the
Hilton Hawaiian Village As part of the off site improvements new street lights will be
constructed along Ala Moana Boulevard from Kalia Road to Kalakaua Avenue Based on the
information provided the new street light poles will be approximately 30 feet in height The
new light poles will generally have two light fixture arms with no traffic light mast arm
We have recently conducted a field exploration program for the off site sewer relief line
project along Ala Moana Boulevard and Kalakaua Avenue The subsurface information
obtained from that project was used to design the new light pole foundations at the project site
Detailed structural loads of the new light poles were not provided for our analysis Therefore
we have assumed that the structural loading of the new light poles will be similar to those light
poles constructed for the Kalakaua Avenue Beautification project

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 for the off site sewer relief line project
The subsurface information obtained from the borings was used to evaluate new light pole
foundations The borings were advanced to depths of about 40 to 41 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our borings generally encountered surface fills over lagoonal deposits and coralline
materials The surface fills generally consisted of medium dense silty sands and soft to stiff
sandy silts extending to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The
surface fills were generally underlain by lagoonal deposits extending to about 29 to 35 feet
below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits generally consisted of very loose
to medium dense silty gravel and sands Underlying the lagoonal deposits dense coralline
materials were encountered in our borings extending to the maximum depths explored of
about 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The groundwater table was encountered at depths of about 4 8 to 5 8 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The water levels measured
generally correspond to between about Elevations 0 3 and 0 2 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
It should be noted that groundwater levels are expected to vary with tidal fluctuations seasonal
rainfall and time of the year
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology are presented in Appendix A
of this letter Descriptions and graphic representations of the materials encountered and water
levels observed in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings in Appendix A
Laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples and the results are presented in
Appendix B
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our field exploration the proposed light pole locations are generally underlain
by dense silty sand and gravel deposits followed by very loose lagoonal deposits Based on
the materials encountered in our field exploration we believe that the proposed light poles may
be supported on drilled shaft type foundations to resist the generally high ground line and
torsional moments induced by loading of the poles
Foundation analyses were conducted based on a combination of axial and lateral loads
in addition to ground line moments using the computer program LPILE Plus for Windows In
addition the drilled shaft foundations were also checked for the torsional moments induced
by the poles The analyses were based on a free head condition and a maximum lateral
deflection of not more than one fourth inch at the top of the shaft

For centerline loads we recommend 30 inch diameter drilled shafts with an

Drilled Shaft Foundations
Based on the loading conditions assumed and the subsurface conditions encountered
we recommend that the poles for the new street lights be supported by drilled shaft
foundations For centerline loading conventional L shape anchor bolt onto a single concrete
drilled shaft at grade may be used
Based on the bottom diameter of the steel poles 24 inches at the base the available
auger core barrel sizes from the local drilling companies and the shaft lengths anticipated
drilled shaft diameters of 30 inches were considered in our foundation analyses for the
single shaft foundations The major considerations in our foundation analyses included the
lateral loads and ground line moments induced in the drilled shaft foundations
In general lateral load resistance of drilled shafts is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the shaft and
induced moment in the shaft Based on a maximum horizontal deflection of
one fourth 4 inch at the top of the drilled shaft subjected to ground line overturning moments
and lateral forces we have evaluated the required drilled shaft lengths using the computer
program LPILE Plus for Windows This program is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite
difference laterally loaded pile program originally developed at the University of Texas at
Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a drilled shaft under
lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis was carried out with the use of internally
generated non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then
computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths The analyses were performed

Torsional Resistance
In general pole foundations will be subjected to torsional moments resulting in torsion
on the drilled shaft foundations For single shaft foundations torsion may be resisted by the
side shear along the drilled shaft surface and adjacent soils In our foundation analyses we
have evaluated the drilled shafts subjected to the assumed torsional moment of 1 kip feet
Based on our analyses we believe that the recommended diameter drilled shafts extending
to the depth recommended should be capable of resisting the torsional moment without
significant movements We wish to emphasize that the sides of the drilled holes in the
surface fills should be relatively free of loose materials and that the concrete for the drilled
shaft should have close contact with the in situ soils after the drilled shaft has been
constructed to provide the torsional resistance required

The performance of drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s method
of construction construction procedures and workmanship As a result we recommend that
a representative from Geolabs be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts during
construction In our opinion the following may have an impact on the effectiveness and cost
of the drilled shaft foundations

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we anticipate that drilling through
loose soils will be required during construction of the drilled shaft foundations To reduce the
potential for caving in of the drilled holes temporary casing of the drilled holes may be
required during construction Installation of the casing may be by vibration driving or twisting
to advance the casing Therefore the contractor should be made aware that temporary casing
of the drilled holes may be needed In addition care should be exercised during removal of
the temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking of the drilled shaft Therefore a
minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be maintained above the bottom of the casing and or
groundwater level during removal of the casing
The load carrying capacity of drilled shafts depends to a large extent on the contact
between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the shaft holes and
in placing concrete in the holes The bottom of all drilled shafts should be cleaned of disturbed
material prior to constructing the shaft The bottom should also be relatively level Backfill
against the shaft foundations should not be allowed
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids slurry is not generally recommended
Groundwater conditions are anticipated therefore placement of concrete by wet construction
methods using a tremie pipe will be required A low shrink concrete mix with high slump
6 to 9 inch slump range should be used to provide close contact between the drilled shafts
and the surrounding soils The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix and to displace disturbed material from
the bottom of the drilled shaft
Placement of concrete using a tremie pipe will likely cause some groundwater and soil
to discharge out from the top of the drilled shaft The contractor should be made aware that
this fluid should be properly disposed of Since the water will be turbid from soil particles
disposal into storm drains will likely not be acceptable The contractor should also be made
aware that a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES permit may be
necessary depending on the proposed method of groundwater disposal The contractor
should consult their independent consultant or the State of Hawaii Department of Health for
the latest regulations and information pertaining to the permit application
