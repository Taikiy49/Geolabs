Dear Mr. Mizuno: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the temporary repair of an existing sinkhole at the Halekulani Hotel from 
December 11 through 14, 2017 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
October 6, 2017.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project consists of providing temporary repair measures for an apparent 
sinkhole observed behind an existing sea wall at the Halekulani Hotel in the Waikiki 
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. A Project Location Map and Site Plan 
are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
During our initial site visit on September 29, 2017, the sinkhole was observed to 
be about 2 feet wide at the existing ground surface and about 2.5 feet deep. This 
apparent sinkhole was generally located behind an existing sea wall at the southwestern 
portion of the property, adjacent to a lawn area between Orchids Restaurant and the 
House Without a Key area. We understand at least two additional sinkholes have been 
observed in the area but were covered with boulders at the time of our initial site visit. 
We understand it is desired to repair these two additional sinkholes at a later date.  
Based on our experience and site observations, we anticipate the sinkhole area 
is underlain by sandy soils. We understand cracks have been observed in the existing 
sea wall adjacent to the sinkhole, and the area has recently experienced relatively 
significant tidal surge and wave action. Since only a temporary repair is desired, field 
exploration consisting of drilling and sampling of borings was not included in our scope 
of work for the project.

SUMMARY OF OBSERVATIONS 
We observed the temporary repair work of the apparent sinkhole at the project site 
from December 11 through 14, 2017. At the on-set of the temporary repair work, existing 
vegetation consisting of mown lawn grass and hedges were removed from the area around 
apparent sinkhole. LandScape Hawaii Inc. was the contractor performing the temporary 
repair work for the project. On December 11, 2017, laborers began hand-excavating the 
area in an effort to expose the apparent void from the sinkhole. The initial excavation was 
about 7 feet long by 5 feet wide and about 3 feet deep. Two apparent voids were observed 
on the western portion of the initial excavation. During these excavation efforts, an irrigation 
line was damaged, and the excavation was subsequently flooded with water. This irrigation 
water was observed to immediately dissipate into the apparent voids to the west, indicating 
the sinkhole was likely larger than anticipated.  
Based on our field observations on December 11, 2017, the void appeared to 
extend at least 6 feet laterally along the existing concrete curb and beneath the grassed 
area towards the existing Orchids Restaurant to the west. An additional void was 
observed at the ground surface under the existing hedge roughly 15 feet to the west of 
the excavation. This void appeared to be connected to the voids observed in the 
sinkhole excavation, as discussed above. Therefore, our recommendation was to 
continue the excavation to the west until the edge of the apparent sinkhole void was 
encountered.  
During our field observations from December 11 through 14, 2017, the 
excavation to expose the limits of the apparent sinkhole void was extended a total 
lateral distance of about 30 feet along the existing hedging towards the Orchids 
restaurant to the west before the edge of the apparent void was encountered. In 
addition, this excavation was observed to range from about 2 to 4 feet wide and up to 
about 5 feet deep. This excavation was then backfilled with No. 3 Fine Gravel wrapped 
in Mirafi 180N geotextile fabric up to a depth of about 12 inches below the existing 
ground surface. We understand topsoil material was used to backfill the remaining 
portion of the excavation up to the existing ground surface. It should be noted that 
excavation and subsequent backfilling of this excavation occurred daily due to safety 
concerns and to reduce the potential for additional voids to occur. Photographs taken 
during our field observations are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.4 of this report.  
Based on our field representative’s observations, it is our opinion that the temporary 
repair of the apparent sinkhole at the project site observed by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report, was performed in conformance with the general intent of the
