INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of the Pono Kai Association of Apartment Owners
we have drilled installed and tested two 2 new injection wells at the subject site In
addition we have abandoned and sealed one 1 existing injection well which has failed
The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our
proposals to the Pono Kai Association of Apartment Owners dated October 19 and
This report summarizes our findings from the drilling installation and testing of the
new injection wells at the project site In addition this report summarizes the
abandonment of the existing failed injection well
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Pono Kai Resort is located in the Kapaa area on the eastern shore of the island of
Kauai Hawaii The site is in an unsewered area and utilizes on site treatment to handle
domestic sanitary wastes generated in the residential complex The wastewater system
uses injection wells for the disposal of the treated effluent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2616 00 Page 2
The system was installed approximately ten 10 to fifteen 15 years ago and the
original injection wells have lost some injection capacity One of the existing wells Well
B apparently failed and could no longer function
In addition Pono Kai Resort is currently expanding with the construction of an
additional residential building Therefore it was proposed to install a new injection well
to replace the lost capacity of the original wells and to provide additional standby disposal
capacity Originally it had been suggested to drill and install one 1 new well to a depth
of about 100 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the review of the available data pertaining to the construction and history
of the original wells it was determined that it would probably be more beneficial to install
two 2 shallower new wells rather than the one 1 well as originally proposed since the
original wells had encountered relatively impermeable sediments at about 50 feet below
the ground surface and there was no indication that permeable materials would be
encountered below the sediments
An application was submitted to the State of Hawaii Department of Health for
approval to construct the two 2 new injection wells A condition of issuance on that
approval was that the failed well be abandoned and sealed along with the construction
of the new wells

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in the Kapaa area in the Kawaihau District on the eastern shore
of the island of Kauai Hawaii The climate of the Kapaa area is mild with an average
annual temperature of about 74 F and an average annual rainfall of about 50 inches per
year
The site is situated on a coastal plain adjacent to the northeasterly portion of the
Linue Depression and east of the northern end of Nonou The topography of this coastal
plain is a very gentle slope down towards the east from the mountains to the ocean
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 3 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas
of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the island of
Kauai These rocks are generally characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded
from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various locales Associated with the
vents are pyroclastic material e g usually forming low cinder cones at the vent
During the Pleistocene there were many sea level changes as a result of
widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to
fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2616 00 Page 7
rose because more water was available The land mass of Kauai remained essentially
stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These
glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower
relative to present sea level on Kauai
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs
The subject site appears to be in an old amphitheater headed valley eroded into
the Waimea volcanics which was subsequently filled by Koloa rocks and Palikea
Sediments The geologic map by Macdonald Davis and Cox 1960 indicates that the
site is underlain by Recent beach sands and noncalcareous sediments The log of the
old industrial supply well indicates that the beach sands extended to depths of about 50
feet below the ground surface and then the well penetrated approximately 100 feet of
sediment before encountering basal basalt rock formation
During historic time the project site was used for industrial purposes i e a
cannery This use most likely resulted in some minor modification of the terrain such as
filling and grading of the site
Foundation Conditions
In general the drilled wells borings encountered a thin surface layer of fill material
overlying medium dense to dense calcareous sands and gravels to a depth of about 50
feet below the existing ground surface where alluvial sediments were encountered The
materials generally have good strength characteristics and are capable of supporting
moderate foundation loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2616 00 Page 8
Since the injection wells are sited in an existing driveway and the foundation
aspects of the existing buildings and Building K which is currently under construction
were addressed in separate geotechnical engineering efforts foundation considerations
are not considered to be of significance for the wells themselves
Earthquake Conditions
The island of Kauai is designated in the 1988 Uniform Building Code as Seismic
Zone Zero Since no new structures will be directly with the wells seismic conditions are
not considered to be significant
Flood Problems
The existing site was engineered and graded for drainage There is an existing
storm drain system at the site and a catch basin inlet for this system is situated
approximately halfway between the two 2 new wells Therefore inundation are not
considered to be of significance to the operation of the wells
Land Use
The site is an area which is designated for residential and resort use The site is
currently used for these purposes and the wells are for replacement of the failed
component of an system Therefore construction of injection wells at the site is
consistent with this use
SYSTEM OPERATION
It is proposed to use the new wells for the disposal of treated domestic sewage
Prior to disposal the sewage will be treated at the existing wastewater treatment plant
The plant is a package plant which is generally of extended aeration design The plant
is supposed to provide secondary level treatment
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2616 00 Page 9
The plant capacity is 68 000 gallons per day with an average flow on the order of
30 gallons per minute and a design peak flow of 180 gallons per minute
The complete injection system will consist of four 4 wells arranged in an inline
series of sump boxes Injection will be under gravity pressure i e the head pressure
within the individual sumps Existing Well C is fitted with an Injectormatic pump which
pressurizes the well when flow requirements exceed gravity capacity and may also be
used to backflush the well One of the new wells will be fitted with an Injectormatic at a
later date Details of the system are shown on Plate 2
Since the system of wells appears to provide more than adequate disposal
Capacity there is no additional standby system nor are monitoring wells planned
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The site is located at Latitude N and Longitude W The
ground surface elevation at the site at the time of well drilling and testing was
approximately eight 8 feet above Mean Sea Level
The logs of the drilled injection wells are presented in Appendix A Generally the
wells encountered coralline sands and gravels overlying clayey silts and silty clay alluvial
sediments
Water levels in the drilled wells were noted to fluctuate slightly in response to the
tides in the nearby ocean Tidal fluctuations and efficiency are estimated to be on the
order of about 25 percent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2616 00 Page 10
The shallow groundwater in the area is brackish water in a sedimentary caprock
aquifer Instrument readings taken in the new wells indicate that the conductivity of the
groundwater ranges from about 500 to about per centimeter which
is roughly equivalent to chloride contents ranging from about 100 to about 500 milligrams
per liter It is believed that the groundwater in the vicinity of the new wells is somewhat
fresher than the groundwater in the region of the site because of the existing injection into
Wells A and C which are roughly upgradient of the new wells This belief is partially
substantiated by a difference in conductivity of about between new
wells D and E with Well D which is closer to the existing injection wells A and C
showing the lowest readings The caprock aquifer which serves as the injection aquifer
is isolated from the underlying artesian basalt aquifer by clayey soils and sediments with
low permeability
PERMEABILITY TESTING
When the new wells had been drilled and cased to their intended depths the wells
were developed by airlifting and tested by the injection of clear fresh water These tests
indicated that the injection formation has a coefficient of permeability k in the range of
500 to about 700 feet per day
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the injection of
Clear fresh water from a nearby fire hydrant at variable rates for a period of approximately
12 hours per well
During the injection of water the changes in water level in the wells were recorded
with respect to time and rate of injection
The results of these tests are presented in Appendix B

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled wells and pump tests
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until operation of the injection wells
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were determined by others We
can not assume responsibility for the accuracy of the location and or elevation of the
actual wells
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report This data has
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors
