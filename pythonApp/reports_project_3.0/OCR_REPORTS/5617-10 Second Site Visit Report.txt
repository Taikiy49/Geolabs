Second Site Visit Report for QA QC Program
Armor and Underlayer Stones from Huang Dao Quarry
Dong Province China
Dear Mr Kanna
This letter provides a summary of our findings from our second trip to the Huang
Dao Quarry and presents our recommendations for the QA QC program for the armor
and underlayer stones from this quarry
The second QA QC trip was made from November 6 to 10 2006 The purpose of
our trip was to visit the proposed quarry site and discuss stone quarry procedures with
the contractor As requested quarry guidelines were generated based on the
specifications for the armor and underlayer stones developed by JJRInc prior to
meeting with the contractor These quarry guidelines were used as a basis for
discussion
On the contractor Greg Hiyakumoto and visited the
Tong Ji University laboratory in Shanghai where we met Professor
Deputy of Geotechnical and Rock Mechanics Department We observed the stone
samples that the laboratory has received A total of twelve samples were sent to the
laboratory by the contractor with a total weight of approximately 90 kg with an average
weight of each sample at about The stone samples showed some variation in
color weathering conditions and seams as shown in Picture 1 Based on our
observation we believe that these stone samples represent the general stone
conditions in the proposed quarry
Based on information provided by the laboratory the first specific gravity test
results may be available this week

Later that evening we met with Mr Jin Quarry Contractor Paul Jordan
Deogaonkar and Raymond Ho to discuss the details of the
scope of work quarry operations and loading procedures Based on the discussion the
Quarry Contractor will be responsible for producing stones packing netting and
transporting to the harbor The local stevedores will be responsible for loading the
packed stones to the ship with supervision by the US trucking agent The US trucking
agent will unload the stones from the ship upon arrival in Honolulu and deliver to the
project site
On a meeting was held of the Shangri La Hotel and was
attended by Paul Jordan Deogaonkar Raymond Ho Mr Jin
and Geolabs The meeting agenda included stone classifications and quantities
contractor s responsibilities stone acceptance criteria for first shipment and owner s
inspection Based on the meeting discussion the contractor generally agreed to the
detailed procedures listed in the Stone Material Production Quarry Guidelines prepared
earlier with following revisions

7 All underlayer stones packed in the nets shall be placed in the staging
area for random inspection The contractor shall provide the equipment
to unpack the net and shall weigh the selected stone pieces using an
on site scale
8 The weight of each net for armor and underlayer stones shall be recorded
in the provided table format by the contractor at the weigh station prior to
storage in the harbor
9 Production stones will be marked with a Green A for acceptance and
Red R for rejection by the engineer In addition yellow dots shall be
painted by the contractor on each net after weighing at the weigh station
