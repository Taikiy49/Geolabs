DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site residual soil encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects
due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site
soil be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and
that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened
and protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
Tot Grading
Cuts and fills more than 7 feet are anticipated for the lot grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where minimal cut or fill is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade
slabs Where cuts expose weathered basalt the over excavation and
replacement by non expansive select fill can be eliminated
The field reconnaissance indicates that the site may be underlain by
a thin layer of silty soil mixed with cobbles and boulders over basalt
rock formation at fairly shallow depths

We believe that much of the excavation into rock for the proposed
facility may be done by bulldozer and ripping equipment The more massive
basalt will likely require special excavating procedures such as hoe ram
to make the proposed cuts or utility trenches
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our seismic velocity data and field visual
observations
The on site cut soil may be used for fill if they are placed below 12
inches from the slab bottom and away from retaining walls and compacted at
least 5 above optimum moisture content
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade for details refer to the Schematic Section illustrated on
Plate 3
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
low to moderately expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty
clay below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to cbserve compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings and seismic survey were
approximately determined by taping from structures indicated on the site
plans provided Elevations of the data points were approximately
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the site plans
provided The physical location and elevation of the field data point
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
