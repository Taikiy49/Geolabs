PRESENT AT SITE Gerald Seki and Marino
As requested a site visit was made today to observe the rain damage at the end of
Road C for the above project The following were observed during our visit
e The slope area at the end of Road C was eroded from the storm runoff
The slope erosion extended beneath the existing sidewalk at the end of
Road C We understand that the catch basin opening at the end of
Road C was covered with an erosion control device that restricted the flow
of storm water into the catch basin Therefore a significant amount of runoff
flowed over the sidewalk and down the slope to Road D
e Voids were also noted beneath the sidewalk between Lot Nos 211 and 212
It appears that the soils beneath the sidewalk were eroded away by the
storm runoff leaving voids beneath the sidewalk slab
Recommendations
1 The slope area at the end of Road C should be reconstructed to finished
grade starting from the low point near Road D up to Road C Soft and or
wet soils should be removed and replaced with compacted fill Fills should
be keyed and benched into stiff material to provide stability of the new fill
against sliding Fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness and should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction The fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Since
it would be difficult to compact fills placed beneath the sidewalk slab at the
end of Road C controlled low strength material CLSM or lean concrete
may be used below the slab

A significant amount of boulders has been generated during the mass grading therefore
we have been asked to evaluate the use of on site boulders within the deep fill area for the
above project Based on the grading plan deep fills of up to 10 to 15 feet in height are planned
near Lot Nos 218 to 225 We believe that the on site boulders may be used in the deep fill area
of the project site provided the recommendations in this letter are followed
For the purposes of discussion on this project boulders are defined as rock fragments
between 12 and 36 inches in largest dimension Guidelines for the use of boulders in fill are
presented below
1 Prior to placement of boulders the subgrade soils should be prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section
of our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Manager s Drive
Development Waipahu Oahu Hawaii dated
2 Some amount of crushing with a hoe ram or heavy equipment may be necessary
to reduce the boulders to acceptable sizes Boulders greater than 36 inches in
size should be disposed offsite
3 Boulders should not be utilized within 5 feet from finished grades beneath house
lots and within 8 feet from slope faces
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed utility
alignments to prevent difficulty in the excavation of the utility trenches at a later
date
5 Boulder fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding 60 inches Spreading and
compaction of each layer of the boulder fill should be accomplished with a
Catepillar D 9 bulldozer or larger for a minimum of 6 to 8 passes to facilitate
seating of the boulders The boulder fill should be watered heavily by water

6 A minimum 18 inch thick choking layer should be placed on the top of the boulder
