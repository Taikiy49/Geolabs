the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site
2 Performance of in situ soil resistivity tests by Engineers from our firm at 25
road stations along the proposed waterline replacement alignment These
road stations generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing maximum
required in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our initial soil resistivity exploration was performed on February 23rd and 24th
under sunny weather and a generally dry surface condition Due to the low resistivity
values obtained at some test locations a second exploration was performed on March
3rd at selected locations to reevaluate the resistivity results obtained from the initial
exploration These additional surveys included Test and 22A
A total of 89 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 25 locations along the
above referenced roads using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata
Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts and 24 watts output into the
ground This method utilizes four surface probes electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average soil
resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacings of generally 3 to 6 feet depending on the depth of the waterline from the
existing ground surface
Among these spacings used one spacing may have more significance than the
others for allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass
of soil to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings are flagged with an asterisk on
Plates 3 1 through 3 5

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result soil resistance
Ohm can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity Ohm cm which is directly
related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion rating are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 5 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristic
with regard to corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater the
potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the resistivity
the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until the new waterlines are installed
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been scaled from the construction plans transmitted to us by your office on
February 14th All elevations were rounded to the nearest foot to emphasize that they
may not be accurate enough for other purposes Elevations and locations of the
presented data should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
