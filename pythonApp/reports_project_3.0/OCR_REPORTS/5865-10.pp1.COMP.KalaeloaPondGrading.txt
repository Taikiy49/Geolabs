Dear Mr. Levins: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and compaction 
testing services performed during earthwork which consisted of excavating and backfilling 
for the site grading of the former pond site from April 9, 2009 to October 27, 2009 in 
accordance with our fee proposal dated April 6, 2009. 
Project Considerations  
The project is at Barbers Point Harbor near Campbell Industrial Park in Ewa on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The entire former Sand Processing Plant facility encompasses 
approximately 200 acres, while the area covered by the existing settling ponds is 
approximately 6 acres.  The former pond site was previously used for Grace Pacific 
quarry operations and consists of several effluent setting ponds and associated berms.  
The site consisted of three stockpile sites identified as Kalaeloa Ponds Disposal Site 
Stockpile Nos. 1, 2, and 3. Site development involved grading of the pond and reusing the 
fine materials from the pond when they became dry and workable.   
Earthwork and Site Grading  
The earthwork for the Kalaeloa Ponds Grading project generally consisted of 
cutting down and excavating existing soil berms and stockpiles at the previous disposal 
sites.  The work also involved the removal of thick deposits of unsuitable materials 
within the former settling ponds.  The work was performed by Grace Pacific Corporation. 
The soft/loose and wet soils in the settling ponds were excavated down until firm 
material was encountered.  The excavations were then backfilled with coralline material 
stockpiled nearby and the excavated soil.

During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing.  
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were determined in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Test Method for Laboratory 
Compaction Characteristics of Soil Using Modified Effort).   The laboratory test results are 
summarized on Plate 3. 
During backfilling operations, the fill and backfill materials were moisture-
conditioned to near optimum moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557. 
During the earthwork, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Test Methods for In-Place Density and Moisture Content 
of Soil and Soil-Aggregate by Nuclear Methods).  Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.18 of this report.  These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes.  These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used.  Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field 
representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, it is 
our opinion that the subgrade preparation for the site grading project were performed in 
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled, " Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Restoration of Settling Ponds, Barbers Point Harbor, Ewa, Oahu, Hawaii” 
dated January 9, 2008. 
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for alterations made to finished grades on the 
project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our knowledge and 
written acknowledgement.
