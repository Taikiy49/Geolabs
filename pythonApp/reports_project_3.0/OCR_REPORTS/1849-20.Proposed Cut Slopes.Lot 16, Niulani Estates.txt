Purpose and Scope of Work
It is our understanding that the level area of Lot 16 needs to extend
about 10 feet towards the rear in order to accommodate the proposed
residential house which presently encroaches into the originally designed
3 1 H V cut slope The purpose of this additional soil engineering
study was to provide general soil data at the rear of Lot 16 to aid the
design of the revised cut slopes Of particular concern would be to
identify the thickness of the highly expansive clay adobe which is
critical in the cut slope design

Field Exploration
Two test pits were dug to depths of 5 and 7 feet below the existing
ground surface with the use of a hoptoe The approximate locations of the
test pits were shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
The existing site condition consisted of bouldery slope with
approximately 5 1 to 6 1 H V slope ratios Boulders larger than 5 feet
in the longest dimension were observed on the surface The existing slope
is covered with heavy vegetation of bushes and trees Hard ripping of
boulders and trees were required for the hoptoe to reach the test pit
locations
Subsurface Soil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions exposed by excavating the two test
pits consisted of about 2 to 5 feet of dark brown clay and boulders
overlying mottled to light brown clayey silt with decomposed basalt
boulders Hard ripping and digging were encountered in excavating the
test pits Detailed descriptions of the subsurface soil conditions are
presented on the attached Test Pit Logs Plate 2
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our field exploration laboratory soil data and past
experience with expansive clay we recommend that the originally proposed
slope ratio of 3 1 H V should be maintained for the portion of the upper
cut slope 5 consisting of dark brown expansive clay and boulders The

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendation is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading especially the cut slopes
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
