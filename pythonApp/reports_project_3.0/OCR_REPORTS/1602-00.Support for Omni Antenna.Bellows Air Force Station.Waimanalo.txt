Soil Engineering Investigation
Support for Omni Antenna
Bellows Air Force Station
Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have performed a soil
engineering investigation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project and its vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a monocone antenna The entire
structure predominantly consisting of a network of cables resembles an
inverted cone with a hexagonal base Each corner of the base is
W O 1602 00 Page 2
supported by a pole which in turn is connected to one to two supporting
guy anchors The anchors are buried underground and will experience both
uplift and lateral forces Vertical loading on the foundations of the
monocone antenna are anticipated to be low
The finished elevation of the antenna structure was unknown at the
time this report was prepared However we believe that only small
amount of grading which may involve both cut and fill will be required
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsoil
condition below the project site and to provide pertinent soils and
foundation design data for the support of the monocone antenna
The scope of investigation include drilling and sampling two 2
borings laboratory testing of the sample obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration log of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located at a distance of about 300 feet to the
southeast of Waimanalo Stream inside Bellows Air Force Station at
Waimanalo on the island of Oahu Hawaii
To the northeast of the site is an abandoned taxiway which runs
perpendicular to the stream The site is primarily an unused open area
which is heavily vegetated with grasses bushes and some tall trees The
existing project site slopes down gently towards the northeast Ground
cover in the vicinity of the drilled boring consists of silty fine sand
with some gravels
The site is also located close to an area with archeological
interests However our borings did not result in any archeological
finds as determined by an archeologist who monitored our field work
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface condition at the site was explored by drilling two
borings to depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on Site Plan Plate 2
Both borings encountered a thin layer of top soil which consists of
medium dense to dense dark brown silty fine sand with some gravels
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1602 00 Page 4
Below the top soil was a layer of light brown to gray fine sand which
extended to the full depth of the boring drilled In general the
consistency of this sandy layer varied from a medium dense to dense
condition above the water table to a medium dense to loose condition
below
Groundwater was encountered during our exploration at a depth of
4 5 feet below the ground surface

Foundation
The project site is underlain by sand with a medium dense to dense
consistency above water table Therefore we believe that the foundation
structures which include the base pier and the guy poles can be
constructed on the recompacted in situ sandy soil

Base Pier
For the base pier an allowable bearing capacity up to 2 500 p s f
may be used The bottom of the pier footing should be embedded at
least three 3 feet below the lowest adjacent grade It is also
recommended that the bottom of pier footing be located above the
water table a criteria upon which the above recommended bearing
capacity is based on
Settlement of the base pier footing under its dead weight and the
anticipated light loading is estimated to be minimal and probably
less than 1 4 inch
Guy Pole
We believe that the guy poles can bear on the in situ sandy
material For end bearing capacity we would recommend that a
value of 2 000 p s f be used However for the total vertical
capacity of the guy pole the frictional resistance generated
between the pole and the surrounding soil should be included A
friction coefficient of 0 35 is recommended for the wood and soil
interface It should be understood that the recommended friction
coefficient does not contain a factor of safety
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1602 00 Page 6
Minimum embedment of the guy pole will depend on the required
vertical capacity For a 12 inch diameter wooden pole embedded
seven 7 feet into the ground the calculated ultimate vertical
capacity is about 4 500 pounds
Settlement under the weight of the guy pole and the loading should
be small and would probably be completed during the construction
period
Up Lift Resistance
Up lift forces with a maximum magnitude of 6 400 pounds inclined
at an angle of 45 degrees from the ground surface is anticipated
for the guy anchor Also some amount of up lift forces may be
experienced by the base pier
To resist these up lift forces the dead weight of the anchor as
well as the weight of the soil above the anchor and frictional
resistance from the soil may be considered A total soil unit
weight of 115 pounds per cubic foot can be assumed For soil below
the water table a submerged unit weight of 53 pounds per cubic
foot should be used
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the soil and
concrete

To determine the allowable up lift capacity a factor of safety of
at least two 2 should be used
Lateral Resistance
Lateral forces due to the horizontal component of the anchor load
is anticipated to act on the guy anchor
Lateral loads acting on the guy anchor may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the anchor
and by friction resistance developed between the bottom of the
foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f This assumes that the soil
around the anchor is well compacted The base frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction
of the base pier and guy anchors The bottom of the excavation should be
thoroughly tamped prior to the placement of the base pier and the guy
anchors For excavation below the water table dewatering may be
required

Excavated on site granular sand material free of any organic and
other expansive component may be used as a source of fill material All
imported fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty
sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All backfill materials should be placed in
maximum of 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 95 of the
maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation support of the antenna
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the excavations for the base pier and the guy
anchors be inspected by the soils engineer prior to placement of concrete
or reinforcing steel to check that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the lot were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck
mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the excavating operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
encountered in the borings are presented in the Log of Borings Plates
A l and A 2 All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
samplers for 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
