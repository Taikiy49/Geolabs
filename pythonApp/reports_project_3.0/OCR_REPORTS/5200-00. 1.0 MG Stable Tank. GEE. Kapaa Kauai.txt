SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 1 0 Million Gallon MG Stable Tank project in the Kapaa
area of the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses for the proposed water tank project These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundations site grading and pavements only The findings
and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located along Kaapuni Road in the lower Kapaa area on the
Island of Kauai Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is
proposed to construct a new potable water tank at the project site The
proposed tank will be located partially overlapping the footprint of an abandoned
square shape concrete tank which will be demolished
We understand that the new water tank will be a reinforced concrete
structure having a diameter of approximately 110 feet Based on the preliminary design
information provided to us the floor elevation will be set at approximately 198 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL and the overflow elevation will be at approximately 214 feet
MSL The finish grade surrounding the tank will be at about 202 feet MSL therefore
the tank will be buried a minimum depth of about 4 feet below the finish grade The
existing ground surface elevation at the tank site resides at approximately 202 to 206
feet MSL A short length access road connecting to Kaapuni Road and a service
perimeter road is also planned for the water tank project

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single dissected basaltic shield volcano
built by the extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series beginning about
5 to 6 million years ago The eruption of the Waimea Volcanic Series ended about
2 million years ago and was followed by a long period of erosion Following the
cessation of this main volcano shield building phase about 14 million years ago there
was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of basaltic lavas of the post erosional
Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the thick alluvial sediments of
the Palikea Formation
The basalt rock of the Koloa Volcanic Series is generally characterized by thick
lava flows of dense basaitic rock extruded from groups of vents aligned in north south
trends in various locales on the eastern half of the Island of Kauai Associated with the
Koloa Volcanic Series lava flows are some localized volcanic deposits consisting of
pyroclastic materials volcanic ash and cinders which usually are encountered as
surficial mantling deposits and accumulations surrounding the cinder cone vents
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the Island of Kauai
including the project site
The project site is located inland on a broad sloping plateau region which is
underlain by weathered soils and basaltic rock belonging to the Koloa Volcanic Series
Multiple meandering stream channels which drain towards the ocean located easterly
of the project site incise the inland plateau region The near surface soils at the project
site consist of residual and saprolitic soils completely weathered rock derived from the
deep in situ weathering of the Koloa Volcanic Series igneous rocks In general the
near surface residual and saprolitic soils typically grade to highly and moderately
weathered basalt rock formation with increasing depth Therefore occasional hard
basaltic rock boulders may be encountered embedded within the deeply weathered
soils

2 2 Site Description
The project site is located along the northern side of Kaapuni Road in the Kapaa
area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
project site is located at an existing County of Kauai Department of Water tank lot
which encompasses approximately 1 2 acres of land An existing square shaped
concrete water tank is located at the site We understand that the existing water tank
has been abandoned and will be demolished
Based on our site observations and the topographic map provided the project
site encompasses an area of graded land with near level ground surface elevations of
about 204 feet Mean Sea Level MSL located at the central portion of the site The
existing concrete water tank resides within the level land area The proposed water
tank will be located within this area as well There is an existing hillslope bordering a
meandering stream channel located at the northeastern portion of the property The
slope is estimated to be about 50 feet in height with an overall inclination of about
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V sloping down towards the northeasterly direction
Ground surface elevations at the project site range from about 180 feet MSL near the
base of the hillslope to about 206 feet MSL within the flattened area at the northern
central portion of the site The Site Plan Plate 2 depicts the existing topographic
conditions in relation to the existing and proposed tank structure locations at the project
site

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered very stiff silty and
clayey soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
time of our field exploration
We understand that the proposed water tank will be buried to a minimum
depth of about 4 feet below the finish grade elevation at the project site Based on the
relatively stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we recommend that a
shallow foundation system be used to support the water tank The soils encountered at
the bottom of the footing elevation should be over excavated a minimum of 12 inches and
replaced with select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We understand that the new tank foundation will overlap a portion of the existing
concrete tank footprint which has a finish floor elevation of about 201 feet MSL The
existing tank foundations and any disturbed soil below should be completely removed and
replaced with structural fill The structural fill materials should consist of select borrow
materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Based on the laboratory test results the surface soils may exhibit a low potential for
shrinkage and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential
for future distress to the slabs on grade we recommend that the slab subgrade soils be
capped with a minimum of 12 inches of compacted select borrow This will also provide
more uniform support for the tank slab The slab subgrade should be kept moist and free
of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of the select borrow and slab concrete

3 1 Water Tank Foundations
Based on our field exploration very stiff residual and saprolitic soils were
encountered at the project site Due to the relatively competent subsurface soils we
recommend that shallow foundations may be used for support of the proposed water
tank An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on 12 inches of select borrow over
the stiff to very stiff clayey silts at the project site This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces The select borrow below the bottom of footing
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades If foundations are located next to utility trenches or
easements they should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement Footings constructed near tops of slopes should be embedded deep enough
to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outside
edge of the footings to the slope face
If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements of the foundations bearing on the
compacted select borrow to be on the order of 1 inch Differential settlements between
footings bearing on the compacted select borrow are estimated to be on the order of
0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive

3 2 Slab On Grade
As previously mentioned the project site is underlain by very stiff clayey and silty
soils Based on our laboratory testing the clayey silt soils generally have low to
moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore to
reduce the potential for distress to the tank floor due to shrinking and swelling of the
surface soils we recommend that the slab subgrade be over excavated a minimum of
12 inches The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with the select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The select borrow should consist of crushed basalt aggregate For the design of
structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per square inch per
inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted select borrow The backfilled
area adjacent to the water tank floor slab and walls should be backfilled tightly against
the slab with compacted on site soils and graded to divert water away from the tank to
reduce the potential for water ponding around the tank perimeter

At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material old
foundations and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
below areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in
landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas beneath demolished and future tank floors and
footings should be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 12 inch
thick compacted select borrow layer below the tank floor and footings The subgrade
soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
shrinkage cracks are observed after preparation of the subgrade we recommend that
the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks and recompacted Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
25 square feet of subgrade area Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a
woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the
subgrade prior to placement of subsequent lifts of fill
The on site soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of
general fill material Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results it
appears that the in situ soils have high moisture contents Therefore drying or aeration
of the excavated materials may be necessary prior to their use as engineered fills
Aeration to lower the soil moisture and more compaction effort of thinner fill lifts to

3 4 Cut and Fill Slopes
Cut and fill slopes planned for the above project should be constructed at an
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills should be keyed and benched into existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with
the fill placement recommendations presented above Excessive surface water should
be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as
soon as the cut and fill slopes are completed to reduce the potential for significant
erosion of the finished slopes
3 5 Pavements
Asphalt concrete pavements are anticipated for the access road to the northeast
of the water tank and the service road around the perimeter of the new water tank It is
anticipated that the access and service roads will be subjected to light vehicular traffic
Assuming that the pavement subgrade will consist of the silty clay and clayey silt
encountered at the project site and the vehicular traffic will be relatively light the
following pavement section may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes until the
finish subgrade material is verified and laboratory testing of this material is conducted
Asphaltic Concrete Pavements

moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

3 6 Water Lines
We envision that new water lines will be required for this project In general for
support of the water lines we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches
of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes The
initial backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material be
used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site clayey soils as
backfill immediately around water line pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finish grade may consist of the on site soils or select
granular material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction
For utility lines located below the footprint of the water tank the utility trenches
should be backfilled with concrete or lean concrete up to the bottom of the select
borrow layer The select borrow layer recommended below the tank floor should be
placed above the concrete to provide more uniform support for the tank slab The
concrete around the utility lines below the water tank structure should be placed neat
against the sides of the excavation

water away from foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding

Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four exploratory borings to depths of about 23 feet to 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
A one inch Ring Swell test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the
swelling potential of the in situ soils The results of the test are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
Two Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil samples
io evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the
direct shear tests are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
