INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed Hawaii National Bank
Building in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

A preliminary Soil and Foundation Engineering Investigation Report
was issued in August 1981 for the subject site Due to the presence of
buildings and inaccessibility at that time the mauka half of the site was
not explored in 1981 Our current additional exploration was performed
after the remainder of the site became accessible by demolition of the
buildings occupying the site The work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated May 9 1988
This report summarizes the geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analysis based on the information supplied to us the
recent field exploration and the previous exploration results performed in

basement excavation shoring purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a seven story reinforced concrete
structure with a basement level in the mauka northeastern half of the
site The basement level is planned at elevation 5 85 feet
Basement excavation up to about 13 feet is anticipated below the
existing sidewalk elevations The structure will be approximately 128
feet by 187 feet in plan dimension
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the entire project site in order to develop a
more detailed generalized soil rock data set to verify the previous
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in our 1981 preliminary
soil and foundation investigation report for the design of the proposed
building foundations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain which
consists of a thick series of interbedded marine and nonmarine sediments
capping the Koolau Basalt The near subsurface soil structure at the site
consists of a broad elevated coral reef partially covered by nonmarine
alluvium and fill These conditions are considered to be the result of a
complex series of Pleistocene sea level changes The present reef
platform is probably a wave cut terrace formed by wave erosion of an older
reef which originally stood over 25 feet above sea level The widespread
reef structure is known locally as the Waimanalo Reef or the plus 25 foot
reef
Subsoil Conditions
The additional subsurface exploration at the project site was
performed by drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging from
23 5 to 65 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our current and previous exploration the subsoils generally
consist of a 2 foot thick surficial fill layer consisting predominately of
silty sand overlying coral formation About three 3 feet of cinder
sands were encountered below the fill material in the makai half portion
of the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The additional exploration encountered coral formation at shallow
depths underneath the project site and the finding is generally
consistent with our previous exploration performed in August 1981
Therefore we believe the recommendations presented in our 1981 report are
still pertinent to the proposed project
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a thick
coral limestone formation at depths of about 2 to 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The upper coral formation coral algal
limestone is generally hard near the ground surface When approaching
groundwater level the lower coral formation reef detritus grades to
gravelly in nature
Spread footing foundations bearing on the in situ coralline formation
may be used to support the proposed structure An allowable bearing value
up to 8000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the coral A
minimum footing embedment of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finished
grade is recommended
The bearing value is for dead plus live loads any may be increased by
one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Estimated settlements for the footings bearing on the lower reef
detritus material are on the order of less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential For footings located on the upper dense
coral limestone material estimated settlement should be less than
one half of an inch total

Although large cavities were not encountered in the borings solution
cavities are common in coral formation In order to reduce the
possibility of future footing settlements due to potential cavity
collapse cavity probing and grouting were performed at the site Probes
were drilled below column footings to depths equal to two times the
footing widths and at 15 foot centers along continuous wall footings
Major loose zones or cavities were encountered at six 6 locations and
were filled with cement grout Detailed descriptions of the probing and
grouting work were presented in our summary report dated
Excavation
Excavations up to about 13 feet deep will be required for the
proposed basement level Some relatively hard ripping maybe required in
the coral formation
Due to the proximity of the surrounding existing structures and
streets appropriate shoring and bracing system may be required for the
excavation in order to reduce settlement and damage to the adjacent
structures The selection and placement of the appropriate shoring system
to maintain the integrity of the adjacent structures utilities and
streets should be the contractor s sole responsibility
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the excavations
Dewatering
Based on the plans supplied to us the elevation of elevator pit
bottom is about 0 85 feet above Mean Sea Level Thus dewatering should
be anticipated for the elevator pit construction Because of the high
permeability of the coral formation below the water level and the
proximity of the site to the Honolulu harbor dewatering are anticipated
to be very difficult

Dewatering is not anticipated for the proposed basement excavation
which is planned at elevation 5 85 feet
Site Grading
Minimum fill is anticipated for the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and
deleterious materials old foundations and asphaltic concrete pavements
The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing
operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to
near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Select on site material such as the excavated coral and cinder sand
should be stockpiled and reused as fill footing and basement wall
backfill material
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Imported select fill material should be non expansive and have less
than 20 fines passing the 200 sieve

Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine crushed rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane is recommended between the
slab and the cushion layer to reduce the possibility of moisture damage to
the floor coverings
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom
of all thickened edge excavations should be wetted and kept moist until
concrete placement The backfill against the concrete slab should be
adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
Basement Walls
Basement walls up to 13 feet high are planned for the proposed
structure The following guidelines are recommended for the basement
wall design
1 Select granular material such as base course or on site cinder
and coral sand should be used as backfill material behind the
walls to reduce compaction effort and to provide drainage to the
underlying coral formation Impervious material should be
utilized for the top 2 feet of the backfill to reduce
infiltration
2 For the basement walls design the following at rest lateral
soil pressures may be used
Upper medium dense silty sand 55 p s f equivalent
fill material fluid pressure
per foot of depth
Hard coral formation 35 p s f equivalent
fluid pressure
per foot of depth

for foundations bearing on the coral formation A minimum
footing embedment of 3 feet is recommended
4 A passive earth resistance of 550 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for wall footings on coral
5 A base friction factor of 0 65 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings and cavity probes Variations of conditions between the field
data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given
in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately
determined by taping from the structures indicated on the site plan
dated provided to us Elevations of the data points
were informed by the on site project surveyor during cavity probing and
grouting work at the site The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between
field data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
