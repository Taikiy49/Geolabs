INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Mililani Skate Park project at the Mililani District Park in the Mililani area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations derived from
our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses for the proposed project
The recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of slabs on grade
retaining structures light pole foundations and site preparation only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located within the existing Mililani District Park in the
Mililani area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a skate park that will
include about 10 000 square feet of a concrete skateboard facility with obstacles Retaining
structures will also be constructed as part of the skate park project In addition accessible
walkways a lighting system and a potable water system are planned for the proposed
project
Based on the proposed finished grades and the existing topography we anticipate
cuts on the order of about 2 feet deep and fills on the order of about 4 feet thick will be
required

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
GEOLABS iNC
W O 4592 00 Page 4
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age and forms the
majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lava banking against its
eastern flank These banked or ponded lava formed a broad plateau referred to as the
Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava of the
Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense
vesicular basalt rock formation with inter bedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The project site is underlain by residual soils consisting of very stiff clayey silts which
grade to hard with depth Our test results indicated that the near surface clayey silts exhibit
a low potential for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore
to reduce the potential for future structural distress of the lightly loaded slabs on grade we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the slabs be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In consideration of the lateral load and moment demands acting on the proposed light
pole foundations we recommend that drilled shafts embedded in the very stiff to hard silty
clays be used to support the proposed lighting system We recommend that the drilled
shafts consist of 24 inch diameter shafts embedded a minimum of 7 feet below the lowest
adjacent finished grades for the proposed light pole foundations located on level ground
conditions Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this report

Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the new skateboard
facility Based on our field exploration and our laboratory test results the project site is
generally underlain by near surface clayey silts with a low expansion potential when
subjected to moisture fluctuations However to reduce the potential for future distress to the
lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey silts due to
changes in the moisture content we recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557
It should be noted that proper moisture conditioning and preparation of the clayey subgrade
soils is critical to the performance of the foundation and slab system
The underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final
placement of slab concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be prepared again as recommended Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage
may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
We understand that some exterior concrete walkways will be required for the
proposed project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches
of select borrow subbase or non expansive select granular fill material The select borrow
subbase or non expansive select granular fill material should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the
width of the walkways with expansion joints provided at right angle intersections
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with the on site soil and graded to divert water away from
the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs

Retaining Walls
We understand that retaining walls are planned at the new skate park The following
general guidelines may be used for preliminary design of the retaining walls planned at the
site
Retaining Wall Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the project site we believe that
shallow continuous footings may be used to support the retaining walls planned at the
site In general foundations may be designed based on an allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf for footings bearing on the
recompacted on site soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
In general the footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches In addition
foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings oriented parallel to the
direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped footings
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below
a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
The soils encountered at the bottom of footing elevation should be recompacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the footing subgrades the material should be over excavated to the
underlying firm or dense materials The resulting over excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted fill material

Light Pole Foundations
In order to develop the required resistance to the lateral load and moment demands
the proposed light pole structures should be supported by a foundation system consisting of
cast in place concrete drilled shafts We envision that the proposed light poles will be of
galvanized steel and or aluminum construction Based on the information provided we
understand that the structural loads acting at the base of the light poles are as follows
e Vertical load 1 000 pounds
e Lateral load 800 pounds
e Overturning moment 23 000 foot pounds
Based on the anticipated structural loads for the new light poles we recommend that
the light pole foundations consist of cast in place concrete drilled shafts with a minimum
diameter of 24 inches Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsurface
conditions encountered at the site we recommend that drilled shaft foundations with an
embedment length of not less than 7 feet below the design finished grades be used for the
new light pole structures located on level ground conditions If the actual loading of the light
pole is higher than the assumed structural loads the drilled shaft foundation should be
re evaluated for the new foundation demands
The cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support principally from
skin friction between the shafts and the surrounding soils An allowable compressive load
capacity of up to 10 kips per shaft may be used for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by 50 percent for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The anticipated settlements of the light pole foundations due to compression loads are
estimated to be on the order of less than 0 25 inches We believe that the settlements are
elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
GEOLABS iNC
W O 4592 00 Page 11
Lateral loads imposed on the light pole foundations may be resisted by resistance of
the shaft soil system to deflect Lateral load resistance of the drilled shaft is a function of the
stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of
shaft and induced moment in the shaft As indicated above each drilled shaft will be
subjected to a lateral load of 800 pounds with an associated overturning moment of up to
about 23 000 foot pounds at the ground surface Based on our foundation analyses the
maximum moment induced in the drilled shaft is about 23 180 foot pounds acting at a depth
of about 0 5 feet below the top of the shaft Our lateral load analyses indicated that the
estimated deflection at the top of the drilled shaft should be less than 0 25 inches
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Concrete should
be placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for segregation of the aggregates from
the concrete mix If precast concrete pole bases are used the gap between the pole base
and the surrounding soils should be backfilled with concrete or lean concrete A
representative from Geolabs should be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts
during construction to confirm the subsurface conditions and embedment depths

Drainage
The finished grades outside the skate park facility and concrete walkways should be
sloped to shed water away from the slabs and foundations and to reduce the potential for
ponding Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided
Planters next to foundations and slabs should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation and slab recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled against the slabs immediately after setting the concrete to reduce water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should
be maintained to drain all surface run off away from slabs and foundations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
