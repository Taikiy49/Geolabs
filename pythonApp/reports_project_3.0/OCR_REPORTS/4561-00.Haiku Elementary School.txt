In accordance with our proposal dated August 14 2000 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program within the existing
Haiku Elementary School in the area of Haiku on the Island of Maui Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the findings from our field exploration and percolation testing at the proposed
septic tank and leach field site and presents our findings and discussions for the proposed
project These findings and discussions are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Haiku Elementary School in the area of
Haiku on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that septic tanks and leach fields
are planned for the northeast area of the school yard Therefore it was desired to conduct
field percolation tests at the proposed site to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design
of the sewage disposal system Because of the large area of the proposed site six percolation
tests were distributed around the site to determine if there were favorable locations for the
septic tanks and leach fields The proposed site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Further details of the proposed septic tanks and leach fields were not available at the
time this report was prepared Based on our knowledge and experience in the vicinity of the
project site we anticipated that the project site may be underlain by residual soils consisting
of silty clay and clayey silt

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed septic tank and leach field locations and to perform field
percolation tests The subsurface information obtained was used to evaluate the subsoil
infiltration characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertaining to
the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal system The work
was performed in general accordance with our revised fee proposal dated August 14 2000
Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to and
from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of six borings to depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the
existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profile
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a geologist
from our firm
4 Performance of six field percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration
characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertaining
to the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal
system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions
7 Coordination of our work on the project by an engineer from our firm
8 Quality assurance of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a principal engineer from our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 9
Soil samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples were
obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD
standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to
drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
Field percolation tests were performed in the borings in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the subsoil
profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the percolation tests
were performed by drilling 4 0 inch diameter boreholes extending to depths ranging from about
5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface

Approximately 1 to 2 inches of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the boreholes
to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During the test the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum height of 6 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of
the percolation rates were based The results of the individual percolation tests are presented
on Plates 10 through 15 of this report
LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from
the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
surficial soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of this test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths and
presented graphically on Plate 16
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration encountered residual soils consisting of soft to very
hard clayey and silty soils The clayey and silty soils grades with more sand with depth
extending to the maximum depths explored of about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are presented on the Logs
of Borings Plates 4 through 9
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that it is planned to construct septic tanks and leach fields at the project
site Therefore we have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the
preliminary design of the sewage disposal system A percolation test was performed at each
of the six borings drilled The approximate locations of the field percolation tests are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 In addition selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration
were tested in the laboratory to evaluate the moisture and density as an aid in Classifying the
materials encountered

Based on the percolation tests conducted at depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the
existing ground surface the percolation rates at the project site range from 1 6 to greater than
60 minutes per inch Based on the test results we believe that the northwest and central
areas of the project site are suitable for siting the proposed leach fields The percolation tests
were performed in the residual soils consisting of silty clay and clayey silt Based on the
results we recommend a percolation rate of about 45 minutes per inch may be used for
design of the proposed leach fields sited at the northwest and central areas of the project site
Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system
should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
In addition based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department of
absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater level
As previously mentioned groundwater was not encountered in our borings at the time of our
field exploration
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter
report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by Fujita Associates
Inc on The elevations of the borings were estimated from the contour lines and
spot elevations shown on the topographic survey map prepared by R T Tanaka Engineers
Inc on The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
