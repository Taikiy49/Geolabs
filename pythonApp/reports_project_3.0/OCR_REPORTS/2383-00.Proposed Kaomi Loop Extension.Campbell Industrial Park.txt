INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the the project site grading
and road construction of the proposed Kaomi Loop Extension in Campbell
Industrial Park in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2383 00 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
shallow subsurface conditions along the proposed road extension
alignment in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of engineering design recommendations pertinent to the
design and construction of the proposed road
The work was done generally in accordance with our fee proposal of
The scope of our work included the excavation and sampling of five
5 test pits along the proposed alignment and detailed geotechnical
engineering reconnaissance of the existing conditions at the project
site Also included were laboratory testing engineering evaluation of
the field and laboratory data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the southwestern part of Campbell
Industrial Park in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The proposed road alignment extends from the end of the pavement at
Kaomi Loop near the existing Hawaiian Cement plant and curves in a
northerly direction to terminate near the existing Brewer Chemical
facility
There is an existing gravel road to Brewer Chemical which
coincides with about half of the proposed right of way Along the
sides of this existing gravel road there are windrows of demolition
debris rubble and other miscellaneous materials

Most of the proposed right of way is vegetated with a heavy growth
of and other scrub brush In the northern
portion of the proposed alignment coral algal limestone is exposed
below the underbrush and there are many cavities or voids in the coral
open to the existing ground surface
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the proposed right of way for
the construction of a roadway to provide access for an industrial
subdivision Exact details of the proposed road grading were not
available at the time of our exploration however we anticipate that
the grading will consist of the placement of about two 2 to four 4
feet of fill In addition the new road will require the construction
of a milti barreled pipe culvert to cross an existing drainage channel
This report has been prepared specifically for the proposed road
The discussions and recommendations presented herein are presented for
the design and construction of the road only and should not be
considered for any other project or development
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas
from two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is
located adjacent to the southern flank of the older volcano Waianae
After the cessation of volcanic activity the island began to
subside The flanks of the subsiding domes formed a salient for the
growth of fringing reefs built by corals and calcareous alga
During the Pleistocene Epoch or Ice Age global sea levels
fluctuated in response to the advance and retreat of the continental
glaciers As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
to fill the ocean basins so the sea fell to stands below the current
level As the glaciers melted and retreated more water was available
so the sea rose to levels often exceeding the current level
During the high stands of the sea sediments and marine deposits
were laid down at elevations above current sea level The coral algal
reef limestones encountered at the project site were believed to have
been deposited during the MSL stand of the sea and
subsequently planed to their current elevation during the Waimanalo
stand
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the
excavation of five 5 test pits to depths ranging between 0 5 and 3 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and by geotechnical engineering
reconnaissance of the proposed road right of way The conditions
encountered by our exploration may be summarized as three generalized
types
Miscellaneous Fill
This condition was encountered in the southerly portion of the
proposed right of way and consists of man made fill intermixed
with demolition debris and other miscellaneous materials This
fill appears to be in a medium dense state with open voids between
the larger fragments of debris and rubble
Lowlying Topsoil Humus
This condition was encountered in a lowlying area ranging from
about 300 to about 625 feet northerly of the existing drainage
channel The area appears to be a broad shallow drainageway
traversing the road right of way and is vegetated by a low dense
cover of picklewort

The surface soil in this area is about one 1 foot of a dark gray
silty sand organic silt that appears to be an accumulation of
topsoil or humus in the broad drainage way Below this thin
surface soil our exploration encountered coral formation The
coral formation was of relatively low hardness and was in a wet
condition

This condition was encountered predominantly in the northern half
of the proposed right of way and consisted of coral algal
limestone exposed at the surface or covered with a very thin 1
foot soil cover The coral encountered was generally of high
hardness however a significant number of cavities or voids open
to the ground surface were observed In some areas the open
voids exceeded about twenty five percent of the net surface area
These surface cavities are indicative of a karstic terrain which
is the result of subaerial weathering of carbonate rocks such as
coral Rainwater is slightly acidic and chemically attacks the
carbonate material causing solution cavities within the rock
matrix When the roof of such a cavity becomes too thin it
collapses causing an open surface cavity or sinkhole
Discussions with personnel from Brewer Chemical indicate that such
collapses have occurred in the existing gravel road A small
collapse was observed along the edge of the gravel road near the
Brewer facility

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings indicate that the development of the proposed road
extension is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view
However special earthwork design and construction will be required for
this project
The coral algal limestone formation encountered at shallow depths
below the surface of the project site should provide adequate bearing
for the proposed road extension culvert pipes and related site work
Due to the observed existence of cavities within the coral formation
we recommend that a program of cavity detection and subsequent filling
be conducted during project construction Filling of large cavities
if encountered near finish grade below the proposed road embankment
should be considered to provide continued support for the new road
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
roadway and right of way grading limits should be cleared of all
vegetation miscellaneous fill rubble debris and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during
the clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing fill be removed to
natural coral formation within the project grading limits If the
stripped material does not contain organics rubbish or other
deleterious materials and if it is generally less than 12 inches in
maximm size it may be re used as general fill material Otherwise
it should be wasted off site

Cavities
The field exploration encountered coral algal limestone formation at
shallow depths below the project site In addition our exploration
encountered a significant number of surface voids Previous experience
indicates that such cavities are commonly found in coral formations in the
Ewa area Experience also indicates that there are usually subsurface
voids in such formations
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential large void situated
below the road subgrade causing settlement of the pavement we recommend
that the following program be implemented
1 After the initial clearing and grubbing of the right of way
existing open voids should be cleaned of all organic matter and
loose rubble The open void should then be backfilled with
compacted granular material such as crushed coral
2 When the existing voids have been filled and the site leveled we
believe geophysical surveys such as locally available ground
penetrating radar should be used to detect anomalies which could
be potential voids The anomalies could then be further probed
with selective track drilling and if they are confirmed to be
actual voids and depending on the depths below the pavement
surface should either be backfilled with portland cement sand
grout if the volume is small or the large voids or cavities be
ripped open and backfilled with compacted granular coral fill
material The sand and cement grout should be injected at low to
moderate pressures in an upstage fashion i e from the bottom
of the probe hole to the top If the grout volumes exceeds
one half of a cubic yard grouting initial should be stopped and
continued later in similar incremental grout placement

Culvert Structure
It is our understanding that a new multi barreled pipe culvert
structure is needed to cross the existing drainage channel Our
exploration at this location indicates that there is about one 1 foot of
soft wet sediment and miscellaneous material in the bottom of the
channel This sediment and miscellaneous material should be first removed
down to the underlying coral formation prior to bedding material
placement
The culvert structure should be constructed either by filling and
then excavating for the pipe installation or backfill fill be placed
around the installed pipes first with granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel up to one 1 foot above the top of the culvert pipes Provision
should be made to divert any flows occurring in the channel and away from
the newly installed pipes during the construction period The culvert
pipes should be bedded in a 6 inch thick granular cushion of 3B Fine
gravel
For the design of culvert wing walls an allowable bearing capacity
of 3 000 p s f may be utilized To provide uniform support a minimum of
six 6 inches of granular bedding material should be placed under the
foundations
The backfill around the pipe structure should consist of self
compacting granular materials such as 3B Fine gravel or screened coral
above the top of pipes Subsequent fill backfill should be placed in nine
9 inch loose horizontal lifts and compacted to 90 maximm dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 To reduce the amount of compactive effort
required and to allow drainage of water from behind the wing walls a
well graded granular material such as screened coral fragments and other
granular material such as select borrow or base course is recommended

Excavation
It is our opinion that excavation for infrastructure installation in
the in situ coral formation encountered at the site will require hard
excavation such as ripping and or hoerams We anticipate that the
material will have a volume increase on the order of 5 to 10 per cent when
excavated and reused as fill material The excavated material may require
some processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater than
twelve 12 inches in maximm diameter prior to its use as fill
material The material will require further processing to removal or
reduce the fragments to less than three 3 inches in size prior to its
being used as backfill
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing should
be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work However
excavations in coral formation will most likely stand unsupported for
temporary construction purposes

The recommended section considers industrial commercial traffic and

IV Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved select
import material Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent
organic matter and other deleterious material and shall not contain
rocks or lumps in excess of six 6 inches in diameter
Representative samples of the materials to be used as compacted fill
shall be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the
proposed materials shall be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least
seven working days prior to its intended jobsite delivery
Vv Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve 12 inches in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer

Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than six 6 inches in diameter
may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in any one
area and that there is sufficient fine grained material to fill
the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than six 6 inches in diameter shall be removed
from the fill unless specifically approved for placement in the
fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around then
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and
in no case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site

4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the

The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either over building the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained

The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted access

No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous

The Contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it

VI Unforeseen Subsurface Conditions
If any conditions not described in the contract documents such
as perched water seepage lenticular or confined strata of a
potentially adverse nature are encountered during grading these
conditions shall be immediately brought to the attention of the
Project Engineers so that supplemental recommendations may be
made to treat these problems
Should excavations encounter loose or unstable conditions the
Contractor shall notify the Geotechnical Engineer and Project
Civil Engineer immediately so that supplemental recommendations
can be given
