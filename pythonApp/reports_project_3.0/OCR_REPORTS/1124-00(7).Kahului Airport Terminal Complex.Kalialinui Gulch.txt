INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our subsurface investigation
for the above project conducted in accordance with the scope of work
outlined in our proposal dated May 3 1983 Our investigation
included drilling 3 borings and excavating 9 test pits along the
proposed realignment performing laboratory tests and evaluating site
conditions for the proposed construction The general area of the
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and design recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project will combine realignment of the Kalialinu Gulch and
the construction of a new Hemaloa Street The proposed realignment of
the Kalialinui Gulch will extend from the existing culvert at
Haleakala Highway to about 3500 feet downstream The majority of the
existing gulch will be filled A new drain channel will be
constructed with a concrete lined ditch about 40 feet wide at the
bottom and 10 feet deep with 2 1 H V side slopes The proposed
gulch realignment is shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2

It is our understanding that the new Hemaloa Street will be
raised approximately 15 feet above the existing grade to tie into the
new terminal roadway parking and service court areas
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located to the west of the existing Kahului
Airport and extends from the intersection of Hemaloa Street and
Haleakala Highway toward the northwest for a distance of approximately
3500 feet The area is a shallow drainageway with banks sloping at
unlined section The existing drainageway was cleared of vegetation
just prior to our field exploration
The Kahului side of the existing Kalialinui Gulch is heavily
overgrown with dense vegetation consisting of haole koa and kiawe
trees with tall grasses It was also noted during our investigation
that an abandoned asphalt concrete road is located near of
the proposed gulch alignment
Large piles of debris were observed on the ground surface along
the top of the bank on the Kahului side of the gulch from about Sta
18 00 to 24 00
In the heavily vegetated areas along the proposed gulch
alignment the topsoil layer which is approximately 1 foot thick
contained a large amount of organic matter

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geological setting of the site has been previously described
in our reports prepared for Airside and Landside improvements of
the Kahului Aiport Terminal Complex Expansion projects dated June 24
1982 and March 11 1983 respectively
The general vicinity of the site is mantled by thick andesitic
basalt rocks of the Kula volcanic series that overlies older alluvial
soils and Honomanu Basalts However the gulch appears to be situated
along an opening between the uppermost andesitic flows
Subsurf ace Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 3 borings and excavating nine 9 test pits at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Samples were obtained for laboratory identification shear
strength consolidation CBR and Proctor tests
The logs of borings and test pits together with the results of
our laboratory tests are presented on the appendices of this report

Soil Conditions
Based on our borings and test pit findings we believe that the
subsurface conditions within the close vicinity of the existing gulch
consist of about 5 feet of sandy silt fill and 15 to 20 feet of brown
silt of alluvium origin containing some fine sand
Generally the brown silt is stiff to very stiff and may be
slightly cemented at the surface zone The thick sandy silt layer
grades to more sandy with depth and also as the deposit approaches
the stream mouth at the coral sand beach of Kahului Bay The sandy
silt becomes soft to medium stiff near the water level at about 10 to
12 feet below the existing gulch bottom or at about Elevation 5
feet The soft to medium stiff zone extends down for about 10 more
feet and is then underlain by medium dense sand or gravel deposits
which probably represent older beach or near shore deposits These
deposits are then underlain by weathered basalt about 25 feet below
the bottom of the gulch
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the realigning of Kalialinui Gulch
will involve a substantial amount of excavation For the Hemaloa
Street improvement a large quantity of fill will be required Since
both the Kalialinui Gulch realignment and the new Hemaloa Street
projects will be combined in one contract it appears that the new
Hemaloa Street subgrade soil will probably consist of fill materials
generated from the gulch realignment excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 5
Clearing Stripping Grubbing
The overgrown areas on the banks of the stream will need to be cleared
and grubbed of all unsuitable deleterious materials When
encountered the top 12 inches of organic topsoil should be stripped
and not be allowed to remain under the new fill or incorporated into
the fill material
As a result of recent clearing work done prior to our field
exploration the bottom of the existing gulch was dry and relatively
free of organic matter at the time of our exploration However
conditions may differ at the time of construction Therefore
provisions to strip or remove unsuitable materials from the existing
gulch should be made in the specifications
The existing Hemaloa Street pavement should be removed if situated
within 2 feet of the future finished roadway grade
Fill Construction
Based on our findings the excavated material from the
realignment of the Kalialinui Gulch will be suitable for fill
However because of the relatively low CBR values of 7 to 14 we
believe that these fill materials would require thicker roadway
pavement sections than those anticipated in our Airside or
Landside Improvement projects
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
Therefore the fill materials with CBR values less than 15 should
be kept at least 2 feet below the finished grade of roadways The
balance of the fill material up to the design subgrade of the roadway
should consist of a higher quality borrow material that could produce
a minimum laboratory CBR value of 15
Any organic compressible or other deleterious materials if
encountered in the excavation should be disposed of off site and not
be incorporated into the fill material Pavement and concrete blocks
when broken up into sizes 8 inches or smaller may be used in the
engineered fill 3 feet or more below the finished grade
All fill materials should be in a proper moisture content prior
to compaction Our laboratory tests show that if the gulch excavated
material is placed too dry for compaction it will create a higher
expansion potential Therefore the material should be moistened or
dried as appropriate to a moisture content within 3 percentage points
of the optimum moisture at the time of compaction This is important
to minimize post construction soil expansion
All fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness Fill should be compacted to a minimun
of 90 of maximum dry density as determined by the ASTM D 1557 78 test
procedure The fill within 2 feet of the roadway pavement if
appropriate should be placed in 6 inch lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 95
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 7
The contractor should route his hauling equipment both loaded
and empty so that the travel path is distributed evenly over the fill
area as much as possible Travel over a single haul route may result
in distressing the fill material Should distress in the form of
pumping occur the defective area should be removed and repaired with
backfill conforming to the above recommended densities
Slab On Grade Construction
Our laboratory CBR and swell test results indicated that the
material has a low expansion potential upon wetting To minimize post
construction swelling potential we recommend that the subgrade
material under concrete slab or its subbase be pre wetted for at least
2 days prior to placing of concrete This point also applies to the
channel lining concrete slabs although most of the channel bottom slab
may rest on the excavated natural soil
The thickened edge slab considerations as presented in our report
for Borrow Site dated will also apply for this type of
material as fill under on grade slabs
Keying and Benching

vertical should be keyed and benched properly Keying and
benching should consist of excavating horizontal benches into the
Slope A typical section showing keying and benching into an
existing slope for this project is shown on Plate 3

Slope Stability
The strength of the soft to medium stiff alluvial sandy silt
under Hemaloa Street and the existing gulch will probably increase
under the weight of the new fill
The maximum height of the fi11 will be about 15 feet When it is
combined with the 10 foot excavation for the Kalialinui Gulch it will
result in a net slope height of about 25 feet Our slope stability
evaluation indicates that the fill slope will be stable having a
factor of safety of about 2 without earthquake condition Under a
Zone III earthquake condition using an estimated 0 15g horizontal
acceleration in our analysis the factor of safety will be reduced to
1 5
Liquefaction Potential
Because of the soft and loose deposits of sandy silt at or below
the groundwater level we have performed a preliminary evaluation of
liquefaction potential which indicated that liquefaction of the soil
could occur at the site during a very strong earthquake such as a
Richter Scale Magnitude 7 5 A more detailed study to further
evaluate the liquefaction potential of the site should first entail a
seismicity study and a risk analysis If the results warrant a
further study additional soil samples should be taken and a dynamic
laboratory testing should be made along with a computerized analysis
of liquefaction potential Such a study is beyond our scope of work

Settlement
The proposed thickness of the new fill will vary from 2 to 15
feet The fill will cause some settlement due to the consolidation of
the underlying soft alluvial silt and loose dune sand deposits
previously encountered in borings drilled under Hemaloa Street in the
Airside improvement portion of the project The estimated
settlements are relatively moderate on the order of 2 to 5 inches
under 5 to 15 feet of fill These settlements should occur rapidly
probably within 3 months after the application of the total fill
weight Remaining long term settlements after construction should be
less than one half of an inch Therefore the mass grading should be
done as soon as possible and at least 3 months prior to the
construction of any structures which may be sensitive to settlement
It is probable that the distribution and thickness of soft silt
and loose sand may vary along the Kalialinui gulch channel and
differential settlement may be significant Therefore it is
advisable that existing utility lines such as sewer water fuel or
drain lines under thick fill in this area should be removed and
re routed as deemed necessary to avoid rupture due to the settlement
of the new fill
Because of the abovementioned potential settlements routing of
water or sewer mains along the new Hemaloa Street or construction of
new lines in the adjacent areas should be delayed as much as possible
and preferably at least 3 months after all fill placement is completed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 10
The actual ground settlement should be verified by monitoring the
settlement observation markers Survey readings of the marker
elevations should be done at least once every two weeks Flexible
joints and shorter sections of pipe should be used for sewer or water
mains along the filled street and between Hemaloa Street and the area
underlain by basalt rock
If any abutting fills of Significant size are to be placed at a
different time differential settlements should be anticipated Such
a situation exists where the Hemaloa Street fill will be placed ahead
of the terminal and parking lot area fills The effect of
differential settlements will depend on the thickness of the fill
magnitude of settlement depth and the thickness of the compressible
layer
For the Hemaloa Street improvements we believe that the
embankment should be overfilled The top of the new fill should
extend at least 25 feet toward the terminal area from the critical
elements of the Hemaloa Street improvements to minimize potential
damage due future settlements

W O Page 11
For fill thickness of 4 feet or less the total and differential
settlements will be small perhaps about one inch between abutting
fills placed in different time Therefore overfilling may not be
necessary
Pavement Design
The laboratory CBR test results conducted in this investigation
indicate that the excavated materials from the gulch would provide a
subgrade support of CBR 7 or R value of 35 for pavement design
This is lower than CBR 15 or R value of 51 previously assumed for
roadway pavement design
It is our understanding that a pavement section consisting of 2 5
inches of asphalt concrete and 6 5 inches of asphalt treated base for
the new Hemaloa Street have been selected by your office In
addition it is our understanding that the completed pavement may be
subject to construction traffic to be generated by the subsequent
phases of the terminal expansion project
Because of the lower subgrade support and the increased traffic
volume as discussed above we recommend that the borrow materials from
the gulch excavation be kept at least 2 feet below the finished grade
of the Hemaloa Street pavement The balance of the fill up to
subgrade should consist of a higher quality borrow material As
indicated above the balance of the fill to subgrade for Hemaloa
Street should consist of borrow fill with a minimum CBR value of at
least 15

Filter Rock
The on site sandy silt is relatively free of clay as binder and
is readily erodible Therefore adequate filter protection should be
provided particularly behind the weepholes on the concrete lining or
retaining walls and also behind joints on sloped concrete lining The
following approaches are recommended
A filter fabric should be placed between the silt soil and
pockets of crushed drain rock This will prevent migration of
silt through the voids between the crushed drain rock particles
The filter fabric may consist of Poly Filter X having an
equivalent U S Standard Sieve opening of No 40 to 100 The
fabric should extend at least 12 inches beyond the edge of the
crushed rock pockets
Alternatively a graded protective filter material consisting of
No 4 Fine crushed rock aggregates may be used to protect the
on site soil material The No 4 Fine rock should be at least 8
inches thick Because the weephole openings are typically 3 to 4
inches coarse rock fragments of at least the size of the
weephole should be used immediately behind the weephole to
further protect the No 4 Fine material

Plans and specifications for the gulch realignment and road
improvements should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing engineered fill
compaction and subgrade preparation be observed by a qualified soils
engineer during the project construction This is to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation If conditions
encountered are different from those presented in this report
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
