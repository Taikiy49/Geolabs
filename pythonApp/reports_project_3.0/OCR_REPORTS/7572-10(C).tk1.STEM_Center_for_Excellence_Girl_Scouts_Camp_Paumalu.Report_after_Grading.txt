This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the earthwork operations for the STEM Center for Excellence project at Camp 
Paumalu from April 16, 2019 through March 18, 2020. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project site is located at the existing Girl Scouts of Hawaii at Camp Paumalu in 
the Haleiwa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. Camp Paumalu is an approximate 
135-acre site, situated roughly 2 miles to the southeast of Sunset Beach, and is accessed
via Comsat Road off Kamehameha Highway.  The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.
The project will involve improvements to the existing facility with the construction of 
a new lodge and supporting infrastructure. The Lodge at Camp Paumalu will serve as a 
STEM Center for Excellence, providing Girl Scouts opportunities to explore STEM fields. 
The ADA accessible Lodge will be a two-story structure accommodating up to 200 guests 
at a time.  In addition, the Lodge will be connected to a single-story New Luggage and Cart 
Staging structure by a covered walkway. A bolted steel water tank, bus loading and

Hensel Phelps performed the site grading operations, footing subgrade preparation, 
pavement construction, and utility line installation. The site grading work consisted of 
subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, pavement 
construction, and utility trench excavation and backfill. 
Following the demolition and clearing operations, the over-excavated subgrades 
and areas designated to receive fills were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and recompacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction, as appropriate.  Fill and backfill materials 
required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the 
optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.  The 
compaction requirement was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction 
for areas subjected to vehicular traffic.  Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry 
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil 
established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content 
(percentage by weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 94 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.4 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the above project 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in 
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our 
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report, dated 
February 5, 2019, and subsequent recommendations.
