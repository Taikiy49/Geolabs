Our rainfall summary is based on a review of the available rainfall data and monthly
precipitation summaries prepared by the National Weather Service Forecast Office

experienced below normal rainfall averaging about 20 to 60 percent of normal The
exception was March 2009 where 5 67 inches of rain was recorded which comprises
about 118 percent of normal rainfall
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels In addition following extended periods of below normal
precipitation earth movements generally decrease slightly
Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the fifteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in the borings at the project site A list of the piezometer installations
for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of All Instrument

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
General reconnaissance of the Kuahea Street Area Movement study area was
conducted on to record current observations pertaining to the existing site
conditions In general visible ground surface deformation and disturbance to fixed
structures observed by Geolabs has shown little visible change with the exception of a few
locations within the study area
Areas that appear to be experiencing slow ongoing surface deformation include
Kuahea Street Area A between Helo and Kuahea Place the Waiomao Landslide head
and Waiomao Road within the Waiomao Landslide footprint These areas show some
visible evidence of compounding ground heave and or subsidence within the paved
roadway areas Surface groundwater seepages were not observed within the study area
at the time of our reconnaissance
Kuahea Street Area A between Helo Place and Kuahea Place
The primary area of ground surface deformation within Kuahea Street Area A was
observed to extend between about 2326 Kuahea Street and about
2221 Kuahea Place Based on our observations the temporary pavement repairs
patches on Kuahea Street are in generally fair condition but show recent
increasing signs of stress as evidenced by widening crack apertures and some
vertical offsets at the joints between adjacent concrete pavement slabs By visual
observation the undulatory condition of the existing concrete pavement and
deflections in the street curbing on Kuahea Street appears to be gradually
increasing in magnitude although no significant pavement surface rupture or
dangerous pavement irregularity was observed A new area of concrete pavement
distress was observed fronting the curbing at the 2307 Kuahea Street address The
distress is caused by an area of pavement movement and may be the beginning of
a developing compression feature at an existing transverse pavement joint within
the roadway The slight buckle trends perpendicular to the street curb for a length
of about 3 to 4 feet Visible pavement deformation in this lower section of Area A
on Kuahea Street had not been encountered previously
The street deformation repairs previously conducted between 2293 and
2311 Waiomao Road at the toe of Area A remain in generally good to fair
condition with some minor undulations in the pavement felt across the repair area
We continue to observe recent spray paint marks on the pavement suggesting
recurring or ongoing underground utility work within the roadway in the vicinity of
the previous pavement repair area

Kuahea Street Waiomao Landslide Head
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide indicates that the roadway pavement and northern road
shoulder continue to experience settlement of the ground surfaces The ground
settlements have produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that
traverse the roadway Vehicular ride across the deformation zone is very rough and
uneven Previous open cracks in the road center surrounding the area of settlement
have been patched Settlement within the southern half of the paved roadway has
produced regions that may be conducive to the ponding of surface rainwater It
appears that some uplift may be occurring along the southern roadside along the
interface between the asphalt pavement and concrete gutter The uplift may be
contributing to the ponding of water along the southern half of the roadway House
reconstruction work was observed at 2402 Kuahea Street immediately adjacent to
the eastern boundary of the Waiomao Landslide
The open landscaped slopes on the northern side of Kuahea Street continue to
show minimal visible evidence of earth movement or ground surface deformation
Waiomao Road Central Waiomao Landslide
Based on our visual observation of the graded and landscaped sloping ground
surfaces no new overt changes in the ground surface deformation were observed
The open landscaped areas on both sides of Waiomao Road show minimal visible
surface evidence of earth movement In general Waiomao Road appears to be
gradually settling at the center of the landslide forming a shallow and gradual dip in
the pavement surface In addition the pavement bumps in Waiomao Road at the
margins of the landslide appear to be gradually becoming more pronounced and
the vehicular ride across the deformation margins is somewhat rough and irregular
Kipona Place
Based on our observations the existing conditions along Kipona Place at the toe of
the Waiomao Landslide appear to be generally static with respect to new pavement
deformation and visible signs of earth movement on the slope abutting the
roadway
