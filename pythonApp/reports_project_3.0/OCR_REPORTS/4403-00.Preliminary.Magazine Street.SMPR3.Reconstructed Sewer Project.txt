PRELIMINARY GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
MAGAZINE STREET SMPR3 RECONSTRUCTED SEWER PROJECT
Dear Mr Nikou
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have conducted
a preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration for the Magazine Street SMPR3
Reconstructed Sewer project This letter report summarizes our work on the project to support
the preparation of the Preliminary Engineering Report for the proposed Reconstructed Sewer
project along Magazine Street on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Magazine Street sewer improvement project is located in the Punchbowl
area along Magazine Street Spencer Street Thurston Avenue Victoria Street and Wilder
Avenue It is our understanding that the existing 6 inch vitrified clay pipe VCP sewer line is
inadequate and the installation of an 8 inch replacement sewer line is being contemplated
The existing 6 inch VCP sewer line consist of inverts ranging from 2 5 to 9 feet below
the existing ground surface
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age
After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys into
the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and
tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located on the southern flank of Punchbowl Hill The Punchbow Hill
Puowaiana is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the end of a spur of the
Koolau Range The tuff is mostly brown palagonitized vitric ash and lapilli with scattered
fragments of coral limestone and Koolau basalt

During the Pliestocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation As the galciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
glaciers meited and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The process of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea level
fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was raised such that
sediments accumulated at higher elevations
EXISTING SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located south of Punchbowl Crater in the Makiki area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located along Magazine Street Spencer Street Thurston
Avenue Victoria Street and Wilder Avenue as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The project site is situated in a residential area Based on the topographic survey
provided by Earth Tech the elevations of the existing ground surface range from 90 to
194 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of about 20 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring No 1 was drilled on Thurston Avenue near the Tree Haven Apartment building
The boring generally encountered the pavement section approximately 8 inches in thickness
consisting of medium dense silty gravel with sand overlain by 4 inches of asphalt concrete
The pavement was underlain by cinders consisting of medium dense to dense sand with some
silt to the maximum drill depth of about 21 5 feet A 5 foot layer of weathered tuff consisting
of stiff silty clay was encountered at a depth of about 5 feet
Boring No 2 was drilled on Spencer Street near the Victoria Mansion Apartment
building The boring generally encountered the pavement section approximately 10 inches in
thickness consisting of dense silty gravel with sand overlain by 2 inches of asphalt concrete
Below the pavement a dense volcanic tuff formation approximately 9 feet in thickness was
encountered followed by cinders consisting of medium dense sand with traces of silt to the
maximum drill depth of 20 5 feet
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the
Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected
soil samples are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our understanding that the existing 6 inch VCP sewer line will be replaced with a
new 8 inch sewer line Based on existing utility plans the 6 inch sewer line is located within
the existing roadways and sidewalk areas with pipe inverts ranging from 2 5 to 9 feet below
the existing ground surface Numerous sewer laterals are connected to the 6 inch sewer line
Based on our field exploration medium dense to dense cinders stiff weathered tuff
and dense voicanic tuff were encountered at the invert elevations
Numerous cracks root intrusions and plugged laterals were observed in the video
survey performed on the existing 6 inch sewer line by the City and County of Honolulu
Department of Wastewater Management in August and October of 1997
Installation of the new pipe using trenchless methods and conventional open trench
method were evaluated for the project Based on our evaluation we believe that pipe bursting
in conjunction with open trench method is a viable option for the installation of the new 8 inch
sewer line Detailed discussions of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Trenchless Methods
Two categories of trenchless methods are available rehabilitation and conventional
trenchless Trenchless rehabilitation consists of reusing the existing pipe or pipe alignment
and includes pipe bursting slip lining and Cured In Place Pipe CIPP methods Since the
inside diameter of the pipe will be reduced using slip lining or CIPP methods these methods
were not considered Conventional trenchless methods consists of installing a replacement
pipe generally parallel to the existing pipe and includes horizontal directional drilling and
microtunneling
Pipe Bursting
Pipe bursting consists of winching a pneumatic powered bursting tool through an old
section of pipe breaking and cracking the old pipe as it progresses A new pipe
section is pulled into place behind the bursting tool replacing the old pipe section High
Density Polyethylene HDPE pipe is typically used with pipe bursting
Conventional open trench method was used for the installation of the existing 6 inch
sewer line The open trench method consists of excavating a trench wide enough for
the sewer line installation placing the sewer pipe and backfilling the open trench with
backfill material During pipe bursting the soil surrounding the bursting tooi will be
compressed In a backfilled trench compaction of the backfill material will generally
occur
We do not anticipate difficulty in bursting of the existing vitrified clay pipe with the
bursting tool

Damage to existing utilities in close proximity of the bursting tool may occur for existing
utilities generally within 3 feet of the bursting tool Based on the existing utility plan
some utility lines are within this zone In these areas the utility crossings may be
exposed and the crossing utility moved further away from the bursting tool during the
pipe installation to reduce the potential for damage
For conditions where the bursting tool is close to the existing ground surface surface
heave may occur We anticipate that a 10 inch HDPE pipe will be required to obtain
an inside diameter of at ieast 8 inches For this HDPE pipe size approximately 4 feet
of ground cover should be provided to reduce the potential for ground heave Based
on the utility plans it appears that the existing pipe along part of Spencer Street near
Wilder Avenue and along Wilder Avenue have less than 4 feet of ground cover
Excavation pits will be required to connect the laterals with the main sewer line
Electrofusion methods may be used to connect the HDPE pipes at the joints Since the
sewer line will probably need to keep active a bypassing system will probably be
required
In summary we believe that pipe bursting is a viable option for the proposed sewer line
rehabilitation provided that the concerns above are addressed
Horizontal Directional Drilling
This trenchless steerable installation method uses a multi axis drilling machine to bore
a small pilot hole The pilot hole is then reamed to a larger size to accommodate the
new pipe The new pipe is pulled back into the reamed hole by the drilling machine
It is generally more difficult to maintain an accurate alignment with horizontal directional
drilling as compared to microtunneling or pipe bursting Since the sewer line is a
gravity type sewer line accurate alignment and grade are critical
It is our understanding that installation of the new 8 inch sewer line using the existing
6 inch sewer line as a pilot hole is being considered Because the diameter of the drill
rods are much smaller than the inside diameter of the existing 6 inch sewer line proper
grade will be difficult to maintain along the alignment It would be difficult to maintain
the center of the drill rod at the center of the existing sewer pipe during the drilling
operation In addition significant loss of drilling fluids would occur during the drilling
operation Another consideration in the above method is that an entry pit of significant
size will be required to accommodate the drilling machine required for the size of pipe
being considered or large excavation pits will be required to tie in the installed sewer
line to the manhole structure
Because of the above reasons we believe that installation of the new 8 inch sewer line
using horizontal directional drilling may not be a viable option

Microtunneling
Microtunneling is an installation method where a pipe is jacked behind a remotely
controlled steerable laser guided tunnel boring machine Entry and exit pits will be
required These shallow pits are generally rectangular in shape to accommodate the
length about three to four tunnel diameters and width about one tunnel diameter of
the tunnel boring machine the jacking frame reaction block and pipe sections to be
jacked into place During the microtunneling process the excavated material is
removed by using a mixture of water and slurry pumped through a pipe for disposal
balanced screw auger or a screw conveyor system
Microtunneling is performed with specialized equipment that is very costly and is
generally limited to the smallest pipe size of 18 inches
In microtunneling selecting the right machine cutter head for the ground condition is
critical Different types of cutter heads are used for various types of soil rock Based
on our field exploration medium dense to dense cinders stiff clayey silt weathered
tuff and dense volcanic tuff were encountered at the invert elevations We anticipate
that the cutter head will bore through different types of soil rock in the same run
creating a mixed face condition which may impede tunneling Should slurry be used
in the microtunneling work significant loss of slurry may occur within the cinder deposit
encountered at the site
Based on the above we believe that microtunneling is not a viable option for this
project
Open Trench Method
Conventional open trench method may be used for the installation of the replacement
pipe on a new alignment Our borings did not encounter groundwater during our field
exploration therefore dewatering is not anticipated We anticipate that cinder sands will be
encountered during trench excavation Because the sands tend to ravel during excavation and
from vehicular traffic vibration temporary shoring will be required for sewer line installation
Should pipe bursting be used installation of the new pipe using open trench method
may be required in certain areas in conjunction with pipe bursting as described above
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based upon
visual observations made during our field exploration and laboratory testing of the soil samples
recovered Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the sampling points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc should be contacted to
re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
