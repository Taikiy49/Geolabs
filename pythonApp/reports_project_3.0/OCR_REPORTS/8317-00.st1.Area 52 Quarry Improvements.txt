In accordance with your request, we provided permeability testing services in 
support of the Area 52 AC Plant Expansion project in Waikoloa on the Island of Hawaii. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
We understand that a new asphaltic concrete plant expansion is planned at the 
existing Area 52 quarry in the Waikoloa area in the District of South Kohala on the Island 
of Hawaii. Based on the information provided, the performance of two subsurface 
permeability tests was desired to assist in the design of the stormwater disposal system. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Two constant head permeability tests, designated as P-1 and P-2, were conducted 
in the excavated test pits to provide data for the design of the stormwater disposal system 
planned at the project site. The permeability tests were performed at a depth of about 6 feet 
below the existing ground surface. The locations of the permeability tests conducted are 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.  The permeability test results are presented in Appendix A. 
Based on our field permeability tests, the subsurface materials generally have 
coefficient of permeability (k) of 0.27 and 1.1 feet per minute at P-1 and P-2 locations tested. 
The coefficient of permeability values roughly corresponds to infiltration rates of about 
0.0046 and 0.018 cubic feet per second per square foot (cfs/ft2) at P-1 and P-2, respectively.

Permeability Tests 
We conducted two permeability tests in excavated test pits using the constant head 
test method. In general, the test pits were about 12 feet long, about 6 to 8 feet wide and 
extended to a depth of about 6 feet below the ground level at the time of the tests. 
The constant head tests were performed by pumping water into the boreholes until 
the water surface stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a sufficient period of time. 
The water height and the rate of pumping were then measured to provide data upon which 
to base calculation of the subsurface permeability. Results of the permeability tests 
conducted in the excavated test pits are presented on Plates A-1 and A-2.
