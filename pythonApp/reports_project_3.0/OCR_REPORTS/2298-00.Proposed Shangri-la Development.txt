SUMMARY
The field exploration disclosed generally
competent foundation bearing soils at the project
site
Shallow spread footing foundations can be used for
structures located on relatively level areas
Where structures are situated over steeper sloping
ground deeper footing embedment or drilled pier
type foundations are recommended
The near surface site soils are moderately
expansive and will require special procedures for
slab on grade construction
Conventional site grading operations can be used
for the cluster project site development except
that harder rock excavation may be required for
the deeper cuts and utility trenches
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
proposed development site is located on the southwestern side of

Oahu Hawaii The site is bordered by Mokapu Saddle Road on the

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes the Waianae and
Koolau Shield The project site is situated on the windward side of the
Koolau Range near the ancient caldera of the volcanoes Numerous dikes
are presented in this area and are visible in the highway cuts and in
exposed portions of the site
The shallow surface conditions at the site basically consists of
residual soil formed by in place weathering and decomposition of the lava
rock formation
Basalt outcrops were noted in portions of the gully where erosion has
removed the surface soils
Some amount of surface colluvium were noted along localized portions
of the gully side slopes and in a drainageway entering the gully area from
beyond the project limits These colluvial deposits were formed by
weathering and deposition of the upper residual soils beyond the project

Subsurface Conditions
Five 5 borings were previously drilled at the site to depths of
about 17 5 to 21 5 feet to explore the subsurface soil conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing Additionally hand probings were
utilized in the steep gully area to evaluate the soils in these steep
areas The present exploration involved the excavation of four 4 test
pits around the site to supplement the previous subsurface data
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results the near
surface subsoils can be generalized as mottled red tan and gray clayey
silt residual soils Weathered rock was encountered at about 17 to
20 foot depths in Boring Nos 4 and 5
Based on visual observations and the test pit data weathered rock
formation may be encountered at shallow depths near the existing gully
area
Groundwater was not noted in the borings or test pits during the
field exploration
More detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the borings
and test pits are presented in the Appendices
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed generally competent foundation
bearing soils at the proposed project site From a geotechnical
engineering point of view the site appears suitable for the proposed
development
Shallow foundations can be used for the proposed structures except
where they are situated on or near the steeply sloping areas particularly
near the existing gully
GEOLABS HAWAII
2298 00 Page 6
The near surface soils are moderately expansive and will require
special procedures for slab on grade construction
Foundations
Spread Footing
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
structures on level or gently sloping areas An allowable bearing
value up to 3000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on the
stiff residual soil and 2500 p s f for footings bearing on compacted
fill The above values are for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased on one third when temporary loads are considered
On level areas the bottom of the above designed footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished grade to
resist erosion and to reduce the effects of the moderately expansive
upper soils
Footings located on or near the tops of steep slopes will be subject
to erosion and slope creep of the surface soils therefore these
footings should be embedded an additional amount in order to provide
a minimum of eight 8 feet horizontal set back distance measured
from the outer edge of the footing to the slope face
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination of passive
pressure and friction Passive pressure may be taken as that exerted
by a fluid of density equal to 300 p c f Friction may be taken as
normal dead loads times a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations

Site Preparation and Earthwork
Cuts and fills generally less than about eight 8 feet are
anticipated for the general project site grading Based on the subsurface
data relatively easy site excavation can be anticipated except in the
deeper excavations where weathered rock may be encountered Rock
formation may be encountered at relatively shallow depths near the
existing gully area
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then scarified
and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density established per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to the above
specified density
Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V benching and keying are required to properly bond the
new fill to the slope The filling operation should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with the
above structural fill requirements For construction details refer to
Plate 3
The on site excavated soils can be used as a source of fill
material However they have moderate swell potential and should be
compacted wet of optimum moisture content to reduce the swelling
potential
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is required it
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
transportation to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed
construction The material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious matter

Slopes
The on site soils are generally silty and very erosive as evidence by
the existing eroded steep cut bank along Mokapu Saddle Road and along the
existing concrete driveway It is also difficult to mlch or plant these
slopes if they are cut too steep and this will also increase erosion
potential For these reasons two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut
and fill slopes are recommended for the site
All fill slopes should be track rolled or over filled and cut back to
obtain a well compacted slope All fill slopes should be properly keyed
and benched as outlined under Site Grading
To reduce erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Basement and Retaining Wall
Retaining walls generally less than eight 8 feet high and basement
walls up to about 15 feet high are planned for the development
Well graded granular material such as base course rock should be
used as backfill material behind the walls Impervious material should be
used for the top two 2 feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under Foundations should be followed for
the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures as
follows
Walls unrestrained at top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2298 00 Page 9
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should be included
where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 may be utilized to determine the sliding
resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be used
However the passive resistance should be reduced by one third when
combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Slab On Grade
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have low to
moderate swell potential therefore slab on grade should have deep
perimeter footings extending down a minimum of 12 inches below the finish
grade to reduce ground movement due to drying and wetting around the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of
90 of maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations When a slab is
located below the outside ground surface a subdrainage system around the
perimeter of the walls is recommended to relieve seepage water
Roadway Pavement
For the proposed access roadway design a 6 inch concrete pavement
with 6 x galvanized wire mesh over 6 inch base course can be used
over a compacted subgrade The subgrade should be compacted to obtain 95
of maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2298 00 Page 10
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should also be
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine
is recommended under pipes to provide uniform support
On site soils or select material may be used for the trench
backfill The backfill from the bedding to about one foot above the pipes
should be granular material and should be lightly tamped to reduce the
possibility of damaging the pipes

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
