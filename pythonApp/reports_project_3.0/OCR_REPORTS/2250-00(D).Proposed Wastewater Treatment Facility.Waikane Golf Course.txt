INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration for the site grading and foundation design of the
proposed Waikane Golf Course development Wastewater Treatment Facility
The project site is located in Waikane Valley in the Koolaupoko District
of windward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in
general accordance with our revised proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Waikane Golf Course development consists of a
twenty seven hole golf course and related facilities The proposed
wastewater treatment facility is a related facility and will provide
treatment for sanitary wastes generated at the golf course club house
facility The treatment facility will consist of several lightly loaded
buildings with concrete slab on grade floors and tanks for the treatment
processes Site grading will consist of cutting into the side of a ridge
about 5 to 10 feet and the placement of about 3 to 4 feet of fill to
create a level area
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain soil rock data for the
the formulation of engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of
the site grading and foundations of the proposed wastewater treatment
facility
The scope of our work included
1 General geologic site reconnaissance making visual observations
of the surface soil conditions

GENERAL STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Waikane Valley in the Koolaupoko
District on the windward shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The Waikane Golf Course project site is rural land that is used
partially for truck farming and pasture Most of the site is fallow land
that is densely vegetated by guava christmas berry and other semitropical
trees shrubs and grasses

In general the topography of the golf course development area is
rolling with locally steep relief where drainageways cross the site The
overall slope and drainage is towards the east
The proposed wastewater treatment facility site is situated in what is
currently fallow land in the lower portion of Waikane Valley to the south
of the existing Roberts Road Waikane Valley Road The site is heavily
covered with guava Java plum vines and other vegetation The southern
half of the site slopes gently down in a southerly direction at a ratio of
about five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V and the northern half is the
base of a ridge with an existing slope of about two and one half
horizontal to one vertical down in a southerly direction
Elevations at the site vary from about 18 to 50 feet above Mean Sea Level

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs Waikane Valley is an amphitheater headed
valley which has been deeply eroded into the eastern flank of Koolau
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell in
response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2250 00 D Page 5
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
During the lower stands of the sea the land surface was eroded to
depths below the current sea level The basalt floor of Waikane Valley is
most likely at one hundred or more feet below sea level Alluvial
deposits were laid down during higher stands of the sea at elevations
above the current base level Most of the project site is thickly mantled
with alluvium Some of this alluvial material has become deeply weathered
and eroded since its deposition
Subsurface Conditions
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions at the project
site appear to consist of surface crust ranging in thickness between four
4 and twenty 20 feet of stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty
clays underlain by about fifteen 15 feet of medium stiff silty clays and
clayey silts This relatively soft horizon is saturated and is
compressible
Below the relatively soft horizon our exploration encountered
primarily stiff mottled brown brown and reddish brown clayey silts to
the maximum depths explored of 36 0 feet below the existing ground
surface representing deeply weathered alluvial materials These soils
were originally stream laid clastic deposits consisting of boulders
cobbles and gravel In situ chemical weathering of these clastic deposits
has reduced the material to clayey silts while retaining most of the
structure of the original boulders cobbles and gravel
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths ranging
between 4 5 and 28 0 feet below the existing ground surface during our
exploration These water levels will most likely fluctuate in response to
precipitation and the surface water levels occurring in the stream
southerly of the treatment facility site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our exploration indicates that most of the site is
underlain by a surface crust of relatively competent subsoils This crust
is underlain by about five 5 to ten 10 of compressible soils
It is estimated that the underlying compressible soils may experience
settlements on the order of one 1 inch under the loads imposed by about
five 5 feet of site filling Therefore delaying construction of the
treatment plant is recommended to reduce the potential of later structural
distresses
It is our opinion that modified site grading techniques may be used
for the majority of the grading work
To minimize construction delay on the treatment facility building
construction the required site filling and grading at the facility site
should be performed under the initial golf course site mass grading
contract
Site Grading
Site grading and fill placement should be initiated as soon as
practicable in order to minimize future fill settlements to the treatment
plant structures which will be supported on spread footings
Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of
vegetation debris boulders and other deleterious materials Topsoil
should be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill materials Any tree roots or stumps greater than
three 3 inches in diameter should be removed In areas designated to
receive fill the prepared ground surface should be scarified to a minimm
depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any soft or yielding areas encountered should be
over excavated and backfilled with on site soil compacted to 85 maximm
dry density
W O 2250 00 D Page 7
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site
subsoils generally have in place moisture contents in excess of the
optimum moisture content Therefore some amount of the drying of
excavated material may be required before its use as fill
Because of the generally high moisture content of the on site
material and because of the site being situated in a high rainfall area
it is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other
locales may be modified to 85 for most of the golf course embankment
fills proposed on this project However the 90 standard i e 90 of
the maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 should be
maintained for structural fills structural backfills and utility
backfills below paved areas We believe the upper five 5 foot of the
surface material generally has a lower moisture content of about 40 which
would be more ideal for the 90 compaction density requirement for the
structural fill under the proposed structures
General fills should be placed in maximum eighteen 18 inch thick
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to the appropriate level of dry
density Sheepsfoot type compaction equipment is highly suited to the
materials encountered at the project site For structural fills the lift
thickness should be reduced to a maximm of twelve 12 inches and for
trench backfills it should be reduced to a maximum of eight 8 inches
We anticipate a shrinkage factor of about 20 when the on site soils
are utilized as fill compacted to 85 maximm dry density This estimated
shrinkage factor does not include losses for handling clearing or
grubbing It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to determine if
it is suitable for its intended use Generally the material should be
non expansive and should not contain boulders organics or other
deleterious materials

Where the existing ground surface is steeper than five horizontal to
one vertical 5H 1V keying and benching are required to properly bond
the new fill to the existing slope See Plate 3 for details The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations
for fill placement
Subdrains should be provided at the base of the new fills to reduce
the potential of water accumulating at the interface between the fill and
the existing subsoils See Plate 4 Such accumulation of water could
create a plane of weakness between the fill and the original ground
potentially causing slippage or creep of the fill
In general cut slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter are recommended for the site grading design Fill slopes should
be constructed at ratios of three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V due
to the reduced compaction level and should be constructed by over filling
past the design slope and cutting back Care should be exercised to avoid
over cutting of cut slopes
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the
finished slopes
In general midpoint slope benches should be provided for slopes
steeper than four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V and having vertical
heights greater than 20 feet
Based on our laboratory data and analyses the proposed site filling
will induce settlements in the underlying compressible soils These
settlements will be discussed in greater detail in a later section of this
report However we suggest that the site grading be performed in
conjunction with the golf course grading and that it be performed as early
as possible to allow the settlements to occur prior to construction of the

Foundation Design
Based upon the soil conditions encountered in our field exploration
it is our opinion that spread and continuous footing foundations bearing
on new fill or on recompacted in situ soils may be utilized to support
the proposed structures The construction of foundations for the
treatment plant should be delayed until the majority of fill settlements
have occurred first after site filling is completed
The bottoms of foundation excavations should be scarified to a depth
of six 6 inches below footing subgrade moisture conditioned to near
optimm moisture content and recompacted to 90 of the maximum dry density
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft wet and or yielding
areas encountered at footing subgrade level should be over excavated to
firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with non expansive material compacted in thin lifts to 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be utilized for the
design of foundations bearing either on recompacted in situ soils prepared
as recommended above or structural fill A minimm footing embedment of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is recommended The above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2250 00 D Page 10
recommended bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
A base friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
recompacted on site soils In addition a passive resistance of 300
p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical faces of the footings may
be used to calculate resistance to lateral loads
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the same depth as the bottom of the trench
to reduce the potential of footing distress resulting from the settlement
of the trench backfill If a continuous footing foundation crosses a
utility trench it should be designed to span the trench to reduce the
potential of foundation distress due to settlement of the backfill
It is anticipated that the sequential batch reactors and the filter
tanks will be embedded to depths between ten 10 and fifteen 15 feet
below finished grade Our field exploration encountered groundwater
levels generally at about an elevation of 15 feet MSL If the excavations
do not extend below about elevation 17 feet MSL generally no special
bedding will be required However if the excavations extend to greater
depth or if unforeseen soft soil conditions or groundwater are
encountered the depth of excavation should be increased and a working
platform of about twelve 12 inches of 3B Fine gravel should be placed
in the bottom of the excavation to provide uniform and adequate bearing
for the structures

Settlements
Our field exploration indicated that in general the relatively
competent surface crust encountered at the sewage treatment plant site is
underlain by some compressible soils which will experience some
settlements under the loads which will be imposed by site filling It is
estimated that the placement of three 3 to five 5 feet of fill will
cause fill ground settlements on the order of about one 1 inch
In order to reduce the potential of structural distresses caused by
fill ground settlements we suggest a fill settlement monitoring program
be installed to observe fill settlements and to determine when building
construction can commence at the treatment plant facility It is our
estimate that a minimm fill settlement waiting of about six 6 months
should be considered for shedding purposes
The fill settlement observation program should be monitored by the
installation and regular surveying of installed settlement gauges The
periodic settlement gauge readings should be transmitted to the project
geotechnical engineer for analyses to determine when the settlements have
reached a tolerable rate Once the settlements have reached a tolerable
rate the construction of the treatment plant may commence
We estimate that the fill settlement observation period on the order
of four 4 to six 6 months will be required depending on subsurface
soil conditions and the amount of settlements the future structures can
tolerate
It is estimated that settlements due to the structural loads of the
chiorination blower building will be on the order of one half of an inch
or less
It is recommended that the building be designed to accommodate these
estimated settlements For buried structures minimal settlements are
anticipated if the fill settlements have been allowed to occur prior to
construction

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that slabs on grade will be utilized for the floors
of buildings within the wastewater treatment facility We recommend that
the slab be separated from the wall footings and the construction he
delayed toward the end of building construction if practicable A
minimum 4 inch thick slab with a 4 inch thick cushion of ASTM C 67B 3B
Fine gravel should be used A moisture barrier membrane should be
considered between the slab and cushion if it is desired to protect
interior floor coverings or material stored within the buildings
The subgrades of all slabs should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement to reduce the potential drying and shrinkage of the subgrade
soils
Infrastructure
The use of underground utility lines is anticipated for this project
Based on our exploration most of the trenching will be in stiff alluvial
soils Therefore it is our opinion that conventional excavation and
backfill techniques may be utilized
However due to the alluvial nature of the on site soils there is a
possibility that some areas of hard excavation such as well cemented
soils and or boulders may be encountered
The on site soils with proper moisture conditioning are generally
suitable for use as trench backfill material Since the project site is
located in a high rainfall area the on site subsoils generally have
in place moisture contents in excess of the optimm moisture content
Therefore some amount of the drying of excavated material may be required
before its use as backfill
Because of the high moisture content of the on site material and
because of the site being situated in a high rainfall area it is our
opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other locales may
