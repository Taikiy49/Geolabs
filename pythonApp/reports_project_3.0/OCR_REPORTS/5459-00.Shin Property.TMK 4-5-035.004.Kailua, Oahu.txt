Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two separate
submarine shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age 4 million years ago and Koolau is

estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age 5 million years ago
After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys
into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by
cinder and tuff cone formations These more recent series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series and include some prominent landform features as the Diamond Head and
Aliamanu Craters
During the latter stages of the Koolau Volcano eruption the main Koolau Caldera
was filled with ponded lavas and a myriad of lava feeding dike structures which are
collectively identified as the Kailua Volcanic Series The Koolau Caldera structure is
believed to have encompassed the entire Kailua area extending roughly from the Koolau
Pali northeastward toward the coastline Following the deposition of the caldera filling
lavas the basaltic rocks of the Kailua Volcanic Series were deeply altered by underground
fluids and gases and eroded by heavy stream flow activity Thus relatively little of the
original caldera structure and filling deposits remain exposed today as a result of the deep
alteration and erosion of the rock
The project site is situated in hilly terrain located near the base of the Nuuanu
Koolau Pali as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on our review of
available geological map information the project site is underlain by highly weathered rock
and soils generally consisting of silty and clayey residual and saprolitic soils with some
embedded remnant rocky material The near surface soils represent the extremely to
completely weathered basaltic rock products belonging to the Kailua Volcanic Series
Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located near the intersection of Auloa Road and the
Pali Highway which is situated approximately 700 feet southerly of the Castle Junction in
the Kailua area of the Island of Oahu as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The parcel is
located on sloping terrain near the foot of a prominent ridgeline located adjacent to the
Pali Highway Ground surface elevations at the Shin property range between about
400 to 440 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The project site is bounded by Auloa Road
located along the northern through western perimeter of the property Auloa Road
terminates at a locked gate located approximately 500 feet southwesterly from the project
site Auloa Road is paved with asphaltic concrete pavement
The approximately 10 716 square foot Shin property resides on inclined terrain
which slopes down toward the north at variable inclinations ranging between about one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V to about 2H 1V Property survey pins or other boundary
markers were not encountered during our reconnaissance of the project site However
based on our review of available property maps the Shin property is bounded by existing
easements related to the old Pali Highway Auloa Road alignment located upslope of the
property and the existing Auloa Road alignment located downslope of the property as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on our observations the upslope easement area

Based on our site reconnaissance we encountered an existing unlined earthen
ditch ranging from about 3 to 7 feet in depth and about 3 to 4 feet wide traversing along

Preliminary Findings and Discussions
Based on our literature research and site reconnaissance performed for the
proposed Shin Property project we anticipate that the proposed project site is underlain by
residual saprolitic soils with some remnant gravelly decomposed rock material These
deposits are typically stiff and firm in consistency and provide suitable support for
residential structures Therefore it is our opinion that the site is suitable for residential
development from a geotechnical engineering standpoint
Existing slopes with inclinations of 1H 1V and steeper were observed near the
boundary of the project site We believe that these steep slopes are not stable in the
long term condition Therefore flattening of the existing slopes to an inclination of about
2H 1V or flatter may be required Alternatively the construction of retaining walls may be
used at the steep slope areas to increase the available building area
We recommend that a detailed geotechnical exploration be performed to develop
specific geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the planned
development of the property and house foundations We envision that the primary
geotechnical engineering considerations for this project may include
Site grading to develop suitable building pad
Use of retaining walls for grade separation
Special house foundations to accommodate possible moderately expansive
soil and steep terrain
