SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered a surface
layer of stiff clay over weathered basalt at
the lot Laboratory testing and previous
experience have demonstrated that the clay has
high swelling potential when saturated from a
dry condition
Special foundation design is thus recommended
to reduce the effects of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation it is
recommended that the expansive clay material be
removed a minimm of 3 feet below the concrete
slab foundation or down to the underlying
weathered rock surface and replaced with
non expansive soil
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation it is recommended that the clay be
over excavated 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual footings or to the in situ rock
formation The over excavation should then be
replaced with compacted non expansive borrow
soils for foundation support
The text of this report should be referred to
for detailed and special design
recommendations

performed for the proposed residence at Lot Ridge Phase

its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed house
will consist of a three level residence on a lot with an existing slope
ratio of approximately H V
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway
Foundation loads are anticipated to be moderate to light and typical for
wooden residential structures
Site grading involving cut and fill up to about 6 feet is
anticipated
PURPOSE _AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the
building foundation design of the subject house The recommendations
are based on the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this
exploration The study consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
borings The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The results
of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in
the engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are presented
in the appendices of this report
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Hawaii Ioa Ridge District of
Honolulu This approximately rectangular shaped 14 895 square foot lot
is fronted by Kaulana Place to the east The current ground elevations
range from about 680 to 730 feet above Mean Sea Level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
reddish brown expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous
experience indicate that this material is highly expansive Therefore
the house foundation will require special design and construction
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the clay
subsoils
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed structure To reduce the effects of the expansive soil we
recommend that the expansive clays be either over excavated down to a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1941 00 Page 4
Dk No 43
minimum depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the individual spread
footings or to the in situ weathered rock formation The bottom of the
spread continuous footing should either bear on compacted backfill or
weathered rock
The footing backfill should consist of non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste select borrow or coral The backfill should be
compacted in 6 inch lifts to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 For details refer to the over excavation
method shown on the Schematic Section on Plate 3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet or
bearing on the in situ rock below the lowest adjacent grade for
details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
foundation design
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
To reduce the swelling effects of the underlying expansive clay
soils we recommend that a minimm 3 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive select borrow material be placed below the slab on grade
foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1941 00 Page 5
Dk No 43
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimm
depth of three feet 3 below finished grade or until the in situ rock
formation is encountered The removal should also extend about 3 feet
beyond the slab perimeter Boulders encountered in the excavation
should be discarded The excavation should then be replaced with
non expansive off site materials such as select borrow crusher waste
or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm ary density per
ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying remaining clay should be covered with
lime and moistened and then maintained in a moist condition
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine cushion fill should be provided below
the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier with 1 inch sand layer on top may be considered below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the
Schematic Section on Plate 4
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway the above recommendations provided for
the concrete slab on grade should be followed However since the
driveway could tolerate some amount of swelling the non expansive
capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet to reduce costs if some
concrete cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should also be kept
moist and covered with lime prior to concrete placement
A minimum 12 inch thick perimeter thickened edge is also
recommended for the driveway

Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete
Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that retaining walls may be used for grade
separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 60 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
5 The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 8 foot minima horizontal set back distance
measured from the toe of the wall out horizontally to the
existing sloping ground surface

6 The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a H V plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines
7 Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
8 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Lot Grading
The fill and backfill within the top 36 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral In areas such as the garage driveway
and the house areas where cut or minimal fill is anticipated the on site
soil should be over excavated to provide the required thickness of
non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade slabs
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
surface boulders vegetation and debris
The fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 9 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill Slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation

is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic Survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
