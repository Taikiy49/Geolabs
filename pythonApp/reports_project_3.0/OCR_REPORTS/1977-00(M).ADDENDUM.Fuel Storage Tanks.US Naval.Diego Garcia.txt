This addendum is to provide recommendations for design of the
Oil Water Separator and Ocean Outfall and to offer our comments
and opinions on using the CLAYMAX liner for Containment Lining
System of the subject project
Oil Water Separator
Based on the borings drilled it appears that the new
oil water separator would be founded on medium dense to dense beach
sand deposits An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may
be used for this material Should liquefaction occur during a
strong earthquake loading the anticipated ground settlements of
the separator are on the order of less than one quarter of an inch
which we believe is tolerable for the structure
Dewatering during construction of the separator is an
ticipated since the bottom of the separator has been set at about
elevation MSL We believe the beach sand deposits could
be very permeable The method and equipment to be used for the
dewatering operations should be the choice of the contractor
within practical limits and safety considerations Alternative
ly tremie concrete may be used in lieu of the dewatering opera
tion

Ocean Outfall
At the ocean outfall facility site it is anticipated that
coral algal limestone would be encountered near the proposed
drainpipe invert level Relatively hard excavation into the
limestone may be anticipated An allowable bearing value up to
3000 p s f may be used on the coral material Minimum settlements
are anticipated for these conditions
Based on the available plans it appears that a one 1 foot
thick concrete encasement surrounding the pipe will be provided
where pipe cover is less than two 2 feet Therefore it is
anticipated that the materials at the invert level along the
proposed drainline may vary from concrete to on site coral algal
limestone and beach sand deposits
Due to the anticipated different materials along the drainline
and to minimize the possibility of damaging the pipe during
backfilling the trench it is recommended that a granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel be provided
under the pipes where no concrete encasement is planned pipe cover
over 2 feet
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The
backfill up to about one 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly
tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one
foot above the pipes to finished grade should be placed in maximum
10 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum
of 90 compaction per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against
potential future ground subsidence
Dewatering during construction of the concrete encasement is
also anticipated since the pipe invert is set at elevation 0 48
ft MSL The method and equipment to be used for the dewatering
operations should be the choice of the contractor
Containment Lining System
Currently it is our understanding that CLAYMAX liner is being
considered for use as the containment lining system CLAYMAX is a
flexible geocomposite liner consisting of polypropylene fabric and
sodium bentonite in a uniform carpet form

Due to the high swelling nature of bentonite when hydrated
with fresh water the CLAYMAX liner has self sealing seams and
overall self healing capability if ripped or punctured according
to the product brochure and the manufacturer In its hydrated
state the bentonite will swell and provide tremendous imper
meability
Based on the information provided by the manufacturer the
installation of the system involves placement of the CLAYMAX liner
and six 6 to eight 8 inches of capping fill first The lining
areas should then be wetted with water to let the CLAYMAX liner
hydrate to form the low permeability layer
Since the bentonite clay is an inert material little chemical
activity between the liner and storage materials is anticipated
if leakages do occur Different storage materials may affect the
permeability of the liner to a small degree
According to the manufacturer the hydrated bentonite layer
is capable of maintaining moistures with the upper six 6 to eight
8 inches of capping fill However it is our concern that under
extended periods of hot weather as experienced in Diego Garcia say
90 degrees for a month the bentonite clay liner may eventually
lose moisture shrink and form cracks We believe the shrinkage
and cracking will have significant impact on the performance and
permeability of the liner
Based on the above concerns it is recommended that the
behavior of the CLAYMAX liner under extended hot weather conditions
be carefully studied and reviewed before adopting the system for
the project The manufacturer should be consulted for further
information and confirmation regarding the behavior of the liner
as discussed above Site specific tests may have to be considered
to evaluate the performance under the actual site conditions The
installation procedures should closely follow the manufacturer s
specifications
Based on the above it is our opinion that some form of
impervious liner such as Hypalon may be required over the clay
liner to keep the clay in a moist expanded condition
