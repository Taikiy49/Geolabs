Compaction Report
Halawa Business Park
Halawa Valley Oahu Hawaii
From to March 5 1987 we observed the earthwork and
grading operations performed at the above referenced site This report
summarizes our observations and compaction tests performed at the
above referenced project
The grading operations at the site consisted of fills generally up
to 25 feet thick and cuts up to 18 feet deep During the fill placement
Operations periodic field density tests were done in accordance with the
American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Sand Cone Method
Based on our observations and test results it is our opinion that
the above referenced subdivision has been constructed to final grade and
compacted adequately The density test results for the in place fills are
indicated in the attached Density Test Summary A general layout plan
indicating the location and mmber of the lots is also attached

Old Fill Areas
During the above project site preparation and filling operations
miscellaneous loose fill was encountered in some areas of the site In
order to have a better understanding of the condition of the fill test
pits were excavated to examine the nature of the fill and to delineate the
limits of the loose fill areas
Based on the test pit data the thickness of the old fill was found
to be up to about 15 feet The fill material generally consisted of
moderately compacted sandy silt with some rubbish broken pieces of metal
wood tires etc underlain by the natural alluvial material consisting
of medium stiff to stiff clay
Based on the field exploration the old fill material was generally
moderately compacted and basically consisted of suitable type of material
silty sandy soils with only a little rubbish
Therefore the following procedure was utilized
a The old fill within the fill slopes along the boundary of the
site was removed and recompacted to form a firm buttress fill
b The old fill within 5 feet of finished grade was overexcavated
and recompacted

Seepage
Seepage water was noted in Lots and 50 during rainy
periods of the mass grading Efforts to locate the source of the water
were unsuccessful We have recommended that subdrains be installed in
these lots to drain the seepage water into the nearest catch basins
However since the subdivision road improvements contract has not been
awarded yet these connections to the catch basins cannot be made The
fine grading for Lot 47 has not been completed pending installation of
the subdrain
Expansive Clay Soils
During the lot excavation expansive clay soils were encountered at
the finished grade of Lot 26 Although not noticed expansive clay may be
present in other cut lots
Special foundation design will be required for structures on the
expansive clay
Recommendations
Building Foundations
The lot purchasers should be strongly advised to obtain the services
of a soil engineer to aid in the design of the foundations of the new
buildings and pavements and to provide observation and testing during
construction

Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within the
lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the observation
of a soils engineer
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this
report without our knowledge and written approval
We strongly suggest that all of the above referenced recommendations
and restrictions be made available to all future lot purchasers of this
subdivision Lot owners should also be advised not to block or alter
surface drainage Professional consultation should be obtained in the
planning design and construction of any future improvements
