Project Considerations 
Potable water for the Manele area of the Island of Lanai will be provided by the 
desalinization of brackish groundwater. The desalinization process will produce a brine 
effluent with a concentrated salinity. Two new disposal wells are being installed for 
disposal of the effluent as industrial wastewater. In accordance with the Hawaii 
Department of Health administrative rule for Underground Injection Control (UIC), it is 
required to have a geologist maintain logs for this work. 
Logging Procedure and Scope of Work 
We provided geological logging services for the wells in a manner appropriate to 
the well construction method and in general accordance with normal professional 
practice and principles.  The logging of the wells was based on visual and textural 
examination of samples of cuttings collected at about 5-foot intervals during the drilling. 
The samples were delivered to our laboratory for examination and logging. 
For logging, cutting samples were removed from the sample bags and spread in 
a laboratory sample tray.  Initial visual observation was conducted to evaluate the origin 
of the material (e.g., alluvium, basalt formation or coralline formation). Closer 
examination was made to attempt to differentiate specifics about the material 
(e.g. pahoehoe, a`a clinker or a`a core). If possible, further description of the formation 
was provided. A written description was made for each cutting sample and the 
descriptions were compiled for longer depth intervals.

Report
The well logs are presented as verbal descriptions on the attached Injection Well
Logs, Plates 1 and 2. In general, the wells at the site penetrated basaltic lava flows of
the Lanai Volcanic Series.
Our observations of the samples indicated that the lava flows consisted of
alternating layers of thinly bedded a‘a flows with associated clinker and thinly bedded
pahoehoe flows. No unusual geological or hydrological conditions appeared to be
associated with the samples examined.
Our work was performed within the agreed upon scope of work. Our work is also
