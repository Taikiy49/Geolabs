Dear Mr. Joshi: 
We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering earthwork observation and 
compaction testing report for work performed during the site grading operations for the 
Solar Parking Shade Structures, Leeward Community College project in general 
accordance with our fee proposal dated December 26, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at Leeward Community College in the Pearl City area 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The general vicinity of the project is presented on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. The project involves the design and construction of new 
photovoltaic (PV) power generating parking shades in the existing parking lots near the 
northern boundary of the campus. 
Site grading generally consisted of minor cuts and fills to install battery pads and 
underground utility lines. Waialua Plumbing and Mechanical, Inc. performed the 
earthwork operations for the project.  
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were thoroughly 
cleared and grubbed. Vegetation, debris, deleterious materials, and other unsuitable 
materials were removed and disposed of properly off-site to reduce the potential for 
contamination of the excavated materials. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density. 
In the roadway areas, the subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density.

During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 2. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.3. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering December 18 
through 31, 2018. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for the Solar Parking Shade Structures, Leeward 
Community College project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by 
this report, were performed in accordance with the general intent of the recommendations 
in the following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction, City and County of 
Honolulu, September 1986. 
3. 
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Solar Parking Shade Structures, Leeward Community College, Pearl City, 
Oahu, Hawaii,” dated July 3, 2018.
