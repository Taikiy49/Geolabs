PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for design
of the foundation for the proposed structure

The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the upper crust of fill and the underlying soil deposit for shallow
foundation design
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling four
4 borings to depths of 15 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation

PROJECT CONSTDERATTONS
It is proposed to demolish some of the existing structure walls and
add new building elements adjacent to the existing facilities
The proposed development consists of a one story structure
approximately 100 by 120 feet in plan next to the existing gymnasium
The finished grade of the proposed building was not available at
this time However minimal site grading is anticipated for the
construction of the proposed building
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is situated at the southeast portion of
the Kalakaua Intermediate School grounds immediately north of the
intersection of McNeill and Kaumualii Streets

SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface soil conditions were explored by drilling and
sampling four 4 borings to depths of 15 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a dense to medium
dense fill about 2 feet thick of silty sand Beneath the fill was a
very stiff to hard silty clay layer of about 3 feet thick which was
in turn underlain by dense to very dense coral formation
Groundwater was not encountered in our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The dense silty sand and stiff to hard silty clay encountered can
generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building
improvements provided that some care is exercised during construction to
minimize the swelling potential of the underlying clay soil
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the hard
silty clay indicate that this material generally has low to moderate
swelling potential depending on the initial in situ moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to minimize
detrimental effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site subgrade soil be moistened with sprinkling and
protected from subsequent drying by evaporation This should be initiated
from the start of slab subgrade construction until the concrete slab is
poured

Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions we believe that an
allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used for spread or
continuous footing bearing on the upper existing fill materials
Footing should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Minimal settlements under the design loading are anticipated with the
dense and very stiff subsoil conditions encountered in our exploration
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
recompacted existing fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive
resistance
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if it is allowed to dry during construction period
prior to concrete placement Therefore from the start of construction
until the slab concrete placement the on site sandy subgrade soil should
be continuously moistened This would protect the underlying expansive
clay from drying via capillary action
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade The bottom of all
thickened edge excavations should also be wetted and kept moist until the
concrete is poured The backfill against the poured concrete slab should
be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter

A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab We believe the coarse gravel will also act asa
capillary break layer between the moist saturated subgrade soil and the
upper concrete slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish and broken up pavements should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the footing or
subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted to the
above mentioned standard
The on site sandy and granular soils may be reused as backfill
material However clay soil is not allowed to be reused as backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Joints are recommended in the concrete pavement and walkway in order
to allow for some relative movement of the concrete slab sections which
may occur due to posssible swelling of the silty clay subgrade
The base course should consist of crushed rock compacted to 90 to
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Additional CBR tests should be perforemd on the actual exposed
subgrade soil samples during construction to verify the above design
section which may need to be revised if necessary
Drainage
The finish grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the building and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundtions and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
