SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Hauula Skate Park Facility at Hauula Community Park in Hauula on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our
field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations
are intended for the design of the site grading and other geotechnical aspects only The
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Hauula Community Park in the Hauula area
on the Island The proposed project involves construction of a Skate
Park facility which will be built at the northern side of the existing softball field and will
be approximately 10 000 square feet in area Based on the grading plan we
understand that site grading for the proposed construction will involve various fill
thickness of up to 9 feet and shotcrete will be used to form the finish surface of the
skating facility
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
site grading for the new Hauula Skate Park facility The work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated March 24 2003 Our scope of work included
the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island After the main shield building phase of Koolau the
volcanic dome became deeply eroded On the windward side of the island combined
wind rain and wave erosion cut the coastline back while forming steep cliffs
The project site is located on the windward northeastern flank of Koolau and its
subsurface conditions are directly related to this deep erosion and littoral processes at
the coast The subsurface conditions within the project area are dominated by beach
and lagoonal deposits which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments The base of
the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
2 2 Site Description
The project site is located on the northern portion of the existing Hauula
Community Park property in the Hauula area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is bounded by an existing softball field to the south existing play area and
recreational building to the east and a wire fence to the north
Based on our field observations the project site is currently a lawn The site is
generally flat with a small dirt pile in the center of the site Based on the topographic
map provided the elevation of the site is approximately 6 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL

Surcharge Program
To aid in the acceleration of the anticipated ground settlements a surcharge
program is recommended The surcharge program should consist of the
placement of an additional 5 feet of temporary surcharge fill over the finished
subgrades or finish grades prior to construction of various improvements Fill
materials required to raise the existing grades to the finish grades should
conform to the recommendations provided in the Site Grading section of this
report
We recommend that a one month waiting period be implemented between
completion of the general site fill and placement of the temporary surcharge
This waiting period is required to allow subsoils to gain strength in order to
reduce the potential for excessive lateral deformation and possible failure within
the soft subsoils

believe that the estimated settlement period could vary by as much as 50 to
100 percent from the actual settlement period required Therefore the actual
settlement rate should be monitored and provisions should be made for potential
delays in the project construction schedule if a longer settlement period is
required

3 2 Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading will involve fills of up to 9 feet for construction of
the new skate park facility Site preparation should be observed by a representative
from Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office observe whether
the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site preparation work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials generated from demolition should be removed and disposed of
off site After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to
placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

In general the on site material generated from the excavation may be reused as
a source of fill and backfill material provided that the materials are screened of
over sized materials greater than 3 inch in maximum dimension and are free of
deleterious materials Where used as fill and backfill material the on site soils should
be properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched
We envision that imported fill materials will be required In general the imported
material should consist of non expansive material such as crushed coral basalt or
cinder sand The imported material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
3 3 Retaining Structures
We understand that retaining walls with heights of up to 5 feet will be constructed
on the proposed project site Design of the retaining wall system will need to take into
consideration both the external and internal stability of the structure In evaluating
external stability the retaining wall must satisfy four stability conditions 1 bearing
failure 2 translational sliding 3 overturning stability and 4 overall slope stability
Geotechnical design parameters to evaluate these stability conditions are presented in
the following subsections

square foot psf may be used for the design of footings bearing on compacted

loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those

Foundations located next to other foundations utility trenches or easements
should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge of the utility trench or they should be extended to a depth as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples to aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate B 1
One Specific Gravity test ASTM D 854 A was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the engineering properties of the material encountered Results of
the tests are summarized on Plate B 2
One Consolidation test with time rates ASTM D 2435 was performed on a
sample of the potentially compressible soils to evaluate the compressibility
characteristics of the materials encountered Results of the consolidation test are
presented on Plate B 3
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 4
