GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
NEW ELEVATOR FOR MAKIKI LIBRARY
Dear Mr Louie
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed new elevator construction at the
existing Makiki Library This report summarizes our work on the project and presents our
findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the
subject project In addition discussions on the excavation and shoring requirements of
the project are included in this report for information purposes The recommendations
presented herein are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Makiki Library within the Makiki District
Park in the Makiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The existing library is a
3 story building with a basement level extending to a depth of approximately 8 feet below
the exterior finished grade The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to install a new elevator as part of
the library renovation project The proposed elevator and machine room will be built near
the existing entry at the southwestern side of the library as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Based on the schematic drawings provided the new elevator shaft will measure
approximately 10 5 feet by 7 5 feet in plan dimensions and extend from the basement
level up to the third floor of the library building In addition the elevator pit will extend to a
depth of about 4 feet below the basement floor elevation The new machine room
measuring approximately 8 feet by 6 5 feet in plan dimensions will be located at the
basement level adjacent to the new elevator structure Detailed information pertaining to
the structural loads of the new improvements was not available at the time this letter report
was prepared

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was used to develop a generalized data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of the new elevator project The work was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated The scope
of work for this exploration included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of one boring extending to a depth of approximately
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the boring by a geologist
from our firm
A Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating
their engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations
slabs on grade retaining structures and site preparation for the project In
addition discussions on the excavation and shoring requirements of the
project are also included in this report for information purposes
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a senior engineer from our
firm
8 Quality assurance of our work on the project and client design team
consultation by a principal engineer from our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the library renovation project consisted of drilling
and sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the boring is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring generally encountered medium dense silty sands extending to a depth
of approximately 18 feet below the existing ground surface Dense to medium dense
cinder sand deposits were then encountered and extended to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to fluctuation due to
seasonal precipitation time of the year and other factors occurring off site Detailed
descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration are
presented on the Log of Boring Plate 4 of this report

subsoils extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the
time of our field exploration

that shallow foundations may be used to support the proposed new elevator structure
and machine room planned at the site An allowable bearing pressure of up to

Foundations
In general we believe that the proposed new construction may be supported on
a shallow foundation support system bearing on the recompacted in situ sandy soils
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used
for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted sandy soils
underlying the project site This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
For footings bearing on the on site soils the bottom of the footing excavations
should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively
firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular fill materials compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing may be extended
down to bear directly on the underlying competent materials
Special attention should be given to the footing depths of the existing library
building to avoid surcharging the new elevator foundations The actual depths of the
existing footings should be confirmed in the field during construction by the project
geotechnical engineer Footings located adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls
should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations
or the planned retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional structural
loads
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements the footings
should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for appreciable foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings supported on the
recompacted in situ sandy soils should be on the order of about 1 inch or less
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
in situ soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted

Slabs on Grade
Based on our field exploration results we believe that concrete slabs required for
the project may be constructed over the in situ sandy soils provided that the slab subgrade
is properly prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented below and in the
Site Preparation section of this report
Since the concrete slabs for the machine room and elevator pit will be subjected
to equipment and machinery vibrations a 6 inch layer of aggregate subbase is
recommended in lieu of the normal 4 inch thick gravel cushion layer The aggregate
subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Slab subgrades should be prepared by scarifying the subgrade soils to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Slab subgrades should be
kept moist until covered by concrete Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with select granular fill materials

Site Preparation
We anticipate that the majority of the site grading work for the proposed project will
consist of excavation of the elevator pit and backfilling behind the elevator shaft walls At
the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials with
the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and
grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled
with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill
material moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch
level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition the excavated subgrades underneath future slab
areas should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a

The excavated on site sandy soils may be re used as a source of backfill
materials behind the elevator shaft walls Imported fill and backfill materials if required
should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral or
basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger
than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the material should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or
less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill
materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase
material required underneath the concrete slabs should meet the requirements of
Section 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications The materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is
important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operation to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the scarification process
and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
Excavation
Based on the anticipated foundation level of the elevator pit we envision that
excavation depths of up to about 15 feet below the existing ground surface may be
required for the project construction We believe that shoring of the planned excavation
may be required if an open cut excavation is not feasible due to physical constraints
Therefore it is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained by the
contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used if needed
Excavation Method
Based on our field exploration results the project site is generally underlain by
medium dense to dense sandy soils extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface In general we believe
that conventional excavation equipment may be used to excavate the sandy soils
underlying the project site The method and equipment to be used for excavation

Excavation Support
Based on the schematic drawings provided the bottom of the elevator pit may
extend to a depth of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the
medium dense to dense sandy subsoil condition encountered we believe that a
1H 1V cut slope ratio may be utilized for the temporary elevator shaft excavation
Where an open cut excavation is not feasible considerations may be given to the
use of interlocking steel sheet piling with horizontal bracing for shoring purposes in
order to reduce the potential for adjacent ground movement
It is important that adequate sheeting be installed prior to excavation and
maintained tight against the excavation walls with proper bracing during
excavation The properly braced sheeting is essential to reduce lateral
movements of the adjacent ground into the excavation which may result in
potential settlements or distress to adjacent structures or other improvements
such as utilities
Underpinning of Existing Building
It is anticipated that the foundations of the existing library building at the site are
supported on a shallow foundation system bearing on the near surface soils Since
the planned excavation may extend below the basement level of the library
building we believe that underpinning of the library building may be required in
localized areas during the project construction Considerations may be given to the
use of Atlas Piers or similar type of foundation support systems for temporary
underpinning of the existing building structure if needed
Installation Considerations
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His her representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequence or techniques etc which may affect
shoring stability

However it must be noted that some minor movements of the shoring system
and the adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
