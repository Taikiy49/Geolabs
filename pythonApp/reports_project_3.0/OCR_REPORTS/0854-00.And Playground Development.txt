INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a subsurface investigation
for the above referenced school building and playfield The
work was performed according to our proposal dated March 27
1979
A previous soil investigation at the site was performed for
the existing school facilities This work was performed by
Walter Lum Associates Inc dated June 1975
GEOLABS HAWAII
The proposed structure includes two story 8 classroom
building approximately 45 by 175 feet in plan dimensions
The new classrooms will be located on an existing terrace
or bench east of existing classroom B The building floor
will be at the approximate existing grade of elevation 1308
feet A sprinkler control building roughly 14 by 14 feet
in plan dimension will be constructed adjacent to the new
playfield
The playfield development will include a new playfield
future asphalt playcourt approximately 100 by 230 feet
in plan a new softball field approximately 230 by 230
feet in plan a stairway asphalt pathway and access
road These playfield developments will be located on
the hillside between the existing school park and ball
fields Based on the development plan finished grades
at the playfield will require small amounts of fill and
cuts up to 10 feet is planned
PURPOSE SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to determine the general
subsurface soil conditions beneath the school additions We
were also to provide recommendations for foundations floor
slab support slope stability paving design grading and
general site development
GEOLABS HAWAII
The scope of work included 10 field borings disturbed
undisturbed and core sampling and a reconnaissance of
the area Standard penetration tests were performed
during the field investigation to measure the relative
density and compactness of the underlying soils encoun
tered The drilling subcontractor was MAS Drilling
Company Appropriate laboratory testing was performed
Based on the field and laboratory test results engin
eering analysis was performed to develop recommendations
for the design and construction of the classroom building
and various improvements This report summarizes our
conclusions and recommendations

Site Plan and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
During the early Pleistocene Period the Haleakala volcano
which forms East Maui was built mainly by eruption of
andesitic lava This Kula volcanic formation generally
consists of aa lava flows together with some olivine
and picrite basalts
Soil Conditions
Based upon the borings the natural materials were generally
divided into two layers a shollow layer of residual soils
and the underlying lava In some cases an upper layer
consisting of clayey silt roughly six feet thick was
encountered beneath the existing ground surface The
clayey silt is medium stiff to very stiff and is slightly
expansive changes in volume with variation in moisture
content The underlying residual silt and sandy silt are
stiff to very stiff and contain varying amounts of gravel
approximate standard penetration value N 20
Below depths of approximately 18 feet highly weathered lava
was encountered Boring 30 was extended into hard rock from
17 to 25 feet using an NX core barrel Boring 23 was ter
minated at 20 5 feet due to hard rock The surface of the
rock appears to be deeper than the planned excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
Minor amount of existing fill soils have been noted and
discussed in the section under Site Description
Although these fills do not appear to be engineered or
controlled to 90 percent minimum relative density they
have weathered in place for several years
Groundwater was not encountered in the exploratory borings
which extended up to 25 feet below existing ground surface
CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the proposed 8 classroom
buildings may be supported by conventional spread footing
foundations
The classroom building will be located immediately adjacent
to the top of the existing slope with stairways and walkways
located on the slope A low retaining wall between the
northern stairway and the slope may be required to provide
a level landing AlI1l foundations building stairs and
retaining wall should be supported on natural soils
Grading paving and building construction in the playfield
development will not involve unusual or difficult problems
Hard rock is relatively deep and the surface materials
generally can be excavated using conventional earthmoving
GEOLABS HAWAII
equipment The natural residual materials are relatively
firm and slopes of normal gradients will remain stable
The one story sprinkler building can be supported partly
on compacted fill and partly on natural materials
Foundations
Spread footing foundations underlain by stiff residual
or natural soils may be used to support the proposed
classroom building Spread footing foundations extending
at least twelve inches into natural materials may be
designed using a net allowable bearing capacity of up
to 3000 pounds per square foot
Spread type foundations underlain by compacted fill or
natural materials may be used to support the sprinkler
control building and the stairway A net allowable
bearing value of up to 2000 pounds per square foot may
be used
Above values of bearing capacity are for total of dead
plus live loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary
lateral loads wind and seismic are considered The bottom
of all footings should be at least 12 inches below finished
floor pavement or lowest exterior grade
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
i
on the order of one half inch Settlement is expected
GEOLABS HAWAII
to occur with the application of load Long term consoli
dation type settlements are not anticipated
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combina
tion of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure
may be taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal
to 250 p c f Frictional resistance may be taken as normal
dead loads times a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that the passive pressure componant is reduced by
one third
Grading
Prior to grading the site should be cleared of all vegetation
and debris The on site silty soils are suitable for use in
the compacted fill and backfill any large cobbles and boulders
which would interfere with compaction should be removed Top
soils contaminated by organic matter should be stockpiled
separately for reuse on the surface of the new ball field
Any imported fill materials obtained from excavations off
site or imported from borrow areas should be non expansive
and tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
importation
GEOLABS HAWAII
After stripping the ground surface should be scarified
and compacted Required additional fill soils should
be placed in maximum nine inch thick loose lifts and
compacted to 90 percent relative compaction using ASTM
Designation D 1557 70 Modified Proctor as the standard
All new lifts must be placed horizontally
The proposed cut and fill slopes generally may be sloped
up to 2 horizontal 1 vertical gradient The cut slope
near Boring 26 should be inspected during excavations to
observe how well the existing fill stands alternately
a 3 horizontal 1 vertical slope may be used near Boring 26
Shallow trenches excavated into the soil and rock formations
less than five feet deep may be left open for short term
construction periods with minor sloughing of the vertical
walls
Walls Below Grade
Retaining walls that are free to translate and rotate may
be designed to resist an active earth pressure equal to a
fluid having a density of 36 pounds per cubic foot Weep
holes should be provided to prevent build up of any hydro
static pressures
GEOLABS HAWAII
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
coarse gravel This slab on grade design requires that
the subgrade remain moist prior to construction Any dry
cracked soils should be excavated and replaced with granular
materials In buildings with a moisture sensitive floor
covering such as vinyl tile a polyethylene moisture
barrier protected by a 2 inch sand cushion can be incor
porated within the layer of base
Access Road Paved Areas
Based upon the results of laboratory CBR tests on the residual
silty soils our recommended pavement design for light medium
traffic is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade Soil
The above paving section assumes good drainage will be provided
adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and playfield construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi
cations
