Dear Mr. Char: 
This addendum to our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
MPSA Building and Track, Mid-Pacific Institute, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated 
January 28, 2020, provides our recommendations for additional subdrains beneath the 
future improved athletic track and field.  
In our report, we recommended providing a perimeter subdrain between the 
existing spring and the new track to mitigate subsurface seepage water from flowing 
beneath the new track and softening the subgrade soils. However, based on the 
memorandum by Tom Nance Water Resource Engineering (TNWRE) entitled 
“Groundwater Data Summary, Analysis, and Recommendations for MPI Planned 
Improvements” dated September 8, 2021, the measured groundwater level in one of the 
monitoring wells within the future track area was near the existing ground surface. Due 
to the potential for high groundwater levels within the future track area, we recommend 
installing additional subdrains beneath the proposed athletic track and field, as shown 
on the attached Site Plan, Plate 2.  
In general, the subdrain recommendations from our previous report may be used 
for the additional subdrains beneath the athletic track and field. However, as 
recommended in TNWRE’s memorandum, subdrains should not be hydraulically

connected to the perched groundwater body because it could drain the existing spring.
Based on the subsurface materials encountered in the nearby Boring No. 103, we agree
with TNWRE’s assertion that the perched water body may be confined within the clayey
gravel layer by approximately 4.5 feet of overlying clayey silt and silty clay. Therefore,
the contractor installing the subdrains should not excavate to the bottom of the clayey
silt and silty clay layer. If clayey gravel and/or groundwater is encountered in the
subdrain excavations, the bottom of the excavation should be sealed with an impervious
material, such as concrete or cement and bentonite before placement of the subdrain
pipe or granular backfill material.
A Geolabs representative should monitor the subdrain installation work to
observe whether the permeable layer has been encountered in the subdrain
excavations and provide recommended corrective action.
