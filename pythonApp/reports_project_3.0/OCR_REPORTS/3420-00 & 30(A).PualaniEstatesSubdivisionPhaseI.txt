Project Considerations
The Pualani Estates Subdivision project site is located east mauka of Queen
Kaahumanu Highway in the Kailua Kona area within the District of North Kona on the
Island of Hawaii The project consisted of developing single family residences on an area
encompassing about 100 acres The Phase 1 portion of the project consisted of
developing approximately 128 residential lots This grading report covers the mass
grading and infrastructure development of Pualani Estates Subdivision Phase 1 only We
understand that the construction of individual residences was observed by others
Keauhou Kona Construction Company was the contractor for the earthwork
operations mass grading and infrastructure installation Based on our records the initial
earthwork operations for the project were conducted between February 1995 and
October 1995 The earthwork operations recommenced in September 2002 and were
completed in September 2004

Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers
an area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the
following five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent
geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of
which are still active
The project site is situated on the southern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
project site appears to consist of a a type lava flows at the ground surface which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Pahoehoe lavas may be encountered at
some depth below the ground surface A a lava typically is characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments
including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or
billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when lavas are still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities
and along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as the primary water transmission
features because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
As previously mentioned the Pualani Estates Subdivision project site is located in
the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the original topography of
the project site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular or hummocky lava
flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by adjacent higher relief
mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
a a clinker Some pockets of volcanic ash were also present near the original ground
surface It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous voids and
cavities were observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should be
noted that near surface lava tubes and cavities detected within the grading limits were

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations at
the Phase 1 portion of the Pualani Estates Subdivision ranged from about 310 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL near Queen Kaahumanu Highway to about along
Road E2 The various building pads at the project site were generally graded to finished
pad elevations of about 327 to 443 feet MSL In general the finished pad elevations
were set at elevations directly below the concrete slabs on grade and cushion fill Mass
grading at the house pads generally consisted of embankment fills of up to about 30 feet
in height and excavations of up to about 15 feet in depth to achieve the design grades
Extensive subgrade preparation procedures were employed within the grading
limits at the commencement of the mass grading operations to reduce the potential for
collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities
and or voids contained in the porous near surface materials
In general the fill areas and finish subgrades in cut areas were generally
proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825 sheepsfoot roller compactor or similar equipment for
about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should
be noted that the mauka portions of many of the residential lots were graded to a cut
condition Therefore the presence of basalt formation and or a a basalt should be
anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in these areas of the lots
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 2 feet of the building pads consisted of structural fill materials General fills
consisting of materials generally smaller than about 12 to 18 inches in dimension were
placed below structural fills It should be noted that large boulders of up to about 5 feet in
dimension generally were placed at least about 4 feet below the finished grades within the
pad areas of the lots and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders were
generally placed about 6 feet or more horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
Base course materials were used as fill and backfill materials under roadways
parking areas within utility trench excavations and other areas Samples of the on site
and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported
to our office for testing where appropriate The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3

The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway and parking
areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method where appropriate
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 25 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the observations by our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading and _ infrastructure
development for the Phase 1 portion of the Pualani Estates Subdivision project that we
observed in the period covered in this report were performed in general conformance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical engineering
recommendations presented in the above referenced geotechnical engineering report
It should be noted the above statement of professional opinion is based on the
grades existing at the completion of the mass grading phase of the project in
assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the
slopes or finished grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing
period without our knowledge and written acknowledgment
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
