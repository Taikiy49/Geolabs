INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site and to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the foundations of the proposed
structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed building is located north of the
intersection of Beretania and Alexander Streets
The site is bordered by a 14 story building on the northeast
Alexander Street on the southeast Beretania Street on the
southwest and houses on the northwest
The site is presently occupied by several 1 and 2 story
residential structures and large trees
In general the site is quite flat and slopes down toward the
south The elevations at the site varied from 20 to 25 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a 17 story high reinforced
concrete structure approximately 110 by 120 feet in plan
Maximum column loads of about 1300 kips are anticipated

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
eR I AE ee RS 1 TRIES
The field exploration indicated fairly competent bearing
soils at the site Shallow foundations can be considered
for the proposed structure if some settlements can be
tolerated
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value
of 4 500 p s f may be used for footings resting on the
stiff near surface soil
J
Due to the relatively heavy building loads estimated settle
ments of about 5 to 6 inches are anticipated To reduce the
settlements it is recommended that the top 5 feet of soil
under the building site be removed and stockpiled for later
backfill use The site should then be proof rolled with a
heavy roller The footings should be placed at this level
about 5 feet below the existing ground surface With this
procedure the estimated settlements would be about 3 inches
total and 1 inch differential
The proof rolling performed upon the proposed foundation
bearing surface requires a minimum of 12 passes with a ten
ton vibratory roller capable of delivering a total impact
force in excess of 45000 foot pounds
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trenches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
Minor grading is planned for the site
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and
old foundations and sidewalks prior to filling
Select on site material should be used for fill and footing
backfill material The fill and backfill should be placed
in horizontal loose lifts of about 8 inches and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Due to the anticipated settlements the ground floor slab
should not be poured until the superstructure is completed
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
subgrade should be kept moist and recompacted prior to
placing the base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 45
may be used at the base of the foundations Additionally
a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may also be used

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations proof rolling
and backfill operations be observed by a soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 4 borings to depths of 28 to 36 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
