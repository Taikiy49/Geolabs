SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the southwest corner of
the intersection of Meheula Parkway and Kamaio Street in Mili
The site is roughly square in plan configuration and encompasses
an area of approximately 6 acres It is bordered on the north
by Kamaio Street on the east by Meheula Parkway and on the west
and south by existing residential lots
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1004 10 Page 3
The overall topography slopes gently towards the southwest at a
grade of less than 3 percent Ground surface elevations on the
Site range from 594 feet in the southeast corner to 612 in the
northeast corner Most of the lot is relatively level graded
area
A continuous drainage swale runs along the west and south sides
of the site with a concrete box drain located in the southeast
corner The maximum difference in elevation between the overall
relatively level surface and the bottom of the swale is 9 to 10
feet located at the concrete box drain
A concrete hollow tile wall about 6 to 7 feet in height runs
alog the west property line A wooden fence about 6 to 7 feet
in height runs along the south property line
During the time of the investigation there were no structures
present on the site Vegetation consisted of a sparse growth of
grass and weeds

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Schofield Plateau which is
an area between the Waianae and Koolau mountain ranges The
plateau was formed during the late Tertiary and early Quater
nary primarily by basaltic lava flows from the younger Koolau
Volcano banking against the eastern Slopes of the older Waianae
dome Since the cessation of volcanic activity chemical wea
thering has formed a thick mantle of residual soil derived from
the parent basaltic rock
Subsurface Exploration
For our current investigation three borings were drilled at the
site to depths between 16 0 and 16 3 feet Three attempts were
made to complete Boring 2 due to boulders encountered at rela
tively shallow depths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1004 10 Page 5
In our previous investigation five borings were drilled at the
Site to depths between 15 0 and 16 5 feet
The purpose of the borings in both investigations was to explore
the subsurface soil conditions and to obtain samples for labora
tory testing
Based on the information obtained from the borings the site is
underlain by hard to very hard red and brown clayey silts and
silty clays throughout the exposed depths There was no notice
able trend to the arrangement of the soil in comparing all of
the borings
Boulderly strata were encountered in two of the borings In the
original Boring 1 boulders were encountered from about 11 to 15
feet In the new Boring 2 boulders were encountered between 5
and 10 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during
either exploration
More detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the
borings are presented in the Appendix

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations encountered competent bearing soils at
the project site Laboratory soil tests indicate that the
on site soils have low swell potential therefore conventional
Slab on grade design may be utilized
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing foun
dations are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on hard in situ soil For foundations
bearing on compacted structural fill an allowable bearing value
of 2500 p s f may be utilized These bearing values are for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
The bottoms of footings should be placed a minimum of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building founda
tions
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small on
the order of less than one half of an inch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1004 10 Page 7
Site Grading
No current grading plan was available at the time of this inves
tigation It is anticipated that most of the site may be cut
from 1 to 3 feet Fill will be limited to the existing drainage
Swale and the southwest corner of the site
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior to the
commencement of earthwork All organic material debris and
other deleterious substances should be disposed of off site
Fill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose horizontal
lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 Moisture content should be
within 3 percent of optimum
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 ver
tical 5 1 benching and keying are required to properly bond
the new fill to the existing slope The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level conm
pacted layers in accordance with the above structural fill
requirements
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 cut and fill Slopes are
recommended

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be required at the proposed truck ramp areas
The wall backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
compaction to reduce future settlement of the concrete Slab
which may be placed over this backfill
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
select borrow should be used to backfill the area immediately
behind the walls Granular backfill is recommended since it
requires less compactive effort and therefore reduces the
build up of additional lateral pressure against the wall
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used to
minimize build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls
The walls may be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
equal to an equivalent fluid pressure of 40 p c f Additional
load due to surcharge loading should be included where appli
cable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 350 p c f can be utilized The passive
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1004 10 Page 9
resistance should be reduced by one third when combined with the
frictional resistance
Cold Storage Areas
It is anticipated that cold Storage areas are planned for the
proposed structures The freezing temperatures developed by
cold storage units could initiate frost action in the subsoils
In order to reduce the possibility of frost up lift action below
the cold storage units a capillary break layer is recommended
below the units to prevent upward migration of water and to act
as a drain for accumulated condensation from the unit
A clean gravel layer such as 3B Fine gravel is recommended
for the capillary break material The thickness of the capil
lary break layer would depend on the effectiveness of the insu
lation and depth of frost penetration Generally a minimum
thickness of 2 feet is recommended
Additionally a provision should be incorporated in the design
for draining condensation from the bottom of the capillary break
to prevent accumulation of moisture

Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of clean
gravel generally less than three fourths of an inch and larger
than one fourth of an inch in Size
To protect against possible moisture damage to the flooring
material the incorporation of a polyethylene vapor barrier
between the cushion and slab should be considered
Parking Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project parking
areas and service roads
Based on the laboratory CBR tests performed on the on site soils
during our previous investigation the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction The above design section
may be revised accordingly if other subgrade soil conditions are
encountered

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling operations be observed by
the soils engineer during earthwork construction particu
larly fill placement to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling to
the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
