In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface scil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing of the
swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the subdivision site
grading and preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The laboratory tests disclosed that the existing near surface soils
could exhibit moderate swelling potential if they are allowed to dry out
excessively and then wetted Therefore some special foundation design
and subgrade preparation are recommended in order to provide suitable
foundation support for the proposed residence
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f may be used on the
residual soil
GEOLABS HAWAIii

Post footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a
depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then
replaced with non expansive imported soils as previously discussed
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding

condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
