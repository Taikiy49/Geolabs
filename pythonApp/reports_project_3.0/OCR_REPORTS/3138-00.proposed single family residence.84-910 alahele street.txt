SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Maunaolu Makaha The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated to the west and downslope of Alahele Street At the time of our
site exploration the site was covered with dense brush although clearing of the site was
in progress Several piles of end dumped fill were observed on the lot close to Alahele
Street
The site slopes toward the west at a gradient of about 6 horizontal to 1 vertical
6H 1V Existing ground surface elevations range from approximately 450 feet above
Mean Sea Level at the west property line to about 516 feet along Alahele Street

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The Makaha Valley is a broad deeply eroded valley on the west flank of the
Waianae range Basaltic bedrock of the Waianae volcanic series is exposed on the steep
side slopes of the valley In the valley floor the basaltic rock is overlain by partially
consolidated alluvial deposits
Subsurface Exploration
Subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by excavating 5 test pits
to depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the test pits encountered cobbles and boulders with matrix material
consisting of dark brown and red brown clayey and sandy silts to depths of about 5 to
10 feet Boulders up to about 4 feet in diameter were encountered The matrix material
was generally found to have low plasticity and low expansion potential
Soil conditions encountered in test pit TP 2 were somewhat different than the other
test pits In test pit TP 2 silty clay with some cobbles and boulders was encountered to
a depth of about four feet 4 The silty clay appears to have high plasticity and based
on laboratory testing appears to be moderately to highly expansive The silty clay
appears to be a localized discontinuous layer
Very difficult excavation conditions in test pits 4 and TP 5 suggest that
either very large boulders or basalt bedrock was encountered in these test pits at depths
of about six 6 to seven feet 7

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed improvements should be feasible from a
geotechnical standpoint provided that our recommendations are followed Preliminary
grading plans indicate that the proposed house pad will straddle a cut fill transition
Considering the localized expansive soils encountered as well as the differing settlement
characteristics in the cut and fill portions of the pad we recommend that the upper 2 feet
of the house pad be constructed with low expansion select fill material Details regarding
the select fill are presented in the Site Preparation and Grading section of this report
Site Preparation and Grading
Clearing and Grubbing Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas
within the grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials Any
organically contaminated topsoil should also be stripped The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping The
existing end dumped fills on the eastern portion of the site may be incorporated into
proposed engineered fills provided these end dumped materials are free of vegetation or
other deleterious material
Graded Slopes We recommend that a keyway be constructed at the toe of the
proposed fill slope in accordance with the attached keyway detail Plate 3 The keyway
should be a minimum of 12 feet wide and extend at least 2 feet into dense material as
determined by the project geotechnical engineer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3138 00 Page 6
We recommend that the proposed cut and fill slopes be constructed no steeper
than two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V Surface runoff should be directed away from
cut and fill slopes to reduce the potential for erosion on the slopes
Over excavation Any soft weak or yielding areas within the proposed fill area
should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted engineered fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein
Fill Material Generally the on site non expansive soils should be suitable for use
as engineered fill provided over sized material greater than eight inches 8 in largest
dimension is screened or separated by other means A substantial amount of over sized
material should be anticipated based on our test pit exploration Oversized material can
be placed in open space used in non structural fill areas or reserved for landscaping
purposes Localized zones of expansive soil if encountered should not be used as
engineered fill Expansive soils should be used only in open space areas or should be
removed from the site
Fill Compaction All fill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 to 10 inches
in loose thickness and moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content The fill
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
House Pad The cut portion of the proposed house pad should be overexcavated
a minimum of 2 feet below pad grade and rebuilt to pad grade with non expansive native
fill compacted to 90 percent relative compaction as specified above The overexcavation
should extend at least 5 feet beyond the horizontal limits of the proposed structure

Compaction of Cut Areas Cut areas underlying the proposed garage driveways
and other pavements should be excavated to rough finish subgrade ripped to a minimum
of 6 inches and compacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as specified above
Foundation Support
It is anticipated that the proposed buildings will be supported on concrete
slabs on grade foundations The house slabs and their cushions may be supported by
fill placed and compacted as recommended above A six inch 6 layer of 3B Fine
gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
The garage slab located in the cut portion of the site may be supported by dense
native materials treated as described in the Site Preparation and Grading section of this
report A six inch 6 layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slab as a
cushion fill
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to any floor
coverings an impervious moisture membrane should be considered below the slabs
provided an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing
It is our recommendation that slab perimeter thickened edges be extended to a
minimum depth of 10 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 p s f may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on select fill or compacted native material This value may be
increased by one third 2 for temporary wind and seismic loads Spread footings or strip
footings should be embedded a minimum of one foot 1 below the lowest adjacent
grade

Excavation Conditions
As noted above cobbles and boulders ranging up to four feet 4 in diameter were
encountered during our site exploration Below depths of six 6 to seven feet 7 very
large boulders greater than 4 feet in diameter and or basaltic rock may be encountered

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations has been included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations The recommenda
tions given in this report are contingent upon our observation and testing during
earthwork construction This is necessary to allow the geotechnical engineer s continued
consultation and design input in the event that unforeseen conditions are encountered or
in the event that workmanship or constructability problems should arise If conditions
encountered during construction are different from those encountered in our exploration
and therefore assumed in the preparation of this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to determine pertinent
engineering properties of the soil materials sampled
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B I
