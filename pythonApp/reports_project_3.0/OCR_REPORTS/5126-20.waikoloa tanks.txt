Dear Mr. Moore:
In accordance with the project specifications and our recommendations, we have
completed the probing and grouting observations for the Waikoloa Tanks 300-2 and
300-3 project. This letter report summarizes our field observations made during the
probing and grouting operations performed for the new water tanks at the project site.
Project Considerations
The project site is located adjacent to the existing water reservoir along
Waikoloa Road within the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii. The project
consists of constructing two, 2.5-Million Gallon (MG) reservoirs as part of the Waikoloa
Beach Resort Development Plan and Potable Water System. We understand that a
paved access road will be constructed around the perimeter of the new reservoirs. In
addition, new water lines and other appurtenances are anticipated for the proposed
project.
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
probing and grouting operations at the new 300-2 and 300-3 waier tank locations only.
The intent of the foundation probing and grouting operations is an attempt to locate and
grout potential subsurface cavities and/or voids beneath the new water tanks.
Probing and Grouting
The 300-2 and 300-3 water tank locations and probe hole locations were
surveyed and staked out by the contractor, Isemoto Contracting Co., Ltd. The probe
holes were marked along the wall footings spaced at a maximum spacing of 10 feet or
less on center. Based on the project plans, a total of 175 probe hole locations were
marked out at each tank location on-site for the probing and grouting operations. At the
time of the probing and grouting operations, the existing ground surface was generally

The probe holes were drilled with an air-track drill rig. The probe holes drilled
were about 3 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 9 to 12 feet below the
existing ground surface. The depths of the probe holes drilled correspond to at least
10 feet below the bottom of the slabs-on-grade elevation. It should be noted that
9 additional probe holes were drilled in areas where probe holes required high grout
intake in an effort to delineate potential voids and/or cavities below the shallow
foundations. Therefore, a total of 359 probe holes (350 planned and 9 additional) were
drilled for the two water tanks, as shown on Plate 3.
In general, the materials encountered during the probe hole drilling operations
consisted of interbedded layers of a’a clinker and basalt formation. Based on our
observations during the probing operations, small cavities and/or voids were
encountered in 6 of the 359 probe holes drilled. Summaries of our foundation probe
drilling and grouting observations for 300-2 and 300-3 water tanks are provided on
Plates 4.1 through 4.4 and Plates 5.1 through 5.4, respectively, of this report.
Following the probing operations, each probe hole was fitted with a 2-inch
diameter PVC pipe to maintain the integrity of the open hole. After completion of the
probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled by injecting grout into the probe
holes at low to moderate pumping pressures (at the pumping pressures from a grout
pump). A total of about 15.3 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 359 probe holes
and cavities encountered. The maximum grout intake was located at Probe Hole
No. A-4a through A-4d, where approximately 1 cubic yard of grout was injected into the
probe holes.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations have been
conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications and the
recommendations contained in the geotechnical engineering exploration report. Based
on our field observations, it is our professional opinion that the cavities encountered at
the water tank locations probed have been grouted and that the subgrade area should
provide the recommended foundation support for the new water tanks.
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all the cavities
and/or voids present beneath the new water tanks. Therefore, some of the cavities
and/or voids may not have been detected even with the probing and grouting program
undertaken for the project. However, the level of foundation preparation undertaken for
