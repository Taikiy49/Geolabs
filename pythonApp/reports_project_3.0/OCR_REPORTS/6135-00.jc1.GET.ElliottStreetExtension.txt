The project involves planning and preliminary design for preparation of
RFP documents for Design Build procurement for the Elliott Street Extension within
the Honolulu International Airport The project locations and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This geotechnical engineering technical memorandum summarizes our findings
and preliminary geotechnical recommendations based on our analyses utilizing the
information obtained from our geotechnical data report prepared for the project Borings
drilled for this effort are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The findings and preliminary
recommendations provided in this technical memorandum are intended to assist the
project team in the preparation of the Request for Proposal RFP documents for the
proposed design build delivery process for the project

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island
The younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in
age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae Volcano
became extinct while Koolau Volcano was still active and its eastern flank was partially
buried below Koolau lava
The project site is on the southern flank of Koolau Volcano and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the glacio eustatic
fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and the genesis of the
Honolulu Coastal Plain
During the later Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the continental glaciers advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell to below its present
stand When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea levels rose to above its present level
Glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations affected the processes of erosion and
deposition The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposition of lagoonal and or marine sediments
in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to
carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments
and calcareous materials caused desiccation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials In addition renewed subaerial
erosion of the upper areas of the volcanic dome deposited terrigenous alluvial soils
under relatively high energy conditions

During periods of no significant sea level changes continued stream action
extended the alluvial delta and fans seaward by deposition of alluvium over the
previously deposited lagoonal sediments
The geology of the project area has been further complicated by the deposition of
recent pyroclastic materials and lava flows from eruptions of vents of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series which included the nearby Aliamanu Salt Lake Makalapa vent These
eruptions were contemporaneous with the Pleistocene sea level fluctuations
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft harbor sediments
along the coast in areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways Urban
development and reclamation projects have since altered the natural formation of the
area

Site Description
The Elliott Street Extension site is to the north of the existing Aircraft Rescue
Firefighting Facility ARFF near the intersection of Elliott Street and Kuntz Avenue
within the Honolulu International Airport on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site
extends through the existing parking lots from the intersection of Elliott Street and
Kuntz Avenue and further south into the existing Aloha Cargo aircraft parking area as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing ground surface within the project limits is primarily covered by either
asphalt concrete or Portland Cement Concrete pavements except the portion of the
proposed parking lots on bare ground conditions The existing Manuwai underground
box culvert traverses through the site in the north south direction Based on our site
reconnaissance and the topographic survey map the existing ground surface elevations
at the project site generally range from about 8 5 to 12 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the existing utility plans provided various underground utilities were
observed within the project site including fuel electric sewer drain and water

Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five borings
designated as Boring Nos 301 through 305 extending to depths of about 16 5 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the results of the field exploration the subsurface conditions consisted
of fill over alluvium and coralline detritus The fill materials generally comprised of
clayey and silty soils in a mixture of coralline sand and gravel in a stiff condition with
soft loose pockets in localized areas The thickness of the existing fill materials varied
form about 5 to 15 feet thickness generally increasing toward the south A layer of soft
to medium stiff alluvium was encountered in the far north end of the site Boring
Nos 301 and 302 with thickness of approximately 6 to 12 feet The coralline detritus
materials generally comprised of loose to medium dense coralline sands and gravel
extending to the maximum depth drilled of about 21 5 feet below the ground surface
We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at depths ranging from about
10 to 14 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The
groundwater levels measured corresponded to about Elevations 1 to 5 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
seasonal precipitation runoff and other factors
For illustration purposes only generalized subsurface profiles depicting the
interpreted subsurface conditions across the project site are provided on the
Generalized Subsurface Profiles Plate 3 The approximate surface location of the
subsurface cross section prepared for this report is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
This geotechnical engineering technical memorandum presents preliminary
foundation design for the Elliott Street Extension
We recommend a rigid pavement section consisting of 7 inches of Portland
Cement Concrete and 6 inches of aggregate subbase course for the design of the
Elliott Street Extension subjected to WB 50 vehicle similar to 5 axle semi trailer We
also recommend a flexible pavement section consisting of 3 inches of hot mixed asphalt
concrete over 6 inches of aggregate base course for design of the new parking lots
primarily subjected to passenger and pick up truck vehicles with occasional heavy
service trucks
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical recommendations for
the project design are presented in the following sections
3 1
Pavements

truck traffic such as WB 50 similar to 5 axle trailer We also understand that the
proposed 900 stall parking lots should be designed for passenger vehicles and light

distribution were not available at the time this report was prepared Design life of the
pavement was assumed to be 20 years

pavement design manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of
Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and Research Branch

design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design for
Concrete Highway and Street Pavements The flexible pavement design methodology

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site to reduce
the potential for contamination of the excavated materials Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft soils
should be disposed properly off site
After clearing and grubbing areas to receive fills and the finished subgrades in cut
areas should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to no less than 90 percent
relative compaction For those areas that will be subjected to traffic no less than
95 percent relative compaction is required Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend thoroughly moistening the soil to close the cracks or the subgrade
should be moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill

Corrosion Potential
Two sets of laboratory corrosion tests including pH and minimum resistivity
were performed to evaluate the corrosivity of the on site soils clayey silty and sandy
soil materials generally encountered The minimum resistivity values of the
near surface soil samples tested ranged from 160 to 2 000 ohm cm based on the
water saturated soil box method with pH value of about 7 5 These relatively low
minimum resistivity values correspond to Corrosion Ratings of 1 to 2 extremely
corrosive to very corrosive based on the guidelines provided by the City County of
Honolulu Board of Water Supply
Therefore we believe that the on site soils may be considered corrosive to
ductile iron pipe As a result we recommend properly designing all metallic
substructures such as ductile iron water lines and steel pipe in contact with the soils to
be protected against potential corrosion It may be appropriate to consult with a
professional corrosion engineer to review the test results and provide detailed
recommendations for corrosion protection
