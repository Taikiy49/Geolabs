INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the foundation and site grading design of the
proposed 1 0 M G storage tank at
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed tank site in order to provide
recommendations for use in the design of the site grading and
foundations of the proposed construction
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
August 12 1986

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Koloa on the island of Kauai
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The tank site is located in a canefield currently under
cultivation by McBryde Sugar Co The field is located immediately to
the north of the Koloa Lawai Road and the tank site is approximately
110 feet from the road
The site slopes moderately towards the east and is covered mostly
with mature cultivated sugar cane A plantation road running roughly
parallel to the Koloa Lawai Road transects the site The existing
ground surface elevation of the site varies from approximately 340 to
355 feet above Mean Sea Level To the south of the tank site the
topography drops into a drainageway which central axis trends towards
the east

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 1 0 million gallon capacity
reinforced concrete tank structure on the site The tank if
constructed will be approximately 96 feet in diameter and 23 feet in
height
The details of the proposed site grading were not known at the
time of our exploration However it is anticipated that 10 0 to
12 0 foot cuts will be required to create a level pad for the tank and
that minimal fills will be required for the portion of the access road
to the south and southeast of the tank structure
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano
built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic
Series during the late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years before
present Following the cessation of this main shield building phase
there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic
lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series Preliminary
reconnaissance of the site and its vicinity indicates that the area is
underlain by deep residual soils derived from the in situ weathering of
Koloa lavas
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling two 2 borings to a depth of 51 5 feet and one
1 hand auger probing to a depth of 10 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings and the
probing are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

In general the borings and probing at the tank site encountered a
surficial layer of very stiff to stiff reddish brown to mottled reddish
brown clayey silts with traces of sand approximately 6 5 to 8 5 feet in
thickness residual soil Below this residual soil the borings and
probing encountered medium stiff to stiff mottled reddish brown to
mottled brown clayey silts with some sand ranging in thickness between
12 0 and 15 0 feet Underlying this stratum similar soils were
encountered to the maximm depth drilled with an increase in moisture
content and consistency grading from stiff to very stiff These clayey
silts are saprolite soils which are soils derived from the in situ
chemical weathering of rock and retain some or all of the original
structures of the parent rock
Groundwater levels were encountered from 29 5 to 37 3 feet below
the existing grade at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration at the tank site encountered slightly
to moderately compressible soils with relatively high in situ moisture
contents To minimize foundation settlements cuts ranging from 5 to
12 feet are planned within the tank site to compensate for the weight
of the new tank structure and fluid loads
Tank Foundations
The borings indicated that at the proposed finished grade the
site is underlain by medium stiff to stiff mottled reddish brown clayey
silts saprolites which could provide adequate support for the
proposed tank loads

An allowable bearing capacity up to 2800 p s f may be used for
the design of foundations bearing on undisturbed in situ soils This
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic
loads are considered
Exterior wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to
utility trenches should extend down to a grade equal to or deeper than
the trench Alternatively the trench backfill which should be
completed first prior to foundation construction could be compacted to
95 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 to minimize
footing settlement later
All footing excavations should be properly backfilled and
compacted to 95 density per above specification
The site grading should also be designed to divert surface water
away from the tank foundations

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting into the
ridge about 5 to 12 feet at the tank site and a small amount of fill
and cut for the access roadway
Due to a combination of high annual rainfall and dense vegetation
the in situ moisture contents of the on site soils are generally high
35 to 50 Therefore some drying out of the excavated material may
be required to attain the proper moisture content for adequate
compaction Alternatively drier off site material may be imported for
use as fill
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the area within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
organic material debris and other deleterious material
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no major fill placement will be required at
the site of the tank structure However some minor amounts of
fill may be required for the access road Over excavation may be
required to provide level bench to receive fill
Fills should be moisture conditioned to near optimm moisture
content placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding eight inches
8 in loose thickness and compacted to 95 maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Slopes should be over filled at
steeper ratio and then cut back to the 2H 1V finish slope ratio

It is anticipated that the site grading for the reservoir
structure will require cuts of up to approximately 12 feet in
depth Cut slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical may be
used Slope benches should be provided at maximm vertical height
intervals of about 20 feet if the final grading plans will
require cuts in excess of 20 feet
Near the finished grade of the slope faces cutting should be done
with extreme care Overcutting beyond the design slope ratio
should be avoided as attempts to backfill and recompact overcut
areas would most likely result in loose areas which would be
subject to later damage by erosion If boulders or small ledges
of rock are encountered near the design slope it may be desirable
to carefully cut or break these materials to match the design
slope rather than risking damage to the slope by loosening the
soils adjacent to the boulders or ledges by hard excavation
Surface run off water from areas above the slope should be
diverted away from the cut slope bank and prevented from flowing
across the slope face The slope should be planted or otherwise
protected as soon as possible to minimize damage and unravelling
by erosion
Due to the relatively high rainfall occurring in the vicinity of
the project site and the nature of the soils encountered in the
borings it is possible that zones of seepage or intermittent
seepage may be encountered If so the geotechnical engineer
should be consulted for recommendations concerning the treatment
of the seepage

Roadway Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the project
roads
Based on the results of our soil exploration program the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction and the above design section revised
if necessary The preliminary recommended section considers light
traffic and is based upon the assumption that good drainage will be
provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading including cut and fills
foundation excavation and backfill placement be observed by the
geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction and cutting have been obtained and that the subsurface soil
conditions and seepage zones if noted are consistent with the design
assumptions and provisions
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations

principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other

information for use for contractors bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and in the

deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may

Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible extra costs

Laboratory Testing

samples as an aid in the evaluation and classification of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring

Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
natural samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils The
test results are presented on Plate B 4
Four Atterberg limits were performed on selected samples to
assist in the classification of the soils and to aid in evaluating
physical properties The results of these tests are presented on
