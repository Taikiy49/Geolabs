As requested, we performed Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing on
Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4 installed for the above project. Based on our field observations
and the CSL test results, the following recommendations are for your review and
consideration.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project specification, Section 511 — Drilled Shafts, requires performing CSL
testing on Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4 installed for the project. We performed the CSL testing
on April 21 and 22, 2020 for Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4, respectively.
Shaft No. 1.2 was drilled on February 25 and 26, 2020, followed by concrete
placement on February 26, 2020. Shaft No. 1.4 was drilled on February 27 and
28, 2020, followed by concrete placement on February 28, 2020. The approximate
locations of the shafts are presented on the Site Plan, Plate 1. Four 2-inch diameter
steel CSL access tubes were installed within each of the reinforcing cage. Details of the
drilled shaft as-built conditions are summarized in the following table:

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 
using the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest. Crosshole Sonic 
Logging tests were performed between all the possible access tube combinations in the 
drilled shaft (4 tubes resulting in 6 profiles). The northernmost access tube was 
designated as Tube No. 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise 
direction. 
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 5.8 and 
5.6 feet above the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for 
Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4, respectively. We understand that potable water was used to fill 
the access tubes after concrete placement. Based on our observation in the field, the 
water levels within the access tubes were above the top of shaft concrete in all access 
tubes at the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from 
the following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. 
The CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used 
with caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not 
accurately known. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, 
computed apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition are 
presented on Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are 
summarized on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube 
combination (profile) and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 
through 4.4. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10%, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less 
than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for 
Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4 with the exception of the areas within the profiles described 
below. It should be noted that changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate 
defects depending on the degree of the reduction and the associated increase in FAT. 
Shaft No. 1.2 (Profiles 1-2, 1-3, and 2-4): FAT increases from 
approximately 10.2 to 154.7 percent from approximately 0.0 to 3.7 feet 
below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.2 (Profile 3-4): FAT increases up to about 23.3 percent from 
approximately 4.2 to 8.6 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.4 (All Profiles, Except 2-4): FAT increases from approximately 
10.4 to 49.1 percent from approximately 0.1 to 5.1 feet below the top of 
concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.4 (Profile 3-4): FAT increases up to about 15.6 percent from 
approximately 10.6 to 15.6 feet below the top of concrete. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for 
Shaft Nos 1.2 and 1.4 ranged from approximately 7,341 to 14,804 and 10,716 to 14,134 
feet per second, respectively. Based on our review of the CSL testing data, we 
recommend performing concrete integrity coring for Shaft Nos. 1.2 and 1.4.  
For Shaft No. 1.2, we recommend that integrity coring be conducted between 
Access Tube Nos. 1 and 2, and between 2 and 4 to a minimum depth of 5 feet below 
the top of the drilled shaft concrete. In addition, we recommend an additional integrity
