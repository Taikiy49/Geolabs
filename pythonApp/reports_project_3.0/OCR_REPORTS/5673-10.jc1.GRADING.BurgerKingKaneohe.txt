Dear Mr Au
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering monitoring and testing
services performed during the slope restoration for the construction of the temporary
access road from September 9 to 25 2009 for the above project in accordance with your
request dated via e mail
The project site is below the rear parking area of the Burger King Kaneohe on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The slope is
approximately 40 feet in height and had experienced some severe erosion as a result of
the heavy spring season rainfall Some existing residential homes including wall
structures are near the toe of the hill slope A segmental retaining wall stabilization was
selected to remediate the eroded failed slope
A temporary access road was constructed through the over steepened slope to
provide access to the bottom of the slope for the construction equipment and working
platform The approximate project limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The temporary access road required backfill to restore the slope after the
construction completion The slope restoration used on site clayey soil with minimum of
90 relative compaction In addition imported sandy material was also used to blend with
on site high moisture soils The compaction was conducted using a sheepsfoot wheel
attached to a Komatsu excavator The geo grid was placed at the bottom of each 24 inch
lift with about an 8 to 15 foot embedment The geo grid was wrapped around the slope
face to the top of each lift with a minimum 3 feet of overlapping to the next geo grid layer
Royal Contracting Company performed the slope restoration Samples of the
backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with American Society of Testing and

Field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM D 6938
and D 3017 During the backfill operations the materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density tests
Periodic field density tests were performed during the backfill operations to confirm our
field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
observed
A total of 15 field density tests were performed using the nuclear density gauge
method in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during construction of the above project are summarized on
the attached Plate 4 Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the slope restoration operations
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general conformance with the intent of the design and our recommendations In
general areas assessed as unsatisfactory were re worked to achieve the specified
minimum compaction
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of monitoring and testing
services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the
tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
requirements and for repairing defects
