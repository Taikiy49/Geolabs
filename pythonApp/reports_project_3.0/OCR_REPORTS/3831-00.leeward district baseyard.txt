INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Leeward District Baseyard project located within the existing
Waipahu Intermediate School on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation and site grading only
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Waipahu Intermediate School in the
Waipahu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new shop
building approximately 60 feet by 100 feet in plan dimension We understand that the
building will be of steel framed construction with metal siding Also included are covered
asphaltic concrete parking and driveway areas
Detailed information pertaining to the structural loads was not available at the time
this report was prepared We anticipate that maximum column loads would be on the
order of about 50 kips with wall loads of about 2 to 5 kips per lineal foot

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3831 00 Page 4
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most
of their original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project site is located along the southern flank of Koolau The subsurface
conditions at the project site are composed of terrigenous alluvial soils deposited by
continuous erosion of the volcanic dome The base of the stratigraphic section in this
area is Koolau Basalt
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed Leeward District Baseyard project is located within the existing
Waipahu Intermediate School in Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain by
very stiff to hard silty clay fills over very stiff to hard alluvium extending to the maximum
depth drilled of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our field
exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing
on the very stiff to hard near surface soils may be used to support the proposed
structures
Our laboratory test results indicate that the near surface soils have a moderate to
high potential for shrink swell when subjected to changes in the moisture content
Therefore special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements
presented in the Slabs On Grade section of this report in order to reduce the potential
shrink swell effects on floor slabs
We anticipate minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less
than 3 feet will be needed in order to provide level areas for the structure

Foundations
The proposed baseyard facility will consist of a new shop building covered parking
areas and driveways Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our
exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings may be used to support the proposed structures
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footings bearing on the stiff near surface fills or alluvium This allowable bearing pressure
is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads
such as temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the
total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than
1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the fills and or alluvial soils These values assume that the
concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished
grade should be neglected
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3831 00 Page 7
Uplift loads especially for the carport structure may be resisted by a combination
of the following 1 dead weight of the structure 2 weight of backfill material above the
footing where applicable and 3 side shear between the sides of the footing and
surrounding soil Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above
the footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 105 p c f For side shear between the
sides of the footings and the adjacent soils a shear value of 150 p s f may be used to
resist uplift loads
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings are
to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should extend to
a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the
below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the
footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed shop
building The near surface soils encountered at the proposed site consist of silty clay fill
and alluvial soils which exhibit a moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore to reduce the potential of structural distress to lightly
loaded slabs we recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material be provided below the concrete slab The non expansive fill should
extend a minimum of 3 feet laterally beyond the edge of the slab The subgrades for
slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select fill recommended below the floor slabs should consist of
imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve All select granular fill materials should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction The slab subgrade should be kept moist until covered by
the cushion fill or base course
To provide more uniform support we recommend that the concrete slab on grade
be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer should consist of
open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which
will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill should be placed as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3831 00 Page 9
soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils as
recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we
recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane we
recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to
placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing
concrete
If concrete slabs for the shop building will be subjected to vehicular traffic we
recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch cushion fill layer and
2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate The
subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base
course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Gradin
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minor grading with cuts and fills on the order of less than 3 feet
relative to the existing ground surface may be expected for the proposed development
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs

soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our exploration
The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
grading work

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture
conditioned to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of improvements subjected
to vehicular traffic such as the parking and driveway areas the compaction requirement
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The on site silty and clayey soils generated from the excavation may be reused as
a source of fill for non structural areas The on site silty and clayey soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched

If imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material should
consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain between 10 and
30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for its intended use Imported materials should be brought to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Pavements
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the driveway and parking areas for the
proposed project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We
have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the silty and clayey soils encountered in the borings with a minimum CBR
value of 15 and swell of less than 1 percent Based on the above assumptions we
recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
6 0 Inch Subbase Course 95 percent relative compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 3 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction If pumping of the subgrade
occurs during construction the subgrade should be proof rolled to a minimum of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3831 00 Page 12
90 percent relative compaction The subbase and base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design section
The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage will be provided for
areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in
or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade below the base and or subbase course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a_ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or the
on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3831 00 Page 13
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface silty and
clayey soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the
footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils Four of the six samples tested were
undisturbed while the remaining two samples were remolded prior to saturation The
results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade support
Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 3
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the near
surface soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
