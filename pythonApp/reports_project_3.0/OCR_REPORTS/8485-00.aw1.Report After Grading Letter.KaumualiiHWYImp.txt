Dear Mr. Gonzales: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the site grading of the Kaumualii Highway Intersection 
Improvements at Waimea Canyon Drive project in the District of Waimea on the Island of 
Kauai from May 4, 2022 through July 27, 2022 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
April 15, 2022.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves improvements to the existing intersection at Kaumualii 
Highway and Waimea Canyon Drive, located in the town of Waimea on the Island of 
Kauai, Hawaii. The improvements project consists of installing a new catch basin and 
drain line along the north side of the highway, widening portions of both Kaumualii 
Highway and Waimea Canyon Drive to provide additional turn lanes, new sidewalks, 
and a traffic signal system. A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on 
Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
performed during the site grading at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from May 4, 2022 through 
July 27, 2022. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 
5 feet for utilities and foundations, and minor fills for new pavements and sidewalks to 
achieve the finished design grades.

At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Goodfellow Bros. was the general contractor and 
self-performed the site grading. During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site 
soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. For the 
pavement areas, the existing subgrade materials were recompacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. Imported granular fill materials from Kauai Concrete and 
Rock utilized below sidewalks and roadways were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent 
relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil 
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. 
Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the 
maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction was obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities, which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill and 
backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above 
grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading work for the Kaumualii Highway Intersection 
Improvements at Waimea Canyon Drive project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the 
period covered by this report, was performed in conformance with the general intent of the 
recommendations in the following references:
