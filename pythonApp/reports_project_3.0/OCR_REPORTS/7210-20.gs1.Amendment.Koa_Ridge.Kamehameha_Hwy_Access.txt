This amendment consists of incorporating the additional boring performed at the cut 
slope area for the above project. The Site Plan, Plate 2, was revised to incorporate the 
change in rockfall mitigation system. The revised Site Plan and additional boring log and 
laboratory testing performed are attached. The recommended rockfall mitigation system is 
provided below. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
We explored the subsurface conditions at the cut slope transition area by drilling and 
sampling one boring, designated as Boring No. 101, extending to a depth of about 40.1 feet 
below the existing ground surface. 
The boring generally encountered residual soils about 35 feet deep underlain by 
basalt rock formation extending to the maximum depth explored of 40.1 feet below the 
existing ground surface.  The residual soils consisted of medium stiff to hard silty clay and 
clayey silt. The basalt rock formation was hard. 
We did not encounter groundwater in the boring at the time of our field exploration.  
However, it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall, 
time of year, seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, and other factors.

ANCHORED WIRE MESH SYSTEM 
Due to the soil conditions encountered in the boring, we recommend installing an 
anchored wire mesh system for the cut slope transition area between Sta. 19+00 and 
20+00. Anchored wire mesh system is a slope treatment that involves the installation of 
steel wire mesh panels anchored onto the slope face with soil/rock nails in an effort to 
prevent the soils and rocks from moving/falling under the long-term rockfall protection 
system. The primary intent of this stabilization system is to closely pin the wire mesh to 
the slope face as a retention system. The use of the anchored wire mesh system is 
recommended at the cut slope between Sta. No. 19+00 and Sta. No. 20+00. 
The slope to be anchored with steel wire mesh must be trimmed of vegetation and 
scaled of loose rocks prior to the installation of the anchored wire mesh system. The 
anchored wire mesh system should be installed prior to the installation of the draped wire 
mesh. Detailed descriptions of the individual components of the anchored wire mesh 
system are presented below: 
High Strength Wire Mesh 
The wire mesh should be alloyed high strength carbon steel wire with a minimum 
tensile strength of 256,000 pounds per square inch (psi). The wire mesh should be 
woven construction, diamond shaped, and made with 4-mm (0.157-inch) diameter 
wire. The ends of each wire should be formed into a loop and should be twisted.  
The loops of the wire mesh should be fastened together to prevent unraveling of 
the mesh.  The wire should be galvanized with a zinc/aluminum coating with a 
minimum weight of 200 grams per square meters (0.655 ounces per square feet).  
The coating should be 95 percent zinc and 5 percent aluminum. 
Compression Claws (Press Claws) 
The compression claws (also known as press claws) should be 6-mm (0.24-inch) 
diameter carbon steel bar and hot dipped galvanized with a minimum layer 
thickness of 85 microns. The smaller (standard) compression claws should be 
used to fasten the mesh panels together, and the bigger (special) compression 
claws should be used to fasten the mesh panels to the boundary ropes. 
Spike Plates 
The spike plates should be made from 10-mm (0.4-inch) thick steel and should be 
hot dipped galvanized with a minimum layer thickness of 85 microns. The spike 
plate should be diamond shaped with a width of 190 mm (7.5 inches) and a length 
of 330 mm (13 inches). 
Boundary Ropes 
Boundary ropes should have a diameter of 12 mm (0.5 inches) and should be PVC 
coated.  The rope should be 6 by 19 construction, IWRC and galvanized with a

Grouted Soil/Rock Anchors 
The grouted soil/rock anchors should consist of Dywidag GEWI bolts with a 
diameter of 32 mm (1.25 inches), Titan 30/11, IBO R38 or equivalent.  The grouted 
soil/rock anchors should have a corrosion allowance of 2 mm (78 mils) zinc 
galvanization included in their diameter. 
The anchored wire mesh system and all associated hardware, such as the high 
strength steel wire mesh, compression claws, and spike plates should be powder coated 
with a black pigment or another color as directed by the owner. Boundary wire ropes 
should be PVC coated in black unless directed otherwise by the owner. All other exposed 
steel elements should be painted with a black colored rubberized paint. The following 
provides a brief outline of the anchored wire mesh installation procedures. 
1. 
Trim slope face of vegetation, loose materials and protruding rock outcrops. 
2. 
Stakeout grouted soil/rock anchors in general accordance with the project 
spacing requirements taking into account the low spots. 
3. 
Excavate the dell for pre-tensioning of the anchored wire mesh before 
drilling the grouted soil/rock anchors. 
4. 
Install the grouted soil/rock anchors (anchor heads should not project above 
the terrain line or predominant slope line). 
5. 
Lay the erosion control matting on the slope. 
6. 
Lay the steel wire mesh panels on the slope over the erosion control 
matting. 
7. 
Join or seam the steel wire mesh panels together. 
8. 
Fit the boundary wire ropes around the perimeter of the anchored wire mesh 
system. 
9. 
Install the spike plates and pre-tension with a torque wrench or hydraulic 
press to the specified forces. 
10. 
Hydromulch all exposed cut slope areas after mesh installation. 
Based on our evaluation for the slope, we recommend that the soil/rock anchor be 
a minimum of 22 feet long, with horizontal and vertical spacings of about 6 and 7 feet, 
respectively. The drilled hole for the soil/rock anchor should be a minimum of 4 inches in 
diameter. The grout and/or epoxy resin should be non-shrink and should have a minimum 
unconfined compressive strength of 4,000 psi or greater. The grouted soil/rock anchors 
should have an ultimate pullout capacity of 14 kips.

The soil/rock anchors should be set in low spots, where possible, in order to pull 
the anchored wire mesh into the depressions and against the ground. This will avoid major 
hollow spaces and reduce excessive quantity of vegetation material during greening of 
the slope face. If the nail pattern does not permit setting soil/rock anchors in low spots, 
supplemental (short) anchors may be installed to achieve this purpose. The supplemental 
grouted soil/rock anchors should consist of Dywidag GEWI bolts with a diameter of 20 or 
25 mm (0.75 or 1.0 inches), Titan 30/16, IBO R25 or equivalent with a minimum 
embedment length of 8 feet. 
Because of the exposed island environment, which is very conducive to corrosion, 
the useful life of the anchored wire mesh system could be highly variable, depending on 
the quality of the steel wire mesh and hardware, corrosion protection, and workmanship 
employed during the anchored wire mesh installation. 
A minimum of ten percent of the grouted soil/rock anchors should be proof tested. 
Proof test should be performed by incrementally loading the anchor to a maximum test 
load of 150 percent of the design pullout capacity. An electronic load cell should be used 
to measure the applied load to the anchor. The anchor head movement during the test 
should be measured and recorded.
