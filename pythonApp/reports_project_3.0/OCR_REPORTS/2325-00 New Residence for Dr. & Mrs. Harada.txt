SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Manoa Valley area of Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii
The site is a single family residential lot having dimensions of
about 95 0 by 88 5 feet and was formerly occupied by a residence which was
demolished prior to our exploration
The topography is nearly level with a very gentle slope towards
Parker Place Ground surface elevations of the site range from about 99
to 102 feet above Mean Sea Level

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soils rock data set for use
in analyses for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design and construction of the proposed
residence
Foundations and Slabs
Our exploration encountered moderately expansive soils at the footing
subgrade elevations on the project site These soils are ina naturally
moist state and should be protected fram drying to reduce the potential of
their dehydrating during the construction phase of the project and then
subsequently swelling after construction
Spread foundations should be embedded a minimm of twenty four 24
inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable bearing capacity of
2 000 p s f may be used for the design of the house spread footing
foundations
Slabs on grade should have thickened edges extending a minimm of
twenty four 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade A minimm of
twenty four 24 inches of select non expansive material should be used
under the slabs and basaltic termite barrier A vapor barrier membrane
should be incorporated between the slab and cushion if necessary to
protect floor coverings and or reduce the potential of moisture
infiltration into interior spaces

Site Grading
The plans provided to us indicate that the project will require fills
of about one 1 to two 2 feet thick for the construction of the
proposed residence To facilitate the placement of the non expansive
select material below the slabs we recommend that the building area be
cut down to a uniform grade allowing for the recommended thickness of
select material The excess material generated by this excavation may be
used for landscaping and yard fill
After the site has been cut down the in situ soils should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to about
three 3 percent above optimm moisture content and compacted to 85 of
its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should then be brought to subgrade level by the placement of
non expansive select material in eight 8 inch compacted lifts at 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 The select

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
sitework excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observations wells Variations of condition
between the field data points may occur and the nature amd extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points shown on the topographic survey shown on
Plot Plan by Kimura Ybl Associates Inc Elevations of the data points
were approximately determined by estimation and interpolation from the
same plan The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil type
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points is given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report This data has been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in
rainfall tides temperature and other factors
