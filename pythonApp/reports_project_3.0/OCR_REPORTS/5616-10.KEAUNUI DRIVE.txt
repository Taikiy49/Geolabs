Dear Mr Lee
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading and roadway and utility construction for the
Keaunui Drive Kapolei Parkway to Fort Weaver Road project from August 4 2006 to
May 21 2007 in accordance with our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is west of Fort Weaver Road east of Kapolei Parkway and south of
Geiger Road in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The roadway project
extends Keaunui Drive from Fort Weaver Road to Kapolei Parkway for a distance of
approximately 2 500 linear feet We understand that the new roadway will service future
residential and commercial areas A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on
Plates 1 and 2 1 and 2 2 respectively
The new roadway consists of two 12 foot wide traffic lanes in each direction with a
landscaped median area In addition 6 to 10 foot wide sidewalks were constructed The
roadway right of ways are about 104 to 114 feet wide The site grading work involved fills
from about 2 to 5 feet in height with finished roadway grades ranging from about 25 to
28 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Utilities were also constructed along the new roadway These utilities included drain
water and sewer lines The drain lines consisted of Reinforced Concrete Pipe RCP from
24 to 66 inches in diameter The sewer lines consisted of 12 inch Polyvinyl Chloride PVC
pipes The water lines consisted of 12 inch diameter portable and 16 inch diameter R 1
water lines

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Keaunui Drive
Kapolei Parkway to Fort Weaver Road project from August 4 2006 to May 21 2007 The
site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills
on the order of about 2 to 5 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades
Royal Contracting Company Ltd performed the site grading and utility installation work
During the site grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The
compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 21 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references
