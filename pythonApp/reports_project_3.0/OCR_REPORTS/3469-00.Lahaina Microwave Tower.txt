REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Maui is composed of two shield volcanoes West Maui and
Haleakala built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the Pleistocene Epoch
East Maui or Haleakala Volcano which is much larger was formed later and banked
against the West Maui mountains A flat isthmus connects the two volcanoes The
proposed project site is located on the West Maui Mountains
The very permeable and primitive Wailuku Volcanic Series of West Maui were
formed during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene time from the extrusion of basaltic
lavas along two main rifts and many fissures The area is underlain by highly
weathered materials derived from in situ weathering of the Wailuku lavas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed microwave tower site is generally
underlain by medium dense sands and dense sandy gravels at shallow depths In
general we believe that the proposed microwave tower may be supported on a mat
foundation bearing on the recompacted sands and gravels encountered at shallow
depths We estimate the total foundation settlements under the anticipated light
structural loads to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements between the center
and the corner of the mat to be on the order of inch
For a drilled pier foundation design we believe that a deep foundation system
consisting of a 5 foot diameter drilled pier may be used to support the proposed
microwave tower structure for the subject project Based on the subsoil conditions
encountered and the structural loads provided a preliminary pier length of about 15 feet
below the existing ground surface is recommended to develop the required
compressive and lateral load capacities for the subject pier Where the basalt rock is
encountered at a depth less than 15 feet below the ground surface the drilled pier
should be socketed a minimum of 2 feet into the underlying basalt rock formation
Temporary casing may be needed to reduce the potential for caving in of the
hole due to the sandy and gravelly materials encountered in the boring during our field
exploration It should be noted that the performance of the drilled pier depends
significantly upon the contractor s method of construction construction procedures and
workmanship Therefore special attention should be given to the Construction
Considerations section of this report Detailed discussion of our geotechnical
engineering design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report

Mat Foundation
Based on the generally medium dense to dense near surface soil conditions
encountered at the project site we believe that a mat foundation bearing on the
recompacted near surface soils may be used to support the proposed microwave tower
structure The requirements for subgrade preparation are discussed in the Site
Preparation section of this report
Based on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500
pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design of a shallow foundation bearing
on the recompacted sand and gravel materials encountered near the ground surface
This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 4 for
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces For mat foundation
design a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection p c i may be used for the mat foundation bearing on the recompacted
sands and gravels The subgrade modulus is the modulus of vertical subgrade reaction
for a 1 foot square test plate
The bottom of the mat foundation should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent finish grade Deeper embedment may be required to provide
the required capacity against uplift and moment reactions at the base of the tower If
foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soils around the mat are well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced
for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slab the passive
resistance from the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction
of 0 4 may be used between the mat and the recompacted sand and gravel materials
Resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the dead weight of the structure
and the mat foundation Additional resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the
weight of the backfill material placed above the foundation if applicable A unit weight
of 120 pounds per cubic foot p c f may be used for the compacted on site sand and

Drilled Pier Foundation
In order to develop the anticipated vertical and lateral load capacities the
proposed new microwave tower may be supported by a foundation system consisting
of a cast in place concrete drilled pier embedded in the upper dense sands gravels and
underlying basalt rock formation The cast in place concrete drilled pier would derive
support principally from skin friction between the drilled pier and the surrounding soils
End bearing capacity of the drilled pier has been discounted due to the potential for
caving in of the sandy soils which would preclude obtaining a clean bottom during
construction
Based on the data obtained from our field exploration and laboratory testing a
drilled pier with a minimum diameter of 5 feet and a length of approximately 15 feet
below the existing ground surface is recommended to develop an allowable
compressive load capacity of 30 kips This value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Load bearing contribution from the upper 5 feet of subsoils below the finish pad
grade has been neglected Where the basalt rock is encountered at a depth of less
than 15 feet below the ground surface the drilled pier should be socketed a minimum
of 2 feet into the underlying basalt rock formation
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead weight of the structure
the weight of the drilled pier and by side shear along the pier surface and the adjacent
soils For intermittent uplift forces an ultimate uplift capacity of 40 kips may be used
to resist uplift loads for the drilled pier Groundwater was not encountered in the boring

Construction Considerations
The performance of a drilled pier will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures In our opinion the following
conditions would have a significant effect on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled
pier foundations
Based on our field exploration the proposed new microwave tower location is
underlain by sandy and gravelly materials in the upper 12 feet To reduce the potential
for caving in of the drilled hole temporary casing for the upper 10 to 12 feet of the
granular soils may be needed during construction of the drilled pier Care should be
exercised during removal of the temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking
of the concrete pier Therefore a minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be
maintained above the bottom of the casing during removal of the casing
The load carrying capacity of a drilled pier depends to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled pier and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling
the pier hole and in placing concrete into the hole A low shrink concrete mix with high

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography at the proposed microwave tower location we
anticipate that minimal site grading will be required for the project construction At the
on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of off site
All areas beneath the future tower mat if this option is selected should be
excavated to the future foundation subgrade level If zones of soft loose or saturated
soils are encountered at the excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be
required to expose firm material The sand and gravel materials resulting from the
over excavation may be stockpiled and re used as a source of backfill material provided
that they are less than 3 inches in maximum particle size
Foundation subgrade should be proof rolled with a vibratory drum roller or similar
equipment and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Generally the on site soils
should be suitable for use as engineered fill If additional imported material is required
the material should conform to the requirements for Select Borrow for Subbase Course
specified in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
dated September 1986 Any imported fill material should be tested and approved for
its intended use by our firm prior to delivery to the project site
Site preparation operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to
evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and

Drainage
The finished grades outside the microwave tower should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations should also be avoided Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off
away from the foundation
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the foundation immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
