SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Diamond Head Road Master Plan project in the District of Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations for the light
poles only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The Diamond Head Road Lighting System project site is located along Diamond
Head Road about 1 500 lineal feet long near Diamond Head Lookout The project
consists of the construction of approximately 12 to 14 light poles for a new lighting system
along Diamond Head Road The general location and vicinity of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
Volcanic activity from Koolau Volcano ceased for nearly 2 million years Volcanic
activity once again resumed from mid Pleistocene Epoch and has lasted through Recent
geologic time These volcanic events known as the Honolulu Volcanic Series have
formed cinder spatter tuff and ash cones throughout the southern portion of the Island of
Oahu
One of the most prominent vents of the Honolulu Series is Diamond Head
Diamond Head is a tuff cone and was generally formed by hydromagmatic explosions
Materials within the tuff cone consist of magmatic debris blocks of Koolau basalt and
fragments of coral limestone torn from the underlying reef The project site is located on
the southern flank of Diamond Head
2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located between Beach Road and Palaoa Place on the mauka
side of the Diamond Head Road in the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Diamond Head Road is an asphaltic concrete AC paved two lane road with one
discontinued parking lane on the makai side The overall site is generally flat Two
lookout areas were observed on makai side of the site The project site contains retaining
walls abutting the ocean wood pilings grass bushes and trees were observed along the
mauka side of the site Coral fills were observed in the unpaved areas along the road

Light Pole Shaft Foundations
Based on the loading conditions provided and the subsurface conditions
encountered we recommend that the light poles be supported by shaft foundations
Based on the preliminary design of the light pole foundations 30 inch diameter
drilled shafts were considered in our analyses The major considerations in our
foundation analyses included the lateral loads and ground line overturning
moments induced in the shaft foundations
In general lateral load resistance for shafts is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the
shaft and induced moment in the shaft Based on a maximum horizontal deflection
of inch at the top of the shaft subjected to ground line overturning moments and
lateral forces we evaluated the required shaft lengths using the computer software
program LPILE Plus for Windows This program is a microcomputer adaptation of
a finite difference laterally loaded pile program originally developed at the
University of Texas at Austin The program solves for deflection and bending
moment along a shaft under lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis was
carried out with the use of internally generated non linear p y curves to represent
soil moduli The lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil
moduli at various depths The analyses were performed for laterally loaded 30 inch
square and 30 inch diameter cast in place concrete shafts The results of our
engineering analyses are summarized in the following table

Light Pole Construction Considerations
The performance of the shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures As a result we recommend
that a representative from Geolabs be present to observe the installation of the
drilled shafts during construction In our opinion the following may have a
significant impact on the effectiveness and cost of the shaft foundations
The load carrying capacity of shafts depends to a large extent on the contact
between the shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling or
excavating the shaft holes and in placing concrete in the holes The bottom of
shafts should be cleaned of loose or disturbed material prior to constructing the
foundation The bottom should also be fairly level The concrete should be poured
neat against the excavation sidewalls Backfill against the shaft foundations should
not be allowed
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch slump range should be used
to provide close contact between the shafts and the surrounding soils The

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths ranging from about 15 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
