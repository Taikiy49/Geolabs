INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
service performed for the proposed warehouse for Suen at 207
North Cane Street Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 This service was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated August 31 1989
The findings of the exploration and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations are based on the soil data obtained during our
exploration These recommendations are provided for design input and may
not be complete for other purposes such as construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
A one story steel framed warehouse is planned to be built adjacent to
an existing approximately 30 foot high one to two story building along
the westerly property line In general the hollow tile wall of the
adjacent building structurally appears sound
This report was prepared utilizing three 3 sheets of a set of
drawings entitled 207 North Cane Street Building Designated A 1 A 3
and A 4 which were provided by Lou Chan Associates Inc Drawings A 1
and A 3 were revised on August 31 1989 Drawing A 4 is not dated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil data and
to provide geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site
grading and foundation design of this project

W O 2389 00 Page 3
The work was accomplished in general accordance with our proposal
dated August 31 1989 The scope of work generally consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts
1
logging and monitoring of the drilling and sampling of three 3
borings to a depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface
Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the general site conditions
Review of available geotechnical and geological information
pertinent to the project site and its vicinity
Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the
field exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
the geotechnical engineering guidelines for site grading and
foundation design of the proposed project
Preparation of this report to present our general findings and
recommendations
The details of our field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located on the west side of North Cane Street and about
300 feet north of Kilani Avenue in Wahiawa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The site is a rectangular shaped area extended 180 feet along

the North Cane Street and 34 feet wide It is bounded on the north by a
damaged residential hollow tile perimeter wall and on the west by an
approximately 30 foot high existing one to two story building with its
hollow tile wall probably built along the property line The southern
portion of this site is a recently completed concrete parking lot
The site is generally level and covered with weeds bushes and some
construction debris
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling of
three 3 borings to a depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of these borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Generally the three 3 borings indicate that the site is underlain
by residual soils consisting primarily of medium stiff to stiff clayey
silt to the maximum depths explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our
field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 Laboratory tests were performed
on selected soil samples obtained The test results are presented in

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
It is our opinion that from a geotechnical engineering point of view
the proposed field exploration generally indicated competent subsurface
soil conditions to support the proposed warehouse using spread footing
foundations and slab on grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2389 00 Page 5
The field exploration generally encountered stiff residual soils
below the site to the 20 foot depth explored our laboratory testing data
indicate that these soils generally have low to moderate shrink swell
potential
The proposed building planned will be built immediately adjacent to
an existing approximately 30 foot high structure along the westerly
property line Therefore the design and construction of this project
should be performed with considerations to maintain the structural
integrity of this adjacent building It is doubtful that the as built
foundation details of this adjacent building can be obtained It may be
possible that the adjacent structural foundation may protrude into this
project boundary Therefore to minimize construction delay in case the
footings of the adjacent building do encroach into this site provision
should be made in the contract document on how to resolve this unforeseen
conditions in the field Some flexibility should also be provided to
determine the final new footing embedment to match the level of these
existing footings located along the westerly property line
Site Grading
The grading plan of the proposed project is not available at the time
this report was prepared However minimm site grading is anticipated
It is our opinion that the site should be properly graded to drain water
away from the foundations
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of vegetation and deleterious materials
The resulting grub and spoil materials should be properly disposed
off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak or
yielding areas exposed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and then backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2389 00 Page 6
Generally the on site soils appear suitable for use as engineered
fill Since most of the on site soils are fine grained they should be
compacted by mechanical compaction Compaction by water jetting or
ponding is not suitable for the on site soils and shall not be used
If additional imported material is required it should consist of
select non expansive material which contains no fragments larger than
three 3 inches in maximum dimension less than 25 passing the 200
sieve and a Plasticity Index less than 20 per ASTM Test Method D 4318 84
Areas requiring fill should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered during
re compaction should be over excavated down to firm ground and then
replaced with properly compacted engineered fill material
The fill should be placed in the maximum 9 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum ary density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
project An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches may be used for spread footings
bearing on the very stiff natural soils or compacted fill material
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than
one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential

If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plan extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
For the footings located along the adjacent structure the footing
bottom should be lowered to match those of the adjacent structure
For other perimeter footings to be located along the other property
lines and particularly adjacent to adjacent lots which are lower than
the project site further embedment of these affected footings should
be considered to minimize future disturbance by later excavation work
which might undermine or influence the structural integrity of the
current project structure
The bottom of all footing excavations should be re compacted to
obtain a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 prior to placement of the reinforcement The
footing excavation should first be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been
obtained prior to concrete placement
Iateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure against the buried vertical faces of the foundation
system and by frictional resistance developed between the bottom of
the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 250 p c f per foot of depth This
assumes that the soil around the foundation is properly compacted A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used to determine the sliding
resistance of the wall

Along the Westerly Property Line
In addition to the general recommendation as above mentioned in this
section the spread footings and continuous wall footings at this
portion of the site should be embedded to the same footing depths of
the adjacent building along the westerly property line Generally
the allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f recommended above may also
be used for these proposed footings It is our opinion that the
additional stress imposed on the soils beneath the footings of the
adjacent building should be within tolerable limit of the stiff
underlying soil
Since the as built foundation details of the approximately 30 foot
high adjacent building including information such as soil bearing
pressure used size and embedment of the footing are not available
it will not be possible to determine the proper final embedment of
the proposed footings located along the westerly property line to
match those of the adjacent structure Also provisions should be
incorporated in the contract documents to handle the situation if the
foundation of the existing adjacent building encroaches into this
project site
Caution and care should be exercised and maintained during
construction of the proposed footing foundations along the westerly
property line to maintain the structural integrity of this existing
adjacent building
Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade may be feasible for the floors of the
proposed buildings We recommend that a minimum six 6 inches of
imported non expansive select borrow material compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maxim dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 should be
placed below the slab The select material should consist of quarry
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2389 00 Page 9
waste select borrow or coral materials free of organic matter debris and
other deleterious material Overlying the above select borrow fill a
4 inch cushion layer consisting of 3 Fine gravel should also be provided
below the slabs The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to
concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration ana
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier might
be considered to be installed below the slabs
The slabs on grade should have thickened edges which extend down to a
minimum depth of 12 inch below the outside finished grade
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
recommended that gutter systems be installed on the proposed buildings and
that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement
areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should also
be avoided
Planters or landscaping next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are generally essential for the proper
performance of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or
loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or footing slab inmediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Utility
At the time this report was prepared the information of the existing
underground utilities were not available Cautions and care should be
exercised and maintained during construction to secure these existing
utilities from damage
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of four 4 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B
Fine is recommended to be placed under the new utility pipes
On site soils or selected material may be used for the trench
backfill However the backfill from the bedding up to about one foot
above the pipes should be granular material 3B Fine gravel coral sand
or equivalent which does not require excessive tamping
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to finished grade should be placed in maximm 8 inch
horizontal lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground
subsidence

Cesspools
There may be buried abandoned cesspools on the project site The
available records should be examined to establish their locations Any
cesspool encountered on the site should be opened cleaned of loose soft
materials and backfilled with granular material such 3B Fine to a level
of two 2 feet below finished subgrade Above this level the cesspool
excavation should be filled with on site clayey silt soils and compacted
to 85 relative densities per the previous section Site Grading
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed project should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction This review is intended to check if the earthwork and
foundation design concepts as presented in this report are properly
included in the project design and specifications We can not assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the discussions and
recommendations presented herein
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services Guring construction of this
project This is to observe the field compliance with the geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in this report and to advise
necessary design changes whenever the unanticipated subsurface conditions
encountered prior to or during construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from a limited number of borings
Variations of subsoil conditions between the borings may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If these variations become evident it will be necessary to
update the recommendations given in this report

The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Site Plan provided by Lou Chan Associates
Inc revised August 22 1989 Their accuracy should be considered only
to the degree implied by the method used Since the topography of the
site is not available at the time this report was prepared the elevations
of the existing grade are unknown
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
