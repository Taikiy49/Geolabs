SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern corner of the
intersection of Hihimanu Street and Ahiki Street in
Waimanalo Oahu
The proposed project site is about three 3 to five 5
feet lower than Hihimanu Street The topography at the
site is relatively flat The ground elevation varies from
about 26 to 31 feet The area is covered with brush and
small trees An occupied house and shack is located on
the southern corner of the project Barbed wire fences
cross the site
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed gymnasium structures are part of the planned
Waimanalo District Park
The proposed gym will be a one story steel framed structure
with CMU walls approximately 80 by 130 feet in plan A
gymnastic meeting building approximately 95 by 110 feet in
plan is planned adjacent to the proposed gymnasium A locker
room approximately 30 feet by 65 feet in plan is also proposed
Estimated maximum column loads of about 120 kips are anticipated
Minor grading is planned for the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Seven borings were drilled at the site to depths of 16 5
to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate
the subsurface conditions and obtain samples for labora
tory testing The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The boring locations
were based on the preliminary building layout Subsequent
to the field exploration the gym building was split into
two 2 smaller buildings and relocated Slightly from the
previous site
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by a surface
layer of moderately expansive stiff dark brown and brown
Silty clay to a depth of approximately seven feet Below
this surface layer is a stiff brown to mottle brown clayey
Silt with pockets of gray clay A layer of mottle brown
sandy silt was encountered at about 28 foot depth to the
bottom of the boring
Groundwater was noted at 12 7 to 14 5 feet below the ground
surface in the three 31 5 foot borings

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is situated over a thick deposit of alluvial
soils The field exploration disclosed fairly competent
subsoils at the site Shallow foundations can be used for
the proposed structures The surface soils are moderately
expansive and should be removed a minimum depth of 1 5 feet
below the slab subgrade and replaced with select non expansive
material
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or com
pacted select fill material over stiff ground This
value of bearing capacity is for total of dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary
lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
Because of the expansion potential of the on site silty
Clay soil the bottoms of all footings should be placed
a minimum depth of 24 inches below the outside finish
grade The concrete should be poured neat against the
in situ soil and all additional footing backfill should
be done as soon as possible to avoid water ponding around
the footing locations

The subgrade soils below slabs Sidewalks and pavements
should be kept moist prior to concrete placement in order
to prevent drying of the soils and reduce future swelling
Soft or loose pockets encountered in the footing excavations
should be removed and replaced with concrete or granular
material compacted to obtain 95 percent relative density
per ASTM D 1557 method
Surface water should be diverted away from building
foundations
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend
to the bottom of the trenches
For the above recommendations it is estimated that settle
ments will not exceed three fourths inch total and three
eights inch differential
Site Grading
Minor site grading is planned to level the site for the
proposed structures
Prior to filling and foundations construction the site
should be cleared of all vegetation and debris

The on site surface soil is moderately expansive and should
be removed for a depth of 1 5 feet below the Slab subgrade
and replaced with select non expansive off site borrow
material This overexcavation and replacement should be
extended for a distance of five 5 feet beyond the slab
perimeter area The subgrade soils should be kept moist
and not allowed to dry prior to concrete placement
Fill and backfill should be placed in 9 inch horizontal
loose lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Slab on Grade
Slab on grade may be utilized over a minimum of 1 5
feet of compacted non expansive fill Slabs should be
placed over a 4 inch layer of base course generally less
than 3 4 inch and larger than 1 4 inch in size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Sidewalks
Sidewalks located outside of the above recommended over
excavation and replacement areas should be placed over a
minimum of six 6 inches of select borrow cushion material
and close joints should be utilized to reduce the effects
of the moderately expansive soils The subgrade soils below

Pavements
The on site surface soils are clayey CH MH soils and
moderately expansive Based on the laboratory CBR test
results the recommended pavement section over these
soils is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils when the location and final elevations of
the roadway pavements are determined
Where fill is required to build up the existing ground to
the final finished elevation select material should be
used for the upper portions of the embankment in order to
reduce the required pavement section
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soil engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the pro
ject plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the fill
placement to monitor the contractor s grading opera
tions
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit
determinations were performed on Selected samples as an aid
in the evaluation and classification of Soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the Strength characteristics
of the subsoils The test result is plotted on Plates B 1
and B 2
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed
ring sample to evaluate the consolidation characteristics
of the soft recent alluvium materials The consolidation
curve is presented on Plate B 3
Three swell tests on one inch thick ring samples with
55 p s f surcharge load were performed on the near surface
soil to evaluate its swelling potential The test results
are summarized on Plates 3 and 4
One CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
on site upper material to evaluate the material as pave
ment support The test results are summarized on Table 1
