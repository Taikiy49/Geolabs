SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Auditorium Renovation at the Roosevelt High School in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our
field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The recommendations
presented herein are intended for the design of foundations slabs on grade and site
grading only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Roosevelt High School campus on the
northern corner of the intersection of Mott Smith Drive and Nehoa Place in Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to renovate the existing school auditorium
building
As part of the renovation a new workshop cooling tower electric rooms fan room
and new restroom facility are planned on the east west and north sides of the existing
auditorium building We anticipate that the new development will be lightly loaded
buildings on the order of one to two stories high Based on the demolition and grading
plan provided we envision that site grading for the proposed construction will involve
minor cuts and fills of less than 2 feet relative to the existing ground level
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was utilized to develop
an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations slabs on grade and site grading
for the new workshop and restroom facilities The work was performed in general

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epoch by the activities of two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Koolau
Volcano is the younger of the two volcanoes and reached the end of its main shield
building phase about 2 million years ago Koolau Volcano was built by the extrusion of
basaltic lava from northwest to southeast trending rift zones These lava flows are
interbedded pahoehoe and a a flows with pahoehoe being the dominant form After the
cessation of the main shield building phase of Koolau Volcano there was a long period
of erosion
During the Pleistocene Epoch there was a renewal of volcanic activity with the
eruptions of vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series Along the crest of the ridge on the
westerly side of Manoa Valley there are the Tantalus Roundtop and Sugarloaf vents
When these vents were active one or more lava flows from these vents spilled down
the westerly wall of the valley and moved across the valley floor The lava flows ponded
and filled the lower portions of the valley The ponding allowed the lava to densify as it
cooled forming dense massive rock
Other eruptive events of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled these lava flows
with pyroclastic material particularly sand sized cinder materials Subsequent fluvial
action has transported minor amounts of alluvial soils onto the site During the
development of the school campus man made fills were placed to grade the site to its
present condition
The project site is underlain by thin deposits of mixed alluvial and colluvial
materials consisting of clay with sand over cinder sand Basalt rock formation of the
Koolau Volcanic Series forms the stratigraphic base of the region and is believed to
underlie the near surface deposits

2 2 Site Description
The project site is situated within the Roosevelt High School to the north of the
intersection between Mott Smith Drive and Nehoa Place in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The proposed developments are located on the east west and north sides
of the existing school auditorium building as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The site for the new workshop building is observed to be located on a slope on the
east side of the existing auditorium building The sites for the new cooling tower new
electric rooms and fan room are located on an existing concrete sidewalk along the north
side of the auditorium building The new restroom facility is planned at the north side of
the existing concrete courtyard located on the west side of the existing auditorium building
Based on our field observations and a review of the topographic survey plan
transmitted by CDS International on the site is gently sloping with existing
ground surface elevations of approximately 134 to 148 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
2 3 Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
extending to depths of approximately 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface layer of fill about 4 5 to
10 feet thick The fill materials encountered consisted of very stiff clayey silts with sand in
Boring No 1 and loose to medium dense sands with cobbles in Boring No 2 The fill layer
was underlain by medium dense to very dense silty sands and gravel extending to the
maximum depths drilled of approximately 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that the groundwater level is subject to fluctuation due to rainfall events
seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and other factors occurring off site which
were not apparent at the time of our field exploration

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by a layer of fill materials
consisting of very stiff clayey silts and loose to medium dense sands with cobbles The fill
layer is underlain by medium dense to very dense silty sands and gravel extending to
depths of about 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not
encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils and or compacted fills may be used to support the new workshop
and restroom facilities In addition we recommend that slabs on grade be supported on at
least 6 inches of select borrow subbase to cap the near surface granular materials to
maintain the surface granular materials in a compacted state due to the nature of the
granular materials at the site Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of
foundations slabs on grade site grading and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing
on the recompacted in situ soils and or compacted fills be used for support of the
proposed new workshop and restroom facilities An allowable bearing pressure of up to
2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on
the recompacted in situ soils and or compacted fills This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent ground level
Based on the information obtained from our subsurface exploration it should be
noted that we encountered about 10 feet of loose fill materials consisting of loose to
medium dense sands with cobbles in Boring No 2 Therefore loose materials may be

3 2 Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade are planned for the proposed new
workshop and restroom facilities As mentioned previously the project site is generally
underlain by cinder sands Due to the nature of cinder sands it would be difficult to
maintain the compacted cinder sands in a dense and unyielding condition at the ground
surface due to the lack of confinement Therefore we recommend that the slab subgrade
be over excavated a minimum of 6 inches and the resulting over excavation should be
backfilled with the select borrow subbase compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to cap the cinder sand materials
In addition we recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction before placement of the select borrow
subbase The select borrow subbase should consist of crushed basaltic aggregates
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill material such as
open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide
uniform support of the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to the floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel
cushion layer
It should be noted that the area adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly
against the slab edges with compacted on site soils and graded to divert water away from
the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slab and foundation

from our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar
to those encountered in our field exploration The following recommendations are
intended to provide guidelines for the design of site preparation work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials generated from demolition should be removed and disposed of off
site After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm and or dense ground and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Prior to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a
depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
In general the on site cinder sands generated from the excavations may be reused
as a source of fill and backfill materials provided that the materials are screened of
over sized materials greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension and are free of
deleterious materials Where used as fill and backfill materials the on site soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction

Permanent fill slopes at the project site may be designed with a slope inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched into the existing slopes to enhance stability
We envision that imported fill materials may be required for the project In general
imported materials should consist of non expansive materials such as crushed coral
basalt or cinder sand The imported material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or
less Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
3 4 Utility Trenches
We envision that new utility connections may be needed for the project In general
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of open graded gravel
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the
initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
the pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper backfill
material around the pipes and improper placement of the backfill could result in settlement
of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site sandy soils or
imported granular materials The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

3 5 Drainage
The finished grades outside the workshop and restroom facilities should be sloped
to shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for excessive water
infiltration into the subsurface
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsurface
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to
drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling two borings extending to depths of approximately 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples to aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected
sample of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 1
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate B 2
One one inch ring swell test was performed on relatively undisturbed sample to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under a surcharge pressure
The results of this test are summarized on Plate B 3
