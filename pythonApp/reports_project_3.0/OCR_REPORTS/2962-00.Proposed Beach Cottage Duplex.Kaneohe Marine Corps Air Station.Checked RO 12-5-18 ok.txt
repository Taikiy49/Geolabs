INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed beach cottage duplex at the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air
Station in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2962 00 Page 2
recommendations presented herein are intended for foundation design and site grading
The findings and recommendations presented herein may not be appropriate to form the
basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the northwest portion of the Kaneohe Marine Corps
Air Station in Kaneohe on the windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed
to construct a beach cottage duplex at the site The proposed cottage will have plan
dimensions of approximately 35 by 41 feet A lanai is planned on the north side of the
cottage as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the cottage will be a one story wood framed structure of post
and beam construction supported by spread footing foundations The proposed cottage
will be lightly loaded Concrete slab on grade are not anticipated for this project We
understand that the project also involves construction of an access road of approximately
150 feet in length The access road will most likely be paved with asphaltic concrete

Air Station on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded on the northwest

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our geotechnical exploration sandy soils with coral gravel were
encountered at the proposed cottage location from the surface to the maximum depth
explored The sands generally grade from medium dense to dense with increasing depth
On this basis the proposed beach cottage duplex may be supported on a conventional
shallow foundation system bearing on the near surface sandy soils
The near surface soils encountered in Boring No 4 consist of reddish brown silty
clays We have assumed that the proposed access road will traverse this area and have
provided preliminary pavement recommendations based on the silty clays for subgrade
soils
Recommendations addressing the above considerations and other geotechnical
aspects of the project are discussed in the following sections of this report
Foundations
We understand that the cottage will be a single story wood framed structure of
post and beam construction supported by spread footing foundations Allowable bearing
pressures of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for footings bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2962 00 Page 6
on the in situ near surface sands A footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade is recommended
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Passive resistance due to
passive earth pressure equivalent to fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot of
depth may be used A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the footings
and supporting subgrade
Footing settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch with
differential settlements on the order of 14 inch
It is recommended that the bottom of all footing excavations be compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose medium dense to dense in situ
sand The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557
Footing excavations should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior
to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing conditions
and the embedment depths
If footings are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2962 00 Page 7
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Site Grading
We anticipate site grading for the proposed cottage will consist only of minor work
to provide a level building pad for construction The following guidelines should be
observed during the grading operations
1 Prior to the commencement of the earthwork the areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
trees debris and other deleterious material snould be removed and
disposed of properly off site
2 Any loose weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
3 Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of eight inches
8 moisture conditioned to at least two percent 2 above optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2962 00 Page 8
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use
by the project geotechnical engineer before delivering to the site If import
fill is needed import fill should consist of granular material and be should
be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than three inches
3 in largest dimension The material should contain less than 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
5 All fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth and
essentially unyielding surfaces
6 The finished grade outside the building should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding
Pavement Design
It is anticipated that the project also involves an access road of approximately 150
feet in length which will most likely be paved with asphaltic concrete At the time this
report was prepared the location of the access road had not been finalized We
anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist of passenger cars only We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the reddish brown silty clay surface soils described in Boring No 4 ff site
grading exposes soil other than that assumed we should perform additional tests to
confirm or revise the recommended pavement sections for actual field conditions If
imported fills are used for the proposed roadways the subgrade on which the pavement
structural section is to be placed will need to have a minimum California Bearing Ratio
CBR of 14 Based on the above assumptions the following pavement sections may be
used for preliminary design purposes

Asphaltic Concrete Pavement

The subgrade soils for the pavement section should be scarified to a depth of at
least eight inches 8 moisture conditioned to at least two percent 2 above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
site grading or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping
in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of two
inches 2 into the subgrade soil below the baserock to reduce migration of landscape
water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed
to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the proposed project A granular bedding
consisting of six inches 6 of selected borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should be used
for the trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2962 00 Page 10
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material and
placed in maximum ten inch 10 horizontal loose lifts mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 to reduce the potential for future
ground subsidence
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids to
evaluate conformance with the intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations
provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations presented
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during project construction to observe footing subgrade preparation
This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared Geolabs Hawaii should
be accorded the opportunity to provide construction observations to confirm our
assumptions in providing the recommendation presented herein
Any imported material required should be tested by the project geotechnical
engineer for conformance with the project specifications prior to hauling to the project
site
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations and elevations of the field borings are approximate having been
estimated from the Topographic Survey for Proposed Beach Cottage along Palakilo
Street prepared by Towill Shigeoka Associates Inc dated March 17 1993 The
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Suzuki Morgan Architects
Ltd and their project consultants for specific application to the project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is
expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the preparation of foundation design for the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
to form the basis for preparation of construction bids A contractor wishing to bid on this
project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of
this report and or in the performance of site specific exploration for bid estimating
purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
GEOLABS HAWAII

or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
