In accordance with our proposal letter dated May 25 1982 we
have performed a soil investigation at the above referenced lot
to evaluate the shallow subsurface soil conditions in order to
provide recommendations for the design and construction of the
foundation for the proposed Single family residence
The investigation consisted of three hand auger probings
visual observations laboratory soil tests and a review of the
Field notes during the site grading of the subject lot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is situated adjacent to the southwestern side
of Kamehameha Highway in Kaluanui The lot which is relatively
level at about elevation 6 to 7 feet is a part of the Kaluanui
Residential Subdivision
There is an existing CRM lined drainage ditch along the western
side of the lot
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story wood structure with
the floor level about 4 feet above the existing lot grade
The foundation system consists of CMU bearing walls along 2
ends of the structure with individual spread footing foundation
For the remainder of the house

Driveway and garage pavement is not anticipated
SOIL CONDITIONS
A review of the subdivision grading operations indicated that
the lot is underlain by about 3 feet of stiff clay 111 over
2 feet of dense fine sand fi11 underlain by loose to medium
sand natural deposit to an undetermined depth
Water level was encountered at about 5 foot depth
Three shallow hand auger probes were drilled in the proposed
house site in order to obtain samples of the upper moderately
expansive clay fill material Laboratory swell tests on these
samples produced ring swell values of 2 5 to 12 9 depending
on the surcharge weight used
The field exploration and a review of the previous grading
operations indicate that the subsurface conditions at the site
can be generalized as follows
0 3 feet stiff clay moderately expansive fill
3 5 feet fine sand fi11
5 feet and deeper fine sand loose to medium
natural deposit
The site is situated in the coastal high hazard district and
may be subject to flooding As a result the allowable bearing
capacity footing embedment and lateral soil resistance must be
designed for the bouyant forces and potential scouring caused
by the flood condition
In accordance with Flood Ordinance No 80 63 the footing foun
dations should be embedded a minimum depth of 3 feet below the
existing grade to resist loss of footing support due to scour
ing of the soil
At this level 3 foot depth an allowable bearing value of
about 3000 p s f could normally be utilized However due to
the buoyant soil weight condition during flooding we recommend
that the allowable soil bearing value be reduced to 1500 p s f
The on site soil is moderately expansive however laboratory
swell tests indicate that the expansion potential is small if
the surcharge load is about 1000 p s f or more Since the
allowable bearing pressure is 1500 p s f Significant soil
swelling is not anticipated
