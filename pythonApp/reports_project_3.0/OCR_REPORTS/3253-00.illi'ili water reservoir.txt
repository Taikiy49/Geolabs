PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new Il ili Water Reservoir is to be located in the southwest portion of the island
of Tutuila American Samoa The proposed project consists of the construction of a one
million 1 MG water tank located west of the Golf Course Road and north of the Airport
Road The proposed reservoir will be approximately 80 feet in diameter
The site is located in a relatively flat area in the Tafuna Plains area of the island
At the time our field work was conducted the tank location had not yet been finalized
However we understand that the proposed water reservoir would be located within a 150
foot radius from the center of the site as shown on the Observation of Probe Drilling

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our services for this project was to observe the probe drilling and
grouting for the proposed new water tank at the subject site The probe drilling was
completed in addition to the preliminary test pit exploration which was confined to the
upper 2 to 4 feet of the ground surface performed by Foundation Engineering
The probe drilling consisted of drilling five probe holes to depths of about 20 feet
below the existing ground surface The probe holes were drilled to evaluate the
subsurface basalt rock formation for the potential existence of voids and cavities below
the water reservoir location Specifically the drilling was observed by a geologist from
our firm to log the drilling rates and potential presence of voids within the rock formation
The probe holes were drilled with a large water well rig provided by the American
Samoa Power Authority ASPA advancing a 6 inch diameter air percussion hammer
Upon completion of the drilling the probe holes were to be backfilled with a specified
grout mix to aid in filling any voids encountered during the probe drilling However the
grouting operations were not observed by our geologist due to time constraints since the
grout was not available at the time of our field work
Since the final tank site was not available at the time of our field work the five
probe holes were confined within a circular area of approximately 150 feet in diameter as
shown on Plate 1

FINDINGS
Based on our observation of the probes drilled it appears that the proposed water
reservoir site has relatively uniform strata at the probe locations The probes confirmed
the presence of 1 to 2 feet of brown clayey silt soil volcanic ash at the surface of the
site An abrupt contact with the underlying basalt formation was encountered in the
probes beneath the soil horizon Based on the cuttings observed at the ground surface
the rock formation appeared to consist of a hard slightly weathered gray and brown
vesicular basalt with seams of loose to moderately hard brownish clinker material Loss
or reduction of air circulation was noted while drilling through the clinker seams
Within Probe 4 and 5 a loose zone with possible voids was encount
ered consistently at depths of about 7 to 9 feet below existing ground level Return air
circulation was observed to be greatly reduced or lost at about these depths In
addition abrupt drops of the drill rods were observed which generally represent the
existence of voids within the rock formation The voids appeared to range in vertical
dimensions from about 2 to 6 inches with the exception of Probe No 2 where a possible
void of about 18 vertical inches was noted by observing the fall of the drill rod
As previously noted we were not able to observe the grouting operations At the
time this report was prepared the only probe hole that had been grouted was Probe
No 2 Through conversations with a representative of ASPA who was present during
the grouting of Probe No 2 a total of about 3 cubic yards of grout was injected to bring
the grout level up to about 6 feet below the ground surface The significant quantity of
grout injected into Probe No 2 appears to confirm the presence of voids within the
subsurface Based on discussions with Mr Hauser of ASPA grouting of the remaining
borings will continue at a later date when equipment and grout are available

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general foundations for water reservoirs are highly sensitive to differential
movements As a result the critical item for design of foundations for water reservoirs
is to provide uniform soil or rock subgrades on which to construct the reservoir We
envision that the foundation for the proposed water reservoir will consist of a perimeter
ring footing with isolated column footings supporting the roof In addition we envision
that the concrete for the slab on grade will be constructed monolithically with the footings
for the proposed water reservoir Therefore it is necessary to provide uniform foundation
subgrade conditions for the footings and the slab on grade
Foundations
Based on the preliminary recommendations provided by Foundation Engineering
and our observations at the site we believe that the proposed water reservoir may be
supported on a shallow foundation system bearing entirely on the basalt rock formation
Alternatively the water reservoir foundation may be supported entirely on a minimum of
12 inches of compacted engineered fill As previously indicated it is essential that the
footings bear on one type of foundation material to reduce the potential for differential
movements between differing subgrade conditions In addition we recommend that the
slab on grade for the proposed water reservoir bear on a minimum of 18 inches of
compacted engineered fill
If the entire foundation is to be founded on basalt rock it should be noted that
clinker layers exposed at the bottom of the footing elevation will need to be removed and
replaced with lean concrete Clinker layers where encountered at the footing subgrade
will need to be over excavated to expose the basalt rock maximum 2 feet below the
footing subgrade and be replaced with lean concrete to provide a uniform rock like
condition for the foundations

Footings bearing on the basalt rock or on compacted engineered fill may be
designed with an allowable bearing pressure of 4 000 pounds per square foot p s f
This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces Footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Where
footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings
should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the
bottom of the below grade structure
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used between the base of the footings and bearing
materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f These values assume
that the concrete for footings is placed directly against well compacted fill materials or
cast neat against basalt rock Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
Probe and Grout
Based on our observation of the probe drilling and preliminary grout take in Probe
No 2 we recommend a program of probing and grouting of potential voids and clinker
zones beneath the tank foundation be implemented during construction The foundation
probing and grouting should consist of drilling 3 inch diameter probe holes 10 feet on
center around the perimeter ring footing of the tank and at the center of each column

below the planned bottom of the planned footing elevation If voids or loose clinker
seams are encountered during the probing operation additional probes should be drilled
at closer spacings to help delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the potential void
The probe holes and any voids discovered should be backfilled with a sand cement grout
injected at low to moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of 1
part sand 1 part cement and 0 7 parts water
Because of the potential for encountering large voids at this site we recommend
that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding In
addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting
operation is complete Since there is a potential for encountering voids at this site the
contractor should be made aware that a longer lag time between probing grouting
operations and foundation construction should be allocated in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the presence
of voids and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take and or
changed conditions are observed
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials for example the surficial ash layer should be removed and disposed of
properly off site to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials

The near surface soil material should be removed from the foundation area and
the underlying basalt rock formation should be exposed The resulting over excavation
should be backfilled with select granular fill material compacted to not less than 95
percent relative compaction The exposed basalt rock subgrade should be scarified to
a depth of 8 inches if possible moisture conditioned and compacted to 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
The fill and backfill materials to be used as select granular fill material should
consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension
Because the on site materials are potentially expansive the on site materials should not
be used as fill or backfill materials
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in
place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to evaluate
whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification
process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those assumed in
the preparation of this report

Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to
advertisement for bidding This review is necessary to evaluate conformance with the
intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations provided herein If this review

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the foundation excavation
subgrade preparation and foundation probing and grouting operations This is to
observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications and or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated during design The recommendations presented in this
report are contingent upon the above observations
Ifthe actual soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
