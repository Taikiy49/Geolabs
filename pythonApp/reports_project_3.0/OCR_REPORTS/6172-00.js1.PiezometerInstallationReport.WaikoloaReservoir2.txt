As requested, we installed the Vibrating Wire Piezometer (VWP) designated as 
PZ-1 at the Waikoloa Reservoir No. 2 project. The following contains the as-built 
information for the installed PZ-1 and summarizes the field installation activities and initial 
VWP reading. 
A borehole was drilled at Station 5+25, surveyed by others on September 7, 2010, 
as shown in VWP Location Plan, Plate1. A Mobile B-53 drill rig equipped with a 6-inch 
hollow stem auger was used to advance the borehole to the specified depth, as shown in 
Photograph No. 1. Drilling began at 11:00 and completed at 13:00. The elevation of the 
ground surface was located at about +3,359 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
In general, stiff fill material consisting of damp to moist clayey and silty soil with 
localized cobbles and boulders was encountered and extended to a depth of about 
27 feet below the ground surface. The fill material was underlain by stiff residual soil to 
the maximum depth drilled of about 32 feet below the ground surface, equivalent to 
elevation about +3,327 feet MSL. We did not encounter groundwater in the borehole at 
the time of drilling. 
At the completion of the drilling, the 6-inch hollow stem augers were removed from 
the borehole. The VWP was installed and grouted on September 8, 2010. Prior to 
installation, the filter stone of the VWP was saturated under water for about 20 hours. The 
VWP was installed into the borehole with tip pointing upward to maintain the saturation, 
as shown in Photograph Nos. 2 and 3. The following presents the backfilling procedures: 
1) 
Bentonite added from Elevation +3327 to +3329 feet MSL; 
2) 
Water added to borehole to hydrate the bentonite; 
3) 
Filter Sand added from Elevation +3329 to +3330 feet MSL with water for 
saturation; 
4) 
Piezometer “Zero” reading was obtained and recorded; 
5) 
Piezometer installed into the borehole and held at Elevation +3331 feet 
MSL; 
6) 
Filter Sand added from Elevation +3330 to +3332 feet MSL with water for 
saturation; 
7) 
Bentonite added from Elevation +3332 to 3334 feet MSL;
