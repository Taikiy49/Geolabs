Preliminary Geotechnical Site Assessment
Portion of Minami Parcel along Luluku Road
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request this letter presents a preliminary assessment of
the site for subdivision development from a geotechnical engineering point of view The
opinions presented are based on our previous experience at the site and its vicinity
The subject site is situated on the western side of Luluku Road southwest of the
existing Keapuka Subdivision and near the Hoomaluhia Park entrance The proposed
development area is approximately 2 acres
The topography at the site slopes down toward the west from Luluku Road
which is the high point of the area From Luluku Road the ground surface slopes down
for about 10 to 15 feet to a relatively level area encompassing the majority of the site
This lower flat area is generally wet with two areas of constantly flowing water The
surface water flows toward the north into an existing concrete channel The site is
presently covered with a thick growth of grass with some banana trees
The subsurface conditions generally consist of a thin surface crust of alluvium or
fill over very soft mud which may extend to depths of 5 to 20 feet below the existing
ground surface Groundwater level is generally within a few feet of the surface The
general area is also known to have artesian water conditions
It is assumed that at least 5 feet of fill would be required to raise the site above
the high water level Since the ground conditions are soft and wet a stabilization layer
consisting of a geotextile and 18 to 24 inches of coarse gravel would be necessary to
provide a stabilized layer for earthmoving equipment Subdrains and culverts would

probably be necessary to transmit the surface and subsurface water to the concrete
channel The weight of the new fill would cause consolidation of the soft soils resulting
in ground settlements for years after the filling is completed In order to accelerate the
settlements and reduce the waiting time a surcharge fill of about 5 feet high could be
used This may reduce the settlement waiting time to about 6 months
The above procedure would probably be required for all of the lowlying area
within the site which is underlain by the soft soil However a small portion of the site
which is above approximately elevation 180 feet may not be underlain by the soft soil
and therefore may not require this stabilization an surcharge
Another constraint within the site is the existing Board of Water Supply water line
which traverses the site from Likelike Highway to Luluku Road This water line is
probably 5 to 10 feet below the ground surface and within the soft soils The addition of
new fills near this water line would probably initiate settlement and possible damage to
the water line One possible method to reduce the influence of the new fills on the
water line would be to install temporary sheet piles along the water line down to a
depth below the soft soils This would isolate the water line from the settlements of the
adjacent ground The sheet piles could be removed once the ground settlements have
stopped
The above opinions have been presented for use in the evaluation of the
feasibility of developing the site for a residential subdivision and are not sufficient for
actual design of the site grading
