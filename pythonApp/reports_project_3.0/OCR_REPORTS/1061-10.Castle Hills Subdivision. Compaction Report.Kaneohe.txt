Following the recent fill slope failure near Lots 131 and 132
additional borings and probings were made near the slip and in
the upper drainageway area above the slip to determine the extent
and strength of the soft subsoils The locations of the borings
and probings are shown on the attached sketch
Findings
Previous borings drilled in this area for the soils investigation
encountered soft brown clayey silt of alluvial origin
The new borings encountered different soil conditions consisting
of very soft black organic clays and silts marsh deposits to
depths of up to about 49 feet below the existing ground surface
Cone penetration tests were performed in the probings and
indicated that these organic soils have very low shear strength
and are significantly weaker than anticipated based on the
previous borings

center of the drainageway are highly organic and are much weaker
than the soils encountered in the previous borings
