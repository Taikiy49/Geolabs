Bridge Foundation
Several options for the bridge foundation were considered in our analyses The
use of H piles was considered for the bridge foundation However because of the
presence of boulders encountered in our borings it is our opinion that it may be difficult
to drive the H piles through boulders into the underlying basalt formation without
predrilling the pile locations Predriiling the H pile locations will reduce the lateral load
capacity and lateral stability of the H piles and will likely require grouting of the
predrilled holes

Construction Considerations
Casing of the drilled piers will likely be required during construction due to the
presence of sandy soils and boulders encountered in our borings Casing will be
needed to keep the drilled pier holes open without significant sloughing and or caving
in of the sandy soils and boulders into the pier holes In addition coring through the
boulders and into the underlying basalt formation will likely be needed to embed the
drilled piers into the basalt formation
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling
the pier holes and in placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing
drilling fluids is not recommended because the fluids could saturate and weaken the
surrounding soil above the groundwater level Since these are end bearing piers the
bottom of the piers should be clean and free and loose materials prior to placement of
concrete A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be present to observe the
installation of drilled piers including drilling rebar cage placement and concrete
placement during construction
A low shrink high slump concrete mix should be used to provide close contact
between the pier shafts and the surrounding soil Since groundwater was encountered
above the basalt formation we recommend that the concrete for the drilled piers be
placed by wet construction method such as the use of a tremie pipe A minimum of
5 feet of concrete head should be maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe at
all times during the concrete placement to facilitate displacement of the groundwater
in the pier hole The concrete should also be placed in a suitable manner to reduce
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix

SITE GRADING
The subject site is located in Kahului on the island of Maui Hawaii A general
layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1 The earthwork operations
were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company The site grading consisted of clearing and
grubbing over excavation of the uncontrolled fill materials for the building pad and parking
areas placement and compaction of engineered fills and utility trench excavation and
backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations in order to provide the 2 feet of
engineered fills at the building pad and parking areas the exposed subgrades were
generally over excavated to about 1 foot below the finish subgrade elevation Then the
subgrades at the over excavated level were scarified to a depth of about 12 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
