PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The high school site is
situated approximately at the southwest corner of the future Makai Villages 7 8 residential
development site The high school site encompasses an area of approximately 40 acres and
is bounded to the north by Kapolei Parkway to the east by the future Makai Village 8 to the
south by the Kapolei Lower Channel and to the west by Fort Barrette Road as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1

MASS GRADING
The mass grading operations for the Kapolei High School site were performed by Oahu
Construction Company under a separate contract In general the mass grading operations
consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation of undocumented fills and placement and
compaction of fills The depths of excavations and thickness of embankments at the site were
on the order of about 2 to 8 feet Much of the fills needed at the site to raise the pre existing
grades to the design mass grading grades consisted of various sources of imported soil
materials Field monitoring services for the mass grading operations were provided by our
office and were documented in our grading report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Observation and Testing Services Kapolei High Schoo Mass Grading Kapolei Oahu Hawaii
dated
FINE GRADING AND BUILDING CONSRUCTION PHASE 1
Subsequent to the mass grading operations performed by Oahu Consiruction
Company additional fine grading and roadway and utility construction for the Kapolei High
School project were performed by Royal Contracting Company Ltd The fine grading work
consisted of placement and compaction of fills on the order of about 2 to 6 feet thick to achieve
the design finished grades for the project
During the fine grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in

results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
Following the fine grading operations the existing ground was generally over
excavated where necessary to approximately 12 inches below the finished subgrades in the
building areas for Phase to provide the recommended 12 inches thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2
percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and
preparation the footing subgrades were compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing stee and concrete
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
noted A total of 1 746 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and
D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 85 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the fine grading foundation
excavation Phase and roadway and utility construction for the above referenced project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
March 24 1999 and subsequent recommendations
