above referenced project in the Kalihi area of the District of Honolulu on the island of

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Eluwene and Kaumualii Streets in the Kalihi
area of the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to install roughly 1 800 lineal feet of new 8 inch
and 4 inch water mains and appurtenances adjacent to the existing utilities Based on
the preliminary project plans and profiles we understand that the new water mains will
be installed at approximately 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground level The ground
surface elevations in the project area range from about 25 to 30 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were
performed to evaluate the corrosion rating of the near surface soils within the depths
of the proposed water lines

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and field engineers to and from
the project site
2 Performance of 21 in situ soil resistivity tests at seven locations Test
Nos 1 through 7 along the sidewalk and shoulder areas of the proposed
water main alignment These test locations generally correspond to the
300 foot intervals maximum required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
3 Coordination of the field work by a Project Engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion
characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of four copies of this letter report summarizing our work on
the project and presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on November 1st for the field survey Our field work was performed in sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Twenty one soil resistivity tests were conducted at about 300 foot intervals at
seven locations of the project site using the in Pin Method and a

resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance
meter is capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output into the ground The
method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned
invert elevations of the water main were assessed by using this method at the
approximate test locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 4 Results of the
resistivity survey are presented on Plates 5 1 and 5 2
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed sidewalk and shoulder areas along the proposed water line alignment The
average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned
at equal spacings of generally 3 to 5 feet at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder and sidewalk areas
The resistance meter impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes causing
an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 5 1 and 5 2 of this report However it should be noted
that the soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the test
locations temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil character
istics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the
lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic
structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute
to corrosion of metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
