INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed administration building at the Waipahu Intermediate School
campus on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between Waikele Road and the existing Building M at
Waipahu Intermediate School in Waipahu Oahu Hawaii
It is proposed to construct a one story administration building about 110 feet by
70 feet in plan dimension and an adjacent parking area at the project site The proposed
new building structure will be of CMU construction with a steel framed roof Estimated
column loads up to 25 kips are anticipated
It is proposed to utilize the existing asphaltic concrete paved playcourt and to
convert it into a new parking area adjacent to the administration building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 3
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed building
development
Our work on the project was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated to the Department of Accounting and General Services Division
of Public Works The scope of work was limited to
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to a depth of 26 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the proposed building location and the
drilling and sampling of two 2 borings to a depth of 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the proposed parking area
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by
a Field Engineer from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and
determining their engineering properties
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 4
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed
development
6 Preparation of six 6 copies of a formal report summarizing our work
on the project and presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design
team consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and
clerical support
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between Waikele Road and the existing Building M at
the Waipahu Intermediate School campus in Waipahu on the island of Oahu
The project site is relatively flat with ground elevations ranging from 35 to 42 feet
above Mean Sea Level
Presently an open grass field and an existing asphaltic pavement playcourt
occupies the project site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling five 5 borings
to depths of 11 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a stiff to very stiff brown silty clay surface layer
approximately 2 2 to 3 6 feet in thickness underlain by a stiff to hard brown clay to a
depth of 8 0 to 10 2 feet Below the clay layer medium stiff to hard brown clayey silt and
sandy silt was encountered to 26 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
In Boring Nos 4 and 5 a 4 inch thick asphaltic concrete pavement underlain from
a medium dense gray to brown clayey sand and gravel layer were encountered to 2 3
to 2 7 foot depths below the surface
Groundwater was not encountered in borings at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test results are
presented in the appendices of this report
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff clayey soils near the surface It
is our opinion that the stiff on site soils can provide adequate bearing support for the
proposed structure and pavement However our laboratory testing also indicates that
these clay soils have moderately high expansion potential Therefore special grading
design and provision will be required to reduce potential distresses to the proposed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 6
structure caused by heaving subsoils Ideally about twenty four 24 inches of off site
select borrow fill should be utilized overlying the existing clayey soils below the footings
24 inches of the on site surface soil and replacement with select material would be
required
Because of the variable pavement thickness lack of an adequately compacted
base course layer and the numerous surface cracks noted in the existing playcourt
pavement we recommend that the existing playcourt pavement not be utilized for the new
parking area
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old pavement and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Due to the moderately high expansion potential of the subsoils encountered near
the anticipated finish subgrade level we recommend that the on site clayey subgrade
soils at the footing bottoms and below the slab subgrade level be over excavated to a
depth of at least twenty four 24 inches and backfilled with select non expansive material
The expansive subgrade soil removal should extend about two 2 feet beyond the on
grade slab perimeter This extended area should be capped with about one 1 foot of
non expansive impervious soils with a ring swell of not greater than 2 A schematic
section is shown on Plate 3
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 7
The bottom of the over excavation level should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and recompacted to
90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to firm
subsoils and then replaced with compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 30 passing the 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The replacement fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings bearing on a minimum twenty four 24 inch
capping layer of select granular material may be used for the proposed structure The
subgrades of all footings should be scarified moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 An allowable bearing value of 3 000
p s f may be used for footings bearing on the compacted select fill material The bearing
value is for total dead load plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 8
For the estimated column loads of 25 kips estimated settlements are on the order
of less than one half of an inch total and one quarter of an inch differential
Individual footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottoms of all footing over excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the select backfill to determine if adequate
bearing has been obtained and that soft loose soils are removed prior to concrete
placement All footing backfill should be placed in about eight 8 inch compacted lifts
with a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the lower floor area of the administration
building
It should be noted that the on site surface soils have a moderately high expansion
potential Therefore the existing surface soils should either be filled over or over
excavated below the slab bottom to a depth sufficient to provide a minimum twenty four
24 inch capping layer of imported non expansive soil between the slab and the natural
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2795 00 Page 9
ground The above removal of subgrade soil should also extend out two feet beyond the
slab perimeter
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A four 4 inch cushion layer composed of No 3 Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
should be provided below the slabs In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Pavements
A new adjacent parking area is planned for the new administration building It is
planned to utilize the existing playcourt for the new parking area
From our field exploration and observations the thickness of the existing asphaltic
concrete paved playcourt ranged from 1 5 to 4 0 inches Below the pavement our
borings encountered a medium dense granular layer consisting of clayey sand and gravel
to 2 3 to 2 7 feet depths Based on laboratory test results the granular material below
the existing pavement does not meet the gradation and compaction requirements for
base course material Numerous surface cracks were also noted near the edges of the
playcourt
Because of the above findings it is recommended that the playcourt not be utilized
for the new parking area
Based on our laboratory test results and the anticipated type and volume of traffic
we recommend that the following new pavement section be used

Existing Utility Lines
Since the project site is located near a developed area it is anticipated that existing
utility lines may be encountered during construction of the proposed project
Therefore we recommend that all existing utility lines be located accurately prior
to construction and that an adequate program be devised to protect the existing utility
lines during construction

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain engineering
properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the
tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs and summarized on
Plate B 1
Sieve Analysis tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are performed to determine the
grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for classification in
the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other engineering
properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 1
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
Structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B 2
