Dear Mr Nakata
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading and roadway and utility construction for the
Kapolei Village 5 Parcel 3 Nohona at Kapolei project from to
August 30 2007 in accordance with our fee proposal dated August 22 2006
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site known as Parcel 3 is located at Kapolei Village 5 within the existing
Villages of Kapolei development in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
planned development consisted of 18 residential buildings generally up to two stories in
height Slabs on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will support the proposed
residential and building structures The project also included the installation of new utilities
including sewer water and drainage lines as well as construction of new roadways to
serve the residential development A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown on
Plates 1 and 2 1 and 2 2 respectively
Based on the topographic survey map the project site is relatively flat Therefore
minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet thick will be required for the grading
work to achieve the final design grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading operations and infrastructure installation
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California
Castle Cooke Homes Hawaii Inc Page 2
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Kapolei Village 5
Parcel 3 Nohona at Kapolei project from to August 30 2007 The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on
the order of about 2 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades Royal Contracting
Company Ltd performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site
grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as
fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The
compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 19 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references
