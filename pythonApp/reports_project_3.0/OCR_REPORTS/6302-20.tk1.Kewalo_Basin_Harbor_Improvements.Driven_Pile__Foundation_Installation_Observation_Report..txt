A geotechnical engineering evaluation was performed by our office for the project
and the findings and recommendations are presented in the above referenced report The
purpose of our geotechnical observation was to monitor the geotechnical aspects of the
pile foundation installation for conformance with the recommendations presented in our
report In addition we provided Special Inspection services by observing the driven pile
foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The project involves the extension of the existing Pier C at Kewalo Basin Harbor as
part of the Ward Village Development in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that the existing Pier C is a
400 foot long catwalk pier without fingers supported on concrete piles It is planned to add
finger piers on each side of the catwalk The new finger piers will be supported on 20 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles

A total of 22 concrete piles are specified to be installed to a tip elevation of 70 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Each pile location was predrilled to facilitate the pile driving
operation and to clear the pile driving path from obstruction A layout of the pile foundation
support system is shown on the Pile Foundation Plan Plate 2
As part of the pile foundation installation program dynamic pile load testing was
performed on four test piles to examine the drivability of the piles with the use of the
selected hammer and to assess the test pile driving criteria that may be modified for use
as the production pile driving criteria The test piles were incorporated into the actual
foundation system upon favorable dynamic load test results meeting the specification
requirements
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
driven pile installation for the Kewalo Basin Harbor Improvements project only Healy
Tibbitts Builders Inc was the general contractor and also installed the concrete piling
foundations for the project

PRODUCTION PILE DRIVING
The production piles for the project consisted of 20 inch octagonal precast
pre stressed concrete piles with a furnished length of 75 feet A total of 22 production
piles designated as P 1 through P 22 were driven with a Junttan HHS9 hydraulic hammer
from October 31 through The locations of the production piles driven
are shown on the Pile Foundation Plan Plate 2
Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled to depths of
about 11 to 20 5 feet below the mudline which correspond to predrilled tip elevations of
about 33 6 to 41 3 feet MSL in order to facilitate the pile driving operations through
potential obstructions at the project site The production piles were driven to depths of
about 45 4 to 52 5 feet below the mudline which correspond to tip elevations of about
69 9 to 71 feet MSL A summary of the pile installation is shown on the attached Pile
Driving Installation Summary Plate 3
The pile driving operations were conducted under the near continuous observation
of our field engineers During the pile driving operations logs were maintained for each
pile which included information pertaining to pile number size and length of the piles date
driven predrilled depth blow counts and observations made during the pile driving
operations Individual pile driving logs are also attached to this report for reference
Based on the observations of our field engineers it is our professional opinion that
the concrete piles for this project have been installed in general conformance with the
project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction
practices
