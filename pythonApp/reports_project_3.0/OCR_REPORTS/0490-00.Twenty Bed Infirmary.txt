FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site three shallow
pits were excavated by hospital staff personnel The locations
of the test pits are shown on the site plan Plate l
An engineer from our firm inspected site conditions and logged
the test pits Disturbed samples were obtained and brought to
our laboratory for testing to aid in the classification of the
Site soils In place torrane shear tests were made in the test
pits to evaluate the strength of the site soils
SITE CONDITIONS
General Geology
The island of Molokai is formed by three volcanoes The western
portion of the island was formed by lava flows in the early
Tertiary Period The eastern portion was also formed by lava
flows in late Tertiary covering a portion of the residual soil
formed from the earlier flows
Page 3
W O 490 00
The Kalaupapa Peninsula was formed by lava flows from a small
volcano at the foot of the windward cliffs in the late Pleistocene
Period
Surface
The site is fairly level with a slight downward slope of about
six feet in a north westerly direction The northern portion of
the site is presently occupied by a wood frame garage A large
banyan tree covers the southern portion The site is covered by
grass and weeds with scattered palm trees Basalt rock is visible
in several locations on the site
Subsurface Soil Conditions
The site is underlain by sandy and clayey silts with basalt rock
gravel and cobbles The amount of rock is variable from 25 to 75
percent by volume The soil matrix is very dense The boring
logs are presented in Appendix A Basalt bedrock should be en
countered within the upper five to ten feet but may be found
closer to the surface in some local areas No groundwater or
evidence of groundwater was found during our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field investigation and laboratory testing the

site will support the proposed construction on conventional

Foundations
Spread and isolated footings should be designed for a bearing
value of 2500 pounds per square foot bottomed a minimum of
12 inches below final adjacent grade A one third increase may
be used when considering wind and seismic loadings
Due to the presence of rock throughout the site soils footing
excavations should be leveled Any voids caused by excavating
over size rock should be left open and filled with foundation
concrete
Settlements total and differential will be less than one
quarter inch
Slab On Grade
The site soils are non expansive and can be used for slab support
provided the upper six inches is compacted to a minimum of 90
of maximum density A waterproof membrane should be provided
beneath the slab to prevent moisture infiltration through the
slab

Site Grading
It is recommended that the building site be cleared of all
existing structures trees and vegetation
The grading of the site can be accomplished using normal earth
moving equipment with ripping teeth The excavated soil from
this site will be suitable for use as engineered fill if large
cobbles and rock are removed
Prior to placing fill the area should be scarified at least
six inches brought to near optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of maximum density
Fills should then be placed in six inch thick maximum lifts and
also compacted to at least 90 of maximum density Any voids
due to the removal of structures or trees should be compacted
as fill
Every reasonable effort was made to determine any adverse sub
surface conditions However no warranty can be made or implied
regarding deep seated geological weaknesses or voids below the
depths explored
Should any unusual condition arise not covered by this report
we should be contacted for instructions prior to continuation
of grading or building operations
