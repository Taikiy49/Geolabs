performed during the micropile installation In general the purpose of our geotechnical
engineering services was to observe the micropile foundation installation for general
conformance with the project documents

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waipahu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The project consists of rehabilitation reconstruction of approximately
2 075 linear feet of 8 inch diameter sewer lines within the project limits The sewer lines
for rehabilitation reconstruction are designated as Sewer Lines A and B As part of
the project approximately 450 linear feet of Sewer Line A will be reconstructed The
approximate location of the sewer line reconstruction is shown on the Site Plan
Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Sewer Line A is located along Kahuanani Street A very soft and relatively thick
subsoil deposit was encountered below the existing ground surface at the project site
To provide foundation support for the new sewer line and manhole structures for Sewer
Line A the installation of micropiles was recommended

Based on available project drawings the micropile cut off elevation ranges from
4 5 to 5 9 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Each micropile is designed to carry a design
load of 48 kips with a factor of safety of 2 0 Permanent steel casings with an outside
diameter of 7 inches were used for the upper section of the micropiles The micropiles
have a minimum design length of about 64 feet below the cut off elevation with a bond
length of about 34 feet Bond length refers to the micropile length measured from
bottom of permanent casing to the tip of each micropile
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
The new sewer line will be supported on a concrete beam and micropile foundation
system The installation of the production micropiles was performed by Schnabel
Foundation Company A total of 61 micropiles were installed from
through Due to the installation of micropiles in the wrong locations two
of the 61 installed micropiles were additional micropiles that were added in order to
provide sufficient support for the new sewer line The two additional micropiles were
installed in the vicinity of and 30 The micropiles were designated as
through 47 for micropiles supporting the new sewer pipes and Micropile
for micropiles supporting the manhole structures and and
30A for the additional micropiles
Based on the project drawings 7 inch diameter micropiles with an embedment
depth of approximately 64 feet below the design micropile cut off elevation will be used to
achieve the allowable compressive load capacity of 48 kips The micropile system
generally consists of a threaded reinforcing bar as a load carrying steel member
permanent steel casing installed within upper soft soil layers to prevent surrounding soils
from caving in and a grout body of neat Portland Cement mix which allows transfer of
tension and compression forces mainly through friction of the threaded bar via the grout
body into the soil
To avoid an existing concrete cradle encountered at the start of construction the
concrete beam design and micropile locations were modified by the City and County of
Honolulu The modifications consisted of relocating the micropiles further apart and
increasing the concrete support beam width These modifications increased the micropile
loading from 48 kips to 70 kips and reduced the factor of safety from 2 0 to 1 36
Prior to drilling operation a trench was dug at the micropile locations The
micropiles were then installed using a Hutte 605 double head drill rig employing drag bit
drilling techniques The Hutte 605 double head drill rig is fitted with two drilling motors that
simultaneously turn separate inner and outer drill strings in opposite directions The inner
drill string or rod has a drag bit attached to it that delivers the majority of the cutting energy
to advance the hole The outer casing is fitted with J teeth welded to the leading piece of
casing and rotated in with down pressure from the drill rig

During the micropile installation the entire length of the drilled hole was cased
using steel casing which was installed simultaneously while the hole was drilled A
threaded reinforcing bar with PVC centralizers at about 10 foot spacing were inserted into
the drilled hole The epoxy coated steel bar consisted of No 10 size Grade 75 reinforcing
bar Neat cement grout was placed through a tremie pipe starting from the bottom of the
hole During grouting the tremie pipe was slowly withdrawn as the grout level in the hole
rose Grouting was stopped and the tremie pipe was withdrawn from the hole when grout
started overflowing and clean grout appeared at the top of the casing Steel casing was
then extracted from the hole until the required permanent casing tip elevation was
reached Additional grout was placed from the top of the hole when the grout level in the
hole dropped during the extraction The micropiles were installed with embedment depths
of approximately 75 feet below the existing ground surface
During the micropile installation the drilling time required to advance each section
of the casing total strokes of grout pumping and number of sacks of cement used were
recorded for evaluation and documentation A summary of the installed micropiles is
presented on the Micropile Installation Summary Plates 3 1 and 3 2
28 and 44 were recommended for proof testing Epoxy coated
No 11 size Grade 75 reinforcing bars were installed for the proof load micropiles The
proof tests were conducted by loading the micropiles in tension to about 72 kips 1 5 times
the design load The micropile movements were measured with each load increment
using two dial gauges In addition the design load of 48 kips was held for 10 minutes to
evaluate the creep effects Based on the proof load tests conducted total movements of
about 0 30 to 0 55 inches were observed at the top of the tested micropiles at maximum
test load The results of the proof tests are provided on Plates 4 1 through 4 3
Based on our field observations and the proof load tests performed we believe the
micropiles were installed in general conformance with the project plans and within the
limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on our observations of the
micropile installation it is our opinion that the micropiles were installed to the sufficient
diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity with a reduced factor of safety of
1 36
