As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of two 2 test pits
excavated and one 1 boring drilled within the proposed building
site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and com
paction test record on the building site This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a one story wood
structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site a split level
design may be utilized Short retaining walls are planned around the
garage Based on the preliminary layout plans the house will set
back about 45 feet from Kahena Street

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and
lanai Post and beam construction with wood floors are planned for
the remainder of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 45 is located in the central portion of the subdivision develop
ment on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded
by Kahena Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the east and
west and the natural valley side slope on the south An existing con
crete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern upper portion of
the lot The lot generally slopes upward from Kahena Street toward
the southwest at about 11 to 18 percent gradient The elevation of
the lot generally varies from about 188 to 262 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation the northeastern
front portion of Lot 45 was covered with a large stockpile of soil
and boulders left over from previous grading operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 4 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil con
sisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been
mixed with some adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicate that the proposed house is underlain by a layer of
more than 7 5 feet of grayish brown expansive clay on the garage area
B 1 and 1 to 2 feet deep on the rear portion of the proposed house
area TP 1 and 2 The test pits drilled borings and site reconnais
sance indicate that better less expansive and gravelly colluvial
deposits is encountered below the surface clay layer below about 2
feet under the rear half of the house
The remaining portion of the lot beyond the house area is underlain by
natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper hillside area
These deposits are typically heterogenous and vary from clayey silt
low expansion potential to clay high expansion potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a layer
of grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on this material
generally indicated that they have high expansion potential There
fore for Lot 45 the slab on grade and foundation construction would
require special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoils
Cuts from about 2 to 6 feet are planned in the rear portion of the
house The field exploration disclosed that better less expansive
soil may be encountered in this area after the site grading
Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions
W Page 5
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the ground surface The footings should be
poured in place and the excavation backfilled and compacted to provide
an impervious layer over the footing
In the rear half of the house the footing embedment may be reduced to
12 inches below the finished grade if better gravelly colluvial soil
is encountered The soils engineer should inspect the footing
excavations to determine if better less expansive soil has been
encountered and the footing embedment may be reduced accordingly
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
I a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and lanai The
on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the
expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
In the lanai area the depth of expansive clay overexcavation may be
eliminated if better gravelly colluvial soil is encountered
W O 865 46 Page 8
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of more than 7 5 feet of expansive soils was encountered in
the front portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveway can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation and replace
ment can be reduced to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow
material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less
than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required The
above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative move
ment of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 10 to
11 gradient
Cuts from about 2 to 6 feet are planned in the rear portion of the
house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
Cut slopes of 2 1 H V are recommended in the stiff colluvial soils
anticipated in the rear portion of the house
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
