Dear Mr Yamada
This letter report summarizes our observations of the probing and grouting
operations at the above project site between July 27 and August 3 2012
Probing and Grouting Operations
Isemoto Contracting Company Ltd performed the probing and grouting operations
in general conformance with the project specifications The purpose of the probing and
grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the residential structures
currently under construction at the site
In general the probe holes were located on a 10 foot nominal on center pattern
along wall foundations and at the centerpoint of individual spread footings The
approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans Plates 1 1
through 1 5 In general the probe holes were drilled to a depth equivalent to about 10 feet
below the bottom of foundation grade
The probe holes about 3 5 inches in diameter were drilled using an air powered
track drill After drilling of the probe holes temporary pipes 2 inch PVC were inserted
into the holes to keep the holes open prior to the grouting operations A total of 616 probe
holes were drilled at the project site
When a suspected void or voids were encountered when drilling a probe four
additional probe holes were drilled around the location to further explore the extent of the
suspected voids Additional probes were drilled at a total of 15 locations as indicated on
the Probe Hole Location Plans Plates 1 1 through 1 5 and the Foundation Probing and
Grouting Summary Plate A 1

The probe holes were backfilled with a sand cement slurry grout supplied by
Jas W Glover Ltd Each probe hole was grouted from the drilled depth upward by low
pressure injection of grout Our engineer observed the grouting operation and recorded
the approximate grout take Grout quantities were estimated using the number of strokes
to fill each probe hole
The probing and grouting operations are summarized on Plate A 1 The results are
tabulated on Plates A 2 through A 56
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the project specifications Based on our field observations it
is our professional opinion that the cavities encountered below the residential structures
subgrades have been probed and grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the buildings
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all cavities and or
voids present beneath the buildings Therefore some of the cavities and or voids may not
have been detected even with the probing and grouting program undertaken for the
project However the level of foundation preparation undertaken for the structures and the
subgrade bearing on the on site basalt formation appears to be reasonable in reducing the
geotechnical risks to the project Performing probing and grouting of the foundations
bearing on the on site materials provides the owner with a higher degree of assurance that
substantial cavities and or voids are not located directly below the buildings
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied It should be noted
that provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type
