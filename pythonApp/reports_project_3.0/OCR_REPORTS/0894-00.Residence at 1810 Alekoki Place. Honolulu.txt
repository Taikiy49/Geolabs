SITE CONDITIONS
The subject property is located at the end of Alekoki Place
in Manoa near the University of Hawaii area The lot slopes
down toward the south at about 5 to 8 percent grades
The existing residence consists of a 2 story main structure
and a one story studio located near the central portion of
the lot A swimming pool is located near the eastern lot
boundary Concrete driveways and garages were utilized for
the residence
The 2 story residence has CMU bearing walls with a plaster
finish and a first floor slab on grade The one story studio
is supported by a slab on grade
Surface drainage swales were not noted during our initial
field observation Low spots where water could pond were
noted next to the house Subsequently drainage swales
were excavated around the rear and western side of the
house

FIELD EXPLORATION
Since the residence utilities and landscaping were already
constructed at the site access for drilling equipment was
very limited Therefore hand operated auger holes were
utilized for the soil investigation Three holes were
drilled around the exterior of the structure and 2 holes
were drilled through the dining room and living room floors
Two additional holes were attempted inside the studio how
ever the drill could not penetrate the wire mesh in the
concrete floor
Disturbed samples were obtained from the auger cuttings
and taken back to our laboratory for testing
The hand auger boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Soil Conditions
The auger holes disclosed a 2 foot layer of brown clay CH
soil over stiff silty or sandy subsoils
The brown clay encountered outside the house was softer
and had a higher moisture content than the Clay encountered
under the house slab This clay soil has high shrink swell
potential as indicated by the results of laboratory one inch
ring swell test performed on a remolded sample of the clay
encountered under the house slab

Foundation Plan Review
The foundation plans for the subject residence indicated
that the footings for the bearing walls were designed to
be embedded 22 inches below the finished grade while
the perimeter slab footings at the doorways and other
areas without bearing walls were designed to be embedded
about 8 inches below the finished grade The slab on
grade for the studio also was designed with an 8 inch
deep perimeter slab embedment
It is our understanding that the footings for the structure
were designed using an allowable bearing value of about
2000 p s f
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The cracking pattern noted at the residence indicates that
there has been differential movement between the heavier
load bearing walls and the lighter partition walls and
slabs
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests it
appears that the foundation movement at the residence is
caused by heaving of the underlying clay soils These clay
soils typically have high expansion potential particularly
when it has low in place water contents The poor site
drainage around the house also contributed to the heaving
problems by allowing water to pond next to the foundations

causing the surface water to infiltrate under the interior
portion of the house slab and thereby causing the underlying
expansive clay to swell
It was noted that most of the damaged areas appeared to
occur where a shallow 8 inch thick perimeter footing
embedment was utilized and the site grading allowed
water to pond
A laboratory swell test on a remolded sample of the clay
soil encountered below the house slab indicated that the
clay still has a potential for further swelling if water
is allowed to penetrate the soil
The main object of any future remedial work undertaken
at the site should be to prevent or minimize water from
entering under the slabs and foundations Also flexible
joints and floor coverings can be used to allow for some
future ground movements It is our Opinion that the
following steps could be adopted to reduce additional
future movement to the residence
1 The ground surface around the house should
be graded to drain water far away from the
foundations and to prevent water ponding
