PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The One Ala Moana condominium project consists of a 16 story high rise
residential tower and two levels of additional parking to be built on top of the existing
parking garage structure on the mauka side of the Ala Moana Center in Honolulu Hawaii
The existing parking garage is abutted by the Nordstrom Building to the west the existing
Ala Moana Pacific Building to the east Kapiolani Boulevard to the north and Kona Street
to the south
Based on the updated design of the tower structure the loading for one of the
columns will increase from 2 000 kips to 5 000 kips Therefore about 20 micropiles were
recommended to augment the existing drilled shaft foundation at the column location to
provide the necessary load bearing support for the new residential tower
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
pre production micropile foundation construction and testing for the One Ala Moana
project only The micropile contractor for the project is Kelikai Inc

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on previous studies performed by others and the information obtained from
Boring No 7 conducted during our 2005 field exploration subsurface conditions at the
column location generally consist of fill materials comprised of medium stiff silty clays at
the surface extending to a depth of about 3 feet below the existing grades The surface fill
materials generally were underlain by lagoonal deposits consisting of loose sandy gravel
and soft sandy clays extending to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface It should be noted that the lagoonal deposits encountered at the project site are
highly compressible when subjected to structural loads
Below the compressible lagoonal deposits our boring generally encountered a
coral ledge on the order of about 14 feet in thickness The lagoonal deposits and coral
ledge generally were underlain by coralline detritus materials consisting of medium dense
silty coralline sands and gravel with localized loose and dense zones extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 128 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the boring drilled at a depth of about 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater level
encountered generally corresponds to about Elevation 1 9 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
PRE PRODUCTION MICROPILE INSTALLATION
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the project vicinity the
production micropiles were specified to extend to a minimum depth of about 60 feet below
the ground level with a 35 foot load bearing grout bulb developed within the coralline
materials and a 25 foot grout zone neglected within the lagoonal deposits To reduce
risk of micropile necking within the loose soft lagoonal material 25 feet of permanent
steel casing was specified to be left in place at the top of each micropile The design
micropile allowable compressive load capacity was specified to be 150 kips
Kelikai Inc installed the sacrificial pre production micropile for testing on
The four reaction micropiles in support of the load test were installed from
July 11 through The approximate location of the pre production micropile for
load testing purposes is shown on the Test Micropile Layout Plate 2
For testing purposes the grout bulb for the pre production pile was reduced from
35 feet to 30 feet to evaluate the design parameters Consequently the pre production
micropile extended to a depth of about 55 feet below the existing ground surface while
embedding about 31 feet into coralline materials Details of the pre production micropile
installation are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The pre production micropile was drilled using a TEl HEMS550R rig equipped with
steel casing to the specified depth Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified
depth was required in order to facilitate the micropile drilling and maintain grouting quality
Air pressure up to 100 pounds per square inch psi was utilized during the drilling
operation to create a bulb around the drilling bit for advancing temporary steel casing

After the drilled hole was extended to the specified depth and cleaned with air
pressure a reinforcing bar consisting of Grade 75 all threaded 18 rebar was placed in the
cased hole with centralizers Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal high shear and
high speed mixer equipped with a grout pump The grout mix
consisted of one sack of 94 pound Portland Cement Type and or pounds of
masonry sand and 5 gallons of water to yield a 28 day design compressive strength of
4 000 psi
The grout was placed through a 1 inch PVC tremie pipe from the bottom of the
cased hole After sound grout appeared at the top of the cased hole the temporary steel
casing was removed one section 5 foot long at a time until the bottom of the steel casing
approached the specified top of the grout bulb zone
Positive grout intake was maintained during the grouting operation to reduce the
potential for necking of the micropile grout bulb The actual grout volume placed in the
pre production micropile was about 205 of the theoretical grout volume
MICROPILE TESTING
As part of the pre construction activities one static load test was conducted on the
pre production micropile to confirm the design parameters and to evaluate the adequacy
of the foundation support system The compression load test was conducted on
July 25 and 26 2013 In general the micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of
about 300 kips 200 percent of the design load of 150 kips in approximately 18 75 kip load
increments The micropile was held at each load increment for about 15 minutes and was
held at the maximum test load of 300 kips for two hours to evaluate creep effects At the
maximum test load of 300 kips the creep movement for the last hour was about
0 003 inches which is less than 0 01 inches typically allowed for the ground anchor
system The load test results are presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 and the micropile
load versus displacement plot is presented on Plate 5 and the test results are
