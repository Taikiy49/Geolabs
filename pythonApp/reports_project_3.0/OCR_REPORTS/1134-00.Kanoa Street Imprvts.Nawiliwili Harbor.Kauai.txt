SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed
cargo handling area is underlain by dredged fill
which consists mainly of medium dense silty coral
sands Kanoa Street is overlain with stiff silty
soils
The laboratory tests indicated that for the silty
coral sand fill material a design CBR value of 15
may be used for the cargo handling area Along
Kanoa Street a CBR value of 8 may be assigned for
the compacted brown clayey silt subgrade soil
For more detailed and specific recommendations
refer to the text of this report
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore the
soil conditions at the sites to provide data to be used in the
pavement design analysis
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance
the drilling of eight 8 new exploratory borings laboratory
testing of selected samples and engineering analysis of various
pavement design methods

Results of our field exploration and laboratory testing which
were used in the engineering analysis that formulated our con
clusions and recommendations are presented in the appendices of
this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed improvement project will consist mainly of site
grading and construction of new asphaltic concrete pavements at
the container handling area at Nawiliwili Harbor Kanoa
Street will also be paved to accommodate future truck traffic
At the container handling site the proposed pavement area will
be approximately 1 2 acres In addition about 500 feet of
Kanoa Street will be paved to obtain a 20 foot wide roadway
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed cargo handling site is located at the southern
corner of Nawiliwili Avenue and Mokihana Street inside the
present Young Brothers shipping facilities at Nawiliwili
Harbor Kauai Hawaii see Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1134 00 Page 3
The site is roughly rectangular in shape and approximately 180
by 290 feet in plan dimension Mokihana Street borders the
site to the north the State Harbors office to the east and the
existing cargo handling area to the west and south
Ground surface elevations range from 6 5 to 8 2 feet with the
site sloping gently to the west
A drainage swale containing three 3 interceptors crosses
the site in an approximate north south alignment
Approximately parallel to the western boundary is an existing
rectangular reinforced concrete slab about 40 by 100 feet in
plan dimension
The site is currently utilized as temporary storage for con
struction equipment incoming and outgoing containers and
vehicles
Kanoa Street is approximately 650 feet southwest from the
container handling yard along Mokihana Street see Plate 3
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1134 00 Page 4
The street which is about 50 feet wide by 500 feet long is
presently not paved Wilcox Road borders the north end
Mokihana Street to the south Union Oil Company to the west
and abandoned structures along the eastern boundary
Ground surface elevations were not available during our inves
tigation However the site slopes gently upward from Mokihana
toward Wilcox Road
SUBSURFACE INVESTIGATION
A total of eight 8 exploratory borings were drilled to depths
ranging from 11 5 to 16 5 feet to investigate the subsurface
conditions at the aproximate boring locations presented on the
Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Soil Conditions
Stiff clayey silt fills were encountered in a majority of the
borings At the cargo handling area 6 to 24 inches of such
material was encountered while approximately 4 feet was
encountered in Kanoa Street
Directly beneath the clay silt layer is a dense to medium dense
coral sand with varying percentages of silts and gravels
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
It appears that this sand fill layer could originate from the
earlier Nawiliwili Harbor dredging and construction This
dredged coral layer extends to a depth of about 10 feet and
overlies an older estuarine sand deposit
The estuarine sands consist of very loose fine basalt and coral
sands extending to the depth of our borings
In the existing developed areas Borings 2 and 4 encountered 5
to 6 inches of coral base course below a 1 to 2 inch thick
asphaltic concrete surface pavement
Groundwater was encountered in all of the borings at a depth of
5 0 to 6 25 feet
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The preliminary grading plans for the cargo handling facility
and Kanoa Street improvements were not available during the
course of our investigation However based on the site recon
hnaissance of the existing site topography very little filling
is anticipated Some amount of cutting might even be required
in some areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1134 00 Page 6
Brown clayey silt up to a depth of 2 feet was encountered in
some of the container yard area If this layer is not removed
below the subgrade level it will reduce the design CBR value
for the compacted sugrade To Support the new container yard
pavement section we recommend that this clayey silt layer be
stripped to the underlying coral sand layer during site grad
ing For the underlying coral sand compacted to a minimum 95
density a design CBR value of 15 may be used
The existing concrete pavement which appears to be an older
structural foundation should be removed
A container stacking arrangement was also not available at the
time of our investigation However it was assumed that the
site usage will be maximized to provide a minimum travel
distance easy access and maximum container storage area In
order to complete the pavement analysis a container stacking
arrangement as shown on Plate C 2 was developed
Along Kanoa Street we recommend a design California Bearing
Ratio CBR value equal to 8 be used for the relatively thick
brown clayey silt subgrade soil

Site Grading
Site Preparation
a a ns
At the onset of earthwork the areas to receive fill
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
vegetation structures debris rubbish and any other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
The existing concrete pavement base course and unsuit
able brown clayey silt fill in the area of the container
handling area should be removed during site grading
Other soft or clayey subgrade soils encountered during the
clearing operations should also be removed and replaced by
backfilling with well compacted select fill
Following the clearing and grubbing all fill areas should
be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557 70
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1134 00 Page 8
Fill Material
Although large amount of fill are not anticipated if
required imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to
the project site In general the fill material should be
non expansive generally less than 8 inches in size and
form a dense stable soil mass when compacted
The pavement design was based on a minimum subgrade CBR
value of 15 for the container yard handling area and 8 for
Kanoa Street Therefore material placed within the top 3
feet below finish grade should match these CBR values and
swell less than 1 when compacted to 95 of the maximum
dry density otherwise the pavement design should be
revised
Fill Placement
All i11 material should be moisture conditioned prior
to spreading and compaction at the time of compaction
Moisture content should be within 3 percentage points of
the optimum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
All fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness Fills
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maxi
mum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 70
The 111 within 12 inches of the finished subgrade level
should have a minimum compaction of 95 for cohesive soils
and 100 for non cohesive soils as established by the
above test
Cut Areas
Subsequent to the rough cutting the surface should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to within 3 percentage points of its optimum
moisture content and recompacted in accordance with the
requirements of fill placement
Since most of Kanoa Street and the container handling facility
will be cut the pavement section will be governed by the
quality of the cut material In our analysis we have used a
CBR of 8 for the brown clayey silt of Kanoa Street and 15 for
the silty fine coral sand at the container handling area
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1134 00 Page 10
In the pavement design the design CBR value selected is some
what lower than the laboratory CBR values in order to allow for
general reduction in soil strength which occurs with time due
to changes in the moisture content of the soils and to account
for natural variations in soil strata
The lower CBR value was also selected since no traffic growth
factor was available for our analysis Although a higher CBR
value may result in a thinner pavement section yet increasing
vehicular traffic may affect its performance which may require
earlier resurfacing of the area

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on Site Plans
Plates 1 and 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings and probings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer who continously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1
through A 8
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
