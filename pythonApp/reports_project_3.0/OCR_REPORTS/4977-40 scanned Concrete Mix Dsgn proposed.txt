Given various factors the small amount of concrete needed for each column how spread
out the column locations are the difficulty of access and the necessity to level and
stabilize each column in place prior to installing concrete it seems best to mix the
concrete for these columns on the hill near each of the column locations We did this
successfully on the Lalea Project for some pillar supports that Earth Tech had us build
under some of the over hanging sections using a portable electric cement mixer This
mixer makes one third of a yard at a time
I cailed Hawaiian Cement and found out the different formulas for 2 500 psi and 4 000
psi concrete Basically they increase the cement quantity by 100 Ibs per yard to go from
2 500 to 4 000 psi What we would like to suggest is that to the bags of pre mixed
concrete that is 2 500 psi we add one third of a 94 Ib bag of Portland Type II cement to
bring the psi up to To this mix we will add the established measured amount
of water to maintain a consistent slump of 4 to 5 Because corrosion is a big concer if
you prefer we can add the appropriate amount of Sika Ferro Guard to the mix for
