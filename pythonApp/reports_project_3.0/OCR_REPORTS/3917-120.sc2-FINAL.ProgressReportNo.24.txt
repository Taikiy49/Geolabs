Our rainfall summary is based on a review of the available rainfall data and monthly
precipitation summaries prepared by the National Weather Service Forecast Office
Honolulu NWSFOH During the past six month monitoring period the project site
experienced variable and generally below average monthly rainfall ranging between about
25 percent and about 192 percent of normal The well above average rainfall was
recorded in March 2012
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels In addition following extended periods of below normal
precipitation earth movements generally decrease slightly
