PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions at
the site to provide pertinent data to evaluate the possbile causes of the
wall settlements and possible remedial measures The scope of our
exploration was limited to only the existing retaining wall
The scope of our exploration which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated May 29 1986 included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of four 4 borings laboratory
soil testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory
data and report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of this report The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing CRM wall is about 6 to 7 feet in height above the
existing ground The wall retains a level backfill on which the 3 story
apartment building is located about 10 feet from the top of the wall
The depth and base width of the foundation of the wall is not known

From plans of the building provided to us it appears that the
foundation design for the building consists of spread footings on the
backfill material behind the wall The bottom of footing appears to be
about 2 5 to 3 feet below the existing sidewalk slab that is located
around the structure
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Moiliili area of Honolulu The
proposed retaining wall is located at the southwest corner of the
Moiliili Gardens property Kuilei Lane is located about 100 feet south
and Kahuna Lane about 200 feet west of the site
The Mother Rice KCAA School yard is located adjacent to the west
side of the lot and a condominium and playground facilities are located
south of the site
Visual observations of the wall indicates it has settled and moved
down and out at the west and south portions of the lot The distressed
wall portion appears to be limited to about 30 feet from the property
corner in the easterly direction and about 10 to 15 feet in the southerly
direction
The 4 foot wide concrete slab along the building has settled several
inches along the north side of the building The corner of the building
appears to have a slight bulge and the exterior partition screen walls
have separated from the main building otherwise the structure does not
appear to have suffered extensive structural damage at the time our
exploration was completed

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the distressed portions of the retaining wall to provide
recommendations for the design of remedial repair of the retaining wall
at these locations
Due to the limited scope of work the discussion and recommendation
presented herewith are only for the foundation design of the existing
retaining wall Other issues which we believe to be critical to the
project such as long term stability of the existing building after the
wall construction will not be studied in detail in this report
In general our borings encountered poor subsoil conditions
underlying the existing retaining wall Based on our currently available
subsoil data and observations it appears that the existing CRM wall has
settled several inches and may continue to settle or collapse unless some
corrective remedial measures are done
Possible Causes of Wall Distress
Our field observations and subsurface information indicate that the
distress to the retaining wall may be due to one or more of the
following
1 Insufficient wall strength The existing wall may not have
sufficient size to resist the soil pressures induced against
it It appears that the retaining wall was constructed in two
separate stages upper 3 to 4 foot section over the previous
wall and may not be adequately tied together
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1713 00 Page 7
Dk No 25
2 Water pressure It appears that the roof drain spouts empty
into the area behind the subject wall During heavy rains
sufficient water pressure could build up behind the wall to
induce large additional lateral pressure on the wall and cause
lateral movements Also the water flow could wash out the finer
soil particles in the backfill causing settlement of the ground
behind the wall
3 Cavity A large cavity was encountered below the site Partial
or complete collapse of a portion of the cavity could result in
settlements and some lateral movement of the retaining wall and
backfill
Stability of Existing Building Foundations
The most critical aspect of the remedial work on the damaged wall is
the stability of the existing 3 story building foundations Based on the
foundation drawings the outer wall of the building is supported by a
continuous footing extending parallel to the retaining wall The depth
of the footing is unknown but is believed to be shallow refer to
Section A A The backfill behind the retaining wall is loose
therefore if the existing wall is removed the backfill would slough out
and could cause settlement of the building foundation Therefore any
remedial work should attempt to maintain the stability of the backfill
Alternative Methods for Wall Repair
The borings disclosed that portions of the wall and possibly the
building are underlain by a large cavity The presence of the cavity
has a significant effect on the wall repairs and an even greater effect
on the building stability Although a study of the existing building
foundations is not included in the scope of our services the condition
of the building foundations has an important effect on the wall repairs
Additional studies on the long term stability of the building over the
cavity should be performed
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1713 00 Page 8
Dk No 25
The most straight forward repair scheme would be to remove the
existing CRM wall and reconstruct a new replacement wall However due
to the proximity of the building foundation wall it is likely that the
building foundations would settle if the present wall is removed
Additionally future collapse of the cavity underlying the wall would
cause large settlements of the new wall This method is not recommended
Generally the repair work should maintain the stability of the
building foundations and mitigate the hazard of the cavity collapse
Several methods for the remedial work are presented below
1 Grout soil behind CRM wall and under building footing The
underlying soils are generally permeable and therefore cement
or chemical grouting may have good penetration The grouting
would be used to cement the soils and loose rock so that the
existing CRM wall could be removed and replaced in small
sections 10 15 without sloughing of the backfill refer to
Plate 5 The underlying cavity could also be grouted to reduce
the hazard of future collapse although the limits of the cavity
are unknown at this time
2 Construct caisson wall in front of existing CRM wall A new
caisson retaining wall could be constructed in front of the
existing wall to support the existing wall and backfill The
wall could be constructed by drilling closely spaced caissons
which would extend below the bottom of the cavity refer to
Plate 6
This method would probably be the most straight forward with the
least disturbance to the existing building foundations
However the new caisson wall would have to be built outside of
the present property lines

3 Construct caisson wall behind existing CRM wall This method is
similar to item 3 above except that the new wall would be
constructed within the property This method would be more
costly than item 3 due to the limited accessibility to the area
for equipment refer to Plate 6
Conclusions
Several alternatives for the remedial work have been presented The
method selected will depend on economic and legal considerations We
recommend that consultation from a structural engineer as well as
contractors be obtained to provde a better estimate of the cost of each
alternative
Regardless of which method is used the drainage from the roof down
spouts should be collected and piped to a suitable outlet to minimize
saturation and washing out of the wall backfill
The stability of the building was not included in our scope of
work In light of the large cavity encountered near the building we
recommend that additional studies be performed to determine if the cavity
continues under the building and if so to evaluate the effect of the
cavity on the building stability
Design Review
Plans and specifications for design of wall foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included

construction to monitor the contractor s construction operations
