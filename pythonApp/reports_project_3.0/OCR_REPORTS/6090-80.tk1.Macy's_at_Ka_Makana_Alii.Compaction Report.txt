PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ka Makana Ali i development approximately 67 acres is in the East Kapolei
area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded
approximately by Kapolei Parkway to the north Roosevelt Avenue to the south the
proposed Phase II North South Road Extension to the east and a drainage channel and
Kapolei Middle School to the west
Based on the information provided the project will involve the development of the
property into a commercial retail center providing approximately square feet of
retail and or commercial space We understand the project will be constructed in
two separate phases The Phase 1 portion of the development will encompass about
50 acres over the northern and central portions of the property The Phase 2 portion of the
development will encompass the remaining 17 acres of the development situated over the

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The mass grading work and building pad construction for the Macy s Store at
Ka Makana Ali i were performed by Royal Contracting Company under a separate project
Jayar Construction Inc performed the footing and plumbing excavations loading dock
and truck apron slab during the store building construction described in this report
The footing bottoms for the store building and loading dock walls were generally
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor Base course and aggregate subbase
underneath the driveway ramp to the electrical room and loading dock approach slab were
generally compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine gravel was used for the
plumbing trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill
The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no
less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 110 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 9 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction of
the Macy s Store at Ka Makana Ali i project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials

construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
