RECOMMENDATIONS
Our geotechnical engineering recommendations are summarized below
Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the design finished grades we anticipate that the
proposed site grading may consist of cuts and fills on the order of about 1 to 7 feet relative to the
existing ground surface Fills up to about 11 feet relative to the existing ground surface will be
required at the new parking lot area
Our field exploration encountered about 3 feet of very soft surface fill beneath the new
toilet storage building To reduce the potential for future settlements and distress to the new building
structure the area beneath the concrete slab and 5 feet beyond the concrete slab edge should be
over excavated a depth of 3 feet from the existing round surface Prior to placing backfill the
over excavated surface should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The backfill material should consist of well compacted structural fill materials
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
The over excavated surfaces finished subgrades in cut areas and areas designated to
receive fills should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches where possible moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted with a minimum 15 ton vibratory drum roller
a minimum of six passes In areas where the scarification requirement is not possible such as in
dense rock subgrades the areas should be proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar
heavy construction equipment for a minimum of six passes to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities and to provide a firm base for the placement of fills For exposed soil subgrades after
clearing and grubbing the subgrade should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations
should be backfilled with compacted fill materials
To facilitate the construction of the proposed structures and the underground utilities planned
at the project site we recommend that well compacted structural fill materials be placed within the
upper 2 feet of the finished subgrade elevations for the proposed structures The structural fill
materials should extend a minimum of 5 feet beyond the limits of the building structure

Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials less than 6 inches
in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to fill the void spaces The requirement of the fines
content with the structural fill materials is necessary to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
structural fill layer in general the excavated materials clinker and basalt may be re used as a

General fill and backfill materials used below the structural fill layer should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in largest dimension
The granular materials should not exceed an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches The
excavated on site materials may be re used as general fill or backfill materials provided that they
meet the requirements for general fill materials as mentioned above If the excavated materials do
not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material off site borrow or on site rock crushing
of large sized rock fragments or boulders should be considered to provide the required gradation
and particle sizes to develop a well graded material
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types
of acceptable compaction equipment Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted using a minimum 15 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes
General fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 18 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted using a minimum 15 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes
Where fill materials with a maximum particle size of 3 inches are used the fills should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Imported material should consist of select granular fill such as crushed mudrock basalt
or cinder sand Select granular fill materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension Select granular fill should have a laboratory CBR
value of 20 or more with a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition select granular fill material should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve

Concrete slabs on grade will be used for the existing BBQ building toilet storage building

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site and the discussions
provided in the Site Grading section of this letter we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used to support the bridge abutment and gazebo column structures planned at the
site An allowable bearing pressure up to 10 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the
design of foundations bearing on the moderately to slightly weathered basalt formation For the
design of footings bearing on well compacted structural fill materials an allowable bearing pressure
of up to may be used This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by 50 percent for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade For footings constructed near the tops of slopes or on slopes the footings should
be embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance measured from the outside edge at
the base of the footing to the face of the slope is not less than 6 feet
Where foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for appreciable foundation settlement
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the footing subgrades they should be
over excavated and replaced with structural fill The over excavation should be extended until
dense materials are exposed The over excavation should be backfilled with structural fill materials
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing may be extended down to the underlying
competent material
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used
for footings bearing on structural fill material The coefficient of friction may be increased to 0 60 for
footings bearing on the moderately to slightly weathered basalt formation Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 450 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth pcf assuming that the soils around the footing are well compacted
The passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of the soil should be neglected unless covered by
pavements or slabs

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea which
are both still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes up
the west central part of the island The lava formation generally encountered in this area is of
a a and pahoehoe basalt type flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean
Aa lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling
a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Typically a denser
GEOLABS INC
W O 4405 00 Page 6
and layered lava rock material is contained within the lava flow core Pahoehoe lava is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular
porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lavas These cavities were
formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes intra flow
cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten
lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for a
large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller in
horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted through
the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along
clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they serve as the
major water transmission features

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling 11 borings

approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with rotary coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 11
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last
12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative quality
of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from a coring
attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of material is recovered
from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be shown on the Logs
of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material in
excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of these tests are
summarized on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
The test results are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content relationships
The test results are presented on Plate B 3
