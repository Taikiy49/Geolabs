INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Advanced SEAL Delivery System Facility project
at the Pearl City Peninsula site on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our limited field exploration laboratory testing and
preliminary engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for planning
and preliminary design evaluation purposes only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It was originally planned to construct a consolidated operations and maintenance
facility in support of the Advanced SEAL Delivery System ASDS on the west side of
Ford Island in Pearl Harbor within the island of Oahu Hawaii A geotechnical engineering
exploration for this project was conducted by our office and the findings and
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration FY97 MCON Project Q 449 Advanced SEAL Delivery System Facility Ford
Island Naval Station Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii dated May 22 1996
Based on the updated information provided to us we understand that the
proposed facility has subsequently been relocated from its original planned location on
Ford Island to an area near the southern tip of the Pearl City Peninsula as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Therefore it was desired to conduct a preliminary
geotechnical engineering exploration at the proposed relocated site to evaluate the
feasibility of adapting the foundation and pavement designs developed for the Ford Island
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3577 10 Page 3
site to the currently proposed relocated site Because of the presence of underground
fuel tanks and piping network it was also desired to perform environmental laboratory
testing on the near surface soils to evaluate the potential for hydrocarbon contamination
at the proposed relocated site
Due to time constraints off shore borings for the pier structure were not planned
for this preliminary phase of the field exploration program Therefore additional on land
and off shore borings will be required at the relocated site during the design level
geotechnical engineering exploration to obtain the necessary information for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
subject project

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3577 10 Page 5
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lava in central Oahu
The project site is located on the southern side of Pearl City Peninsula in Pearl
Harbor on the south central coastal plain of Oahu The geologic conditions underlying
the Pearl Harbor lochs were the result of a complex series of depositional and erosional
events due to glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Epoch
The Koolau volcanic shield was built during the early Pleistocene Epoch by thinly
bedded basaltic lava flows Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano
alluvial materials were deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the area of
Pearl Harbor The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet below
and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level As Oahu sank coral reef grew
around the shorelines keeping pace with the subsidence of the island
During the Recent Epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the Middle Loch
area was covered with additional sediments consisting primarily of cohesive soils with
localized granular soils over the underlying alluvium Land development and reclamation
projects in the past 40 to 60 years have added man made fills along the shoreline
bringing the site to its present configuration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our preliminary field exploration at the proposed relocated site for the Advanced
SEAL Delivery System Facility indicated that the proposed building footprint is generally
underlain by coralline materials of varying densities extending to a depth of about
11 meters below the existing ground surface Below the coralline deposits our boring
encountered alluvial soils consisting of very stiff to hard silty clays extending to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 16 meters below the existing ground surface
Due to time constraints off shore borings were not performed along the proposed pier
alignment during this preliminary field exploration program
Based on the preliminary field exploration it appears that the building structure
with the exception of the columns supporting the crane loads may be supported on a
shallow foundation system bearing on the recompacted near surface granular soils An
allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for design of
spread and or continuous footings bearing on the recompacted surficial granular soils
Based on our research of old records the proposed building structure may straddle
across natural ground and coral fills placed to extend the original shoreline to its present
location as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Depending on the quality of the previously
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3577 10 Page 8
placed coral fills reclaimed land the building foundations may need to be structurally
connected with tie beams to reduce the potential for differential movements In addition
over excavation of loose coral fills and replacement with engineered fills may also be
required in localized areas This will need to be further evaluated in the design level
geotechnical exploration
Based on our preliminary field exploration and engineering analyses we believe
that on land piles consisting of 419 mm octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles
may be used to support the building columns with the crane loads and to support the
abutment for the pier For the proposed pier structure we recommend that off shore piles

Building Foundations

is our opinion that the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in our report
entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration FY97 MCON Project Q 449 Advanced
SEAL Delivery System Facility Ford Island Naval Station Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
dated May 22 1996 may be adapted for the subject site
We believe that the one story steel framed structure with the exception of the
columns with the heavy crane loads may be supported on shallow foundations bearing
on the recompacted near surface granular soils Columns with the heavy crane loads
should be supported by pile foundations designed in accordance with the

the preliminary exploration results probing and grouting for cavities and or voids at the
footing locations would not be required for this site
Our review of the civil drawings prepared for Victor Dock 4 indicated
that the proposed building structure may straddle across natural ground and coral fills
placed to extend the original shoreline to its present location reclaimed land The
approximate extent of the previously placed coral fills within the proposed building
footprint is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Depending on the quality of the previously
placed coral fills the building foundations may need to be structurally connected with
tie beams to reduce the potential for differential movements In addition over excavation
of loose coral fills and replacement with engineered fills may also be required in localized
areas A design level field exploration should be conducted to obtain additional
information pertinent to the foundation design and footing subgrade preparation for the
proposed building
Based on our preliminary exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to
120 kPa may be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted
surficial granular soils at the project site This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 500 millimeters below the lowest
adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3577 10 Page 10
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footings and the existing concrete slab and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure
of 50 kilonewtons per square meter per meter of depth kKN cu m for footings embedded
in structural fill Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 300 millimeters should be neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used
for footings bearing on the recompacted surficial granular materials
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted surficial granular soils as recommended herein to be
less than 25 millimeters total with differential settlements on the order of about
13 millimeters
Footing preparation work should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines such as sewer and water lines may be required
for the project It should be noted that groundwater was encountered between 1 5 and
2 0 meters below the existing ground surface Therefore any trench excavation
extending deeper than 1 5 meters below the existing ground surface depending on
location and surface elevation may encounter the groundwater level In general a
granular bedding consisting of 150 millimeters of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes If soft loose soils are encountered at the
proposed pipe inverts an additional 500 millimeters of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a
filter fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer for
more uniform support Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about 300
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3577 10 Page 14
millimeters above the pipes or 300 millimeters above the groundwater level to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus
reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 300 millimeters above the
pipes or groundwater level to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of
select granular material or the on site granular soils The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum
200 mm level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 600 millimeters of the trench backfill below the
pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Existing Underground Utilities
Based on our field observations and the information provided we understand that
the project site is underlain by various underground utilities and fuel lines especially in
the area between Building No 691 and Victor Dock 1 at the south side of the project site
We recommend that all existing utilities within the project site be accurately located by the
contractor prior to construction The contractor has the sole responsibility to devise and
implement an effective program to protect the utilities during construction
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in
200 mm loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be
backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure

2 0 to 36 6 meters below the existing ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with hollow stem augers and PQ casing Five additional shallow borings
designated as Boring Nos 3 through 7 were hand augered to a depth of approximately
1 5 meters below the existing ground surface for the collection of soil samples for
environmental laboratory testing The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
California sampler with a 63 5 kilogram hammer falling 762 millimeters The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the last 304 8 millimeters of a drive are shown on

sampled during our field exploration and tested for indication of potential contamination by

environmental laboratory testing The sampling tools were washed with Liquinox
detergent solution prior to each soil sampling event
