INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil investigation
for the proposed Haleiwa Road Drainage Improvement in
Haleiwa Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this study is to determine the general
subsurface conditions and develop pertinent soil data
and soil engineering recommendations for the design and
construction of the proposed drainage improvement

HALEIWA ROAD DRAINAGE IMPROVEMENT P 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed channel improvement alignment is about 3 300
feet long The present plan calls for widening and deepen
ing the existing ditch The new ditch will have a trapezoidal
section with bottom width varying from 30 feet at Paukauila
Stream to 20 feet at the cane haul road
The invert slope begins at 3 0 elevation at the outlet and
slopes up stream at 0 1 percent slope For this improvement
less than 10 feet of cut and less than 5 feet of fill along
the existing stream bank will be required
Major structural improvements consist of a 20 by 8 feet box
culvert at the cane haul road crossing and new wing walls at
the existing box culvert with a concrete bottom lining at the
Paalau Road crossing
SITE CONDITIONS
In general the 3 300 feet proposed improvement section invert
differs less than 2 feet between the two ends
The width of the existing stream is about 15 to 40 feet with
2 1 horizontal to vertical to 1 1 slope banks The slope
surfaces along the stream alignment are covered with dense
vegetation Dense vegetation also covers portions of the
stream bed

Geology
The Watalua Haleiwa area geology is typified by alluvium at
the higher ground and massive sediments of sand and coral
near the shoreline locations These sedimentary deposits
overlie lava flows and confine the underlying artesian
aquifers
The average depth of sediment can be as thick as 200 feet
at Waialua where Kiikii Stream Kaukonahua Stream and
Poamaho Stream brought in huge amount of deposits from
upper plateau areas These deposits thin out toward
Anahulu River With changes of sea level in the past
this area has experienced deposition of both swampy and
coral materials

Soil Conditions
In general the proposed drainage alignment area is composed
of two alluvial layers The upper alluvium varies from 3 to
8 feet in thickness and is a soft to medium stiff reddish
brown to brown silty clay This upper alluvium is directly
underlain by the lower alluvium which consists of dark gray
organic clays and silts interbedded with sand and gravel
layers The consistency of this lower alluvium is soft and
moist The upper soil layer near the stream bank is possibly
fill material that was brought in from nearby high ground
areas to fill up some of the low areas during cultivation
of the sugar cane fields
At Boring 2 a coral sand layer is noted under the upper
alluvium The coral sand layer is dense to hard with some
cavities At Boring 3 a coral sand layer is encountered
under the gray organic silt layer at about 13 feet below
the existing ground surface The sand layer grades to
very loose down to about 43 feet deep before it encounters
a medium dense coral sand layer
At Boring 5 next to Paalaa roadway crossing below the
upper alluvium soil a loose to medium dense tannish
brown silty sand and coral gravel layer is noted from
8 feet down to the bottom of the boring at 20 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Poor subsurface conditions are encountered along the proposed
alignment Therefore all the new imposed loads created by
fill and structure should be kept to a minimum
Earthwork
To construct the proposed stream improvements some excavation
is anticipated to obtain the desired alignment and invert
elevation The earthwork excavation will mainly be in the
alluvium formations No major fills are anticipated for this
project A berm 2 to 3 feet high will be placed on the low
ground areas along the stream bank
GEOLABS HAWAII
HALEIWA ROAD DRAINAGE IMPROVEMENT P 6
Due to the poor subsurface conditions we recommend that
the proposed fill berm for the low area be placed as early
as practicable before the excavation operation This will
allow the underlying soft organic layer time to stabilize
under the new imposed load of the fill berm
Areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and loose top soil Prior to placement
of fill the exposed surface should be wetted to near opti
mum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Additional fill should then be placed in 9 inch loose lifts
and compacted to the above specified density
Slopes
Based on the results of our field study laboratory testing
and review of the available data the presently planned 2 1
horizontal to vertical cut and fill slope ratio is accep
table for the proposed stream bank However unanticipated
loose soil conditions are common in this type of soil and
unstable soil conditions may be created during construction
in localized areas If unstable soil conditions are encoun
tered during excavation they can be stabilized in the field
by flattening the slope or by over excavating the unstable
soilsand replacing them with select borrow material to retain
the loose soils

HALEIWA ROAD DRAINAGE IMPROVEMENT P 7
To reduce the soil erosion along the stream bank slope
erosion protection should be provided during and after
construction
Culvert Structure at Cane Haul Road
Different soil formations were encountered in the two
borings which were drilled on each side of the roadway
A dense to hard coral sand layer with voids was noted
in Boring 2 A coral sand layer was encountered under
a soft organic layer in Boring 3 However the coral
sand layer in Boring 3 was graded from medium dense to
very loose down to 43 feet below the existing ground
surface
Structures supported on different types of soils will
be subjected to differential settlements To minimize
the differential settlement the structure should be
designed for a low bearing value of less than the weight
of soil to be removed for construction at the roadway
crossing At present the embankment at the roadway
crossing acts as a surcharge fill over the area There
fore the planned culvert structure is appropriate for
this subsurface soil condition The allowable bearing
value depends on the size of the structural opening
GEOLABS HAWAII
HALEIWA ROAD DRAINAGE IMPROVEMENT P 8
Based on the proposed 8 by 20 feet box culvert the
bearing value should not exceed 800 p s f This bear
ing capacity may be increased by 25 percent when tem
porary loading conditions are considered To provide
a uniform support a 2 foot layer of granular material
should be provided under the structure Due to the very
loose and soft soil conditions that was encountered along
this drainage alignment the above granular material should
consist of crushed rock material passing the ASTM Designa
tion No 67 gradation requirement
Holes should also be incorporated throughout the slab to
release the hydrostatic pressure build up under the struc
tural slab
If a cavity is encountered in the hard coral sand layer
during the foundation excavation the cavity should be
grouted with lean concrete
Settlement predictions in this type of non uniform soil
formation are empirical With no future additional load
ing on the cane haul road crossing such as raising grades
the settlements should be relatively small The utility
line connected to the structure should be allowed to
adjust its elevation due to differential settlements
For a structure with bearing pressure exceeding 800 p s f
pile foundation may be required

Wing Wall
a Cane Haul Road Crossing
Wing walls should be designed to receive active
lateral pressure equivalent to a fluid density
of 40 pounds per cubic foot if granular soils
are used as backfill material
A bearing value of 800 p s f may be used for
the wing wall footing foundation design To
provide a uniform support under the footing
two feet of the above specified crushed rock
bedding layer is recommended under the wing
wall Since differential settlement will
occur between the walls and the main structure
some provision should be provided at the connec
tion
A coefficient of friction factor of 0 3 can be
utilized to determine sliding resistance of the
retaining structure foundation Weepholes or a
drainage system should be incorporated to prevent
the build up of excessive hydrostatic pressure
behind the wall

Construction Observation
To a degree the performance of any structure is dependent
upon construction procedures and quality Hence observa
tions of site clearing fill placement inspection of foun
dation excavations and stream channel excavation should be
carried out by a soils engineer to permit correlation between
the preliminary soil data obtained during this investigation
and the actual soil conditions encountered during construc
tion
