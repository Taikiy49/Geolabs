INTRODUCTION
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses for the design
of new pavements for the Alii Drive Realignment project between Hawaii Belt Road and Alii
Drive at Keauhou Shopping Center located in the Kailua Kona area in the District of North
Kona on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our literature research and site
reconnaissance and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from
our analyses for the design of pavements only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Kailua Kona area on the west coast of the Island of
Hawaii The existing Alii Drive is located near the shoreline between Kailua Kona and
Keauhou It is proposed to realign the existing Alii Drive by constructing a new two lane
highway toward the mauka side of the existing Alii Drive between Hawaii Belt Road and Alii
Drive at Keauhou Shopping Center Based on the information provided the new alignment will
be approximately 4 5 miles in length and will be approximately 100 feet in width The
approximate limits of the Alii Drive Realignment project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the guidelines provided in the Pavement Design Manual prepared by the
State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and
Research Branch dated May 1995 we understand that a detailed economic comparison of
pavement types is not required if the area of new pavement is less than 4 lane miles and or
a short highway to highway connection is being made between pavements of the same type
Since the new pavements for the project will be on the order of about 9 lane miles we
understand that both flexible and rigid pavements would need to be considered for the
proposed roadway realignment project

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of approximately
4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield volcanoes These are
Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic time
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes up
the west central part of the island The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be
of pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean Pahoehoe is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular
porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lavas These cavities were
formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes intra flow
cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes intra flow cavities are
formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure
which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and pockets
inter flow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions Water is transmitted through the porous
rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along clinker layers
The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they serve as the major water
transmission features
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
As previously indicated the project site is located in the Kailua Kona area on the west
coast of the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 A general
surface reconnaissance was performed in conjunction with seismic refraction surveys along
the Phase portion of the Alii Drive Realignment from July 29 through The
Phase portion of the alignment explored extends from Hawaii Belt Road Queen Kaahumanu
Highway Station 10 00 to Royal Poinciana Drive Station 96 50 for a stretch of
approximately 8 650 lineal feet The proposed alignment had been staked in the field prior to
GEOLABS INC
our site visit however due to the length of time that elapsed since the alignment survey we
were unable to identify the flagging and stake locations in the field at the time of our site
reconnaissance and seismic survey Therefore the alignment locations were generally
identified by reference to the existing street highway crossings landmarks and archeological
sites which were labeled in the field
In general the Phase realignment extends over undeveloped terrain consisting of
historic pahoehoe lava flows erupted from Hualalai Volcano located to the northeast of the site
The site slopes gently to moderately toward the southwestern coastline with elevations ranging
from about 30 feet Mean Sea Level MSL to about 350 feet MSL The ground surface
topography ranges from moderately rocky and irregular to relatively smooth along the
alignment depending on the localized lava flow characteristics
The alignment trends downslope from the intersection with Hawaii Belt Road Queen
Kaahumanu Highway from Station 10 00 to about Station 46 00 Ground surface elevations
range from about 346 feet MSL to about 90 feet MSL In general the overall slope gradient
of the existing ground ranges between about 9 and 11 percent down toward the west The
westward sloping ground surface was observed to have an undulating topography with
elevated mounds which were separated by depressions and troughs The majority of the
ground surface was observed to be covered by loose rocks and some soils representing the
surface clinker lava flow surface However smooth pahoehoe rock formation with little or no
clinker and soil cover were observed in other areas
At about Station 46 00 and extending to Station 96 50 the alignment becomes
approximately parallel to the existing general slope Ground surface elevations along this
portion of the alignment range from about 90 feet MSL to about 38 feet MSL gradually
declining toward the south and west In general the overall slope gradient of the existing
ground ranges between about 1 and 4 percent with localized steeper mounds and depressions
forming an undulating surface topography The majority of this portion of the site was
underlain by relatively smooth pahoehoe rock at the surface with some loose surface cobbles

In general based on observations of the exposed road cuts in the vicinity of the
proposed alignment the site was observed to generally consist of thinly bedded about 1 to
3 feet thick pahoehoe basalt lava flows separated by thin beds of highly irregular clinker
seams The near surface rock exposed in the roadside cuts appeared to range in hardness
from medium hard to hard with frequent irregular clinker seams and voids Indication of very
large lava tubes or exposed voids was not observed at the accessible portions of the

The ground surface was observed to exhibit localized deposits of brown silty ash soils
mantling the underlying rock formation The soil deposits appeared to occur more frequently
in the northern portion of the alignment and in topographic depressions such as broad basins
and localized pockets in the ground surface
Vegetation at the site consists mainly of grasses and shrubs with scattered kiawe and
monkey pod trees Vegetation on the portion of the alignment from about Station 11 00 to
about Station 69 00 was observed to be relatively sparse with good visibility and exposures
of the ground surface Beginning around Station 69 00 and extending through about
Station 84 00 the vegetation was observed to become relatively dense with much of the
ground surface covered by grass and shrubbery and greater occurrences of large kiawe and
monkey pod trees
Where the alignment passes through the existing residential subdivision near Kahakai

residential lots Because the area is developed the vegetation becomes sparse and

Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on our site reconnaissance experience in the area and the current design
concept we anticipate that the pavement subgrade soils will likely consist of compacted 6 inch
minus granular fill materials and or in situ basalt rock formation for the proposed roadway
realignment project Based on our experience with similar subgrade materials we believe that
the 6 inch minus granular fill materials and the in situ basalt rock formation would exhibit a
Resistance R value equivalent to that of aggregate subbase material Therefore a design
R value of 60 was adopted in our pavement analyses for the subgrade materials

Subgrade Preparation Below Pavement Section
Based on the preliminary plans and profiles we understand that cuts and fills up to
approximately 30 feet will be needed for the proposed construction In general the area within
the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of
earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials such as the surface
volcanic ash soils should be removed and disposed of properly off site or disposed of in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive
fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose dense materials The resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted granular fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated where
necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the asphalt concrete base layer
In areas where the pavement subgrades expose dense clinker materials or basalt rock
formation the subgrades should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory roller for a
minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should
be backfilled with well compacted granular fill materials The proof rolling operation should
also be performed at the finished subgrades in cut areas The vibratory drum roller should
travel at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet per minute Due to the potential for presence of
cavities lava tubes blisters pockets etc and soft and or wet soils contract documents
should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and structural fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
If needed a leveling course consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation may be provided on the prepared pavement subgrade prior to the placement of the
ACB The leveling course should be tamped with a vibratory plate roller prior to placement of
the ACB The ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt aggregate placed in one
layer not exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a density of at least
91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity determined in accordance with
AASHTO T 209 or ASTM D 2041
Fill Materials
As previously mentioned we have assumed that the recommended pavement section
will be underlain by structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills in the fill areas For this
project we recommend that the upper 2 feet of fill below the pavement areas located above
a fill section should consist of structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills In general
structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials generally less than
6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass
GEOLABS INC
Imported fill material if required should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The material should have a CBR
value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less Imported
fill materials should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery
to the project site for the intended use
Compaction Requirements
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of rocks
boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of passes bya
compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be conducted at the start
of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a representative from Geolabs
Based on this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion
for adequate compaction For preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be
accomplished by using a large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 15 tons
traveling at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill
surface at least eight times for structural fill

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the aggregate subbase and or subgrade soils The basalt rock formation in this
area generally consists of vesicular porous pahoehoe lava The basalt rock formation in this
area of the Island of Hawaii drains well and may be considered to be highly permeable
Considering the permeable nature of the subgrade materials granular fill materials or basalt
rock pavement drainage for this project is not as critical as in other areas As a minimum
the pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to Carry
surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding
should not be allowed on the site during or after construction Where landscaping is planned
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed to
collect excess water from landscaping irrigation and to reduce the potential for migration of
excessive landscape water into the pavement section
Subdrains should be provided in areas where seepage is observed during and after
grading or as recommended by the Engineer during construction Subdrains should conform
to the requirements of Section 605 Underdrains of the State of Hawaii Standard

Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction minimum

Subsection 706 12 of the HSS with the perforations facing down The subdrains should be
daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for proper discharge
