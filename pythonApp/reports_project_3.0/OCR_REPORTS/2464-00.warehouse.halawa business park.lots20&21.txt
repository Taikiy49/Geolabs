INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the two 2 proposed warehouse buildings at Lot
20 and 21 of the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in
general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes This report was prepared solely for Iots 20 and 21
of the Halawa Business Park The findings and recommendations presented
herein should not be considered applicable to any other lot in the same
vicinity
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct one 1 warehouse building approximately
40 feet by 72 feet in plan dimension on Lot 20 and one 1 warehouse
building approximately 55 feet by 200 feet in plan dimension on Iot 21
of the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided to us the office warehouse buildings will be of
steel frame construction with exterior CMU walls Concrete slab on grade
will be utilized for the building floor Construction of new utility
lines and flexible asphalt pavements for the access roadway and parking
areas are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project

lots The topography of the site is generally level Along the property
boundaries the lots are separated from each other and from other

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the
surface It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide
adequate bearing support for the proposed structure and pavement
However our laboratory testing indicates that these clay soils have
variable expansion potential ranging from low to moderately high
Therefore special grading design will be required to reduce potential
distresses to the proposed structure caused by heaving subsoils Ideally
about 12 inches of off site select borrow fill should be utilized
overlying the existing clayey soils at the future building slab on grade
area Otherwise over excavation of 12 inches of the on site surface soil
and replacement with select material would be required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2464 00 Page 5
The proposed building and retaining wall for both lots are located
adjacent to an existing concrete lined drainage channel at the rear of the
lots To prevent over stressing the channel walls which may cause wall
movement and subsequent foundation settlement the footing foundations
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the channel
As an alternative drilled piers which extend below the depth
specified above may be used below the footings A structural slab may be
used to span the existing rear slope Hard drilling for the piers should
be anticipated since cobbles and boulders were encountered in the borings
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is planned
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
Due to the variable expansion potential of the subsoils encountered
near the anticipated finish subgrade level we recommend that the areas
below the buildings be over excavated to a depth of at least twelve 12
inches below the slab subgrade level and backfilled with select
non expansive material
The bottom of the over excavation level should be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to above optimm moisture
content and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2464 00 Page 6
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than three 3
inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
The replacement fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structures The subgrades of all footings should be scarified and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
footings bearing on the select fill material which has been recompacted
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Foundations located next to the existing drainage channel should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom
edge of the drainage channel
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
removed prior to concrete placement

W O 2464 00 Page 7
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the
foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Drilled Piers
As an alternative drilled piers which extend to a depth below the
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane specified
previously may be used to support the rear of the building adjacent
to the drainage channel The use of the drilled piers would greatly
reduce the excavation and backfill required behind the channel walls
Hard drilling should be anticipated since numerous cobbles and
boulders were encountered during our field exploration
Estimated column loads of 27 kips and wall loads of 3 kips per foot
were provided by the structural engineer Allowable vertical loads
of 15 kips for an 18 inch diameter drilled pier and 40 kips for a
30 inch diameter pier are recommended A structural slab to span
over the existing slope located along the rear lot areas is
recommended to eliminate lateral earth pressures on the piers
If this drilled pier alternative is selected additional information
on the structural details and grading is needed to evaluate lateral
loading on the drilled piers and to finalize the pier design
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2464 00 Page 8
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the
geotechnical engineer in order to see that adequate foundation
support is achieved Since the majority of the loading capacities
come from the frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ
soils extra care should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole
selection of concrete mix design and the placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is
completed is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The
concrete mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to
maintain high frictional contact between the in situ soils and pier
concrete The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to
avoid segregation of the aggregates
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are planned along the east west and north borders of
Iots 20 and 21 The guidelines for the retaining wall designs are listed
below
1 The following lateral earth pressures may be used
Walls unrestrained at top 40 p c f equivalent
active case fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 55 p c f equivalent
at rest case fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be included
where applicable
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff
on site soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2464 00 Page 9
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on or adjacent to slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
On sloping areas the wall footing should be embedded an
additional amount to provide a minimum 10 foot horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the wall
footing to the slope face
5 The backfill behind the retaining wall should consist of
non expansive granular materials generally less than 3 inches in
maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine
is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in
maximum 10 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimm of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce
future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also be
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2464 00 Page 12
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Revi
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading drilling of
the pier holes and construction of the foundations This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
We recommend that the drilling of the pier holes be inspected by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained
