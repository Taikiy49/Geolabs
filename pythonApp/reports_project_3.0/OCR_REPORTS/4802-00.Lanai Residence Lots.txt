SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Lanai Residence Lots located in Lanai City on the Island of
Lanai Hawaii The site is located on a 50 acre lot portion and
14 01 portion The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of the mass
grading construction single family house structure foundations retaining wall foundations
road pavements utilities excavation and fill placement only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
It is proposed to develop the subject lot into a single family residential subdivision
with 35 lots It is anticipated that the proposed residences will consist of light one and
two story structures A grass lined privately maintained drainage channel will be
constructed along the northern project boundary Site improvements will include
roadways lot grading drainage system water system street tree planting and lighting
system Details for the proposed structures were not available when this report was
prepared

2 1 Site Description
The project site is located at the west end of Fifth Street in Lanai City on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii The project site covers an area approximately 10 acres and is
bounded by existing single family houses to the east and undeveloped pasture land on
the north south and west sides
The site is generally flat and appeared to be used as a pineapple plantation for
many years as indicated by a few old irrigation ditches and scattered pieces of black
plastic sheeting used for plant protection Currently the site is an open and unused
parcel with some wild deer grazing freely Dried shrubs cane grass and berry bushes
were observed throughout the project site A recently formed drainage ditch was
observed that appears to carry the runoff from the new subdivision at the eastern
boundary of the project site It was also noted that the eastern edge of the project site
was used for gardening storage and as a disposal site for residents living along
western side of lliahi Street
Based on the topographic plan provided the existing ground surface elevation at
the site ranges from approximately 1 600 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the northeast
corner of the property to approximately 1 562 feet MSL at the southeast corner of the
property

3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the proposed single family
house structures may be supported on shallow foundations The shallow foundations
should be embedded a minimum of 30 inches and bear on properly compacted fill or
in situ material
Due to the potential for differential settlement caused by varying cut fill thicknesses
along the cut fill boundary we believe that the shallow fill and cut portion of the lots should
be over excavated by 2 feet below the finish grade and recompacted
Our laboratory testing on the clayey silt silty clay materials indicated moderate to
high expansion potential for shrink swell when subjected to changes in the moisture
content To reduce the potential for structural distress to the future slabs on grade
resulting from the potential swelling of the surface clayey soils we recommend that
concrete slabs on grade be supported on at least 30 inches of low expansive select fill
materials Special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report to reduce the potential for
shrink swell effects on concrete slabs
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Our laboratory test results indicated a moderate to high swell potential of the on site
silty clay material Therefore the on site silty clay material generally will not be
suitable for fill material within 3 feet of finish grade unless special foundation design

materials The fill material below 5 feet from finished subgrade may consist of
larger sized material 12 inches in largest dimension provided that the material is
well graded
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Inc during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining
the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where
compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be applied
with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the fills and
subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important
requirement for the use of the on site clayey soils

Cutand Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the test pits it appears that
most of the cut slopes would expose soils consisting of medium stiff to very hard
silty clay In general we believe that cut slopes into the soil material may be cut at
an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Slope
benches are recommended at maximum 20 foot vertical height intervals Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
We recommend that fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed
by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted
slope face Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be
accepted In the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching
requirements should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design
grade with sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for
erosion of the finished slopes

3 3 Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface clayey
soils with moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting
from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the single family houses be properly prepared
and capped with at least 30 inches of non expansive select granular fill material capping
fill below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new
slab on grade footprint a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and
the underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final
placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped at 2 to 5 to
shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Gravel strips
adjacent to slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils

foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Test Pits at the
appropriate sample depths
Eight one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively remolded samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were
run on air dried samples Results of these swell tests are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits Test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Graphic representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a
selected sample of the near surface soil to determine the maximum dry density and
optimum moisture of the on site soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement
subgrade support Results of these CBR tests are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
