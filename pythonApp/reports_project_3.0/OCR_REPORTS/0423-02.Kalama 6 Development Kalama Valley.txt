GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Approximately 80 of the area investigated was covered by
large boulders ranging in size from 1 to 700 cubic feet
These surface boulders cover an average of 2 to 3 feet of
black highly plastic boulder CLAY with slickensides re
ferred to as adobe type clay in our previous reports
As much as 9 feet of this black CLAY with slickensides
was observed in test pit 20 A
Underlying the black boulder CLAY large boulders to 700
cubic feet with point to point contact and having a small

the investigation
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The upper western portion of the property at the head of
Kalama Valley has generally steeper slopes than
the lower west slope Approximately 2 feet of black highly
plastic creep susceptible CLAY with slickensides was dis
closed in the test pits This creep susceptible material
was observed to a depth of 9 feet in TP 20 A Very large
to 700 cubic feet boulders were encountered both on and
below the ground surface

necessary removal of the creep susceptible material

1 Grading

susceptible CLAY must be over excavated to firm material
tuffor rock and replaced with a compacted structural
fill see Plate 2
Cut slopes in moderately weathered basalt should be no
steeper than 1H 1V Cut slopes in competent unweathered
basalt should be no steeper than 4 H 1V
All cut slopes in rock should be inspected by the soils
engineer and or geologist during construction to deter
mine the competence of the material with regard to
proposed slopes
