2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in
age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed
a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau
During the later stage of its activity the composition of the lavas from Waianae
shifted towards andesitic or trachytic basalts A series of events along the southerly rift
zone formed several composite cinder and lava cones including Puu Makakilo and Puu
Palailai Lavas in these cones are typically very dense with a platy structure resulting from
horizontal jointing The project site is situated on the southern facing slope of Puu
Makakilo
In situ chemical weathering of the cinder and lava formed a mantle of residual soils
that grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material
and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks Spheroidally weathered
boulders are typical in the residual soils and saprolite The saprolite grades to basaltic
rock formation with depth
Rapid erosion of the residual soils possibly due to deforestation or agricultural
practices has stripped most of the residual soils on Puu Makakilo Numerous
lag deposited boulders are found on the ground surface Poor drainage along with arid
weathering conditions has resulted in the deposition of expansive montmorillonitic clays
Adobe in many surface areas

2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located east of Makakilo Drive south of Panana Street and to the
north and east of the Makakilo Fire Station in the District of Kapolei on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii
In general the site slopes down toward the south at gradients of about 8 to
15 percent Local gradients within the site approach 50 percent The eastern boundary
of the site borders a drainage course which drains Panana Street and the surrounding
residential developments The ground elevations range from 628 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL at the southern end of the project site to about 728 feet MSL near the
northwestern corner of the project site at the intersection of Makakilo Drive and Panana

The majority of the project site was covered with a sparse growth of dry grass and

north to south Outcrops of in situ weathered basalt formation could be seen at some
locations on the site At the time of our field exploration it was noted that the soils in the
large drainage course on the eastern side of the site were very wet Vegetation in this
drainage course was lush when compared to the remainder of the site and its vicinity

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material Previously
placed fill was noted in the southern portion of the site These fills should be
over excavated and recompacted
Keying and Benching
Although the average slope on the site is on the order of about six horizontal
to one vertical 6GH 1V there are local areas of steeper slopes In addition the silty
clay soils slopewash and adobe near the surface of the site may not provide
stable support for the deep fills planned at the site Therefore extensive keying
and benching is recommended for the earthwork to be performed at the site
These keys and benches should be firmly seated in the weathered basalt rock
formation underlying the site
At the toe of the mass fill a keyway should be cut down to the weathered
rock formation This key should be a minimum of 5 feet in depth at the toe of the fill
and should be a minimum of 25 feet in width extending back under the toe of the fill
The floor of the key should be sloped back under the base of the fill at about
10H 1V to bond the fill into the existing slope
As fill placement progresses up the existing slope benches should be cut
into the existing slope to bond the mass of the fill to the existing ground These

Fills and Backfills
General fills below 3 feet from finished grade may consist of soil and rock
materials less than 6 inches in size with a CBR value of 5 or more and with a
maximum swell of less than 3 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 Structural fill and imported fill should be used for the top 3 feet of fill
and consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral mudrock
basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use The
thickness of the structural fill layer should be a minimum of 3 feet Imported fill
materials should be observed and or tested by Geolabs for their suitability prior to
being transported to the site for the intended use

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill structural fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill materials is an
integral part of the fill placement recommendations provided herein Compaction
should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of
acceptable compaction equipment
Base course and_ select borrow subbase materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction

3 2 House Foundations
The planned site grading for this project calls for the placement of fill over the
majority of the site Foundation conditions will depend largely upon the quality of the
structural fill used to cap the deeper general fill materials Therefore these
recommendations should be considered to be preliminary and subject to revision if the
capping fill differs from the materials described above If our recommendations for
structural fill are followed we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings
may be used to support the proposed single family residential homes
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of single family residential house foundations bearing on properly
compacted structural fill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the existing grade Footings constructed near tops of slopes
or on sloping ground should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope
face
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted structural
fill as recommended herein to be less than one 1 inch total Differential settlement is
estimated to be less than one half 2 inch
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should
be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be deepened to the underlying firm materials

Footings located adjacent to retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to

Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded

3 5 Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed roads The
preliminary recommended pavement section consists of 2 inches of asphaltic concrete and

3 6 Utilities
It is anticipated that utilities will be constructed for the proposed project The
methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all
applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and
installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing
should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site fill soils or select granular material
The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project
site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill
material

3 7 Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the house siructures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be
diverted far away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to
slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away
from walls and foundations
