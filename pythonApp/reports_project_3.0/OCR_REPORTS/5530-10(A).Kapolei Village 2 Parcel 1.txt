PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site known as Parcel 1 is located at Kapolei Village 2 within the
existing Villages of Kapolei development in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The planned development consists of nine residential buildings generally up to
two stories in height Slabs on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will
support the proposed residential structures The project also includes the installation of
new utilities including sewer water and drainage lines as well as construction of new
roadways to serve the residential development A Project Location Map and a Site Plan
are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
Based on the topographic survey map the project site is relatively flat
Therefore minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet thick will be required for
the grading work to achieve the final design grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Kapolei Village 2
Parcel 1 The Villas at A eloa project from to The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills
on the order of about 2 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades Paradigm
Construction LLC performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site
grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported materials utilized
as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method The compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1
through 4 14 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs
and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation including a licensed geotechnical engineer should be
obtained in the planning design and construction of future houses and improvements
