PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 78 Kahoa Road in the Honolii Pali area of South Hilo on
the Island of Hawaii The project site consists of an existing residential dwelling situated at
the top of a cliff overlooking the ocean The cliff is estimated to be on the order of about
100 feet high The dwelling includes an existing deck that overlooks the cliff and ocean
The existing deck has experienced some structural distress as evidenced by some
floor cracking settlement and possible creep movement Approximately half of the deck
structure is supported by slab on grade construction and the other half is supported by steel
posts and beams with floor joists We understand that it is desired to rehabilitate or
reconstruct the existing deck located at the subject site

The project site is located on the lower eastern flank of Mauna Kea The area is
typified by some tall and steep cliffs composed of weathered volcanic rock bordering the
ocean The regional volcanic deposits consist of lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
that are approximately 200 000 years old The lava flows have been deeply weathered to
form in situ residual and saprolitic soils completely weathered rock that grades to less
weathered basalt rock materials with increasing depth With the passage of time the
geomorphic evolution of the cliff is believed to occur by repeated slope failures landslides
acting on the cliff face and ocean wave erosion of the cliff base
Based on our observation of the cliff exposures present at the project site it appears
that the site may be underlain by some near surface clayey residual and saprolitic soils
overlying weathered basalt rock materials The upper half of the exposed cliff appears to be
composed of the residual and saprolitic soils The lower half of the exposed cliff appears to
consist of moderately to highly weathered basalt rock Deposits of rounded basalt rock
boulders cover the base of the cliff shoreline and the near shore ocean floor
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the crest of the east facing Honolii Pali located in the
vicinity of Honolii in South Hilo on the island of Hawaii The project site is bounded toward
the east by a steep cliff Honolii Pali and the ocean Kahoa Road bounds the project site
toward the westerly direction The existing home is approximately 30 years old and
consists of slab on grade construction situated on a relatively flat to gently sloping parcel
located at the top of the Pali cliff overlooking Hilo Bay

Cliff Description
The upper half of the exposed cliff face including the slope bench area previously
mentioned has been coated with gunite therefore visual observation of the soil rock
materials composing the upper slope face was not possible Based on the information
provided we understand that the gunite facing may have been constructed following a
landslide that occurred several years ago Based on our observation the gunite facing
was constructed with a wire mesh base and contains multiple small diameter drain pipes
protruding from the gunite at a distance of about 20 feet below the top of the cliff The
drain pipe installations appear to provide for drainage of seepage water from behind the
gunite facing
Based on our observations the upper portion of the cliff appears to be composed
of stiff clayey silt representing residual and saprolitic soils overlying weathered basalt rock
formation The harder basalt rock was observed to be exposed on the lower portion of the
cliff face The cliff appears to stand at an inclination of about one quarter horizontal to one
vertical 4H 1V or steeper with some slight overhang and undercut segments Several
concave shaped erosional features are located along the top and upper portion of the cliff
face These features generally indicate that some slope failures have occurred in the past
The most notable of these features is located where the deck is supported by the post and
beam construction Available records do not indicate whether the present deck was
reconstructed with the post and beam foundation following a slope failure event
Based on our observations the coastal cliffs in the vicinity of the project site are
mostly free of vegetation and show similar topographic features and geologic exposures
It appears that the upper 15 to 20 feet of the cliffs may be most susceptible to slope failure
and small scale soil avalanches in response to natural weathering and erosion processes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our observation and evaluation of the existing site conditions we believe
that the structural distress of the deck may be caused primarily by the deterioration of the
existing steel post and beam components of the deck construction The slab on grade
portion of the deck appears to be in fair condition with generally few cracks in the tile floor
surface The cracks that were observed were located near the transition of the deck
It is our opinion from a geotechnical engineering viewpoint that rehabilitation or
reconstruction of the deck may be very costly due to the precarious position of the deck
located along the top edge of the existing cliff Furthermore under the present conditions
the margin of safety against future slope regression in response to naturally occurring slope
erosion is minimal A slope failure occurring along or near the top of the slope fronting the
existing structure could significantly jeopardize the integrity and stability of the dwelling In
addition the potential for seismically induced ground vibrations is a strong possibility at the
site given the seismically active nature of the Island of Hawaii Therefore we believe that it
would be prudent to avoid supporting any new improvements within a zone of about 20 feet
laterally measured from the top of the cliff
Although the presence of the gunite over the cliff face is a positive element the thin
layer of gunite is not a permanent solution for stabilizing the steep cliff face composed of
weathered rock materials Given the site conditions we believe that it would take a
substantial foundation system in order to support the new improvements located within
20 feet laterally of the top of the cliff Therefore a more extensive geotechnical exploration
program involving deep borings should be implemented if any new construction is proposed
at the site to further evaluate the stability of the cliff face and subsurface materials In
addition we recommend that a lateral setback distance of at least 20 feet measured laterally
from the top of cliff face be maintained for planning of new structures at the site
Consideration should also be given to replacing the existing dwelling with a new
residence which should be constructed by taking into consideration the existing site
conditions and constraints related to the cliff face At this time we believe that new
construction within proximity to the cliff face will need to be supported on a drilled shaft
foundation system The depth of the drilled shafts will need to be determined based on
further geotechnical exploration and analyses which is beyond the scope of our current
services Construction of structures and decks within the zone between the cliff face and
20 feet of the top of cliff will need to be cantilevered from the more stable zone behind the
preliminary setback line If it is desired to reconstruct the existing deck a detailed
geotechnical engineering subsurface exploration should be conducted to evaluate the
subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed construction and foundation design
