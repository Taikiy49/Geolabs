INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our percolation testing performed for the design
of the dry wall system of the proposed Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in the
town of Hilo on the island of Hawaii
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated January
2 1991
The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
proposed layout of the proposed church building and parking areas is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized soil data set to aid in the design
of the dry wells for the development
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of two 2 borings at the
project site to depths ranging between 18 5 and 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface percolation testing and engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
the development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on kilauea Avenue in the town of Hilo on the island
of Hawaii
The site currently has three 8 buildings and one 1 satellite dish existing upon
it The buildings have an A C driveway and parking stalls around them
The southern and eastern boundaries of the site are covered with grasses and
some trees The site is generally level with the exception of the southeast corner which
slopes down gradually towards the southeast

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Subsurface Runoff Disposal
It is our understanding that dry wells are required for surface runoff disposal at the
project area The most appropriate drainage layer is the clinker layer generally
encountered at about 15 to 20 feet deep in our exploration
Based on our field percolation test performed in Boring B 3 the measured
coefficient of permeability is on the order of 5 X The actual size of the
subsurface disposal system will depend on the design quantity of the future surface
runoff The details of the field percolation test are presented in Appendix C
It should be pointed out that for the subsurface disposal system the loss of
infiltrative Capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors Other factors such as rainfall perched groundwater
level etc may also affect the permeability to some extent Therefore the disposal
system may probably require periodic maintenance

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction phases of the dry well installation
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommenda
tions and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as borings Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations given in this report

The location of the field data points were determined by taping from structures
indicated on the Site Grading Plan by Paul Louie and Associates dated September 1990
Elevations of the data points were determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the provided plan The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross section are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two 2 borings to
depths of 18 5 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soils
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples were obtained by an HQ wireline core barrel The core recovery
REC and rock quality designation RQD values are shown on the boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
