REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age
GEOLABS INC
W O 4374 00 Page 5
As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau banked against its eroded
eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site
is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to the
south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine sediments and
corai aigai reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the sedimentary
wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to over 1 000 feet at
Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or
caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition of sediments has
continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at correspondingly
higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by
fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and
reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused
consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials
Agricultural development within the last 100 years and recent mass grading work have brought
the Kapolei area to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site and the design finished
floor elevation we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the proposed new Palama Meats Facility Foundations
for the new Palama Meats Facility should bear on the recompacted on site soils or new
compacted fills Due to the expansive nature of the on site soils footings should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grades
Based on our laboratory tests the surface clayey fills exhibit moderate to high
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture content Therefore
special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements and slab on grade
design for this project To reduce the potential for distress to the future slabs on grade
resulting from swelling of the clayey soils we recommend that a minimum of 24 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material be provided below the building slab cushion We
also recommend that the 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill material be provided
below the sidewalk slabs

foundations resulting from water infiltration into the subsurface we recommend that a concrete

Palama Meats Facility The 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill recommended

this report
Observations during our field exploration indicate that abundant shrinkage cracks exist
at the existing ground surface across the project site The existing shrinkage cracks if left
unattended may increase further in size under prolonged dry weather conditions Shrinkage
cracks located adjacent to the building foundations are potentially detrimental since water can
readily infiltrate through the crack opening and soften the subsoils around the building
foundations thus affecting their performance In addition the near surface soils appear to
have a moderate collapse potential As a result special attention should be given to the
recommended site preparation procedures prior to the fill placement operation
In general we recommend that the exposed subgrades after clearing and grubbing
be proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist
in detecting and collapsing near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage
cracks Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation should be over excavated
and backfilled with general fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative

Based on the grading concept and the generally stiff dense subsoil conditions
encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be
used for support of the proposed new building construction An allowable bearing pressure
of up to may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the
recompacted stiff dense on site soils or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished
grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the stiff dense on site soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation may be backfilled with the on site soils compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction or the bottom of footing may be extended down to the underlying
competent materials For footings bearing on new compacted fills the bottom of the footing
excavations should also be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior
to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope

Slabs On Grade
As previously indicated the majority of the project site is generally underlain by a
surface layer of moderately to highly expansive clayey fill soils Unless slabs on grade
constructed above these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey
soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress
resulting from swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be
properly prepared prior to fill placement In addition a minimum of 24 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material should be provided below the building slab areas
The non expansive select granular fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the building
a minimum of 24 inches or extend to the edge of the concrete sidewalk surrounding the
building and should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
To further reduce the potential for distress to the building slabs on grade and
foundations resulting from water infiltration into the subsurface we recommend that a concrete
sidewalk with an 8 inch deep thickened edge be provided around the perimeter of the
proposed new Palama Meats Facility The 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill
recommended under the building slabs should extend to the edge of the concrete sidewalk
Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the sidewalk with
expansion joints at right angle intersections In addition landscape gravel strips should not
be installed adjacent to the building
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation be placed below the slabs The open graded gravel cushion fill would serve as a
capillary moisture break and would provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce the
potential for future moisture infiltration through the slab and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist sand may
be considered above the plastic membrane to provide for protection of the membrane and to
aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the 2 inches of fine sand above the vapor

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressures

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or yielding
areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill

Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
Our field observations indicate that abundant shrinkage cracks are present at the
existing ground surface across the project site In general shrinkage cracks will
develop in the ground due to the lack of maintenance of the soil moisture content as
the surface soils start to dry out under the sun and dry weather conditions The
development of shrinkage cracks in the ground was apparently intensified by the
prolonged dry climatic conditions characteristic of the Kapolei area resulting in large
fluctuations of the soil moisture content
If the existing ground cracks are left unattended for an extended period of time and are
allowed to penetrate deeper below the surface the ground cracks could adversely
affect the performance of the future building foundations The ground cracks could
serve as conduits for water to percolate into the foundation soils and cause loss of
strength of the foundation soils resulting in potential settlement of the building structure
In addition migration of the foundation soils into the openings of the shrinkage cracks
could undermine the foundation system and result in potential settlement of the building
structure In addition the near surface soils appear to have a moderate collapse
potential Therefore remedial work to correct or mitigate the observed ground cracks
should be implemented prior to fill placement and construction of the proposed

Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a

by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill materials recommended below the
building slabs and sidewalk slabs should be placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement of the select
granular fill for the pavement structural section should be increased to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Aggregate base course and subbase materials should
be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
New fills placed against sloping grounds steeper than 5H 1V should be benched into
the existing slope to provide a well compacted and stable mass The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above fill placement requirements Where new fill slopes will be
required for this project the fill slopes may be designed using a slope inclination of
2H 1V or flatter
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are critical
elements of the earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by
Geolabs during the site grading operations to assist the contractor in obtaining the
required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content on each fill lift Where
compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be applied with

In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes for support of the utility lines
The initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material be used as the

initial trench backfill to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes

finished subgrade should consist of the approved on site soils or general fill materials The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content

compact the backfill materials at the project site Compaction efforts by water tamping jetting
or ponding should not be allowed considering the expansive soil conditions at the site
Drainage
As previously indicated proper surface drainage is critical to the performance of
foundations and slabs constructed on expansive soil conditions Therefore the finished
grades outside the structure should be sloped to shed water away from the foundations and
to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems be installed around
the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters
next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the
potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from foundations and
slabs

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling eight
borings extending to depths ranging from about 6 4 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground

truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered in the
borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8

samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Nine one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and remolded
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under surcharge loads
These tests were performed on air dried samples The results of these tests are summarized
on Plates B 1 and B 2
Nine one inch ring volumetric shrinkage tests were performed on relatively undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the shrinkage potential of the near surface soils when
air dried The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 3 and B 4
One one inch ring compression collapse test was performed on a relatively undisturbed
sample to evaluate the compression potential of the upper desiccated material under
surcharge loads without saturation and to evaluate the collapse potential under surcharge
load upon saturation Results of the compression collapse test are presented on Plate B 5
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on
Plate B 6
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 ASTM D 1140 and one hydrometer test
ASTM D 422 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation
characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain
size distribution is provided on Plate B 7
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
The test results are presented on Plate B 8
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk sample
of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content relationships The
test results are presented on Plate B 9
