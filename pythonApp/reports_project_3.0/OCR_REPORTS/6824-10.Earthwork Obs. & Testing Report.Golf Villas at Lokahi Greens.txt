Dear Mr. Soong: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during construction for the Golf Villas at Lokahi Greens project from 
January 6 through December 5, 2014 in accordance with our Additional Services 
Amendment (ASA) dated January 27, 2014. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is in the Ewa Villages area of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, 
with access from Malako Street. The interior lots of the subdivision were graded and 
houses were constructed several years ago. The project consists of constructing one 
and two-story house structures on the perimeter lots around the subdivision along the 
existing golf course. A total of 54 lots ranging from 5,000 to 8,153 square feet in area 
comprise the project. We understand these perimeter lots were mass graded previously 
with the interior lots of the subdivision. The project location and general vicinity are 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1, and a layout of the project site is shown on 
the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the project from 
January 6 through December 5, 2014. Site grading for the project generally consisted of 
over-excavation and removal of up to about 3 feet of on-site clayey soils beneath the 
house structures, walkways, and concrete driveway pavements and replacement with 
non-expansive, select granular fill material. In addition, the project included excavation and 
backfilling of utilities, and pavement subgrade and subbase course preparations.

At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Royal Contracting Company performed the site 
grading. During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site soils and imported fill 
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. Prior to 
fill placement, the subgrade of over-excavation and replacement areas (building slab, 
walkways, and driveways) was scarified to a depth of about 12 inches, moisture 
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 
85 percent relative compaction. In the driveway pavement areas, the select granular fill 
and aggregate base course materials at finished grade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum 
moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum 
dry density. 
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests 
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.35. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
Areas that did not meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until 
the required minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of full-time and part-time field observation of the contractor’s 
grading activity which includes: subgrade preparation, utilities installation, fill and backfill 
placement, compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed 
during the above grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the Golf Villas at 
Lokahi Greens project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report, were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.
