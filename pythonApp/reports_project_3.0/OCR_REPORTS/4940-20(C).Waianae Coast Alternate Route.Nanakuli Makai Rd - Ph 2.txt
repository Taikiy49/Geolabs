the Project Location Map Plate 1

and the results were presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering

drilled shaft foundation and to monitor the drilled shaft foundation construction at the
Nanakuli Makai Road Phase 2 project for conformance with the project plans and
specifications
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of emergency access roads along the
Waianae Coast on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed emergency access roads
will involve improvements of existing roads and construction of new roads to interconnect
existing roads for community use during emergency situations
The Phase 2 portion of the Nanakuli Makai Road project will involve the
construction of the remaining roadway extension for approximately 700 feet The
remaining roadway extension will generally consist of two 12 foot wide travelways with a

6 to 8 foot wide grassed area on both sides As part of the project construction a new
135 foot long double span concrete bridge will replace the existing footbridge over the
Nanakuli Stream crossing Based on the plan and profile drawings provided we envision
that new fills on the order of up to about 8 feet thick will be required to raise the existing
ground surface to the design finished grades along the areas leading up to the new
bridge abutments
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loads provided
deep foundations consisting of drilled shafts were used for foundation support of the new
concrete bridge structure The drilled shafts were designed to derive support principally
from skin friction between the drilled shaft and the upper medium dense sands and
underlying cemented sandstone and soft to medium hard coral formation encountered in
our borings The contribution from end bearing of the drilled shafts has been discounted in
the design due to difficulties associated with obtaining a clean bottom below the water
level during construction
Based on the construction drawings for the project the new bridge structure will be
supported by a foundation system consisting of 36 inch diameter cast in place concrete
drilled shafts Each abutment will be supported by three drilled shafts with a design tip
elevation of 45 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The center pier will be supported by four
drilled shafts with a design tip elevation of 52 feet MSL The drilled shafts for Abutment
No 1 Center Pier and Abutment No 2 were designed based on allowable compressive
load capacities of 250 245 and 305 kips respectively The identification numbers and
approximate locations of the drilled shafts are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 As part of
the construction requirements a bi directional axial load test using an expandable load cell
Osterberg Load Cell was conducted to evaluate the adequacy of the bridge foundation
design
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our deep borings drilled in the proximity of Abutment No 1
Boring No 1 and center pier Boring No 2 locations for the new bridge structure
generally encountered medium dense sands to a depth of about 35 feet below the existing
ground deck surface Cemented sandstone and soft to medium hard coral formation were
then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately
80 5 feet below the existing surface Boring No 3 drilled at Abutment No 2 generally
encountered medium hard coral reef formation interbedded with cemented sandstone
along the entire depth explored of approximately 78 feet below the existing ground
surface
Water level was encountered in the borings drilled at depths of about 5 8 to
10 4 feet below the existing ground deck surface at the time of our field exploration These
depths generally correspond to about Elevation 0 5 and 2 2 feet MSL It should be

LOAD TEST PROGRAM
As part of the project requirements a dedicated load test shaft was installed near
Abutment No 2 at the project site The load test shaft also allowed the contractor to
demonstrate the adequacy of the drilling method and equipment proposed for the
subsurface conditions at the project site The load test shaft was excavated by CMZ of
Hawaii Inc starting on March 28 2005 with the final cleanout and concreting performed
on March 31 2005 The 36 inch test shaft was started by pre drilling approximately
30 feet below the existing ground surface and then installing a 39 inch O D temporary
steel casing to a tip elevation of about 27 feet MSL The remainder of the test shaft was
then excavated with a rock auger under polymer slurry and the bottom cleaned with a
cleanout bucket After cleaning the bottom a reinforcing steel cage equipped with a
13 inch Osterberg load cell and embedment strain gages was placed inside the excavation
and temporarily supported at the surface Concrete was then delivered to the bottom of
the test shaft using a 4 inch diameter steel tremie pipe until the top of the concrete
reached the ground surface The temporary steel casing was removed immediately after
concrete placement
During the test shaft excavation a log of materials encountered was maintained by
an engineer from our office Logs of the load test shaft construction and materials
encountered are provided on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 Detailed information and results of the
load test program are presented in the report prepared by LoadTest Inc entitled Report
on Drilled Shaft Load Testing Osterberg Method Test Shaft 1 Nanakuli Makai
Improvements Phase 2 Waianae dated
PRODUCTION SHAFT INSTALLATION PROGRAM
Based on our observations of the load test program the demonstrated excavation
method and equipment were accepted for installing the production drilled shafts In
addition the design tip elevations were unchanged for the production shafts based on the
results of the load test program
The installation of the production shafts was also performed by CMZ of Hawaii
Inc The contractor began installation of the production shafts on and
completed production shaft installation on May 25 2005 The as built dimensions of the
production shafts are summarized on Plate 3 of this report Representatives from
Geolabs observed the excavation placement of temporary casing and reinforcement
cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production shafts After
excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcement
cages were placed into the drilled holes by use of a crane and temporarily supported at
the surface Rebar cage spacers were used to provide the clearances associated with
a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches between the sidewall of the drilled hole and the

Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for this project
have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and
within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on the load test
results and our observations of the materials encountered during the shaft excavation it is
our professional opinion that the drilled shafts for the project have been installed to
sufficient depths to provide the design compressive load capacities with a minimum safety
factor of 2
