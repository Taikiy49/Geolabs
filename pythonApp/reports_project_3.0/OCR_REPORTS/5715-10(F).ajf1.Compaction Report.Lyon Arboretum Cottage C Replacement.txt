This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during the site grading of the Lyon Arboretum Cottage C

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the renovation and replacement of Cottage C in the
University of Hawaii at Manoa UHM Lyon Arboretum in Manoa Valley on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project site is located in the rear of the valley above Paradise Park A
Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading at the project site
SITE GRADING
We observed the site grading work for the project from through
Site grading for the project generally consisted of cuts up to about 12 feet
deep and fills and backfills up to about 10 feet thick to achieve the finished design grades
In addition the project included pavement subgrade aggregate subbase and aggregate
base course preparations and utility line excavation and backfill
At the on set of earthwork the area within the limits of grading was cleared and
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials Constructors Hawaii Inc was the General
Contractor for the project and RHS Lee Inc performed the site grading During the site

grading operations samples of the on site soils and imported fill materials were obtained
and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In the parking
and driveway pavement areas the subgrade and aggregate subbase and base course
materials at finished grade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
The exception was the building pad subgrade in which high in situ moisture content
soils were encountered and the minimum 90 percent relative compaction requirement
could not be achieved without significant drying and or aerating In addition the project site
is located in a high rainfall environment throughout the year and we believe significant
drying and or aerating of the on site soils would not be feasible and or practical Therefore
we reduced the minimum relative compaction requirement for the building pad subgrade to
85 percent provided the subgrade soils were capped with a minimum 12 inch thick layer
of aggregate subbase material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction It should be noted that the minimum compaction requirement of 90 percent
for the building footing subgrades remains unchanged
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 12 Test locations and elevations were estimated
using on site survey stakes for reference These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas that did not
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required
minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded
Our services consisted of part time as requested field observation of the
contractor s grading activities which include foundation walkway and pavement subgrade
preparation fill and backfill placement compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it

Cottage C Replacement project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered
by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
