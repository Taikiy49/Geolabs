Dear Mr Griffith
Based on discussions with from Sato Associates Inc we
understand that there may be some cut and fill slopes at the project site We believe that the
planned cut and fill slopes may be on the order of about 6 feet or less Therefore we are
providing the following supplemental geotechnical recommendations for the design of cut and
fill slopes planned at the site
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that cut slopes
would expose the stiff clayey soils encountered at the site In general permanent cut slopes
planned at the site should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes constructed using the on site materials or imported fill materials
may also be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes
steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability
of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the
design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
