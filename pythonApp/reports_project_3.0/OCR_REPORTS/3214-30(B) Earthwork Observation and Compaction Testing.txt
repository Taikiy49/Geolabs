performed during construction of the Four Seasons Resort Hawaii project in Kaupulehu
North Kona Hawaii from October 1994 through June 1996 in general accordance with
our Master Services Agreement dated
SITE GRADING
The Four Seasons Resort Hawaii project is located to the north of Kailua Kona in
the District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii The proposed hotel resort site
encompasses an area of approximately 35 acres A portion of the site previously
contained the partially constructed Four Seasons Hotel which has since been
demolished A general layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1
The site was graded for the construction of the Four Seasons Resort Hawaii
project which consists of the following major structures a lobby building Building A
golf clubhouse Building B a ballroom building Building C a tennis center
Building D Complex a restaurant building Building E 37 bungalow units pool grill

The original ground surface elevations at the site ranged from near sea level to

feet in depth and fills ranged from about 2 to 12 feet in thickness
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were cleared
and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials were
removed and disposed of The existing stockpiles of earth materials or brush were
removed to expose firm natural ground prior to grading
In areas near the shoreline the loose beach sand deposits were removed to
expose firm natural basalt formation prior to placement of structural fills for the
bungalow units in the vicinity and the restaurant building Building E The removal of
the loose sand deposits was recommended to reduce the potential for migration or
washing out of the loose sands beneath the new fills in the event of a tidal surge or
tsunami
In areas that received fill the subgrade was scarified to a depth of about
12 inches where possible and compacted to the specified relative compaction based
on visual observations Otherwise the subgrade was proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or similar heavy equipment minimum 4 passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof
rolling operations were over excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled with
compacted fill
Fills and Backfills
The fill and backfill materials used generally consist of well graded granular
materials less than 6 inches in largest dimension structural fill The processed on site
cut and or stockpiled materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension
were used as fill or backfill materials within the upper 2 feet of the finished grades In
GEOLABS HAWAII
Hualalai Development Company Page 3

some areas as indicated on the grading plans the structural fill thickness was
increased to 4 feet below the finished grades to facilitate construction of underground
utilities In general boulders rock fragments greater than 12 inches in dimension were
used as rock fills at the bottom of deeper fills spread out at the bottom and compacted
by tracking using a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar equipment along with heavy
applications of water
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 2 of this report Some of the test results of the imported
materials were provided to us by the supplier of the materials
In general the structural fills and backfills were placed in level lifts of
approximately 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction Backfills behind retaining walls were usually
compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D
1557 Compaction was generally accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory plates
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of similar compaction equipment
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rock boulders and or cobbles For fill materials consisting of these the fills were placed
in near level lifts and compacted to the specified degree of compaction by tracking using
a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar equipment The degree of compaction was based
on visual observations since the over sized nature of the fill material precluded field
density testing of the fill materials
Periodic field density tests were taken when possible during the site grading
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and
soil conditions were noted The field density tests were taken to determine the relative
compaction of the fills placed for conformance with the specified minimum compaction A
total of 266 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the
nuclear probe method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The
results of the compaction tests taken during construction of the Four Seasons Resort
Hawaii project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 16 of this report These tests

Structures
The following provides a brief description of the earthwork and foundation
construction activities observed by Geolabs Hawaii at the various major structures across
the site
BUILDING A
The grading at the location of Building A Lobby Building generally consisted of
placing structural fill materials 6 inch minus materials to raise the pre existing
ground surface to the design elevations The pre existing concrete floor slab
located within the limits of Building A was demolished and removed to expose the
underlying ground Prior to the placement of structural fill the pre existing ground
surface was proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9 or similar heavy equipment a
minimum of four passes to help in detecting and collapsing potential lava tubes
and or cavities near the existing ground surface Based on the observations of our
field representatives there were no obvious signs of voids and or cavities noted
during the proof rolling of the subgrade at Building A
BUILDING B
Site grading for Building B Golf Clubhouse consisted of ripping and cutting
down the pre existing grades to the design grades of approximately 10 feet MSL
Foundations for Building B were designed to bear on the native basalt rock
formation therefore a program of probing and grouting the foundations of Building
B was implemented The probing operations were conducted by Goodfellow
Brothers Inc using an air track drill on and the grouting operations
were conducted on The grout used to fill the probe holes consisted
GEOLABS HAWAII
Hualalai Development Company Page 5
W O 3214 30 B
of a sand cement grout mix supplied by West Hawaii Concrete Company Based
on injection times needed to fill the probe holes and cavities encountered we
estimate approximately 7 cubic yards of grout was used fo fill the 27 probe holes
BUILDING C
The grading at the location of Building C Ballroom Building consisted of
excavating some pre existing rubble fill from within the footprint of the building
excavating the native basalt rock formation exposed at the surface to a depth of
about 3 feet below the bottom of footing elevations and replacing the over
excavation with about 3 feet or more of structural fill materials The purpose of the
over excavation of the basalt rock subgrade to a depth of 3 feet below the bottom
of footing elevations is to eliminate the need for probing and grouting the footing
locations for this building As a result the foundations for Building C were
designed based on a fill condition Our recommendations for this change in the
foundation design were presented in a letter entitled Ballroom Building Building
C Foundations dated March 21 1995
After over excavation of the basalt rock subgrade to the minimum depth of 3 feet
below the bottom of footing elevations the exposed basalt rock subgrade within
the footprint of Building C was proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9 or similar heavy
equipment with a minimum of four passes to help in detecting and collapsing
potential lava tubes and or cavities near the over excavated subgrade level There
were no obvious signs of voids and or cavities noted by a field engineer from
Geolabs Hawaii during the proof rolling and grading operations of the basalt rock
subgrade at the site for Building C After the proof rolling operations the over
excavation was backfilled with structural fill materials consisting of relatively well
graded 6 inch minus granular fills placed in near level lifts and compacted to the
specified degree of compaction Due to the over sized nature of the 6 inch minus
structural fill material field density testing of the fill materials was inappropriate
therefore relative compaction was based on visual observations
BUILDING D
The area existed as native rocky ground with no prior earth fills in some areas and
areas with fills and rubble fills within the footprint of Building D and the tennis
center The rubble fills consisted of large pieces of concrete slabs columns
footings etc with reinforcing bars and other construction debris These rubble fill
materials were over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting over

slabs columns and footings were re used as a source of fill material by placing

then moisture conditioned and compacted The rubble fills below Building D pad
were placed under the observation of our field engineer and in general accordance

BUILDING E
The grading at the location of Building E Restaurant Building generally
consisted of placing structural fill materials 6 inch minus materials to raise the
pre existing ground surface to the design elevations Since the building site is
located over some beach sand deposits the beach sand deposits were excavated
to expose dense basalt formation prior to placement of any fills for the building
pad After excavation of the beach sand deposits to expose the basalt rock
subgrade the basalt rock subgrade was proof rolled to help in detecting and
collapsing potential lava tubes and or cavities near the existing ground
surface No obvious signs of voids and or cavities were noted by our field
engineer during the proof rolling of the subgrade After the proof rolling
operations the ground surface was raised to the design grades by filling with
structural fill materials consisting of relatively well graded 6 inch minus granular
fills The structural fill materials were placed in near level lifts and compacted to
the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations since the
over sized nature of the 6 inch minus structural fill material precluded field density
testing of the fill materials
BUILDINGS L AND Q
The pads for Buildings L and Q consisted of only minor cuts and fills to achieve
the design pad elevations The fills were generally placed in controlled lifts
moisture conditioned and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
based on field density tests and visual observations by a field engineer from
Geolabs Hawaii The grading operations at the site for the Buildings L and Q
pads occurred in February 1996

BUILDING P
The area at the Building P site existed as native basalt rock formation consisting
of pahoehoe and a a lava flows with minimal prior earth fills The ground surface
elevations range from about 4 to 16 feet MSL The pad for Building P was
excavated to the design pad elevation of about 4 feet MSL The footings for
Building P were excavated in basalt rock formation and the irregular footing
excavations were backfilled with lean concrete to the bottom of footing elevations
The foundations for Building P were constructed directly on the lean concrete
bedding After the walls for Building P were erected backfills behind the walls
were placed in controlled lifts moisture conditioned and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction based on field density tests and _ visual
observations by a field engineer from Geolabs Hawaii between May and
July 1995
BUNGALOW UNITS
In general the building pad construction of Bungalows 1 through 37 consisted of
minor cuts and fills Where the beach sand deposits were encountered below the
bungalow pads the loose sand deposits were removed to expose dense basalt
rock subgrade The basalt rock subgrades were proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or other similar equipment Where a cavity or void was detected the
cavity or void was ripped open to expose the lateral extent of the cavity or void and
backfilled with 12 inch minus material to existing grade Structural fills were then
placed in 1 foot lifts moisture conditioned and compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction Tops of pads were finished off with structural fill consisting of
2 inch minus materials in 6 to 8 inch lifts
Utilities
Installation of the new utility lines generally included excavation in hard basalt
rock formation or fill followed by backfilling the trench excavation after placement of the
utility line In general after placement of pipes in the trenches free draining granular
materials were used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
Trenches were then backfilled with 3 inch minus granular material and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches were located in paved areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the road subgrade was compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Field density tests were taken as
appropriate and are reported on Plates 3 1 through 3 16
GEOLABS HAWAII

Paved Areas
Typically the subgrade materials under the pavement areas were scarified to
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to at least 95 percent relative compaction Field density tests were performed on the
finished subgrade with results presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 16
During excavation for the hotel parking lot very dense basalt was encountered
Therefore the grade was raised and 4 inches of base course material was placed
instead of the typical section The materials were moisture conditioned and compacted
with a single drum vibratory roller to at least 95 percent relative compaction Field
density tests were taken at the finished subgrade
EVALUATIONS
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii during construction of the Four Seasons Resort Hawaii project in the
period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
plans and specifications and the recommendations presented in the above referenced
Geotechnical Engineering Report dated
LIMITATIONS
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities

without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
