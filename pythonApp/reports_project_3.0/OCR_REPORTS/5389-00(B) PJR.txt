Our field exploration along the highway improvement alignment generally
encountered medium stiff to soft surface fill soils on the order of about 6 feet thick
underlain by soft and or loose lagoonal deposits and coralline detritus materials extending
to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 4 7 and 4 8 feet below the existing

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the traffic data

improvement project may consist of 4 inches of AC on 6 inches of ACB over 6 inches of
UTPB on 12 inches of ASB If the incorporation of a drainage layer is not practical the

confirm that the above pavement design sections are adequate
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures For the pavement option incorporating a
drainage layer we recommend that a pavement edge drain system hydraulically
connected to the untreated permeable base be provided along the edges of the new
pavements to collect infiltrated water in the pavement structural section
Due to the shallow groundwater table and the potential presence for soft and or
loose soils at the pavement subgrade level pumping conditions may be encountered
during compaction of the subgrade soils If pumping conditions are encountered during
compaction of the subgrade soils the subgrade may be proof rolled and a woven
geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade
prior to placement of the aggregate subbase layer

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes came to an
end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Koolau Range and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt The Koolau volcanic shield was built by
thinly bedded lava flows during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs The main
shield building activities ceased approximately 2 2 million years ago
After a long period of cessation of the shield building volcanic activity returned with
the formation of a series of lava flows cinder cones and tuff cones such as those
associated with Koko Crater Many of the eruptions were rather explosive in nature due to
the interaction of groundwater with the rising magma resulting in steam explosions which
expelled large quantities of pyroclastic material predominantly ash and cinder Some
explosive eruptions produced ash that cemented together to form a firm rock known as
tuff locally known as mudrock
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level

After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated where

prior to placement of the aggregate subbase layer

3 6 Fill Materials
In general the excavated on site materials may be re used as a source of general
fill and backfill materials up to the bottom of the aggregate subbase layer provided that
they are free of organic materials and contain no lumps or rock fragments greater than
6 inches in largest dimension The on site fill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock or basalt The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more with a maximum swell value of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with AASHTOT 193 or ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 test procedures Select granular fill material should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve In addition the material should
have a minimum R value of 20 or more Imported materials should be brought to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Surfaces should be wetted finished to create relatively smooth unyielding
subgrades and kept moist until covered by pavements Imported materials should be
tested and approved by Geolabs prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use

two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 21 5 feet

equipment equipped with continuous flight augers

examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
