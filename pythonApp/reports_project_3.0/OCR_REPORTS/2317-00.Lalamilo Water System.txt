SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the
activity of 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long
extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
still active
The site is located on the western flank of Mauna Kea mountain which
makes up the northern part of the island and consists partly of the older
Hamakua Volcanic Series The lava flows at the sites are Aa basalt
types with dense zones of hard rock and loose zones of clinker The
clinker phase is highly permeable Lava tubes are generally not
encountered in Aa flows
A thin dry desiccated and erodible layer of volcanic ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash covers the surface of the flows

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations disclosed that the two 2 sites are underlain
by a thin generally less than 2 feet thick layer of volcanic ash mixed
with cobbles and boulders over clinker and lava rock formations From a
geotechnical engineering point of view the sites are feasible for the
proposed construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2317 00 Page 5
The underlying volcanic rock can provide adequate bearing for the
proposed structures
1 0 M G Reservoir
The borings located about 200 to 350 feet west of the new reservoir
site disclosed a thick layer of clinker extending a minimum of 20 feet
below the existing ground elevation Based on the boring information the
clinker appears to be tightly packed with only a little soil matrix
Therefore it is our opinion that the reservoir may be constructed on the
dense clinker layer
Volcanic ash soils extending down to about 7 to 8 foot depths were
encountered at one location west of the 1 0 M G reservoir site
Test data from similar soils in other areas indicate that the ash
soil structure may be subject to collapse when completely saturated
Therefore in order to minimize the possibility of settlement where thick
ash is encountered below the site we recommend that the ash deposit be
removed and stockpiled and the excavation backfilled and compacted with
on site select granular materials Some of the ash soils can be mixed
with the excavated rocky materials for backfilling
Foundation
For the footings bearing on the compacted clinker rock fill or
natural dense clinker an allowable net soil bearing pressure of 4000
p s f may be used This value may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to a grade equal to or deeper than the trench
Alternatively the trench backfill which should be completed first
prior to foundation construction could be compacted to a minimm of
95 of its maximm dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 or
filled with concrete

All footing excavations should be properly backfilled and compacted
to 95 density per the above specifications
The site grading should also be designed to divert surface water away
from the tank foundations
Settlement
Based upon the boring data and the anticipated reservoir loads the
estimated settlements would generally be on the order of less than
one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 4 may
be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the structure
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible loose
pockets or cavities in clinker or lava tubes in the underlying lava
rock the footing excavation should be probed at approximately 10
feet on centers One probe should be drilled at each spread footing
location The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches
and extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 10 feet The
probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extend of the
cavity The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by
the soils engineer or the project inspector to see that these
recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any potential
voids near the footing excavations
Additional soil explorations should be done at the 1 0 M G reservoir
site to verify our recommendations prior to final design

0 5 M G Reservoir
The borings disclosed a thin layer of clinker and volcanic ash
extending about 6 to 8 feet below the existing ground elevations Below
this was a dense hard basalt flow that was more than 15 feet thick
Based on the proposed finish floor elevation for the reservoir at 1085 5
feet the reservoir tank would bear on the dense clinker which could

Foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 4000 p s f may be used for the
design of foundations bearing on undisturbed clinker formation This
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic
loads are considered
Depending on the structure of the underlying rock formation a
choking layer consisting of well graded rock materials may be
required between the underlying rugged rock surface and the upper
cushion fill or concrete slab
Due to the possible occurrence of cavities and lava tubes in the
underlying lava flows we recommend that the footing construction
portion of the project include a program of probing and grouting to
detect potential voids below the reservoir foundations The wall
footing excavation should be probed at approximately 10 feet on
centers One probe should be drilled at each individual spread
footing The probe holes should have a minimm diameter of 2 inches
and extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 10 feet The
probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extend of the
cavity The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the hillsides
from little to 25 feet for the 1 0 M G reservoir site and only about
1 to 3 feet for the 0 5 M G reservoir site Significant fills are
not anticipated
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material
Backfill
If required backfill should consist of select granular material
such as base course or select borrow The backfill should be
compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Fill Material
In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches in size
may be used as general fill material If the excavated rock material
does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material
the surficial ash soil may be mixed into the excavated rocky material
to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better graded
material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills For
fills within 2 feet of finished grade below the structures and for
subsequent structural backfill within the reservoir limits the
maximum size of particle in the fill should be reduced to 6 inches to
facilitate compaction

Fill Placement
Embankment fills if required should be placed in horizontal loose
lifts up to 18 inches thick and compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximum density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
tests strips to determine the lift thickness and number of passes of
a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project The mmber of passes can then
be used as the criteria for compaction
Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the 1 0 M G reservoir site is
underlain at fairly deep depths by dense Aa basalt mantled with a
thick layer of clinker rock mantled with volcanic ash
It is anticipated that the ash and clinker may be excavated with
normal excavating methods Some hard digging may require the use of
a hoe ram and even some blasting if large accretionary lava balls
boulders are encountered Also much of the upper fractured rock
layers can be ripped with heavy equipment
The field exploration indicates that the 0 5 M G reservoir site is
underlain at fairly shallow depths by dense Aa basalt
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the boring core data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine the rock outcrops and the boring data and to make
their own interpretation

Slopes
At the 1 0 M G reservoir site it appears that most of the cut
Slopes will be in varying materials such as ash clinker and dense
rock An average design cut slope of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V is recommended for cuts less than 25 feet high
Soil slopes should be protected as soon as possible after
construction to minimize the erosion potential
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away from
the cut and prevented from flowing across the slope face
Roadway Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the project roads
Based on the results of our soil exploration program and recommended
fill material the recommended pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The recommended sections considers light traffic and is based upon
the assumption that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved
areas
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction and the above design section revised if
necessary

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
_ I actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from our site reconnaissance and soil
information from the borings variations of conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from data
points or structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of
the borings were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
