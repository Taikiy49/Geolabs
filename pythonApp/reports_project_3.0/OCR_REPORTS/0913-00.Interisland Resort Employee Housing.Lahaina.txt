INTRODUCTION
This report presents our preliminary foundation design
recommendations for the proposed employee housing at the
subject site
A total of six borings ranging in depth from 15 1 to 22 0
feet below the existing ground surface were drilled Addi
tionally one test pit was excavated to expose and visually
examine the subsurface materials underlying the site
InterIsland Resorts P 2
The field exploration work was completed on December 20
1979 The boring and test pit locations are shown on
the attached Site Plan Plate 1 Description of the
materials encountered along with laboratory test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings and Test Pit Plates
A 1 through A 7
Preliminary design data was verbally transmitted to Mr
Ramesh Chandra of Brian J Hughes Associates Inc on
to expedite the design for submittal
ITE CONDITIONS
Generally the site is underlain with loose to medium
dense silt alluvium with some clay pockets occasional
cobbles and boulders This material appears to be uncon
solidated and dry at its natural state exhibiting low
strengths This material was encountered in all the
borings and test pit from existing ground surface down
to the depths explored Ground water was measured in
Boring Nos 4 and 5 at depths of 15 0 and 15 2 feet
respectively below the existing ground surface

InterIsland Resorts P 3
DESTGN RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is underlain with loose to medium dense silt
Maximum design column loads of 200 kips may cause large
amount of settlements if loads are transmitted to the
underlying materials in their natural stage
However removal of overburden soil to achieve the proposed
basement elevation of and densifying about an
additional 3 feet of material under the footing bottom
elevation will help to reduce the potentially large anti
Cipated settlements
The following preliminary recommendations should be followed
for the design of foundations and construction at this
project
1 Foundation Spread and wall footings could be used
to transmit the column loads to the underlying soils
After the removal of material for the proposed base
ment an additional 3 feet of material under the
footing and 2 feet beyond the footing should be
overexcavated and recompacted in thin 6 inch lifts
Each lift should be compacted to a minimum of 95
percent of its maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 test designation
GEOLABS HAWAII
InterIsland Resorts P 4
The bottom of all exposed overexcavated area should
also be compacted to the above specified density
prior to backfilling with the excavated material
The spread footings should be proportioned and
designed using an allowable bearing pressure of
2 500 p s f for the above specified backfill
The allowable bearing value may be increased
up to one third for temporary wind and seismic
loading conditions
The footings should be embedded a minimum of
two feet below the lowest adjacent finish grade
It is estimated that settlements of foundation
up to l inch total are anticipated with a
differential settlement equal to about half
the total settlement
2 Basement Walls Basement walls up to 4 feet high
are planned for the proposed structure The following
parameters may be used for the basement wall design
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
GEOLABS HAWAI
InterIsland Resorts Pp
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should
be included where applicable To prevent hydro
static pressure build up behind the walls a
subsurface drainage system should be used to
relieve the seepage water
over a 4 inch layer of gravelly material such
as 3B Fine rock after proofrolling the entire
slab subgrade to obtain 95 compaction The
gravelly material would provide a capillary
break under the slab during period of high
rainfall
4 Asphaltic Concrete Pavement Based on using the
on site silty material as subgrade for parking
and driveway areas the following pavement
sections are recommended
A C Base
Light Traffice and Parking 1 4
Heavy Traffic 2 6
Subgrade soils should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent of maximum density
GEOLABS HAWAII
InterIsland Resorts P 6
5 Earthwork and Compaction Requirements The site
should be cleared of all the vegetation and debris
prior to any excavation or filling
Excavated select on site silty material should be
used for fill and footing backfill The remaining
Site filling and retaining wall backfill should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 8 inches
thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
test method D 1557
6 Design Review The plans for foundations site
grading and utility construction should be for
warded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in
the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is again recommended that excavations be inspected by
the soils engineer prior to the placement of compacted
fill or footing backfill and foundation concrete to insure
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation and that ade
quate compaction can be obtained for this project
