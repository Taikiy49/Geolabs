INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the three proposed improvements for Kalaheo
High School This report summarizes our findings and conclu
sions
W 22 March 28 1975
Site Description
Kalaheo High School is located on the east facing slope of the
ridge separating Kaneohe Bay and Kawainui Swamp The campus
is at the base of the slope adjacent to Mokapu Saddle Road
The three areas of the campus covered by this investigation
are 1 the proposed parking area on the hillside at the rear
of the Administration Library and Classroom Building
2 the athletic field improvements consisting of extending
the existing athletic field to the south and 3 the ground
improvements consisting of three retaining walls and miscel
Taneous fills along the southeastern property line adjacent
to Mokapu Saddle Road The proposed projects are essentially
independent and will therefore be discussed separately within
this report
Geology
The site is near the center of the ancient Koolau Volcanic
Caldera The mass of breccia that caps Ulumawao Peak and
forms a large portion of the ridge to the north between the
Kawainui Swamp and Kaneohe Bay is composed of angular rock
fragments A maximum thickness of 525 feet of breccia is
exposed and for the most part is quite massive This is com
monly called the Kailua Volcanic Series and is abundant in
quartz and zeolite
Ke
W 3 March 28 1975
The lower portion of the site is bordered by the deposit of
alluvium near the mouth of the Kawainui Canal consisting of
poorly sorted lithofied conglomerate and marine deposits of
stratified clay with lenticular beds of sand and gravel

with the boring logs and laboratory test results

Proposed Construction
The Site Plan Plate 1 shows the scope of the proposed con
struction and the five boring locations It is proposed to
create 105 parking spaces on the hillside slope at the rear
of the Administration Library and Classroom Building The
parking spaces will be constructed on two levels with an
access road leading from Ilipilo Street
The grading will include an upper cut slope two retaining
walls and a lower fill slope The cuts and fills will gen
erally be less than ten feet in height The entrance access
road will be constructed essentially at grade to connect the
parking area to the lower streets
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed essentially uniformed soil condi
tions within the proposed construction area The site is
generally blanketed by 1 to 3 feet of dark brown clayey silt
topsoil This material is stiff in consistency and was
underlain to the depth of the explorations by an extremely
stiff to hard decomposed rock composed of light brown to gray
clayey silt with rock chips
No ground water was encountered within the subsurface explo
rations

General
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the site is underlain by competent decomposed rock which
should provide excellent support for the proposed construction
Foundations

designed for allowable bearing pressures of with
estimated settlements not exceeding inch total and inch
differential

Lateral Earth Pressures
The retaining walls should be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of 35 pcf if compacted on site soils are uti
lized for backfill This equivalent fluid pressure applies
to both the upper wall with a sloped backfill and the lower
wall with level backfill
The retaining wall foundations should bear a minimum of two
feet below the adjoining grade where a passive equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pcf can be added to the sliding resis
tance

field expansion Boring 6 drilled in approximately the cen

Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and any previously placed fills The
ground surface should then be keyed and benched to properly
bond the newly placed fill to the existing soil Prior to
the placement of any new fill the upper six inches of the
existing ground surface should be scarified brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 per
cent of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
D 1557 72T Additional fills should be placed in horizontal
lifts and compacted to the specified density
The backfill behind the retaining walls should be controlled
so that fill is compacted to between 90 and 95 per cent of the
maximum density Excessive compaction effort above the 95
per cent level can significantly increase the lateral earth
pressure above the recommended design values
Compaction operations should be inspected on a full time
basis to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is
consistently achieved The key and bench areas should also
be inspected prior to the placement of any fill for indica
tions of wet zones or zones of seepage Subsurface drains
consisting of 4 inch diameter perforated plastic pipes should
be utilized for the granular backfill to provide for prompt
egress of all subsurface waters

inspected prior to the placement of the concrete or rein
forcing to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
finding of our subsurface investigation
As previously discussed the backfilling and earthwork oper
ations should be inspected on a full time basis This inspec
tion should include the necessary testing to assure that the
proper degree of compaction is consistently achieved Ex _

the above inspections
