INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Elderly Housing project on Philip
Street in Pawaa McCully area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site for an elderly housing project
Based on the information provided to us the proposed building will be a
6 to 7 story structure using masonry walls with concrete planks The
anticipated structural load of shear walls is about 15 to 20 kips per foot
on the average Concrete slab on grade for the ground floor is
anticipated and no basement is planned A parking lot is planned on the
side of the building
Minimal site grading involving about two 2 feet of fill is
anticipated

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project

Okada Partners Inc dated The scope of work consisted
of the following

feet below the existing ground surface

observations of the surficial soil conditions

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the makai end of Punahou
Street fronting Philip Street in the Pawaa McCully area of Honolulu The
Makiki Drainage Canal runs along the western boundary of the site
The terrain of the site which has been previously used as a
contractor s yard is generally level The southern portion of the site
has been used for vegetable growing The existing ground elevation ranges
from about seven 7 to nine 9 feet above Mean Sea Level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
the upper fill and medium dense lagoonal silty sand deposits are not
adequate to support the foundation loads of the proposed 6 to 7 story
masonry structure on spread footing foundations without inducing excessive
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2309 00 Page 5
_ settlements Therefore pile foundations are recommended to support the
proposed structure In general we believe the underlying coral formation
should provide adequate support for the above driven piles
The field boring exploration indicated variable subsoil conditions
below the proposed building site Therefore different pile lengths are
anticipated in order to provide the required capacity for the project
Moreover due to the erratic nature of coral formation it is also
anticipated that locally weak pocket or absence of coral ledge may be
present and some piles may even have to be driven further down into the
older alluvium in order to finally develop the required friction pile
capacity between the surrounding soils and pile shaft
It is also recommended that a test pile program be carefully planned
and executed in order to determine the most economical production pile
lengths and to delineate different production pile length areas
Pile Foundations
Since coral algal formation was encountered in the borings pile
foundations penetrating the upper loose lagoonal deposits and bearing on
the upper 30 foot coral may generally be utilized to support the
structure As discussed above same piles may have to be driven further
down into the underlying older alluvium in order to develop the required
pile capacity from pile friction between the pile shaft and surrounding
soils if local coral ledge is absent or not competent enough
For a 12 inch square prestressed concrete pile an allowable pile
capacity of 60 tons per pile may be used for design Estimated pile
settlements are on the order of less than one quarter of an inch for
friction piles driven into the older alluvium and minimm for piles
bearing on the upper coral formation
Based on the exploration results we estimated that production pile
lengths ranging from about 30 feet to the upper coral formation to 65
feet into the older alluvium below the existing ground surface may be
required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
at least 27 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an energy
level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be reduced to
a lower energy level to minimize the build up of excessive tensile
stresses within the piles which could cause damage to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within loose
soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced
energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the
pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily
increase with depth and reach 8 blows per inch for the last inch
or when aggregated blow counts are 40 blows for the last foot
The above pile driving criteria is based on our preliminary wave
equation analyses performed for a diesel hammer delivering at least
27 000 foot pounds rated energy to drive a 12 inch square concrete pile
for a design load of 60 tons The results of the wave equation analyses
for pile driving are presented in Appendix C
The purpose of the wave equation analyses was to provide the
contractor an idea in selecting hammers for production pile driving
Since the results will vary depending on the equipment the contractor
uses a final wave equation analysis of pile driving should be performed
by the project geotechnical engineer when the actual hammer data are
available

Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles with the use of one or more
selected hammers
2 To verify that predrilling through the surficial fill layer is
not required
3 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths and
delineate different areas for different pile lengths
4 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that a minimum of 10
percent of the total piles be test driven at the site The test piles
should be casted 10 feet longer than the anticipated pile lengths These
test piles may later be incorporated into the actual foundation
Splice cans should be provided at the site during the test pile
driving period Splice cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high
grade steel and should be at least five 5 feet long
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be consulted
for test pile locations and estimated pile lengths when the final
foundation plan is available Also the test pile program should provide
the flexibility of addition or relocation of the test piles as needed in
order to suit the variable ground conditions

Site Grading
Minimal site grading involving about two 2 feet of fill is
anticipated We recommend that the required site fill within the building
footprint be placed as soon as possible prior to pile driving and building
construction in order to allow fill settlements to occur first
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation rubbish old footings and slabs
and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
The ground should then be scarified to a minimm depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated
and then replaced with compacted non expansive select materials
All imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximm size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as select borrow crusher waste

The fill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to

maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Site filling should
be performed prior to test pile driving
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade would be used for the
ground floor of the proposed housing
To reduce potential post construction swelling of the underlying
on site soils we recommend that the on site soils below the upper fill
materials be moistened and protected from drying from the start of
construction until the slab concrete placement The thickened edge of the
slab should have a minimum embedment of eighteen 18 inches Existing
top soil layer within the previous vegetable growing area should be
removed for future landscape use
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slab
as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier
should be incorporated below the slab
We also recommend that concrete placement of slab on grade be delayed
toward the end of construction preferably after the building
superstructure is completed to minimize differential ground settlements
due to new site filling

Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the
development A granular bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASIM C 33
No 67 gravel 3 Fine is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill
up to about one foot 1 above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade of finished grade should be placed in
maximum 8 inch compacted horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 compaction per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect
against potential future ground subsidence
Any existing utility lines should be located and protected during
construction

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and pile
driving of the project This is to observe compliance with the
recommendations design concepts or specifications and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using Program
WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP originally
written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway Administration to
solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and a dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides ultimate resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate pile capacity of the pile
The analysis was performed for driving 12 inch square piles with a
DEIMAG D 22 hammer The relationships of ultimate capacity and hammer
blowcounts commonly known as the bearing graph are presented on Plate C 1
together with input information The maximum compressive and tensile
stresses are presented on Plate C 2
