INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed multi family affordable housing development within Lot L 5
of the Village 5 of Kapolei Development area The site and its vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for foundation pavement and site grading design
and may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ended lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and further south
these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef
formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the sedimentary wedge
ranges from zero in the area of the H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This
wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock over the
artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued from
earlier geologic time through the present

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
Laboratory test results indicate that the near surface silty clay soils are moderately
to highly expansive In order to minimize the potentially detrimental swelling and
shrinkage effects of the underlying expansive soils on the building and pavement special
site grading preparation procedure and placement of non expansive borrow fill capping
over the underlying soils are recommended
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble and organic matter should be
removed and properly disposed of off site A minimum of two 2 feet of new select
borrow capping materials should be placed in the building areas The Capping material
should be extended to a minimum of one foot beyond the slab area After the required
over excavation of expansive clayey subgrade soils are removed below the future building
areas and parking subgrades to depths of two feet 2 and one 1 foot below the current
finish subgrade elevations respectively the proposed building and pavement areas should
then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches The scarified subgrade should be
thoroughly wetted to two 2 to three 3 percent above the optimum water content and
re compacted to obtain about 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The subgrade
should then be capped with properly compacted non expansive materials such as
crushed waste cinder or coral materials The fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to about 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2859 00 Page 8
The spread line footing foundations if required are expected to be placed at lower
levels than the floor slabs For the proposed parking lot and driveway areas the non
expansive capping fill may be reduced to a minimum of one foot of non expansive select
fill material The select fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches in loose thickness over the moist on site soil and then compacted to about
90 percent instead of the normal 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The non expansive select fill material recommended should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed residential structures The footing foundation should bear on the lower portion
of the above properly compacted non expansive select fill material An allowable bearing
pressure of 2 500 p s f may be used for the above footing foundation design The
footings should have a minimum of 12 inch embedment below the lowest adjacent outside
finish grade
The recommended bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Footing settlements under design loadings are anticipated to be on the order of
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing
W O 2859 00 Page 9
material has been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to placement of
concrete
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed buildings may be provided by
passive soil pressures and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A frictional coefficient of 0 85 may be used to
estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings and the compacted
select non expansive capping material
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce the potential for footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to firm material and replaced with select non expansive
borrow material with each lift compacted to obtain about 85 to 90 percent of its maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the in
situ subgrade soils due to their swelling characteristics

The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

non expansive selected material during construction to verify the adequacy of the
recommended pavement section The above pavement section may be revised if
necessary depending on the results of the laboratory tests The recommended section
considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the
paved areas
Drainage
The finished grades outside footings slabs and pavements should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the structures and that the gutter run off water be
diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and pavement section should be avoided To
provide relief of subsurface water seepage under the base course layer within the parking
areas weepholes at the subgrade elevations should be provided at the drainage inlet
structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2859 00 Page 12
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Planters close to the foundations should be avoided or they should have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Preliminary and final plans and specifications for site grading utility and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and
written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by the
project geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration The additional site grading required should also be monitored by the project
geotechnical engineer to verify compliance to the above site grading requirements and
recommendations
