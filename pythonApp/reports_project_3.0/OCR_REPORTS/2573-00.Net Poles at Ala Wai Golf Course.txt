INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed stabilization and or replacement of recently erected net poles
at the new driving range of the Ala Wai Golf Course The general location of the project
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was
performed in accordance with our fee proposal to Engineers Surveyors Hawaii dated
August 21 1990
This report summarizes the exploration findings and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is Our understanding that the proposed project consists of stabilization and or
replacement of recently erected net poles at the new driving range of the Ala Wai Golf
Course which have become tilted due to lateral loads Anticipated high lateral loads will
impose an equivalent design overturning moment of 186 foot kips at the base of the
50 foot high net poles at the existing ground surface
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design
of the project

The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal to Engineers
Surveyors Hawaii dated August 21 1990 The scope of work was limited to the
following
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surficial soil rock conditions

to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is in the new driving range of the Ala Wai Golf Course The golf
course is located in the Kaimuki district of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii and is bound by Date
Street to the north Kapahulu Avenue to the east Manoa Palolo Drainage Channel to the
west and Ala Wai Canal to the south The new driving range is located in the southeast
ern portion of the golf course as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths of 25 3 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a crust
approximately 5 feet thick of loose to medium dense brown silty fine sand overlying
lagoonal deposits ranging from very loose to medium dense gravelly coralline sand to soft
Clayey silt to the maximum depths of the borings drilled A moderately hard coral
formation was also encountered at depths of about 18 and 11 feet below the existing
ground surface in Borings 1 and 2 respectively
Groundwater level was encountered at depths of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface in the borings at the time of our field exploration Due to close proximity
of the site to the Ala Wai Canal it is anticipated that groundwater levels will fluctuate in
response to tidal influences and flood conditions in the canal
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration are presented in Appendix A
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained The test results are
presented in Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered poor soil conditions consisting primarily
of loose to medium dense silty sand lagoonal deposits
Both mat and drilled pier foundations have been evaluated for the foundation
supports of the subject net poles Ideally a boring should be drilled at each net pole
location to determine the necessary embedment depth of the pole foundation However
due to budget restraints only two 2 borings were drilled on each side of the driving
range to evaluate the general area subsurface conditions Therefore provisions should
be made to allow some flexibility during the foundation construction should unanticipated
soil conditions be encountered
Details of the recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Mat Foundations
A mat foundation with sufficient dead weight may be used to resist the lateral
loadings on each net pole and to provide vertical loading support to each net pole Our
analysis indicates that a mat foundation with dimensions of 12 by 12 feet square by 4 feet
thick would be adequate in resisting the given design moment of 186 foot kips The
following soil parameters may be used for the mat foundation design
Allowable bearing value 1500 psf
Passive soil pressure 345 pcf
Active soil pressure 38 pcf

Mat foundations below the groundwater table are not recommended due to the
difficulty of construction and requisite reduction in soil strength parameters
Proper compaction of the mat backfill is critical to develop the required lateral
resistance A minimum of 95 relative compaction density should be obtained as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method on the backfill around the mat
If soft or loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the mat excavation
it should be over excavated to dense in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density If groundwater level and loose
soil pockets are encountered we recommend that a minimum 12 inch over excavation
of the loose submerged soils be effected and replaced with No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C
33 No 67 cushion below the mat to develop a firm stabilized bearing surface
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid ponding of
surface run off water that may weaken the soils around the foundations
Soil Anchors
It is our understanding that guy anchors in soil secured by concrete deadman may
be used to resist the anticipated high lateral loads on the net poles and to reduce the size
of mat foundations supporting the net poles
The above recommended soil strength parameters for the mat foundation design
can also be used in the design of concrete deadmen
Drilled Pier Foundations
As an alternative to mat foundations drilled pier foundations may be used to
support the subject net poles to minimize the volume of concrete which is required in
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2573 00 Page 7
large quantity in mat foundations However construction cost with temporary casing and
tremie concrete placement as required for drilled pier installation may offset the savings
in concrete
The method and equipment used for drilled pier construction should be the choice
of the contractor within practical limits and safety consideration The use of temporary
steel casing will be required in the loose submerged soil conditions Extreme care must
be exercised during removal of the casing while placing concrete in order to avoid
contamination or pinching off of the concrete caused by inflow of the surrounding soft
or loose soil into the concrete An alternative is to install the drilled pier with a permanent
steel casing left in the ground
The concrete mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to maintain
high frictional contact between the soil or steel casing and pier concrete The concrete
should also be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the aggregates
Due to anticipated high lateral loadings with design overturning moment of 186
foot kips on the subject net poles it is recommended that 3 foot diameter 20 foot long
drilled piers be used as foundation supports of the net poles The depth of the drilled
piers may be reduced provided that the drilled piers penetrate a minimum of 5 feet into
dense coralline sand and gravel or moderately hard coral
Lateral resistance of the drilled pier was analyzed by using a finite difference
program PILEDG The calculated lateral deflection of the pier head is less than
one quarter of an inch and the maximum induced moment on the pier shaft is about 200
foot kips

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the construction of the foundations This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at th site were explored by drilling four 4 borings to
depths of 25 3 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations the classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 to A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 5 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
