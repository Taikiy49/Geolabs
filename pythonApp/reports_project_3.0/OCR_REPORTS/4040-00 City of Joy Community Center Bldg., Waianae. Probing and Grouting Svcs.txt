Dear Pastor Bayudan
This letter is to summarize the discussions and activities regarding the probing and
grouting services for the project
Cavities are commonly found in coral formations such as that underlying the site
The sizes of the cavities vary but are generally small and do not affect surface
improvements However on rare occasions a large cavity may collapse causing damage
to surface improvements Therefore to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of large cavities probing and grouting below the structure
foundations is often recommended
During the field exploration for the project in August 1998 15 probes were drilled at
column locations to attempt to locate large cavities or soft areas The probes at two of the
footing locations indicated possible cavities or soft spots Therefore additional
probeholes were recommended at these two locations along with cement grout pumped
into the holes to fill cavities that are encountered Since the building footing locations
were revised subsequent to the field exploration we recommended that all of the footing
locations be probed and grouted The proposed cost for the probing and grouting
services was However due to budget constraints the probing and grouting
operations were not carried out
It is our opinion that the risk of building damage due to cavity collapse is small
but the probing and grouting would have reduced this risk even lower In saving the
cost for the probing and grouting the owner accepts this higher risk
