At your request a site visit was conducted on to evaluate the
Main House and the detached Ohana of the Lindsey residence for potential signs of
structural settlement
Previous reports by Island Geotechnical Engineers dated August 7 2009 and
Weidig Geoanalysts dated discuss problems associated with poor site
grading and drainage bubbling of the flooring material cracking of the walls and
substandard compaction under the slab on grade The purpose of our site assessment
was to further evaluate the Main House and the Ohana floors for levelness If particular
locations are found to be suspect as signs of settlement then a test pit program would be
conducted to evaluate the soil conditions under the footing at the suspect locations
Initially survey equipment was set up within each room to establish zero point
locations where no signs of settlement were observed and where the room could be
covered by the line of sight of the survey equipment Then the survey was conducted in
each room to determine whether the floor showed signs of movement in reference to the
zero points Plates 1 2 illustrate the results of the floor level survey of the
Lindsey Residence Main House and in the Ohana

TEST PIT PROGRAM
The test pit program was carried out with the use of a Komatsu PC15 mini
excavator to excavate along side of the foundation where the floor level survey indicated
lower points of the floor Once the soil below the bottom of footing was exposed soil
samples were collected with 3 inch x 3 inch ring samplers along with bulk bags to perform
laboratory analyses for unit weight density moisture and Atterberg limits for soil
classification Once the observations and sampling were completed the test pits were
backfilled in approximately 3 to 4 inch layers and compacted with the use of the excavator
bucket Once the tamping and tracking of the backfill was completed the surface was
sloped away from the structure

SUMMARY OF FINDINGS
The report by Weidig discussed visible separations of the crown molding in the
main house A slight gap was observed during our site assessment between the edge of
the crown molding and the ceiling of the living room However it was also observed that
no caulking was placed along the seams between the crown molding and the ceiling
Typically caulking is placed along these edges to seal these types of gaps from
imperfections in the drywall molding prior to painting In addition no cracks at these
locations were observed Therefore the cause would be deemed as poor finish
workmanship rather than signs of settlement
Weidig s report also mentions that bubbling of the linoleum floor was observed
During this site assessment no signs of this effect were found The investigation by IGE
was conducted at these locations therefore perhaps they were removed when the
concrete slab was cut out for the subgrade investigation Although additional observations
that were made of the underside of the linoleum flooring found that very little adhesive was
used and the surface of the slab appeared dusty Therefore the bubbling could also be a
result of poor workmanship during the flooring application
Observations of the lot grading found that the lot consisting of the Main House and
Ohana are situated in a lower elevation than the surrounding homes and roadway
Therefore surface runoff from heavy rainfall could flow towards the Lindsey main
residence and Ohana The Lindsey residence lot contains a gentle slope towards the
house structure In addition the grading on the southern side of the main residence is
sloping towards the house from the adjacent property The main house and Ohana both
were observed to have small trenches eroded in the surface soil around the perimeter of
the roofline This is typical of rain runoff from the roof to the ground surface Neither the
Ohana nor the Main House had a gutter system to divert the water runoff from the roof
The results of the floor level survey have indicated that the majority of the
Main House and Ohana are relatively level and well within the industry standards
However three locations of the main house Office Master Bedroom and Bedroom 3
were found to have a difference of more than 1 4 inch from the reference zero point
Therefore further investigation was conducted to determine the in situ soil conditions
under the footing at these locations

Master Bedroom
The floor level survey indicated a 1 16 to 3 8 inch drop from the reference zero point
suggesting a slope in the floor Therefore a test pit was excavated to further
investigate the soil conditions at the bottom of footing grade
The laboratory test results concluded that the soil under the footing at the
southeastern corner of the master bedroom consisted of a reddish brown clayey silt
material which is classified as MH in the Unified Soil Classification System and
was found to have a compacted density of 90 0 In addition the moisture content
was determined to vary from 2 4 near surface to 5 4 at bottom of footing
above the optimum state This suggests that adequate compaction under the
footing was achieved
Since the interior wall and the exterior CMU blocks did not show any signs of
structural cracking it is most likely that the concrete slab was poured with a slope
towards the eastern exterior wall Therefore this location does not seem to have
settled
Office
The floor level survey indicated that the majority of the room is relatively level
However a 3 4 inch drop from the reference point was found at the northeastern

the base board at the corner Since there were no cracks found in the walls of this
area and the baseboards were level this suggests that again the concrete slab
was placed with a slope towards the eastern exterior wall of the room Therefore
this location does not seem to have settled

RECOMMENDATIONS
Main House and Ohana
Based on these findings the large majority of the Main House and the Ohana do
not show significant signs of settlement under the footings The minor variances of the
floor level survey are well within the accuracy of the survey equipment and industry
standards of variances The findings along the north wall of Bedroom 3 of the
Main House appear to be inconclusive without further observations The compaction under
the footing at Test Pit No 1 was inconclusive However we understand that the house is
over 1 year old and the majority of the settlements should have occurred by this time
Therefore we recommend creating a punch list of the various items observed by
the owner for the general contractor to refinish repair Furthermore a period of time should
be allowed to monitor these locations as well as the north wall of Bedroom 3 to
determine whether additional movement cracking occurs At which point further
investigation can be made to determine whether more extensive remedial work is
necessary
