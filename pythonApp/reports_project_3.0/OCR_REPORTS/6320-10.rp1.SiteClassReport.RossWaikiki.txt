PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work is to obtain seismic shear wave velocity estimates of the
subsurface materials using the Refraction Microtremor Arrays SeisOpt ReMi method
The seismic shear wave velocity estimates would extend to a depth of about 100 feet
below the ground level using a combination of commonly available seismic refraction
equipment recording with no source a wavefield transformation data processing
technique spectral analysis of surface wave SASW and multi channel analysis of
surface wave MASW techniques To accomplish this the following tasks and work
efforts were conducted

GEOPHYSICAL SURVEY
A surface geophysical survey utilizing refraction micro tremor ReMi methods was
performed for the project In general an array of 24 geophones was placed at the ground
surface at about 8 feet spacing The array runs from the northern corner of the project site
to the southern corner The approximate location of this array is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The ReMi method determines variations in surface wave velocities with
increasing distances and wavelengths The data from these measurements were used
to estimate the shear wave velocities of the subsurface This information can then be
used to infer rock soil types stratigraphy and soil conditions
The ReMi survey requires a seismic source to generate surface waves and at
least 24 geophones to measure the ground response at increasing distances from the
source Surface waves is a special type of seismic wave whose propagation is confined
to the near surface medium The depth of subsurface penetration of a surface wave is
directly proportional to its wavelength In a non homogeneous medium surface waves
are dispersive i e each wavelength has a characteristic velocity stemming from
subsurface heterogeneities The relationship between surface wave velocity and
wavelength is used to calculate the shear wave velocity of the medium with increasing
depth
The seismic source can be either active or passive depending on the application
and location of the survey Examples of active sources include explosives weight
drops and vibrating pads Examples of passive sources are drill rigs road traffic micro
tremors and water wave action in near shore environments The geophone measures

the arrival time of the various components of the surface wave train traveling from the
seismic source
The surface wave velocity with respect to frequency known as the dispersion
curve is determined by measuring the delay time in wave propagation between the
geophones The dispersion curve is then matched to a theoretical dispersion curve using
an iterative forward modeling procedure The result is a profile of shear wave velocity
versus depth This shear wave profile can be used with other parameters such as density
to estimate the dynamic shear modulus of the medium as a function of depth
A Geometrics Geode 24 channel digital seismograph with acquisition software
coupled with 24 Mark Products 4 5 Hertz vertical geophones placed at 8 feet spacings
were employed for the performance of seismic refraction survey In addition to ambient
road noise a 20 pound sledge hammer impacting a metal plate was used to generate
seismic energy source for the survey
Experienced personnel capable of interpreting the raw data using the SeisOpt
ReMi Version 2 0 software processed the data collected for this project A colorized
dispersion modeling image was generated along with a dispersion curve depicting the
variation in material across the profile area Both images are presented on the attached
Refraction Microtremor Survey Plate 4
Our interpretation of the geophysical survey results is that the project site is
underlain by a medium dense layer up to 5 feet below grade Layer No 1 overlying a
layer of loose to medium dense layer up to about 40 feet below grade Layer No 2
followed by a dense layer Layer No 3 at greater depth
SEISMIC SITE CLASS DETERMINATION
Based on the geophysical survey results the average shear wave velocity value of
the top 100 feet of subsurface materials Vs 100 is approximately 1 847 feet per
second feet sec Therefore the project site may be classified as a Very Dense Soil and
Soft Rock profile for seismic design considerations and may be designed based on Site
Class C in accordance with Table No 1 International Building Code
2003 Edition
It should be noted that there is no strong correlation between the Standard
Penetration Test SPT blow counts and shear wave velocity estimates especially in
coralline deposits Therefore it is quite common that shear wave velocity estimates
derived from site specific testing provide higher values and correspondingly better
seismic site classifications than conventional SPT blow counts
