The 6 inches of compacted base course should extend a minimum of 12 inches
laterally beyond the limits of the tank structures The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In addition the bottom of the foundation excavations below the base course layer
should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm
and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of the base course material If soft and or
loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the base course elevation they should be
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with on site granular soils up to the bottom of the base course elevation and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used for foundations bearing on the compacted base course
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils
around the footings are well compacted The area adjacent to the tank slab should be
backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively impervious materials and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slab and
foundation Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general the foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the

Pavement Design
We understand that a new parking lot with asphalt concrete paving is also planned
as part of the improvement project In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for the
parking lot for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks
Based on our site reconnaissance and review of the available soil boring information we
anticipate that the pavement subgrades would generally consist of the dense silty sands with
relatively good pavement support characteristics Based on the above assumptions we
recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary design
purposes
Flexible Pavements at Parking Lot
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests
should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm
the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches
into the subgrade soil below the base course layer to reduce the potential for migration of

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the water tank foundations and pavement
subgrade preparation This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The
recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
