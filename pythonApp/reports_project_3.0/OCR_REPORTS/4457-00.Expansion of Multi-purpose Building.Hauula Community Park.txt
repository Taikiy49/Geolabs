INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Expansion of Multi Purpose Building project at the Hauula Community Park
located in Hauula on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings based on the field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the
proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of building
foundations slabs on grade site grading pavements and individual wastewater systems only
and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located within the Hauula Community Park in the Hauula
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that it is proposed to construct a new
one story building within the Hauula Community Park site The new building will be located
to the north of the existing playcourts Based on the information provided the finished floor
elevation will be set at approximately 7 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL We anticipate that
the maximum column and wall loads are approximately 7 kips and 0 3 kips per lineal foot
respectively The new building will be constructed of concrete masonry units CMU with
concrete slabs on grade We understand that it is also planned to install a leach field as part
of the project In addition the existing parking area will be extended at the southern end to
provide for additional parking
Based on the Site Plan the existing ground surface elevations at the site are about
6 to 7 feet MSL We anticipate that minimal site grading work on the order of about 1 foot
or less may be required to achieve the design finished grades

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the western
third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern two thirds
of the island After the main shield building phase of Koolau the volcanic dome became
deeply eroded On the windward side of the island combined wind rain and wave erosion
cut the coastline back while forming steep cliffs
The project site is located on the windward northeastern flank of Koolau and its
subsurface conditions are directly related to this deep erosion and littoral processes at the
coast The subsurface conditions within the project area are dominated by beach deposits
which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments The base of the stratigraphic section in this
area is composed of Koolau Basalt

below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits generally consisted of very loose

Building Foundations
Based on the relatively light loads of the new one story building we recommend that
a shallow foundation system consisting of inter connected grids of continuous footings be used
to support the new one story building Isolated spread footings should not be used unless they
are structurally connected together by the use of grade beams Based on the borings drilled
soft to medium stiff soil pockets were encountered within the surface clayey fill materials To
provide a firm and unyielding footing subgrade we recommend that the footing subgrades be
over excavated by a minimum of 24 inches In addition the over excavation below the
footings should extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond the sides of the footing The
over excavation should be backfilled with free draining granular materials such as No 2 rock
or No 3B Fine gravel The free draining granular materials should be wrapped in a
non woven filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent The top of the free draining
granular materials should be densified with a vibratory plate tamper or similar type equipment

An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used
for the design of footings bearing on the 2 foot thick free draining granular materials This
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general we recommend that the foundations be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Due to the presence of a shallow groundwater
table the footing embedment depths should be kept to a minimum to facilitate the over
excavation of the in place soils and the placement of the 2 foot thick free draining granular
materials below the new footings Where foundations are located next to utility trenches
and or easements they should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or embedded as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings bearing on the 2 foot thick
free draining granular materials to be on the order of about 1 inch We estimate that
differential settlements between adjacent footings supported on the 2 foot thick
free draining granular materials to be on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 40
may be used for footings bearing on the 2 foot thick free draining granuiar fill materials
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure
of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf above the groundwater level This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted or are relatively undisturbed
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive pressure resistance in the upper

Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be provided for the new building The
near surface soils encountered at the project site consist of clayey soils Based on our
laboratory tests the clayey soils encountered in our borings exhibit a low to moderate potential
for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the
clayey soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at
least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab
cushion
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the underlying subgrade soils
should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks
are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade
due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted fill
In addition we recommend that a cushion fill layer consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be provided below the slab for more uniform support
and to serve as a capillary break for moisture vapors A moisture vapor membrane should be
incorporated above the capillary break to reduce the potential for damage to moisture sensitive
floor coverings by moisture infiltration It is advisable to place a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand
GEOLABS INC
W O 4457 00 Page 9
above the vapor membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in curing of the concrete
Provisions for the 2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the vapor barrier should be left up to
the discretion of the project structural engineer
We understand that exterior concrete walkways may be constructed for the proposed
project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of non expansive
select granular fill or aggregate subbase The select granular fill or aggregate subbase should
be compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slabs
should be backfilled tightly against the edges of the slabs with the on site soil and graded to
divert water away from the slabs to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slab
edges
Site Preparation
Based on the existing site topography we anticipate that minimal site grading work
would be required to achieve the design finished grades In general areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork
Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should
be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted fill
Concrete slabs and exterior walkways that are to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled
with compacted fills Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
GEOLABS INC

of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed
structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
Due to the high moisture contents of the existing on site soils scarification of the
subgrade soils at the proposed building location is not recommended Areas to receive fills
and finished subgrades at the building location should be proof rolled and compacted to obtain
a firm and unyielding surface It should be noted that use of the vibratory action of a
compactor on the high moisture soils should be carefully controlled to avoid inducing a
pumping subgrade condition If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation
the pumping subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for
pumping subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
25 square feet of subgrade area Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a woven
geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior
to placement of subsequent lifts of fill
Loose and or soft materials encountered at the subgrade level of the slabs should be
over excavated to expose firm material and backfilled with well compacted fill materials
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill
quantities The subgrade preparation should be observed by a representative from Geolabs
to evaluate the presence of soft and or yielding areas at the subgrade level
The excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or backfill
materials provided that they are screened of deleterious materials vegetation In addition
the materials should be processed and reduced to a relatively well graded select granular
material with a maximum particle size of about 3 inches The 6 inch thick non expansive
select granular fill layer required under the building slabs and imported fill materials if
required should consist of select granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or
cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater
than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more

Underground Utility Lines
We anticipate that new utility line trenches planned for the proposed project will be
excavated in the upper fill materials In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches
of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at or near the invert of the pipes an additional
layer of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a non woven filter fabric Mirafi 18O0N or equivalent
should be provided below the bedding layer for more uniform support Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for
the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes or above the groundwater
GEOLABS INC
W O 4457 00 Page 12
table It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and placement could result in settlement
of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes or
groundwater table to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the
excavated on site soils with a maximum particle size of 6 inches The backfill should be

compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are

should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building structure should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsurface
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration In
addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained
to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Field Percolation Tests
Two field percolation tests Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 were performed at the
proposed leach field location to evaluate the subsurface permeability characteristics of the
subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent Field percolation tests were performed
in general accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards The
percolation tests were performed by drilling 4 inch diameter boreholes to a depth of about
2 75 feet below the existing ground surface The boreholes were terminated at these shallow
depths in order to provide approximately 12 inches of percolation distance between the bottom
of the borehole and the natural groundwater table As previously mentioned groundwater was
encountered at depths ranging from about 3 7 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface
The locations of the borings drilled and percolation tests performed are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rates at Boring Nos P 1 and
P 2 are approximately 15 minutes per inch and 60 minutes per inch respectively The results
of the individual percolation tests are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2 in Appendix C of this
report Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal
system should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
Based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department of Health s
Administrative Rules Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems the bottom of the
absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater
level Due to the shallow groundwater levels measured in the drilled borings it is our
opinion that the feasibility of using a septic tank and leach field for disposal of wastewater
effluent should be carefully evaluated for the site

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths ranging from
approximately 16 5 to 27 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 of Appendix A
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer
tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
Two field percolation tests were performed at the proposed leach field location in
general accordance with the procedures described in the State of Hawaii Department of
the Ten States Standards To evaluate the permeability of the subsurface materials for
disposal of wastewater effluent the percolation tests were performed by drilling 4 0 inch
diameter boreholes extending to a depth of about 2 75 feet below the existing ground surface
The percolation tests were performed in the upper clayey soil materials The approximate
locations of the two tests Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Approximately 1 to 2 inches of gravel was placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring the sediments The materials in the boreholes
were soaked for a minimum of 4 hours prior to commencing with the percolation test During
the test the holes were carefully filled with water to a minimum height of 6 inches above the
gravel pack The time intervals and water level drops were then recorded to provide data upon
which calculation of the percolation rates were based The results of the individual percolation
tests are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
