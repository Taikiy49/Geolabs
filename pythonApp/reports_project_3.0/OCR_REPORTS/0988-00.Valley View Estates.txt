SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the northern corner
of the intersection of Kahekili Highway and Hui Iwa Street
in Heeia Oahu Hawaii
The entire site consists of an area of roughly 5 acres and
is bordered on the southwest by a concrete lined drainage
channel running along Kahekili Highway

At the northwest boundary the site is bordered by open
pasture land of lower ground elevations In the north
east portion of the site is bordered by conservation lands
generally in the higher elevations The southeast portion
of the site is bordered by existing townhouses and Hui Iwa
Street
Ground elevations throughout the site range from 80 feet in
the southwest to 220 feet in the north Ground surface slopes
vary throughout the site from the relatively level central
area to 2 1 H V slope in the higher elevations in the
northeast Cut slopes above the existing concrete drainage
channel are at a steep 1 1 H V ratio
The general site topography is basically hilly The central
portion of the site which is relatively level is at a lower
elevation than the surrounding areas It appears to be part
of an old drainageway Probably this was a natural drain
ageway once more evident before the development of the con
crete drainage channel
There were no structures noted on the site at the time of
our field exploration Several wooden electric poles run
through the site in a northwest southeast direction A
HECO electric box is located in the southeast corner near
Hui Iwa Street

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated in an area that is geolo
gically mapped to be between an older alluvium and a
younger alluvial deposit The older alluvium generally
occur in the hilly higher elevations of the site while
the younger alluvium generally is deposited in the lower
central portion of the site especially adjacent to the
existing drainage channel
Older alluvium in these areas are known to consist of a
mottled brown to reddish brown colored silty soil deve
loped from the deep weathering of Koolau caldera basalts
that formed these areas This older alluvium is usually
expressed as a residual soil showing the original parent
rock structure in exposed cuts
Younger alluvium in these areas are generally found in low
lying areas like drainageways depressions between knolls
and along valley floors These younger alluvial deposits
are comprised of reworked older alluvium deposited as sands
silts and clays In lowlying wet and swampy areas these
deposits are commonly accompanied by soft organic materials
of varying percentages

Subsurface Conditions
Encountered at the hilly areas of the site were basically
residual clayey soils with some signs of surface erosion
and in some areas a relatively thin surficial layer of
recent transported soils
In the lower central area a relatively thick layer of
soil eroded from the higher slopes and possibly some
fill overlies a soft partly organic clayey deposit
probably associated with an older drainageway These
findings generally conform to the geologically mapped
conditions
Boring Nos 1 and 2 drilled adjacent to the proposed high
cut area revealed a mottled reddish brown to gray clayey
Silty soil extending to the bottom of both borings
For more detailed description of the soil conditions refer
to the appendix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent foun
dation bearing soils at the proposed project site From
a soil engineering point of view the site is suitable
for the proposed residential development
Shallow foundation can be used for the proposed structures
except where they are situated on steeply sloping areas
Where the structures are located near the top of the exis
ting 1 1 H V steep slope cut above the drainage channel
deep pier type foundations are recommended see Plate 2
The near surface soils are moderately expansive and will
require special procedures for slab on grade construction
Foundations
Spread Footing
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for the structures on level or gently sloping areas
An allowable bearing value up to 4000 p s f is
recommended for footings bearing on stiff residual
soil and 2500 p s f for footings bearing on compacted
fill The above values are for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary
loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
On level areas the bottom of the above designed
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the outside finished grade to resist erosion
and to reduce the effects of moisture intrusion into
bearing area
Drilled Pier
Footings located on or near the tops of sloping bank
adjacent to the existing drainage channel will be
subject to severe erosion and slope creep of the
surface soils therefore these footings should be
embedded an additional amount to help resist down
hill movement and to develop the required bearing
capacity of the soils
The requirements of drilled piers will depend on the
final grading and structural foundation layout for
each building structure
In general the piers should be drilled down to obtain
sufficient depths below a 14 1 H V slope line esta
blished from the top of the drainage channel up toward
the upper roadway See sketch shown on Plate 2 We
recommend that the soil engineer should review the final
foundation layout plan for the future units which will
be located along this drainage channel and slope bank
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
Assuming an 18 inch diameter drilled pier an allow
able end bearing capacity of 7 kips and a perimeter
shearing resistance of 3 kips per foot of embedment
can be used The top portion of the pier embedment
above the imaginary 14 1 H V slope line should be
neglected in determining the perimeter shearing
resistance
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead load times a
coefficient of friction equal to 0 45
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Anticipated settlements for foundations bearing on natural
ground or properly compacted fill are estimated to be on
the order of 1 2 inch total and 1 4 inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Site Grading and Earthwork
Site grading in the upper area includes Slope cuts up to
elevation 155 feet Roadway embankment fill up to 16 feet
high is anticipated for the lower project site grading
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 988 00 P 10
Based on the boring data and visual observation of the
existing exposed cut slopes relatively easy site exca
vation can be anticipated except in the deep cut areas
where decomposed rock formation may be encountered
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris
then scarified and recompacted to 85 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall
area the on site subsoils have in place moisture
contents about 10 to 30 percent above its optimum
moisture content
Therefore some amount of drying of the excavated
material will be required before using them as
fill material in the embankment
Because of the high in situ moisture contents of the
on site borrow material and the site being located
in a high rainfall location it is our opinion that
the normal 90 compaction standard used in other drier
areas should be modified to 85 for this project
Laboratory shear strength tests on compacted samples
indicate that 85 compaction will be adequate for the
roadway embankment fill and light building loads are
anticipated

W P
The fills should be placed in about 10 inch maximum
compacted lifts Sheepsfoot type of compactor is
highly recommended for this type of residual soil
Based on the results of our in place densities and
maximum density determination it appears that a
shrinkage factor of 5 to 7 percent should be con
sidered for the on site cut and fill quantities
The in place dry densities averaged about 66 p c f
compared to 68 p c f at 85 of its maximum dry
density
If additional off site borrow is required the
material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to transportation to the site
The material should generally be non expansive and
should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or deleterious material
Fill Placement
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed
and benched into the existing firm ground to
properly bond the new fill to the slope For
details see Plate 3 This detail is essential
for the stability of the roadway fill particularly
in the drainageway areas

The filling should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level compacted layers
Presently the grading plan indicates no fill will be
placed in the lowlying level area in the vicinity of
Probing Nos 2 and 9 Should future fill and building
structures be located in this area additional soil
investigation should be taken to delineate the extent
of the underlying soft gray clay and to furnish addi
tional information for anticipated magnitudes and dura
tion and for ground settlement of the buildings
Subdrains
Subdrains consist of perforated pipe and gravel should
be provided in fill areas where possible water seepage
will be encountered The approximate locations of the
subdrain and section details were plotted on the Site
Plan Plate l
The on site soils are generally silty and erosive as
evidenced by the steep existing cut bank slope 1 1
over the existing concrete lined channel and the exposed
natural slopes on the project site

Slab On Grade
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have low swelling potential Slab on grade should have
perimeter footings extending down a minimum of 8 inches
below the finish grade to reduce ground movement due to
drying and wetting around the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
When a slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the wall is
recommended to relieve seepage water

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content Atterberg Limit and unit weight
determinations were performed on selected samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Probing and
Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 13
One CBR Swell test was performed on the subsurface
soil to evaluate the suitability of the soils as pavement
support The results are presented on Plates B 1 B 2
One compaction test was made per ASTM D 1557 70 test
method modified from wet to dry to evaluate the maximum
density and optimum moisture content of the site soil
The results are presented on Plate B 1
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on
selected samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
