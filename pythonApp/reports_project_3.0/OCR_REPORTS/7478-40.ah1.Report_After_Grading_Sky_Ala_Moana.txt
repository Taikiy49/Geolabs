performed during the site grading and infrastructure installation work for the Sky Ala

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at 1388 Kapiolani Boulevard (formerly 1400 Kapiolani 
Boulevard) between Kapiolani Boulevard and Makaloa Street in Honolulu on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves the development of two high-rise towers of up to 
43 stories in height atop a podium parking structure. The podium structure includes 
commercial spaces on the ground level and six stories of parking levels for about 916 
parking stalls. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1. 
The high-rise and podium parking structures are supported on a deep foundation 
system consisting of a combination of 18- and 24-inch diameter Augered Cast-In-Place

(ACIP) piles with allowable compressive load capacities of 500 and 1,200 kips per pile, 
respectively. The layout of the new high-rise development project is shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of less than 
about 3 feet thick. The project also included the installation of new utility connections, 
backfilling around pile caps, and pavement construction, among others. The scope of 
work discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing during 
the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the project only. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Albert C. Kobayashi, Inc. was the General Contractor for the project. 
Koga Engineering & Construction, Inc. performed the site grading construction, utility 
line installation, and pavement installation. The site grading work consisted of subgrade 
preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, utility trench excavation 
and backfill, and pavement construction. 
Following the demolition, clearing, and foundation installation operations, fill and 
backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, 
brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 or 
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas, respectively, of the maximum 
dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
New utility lines, such as water, sewer, and drain lines, and utility line 
connections adjacent to the project site were installed as part of the development. To 
reduce the potential for appreciable settlement of the pipelines, the bottom of the utility 
lines and below-grade structures were over-excavated for installation of a stabilization 
layer below the bedding layer, consisting of about 24 inches of No. 2 Rock (ASTM C33, 
No. 4 gradation) or No. 3B Fine gravel (ASTM C33, No. 67 gradation) wrapped in a non-
woven filter fabric (Mirafi 180N), where the soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits were 
encountered. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized 
on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations with variations in construction 
procedures and soil conditions were noted. A total of 729 field density tests were 
performed using the nuclear gauge method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.40 of this 
report. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from 
on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
