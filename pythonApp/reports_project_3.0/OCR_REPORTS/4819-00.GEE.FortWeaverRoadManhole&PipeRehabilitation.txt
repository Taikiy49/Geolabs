SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Fort Weaver Road Manhole and Pipe Rehabilitation project located in
the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the sewer
manhole and pipe rehabilitation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located along Fort Weaver Road between Renton Road and
Geiger Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is shown on
the General Site Plan Plate 2
The project includes the rehabilitation of approximately 3 375 linear feet of 18 to
30 inch diameter reinforced concrete pipe and associated corroded manholes Manhole
inspections performed between 1995 and 1997 disclosed that nine segments of the line
reveal severe corrosion We understand that deterioration is limited to the inside lining of
pipe and minimal cracks appear in the sewer line The objective of this project is to
correct hydraulic and physical deficiencies in portions of the sewer line Alternative
methods of pipe rehabilitation repair or replacement will be presented in this report
1 3 Purpose and Scope

sewer alignment for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the alternative designs for the rehabilitation repair and or replacement of the

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is comprised largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials The project site is underlain by both
calcareous reef deposits and softer deltaic material Land development and reclamation
projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present form
2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located along the western side of Fort Weaver Road in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The existing Fort Weaver Road is an
asphalt concrete paved four lane thoroughfare two lanes in each direction that begins at
H 1 Freeway to the north and terminates at Ewa Beach Park to the south The project site

2 3 Subsurface Conditions
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
12 borings to depths of approximately 25 3 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
Our borings indicate that the project site is generally underlain by dense clayey
gravel base course overlying a stiff to very stiff silty clay layer The silty clay layer
extended to approximately 3 to 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface on the Geiger
Road side of the project site and approximately 8 to 15 feet below the existing ground
surface on the Renton Road side This layer is generally underlain by coral formation
and or coral limestone to the maximum depth explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the time of our exploration
However wet soil conditions were encountered in Boring No 7 at a depth of about 25 feet
below the existing ground surface and in Boring No 8 at a depth of about 10 feet below
the existing ground surface The wet soil conditions are believed to be due to seepage
water and may represent perched groundwater

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Laboratory soil analyses were performed on the subsurface materials along the
sewer line along the two portions of Fort Weaver Road Selected soil samples retrieved
from our field exploration program were tested by Oceanic Analytical Laboratory Inc to
determine the resistivity pH value and the chloride and sulfate contents The resistivity
was determined in general accordance with test method The pH value was
determined in general accordance with EPA SW9045B test method The chloride and
sulfate contents were determined in general accordance with and
test methods respectively Results of the soil analyses resistivity pH chlorides and
sulfates conducted on selected soil samples are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
Based on the resistivity values pH values and sulfate and chloride content values
of the subsurface materials at the site the subsurface materials at the site may be
classified as moderately corrosive for buried concrete structures
Installation of the new pipe using trenchless methods and conventional open trench
method were evaluated for the project Based on our evaluation we believe that both
open trench and trenchless rehabilitation are viable options Detailed discussions of these
items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following
sections of this report
3 1 Open Trench Method
Conventional open trench method is a viable option and may be used for the
installation of the replacement pipe on a new alignment We envision that the invert
depths of the new sewer line will range from about 6 to 16 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in
our borings
Excavation Method
It is anticipated that conventional excavation techniques may be used for the
proposed excavations However as previously mentioned very dense coral

Backfill
In general trench backfill should be performed in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction Department of Public Works City
and County of Honolulu September 1986 Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing
the possibility of damaging the pipes Compaction by water ponding or jetting
should not be allowed for this project Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils with
particles less than 6 inches in maximum dimension or select fill material It
should be noted that cobbles and boulders were encountered in our borings at
shallow depths Boulders encountered in the trench excavations should not be
used for trench backfill
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the

Methods
Two categories of trenchless methods are available rehabilitation and conventional
trenchless Trenchless rehabilitation consists of reusing the existing pipe or pipe
alignment and includes pipe bursting slip lining and Cured In Place Pipe CIPP methods
Conventional trenchless methods consist of installing a replacement pipe generally parallel
to the existing pipe and includes microtunneling and horizontal directional drilling
Slip Lining
Slip lining consists of inserting a new pipe of smaller diameter into the deteriorated
host pipe The new pipe is inserted either by pushing or pulling the pipe into the
deteriorated pipe The annular space between the existing pipe and the new pipe
is filled with grout Since the new pipe diameter is smaller than the existing pipe
diameter the hydraulic capacity of the new pipe may be reduced In some
instances the new pipe may have an increase in hydraulic capacity due to the
smoothness of the new pipe
This method may be used where the host pipe does not have excessive joint
settlements severe misalignment and large deformations In addition the
hydraulic capacity of the new pipe should be evaluated to determine whether
there is an increase or decrease with respect to the hydraulic capacity of the old
pipe
CIPP consists of inserting a liquid thermoset resin saturated material into the host
pipe The liquid thermoset resin saturated material is inserted into the host pipe by
hydrostatic or air inversion or by mechanically pulling with a winch and cable The
material is then heat cured in place Since the diameter of the cured in place pipe

will be smaller than the existing pipe the hydraulic capacity of the cured in place
pipe may be reduced In some instances the new pipe may have an increase in
hydraulic capacity due to the smoothness of the cured in place pipe CIPP may be
a viable option for this project
Pipe Bursting
Pipe bursting consists of winching a pneumatic powered bursting tool through an
old section of pipe breaking and cracking the old pipe as it progresses A new
pipe section is pulled into place behind the bursting tool replacing the old pipe
section High Density Polyethylene HDPE pipe is typically used with pipe
bursting During pipe bursting the soil surrounding the bursting tool will be
compressed In a backfilled trench compaction of the backfill material will generally
occur
Damage to existing utilities in close proximity of the bursting tool may occur for
existing utilities generally within 3 feet of the bursting tool Based on the existing
utility plan the closest utility line is approximately 10 feet away from the sewer line
However if any utility lines appear within 3 feet of the bursting tool during
excavation the utility crossings may be exposed and the crossing utility moved
further away from the bursting tool during the pipe installation to reduce the
potential for damage
For conditions where the bursting tool is close to the existing ground surface
surface heave may occur Enough ground cover should be provided to reduce the
potential for ground heave
Excavation pits will be required to connect the laterals with the main sewer line
Electrofusion methods may be used to connect the HDPE pipes at the joints
Since the sewer line will probably need to be kept active a bypassing system will
probably be required

In summary we believe that pipe bursting is a viable option for the sewer line
rehabilitation provided that the concerns above are addressed
Horizontal Directional Drilling HDD
This trenchless steerable installation method uses a multi axis drilling machine to
bore a small pilot hole The pilot hole is then reamed to a larger size to
accommodate the new pipe The new pipe is pulled back into the reamed hole by
the drilling machine
It is generally more difficult to maintain an accurate alignment with horizontal
directional drilling as compared to microtunneling or pipe bursting Since the sewer
line is a gravity type sewer line accurate alignment and grade are critical
Another consideration in the above method is that an entry pit of significant size will
be necessary to accommodate the drilling machine required for the size of pipe
being considered or large excavation pits will be required to tie in the installed
sewer line to the manhole structure
Based on the above we believe that installation of the new sewer line using
horizontal directional drilling may not be a viable option
Microtunneling
Microtunneling is an installation method where a pipe is jacked behind a remotely
controlled steerable laser guided tunnel boring machine Entry and exit pits will be
required These shallow pits are generally rectangular in shape to accommodate
the length about three to four tunnel diameters and width about one tunnel
diameter of the tunnel boring machine the jacking frame reaction block and pipe
sections to be jacked into place During the microtunneling process the excavated
material is removed by using a mixture of water and slurry pumped through a pipe
for disposal balanced screw auger or a screw conveyor system

In microtunneling selecting the right machine cutter head for the ground condition
is critical Different types of cutter heads are used for various types of soil rock
Based on our field exploration stiff sandy silts and clays and hard coral were
encountered within the anticipated sewer line inverts Mixed face conditions may
be encountered This may increase the jacking forces and cause misalignment of
the tunneling machine
We believe that it would be very costly to install the sewer line by microtunneling
Therefore microtunneling may not be a viable option for this project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling 12 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 12 extending to depths
ranging from about 25 3 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of these borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 2 and 2 3
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig and track drill equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 12
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
