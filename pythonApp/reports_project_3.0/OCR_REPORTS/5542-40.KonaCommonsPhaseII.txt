Discussion and Recommendations
The project site was mass graded to a finish pad elevation of about
39 25 feet MSL Mass grading at the Phase II development site generally consisted of
placing up to about 7 feet of new fills and excavating up to about 6 feet deep to achieve
the design grades Details of the mass grading activities including site preparation fill
materials and fill compaction are contained in the above referenced Building Pad
Construction Report
Additional site grading conducted after the mass grading phase generally
consisted of minor cuts and fills of less than 1 to 2 feet in thickness The additional cuts
and fills were generally required for the construction of parking areas sidewalks utility
lines and other appurtenances
In general fill materials consisting of structural fill were placed in the fill areas of the
project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally smaller
than 6 inches in dimension Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a
vibratory drum roller for about eight passes
Base course materials were used as fill and backfill materials within utility trench
excavations roadways parking lot and other areas Samples of the on site crushed
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing where appropriate The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials generally were moisture conditioned to above
optinum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge Method where
appropriate Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 12 herein
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
