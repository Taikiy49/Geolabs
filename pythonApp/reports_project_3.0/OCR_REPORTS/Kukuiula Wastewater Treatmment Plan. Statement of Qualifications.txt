1. FIRM QUALIFICATIONS
Geolabs, Inc. was established in 1975 and is a Hawaii-based full service
geotechnical engineering and exploration firm with a branch office in Oakland (California).
In June 2001, we opened our Maui office. Since 1975, we have been serving clients,
such as the City and County of Honolulu, State of Hawaii, U.S. Navy, U.S. Army Corps of
Engineers, and various other counties and municipalities. We also provide services to
architects, engineers, developers, contractors, and other governmental agencies. Our
extensive project experience spans not only throughout the State of Hawaii but includes
Micronesia, Guam, Saipan, Republic of Palau, the Philippines, American Samoa,
Indonesia, China, Japan, and Taiwan, among others. The average number of employees
during the past 5 years is 44.
Since 1982, we have provided environmental drilling, monitoring well installation,
and geophysical services to environmental consultants. We continuously strive to
upgrade our skills and equipment in order to stay abreast of current requirements for
quality geotechnical and environmental services. As a result, over the years, we have
emerged as one of the leading provider of these and related services throughout the
Pacific Basin and, recently, in Asia.
Our Maui office is located at 60 E. Wakea Street, Unit 111 in Kahului (808)877-1899.

4. EXPLORATION CAPABILITIES
Geotechnical
Geolabs, Inc. has in-house drilling capabilities, which allow us to provide cost
savings and scheduling flexibility without the time delay which frequently results from
subcontracting the drilling services to outside contractors.
Our inventory of drilling capability includes twenty drill rigs. These equipment have
rated drilling capacities of up to 250 feet of auger and over 900 feet of core drilling tools.
These rigs can perform drilling in water, in buildings, and in areas only accessible by
helicopter. All of our rigs are equipped to obtain soil and rock samples for laboratory
testing and most of the above can be used for auger, diamond core drilling, rotary, air
rotary, and air percussion hammer drilling. We maintain air percussion hammers in sizes
ranging from 3 to 10 inches in diameter, for the drilling and installation of wells in rock
formation.
We have experience with and the equipment for a wide range of drilling methods
including diamond core drilling, rotary, air rotary, air percussion and auger. Our inventory
of hollow-stem augers includes inside diameters of 3’, 4%, 65/8, and 10% inches. We
also have a wireline system for use with our 4% and 65/8-inch I.D. hollow-stem augers.
This system dramatically reduces the time needed for sampling events, which is
beneficial to our environmental clients.
Our drillers have a combined experience of over 100 years of drilling in Hawaii and
the Pacific Region. They can evaluate and select the most suitable drilling equipment to
expedite subsurface explorations rapidly and efficiently while obtaining the maximum
amount of subsurface data on an individual project basis.
Environmental
Geolabs has the capability and experience to provide environmental drilling
services for investigations of sites, which are potentially contaminated with hazardous or
toxic substances.
Geolabs adheres to ASTM and EPA regulations pertaining to the collection and
handling of samples of potentially hazardous materials, disposal of cuttings from borings
in these environments (IDW), and requirements related to decontamination of equipment
after drilling and sampling operations in these locations.
Geophysical Exploration Capabilities and Experience
Geolabs, Inc. offers a wide range of geophysical services, which provide as part of
our own exploration work or on a contract basis. Geophysical techniques may be used to
obtain valuable data not normally developed from drilling exploration.

In-Situ Testing
Geolabs also has a large inventory of in-house in-situ testing instruments to
supplement and refine the data from borings and geophysical surveys.
In-situ testing is a method by which soils are tested under their field conditions.
This test can save time and money. Some of the state-of-the-art in-situ testing equipment
owned by Geolabs are: Field Vane Shear Device, Cone Penetrometer, Pressuremeter,
and Guelph Permeameter.
We also have the necessary instrumentation and equipment to perform other field
tests such as plate load, field CBR, and field pumping tests. We have considerable
experience with inclinometers used to monitor lateral earth movements, a procedure
valuable for remediation work involving deep excavations and landslide studies. We have
automated piezometers and electric settlement monitoring apparatus. In addition,
portable computers enable us to acquire and process data on site.

6. LABORATORY TESTING
Geolabs, Inc. has an automated, state-of-the-art testing facility installed in an
environment-controlled laboratory to facilitate testing of samples for sensitive engineering
parameters and to reduce the potential of errors in data processing.
The system includes an ELE Direct Shear Device, BK pneumatic consolidometers
and. Karol Warner triaxial load frame and cells. All of these testing devices are
instrumented with load cells, pressure transducers and LVDT’s for computer data logging.
The BK consolidometers are modified so that the consolidation pressure can be
controlled by the computer through an electric pneumatic transducer. Fully automated
consolidation tests can be performed with our current set up.
Our Honolulu and Maui laboratories, which have been accredited as a Soil and
Concrete Testing Agency by the American Association of State Highway and
Transportation Officials (AASHTO) and approved by the U.S. Department of the Navy
and Army Corps of Engineers, is staffed with personnel who are trained to provide a
complete range of testing of soils, aggregates, concrete and asphaltic products in
accordance with ASTM, Military or AASHTO standard procedures, as appropriate. We
can also provide testing of water and environmentally sensitive substances. Specialized
tests can also be developed for unusual conditions and project specifications.

8. QUALITY CONTROL PRACTICES/TECHNIQUES
The project engineers and geologists are responsible for the technical adequacy
and accuracy of our recommendations and for the preparation of final reports. Work
prepared by our staff is subject to independent review by other staff members throughout
its preparation. Each final submittal is again reviewed critically for accuracy, applicability
and completeness prior to finalization and transmittal.
Parametric studies are conducted by computer analyses to determine critical input
parameters and the effect of variation of these parameters. Hand calculations and chart
solutions are used to assess the reasonableness of computer solutions.
All work is subject to internal peer review prior to submittal. This is fostered by an
open-office environment where project personnel are encouraged to discuss their projects
in a manner similar to that used in value engineering efforts. Supervisory review provides
another level of quality control and refinement.
In addition, we have quality control procedures in-place to standardize methods
and procedures pertaining to subsurface exploration and laboratory testing. Orientation
of new employees and reorientation of existing ones adhere to these procedures.
