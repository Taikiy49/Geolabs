INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed emergency generator
building at the Makakilo City wastewater pump station on Nohona Street in
Makakilo Oahu Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized
soils rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design and construction of the proposed

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed generator building site is located within the existing
Makakilo City Wastewater Pump Station site to the north of the existing
pump station structure The proposed building site is for the most part
a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical slope which slopes up away from the pump
station structure Ground surface elevations range from approximately 334
to 343 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an emergency generator building on the
site to provide emergency standby power for the existing wastewater pump
station in the event of interruption of the normal power supply It is
anticipated that this emergency generator building will be a single story
masonry structure with a slab on grade floor It is anticipated that
construction of the proposed building will require cutting about nine 9
feet into the existing slope

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes
Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end
of its activity before Koolau
The project site is located on the arid southern flank of Waianae
between two cinder cones on the southern Waianae rift zone Throughout
the area exposures of the Upper Member of the Waianae Volcanic Series
crop out through the thin residual surface soils
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths ranging between 11 0 and
15 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring 1 encountered approximately one 1 foot of fill composed of
stiff reddish brown clay with sand and gravel underlain by approximately
seven 7 feet of very stiff reddish brown clay with some sand and
gravel At about eight feet below the existing ground surface the boring
encountered extremely weathered basalt formation to the maximum depth
drilled of 11 0 feet
Boring 2 encountered approximately one and a half feet of fill
consisting of loose to medium dense gravel and cobbles underlain by
weathered basalt formation to the maximum depth drilled of 15 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
In addition to the two drilled borings visual observations were made
of the existing slope face These observations indicated that this slope
was cut in weathered rock formation

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering standpoint Based on our field exploration it
is anticipated that the foundations of the proposed structure if
constructed at the currently proposed location will bear on weathered
basalt rock formation
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that the
existing slope area will be cut down to provide a level pad for the
construction of the proposed generator building
The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading
1 Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The
resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill to the proposed building subgrade level
2 Due to the nature of the weathered rock formation encountered at
the site it is anticipated that some hard excavation e g
hoerams and or chipping will be required

3 Slopes in weathered rock formation may be cut to a ratio of 0 5H

4 Imported fill material if required should conform to the

Foundation Support
We recommend that the spread or strip footings bear on in situ
weathered basalt formation For the design of foundations bearing on the
in situ basalt formation an allowable bearing capacity of 4000 p s f may
be utilized
This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent outside grade
Settlements
Minimal settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the
weathered basalt formation
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor s of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions may be supported by
the in situ weathered basalt formation A 4 inch layer of base course
rock should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab
subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points shown on the Site Plans prepared by

Elevations of the data points were approximately

and Hara Incorporated revised to As Built Plans dated January 15
1982 The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths ranging from 11 0 to 15 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 A truck mounted rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual observation and testing in the laboratory All
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock samples were recovered using an NX double tube diamond core
barrel The core recovery REC and rock quality determination RQD are
shown at the appropriate sample depths
