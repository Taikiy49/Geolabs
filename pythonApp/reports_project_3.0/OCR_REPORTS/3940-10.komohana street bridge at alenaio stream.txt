Komohana Street Bridge at Alenaio Stream
Hilo Island of Hawaii
Dear Ms Kishimori
This letter report presents the results of the probing and grouting operation at
Komohana Street Bridge at Alenaio Stream on 2000 The work was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
A total of 40 probeholes were drilled below the proposed bottom of footings for the
proposed new box culvert at the Alenaio Stream crossing at Komohana Street in Hilo on
the Island of Hawaii The new box culvert which will be approximately 32 feet wide and
85 feet long and 12 feet high will replace a metal pipe culvert which was washed away
after heavy rains during the previous month The new culvert will be supported by a
concrete footing and slab with wingwalls at the inlet and outlet Thirty seven of the
40 probeholes spaced at approximately 8 feet on center were drilled below the concrete
slab The remaining three boreholes spaced at approximately 10 feet on center were
drilled at the downstream end of the culvert Since the existing ground surface at the time
of our probing and grouting work was approximately 1 to 2 feet higher than the proposed
bottom of the footings the probeholes were extended to depths approximately 11 to 12
feet below the existing ground surface Materials encountered consisted of fractured
basalt rocks with sand and gravel on the upper 3 to 4 feet underlain by basalt rocks
Probing was performed on December 18 and 19 2000 using an Ingersoll Rand air track
drill with 3 inch diameter percussion hammer After the probing operation the
probeholes were immediately backfilled with sand cement grout applied at low pressure
Significant cavities were not encountered during the probing work However the
amount of grout materials used for and 26 exceeded the
theoretical volumes by approximately 4 and 3 cubic yards respectively Since there
were no signs of cavities or voids observed in this area during probing and only
