INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed CSK Auto Store in Kalihi in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials
caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials The project site is situated in an area where the subsurface soil
deposits are dominated by calcareous reef deposits Land development and reclamation
projects within the last century have brought the project area to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As indicated above our field exploration generally encountered surficial fill materials
consisting of medium dense to dense silty sandy gravel underlain by stiff to hard clayey silts
varying from approximately 1 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface The clayey silt
soils fills encountered exhibit a slight expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in
the moisture content Medium dense to very dense consolidated marine sediments
consisting of silty coral sands and coral gravel coral detritus formation were encountered
below the silty fill soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted near surface soils and or new compacted fills may be used to support the
proposed auto store building It should be noted that cavities and or voids are commonly
encountered in the coral detritus formation encountered at the site Due to the presence of
the coral detritus formation at relatively shallow depths below the new foundations for the
store building we recommend that a program of probing and grouting below the foundation
locations be conducted during construction to reduce the potential for loss of foundation
support resulting from collapse of cavities and or voids below the foundations

Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed store building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square
foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils
or new compacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces Due
to the presence of the coral detritus formation near the foundation bearing materials we
recommend that a program of probing and grouting the foundation locations be implemented
for the proposed building during construction Detailed discussion on the probing and
grouting requirements are presented in the following subsection of this report
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new fills the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior
to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil

Foundation Probing and Grouting
As previously mentioned cavities and or voids are commonly encountered within the
coral detritus formations in Hawaii In addition the coral formation typically encountered in
the Kalihi area is generally susceptible to the presence of cavities and or voids To reduce
the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities and or voids
W O 4215 00 D Page 9
below the foundations we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting be
conducted below the footing locations
For preliminary estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled along
the perimeter footing spaced approximately at 10 feet on center and at each isolated spread
footing location The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend
to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of footing elevation If cavities and or voids are
encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional probe holes should be
drilled at closer spacing to help delineate the vertical and lateral extend of the cavity and or
void Following the drilling of each probe hole a 2 inch PVC pipe should be inserted into the
probe holes to preserve the open hole integrity for grouting A field engineer or geologist
from our office should be present to log the probe holes and to determine the locations of
additional probe holes needed if cavities and or voids are encountered
The probe holes cavities and or voids discovered should be backfilled with a fluid
sand cement grout We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture with an
approximate 6 to 9 inch slump range pumped at low to moderate pressures be utilized for
the grouting operation In lieu of the sand cement grout a highly fluid lean concrete such
as controlled low strength material flowable fill may also be used for the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting
operations are complete The contractor should be made aware that a longer lag time
between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be required in the
construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be a Special Inspection item and should
be conducted under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would
allow our firm to monitor the presence of cavities and or voids and to allow additional
recommendations to be made if excess grout take and or change conditions are observed

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed auto store
building As previously indicated our laboratory tests indicate that the clayey silts
encountered below the surficial granular fills in our borings exhibit a slight potential for
shrink swell when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural
distress of the slabs on grade resulting from swelling of the clayey silt soils we recommend
that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be properly prepared prior to placement
of the slab cushion
The subgrade soils below slabs on grade should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils should
be wetted and kept moist until placement of the slab cushion and concrete Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be
thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and
poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
For interior slabs such as the store slab we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick
cushion layer consisting of free draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab To
reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings
an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top
of the cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the plastic
membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively impervious soils on site soils and graded

Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site preparation work will be required for the proposed
project construction In general we recommend that the following guidelines be used for the
minor site grading anticipated At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris rubbish asphalt concrete
pavement and other unsuitable materials within the grading limits should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities Existing
underground utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with on site fill materials moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and demolition areas beneath the future slabs should be scarified to
a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In
addition the subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until placement of the slab
cushion and concrete In areas where the finished grades will be subjected to vehicular
traffic such as the pavement areas the subgrade shouid be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding tis also advised that gutter systems
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundations
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should

and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils

foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly

the potential for water infiltration
In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface water run off away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 5 3 to
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaiuation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 inch ring swell test was performed on a natural sample to evaluate the swelling
potential of the near surface soils The test result is presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of this
test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationships The results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR ASTM D 1883 test was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
One set of chemical analysis tests including Chlorides EPA Test Method 325 2 and
Sulfates EPA Test Method 375 2 was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate their corrosion potential The chemical analysis tests were performed by
Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific Results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 5
