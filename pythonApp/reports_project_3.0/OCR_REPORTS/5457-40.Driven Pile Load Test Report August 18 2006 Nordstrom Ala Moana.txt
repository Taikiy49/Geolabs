In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our revised fee proposal dated
a static pile load test was conducted on a precast prestressed concrete
pile driven for the Nordstrom Ala Moana building project in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii This letter report presents the results of our field observations and
measurements during the static pile load test program conducted on
Twenty six 26 test piles consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete piles were driven by Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company using a
hydraulic hammer from April 7 through 12 2006 The pile load test was
conducted on a selected test pile designated as Pile No 53 TP The load test pile was
driven on and is located at Gridline B 8 of the Nordstrom Building
gridlines A record of the pile driving log for the load test pile is provided on Plate 1 of this
letter report for reference
The selected test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the pile was
planned at about 300 kips However the pile was loaded to a maximum test load of
169 kips in 18 8 kip load increments where the pile experienced continued downward
movement with increased loading In general each load increment was held for at least
8 minutes In addition a 90 minute hold was maintained at the 150 kip design pile
capacity load increment to evaluate the creep effects of the pile After loading the pile
to the maximum test load the test load was unloaded in approximately four equal
decrements and held for at least 8 minutes at each decrement Due to the premature
failure of the load test pile the load test pile was reloaded to about 94 kips to observe
the pile performance after the pile experienced continued movement with increased
loading at the maximum test load of 169 kips

The load was applied to the test pile utilizing a hydraulic jack system acting
against a weighted platform load frame A load cell was used to observe the loads
applied during the test The settlement of the test pile was observed using two dial
gauges attached to two reference beams located on each side of the test pile The
needle of each dial gauge was set vertically on a fixed horizontal plate attached to the
test pile As the test pile moved downward the dial gauge readings decreased
As required by the test standards the secondary method to measure the pile
settlement consisted of a 1 100 inch division ruler set on the pile head along with a
surveyor s level to observe the pile settlement
In addition one 1 100 inch division ruler was set on each of the reference beams
and a remote location on a reference test pile The remote location was set outside the
immediate test area and the area of potential settlement from the reaction weights for
the load test set up The surveyor s level was used to observe movements of the
above mentioned locations

A plot of the pile movement measurements at the top of the load test pile with
increased test load is provided on Plate 3 of this letter report Based on the shape of the
load test curve it appears that the 16 5 inch octagonal concrete pile Pile No 53 did not
fail at a test load of 150 kips the design load However the load test pile Pile No 53
did not sustain a load equal to or greater than two times the design load or 300 kips
The load test pile failed in plunging continued downward movement upon increased
loading at a test load of about 169 kips
