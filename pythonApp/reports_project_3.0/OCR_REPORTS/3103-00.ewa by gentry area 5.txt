PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was in general conformance
with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and subsequent
amendments and additions
The scope of our work consisted of the following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site
2 Performance of in situ soil resistivity tests by Engineers from our firm at 14
locations in Area 5 These locations generally correspond to the 300 foot
spacing required in the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
Our field work on both areas was then performed on successive sunny
days with daily high temperatures of approximately 87 F and a generally dry surface
condition Unusual ground surface conditions are foot noted on the plates
Forty two 42 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 14 locations in the above
referenced area using the Wenner four pin method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts voltage output and 24 watts power
output into the ground This method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to
evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of four 4 five 5 and six 6 feet Among these three
spacings used one spacing or two may have more significance than the others for
allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass of soil
to the proposed pipe invert Such spacing s are flagged with one asterisk on the
attached plates
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them and the voltage drop between the

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this report
Elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate having been
estimated from those marked on the stakes as existing surface elevations and scaled of
the construction plans prepared by Gentry Hawaii dated April 1993 as approximate pipe
inverts All elevations were rounded to the nearest 4 foot to emphasize that they may not
be accurate enough for other purposes Thus elevations and locations of the presented
data should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
