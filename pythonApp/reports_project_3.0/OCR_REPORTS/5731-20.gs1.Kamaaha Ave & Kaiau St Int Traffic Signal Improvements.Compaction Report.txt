This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing

our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the intersection of Kamaaha Avenue and Kaiau Street and
adjacent to Kapolei Village 6 Parcel 2 within the existing Villages of Kapolei development
in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii As part of the planned development for
Kapolei Village 6 Parcel 2 traffic signal improvements are planned at the intersection The
project includes the reconstruction of three existing curb ramps and an adjacent sidewalk
A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading operations during reconstruction of the existing ramps and sidewalk
SITE GRADING
We observed the site grading work for the Kamaaha Avenue and Kaiau Street
Intersection Traffic Signal Improvements project from May 6 to 12 2010 The site grading
work consisted of the placement and compaction of the aggregate base course material
below the new curb ramps and sidewalk Paul s Electrical Service Inc performed the site
grading work We obtained a sample of the imported aggregate base course material

utilized as fill material and transported the sample to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were established in our laboratory in
general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The aggregate base course material was moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 4 These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
The existing subgrade soils beneath the aggregate base course at the sidewalk area
adjacent to the southeast ramp were wet and soft The wet and soft subgrade soil was
removed and replaced with suitable compacted fill material
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references
