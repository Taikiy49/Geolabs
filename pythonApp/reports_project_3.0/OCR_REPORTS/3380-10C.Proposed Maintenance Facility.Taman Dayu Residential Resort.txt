Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Proposed Maintenance Facility
Taman Dayu Residential Resort
Pandaan Java Indonesia
As requested we are providing the following geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the design of the proposed Maintenance Facility within the
Taman Dayu Residential Resort Project in the Pandaan area on the island of Java
Indonesia
The recommendations provided herein are based on a review of the boring logs of
the area prepared by Testana Engineering Inc and our observations during our site visit
in March 1996
Project Considerations
The proposed Maintenance Facility project will be located near the Sewage
Treatment Plant on the southeast side of the Taman Dayu Residential Resort site as
shown on the Boring Location Map Plate 1 The proposed Maintenance Facility will
entail construction of a one story maintenance building several small concrete pads and
light building structures a rock masonry retaining wall along the back of the maintenance
building and asphalt concrete paved areas In general we anticipate the proposed
structures to be lightly loaded and the retaining wall to be up to about 5 meters high We
understand that the finish floor elevation of the maintenance building structure is set at
elevation 47 meters The finish elevations of the other improvements are set at about
46 meters Based on these finish elevations we anticipate that a large portion of the

Subsurface Conditions
Based on our review of the logs of the two test borings B 38 and B 39 drilled in
the vicinity the subsurface conditions at the site generally consist of medium dense to
dense sands and gravels with cobbles and boulders extending from the ground surface to
the maximum depth of the borings of about 10 meters In Boring B 38 inorganic clays
and silts were encountered near the ground surface to a depth of about 2 meters followed
by the medium dense to dense granular soils For more detailed descriptions of the
subsoils encountered in the two test borings B 38 and B 39 please refer to the Boring
Logs presented on Plates 2 and 3
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of the field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the two test borings B 38 and
B 39 drilled in the vicinity we anticipate that the planned excavation to attain the final
design grades will expose the medium dense to dense granular soils consisting of sands
and gravels with cobbles and boulders at the proposed finish subgrades Therefore we
believe that the proposed maintenance facility structures may be supported on a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
medium dense to dense granular soils encountered in the test borings An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 20 tons per square meter tons m may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the medium dense to dense sands and gravels This allowable
bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for
transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces The allowable bearing
pressure provided is a net value therefore the weight of the foundations may be
discounted when sizing the footings
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 600 mm below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or
utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane
projected upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement
is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional

structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement We estimate that
the total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of
25 mm with differential settlements of less than 15 mm
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 5 tons per cubic meter tons m for footings embedded in the
granular soils and or compacted fills These values assume that the concrete for footings
is placed directly against the footing excavations or the soil around the footing is well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
300 mm below finished grade should be neglected
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 Dead weight of
the structure and 2 Weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable
Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be
estimated using an unit weight of 1 7 tons m
After excavating to the design subgrade elevation the excavation may expose
cobbles and boulders at the subgrade level If cobbles and boulders are exposed at the
subgrade level the cobbles and boulders should be over excavated by at least
300 mm below the subgrade level and replaced with the compacted sands and gravels
excavated from the site This is to provide a more uniform bearing surface for
foundations slabs and other improvements at the site All footing subgrades subgrades
for improvements and engineered fills should be compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction ASTM D 1557 prior to construction of improvements
If soft and or unsuitable materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the soft and or unsuitable materials should be over excavated to expose firm
materials and replaced with compacted fill Therefore footing excavations should be
observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete to confirm the competency of the bearing materials and embedment depths

Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
Maintenance Facility structures We understand that the standard practice for the section
below slabs on grade generally consist of 75 mm of lean concrete overlying a
50mm sand bedding As an alternative to the lean concrete over a sand bedding
slabs on grade for the maintenance facility may be supported on a 150 mm layer of
aggregate base course compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction based on
ASTM D 1557 The subgrade below the aggregate base course should be scarified to a
depth of at least 200 mm moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557 Provisions should
be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular
traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded
at least 300 mm below the lowest adjacent grade
Retaining Wall
We anticipate that rock masonry retaining walls of up to 5 meters in height will be
constructed at the rear of the maintenance building structure for grade separation
Foundations for the retaining wall should be designed in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Foundations section of this letter report
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due
to adjacent soils and surcharge effects For the rock masonry retaining walls the
recommended active lateral earth pressure for design expressed in equivalent fluid
pressure is 0 55 tons m for an assumed level backfill In general the active condition
may be used when the op of walls are free to deflect beyond about 0 5 percent of the
wall height
The values provided above are for the on site granular soils or granular backfill
materials Backfills behind retaining walls should be limited to granular materials less
than 50 mm in maximum size The backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture and compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 Over compaction of the wall backfill should be avoided
The lateral earth pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by
groundwater trapped behind the wails
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 300 mm wide zone of permeable

Gradi
In general we anticipate that the majority of the proposed Maintenance Facility
area will be in a cut condition with the exception of a small area where fills are anticipated
The following guidelines are provided for the anticipated mass grading operations
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site or at a designated area After
excavation of the site to the design subgrade elevation the exposed subgrade should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 200 mm properly moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Modified Proctor Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
structural fill compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction with moisture
contents above the optimum moisture Prior to placement of the backfill the
over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at least 200 mm

moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site or at a designated area
If cobbles and boulders are exposed at the over excavated subgrade elevation
the cobbles and boulders should be over excavated to a minimum depth of
300 mm below the subgrade elevation and be replaced with compacted engineered fill
This requirement is to reduce the potential for point loading of the footings and or slabs
and to provide more uniform bearing support The compacted engineered fill should
consist of the excavated on site granular soils such as sands and gravels The
engineered fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
The excavated on site granular soils may be reused as a source of fill materials If
the inorganic clays and silts encountered in Boring B 38 are exposed at the site the
inorganic clays and silts should not be reused as fill material unless further testing is
performed to evaluate the suitability of the materials as fills All fill materials should be
properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts
of approximately 150 to 200 mm in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction As a minimum inorganic clays and silts to be used as fill
materials at the proposed Maintenance Facility should have a CBR value of at least
15 with a swell value of less than 1 5 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883
Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
the fill placement should be keyed and benched into the native soils to properly bond the
fills to the native ground as the fill is brought up in layers Filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with
the above recommendations for fill placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over
filling beyond the design slope limits and cutting back to the design slope to provide
adequate compaction at the edge of the slope Fill slopes may be designed with a slope
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Cut slopes may also be designed with a slope ratio of
2H 1V or flatter
Surface water should be directed away from the slopes and drainage over the top
of any slope should be avoided Earth berms or V ditches should be provided at the top
of slopes to reduce the potential for drainage over the top of the slope In addition the
finish grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from foundations
and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the

Where shrinkage cracks are noted at the subgrade level after compaction we
recommend that the subgrade be scarified to a depth of about 200 mm
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to at

yielding of the exposed soil subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas to expose firm ground and replacement with
compacted fill
Design Review
It is recommended that preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the
proposed construction be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments
prior to advertisement for bids This review is necessary to evaluate conformance of the
plans and specifications with the intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations
provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of our recommendations
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from test borings drilled in the vicinity Variations of subsoil
conditions between the test borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident Geolabs Hawaii should be contacted to reevaluate the recommendations
presented in this letter report
