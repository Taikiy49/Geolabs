Dear Mr Higham
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations and utility installation for the West Kalaeloa Business
Park project from June 15 through August 17 2009
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The West Kalaeloa Business Park site is in the Kalaeloa area in the District of Ewa
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is approximately bounded by Roosevelt Road the former
Oahu Sugar Railroad tracks to the north Kalaeloa Boulevard to the east and open land
to the south and west
The previously proposed West Kalaeloa Business Park development was for
47 industrial lots totaling approximately 100 acres The planned development also
included site grading work for a 22 5 acre film studio property at the south end of the
project site Infrastructure improvements including circulation roads lighting system and
underground utilities such as sewer water and drain lines were also planned to serve
the previously proposed development

Site grading work for the construction would entail cuts and fills on the order of

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The site was partially mass graded from June 15 to August 17 2009 under our field
observations Delta Construction Corporation performed the site grading operations and
utility installation work during this time period The site grading consisted of clearing and
grubbing subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills and utility
trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades and areas
designated to receive fills were scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor Where coral formation was exposed at
the cleared and grubbed subgrade level the subgrade was proof rolled with a
Caterpillar 825 sheepsfoot compactor a minimum of eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of the on site clayey soils were generally placed in
near level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness brought to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Fill materials consisting of the on site excavated coralline materials were
generally placed in near level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
New sewer lines were installed to serve the previously proposed development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 96 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading and
utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report and addendum dated August 21 and 25 2008 respectively and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
