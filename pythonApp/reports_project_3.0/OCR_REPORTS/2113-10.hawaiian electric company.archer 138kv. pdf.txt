INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Archer 138 kV Underground
Transmission Line in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The our work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new transmission line from the Iwilei
and Liliha Substations to a proposed substation near Archer Lane at the
Hawaiian Electric Company s Ward Avenue facility
The transmission line will be a 138 kV system It is anticipated
that the line will be installed about 6 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface and the trench will generally be constructed by
conventional cut and cover method
Based on the plans provided to us the transmission line would
consist of two 2 steel pipes side by side approximately 3 feet apart
The pipes would be supported by concrete blocks at 15 foot spacing and
enclosed in a reinforced concrete jacket Generally a 6 inch thick base
cushion layer is planned However special earth backfill for thermal
considerations may be required around the pipes in some areas

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to trench excavation and backfill
for the proposed Archer 138 kV Underground Transmission Line
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1
Obtaining excavation and utility clearances for the drilling of
the borings in public rights of way
Drilling and sampling twenty four 24 borings to depths of 10 5
to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing trench excavation and backfill
recommendations
Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed transmission line corridor begins at the existing Iwilei
and Liliha Substations and follows Dillingham Boulevard and Liliha Street
to the intersection of Liliha Street and Vineyard Boulevard At this
intersection the corridor turns and follows Vineyard Boulevard to its
intersection with Iusitana Street It then follows Lusitana Street to
Alapai Street Alapai Street to King Street and King Street to Archer
Lane where it will terminate at the proposed Archer Substation
The terrain along the proposed corridor ascends from the lowest point
elevation 6 4 feet on Dillingham Boulevard to the highest point
elevation 55 5 feet on Insitana Street then drops to about elevation
9 0 feet on King Street Most of the land along the proposed route is
developed for urban and or suburban uses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling
twenty four 24 borings to depths of 10 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered varying subsoil
conditions along the proposed transmission line corridor Along Liliha
Street the borings encountered gray clay adobe overlying either
boulders or basalt formation Toward Dillingham Boulevard the borings
encountered silty sand fill material overlying soft lagoonal deposits
Along Vineyard Boulevard the borings encountered stiff alluvial
deposits consisting of gray clay silty sand and brown clayey silt with
occasional weathered cinder sand formation From Lusitana Street toward
Alapai and King Streets the subsoils consist mainly of black sand cinder
formation overlying coral formation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered relatively competent
subsoil conditions consisting of stiff alluvium and medium dense cinder
sand along most of the proposed corridor with the exception of very soft
lagoonal deposits encountered at the western end of the line Dillingham
Boulevard
Due to the potential heat generated from the high voltage system of
the transmission line corrective thermal backfill may be required as
backfill material for the duct line The recommendations on selection and
placement of the thermal backfill material are not within our scope of
work Based on the preliminary information supplied to us granular
materials are preferred
It is our understanding that the anticipated depth of trench
excavation will be about 6 to 10 feet below the existing grade Due to
the existing urban development along the route it is also anticipated the
proposed transmission line will frequently pass below existing utility
lines where the alignments of the proposed transmission line and the
existing utilities cross
Proper trench shoring and bracing are recommended for trenching to
minimize potential damage to the existing roadway pavement underground
utilities and surface structures

Trench Excavation
The anticipated depth of excavation is about 6 to 10 feet The
method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for most of the alignment
Harder excavation is anticipated near the Liliha Substation along Liliha
Street where boulders or basalt formation were encountered in the borings
Trench Wall Supports
It is our opinion that the trench walls in the medium dense to loose
alluvial silty sands and cinder sands may require shoring Shoring may
also be required for the portion of the line which encountered silty sand
fill and lagoonal deposits in the vicinity of Dillingham Boulevard
The excavations should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with applicable safety requirements The design and safety of the trench
supports should be the responsibility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut back ata
temporary slope of H V in lieu of the above wall supports
However greater amount of vertical pressures will be transmitted to the
duct line by widening of the trench as discussed below
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the top of
the open trench
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such monitoring is
particularly critical in the areas where existing structures are adjacent
to the trench excavation

Vertical Earth Pressures
Vertical loads on a buried duct are influenced by the width and depth
of the trench the size and flexibility of the duct the unit weight of
backfill material and the frictional resistance between the backfill
material and the trench side walls To compute the vertical loads on the
duct a unit weight of 120 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25
are recommended Earth forces upon the duct increase rapidly as the width
of the trench increases Therefore the width of the trench should be
kept to a minimun
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The new transmission line construction in general will not
materially increase the vertical loads on the underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface i e if the
existing grade is not raised by the construction of the duct line The net
difference in loading between the weights of the excavated material and
backfill will be relatively small Therefore significant settlements are
not anticipated if proper construction techniques are utilized
Duct Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a duct has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the duct Therefore the duct
bedding is an important design consideration
For ducts bearing on basalt stiff alluvium and cinder sand a
minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material is
recommended underneath the duct The granular bedding should extend
beyond the sides of the duct and should extend half way up the sides of
the duct The bedding material should consist of clean granular material
such as 3B Fine gravel or the corrective thermal backfill as recommended
by others
For ducts on the soft lagoonal deposits Boring locations 4 through 6
along Dillingham Boulevard near the Iwilei Substation an additional
12 inch stabilization layer of granular material is recommended below the

Nuuanu Stream Crossing
Based on the information provided to us the latest plan for the
crossing transmission line over Nuuanu Stream is to use the existing
bridge to support the ductline and oil retum line It is our
understanding however that the plan is pending on the State s approval
Should the current plan change so that a siphon crossing of the
stream bottom or a separate foundation system is required for the line
additional exploration will be needed in order to provide recommendations
regarding dewatering considerations and foundation design for the stream
crossing section of the transmission line The project geotechnical
engineer should be retained for additional work and consultation should
that situation arise
Dewatering
Dewatering may be required due to the high groundwater levels noted
in Boring 6 on Dillingham Boulevard and Borings 21 through 24 along
Alapai King and Cooke Streets
At the western end of the transmission line Boring 6 difficulty in
dewatering is not anticipated if the excavation is entirely within the
clayey silt deposits due to the relatively low permeability of this
material However if the trench excavation depths are greater than about
10 feet in this area it should be noted that the subsoils grade to sandy
silts which are relatively permeable These sandy silts may be
susceptible to piping and quick conditions
At the southern end of the transmission line Borings 21 through 24
groundwater level was encountered at about 6 5 to 10 feet below the
existing grade Due to the relatively high permeable nature of the cinder
sand dewatering if required is anticipated to be difficult

The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained It should be
coordinated with the excavation support system so that the stability of
the excavation is not jeopardized
The dewatering operation should be done without pumping out the soil
fines pumping clear water only The operation should be carried out
without creating quick conditions or softening the bottom of the
excavation
Trench Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
Granular backfill should be used up to about one foot above the duct
line to reduce the compactive effort required and possible damage to the
duct
Except for the Adobe clay boulders and soft lagoonal clayey silt
most of the excavated on site soils may be reused as the upper portion of
the trench backfill from above one foot or as recommended by Geotherm
Inc over the duct to within 2 feet of the finished grade Backfill
should be moisture conditioned and mechanically compacted to a minimm of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Below
roadway areas the backfill within 2 feet of the finished grade should
consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of its maximm dry
density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water quring
backfilling Should backfilling under water be necessary granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel should be utilized in lieu of the
above mentioned backfill materials to minimize excessive ground
settlements of the trench backfill in the future

Existing Utility Lines
Due to the urban suburban nature of the proposed transmission line
corridor it is anticipated that many existing utility lines would be
encountered during construction of the duct line All existing utility
lines along the route should be located and protected during construction
It is also anticipated that the proposed transmission line will
frequently pass below existing utility lines where the alignments of the
proposed transmission line and the existing utilities cross
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should he
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during trench excavation and
backfill placement to see that adequate support and compaction have been
obtained This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from objects indicated on the aerial photomaps undated provided
by R M Towill Corporation Elevations of the data points were
approximately estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the
same photographs The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples as
an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths on Plates A 1 through A 26
Four 4 one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded samples
to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
Two 2 Atterberg limits and one 1 sieve analysis test were
performed to aid in the soil classification The test results are
summarized on Plate B 2
Five 5 direct shear tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsoils The test results
are presented on Plate B 3 through B 6
One 1 consolidation test was performed on the soft lagoonal
deposit The test result is presented on Plate B 7
