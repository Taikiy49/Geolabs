Soil Engineering Report
Lot 168 Royal Summit Subdivision Phase III
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three 3 shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing of the subdivision site grading and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
Proposed Construction
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a two story structure approximately 50 by 87 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab is planned for the garage driveway and the main floor
level of the house The house will be of wood framed construction

Discussion and Recommendations
General
The very stiff to hard reddish brown clayey silt encountered
generally can provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to reduce the
swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the clayey silt soil obtained from the
borings indicate that this material generally has low swelling potential
if maintained in a moist condition
Therefore to reduce effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the swelling
potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
About 1 to 8 feet of fill is planned across the lot The available
on site material at the Royal Summit Subdivision area generally has
moderate to high swelling potential Therefore if this material is used
it should be kept below 24 inches of the finished lot grade and the soil
should be placed and compacted on the wet side of optimum moisture
content The remaining fill should consist of non expansive soil such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is
anticipated such as the garage driveway and the lower house areas the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping

House_ Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
stiff natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade

If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 10 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional amount of embedment is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore new fills within 24
inches of the finished lot grade should consist of non expansive fill and
the on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches
below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete
placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and sprinkled
with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
small slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be maintained
in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete
placement

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by scaling from the site plan Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by scaling from the site plan The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
