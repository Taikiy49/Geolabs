GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The utility trench backfill compaction and pavement sections work for the project were
observed by Geolabs Inc from February 16 to May 22 2000 The utility trench backfill
compaction and pavement sections work were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company During
the operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the select fill placement under the roadways and curbs the select fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The fill and select fill
materials under sidewalk slabs were compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
During the utility trench backfill compaction and pavement section operations a total
of 196 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 10 of this
report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained based
on testing or visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the

above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
