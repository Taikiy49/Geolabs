This letter report summarizes our field observations and preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations with respect to the rockfall event which occurred on
at Building 16 of the Kalihi Valley Homes public housing complex
The information provided is intended to inform the owner about the probable cause
of the rockfall event and both short term and long term considerations for potential rockfall
hazards at the project site The preliminary recommendations presented herein are subject
to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report Our work was performed as
emergency service for geotechnical engineering consultation
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A dangerous rockfall event occurred at the Kalihi Valley Homes KVH public
housing facility at approximately 0830 hours on Two very large boulders
came loose from the adjacent hillside and impacted the rear of Building 16 at KVH The
boulder impacts caused significant damage to two housing units near the middle section of
Building 16 No injuries were reported however families residing in the nine housing units
at Building 16 were evacuated until further notice The KVH housing facility is owned and
operated by the State of Hawaii Public Housing Authority HPHA The upslope lands
where the rockfall had emanated are believed to be owned by the City and County of
Honolulu Board of Water Supply BWS
Geolabs Inc was contacted by HPHA under emergency procurement procedures
and was requested to conduct a post rockfall incident evaluation of the existing site
conditions with respect to the stability of the slope affected by the rockfall event
Permission was obtained from the BWS to enter the upslope lands above the

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
At the rear of Building 16 two massive blocks of intact basaltic rock boulders with
diameters on the order of about 8 feet each were observed at the impact area The
boulders had struck and damaged the rear reinforced concrete building wall though did not
penetrate the building interior The boulders may be characterized as slightly to
moderately weathered dense vugular basaltic rock containing few fracture discontinuities
Fresh roughly planar to slightly irregular broken rock fracture faces were observed on a
single side of each fallen block Photographs of the fallen boulders are presented on
Plate 4
Based on our observations the rock fall line path was clearly discernable extending
from the graded slope at the rear of Building 16 up towards the failed ledge rock
outcropping representing the rockfall source within land believed to be owned by the BWS
The source ledge rock outcropping is at about mid slope near about Elevation 360 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL on the steep rocky hillside The location of the source ledge rock
outcropping and the fall line path with respect to Building 16 is depicted on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Based on our observations the failed outcropping exhibited a fresh rock surface
exposure measuring about 15 to 18 feet wide by about 10 to 12 feet high and nearly
vertical in orientation Based on our observation of the source rock outcropping with
respect to the character of the fallen blocks it appears that the source rock outcropping
experienced a break failure along a pre existing rock weakness contained within the
interior of the rock ledge outcropping at a depth of about 6 to 8 feet from the ledge face
Based on our observation and interpretation of the rock failure surfaces the rock ledge
may have had a pre existing poorly supported or unsupported overhang condition of up to
about 6 to 8 feet deep along the base of the ledge A schematic sketch of the observed
outcrop profile and outcrop failure mechanism is presented on Plate 3

The overhanging ledge condition is common where erosion prone rock clinker
erodes away beneath a layer of erosion resistant dense rock material leaving the overlying
dense rock poorly or unsupported and at risk for breakage along natural rock
discontinuities such as rock fractures or joints Photographs of the failed rock outcropping
are presented on Plates 5 through 7
Based on our brief reconnaissance and observation of some other adjacent ledge
rock outcrops in the vicinity of the failed outcrop similar dense rock overhanging
conditions possessing varying degrees of instability should be anticipated due to the
bedded geologic outcroppings protruding on the steep hillside The typical geologic
structure of the hillside is the result of sequential deposition of alternating dense rock and
weaker clinker lavas The weaker clinker rock materials erode faster than the surrounding
dense rock leaving prominent hard rock ledge features protruding from the hillside The
clinker materials and derived soils may erode to form gently to moderately sloping terraces
in between the ledge rock escarpments
Based on our reconnaissance along the boulder fall line path below the failed rock
outcropping a number of potential rockfall hazards were encountered The potential
hazards are not considered imminently dangerous however the rocks should be
completely demolished or stabilized prior to re occupying Building 16 It is our opinion that
these boulders should be demolished or removed from their present settings to reduce the
potential for additional falling rock hazards to affect the down slope development
The potential rockfall hazards consist of three visually prominent very large blocks
of rock boulders observed in precarious slope settings on the lower slope approximately
midway between the upslope KVH property boundary and the failed rock outcrop The
three large boulders appear to range between about 8 and 14 feet in larger dimension
The three potential rockfall hazards occur as a single boulder situated lower on the slope
and two other nested boulders situated higher on the slope just above the single boulder
All three boulders are adjacent to the boulder fall line path we observed on the hillside We
observed that the eastern edge of the single boulder was clipped by one of the boulders
involved in the rockfall event The boulder impact appeared to have displaced the single
boulder about 1 inch laterally indicating the potential for future movement The dual
nested boulders were not affected by the rockfall event however the boulders are
precariously resting against each other on a sloping ground surface Photographs of the
three large boulders recommended for immediate demolition are presented on Plates 9
and 10
In addition perhaps about 10 to 15 loose surface boulders ranging between about
2 and 4 feet in dimension were observed on the moderately sloping vegetated ground
surface just below the failed source rock outcropping These boulders appear to have
been broken and generated by the outcrop failure and falling rock activity The boulders
appear to be at risk for possible renewed sliding or rolling on the existing slope and are
just above an existing 10 to 15 foot high rock ledge escarpment capable of acting as a
topographic launch feature A photograph of the scattered surface boulders located within
the fall line below the failed rock outcropping is presented on Plate 8 These loose surface

PRELIMINARY FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our preliminary evaluation of the existing site conditions we believe that
there is generally a low potential for additional outcrop instability and hazardous falling
rock conditions associated with the failed rock outcrop However it is our opinion some
rock stabilization work within BWS land should be performed in the short term within a
couple weeks to reduce the potential for additional falling rock hazards in the vicinity of
Building 16 Once the rock demolition and removal is completed and accepted the
residents may be allowed to return to Building 16
Based on our observation of some selected rock outcrops adjacent to the failed
rock outcrop it appears that similar though not as severe potentially unstable rock
outcrop conditions may exist across the natural hillside above the Kalihi Valley Homes
complex Evidence of past fallen boulders some involving similar very large blocks of rock
was observed indicating the potential for future falling rock to occur In addition erosional
undercutting and slow loss of support below other ledge rock outcroppings should be
anticipated due to similar though less severe rock overhang conditions observed during
our brief reconnaissance Because of the potential for future rockfall activity to occur and
affect the KVH complex we strongly recommend conducting a comprehensive potential
rockfall hazard study to evaluate the existing hillside conditions and possible mitigation
alternatives that could be implemented to protect the existing downslope development
from falling rock hazards The study limits should encompass the upslopes extending from
the KVH complex towards the top of the ridge
Short Term Rock Stabilization
We recommend performing some rock stabilization work on the hillside within the
BWS property prior to re occupying Building 16 Because the work area is believed to be
within BWS land the work must be coordinated with the property owner We recommend
a complete demolition of the three 3 large blocks of rock discussed in the preceding
report section The demolition should include breaking of the subject rocks into smaller
fragments and manual redistribution of the rock fragments into suitable stable topographic
settings to reduce the potential for falling rock activity A rockfall specialty contractor
should be retained for this controlled rock demolition work
For controlled rock demolition we recommend the use of a hydraulic rock splitter
acting upon holes drilled in the rock to carefully pry and split the large boulder into smaller
pieces suitable for manual relocation on the slope A chemical expansion compound
inserted into drilled holes in the rock could also be used provided additional safety
measures are in place to control the timing of the rock splitting while demolition personnel
are on site to control the rock splitting reaction Temporary rockfall control safety netting or
barrier net fencing may be erected around and below the work area to control the
