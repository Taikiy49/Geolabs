INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading and preliminary
house foundation evaluation of the proposed Kalihi Subdivision development
in the upper Kalihi Valley area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of design
recommendations pertaining to the design of the proposed site grading
infrastructure access roads evaluation and preliminary building
foundations
The scope of our exploration included but was not limited to
1 review and compilation of the available soil and geologic data
pertinent to the general project area and its vicinity
2 field reconnaissance

The project site is located in upper Kalihi Valley on the island of

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed 4 3 acre site is located between Likelike Highway and
Kalihi Street in upper Kalihi Valley Oahu Hawaii Ground elevations on
the site range from 375 feet above Mean Sea Level in the southern portion
to approximately 525 feet above Mean Sea Level in the northern portion of
the site The topography slopes down towards the south with an average
gradient of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V to four horizontal to
one vertical 4H 1V
The area is densely covered with subtropical vegetation which
includes grass ferns heliconia various palms eucalyptus guava
banyan hao ironwood and other trees

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Kalihi Valley was formed by stream erosion of Tertiary Age Koolau
Range basalt flows This initial period of erosion and valley carving was
followed by several periods of relative rise and fall of sea level which
permitted valley filling by alluvium followed by subsequent periods of
erosion During the Quaternary Age Honolulu Volcanic Series lava flowed
down the valley and was subsequently partially covered by Quaternary Age
alluvium Continued recent alluvial processes have covered part of the
site with black to dark gray boulder clay
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling seven 7 new borings to depths ranging between 19 5
and 33 8 feet below the existing ground surface Five 5 new test pits
were excavated and sampled to depths ranging between 7 0 to 10 0 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the new
borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil Conditions
In general the borings encountered a surface layer of stiff dark
brown to gray adobe clay varying between 0 to 10 5 feet in thickness
Below the surface adobe layer mottled brown stiff to hard silty clays and
clays older alluvium colluvium varying between 7 0 to 19 0 feet in
thickness was encountered The older alluvium colluvium layer was
underlain by grayish brown medium dense to dense highly weathered basalt
to the maximum depths drilled
The surface adobe clay layer was not encountered in portions of the
southern area of the site

CONCIUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our exploration disclosed a surface layer of expansive clay adobe
which varied in thickness between 0 to about 10 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Portions of this Adobe soil appear to be overcast
material placed during the construction of the adjacent Likelike Highway
Generally the thicker adobe layers were encountered in the westem
portion of the site
Because of the shrink swell potential and downslope creep movements
of these Adobe soils it is our recommendation to strip this material
from the site during earthwork and haul off site for disposal The
stripped material will have to be replaced with properly compacted select
material or the final finish grades will have to be lowered from those on
the present grading plans
Site Grading
Cuts up to about 30 feet in depth are planned for the eastern portion
of the site and fills up to about 20 feet in vertical thickness are
planned for the western portion of the site
The surface adobe layer should be stripped and removed from the
site prior to fill placement
The following recommendations present general guidelines for site

Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared of vegetation

House Foundations
Normal spread footing type foundation bearing on the older
alluviun colluvium deposit or compacted fill may be used for houses on
relatively level lots However on the steeper two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V slopes deeper pier type foundations will be necessary to
reduce erosion and subsequent loss of bearing support for the post footing
foundations

General guidelines for house foundation design are as follows
1
Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade to resist erosion and
surface soil shrinkage heaving
Footings on sloping areas should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
The set back distance is the horizontal distance measured from
the outer edge of the footing cut to the slope face For a two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V slope ratio a minimm of 2 5
feet embedment at the lower side would be required This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist downhill
slope creep and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of
the soil To eliminate difficulty of backfilling the excavation
we recommend that concrete be used to construct the deep pier
footings with column straps buried in the concrete backfilled
excavation Refer to Plate 7 for details
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f on the stiff natural
soil or compacted fill is recommended for the anticipated light
loads
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleared of
loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be
observed by a geotechnical engineer to see that adequate bearing
soil has been obtained
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
It is recommended that a 12 inch embedment be utilized for
thickened slab edges to reduce future water infiltration under
house slab areas

house construction

resistance should be reduced accordingly The passive soil resistance

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the access road Due to
the wet site conditions we recommend that the A C pavement section
consist of the following
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
6 Inches Select Borrow
14 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on
Compacted Moist Subgrade
Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to verify the
adequacy of the above section The recommended section considers medium
traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved
areas Weepholes should be provided at the base course level in the
drainage inlet structures in order to relieve the seepage water which may
accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping and
estimation from reference points Elevations of the borings were
approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on the
topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

II Special Considerations
A
Cut slopes shall be excavated to the lines and grades indicated
on the contract drawings and where necessary adjusted in
accordance with the recommendations of the Geotechnical Engineer
during construction
All trench backfill placed above 12 inches of the top of the
pipes shall be compacted in maximm lift thickness of 8 inches
unless specifically authorized by the Geotechnical Engineer
The Geotechnical Engineer may require thinner lifts should
compaction operations consistently fail to produce the specified
compaction requirements In any event the required levels of
compaction shall be obtained throughout the full depth and width
of each layer of backfill
Jetting and ponding of the backfill or fill materials shall
not be permitted on this project unless specifically authorized
by the Geotechnical Engineer in writing
Upon completion of grading or temporary interruption of grading
and the subsequent termination of observation by the
Geotechnical Engineer no further filling or excavating shall be
performed without notifying the Geotechnical Engineer in advance

III Site Preparation
A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be
disposed of in accordance with Section 10 CLEARING AND

All vegetation and deleterious materials encountered within the
Grading Limits shall be cleared and disposed of off site This

Organically contaminated topsoil shall be stripped prior to
actual grading Materials determined as being unsuitable for

off site Any material incorporated as a part of the compacted

cisterns tunnels septic tanks wells pipelines or others not
located prior to grading are to be removed and the resulting
depression backfilled and compacted in accordance with these
specifications

be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be observed and approved by the Geotechnical
Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimm moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them

2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and
in no case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
F The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or

producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
G The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
H The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
