From to we have observed the
earthwork and grading operations at the above referenced project This
report summarizes our field observations and compaction test results
The general grading procedure consisted of overexcavating on site
expansive clay on the northern and western portions removing the rock
pile along portions of Road A and capping the site with imported low
W O 1519 10 Page 2
expansion potential borrow fill In general the expansive clay soils
including adobe were removed within 3 feet of the finished grade Some
of the expansive clay were utilized in the lower portion of the fills a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade on the eastern side of the
site However imported low expansion potential materials were used in
the upper 3 feet of fill to provide a capping layer over the expansive
clay
Periodic field density tests were done in accordance with the
American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM test designation D 1556
Sand Cone Method Since some of the fill material consisted of large
gravel and therefore were too large to perform compaction tests visual
observations were used to supplement the conventional compaction testing
Based on our test results and observations it is our opinion that
the fills have been compacted adequately
During the grading period on site and borrow soils were tested
prior to being used in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in accordance with
ASTM test designation D 1557 modified Proctor and the test results are
as follows

RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
General house foundation recommendations for preliminary design
purposes are as follows
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 10 Page 4
1 Footings located on or near slopes should penetrate down a
sufficient depth in order to develop a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of
the footing foundation out to the slope face
Individual footings on level should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the ground surface to minimize the
effect of subsequent soil shrinkage associated with moisture
loss due to drying under the roof area All footing backfill
should be properly compacted to obtain 90 compaction and
graded to drain water away from the footing
2 An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soil
3 Slab on grade construction should be set back at least 6 feet
from the tops of slopes
Twelve inch deep perimeter footings below the lowest adjacent
grade are recommended to act as a cut off wall against the
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting
around the slab
For all of the houses it is critical that the subgrade soils below
the foundations be kept moist for a few days prior to concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 10 Page 5
It is imperative that all the lots be graded to drain water away
from house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the
subgrade soils
The individual house foundation plans should be reviewed by a soils
engineer on a lot by lot basis to see if the above general recommenda
tions need to be revised for specific house and lot conditions
We recommend that the soils engineer be present during the
foundation construction to see that the soil conditions are consistent
with our previous findings He should test and approve all imported fill
Material inspect the over excavation saturation backfill placement and
recompaction and footing excavations prior to concrete placement
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within
_ the lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the
observations of a qualified soil technician
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the final mass grading
without our knowledge and written approval
