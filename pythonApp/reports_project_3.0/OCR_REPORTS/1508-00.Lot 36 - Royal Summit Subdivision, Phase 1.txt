In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near surface
soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation reconmendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing of the
soils samples obtained a review of the subdivision site grading plans
and the preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approximately 37 by
76 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage Concrete slab on grade is
planned for the entire structure
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northern end of Leke Place in the Royal
Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Cahu Hawaii The lot is generally
level in the proposed residence structure area The northern portion of
the lot gently slopes downward from Leki Place towards the north at a 6 1
H V ratio
A review of the previous site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 36 had cuts up to 6 feet deep at the front portion and fills up
to 8 feet deep along the rear portion of the lot
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings which were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan encountered 5 feet of reddish brown clayey silt soil residual
soil
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface material indicated
that the underlying residual soil ranges from dry to moist 30 to 42
percent moisture contents and exhibits low to moderate expansion
potential in its natural state However previous experience indicated
that this type of material could have high swelling potential if allowed
to dry out first and then get saturated Therefore measures should be
taken to prevent excessive drying of the underlying soil

low to moderate swelling potential therefore some precautionary
measures are recommended for foundations and on grade slabs placed on the
existing soil
The garage and the house finished grade will be close to the existing lot
grade and therefore will bear on the existing soils Foundation and
subgrade preparation reconmendations are presented in the following
sections

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and driveway
A deeper 18 inch footing embedment and special subgrade preparation with
lime saturation is recommended The general procedure listed hereunder
should be followed
1 The slab subgrade scils should be saturated with hoses with small
holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying
subsoil
2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the slab perimeter
footing excavation and thickened edge of the driveway prior to the
above water saturating of the subgrade scil We believe that about
ten to fifteen 50 lb bags of hydrated lime would be adequate to
cover the slab subgrade It is important that the soils engineer
should inspect the above subgrade preparation and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing

3 The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose
soils It is anticipated that the very soft silt and lime mud
deposited at the footing after saturation should be removed just
prior to concrete placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and final
concrete placement at each lot should be kept to a mininum in order
to prevent excessive subgrade drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating with lime on the subgrade
should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio slabs
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of 3
Fine rock cushion fill base course and should be separated from
foundation
A plastic vapor barrier placed over the base course layer may be used
should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect floor covering
Lot_ Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
conditions Also the owners should be advised to install a gutter

00 Page 7
system around the house and that the gutter water should be diverted far
away from the footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper performance of
the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
Grain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
