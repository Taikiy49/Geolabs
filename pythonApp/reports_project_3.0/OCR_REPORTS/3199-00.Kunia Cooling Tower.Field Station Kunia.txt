INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new water cooling system at Field Station Kunia near
Schofield Barracks on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
GEOLABS HAWAII

design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Field Station Kunia near Schofield Barracks on the
island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to replace the existing once through open loop
engine cooling system with a recirculating evaporative water cooling system to support
the existing diesel engine generators The project will involve the construction of new
cooling towers pump house and associated pipelines in close proximity to the existing
cooling towers We understand that site grading is not required for the project
construction Considering the extensive existing improvements at the site it is likely that
portions of the site could be underlain by previous fill or backfill

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Field Station Kunia near Schofield Barracks on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The site is situated about 800 feet west of Kunia Road and about
1 mile north of the village of Kunia The site is fenced and existing site improvements
include a water cooling tower buried fuel tanks microwave antennae water tanks etc
The site slopes gently toward the east No vegetation exists at the proposed
cooling tower site although the surrounding areas are planted with sugar cane
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Koolau Volcanic Series
which were deposited during the Pleistocene Stearns and MacDonald 1946 In this
area of Oahu the Koolau lava that ponded against the older Waianae volcanic rocks have
experienced relatively rapid weathering leading to the formation of a thick profile of
residual and decomposed saprolitic rock
Subsoil Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring to a
depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Our boring encountered a deposit
of residual soil approximately 15 feet thick consisting of very hard clayey silt material
This was underlain by saprolite deposit extending to the maximum depth explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our field
exploration Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time of
GEOLABS HAWAII

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration the project site appears to be underlain by a
deposit of very hard residual soil overlying saprolite material It is our opinion that the
proposed improvements is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view provided
that the recommendations presented in this report are followed
We believe that the hard residual soils encountered at the project site would
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed cooling tower and appurtenant
improvements It appears that shallow footings and slab on grade foundations may be
used to support the new facilities Detailed geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the foundation design of the proposed improvements are further discussed
in the following sections of this report
Shallow Foundations
Because of the competent residual soils encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings and slab on grade foundations may be used
for support of the proposed new improvements
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on the hard in situ residual soils
encountered at the project site This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3199 00 Page 6
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the lowest adjacent
finish grade However footings adjacent to the existing structures should extend at least
to the same depth as the existing structure footings The actual depths of the existing
footings are not known at this time and they should be confirmed in the field during
construction by the project geotechnical engineer
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the hard in situ soils to be less than 1 inch total and inch differential
If foundations are located next to utility ttenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the hard in situ soils
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over excavation should
either be backfilled with select granular fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction or the footing bottom be extended down to the firm in situ soil
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3199 00 Page 7
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with procedures
described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Footing excavations should be observed by a soils engineer or representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm
foundation bearing conditions and the embedment depths
Slab On Grade
We anticipate concrete slab on grade may be used for the interior portions of the
proposed cooling tower and pump house structures We understand that these slabs
may be subjected to heavy loadings and vibration impacts from the operation of the
equipment therefore a 6 inch layer of base course aggregate is recommended below the
concrete slab to serve as a slab cushion
The subgrade should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations and
guidelines discussed in the Site Preparation section of this report We recommend that
the concrete slab be designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and should
be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some amount of differential
movements
The base course supporting the concrete slab should consist of crushed basalt
aggregates compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For design of
structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i
may be used for the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to
any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade

Site Preparation
Minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project construction At the on set
of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of existing structures that are to be demolished should be
completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
horizontal lifts of compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are to be abandoned
in place should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath the future slabs should
be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the subgrade level over
excavation may be required to expose firm material The resulting excavation should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material and to raise the ground
surface to the proposed finish subgrade elevation should consist of non expansive
granular material placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
The select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such
as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3199 00 Page 9
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Any imported fill material should be independently tested and approved for its intended
use by a soils engineer prior to delivery to the project site
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about 1 foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material The
backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3199 00 Page 10
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to
advertisement for bidding This review is necessary to evaluate conformance with the

recommendations

specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at
the time this report was prepared

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field boring Variations of subsoil conditions across the
site may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field boring indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from features shown at the project site The physical location
of the boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the boring was measured at the times given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Log Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Boring Log at the
appropriate sample depths
