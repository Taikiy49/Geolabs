INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed BMW Dealership located at 777 Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing
and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 777 Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided it is proposed construct a new BMW
automobile dealership at the subject site The project site currently exists as an abandoned
warehouse structure
We understand that the proposed project will consist primarily of major renovations
to the existing warehouse building Based on the available information some of the existing
columns will be removed or relocated and new columns will be added to support the roof
structural loads We also understand that the existing concrete slabs on grade will be
restored and reused for the new automobile dealership A portion of the existing warehouse
will be raised about 18 inches In these areas we understand that new fills will be placed
over the existing concrete floor slabs In addition we anticipate that new roofing parking
areas and utility lines will be installed for the proposed development
Details of the project plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate that minor site grading consisting of less than 2 feet of cuts and fills
will be required to achieve the design finished grades

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation Most of the existing coastal plains developed during the
Pleistocene Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below
the present stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water
became available such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly
lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level
was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments and coral algal reef
growth accumulated at higher elevations

The project site is located near the coastal plain of southern Oahu The coastal plain
was built on the eroded flanks of Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern two thirds of the

placed over these deposits for the commercial developments within the last century
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed BMW auto dealership project site is located at 777 Kapiolani Boulevard
in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Kapiolani
Boulevard to the northeast Dreier Street to the northwest and Waimanu Street to the

An existing warehouse building currently occupies the majority of the project site The

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 Three of the five borings were located
inside the existing warehouse building and the remaining two borings were drilled outside
of the warehouse building in conjunction with the environmental investigation The borings
were drilled to depths ranging from about 14 9 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Generally our field exploration encountered surficial fills consisting of medium dense
silty sands with gravel up to about 5 feet below the existing ground surface Some pockets
of loose silty sands were encountered within the surficial fills at localized areas Below the
surficial fill layer medium dense cinder sands were generally encountered extending to about

ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater levels measured

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by up to about 5 feet of
surficial fills consisting of medium dense silty sands with gravel It should be noted that
localized pockets of loose fill materials were encountered in some of the borings drilled The
fills were generally underlain by medium dense cinder sands extending to about 8 to 14 feet
below the existing ground surface Below the cinder sand deposits medium dense to dense
coral sands and gravel were encountered extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 25 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was encountered at
depths of about 5 to 6 feet below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration
In general the new and relocated columns for the proposed renovation of the
warehouse building may be supported on spread footing foundations However due to
possible presence of loose materials near the ground surface some of the column footing
subgrades may need to be over excavated by up to about 24 inches and replaced with
compacted fills depending on the subgrade conditions exposed at each footing location In
GEOLABS HAWAII

addition new footings to be located adjacent to the existing footings should be lowered to
the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper to avoid surcharging the
existing foundations In general the new footings should not extend deeper than the existing
footings by more than about 6 inches to avoid undermining the existing footings
We understand that new fills will be placed in some portions of the warehouse building
to raise the finished floor elevations The new fills may be placed directly on the existing
concrete floor slab however we recommend that some holes be punched into the slab to
allow for drainage of potential seepage water that may become trapped in the new fills We

the new fills planned at the site

Building Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed renovation of the structure will involve
relocation of some existing columns and construction of new columns Based on the
subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread footings may be
used for support of the proposed new columns However our borings encountered pockets
of loose fill materials near the existing ground surface Therefore some of the column
footing subgrades may need to be over excavated by up to about 24 inches and replaced
with compacted select granular fill materials minimum 90 percent relative compaction
depending on the subgrade conditions exposed at each footing location The requirement
for over excavation at the footing location should be evaluated by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii during construction Therefore contract documents should include additive
and deductive unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for
variation in the over excavation quantities
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we recommend that an allowable
bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf be used for the design of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4243 00 Page 8
foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils This bearing value is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade New footings to be located adjacent to the existing footings should be
lowered to the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper to avoid
surcharging the existing foundations with additional structural loads In addition the new
footings should not extend deeper than the existing footings by more than about 6 inches
to avoid undermining the existing footings
The bottom of footings should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete A representative of
Geolabs Hawaii should be present to evaluate the bearing materials and depth of
embedment of the footings If unsuitable materials are encountered in the footing
excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils and replaced
with well compacted fill
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately
0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the building may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils or compacted fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf for footings embedded in the
surficial fill materials This assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted

should be neglected
Slabs On Grade
We understand that most of the existing slabs on grade will be restored and reused
for the new automobile dealership We also understand that a portion of the existing
warehouse will need to be raised by up to about 18 inches and new concrete
slabs on grade will be constructed in these areas In these areas we believe that the
existing slabs may be left in place and the new fills may be placed directly on the concrete
slabs However in order to reduce the potential for seepage water that might be trapped
between the existing and new slabs adequate drainage should be provided To relieve
the potential seepage water pressures that might develop we recommend that 2 inch
diameter holes spaced at about 15 to 20 feet on center be punched through the existing
slabs to promote better drainage
To provide more uniform support under the new concrete slabs we recommend that
the new concrete slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill The
cushion fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary moisture break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration
of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the
slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane placement of a 2 inch layer
of moist fine sand may be considered above the membrane If the 2 inch sand layer is
desired the sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing the slab concrete
Site Preparation
We anticipate that only minor site grading consisting of less than 2 feet of cuts and fills
would be needed to achieve the design finished grades for the proposed project Site
grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4243 00 Page 10
important that a representative from our office be present to observe the grading operations
to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process
and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our
exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other deleterious and organic
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Existing pavements to be
demolished should be completely removed The over excavation resulting from demolition
should be backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be
abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled
with select granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition areas at grade or are designated to receive fill should
be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Based on our field exploration the excavated on site soils are generally granular in
nature and may be re used as a source of general fill and backfill material Fill and backfill
materials required for the project construction should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction
requirement for the finished subgrades of pavement areas should be increased to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Where needed imported materials should consist of non expansive select granular
fill materials such as crushed coral and basalt gravel The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Fill materials should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to hauling to the
project site for the intended use
Pavements
Based on the proposed renovation and construction we envision that some minor
areas with asphalt concrete pavements may be constructed at the project site We anticipate
that the vehicle loading for this project and the new pavement areas will consist primarily
of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We have made our
preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the
medium dense silty sand soils encountered in the borings Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary design
purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Laboratory
CBR and field density tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design section The
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4243 00 Page 12
recommended pavement section is based on the assumption that good drainage will be
provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches
into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration of
landscape water into the pavement section
Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines such as electric duct lines may be required for the
proposed project In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches
of sand or S4C be used under the electrical duct lines for uniform support The initial backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes should also consist of free draining materials such
as sand or S4C to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the
backfill The sand and or S4C should be densified using a vibratory plate or similar light
compaction equipment Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and
damage to the improvements above the trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site granular soils or
select granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future
ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
