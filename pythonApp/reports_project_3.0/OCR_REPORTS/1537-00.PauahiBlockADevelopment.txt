PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This report presents the results of our subsurface investigation
performed at the site of the proposed Pauahi Block A Development
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and groundwater conditions at the proposed site to provide soil
data for use in the design of the foundations for the proposed
structures

Our study consisted of drilling and sampling five 5 borings
within the proposed development site laboratory testing and field
reconnaissance The results of our field exploration and laboratory
tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusion and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this
report
Boring data from a previous soil investigation report by our firm
entitled Soil and Foundation Investigation Proposed Pauahi Parking
Structure Block A Pauahi Project No Hawaii R 15 Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii of March 17 1981 was also used in our study
PROJECT DEVELOPMENT
The proposed development consists of two 23 story towers about 60
by 160 and 60 by 190 feet in plan dimension surrounded by a 5 story
parking structure with one level of underground parking
The finished floor elevation of the basement parking varies from 8
feet along the northern River Street boundary of the site to 5 feet in
the central portion then to 9 5 feet at the Maunakea Street boundary
The estimated column loads for the tower and parking structure are
1200 to 1600 kips and 650 kips respectively
SITE CONDITIONS
Site Description
The project site covers most of the City block surrounded by
and River Streets in Honolulu Hawaii
The northern portion of the site is presently vacant and used as a
parking lot There are several existing buildings in the southern
portion of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1537 00
I D 1629 Page 3
The site is terraced with the lower northern portion at elevation
8 to 9 feet while the upper southern areas are at elevation 11 to 18
feet above MSL
Geology
The project site is situated over the edge of a former stream which
cut through this area
The base member or bedrock consists of basaltic lava flow from the
Honolulu Volcanic Series The bedrock was deeply scoured by stream
erosion at the time when the sea level was lowered to as much as 300
feet below its present elevation As the great glaciers melted and the
sea level rose erosion of the bedrock ceased and extended periods of
deposition followed The deposits generally consisted of alluvium and
ash washed down from the upper valleys
The alluvium was subsequently covered by a sandy beach deposit
The area was eventually covered by a coralline reef formation that
developed from accretion of coralline debris when the sea level rose to
an elevation higher than its present level As glaciers formed causing
the sea level to drop the reef deposit was subjected to cementation
As the sea level continued downward the upper coralline and alluvial
deposits were disected by stream erosion The stream erosion cut
through the northern portion of the site to depths of about 25 to 60
feet
As the sea level again rose to its present level the stream
channels were filled with fine grained organic soils which were
deposited in a quiet water lagoonal environment
Finally in recent time the area was filled for development

Subsurface Exploration
The field exploration disclosed complex soil conditions below the
project site
Soft lagoonal deposits consisting basically of clays silts and
sands were encountered below the northern portion of the site toward
River Street The soft deposits extend from near the ground surface
down to depths of 55 to 60 feet along the northwestern River Street
side of the structure Toward the southeast Maunakea Street
direction the soft deposits taper off to 10 to 15 foot depths
Generally these soft deposits have high moisture content and exhibit
high compressibility under loading
A layer of stiff alluvium was encountered below the upper lagoonal
deposits and over the lower basalt rock formation which was encountered
at 60 to 80 foot depths
Along the southern Maunakea Street side of the proposed
structure the soft lagoonal deposits were not encountered In this
area a generally hard coral layer was encountered in the upper 15 to
20 foot of the borings This coral layer was underlain by medium
density to loose silty sand to a depth of about 30 feet The upper
coral and sand layers were then underlain by stiff alluvial deposits to
depths of 65 to 105 feet down where a basalt rock formation was
encountered
Groundwater level was encountered in the borings at 1 0 to
2 0 feet elevation

and 4

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is situated at the
transition between the soft lagoonal deposits and the coral formation
Our recent Boring A confirmed the previous boring information in
the area indicating that the northern portion of the site is underlain
by soft soil condition and therefore the Ewa Tower and surrounding
parking structure should be supported on pile foundation
Our investigation was concentrated near the Diamond Head Tower
where limited boring data from a previous exploration in the area was
available and competent coral formation was anticipated
The four recent borings B D F and G confirmed the presence of
the upper coral formation However due to the depth of the basement
excavation into the coral formation and the moderately compressible
deposits under the coral excessive settlements are calculated for a mat
foundation for the highrise tower Therefore pile foundations are
recommended for the entire development
Building Foundation
The northern portion of the site is underlain by soft compressible
lagoonal deposits which are unsuitable for foundation support for the
proposed highrise Ewa Tower and the multi level parking structure Pile
foundations extending through the upper lagoonal and alluvial deposits
down to the basalt rock formation encountered at 60 to 80 foot depths
are recommended
The southern portion of the site is underlain by a hard near
surface coral layer which extends down to near the natural water level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1537 00
D 1629 Page 6
A mat foundation was evaluated for the highrise Diamond Head Tower
however since the proposed basement excavation would remove much of the
hard coral layer and the underlying sand and alluvium below the coral
which was found to be moderately compressible the calculated
settlements were excessive Therefore pile foundation down to the
basalt rock formation at 65 to 105 foot depth are recommended
It appears that portions of the S5 story parking structure could be
supported by spread footing foundation on the upper coral layer
However discussions with the design team indicated that it would be
preferable to have a uniform foundation system for the various
structures Therefore pile foundations are also planned for the
parking structure
Previous highrise projects in the nearby areas Blocks G and F
encountered thick dense boulder layers above the rock formation which
prevented penetration of the piles down to the rock formation In the
borings for this project the dense boulder layers were not
encountered River deposits could be identified however they
predominantly consisted of rounded gravel with only a few cobbles and
boulders Based on the boring data it is our opinion that the piles
may be driven down to the basalt rock formation An extensive test pile
program is recommended to confirm the anticipated driving performance

Based on the present subsurface soil data from the borings it is
anticipated that the pile lengths would vary from about 60 to 80
feet below the existing ground surface in the northern portion of
the site and 65 to 105 feet below the existing ground surface in
the southern portion of the site
Predrilling through the upper dense coral layer would be required
for the Diamond Head Tower and the parking structure in the
southern portion of the site The estimated limits of the coral
formation requiring predrilling is indicated on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The anticipated predrilling depth would be about 20 to 25
feet below the existing ground surface
Due to the variable soil conditions we recommend that a minimum of
about 30 to 40 test piles be first installed to determine the
drivability of the piles and to estimate the final production pile
casting lengths for the project The lengths of these test piles
should be about 75 and 100 feet for the northern and southern
portions respectively
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
30 000 foot pounds energy The type of hammer to be used for
production pile driving should be the same one used for test pile
installation
After the test piles are installed two piles should be selected by
the soils engineer for load testing to two times the design loads
of 125 tons in order to further verify the competency of the
bearing stratum
The 125 ton test piles should be driven to a final driving
resistance of about 15 blows per inch for the last two inches or to
about 100 blows for the last foot

Lateral Soil Pressures
The basement walls will be subjected to lateral earth pressures
They would be laterally restrained and therefore at rest soil
pressures of 50 p c f are recommended for the wall design For the
sections of basement wall in coral the at rest lateral soil pressure
may be reduced to 35 p c f if required
Passive soil pressures of 200 p c f soft to medium river and
lagoonal deposits and the pile lateral resistance could be utilized to
resist the lateral earth pressures Again this passive soil pressure
may be increased up to 500 p c f for the coral formation
Basement Floor Slab
The borings indicate that the proposed basement level of 5 to
8 5 feet elevation should be above the soft stream deposits however
variable fill material is anticipated The bottom of the excavation
should be proof rolled and any soft areas detected should be
over excavated for a depth of 2 feet and backfilled with select granular
material such as base course rock

Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel base course material
The slab on grade should be separated from walls and foundations
Earthwork
Cuts up to about 10 feet are planned for the proposed basement
wall Easy excavation is anticipated for the northern portion of the
site Harder excavation will be encountered along the southern portion
of the development in the coral material
Minimum fill is anticipated for the proposed project The site
should be cleared of all debris old foundations and asphaltic concrete
pavements prior to any filling
Select on site material such as the excavated coral sand should
be stockpiled and reused as fill footing and basement wall backfill
material The fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal loose
lifts of 8 inch maximum and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 All other fill and
cushion materials should be approved by a soils engineer prior to being
used on the site
Pavements
Concrete and asphaltic concrete pavements may be utilized for the
development Generally variable existing fill material is anticipated
in the northern portion of the site while competent coral formation
should be encountered at finished grade in the southern portion

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The pile driving operations along with the predrilling of the pile
locations should be monitored on a full time basis by a qualified soils
technician under the direction of a soils engineer to verify that the
piles have been driven in accordance with our recommendations
It is recommended that excavation and backfill operations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
