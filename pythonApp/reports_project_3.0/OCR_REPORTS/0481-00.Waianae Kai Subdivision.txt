DISCUSSION
All deleterious material such as lumber logs brush roots
and rubbish were disposed of offsite prior to placing fill
All loose and compressible materials were removed to solid
natural ground or bedrock in areas to receive fill
Surfaces to receive fill were scarified and bladed to a
depth of 6 inches watered and mixed as required to achieve
optimum moisture conditions and compacted to at least 90
of maximum density with a Wagner Tamper
The fill material was placed in horizontal lifts restricted
to six inches in thickness watered as required to achieve
10

optimum moisture conditions and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of maximum density with a Wagner Tamper
Field density tests were taken at vertical intervals of
approximately 2 feet Areas of fill which were found to
contain insufficient moisture and or failing tests were
reworked until passing test results were obtained
Fill placed on surfaces having a slope gradient steeper
than 5 1 were keyed and benched into firm material in
accordance with recommendations of the above referenced
preliminary report
Compaction on the face of fill slopes was achieved by
track rolling with a D 9 dozer
The transition zone of fill over cut slopes was stripped of
all topsoil prior to placing fill
The maximum vertical depth of fill is 23 feet which is
located on Lot 145

containing expansive soils The lots with this cap are as
follows

Lots 114 through 134

This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations
made to the slopes or pads on the subject tract subsequent
to the issuance of this report without our knowledge and
written approval All ramps made through slopes and pads
and other areas of disturbance which require the place
ment of compacted fill to restore them to the original
condition will not be certified unless such backfilling
operations are performed under our inspection and super
vision and tested for required compaction Loose material
cast over certified compacted slopes shall negate our
certification of slope face compaction unless material is
removed in accordance with our instructions

trenches and behind retaining walls then such backfill
ing operations must be performed under our inspection
and supervision and tested for required compaction Of
major concern are utility trenches beneath or adjacent
to structures Trenches in excess of three feet in depth
will require a density test for every two feet of com
