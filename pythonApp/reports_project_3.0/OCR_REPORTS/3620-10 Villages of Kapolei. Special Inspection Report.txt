This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the pavement and utility construction for a portion of Road A within the future Makai

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is generally bounded
by Kapolei Parkway to the north Kapolei Intermediate School to the east and the future
Makai Villages 7 8 to the west and south
The roadway and utility construction for the subject portion of Road A alignment
were performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd The earthwork operation consisted
of clearing of the existing ground over excavation of the surficial expansive clays
preparation of pavement subgrades placement and compaction of select borrow and
base course and utility trench excavation and backfill

Following the clearing operation the existing ground was generally over excavated
to approximately 20 5 inches below the finish grades to provide the recommended
pavement section The over excavated subgrades were generally scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor Select borrow and base course were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
of the maximum dry density
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 130 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2 1
through 2 5 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction
plans These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the roadway and
utility construction for the above referenced project observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated August 12 1996
and subsequent recommendations
