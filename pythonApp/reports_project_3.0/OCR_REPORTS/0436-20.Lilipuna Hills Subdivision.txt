Field Compaction Report
Lilipuna Hills Subdivision
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
1976 to June 1977 periodic inspections and
density tests were made at the subject site grading
which included filling Phase II parking and roadway
areas This report summarizes our inspections and
testings
Compaction test results for the in placed
fills are enclosed in the Compaction Test Summary
During the field operations periodic field density tests
were made in accordance with the American Society of Testing
and Materials Test Method D 1556 Sand Cone Method
The
embankment fill construction for Phase II was accomplished
by utilizing the on site cut materials from the Phase 1
upper hilltop wetted and compacted with sheepsfoot roller
1977
Keying and benching methods were incorporated where filling
was required on natural slopes greater than 5 1 slope ratio
Prior to fill placement the original ground surface was
undercut to remove existing vegetation and other deleterious
materials
The fill was then placed in essentially horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum 90 of the maximum density determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 The maximum dry
densities of various soils were determined with values rang
ing from 88 to 93 p c f
In the Phase II fill area subdrains were installed to release
hydrostatic pressure build up and seepage water at the lower
area Type A subdrain was used as a major subdrain line and
Type B subdrain was used as a branch line The Type B sub
drain was used to connect the existing subdrain outlet from
the shopping center parking Jot to the Type A subdrain The
Type A subdrain pipe ran along the lowlying area of Phase II
following the existing 30 inch diameter drain pipe to the new
box culvert extension at Lilipuna Road
Additional inspection and approval will be required at areas
of disturbance during the future building construction
Utility trench backfill operations within the building pad
should be properly backfilled and compacted to match the
adjacent compacted subgrade Such backfilling operations
