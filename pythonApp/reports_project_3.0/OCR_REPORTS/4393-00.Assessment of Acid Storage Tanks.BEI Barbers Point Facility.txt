In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration to evaluate the foundation stability and integrity of the
existing acid storage tanks after a recent release of sulfuric acid from one of the storage tanks
This letter report summarizes our work and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering
assessment for the proposed project These findings and discussions are intended for
evaluation purposes only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Brewer Environmental Industries
BEI facility at Loop in Campbell Industrial Park at Barbers Point on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The BEI facility encompasses approximately 4 19 acres and is
approximately bounded by the Chevron Hawaii Refinery to the north undeveloped parcels to
the south and east and the Pacific Ocean and adjoining shoreline to the west The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
BEI currently uses the Barbers Point facility for the handling and storage of bleach
sulfuric acid various fertilizers and other industrial chemicals The BEI facility consists of
six main buildings 1 a mixing and packaging warehouse 2 a battery acid plant 3 a small
warehouse 4 a bulk warehouse 5 a former iron sulfate plant and 6 an administrative
building In addition the facility also contains three large sulfuric acid above ground storage
tanks as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided Tank No 1 reportedly released approximately
30 tons of sulfuric acid on Since the project site is located in an area
generally underlain by coral deposits consisting of calcium carbonate at shallow depths we
understand that there is a concern that cavities and or voids may have been created as a
result of the chemical reaction between the sulfuric acid and the coral deposits Therefore it

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions in the vicinity of the acid storage tanks and assess the foundation stability and
integrity of the subject tanks after the recent sulfuric acid release Our work was performed
in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
The scope of our work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house soil and boring data pertaining to the
project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of portable drilling equipment and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of four borings to depths of about 6 5 to 17 feet below the
existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
field engineer from our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering assessment pertaining to
the foundation stability and integrity of the existing sulfuric acid storage tanks
7 Quality assurance and design team consultation by a principal engineer from
our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring 8A and 12 extending to depths of about 6 5 to 17 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2

In general our field exploration encountered a very thin about 6 inches fill layer of
loose cinder gravel at the existing ground surface This was underlain by approximately 5 feet
of granular fill material consisting of medium dense to dense silty basalt gravel Below the
surface fill materials our borings generally encountered dense to very dense coral deposits
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 17 feet below the existing ground
surface
Based on our field exploration some thin soft zones were encountered in Boring No 7
between depths of about 5 and 9 feet below the existing ground surface In addition acid
water was encountered in Boring No 8 at a depth of about 4 5 feet below the existing ground
surface However cavities and or voids were not encountered throughout the depths explored
in the borings drilled for the subject project
Groundwater was encountered in the majority of the drilled borings at depths ranging
from about 8 3 to 9 8 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the proximity of the
Pacific Ocean to the project site and the generally permeable coralline subsoil conditions it
is likely that groundwater levels measured in the borings will be affected by tidal fluctuation
of the sea level Furthermore it should be noted that the groundwater levels may be
influenced by seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors that may occur
outside of the project area
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the
Boring Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 7 A sieve analysis test was
performed on a selected soil sample obtained and the test results are presented on Plate 8
Discussion and Evaluation
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the vicinity of the acid storage
tanks it appears that cavities and or voids were not encountered throughout the depths
explored in the borings drilled for the subject project In addition we understand that similar
findings were obtained from the direct push boreholes performed by others for the Phase II
Site Investigation conducted under separate contract
Our field exploration indicated that Boring No 7 did encounter some thin soft zones
within the coral deposits between depths of about 5 and 9 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on the high penetration resistance obtained during our field exploration we
believe that the structural integrity of the coral formation underlying the site has not been
significantly affected by the presence of these thin soft zones
In addition our field exploration indicated that the area is generally underlain by
approximately 5 feet of fill materials consisting of medium dense to dense silty basalt gravel
below the existing ground surface We believe that the subject acid storage tanks were
underlain by similar materials to about the same depth This surface layer of compacted

granular fill material serves to provide a uniform bearing support layer for the acid storage
tanks and is more resistant to acid degradation than coral materials
Based on our field exploration results and the discussions presented above we believe
that the existing acid storage tanks are adequately supported by the compacted granular fill
material and the underlying coral deposits Therefore the recent sulfuric acid release did not
appear to have affected the foundation stability and integrity of the ground below the existing
acid storage tanks
Limitations
The findings and discussions submitted in this letter report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident unless exposed by excavation If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by Brewer
Environmental Industries The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
