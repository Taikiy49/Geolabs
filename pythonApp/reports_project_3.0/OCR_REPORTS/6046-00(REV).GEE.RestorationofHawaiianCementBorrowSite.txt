Our field observations indicate that the existing pits were excavated into dense
coral formation and cemented coralline deposits There are many loose stockpiles around
the site However our test pit exploration generally encountered competent sand and
gravel material in the stockpiles A limited amount of deleterious material including
abandoned equipment concrete slabs and tires were noted
Significant fills up to about 25 feet thick and cuts up to 30 feet deep will be needed
in order to achieve the design grades for the proposed project With proper keying and
benching and compaction effort the existing soils in this area should be able to support
the high fills
Some settlement may be anticipated from the compression of the fills and in situ
soils Sufficient time should be allowed for the high fills to settle and come to equilibrium
prior to construction of permanent structural elements on the site It is anticipated that the
fill will be placed incrementally over a 2 year period therefore a significant settlement
waiting time may not be required An assessment of the rate of settlement should be
conducted at the completion of filling and prior to construction of permanent structures
Specific recommendations for structural design elements such as_ building
foundations and road pavements will depend on the type of material used to fill the site
Therefore these structural details should be determined when the fill is completed
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed discussion and specific
recommendations for the design of this project

SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Restoration of Hawaiian Cement Borrow Site project in the Honouliuli
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the mass grading construction only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is northeast of Barbers Point Harbor and south of the existing
railroad easement in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site was
previously used by Hawaiian Cement as a borrow site for aggregates Borrow pits up to
30 feet deep have been excavated Stockpiles of various materials were noted in and
around the borrow pits It is desired to fill the borrow pits to restore the site
We understand that the pits may be filled on an intermittent basis as suitable fill
material becomes available However the filling and restoration will be completed by
The site will be developed when the pits are filled to near the
existing ground surface
Geotechnical consultation is desired to provide recommendations for suitable fill
material and placement procedures It is important that the fill material and placement
be documented properly to provide an accurate record of the fill construction

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau The site is on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and to
the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine sediments
and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the Interstate Route H 1 Highway area to over
1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining
formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition
of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present Agricultural
development within the last 100 years has brought the area to its present form
2 2 Site Description
The project site is northeast of Barbers Point Harbor and south of the existing
railroad easement in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site was
previously used by Hawaiian Cement as a borrow site for aggregates Borrow pits up to
30 feet deep have been excavated Stockpiles of various materials were noted in and
around the borrow pits Access to the site is via dirt roads from Kalaeloa Boulevard or
Barbers Point Harbor
The project site consists of several borrow pit areas interconnected by dirt roads
The exposed side walls of the pits vary from about one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V to near vertical and consist of medium dense to dense coralline formation
varying from cemented sand to cemented beach rock and coral The bottoms of the pits

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the general geology of the area and previous explorations indicate
that the project site is within a coastal plain of deep calcareous marine deposits
primarily coral formation Field observations of the existing excavated pit walls and the
test pit exploration confirmed the presence of coral formation within and below the site
In general the site is suitable for the proposed deep fills and cuts Relatively
small settlements are anticipated due to compression of the underlying coral material
from the new fill weight and compression of the fill due to its self weight Due to the
incremental filling planned the settlements are not anticipated to pose a significant
problem to the project schedule We recommend conducting a settlement potential
assessment when the fill level approaches the finished grade to determine if a
settlement waiting period or special foundation design is required for the planned
structures
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Ground Settlement
Fills up to 25 feet thick should be placed for the proposed development Some
ground settlements are anticipated due to the thickness of the fills The anticipated
ground settlements are primarily the result of the following two processes
compression of the fill material under its own weight
e compression of the in situ soils induced by the new fill loads
Due to the incremental filling planned and competent subsurface conditions the
settlements are not anticipated to pose a significant problem to the project schedule
We recommend conducting a settlement potential assessment when the fill level
approaches the finished grade to determine if a settlement waiting period or special
foundation design is required for the planned structures

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material

After clearing and grubbing the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of
10 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1551 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend thoroughly moistening the soil to close the cracks or preparing again
as recommended above This will be particularly important for this project due to
the incremental filling Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade
due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill A Geolabs
representative should evaluate the need for over excavation in the field The
excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for
fill material
Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of materials less than 6 inches in largest
dimension and be free of vegetation and deleterious materials The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 5 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 3 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 The fill material
within the top 5 feet of finished grade should have a low expansion potential
Fill material for the top 5 feet should consist of low expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction This higher
compaction requirement is to reduce the long term fill settlement and the potential
for differential settlement
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Since moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey soils are critical elements
of earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil density tests during
site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction
and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required
additional compaction effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content
as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the
moisture requirement of the fills and subgrades about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture is an important requirement for the use of clayey soils within the
general vicinity of the project
Keying and Benching
Due to the slope conditions keying and benching will be required for earthwork at
the site These keys and benches should be firmly seated into existing ground
At the toe of fills on ground where the existing slope is steeper than five horizontal
to one vertical 5H 1V a keyway should be cut down into the in situ material This
key should be a minimum of 2 feet in depth at the toe of the fill and should be a
minimum of 8 feet in width extending back under the toe of the fill The floor of the

As fill placement progresses up the existing slope benches should be cut into the

Excavation of Upper Bench
Some places of the existing pit sidewalls are near vertical Therefore there is a
potential for significant differential settlement due to the differences between the
compressibility of the deep fills and the existing coral formation In order to reduce
this potential for differential settlement we recommend excavating a 10 foot wide
bench to enlarge the fill area to remove the abrupt change in fill thickness when the
fills reach 5 feet from the finished grade
