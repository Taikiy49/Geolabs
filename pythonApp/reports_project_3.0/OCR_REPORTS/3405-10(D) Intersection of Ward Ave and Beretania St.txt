In accordance with your request we have evaluated the stockpile materials the
contractor plans to use as fill materials at the above referenced project site At the time
of our site visit on August 22nd there were two stockpiles of the on site soils We
understand that the two stockpiles at the site were generated by mixing the on site
excavated soils with other on site and imported materials It should be noted that we
have excluded the clayey clumps of materials within the stockpile materials in collecting
the composite soil sample
The following is a summary of the laboratory test results performed on the
composite sample of the two stockpiles A Modified Proctor test ASTM D 1557 and
a sieve analysis test were performed on a composite sample of the stockpile materials
retrieved from the site The test results are presented on Plate 1 Based on our
laboratory test results the composite sample of the stockpile materials generally consist
of silty sands with gravels Based on the results of our laboratory tests it is our opinion
that the stockpile materials may be reused as a source of fill materials provided the
clayey clumps and over sized materials greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension
be removed from the fill material
Generally the fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to the relative compaction stipulated in the plans and specifications for the
