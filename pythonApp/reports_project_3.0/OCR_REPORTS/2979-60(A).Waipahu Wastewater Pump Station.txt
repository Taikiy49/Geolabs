In accordance with the project specifications a test pile program was undertaken for
the Waipahu WWPS Modification project between May 23 and May 27 The test pile
program included performing dynamic load tests on six test piles This report presents the
results of our wave equation analyses field observations during the test pile program and
recommendations for driving of the production piles
Based on the results of the test pile program we recommend that the new estimated
pile lengths presented herein be used for the production piles for the project The dynamic
load test results indicate that the design compressive load capacities can be achieved with the
shorter pile lengths

expand and improve the existing Waipahu WWPS to meet the growing needs due to

WAVE EQUATION ANALYSES
A wave equation analysis was performed on the pile driving equipment proposed for
the project The wave equation analysis was conducted based on the pile and hammer
information provided by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc dated March 10 2000 and the subsurface
conditions contained in the plans for the project
Subsurface Conditions
The borings drilled at the site generally encountered about 7 to 10 feet of surface fill
materials underlain by thick soft compressible alluvial soil deposits extending to approximately
93 to 99 feet below the existing ground surface The alluvial soil deposits recent alluvium
consisted of silty and clayey soils Medium stiff to very stiff older alluvium was encountered
below the recent alluvium and extended to depths of approximately 193 feet below the ground
surface The older alluvium consisted of clayey silts and sandy silts with gravel and cobbles
Very stiff to hard saprolitic soils partially weathered rock materials were encountered below
the older alluvium extending to the maximum depth of the borings of approximately 238 5 feet
below the ground surface
The site of the proposed new emergency generator building and underground fuel
storage tank is generally underlain by about 2 5 to 4 feet of surface fill materials The surface
fill materials are underlain by soft compressible recent alluvium extending to depths of about
85 to 96 feet below the existing ground surface The recent alluvium generally consisted of
silty and clayey soils Medium stiff to hard older alluvium underlie the recent alluvium and
extend to the maximum depth of the borings of about 181 5 feet below the ground surface
It should be noted that a void space of approximately 1 foot in height was observed
between the bottom of the floor slab of the existing pump station and the underlying subgrade
soils We believe that this void space is likely the result of on going ground settlements due
to consolidation of the thick soft soils at the site

TEST PILE PROGRAM
A test pile program which consisted of driving six piles at various locations across the
site was conducted between May 23 and 27 The purpose of the test pile program was to
establish the driveability of the piles given the subsurface conditions at the site driving
equipment and methods of installation In addition the program also provides information on
the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing stratum across the project site which
was used to estimate the lengths of the production piles
The six test piles were driven on May 23 and 24 and they were re tapped three days
later on May 27 The test piles were instrumented with a pile driving analyzer PDA for
dynamic load testing purposes The dynamic load tests were conducted by a representative
from Applied Foundation Testing Inc

DYNAMIC LOAD TESTS
As previously mentioned dynamic load tests were performed on each of the six test
piles as required by the project specifications Each test pile was instrumented for monitoring
by a pile driving analyzer PDA to evaluate the stresses that developed in the test piles and
to evaluate the pile bearing capacity

accelerometers to the pile head approximately 2 to 3 pile diameters below the pile head

PRODUCTION PILE LENGTH AND DRIVING CRITERIA
Based on our observations of the test pile program we believe that the driving
equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the specified production
piles in general accordance with the project specifications Based on our observation of the
hammer performance pile driving behavior and dynamic load test results it is our
professional opinion that the test piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance
to provide the design load capacity
Production Pile Length
Based on the test piles driven we believe that the project site may be divided into three
distinct areas for the production pile lengths
Area 1 Northern portion of the project site including the transformer pad
control room addition and partial header vault Pile Nos 1 through 29
e Area 2 Central portion of the project site including the area of the pipelines
supported on 12 inch square concrete piles Pile Nos 30 through 36
e Area 3 Western portion of the project site including the underground fuel
storage tank and emergency generator building Pile Nos 37 through 74
There are a total of 74 piles located within these three areas Of the 74 piles there
were two test piles in Area 1 and four test piles in Area 3 Therefore there are 68 piles
remaining to be installed as part of the production pile installation
Based on our analyses we believe that the piles in Area 1 should be 150 feet long to
achieve the required capacity For Area 1 we recommend that the piles consist of 70 and
80 foot pile segments to achieve the desired 150 foot long piles In Area 2 and Area 3 the
piles may be cast at about 130 feet long In order to achieve the 130 foot long piles we
recommend that the two segments consist of 70 and 60 foot long segments Based on the

Pile Driving Criteria
Based on our analyses and observation of the test pile driving we believe that the
following pile driving criteria may be used for the production pile driving
16 5 Inch Octagonal Piles Based on Pump Setting 2
1 Drive to the full depth of the production pile ordered or
2 When the aggregate blow counts reach 55 blows per foot for the last foot
of penetration or
3 When the aggregate blow counts are at least 6 blows per inch for the last
6 inches or
4 When requested by the field representative from Geolabs
12 Inch Square Piles Based on Pump Setting 1
1 Drive to the full depth of the production pile ordered or
2 When the aggregate blow counts reach 53 blows per foot for the last foot
of penetration or
3 When the aggregate blow counts are at least 6 blows per inch for the last
6 inches or
4 When requested by the field representative from Geolabs
It should be noted that the pile driving criteria may be modified during the production
pile driving to accommodate variation in the soil conditions It should also be noted that each
pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected or its design
capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more than 4 hours and
the pile cannot be driven to the required depth During soft driving such as when the pile tip
is within the soft soils the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that
significant tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
