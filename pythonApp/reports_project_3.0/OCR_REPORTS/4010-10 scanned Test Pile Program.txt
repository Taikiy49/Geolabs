PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Pier 1C Extension project is located to the west of the existing Pier 1C at
Kahului Harbor on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that the
project will involve the construction of an extension to the existing pier The new concrete
pier deck will measure approximately 300 feet long and 47 feet wide
The new pier deck will be supported by both vertical and battered precast concrete
driven piles A total of 151 precast concrete piles consisting of 114 vertical piles and
37 battered piles are planned to support the new pier extension The location and
designation of the pile foundation support system are shown on the Foundation Plans
Plates 2 1 through 2 3
The pier extension will have a finished deck elevation set at approximately 8 90 to
9 28 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW The area in front of the new pier extension
will be dredged to an elevation of approximately 35 feet MLLW in order to accommodate
the docking of future vessels

TEST PILE PROGRAM
The test pile driving program was conducted between and
under the near continuous observations by a field representative from our
office The purpose of the test pile driving program was to establish the driveability of the
piles given the subsurface conditions at the site driving equipment and methods of pile
installation In addition the program also provided information on the driving resistance
and contours of the load bearing stratum across the project site which was used to
estimate the lengths of the production piles
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company used a single acting
diesel hammer for the test pile driving operations Our field observations indicated that
fuel pump setting number 4 was used for the pile hammer with a maximum rated energy of
approximately 69 900 foot pounds The test piles consisted of 20 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles with a total length of about 125 feet The piles were cast in two
pieces and were connected using a Sure Lock mechanical splice The top and bottom
segments were 75 and 50 feet in length respectively
A total of 13 test piles including Pile 116 121
135 142 and 149 designated as TP 1 through TP 13 were driven to a tip elevation
ranging between about 77 4 and 102 7 feet MLLW It should be noted that three test
piles Pile and 142 were broken in an accident involving a barge at the site
after their installation Therefore replacement piles will have to be driven for these three
piles during the remaining production pile program Individual pile driving records have
been transmitted to your office during the course of the test pile driving operation The
foundation plan test pile driving summary and individual pile driving records are attached
to this report for your reference
Static Pile Load Tests
In accordance with the project specifications compressive pile load tests were
conducted on two selected test piles designated as Pile 6 and
between January 11 and 23 2002 The test piles were loaded to the maximum test load
of 640 kips in 80 kip load increments in general accordance with the Standard Loading
Procedure of ASTM Test Designation D 1143 Detailed discussions and results of the
static pile load tests are presented in our report entitled Compressive Pile Load Tests
Pier 1C Extension Job No H C 3296 Kahului Harbor Maui Hawaii dated
transmitted under separate cover
Dynamic Monitoring Program
As required by the project specifications a dynamic monitoring program was
implemented during the test pile program by a representative from InSituTech Ltd Each
of the 13 test piles was instrumented for monitoring by a pile driving analyzer PDA to
evaluate the stresses that developed in the test piles and to evaluate the pile bearing

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION PROGRAM
Based on our observations and the results of the test pile program we believe that
the driving equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the specified
production piles in general accordance with the project specifications Based on our
observation of the hammer performance pile driving behavior static load tests and
dynamic monitoring results it is our professional opinion that the test piles have been
driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of
160 tons with a factor of safety of 2 Geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the production pile installation program are presented below
Predrilling Spudding
Based on our field observations during the test pile program it appears that a very
dense soil ledge on the order of about 7 to 10 feet thick was encountered between about
Elevations 55 and 75 feet MLLW for some of the test piles Test Pile 71
and 149 during the test pile driving operations It is our opinion that the production piles
for the project should penetrate this thin soil ledge and bear on the basalt rock formation
anticipated at greater depths
In order to facilitate the pile driving operations through this dense soil ledge we
recommend that vibratory spudding be conducted at the pile locations located from
Bent 163 to Bent 172 extending to depths of about 75 feet MLLW prior to the pile driving
operations at this time Because some of the originally proposed test pile locations have
been deleted from the project a more accurate delineation of the spudding extent could
not be made at this time Therefore additional vibratory spudding may be required and
should be expected at some of the pile locations beyond Bent 172 during the production
pile driving operations Recommendations for predrilling spudding beyond Bent 172 would
be provided based on the progress of the predrilling spudding limits currently
recommended

Pile Driving Criteria
A single acting diesel hammer with fuel pump setting number 4
should be used for the production pile installation program Based on our engineering
analyses and the test pile program conducted for the subject project the following pile
driving criteria may be used during the production pile driving operations
GEOLABS INC

1 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the pile tip elevation
reaches below 75 feet MLLW and the aggregate blow counts reach
135 blows per foot for the last foot of penetration
2 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the pile tip elevation
reaches below 75 feet MLLW and the blow counts reach 13 blows per inch
for the last 6 inches of penetration
3 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the top of the pile is
driven to an elevation equal to the cut off elevation plus the 24 inch strand
extension
4 The pile driving operation may be terminated when requested by the field
representative from the State
It should be noted that the pile driving criteria may be modified during the
production pile driving to accommodate variation in the soil conditions In addition each
pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected or its design
capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more than 4 hours
and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth and or penetration resistance During
soft driving such as when the pile tip is within the soft soils the hammer stroke or energy
should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile stresses do not develop within
the pile
