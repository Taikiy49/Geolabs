INTRODUCTION
We have performed a preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration at the site
of the proposed Hokukano Golf Course project This preliminary exploration was
conducted in general accordance with our proposal dated to provide
information for the preliminary golf course mass grading design and for obtaining a
grading permit for the project A second phase of exploration is recommended to provide
additional data for final design and for use in the development of contract documents
This report presents our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
and rock conditions within the proposed golf course grading area in order to provide data
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design
of the proposed golf course mass grading for the development
Proposed clubhouse structures maintenance facilities or subdivision development
related to the golf course have been specifically excluded from the scope of this
exploration and the recommendations presented herein should not be considered
applicable to those related developments
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of the available soils and geologic information pertinent to the
project site and its vicinity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 3
2 Reconnaissance of the site by a Field Engineer Geologist from our firm
3 Excavation and logging of fourteen 14 shallow test pits within the
proposed golf course grading limits using a combination loader backhoe
4 Coordination of the field work and logging of the test pits by a Field
Engineer Geologist from our firm
5 Engineering analyses of the exploration data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed golf course grading
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and design team consulta
tion by a Principal Engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The Hokukano Golf Course project site is located at the approximate boundaries
of the North and South Kona Districts in Kealakekua on the big island of Hawaii The
area is situated approximately 3 000 feet east upslope of Kuakini Highway and is
accessible by jeep trails and unimproved access roadways
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 4
The ground surface elevations at the site range from approximately 1900 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL along the westerly boundary to approximately 2550 feet
above MSL along the easterly boundary The site encompasses an area of over 400 acres
and is characterized by gently rolling terrain with drainage in a westerly direction Two 2
prominent drainageways traverse the site in a westerly direction through the central and
northern portions of the proposed golf course area
The area is lushly vegetated with grasses and Ohia trees with the vegetation less
extensive in the eastern upland areas The eastern and northern portions of the site are
characterized by exposed weathered basaltic lava flows of the pahoehoe type elsewhere
the surface of the site is mantled with a relatively thin layer of silts with localized
outcroppings of basalt cobbles and rocks
The proposed golf course development area is traversed by ranch fences and a
few jeep trails
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the project site as an eighteen 18 hole golf course It
is anticipated that the development will also include other site improvements such as a
clubhouse maintenance facilities support facilities and infrastructure These other
improvements were specifically excluded from the scope of this exploration
Based upon the topography and the preliminary grading plans provided for our
exploration it is anticipated that the golf course grading will involve cuts of up to forty 40
feet in depth and fills of up to ten 10 feet in height
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 5
This current exploration was limited to exploring the relatively shallow subsurface
conditions of the site for preliminary design of the project mass grading It is anticipated
that further exploration will be conducted for deep cuts and structural improvements
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the western flank of Mauna Loa which is the second
youngest of the five 5 shield volcanoes forming the island of was
built during the Pleistocene and Recent Epochs by basaltic lava flows from the Kau
Volcanic Series
In many areas the Kau lavas are mantled with a volcanic ash soil referred to as
Pahala Ash This ash soil when in situ moisture contents are high enough has relatively
low bearing and shear strength and exhibits thixotropic properties i e loses strength
when remolded
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site within the grading limits of the proposed golf
course were explored by the excavation of fourteen 14 shallow test pits by a John Deere
510C backhoe ioader
In general our exploration encountered a surface layer of Pahala Ash varying
between one 1 to three 3 feet in thickness of moist brown sandy silt with traces of
organics and weathered gravel Generally the gravel content increased with depth
Below this surface layer of brown sandy silt our exploration indicated in situ volcanic rock
formations consisting of basaltic lava and clinker to the maximum depths explored
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 6
The surface layer of sandy silt and gravel was encountered in the southern and
western portions of the site in the eastern and to a much greater extent in the northern
portions of the site the basaltic flows of the pahoehoe type were exposed at the existing
surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits during our exploration
However in the general vicinity of the proposed driving range and the upper portion of
the proposed golf course the surface soils were noted to be very wet There also
appeared to be some surface seepage
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and the test pit logs are presented in
Appendix A of this report
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analyses it is our opinion that development of the
proposed golf course is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view The
volcanic formation encountered at the project site should provide adequate bearing for
the proposed site improvements provided that our recommendations are followed
The surface soils encountered throughout portions of the site generally appear to
be suitable for use as topsoil or as a capping layer over rock fill consisting of excavated
volcanic rock formation Whenever possible the surface soil should be stockpiled for use
as topsoil rather than used as embankment fill material
As discussed above the surface soils at the site are Pahala Ash This type of soil
generally has low bearing and shear strength and exhibits thixotropic properties i e
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2725 00 Page 7
loses strength when remolded Therefore it is generally not considered suitable for
embankment fills more than a few feet in thickness Efforts should be made to construct
the majority of the embankment of the more competent excavated basalt and clinker
materials reduced to less than 12 inches in size
Our reconnaissance of the site indicates that there may be surface and
groundwater seepage in the upper easterly portion of the proposed golf course
development area This seepage was generally noted in the vicinity of Hole Nos 13
through 17 and in the proposed driving range area It may be necessary to install a
subsurface drainage system to intercept and divert this seepage It is anticipated that this
will be addressed in a subsequent exploration phase
Site Preparation
Prior to commencement of grading the areas to be graded should be cleared of
all vegetation and other deleterious materials Due to the potential for erosion of the
exposed surface soils clearing and grubbing should be scheduled as closely as possible
ahead of the grading work i e leaving the vegetation in place in given areas until shortly
before the grading in that area is to commence The resulting grub and spoil material
should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
It is our recommendation that whenever possible the surface ash soil as should not
be used as embankment fill It is more readily suited for use as landscaping The surface
ash soil may be either stripped and stockpiled for use as topsoil or thoroughly blended
into rockfill material as discussed below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 8
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm natural material and the
resulting depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in its vicinity
indicate that the site is most likely underlain by ledges of basalt at relatively shallow
depths
It is anticipated that some hard excavation and or ripping may be encountered in
localized cemented areas or when ledges are encountered Cemented clinker and ledge
basalt formation will most likely require some hard excavation such as heavy ripping
hoerams or blasting
Generally the excavation method should be left to the contractor Excavation by
ripping is dependent upon the jointing of the formation the configuration of the rippers
and the skill of the operator Blasting may be considered to expedite the excavation
process Generally consideration should be given to the economics of blasting to initially
reduce the size of the excavated rock material and to facilitate excavation as opposed to
extended periods of heavy ripping at high equipment rates However care should be
exercised to avoid overshooting which could disrupt the clinker or rock formation resulting
in a loss of strength
Earthwork Quantities
Based upon our previous experience in the vicinity of the project site it is
anticipated that the rock materials at the project site will experience a volume change
when excavated processed and used as fill material In general the net change will
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 9
depend on whether the material is loose clinker versus dense basalt the average particle
size of the material and the amount of void space in the fill
Discussions with contractors whom have worked with similar materials indicate that
volume change can range from a factor of about 0 75 for loose clinker to as high as 1 5
for massive dense basalt The increase would be the greatest when the excavation
encounters a significant amount of dense basalt and this material is used as boulder fill
Fill Placement
In general the excavated soil and rock materials when reduced to less than 12
inches in size may be considered to be suitable for use as fill material The on site
material if reduced to less than six 6 inches in size should be adequate as a source
of trench and structural backfill if needed Imported material if required should be free
of boulders organic debris or other deleterious materials and be non expansive
The excavated rock material may not contain sufficient fines to produce a
well graded material therefore the surface soil could be mixed into the rocky excavated
material to increase the percentage of fines and to create a better graded fill material
However whenever possible it is preferable to utilize the surface soil as topsoil
Generally embankment fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Trench and structural backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two 2 feet of
fills and backfills below road and structure subgrade level should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 10
Alternately large boulders or rock fragments could be utilized as rockfills or
boulder fills provided that they are not nested and that they are well tracked with a heavy
bulldozer to lock the rock fragments and bouldery materials into grain to grain contact
A choking layer at least eighteen 18 inches thick for yard areas and a minimum of
twenty four 24 inches thick for road or structural areas is used to cap the rockfill to
reduce the potential of loss of fines from the upper portion of the fill The choking layer
should consist of a relatively well graded select granular material with an average
maximum size of about six 6 inches and an absolute maximum size of twelve 12
inches Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in locations which would
conflict with future utility lines In general boulder or rock fragments larger than 24 inches
in size should be placed at depths greater that four 4 feet below finish subgrade level
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
excavated volcanic material Instead a testing program to determine the number of
passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project On the basis of this testing
program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate
compaction
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the underlying consolidated clinker
or basalt formation to lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The keys and
benches should not be based in the upper surface ash soil or in loose clinker
Subdrains
To reduce piping and seepage buildup subdrains should be placed in areas which
exhibit seepage prior to filling The actual locations of the subdrains should be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 11
determined in the field depending upon the site conditions encountered during the
grading procedures A typical subdrain detail is presented on Plate 4
During our reconnaissance of the project site it was noted that the surface soils
in the upper easterly portion of the site were very wet and that there was evidence that
there was groundwater seepage in this area Although groundwater was not encountered
in the test pits excavated for our field exploration it is our opinion that encountering
groundwater seepage during construction could impact the progress of work and that
continued seepage after construction could affect the performance of the golf course
Therefore we recommend that additional exploration be conducted to determine the
extent if any of the seepage and to provide information for the design of a subsurface
drainage system to intercept and divert seepage encountered
Slopes
Provided that the above recommended fill placement procedures and granular
materials are utilized design fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
are recommended Fill slopes should generally be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio
Water should be diverted away from the top and face of the slopes Seepage is
occasionally encountered through gravelly layers exposed in cut slopes For
small intermittent seepage the water may be diverted into swales or subdrains If large
seepage flows are encountered other methods such as horizontal drains may be
required
For cut slopes the following ratios are recommended

Any cuts or fills greater than 20 feet in height should have a bench at the midpoint
of the slope
It is recommended that cut slopes exposing surface soils be immediately protected
by planting or other means to reduce the potential of erosion of the exposed soils
Temporary Sediment Basins
Based upon the preliminary grading plans it appears that embankments are to be
constructed along downslope edges of the temporary sediment basins Sediment basin
embankment fills should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Gravel and rock from on site
excavations may be utilized as the embankment fill for these structures provided that the
materials are well graded and have sufficient fines to bind the particles of the compacted
mass together and that the individual particles do not exceed twelve inches dimension
Subdrains through the sediment embankments should be considered to minimize
the potential of the build up of excessive pore pressures in the embankment

Additional Exploration
This exploration was based upon a conceptual design and was performed to
provide preliminary design evaluations for the initial site grading design of the subject
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2725 00 Page 14
project As such the locations of the test pits may not coincide with the final location of
the actual grading of the proposed project and the analyses used to formulate the
findings and preliminary recommendations presented herein may not be sufficient or
proper for the final project design In addition the depth of our current exploration was
limited by the method used for the exploration and no data is available pertaining to the
subsurface conditions at greater depths
Therefore it is recommended that additional exploration and geotechnical
engineering analyses will be performed when the design details for the subject project are
available Of particular interest in such additional exploration work would be refinement
of the grading recommendations the depth to the various bearing strata and development
of design recommendations for future structural improvements such as a clubhouse and
maintenance facility and construction criteria
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the excavation and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifica
tions or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
GEOLABS HAWAIl

conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as test pits Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined by triangulation
and measurement from reference points shown on the Hokukano Ranch Golf Course
Erosion Control Plan Details provided by Belt Collins and Associates Inc The physical
location of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2725 00 Page 16
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions

Collins and Associates for specific application to the proposed Hokukano Ranch Golf
Course in Kealakekua Hawaii in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in
the design of the proposed project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data
or the proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor wishing to bid
on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the
interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific
exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
