REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the
island The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was
partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked
or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which
the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3364 00 D Page 5
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration indicates that the proposed project site is
underlain by near surface clayey fills over very stiff to hard silty clays and clayey silts
to the maximum depths drilled of about 30 to 31 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater was not encountered in the two borings at the time of our field
exploration Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory testing we
believe that the new tank structure may be supported on a 1 foot thick gravel or sand
bedding on very stiff residual soils
To remove the existing underground fuel storage tank and to construct the new
tank we envision that excavation on the order of about 8 to 10 feet below the ground
surface will be required Based on the very stiff soils encountered in our borings we
believe that an open cut may be used for the proposed excavation if construction space
GEOLABS HAWAII

space is available with a slope inclination ratio of 1H 1V or flatter If the construction
space is limited and or existing structures are nearby then shoring of the excavation
during construction may be needed The temporary shoring support should conform
to the requirements of local state and federal regulations
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundation site
grading excavation and backfill and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
presented in the following sections of this report
Tank Foundation
As previously mentioned based on the results of our exploration we believe that
the proposed new underground fuel storage tank may be supported on a minimum
1 foot thick gravel or sand bedding placed on the stiff residual soils encountered The
gravel layer should consist of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
similar material If a sand bedding is used the sand should be compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 If a gravel bedding
is used we recommend that the gravel be compacted with a vibratory plate prior to the
installation of the new tank
We estimate the total settlement of the tank structure to be on the order of less
than inch We believe that most of the settlements should occur during the
construction period as the excavation is backfilled
Site Grading
Details of site grading were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate excavation and backfill of up to about 8 to 10 feet relative to

the existing ground surface will be required for the project construction Site grading
operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important
that a representative from our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during
excavation are similar to those encountered in our exploration The following
recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site grading work
1 At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of off site
2 The on site soils generated from the excavation may be reused as a
source of fill and backfill material provided that they are free of particles
greater than 3 inches in largest dimension However if soft or wet soils
are encountered during the excavation they should not be reused as fill
or backfill material
3 Imported fill and or backfill material should consist of well graded granular
materials of less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or
more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition
it should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance
with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use

4 All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Fill placement should commence at the lowest
point and continue upward in level compacted lifts
Excavation
In order to replace the existing underground fuel storage tank with a new tank
we anticipate that excavations on the order of about 8 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface will be required for the project construction In general the method and
equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject
to practical limits and safety considerations
In general our field exploration encountered near surface fills over very stiff to
hard clayey and silty soils Therefore we anticipate that conventional excavation
techniques may be used for the proposed excavations However it should be noted
that boulders may be encountered in the residual and or saprolite soils within the
depths of the planned excavation If construction space is available it is our opinion
that open cuts may be used for the proposed excavations A slope inclination of 1H 1V
or flatter may be used for the temporary cut in the very stiff fills or residual saprolite
soils
If the open cut method is not practical for the proposed excavation we
recommend that a temporary shoring system be used to support the proposed

excavations The temporary shoring support should conform to the requirements of
local state and federal regulations Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment
traffic or sloping ground conditions adjacent to the excavations should be added to the
design earth pressures Stockpiling of excavated on site and or imported materials
within a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the
excavation should be avoided
Pavements
We envision that new flexible pavements would be needed for the proposed
project The new pavements should match the existing pavement sections or use the
following pavement section for preliminary design
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase Course 95 percent relative compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction The base and subbase course should consist
of crushed basalt aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site The recommended pavement section assumes that good
drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements Surface water ponding
should not be allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the project In general for
support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the approved on site soils Where encountered soft and or wet excavated soils should
not be used as trench backfill The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

strength of the in situ material Results of the direct shear test are presented on
Plate B 3
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade
support Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 4
