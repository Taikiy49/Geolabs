REPORT SUMMARY
During the past six month monitoring period a period of anomalously heavy and
prolonged rainfall was experienced that clearly affected the earth movements at the project
site Most inclinometers recorded an increase in earth movements following the
month long rainfall events The Hydronet rain gauge at the Palolo Fire Station recorded
18 9 inches of rain during the month of March 2006 More typical rainfall levels were
experienced prior to and following the March 2006 rainfall events As a result of the
sudden spike in earth movements significant ground surface deformations and related
pavement damage was experienced within a portion of Area A at Kuahea Street
Based on our review of the inclinometer records during the past six month
monitoring period it appears that earth movements recorded within the
Waiomao Landslide also experienced a spike of less severe moderate movement
following the heavy rainfall events which occurred in March 2006 Earth movements
appear to have resumed at generally steady slight to moderate levels which are typical of
the on going earth movement within the Waiomao Landslide
Within Area A at Kuahea Street recorded earth movements experienced a spike
of high to very high movement related to the March 2006 rainfall events Earth movements
have since substantially decreased to only slight levels following the period of accelerated
movement As a result of the accelerated earth movement significant pavement
deformation consisting of concrete pavement settlement and heave and buckled road
curbing was observed In addition cracks and separations in some private driveways and
walls were observed Localized failures of some CRM retaining walls on private property
also were observed
The ground surface deformation of the paved areas along the head of the Waiomao
Landslide at Kuahea Street and within a portion of Area A at Kuahea Street is
pronounced and continues to compound with the passage of time The existing open
stress cracks separations and buckled pavements along Kuahea Street should be
repaired and sealed at the surface to reduce the amount of surface water infiltration into
the roadway subgrade
Other inclinometers at the lower western flank of the Waiomao Landslide within
Area B recorded generally steady slight to moderate earth movements with small
increases detected following the March 2006 rainfall events Observed ground surface
deformation within Waiomao Road at the western flank of the Waiomao Landslide does
not appear to have increased as a result of the extreme rainfall and elevated movements
recorded at the other upslope regions of the project site

Based on our review of the inclinometer data recorded during the past monitoring
period Inclinometer Nos 5 and I 13 at Kuahea Street continue to clearly indicate the
presence of appreciable subsurface earth movement within Area A of the project site

ranged between relatively slight and very high rates of movement A spike of relatively
high to very high movement accompanied by significant ground surface deformation

only slight levels of movement which is generally less than the typical moderate to high
levels experienced preceding the March 2006 spike

Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the sixteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in drilled borings at the project site A list of the piezometer
installations for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of
Instrument Installations The sixteen piezometers are installed in the borings identified as
Boring Nos P 1 through P 8 as shown on the Instrument Location Plan Plate 2
Three piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8 consist of dedicated vibrating
wire piezometer installations that provide a near continuous record of the water level
encountered in the well casing The remaining 13 piezometers consist of simple standpipe
well casings which are monitored manually on a bi monthly basis
