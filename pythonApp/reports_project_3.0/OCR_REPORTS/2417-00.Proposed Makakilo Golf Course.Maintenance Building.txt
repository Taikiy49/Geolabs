INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Makakilo Golf Course Maintenance Building The project site
is located on the eastern slopes of Puu Makakilo in the Ewa District of leeward Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for grading foundation and pavement
design input and may not be appropriate for final construction cost estimation or for
construction contract bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new Maintenance Building to serve the Makakilo Golf
Course development It is our understanding that the proposed maintenance facility will
consist of the following
1 A one 1 story prefabricated metal building based on a concrete
slab on grade The proposed finish floor elevation is at 468 feet above
Mean Sea Level MSL The proposed Maintenance Building will be
constructed on existing sloping terrain with existing ground elevations
ranging from about Elevations of 459 to 471 feet above MSL Overall site
grading involves cuts and fills of about 16 feet
2 A parking area located on the eastern side of the Maintenance Building

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs
Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of the Waianae shield along the
southern rift zone
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events that produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into a moderately strong rock known as tuff
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows which cooled to
form very dense basalt formations interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally
Puu Makakilo is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt
The units are discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 00 Page 6
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles most of the site This residual soil grades rapidly to
the underlying volcanic materials

exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and geotechnical engineering analyses it is our
opinion that development of the proposed golf course maintenance facility is feasible from
a geotechnical engineering point of view provided that the recommendations in this report

are followed The surface soils cinder and basalt formations encountered at the site
should provide adequate foundation support for the proposed Golf Course Maintenance

Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Grading
Grading for this project requires site preparation installation of subdrains
construction of embankments about sixteen 16 feet high and excavation of a cut bank
also about sixteen 16 feet high both measured vertically from toe to top of bank These
items are discussed in the following paragraphs
Site Preparation
ee
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting
grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Vegetative
material suitable for the purpose may be separated from the earthen materials for
chipping shredding and stockpiled for use in landscaping
Any soft or yielding soil conditions located below any areas designated to receive
fill which are encountered during clearing and grubbing operations should be
over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V which are to
receive embankment should be keyed and benched as shown on Plate 3 into firm
in situ material to lessen potential instability of the new fill The filling operations
GEOLABS HAWAI

should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the following recommendations for fill placement
Installation of Subdrains
Subdrains as shown on Plate 4 should be installed at existing natural
drainageways and at locations where seepage is encountered during site grading
Subdrains should be installed prior to fill placement in order to greatly reduce the
potential of seepage accumulation and subsequent weakening of the new
embankment fill materials
Embankment Materials
In general the excavated on site soils and rock materials such as basalt and
welded cinder processed if necessary to less than twelve 12 inches in size may
be suitable as fill material The excavated granular material if reduced to less than
three 3 inches in maximum size should be adequate as a source of trench and
structural backfill
The excavated granular materials resulting from the golf course mass grading
should provide a suitable source of fill rnaterial Fills on existing sloping terrain
should be properly keyed and benched to bond the new fills to the existing slopes
The stockpiled quarry waste fram the existing adjacent quarry should also be
suitable as fill and backfill material if properly processed

The cinder and basalt materials may not have sufficient fines to produce a well
graded fill material Therefore some mixing and handling of the excavated
materials may be required
Imported material if required should be non expansive with no particles greater
than three 3 inches in size and no more than fifteen 15 percent passing the
200 sieve If off site borrow is required it should be tested by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine its suitability for the purpose intended prior to
its delivery to the site
Embankment Construction
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fil
material will be necessary It is also advisable to institute a dust control program
during grading operations
Provided that granular materials 12 inch minus are utilized design fill slopes of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are recommended Fill slopes
should be constructed by overfilling and subsequent cutting to final grades in order
to secure a stable fill slope Slope planting should be provided to reduce potential
erosion

Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in its vicinity
indicate that the site is most likely underlain by a surface layer of residual soil or
fill material over dense cinder and ledges of dense basalt at relatively shallow
depths
It is anticipated that the residual soil and most of the cinder materials may be
excavated with bulldozers Some hard excavation and or ripping may be found
to be required in localized well cemented areas or when ledges of dense basalt are
encountered
The underlying basalt formations may require some hard excavation such as heavy
ripping hoerams or possibly blasting
For cut slopes the following maximum slope ratios are recommended
a Residual Soil 2H 1V
b Cinder
C Dense Basalt 1H 1V
Water should be diverted away from top of the slopes to minimize erosion and
instability of the slope As required for all slopes slope planting should be utilized
to reduce potential erosion
Installation of Utility Lines
It is anticipated that underground utilities will be installed as part of the
development work Based on the results of our field exploration excavation for utility line
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 00 Page 11
trenching should pose no untoward difficulty However dense basalt ledges may be
encountered at shallow depths which may require hard excavation and or hoeram
digging
Proper shoring is required for all trenches particularly if areas of loose soil or cinder
are encountered Shoring design and installation conforming to the appropriate health
and safety requirements should be made the responsibility of the contractor
A minimum 4 inch thick bedding layer consisting of 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended below the bottom of utility pipes To minimize the
compaction effort for trench backfill we recommend that the first lift from the bottom of
the pipe to 12 inches above the pipe barrel should consist of 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 or select on site sandy soil which does not contain particles greater
than one 1 inch in size
Trench backfills from 12 inches above the pipe to finish subgrade level should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Foundations
Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
compacted structural embankments or on the in situ dense cinder or rock formation
properly prepared A minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade in recommended The bearing value is for dead plus live
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 00 Page 12
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary loads winds and seismic are
considered
Structural embankments upon which foundations are to bear if loosened should
be recompacted to obtain a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If soft soil or loose cinder pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations they should be over excavated to firm dense in situ material and replaced
with granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill Utility
lines that must pass through structural footing concrete shall be installed in sleeves as
indicated on the preliminary drawings provided to us and as approved by the project
structural engineer
Provided the above recommendations are followed negligible footing settlement
is anticipated

It is anticipated that the concrete slab on grade will be supported partially on
compacted embankment and partially on excavated areas To reduce differential

The thickened edges of the concrete slab which form the column and wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and foundation construction phases
of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions may occur and

underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

determined from reference points shown on the Site Grading Plan undated prepared
by Belt Collins and Associates Inc and from field stake out points The physical
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
