This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kapolei Lofts Apartments site is west of Fort Barrette Road north of Kapolei
Parkway and south of Kamaaha Avenue in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project involves the development of approximately 16 acres A Project Location Map
and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The site development will consist of residential and club house structures The
residential structures will consist of constructing three story buildings with tuck under
garages A total of about 499 dwelling units will be constructed A one story club house
structure will also be constructed
A pedestrian mall parking areas and driveways are also planned In addition
underground utilities such as water sewer and drain lines will be installed
Based on the grading plans the existing ground surface elevations generally range
from about 55 to 64 feet Mean Sea Level MSL and finish grades will range from about
60 to 67 feet MSL Therefore fills up to about 6 feet will be needed to achieve the
design finished grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the mass grading construction at the project site

MASS GRADING
We observed the mass grading work for the Kapolei Lofts Apartments project from
to March 25 2015 The mass grading work consisted of clearing and
grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 6 feet thick to
achieve the finished grades Delta Construction Corporation performed the mass grading
work During the mass grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
Prior to fill placement the existing clayey subgrade soils were scarified and
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density In the roadway areas the fill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade
were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the mass grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 27 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass grading work for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references
