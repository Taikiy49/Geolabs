The following contains our proposed vibration monitoring work plan procedures, 
methods, and equipment to be used during the water tank construction, which is part of 
the Repair Administrative Spaces project. The project site is located at Aliamanu Military 
Reservation in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  
Project Considerations 
The project consists of constructing a water tank.  The anticipated subsurface 
condition at the water tank site generally consists of a relatively thin layer of silty soil 
material over medium hard to hard volcanic tuff formation. Based on the available 
grading plans for the water tank structure, deep cuts into the volcanic tuff will be 
required to achieve the finished grades.  Due to the presence of volcanic tuff, hard 
excavation condition is anticipated at the site.  Therefore, hoe-ramming and ripping with 
large, heavy equipment would likely be required to excavate into the volcanic tuff.  In 
addition, new rock slopes will be required to install rock anchors into volcanic tuff rock.   
We understand that an existing Heiau site is located to the southeast of the water 
tank site. Concerning potential excessive vibration generated from hard rock 
excavation, we understand that it is desired to monitor vibrations for the entire duration 
of the water tank construction in the vicinity of the Heiau site to ensure that the potential 
for damaging the Heiau is reduced.  Details of the vibration monitoring are provided in 
the subsequent sections. 
VIBRATION MONITORING 
Vibration monitoring will be conducted using Instantel Minimates and/or Micromates 
equipped with a 3-component geophone and a signal conditioner/recorder unit. The 
geophone senses ground motion along three mutually perpendicular (orthogonal) axes;

therefore, it provides detection of ground motion in the longitudinal, vertical, and 
transverse directions. The signal conditioner/recorder unit receives and processes these 
signals to provide a graphic record of the vibrations. Resultant peak particle velocities - 
peak vector sum (PPV PVS) in units of inches per second are automatically computed 
by vector summation of the motions along the orthogonal axes. The data is stored in the 
conditioner/recorder unit’s onboard memory. 
The vibration monitoring unit will be set at a scanning interval of 15 minutes and 
continuously monitor vibrations while activated. During the 15-minute scanning interval, 
the vibration monitoring unit samples the vibration data at a rate of 1,024 data points per 
second and stores the resultant peak level of vibration, which occurs during that 
15-minute interval. At the end of every scanning interval, a record is produced 
representing the highest vibration level observed. The magnitude of the highest 
vibration level observed, and its corresponding time are plotted, and a histogram is 
generated.  
We understand that the Heiau site is within a fenced area.  The Heiau generally 
consists of dry stack rocks estimated to be no more than 3 feet high.  Along the northern 
perimeter fence line is an existing asphaltic concrete access road.  The dry stack rocks are 
about 40 to 50 feet from the access road.  Based on our observations, the Heiau is located 
on sloping ground.  The existing ground surface within the fenced area slopes upward 
from the access road. Therefore, the dry stack rocks are several feet above the roadway 
elevations. 
We understand that access to the fenced area is prohibited.  Therefore, vibration 
monitoring units will not be installed within the fenced area.  We propose to utilize three 
vibration monitors (VM-1 through VM-3) placed along the northern perimeter fence of 
the Heiau site, as shown on the attached Site Plan, Plate 1. The specifications of the 
vibration monitoring units are shown on the attached Micromate and Minimate 
Specifications, Plates 2.1 through 2.4.  Each unit will be mounted on a steel post 
embedded in a weighted 5-gallon bucket with concrete.  Prior to the start of work, the 
vibration monitoring devices will be activated to measure the background vibration for a 
minimum of 24 hours as baseline readings. 
We understand that the Contractor may utilize the existing access road for 
construction equipment such as a concrete pump truck. The movement of construction 
equipment along the access road could generate some vibration. Since vibration 
amplitudes for a given energy source attenuate with increasing distance, we plan to place 
two additional vibration monitoring units along a line perpendicular to the access road to 
measure the peak particle velocities induced by the construction equipment in order to 
generate the vibration attenuation relationship between the source and the Heiau. The 
additional vibration monitoring units will be placed in line with VM-3 at distances of about 
40 and 90 feet from the edge of the access road.  These measurements will be conducted 
when the Contractor plans to mobilize construction equipment on the access road.

In general, vibration thresholds are based on the type of construction, age, and
general condition of structures. These thresholds are adopted from various studies of
actual damage thresholds observed for blasting operations and other vibration-inducing
sources. Based on the Transportation and Construction Vibration Guidance Manual
(September 2013) prepared by the California Department of Transportation, the
damage threshold for objects of historic interests or critical sites ranges between about
0.1 and 0.12 inches per second for sustained vibrations and about 0.3 inches per
second for transient vibrations (moving traffic). We recommend the vibration threshold
be set to a peak particle velocity of 0.1 inches per second for sustained vibrations and
0.3 inches per second for transient vibrations at the Heiau location. The attenuated
correlation from the pre-construction baseline measurement would provide the means to
adjust the site-specific vibration threshold to be used during the production construction.
A pre-alarm_ vibration monitoring value will be determined after the
pre-construction baseline measurement to be set to trigger an alert notification. In the
event the recorded vibrations reach or exceed the pre-alarm value, our field personnel
will immediately notify the General Contractor. Upon receiving a vibration monitoring
notification, the General Contractor can investigate the vibration source, inspect the
Heiau for any potential movement, and, if necessary, discuss methods to reduce the
vibrations. Samples of Vibration Histogram and Event Printouts are included on Plates
3.1 and 3.2 as examples.
