PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the project involves the construction of two new nine story mixed use
live work space buildings The two nine story structures will be located on the eastern and
western boundaries of the project site with an adjoining two level parking structure and
courtyard The project site is situated between Kawaihao and Waimanu Streets about
mid block between Cummins and Kamakee Streets The general project vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project we
recommended supporting the new buildings on a deep foundation system consisting of
18 inch diameter augered cast in place ACIP piles ranging from about 42 to 74 feet long
with allowable compressive load capacities of 200 to 500 kips 100 to 250 tons per pile

STATIC COMPRESSIVE PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the
ACIP pile foundation a static compressive pile load test was conducted on one dedicated
18 inch diameter ACIP pile designated as Test Pile No 1 The load test pile was installed
to a depth of about 74 feet below the existing ground surface by Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company HDCC using a Manitowoc 4100W drill rig equipped with automatic
monitoring equipment providing the drilling information such as drilling depth drilling rate
auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis
The test pile was drilled utilizing continuous flight augers As the augers were
removed the hole was grouted from the bottom upwards A truck mounted hydraulic
concrete pump was used to pump the grout through the continuous flight augers After
sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the top of the pile a
reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole A 36 inch diameter steel casing with a
length of 3 feet was then set around the top of the rebar cage extending about 12 inches
above the existing ground surface providing a stable platform for supporting the load
testing apparatus including the hydraulic jack load cell spherical bearing and steel
spacer plates The approximate location of the load test pile is shown on the ACIP Test
Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The load test pile installation record is presented on Plate 3
To provide reaction support for the load test two 18 inch diameter ACIP reaction
piles each with lengths of about 74 feet long were installed in a similar fashion as the test
pile with the exception of using 3 inch diameter center bars in lieu of rebar cages The
load test pile was completed on while the reaction piles were
completed on
To better understand and predict the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil
interface the load test pile was instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain
gages Model No 4200A 2 manufactured by Geokon Inc The strain gages were
connected using a multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 data acquisition system to record the
strains during the compressive load test The embedment strain gages were installed in
the load test pile at the following depths below the existing ground surface

The ACIP piling subcontractor HDCC provided load testing equipment including a
reaction beam hydraulic jack hydraulic pump reference beams and other accessories
Geolabs Inc provided the load cell digital dial gauges survey level equipment and a
spherical bearing The testing loads were measured by a Model 3000 500 ton capacity
load cell manufactured by Geokon
Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a multiplexer data logger
and data acquisition software program to collect the strain gage readings during the load
testing The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses and evaluation of the
static compressive load transfer Displacements at the top of the load test pile were
measured and recorded by the digital dial gauges
The pile load test was conducted in general accordance with the Quick Load Test
Method of ASTM D1143 and modified by our office to suit the design intents In general
the test pile was loaded to about 1 125 kips in approximately 62 5 kip load increments
Each load increment was held for at least 15 minutes or until the rate of the pile head
movement has generally stabilized In addition the static load test was held for
evaluate the effects of creep After reaching the maximum test load of 1 125 kips the load
test pile was unloaded in approximately nine decrements and held for at least 5 minutes at
each decrement The results of the compressive pile load test are summarized below

and 5 respectively Based on the shape of the load displacement curve and our
interpretation of the load transfer mechanism of the compressive load test it appears that
the 18 inch diameter ACIP load test pile underwent minimal creep displacement over a
2 hour period at 200 of the design load of about 1 000 kips In addition the 18 inch
diameter ACIP load test pile did not fail at the maximum compressive test load of about
1 125 kips The favorable test results confirmed our design parameters contained in the
above referenced report for the 18 inch diameter ACIP piles
