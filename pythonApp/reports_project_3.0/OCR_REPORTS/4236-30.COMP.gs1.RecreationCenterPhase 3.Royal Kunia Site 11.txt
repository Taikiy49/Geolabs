PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Royal Kunia Recreational Center project is within the Royal Kunia Residential
Development area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located near the
northern intersection of Anonui and Anoiki Streets as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The new development will include the construction of a one story recreation
center building and a parking lot extension In addition the installation of utilities will be
performed The site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills up to about 2 feet in
thickness The grading plan for the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation at the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Royal Kunia
Recreational Center project from to August 13 2010 The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills up to
about 2 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project
Bobby K Construction performed the site grading and utility backfilling work During the
site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and

backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the parking lot and roadway areas the
fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
Royal Kunia Recreational Center project observed and tested by Geolabs Inc in the
period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of

LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for alterations made to slopes or finished
grades at the project site subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
