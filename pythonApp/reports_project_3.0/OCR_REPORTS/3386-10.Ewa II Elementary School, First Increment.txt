This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services performed
during the site grading and roadway and utility construction at the above referenced project
from August 1995 through June 1996 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
August 24 1995
EARTHWORK
The subject site is located along Keaunui Drive adjacent to the Summer Hill
Subdivision of the Ewa by Gentry development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The project consists of construction of a new elementary school facility including
buildings driveways parking lots fire lanes and playground on an 8 acre vacant lot A
general layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1
The earthwork at the site consisted of clearing and grubbing placement and
compaction of engineered fills on the order of about 4 to 5 feet throughout the site and utility
trench excavation and backfill The earthwork construction was performed by Tower
Construction Company

At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were cleared and
grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials were removed and
disposed of off site
Following the clearing operations the exposed subgrades were scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches brought to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society
of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill materials used in the building areas generally consist of well graded basalt
sandy materials less than 1 inch in largest dimension structural fill In the roadway and
parking areas coral sand and gravel with silt was used as fill material up to 2 feet below the
finish subgrade Well graded basalt sandy materials were then used as fill materials within
the upper 2 feet of the finished grades in these areas
During the site grading operations samples of the imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill and backfill operations the fill and backfill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts of about 8 to 10 inches in loose thickness brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 The fill and backfill materials
within the upper 2 feet of the finish subgrade in the building pad roadway and parking lot
areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Periodic field density
tests were taken during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of 303 field density tests including retests of the failed areas were performed
using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and
D 3017 The results of the compaction tests taken during site grading are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 18 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuousiy observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was performed
without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of
the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans
and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
