In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story structure approximately 42 by 70 feet in plan dimensions
The general layout of the house foundation is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
W O 1450 00 Page 2
The house will be of wood frame construction with post and beam
foundation planned
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway lanai and
covered entry
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls generally less than 4
feet high are planned extending along the front portion of the house
perimeter to provide grade separation between the garage and the house
Minimal site grading generally less than 2 feet which consist mainly
of backfill behind the retaining walls is anticipated
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southwestern end of Naalii Street in
the Royal Summit Phase II Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad is situated along the front portion of the
Site The ground surface then slopes downward at about 5 1 H V to a
second level pad at the rear of the lot
A 5 foot wide drainage easement with a concrete lined drainage ditch
is situated along the back of the lower pad about 80 feet from the
road

A 10 foot wide sewer easement is situated along the eastern boundary
of the lot
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 86 to depths ranging from 6 5 to
11 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt underlain by highly weathered basalt to the full depth of our
borings 2 and 3 A review of the mass grading plan prepared by
Community Planning Inc dated for Royal Summit
Phase II indicated that Lot 86 was cut up to 4 feet below the natural
grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1450 00 Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to medium swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected
from drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete
placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral The on site soil under the garage
driveway and lanai areas should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch
minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
GEOLABS HAWAII
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
where the slope is steeper than 5 1 H V and should be keyed and
benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the garage driveway and lanai will be of
planned for the house
If the on site soils are capped with at least 12 inches of
non expansive select fill as recommended in the LOT GRADING
section then spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be
used on the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1450 00 Page 6
For post and wall footings located on the existing slope they should
be embedded further down below the slope face at least 2 5 feet or
sufficiently down enough to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to
the slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help
resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the required
confinement An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used
for footings on stiff on site soil All footing excavation should be
examined and approved by the soils engineer
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and lanai
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils are moderately
expansive afd unsuitable to provide direct slab support Therefore
the on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches
below the slab bottom first wetted and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened
edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade and bear on the above select borrow
backfill
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clayey silt
soils are recommended for the lanai slabs if constructed on grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1450 00 Page 7
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from
wall foundations if any
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally less than 4
feet high may be required at the front of the proposed residence to
provide grade separation The guidelines for the retaining wall
designs are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on a 2 1 H V steep sloping area the foundation
should be embedded down an additional amount to obtain a minimum
6 feet horizontal set back distance measured from the outer edge
of the footing to the slope face

3
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff
on site soil
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
Backfills behind the retaining wall should consist of granular
non expansive material such as select borrow crusher waste or
3B Fine gravel and extend a minimum horizontal distance of 5
feet behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1450 00 Page 9
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss
of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
