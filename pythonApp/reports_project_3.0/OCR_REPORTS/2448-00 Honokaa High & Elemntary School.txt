INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engi
neering exploration for the proposed automotive shop at the Hono
kaa High and Elementary School at Honokaa Hamakua on the island
of Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in
general accordance with our revised proposal dated March 1 1990
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of
the subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop
a generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechni
cal engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our revised
proposal dated March 1 1990 The scope of work consisted of the
following
1 The drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths
between 10 5 and 19 6 feet below the existing ground
surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered a surface layer approxi
mately five 5 to ten 10 foot thick consisting of medium
stiff to stiff clayey silt volcanic ash with sand and gravel
overlying hard vesicular basalt and clinker
Spread or continuous footings and slab on grade foundations
may be used to support the proposed automotive shop building In
order to reduce the potential differential settlement between the
portions of the concrete slabs bearing on ash and rock formation
we recommend that where encountered the rock formation below the
Slabs be over excavated a minimum of 2 feet and replaced with
select granular borrow material
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following
sections

Foundations
Based on the encountered soil rock conditions it is our
opinion that spread continuous footing foundations may be used to
support the proposed automotive shop An allowable bearing capac
ity of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the
in situ clayey silt with a minimum embedment of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade A higher allowable bearing
capacity of up to 6 000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on
in situ clinker or hard vesicular basalt However it would be
difficult to estimate the limits of where the rock formation may
be encountered
The bearing capacity value recommended is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary loads wind
and seismic are considered
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of
the future footing excavation it should be over excavated to
stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow com
pacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated
total and differential footing settlements under a column load of
up to 65 kips are less than one half of an inch
Slab On Grade
Comparison of the existing ground elevations with the pro
posed finished floor elevation of 1233 feet indicate that the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2448 00 Page 7
front northern portion of the building site will be cut about 2
feet while the rear southern portion will be cut about 10 feet
Based on the encountered soil rock conditions disclosed by our
field borings it appears that basalt formation may be encountered
in the rear portion of the building subgrade and volcanic ash in
the remaining areas Therefore in order to reduce the potential
Slab cracking due to differential settlement between the portions
of the concrete slabs bearing on ash and the portion bearing on
rock formation we recommend that where encountered the rock
formation below the slabs be over excavated a minimum of 2 feet
and replaced with compacted select granular borrow material
In addition we recommend a minimum 12 inch thick layer of
3B Fine ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion fill be provided below the
Slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
granular non expansive fill or in situ soil with a minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
Major cut is anticipated for the proposed project Com
parison of the existing ground elevations with the proposed fin
ished grades indicate cuts ranging from 1 to 10 feet will he
required
Prior to the commencement of earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material
should be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yield
ing areas disclosed at the proposed finished grades should be

over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavations should
be backfilled with well compacted engineering fill
Cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
are recommended Cut slopes into the dense clinker basalt forma
tion if encountered can be steepened to one horizontal to one
vertical 1H 1V
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to being used in the site
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch
thick loose level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and avoid ponding condition
Sewage Disposal System
It is our understanding that a septic tank and leachfield
system for the on site disposal of sanitary waste generated is
planned below the new parking area
Based on our field exploration Appendix A and percolation
test result Appendix C it appears that the proposed on site
sewage disposal is feasible provided that the absorption area of
the leachfield penetrates into the underlying clinker layer which
was encountered at depths of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface in Boring 1 which was located near the proposed
new parking area An absorption rate of 3 gallons per square
foot per day may be used to design the required absorption area of
the disposal system

responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four 4 borings to depths of 10 5 to 19 6 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rig was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our geologist or engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
Classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A summary
of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths Rock core samples
were obtained by using a rotary wash NQ3 core barrel The core
recovery REC and rock quality designation RQD are also
presented on the Boring Logs
