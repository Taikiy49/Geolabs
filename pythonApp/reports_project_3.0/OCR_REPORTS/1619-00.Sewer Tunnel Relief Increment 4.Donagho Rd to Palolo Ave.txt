The entire Hawaiian Archipelago fonns a southeast trending line of

along a 404 mile line at the southeasterly end of the chain oahu the
third largest of the eight major islands of the Hawaiian Archipelago

fonned by the volcanic activity of two large shield shaped volcanoes
The island of oahu is divided into four main areas Waianae Range
Koolau Range Schofield Plateau and Coastal Plains

The chief topographic features of the island of Oahu are the two elongated
basaltic domes
1 the Waianae Range on the west side and 2 the Koolau
Range on the east side The Schofield Plateau is located where the two
ranges merge The Coastal Plains adjacent to the ocean are nearly level
and lie on the ponded lavas of the Koolau Volcano The Honolulu Plain
extends easterly from Pearl Harbor and is mostly occupied by the City of
Honolulu
The Koolau Volcanic Shield was built during the late Pliocene and
early Pleistocene by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows fed by the dike
complex in a rift zone that roughly parallels the existing mountain
trends The main Koolau Shield building activity ended approximately 2 5
million years ago
Ihe project site is located within the Manoa Moiliili Kaimuki
District which lies on the southern flank of the Koolau Range that forms
the southeastern portion of Oahu Plates 1 and 2 Tne stratigraphic
history of the Manoa Moiliili Kaimuki area is quite complex due to a
series of sea level changes and the secondary volcanic activity that
superimposed on the topography of the Koolau Range
During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were many sea level
changes The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of reef
deposits at corresponding higher elevations The lower sea stands caused
streams to carve valleys through the sediments ai ld reef deposits v 1hen
the sea stood about 40 feet higher than the present level a long line of
sea cliffs were cut along the southern coast During this time gravel
terraces and fans were built at the base of the cliffs by the streams that
cut through these cliffs

APPENDIX
C
Groundwater level Monitoring Records and Rainfall Data
A total of eleven 11 single level piezometers were installed in
different boreholes to allow long te rm monitoring of groundwater levels
The piezometer generally consists of a three fourths of an inch
diameter PVC riser pipe with a bottom slotted section enclosed by
fiberglass screen Backfill around the slotted section consists of either
silica sand No 20 black manufactured sand or black sand and gravel
Above and generally below the slotted section bentonite seals were
applied
A graphical presentation of the piezometer details which includes
the depths and different types of backfill materials is shown on
Piezometer Details in Appendix A with the logs of borings
Periodic readings of the piezometric levels were made The readings
taken for all the piezometers and some uncased boreholes since the
beginning of 1986 are surmnarized on Plates C 1
Rainfall data for the current project were obtained from rain gauges
installed within close vicinity to the project These gauges include one
at the University of Hawaii Manoa campus one at Punchbowl Crater and one
at Tantalus
Average monthly precipitation from 1918 to 1983 and monthly total
precipitation from November 1985 to May 1986 were compiled and are
presented on Plate C 2 More detailed daily precipitation from November
1985 to May 1986 are shown on Plates C 3 through C 6 All the data were
obtained from the Division of Water and I and Development Department of
Land and Natural Resources State of Hawaii
All groundwater data recorded and discussed in this report were
obtained during a low rainfall period
