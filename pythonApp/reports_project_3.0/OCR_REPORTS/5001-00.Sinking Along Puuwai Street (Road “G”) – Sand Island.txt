In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration at Puuwai Street on Sand Island in an attempt to
evaluate the probable cause s and potential remedial measures pertaining to the reported
settlement of the pavements at localized areas of the project site The following summarizes
our field observations and findings during our site visit and field exploration and presents our
discussions and recommendations with respect to the observed settlement of the pavements
at the project site
Project Considerations
The project site is located along the east side of Puuwai Street on Sand Island in the
Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that localized areas of
the existing pavements have experienced appreciable settlement over the years Therefore it
is proposed to conduct an assessment of Puuwai Street to aid in determining the probable
cause s of settlement observed at the project site The assessment of the probable
causes s would be used to formulate geotechnical engineering recommendations to mitigate
the settlements along Puuwai Street
Based on the information provided appreciable settlement of the existing pavements
along Puuwai Street have occurred in front of Lot and 65 The pavement
settlement fronting Lot No 59 has occurred along the parking lane area and the pavement
settlement fronting Lot Nos 64 and 65 have occurred from the centerline of the roadway to
the edge of the parking lane We understand that the remedial construction work in the
subject areas consisted of overlaying these areas with a layer of asphaltic concrete to match
the adjacent existing pavement grades However we understand that the pavements are still
continuing to settle
Our initial review of the available in house soil and geologic information related to the
project site indicated that the project site would be underlain by fill materials extending to a
depth just above the groundwater table about 7 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface It
was expected that the fill materials would be underlain by soft and or loose lagoonal deposits

Piping of the Upper Fill Materials Due to Leakage of Sewer Effluent
Based on the available plans provided we understand that a 60 inch diameter ductile
iron sewer force main is located on the north side of Puuwai Street traversing in an east west
direction The top of the sewer force main is about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Because of the relatively old age of the sewer force main there is a possibility that the
ductile iron pipe may have corroded to a point where leakage of the sewer effluent has
occurred Because of the size of the pipe and the pipe being pressurized we have considered
the possibility that the leaking effluent may have eroded the fines piping of the surrounding
fill soils causing a loss of ground below the pavement structures However since the
settlement is occurring at the opposite south side of Puuwai Street we believe that the
settlements along Puuwai Street are not caused by the erosion of fines from the existing fill
materials

Consolidation of the Lagoonal Deposits Due to the Fill Loads
Our field exploration generally encountered fill materials about 6 to 8 5 feet in
thickness The fill materials were underlain by shallow marine and lagoonal deposits
consisting of medium dense silty sand and gravel with layers of soft clayey and sandy silts
The shallow marine and lagoonal deposits extended to a depth of about 33 feet below the
existing ground surface
Ground settlements would occur when the fills are placed over the underlying
shallow marine and lagoonal deposits at the project site causing the deposits to
consolidate However because of the granular nature of the shallow marine and lagoonal
deposits we believe that the shallow marine and lagoonal deposits would achieve 80 to 90
percent consolidation within a period of about 8 months after placement of the fills
Therefore we would expect that the consolidation of the shallow marine and lagoonal
deposits would have ceased years ago However we understand the settlement of the
pavements has been on going In addition we would anticipate that the existing
pavements should have settled more uniformly due to consolidated settlements however
settlement has occurred only in localized areas of the pavements Because of these
considerations we believe that settlement of the pavements is not caused by consolidation
of the shallow marine and lagoonal deposits due to the existing fill loads
Consolidation of the Lagoonal Deposits Due to Groundwater Fluctuation
It is our understanding that Ocean Seafoods has a well to pump out groundwater
within their property Ocean Seafoods is located at Lot No 67 on the south side of Puuwai
Street According to a representative from Ocean Seafoods the well extends to a depth of
about 60 to 80 feet below the existing ground surface We understand that the well is not
operated continuously and is used only when water is needed
Significant changes in the level of the groundwater table can cause consolidation of
the underlying compressible shallow marine and lagoonal deposits at the project site
When drawdown of the groundwater table occurs the unit weight of the soils located
between the original groundwater table and the lowered groundwater table increases from
the effective unit weight buoyant weight to a total unit weight weight of soil and water
The increase in unit weight of the soils above the lowered groundwater table imposes
additional load on the compressible deposits below the groundwater table causing the
compressible deposits to consolidate
If ground settlements were caused by drawdown of the groundwater table then we
would anticipate areal ground settlements concentrated around the area of the well In
addition we would anticipate that the settlement near the well would be much greater than
the settlement away from the well Also because of the granular nature of the small
compressible deposits we would anticipate that the drawdown level would be small at the
Because of these considerations we believe that settlement of the pavements is not

Condition of the Existing Fill Materials
Based on our field exploration we believe that the pavement settlements are due to
the loss of ground between the pavement structure and the near surface silty sand fills
Based on our experience the use of debris as fill material will increase the potential for the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass of fill We believe that the voids were probably
present in the organic and construction debris fill layer after compaction of these materials
during the site grading at the project site The surrounding silty sand fills may have migrated
into the voids that may have been present in the debris fills As a result loss of ground
between the pavement structure and the silty sand fills has occurred at the project site
In addition to the voids that were likely present in the debris fills after the grading
operations the decomposition of wood and paper debris may have likely caused voids
within the recently placed fill materials The silty sand fills above the voids that may have
occurred from the decomposition of wood and paper debris most likely migrated down into
these voids causing a loss of ground between the pavement structure and the near surface
silty sand fills Additionally some of the fills below about a 2 foot depth were in a loose
condition and susceptible to densification from heavy traffic
Remedial Recommendations
It is our opinion that the settlement of the asphaltic concrete pavements is the result
of loss of ground between the pavement structure and the near surface silty sand fills
caused by the migration of the silty sand fills into voids that may be present within the
debris fills There is a higher potential for the occurrence of voids in compacted fill
consisting of debris material such as concrete fragments metal wood fibers and paper
The following alternatives to mitigate the settlements of the pavements along Puuwai
Street was evaluated
Remove and replace the existing pavement structure and fills at the areas
where settlement have been observed
Grouting the underlying fill materials

Removal and Replacement of Existing Fills
One method to reduce the long term settlements of the pavements along Puuwai
Street due to the presence of possible voids in the existing fills caused by significant amounts
of debris is to remove the existing pavement structure and over excavate the underlying silty
sand and organic and construction debris fill materials at the locations where settlements
have occurred The resulting over excavated locations should then be backfilled with
imported select granular fill or the excavated on site soils provided that the excavated on site
soils are processed so that they are free of vegetation and deleterious materials
The disadvantages of this method are the relatively high costs due to significant
excavation shoring and earthwork potential for encountering underground utilities and
potential for the new pavements to settle due to consolidation of the shallow marine and
lagoonal deposits caused by the difference in the unit weight of the lighter excavated soil
materials and the heavier compacted backfill material
Grouting
There are several types of grouting that may be used to densify the subsoils either by
compaction or cementation Grouting generally consists of drilling holes to the desired
depths and injecting grout cement or chemical into the subsurface materials under
pressure
One grouting method that may be considered to fill voids at the project site is
compaction grouting Typically compaction grouting injects low slump about 1 inch low
mobility cement grout into loose granular soils to densify the granular soils However
compaction grouting can be used for void filling also A slightly higher slump of about 4
inches may be used for compaction grouting to fill voids The disadvantages of compaction
grouting are the relatively high costs the potential for damaging underground utilities and
because of the relatively thin overburden there is the potential for ground surface heave
Micro fine cement may be injected into the loose sands to penetrate and cement the
sand particles creating a stiffer material with more resistance to compression The
disadvantage of micro fine cement grouting is the potential difficulty in achieving complete
penetration of the loose sand due to the amount of fines present in the layer
Chemical grouts may also be injected into the existing fill materials in lieu of cement
grouts The Uretek Method uses a chemical grout to inject into the underlying subsurface
materials The Uretek Method consists of drilling holes and injecting high density
polyurethane foam below the pavement structure and into the underlying fill materials The
polyurethane foam will expand to fill voids that are present below the pavement structure
The polyurethane foam is allowed to cure to form a strong and stable material Based on our
sieve analysis tests the upper granular fill materials have between 8 to 17 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve Because of the amount of fines in the existing fills we believe that
it would be more practically feasible to use chemical grouts in lieu of cement grouts because
