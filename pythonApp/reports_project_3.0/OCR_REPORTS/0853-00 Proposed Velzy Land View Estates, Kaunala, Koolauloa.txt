This report presents the results of our percolation testing
for the proposed subdivision development on Kamehameha Highway
Si
C3
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site and to evaluate the
in situ seepage characteristics for private sewage disposal
system design and construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in adjacent
to and southeast mauka of Kamehameha Highway The site is
heavily vegetated with shrubs and trees except for a dirt trail
through the center of the site cut for the purpose of this
investigation
and
William Magers
W
P 2
The site is bounded to the northeast and southwest by residential
developments similar in character to the proposed development
Bounding the site to northwest is Kamehameha Highway and to the
southeast is a lower flank of the Koolau Mountain Range
The lower makai half of the site is relatively flat sloping up
toward the east at a gradient of about 1 foot in 60 feet The
upper mauka half of the site is moderately steep sloping up
at a 15 gradient Surveyed elevations of the development por
tion of the site range from about 20 to 70 feet above sea level
FIELD EXPLORATION
Three test borings were drilled to a depth of 35 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1
The drilling was accomplished with 4 inch diameter continuous
flight augers a 3 inch diameter tricone bit or a BX diamond
coring bit as detailed on the Boring Logs Plates A 1l through
A 3 The drilling operation was monitored by our geologist
who also logged the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
pie et LUND
Based on the test results Plate 2 the percolation rates
are greatest where the test holes encountered basaltic rock
in the upper mauka half of the site It is estimated that
cesspools extending at least 5 feet into the hard rock for
mation will provide adequate percolation rates for private
cesspool sewage disposal systems From an analysis of the
Subsurface conditions it is estimated that Lots 5 through
14 inclusive are situated where basaltic rock may probably
be encountered less than 30 feet below the ground surface
The remaining lower lots have subsoil conditions that provide
Marginal percolation rates for Single cesspool design Based
on the present data it is our opinion that additional cesspools
Or alternate sewage disposal designs should be considered for
these lots
