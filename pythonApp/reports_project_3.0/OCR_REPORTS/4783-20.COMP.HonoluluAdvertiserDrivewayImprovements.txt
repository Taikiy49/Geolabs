SITE PREPARATION
The mass grading work for the project consisted of fills ranging from about 3 to
6 feet thick in order to achieve the design finished grades for the project The mass
grading operations and infrastructure installation was performed by Jayar Construction
Inc
Observations during our geotechnical engineering exploration indicated that
abundant shrinkage cracks and remnants of previously excavated and backfilled test pits
existed across the project site Shrinkage cracks are potentially detrimental since water
can readily infiltrate through the crack opening and soften the subsurface soils underneath
the pavement structural section thus affecting their performance In addition it was not
Clear whether the previously excavated test pits have been properly backfilled Therefore
special subgrade preparation procedures were followed during the site grading operations
for the project development
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades were proof rolled with a 10 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing
near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks The exposed
subgrades were proof rolled without moisture conditioning of the subgrade soils at the
pre existing moisture contents Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation
were backfilled with general fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The proof rolling operations were conducted under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades were scarified to a depth
of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Following the subgrade preparation stockpiled materials from the Marketplace at
Kapolei project were placed as general fill at the project site During placement of the
general fill the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor Select borrow and base course were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
of the maximum dry density

During the mass grading operations samples of the imported and stockpiled
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optinum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 116 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of this report
The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the roadway and
utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
