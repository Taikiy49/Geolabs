Based on our review of the geotechnical engineering report the field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions indicated in the original geotechnical engineering
report we recommend that drilled shaft foundations embedded in the stiff clayey and silty
soils be used to support the proposed lighting system We recommend that the drilled shafts
be embedded a minimum of 4 to 7 feet below the lowest adjacent finished grades for the
different types of light poles and different ground conditions Detailed discussion of these
items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following
sections of this letter report

Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend upon the contractor s method of
construction and construction procedures In our opinion the following conditions would
have a substantial effect on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft foundations
Hard Materials
Based on the original field exploration the proposed new light pole locations are
generally underlain by basalt rock lava formation near the existing ground surface
Due to the potential for variation in the depths to the rock surface hard materials in
the form of basalt rock may be anticipated at the site within the depths of the drilled
shaft foundations Therefore coring into potentially hard materials may be required
during the drilled shaft construction Appropriate drilling equipment and coring tools
should be utilized by the drilled shaft contractor to install the drilled shafts to the
depths and dimensions recommended herein
In the event that loose and or soft soils are encountered during drilled shaft
construction temporary casing may be required during the foundation construction
work in order to reduce the potential for caving in of the drilled holes Therefore we
recommend that temporary casing of the drilled shaft holes be included in the
contract documents
In addition care should be exercised during removal of the temporary casing to
reduce the potential for necking of the concrete shaft Therefore a minimum
5 foot head of concrete should be maintained above the bottom of the casing during
removal of the casing At this time it is not anticipated that the groundwater level
will be encountered within the depths of the drilled shafts However placement of
concrete by wet construction methods using a tremie pipe will be required where
groundwater is encountered in the drilled holes more than 1 foot of water above

Workmanship
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the contact
between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the shaft
holes and in placing concrete into the holes
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soil The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for segregation
of the aggregates from the concrete mix If pre cast concrete pole bases are used
the gap between the pole base and the surrounding soils should be backfilled with
concrete or lean concrete A representative from Geolabs should be present to
observe the installation of drilled shafts during construction to confirm the subsurface
conditions and embedment depths

We understand that new asphaltic concrete paved parking areas are planned along
20 Avenue adjacent to Waialae Elementary School In general we anticipate that the
vehicle loading for the pavements will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light
pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming that the
pavement subgrade will consist of the re compacted on site materials encountered near the
surface Based on the above assumptions and CBR test results conducted by Hawaii
Geotechnical Group Inc presented in the Soil Exploration Report dated

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the light pole
foundation construction and pavement subgrade preparation Other aspects of earthwork
construction should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite
suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The
recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations If the
actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
