INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kekaulike Revitalization Ewa Block project in the Chinatown
area of downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses of the data obtained from the field exploration and from the laboratory
testing of selected samples
The recommendations contained herein are intended for design input and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu
on the island of Oahu It is proposed to demolish the pre existing buildings and construct
a four story commercial and residential building complex on the site as a public housing
development
The proposed housing development would consist of an irregularly shaped
structure with an open courtyard at the center It is also anticipated that concrete
columns with masonry walls and concrete floor construction will be utilized for the
proposed building complex A basement for vehicular parking has been considered as
part of the proposed project development during the preliminary phase of our
geotechnical exploration work However we understand that the basement parking is not
GEOLABS HAWAII

included in the current project design Supporting facilities will include paved parking
areas walkways grade adjustment walls and site utilities
Based on the information provided to us by the structural engineer the maximum
column loads are estimated to be on the order of 330 kips for the proposed building
complex The finish grade of the proposed building will vary from approximately 7 5 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL near River Street to 11 5 feet MSL near Kekaulike Street

Groundwater Level
The groundwater levels in the borings were measured at different times during our
current field exploration and were encountered at depths of approximately 7 to 13 feet
below the existing ground surface These depths generally correspond to elevations of
approximately 4 7 to 5 7 feet MSL
It is Our experience that relatively low groundwater levels have been observed in
the vicinity of the project site and this could have been caused by subsurface pumping
in the neighboring areas For planning and design of the project we believe that the
groundwater level should be assumed to occur at an elevation of approximately 2 feet
MSL Contractors bidding on the project are urged to obtain additional information on

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our current field exploration indicated that the project site is generally underlain by
two distinctly different types of subsoils near the existing ground surface The
northeastern portion of the site is underlain by a hard coral surface crust while soft
lagoonal deposits are encountered at the southwestern portion of the project site
Because of the heavy structural loads anticipated for this project a shallow foundation
system used to support the entire proposed building complex would be subjected to
appreciable total and differential settlements
However it is our opinion that shallow foundations bearing on the hard coral
surface crust may be used to support the northeastern portion of the proposed building
complex In order to evaluate the feasibility of this shallow foundation option we have
recommended that additional subsurface exploration be conducted at the northeastern
part of the project site along Hotel Street to evaluate the lateral and vertical extent and
quality of this near surface coral deposit Based on our current understanding this option
is not under consideration following a cost analysis by the client As a result we
recommend that a deep foundation system consisting of friction and or end bearing piles
be used to support the entire proposed building complex Special attention should be
given to the installation procedure of the piles due to the presence of the near surface
coral deposit
We understand that structural fills will be required to backfill the archaeological
trenches and to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed building pad elevations
The amount of fills required vary significantly across the project site due to the
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2874 10 Page 9
archaeological excavation work performed Based on our rough estimates fills on the
order of two 2 to seven feet 7 may be required within the limits of the proposed
structure
Detailed geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design

report
Pile Foundations
Because of the soft subsoil conditions encountered at the southwestern portion of
the project site and the anticipated high column loads for the proposed building complex
we recommend that deep pile foundations deriving support from skin friction between the
pile shaft and the alluvial deposits and or end bearing on the underlying basalt formation
be used to support the proposed structure Based on the data obtained from our field
exploration and laboratory testing piles should be driven to a depth of approximately 70
feet below the bottom of pile caps to develop the recommended capacity for support of
the proposed structure
Vertical Pile Capacities
We recommend that 16 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with
an allowable compressive load capacity of 55 tons per pile be used to support the
proposed building structure The piles will derive support from the frictional
resistance between the pile shafts and the alluvial deposits and or end bearing on
the underlying basalt formation To develop the recommended pile capacity a pile
embedment length of approximately 70 feet below the bottom of pile caps will be
required A minimum pile spacing of three feet 3 center to center is
recommended to avoid reduction in pile capacity due to group action
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2874 10 Page 10
An allowable uplift capacity of 25 kips per pile may be used in design The
recommended pile capacities are for sustained loading conditions For transient
loads such as wind or seismic forces the compressive and uplift load capacities
may be increased by one third
The above pile lengths are for initial cost estimation purposes The actual
production pile lengths should be estimated after completion of a test pile program
Foundation Settlements
Pile settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than one inch 1 with
differential settlements between piles not exceeding about one half that amount
Settlements should be elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
Predrilling and Backfilling
Since our borings indicated that the northeastern portion intersection of Hotel and
Kekaulike Streets of the site is underlain by a near surface hard coral deposit it
is our opinion that predrilling will be required at these locations to facilitate the pile
driving operation in the erratic coral environment and help to maintain plumbness
of the piles Based on our current estimation the predrilling depths may vary from
approximately 30 feet at the northeastern portion of the site to approximately 15
feet near Hotel Street and to approximately five feet 5 near King Street
The predrilled holes should have a minimum diameter of 20 inches for the 16
inch octagonal piles The void space between the predrilled hole and the pile
should be backfilled with pea gravel prior to the pile driving operation Detailed
GEOLABS HAWAII

Pile Driving Criteria
Because of the potential for encountering resistant layers such as cemented sands
and cobbles in the alluvium the piles should be driven with a hammer capable of
delivering a rated energy of at least 50 000 foot pounds The hammer should be
equipped with an energy control level such that driving within the loose and soft
deposits may be performed at a lower energy level to reduce the development of
tensile stresses within the piles which could cause damage cracking to the piles
Because local variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the
bearing soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field
during driving This should be accomplished by using a driving resistance
procedure such as the Engineering News Record ENR formula or the more
sophisticated Wave Equation Analysis to evaluate pile bearing capacity The
driving criteria will be developed after the selection of the pile driving equipment for
production pile driving Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose sandy soil the
hammer stroke should be carefully controlled with reduced energy levels so
that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2874 10 Page 12
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be driven
This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy to the top of
the pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs Hawaii should review the pile
driving equipment proposed to evaluate if the equipment is capable of installing the piles
to the recommended capacity Due to possible variation in the length of the piles unit
prices should be obtained during bidding for add ons shorter piles additional welds if
used at the connection etc
Test Pile Program
We recommend that a test pile program be conducted to satisfy the following
objectives
1 To evaluate the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
2 To estimate production pile lengths and to further confirm delineate
potentiai areas requiring different production pile lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify it necessary the driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program consist of
a minimum of ten percent 10 of the total number of piles for the project Test piles
that meet the specified driving criteria may be incorporated into the actual foundation
system on approval by the engineer
When the final foundation plan is available Geolabs Hawaii should be consulted
for selection of the test pile locations The initial test piles should be casted at least ten
feet 10 longer than the estimated pile length of 70 feet In addition the test pile
GEOLABS HAWAII

program should provide flexibility to allow for addition or relocation of the test piles as
deemed necessary by Geolabs Hawaii to suit the conditions encountered during the test
pile driving
A number of splice cans should be made available at the site during the test pile
driving program Splice cans may be fabricated with 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least five feet 5 long
A minimum of one 1 pile load test should be included in the test pile program
The selected pile should be load tested to twice the allowable load capacity in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for
production pile driving The test pile program should be conducted under close and
near continuous field observation by Geolabs Hawaii to evaluate the pile bearing capacity
and to provide guidance should revised or additional procedures be required due to

During the test pile and production pile driving operations certain piles may be
retapped to evaluate the effect of soil set up freeze on pile capacity Geolabs Hawaii
should be consulted to evaluate the final pile bearing capacity and production pile lengths
based on the results of the test pile driving and the pile load test
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads imposed on foundations may be resisted by a combination of the
resistance of the pile soil system to deflect and passive resistance on the front side of pile
caps and grade beams
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2874 10 Page 14
The lateral load resisting capacity of the 16 inch octagonal pile is estimated to
be on the order of 12 kips per pile with a maximum induced moment of about 55 kips
feet This assumes a lateral deflection of inch at the top of the pile for a free head
condition For passive soil resistance an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth may be used
Slab On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the commercial areas at the ground
floor level of the proposed building structure Based on the proposed site grading
scheme the project site would be covered with a layer of compacted structural fill at the
anticipated finish subgrade elevations
We recommend that a minimum four inch 4 thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be installed below the slab to provide
uniform support for the slab
To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings
an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on
top of the gravel cushion layer One inch 1 of moist fine sand may be placed above
the plastic membrane to help concrete curing

Site Grading
Because of the presence of the deep trenches and excavated pits resulting from
the on going archaeological work special procedures should be observed and
implemented for the site preparation and grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the bottom of all trenches and pits should be scarified
to a depth of at least six inches 6 moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures Loose or soft spots encountered during the scarification process should
be removed and replaced with compacted non expansive material
Structural fill material needed to backfill the archaeological excavation and to raise
the ground surface to the proposed finish grades should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction It should consist of imported granular material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than three inches 3 in largest dimension The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than one percent 1 when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 Imported fill material should contain a maximum of 25 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of five
5 borings to depths ranging between 78 and 91 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
by truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary and core
drilling tools as appropriate The entire field exploration involved a total of approximately
431 5 lineal feet of drilling and sampling
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The boring logs are shown
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is rotary drilling method
which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation the rock material left in the hollow
core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of four inches 4 in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound materials is recovered from a 5 0
foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
