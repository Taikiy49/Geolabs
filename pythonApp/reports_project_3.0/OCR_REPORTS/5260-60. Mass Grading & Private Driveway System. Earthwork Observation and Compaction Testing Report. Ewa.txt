PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The llima at Leihano project is located at the western side of the intersection of
Fort Barrette Road and Kamaaha Avenue in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project will involve development of a 40 acre site that is divided into Phases 1 and 2 with each
phase approximately 20 acres in area Phase 1 of the project includes mass grading of the
southern half of the site stockpiling select granular fill material on a portion of the northern half
of the site and utility and roadway installation The location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 and on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 6
The mass grading consisted of cuts up to about 3 feet in depth and fills up to about
6 feet in height During the mass grading work approximately 72 600 cubic yards of import
borrow material consisting of select granular fill material was stockpiled in the northern half of
the project site within the Phase 2 area

MASS GRADING AND UTILITY AND ROADWAY INSTALLATION
The mass grading and utility and roadway installation work for the Ilima by Leihano
Phase 1 project was observed by Geolabs Inc from March 27 2013 to
The mass grading and utility and roadway installation consisted of clearing and grubbing and
placement and compaction of fills and backfills up to about 6 feet in depth to achieve the
design finished grades for the project Kiewit Infrastructure West Company performed the
mass grading and utility and roadway installation work During the mass grading and
backfilling operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the pavement areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
During the mass grading and backfilling operations periodic field density tests were
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 26 of this report These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at
the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
A future channel excavation area near Kalaeloa Harbor was used to generate borrow
materials consisting of select granular fill and general fill materials Select granular fill material
was stockpiled at the northern portion of the site
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s fill
and backfill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures during the above
grading operations
