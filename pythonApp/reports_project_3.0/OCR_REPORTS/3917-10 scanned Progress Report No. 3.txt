This third quarterly report is presented to summarize our progress on the Kuahea
Street Area Movement project located in Palolo Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report provides summary information
pertaining to the recorded earth movements and subsurface water levels detected to date
during the Phase III instrument monitoring effort
PHASE II FIELD EXPLORATION
The completed field exploration consisted of drilling 15 borings ranging in depths from
about 40 to 100 feet below the existing ground surface Of the 15 borings drilled
inclinometer casings identified as Boring Nos 1 through 8 were installed in eight of the
borings The inclinometer casing installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 92 feet
below the existing ground surface
Seven of the 15 borings were converted to nested piezometers identified as Boring
Nos P 1 through P 7 The nested piezometers consisted of screened pipe casings set to
monitor the potential for groundwater in discrete 10 to 15 foot length intervals in the
subsurface The piezometer installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 69 feet below
ground level
The attached Instrument Location Plan Plate 2 shows the locations as built of the
borings and instruments installed at the project site The official start date of the 12 month
instrument monitoring program commenced in February 2000 A few instrument readings
were taken beginning in October 1999 as the instruments were installed to provide some
baseline survey data

During the past quarterly monitoring period the water level in some of the standpipes
was raised and lowered in an effort to evaluate the recharge and discharge capacity of the
screened well points The on going evaluation is intended to monitor whether recharge or
discharge of water is freely occurring at the well point and whether the surrounding strata is
hydraulically connected with the well point The manipulation of the water level in the wells
was achieved by either adding or removing a known volume of water from the well and
monitoring the discharge or recharge of water within the well over time Based on the data
evaluated thus far it appears that the piezometer well points are in proper functioning order
Based on the water seepage observed in the standpipes during the second quarter
monitoring period vibrating wire piezometers were installed in Piezometer Nos P 2A and
P 4B to monitor the water level fluctuation in the subject standpipes
Based on our review of the near continuous head level data recorded for Piezometer
No P 2A the water level data show periodic upward spikes of about 1 to 5 inches that are
believed to be related to periods of rainfall The upward spikes slowly drop back to
preexisting levels about 5 feet below ground level over a period of several days to maintain
an overall generally constant head level in the standpipe
Based on our review of the near continuous head level data recorded for Piezometer
No P 4B the water level data show a relatively constant head level recorded at a depth of
about 45 feet below ground level Unlike the vibrating wire piezometer data recorded for
P 2A sudden upward spikes of the head level were not recorded probably as a result of the
greater depth below the ground surface of the well point The greater depth below the
ground surface is believed to buffer the effects of influxes of water due to periodic rainfall
events

Rainfall Summary
A review of the National Weather Service NWS rainfall data for the Palolo Valley
area between August and October 2000 was performed Based on our review of the
available data the rainfall recorded at the Palolo Valley gauge was generally average for the
Palolo Valley area The month of August received about 2 63 inches of rainfall The months
of September and October received about 1 69 inches and 1 89 inches of rainfall
respectively The rainfall was more frequent and briefly heavy as a result of the passage of
several tropical storm remnants and upper level low pressure systems in the vicinity of the
Hawaiian Islands Abnormally large rain producing storm systems did not affect the Palolo
Valley area during the last quarterly monitoring period
Pavement Structural Deformation
The conditions described in the first and second quarterly progress reports remain
essentially unchanged from the conditions observed during our recent site visits New crack
patterns or new ground surface disturbance was not observed over the past three months
The existing pavement cracks located along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide appear to be slowly widening In addition the pavement surfaces
located within the landslide footprint appear to be experiencing continued settlement and
scarp development
Subdrain Performance Seepage Condition
On November 17 a site visit was made to investigate the performance of the subdrain
located within the buttress at Kipona Place The storm drain manhole located at the end of
Kipona Place was opened and a steady light flow of water was observed draining into the
basin from an approximately 18 inch diameter discharge pipe The flow rate was estimated
at about 0 5 gallons per minute Based on our observation of the discharge it appears that
the subdrain is in a functioning condition
In response to a report of surface water seepage at 2336 Kipona Place a site visit
was made on November 17 to evaluate the reported condition Based on our observations
and discussion with the resident the surface water seepage has ceased since some water
line maintenance work was performed by the Board of Water Supply BWS The resident
reported that the BWS located and repaired several pipe perforations fronting the subject
address therefore it appears that a leaking water line may have been the cause of the
seepage water Signs of ground surface disturbance or structure deformation were not
observed at the site
The resident at 2336 Kipona Place also commented that it seemed to them and their
neighbors that the existing hillslope located across the street may be slowly advancing
toward the street since the slope grading was performed The resident reported that the
