INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have observed the testing of the proposed
seepage pit for the above referenced project The approximate location of the project site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed
in general accordance with our revised proposal dated March 6 1990
This report summarizes our findings for the installation of seepage pits at the
project site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our observation of the testing of the seepage pit was to provide
engineering and geohydrologic data for the preparation of an engineering report of the
installation of the seepage pit for the disposal of treated effluent
The scope of our work on the project included but was not limited to observation
of the percolation testing of the proposed seepage pit analyses of the percolation test
data and preparation of this report
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The ground surface elevation at the site at the time of percolation testing was
approximately 600 feet above Mean Sea Level
The log of the excavated seepage pit is presented in Appendix A Generally the
pit encountered surface soils consisting of volcanic ash and gravel overlying dense basalt
formation Below the dense basalt the pit penetrated gravelly material which appeared
to be basalt clinker to the completion depth of about twelve 12 feet below the existing
ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the excavated pit at the time of our testing
of the pit

PERMEABILITY TESTING
The pit was excavated by the sitework contractor as a rough pit approximately
eight and one half 8 5 feet square with a depth of about twelve 12 feet below the
existing ground surface When the pit had been excavated to the intended depth it was
tested by the injection of clear fresh water The excavated pit drained approximately 1900
gallons of clear fresh water in about fifteen 15 minutes This test indicates that the
formation penetrated by the seepage pit has extremely rapid permeability and should be
suitable for the disposal of effluent from the on site septic system
In summary the excavated pit drained approximately 1900 gallons of clear fresh

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as an excavated pit and percolation
testing Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until operation of the seepage pit If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were determined by others We
can not assume responsibility for the accuracy of the location and or elevation of the
actual pit
