7 Leihoku Elementary School is located off of Leihoku Street in the Waianae area on
the sland of Oahu Hawaii The general vicinity of the site is shown on the Project

15 years ago The building is a one story structure with masonry walls and concrete
slab on grade Cracks have developed in the concrete floor slab and some of the
masonry walls in one end of the building
Geolabs Inc conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration to evaluate the
probable causes and determine potential remedial measures for the distressed portion of
the building The findings of the exploration were presented in the report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Building C Structural Evaluations Repairs
dated Based on the results of the exploration it is believed that the
building distress is due to swelling of the expansive adobe clay soils under the southern
end of the building Several alternatives for the structural repairs were evaluated
including removal and replacement of the adobe clay soils chemical stabilization of the
expansive soils and structural slab system supported on continuous footings or
micro piles The system selected was the structural slab with micro piles

conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed micro pile remedial work on the
building Our scope of work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of as built drawings and available in house
geotechnical reports in the area
2 Mobilization and demobilization of trailer mounted drilling equipment and
two operators to and from the project site

the existing cafeteria floor
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
field engineer geologist from our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Analyses of field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed remedial work
7 Preparation of six copies of this report summarizing our work on the project

principal engineer from our firm
10 Review of plans and specifications for general conformance with our
geotechnical engineering recommendations
11 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Field Exploration
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of 26 5 and 61 5 feet below the
existing cafeteria floor surface The borings were drilled using a trailer mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Laboratory Testing
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density plasticity and strength characteristics
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the
field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on selected sample
of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distributions is provided on Plate 6
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength of the natural soil Graphic presentation of the
strength parameters is provided on Plate 7

Subsurface Conditions
Previously two borings were drilled within the distressed floor area and
encountered 2 5 to 4 5 feet of medium dense basalt gravel with sand and silt over very stiff
adobe clay to the maximum drilled depth of 10 feet The current exploration encountered
somewhat different subsurface conditions Boring No 101 was drilled within the
distressed concrete slab area and encountered about 3 5 feet of dense basalt gravel and
silty sand over very stiff adobe clay to about 19 feet Below this alternating layers of
coral detritus and alluvium consisting of clayey silt with cobbles were encountered to about
36 feet underlain by coral detritus to 61 5 feet the maximum depth drilled Boring No 102
was drilled within the remedial work area but north of the distressed slab and encountered
about 3 feet of basalt silty gravel over coral formation to 26 5 feet the depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors
Discussion and Recommendations
The selected method of repair consists of replacement of the concrete slab on
grade with a structural reinforced concrete slab supported by a grade beam micro pile
foundation system The reinforced concrete slab will be suspended about 6 inches above
the ground surface to allow future swelling of the underlying highly expansive soil In
general the micro pile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually less than
8 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcirig The micro pile foundation is
typically constructed by drilling a hole placing reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting
the hole Micro piles are desirable because they can be readily installed in access
restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and ground conditions In addition
installation of the micro piles generally causes minimal disturbance to adjacent structures
the adjacent soils and the environment
The results of the field exploration confirm that the structural slab with micro pile
system is a viable repair option for the building However variable subsurface conditions
should be anticipated during the installation of the micro piles The subsurface conditions
ranged from granular structural fill to very stiff adobe clay with cobbles to hard coral
formation with sandy zones
We estimate that a micro pile system with a minimum grout bulb diameter of
6 inches may be used to support the structural slab loads An allowable compressive load
capacity of 26 kips per micro pile is required The allowable compressive load capacity for
the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as wind or seismic forces We recommend that the micro piles extend a
minimum of 30 feet below the grade beam bottom If coral formation is encountered the
micro pile length may be reduced to 15 feet

Lateral Load Resistance
It should be noted that the micropile foundation system provides low lateral load
resistance due to the relatively small diameter of the micropile An ultimate lateral load of
600 pounds may be resisted by the lateral load capacity of the micro pile system Passive
pressure resistance against the vertical faces of the pile cap and grade beams may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth pcf above the groundwater level Additional resistance to lateral loads may be
provided by shear resistance between the sides of the pile caps and grade beams and the
adjacent soils A side shear resistance of up to 150 psf may be used for transient loads
such as wind or seismic forces only
Micropile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of the micropile foundations will result primarily from elastic
compression of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total
settlement of the pile supported foundations to be less than 0 5 inches with differential
settlements between columns supported on micropiles not exceeding about one half of the
total settlement We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should
occur as the loads are applied
Construction Considerations
Installation of the micropiles should be performed by a specialty subcontractor
experienced in the construction of a micropile foundation system Due to the specialized
nature of the micropile foundation construction observation of the micropile foundation
installation system and testing of the micropiles should be designated a Special
Inspection item Therefore observation of the micropile installation operations by
Geolabs Special Inspector is necessary to confirm our design assumptions and should
be designated a Special Inspection item in accordance with Section 1701 of the
Uniform Building Code 1997

Limitations
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Topographic Plan
transmitted by Park Engineering dated The approximate elevations of
field borings were estimated from the spot elevations shown on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As previously mentioned groundwater was encountered in our borings at the time of our
exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
