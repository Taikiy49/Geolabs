General
This report presents the results of our soils engineering
and geologic investigation for the proposed Kailua Effluent
Force Main from the Kailua Effluent Pump Station to the
Mokapu Ocean Outfall
The investigation consisted of subsurface borings geologic
reconnaissance laboratory testing and engineering analyses
as outlined in our proposal dated and in
general accordance with our contract
Project Description
The proposed 48 inch Effluent Force Main extends approximately
15 000 feet from the Kailua Sewage Treatment Plant Pump Station
to the Mokapu Ocean Outfall see Plate 1
In general the alignment proceeds east along a coral road
from station 0 00 at the pump station to approximately station
50 00 There it turns north along the same road which runs
through an area of loose beach and dune sand parallel to
Kailua Bay to approximately station 84400 At that point
the alignment turns northwest to north inland away from the
beach and crosses an area of loose sands and muds to approxi
mately station 113 00

a subsurface exploration program of 22 borings ranging in depth
from 10 5 to 19 feet

ing equipment with continuous flight hollow stem augers and a
NX core barrel The borings were continuously inspected by an
engineer or geologist from our firm The approximate boring
locations are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented
in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test 2 5 inch 0 D

Loads on Pipe
Loads on conduits due to gravity earthforces increase with
increasing trench width It is therefore advisable to keep
the trench width 13b to a minimum or employ sloping sides
or subditch see Plate 2
The unit weight of the backfill material and the coefficient
of friction between the backfill material and the trench
sides also directly influence the earth loads imposed on
the conduit The applicable unit weights and friction
parameters for the variation material in the proceeding
section III B or the maximum unit soil weight of 130pcf
and a K tan of 15 may be used for load calculations
Superimposed loads on the conduit due to concentrated or
distribute loads should also be considered Special
attention should be given in the area where heavy military
equipment will cross the alignment

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe
has a great effect on the load supporting capacity of the
pipe Pipe bedding the contact between the foundation and
the pipe is therefore an important design consideration
We recommend Class B Bedding which can be achieved by two
1
2
methods
_ Shaped Bottom with Tamped Backfill The bottom of the
trench excavation should be shaped to a cylindrical
surface with a radius 2 inches greater than the radius
to the outside of the pipe and sufficient to allow
60 percent of the width of the pipe barrel to be bedded
in fine granular fill placed in the shaped excavation
Carefully compacted backfill shall be placed at the sides
of the pipe to a thickness of at least 12 inches above
the top of the pipe

Construction of Sewer
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits Depth of
excavation in this operation is on the order of 12 feet
Coral LIMESTONE and beach rock encountered in most of the
borings will be difficult to excavate Hard ripping or special
equipment should be anticipated in some areas Light blasting
should be permitted provided it does not disturb adjacent
existing structures or utilities
The organic CLAYS and loose SANDS will also present excavation
problems Relatively flat trench slopes will be necessary
unless vertical walls with sheeting and bracing are utilized
_ Excavated material should not be stock pile within 10 feet
or a distance equal to one half the depth of excavation
which ever is greater from the edge of the trench
13
Sheeting and Bracing
Sheeting and bracing is recommended in the area where SAND
and soft Clay encountered to prevent cave in of the trench
wall and subsidence of area adjacent to the trench
Sheeting and bracing must comply with all applicable safety
requirements The design and safety of the sheeting and
bracing should be made the reponsibility of the contractor
since his representatives are at the construction site to
observe any changing conditions which might affect stability
The design of the support system should be based on the
Apparent Earth Pressure Diagram for the respective soils
shown on Plate 4 These values do not includes loads due
to surcharges
Dewatering
Ground water was encountered in most of the borings and is
approximately 0 to 6 feet above presently proposed invert
elvation The water level will probably fluctuate with sea
level as the alignment nears the ocean
Dewatering of the trench should be continuous for concrete
placement pipe laying and as long as necessary to ensure
a safe and satisfactory installation The method of dewater
ing should be left open to the contractor provided excavation
stability and satisfactory drawdown are maintained
14
Backfill
Backfill should be placed so that the pipe will not be
displaced or damaged Backfill material to a depth of
one foot over the crown of the pipe should consist of
selected free draining well graded material and be placed
by hand or suitable equipment and compacted to a density
consistent with design assumptions
The upper part should also be compacted to minimize sub
sidence 90 compaction is recommended The native materials
are suitable for backfilling provided that compressible
organic materials rubbish debris and other objectionable
material are first removed Rock greater than 12 inches
should not be permitted in the backfill
In areas to be paved the upper zone of backfill should be
constructed in a manner corresponding the design assumptions
for the subgrade
Before and during the backfilling operation precautions
should be taken to prevent floatation of pipelines due to
the entry of the large quantities of water into the trench
Settlements
Settlements of the pipe should be minimal provided good
construction practices are followed
Settlements of ground adjacent to trenches can be avoided
by preventing water ground losses due to oversteepened slopes

a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from

b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils

ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
