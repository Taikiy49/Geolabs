This report presents the results of our boring exploration
and percolation testing at the Kapaa Shopping Center on
Kauai
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site to evaluate the
in situ seepage characteristics for sewage seepage pit
design and construction The proposed shopping center
expansion project includes a wastewater treatment plant
and injection well disposal system A seepage pit disposal
system is required for a back up disposal system

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the mauka side of Kuhio
Highway in Kapaa Kauai The existing shopping center
consists of 3 separate buildings with a large parking
area The area chosen by the project Civil Engineer
Austin Tsutsumi Associates Inc was the parking
area situated in front of the existing Building B This
area is covered with asphaltic pavement and is relatively
level
The existing ground elevations in this area are about
9 feet
FIELD EXPLORATION
Three 3 4 inch diameter test borings were drilled to
depths ranging from 18 to 20 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
The drilling operations were monitored and logged by our
field engineer The boring logs are presented on Plates
A 1 through A 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed that the site is under
lain by fairly uniform fine to medium sized sand to the depth
of the borings
Groundwater levels were encountered in the borings at about
7 to 74 foot depths
For more detailed description of the soils encountered in
the borings refer to the boring logs in the appendix

PERCOLATION TESTING
Constant head percolation tests were performed in each of
the three borings
After completion of the bore hole drilling a 2 inch diameter
PVC casing was placed into the drilled hole The bottom 10
feet of the casing was perforated Gravel or sand packing
was used around the outside of the casing Water was then
introduced into the 2 inch casing and the change in water
level with time was measured
A constant head was developed in all three tests at inflow
yates of 6 2 to 8 gallon per minute
The percolation test data was presented in Plates C 1 through
C 3
CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The borings encountered fine to medium sands below the pro
posed seepage pit site indicating relatively good percola
tion properties The percolation tests performed in the
borings confirmed the relatively good percolation rates of
the subsoils
Based on the boring data laboratory test results and per
colation testing it is our opinion that the site is suitable
for the design of the seepage pit Based on the data obtained
it appears that two seepage pits approximately 6 feet dia
meter and 12 to 15 feet deep could be utilized to dispose
of the design flow of
We recommend that a full scale test on a seepage pit should
be made to confirm our preliminary recommendation
