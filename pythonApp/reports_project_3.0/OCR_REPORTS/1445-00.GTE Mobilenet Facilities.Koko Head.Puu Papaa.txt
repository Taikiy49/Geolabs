GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED GTE MOBILNET FACILITIES
AT KOKO HEAD PUU PAPAA AND WAIPAHU
ISLAND OF OAHU HAWAIT
In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above three referenced sites Our work
was done in general accordance with our proposal of July 6th
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the three sites to provide soil data for use in the
foundation design of the proposed antenna pole and building
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The GTE Mobilnet network for the Island of Oahu includes four cell
sites each serving a sector in the more densely populated regions of the
island With the exception of the cell located in downtown all three
others are considered in this project Their relative locations are as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Koko Head Facility
The facility will include the construction of a 500 square foot
masonry block building for equipment The building will be half buried
below the original ground level with the excavated material used to form
a sloping silhouette from the roof line out to the existing ground
level A wooden antenna pole approximately 33 feet high will be placed
on the downhill side of the building Minimal grading for this site is
anticipated The Koko Head cell site is shown on the Cell Location Map
Plate 2
Puu Papaa Facility
This facility will also include the construction of a 500 square
foot masonry block building which will fit on the inside of the access
road curve at approximately 450 feet above MSL The building consists of
structural slab and beam members supported by columns and spread footings
bearing on weathered rock formation
Site grading required will involve cuttings with slopes up to 20
feet in vertical height Three antenna poles 50 to 60 feet high will
be erected at the upper road level and adjacent to the existing Hawaiian
Telephone Company Antenna Tower The Puu Papaa cell site is shown on the
Cell Location Map Plate 3

Waipahu Facility
The Waipahu facility will be sited within a new condominium
warehouse unit purchased by GTE in the Waipahu Industrial Park A
cylindrical mono pole structure approximately 150 feet high will be
installed behind the warehouse location which measured at about 28 5 by
32 5 feet
Pile foundation will be used to support the proposed structure
The Waipahu cell site is shown on the Cell Location Map Plate 4
SITE GEOLOGY
Koko Head Site
This site is located on the southeastern tip of Oahu near the top
of a tuff cone Koko Head belonging to the Honolulu Volcanic Series
These volcanics were erupted and deposited on a much eroded mature
topography which had been carved from the Koolau Volcano that formed the
eastern portion and considerably more than one half of the Island of Oahu
Koko Head is complex mass with depressions that suggest several
separate vents It lies on the northeast southwest rift zone which
includes other vents along the rift line
Puu Papaa Site
This site is located on the eastern side of Oahu just south of
Mokapu Penninsula near the top of the ridgeline These volcanic rocks
are part of the Caldera Complex of the Koolau Volcanic Series The
present interpretation is that the lavas were accumulated as flows in the
caldera of the Koolau volcano
The rocks consist of vesicular basalt flows that have undergone
some hydrothermal alternation from being in the path of ascending gases
in the vent of the volcano The vesicles have been filled with secondary
mineral deposits from the gases

Waipahu Site
This site is located in a alluvial formation of Recent period
identified as a valley flat that abuts the lower southwest flank of the
Koolau Volcano This younger alluvium bay mud was formed during the
most recent period of natural sediment deposition reworked stream clays
and silts
Man made fills were placed since the development of the area
SITE DESCRIPTION
Koko Head Site
The site is near the center of the ridge and between the existing
Hawaiian Telephone Company facility and the Federal Aviation Agency s
VORTAC facility A paved road provides access to the site Volcanic
tuff locally called mudrock is exposed at the site No surface water
was detected
Puu_Papaa Site
An existing single lane concrete access road leads up to the
building and antenna sites The hill is very steep and densely overgrown
with koa haole brush No surface water was detected
Waipahu Site
The site is located at the back of the newly constructed warehouse
facility The area is flat and covered by fill material A 7 foot high
fence constructed on a 4 foot high wall separates the site from the GTE

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
A total of 5 borings were drilled at the three cell sites to depths
of 15 to 61 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 5 through 7
and the boring logs are presented in the appendix
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions encountered at each cell site are generally
summarized below
Koko Head
The site is covered by 7 to 8 feet of dry and very stiff brown
silty clayey soil originated from the weathering of volcanic tuff
Underneath the layer lies the very hard weathered volcanic tuff
which extends to the full depth of our boring
Puu Papaa Site
Both borings drilled at this site encountered uniform soil
condition which consists of one foot of silty gravel underlain by 3
to 4 feet of soft highly weathered basaltic rock Beneath the
weathered rock to the end of our borings lies the good quality
hard basaltic rock
Water was not encountered during our exploration

Waipahu Site
In general the site is covered by 6 to 8 feet of stiff compacted
clayey silt fill material Underneath the fill lies about 21 feet
of soft and poorly graded mixture of clayey silt with sand and
gravel A thin layer of gravelly silt was encountered at about 35
feet from surface From there on stiff material was found down to
55 feet below ground where the soil began to turn medium stiff
Water table at the time of our exploration was found to vary
between 6 5 to 7 5 feet below ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the field exploration disclosed competent subsoil at
the proposed sites at Koko Head and Puu Papaa However a significantly
thick layer of soft material was encountered in the Waipahu cell site
which is anticipated to create some difficulties in the design of the
proposed foundation structure Recommendations for the individual
building foundation are presented below
Koko Head Site
Building Foundation
This site is underlain by stiff sandy silt originated from
weathered volcanic tuff
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
control building bearing on the stiff in situ tuff material
We recommend that spread footings and on grade slab be separated

A 4 inch cushion layer of compacted base course aggregate is
recommended under the slab on grade Also adequate waterproof ing
and sealing at the joints between the top of footing and the CMU
wall should be provided
Due to the anticipated light loading from the proposed building it
is our opinion that a bearing value of 3500 p s may be used for
the weathered volcanic tuff Minimal settlements are anticipated
for the proposed structure
Slope of 3 1 H V is recommended for the berm il1 which will form
the silhouette from the roof line of the control building out to
the existing ground level Excavated on site material can be used
as fill for the berm
Lateral earth pressure on the portion of the building located below
grade is anticipated to be small and can be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 30 p c f
To prevent pore water pressure building up against the below grade
structure we recommend that subdrains be constructed around the
bottom of the spread footing The subdrain should be designed to
drain daylight on to slope surface
Antenna Foundation
A wooden antenna pole approximately 33 feet high will be placed on
the downhill side of the building
The pole will be installed by extending into a pre drilled hole
which should penetrate to a sufficient depth into the mudrock The
hole should then be grouted in place to act as a caisson foundation
for the antenna pole A bearing capacity of up to 8000 p s f if
required may be used for the mudrock

Antenna Foundation
Three antenna poles 50 to 60 feet high will be erected at the
upper road level It is our understanding that the antenna poles
will probably be installed in pre drilled holes Relatively
competent bearing support can be developed provided that the space
between each pole and drilled hole is tightly backfilled with

Weathered Rock 700 tons cu ft
Hard Basaltic Rock 70 000 tons cu ft
Waipahu Site
A cylindrical mono pole structure approximately 150 feet high
will be installed directly behind the condominium warehouse unit It is
our understanding that the total weight of the pole and its supporting
structure is anticipated to be about 170 tons
Our boring encountered a 21 foot thick layer of soft alluvial
deposit at depths of 12 to 33 feet below the surface underlain by stiff
alluvium to 60 foot depth Since a hard layer suitable for end bearing
was not encountered friction pile foundation would be required at this
site to support the abovementioned large loading condition
Large moment due to lateral loadings will also alter the vertical
applied load on the foundation Maximum compressive load on one pile is
anticipated to be about 45 tons and maximum uplift is anticipated to be
about 15 tons

Based on preliminary design data the pile foundation system may
require a total of 16 piles with a 5 foot thick 20 foot square pile cap
embedded at a depth of 8 to 10 feet below the ground surface In
addition a 6 foot thick pedestal may be required on top of the pile cap
to accommodate a series of 10 foot long anchor bolts embedded within the
pedestal pile cap section
Pile Foundation
To develop friction resistance of the pile through the underlying
gravelly silt we recommend that inch octagonal concrete
piles be used To develop an individual design pile capacity of 45
tons per pile we recommend the pile be driven to about 70 feet
below the ground surface Uplift capacity per pile is estimated to
be about 22 tons These capacities may be increased by one third
when considering short duration wind or seismic loading
Capacity reduction for efficiency of pile groups should be
considered For piles spaced a minimum distance of 3 pile
diameter we recommend a reduction of 20 be used
Lateral Capacity
The lateral loading imposed on the foundation will be resisted by
the passive pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of
the foundation system For the laterally loaded pile analyses a
representative soil modulus of 50 tons sq ft can be used for the
top 33 feet of soil from the ground surface For soil below 33
feet a value of 100 tons sq ft is recommended Provided the
soil around the buried pile cap and pedestal is well compacted a
passive earth pressure equivalent to 300 p c f of fluid pressure
can be used

Settlement
Settlement of the pile group under the dead load of the structure
is estimated to be less than an inch Ground settlement may be
resulted due to densification of the soft layer during pile driving
Pile Driving
In order to minimize the amount of vibration induced to the
surrounding structures pre drilling through the upper fill crust
prior to pile driving is recommended The piles should be driven
with a hammer that develops not less than lbs of energy
per blow For pile groups the sequence of driving should be
outward from the center
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified
when the driving process is interrupted for more than 4 hours The
pile driving may be terminated when the total depth of embedment
reaches 70 feet or when the blow counts are steadily increasing
with depth and reach 10 blows per inch for the last 6 inches
Pile Cap Installation
For the installation of pile cap excavation is anticipated Due
to the limited space available at the site vertical excavation
should be supported by sheet pilings Contractors should be made
aware of the existing spread footing foundation and utilities
during the installation of the sheet pile and the excavation
Also because of high water table dewatering may be required
Since the pile cap will be constructed over soft material a
working platform consisting of 12 inches of 3B fine gravel may be
required

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that sheet piles pile and pile cap installations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate pile driving and or soil compaction have been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples

disturbed and undisturbed samples obtained from the Waipahu site to
further evaluate the soil strength characteristics The peak compressive
strength values for these tests are summarized on Plates B 3 and B 4
