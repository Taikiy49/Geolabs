In accordance with our proposal dated August 3 1983 we have made a
study of the near surface soil conditions at the above referenced site
in order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed CRM
retaining wall The study consisted of excavating two shallow test
pits performing laboratory testing and reviewing the preliminary CRM
wall foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary retaining wall plans the proposed CRM wall
will be from 3 to 22 feet high and 212 feet long in plan dimension
Fills and backfills up to 18 feet high are anticipated along the rear
of the wall

SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located on the northern end of Waihona Street in the Pearl
City Industrial Park
At the time of our field exploration the project site was located at
the toe of an existing H V slope and bounded by Waiawa Stream
to the north
Ground cover consisted of thick haole koa and plun trees with tall
grasses and weeds Several trash and debris piles were noted
throughout the site along the slope face
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Geology
The project site is located in the Waiawa Stream valley which has been
cut into the southwestern flank of the Koolau Mountains The bottom of
this valley had been filled with alluvium deposited by Waiawa Stream
In recent times portions of the valley have been graded and filled by
man

Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating and
sampling two 2 test pits at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 Description of the field exploration and
laboratory testing along with the test pit logs and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
The test pits encountered a layer of fill overlying the older stream
deposits This fill consisted generally of soft to stiff brown clayey
silt with subrounded cobbles and boulders grading to rounded at about
the river level
Many large 5 diameter massive and dense basalt boulders were
encountered both above and below the existing ground surface Also
trash and debris were observed in the slope face throughout the site
Groundwater was encountered in Test Pit No 1 at a depth of 5 0 feet
below the existing ground surface More detailed descriptions of the
soil conditions enountered are shown on the Log of Test Pits in the
Appendix of this report
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration and laboratory testing of the brown clayey silt
and boulders generally indicates that they can provide foundation
support for the proposed CRM wall foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1294 00 Page 4
The field exploration further indicates that dewatering and boulder
excavation may be necessary during the wall footing construction due
to the close proximity of Waiawa Stream in relation to the site
The wall footings should be embedded down a sufficient amount to obtain
an 8 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the outer
edge of the footing out to the Slope face see Plate 3 for details
This embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
The soil at the bottom of the footing excavation should be compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 If boulders are encountered at the bottom of the
excavation granular material such as select borrow should be used to
fill in the void spaces between boulders prior to compaction of the
trench bottom
Any loose or soft material encountered at the bottom of the excavation
should be removed and replaced with compacted granular material such as
select borrow
The existing loose overcast fill and trash should be removed prior to
backfilling Backfill should consist of well graded granular material
generally less than 6 inches in size The fills should be placed in
maximum 10 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

3 A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be

4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
5 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made
in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on
our site reconnaissance soil information from test pit data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as soft spots may occur in localized areas and may require additional
