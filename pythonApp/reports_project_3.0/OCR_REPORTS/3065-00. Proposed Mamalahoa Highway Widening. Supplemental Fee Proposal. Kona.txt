Project Considerations
The preliminary plans indicate that Mamalahoa Highway would be widened about
10 feet on the east side of the roadway Cut slopes up to about 14 feet high are planned
with slope inclinations as steep as about 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
Based on our discussions with Richard Wakida we understand that the
Department of Transportations would like a 10 foot wide debris catchment shoulder
added to the roadway However the Department of Transportation would be willing to
eliminate the debris catchment shoulder provided the geotechnical engineer for the
project can determine that rockfall from the slope is not a significant hazard

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of the proposed geotechnical reconnaissance would be to examine
conditions exposed along the existing Mamalahoa Highway cut slopes at the site and
develop findings and recommendations regarding rockfall hazards for the proposed
highway widening Our scope of services would include the following
1 Review of previous geologic and geotechnical maps and reports pertinent
to the site
2 Geologic reconnaissance of the road widening area by an engineering
geologist from our office Soil and bedrock conditions exposed along the
existing cut slopes would be characterized and the apparent past
performance of the existing cut slopes described
3 Preparation of a letter summarizing our findings and our conclusions
regarding potential rockfall hazards
4 Coordination and review by a Principal of our firm
