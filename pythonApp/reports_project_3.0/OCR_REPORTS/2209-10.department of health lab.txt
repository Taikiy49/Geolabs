INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our recent additional geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Department of Health Laboratory in the Pearl City
district on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Previously we conducted a preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration of the
project site and presented our findings and recommendations in our report dated
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2209 10 Page 2
March 20 1989 The building location and configuration had not been established at the
time the former exploration was performed Therefore our previous borings were located
to provide information regarding the overall subsurface conditions of the project site
The building location and configuration have now been established and it is
apparent that none of our previous borings were drilled within the building footprint The
major retaining walls planned for the structure necessitates determination of allowable
bearing capacities at the specific building location and therefore the additional exploration
was performed to verify or revise pertinent foundation design recommendations previously
provided
This report summarizes the findings of our additional field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on
data obtained during the field exploration and laboratory testing The recommendations
are intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
final construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new laboratory and office building for the Hawaii State
Department of Health in Pearl City on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is our understanding that the new laboratory facility will be a three 3 story
concrete structure The finish floor elevation of the first floor will be at elevation 643 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL Site grading involving major excavations as deep as about
30 feet and minor fills are anticipated for the construction of the proposed structure
Considerable vehicular parking and access roadways are also to be provided

The purpose of the present additional exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions within the proposed building footprint in order to develop a
generalized soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to foundation design of the project
The work was performed in accordance with our fee proposal dated

site and its vicinity

of the site conditions

existing ground surface

to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing foundation design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design recommenda
tions

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the existing Department of Health Waimano Training
Facility in the upper ridges of Pearl City on the island of Oahu In general the site has
a gentle topographic relief and slopes down in a southerly direction at about twelve 12
to seventeen 17 percent gradients with elevations ranging from approximately 725 to 610
feet above MSL
Currently about one third of the site is covered with grass and is used for the
grazing of livestock such as cattle and horses The remainder of the site is covered with
guava thickets
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The current exploration program consisted of drilling and saripling six 6 borings
the proposed building footprint The approximate locations of the previous and additional
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the current borings encountered uniform soil conditions consisting of
very stiff to hard brown clayey silt from the existing ground surface to the maximum
depths drilled The consistencies of the subsoils at the specific site of the structure are
somewhat stiffer than those encountered over the remainder of the site
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It appears that the very stiff to hard in situ clayey silt soil
encountered at the project site should provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed structure
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey silt soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition Therefore a minimum
six 6 inch thick layer of non expansive capping material is recommended underneath the
four 4 inch thick cushion fill below the concrete slabs on grade
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 4 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
in situ very stiff to hard clayey soil A minimum footing embedment of eighteen
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade is recommended Uplift wind seismic
or other loading conditions may require that this minimum footing embedment be
increased For sloping ground the footing embedment should be extended deeper to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 6
obtain a minimum eight 8 foot horizontal set back distance measured horizontally from
the toe of the footing to the face of the slope
The recommended bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
Footing settlements under design loadings and a bearing value of 4 000 p s f are
anticipated to be on the order of one 1 inch total and one half 14 inch differential
Our analysis indicates that if the previously recommended bearing capacity of
3 000 p s f is used to size the footings magnitudes of anticipated footing settlements will
be reduced by about 15 percent Therefore it is preferable to use the lower bearing
capacity of 3 000 p s f in footing design unless the footing foundations become
unreasonably large and require a higher bearing capacity of 4 000 p s f
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed building may be provided by
passive soil pressures and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to
estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings and the in situ soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 7
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce potential footing settlement which may be caused by consolidation
of the trench backfill
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the soil strength due to the swelling
characteristics of the soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed laboratory facility
A minimum four 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C33 No 67
cushion fill should be provided below the slabs In order to protect against moisture
infiltration a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slabs A minimum one
1 inch thick layer of moist fine sand on top of the plastic vapor barrier is recommended
to aid in curing of the concrete
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a minimum six 6 inch thick capping
cushion fill layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 8
The non expansive capping material should consist of non expansive imported
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral and should be compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The underlying clayey soil
should be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist condition prior
to placement of the capping material
The thickened edge of the slabs can bear on the compacted in situ soil The
thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade

Excavation Support
It is recommended that the excavation for the construction of retaining walls should
be supported by an adequately designed shoring system
The excavation method and excavation support system should be the choice of
the contractor and must comply with all applicable safety requirements The adequacy
and safety of the excavation support should be made exclusively the responsibility of the
contractor His representative should be continuously present on site to observe any
Changing conditions which might affect the excavation stability during construction
Site Grading
Site grading involving cuts and fills will be required for the proposed construction
At the on set of construction all areas within the grading limits should be cleared
of vegetation rubbish and other deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 11
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the scarifying operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill However
laboratory swell tests indicate that the on site soils have low to moderately expansion
potential Therefore the on site soils should not be used for fill within the uppermost foot
of the finish subgrade If additional imported material is required the material should be
non expansive select material less than three 3 inches in maximum dimension with less
than 25 passing the No 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index less than 20 The material
should be free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical should
be keyed and benched to bond the new fills to the existing subsoils This is graphically
illustrated on Plate 3 Sketch of Keying and Benching
Slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical or flatter may be used for the design
of both cut and fill slopes
GEOLABS HAWAII

The site should be graded to drain water away from building foundations and
pavement areas
Oxidation Ponds
It is our understanding that there are existing oxidation ponds located within the
project site The sludge should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Underlying saturated contaminated or loose material should also be removed to
expose stiff natural ground and the area backfilled with compacted fill material as
recommended in the above sections
Cesspools
There may be buried abandoned cesspools on the project site The available
county and state records should be researched to determine their locations Any
cesspool found on the site should be open cleaned of loose soft materials and
backfilled with granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
to two 2 feet below finished subgrade Above this level the cesspool excavation
should be backfilled with compacted fill as recommended above

subgrade soils encountered during construction and the above section may be revised

that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trenches
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the proposed project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum ten 10 inch thick horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as determined in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential
future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grades should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and
pavements to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that a gutter system be
installed and that the gutter run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations
and pavements as feasible Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
pavements should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 14
Planters next to the foundations and pavements should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and pavement
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
proposed construction since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and pavements
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be held responsible
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during foundation construction of the project This is
to observe compliance with the recommendations design concepts or specifications and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 10 Page 15
If subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from those
assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should
be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were staked out by ParEn Inc in
September 1991 The elevations of the borings are approximate having been determined
from the elevations and contours shown in the topographic survey map prepared by
Paren Inc dated March 22 1988 The physical location and elevation of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
