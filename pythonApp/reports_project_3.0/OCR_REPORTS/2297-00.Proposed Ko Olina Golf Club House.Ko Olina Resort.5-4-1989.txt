INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration for the design of foundations retaining walls
on grade slabs and pavements of the proposed Ko Olina Golf Club House and
related site improvements The project site is located in the Ko Olina
Resort development in the Ewa District of Leeward Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Ko Olina Resort development area
Previously during the mass grading efforts for the resort development
the site was partially graded by the placement of approximately four 4
feet of fill It is proposed to construct a club house facility at the
site to serve the Ko Olina Golf Course
Preliminary plans for the club house facility indicate that the
structure will be a one and two story structure of masonry and wood
construction It is anticipated that there will be extensive paved
parking and staging areas surrounding the club house It is anticipated
that retaining walls will be used for grade separation and lower
level basement walls
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project is to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of the final site grading foundations retaining walls on grade
slabs and pavements of the club house and related structures

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Ko Olina Resort development in the
Ewa District of Leeward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site was formerly used for the cultivation of sugar cane
During the recent mass grading work for Ko Olina Resort the area was
partially graded with the placement of approximately four 4 feet of fill
and is now covered with low grasses weeds and shrubs The fill material
appears to come from the on site excavation areas
In general the topography is generally level with the club house pad
being a lowlying area approximately three 3 feet below the upper area
where the parking and staging areas are to be constructed

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs The Ko Olina Resort development area is
situated on a broad coastal plain built on the southern flank of Waianae
This coastal plain is composed of interbedded terrestrial and marine
sediments including coral algal reefs
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2297 00 Page 5
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell in
response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
During the lower stands of the sea the land surface was eroded to
depths below the current sea level Alluvial and marine deposits were
laid down during higher stands of the sea at elevations above the current
base level Most of the project site is thinly mantled with alluvial clay
soils overlying coral algal reef material
Subsurface Conditions
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions at the project
site appear to consist primarily of very stiff reddish brown and dark
gray brown silty clays overlying dense coral sands and gravels These
clays are expansive and generally exhibit high shrinkage and swelling
potential when dried and wetted
Groundwater was not observed in the drilled borings at the time of our
exploration

Our exploration also indicates that most of the site is underlain by

Foundation Design
Based upon the soil conditions encountered in our field exploration
it is our opinion that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations
may be utilized to support the proposed structures The bottoms of
foundation excavations should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
below footing subgrade moisture conditioned to above optimm moisture
content and recompacted to about 90 of the maximum dry density determined
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft wet and or yielding areas
encountered at footing subgrade level should be over excavated to firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
non expansive material compacted in thin lifts to 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be utilized for the
design of club house foundations bearing on recompacted in situ soils
prepared as recommended above The allowable bearing capacity for
retaining walls may be increased to 3 000 p s f when the toe pressure is
considered A minimum footing embedment of 36 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade is recommended The above recommended bearing value is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Alternately the footing embedment may be reduced to twenty four 24
inches if the footings are underlain by a minimm of twelve 12 inches
of non expansive select material such as base course select borrow or
coral The select material should extend a minimum of one foot outside
the footing edges and should be backfilled in about 6 inch compacted lifts
at moisture contents near the optimum moisture content
The in situ clayey soil below the above recommended 12 inch select
borrow backfill should be scarified a minimum of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of maximum dry density per ASTM Test

Method D 1557 78 All footing subgrades should be kept moist prior to
placement of select borrow and or concrete to reduce the swelling
potential of the underlying on site clay soils
Based on our exploration and laboratory testing estimated foundation
settlements are on the order of one fourth of an inch total and
differential
A base friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
recompacted on site soils or the select borrow backfill In addition a
passive resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical
faces of the footings may be used to calculate resistance to lateral loads
if the backfill around the footings is compacted to 90 relative density
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the same depth as the bottom of the trench
to reduce the potential of footing distress resulting from the settlement
of the trench backfill If a continuous footing foundation crosses a
utility trench it should be designed to span the trench to reduce the
potential of foundation distress due to settlement of the backfill
For spread line footings located along the edge of the golf course
pond the footings should extend down further and bear either on the
underlying coral sand or obtain a minimum set back distance of fifteen
15 feet away measured horizontally from the outer edge of footing out to
the slope faces

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that slabs on grade will be utilized for the
lower floors of the club house and related structures
Slabs on grade including sidewalks or walkways should be
underlain by a minimm of three 3 feet of non expansive select
material that conforms to the requirements of Section 30 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Select Borrow
for Subbase Course The select material should be placed in 8 inch
lifts and compacted to between 85 and 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The underlying silty clay material
should be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted at about 3
above optimum moisture and kept moist prior to the placement of the
capping select borrow fill to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying clay soils The select material should extend a minimm of
two 2 feet outside and around the slab area
The above recommended 3 foot thick non expansive select borrow
capping fill under the sidewalks or walkways may be reduced to an
18 inch thick minimm capping layer if the in situ clay subsoils are
wetted and open graded aggregate gravel such as 3B Fine is used as
the 18 inch capping fill Prior to concrete placement the subgrade
clay soil should be saturated by thorough continuous sprinkling of
water for three 3 days through the 3B Fine aggregate
A minimum 4 inch thick slab with a 4 inch thick cushion of ASTM
C 67B 3B Fine gravel may be used A moisture barrier membrane
should be considered between the slab and cushion if it is desired to
protect interior floor coverings
All subgrades of all slabs should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement to reduce the potential drying and shrinkage of the subgrade
soils

Site Grading
Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of
vegetation debris boulders and other deleterious materials Topsoil
should be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill materials Any buried tree roots or stumps
encountered that are greater than three 3 inches in diameter should
be removed In areas designated to receive fill the prepared ground
surface should be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches
brought to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimm
of 85 and maximm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any soft or yielding areas encountered should be
overexcavated and backfilled with on site soil compacted to 85 to 90
maximum dry density
Since the project site is located in a low rainfall area the
on site subsoils generally have in place moisture contents below the
optimum moisture content Therefore some amount of moistening of the
on site soils may be required Generally the material should be
moisture conditioned to about 3 by dry weight above optimum to reduce
the swelling potential of the clay soils
General fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 10 inch
thick loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimm of 85 and
maximum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 For structural fills and trench backfills the lift
thickness should be reduced to a maximum of eight 8 inches
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is required
it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to determine if it is
suitable for its intended use Generally the material should be
non expansive and should not contain boulders organics or other
deleterious materials

Golf Course Water Feature
Although the adjacent golf course water feature is not a specific part
of the scope of work on the club house project the interrelationship
between the two dictates that we make the following comments regarding the
pond
Since a portion of the club house extends into or is very close to the
pond and the soils encountered are expansive soils which will lose
strength if saturated we recommend that an impermeable membrane such as
W O 2297 00 Page 13
Hypalon Gundline or similar be used to line the pond This liner should
be tied into the external wall of the Bridge Bar to reduce the potential
of seepage of water from the pond into the subgrade soils below the club
house
In addition the membrane should be placed over a cushion of
free draining granular material Drainage should be provided by the
installation of subdrains below the pond bottom
Based on our review of Lake Edge Detail for the Ko Olina Golf Course
water feature lake construction it is our understanding that a concrete
veneer had been installed along the lake s shoreline up to about 4 inches
above the proposed water surface elevations
If further clarification of these matters are needed we will be
pleased to provide additional consultation on the pond liner and subdrain
design
Infrastructure
The use of underground utility lines is anticipated for this project
Based on our exploration most of the trenching will be in stiff alluvial
soils and or coral Therefore it is our opinion that conventional
excavation and backfill techniques may be utilized
However due to the nature of the on site soils there is a
possibility that some areas of hard excavation such as well cemented
soils and or boulders may be encountered
The on site soils with proper moisture conditioning are generally
suitable for use as trench backfill material Since the project site is
located in a low rainfall area the on site subsoils generally have
in place moisture contents below the optimm moisture content Therefore
some amount of moistening of the excavated material may be required before
its use as backfill

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
To evaluate the swelling potential of the underlying soils below the
existing ground surface eight 8 one inch thick undisturbed and
remolded soil samples were air dried overnight prior to saturation under
a surcharge load of 55 P S F The test results are summarized on Plate
B 1
Two 2 Atterberg Limits were performed on selected samples of the
soils to aid in the classification of the materials The test results are
presented on Plate B 2
Two 2 California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed to
determine the strength and pavement support characteristics of the
near surface soils at the parking lot The test results are shown on
Plate B 3
