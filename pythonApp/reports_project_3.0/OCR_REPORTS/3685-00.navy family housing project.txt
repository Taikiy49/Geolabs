INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the 60 Unit Wholehouse Revitalization project located within Naval
Communications Area Master Station Eastern Pacific NCTAMS EASTPAC in the
Wahiawa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Naval Communications Area Master Station Eastern
Pacific NCTAMS EASTPAC in Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii We understand
that it is proposed to renovate and modify the 60 existing family housing units to conform
to design standards for military family housing
Based on the information provided the major work items of the proposed
revitalization project will consist of construction of new additions to the existing houses to
provide additional floor area construction of new carport structures and various interior
renovations for kitchen bathroom and closet In addition we understand that new
driveway and parking lots are also planned for the proposed housing revitalization project
Details pertaining to the proposed construction were not available at the time this
report was prepared However we anticipate that site grading of the proposed project will

involve minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet relative to the existing ground
surface in order to attain the design finish grades for the proposed construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct
while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of
the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 5
the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth The
saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within Naval Communications Area Master Station
Eastern Pacific NCTAMS EASTPAC in Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is bounded by existing buildings to the east and west by Center Street to the
north and by vacant land to the south as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on our
field observations and a review of the topographic survey map the site is relatively level
with existing ground surface elevations of approximately 1 174 to 1 180 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL The project site slopes down gently from the east to the west

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered stiff to very stiff silty clay and clayey silt
throughout the depths of the borings drilled at the subject site Based on the relatively
competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills may be used for
support of the proposed new carports and building additions The shallow foundation
footings Laboratory test results indicate that the near surface soils exhibit slight to
moderate swelling and shrinking potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore we recommend that slabs on grade be supported on at least 6 inches of select
granular fill material to reduce the shrink swell potential of the slight to moderately
expansive near surface soils Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow
for support of the proposed new carports and building additions An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills needed to achieve the
final design grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces In

general footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grades For the new additions adjacent to the existing houses new footings should
extend to the same elevation as the bottom of footings for the existing houses to reduce
the potential settlements due to surcharge effects The excavations for the new footings
adjacent to the existing houses should not extend below the existing house foundations
For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground the footings
should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of
8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face If foundations
are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded below a one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom
edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills as recommended herein to
be less than one inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material

Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations
located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used
for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead weight of the structure
and weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable Contribution to uplift
resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be estimated using a
unit weight of 110 p c f
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new
building additions The near surface soils encountered at the site exhibit slight to
moderate expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content
Therefore to reduce the potential of structural distress to lightly loaded slab on grade
construction resulting from swelling of the on site expansive soils we recommend that the
subgrade soils be properly prepared and a minimum of 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material be provided below slabs on grade The non expansive select fill
should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the limits of the structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 9
In general the subgrade to receive the non expansive select granular fill should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The 6 inch non expansive select granular fill recommended below the floor slabs
should consist of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or
cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
larger than 3inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory
California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or
less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain
between 10 to 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve All select granular fill
materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction The slab subgrade should be kept moist until covered by
the cushion layer base course or concrete slabs
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab above the 12 inch thick select
granular fill to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce future moisture
infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor
barrier is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand
should be placed above the vapor barrier to provide protection of the vapor barrier and to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 10
aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl
should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Where slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as garages and driveways
we recommend that a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction be provided below the slabs in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer
The 2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted for these slabs The base
course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of about 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base
course A minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any
unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
If exterior concrete walkways are required for the proposed project we
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase compacted to 95 percent relative compaction be provided below the concrete
walkway slab Contraction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundation
Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the preliminary plans we anticipate that site
grading work for the proposed construction will consist of minor cuts and fills on the order
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 11
of less than 2 feet relative to the existing ground surface Site grading operations should
be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative
from our office observe the site grading operation to evaluate whether undesirable
materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification process and whether
the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris resulting from demolition
operations rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The finish subgrade should be kept
moist until covered by cushion material base course and slabs If shrinkage cracks are
noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the subgrade soil be
moisture conditioned and recompacted to close all cracks In addition saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well
compacted engineered fill
Based on our field exploration the excavated on site soils may be reused as a
source of general fill material However due to the slight to moderate expansive nature
of the material the excavated on site clayey and silty soils should not be used as the
non expansive select granular fill recommended below the slabs The excavated clayey
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 12
and silty material may be used as general fill material up to the bottom of the
non expansive select granular fill layer
We envision that imported fill materials will be required for the non expansive
select granular fill material In general the imported material should consist of
non expansive material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill material should be tested
by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
In general the on site clayey and silty material to be used as fill should be
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The select granular fill materials should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The base course and subbase should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the project Our field
exploration disclosed that the site is generally underlain by stiff to very stiff clays and silts
to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the surface It is anticipated that the stiff clays and
silts encountered at the site may be excavated by normal earthmoving equipment
However the method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations All trench excavation
and construction works should be conducted in conformance with all applicable Federal
State and other safety requirements
In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be
used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical that the free draining material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support around the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optinum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3685 00 Page 15
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new structures should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations

APPENDIX A
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drilling rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Four ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the swelling
potential of the near surface soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate its liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the tests are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the near
surface soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plates B 4 and 5
