Test Pile Driving Report
Proposed Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant Modifications
Phase Ill
Kailua Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated we have observed the test pile
driving at the following seven 7 proposed structures for the Phase Ill construction of the subject
project
A Delmag D 30 23 diesel pile hammer was used to drive the test piles at the locations
shown on the attached pile location plans The piles were 15 inch square precast and
prestressed concrete piles which were cast to 45 and 75 feet length It is our understanding that
the pile hammer was set to fuel setting 2 during driving to deliver rated energy of between
approximately 40 000 and 50 000 foot pounds One copy of the test pile driving records are
attached for your reference
Test Pile Observations
Biotower No 1
A total of eight 8 test piles were driven to approximately 12 to 36 feet below the design
cut off elevations at this tank
Biotower No 2
A total of eight 8 test piles were driven to approximately 4 to 19 feet below the design
cut off elevations at this tank
Biotower Pump Station
A total of four 4 test piles were driven to approximately 4 to 20 feet below the design
cut off elevations at the pump station

Solids Contactor Reaeration Tanks

Secondary Clarifier No 3
A total of ten 10 test piles were driven to approximately 79 to 90 feet below the design

However it was observed that Test Pile No C3 23 could not be driven more than 46 5
feet below the existing ground elevation which was at approximately 42 5 feet below Mean
Sea Level It was also noted that Test Pile No C3 64 was broken during the test pile
driving We recommend that Test Pile No C3 64 be redriven during future production pile
driving program at Clarifier Tank No 3
Minimum Pile Embedment
High blow counts were recorded during the driving operation of the test piles at the two
2 Biotower Tanks It appears that short pile embedment will be resulted by using the specified
driving criteria of three 3 blows per inch for the tanks This potential problem of short pile
embedment has been addressed to and discussed with your office and the general contractor
Kiewit Pacific Company
It was indicated by the contractor that a minimum of five 5 feet embedment below pile
cut off is required in order to avoid loosening up the driven piles during subsequent pile cutting
lt was therefore concluded that the production piles at these tanks and possibly at the other
proposed structures may be driven to a maximum of 20 blows per inch if necessary in order
to achieve the minimum five 5 feet embedment requirement for construction purposes
Static Pile Load Tests
In accordance with the project specifications a static pile load test was performed on a
selected test pile driven at each of the seven 7 proposed structures The selected load test
piles were as follows

Per our telephone conversations the contract pile vendor
would be able to furnish the 15 inch square pile in 90 feet
length per piece It is also our understanding that the
existing ground at the clarifier sites will be excavated and
backfilled with a 2 foot thick gravel cushion to approximately
elevation 12 5 feet below Mean Sea Level MSL prior to the
production pile driving at the tank sites
Recognizing the potential cost savings by reducing the number
of splice cans needed we recommend that 90 foot long piles in
one length be used for the production pile driving at these
two clarifier tanks except in the 95 foot zones indicated
The piles should be driven from the presumed ground elevation
of 12 5 feet below MSL until the specified driving criteria
is reached
Based on the test pile driving data we recommend that 95 foot
long piles be used in the 95 foot zones indicated The longer
piles should be casted in two 2 separate sections of 45 and
50 feet lengths respectively and spliced together during pile
driving
To minimize the use of splice cans 90 foot long piles may
also be used in the 95 foot zones provided if when necessary
the contractor can chase the piles a few feet below the top of
the gravel cushion by means of a follower or chaser in order
to achieve the driving criteria The chased piles should be
built up to the required cut off elevations
However it should be recognized that if the chased piles
could not be built up due to construction difficulty and would
need to be spliced the piles would have to be evaluated on an
individual basis and may be rejected due to insufficient
length above the spliced connection Therefore if 90 foot
long piles are also used in the 95 foot zones indicated the
contractor would have to accept the above indicated risk
