This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services 
performed during the site grading and infrastructure installation work for the Department 
of Veterans Affairs Multi-Specialty Outpatient Clinic project at 91-1051 Franklin D. 
Roosevelt Avenue from February 2, 2022 through December 4, 2023. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located south of the intersection of Kamokila Boulevard and 
Franklin D. Roosevelt Avenue within the former Naval Air Station Barbers Point. The 
project consists of constructing an approximate 88,675-square foot, one-story building 
structure for a Department of Veterans Affairs Multi-Specialty Outpatient Clinic. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The project also includes constructing a large parking lot consisting of 528 
parking spaces and supporting infrastructure, such as underground water, sewer, 
drainage, and electrical utilities to serve the development. The layout of the new clinic 
development is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of up to about 
2 feet thick. The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Nan, Inc. was the General Contractor for the project and performed the site 
grading construction. The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, 
placement and compaction of fills and backfills, utility trench excavation and backfill, 
and pavement construction. 
Following the demolition and clearing, fill and backfill materials required for the 
project generally were placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 or 95 percent under the building slab and 
pavement areas, respectively, of the maximum dry density determined in accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized 
on Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations with variations in construction 
procedures and soil conditions were noted. A total of 520 field density tests were 
performed using the nuclear gauge method in accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear 
Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.23 of this report. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas 
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction 
was obtained. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading, utility line 
installation, and pavement construction for the above project observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with 
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations 
presented in the above-referenced geotechnical engineering report and subsequent 
recommendations.
