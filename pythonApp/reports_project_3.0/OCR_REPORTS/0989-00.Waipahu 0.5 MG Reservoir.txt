SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS

competent bearing soils at the reservoir site

be used on the stiff in situ soils

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
design and construction of the proposed reservoir structure
and adjoining control building
Our investigation was commenced in November of 1980 with the
drilling of one test boring in the then accessible area of
the reservoir site The remainder of the site was planted
in sugar cane at that time and therefore inaccessible to
drilling equipment Preliminary recommendations were issued
in our letter of
The sugar cane was harvested in mid August of 1981 and we
completed two additional test borings at the proposed reser
voir site

This report compiles the information obtained by the three
test borings composing our investigation and summarizes our
findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed reservoir site is located in the Waikele
District of Oahu approximately one mile north of the Wai
pahu Sugar Mill The reservoir site is situated in a
cleared portion of cultivated sugar cane land east of Wai
kele Stream Access to the site is by cane haul roads from
Waipahu The site slopes uniformly to the southeast at a 5
percent gradient Ground surface elevations range from 206
feet to 218 feet above sea level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development plans show a 0 5 M G capacity
reinforced concrete reservoir structure and adjoining
one story control building to be constructed on the site
The 0 5 M G tank structure will be about 18 feet high and
about 70 feet in diameter The anticipated column load is
120 kips and the perimeter wall load is about 9 0 kips per
foot
The site will have its own asphaltic concrete access roadway
to and around the reservoir structure
Site grading consists of cuts up to 8 feet No structural
fills are anticipated

Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled to depths of 19 0 to 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface
conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
The approximate locations of the test borings are shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 Detailed information
pertaining to the field exploration and laboratory testing
is presented in the Appendix along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results

Regional Geology
The project site is located on the southern slope of the
Schofield Plateau This plateau was built up by lavas ori
ginating from the Koolau Shield volcano The soils in this
area are typically residual derived from the in situ wea
thering of basic igneous rock
Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil
conditions at the site Based on the test borings there is
a surface layer of very stiff to hard reddish brown clayey
silt soils ranging from 10 5 to 13 0 feet in thickness
Below this surface layer the test borings encountered stiff
brown mottled silts retaining the Original rock structures
saprolite This material extended to the depths drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during
the ield exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration laboratory test results and
analysis it is our opinion that the site is suitable for
the proposed reservoir construction
Foundation
Spread or continuous footings may be used to support the
Structures All footings should bear on undisturbed very
stiff residual brown clayey silt soils An allowable
bearing value of 3500 p s f may be used for footings
resting on the very stiff residual soils The footings
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade The ground surface around the
footings should not be excavated or allowed to erode If the
footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend down to the same grade as the bottom of the utility
trench Surface water should be diverted away from the
foundations
Based on the test data and estimated loads minimal settle
ments are anticipated for the proposed structures

Lateral Resistance
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be utilized to deter
mine the lateral resistance of the structure and founda
tion Additionally a passive soil resistance of 250 p c f
may be used
Site Grading
The site should be cleared of all surface vegetation debris
and loose topsoil prior to the commencement of site grading
operations
Based on the site exploration and analyses a cut slope
cut slope be inspected by a soils engineer during construc
tion to verify if the actual slope materials are as antici
pated Some slope adjustment may be required in the field
if soft pockets or seepage is encountered during slope exca
vation
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and slope protection such as planting should be
utilized as soon as possible to reduce slope erosion
Roadway Pavements
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the near surface soils
at the site and light traffic conditions the following
preliminary pavement design is recommended for the access
roadways
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils at the time of the roadway construction and
the above design sections can be revised on the basis of the
additional tests if required
The roadway subgrades should be compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 and shaped to drain

Design Review
The plans for the site grading and reservoir foundations
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and speci
fications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the subgrade prepara
tion for the reservoir sites
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The rcommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 borings to depths of 19 0 to 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 The borings were performed by a truck moun
ted rotary hydraulic drill rig advancing 4 inch diameter
continuous flight augers
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examinations in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in our laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
either a standard split spoon sampler or a 3 inch O D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifica
tion and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 3
Three direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
soils The test results are plotted on Plates B 1 through
B 3
Two swell tests on one inch thick ring samples with
55 p s f surcharge load were conducted to get an indica
tion of the swelling potential of the near surface soils
One CBR swell test was performed on a near surface sample
to evaluate its subgrade strength for roadway pavement
analysis The results are presented on Plate B 4
