SITE PREPARATION
As mentioned previously the mass grading work consisted of cuts and fills on the
order of about 2 to 8 feet thick in order to achieve the design finished subgrades for the
project The mass grading operations were performed by Jayar Construction Inc
After clearing and grubbing the area within the new building footprint and 10 feet
on its periphery was over excavated where necessary to about Elevation 65 feet MSL
The over excavated subgrade was then scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Following the subgrade preparation limestone fines imported from Grace Pacific
Corporation was placed as fills from about Elevation 65 feet MSL to Elevation 67 5 feet
MSL The limestone fines generally was placed in approximately 6 inch lifts brought to
about the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction The limestone fines was then capped with 6 inches of base course imported
from Makakilo Quarry compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades at the parking areas were
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction On site excavated coralline materials were then used to raise the existing
ground surface to the design finished subgrades i e bottom of the base course layer The
coralline materials generally were placed in approximately 6 inch lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
During the mass grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
