PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the relocated septic tanks and leach field locations and to perform field
percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics for the formulation of
preliminary recommendations pertinent to the design of the disposal components of the
on site sewage disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated March 17 1999 Our scope of work included the following
tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of two borings to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profiles at the relocated
leach field location
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a geologist
from our firm
4 Drilling or excavating six boreholes test pits to a depth of about 2 0 feet below
the existing ground surface for field percolation testing
5 Performance of five field percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration
characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertinent
to the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal
system
6 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
7 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a principal engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and leach
field method are planned within the Hauula Elementary School site Therefore we have
conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of the
sewage disposal system at the relocated site A total of five field percolation tests were
performed at selected locations across the project site The approximate locations of the
field percolation tests designated as P 6 through P 11 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
to a depth of about 2 0 feet below the existing ground surface The boreholes test pits were
terminated at these shallow depths in order to provide approximately 12 inches of percolation
distance between the bottom of the borehole test pit and the natural groundwater table
Approximately one inch of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the boreholes test
pits to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 12 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation
of the percolation tests was based
Test Results and Discussions
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rates generally range from
about 1 to 2 minutes per inch at all the test locations except at test locations P 7 and P 11

Our percolation tests at these two locations suggested that the subsoils may be relatively
impermeable with percolation rates as low as 40 minutes per inch Results of the individual
percolation tests are presented on Plates 6 through 11 of this report Due to the variability
of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed
by additional percolation tesis during construction
Based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department of Health s
Administrative Rules Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems the bottom of the
absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater level
Based on the shallow groundwater levels measured in the drilled borings it is our opinion
that the feasibility of using septic tank and leach field method for sewage disposal should be
carefully evaluated for the subject site
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings test pits and percolation tests Variations of
subsoil conditions between and beyond the borings test pits may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions
provided in this report
The locations of the field borings test pits indicated in this report are approximate
having been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by Fujita
Associates Inc Elevations of the borings test pits were interpolated from the elevation
points shown on the same pian The physical locations and elevations of the borings test pits
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
