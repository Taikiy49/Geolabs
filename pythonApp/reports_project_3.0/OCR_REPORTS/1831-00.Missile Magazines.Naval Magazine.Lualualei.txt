INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed missile magazines and access road
at the Naval Magazine in Lualualei West
Nea
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
foundation design of the proposed structures and the pavement design of
the access road to the proposed structures
The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling eighteen
18 borings to depths of 4 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct three Type E earth covered box magazines
strictly for the use of storing Tomahawk cruise missiles The proposed
magazines each about 97 by 56 feet in plan will be reinforced concrete
structures with concrete slab on grade The proposed structures will be
designed to withstand blasting overpressure loads resulting in heavy
column loads and the need for heavy rolling doors
An access road about 720 feet long is planned for the proposed
structures
The layout of the proposed construction is shown on the Site Plan

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
On the island of Oahu the main shield building activity ended in
the late Pliocene about 25 million years ago Since then the island has
subsided up to 1200 feet As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the
shorelines keeping pace with the subsidence
The site is predominantly underlain at shallow depths by coral reef
which indicates that this area was once in a shallow marine environment
During the Pleistocene fluctations of sea level were caused by
accumulation and recession of the polar glaciers
Contemporaneous with these sea level fluctuations were post
erosional pyroclastic eruptions from the Salt Lake and Makalapa vents
The volcanic ash consolidated to form tuff over some of the coral
formation at the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
I D 2080 Page 4
In more recent times as the sea level dropped the coral reef emerged
and were later covered locally by man made fills
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
eighteen 18 borings to depths of 4 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a surface layer
of up to about 3 feet thick of miscellaneous fill materials including
stiff sandy silt and dense sandy coral gravel Below the thin surface
fill layer was medium hard to hard coral formation consisting generally
of unweathered to slightly weathered coral reef over well cemented beach
sands or sandstone to the drilled depths of the borings
A small cavity about 1 foot high was encountered from the depths
of about 3 to 4 feet in the hard coral formation in Boring 5
In addition the hard coral formation in Boring 15 was encountered
to be overlain by a thin layer of highly weathered tuff which was
further overlain by a thin layer of coral detritus under the thin surface
fill materials
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our surface exploration generally encountered a thin stiff dense
surface fill layer over medium hard to hard coral reef formation at the
project site

Based on the soil conditions the anticipated finished grade
elevations and design loadings we believe that the proposed structures
may be supported by shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing
directly on the hard coral formation the recompacted on site surface
fill coral detritus or the compacted select fill
The presence of cavities in the hard coral formation should be
further evaluated by probings drilled below the column wall footing
bottoms to reduce the possibility of excessive footing settlement
If encountered the extent of the cavity should be delineated and
properly backfilled with concrete or low pressure grout to ensure
sufficient support to the footing foundation above
Foundation
For spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly on the hard
coral formation an allowable static bearing capacity of 5000 p s f may
be used
Alternatively an allowable static bearing capacity of 3000 p s f
may be used for spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly on
the recompacted on site surface fill coral detritus or the compacted
select fill If a higher bearing capacity of 4000 p s f is required
imported select borrow fill may be used
The allowable static bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
or seismic loading
For very short term blasting overpressure loads a lower factor of
safety may be used for the design of the footings Considering the
probability of loading occurrence short loading time and assuming that
some amount of damage to the structure are tolerable we believe that the

Cavity
During our field exploration a 1 foot high cavity was encountered
in the hard coral formation in Boring 5 which was drilled at the
southeastern corner of one of the proposed magazine sites The cavity
was encountered from a depth of 3 0 to 4 0 feet below the existing ground
surface

Cavities are often formed in coral formation due to the presence of
fresh water streams during the growth stages of the coral which prevent
the growth of the coral It is possible that the cavity may be part of a
small old stream and therefore may extend horizontally through the site
rather than being an isolated occurrence
Probing
To reduce the possibility of excessive footing settlement due to
possible cavities encountered near the footing bottom elevation in
the underlying coral formation drilled probes are recommended at
each column footing location and at 15 foot centers for continuous
wall footings The probe holes should be a minimum of 3 inches in
diameter and extend below the bottom of the footing to a minimum
depth equal to two times the footing width or 10 feet whichever is
less
Tf a cavity is suspected at a depth of more than 3 feet below a
footing bottom additional probe holes should be drilled to
delineate the horizontal extent and thickness of the cavity
Cavities greater than 6 inches in diameter and shallower than the
abovementioned depth should be excavated and backfilled with
concrete If encountered at greater depth the cavities should be
grouted as recommended by the soil engineer
The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by the soil
engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are carried
out properly

Lateral Earth Pressure
It is our understanding that the proposed missile magazines will
have an earth cover around and on top of each structure As a result
the magazine walls and wing walls on both sides of the magazines should
be designed as retaining walls
The on site gravelly soils or imported granular materials such as
coral select borrow crusher waste or 3B Fine gravel may be used to
cover the proposed missile magazines and the following lateral earth
pressures may be used for design of the retaining walls
On magazine walls at rest condition 55 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
On wing walls active condition 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the retaining
walls The subdrains should be properly constructed to drain water away
from the walls
Site Grading
Minor site grading including minimal cut and up to about 2 5 feet of
fill is anticipated for the construction of the proposed structures and
access road
At the on site of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such as
vegetation trees debris and rubbish should be removed and disposed of
properly off site

After clearing the areas to receive footing and slab on grade or
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the footin
bottom or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
select on site gravelly soil or with suitable imported material compacted
to the abovementioned standard
The on site sandy gravel surface fill may be reused as fill and
backfill material Imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular fill Unified Soil Classification SM SW
GM less than 3 inches in maximum size
Fill layers should be placed in compacted thickness not exceeding 8
inches and compacted to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density of the
fill material
