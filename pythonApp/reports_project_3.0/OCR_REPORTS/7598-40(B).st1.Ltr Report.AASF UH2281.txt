Dear Ms. Miyasato: 
This letter report presents the results of our permeability testing conducted for the 
Hawaii Department of Defense, Army Aviation Support Facility (AASF), Underground 
Injection Control (UIC) project in the Hilo airport area on the Island of Hawaii. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This report summarizes our field observations and presents the results of our 
permeability testing and engineering analyses for the project. These findings are intended 
to support the preparation of a UIC Injection Well Status Report for UIC Permit No. 
UH-2281. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the 
limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the information provided, the Hawaii Department of Defense, AASF 
operates one drywell under UIC Permit No. UH-2281 for the on-site disposal of septic 
effluent. We understand that observation and permeability testing services are desired in 
support of the preparation of a UIC Injection Well Status Report for this drywell. 
PURPOSE AND SCOPE 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to obtain an overview 
of the existing condition of the well by visual observation and short-term permeability 
injection testing in support of a UIC Injection Well Status Report prepared by others for 
the project. Generally, the scope of work included the following tasks:

EXISTING WELL AND SITE CONDITIONS 
One drywell for Permit No. UH-2281 is covered under this report. The drywell is 
located within AASF as approximately indicated on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Our engineer conducted the inspection and testing of the well on 
December 16, 2021. The well cover was removed by AASF personnel. The wellhead and 
septic tank manholes are sited on generally level ground with bollards surrounding their 
perimeter. 
We observed that the surrounding ground surface contained no ponded water 
accumulation. The surface completion of the well consists of a steel manhole cover set 
flush with the ground surface. Below the manhole cover, access to the well is via a 
concrete plug cover. 
With the well cover open, we sensed a mild to moderate sewage odor emanating 
from the well. The existing fluid level in the well was measured at 12.2 feet. We measured 
the existing depth of the well at about 14.2 feet below the existing ground surface. 
Therefore, there was an approximate 2 feet of fluid in the well prior to our testing. The 
accumulated fluid contained in the well appeared relatively clear. 
We observed a 6-inch diameter PVC effluent discharge pipe entering the well 
sidewall at about a 7-foot depth below the ground surface. A slight discharge fluid was 
observed intermittently emanating from the pipe during our inspection and testing.

PERMEABILITY TESTING 
Our evaluation of the well permeability characteristics was conducted on 
December 16, 2021, between the hours of about 0835 and 1049. The weather was cloudy 
during the majority of the testing, with some intermittent light rain. 
Permeability testing for the drywell generally involved the short-term gravity 
injection of clear water from a water tanker truck, followed by measurement of the falling 
head in the well. The permeability testing was conducted in an effort to evaluate the 
drainage (infiltration) functionality of the existing well. 
A slight stream of effluent was observed intermittently discharging into the drywell 
during the test. The tests were conducted by discharging two loads of water from a 
4000-gallon water truck. It should be noted that discharging of the second load of water

When the short-term injection discharge ended with each truck load of water, a 
falling head test was performed to record the fall of the accumulated water level within 
the well. Based on the results of the falling head test, a time elapse of about 134 minutes 
was needed to drain the well to a head depth of about 2.4 feet in the well. The resulting 
head at the end of the falling head test was approximately 0.4 feet above the pre-test 
head level of about 2 feet measured in the well. 
Based on the results of the short-term injection and falling head permeability test, 
the operation and capacity of the well appear to be fair and within the needed capacity 
for the existing facility.

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the results of our visual observations and the short-term permeability 
testing, the drywell appears to be operating satisfactorily with no overt indication of 
potential failure. 
Based on the falling head permeability testing conducted, the drywell has an 
estimated effluent capacity of about 145 gpm, equivalent to about 210,000 gallons per 
day (gpd), which exceeds the estimated design flow of 1,800 gpd. A graphical 
presentation of the injection test results is presented on Plate A-1 in Appendix A.

Based on our observation of the existing well site and vicinity conditions, we 
recommend the following to maintain the performance of the wells: 
Perform routine inspection of the fluid levels contained in the septic tank to 
gauge when servicing may be needed and schedule servicing to include the 
pumping and cleaning of the septic tank system on a regular basis. 
Periodically inspect any upstream flow control valves to ensure operation 
and functionality. 
Continue to conduct annual injection well inspections as per the UIC permit 
conditions. 
Review and abide by the best practice guidelines for effluent discharge that 
was provided in the Environmental Notice dated December 2016 and 
provided to the facility operator by the State of Hawaii Department of Health, 
Safe Drinking Water Branch, Underground Injection Control (UIC) Program.
