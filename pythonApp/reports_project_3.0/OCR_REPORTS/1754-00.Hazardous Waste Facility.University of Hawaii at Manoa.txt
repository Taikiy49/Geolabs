SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the
subsurface soils consist of about 5 to 20 feet of
medium stiff to stiff brown clay over weathered
clinker saprolite and hard basalt
laboratory tests performed on the stiff clay soil
indicate that this material has medium to high
swelling potential depending on the in situ
moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent
saturation Therefore care should be exercised
during construction to reduce the swelling
potential of the clay soil
About 3 to 8 feet of fill is planned for the
proposed site grading Therefore the quality of
the fill material will govern the foundation and
pavement design of the new facility Select
non expansive import fill material should be used
in order to provide competent foundation support
of the new building The clay subgrade soils
underneath the select fill should be kept moist
and protected from drying from the start of
construction until the fill placement
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering study was to explore
the subsurface soil conditions for the design of the foundation to
Support the proposed building

The main objectives of our work were to explore the thickness and
competency of the underlying soil for spread footing foundation support
and to investigate the swelling or expansion potential of the surface
soil and its effect upon the the new building slab and driveway
The scope of our work included logging and sampling four 4
to 24 8 feet below the existing ground surface MAS
Drilling Company was contracted by the State to drill the borings Also
included were laboratory testing of the selected samples obtained
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of
design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed building would be about 35
by 73 feet in plan and consist of an office storage and laboratory
facility with a loading dock for container Corrugated metal roofing
CMU bearing walls and slab on grade are planned for the building
Concrete pavement is planned adjacent to the loading dock New A c
pavement is planned for two parking stalls and the area between the new
concrete pavement and edge of the existing A C pavement A short
retaining wall along the edge of the new concrete and A C pavements is
anticipated due to site grading requirement

The proposed finished floor of the new building is at elevation 128
feet above Mean Sea Level Therefore the entire building site will be
filled from about 3 to 8 feet in height A 5 foot high retaining wall
for grade separation is planned along the southern boundary of the site
next to an existing shallow drainage swale
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is situated near the northern boundary line of the
University of Hawaii at Manoa on the island of Oahu The Biomedical
Science Building is located on the southwestern side of the project site
adjacent to Malama Place The site is surrounded by existing structures
including quonset huts wooden buildings greenhouse and warehouse and
is presently covered with grasses plants and trees
The area is generally flat and slopes down gently in the southern
direction with gradients flatter than about 10 1 H V The present
elevation of the site varies from about 130 to 118 feet above Mean Sea
Level
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
four 4 borings to depths of 12 5 to 24 8 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings encountered 5 to 20 feet of medium stiff to stiff
expansive clay with traces of sand and weathered gravels below the
existing ground surface The upper 2 feet of the clay layer encountered
was wet and soft
Gray dense basalt was encountered beneath the clay layer in Borings
1 and 2 A layer of clayey sand and gravel weathered clinker or
saprolite was encountered beneath the clay layer in Borings 3 and 4

Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings at the time of
our field exploration
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Taboratory tests performed on the near surface stiff clay soil
indicate that this material generally has medium to high swelling
potential depending on the in situ moisture content of the soil prior to
subsequent subgrade saturation Therefore to reduce the effects due to
possible soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the
on site soil be capped with at least 3 feet of non expansive select fill
and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be kept
moist and protected from drying from the start of construction until fill
placement and or slab concrete placement The required new structural
fill would serve this purpose
About 3 to 8 feet of fill is planned in order to provide a finished
floor for the proposed building at elevation 128 feet above Mean Sea
Level Select non expansive import fill material should be used to
provide adequate foundation support of the new building and to reduce
possible soil swelling
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished floor of the proposed
building will be at elevation 128 feet Therefore 3 to 8 feet of fill
is planned for site grading Settlement due to 8 feet of fill is
estimated to be on the order of less than one inch The settlement is
expected to occur rapidly We recommend that the settlement due to the
new fill should be monitored by installing settlement gauges to determine
when the majority of the settlement has occurred and when building
construction can begin

One existing quonset hut on elevated floor and one greenhouse on
gravel bed floor are planned to be demolished Prior to site filling
the existing vegetation trees broken structures and any concrete slabs
encountered should be removed The exposed subgrade soil should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches Rubbish deleterious
materials such as large roots should be removed The scarified
subgrade should be proof rolled moisture conditioned and compacted to
85 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any
soft materials encountered during proof rolling should be removed
Laboratory tests performed on soil samples from the existing upper 3
feet of clayey soil indicate that this material generally has medium to
high swelling potential with localized pockets of very expansive clayey
soil Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling we recommend
that the subgrade soils underneath the structural fill and pavement be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
placement of structural fill and or select borrow for pavement
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive
soils and submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory testing
and approval prior to its intended delivery to the project site
Selected non expansive granular material with maximum size less than 3
inches are recommended for the structural fill material
Fills should be placed in maximum 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78 test method
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure

An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for the
anticipated light foundation loads on the compacted structural fill The
bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for short duration wind and seismic loading
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used between the foundation and the compacted fill
Minimal footing settlement is anticipated due to the light building
loads
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire structure
A 4 inch thick base course cushion should be provided below the
4 inch concrete slab and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as
established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier should be
incorporated on top of the cushion fill below the concrete slab

Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement

recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with our findings in this report
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
eaten
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 12 5 to 24 8 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
MAS Drilling Company was contracted to drill the borings and a
truck mounted drill rig was used
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
A Rock core sample was obtained by coring with a NX core barrel
The percentage of core recovery REC and the rock quality designation
RQD for each run are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depth

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
eh Ai
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits were performed on selected samples of the soils
to evaluate liquid and plastic limits The test results are shown on
Plate B 1
One laboratory CBR test and one compaction test were performed on a
bulk sample to evaluate the subgrade support and compaction
characteristics of the soil respectively for pavement design The test
results are summarized on Plate B 1
Eleven one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plates B 2 and B 3
One direct shear test was performed on a select undisturbed sample
to evaluate the shear strength parameter of the soil The test result is
plotted on Plate B 4
One consolidation test was performed on a selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the subsoil The
curve is presented on Plate B 5
