foundation investigation for the proposed residence in

conditions at the site and recommend an appropriate type of
foundation for the proposed new residence

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface soils immediately below the existing ground are very
soft wet and compressible Settlements greater than one inch
could be anticipated if the proposed residence was constructed on
the existing site and subsurface soils This soft material may be
fill or alluvial hill wash deposits
It is our opinion that the proposed residence cannot be adequately
supported on the existing site with shallow continuous or spread
footings unless the soft materials are removed and replaced with
a structural fill This will necessitate removal to a depth of at
least 10 feet Because of the close proximity to adjacent structures
and the existing street this method may be too impractical and
costly It is therefore recommended that if the removal and re
compaction method does prove to be uneconomical the proposed structure
be supported on a concrete caisson foundation bearing in the firmer
soils or rock found at about 12 to 16 feet below the existing ground
Caissons should have a minimum diameter of 12 inches If a caisson
foundation is used then the ground floor should use a suspended
floor system and not rest directly on the existing ground
After the existing structure is removed from the site at least
2 borings should be made on the makai side to verify a that the
rock encountered in our Boring is bedrock and not a boulder which

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The additional borings drilled at the proposed building site
did not encounter a competent basalt bedrock layer within the
explored depths Boring 3 indicated subsurface stream 12 5
feet below the existing ground surface with basalt boulders
near the water level The entire site appears to be underlain
by about 20 feet of soft compressible soils
In our preliminary investigation we recommended the use of
concrete cast in place piles or caissons for support of the
proposed structure In view of our additional borings which
encountered the underground stream this foundation type may
prove to be costly due to the special construction methods
required for the presence of subterranean stream
In view of the added difficulties and costs involved we have
evaluated two other foundation types However these other
types will require a more flexible structure in order to
tolerate greater future settlements

Caisson Foundation
Cast in place concrete caisson should have a minimum penetra
tion of 5 feet into the stiff silty clay or decomposed rock
which is encountered at depths of 10 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface A design bearing capacity of 10 kips
may be used for a 12 inch diameter caisson The perimeter
caisson should be tied to a reinforced concrete grade beam with
a structural slab The caisson length is estimated to vary
between a minimum of 15 feet to as much as 25 feet long Some
boulders will be encountered in the upper fill and alluvial
deposits Maximum settlement will be on the order of one half
inch with differential settlements of less than one fourth
inch Due to the presence of flowing groundwater the drilled
hole will require casing or drilling mud to prevent a cave in
Concrete will have to be placed by the tremie method
The following alternate foundation system will perform satis
factorily if the proposed structure is capable of absorbing
larger total and differential settlements This may require
a more flexible structure such as a wood frame residence
Preloading With a Surcharge Fill

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
The materials encountered on the project site and utilized in
our evaluation are believed representative of the total area
however soil materials may vary in characteristics between
boring locations Should other soils conditions be encoun
tered during construction a foundation engineer should be
consulted and appropriate construction modification implemented
if necessary Due to the possible variations that may be
encountered in the existing fill and onsite soils we recommend
