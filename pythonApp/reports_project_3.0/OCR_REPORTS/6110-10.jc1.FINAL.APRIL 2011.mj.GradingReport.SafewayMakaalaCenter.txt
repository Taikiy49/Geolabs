This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading and building construction for the Makaala Center project from
March 23 2010 through March 10 2011 in general accordance with our revised fee
proposal dated March 03 2010
Project Considerations
The project site is adjacent to the existing Waiakea Center in the Hilo area on the
Island of Hawaii The Makaala Center is comprised of a single parcel and is approximately
bounded by an industrial development to the north Railroad Avenue to the east East
Makaala Street to the south and the existing Hilo Wal Mart store to the west
The site is approximately L shaped and provides approximately 220 000 square
feet of commercial space consisting of about 60 000 and 160 000 square feet of store
space for the Safeway and Target Stores respectively as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In addition the project entails construction of a parking lot for both stores accommodating
over 900 cars In general shallow foundations consisting of spread and continuous

Earthwork Operation
Based on the grading plans of the project the finished floor elevation of the stores
is at about 76 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In general the original topography of the
project site prior to site grading activities consisted of previously developed terraces
overgrown by tall grasses

parking driveway subgrade preparation were performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc At
the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation
procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential for collapse
of near surface cavities and or voids within the site The intent of the subgrade preparation
procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids
contained in the severely fractured basalt formation It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of
practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to assist in
detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the severely
fractured basalt formation After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground was
generally leveled with the use of a D 10 bulldozer working the surface for about four
passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with the
bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a sheepsfoot roller compactor
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids
In general fill materials consisting of structural fills were placed in the fill areas of
the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally
smaller than 6 inches in dimension
Subgrades in cut areas generally were scarified by about 3 feet and leveled with
a D 10 bulldozer for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface The
scarification operation was omitted in the areas exposing dense basalt formation After
the scarification and leveling process the subgrade in the cut areas was proof rolled
with a sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Structural fills consisting of well graded granular materials generally smaller than
3 inches in dimension were used as fill and backfill materials under the building pad within
utility trench excavations and other areas Samples of the on site crushed materials and

of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the above geotechnical engineering report and project plans and
specifications
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
