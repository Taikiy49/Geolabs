MICROPILE INSTALLATION  
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation services was to observe 
the micropile installation and testing for general conformance with project plans and 
specifications.  
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site, the micropiles were 
specified to extend through surface fills and soft lagoonal deposits and to provide resistance 
from competent stiff alluvium and coralline deposits via skin friction between the micropile 
grout and surrounding soils. 
The micropile contractor, Kelikai, Inc., installed a total of 14 production micropiles 
from March 3 through 17, 2021 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile 
Location Plan, Plate 2. Details of the micropile installation are presented in the Micropile 
Records in Appendix A.  
In general, the micropiles were installed using a C6 XP2 Casagrande drill rig 
equipped with a down-the-hole hammer and under the near-continuous (full-time) 
observations by our field engineers. Steel casing with 7.0-inch OD (Outside Diameter) was 
advanced to the bottom of the drilled hole in order to facilitate the micropile installation. 
Upon the drilled holes extended to the specified depth and the cutting cleaned out, 
the drilled holes were initially grouted. Grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal 
high-shear and high-speed ChemGrout CG500H mixer equipped with a grout pump. The 
grout mix consisted of one sack of 94-pound Portland Cement Type I and/or II, 87 pounds 
of masonry sand, and 5 gallons of water. 
The grout was placed in the drilled hole through a PVC tremie pipe from the bottom 
up. After clean grout appeared at the top of the cased hole, a reinforcing bar with centralizers 
was placed through the grouted hole. 
The steel casing was removed one section at a time and re-grouted as necessary to 
maintain the grout level near the top of the casing. This process continued until the bottom 
of the steel casing reached the top of the specified bond zone. 
Based on our field observations of the production micropile installation performed by 
the contractor, it is our professional opinion that the specified micropiles for the new Kailua 
Regional Wastewater Plant Electrical Improvements have been installed to provide sufficient 
load resistance meeting the project specifications.
