PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the soil conditions
at the site to obtain pertinent soil data to assist in the shoreline
erosion study of the proposed hotel development
The main objectives of the investigation were
1 To determine the soil material types that will be encountered
near the shoreline of Kawela Bay
2 To determine the excavation difficulties and depths of water at
the site and
3 To perform some laboratory tests to determine the particle sizes
for classification of soils in the Unified Soil Classification
System
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located between Kakipi Place and the shoreline of Kawela
Bay The borings are generally located on the ocean side about 50 to 60
feet from the water s edge behind of a row of abandoned houses The
borings were spaced about 225 to 400 feet apart along a stretch of
shoreline about 1500 feet in length The boring sites are generally
level
Entrance to the area is from Kamehameha Highway along Hanupu Street
and Kakipi Place

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Six 6 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 13 5 to 21
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In general the borings
encountered a surface layer approximately 8 to 13 feet thick of tan
white medium to fine grained coral sand Unified Soil Classification SP
Below this in Borings 1 through 4 was tan white coarse grained coral
sand with coral gravel SP SM to 15 to 21 feet the depths drilled A
pocket of brown silty clay MH was noted in Boring 1 at 15 5 to 19 5 feet
below the existing ground
In Borings 5 and 6 a hard layer of coral reef or beachrock was
encountered at 9 5 to 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Pockets of coral gravel and cobbles GM were noted in some of the
borings
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 8 to
11 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is generally underlain
by 8 to 13 feet of coral sand above a deposit of silty coral sand mixed
with coral gravel The easterly side of the site is underlain at fairly
shallow depths by a hard coral reef or beachrock formation Outcrops were
visually noted near these boring sites

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six
6 borings to depths of 13 5 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were calssified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
