Geolabs Hawaii has performed a geotechnical engineering exploration for
evaluation of the building distress at Building T 126 at the above referenced site This
letter report summarizes our findings and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for evaluation of the building distress observed at the existing Building
T 126 at the subject site The work was performed in general accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the existing Building T 126 within the Fort Shafter
Military Reservation of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided building distress including
cracked concrete structural columns and shifted walls on the south side of the building
were observed at the subject building Therefore a geotechnical engineering exploration
was conducted in an attempt to evaluate the probable cause s of the distresses
observed and to formulate remedial recommendations pertaining to the existing
distresses at the subject site

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the site in an attempt to develop a soil and groundwater data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the existing
distress problems at the subject site Our program consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Research and review of in house soils and boring data in the vicinity of the
project site
2 Review of available construction plans specifications and other documents
pertaining to the subject project
3 Reconnaissance of the project site to observe the existing distresses and
surrounding site conditions by Project and Principal Engineers from our
firm
4 Mobilization and demobilization of a drill rig and operators to and from the
project site
5 Drilling and sampling of two borings at selected locations of the project site
to a depth of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of
one day of field exploration
6 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Engineer from our firm
7 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
8 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the distress problems at the
subject site
9 Preparation of a letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations

Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located on the mauka side of Moanalua Highway within the Fort
Shafter Military Reservation of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The existing
Building T 126 is a one story structure constructed with masonry walls and wooden
roofing The terrain at the subject site is relatively level and the yard area around the
building is covered with grass Based on our observations cracks were noted on the
three structural columns on the south side of the building The three columns buckled
outward at about the same height In addition it was noted that the walls which support
the cracked columns were shifted about 2 to 3 inches outward from the foundation
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling
two borings extending to a depth of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings encountered stiff to very stiff silty clays to depths of
approximately 5 5 and 9 feet below the existing ground surface Below the clayey soils
very dense silty sands with weathered basalt gravel were encountered extending to the
maximum depth drilled of about 16 5 feet below the surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that the groundwater levels are subject to change due to
seasonal precipitation tidal fluctuations storm surge conditions and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 of Appendix A Laboratory testing was performed on
selected samples obtained and the test results are presented in Appendix B

Findings and Recommendations
Our field exploration indicated that the subject site is generally underlain by silty
clays over dense silty sands at the subject site The consistency of the near surface
clayey soils encountered at the site ranged from stiff to very stiff Based on our field
exploration the competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site should provide
adequate foundation support for the existing building structure Our laboratory test results
indicate that the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate swelling and shrinking
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations However based on the pattern of the
distresses observed we believe that the swelling shrinkage effects of the near surface
expansive soils is not a contributing factor to the distresses observed at the existing
building
Based on our field exploration and engineering evaluation it is our opinion that the
structural distresses observed are not a result of soil movement or ground instability
Therefore it is our opinion that remedial work is not necessary for the foundation system
of the existing building at this stage
In addition we recommend settlement markers be installed after repairs at
selected locations to monitor potential future movements of the existing building The
settlement markers should be read periodically by a qualified land surveyor
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from the test borings Variations of the subsurface
conditions between and beyond the test borings may occur both vertically and laterally If
variations appear evident during construction Geolabs Hawaii should be contacted to
reevaluate the recommendations presented in this letter report

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate2 The borings were drilled using a drill rig
equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One ring swell test was performed on a selected sample to evaluate the swelling
potential of the near surface soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed ona selected sample of
the soils to evaluate its liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the test are presented on Plate B 2
One unconfined compression test was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength of the soils The results of the test are presented on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
