INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Xtermco building and base yard in the Kalihi Kai area within
the district of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project
site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained through the field exploration and laboratory
testing
These recommendations are intended for design input only and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated at Street in the Kalihi Kai area in
the district of Honolulu on the island of Oahu It is proposed to construct a new building
with facilities for office and storage Based on the current design concepts approximately
37 truck parking stalls are also planned
The project site is currently occupied by two residential lots
Street within a relatively flat area of about 8 000 square feet as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The currently proposed building will consist of a two story office structure to be
located to the southwest corner of the project site The remaining areas will be used for
parking It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavement will be designed for the on
grade parking facility Based on the development plan the perimeters of the project site
will consist of six foot walls

Based on information provided by the project structural engineer column loadings
on the order of 206 kips are anticipated Structural loads of approximately eight 8 kips
per lineal foot are anticipated along the perimeter CMU walls
We anticipate that site grading would consist of minor cuts and fills on the order
of two feet 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site and to develop an idealized subsurface data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation
design and site grading for the proposed building construction
Our work was done in general accordance with our revised proposal dated
to Innovative Architectural Designs Inc Our scope of work
consisted of the following
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a portable drilling equipment and
operators to and from the project site Due to the limited drilling space a
portable drilling equipment was used instead of the proposed truck
mounted drill rig
2 Drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging between 5 and 11
feet below the existing ground surface Due to the presence of coral
formation at shallow depths borings were generally terminated when a
minimum of five feet 5 of dense coral formation were retrieved Due to
the limited drilling space our exploration was also reduced to drilling of five
5 borings outside the existing building

3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
geologist of our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the

developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration laboratory test results and the anticipated column
loadings of up to 206 kips we recommend that the foundations of the proposed building
be supported on the underlying coral formation Therefore the proposed building may
be supported on aé_ conventional shallow foundation system consisting of
spread continuous footings bearing on the underlying coral formation provided the
recommendations in the following sections of this report are followed
Based on data obtained from borings Boring Nos B 1 B 2 and B 4 located at
the perimeters of the proposed building site the site is underlain by medium hard coral
formation at about 1 to 24 feet below the existing ground surface
During our field exploration cavity was encountered in Boring No 2 To reduce
the possibility of potential large voids situated below footing foundations we recommend
that the footing foundation subgrades be probed
Recommendations addressing the above geotechnical considerations and other
geotechnical aspects of the proposed construction are discussed in the following sections
of this report
Site Grading
Detailed information pertaining to the grading of the project site was not available
to our office during the preparation of this report However we anticipate that minor cuts
and fills of up to two feet 2 would be required for the building and parking area
constructions The following recommendations are presented to provide general
guidelines for the site grading work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3121 00 Page 7
1 The area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
2 Any loose soft spots encountered during site grading should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and replaced with compacted non
expansive select material The material resulting from over excavation
should be removed and properly disposed of off site
3 Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of six inches
6 moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and
compacted to unyielding condition with at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
4 Imported fill material should consist of non expansive granular materials less
than three inches 8 in maximum size with less than 20 percent passing
the No 200 sieve Any imported material for fill and backfill if necessary
should be tested for suitability for its intended use by Geolabs Hawaii prior
to delivery to the site
5 Structural fill material should consist of well graded granular material such
as base course or equivalent with particles less than three inches 3 in
maximum size with less than 15 percent passing No 200 sieve
6 All fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal lift not exceeding 10 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3121 00 Page 8
a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 91 All structural fill should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
7 Measures should be incorporated into the grading of the project site to
reduce the potential for erosion and to prevent concentration of water
around structures The ground surface around structures should be sloped
away from structures
Footing Foundations
Based on our exploration data and laboratory test results we believe that shallow
foundations bearing on underlying coral formation may be used to support the proposed
building and CMU perimeter walls
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 4 500 p s f may be used for the design of
foundation bearing on the underlying coral formation provided the footings are embedded
a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bearing pressure
provided is for dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3
for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be firm and unyielding If medium
hard coral formation are not encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they
should be over excavated to expose competent coral formation and backfilled the
excavation with structural fill material such as base course or equivalent compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement Also where
GEOLABS HAWAII

utilities cross perimeter footing lines the utility trench backfill should consist of a vertical
barrier of impervious type material or lean concrete
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by a combination of passive
earth pressure exerted against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the
base of foundations and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure equal to an
equivalent fluid weighing 350 pounds per cubic foot p c f and a base friction factor of
0 40 may be used for design Unless covered by slabs or pavement passive soil
resistance in the upper 12 inches should be neglected
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate
total settlements to be less than one inch 1 with differential settlements of less than 1 2
inch
Footing excavations should be observed by representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to allow verification of the bearing material
and the embedment depths
Cavity Probing
The field exploration generally encountered coral formation at approximately 11
to 3 feet below the ground surface throughout the project site Cavity was encountered
in Boring No 2 during our field exploration Previous experience indicates that coral
formations are discontinuous and that soft pockets and or cavities are commonly found
in coral formations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3121 00 Page 10
In order to reduce the possibility of potential large voids situated below a
foundation causing future footing settlement and or foundation failure we recommend
that the footing foundation subgrades be probed The probes should consist of three
inch 3 minimum diameter probe holes drilled to a depth equivalent to twice the width
of the footing or 10 feet below the bottom of the footing whichever is greater Probe
holes should be drilled at each column footing location and at 15 foot centers along
continuous footings
The probe holes should be subsequently backfilled with a neat cement grout
injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage fashion i e from the bottom of the
probe hole up to the top If large cavities greater than one foot in height are
encountered during the probe drilling or if grout takes exceeds four 4 times the
theoretical volume of the probe hole a mortar like grout one part sand to one part
cement should be injected instead of the neat cement grout If the grout intake exceeds
one half cubic yard it should be stopped and the grout allowed to set prior to
continuation of the remaining grouting backfilling operations
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment it is most likely preferable
to perform the probing and grouting operations prior to structure and footing excavation
The probing and grouting operations should be conducted under the full time
observation of a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to allow additional evaluation field
consultation and possibly field adjustment of the above recommendations if necessary

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench
backfill up to about one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing potential damage to the
pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site material or granular
material if necessary The backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch loose level lifts
and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for future ground subsidence

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling five
5 borings to depths of between 5 and 11 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled by portable drilling equipment
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by an engineer from our firm Logs of the borings are
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based
on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
