SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Lot 7248 Map 595 of Land Court Application 1069
within the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is about 2 794 acres in area and is located between Kamokila Boulevard and the
future extension of Haumea Street and between Wakea Street and the future Road E
Based on our field reconnaissance the project site presently consists of vacant
land with a sparse cover of dry vegetation and small piles of deleterious materials and
debris scattered over the existing ground surface Our field observations indicated that dry
and loose soils were exposed at the existing ground surface with potholes and trenches
caused by soil shrinkage in the surface clayey soil
_ SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 25 5 to
26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain
by surface fills and alluvial deposits consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clays and clayey
silts extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 26 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal
precipitation surface water runoff and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our site reconnaissance and field exploration the project site is generally
underlain by surface fills and alluvial deposits consisting of stiff to very stiff clayey silts and
silty clays The stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays extend to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was
not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed new buildings planned at the site Shallow foundations for the
building structures should bear on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills
needed to raise the site to the finished grades In general footing subgrades should be
recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and
smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete

In order to reduce the potential for distress due to differential movements between
the heavily loaded foundations and the lightly loaded building slabs we recommend that
free floating slabs on grade with no structural connections to the wall and column
foundations be used To further reduce the potential for distress to the building
slabs on grade and foundations resulting from water infiltration into the subsurface from
areas immediately adjacent to the building foundations we recommend that a concrete
sidewalk be considered around the perimeter of the new buildings It should be noted that
proper surface drainage and measures to reduce the potential for excessive water
infiltration into the subsurface are integral components of the recommendations provided
in this report
Based on our laboratory test results the surface clayey soils exhibit moderate to
high shrink swell characteristics when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture content
As a result we recommend that a minimum of 18 inches of non expansive select granular
fill material be provided below the building slab areas and sidewalk slabs In addition we
also recommend that 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill in lieu of aggregate
subbase course be provided below the pavement areas due to the expansive nature of
the on site clayey soils
Special attention should be given to the recommended procedures for subgrade
soil preparation below the slabs and pavements in order to reduce the potential
shrink swell effects of the surface clayey soils Because moisture conditioning and
compaction of the clayey subgrade soils and new fill materials are critical elements of
earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by Geolabs during site
grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the
proper moisture content on each fill lift
Observations during our field exploration indicate that abundant shrinkage cracks
and ground depressions exist at the existing ground surface across the project site The
existing shrinkage cracks if left unattended may increase further in size under prolonged
dry weather conditions Shrinkage cracks located adjacent to the building foundations are
potentially detrimental because surface water can readily infiltrate through the crack
openings and soften the subsurface soils around the building foundations thus affecting
their performance
As a result special attention should be given to the recommended site preparation
procedures prior to the fill placement operation In general we recommend that the
exposed subgrades after clearing and grubbing be proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing
near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks and ground
depressions After the proof rolling operations the proof rolled subgrades should be
scarified moisture conditioned and compacted which is further discussed in the Site
Preparation subsection of this letter report

Building Foundations
In general we believe that the proposed new construction may be supported on a
shallow foundation support system bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new
compacted fills placed to achieve the finished grades at the site Based on the generally
stiff subsurface soil conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread
and or continuous footings may be used for support of the new building construction An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new
compacted fills needed to achieve the finished grades This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the on site soils or new compacted fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to
provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of reinforcing
steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavations the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation may be backfilled with the on site soils compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing may be extended
down to bear directly on the underlying competent materials
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
conditions should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 6 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
Footings located adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls should be embedded
deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the planned retaining
walls should be designed to resist the additional structural loads
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements the footings should
be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench or the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
Structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement

Slabs On Grade
As indicated previously the project site is generally underlain by a surface layer of
moderately to highly expansive clayey soils Unless slabs on grade constructed above
these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the
lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due
to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting
from swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly
prepared prior to fill placement In addition a minimum of 18 inches of non expansive
select granular fill material should be provided below the building slab areas The
non expansive select granular fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the building
a minimum of 24 inches or extend to the edge of the concrete sidewalk surrounding the
building if applicable
We also recommend that the building slabs on grades be reinforced with
reinforcing bars spaced at least 18 inches in each direction in lieu of the standard welded
wire fabric This recommendation is provided to reduce the potential for structural distress
due to differential movements of the slabs due to the nature of the on site soils
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic or machine vibration
we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of open graded
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs The open graded gravel
cushion fill would provide uniform support of the slabs and would serve as a Capillary
moisture break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration through the slab and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier such as a plastic

In order to reduce the potential for distress due to differential movements between

compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The concrete sidewalk slab may be
supported directly on the 18 inch thick non expansive select granular fill Construction
joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the sidewalk with expansion
joints at right angle intersections
Based on our experience in the Kapolei area minor differential slab movements
between the building slab and the abutting sidewalk slabs have been observed on several
occasions We believe that this may be attributed to the lack of maintenance of the
sidewalk subgrade moisture content after the initial subgrade preparation It should be
noted that the moisture content requirement of the clayey subgrades at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture is an important requirement considering the moderately to
highly expansive nature of the on site clayey soils Therefore the subgrade soils
underneath the sidewalk should be properly moisture conditioned and maintained moist
until the placement of the select granular fill and concrete In addition consideration may
be given to structurally connecting the two abutting slabs with dowels or other structural
connections especially at the entrances to the building and other openings in the walls
It should be emphasized that the areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled
tightly against the slab edges with low expansion relatively impervious soils These areas
should also be graded to divert water away from the slabs and to reduce the potential for
water ponding around the slabs and foundations

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site and or used in
landscape areas where appropriate Contract documents should include additive
and deductive unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Our field observations indicate that abundant shrinkage cracks and ground
depressions are present at the existing ground surface across the project site In
general shrinkage cracks will develop in the ground due to the lack of maintenance
of the soil moisture content as the surface soils start to dry out under the sun and
dry weather conditions The development of shrinkage cracks in the ground was
apparently intensified by the prolonged dry climatic conditions characteristic of the
Kapolei area resulting in large fluctuations of the soil moisture content
If the existing ground cracks are left unattended for an extended period of time and
are allowed to penetrate deeper below the surface the ground cracks could
adversely affect the performance of the future building foundations The ground
cracks could serve as conduits for water to percolate into the foundation soils and
cause loss of strength of the foundation soils resulting in potential settlement of the
building structure In addition migration of the foundation soils into the openings of
the shrinkage cracks could undermine the foundation system and result in potential
settlement of the building structure Therefore remedial work to correct or mitigate
the observed ground cracks should be implemented prior to fill placement and
construction of the proposed structures

After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades should be proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting
and collapsing near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks
and ground depressions The exposed subgrades should be proof rolled without
moisture conditioning of the subgrade soils at the pre existing moisture contents
Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation should be backfilled with
general fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The proof rolling operations should be conducted under the near continuous
observation of a representative from Geolabs
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades should then be scarified
to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the subgrade soil be prepared again as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted fill The need for over excavation due to soft
subgrade soil conditions should be evaluated by a representative from Geolabs in
the field
Fill Materials
Based on the existing topography at the project site new compacted fills up to
about 5 feet thick may be required to raise the existing ground to the finished
grades of the proposed development Imported general fill materials should consist
of materials with particle sizes of 3inches or less in maximum dimension and
should be free of deleterious materials The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 8 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 2 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should be noted that the general fill
requirements presented herein are intended as guidelines only and may be
modified based on additional laboratory testing and field observations on the
available fill materials conducted by Geolabs
The 12 and 18 inch thick non expansive select granular fill required under the
pavement areas and building sidewalk slabs should consist of non expansive select
granular material such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well
graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select granular fill materials recommended below the building
slabs and sidewalk slabs should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction Aggregate base course and subbase
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of the earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs during the site grading operations to assist the contractor in
obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content on
each fill lift Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort
should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the
specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture content requirement of
the general fills and subgrades at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture is
an important requirement considering the moderately to highly expansive nature of
the clayey soils
