INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed bridge crossing and access road for the Maintenance
Building of the Royal Hawaiian Country Club Phase II Course in Maunawili Kailua on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our fee proposal dated May 16 1991
This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and our geotech
nical engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration The recommendations are intended for design input and
are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed bridge will be about 180 feet long spanning across Makawao
Stream It is our understanding that one abutment will be provided on each side of
Makawao Stream to support the proposed bridge A center pier will also be provided
near the stream bottom to support the middle portion of the bridge
Site grading involving cuts and fills will be required for the proposed access road
construction Excavations up to about ten 10 feet deep below the existing grade will be
provided for the construction of the approximately 526 feet long access road Fills behind
the northern bridge abutment will be approximately sixteen 16 feet above the existing
ground surface

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the earthwork and foundation design of the proposed bridge crossing and
access road
The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
May 16 1991 The scope of work comprised the following work items
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the site conditions
3 Drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 21 5 to 51 6 feet below
the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing earthworks and foundation design recommendations

6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design recommenda

Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the Phase Il golf course of the Royal Hawaiian
Country Club in Maunawili Kailua on the island of Oahu The southern side of the project
site is bounded by an existing private road adjacent to the southern bank of Makawao
Stream The Makawao Stream is about 40 feet wide and flows in the westerly direction
To the north of the stream the site rises from approximately 100 feet to 148 feet above
Mean Sea Level along the center line of the proposed access road
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths ranging from 21 5 to 51 6 feet below the existing ground surface within the
proposed project boundary The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered alluvium consisting of stiff clayey silt with
weathered basalt gravel to the maximum depth of the drilled borings Layers of boulders
and cobbles were also encountered randomly in the drilled borings
The groundwater levels were encountered in the borings at depths ranging from
6 0 to 36 8 feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It appears that the underlying alluvium consisting of stiff clayey
silt with weathered basalt gravel boulders and cobbles can provide adequate support for
shallow foundations for the abutments provided the recommendations presented herein
are followed
Due to the potential for deep scouring of the river bed from Makawao Stream it
is recommended that pile foundations be utilized to support the center pier of the
proposed bridge
Detailed recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Site Grading
It is Our understanding that excavations and fills will be required for the
construction of the proposed bridge crossing and access road

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation rubbish and other deleterious materials The resulting
grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
Fill Material
The field exploration and laboratory test results indicated that the on site soils have
high in place moisture contents ranging from about 40 to 60 percents which are
well above the optimum moisture content approximately 34 to 38 percent
Therefore drying of the excavated material may be required prior to its use as a
fill material
The excavated on site material less than eight 8 inches in diameter may be used
for fill material
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Since the site is located in a high rainfall area and the in situ soil have high in place
moisture content the normal 90 compaction standard could be modified to 85
for fill consisting of in situ soil placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to maximum
10 inches thick The fill backfill consisting of granular material such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up
to maximum 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
All fill or backfill below the water level should consist of clean granular material
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 This material is generally self
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2722 00 Page 7
compacting Additional densification may be obtained by use of a vibratory
compactor on the final level of the material
Cut slopes of one and one haif horizontal to one vertical are
recommended Care should be exercised not to over cut the slopes since
it will be very difficult and expensive to patch or to provide other remedial
measures for the over cut slopes
Fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are recommended Fills
to be placed on existing slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to
one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm in situ soil to
lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement and compaction requirement Fill slopes should be constructed
by overfilling beyond the fill slope and subsequent cutting to final grades in
order to secure a stable fill slope
An eight 8 foot wide bench is recommended for cut or fill slopes having
vertical heights in excess of twenty 20 feet
Water should be diverted away from the top of the slopes Slope planting
should also be utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the
finished slope surfaces

Subdrains are recommended at existing natural drainageways and locations where
seepage is encountered during site grading If required subdrains should be
installed prior to fill placement in order to minimize potential seepage accumulation
and subsequent weakening of the new embankment fill materials
Abutment Foundation
The borings in the abutment locations generally encountered stiff clayey silt with
decomposed gravel to the maximum drilled depth Spread footing foundations bearing
on the stiff in situ soils may be utilized to support the bridge abutments
An allowable bearing capacity up to 4 000 p s f may be used for footings bearing
on the stiff in situ soil or properly compacted fill A minimum footing embedment of three
3 feet is recommended However additional embedment is recommended for footings
on or adjacent to a slope to provide a minimum ten 10 feet horizontal set back distance
measured from the footing toe to the slope face
We recommend that the excavated footing subgrade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to
provide uniform foundation support
If soft or loose soil conditions are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation the soft and loose soils should be over excavated to stiff and firm natural
ground The resulting depressions should then be backfilled with select borrow coral
crusher waste or base course material compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 78
Estimated footing settlements under the anticipated abutment loads are on the
order of less than one 1 inch total

Alternate Foundation
Pile foundations were evaluated for support of the proposed bridge abutments as
an alternate to footing foundation The following paragraphs provide the
preliminary design considerations Additional recommendations will be provided
if it is decided to use pile foundation to support the abutments
Based on the boring information many boulders and cobbles were encountered
throughout the drilled depths and appeared to be prevalent below the existing
stream bottom elevation of 94 feet MSL Therefore difficulties during pile
installation such as early refusal or high driving blow counts may be anticipated if
predrilling is not provided As a result some piles may not achieve enough
penetration to satisfy erosion scouring considerations
Therefore for driven pile foundations designed to support the bridge abutments
it is recommended that predrilling be utilized to facilitate pile driving through the
boulders and cobbles Predrilling down to an elevation of 85 feet MSL is
recommended at each pile location The predrilled hole should be properly
backfilled with sand after placement of pile and prior to driving The backfill is
important in providing lateral resistance of the pile
For preliminary design of pile foundations sixteen and one half 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with a vertical Capacity of 60 tons
may be used The piles may bear on boulders and cobbles in the stiff alluvium
A lateral pile capacity of 10 kips per pile may be used if the predrilled holes are
properly backfilled prior to driving

Lateral Earth Loads
The retaining walls for grade separation and abutment walls of the proposed bridge
will be subjected to lateral earth loads It is our opinion that the abutment walls should
be designed for at rest condition walls restrained at the top For retaining wall and
abutment wall design the following lateral earth pressures are recommended
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent fluid
Active condition pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equivalent fluid
At rest condition pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should be included where necessary
It is recommended that granular backfill material such as select borrow or coral
should be used behind the retaining and abutment walls to reduce the compaction effort
required The granular backfill materials should be compacted to a minimum of 90
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D or
weepholes should be provided to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the
walls
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads may be obtained by passive pressure developed by the
earth against the buried portions of the retaining and abutment walls We recommend
that a passive earth resistance of 300 p c f may be used where the ground in front of the
wall is level If a sloping ground of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V is in front of the
wall a reduced passive earth resistance of 150 p c f may be used

Center Pier Foundation
Since the center pier is located within the stream bed there is a high potential for
scour around the pier Therefore it is our recommendations that pile foundations should
be utilized to support the proposed center pier of the subject bridge
Pile Capacities
Twelve 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable
vertical pile capacity of 40 tons per pile may be used The piles may develop the
bearing capacity by a combination of end bearing on the underlying bouldery
alluvium and frictional resistance from the pile shafts embedded in the surrounding
alluvium A minimum pile spacing of three 3 feet on centers is recommended
An allowable vertical uplift capacity of 10 kips per pile and a lateral pile Capacity of
10 kips per pile are recommended
Pile Lengths
Based on the exploration results a minimum pile length of 25 feet below the
existing ground surface is recommended Normally a test pile program is
recommended to determine the actual lengths of pile to be ordered However due
to the limited number of piles required to support the proposed center pier a test
pile program may not be practical Instead a somewhat longer pile length may be
used Therefore a precast pile length of 40 feet is recommended
Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of about 30 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2722 00 Page 12
energy level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be performed at
a reduced energy level to minimize the development of tensile stresses within the
piles which could cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within loose sandy soil or soft
clayey silt the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced
energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach 3 blows per inch for the last three inches or when
aggregated blow counts are 50 blows for the last foot A minimum pile
embedment length of 15 feet below the cutoff elevation is recommended

Erosion Protection
The bridge abutment footings will be located at areas adjacent to Makawao
Stream which are susceptible to periodic flooding Therefore it is our opinion that some
amount of erosion protection should be provided
A CRM facing or 4 inch thick concrete lining reinforced with welded wire mesh
installed in areas susceptible to periodic flooding could provide resistance to scouring
A 6 inch layer of base course material is recommended below the lining to provide a filter
layer between the lining and underlying soil
The lining should also incorporate cutoff walls along the ends in order to minimize
scouring below the lining The cutoff walls should extend a minimum of 3 feet below the
water level and ground surface
Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the levei one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading footing excavation and pile driving
of the project This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design
concepts or specifications and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by taping from
objects indicated on the topographic survey map undated transmitted by GMP
Associates Inc Elevations of the field borings were interpreted from the same map The
physical location and the elevation of the field borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
