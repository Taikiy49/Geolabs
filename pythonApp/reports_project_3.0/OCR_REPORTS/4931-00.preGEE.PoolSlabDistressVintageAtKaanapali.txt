PRELIMINARY GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
POOL SLAB DISTRESS VINTAGE AT KAANAPALI
Dear Mr Asao
In accordance with our recommendations and fee estimate dated May 20 2002
and your verbal authorization to proceed on we have performed a
preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration for the pool slab distress at the Vintage at
Kaanapali in the Kaanapali area on the Island of Maui Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents our
findings and preliminary recommendations for the proposed project These findings and
recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Vintage at Kaanapali is a residential subdivision located in Kaanapali town on
the Island of Maui Hawaii The swimming pool is located at the recreation area within the
subdivision A 5 inch thick concrete deck slab surrounds the pool and a spa is located
about 4 feet from the southwest corner Beyond the concrete deck slab is a landscaped
planter which outlines the perimeter of the pool area At the edge of the planter the soil is
about 1 to 3 inches above the bottom of the concrete deck slab
The General Manager for the Vintage at Kaanapali recently observed problems
with the concrete deck slab Beginning in about October of 2001 the concrete deck slab
on the northwest side of the swimming pool began to uplift During the next six months
uplifting of the concrete slab was also observed on the east and west sides of the pool
During a visit to the site in May of 2002 the concrete deck at the northwest side of
the pool was observed to be about 1 25 inches above the concrete trim of the pool The
concrete on the east side of the pool had been previously patched however small cracks
were visible and were not previously noticed by the General Manager or the contractor
The concrete deck on the west side of the pool near the spa area was uplifted about

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain
an overview of the surface and shallow subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface
information obtained was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the evaluation of possible
causes of the slab distress To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Hand drilling and sampling of four probes extending to depths of about 21 to
24 inches below the existing pool deck
2 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a geologist
from our firm
3 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
4 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering opinions pertaining to the possible causes of the deck damage
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and preliminary recommendations
6 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
7 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and Logs of Probes are presented on
Pilates 3 and 4 of this letter report The results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soil samples are presented on Plate 5

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of hand drilling and sampling four probes
designated as B 1 through B 4 extending to depths of about 21 to 24 inches below the
existing pool deck The concrete pool deck was first cored then a hand auger was used
to advance the probe holes Both undisturbed and disturbed samples were obtained from
the probes The approximate locations of the probes drilled at the site are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the probes were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture plasticity and swell potential The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field
exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results
are presented on the Logs of Probes at the appropriate sample depths
Eight 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized on
Plate 5
One Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Probes at the appropriate sample depth
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the geotechnical information for the project indicated that the
subsurface conditions generally consisted of a thin layer of residual soil over weathered
rock formation it is our understanding that the pooi was excavated into weathered rock
formation Therefore our field exploration concentrated on the near surface soil
conditions since we believed that the pool deck movements would be related to the
near surface soil instead of the rock formation
Our field exploration encountered 5 inches of concrete over 2 to 4 inches of gravel
cushion underlain by medium to stiff silty clay to about 24 inches below the pool deck
surface

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field observations indicate that portions of the concrete pool deck slabs have
been uplifted The laboratory swell tests indicate that most of the silty clay soils under the
pool deck have low swell potential however isolated pockets of moderately expansive soil
may be present The swell potential of the soil may also increase with higher density
increased compaction of the soil Therefore it is our opinion that the distress noted in
the concrete pool deck is due to swelling of localized pockets of moderately expansive soil
Generally options for remedial work may consist of complete removal and
replacement of the existing silty clay soils below the concrete pool deck or limited removal
and replacement of the affected areas with the understanding that some future
movements may occur
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field probes Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the probes may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field probes indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the sketch from Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company
on May 20 2002 The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings at the time of our exploration
However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to tidal
fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the contractor and
owner in the evaluation of possible causes of the noted distress Therefore this report
may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for
preparation of final design documents or construction cost estimates
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
