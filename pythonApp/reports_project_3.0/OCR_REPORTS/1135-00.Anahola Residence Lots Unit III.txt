INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for
use in the design of the site grading for the proposed subdivision
development The location of the project site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The scope of this investigation included the drilling of three test
borings and six hand auger probes visual observations of the existing
site conditions laboratory testing and engineering analysis

The project site is located on the southeast coast of the island of
Kauai The bulk of the island was built during the Pliocene by a
single shield volcano extruding basaltic lavas These lavas have been
designated as the Waimea Volcanic Series Following the cessation of
this main shield building phase the island was subjected to a long
period of erosion and subsidence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1135 00 Page 4
During the Pleistocene volcanism was renewed and new basaltic
lavas mantled most of the eastern portion of the island These
post erosional volcanics have been designated as the Koloa Volcanic
Series
The project site is underlain by residual soils developed from the
in situ weathering of Koloa lavas
Field Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the truck accessible portion of the site
to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface in order to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing
In addition six hand auger probes were drilled to depths of 6 0 feet
below the existing ground surface to provide supplemental subsurface
information in areas inaccessible to truck mounted drilling equipment

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of grading the site should be
thoroughly cleared of vegetation boulders rubbish and other
deleterious materials This grubbed matérial should be disposed
of properly off site
In areas designated to receive fill the ground should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to optimum
moisture content and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78

Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to minimize
ground settlement along the utility alignments On site material
generally less than 6 inches in size may be utilized as backfill All
backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of the on site material
is fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction by water
jetting or ponding alone and therefore would require mechanical
compaction

Pavement
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavements will be used for
the project
Laboratory CBR tests indicated a range of values between 10 and 19
with swells between 0 and 3 2 percent This wide range indicates that
the pavement sections may vary from 8 to 14 inches thick
For preliminary cost estimate the following preliminary pavement
section may be utilized
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The actual pavement sections to be used should be determined by
additional CBR tests performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered at the time of construction
The above recommended section also considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 3 borings to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface using truck mounted drilling equipment In areas
inaccessible to vehicles the site was explored by drilling
6 additional probes to a depth of 6 0 feet utilizing hand
augers The approximate locations of the borings and probes
are shown on the enclosed site plans Plates 2 and 3
The soils rock encountered were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who contin
uously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the materials encountered is presented on the Logs
of Borings and Hand Auger Probes Plates A 1 through A 5S
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving

barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Four 4 laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate its subgrade
Support capacity as compacted fill The test results are
summarized on Plate B l
Two 2 sieve analyses were performed as an aid in the
classification of the on site soils The test results are
shown on Plate B l
One 1 Atterberg limit test was performed as a further
aid in the classification of the on site soils The test
result is shown on Plate B l
Two 2 procter tests as specified in ASTM D 1557
were performed to determine the optimum moisture content for
Iaximum density The test results are shown on Plate B l
Three 3 direct shear tests were performed to determine
the allowable bearing capacity and slope stability The test
results are shown on Plates B 2 through B 4
Seven 7 swell tests were performed on undisturbed
and remolded one inch thick ring sample to evaluate the
expansion characteristics of the soil The test results are
summarized on Plate B 5
