In accordance with the request from R M Towill Corporation a soil resistivity
survey was conducted for the Phase 1 development of the above referenced project in
the Kapolei area of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii Our work was
performed in general accordance with the Professional Services Agreement entered into
on This report presents the results of our soil resistivity survey and
summarizes our work on this portion of the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development in
the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 As part of the infrastructure
installation for the Phase 1 development it is proposed to install roughly 8 500 lineal feet
of new 4 8 and 12 inch waterlines along the planned roadway alignments within the
project site It is our understanding that ductile iron pipe and or PVC pipe would be used
for the waterline construction
Based on the preliminary project plans and profiles we understand that the new
waterlines will be installed at approximately 3 to 6 feet below the existing ground level
The ground surface elevations in the project area range from about 55 to 60 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were
performed to evaluate the corrosion rating of the near surface soils within the depths of
the proposed water line

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in
the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The
scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and Field Engineer to and from
the project site
2 Performance of 69 in situ soil resistivity tests at 23 separate locations Test
Nos R 1 through R 23 along the planned roadway alignments The test
locations have been staked out in the field by R M Towill Corporation prior
to our field mobilization These test locations generally correspond to the
300 foot interval maximum required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
3 Coordination of the field work by a Project Engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the electrical resistivity data pertinent to the
corrosion characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of three copies of this letter report summarizing our work on this
portion of the project and presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
December 9 and 10 1996 for the field survey Our field work was performed under sunny
weather and generally dry ground surface conditions Surface soil conditions were
observed to consist of mainly clayey materials

Sixty nine soil resistivity tests were conducted with intervals ranging from about
250 to 300 feet at 23 locations at the project site using the in Pin Method
and a resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The
resistance meter is capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output power into the
ground The method utilizes four surface electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity
an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivities of the
soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of
the waterline were assessed by using this method at the approximate test locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed waterline alignment The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test
location with the electrodes aligned at equal horizontal spacing of generally 2 to 7 feet
between electrodes
The resistance meter impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes causing
an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 3 through 7 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects However it should be noted that soil resistivity may be influenced by
temperature variations weather conditions and other factors
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
