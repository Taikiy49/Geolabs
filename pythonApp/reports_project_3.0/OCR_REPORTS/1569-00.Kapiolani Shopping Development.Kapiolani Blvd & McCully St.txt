In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the above subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project

our proposal of August 1 1985

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed development consists of a two story commercial
office building with a full basement approximately 50 by 340 feet in
plan
W Page 2
The building will occupy the rear half of the site A parking lot
is planned for the rest of the area
For future development not included in this project a fast foods
restaurant will be located at the western corner of the site adjacent to
Kapiolani Boulevard
It is our understanding that the existing ground surface elevations
at the building will be raised from about 4 0 feet MSL to the finished
grade of about 9 0 feet MSL and the finished ground would slope gently
towards Kapiolani Boulevard The fill will be supported by a short
retaining wall at the front property line of the site
A general layout of the development is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for
the design of the structure foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 3
The main objectives of the investigation were
l to determine the settlement properties of the loose harbor
sediments lagoonal deposits to evaluate the potential
settlement of the structure and the fill and
2 to determine if suitable bearing layers are present at deeper
depths for pile foundation design should the anticipated
ground and foundation settlement exceed tolerable limits
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling five 5
to 65 feet below the existing ground surface Also included
were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is situated on the mauka side of Kapiolani
Boulevard between McCully and Pumehana Streets and is presently used as
a new car lot

The property limit at the back of the site is lined by a 200 foot
chain link fence together with two 78 foot short tile walls on both
sides The wall at the north eastern side is about 2 feet high while the
wall at the northwestern portion of the site is about 4 5 feet high A
4 story building and several one story houses are also located in close
proximity to this property line
The site is relatively flat and the ground slopes down slightly
toward the north Ground surface elevations at the limit of the proposed
structure are about 4 feet MSI
Ground cover consists mainly of asphalt concrete pavement and lawn
grass Several trees are also existing in the site

End Bearing Pile Foundation
Since a suitable end bearing layer is present at relatively shallow
depths we recommend that end bearing pile foundations be used to support
the structure
The piles should be used to support the office building in
conjunction with a structural slab at the bottom of the basement They
would develop their load capacity primarily from end bearing on the hard
coral formation
Sixteen and one half inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles are
recommended The piles should extend into the hard coral formation
anticipated at a total depth of about 17 to 22 feet
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 100 blows per
foot for the last foot or 10 blows per inch for the last 2 inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 100
tons per pile may be used However it is our understanding that the
site filling may be conducted simultaneously with the foundation
construction therefore an allowance for potential downdrag due to the
5 0 feet fill is required The allowable pile capacity should be reduced
to 86 tons per pile to allow for the downdrag forces Pile settlements
on the order of less than one half of an inch are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot of depth The backfill against
the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with select
materials compacted tc a dry density of at least 95 of the maximum
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Basement Excavation and Construction
The anticipated depth of excavation is about 6 feet The method
and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the contractor
within practical limits
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for the soils above the
coral Approximately 2 feet of excavation would extend into the loose
deposits underwater
Excavation Support
It is our opinion that the excavation in the soft soils may
require sheet piling or other supports It is important that
adequate sheeting work be installed prior to the excavation and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 10
maintained tight against the excavated surfaces with proper bracing
to minimize lateral movements of the adjacent ground into the
excavation and resulting settlements of adjacent structures
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with the anticipated loading conditions existing adjacent
properties traffic construction materials stockpile and
equipment and to satisfy the applicable safety requirements The
design and safety of the excavation supports should be the
responsibility of the contractor
The sheeting should extend down to the coral layer to increase the
stability of the excavation bottom and to minimize the possibility
of bottom heaving or quick conditions
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks cr dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the excavated surfaces Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas where the adjacent
properties especially the 4 story building the tile walls and
other utilities are adjacent to the excavation It is our
understanding that the 4 story building was supported on about
30 foot length piles

Groundwater levels were noted at shallow depths throughout the
project site The method of dewatering should be left open to the
contractor provided adequate stability of the excavation is
maintained The deposits encountered in the borings consisted
mostly of silty coral gravels which are moderately permeable with
portions susceptible to quick conditions
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with the excavation shoring support so that the
stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or
softening the bottom of the excavation Based on the soil
classification it appears that the permeability of the silty
gravelly soils may vary from 1074 to 1071 cm per second
Lateral Pressures
The lateral earth pressure acting on the basement will depend on
the type of backfill used the extent of the backfill material and the
compactive effort required on the material behind the structure walls
Granular material such as the select on site sandy coral gravel is
recommended for the backfill adjacent to the structures in order to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 12
reduce the compactive effort required and to minimize the build up of
additional lateral earth pressures
The basement should be designed to resist lateral earth pressure
plus applicable surcharge load from the existing adjacent properties
anticipated parking vehicles traffic construction materials stockpile
and equipments
We believe that a lateral earth pressure from the soils alone of 55
p s f per linear foot of depth may be used for the design of the portion
of the basement above water and a lateral earth pressure from scils
alone of 25 p s f per linear foot in excess of hydrostatic pressure may
be used for that below water
Site Grading
The existing grade of about 4 feet MSL at the building limit is
proposed to be filled to the finished grade of about 9 0 feet MSI and
the finished ground would slope generally towards the south There
fore a total of about 2 to 5 0 feet of compacted select material would
be required for the proposed parking lot

Fill Material
Except for the lagoonal deposits the organic clays and silts most
of the excavated on site sandy and gravelly soils can be re used as
a source of fill material below the base course of the pavement
All imported fill material should consist of select non expansive
silty sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with
less than 20 passing the 200 sieve The fill material should be
placed in maximum of 10 inch horizontal loose lifts and campacted
to about 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fill Settlements
Based on our findings we believe that total primary settlements of
the order of six inches may occur due to the fill A large
percentage of these settlements would probably occur within a
period of about three months after placement of the fill

construction of the pavement for the parking lot We recommend
that a 6 inch to 12 inch additional fill of compacted select
material be placed above the proposed finished grade
Surcharge Fill
If practicable a surcharge program may be used to accelerate the
settlements and reduce the long term settlements If sufficient
surcharge fill can be placed over the fill it is our opinion that
a large percentage of the anticipated long term ground settlements
due to the fill could be eliminated
The total height of surcharge should be at least 2 5 feet above the
proposed finished grade of 9 0 feet MSL The length of the
surcharge period will depend on the amount of surcharge used If
this 2 5 foot high surcharge is used our estimated surcharge time
should be on the order of 1 to months
Fill Settlement Monitoring
Settlement gauges should be installed in the area and periodic
survey readings of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual
settlement The readings should be taken by the project surveyor and
transmitted to the soils engineer to determine the actual surcharge time
required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 15
It should be understood that although a surcharge fill might help
to minimize primary settlement due to the fill long term secondary
settlements on the order of 1 inch may still occur
Utility Construction
As a result of the anticipated settlements it should be pointed
out that construction of utility lines should be delayed after a large
percentage of the settlement has occured and they should be connected
with flexible connections to the structure Additionally the pavement
areas should be designed with positive slopes to drain away from the
structures

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of scil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Four sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples
of the subsoils to evaluate its grain size distribution The test
results are presented on Plate B l
Five consclidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of
subsoils The test results are presented on Plates B 2 through B 6
One direct shear test and two unconsolidated undrained
triaxial tests were performed on selected undisturbed samples to evaluate
the shear strength parameters of the subsoils The test results are
plotted on Plates B 7 through B 9
