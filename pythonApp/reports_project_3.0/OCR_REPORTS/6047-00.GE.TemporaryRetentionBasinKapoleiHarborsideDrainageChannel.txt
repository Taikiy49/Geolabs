Project Considerations
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to alleviate
the flooding problem in the Kapolei area by constructing a temporary retention basin
along a portion of the future Kapolei Harborside Drainage Channel alignment situated
between Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kalaeloa Harbor in the Kapolei area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The materials generated from the excavations into the future drainage
channel alignment will be stockpiled as an embankment adjacent to the temporary
retention basin We envision that the excavated materials will be segregated into
granular and cohesive soils during the embankment construction to facilitate future use
as borrow soils

400 feet wide in plan dimensions Based on the grading plan provided we understand
that the finish grades at the bottom of the temporary retention basin will be set at about
6 to 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the existing topography at the site
excavations on the order of about 20 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface are
anticipated for the temporary retention basin construction
We understand that the excavated materials will be stockpiled adjacent to the
temporary retention basin by constructing an embankment measuring approximately
2 000 feet long and 600 feet wide Based on the grading plan provided the finish grade
at the top of the embankment is set at about 55 feet MSL Based on the existing

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
In general the previous field exploration conducted within the project limits
generally encountered a surface fill layer up to about 5 feet thick The surface fill layer
generally consisted of soft to medium stiff sandy silts and very stiff clayey silts underlain
by coralline deposits extending to depths ranging from about 30 to 40 feet below the
existing ground surface The coralline deposits generally consisted of very soft to stiff
sandy clay very loose to dense sands and gravels soft clayey silt with sand and gravel
soft to hard coral formation and hard sandstone formation In two of the borings an
alluvial deposit was encountered below the coralline deposits extending to the
maximum depth drilled of about 42 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
alluvium was comprised of medium stiff clay and silty clay
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of the previous field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate depending on tidal
fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors
Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Based on the results of our previous field exploration as well as our knowledge
and experience in the project vicinity the proposed temporary retention basin site
appears to be underlain by surface fill coralline deposits and alluvial soils These
subsurface conditions should provide adequate support for the proposed retention
basin drainage channel project provided that the recommendations presented herein
are followed
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions we envision that conventional
earthmoving equipment may be used for excavation of the surface fills and the
underlying alluvial deposits It is anticipated that deeper portions of the channel
excavation will encounter dense sandstone and hard coral ledge and or formation For

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing in areas
designated to receive fills should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that they
meet fill material requirements
Areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moisture
conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at

Keying and Benching
Although the topography at the site is relatively flat there are local areas of
steeper slopes Therefore keying and benching is recommended for fills that will
be placed at the site in areas where the existing slope is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
Based on the site conditions encountered we recommend moisture conditioning
and proof rolling the keys and benches prior to the placement of the
embankment fill with a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment for a
minimum of six passes to help verify that adequate support for the fill has been
achieved The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a
Geolabs representative Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during
clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with compacted fill
materials
Fill Materials
We envision that the temporary retention basin will be constructed by excavating
into the existing ground New fills to raise the existing ground surface for the
retention basin drainage channel construction are not anticipated for the project
If new fills are required for the retention basin drainage channel construction we
recommend utilizing select fill material with proper slope and channel protection
Fill materials should consist of non expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a minimum CBR value of 10 The fill material should also
be free of vegetation and deleterious materials Geolabs should observe and or
test imported fill materials for their suitability prior to being transported to the site
for the intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and

Excavation
The temporary retention basin drainage channel excavation will penetrate the
near surface soils and may involve cuts into the underlying coral and sandstone
formation It is anticipated that most of the excavation work may be accomplished
with normal heavy excavation equipment such as bulldozers and scrapers
However excavations into the coral and sandstone formation may require the
use of heavy ripping or hoerams If heavy excavation is required care should be
exercised to reduce the potential of overbreak particularly in the retention
basin channel walls and floor
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are
based on field data obtained from the field exploration performed at the project
site Contractors bidding on this project should be encouraged to examine the
site conditions and soil data to make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our previous field
exploration it appears that most of the cut slopes would expose stiff alluvial soils
medium dense coralline sands and gravel to hard coral and sandstone
formation In general we believe that cut slopes for the retention basin drainage
channel may be made at an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter provided that erosion
control and slope protection is provided for the cut slopes in the final
configuration
Fill slopes for the embankment construction should be designed with a slope
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter If necessary slope benches are recommended at
maximum 15 foot vertical height intervals If the existing ground surface is
steeper that 5H 1V fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope as
discussed above to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations

Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design

Slope and Channel Protection
Our previous field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by alluvial soils
at varying depths below the existing ground surface Where alluvial soils are
exposed in the retention basin channel walls and subject to water flow the
alluvial soils should be protected by riprap or other lining when the drainage
channel is in its final configuration
It is our opinion that the overall stability of the proposed channel cut slope is
satisfactory However erosion will occur where the coral formation has minimal
cementation and the water velocity is high Therefore maintenance will be
required following large storm events This is typical of unlined channels in coral
If the channel is to be virtually maintenance free then a structural lining should
be considered
If it is desired to keep the channel unlined then periodic maintenance will be
required In order to reduce the maintenance selective portions of the channel
could be lined to reduce erosion Slope protection lining should be installed
where the water velocities would be high such as the outlets of culverts into the
channel and where large areas of coral with minimal cementation are
encountered
Shotcrete grouted rubble pavement GRP and articulating concrete blocks may
be considered for the channel lining The costs for these alternatives are believed
to be similar with shotcrete probably a little less costly than the others The
shotcrete and GRP are probably more flexible in terms of installation in localized
areas since the articulating concrete blocks need to be installed in mats covering
the entire slope The articulating blocks are desirable due to its ability to allow
water percolation into the subsurface It is our opinion that shotcrete lining may
be most appropriate for the drainage channel at its final configuration
