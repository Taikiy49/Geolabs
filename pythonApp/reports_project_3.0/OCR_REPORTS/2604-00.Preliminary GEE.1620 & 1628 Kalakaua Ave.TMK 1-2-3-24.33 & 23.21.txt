INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed to provide the basis for a pre purchase evaluation of the two
Subject properties at 1620 and 1628 Kalakaua Avenue respectively The general
locations of the property sites of these two subject lots are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data
obtained from two 2 borings one each drilled in the two subject properties These
recommendations are intended to provide input for the evaluation of the purchase of the
subject properties under consideration and as such are not intended for development
or design purposes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the available boring data it appears that the subsoil conditions at the
project site are generally competent and could provide support for either shallow or deep
foundations depending on the types of structures to be built and the anticipated structural
GEOLABS HAWAII

loadings The bearing value required for foundation design will depend on the final
column loads and uniformity of the underlying subsoils within the project site
Shallow Foundations
Based on the preliminary boring data it is our opinion that shallow spread or
continuous wall footing foundations may be used to support one 1 to two 2 story light
weight structures of timber post and beam construction For heavier masonry structures
and or tall buildings higher than two 2 stories pile foundations should be considered
if footing settlements exceeding one 1 inch or more are not acceptable
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 000 p s f may be used for spread or
continuous footings bearing on the upper fill crust with a minimum footing embedment of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The thickness and uniformity of the
upper fill crust material supporting the shallow footing foundations should be verified by
additional exploration and soil testing when the scope of construction is more fully
defined
Footing settlements are estimated to be less than one inch total provided that the
above recommendations are followed and that the column load is limited to about 45 kips
or less
Pile Foundation
Based on our preliminary field exploration a short pile foundation system appears
to be the most feasible means to transmit heavy multi level structural loads to the
underlying coral formation The thickness uniformity of and the depth to the underlying

Additional Exploration
The proposed project site is located adjacent to the existing Makiki Stream channel
and at the junction of at the old Kaheka and Makiki alluvial channels The scope of the
present exploration was extremely limited one boring at each property and hence cannot
