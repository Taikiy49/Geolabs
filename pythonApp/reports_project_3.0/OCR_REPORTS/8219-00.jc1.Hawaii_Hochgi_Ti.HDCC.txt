We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering Earthwork Observation and 
Compaction Testing report for work performed during the site grading operation of the

dated March 25, 2020. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Hawaii Hochi Ti project is located at 2736 Waiwai Loop in the Honolulu 
area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The 
construction involved regrading and paving for the new entrance, load dock, driveway, and 
parking lots within the strip between two existing concrete/CMU metal warehouse buildings, 
as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The site grading consisted of minor cuts and fills generally less than 5 feet. JJS 
Construction performed the earthwork operations for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a smooth vibratory drum roller or jumping jack.

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with compacted imported select borrow material. 
In general, the subgrade was scarified and recompacted to a minimum of 90 
percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. The 
fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density.  In the 
roadway/parking areas, the subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for the project, including compaction of entrance, ramp, driveway, and 
parking lots.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.  These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering November 6, 2020 through 
August 17, 2021. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for Hawaii Hochi project at 2736 Waiwai Loop, 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in 
accordance with the general intent of the plans and requirements in the following
