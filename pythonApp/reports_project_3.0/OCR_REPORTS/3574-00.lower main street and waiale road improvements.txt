INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Lower Main Street and Waiale Road Improvements project in
the Wailuku area on the island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and _ geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations retaining
walls pavement structures and site grading only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Maui the second largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago was formed by the
activity of two volcanoes East Maui or Haleakala and West Maui The broad gently
sloping plain connecting the two volcanoes known as the Maui Isthmus was formed
when lavas of the Haleakala Volcano banked against the already existing West Maui
Volcano
The East Maui Volcano or better known as the Haleakala Mountain makes up the
eastern portion of the island Haleakala Mountain is considered to be dormant with the
last eruption taking place on the southerly side of the mountain around 1790 Haleakala
Mountain consists largely of the Honomanu Volcanic Series that built the mountain from
three rift zones probably during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs
The volcanic rocks of the West Maui Volcano have been divided into three
volcanic series The Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and comprises of the basaltic
lava flows and associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major shield
volcano The Wailuku Volcanic Series was covered by a thin discontinuous frosting of
andesitic and trachytic flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as the Honolua
Volcanic Series After a long period of inactivity where erosion incised deep valleys into
the volcanic flows volcanic activity returned with the eruptions that produced the flows
and cones of the Lahaina Volcanic Series

The project site is situated on the southeastern flank of the West Maui Mountain
This portion of the island is dominated by dune sands and alluvial soils at the surface
Land development and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the project site is
generally underlain by medium dense to dense sands overlying very stiff to hard clayey
and silty soils The generally dense sandy subsoils encountered at the site should
provide adequate support for the proposed replacement bridge and retaining walls on a
shallow foundation system

In order to construct the proposed replacement bridge the existing bridge will need
to be demolished It is our understanding that a temporary crossing structure is not
planned during construction of the replacement bridge We understand that demolition of
the existing abutments and construction of the new abutments will involve excavations on
the order of about 15 feet and subsequent backfilling for the proposed construction

Bridge Foundations
Based on the current design concept the footings for the abutments will be
embedded approximately 5 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to an
elevation of about 205 feet MSL Based on the subsoil conditions encountered in
Boring Nos 3 and 4 drilled near the east abutment of the existing bridge we believe that
a shallow foundation system bearing on the recompacted sandy soils may be used to
support the abutments of the new replacement bridge A bearing pressure of up to
4 000 p s f may be used to evaluate the service limit state of the footings bearing on the
recompacted sandy soils To evaluate the strength limit state ultimate condition of the
footings a bearing pressure of up to 12 000 p s f may be used
As a minimum footings should be embedded at least 2 feet below the lowest
adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3574 00 Page 9
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted sandy soils as recommended herein to be less than
one inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
The sandy subgrade soils below the footings should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density as determined by AASHTO Test Method T 180 ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of
footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm dense
materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or with concrete
Lateral loads acting on the bridge structure may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 400 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the
soil around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of
0 45 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted sandy soils
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Retaining Walls
Based on the information provided we understand that retaining walls will be
needed along the west and northwest sides of the roadway for grade separation In
addition the abutment walls for the replacement bridge should also be designed as
retaining walls to resist the lateral earth pressures due to the adjacent soils and any
surcharge effects caused by loads adjacent to the abutments Foundations for grade
separation retaining walls should be designed in general accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Bridge Foundations section of this report
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due
to adjacent soils and surcharge effects Retaining walls that are restrained at the top from
deflection should be designed for an at rest condition while retaining walls that are free to
deflect at the top may be designed for an active condition Generally where tops of walls
are free to deflect beyond about 0 5 percent of the wall height the wall may be designed
for the active condition Near level backfill conditions are assumed to have slope
inclinations of 5H 1V or flatter The recommended lateral earth pressures for design of
retaining walls expressed in equivalent fluid pressures are presented as follows

At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce
potential contamination of the excavated materials
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing and grubbing

excavation should be backfilled with select fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement of backfill the over excavated
subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling eight
borings to depths of about 16 5 to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 10 The borings were

examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils

APPENDIX B
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 is used as an aid to soil classification and to aid
in evaluating certain properties such as relative saturation The moisture content
expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the tested sample and the unit weight are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Three laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
selected samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils Results of the tests are presented on Plates B 2 through B 4
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the near
surface soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 5
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 6
