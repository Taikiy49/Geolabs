INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations to support the proposed struc
ture
W 1975
Site Description
The proposed site is situated between Wailua Beach Road and Au
Street between their intersections with Aweoweo and Apuhihi
Streets It is composed of three rectangular lots with a com
bined area of approximately 46 400 square feet The grass and
weed covered ground surface is essentially level varying from
a low of approximately elevation 7 7 feet at the northeast
corner to a high of approximately elevation 9 9 feet at the
southeast corner
Proposed Construction
The proposed development will consist of a three story wood
frame structure over a one level parking garage Columns with
spread foundations are planned within the parking structure
Column loads will vary from 55 kips on the exterior to 225 kips
on the central interior columns Portions of the structure
will be supported upon continuous line footings with loadings
of approximately three kips per lineal foot
It is anticipated that the structure will be constructed at or
near the existing grade with minimal cuts of less than five
feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The proposed site is near the shoreline of Mokuleia Beach on
the northern coast of the Island of Oahu The predominant soil
is wind and water deposited calcareous sand containing numerous
shell fragments It is generally underlain at a shallow depth
by coral reef formations
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored using four
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the field explo
ration methods and accompanying laboratory testing is given in
the appendix along with the boring logs and laboratory test
results
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by 3 to 5
feet of sandy fill In all except Boring 2 the fill included
2 to 4 feet of boulders Boulder was not encountered in
Boring 2
The fill is underlain by 3 to in excess of 21 feet of calcare
ous sand with gravel and shells which is in turn underlain by a
W O

was between elevations 2 5 and 1 5 The coral was not
encountered in Boring 1 within a depth of 26 5 feet the limit
of the exploration
The fill has erratic densities varying from loose to medium
dense The sand deposits underlying the fill vary from medium
dense to dense The coral formation was generally hard and
massive but did contain some areas of cemented sand
The groundwater level measured in the borings varied consi
derably between a low of elevation 0 at Boring 1 to a high of
elevation 7 at Boring 2 It appears that the groundwater con

Groundwater should not materially affect construction operations
above elevation 2 It is expected that some wet zones may be
encountered

Foundations
Due to the heterogeneous nature and irregular densities of the
existing fill it will not provide uniform support for the pro
posed foundation loadings The uppermost competent bearing
material is the light tan calcareous coral sand encountered
immediately below the fill This soil has thicknesses varying
from 3 to in excess of 21 feet and was consistently medium dense
to dense This calcareous sand can support allowable contact
bearing pressures of without detrimental settlements
Based upon this loading it is estimated that the maximum
settlements would not exceed l inch total with differential
settlements between adjoining columns of less than 44 inch
The allowable bearing pressure can be increased by one third
when considering seismic and wind loadings The foundations
should bear a minimum of two feet below the final adjacent
grade
W O
Foundation Construction
It is anticipated that excavations into the existing soil will
be difficult due to the presence of boulders within the fill
These materials will also affect the subgrade support capacity
for the parking pavement area It appears that it will be more
expedient to remove the fill prior to the foundation construc
tion The site preparation would consist of removing the exist
ing fill and backfilling with compacted soil without boulders
It is anticipated that the fine grained portion of the on site
soils could be utilized as backfill
Compaction Requirements
All fill within structural areas should be compacted to a mini
mum of 90 per cent of the maximum density established by American
Society for Testing Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557
72T The earthwork placement and compaction should be inspected
to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is consis
tently achieved
Foundations on Recompacted Fill
In lieu of foundations bearing upon the calcareous sand below
the fill the structure could be supported upon foundation bear
ing upon the fill and designed for maximum contact bearing

Floor Slabs
Laboratory testing of the on site surface soils show that they
are non expansive Slab on grade construction can therefore be
utilized without special precautions It is recommended that
all subgrades to receive slabs on grade be compacted to a mini
mum of 90 per cent of the maximum density Due to the granular
nature of the on site soils a granular vapor barrier will not
be required All slabs with moisture sensitive floor coverings
should be protected by a plastic vapor barrier to eliminate
capillary moisture
Retaining Walls
Should low retaining walls be utilized for the proposed struc
tures they should be designed for an equivalent fluid pressure
of 35 pounds per cubic foot and a friction factor of 0 55 if the
on site soils are used as backfill

The backfill should be compacted to between 90 and 95 per cent
of the maximum density
Paving
Based upon the classification of the on site soils and our previous
experience in this area the following pavement section should be
considered for the preliminary design of the paved areas
2 in asphalt concrete
_6 in base course
14 in total pavement thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR greater than 85 The final pavement section should be
based upon the actual subgrade CBR measured during construction

Any earthwork utilized for structural support should also be in

FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
4 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the
borings varied from 11 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 I D or a
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depth
