PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The University of Hawaii West Oahu UHWO Campus covers approximately
103 5 acres and is south and east of Farrington Highway and west of North South Road
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The mass and site grading for the project were completed in August 2012 The
existing clayey subgrade soil was scarified and moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum water content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
compaction prior to fill placement A minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material was placed below the concrete slabs on grade
The Campus Center consists of an L shaped 2 story building with a high attic
located near the center portion of the campus as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
building is supported on shallow spread and continuous footings and concrete
slabs on grade

Based on available drawings there are two 4 inch sewer lines that extend
beneath the Campus Center South Wing building from the west side of the building
One of the sewer lines is located near the center of the South Wing and the other sewer
line is located near the south end of the South Wing building In the southern portion of
the South Wing building runoff water from the roof is piped down using rain gutter pipe
to the existing ground surface At the existing ground surface the rain gutter pipe is
placed inside a collector pipe with gravel The collector pipe is connected to a drain
pipe that runs perpendicular with the building and drains into a ditch area about 50 feet
from the building
Building distress was noted at the lower portion of the Campus Center South
Wing building Based on initial observations it was recommended to perform the
distress study in two phases The first phase would consist of reviewing project
drawings perform a site reconnaissance and floor level survey Based on the results of
the Phase 1 study a Phase 2 exploration would be performed if needed to evaluate
and determine the potential causes of the observed distress
OBSERVED DISTRESSES
The distresses reported were located at the lower portion of the South Wing of the
Campus Center Building The observed distresses were generally located above and on
the sides of door openings along the corners and interior of the walls at the ceiling
corners and between the cabinet and upper baseboard The distresses were noted on
the first and second floor levels of the building We understand that some of the office
doors are difficult to close In addition a distress was noted at the building exterior at the
southwest corner of the building
The observed distresses consisted of separations ranging from hairline to about
¼ inch wide Several of the observed separations were between adjoining gypsum
boards Hairline separations were noted in and between the CMU blocks at the southwest
corner of the building Photographs taken of the observed distresses are presented on
respectively The locations of the photographs are provided on the First Floor Level
Survey Plan and Second Floor Level Survey Plan Plates 3 1 and 3 2 respectively
FLOOR LEVEL SURVEY
A floor level survey was performed on March 22 2016 on the first and second floor
levels of the lower portion of the Campus Center South Wing building The floor level
survey equipment utilizes a closed hydraulic tube system that measures the difference in
pressure between the zero location and other measurement locations to determine the
difference in elevations The zero location was selected at Gridline 16B between
Gridlines J and K where building distresses were not observed

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The results of the floor level survey performed generally indicates that the first floor
level appears to be lifting between Gridlines J and M and between Gridlines 11B and 15B
In addition the first floor level appears to be settling between Gridlines M and N and
between Gridlines 11B to 13B at the southern end of the building For the second floor
level it appears that the floor is lifting between Gridlines G and H and between
Gridlines 11B and 14B Gridline L between Gridlines 14B and 15B and at Gridline M and
Gridline 11B
Based on the floor level survey results it appears that water may have gotten into
the underlying clayey subgrade soils causing the clay soils to swell and soften Based on
our review of the available drawings potential sources of water include the roof drain
system at the southwest side of the South Wing building and the 4 inch sewer lines
running beneath the same area of the building We understand that during construction
rain water was ponding in this area since rain water was flowing out of the connection
pipes at the ground surface and not flowing into the pipes draining into the ditch area
Based on the results of the Phase 1 Study we recommend that a Phase 2 study be
implemented to determine the potential causes of the observed distresses We
recommend that the Phase 2 Study include the following items
1
Drilling and sampling of three borings to depths of about 15 feet below the
existing ground surface Two borings will be drilled on the southwest side of
the South Wing building near the roof drain connection pipes One boring
will be drilled at the southwest corner area The borings will be used to
observe the subgrade condition at these locations
