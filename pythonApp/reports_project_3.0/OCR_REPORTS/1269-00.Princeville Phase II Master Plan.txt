PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore
the subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for
use in planning and design of the proposed Princeville Phase II
development

SITE DESCRIPTION
The Princeville Phase II site is located along the northern coast of
the island of Kauai The area is situated to the north of Kuhio
Highway to the east of the existing Princeville development area and to
the west of the Anini Vista Subdivision The location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
In general the terrain is a rolling plateau which slopes down to
the north at less than 10 grades The plateau is traversed by many
northward tending drainageways with side Slopes that locally approach
50 or steeper The project area is roughly bisected by the Anini
Stream Canyon which has side slopes of about 50 and locally approach
vertical
Along the northern boundary of the project area the ground slopes
down steeply towards the beach area These Slopes range generally
between 50 and 75 percent with some vertical cliffs
The site is currently used as pasture for the grazing of cattle and
horses The area was formerly used for the cultivation of pineapple

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to subdivide the Phase II area into 10 parcels
of which 7 to 8 parcels would be scheduled for single family residences
with the remainder planned for resort and or commercial development A
preliminary layout of these parcels are shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 However the final layout and grading plans had not been prepared at
the time of our investigation It is anticipated that the majority of
grading work will be in conjunction with road construction and filling
of gullies
In addition to the grading work the development will require
construction of underground utilities Included in this utility
construction will be sewage lift stations At this time the locations
and depths of the lift stations are not available However it is
anticipated that the stations will be located in the lower areas and may
range in depth from 20 to 30 feet below grade

Regional Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau along
the north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built during
the late Pliocene and Pleistocent by the extrusion of basaltic lavas
from the Pooku Kapaka and other now obscure vents of the Koloa Volcanic
Series Intercalated with the lava flows are ash beds and related
pyroclastics
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
Soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which
retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
Alluvial soils were encountered along the edges of Anini Stream
Canyon and in the bottoms of several smaller drainageways These
alluvial soils are very similar to the residual soils but frequent ly
have a higher clay content
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
Along the northern boundary of the project area there are steep
slopes approximately 200 feet high which are the erosional remnants of
wave cut sea cliffs formed during a higher stand of the sea The bases
of these cliffs are now protected from wave action by a strand of beach
a colluvial apron and or a fringing reef
Soil Conditions
In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of our drilled borings encountered
a surface layer between 1 and 2 feet thick of stiff highly organic
silt topsoil deposited as a result of the current agricultural usage of
the project site
Below the topsoil layer the borings generally encountered stiff to
very stiff reddish brown clayey silts and silts varying in thickness
between 3 and 28 feet These are residual soils that derived from the
in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally structureless
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered stiff
slightly cemented saprolite soils consisting of silts and sandy silts
having predominantly brown to red brown color mottled with gray or black
spots or streaks Intercalated with the saprolites were discontinuous
beds of ash soils consisting of red stiff silts

In and 20 the surface soils consisted of alluvial
soils consisting of brown stiff clayey silts with trace of sand
Groundwater Conditions
At the time of our exploration groundwater was encountered only in
Boring 12 at a depth of about 29 feet below the ground surface
However numerous soil samples from other test borings showed wet seams
which appear to indicate zones of seepage Additionally previous
explorations in this area had encountered evidences of intermittent and
perennial springs in some of the drainageways Therefore intermittent
perched water conditions may exist in various locales especially during
wet season
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the field exploration encountered generally suitable
subsoils for the proposed development in the project area Generally
shallow spread footing foundation systems could be used for light
structures such as single family homes Conventional grading
techniques with some modifications may be required for the earthwork due
to the relatively high in place moisture content of the soil and the
project being located in a high rainfall area

Site Preparation
At the commencement of earthwork the site should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed of vegetation debris and other deleterious
materials This grubbed material should be removed and wasted
off site In addition the upper 12 to 14 inches of topsoil containing
organic matter should be stripped and stockpiled for use as topsoil
After stripping the area to receive fill the ground should be
scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned and compacted to
densities recommended under FILL PLACEMENT section of this report
Areas of soft soil or pumping detected during site grading should be
excavated to firm natural soil and backfilled with suitable on site
material compacted as stipulated in the following section
Fill Placement
The project site is located in a high rainfall area At the time of
our exploration most of the soils had in situ moisture contents of 30
to 50 within the top 10 feet of the ground and 40 to 65 below the top
10 feet These moisture contents are considerably higher than the ASTM
D 1557 78 optimum moisture contents which ranged from 24 to 29
Therefore considerable amount of drying of the excavated materials will
be required prior to their use as fill materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1269 00 Page 9
Based on our past experience with earthwork in Princeville area
such a requirement does not appear to be difficult to meet for a small
quantity of cut and fill work However in view of the relatively
frequent rainfall that are anticipated in the area throughout the year
drying of large quantities of soils might not be as readily achieved
during mass grading operation It is further recommended that the cut
areas as well as other borrow area be shaped to drain readily to
facilitate drying If weather permits the on site soils for fill use
should be aerated by discing and windrowing for an adequate amount of
time so that the moisture contents can be reduced to below 40 The
drier surface soil layer can then be stripped for fill use exposing the
wetter soil layer beneath for further drying
Soils with a natural moisture content between 35 and 40 should be
compacted to 85 of the maximum density determined by ASTM D 1557 78
designation Soils with natural moisture contents less than 35 should
be compacted to 90
In determining the maximum dry densities in the laboratory it is
important that soil samples are worked from wet to dry removing only
the predetermined amount of water by drying as stipulated under
procedure Complete air or oven drying of the sample in the laboratory
should be avoided since during the process of such a test the soil
sample would again be moisture conditioned
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1269 00 Page 10
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in
the drier area can be modified to 85 for this project The 85
compaction standard will be adequate for the roadway embankment fill and
light building loads anticipated
Fills within 12 inches below the roadway subgrade should be
compacted to 95 minimum
Fill material should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to densities recommended in the above sections
Tamping foot rollers are recommended for compacting the on site soils
Generally significant cuts and fills are not anticipated at this
time We wish to be consulted if any fill or cut exceeding 10 feet in
height are planned in the future

to the slope see Plate 3 The filling operation should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level layers compacted in accordance

It is noted that most of the on site soils have dry densities in the
range of 60 to 80 p c f When they are compacted to higher densities
corresponding volume reduction will occur For earthwork quantity

A shrinkage factor of 20 to 25 for densification alone should be

A higher shrinkage factor may result if the soils are further dried and
compacted to 90 or more of the maximum dry density

Foundations
For the light loads anticipated from the Single family structures
an allowable bearing pressure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
resting on very stiff in situ soil or compacted fill Thickened edge
slab footings should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade for the exterior footings and 8 inches for the interior
footings
If the soil is allowed to dry such as in the case of post and beam
construction the interior footings may settle more than the exterior
footings since the subsoil will dry out more in the interior portion
Thus a deeper footing embedment should be made for interior footings if
post and beam construction is used The depth of the footing embedment
will depend on the individual lot grading and the soil moisture In
general we recommend a minimum footing embedment of 18 inches be
considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 14
To minimize the effects of slope creep or erosion slabs should be
set back a minimum distance of 10 feet away from the top of filled or
cut slopes Also if post footings are to be located close to the top
of these slopes they should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 10 feet away from the slope face
out to the bottom edge of the footing For post footings located 10
feet or more away from the top of the Slopes the footing embedment
requirements recommended above should be used
structures and roadways should be set back a minimum of 10 to 20 feet
from the top of slope depending on the vertical height of the slope and
its steepness Specific set back distance for a road or a structure
should be reviewed according to each special case These special areas
generally occur along the northern boundary of the project site along
Anini Stream Canyon and in localized areas of drainageways
If a Footing is located next to a utility trench the footing bottom
should extend to the level of the utility trench bottom
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
Individual home owners should be advised to consult a qualified
soils engineer for the design of their house foundations and other site
grading work which might be required

On Grade Slab
Slab on grade construction may be utilized in the project The
on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted base
course The thickened edge of the on grade slabs should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Our laboratory test results indicate that the on site soils in
general have low swelling potential
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out
prior to concrete placement
Utilities
Underground utilities are planned for this development It is
anticipated that most trenching will be in residual or saprolite soils
However occasional boulders may be encountered
On site soils are generally suitable for trench backfill and should
be compacted as recommended in the FILL PLACEMENT section Mechanical
compaction is recommended as the fine grained on site soils are not
conducive to water tamping or jetting

Culvert Structures
Culverts are planned for the road crossings of drainageways
In order to provide sufficient compaction around the culvert
structures and to minimize possible damage from compaction operations
the general embankment fill should first be placed in the drainageway
to at least 2 feet above the top of the culvert pipe A trench should
then be excavated through the fill and the pipe placed in the trench
Trench Backfill
In general the on site soil is silty and is not suitable for water
tamping Therefore mechanical tamping would be required if the
on site soil is used as trench backfill Alternatively granular
material such as sand or select borrow could be utilized with water
tamping
A six inch cushion layer of 3 Fine gravel is recommended below the
culvert structures to provide uniform support

Sewage Lift Stations
It is anticipated that sewage lift stations will be required to
transport sewage effluent from various widely spaced areas
Our field reconnaissance and borings disclosed that the site is
mantled with thick stiff residual soils with occasional boulders Thus
ho major construction problem is anticipated at this time
The walls of sewage lift stations are generally relatively
non yielding and they should be designed to at rest earth pressures of
55 p s f per foot of depth Where groundwater is located above the
bottom of the lift station hydrostatic pressures should be added to the
lateral earth pressures
For temporary condition during construction shoring requirements
are generally anticipated to be minimal in stiff residual soils
although specific soil conditions at each site may dictate different
approach We believe that the contractor should be responsible in
developing his shoring method It is presumed that there will not be
any lift stations located in the stream or gulch where potential uplift
condition may need to be considered
Groundwater conditions will have a significant impact on the lift
station construction where deep excavations are usually required
However our field data indicate that except in Boring 12 groundwater
was not encountered within the depth of our borings that extended about
30 to 50 feet deep
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1269 00 Page 19
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 12 at a depth of 29 feet
appropriate dewatering provision should be made if the excavations are
to penetrate below the groundwater levels
We believe the on site brown clayey silt soil may be used as
backfill for the lift station provided that the moisture content of the
soil material is lowered to 5 percentage point of the optimum To
minimize later ground settlement around the lift station the backfill
material should be placed in compacted lifts not exceeding 9 inches each
and mechanically compacted to obtain the recommended densities required
under the FILL PLACEMENT section Clean granular materials such as
base course No 3 Fine sand coral may be used as backfill that
requires relatively low compactive effort to attain the required
density However the top 2 feet of backfill should consist of the more
impervious on site soils to provide a seal against water infiltration

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ho
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics 11 Atterberg Limit
tests were performed The test results are presented on Plates B 1
through B 4
Six laboratory compaction tests modified Proctor were performed
on bulk samples of the on site soils to examine their compaction
characteristics The test results are summarized on Plates B 1
through B 4
Five laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the
on site soils to evaluate their subgrade support characteristics The
test results are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 4
Eleven direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsoils The test
results are presented on Plates B 5 through B 15
Two consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the subsoil
The test results are presented on Plates B 16 and B 17
Fifteen one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plates B 18 and B 19
