INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation for the proposed two parking structures at the Hyatt Regency
Maui The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct two parking structures one at the Napili
western end and one at the Lahaina eastern end of the Hyatt Regency
Maui The Napili parking structure will have two stories and be about 125
by 330 feet in plan dimensions Estimated column loads up to 120 kips are
anticipated The Lahaina structure will have four levels with plan
dimensions of 165 by 280 feet Estimated column loads up to 365 kips are
anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil parameters for the foundation
design of the structures
The scope of our evaluation included drilling and sampling nine 9
borings to depths ranging from 34 to 59 feet below the existing ground
surface The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests are
presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed sites are located in west Maui adjacent to the Napili
and Iahaina Towers of the Hyatt Regency Maui Both areas are covered with
A C pavement and currently used as parking lots for the patrons of the
hotel The ground structures at both areas are generally level but the
actual elevations of both sites were not available at the time of our
report preparation

DISCUSSION AND _ RECOMMENDATIONS
Napili Parking Structure Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and colum loads from the
proposed structure we believe that shallow spread footing foundation
bearing on the recompacted in situ medium dense to dense sand may be used
A minimum footing embedment of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade is recommended An allowable bearing capacity of 3500
p s f may be used for the foundation design The bearing capacity value
is for total dead plus live load only and may be increased by one third
for short duration wind seismic loading Settlements under the design
loading are anticipated to be on the order of one half of an inch total
and one quarter of an inch differential

Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be obtained
by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional

dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used against the buried footing of the structure to
determine the passive soil resistance
Lahaina Parking Structure Foundation
Pile Foundation
The borings encountered somewhat variable soil conditions below this
site Due to the relatively high column loads pile foundation
bearing on the basalt rock formation were initially considered for
foundation support of the structure
However due to the many boulders encountered in the deposits
pre drilling would probably be required thereby increasing the
costs Additionally there was a concern that some of the piles may
not be able to penetrate the buried stream bed boulders to bear on
the rock formation thereby resulting in pile settlements Due to the
above uncertainties an extensive test pile driving program would be
required well in advance of the final design to verify the actual
pile capacity Another concern was the possibility that the
vibrations from the pile driving could cause some damage to the
adjacent Banquet Facility which is supported on spread footing
foundations
For the above reasons it was our opinion that a pile foundation
could involve some risks and a spread footing foundation may be more
appropriate and economical

Spread Footing Foundation
Spread footing foundation with special footing excavation and
backfilling procedures may be used to support the structure if some
settlements are tolerable
The field exploration generally indicated a layer of boulders from 3
to 5 feet below the existing ground level These near surface
boulders probably were disposed on the current site during earlier
development of the golf course to the north The site was then
filled up to the current grade and covered with A c pavement for
parking usage
In order to reduce potential settlements due to the possible piping
of the upper fill material into the voids between these near surface
boulders the following footing excavation and backfilling procedures
are recommended for shallow spread footing foundation
We recommend that the areas to receive foundation be over excavated a
minimum of 5 feet below the existing ground surface and replaced with
compacted granular backfill to increase the bearing capacity of the
footing subgrade The bottom 6 inches of the excavation should be
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of the maximm
dry density Particles in the bottom 6 inches of the excavation with
dimension over 3 inches should be discarded to provide uniform
subgrade support
The backfill should be placed in 12 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78 On site sandy soil may be used as backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive granular soils with particles less than 3 inches in
maximum size and less than 20 passing the 200 sieve A schematic
section of the footing excavation and backfill is shown on Plate 3

Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished grade levels of the Napili
and Iahaina Parking Structures are very close to the existing grades
Therefore minimal cut and fill are anticipated for both sites
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
debris and other unsuitable materials in the area should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
The areas which are to receive pavement should be scarified to a
depth of at least 6 inches below the cleared ground surface moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density

For both Napili and Lahaina sites the excavated footing areas should
be kept moist and tamped prior to backfilling and or concrete placement if
they are loosened during excavation
Any loose or soft spots encountered at the footing locations or
subgrade level should be recompacted if it is sandy or removed if it is
clayey or organic and then replaced with select on site sandy soil or

given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from existing parking stalls indicated on the topographic
survey Elevations of the data points were approximately determined by
interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey map The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
