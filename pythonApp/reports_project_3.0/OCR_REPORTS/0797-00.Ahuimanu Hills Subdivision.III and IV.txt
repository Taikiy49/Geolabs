INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to develop soil para
meters for the design of the site grading for the pro
posed industrial subdivision
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed industrial subdivision consists of 32 lots
Relatively light structures are anticipated
The proposed site grading consists of cutting the upper
ridge areas and filling some of the lower areas Cuts
up to 30 feet and fills up to 20 feet are planned Except
where a proposed road crosses one of the drainageways the
2 large drainageways in the area will not be graded A
drain culvert will be placed under the fill over the drain
ageway
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located near the base of the Koolau
mountain range in The site
is bounded on the north by the Ahuimanu Hills Phase II
subdivision and on the east by Valley of the Temples
On the south and west the site is bounded by undeveloped
land similar in character to that of the project site
The site is generally hilly with a topographic relief of
over 300 feet over the development with elevations ranging
from 170 to over 400 feet The project site consists mainly
of two partly graded ridges and three adjacent valleys trend
ing in a northwest southeast direction The crests of the
graded ridge are mostly open grassed areas The valleys are
very heavily vegetated with trees and shrubs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 3
Drainage from the site is in a generally northward direction
through the valleys and the perennial stream located in
the valley between the ridges
Access to the site is through the Ahuimanu Hills Phase II
subdivision by way of Hui Iwa Street
There are no know existing structures on the site
Geology
The geology of the Koolaupoko area is characterized by small
ridges and valleys formed in weathered alluvium and colluvium
at the base of the Koolau mountain range
The small ridges remain as erosional remnants of a large
deposit of mainly colluvial material derived from the erosion
of the windward flank of the Koolau range Valleys cut by
stream erosion tend to be filled with alluvial sand and
gravels in their lower reaches A Recent alluvium consist
ing of clays and silts has covered the older sand and gravels
in the valley bottoms
Small amounts of organic material and peat exist in the low
lying areas

Subsurface Exploration
Eleven test borings were drilled and one test pit dug
at the site to explore the subsurface conditions and
to obtain samples for laboratory testing The approxi

Soil Conditions
The field exploration encountered brown to reddish brown
clayey silt with some decomposed rock over most of the
site River gravel and boulders with a soft clay matrix
were encountered in the large drainageway
Water was encountered at about 6 feet depths in the drain
ageway
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the upper borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration indicated that the proposed develop
ment is feasible from a soils engineering point of view
The site is underlain by medium to stiff soil which may
be developed by normal grading operations
Site Grading
Stockpiles of material from previous grading operations
in the area may be present at the site After clearing
and grubbing operations field observations should be
made to attempt to locate possible uncompacted stockpile
areas
The site to be graded shall be cleared of vegetation
debris and other deleterious materials Prior to fill
ing the existing ground should be scarified to a minimum
of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maxi
mum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site materials have in place moisture contents about
15 to 35 percent above its optimum moisture content There
fore drying of the excavated material may be required before
using it as fill material
W P 6
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard
used in other drier areas should be modified to 85 for
this project The 85 compaction standard will be adequate
for the roadway embankment fill and light building loads
anticipated
Based on the results of our in place densities and maximum
density determination it appears that a shrinkage factor
of 15 to 20 percent should be considered for the on site
cut and fill quantities The in place dry densities aver
maximum dry density
If additional off site borrow is required the material
should be tested and approved by the soil engineer prior
to transportation to the site The material should gene
rally be non expansive and should not contain large per
centages of boulders organic or deleterious material
To reduce piping and seepage pressure buildup subdrains
should be placed in the bottom of the drainageways prior
to filling For details see Plate 2
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched
into the existing firm ground to properly bond the new
fill to the slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
The filling operation should start at the lowest point
and continue up in level compacted layers For details
see Plate 3
Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes
are recommended for the site
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having a
vertical height greater than 20 feet
The upstream fill slope in the drainageway should be pro
vided with rip rap or other slope protection
Foundation
Light warehouse and one story structures are anticipated
for the development If large or heavy structures are
planned particularly near the tops of the existing drain
ageways additional explorations should be performed for
the individual structure
For the light structural loads anticipated an allowable
bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended for footings
resting on the stiff natural soil or compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
Footings should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the
finished grade Footings located near the top of slopes
should have a minimum horizontal setback distance of 8
feet measured from the outer edge of the footing founda
tion out to the slope face
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the
footing should extend to the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from building foun
dations
Drainage Structure
About 20 feet of fill is planned in the eastern drainage
way for a roadway crossing A pipe culvert is planned
through this high roadway fill
The field exploration encountered alluvium consisting
mainly of river gravel and cobbles with soft clay in the
drainageway Settlement predictions in this type of sub
soil are very difficult Based on the field exploration
and previous experience in the area it is estimated that
up to about 2 to 3 inches of settlement may take place at
the center of the pipe Less settlements should occur at
the ends of the pipe Due to the granular nature of the
deposit most of the settlement should occur within 1 month
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
If these settlements are not tolerable surcharging can be
used to reduce the settlements
Wing walls will be constructed at both ends of the drain
pipe An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f is recom
mended for the wall footings resting on alluvium To pro
vide uniform support a minimum of 12 inches of granular
bedding material should be placed under the structure
The above bearing value can be increased by placing a thicker
layer of granular bedding material under the footing We
would be happy to consult with you if a higher bearing value
is required
For lateral earth pressure design an equivalent fluid pre
ssure of 45 p c f is recommended for walls with a level
backfill and 70 p c f is recommended for a 2 horizontal to
1 vertical backfill slope The above values assume that
granular material is used as backfill material A value of
0 35 is recommended for the coefficient of friction between
soil and concrete
There is an existing dirt road crossing over the drainage
way near Boring No 11 The crossing appears to have been
formed by filling the drainageway with on site material A
drain pipe was not noted through the fill The effect of the
fill on the hydraulic capacity of the drainageway should be
evaluated

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade can be used on level areas away from the
tops of slopes Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch
layer of compacted base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring ma
terial a polyethylene moisture barrier should be con
sidered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the on site soils
the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section
31 2 Standard Specification for Public Work Construction
or equivalent Non expansive select borrow should have
a minimum CBR value of 25 and consist of crusher run
quarry waste mudrock coral sand or cinder material
GEOLABS HAWAII
10
W _P 11
Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted
to 95 of its maximum density
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of roadway construction
and the above design section can be revised accordingly
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soil engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the pro
ject plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading opera
tions
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

October 1978 by drilling eleven test borings utilizing truck
mounted drilling equipment and by digging one test pit with a
backhoe in an area inaccessable for the drilling equipment

below the existing ground surface The test pit terminated
at a depth of 10 0 feet
The approximate locations of the borings and test pit
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1

monitored the test pit excavation and drilling operations The
classifications were made in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils is
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 12 A descrip
tion of the soils encountered in the test pit is presented in the
Test Pit Log Plate A 13
