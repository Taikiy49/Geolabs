Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger Koolau Volcano forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae volcano became extinct
while the Koolau volcano was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below
Koolau lavas in Central Oahu
The project site is located on the western side of the Waianae Mountain Range
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea level fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the
sea levels rose above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at corresponding higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and
reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present
form

Concrete Driveways
We understand that pervious concrete pavement is desired for the driveways at the
house lots for the project development In general we do not recommend the use of an
open grid system i e the water is allowed to infiltrate freely through the
pervious porous pavement section and into the subgrade soils because the infiltrated
water likely will saturate the subgrade soils which could affect the pavement supporting
capability Secondly the subgrade soils in an open grid system generally is not well
compacted in order to keep the permeability high to allow as much infiltration as
possible which also could affect its pavement supporting capability Therefore we
believe that a closed grid system with perforated pipes collecting the infiltrated water in
the permeable subbase layer and discharging into appropriate outlets should be
considered for the project Based on our evaluation we believe the following
pervious porous pavement section with a subdrain system may be considered for the
project if deemed appropriate

Site Grading
Due to site drainage requirements we understand that new fills up to about
5 feet thick will be required to raise the existing ground surface to the design finish
grades across the majority of the project site for the proposed development At the
on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be cleared and
grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Soft and or yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill should
be over excavated to expose firm materials and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
disposed properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Abundant shrinkage cracks are present at the existing ground surface across the
project site In general shrinkage cracks will develop in the ground due to the lack of
maintenance of the soil moisture content as the surface soils dry out under the sun and
dry weather conditions The development of shrinkage cracks in the ground was
apparently intensified by the prolonged dry climatic conditions that are characteristic of
the Waianae area resulting in large fluctuations of the soil moisture content

If the existing ground cracks are left unattended for an extended period of time
and are allowed to penetrate deeper below the surface the ground cracks could
adversely affect the performance of the future driveway slabs and roadway pavements
The ground cracks could serve as conduits for water to percolate into the bearing soils
and cause loss of strength of the bearing soils resulting in potential settlement of the

the observed ground cracks should be implemented prior to fill placement and
construction of the proposed development
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades should be proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and
collapsing near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks The
exposed subgrades should be proof rolled without moisture conditioning the subgrade
soils at the pre existing moisture contents Loose areas disclosed during the
proof rolling operation should be backfilled with general fill material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction A Geolabs representative should monitor
the proof rolling operations on a near continuous basis
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks develop in the subgrade soil after compaction we

Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling five borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths ranging from about 10 to 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck mounted drill equipment
equipped with continuous flight auger
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
