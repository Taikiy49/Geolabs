Project Considerations
The project site is located at the existing Shriners Hospital For Children in the
Punahou area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located at
the southwest corner of the intersection between Punahou Street and the Punahou
Street Off Ramp for the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
Based on the information provided the new parking lot planned as part of the
Honolulu Replacement Hospital Project will be constructed over an existing box culvert
The new parking lot will be located on the west side of the new replacement hospital
We understand that it is planned to construct two bridge structures to span over the
existing box culvert in areas where moving vehicular traffic is anticipated to reduce any
additional loading imposed on the existing box culvert It is planned to construct
one bridge structure at the north side of the new parking lot and one bridge structure at
the south side of the existing parking fot Details for the existing box culvert structure
were not available during the preparation of this fee proposal
Four boreholes will be drilled to depths of approximately 30 feet below the existing
ground surface Two boreholes will be drilled at each planned crossing Drilling will be
performed using a truck mounted drill rig and soil samples will be collected using split
spoon samplers

Scope of Work
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration is to obtain an overview of
the surface and subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information
would be used to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the design of the crossings over the existing
box culvert To accomplish this we propose a program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to the project site and back Please note that we have waived
the mobilization and demobilization fee
2 Drilling and sampling of four test borings extending to depths of about
30 feet below the existing ground surface Two borings will be drilled at
each planned crossing
3 Soil rock samples remaining after testing will be kept in plastic bags soil or
boxes rock and delivered to the Shriners Hospital for storage
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by an
engineer geologist from our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the presentation of our findings
and geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the design of the new
crossings over the existing box culvert
7 Preparation of a formal report six copies summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
9 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
10 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
11 Review of plans and specifications for general conformance with the
geotechnical report as required by the City and County of Honolulu
