In accordance with the project requirements a test pile program was undertaken for
the USS Arizona Memorial Visitor Center Replacement project at Pearl Harbor on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii between March 27 and The test pile program
included driving 12 test piles performing dynamic load testing on all test piles and
performing a static compressive pile load test on a selected test pile This report presents
the results of our field observations during the test pile program and recommendations for
production pile lengths and driving of the production piles It should be noted that the
recommended production pile lengths contained herein are for Buildings A C D and F
only Results of the static compressive pile load test have been submitted by Hirata
Associates Inc in a separate report entitled Pile Load Test Report USS Arizona
Memorial Visitor Center Replacement Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the northern bank at the downstream end of
Halawa Stream in Pearl Harbor Due to the ongoing settlement of the existing visitor
center supported on shallow foundations a new USS Arizona Memorial Visitor Center
is planned at the site
The new visitor center will consist of several structures for orientation services
exhibits AV theaters retail areas restrooms and basic office space These structures
will consist of small one story buildings Exterior concrete flatwork or gravel walkways
connecting each structure will allow visitors to traverse the visitor center
Due to the on going consolidation at the site we recommended supporting the
new buildings and concrete slabs both interior and exterior on driven 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable compressive load
capacity of up to 75 tons 150 kips per pile The on going consolidation of the
estuarine deposits would result in negative skin friction forces downdrag forces acting

Hammer and Driving System
A Junttan HHS hydraulic impact hammer was proposed for the pile driving
operations by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc Based on the pile driving equipment data
form provided the Junttan HHS9 hammer has a ram weight of 19 8 kips and a
maximum stroke of 4 feet The maximum rated energy of the hydraulic impact hammer
is about 79 2 kip feet when set at the 4 foot stroke
The hammer cushion consists of Monocast with a total thickness of 8 inches
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 6 inches of plywood cushion blocks was proposed for the pile
driving operations We understand that the Monocast hammer cushion and plywood
pile cushion blocks have cross sectional areas of approximately 445 and 342 square
inches respectively
Pile Details
The test piles generally consisted of 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete piles with lengths of about 135 155 and 210 feet The 210 foot long piles
were driven in three segments consisting of a 90 foot bottom segment a 65 foot middle
segment and a 55 foot top segment The 155 foot long piles were driven in
two segments consisting of a 80 foot bottom segment and a 75 foot top segment The
proposed 135 foot long pile should have consisted of 70 foot bottom segment and a
65 foot top segment however the contractor used the 65 foot top segment as the
middle segment for a 210 foot long pile Pile No 44 A 55 foot top segment was used
above the 70 foot bottom segment which resulted in a 125 foot long test pile The pile
segments will be spliced with 6 foot long non mechanical splice cans

DYNAMIC LOAD TESTING
As previously mentioned dynamic load tests were performed on each of the
twelve test piles as required by the contract documents The dynamic load testing of
each test pile was conducted by Foundation and Geotechnical Engineering LLC Each
test pile was instrumented for monitoring by a pile driving analyzer PDA to evaluate
the stresses that developed in the test piles and to evaluate the pile bearing capacity
during initial driving and re striking
Re striking of the test piles were conducted generally a minimum of 3 to 6 days
after the initial pile driving as part of the test pile program

Pile Driving Criteria
In general the Junttan HHS9 hydraulic impact hammer with a 3 foot hammer
stroke should be used for the production pile installation Based on our engineering
analyses and the test pile program conducted for the project the following pile driving
criteria may be used during the production pile driving operations
1 Drive to the full depth of the production pile ordered 125 feet 145 feet
and 200 feet below the existing ground surface with the penetration
resistance for the last foot of penetration of at least 14 blows per foot
If the penetration resistance for the last foot is less than 14 blows per foot
the pile should be re striked a minimum of 7 days after initial driving of the
pile A penetration resistance for the last 3 inches of at least 11 blows per
inch should be achieved for the pile to be considered acceptable
2 The pile driving operation may be terminated for the 200 foot long piles
when the pile driving resistance meets the following blow counts at a
minimum depth of about 140 feet below the existing ground surface
e Last foot of penetration of at least 140 blows per foot
e Last 6 inches of penetration of at least 13 blows per inch
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected
or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more
than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth and or penetration
resistance During soft driving such as when the pile tip is within the soft soils the
hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile
stresses do not develop within the pile In general a stroke of 1 foot or less should be
used when the pile tip is within the soft soils
