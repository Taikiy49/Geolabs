INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed project The study consisted
of subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses as outlined in our proposal dated
Based on the results of our engineering studies conclusions
and recommendations pertinent to site development are
presented
Project Description
The property is located on the east corner of Palama and
Kanoa Streets in the Palama area of Honolulu The site is
feet wide and relatively flat The site is presently occupied
with a two story residential building unpaved parking area
lawns and few trees
Page 2
W O 447 00
A four story apartment building and parking facilities are
presently proposed to be constructed on this site The
building is proposed to be a concrete structure approximately
100 feet long by 100 feet wide with parking at grade beneath
the building
The structural engineer Mr Jim Cotton of J Brian Hughes
and Associates has informed us that the structural details
of the proposed building is not available However he has
indicated that anticipated magnitude of wall loads would
vary between 9 to 18 kips per lineal foot and column loads
would be on the order of 300 kips
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of two 2 borings ranging
in depth from 16 5 to 17 feet The drilling was accomplished
using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment The borings
were continuously inspected by a geologist from our firm
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Where applicable Standard Penetration Tests were made at selec
ted depth intervals The Standard Penetration Test consists of
Page 3
W O 447 00
driving a 2 inch O D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12
inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N
and is an approximate measure of the relative density or
consistency of a soil These resistance values are plotted
on the boring logs Samples obtained were visually classi
fied in the field and representative material was placed in
air tight containers and returned to our laboratory for
inspection and testing
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A Geology
During the medial to late Pleistocene Age much of the
Nuuanu Valley including the proposed building site was
covered with a very hard basalt flows These flows were
subsequently covered by alluvial debris from seaward
flowing streams Then relative rise in sealevel inundated
the area and marine clays and coral algal reefs were
deposited over the site Later the sea withdrew and the
area was covered by a shower of cinders from Tantalus
Page 4
W O 447 00
and Punchbowl Crater eruptions More recently these cinders
have been reworked and redeposited over the site along with
alluvial debris
Soils
At least four main soil types were encountered in the sub
surface on the proposed building site
1 Very hard black basalt was encountered at depths of
16 to 17 feet below present grade in borings 1 and 2
respectively
2 Hard sandy pebbly olive to brown black CLAY was
present in boring 1 at depths ranging from 13 to 17
feet below present grade
3 Medium hard tan clayey CORAL ALGAL REEF was encoun
tered at depths of 4 to 5 feet below present grade
The thickness of this material ranges from 8 feet in
boring 1 to 12 feet in boring 2
4 Very stiff brown silty sandy black sand gravelly
CLAY Alluvium comprises the upper 4 or 5 feet
approximately of the present site The surface soil
is not considered to be significantly expansive

No subsurface water was observed during drilling Some
of the soils were moist so there may be a slow influx of
water onto the site in the subsurface

is feasible from an engineering standpoint provided the
recommendations included in this report are followed
It is our professional opinion that the onsite Coral Algal
Reef underlying 4 to 5 feet of clayey sandy soils in
general is capable of supporting the proposed structure

Fill Material
The onsite surface layer of brownish clayey sandy soil
may be utilized for compacted structural fills along
with the underlying Coral Algal Reef material
Imported fill material must be tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to the importation Imported
fill shall be of a granular non expansive nature
Structural Fill Placement
Approved fill material should be placed in maximum
8 inch lifts and compacted to 90 relative compaction
using ASTM D 1557 70 as a standard
Finish Grade
In cut and natural grade portions the upper one foot
of soils beneath the finish subgrade over the entire
structural areas building pads should be recompacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density

The proposed structure can be supported on spread footings

from wind or seismic effect

Lateral Earth Pressure
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive earth pressure on sides and friction at the
bottom of footings Passive pressure may be taken as that
exerted by a fluid of density equal to 375 pounds per cubic
foot Surface one foot of soil should be neglected for
passive pressure Friction may be taken as normal dead load
times a coefficient of friction equal to 0 45
Concrete Floor Slab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should be
scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density A moisture barrier consisting of
polyvinyl chloride membrane should be provided under the
slab areas which will receive covering such as carpeting
or tiles one inch layer of clean sand above the membrane
will protect the membrane during construction The concrete
floor slab on gyrade should be a minimum of 4 nominal
thickness and be reinforced with a minimum of 6 X 6
No 10 X 10 welded wire mesh

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site
1 Classification Tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
INC
test was performed to determine the distribution of the
grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
Scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
Shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
c Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time and
consolidation were obtained during each load increment and
GEOLARS HAWAI I ING
rebound was measured during the unloading portion Consoli
dation test results are plotted in terms of percent settlement
versus applied loads
d Swell Tests The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 100 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and final
sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed as
a percentage of the initial height of the sample
e Maximum Density Test Samples of soil are compacted in
a mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at different
moisture content are run to determine the maximum density
and optimum moisture content The test is run in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 70
