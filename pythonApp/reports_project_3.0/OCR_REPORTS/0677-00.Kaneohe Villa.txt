DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It ts our opinion that the site is suitable for the construction
of the proposed cluster development as currently planned provided
the project design and construction incorporates the recommenda
tions presented in the previous reports However certain measures
must be taken to insure the overall stability of the talus slopes
and unstable areas designated as Recent and Ancient Landslides
in the referenced reports
It is essential that the unstable areas be closely inspected by
this firm after they are cleared of vegetation debris and un
stable materials to ensure that no planes of weakness exist within
the unstable areas prior to placing compacted fill In addition
the extent of the landslide areas and talus slopes should be veri
fied in the field during grading operation
Continuous inspection and testing during construction for the sta
bilizing fill and building construction are critical for the future
project performance
This report was prepared specifically for the proposed cluster
development Use of special construction procedures was assumed
throughout our analysis and it represents one of the bases of the
recommendations presented henceforth Should there be any altera
tions to the proposed development as stated in this report we
should be informed so that we can review such changes and submit
additional recommendations Should there be aay conflicts between

Slope Stability
Based on the results of our review of the latest development
plans and available soil investigation reports previously per
formed it is our opinion that the slope gradients as recommended
areadequate All cut slopes should not be planned steeper than
14 1 horizontal to vertical and all fill slopes should not be
steeper than 2 1 horizontal to vertical
In addition the grading operations of both cut and fill slopes
within the landslide areas and talus slopes should incorporate
the stabilizing scheme as outlined below
Construction Specifications for Cut and Fills lopes
The specification for cut and fill slope construction was submitted
previously as referred above We believe that the proposed grading
scheme as shown on the enclosed grading plan is acceptable provided
that keying and benching operation ts implemented for all slopes
steeper than 5 1 horizontal to vertical as the fill soils are
brought up to ensure that adequate bonding is achieved with the

native soil In addition for slopes within and in the vicinity

Drainage
Runoff from roofs and paved areas should be collected and drained
to a suitable discharge point Usually drainage is connnected to
the storm drainage system Water should not be allowed to pond
adjacent to foundations and at the top of the slopes The site
should be graded to provide positive drainage away from all struc
tures and prevent any runoff over the slopes

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
KANEOHE VILLA CLUSTER DEVELOPMENT
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas SAGenent and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
Li Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
ITl
IV
oo
Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
ne
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
Sad
es
F
emt
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
Seasonal Requirements
A
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
