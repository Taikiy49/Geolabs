Waianae Mall Pavement Failure
Waianae Oahu Hawaii
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed ai the pavement failure site at Waianae Mall Oahu Hawaii The general area
under consideration is shown on The Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the pavement failure site to form the bases for identification of the possible
cause of and recommendations for the corrective action to be taken
Project Consideration
The pavement failure site is located at the Waianae Mall near the Big Way Store
in Waianae Oahu
It is our understanding that the pavement failure site has been previously repaired
by removal of failed material and replaced with eighteen 18 inches of select borrow and
new asphalt pavement This area is shown on The Site Plan Plate 2
The scope of our work consisted of identification of the possible cause of the
recent pavement depressions and recommendations for the repair of the failed area

Subsurface Conditions
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths ranging from 10 5 to 11 1 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings drilled within the previously repaired area Borings No B 1 and B 4
and the boring located east thereof Boring No B 3 generally encountered a 3 0 inch
thick asphalt concrete surface underlain by a 12 inch thick granular rock layer This 12
inch thick layer of granular rock was not encountered in Boring No B 4 Below the
granular rock a stiff brown gray clay Adobe layer approximately 4 5 to 5 5 feet thick was
encountered followed by medium dense tan white sandy to clayey coral gravel to a depth
of approximately 10 5 feet
Boring No B 2 drilled west of Boring B 1 and outside of the previously repaired
area encountered a 3 inch thick asphaltic concrete surface underlain by a 12 inch thick
granular rock layer Below the granular rock layer a very dense tan white sandy coral
gravel layer approximately 2 5 feet thick was encountered followed by a 4 5 feet thick
layer of very still brown and tan white silty clay with some weathered coral gravel
underlain by tan white coral sand and coral to 11 1 feet depth the maximum drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of the field
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are attached
Findings
It appears that at the present time the pavement failure consists of two
depressions one about 14 x 18 in plan dimension with a maximum pavement
depression of approximately 7 inches The other is about 18 x 32 in plan dimension with
a maximum pavement depression of approximately 12 inches These two depressions
are about 25 inches apart and are located as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is our understanding that the depressions occurred when a trailer loaded with
containers of juice separated from its tractor thus dropping the trailer s two steel dolly
wheels onto the pavement at the locations of the depressions

Laboratory testing of the adobe clay indicates that this material has a high swelling
potential when saturated from a dry and compacted condition
Findings and Recommendations
The pavement depressions were apparently caused by the sudden concentrated
loads imposed upon the pavement by the impact of the steel dolly wheels when the
container trailer dropped
We recommend that corrective action consist of removal of the A C pavement
section to a depth allowing its replacement with the following section
3 Inch Asphalt Concrete
12 Inch Base Course
18 inch Select Borrow
33 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
On Moisture Conditioned Subgrade
Prior to placing the select borrow material the adobe clay subgrade would be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 above its optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The select borrow material should consist of two 9 inch lifts of non expansive
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 25 passing the
200 sieve and with a minimum CBR value of 25
Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
