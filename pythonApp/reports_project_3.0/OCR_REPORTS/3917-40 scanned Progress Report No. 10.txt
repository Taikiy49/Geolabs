approximately 1 03 inches as of
The cumulative displacement record indicates a reduction in casing deformation
from a high of 1 16 inches which was recorded in both February and May of 2005 The
decrease in cumulative displacement is believed to be a result of the on going complex
and irregular earth movement pattern that has been recorded since the instrument was
installed Overall the detected earth movements are considered to be slight in magnitude
Because the inclinometer site and vicinity consists of an undeveloped hillslope vegetated
with tall grass overt signs of possible ground surface deformation were not observed
The long term pattern of movement appears to show intermittent periods of active
displacement followed by short term periods of relative inactivity or apparent reversal of
movement as was recorded during the past 6 months The cumulative displacement
recorded by the inclinometer continues to slowly increase however the rate of movement
during the past six month monitoring period indicates the movement was relatively inactive
or may have changed direction It is our opinion the subsurface earth movement recorded
at Inclinometer No 3 is complex and may be composed of various directional vectors of
earth movement including some slight ground uplift The complexity also may be related
to the close proximity of the western lateral boundary of the Waiomao Landslide
