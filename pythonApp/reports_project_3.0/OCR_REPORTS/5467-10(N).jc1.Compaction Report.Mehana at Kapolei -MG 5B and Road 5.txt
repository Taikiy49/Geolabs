PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development Mehana is a mixed residential subdivision
project bordered by Renton Road to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east the future
James Campbell Estate residential development to the north and the Ronald T Y Moon
Judiciary Complex to the west
Mehana at Kapolei Mass Grading 5B is a 17 unit multi family residential subdivision
parcel located at the southwestern end of the Mehana development It is bordered on the
east by Road K Kama aha Avenue Road A Kakala Street to the south and the future
Wai Aniani Way extension to the west The graded area of Phase 5B encompasses
approximately 8 6 acres as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Arterial Roadway Phase 5 includes Road C and the remaining segment of Road A
from to 15 The layout of the roads is shown on the Roadway
Grading Plan Plate 2
The grading and construction of this portion of the arterial roadway system generally
consisted of trench excavations for utility installation including backfills subgrade cuts and
fills and pavement subbase and base course preparations

SITE GRADING
The mass grading 5B at Mehana at Kapolei generally consisted of clearing and
grubbing removal of existing stockpiles and site grading for cuts and fills less than 5 feet
in thickness Royal Contracting Co Ltd performed the earthwork operations for the
project
At the onset of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from
the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing
operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finished grades were compacted using
a sheepsfoot and or smooth vibratory drum roller In general the site was capped with
3 feet of coralline materials
Areas along original drainage ways were over excavated to remove soft sediment
soil Depths of the over excavation varied from 2 5 to 8 feet The over excavation was
backfilled with compacted general fills
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum 95 percent of
the maximum dry density
Samples of the materials utilized as fill were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor The test results
are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field density tests were performed on compacted subgrade on fills and
utility backfills in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The
test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 43 The test locations and elevations
were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Compacted areas that did not conform to the required moisture and density were
reworked until the measured relative compaction met or exceeded the minimum specified
for the project
Our services consisted of part time field observation and testing for subgrade
preparation fill and backfill placement compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above site grading operations
