PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Wailea area on the Island of Maui Hawaii Based
on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to develop the project site
into a residential area consisting of several two and three story buildings and a six story
multi family building In addition several retaining walls along the property lines and at
various locations across the site are planned for separation of grade
We understand that the storm water runoff disposal method planned for the project
sites will consist of drainage inlet structures interconnected with underground piping which
leads to two underground collection areas located to the south of Villa A and in the
parking lot located to the south of Villa D The collection areas will consist of several
underground 5 foot diameter enclosed tanks that will be able to store the volume of water
from a 100 year storm The water stored in the underground tanks will exit the tanks and
percolate into the subsurface materials at the project site Therefore we understand that
the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered at the croposed
collection areas for the storm water runoff will need to be evaluated
Based on the plans recently provided large retaining walls are planned for the
subject project We understand that it is desired to determine whether the proposed walls
will retain basalt rock formation Therefore several borings were drilled along the

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Wailea area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The
project site is bounded by The Outrigger Wailea to the north The Shops at Wailea to the
east and The Grand Wailea to the south as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The central portion of the project site consists of uncontrolled fill materials
consisting of boulders cobbles and concrete fragments in a soil matrix The vegetation at
the project site consists of trees shrubs and grass The area near the existing shoreline
consists of lawn grass
Based on the topographic survey map the project site generally slopes from
northeast to southwest The existing ground elevations at the project site range from
about 9 to 76 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 and 102 extending to depths of about 21 to 35 3 feet
below the existing ground surface for the storm waiter disposal system and five borings
designated as Boring Nos 103 through 107 extending to depths of about 9 5 to 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface for the retaining walls along the southern portion of the
site The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers and rotary coring tools The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring No 101 generally encountered fill materials consisting of boulders and
cobbles in a clayey silt matrix extending to a depth of about 7 feet below the existing
ground surface The boulder cobble fill was underlain by silty gravel and sand fill with
varying amounts of cobbles extending to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing
ground surface Below the silty gravel and sand fills were closely to moderately fractured
hard basalt rock formation extending to a depth of about 28 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Clinker materials were encountered below the basalt rock formation to
the maximum depth explored of about 35 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Boring No 102 generally encountered fill materials consisting of very stiff silty clays
with cobbles extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the fill materials was slightly fractured hard basalt rock formation extending to a
depth of about 11 5 below the existing ground surface The basalt rock formation was
underlain by highly weathered basalt rock formation extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 21 feet below the existing ground surface

Retaining Walls
As previously mentioned it was desired to determine whether Retaining Wall
Nos 1 through 4 would retain basalt rock formation The following general guidelines may
be used for preliminary design of the retaining walls mentioned above
Retaining Wall Foundations
In general we believe that Retaining Wall Nos 1 through 3 and Retaining Wall
No 4 with vertical heights greater than 10 feet may be designed based on rock
parameters An allowable bearing pressure of up to 6 000 pounds per square foot
psf may be used for design of foundations bearing on the basalt rock formation
This bearing value may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
those caused by wind and or seismic forces
In general the foundations should have a minimum width of 18 inches and should
be embedded a minimum depth of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the retaining walls may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
foundations A coefficient of friction of 0 5 may be used for foundations bearing on
basalt rock Resistance to lateral loads due to passive pressure may be estimated
using a rectangular distribution of It should be noted that the above
values for lateral load resistance assume that the concrete for the footings are cast
neat against the basalt rock formation

not mentioned above should be designed using the recommendations in our
