Dear Mr. Hiraga: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations at 84-090 Maiola Place of the Mauna`olu Estates project 
from July 30 through August 21, 2018. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project site is located at 84-090 Maiola Place within the Mauna`olu Estates 
development in the Makaha area in the District of Waianae on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. Based on the information provided, we understand that up to about 7 feet of 
new fills will be placed to create a building pad for the construction of two model homes.  
The proposed grading work is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. 
RHS Lee, Inc. performed the site grading operations during the building pad 
construction for the project. The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, 
subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fill materials. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the exposed subgrades were 
scarified to a depth of about 12 inches, moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent above the

optimum moisture content, and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum 
dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
New fills required for the project generally consisted of 3-inch minus non-expansive 
select granular material imported from Grace Pacific Quarry.  During placement of the 
non-expansive select granular fill material, the fill materials were generally placed in 8-inch 
near-level loose lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture content, and compacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with 
ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). The compaction requirement for the fill materials below 
the driveways was increased to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory 
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are 
summarized on Plate 2 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill operations on an as-need basis to 
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions 
were noted. A total of 49 field density tests, including retest of failed areas, were 
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.3 of this report. 
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills at 
the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from 
on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only 
to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were 
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading for 
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report 
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced report dated June 22, 2018 and subsequent recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
