Project Considerations
The Hualalai Resort project site is located about 8 5 miles northeast of Keahole
International Airport in the Kaupulehu area within the District of North Kona on the Island
of Hawaii As part of the Phase II development of Hualalai Resort construction of various
residential subdivision lots and infrastructure improvements have been on going since
last year
During the mass grading operation of the Phase II development a large lava tube
was encountered at Parcel 4 We understand that a portion of the lava tube was
collapsed and backfilled to near the property line of Lot Nos 2 and 3 of Parcel 4 Based
on our field observations and our experience with similar conditions we recommended
that further collapsing and backfilling was not necessary for the lava tube with about
14 feet of competent basalt formation roof We recommended that the face of the lava
tube be adequately sealed and the location and elevations at the face of the lava tube be
surveyed for proper record keeping
Based on the underground survey of the lava tube the lava tube appears to
traverse across Lot Nos 3 and 4 of Parcel 4 In order to evaluate the competency of
materials above the lava tube and for record keeping drilling borings above the lava tube

Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
site appears to be of both a a and pahoehoe type flows which spread and ponded as they
approached the ocean Aja lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and
irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including
cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy
surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock

Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe lava
These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they represent both
lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes
are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and
pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling 14 borings
designated as Boring 4A 4B and 5 extending
to depths of about 10 to 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Lava Tube Location Plan Plate 2
As previously mentioned the intent of the borings was to evaluate the competency
of the basalt rock formation above the lava tube at the site An underground survey of the
lava tube was conducted to delineate the limits of the lava tube Boring Nos 1 through 5
were drilled at surveyed locations over the lava tube to evaluate the roof materials The
lava tube was encountered in Boring No 5 from about 20 to 26 feet below the ground
surface However Boring Nos 1 through 4 did not appear to encounter the lava tube
Therefore additional borings were drilled using a 5 to 15 foot lateral offset from the original
boring location in an effort to locate the lava tube Those borings are designated as
Boring and 4B Based on the borings drilled it
appears that Boring and 5 encountered the lava tube ranging from about
3 5 to 6 5 feet in height
We understand that the underground survey of the lava tube was performed
manually without the use of electronic surveying equipment Due to the method of survey
utilized for the underground survey of the lava tube we understand that the exact location
of the lava tube may not be as shown on the Lava Tube Location Plan Plate 2 Therefore
the lava tube was not encountered in many of the drilled borings
Based on the drilled borings the project site is generally underlain by surface fills
placed during the mass grading operations over native basalt lava flows The surface fills
generally consisted of dense gravel and cobbles with some sand and silt extending to
about 2 5 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface The borings generally encountered
native basalt formation below the surface fills extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The basalt formation was generally
medium hard to hard and ranged from unweathered to moderately weathered
Interbedded clinker layers and small voids of less than 1 foot in height were encountered

Discussion and Recommendations
A total of 14 borings were drilled to evaluate the competency of the native basalt
formation over the lava tube In Boring and 5 the lava tube was
encountered from about 20 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
materials above the lava tube in those borings generally consisted of medium hard to
hard unweathered to moderately weathered basalt formation of over 16 5 feet in
thickness The borings that did not encounter the lava tube generally encountered
materials with similar composition to those mentioned above
It should be noted that the original ground surface prior to mass grading was
prepared in general conformance to the subgrade preparation requirements of the
project The subgrade preparation generally consisted of ripping the original subgrade to
a minimum depth of 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to assist
in detecting and collapsing cavities and or voids contained in these porous near surface
materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground was generally leveled with
the use of a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar size bulldozer working the surface a
minimum of four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged
surfaces with a bulldozer the fill subgrades were generally proof rolled with a large
vibratory drum roller minimum 20 tons for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids Yielding areas loose areas or cavities
disclosed during the clearing and proof rolling operations were generally over excavated
and backfilled with compacted fill materials It should be noted that the basalt formation
over the large lava tube traversing across Lot Nos 3 and 4 of Parcel 4 generally did not
yield during the proof rolling operations
Based on the borings drilled we believe that the dense basalt formation at the
roof of the lava tube generally does not need to be collapsed and backfilled We believe
that the on site basalt formation should be able to support the lightly loaded one to
two story structures anticipated for development at the site However due to
uncertainties regarding the layout of new structures and detailed structural loading we
recommend that owners retain a qualified geotechnical engineer to evaluate the site
conditions for the planned development

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings and test pits Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until later time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from survey stakes in the field corresponding to survey points shown on the
Location of Lava Tube Parcel 4 plan transmitted by Hualalai Development Company on
The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Elevations of the borings were obtained
from the same plan
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report Groundwater was not
encountered in our borings or test pits However it must be noted that fluctuation may
occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil rock conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft
deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional
probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a
properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
