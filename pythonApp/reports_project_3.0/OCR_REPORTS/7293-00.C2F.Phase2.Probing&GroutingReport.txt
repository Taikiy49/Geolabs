Dear Mr. Marquez: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for Command and Control Facility 
(C2F) Complex Phase 2 - Main Operations project at Fort Shafter from January 12 to 21, 
2016. The probing and grouting observation work was performed in general accordance 
with the project specifications. 
Probing and Grouting Program 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the project in 
general accordance with the project plans and specifications. It should be noted that the 
probing requirements were modified in accordance with the government responses to 
RFI-0038 dated January 13, 2016, which requires the probe holes to be extended 2 times 
of the footing width or 10 feet into the underlying basalt rock below the bottom of the 
footing. The general contractor, Watts Constructors, surveyed the footing layout and 
marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations. The 
approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan, Plate 1. 
Pacific Ground Systems, Inc. performed the probing work for the project using a 
track-mounted drill rig equipped with air pneumatic percussion hammers.  The probe holes 
were about 3 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 28.5 to 40 feet below the 
existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 10 to 26 feet below the design 
footing bottom elevations.  After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe

holes were filled with a sand-cement pumpable grout.  The grout used to fill the probe

Our field engineer observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded the 
probe depths and approximate grout take. A total of 30 probe holes with an aggregate 
length of about 1,060 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled for the 
project.  Approximately 14.0 cubic yards of sand-cement grout was used to fill the probe 
holes drilled for the project.  A summary of our foundation probing and grouting 
observation is provided on Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
It should be noted that four probes, designated as Probe Nos. 12, 13, 14, and 23 
were unable to advance to the required depth below footing due to extremely hard drilling 
conditions. The probes were offset 12 inches from the original locations and redrilled as 
Probe Nos. 12R, 13R, 14R, and 23R. Individual probing and grouting logs are provided on 
Plates A-1 thru A-26. 
Based on our observation, we believe that all required probe holes were drilled to 
the depths meeting a minimum of 10 feet below the bottom of footing into the underlying 
basalt rock formation. No signification void and/or cavity was encountered during the 
probing and grouting operation.
