This letter report summarizes exploratory probing conducted at the Pier 2 Honolulu
Harbor project site. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter report.
Project Considerations
The project consists of constructing new communication monopoles at various
locations throughout the State of Hawaii. As part of the project, a new monopole of about
80 feet in height will be constructed at Pier 2 of Honolulu Harbor. Foundations for the new
monopole at Pier 2 consist of 66-inch diameter drilled shaft extending to about 40 feet
below the existing ground surface.
During the excavation of the drilled shaft foundation, significant undermining of the
- areas surrounding the temporary casing was observed. About 10 cubic yards of controlled
low strength material (CLSM) was placed to fill the undermined areas. To protect the
integrity of the drilled shaft, the temporary steel casing was abandoned in place. The
crosshole sonic logging (CSL) test results appeared favorable and the drilled shaft
foundation was recommended for acceptance. In order to confirm the assumed condition
of the area surrounding the drilled shaft foundation, a subsurface exploration consisting of
exploratory probing was desired.
Exploratory Probing
The subsurface conditions surrounding the installed drilled shaft were explored by
advancing six blunt-tip probe holes, designated as P-1 through P-6, extending to depths of
about 26.8 to 30.8 feet below the existing ground surface. The approximate probe

truck-mounted drill rig equipped with a 2-inch diameter blunt-tip rod with a standard
140-pound hammer falling 30 inches. Logs of Probings are provided on Plates A-1 through

The purpose of the probing was to evaluate the potential for presence of significant
voids or cavities in the areas surrounding the drilled shaft that may have a negative impact
on the monopole structure foundation. The probe holes indicate that the subsurface
materials generally consist of fill materials underlain by loose to very loose lagoonal
deposits extending down to about 26.5 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface.
Generally, these subsurface conditions are consistent with the anticipated subsurface
materials at the site. Vertical drops of up to about 6 to 18 inches of the blunt-tip rod were
observed in some of the probe holes at various depths. Considering the granular nature of
the lagoonal deposits and presence of shallow groundwater levels, we believe these areas
generally represent very loose zones of silty sands and gravel as opposed to an open void
or cavity.
Based on the results of the exploratory probing, we believe that the voids and/or
cavities observed during the drilled shaft foundation installation appear to have been
exposed during the drilled shaft excavation and filled with CLSM. Based on the probing
results, we do not believe that significant cavities and/or voids affecting the performance of
the monopole structure foundations are present in the areas surrounding the drilled shaft.
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based, in part,
upon information obtained from the field probing. Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the probe holes may occur. The field probe hole locations indicated
in this report are approximate, having been estimated by taping from visible features
shown on the aerial photography map dated July 2013 from Google Earth. The physical
location of the probe holes should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the methods used.
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the probe holes
depict the approximate boundaries between soil and/or rock types and, as such, may
denote a gradual transition. Water level data from the probe holes were measured at the
times shown on the graphic representations and/or presented in the text of this report.
However, it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides, rainfall,
groundwater withdrawal, and other factors.
