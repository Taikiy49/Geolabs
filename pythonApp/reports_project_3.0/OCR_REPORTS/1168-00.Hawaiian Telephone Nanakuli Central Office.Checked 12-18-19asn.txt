SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the western side of the Waianae
Mountain These mountains are the erosional remnants of the
Waianae Shield Volcano which was built by basaltic lava flows
Following the cessation of volcanic activity the volcano was
deeply dissected by erosion Following the initial period of
erosion the volcano began to subside This subsidence was
gradual and amounted to over 1200 feet During the subsidence
the original valleys cut into the Waianae Shield became drowned
and filled with coral algal reef deposits
High sea levels during the Pleistocene caused these coral algal
reefs to grow to elevations above the current sea level Recent
weathering and erosion have shaped these reefs to the present
surface topography

Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the site to depths between 15 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface soil conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 2
Description of our field exploration along with log of borings
and and laboratory testing are presented in the appendices of
this report
Soil Conditions
The three borings encountered a thin surface topsoil fill layer
about 6 to 12 inches thick of brown clayey silt over dense tan
color coral reef deposits to depths of about 15 feet
Groundwater was encountered in two of the borings ranging from
15 5 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
NAL LUNG
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a
thin surface layer of clayey fill over dense coral reef deposit

Laboratory tests indicate that the upper 6 to 12 inches of the
on site clayey surface soils generally exhibit a moderately
high swelling potential when compacted and then let saturated
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it
is our opinion that spread footing foundation will bear on the
underlying coral For slab on grade and parking area the upper
expansive clay should be removed down to the coral layer and
replaced with select borrow fill

Building Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations can be used to support
the
proposed structure

Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade and should bear on the dense coral reef
deposit Along the existing structure the new footing should
bear on the same adjacent footing level
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for the
light foundation loads on the dense coral sand The bearing
capacity value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind and or seismic
loading
Minimum footing width of 18 inches for spread footings and 12
inches for continuous footings are recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench the footing
should be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent Footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Estimated total and differential settlements are anticipated to
be on the order of less than one fourth of an inch

Lateral Pressures
The cable vault wall should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressure of 40 p c f equivalent fluid pressure New vault
footing excavation adjacent to the existing structure should be
done with care to avoid disturbing the existing shallow footing
Slab On Grade
Generally the on site brown clayey surface soils have a moder
ately high swell potential After its removal and replacement
with select fill such as coral gravel slab on grade construc
tion may be utilized
For the anticipated static floor loads a 4 inch slab over 4
inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended
above the underlying coral reef deposit
A plastic vapor barrier over 4 inches of 3 fine gravel may be
substituted for the base course should a capillary break be
deemed necessary to protect the floor covering A thin layer
of sand should be placed over the plastic barrier to aid in the
concrete curing and minimize shrinkage cracks
The subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement

Pavement
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavement will be used
For such a design a 2 inch A C on 4 inches of compacted
aggregate base course over coral subgrade soil is recommended
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading and
foundation construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and
backfilling operations be observed by a soils engineer during
construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon the above inspections
