Dear Mr Crabb
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of
the study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface
soil as foundation support for the proposed house The study
consisted of four hand auger probes within the proposed build
ing site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading
and compaction test record on the building site This letter
summarizes our observations and recommendations

living area is planned Based on the preliminary plans the
house will set back about 30 feet from Kahena Street

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lots are underlain
by more than 4 5 feet of grayish brown clay The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they
have moderate to high expansion potential Therefore for

Lot No 28 the slab on grade and foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly
desirable and recommended due to the sloping terrain and
expansive soil conditions Concrete slab on grade and drive
way should also be properly designed for expansive soil con
ditions
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should be extended to a minimum depth of 36 inches below the
ground surface The footings should be poured in place
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the house
to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible satur
ation and settlement or heaving of the foundations soils

Slab on Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage It appears
that the rear portion of the garage area would require cut up
to about 6 feet in order to obtain sufficient level area for
the garage Therefore better less expansive material may be
encountered in the rear portion of the garage excavation
The front portion of the garage will have less cut and would
encounter the expansive clay The on site clay soils are
expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils In order
to reduce the expansive effects of the clay where encountered
the expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth
of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and replaced with non expan
sive borrow soils such as select borrow or crusher waste com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 This overexcavation and replacement should be extended
for a distance of 3 feet beyond the perimeter of the slab
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer more than 4 5 feet thick of expansive clay soils were
encountered in the front portion of the lot driveway area
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay it should
be kept a minimum of 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay

Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive
material is required The above requirements should be fol
lowed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
If a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation
and replacement similar to the above driveway design is recom
mended to reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generallly slopes down at
about 6 1 H V ratio or flatter
Minor site grading is planned for the lot development
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material The on site clay soil should not be used
for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts

Basement Walls
Basement walls are planned around the garage level to retain
the proposed cuts of up to about 6 feet
1 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of
24 inches below the outside finished subgrade
2 Granular backfill such as select borrow or crusher
waste should be used behind the walls This would
improve drainage and reduce the compactive effort
required for the wall backfill which will help to
reduce the build up of excess lateral pressures behind
the walls
3 Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be
used only for the top one foot of backfill to reduce
seepage of surface run off behind the walls
4 A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes
should be utilized behind the walls
