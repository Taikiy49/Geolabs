This memorandum provides the results of our site reconnaissance and our pole
foundation recommendations for the Kalaeloa Boulevard Pole project in the Ewa district on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and project vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 and the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that the Kalaeloa Boulevard Pole project is part of the Kapolei
Interchange Complex Phase 2 project located in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii Ve understand that an existing electric power pole is tilting due to excavation
work that was performed adjacent to the pole
Based on our understanding the project structural engineer will be providing the
remedial measures to be taken Furthermore we understand that only the lateral bearing
pressure parameter based on the International Building Code IBC 2006 for the existing
soil conditions is needed from the geotechnical engineer
SITE RECONNAISSANCE
A site reconnaissance was performed on to observe the existing
distress pole condition The distressed pole was identified as Pole No 109X The
distressed pole was leaning towards the makai direction We understand that the pole is
made of fiberglass and the initial 60 foot pole length was reduced in length by 15 feet prior
to installation
A large gap between the embedded pole and the side of the ground about
4 5 inches in width was observed at the mauka side of the embedded base of the pole
The estimated depth of the separation between the pole and the existing ground extended
about 3 feet 9 inches down from the ground surface Photographs taken during our site
visit are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our site reconnaissance and available boring information we anticipate
that the embedded portion of the distress pole is within fill materials consisting of stiff
reddish brown clay with some sand and gravel We do not anticipate encountering
groundwater within the pole embedment depth
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The distressed pole Pole No 109X is an end pole for two parallel pole alignments
Therefore we believe that the structural loading to this pole is greater than the other poles
inside the parallel pole alignment with the critical loading in the makai direction We
understand that excavation work was performed on the makai side of the embedded pole
Based on our site observations and the identification tag on the pole it appears that
the pole embedment is about 5 feet 8 inches and 4 feet 2 inches on the mauka and makai
sides of the pole respectively Based on the separation measurement taken on the
mauka side of the pole it appears that the embedded portion of the mauka side of the pole
is about 1 foot 11 inches Based on the above the probable cause of the pole distress is
the inadequate pole embedment for the structural loading on the pole
We understand that the remedial solution being recommended by the structural
engineer is to enlarge the pole footing with concrete within the embedded depth In
addition we understand that the structural engineer will be using the pole equations in IBC
2006 and the recommended lateral bearing pressure is needed for his analysis
Based on the anticipated soil conditions a lateral bearing value up to 100 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth may be used This lateral bearing value may be
increased by one third ⅓ for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces The recommended lateral bearing value provided assumes that the embedded
portion of the pole will be in direct contact with the adjacent soil Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the lateral bearing resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected due to potential shrinkage of the clayey subgrade soils In addition it is
assumed that the pole foundation will be in a level ground condition
