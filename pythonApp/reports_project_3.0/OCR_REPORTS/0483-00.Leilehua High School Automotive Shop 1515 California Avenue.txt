INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation inves
tigation for the proposed Leilehua High School Automotive Shop
building The study consisted of subsurface borings laboratory
testing and engineering analyses as outlined in our proposal
dated Based on the results of our engineering
studies conclusions and recommendations pertinent to site devel
opment are presented later in this report
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The property ig located on the east side of the Leilehua High
School complex The building site approximately 80 by 52 feet
is located in the existing parking lot between the Metal and Wood
shops Its specific location is indicated on the attached site
plan Plate le The site generally drains towards the west al
though the relief is quite small Except for a portion on the
north side the area is presently covered with an existing concrete
pavement slab
The building is to be a one story concrete block building with
a small classroom mezzanine at the southwesterly corner The
maximum column loads are expected to be on the order of 60 kips
while the maximum wall loads are expected to be on thevorder of
4 kips per lineal foot There will be a concrete floor slab on
grade Minor grading operations will be required to prepare the
site for construction

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of three 3 borings ranging
in depth from 30 to 39 5 feet The drilling was accomplished
using a truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with 6 inch
diameter continuous flight auger The borings were continuously
inspected by an engineer from our firm The drilling contractor
was Northwest Exploration Services Inc
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all inch I D ring samples or Standard Penetra
tion Test samples were taken at selected depth intervals The
Standard Penetration Test samples were taken by driving a 2 0
inch 0 D split spoon sampler for 18 inches with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows
required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is an approxi
mate measure of the relative density or consistency of a soil
The number of blows to drive the ring sampler for 12 inches were
converted to equivalent SPT blow count These resistance values
are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were visually
classified in the field and representative material was placed
in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory for inspec
tion and testing

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
After the formation of the Koolau Volcanic Range whose consti
tuent lava flows interfinger and overlap the Waianae Range flows
the entire volcanic complex was deeply dissected by erosion
Subsequently the lava flows were subjected to an extended
period of weathering and they developed a red residual soil
containing spheroidally weathered implace boulders overlying
highly weathered basalt bedrock
Soils
The soils underlying the proposed school site can be roughly
subdivided into three strata
1 Very stiff red to dark brown clayey SILT overlies the site
to an average depth of 5 to 15 feet
2 An approximately 10 to 15 foot thick layer of stiff red to
multi colored clayey SILT with weathered COBBLES and
BOULDERS underlies the red surface SOILS
3 The bouldery clayey SILT overlies stiff to medium red to
multi colored clayey SILT weathered BASALT at a depth of
approximately 30 feet below the surface

recommendations are presented The foundation recommendations
are based on anticipated loads

conventional shallow footings Slab on grade may be used
for floor support

Preparation of Ground
If any local soft spots are encountered in areas to
receive fill they should be removed and replaced with
compacted structural fill Areas to receive fill
should be scarified to a depth of 9 inches and should
be watered and recompacted according to the requirements

Fill Material
The onsite clayey silt soil can be utilized for conm

to achieve planned floor slab elevation the material
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to importation Imported fill shall be of a
granular nonexpansive nature and should meet the follow
ing specifications

b Less than 5 by weight passing the 200 sieve
c Maximum particle size 6 inches but not less
than 90 passing the 3 4 sieve
d Percent expansion less than 4 under a surcharge
load of 60 pounds per square foot

Structural Fill Placement
Approved fill material as described in item B 3 above
should be placed in maximum 9 inch lifts and compacted
to 90 relative compaction using ASTM D 1557 70 as a
standard All lifts must be placed horizontally
Finish Grade
To provide for slab support portions the upper one foot
of soils beneath the finish subgrade over the entire
building pad should be removed and recompacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density If fill is
required to achieve floor slab grade it will meet this
requirement
Compaction Equipment
Due to the relatively fine grained nature of the anti

foot roller should prove to be effective compaction
equipment

Expansive Soil
Laboratory tests indicate that the onsite soils are moderately

Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 250
pounds per cubic foot The surface one foot of soil should
be neglected for passive pressure unless it is confined
beneath a concrete floor slab Friction may be taken at

LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
_ project are included in this Appendix

Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests

Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve

Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
4
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sampleswas placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
