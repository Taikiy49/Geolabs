I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our preliminary
feasibility investigation to determine the subsurface
soil and foundation conditions for the project The pur
pose of the investigation was to determine if the subsurface
soil conditions are adequate to support the proposed struc
ture The study included subsurface exploration and soil
sampling laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The report summarizes our work on the project and presents
te
preliminary general recommendations pertinent to development
Final recommendations will be provided in the second stage
of the investigation
Project Description
The proposed site is located on the north side of Ohai Street
between Wainaku Street and Puneo Street The property is
approximately 145 feet wide along Ohai Street 190 feet wide
along Waipilopilo Stream and approximately 345 feet deep
The proposed building is planned as a 3 story wood frame
building approximately 110 feet wide by 200 feet long and
will be located near the front of the site

II FIELD EXPLORATION
_ Two subsurface borings 17 and 29 feet deep were drilled on
September 1 and 2 1972 under the supervision of our firm The
borings were accomplished using a portable minuteman rotary
drilling equipment with continuous flight auger
The boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs
are presented in Appendix A
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were made at regular
intervals The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving
a 24inch 0 D split spoon sampler 24 inches with 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of
blows required to drive the samples the middle 12 inches is
termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of
a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field in accord
ance with the Unified Soil Classification The representative
material was placed in air tight containers and returned to our
laboratory for inspection and testing

III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site is underlain by approximately 20 to 30 feet of clayey
SILT Pahala Ash overlying a basalt flow
The area north of the Wailuku River in Hilo is underlain by the
lava flows of Mauna Kea During the last eruptive phase of
Mauna Kea large amounts 20 to 30 feet thick of volcanic ash
were deposited over the older basalts
Intense tropical weathering and high over 100 inches a year
rainfall has resulted in the Pahala Ash being weathered to a
Clayey SILT soil with a relatively high moisture content up to
211 of dry weight

A General
The subsurface condtions encountered during the investigation
were marginal and special site preparation will be required
However it is our opinion that the proposed development is
feasible from an engineering standpoint provided our recon

Grading
Site grading should be kept to a minimum Any grading
performed on the site will require special procedures to
minimize disturbance of the underlying SILT Pahala Ash
All deleterious material such as topsoil etc will have
to be wasted from the site Also it appears that the on
site material will not be suitable for structural fill
Detailed grading and site preparation recommendations can
be presented after site grading plans and the final soils
investigation have been completed
Foundations
_ Light to moderate structural loads may be supported on spread
or continuous footings The SILT Pahala Ash has a rela
tively low allowable bearing capacity and settlement on the
order of 1 to 2 inches should be anticipated
If footings become excessively large and or the relatively
high settlement values are not tolerable a pile support
system could be utilized Piles founded on the underlying
hard basalt could be designed for relatively high capacities
with negligible settlement

Db Slope Stability
_ Slope stability determination was outside the scope of
this investigation Existing slopes near the back of the
property should be safe as long as they are not excessively
disturbed or oversteepened during construction Buildings
and or fills should be set back at least 25 feet from the
top of the existing slope
E Drainage
Provisions for site drainage both during and after construc
tion must be considered to prevent slope erosion
F Additional Investigation
At least 2 additional subsurface borings will be required
to adequately define the subsurface conditions Additional
laboratory testing and engineering analyses will also be
required before final recommendations can be presented
