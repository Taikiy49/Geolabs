Building Subgrade Preparation
Proposed Kahaluu Commercial Development
Recently we have made visits to the above referenced project site to
observe and test the preparation of the subgrade for structural fill
placement The contractor has removed most of the previously existing
surcharge materials to expose soils which are soft and saturated at near
original subgrade elevation
Our previous subsurface exploration of the project site disclosed
that the upper five 5 to six 6 feet of soils were stiff and
competent During our latest visit a hand auger boring revealed that
these conditions appear to be unchanged below the top one foot of surface
soils with the exception of some isolated areas Laboratory testing of
the soils immediately below those disturbed by recent earthwork indicate
that the moisture content of these soils is in the range of 35 of their
dry weight which would allow for adequate compaction It is our opinion
that the existing surface soils are saturated and soft due to wet weather
conditions trafficking of the site with tire loaded equipment causing
ruts with standing water and the failure to properly compact the subgrade
prior to surcharge placement
It is our opinion that the existing subgrade condition can be
improved by grading the site so that water does not collect on the
building pad and aerating the soils during dry weather conditions to bring
them to near optimm moisture content Utilization of track mounted
construction equipment in lieu of tire mounted will distribute the loads
more evenly and allow the soils to set up more readily
