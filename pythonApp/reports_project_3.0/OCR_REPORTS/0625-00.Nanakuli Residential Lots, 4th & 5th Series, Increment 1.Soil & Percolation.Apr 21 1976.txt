SUMMARY
The subsurface explorations disclosed competent
boulderly and weathered rock formation near the
surface at the existing site The on site surface
soil is moderately expansive
The percolation tests indicated low to moderate
subsurface permeability rates
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface
conditions at the site and to provide recommendations for
design and construction of the proposed single family sub
division development The percolation tests were used to
measure the in situ soil seepage characteristics This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed fourth and fifth series of Nanakuli residence lots
are located south of Nanakuli High School in Nanakuli Valley
Oahu Hawaii The sites are about mile mauka of Farrington
Highway and along the north side of Nanakuli Stream
Page 2
W
The makai portion of the development is located across the
Nanakuli High School tennis courts and baseball field near
an existing pump station The remaining portion of the site
extending 300 feet mauka along the Nanakuli Avenue Extension
The existing grounds generally slope from the Nanakuli Avenue
boundary down toward the Nanakuli Stream bank at about 5 to
15 gradients In some area near the boundary steeper slopes
up to 65 gradients are noted down to the stream channel
There was no visible sign of slope movement or instability
in this area at the time of our field exploration
An existing roadway which extends from Nanakuli Avenue runs
along the eastern property line There is an extension road
way from Nanakuli Avenue to the abandoned quarry south of the
project site Exposed weathered rock was noted on the higher
portion of this roadway At the lower southern portion there
is A C paving The whole site is generally covered with grass
brush and trees Scattered cobbles and boulders were noted on
the ground surface A wire fence was observed running along
Nanakuli Avenue
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface investigation for the proposed development site
consisted of excavating a total of 8 test pits and drilding Zz
test borings The test pit and boring locations are shown on
the enclosed site plan Plate l
Page 3
All backhoe test pits were less than 4 feet deep due to the
encountering of boulders or weathered rock at the bottom of
the test pits
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix along
with the boring and test pit logs and laboratory results
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The two parcels which make up Series 4 and 5 in Increment I
will provide a total of 45 single family residential lots
Trunk sewer connections are not available for the proposed
development at this stage and sewage disposal by means of
cesspool is anticipated
Site improvement will include the construction of necessary
roadways to the subdivision lots from Nanakuli Avenue
The final grading plan is not available at this time It
is anticipated that cut and fill will not exceed 15 feet
high
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Geology
The Nanakuli Valley geology consists of successions of coral
alluvium talus and flows of lava in the middle of the Waianae
volcanic series The valley area consists of mostly younger
alluvium and reworked older alluvium near stream channels with
talus from near by Puu Manawahua The talus is poorly unsolidated
gravelly clay and boulders The permeability of these rocks are
Page 4
moderately permeable
Soil Gonditions
The exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil conditions
at the project site Generally a 2 foot deep soil layer
covers most of the site Below this surface soil and coral
were encountered at the makai parcel In the mauka area
weathered rock boulders and eouluyatam formations were noted
in the test pits and boring
The surface soils in the makai portion of the development are
expansive and consist of brown clayey silt and silty clay
Brown clay and gray Adobe clay surface soils were encountered
in the upper mauka area
At the makai portion coral was noted at test pit no 1 2 feet
below the existing ground surface and in boring no 1 it was
encountered from 13 to 18 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not noted during the field exploration
PERCOLATION TESTS
Percolation tests were performed in 4 inch diameter bore holes
Prior to testing the bore holes were filled with water and the
soils were allowed to become saturated Each of the holes was
then refilled with water to the top and the levels were recorded
at various time intervals Plate A 4 in the appendix summarizes
the results of the percolation tests
Page 5
The percolation test at boring no 1 where coral was
encountered indicated a percolation rate of 11 6 gallons
per hour or about 0 65 gallon per square foot per hour
A lower percolation rate was recorded in boring no 2
which extended to a depth of 20 feet The coral material
encountered at boring no 1 area was not present at this
location Instead dense decomposed rock and colluvium
deposits exist below this mauka area The computed perco
lation rate obtained is about 0 03 gallon per square foot
per hour
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The mauka project site is covered by a 2 foot layer of
moderately to highly expansive clay soils Ideally site
grading should be designed to remove this surface clay soils
and to bury them in deep fill areas
If slab on grade or roadways should have to be placed on the
upper clayey soil layer they should be designed for the under
lying expansive soil conditions
Site Grading
If possible it is recommended that the expansive surface soil
layer be removed from the proposed building pad areas and the
removed material either be wasted or placed in deep fill area
a minimum of 2 feet below slab and roadway subgrades
Page 6
W
If borrow fill is required it should be non expansive
materials
At the makai side reddish brown surface soils are less
expansive and can be used as a source of fill material
up to two feet below finish grade The decomposed rock
formation encountered at the upper mauka portion of the
site is another source of select fill material
Prior to receiving fill the existing ground surface should
be grubbed to removed the surface vegetations topsoil and
any previously placed loose fills The upper six inches of
the existing ground surface should then be scarified brought
to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D1557
7a
For ground surface greater than 5 horizontal to l vertical
slope ratio the ground surface should be keyed and benched
to properly bond the new fills to the existing slope
Additional fills should than be placed in 9 inch horizontal
lifts compacted to the above specified density Our general
specifications for fill placement compaction and earthwork
supervision are contained in the grading specifications iné
cluded with this report
Page 7
W
Trench Excavations
Isolated boulders and weathered rock can be expected through
out the site during utility line excavations Extremely hard
rock is not anticipated at this site However due to the
difficulties of interpreting subsurface boring explorations
_in boulders and weathered rock formation it is recommended
that a hard rock clause be included within the contract
documents
Slopes
Cut slopes into the underlying colluvium and decomposed rock
can be designed up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical ratio 1 1
up to a maximum height of 8 feet For cuts exceeding 8 foot
height a 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 14 1 slope ratio can
be used
Fill slopes can be designed at a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
2 1 ratio For slopes greater than 15 feet high benches
should be placed at 15 foot high interval
To minimize erosion of the on site soils slopes should be
planted immediately after construction
Foundations
The light single family structures are of wood framed con
struction Allowable boing pressure of 2 000 pounds per
square foot may be used for the footing bearing upon the
stiff on site soil or on compacted fill
Page 8
W
Buildings next to existing slopesalong the stream bank should
have a minimum of 10 feet set back from the top of the slope
or its footing should extend below the slope surface such
that the outer edge of footing should have a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet to the slope surface For erosion
protection the footing excavation should be backfilled with
concrete to match the upper slope surface
For fill less than 10 feet high a 5 foot building slab set
back would be adequate
Retaining Wall
High retaining wall with fill on the existing slope surtace is
generally not recommended unless investigated on an individuai
basis However it is anticipated that some retaining wall up
to about 8 feet high might be required in some lots along the
Nanakuli Avenue
Based on the present soil information it appears that the wail
footing will bear on the stiff underlying colluvium or decomposed
rock formation
For design of such a retaining wall it may be assumed that the
soil will impose a lateral pressure equal to that developed by
a fluid with a density of 35 pounds per cubic foot if non expan
sive soil is used for backfill The on site adobe soil should
not be used to backfill behind such retaining wall Resistance
to horizontal sliding may be provided by a combination of passive
Page 9
W O 625 00
pressure and friction along the bottom of footings Passive
pressure may be assumed equal to the equivalent fluid density
of 300 pounds per cubic foot The coefficient of friction
between concrete and site soil may be taken at 0 4 Adequate
drainage system should be designed to prevent the build up of
excessive pore pressure behind retaining wall
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade can be utilized without special provision for
expansive soil if the slabs are placed on the underlying de
composed rock A 4 inch layer of S4C cushion material should
be placed above the rock surface Slab on grade with floor
covering sensitive moisture should be protected with a plastic
vapor barrier
For floor slab supported on expansive clay soil a 4 inch
layer of base course rock should be provided along with a
minimum 18 inch capping of non expansive materials The
underlying colluvium can be utilized as a source of non
expansive fill
The subgrade should be compacted wet of its optimum moisture
content to a minimum 90 of its dry density as established by
designation
At the makai half of the site where the existing surface soil
is moderately expansive slab on grade with 18 inch deep rein
forced perimeter footings should be used if the slab subgrade
is located on this surface layer Plates B 1 and B 2 illustrate
Page 10
W
the details of this kind of slab construction
Roadway Pavement
Surface samples of the on site clayey soils were tested in
the laboratory The CBR test results indicated low values
of 2 4 to 3 6 percent with a high expansion values of 6 to
8 percent Based upon the present data the following pave
ment section may be used if the pavement is located on clayey
fill soils
2 Asphalt Concrete
6 Base Course
6 Select Borrow
18 Borrow
32 Total Pavement Thickness
However the low CBR value surface clay soil is only 2 feet
thick at the site therefore the roadway subgrade elevation
can be lowered to bear on the underlying boulderly and weathered
rock formation or upon select roadway fill These will sub
stantially reduce the pavement section to 2 inch asphalt concrete
on 6 inch base course if non expansive select borrow or under
lying decomposed rock and colluvium materials are used
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR of 85 and conforming with section 31 2 Standard Speci
fication for Public Work Construction or equivalent Non expansive
select borrow should have a minimum CBR value of 12 and a Plasticity
Index less than 20 Additional CBR tests should be performed on
actual subgrade soil samples at the time of construction and the
above design sections may be revised accordingly

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
The earthwork should be inspected by a soil engineer to
monitor the removal of the surface clay soil and the use
of it only in the deep fill area 3 feet below future

posed rock or other approved borrow fill materials The
subgrade soil should be kept moist prior to base course and
slab construction
All imported fill should be non expansive material and
approved by the soil engineer prior to placing on the site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 2 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
depths of the borings terminated at 19 0 to 20 0 feet below
the existing ground surface In addition 8 test pits were
dug down to depth of 4 feet or less below the existing ground
surface with a backhoe The locations of the borings and
test pits are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plates 1 and 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling and excavating operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring and Test Pit Logs
Plates A 1 through A 3
In the boring soil samples were obtained by driving a standard
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a dis
tance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appro
priate sample depth
The percolation tests were performed by filling the borings with
water and allowing the soils to become saturated The holes were
then refilled with water and the drop of water level was recorded
A summary of the percolation rates is included in Plate A 4
Appendix A 2

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
GEOLABS HAWAII
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
TE Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
Ve
ae
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of Seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
295
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
VI
qe
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
