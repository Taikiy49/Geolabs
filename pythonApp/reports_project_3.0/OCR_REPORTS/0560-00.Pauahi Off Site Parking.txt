INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary soil and
foundation investigation for the proposed Offstreet Public
Parking Facility to be constructed on Parcels A 2 and A 3 of
Block A Pauahi Project
The purpose of the subsurface investigation is to define general
subsurface conditions beneath the site by drilling a limited
number of borings within the available open areas These explo
rations provide general subsurface soil information to assist in
the initial foundation design and feasibility studies
W Page 1975
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The investigation is limited to Parcels A 2 and A 3 of Block A
Pauahi Project No Hawaii R 15 at the northeast corner of the
existing Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu It is bound by
Beretania Street to the north Maunakea Street to the east
Pauahi Street to the south and River Street and Nuuanu Stream
to the west
It is presently planned to develop Parcels A 2 and A 3 of Block
A into a parking facility with an area of approximately 72 000
square feet The remaining 17 683 square feet of Block A will
be for commercial purposes River Street and Pauahi Street
will be converted into pedestrian malls
Site Conditions
Presently the site is occupied by apartments theatres restau
rants and wood frame dwellings generally one to two stories in
height Several vacant lots are included and are presently
used for parking The site has a gentle approximately 5 per
cent slope towards Nuuanu Stream It varies in elevation
from elevation 19 5 on Maunakea Street to approximately eleva
along River Street
The presently proposed construction will consist of a multi
level parking facility Future development may include high
rise housing

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The soils at the proposed site are derived from both volcanic
and alluvial depositions Early eruptions of the Honolulu Vol
canic Series took place at the head of Nuuanu Valley The lava
flows extended far beyond the mouth of the valley at least as
far as Iwilei where borings near the pineapple canneries pene
trated 45 feet of lava beneath two layers of coral limestone
G A MacDonald 1970 The lava pebbles and organic silts
encountered in this area are indicative of a low energy deposi
tional environment similar to the present day Kaneohe Bay As
the island sank alluvial sediments accumulated forming layers
of silt and sand and in some cases organic materials
These deposits may or may not be underlain by coral reefs which
also develop in the shallow water environments It appears that
the initial coral reefs were subsequently smothered by silt and
clay size sediments from the continuous erosion of the Koolau
Mountain Range
Subsurface Explorations
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing four
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 The existing structures limited the site
access Boring 1 was therefore drilled outside Parcels A 2 and
W Page 1975
A 3 to provide information on subsurface conditions near Nuuanu
Stream A detailed description of the field explorations methods
and accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and the laboratory test results
The findings of the subsurface explorations indicate two general
subsurface profiles Borings 1 and 2 penetrated thick soft
alluvial deposits overlying hard coral or decomposed basalt en
countered more than 55 feet below the present ground surface
The alluvial deposits are predominantly silty but contained clay
sand gravel and organic materials They vary from very soft to
stiff in consistency where cohesive and are loose to medium dense
where granular
Borings 3 and 4 encountered distinctly different subsurface condi
tions At these borings the site is blanketed by two feet of
cohesive fill underlain by a medium to very dense silty sand
with coral gravel The sand is underlain by a hard coral which
extended to a depth of 25 feet in Boring 4 the limit of the
explorations and to a depth of 28 feet in Boring 3 Boring 3
encountered extremely stiff silty clay and decomposed rock be
neath the coral

Laboratory Testing
A series of consolidation tests were performed on samples from
Boring 1 to measure the response of the soil to vertical load
ings These tests indicate that the alluvial soils are satu
rated and highly compressible The per cent consolidation
varied from 16 to 18 per cent at 10 kip per square foot load
ings A direct shear test was also run on a sample of near sur
face soil from Boring 2 The test showed a cohesion of 90 pounds
per square foot and a friction angle of 39 degrees which is due
in part to high sand content
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The two different subsurface conditions at the site necessitate two
types of foundations The deep compressible soils encountered
in Borings 1 and 2 cannot provide adequate support for even lightly
loaded structures without large settlements Foundations pene
trating the upper soil and extending to the lower coral or rock
will be required for the support of the proposed structures
In the vicinity of Borings 3 and 4 the medium dense to very dense
sand will provide adequate support for lightly loaded foundations
High capacity piles can be developed at a relatively shallow
depth upon the underlying coral
W O 560 00 Page 1975
The preliminary investigation provides general indications of
the subsurface conditions When the actual building locations
are established it will be necessary to conduct a more detailed
subsurface investigation to determine the uniformity of the soil
deposits and to delinate and define the transition between the
two different subsurface conditions
Foundations near Borings 1 and 2
As previously discussed Borings 1 and 2 encountered 67 and 56
feet respectively of compressible alluvial soils containing
layers of organic materials The consolidation tests show that
the soils are highly compressible and slow draining This can
result in large settlements over an extended period of time for
foundations upon these soils
Foundations in this area should therefore consist of piles
driven through the upper compressible alluvium to the under
lying coral or rock deposits In Boring 1 the coral was
encountered at a depth of 67 feet at elevation 60 feet In
Boring 2 the coral was not encountered and was replaced by a
layer of hard decomposed basalt at a depth of 56 feet eleva
tion 47 feet It is estimated that a 100 ton capacity pile
can be developed with a 16 inch precast octagonal concrete
pile driven to the decomposed basalt or coral layer
Fills or large surface loadings should be avoided in this area
to prevent the large settlements which they would produce
W O 560 00 Page 1975
Even shallow fills over large areas have the same effect as
heavily loaded foundations
Foundations near Borings 3 and 4
In the vicinity of Borings 3 and 4 lightly loaded spread or
line foundations can be designed to bear upon the medium to very
dense coral sand encountered approximately two feet below the
existing ground surface This material can support maximum
contact bearing pressures of 4 000 pounds per square foot with
out detrimental settlements
Higher capacity foundations can be obtained by bearing upon
the underlying hard coral encountered at a depth of 12 to 14
feet below the existing ground surface elevation 4 feet to
elevation 1 feet It is anticipated that this material will
provide a 40 ton design capacity with a 10 inch square concrete
pile A 12 inch square concrete pile should develop approxi
mately 60 tons and a 16 inch octagonal pile approximately 100
tons
Groundwater Conditions
At the time of our subsurface investigation the groundwater
levels were measured between elevations 0 and 2 feet This
is near or below the surface of the coral in the vicinity of
Borings 3 and 4 It therefore appears feasible to excavate to
the coral layer without extensive dewatering This would per
mit spread foundation to be used with deep excavations Some
W Page 1975
artesian flow was encountered in Boring 1 at a depth of approxi
mately 50 feet but should not affect near surface excavations
Floor Slabs
The subsurface explorations did not encounter expansive soils
Slab on grade construction can therefore be utilized In the
vicinity of Boring 1 and 2 significant settlement may occur if
the compressible soils are loaded with more than 100 pounds per
square foot All floor slabs should be divorced from load bear
ing members to minimize distresses due to small differential
settlements
Retaining Walls
The subsurface conditions similiar to those encountered in
Borings 1 and 2 are not conducive to basements or below ground
parking levels Retaining walls within this area would require
pile foundations for support The wet soils would also produce
high lateral forces equal to approximately 60 pounds per cubic
foot equivalent fluid pressure
Retaining walls supporting the silty sand near Borings 3 and 4
could be designed for equivalent fluid pressures of 35 pounds
per cubic foot In addition if the excavation is extended to
the surface of the coral the foundations could be designed to
bear upon the coral with maximum allowable contact bearing
pressures of 8 000 pounds per square foot and a frictional fac
tor of 0 5
W Page 1975
Site Preparation
It will be necessary to remove the existing foundations and
concrete floor slabs prior to the new construction The site
should be proof rolled to delineate any surface soft zones
which should be removed and replaced with compacted granular
soils The site should be checked for previously filled exca
vations such as abandoned cesspools or cisterns
Investigation Limitations
The findings and conclusions given in this report are based
upon the available information and are intended as a prelimi
nary investigation to provide general information for the
initial foundation design and site feasibility study
Additional test borings and analysis are recommended to develop
the final design recommendations Particular attention should
be given to the delineation of the extent of the two subsurface
conditions disclosed by this investigation

FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings varied from 25 0 to 77 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan and Boring Locations Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A11 soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs Plate A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ora
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free fall
ing a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depth
