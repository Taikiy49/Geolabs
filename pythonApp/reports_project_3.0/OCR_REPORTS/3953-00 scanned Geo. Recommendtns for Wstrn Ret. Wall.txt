Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the observations made at the site visit and our experience with the soils
in the general vicinity of the project site it appears that the slope at the western side of
the property was constructed by placing a poorly compacted sliver fill onto a steep slope
The residual undisturbed soil material appears to be similar in composition to the soft
uncompacted fill material It is likely that this fill material was taken from a nearby cut
during the construction of this subdivision In general the exposed soil material appeared
to be reddish brown clayey silt The reported slope movement is probably due to the
uncompacted fill material It should be noted that this proposed retaining wall at the
property line should stabilize the ground uphill of the wall but not at the slope of the
neighboring lot below the retaining wall
The excavation revealed very stiff undisturbed reddish brown clayey silt and soft
uncompacted reddish brown clayey silt fill In general we believe that the proposed
retaining wall may be supported on a shallow foundation system bearing on the very stiff
undisturbed reddish brown clayey silt At the southern 20 foot length of the retaining wall
footing the footing should be deepened past the fill material to an additional depth of
3 feet For this deepened footing a stepped footing should be constructed
Due to the potential for undermining of the neighbor s rock wall at the southern end
of the proposed retaining wall grouting or backfilling of the voids at the rock wall footing
should be performed During construction proper shoring or an adequate set back
distance should be maintained to avoid damage to the rock wall from undermining

An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may
be utilized for the maximum edge pressure for foundations bearing on the very stiff
undisturbed reddish brown clayey silt These bearing values are for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces Estimated total settlements are on the order of 1 inch
The embedment depth at the downhill side of the retaining wall should be
maintained at a minimum of 2 feet The minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet
measured from the outside edge of the footing to the slope face should be maintained
Site grading at the neighboring lot should be limited to accommodate these
recommendations
If any addition to the existing house or any additional structures are planned within
a 1H 1V plane projected upward from the retaining wall heel the current proposed
retaining wall should be designed for these anticipated loads
An active earth pressure expressed in equivalent fluid pressure of 45 p c f for
on site level backfill may be used for walls that are free to deflect by as much as
0 5 percent of the wall height If the top of the wall is not free to deflect beyond this
degree the design active pressure should be increased by 50 percent This lateral earth
pressure does not include hydrostatic pressure that might be caused by groundwater
trapped behind the wails
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 150 p s f
This assumes that the soil around the footing is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
The passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient
of friction of 0 4 may be used
Installation of a root barrier may be considered to inhibit root growth towards the
retaining wall that may create pathways for water and possibly a hydrostatic condition
Retaining wall backfill should consist of either the on site reddish brown clayey silt
or select borrow material The backfill should be compacted to a minimum 90 percent
relative compaction ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 to prevent water infiltration
Over compaction of the retaining wall backfill should be avoided
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately around a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall
