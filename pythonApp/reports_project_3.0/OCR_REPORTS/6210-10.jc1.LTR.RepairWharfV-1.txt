This report summarizes our construction quality control CQC geotechnical
specialist services performed during the concrete fender pile installation for Repair V1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at Victor Dock 1 within the Pearl City Peninsula area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consisted of installing 24 inch square precast
prestressed concrete fender piles A total of 49 concrete fender piles were planned for the
wharf repair project The approximate locations and identification numbers of the concrete
piles are shown on the plans Sheet S 401 and S 402 for the reference

It should be noted that all elevations referenced in this report are based on the
U S Navy Datum where Elevation 100 feet is equivalent to Mean Lower Low Water We
understand that the top of pile elevation is set at about 108 83 feet Healy Tibbitts
Builders Inc conducted the construction of the fender pile installation
Fender Pile Installation
A spudding operation consisting of advancing the spud at 25 fender pile locations
was performed to refine and or confirm the fender pile lengths prior to casting the concrete
piles for the production piling work In general a 100 foot long 12 inch diameter close end
cylindrical steel spud was used with a total weigh of about 14 000 pounds The spud was
raised a minimum height of 5 feet and then dropped free fall The spudding operation was
terminated when it reached the design fender pile tip elevation or refusal condition little or
no penetration
The spudding operation was conducted on November 11 and 12 2009 under the
near continuous observations by an on site CQC specialist from our office The spudding
logs were previously transmitted to Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc and is also included in
Appendix A of this report
Based on the results of the probing operation the tip elevations of the production
fender piles were revised as summarized in the following table

completed the production fender pile installation on The on site CQC

predrilling logs and pile driving logs are presented in Appendices B and C respectively
The fender pile locations were predrilled prior to driving In general the contractor
used a SEECO lead mounted drill supported by a crane for the predrilling operation The
purpose of the predrilling is to clear the pile driving path from obstructions and to reduce
the need for hard driving conditions to install the fender piles to the recommended tip
elevations

It should be noted that the predrilling encountered a layer of very stiff to hard
alluvium interbedded with cobble gravel To reduce potential hard driving conditions and
potential pile damage due to the hard driving the contractor requested to extend the
predrilling tip from 41 feet as previously recommended to 38 feet for Pile Nos 1 through
29 and to 33 feet for Pile Nos 30 through 49 referring to RFI No 022D Additional field
adjustments were made by further deepening the predrilling to elevations about 36 to
39 feet to reduce potential hard pile driving condition for Pile Nos 1 though 29
Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc used a Junttan HHK7a hydraulic impact hammer for the
fender pile driving operations The pile hammer has a maximum rated energy of
approximately 61 600 foot pounds with a maximum stroke of about 4 feet A total of
49 fender piles were driven to the required tip elevations of about 33 and 30 feet
Limitations

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation services were performed

warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is
not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to plans and specifications
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
