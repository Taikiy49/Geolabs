INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings of our injection well performance evaluation for the
Kuilima Resort Wastewater Treatment Plant in Kahuku on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
findings and recommendations presented herein are based on site observations made during
a site visit the performance of short term injection testing of the wells and personal
communication with operators and managers of the treatment plant facility The approximate
locations of the treatment plant facility and the injection well field are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated May 22 2000
and the State of Hawaii Department of Health Underground Injection Control UIC Injection
Well Status Report Guidelines Version 1 dated June 1992
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kuilima Resort Wastewater Treatment Plant injection wells are utilized for the
disposal of secondary treated effluent generated within the Kuilima Resort development The
majority of the effluent treated at the plant is used for irrigation of the resort golf course within
the Kuilima Resort complex Therefore the wells are used for the injection of treated effluent
only on a stand by basis during periods of time when the effluent cannot be utilized for
irrigation purposes generally due to wet weather conditions
As a condition of the Underground Injection Control UIC permit UIC Permit
No UO 1532 issued for the facility it is required that periodic monitoring of the wells be made
by a professional geologist or engineer familiar with injection well operations Geolabs Inc has

DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
The wells are located on the easterly side of the tee boxes for the 10th Hole of Golf
Course G 2 The wells are situated in an open grass area between the tees and the moat
around Punahoolapa Marsh The topography of the well field area is generally level with some
topographic mounds and depressions associated with the adjacent golf course terrain Based
on a topographic map of the well field and vicinity ground surface elevations range from about
5 to 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL At the time of our site visit on the weather
conditions were dry under mostly sunny skies The ocean tide levels were noted as being low
on the order of about 0 1 feet MSL at the time of our site visit No evidence of surface water
ponding was visible in the vicinity of the well field or well vaults
The well heads are enclosed in below ground sump vaults of approximately 4 feet by
4 feet in plan dimension and about 5 feet in depth The vaults are covered with hinged steel
plate doors and are approximately flush with the adjacent grass covered ground surface
Inside the vaults the well heads consist of steel tees with flange plates bolted to their
tops for security and to allow the build up of additional head if needed These plates were

1994 two inch diameter inspection ports were installed in the well covers to eliminate the need
for removal of the entire top plate during future inspection testing However to allow complete
access to the well interior for the performance of the short term injection tests and visual
inspection of the exposed well casing the top plate of each well was removed
Well Field Usage
In general only two of the three wells are placed in use for effluent disposal at any time
The inactive well is taken out of operation on a rotating basis so that at any given time only
two wells will be in operation Flow to an individual well is controlled by a valve located
adjacent to each well head A main control valve diverts the effluent flow from the treatment
plant to either the well field or the lagoon which serves as storage for golf course irrigation
water
Based on the available Daily Operating Log records for the past 12 months the
following generalizations regarding effluent flow to the well field may be made
For the period from June 1999 through mid October 1999 injection well
usage was intermittent with relatively low flow volume averaging about
a day or two per week During this period of time the average daily flows
were generally less than about 40 000 to 50 000 gallons per day
For the period from mid October 1999 through mid November 1999 the
injection well usage generally increased to several consecutive days in
a row During this time period the daily flow varied considerably but
averaged on the order of about 250 000 gallons per day There were
some intermittent days of no flow to the well field and a few days
reaching about 400 000 to per day

the need for irrigation is lower and as a result more effluent is directed to the well field for
injection disposal
Based on our review of the Daily Operating Logs the individual injection wells are
placed in service on a rotating basis The current frequency of well rotation appears to be
adequate to maintain the generally good operational condition of the injection well system
Injection Well Redevelopment
Based on our conversation with the treatment plant supervisory personnel the injection
wells were redeveloped in early May 2000 in an effort to maintain the disposal capacity of the
system A 130 cubic feet per minute cfm air compressor was utilized to blow out the injection
wells by lowering the air line into the bottom of the well The discharge of well water produced
by the airlift cleaning was contained within the well vault and pumped to a tanker truck for
proper disposal

Plant personnel reported that the well cleaning procedure was effective in removing
some fine sand and silt sediment from the wells Furthermore following the well cleaning
efforts the measured head levels during injection operation were observed to be slightly lower
than previous measurements recorded The lower head levels during operation indicate that
the injection wells appear to have sustained some increase in effluent disposal capacity as a
result of the well cleaning

Injection System
To regulate the system valves are used to isolate individual wells so that one or a
combination of the wells may be used for the injection of effluent At the time of our site visit
the system operation was set up such that Well Nos 2 and 3 were on line as the active wells
for the injection of effluent Well No 1 was isolated from the system to serve as an emergency
back up to the system
The system is equipped with a flow totalizing meter which measures the flow volume
in gallons This meter measures the total flow to the well field rather than to individual wells
In addition the meter registers the cumulative flow in gallons Observation of specific valves
that are open during an injection period may be used in conjunction with the totalizing meter
readings to roughly estimate the flow to an individual well
At the time of our site visit the totalizing meter registered a cumulative reading of about
on
Daily Flow to Well Field
Based on a review of the Daily Operating Logs of the injection well system for the
preceding 12 months daily flows ranged from zero to a maximum single day flow of about
524 000 gallons per day Average daily flows appear to be on the order of about 200 000 to
300 000 gallons per day during heavy usage of the wells In general the months of December
and January appear to be the most active periods of injection well usage due to the higher
incidence of rainfall and reduced irrigation needs for the golf course
Short Term Injection Tests
Short term injection tests were performed on each of the three wells by diverting treated
effluent from the treatment plant into each well separately Each well was isolated from the

individual wells
During injection testing the head level response was measured and recorded at
intervals of 1 minute to several minutes using a tape measure with the datum reference
established at the top of the well casing Readings from the totalizing meter were recorded
during the short term injection test to obtain the total quantity of injected effluent and to
estimate the average flow rate into each well
The duration of each short term injection test was approximately 40 minutes During
that time the measured head levels were noted to very slowly increase Steady state
equilibrium of the head levels was not achieved during the duration of the short term tests
Neither overflow from the 3 wells occurred at the maximum attainable flow rate nor did it occur
during the opening and closing of the valves of adjacent wells during the injection testing The
average flow rates injected to the wells ranged from about 500 to 700 gallons per minute
Based on our overall observation of the injection well system operation it is believed that as
an injection well is opened or closed some delay and surge of flow is encountered which could
affect the short term head level response

Recommendations
Based on our observations the injection test results and the well usage data we
believe the injection wells have maintained their disposal capacity since the last inspection
which occurred in April 1999 The maintenance of the injection capacity is believed to be a
result of the recent well cleaning performed on the wells and the continued rotation of the
injection well usage
It is a known fact that injection wells sustain losses in capacity over time due to natural
biological and chemical fouling processes or other introduced factors such as suspended
solids or debris contained in the effluent In addition extended periods of well inactivity may
cause acceleration of the normal losses in capacity
It should be noted that some loss in capacity may be permanent and irreversible
however it has also been proven that redevelopment of injection wells can aid in the recovery
of lost capacity provided that the proper methods are utilized on a regular basis
Based on our injection well evaluation we recommend that the owner operator continue
to operate the injection wells on a routine rotating basis in an attempt to maintain the disposal
capacity of the system In addition we recommend that the owner operator closely monitor
and document the operation of each of the wells to assure that overflows or back ups related
to a well failure are detected prior to their occurrence In the event an overflow of a well or
other failure occurs and the situation cannot be remedied the owner operator should promptly
notify the Department of Health Underground Injection Control Branch
We believe that by increasing the use of the wells on a rotating basis especially during
the drier months of low effluent availability the current operational status of the wells could be
maintained Keeping the wells operational is not normally a problem during the wetter winter
months when effluent is more readily available for injection However we believe it is crucial
GEOLABS INC
Injection Well Performance Evaluation Page 14
W O 3295 60
to maintain rotating operation of all the wells during the summer months despite the lack of
available effluent
We recommend that the owner operator consider recording additional field observations
such as the following 1 notes regarding the overall system operation 2 documentation of
well maintenance performed 3 observation of the head levels within the wells prior to and
during injection events 4 daily flow volumes and 5 a log of individual well usage This
additional information will be used to evaluate the future performance of these wells In
addition the results of our short term injection testing of the wells may be used to evaluate the
operation of the wells with respect to prior well inspection data
Generally redevelopment should be considered if head levels continue to increase over
time or when well failures such as overflow or system back ups may be anticipated However
when the wells begin to fail it often times is too late to restore the capacity Therefore based
on the effectiveness of the recent well redevelopment we believe it may be prudent to
consider scheduling well redevelopment at annual intervals
Well redevelopment should be performed by a professional experienced with coastal
injection well construction and rehabilitation The redevelopment method selected should be
carefully evaluated based on the existing well design proper containment and disposal of
wastewater generated by the activity and other factors specific to the site and operation of the
wells
