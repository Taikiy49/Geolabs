In accordance with your request this letter presents our opinions regarding the
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the report by Hawaii
Geotechnical Group HGG entitled Kahekili Highway Widening Likelike Highway to
Vicinity of Kahuhipa Street Project No Kaneohe
dated May 12 1994 In particular we evaluated the recommendations for the new
concrete retaining wall planned adjacent to the existing CRM retaining wall in private
property
The geotechnical report addresses this condition on page 28 under the
subheading Existing Walls Pertinent recommendations include Wall construction
should be done in small increments and Shoring and or underpinning of existing
structures should be the responsibility of the contractor These are appropriate
recommendations and normally included in the geotechnical engineering reports for
construction next to existing structures
The concern for the construction adjacent to the existing high CRM wall is further
clarified in the plans as follows
The profile drawing on Sheet W8 ADD 121 includes a note that Footing
Excavation in this area Front of exist CRM Wall shall be done in small
increments 15 as required to prevent failure of exist CRM Wall The actual
small increment dimension would depend on the contractors method of
shoring time the excavation was left open the actual soil and water conditions
and other site conditions Therefore the 15 dimension is only a guide and would
vary somewhat depending on the contractor s method of construction The main
purpose of providing a dimension is to warn the contractor to approach the
construction in this area with caution The plan drawing on the same sheet W8

ADD 121 references Note 8 on Sheet W4 for construction work adjacent to
existing structures Note 8 directs that Construction work adjacent to existing
structures shall be performed in accordance with section 2903 b protection of
adjoining property of the Uniform Building Code dated 1991 This portion of the
UBC requires that the person making an excavation up to 12 feet deep shall
protect the excavation to prevent cave in or settlement The contractor did not
install shoring or underpinning for the excavation as required by UBC
Conclusions
Based on a review of the geotechnical engineering report recommendations
subsurface conditions encountered in the borings and construction plans for the project it
is our opinion that the recommendations and cautions for the construction adjacent to the
existing retaining walls made by HGG were appropriate and in general conformance with
the state of the practice in Hawaii Additionally it was reasonable to expect that the
excavation would be shored and supported to prevent instability of the adjacent property
It is also our opinion that the failure of the CRM wall was a slope failure caused by
insufficient shoring of the excavation which extended below the bottom of the wall
footing Proper lateral support of the excavation could have prevented the failure
