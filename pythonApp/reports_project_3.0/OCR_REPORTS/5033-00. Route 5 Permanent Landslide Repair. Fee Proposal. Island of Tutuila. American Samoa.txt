Project Considerations
The project involves repair of the Roi Namur fuel pier facility for the berthing of fuel
barges All supplies are shipped to Kwajalein Island Supplies and fuel are trans shipped
to Roi Namur Island periodically The fuel barges carries from 270 000 to 300 000 gallons
of diesel fuel each trip and discharges at a rate of 650 gpm at 55 psi reduced pressure to
avoid possible damage to existing piping To meet the current demands of the trans
shipping operations the existing pier which has shown deterioration in several areas will
be improved A sheet pile wharf approximately 80 feet long with five mooring dolphins
will be constructed with the surrounding areas dredged to 4 0 meters
Our geotechnical engineering exploration will focus on the new wharf and mooring
dolphins Five boreholes will be drilled to depths of approximately 30 to 70 feet below the
existing mudline Four borings will be drilled on the pier for the dolphin design and
one boring on the shore for the sheetpile wall Drilling will be performed from the existing
pier using a D 25 skid mounted drilling rig We anticipate that the site is underlain by
marine deposits overlying coral formation
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration is to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions to develop a soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the repair of the existing pier In order to accomplish this
we propose a program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
