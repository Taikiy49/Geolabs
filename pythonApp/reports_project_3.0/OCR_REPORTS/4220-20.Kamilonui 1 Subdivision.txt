Project Considerations
The Kamilonui 1 Subdivision development project also known as Leo lani is
situated at the base of the eastern flank of Kaluanui Ridge near the mouth of Kamilonui
Valley in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The subdivision site is
located along the western side of the Hawaii Kai Drive Extension at the northern end of
the existing Hawaii Kai Drive The parcel encompasses approximately 17 4 acres in land
area and is situated at the base of the western hillside The project involved the
development of the site into a residential housing project with sixty single family house
lots
A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted by our office for the design
of this project Our findings and recommendations were presented in our report entitled

Hillside Boulder Sweep
The boulder sweep activity was generally conducted by Royal Contracting
Company under the field observation and direction of a geologist from our office A total
of 14 site visits were made by our office between and March 15 2004 to
identify the boulders for removal by the work crew from Royal Contracting Company The
general criteria employed to select the individual boulders for removal were loose
round shaped non embedded or partially embedded boulders larger than about
12 inches in size located on sloping ground or near steep ledge rock within the property
limits that have moderate to high risks of future rockfall potential
Over 2 000 boulders meeting the above criteria were observed and identified by
our geologist for removal by the contractor Approximately 90 percent of the boulders
identified for removal were about 2 to 4 feet in diameter The remaining 10 percent are
either 1 to 2 feet or 4 to 6 feet in diameter with occasional boulders greater than 6 to
8 feet in diameter Based on our field observations the majority of the boulders were
removed using manual labor such as hand prying In addition mechanized equipment
such as hydraulic jacks was also used to dislodge some of the large boulders from the
surrounding formation for removal The loosened boulders were generally rolled downhill
until they fell down to the bottom of the new cut slope In areas where the cut slope had
not been graded at the time of the boulder sweep the loosened boulders were rolled
downhill until they reached the grading limits for later removal during the cut slope
construction
Upon the completion of the boulder removal activity a reconnaissance of the site
by our geologist indicated that the potentially hazardous boulders had been removed in
accordance with our recommendations It should be noted that some boulder deposits
remain on the hillside of the property however the remaining boulders located within the
property limits were visually assessed and judged to be generally stable and should pose
relatively low risk for future rockfall potential
Rockfall Mitigation Measures
In addition to the performance of a boulder sweep the following rockfall mitigation
measures have also been implemented for the development including the installation of
a boulder fence and the construction of a boulder catchment ditch
A boulder fence approximately 1 500 lineal feet in length was installed along the
top of the new cut slope at the western hillside by Prometheus Construction in
January 2004 The boulder fence consisted of a 6 foot high chain link fence with double
twist hexagonal wire mesh supported by steel posts and lateral anchors with a breaking
strength of about The steel posts are inch in diameter and embedded
4 5 feet into the existing ground The boulder fence would serve to stop or slow down
rolling boulders prior to falling down into the boulder catchment ditch located along the toe
of the cut slope

As mentioned above a boulder catchment ditch measuring 15 feet wide and 8 feet
deep was also constructed along the toe of the cut slope to catch falling boulders from
the hillside above The boulder catchment ditch would serve to greatly reduce the
potential for boulder encroachment into the residential homes
We recommend that periodic inspection should be conducted on the conditions of
the boulder fence and catchment ditch to ensure that these rockfall mitigation measures
are functioning properly Damaged sections of the boulder fence resulting from rolling
boulders should be removed and replaced Fallen boulders accumulating inside the
catchment ditch should be removed to maintain adequate bounce height for the
catchment ditch It is our opinion that the effectiveness of these rockfall mitigation
measures is highly dependent on the implementation of these periodic inspection and
maintenance work efforts
It is our opinion that the mitigation measures implemented including the boulder
sweep boulder fence catchment ditch and wall have greatly reduced the danger of
rockfalls at the subdivision
Limitations
lt must be pointed out that regardless of the methods used an inherent risk of
falling boulders could still exist for the area adjacent to the sloping hillside The existing
rock outcroppings that compose the steep hillside located westerly of the project site may
produce some falling rocks that would contribute to future boulder accumulation on the
hillslope located within the property limits Therefore there is a future potential for
boulders to loosen and roll down the hillside onto the development The state of the art of
geotechnical engineering practice has not advanced to a point that a permanent solution
is available for this area In addition it should be pointed out that no guarantee against
future falling boulders can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions with time
weathering erosion earthquake and construction vibration and improvements and
construction activities at and around the site Some existing boulder deposits that were
observed to be generally stable at the time of our evaluation may become unstable as a
result of the afore mentioned activities and events
