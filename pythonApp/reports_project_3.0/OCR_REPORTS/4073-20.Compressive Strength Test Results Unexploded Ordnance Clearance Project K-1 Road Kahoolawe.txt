Dear Mr Au
In accordance with your request we have conducted a series of compressive
strength tests on soil cement specimens to determine the cement content for the cement
treated base proposed for the above referenced project Based on the project
specifications we understand that the cement treated base should have a minimum
compressive strength of MPa However we understand that it is desired
to determine the cement content that would yield a cement treated base that would be
capable of withstanding the traffic anticipated at the site and the abrasion from surface
water runoff The cement treated base will be used as a final wearing surface
We understand that the source of aggregate base course to be used for the project
will be purchased from the Ameron Kapaa Quarry on the Island of Oahu Therefore in
order to establish a baseline on the aggregate base course tested when compared to the
actual aggregate base course in the field a sieve analysis was performed on the aggregate
base course The sieve analysis test was performed in general accordance with ASTM Test
Methods C 136 and D 1140 to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soil and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate 1
In order to determine the cement content needed to achieve the required
compressive strength the moisture density relationship of the aggregate base course was
determined in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 Graphic presentation of the test
results is provided on Plate 2 The moisture density relationship of the aggregate base
course was used as a basis to determine the molding wet density of the soil cement test
specimens
Twelve soil cement test specimens were prepared with variations in the
cement content The soil cement test specimens were prepared in general accordance with
ASTM D 1632 with the exception that the test specimens were 100 millimeters mm in

Finally the soil cement specimens were tested for the unconfined compressive

7 days the test specimens were immersed in water at room temperature for a period of
4 hours and then capped before being tested The laboratory test results performed on the
soil cement specimens are summarized on Plate 3 of this report The relationship between
cement content and unconfined compressive strength is provided on Plate 4 for reference
Based on our test results we believe that a cement content of 10 percent cement by
weight would readily achieve a compressive strength of 7MPa which is higher than the
required by the specifications We understand that the cement treated base will be
mixed manually on site Therefore to account for the field environment which is not as ideal
as in the laboratory the cement content may be increased by 2 percent for field conditions
For this project we recommend that a cement content of 12 percent by weight be used for
the cement treated base for the project
