INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
investigation performed for the proposed repair of the Rivera Point Wharf
at the U S Naval Ship Repair Facility in Subic Bay Republic of the
Philippines The general location of the project and its vicinity is
Shown on the Project location Map Plate 1 A general layout of the
wharf area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The objective of the present phase of the repair work is to
initiate repairs along the pile supported deck in order to enable the
removal of all restrictions and bring the wharf back to full use as soon
as possible This would involve repair of the crane rails deck slabs
and beams fender system and installation of replacement piles along the
pile supported deck portions of the wharf The focus of this report is
on the proposed replacement piles
Based on previous damage surveys by the PWC 1982 and an
Underwater Construction Team 1981 the Rivera Point Wharf repair work
originally included the installation of a number of new piles to replace
12 to 20 damaged or missing piles along the existing wharf However it
is our understanding that as of June of this year the number of piles to
be replaced has been revised to seven 7 along the outermost row of the
pile bents The locations of these piles are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Two new replacement piles will be installed on either side of
each existing pile to be replaced and will be designed to a maximum
Capacity of 35 kips per pile
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of
the new replacement piles
GEOLABS HAWAII

The main objectives of the investigation are
1 to determine the bearing properties of the various soil layers
below the site in order to determine the allowable pile
capacities and depths and anticipated pile driving
characteristics and
2 to determine the settlement and shear strength properties of
the soft harbor clays to evaluate potential downdrag forces on
the new piles Of particular concern is whether the soft clays
are still settling due to the weight of previous landfill
For this investigation four 4 borings were drilled to depths
varying from 100 to 207 feet below the deck level at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 These boring locations were
selected based on the previous damage survey intormation on the
anticipated replacement pile locations
The drilling was done under the technical supervision of a field
engineer from our Honolulu office
A principal engineer and a geologist from our Honolulu office
visited the site in April and March of this year respectively they
visually inspected the site conditions obtained a number of as built
drawings arranged for subcontract drilling of one of the test borings
and made necessary clearance to receive shipments of equipment and
materials to Subic Bay through Manila

The project site is located in the eastern portion of the U S
Naval Station in Subic Bay The Naval Station and Olongapo City to the
horth are situated on the estuarine delta of the Kalaklan River see
Plate 1
Examination of the geomorphology of the area leads to the
conjecture that this delta is located in a valley which was cut into
pyroclastic rocks during a lower stand or stands of the sea by older
streams originating primarily in the Mount Balakibok Volcanic area As
sea level rose to its current stand this valley became infilled with
terrigenous soft and compressible sediments
Owing to the low energy estuarine or deltaic environment of
deposition these soft and compressible sediments are poorly consolidated
clays and silts underlain at depths from 40 feet to about 130 feet
below MLLW in Borings 3 and 1 respectively by more weathered
pyroclastic rocks
Examination of old maps and plans in the 0 1 C C files at Subic
Bay indicated that the Rivera Point was probably a natural small
peninsula or a submerged island where a rock ridge may have extended
into the bay at a relatively shallow depth Later the Rivera Point area
was enlarged by filling behind the bulkhead along the back of the present
wharf decks Therefore most of the Rivera Point Wharf area is underlain
by man made fills placed over the older tidal marsh deltaic deposits

Soil Conditions
Based on our borings the subsurface soils along the Rivera Point
Wharf deck area may generally be classified into the following three
groups
FILL These were encountered in all of the borings under the
wharf deck area and typically consisted of gray brown silty
sand gravel and cobbles with some boulders They were
generally in a loose state with some medium dense pockets
ESTUARINE SEDIMENTS These were also encountered in all of our
borings and consisted of loose gray silty and or clayey sand
or soft gray silty clay The depth to the bottom of these
estuarine sediments below the top of the deck varied from about
50 feet in Boring 3 to 150 feet in Boring 1
A pocket of loose boulders and cobbles were encountered at the
bottom of the estuarine sediments at a depth of about 135 feet
in Boring 4
Generally the estuarine sediments are highly compressible in
nature
GEOLABS HAWAII

3 WEATHERED TUFF AND AGGLOMERATE ROCKS All of our borings
encountered highly weathered to moderately weathered tuff

the estuarine sediments The upper portion of the rocks was
generally highly weathered and frequently become saprolite

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings disclose that the wharf is underlain by fill over soft
estuarine deposits to depths varying from 50 feet southern side of
wharf to 150 feet northern side of wharf Significant amounts of
settlement would be anticipated in the soft sediments due to the weight
of the initial fill placed for the wharf construction
The scheduled pile tip elevations indicate that many of the
existing piles may not have penetrated through the soft compressible
estuarine sediments down to competent bearing soils Therefore many of
the existing piles may have settled along with the consolidation of the
soft sediments below the pile tips and due to additional loads on the
pile caused by downdrag from the upper soft sediments
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Q 1215 10 Page il
Recommendations for the replacement piles depend on the present
settlement characteristics of the piies and deck slab at the replacement
pile location If the existing piles and deck do not settle in the
future then the replacement pile design can be based solely on the
design load capacity However should the existing piles in the area be
still settling then consideration must be given to potential
differential settlement between the existing and replacement piles For
example if the replacement piles are driven down to the dense tuff or
agglomerate the new pile will settle much less than the existing piles
and become a hard spot thereby causing differential settlements in the
deck The differential settlements could be reduced by driving the
replacement pile to the same depth as the existing piles However if
the deck settlements continue differential settlements could still occur
between different portions of the deck which settle at different rates
In light of the above we believe it is important to consider the
potential future settlements of the existing piles in the vicinity of the
new replacement piles in order to determine their recommended pile
capacities and lengths

reading is being undertaken at various locations around the wharf by the
PWC

The limited boring and pile tip data generally agree with the above

possibly primary settlements may be occurring in the soft deposits in the

The proposed repair work at the wharf should consider the
anticipated future settlement characteristics of the existing pile
foundations Where the settlements appear to have stopped or slowed
down we generally believe that the repairs could proceed However
where the wharf settlements are continuing such as along Bents 121
through 135 future differential settlements could limit the life of the
new repairs
The seven proposed replacement piles are generally located in the
better area where the settlements appear to have stopped or slowed down
considerably
Ideally tor the proposed wharf repair the new replacement piles
should be driven to a comparable depth as the existing piles to be
compatible with the settlement as well as overall load carrying
characteristics of the existing piles
Pile Splice and Section Lengths
For this project the design and location of the splices are
particularly important Where splice is required the upper and the
lower pile sections should be securely joined together both in tension
and compression Otherwise the lower section may become separated when
driving through the soft estuarine deposits
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 1215 10 Page 15
The splice should be located at least 15 feet below the mud line
along the outboard edge of the deck to minimize deterioration and provide
lateral support for the pile
The maximum precast pile length that can be produced and
transported on land to the project site may be limited Thus it may be
difficult to properly position the splice within the desired zone below
the mud line for piles with lengths of 70 to 85 feet In addition all
our borings encountered weathered rock layer within a short distance
below the bottom of the soft estuarine deposits Thus this may not
allow extra pile penetration to position the splice at the desired
depth For example if rock is situated at a depth of 75 feet the upper
section would need to be 50 feet long since there is about 35 feet
between the deck and the mudline in order that the splice would be
located 15 feet below the mud line after driving However this will
result in having the lower section length of only 25 feet Therefore
the new piles may need to be spliced to the full 75 feet length before
they are picked up and driven This may create a handling problem due to
the weight limitations on the decks

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional
tests were performed These tests include one set of Atterberg
Limits test and 4 sieve analyses to determine the grain size
distribution The results of these tests are presented on
Plate B 1 The grain size distributions are shown graphically
on Plate B 2
Two sets unconsolidated undrained triaxial compres
Sion tests were performed on select undisturbed samples to
evaluate the soil strength characteristics of the soil The
test results are plotted on Plates B 3 and B 4
Four consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics
of the natural soils The test results are presented on
Plates B 5 through B 8
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected
Samples to further evaluate the strength characteristics
of the subsoils The test results are presented on Plates
B 9 and B 10
