Probing and Grouting Report
Kunia 228 Reservoir No MG
Dear Mr Crago
This letter presents the results of the probing operation at the Kunia 228 Reservoir
No 2 site which was performed on The work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal
A total of 28 probeholes were drilled below the column footings and ring wall
foundation of the proposed Kunia 228 Reservoir No 2 in the Ewa District on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The reservoir is approximately 106 feet in diameter and 26 feet in
height The subgrade was excavated by RCI Inc to the required bottom of footings
Materials encountered were dark gray basalt rock The probes were drilled below each of the
12 column footings and at 10 foot centers for continuous ring wall footings to a depth of
approximately 5 feet below the existing ground surface The revised probe depth of 5 feet
instead of 10 feet below the footing elevation was as per agreement between RCI Inc and
BWS site inspector Probing was performed using a 4 inch diameter air hammer A
representative from Geolabs Inc was present during the probing operation to observe the
rate of penetration of the air hammer to check the consistency of the underlying ground and
the possible presence of cavities or clinker zones No cavities or clinker zones were
encountered during the course of our probing work Filling of the probeholes and recording
of the actual volume of grout placed in each probehole were performed by RCI Inc
