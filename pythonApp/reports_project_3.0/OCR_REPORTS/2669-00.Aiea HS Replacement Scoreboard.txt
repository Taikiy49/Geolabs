INTRODUCTION
The geotechnical engineering exploration was performed for the Proposed
Replacement Scoreboard at Aiea High School in the lower portion of the Aiea Heights
area of the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates or for
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Replacement Scoreboard larger than the existing
scoreboard for the athletic field at Aiea High School The existing scoreboard is located
at the northwesterly end of the field behind a retaining wall at the base of a cut slope It
is anticipated that the new scoreboard will be built either on the slope behind the existing
retaining wall or on the level ground in front of the retaining wall
The new scoreboard measures approximately seven 7 feet by twenty 20 feet
Its lower edge will be approximately seventeen 17 feet above ground It weighs
approximately four hundred fifty 450 pounds An overturning moment of approximately
60 kip feet will be produced at the bases of its two 2 vertical supports by the design
wind loads

School on the island of Oahu Hawaii The field was constructed by cut and fill into the
hillside mantled with residual soils

slope The board is supported on two vertical members founded in concrete piers cast
against the back of the wall Cracks were observed at the time of our field exploration
on the face of the wall in front of the piers
The maximum free standing height of the retaining wall is approximately six 6 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Since the new scoreboard will be elevated above the ground surface the lateral
forces and overturning moments resulting from wind loading are important design
considerations for the scoreboard foundations We recommend that drilled piers be used
Lateral pier analyses were performed to aid in selection of the size and the depth of the
pier foundations recommended
Pier Size And Embedment
Since the weight of the new scoreboard is relatively light the size and embedment
of the drilled pier foundations are governed by the design overturning moments imposed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2669 00 Page 6
on the piers by the anticipated wind loads and the shear strength of the surrounding soil
and rock The resistance of the piers to these overturning moments will increase with
increasing cross sectional area with a corresponding decrease in pier deflections
Based on the subsoil profile encountered we have performed lateral pier analyses
utilizing a computer program PILEDG which is based on the P Y curve approach
developed at the University of Texas at Austin for the analysis of both piles and piers
For the design loading condition with an overturning moment of 60 kip feet the
program calculated pier deflections by iterations using relationships between lateral
deflection and lateral subgrade modulus values of the soil and rock along the embedded
length of the piers The deflection versus subsoil modulus relationships were represented
by P Y curves generated by the program using input soil rock parameters The pier
responses corresponding to the pier deflections including bending moments and shear
forces induced along the piers were then computed
Based on the lateral pier analyses we recommend that drilled piers with a
minimum diameter of two 2 feet be used The resulting lateral deflection of the piers at
the ground surface will be less than one quarter 1 4 inch
The drilled pier foundation embedment required depends on the location selected
for the Replacement Scoreboard In general we recommend that the new scoreboard
be located as far as possible behind the existing retaining wall to reduce potential adverse
effects on the wall due to the movements of the pier foundations If the new scoreboard
will be built close behind the existing retaining wall the piers should be drilled deeper
below the lowest adjacent finished grade to reduce the potential for further damage such
as increase in the severity of the existing cracking of the wall

Based on the results of our lateral pier analyses we recommend that the piers be
drilled to a minimum of fifteen 15 feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade if they
are located less than three 3 times the proposed pier diameter or six 6 feet from the
back of the existing retaining wall If the clearance between the back of the wall and the
drilled piers is six 6 feet or more a lesser minimum embedment of ten 10 feet below
the lowest adjacent finished grade may be used
The lesser ten 10 foot embedment may also be used for the drilled pier
foundations if the Replacement Scoreboard is located in front of the existing retaining wall
It is considered that these recommended embedments will result in development
within the drilled piers of sufficient vertical capacity to support the four hundred fifty 450
pound weight of the new scoreboard
Drilled Pier Construction
The loading capacities of a drilled pier depend to a large extent on the contact
between the pier shaft and the surrounding soil and or rock Therefore proper
construction techniques are important We recommend that the drilled pier construction
be monitored by the project geotechnical engineer
The contractor should exercise the utmost care in drilling the pier holes and placing
the proper concrete mix into the holes
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered we believe that a viable method of
advancing the pier holes would be by auger drilling through the silty clay overburden
followed by rock bit chopping in the basalt rock formation Drilling by methods utilizing
drilling fluids should be avoided because the fluids could saturate and weaken the
surrounding soil and rock
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2669 00 Page 8
A low shrinkage concrete mix should be used to ensure close contact between
the pier shafts and the surrounding soil and or rock The concrete should be placed as
promptly as possible in the predrilled holes to reduce the potential of hole cave in The
concrete should also be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the
aggregates from the concrete mix
To reduce the possibility of further damage to the existing retaining wall no attempt
should be made to remove the pier foundations supporting the existing scoreboard
Removal of this facility should be limited to cutting off the vertical supports at the top of
the piers

It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to monitor the
drilled pier construction including pier hole drilling and concrete placement This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
also to facilitate appropriate design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions as indicated

necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two 2 borings to
depths of 18 5 to 22 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted and also portable equipment were used
to drill the borings

operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard penetration
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches where available are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a HQ wireline core barrel The core
recovery REC and rock quality designation RQD values are shown on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
