MICROPILE OBSERVATION REPORT
NEW WEDDING CHAPEL AT HILTON HAWAIIAN VILLAGE
Dear Ms Okino
In accordance with your request Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering
observation and consultation services during the micro pile foundation installation for the
New Wedding Chapel at Hilton Hawaiian Village project located in the City of Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering
observations performed during the micropile installation for the above project In general
the purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the micropile
foundation installation for general conformance with the project documents
Geotechnical engineering recommendations regarding micropile foundations were
provided by Geolabs Inc and our recommendations were presented in our geotechnical
report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration New Wedding Chapel at Hilton
Hawaiian Village Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated March 31 2005
As part of the project one load test was performed on a sacrificial micropile prior to
production micropile installation The results of the load test program were presented in
our report entitled Micropile Load Test Report New Wedding Chapel at Hilton Hawaiian
Village Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated August 3 2005
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Hilton Hawaiian Village Resort Complex on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located west of the Tapa Tower swimming
pool and north of an existing retail utility building A landscaped area including a pond
and waterfall walkways and a gazebo structure occupies the new wedding chapel
building site The approximate project limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the new wedding chapel building will consist of a two story
structure The lower floor level will be constructed mainly of masonry units and the
upper floor level constructed of a steel framed structure The chapel building will be
approximately 50 feet by 75 feet in plan dimension

Based on available project drawings the bottom of footing elevation is about
3 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the new chapel structure with lower footing
elevation at the elevator shaft and mechanical room We understand that the typical
column loads for the perimeter columns are about 11 to 15 kips with interior column
loads of about 22 to 61 kips
The new building will be supported by a micropile foundation system Each
permanent micropile is designed to be able to carry a design load of 70 kips with a
minimum embedment depth of about 15 feet into relatively hard coral formation
underlying the site In addition a temporary micropile is also planned at the project site
to provide temporary support for the existing structure during the new basement
excavation period
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
The installation of the production micropiles was performed by Pacific Ground
System A total of 34 permanent micropiles and one temporary micropile were installed
for the project from through The micropiles were designated
as through 34 for permanent micropiles and Micropile No A 1 for the
temporary micropile
The permanent micropiles were generally installed at the site using Titan 40 16 drill
rods reinforcing steel with 4 inch drilling bits The temporary micropile was installed using
micropiles were installed about vertical while the temporary micropile was installed at an
inclination of about 10 degrees The approximate locations of the micropiles are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The Titan micropile system consists of hollow steel rods that are continuously
advanced into the ground These drill rods are abandoned in the hole and act as the steel
reinforcing for the micropile The micropiles were drilled using a backhoe mounted rotary
percussion drill rig Drilling fluid consisting of a cement grout mixture was pumped through
the hollow drill rods and through the drill bit to create a grout bulb The drilling of the hole
installation of the reinforcing member and grouting of the micropiles occured in a single
operation
The permanent micropiles generally extended to depths ranging from about 45 to
54 5 feet below the existing ground with micropile tip elevations of about 49 to 38 5 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Based on our field observation it appears that the permanent
micropiles were extended about 5 to 15 5 feet into the underlying medium hard to hard
coral formation Selected permanent micropiles with less than the minimum embedment
depth into the coral formation were proof load tested The temporary micropile extended
to a depth of about 38 5 feet below the existing ground surface with a corresponding tip
elevation of about 34 feet MSL A Micropile Installation Summary is presented on Plate 3

Based on our field observations 34 were
recommended for proof testing In addition and 14 were recommended
for proof testing due to discrepancies observed during micropile installation It was
observed that a 3 5 inch sacrificial drill bit was used to install Micropile No 6 instead of the
4 inch drill bit used in the load test by the contractor During installation of Micropile
No 14 the contractor forced the Titan rods into ground with a percussion hammer instead
of rotating the drill rods to obtain the required hole diameter
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles proof
load tests were conducted on 21 24 and 34 The proof tests
were conducted by loading the micropiles in tension to about 52 5 tons 1 5 times the
design load The micropile movement was measured with each load increment using
two dial gauges and a surveyor s level In addition the maximum load was held for
15 minutes to evaluate the creep effects Based on the proof load tests conducted total
movements of about 0 3 to 1 0 inches were observed at the top of the tested micropiles at
maximum test load The results of the proof tests are provided on Plates 4 1 through 4 14
Based on our field observations and the proof load tests performed we believe that
the micropiles for the project have been installed in general conformance with the project
plans and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on our
observations of the micropile installation it is our opinion that the micropiles have been
installed to the sufficient diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity
