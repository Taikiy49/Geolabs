performed for the proposed Maalaea Lahaina Third 69kV Transmission Line project
located between Maalaea and Lahaina in West Maui on the island of Maui Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map

proposed transmission line project only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Maui the second largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago was formed by the
activity of two volcanoes East Maui or Haleakala and West Maui The broad gently
sloping plain connecting the two volcanoes known as the Maui Isthmus was formed
when lavas of the Haleakala Volcano banked against the already existing West Maui
Volcano
The East Maui Volcano or better known as the Haleakala Mountain makes up the
eastern portion of the island Haleakala Mountain is considered to be dormant with the
last eruption taking place on the southerly side of the mountain around 1790 Haleakala
Mountain consists largely of the Honomanu Volcanic Series that built the mountain from
three rift zones probably during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs
The volcanic rocks of the West Maui have been divided into three volcanic series
The Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and comprises of the basaltic lava flows and
associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major shield volcano The
Wailuku Volcanic Series was covered by a thin discontinuous frosting of andesitic and
trachytic flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as the Honolua Volcanic Series
After a long period of inactivity where erosion incised deep valleys into the volcanic flows
volcanic activity returned with the eruptions that produced the flows and cones of the
Lahaina Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3469 00 Page 9
The project site is situated on the southern flank of West Maui mountain The
processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows to be highly
weathered and mantled by residual soils The coastal portions of the West Maui
mountain are flanked by fringing reefs and alluvial deposits

Recent Alluvium Ra
The recent alluvium or younger alluvium generally consists of soft to medium stiff
brown to dark gray clayey silts and organic silts with varying amounts of sand and
infrequent pockets of gravels and cobbles These soils are generally the result of
sedimentation in a low energy environment such as a lake marsh low land and
coastal areas Frequently the soils assigned to this geologic unit accumulated
very rapidly under water and may not have been exposed to desiccation
Therefore there is a tendency to encounter underconsolidated sediments which
are still settling under their own weight or the weight of minimal overburden
Based on Boring No 1 drilled during our field exploration it appears that the recent
alluvial soils along the proposed transmission line alignment generally consists of
about 15 feet of stiff clayey silt soils overlying dense silty sands at greater depths
Our geologic mapping suggests that the recent alluvial soils are generally present
between the Maalaea Power Plant Substation and the Honoapiilani Highway as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
m luvium Qa
The quaternary alluvium or older alluvium generally overlies the basalt rock
formation and consists of terrigenous sediments which have been transported by
stream action from the upper reaches of the streams The older alluvium is
generally located at the foot of mountain ranges and steep slopes and at the
heads of large eroded valleys The material is more granular in nature when
compared to recent alluvium and consists of alluvial and colluvial fans talus and
debris flows of material generally consisting of sand gravel cobbles and boulders
with clays

The Tertiary Wailuku Volcanics generally composed of in situ basalt rock with
interbedded clinker layers and their weathered products derived from lava flows
and volcanic eruptions The material varies in degree of weathering and is
typically overlain by residual or saprolite soils of extremely weathered rock Bare
rock may also be exposed at the ground surface with no soil cover The
Honomanu Volcanics generally composed of volcanic ash cinders basalt rock
and their weathered products The materials may have a chalky white or tan
appearance and are typically found near cinder cones and other volcanic vents
Based on Boring and 16 through 18 drilled during our field

alignment generally consists of hard basalt rock formation blanketed by a cover of
saprolitic materials consisting of hard clayey silts of up to about 10 feet in

Site Plans Plates 2 and 3

Construction Considerations
The performance of drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures As a result we recommend that a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts
during construction General guidelines for the drilled shaft construction are presented in
Appendix C of this report for reference In our opinion the following may have a
significant impact on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in some of the borings drilled for
the project we anticipate that drilling through dense boulders or hard ledges will be
required The drilled shaft contractor will need to have the appropriate equipment and
tools to drill through the dense boulders Appropriate measures by the contractor will also
be needed to avoid dislodging any boulders into the drilled shaft excavation
Based on our field exploration soft loose soils may be encountered during the
construction of some of the drilled shaft foundations To reduce the potential for caving in
of the drilled holes temporary casing of the drilled holes may be needed during
construction The contractor should be made aware that temporary casing of the drilled
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3469 00 Page 21
holes may be needed In addition care should be exercised during removal of the
temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking of the drilled shaft Therefore a
minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be maintained above the bottom of the casing
during removal of the casing
The load carrying capacity of drilled shafts depends to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the
shaft holes and in placing concrete in the holes
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is generally not recommended Because
of the high groundwater anticipated near the Maalaea Power Plant Substation site
placement of concrete by wet construction methods using a tremie pipe will likely be
required for some of the drilled shaft foundations located in this area A low shrink
concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch slump range should be used to provide close
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils The concrete should be
placed in a suitable manner to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the concrete
mix

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions along the proposed transmission line alignment were
explored by drilling and sampling 15 borings to depths ranging from 15 to 31 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 and 3 The borings were drilled using both truck mounted drill rig and skid
mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description Due to
the lack of water at the remote boring location the core barrel was advanced using
compressed air as a drilling fluid in lieu of water during our coring operations
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 653 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Nine Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Nine sieve analysis tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils Graphic presentation of the
test results are provided on Plates B 2 through B 4
Four unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected
samples to evaluate their shear strength characteristics Results of the tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX C
Guide Specifications for Drilled Shafts
GEOLABS HAWAII
GUID CIFICATIONS FOR DRI F
I L TION D DIM
Drilled shaft foundations shall be constructed at the locations to the lengths and
in accordance with the details and notes shown on the plans As built shaft lengths shall
extend to the depths shown on the plans and shall not be deeper by more than 5 feet
without prior written permission from Geolabs Hawaii Shaft diameters shall be as shown
on the plans for each individual drilled shaft The diameter shown shall be the diameter of
the drilling bit or bucket
li ALLOWABLE TOLE O A
PLUMBNESS
The center of the top of the shaft and reinforcing cage shall not deviate from the
location shown on the plans by more than 2 inches in any direction
The drilled shaft shall not deviate from a plumb line referenced to the center of the
drilled shaft as described above by more than 3 percent of the drilled shaft length from
the top to the point of interest
The clear distance between reinforcing steel and the drilled shaft hole sidewall
shall not be less than 3 inches
Hil EQUIP N Tl
Drilling casing setting of reinforcing cages pumping bailing concrete placement
pulling casing and all other phases of the shaft construction operation shall be performed
with equipment and tools capable of meeting the specifications
All loose material shall be removed from the hole prior to placing concrete
Cleaning shall be by machine or hand operated equipment Drilling and placement of
concrete shall be scheduled such that concrete is placed in each drilled shaft hole on the
same day in which drilling is completed Holes spaced closer than three shaft diameters
center to center shall not be drilled on the same day unless approved by Geolabs
Hawaii
W O 1996
PLATE C 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
Guide Specifications for Drilled Shafts continued
IV CASING
Casing shall be used where needed to maintain the integrity of the drilled shaft
hole during drilling installation of reinforcing steel and placement of concrete
Where casing is used the hole shall not be drilled ahead of the casing any further
than the hole will stand without sloughing At any time sloughing occurs the hole shall be
cased to the bottom of the sloughing area immediately before proceeding to drill the hole
any deeper
Inside casing diameter shall not be less than the required drilled shaft diameter
The hole shall be filled with concrete and the casing removed as specified hereafter All
casing must be removed Failure to remove all casing will result in rejection of the drilled
shaft
V CONCRETE
Concrete shall comply with all provisions of the project plans At the time of
placement the concrete shall have a slump necessary to produce the specified strength
and should be determined by the cone slump test ASTM D 143 Unless a higher
compressive strength is specified elsewhere the concrete shall have a minimum
compressive strength of 3 000 p s i at 28 days as determined by the concrete cylinder
compression tests ASTM C 39 9
Vi REINFORCIN
The reinforcing cages shall be braced and tied in such a way that the cages are
not deformed or distorted during fabrication placement of the cage in hole and concrete
placement
Any reinforcing cage which is bent collapsed or distorted or which varies from a
straight line by more than 3 inches at any point shall be rejected
Reinforcing cages shall be set in the hole in such a way that they do not rake the
sidewall and cause sloughing Reinforcing cages shall not be spliced in the drilled shaft
holes instead they should be fabricated to the full length prior to placement
W O 1996
PLATE C 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
Guide Specifications for Drilled Shafts continued
The reinforcing cage shall be hung from a solid support at the top of the hole so
that it does not bear on the bottom of the hole and hangs free in the hole The
reinforcing cage shall be set at the required location and elevation prior to beginning
placing concrete and shall be held and supported in such a way that it does not move
during concrete placement
Reinforcing dowels which are to be set in the concrete after completion of
placement if required are to be installed immediately following placement of concrete
and prior to the initial set of the concrete The concrete shall be vibrated after setting the
dowels The dowels shall be securely fixed with templates or by other means in order
that they are not displaced during vibration of the concrete Driving dowels into the
concrete after the initial set is forbidden In the event dowels are not placed prior to the
initial set of the concrete they shall be placed by drilling and grouting as directed by the
Structural Engineer
If the hole is filled in the dry concrete shall be placed through a hopper and
elephant trunk positioned in the center of the reinforcing cage The elephant trunk shall
be long enough and be of sufficient diameter to prevent segregation The hopper shall be
of sufficient size to prevent spilling of concrete outside of the elephant trunk
For placing concrete in a cased hole a minimum head of 5 feet of concrete shall
be maintained in the casing at all times Where staged casing is used the casing shall be
pulled and removed in units in such a way that after pulling one unit of casing there is at
least 5 feet of concrete in the remaining casing until the hole is topped out at the cut off
grade
Unless otherwise noted vibration of the concrete is not required until the concrete
reaches an elevation of approximately 10 feet below the top of the drilled shaft After this
elevation is reached the concrete shall be vibrated continuously to the top of the drilled
shaft and until good densification of the concrete has been achieved Concrete shall be
added as required to compensate for consolidation and expansion into the hole The
concrete vibrator and the vibration operation shall conform to all requirements for internal
concrete vibrators and vibration of concrete given in the job specifications

The elapsed time from beginning the placement of concrete until completion shall
not exceed 1 hour Placement of concrete shall not commence until sufficient concrete to
complete the drilled shaft is on the job site waiting to be placed
F DRILLI
Soil obtained from drilling shall not be used as fill unless otherwise approved by
Geolabs Hawaii and it shall be disposed of off the site or used in landscaped areas as
appropriate as designated by the Architect or Civil Engineer
IX BSERVATI
The drilling of holes setting of reinforcing setting and pulling of casing
dewatering placing concrete and any other operation involved in the construction of the
drilled shafts must be performed under the observation by a representative from
Geolabs Hawaii The contractor shall give notice at least 48 hours in advance of his
performing such work He shall do no such work in the absence of a representative from
Geolabs Hawaii
