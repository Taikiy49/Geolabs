SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The soil exploration disclosed that the upper
area of the site is underlain by medium dense
tuff over stiff alluvium while the lower areas
are underlain by variable thicknesses of soft
lagoonal deposits
The tuff and alluvium are generally competent
Materials having good bearing qualities
therefore normal site grading and foundation
design are anticipated for this area
Fills over the lower areas will initiate
significant ground settlement Previous
experience in the area and laboratory tests
indicate that the underlying soft soils have
slow rates of settlement
Surcharge fills may be used to shorten the
waiting period for surface construction and to
reduce long term settlements
Post and beam construction or post tensioned
slabs are recommended for structures constructed
over the soft deposit areas
The text of this report should be referred to
for special and detailed site grading and
foundation recommendations

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for use
in the design of the site grading for the proposed subdivision
development The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we used
in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report Boring
and laboratory data from the nearby borings drilled previously for the
Kahuapaani Street and H 1 Freeway projects were also used in our study
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the north side of Kahuapaani Street
between the Halawa Stream crossing and the H 1 Freeway viaduct section
The site is bounded by Kahuapaani Street on the south and the H 1
Freeway and ramps on the north west and east
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 3
The topography in the higher southern portion of the site consists of a
relatively flat terrace area along Kahuapaani Street The ground
elevations in this area vary from about 70 to 85 feet This area is
presently covered with haole koa trees
From the higher area the ground surface slopes down steeply for 40 to
50 feet to a lower area This lower area is relatively flat with ground
elevations ranging from 30 to 35 feet and covered with a very thick
growth of haole koa trees
There is an easement for a communication cable extending in an east west
direction across the site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a multi family
residential development A final layout scheme was not available at the
time of our investigation however four and eight plex townhouse
structures with access roads and parking areas are anticipated
The proposed grading plan was also not available however preliminary
discussions with the project civil engineer indicates that cuts up to 20
feet deep are planned for the upper area and fills up to 20 feet thick
are planned for the lower area to produce a gentle overall slope from
Kahuapaani Street down toward the northern project boundary

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Nine borings were drilled at the site to depths of 16 5 to 95 feet below
the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory testing is
covered in the appendix along with the logs of borings and laboratory
test results
Geology
The project site is located on the northwest and northeast flanks of the
Makalapa Aliamanu Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with the
groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions
which deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form
tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid down
when the sea level was higher than the present The Aliamanu eruptive
event deposited its material over alluvium originating from Halawa
Stream and in doing so dammed the course of the stream causing
deposition of alluvium on the northern flank of the Aliamanu cone
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1358 00 Page 5
After that phase of volcanic activity ceased sedimentary deposits
accumulated over the Aliamanu tuff before the next phase began Then
the Salt Lake and Makalapa Volcanic eruptions began when the sea was
receding below its present level This was an air laid ash deposit that
overlies the older alluviun
Stream erosion and diversion of the course of Halawa Stream has shaped
the area to its present topography With the most recent rise in sea _
level soft compressible sediments were deposited in the drowned stream
channels

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the upper portion of the site is
underlain by competent tuff mudrock and alluvium Normal site grading
and foundation design may be utilized in this area
The lower portions of the site are underlain by soft compressible
deposits which increase in thickness away from the upper area
Significant ground settlements are anticipated due to the weight of the
new fill placed over this area The height of the fills over this lower
soft ground area should be minimized in order to minimize ground
settlements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1358 00 Page 7
To minimize the effects of long term settlements the buildings should
be located closer to the upper terrace area away from the thick soft
deposits While the roads parking areas and other improvements that
are less sensitive to settlements should be situated over the thicker
soft deposits
Surcharge fill should be utilized to accelerate the rate of settlements
and reduce future long term settlements
Settlements
Fills up to about 20 feet high are planned over the lower areas Based
on the laboratory consolidation test results ground settlements of
about 3 feet may occur along the northern portion of the site where up
to 60 feet of soft deposits are present The ground settlements would
reduce toward the south where the soft deposits are thinner The
estimated limits of the soft deposits are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The laboratory tests as well as previous settlement monitoring
experience in the area indicate that the underlying soft deposits have
slow rates of settlement It is very difficult to accurately determine
the time required for the settlement to take place since it is affected
by minute details of the soil structure and the presence of more
permeable sand layers We estimate that the time required for 95 of
the primary settlements to occur would be about 2 5 years Several
inches of long term settlements may occur after the 2 5 years
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1358 00 Page 8
In order to reduce the waiting period and some of the subsequent long
term settlements a temporary surcharge fill should be utilized To
avoid lateral squeezing of the soft subsoils a maximum surcharge height
of 10 feet is recommended
Surcharge fill may be placed loose at slopes of horizontal to 1
vertical
Settlement gauges should be installed prior to filling in order to
monitor the settlement rate and determine when building and utility
construction can proceed
The time required for the estimated settlement to occur are very
difficult to predict and may vary due to the various consistency of the
subsoils It is estimated that a 12 month surcharge period with 10 feet
of surcharge fill will be required for the project The actual
surcharge period will be determined by the settlement gauge readings
Slope Stability
Slope stability analyses were performed to evaluate the stability of the
proposed fill and surcharge over the lower areas The analyses indicate
that the critical section would be along the northern boundary where
about 20 feet of fill and 10 feet of surcharge is planned next to the
existing highway ramp and the drainage channel The calculated factor
of safety against slope failure for this area is 1 3 The slope
stability computer print out and critical slip circle is presented in
Appendix C
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
In order to increase the factor of safety against Slope failure the
placement of the surcharge fill should be delayed to allow the soft
subsoils to settle and gain strength due to the weight of the 20 feet of
fill
Three piezometers should be installed in the soft deposit area to
monitor the build up of excess pore pressure to determine when the
surcharge can be placed and to aid in the determination of the rate of
settlement
Additionally three slope indicators should be installed in the fill
slopes to monitor possible lateral movement during filling
Site Grading
The preliminary grading information available at the time of our
investigation indicated that the grading will involve fills up to 20
feet thick in the lower areas and cuts up to approximately 20 feet in
the upper areas Excess fill material will be generated from the
proposed grading
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill
should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
density as established by ASIM D 1557 test method
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
1358 00 Page 10
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to obtain 90 of the above specified maximum density
For roadway areas the subgrade level should be compacted to a
tminimun of 95 of its maximum dry density
The fills should be moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content prior to compaction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soils
Fills over the lower area should be compacted to about 3 feet above
the finished grade to allow for the anticipated ground settlements
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into firm exiting ground See Plate 5
for details
Fill Material
Excavated on site material including tuff generally less than 8
inches in size may be used as a source of fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 11
Shrinkage Factor
Based on the average in place density of the soil samp les obtained
from our test borings and the maximum density determination it
appears that the computed shrinkage factor between the on site cut
and compacted fill quantities varies from about 15 percent
shrinkage for the alluvium to about 6 percent swell for the tuff
Excavation
Based on our previous experience we believe the tuff material can
generally be ripped by a D 8 dozer or its equivalent However
some isolated pockets of hard ripping may be encountered Care
should be exercised during grading to avoid over ripping and
undercutting of the proposed finished grades particularly on the
slopes Any areas which are over excavated need to be backfilled
to obtain 90 of its maximum density as determined by ASTM
D 1557 78
Slopes
Steeper cut slope ratios up to 1 1 H V may be used in the upper
weathered tuff mudrock formation For cut into the underlying
undisturbed alluvial materials the cut slope ratio should be reduced to
2 1 H V
Slope ratios of 2 1 H V can be used for the proposed embankment
construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1358 00 Page 12
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical height 8 foot
drainage benches should be placed at the mid height of the slope
Surface runoff water should be diverted away from the top of the slope
Slope planting should be incorporated as soon as practicable to reduce
erosion on fill slope as well as alluvial soil exposed on the cut slope
surface
Utility Trenches
Tuff formation was encountered in the upper portions of the site If
utility trenches are required to extend through these materials
moderate to hard excavation may be anticipated Some special excavating
techniques such as hoerams may be needed to excavate the utility
trenches
Normal excavation is anticipated in the very stiff to hard alluvial
materials encountered in the lower portions of the site
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize subsequent
ground surface settlement along the utility alignments All backfill
should be compacted to 90 of its maximum density as determined by ASTM
D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1358 00 Page 13
Utility lines running through the lower area will be subjected to ground
settlements Eight inch thick pipe cushion is recommended under these
lines to accommodate some possible leakage which may occur due to the
settlement
Existing Communication Cable
The existing communication cable extends through a portion of the lower
area which may be underlain by about 40 feet of soft deposits B 2 A
significant amount of settlement approximately 12 inches is
anticipated due to the weight of the proposed new fill If the cable
cannot tolerate these settlements it may have to be relocated away from
the soft deposit area
Building Foundation
For the residential structures in the upper area spread footing
foundations or slab on grade may be used An allowable bearing value of
4000 p s f may be used on the weathered tuff or very stiff soil below
the tuff
For the structures in the lower area spread footing foundation or
slab on grade may be used for fill areas over medium to stiff alluviun
Where thick deposits of soft soil are present spread footing
foundations or a stiffened slab reinforced or post tensioned is
recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1358 00 Page 14
For the light structural load anticipated on this project an allowable
bearing pressure of 2000 p s f may be used for the footings bearing on
the compacted fill
All footings on level ground should extend a minimum of 12 inches below
the final adjacent grade
Building construction near the top of the slope area should have a
minimum set back distance of 6 feet from the outer edge of the footing
foundation measured horizontally out to the slope surface
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the
backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted base
course The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier may be considered
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed to dry
out prior to concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1358 00 Page 15
The site should be graded to drain water away from the house foundations
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be utilized in the development
compacted fill are recommended
Well graded granular material such as base course rock No 3B fine
gravel sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly
behind the walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for
the backfill and the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows

A friction factor of 0 35 between concrete footing and soil can be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally where sufficient soil is available in front of the wall a
passive soil resistance of 300 p c f for soil can be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Structures located close to or along slopes will be subjected to the
effects of downhill slope creep To reduce these effect retaining
walls located on or near the tops of slopes should be embedded such that
there is a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from
the outer edge of the footing foundation out to the slope face
Roadway Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for most of the project roads
Based on the laboratory CBR test results on the on site soils the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

during construction and the above design sections may be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the
depths
To further identify soil characteristics 3 mechanical sieve
analyses and 3 Atterberg Limits tests were performed The results are
presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Two laboratory compaction tests modified Proctor were
performed on bulk samples of the on site soils to examine their
compaction characteristics Two laboratory CBR tests were also
performed on the same bulk samples to evaluate their subgrade support
characteristics The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 and
B 2
Eleven consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the
subsoil The test results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 13
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plate B 14
