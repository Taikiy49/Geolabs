SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be required for the Honoapiilani Highway Improvements
project between Mile Post 11 and Mile Post 19 Due to the silty and clayey nature of the
subgrade soils encountered at the project site we believe that a permeable drainage layer
will be required for the pavement design of the new highway lanes Therefore we have
only considered pavement structural sections with a permeable drainage layer in our
analyses for the project
Based on the traffic data provided the R Value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses performed we recommend that a flexible pavement structural section
be used for the new pavements Based on our analyses we recommend that a flexible
pavement section consisting of 4 inches of AC placed on 8 inches of ACB over 6 inches of
UTPB over 6 inches of ASB be used for the design of pavements for the Honoapiilani
Highway Improvements project
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
shouid be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures To collect infiltrated water into the
pavement structural section we recommend that a pavement edge drain system
hydraulically connected to the untreated permeable base be provided along the edges of
the new pavement areas
The text of this pavement justification report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and specific recommendations for design of pavements

1 1 Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses performed in
support of the design of new pavements for the Honoapiilani Highway Improvements
project between Mile Post 11 and Mile Post 19 located in the Lahaina area on the Island of
Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field reconnaissance and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the design of
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located along Honoapiilani Highway in the Lahaina area on the
Island of Maui Hawaii The project consists of the conceptual design of improvements to
approximately 8 miles of highway along Honoapiilani Highway The project will begin from
the western portal of the existing highway tunnel Mile Post 11 to near Lahainaluna Mile
Post 19 Because the project is only in the conceptual design phase details of the scope
of work are not well defined at this time
We understand that the highway improvements project may include roadway
realignment lane and or shoulder widening intersection improvements and or drainage
improvements Because new pavements will be required for the improvements project a
pavement justification report was desired for the planning and design of the pavement
structure as is common for federal aid projects

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui Tertiary
Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene Epoch
The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these two
volcanoes The project site is located along the south and southwest flanks of West Maui
The volcanic rocks of the West Maui have been divided into three volcanic series
The Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and comprises of the basaltic lava flows and
associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major shield volcano The Wailuku
Volcanic Series was covered by a thin discontinuous frosting of andesitic and trachytic
flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as the Honolua Volcanic Series After a
long period of inactivity when erosion incised deep valieys into the volcanic flows voicanic
activity returned with the eruptions that produced the flows and cones of the Lahaina
Volcanic Series
In situ chemical weathering of the lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million
years The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual and saprolitic soils In
general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with
increased depth
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock mass from the slopes and movement of the material
downslope toward the axis of the valley by gravity to form colluvial deposits Once the
colluvium reaches streams in the valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium Large areas of alluvium can be
a
found in the lowland areas and bordering streams
When alluvial and colluvial deposits are left in place or buried for long periods of
time chemical processes begin to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown

2 2 Site Reconnaissance
The project site is located along an 8 mile stretch of Honoapiilani Highway in the
Lahaina area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project will begin from the west portal of
the existing highway tunnel Mile Post 11 to near Lahainaluna Mile Post 19 The
location of the project site is shown on the Overall Site Plan Plate 2
A site reconnaissance was conducted along the proposed project limits Based on
our reconnaissance the highway consists of a two lane highway with one lane and
shoulder in each direction The existing pavements of the highway consist of asphaltic
concrete and the pavements appeared to be in fair condition with no areas of visible
severe distress At the time of our reconnaissance some of the underground drain lines
traversing Honoapiilani Highway appeared to be cracked obstructed with debris and had
a substantial growth of vegetation at the inlets and outlets The existing alignment from
about Mile Post 11 3 to Mile Post 19 traverses along low relief topography The existing
ground surface elevations range from about 7 to 23 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the current design concept and guidelines we envision that new
pavements will be constructed for the highway improvements project Due to the clayey
and silty nature of the subgrade soils anticipated at the project site we recommend that a
permeable drainage layer be incorporated into the pavement design for the new highway
lanes Based on the traffic data provided the R value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses we recommend that 4 inches of asphaltic concrete AC placed on
8 inches of asphalt concrete base ACB over 6 inches of untreated permeable base
UTPB over 6 inches of aggregate subbase ASB be used for the new pavements for the
project The following sections of this report provide discussion on the areas necessary for
pavement analyses and design
3 1 Methodology of Pavement Design
Two types of pavement structural sections flexible and rigid pavements were
considered in the pavement analyses for this project The flexible pavement sections
presented in this report were generally determined based on the methodology described in
Chapter 3 of the revised Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002 The Pavement
Design Manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation
Highways Division Material Testing and Research Branch The pavement design
methodology is based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the
California Department of Transportation Caltrans
The design procedures for the rigid pavement sections are generally based on the
design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design for
Concrete Highway and Street Pavements

Preparation Below Pavement Section
We anticipate that the majority of the new highway pavements will be constructed
near the existing grade In general the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious
material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site or disposed of in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
below areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose stiff and or dense materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
aggregate subbase materials The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site

After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated where
necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate subbase course
layer The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less

determined in accordance with AASHTO Test Method T 180 ASTM D 1557 Optimum

If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide stabilization by cement treatment may consist of
one sack of cement for approximately 25 square feet of subgrade area Geolabs should
be contacted to evaluate the pumping subgrade conditions and to evaluate whether the
pavement sections need to be revised and or modified based on the exposed subgrade
conditions
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be scarified and re prepared as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather

3 7 Compaction Requirements
In general fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Aggregate subbase
materials required for the new pavements should conform to the requirements stipulated in
Subsection 703 16 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and
Public Works Construction 1994 HSS The aggregate subbase should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to not less than 95 percent of the maximum density according to
Section 106 09 Special Test Methods of the HSS
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basaltic
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to at least 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity determined in

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils It should be noted that the clayey
and silty subgrade soils are susceptible to softening and loss of strength upon saturation
Therefore special attention should be given to the surface drainage of the pavements As
a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after construction To
collect infiltrated water into the pavement structural section we recommend that a
pavement edge drain system be provided along the sides of the new travelway
We envision that an edge drain will be needed to collect and discharge infiltrated
water into the pavement structural section from the untreated permeable base layer
The edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations facing
down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable separator non woven filter
fabric conforming to Section 648 of the HSS should be placed around the untreated
permeable base to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage material as
shown on Plate 3 In addition a permeable separator should also be provided between
the untreated permeable base and aggregate subbase Care should be exercised when
placing the subbase base and or construction of the surface course to avoid damaging
the edge drain collector pipe A typical edge drain detail is provided on Plate 3 for
reference The edge drains should be daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for
proper discharge
