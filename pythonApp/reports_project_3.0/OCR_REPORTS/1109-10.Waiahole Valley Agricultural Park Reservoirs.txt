This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the two relocated reservoir sites in Waiahole
Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general locations of the sites
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the relocated reservoir sites in order to provide
recommendations for use in the design of the foundations for the proposed
structures
A previous investigation was performed by our firm for the original
reservoir locations and indicated that the reservoir tanks would
experience excessive settlements at the planned location and elevations

Subsequently the reservoirs were relocated such that the increased site
cutting could compensate for the weight of the reservoir loads thereby
minimizing the settlements

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2
and 3

The proposed project consists of the following
1 A 0 2 M G steel reservoir structure approximately 33 feet in
diameter and 32 feet high Cuts from 13 feet up to about 22
feet are planned so that most of the weight of the reservoir
would be compensated by the removal of overburden and a level

2 A 1 0 M G steel reservoir structure approximately 66 feet in

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed 0 2 M G reservoir is located on an upper knoll to the
north of Uwau Stream near the end of Waiahole Valley Road while the
proposed 1 0 M G reservoir is located on a lower gentle slope to the
north of Waiahole Valley Road and approximately 1 100 feet to the west of
a small bridge across the Waiahole Stream
The topography of the 0 2 M G reservoir site is generally flat
sloping only slightly to the south The existing level area may have been
created by previous fill placement The site is sparsely vegetated with
grasses The ground elevation of the upper reservoir site varies from 363
to 350 feet above Mean Sea Level
The 1 0 M G reservoir site is on a gentle natural Slope 8H 1V
down to the east The site is heavily vegetated with small to tall trees
and grasses The ground surface varies from elevation 226 to 220 feet
above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project sites are located on the Waiahole coastal plain which
is essentially a broad and fairly flat windward island fringe on Oahu
fronting two drowned amphitheater headed valleys
After the Koolau Volcano ceased its activity it was deeply incised
by amphitheater headed valleys subsequently drowned and partly filled
with sediments during Pliocene time Later glacio eustatic sea level
changes during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of hundreds of
feet of marine and non marine sediments
The sites lie on gently sloping upland slopes that consist of
coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau and Kailua Ranges to the south of
the site

Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the project sites were explored by drilling
and sampling six 6 borings to depths of 31 to 41 feet Borings 2A to 4A
were drilled at the 0 2 M G reservoir and Borings 6A to 8A at the 1 0
M G reservoir
The 0 2 M G reservoir site is generally underlain by relatively
uniform medium to very stiff reddish brown to mottled orange clayey silt
which has gravel locally at depths as indicated by Borings 2A to 4A
The 1 0 M G reservoir site is generally underlain to the maximum
drilled depth by relatively uniform medium to very stiff brown to
mottled brown clayey silt which has same gravel or decomposed gravel at
depths as indicated by Borings 6A to 8A
No groundwater was encountered in all the borings during the time of
our investigation but the soils have high in situ moisture contents
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration at the two reservoir sites generally
encountered moderately compressible in situ soils with high natural
moisture contents To minimize the foundation settlements cuts from 13
to 27 feet are planned to compensate for the weight of the reservoir
structure
Reservoir Foundations
The borings disclosed that at the proposed finished grade the site
is underlain by a stiff reddish brown to mottled orange clayey silt which
could provide adequate foundation support for the proposed reservoir
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1109 10 Page 5
Typically the steel reservoir tank foundations consist of a deep
perimeter ring footing and a center column footing An allowable
bearing value of 3 000 p s f may be used for the foundation design
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 48 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
stiff undisturbed soil or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to minimize footing settlement
The bottom of all footing excavations and trench backfill should be
compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to concrete placement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundations
Settlement
Based on the boring and laboratory test data and reservoir
anticipated loads the estimated settlements would generally be on
the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 3 can
be utilized to determine sliding resistance of the structure and
foundation

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the ridge about
13 to 27 feet at the reservoir sites and a small amount of fill and cut
for the access roadway
Due to a combination of high annual rainfall and dense vegetation
the natural moisture content of the on site soils are generally high 44
to 76 Therefore sone drying out of the excavated material would be
required to attain the recommended compaction criteria Alternatively
drier off site may be used for the fill
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material
Fill Placement Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no filling will be required at the tank
site Some filling for the access roadway may be necessary
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical fill placed should be keyed and benched into the existing
firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope For
details see Plate 5 This detail is essential for the stability of
the fills particularly any large fills The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have in place moisture contents Therefore it may
not be practicable to dry the excavated soil to near the optimum
moisture content Due to the high in situ moisture content and

since the proposed fills are low it is our opinion that the normal
90 compaction standard used in other drier areas should be modified
to 85 for this project
Slopes
It is anticipated that the existing ridge to the west of the 0 2
M G reservoir will be cut about 22 feet and that of the 1 0 M c
reservoir be cut about 27 feet Cut slopes up to 2 1 H V may be
used with slope benches provided at 20 foot maximum vertical height
intervals
Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with extreme care
by the contractor A backhoe should be used to provide the final
finishing of the cut slope surface Overcutting beyond the design
Slope should be avoided since backfilling and recompacting would
create a very loose surface layer which would be easily eroded
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away and
prevented from flowing over the slope face The slope should be
planted as soon as practical to prevent unravelling due to erosion
Due to the high rainfall in the area it is not uncommon to
encounter seepage zones through gravelly layers exposed in the cut
slopes If encountered special provisions such as horizontal
drains or toe drains may be required to stabilize the cut slopes

Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should generally be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
