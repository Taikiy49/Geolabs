INTRODUCTION
This reports presents the findings and conclusions of our soil
investigation for the proposed widening of Haiku Road between
Kamehameha and Kahekili Highways in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The
purposes of the investigation was to lineate subsurface soil
conditions to measure the subgrade support capacity and to
W 1975
provide recommendations for the design and construction of the
proposed roadway pavement
The investigation included a geologic reconnaissance of the
area seven test borings with logging and sampling of the soils
encountered laboratory testing and engineering analysis
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed project consist of up grading and widening of the
existing 25 foot wide Haiku Road to a paved width of approxi
mately 48 feet The section under investigation has a total
length of 3 493 feet
The existing roadway begins at Kamehameha Highway station 0 00
and rises from elevation 92 feet at a gentle slope of less than
10 per cent to Kahekili Highway approximately 3 500 feet away
where it reaches an elevation of approximately 176 feet The
road widening will entail shallow side cuts and fills of less
than five feet and will essentially maintain the existing
centerline grade
The existing highway is located on the windward side of the
island of Oahu The soils generally consist of well drained
gravelly colluvium and alluvium eroded from the nearby Koolau
Volcanic Range The soils were deposited during periods of high
percipitation and their deposition was influenced somewhat by
W O
fluctuating sea levels Due to the short water transported
distance the soils contain gravel and some rock fragments
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATIONS
Seven borings were drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 All borings extended to a depth of 10 feet
below the existing ground surfaces
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in our laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic representation of the soils encountered is
presented on the appended boring logs Plate A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch diameter
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
The subsurface explorations reveal that the soil conditions
along the proposed alignment consist of two basic soil types
The upper soil is a dark brown clayey silt varying from 2 to 8
feet in thickness at the test boring locations It is underlain
W 1975
to the limit of the explorations by orange brown clayey silt
with some gravel and rock fragments Both soils vary from very
stiff to extremely stiff in consistency and had natural moisture
contents generally varying from 50 to 60 per cent
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture and density determinations and atterberg limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification and group
ing of the soils The results of these tests are presented on
the boring logs at the appropriate sample location
Following the initial classification the soils were grouped
into two types based upon similar properties and descriptions
Each of these samples was subjected to moisture density and
CBR testing The CBR test results are given on page 5
In addition to the above testing four swell potential tests
were conducted on selected samples to provide additional indica
tions of the swelling potential of the existing soils These
tests conducted at the natural moisture content resulted in
swells of less than one per cent

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface explorations revealed that the areas of the pro
posed Haiku Road widening are underlain by clayey silts of high
plasticity These materials are generally rated as fair to poor
subgrades The CBR testing produced values of 3 to 10 with a
maximum expansion of 12 per cent for the lower orange brown
clayey silt
The large expansion of the compacted samples from Boring 1 is
due in part to the materials being compacted at a moisture con
tent of approximately 37 per cent substantially below the
natural moisture content of 50 to 60 per cent Swell potential
testing of the soils at their natural moisture content indicated
little or no expansion
It is therefore recommended the proposed pavement be designed
for a reduced expansion value of 7 and a CBR value of 3 It will
be necessary to compact all fills wet of optimum to minimize the
expansion Based upon the above design values the following
pavement section is recommended for Haiku Road
245 In Asphalt Concrete
6 In Base Course
18 In Select Borrow
2645 In Total Pavement Thickness
W
The base course should have a minimum CBR value of 85 the
select borrow should be compacted to 95 per cent of the maxi
mum density and should be composed of crusher run waste mudrock
coral sand or cinders
Earthwork and Compaction
In all areas to receive fill the organic topsoil and other dele
terious materials should be removed and the exposed surface
proof rolled to delineate localized soft zones Any soft zones
exposed by the proof rolling should be undercut and backfilled
with compacted soil
Following the proof rolling the approved surface should be
scarified to a depth of six inches and compacted Where the
existing ground surface exceeds a slope of five horizontal to
one vertical 5 1 the existing slope should be keyed and benched
to properly bond the newly placed fill to the existing soil
To minimize the expansion of the compacted on site soils it is
recommended that they be compacted 3 to 5 per cent wet of the
optimum moisture content to 90 per cent of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T
Based upon our laboratory testing the maximum dry density for
the upper dark brown clayey silt is estimated at 91 pcf with an
optimum moisture content of 33 per cent
W O
Cut and fill slopes should be constructed no steeper than two
horizontal to one vertical 2 1 for the on site soils
SITE INSPECTION
It is recommended that the earthwork and compaction operations
be inspected to assure that the subsurface conditions are con
sistent with the findings of the explorations and to verify that
the specified degree Of compaction is consistently achieved for
the fills and roadway sections The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such inspections
Should you have any questions concerning our conclusions or
recommendations or need additional clarification regarding any
part of this report please do not hesitate to contact us
