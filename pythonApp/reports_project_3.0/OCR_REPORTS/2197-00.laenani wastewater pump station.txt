The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized

building

The proposed pump station site is located in the mauka Kahuku corner
of the City and County s Kahaluu Beach Park in a relatively level grassed

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a wastewater pump station on the site to
serve Laenani area by pumping wastewater to a sewer main leading to the
Ahuimanu Wastewater Treatment Plant It is anticipated that this pump
station building will have a single story masonry structure above grade
with a basement level invert about eleven feet 11 below the finished
floor The force main will connect the pump station with an existing sewer
main below Kamehameha Highway
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the northeastern side of the Koolau
Range which is the eroded remnant of a basaltic shield volcano The site
is believed to be situated within the caldera complex of this volcano where
much of the original basaltic rock was hydrothermally altered by gas and
steam action during the eruptive life of the Koolau volcano This
hydrothermal alteration generally made the rocks susceptible to erosion
when exposed After the cessation of eruptive activity the Koolau dome
became deeply eroded
During the Pleistocene epoch sea levels throughout the world
fluctuated in response to the advances and retreats of the great
continental glaciers As the glaciers advanced and spread less water was
available to fill the ocean basins and sea levels fell below the present
level As the glaciers melted and retreated more water was available and
sea levels rose to stands above the present These fluctuations resulted
in changes in the local erosional and depositional base levels
Our exploration encountered what appear to be sediments which were
deposited in response to the fluctuating sea levels In the deepest
boring at the pump station site what appears to be highly weathered
basalt was encountered below these sediments at a depth of about 25 to 30
feet below the existing ground surface

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging between 15 5 and
49 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring drilled at the proposed pump station location Boring 5
encountered approximately two feet 2 of fill composed of medium stiff
brown clayey silt underlain by approximately seven feet 7 of very loose
tannish brown sand with gravel representing a beach sand deposit At about
nine feet below the existing ground surface the boring encountered very
soft to soft alluvial clays and clayey silts which extended to a depth of
about sixteen feet below the existing ground surface and were underlain by
about five feet 5 of stiff alluvial clayey silts Below the alluvial
soils our boring encountered very stiff residual clayey silts which graded
with depth to saprolites or highly weathered basalt Groundwater was
encountered in this boring at a depth of about 3 6 feet below the existing
ground surface Due to the relative proximity of the ocean to the site it
is anticipated that this water level will fluctuate in response to tides
storm surges and other influences
The remaining four 4 borings were drilled along the proposed force
main alignment Borings 1 through 3 encountered a surface pavement section
consisting of about four inches 4 of asphaltic concrete underlain by
about eight inches of crushed basalt base course Below this pavement
section the boring penetrated stiff to very stiff clayey silts believed
to be alluvial soils to the maximum depths drilled in these
feet Boring 4 encountered about four feet 4 of medium stiff clayey
silt which is most likely fill underlain by stiff to very stiff sandy
silts to the maximum depth drilled of 15 5 feet Groundwater was not
encountered in these drilled borings at the time of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering standpoint Based on our field exploration it
is anticipated that the foundations of the proposed structure if
constructed at the currently proposed location will bear on soft alluvial
soils unless they are extended to a depth of about 16 feet below the
existing ground surface
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that some
grading will be necessary to provide a level pad for the construction of
the proposed pump station building
The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading
1 Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The
resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill to the proposed building subgrade level
2 Cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may
be utilized for the design of the project site grading In order
to achieve properly compacted slope faces fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design grade

3 Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction Select Borrow for
Subbase Course dated September 1986
4 Fills and or structural backfills should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding eight 8 to nine 9 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Foundation Excavation and Dewatering
The excavation for the proposed pump station and portions of the
trenches for the connecting sewer lines in the vicinity of the pump
station will encounter poor soil conditions and high groundwater levels
It is anticipated that shoring will be required to support the excavation
sidewalls during construction Generally the method and design of the
shoring should be left to the contractor However it is our opinion
that due to the relative proximity of road embankments and residential
structures braced shoring and or sheetpiles should be considered
It is also anticipated that it will be necessary to dewater the
excavations to allow construction to be performed in the dry Again
the dewatering method should be left to the contractor However we
anticipate that the beach sand stratum encountered will be highly
permeable and may present dewatering difficulties
The contractor should avoid deep areal drawdowns of the groundwater
table to reduce the potential of ground subsidence or settlements In the
vicinity of structures recharging of the water may be required to
maintain groundwater levels at near the existing levels
Dewatering operations should be performed so that only clear water is
pumped Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a quick
condition or blowing of the excavation bottom or sides

Foundation Support
In order to provide uniform support for the proposed structure we
recommend that the mat foundations for the lower level of the pump station
bear entirely on in situ stiff alluvial soils
It is anticipated that the foundations of the emergency generator room
portion of the pump station building will bear on existing fill or on the
backfill of the lower level of the structure This portion of the pump
station building should be structurally separated from the portions of the
pump station supported by deep mat foundations The emergency generator
foundations should bear on a minimum of two feet 2 select non expansive
material compacted to a minimm of 90 maximm dry density as established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
For the design of foundations bearing on compacted fill or in situ
stiff alluvial soils an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be
utilized This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Perimeter foundations should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent outside grade
Settlements
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on compacted fill or
stiff alluvial soils are on the order of one half inch 1 2 total and
one quarter inch 1 4 differential
Buoyant Forces
Due to the high groundwater level encountered in the boring at the
proposed pump station location it is anticipated that the pump station
will be subject to buoyant uplift forces when there is no fluid in the
station It is our opinion that due to the relatively poor soil
conditions encountered at the site these forces should be resisted
entirely by the weight of the structure rather than by frictional
resistance between the pump station walls and the surrounding soils

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor s of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions may be supported by
the in situ weathered basalt formation or stiff alluvium A 6 inch layer
of 3B Fine crushed rock should be provided below the slabs as cushion
fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to floor coverings generating equipment or control equipment an
impervious moisture membrane is recommended below the slabs

Force Main Excavation and Backfill
Based upon our exploration it is our opinion that most of the
proposed force main may be installed by the conventional cut and cover
method Excavation in soft materials with high groundwater levels may be
encountered in the vicinity of the pump station structure
Most of the excavation will be in stiff soils and it is anticipated
that the groundwater levels will generally be lower than the trench
bottom However higher levels may be encountered depending upon weather
tides and other influences

to be performed in the dry Again the dewatering method should be left

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction
are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made

in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If

recommendations given in this report

considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
