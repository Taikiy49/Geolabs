SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our previous field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by dense to very
dense silty gravel fill material extending to about 2 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Alluvium consisting of stiff clay was encountered below the fill layer extending to
a depth of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface Below the stiff clay our borings
encountered very dense silty sand extending a depth of about 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The silty sand was underlain by very stiff clayey silt extending to the
maximum depth drilled of about 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
We did not encounter groundwater at the time of our field exploration However it
should be noted that groundwater levels may vary due to seasonal precipitation and other
factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the information provided a new electric pole will be installed to replace
the existing wooden pole at the project site Based on the loading conditions provided by
the project structural engineer and subsurface soil conditions encountered during our
previous field exploration we recommend supporting the new electric pole on a single
cast in place drilled shaft foundation Detailed discussions and recommendations for
foundation designs are presented in the following sections
Drilled Shaft Foundations
Based on the information provided a drilled shaft diameter of 60 inches was
considered in our foundation analyses for the single shaft foundations The primary
geotechnical considerations in our foundation analyses included the lateral loads and
ground line moments demands on the drilled shaft foundations
In general lateral load resistance of drilled shafts is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the shaft and
induced moment in the shaft Based on a maximum allowed horizontal deflection of
0 5 inches at the top of the drilled shaft subjected to ground line overturning moments and
lateral forces we have evaluated the required drilled shaft lengths using the computer
program LPILE Plus for Windows The results of our engineering analyses are

CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures The construction
workmanship has a substantial effect on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft
foundations
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the
shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids
is not recommended
During concrete placement a minimum of 5 feet of concrete head should be
maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe A low shrink high slump 7 to 9 inch
slump range concrete mix should be used to provide close contact between the drilled
shafts and the surrounding soils The concrete should be placed promptly after drilling to
reduce the potential for softening and caving in of the sides of the drilled holes A Geolabs
representative should be present to observe the drilled shaft installation during
construction to confirm the subsoil conditions and embedment depths
