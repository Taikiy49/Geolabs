INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil engineering evaluation
performed for the subject project

The rectangular shaped 3 800 square foot lot is located about 700
feet mauka of the Ala Wai Canal It is relatively level and presently
occupied by a single story wooden house
At this time the proposed building size and dimensions have not
been firmly established However it is our understanding that the

parking area and driveway will be paved with asphalt concrete
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design
of the foundations of the structure particularly to evaluate if there
would be an adequate crust with sufficient thickness to support a spread
footing type of foundation
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling three
3 borings to depths between 11 5 to 31 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Our drilled borings indicate that a 3 to 4 foot thick highly
compressible layer of lagoonal clayey silt deposit exists below the upper
2 foot surface fill crust at the proposed building site

We believe that the thin upper fill layer and the underlying highly
compressible deposit cannot provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed building and significant settlements may occur under the new
building loads In order to provide adequate bearing for the proposed
building structure it is our opinion that either the soft clayey silt
deposit at the footing foundation locations be over excavated and
replaced with selected compacted structural fill or pile foundations
should be considered
Shallow Spread or Continous Footing Foundations
For the proposed wooden structure spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used However to reduce potential excessive
settlements and the possibility of ground bearing failure we recommend
that the footing excavations be extended down to a minimum depth of about
5 feet below the ground surface to remove the soft marsh deposit The
over excavation should also extend a minimum of 1 5 feet beyond the edge
of the footings See Plate 3 The excavation should first be lined with
a layer of geotextile fabric and then backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to
raise the backfill a minimm 12 inches above the groundwater level Then
non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste or coral should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts not exceeding 8 inches thick and
compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78 prior to footing construction The top 1 foot below finish
subgrade level should be backfilled with impervious non expansive soil
compacted to the above density See Plate 3
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on compacted structural fill
Footings should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation

For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements for
a column load of 20 kips are on the order of one half of an inch total
and one quarter of an inch differential
All footings should be tied together with grade beams to improve the
rigidity of the foundation hence reducing the differential settlement
Pile Foundation
A short pile foundation system can also be considered at this site
as an alternative to the footing over excavation procedure
We believe relatively short piles would develop sufficient load
carrying capacity primarily from end bearing resistance obtained from the
dense underlying coral reef formation
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles could be used for the
pile capacity recommended The piles should extend through the soft
layers and driven into the dense coral material encountered from about 12
feet below the existing ground surface The estimated average pile
length is about 15 feet below ground surface elevation
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 80 tons
per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order of less than
one half of an inch are anticipated
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 8 blows per
inch for the last 3 inches
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation

To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth The backfill
against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with
select on site sandy materials The backfill should be placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a dry density of at least 90 of the maximum density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Site Grading
Site filling should be avoided or at least minimized to less than
one foot since the weight of the new fill would initiate long term ground
settlements for spread footing and additional downdrag loading on the
driven piles
The ground surface below the slabs should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft pockets encountered in
the initial site preparation should be removed and backfilled with select
backfill material
The upper sandy fill materials may be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction usage to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavation and
footing backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained competent
bearing soil has been reached and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
