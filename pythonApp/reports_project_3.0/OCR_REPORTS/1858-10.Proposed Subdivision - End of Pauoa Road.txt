This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed subdivision in Pauoa Valley Oahu
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was perform in general
accordance with our proposal dated This report
complements our preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Reconnaissance Report
dated May 12 1987 for the above referenced project
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized subsoil data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading
retaining wall foundations and pavement of access roads
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Park
Engineering Inc dated The scope of work was limited
to
1 drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging from
14 5 to 15 5 below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing recommendations for site grading retaining
wall foundations and pavement design and
6 preparation of a report presenting our design recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located at the eastern end of Pauoa Road in Pauoa Valley
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by the Pauoa Stream on
the east and Booth Road on the north The slopes next to the stream are
generally steep and covered with some surface boulders
The original ground elevations vary from about 250 feet southeastern
portion to 284 feet northwestern portion above Mean Sea Level At
present the site is being graded and the retaining walls are under
construction
SUBSOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
a rilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 14 5 to 15 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the exploration encountered a surficial silty clay layer
overlying stiff clayey silt with traces of sand and gravel Boulders and
cobbles were encountered in Boring Nos 3 and 4 southeastern portion of
the site from about 5 to 15 feet deep
Groundwater was encountered in Boring Nos 3 and 4 at depths ranging
from 6 8 to 9 4 feet below the original ground surface during our
drilling It is anticipated that the groundwater will be affected by the
water level change of the nearby Pauoa Stream

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the near
surface on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has low
to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content
of the soil prior to subsequent saturation
More detailed geotechnical engineering evaluations are thus
recommended for individual lots to provide detailed design criteria for
house foundations
Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fills are anticipated
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris and other
deleterious material In areas to receive fill the ground should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 density per ASTM D 1557 70 test
method
After the above site preparation the fill should be moistened to
above optimum moisture content placed in maximum 10 inch lifts and
compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of its maximum dry density established
by the above ASTM test designation
Tt is recommended that if additional off site borrow is required it
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
transportation to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed
construction The material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious matter

House Foundations
The following general foundation guidelines are presented for
preliminary design purposes Borings or probings are recommended prior to
the start of house construction to determine whether the soil conditions
at each lot require modifications to the preliminary guidelines below
1 Footings located on or near slopes should penetrate down a
sufficient depth in order to develop a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of the
footing foundation out to the slope face
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the ground surface to reduce the effects of
subsequent soil shrinkage or swelling All footing backfill
should be properly compacted to obtain a minimum 90 compaction
and graded to drain water away from the footing
We recommend that a soils engineer be retained to observed the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement

For all the houses it is critical that the subgrade soils below the
foundations be kept moist for a few days prior to concrete placement
It is imperative that all the lots be graded to drain water away from
house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the subgrade
soils

Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher

should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce
water infiltration behind the wall

Access Road Pavement
Based on the CBR test in our previous reconnaissance report dated
May 12 1987 and experience in the area the following preliminary
pavement design section is recommended for the access roadway extending
from the end of Pauca Road
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 0 Inch Select Borrow
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The select borrow may be reduced or eliminated if the top 2 feet
below the subgrade is fairly non expansive material
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the earth

geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations

to monitor the contractor s grading operations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from objects indicated on the grading plan dated October 15
1987 provided by Park Engineering Inc Elevations of the data points
were approximately determined by estimation from the plan The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
