SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 29 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE II
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory testing
and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a one story structure approximately 64 by 80 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and the
house Wood framed construction is anticipated for the proposed house
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls generally less than
4 feet high are planned along the rear portion of the house to provide
grade separation

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located at the northern end of Ieialii Street in
the Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad of about 64 by 96 feet in plan is situated
along the front portion of the site The ground surface then slopes
downward at about 2 1 H V in the north and west directions from
elevations 488 to 460 and 450 feet respectively Benches of about 8 feet
wide are provided at approximate elevations of 474 and 460 feet There is
a 2 foot wide concrete drainage ditch along the sloping ground in the
southern boundary
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 29 to depths of 5 to 10 feet
below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered medium to very stiff reddish
brown clayey silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass
grading plan for Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II dated
prepared by Community Planning Inc indicated that Lot 29 received up to
20 feet of compacted fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered
generally can provide suitable foundation support for the proposed
residential structure provided that some care is exercised during
construction to minimize the swelling potential of the underlying soil due
to wetting
laboratory tests performed on the clayey silt soil indicate that
this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential depending
on the in situ moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent subgrade
saturation
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive granular soils should also be used as backfill
behind the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
A surface hole approximately 2 by 3 feet in plan and 3 feet deep
was observed during our site exploration We believe that it was a
result from drainage erosion of the surface soil by rain water runoff
This hole or possible voids if any encountered during construction
should be over excavated to suitable size and backfilled with select
non expansive granular soils or on site silty soil

Lot Grading
The surface soils appeared to be somewhat loosened by the prolonged
exposure to wetting and drying conditions Therefore we recommend that
prior to filling the surface the lot should be recompacted to a minimum
of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive granular soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of the above specified non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive therefore we recommend the on site soil be
over excavated down to a depth of twelve 12 inches below the slab
bottom The exposed subgrade should thoroughly be moistened and then
replaced with twelve 12 inches of non expansive imported soils below the
4 inch cushion fill The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
subgrade soil below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week
prior to concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two
days and sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
We recommend a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel be provided as the
cushion fill below the slab The 3B Fine gravel layer will also act asa
capillary break To protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced with a 4 inch
base course layer and an 8 inch thick perimeter thickened edges It is
critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition
and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints at about 8 to 10 foot intervals are recommended in the
driveway slab in order to allow for some relative movement of the
concrete
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally less than 4
feet high would be required at the rear of the proposed residence The
guidelines for the retaining wall designs are listed below

Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent

Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure

An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff
on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls on slopes

Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent excessive water from entering
the foundation slab subsoils
We believe these drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could
cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss
of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible after footing
backfill and maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab
and footing foundation

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be cbserved by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with our findings presented in this report
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
