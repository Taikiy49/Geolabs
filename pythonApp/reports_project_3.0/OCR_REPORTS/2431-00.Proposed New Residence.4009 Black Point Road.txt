INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed new residence at 4009 Black Point
Road in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated November 7
1989
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived fron our analysis based
on the data obtained during our exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on schematic plans provided to us the proposed new residence
will be a two story structure approximately 65 to 110 feet in plan
dimensions It is anticipated that the residence will be of wood and
masonry construction with slabs on grade for the lower level floors A
swimming pool is planned at the southwest portion of the site

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to
Tanabe Corporation dated Our scope of work was limited
to the following tasks and work efforts

and operators to and from the project site

depths ranging between 6 5 and 12 0 feet below the existing
ground surface

by a Field Engineer Geologist from our firm
4 Iaboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid to classifying the materials encountered
and determining their engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
proposed single family residential development

the island of Oahu The lot is rectangular shaped and about 14 400 square

feet in plan dimensions A concrete driveway was noted on the

10 feet in plan was noted on the southeastern portion of the site The
remaining areas were covered with grass and trees

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff underlying on site soils encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site surface silty clay indicated that this material generally has low
to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content
of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the
effects due to soil swelling we recommend that the on site silty clay
soils below on grade slabs be removed or be capped with at least twelve
12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
below the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
during construction until the slab concrete placement and backfill
An existing pond was noted on the southeastern portion of the site
and is within the new house boundary Any loose or soft soils within the
pond area should be over excavated down to firm subsoils and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material

lot Grading
The subgrade soil and backfills within the top twelve 12 inches
below the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill material should be tested for suitability by the
project geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at the subgrade areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill should be placed in maximm eight inch 8 thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM D 1557 78
House Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the on site stiff soils or compacted fill The
bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and or seismic loads are considered
Estimated settlements are on the order of less than on half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimm of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of excavated footing locations should be tamped to a dense firm
consistency

If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines Loose utility line
backfills adjacent to the new footings should be properly removed and
recompacted to a minimm of 90 relative density per ASTM D 1557 78
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and to see that loose
soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Iateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3
may be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance
in the upper 18 inches of soils should be neglected
Slabs On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry and then saturated Therefore the
existing soils should either be filled over or over excavated down below
the slab bottom to provide a minimum 12 inch capping layer of
non expansive imported soils between the slab and the natural ground
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of twelve
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist during construction and prior to concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Pavements
It is anticipated that concrete pavement sections are planned for the
new driveway
Based on the laboratory CBR test the following pavement section may
be utilized
Concrete Pavement Driveway
5 Inches Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inches Aggregate Base Course
11 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
at the time of construction to confirm the above design
The recommended section also considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located at the site If encountered the
cesspool locations should be flagged in the field To reduce future
settlement of structures where existing cesspools are located the sludge
should be removed and filled with compacted granular backfill such as
broken up coral or cinder The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the
finished grade should be removed to prevent hard spots and subsequent
differential settlement at the finished grade

Swimming Pool
Swimming pool design and construction are generally the
responsibility of the swimming pool builder This contractor is also
generally responsible for quality control and performance of the completed
pool
Since low to moderately expansive soil was encountered non expansive
select fill material is recommended under the perimeter walkways and slab
areas The pool walls and bottom should be properly designed to resist
possible swelling pressures due to future saturation of the exposed
subgrade soils We recommend that the swimming pool contractor should
seek design guidance from qualified consultants to help them to properly
design and to monitor the proposed pool construction
Design Revi
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines show on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
