This report presents the results of our geologic reconnaissance
and test pits at the subject development site located on the
northern slope of Punchbowl Crater adjacent to Prospect Street
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is presently planned to construct three story cluster single
family housing development on the subject parcel The preliminary
development scheme is to locate the units around the perimeter of
the site with an access roadway from Prospect Street through the
center of the site

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The project site is located on the northern slope of Punchbowl
Crater above Prospect Street near the intersection of Prospect
and Puowaina Streets The site is presently heavily overgrown
with trees shrubs and grasses Located in the northeast corner
of the site is a burned out house and associated concrete slab
two loose rock walls and a walk and stairway leading to Prospect
Street
The site is fairly steep sloping up from the street level at
a gradient of 3 horizontal to 1 vertical Surveyed elevations
range from about 195 at the street to 264 feet at the southeast
boundary
Geology and Soil Conditions
Punchbowl Crater is composed of a vitric volcanic tuff formed
during a violent phreatomagmatic eruption of agh similar to Salt
Lake Crater and Diamond Head Crater Volcanic tuff forms a
brown hard glassy rock known locally as mudrock
Hadley Pruyn Developers Inc
W
P 3
The site is underlain entirely by the Punchbowl tuff Exposed
rock outcrops can be found along Prospect Street in a 10 to
20 foot nearby vertical cliff Outcrops are also present in
a 3 to 5 foot ledge behind the derelict dwelling and on the
ground surface around the dwelling The tuff is highly frac
tured and jointed Bedding planes are evident dipping appro
ximately 40 degree in test pit 2 to 70 degree in the rock wall
at the street level
Over most of the site the tuff is highly weathered up to a
depth of about 8 5 feet test pit 2 and easily excavated
with a backhoe The weathered tuff is very highly fractured
and breaks easily into a platy gravel sand and silt mixture
when excavated Overlying the tuff over most of the site
a relatively thin layer of recent alluvium and slope wash
material consisting of a 1 to 3 foot layer of brown clayey
silt
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our test pits and geologic reconnaissance
the site appears to be underlain by competent subsoils and it is
our opinion that the site can be devéloped for the proposed resi
dential development provided soil engineering recommendations are
followed
Hadley Pruyn Developers Inc
W O 866 00
P 4
Building Foundations
Relatively light structural loading is anticipated from the
proposed residential buildings Based on the preliminary
soil information we anticipate that conventional shallow
foundations bearing on the weathered mudrock formation or
compacted fill can be utilized
Some of the on site soil may be moderately expansive and
may require deeper perimeter footings around the slab on
grade Additional borings and swell tests should be con
ducted during the design phase soil investigation to evaluate
the slab design
Retaining Wall Foundation
High retaining walls are planned for the development From
the preliminary soil data it appears that the wall foundations
could bear on the underlying dense mudrock formation
Tiebacks anchored into the dense mudrock could be used to help
resist the lateral soil pressures against the walls Additional
studies should be made to determine the capacity of the tiebacks
and evaluate the bedding planes of the mudrock

Site Grading
The proposed cuts will be mostly in weathered to dense mudrock
The weathered mudrock is easily excavated however some hard
ripping may be encountered in the underlying dense mudrock
formation
Excluding the material larger than 6 inches the excavated
mudrock should be satisfactory as fill material
Additional Studies
Additional soil engineering and geologic studies as well as
borings drilled within the final location of the building and
retaining wall should be made in order to obtain specific
engineering recommendations for the design of the foundations
and site grading
This report was prepared to assist the architect and engineer
for this planned project and DLU application In the event
that changes in the design or location of the structures are
made the soils engineer should be provided the opportunity
of reviewing the changes in order that modifications could be
made on their previous conclusions and recommendations
