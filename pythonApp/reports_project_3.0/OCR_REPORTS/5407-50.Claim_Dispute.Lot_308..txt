Dear Ms Nagata
As requested we reviewed the report from JPB Engineering Inc dated
March 10 2015 regarding a geoanalytical investigation conducted for the Ku residence at
Lot 308 of the above project site Based on our review and field observation records during
the mass grading construction we provide the following opinions for your review and
consideration
The mass grading operation in the vicinity of Lot 308 was completed in late 2013
A claim from the Ku residence was submitted around October 2014 for the concrete
slab damage during the house expansion project It was concluded that the concrete
slab damage was caused by the improper construction sequence by the homeowner s
house expansion contractor It was observed that the slope toe was removed to install a
CRM retaining wall and boulder debris and heavy excavator was placed at top of the
slope as shown in the attached photographs taken in October 2014 Consequently the
initial claim was denied
Approximate locations of slope toe removal and heavy weight surcharge are
illustrated on the house plan Sheet S 7 1A for reference
The homeowner hired JPB Engineering Inc in March 2015 to investigate the
cause of the concrete slab damage Based on the geoanalytical investigation report the
homeowner resubmitted the claim in May 2016 stating that expansive soil placed under
the house structure caused the damage
Based on our review of the geoanalytical investigation report we found several
inaccurate improper engineering interpretations and assumptions resulting incorrect
slope stability analyses Our findings are summarized as follows

1
The report generalizes the subsurface conditions consisting of loose to
very loose gravelly sand over stiff silty clay and dense to very dense
cobbles boulders This generalized soil profile is incorrect The loose
gravelly sand was only encountered in Boring No 1 where the ground
was raised during the home expansion by the homeowner The loose
gravelly sands were not encountered in Boring Nos 2 and 3 Approximate
boring locations are also transformed and shown on house foundation
plan Sheet S 7 1A
2
It is also suggested that the backfill of the rockwall installed by the
homeowner s contractor was not properly compacted
3
The soil profile presented in section A A is incorrect The stiff silty clays
were encountered in Boring Nos 2 and 3 but not in Boring No 1
However the soil profile in Section A A artificially assumed the stiff silty
clays extended from top to bottom of the slope crossing all three borings
4
Footings were embedded at 4 feet depth with 8 feet of horizontal setback
distance between edge of footing and face of slope as illustrated on
house section Sheet S 7 3A The assumptions of the footing embedment
depth shown on the section A A are incorrect
5
Based on our field observation during the mass grading operation on site
crush rock material was placed within the entire house footprint with
sufficient compaction The silty clay was only used for retaining wall
backfill
6
Consequently the slope stability analyses were incorrectly conducted
along a cross section with a false silty clay layer resulting in low factors of
safety for critical failure circles
7
The slope stability analyses indicated a factor of safety of 1 187 under
static condition and 0 983 under seismic condition However the factor of
safety of 1 187 suggested the existing slope did not fail factor of safety
should be less than 1 0 for failure under the service load condition Also
we have not experienced an earthquake on Oahu since 2006 The factor
of safety of 0 983 does not prove the concrete slab damage was caused
by the slope instability
8
Level survey in the garage and living areas showing most differential
settlement concentrated on the Diamondhead side of the house was
conducted on when the home expansion project was in
progress As mentioned previously the toe of the slope was removed and
the heavy excavator and boulder debris were placed on the top of the
slope along the Diamondhead side of the property The above improper

construction sequence compromised the house footings and caused the

Based on the above discussion we believe that the geoanalytical investigation
report provided incorrect inappropriate interpretation for the generalized soil profile The
slope stability analyses were conducted based on incorrect assumptions resulting in
lower factors of safety The results of the geoanalytical investigation did not conclude
that the damages were caused by the slope instability and expansive soil It should be
noted that the report did not conclude that the soil under the house is expansive
Furthermore we believe that the differential settlement presented in the report
was caused due to improper construction sequence by the house expansion contractor
We believed the above construction sequence most likely compromised the house
footings and may introduce more damage due to lack of compaction effort on the
retaining wall backfills
