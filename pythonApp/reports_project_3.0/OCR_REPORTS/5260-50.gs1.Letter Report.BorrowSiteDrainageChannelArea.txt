PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A potential borrow site for fill material to be used for the Ilima at Leihano project
is between Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kalaeloa Harbor in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The borrow site is about 2 400 feet long by 380 feet wide in plan dimension and
is in a future drain channel site We understand that the bottom of the future channel will
be about Elevation 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the north end and about 6 feet
MSL at the south end We understand that the borrow site is divided into two areas
Area A and Area B as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In addition we understand
that the area available to the Ilima at Leihano project has not been determined
Therefore our field exploration was performed in both Area A and Area B

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau
Volcano reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating
sediments that were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and further north
these alluvial sediments were interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef
formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from zero feet in the area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by excavating and
sampling six test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 6 to depths of about 10 to
16 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate test pit locations are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered coralline and alluvial lagoonal
deposits extending to the maximum depth explored of about 16 feet below the existing
ground surface The coralline deposits generally consisted of sands and gravels with
boulders gravel with some sand and clay silty sand clayey gravel and coral formation
Three of the test pits encountered alluvial lagoonal deposits consisting of clay sandy
clay and gravel and clay soils
We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in the test pits are presented on the Logs of
Test Pits Plates A 1 through A 6 in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests
performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed borrow excavation site is underlain by coralline deposits and
alluvial lagoonal deposits We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time
of our field exploration Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the
majority of the materials within the borrow excavation site is suitable for use as
non expansive capping fill material
The coralline deposits consisting of sands and gravels with boulders gravels with
some sand and clay silty sand clayey gravel and coral formation may be considered
suitable for non expansive capping fill material The coral fragments and boulders
should be reduced to less than 3 inches in largest dimension The elevation contour
plan that was provided was not to scale The contour plan was fitted over the aerial
photograph of the borrow site to obtain an approximate scale drawing Based on this
approximate drawing it is estimated that about 60 and 75 percent of the excavated
materials from Areas A and B respectively of the proposed borrow site may be used
as non expansive capping fill material
Three of the test pits Test Pit and 5 encountered highly expansive
alluvial lagoonal soils from about 0 to 7 foot depths It is estimated that about 40 and

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field test pits Variations of conditions between and
beyond the field test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented herein
The test pit locations in the field were estimated by using the elevation contour
plan The field test pit locations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the test pits depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the test pits was measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data
have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation herein However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tidal fluctuation seasonal
rainfall and other factors

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 was performed on selected soil samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented
on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths
Thirteen Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are presented on the
Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test
results is provided on Plates B 1 and B 2
Thirteen Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soil and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plates B 3
through B 5
Thirteen laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the engineering
characteristics of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture
content of the soils Compaction of the samples was performed by dropping a 10 pound
hammer from a height of 18 inches The samples were compacted in five equal layers
with each layer receiving 56 blows of energy The samples were soaked in water prior
to the penetration test The test results are presented on Plates B 6 and B 18
