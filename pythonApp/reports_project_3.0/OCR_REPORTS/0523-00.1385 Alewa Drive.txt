This report presents the results of our soil investigation for
the proposed residence in Alewa Drive Honolulu Hawaii
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface con
ditions at the site determine foundation requirements for the
residence and to provide recommendations for the retaining wall
construction
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site one boring
was drilled utilizing portable rotary wash equipment The
location of the boring is shown on the site plan Plate 1 A
geologist from our office was present throughout the field

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed building site is a newly subdivided lot Lot nBt
located on the lower sloping parcel in Alewa Heights Honolulu
The lot slopes down from an elevation of 564 on the northern
end approximately 2 horizontal to 1 vertical to elevation
530 at its southern boundary adjacent to Alewa Drive The
slope is presently covered with grass and two mango trees
located on the ewa end of the lot area The slope did not
exhibit any signs of instability at the time of our investiga
tion
SOILS
The site is immediately underlain by stiff brown clayey silts
with decomposed basalt rock about 12 feet in thickness The
decomposed basalt graded into a weathered rock to 15 feet
Generally the upper surface two feet of the sloping surface
is moist and may contain pockets of loose soil due to vegeta

General
Except for pockets of loose soils on the slope surface the
site appeared to be firm original ground No evidence of deep
fill was found along the existing slope therefore it is our
opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed residence
supported on post and beams and continuous strip footings
The proposed foundations should bear into stiff clayey silt
soils and bottomed approximately two feet below adjacent
ground A bearing value of 1500 pounds per square foot may
be used for dead plus live loads Continuous footings should
have a minimum width of 12 inches Pressures may be increased
by one third when considering wind or seismic loads Indivi
dual post footings for the residence should extend below the
slope surface such that the outer edge of footing should have
a minimum horizontal set back distance of 4 feet to the slope
surface We recommend for erosion protection the footing
excavation should be backfilled with concreté to match the
upper slope surface

Retaining Wall
It is anticipated that maximum height of retaining wall will
be on the order of 12 vertical feet Based on boring informa
tion it appears that full depth of the retaining wall cut will
be through stiff residual clayey silt and some occasional
decomposed rock It is recommended the cut slope be inspected
by a geologist or soils engineer from our firm to verify that
no geologic planes of weakness or groundwater seepage exist
below the present slope and subsurface conditions are as anti
cipated For design of retaining wall it may be assumed that
the soils will impose a lateral pressure equal to that devel
oped by a fluid with a density of 60 pounds per cubic foot
Resistance to horizontal sliding may be provided by a combina
tion of passive pressure and friction along the bottom of
footings Passive pressures may be assumed equal to the
equivalent fluid density of 300 pounds per cubic foot The
coefficient of friction between concrete and site soil may be
taken at 0 4 The backfilled immediately against the wall
should be an open grade compacted granular material and adequate
drainage system should be designed to prevent the build up of
excessive pore pressure behind retaining wall

Because of the change in height of the proposed retaining wall from
a maximum height of fourteen feet to the present height of about
eight feet the following retaining wall design parameter should
be used
1 Bearing value of 2000 pounds per square foot may be used for
dead plus live loads
2 An additional surcharge load of 100 pounds per foot of wall
shall be used due to automobile traffic behind drive way retain
ing wall The minimum distance from wall to tyre is assumed
to be 1 5 feet away
3 It may be assumed that the soils will impose a lateral pressure
equal to that develop by a fluid with a density of 40 pounds
per cubic foot
4 Individual retaining wall footing should extend below the slope
surface such that the outer edge of footing should have a mini
mum horizontal set back distance of 4 feet to the slope surface
5 The backfill and new fill behind wall should be well drain
granular materials with adequate drainage system
