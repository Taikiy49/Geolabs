REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
voicanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau shield reached the
end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Koolau Range and is
composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava from the Koolau Volcanic Series
These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt
interbedded with thin clinker layers The lava flows have weathered in place and are
characterized by the red and orange colors of the soil

House Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that
a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be
used to support the proposed new residential structure An allowable bearing pressure of
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be utilized for the design of footings bearing on
stiff to hard on site silty clay materials This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces To reduce the risk of damage from slope movements structures and
pavements should be set back a minimum of 12 feet from the top of the southern slope
Also perimeter footings and slab edges within 20 feet from the top of the southern slope
should be embedded a minimum of 36 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For other
footings a minimum embedment depth of 24 inches is recommended
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on the stiff to hard on site silty
clay materials as recommended herein to be less than inch total Differential
settlement is estimated to be less than one half inch
If soft ioose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying
competent material
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by silty
clay materials with low to moderate swelling potential Unless concrete slabs supported
by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the
lightly loaded slabs on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of
these soils due to changes in moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the on site clayey soils we recommend that
non expansive select granular fill material be provided for a minimum depth of 18 inches
below the bottom of the slab cushion
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed immediately after the
moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils The prepared select
granular fill and the underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to
final placement of concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report Compaction
requirements for the subgrade soils and select granular fill are also discussed in the same
section of this report

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential of water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away
from the slab and footing foundations
