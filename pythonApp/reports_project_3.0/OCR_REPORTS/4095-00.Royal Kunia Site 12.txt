This report summarizes the geotechnical engineering observations for the Royal Kunia
Site 12 development excluding Lot Nos 1 through 5 and 8 through 81 within the Pan Handle
Area located at the western corner of the development
Fine grading of Lot Nos 108 through 143 was observed by Geolabs Inc at the Royal
Kunia Site 12 development from January 1999 through April 1999 The finished grade
compaction at Lot Nos 88 through 107 and 188 through 195 was tested by Geolabs Inc at
the Royal Kunia Site 12 development in July 1998
Mass grading and utility installation for the remaining lots excluding Lot Nos 1 though
5 and 8 through 81 within the Pan Handle Area was observed by Hawaii Geotechnical
Group Inc at the Royal Kunia Site 12 development from April 1995 through August 1995
The grading report by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc covering the mass grading and utility
installation operations at the Royal Kunia Site 12 development from April 1995 through
August 1995 is attached to this report This grading report by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc
is entitled Royal Kunia Site 12 Lots 1R thru 70R 1C thru 620 1 thru 8 82 thru 87 144 thru

The subject site is located southeast of Ano iki Street in Waipahu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii A copy of the site plan is attached for your reference
The mass grading and utility backfilling work was performed by Oahu Construction
Company Ltd The mass grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills less than 10 feet
in height and depth The fill materials were generally imported from nearby Royal Kunia
project sites and generated from cuts on site The available fill materials generally consisted
of reddish brown silty clay

The fine grading work at Lot Nos 108 through 143 was performed by Royal Contracting
Company inc The fine grading work generally consisted of recompacting the subgrades and
fine grading the pads The fine grading consisted of minor fills and cuts of less than 1 foot in
height and depth
The fill and backfill materials were brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the fine grading period and testing of finished grades at Lot Nos 88 through
107 and 188 through 195 the Modified Proctor test performed by Hawaii Geotechnical Group
Inc for the on site reddish brown clayey silt was utilized The maximum dry density and
optimum moisture content relationships were established by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fine grading operations and testing of finished grades at Lot Nos 88 through
107 and 188 through 195 periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance
with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests on the finished grades of the pads are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractors fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Our test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used Where low test results were noted the areas were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on our field observations by our field technician the fine grading work at
Lot Nos 108 through 143 and testing of the finished grades at Lot Nos 88 to 107 and 188
through 195 for the project indicate that the work was performed in accordance with the
general intent of the recommendations in the following references
1 Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended
2 Report by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc entitled Preliminary Soil Report

Grading Memorandum
The mass grading work for the above project specified lots was generally constructed
with on site soils The fill was placed and compacted in thin layers A soil technician
from our office was present at the site on an intermittent basis to observe grading
progress and to take density tests Whenever fill operations were on a continuous basis
a soil technician usually visited the site daily
Grading Plans dated made by ParEn Inc were used as a guide for soil
testing purposes
A tabulation of the field density test results is attached Where low tests were noted the
area was rerolled and in most cases retested The density test results at the time and at
the locations taken were in our opinion in substantial conformance with the density
requirements of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended and with the
recommendations in our Preliminary Soils Report Royal Kunia Site 12 dated
as applicable
Bearing values for light residential structures of about may be used for footings
on stiff natural ground or on compacted select fill
Even though in our opinion the field density tests by our office conform to the density
requirements of the City s Ordinance the passage of time natural causes or construction
at or adjacent to the site may result in changes in soil conditions and we suggest the
following precautions
1 Some creep or settlement may occur near the tops of slopes Foundations
near tops of slopes or over sloping ground should be avoided or designed
under the guidance of an Engineer
