INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation and
percolation testing for the proposed residential developments
The purpose of this investigation was to define general subsur
face soil conditions to determine the foundation requirements
for the proposed dwellings and to evaluate the stability of the
on site soils for fill and site grading Percolation tests were
performed to measure seepage characteristics of the subsoil for
the proposed cesspools
W O 558 00 Page 1974
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to develop 76 residential lots for one
story single family dwellings with concrete slabs on grade The
present streets from the existing subdivision will be extended
One to three feet of fill is anticipated to obtain the proposed
final grades
SITE CONDITIONS
Site Description
The proposed development site is located on a triangular shaped
tract bordered by cane hauling roads on the southeast and south
west and Kaliuna Road on the north The site is about 1 4 mile
north of the Waialua Sugar Mill in Oahu Hawaii It is presently
covered by matured sugar cane and is traversed by several irriga
tion ditches The sugar cane field is relatively level and moder
ately undulated The surface of the 12 6 acre site is covered
by a thick layer of organic material
Geology
The oldest rocks of northern Oahu are lava flows of the Waianae
Volcano which are partly overlapped by younger flows of the Koolau
Volcano and separated locally by alluvium from the younger flows
Erosions has diminshed the Waianae and Koolau Volcanoes The
eroded material forms extensive alluvial deposit in the lowlands
Sand consisting of fragments of calcareous algae coral foraminifera
W O 558 00 Page 1974
and other marine organism form extensive beaches along the coast
Coral and coral rubble are also found underlying part of the
Waialua Bay area
Groundwater
Groundwater in the Waialua Haleiwa area moves generally seaward
at gradient of about one foot per mile and is confined by allu
vium as far as two miles inland The alluvium and marine sedi
ment coral and sand thicken seaward and extend to more than 200
feet below sea level at the coast These sediments confine ground
water less effectively than the material in the Mokuleia area be
cause there is more coralline limestone which allows water to dis
charge from the underlying basalt to the ocean Coral and coral
rubbles are generally fairly permeable It is expected that the
extensive irrigation areas add substantial amounts of water due
to infiltration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site six borings were
drilled at the locations shewn on the Site Plan Plate 1 A more
detailed description of the field exploration and laboratory test
ing are contained in the Appendix along with boring logs and labo
ratory test results
W O 558 00 Page 1974
The borings revealed that the site is blanketed by approximately
10 feet of medium to extremely stiff reddish brown clayey silt
The medium clayey silt was found in and 6 and is
apparently due to saturation of the soils by the irrigation ditches
3 are located on the edge of the cane field and con
sequently have less water seepage and stiffer soil conditions
Underlying the reddish brown clayey silt is tan to light brown
coral gravel and rubble approximately 5 6 feet in thickness This
ceral gravel zone is below the groundwater level and is lightly ce
mented These coral gravels are medium dense and relatively easily
penetrated by augers
Below the coral gravel is tan gray cemented coral locally called
ledge coral This hard ledge coral is generally quite porous since
it contains numerous sand filled voids The ledge coral cannot
readily be penetrated by augers
Groundwater was encountered at approximately elevation 1 feet and
it is anticipated that this water level will be subject to tidal
influence because of the nearness to the coast No indication of
subsurface water flows above elevation l feet were noted during
our investigation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed resi
dential lots development with conventional shallow foundations
supported on fill or the underlying very stiff clayey silt The
existing organic uncompacted soil within the irrigation ditches
should be removed and if necessary replaced with compacted soil
Foundations
Conventional spread or continuous footings bearing in fill or the
very stiff clayey silt may be used to support the proposed dwellings
The foundations should bear a minimum of 18 inches below the adja
cent final grade where they may be designed for maximum bearing
pressures of The estimated settlements for these founda
tions should not exceed inch total and inch differential
Slabs On Grade
It is recommended that the slabs on grade be placed on a minimum of
four inches of granular material In areas where dampness in the
W O 558 00 Page 1974
floor slab would be undesirable or where moisture susceptible
floor coverings are to be used a plastic membrane should be uti
lized as a vapor barrier
Combination floor slabs with thicken edges can be utilized for
the structural support
The expansion tests show that the on site soils are not expansive
with less than one percent swell Special undercutting or design
for swelling pressures will not be required
Site Grading and Earthwork
Prior to any earthwork or foundation construction the site should
be cleared of the existing cane roots and other deleterious mate
rials This may require undercutting of some areas where the or
ganic materials extend below the existing surface
The soils should then be brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted in place to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum
dry density established by American Society for Testing and Materials
ASTM Test D 1557 72T
A moisture density test performed on a surface sample in accordance
with the above specification results in a maximum density of 95 2
pounds per cubic feet at an optimum water content of 29 0 per cent
The existing soils at the site have water contents above optimum
and must be dried or mixed with drier soils for proper compaction
W O 558 00 Page 1974
It is recommended that the earthwork operations be inspected on
a full time basis to assure that the organic materials are properly
removed and that the specified degree of compaction is consistently
achieved
The foundation excavations should also beinspected for localized
soft pockets prior to the placement of the reinforcing or concrete
Where soft zones are encountered they should be removed and re
placed with compacted granular material The extent of the removal
if required must be established during construction
Pavement Design
The following pavement section should be considered for the preli
minary design The final design would be determined by the strength
and characteristics of the fill material Assuming that on site soils
are used with a CBR value of 5 to 8 the required pavement section

Cesspool Depth
It is recommended that the cesspool excavations extend a minimum
of four feet into the coral layer The upper portion of the coral
within the borings was only lightly cemented and can generally

be removed without blasting Some localized hard zones may be
encountered If encountered the lower ledge coral is hard and
will require special excavation methods for removal

Gentlemen
This letter summarizes our May 14 1975 site discussions of
the general guidelines for the cesspool construction
Approximately 75 cesspools are planned for on site sewage
disposal The cesspools should penetrate the approximately
ten foot thick soil mantle and extend four feet into the
underlying coral formation
The cesspool excavation should be minimized to the extent
necessary for construction access and safety Following
the cesspool wall construction the annulus outside of the
cesspool wall should be backfilled with compacted soil
The on site soils at their excavated water content far exceed
the optimum moisture content for proper compaction and must
be dried prior to use The materials are quick drying but
become hard as the moisture content is lowered The soils
are cohesive and will require greater compactive effort and
moisture controls to obtain adequate compaction
The contractor should be cautioned not to over compact this
backfill material The material should be placed and com
pacted to between 80 and 85 percent of the maximum density
s i
4
W 2 May 16 1975
established by ASTM Test Designation 1557 72T Exceeding
this compaction level can more than double the earth pressures
against the cesspool walls
Granular free draining materials are preferred as backfill
outside the wall These materials can be easily placed with
minimum compactive effort and with little consideration of
the fill moisture content A light tapping of these mate
rials or placement by jetting will provide adequate densities
to minimize surface settlements These densities would be
obtained without exerting undue pressures on the cesspool
walls
In summary it is recommended that the lateral extent of the
cesspool excavations be held to a minimum and that the space
between the outside of the wall and the soil be backfilled
with free draining granular material compacted to between 80
and 85 percent of the maximum density These recommendations
will minimize the earth pressures against the wall while pro
viding adequate support for the surface and eliminating
excessive surface settlements

Submitted herewith is our compation report pertaining to the
roadway utility trench backfill water lines and storm drain
age lines at the above mentioned project Continuous inspection
was provided during trench backfill In place density tests
were taken at intervals as needed at each lift
The trenches were excavated in the subdivision roadways to the
desired depth and air dried until the sides of the trenches be
came stiff S 4C material was utilized beneath the water lines
and storm drains to provide the desired pipe bedding support
In addition S 4C granular material was used as backfill to the
top 6 inches above the pipe
Beyond that on site material was placed in 8 inch compacted
lifts The backfill material was watered to optimum moisture
