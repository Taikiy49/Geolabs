INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed Cluster Development at Manoa Valley
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The plan for the proposed Cluster Development consists of the
construction of fifteen 15 two story wood frame houses supported on a
post and beam foundation system These structures will be relatively
light and flexible with regard to settlements
A new bridge across Waihi Stream will be constructed to connect the
parce with Waakaua Street Also two subsidiary roadways A and B will
be constructed te serve the houses
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W Page 2
Site grading will consist of shallow fillings in the low lying
areas adjacent to the streams and minor cutting at the high ground area
The proposed development will also provide riprap linings along the
stream banks
PROJECT HISTORY
Three 3 subsurface investigations were performed for the project
site by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Itd FGE in
1978 and April 1979 for the previcus project developers Following the
recommendations suggested by FGE site grading and demolition operations
began in mid to late 1980 but were terminated in October 1980 after
approximately 3 to 4 feet of compacted fill was placed over the low lying
area adjacent to Waiakeakua Stream Installation of subsurface drains
and settlement markers were supposedly installed at the area however we
are not able to verify the above works
Construction work resumed recently However due toe the fact that
FGE terminated their current involvement with the project Geolabs Hawaii
was contracted to oversee and monitor the remaining geotechnical
activities of the on going construction

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this Geolabs Hawaii s investigation was to further
evaluate the subsurface conditions at some critical locations at the site
in order to provide additional information about the existing site
condition and the current state of construction The investigation would
also supplement and if necessary provide amendments to the recommenda
tions proposed by FGE regarding site grading and foundations of the
proposed structures Monitoring of both vertical settlements and lateral
ground movements due to the new construction along the stream bank area
was considered critical and therefore became an important consideration
to be included in the current soil investigation and instrumentation
program
The scope of our investigation included review of available
information and data about the subsurface condition at the site field
reconnaissance Grilling and sampling of five 5 borings installation
of two 2 inclinometers and three 3 settlement markers laboratory
testing of soil samples cbtained engineering analysis and report
preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of the report

EXISTING SITE CONDITION
The proposed site is located at the end of Waaloa Way at the head
of Manoa Valley Honolulu Cahu Hawaii The site is bounded to the
north southwest and southeast by Waaloa Way Waihi and Waiakeakua
Streams respectively The current topography consists of low lying
filled area along the stream banks and upper level high ground at the
north central section of the site
At the time this report was prepared the following site conditions
were observed
1 Site grading operation for both the high ground and low lying
area were near completion
2 The two major roadways were shaped
3 Sewer manholes and sewer and water line underneath Roadway A
were installed
4 Excavation for the bridge abutment were carried out and
preparations were made to construct the northeastern wing
wall
5 Excavated and imported materials were stockpiled along the
bank of Weiakeakua Stream

EXISTING SUBSOIL CONDITION
Our scope of work does not include a subsurface exploration of the
complete project site Therefore drilling and sampling were only
performed at locations which in our opinion were more critical The
subsoil conditions at other parts of the site were assumed to be similar
to those described in the FGE reports
Five 5 borings were drilled and sampled to depths of 15 to 20
feet at the approximate locations shown on Site Plan Plate 2 Based on
the borings the area adjacent to the Waiakeakua Stream was underlain by
Zz to 4 feet of recent fill consisting of a mixture of coral sand silt
and gravels Underlying the fill lay the alluvial deposits consisting
mainly of sandy to clayey silt with weathered cobbles and boulders At
Borings 3 and 4 respectively 3 to 6 feet of soft slightly organic
silty clay were encountered at 4 feet from the ground surface The high
ground area is underlain by stiff residual soil down to the full depth of
the boring at 20 feet
Groundwater levels were monitored during our drilling operation and
they were indicated on individual boring logs In general they varied
from 8 to 11 5 feet below the ground surface At Boring 5 located on the
high ground groundwater was not encountered

INSTRUMENTATION
Special instrumentation was installed in the bore holes to provide
a means of accumulating long term data at the site The instrumentation
consisted of the following
General
Inclinometer casing were installed in Borings 2 and 3 to
depths of 18 5 and 15 feet respectively The purpose of the
inclinometer casing is to allow the lowering of a special
instrument into the hole to measure the lateral deflection of
the soil at various depths in order toe determine if there is
any lateral earth movements toward the stream directions The
inclinometer reading will be taken on a regular time interval
of approximately two weeks
Three settlement platforms were installed near
and 3 The purpose of the settlement platform is to monitor
vertical ground surface movements which may occur due to
consolidation of the underlying compressible layer The
settlement platform will be optically surveyed approximately
on a weekly basis
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Our soil and engineering investigation has concluded that the
recommendations propesed by FGE in general also reflect our opinions on

W Page 7
the design and construction of the project Additional recommendations
and the amendments to some previous recommendations are presented in this
report
Our borings indicated that the low lying area along Waiakeakua
Stream was underlain by 2 to 4 feet of fill material Therefore on the
average 12 inches of additional new fill material is required to raise
the area to its design elevation Underlying the fill two of our
borings also encountered 3 to 6 feet of soft compressible clay layer
Long term settlement due to the new fill and building units are
anticipated Therefore the new fill should be placed as soon as
possible and if practicable the area should be surcharged The effect
of the site filling and possible surcharging can be monitored by our
field instrument
Due to the presence of the soft layers along the banks of
Weiakeakua Stream Building Units 5 to 12 should be of post and beam
construction and supported by individual spread footings We would like
to point out that without surcharging of the building sites re leveling
of structural columns may be needed in the future Other special
considerations of the building foundations are discussed under the
section Building Foundation

All retaining structures for this project should be backfilled with
imported granular materials For pavement design of the access roadways
minimum thicknesses can be used
Site Grading
Site grading should continue as previously designed Areas with
insufficient thickness of fill should be brought up to grade as soon as
possible This will provide sufficient time for ground settlements to
occur while the remaining site works continues prior to house
construction The abovementioned applies especially to the area ad jacent
to the Waiakeakua Stream where an average of 12 inches of additional
compacted fill is required for the construction of building units
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as ea source of fill material All imported fill
material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or
granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve For the fill material behind the wing wall and the abut
ment of the new bridge we recommend the use of granular material such
as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand or equivalent
All fill and backfill materials should be placed in maximum of
10 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to about 90 of the
specified maximum density Fill placed on the slope with gradient
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W Page 9
greater than 5 1 H V should be keyed and benched into the firm ground
to properly bond the new fill to the slope All fill slopes should not
exceed a slope ratio of 2 1 H V
Large stockpiling of excavated material along the stream bank
should be avoided in order to prevent any slope instability However it
is our opinion that stockpile less than 8 feet in height and placed at
least 10 feet from the edge of the slope can be utilized to provide some
amount of preloading for the proposed building pads located along the
stream bank The maximum amount cf such surcharging from the stockpiles
can be worked out in the field with the help from the response of the
inclinometer

According to FGE Report dated these units will be
constructed on old fill material with thickness of approximately 7
to 8 feet The previous grading at this area was intended to

Based on the above information and our field reconnaissance it is
our opinion that an allowable bearing capacity of can be

Prior te the construction of the building units the ground should
be scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches The soil showld then
be moisture conditioned and recompacted to at least 90 of its

A minimum footing embedment of 12 inches is recommended Should
soft and expansive materials be encountered at the bottom of the
footing they should be removed and backfilled with compacted fill
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAIli
W Page 12
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying prior
to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of maximum dry density In order to protect against moisture damage of
the flooring material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be
incorporated within floor slabs When the slab is located below the
outside ground surface a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the
walls is recommended to relieve seepage water
New Bridge
At the time this report was prepared the bridge foundation had
already been constructed Our office had monitored the bridge footing
excavations and the spread foundations generally bear on the underlying
boulder layer per previous soils report reconmendations
In order to reduce the required compaction effort and the build
up of additional lateral pressures on the bridge wing walls all the
backfills behind the walls should consist of well graded granuler
materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand or
equivalent The granular backfill showld be placed in the area within or
line drawn at 45 degree angle from the heel of the abutment foundation
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used to minimize
build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls

Backfill underneath the ripraps placed in front of the bridge
abutment should be compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method

subgrade material the following pavement section is recommended for the
roadways
