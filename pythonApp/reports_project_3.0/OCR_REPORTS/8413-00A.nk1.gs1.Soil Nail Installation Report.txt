Dear Mr. Urasaki: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the pre-production sacrificial and production soil 
nails for the Interstate Route H-2 84-Inch Emergency Culvert Repair project located in 
the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity 
are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the soil 
nail installation and testing performed for the project from December 20, 2021 through 
December 24, 2021. Ke Nui Construction, LLC was the general contractor for the 
project, and Rousseau Services LLC was the specialty subcontractor responsible for 
installation and testing of the grouted soil nails. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located on the east side of the Interstate H-2 Freeway near the 
Ka Uka Boulevard off-ramp in the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We 
understand that on December 6, 2021, a large rainfall event occurred that caused the 
collapse of a section of the existing 84-Inch diameter corrugated metal pipe (CMP) 
culvert and headwall near the outlet on the east side of the Interstate H-2 Freeway. 
Geolabs conducted a site visit on December 8, 2021 to evaluate the extent of the 
damage.  
Based on our site visit and conversations with the design team and general 
contractor, a soil-nailed retaining wall system was chosen to stabilize the near-vertical

soil face created by the culvert and headwall collapse. Due to the time-sensitive nature

conformance with the project plans and specifications. 
SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
The soil-nailed retaining wall system consisted of 20 individual reinforcing bars 
grouted into drilled holes to stabilize the existing slope. One additional sacrificial soil nail 
was installed for pre-production verification testing. 
The soil nail installations, verification tests, and proof tests were performed by 
Rousseau Services LLC. The approximate locations of the grouted soil nails are shown on 
the Soil Nail Location Plan, Plate 2. Installation and testing of the nails were conducted 
from December 20, 2021 to December 24, 2021. A summary of the soil nail installations 
and testing are presented on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. 
Soil Nail Installation 
A Geolabs’ representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during 
construction of the soil nails. The nails were drilled using custom wagon-mounted drill rigs 
equipped with down-the-hole hammers and a 4.5-inch underreamer percussion bit. 
The nail holes were drilled to depths of 12 to 30 feet from the front face of the slope 
facing with an inclination about 15 degrees from horizontal. Eight nails at Grid Lines D, E, 
G and H were also drilled at horizontal batter angles of about 10 and 30 degrees to avoid 
the existing culvert and backfill material. After the hole was drilled to the specified depth, a 
threaded galvanized bar was inserted into the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along 
the threaded bar at 5-foot intervals. 
A flexible rubber grouting tube was inserted into the drilled hole along the threaded 
rebar. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top through the grouting tube until 
fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting tube was removed after 
completion of the grouting operation. The grout was designed with a minimum 28-day 
compressive strength of 4,000 psi. 
Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, the verification soil nail was loaded to 
200 percent of the design load (23.0 kips) and held for 60 minutes at 150 percent of the 
design load (34.5 kips). Based on the project specifications, ten percent of the production

nails (2 nails) were selected for proof testing. The production test nails had at least 3 feet 
of unbonded length for testing. Proof test nails were loaded to 150 percent of the design 
load (19.0 kips), and the load was maintained for 10 minutes to measure the creep 
movements. The criteria for acceptance of the soil nails consist of the following: 
For the verification tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.08 inches 
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60-minute readings is measured 
during creep testing, and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
For the proof tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.04 inches is 
measured between the 1 and 10-minute readings or a total creep 
movement of less than 0.08 inches is measured between the 6 and 
60-minute readings, and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent 
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length. 
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load. 
In accordance with the project specifications, a verification test was performed on 
the pre-production sacrificial Soil Nail No. T-1 on December 24, 2021. Soil Nail No. T-1 
was loaded to the maximum test load of approximately 46.0 kips (200 percent of the 
design load). The head movement for the soil nail at each load increment up to the 
maximum test load was measured and recorded. After testing the soil nail to 200 percent 
of the design load, the test nail was unloaded in four load decrements. The total measured 
head movement at 200 percent of the design load was about 0.449 inches. Results of the 
verification test, including Load versus Displacement and the Creep Movement versus 
Time curves, are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. 
In accordance with the project specifications, proof tests on selected production 
nails were performed on Soil Nail Nos. B-1 and H-2 on December 24, 2021. Maximum 
total head movement measured for Soil Nail Nos. B-1 and H-2 were 0.114 inches and 
0.166 inches, respectively. Results of the proof tests, including Load versus Displacement 
and the Creep Movement versus Time curves, are presented on Plates 5.1 through 5.6. 
Based on our observations, we believe that the soil nails observed by Geolabs 
have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and 
within the limits of generally accepted construction practices. Based on our observations of 
the soil nail tests performed by the contractor, it is our professional opinion that the soil 
nails have been installed to provide sufficient load capacity with tolerable movements.

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some of the work for the soil

we believe that the level of geotechnical observation provided is reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project.
