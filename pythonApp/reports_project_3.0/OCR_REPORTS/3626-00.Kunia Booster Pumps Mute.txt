INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed mute structure booster pump building located within the
Kunia Pump Station in the Waipahu area of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu

this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is proposed to construct a new mute structure to enclose
two existing booster pumps at the Kunia Pump Station in the Waipahu area on the island
of Oahu Hawaii in order to meet the Department of Health noise requirements Based
on the information provided the proposed mute structure is to consist of CMU walls with a
removable lightweight panel roof with easy access for maintenance and replacement
and with provisions made for future extension of an additional booster pump Expected
footing loads are on the order of about 1 200 pounds per lineal foot of wall We
understand that the proposed new structure will include possible mechanical and
electrical utility work
Based on the plans of the existing pump and piping network we understand that
the top slab elevation of the existing booster pumps is currently at 420 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL From the preliminary design proposed we anticipate that minor cuts on the
order of about 1 to 2 feet relative to the existing ground surface may be needed for the
wall footings Existing structures such as above ground and subsurface piping

Based on our experience in the area the project site may be underlain by very stiff

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3626 00 Page 5
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a
broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of
the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In
general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with
increasing depth

in Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The existing booster pumps site is bounded

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain by
stiff clayey fills over residual soils which grade to weathered basalt with increasing depth
In view of the generally stiff soil conditions encountered we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the stiff to very stiff near surface fills may be used to
support the proposed mute structure Our laboratory test results indicate that the near
surface fill soils have a slight potential for shrink swell when subjected to changes in the
moisture content Therefore special attention should be given to the subgrade
preparation and embedment depth requirements presented in the following sections of
this report to reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the fill materials on the proposed
footings
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundation site
preparation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
We understand that the proposed mute structure is to be a CMU structure with a
removable lightweight panel roof Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our
exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of continuous footings
may be used to support the proposed mute structure An allowable bearing pressure of
fills This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
We estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on
the order of less than inch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3626 00 Page 8
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the continuous footings and the bearing materials and by passive
earth pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
footings A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings
and the supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f
for footings embedded in the fills and or residual soils These values assume that the
concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished
grade should be neglected
Uplift loads for the roof of the structure may be resisted by a combination of the
following 1 dead weight of the structure 2 weight of backfill material above the
footing where applicable and 3 side shear between the sides of the footing and
surrounding soil Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above
the footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 105 p c f For side shear between the
sides of the footings and the adjacent soils a shear value of 150 p s f may be used to
resist uplift loads
The bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum of
80 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If shrinkage
cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly moistened to
close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete placement If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
should be backfilled with suitable on site fill material or select granular fill material

moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Where footings are to be located above subsurface utilities we recommend that
the maximum design loads for the utilities be assessed for the additional loading due to
the proposed mute structure and any additional loading due to construction traffic and
stockpiling of materials during construction If excavation of the utilities is required we
recommend that the pipe be jacketed for added protection from the additional structural
loads In general the backfill from the bottom of the pipes up to about 1 foot above the
pipes should consist of free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the
backfill The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of the on site soils or select
granular material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level foose lifts
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
mechanically compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential
for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below footings the upper 2 feet of the
trench backfill below the footing subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95
percent relative compaction
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope

We noted that the proposed mute footings are to be constructed above existing
subsurface utilities and adjacent to and on top of existing concrete slabs that support
above ground pipes and pumps Where footings are located adjacent to existing
concrete slabs any voids exposed during excavation should be filled with concrete grout
to prevent any undermining of the existing foundation
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii

bearing materials and embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We do not anticipate that new concrete slabs on grade will be used for the
proposed structures Based on the preliminary drawings we understand that the mute
structure is to be constructed on an existing slab However if a new slab on grade
structure is constructed we recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared and kept
moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete Since the near surface soils
encountered at the proposed site consist of clayey fill soils which exhibit a slight
expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations we recommend that the
subgrades for slabs on grades be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction
Site Preparation and Grading
During our field exploration we noted the proposed construction site to have a
ground cover consisting of 2 inch minus basalt gravels overlying a woven geotextile on
the existing ground surface At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3626 00 Page 11
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed and all unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of off site
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to
placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The on site clayey and silty soils generated from the excavation may be reused as
a source of fill and backfill materials The on site clayey and silty soils should be properly
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
If imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material should
consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 12 or more and
should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain between 10 and
30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for its intended use Imported materials should be brought to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey and
silty soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results are summarized on
Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 was performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
