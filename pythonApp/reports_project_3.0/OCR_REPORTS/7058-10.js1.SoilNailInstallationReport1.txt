This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the soil nails for the Kamehameha Schools – Puna 
Gate Repair project located in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the soil 
nail installation and testing performed on the soil nails for the project from 
June 23, 2015 through July 7, 2015.  Goto Construction Inc. was the general contractor 
for the project, and Structural Systems was the specialty subcontractor responsible for 
installation and testing of the soil nails. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Puna Gate Repair project is located on the southeastern side of 
Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Campus. The Puna gate serves as a secondary 
school access along the eastern bank of an existing gulch. An existing Concrete Rubble 
Masonry (CRM) retaining wall is located parallel to and supports the access road. A soil 
nail retaining wall was designed to strengthen the existing CRM retaining wall. 
Geolabs, Inc. conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration of the project 
site, and our recommendations are presented in the report entitled "Geotechnical 
Engineering Exploration, Kamehameha Schools – Puna Gate Repair, Honolulu, Oahu, 
Hawaii," dated March 6, 2015.  The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services 
during construction of the project was to observe the construction and testing of the soil 
nails for general conformance with the project plans and specifications.

SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
As part of the project requirements, a reinforced shotcrete soil nail retaining wall 
system was designed, consisting of five individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled 
holes to stabilize the existing CRM wall.  A structural shotcrete layer was specified to be 
placed on the face of the existing CRM wall, in addition to an architectural (sculpture) 
shotcrete layer for aesthetic appearance. 
A 3-inch thick fiber reinforced shotcrete facing was constructed in front of the 
existing CRM wall prior to the soil nail installation. This fiber reinforced shotcrete facing 
was constructed to provide a level working surface for drilling and testing of the soil nails. 
Production soil nail installation and testing was performed by Structural Systems. 
The approximate locations of the soil nails are shown on the Soil Nail Location Plan, 
Plate 2. Installation and testing of the soil nails progressed from June 23, 2015 through 
July 2, 2015. A summary of soil nail installation and testing are presented on Plate 3. 
Soil Nail Installation 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during 
construction of the soil nails. The soil nails were drilled using a TEI Platform Rock Drill 
mounted on a Komatsu PC 210 LC excavator. The TEI drill rig was equipped with a down 
the hole hammer and an 8-inch underreammer percussion bit. 
The soil nail holes were drilled to depths between 16.5 and 21.5 feet from the front 
face of the fiber reinforced shotcrete facing with an inclination between about 14.3 and 
15 degrees from perpendicular to the shotcrete facing. An 8-Inch OD temporary steel 
casing was installed to depths between about 2.5 and 13.8 feet behind the existing CRM 
wall to maintain stability of the drilled hole. After the hole was drilled to the specified 
depth, the soil nail tendon was inserted into the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed 
along the soil nail at 10-foot intervals. 
A 1½-inch diameter high density plastic grouting tube was inserted into the drilled 
hole along the soil nail tendon. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top 
through the grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting 
tube was removed after the completion of the grouting operation. Temporary casing was 
withdrawn during the grouting process.  
The volumes of grout placed in the drilled holes ranged between about 5.1 and 
6.8 cubic feet (ft3), approximately 1.0 to 1.3 times the theoretical volume of the drilled 
holes. Logs of the individual soil nails recording our observations and other pertinent 
information by our representative for each soil nail installed are provided in the Soil Nail 
Installation Records, Plates 4.1 thru 4.5.

It should be noted that due to a miscommunication, the contractor installed the 
wrong size soil nail tendon at Soil Nail No. 2. The bar installed was an epoxy coated #10 
bar (1.25-inch diameter) instead of the 1-inch diameter threaded galvanized bar with

project structural engineer, the installed bar was acceptable.  
Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, the soil nails shall be loaded to 
150 percent of the design load (24.0 kips) and held for 60 minutes for verification test and 
10 or 60 minutes depending on creep movement for proof test.  In addition, the 
verification testing soil nail shall be continuously loaded to the maximum testing load of 
200 percent of the design load. The criteria for acceptance of the soil nails consist of the 
following: 
For the verification tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.08 inch 
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60-minute readings is measured 
during creep testing and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
For the proof tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.04 inch is 
measured between the 1 and 10 minute readings or a total creep 
movement of less than 0.08 inch is measured between the 6 and 
60-minute readings and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent 
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length. 
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load. 
In accordance with the project specifications, a verification test was performed on 
the first installed soil nail (Soil Nail No. 2) on June 30, 2015. The soil nail was unable to 
maintain at 150% of the design load of 24.0 kips without excessive pullout displacement. 
Subsequently, the test nail was able to maintain at about 30.0 kips of the test load without 
additional movement. Results of the verification test including Load versus Displacement 
and the Creep Movement versus Time curves are presented on Plates 5.1 thru 5.3. 
The load demand of Soil Nail No. 2 was evaluated based on the as-built data 
obtained during installation. Based on our engineering analysis, the design load capacity 
of Soil Nail No. 2 was reduced from an allowable design load capacity of 24.0 kips to 
10.0 kips. 
A proof test was performed on Soil Nail No. 2 on July 7, 2015 to verify the reduced 
load capacity.  In general, the soil nail was loaded to the maximum test load of about 
15.0 kips, 150 percent of the reduced load capacity (10.0 kips). The head movements for 
the soil nail at each load increment up to the maximum test load were measured and

construction practices. Based on our observations of the soil nail tests performed by the 
contractor, it is our professional opinion that the soil nails have been installed to provide 
the sufficient load capacity with tolerable movements.
