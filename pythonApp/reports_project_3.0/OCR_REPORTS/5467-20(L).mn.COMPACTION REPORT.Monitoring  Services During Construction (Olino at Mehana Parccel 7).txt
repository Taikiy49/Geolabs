PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development Mehana is bordered by Roosevelt Avenue
to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east and James Campbell Estate to the north and
west Olino at Mehana Parcel 7 is located at the middle southwestern portion of
Mehana It is bordered by Road A on the north Road W on the east the Makai drainage
channel to the south and Road K to the west as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Olino encompasses an area of approximately 14 36 acres
Our monitoring services during construction consisted of in situ moisture and
density testing to determine adequacy of compaction of fills and backfills for infrastructure
and utility installations including compaction testing for building pads garages walkways
and driveways The Site Grading Plans for Olino at Mehana Parcel 7 are shown on Plates
2 1 through 2 5
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for
the pad grading utilities and infrastructure installation at the Olino at Mehana Parcel 7
project

MASS GRADING
Parcel 7 encompasses the southwestern portion of Mehana development at
Kapolei The mass grading consisted of clearing and grubbing subgrade preparation
cut and fill operation with cuts on the order of up to about 6 feet and fills of up to 7 feet
On site cut clayey silt and coralline materials were used as general fill to raise the lower
portions of the site to design grade
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density The top soil layer within the landscaping and
other open non buildable areas were compacted to a minimum of 85 of their
Maximum Dry Density
SITE GRADING
Samples of the materials utilized as fill were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The test results are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 50 These tests were performed on moisture conditioned and
compacted lifts of fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated using
on site survey stakes for reference These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas deemed
unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative compaction conformed to or
exceeded the minimum requirements for the project
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the
contractor s Paradigm Construction LLC subgrade preparation fill and backfill
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above site grading operations

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the house structures to collect and discharge water away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils We understand that gravel strips were placed adjacent to slabs We also
understand that the gutter downspouts were located to discharge beyond the gravel
strips and the sprinkler heads were oriented to spray away from the houses
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be backfilled tightly against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible
and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
