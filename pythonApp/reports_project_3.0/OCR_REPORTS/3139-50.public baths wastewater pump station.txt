As requested we have performed geotechnical engineering probing services to
evaluate the approximate location and depth of the concrete structure crossing
Kalakaua Avenue for the above referenced project Data regarding the condition of the
soils below the structure was also obtained for further evaluation for construction of the
project This letter report serves to summarize our work and present our findings for the
project
Project Considerations
The construction of a 16 inch diameter replacement force main along portions of
Kalakaua Avenue in Waikiki is currently in progress The Contractor is currently utilizing
microtunneling methods for the installation of the pipeline for the force main The design
invert elevation of the force main is currently at 8 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL or
approximately 16 feet from the existing ground
A concrete structure crosses Kalakaua Avenue between Ohua Street and
Paoakalani Street in the vicinity of the alignment Probe holes were previously drilled by
others in the area to identify the approximate limits of the structure however the current
alignment has been shifted slightly from the previous alignment We understand that it is
desired to confirm the location of the structure near the proposed force main alignment
determine its depth and obtain additional data of the soil conditions below the structure

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our probing services was to determine the location and depth of
the structure and to obtain information about the soil conditions below the structure with
respect to the proposed force main installation To accomplish this we performed the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house soil and boring data pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment to and
from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of six borings to depths ranging from about 13 to
23 feet below the existing ground surface Four additional probe holes
were also drilled in our efforts to determine the limits of the structure
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
Field Engineer from our firm
5 Preparation of this letter report to present our findings pertinent to the
construction of the proposed force main
6 Quality assurance and design team consultation by a Principal Engineer of
our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Existing Site Conditions
This portion of the project site is located on the mauka side of Kalakaua Avenue
between the intersections with Ohua Street and Paoakalani Street in Waikiki on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The force main alignment is generally parallel to Kalakaua Avenue at
this location and is located near the edge of the sidewalk top of curb fronting the
Hawaiian Regent Hotel Newsstands and planters are located along the sidewalk near
the project site
Our probing program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings extending to
depths ranging from about 13 to 23 feet below the existing ground Four probe holes

were also drilled to depths ranging from about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface during our efforts to locate the limits of the concrete structure Probe
Hole and 4 were terminated when the structure concrete was not encountered
and Probe Hole No 3 was terminated when the probe hole encountered a void once it
penetrated through the upper portion of the structure The approximate locations of the
borings and probe holes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The borings generally encountered concrete from the structure to depths of about
12 5 to 13 feet below the existing ground The structure is covered by approximately 6 to
18 inches of medium dense to dense brown sand with gravel covered by either
asphalt concrete or concrete Boring No 104 was terminated at the bottom of the
concrete Metal I shaped fragments were encountered in Boring Nos 102 and 103
Underlying the concrete was gray sandy coral gravel lagoonal deposits
extending to the maximum depths drilled of up to 23 feet in Boring 103
105 and 106 The sandy coral gravel was in a loose to medium dense condition below
the structure and graded to a looser condition with depth
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about 7 2 to
7 4 feet during the field exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels could
fluctuate depending on factors such as tidal fluctuations seasonal rainfall groundwater
withdrawal and or injection and other factors Detailed descriptions of the materials
encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings in Appendix A
A generalized subsurface profile is presented on Plate 2
DISCUSSION
Based on the locations of our borings and the subsurface conditions encountered
the plan location of the structure in the vicinity of the proposed force main alignment is not
as shown on the plans The structure is about 47 feet in width and located approximately
8 feet further toward Diamond Head than that currently shown on the plans The depth to
the bottom of the structure ranges about 12 5 to 13 feet from the ground surface or at
elevation 5 to 5 5 feet MSL The subsoil conditions range from medium dense directly
below the structure to loose with depth and should be evaluated with respect to the
Contractor s microtunneling procedures Due to the critical nature of these elevations we
recommend that the locations and elevations of the borings be professionally surveyed to
provide more accurate information to the Contractor
The discussions submitted in this letter report are based in part upon information
obtained from our field borings and probe holes Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings and the probe holes may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until excavation is underway The stratification lines shown on

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings and four probe holes designated as Boring Nos 101 through 106 and
Probe Hole Nos 1 through 4 at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 The depths of our borings ranged from about 13 to 23 feet below the existing
ground surface The probe holes extended to depths ranging from about 5 to 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer and geologist who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed
visually in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 10
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths
