Wastewater Improvements
Dear Mr Nomura
This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed in
support of the design of the Wailua River State Park Wastewater Improvements in
Wailua on the Island of Kauai Hawaii
Project Considerations
The project involves improvements to the existing wastewater system in the vicinity
of the Fern Grotto comfort station at the Wailua River State Park With the current
wastewater treatment system a septic tank discharges to the old cesspool and the
overflow drains to the adsorption trench called an overflow pit The pipe trench is about
five feet underground A second identical parallel trench is planned alongside the
existing trench as part of the proposed improvements
Site Description
The project site is located adjacent to and parallel to the existing adsorption trench
for the wastewater system of the Fern Grotto Comfort Station at the Wailua River State
Park in Wailua on the Island of Kauai Hawaii
Ground surface elevations in the area are generally level ranging from 11 to
12 feet above Mean Sea Level The general morphology of the area is classified as an
alluvial terrace or flood plain adjacent to the meandering Wailua River The area is
heavily vegetated with tall trees lush ginger and large vines
Percolation Testing
Three boreholes were drilled using hand augers ranging in depths of
approximately 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface The soils encountered
consisted of brown clayey silts with gravel of an alluvial stream deposited origin

Groundwater was encountered in Boring No 2 at a depth of approximately 5 2 feet
below the ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 3
Field percolation testing was performed in each borehole in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the
permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The test was
performed in a 3 5 inch diameter borehole with approximately 1 inch of gravel placed at
the bottom of the borehole to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and
sediments During testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to a depth of
about 6 inches above the gravel and refilled as necessary The time intervals and
water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the
percolation test was based
Based on the Manual of Septic Tank Practice prepared by the U S Department
of Health Education and Welfare and the proposed leach field location and the
percolation rate obtained at the site the site is suitable for the siting of the absorption
system The results of the percolation tests are shown on the attached Site
Evaluation Percolation Test Plates 1 through 4
