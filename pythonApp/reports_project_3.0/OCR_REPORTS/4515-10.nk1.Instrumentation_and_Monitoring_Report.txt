PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Castle Hill Access Road Drainage Improvements project consists of the
construction of a new drop structure downstream of the existing drainage outlet A
cofferdam consisting of sheetpile cut off walls was installed to create the working space
necessary to construct the micropile supported concrete drop structure Gabion
retaining walls along both sides of the existing stream banks at the downstream side of
the drainage outlet were also constructed
Due to past ground settlement and slope movement it was desired to establish
an instrumentation monitoring program to track potential ground movements prior to
during and after construction in order to differentiate between the baseline movements
and the movements induced by construction The instrumentation consisting of five
inclinometers and series of ground settlement monitoring points were installed around
the project site adjacent to the existing private road private properties City roadways
and State Streets Geolabs conducted the inclinometer installation and monitoring The
ground settlement monitoring points were surveyed and documented by others

A vibration monitoring program was also implemented during the construction of
the cofferdam to assess and document excessive ground movement due to the
installation of the sheetpiles using a vibratory hammer that may cause damage to
nearby structures Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc was the sheetpiling contractor
INCLINOMETER INSTALLATION AND MONITORING
Five inclinometers designated as I 1 through I 5 were installed at the selected
locations to monitor lateral ground movement prior to during and after construction as
part of the geotechnical instrumentation program Details of the inclinometer installation
were presented in the report entitled Inclinometer Installation and Monitoring Report
dated August 10 2012 The approximate inclinometer locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Slope inclinometers manufactured by Slope Indicator Company were used for
the lateral ground movement monitoring The inclinometer casings made of ABS semi
rigid plastic with an outside diameter of 2 75 inches were installed in the boreholes to
depths of 87 to 100 feet below the ground surface The inclinometer casing provides
access to the inclinometer device to measure the lateral ground movement at various
depths The collected data is then used to determine where earth movements are taking
place as well as the magnitude of the movement
In general the inclinometers recorded lateral earth movements perpendicular
A Axis and parallel B Axis to the adjacent streets Kupohu Street and Pilina Place
as shown on Plates 3 through 7 The inclinometers were read at varying time intervals
to measure the rate of ground lateral movement induced by the construction activities
The cumulative displacements measured by the inclinometers are presented on
Plates 3 through 7

VIBRATION CRITERIA
Ground motion thresholds due to vibrations caused by sheetpile construction are
not specifically established in the industry Numerous researchers have presented a
variety of proposed criteria Typically the criteria that we recommend are related to the
type of construction age and general condition of nearby structures These criteria are
also adopted from studies of actual damage thresholds observed for blasting operations
and other vibration inducing sources Based on Chae 1978 the following
recommended damage threshold criteria were used for the project

Based on available information and our field observations the structures
adjacent to the sheetpile operations appear to be residential structures and in fair

the nearest structure is about 100 feet from the closest sheetpile driving location
VIBRATION MONITORING INSTRUMENTATION
The vibration monitoring equipment consisting of Instantel Minimate Plus units
was used to monitor the ground vibration during the sheetpile driving operations The
units consist of a 3 component geophone and a signal conditioner recorder units The
geophone senses measured ground motions along three mutually perpendicular
orthogonal axes and therefore provided ground motion readings in vertical
transverse and longitudinal directions The signal conditioner recorder unit received
and processed these signals to provide a record of vibrations Resultant peak particle
velocities RPPV were automatically computed by vector summation of the motions at
all three axes
The geophones were placed at varying distances from the sheetpile driving or
removal locations to develop a relationship between the magnitude of the induced

VIBRATION MONITORING RESULTS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general vibration magnitudes decrease exponentially with increasing
horizontal distance from the vibration energy source Therefore the RPPV versus
horizontal distance were plotted on a log log scale as shown on the Sheetpile Vibration
Attenuation Plot Plate 8 The RPPV induced by the sheetpile driving operation were
measured at multiple locations during gabion walls and concrete drop structure
construction spanned between 2013 and 2016
The vibration magnitudes generated by the sheetpile driving operations ranged
from about 0 01 to 0 68 inches per second at a horizontal distance of about 270 to
20 feet from the vibration source respectively It should be noted that the maximum
RPPV measured at a horizontal distance of about 100 feet from the vibration source
equivalent to the horizontal distance to the closest residential houses was less than
0 5 inches per second Based on the results of the vibration monitoring the vibration
magnitudes generated during the sheetpile driving and removal operations were
substantially lower than the Class III threshold of 1 0 inch per second mentioned
previously Based on this we believe that the sheetpile driving and removal operations
had a minimal impact on the nearby residential structures
