Additional Slope Stability Analysis
Relocated Hangar and Aircraft Parking Apron
MCON P Point Philippines
In accordance with our proposal dated we have
performed additional slope stability analyses based on the revised
layout of the proposed maintenance hangar and apron
The original hangar and apron locations would require substantial
cuts into the existing hillside Our geotechnical investigation
disclosed that the proposed high cuts would have marginal factor of
safety against slope failure during a combination of extended periods
of heavy rain high water level and earthquake Therefore it was
recommended that the improvements be relocated to minimize the
required hillside cuts Additionally it was recommended that the
hangar be relocated towards the east where better soil conditions
were encountered

Page 2
A preliminary evaluation was made based on the revised layout
which was delivered to our office on The
preliminary evaluation disclosed that it would be advantageous to
raise the relocated road Argonaut Highway above the hangar and apron
level Following further discussion and some modifications in the
layout additional slope stability analyses were performed using the
layout shown on the attached Plate 1
Our additional slope stability analyses indicate that the revised
layout cut slope has a factor of safety greater than 1 15 when
earthquake and saturation conditions are considered This factor of
safety is considered the minimum acceptable by the Navy as indicated
in DM 7 1
The major considerations and parameters used in the slope
stability analysis are described below
1 Groundwater Conditions Our analysis indicates that groundwater
level is one of the most important parameters affecting the
stability of the slope As discussed in our previous report it
is believed that significant rise in groundwater level would occur
temporarily during heavy rains In an extreme condition the
water level could possibly rise to the ground surface
Parsons International
Page 3
Our analysis indicates that drainage of the top 20 feet of soil
below the finished grade surface is essential for slope
stability Therefore 20 foot deep trench drains as recommended
in our report should be incorporated in the design
In our drain design we have assumed that the groundwater level in
the entire area could rise to the ground surface during extreme
rainfall conditions This is a somewhat conservative assumption
A ridge extends through the eastern portion of the site generally
in a north south direction while a drainageway extends through
the western portion It seems likely that the groundwater level
in the drainageway area could rise to the surface during heavy
rains However in the ridge area this high water level
assumption seems less likely to occur Recent piezometer readings
August 18 1983 at the site following a period of moderate
rains seems to substantiate this In Borings 23 and 27 located
in the drainageway water levels were measured at 15 foot depths
which is 18 feet higher than those readings obtained initially
during the dry season Minimal rise in water level were measured
in Borings 8 and 20 located within the ridge area during the
Same period

Long term piezometer monitoring particularly following heavy
monsoon rains is recommended to determine the characteristics of
groundwater level fluctuations in the area If the piezometer
monitoring can substantiate the presence of lower groundwater
levels in the ridge area then the trench drains may be reduced in
this area
For the present analysis we have assumed that the water level
could rise to the surface even in this ridge area and both trench
and horizontal drains are required
Earthquake Forces Based on discussions with 0 1 C C Manila
recommended earthquake coefficient value of 0 25 was utilized in
our slope stability analysis
Cut Slope Ratio Various cut slope ratios were evaluated Our
slope analysis indicated that 2 1 H V slope ratios could only be
used for limited slope heights Two horizontal to 1 vertical
2 1 slope ratio was used for the lower slopes below the
relocated Argonaut Highway

Four horizontal to 1 vertical 4 1 slope ratio was also
evaluated However this resulted in only a small increase in
safety factor with considerable increase in the cut area and
corresponding grading costs
4 Soil Conditions The soil conditions were interpreted from our
previous borings In general there is adequate boring coverage
for the revised layout except near the intersection of Tarlac
Road and Argonaut Highway and the new hangar site Additional
borings should be drilled in these areas prior to final design in
order to confirm the assumed soil conditions
The soil strength parameters used for each of the major soil types
are the same as those presented in our previous soil report
Stability Analysis
Based on the above parameters slope stability analysis was
performed using the five generalized cross sections presented on
Plates 2 through 6
The minimum calculated factors of safety and critical slip circles
are also presented on the cross sections

It should be noted that the critical slip circles extend beyond
the top of cut In our stability analyses we have assumed that the
groundwater level would be 20 feet below the ground surface within
most of the failure zone Therefore some of the trench drains would
have to be extended some distance beyond the actual top of cut The
approximate limit of the upper level of drains is indicated on the
Site Plan
During the course of our analyses a special problem arises in the
drainageway area It was noted that the natural hillside even
without cutting has a low factor of safety considering the potential
of high water level at the surface simultaneously with the earthquake
forces Therefore although the proposed cuts in this area are
relatively small the trench drains would have to extend upslope a
considerable distance to an area near Tarlac Road in order to obtain
the required factor of safety Our stability analysis extended only
up to Tarlac Road since boring and survey data are not available
beyond that area
