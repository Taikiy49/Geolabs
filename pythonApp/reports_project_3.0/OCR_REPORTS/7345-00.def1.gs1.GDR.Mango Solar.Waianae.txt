REGIONAL GEOLOGY 
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from 
the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes.  The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to 
be middle to late Pliocene in age, and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early 
Pleistocene in age. The rocks of the Waianae Volcanic Series are divided into Lower, 
Middle and Upper Members.  The lavas of the Lower Member consist largely of thinly 
bedded pahoehoe flows whereas the Middle Member consists of interbedded pahoehoe 
and a`a flows with a higher occurrence of the a`a form.  The Upper Member consists 
primarily of massive a`a lava flows.  
Erosion deeply dissected the Waianae Shield removing much of the western 
slope of the dome resulting in broad amphitheater headed valleys. During the 
Pleistocene Epoch, sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of continental 
glaciation.  As the glaciers grew and advanced, less water was available to fill the 
oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea.  When the 
glaciers melted and receded, an excess of water became available such that the sea 
levels rose to above the present sea level. 
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at 
correspondingly higher elevations.  Subaerial exposure of the sediments and 
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of 
the calcareous reef materials. 
The project site is on the floor of Waianae Valley and is underlain by alluvial 
materials.  Chemical and mechanical weathering of the volcanic rocks of the nearby 
ridges has resulted in the deposition of an apron of colluvium and alluvium in the lower 
portions of the valley.  These deposits generally consist of remnant boulders and highly 
expansive clay.  In the lower areas where the topographic gradient decreases, the clay 
soils dominate but boulders and cobbles are still present within the soil matrix. 
FIELD EXPLORATION 
A total of six borings were drilled, designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 6, 
extending to depths of about 20.0 to 21.5 feet below the existing ground surface. The 
borings were drilled using truck-mounted drilling equipment equipped with auger 
equipment. The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and 
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488, Standard 
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils, and observed the drilling operations 
on a near-continuous basis. These classifications were further reviewed by visual 
observation and testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general conformance 
with ASTM D2487, Standard Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes 
(Unified Soil Classification System), as shown on the Soil Log Legend, Plate A-0.1 of 
Appendix A. Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with ASTM D2487

Relatively “undisturbed” soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in

accordance with ASTM D1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by 
driving a 2-inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop. 
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18-
inch drive are shown as the “Penetration Resistance” on the Logs of Borings at the 
appropriate sample depths. 
LABORATORY TESTING 
Laboratory testing was performed on selected soil samples obtained during the 
field exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their engineering 
properties. The tests performed are described below. 
Moisture Content (ASTM D2216) and Unit Weight (ASTM D2937) determinations 
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of 
soil properties. The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate 
sample depths. 
Three Atterberg Limits tests (ASTM D4318) were performed on selected soil 
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits. The test results are summarized on the 
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths. Graphic presentations of the test 
results are provided on Plate B-1 of Appendix B. 
One Sieve Analysis test (ASTM C117 and C136) was performed on a selected 
soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil 
classification. Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution of the soils are 
provided on Plate B-2 of Appendix B. 
Three Direct Shear tests (ASTM D3080) were performed on selected samples to 
evaluate the shear strength parameters of the soils encountered at the project site. 
Direct Shear test results are presented on Plates B-3 through B-5 of Appendix B. 
Four Corrosivity tests (pH - ASTM G51 and Minimum Resistivity – ASTM G57) 
were performed on selected samples to evaluate the corrosion potential of the soils 
encountered at the project site. The test results are presented on Plate B-6 of 
Appendix B.

LIMITATIONS 
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate, having 
been estimated by taping from the features shown on Topographic Survey Map dated 
March 23, 2016 by Sam O. Hirota, Inc. The elevations of the borings were estimated 
based on interpolation between the spot elevations shown on the same plan. The 
physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to 
the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict 
the approximate boundaries between soil types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition. Water level data presented in the text of this report were based on 
measurements recorded in the borings at the time of our exploration. However, it must 
be noted that groundwater levels may vary with seasonal rainfall, temperature, and 
other factors. 
This data report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Greenpath 
Technologies, Inc. for specific application to the proposed Ground Mount Solar 
Systems, Mango Solar System project in accordance with generally accepted 
geotechnical engineering principles and practices.  No warranty is expressed or implied. 
This report has been prepared solely to assist the designers in the design of the 
foundations for the proposed ground mount solar arrays for the project only. Therefore, 
this report may not contain sufficient data, or the proper information, to form the basis 
for construction cost estimates or contract bidding. A contractor wishing to bid on this 
project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of 
this report and/or performance of site-specific exploration for bid estimating purposes. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are 
commonly encountered.  Unforeseen subsurface conditions, such as soft deposits, hard 
layers, cavities, or perched groundwater, may occur in localized areas and may require 
additional probing or corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to 
attain a properly constructed project.  Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is 
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs. 
This geotechnical data report was not intended to investigate the potential 
presence of hazardous materials existing at the site. It should be noted that the 
equipment, techniques, and personnel used to conduct a geo-environmental exploration
