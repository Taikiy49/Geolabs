In accordance with the request of Princeville Development Corporation we have
drilled and installed a test well at the subject site and conducted percolation testing in the
well

project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Princeville area on the northern side of the island
of Kauai Hawaii The site is used for the clubhouse facilities of the Prince Golf Course
The site is in an unsewered area The development will utilize centralized on site
treatment to handle domestic sanitary wastes generated in the clubhouse facility It was
proposed to use injection wells as the primary method of disposal of the treated effluent
Design of the wastewater treatment plant is currently in progress The wastewater
treatment plant will be a package plant generally of the extended aeration type

The area was previously graded for the clubhouse facilities The majority of the
project earthwork was relatively shallow cuts and fills to grade the site

GENERAL INFORMATION
The project site is located in the Princeville area on the northern side of the island
of Kauai Hawaii The general topography of the site and the location of the wells on the
site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
There are no known active drinking water supply wells within a one quarter 1 4
mile of the site Drinking water in this portion of the island of Kauai is obtained from wells

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in the Princeville area in the northern portion of the island of
Kauai Hawaii The climate of the area is mild with an average annual temperature of
about 74 F and an average annual rainfall of about 78 inches per year
The site is situated on a plateau to the north of the central mountainous portion of
the island The topography of this plateau is a gentle slope down towards the north from
the mountains to the ocean
The site was formerly used for agricultural purposes and has lain fallow for several
years Recently the site was developed and graded for the construction of the golf
course clubhouse and related facilities The site is sloping and at the time of
constructing the injection wells was covered with landscaped grass
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 3 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas
of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the island of
Kauai These rocks are generally characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2797 00 Page 5
from groups of vents align in north south trends in various locales Associated with the
vents are pyroclastic material e g usually forming low cinder cones at the vent
During historic time the project site was used for agricultural purposes i e
cultivation of sugar cane or pineapple followed by use as a pasture for cattle This use
most likely resulted in some minor modification of the terrain such as filling and grading
of the site
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The ground surface elevation at the site at the time of well drilling and testing was
approximately 320 feet above Mean Sea Level
The log of the drilled well is presented in Appendix A Generally the wells
encountered what appears to be weathered cinder and basalt from the Koloa Volcanic
Series
Water levels in the well appeared to be relatively stable and were not noted to
fluctuate in response to the tides in the ocean approximately one 1 mile from the site
Some seasonal fluctuations may occur in response to periods of extended precipitation
It was noted that groundwater was encountered at a depth of about 31 feet below
the existing ground surface during our exploration This depth roughly corresponds to
an elevation of about 290 feet above Mean Sea Level This high water level appears to
be anomalous and seems to indicate that the groundwater is in a perched condition
Therefore it is unlikely that the shallow groundwater encountered below the site is in
direct communication with either the basal Waimea aquifer or the coastal waters to the
north

Instrument readings taken in the new wells indicate that the conductivity of the
groundwater ranges from about 92 to about 138 microSiemens per centimeter which
indicates that the water is relatively fresh
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Permeability Testing
During the drilling of the test well falling head percolation tests were performed
when the boring had reached depths of about 39 0 and 60 0 feet below the existing
ground surface These tests indicated that the upper residual soil and saprolite have
coefficients of permeability in the range of about 4x10 centimeters per second This
value is considered to be relatively low for disposal of sewage effluent through injection
wells or seepage pits
Upon completion of the boring four 4 inch diameter Schedule 40 well screen and
casing were installed in the boring The upper 12 feet of the well was cased and the
remaining interval was screened to allow water to flow from the well bore to the
surrounding formation A gravel pack was installed in the annular space between the well
screen and the formation Bentonite and grout seals were installed above the gravel pack
to the ground surface
After installation the well was developed by airlift pumping and surging until a
minimal amount of fines were noted in the discharge from the well Following
development the static groundwater level was measured at about 47 feet below the
existing ground surface A constant head injection test was then conducted in the well
for a period of approximately three 3 to four 4 hours Calculations of the data obtained
during this test indicate a hydraulic conductivity on the order of about 4x10 centimeters
per second
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2797 00 Page 7
These tests indicate that the test well is able to accept approximately ten 10
gallons per minute with a head build up of about 47 feet i e an injection rate of ten 10
gallons per minute fills the test well completely to the ground surface This is considered
a very low injection rate
The results of these tests are presented in Appendix B
Effluent Disposal
Based on data provided to us by the wastewater treatment plant designer Belt
Collins and Associates the capacity of the proposed treatment plant will be 20 000
gallons per day with a peak flow rate of 20 gallons per minute
However a low injection rate of about ten 10 gallons per minute completely filled
the well during our injection testing Our testing was conducted with clean potable water
Since it is not possible to treat sewage to provide completely clean effluent some
particulate matter will be introduced into the injection well during operation This along
with biological activity in the well will tend to clog the injection well and cause it to fail
It is Our opinion that it is not prudent to use injection wells as the sole disposal
method at the project site since failure of an injection wells could greatly impact the
operation of the wastewater system and thereby impact the operation of the clubhouse
facility
Discussions with Mr Leland Lee of Belt Collins and Associates indicate that it may
be possible to utilize a large leachfield or to utilize effluent irrigation as alternate methods
of disposal depending upon the cost factors Consideration could also be given to the
GEOLABS HAWAII

use of evapotranspiration beds for disposal of the treated effluent We recommend that
these alternates be explored for use as the primary disposal method at the site
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled wells and pump tests
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until operation of the injection well
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were determined by others We
can not assume responsibility for the accuracy of the location and or elevation of the well
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report This data has
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors
