PURPOSE AND SCOPE
We understand that the North Kohala Ranch Agricultural Development
will be comprised of 250 agricultural lots varying in size from 3 to 28
acres and covering an area of approximately 2100 acres Approximately 12
miles of paved access roads will be constructed to service the lot
owners The general location of the project and its vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of this soil engineering and geologic reconnaissance was
to explore the surface and subsurface conditions at the site in order to
provide data for use in the site grading design and construction of a
roadway system and related utilities It is our understanding that the
lots will be developed on an as is basis
This study consisted of a review of available soils and geologic
maps and literature concerning the area geologic reconnaissance test
pits 20 seismic refraction surveys laboratory testing and engineering
analysis
The results of our field reconnaissance and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recomendations are presented in the appendix of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the North Kohala district on the
Island of Hawaii and extends from the Akone Pule Highway approximately 4
miles north of Kawaihae in a northeasterly direction towards the
Waimea Kohala Road a distance of about 6 miles The approximate location
of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography is usually characterized by fairly smooth rolling
hills and in some cases poorly defined gullies and drainageways Local
relief is generally less than 60 feet
The site is an arid type pasture land that is sparsely covered with
dry grasses and with keawe trees in the lower areas
The upper one mile below Waimea Kohala Road has higher rainfall and
the pasture land is greener than the lower arid areas
Access through the area is by 4 wheel drive vehicles over
unimproved narrow roads
Drainage is down towards Akone Pule Highway ocean side The
overall relief between the two highways averages about 10 horizontal to 1
vertical Several gulches cross through the site generally trending from
the northeast towards the southwest
GEOLABS HAWAII
The ground elevations range from about 140 at Akone Pule Highway to
about 3180 feet at Waimea Kohala Road
There are two existing deep water wells located along the existing
access road near the middle of the site
An existing reservoir tank is located about 1000 feet north of the
end of Ala Kuhua Drive which crosses through the existing Kohala Estates
Subdivision
In general the ground surface is covered with basaltic gravel and
cobbles The ash soils underlying the surficial rocks are highly
desiccated which is consistent with the arid climate
Rock outcrops are mostly limited to exposures in the gullies and
cuts made for access roads
CLIMATIC CONDITIONS
The average annual rainfall at the project site vary from about 5 to
10 inches for the lower one third to about 10 to 20 inches for the middle
one third and about 20 to 50 inches for the upper one third It is our
understanding that strong easterly winds occur fairly frequently in this
area

PHYS IOGRAPHY
The study area is situated in the northwestern part of the island of
Hawaii The most prominent feature of the area is Kohala mountain which
is one of the five volcanoes that formed the island Water is abundant on
the mountain slopes but the leeward side around the South Kohala Coastal
Slope is semi arid The geology of the Study area is described in the
following section
GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of The Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the
activity of 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct
Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times Hualalai
which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The Kohala mountains which make up the northern part of the island
consist largely of the Pololu Volcanic Series that are mostly olivine
basalt with intercalated beds of ash After this series erupted the
mountains were deeply eroded on its windward side and a deep soil layer
covered its other slopes Later andesite and trachyte lava flows known
as the Hawi Volcanic Series were erupted
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1328 00 Page 7
The Late Activity of the Younger Pleistocene age Hawi Volcanics
pertaining to the rock formations at the site consists of fractured
vesicular andesite with thick beds of clinker that did not travel far from
their source The eruptions were moderately explosive and formed large
cinder cones that dot the surface of the volcano The flows developed
from Aa type lava flows are mostly maugearite with some thin beds of ash
or lenticular deposits of gravel laid down by streams Andesites in
Hawaii are not the same as the common andesites of continental areas which
are richer in silica and sodium Macdonald and Abbott 1970
A thin dry desiccated and erodible layer of volcanic ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash covers the surface of the flows

Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed that the site is covered with a thin
less than 3 foot thick surface soil layer of dry desciccated clayey
silt volcanic ash over clinker and lava rock formations
The subsurface conditions throughout the site appear to be
relatively uniform
The surface ash layer is very dry and friable with little cohesion
and is highly susceptible to wind and water erosion The ash layer is
generally 2 to 3 feet thick
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1328 00 Page 9
The lower portion of the ash deposit appears to grade into an
unconsolidated clinker deposit that may vary from a few feet to 15 feet or
more in thickness The clinker consists of cobble and gravel sized
andesitic fragments mixed with silty ash soils Occasionally boulders
are scattered on the clinker layers The clinker materials generally
appear to be weakly cemented
Below the clinker layer is the Aa lava flow which is composed of
dense fractured to massive andesite basalt It is anticipated that
individual flows may be up to 10 to 15 feet thick and underlain with other
clinker layers
No water was encountered in the test pits during the field
exploration

shallow depths by clinker and lava rock formations
The volcanic materials should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed roads and related utilities

to be disposed of off site An overall swell factor of 15 to 25 may be
considered for the various materials

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to construction of roadway grading surface vegetation and
organic material should be removed and wasted off site In areas to
receive fill or at grade construction tree roots should be removed
to a minimum depth of 3 feet and the resulting excavation backfilled
and compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
In fairly level areas prior to receiving fill the existing ground
should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moistened and compacted
to 90 maximum density as per ASTM D 1557 78
Due to the dry and windy site conditions the contractor should have
an adequate water supply for moisture conditioning the soils in the
grading area It is also advisable to institute a dust control
program during grading to minimize wind erosion and airborne dust
nuisance

In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches in
size may be used as general fill material If the excavated rock
material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded
material the surficial ash soil may be mixed into the excavated
rocky material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a
better graded material for compaction and increase the stability of
the fills
Because of the scarcity of soils in the area excess ash soils may
be stockpiled and saved for later use as topsoil for the development
of the lots
It may be necessary to stockpile the clinker material while the deep
cuts are being made so that the boulder sized material may be
disposed of in the deeper portions of the fill
Boulders encountered in excavation may be used in deep fills
provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able
to compact around them

W O 1328 00 Page 12
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be
Closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension should be
placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size of
larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later
excavation of the utility trenches
Fills should be placed in horizontal loose layers not to exceed 18
inches in thickness
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
test pits to determine the number of passes of a compactor required
to achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start
of the project The number of passes can then be used as the
criteria for compaction

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the site is underlain at fairly
shallow depths by dense andesite Aa basalt mantled with partly
consolidated clinker and a thin layer of ash soil
It is anticipated that the clinker may be excavated with normal
excavating methods Some hard digging may require the use of a hoe
ram and even some blasting if large accretionary lava balls
boulders are encountered Also much of the upper fractured rock
layers can be ripped with heavy equipment
The more massive andesite will likely require special excavating
procedures such as hoe ram or blasting to make the proposed cuts
or utility trenches

Laboratory Testing

classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of the

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing preparation
of fill areas placement and compaction of fill and removal and
backfilling of underground structures These specifications are of a
general nature and may be superseded by specific requirements or
recommendations within the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of trash
debris organic matter and all other deleterious Materials These
materials shall be removed and disposed off site
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose competent
natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be undercut and
backfilled with compacted select materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill
Standard Grading Specifications cont d 2
Ill
IV
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of
twelve 12 inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a Slope of 5 horizontal to j
vertical 5 1 the ground surface shall be keyed and benched into
competent soil to bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the soils
engineer where springs or zones of seepage are encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Imported material shall be tested and approved a minimum
of seven 7 working days prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks or lumps in
excess of twelve 12 inches in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers not to
exceed eighteen 18 inches in thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of material
and water content within each layer

Standard Grading Specifications cont d 3
B Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve 12 inches in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in any
one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material to fill
the voids between the rocks
C Boulders encountered during excavation may be used in deep
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed in such a way that the
compaction equipment will be able to compact around them
Z No part of the boulder shall encroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and
in no case be closer than 3 feet to finished grade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension
shall be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in
the proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in
the later excavation of the utility trenches

Standard Grading Specifications cont d 4
D Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
test pits to determine the number of passes of a compactor required
to achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start
of the project The number of passes can then be used as the
criteria for compaction
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tired rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to the
fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns septic tanks
wells pipe lines etc shall be demolished and removed as
directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the excavations
Shall be in accordance with these specifications
VI Other Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous fill
surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
8B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that
it will be free draining and will not pond surface water
