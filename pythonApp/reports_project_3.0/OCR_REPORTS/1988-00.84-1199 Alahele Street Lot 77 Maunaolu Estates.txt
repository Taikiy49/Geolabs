In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot to provide foundation
recommendations for the proposed residence The general location of the
lot is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our study consisted of excavating six shallow test pits laboratory
testing of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans Detailed
descriptions of our field exploration and logs of the test pits along
with the laboratory testing are presented in the appendix
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is situated on a talus
deposit on the lower slopes of Makaha Valley The subsurface material may
consist of dense gray gravel cobbles and boulders with a clayey silt
matrix between the rock particles The clayey silt is more predominant in
the upper few feet below the ground surface Laboratory soil tests
indicate that the clayey silt matrix material is low to moderately
expansive Generally conventional grading can be used at this site
Some special subgrade preparation are recommended in order to provide
suitable foundation support for the proposed house
Our review of the site grading plans and field exploration indicated
that a portion of the lot had been previously graded and the material on
the western portion of the proposed house and on the adjacent slope was
soft indicating poor compaction The owner was informed about this and
subsequently the loose fill under the proposed house was overexcavated
backfilled and recompacted under our observation However the above
Slope area still needs to be overexcavated and properly backfilled after
the retaining wall adjacent to the Slope has been constructed
Site Grading
Prior to additional filling the house site should be cleared of
vegetation and debris then scarified moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78

The on site material generally less than 6 inches in size can be
used for the fills In general boulders in excess of 6 inches should be
crushed to minus 6 inch size prior to being used in the fill
Imported materials for general fill should be of the similar quality
as the on site material or better Boulders or rock fragments larger than
6 inches should not be included
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum horizontal lifts compacted
to the above specified density Sufficient water should be incorporated
in the fill material to maintain the moisture content within 5 percentage
points wet of the optimm during compaction After the fill is placed
the subgrade soil should be kept moist to reduce swelling potential
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground as
shown on Plate 3
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for cut or
fill slopes
We recommend that an experienced soils technician be utilized to
observe the filling to verify that proper type of fill material is used
and that the fill is compacted to the above specified density
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed house
construction The footings should bear on firm natural ground or
compacted soil

Generally footings on level areas should be embedded a minimm of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The interior footings
protected by on grade slabs on all sides may be embedded a minimm of 12
inches below the adjacent finished grade
An allowable net soil bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used with a
one third increase allowed for wind or seismic loading
If footings are located on a sloping area the footing should be
embedded an additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimm horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the
Slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist
the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house pavilion lanai
garage and the swinming pool constructions It is recommended that a
minimum 12 inch thickened edge embedment be utilized to provide a
protective barrier against future water intrusion under the house and
covered lanai slab area
To reduce the swelling potential of the soil the subgrade soils
should be thoroughly saturated and kept moist from the start of
construction to the concrete placement
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer
of crushed aggregate base course and separated from wall foundations
A vapor barrier membrane may be considered over the base course layer
if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls up to 8 feet high are
planned along the eastern and the western boundaries of the lot to provide
grade separation The guidelines listed below for wall design should be
followed
1 For lateral earth pressure the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent

backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff soil
3 A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
does not apply where the ground slopes down in front of the wall
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can be used against sliding of the
wall
5 The retaining wall foundations on relatively flat areas less
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the outside finished grade
Wall foundations situated on slopes should be embedded an
additional amount to maintain a minimum 8 foot horizontal
set back distance measured from the outside slope face to the
edge of the wall footing

Sw gq Pool
For the planned swimming pool a lateral earth pressure of 55 p s f
equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth should be used for walls
restrained at the top at rest condition with level backfill
Typically the swelling potential of the on site soil increases with
decreasing moisture content therefore the swimming pool excavation
should be kept moist at all times prior to construction of the pool walls
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent excess water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations and on grade slab since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or
loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations
Concrete Driveway
The concrete driveway should have a minimm thickness of 4 inches
car traffic only with 8 inch thickened edges The subgrade soils should
be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and a discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and house foundation should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading benching filling footing
excavation and slab subgrade moistening operations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our evaluation The soils engineer s observation should also
include tests to record compaction of the fill
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from test pit excavations and data from
laboratory tests of soil samples Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in
this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling from
the topographic map provided Elevations of the data points were
determined by the topographic map The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
