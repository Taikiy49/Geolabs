Project Considerations
The project site is located on the eastern side of the Neal Blaisdell Center NBC in
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on Project Location Map Plate 1 The
existing three story concrete parking structure was designed and constructed in the late
1980s with provision to add two additional parking levels However the current
International Building Code IBC 2006 has increased the seismic demand on the structure
and now requires soil behavior input parameters Therefore it is desired to re evaluate the
subsurface conditions under the current building codes to assist the structural engineer for
a feasibility study of constructing additional floors to the parking structure
Based on the previous geotechnical engineering exploration conducted by others
the subsurface conditions consist of a layer of fill over a generally hard coral formation
underlain by alluvium at great depths Based on the information provided we understand
that the existing parking structure is supported by relatively short 16 5 inch precast
prestressed octagonal concrete piles bearing on the coral formation
In order to provide the necessary subsurface parameters for the seismic analysis
we conducted shear wave velocity profiling in an effort to more closely analyze the seismic
design considerations We conducted Seismic Cone Penetrometer Testing SCPT with

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
two borings to about 118 and 120 feet below the ground surface In general the project
site is underlain by existing fill materials and an upper coral ledge overlying coralline
detritus alluvium deposit and a lower coral ledge The upper coral ledge appeared slightly
fractured and hard from about 12 to 22 feet below the ground surface However the lower
coral ledge was encountered at depths from 45 to 98 feet with erratic consistency varying
from severely to slightly fractured and soft to hard The coralline detritus encountered
varied from loose to medium dense
We encountered groundwater at depths varying from 6 5 to 8 feet below the ground
surface in the borings at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that
groundwater level changes may occur due to variation in tides rainfall storm surge
condition temperature and other factors
After the borings were drilled to the planned depths they were backfilled with
0 25 inch coated bentonite pellets using the casing as a tremie pipe When the bentonite
pellets encountered water they swelled and softened as a coupling medium from the cone
penetrometer to the surrounding soil Shear wave velocity testing using the cone
penetration test method was performed in two boreholes designated as CPT 1 and
CPT 2 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The tests were performed to depths of about
118 to 120 feet below the existing ground surface using seismic cone testing equipment
SCPT
The seismic shear wave velocity test consists of hydraulically pushing a 10 ton
capacity steel electronic subtraction cone with an apex angle of 60 degrees and an area of
1 55 square inches 10 square centimeters connected to a friction sleeve with an area of
23 25 square inches 150 square centimeters into the ground The depth of penetration
was recorded at about 2 inch 5 centimeter intervals using a depth sensor attached to
the cone rod
The cone carries a uniaxial horizontal geophone to detect the arrival of a shear
wave propagated from the ground surface The seismic measurements are made when
the SCPT has stopped and a shear wave is sent into the subsurface A shear wave is
generated at the surface by striking a loaded plank with a switched hammer The
propagation time of the wave from the hammer blow to the cone was measured at each

Seismic Design Considerations
To analyze the subsurface soil parameters for seismic design considerations the
shear wave velocity profiling using seismic cone penetration testing SCPT equipment
was performed at discrete intervals extending to approximately 118 and 120 feet below the
existing ground surface in two drilled boreholes Based on the shear wave velocity
profiling the average shear wave velocity for the subsurface materials within the upper
118 and 120 feet of the profile is on the order of about 1 760 to 2 230 feet per second at
the test locations
The shear wave velocities measured and the weighted average shear wave
velocity of the soil profile at the location is presented on Plates 6 1 and 6 2 The weighted
average shear wave velocity was calculated based on the average shear wave velocity
method of the International Building Code IBC 2006
Based on the shear wave velocity test results obtained by using seismic cone
penetration testing SCPT equipment we believe the project site may be classified from a
seismic analysis standpoint as being a Very Dense Soil and Soft Rock site
corresponding to Site Class C based on IBC 2006 Table No Based on
Site Class C the following spectral response parameters may be used for the seismic
analysis

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the seismic cone penetration test Variations of subsurface soil
conditions and shear wave velocities beyond the test probe may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
provided in this letter report
The location of the field cone penetration tests indicated in this report is
approximate having been taped from features shown on the Site Plan transmitted by
Pacific Architects Inc on The physical location of the cone penetration test
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
