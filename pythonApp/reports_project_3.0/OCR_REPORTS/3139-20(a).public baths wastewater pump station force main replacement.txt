PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waikiki area of the District of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii It was originally proposed to construct a new sewer force main at
the project site utilizing conventional open trench construction methods Due to the
various construction constraints associated with open trench construction such as
subsurface utility conflicts relocations and surface disturbance to affected property
owners and traveled roadways we understand that it is currently desired to utilize
microtunneling as the construction method for the proposed force main replacement
project with the exception of an approximately 50 foot long section near the end of the
proposed alignment at Ohua Avenue which will be constructed by conventional open
trench method _

The proposed force main alignment begins at the intersection of Ohua and Kuhio
Avenues extends southerly along Ohua Avenue to the intersection of Ohua and
Kalakaua Avenues and then follows Kalakaua Avenue to the existing Public Baths
Wastewater Pump Station WWPS located on the makai side of Kalakaua Avenue An
existing 12 inch diameter force main currently extends along Kalakaua Avenue from the
intersection of Kapahulu and Kalakaua Avenues to the Public Baths WWPS
Based on the new design concept the 14 inch diameter force main originally
planned has been replaced by larger sewer pipes consisting of a 16 inch diameter force
main excluding the 50 foot section to be constructed by open trench method which will
consist of an 18 inch diameter gravity sewer Based on the existing topography and the
anticipated pipe invert elevations ground cover above the top of the sewer pipes may
range from about 8 to 12 feet in depth The approximate location of the proposed
alignment is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Dewatering will be required at the jacking and receiving pits and also when
launching the tunneling machine and advancing into the receiving pits Based on the
anticipated depths of the jacking receiving pits and the relatively high groundwater table
dewatering with a cut off wall and or tremie concrete plug is planned Since the
excavation dewatering may involve discharge of groundwater from the dewatering
operation into the adjacent drainage system a NPDES permit will be necessary In order
to obtain general baseline groundwater data for the preparation of the permit application
it is desired to sample and test the groundwater underlying the subject site
The groundwater samples were collected from two pre existing monitoring wells
which were installed in November 1993 by Geolabs Hawaii during an initial exploration for
this project Originally it was proposed to collect groundwater samples from three pre
existing monitoring wells however at the time of sample collection it was found that the
third monitoring well had been destroyed and covered by pavement Therefore it was no
longer accessible for sampling Only the two remaining monitoring wells could be
sampled and tested

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed Public Baths WWPS Force Main Replacement project is located in
the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The force main replacement
alignment begins at the intersection of Ohua and Kuhio Avenues runs along Ohua
Avenue to the intersection of Ohua and Kalakaua Avenues and then extends along
Kalakaua Avenue to the Public Baths WWPS on the makai side of Kalakaua Avenue as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Ohua Avenue consists of asphalt concrete paved roadways with commercial
development on both sides of the street Kalakaua Avenue is a major asphalt paved
roadway leading into the Waikiki area Heavy traffic is expected on this roadway High
rise hotels presently occupy the mauka side of Kalakaua Avenue and a public beach is
on the makai side
The site along Kalakaua Avenue toward the Public Baths WWPS past Kapahulu
Avenue exists generally as open space On the mauka side are Kapiolani Park and the
Honolulu Zoo grounds with the public beach extending to the Public Baths WWPS on the
makai side
In general Ohua Avenue gains elevation towards the southwest from an existing
elevation of about 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the intersection of Ohua and Kuhio
Avenues to an elevation of about 9 feet MSL at the intersection of Ohua and Kalakaua
Avenues Kalakaua Avenue is generally flat between Ohua and Kapahulu Avenues

towards the existing Public Baths WWPS The elevation at the intersection of Kalakaua
and Kapahulu Avenues is about 9 feet MSL and the elevation on Kalakaua Avenue
near the pump station is about 5 5 feet MSL

Monitoring Well Installation
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted in
November 1993 The environmental monitoring wells consist of 4 inch diameter PVC
pipes installed to depths of about 25 feet below ground level The wells consist of about
20 feet of perforated casing and about 5 feet of solid casing extending to just below the
ground surface The surface completion consists of flush mount traffic rated covers with
tamper resistant locking caps
A monitoring well detail which depicts the installation of the monitoring wells for the
project is provided on the attached Plate 3 The monitoring wells sampled are identified
as MW 5 and MW 7 The monitoring well which was destroyed and not sampled is
identified as MW 1 The approximate locations of the three monitoring wells are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Drilling of the monitoring wells was accomplished using a truck mounted drill rig
and associated drilling and sampling tools were washed by steam cleaning prior to the
drilling of the borehole In addition the sampling tools were washed with a Liquinox
detergent solution prior to each soil sampling event

Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were encountered during the drilling and
monitoring well installations Due to the close proximity of the shoreline it is likely that the
groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration will be affected by
tidal fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at the project site
may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
The analytical laboratory results indicate that no significant adverse water quality
conditions were detected in the analyses of the water samples that were tested The
water sample test results are provided in Appendix B and include the official laboratory
report and the completed Department of Health NPDES NOI Form A
The test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents appear to
be within normal values for typical brackish to saline groundwater In addition the test
results for the NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents including Benzene Toluene
Ethyl Benzene Total Xylenes and Lead Cadmium metals all revealed levels which
were less than the reporting limit
In general the test results indicate the reporting limit which refers to the detection
limit of the laboratory equipment The reporting limit is dependent on the testing
equipment and the statistical control applied by the laboratory to the test results
Therefore laboratories adopting the same standard test method may have different
reporting limits
Abandonment of Monitoring Wells
Abandonment of the monitoring wells is not included as part of our current scope
of work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the three monitoring wells
installed be included in the construction documents In general the monitoring wells
should be properly abandoned and sealed with cement grout
