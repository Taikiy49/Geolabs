INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Module D of the Halawa High
Security Facility on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed Module D will be a
two story structure with plan dimensions of approximately 125 by 150
feet A basement level is planned for the eastern half of the building
Site grading involving cuts and fills up to 5 and 11 feet
respectively is planned for the first floor and basement level
Retaining walls will be utilized for grade separation The anticipated
maximm column load is about 170 kips
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil parameters regarding site grading
and foundation design of the structure
The scope of our work included supervising field drilling of seven
7 boring to depths of 15 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface
engineering analysis of field and laboratory data and preparation of this
report Hawaii Test Borings Inc performed the drilling and sampling
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were used
in our engineering analysis are presented in the appendices of this
report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the Halawa Correctional Facility
between Quarry Access Road and South Halawa Stream on the island of
Oahu The proposed Module D will be constructed adjacent to the
existing Halawa High Security Facility

The topography of the site generally slopes down from Quarry Road
with elevations varying from 226 to 216 feet above Mean Sea Level The
eastern half of the site is covered with A C pavement for parking usage
while the western half is covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Seven 7 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15 to 30 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration generally encountered 2 to 3 feet of variable
fill material consisting of clayey silt clayey gravel and silty sand near
the surface over stiff grayish brown Adobe clay with boulders and
cobbles
Groundwater was encountered at 11 5 foot depth in Boring B 2 while
the remaining borings did not encounter groundwater It appears that the
observed groundwater level in Boring B 2 may reflect a localized seepage
condition
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
variable fill and grayish brown Adobe clay Our laboratory test
results indicate that the Adobe clay and the existing fill are highly to
moderately expansive when saturated from a dry condition Therefore
special procedures are recommended for site grading and foundation design
in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the expansive soils

Site Grading
Based on the plans provided to us fills varying from 1 to 11 feet
and cuts up to 5 feet are planned in site grading to prepare level areas
for the first floor and basement level The existing A C pavement within
the building limit will be retained according to the plan Retaining
walls will be utilized for grade separation
Ground Preparation

At the on set of earthwork the area within contract grading limits
should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas should be
recompacted if it is sandy or removed if it is clayey or organic

filling to avoid forming a water barrier

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
The fill should be placed in 12 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of ASIM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Fill on areas where the existing surface exceeds a Slope ratio of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the Slope for
details see Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of
the fills particularly to reduce the potential sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers
Where the proposed new fill is less than 3 feet in thickness we
recommend that the existing ground be over excavated before filling
so that a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of structural fill is
attained below the finished grade

Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure

To reduce the effects of the expansive soils we recommend that the
expansive clays be over excavated down to a minimum depth of 3 feet below
the bottom of the individual spread footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The backfill should be compacted in 6 inch lifts to a minimum of
90 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density For details refer to the
over excavation method shown on the Schematic Section on Plate 4
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below
the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic Section on
Plate 4
An allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f and a minimum footing
embedment of 12 inches may be used for foundation bearing on compacted
fill The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind seismic loading Estimated
settlements under the anticipated column load are on the order of one half
of an inch total and one quarter of an inch differential
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill must be well
compacted to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from all foundations This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
At basement level where cuts up to 5 feet are planned expansive
clays are anticipated below the slab In order to reduce the expansive
effects of the soil we recommend that a minimum 3 foot thick capping
layer of non expansive material be placed below the slab on grade
foundations

The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimm
depth of three 3 feet below finished grade The removal should also
extend about 3 feet beyond the slab perimeters Boulders encountered in
the excavation should be discarded The excavation should then be
replaced with non expansive off site borrow materials such as crusher
waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density The underlying remaining clay should be
moistened and maintained in a moist condition
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine cushion fill should be provided below the

non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimun of

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls with height varying from
3 to 10 feet are planned for grade separation Because of the anticipated
fills and cuts retained materials would consist of new compacted fill
existing fill and expansive Adobe clay The guidelines for retaining
wall design are listed below
1 In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive adobe clays
if encountered be over excavated down for a depth of 2 feet
below the bottom of the footing and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow
or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The over excavation and replacement
should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond the toe of
the footing if possible

To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive clay should be
over excavated at least 4 feet behind the wall or equal to the
height of the wall whichever is larger refer to Plate 6 for
details
The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum depth of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Therefore the
total depth of the above over excavation and replacement would be
3 feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade where adobe
clay is encountered refer to Plate 6 for details
The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 8 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the toe of the wall out horizontally to the
existing sloping ground surface

Existing Pavement
Based on the relatively good condition of the existing pavement it
is our opinion that a significant service life is still available from the
pavement
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the building and that gutter water be diverted far away from the structure
foundation and walkways Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1947 00 Page 10
Dk No 46
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
by taping from structures indicated on the topographic survey provided
by Control Point Surveying and Engineering Inc Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
May occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
