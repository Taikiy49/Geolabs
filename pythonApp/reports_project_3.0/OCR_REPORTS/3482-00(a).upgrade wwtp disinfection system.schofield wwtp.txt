INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Upgrade Wastewater Treatment Plant WWTP Disinfection
System at the existing Schofield WWTP in Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is within the existing Schofield WWTP located at Wheeler Air
Force Base in Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 We understand that it is proposed to upgrade the current disinfection
system Based on the information provided the proposed construction will consist of a
new chemical feed building adjacent to the existing chlorinator building at the WWTP In
addition new piping will be installed to accommodate the new disinfection system Based
on the preliminary site plan provided the proposed chemical feed building will have a
footprint of about 312 square feet The proposed building will be a one story structure
constructed of concrete masonry with an on grade concrete floor slab
Further details pertaining to the proposed construction were not available at the
time this report was prepared We understand that the site was previously graded and is
relatively flat In addition the proposed building will be constructed near the existing
ground elevation Therefore we anticipate that the site grading for the proposed project
will involve minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 foot relative to the existing
ground surface in order to attain the design finish grades for the proposed structure

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the
Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years forming saprolite In
general saprolite is composed mainly of silty materials and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring to a
depth of about 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of
the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In general our boring encountered a
surficial fill layer consisting of very stiff clayey silts and silty clays extending to a depth of
approximately 4 5 feet below the existing ground surface The near surface fills were
generally underlain by very stiff saprolites extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change
due to seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
For more detailed descriptions of the materials encountered please refer to the
Log of Boring presented on Plate A 1 of Appendix A Laboratory testing was performed
on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered very stiff clayey silts over very stiff silty
clays at the subject site Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered in
our boring we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the very stiff near surface soils may be used for support of
the proposed building at the site Laboratory test results indicate that the near surface
soils exhibit a slight to moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on at
least 12 inches of non expansive capping fill material such as select borrow subbase to
reduce the effects of shrink swell potential of the slightly to moderately expansive
near surface soils

Foundations
Based on the stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed
building at the project site An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per
square foot p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the stiff
near surface soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces In
general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grades
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility tench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the stiff near surface soils as recommended herein to be less than
one inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for foundations bearing on the stiff near surface soils
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid

pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the
soils around footings are well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
For footings bearing on the near surface soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with
select granular fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or
the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent material
Footing subgrades should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
chemical feed building The near surface soils encountered at the site exhibit a slight to
moderate expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content
Therefore in order to reduce the potential for structural distress to slabs on grade
resulting from swelling of the slightly to moderately expansive on site soils we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and a minimum of 12 inches of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3482 00 A Page 8
non expansive capping fill such as select borrow subbase material be provided below
concrete slabs on grade
The subgrade to receive the select borrow subbase should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density The slab subgrade should be kept moist until
covered by the select borrow subbase course
Considering that the chemical feed building slab may be subjected to equipment
vibrations we recommend that the slab be placed directly on the 12 inches of select
borrow subbase compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The
select borrow subbase material should consist of imported granular material such as
crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and no more
than 15 percent passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR
value of 25 or higher and a swell value of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
In addition the thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should
be embedded at least 8 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The area adjacent to
the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with non expansive impervious soil
and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundation

Site Grading
In general we anticipate that grading for the proposed construction will consist of
minor cuts and fills less than about 1 foot relative to the existing ground surface Site
grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is
important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operations to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification processes and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of
off site
Areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density The finish subgrade should be kept moist until covered by select borrow
subbase and or slabs If shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade
we recommend that the subgrade soil be moisture conditioned and recompacted as
recommended above to close all cracks
The on site silty and clayey soils generated from on site excavations may be
re used as fill and backfill materials up to the bottom of the select borrow subbase

material The on site soils should be properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in level loose lifts not exceeding 8 inches

In the event that imported fill materials are needed for the site grading the

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
It is critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around the sides of
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils The

backfill should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement surface should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new facility should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundation and
slab should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
foundations
