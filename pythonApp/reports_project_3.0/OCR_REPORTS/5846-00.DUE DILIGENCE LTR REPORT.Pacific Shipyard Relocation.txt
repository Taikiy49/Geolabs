analyses performed in support of the due diligence study for the Pacific Shipyard Relocation

subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the relocation of Pacific Shipyard s facilities which is currently
located in the vicinity of Pier 41 and Pier 42A The existing wharf frontage is barely
adequate and the total laydown area is insufficient to meet future needs The proposed
new site will be between Pier 24 and Pier 26 and between Pier 27 and Pier 28 Transit
sheds that once occupied these areas have been demolished
The new site would include new warehouses and heavy duty shops and light poles
Purpose and Scope

these objectives we conducted an exploration program generally consisting of the following

EXISTING PAVEMENT CONDITIONS
The project site is located between Pier 24 and Pier 26 and between Pier 27 and
Pier 28 on the makai side of Nimitz Highway in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on our field reconnaissance the project site is generally level and is presently covered
with asphalt concrete pavements and Portland cement concrete pavements
Based on our site reconnaissance the existing asphalt concrete pavements were
generally deteriorated Although it appeared that the asphalt concrete have been overlaid
several times the existing asphalt concrete pavements exhibited moderate to severe
structural distresses including raveling and weathering potholes alligator cracks and
localized depressions We believe that the asphalt concrete pavements should be
reconstructed to ensure good service for the new business operation
Majority of the existing Portland cement concrete PCC pavements were in relatively
good condition with minor cracks observed on the pavement surface at the time of our field
reconnaissance However deteriorated asphalt concrete patches over PCC pavement were
present in areas near Pier 28 We suspect that the underlying PCC pavement in these areas
may show structural distresses although the condition of the underlying PCC pavement
could not be observed at the time of our field reconnaissance
Light poles were also observed at the project site We believe that the existing light
poles are in good condition

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We researched and reviewed available soil and boring data in the project vicinity to
evaluate the subsurface conditions at the project site
In general the ground surface elevation at the site is approximately 6 to 8 feet
Mean Sea Level Based on the information obtained from previous borings drilled near the
project site we believe that the project site is underlain by fill materials of various
thicknesses overlying coralline sand and gravel Coral formation may be present at about
15 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater is anticipated at shallow depths below the existing ground surface
corresponding to elevations of 0 to 2 feet Mean Sea Level However groundwater levels
can vary significantly depending on tide seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or
injection and other factor
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance and review of the available information we believe
that from a geotechnical engineering point of view the major costs of the new development
will be generated from pavement repairs and foundations for new buildings and light poles
These items are further discussed in the following subsections of this letter report
Pavement Sections
Based on our field reconnaissance the existing pavements consist of asphalt
concrete pavement and Portland cement concrete pavement Based on a pavement repair
plan dated December 1985 the existing asphalt concrete pavement section consists of
3 inches of AC over 6 inches of AC treated base course According to the pavement repair
plan for Pier 26 dated August 1993 the existing PCC pavement sections consist of Portland
cement concrete with thicknesses ranging from 5 to 12 inches over base course Based on
the existing pavement conditions and anticipated future heavy traffic loads the existing
asphalt concrete pavement and PCC pavement need to be reconstructed in order to serve
the new business operation Estimate of the new pavement sections are represented below

passenger cars and pickup trucks We have made our preliminary pavement designs
assuming that the pavement subgrade soil will be dense silty sand as encountered in
the previous borings drilled in the project vicinity Based on the above assumptions
we recommend using the following pavement sections for cost estimate purposes

Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that the
proposed new structures may be supported on a shallow foundation system consisting of
spread and or continuous footings We believe that column loads for the new structures
supported on shallow foundations should be limited to about 300 to 400 kips at this time An
allowable bearing value of 3 000 pounds per square foot may be used for conceptual design
of the new warehouses
Based on the site plan provided to us it appears that the new structures are planned
on the existing asphalt concrete pavements We envision that the asphalt concrete on the
surface can be crushed and left in place The existing cohesionless fill materials anticipated
on the project site may be recompacted and used as structural fill under the new building
footings However loose fill materials may be encountered during footing excavation If
loose materials are encountered they should be over excavated and the over excavation
should be backfilled with structural fills
The potential need for over excavation under the structure foundations should be
further evaluated based on the actual subsurface conditions at the structure locations
Therefore we recommend evaluating the subsurface conditions by conducting a field
exploration program consisting of drilling and sampling borings during the design phase of
this project when the type and distribution of the proposed structures are better defined
Light Pole Foundations
Based on the information provided to us the new light poles will be about 80 feet high
The structural loads were not available at the time this letter report was prepared
Based on our previous experience we assume that the 80 foot high light poles will
have the following load demands acting at the base of the light pole
Vertical Load 5 kips
Lateral Load 5 kips
Overturning Moment 250 foot kips
In order to develop the required resistance to the lateral load and moment demands
the proposed light pole structures should be supported by a foundation system consisting of
vertical support principally from skin friction between the shafts and the surrounding soils
Lateral loads imposed on the light pole foundation may be resisted by resistance of the
shaft soil system to deflect
Based on the anticipated structural loads for the new light poles we recommend that
the light pole foundation consist of a cast in place concrete drilled shaft with a minimum
diameter of 60 inches Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsurface
conditions at the site we recommend embedding the drilled shaft foundation 2 feet into coral
