INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for Lots 19 and 20 of the Kahala Kua Subdivision in the Waialae Iki area of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations based on
our review and analyses of the site conditions the grading plan and the site reconnaissance
conducted for this project The geotechnical recommendations are intended for the design
of the site preparation and grading for the subject project only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Lots 19 and 20 of the Kahala Kua Subdivision are located on the eastern side of
Poola Street in the Waialae Iki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The proposed site grading involves the construction of
a driveway to provide common access to the two lots
The project site slopes upward from Poola Street The proposed site grading would
require cuts of up to about 5 feet deep and the placement of shallow fills of up to about 5 feet
thick Further details of the proposed site development were not available at the time this
report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located on a ridge which is an
erosional remnant of the Koolau shield The subsurface materials at the site consist of in situ
moderately weathered basalts with thin pockets of clayey surface soils The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is composed of basalts from the Koolau Volcanic Series
The surface of the site consists of exposed rock formation with a relatively thin
vegetative cover Majority of the runoff generated at the site moves as sheetflow to
Poola Street which forms the western boundary of the site The runoff is then intercepted
by the municipal storm sewer system

The project site is located on the eastern side of Poola Street in the Kahala Kua
residential subdivision in the Waialae Iki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is bounded by other residential lots to the north south and east and
Poola Street to the west The site and its immediate surroundings are shown on the

The project site is underlain primarily by competent basalt rock formation Based on
our site reconnaissance it appears that portions of the site have been partially graded
At the time of our reconnaissance the site was vacant without structures and or
developed infrastructure The surface of the site was thinly vegetated with haole koa kiawe
and dry grasses Prior to our site reconnaissance some grading work had been performed
which consisted primarily of constructing an access driveway into the project site The work
involved relatively shallow cuts of up to about 5 feet deep and the placement of thin fills of

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our reconnaissance program consisted of making visual observations of the exposed
slopes at the site These observations indicated that the subsurface conditions consist of
moderately weathered basalt rock formation with shallow pockets of clayey soils on the
surface Some thin fills had been placed on the site during previous grading work
Groundwater or seepage was not observed during the reconnaissance However
groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on seasonal precipitation groundwater
withdrawal and or injection and other factors Given the nature of the rock formation
encountered at the site there is a potential for encountering ephemeral groundwater or
seepage in clinker beds and along interflow lenses following periods of rainfall
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance the project site is generally underlain by basalt
rock formation These materials are competent and should provide adequate support for the
proposed site grading work if the recommendations presented herein are followed
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the grading planned at the site
the principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the site include site preparation and
grading consisting of shallow cuts of up to about 5 feet deep and placement of fills of up to
about 5 feet thick

Some previous grading work has been performed at the site This work resulted in
the placement of thin fills of up to about 5 feet in thickness These fills appeared to be
placed without proper subgrade preparation or adequate compaction
We recommend that clayey surface soils be removed and disposed of at an
appropriate offsite location The existing fills on the site should be removed and reworked
in accordance with the discussions and recommendations presented in this report Loose
boulders on the surface and or produced during site grading should be either removed from
the site or reduced to less than 6 inches in maximum dimension for later incorporation into
the fill embankment Further discussion of the site preparation is provided in a subsequent
section of this report Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during the site
preparation operations should be also over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill
material
We also recommend that the fill embankment consist of structural fill materials 6 inch
minus granular materials to provide a firm base for the proposed access driveway Detailed
discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report

Fills and Backfills
As previously mentioned we recommend that the fill consist of well graded granular
materials less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
Compaction of the fills should be performed in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements subsection of this
report
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill materials 6 inch minus fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill
materials is an integral part of the fill placement recommendations provided herein
Compaction of the fill should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
or other types of acceptable compaction equipment

Cut and Fill Slopes
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the structural fill materials recommended herein
may be designed with a slope inclination of one and one half horizontal to one vertical
or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and
benched The fill slope face should be finished to provide a relatively smooth and
well compacted face In addition slope planting or other means of slope protection
should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the
finished slopes
Based on the current grading scheme cut slopes are planned for the proposed
project Cut slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter A
representative from Geolabs Inc should observe cut slopes during construction to
evaluate whether the exposed cut slope materials are consistent with our
assumptions
Surface Drainage
The project site is located in a relatively humid area of Oahu Rainfall in this region
generally occurs on an intermittent basis with occasional large events on an infrequent basis
It is recommended that site grading should divert water from the faces of fill slopes to reduce
the potential of destabilizing the slopes
In addition the site is situated below relatively steep slopes on the western side of
the properties Runoff from these slopes could cause erosion of cut slopes and infiltration
of the runoff into new fill could result in piping and loss of stability of the fill subgrade It is
recommended that site grading include a provision for interception of runoff from the upper
slopes
