In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated May 2 2003 we have

Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes our work on the project and presents our
findings
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located approximately between Milepost 10 2 and Milepost 13 4 on
the Interstate Route H 1 highway an interstate highway with eight lanes in the District
of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The study of this project focuses on the eastbound lanes only of H 1 highway
between the Waiau Interchange and Kaimakani Street We understand that the traffic
lanes within this stretch of the roadway mainly consist of Jointed Portland Cement
Concrete pavement and the shoulder lanes were constructed with asphaltic concrete
pavement The project consists of repairing the damaged pavement areas and
restoring and strengthening the shoulders to provide an adequate section for traffic use
where necessary
In order to identify damaged and deficient pavement areas a pavement condition
survey was conducted along this stretch of the roadway However the pavement
condition survey was not conducted for the five areas with asphalt concrete pavement
overlying concrete pavement to accommodate the pavement settlements as the
recommendations for repair of these areas will be presented in a separate report

The pavement condition survey was basically conducted by visually inspecting
and identifying pavement distress type and severity to gather the necessary information

PAVEMENT CONDITION SURVEY METHODOLOGY
The pavement condition survey for this project was conducted in October 2003
The pavement surveyed included both Jointed Portland Cement Concrete pavement
and asphaltic concrete pavement shoulder lanes During the survey the inspector
visually observed the pavement conditions determined pavement distress types and
severities through visual classification and recorded approximate pavement distress
locations with the aid of the GPS surveying system A site plan with surveyed distress
locations was later provided by Control Point Surveying Inc after the pavement
condition survey was completed
The visual distress classification of Jointed Portland Cement Concrete and
asphaltic concrete pavements was performed in general accordance with the definitions
described in AASHTO Guide for Design of Pavement Structures 1993 published by
the American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials

Slab curling is a phenomenon where the edges and corners of the
concrete slab dish upwards or downwards without external forces and
loads except for gravity It occurs when concrete hardens at a
non uniform rate throughout the thickness of the slab due to different
moistures and temperatures present at the slab top and bottom
At the early stage of the curing the slab top generally loses more moisture
and is also subjected to more significant temperature change due to better
ventilation while the bottom retains moisture and remains at a relatively
constant temperature Therefore the slab top shrinks at a faster pace
than the bottom If this condition persists the slab edges and corners will
tend to curl upwards due to lack of constraints

concrete slab dish upwards or downwards without external forces and
loads except for gravity It occurs when concrete hardens at a
non uniform rate throughout the thickness of the slab due to different
moistures and temperatures present at the slab top and bottom
At the early stage of the curing the slab top generally loses more moisture
and is also subjected to more significant temperature change due to better
ventilation while the bottom retains moisture and remains at a relatively

tend to curl upwards due to lack of constraints

As a result of curling the slab edges and corners lose direct contact with
the subgrade materials and form a cantilever condition without support
from the subgrade When subjected to traffic loads the edges and
corners tend to break off from the majority of the slab and causes slab
distresses such as corner break and spalling at corners and edges which
comprise a major portion of the distresses observed at the project site
additio stretches of the
roadway as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 27 Based on
our field observation the joint faulting generally repeated a pattern that the
joint slab edge on the traffic approach side is higher than the edge on the
down side of the joint
In addition joint faulting was observed throughout several
We believe that joint faulting is generally caused by a combination of slab
curling and lack of effective load transfer system When subjected to
traffic loads the joint slab edges tend to settle significantly due to lack of
subgrade support Meanwhile in the case of lack of effective load transfer
system the loads exerted by wheels differ across the slab joint
Therefore the two edges across the joint settle in different rates and
magnitudes and forms joint faulting
Based on the results of the pavement condition survey cracks with
various severities were also present on the project site such as
longitudinal cracks transverse cracks and diagonal cracks The causes of
these cracks were not obvious due to the restrictions of visual inspection
e Distresses of AC Pavement
In general the AC pavement exists in the shoulder lanes We believe that
the majority of the distresses observed on the AC pavement during the
pavement condition survey conducted for this project were related to
heavy traffic loads
We understand that most of the AC pavement was designed as shoulder
lanes However these shoulder lanes were transformed into traffic lanes
to accommodate the increasing traffic demands and were subjected to
heavier traffic loads than intended Therefore as a result fatigue distress
potholes cracks and weathering and raveling were observed on these
areas
We also understand that it has been planned to reconstruct the
AC pavement If properly designed these distresses could be significantly
reduced
