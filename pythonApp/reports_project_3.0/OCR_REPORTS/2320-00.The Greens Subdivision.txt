INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed The Greens Subdivision in
Princeville on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction
estimation or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the project site as a residential
subdivision No site grading plans are currently available however we
anticipate that grading consisting of cuts and fills up to about ten
10 feet in depth or thickness will be required for the site
improvements It is however our anticipation that relatively deep
excavation on the order of 25 to 30 feet may be required if sewerage
pump stations are installed within the subdivision
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading
foundations and pavement design of the proposed residential subdivision
project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Princeville area on the
northern coast of the island of Kauai Hawaii The project site is
located to the east of the existing Princeville development area in the
Princeville Phase II Master Plan area to the north of Kuhio Highway
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Formerly the site was used for the cultivation of pineapple and
then as a pasture for the grazing of cattle and horses Currently the
site is being maintained as a well trinmed open field
Based on our field reconnaissance the available topographic map
and site plan it appears that the site slopes gently towards the north
and the existing ground elevation ranges from about 220 to 300 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau
along the north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built
during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the extrusion of basaltic
lavas and related pyroclastics from the Pooku vent of the Koloa Volcanic
Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which
retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
In former pasture areas the upper foot or so of soil has been
reworked as a result of the past agricultural activities and generally
has a high organic content
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
five 5 borings to depths of 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of our drilled borings encountered
a thin surface layer of brown clayey silt topsoil
Below the topsoil layer the borings generally encountered stiff to
very stiff reddish brown to brown clayey silts varying in thickness
between 4 0 and 9 0 feet These are residual soils that derived from
the in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally structureless
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2320 00 Page 5
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered generally
medium stiff to stiff saprolite soils consisting of clayey silts and
silts having predominantly brown color mottled with black spots or
streaks to the maximum drilled depth of the borings Occasional soft
pockets and or seams were encountered in the saprolite layers at depths
generally less than 15 feet below the ground surface
During our field exploration groundwater was measured in Boring
305 at a depth of 7 2 feet below the existing ground surface This
groundwater appears to be related to surface seepage which was noted at
the drilling site
However groundwater has been encountered in deep borings in other
areas of Princeville This groundwater is in a perched condition and is
generally the result of low permeability ash beds or lenses within the
Koloa Volcanic Series Therefore it may be possible that isolated
lenses of groundwater may be encountered in deep excavations within the
project site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils
within the project site However modified grading techniques are
required for the earthwork portion of the proposed site improvements due
to the relatively high in situ moisture content of the subsoils and the
relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that shallow spread footing foundations be used to
support the proposed residential structures
Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and detailed
grading plans are not available However cuts and fills of up to about
10 feet in depth or thickness are anticipated along the northern
boundary of the site

Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area most of
the subsoils within the top 15 feet had in situ moisture contents
of about 35 to 55 per cent at the time of our exploration These
moisture contents are considerably higher than the anticipated
optimum moisture contents which range from about 32 to 36 per cent
for similar soils in the Princeville area Therefore considerable
arying of the excavated materials would normally be required prior
to their use as fill materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally
occurs in the area throughout the year drying of large quantities
of soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading
operations To facilitate construction it is our opinion that the
normal 90 ASTM D 1557 78 compaction standard for general site
fills as used in drier areas may be reduced to 85 for this
project Our previous experience in Princeville and other similar
areas indicates that 85 compaction is generally adequate for site
fills under roadway and light building loads
Fills within 12 inches below roadway or footing subgrade should be
compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Topsoil should be
stripped and stockpiled for later use in landscaping Any
unsuitable materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other
deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site

The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimm depth of
6 inches below the cleared grade level air dried if necessary
and recompacted to the above recommended minimm of 85 of its
maximim dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The ground surface below pavements or footings should be scarified

firm ground and replaced with air dried select on site soil or
imported backfill compacted to a minimm of 85 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Backfill within 12 inches
below roadway or footing subgrade should be compacted to a minimm
of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is
our opinion that the on site soils have low expansion potential and
may be used as a source of fill and backfill materials If
additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling
to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed
construction The material should generally be non expansive and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or
deleterious matters
It is noted that most of the on site soils within the top five 5
feet have dry densities in the range of about 73 to 91 p c f When
they are compacted to higher densities corresponding volume

Subdrains
During our field exploration areas of surface seepage were noted
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Due
to the wet climate conditions and based upon previous experience of
encountering seepage in drainageways in the Princeville area we
recommend that subdrains be installed in the base of fills in these
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2320 00 Page 9
areas and in the bases of drainageways to relieve seepage which may
occur or accumulate along the interface between the existing
natural ground and the new fill See Plates 4 and 5 for details
Such accumulation of water could create a plane of weakness between
the fill and the existing subsoils potentially causing slippage or
creep of the fill The final location and quantity of subdrains
should be determined in the field by the project geotechnical
engineer
Foundations
Based on the soil conditions we believe that shallow spread and
continuous footing foundations bearing on recompacted in situ subsoils
may be used to support the proposed residential structures Foundation
subgrades should be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of up to 2 500 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic
loading
For buildings to be constructed on or adjacent to slopes the
building should be set back or the depth of the footing embedment
extended so that there is a minimm of ten 10 feet between the outer
edge of the footing and the slope face
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
W O 2320 00 Page 10
Based on the boring data estimated total footing settlements under
the design loads are anticipated to be on the order of less than one 1
inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the recompacted
in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth against the buried vertical faces of the footings may
be used to determine the passive soil pressure
Slabs On Grade
Slab on grade construction with 4 inch concrete slabs placed over a
4 inch layer of compacted base course may be used for the proposed
residential structures The thickened edges of the on grade slabs
should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent ground
surface For slabs located adjacent to slopes the building should be
set back or the depth of embedment extended so that there is a minimm
of ten 10 feet between the outer edge of the thickened edge and the
slope face
To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material
a moisture barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
Our laboratory test results and previous experience at Princeville
indicate that the on site soils in general have low swelling potential
at their natural moisture contents and exhibit a tendency to shrink when
allowed to dry Therefore the slab subgrade should be kept moist and
not allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement

Utilities
Underground utilities are anticipated for this development It is
anticipated that most trenching will be in medium stiff residual or
saprolite soils
On site soils are generally suitable for trench backfill and should
be compacted as recommended in the FILL PLACEMENT section Mechanical
compaction is recommended as the on site fine grained soils are not
conducive to water tamping or jetting
Retaining Walls
If sewerage pump stations or other buried structures are to be
installed as part of the site improvements the walls should be designed
as retaining walls under a restrained condition
An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f is recommended for the
design of wall foundations bearing on fill or natural soils compacted to
90 of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Granular materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand
or similar should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist a lateral earth
pressure of 55 p c f equivalent fluid pressure under restrained
condition
A subdrainage system and waterproofing should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls and to reduce the
potential of moisture migration through the walls to the interior of the

A friction factor of 0 4 between the concrete footing bottom and
soil may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure may be used
Parking Driveway Pavements
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavement will be used for
the project parking area and driveways
Based on the anticipated subgrade conditions the following
preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
12 Inch Select Borrow
20 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above pavement sections are assumed to be placed over the
subgrade of on site materials having a relative compaction of at least
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 within 12
inches below the subgrade
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils during construction and the above design sections may be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas Due to the high
rainfall in the area it is essential that the road subgrades and
shoulders be shaped to drain water away from the pavement sections
Weepholes should be provided in the parking area drainage inlet
structures in order to relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate
in the subgrade

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

The locations of the field data points were determined by measuring
from reference points shown on the plan entitled Princeville Phase II
Increment I Subdivision by Belt Collins Associates dated February
1989 Elevations of the data points were determined by interpolation
from contours and elevation points on the same plan The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
in water level may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which will not
exceed ten inches 10 in loose thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 85 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2320 00 Plate C 8
Appendix C Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
