In accordance with your request we have performed a preliminary
geologic reconnaissance of the near surface soil conditions at the upper
wells and reservoir site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the reconnaissance was to explore the near surface
soil conditions at the project sites in order to provide preliminary
recommendations for foundation design of the tanks reservoir retaining
walls and buildings pavement design and site grading
The scope of our work on the project consisted of reviewing available
soils geologic maps and literature concerning the area geologic field
reconnaissance shallow auger probing laboratory testing engineering
analysis and report preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A 200 000 gallon steel reservoir structure and chlorinator
building near elevation 750 feet MSL along Waianae Valley Road
2 Two 10 000 gallon water tanks and control buildings one at each
existing well site near elevations 1155 and 1340 feet MSL along
Waianae Valley Road Retaining walls are also planned for each
site
GEOLOGY
The project sites are located in the upper reach of Waianae Valley
which is an amphitheater headed valley eroded into Waianae Basalts The
valley was deeply cut and then partially filled with alluvial and
colluvial sediments derived from the steep cliffs of the Waianae Range
The sites lie on the slopes of a colluvial alluvial fan
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the three sites were explored by
hand augering with a 4 inch diameter auger to help probe the near surface
soil conditions and to obtain samples for testing

SITE DESCRIPTIONS
200 000 Gallon Tank Site
The reservoir site consists of a broad gently sloping area of land
located 50 to 100 feet north of Waianae Valley Road The ground
elevations vary from approximately 740 to 760 feet above Mean Sea Level
In general the topography slopes downward from east to west at about 7 1
H V The area is vegetated with small Keawe trees and tall grasses
About 20 of the site is covered by 3 to 4 foot size boulders Access to
the site is blocked by a 4 to 5 foot high rock wall and a barbed wire
fence
Well Site No 1
Well Site No 1 is located on a gently sloping area along Waianae
Valley Road between elevations 1152 and 1156 feet MSL In general the
topography slopes from the southeast down towards the northwest The
slope to the southeast of the proposed project site slopes down towards
Waianae Valley Road at about 5 1 H V The slope to the northwest of the
site slopes down from Waianae Valley Road at 3 1 H V Most of the
project site lies on a nearly level road cut area Aside from the gravel
road the area is vegetated with large trees coffee trees and tall
grasses Small 3 to 4 foot boulders cover about 20 of the sloping
sections of the area
Well Site No 2
Well Site No 2 consists of a gently sloping area along Waianae
Valley Road between elevations 1334 and 1342 feet In general the
topography slopes downward from east to west at about 3 1 to 4 1 H V
Most of the proposed building locations are situated on a nearly level
previously graded cut and fill area which is covered with gravel
Aside from this gravel covered area the surrounding area is heavily
vegetated with tall trees shrubs and grasses About 15 of the site is
covered with boulders A stream channel was noted about 100 feet south of
the site

SOIL CONDITIONS
200 000 Gallon Tank Site
From visual observations of soil rock exposures in the road cut and
from the hand auger probes it appears that most of this area is covered
with a fairly thick surface soil layer of alluvium or colluviun
The alluvium generally consists of dry to slightly moist brown clayey
silt to silty clay soils with abundant cobbles and boulders The soils
generally had a consistency in the range of medium stiff to stiff
Colluvium is slope wash or previous landslide debris which may be
locally intermixed with the older alluvium It generally consists of
unweathered to extremely weathered basalt cobbles and boulders in a medium
stiff to stiff clayey silt matrix
Well Site No 1
Most of this area is covered by a thin 3 inch thick layer of dense
gravel fill This is underlain by brown clayey silt with gravel and
cobbles of an alluvial colluvial origin The soils encountered were
slightly moist to moist and of a medium stiff to stiff consistency
Well Site No 2
From the auger probes drilled at the site and visual observations of
the cut slope to the east of this Site it appears that most of the area
to the east of the 1340 feet elevation line is underlain by natural
colluvial and alluvial soils These soils consist of stiff dark brown
silty clay colluvium of an average thickness of 3 feet underlain by stiff

Considerations and Conclusions
Based on the available data it appears that the majority of the
alluvial colluvial soils observed at the sites could provide adequate
support for the proposed structures except where expansive clay soils may
be encountered In the areas where these soils are encountered site
preparation particularly the stripping of surface expansive clayey soils
should be considered
The soil and rock materials excavated during earthwork should provide
adequate fill material for the embankments Expansive soils if
encountered should be placed a minimum of 3 feet below finished grade and
away from slope areas
Site Grading
The sites should be cleared of vegetation debris and soft
materials Prior to filling or footing excavation the existing ground
should be scarified a minimum of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Based on the field reconnaissance and laboratory test results most
on the on site soils are in general suitable for use as fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1999 00 Page 6
Dk No 51
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in non structural
fills provided that they are not allowed to nest together and the voids
between boulders are filled
If additional off site borrow is required the material should he
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to the
site The material should generally be of low expansion potential and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious
materials
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground to
properly bond the new fill to the slope for details see Plate 5 This
detail is essential for the stability of the fills particularly to reduce
the potential of sliding along the fill in situ ground contact The
filling operation should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level compacted layers
Proper drainage is essential for satisfactory performance of the cuts
and embankment fills as excessive water will weaken the clayey silt soils
Foundation
200 000 Gallon Reservoir
The reconnaissance disclosed that the proposed reservoir site is
underlain by stiff brown clayey silt with cobbles which could provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed reservoir The proposed
grading plan indicates that the tank will be entirely in cut
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on the stiff natural soil
is recommended for the anticipated tank loads

Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
stiff undisturbed soil or be structurally designed to span over the
compacted trench backfill to reduce footing settlement
A 6 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine is recommended
below the structure to provide drainage and uniform support
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of all footing excavations
and trench backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from
the reservoir foundation
Based on our preliminary reconnaissance and the anticipated tank
loads it is our opinion that settlements would generally be less
than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are proposed in conjunction with the site grading
structures and roadways These walls will support existing hillside
slopes
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill or
natural soil may be used for the design of the retaining wall foundations
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This would minimize the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls
The backfill material should be placed in 8 inch maximum lifts and
compacted to at least 90 of its maximum dry density as established by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls a
subsurface drainage system should be provided

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Detailed construction observations are of paramount importance in
verifying the retaining wall structure foundation and pavement design
assumptions during the site grading
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of

The locations and elevations of the field data points were determined
by scaling from the topographic map provided The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors
