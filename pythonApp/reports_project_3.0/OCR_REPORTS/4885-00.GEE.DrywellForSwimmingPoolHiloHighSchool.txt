PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a drywell for the disposal of the swimming pool water at
Hilo High School in the Hilo area on the Island of Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The proposed
drywell will be located near the existing dressing room and new P E locker shower
building as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Structural details for the proposed drywell
were not available at the time this report was prepared Based on the drawing provided
the existing ground elevation at the drywell location is at about 153 feet Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the drywell location were explored by drilling and
sampling one boring extending to a depth of approximately 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
One constant head permeability test was conducted in the boring drilled to
provide data for design of the proposed drywell planned at the site The permeability
test was performed at a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface to
evaluate the permeability characteristics of the basalt rock formation underlying the
project site Results of the permeability test are presented on Plate 5 of this report
The permeability test results indicated that the basalt rock formation encountered at
the site generally has a coefficient of permeability k of about 3 0 x 10 feet per minute or
about 1 5 x 10 centimeters per second This coefficient of permeability generally
corresponds to a water disposal capacity of about 0 05 cubic feet per second cfs or about
23 gallons per minute gpm for a 5 foot diameter 20 foot deep drywell It should be noted
that the percolation rate in basalt rock formation will depend significantly on the amount of
fractures and the presence of clinker layers Therefore the actual disposal capacity of the
drywell may vary significantly from the calculated value since the fracturing characteristics
of the basalt rock formation may not be well represented in a 4 inch diameter borehole
Considering the highly variable percolation rates of the subsurface conditions and the poor
performance of drywells in the vicinity of the project site we strongly recommend that
testing of the drywell be performed during construction to confirm the design disposal
capacity
Drywells on the Island of Hawaii are typically 5 feet in diameter and 20 feet in
depth The actual size and depth of the planned drywell will depend on the design
quantity requirement Extension of the drywell from the planned 20 foot depth may be
required depending on the specific site conditions at the actual drywell location
Therefore we recommend that unit prices for additional drilling be obtained during bidding
Provisions should be incorporated into the construction documents to extend the drywell
depth from the planned 20 feet to about 60 feet below the ground surface using a
minimum 15 inch diameter extension hole or 5 foot diameter drilling equipment The unit
prices for drywell extension should be obtained in 20 foot increments Due to the potential
for encountering clinker materials during drilling consideration should also be given to the
installation of concrete rings in the clinker layers to reduce the potential for collapse when

In general the type of drilling operation may alter the hydraulic characteristics of the
formation materials at the edges of the borehole and may result in significant reduction of
the hydraulic conductivity of the drilled hole We envision that the drilling fluid may seal the
permeable surfaces of the drywell Therefore pressure washing or water jetting to remove
the seal should be included in the specifications if the drywell is to be installed by drilling
The contractor should forward the proposed excavation method and equipment to be used
on the project to Geolabs for evaluation and comments Geolabs should review the
submittal and assess the impact of drywell construction procedures on the permeability
characteristics
The drywell installation and testing program should be conducted under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs This would allow our firm to monitor the
subsurface conditions to evaluate the disposal capacity and to allow for additional
recommendations to be made The drywell design recommendations presented herein
are contingent upon such observations and additional tests The owner client should be
aware that substantial variations in subsurface rock conditions may occur Therefore a
contingency fund is recommended to accommodate the potential extra costs associated
with these variations
LIMITATIONS

evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
