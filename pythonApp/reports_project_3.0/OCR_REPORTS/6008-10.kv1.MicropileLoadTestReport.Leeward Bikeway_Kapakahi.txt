MICROPILE LOAD TESTING REPORT 
MICROPILE TP-2, LEEWARD BIKEWAY 
PHILIPPINE SEA ROAD TO WAIPAHU DEPOT STREET 
WAIPAHU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Ms. Armstrong: 
To evaluate the micropile ultimate load capacity at the Kapakahi Stream Bridge, a 
compressive load test was conducted on a pre-production micropile for the Leeward 
Bikeway project. This letter report presents the results of our field observations, the test data 
collected during the load testing conducted on August 23, 2023, and our analyses and 
recommendations. 
The project site is generally located along the former Oahu Railroad and Land 
Company (OR&L) railroad, extending from the Waipio Point Access Road in Waipahu to 
Lualualei Naval Road in Nanakuli. Based on the contract documents provided, we 
understand that the project includes the construction of two single-span bridges (Kapakahi 
Stream and Waikele Stream Bridges). The two bridges are to be supported by 7.625-inch 
diameter micropiles with an ultimate compressive load capacity of 200 kips. The general 
location and project vicinity of the Kapakahi Stream Bridge are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1. 
A pre-production test micropile, designated as Micropile TP-2, was installed west of 
the Kapakahi Stream Bridge along the former OR&L railroad. The approximate location of 
the pre-production Micropile TP-2 is shown on the Micropile Location Plan, Plate 2. The 
pre-production micropile was installed by Keilikai, Inc. on August 9, 2023, utilizing a 
Casagrande C6 track-mounted drill rig.  In general, the pre-production Micropile TP-2 was 
installed extending to a depth of about 121.8 feet below the ground surface with about 78.7 
feet of 7.625-inch OD steel casing left in place (about 2.1 feet of the steel casing sticking up 
above the ground surface). A record of the pre-production micropile installation is provided 
in this letter report for reference, as shown on Plates 3.1 through 3.3. 
Based on our field observations, the pre-production Micropile TP-2 was installed by 
advancing the steel casing to a depth of about 121.8 feet below the ground surface. The 
pre-production micropile generally encountered very soft marsh deposits overlying stiff

silty/clayey alluvial soils and weathered basalt. Upon reaching the tip elevation, the cement 
grout was pumped via a tremie pipe from the bottom to the top of the casing, displacing the 
groundwater inside the steel casing. After the initial grout application, a 1.75-inch diameter 
Grade 150 all-threaded rebar with centralizers, spaced at every 10 feet, was installed to the 
full depth of the borehole. 
The steel casing was extracted from the bottom 45.1 feet of the pre-production 
micropile, providing the bond length within the stiff alluvial soils and weathered basalt, while 
the remaining steel casings (about  78.7 feet) were left in-place at the top of the micropile 
(unbonded zone) as permanent casings.  
The pre-production load test was conducted as a compressive load test in general 
accordance with ASTM D1143.  The ultimate compressive load capacity was specified as 
200 kips for the micropile. The maximum compressive test load of about 200 kips 
(100 percent of the ultimate compressive load capacity) was applied to the Micropile TP-2. 
A hydraulic jack system reacting against a reaction beam was used to apply the testing load 
to the load test micropile. In general, the reaction beam was supported on a cribbing system 
placed at two ends of the beam. 
A load cell was used to measure the testing loads applied to the micropile.  The 
micropile displacements were measured with two dial gages on the left and right sides of 
the load test micropiles, and a 1/100 division ruler was set onto the micropile rebar above 
the hydraulic jack.  Each dial gauge was attached to an independent reference beam with a 
1/100-inch division ruler to verify reference beam movements that could affect the dial gauge 
readings.  In addition, a 1/100-inch division ruler was set on a remote reference to verify 
potential ground displacement during the load testing that could affect the dial gauge 
readings. The surveyor’s level was used to observe the rulers and the remote reference for 
movement. 
In general, the pre-production Micropile TP-2 was loaded to the maximum test load 
of 200 kips in approximately 10-kip load increments (about 5 percent of the ultimate 
compressive load capacity).  Each load increment was held for at least 4 minutes or until a 
stable reading was observed. The maximum test load of 200 kips (the ultimate load capacity) 
was held for 240 minutes to evaluate the effects of creep. The gross rate of displacement at 
the 200-kip load increment was less than 0.02 inches per hour. Subsequently, the 
pre-production micropile was unloaded in approximately four equal decrements, each held 
for at least 4 minutes. The results of the pre-production Micropile TP-2 load testing for the 
Kapakahi Stream Bridge are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.4 and also summarized in 
the following table:

Total micropile head displacement at the ultimate compressive load (200 kips) was

Based on the above load test results, we believe the 7.625-inch OD pre-production
micropile installed at the Kapakahi Stream Bridge with a bond length of about 45 feet in the
stiff alluvial soils and weathered basalt is capable of supporting the ultimate load capacity of
200 kips without approaching compressive failure. Therefore, we recommend the design
production micropile bonded length of 45 feet to remain.
