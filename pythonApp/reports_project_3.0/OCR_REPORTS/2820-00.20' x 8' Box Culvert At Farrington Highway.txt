INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 20 x 8 box culvert across Farrington Highway in Kapolei on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration and site reconnaissance These recommendations are
intended for preparation of design drawings and may not be appropriate for the
contractor to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to provide a new concrete box culvert carrying runoff from Makakilo
Gulch under the Farrington Highway Based on the current design concept the new box
culvert will be located across Farrington Highway at about 150 feet southwest of the
intersection of Farrington Highway and Kealanani Avenue This box culvert will consist
of twin boxes each with a width of about 16 feet and a height of about 5 feet The
thickness of the concrete box culvert will vary between 14 to 16 inches Flows in the new
twin box culvert will pass underneath Farrington Highway and through a transition section
to a 20 by 8 box culvert which will be connected to an existing box culvert passing
under Kealanani Avenue flowing towards Kapolei Golf Course
Based on the preliminary Site Grading Erosion Plan the Farrington Highway right
of way is to be widened by approximately 40 feet and a new 160 foot long by 60 foot
wide by approximately 13 foot deep sedimentation basin will be constructed on the
upstream side of the widened highway An inlet structure will be constructed providing
a transition from the new basin to the new box culvert

SITE DESCRIPTIONS
The proposed project will involve the construction of a new concrete twin box
culvert within the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project location is
situated at about 150 feet southwest of the intersection of Farrington Highway and
Kealanani Avenue as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Presently there is a single box
culvert crossing Farrington Highway at approximately 350 feet southwest of the
intersection Based on the topographic map of the project site the existing ground
surface elevations range from approximately 98 to 105 feet Mean Sea Level at the
proposed new culvert location
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling 2 borings to depths of
40 and 41 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings encountered an approximately 8 foot thick surficial layer of
stiff to hard silty clays overlying about 7 feet of very hard clayey silts overlying alternating
layers of silty sand sandy and clayey silts and silts with sand or gravel to the maximum
depths explored of 40 and 41 feet for Boring Nos 1 and 2 respectively The sands
were generally medium dense to very dense and the silts and clays were generally stiff
to very hard
Groundwater was not encountered in either boring during our field exploration
However groundwater leveis generally are expected to fluctuate with rainfall and time of
year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The drilled borings were located at about 200 feet from the box culvert alignment
Based on our site reconnaissance and experience within the project vicinity it appears
that similar subsoil conditions will likely be encountered at the box culvert site On this
basis we are providing the following recommendations for the design of the box culvert
and inlet structure
Box Culvert
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered during the exploration and the site
reconnaissance it is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It appears that the underlying dense to very dense silty sands
and hard to very hard clayey silts encountered in our drilled borings at the proposed base
of structure elevation should provide adequate bearing support for the proposed culvert
structure at the project site
Box Culvert and Inlet Structure

dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loadings

Backfill Behind Box Culvert
Backfill material up to about one foot above the culvert should consist of select
well draining self compacting granular materials with sizes generally between
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The backfill should be lightly tamped to reduce the
possibility of damaging the culverts
The upper portion of the backfill from the level one foot above the culvert to the
finished grade should consist of non expansive material such as the excavated on
site sand the on site near surface silty clay is considered to be expansive and
should not be used as backfill material To reduce possible future ground
subsidence the fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Where the backfill material will support
pavement structures the upper 2 feet of the backfill below pavement subgrade
should be compacted to at least 95 percent of the maximum dry density
The top 1 foot of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material to
reduce infiltration of surface water behind the wall
Backfill Behind Inlet Structure
An inlet structure is anticipated at the inlet of the proposed box culvert at the
interface with the new spillway basin Well graded granular materials such as No
3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation select borrow material base

Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading for the proposed project will consist of backfilling
the existing ditch and excavation to construct the box culvert and drainage basin
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
material should be removed and disposed of off site
Areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to a least 2 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Fill material should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be
keyed and benched
The select granular fill material snould consist of imported granular material such
as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 00 Page 9
Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Any imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use by the
project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
Permanent cut slopes may be designed with a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical slope
inclination or flatter Cut slopes should be protected with ground cover to prevent erosion
as soon as practicable
Excavation
Based on the existing design concept excavation depths on the order of 20 feet
below the existing ground surface are anticipated Based on our borings excavation in
the subsoils utilizing conventional mechanical excavating equipment should be possible
The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
Excavation Support
The excavation support and shoring system used must comply with all applicable
safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who shouid be required to be
continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will have the
best opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during construction
such as unforseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table inappropriate
construction sequence or techniques etc which may affect shoring stability
GEOLABS HAWAII

However it must be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation Due to the compiexity of the stress changes it is difficult to predict
accurately the magnitude of movement The magnitude also depends greatiy upon
workmanship e g how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports are
installed and on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the rate of
excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed
properly and as early as practicable if necessary and that the adjacent ground
should be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
movements with instruments

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings and site reconnaissance Variations of conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this
report
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been estimated by taping
from visible features shown on the preliminary Site Grading Erosion Control Plan
prepared by your office and transmitted on The plan was not dated The
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in evaluating pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampied
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture content ASTM Test Method D 2216 90 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in the evaluation of soil properties The
moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the test sample and
dry unit weight are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs Plates
The Atterberg Limits test ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 is used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of this test are
shown at the appropriate sample depths on Boring Log A 1 1
Four 1 inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate their
swelling characteristics Results of the swell tests are summarized on Plate B 1
Three Direct Shear tests ASTM Test Method D 3080 72 are used to estimate the
angle of internal friction and cohesion in a soil sample Results of the three direct shear
tests are presented on Plates B 2 through B 4
