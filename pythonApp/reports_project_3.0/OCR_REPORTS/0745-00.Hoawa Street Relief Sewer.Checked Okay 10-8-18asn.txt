INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil investigation of
the proposed Hoawa Street Relief Sewer located in Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii The purpose of this study is to determine
the general subsurface soil conditions and to develop per
tinent soil data and engineering recommendations for sewer
design and construction cost estimate
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
HOAWA STREET RELIEF SEWER P 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Hoawa Street relief sewer begins at the inter
section of Date and Isenberg Streets and runs makai along
Isenberg Fern and Hoawa Streets before coming to Kapiolani
Boulevard The total length of the proposed sewer is approx
feet The sewer invert elevation begins at about
2 feet at the makai end on Kapiolani Boulevard and slopes
down to elevation 10 feet at the Date Street intersection
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the lower Moiliili district
mauka of the Marco Polo condominium as shown on the Area Map
Plate 1 The relief of the surrounding area is essentially
level and all the above mentioned roadways within the project
site have no noticeable grade Underground utility lines are
situated throughout the site servicing the residences along
the roadways
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is basically underlain in its upper depths
below the fill by a formation comprised of shallow quiet
water deposits usually developing along emerging coastlines
The deposits have various origins but the two most apparent
ones are clays and silts associated with stream deltas and
shallow water marine sediments Sand and coral layers
occurring at various depths indicate that the site has been
exposed to different stands of the sea
GEOLABS HAWAII
HOAWA STREET RELIEF SEWER P 3
The site s and surrounding areas most recent and natural
condition would best be described as a swampy area inland
from the coastline where streams end and deposit their
sediments
Subsurface Exploration
Subsurface conditions along the alignment were explored by
drilling 7 borings ranging from a depth of 16 0 to 31 5
feet below the existing ground at the locations shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 2
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration and the accom
panying laboratory test methods are given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Along the proposed sewer alignment the borings indicated that
the site area is blanketed by a surface fill layer which consists
mainly of a brown silty soil about 4 to 6 feet thick Below
this fill layer is a soft to very soft deposit of gray clayey
and silty marine sediments along with coral gravel and sand
The encountered thickness of this soft layer was about 9 to
20 feet Generally it was noted that the soft layer appears
to be shallower at the mauka end near Isenberg Street Boring
No 1 drilled at this end indicated that the soft layer below
the surface fill is only about 2 feet thick and consists of
gray organic silt

Beneath the soft marine clay layer a medium dense to very
dense silty sand and coral gravel formation was encountered

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Poor subsurface soil conditions were encountered along the
proposed sewer alignment except for the section near Boring
No 1 near the Date and Isenberg Streets intersection South
of Fern Street the soft marine deposits extended down about
5 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface

Based on the planned sewer invert elevations sheeting and
bracing are recommended to prevent cave in of the sewer
trench excavation
Throughout the entire project area the proposed sewer inverts
will extend below the groundwater level and therefore require
dewatering
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
trench the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill
material and the friction resistance between the backfill
material and the trench walls To compute the vertical
loads on the pipes a unit weight of 120 p c f and a co
efficient of friction of 0 25 is recommended Earth forces
upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the trench
Therefore the width of the trench Bd should be kept to
a minimum The enclosed Plate 3 indicates three alternate
trench types
Additional traffic load on the sewer conduit should be
considered if the sewer conduit is within the major traffic
roadway area
Bearing Capacities and Settlement
Except the short mauka section on Isenberg Street the
existing soils at the proposed invert elevation throughout
the remaining portion of the site have very low bearing
capacities due to their high moisture contents and low shear
GEOLABS HAWAII
HOAWA STREET RELIEF SEWER P 6
strengths The new sewer line construction in general will
not materially increase the vertical loading upon these under
lying soils if light weight backfill materials are used and
if no additional fills are placed upon the existing ground
surface The resulting net loading between the weight of
the excavated material and backfill will be essentially small
Based on the laboratory test results the estimated settle
ment in general will be Jess than 3 4 of an inch if the sewer
is properly constructed
Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe has
a great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe
Pipe bedding the contact between the foundation and the pipe
is therefore an important design consideration
Due to the soft conditions throughout the entire section
except in the area near the intersection of Date and Isenberg
Streets it is recommended that a two foot thick crushed rock
cradle be utilized for the proposed sewer line This material
should be well compacted and brought up to within six inches
of the invert grade or to the bottom of any concrete jacket
or cradle
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed upon the crushed rock cradle and the pipe
placed on a flat trench bottom The granular bedding should
extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth

pipe sides The remainder of the trench backfill should be
in accordance with the City and County Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction Section 11 3 The recommended
bedding method is illustrated on the enclosed Plate 4
In the Isenberg Street section the same pipe bedding should
be utilized but in this area where a firm trench bottom is
expected the two foot rock cradle can be reduced to a one
foot thickness

general practices are recommended
a Excavation should be made in short sections and then
backfilled as soon as possible
b Trench sheeting should be wedged tight against the
sides of the trench walls The ground adjacent to
and near the top of excavation should be continuously
monitored for cracks or dips in the ground as this
may give some warning where local sloughing might
occur Adjustments in work methods should be made
if soft spots are encountered in localized areas
c Dewatering should be done without moving soils
pumping clear water only
d If the contractor s work methods are such that dewatering
becomes impracticable in sections through medium to coarse
grained material it may be advisable to place the pipe
in a wet trench

e Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away

g The same type of crushed rock cradle for sewer pipe
should also be used under each sewer manhole in order
to provide a stabilized base in the low shear strength
soil strata
CONSTRUCTION CONTROL
Field inspection and engineering observation during the sewer
construction are important for this sewer project The proposed
Open trench sewer excavation which is along a high traffic
street adjacent to residential structures on spread footing
should be closely monitored by an engineer As mentioned
above the sewer should be constructed in short sections
and then backfilled as soon as possible
Rock cradles excavation and backfilling operations should
be inspected by a soils engineer
LIMITATIONS

conditions along the proposed alignment Variations in the sub
surface condition can be expected due to the changing geologic
environment during the deposition of the soils

Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7

penetration or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow

the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING

samples as an aid in the soil classification and in evaluating
soil properties Unit weights and Atterberg Limits were run

on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample locations

sample to determine the consolidation characteristics of the
organic stilt layer The consolidation curves are shown on
Plates B 1 and Be2

on site soil to evaluate the grain size characteristics of
the sandy and gravelly material The grain size distribution
curves are shown on Plate B 3
Three direct shear tests were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
soils encountered the test results are shown on Plates B 4
through B 6
