Soil Investigation Report
Elks Club Parking Lot Repairs
Waikiki Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
SA ERE SO
This report presents the results of our soil engineering investigation
for the Elks Club parking lot repairs at Waikiki Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on
Plate 1 Project Location Map
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the soil conditions at
the site to provide data to evaluate possible causes of the existing
asphaltic concrete pavement cracking and possible remedial measures
The scope of our investigation was performed in general accordance
with our proposal dated which included field
reconnaissance the drilling of six exploratory borings laboratory
testing of selected samples engineering analysis and report
preparation
W O 1218 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing which
were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions
and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the 2900 block of Kalakaua Avenue
at the present Elks Club Lodge in Waikiki Oahu Hawaii It is
bounded by Kalakaua Avenue to the north Outrigger Canoe Club Beach
to the south and two existing multi story structures to the east and
west
The existing parking area is relatively flat However existing
ground surface elevations were not available at the time of our
exploration
The parking area is roughly Square in shape and about 162 5 by 172 0
feet in plan dimension and contains three medial planting areas
parallel to Kalakaua Avenue
During our investigation some amount of heaving and cracking of the
existing asphalt pavement were noted adjacent to the two planters
closest to Kalakaua Avenue It appears that this was mainly due to
root encroachment from large trees located at the end of the planters

There also appears to be two utility trench excavations under the
pavement in the middle portion of the site and perpendicular to
Kalakaua Avenue as evidenced by linear pavement cracking along the
trench edge
The parking lot pavement is generally deteriorated as evidenced by
alligator cracking in many areas Some cracking and settlement were
visible along the main driveway areas
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to repair the existing parking lot by replacement or
resurfacing of the existing asphaltic concrete pavement It is also
anticipated that measures will be taken to minimize future cracking of
the repaired pavement
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
A total of six 6 exploratory borings were drilled through the
asphaltic concrete parking lot pavement to a depth of 5 5 feet below
the existing surface to investigate the subsurface conditions The
approximate boring locations are presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed description of our field exploration and the log of borings
together with a brief description of the laboratory testing and
summary of the test results are presented in the appendices of
this report

Soil Conditions
The six borings encountered 1 to 2 inches of asphaltic concrete
underlain by 4 to 5 inches of base course material consisting of
either coral or crushed basalt
In Borings 1 through 5 which were drilled around the perimeter of the
Site the base course was underlain by a layer of fill consisting of
very stiff brown clayey silt that varies between 1 and 1 5 feet in
thickness Below the fill the borings encountered medium dense beach
sand
In Boring 6 which was drilled approximately in the center of the
site the base course rested directly on the medium dense sand
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our field exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Much of the existing pavements have deteriorated and are in a rela
tively poor condition We believe that most of this deterioration is
due to the age of the pavement and normal wear from the volume of
traffic Alligator cracking is visible in many areas particularly
in the major driveway However in localized areas other conditions
have contributed to the pavement deterioration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1218 00 Page 5
Based on our investigation finding the following are some probable
causes of the observed pavement cracking
1 Normal deterioration of asphalt concrete surface The
deterioration of the pavement may have been accelerated
somewhat by the relatively thin pavement section originally
used 1 to 2 inch A C over 4 inch base course Based on
our laboratory CBR testing a pavement section 2 5 inch
A C over 6 inch base course would normally be recommended
2 Many of the observed cracks in the pavement appear to be
the result of root uplift Field observations revealed
that planting areas in and around the parking lot have
several monkey pod trees It should be noted that this
particular type of tree tends to develop large near surface
roots which can lift and crack paving materials
3 It appears that some of the cracks in the pavement are a
result of settlement due to poor utility trench backfill
Linear cracks were noted in the parking lot running from
Kalakaua Avenue toward the Elks Club building
Our field observations and testing indicate that the parking lot
is probably affected by several conditions Therefore remedial
work should be attempted to correct or minimize these conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1218 00 Page 6
For the general parking lot pavement repair two alternatives are
presented below
1 Overlay existing pavement with 2 5 inch of asphaltic
concrete This method would require reconstruction of the
driveways to match the new higher finished grade of the
parking lot pavement
The northern driveway pavement is highly deteriorated see
Plate 2 The asphaltic concrete in this area should be
removed the base course level recompacted and a new
thicker asphaltic concrete section be placed in this area
to match the finished grade
Remove and replace the existing pavement section If it is
desired to maintain the present finished grade the existing
pavement could be removed down to a depth of 8 5 inches
below the finished parking lot elevation and replaced with
a new pavement section
The existing base course material could be stockpiled for
reuse in the new pavement section The subgrade soils
should be moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The
subgrade should be shaped to drain water into suitable
outlets to prevent ponding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1218 00 Page 7
The recommended new pavement section is as follows
2 5 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 inch Base Course
8 5 inch Total Pavement Thickness
In addition to the above we believe that the following
localized repair work is also required with either of the above
alternatives
1
Where cracks are noted along the utility trenches the
existing asphaltic concrete surface course base course
and loose backfill should be removed and recompacted
prior to general pavement repair
Where cracking due to root encroachment is noted the
existing asphaltic concrete should be removed and the
underlying root grubbed out The resulting depression
should be backfilled with compacted base course and then
repaved
Included in the remedial work should be measures to
prevent future root damage The most straightforward of
the possible alternatives would be to remove the monkey
pod trees and to replace them with plantings that do
not develop large surface root systems A landscape
architect should be consulted for recommendations
regarding the best type of replacement plantings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1218 00 Page 8
Another alternative would be to construct buried cut off
walls around the existing trees to prevent the spread of
the root system However the construction of such a
cut off wall would require severe cutting back of the
existing root system which could have adverse effects upon
the life and or growth of the tree Additionally if the
tree survives it could conceivably develop enough root
pressure to break through the buried wall We recommend
that a landscape architect be consulted on this matter
5 Existing pot holes should be cleared of loose material and
backfilled with base course rock compacted to 90 of ASTM
D 1557 78
Design Review
Plans for parking lot repairs should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the parking lot repairs be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 6 borings to depths of 5 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted rotary hydraulic drilling equip
ment advancing continuous flight augers was used to drill
the borings
Two bulk samples of the subgrade soils were taken one
from the surface of the planting areas and one from auger
cuttings between depths of 2 0 to 5 5 feet
The material encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general con
formance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered is presented on the
Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soils samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples to aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
One Atterberg Limit test was performed as a further
aid in the classification of the on site soil The test
result is shown on Plate B 1
Two laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples
of the near surface materials obtained from the subgrade
elevation to evaluate their swelling potential and subgrade
Support capacity The test results are summarized on Plate B l
Two ASTM D 1557 tests modified proctor were performed
on bulk samples of the near surface existing material to
evaluate their compaction characteristics The test results
are summarized on Plate B l
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed to evaluate
the swelling potential of the existing soil The test result
is summarized on Plate B 2
