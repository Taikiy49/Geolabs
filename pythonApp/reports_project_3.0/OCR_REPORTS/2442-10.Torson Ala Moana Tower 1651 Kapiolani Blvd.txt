INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings and recommendations resulting from our
additional geotechnical engineering exploration performed for the design of the proposed
high rise development located at 1651 Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii This report should be used as an addendum to our previous geotechnical
engineering exploration report dated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our additional exploration was to verify the existence and
competency of the upper coral ledge which is anticipated to provide foundation supports
for short piles bearing on this ledge at depths of about 25 to 30 feet below the existing
ground surface An additional purpose of the additional borings is to provide better
delineation of the boundary between the buried former channel areas where long piles
will be needed and the upper coral layer where short piles could be utilized
The work was performed in accordance with our proposal of
The scope of work was limited to
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Drilling and sampling of five 5 additional borings B 101 to B 105 to
depths ranging from 46 to 192 feet below the existing ground surface

3 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
4 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
updating our pile foundation design recommendations and
5 Preparation of this report presenting the additional findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Five 5 additional borings B 101 to B 105 were drilled to depths ranging from 46
to 192 feet below the existing ground surface after demolition of the previously existing
buildings The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the additional borings encountered soil conditions similar to those
encountered in the previous borings which can be generalized as follows a 4 to 6 foot
thick crust of medium stiff clayey silt overlying lagoonal and alluvial deposits varying from
loose to very loose silty sand with coral gravel to medium dense moderately cemented
coral detritus to medium stiff clayey silt However the relatively competent coral ledge
material at depths of 25 to 45 feet in the easterly half of the site as noted in our previous
report of was discovered by the recent additional borings to be not
as areally consistent as previously assumed Instead a relatively competent coral ledge
of only about ten 10 feet thick was encountered in the recent borings at about 25 to 35
feet below the existing ground surface Moreover the white coral and tan cemented coral
sand and silt encountered at depths of about 130 to 155 feet in the previous borings were
not encountered in the recently drilled additional deep boring B 105 Medium dense coral

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Pile Foundations
Sixteen and one half inch inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles are
recommended to support the proposed structure
The recent additional borings did not encounter the anticipated competent coral
ledge at depths of 25 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface Instead a coral ledge
only about ten 10 feet thick with moderate and variable competency was encountered
at depths of about 25 to 35 feet
It is our understanding that approximate pile deformation will be about 2 inches for
the 150 foot long prestressed concrete piles and about 0 5 inch for the 30 foot long piles
These deformations do not include pile settlements due to elastic compression or
consolidation of the subsoils stressed by the pile foundations Our estimated pile
settlements will be on the order of 1 5 to 2 inches for a group of three hundred 300 150
foot long piles on the order of 0 5 to 1 inch for a group of twelve 12 150 foot long piles
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2442 10 Page 4
or for a group of four 4 30 foot long piles
In light of the additional subsoil information recently obtained and the anticipated
differential settlement between the long 150 foot and short 80 foot piles it is our
Opinion that the proposed tower structure should be supported entirely by long piles
about 130 to 150 feet in length The long piles will develop their vertical load support
capacity primarily from the pile shaft friction with some end bearing contribution
Therefore only a small portion of the pile foundations about 80 piles along and
adjacent to the eastern perimeter wall can be constructed utilizing short piles about 30
feet in length The approximate boundary between the long and short piles which may
be revised after test pile program is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
It is our understanding that due to the anticipated large differential settlement
between long and short piles it is proposed to construct majority of the tower structure
before construction of the perimeter wall and portions of the parking structure supported
by short piles With this construction sequence it is anticipated that a majority of the
settlement of the long piles supporting the tower structure will be completed prior to
construction of structural members supported by short piles Therefore differential
settlement between the long and short piles can be minimized
Predrilling
Predrilling to a depth of forty five 45 feet below the existing ground surface to
penetrate through the erratic upper coral ledge was recommended in our previous soils
report Based on the subsoil conditions encountered in the recent additional borings it
appears that the coral ledge grades to medium dense coral sand and gravel with poor
competency at depths of about 35 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface
Therefore it is our opinion that the predrilling depth can be reduced to thirty five 35 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2442 10 Page 5
below the existing ground surface provided that some occasional hard pile driving through
depths of 35 to 45 feet are tolerable We believe that the reduced predrilling depths will
have economical advantages over some hard pile driving The adequacy of the 35 foot
predrilling can be determined during the initial test pile program and the predrilling depth
may be revised if necessary
Ground Excavation
Due to proximity to the high groundwater level and the encountered soft subsoil
conditions excavation more than about two 2 to three 3 feet below the existing ground
surface i e below Elevation 3 feet MSL is not recommended If excavation more than
3 feet is performed prior to pile driving not only dewatering will be required immediately
but also a working platform consisting of No 3B Fine ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel may be
required to support the heavy pile driving and construction equipment This will prolong
the project dewatering period which is not generally desirable Besides construction
may be delayed due to possible equipment damage from flooding conditions should there
be any power shut down for the dewatering equipment Therefore ground excavation
below Elevation 3 feet MSL prior to pile installation does not appear to be advisable
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction as well as pile location plans
should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for his review and to determine the test
pile locations prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the
earthwork and foundation recommendations given in our reports If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the earthwork and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data collection points such as the drilled borings
Variations of conditions between the field data collection points may occur The nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway
lf variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The location of the field data collection points are approximate having been
determined by taping from reference points shown on the available topographic survey
map prepared by Control Point Surveying and Engineering Inc dated December 7
1989 Elevations of the field data collection points were approximately determined by

collection points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data collection
points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote
a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data collection points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may
occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
