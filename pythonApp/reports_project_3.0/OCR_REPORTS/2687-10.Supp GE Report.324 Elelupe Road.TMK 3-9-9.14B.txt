visit the upper one third of the project site on the Ewa side consisted of a fairly level

along the makai property line at the upper portion of the site

Subsurface Conditions
Our review of the geotechnical engineering exploration performed in 1991
indicated that four borings designated as Boring 11 and 13 were drilled within
the limits of the proposed residence site The boring depths ranged from about 8 3 to
26 5 feet below the ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the boring information the subsurface conditions at the site generally
consist of stiff to very stiff dark brown clay adobe overlying soft to medium hard coral
formation The thickness of the adobe clay layer varies from about 7 feet at the Koko
head side to about 20 feet at the ewa side of the project site
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the field
exploration However it should be noted that water levels may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation tides storm surge conditions and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in the boring are presented on the Logs of Borings in Appendix A
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our review of the previous geotechnical engineering exploration
performed at 324 Elelupe Road and the construction plans provided we believe that the
proposed concrete retaining walls may be used at the property lines for grade separation
The proposed enlargement of the existing CRM wall by building on behind the existing
wall is also feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view Detailed discussions
and geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of
this supplemental report Recommendations presented in our previous report of
should also be followed for other geotechnical aspects not covered in this
supplemental report
Concrete Retaining Walls
Based on the boring information the site is underlain by a relatively thick layer of
highly expansive adobe clay Water infiltration into the adobe subsoils may result in
significant loss of strength and or heaving due to saturation Therefore it is both critical
and imperative that the subgrade soils be prepared as recommended and that
adequate drainage be provided to divert surface runoff away from the site to reduce the
potential for ponding and moisture fluctuations The following guidelines may be used
for design of the concrete retaining walls

Existing CRM Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that the existing CRM wall along the eastern boundary line
had been constructed in general accordance with the recommendations presented in
Section 2 of Plate 3 of our report dated The details in Section 2 of Plate 3
indicate that the wall subgrade would have been over excavated laterally a minimum of
10 feet and vertically a minimum of 1 5 feet into the coral formation Aggregate base
course materials were used to backfill the over excavation The wall excavation also
extended behind the wall to a boundary plane of 1H 1V projected upwards from the
recommended 10 foot wide over excavation of the wall subgrade
It is proposed to build on the existing CRM wall to modify it into a larger wall with
finish ground elevations at about 23 feet MSL behind the wall Based on the above
understanding and the modified wall geometry a typical section through the wall was
developed for stability analysis The stability analysis was performed using the computer
program XSTABL originally developed by Purdue University at West Lafayette Indiana
In performing the analysis it was assumed that the adobe subsoil would be wetted or
saturated and appropriate long term strength parameters were selected for use The
computer analysis results are included as Appendix B of this letter report Based on the
results obtained it appears that the modified CRM wall should have factors of safety
greater than 1 5 against slope stability failure Normally a factor of safety of 1 5 is
desired for slope stability The recommendations presented in the Concrete Retaining
Wall section of this letter report should also be followed for the CRM wall design and
construction
in constructing the added CRM wali the new wail excavation should also be
performed to a 1H 1V imaginary boundary plane measuring upwards from the heel of the
new wall section and backfilled with granular materials compacted to between 90 and
95 percent relative compaction The granular backfill should consist of non expansive
select granular fill materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 30 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
It is very important that the previous over excavation and compacted granular
backfill for the existing CRM wall can be observed and confirmed during construction of
the modified CRM wall This is to observe compliance with our assumptions and to
expedite remedial recommendations in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated at the time this letter report was prepared

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed Lee Residence The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation
of subgrade preparation for footings and foundation excavations It is very important that
the previous over excavation and compacted granular backfill for the existing CRM wall
be observed and confirmed during construction of the modified CRM wall This is to
observe compliance with the analysis assumption design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this letter
report was prepared Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed
by a representative from Geolabs Hawaii The recommendations provided in this report
are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
