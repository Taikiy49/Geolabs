INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed new quarry in Ngeremlengui State on the Island of
Babeldaob Republic of Palau The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes the findings
resulting from our field exploration and laboratory testing The findings presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed quarry project site is located within Ngeremlengui State on the
island of Babeldaob in the Republic of Palau The site currently exists as undeveloped
land consisting of a steep prominent hill composed of volcanic andesite breccia rock
We understand that it is desired to develop the site as a new quarry for the production of
construction quality aggregate
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical evaluation was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the proposed quarry site to develop an idealized rock quality
data set in an effort to evaluate the rock material for possible development as an
aggregate source The work was performed in general accordance with our revised fee
proposal dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and work

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on undeveloped land in Ngeremlengui State on the
island of Babeldaob Republic of Palau The project site is located approximately 2 miles
southeast of the landmark and generally northeast of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4034 00 Page 3
Karamando Bay The site encompasses a portion of an elongated northwest to
southeast trending ridgeline The hilltop ground surface elevation is estimated from
regional maps to be on the order of approximately 460 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The hill consists of steep densely forested slopes with rock exposures visible on the
flanks and near the summit
The site is accessed by an east to west trending dirt road that intersects the
existing North South Babeldaob Road The hill summit in the vicinity of Boring No 2 was
observed as generally mildly sloping and accessible by vehicle under dry ground
conditions The side hill slopes appear to be very steep with scattered rock outcrops
Some hill slope areas appear to support a thin surficial soil cover
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean Basin are separated by a series of
elongated submarine ridge structures The Palau Islands are situated at the exposed
crest of the southwestern most submarine ridge of the area The Palau Ridge is part of
the Palau Kyushu Ridge which extends from Kyushu Japan south towards Palau
where a complementary trench structure forms to the southeast of Palau These ridge
and trench structures constitute the Palau Arc
The andesite line which marks the margin of the western Pacific Basin has
been defined along the eastern edge of the Palau trench This demarcation separates
the region of predominantly andesitic volcanism to the west from the Pacific Basin
region of basaltic volcanism The area is also a major structural and physiographic
boundary between the two regions
The structure of the Palau Islands reflects their situation upon the exposed crest
of the Palau Ridge The islands elongated form have resulted from volcanic emissions
and deposition of pyroclastic materials along the arcuate ridge structure Due to the
varied locations of the volcanic sources and the amount of emissions the successive
accumulation of pyroclastic material was highly irregular in occurrence In addition
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 4034 00 Page 4
most of the eruptions were subaqueous resulting in submarine landslides and slumps
contributing to the deformed and highly irregular character of the depositional surfaces
GEOLOGY OF BABELDAOB
Babeldaob Island exists as a result of the accumulation of volcanic materials
along the crest of the Palau Ridge The form of the island is principally controlled by
many factors including 1 fauiting associated with past tectonic activity 2 numerous
historic land submergence and emergence episodes as a result of sea level fluctuation
3 submarine erosion 4 erosion by surface stream flow and 5 deep tropical
weathering of volcanic materials comprising the island
In general Babeldaob consists of a central strip of the oldest volcanic rock
overlapped on the east by the next younger unit and separated by a fault zone on the
west from the youngest volcanic rocks The youngest rock formations are generally
arranged in a rough circle centered about a former caldera located in the vicinity of
Karamando Bay The regional ridge and trench structure extensive volcanism and the
presence of numerous faults cutting the pyroclastic deposits confirm past tectonic
activity However at the present time there is little evidence of on going deformation of
the island arc system
Based on the Geology Map of Babelthuap and Adjacent Islands Palau Islands
prepared by the U S Army and the U S Geological Survey the geologic maierials
mapped on Babeldaob include various volcanic alluvial and estuarine lagoonal or
beach deposits The volcanic materials comprise the oldest deposits and consist of the
Babeldaob Aimeliik and Ngeremlengui Formations These volcanic units comprise the
bulk of the land area on Babeldaob Island
Based on available geologic maps the project site is believed to be underlain by
the Ngeremlengui Formation and related intrusive rock bodies consisting of andesitic
and dacitic volcanic breccia together with some volcanic tuff breccia flows rock
conglomerates and derived sediments The Ngeremlengui Formation is divided into
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4034 00 Page 5
three members 1 the Nghemesed dacitic volcanic breccia 2 the Medorm
andesitic dacitic volcanic breccia and 3 the Arakabesan andesitic volcanic breccia
U S Army 1956 Volcanic breccia may be defined as a coarse grained volcanic rock
composed of various broken rock fragments called clasts that are welded together by
heat and pressure with a fine grained mineral rock matrix Breccia rock is typically
formed by volcanic explosions and or volcanic debris flows consisting of super heated
rock masses The Ngeremlengui Formation occurs as a broad strip of area along the
west central and northwest side of Babeldaob and along the northern tip of Arekalong
Peninsula
Other volcanic rock types include intrusive rock bodies such as geologic dikes
sills and volcanic plugs These rock bodies occur widely distributed in the volcanic
units described above The larger intrusive rock structures can be found in the area
around Karamando Bay associated with the most recent volcanic center of that area
U S Army 1956 Many of these intrusive features are less weathered than their
surrounding host rock material and are erosional remnants of eruptive centers
exhibited as prominent rock outcroppings
The hill comprising the project site has been mapped previously by others as
representing volcanic intrusive rock Evidence suggesting an intrusive origin includes
the generally massive non bedded and weathering resistant character of the rock
mass Furthermore the rock mass contains a porphyritic mineral texture essentially
consisting of coarse mineral grains set in a matrix of very fine grained mineral ground
mass
The volcanic rocks of Babeldaob have been extensively faulted during and
following their deposition Faulting is believed to have been a major factor in determining
the distribution of volcanic formations as many geologic contacts occur along fault lines
U S Army 1956 Post volcanic faulting has modified the distribution of the geologic
formations and is responsible for many physiographic features Movement along the
faults appeared to be similar to earlier tectonic movement however the most recent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4034 00 Page 6
movement is believed to be in response to new tectonic forces which have since
developed U S Army 1956 Based on the geologic map of Babeldaob the larger fault
zones trend to the northeast and northwest across the island forming concave arcs
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our subsurface exploration at the proposed quarry site consisted of core drilling
and sampling three borings to depths of approximately 50 and 100 feet below the existing
ground surface Boring Nos 1 and 3 were drilled to a depth of about 50 feet below the
existing ground level Boring No 2 located highest on the hill slope was drilled to a
depth of about 100 feet below the existing ground level Based on field measurements
utilizing a hand level and stadia rod Boring No 1 was drilled at an elevation of about
104 feet below the summit elevation at the site Boring No 2 was drilled at an elevation
of about 9 feet below the summit elevation and Boring No 3 was drilled at about 96 feet
below the summit elevation The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring exploration encountered a thin cover of soil ranging in depth from about
0 5 to 4 feet in thickness overlying volcanic rock formation The rock formation
encountered extends to the maximum depth drilled in each of the three exploratory
borings Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration however it should be noted that the groundwater level at the project site may
be influenced by seasonal precipitation and subterranean seepage conditions
In general the borings encountered a light greenish gray andesitic breccia
intrusive formation possibly representing the erosional remnant of a volcanic reservoir or
conduit structure The rock mass appears to have experienced varying degrees of
secondary hydrothermal alteration created by volcanic heat and fluids moving through or
around the rock during its evolutionary history
The rock is composed of a mixture of coarse and fine grained minerals with
inclusions of gravel to cobble sized clasts floating in a mineral matrix Hence the rock is
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 4034 00 Page 7
believed to represent a volcanic breccia with a geologically intrusive origin
The secondary alteration or weathering of the rock has created a transformation of some
of the rock mineralogy and may contribute to the reduction in rock soundness properties
and ability to withstand abrasion

DISCUSSION OF FINDINGS
Based on the rock recovery and Rock Quality Designation RQD values obtained
from our borings the volcanic rock appears to be generally massive to slightly fractured
Some zones of moderate to close fracturing were encountered primarily at deeper depths
of the borings Many of the fractures visible in the rock core were closed fractures
containing some in filling of secondary minerals The laboratory tests performed on
selected samples of the rock core included eight specific gravity tests two soundness
tests and one Los Angeles LA abrasion test The tests were performed in general
accordance with the American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM standards as
indicated The following is a brief discussion of the test results

ASTM Test Method C 131
Based on the ASTM standard specification for coarse aggregate for concrete
ASTM C 33 Specification the maximum allowable percentage loss soundness test
when subjected to 5 cycles of magnesium sulfate solution is 18 percent The maximum
allowable loss when subjected to the LA abrasion test is 50 percent for concrete
Based on the ASTM standard specification for bituminous paving mixtures for
coarse aggregate ASTM D 692 Specification the weighted loss shall not be greater
than 18 percent for the 5 cycle magnesium sulfate soundness test In addition when
subjected to the LA abrasion test the loss shall not exceed 40 percent for surface
courses and 50 percent for base courses
In summary the specific gravity of the rock ranges between 2 362 and 2 538 and
falls within generally acceptable ranges for aggregate materials However the
soundness tests indicate a range of rock quality spanning from 13 7 percent loss to
27 1 percent loss for the two samples tested The 27 1 percent loss may be considered
high for certain applications The LA abrasion test result appears relatively high but
generally falls within an acceptable range for aggregate use Visual observation of the
rock cores indicate that the rock appears to have experienced significant secondary
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4034 00 Page 10
mineral alteration that may account for the reduced soundness and abrasion parameters
over what may have been expected from the generally solid appearance of the rock
It is difficult to evaluate a trend in rock quality versus depth based on the limited
testing of rock core completed to date In order to obtain sufficient quantity of rock
material to perform the soundness and abrasion tests in accordance with the ASTM
standards composite samples of the rock core were utilized Furthermore because rock
core was used approximately 5 feet of rock core was required to meet the quantities
necessary for each test Therefore rock quality changes over depth may not be clear
due to the averaging effect created by the nature of the composite sample Based on our
analyses of the rock soundness test results in relation to boring location depth it appears
that the rock soundness may decrease with increasing depth
It should be noted that the slightly higher percent loss from one of the soundness
tests indicate that the rock quality at this site may be considered to be somewhat variable
since the other soundness test indicated an acceptable value of percent loss from the
soundness test In order to develop the explored site as a proposed quarry additional
quality control tests will need to be conducted to determine the areas where rock of
variable quality exists within the proposed quarry development The variable quality rock
may exist in localized areas of the proposed quarry development however this will not be
evident until the rock is exposed and further tests are conducted

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by core drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of approximately 50 and 100 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt If 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
