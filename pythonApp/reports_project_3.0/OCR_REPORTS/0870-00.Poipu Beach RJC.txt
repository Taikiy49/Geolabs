SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the mauka side of Hoone Road
between Kuai Road and Pane Road in the Poipu Beach area of
Kauai Many trees and heavy vegetation covered the site
at the time of our field exploration
The eastern two thirds of the site is fairly flat with
elevations ranging from about 6 to 8 feet There is an
existing one story wood framed dwelling and remnants of
a concrete slab in the central portion of the site where
Buildings A and B are located Existing cesspools
were noted in this area

conditions The test pits were dug to depths of 3 to 9 feet

Soil Conditions
Based on the test pits the western two thirds of the site
appears to be underlain by about 5 to 8 feet of loose to
medium dense dune sand deposits underlain by a rock forma
tion The upper 2 feet of sandy deposit is generally loose
Many roots were noted in the upper 2 to 2 feet of the sand
deposit Water seepage was noted in the deepest test pit
at about 8 foot depths

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed uniform sandy subsoils over
most of the project site
If the upper 2 to 2 feet of loose sandy soils with abundant
roots are excavated and replaced with compacted clean on site
soils conventional shallow foundations can be utilized
Alternately footings established at about 3 feet below the
existing ground surface may also be used for the proposed
buildings
Soft clayey soils were noted in the low area situated in
the northwestern corner of the site however structures
are not presently planned in this area
Some settlements should be anticipated if additional
loading by fill or structures is imposed over these
soft subsoils Additional exploration and analysis is
required if structures are planned over this lowlying
area designated as Lot 8
W O 870 00 P 5
Site Preparation and Grading
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris
existing concrete slabs and old foundations from demolition
of the existing dwelling The strippings may be stockpiled
for future landscaping use All others should be either
hauled off the site or properly disposed of All existing
cesspools should be properly cleaned and backfilled in
accordance with County requirements
Many roots were observed in the top 2 to 24 feet of the
dune sand deposit To reduce future settlements due to
decay of these roots we recommend that at least the top
2 feet of the upper soils be overexcavated stockpiled
processed to remove the roots and reused as structural
fill The overexcavation of the upper 2 to 24 feet of
soils should extend 5 feet beyond building areas
After the recommended excavation the building sites should
be moistened and proofrolled with a heavy vibratory roller
to densify the underlying sandy soil prior to any filling
operation
Fills should be placed in 8 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Any imported fill material should be non expansive and approved
by the soil engineer prior to hauling to the site
W O 870 00 P 6
If future improvements of the low northwestern corner of
the site is planned this area should be filled as early
as practicable to allow the soft underlying materials to
settle as much as possible prior to any actual improvements
Additional recommendations should be developed for this
area if any future improvements are planned
Foundation
If the site is prepared as outlined above conventional
spread footing foundations are recommended for the light
wood framed residential structures
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings established at the compacted sandy subsoils The
footings should be at least 12 inches wide and should extend
a minimum of 18 inches below the finish grade Surface water
should be diverted away from building foundations
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the trench
Alternately conventional spread foundations established in
the medium dense sand below the root lines at about 3 feet
below the existing ground surface may be used if overexcava
tion and recompaction of the upper 2 feet of the upper soil
prove to be non economical However proper compaction for
the upper 12 inches of subgrade should be prepared as outlined
under Grading for slab on grade support Slabs should be
structurally free from all foundations
W O 870 00 P 7
A bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used if footings are
established below the root lines
Lateral Loads
Resistance of lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead loads times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4 four tenth
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade can be used on the properly compacted subgrade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools are located at the central portion of
the site The cesspool locations should be verified in
the field Where existing cesspool are located below or
adjacent to footings and slabs the sludge should be removed
and the cesspool filled with compacted granular backfill
The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should
be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soil engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
