SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
At the proposed invert elevation the force main
will be supported upon loose to medium dense silty
sand with coral gravel
A crushed rock cradle will be required to provide
adequate support without excessive settlements
The excavations will extend below the groundwater
level and dewatering is recommended It appears
that wellpoints and sloped sidewalls would be the
most expedient construction method
INTRODUCTION
The subsurface investigation was undertaken to define sub
surface conditions along the proposed force main alignment
on Sand Island and to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the pipe
W O
The subsurface investigations for the remaining sections of
the Ala Moana Force Main are covered by separate reports
Proposed Construction
The enclosed General Location Map shows the proposed force
main location It will extend from a new pump station near
Ala Moana Boulevard thru the Matson Container Yard across
Honolulu Harbor Channel and across Sand Island to the new
treatment plant presently under construction
The Sand Island Section covered by this report consists of
approximately 5 600 feet of 78 inch reinforced concrete pipe
extending from the Diamond Head end of Sand Island at the
entrance to Honolulu Harbor to the site of the new sewage
treatment plan
Two possible alignments are being considered for the initial
2 700 foot section at the Diamond Head end The makai route
parallels the ocean for approximately 1600 feet before turn
ing inland The mauka route goes inland immediately for a
distance of approximately 1600 feet and then turns Ewa
parallel to the ocean for the remaining distance to the
treatment plant
W O
Site Description
The brush covered ground surface is essentially level at
approximately elevation 6 feet Numerous areas of rubbish
junk and other debris are present throughout the site The
island is criss crossed with unpaved roads and paths
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
This section of the force main will be constructed on an
essentially man made island built upon a shallow coral reef
Initially the island was much smaller consisting of a
shallow water coral reef formed within a few feet of sea
level
The portion fronting the ocean in the breaker zone is com
posed of more resistant coral Behind this coral zone the
island appears to be composed of sand and coral debris
deposited in calmer water behind the surf zone
Recent filling and dredging operations have given the island
its present configuration The Diamond Head end was filled
with materials dredged from the harbor entrance channel in
the early part of the century The major portion of the
W O
remaining section was filled with material dredged from the
seaplane runways constructed adjacent to the Ewa end of the
island
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions along the proposed alignment were
investigated utilizing eleven test borings drilled at the
locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 Borings
1 through 4 were drilled approximately 100 feet west of the
indicated alignment in anticipation of a revision in the
force main location
The remaining borings were generally drilled along the align
ment or as near as site conditions would permit
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the subsurface conditions are relatively consistent at
the boring locations where two major layers of soils were
encountered within the limits of our explorations
W O dune 5 1975
The upper layer varying from approximately 7 to 12 feet in
thickness consists of a mixture of light tan sand and coral
gravel with some silt This layer contained seams of dense
ledge coral generally between 1 and 4 feet in thickness The
sand and coral gravel varied from loose to medium dense
The upper layer of soil may be dredged material from the
excavations for the harbor entrance and the seaplane runways
The lower layer consists of light gray silty sand with some
coral and coral gravel This layer was predominantly loose
to medium dense but did contain thin layers of dense coral
Additional coral layers were also encountered beneath the
silty sand at the depth of 30 feet which was the limit of
our borings
Groundwater was encountered at approximately mean sea level
but varied with tidal fluctuations
The findings of the subsurface explorations are summarized on
page 6 Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered
are given in the appended boring logs along with the labora
tory test results

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The force main invert will be approximately twelve feet
below the existing ground surface With normal bedding the
trench excavation will completely penetrate the upper soil
layer and the pipe will be supported upon the loose to
medium dense silty sand with coral gravel
Stabilized bedding will be required to provide adequate
support for the proposed pipe
The trench excavations will extend up to ten feet below the
groundwater level and dewatering will be required to main
tain a dry excavation
Earth Pressures
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
excavation and the unit weight and type of backfill material
To minimize the loads on the force main the trench width
should be maintained at the minimum necessary for construc
tion operations
W 1975
With a trench excavation width of approximately 40 feet at
the top of the pipe the maximum width which should be
permitted and utilizing granular backfill it is estimated
that the earth pressure on the conduit will be approximately
16 kips per lineal foot Additional surface loads such as
truck traffic or material storage must be added to this
value
Pipe Support
The silty sand and gravel upon which the pipe will be bearing
is predominantly a loose deposit This type of material can
produce vertical settlements if subjected to additional
loadings or vibrations Irregularities in the soil densities
over short distances can also increase the differential
settlements
The pipe should therefore be supported upon a crushed rock
cradle to provide more uniform support minimize differential
movements and permit spanning of localized soft spots The
thickness of the rock cradle will depend on the trench
dewatering
Crushed Rock Cradle
With dewatered excavations the crushed rock cradle should
have a minimum thickness of two feet providing one foot of
W O
the natural deposits can be densified to a minimum of 85
percent of the maximum density as established by ASTM Test
D1557 72T If the existing materials cannot be densified
to this minimum density the rock cradle thickness should be
increased to three feet
If dewatering is not utilized for the trench excavations
the rock cradle should have a four foot minimum thickness to
provide adequate support
The cradle should be constructed of compacted crushed rock
meéting the requirements for No 3 Coarse Bedding Section
15 Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
City and County of Honolulu
Settlements
The settlements should not exceed 1 inch total and inch
differential between adjoining sections utilizing the above
recommendations
Additional strengthening of the pipe or a reinforced concrete
bedding may be necessary for the transition between pipes
supported by the crushed rock cradle and any rigid non
yielding structure such as the treatment plant
W 1975
Construction Excavations
It is anticipated that the trench excavations can be accom
plished utilizing conventional methods Some difficulties
may be encountered penetrating the dense ledge coral These
layers are relatively thin and should be removed utilizing
busters or similar methods to fracture the coral It is not
anticipated that blasting would be required
Due to the difficulties of driving sheeting within the essen
tially granular materials it is anticipated that sloping
trench sidewalls will be the most economical method of tem
porary support The side slopes should not exceed 1 hori
zontal to 1 vertical for the temporary construction conditions
if dewatering is utilized
It is anticipated that shoring or sheeting will be required
if dewatering of the trench is not provided
It is anticipated that the trench excavations can be dewatered
utilizing wellpoints placed along each side of the excavation
The watertable should be maintained at least three feet below
the proposed trench bottom
Care must be exercised during the construction operations to
minimize the disturbance of the underlying soils
W O
It is recommended that the project be designed to utilize
wellpoint dewatering and slope sidewalls of 1 to 1 slope
Each contractor should be given the option of proposing
alternative construction methods and equipments subject to
the review and approval of the engineer
The following soil parameters should be utilized for any
proposed alternative trench support methods
Wet Unit Weight 100 pcf
Submerged Unit Weight 60 pcf
Moisture Content 15 30 percent
Cohesion 0
Angle of Internal Friction 26 degrees
Contruction Plan Review
The proposed dewatering methods should be reviewed and
approved prior to construction to assure that adequate exca
vations stability is maintained and that the proposed
dewatering methods will not cause detrimental settlements
beneath nearby structures
Generally the project is in isolated areas where existing
structures are not present The dewatering methods must be
reviewed with particular attention to the Ewa end of the
W O
force main where the pipe will connect into the treatment
plant presently under construction Care must be exercised
to prevent additional settlements of the treatment plant
which should be nearing completion at that time
Backfill
The backfill material around the conduits should consist of
select free draining well graded materials compacted to
between 90 and 95 percent of the maximum density It is
anticipated that jetting would be the preferred method of
compaction however mechanical equipment can be utilized in
dewatered excavations
The excavated on site materials will be essentially granular
and would be suitable for the trench backfill above the pipe
providing the large coral fragments are removed
Inspections During Construction
It is recommended that the trench excavations rock cradle
construction pipe placement and backfill compaction be
inspected on a full time basis to assure that the recommen
dations contained in this report are correctly implemented
Construction inspections should verify the compaction levels
and also verify that the subsurface conditions are consis
tent with the findings of our investigation
W O
If unforeseen or undetected soil conditions such as concrete
rubble pockets of soft cohesive soils or unusual water
conditions are encountered corrective measures should be
made in the field as they are detected
The area through which the force main will be constructed is
an essentially man made island and uniform conditions are
not anticipated even though the test borings produced rela
tively consistent findings
Subsurface conditions quite different from those encountered
in the subsurface explorations may be disclosed during the
construction operations Since these conditions are prob
ably man made their extent or location cannot be readily
predicted Provisions should be made in the contract docu
ments to handle unforeseen subsurface conditions

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
ing eleven borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
depths of the borings varied from 20 to 30 feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings are
shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the soils encoun
tered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through
A 11
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch 1 D
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depth
W Appendix 1975
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture density determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification of the
soils The results of these tests are presented on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample location
Three grain size analyses were performed on selected samples
of the granular soils to aid in the soil identification The
test results are given on Plates A 12 A 13 and A 14
