This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the temporary remedial solution for the slope failure behind

presented herein are based on a site reconnaissance a limited field exploration program
and our engineering analyses The recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing slope behind Building 33 at the Kalihi Valley Homes public housing
facility is located in the upper portion of the development Based on our field observations
an approximately 45 foot wide area of the existing slope has failed where the upper
portion of soil materials slid down and accumulated near the low retaining wall at the toe of
the slope We understand that it is desired to evaluate the existing site conditions and
formulate temporary remedial recommendations for the slope repair work
FIELD EXPLORATION
A site meeting was held on with representatives from the
Hawaii Public Housing Authority Engineering Concepts Inc and T Iida Contracting Ltd
to observe the existing site conditions and to discuss potential remedial approaches to the
slope repair work

geologist to evaluate the depth to competent subsoils in the proximity of the slope failure
area by hand probing with a 3 foot long 0 5 inch diameter steel T bar with pointed tip at

TEMPORARY REMEDIAL SOLUTION FOR SLOPE REPAIR
Based on the information provided we understand that a permanent repair scheme
for the slope failure will be implemented during the future construction phase of the
housing project Therefore the remedial solution presented herein is for a temporary
purpose only with the intent of stabilizing the exposed vertical slope face along the upper
concrete walkway
Based on our field observations and field exploration results we have identified the
approximate limits of the slope area requiring repair work as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 Based on our evaluation and analyses we recommend constructing a gravity
retaining wall consisting of Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM at the top of the slope
to protect the upper concrete walkway from further undermining and movement The
gravity wall should measure approximately 4 feet wide at the top 6 feet wide at the
bottom and 8 5 feet in height The bottom of the gravity wall at the front face should be
embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the adjacent finished grades The bottom of the
gravity wall should slope down towards the mauka side at a gradient of twelve horizontal to
one vertical 12H 1V to improve the wall stability against sliding and overturning
The gravity wall should be well drained to reduce the potential for build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the wall which could result in potential movement and or
failure of the gravity wall A drainage system consisting of 2 foot wide geocomposite
drainage strips spaced at 5 foot on center should be provided behind the gravity wall to
collect subterranean seepage The bottom of the geocomposite drainage strip should be
connected to a 4 inch diameter PVC pipe and daylighted at the front face of the gravity
wall through weepholes for proper water disposal A schematic sketch of the proposed
gravity wall section is illustrated on Plate 3
Prior to the placement of the CLSM the bottom of the gravity wall should be
recompacted to a firm condition as much as practical The sloping ground in front of the
gravity wall after wall construction should be re graded and compacted to a firm condition
to provide the necessary passive resistance to the gravity wall
