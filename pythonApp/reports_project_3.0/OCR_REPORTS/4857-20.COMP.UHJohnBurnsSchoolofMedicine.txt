EARTHWORK AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
The general contractor for the John A Burns School of Medicine project is
Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company The site grading operations pavement
construction and utility installation were performed by RHS Lee Inc The site grading
consisted of the demolition of existing structures and pavements over excavation of the
surface expansive clays preparation of slab and pavement subgrades placement and
compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and
backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 18 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 18 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new development In general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches
of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and manholes for uniform support
Based on our field observations soft and wet soils were encountered below the bedding
layer along the utility lines and manhole structures at the project site A stabilization layer
consisting of 24 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a non woven filter fabric Mirafi
180N was provided below the bedding layer for more uniform support Free draining
granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up
to about 12 inches above the pipes or groundwater level to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill The
trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on the attached Plate 2

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 1816 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017
Compaction tests results are summarized on the attached Plates 3 1 through 3 93 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on our field representatives observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading pavement and utility
construction for the above project that we observed and tested in the period covered by
this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the
referenced report dated and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
