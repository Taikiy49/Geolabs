Supplemental Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
CKSS Associates Kaimuki Office Building
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report presents the results of our supplemental geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed CKSS Associates Kaimuki Office Building project
in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Due to the presence of existing structures during our initial geotechnical exploration
in June 1992 the preliminary exploration was limited to three 3 borings located at the
perimeter of the existing structures The purpose of our supplemental exploration was
to further evaluate the extent of the competent basalt formation and to confirm our
preliminary geotechnical recommendations
Project Considerations
The proposed project involves the construction of an office building of four 4
stories and a roof story above four 4 split levels of subsurface basement parking
According to the building plans it is anticipated that the building will be of reinforced
concrete construction A basement excavation on the order of approximately 30 feet will
be required to construct the basement parking structure

Findings and Recommendations
General
Based on the supplemental geotechnical exploration the underlying basalt
formation is anticipated to vary from soft to very hard Laboratory unconfined
compression tests on selected rock core samples indicated that the unconfined
compression strengths range from 150 to 1 470 kips per square foot k s f Based on
the laboratory results it is our opinion that hard excavations may be required in the
basaltic rock formation from depths of about 2 to 2 feet below the existing ground
surface to the bottom of the planned excavation
Our laboratory test results also indicate that the surface layer of silty clay exhibits
moderate swell potential It is our recommendation that the clayey material be used only
as top soil or be removed and disposed of properly off site
Design Recommendations
In general the recommendations presented in our report entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Exploration Proposed CKSS Associates Kaimuki Office Building Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii dated are appropriate for design of the proposed structure
with the following additional recommendations
Pavement

inch 4 aggregate cushion fill may be eliminated below the base course of the
pavement section Where soil condition is encountered at the subgrade level in

Retaining Walls
For the backfill of exterior retaining walls not adjacent to paved areas granular
backfill material should be placed up to a height of two feet 2 below the finish
grade We anticipate the granular backfill material will consist of crushed basalt
generated from on site excavation Therefore the remaining backfill should consist
of impervious soil overlying woven filter fabric such as or equivalent
The filter fabric serves to reduce the potential migration of fines through the voids
of the granular backfill material Alternately filter fabric may be eliminated if well
graded granular materials are used as backfill material behind the retaining wall
Underpinning
The project site is bounded on the west by two story buildings and on the south
by approximately six foot high CMU retaining walls on the adjacent lot as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
Based on our initial and supplemental exploration the surficial layer of silty clay
may extend between approximately 2 to 7 feet below the existing ground Therefore
it is our opinion that the foundations of the adjacent structures may not bear directly on
the underlying basalt formation Based on conversation with the structural engineer
information regarding the foundation system depth of embedment and bearing materials
of the adjacent structures were not available

We therefore recommend further exploration of the foundation system of the
adjacent structures be conducted in order to evaluate the need for underpinning the
adjacent structures for the planned basement excavation We also anticipate that
excavation in basalt formation would result in an irregular finish face of the excavation that
may exceed the excavation limits indicated on the plans Therefore the adequacy and
safety of the excavation support or underpinning that may be required should be made
the sole responsibility of the contractor subject to practical limits and safety consideration
including all applicable regulations
Monitoring
Regardless of the type of excavation support or underpinning we recommend that
a monitoring program be established for the project to assess that the excavation
operations are not adversely affecting the surrounding area The monitoring program
may consist of the following
1 A damage survey of the existing buildings and structures adjacent to the
construction site to document the existing conditions of the structures
before excavation commences The survey should include but not limited
to photographs and crack surveys
2 Horizontal and vertical monitoring reference points should be established
around the perimeter of the site and at locations in the adjacent areas The
reference points may be surveyed at intervals of about one 1 week for the
first three 3 months and less frequently thereafter if no significant
movements are indicated
