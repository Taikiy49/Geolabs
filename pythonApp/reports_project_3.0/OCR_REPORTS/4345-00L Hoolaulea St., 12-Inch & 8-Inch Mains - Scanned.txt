PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project

system improvement The water system improvement areas are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known at
this time although they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was conducted
along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions and results of our
corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

the subsurface soils along the proposed water mains planned at the project site Our work
was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95 and the
requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project site
on for the field survey Our field work was performed under clear and sunny
conditions with generally dry to moist surface conditions
Thirty six soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals at
nine locations designated as R 1 through R 9 along the sidewalk of the street using the
in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter manufactured by
Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes
to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The
average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the
test depth was assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
sidewalk of the existing street of the proposed water mains The average soil resistance along
the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at
equal spacings of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface The depth of penetration

of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing Since the depth of the water
main invert was not available at the time of our field exploration spacings of 3 to 9 feet were
used for the electrode spacing for each soil resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Mode 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in onm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report Field Resistivity Testing Summary is presented
on Plate 4 It should be noted that the soil resistivity may be influenced by temperature
weather conditions existing underground facilities and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the tests indicate the
subsurface soils along the proposed water mains within the project site have corrosion ratings
of 2to 3 This indicates the subsurface soils have very corrosive to corrosive potential
Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 1 and 4 were noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and beyond
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
The test locations R 1 through R 9 presented in this report were estimated by
measuring the linear distance from the test location R 1 along Hoolaulea Street towards
Komo Mai Drive referenced on the Site Plan Plate 2 Elevations of the test locations were
estimated in the field from the features and reference points shown on the plans provided by
Board of Water Supply on Therefore locations of the data presented
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
