INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed at the Royal Summit Subdivision Lot 146 Aiea Oahu Hawaii for the
proposed residence of Dr and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2609 00 Page 2
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the exploration findings and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed residence will be a split ievei
wood framed structure approximately 117 by 50 feet in overall plan dimension The
structure is of a modified L configuration resulting in a footprint area of
approximately 3 500 square feet A roughly triangular swimming pool of
approximately 20 by 24 foot base leg dimensions with an adjoining jacuzzi facility is
located near the mid point of the southwesterly lot line Due to the topography of the
Site it is anticipated that the northwesterly half of the structure will be slab on grade
while the southeasterly half will be supported by concrete columns supported on
drilled piers and a structural floor slab Particular attention must be given to drainage
requirements

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located at Lot 146 near the end of Kaahele Street in the Royal
Summit Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu Based on drawings provided to
us by Design Partners Incorporated dated the lot is approximately
460 feet in the longer dimension average northwest to southeast and approximately
140 feet in the shorter northeast to southwest dimension The lot has been graded
such that starting at the northwest end of its longer dimension Kaahele Street there
is a virtually level bench approximately 40 feet wide at an elevation between 954 and
955 feet This is followed by a slope approximately two and one half horizontal to
one vertical to a second virtually level bench approximately 140 feet wide
at an approximate elevation of 950 feet The benches siope approximately 0 05 to
a maximum of approximately 2 8 to the southeast It is also noted that both
benches are bounded by upward slopes to the longitudinal property lines
approximately eight 8 feet wide on the southwest side and twenty 20 feet wide on
the northeast side
These two benches occupy the northwesterly half of the property
The southeasterly half of the lot slopes rather steeply one horizontal to one
vertical 1H 1V to near vertical down the side slopes of the valley below
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings to
depths of 11 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 it is noted that four of
the six borings are located outside the footprint of the proposed structure This is

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the competent residual soil can provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed residence provided our recommendations are
followed
Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey silt soils indicate that the
material generally has a low to moderate shrink sweil potential To reduce potential
foundation movements due to the cyclical soil shrink swell we recommend that all
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2609 00 Page 6
foundations be cushioned on a minimum of twelve 12 inches of imported non
expansive granular material
Detailed discussions are presented in the following sections
House Foundations
Northwesterly Portion
The northwesterly portion of the structure will be founded on the level bench
previously noted at an approximate elevation of 950 feet It is our opinion that
spread or continuous wail foundations may be used to support this portion of
the proposed residence
In order to reduce the potential effects of soil shrink swell we recommend that
all footings be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade and cushioned on a minimum of twelve 12 inches of imported
non expansive granular material An allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f
is recommended for the design of all footings This value may be increased
by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
The bottom of footing excavations should be at a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Scarification to a depth of six 6 inches and recompaction should be
accomplished if necessary to achieve this requirement
lf soft or loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing or
floor slab bearing elevations they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2609 00 Page 7
and replaced with granular select material compacted to a minimum of 90
maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line the embedment should be extended
as deep as the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Southeasterly Portion
The southeasterly portion of the structure will be as much as nineteen 19 feet
above the existing ground surface This is because of the steep siope
beginning approximately 112 feet from Kaahele Street This portion of the
structure will therefore require columnar supports the unsupported lengths
of which should be appropriately braced by diagonal members
It is our opinion that the columnar supports for this portion of the structure
should be founded on drilled pier foundations bearing at elevations at least five
5 feet below the adjacent finished grade elevations
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for the design of the
drilled piers The drilled shaft construction should be constantly observed by
the project geotechnical engineer to provide expert field monitoring and
evaluation regarding attainment of adequate bearing Settlements may be
anticipated to be less than one half of an inch both overall and differential
Drainage
The topography of the site described hereinbefore is such that much of the
precipitation falling on the site will flow toward to proposed structure Especial

attention should therefore be given to drainage particularly at the northwest end
Rectangular concrete trenches appropriately covered with metal grills to intercept
surface run off may be considered to preclude flooding of the residence during heavy
storms
Pool and Jacuzzi
The walls of the pool and jacuzzi should be designed as retaining walls
restrained at the top The following design parameters are recommended
1
Lateral earth pressure 62 4 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of
depth
An allowable bearing capacity up to 3000 p s f for retaining wall
foundations bearing on undisturbed natural soils or on compacted fill
is recommended
A passive earth pressure resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for wall footings
A base friction factor of 0 4 may be used to determine the resistance of
the walls against sliding
The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to
minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2609 00 Page 9
Subdrains should be constructed properly to drain water away from the
walls
7 Select granular material e g select borrow or base course is
recommended as backfill behind the retaining walls to reduce the
amount of compactive effort required The upper two 2 feet of backfill
behind the walls may consist of relatively impervious on site clayey
material to reduce the infiltration of surface water behind the walls
Care should be taken in the siting of proposed walls and proposed
foundations for structural elements above or behind the walls so that
the foundation loads do not impose an excessive surcharge pressure
on the walls
Slab On Grade
The proposed residential slab on grade should be cushioned with a minimum
twelve inch layer of non expansive granular materials such as select borrow or base
course aggregates The slab subgrade should be thoroughly saturated several days
prior to the placement of cushion fill to reduce the swell potential of the underlying
Clayey soils in addition a four 4 inch layer of No 3 Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No
67 gravel and an impervious moisture barrier should be provided below the slab
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the proposed structural grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or
soft spots encountered in areas to receive fill should be over excavated to firm soil
and then replaced with compacted select non expansive material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2609 00 Page 10
Areas to receive fill if any should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills on existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched into the existing slopes in order to bond the new fills
to the existing slope
Cut or fill slopes should not be steeper than two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V
All imported fill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in level lifts with a maximum loose thickness of 10
inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Concrete Driveway
A concrete driveway parking area and garage are planned for the proposed
residence
It should be noted that the on site clayey soils have low to moderately high
expansion potential if allowed to dry Therefore the on site subgrade soil should be
thoroughly moistened several days prior to pavement construction and then kept
moist prior to the placement of the concrete slab

is recommended A minimum six 6 inch thick layer of compacted base course
should be provided below the slab to reduce future potential heaving of the concrete
driveway

Walk and Lanai Deck
A concrete walk eight 8 feet wide and a lanai deck including walks three 3
feet wide are shown on the plans provided to us A thickness of four 4 inches
unreinforced is typical for sidewalk construction and is recommended for the project
Contraction joints consisting of grooves formed in the surface prior to the initial
set of the concrete should be provided at a maximum of four 4 foot spacing
throughout the eight 8 foot wide walk Expansion joints should be provided at
locations where the new walk abuts other structures
The lanai deck consists of concrete walks three 3 feet wide along the
northwest and southwest sides of the pool and an irregularly shaped concrete paved
area at the southerly edge of the pool and jacuzzi facility
The three 3 foot wide walks should be of four 4 inches unreinforced
concrete Contraction joints consisting of grooves formed in the surface prior to initial
set of the concrete should be provided at three 3 foot intervals Expansion joints
should be provided along the pool edges at the intersection of this walk and the
eight 8 foot wide walk noted hereinbefore at the right angle intersection and at the
juncture of the three 3 foot walk and the irregularly shaped lanai deck Due to its
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2609 00 Page 12
irregular shape the lanai deck which may be four 4 inches thick should be
reinforced with welded wire mesh to control shrinkage cracks
All these pavements should be based on a six 6 inch layer of non expansive
material
Utility Trenching
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting
of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel or 3B Fine is recommended under the
pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The backfill up to
about one 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility
of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of one 1 foot above the
pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch
horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to minimize future
subsidence
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction
This review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications
or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report the appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from features indicated on the drawing provided to us The physical location
of the field data collection points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
