This report presents the results of a subsurface investigation
conducted at the site of the proposed warehouse and office
building located along the extension of Kilihau Street within
Shafter Flats Honolulu Hawaii This study was conducted to
determine the soils underlying the site to ascertain their
engineering properties and to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the substructural elements of the
proposed structure and other related facilities
This investigation consisted of conducting three exploratory
test borings laboratory tésts and engineéring analyses and
evaluation of both field and laboratory test results Location

STRUCTURAL CONSIDERATIONS
Information regarding the proposed structure and the existing
site conditions was furnished by Village Architects and Associates
for Quality Pacific Ltd For the office building column and
wall loads will be on the order of 140 kips and 4 kips per
lineal foot respectively The warehouse is expected to have
a wall load of 1 5 kips per lineal foot Information on expected
loading conditions were provided by Nishimura Oki Engineers Inc
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject property covers an area of about 21 400 square feet
The nearly flat area is bounded on both sides by two existing
building The property runs along Kilihau Street extension
and is bounded at the back by Moanalua Stream The bank of
the stream adjacent the site is stabilized by a stone revetment
The site has already been cleared

FINDINGS
Both field and laboratory data shows that the site is under
lain by two distinct horizontally bedded strata The top
ten feet consists of granular material The upper 7 feet of
this layer consist of medium dense sand and gravel size coralline
material with negligible amount of scattered slightly plastic
soil The lower three feet consist of loose fine coralline
sand These materials may be considered to have an angle of
internal friction of 30 degrees
The ground water surface is found in this layer at a depth of
5 feet below natural ground surface It may be mentioned in
passing that the stream adjacent to the Site appears to exhibit
a tidal range of 2 feet
Below the ten foot granular material is soft organic gray
clay A thin soft black organic silt layer was found within
this layer at a depth of about 12 feet A closer look at the
soil samples recovered revealed the presence of partially
decayed lenses and pieces of wood Sounding tests using a

CONCLUSTONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 4
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions and
recommendations are presented
A General
The proposed site is marginally suitable for supporting
light structural loads on conventional spread or continuous
Page 6
W
footings because of the underlying very soft highly
compressible clay deposit
Substantial settlements of the proposed structure should
be anticipated
If the settlements are not acceptable for the type of
structure planned an alternate foundation support system
should be investigated
Foundations
Spread or continuous footings founded in the medium dense
to dense sand fill should be at least 18 inches but no more
than 36 inches below the existing ground surface
using the SETTLEMENT VS FOOTING WIDTH CHARTS presented
on Plates 2 and 3 The charts can be utilized by choosing
the maximum settlement which is acceptable for the structure
and designing all footings along that particular settlement
line
The maximum allowable bearing pressure should not exceed
2000 psf and the minimum footing width should be no less
than 12 inches The allowable bearing pressures may be
Page 7
W
increase by 1 3 for short term wind or seismic loading
conditions
Footings adjacent to existing buildings should be founded
at the same elevation as the existing footings provided
_they are within the 18 to 36 inch limit The new footings
should be at least 36 inches clear distance from existing
footings to reduce the stress influence on the underlying
soils If this clear spacing is unacceptable the design
pressures should be reduced by at least 1 3 for these
particular footings
Settlements
Total settlements in excess of 5 inches may occur with an
allowable bearing pressure of 2000 psf and large footings
see Charts Plates 2 and 3 however large total settle
ments can often be tolerated Differential settlements
which may cause unsightly cracking and overstressing of
structural members should be minimized If all footings
are designed for a uniform total settlements as recommended
in Section B differential settlement should not exceed
1 2 inch

Floor Slabs
Floor slabs should be reinforced with wire mesh and should
be free floating i e not connected in any way to the
building footings A vapor barrier i e polyethylene
plastic should be used to minimize floor dampness
Floor areas should be loaded uniformly as much as possible
to minimize differential settlement
Lateral Design
Resistance to lateral loading may be provided by friction
acting at the base of foundations An allowable coefficient
of friction of 0 4 may be used with dead loads Passive
earth pressure shall be neglected
Inspection
It is recommended that all excavation for footings and slabs
be inspected by a soils engineer or his representative
prior to placing of forms concrete or reinforcing steel
The subgrade especially at footing location shall be
compacted to at least 95 of the maximum density as determined
by ASTM D 1557 70
