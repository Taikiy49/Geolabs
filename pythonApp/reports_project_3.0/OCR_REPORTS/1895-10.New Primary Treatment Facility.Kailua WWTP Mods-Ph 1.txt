Geotechnical Engineering Report
New Primary Treatment Facility

Phase 1
Kailua
It is our understanding that the proposed new Primary Treatment
Facility for Phase 1 of the subject modification project has been
relocated subsequent to our previous geotechnical exploration in 1987
The new facility site is to the northeasterly side of the existing
Laboratory and Administration Building of the treatment plant
In general accordance with our verbal agreement to perform a
geotechnical engineering exploration four 1 thru RB 4
have been drilled and sampled at the new facility site to depths of about
30 0 to 50 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1 Selected soil samples
were tested The boring logs and the laboratory test results are also
attached
Subsoil Conditions
The drilled borings indicate that the new facility site is generally
underlain by highly expansive clay fill or alluvium over very stiff to
hard clays and silts residual soil and saprolite underlain by hard to
very hard weathered basalt formation only encountered in
and RB 3

In Boring RB 1 located at the northern corner of the new structure
thin layers of soft clay and loose to medium dense sand and gravel were
encountered between the depths of about 9 0 to 15 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
During the field exploration groundwater level was measured in the
borings at depths varying between 7 0 to 19 3 feet below the existing
ground surface
Foundation
Based on the boring data we believe that the following foundation
design guidelines may be used for the proposed New Primary Treatment
Facility at its new location It is our understanding that most of the
new facility will be embedded up to about 20 feet below the existing
ground and the remaining portions will be built on a capping fill layer of
about two 2 to five 5 feet thick above the existing ground surface
Based on the soil conditions and the anticipated maximum embedment
depths about elevation 3 feet MSL of the new facility we believe that
the embedded portions of the new Primary Treatment Facility may be
supported by a mat foundation bearing partially on soft loose deposits and
partially on stiff saprolite or hard weathered basalt The use of a rigid
mat foundation to support the structure would help to reduce differential
settlements of the structure due to the different foundation bearing
subsoils
It is anticipated that a large amount of overburden soil will be
removed by deep excavation for construction of the proposed facility We
believe that the pressure relief at the bottom of the excavation due to
earth removal may compensate for much of the foundation bearing pressure
generated by the embedded structure plus its future sewage loads

Therefore we recommend that an allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f be
used for design of the mat foundation The mat foundation settlements
under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be small
Since the other portion of the new facility site will be raised by a
capping fill layer of at least two 2 feet thick it appears that the
anticipated fill thickness over the in situ expansive clay would be
sufficient to reduce the potential subsoil swelling effect on the new
structure Therefore we believe that shallow spread or continuous
footing foundations bearing on the compacted capping fill layer may be
used for the raised portions of the new structure
We recommend that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f be
used for the footing foundation design The footing foundation
settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less
than one inch total and one half inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the structure may be provided by
frictional resistance and passive earth pressure acting on the structure
A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottom of the mat or footing foundations and the
recompacted subgrade soils An equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive earth resistance
against the vertical faces of the embedded structure or footings
Site Grading
Based on the available finish grades it is our understanding that
the northern portion of the structure site will be raised by up to about 2
feet Due to the interbedded soft loose subsoil conditions encountered in
the boring RB 1 drilled in this area it is our opinion that new fills
should be placed at this portion of the site as early as possible to allow
most of the induced ground settlements to occur

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
foundation construction of the project This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the project
site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from the
existing structures indicated on the grading plan dated November 18
1988 of the project site prepared by R M Towill Corporation Elevations
of the borings were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points or contours on the plan The physical location and
elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of R M Towill
Corporation for specific application to the foundation design of the
proposed structure in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or
implied is made
