SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Leung Memorial at Valley of the Temples project in
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and _ geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the family memorial The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within the southern portion of the Valley of the
Temples Mortuary along Chapel Hill Road The project site comprising of three lots with
a total land area of 0 85 acres is bounded by other lots on both east and west sides
We understand that the project site was graded a few years ago The project generally
involves redirecting the course of the existing drainage pipe extending the plateau
possibly constructing a retaining wall and installing granite lined stairs to the
granite lined monuments

2 1 Site Description
The project site is along the southern side of Chapel Hill Road in the southern
portion of the Valley of the Temples Mortuary in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The Tax Map Key TMk for the site is 01 and was assigned as Lot
276 A At present the southern two thirds of the site are relatively open and flat and the
northern one third consists of a bowl shape depression Based on the topographic plan
provided the existing ground surface elevation at the southern portion ranges from
approximately 218 to 228 feet Mean Sea Level MSL sloping up from the north to
the southwest while the ground elevation of the depression in the northern portion is
about 205 feet MSL
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 each to a depth of about 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Boring No 1 is near the east rim of the bowl shape depression
while Boring Nos 2 and 3 are on the southern flat area The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered recent fill material consisting of stiff
clayey silt from the existing ground surface to about 8 to 15 feet below the existing
ground surface Alluvial soils consisting of stiff clayey silt interbedded with organic
matter and cobbles were encountered below the fill material in Boring Nos 1 and 2 and
extended to the maximum depth drilled of about 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface In Boring No 3 completely weathered basalt rock saprolite was encountered
below the recent fills to the maximum depth explored of about 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
Based on the information of this project site the fill layer thicknesses were
consistent with our previous field exploration dated May 5 1998

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicated that the project site is generally underlain by
medium stiff to stiff fill material underlain by stiff alluvial soil and or saprolites to the
maximum depth explored of about 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based
on the results of our exploration we believe that the proposed monument structures and
retaining walls may be supported on shallow foundations bearing on a minimum of
8 inches of select borrow and embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the finished
grade
Our laboratory testing on the on site soils indicated high moisture content above
55 percent generally associated with high to very high average annual rainfall at the
project site Since the project site is in a high rainfall area substantial aeration of the
in situ soils may not be feasible prior to their use as fill backfill material in order to meet
the normal 90 percent minimum compaction requirement Based on our experience with
similar high moisture tropical soils we believe that a reduced compaction criterion of
85 percent relative compaction may be used in order to facilitate placement of fills and
to reduce the potential for pumping condition We recommend extensive keying and
benching to properly bond the new fills to the existing slope Permanent fill slope should
be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
We understand that a CRM wall up to 3 5 feet is planned An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be utilized for the design of
foundation bearing on 8 inches of select borrow material over the in situ soils or new
compacted fills
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections herein

Keying and Benching
Based on the information provided we understand that the bowl shape depression
at the northern portion of the site will be backfilled and sloped in order to extend the
plateau Base on our exploration the current soil slope at this location is very steep
Keying and benching is recommended to firmly seat the new fill into the native
subsoil to reduce the potential for sliding and or slope failure
As fill placement progresses up the existing slope benches should be cut into the
existing slope to bond the mass of the fill to the existing ground A sketch of keying
and benching is shown on Plate 3
Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials Since the project site is located
in a high rainfall area the average moisture content of the on site material is about
15 to 30 percent above optimum Therefore significant drying of excavated
materials will be required prior to their use as fill and or backfill material
Imported structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Aggregate subbase materials required under the CRM wall foundations should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to Section 31 of the
Standard Specification for Public Works Construction Department of Public Works
City and County of Honolulu should test imported fill

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Due to the high rainfall in the area the normal 90 percent minimum compaction
requirement may not be feasible when using on site soil As a result the
compaction requirements for general fill placement should be reduced in order to
aid in the earthwork construction using the high moisture tropical soils in this wet
environment Based on our experience with similar high moisture tropical soils we
recommend using a reduced compaction criterion of 85 percent relative compaction
in order to facilitate placement of fills and to reduce the potential for pumping
condition
General fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to at least 85 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Based on experience with similar soils
a shrinkage factor of about 20 percent due to the differences between the natural
and compacted soil densities may be considered for on site cut and fill quantities
The contractor should be aware that aeration to lower the soil moisture and
additional effort with thinner fill lifts to achieve the specified compaction will reduce
the rate of fill placement for this project Even with a reduced 85 percent minimum
compaction requirement we believe substantial moisture reduction would still be
required along with the use of light compaction construction equipment
Compaction should be accomplished by using sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact the
on site silty soils

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that cut
slopes would expose soils consisting of medium stiff to hard clayey silts In general
we believe that cut slopes into the soil material may be cut at an inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes constructed using the on site materials should be designed
with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter As discussed above fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance
with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet high should be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Track rolling
of slopes in excess of 5 feet high should not be accepted In the event that
over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be
implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes

3 2 Ground Settlement
Ground settlements are anticipated when new fills are placed over the existing
ground to raise the site to the proposed finished elevations These settlements will
affect the construction schedule and the earthwork quantity estimates Based on the
results of our exploration the anticipated ground settlements are primarily the result of
the following two processes
e Compression of the fill material under its own weight especially since the fill
materials are placed at a lower degree of compaction
e Consolidation of the in situ soils and previously placed fills induced by the
new fill loads
Based on our experience we estimate that settlements on the order of about 1 to
2 inches may occur for new fills up to 8 feet in thickness It should be noted that some of
the estimated ground settlements would occur as the fill loads are applied during the
earthwork operations
As previously indicated some settlement of the fill material under its own weight
should be expected due to the reduced compaction criteria for fill placement In order to
reduce the effects of the anticipated settlements on the future structures and other
improvements constructed on fills a settlement waiting period on the order of about
one month should be implemented after placement of the fills and prior to construction
of any improvements

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
placed directly around a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the base of
the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or Enkadrain may be used
instead of drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should also be
connected hydraulically to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall Unless covered
by concrete slabs the upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively
impervious material such as the on site silty soils to reduce the potential for
significant water infiltration behind the retaining wall

3 5 Drainage
Since the project site is in a high rainfall area special attention should be given
to site drainage during and after construction The finished grades adjacent to the
retaining wall structures should be sloped to shed water away from the foundations and
to reduce the potential for ponding
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be backfilled properly against the footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away
from walls and foundations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings each to a depth of approximately 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The borings were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger
equipment at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the field exploration operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Log Legend Plate A
Graphic representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
