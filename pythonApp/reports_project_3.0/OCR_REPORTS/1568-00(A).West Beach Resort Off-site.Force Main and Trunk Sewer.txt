Soil Engineering Investigation
West Beach Resort Off Site
Force Main and Trunk Sewer
Hawaii
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions along the proposed force main and trunk sewer alignment to
provide soil data for use in the design and construction of the proposed
project The approximate location of the alignment is shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
W O 1568 00 A Page 2
GENERAL PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed sewer will service the new West Beach Resort
Development The proposed alignment will extend from the proposed WWPS
No 1 to be covered in a separate report located on the western side of
the development site along Road A then cross the existing railroad
right of way between Roads E and F The alignment then parallels the
railroad right of way to Barbers Point NAS Access Road where it connects
to the existing Makakilo Interceptor Sewer
The first 9100 feet from the WWPS will be a 16 inch diameter force
main while the remaining portion will be a 30 inch gravity sewer
The pipe inverts will generally be less than 10 feet below the
existing ground surface
SITE DESCRIPTION
The alignment of the sewer line extends mostly through abandoned
and cultivated sugar cane fields Most of the alignment follows along an
existing railroad right of way which also includes an 8 inch fuel line

Four 4 borings were drilled along the portion of the sewer line
outside of the West Beach Resort Development area Four of the borings
drilled for the West Beach Resort mass grading design were also used for

Based on the borings field observations and general geology of the

sand or coral formation The cemented sands and coral formations are
generally hard however softer weathered zones are common

remaining borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generally relatively competent soils were encountered along the
proposed force main and trunk sewer alignment Stiff to dense cemented
sand and coral formation were encountered within the excavation depth for
the sewer
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
frictional resistance between the backfill material and the trench wall
To compute the vertical loads on the pipes a unit weight of 110 p c f
and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are recommended Earth forces upon
the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the trench Therefore the
width of the trench should be kept to a minimum The enclosed Plate 2
indicates three alternative types of trenches
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The cemented sand and coral soils encountered along the alignment
have good bearing capacity and therefore minimal settlements are
anticipated
The sewer line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface The

backfill will be essentially small and therefore significant

Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and therefore is
an important design consideration
A minimum of six 6 inches and ten 10 inches of compacted
granular bedding material should be placed below the 16 inch and 30 inch
diameter pipes respectively The granular bedding should extend beyond
the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter
and extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding material should
consist of well graded granular material such as 3 Fine gravel refer to
Plate 3 for other details

Due to the depth of the excavation anticipated shoring of the
trench walls are recommended to minimize caving in of the trench
walls If sufficient space is available the upper portion of the
excavation may be cut back at a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in
lieu of the above wall supports see Plate 2 for details
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches The ground adjacent to and near the top of the
excavation should continuously be monitored for cracks or dips and
other indications of movements and possible sloughing of the
trench walls This monitoring is particularly critical in the area
where structures or utilities are adjacent to the trench
excavation
Backfill
a8 Sa a
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction May 1975
We recommend that granular material such as sand 3 Fine gravel
or crusher waste should be used for the backfill up to about one
foot above the pipe to reduce the compactive effort required and
possible damage to the pipe We believe water tamping can be used
to compact the select granular backfill materials

Most of the excavated on site soils less than 8 inches in diameter
can be re used as the upper portion of the trench backfill from
above one foot over the pipe to within 2 feet of the finished
grade Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Below the roadway areas the backfill within 2 feet of the finished
grade should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the trench excavation and backfill be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 8
borings to 11 5 to 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 A truck mounted
drilling rig was used to drill the 7 10 and 17 were
drilled in conjunction with the soil investigation for the mass grading
of the West Beach developments Borings 29 through 32 were drilled for
the off site portions of the sewer
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the exploration operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Borings
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
