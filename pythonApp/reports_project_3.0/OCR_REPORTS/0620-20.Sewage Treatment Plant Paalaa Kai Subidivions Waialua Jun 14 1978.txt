INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation and percolation tests performed at the subject
site in Waialua Oahu Hawaii The purpose of the soil study
was to investigate subsurface conditions evaluate foundation
requirements and probable behavior of foundations for the
proposed plant The purpose of the percolation test was to
determine in situ subsurface drainage characteristics and
obtain percolation data for injection well design and con
struction

These conditions imposed geological constraints to safe

Water Resources Section 3 301 Waste Disposal Facilities
The use of injection wells at the northwestern section of

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site is located in the proposed Paalaa Kai Phase II
Subdivision which lies at the circular junction between
Kaukonahua Highway and Waialua Beach Road in Waialua Oahu
Hawaii The sewage disposal plant is located at the north
west corner of the subdivision fronting Waialua Beach Road
The area surrounding the sewage disposal plant is cultivated
with sugar cane generally flat with an average elevation
of 15 feet The ground slopes gently to the northwest
An unmaintained access road trending southeast runs through
the center of the plant site
The surface soil is reddish brown color stiff clay with
relatively poor drainage characteristic
Geology
The Waialua Haleiwa area geology is typified by a section
of recent alluvium and calcareous marine deposits overlying
the Koolau basalt basement rock Geologic maps of the
area and preliminary borings indicate the basement rock is
very shallow in the mauka south section of the subdivision
site The interbedding of alluvial deposits of differing
origins and the location of former ridges and stream valleys
make the geologic picture complex

W Ps
Generally in this area the basalt formation slopes down
toward the ocean so that the alluvium and marine materials
will be thickest at the northwestern end of the subdivision
site
Subsurface
Subsurface exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil
condition within the proposed sewage treatment plant area
The site is in general covered by a blanket of stiff
to very stiff brown silty clay layer approximately 10
feet thick This surface layer is underlain by a series
of medium dense to dense silty coral sands and gravels
to a depth of about 50 feet Below this relative porous
layer to the extent of the exploration are a series of
stiff clays and silts with some organic layers The deep
boring B 13 terminated at 115 3 feet below the existing
ground level did not encounter basalt
Groundwater was encountered in all of the bore holes and
injection wells within the plant site approximately 10
feet below the existing ground surface For details
refer to the enclosed boring logs in the appendix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the present plan minimum site grading is antici
pated for the STP site It is recommended that the struc
tures be supported on spread foundations bearing in the
stiff brown silty clay located at about 2 feet below the
existing ground surface
The site surface soil is highly expansive when remolded
dried and let saturated Lightly loaded slab on grade
should bear upon a minimum 2 foot thick of non expansive
select borrow layer
Foundations
The stiff to very stiff brown silty clay found from 2 feet
below the existing surface will provide adequate bearing
for building pump and tank foundations A design bearing
pressure of 2000 pounds per square foot dead plus live
loads may be used for footings placed a minimum of 24
inches below final adjacent grade A one third increase
of this value may be used when considering wind and seismic
loadings It is our recommendation that all footing exca
vation bottoms be tamped prior to placing steel or concrete
to ensure adequate uniform bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
For footing on compacted engineered fill a design bearing
pressure of 1500 pounds per square foot may be used and
placed a minimum of 24 inches below final grade
Lateral loads may be resisted by either passive pressures
developed by the soil or by friction along the base of
footings For design purposes the passive pressure of
soils may be assumed to be 250 pounds per square foot of
depth The coefficient of friction between the footings
and the soil will be 0 40
Due to the low moisture content of the site expansive
silty clay soil and its potential to heave when saturated
slab on grade should be designed for expansive soil condi
tion
It is our opinion that all footing excavations be inspected
by a qualified soils engineer to confirm the conditions on
which our recommendations are based
When encountered local soft areas should be compacted to
a minimum of 90 of maximum density
Settlements
Maximum ultimate settlements on the order of one quarter
of an inch may be expected for the design loadings
Differential settlements will be minimal

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade for buildings and tanks should not be supported
directly on the existing brown silty clay CH without a 2 foot
non expansive fill cushion below the base course and slab area
In the cut area where the slab subgrade bears on the brown
clay soil the subgrade should be over excavated and backfilled
with a 2 foot minimum non expansive select fill The select 3
fill could be coral sand quarry waste or aggregate Prior
to the placement of the select borrow the subgrade soil
should be kept moist
In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be unde
sirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are
to be used a minimum of 4 inches of gravel should be placed
beneath the slab to act as a cappillary break In addition
a moisture proof membrane should be placed over the gravel
to act as a vapor barrier The membrane should be covered
by one inch of sand to protect it during construction and
to aid in curing the concrete
The site soils are highly permeable and it is recommended
that surface runoff and gutter roof water be drained away
from the slab and foundation areas

Site Grading
It is estimated that the site grading for the sewage treat
ment plant area will probably be initiated at the time of
the Paalaa Kai Subdivision grading construction
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation
Prior to placement of compacted fill local soft spots and
existing drainage etruetunee encountered within building
areas should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
The upper 6 six inches of the existing ground surface
should be scarified brought to near optimum moisture
content and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D1557 Additional
fills should then be placed in 9 nine inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to the above specified density
We recommend that all site preparation and grading be done
under the continuous observation of a qualified soils engin
eer to ascertain that the organic and topsoils are removed
before fills are placed and to inspect that the fills are
compacted properly
Periodic density tests should be taken to assure that suffi
cient compaction density has been obtained in the subgrade
preparation and fill layers
All imported fill and select borrow materials should be
approved by the soils engineer prior to bringing on the site

INJECTION WELL AND PERCOLATION TESTS
a a a Na nen
One 9 inch diameter Well No 1 and two 6 inch diameter
Well auger borings were drilled down to 45
feet below the existing ground surface The drilled holes
were then cased with 4 inch diameter PVC pipe perforated
in the bottom 35 feet The drilled holes were backfilled
with fine gravel around the pipe Each well was developed
by first flushing and then pumping to clean out the fine
materials The locations of the wells are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
Percolation tests were performed on each of the three
wells by pumping water down the wells using a portable
2 inch water pump and a 2500 gallon water trucks Flow
rates were measured with an inline flow meter
Well accepted an injection rate of 95 g p m
for 10 minutes with a head buildup of 14 inches and 12
inches respectively Well No 1 overflowed at 95 g p m
so flow was restricted until a constant level was main
tained at the top of the casing The flow rate was then
determined to be 65 g p m

FIELD EXPLORATION
AND
LABORATORY TESTING
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
one deep boring to 115 3 feet and two shallow borings to 25 and 18
feet below the existing ground surface In addition one 9 inch and
two 6 inch diameter injection wells were drilled to a depth of 45
feet The location of the borings and injection wells are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer or geologist who continuously supervised
the drilling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the boring logs
Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depth No ring or penetration sampling was done
during the drilling of the injection wells however the drill cutting
were monitored to insure the subsurface conditions conformed to
previous boring logs
