This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing

Building, University of Hawaii at Manoa project from January 29, 2018 to May 13, 2020 in

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Life Sciences Building, University of Hawaii at Manoa project site is located 
at the old Henke Hall location in the central area of the Manoa Campus on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii.  The new building is approximately 70,000 square feet gross floor area 
and consists of a three-story concrete and steel-framed structure.  In addition, a borrow 
pit was excavated and backfilled south of Keller Hall. The Project Location Map and Site 
Plans are provided as Plates 1, 2 and 3, respectively. 
The site grading work consisted of cuts and fills up to about 8 feet within the 
building area and excavation and backfill up to about 22.5 feet at the borrow pit.  
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading and foundation construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING & FOUNDATION CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading and foundation construction work for the Life 
Sciences Building project from January 29, 2018 to May 13, 2020.  The site grading work 
consisted of placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 5 feet thick to 
achieve the finished grades for the project. Jayar Construction performed the site grading 
and foundation preparation work. During the site grading operations, we obtained samples 
of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported 
them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture 
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 4. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and foundation preparation work, periodic field density tests 
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 5.1 through 5.26 
and Plates 7.1 through 7.5 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared 
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on 
visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and foundation preparation work for 
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report 
were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the 
following references.
