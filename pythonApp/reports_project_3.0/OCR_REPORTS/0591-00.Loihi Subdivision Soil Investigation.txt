INTRODUCTION
The subsurface investigation was undertaken to define the
location and consistency of the soft soils in the low lying
areas and to provide samples for laboratory evaluation of
the load settlement characteristics of the compressible
soils
This finding were utilized to estimate the settlement rate
and magnitude and to evaluate the fill slope stability
_ during the critical construction period
Site Description
The proposed subdivision is to be located at the extreme
northern end of Loihi Street in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The
ground surface of the approximately 5 7 acre site drops from
elevation 164 feet along the western property line to approx
imately elevation 128 at the eastern property line adjacent
to the drainage channel
A large soil stockpile extending up to approximately eleva
tion 149 was placed in the center and southern portions of
the site
W 1975
The western one third is occupied by two wood frame dwellings
and miscellaneous small buildings This area is relatively
well drained and covered by grass shrubs and trees
The marshy eastern one third adjacent to the channel is grass
and weed covered and nearly level The stream flow was once
diverted into this low lying area to create a lily pond
Proposed Construction
The anticipated construction will consist of 28 residential
lots with the necessary streets drains etc The proposed
grading varies from approximately 2 feet of cut along the
western portion to 6 to 10 feet of fill along the eastern
property line
Previous Investigation
A subsurface investigation consisting of six test borings
was conducted in July 1973 to delineate subsurface condi
tions and provide recommendations for the proposed develop
ment

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is generally located within a wide drainage basin
The surface soils appear to be recent alluvium deposited in
the low lying areas by the present stream The recent allu
vium is generally underlain by an older alluvium and marine
clays deposited during periods of higher sea level
Subsurface Explorations
Two test borings were drilled at the locations shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 to provide information on the
soft soils underlying the lower portion of the site The
boring locations along with the locations of the previously
drilled borings have been superimposed upon the site plan
provided by Watson Lee Inc
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the lower site is
generally blanketed by a thick layer of gray clayey silt or
silty clay extending to approximately elevation 110 This
soil has a firm crust but is predominantly very soft to soft
in consistency and contains pockets of peaty soil and some
interspersed layers of loose decomposed rock The surface of
the low lying area is blanketed by 1 to 4 feet of organic
peaty material
At approximately elevation 110 the soil changes from the dark
gray soft deposits to a reddish brown clayey silt varying
from medium to stiff in consistency Decomposed rock and
boulders were encountered at approximately this level in the
previous investigation
A detailed description of the soils encountered within our
subsurface explorations is given in the appended boring logs
Groundwater
Groundwater was encountered at the time of our investigation
6 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The previous
investigation encountered water from 0 to 11 feet below the
ground surface

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The lower portion of the site is underlain by soft compress
a ible soils The weight of 6 to 10 feet of fill will result
in large settlements and high pore water pressures If the
f rate of fill placement is controlled the slopes should main
tain adequate stability and with proper drainage the consol
idation beneath the fill will occur within a reasonable
rl period
The recommended site preparation and construction procedures
are presented in detail in the following paragraphs Each
item must be properly implemented and closely controlled to
assure success
Site Preparation
Initially the site should be cleared of the surface vegeta
tion and the highly organic surface materials It is esti
mated that the undercutting of the organic materials will
average approximately six inches over fill portions of the
site It is anticipated that only limit area would be under
Lo cut more than six inches

The soil stockpile has apparently diverted the surface water
and permitted the lower portion of the site to develop a
firm soil crust This upper crust is relatively thin
generally less than 2 to 3 feet and does not have sufficient
strength to support heavy construction equipment For this
reason it is recommended that light equipment be utilized for
the initial site clearing
It appears more expedient to delay the clearing and grubbing
of the area beneath the stockpile until the lower area has
been completed and the drains installed The stockpile can
then be transferred on to the lower area to permit the grass
and other vegetation to be removed
If possible the construction operations should be conducted
during the dryer summer months when the soil crust should
have its greater strength Extended periods of percipitation
such as occurs in this vicinity during the rainy season can
soften the soil crust until it would no longer support even
lightly loaded equipment
Subsurface Drains
As the weight of the newly placed fill compresses the under
lying saturated soils water is forced from the pores of the
XN
W 1975
soil The permeability of the soil limits the rate at which
this water can escape and produces high pore water pressures
The magnitude of the pore water pressure is a measure of the
percent consolidation The more rapid the dissipation of the
pore water pressure the faster the settlements will occur
A drainage blanket with perimeter and internal drains will be
required to facilitate the drainage which in turn will acce
lerate the settlements and decrease the time period necessary
to reach equilibrium Prior to the drain installations the
surface of the site should be graded to remove irregularities
and to slope the terrain toward the proposed drain locations
Drainage Blanket and Perimeter Drain
The construction details for the perimeter drain and the
drainage bianket are shown on the enclosed Plate 2 The
perimeter drain to be located beneath the top of the fill
embankment slope protects the toe area from excessive pore
water pressures This drain should have a minimum depth of
four feet a minimum width of one foot and contain a 4 inch
perforated pipe
The drainage blanket and drains should be constructed with
granular material Tess than 1 inch maximum diameter and with
less than 10 percent passing a No 200 sieve By placing a
om
W 1975
layer of Mirafi 140 Fabric below the drainage blanket the
blanket thickness can be reduced to 12 inches Without the
Mirafi Fabric the blanket thickness should be 18 inches
Interior Drains
Plate 3 illustrates the construction details for the interior
drains Their approximate locations are shown on Plate 4
The interior drain consists of a shallow trench approximately
12 inches by 12 inches containing a 4 inch perforated pipe
The perforated pipe is utilized to collect and remove water
from the drainage blanket and the interior portions of the fill
Construction Instrumentation
Following installation of the basic site drainage it is
recommended that three settlement gauges and two piezometers
be installed to monitor the pore water pressures and surface
settlements The pore water pressure buildup will control
the rate of fill placement due to its effect on the stability
of the slopes
The settlement gauge installations can be part of the contract
for site preparation and fill placement Due to the complex
ity and sensitivity of the piezometer installtions it is
recommended that they be installed and monitored by a
W 1975
soils engineer The monitoring of the settlement gauges
can be performed in cooperation with Watson Lee Inc
The fill operations can be started upon completion of the
instrumentation and the site drainage It appears that the
stockpile should be first utilized in the low lying area
This material should be placed in maximum six inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry
density established by American Society for Testing and Mate
rials ASTM Test Designation D1557
It is estimated that the fill placement can proceed in the
low lying areas at a rate of approximately two feet per week
The actual rate will be established during the construction
operations by evaluating the settlement gauges and piezo
meter readings
After the stockpile and on site cuts are utilized additional
off site borrow will be required It is recommended that
this material be tested and approved prior to transportation
to the site to assure that it will be suitable for the pro
posed construction The material should be non expansive

deleterious materials

Settlements
Based upon our laboratory testing of the soil samples obtained
from Borings 1 and 2 it is estimated that the primary con
solidation at the site under the proposed fill loadings will
be approximately seven inches providing that the near surface
peaty deposits are removed With the peaty material in place
the total settlements could increase to approximately ten
inches The additional three inches of settlement would be
long term secondary compression
With the drainage blankets described above it is estimated
that approximately 7 to 10 months will be required to obtain
a 90 consolidation level The remaining consolidation
estimated to be a maximum of one inch would occur over an
extended period
As previously recommended the piezometers and settlement
gauges should be monitored during the construction program to
prevent the buildup of excessive pore water pressures while
maintaining the maximum construction rate Upon completion

The information obtained from the monitoring program will

the recommended point at which the construction of the surface
improvements can be undertaken
These recommendations apply to the lower area below the stock
pile Construction in the upper and stockpile areas can be
initiated upon completion of the site grading
Following the 7 to 10 month waiting period during which the
consolidation of the underlying materials take place the
construction in the lower area can be initiated for the
street drains and other improvements sensitive to vertical
movements
Slopes
The stability of the perimeter slopes is a direct function of
the magnitude of pore water pressures within the underlying
soils It is estimated from our laboratory testing that the
construction rate of approximately two feet per week will not
develop excessive pore water pressures and will maintain a
od

of safety will increase with the dissipation of the pore
water pressures
Utilities
Utilities construction should be delayed until the 90 con
solidation level is achieved Pipes placed within the fill
after this time should be designed for a maximum total
settlement of approximately one inch This settlement should
be a relatively uniformed movement with maximum differentials
of approximately inch in 25 feet
The recommendations for the pipe support headwalis and other
special requirements will be covered in a separate report
following a review of the proposed construction plans It
is anticipated that additional rock bedding will be required
in the areas where the pipe will be supported upon the soft
soils below the fill
Pile or other special foundations may be required for the
sewer crossing at the drainage channel These items will be
covered in detail in the supplemental report

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
ing two borings utilizing portable auger equipment The
depths of the borings varied from 15 5 to 22 0 feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings are
shown on the enclosed Site Plan Pilate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer or geologist who continuously
supervised the drilling operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and
A 2
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring
logs at the appropriate sample depth
