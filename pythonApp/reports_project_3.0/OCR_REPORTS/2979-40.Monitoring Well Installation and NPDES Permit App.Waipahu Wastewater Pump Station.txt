This letter report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services in
support of the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES construction
dewatering permit application for the subject project Our work was performed in general
accordance with our revised fee proposal dated The analytical
laboratory testing of the groundwater sample was performed by Environmental Laboratory

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located adjacent to Waipahu Depot Road in the
Waipahu area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to expand
and improve the existing Waipahu Wastewater Pump Station to meet the growing needs
for increased sewage in the Waipahu area
We understand that various modifications to the existing pump station are desired
Based on information provided we understand that an underground fuel storage tank located
adjacent to the proposed new emergency generator building will be constructed at a depth
of approximately 12 to 15 feet below the design finished grades Therefore an excavation
on the order of about 18 to 20 feet below the design finished grades and subsequent
dewatering will be required in order to install the proposed underground fuel storage tank
We understand that steel sheet piles will be used for construction of the underground fuel
storage tank
Because the excavation dewatering may involve the discharge of groundwater
effluent into an adjacent storm drainage system an NPDES permit may be required In
order to obtain general baseline groundwater quality data for the preparation of the permit
application it was desired to sample and test the groundwater underlying the subject site
For this purpose a monitoring well was installed to a depth of approximately 20 feet below
the existing ground surface for the collection of water samples for NPDES permit application

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this project was to provide drilling sampling and
monitoring well installation services and consultation to facilitate the preparation of the
NPDES permit application for this project Our scope of work generally consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and two operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of one borehole to a depth of approximately 20 feet below
the existing ground surface
3 Installation of a 2 inch diameter Schedule 40 PVC pipe for the monitoring well
consisting of approximately 10 feet of screen with 0 020 inch opening and
10 feet of solid casing
4 Sampling of groundwater from the monitoring well after a minimum of 5 days
following well installation The groundwater sampling was performed by a field
engineer geologist from our office and a new disposable bailer was used to
collect samples from the well
5 Analytical laboratory testing of the groundwater samples for the following NOI
Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents and NOI Part B Toxic Constituents
as directed by the owner client
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings in support of the preparation of the NPDES permit
applications for the project
7 Coordination consultation and meetings during preliminary and final design
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Monitoring Well Installation
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted on
November 8 and 9 1999 The groundwater monitoring well consisted of 2 inch diameter
Schedule 40 PVC pipes installed to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground
surface The well generally consisted of about 10 feet of screen with 0 020 inch opening and
10 feet of solid casing The surface completion consisted of an 8 inch well cover that was
cemented flush with the existing ground surface
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck mounted drill rig
associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned prior to the drilling of the borehole
The monitoring well is identified as Monitoring Well No and the approximate
location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A detail of the monitoring well installation is
provided as Plate 3 of this report
Subsurface Conditions
The monitoring well was installed in the area of the proposed underground fuel storage
tank Subsurface materials were examined and classified by a geologist from our firm
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths

Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured at the project site Due to
the relatively close proximity of the project site to the coastline it is likely that groundwater
levels measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level In
addition the groundwater levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation and storm surge conditions
Based on our review of the analytical laboratory results it appears that no significant
adverse water quality conditions were detected in the analyses of the water samples that
were tested The water sample test results are provided in Appendix B and include the
Department of Health NPDES NOI Form A and the official laboratory report
Most of the test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents
appear to be within normal values for typical brackish groundwater For the NOI Form A
Part B Toxic Constituents cadium lead benzene toluene ethyl benzene xylene cyanide
and other analytes were tested The test results were generally reported as non detect
which means the sample results were below the reporting limit for the particular test method
and analyte unless noted otherwise
The reporting limit is dependent on the testing equipment and the statistical control
applied by the laboratory to the test results Therefore laboratories adopting the same
standard test method may have different reporting limits
Abandonment of Monitoring Well
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the monitoring well be included in the
construction documents In general the monitoring well should be properly abandoned and
sealed with cement grout

LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from the field monitoring well Variations of conditions beyond the monitoring well may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the variations
