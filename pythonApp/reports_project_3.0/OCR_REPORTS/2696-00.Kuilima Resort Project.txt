Site and Subsurface Conditions
The proposed site is located on a slope which grades from approximately 146 feet
above mean sea level on the upper South side to approximately 114 feet above mean
se level on the lower north side
An engineer from our firm observed the excavation of three 3 test pits at the
project location to a maximum depth of five feet The locations of the test pits are shown
on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
The test pit exploration indicated that the site is generally underlain by a surface
layer of expansive adobe clays with some cobbles to a depth of about three to five feet
below the existing subgrade overlying recent colluvium consisting boulders in a clay
matrix
Discussion and Recommendations
Grading Considerations
A review of the proposed grading plan for the project site indicates that a 2 1 H V
slope is to be cut into the existing slope above the pad for the well head and the eastern
portion of the service road Due to the nature of the soils which are anticipated to be
exposed along the face of the cut slope i e expansive clays we recommend that the
2H 1V slope grade be flattened to 3 1 H V to increase the stability of the clayey soils and
to reduce the potential for future slope failure

Structural Considerations
Due to the critical expansive nature of the surface soils adobe clays encountered
at the project site we believe that special subgrade preparation procedures should be
considered for the concrete lined interceptor ditch the service road the concrete pad at
the well head and the drain line traversing the site
Service Road Extension
Based upon the subgrade conditions the following pavement section design is
recommended for the service road extension
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
18 Inch Select Borrow Subbase
26 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on
on compacted subgrade
The subgrade for the service road extension should be compacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The base course
and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of their maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that the slab on grade at the well head be over excavated to
a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the bottom of the slab subgrade and the
exposed subgrade soils be moisture conditioned to 2 to 3 on the wet side of the
optimum moisture content to reduce the potential for future uplifting upon the structure
due to the expansion of the subgrade soils when wetted and compacted to 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The over
excavated soils should be replaced with non expansive select borrow material compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 A
thickened edge with an embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the adjacent subgrade
is also recommended to reduce water intrusion under the slab
