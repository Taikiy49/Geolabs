SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development site is located near the eastern
side of the intersection of Mokapu Saddle Road and Kaneohe
Bay Drivein Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The site is bordered on
the northwest by adjacent residential lots and on the south
west by a strip of land beyond which lies Mokapu Saddle Road
The remainder of the site s perimeter is bordered by undeve
loped land
The site is basically situated on the side slope of a large
ridge On this slope surface three smaller secondary ridges
are present upon which the development is planned to be lo
cated At the base of these smaller ridges the site is
relatively level The proposed access drive is planned to
be situated along this level area

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The ridge on which the project site is situated represents
a once continuous larger ridge that extended down from the
Koolau mountain range This larger ridge was primarily
formed by stream erosion on the Koolau Shield Volcano
The present locations of the ridge and site lies within the
limits of the ancient Koolau Caldera crater In and around
the caldera area steam and other hot volcanic gasses lead
to a relatively rapid weathering of the rock masses resulting
in thick residual soil formations Because of these thick
soil formations and steep slopes in certain areas it is not
uncommon to encounter soils which resulted from previous land
slides and other soil movements of various magnitudes
Encountered at the site is a combination of both transported
soils and residual soils showing the continual weathering
and eroding process

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Except for the upper near surface material the field
exploration disclosed stiff to very stiff clayey silt resi
dual soil materials at the proposed project site

From a soil engineering point of view the site could be
developed as planned provided the project design and con
struction incorporates the recommendations presented in this
report
Spread or pier footing foundations can be used to support
the proposed parking and townhouse structures Deeper
footing embedment would be required where they are situated
on steep sloping slide and existing gully areas
The near surface and underlying soils are relatively dry and
have high expansion potential when used as a recompacted fill
These site soils when exposed on steep sloping topography
can erode readily especially if subject to intense rainfall
and run off Proper site drainage ditch should be provided
to intercept the up slope run off
Adequate site drainage and slope planting will be required
Soil stabilization measures are recommended for the recent
landslide and talus slope areas
The near surface and underlying soils being quite expansive
will require additional design and construction procedures
for slab on grade construction It could be disposed of in
the deeper portion of the site fill
It is essential that the unstable areas be closely inspected

exist within the unstable areas prior to placing compacted

Continuous inspection and testing during construction for
the stabilizing fill and building construction are critical
for the future project performance
Site Preparation and Earthwork
Cuts and fills generally less than about 12 feet are anti
cipated for the general project site grading Based on the
boring data relatively easy site excavation can be antici
pated except in the deeper utility excavations where weathered
rock may be encountered Decomposed rock formation may be
encountered at relatively shallow depths near the existing
gully and ancient slide areas
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris first
moisture conditioned then scarified and recompacted to 90
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density

W O 677 10 P ll
For slopes within and in the vicinity of recent landslide
and talus slope areas the loose soil should be undercut and
backfilled with select on site material The removal and
backfill details are shown on Plates 2 and 3
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to properly
bond the new fill to the existing slopes
The filling operation should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with the
above structural fill requirements For construction details
refer to Plate 4
The on site excavated soils can be used as a source of fill
material However they have moderate to high swell potential
and should be compacted 2 wet of optimum moisture content to
reduce the swelling potential
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is re
quired it should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to transportation to the site to assure that it is
suitable for the proposed construction The material should
generally be non expansive and should not contain large per
centage of boulders organic or deleterious matter

Slopes
The on site soils are generally silty and very erosive as
evidenced by the existing eroded steep cut bank along Mokapu
Saddle Road and along the southern property boundary
It is also difficult to mulch or plant these slopes if they
are cut too steep and this will also increase erosion poten
tial For these reasons 2 horizontal to 1 vertical cut and
fill slopes are recommended for the site
All 1i11 slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and cut
back to obtain a well compacted slope All fill slopes should
be properly keyed and benched as outlined above and as shown
on the enclosed Plate 4 titled General Grading Recommenda
tions Typical Fill On Natural Slope
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Recent Landslide Area
The upper near surface clayey silt soils which range from one
foot in Probe No 1 to about 7 feet in Probe No 2 are a
part of the unstable landslide mass The underlying residual
soils appear to be relatively stable at the time of our recon
naissance The present grading required to accomodate the
parking and townhouse structure will provide large cuts into
the recent landslide area and thereby will remove substantially
the upper unstable layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 677 10 P 13
New fill required should be constructed by proper keying and
benching operations in accordance with the procedures out
lined above in this report and as shown on Plate 4
This talus slope area appeared to be underlain by a very highly
weathered residual soil near the slope surface with a thin
one to four feet layer of transported soils covering the
existing surface in this general slope area
Major cuts into the hillside are planned in this area thereby
removing most of the upper unstable surface layer We recom
mend that they be closely monitored by a soils engineer
during grading construction to determine the extent of the
surface soil removal above the cut and to inspect if planes
of weakness may exist within the residual soils Special sta
bilization procedures such as removal of the unstable materials
from the slope and backfilling with new material by properly
keying and benching into the stable slope might be required if
other unstable layers are detected

Site Drainage
The existing drainage gully on the southeastern lower portion
of the site collects the surface run off from the ridge top
between talus slope and ancient slide area and drains to
wards Mokapu Road drainage exit
Prior to construction the run off from the upper portion of
the ridge should be intercepted and channeled to a suitable
outlet To minimize erosion water should not be allowed to
run over slope surface or pond near foundations Drainage
swale should be provided around the structures to shed water
away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the structures on level or gently sloping areas An allowable
bearing value up to 3000 p s f is recommended for footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 677 10 P 15
bearing on the stiff residual soil and 2500 ps f for footings
bearing on compacted fill The above values are for total
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary loads are considered
On level areas the bottom of the above designed footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside
finished grade to resist erosion and to reduce the effeets
of the expansive site soils
To develop the bearing capacity of the footings located on
or near the tops of steep slopes the footings should be
embedded an additional amount to help resist the downhill
slope creep movements We recommend that for the footings
on slopes a minimum horizontal set back distance of six
6 0 feet be maintained between the outer edge of footing
and the outer slope oraminimum of 3 foot depth
Anticipated settlements for foundations bearing on natural
ground or on properly compacted fill are estimated to be on
the order of half an inch and one quarter inch differential
Foundations resting upon cut and fill should be avoided if
practicable Where the structures will be located partly
on fill and cut 12 inch over excavations and recompaction
to 95 density would be required at the footing excavations
on cut condition

Basement and Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that parking structure basement
retaining walls generally less than 8 feet high would be
used at the project The recommendations under FOUNDATIONS
should be followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls up to 8 feet high should be designed to resist
the following lateral earth pressures
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should be inclu
ded where applicable
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundations
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can
be used However the passive resistance should be reduced
by one third when combined with the frictional resistance
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used to
prevent the build up of excessive hydrostatic pressure behind
the walls
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls

W O 677 10 P 17
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet of
the backfill
Slab On Grade
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have moderate swell potential therefore slab on grade if
used should have deep perimeter footings extending down a
minimum of 18 inches below the finish grade to reduce ground
movement due to drying and wetting around the slab A hori
zontal set back distance of six 6 0 feet between the
outer edge of slab footings and the outer slope face should
be maintained
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry density
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations When a
slab is located below the outside ground surface a sub
drainage system around the perimeter of the walls is recom
mended to relieve seepage water

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED HAELANI TERRACE DEVELOPMENT
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
III Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
236
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
V Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
VI Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
