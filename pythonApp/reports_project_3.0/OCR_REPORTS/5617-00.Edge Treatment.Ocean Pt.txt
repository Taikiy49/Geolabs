Edge Treatment Recommendations
Marina at Ocean Pointe
Dear Mr Kanna
Recommendations for the cut along the edge of the marina are presented in this
letter These recommendations are based on borings drilled adjacent to the western
marina edge and discussions with the marina design team
The western portion of the marina was excavated in hard coral The side slopes
along the marina edge were cut at variable slopes up to about one and one half
horizontal to one vertical For safety and aesthetic reasons a very steep
slope along the marina edge is desired Due to the competency of the coral formation a
vertical cut slope has been used in the preliminary design of the marina edge treatment
To allow for variability of the coral formation and some future erosion we previously
recommended that the design of the edge treatment incorporate an allowance for
possible sloughing of the vertical slope to a plane The planned steep edge
condition generally consists of a vertical cut from elevation 5 or 6 down to 5 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Portions of the cut will be covered with a concrete cantilever
promenade slab while the remaining area will be graded level for a short distance then
at a low slope ratio
Five borings were drilled along the western marina edge each to a depth of
about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The general location and project
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The borings encountered coral formation consisting of hard coral and cemented
reef rock with zones of less cemented sand and gravel A boring log legend and logs of
borings are attached as Plate 3 and Plates 4 thru 8 respectively Photographs of the
cores recovered are presented on Plates 9 through 13

The boring information confirmed the preliminary design assumptions The coral
formation is generally hard and competent but with zones of less cemented sand and
gravel Therefore the planned vertical cut could be used however sloughing of less
cemented zones should be anticipated Over time the vertical cut face may not
maintain a smooth surface and will appear more irregular We recommend that the
design assumption of a long term slope be maintained We do not anticipate
that most of the vertical cut face will progress back to a slope but portions of
the cut slope may revert to this flatter slope over time
