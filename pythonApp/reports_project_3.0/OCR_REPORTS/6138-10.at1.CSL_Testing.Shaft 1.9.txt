CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using 
the CHAMP-Q CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Pile Dynamics, Inc. (PDI). 
Crosshole sonic logging tests were performed between all the possible access tube 
combinations in the drilled shaft (four tubes resulting in six profiles). The northernmost 
access tube was designated as Access Tube No. 1, and subsequent tubes were 
numbered in a clockwise direction. 
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 0.74 feet 
above the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for Shaft No. 1.9. We 
understand that potable water was used to fill the access tubes after concrete placement. 
Based on our observation in the field, the water levels within the access tubes were above 
the top of the shaft concrete at the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the 
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. The 
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, are presented on 
Plate 2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on Plate 3. The 
individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile) and a plan showing the 
tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.6. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10%, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less 
than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all profiles for Shaft No. 1.9 
except for the areas within the profile as described below. It should be noted that changes 
in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of the 
reduction and the associated increase in FAT. 
Profile 1-3: FAT increases up to 12.8 percent from approximately 9.1 to 
10.3 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 3-4: FAT increases up to 17.8 percent from approximately 7.3 to 
9 feet below the top of concrete. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for 
Shaft No. 1.9 ranged from approximately 11,588 to 14,659 feet per second. We 
understand that during the concrete operations, the drilled shaft consisted of an overpour 
extending approximately to the road surface, and concrete chipping will need to be 
performed down to the shaft cutoff elevation of approximately -0.52 feet Mean Sea Level. 
Based on our review of the CSL testing data, we recommend performing concrete integrity 
coring for Shaft No. 1.9. 
We recommend that the integrity coring be conducted per project specifications 
between Access Tube Nos. 3 and 4, extending a minimum depth of 4 feet below the shaft 
cutoff elevation. Core samples may be selected for unconfined compressive strength 
testing.
