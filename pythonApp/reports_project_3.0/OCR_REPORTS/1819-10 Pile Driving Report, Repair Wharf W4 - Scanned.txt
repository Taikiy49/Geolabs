Pile Driving Report
PCAS Services
Repair Wharf w4
Naval Magazine
In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated we have observed the pile driving and a
portion of the predrilling for the test piles and production piles of the
proposed fender system at Wharf W4
A Vulcan 01 pile hammer was used for test pile and production pile
driving for the eighteen inch square precast and prestressed concrete
fender piles A total of forty six 46 piles which included five 5
test piles and forty one 41 production piles were driven to the general
depths of about 46 to 70 feet below the design cut off elevation at about
108 9 feet U S Navy Datun
The pile locations were generally predrilled to a depth of about 55
to 60 feet prior to pile driving However where a hard layer was
encountered at shallower depths the predrilling was stopped after about
1 foot penetration into the hard layer
The locations of all the piles are shown on the attached Pile
Locations Plan
Based on our observations we believe that both the production and
test piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to
support the weight of the piles and provide the design lateral capacity
