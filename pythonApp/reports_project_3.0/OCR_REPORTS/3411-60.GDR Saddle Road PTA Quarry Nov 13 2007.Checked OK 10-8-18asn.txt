PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
This project is part of the overall Saddle Road improvements project As part of
the development efforts of the Saddle Road improvements project it is planned to
evaluate the basaltic rock materials at the designated quarry location within the
Pohakuloa Training Area PTA for use as aggregate materials The PTA Quarry is in the
vicinity of about one half of a mile south of Mile Marker 42 along the existing
Saddle Road The PTA Quarry location is about 45 miles from the Town of Hilo
Based on the Statement of Work SOW dated May 23 2007 a total of 25 borings
will be drilled to a maximum depth of about 35 feet below the existing ground surface A
geologist will observe the drilling and sampling operations and maintain a log of the
materials encountered in each boring In addition photographs of the rock cores
obtained from the borings and the drill rig set up at each boring location will be taken

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work is to provide geotechnical drilling and sampling services
In order to accomplish this we conducted a program generally consisting of the following
tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig presently on the
Island of Hawaii and two operators from Honolulu to the site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of 25 borings extending to depths of about 30 0 to
41 5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of 928 lineal feet of
drilling and sampling
3 Logging of the borings by our geologist
4 Placing and labeling of rock core samples in waxed core boxes
5 Photographing of the drill rig set up and rock core samples collected during
our drilling and sampling operations
6 Drafting of each boring log record using the standard gINT computer
program

findings

FIELD EXPLORATION
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
25 borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 127 and 129
extending to depths ranging from about 30 0 to 41 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings drilled at the site generally encountered clinker consisting of medium
dense basaltic boulders cobbles and gravel extending to depths of about 3 to 24 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Basalt formation with occasional layers of clinker was
encountered below the surface clinker and extended to the maximum depths explored
The basalt formation encountered in the borings generally was unweathered and the
relative hardness ranged from hard to very hard

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling 25 borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 127 and 129 extending to depths of about 30 0
to 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 We drilled the borings using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with HQ
wireline coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 25
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the project site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would
be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
