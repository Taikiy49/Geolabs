WATER TANK PAD COMPACTION REPORT
STABLE WATER TANK
Dear Mr Shibuya
As requested we observed the water tank pad preparation and fill placement at the
site on an intermittent basis from January 2009 to February 2009 This letter summarizes
our observations and compaction test results
SITE GRADING
The project involves the construction of a concrete reservoir tank The tank
pad was filled about 2 feet above the existing ground
Prior to filling the existing ground was scarified and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
The fill materials consisted of base course and were moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion the site grading work and pad preparation for the water tank pad observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations presented in our
geotechnical engineering report and are satisfactory for the tank construction
