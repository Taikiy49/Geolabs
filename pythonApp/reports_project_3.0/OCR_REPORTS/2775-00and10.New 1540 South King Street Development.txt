INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new development at 1540 South King Street Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
The initial exploration of the subsoil conditions at the site was performed on
and 18 1991 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated September
4 1991 to the Painters Union c o Spectrum Property and our recommendations for the
drilling of additional borings described in our preliminary report dated
An additional exploration by excavating test pits was also performed in accordance with
our proposal dated to KOP Hawaii Inc to explore the actual location
and configuration of the easterly side of an existing underground box culvert along the
western project boundary of the site
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory tests and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from analyses of the data obtained These recommendations are provided as information
for the designer s use and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two level masonry building at the project site The
ground level will comprise a mechanical room an entryway stairwell and an asphaitic
parking area while the second level will be used for office conference and training space

The project site is essentially flat Minimal site grading is anticipated to be required
for the proposed construction

occupies a lot covering approximately 6 673 square feet 0 153 acre between the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation Design
Based on the generally competent subsurface materials existing at the project site
it is our opinion that spread and or continuous footings may be used to support th
proposed building Deep embedment of the footing foundations is recommended on the
side of the building next to the underground box culvert while a shallow footing
embedment of twenty four 24 inches is considered to be sufficient on the other three 3
sides
W O Page 8
Footing Capacity
We believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used to
design both the shallow and deep footing foundations for the proposed building
The allowable bearing value is for total dead and live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for short term loads such as those caused by
seismic activity and or wind A total footing settlement of less than one 1 inch
and a differential footing settlement of less than one half 1 2 inch should be
anticipated
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
We recommend that all footing excavations be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed building may be obtained by passive
earth pressure and frictional resistance developed against the embedded building
footing foundations We recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth be used to determine the passive earth pressure developed
against the embedded footings A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to
compute the frictional resistance developed between the footing bottoms and the
recompacted footing subgrade

Footing Embedment and Set Back
As noted above the footings on the north east and south sides of the building
may be embedded at relatively shallow depths at twenty four 24 inches below the
adjacent finish grade The embedment and location of the footings on the western
side of the building next to the box culvert are however influenced by the location
and type of construction of the underground easterly culvert wall and the nature
of the subsurface conditions in its immediate vicinity
Taped measurement of the easterly culvert wall in the test pits excavated indicates
its base is located at a depth of approximately 12 feet and a distance of
approximately thirty 30 inches to the east of the approximate edge of drainage
easement shown on the cited topographic plan provided to us Assuming that the
excavation fox construction of the culvert was sloped not steeper than one to one
1 1 slope ratio the backfill would extend at the surface a distance of at least
14 5 feet from the cited easement line
The recommendations for embedment of the deep footings are governed by this
assumed extent of the poorly compacted backfill the apparent poor condition of
the CRM wall and the following additional considerations
1 The nature and degree of uncertainty regarding factors governing the
recommendations necessitate a conservative design i e one with a low
degree of risk
2 The current building code requires that if footings are embedded less than
twelve 12 feet below the existing ground surface the expense of

underpinning of such footings that may be necessitated by adjacent
excavation must be borne by the owner of the footings
3 Such an excavation may be performed during the anticipated life of the

4 The outer edge of the footings should be located no closer than 1 5 feet
from the base of the easterly culvert wall to allow for possible repair or

Therefore we recommend that the footings along the western edge of the building
be embedded to the same elevation of the bottom of the easterly culvert wall and or
located below an imaginary plane extending from the eastern edge of the base of the
easterly culvert wall
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction on this generally
flat lot except for the excavations required for the deep footing foundation construction
recommended adjacent to the existing underground box culvert
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the building grading limits should
be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable material should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and completion of any excavation that may be required the resulting
surface should be scarified to a depth of at least six 6 inches and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 11
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to receive the pavement for the
ground floor parking area for the proposed building
Soft loose spots encountered at the prepared grade level should be removed and
replaced with material compacted to 95 maximum dry density as indicated above
If required fill materials should be compacted in loose level lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches in loose thickness to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
to receive the parking pavement
The excavated on site soils may be used for backfill and fill material except that
concrete fragments and similar material such as that encountered in Boring No 3 should
be used
Excavation Precautions
Excavation in the subsoils encountered in our borings by utilizing conventional
earthmoving equipment should present no untoward difficulty The method and
equipment to be used for excavation should be left to the choice of the contractor within
practical limits and safety considerations
It is anticipated that excavations on the order of 12 feet deep will be required to
construct the footings adjacent to the box culvert Although there may be sufficient room
to excavate the ground at the deep footing locations to sufficiently flat and stable slopes
the contractor may elect to utilize vertical or near vertical excavations We believe that
shoring will be required to ensure the stability of the walls of these excavations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 12
The adequacy and safety of the shoring system if used must comply with all
applicable regulations and or safety requirements The design installation performance
and removal of the shoring system should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor His qualified representative who should be required to be continuously
present at the site will have the best opportunity to promptly observe any unanticipated
or changing conditions during construction such as subsoils differing from those
encountered during our exploration unexpectedly high groundwater table unsuitable
construction sequence or techniques etc which might affect the stability of the shoring
It must also be noted that some movement of the shoring system and thus the
adjacent ground might occur because earth stresses will change during excavation The
magnitude of movement depends greatly on workmanship the subsoil conditions and
the size and rate of excavation and is thus very difficult to predict with accuracy
Therefore it is important to realize that the shoring to support the excavation
should be installed properly and as early as practicable and that the adjacent ground
should be continuously monitored for cracks dips or other indications of movement
Backfill
Backfill of excavations within two feet of the box culvert should consist of No 3B
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel This material is generally self compacting so
that mechanical tamping can be held to a minimum to preclude excessive forces on the
easterly culvert wall
If large excavations are used at deep footing locations the on site sandy and
Clayey silts may be used as a source of backfill for up to approximately two 2 feet from
the adjacent culvert wall except that concrete fragments and similar material such as that
encountered in Boring No 3 should not be used The on site backfill should be

compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined by the
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any additional fill material required should be select non expansive material with
a maximum particle size of three 3 inches and having not more than twenty 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
Excavation Monitoring
As the project will involve deep excavation adjacent to existing developments we
believe that excavation monitoring including field observations and engineering
supervision should be made an essential part of the project It is our opinion that a
properly executed excavation monitoring program may significantly reduce the potential
of jeopardizing the existing box culvert and the book and record store adjacent to the site
The extent of excavation monitoring required may vary with individual contractors
However we believe that the following items of work should be considered
Prior to Excavation
1 A damage survey including photographs and crack surveys of the existing
theater and book and record store buildings should be conducted prior to
the commencement of construction
2 Settlement points should be installed along the westerly property line
adjacent to the deep footing excavations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 14
During Excavation and Backfilling
1 The contractor s field engineer should frequently observe the conditions of
the existing adjacent structures for signs of distress and obtain data from
the installed settlement points on a regular basis such as daily The
recorded data should be immediately reviewed and interpreted and any
unusual findings should be immediately reported to the contractor s project
superintendent who should execute necessary remedial actions at his
judgement and also inform the project geotechnical engineer without delay
2 A qualified geotechnical engineer should be hired by the contractor to see
that the following critical items of work are performed properly
a All shoring and bracing if used should be installed properly in a
timely fashion during excavation operations to reduce the potential
of adjacent ground movement
b All excavation backfill should be placed and properly compacted
prior to removal of shoring within the completed area
Cc All backfill should be tested for suitability prior to placement Field
density tests should be performed on the compacted backfill to verify
the degree of compaction
Post Construction Monitoring
