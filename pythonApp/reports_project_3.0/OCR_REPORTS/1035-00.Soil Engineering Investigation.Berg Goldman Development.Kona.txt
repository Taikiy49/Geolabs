The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site in order to provide data for use in design and con
struction of the building foundations The work was performed

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along the western side of Alii
Drive north of Kahakai Road The site is rectangular in
shape with a frontage of about 265 feet along Alii Drive
and lot depths of about 280 to 300 feet
The existing ground surface generally slopes down toward the
west It appears that some cobble and boulder fill has been
placed over the sloping site to form two relatively level
terraces
The eastern portion contains a miniature golf course two
concrete siabs and two small wooden structures one of which
is two stories high The site elevation is approximately 34 feet
above sea level The western half is approximately 25 feet

and weeds
PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed development consists of 2 levels of parking over
most of the site and two 3 story masonry structures approxi
mately semi circular in shape over the parking level Each
building will contain about 12 units per floor
Along Alii Drive higher portion of the site the two level
parking would require basement excavation at a depth of about
18 feet In the western half of the site lower area base
ment excavation would not be required

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered dense lava rock formation
below the surface fill layer of boulders and cobbles It
appears that most of this surface boulder and cobble fill
will be removed during the basement level excavation There
fore spread or continuous footing foundation can bear on the
underlying dense rock formation
Very hard rock excavation is anticipated for the proposed
deep basement cuts
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the in situ
rock formation is recommended for the proposed structures
The existing boulder fill contains many voids and is not recomn
mended for structural support
For the proposed structures an allowable bearing value of
10 000 p s f can by used on the dense rock formations The
bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be increased
one third when temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are
considered

P 5
Footings should be a minimum of 12 inches wide
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundation
Minimum settlements are anticipated for the proposed founda
tions bearing on the dense rock formation
Although large cavities were not noted in the borings cav
ities may be present in the rock formation
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to pos
sible large cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava
rock major footing locations should be probed The probe
holes could be drilled with a track percussion drill The
probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
should extend below the bottom of footing a depth equal
to two times the footing width or a minimum of 5 feet
The probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the
hole
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly and to locate any potential

Site Grading
Minimal filling is anticipated for the project
Deep cuts up to 18 feet are planned for the eastern half
of the site for the basement parking levels Most of the
excavation will be in dense rock formation therefore very
hard ripping or blasting is anticiapated
It is estimated that a small percentage of excavated rock
material less than 3 inches in size can be used as back
fill material
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557
Slab on Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of well graded gravel
Floor slab should be structurally separated from the founda
tions
Basement Walls
Basement walls up to 18 feet high are planned for the lower
parking levels

The wall foundations should bear on the rock formation An
allowable bearing value of 10 000 p s f is recommended for
dense rock formation
Well graded granular material such as sand excavated rock
or clinker less than 3 inches in size should be used as
backfill material to reduce the compactive effort required
and the build up of additional lateral pressure Impervious
material should be used for the top 2 feet of the backfill
A subdrainage system should be utilized to minimize the build
up of hydrostastic pressure behind the walls
Assuming that the boulder and cobble fill will be removed from
the site the basement walls will Support dense rock formation
therefore small lateral earth pressures are anticipated For
design purposes an equivalent fluid pressure of 10 p c f
can be used
A friction factor of 0 6 for the rock can be utilized to deter
mine the sliding resistance of the wall foundations Addition
ally a passive soil resistance of 500 p c may be used for
rock
Pavement

It appears that the pavement subgrade material will consist

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations The necessary inspections include the observa
tion of the site grubbing fill placement thickness quality
of the excavated fill materials and the verification that
sufficient passes have been performed to obtain the required
density on each fill lift
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 15 to 25 feet utiliz
ing truck mounted drilling equipment The approximate loca
tions of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan
The soils rock were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the labor
atory All soils were classified in accordance with the Uni
fied Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of
the soils encountered in the borings is presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NWG core barrel The core recovery and rock quality designa
tion RQD is presented on the Boring Logs
