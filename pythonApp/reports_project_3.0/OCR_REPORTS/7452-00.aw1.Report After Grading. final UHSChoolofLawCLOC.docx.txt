Dear Mr. Weaver: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations and pavement construction for the University of Hawaii at 
Manoa – William S. Richardson School of Law, Community Legal Outreach Center (CLOC) 
project from December 2, 2016 through January 18, 2018 in accordance with the scope of 
services outlined in our fee proposal dated November 29, 2016. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is bounded by Dole Street to the north, Lower Campus Road to the 
west, Lower Campus Parking Structure to the south and the existing Law School West Wing 
to the east. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. The project involves constructing a new two-story building within the existing parking 
lots adjacent to the building of Law School West Wing. The layout of the new construction 
is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The new building structure is supported on shallow foundations. Site grading 
generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet in thickness.  The project 
also included the installation of new underground utility connections, backfilling around 
underground utility vaults, pavement construction, among others.  This report addresses our 
field observation and compaction testing services conducted during the earthwork and 
infrastructure installation for the CLOC project.  
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
F&H Construction, Inc. (F&H) is the general contractor for the project. Zoe Builders 
LLC performed the site grading operations, pavement and utility line installation. The site

grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fills and 
backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and backfill. 
Following the demolition and clearing, the excavated subgrades at the bottom of the 
shallow foundations and future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 
8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and recompacted 
to obtain a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to 
the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). Optimum moisture is the 
water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results 
are summarized on Plate 3. 
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level 
lifts, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 
90 or 95 percent relative compaction, in generally accordance with the project specifications 
and documents. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm subgrade soil conditions exposed during the earthwork 
operations. A total of 253 field density tests, including retests of failed areas, were performed 
in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.19 of this report. 
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and 
backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes 
for reference. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not meet the required moisture and 
density conditions were reworked until the required minimum compaction was obtained or 
exceeded. 
It should be noted that the underground utility trenches connecting the project site to 
existing electrical and telecommunication vaults crossing Lower Campus Road and within 
the Lower Campus Road parking lot, north of the Music Department Building, were backfilled 
to grade with the original trench cut. Temporary asphalt patch was placed. The backfill 
placed within the trench meets the requirements of the project specifications and 
documents. The trench was not over cut by 1-foot on both sides for the pavement structural 
section as shown on the ‘Typical Trench Restoration (Private)’ detail on Sheet C500 of the 
project plans. F&H Construction notified Geolabs that the pavement restoration will be 
certified by others.
