Dear Mr Gomes
As requested we performed a subsurface exploration at the abutment locations for
the new Waiohonu Stream temporary detour bridge A subsurface investigation was
previously performed by E K Hirata and Associates titled Soil Investigation Waiohonu
Bridge Replacement Hana Highway The report recommendations
were intended for the design of the replacement bridge and not necessarily for the
foundation of the temporary detour bridge Therefore the project specifications
Section 660 required that the contractor obtain a structural engineer to design the
foundation of the temporary detour bridge In turn the purpose of our exploration was to
verify the soil rock conditions and provide recommendations for the structural engineering
consultant Scott Listavich Engineering in support of the design for the abutment
foundation of the detour bridge to be in compliance with the project specifications
Furthermore a design bearing value for Extreme Event Limit State of 15 000 pounds per
square foot was specified in the project plans
SUBSURFACE INVESTIGATION AND FINDINGS
Our subsurface exploration consisted of a series of exploratory test pits to observe
the subsurface soils at each abutment location A Case 580 rubber tired backhoe was
below the existing ground surface Our field representative logged the lithology performed
stiffness verifications and collected bulk samples for laboratory analysis of the physical
properties
In general the exploration revealed that the subsurface material consisted of dark
brown clayey silt at the surface extending to an approximate 1 to 3 foot depth This
material is probably a result of agricultural grading from the cattle ranch nearby Below the
surface layer the soil consisted of light brown silts with sand weathered gravel and
cobbles This material is commonly known as Saprolite which is an extremely weathered

RECOMMEDATIONS
Based on the material observed during our exploration the abutment footing could
bear on the Saprolite highly weathered basalt rock formation The following design
parameters may be used for the abutment footing bearing on the Saprolite weathered
basalt
1 BEARING CAPACITY
a Extreme Event Limit State 15 000 pounds per square foot psf
b Strength Limit State
c Service Limit State
2 FRICTION FACTOR
a Extreme Event Limit State 0 55
b Strength Limit State 0 44
3 PASSIVE EARTH RESISTANCE
a Extreme Event Limit State 500 pounds per cubic foot pcf
equivalent fluid pressure
b Strength Limit State 250 pcf
c Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected
Based on these findings we anticipate that the footing should extend to a depth of
at least 6 feet below the existing ground surface to obtain these recommended bearing
values A representative from our office should be present on site during the footing
excavation to verify whether the material encountered is in agreement with these
recommendations
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field test pit exploration Variations of conditions between
and beyond the field test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented herein
The test pit locations in the field were determined after the proposed pad location
was laid out by the client A topographic survey has been conducted at the project site
and therefore the elevations were interpolated during the time of our exploration as well
as the time this report was made The locations and elevations of the field test pits should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
