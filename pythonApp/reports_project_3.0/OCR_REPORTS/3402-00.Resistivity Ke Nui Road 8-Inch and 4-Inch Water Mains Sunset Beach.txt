PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site Ke Nui Road is located to the northwest of Kamehameha
Highway and along the northwestern Sunset Beach shoreline in the District of
Koolauloa on the island of Oahu as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is
proposed to construct new 2 inch 4 inch and 8 inch water mains and appurtenances
along Ke Nui Road from Kaena End to Kahuku End
Based on the information provided to our office approximately 6 150 lineal feet
of 8 inch water lines 300 lineal feet of 4 inch water lines and 690 lineal feet of 2 inch
water lines will be installed for the project In addition the project will include the
connection of existing mains cutting and plugging of mains to be abandoned
reconnection of existing service laterals and installation of new service laterals and
installation of new fire hydrants n general the depths of the proposed water lines are
anticipated to be between approximately 3 and 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and field engineers to and from
the project site
2 Performance of 85 in situ soil resistivity tests at 28 locations along the
proposed waier line alignment on Ke Nui Road These test locations
generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing maximum required in the
Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated
1991
3 Coordination of the field work by a Project Engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion
characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of six copies of this letter report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on 13th and March 20th Our field work was performed in sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions

Eighty five soil resistivity tests were conducted at about 300 foot intervals at
28 locations at the project site using the in Pin Method and a resistance
meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance meter is
capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts output into the ground The method
utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an
indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivities of the soil
mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the
water lines were assessed by using this method at the test locations listed on
Plates 8 1 through 8 6 The approximate test locations are shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 through 7
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed roadway shoulder at the test locations shown on the Site Plans The average
soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacings of generally 3 to 6 feet at the existing ground surface along the shoulder of
the roadway Among the spacings used one may have more significance than the
others for allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest
mass of soil to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings are approximately equal to the
proposed pipe invert depths and are flagged with an asterisk as shown on Plates 8 1
through 8 6
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 8 1 through 8 6 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In
general the lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried
metallic structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will
contribute to corrosion to metallic objects
