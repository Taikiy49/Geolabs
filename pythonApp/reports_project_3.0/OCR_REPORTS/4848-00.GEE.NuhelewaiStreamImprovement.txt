In accordance with our proposal dated August 6 2001 we have performed our
geotechnical engineering exploration services for the Nuhelewai Stream Improvement
project in the Alewa Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents
our findings and recommendations for the proposed project These findings and
recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Nuhelewai Stream which is located in the Alewa
Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The portion of the stream to be improved is located between Naio Street
and Aupuni Street Currently the stream is unlined and has undergone some erosion and
scour We understand that it is planned to provide improvements within the stream to
reduce the potential of scour and erosion and to dissipate energy during the flow of stream
water
Based on the information provided to us approximately 245 feet of Nuhelewai
Stream between Naio Street and Aupuni Street will be improved The improvements will
consist of lining the bottom of the channel with a concrete slab A concrete lined ditch is
also planned along the middle portion of the stream
In addition new concrete retaining walls will be constructed along the sides of the
stream with a maximum height of about 10 feet The retaining wall on the north side of the
stream will retain a cut condition and the retaining wall on the south side of the stream will
retain a fill condition

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration services was to obtain an
overview of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information
obtained was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of retaining walls slabs on grade
and site grading only To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of in house soils and other available boring data in the
vicinity of the project site
2 Reconnaissance of the project site to observe the surrounding site
conditions by an engineer from our firm
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our updated findings and recommendations
4 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions described herein were determined from a review of
geological maps two borings Boring Nos 4 and 5 previously drilled by Geolabs Hawaii
for the Kamehameha Heights Drain project and from observations of the surface soil
conditions at the project site to obtain an indication of the subsurface conditions at the
project site The approximate locations of the previously drilled borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our review and reconnaissance the project site may be underlain by
alluvial deposits consisting of sandy clay and silt with varying amounts of boulders

Retaining Walls
We understand that retaining walls are planned for the project The following
general guidelines may be used for preliminary design of retaining walls
Retaining Wall Foundations
In general we believe that retaining walls may be supported on shallow continuous
footings An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for footings bearing on the stiff alluvium or weathered rock This
vaiue may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused
by wind and or seismic forces
If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the bottom of footing elevations the
soft and or loose soils should be over excavated to the underlying firm or dense
materials The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with select granular
fill materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The
bottom of footings may be extended down to the underlying firm or dense materials
in lieu of backfilling the excavation
The foundations should have a minimum width of 18 inches and should be
embedded a minimum depth of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Wall footings oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in
stepped footings Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings may be on the
order of about 1 inch We estimate differential settlements between adjacent
footings to be on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the wall may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff alluvium or
weathered rock Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
This assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted The passive
pressure should be reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by

Drainage
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system should consist of about 1 to 2 foot wide zone
of permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
placed immediately around the perforated pipe perforations down at the base of
the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
in lieu of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should also be
hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall Unless
covered by concrete or asphaltic concrete the upper 12 inches of backfill should
consist of relatively impervious materials such as the on site clayey and silty soils
to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration behind the walls
Dynamic Lateral Earth Pressures
Dynamic lateral earth pressures due to seismic loading may be

pressures presented above are in addition to the static lateral earth pressures An
appropriately reduced factor of safety may be used when dynamic lateral earth
pressures are accounted for in the design of the retaining walls
Stream Channel Invert Slab
As mentioned previously the bottom of stream channel will be lined with a concrete
slab as a counter measure to reduce the potential for scour and erosion along the stream
Based on our observations the streambed consists of brown sandy clay and silt with
varying amounts of boulders cobbles and gravel in a dry condition To provide uniform
support we recommend that that a cushion layer consisting of a 6 inch thick layer of
open graded gravel such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used below the concrete
slab If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the cushion layer
the soft and or loose materials should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material The resulting over excavation should be lined with a non woven filter fabric
Mirafi 180N or equivalent and backfilled with open graded gravel such as ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation

Site Grading
Based on the available plans we anticipate that the site grading work for the
proposed project will generally consist of cuts on the order of about 2 feet deep along the
streambed and about 12 feet deep along the north side of the stream Fills behind the
retaining walls will be on the order of about 5 to 9 feet thick
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials should
be removed and disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing
below areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with engineered fill
The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
After clearing the areas to receive fill and finished subgrades in cut areas should
be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density In areas where scarification is not
possible such as in localized areas of the weathered rock the subgrades should be
proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory roller for a minimum of eight passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed during
clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with well compacted select
granular fill material
The on site soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of
general fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and
rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension Boulders were observed
along the streambed Boulders encountered during excavation should not be used as fill
or backfill material Imported material should consist of select granular fill materials The
materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches
in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more
and should have a maximum swell of one percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill materials should be tested for conformance
with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Imported fill materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts

Excavation
Based on the available borings and our reconnaissance at the project site the
lower portions of the site is underlain by alluvial deposits consisting of sandy clay and silt
with varying amounts of boulders cobbles and gravel The alluvial deposits are underlain
by weathered basalt rock Slightly weathered basalt rock may be encountered at deeper
depths and along the channel side slopes It is anticipated that the near surface materials
may be readily excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as backhoes
and ripping with large bulldozers However the deeper excavations into the weathered
and slightly weathered basalt rock and basaltic boulders may require the use of hoerams
or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt rock and field data from the borings
performed at the site We recommend that contractors be encouraged to examine the site
conditions and the subsurface data to make their own interpretation
Due to the proximity of the existing residences to the work area excavations in
hard basalt rock should be done with care A damage survey of the existing buildings and
Structures adjacent to the proposed improvements should be conducted including
photographs and crack surveys prior to commencement of construction
