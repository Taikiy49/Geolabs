Soil Engineering Services
Proposed New Classroom Administration
Phase 1
Makakilo Baptist Church
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions at the proposed new Classroom Administration Phase 1 site at
Makakilo Baptist Church The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
The purpose of the study was to evaluate the suitability of the
near surface soils as foundation support for the proposed structure
The study consisted of drilling four 4 hand augered borings within the
proposed site and performing laboratory soil tests This report
Summarizes our findings and recommendations
W O 1380 00 Page 2
Project Considerations
It is proposed to construct a one story concrete masonry structure
with concrete on grade slab Included in this investigation is a
restroom addition to the existing classroom The foundation loadings
are anticipated to be light The general layout of the proposed
construction is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site Conditions
At the time of our field exploration the proposed classroom
administration area was a concrete paved parking area with an unpaved
gravel filled area on the northern side The concrete pavement appeared
to be in a fair condition with moderate amount of cracking The western
limit of the site was near the toe of a small slope where the ground
surface rose gently to the west The proposed restroom addition was a
concrete paved area to the west of the existing restrooms
Subsurface Conditions
The geological formation of the site is identified as the upper
member of Waianae basalt In general the soil cover is believed to be
relatively thin
Our hand augered borings disclosed that the subsurface conditions
consisted mainly of medium stiff to stiff dark brown clay with moderate
to high expansion potential The clay soil extended to depths of 2 0 to
2 5 feet and was underlain by very stiff to hard rocky soil boulder or
rocks that the hand auger was unable to penetrate

Based on the HB 4 drilled nearby we believe that the proposed
restroom addition area was underlain also by the expansive soils covered
by concrete slabs
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the hand augered
borings are presented on the attached Log of Borings Plates A 1 through
A 4 The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that
the underlying soils are classified as clays CH according to the
Unified Soil Classification System and have a moderate to high
shrink swell potential The laboratory test results are summarized on
Plate B 1
Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is probably underlain
by dark brown clay to depths of 2 to 2 5 feet over stiffer materials
The laboratory test results on this material generally indicate that it
has a high expansion potential Therefore the slab on grade foundation
would require special design and construction procedures in order to
reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Similar soil conditions are anticipated in the restroom addition
area and in such a case the following recommendations will also apply

Foundation Support
Most of the proposed new classroom administration area was covered
by the existing concrete slab Although the existing concrete slab
appears to be in a fair condition with small to moderate cracking the
borings around the slab indicate that the subgrade soils below the slab
may be highly expansive and can heave considerably if saturated in the
future Therefore we recommend that the existing slab be removed and
the expansive clays below the future building area be over excavated
down to non expansive soil rock or to a maximum depth of 3 feet below
the bottom of the new floor slab whichever is the shallower The
over excavation should extend a minimum of 1 5 feet beyond the edge of
the slab
The over excavation should be backfilled with non expansive borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches thick and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
on site gravel or granular base under the slab if any may be used as
fill if not contaminated with the expansive soil
The top 8 inches of backfill for the over excavation outside the
slab area should consist of compacted non pervious soils of low
expansion potential to reduce infiltration of surface water beneath the
slab area

The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill and should be embedded a minimum of 10 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted fill
Surf ace water should be diverted away from slab edges and
foundations
Slab On Grade
As previously discussed the expansive on site soil under the slab
areas should be completely removed or over excavated to a depth of 3
feet and replaced with non expansive borrow soils in order to reduce the
effects of soil heaving on the slabs
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils
are recommended for the walkway and slabs if required
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils if any
below the over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to
concrete placement

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The slabs should be independent and free to move from the walls
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slabs and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition
Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around the
structures and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways including the existing structures Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above mentioned foundations since ponded water could
cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or
loss of strength The site drainage should be maintained throughout the
life of the house

The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during the
over excavation under the slab on grade areas and fill placement
Footing excavations should be examined by a soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from hand augered boring
and laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of the foundation design of this project
