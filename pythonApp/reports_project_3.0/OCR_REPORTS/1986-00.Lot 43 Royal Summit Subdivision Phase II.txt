SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 43 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE ITI
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three borings performing laboratory testing and
reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation
plans This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a one story structure approximately 37 by 110 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and the
house Wood framed construction is anticipated for the proposed house
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls generally less than
8 feet high are planned along the middle portion of the house to provide
grade separation Reinforced concrete retaining walls with a maximm
height of 18 feet are planned along various portions of the lot

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located at the northern end of Lanikuakaa Street
in the Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad of about 42 by 42 feet in plan is situated
along the front portion of the site The ground surface then slopes
elevations 505 to 514 and 520 feet There is a 2 foot wide concrete
drainage ditch along the sloping ground at the southern boundary
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 43 to depths of 5 to 20 feet
below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered medium to very stiff reddish
brown clayey silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass
grading plan for Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II dated
prepared by Community Planning Inc indicated that Lot 43 received up to
9 feet of compacted fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The medium to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered
generally can provide suitable foundation support for the proposed
residential structure provided that some care is exercised during
construction to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying soil

Laboratory tests performed on the clayey silt soil indicate that
this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential depending
on the in situ moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent subgrade
saturation
Therefore to reduce the soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the
select fill be moistened and protected from drying from the start of
construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive granular soils should also be used as backfill
behind the retaining walls to reduce the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Some shrinkage cracks were observed at the northwest corner of the
lot during our site exploration We believe that this was a result of
long term exposure to wetting and drying conditions These cracks and any
others encountered during construction should be over excavated and
backfilled properly with select non expansive granular soils or on site
silty soil
lot Grading
The surface soils appeared to be somewhat loosened by the prolonged
exposure to wetting and drying conditions Therefore we recommend that
prior to filling or foundation construction the surface of the lot should
be recompacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive granular soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of the above specified non expansive select fill capping

All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding
10 inches and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house would be of slab on grade
construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used to support the
proposed residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used
on the compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior spread footings on level areas should be poured in place
and be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the finished grade
Perimeter wall footings should be embedded a minimm of eighteen 18
inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Slab On Grade
Some of the on site soils are moderately expansive therefore we
recommend that the on site soil be over excavated down to a depth of
twelve 12 inches below the slab bottom The exposed subgrade should be
thoroughly moistened prior to placement of the twelve inch capping layer
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
subgrade soil below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week
prior to concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two
days and sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
We recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel be provided as
the cushion fill below the slab The 3B Fine gravel layer will also act
as a capillary break To protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced with a 4 inch
base course layer and an 8 inch thick perimeter thickened edges if some
slab cracking is tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints at about 8 to 10 foot intervals are recommended in the
driveway slab in order to allow for some relative movement of the
concrete

Swimming Pool
For the swimming pool planned at the rear of the residence a
lateral earth pressure of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of
depth may be used for walls restrained at the top at rest condition with
level backfill
The on site soil have low swelling potential if maintained at a
moisture content above optimum approximately 30 However the swelling
potential increases with decreasing moisture contents and therefore the
swimming pool excavation should be kept moist all the time prior to
concrete placement
Swimming pool construction should be properly coordinated with the
adjacent retaining wall backfill operation Potential excessive water
leaking from the swimming pool could create an impact on the stability and
Support of the adjacent retaining wall Therefore drainage details
should be provided in the swimming pool design to efficiently divert bleed
seepage water away through the weepholes of the wall and daylight out to
the slope face through a series of drain outlets in front of wall
footings
The future pool will impose additional lateral forces on the wall
which may cause horizontal movements and damage to the pool In order to
reduce this risk the pool walls should be designed to be independent of
the retaining wall i e do not depend on the retaining wall for support
Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent excessive water from entering
the foundation slab subsoils

We believe these drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could
cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss
of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible after footing
backfill and maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab
and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot grading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with our findings presented in this report
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings

The location of the field data points were determined by scaling

Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
