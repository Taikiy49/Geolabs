INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed Parkview Gem Warehouse
located in This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations

The proposed warehouse building site is located at the makai

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building site consisted
of drilling five test borings The borings were drilled at the

laboratory testing is given in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and testing results
Soil Condition
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
by approximately six to seven feet of loose to medium density
man made fills consisting of clay silt sand and gravel with
some timber tar rubber etc

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
The upper recent land fill and the underlying loose lagoonal de
posits can provide a very low bearing capacity without excessive
building settlements For a building with new fills and heavy
column loadings large size spread footing might be uneconomical
and difficult to design for large settlements Pile foundation
would be required
If the site grading can be minimized to no more than 1 foot the
bearing capacity for spread footing can be increased to 1 500
p s f provided that the entire warehouse area is undercut Tefo0t
and the exposed subgrade surface subjected to 8 passes of a heavy
vibratory roller prior to backfilling to the proposed subgrade
level The vibratory compactor must deliver a total impact loading
in excess of 30 000 foot pounds energy

W
This method of proof rolling creates a compacted mat having
a thickness equavalent to 4 feet of backfill plus an additional
1 foot of on site parking base course and broken up A C pave
ment backfill
Building foundation resting upon this 5 feet of stabilized
material will be essential bearing upon a modified mat foun
dation which will reduce the differential settlements
It is essential for the utilization of this method that the
proof rolling and compaction operations be closely monitored
by the Soil Engineer to assure the proper implementation of
the construction recommendations The spread footing recommen
dations in this report are contingent upon such inspections
Foundation
Based on the boring and laboratory test results a spread footing
foundation placed at minimum depth upon the recompacted layer
can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 1 500 pounds
per square foot Due to the non uniform condition of the under
lying on site fill material 2 feet of compacted granular material
should be placed below the bottom of all footings The backfill
material should be of suitable on site granular soil or quarry waste
material
Based on the above bearing pressure the calculated total settle
ment would be about 13 inches for the columns The estimated di
fferential settlement would approximately be one half of the above
figure

PileEquadatian
For pile foundation support a 12 inch square concrete pile
extending 15 feet into the lower very stiff clayey or coral layer will
develop an allowable bearing capacity of 40 tons per pile
This pile should be driven to a final driving resistance of
65 blows per foot for the last two feet or 8 blows per inch
with a hammer delivering a rated energy of 15 000 foot pounds
For cost estimate and comparison purposes the estimated average
pile length will be about 40 feet
Prior to production pile driving 3 test piles of 45 feet long
should be driven to establish more accurately the order lengths
and to establish final driving resistance criteria
The piles should be spaced no closer than three pile diameters
otherwise group action may reduce the pile capacity They may
be considered to be fixed at 5 foot depths from present grade
The driving criteria may be modified during construction if ex
cessive resistance is encountered

EARTHWORK SPECIFICATION FOR THE
PARKVIEW GEM WAREHOUSE BUILDING
Description
This item pertains to subgrade preparation of the entire warehouse
area consisting of performing all operations necessary to undercut
a minimum of 1 foot below the existing A C pavement and proof roll
with a vibratory roller to backfill with suitable excavated on site
materials including the placement of additional fill up to the base
course elevation to perform all operations for footing excavations
and backfill to backfill the depression and ditches resulting from the
removal of obstructions and to subsidiary work for grading and con
struction of A C parking area
Cleaning Grubbing Preparations of Warehouse Area to be undercut
proof rolled
The existing A C pavements shall be undercut 1 foot and the suitable
excavated materials shall be stockpiled for backfill uses
Rubbish fill and soft clayey pockets encountered in this layer shall
be separated and removed from the suitable stockpile material Prior
to placement of backfill and new fill construction the grounds shall
be rough graded then thoroughly watered and proof rolled to uncover
soft spots
Proof rolling shall be done with 8 passes by a vibratory compactor
capable of deliveringa minimum total applied force of 30 000 pounds
The entire warehouse area shall be proof rolled with each succeeding
pass overlapping the previous pass
Earthwork Specifications 2 W
Soft spots shall be excavated to a maximum depth of 2 Feat and
backfilled with select borrow such as quarry waste or approved
on site excavated materials
Proof rolling and removal of soft spots shall be monitored by a
Soil Engineer
When unsuitable material is removed and disposed of the resulting
space shall be refilled with approved materials as specified and
the approved material shall be placed in 6 inch layers compacted to
a minimum of 95 compaction
Placing Spreading Compacting Structural Backfill Material
After the entire building area has been adequately proof rolled
suitable on site or select fill materials shall be placed in level
layers which when compacted shall not exceed 6 inches Each layer
shall be spread evenly and blade mixed during the spreading to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Rock or boulders exceeding 6 inches in diameter shall not be allowed
in the fill
When the water content of the backfill materials is well below the
optimum for compacting purposes water shall be added until the
water content is near the optimum
After each layer has been placed mixed and spread evenly it shall
be compacted to 95 of maximum density in accordance with ASTM Test
Method D 1557 72T
The rolling of each layer shall be continuous over its entire area
and the roller shall make sufficient passes to obtain the desired
density
Earthwork Specifications 3 W
Field density tests shall be performed by the Soil Technician
to get an indication of the compaction of the backfill
The backfill operation shall continue in 6 inch compacted layers
as specified above up to the top of the base course layer
Footing Excavation
Individual spread footing excavation shall be initiated after
the completion of proof rolling and backfilling up to grade
Foundations shall be over excavated 2 feet below the footing
elevations shown on the foundation plans The contractor shall
in no way disturb the ground below the bottom of the excavation Should
the contractor disturb the ground below these elevations he
will be required to excavate all such loosened ground backfill
in 6 inch compacted lifts to 95 compaction up to its required
footing bottom elevation
Any additional soft clayey pockets or rubbish fill encountered
in excavations shall be removed and replaced with compacted back
fill compacted to 95 compaction The backfill material shall be
of suitable on site soil or quarry waste materials
After concrete is in place footing backfill shall be compacted in
6 inch lifts to 95 compaction Unsuitable on site material shall
not be put back in backfill
