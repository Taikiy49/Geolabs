STATES DESCRIPTION
The site is located southeast of the intersection of Kaumualii
Highway and Halewili Road in Kalaheo Kauai It is bordered
by Kaumualiit Highway to the north Halewili Road to the west
Ipuolono Reservoir to the southwest and a drainageway leading
to the reservoir to the southeast and east
The northern portion of the site is generally flat with gradients
of about 5 percent down toward the south The southern portion
of the site generally slopes down to the south at gradients of
about 5 to 20 percent Beyond the southwestern portion of the
site the ground slopes down into the reservoir at slope ratios
20 to 30 feet higher than the water level in the reservoir
during the field exploration The drainageway along the
eastern and soucheaster 0 boundaries of the site is generally
about 50 to 100 feet from the project boundary and about 15 to
30 feet lower A few feet of water was noted in the drainageway
during the field exploration
The western and northwestern portions of the site are generally
at the same level as the bordering roadways The northeastern
portion of the site next to Kaumualii Highway is up to about
15 feet higher than the roadway level
The northern and eastern portions of the site were covered with
abandoned sugar cane The rest of the site was covered with
grass and some trees near the drainageway and reservoir

A 60 foot wide power line easement generally extending in an

the site A 35 foot wide ditch easement extends through the

Another ditch easement 10 feet wide extends through the central

few inches of water was noted near this easement during the

The elevation at the site generally varies from about 610 to

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general competent bearing soils were encountered at the
site Except for the northwestern portion of the site spread
footing foundation or slab on grade may be used
In the northwestern portion of the site a surface layer about
1 to 3 feet of brown silty clay was encountered Laboratory
tests indicate that this material has moderate expansion po
tential in its natural state Upon wetting and remolding
IE
Brydeswood Terrace Subdivision ero
it loses most of its expansive potential When encountered
below slabs this material should be scarified a minimum
depth of 12 inches and recompacted wet of optimum The
subgrade should not be allowed to dry out prior to pouring
the slab Otherwise post and beam construction should be
considered in this area
Earthwork and Grading
A preliminary grading plan for the site was not available
during our study
From the topography of the site it appears that in general
the northern portion of the site may be cut and the lower
southern portion filled
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to the above specified density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing
firm ground particularly near the reservoir and drainageway
areas see Plate 2
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests no major
settlement problems are anticipated for fills up to 10 feet

Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for
cut and fill slopes Surface water should be diverted away
from slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Foundations
For the proposed light residential structures spread footing
foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs should be
set back from the tops of slopes a minimum distance of 5 feet
Post footings located close to the tops of slopes should have
a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope
face Post footings away from the tops of slopes should be
placed 12 inches below the finish grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure
of 2000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the stiff
natural soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench Surface
water should be diverted away from building foundations
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
BRYDESWOOD TERRACE SUBDIVISION
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
Il Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
iy

Qa
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer

8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are

duce a uniformly compacted fill
