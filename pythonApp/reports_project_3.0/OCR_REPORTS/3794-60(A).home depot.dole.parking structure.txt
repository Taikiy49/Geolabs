A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed for the subject project and
the results are presented in the above referenced report The purpose of our
observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation
installation for conformance with the recommendations presented in our report The pile
driving contractor was Lahaina Pier Pile Company Inc
Project Description
The project site is located adjacent to the Dole Cannery Outlet Center near the
intersection of Alakawa Street and Nimitz Highway in the Iwilei area of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to
construct a new Home Depot retail facility consisting of a retail building a garden center
a two story parking structure and other infrastructure

A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted at the site in July of 1997
and March of 1998 A report of our findings was issued on and an
additional letter report was issued on Based on our geotechnical
engineering exploration and the design concept it appears that the soft loose and
compressible deposits underlying the site cannot provide adequate support for shallow
foundations such as mat or spread footings without inducing excessive settlements
under the high structural loads of the proposed two story parking structure Therefore pile
foundation was recommended for foundation support of the project A deep foundation
system consisting of 16 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed concrete piles with a
design vertical compressive load capacity from 50 to 100 tons per pile was recommended
for support of the parking structure
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 25 piles at various locations across
the site was conducted from November 23 through 25 1998 The purpose of the test
pile program was to establish the driveability of the piles given the subsurface conditions
at the site driving equipment and methods of installation In addition the program also
provides information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing stratum
across the project site which was used to estimate the length of production piles A pile
load test was conducted on on a selected test pile to evaluate the pile
driving criteria and to confirm the design bearing capacity Results of the pile load test
program are presented in our Pile Load Test Report dated
Based on the test pile driving program and the pile load test order lengths of
production piles were determined
Production Pile Driving Observations
The production pile locations were generally predrilled to a depth of about 15 to
25 feet below the existing ground surface to clear obstructions prior to pile installation
Obstructions such as concrete footing basalt boulders and coral ledges were
encountered at many locations during predrilling In most cases these obstructions were
removed by excavating up to 8 feet below the existing ground surface A jack hammer
was used to break the concrete footing for removal
A Delmag single acting diesel hammer Model D30 23 mounted on the fixed leads
of a Manitowoc crane was used to drive the 16 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed
concrete piles The hammer has a rated energy between 35 000 and
69 000 foot pounds Based on our field observations the pile hammer was generally
delivering a driving energy of approximately 47 000 to 55 000 foot pounds during the
majority of the pile driving operations The hammer was equipped with variable fuel
settings so that driving within soft loose deposits may be achieved at a reduced energy
level to limit the tensile stress within the pile which could cause damage to the pile

A total of 283 production piles were driven between and
for the subject project The pile driving operations were conducted
under the near continuous observation of a field representative of our firm During the
pile driving operation logs were maintained for each pile which included information
pertaining to pile number date driven predrilled depth blow counts observations size
and length of the piles The locations of the production piles driven are shown on the
attached Pile Location Plan Plate 2 This included one replacement pile Pile No 308
to replace the original pile 178 which broke during the test pile driving operations
The Pile Driving Summary is presented in Plates 3 1 thru 3 9 Individual pile driving
records were transmitted to Fletcher Pacific and Lahaina Pier Pile Company Inc
during the course of the pile driving operations
It is our opinion that the piles for this project have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices Based on our observations of hammer performance and
pile driving behavior and the pile load test results it is our professional opinion that the
piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design vertical
compressive load capacity
