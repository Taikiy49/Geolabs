PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Ka Medical Center in Hilo
on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided it is desired to
provide foundation design parameters pertaining to the installation of the new MRI
machine inside the existing medical center We understand that the new MRI machine will
weigh about 92 000 pounds The concrete mat slab foundation supporting the new MRI
machine will measure approximately 16 feet long 12 feet wide and 5 feet thick

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the MRI Suite Renovation project consisted of
drilling and sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of
about 20 feet below the existing ground surface in the crawl space below the suspended
slab of the MRI suite The approximate location of the boring is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring generally encountered a 12 inch thick layer of surface fill consisting of
medium dense silty sands The surface fill was underlain by severely fractured
moderately to highly weathered basalt of soft to medium hard in consistency extending to
a depth of approximately 4 5 feet below the existing ground surface Hard to very hard
basalt rock formation was then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration encountered basalt rock formation at about
12 inches below the existing ground surface in the crawl space below the suspended slab
at the MRI suite Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of our
field exploration
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses it is our opinion that the
mat slab foundation for the MRI machine should extend through the surface fills on the
order of about 12 inches thick and bear on the underlying hard basalt rock formation
Cavities and or voids were encountered at about 3 and 10 feet below the existing ground
surface in the boring drilled at the MRI machine site Therefore we recommend that a
program of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation location be conducted during
construction to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse
of the cavities below foundations Detailed discussions of these items and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
MRI Machine Foundations
In general we believe that the proposed new MRI machine may be supported on
a mat slab foundation extending through the surface fills and bearing on the underlying
hard basalt rock formation anticipated at about 12 inches below the existing ground
surface in the crawl space below the suspended slab at the MRI suite site An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
the design of the mat slab foundation bearing on the hard basalt rock formation
underlying the project site This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade For footings bearing on the underlying basalt
rock formation the footing bottom should be relatively level and clear of sharp points
that may result in concentrated loading Depending on the depth of the basalt rock
formation below the finished grades deeper footing embedment depths may be
required at localized areas to provide a competent bearing surface for the footing
foundation

If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements the footings
should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for appreciable foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings supported on the in situ
basalt rock formation should be on the order of about 0 25 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used for footings bearing on the hard basalt rock
formation Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Foundation Probing Program
Based on our exploration and experience in the area voids and or cavities appear
to be present in the basaltic lava flows at the project site To reduce the potential for loss
of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below foundations we
recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting be implemented during
construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at the mat slab
foundation with one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing area The probe holes
should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the
planned bottom of foundation elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or
suspected during the probing operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer
spacing to aid in delineating the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The
probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected
at low to moderate pressures We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout with
a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting operations In lieu of the
sand cement grout fluid lean concrete such as controlled low strength material
flowable fill may also be used for the grouting operation
