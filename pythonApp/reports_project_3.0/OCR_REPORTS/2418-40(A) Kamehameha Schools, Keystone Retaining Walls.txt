PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Kamehameha Schools in the Kapalama
Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The multi purpose athletic field
is located at the former football field location and to the west of the baseball field
Mauwaena Field
One of the major items of construction for the project was the construction of one
and two tiered Keystone Retaining Walls on the western and southern portions of the
athletic field The walls consisted of masonry units Keystone Blocks geogrids
Stratagrid and compacted select backfill materials Wall heights for the one and
two tiered Keystone retaining walls range from about 5 to 19 feet and from about
24 to 32 feet respectively The total length of the walls was approximately 1 250 linear
feet

KEYSTONE RETAINING WALL CONSTRUCTION
The Keystone retaining wall construction work for the Multi Purpose Athletic Field
project was observed by Geolabs Inc from August 8 through The site
was cleared and grubbed prior to August 8 2000 The grading work for the wall
construction was performed by Koga Engineering and Construction Inc and the Keystone
wall construction work was performed by Dura Constructors Inc During the grading and
backfilling operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 2
In general the subgrade and leveling pad materials for the Keystone retaining wall
were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 In addition the reinforced fill and retained fill materials were
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
During the leveling pad reinforced fill and retained fill construction periodic field
density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922
and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge Method The results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 17 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
It should be noted that an abandoned cesspool structure was discovered while
excavating for the Wall B footing near Station 0 65 Based on our recommendations
the cesspool was backfilled with lean concrete and the last four feet was backfilled with
No 3B Fine gravel

blocks and the height of the wall the top of Wall A was about 3 inches higher than the
finished grade shown on the plans Because of the above the top layer of the geogrid for
Wall A was lowered by one Keystone block course down about 8 inches below the
