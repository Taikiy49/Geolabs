This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the Home Depot Dole from
May 1998 through June 1998 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The Home Depot Dole project is located in the Iwilei area in the vicinity of the
intersection of Nimitz Highway and Alakawa Street on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
facility will consist of a retail building with a planned area of approximately
130 000 square feet a garden center of approximately 25 000 square feet a parking area
with 607 spaces and other infrastructures This report generally relates to the retail and
garden center building pad area A general layout of the project site is provided on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The contractor started the earthwork by clearing and grubbing areas within the
contract grading limits in June 1998 Old footings and old slabs pavement vegetation
and other unsuitable materials were removed and disposed of Following the clearing
and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were generally moisture conditioned
and compacted

In areas that received fill the original ground was scarified to a depth of about
6 inches where possible moisture conditioned and compacted to the specified relative
compaction based on visual observations Loose areas disclosed during clearing
operations were over excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled with
compacted non expansive fill
Fill materials and the first foot of surcharge material consisting of off site borrow
were placed in near level lifts of approximately 10 inches in loose thickness moisture
conditioned as needed and compacted to the project requirement of at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Compaction was generally
accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers and vibratory drum rollers
To aid the acceleration of anticipated ground settlements 5 feet of temporary fill
was placed over the building fill area The surcharge fill was placed in level lifts of 1 foot
high and extended a minimum of 2 feet beyond the footprint of the fill area The first layer
of the surcharge fill was compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The imported fill material used generally consisted of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve select borrow from Kapaa Quarry During the site grading
operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed when possible during the site grading
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and
soil conditions were noted The field density tests were performed to determine the
relative compaction of the fills placed for conformance with the specified minimum
compaction A total of 113 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe
method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the
compaction tests performed during mass grading operations for the Home Depot Dole
project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes where available and sighting from plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used

EVALUATIONS
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii during the earthwork and grading operations of the Home Depot Dole
project in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the plans and specifications and recommendations stated in the
above referenced report by Geolabs Hawaii dated
LIMITATIONS
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
