PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the area at and near the vicinity of the parking lot for
the Bernice Pauahi Bishop Memorial Chapel at the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama
Campus in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The proposed building will be situated on the slope located above the existing
Chapel parking lot Based on the available grading plan the finished floor elevation is
approximately 547 21 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Since the project is on sloping
ground relatively high cut slopes are required to level the site for the new building
structure Based on the proposed finished floor elevation the cut slopes may range
from about 25 to 40 feet high The face of the cut slope will be shotcreted and will be
referred to as Shotcrete Wall No 1 A portion of the shotcrete faced cut slope is
referred to as Shotcrete Wall No 2 at the west end of the wall alignment
As required by the City and County of Honolulu a geological and engineering
evaluation of the existing hillslope conditions above the proposed development was
conducted to evaluate potential rockfall hazard conditions at the project site

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was formed by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau Shield Volcanoes beginning in the mid Pliocene Epoch The older Waianae
Volcano was the first to develop closely followed by the development of Koolau Volcano
For a period of time Oahu was comprised of two islands formed by each of the volcanic
centers The volcanoes continued to erupt and banked against the slopes of each other
with Waianae terminating its eruptive activity prior to the cessation of Koolau The main
shield building activities ceased approximately 3 5 to 2 5 million years ago
The project area is generally composed of basaltic rock built by extrusion of the
lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual and saprolitic soils In general
saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located at the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Campus in the
Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is located in the area at and near
the vicinity of the parking lot for the Bernice Pauahi Bishop Memorial Chapel The project
location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The proposed building will be situated on a hillside located above the existing
Chapel parking lot The area generally slopes downward from north to south with
existing ground surface elevations ranging from about 525 to 625 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL The hillside is densely vegetated with trees and brush and areas of heavy vine
growth The majority of the ground surface was covered with pine needle debris on the
order of about 6 to 12 inches thick Other areas along the hillside exposed soil with widely
scattered rock outcroppings and few surface boulders Construction debris was also
observed on the hillside
SITE RECONNAISSANCE
A geological engineering reconnaissance was conducted in an effort to evaluate
and map the existing field conditions pertaining to the occurrence of surface rock
outcroppings and boulder deposits which may present potential rockfall hazards for the
proposed development Our geologist performed the site reconnaissance under dry
weather and ground conditions using the available field reference staking supplemented
with a handheld Garmin Global Positioning System GPS a barometric altimeter and
compass navigational aids Due to the dense canopy of tree cover the GPS device was
of limited use in locating features
The purpose of our mapping effort was to obtain an overview of the general
location size and spatial distribution of the surface boulders and rock outcroppings
residing on the hillside
Based on our site reconnaissance and foot traverse of the natural hillslope located
within the project site boundaries we observed an area of anomalous nested boulder
deposit near the top of slope northeasterly of existing longitudinal oriented drainage
ditch The nested boulders appear to have spread and seated during earlier adjacent site
construction activity In addition two boulder nests were observed near the bottom of the
slope on the east and west sides of the existing drainage ditch These boulders were
generally about 1 to 3 feet in diameter The boulder nests encountered at the lower
portion of the slope should be removed during the mass grading of the site

We observed near continuous across slope remnant rock ledge outcropping

Potential Rockfall Hazards
The anomalous nested boulder deposit near the top of slope northeasterly of
existing longitudinal oriented drainage ditch appears to have been spread and seated
during earlier adjacent site construction activity Some boulders are loose and movable but
the risk for movement is low due to interlocking with other boulders and vegetation acting
as natural barriers In addition the inclination of the slope appears to flatten below the
nested boulders providing additional safety factor against possible rockfall to the proposed
development In our opinion the anomalous nested boulder deposits appears to be stable
and should have a low potential for rockfall hazard to the proposed development The
nested boulders located at the lower portion of the slope should be removed during the
mass grading operations
Based on the grading plan portions of rock ledge observed between
Elevations 568 and 578 feet MSL will be removed by cut Other portions of the rock
ledge appear to extend outside of the grading limits and may reside along the crest of the
new developed Shotcrete Wall No 1 between Stations 1 70 and 2 00 The rock ledge
and boulders remaining at the crest of the new Shotcrete Wall No 1 are considered
potentially hazardous for long term stability
Rockfall Mitigation
Due to the proximity of the project site to the top of new shotcrete walls and the size
and potential instability of some components of the rock ledge and boulders above the
rock ledge we recommend extending mechanized grading limit to encompass the rock

LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from visual site observations only Variation in the surface and
subsurface conditions between our observation points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until additional field exploration or
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the findings and recommendations provided herein
It should be noted that slopes composed of rock materials rock slopes deteriorate
with the passage of time due to natural weathering processes wet dry and hot cold
cycles and erosion conditions Due to the inherent deterioration of rock slopes resulting
from natural processes weathering wet dry and hot cold cycles and erosion conditions
the potential for rockfall hazard at any site changes with the passage of time Therefore
the findings and recommendations contained in this letter report may be used only within a
reasonable time from the date of issuance of this letter report Land use site conditions
and or other factors may change with the passage of time Therefore additional work to
evaluate the applicability of the findings and preliminary recommendations contained in
this letter report due to changes with the passage of time will be required Finally there
are no guarantees in the professional engineering and architectural design fields with
respect to potential rockfall hazards due in large part to the unpredictable nature of rockfall
activity which is affected by many external variables including natural and man induced
causes
