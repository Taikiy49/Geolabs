PHASE 3 MAKAI
The project site has been previously mass graded as part of the Villages of
Kapolei mass grading work by Oahu Construction Company Ltd This mass grading
work involved the placement of up to about 5 feet of fill at the project site The fill
materials consisted generally of clayey soils with moderate to high swelling and
shrinkage potential Subsequent to the mass grading work the site preparation process
consisted of scarifying the pad subgrades to a depth of about 10 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
between 85 and 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor Following the subgrade preparation approximately 12 inches of non expansive
coral capping fill was placed and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction within the building pad areas
The over excavated subgrades at the driveway slabs and motorcourt areas were
generally scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to about 90 percent relative
compaction Following the subgrade preparation approximately 12 inches of
non expansive coral capping fill was placed and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction within the driveway slabs and motorcourt areas
During the site preparation operations samples of the on site and imported fill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
for the various phases of construction are summarized in Appendices A through of this
report
Field density tests were performed on a periodic basis during the fill and backfill
placement operations A total of 1 734 field density tests including retests of failed areas
were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed for the various phases
