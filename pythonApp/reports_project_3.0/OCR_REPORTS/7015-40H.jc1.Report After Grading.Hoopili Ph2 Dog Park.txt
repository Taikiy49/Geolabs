PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of Interstate Route H-1 
Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
Ho’opili development encompasses approximately 1,500 acres. The project site is gently 
sloping and was previously used for agricultural cultivation. 
The Phase 2 area is located at the west-central portion of the development, east of 
Kualakai Parkway (formerly North-South Road), encompassing approximately 34 acres for 
residential and commercial use, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The 
development of the private dog park is located on the northeastern corner of the Phase 2 
development, encompassing approximately 0.38 acres in land area, as shown on the Site 
Plan, Plate 2. 
The site grading construction consisted of cuts and fills of less than about 5 feet to 
achieve the design finished grades within an area of about 0.38 acres. Royal Contracting 
performed the earthwork operations for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. The project site was previously 
mass-graded, consisting of removal of the upper 12 inches of cultivated soils below the 
existing ground surface, creating a 2-foot thick rework zone which elevated the in-situ soil 
moisture content to about 2 to 4 percent above the optimum moisture content and 
compacting to about 85 to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557. Compaction effort was established using a sheepsfoot 
and/or smooth vibratory drum roller.  
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineering fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the concrete walkway areas, the 
subgrade was capped by 24 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills during the mass grading operation.  
The test results are summarized on Plate 4. These tests were performed on 
moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering April 3 through 15, 2020.
