PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of the Interstate Route 
H-1 Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
The Ho`opili development encompasses approximately 1,500 acres, as shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. The project site is gently sloping and was previously used for 
agricultural cultivation. 
The Phase 5 Backbone Roadway includes Roads “E”, “O”, “D”, “5”, and Iwi Kuamo`o 
Street for a total of approximately 4,925 linear feet of infrastructure construction, as shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The grading and construction of the roadway system generally consisted of trench 
excavations for utility installation and backfills, subgrade cuts and fills, and pavement base 
course and/or subbase preparations. Royal Contracting Co., Ltd. performed the earthwork 
operations for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a sheepsfoot and/or smooth vibratory drum roller. It should be noted that the upper 2 feet 
of the subgrade was previously moisture-conditioned to about 2 to 4 percent above the 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 85 to 90 percent of the 
maximum dry density, forming a 2-foot thick rework zone during the mass grading 
operation. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineering fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the subgrade was 
capped by 18 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum 95 percent of 
the maximum dry density.  In the concrete sidewalk areas, the subgrade was capped by 
24 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the 
maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.53. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering September 3, 2019 through 
June 24, 2021.
