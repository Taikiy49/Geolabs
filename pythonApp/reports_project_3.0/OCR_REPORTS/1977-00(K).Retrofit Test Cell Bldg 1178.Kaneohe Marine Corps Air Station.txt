INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed addition to Building 1178 in the
Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

W O 1977 00 K Page 2
This report summarizes our findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
exploration data obtained These recommendations are intended for design
input and not intended for construction estimate or bidding
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to provide an addition to the existing test cell
Building 1178 at the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station
Detailed project data about the proposed building addition were not
available at the time of our study However it is anticipated that the
proposed structure will be a single story concrete or CMU building with a
slab on grade floor
Minimal site grading is anticipated due to the existing flat grades
at the project site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation and
Slab design of the proposed building addition
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated June
30 1989 to Department of the Navy The scope of work included
1 drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging from 10 5
to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located adjacent to the existing Building 1178
and on the eastern side of the existing Building 1414 in the Marine Corps
Air Station in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The site is presently an open storage area for abandoned building
materials including rebars rebar cages and masonry blocks
The site is generally level with ground elevations at about 109
feet U S Navy Datum
The project site is sparsely covered with vegetation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1977 00 K Page 4
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed building addition site were
explored by drilling six 6 borings to depths of 10 5 to 31 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of these borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the project site is underlain by highly compressible
lagoonal clay and sandy coral detritus subsoils below a thin surface crust
of loose to medium dense sand and gravel fill The highly compressible
layers include approximately 2 to 4 feet of soft to very soft clay and
approximately 22 to 25 feet of generally loose to very loose sand and
gravel
Medium stiff to stiff clayey silt was encountered below the loose
deposits to the maximum drilled depth of 31 5 feet below the existing
ground in the two 2 deepest borings
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at the approximate depths
of 8 0 to 8 3 feet below the existing ground surface during our field
exploration
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed highly compressible subsoil
conditions below a thin fill crust at the proposed building addition
site However we believe that shallow footing foundations may be used to
support the proposed single story structure provided the allowable footing
bearing capacity and the footing size are limited
Site filling is not recommended since it may induce significant
ground settlements due to compression of the underlying highly
compressible subsoils

W O 1977 00 K Page 5
Footing Foundation
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered we believe that shallow
footing foundations may be used to support the proposed light weight
structure provided the footing bearing capacity and the footing size are
limited and some settlements are tolerable
An allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design if the maximum footing width is limited to 2
feet This value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration wind or seismic
load considerations
New footings should be placed at the same level as the adjacent
footings of the existing building In general a minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is
recommended
A larger footing size and or deeper footing embedment are not
recommended since it would generate higher bearing stresses on the
underlying soft clay deposit resulting in larger footing settlements
The bottoms of the footing excavations in the generally loose to
medium dense fill crust should be recompacted to at least 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Provided the above design guidelines are followed footing
settlements under the anticipated design loading are estimated to be on
inch differential

Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend
that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used
to calculate the passive soil pressure developed against the buried
vertical faces of the footing foundations and a frictional coefficient of
0 4 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the
footing bottoms and the recompacted footing subgrade
Site Grading
Site grading information for the subject project was not available at
this stage of the geotechnical study However it is anticipated that
minimal site grading will be used due to the existing flat grade at the
project site
Site filling is not recommended since it may induce significant
ground settlements caused by compression of the very soft loose subsoils
under the weight of the new fill
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as rubbish vegetation and other deleterious materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area to receive slab on grade should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches below the subgrade level moisture conditioned and
proof rolled recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any loose soft or yielding spots encountered at the footing or slab
subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm ground and
replaced with in situ or imported backfill compacted in 8 inch loose lifts
to a minimm of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78

New imported backfill materials if needed should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
Based on the soil conditions encountered and the laboratory CBR test
results of the surface soils we believe that the following slab on grade
section may be used for design provided the subgrade is proof rolled or
recompacted to at least 95 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 and vehicular traffic is not anticipated
Four 4 inch concrete over four 4 inch base course
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comment prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phases of the project This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to the start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations

Imported fill material if required should consist of select
non expansive material such as select borrow coral or cinder and should
be tested for suitability as fill material by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to its delivery to the project site
If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings in the project site were determined by
taping from the existing structures as indicated on the undated Existing
Site Plan of the project site provided by Department of the Navy
Elevations of the borings were determined by interpolation from elevation
contours on the plan The physical locations and elevations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on any profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
