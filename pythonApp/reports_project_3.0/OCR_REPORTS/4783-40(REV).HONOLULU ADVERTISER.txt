This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the fine grading building foundation and infrastructure installation work for
The Honolulu Advertiser Production and Distribution Facility project at Kapolei from
through March 9 2004 in accordance with our fee proposal dated
and additional budget request dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that the Honolulu Advertiser is
developing a new production and distribution facility adjacent to the City of Kapolei in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property of the new facility is
bounded approximately by Kapolei Parkway Extension future to the north
Kalaeloa Boulevard to the east an old Oahu Sugar Railroad track to the south and
open undeveloped land to the west
Based on the site plan provided we understand that the proposed development
will consist of a new building that will house a mailroom newsprint room plant room
and a press hall In general shallow foundations consisting of spread and continuous
footings will be used to support the interior columns and perimeter wall loads of the
proposed structure In addition an asphalt concrete paved parking lot and service

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
As previously mentioned the fine grading work consisted of placing 2 feet of
non expansive select granular capping fill materials across the project site to achieve
the design finished grades Koga Engineering performed the fine grading operations
Prior to placing the capping fill the subgrades were scarified to a depth of about
12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Following the subgrade preparation limestone fines from Grace Pacific
Corporation was used for the 24 inch thick non expansive capping layer required below
the building slab and pavement areas The 24 inch thick non expansive capping fill
under the building slab and pavement areas were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 and 95 percent relative compaction The bottom of the footing excavations were
recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as described
above
During the earthwork operations we obtained samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our
laboratory for testing We established the maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations
to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil
conditions were observed A total of 1 378 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with
ASTM D 2922 and D3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 74 of this report
The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing
and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the fine grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
