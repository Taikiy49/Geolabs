Dear Mr Phillips
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations for the Kapolei West Golf Course Grading project from
through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is northeast of Barbers Point Harbor and south of the existing
railroad easement in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site was
previously used by Hawaiian Cement as a borrow site for aggregates Borrow pits up to
20 to 30 feet deep have been excavated The project consists of filling the borrow pits
up to the design finished mass grades The filling of the borrow pits was conducted on
an intermittent basis
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations for the project site
Aina Nui Corporation
Page 2
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
C J Peterson Services Inc performed the site grading operations The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills to achieve
the design finished mass grades During the site grading operations samples of the
on site and imported materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to
our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 24 of this report
These tests were performed on the constructed fills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representatives observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading work for the above
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
