As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study con
sisted of five 5 hand auger probes within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story
wood structure approximately 45 by 45 feet in plan dimension
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage will
be set back about 20 feet from Kahena Street

portion of the house Concrete slab on grade is planned for

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 12 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of
the subdivision development about 800 feet north of Ainapo
Street It is bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest sub
division lots on the southeast and northwest and a concrete
drainage channel on the northeast The lot slopes downward
from Kahena Street toward the east at about 17 percent slope
The elevation of Lot 12 generally varies from 162 to 182 feet
The existing rectangular concrete drainage channel is about 10
feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending
across the rear portion of the lot
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations Lot 12 was
covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over
from previous grading operations in the area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicates that Lot 12 is underlain by a mixture
of moderately to highly expansive brown clay and gravel The
amount of cobbles and gravel appears to increase at about
2 foot depth However significant amounts of expansive clay
were still noted at that level
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the hand auger
probes are presented on the attached Log of Hand Probes Plates

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay and gravel fill The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have
moderate to high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 12
the slab on grade and foundation construction would require
special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results
in lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Concrete slab on
grade and driveway should also be properly designed for
expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 70 The spread footings can then be placed on the
compacted non expansive soil

Alternatively the individual spread footings can be embedded a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the compacted select material or stiff
existing ground
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Short retaining walls generally less than 4 feet high are
anticipated around the garage
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be
overexcavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 The overexcavation
and replacement should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches
beyond the footing width

The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 18
inches below the outside finished grade Therefore the total
depth of the above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 5
feet below the outside finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
Off site non expansive soil should be used as backfill over
the wall footing and behind the wall
The wall should be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of
35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the
footing and compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral
resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the wall
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage Finish grade
elevations were not available at the time of our investiga
tion However it appears that the garage area would require
several feet of fill in order to obtain sufficient level area
for the garage Select non expansive borrow soils should be
used for fills The on site clay soils are expansive and are
unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the
expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3
feet below the slab subgrade and replaced with non expansive
borrow soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 This overexcavation and replacement should be
extended for a distance of 3 feet beyond the perimeter of the
slab
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered in the driveway area
Since the driveways can tolerate some amount of heaving the
clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet
below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of
fill is planned select non expansive borrow material should be
used to cap over the expansive clay

Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive
material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in the
driveway in order to allow for some relative movement of the
concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the above driveway design is recommended
to reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
6 1 H V ratio or flatter
Minor site grading is planned for the lot development
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material The on site clay soil should not be used for
fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling

Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM D 1557
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements and founda
tions should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations
and to avoid ponding conditions near the slab and foundation
area
