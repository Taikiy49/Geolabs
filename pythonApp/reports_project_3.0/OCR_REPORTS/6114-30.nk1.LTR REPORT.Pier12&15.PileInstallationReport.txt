PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed waterfront improvements project is at Piers 12 and 15 along
Nimitz Highway in Honolulu Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It was proposed to
increase the berthing capacity of Piers 12 and 15 for the Marine Spill Response Corporation
MSRC and Clean Island Council CIC primary spill response vessels and barges as part
of the Honolulu Harbor Redevelopment The construction of the waterfront improvements
involved installation of new berthing and mooring structures to create approximately 300 and
500 linear feet of berthing spaces adjacent to the existing Piers 12 and 15 respectively
To support new mooring dolphins and loading platforms eighty seven 87 20 inch
octagonal precast prestressed battered concrete piles off shore were required to be
installed to tip elevations of 90 and 110 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at Piers 12 and 15

fifteen 20 inch octagonal off shore piles from August 10 to The purpose
of the test pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles with selected
driving equipment at the project sites and confirm modify the pile length for the production
pile installation

20 inch octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles The maximum rated energy of the
Junttan HHS9 is approximately 79 200 foot pounds at a maximum stroke of 4 feet A wave
equation analysis was performed to examine drivability of the piles with the above pile
driving hammer The wave equation analysis report was submitted by Geolabs dated

MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
A total of thirty eight 38 micropiles designated as MP12 1 through MP12 38 were
installed between May 27 2015 and at Pier 12 to support the structural
slab for the project The approximate locations of the micropiles are shown on the Plate 1
In addition four micropiles were installed at Pier 15 to support the new shed roof column
We observed the micropile installation at Pier 12 however the micropile installation at Pier
15 was observed by others
In general the micropiles were installed by drilling 7 inch diameter steel casing with
a Casagrande C6 track mounted drill rig and or a Beretta T59 track mounted drill rig Drill
rigs were equipped with a down the hole hammer drill bit The steel casing was extended
down to a tip elevation providing a minimum bond length of 35 feet in the coralline material
Cuttings within the casing were cleaned out using compressed air pumped through the
hollow drill rods After the drilled hole was cleaned a threaded reinforcement bar was
inserted into the drilled hole down to near the bottom of the hole It should be noted that the
bottom 40 feet of steel reinforcement bar was coated to prevent steel corrosion The
micropile was then grouted using tremie methods until relatively clean grout was observed
at the top of the steel casing The steel casing was extracted within the bonded zone and
left in the unbonded zone as a permanent casing Grout level was maintained by topping
off at the top of the casing while extracting each section of the casing
The micropiles generally extended to depths ranging from about 63 to 67 feet below
the existing ground surface Excessive grout volumes were required in some of the
micropile locations due to erratic coralline consistency Grout ratios between the actual
