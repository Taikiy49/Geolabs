Based on our review of the available rainfall data and monthly precipitation
summaries by the National Weather Service Forecast Office Honolulu NWSFOH the
past six month monitoring period generally experienced normal rainfall conditions at the
project site Extraordinary precipitation events were not experienced Precipitation
generally increased during the winter months of October 2006 through March 2007
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels In addition following extended periods of below normal
precipitation earth movements generally decrease slightly

Landslide Head Area
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide indicates that the roadway pavement and northern road
shoulder continue to experience settlement of the ground surfaces The ground
settlements have produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that
traverse the roadway Open cracks and holes about 2 to 4 inches wide and
localized settlement depressions of about 1 foot in diameter have formed along the
existing pavement repair patches in the center of the road Void spaces underlying
the larger open cracks in the roadway were observed Settlement within the
southern half of the paved roadway has produced regions conducive to the ponding
of surface rainwater In addition older pavement patches are beginning to show
signs of deterioration with e inch to inch open cracks developing over the
existing patchwork Based on our observation of the slowly increasing roadway
deformation re leveling of depressed sections of roadway and patching of the open
cracks is recommended to reduce the ponding and infiltration of runoff into the
pavement subgrade
Finally the open landscaped slopes on the northern side of Kuahea Street show
minimal visible evidence of earth movement
Kuahea Street Area A
The primary area of ground surface deformation within Kuahea Street Area A was
observed to extend between about 2326 Kuahea Street and 2221 Kuahea Place
Based on our observations the temporary pavement repairs on Kuahea Street are
in generally fair condition with some thin cracks visible in the elongated narrow
concrete patches The undulatory condition of the existing concrete pavement on
Kuahea Street appears to be slowly increasing in magnitude although no
significant pavement surface rupture was observed The pavement settlement on
Kuahea Place continues to slowly increase with clearly defined lateral movement
boundaries adjacent to the area of settlement Ongoing repair of the existing home
and CRM retaining wall fronting 2334 Kuahea Street were observed
The street repairs conducted along a portion of Waiomao Road remain in good
condition with minimal visible evidence of renewed ground deformation
