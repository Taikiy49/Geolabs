PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the southern portion of the Valley of the Temples Mortuary
along Chapel Hill Road The project site has a total land area of 0 85 acres and is bounded
by other lots on both the east and west sides Available information indicated that the project
site was previously graded prior to the design and construction of this project The project
generally involves redirecting the course of the existing drainage pipe extending the plateau
possibly constructing a retaining wall and installing granite lined stairs to the granite lined
monuments A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
Due to a misunderstanding between the design team and the project contractor
Geolabs Inc was not retained to provide earthwork observations during construction at the
site Work at the site was essentially completed when this oversight became apparent and
Geolabs Inc was requested to conduct an exploration to assess where fill stability had
been attained during the earthwork This exploration consisted of performing compaction
tests in test pits excavated at the site and the installation and monitoring of settlement
gauges at selected locations on the site

COMPACTION TESTING
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 4
On Geolabs Inc excavated three test pits at selected locations
within the project site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Field density tests were performed
in the excavated test pits in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge
Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plate 5 of this
report These tests were performed on prepared ground surfaces in the pits A total of
eight tests were performed Of these tests four failed to meet the compaction requirement of
85 of the maximum dry density established in our laboratory However review of the
geotechnical engineering report for the project indicated that the field densities encountered
in the test pits generally exceeded those of the existing fill present at the site
SETTLEMENT MONITORING
Four settlement points were installed at selected locations on the site as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Initial elevation readings were taken with optical survey equipment on
Subsequent survey readings of the settlement points were taken on
August 10 2009 August 8 2009 and September 21
2009 The survey readings were taken by ParEn Inc The readings were consistent and did
not indicate settlement
CONCLUSIONS
The project is essentially a grave site As such there are no significant structural
requirements for the improvements at the site Site grading was performed primarily for
aesthetic purposes and the infrastructure work was generally limited to realignment of an
existing drainline Therefore typical performance of fill such as for a residential or
commercial site is not a requirement for this project
