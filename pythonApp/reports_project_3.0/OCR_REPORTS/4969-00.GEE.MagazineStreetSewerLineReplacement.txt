SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Magazine Street Sewer Line Replacement project in the
Punchbowl area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the proposed sewer line replacement project These recommendations are
intended for the excavation and installation of the new replacement sewer line and sewer
manholes only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located along Magazine Street Spencer Street Thurston
Avenue Victoria Street and Wilder Avenue in the Punchbowl area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The proposed project consists of the replacement and rehabilitation of
approximately 4 200 linear feet of 6 inch vitrified clay pipe VCP Based on the
information provided we understand that the existing 6 inch VCP sewer line along
Spencer Street and Victoria Street will be replaced with approximately 625 linear feet of
8 inch VCP sewer line using cut and cover methods In addition the installation of four
new sewer manhole structures are planned Also we understand that the existing VCP
sewer line along the remaining areas will be rehabilitated using Cured In Place Pipe
CIPP

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in
age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island After a long period of
volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield
volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone
formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located on the southern flank of Punchbowl Hill The
Punchbowl Hill Puowaiana is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the
end of a spur of the Koolau Range The tuff is mostly brown palagonitized vitric ash and
lapili with scattered fragments of coral limestone and Koolau basalt
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The process of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was raised
such that sediments accumulated at higher elevations

volcanic cinders to the maximum depth drilled of 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The volcanic cinders generally consisted of medium dense to very dense sands
and cemented sand
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels may vary significantly depending on

the limited space available Because poorly graded sands were encountered in our

Because the site is underlain by very dense and hard deposits installation of driven
sheet piles may be difficult The excavation support and shoring system used must
comply with all applicable safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified

shoring system used The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should
be made the sole responsibility of the contractor

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipes
Loads on buried pipes are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between
the backfill material and the trench walls To calculate the vertical loads on the
buried sewerline pipes we recommend that an average unit weight of
120 pounds per cubic foot pcf for the backfill material and a coefficient of friction
of 0 25 be used Earth forces upon the pipe generally increase rapidly with the
width of the trench Therefore the width of the trench should be kept to a
minimum Traffic loads on the buried pipes if applicable should also be
considered for the portions of the pipes located in roadway areas
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great effect upon the load
supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore pipe bedding is an important design
consideration In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes and
manhole structures located over stiff dense soil conditions The granular bedding
should extend beyond the sides of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth the
outside diameter of the pipe A typical section of the trench detail is presented on
Plate 3 for reference
Backfill
In general free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to
about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce the need for compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes Compaction by water ponding or jetting should not be
allowed

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to

of utility trench backfill a temporary pavement section may be considered at the
completion of the trench work A permanent pavement section may be placed after
the backfilled area has come to equilibrium under backfill earth loads
Pipe Settlement
Primary settlement of the new pipe installation is generally caused by the
difference in the unit weight of the lighter excavated original earth and the
heavier compacted backfill material placed over the pipeline The net increase in
loading may cause settlement of the underlying subsoils below the trench invert
Based on our exploration and analyses small primary settlements on the order of
about less than 1 inch may be anticipated for the new sewerline alignment

The manhole excavations will need to be properly backfilled The excavated

to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to

D 1557 Within the roadway area the top 3 feet below the finished subgrade
