SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Koele Wellness Facility project located in Lanai City on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the mass grading construction one and two story building structure foundations road
pavements utilities excavation and fill placement only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located on the northern side of Koele Lodge The area is
currently covered with a well manicured lawn and concrete walkways The project
involves the construction of a complex of one and two story buildings along with a new
parking area Structural details were not available at the time this report was prepared
The project will involve site grading and infrastructure installation including driveways
parking areas sidewalks and utilities
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of the
Koele Wellness Facility Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated May 1 2006 The scope of our work included the following tasks and
work efforts

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is located on the northern side of the Lodge at Koele on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii At present the site is covered with a well manicured lawn and
concrete walkways
In general the site consists of a relatively flat open area with the eastern portion
sloping from east to west Based on the topographic plan provided the existing ground
surface elevation at the site ranges from approximately 1 741 to 1 747 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 1 and 3
were drilled at the proposed parking area to about 6 feet below the existing ground
surface and Boring Nos 2 and 4 were drilled at the proposed building area to about
20 feet The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Generally stiff to hard clayey silt material was encountered during our field
exploration Highly weathered basalt rock was encountered in the silty clay matrix at a
depth of about 19 feet in Boring No 2 to the maximum depth explored below the
existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may
fluctuate depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions
of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in Appendix A Results of the
laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our laboratory testing on the on site soils indicated low expansion potential when
subjected to changes in the moisture content Based on our field exploration and
laboratory test results we believe that a shallow foundation may be used to support the
proposed structures The shallow foundations should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches and bear on properly compacted fill or selected stiff in situ material
To reduce the potential for structural distress to the future slabs on grade
resulting from the potential swelling of the surface clayey soils we recommend
supporting concrete slabs on grade on at least 12 inches of structural fill materials
Special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements presented
in the Site Preparation section of this report to reduce the potential for shrink swell
effects on concrete slabs
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Based on the topographic plan provided we anticipate that the mass grading

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material We recommend

After clearing and grubbing the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of
10 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
For pavement subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction in the area of future roadway and parking area to
confirm the adequacy of the pavement section
If zones of soft loose or saturated solids are encountered at the subgrade level
deeper excavation maybe required to expose underlying firm material The
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill In the
event shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade the soil
should be thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again as
recommended above The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it
meets the requirements for fill material

Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of materials less than 3 inches in largest
dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should have a
low expansion potential and should be free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Our laboratory test results indicated a low swell potential for the on site clayey silt
material Therefore most of the on site clayey material generally may be suitable
for fill material within 1 foot of finish subgrade below slabs on grade
Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to the County of Maui Department of Public Works
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated September 1986
Structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Geolabs Inc should test imported fill materials for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture
Hawaii California

is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork Geolabs Inc should perform observations and soil
density tests during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required
degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less
than required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of
moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be
noted that the moisture requirement of the fills and subgrades about 2 percent
above the optimum moisture is an important requirement for the use of on site
clayey soils
Excavation
Based on our field exploration utility installation excavation may involve cuts into
very stiff residual soil deposit consisting of clayey silt with some gravel It is
anticipated that the residual soils may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment However isolated remnant boulders may be encountered which may
require overexcavation for their removal or breaking to extend the trench
Our discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on field
data obtained from the borings drilled at the site We recommend encouraging
contractors bidding on this project to examine the site conditions and soil data to
make their own interpretation
Cutand Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that cut
slopes would expose soils consisting of stiff to hard clayey silts In general we

2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the

Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet high should be constructed by overfilling and then

Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet high should not be accepted In the event

Water should be diverted away from the top of slopes and slope planting should be
provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes
3 2 Building Foundations
Based on the soil condition at the project site we believe that shallow spread
and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed structures
provided they are embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the finished grade to
reduce the effects of the potential expansion of clayey soils
An allowable bearing pressure up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of residential building foundations bearing on properly compacted fill
or stiff on site clayey silts This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces The bottom of foundation should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete
Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the existing grade Footings constructed near tops of slopes
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
or on sloping ground should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum 5 foot
horizontal setback distance measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope
face
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fill or
on site stiff to hard clayey silts as recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total
Differential settlement is estimated to be less than one half 7 inch The foundation
system shall be designed to resist the total and differential settlements
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should
be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be deepened to the underlying firm materials
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 psf
This assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 6 inches of soil
should be neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on
properly compacted fill or on site stiff clayey material
We recommend that a Geolabs representative observe footing excavations prior to
placement of reinforcement steel and concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths

3 3 Slabs On Grade
Stiff to hard clayey soils with low expansion potential underlies the proposed
structures however some soil expansion shrinkage may occur due to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils we recommend
properly preparing and capping the subgrade soils below the one and two story
buildings with at least 12 inches of structural fill material capping fill below the slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a
minimum of 12 inches
We strongly recommend placing the non expansive select granular capping fill as
soon as possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The
completed capping fill and the underlying subgrade soils should be maintained above
optimum moisture content prior to placement of concrete We believe it is very critical to
maintain the moisture content in order to minimize future settlement Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend thoroughly
moistening the soil to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
In general we recommend placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation between the concrete
slab and capping fill to serve as a capillary break and to provide more uniform support
of the slab To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion
fill
To reduce the potential for water infiltration into the slabs and foundations we
recommend embedding the thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas at least
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should

3 4 Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed driveways and
parking areas In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and pick up trucks occasionally with heavy trucks Based on the
CBR value of the on site natural clayey subgrade material and generally light traffic
anticipated for the project we recommend using the following pavement section for
preliminary budgeting purposes until the finish subgrade material is verified and
laboratory testing of this material is conducted
Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soil below the pavement area should be moisture conditioned to
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction The subgrade should be thoroughly moistened and kept moist until
covered by select borrow subbase and base course Base course and select borrow
subbase materials should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density test should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above sections

3 5 Utilities
We envision that new utility lines and utility connections may be required for the
proposed project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes for support
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for
the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes We believe it is critical to use a
free draining granular material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use
of on site soils as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended and
could result in pipe damage
In general the excavated on site soil or select granular material may be used
from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to the finish subgrade The backfill material
should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts about 8 inches in loose thickness and mechanically
compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the
materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to
compact the backfill material
3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is highly recommended
that gutter systems be installed around the structures to divert discharge away from the

foundation and pavement areas Blockage or clogging of drainage system could cause
ponding of water at the house structure and foundations resulting in foundation and

Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be
avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and

Because ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths of about 6 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface The
borings were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger equipment at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the field exploration operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designations D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively natural and remolded
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These
tests were run on air dried samples The test results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in_ soil classification
Graphic representation of the test result is provided on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
