In accordance with your request a site visit was made by a Project
Geologist from our firm to make observations of the cavities encountered
in the fueling rack apron subgrade excavation ies
It was noted that at least four 4 cavities had been encountered and
exposed at the time of the site visit One of the cavities in the
southern corner of the excavation appeared to be about 3 to 4 feet in
diameter and extended under the existing asphalt road adjacent to the rack
area A cavity in the northern corner exposed in a excavated trench
appeared to be about 5 feet in diameter and extended below the apron
area The other two voids were not well exposed A sinkhole was also
found across the road from the apron area Probing and grouting as
recommended in our report had not been performed
Based upon our observations we recommend that the exposed voids in
the apron area be openéd and backfilled with non expansive granular
material placed in thin lifts and compacted to 95 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The large void extending under the roadway should have two 2 or
more holes drilled into its roof amd lean concrete or grout should be
injected into the cavity to backfill it This would eliminate the need to
excavate through the existing road surface

We also recommend that the existing and proposed road areas be

It is our opinion that the use of geophysical methods i e ground
penetration radar would be quicker and more economical than the
traditional drill probing and grouting method By using this geophysical
method potential cavity locations could be delineated and assessed
Potential cavities with sufficient roof thickness could be left alone and
the ones with thin cover could be spot treated by drilling into the
cavities and injecting grout to backfill them
We estimate that two to four days of radar surveys would be required
