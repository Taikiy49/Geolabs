Subject Soil Engineering Investigation Report
Proposed Addition to Satellite Communication Terminal
Islands
Gent lemen
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation
performed for the above referenced project
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface soil
conditions by drilling test borings Data obtained from the borings
and laboratory testing of the selected soil samples recovered along
with a review of previous soil investigations were utilized in our
analysis to provide recommendations for the design of the foundations
for the proposed building addition

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project is located in a relatively level parking lot adjacent to
the northern side of the existing Satellite Communication Terminal
Building The parking lot is paved with asphalt concrete
Outside of the A C parking is a lawn area
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed building addition will consist of a rectangular one story
structure about 44 by 60 feet in plan dimensions adjacent to the
existing Satellite Communication Terminal Building
It is anticipated that the building will be of reinforced concrete
construction Maximum column loads are anticipated to be 80 kips each
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the northwestern side of the island of
Guam the largest of the Mariana Islands The approximate location
of the project is shown on Plate 1
The foundation of the island is composed of volcanic rocks inc luding
pyroclastic materials which were extruded during early to mid Tertiary
time Volcanic activity ended during the Oligocene epoch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1173 00 Page 3
Following the cessation of this volcanic activity extensive coral
algal reef deposits were laid down in the area of what is now the
northern half of the island from the Miocene through Pleistocene
epochs
Tectonic uplifts has raised these old reef deposits to form a plateau
upon which the project site is located
Subsurface Exploration
Two borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15 4 and 15 5 feet
below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface soil
conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing The
aproximate locations of the new borings and previous boring for the
existing terminal structure are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
A description of our field exploration along with the new and
previous boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in
the appendices of this report
Soil Conditions
After penetrating a surface layer of inches of asphaltic
concrete our borings encountered medium dense to dense Fill material
consisting of reddish brown to tan sandy coral gravel with brown
Silt This layer of fill varied from 2 5 to 4 0 feet in thickness

Underlying this man made fill our borings encountered dense white
coral reef deposits to 15 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
No groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Ee RERUN LON
The field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by a layer
of medium dense to dense sandy gravel fill over dense coral reef
deposits
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results shallow
foundations and slab on grade construction may be utilized for the
proposed structure
Buildi ng Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
Footings should bear a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade Along the existing structure the new footings should bear on
the same level as the adjacent existing footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1173 00 Page 5
For footings bearing in the existing fill material an allowable
bearing value of 3000 p s f may be utilized provided that the bottom
of the footing excavation is recompacted to 95 maximum density as per
ASTM D 1557 78 prior to the placement of reinforcing steel
If the footings are extended to bear on the underlying dense coral
reef deposits the bearing value may be increased to 6000 p s f
The above bearing capacity values are for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads
are considered
Minimum footing widths of 24 inches for spread footings and 18 inches
for continuous footings are recommended
The bottom of ali footings should be free of loose soils and inspected
by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing should
be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must
be overexcavated a minimum of 4 feet and backfilled with well compacted
granular structural fill material to prevent footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1173 00 Page 6
Estimated total and differential settlements are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half inch for footings bearing entirely on
upper fill and less than one fourth inch for footings bearing entirely
on dense coral reef deposits
Cavity Probing
Although no cavities were encountered in our investigation previous
experience has shown that solution cavities commonly occur in coral
reef deposits In order to lessen the possibility of footing settle
ment due to collapse of any potential cavities located close to the
footing excavations we recommend that drilled probes be made at each
major column location to detect any voids which may exist below the
footing
The drilled probes should be 4 inches in diameter and extend at least
8 feet below the bottom of the footing The resulting drilled holes
should then be backfilled with Portland cement grout injected at low
pressures to fill and detect any possible cavities
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth
pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation
system and by frictional resistance developed between the bottom of
the foundations and the bearing soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p c f This assumes that the granular
soil around the foundations is well compacted
The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a coefficient of
friction of 0 4
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety
Slabs On Grade
Generally the on site fill material has low expansion potential The
existing A C pavement and the underlying subgrade fill appear dense
If necessary the new slab on grade can be placed above this A C
surface
For the anticipated static floor loads a 4 inch slab over 4 inches of
well graded aggregate base course is recommended
A plastic vapor over 4 inches of 3 Fine gravel 1 2 to 3 4 inch size
rock aggregate may be substituted for the base course should a capil
lary break be deemed necessary to protect the floor covering A thin
layer of sand should be placed over the plastic barrier to aid in
concrete curing and to minimize shrinkage cracks

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
t is recommended that the footing excavations cavity probing and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construc

subsurface investigation Modification may be needed if the actual
conditions are substantially different from those anticipated
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 to 15 4 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on Site Plan
Plate 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings and probings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were Classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is pre
sented on the Log of Borings Plates A l through A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a standard penetration Sampler or a 2 4 inch O pD split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
