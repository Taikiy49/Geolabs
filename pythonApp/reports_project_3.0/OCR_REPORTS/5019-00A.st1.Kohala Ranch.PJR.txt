SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses performed
for the design of new pavements for the Intersection Improvements project at Kohala
Mountain Road and Kohala Ranch Road located in the District of North Kohala on the
Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This pavement justification report presents our geotechnical recommendations
derived from our analyses for the design of pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The Kohala Ranch Project IV project site is located at the intersection of Kohala
Ranch Road and Ala Kahua Road in the District of North Kohala on the Island of
Hawaii The project site encompasses an area of approximately 74 acres of
undeveloped land We understand that it is desired to provide infrastructure for the
future residential lots at the project site As part of the project improvements will be
made at the intersection of Kohala Ranch Road and Kohala Mountain Road Therefore
this pavement justification report was prepared in support of the design of the new
pavements for the project
It should be noted that a separate geotechnical engineering report has been
prepared in support of the design of subdivision development and other elements of the
project Detailed discussion and geotechnical recommendations for design of the other
elements of the project are presented in the Geotechnical Engineering Report for the
project prepared by Geolabs Inc

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five
shield volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna
Kea which has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in
1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Kohala Mountain which has
formed the northern portion of the island The project site is underlain by basaltic lava
flows of the Hawi Volcanic Series of Kohala Mountain which were deposited during the
Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and Macdonald 1946 The Hawi Volcanic Series is
typically composed of basaltic lava flows that are overlain by a relatively thin layer of
palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil at the project site
differs from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the Island of Hawaii in that the in situ
moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a dry friable state The soils at
the site have been subjected to wind erosion that has resulted in the formation of a
desert pavement on the surface of the site This desert pavement is a surface layer of
cobbles and gravel which were left behind as the finer ash was blown away
The lava formation at the project site appears to be of a a and pahoehoe basalt
type flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean Ava is typically
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Typically a more
dense and layered lava rock material is contained within the lava flow core Pahoehoe
is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lavas These
cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava
tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are
formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like

2 2 Surface and Subsurface Conditions
The project site is located in the District of North Kohala on the Island of Hawaii
The Intersection Improvements project site is located at the intersection of Kohala
Ranch Road and Kohala Mountain Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The ground surface at the project site generally is irregular Based on the
topographic map provided the project site is sloping downward from east to west The
existing ground surface elevations at the project site vary from about 3 120 to
3 190 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The majority of the project site is underlain by a a
basalt mixed with varying amounts of volcanic ash materials at the ground surface A
dense layer of surface vegetation consisting of shrubs and grasses was observed
during our site reconnaissance of the project site Large trees up to about 3 feet in
diameter lined both sides of Kohala Mountain Road
We anticipate that the project site generally is underlain by volcanic ash materials
intermixed with a a basalt The a a basalt is generally interbedded with layers of dense
pahoehoe basalt formation It should be noted that lava tubes and cavities are
commonly encountered in the basalt formations Therefore the presence of lava tubes
and or cavities should be anticipated and expected at the project site
Groundwater is not anticipated at or near the pavement subgrade elevations
However groundwater levels may change due to seasonal precipitation surface water
runoff and other factors

3 1 Methodology of Pavement Design
Two types of pavement structural sections flexible and rigid pavements were
considered in the pavement analyses for this project The flexible pavement sections
presented in this report generally were determined based on the methodology described
in Chapter 3 of the revised Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002 The
Pavement Design Manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of
Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and Research Branch The
pavement design methodology is based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed
and used by the California Department of Transportation Caltrans
The design procedures for the rigid pavement sections generally were based on
the design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design
for Concrete Highway and Street Pavements

Preparation Below Pavement Section
We anticipate that the majority of the new roadway pavemenis will be
constructed at or near the existing grades In general the area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork
Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site or disposed of in a designated area to reduce
the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
stiff and or dense materials The excavation resulting from the over excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted granular fill The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site

3 6 Fill Materials
In general we recommend that fill materials for the project consist of structural fill
materials 6 inch minus granular fills In general structural fill materials should consist
of well graded granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension
with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
Imported fill materials where required should consist of non expansive granular
materials such as crushed basalt or cinder sand The materials should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The
materials should have a CBR value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell
value of 1 percent or less In addition the materials should have a minimum R value of
60 or more Imported fill materials should be tested for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
3 7 Compaction Requirements
Conventional compaction testing generally is not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of
passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of passes
may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For preliminary

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils It should be noted that the
pavement subgrade soils may be susceptible to softening and loss of strength upon
saturation Therefore special attention should be given to the surface and subsurface
drainage of the pavements As a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped and
drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to
appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the
site during or after construction To collect infiltrated water into the pavement structural
section we recommend that a pavement edge drain system which is hydraulically
connected to the untreated permeable base be provided along the sides of the new
roadway
We envision that an edge drain will be needed to collect and discharge infiltrated
water into the pavement structural section from the untreated permeable base layer In
general the edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with
perforations facing down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable
separator non woven filter fabric conforming to Section 648 of the HSS should be
