INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration and
analysis performed for the proposed residence for Mr and located
at 4623 Kahala Avenue in the Kahala district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed residence will consist
of a two 2 story masonry building with a swimming pool vehicular driveway and parking
stalls
planned for the proposed two 2 story building
The site is essentially level cut and fill operations for site grading will be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the building foundations swimming pool and pavement
design for the proposed residence The recommendations presented herein are based
on the soil information obtained from borings drilled and laboratory testing performed in
connection with this exploration
The study consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings laboratory testing of
selected samples engineering analyses and report preparation The approximate
locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in the
engineering analyses to formulate our recommendations are presented in the appendices
of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 4623 Kahala Avenue in the Kahala district of Honolulu
This approximately rectangular 42 178 square foot lot is fronted by Kahala Avenue along
its northerly boundary and the existing shoreline along its southerly boundary Existing
residential lots and structures bound the site on both the east and west sides The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography of the project site is generally level with current ground elevations
ranging from about six 6 to nine 9 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Previously existing improvements including structures vehicular pavements fenced
tennis courts and sidewalks had been demolished at the time of our field exploration

It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
compacted in situ beach sand or compacted engineered embankment A footing
embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is
recommended Deeper footing embedment is generally not recommended due to the
high groundwater level the underlying thin lenses of loose coral detritus and cavities
encountered in the coral algal formations found in our drilled borings
We recommend that all footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The resulting
depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2638 00 Page 6
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to minimize the potential of footing
settlement due to consolidation of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Total footing settlement for column loads up to 50 kips is estimated to be less than
one 1 inch provided the above recommendations are followed
Cavity Probing and Grouting
It is our opinion that the foundations and the structural integrity of the proposed
residence would probably not in the short term be detrimentally affected by the presence
of the cavities located with in the underlying coral algal formations However we
recommend that probing and grouting of the cavities at the footing locations be
considered to strengthen the foundation subsoils to enhance earthquake resistance
Grouting of the voids and cavities would help to minimize their potential collapse and
resultant ground subsidence which may occur if the subsoils are stressed or adversely
impacted by vibration resulting from probable seismic or tsunami activity in the future
The probes should be drilled at each column location and on ten 10 foot centers
for line footings The probe holes should be a minimum of three 8 inches in diameter
and should extend a minimum of ten 10 feet below the footing bearing elevations or
about twelve 12 feet below existing ground elevation whichever is deeper
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2638 00 Page 7
If a cavity or void is encountered at a footing location additional probes should be
drilled to further delineate the areal extent of the void
In order to avoid having to drill the probes inside the footing excavations which
would increase drilling costs it is suggested that the probing and grouting work be
performed after site clearing or grading and prior to the commencement of footing
excavation work
The probing and grouting operations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer to see that the above recommendations are properly implemented and to
evaluate other possible solutions for treatment of the cavities and voids
Slab on Grade
The ground floor of the proposed residence is anticipated to be concrete
slab on grade
A four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum four 4 inch thick cushion
layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier should be incorporated below
the slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to
help concrete curing
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

Concrete Driveway
It is our understanding that concrete pavements will be provided for the proposed
driveway and parking stalls at the new residence
Based on the assumption that anticipated traffic will be composed of light vehicles
the following pavement section is recommended
4 Inch Concrete

8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

The thickened edges of the concrete driveway should be a minimum of twelve 12
inch thick
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in
order to control shrinkage and contraction cracks and to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete slabs The length to width ratios of the slabs should not
exceed 1 0 to 1 25 Slabs exceeding this ratio or which are otherwise not rectangular in
shape should be provided with welded wire mesh reinforcement
Swimming Pool
A swimming pool is to be constructed at the southern end of the proposed
residence It is our opinion that substantial dewatering will be required due to the fact that
the proposed swimming pool bottom will be well below the groundwater level and the on
site material is highly permeable The groundwater level was encountered in our drilled
ground surface Based on elevations estimated from the available topographic survey
map the measured groundwater levels ranged from elevations of about 0 7 to 1 2 feet

above MSL at the time of our field exploration Shoring of the excavation will therefore
be required for the proposed swimming pool construction
The swimming pool design should include provision for resistance against
hydrostatic uplift during periods when the partially submerged swimming pool is emptied
for maintenance purposes A minimum groundwater level up to an elevation of two 2
feet above MSL is recommended for design purposes
Considering the effect of backfilling the following lateral earth pressures may be
considered for swimming pool wall design

Walls restrained at the top 20 p s f equivalent

Additional lateral earth pressures due to adjacent surcharge or foundation loads
should be considered where applicable
Scheduling of construction of the swimming pool should be considered as it relates
to the schedule for foundation construction We feel that the swimming pool should be
constructed prior to construction of footings in the immediate vicinity to preclude possible
loss of support of those footings caused by dewatering and excavation for construction
of the swimming pool

Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris rubble and organic matter encountered during
grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The proposed building and pavement areas should then be scarified to a depth of
six 6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture conditioned and
recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 To minimize disturbance to adjacent existing
residences and to minimize pore water pressure build up in sandy subsoils causing
possible soil weakening use of heavy vibratory compaction equipment should be
avoided
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the site
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10 inches loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter run off
water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to being transported to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
