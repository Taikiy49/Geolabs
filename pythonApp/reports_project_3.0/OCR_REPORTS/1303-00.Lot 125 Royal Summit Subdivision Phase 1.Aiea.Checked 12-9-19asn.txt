In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling four shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
one story split level structure approximately 48 by 60 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage
driveway and the entire house The house will be of wood frame
construction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered will generally be
suitable for foundation support of the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that its swelling potential may increase to moderate if
allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize the swelling potential
the subgrade soil should be kept moist and protected from drying
during the period of time after excavation up until the final
concrete placement
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill below the slab to minimize the swelling
potential of the natural soil
The garage and driveway will probably bear directly on the on site
soils rather than the non expansive granular fill The special
foundation and subgrade preparation recommendations for these areas
are listed in the following sections

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
Capacity of the soil
The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the SLAB ON GRADE section is also required
Slabs On Grade
Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the garage driveway and the
entire house Where a minimum of 1 foot of imported non expansive
soil such as crusher waste is used below the slab the subgrade
soils should be kept moist for a period of at least 2 days prior to
concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
Where on site soil is encountered at subgrade level garage and
driveway special subgrade preparation with lime saturation are
recommended to minimize the swelling potential of the soils The
general procedures listed hereunder for lime saturation should be
followed
1 Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations at a rate of about one pound per square foot
prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
2 The subgrade soils should be water saturated by constant sprinkling
for at least 2 days prior to concrete placement to reduce the
swelling potential of the underlying subsoil It is important that
the soils engineer should be notified to inspect the above subgrade
preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying soils
prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
5 The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying

5 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
slabs
It is recommended that slabs on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded base course aggregate as a cushion layer and should be
structurally separated from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that CMU retaining walls are planned for the
proposed residence The guidelines for wall designs are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on imported select borrow
or on stiff on site soil

3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter
systems should be installed around the house and that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the foundations and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1303 00 Page 8
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall
or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
