in the Ka anapali area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The existing resort facility is
approximately rectangular in shape encompassing approximately 15 43 acres in land
area Based on the information provided the project will be developed in two phases
generally within the existing on grade parking lots on the Lahaina and Napili sides of the
Maui Marriott premises In addition we understand that the existing ballroom parking

Based on the moderate to heavy column loads anticipated for the Lahaina Tower
and Garage we recommended a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch
octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles bearing on the dense boulder layers and or
hard basalt rock formation be used for support of these building structures
The 16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles were designed based on an allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 120 tons per pile for the Lahaina Tower and Garage
structures The 16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles would need to extend to average
depths of about 120 feet below the existing ground surface in order to achieve the
allowable compressive load capacity Based on our field exploration some dense zones
were encountered in the shallow subsurface in some of the borings drilled across the
project site Therefore we recommend predrilling to depths on the order of about 40 feet
below the ground surface be implemented at each pile location
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
driven pile installation for the resort development only The general contractor was
Swinerton Builders and the concrete pile installation subcontractor was Healy Tibbitts
Builders Inc
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 13 concrete piles at various locations
across the site was conducted from March 23 through 28 2006 The locations of the test
piles are shown on the Pile Location Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 The purpose of the
test pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles given the
subsurface conditions the selected driving equipment and methods of concrete pile
installation In addition the test pile program also provided information on the driving
resistance and contours of the load bearing strata across the site which was used to
estimate the length of the production concrete piles
A Junttan HHS9 hydraulic impact hammer was used to drive the 16 5 inch
octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles test piles Our field observations
indicated that a stroke height of about 3 5 feet had a maximum rated energy of
approximately 69 500 foot pounds per blow of the hammer

Static Pile Load Test
In accordance with the project specifications a compressive pile load test was
conducted on a selected test pile designated as Pile No 91 on April 1 and 2 2006 by
Malama Civil Engineering The test pile was loaded to a maximum test load of 480 kips
200 percent of the design load of 160 kips in 30 kip load increments in general
accordance with the Quick Loading Procedure of ASTM D 1143 as modified by our office
Results of the static pile load test are presented in the Pile Load Test Report prepared by
Malama Civil Engineering
Pile Driving Acceptance Criteria
Based on our field observations and the results of the test pile program we believe
that the driving equipment Junttan HHS9 used and methods of pile installation are
acceptable for installing the specified production piles in general accordance with the
project specifications In addition it is our professional opinion that the test piles have
been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of
240 kips 120 tons with a factor of safety of 2 0
Based on the results of the test pile program we believe that the following
production pile driving criteria should be used for the above project The production piles
should be driven with the same type of hammer Junttan HHS9 with a stroke height of
3 5 feet at the end of the pile driving operations The pile driving operation may be
terminated when the aggregated blow counts are at least 100 blows per foot for the last
foot or 10 blows per inch for the last 3 inches subsequently modified to 6 inches In
addition the pile driving also may be terminated as requested by the Geolabs field
representative
Production Pile Driving
Based on the test pile driving program and the static pile load test we
recommended production piles on the order of about 120 feet in length be used In
addition a predrilling depth of 45 feet was used to facilitate the pile driving operation
through the surface fill crust and dense zones in the shallow subsurface
The production piles were driven with the same hammer Junttan HHS9 with a
stroke height of about 3 5 feet at the end of the pile driving operations A total of
277 production piles excluding the 13 test piles consisting of 16 inch octagonal
precast pre stressed concrete piles were driven from March 28 through May 26 2006
