Dear Mr Mansour
At your request we have completed the observation of the foundation probing and
grouting for the subject project
The project involves the construction of a Sewage Treatment Plant STP near Hole
No 9 of the Hualalai Golf Course within the Four Seasons Resort Hawaii at Hualalai
development located to the north of Kailua Kona in the District of North Kona on the island of
Hawaii Foundation probe drilling and grouting services were conducted by Kiewit Pacific
Company between January 31st and February 8th in an attempt to locate and fill subsurface
cavities beneath the foundations of four structures including a Clarifier Chlorine Contact
Basin Control Building and Belt Filter Press
The foundation probe holes were drilled with an air track drill to an average depth of
about 10 feet below the surface corresponding to the minimum depth below the bottom of the
foundation subgrade level A total of 43 probe holes were drilled totaling 432 5 lineal feet
The approximate locations of the probe holes are shown on the attached Foundation Plan
Plate 1
Based on our probe drilling results cavities and or pockets of soft soils were generally
encountered in most probe holes Cavities encountered ranged from 0 5 to 2 feet in vertical
height At the Clarifier site mostly soft materials were encountered At the proposed Belt

After the probe drilling operations each probe hole was grouted from the bottom of the
probe hole upwards by low to moderate pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit
which was retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled If the grout take was noted to
exceed about 0 5 cubic yards in one probe hole the injection was suspended The purpose
for grout interruption and reintroduction was to allow the grout to set slightly prior to
successive injection in an attempt to control excess migration of the grout off site or away
from the area of concern
Several injection passes were needed to grout those probe holes where large cavities
were encountered at the Control Building and Belt Filter Press sites The grout used to fill the
probe holes consisted of a 3 1 grout mix supplied by Hawaiian Cement Based on injection
times needed to fill the probe holes and cavities encountered we estimate approximately 29
cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 43 probe holes A summary of our observation of the
grouting operations and the estimated grout volume is provided on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted in
general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical engineering
report

Kaupulehu Sewage Treatment Facility

We understand that the foundation probing and grouting operations for the
Sewage Treatment Plant is scheduled for tomorrow We understand that Kiewit Pacific
Company will be conducting the probing and grouting operations in general accordance
with our report for the facility
Based on our previous experience observing probe and grouting work conducted
by Kiewit Pacific Company for the reservoir site we believe their work is often
performed contrary to standard practice for foundation probe and grouting work We
wish to bring this to your attention such that the work scheduled for tomorrow at the
Sewage Treatment Plant will commence according to generally accepted standard
practice The following is a general description of the standard probing and grouting
practice
Once the probe hole is drilled to the final depth typically greater than 3 inches
in diameter a temporary 2 inch diameter PVC pipe is inserted in the probe hole
to maintain an open bore for later grouting work If the temporary PVC pipe is
not inserted and the probe hole caves in the probe hole will need to be cleaned
out prior to grouting operations Grouting usually occurs once all the probe
holes have been drilled and logged
Immediately prior to probe hole grouting the temporary PVC pipe is removed to
allow for the insertion of the grouting probe The grouting of probe holes is

conducted by the injection of the grout mixture under low to moderate pressure
via a probe which is inserted into the open probe hole The grouting probe is
retracted about 1 to 2 feet from the bottom of the hole and the injection of grout
occurs from the bottom upwards The probe must be lifted slowly from the hole
as the grout fills the hole and surrounding voids where encountered Grouting
is complete once the grout becomes visible at the ground surface and no further
settlement is observed Excessive grout take should be monitored and
reapplication of grout may be necessary to completely fill voids surrounding the
probe hole
We expect that the foundation probing and grouting for the Sewage Treatment
Plant structures will be conducted in general accordance with the standard procedure
presented above to minimize incomplete filling of the voids encountered by the probe
holes
