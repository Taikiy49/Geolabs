Dear Mr. Scherman: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during the site grading of the Uluniu Solar Parking Canopy project 
from May 25, 2017 through October 24, 2017 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
May 31, 2017. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves the construction of a solar parking canopy over a porous 
concrete pavement at 340 Uluniu Street in Kailua on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. A 
Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively.  
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from May 25 through October 24, 
2017. Site grading for the project generally consisted of fills and backfills generally less 
than 2 feet thick to achieve the finished design grades.  
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Cavalier Construction, Inc. performed the site 
grading. During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site soils and imported fill 
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general

The fill and in-situ materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative compaction for the 
footing subgrade and pavement subgrade, respectively. Relative compaction refers to the 
in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as 
determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry 
weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as requested, field observation of the 
contractor’s grading activities which include foundation, walkway, and pavement subgrade 
preparation, fill and backfill placement, compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading work for the Uluniu Solar Parking Canopy project, 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in 
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.

Multi-Family Housing, 340 & 340A Uluniu Street, TMK: 4-3-052:017 & 018, 
Kailua, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated August 7, 2015. Note that the development 
plan for the project was revised subsequent to the issuance of this report.

project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our knowledge and
