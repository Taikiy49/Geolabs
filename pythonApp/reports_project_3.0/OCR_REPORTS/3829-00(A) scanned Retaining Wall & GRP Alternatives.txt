In accordance with your request we have performed a conceptual evaluation of the
feasibility of installing a retaining wall or GRP lining to reduce the risk of rockfalls at the
site This letter presents our preliminary findings Our recommendations for alternative
boulder barrier systems were presented in our report dated
Retaining Wall Alternative
The site conditions present severe limitations for the construction of a retaining wall
The retaining wall would have to be situated in front of the toe of slope to avoid massive
cuts into the steep hillside which would trigger additional instability It is not feasible to
construct a retaining wall to the total height of the existing slope approximately 50 feet
high Therefore the retaining wall would be limited in height to about 20 feet with a
sloping backfill at a maximum slope of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical This condition is
illustrated on the attached sketch
Sufficient space is currently not available behind a few of the houses in the affected
area to construct such a retaining wall Additionally the retaining wall and sloping backfill
would create a launching pad for boulders rolling down the hillside increasing the danger
to the houses below
The very limited accessibility would make this alternative expensive We do not
have experience with a comparable situation however a very rough estimate would be on
the order of 3 000 per lineal foot of retaining wall

Grouted Rubble Paving GRP
The large height of slope limited access steepness of slope and very irregular
slope surface make a GRP lining impractical for this project The present slope face is too
steep and irregular for rock placement Therefore the slope would need to be flattened
and the undercut areas filled with concrete shotcrete or grouted boulders prior to
placement of the GRP The GRP rocks would have to be placed individually with the
assistance of a crane greatly increasing the costs We do not feel that this is a practical
alternative to reduce the falling rock problem at this site
