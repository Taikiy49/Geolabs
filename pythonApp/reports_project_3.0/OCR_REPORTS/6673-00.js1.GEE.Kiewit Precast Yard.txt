Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano
ceased lava flows from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope
forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is on the
southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of the Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and to
the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to
over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of

Site Description
The project site is in the Kalaeloa area in the District of Ewa on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project site is approximately 1 700 feet north of Malakole Street and
is bounded by Kalaeloa Boulevard to the east and open land to the north south and
west as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The proposed development encompasses
approximately 35 acres
Based on available information and our previous experience in the area the
project site was previously partially graded in preparation for a new industrial park
planned for the area Prior to grading the topography across the project site appeared
to be gently sloping down toward the south with ground elevations ranging from about
40 feet Mean Sea Level MSL in the north to about Elevation 27 feet MSL in the
south with the exception of a depression with topographic lows of about
Elevation 16 feet MSL near the northeastern portion of the site Compacted fill was
used to raise the depression grade up to an elevation of about 38 feet MSL
Based on our field observations the topography across the project site appears
to be gently sloping down toward the south with existing ground elevations ranging from
about 40 feet Mean Sea Level MSL in the north to about Elevation 28 feet MSL in
the southwest corner with the exception of an existing drainage swale that traverses
the central portion of the site in a northeast southwest direction

Coral Outcrop CR
Coral outcrop consists of coral or cemented calcareous sand formed in shallow
ocean water during the time the ocean stand was at a higher level It is exposed
on the ocean shore on the coastal plains and at the foot of the uplands Coral
outcrop makes up about 80 to 90 percent of the acreage The remaining 10 to
20 percent consists of a thin layer of friable red soil material in cracks crevices
and depressions within the coral outcrop
Mamala Stony Silty Clay Loam 0 to 12 Percent Slopes MnC
This type of soil was formed in alluvium deposited over coral limestone and
consolidated calcareous sand It is dark reddish brown in color neutral to mildly
alkaline with moderate permeability Runoff is very slow to medium and the
erosion hazard is slight to moderate

coral formations The surface fills generally consisted of very stiff to hard sandy silts and

Based on the International Building Code 2009 Edition IBC 2009 as amended
the project site may be subject to seismic activity and seismic design considerations will
need to be addressed The following subsections provide discussions on the seismicity
and soil profile type for seismic design
Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes throughout the world are caused by shifts in the tectonic
plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is primarily linked to volcanic
activity therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or during
volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground

Island of Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most are so
small that they can only be detected by sensitive instruments However some of
the earthquakes are strong enough to be felt and a few cause minor to moderate
damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on the
Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on
the Island of Hawaii Because the majority of earthquakes in Hawaii
over 90 percent are related to volcanic activity the risk of seismic activity and
degree of ground shaking diminishes with increased distance from the Island of
Hawaii The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes greater than
Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the Island of
Hawaii
To a lesser degree the Island of Maui has experienced several earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 Therefore moderate to strong earthquakes have
occurred in the County of Maui The effects of earthquakes occurring on the Islands
of Hawaii and Maui may be felt on the Island of Oahu For example small
landslides occurred on the Island of Oahu as a result of the Maui Earthquake of
Some houses on the Island of Oahu were reportedly damaged as a
result of the Lanai Earthquake of 1871 M7
In the last 150 years of recorded history we are not aware of earthquakes greater
than Magnitude 6 that have occurred on the Island of Oahu An earthquake of
Magnitude 4 8 to 5 0 occurred along the Diamond Head Fault in 1948 on the Island

walls and broken underground water mains

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry

Cut Slope Design
In general we envision most of the cut slopes would expose the weathered coral
formation and fill materials We believe the cut slopes planned may be designed
with a slope inclination of one and one half horizontal to one vertical or
flatter
Excessive surface water runoff over the slope face may cause erosion of the
exposed soils thus potentially jeopardizing the long term stability and performance
of the cut slope Therefore cut slopes should be protected by appropriate slope
planting or by other means such as placement of geotextile fabrics on the slope
face shortly after the slope is excavated to reduce the potential for significant
erosion of the exposed soils

Pavement Joints
Due to the rigidity of the Portland Cement Concrete PCC significant stresses
may develop in the concrete from variations in temperature and moisture content In
order to relieve the high level of stress and to reduce the potential for significant
cracking adequate joints should be provided in the PCC The following describes the
various types of joints and general guidelines that should be implemented in the design
of concrete pavements Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the
slab joints
Crack Control Joints Contraction Joints
In general decreases in temperature or moisture content such as that during
concrete curing will cause the concrete to contract and possibly crack Crack
control joints are used to provide controlled cracking due to volume changes and
relieve the stresses caused by curling or warping Crack control joints such as saw
cut joints or formed grooves are provided to a depth of generally one fourth ¼ the
slab thickness These saw cut joints or formed grooves crack control joints
provide a weakened plane which likely will crack through the full depth as the
concrete shrinks during the curing process
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
W O 6673 00
GEOLABS INC
Page 21
Hawaii California
In general the spacing of crack control joints should be limited to a maximum of
15 feet Smaller joint spacing should be used if the concrete pavement will be
subjected to extreme temperature gradients during the placement and or life span
of the pavement The crack control joints should be provided in both longitudinal
and transverse directions The transverse joint spacing should not vary from the
longitudinal joint spacing by more than 25 percent
Expansion Joints Isolation Joints
Expansion joints are typically used to relieve compressive stresses developed at
critical locations due to fluctuations in temperature Expansion joints are cut to the
full depth of the concrete slab and compressible filler materials are placed in the
cut These joints allow for isolated lateral movement of the slabs and reduce the
potential for damage to the adjoining concrete spalling of the edges of the concrete
slabs
Expansion joints should be used at pavement intersections and at intersections of
pavements with fixed structures such as the footings etc Because expansion
joints generally do not provide for load transfer across the joint placement of
dowels with lubrication on one end of the dowel should be considered for load
transfer across the expansion joint where required
Construction Joints
Construction joints generally are required when two abutting slabs are placed at
different times Therefore construction joints usually form the edges of each day s
work For the relatively thick concrete slab planned for this project the construction
joints should consist of a butted joint In general construction joints should be
aligned with the crack control or expansion joints to reduce the number of
pavement joints required

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
Two 2 Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 were performed on a selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limit and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths and
graphic presentation of the test results are provided on Plate B 1
Six Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 136 D 1140 were performed on selected
soil samples and selected bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the
gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic
presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on Plates B 2 and B 3
Two fines content tests were conducted on selected soil samples to evaluate the
percent passing the No 200 sieve The tests were performed in accordance with
ASTM D 1140 The test results along with those obtained from the sieve analysis tests
are summarized on Plate B 4 and on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested The test
results are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
Four laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 7 and B 10
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 11 through B 12
