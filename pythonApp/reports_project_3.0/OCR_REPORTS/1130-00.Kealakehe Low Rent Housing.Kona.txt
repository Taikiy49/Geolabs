PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the subsur
face conditions at the project site in order to develop data
for site planning and design recommendations for grading and
foundation construction
Two percolation tests were performed for design of future
effluent disposal by means of cesspools
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring Loca
tion Plan Plate 1 and the appropriate logs are presented in
Plates A 1 through A 5 Detailed information regarding the
field exploration and percolation testing are presented in the
appendix
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the western side of Kealakaa
Street at the northern end of the street The proposed first
phase development is located in the northeastern part of the
parcel and encompasses approximately 183 000 square feet
The ground surface is hummocky and slopes generally to the
west The topography generally reflects the original surface
of basaltic lava flows
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1130 00 Page 3
The area is densely vegetated by Christmas berry guava lili
koi and other trees and grasses The site has been used as
pasture land for cattle Access to the site for drilling
equipment was through trails cut by a bulldozer contracted by
our firm
The ground surface elevations of the site vary from about 738
to 788 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of four 2 story wooden
framed apartment buildings each approximately 35 by 160 feet
in plan dimension In addition a one story community center
building and three driveway parking areas are planned
Sewage disposal will be through a cesspool system
A grading plan was not available at the time of our investiga
tion however we believe that site grading will be minimal

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the
prehistoric member of the Hualalai Volcanic Series These
flows are generally of Holocene age and were extruded by the
now dormant Hualalai Volcanic
Basaltic lava flows generally occur in two forms pahoehoe and
ata Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth ropy or billowy
surface and frequently contains voids or lava tubes A ta is
characterized by a clinkery surface and has a dense inner core
The lava flows at the project site are a transitional phase
between the two forms and have characteristics of each
Subsurf ace Explorations
Five borings were made on the project site by utilizing a
truck mounted rotary hydraulic drill rig advancing an NXM
core barrel or a inch diameter tricone roller rock bit
Core samples were obtained from the borings for classification
and testing
Subsequent to our field explorations in May a revised layout
plan which was received by us on indicated that
the building locations have been revised from those upon which
our field exploration was based However we feel that the
subsurface condition encountered should generally reflect the
dominant conditions throughout the site

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered volcanic formation from the ground
surface to the maximum depth drilled The volcanic formation
consisted generally of medium hard gray vesicular basalt with
interbedded layers of medium dense coarse gravel clinker
Voids between 0 5 and 1 0 foot in diameter were encountered in
four of the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the
time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the
borings are presented in the Appendix
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent foundation
bearing volcanic formation at the proposed project site From
a soil engineering point of view the site is suitable for the
proposed residential development
Shallow foundations may be used for the proposed structures
The volcanic formation is non expansive therefore slab on
grade construction may also be utilized

Site Grading
Relatively shallow cuts and fills are anticipated for the site
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and other
deleterious material prior to the commencement of earthwork
These materials should be wasted off site
Prior to the placement of structural fill the existing ground
surface should be track rolled with a heavy dozer D8H or
larger for a minimum of 4 passes This will serve to densify
any loose surface materials and to collapse any near surface
voids
Fill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to obtain 95 of its maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM D 1557
Fills placed on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into firm natural
ground
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 fill slopes are recom
mended Cut slopes of one horizontal to one vertical 1 1
may be utilized
GEOLABS HAWAII
Based on previous experience with similar material it is our
opinion that the on site volcanic formation is generally ripp
able by a D9G dozer or its equivalent The excavated rock
material can for the most part be broken down to workable
size by dozer tracking
Excavated on site volcanic material generally less than 12
inches in size may be used as fill material In this case
the fill may be placed in 18 inch loose horizontal lifts and
track rolled with a heavy dozer until the required density is
obtained usually seven to nine passes Due to the coarse
granular nature of this material and the resulting difficulty
in taking density tests we strongly recommend that a soils
engineer continuously monitor the placement of all rock fills
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the proposed structures
The bottom of all footing excavations should be compacted to
95 maximum dry density prior to the placement of reinforcing
steel except when the footing will bear directly on in situ
rock
GEOLABS HAWAII
An allowable bearing value of 5000 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on in situ volcanic formation For footings
bearing on compacted structural fill a bearing value of 2500
p s may be used to design the footings The above values
are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
A minimum footing width of 18 inches should be utilized and
the bottom of all footings should be placed a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade In addition the out
side heel of the footing should be set back a minimum of 5
feet from the nearest outside slope face This set back may
require deepening of the footing embedment to maintain the
above specified minimum set back
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
possible large cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava
rock drilled probes are recommended below every major footing
or on 10 foot centers for continuous footings The probe
holes which could be drilled with a track percussion drill
should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and extend below
the bottom of the footing a depth equal to two times the
footing width or a minimum of 6 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1130 00 Page 9
If a cavity is suspected below a Footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the
cavity Voids greater than 6 inches in diameter should be
exposed or grouted on the recommendations of the soils
engineer The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that the aforementioned
recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any
potential voids near the footing excavations
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foun
dations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
on the order of less than one half of an inch for structures
bearing on rock or on compacted structural fill
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 5
between the foundations and underlying rock materials is
recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 400 p c f may be
used

Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than three fourths of an inch and larger than
one fourth of an inch in size
Slab on grade should have perimeter Footings extending down a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade to reduce
moisture infiltration below the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement in order to minimize slab
cracking due to dehydration Subgrade should be compacted to
a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557
Pavements
Based on observation of the on site materials and our previous
experience with these types of materials the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

as they are encountered in the field during construction
particularly if imported material is used The above design
section may be revised on the basis of the tests
PERCOLATION TESTING
A cesspool system is planned for the development
Percolation tests were performed in Borings 1 and 4 to eva
luate the suitability of the underlying formation for cesspool
disposal
The percolation testing consisted of discharging water from
a fire hydrant into the drilled holes and observing the
increase in water level or head in the drilled hole Injec
tion rates of 30 and 22 gallons per minute were used in
Borings 1 and 4 respectively The tests were run for a mini
mum of 30 minutes in each boring The injected water dissi
pated without an appreciable build up of the groundwater
level The percolation test results are included in the
Appendix
GEOLABS HAWAII

Based on our test results it is our opinion that cesspool
disposal is feasible For estimating purposes it may be
assumed that the cesspools will extend to depths of 10 to 12
feet below the existing ground surface
Design Review
Plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construc
tion to see that the intent of our recommendations is in
cluded in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading probe drilling
footing excavations and backfill operations be monitored and
inspected by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing and compaction have been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the find
ings of our investigation The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon the above inspections

Any imported fill material should be non expansive and meet
the approval of the soils engineer A sample of any pro
posed import material should be submitted to the soils
engineer for testing and approval a minimum of 10 working
days prior to its intended delivery to the project site

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 16 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was
used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling at a height of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NXM series core barrel with a diamond bit The core recovery
and rock quality designation RQD are presented on the logs of
borings
