SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Deposits of expansive clay soils are frequently found at the
bases of ridges and steep slopes in Hawaii These clay soils
have low shear strength values and are often subject to
downslope creep movement if the soils become saturated or
have surcharge loads placed on them
Based our findings removal and replacement of the clay soils
would minimize the potential of downslope creep movement
Since the surface layer of clay soils encountered at the site is
thin and there are large quantities of coral materials in the
lower portions of the site we believe that it is economically
feasible to remove the clay soils from the development area
and grade the site with select material generated from the
excavation of the coral materials Therefore it is our opinion
that development of the entire proposed residential
subdivision area up to Elevation 200 feet MSL is feasible from
a geotechnical engineering point of view
Where the adobe clay soils extend up slope beyond the
proposed property limits special stabilization methods will be
required to reduce the down slope creep potential of this
material

special design recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the base of Puu o Hulu Kai and Puu o Hulu Uka in
the Waianae District on the leeward coast on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed
to subdivide and develop approximately 130 acres of the site for the construction of
approximately 953 single family and multi family dwelling units in both the affordable and
market price ranges
It is proposed to grade the site to create level residential lots Based on the
preliminary grading concepts it appears that fills up to about 60 feet in vertical height and
cuts up to about 40 feet in depth will be required in the upper hillside portions of the site
to build the level lots as planned

preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed
residential development with particular emphasis on the steeper hillside portions of the
site

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Maili area on the Waianae side of the island of
Oahu Hawaii The site is located about one half mile inland from Maili Point and
encompasses approximately three hundred 300 acres of land area at the base of and
on the northern flank of Puu o Hulu Kai and Puu o Hulu Uka The terrain ranges from
nearly level in the northern portion of the site to slopes of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or steeper in the southern portions on the northerly facing flank of Puu o Hulu
Kai and Puu o Hulu Uka The existing ground elevations within the proposed
development area range from about 30 to 200 feet above Mean Sea Level
The site is presently undeveloped Most of the site is covered with a moderately
thick cover of dryland vegetation such as keawe haole koa and grasses
Numerous boulders were noted at the surface in the southern portion of the site

Regional Geology
The isiand of Oahu was built up mainly by two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau Waianae which forms the west side of the island is the oldest of the two
and was built during the Pliocene Epoch by the extrusion of the lavas of the Waianae
Volcanic Series
The rocks of the Waianae Volcanic Series are divided into Lower Middle and
Upper Members The lavas of the Lower Member consist largely of thin bedded
pahoehoe The Middle Member basalts closely resemble those of the Lower Member
except that the Middle Member contains more a a flows The Upper Member consists
largely of massive a a flows
Erosion has deeply dissected Waianae and removed most of the western slope of
the shield forming the Waianae Range Following a great amount of erosion large
accumulations of alluvium and colluvium were deposited in the deep amphitheater headed
valleys of Lualualei and Waianae
Puu o Hulu Kai and Puu o Hulu Uka are two peaks on an erosional remnant ridge
from the Waianae Range These promontories are composed of Lower and Middle
basalts with numerous dike intrusions
During the Late Pleistocene Epoch emerged coral reefs were formed when the sea
stood about 100 feet higher than it does at present These deposits of coral limestone
extend approximately two 2 miles inland and underlie the project site

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Deposits of expansive clay soils are frequently found at the bases of ridges and
steep slopes in Hawaii These clay soils have low shear strength values and are often
subject to downslope creep movement if the soils become saturated or have surcharge
loads placed on them Such downslope creep movement has resulted in significant
property damages at locations such as Manoa and Kuliouou At all
of these sites the subdivision developments were underlain by thick deposits of clay soils
on sloping terrain and the clay soils were left in place during the development of the site
At the Maili Kai project site our field exploration indicated that the deposits of clay
soils are generally relatively thin Based on the findings from our analyses based on the
data from the exploration and our laboratory testing removal and replacement of the clay
soils would minimize the potential of downslope creep movement Since the on site layer
of clay is relatively thin and there are large quantities of coral materials in the lower
portions of the site we believe that it is economically feasible to remove the clay soils
from the development area and grade the site with select material generated from the
excavation of the coral materials Therefore it is our opinion that development of the
entire proposed residential subdivision area up to Elevation 200 feet MSL is feasible from
a geotechnical engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented
in this report are followed Due to the expansive nature of the surface soils the relatively
low shear strengths of the clay soils and the potential for slope movement it is essential
that the site preparation and grading recommendations presented herein be closely
followed

Grading Design
In the early part of our exploration and analyses we had felt that it would be
possible to remove and replace only part of the adobe clay soils to obtain stable slopes
In our Preliminary Findings and Recmmendations Letter dated we
presented a grading scheme which involved the removal and replacement of the clay soils
down to a slope of ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V and subsequent construction
of embankment fill slopes over the remaining adobe clay soils This scheme was based
on the assumption that the lots would have slopes of about 20 to 25 percent slope ratio
to limit the amount of fill on the relatively steep terrain
Based on the recommendations presented in the letter a
preliminary grading concept was developed This subsequent preliminary grading
concept indicated that large fills up to sixty 60 feet in height were planned for the
southern portion of the project site to create level lot pads for on grade type of residential
construction Due to the high shrink swell potential and low shear strength potential of
the adobe clay our analyses indicate that there is a strong potential for future movement
of the clay soils when wetted and under the large surcharge loading from the thick
overlying fills planned Therefore we strongly recommend the use of the following revised
grading concept
Revised Grading Concept
Our field exploration indicates that the expansive clay soils occurring at the surface
of most of the southern portion of the site generally form a relatively thin mantle
over more competent materials
The current preliminary grading concept for the southern portion of the site
indicates that fills up to sixty 60 feet in height as measured vertically from the toe
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2716 00 Page 11
to the top of the fill are planned to create level residential lots It is also planned
to construct the fills with two and one half horizontal to one vertical
slopes
Due to the shrink swell potential and potential of long term downslope creep
movement of the wetted adobe soils under the large surcharge loads from the
thick fills planned we strongly recommend the use of this revised grading concept
which would minimize the potential of both structural distresses from the swelling
of the expansive soils and long term downslope movement of the clay soils

Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling down the hillside Falling
boulders are a general phenomena occurring along all ridge slopes on Oahu Various
methods of boulder barriers or catchments may be used to reduce the hazard of falling
boulders For the project site there are various attempts or efforts which may be
considered to minimize the impact of falling boulders into the development area It is our
opinion that the following may be considered
1 A boulder catchment area about ten 10 to fifteen 15 feet wide with a
fence or wall could be utilized near the property boundary to catch rolling
boulders An interceptor ditch along the upper boundaries of the site could
be used in this capacity
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries of the site could be performed
periodically to identify and remove loose boulders that appear likely to roll
down the slopes
3 The natural trees and vegetation along the upper portions of the site should
be retained to act as a natural barrier against the boulders and to reduce
erosion
4 Where practicable structures should be set back away from the toe of the
hillside slope to create an additional boulder catchment area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods or combinations of effort used there
is still an inherent risk of damage due to falling boulders for hillside lots The state of
geotechnical engineering practice is not at the point that an absolute solution is available
for this concern It is our opinion that no matter what actions or precautions are taken
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2716 00 Page 18
there is always the risk of boulders rolling down the hill if not from areas near the house
from areas higher up on the slopes Whatever measure is chosen it only reduces the
risk To point out that no guarantee against future falling boulders is possible due to
changes in soil and rock conditions with time weathering seepage and erosion
earthquake and construction vibration and improvements and construction activities at
and around the site
The potential purchasers or owners of these hillside lots should be made aware
of the aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future improvements in the upper
areas of the lots which may disturb the stability of the existing boulders

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs and Test Pit Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs summarized on Plates
Sieve Analysis tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are performed to determine the
grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for classification in
the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other engineering
properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 3
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of asoil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B 6
