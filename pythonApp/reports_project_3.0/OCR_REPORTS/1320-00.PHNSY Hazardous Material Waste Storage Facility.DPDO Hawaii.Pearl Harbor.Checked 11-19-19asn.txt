PEARL HARBOR NAVAL SHIPYARD OAHU HAWAII
This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the proposed Hazardous Material Waste Storage Facility
DPDO Hawaii at Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions by drilling and sampling borings Data obtained from the
borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were utilized to provide
recommendations for the design of the foundation of the project Our
work was performed in general accordance with the scope of services
outlined in our proposal dated
W O 1320 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct an incombustible building for
hazardous material waste storage along with loading and unloading
areas with containment sumps and an attached PWC facilities building
The building will be of one story prefabricated rigid frame steel
structure on concrete slab with CMU perimeter walls The proposed
layout of the building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
An above grade storage tank which will be 30 by 88 feet in plan
dimension and about 6 feet high may be required for the project It
will be located east of the loading and unloading area
It is our understanding that the concrete floor slabs and the
joints are to be constructed liquid tight and serve operation of
4000 1b capacity forklift trucks
The maximum column loads are anticipated to be on the order of 34
kips per column and the wall loads on the order of 2 6 kips per linear
foot

The loading and unloading area will have 6 inch high curbs and
pavements capable to serve fully loaded tractor trailer
and or forklift trucks The forklift trucks could result in
a total load of with 80 to 90 percent concentrated on one
axle It is our understanding that the loading and unloading area will
also be paved with Portland cement concrete
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located just west of the intersection of 6th
Street and South Avenue inside the Naval Shipyard in Pearl Harbor At
present the site is occupied by various types of structures which
include 1 a high counterfort concrete wall 2 two one story concrete
and masonry buildings 3 a reinforced concrete coal bin and 4 other
metal sheds and a quonset shed The counterfort wall approximately
divides the site into northern and southern halves The northern half
is a part of the existing scrap yard and is covered by asphalt concrete
A C surfacing over thick concrete slabs The southern half is
mostly covered with steel plates over A C surfacing and buildings A
few trees are located just outside the existing concrete and CMU
buildings

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the Hickam Makalapa area which consists of
emerged reef limestone deposits coral mantled by Makalapa Salt Lake
post erosional volcanics tuff These deposits were subsequent ly
overlain by detritus coral debris when the sea level rose again to the
Waimanalo High 25 Stand of the sea Later man made fills covered
the entire site
Subsurface Exploration
To explore the subsurface conditions four borings were drilled
and sampled to depths ranging from 15 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Our borings indicated that the northwestern half of the site was
covered by 2 to 4 inches of asphalt concrete underlain by 8 to 12
inches of Portland cement concrete slabs and 4
The southeastern half of the site was mostly covered by steel
plates and underlain by 2 to 2 5 inches of A C over 8 to 24 inches of
crushed rock and sand fill As indicated on Navy Drawings I N5 1228
and 1229 it appears that these materials might be underlain by
concrete slabs in most of the metal plated area
GEOLABS HAWAII
Underneath the concrete slabs and the A C all our borings
encountered a layer of tan to light brown dense silty coral sand and
gravel of about 6 feet thick underlain by gray medium dense volcanic
cinder and tuff which was generally weakly cemented at the top but
became increasingly well cemented with depth The volcanic tuff
deposit extended to at least 16 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at a depth of about 10
feet at the time of our exploration The depths measured are shown on
the boring logs It is anticipated that the groundwater level will
fluctuate somewhat with tides
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the floor slabs of these structures
will be liquid tight for hazardous material waste storage while
allowing the operation of capacity forklift trucks
The new concrete slab will be located over existing structures
such as concrete slabs A C pavement wall footings etc and in
places a soil layer That abuts against the concrete or A C pavements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1320 00 Page 6
To minimize the problem of cracking the new slab that may overlie
hard and soft areas we recommend that a layer of granular base be
provided This can be achieved by raising the finished floor grades so
that at least 6 inches of granular base can be placed under the new
slab To provide drainage of any free water that may be trapped under
the new slab which could weaken the slab subgrade support the existing
A C or concrete slabs should be perforated with 4 inch minimum
diameter holes at 8 feet intervals The perforated holes should be
backfilled with No 3 Fine crushed rock
Another alternative would be to completely remove the existing
thick concrete slabs in the existing scrap yard for the proposed
warehouse construction
If the new concrete slabs are to be placed over the existing wall
footings or small concrete blocks then these concrete structures
should be removed to at least 6 inches below the bottom of the new
slabs
In general the site conditions are favorable for spread footing
Support except for the obstacles posed by the existing structures

Site Preparation
All vegetation trees debris and other deleterious materials
including rail ties see Navy Drawings I N5 1228 and 1229 should be
cleared and grubbed and removed from the site The existing steel
plates should be removed and the underlying A C should be broken up
The existing concrete slabs and A C overlay in the scarp yard area
should be perforated if they are to be left in place under the new
fill The existing structures such as concrete walls buildings sump
pits etc should be removed to 6 inches below the ground surface or 6
inches below the bottom of the new Slabs whichever is the lower
The existing slabs in the one story buildings should be removed
Any soft or weak spots disclosed during the course of site
preparation should be repaired by excavating the weak soil and then
backfilling with compacted on site granular coral material
After demolition clearing and grubbing the surface of the soil
to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches
moisture conditioned and then recompacted to at least 95 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density Scarification and recompaction would not be
necessary over the existing A C concrete slab or footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1320 00 Page 8
In the new loading and unloading area where heavy truck trailer
and forklift truck operations are planned the top portion of the
existing coral sand and gravel material within 2 feet below the
finished grade should be compacted to 95 relative compaction If a
portion of this area is situated in the cut area we believe that such
a densification can be achieved by rolling the in place material from
the stripped surface with a heavy vibratory roller
The on site coral material may be used as engineered fill provided
that large particles are screened off or crushed down to 3 inches or
smaller in sizes Oversized fragments should not be used as fill
All imported materials for fill under the pavement or warehouse
areas should be granular materials with maximum particle size not
exceeding 3 inches and have a minimum laboratory CBR value of 30 when
compacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Any material to be imported should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
All fill and backfill material should be placed in horizontal
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density

Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that the dense silty coral sand
and gravel at shallow depths will be suitable for spread footing
foundation support
The footing should bear in dense coral sand and gravel and the
footing bottom embedded at least 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade The disturbed bottom of the excavations should be recompacted
to at least 95 relative compaction based on ASTM D 1557 78 designation
An allowable soil bearing of up to 3500 p s f may be used It
may be increased by one third when considering temporary wind or
seismic loads
It is likely that the majority of the new foundations will
encounter existing slabs or foundations In general we believe that
if these structures are encountered they should be removed The
resulting cavity or void should be backfilled with granular material
compacted to 95 relative compaction

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing engineered fill
placement and compaction foundation excavation backfilling subgrade
preparation and pavement construction be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
me oe om ae we me es
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drilling rig was used to drill the borings In addition a bag of
near surface sample was obtained from an area along the edge of the
roadway for evaluation of subgrade support
The soils and rock encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by one of our
engineers who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation
of the materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates
1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split
hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
for 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths

Laboratory Testing

tests were performed The test results are presented on Plate B 1

to evaluate its subgrade supporting characteristics The test results
are summarized on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the subgrade soil The
test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
