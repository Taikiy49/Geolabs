INCREMENT 1
The following presents the results of our supplementary soils
investigation for the construction of the proposed new rectangular open
channel section located downstream of Kelikoi Street in Kakaako Oahu
Hawaii This report is a supplement to our previous soil engineering
investigation report of The approximate location of
the open channel section and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
March 20 1984
W O 1314 10 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the planned rectangular drainage
channel will be 30 feet wide 15 feet deep and about 650 feet long
located between the intersection of Kelikoi and Keawe Streets and the
ocean Its alignment will follow the existing unlined open drainage
channel Tentatively two alternative schemes are being considered for
this open channel section The first scheme will consist of a U shaped
reinforced concrete section The second scheme will consist of driving
vertical sheet piles which act as side members and placing a riprap layer
for the channel bottom lining
The inverts of the open channel range from 8 to 9 feet below the
Mean Sea Level
The eastern portion of the open channel will be adjacent to a large
stockpile consisting of various types of soil
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions in the open channel project area and to provide geotechnical
design recommendations for the proposed construction For this
supplementary investigation two additional borings were drilled to a
depth of 20 feet each at the approximate locations shown on the Geologic
Site Plan Plate 2 Subsoil samples obtained were examined and tested
in our laboratory
W O 1314 10 Page 3
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the borings
along with the laboratory test data are presented in the appendices of
this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
General
The existing unlined channel is approximately 7 to 12 feet deep
with sloped riprapped banks The top of the banks are at about elevation
6 feet above Mean Sea Level except for the downstream 400 foot portion
where the eastern channel bank abuts a large earth stockpile about 24 to
34 feet high refer to Plate 3 It is our understanding that a portion
of the stockpile next to the new lined drainage channel would be removed
for the channel construction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The results of the two borings and review of the previous soils
report indicate that medium dense silty sand and gravel of estuarine
deposit underlies the surface fill and overlies coral reef rock During
the future channel excavation we believe that this estuarine deposit
could become unstable under the existing heavy stockpile loading
Therefore we recommend that during construction the existing high
stockpile on the eastern bank be removed at least 30 feet away from the
edge of the excavation If the stockpile is to be placed back adjacent
to the channel higher lateral earth pressure should be considered to act
on the channel structure

Stability During Construction
During the construction of the open channel the required
excavation may extend 11 to 13 feet below the sea level The results of
our stability analyses indicated that the factor of safety against
translational slide will be about 1 0 if the excavation is made next to
the large stockpile Therefore for stability consideration the
existing stockpiled material should be removed at least 30 feet away from
the top of the channel excavation Presently the top of the stockpile
is at about Elevation 30 feet MSL If the stockpile height is raised
the factor of safety against failure may be reduced further
The density and soil engineering characteristics of the stockpiled
materials are not known at this time However the side slopes of the
opinion that probably a similar slope ratio may be used after removing a
portion of the stockpile During construction we believe some
provisions should be considered to flatten the slope of the stockpile
We believe that shoring will be required for the channel
excavation It is critically important that adequate sheeting and
shoring be installed and maintained during construction by the
contractor The recommendations presented in our previous report under
sections Trench Wall Support Dewatering and Backfill could be
utilized by the contractor as a guide

For the construction of the second design scheme in which sheet
piles will be utilized as permanent walls for the open channel we
believe it may be difficult to drive sheet piles down sufficiently
into the underlying coral reef rock The sheet piles could be
installed by first excavating into the coral placing the sheet
piles then grouting the sheet piles in place However we
anticipate that it would be difficult to keep the sandy soils in
the trench walls from falling into the excavation A variation to
this second scheme would be a system consisting of soldier piles
with laggings in between The soldier piles can be installed in
pre drilled holes cased through the upper sand and gravel materials
that may otherwise cave into the holes
For the design of the soldier piles lagging system the same active
lateral earth pressures recommended for the first scheme may be
used

The lateral passive pressures on the drilled shaft of the soldier
pile in coral may vary according to the size and spacing of the
drilled hole For shafts of up to 2 feet in diameter with spacing
of at least 3 diameters the passive earth pressures should be 4500
p s f at the top of the coral after dredging increasing at a
rate of 160 p s f per foot of depth below the top of the coral
These pressures are the calculated ultimate capacities and do not
contain factor of safety beyond unity Therefore the calculated
embedment depth below the top of the coral should be increased to
provide a suitable factor of safety The above values are provided
to aid in developing rough cost estimates only Since the passive
pressure on the soldier pile is a function of the ratio between the
shaft spacing and its diameter more specific passive pressures
should be developed after the shaft size and spacing are selected
Appropriate hydrostatic pressures should be added on to the lateral
earth pressures Surcharge loads if any may be converted at a
rate of 1 foot of increased backfill height per 100 p s f of
surcharge load
Cushion Layer
A cushion layer of clean granular material such as 3B Fine is
recommended below the channel structure to provide uniform support
Where coral is encountered at the bottom of the structure a minimum
cushion thickness of 6 inches is recommended Where loose to medium
density sands are encountered the cushion thickness should be increased
to a minimum of 12 inches

Water Level Considerations
Groundwater levels will affect the design particularly where there
are differences in water pressures inside and outside the channel lining
One of the conditions to be considered may include a drawdown or
surge of channel water level due to tsunami conditions while the
groundwater level outside the channel remain unaffected
An extreme drawdown condition may occur if the water inside a
portion of the channel is removed by dewatering during construction of
the adjacent section
Channel Excavation
The channel excavation may extend 12 to 13 feet below the sea level
to provide the bottom lining and the underlying cushion layer Thus
portions of the excavation may encounter coral reef rock However
excavation in coral is not anticipated to be exceptionally difficult No
underwater blasting is anticipated at this time
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
W O 1314 10 Page 10
Constriction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading excavation and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
