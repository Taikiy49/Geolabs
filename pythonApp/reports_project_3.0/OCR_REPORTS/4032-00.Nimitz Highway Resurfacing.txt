INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed for the proposed resurfacing of Nimitz and Kamehameha Highways from
Rodgers Boulevard to Ahua Street and the proposed resurfacing of the Service Road in
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii For this project Nimitz Highway includes the
eastbound travel lanes below the Interstate Route H 1 viaduct and Kamehameha
Highway consists of the westbound travel lanes also below the Interstate Route H 1
viaduct The general location of the project area is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings from our site reconnaissance and our review
of available information and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses for the proposed project These geotechnical findings and
recommendations are intended for pavement design purposes only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age and forms the
majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lava
During the later Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation As the continental glaciers advanced less water was available
to fill the oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell to below its present stand When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above its present level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic
sea level fluctuations The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and
fans of terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposition of lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused desiccation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials In addition renewed subaerial
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4032 00 Page 5
erosion of the upper areas of the volcanic dome deposited terrigenous alluvial soils under
relatively high energy conditions
During periods of no significant sea level changes continued stream action
extended the alluvial deita and fans seaward by deposition of alluvium over the previously
deposited lagoonal sediments
The geology of the project area has been further complicated by the deposition of
recent pyroclastic materials and lava flows from eruptions of vents of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series which included the nearby Aliamanu Salt Lake Makalapa vent These
eruptions were contemporaneous with the Pleistocene sea level fluctuations
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft harbor sediments
along the coast in areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways Urban
development and reclamation projects have since altered the natural formation of the
area

Methodology of Pavement Design
The methodology used to determine the required AC overlay thickness for
rehabilitation and thickness of reconstructed pavement sections generally follows that
outlined in the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Material Testing and
Research Branch s Pavement Design Manual dated May 1995
In the method outlined for AC overlays the strength of the existing AC and base
materials are evaluated based on the type and severity of the distresses observed For
example the existing AC may be reduced to that of ACB untreated base or may be
assumed to have similar strength characteristics as new AC For this project we have
assumed that the existing AC is reduced to the strength characteristics of new ACB
Reconstruction of localized portions of the existing pavements will be required in
those areas where the observed distresses were likely caused by failures in the
underlying base subbase and or subgrade materials New pavement sections were
determined based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of the pavement design
manual which is based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the
California Department of Transportation

Design Subgrade Conditions
Since exploratory borings were not drilled for the project the subgrade soil design
parameters were estimated based on our review of available information and our
experience in the area Based on our review of available subsurface information we
believe that the subgrade soils in the area generally consist of silty sand sandy gravel
fill over coral with sand or weathered volcanic tuff Although soft lagoonal deposits
underlie portions of the site it is our opinion that the lagoonal deposits are located at
relatively deep depths in terms of pavement design within the limits of the project
For evaluating rehabilitation options we believe that an R value of 30 which
corresponds to a California Bearing Ratio value of about 12 0 based on correlations by
the State of Kentucky would be appropriate for the silty sand sandy gravel fill soils below
the existing pavement structure

Structural Repair of Base Failures
As previously discussed in the Existing Pavement Sections and Conditions
section of this report some of the observed distresses in localized areas of the subject
roadways are likely caused by failures in the underlying base subbase and or subgrade
materials In general we recommend that these areas be reconstructed with new
pavement sections in lieu of an AC overlay section In addition we recommend that
these distressed areas be repaired prior to the AC overlay work for the entire roadway
To reduce the potential for disturbance to vehicular traffic on this busy roadway
and to reduce the potential for exposing or damaging utilities a full depth pavement
section consisting of AC over ACB is recommended for reconstruction The use of ACB
as the base material will also facilitate the reconstruction work to be performed in a timely
manner Based on our engineering analyses we recommend that the following pavement
sections be used for the structural repair of these areas Detailed calculations and
analyses are presented on Plates A 7 through A 9 in Appendix A

Pavement Surface Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the subbase base course and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement
surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed anywhere during or after construction Development of good shoulder drainage
along with a program to prevent obstructions in the drainage structures is strongly
recommended to reduce the potential for pavement deterioration or premature failure of
the pavements
