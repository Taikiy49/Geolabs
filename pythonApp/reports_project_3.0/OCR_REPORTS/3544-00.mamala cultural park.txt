INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Malama Cultural Park project located at the southwest corner
of the intersection of Kaunakakai and Hio Places in the Town of Kaunakakai on the island
of Molokai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations retaining walls site
grading and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Molokai was built during the early to middle Pleistocene Epoch by
basaltic lava flows from two shield volcanoes East Molokai Mountain and West Molokai
Mountain The project site is located on the Hoolehua Plain between the East and West
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3544 00 Page 6
Molokai Domes The Hoolehua Plain or isthmus of Molokai is composed of lava flows
from the East Molokai Volcano banking against the older West Molokai Volcano
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great
continental glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water
available to fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global
sea levels rose because more water was available The land mass of Molokai remained
essentially stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature
These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher
and lower relative to the present sea level on Molokai
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef
deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused
consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials In addition renewed subaerial erosion of the upper areas of
the volcanic dome deposited terrigenous alluvial soils under relatively high energy
conditions The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments at correspondingly higher elevations in areas protected by fringing
reefs and beach strands During periods of no significant sea level changes continued
stream action extended the alluvial deltas and fans seaward
The project site is located along the coast line and is underlain by alluvial soil
deposits The alluvial soils generally consist of fine grained sands and silts with gravels
and may be of substantial thickness

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the southwest corner of the intersection of
Kaunakakai Place and Hio Place in the Kaunakakai area on the island of Molokai Hawaii
The project site is bounded by Hio Place to the north by Kaunakakai Place to the east
by a vacant area to the west and by the Pacific Ocean to the south as shown on the Site

The majority of the project site is presently vacant However at the time of our

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings to a depth of about 21 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a thin surficial fill layer consisting of
medium dense sands and stiff silts to depths of about to 4 feet below the existing
ground surface Below the near surface fill layer alluvial deposits consisting of loose to
medium dense sands and medium stiff to stiff clayey silts were encountered to the
maximum depth explored of about 21 feet below the existing ground surface The
granular alluvial deposits generally grade from medium dense to loose with increasing
depth in the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site consist
generally of a thin surficial fill layer overlying medium dense to loose sands and medium
stiff to stiff silts Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a
shallow foundation system bearing on the thin near surface fills consisting of medium
dense sands and stiff silts may be used to support the proposed structures The
proposed Toilet Arts Crafts Concession and Hale Malama structures may be
supported by a shallow foundation system bearing on the new fills needed to raise the
grades in these areas The other proposed structures may also be supported on a
shallow foundation system bearing on the near surface soils or new fills needed to raise
the grades at the site Special attention should be given to the potential for ground
settlements and the effects on foundations due to placement of new fills at the building
locations especially the Toilet Arts Crafts Concession and Hale Malama structures

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that a
shallow foundation system consisting of shallow spread and or continuous footings may
be used to support the proposed structures The proposed Toilet Arts Crafts
Concession and Hale Malama structures may be supported by a shallow foundation and
slab on grade bearing on the new fills needed to raise the grades in these areas provided
that a waiting period of about 6 weeks is provided after placement of the fills to allow
potential settlements to occur prior to construction of the building structures The other
proposed structures may also be supported by a shallow foundation system bearing on
the near surface medium dense sands and stiff silts and or on new fills
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may
be used for the design of footings bearing on the near surface medium dense soils and or
on compacted fills This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic
forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade for the building foundations The embedment depth should be increased
to at least 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for retaining walls including the
perimeter building retaining walls
Due to the proximity of the site to the shoreline the project site may be subjected
to the effects of tidal surges tsunamis and or flooding As a result the effects of scour
potential on the foundations of the proposed improvements should be taken into
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3544 00 Page 10
consideration in the design The minimum embedment depths recommended above for
foundations did not take into consideration the effects of scouring Therefore we
recommend that the footings for the new structures be embedded below the anticipated
scour depth to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support To reduce the effects
of scour on the foundations the area around the structure foundations may be protected
from scour by asphalt concrete concrete aprons and or other non erodible surfaces
New fills of about 2 to 3 feet will be required to provide a level building pad for
the proposed Toilet Arts Crafts Concession and Hale Malama structures Because of
the loose to medium dense sands encountered from depths of about 10 to 21 feet
below the surface we believe that the new fills planned will induce ground settlements
resulting from compression of the underlying loose to medium dense soils in these areas
Based on a fill thickness of about 2 to 3 feet and subsoils encountered we estimate
settlements to be on the order of 1 to 2 inches as a result of new fill loads To reduce the
potential for distress to the building structures and retaining walls due to ground
settlements we recommend that construction of the building structures and retaining
walls be delayed until after the completion of the ground settlement under the new fill
loads Therefore a waiting period of 6 weeks should be maintained after placement of
the new fills to allow the anticipated ground settlements to occur prior to construction of
the structures
It should be noted that placement of new fills greater than 1 to 2 feet above the
existing grades will likely result in some settlement of the ground surface Therefore new
fills should be kept to a minimum to reduce the potential for ground settlements Where
improvements are located on fills greater than 2 feet above the existing ground surface
Geolabs Hawaii should be consulted to reevaluate the site conditions and to provide
additional design recommendations as appropriate

Footing subgrades should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If loose and or soft soils
are observed at the footing subgrade level they should be over excavated to expose firm
ground and the resulting over excavation should be backfilled with open graded material
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or lean concrete
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward
from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement

herein total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads are estimated to be on

between the base of the footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth pressure

Slabs On Grade
As previously indicated concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
Toilet Arts Crafts Concession and Hale Malama structures Based on the finish floor
elevations provided we anticipate that fills on the order of about 2 to 3 feet relative to
the existing ground surface will be required to provide a level building pad for the
proposed structures
Prior to the filling operation the subgrade to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction In general the fill material
needed to raise the grades to the finish subgrade may consist of the on site sandy soils
and or imported non expansive granular material The fill material should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
As previously noted a waiting period of 6 weeks is recommended prior to
construction of any improvements on fills greater than 2 feet thick After the waiting
period the slab subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction For more uniform support of interior slabs we
recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill
The cushion fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3544 00 Page 13
slab cushion fill layer should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and
recompaction of the subgrade soils
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of
moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the
slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a
2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab
concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
If exterior concrete walkways are planned for the proposed project we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be provided below the
concrete walkway slab for more uniform support The subgrade below the concrete
walkway should be prepared as recommended previously Construction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right
angle intersections
Retaining Walls
Based on the information provided we anticipate that retaining walls with heights
of up to about 4 feet will be constructed for grade separation at some of the proposed
building locations especially the Toilet Arts Crafts Concession and Hale Malama
structures We understand that the proposed retaining walls will be concrete masonry
retaining walls General guidelines for the design of the retaining wall structures are as
follows
1 Foundations for the retaining walls should be designed in general
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3544 00 Page 14
section of this report The footing embedment for retaining walls should be
a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Special
attention should be given to the required waiting period after placement of
new fills and prior to construction of improvements including retaining walls
slabs on grade and utilities within the fill areas
Where wall footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or
utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary
45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the below grade
structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
2 All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressures due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects An active lateral
earth pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot p c f expressed in equivalent
fluid pressure is recommended for design of cantilever walls For walls that
are restrained an at rest earth pressure of 50 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure may be used
The value provided above is for the on site sandy soils or imported granular
soils Backfills behind retaining walls should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to between 90 and
95 percent relative compaction Over compaction of the wall backfill should
be avoided This lateral earth pressure does not include hydrostatic
pressures that might be caused by groundwater trapped behind the walls

3 The surcharge effect due to traffic areal surcharges line loads and point
loads within a horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be
considered in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the
loaded side of the wall a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure
equal to 30 percent of the surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of
the wall which is free to deflect cantilever may be used in the design
4 Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide
zone of permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe
perforations down at the base of the wall discharging to a discharge facility
or weep holes A layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent
should be placed between the drainage material and the compacted backfill
material The drainage material should extend from the base of the wall to
a height within 2 feet below finish grade The remaining 2 feet of backfill
should consist of an impervious capping of select non expansive soil to
reduce water infiltration behind the walls

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations
should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into
the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately
after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
