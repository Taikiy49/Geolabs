REMARKS This facsimile memorandum provides geotechnical recommendations for
design of the new retaining wall structure near Henry Street for the above
project The new retaining wall will be about 460 linear feet with wall
heights ranging from about 3 5 to 15 5 feet The retaining wall structure
will be constructed on the Mauka side of Queen Kaahumanu Highway
from Station to 115 23 We understand that Controlled
Low Strength Material CLSM will be used to backfill behind the new wall
structure
The new wall structure will be constructed adjacent to an existing HELCO
wall enclosure In addition the extension of three existing 84 inch diameter
Corrugated Metal Pipe CMP structures will be required An existing
Concrete Rubble Masonry CRM wall is located between the HELCO wall
enclosure and the CMP structures An earth berm covered by Grouted
Rubble Paving GRP has been constructed at the toe of the existing CRM
wall structure Granular fill material was observed beneath the GRP earth
berm cover during probes made by the Contractor into the existing berm
Based on the borings drilled and our visual observations we anticipate the
existing highway embankment consist of granular fill materials In
addition we anticipate the top of the basalt rock formation may be present
at a relatively shallow depth below the toe of the highway embankment

Dynamic Lateral Earth Forces
Because the project site is located in a seismically active area on the Island
of Hawaii dynamic lateral earth forces will need to be considered in the
design of the retaining structures Dynamic lateral earth forces due to
seismic loading may be estimated by using 17H pounds per
linear foot of wall length for level backfill conditions where H is the height of
the wall in feet It should be noted that the dynamic lateral earth forces
provided assume that the wall will be allowed to move laterally by up to
about 7 to 8 inches in the event of an earthquake
If this amount of lateral movement is not acceptable the retaining structure
should be designed with higher dynamic lateral forces For rigid abutment
walls less than 1 to 2 inches of lateral movement dynamic lateral forces
due to seismic loading may be estimated using 58H pounds per lineal foot
of wall for level backfill conditions
The dynamic lateral earth forces presented above are in addition to the static
lateral earth pressures The resultant force should be assumed to act
through the mid height of the wall An appropriately reduced factor of safety
may be used when dynamic lateral earth forces are accounted for in the
design of the retaining structures
Drainage
Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressures A prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain
or EnkaDrain may be used behind the CLSM backfill The prefabricated
drainage product should be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe
near the base of the CLSM backfill and drained out to the front of the new
wall with solid pipes at spacing of about 8 feet For the existing CRM wall
area the existing weepholes should be extended through the CLSM backfill
out to the front of the new wall

REMARKS This facsimile memorandum provides retaining wall recommendations for
design of the new wall structure near Henry Street for the above project
Previously we recommended using a segmental retaining wall SRW
system for the new wall structure We understand it is not desirable to use
the segmental retaining wall system because of the existing utilities that
would need to be relocated for the wall installation
An alternate retaining wall system that may be considered consists of a
reinforced concrete wall with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM
backfill In the embankment area the embankment behind the wall should
be sloped back at a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V inclination or
flatter and the area between the new wall and slope should be backfilled
with CLSM In addition a subdrain should be installed near the bottom of
the slope as shown on the typical section provided on the attached Plate 1
For the existing CRM wall and GRP slope area the area between the
existing CRM wall and GRP slope should be backfilled with CLSM A
subdrain also should be installed near the bottom of the slope Please refer
to the attached Plate 2 for a typical section against the existing CRM wall
and GRP slope
We believe that the 1H 1V slope into the granular embankment material and
the existing CRM wall structure are stable units independent of the new
concrete wall In addition we believe that the static lateral pressure from the
hardened CLSM backfill would be minimal Because of the above we
believe that the static lateral earth pressure against the concrete wall will be
minimal However the concrete wall should be designed for the seismic
inertial forces of the concrete wall and CLSM backfill
