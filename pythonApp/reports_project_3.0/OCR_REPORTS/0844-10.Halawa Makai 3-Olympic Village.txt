SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Most of the site is underlain by a layer of
compacted fill followed by a natural crust
of clayey silt and a thick deposit of soft
compressible silts
We recommend surcharging of the future
building sites to reduce differential
settlements prior to the construction of the
proposed structures and concrete slabs
Maximum allowable soil pressure of 2000 psf
may be used for spread footing and retaining

foundations vary with locations and loads

estimated long term differential settlement
using shallow footings could be in the

is removed

should be made aware that these settlements
will continue for many years and that some
building maintenance and structural adjust
ment may be required as a result of these
future settlements

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The new borings generally confirm the previous boring
information the site is underlain by the following successive
layers
1 Fill compacted silt and gravel
1 to 8 feet thick
2 Crust natural clayey silt stiff and
desiccated 4 to 25 feet thick
3 Silt soft and compressible shelly silt
0 to 50 feet thick
4 Mudrock and gravel layers of hard volcanic
tuff and dense silty gravel 1 to 10 feet
thick and
5 Older alluvium stiff silt encountered
at depths ranging from 30 to 90 feet
below existing grade
It should be noted that the upper natural clayey silt crust
is both relatively thick and moderately expansive changes
in volume with changes in moisture content According to

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the underlying foundation
conditions are found to be heterogeneous and generally
confirms the previous soil investigations Because of the
soft compressible silt layer settlements will occur due to
the recent fill placement outside the building locations and

We recommend surcharging of the proposed building sites with
5 to 6 feet of fill above building subgrade levels for a
period of 6 to 9 months and installing settlement gauges to
monitor the rate of settlement This surcharge will help to
reduce the future areal settlement However long term
secondary settlement could still occur subsequent to the
removal of surcharge fill
Since it is not practical to wait for a period of several
years before the construction of the proposed buildings we
are recommending that the building structures be designed
for their long term settlement Calculated ultimate settle
ments of shallow footing foundations vary with locations
and loads It is estimated that long term differential
settlements after building construction could be in the
order of 14 to 2 inches Therefore column footings
retaining walls and slab on grade should be designed to
accommodate this anticipated long term settlement
Reinforced concrete retaining walls should be designed
with adequate horizontal reinforcements and slab on grade
should be reinforced in both directions with adequate
control joints to minimize potential cracks due to uneven
settlements

Site Grading
A surcharge or preload fill is recommended throughout the
building site areas 5 0 out minimum from building limits
where it is underlain by soft silt layer the weight of new
fill and surcharge will cause the ground to settle over a
period of many years A surcharge height of from 5 to 6 feet
loose fill is recommended to be placed above the finish grade
Settlement gauges should be installed during new filling to
monitor the settlement rate determine when surcharge may be
removed and when building construction using shallow footings
can proceed
To minimize delay of future construction the filling and
surcharge should be started as soon as possible
The time required for the estimated primary settlement to
occur are very difficult to predict because they depend upon
variations in texture and consistency of the subsoils It
is estimated that a 6 to 9 month surcharge period will be
required for the project The actual surcharge period will
be determined by the settlement gauge readings
The settlement gauges should be installed by the soils
engineer Settlement readings should be accurately
surveyed by the project surveyor at bi weekly intervals for

Retaining Wall
Free standing retaining walls that are structurally separated
from the roller rink building may be designed for 40 pounds
per cubic foot equivalent fluid pressure for level backfill
and equivalent fluid pressure of 55 pounds per cubic foot
may be used for sloping 2 horizontal to 1 vertical backfill
Retaining walls incorporated into the structure of the rink
building and any other walls that are restrained from
deflecting should be designed for 55 pounds per cubic foot
equivalent fluid pressure for level backfill an equivalent
fluid pressure of 75 pounds per cubic foot may be used for
2 horizontal to 1 vertical sloping backfill
