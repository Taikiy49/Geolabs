Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of an open flow equalization basin and
appurtenant structures at the flow equalization facility of the Mililani Diversion Relief
Sewer project in Mililani on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The open basin will be about
280 feet long by 120 to 170 feet wide and about 13 feet high The basin will be
constructed primarily by cutting into the existing ground to a depth of approximately
13 feet An impervious lining will be installed along the bottom and sides of the basin
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance for the subject project
was to obtain an overview of the subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a
subsurface data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the design of the equalization basin Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated August 31 2001 The scope of work included the
following tasks and work efforts

Site Description
The project site is located within the Mililani Sewage Treatment Plant at the
southeastern end of Makapipipi Street The proposed basin is located at the
southeastern end of the STP Facility as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 2
The site is presently an open area The existing ground surface slopes down
gently toward the east at gradients of about 2 to 3 percent The existing ground
elevations range from about 472 to 477 feet above Mean Sea Level
Subsurface Conditions
Previous borings in the area generally indicate that the site is underlain by stiff
residual soils consisting of reddish brown silty clay and clayey silt with occasional remnant
weathered boulders
Groundwater is not anticipated within the depths of the excavation
Discussion and Recommendations
Available subsurface information indicates that the site is underlain by stiff
residual soils consisting of reddish brown silty clay and clayey silt with weathered
boulders It is our opinion that from a geotechnical engineering standpoint the site is
suitable for the basin

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork vegetation and deleterious material should be stripped
and removed from the site
The sides and bottom of the basin should be a relatively smooth surface free of
rocky materials and particles which may puncture the impervious liner system The
finished subgrades of the slope face and bottom of the basin should be proof rolled
with a minimum 10 ton roller for a minimum of six passes to provide a relatively
smooth surface for the placement of the underlining and impervious liner
Excavation
Based on our site reconnaissance and previous subsurface exploration the project
site is underlain by silty clay and clayey silt residual soil It is anticipated that the
residual soil may be excavated with normal excavation equipment such as
backhoes and bulldozers
Cut Slopes
Cut slopes to be covered with the impervious liner should be designed with an
inclination of two horizontal one vertical 2H 1V or flatter for the proposed irrigation
lake This is to facilitate the installation of the impervious liner
Impervious Liner and Underlining
We understand that a synthetic liner will be provided for the basin in order to reduce
the potential for water infiltration through the underlying material It is our opinion that the

material type performance installation and protection details of the impervious liner
should be designed in accordance with the recommendations by the liner manufacturer It
is critical that the liner maintain its integrity to prevent saturation and build up of seepage
water pressures within the slopes
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicate that there may
sometimes be a tendency for the liners to detach at the top of the water impoundment It
appears that this may be the result of inadequate anchoring of the top of the liner and
insufficient flexibility of the liner material We recommend that the liner manufacturer be
consulted regarding proper construction detailing and installation of the liner with
particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the potential for liner failure Effects of
fluctuating water levels on the performance of the impervious liner near the top anchoring
should also be considered The anchorage trench should be backfilled with base course
material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Technical representatives of the manufacturers of the impervious liner should be
required to be on site at all times during their installation to assure that proper construction
procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative of the supplier s may
not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function
An underlining consisting of a heavy duty geotextile fabric is recommended beneath
the impervious liner to increase the puncture resistance of the liner As a minimum
requirement the geotextile fabric should have a minimum weight of 8 ounces per square
yard when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3776
Slope Stability
Based on the soil rock conditions encountered in our field reconnaissance and the
maximum recommended slope inclination of 2H 1V it is our opinion that the proposed
slopes for the basin should have adequate factors of safety against sliding and rotational
failures

Limitations

information obtained from the field observations Variations of subsoil conditions beyond
the field observations may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
