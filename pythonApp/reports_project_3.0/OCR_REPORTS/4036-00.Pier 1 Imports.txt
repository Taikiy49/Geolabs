INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Proposed Pier 1 Imports project at the existing Hawaii Kai Executive
Plaza II in Hawaii Kai on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located at the old Hanatei Restaurant building within the
existing Hawaii Kai Executive Plaza Il in Hawaii Kai Based on the information provided
the existing building is approximately 7 000 square feet of slab on grade construction
It is proposed to expand the building along the mauka side to provide an additional
2 000 square feet of retail space for the Pier 1 Imports retail store In addition a
loading dock area will be constructed at the mauka Koko Head corner of the building
We understand the building extension will also include slab on grade construction
with a finish floor elevation to match the existing building Based on the preliminary site
plan provided the approximate finish floor elevation is at about 9 0 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL Therefore we envision minimal site grading consisting of demolition of the
existing parking area and recompaction of the subgrade for the slab on grade
construction will be needed In addition we envision that new pavement construction will
be needed for the parking and loading dock area of this project Further details pertaining
to the proposed construction were not available at the time this report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes came to an
end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Koolau Range and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt The Koolau volcanic shield was built by
thinly bedded lava flows during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs The
main shield building activities ceased approximately 2 million years ago
After a long period of cessation of the shield building volcanic activity returned
with the formation of a series of lava flows cinder cones and tuff cones such as those
associated with Koko Crater Many of the eruptions were rather explosive in nature due
to the interaction of groundwater with the rising magma resulting in steam explosions
which expelled large quantities of pyroclastic material predominantly ash and cinder
Some explosive eruptions produced ash that cemented together to form a firm rock
known as tuff locally known as mudrock
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea leveis fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 5
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposition of lagoonal marine sediments in
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials or coral detritus
Land development and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the
project site to its present form

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings to
depths ranging from about 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
in general our field exploration encountered a 2 inch surface layer of asphalt
concrete underlain by dense to very dense silty and sandy fill materials The fill layer
ranged from approximately 8 5 to 13 5 feet thick Underlying the fill materials coral
detritus was encountered to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface In general the coral detritus consists of medium dense coral
sand gravel and silt with shell fragments In Boring No 3 a 2 foot thick layer of stiff
lagoonal deposit consisting of clayey silt was encountered below the fill layer and above
the coral detritus
Groundwater was encountered at depths varying from approximately 4 5 to
6 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should
be noted that due to the close proximity of the project site to the shoreline groundwater
levels are subject to change due to tidal fluctuation seasonal precipitation storm surge
conditions and other factors
For more detailed descriptions of the materials encountered please refer to the
Logs of Borings presented on Plates A 1 through A 3 of Appendix A Laboratory tests
were performed on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented in
Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed Pier 1 Imports site is generally
underlain by dense to very dense fill materials consisting of silty and sandy soils
overlying medium dense coral detritus Based on the generally dense near surface soil

conditions encountered in our borings we believe that a shallow foundation system may
be used for support of the proposed new building addition
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade We anticipate the bottom of footings will be above the
groundwater table New footings located adjacent to the existing structure should be
lowered to the same depths as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the
existing foundations Detailed discussions of these items and other geotechnical aspects
of the project including our geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in
the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the relatively dense near surface soil conditions encountered at the
project site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for
support of the proposed new building addition An allowable bearing pressure of up to
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on
the recompacted in situ soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades Based on the preliminary plans provided we anticipate that the
finish floor elevation of the new Pier 1 Imports will be at an elevation of about 9 0 feet
MSL Therefore we anticipate the bottom of footings will be above the groundwater
table New footings located adjacent to the existing structures should be lowered to the
same depths as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the existing
foundations The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed in the field
during construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 8
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted near surface soils as recommended herein to be
less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system
A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf above the
groundwater table and 150 pcf below the groundwater table This assumes that the soil
around footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Footing subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with test
procedures In addition the footing subgrade should be firm and unyielding
Where soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 9
The over excavation should be backfilled with granular fill material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing should be extended down to
the underlying competent material The over excavated soils may be used as a source
of backfill material provided that they are well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and free of deleterious materials
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to evaluate the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed Pier 1
Imports project In general the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Details of subgrade
preparation are presented in the Site Grading section of this report
For more uniform support of the interior slabs we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill layer The cushion fill
layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab
cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and compaction of
the subgrade soils
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab Two inches of moist fine sand
may be considered on top of the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane
and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet
vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 10
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of select borrow subbase
material The select borrow subbase material should be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils underneath the sidewalk slabs
should also be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Site Grading section of this report Construction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the slab and foundation
Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades site grading
work will consist of minor cuts and fills At the on set of earthwork the area within the
contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared All debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Pavements and slabs of the existing structures to be demolished should be
completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material
placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Utilities to be abandoned in place
under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low
pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 11
After demolition and clearing exposed subgrades and areas to receive fills should
be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction The finish subgrade should be kept moist until covered by cushion material
subbase course base course and or concrete If shrinkage cracks are noted after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the subgrade soil be
moisture conditioned and recompacted to close all cracks In addition saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
Based on our field exploration we believe that the excavated on site fill soils
may be reused as a source of general fill material provided that they are
moisture conditioned and compacted as recommended herein In general the on site
fill material to be used as general fill should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
It should be noted that due to the relatively shallow groundwater table the excavated
on site materials may require aeration prior to being reused as fill in order to achieve
the required compaction
If imported fill materials are required the material should consist of granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should
be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the

optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction
Pavements
New flexible pavements are planned for the parking areas for the proposed
project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for these areas of the project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks
In addition rigid pavements are planned for the loading dock area where vehicle loading
will consist of heavy trucks Based on the laboratory test results the near surface fill soils
at the site have a CBR value of about 17 with a swell value of less than 1 percent We
have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the material tested Based on the above assumptions we recommend that
the following pavement sections be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Rigid Pavement
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The subbase and base course material should also
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the recommended design sections If
GEOLABS HAWAII

the results of the laboratory CBR tests performed on actual subgrade soils are different
than assumed above the recommended pavement sections should be revised to reflect
the actual subgrade soil conditions The recommended pavement sections assume that
good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site
during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a_ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the proposed project and
some of the utility trenches may extend below the groundwater table Therefore if the
majority of utilities are to be located below the groundwater table dewatering
procedures should be considered Where dewatering is needed for construction of the
proposed project the selection of equipment and methods of dewatering should be left
up to the contractor The dewatering method selected should have minimal impact on
the groundwater level surrounding the proposed excavation and maintain stability of
and also provide safe and dry working conditions in the excavation In addition the
dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications to
accommodate various ground conditions
In general for utility trenches a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes
If loose soft soils are encountered at or near the invert of the pipes an additional
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4036 00 Page 14
18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a filter fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent
should be provided below the bedding layer for more uniform support Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes or about 12 inches above the groundwater level to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compactive effort of the
backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical that the
free draining material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes or
groundwater level to the top of the subgrade may consist of the excavated on site soils
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches
will be located below paved areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths ranging from 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with solid and hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by

Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two sieve analysis tests were performed to evaluate the gradation characteristics
and to aid in soil classification The tests were performed on selected soil samples in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation C 136 Graphic presentation of the test results
is provided on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
near surface soil sample to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 2
