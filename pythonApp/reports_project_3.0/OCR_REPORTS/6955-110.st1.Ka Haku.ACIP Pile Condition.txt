PROJECT HISTORY 
The new high-rise condominium and parking structure planned at the site was 
designed to be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 24-inch ACIP piles with 
an allowable compressive load capacity of up to 1,200 kips per pile. Most of the ACIP piles 
would extend through the soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits (near the surface) and derive 
support primarily from skin friction and end-bearing within coral ledges and/or coralline 
detritus materials encountered below the lagoonal deposit. The ACIP pile length of up to 
about 100 feet was installed for the 1,200-kip capacity piles.  Geotechnical engineering 
recommendations for the project are summarized in our geotechnical engineering report 
dated June 14, 2019, referenced above. The following table summarizes the ACIP pile 
foundation lengths installed for the project.

A total of 185 ACIP piles (including 5 tower crane piles), were installed from 
March 2, 2020 through April 20, 2020 under the near-continuous (full-time) observations 
by field engineers from our office.  
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction. Based on our recommendations, non-destructive integrity testing utilizing Low 
Strain Impact (LSI) Tests was performed on the completed ACIP Pile Nos. 1 through 10 on 
March 20, 2020. Based on our analysis of the test results, we recommended acceptance 
of the constructed Pile Nos. 1 through 10.  Details of the LSI testing performed on ACIP 
Pile Nos. 1 through 10 is summarized in our above-reference report dated April 29, 2020. 
After the completion of the LSI testing, Geolabs issued the above-referenced 
Foundation Installation Report dated May 1, 2020, providing our professional opinion that 
the production ACIP piles have been installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to 
provide the allowable design load capacities. 
Shortly after the completion of the foundation installation, the construction of the 
project was halted.  The majority of the installed ACIP piles and reinforcement bars were 
located below the ground surface and fully encased in grout.  Some installed ACIP piles

CURRENT CONDITION OF ACIP PILE FOUNDATIONS 
We understand that it is desired to resume construction of the high-rise building and 
parking structure at the project site.  The design of the new building and parking structure 
will be very similar to the previously designed project in 2020 with some minor design 
changes that affect the superstructure.  Based on the information provided, the locations, 
depths and design capacities of the ACIP pile foundations will remain the same from the 
previously designed project. 
During a site visit on February 28, 2023, ACIP piles that were encased in concrete 
were observed.  The Sonotube forms were still mostly intact and reinforcement bars were 
still encased in concrete.  The contractor has since exposed seven ACIP piles by chipping 
away the concrete around the reinforcement bars.  In general, the integrity of the exposed 
ACIP pile grout appears to be sound.  We understand that the structural engineer will assess 
the corrosion of reinforcement bars as more ACIP piles are exposed. 
Based on our review and evaluation of the available information, we believe that the 
ACIP pile foundations are currently still in a good condition.  Therefore, it is our professional 
opinion that the ACIP pile foundations installed at the site in 2020 still has sufficient 
resistance to provide the allowable design load capacities at the installed depths and 
locations.  Geolabs should be retained during construction of the project to further assess 
the condition of the ACIP pile foundations as the piles are exposed at finish cut-off 
elevations.
