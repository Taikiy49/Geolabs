Based on our literature research site reconnaissance and preliminary field
exploration performed for the proposed Palau Compact Road project we anticipate that
the proposed roadway alignment will traverse various volcanic sedimentary and
alluvial lagoonal deposits ranging from very dense volcanic breccia and hard tuff rock
formation to medium stiff to very stiff saprolite and old alluvial soils and to very soft
and or loose recent alluvial mangrove swamp and coralline lagoonal deposits
Our site reconnaissance indicates that potential hazards such as slope failure or
block type landslides settlement of soft soil conditions seepage of groundwater flooding
associated with high rainfall storm events and tsunami and storm surge inundation may
have adverse impacts on the project construction and future roadway maintenance for the
subject project Special attention should be given to reduce the adverse impacts of these
potential hazards during the design and construction of the proposed roadway project
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated along the proposed roadway
alignments we believe that slope inclinations of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
and two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used for the preliminary design of the
planned cut and fill slopes respectively Where a steeper slope inclination is desired for
the planned cut and fill slopes Geolabs Hawaii should be consulted to provide site

the high moisture soils will be required in order to achieve the normal 90 percent

estimate that the on site saprolite materials may experience a decrease in volume on the

will settle due to compression by self weight Therefore a settlement waiting period and
a settlement monitoring program is recommended for deep fills during construction prior
to pavement construction

A Project Considerations
We understand that it is desired to improve the existing roadway system on the
island of Babeldaob in the Republic of Palau with the construction of approximately
85 kilometers of new roadway and associated structures known as the Palau Compact
Road project The proposed Palau Compact Road project will involve the design and
construction of a two lane two way rural collector road to U S standards with optimum
operation and maintenance characteristics including pavements roadside drainage cross
drainage bridges shoulders guardrails pavement markings signage drainage markers
mile markers and intersections Roadway lighting and signal lights sidewalks curbs
and gutters are not required elements for the proposed project

The islands of Palau are a part of Micronesia located in the western portion of
the north Pacific Ocean as shown on the Project Location and Vicinity Map Plate 1 It
lies approximately 885 kilometers to the east of Mindanao Philippines and about
1 311 kilometers to the southwest of Guam The Palau Islands consist of a small
elongated arc shaped island chain in the Western Caroline Islands which trend
generally north to south extending from Latitude 8 degrees 12 minutes North to
Latitude 6 degrees 53 minutes North and from Longitude 134 degrees 8 minutes East
to Longitude 134 degrees 44 minutes East The major islands comprising the Republic
of Palau generally trend in a north south direction and consist of Babeldaob Koror
Peleliu and Angaur The general location and vicinity of the
Republic of Palau is shown on the Project Location and Vicinity Map Plate 1
The proposed Palau Compact Road project is located on the island of
Babeldaob which lies due north of Koror the capital of the Republic of Palau The
island of Babeldaob is composed of ten states each with its own governor including
Ngatpang
Ngchesar Aimeliik and Airai as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Babeldaob was
connected to Koror by a bridge locally known as the Koror Babeldaob K B Bridge
which provided access for vehicles between the islands In September 1996 the
bridge catastrophically collapsed leaving the islands separated and without means for
vehicular traffic Presently the primary means of commuting between the islands is by
government operated barge service and boats
A Regional Geologic Setting
The Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean Basin are separated by a series of
elongated submarine ridge structures The Palau Islands are situated at the exposed
W O 3595 00 Page 13
GEOLABS HAWAII
SECTION Ill SITE CHARACTERIZATION
crest of the southwestern most submarine ridge of the area The Palau ridge is part of
the Palau Kyushu ridge which extends from Kyushu Japan southward towards Palau
where a complementary trench structure forms to the southeast of Palau These ridge
and trench structures constitute the Palau Arc The eastern slope of the Palau ridge
slopes steeply to the Palau trench which plunges to about 8 230 meters at about
48 kilometers east of the north end of the Palau Islands The west flank of the ridge
slopes gradually to depths of about 5 490 meters at about 400 kilometers to the west
The andesite line which marks the margin of the western Pacific Basin has
been positioned along the eastern edge of the Palau trench This demarcation
separates the region of predominantly andesitic volcanism to the west from the Pacific
Basin region of basaltic volcanism The area is also a major structural and
physiographic boundary between the two regions
The structure of the Palau Islands reflects their situation as the exposed crest of
the Palau ridge The island s elongated form has resulted from volcanic emissions and
deposition of pyroclastic materials along the arcuate ridge structure Due to the varied
locations of the volcanic sources and amount of emissions the successive
accumulation of pyroclastic materials was highly irregular in occurrence In addition
most of the eruptions were subaqueous resulting in submarine landslides and slumps
which contributed to the deformed and highly irregular character of the depositional
surfaces
B Geology of Babeldaob
Babeldaob Island exists as a result of the accumulation of volcanic materials
along the crest of the Palau Ridge The form of the island is principally controlled by the
faulting associated with past tectonic activity numerous historic submergence and
W O 3595 00 Page 14
GEOLABS HAWAII
SECTION III SITE CHARACTERIZATION
emergence as a result of sea level changes and tectonism marine erosion erosion by
streams and the effects of deep tropical weathering of the volcanic materials
comprising the island
In general Babeldaob consists of a central strip of the oldest volcanic rock
overlapped on the east by the next younger unit and separated by a fault zone on the
west from the youngest volcanic rocks which are arranged in a rough circle around a
former caldera at Karamando Bay The regional ridge and trench structure extensive
volcanism and the presence of numerous faults cutting the pyroclastic deposits confirm
past tectonic activity However there is little evidence of on going deformation of the
arc at the present time
Based on the Geology Map of Babelthuap and Adjacent Islands Palau Islands
prepared by the U S Geological Survey the geologic materials mapped on Babeldaob
include various volcanic sedimentary and lagoonal or beach deposits The most
recent geologic materials include man made fills mangrove swamp mud alluvial
sediments and coralline beach deposits These materials continue to be deposited at
the present time Sedimentary rocks deposited in historic Miocene through Pleistocene
time include the Airai clay with lignite and Palau limestone The volcanic materials
comprise the oldest deposits which consist of the Babeldaob Aimeliik and
Ngeremlengui formations and make up the bulk of the land area on Babeldaob Island
The Babeldaob formation consists of massive basaltic andesitic volcanic breccia
with some tuff breccia and layers of tuff Breccia is a coarse grained voicanic rock
composed of angular broken rock fragments or clasts welded together in a finer
grained mineral rock matrix Breccia is formed by volcanic explosions and or debris
flows of super heated rock mass Rocks and soils associated with this formation exist

as a large rectangular shaped area extending north northeast to south southwest in
central Babeldaob
The Aimeliik formation consists of andesitic basaltic volcanic breccias tuff
breccia and bedded tuffs and is divided into the Ngarsul and Ngardok members which
are composed of varying amounts of tuff and breccia materials The formation
underlies most of northeastern eastern and southern Babeldaob
The Ngeremlengui formation consists of andesitic and dacitic volcanic breccia

C Geography
The Palau Islands form a curved chain of islands totaling about 350 in number
and covering approximately 458 square kilometers in area The island chain extends
approximately 161 kilometers in length and 64 kilometers in width Babeldaob the
largest island of the chain is an elongated volcanic island trending north to south and
encompasses about 208 square kilometers of land area Elevations on Babeldaob
range from about sea level along the coastal areas to about 242 meters above mean
sea level at Mt Ngerekelehuus on the northwestern side of Babeldaob
The Palau reef encircles the island of Babeldaob as a barrier and fringing reefs
creating extensive quiet water lagoons and bays with some deep channels and
passages adjacent to the island Beyond the fringing reefs the ocean floor drops
steeply to greater than 300 meters in depth within a distance of about 1 kilometer

The island of Babeldaob is primarily of volcanic origin with some localized
limestone formation It is characterized by rolling uplands and incised streams in
extremely weathered voicanic materials Flat alluvial areas are mainly restricted to the
mouths and banks of larger streams and along the coastline situated behind sand
beaches where inland hills do not abruptly terminate at the shoreline More rugged
terrain consisting of sharp ridges and cliff lines occur on the western and southeastern
portions of the island Rounded hilltop knobs and ridge Jines rise above the rolling hills
at scattered locations offering prominent landmarks amongst the generally
homogenous backdrop of rolling topography
The coastline of Babeldaob is irregularly shaped with many small bays and
headlands Swamp areas consisting of dense mangrove within shallow tidal influenced
flatlands exist throughout approximately 70 percent of the coastline areas The width of
the mangrove swamp varies from less than 10 meters to greater than 800 meters in
some places The mangrove swamps encompass the lowland coasts and large areas
of the bays in addition to some inland areas surrounding major rivers and streams
The drainage pattern on Babeldaob is essentially trellised with the larger streams
and rivers aligned with the longitudinal axis of the island Ngeremeskang River is the
longest river at about 15 8 kilometers in length which flows into Karamando Bay from
the northeast The Ngardok River is the largest watershed on Babeldaob
encompassing some 29 square kilometers in area

Geogrids are generally polymer grid structures with a tensile strength comparable
to steel It generally provides a cost effective solution to slope stability problems which
may include the following insufficient right of way high surcharge loads poor quality
fills high seismic forces steep slopes or difficult landslide repairs When geogrids are
placed in soil their grid geometry interlocks with adjacent soil creating a soil geogrid
composite with greatly enhanced engineering properties Different grid configurations are
available to provide optimum soil grid interaction for a range of soil types and slope
reinforcement applications The construction methodology is briefly described as follows
As the slope is constructed horizontal layers of geogrids are placed in the
compacted fill at predetermined levels The lengths of the geogrid layers are designed to
anchor potential failure zones into stable interior sections of the embankment or hillside
As forces develop within a soil mass the high modulus geogrids are immediately pulled
into tension The geogrids transfer this tensile force from the unstable soil back into less
stressed portions of the slope and stability is thus maintained

1 Lime Stabilization
Lime has been used to improve the properties of fine grained soils for many years
The general objectives of lime stabilization are to modify and improve the
characteristics of the soils thus expediting construction and improving the
strength and durability of the soils In particular it is desired to control the volume
stability swelling and shrinkage characteristics and to reduce the plasticity of the
soils
When lime is added to clayey soils several chemical reactions are initiated These
reactions are cation exchange and flocculation agglomeration they are also
pozzolanic In the cation and flocculation agglomeration reactions the clay
particles tend to bond together to form larger sized particles These reactions tend
to decrease the liquid limit increase the plastic limit decrease the plasticity index
increase the workability and improve the strength and deformation properties of
the clay
The pozzolanic reactions involve the formation of cementitious materials between
lime and the silica and alumina of the clay The end products are a series of
calcium silicate hydrates It is important that a sufficient amount of lime be present
to provide a high environment In the long term the high pH causes a
breakdown of the crystal lattice of the clay and the formation of cementitious
products These reactions can continue for a long period of time

Construction fabrics have been used in recent years to provide a cost effective

J Pavement Drainage System
One of the primary distress mechanisms observed in pavement structures is
pumping due to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Based on our
experience with projects having similar soil conditions there is a strong tendency for
water to accumulate at the interface between the granular subbase material and the
underlying clayey pavement subgrade soils In addition free water voids in the
pavement section repeated heavy wheel loads and an erodible base can also cause
pumping Unless the pavement subgrade soils are properly prepared and adequate
drainage is provided the accumulated water will likely soften the silty and clayey
subgrade soils and cause premature failure of the new pavement Therefore paved
areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all surface water off

Samples recovered from our preliminary field exploration program performed in
Babeldaob Republic of Palau were visually examined and classified in our laboratory in
Honolulu Hawaii In addition strength characteristics of the cohesive soil samples
recovered were evaluated using Pocket Penetrometer and Torvane apparatus
Selected samples were tested to evaluate the physical characteristics and engineering
properties of the soils encountered The laboratory testing program was formulated
with the emphasis on evaluating the moisture density plasticity gradation
compressibility shear strength and compaction characteristics of the soils
encountered The laboratory tests performed include determination of dry density and
natural moisture content ring swell specific gravity Atterberg Limits hydrometer direct
shear triaxial compression consolidation modified consolidation collapse potential
Modified Proctor compaction and California Bearing Ratio CBR tests Results of the
tests performed are summarized in Tables 1 through 3 of Appendix B Detailed
descriptions of the various tests performed for this preliminary exploration are
presented in the following sections
Moisture and Density
Moisture content and dry density tests were performed on soil samples to aid in
defining the interface between various soil layers encountered and correlating the
layers between test borings The moisture content tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2216 The results of these tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Ring Swell
One dimensional swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed soil
samples to evaluate the magnitude of the swelling potential of the in situ cohesive soils
Tests were performed in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 4546 and the
results are summarized on Plates B 1 and B 2

Specific Gravity
Specific gravity tests were performed on selected samples to aid in the
determination of phase relationships of soils that is the volume of solids to water and
air in a given volume of soil The tests were performed in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 854 The test results are summarized on Tables 1 through 3 and on
Plate B 3
Atterberg Limits
Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the
liquid limit LL and plasticity limit PL as an aid in classifying the soils and interpreting
the test results The Atterberg Limits tests were performed in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 4318 The test results are summarized on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of the test results are
provided on Plates B 4 through B 6
Hydrometer
Hydrometer tests were performed on the fine grained soils less than 75 ym to
evaluate the gradation characteristics of the fines and to aid in soil classification The
tests were performed in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 422 Graphic
presentation of the test results are provided on Plates B 7 through B 10
Direct Shear
To evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the on site soils as fill materials
strained controlled consolidated drained direct shear tests were performed on
remolded soil samples in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3080 The test
results are shown on Plates B 11 through B 13
Triaxial Compression Unconsolidated Undrained
To evaluate the undrained shear strength of the clayey soils unconsolidated
undrained triaxial compression tests were performed on selected samples in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2850 The approximate in situ effective
overburden pressure was used as the applied confining pressure The test results and
stress strain curves are presented on Plates B 14 through B 19
W O 3595 00 Page 2

Consolidation
Consolidation tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the
compressibility characteristics of the on site soils These tests were performed in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2435 and the test results are plotted on
Plates B 20 and B 21
Modified Consolidation Collapse Potential
The conventional consolidation tests were modified to evaluate the
compressibility characteristics of remolded soil samples under different surcharge
loads A remolded soil sample is allowed to settle until it has stabilized under a
surcharge load Water is then added to the soil sample to evaluate its collapse or swell
potential under a saturated condition The test results are presented on Plate B 22
Modified Proctor Test
Laboratory compaction tests were performed on the bulk samples obtained to
evaluate the relationship between the moisture content and the dry unit weight of the
near surface soils The Modified Proctor tests were performed in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 The test results are presented on Plates B 23 through
B 25
California Bearing Ratio CBR
CBR tests were performed on the near surface soils to evaluate the strength of
the in situ cohesive materials for pavement support The tests were performed in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The test results are presented on
Plates B 26 through B 38
Corrosion
A series of tests were performed to provide information on the corrosive potential
of the soil These tests include measurements of pH ASTM D 2976 minimum
resistivity ASTM G 57 chloride content and sulfate content
The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 39

APPENDIX C
Geotechnical
NX
t
Survey Maps
Geotechnical engineering efforts including literature research _ site
reconnaissance and field exploration were undertaken to obtain a general overview of
the surface and subsurface conditions along the preliminary roadway alignments for the
proposed Palau Compact Road project The data and information obtained were
compiled and are presented in the Geotechnical Survey Maps Plates C 1 through C 10
of Appendix C A key to the ten Geotechnical Survey Maps is provided on Plate C
Our literature research efforts included the review of available soils and geologic
information pertinent to the project site and its vicinity such as geology maps aerial
photographs conference paper and reports In addition to the background research a
general site reconnaissance was conducted by a Project Geologist on the island of
Babeldaob in the Republic of Palau between December 5 and 19 1996 to collect
pertinent data and information for the proposed Palau Compact Road project
The site reconnaissance was conducted by driving along the existing dirt roads
and access trails which were located on and or nearby to the proposed alignment
route Observations related to soil rock types geologic structure and potential
geotechnical hazards were recorded on the project field maps Areas which could not
be accessed by vehicle were explored by limited walking traverse Due to the relatively
large size of the overall project site the scale of available field maps and the variety of
soil and rock types identified in the field efforts were made to consolidate the various
earth material types as shown on the Geotechnical Survey Maps These information
were supplemented and or confirmed with the boring data obtained from the limited
borings drilled for this preliminary exploration program
The purpose of the maps is to show the relationship between observed soil and
rock types and the general location of the Palau Compact Road alignment Additional
information such as the location of borings and bulk sample collection points existing
drainages planned bridges and the location of key photographs taken were also
included in the maps It should be noted that the location of features and geologic
contacts mapped between soil and rock of different character are only approximate and
may be gradational in occurrence The locations of these soil rock types features and
structures shown on the Geotechnical Survey Maps are approximate having been
estimated with the use of aerial photographs mileage relationships topography and
other instrumentation such as compass altimeter and Global Positioning System
GPS equipment
