Dear Mr Luke
As requested Geolabs Inc has provided geotechnical engineering services
during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new light pole structures at the
above project site This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations
performed during the drilled shaft installation for the above light pole structures In
general the purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled
shaft foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and
specifications
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Pier 51B container yard site is between the Pier 51A and Pier 51C yard sites
at Honolulu Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves the replacement
of the pavement system at the container yard installation of drainage and water
systems and the installation of light pole structures The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A total of 10 new light pole structures were installed for the project We
understand that the structural loading at the top of the foundation consists of a
horizontal load of 10 2 kips and applied moment of 540 foot kips Based on the
subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural loading a deep
foundation system consisting of 60 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled shafts
was utilized for foundation support of the light pole structures A minimum drilled shaft
length of 25 feet below the finished concrete pavement was used The approximate
locations of the drilled shafts for the light pole structures are shown on the Drilled Shaft
Identification Plan Plate 2

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company performed the drilled shaft
installation Ten 60 inch diameter drilled shafts designated as FLP 1 through FLP 10
were installed between and May 23 2008 A summary of the
drilled shaft installation is provided on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary Plate 3 In
general a Geolabs representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel
reinforcements and concrete placement operations during the drilled shaft installation
For two of the drilled shafts FLP 6 and FLP 7 shaft excavation was not observed due
to the late notification of the excavation work
The production shafts were excavated using a Soilmec R625 track mounted drill
rig equipped with 60 inch diameter auger 52 inch drilling bucket 60 inch diameter core
barrel and 36 inch and 55 inch cleanout buckets Temporary casings with a diameter
of 65 inches were utilized to case the upper portions of the drilled shafts during the shaft
construction The drilled shafts were excavated to elevations ranging from about 17 2
to 19 2 feet MSL
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 4 inches between
the sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a
4 inch OD steel pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the
tremie pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the
specifications
For four of the drilled shafts FLP 5 through FLP 8 the measured concrete
slump was 9 75 and 10 inches These measured slumps are above the maximum
concrete slump of 9 inches in the project specification We understand that the
concrete cylinder breaks were well above the design concrete strength Based on the
above the high concrete slump for these shafts was considered satisfactory
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the light
pole foundations have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
location of the top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to
verify the shaft position is within the tolerance in the project specifications Based on
our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our opinion that the drilled shafts
have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
