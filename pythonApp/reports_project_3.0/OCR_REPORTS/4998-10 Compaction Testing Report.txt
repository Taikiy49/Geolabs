This report summarizes our compaction testing services performed during

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Pavilion project is located within the Kapolei area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located across the parking structure for the
Hotel The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The new one story building will be approximately 20 000 square feet in
gross area with attached restrooms a food plating area and storage area In addition
new walkways to the new pavilion will be constructed The layout of the new pavilion is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
EARTHWORK CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork construction at the Pavilion project consisted of
site grading utility and footing construction The site grading work consisted of clearing
and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills up to about 2 feet in thickness to
achieve the design finished grades for the project The site grading and utility backfilling
work were performed by Jayar Construction Inc During the site grading operations
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor In addition maximum dry density and
optinum moisture content information received from the Makakilo Quarry were used The
test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
