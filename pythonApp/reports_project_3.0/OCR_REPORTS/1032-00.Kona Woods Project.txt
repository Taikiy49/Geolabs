PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site in order to provide data for use in design and con
struction of the building foundations Injection well dril
ling and testing were also included in the scope of our work

SITE DESCRIPTION
The report site is located on the eastern corner of the inter
section of Alii Drive and Royal Poinciana Road It is roughly
L shaped with a frontage of about 660 feet along Royal Poin
ciana Road and 240 feet along Alii Drive
The site is bounded by Royal Poinciana Road on the north Alii
Drive and an existing condominium and tennis courts on the
west single family homes on the east and a wide gravel road
on the south
The ground surface is gently sloping upward to the east at a
gradient of less than 10 The ground elevation varies from
14 feet next to Alii Drive to 38 feet on the eastern boundary
of the site
Moderate amounts of broken concrete slabs and gravel piles

on the site These materials are located mostly along the

The proposed development consists of seven 2 and 3 story wood
framed condominium buildings The proposed structures vary
from about 50 by 100 feet to 50 by 200 feet in plan
Asphaltic concrete driveway and parking areas are planned
along most of the perimeter of the site
Two tennis courts are planned in the southeastern portion of
the site

2 feet are planned for most of the site Fills up to about
6 foot high are planned for portions of Building Nos 3 and
4 in this area
Sewage disposal through injection well Systems are planned

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered dense lava rock formation
at shallow depths Spread or continuous footing bearing on
the rock formation is recommended
Injection well testing in Boring No 1 indicated suitable per
colation rates for injection well effluent disposal
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation is recommended for the
proposed structures
For the proposed structures an allowable bearing value of
10 000 p s f can be used on the dense rock formation The
bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be increased
one third when temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are
considered

Building No 4 will be situated over 2 to 6 feet of fill
If select well graded imported material is utilized for the
fill the building foundations can bear on the compacted fill
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used on com
pacted fill
Footings should be a minimum of 12 inches wide
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundation
Minimum settlements are anticipated for the proposed founda
tions bearing on the dense rock formation and on the well
graded structural fill
Probing for Cavities
Although large cavities were not noted in the borings a
few small cavities were encountered
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to pos
sible large cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava
rock every major footing location should be probed The
probe holes could be drilled with a track percussion drill
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches
and should extend below the bottom of footing a depth
equal to two times the footing width or a minimum of 5 feet

The probe holes should be grounted from the bottom of the
hole
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly and to locate any potential
voids near the footing excavations
Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fills are planned for the develop
ment
Rock formation was encountered at 0 to 2 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Therefore hard excavation should
be anticipated in some of the cut areas and utility excava
tions Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vege
tation debris and rubble
The existing upper silty material should be scarified for a
depth of 6 inches moistened and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density
It appears that imported borrow material will be required for
the project Imported fill should generally be granular well
graded non expansive material less than about 8 inches in size

Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch capillary break

Retaining and basement walls up to 8 feet high are planned
for the structures
The wall foundations should bear on the near surface rock
formation An allowable bearing value of 10 000 p s f is
recommended for dense rock formation
Well graded granular material such as black sand cinder or
clinker should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet of the
backfill
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure

A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the

Percolation Testing
One 5 inch diameter injection well was drilled to a depth of
41 5 feet The upper 10 feet was cased with a 4 inch diameter
PVC pipe and the outside was backfilled with cement grout
Rock formation was encountered at 1 foot depth Generally
the upper portions of the rock formation was very dense and
the more porous zone occurred at the lower weathered rock for
mation which had RQD Rock Quality Designation values of
62 to 73
The percolation testing consisted of pumping water from a fire
hydrant into the drilled hole and observing the increase in
water level in the hole An average inflow rate of 285 g p m
was utilized during the 70 minute testing period A two foot
increase in head was measured during the test
Based on the above test results it is our opinion that the
lower fractured rock formation could provide suitable perco
lation for injection well sewage disposal
Periodic flushing and surge pumping inside the injection well
is recommended to maintain the effluent percolation

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to minitor the contractor s grading
operations The necessary inspections include the observa
tion of the site grubbing fill placement thickness quality
of the excavated fill materials and the verification that
sufficient passes have been performed to obtain the required
density on each fill lift
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion
