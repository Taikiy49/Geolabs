utilizing shallow continuous footings for foundation support of the new retaining wall planned 
at the site. Based on our field exploration, we believe that the following values may be used 
to evaluate the bearing support, sliding resistance, and passive pressure resistance of the 
planned retaining walls based on LRFD design methods.

Dynamic Lateral Earth Forces 
Dynamic lateral earth forces due to seismic loading will need to be considered in the 
design of the retaining structures based on AASHTO LRFD methods. Seismic loading 
(PGAm = 0.331g) is used to estimate the dynamic lateral earth pressures based on a peak 
bedrock acceleration of 0.258g. The table below summarizes the dynamic lateral earth 
forces acting on the retaining structure in the event of an earthquake with the estimated 
lateral displacement.
