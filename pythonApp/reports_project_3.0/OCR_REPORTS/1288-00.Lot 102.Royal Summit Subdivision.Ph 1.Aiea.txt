In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
one story split level structure approximately 46 by 52 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage
driveway and the entire house The house will be of wood frame
construction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the grading records and laboratory soil tests on the near
surface soils indicate that Lot 102 was filled with reddish brown
clayey silt the same type of soil that exist as natural soil at the
site Laboratory swell tests on these surface materials indicate that
these soils have low to moderate swelling potential if they are
maintained at or above optimum moisture content Therefore some
special foundation design and subgrade preparations are recommended
It is anticipated that up to 4 0 feet of additional fill will be
required in the middle portion of the lot to match the existing level
pad along the front portion of the site

Ideally select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral should be used for fill within 2 feet below the
cushion material under the slab This would provide a competent
bearing layer for the foundations in this portion of the house
The stockpile of reddish brown clayey silt material in the Royal
Summit Subdivision Phase II area may be available for fill at lower
costs Although this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential it could have been contaminated with other expansive soil
during handling Therefore it is recommended that these soils be
used only up to 2 feet below the slab subgrade and the remaining upper
2 feet should consist of select borrow non expansive granular soil
The garage driveway and rear portion of the house will probably bear
on the natural soils rather than fill The foundation and subgrade
preparation recommendations are listed in the following sections
Foundations on Imported Select Borrow
Foundations bearing on the minimum 2 foot cap of compacted
non expansive select borrow material may use an allowable bearing
value of 3000 p s f with the individual footing bottom embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Conventional on grade slab design may be used on the select material

To reduce cost the remaining fill below the upper 2 foot cap of
non expansive fill should consist of the on site select fill from the
Royal Summit Subdivision grading The material should first be
approved by the project soils engineer prior to its use as fill
Foundations on On Site Soil
Laboratory test results of the stiff existing soils indicated that
these materials have a low to moderate swelling potential Therefore
special precautionary measures are recommended for foundations placed
on the on site soils
The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the SLAB ON GRADE section is also required
We further recommend that a deeper footing embedment of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade for individual footings and
18 inches for perimeter slab footings be used for the stiff on site
soils An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
Footings located on steep 2H 1V sloping areas should be embedded an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the closest edge of the footing to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help minimize
the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended
bearing capacity of the soil

The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be observed by
a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been obtained
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and
then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by the ASTM D 1557 78 test
If the reddish brown clayey silt from Royal Summit is used for fill
it should first be approved by the project soils engineer and should
generally be placed wet of optimum moisture content to minimize the
swelling potential
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
Slabs On Grade
Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the garage driveway and the
entire house Where a minimum of 2 feet of imported non expansive
soil such as crusher waste is used for the f i11 conventional
on grade slab design with an 8 inch deep thickened edge may be used
Where on site soil is encountered at less than 2 feet below the
subgrade level a deeper 18 inch perimeter footing embedment and
special subgrade preparation with lime saturation are recommended

The general procedures listed hereunder for lime saturation should be
followed
1
Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
We believe that about ten 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would be
adequate to cover these areas
The subgrade soils should be water saturated with perforated hoses
by constant sprinkling for at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying
subsoil It is important that the soils engineer should be notified
to inspect the above subgrade preparation and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying

Slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded base course aggregate as a cushion layer and should be
structurally divorced from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter
systems should be installed around the house and that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the foundations and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against slab
footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
