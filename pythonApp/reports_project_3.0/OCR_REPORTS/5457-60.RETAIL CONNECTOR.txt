DRIVEN PILE LOAD TEST REPORT

ALA MOANA EXPANSION
Dear Ms Thom
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we conducted a
compressive pile load test on a selected test pile Pile No 141 for the Retail Connector
portion of the Ala Moana Expansion project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
This letter report presents the results of our field observations and the test data collected
during the static pile load test conducted on May 18 and 19 2006 for the above project
The static pile load test was conducted on a 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete pile The pile was driven on May 10 and 11 2006 using a
hydraulic impact hammer delivering about 33 000 foot pounds of energy
per blow of the hammer During the driving operations of the load test pile our field
engineer maintained a log of the blow counts needed to advance the pile The pile driving
record is provided on Plate 1
The load test pile was loaded incrementally in general accordance with
ASTM D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the pile was planned
at 300 kips In general the test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of 300 kips
200 percent of the design load of 150 kips in 15 kip load increments
In general each load increment of the load test was held for at least 8 minutes or
until the rate of settlement was generally stable In addition 2 hour and 2 5 hour holds
were maintained at 150 kip design load and 300 kip maximum test load load
increments respectively to evaluate the creep effects of the pile After loading the pile to
the maximum test load 300 kips the test load was unloaded in approximately four equal
decrements and held for at least 8 to 15 minutes at each decrement The load test results
are presented on Plates 2 1 through 2 3 and are summarized as follows
