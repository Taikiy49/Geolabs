INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Driveway Resurfacing Area 01 project within the premises
of the existing Anuenue Elementary School in the Palolo Valley area of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of pavements and site
preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Anuenue Elementary School in the
Palolo Valley area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii Extensive pavement
failures such as alligator pattern cracking depressions and potholes were observed
along the existing asphalt concrete pavement in Area 01 near the entrance from
Ahe Street Due to the deteriorated condition of the existing pavements we understand
that it is desired to evaluate and formulate remedial measures pertaining to the pavement
resurfacing and or repair work at the subject site
It is our understanding that the current project scope also includes reconstruction
of concrete sidewalks adjacent to the subject asphalt concrete pavement
Special attention was given to the pavement support characteristics of the clayey
subgrade soils for this geotechnical engineering exploration

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level The processes of erosion and
deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic sea level fluctuations When the sea
level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly lower and valleys were
carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level was high the erosional
base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and transportation downslope
toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materiais reach
the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3911 00 Page 5
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium In general stream flows in Hawaii
are intermittent and flashy such that the stream flows transmit large volumes of water for
very short duration Because of this transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of
the stream s hydraulic load consists of a poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles
gravel sands and fines When the erosional base levels change these sediment loads
are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes begin
to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the material
Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse grained portion
of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or conglomerate
Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors and upstream in
headwaters This continued erosion generates material which is transported downslope
covering the older alluvial deposits Depending on the local base level and rate of
transport these newer sediments are generally transient in terms of geologic time In
addition their consistency and density are generally less than those of the older partially
consolidated deposits
The project site is located in Palolo Valley an amphitheater headed valley carved
by erosion into Koolau Basalt The base of this valley is filled with a thick sequence of
alluvium interbedded with deposits of lava and pyroclastics of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Severe pavement distresses and failures including alligator cracks depressions
and potholes were observed on the existing asphalt concrete pavements Based on our
field exploration the existing pavements are generally underlain by highly expansive
clayey subgrade soils adobe at shallow depths In addition our drilled borings indicated
that a compacted base course layer is not evident below the asphalt concrete paving
across the project site We believe that the pavement distresses and failures observed at
the project site were caused by traffic loads over an inadequate pavement section
underlain by highly expansive subgrade soils with poor pavement support characteristics
Therefore it is our opinion that the existing deteriorated pavements should be removed
and replaced with a new pavement structural section constructed on a properly prepared
subgrade Special attention should be given to the drainage requirements since water
infiltration into the underlying highly expansive clayey subsoils may result in subsoil
softening and loss of bearing support Detailed discussion of these items and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration performed for the subject project the existing
pavement structure is generally underlain by expansive clayey subsoils with poor
pavement support characteristics at shallow depths Therefore complete removal of the
GEOLABS HAWAI

existing pavement sections to expose the subgrade areas and replacement with
compacted base and subbase materials are recommended for reconstruction of the
severely deteriorated pavement Considering normal traffic anticipated for the school it
is our opinion that reconstruction with the following new pavement sections may be used
for the subject project
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Basaltic Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inches Select Borrow 95 Percent Relative Compaction

6 0 Inches Portland Cement Concrete
12 0 Inches Select Borrow 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade

project In the event that sufficient project funding is not available and the pavement can
be designed for a shorter life the following minimum alternate pavement section may be
considered
Flexible Pavement Section Alternate Design

6 0 Inches Select Borrow 95 Percent Relative Compaction

The pavement subgrades should be prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The base
course and select borrow subbase for the flexible pavement section should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density CBR and field density tests
should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to
confirm the adequacy of the above sections
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and current scope of work we anticipate that
minimal site grading work will be required for the proposed pavement reconstruction
project At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All asphalt concrete paving vegetation debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and yielding areas encountered below pavement areas should be over excavated to
expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing grubbing and demolition the future pavement areas should be
excavated where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the select
borrow layer The pavement subgrades should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density If the
subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping subgrade may be
stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for subgrade pumping As a guide
one sack of cement may be used for approximately 25 square feet of subgrade area
Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a woven geotextile fabric such as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3911 00 Page 10
Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement of the
select borrow layer
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be re scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted as
recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due
to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas
and replacement with well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Select borrow and base course materials required for the new pavements should
be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in 8 inch level loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Site grading operations
should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a
representative from our office observe the site grading operation to evaluate whether
undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and whether the
exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered during our field exploration
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement
surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed anywhere on site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to
isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs
be extended a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soil below the base and subbase
course layers to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section
Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive water from
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3911 00 Page 11
landscaping irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a subdrain
system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas
Concrete Sidewalk
It is our understanding that the current project scope includes reconstruction of
concrete sidewalk adjacent to the project pavement repair area Based on our field
exploration performed for the project the site is generally underlain by expansive clayey
subsoils with poor pavement support characteristics at shallow depths Therefore a
4 inch concrete slab over 12 inches of select borrow compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction is recommended for the new concrete sidewalk section
Subgrade preparation should follow recommendations presented in the Site Preparation
section of this report

sampling four shallow borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths of up to

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Four 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on either relatively undisturbed or
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 1
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
