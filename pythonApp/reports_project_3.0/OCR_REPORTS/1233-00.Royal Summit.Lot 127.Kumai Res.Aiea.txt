In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in
order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed
residence The study consisted of drilling two shallow borings
laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the soils a
review of the subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 50 by 60 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage and a
covered lanai Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire
structure with a concrete rock masonry retaining wall at the
rear and a portion of both sides of the lot

will receive about 3 to 4 feet of fill in order to match with

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the southern side of Aloalii Street in the
Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu
Hawaii The lot has two generally level pads with a
transistional slope measuring about 5 1 H V between then The
ground then slopes down toward the south at a 2 1 H V ratio
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 127 required fill from 5 to 12 feet deep
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered about 5 feet of recently placed fill
consisting of a reddish brown clayey silt to the full 5 0 foot
depth of our borings
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface material
indicated that these soils have generally low swelling potential
if they are maintained at or above the optimum moisture content

However previous experience indicates that this type of
material could have moderate swelling potential if allowed to
dry out Therefore measures should be taken to prevent
excessive drying of the soil
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils have low to moderate swelling potential therefore some
precautionary measures are recommended for foundations placed on
the existing soil
It is anticipated that 3 to 4 feet of fill will be required
under a major portion of the house foundation We feel that the
key element regarding the foundation design will be the type and
amount of fill material used
Ideally select non expansive soils such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral should be used for fill within 2 feet of
the house finished grade
The on site stockpile of excess material from the Royal Summit
Subdivision grading work may be available at a lower cost
However this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential and also could be contaminated with expansive soil
during handling Therefore special grading preparation and
foundation procedures described under the SITE GRADING section

Site Grading
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill it
should be placed wet of optimum moisture content 30 to
reduce the swelling potential
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and
driveway
Where a minimum of 2 feet of off site non expansive soil such
as crusher waste is used for the fill conventional
Slab on grade design with an 8 inch deep perimeter may be used
Where on site soil is used a deeper 18 inch footing embedment
and special subgrade preparation with lime saturation is
recommended The general procedure listed hereunder should be
followed

1 The slab subgrade soils should be saturated with hoses with
holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to
concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil
2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation and thickened edge of the
driveway prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil We
believe that about ten 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would be
adequate to cover the slab subgrade It is important that
the soils engineer should inspect the above subgrade
preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying
soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
3 The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free
of loose soils It is anticipated that the very soft silt
and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation should
be removed just prior to concrete placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in
such a way that the time period between the initial
excavation and final concrete placement at each lot should be
kept to a minimum in order to prevent excessive subgrade
drying

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that CRM retaining walls are planned
along the rear and a portion of both sides of the lot in order
to retain the proposed fill of up to about 5 feet The
guidelines listed below should be followed
1 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished
grade
Wall situated on slopes should been embedded an additional
amount to maintain a minimum 8 foot horizontal setback
distance measured from the outside slope face to the edge of
the wall footing

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install gutter around the house and they should also be made
aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
perimeter footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
Mr Mrs L Kumai
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper perfor
mance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength or heaving The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
