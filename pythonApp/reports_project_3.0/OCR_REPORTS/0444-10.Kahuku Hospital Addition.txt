At your request we have drilled an additional deep boring at
the lower makai area of the hospital site near Boring 8 which
was drilled for our earlier reference report This additional
boring was made at the request of the Board of Water Supply
as a condition of obtaining permission for a cesspool sewage
disposal system for the proposed hospital addition
The purpose of the deep boring and percolation testing is to
obtain onsite seepage characteristics of the alluvial clays
below the coral sand formation and to verify the thickness of
these clays and their functioning as an impervious layer to
prevent well contamination from cesspool effluent

FIELD EXPLORATION
The boring was drilled to a depth of 101 feet on
and 10 1974 in the abandoned cane field located approximately
40 feet from the edge of the existing A C roadway as shown
on the Site Plan The boring was made with a B 61 truck mounted
rotary drill rig with rock coring attachments NX casing was
used to seal off the upper sands and corals for percolation
testing of the lower clayey material
Standard Penetration Tests were made in the alluvial clayey soil
layer and split spoon samples and cuttings from drill bits were
obtained for visual classification and Atterberg Limit testing
Percolation tests were run at two elevations in the clayey
materials under the observation of an inspector from the Board
of Water Supply of the City and County of Honolulu
SOIL CONDITIONS
Generally the soil underlying the site at Boring 9 consists
of a 2 feet thick surface layer of brown silty clay Beneath
this top layer calcareous sands with coral fragments were
encountered to a depth of 45 feet Light brown silty clays
with coral fragments were noted below the coral sand material
to about 55 feet depths which was further underlain by a very
impervious layer of reddish brown silty clays to about 87 feet

GROUNDWATER
The natural groundwater level was recorded at a depth of about
8 5 feet below the existing ground elevation which is approxi
mately at elevation 10 5 feet The water level measured
inside the 63 feet of casing at the completion of our boring
was about 5 feet below the existing ground elevation The
difference in the groundwater elevation seemed to indicate
slight artesian conditions in the lower rocker material
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The deep boring drilled at the proposed cesspool site indicated
at least 30 feet of very stiff alluvial clays underlying the
coral and sand formation
It is our opinion that these clayey layers will act as an im
pervious layer to prevent any potential sewage effluent from
contaminating the fresh water supply which is located below
the caprock material The percolation test results in the
reddish brown clayey layer confirm this impervious seepage char
