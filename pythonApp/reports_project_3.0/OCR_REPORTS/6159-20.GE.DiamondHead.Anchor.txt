DIAMOND HEAD STATE MONUMENT LOOP TRAIL DEVELOPMENT &

HONOLULU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Ms. Kodama: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the rock anchor installation and testing for the Diamond Head State 
Monument Loop Trail Development & Ledge Trail Improvements project in Honolulu on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.   
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and the results of testing 
performed for the rock anchors from October 17 through November 7, 2011.   
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is within Diamond Head Crater on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
Ledge Trail is about 90 linear feet in length and extends from the existing Fire Control 
Bunker to the Lookout Landing. The new trail will consist of a reinforced concrete slab with 
a minimum width of 3 feet. The concrete slab will be supported on rock anchors. In 
addition, a new railing will be installed and embedded into the concrete slab along the 
length of the trail. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1. 
The specified rock anchors were 12 feet in length, with a minimum hole diameter of 
2.25 inches.  The anchor is composed of No. 10, Grade 75 Threaded Stainless Steel 
(SS316) anchors and neat cement or sand-cement grout with a minimum 28-day 
compressive strength of 4,000 psi. In addition, one sacrificial anchor was specified to verify 
anchor capacity.

A total of 27 production anchors were designed to support the new concrete slab 
and railing system. The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe 
the installation and testing of the rock anchors for general conformance with the project 
plans and specifications developed for the project. 
ROCK ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Rock Anchor Installation 
Pre-Production and production rock anchor installation was performed by Hi-Tech 
Rockfall Construction.  A total of 1 pre-production anchor and 27 production anchors, 
designated as Anchor Nos. 1 through 27 were installed for the project. The approximate 
rock anchor locations are shown on the Rock Anchor Identification Location Plan, Plate 2. 
As part of the contract requirements, a pre-production rock anchor, designated as 
Pre-Production Anchor No. 1 was installed and tested prior to the production rock anchor 
installation to observe the contractor’s installation method and verify the rock anchor 
capacity. Because the installation of the pre-production anchor was not observed by 
Geolabs, Inc., the anchor was not accepted. Another pre-production anchor was installed 
on October 17, 2011 and tested on October 25, 2011. The anchor was converted from a 
pre-production anchor to a production anchor. This rock anchor is designated as 
Anchor No. 27. 
The drilling equipment used for installing the rock anchors was a Toyo CR60 air 
hand drill equipped with a 2.5-inch diameter drill bit. The rock anchors were installed from 
October 17 to 27, 2011. The rock anchors were drilled vertically to lengths of about 
12 to 12.7 feet below the existing ground surface. After the hole was drilled to the specific 
depth, the hole was cleaned by blowing out the cuttings and loose material in the drill 
holes. A No. 10, Grade 75 SS316 threaded bar was installed in the drilled hole. 
Two schedule 40 PVC centralizers were placed along each rock anchor, with one placed 
about 1 foot from the bottom and the other placed about 2 feet from the top of the 
threaded bar. 
A ½-inch diameter rubber grouting tube was installed along the reinforcing bar for 
grouting purposes. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top through the 
grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting tube was 
removed after the completion of the grouting operation. Quikrete commercial grade 
non-shrink precision grout was used with water cement ratios ranging between 0.17 and 
0.28. 
It should be noted that Anchor No. 17 was relocated 3 inches closer to the 
centerline due to its close proximity with the edge of the slope. Also, the installation of 
Anchor No. 25 was not observed by Geolabs, Inc. due to late notice by the contractor.   
A summary of the rock anchor installation is provided in the Summary of Rock 
Anchor Installation and Testing, Plates 3.1 and 3.2.  In addition, the rock anchor 
installation records are provided on Plates 4.1 though 4.27.

Rock Anchor Testing 
In accordance with the project documents, the pre-production rock anchor was 
subjected to a performance pullout test while the production rock anchors were subjected 
to proof testing. Due to the narrow working conditions and space limitations, four of the 
27 production rock anchors, designated as Anchor Nos. 16, 17, 18, and 20, were not proof 
tested. The anchor testing was performed by Hi-Tech Rockfall Construction.  
In general, the preproduction and production rock anchors were loaded to 
133 percent of the design load (14.25 kips), which is equivalent to a maximum test load of 
about 18.9 kips. The anchor head movements at each load increment up to the maximum 
test load were measured and recorded.  
Based on our observations, the pre-production rock anchor subjected to 
performance test and production anchors subjected to proof testing met the acceptance 
criteria specified in the project specifications, consisting of the following: 
The measured elastic movement shall be greater than 80 percent of the 
theoretical elastic elongation of the unbonded length at the maximum test 
load. 
The creep rate per log cycle shall be less than 0.040 inch between the 1 and 
10-minute readings or 0.080 inch between the 6 and 60-minute readings.  
The results of the rock anchor testing are provided in the rock anchor test data 
sheets, Plates 5.1 through 5.23.   
Selected photographs taken during rock anchor installation and testing are 
presented on Plates 6.1 and 6.2. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some of the work for the rock 
anchor installation and testing may have occurred without our direct observation.
