Revised Recommendation on Sheetpile Penetration Depth
Shoreline Protection to Substation W 5
FY92 MCON P 351
Electrical Distribution System Improvements
Naval Inactive Ship Maintenance Facility
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
Based on our field observation and discussion with the contractor it appeared that
the contractor has encountered some difficulty in achieving the recommended penetration
depth which was developed at the time of soils report preparation during the current
project sheetpile driving operation for the above referenced project
According to our preliminary estimate as presented in our soils report for the
subject project the steel sheetpile wall may need to be driven to a minimum of 45 feet
below the existing ground surface However current our field observation of the on going
sheetpile driving operation indicated that it appears a significantly stiffer clayey deposit
was encountered than originally anticipated from our field exploration results This very
stiff clayey deposit has limited the penetration of the sheetpile wall as a result of an
increase in frictional resistance between the sheetpile wall and the surrounding soils
Based on our revised geotechnical engineering analysis using the updated soil
parameters it is our opinion that the penetration depth for the sheetpile wall may be
reduced to a minimum of 28 feet below the existing ground surface which corresponds
to a tip elevation of 88 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW level per U S Navy Datum
We also recommend that the project structural engineer be consulted pertaining to
comments on the structural aspects of the sheetpile wall with our revised shallower
sheetpile embedment depth
