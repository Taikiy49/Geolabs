SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Various Structures at Ocean Pointe Golf Course project in the
Ewa Beach area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of building foundations
slabs on grade and site preparation only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project includes various support structures at the Ocean Pointe Golf Course
facility to supplement the Hoakalei Country Club in Ewa Beach on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The new developments generally consist of low rise support buildings including
a maintenance building comfort stations irrigation well sites and an irrigation pump
building The proposed building locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Structural
details were not available at the time this report was prepared
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a soil rock data set to formulate geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the design of building foundations slabs on grade
and site preparation for the proposed support buildings In order to accomplish this we
conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the
island The younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene Ice Age in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the
island As volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows from Koolau Volcano
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau known as Schofield
Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast These sediments accumulated and
interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a
sedimentary wedge This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining
formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition
of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present

materials

SECTION 2 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by fills
on the order of about 1 to 7 feet thick consisting of dense to very dense coralline gravel
and sand Below the surface soils coral formation was encountered and extended to
the maximum depth explored of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The coral formation generally appeared to be moderately weathered and soft to
hard in nature It should be noted that the strength characteristics of coral formation are
highly variable The depositional nature of the formation may create localized areas of
hard crystalline coral intermixed with soft severely fractured detritus It should be noted
that coral formation is frequently cavernous or contain pockets of voids Although
cavities were not encountered in our drilled borings the potential for the presence of
cavities and or voids exists at the project site
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
tides seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors In addition it
should be noted that water seepage is commonly encountered in coral formations due
to surface water infiltration

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered surface fills overlying coral formation
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Since cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in coral
formations we believe that the primary geotechnical considerations for the proposed
construction will include the following 1 probing and grouting for potential cavities
and or sinkholes beneath the building foundations and 2 proof rolling of future slab
subgrades to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities sinkholes or loose
zones
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used
to support the new structures and other improvements Because of the light loads of the
one to two story buildings we believe that probing and grouting at the foundation
locations may be omitted In lieu of probing and grouting the foundation locations we
recommend proof rolling the ground surface finished mass graded elevations with a
vibratory drum roller minimum 10 tons static weight to aid in detecting and collapsing
near surface cavities and or voids over the cut subgrades The proof rolling operations
should be conducted on the finished subgrades in the cut areas and the ground surface
in fill areas in the presence of a Geolabs representative
Detailed discussions and recommendations pertaining to these items are further
presented in the following sections herein
3 1 Building Foundations
Based on the design concept and the subsoil conditions encountered at the site
we believe that spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils
or new compacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be

For footings bearing on the in situ stiff dense soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to
provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing
steel or concrete Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation may be backfilled with on site soils compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the
underlying competent materials For footings bearing on new compacted fills the
bottom of the footing excavations should also be recompacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades Footings adjacent to the planned retaining walls should
be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the
retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional structural loads
Foundations next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a
45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench
or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement

footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills as

3 2 Foundation Probing Optional
Our experience suggests that cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in
coral formation A program of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation locations will
greatly reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the
cavities and or sinkholes below foundations However because of the light loads of the
buildings our opinion is that foundation probing program may be omitted for this project
due to the reduced risk
If a foundation probing program is desired we recommend drilling probe holes at
each spread footing location one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing area
and at 10 feet on center along wall foundations The probe holes should be at least
3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of
foundation elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the
probing operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in
delineating the vertical and lateral extent of the cavities and or voids The probe holes
and cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to
moderate pressures We recommend utilizing a low strength sand cement grout with a
slump range of 6 to 9 inches for the grouting operations In lieu of the sand cement
grout fluid lean concrete such as controlled low strength material flowable fill may
also be used for the grouting operation

3 3 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the floors of the new
structures For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we
recommend providing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill below the slabs for
uniform support The cushion fill should consist of open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation and also would serve as a capillary moisture break Where slabs are
subjected to vehicular traffic or sustained vibrations a minimum of 6 inches of
compacted aggregate base course should be provided below the slabs in lieu of the
4 inch thick cushion fill layer The aggregate base course layer should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
To reduce the potential for excess moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel
cushion layer Flexible floor coverings should be considered above the floor slab
because they can better mask minor slab cracking
In addition we envision that exterior concrete walkways and exterior flatwork
may be required In general we recommend providing a minimum 4 inch thick cushion
layer of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation below the exterior concrete
slab As an alternative the exterior concrete slab may be constructed directly on the
compacted structural fill subgrade provided that the subgrade is properly re compacted
To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the slabs crack control joints
should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways or slabs with
expansion joints at right angle intersections

3 4 Site Preparation
Based on the grading plan provided we anticipate that the majority of the site
preparation work will consist of minor cuts and or fills on the order of about 2 feet thick
to achieve the design finish grades As previously mentioned the project site may be
underlain by cavities and or sinkholes near the existing ground surface Therefore
special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation organic debris construction debris
deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the
excavated materials with the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be disposed properly off site
Finished subgrades in both cut and fill areas from the mass grading should be
proof rolled with a vibratory drum roller 10 ton minimum static weight a minimum of
six passes to provide a dense and unyielding surface for construction of the buildings
The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a Geolabs
representative Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during the clearing and
proof rolling operations should be over excavated to expose dense materials and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill materials Due to
uncertainties in the over excavation and backfill quantities contract documents should
include unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
Fill materials required for the project construction should consist of
non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral and basalt The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of one percent or less when tested in accordance with

A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort on the backfill thus reducing the possibility of pipe damage It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids
below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement could result in backfill settlement and pipe
damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils or select
granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts
and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas
the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the new buildings should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the buildings and to divert discharge away from the
foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
backfilled properly against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface runoff away
from the slab and footing foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling eight borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 8 each extending to depths ranging from about
15 2 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck mounted
drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
