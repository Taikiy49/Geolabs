performed for the Radford Terrace portion of the Hawaii Regional Housing Privatization

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano
ceased lava flows from Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope
forming a broad plateau now known as Schofield Plateau
The Radford Terrace project site is located on the south side of the Aliamanu
vent and on the southwestern flank of the Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with
the groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions which
deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form tuff formation
locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid down when the
sea level was higher than the present level The Aliamanu eruptive event deposited its
material over alluvium originating from Halawa Stream After this phase of volcanic
activity ceased sedimentary deposits accumulated over the Aliamanu tuff before the
next phase of volcanic activity began Then the Salt Lake eruptions began when the
sea level was at about 40 to 60 feet below its present level The Salt Lake eruptive
event deposited its material over earlier tuffs from the older Aliamanu events
Lagoonal deposits and alluvial deposits associated with Moanalua Stream have
since accumulated along the eastern areas of the project site Subsequently
man made developments in the last century have brought the site to the existing
conditions

3 2 Settlement Monitoring Program
For a deposit of compressible soils such as the alluvial and lagoonal deposits
encountered at the project site we believe that the estimated settlement period could
vary by as much as 50 to 100 percent from the actual settlement period required
Therefore the actual settlement rate should be monitored and provisions should be
made for potential delays in the project construction schedule if a longer period is
required

settlement gauges should be established on the existing ground surface prior to the
filling operations A baseline elevation of the gauges should be established by two
surveyed readings taken a minimum of 24 hours apart Subsequently readings should
be taken once a week until the settlement rate has stabilized

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the housing project site were explored by drilling
and sampling eight borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 8 The borings were
advanced to maximum depths of about 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers and coring tools The approximate locations of the borings drilled for the project
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in drilled
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket
penetrometer tests performed on selected samples are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and
description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Six One Inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized
on Plate B 1
Four Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected
sample of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on
Plate B 3
Three laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed
on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The samples were remoided to near the optimum moisture
content and saturated Results of the tests are presented on Plates B 4 through B 6
Three Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 7 through B 9
Two Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on selected
rock core sample to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the rock formation
encountered Results of the unconfined compression test are presented on Piate B 10
