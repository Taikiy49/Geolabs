Dear Mr. Placencia: 
As requested, Geolabs, Inc. conducted a static compressive load test on a 
dedicated drilled shaft, designated as Test Shaft No. 1 (TS-1), for the Kailua RWWTP 
Tunnel Influent Pump Station and Headworks Facility project on July 7, 2016. This letter 
report presents the results of our field observations and the test data obtained during 
the drilled shaft load test. 
The compressive load test was conducted on a 30-inch diameter drilled shaft 
(designated as Test Shaft No. 1), and the approximate location of the test shaft is 
shown on the Test Shaft Location Plan, Plate 1. The ground elevation during the test 
shaft installation was estimated at about +11.5 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The test 
shaft was excavated to a tip elevation of about -65.2 feet MSL using a Watson 3100 drill 
rig equipped with a 30-inch diameter auger. After augering to near the tip elevation, 
approximately 80 feet of 30-inch diameter temporary steel casing was installed using a 
vibratory hammer followed by removal of the spoils from within the casing. Reinforcing 
steel and concrete was placed for the test shaft using a concrete pump discharging via 
a tremie pipe on June 20, 2016.  
It should be noted that the reinforcing cage dropped about 11 feet below the 
ground surface while the temporary steel casing was removed from the drilled hole. A 
concrete form was used to bring the concrete level to approximately 2.8 feet above the 
existing grade to provide a platform for the load testing apparatus. During the test shaft 
installation, a Geotechnical Quality Control Specialist from our office maintained a 
record of our field observations, which is presented on Plates 2.1 through 2.4. 
The test shaft was instrumented with 10 vibrating wire embedment strain gages 
(Model No. VCE-4200) manufactured by Geokon. The embedment strain gages were

A multiplexer was used to connect the 10 embedment strain gages to a Geokon 
Micro-10 datalogger, which recorded the strains in the embedment strain gages during 
the compressive load test. During extraction of the temporary casing and the 
subsequent drop of the rebar cage, Strain Gage Nos. SG-1, SG-3, SG-5, SG-7, SG-9, 
and SG-10 appear to have been damaged and resulted in erroneous readings. 
Therefore, the results from these strain gages have been excluded from our analysis. 
The test shaft was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143/1143M 
Procedure A – Quick Test procedure. Test Shaft No. 1 (TS-1) was loaded to a 
maximum test load of 240 kips in approximately 6-kip load increments. Each load 
increment was held for at least 8 minutes, at which point, the rate of the test shaft head 
movement was stable. In addition, the maximum test load was maintained for at least 
1 hour to evaluate the effects of creep. Subsequently, the test load was unloaded in 
five decrements, and each unload decrement was held for about 4 minutes. Two dial 
gages supported on two reference beams installed on opposite sides of the test shaft 
and a survey level set at the center of the shaft were used to measure the shaft head 
displacement. The load test readings and the load-displacement curve are presented on 
Plates 3 and 4, respectively. The compressive load test results are summarized as 
follows.

Based on the measured displacements of the compressive load test, the 30-inch 
diameter test shaft did not fail at the maximum test load of 240 kips. Strain changes 
from the four functioning embedment strain gages were recorded using a Micro-10 
datalogger during the compressive load test. Based on tests conducted on the day of 
the load test, the unconfined compressive strength (f’c) of the test shaft concrete was 
reported at 7,000 psi. Using the ACI formula (Ec = 57,000 √f’c), the elastic modulus of 
the shaft concrete was estimated at 4,769,000 psi or 4,769 ksi. Using a shaft stiffness 
(AE) of 3,371,000 kips, the interpreted load transfer is presented graphically on Plate 5. 
In addition, the mobilized net side shear versus displacement curves based on the 
available strain gage data and estimated shaft stiffness are presented on Plate 6.  
A printout of the strain gage readings is presented in the Appendix. It should be 
noted that calibration records for the load cell and dial gages used in the load test have 
been submitted previously under separate cover prior to conducting the load test.
