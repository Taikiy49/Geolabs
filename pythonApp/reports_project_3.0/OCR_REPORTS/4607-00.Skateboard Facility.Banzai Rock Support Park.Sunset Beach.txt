In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Skateboard Facility at the Banzai Rock
Support Park on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from
our field exploration at the project site and presents our findings and recommendations for
the proposed project These findings and discussions are intended for design input only and
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Banzai Rock Support Park at Sunset Beach in the
Pupukea area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct a skateboard facility
consisting of a Portland cement concrete paved skating area The skating area will have
plan dimensions of approximately 120 feet by 70 feet as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The skating area will be set at approximately Elevation 21 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with
the low areas set at approximately Elevation 16 feet MSL Based on the available grading
plans the skating area will consist of cuts and fills of about 5 feet or less
In addition a parking area and driveway are planned as part of the project We
envision that the parking area and driveway will be constructed of asphaltic concrete
Exterior concrete walkways are planned from the parking area to the skating area

of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of slabs on grade site grading and pavements
only To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following
tasks and work efforts

at Sunset Beach in the Pupukea area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Banzai Rock

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 10 3 to 20 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled at the site are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered a layer of alluvial soil deposits consisting
of stiff to very hard silty clays with varying amounts of sand and gravel extending to depths
of about 5 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface The alluvial soil deposits were
underlain by coralline detritus consisting of dense to very dense silty coralline gravel and
sand extending to depths of about 10 to 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
coralline detritus was underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation extending to the
maximum depths explored of about 10 3 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
basalt formation appeared to be moderately weathered Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered from our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7
Groundwater was encountered in Boring No 2 at a depth of about 12 9 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration However groundwater levels are
subject to change due to seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors

Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the new skateboard
facility To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from shrinking and swelling of the general fills and or on site soils due to changes
in the moisture content we recommend that a 12 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase
course be provided below the slabs The 12 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase course
should also extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond the limits of the slabs The aggregate
subbase should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The aggregate subbase course should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Site Preparation subsection of this letter report The aggregate subbase course and the
underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or that the subgrade soils be prepared
again Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement
with well compacted fill
We understand that some exterior concrete walkways will be required for the
proposed project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches
of aggregate subbase course The aggregate subbase course also should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints provided at right angle
intersections
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slabs should
be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slabs to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials within
the grading limits should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft loose
and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill
or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense material
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities
Slabs and foundations of the existing structure to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with well compacted fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned within
the skate park footprint and or limits of the proposed improvements should be
removed The resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill materials
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch
loose level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled
by pumping lean concrete or CLSM Controlled Low Strength Material under low
pressure
After clearing and grubbing finished subgrades and areas to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction in areas subjected to vehicular traffic Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and prepared
again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed

Fill Material and Placement
The on site soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of general
fill material up to the bottom of the aggregate subbase course provided that
deleterious materials vegetation are removed and over sized materials 3 inches in
maximum dimension are screened Imported general fill materials should consist of
materials with particle sizes of 3 inches or less in maximum dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR greater than 8 and a swell
potential of less than 2 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course materials required under the
concrete slabs and pavement areas should meet the requirements of Subsections
703 17 and 703 06 respectively of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road
Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 HSS Imported fill materials should
be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for the intended use
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase
and aggregate base course materials required under the concrete slabs and
pavements should be placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement of the aggregate
subbase and aggregate base course materials should be increased to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction for the pavement areas
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction
should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of
acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be
allowed to compact the fill materials

Pavements
We understand that a driveway and parking area are planned for the project We
anticipate that the driveway and parking area will consist of asphaltic concrete In general
we anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger
vehicles and light pick up trucks Based on our field exploration silty clay soils were
encountered at the parking area We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming
the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the silty clay materials CBR value of 4 and an
expansion value of about 5 8 percent encountered in our borings Based on the above
assumptions we recommend that the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course
materials should meet the requirements of Subsections 703 17 and 703 06 respectively of
the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994
HSS The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course for pavements should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the recommended pavement design sections
Field density tests should be performed on the compacted subgrades to confirm the
compaction requirements of the subgrades The recommended pavement sections assume
that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements

Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration into the subsurface In addition
drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain
surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs
