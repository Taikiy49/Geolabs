Evaluation and Discussions
In our geotechnical engineering report prepared for the subdivision we have
addressed the importance of the prevention of water ponding adjacent to the house
foundations To reduce the potential for water ponding against the house foundations the
lot grades were designed to carry surface water runoff away from the building foundations
Ponding of water against foundations sidewalks and or driveways should be prevented
Based on our field observations it appears that the house structures and foundations are
subjected to the following sources of water
e Water runoff from the roof is collected by a gutter system installed at
Lot No 160 However the collected water is discharged directly onto the
area adjacent to the house slab and foundation
Water runoff from the roof is discharged directly over the eaves onto the
area around the house structure at Lot No 161
Irrigation water from the landscaping adjacent to the exterior of the house
structures
These potential sources of water will infiltrate flow toward and pond against the
house as a result of the following existing features observed at the site
e The areas around the house structures appear to consist of a porous gravel
strip which allows the roof discharge and other water sources to infiltrate
into the foundation soils
The existing grades of the landscaped yard appear to be higher around the
perimeter of the property relative to the house structure The negative
drainage established by the raised grades of the yard adjacent to the house
structure will divert water toward the house slab and foundation
e The lack of well defined drainage swales at the properties for carrying
surface water runoff away from the houses It appears that the drainage
swales established during the project construction have apparently been
removed during the landscaping work at the properties
Based on our field observations and the above discussions it appears that part of
the observed distresses may be attributed to moisture infiltration into the subsurface soils
The water infiltration or excess moisture once trapped in the subsoils along the perimeter
of the house structures are not able to evaporate as a result of the protective cover
provided by the concrete house slab Due to the clayey nature of the subsoils at the
project site the clayey soils exhibit swelling and softening upon wetting and likely resulted
in lifting of the edges of the perimeter building walls causing some of the observed
distresses described above

Preliminary Recommendations
In order to reduce the potential for the observed distress from worsening and or
recurring we recommend that positive drainage away from the house structures be
re established at the residences This will help to reduce the potential for swelling and
loss of bearing strength of the underlying clayey soils due to saturation Therefore we
recommend that the following measures be implemented at these lots as soon as
possible preferably prior to the cosmetic repairs of the existing house distresses
1 The finished grades of the yard areas outside the house structures should
be regraded and sloped to shed water away from the house foundations It
is Critical to re establish positive drainage away from the house structures at
the lots in order to reduce the potential for ponding around the house
structures and foundations
2 A perimeter thickened edge sidewalk 12 to 18 inches deep in lieu of a
porous gravel strip should be considered to reduce the potential for water
infiltration toward the house structures The sidewalk slabs should be
sloped to shed water away from the house foundations and should not
dowel into the building walls
Well defined drainage swales should be re established and maintained to
drain surface water runoff away from the house slab and _ footing
foundations Blockage or clogging of these drainage features could cause
ponding of water at the house structure and foundations resulting in further
potential foundation and house distress
4 Gutter systems should be installed around the building and the discharge of
water should be diverted away from the foundation discharging to an
appropriate outlet
5 Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils In
addition care should be exercised by the homeowner with the use of
automatic sprinkler systems since there is a potential for over watering with
the system left unattended
These drainage requirements and recommendations are essential for the proper
performance of the foundation support system since ponded water could cause
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The clayey soils in
the Ewa Plain area are susceptible to heaving or loss of strength when saturated These
