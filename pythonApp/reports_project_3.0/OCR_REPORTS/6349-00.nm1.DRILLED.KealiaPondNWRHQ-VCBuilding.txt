As required by the County of Maui and the project specifications we provided
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services Item No 11 of the
Special Inspection Form during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the above
project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during
the drilled shaft foundation installation for the Kealia Pond National Wildlife Refuge HQ VC
Building project in the Kihei area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of our work was to provide engineering observations in support of the
Special Inspection Team for Section 1704 of the project s Specifications Division
titled Quality Control More specifically we provided Special Inspection services for
Item No 1704 9 Pier Foundations by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation
which included the drilling operations and placement of concrete at the Kealia Pond NWR
HQ VC Building project site for general conformance with the project plans and
specifications
Project Considerations
The project generally involves the construction of a single story slab on grade
building structure for use as a headquarters and visitor center at the Kealia Pond National
Wildlife Refuge The new building foundation consisted of a combination of spread footing
and deep foundation systems with an embedment requirement of 7 feet minimum below
the footing elevations This embedment of the deep foundation was installed as a drilled
shaft foundation system The drilled shafts consisted of 30 inch diameter holes drilled to
depths of 5 6 to 8 4 feet below the existing ground surface

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of shallow surface
fills and residual soils overlying highly weathered to fresh basalt rock formation The
surface soil materials were previously placed by the general contractor Bodell
Construction and generally consisted of imported quarry select granular fill The residual
material generally consisted of medium stiff to very stiff sandy silt silt with sand soils
The surface fills and residual soils extend to depths of about 1 to 7 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Underlying the residual soils is highly weathered rock formation
This material generally consisted of hard dense clayey silts and silty sand material that
occur as veins and pockets within a moderately to highly weathered basalt rock formation
The depth from ground surface of the highly weathered rock formation varied from 1 to
4 5 feet The weathered rock material progressively decreases in weathering with depth
and the rock formation progressively increases in hardness until a very dense hard fresh
basaltic rock formation was encountered The depths to rock formation varied from 1 6 to
6 0 feet from the existing ground surface at Shaft through DS 16 We did not
encounter rock at Shaft and DS 2
Summary of Production Drilled Shaft Installation
The drilled shaft contractor installed a total of 16 production drilled shafts for the
building foundation A Geolabs representative observed the production drilled shaft
installation which commenced on and completed on October 18
2010 The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Location
Plan Plate 2 The as built drilled shaft logs are presented on the Drilled Shaft Records
excavation placement of rebar cage and concrete operations during the installation of the
production
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using an Altec HDT
were drilled to the full depth of 7 1 to 7 5 feet with the 30 inch auger as no rock formation
was encountered As drilling continued on the remaining shafts DS 3 through DS 16 the
auger encountered hard rock formation and refusal at depths ranging from 1 6 to 6 0 feet
from the existing ground surface at which point the drilling contractor notified the general
contractor that rock formation had been encountered The drilling contractor switched to a
rotary core drilling method with a 30 inch diameter core barrel and continued to drill the
shafts until the final tip elevation was achieved After the drilling excavation had reached
full depth the contractor was unable to remove the cored rock section with the barrel This
resulted with several shafts still containing the rock core plug Therefore the shafts were
later excavated and cleaned out to full depth with the use of pneumatic rock jack hammers

and hand cleaning tools to provide a relatively flat and clean bottom of shaft free of loose

After excavation and cleanout of the production shafts were completed the
rebar column cages were lowered into the drilled holes with the use of a boom truck to the
appropriate depth below the existing ground surface and use of 6 inch diameter wheel
centralizers for sidewall clearance and cement dobey blocks to elevate the cages above
the bottom of shaft by 3 inches After the rebar cages were installed in the drilled hole
concrete was placed by the tremmie method and pump truck During concrete placement
we observed that the shaft hole remained clear of sidewall material cave in or debris
Concrete with slump ranging from about 3 25 inches for Shaft and DS 2 to
6 25 inches was used for the drilled shafts The concrete arrived on the project site with a
slump of approximately 4 inches Then the slump was increased by adding Gelinium a
super plasticizer which increases the flowability of the concrete without the addition of
water The addition of the plasticizer into the concrete truck was monitoring by the
project s special inspector Richard Miyahira It should be noted that the concrete placed in
the drilled shafts was tested in accordance with the project specifications as well as
standard industry practices for the performance of slump temperature and compressive
strength tests
Based on our field representatives observation our professional opinion is that the
production drilled shafts supporting the building structure for the Building
project were installed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
