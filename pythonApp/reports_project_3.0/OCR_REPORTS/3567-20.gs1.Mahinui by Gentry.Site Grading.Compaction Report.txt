This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction for
the Mahinui by Gentry project from to in accordance with our
fee proposal dated and additional budget request dated August 1 2013
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mahinui by Gentry site is in Kaneohe within the District of Koolaupoko on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is north of Mahinui Road and east of Apuakea
Street A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1 and 2 1 and 2 2
respectively
The project involves mass and site grading 18 house pads for a single family
residential development on a parcel that is approximately 3 65 acres In addition the
construction of infrastructure including a roadway and utilities was performed The site
grading work involved cuts and fills up to about 13 feet thick
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction at the project site
MASS AND PAD GRADING AND UTILITY AND ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION
We observed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction work
for the Mahinui by Gentry project from to The mass and site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on
the order of up to about 13 feet thick to achieve the finished grades Royal Contracting
performed the mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction work During
the mass and site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials

The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 22 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass and site grading and utility and roadway
construction work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the

grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
