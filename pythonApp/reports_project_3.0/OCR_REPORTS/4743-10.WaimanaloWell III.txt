PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Waimanalo Wells III project is located in the Waimanalo area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is planned to
install a 12 inch diameter transmission main and appurtenances from the Waimanalo
Well Ill to an existing water main In addition a reservoir tank and control building are
planned at the well site
The transmission main will be divided into three sections The first section
Waterline A will begin near the Waimanalo 230 Reservoir and extend about 1 380 feet
to the east The second section Waterline B will continue from the termination point of
Waterline A and will extend to the east about 500 feet It will bend to the south and
continue for about 900 feet Then it will bend to the northeast and continue for about
900 feet Finally it will bend to the east and extend about 450 feet The third section
Waterline C will continue from the termination point of Waterline B and will extend to
the east about 950 feet Then it will bend to the south and continue for about 1 400 feet
where it will terminate at the proposed control building and reservoir tank area

The reservoir tank will be a 20 000 gallon rectangular shaped concrete structure
The control building will be a single story structure consisting of concrete masonry unit

Road C is also planned We understand that gravel surfaced pavements flexible

constructed for the new access road
Earthwork Operations
Geolabs Inc observed the earthwork operations from through
on an on call intermittent basis The work consisted of subgrade
preparation and placement and compaction of fill and backfill materials to achieve the
design finished grades During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and some
of the imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents of the on site soils and some of the imported material were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The maximum
dry density and optimum moisture content values of the base course and select borrow
from Ameron Hawaii s Kapaa Quarry were determined from the tests performed by
Ameron Hawaii The Summary of Optimum Moisture Maximum Dry Density Relationships
is presented on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 as appropriate
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill and
backfill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the grading operations It should be noted that
waterline backfilling operations were not observed and field density tests on the
compacted backfill material for the waterline were not performed

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
