SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A potential rockfall hazard evaluation was conducted for the proposed Palehua
East B Subdivision in an effort to study the potential rockfall hazards present at the project
site Our site reconnaissance and mapping effort consisted of identifying potential rockfall
hazards located on the hillside in support of the preparation of a rockfall hazard mitigation
plan to reduce the risks associated with potential rockfall on the development located
downslope of the potential rockfall source areas of the project site
Based on our site reconnaissance and mapping effort potential rockfall hazards
involving imminently hazardous conditions which would require immediate remedial
attention were not encountered Based on the mapping effort we identified approximately
300 potential rockfall hazards at the project site The potential rockfall hazards consist of
loose surface boulders and erosional remnant rock outcroppings Due to the dense
vegetation coverage encountered at the project site we estimate that there may be about
100 to 200 additional potential rockfall hazards located on the hillside which may have
been missed by the boulder mapping effort The potential rockfall hazards identified at the
project site consist of surface boulders and remnant basalt rock outcroppings which were
observed protruding from the surrounding ground surface
Based on our evaluation of the existing site conditions and our computer
simulation modeling of the potential rockfall activity we recommend the construction of a
rockfall impact barrier along the upper property boundary of the project site just downslope
of the existing hilltop homes neighboring properties It is our professional opinion that the
location and the design capacity of the rockfall barrier recommended herein should be
capable of containing the potential rockfall hazards located in the backyard areas of these
existing homes In addition we recommend that the remaining hillside area located
downslope from the proposed rockfall barrier be swept of existing surface boulders and
surface rock outcroppings which may present future rockfall hazards We believe that the
combination of an upper slope rockfall barrier and the removal of potential rockfall hazards
from the remaining lower hillside is a viable remedial alternative which maximizes the use
of the hillside for landscaped open space and maximizes the usable area for the future
downslope subdivision house lots

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two main
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older shield volcano Waianae Volcano is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene Epoch in age and forms the bulk of the western
third of the island The younger shield volcano Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene Epoch to early Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age in age and forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island The Waianae Volcano became extinct while the
Koolau Volcano remained active Therefore the older Waianae Volcano s eastern flank
was partially buried below the younger Koolau Volcano lavas that banked against the
Waianae Volcano s eastern flank
The end of volcanic activity associated with the Koolau Volcano was followed by
a long period of landmass erosion whereby deep stream valleys were carved into the
dome shaped Koolau Shield Volcano The erosional valleys grew larger by the
processes of stream erosion and mass wasting landslides and rockfall emanating from
the steepened valley sidewalls The deeper valley floors were filled with accumulations
of alluvial sediments and colluvial landslide materials as the island eroded and slowly
depressed beneath the weight of the new landmass
Approximately 850 000 to years ago some limited volcanic activity
resumed with the eruption of the Honolulu Volcanic Series from secondary vents
scattered across the eastern portion of the Island of Oahu The eruptions occurred
along the established secondary rift zones of the Koolau Volcano Some of the most
notable landmarks located on the Island of Oahu such as Punchbowl and Diamond
Head Craters were created by the eruption of the Honolulu Volcanic Series

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field mapping and analyses we do not believe that there are
rockfall hazards present at the project site that are in immediate need of emergency
mitigation However due to the significant number of surface rock boulders and
fractured rock outcroppings located on the hillside we recommend that a rockfall hazard
mitigation plan be implemented to provide some level of protection for the downslope
development from the potential rockfall hazards located at the hillside site
We understand that rockfall protection for downslope development is a significant
consideration for this project however we also understand that the location and visual
appearance of the proposed rockfall protection scheme is an important consideration for
the design of the rockfall mitigation In addition we understand that encumbrance of
land to be developed as future house sites should be considered and kept as minimal
as practical without impeding the effectiveness of the rockfall protection system As
mentioned previously it remains very difficult if not impossible for engineers and
geologists to predict when rockfall activity may occur as many variables have an effect
on the generation of rockfall events
Based on our evaluation of the existing site conditions and other project
considerations we believe that the rockfall hazard mitigation scheme for this project
should consist of two parts The first part should consist of the construction of a rockfall
impact barrier located along the upslope property boundary The second part should
consist of the performance of a hillside boulder sweep encompassing the sloping terrain
located below the proposed rockfall impact barrier By combining the two remedial
measures the potential hazards related to possible rolling boulders affecting downslope
development and roadways should be effectively reduced It should be noted that there
are no guarantees in the professional engineering and architectural design fields with
respect to potential rockfall hazards However the finished work rockfall impact barrier
when combined with a boulder sweep downslope of the rockfall barrier as described in
this report will provide a high level of safety against rockfall hazards for the Palehua
East B Subdivision based on past applications of similar mitigation methods

3 1 Rockfall Impact Barrier
As mentioned previously surface rocks including boulders and_ rock
outcroppings located within the backyard areas of the existing upslope homes
neighboring properties may pose potential rockfall hazards to Makakilo Drive and
other downslope development Because it is generally undesirable to disturb the

loose from the hillside with the passage of time It is desired to place the rockfall barrier
as close as practical to the upslope property boundary so that the responsibility of rock

maintenance of the proposed landscaping

3 2 Hillside Boulder Sweep
The second part of the proposed rockfall mitigation scheme consists of
performing a boulder sweep on the sloping hillside terrain outlined on the Rockfall
Mitigation Plan Plate 4 The area identified for the boulder sweep generally is located
adjacent to and upslope of Makakilo Drive and several proposed house lots Lots 1
through 9 encompassed by the Phase Palehua East B Subdivision project The area
encompassed by the proposed boulder sweep encompasses 4 12 acres of land located
upslope from the proposed project grading limits
The area currently resides as a natural hillside region that is densely vegetated
by tall grass shrubbery and scattered trees The thick vegetation growing on the
slopes generally obscures most of the surface boulders and rock outcrops residing on
the ground surface The purpose of the boulder sweep is to demolish and remove from
the project site the precariously positioned existing surface boulders and surface rock
outcroppings located on the hillside The surface rock removal is necessary to reduce
the potential of the exposed rocks to come loose and roll down the hillside

Based on our site reconnaissance the rock features identified by our boulder

the existing homes
