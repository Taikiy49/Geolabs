INTRODUCTION
This report presents geotechnical data from our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the Seawall at Hickam Harbor project at Hickam Air Force
Base on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report provides subsurface information in the project vicinity derived from
our field exploration and laboratory testing The subsurface information presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

Harbor area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is south of Worchester

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which
time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a
series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred
to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a complex series of
depositional and erosional events due to glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch The Koolau Volcanic
Shield was built during the early Pleistocene Epoch by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial soil materials were
deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the area of Pearl Harbor The sea
level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet below and rising about 100 feet
above the present sea level
During the Recent Epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with additional sediments consisting primarily of soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits Land development and reclamation projects in the past 40 to 60 years have
added man made fills bringing the site to its present configuration
SOIL SAMPLING AND FIELD TESTING
A total of four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extended to
depths of about 24 to 29 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment with continuous
flight augers Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual
and textural examination in the field and observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and laboratory testing Soils were classified in general conformance with
the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A 0 1 of Appendix A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered in the drilled borings are presented on the
Logs of Borings and Corings Plates A 1 through A 4 of Appendix A

Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
A pocket penetrometer test was performed on a selected cohesive soil sample
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample The pocket penetrometer test
reading is presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depth
LABORATORY TESTING
Laboratory testing was performed on selected soil samples obtained during the
field exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their engineering
properties The tests performed are described below
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate B 1
Four Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation test results are provided on Plate B 2
Four Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Direct shear test results are presented
on Plates B 3 through B 6
