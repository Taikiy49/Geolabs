INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed to evaluate the damages at Avenue on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration site
observations and geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended for
construction estimation or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided to us and visual observations the
subject dwelling has suffered damages from distresses due to settlements
of the Ewa and Mauka end walls Signs of distress include 1 a vertical
crack under the kitchen window 2 the kitchen counter is not level and
slopes toward the Ewa end wall and 3 the end wall has pulled away from
the second floor beam by about one fourth inch
It is our understanding that this unit had experienced distress
previously and the Ewa end wall was underpinned with the addition of a
concrete footing in 1977 It is our understanding that the structural
engineer has determined that there is no imminent concern of the
structural integrity at this moment
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the repair project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Tropic
Gardens I Association of Apartment Owners c o Ferguson Properties Inc
dated The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Drilling and sampling three to 42 inches deep
inside the unit below the existing slab and seven 7
continuous penetration probes 1 to 6 feet deep outside of the
building structure
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

exploration to determine the engineering properties and

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject dwelling at Avenue is a unit of the Tropic
Garden I townhouse condominium development located in the southeastern
part of Oahu The unit is bounded by parking lot No 5 to the north an
adjoining apartment to the east and sidewalk areas to the west and south
A landscape area is present between the unit and the sidewalk areas The
terrain of the area is level with some gentle slopes

Subsoil Conditions
In general the borings inside the unit encountered a 4 5 inch thick
concrete slab and a 6 inch cushion layer overlying dark brown clay with
sand in a slightly moist condition The probes encountered stiff to very
stiff subsoils along the Ewa end wall location

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
A hand level survey was conducted within the unit at the time of our
field exploration to measure the relative elevations of the floor slab
The hand level survey confirmed the visual observations that the Ewa end
wall was about one 1 inch lower than the interior unit wall This
elevation difference could be due to heaving of the interior slab or
settlement of the exterior Ewa end wall
The field exploration and laboratory testing generally indicated that
the clay subgrade soils below the existing concrete slab can experience
high expansion if they are saturated However the clay soils below the
slab were found to be only slightly moist not saturated Therefore it
does not seem that the foundation movements are a result of saturation and
heaving of the clay soils

The probes encountered stiff to very stiff soils which indicated that

It is our understanding that the Ewa end wall was underpinned with
the addition of a concrete footing sometime after 1977 However we are
not aware of the quality of the construction and the degree of disturbance
to the existing foundation system during construction
We believe that the performance of the end wall would depend heavily
on the quality of the underpinning construction In particular the
contact area between the old foundation and the underpinning portion would
play an important role since small gaps or discontinuities may form if the
underpinning concrete was not placed tight against the existing wall
Settlements could develop due to the imperfect contact of the old and new
foundation systems However it is our opinion that this type of movement
should come to an equilibrium eventually
Based on our observation and exploration results we feel that the
majority of the settlements may have occurred and cosmetic repair of the
distressed areas may be adopted Major improvement of the slab foundation
does not appear practical at this stage
To verify if significant future settlements are occurring we
recommend that the structure be properly monitored for signs of additional
settlements structural distresses propagation of existing cracks etc
Inexpensive crack monitors are available for this type of monitoring
should unexpected additional settlements occur causing structural damages
the geotechnical engineer should be consulted for the causes and remedies

It should be noted that our laboratory test results indicated that
the slab subgrade clay soils may have a high swelling potential if allowed
to become saturated Therefore it is recommended that the drainage
system around the unit be properly maintained and divert discharge of
water away from slab and foundation areas Excess watering in the
landscape area should also be avoided
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
project This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings and probes
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
sometime in the future If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings and probes were approximately
determined by taping from structures indicated on the floor plan provided
by Ferguson Properties Inc The physical locations of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
