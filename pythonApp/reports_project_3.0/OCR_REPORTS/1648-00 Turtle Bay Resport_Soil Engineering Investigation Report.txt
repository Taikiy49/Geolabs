present the coral reefs grew to elevations above present sea level The
emerged coral reef fonnations encountered at the project site have been

SUbsurface Soil Conditions
OUr test borings revealed a 4 5 to 7 5 foot thick surface layer of
stiff clayey silt which is underlain by coralline reefrock The strength
and character of the reefrock varies with depth from a dense gravel
approximate depth 5 to 13 feet to hard coralline reef rock which
contained small cavities or voids approximate depth 13 to 40 feet to a
loose to medium dense sand and gravel sized material approximate depth 40
feet to bottom of hole The depths of the bottom of the holes ranged
from 44 5 to 61 5 feet
The consistency of the hard coralline reefrock layer also varied
laterally from one boring to the next The rock from this layer from the
two borings which were cored by the same method and are horizontally
separated by 60 feet ranged from massive well cemented hard to
moderately hard rock with some voids in B 14 to medium dense sand and
gravel sized coralline fragments in B 16 In B 15 this layer was sarrpled
with a split spoon sarrpler The resulting sand and gravel sized fragments
may well have been a consequence of crushing of the existing massive rock
by this sarrpling method This crushing inte rpretation is supported by the
high Standard Penetration values up to 28 blows for o 2 feet in this

The consistancy of the coralline reefrock layer varies laterally
from a hard massive rock to medium dense sand and gravel as was discussed
in the SUbsurface Soil Conditions section Given the variable nature of
the coralline reefrock and the need to excavate to a depth 20 feet below
the existing groundwater level it is anticipated that some special
procedures for the dewatering and shoring may be required for the site
construction
Dewatering the excavation site is considered necessary for the safe
and efficient construction of the pumping station Due to the depth of
excavation and the anticipated large volume of water a deep well
dewatering system with wells located around the perimeter of the
excavation site is recommended The deep well pumps should be placed at
mininum depths of about 10 to 15 feet below the excavation floor Since
sheetpiles cannot be driven into the hard coral reefrock either a grout
curtain or drilled in soldier piles with timber lagging may be required
for support of the excavation walls
Dewatering
Excavations below the groundwater table require groundwater control
to pennit construction in the dry and maintain the stability of the
excavation floor and walls This is accomplished by controlling
seepage into the excavation and controlling artesian water pressure
below the bottom of the excavation

The groundwater level was encountered at an approximate depth of 13
feet in this area therefore dewatering is reconnnended for the pump
station excavation which will extend about 35 feet below the
existing ground surface Due to the high penneability of the
subsoils the depth to the water table and the proposed depth to the
excavation floor we consider a deep well system which is installed
deeper than the excavation floor to be an effective method to
dewater the excavation A deep well system should be capable of
pumping relatively large volmnes of water
Placing the deep
dewatering wells around the perimeter of the excavation is
considered to contribute to a less congested excavation site
Typically a deep well dewatering system consists of a series of
wells installed in holes which have been drilled to a sufficient
diameter to accornodate a pipe column and filter and depths that
will allow the necessary drawdowns
The wells are screened to prevent clogging by loosened fragments
and a filter pack is placed around the screen to prevent migration
of sand into the well and improve well efficiency
Pumping is accomplished using either submersible or turbine pumps
The above discussion presents a possible dewatering system which lila Y
be used The actual method of dewatering should be left open to the
contractor provided adequate stability of the excavation is
II1aintained
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines pumping clear water only It should be coordinated
with the excavation support system so that the stability of the
operation is not jeopardized
The operations should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening the excavation
bottom

Coral fonnations often contain voids or cavities which can transmit
tremendous quantities of water into the excavation
If these voids
or cavities are present it may not be practical to dewater the
excavation with nonnal pumping methods therefore provisions should
be made to include subsurface grouting where very porous zones or
cavities are encountered
To create a relatively impenneable barrier between the excavation
which is to be dewatered and the surrounding groundwater a grout
curtain may be constructed surrounding the excavation site
An
advantage of a grout curtain which is a non removeable structure
is the increased strength of the subsurface soil both during and
preceeding construction
Ihe grout injection holes should be drilled to a depth below the
excavation floor in a pattern selected by the contractor as best
suited to the variable penneability of the reefrock the particular
grout chemistiy and set ti mes being employed A corrplete positively
grouted cutoff is often difficult and costly to attain and
therefore it is recommended to only grout the porous zones to
reduce the water inflow

It is anticipated that some of the reefrock underlying the site is
hard enough that hoe rams and perhaps blasting will be needed for
efficient excavation
Ihe anticipated depth of excavation is 33 to
35 feet below the existing ground surface which will provide an
excavation floor at an approximate elevation of 20 feet
Some
difficulty and special provisions are anticipated due to the need to
excavate approximately 20 feet below the existing groundwater level
in combination with the variable consistency and character of the
subsurface coralline reefrock
