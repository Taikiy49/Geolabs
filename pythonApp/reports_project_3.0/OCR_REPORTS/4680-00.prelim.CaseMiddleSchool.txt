Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling 23 borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 23 extending to depths ranging from about 8 5 to
47 feet below the existing ground surface
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain
by surface fill materials overlying saprolitic materials at greater depths The majority of the
materials encountered consisted of medium dense to dense silty sands and silty gravel as
well as stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays Varying amounts of cobbles and boulders
as well as basalt rock ledges and or formation were encountered at varying depths and in
localized areas across the project site
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However our experience in the area suggests that subterranean seepage is
common in the area due to the sloping terrain and the rocky subsurface conditions at the site
It should be noted that groundwater levels are also subject to change due to rainfall time of
year seasonal precipitation surface water runoff storm surge conditions and other factors

Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by variable fill
materials overlying saprolitic soils with cobbles boulders and basalt rock ledges embedded
at varying depths across the project site To reduce the potential for differential movements
and cracking of the slabs resulting from differing subgrade materials we recommend that the
subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with select granular fill The select granular
fill capping fill material should extend to a depth of at least 12 inches below the slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a
minimum of 24 inches
Our laboratory test results indicated that the clayey soils encountered at the site
exhibit moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade we recommend
that provisions be made during construction to increase the capping fill layer underneath the
slabs on grade from 12 to 18 or 24 inches in areas where the highly expansive clayey soils
are exposed at the slab subgrade level
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are properly prepared The prepared capping fill and the underlying subgrade
soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of concrete Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be
thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation
of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
For the building slabs we recommend that the building slabs on grades be reinforced
with reinforcing bars spaced at 18 inches in each direction in lieu of the standard welded
wire fabric This recommendation is provided to reduce the potential for structural distress
due to differential movements of the slabs due to the expansive nature of the on site soils
Due to the sloping terrain and the potential for seepage conditions at the project site
we recommend that underdrains be installed under the building slabs on grade excluding
the deck areas outside the building to relieve the potential for build up of subterranean
seepage water The underdrains should consist of 4 inch diameter perforated pipes
wrapped in a non woven filter fabric with perforations placed facing down The underdrains
should be spaced at a maximum of 40 foot intervals and daylighted into appropriate drainage
structures for proper discharge A sketch of the Typical Underdrain Detail is presented on
Plate 1 of this letter

optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and

at least 90 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase and base course

placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Cut and Fill Slopes
Permanent cut or fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or
flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into
the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill
slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio
to obtain a well compacted slope face It should be noted that materials greater than
6 inches in largest dimension should not be exposed at the fill slope face
In the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements
should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver
fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting
should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for significant erosion
of the finished slopes
