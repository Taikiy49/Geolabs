INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils and geologic inves
tigation for the proposed sewer line along Ewa West Loch Access
Road in The alignment is located between
Barbers Point Naval Air Station and Pearl Harbor as shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
The purposes of this investigation was to evaluate the geologic
and soil conditions along the alignment and to provide recommen
dations for the design and construction of the proposed sewer
line

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed sewer line begins at the site of the future sewage
treatment plant located at Barbers Point NAS and continues for
12 600 feet in a westerly direction along Ewa West Loch Access
Road The sewer line will be a gravity flow 84 inch Reinforced
Concrete Pipe with its invert from 13 to 20 feet below the
existing grade The alignment of the line is shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
SURFACE
The alignment runs parallel to an existing paved road that
traverses through sugar cane fields The alignment surface is
fairly level A concrete lined ditch and box culvert is
located on the Ewa end of the proposed alignment
GEOLOGY
The Ewa Plain was formed during the Pleistocene Period by the
process of coral reef formation and alluvial plain formation
Stream sediments were washed into the Pearl Harbor embayment
from the Waianae and Koolau Volcanic Ranges as the region sank
and were deposited in layers and voids as the reef was forming
Page 3
W O 445 00
A later lowering of the sea level or elevating of the land has
left the reef exposed
SOILS
The site is immediately underlain by inter bedded coral and
clay The clay was encountered in our borings up to four feet
in thickness The moderately hard to soft coral algal reef
was found to the depth of our borings but generally extends
down to 50 feet or more
The encountered clay may be in layers or pukas However
because of the variability of reef environments over small
distances it would be difficult to predict the extent and
thickness of the clay
The surface reef material are relatively hard due to the
redeposition of calcium carbonate
No groundwater was encountered in any of our borings or is it
anticipated to be found above El 3 which is below the
presently planned invert elevation The groundwater level
along the alignment should be subjected to tidal influences

LOADS
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of exca
vation unit weight of backfill material and trench walls
If the ditch is made too wide the pipe may be subjected to
excessive vertical load We therefore recommend that the
trench width be kept to a minimum The distance between the
pipe and trench walls generally should not exceed one fourth
of the pipe diameter The Trench and Bedding Diagram is
illustrated on Plate 2 The engineering properties of the
site soils presented in the Appendix can be used for deter
mining loads on the conduit
A coefficient of friction between the fill material and trench
walls of the ditch can be assumed to be 0 7
Live loads due to traffic loadings or any other concentrated
loads should also be considered in the design
Manholes should be designed to resist an active lateral pressure
developed by a fluid weighing 35 pounds per cubic foot

W O 445 00
BEARING CAPACITIES
The relatively hard coral which was encountered at the site
will provide good supporting capacity A value of 4000 pounds
per square foot can be used for bearing capacities However
the existance of pukas could create differential settlement
problems If empty pukas are encountered at trench bottoms
they should be filled with a granular material and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum capacity If the puka is
filled with coral material this material should also be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum density for at least
12 inches below the trench invert
Based on the above value total and differential settlements
will be less than one quarter inch
BEDDING
Bedding conditions will effect the distribution of stresses
beneath the pipe and its load carrying capacity The contact
between the pipe and foundation material is therefore an
important design consideration
We recommend that a Class B bedding be used to support the
pipe This can be achieved by two methods
1 Shaped Bottom with Tamped Backfill The bottom of the
Page 6
W O 445 00
trench excavation should be shaped to a cylindrical surface
with a radius 2 inches greater than the radius to the out
side of the pipe and sufficient to allow 60 percent of the
width of the pipe to be bedded in a sand fill placed in
the shaped excavation Carefully compacted backfill shall
be placed at the sides of the pipe to a thickness of at
least 12 inches above the top of the pipe It may be
difficult to shape the bottom of the trench due to the hard
coral
2 Compacted Granular Bedding with Tamped Backfill The
pipe should be bedded in compacted fine granular material
placed on a flat trench bottom The bedding should have
a minimum thickness of 6 inches under the conduit The
granular bedding should have a width of one fourth the
outside pipe diameter on each side and should extend halfway
up the pipe at the sides See Plate 2 The remainder of
the side fills should be filled with compacted material
CONSTRUCTION
EXCAVATION
The maximum depth of excavation for this project will be approx
imately 20 feet and no groundwater conditions are anticipated

Construction methods and equipment used for trench excavation
should be left up to the Contractor within practical limits
The coral algal reef encountered in our borings will contain
local hard areas that may require hard ripping blasting or
jack hammering
Groundwater was not encountered in any of our borings above
the invert elevation and should not be a problem during con
struction
SHEETING AND BRACING
It is our opinion that the trench side walls will generally
stand vertically to the proposed excavation depths for short
periods provided no surcharge loads from construction traffic
or stockpiled materials are placed within 10 feet of the
excavation The excavation should be provided with bracing
in accordance with all applicable safety requirements The
design and safety of the sheeting should be the responsibility
of the Contractor
Because the coral reef is highly variable thick stratum or
pockets of silty clay may be encountered Local sloughing may
occur at sand filled pukas or at thick strata of silty clay
These conditions may require alternate trench stability

BACKFILL
Backfill material around the conduit should consist of selected
free draining well graded material and should be placed with
care by hand or suitable equipment so that the pipe will not
be displaced or damaged Backfill should be compacted by
jetting or mechanical equipment to a density consistent with
design parameters We recommend a minimum of 90 percent of
maximum density
The onsite materials are generally suitable as backfill material
provided organic materials debris and other objectional
material is not included in the backfill Rock greater than
12 inches in diameter should not be allowed in the backfill

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
Subsurface soil conditions along the alignment were explored
by drilling six test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary
auger equipmént The depth of the borings ranged from 25 to
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface Locations of
the borings are shown on Plate 1 in the text of this report
The Boring Logs are presented in Plates A 1A to A 1F in this
appendix
In all borings Standard Penetration Test SPT samples were
taken at selected depths The SPT samples was driven with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12
inches were recorded and are plotted at the appropriate depths
on the boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified
in the field The classifications were verified by inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
