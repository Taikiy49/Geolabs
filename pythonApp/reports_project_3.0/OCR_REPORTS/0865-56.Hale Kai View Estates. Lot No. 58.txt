As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three
3 borings within the proposed building site performing laboratory
soil tests and reviewing of the grading and compaction test record on
the building site This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage
will be set back about 10 feet from Kahena Street

Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire house and driveway
Minimal fill is anticipated for the lot development
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 58 is located in the southern portion of the subdivision develop
ment on the eastern side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded by
Kahena Street on the south subdivision lots on the north south and
west An existing concrete drainage ditch is located on the south
western boundary of the lot
The lot generally slopes upward from Kahena Street towards the west at
about 1 5 to 5 percent gradient The elevation of the lot varies from
approximately 129 to 135 feet
Minimal site grading was required for this lot during the recent
subdivision grading operation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on this material
indicated that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot
58 the slab on grade and foundation construction would require
special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect
of the subsoils
House Foundation
The laboratory tests indicate that the on site soils have high expan
sion potential To reduce the expansive effects of the clay soils we
recommend that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material
be placed below the house and garage concrete slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
The expansive clay below and for a distance of 3 feet around the slabs
should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below the outside
finished grade and replaced with compacted non expansive off site
borrow material such as quarry waste or select borrow The under
lying remaining clay soil should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content and recompacted moderately 85 compaction
prior to backfilling with a layer of 3 foot capping select fill
Normal slab on grade construction with a 12 inch deep thickened edge
can then be utilized for the garage
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel
base course
In order to reduce the above specified over excavation and replacement
type of house construction be considered
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive
soil Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a

minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished ground surface The
footings should be poured in place and backfilled to form an imper
vious layer over the foundations
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff existing ground or compacted select material
The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose
soils or debris
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition
near the slab area Also the owner should be made aware that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the perimeter footings and
walkways
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
For concrete driveway and sidewalk recommendations provided for
concrete slab on grade should be followed However since the
driveway and sidewalk could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet All
subgrade should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII

Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil Over excavation beyond the edge of
the walkway may not be necessary
Site Grading
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
clay soil should not be used for fill material below structures and
behind retaining walls
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and debris
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earth
work construction particularly the fill placement to monitor the
contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
