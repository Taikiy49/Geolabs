SAMPLE STORAGE 
Samples will be stored while field investigations and laboratory testing are on-going, and while 
associated geotechnical data and engineering reports are completed and accepted by the City.  
Samples will be stored until written consent and/or release is obtained from the City prior to 
transfer and/or disposal of any stored samples. Samples will be stored in our laboratory and 
warehouse storage facilities and will be readily available for the project team and HART. Both 
facilities are enclosed, safe, secure, and suitable for maintaining a controlled environment to 
protect stored samples from the elements and unauthorized access.  
Soil Samples 
Soil samples collected from split-spoon standard penetration samplers, split-spoon modified 
California samplers, and thin-walled tube samplers will be stored in designated areas within our 
soil laboratory until completion of testing as shown on Plate 1 – Laboratory Sample Storage 
Layout. The soil laboratory is located at 94-429 Koaki Street, Suite 200, Waipahu, HI 96797. 
Thin-walled tube samples, brass liners, and ring samples will be stored in a vertical position 
until tested. Samples brought into the laboratory will be reviewed and checked against the 
boring log for any labeling or description inconsistencies. Following the sample review, 
laboratory tests will be assigned by the project engineer, and laboratory testing will be 
performed. Upon completion of laboratory testing, extra samples that were not tested will be 
moved to storage racks within our warehouse storage facility, as shown on Plate 2 – 
Warehouse Sample Storage Layout.  
Core Samples  
Core Samples will be stored in HART-approved core boxes at our warehouse storage facility on 
storage racks, as shown on Plate 2 - Warehouse Sample Storage Layout.  A storage area within 
the facility will be designated strictly for Honolulu Rail Transit Project (HRTP) core boxes.  Our 
warehouse storage facility is located at 94-429 Koaki Street, Suite 200, Waipahu, HI 96797. 
The outside of individual core boxes will be clearly marked with the boring number, date, and 
starting and ending depths.  Core boxes will be stacked on our storage shelves and limited in 
height so that it is stable and secured against sliding or collapse.  Aisles between storage racks 
will be kept clean and clear to allow access to the samples at all times.  
Sample Index 
Geolabs will record and maintain sample storage records as shown on Plate 3 – Laboratory Soil 
Sample Storage Record (temporary storage until completion of testing), Plate 4 – Warehouse 
Soil Sample Storage Record (unused samples after completion of testing), and Plate 5 – 
Warehouse Core Sample Storage Record. Sample storage records will be maintained within the 
sample storage areas.
