INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new Weight Room Facility of the Waipahu High School in the
Waipahu District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The field exploration was
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2800 00 Page 2
performed on in accordance with our fee proposal dated November
4 1991
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on data obtained
during the field expioration and laboratory testing The recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction
cost estimates nor for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new Weight Room Facility within the Waipahu High
School Campus on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is our understanding that the new Weight Room Facility will be a single story
concrete structure with CMU walls and concrete roof The New Weight Room Facility will
be approximately sixty two 62 feet by forty 40 feet in plan dimension An estimated
continuous wall footing load of 4 1 kips per lineal foot is anticipated
Due to the existing level topography it is anticipated that site grading will be limited
to that required to prepare the foundation and slab on grade bearing surfaces and to
provide positive drainage
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design
of the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the Waipahu High School Campus north of the
existing gymnasium The project site is to the south of an existing physical education
classroom building
The site is bounded on the east by an existing concrete walkway To the west of
the site is an existing portable classroom building
At the time of our field exploration the site was occupied by two 2 additional
portable classroom structures The areas outside the classroom structures are essentially
level and covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 borings to
depths of thirty one and one half 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface within the
proposed project boundary The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered uniform soil conditions consisting of very stiff
to hard brown silty clay to clayey silt to the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the field exploration

Logs Plates A 1 and A 2 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It appears that the very stiff to hard in situ clayey soil
encountered at the project site should provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed structure
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition Therefore a minimum
one 1 foot thick non expansive capping layer is recommended underneath the four 4
inch cushion fill recommended to be placed under the concrete slabs on grade
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
in situ stiff clayey soil A minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade is recommended Uplift wind seismic or other loading
conditions may require that this minimum footing embedment be increased
The recommended bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Footing settlements under the design loadings are anticipated to be less than one
half inch total and one quarter 4 inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2800 00 Page 6
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed building may be provided by
passive soil pressures and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations A frictional coefficient of
0 4 may be used to estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings
and the in situ soils
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce potential footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the strength and to reduce the
detrimental effects that may result due to the swelling characteristics of the soils around
the foundation
Slab On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed new Weight Room
Facility
A minimum four 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
cushion fill should be provided below the slabs In order to protect against moisture
infiltration a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slabs A one 1 inch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2800 00 Page 7
thick moist fine sand is recommended on top of the plastic vapor barrier to help concrete
curing
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a minimum one 1 foot thick layer of
non expansive capping material be placed below the four 4 inch cushion fill layer
The non expansive capping material should consist of off site borrow materials
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral and should be placed in horizontal loose
level lifts up to a maximum of ten 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
underlying clayey soil remaining should be moistened by water sprinkling and then
maintained in a moist condition until the capping material is placed
The thickened edge of the slabs can bear on the compacted or stiff in situ soil
The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade
Concrete Walkway
A covered pedestrian concrete walkway is planned for the new Weight Room
Facility The one 1 foot thick capping layer of non expansive materials previously
recommended underneath the concrete slab on grade should also be followed for this
walkway Since the walkway slab can tolerate some amount of relative movements the
non expansive capping layer could be eliminated to reduce construction costs if some
slab cracking is tolerable
A minimum six 6 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the walkways

Joints are recommended at close intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow
for some relative movements of the concrete
Site Preparation
Due to the generally level topography of the project site it is anticipated that site
grading will be limited to that required for preparation of the foundation and slab on grade
bearing surfaces and to provide positive drainage
At the on set of construction the area within the construction limits should be
cleared of vegetation rubbish and other deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and
spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
The exposed subgrade after the recommended one 1 foot over excavation for
provision of the recommended non expansive capping material should be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The prepared subgrade
should be kept moist until the capping material is placed
The non expansive capping material should consist of off site borrow materials
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral The capping material should be placed
in horizontal loose level lifts up to a maximum of ten 10 inches thick and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Drainage
The finished grades should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed and that
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2800 00 Page 9
the gutter run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations as feasible Ex
cessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during foundation construction of the project This is
to observe compliance with the recommendations design concepts or specifications and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different from those
assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should
be made

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report

taping from objects indicated on the preliminary Site Plan prepared by W H T Leong and
Associates Architects Inc dated The elevations of the field borings
were estimated from the topography site plan undated transmitted by J Brian Hughes
Associates Inc on The physical location and elevation of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two 2 borings to
depths of 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
