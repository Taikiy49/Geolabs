As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of three 3 borings
drilled within the proposed building site laboratory soil tests and
a review of the grading and compaction test record on the building
site This report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure approximately 28 to 51 feet in plan dimension with a 22 by
23 foot garage situated in front of the house Based on the
preliminary plans provided to us the garage will be set back about 15
feet from Kahena Street

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway
QMU retaining walls are planned around the southeastern and
southwestern side of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 9 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of the
subdivision development about 550 feet northeast of Ainapo Street
It is bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest subdivision lots on
the southeast and northwest and a concrete drainage channel on the
north The lot slopes downward from Kahena Street toward the east at
about 5 to 7 percent gradient The elevation of Lot 9 generally
varies from 151 to 165 feet The existing rectangular concrete
drainage channel is about 10 feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending across the
rear portion of the lot
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations most of Lot 9 was
covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from
previous grading operations in the area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay fill The laboratory test results on this material
generally indicated that they have high expansion potential
Therefore for Lot 9 the slab on grade and foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
The laboratory tests indicate that the on site soils have high expan
Sion potential To reduce the expansive effects of the clay soils we
recommend that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material
be placed below the house garage and lanai concrete slabs
In the garage areas where minimum site fill is required for lot
grading the expansive clay below and for a distance of 3 feet around
the slabs should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below the
outside finished grade and replaced with compacted non expansive
off site borrow material such as quarry waste or select borrow The
underlying remaining clay soil should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content and recompacted moderately 85 compaction
prior to backfilling with the 3 foot capping select fill

Normal slab on grade construction with a 12 inch deep thickened edge
can then be utilized for the garage
For the proposed residence 1 to 4 feet of fill is planned In the
portion of the residence where less than 3 feet of fill is required
the existing ground should be overexcavated for a depth of 3 feet
below the finished floor elevation and replaced with select
non expansive material such as quarry waste The underlying
expansive clay subgrade should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content then recompacted to 85 density prior to
backfilling Where more than 3 feet of fill is planned additional
overexcavation below the existing ground is not required The clay
subgrade soils must be kept saturated prior to fill or footing
concrete placement
The perimeter house retaining wall footing should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the outside finished grade to provide a
cut off wall to reduce future infiltration of outside moisture under
the slab area
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel
base course

In order to reduce the above specified overexcavation and replacement
type of house construction be considered
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive
soil Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished ground surface The
footings should be poured in place and backfilled to form an
impervious layer over the foundations
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff existing ground or compacted select material
The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose
soils or debris
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition
near the slab area Also the owner should be made aware that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the perimeter footings and
walkways

If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
For concrete driveway and sidewalk recommendations provided for
concrete slab on grade should be followed However since the
driveway and sidewalk could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet All
subgrade should be kept moist prior to concreting
Site Grading
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
clay soil should not be used for fill material below structures and
behind retaining walls
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and debris
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earth
work construction particularly the fill placement to monitor the
contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
