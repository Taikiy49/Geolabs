INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Papa a Bay Ranch 50 000 gallon tank well booster pump project on the
Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 50 000 gallon steel tank with a concrete floor well
pump pad booster pump pad and access road at the project site Based on the
information provided the approximately 25 foot diameter steel water tank will be situated
on or near the existing ground surface with minimal excavation An asphalt concrete paved
road will be constructed to provide access to the site and surrounding the proposed tank
structure
The finished floor elevation for the new water tank structure is set at approximately
198 67 feet Mean Sea Level MSL to match the existing 50 000 gallon water tank
elevation Based on the project plans the existing ground surface elevations at the
proposed tank site vary from about 197 to 199 feet MSL Therefore we anticipate that
only minor site grading will be needed for the construction of the proposed tank and
associated supporting structures

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is believed to be composed of a single large shield volcano built
by the extrusion of basalt lava belonging to the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series In general
the lavas erupted over a long period of time during the Tertiary Pliocene Epoch more than
2 million years before present A long period of voicanic inactivity combined with deep
erosion of the existing land surface occurred that was then followed by renewed volcanic
activity with the extrusion of basaltic lava belonging to the younger Koloa Volcanic Series
along with concurrent deposition of alluvial sediments of the Palikea Formation The age of
the Koloa Volcanic Series is believed to be early Quaternary Pleistocene Epoch in time
Basalt formation belonging to the younger Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the
ground surface of the eastern half of the Island of Kauai including the Anahola area The
rock formations are characterized as generally thick lava flows of dense basalt extruded from
vents aligned as north south and northeast southwest rifts Associated with the Koloa vents
are pyroclastic materials such as cinder and volcanic ash which typically form isolated
cones central to the vent center
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were multiple sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to fill
the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose
because more water became available The land mass elevation of Kauai remained
essentially stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These
glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea that were both higher and lower
relative to the present sea level
The higher and lower fluctuating sea level stands created depositional and erosional
environments at varying locations and periods of time The results of sea level fluctuation
included the formation of river deltas and alluvial fans composed of terrigenous sediments
at the heads of the old bays and the accumulation of reef deposits at correspondingly higher
and lower ground surface elevations In addition the varying sea levels reacted to repeated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4274 00 Page 5
floods and exposed broad bay areas which led to cycles of deposition and erosion of marine
sediments and coral deposition in the quiet waters protected by fringing barrier reefs
The project area is composed of basalt rock built by the extrusion of lava of the Koloa
Volcanic Series The rocks are generally characterized by dense flows of jointed vesicular
basalt interbedded with thin clinker layers The tropical weathering process over thousands
of years has created a deep mantle of completely weathered rock defined as saprolite or
residual soil The residual soils grade to saprolite materials interbedded with highly
weathered rock with increasing depth In general saprolite materials are composed mainly
of silty and sandy materials and are typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The
saprolite materials gradually grade to less weathered basalt rock with increasing depth
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project site is located at the northeast corner of the intersection
between Kuhio Highway and Papa a Road in the Papa a Bay area on the Island of Kauai
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Papa a Bay is located further to the
east of the project site The existing Aliomanu Estates is located to the south of the Papa a
Bay Ranch development
The project site is accessed by a dirt trail starting from the existing narrow paved road
leading to Papa a Bay Ranch The dirt trail is relatively steep on the order of about
15 percent slope near the intersection with the narrow paved road The project site has
been partially cleared for the deep well installation and is partially vegetated with bushes and
trees At the time of our field exploration the deep well installation operations were on going
Based on our field observations and the Preliminary Site Plan provided by Tom Nance Water
Resource Engineering the project site is relatively level with ground surface elevations
between about 197 and 199 feet MSL

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating and sampling two
test pits extending to a depth of approximately 10 feet below the existing ground surface
using a standard backhoe provided by the owner The information obtained was used to
evaluate the subsurface conditions for design of the proposed project The approximate
locations of the two test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered reddish to dark brown medium stiff to stiff
silty clays extending to the maximum explored depth of approximately 10 feet below the
existing ground surface The materials encountered represent the residual soils typically
encountered in this area Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our
field exploration However it should be noted that groundwater conditions at the site could
change due to variations in rainfall groundwater withdrawal construction activities and or
other factors not apparent at the time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the test pits are presented on
the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 and A 2 Laboratory tests were performed on selected
samples obtained and the results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by medium stiff
to stiff silty clays extending from the surface to the maximum depth explored of about 10 feet
below the existing ground level Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the
project site we believe that shallow foundations consisting of a concrete pad with
thickened edge footings or a perimeter ring footing bearing on 6 inches of compacted select
borrow subbase may be used for support of the proposed tank structure The select borrow
subbase should extend beyond the edges of the tank structure a minimum of 12 inches
In addition to the tank structure we also envision that concrete slabs on grade will be
used for support of the other proposed structures such as the well pump pad and booster
pump pad To provide for uniform support of the concrete pads we recommend that the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4274 00 Page 7
concrete pad be supported on 6 inches of select borrow subbase In addition the subgrade
soils below the select borrow subbase should be properly prepared and kept moist prior to
placement of the select borrow subbase Detailed discussion of these items and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of a concrete pad with thickened edge footings or a perimeter
ring footing bearing on 6 inches of compacted select borrow subbase may be used for
support of the proposed tank structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for the design of the thickened edge footings or perimeter
ring footings bearing on 6 inches of compacted select borrow subbase This bearing value
is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such
as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The select borrow subbase below the concrete pad and footings should be compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction In addition the select borrow subbase layer
should extend beyond a minimum of 12 inches laterally beyond the edges of the tank
structure Due to the relatively high moisture content of the silty clay subgrade the subgrade
soils below the select borrow subbase should be proof rolled or compacted to provide a
relative firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of the select borrow subbase
material If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the select borrow
subbase elevation they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm material
The over excavation should be backfilled with on site soils up to the bottom of the select
borrow subbase elevation compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4274 00 Page 8
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement
is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements would be on the order of about 1 inch
We anticipate the differential settlements across the concrete pads to be on the order of
about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used for foundations bearing on the compacted select borrow
subbase Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depth
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed tank floor
well pad and booster pump pad To provide uniform support of the concrete slab we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the slab concrete be properly prepared and
capped with at least 6 inches of select borrow subbase The select borrow subbase should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4274 00 Page 9
slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used for the compacted select borrow subbase
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Thickened edges that are used
as footings to support structural loads should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade as recommended in the Foundations section of this report
Areas adjacent to the slab edges should be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively
impervious soils on site soils and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the design finished floor elevation of the
proposed tank structure we anticipate that the majority of the site grading work will consist
of minor cuts and fills on the order of 1 to 2 feet to attain the design finished grades at the
site At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated to
expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of
off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas beneath the future concrete pads should be
over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 6 inch thick compacted select
borrow subbase below the slab concrete We envision that the existing silty clay subgrade
soils exist in a relatively moist condition Therefore we recommend that the very moist
subgrade soils be proof rolled or compacted to obtain a firm and unyielding subgrade prior
to placement of the select borrow subbase If shrinkage cracks are visible at the silty clay
subgrade level the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4274 00 Page 10
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In areas where the finished grades will be
subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pavement areas the compaction requirement
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the subgrade we recommend
that the soil be prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
The select borrow subbase material recommended below the concrete pads should
conform to Section 30 Select Borrow for Subbase Course of the Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction 1986 If imported fill materials are needed at the site
imported fills should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral
mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR
value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill
material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Cut and fill slopes planned at the project site should be constructed at an inclination
of 2H 1V or flatter Fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point
and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers Water should be diverted away from
the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as the fill slopes are
completed to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes

Pavement Design
We understand that flexible pavements are planned for the access road leading to the
site and around the tank In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the proposed
project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles pick up trucks and occasional moderately
heavy trucks Based on the above assumptions and our laboratory tests we recommend
that the following pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils for the pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture compacted and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should consist
of crushed basalt aggregate compacted not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR
and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches
into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration
of landscape water into the pavement section

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines and utility connections may be required for the
proposed project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes for support Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also
be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material
to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in about
8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below
pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction It should be noted that the on
site soils exist in a relatively moist condition Additional compactive effort along with thinner
backfill lifts may be necessary in order to compact the on site soils to the specified
compaction requirements

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of this
test are summarized on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationships The results of the test are presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
