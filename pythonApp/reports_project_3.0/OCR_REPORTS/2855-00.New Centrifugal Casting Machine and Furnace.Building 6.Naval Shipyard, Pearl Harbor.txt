INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new centrifugal casting machine foundation and the proposed
W O 2855 00 Page 2
furnace foundation within Building 6 in the Naval Shipyard in Pearl Harbor on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration for the project These recommendations are intended for
design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided to us the proposed new centrifugal casting
machine foundation will be a 7 3 foot square mat footing approximately 7 0 feet in
thickness It will support a total load of approximately 30 000 pounds
The proposed furnace foundation will be a 14 0 by 10 0 foot rectangular mat
footing approximately 2 feet in thickness It will support a total load of 36 000 pounds
It is our understanding that the centrifugal casting equipment and furnace
foundations will be designed with no required uplift soil resistance for the soil surrounding
the equipment foundations
Generally no site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction of machine
foundations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the existing Foundry Building Building 6 in the
Naval Shipyard in Pearl Harbor
The new centrifugal casting equipment will be located between column lines 16 and
17 near the existing oven The new furnace will be located adjacent to the centrifugal
casting equipment and between column lines 15 and 16
The proposed equipment locations within the existing workshop are generally flat
and is covered with a 4 inch thick concrete slab

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the equipment sites were explored by drilling and
sampling two 2 borings to a depth of 15 feet beiow the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed that the equipment sites are underlain by a fill layer
consisting of loose silty fine sand with some gravel below a 4 inch thick concrete slab
The thickness of the fill layer varied from about 3 5 feet in Boring No 1 drilled at the
furnace location to about 4 feet in Boring No 2 drilled at the new centrifugal casting
machine location
Below the fill layer moderately hard to hard mudrock tuff was encountered to the
maximum drilled depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface in the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the new centrifugal casting machine and
furnace sites are generally underlain by a 4 0 inch thick concrete slab followed by a 3 5
to 4 0 foot thick loose sandy fill layer overlying a hard mudrock deposit
We recommend that the centrifugal casting and furnace mat foundations should
extend through the upper loose sandy fill layer and bear on the underlying competent
mudrock deposit
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2855 00 Page 6
Hard excavation may be anticipated for the foundation extending into the hard
mudrock deposit
Foundation Support
Based on information provided to us the bottoms of the centrifugal casting and
furnace mat foundations are anticipated to be placed at a depth about 7 0 and 4 0 feet
below the top of the existing concrete slab respectively It appears the bottoms of the
footings will likely be close to the top of or extending into the underlying dense mudrock
deposit encountered in the borings
Because of the potential for excessive settlement of the mat foundations bearing
on the upper loose fill layer and the relatively shallow depth to the competent mudrock
deposit the mat foundations should bear on the mudrock deposit encountered at about
3 5 to 4 0 feet below the top of the existing concrete slab
For footings bearing on the hard mudrock an allowable bearing value of about
5 000 p s f may be used
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of less than one half inch
total and one fourth inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the sandy soil around the
GEOLABS HAWAII

foundation is properly backfilled and compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between
the bottom of the footings and the mudrock bearing material may be used
Based on our field exploration hard excavation may be anticipated for excavation
of the hard mudrock encountered approximately four 4 feet below the top of the existing
concrete slab The contractors wishing to bid on this project should make their own
independent evaluation of the excavation conditions to be encountered to form the basis
of their bid
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade are planned for the car rail sections on the centrifugal
casting equipment and also adjacent to the new furnace
To reduce the settlement of the concrete slab bearing on the loose sandy fill layer
we recommend that the new slab s sandy subgrade soils be over excavated a minimum
depth of 24 inches and proof rolled to a firm subgrade at the over excavation bottom
The previously over excavated in situ sandy soils may then be reused to backfill the over
excavation However the backfill should be placed about 8 inch loose lifts each
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If additional imported material is required the material should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than one and one half 1 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should
be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the foundation construction of the project This
is to observe the over excavation and subsequent backfill in compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material if required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of two
2 borings to a depth of 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by truck mounted
rotary drill rigs advancing continuous flight augers or rotary and core drilling tools as
appropriate
The material encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
are shown on Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with ASTM
Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and
testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs s REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved form in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0
