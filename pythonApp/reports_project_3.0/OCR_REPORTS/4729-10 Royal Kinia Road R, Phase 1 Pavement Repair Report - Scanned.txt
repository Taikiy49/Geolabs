PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the northwest portion of the Royal Kunia
Development Area in the District of Kunia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The name of
Road R has been changed to Anoiki Street Several pavement distress areas were
observed along Anoiki Street near the intersection of Anoiki Street and Kakahi Street
Most of the distress areas were located in the southbound lanes of Anoiki Street There
appears to be three areas of noticeable distress and one area that has previously been
repaired A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
Based on the construction plans the existing Anoiki Street near Kakahi Street is a
56 foot wide roadway with existing top of pavement elevations ranging from about 425 to
434 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The design pavement section consisted of 2 inches of
asphalt concrete 8 inches of aggregate base course and up to 18 inches of select borrow
material depending on the soil test data
The pavement repair will consist of removing the existing asphalt concrete
scarifying and then re compacting the existing base course layer at three repair areas
The repair areas are about 37 feet 8 inches by 20 feet 10 feet by 8 feet and 160 feet by
40 feet in plan dimensions In addition several sidewalk slabs will be removed and
re constructed
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading during roadway and sidewalk reconstruction for the project

ROADWAY AND SIDEWALK RECONSTRUCTION
The site grading during roadway and sidewalk reconstruction work for the Royal
Kunia Road R Phase 1 Pavement Repair project was observed by Geolabs Inc from
to March 3 2003 The site grading work consisted of scarification and
re compaction of the existing base course layer within the roadway areas and
re compaction of the existing subgrade and placement and compaction of base course
within sidewalk areas to achieve the design finished grades for the project The roadway
and sidewalk reconstruction work were performed by Albert C Kobayashi Inc The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content of the base course material from
Makakilo Quarry was obtained from Grace Pacific Corporation and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the
base course material was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 of this
report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from existing structures These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Soft subgrade material was encountered in the northbound lanes of Anoiki Street
near the intersection of Anoiki Street and Kakahi Street The soft subgrade area
measured about 10 feet by 70 feet and extended about 2 feet below the finished subgrade
level The soft material was removed and replaced with compacted aggregate base
course material Also a soft subgrade area approximately 9 feet by 30 feet by 1 5 feet
deep was encountered in the southbound lanes of Anoiki Street near the intersection of
Anoiki Street and Kakahi Street The soft subgrade material was excavated and replaced
with compacted aggregate base course material
Subsequent to paving the northbound lanes of the repair area a crack on the top of
the new asphalt concrete pavement was noted in an area outside of the soft subgrade
area encountered previously The new asphalt pavement and base course material was
removed within an area about 11 feet by 26 feet Soft subgrade material extending to a
depth of about 4 inches below the finished subgrade level was encountered The soft
material was excavated and replaced with compacted aggregate base course material
