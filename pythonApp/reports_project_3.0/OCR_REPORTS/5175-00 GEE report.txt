PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Kauai Community College campus in Puhi on the
Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
The proposed project is a One Stop Center OSC for the Kauai Community
College The OSC will be a two story structure with approximately 40 000 square feet
accommodating several offices Details for the proposed structure were not available
during the preparation of this letter report

obtained was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical

grading and pavement design To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling four borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 20 5 and 21 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled at
the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered medium stiff to hard
residual soils consisting of silty clay extending to the maximum depths drilled of
approximately 20 5 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water run off and other factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we recommend
that shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and or continuous footings be used
to support the new building planned for the project An allowable bearing pressure of up to
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations of the new
structure bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills The bottoms of
the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grades To reduce the potential for future structural distress of the slabs on grade
resulting from moisture infiltration we recommend that the subgrade soils below concrete
slabs on grade be properly prepared and capped with select granular fill In general the
select granular fill should extend a minimum of 6 inches below the slab cushion Detailed
discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of building
foundations site grading and other geotechnical engineering aspects are presented in the
following sections of this letter report
Building Foundations
As previously mentioned we understand that it is planned to construct a two story
building at the project site Based on the relatively competent subsurface soil condition
encountered on the project site we recommend that the proposed structure be supported
on shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings An allowable
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
AM Partners Inc Page 5

bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for design of footings
bearing on well compacted structural fill or in situ soils The bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
those caused by wind and seismic forces The bottom of the footing should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm or dense materials The resulting
excavations should be backfilled with granular fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
the soils as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Foundations located adjacent to slopes or on sloping ground should be embedded
a sufficient depth to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 6 feet measured
from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope If foundations are located
next to utility trenches and easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal
to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench Alternatively the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
appreciable foundation settlements
If the foundation is designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements of the foundations bearing on the
well compacted structural fill or in situ soils to be less than 1 inch Differential settlements
between footings are estimated to be on the order of 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and bearing materials and by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 38 may be used for footings bearing on well compacted structural
fill or in situ soils Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf for footings
embedded in the well compacted structural fills or in situ soils This value assumes that
the concrete for footings is placed directly against the well compacted structural fill
materials or in situ soils Unless covered by pavement or slab the passive resistance in
the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Slabs On Grade
We envision that the new building structure will utilize slabs on grade Based on
our laboratory test results the on site clayey soils exhibited a slight expansion potential
when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future structural
distress resulting from swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that the slab
subgrade be properly prepared and capped with select granular fill
In general we recommend a minimum of 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill be provided below the building slabs The non expansive select granular fill
should be moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In addition we recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified to a depth of
8 inches and moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of the
select granular fill Optimum dry density refers to the moisture content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill material such as
open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide
uniform support for the slab To prevent the future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of
the gravel cushion layer
It should be emphasized that the areas adjacent to the slabs should be tightly
backfilled against the slab edges with low expansive relatively impervious soils such as
the on site soils These areas should also be graded to divert water away from the slabs
and to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs and foundations
Site Grading
Based on the existing site condition we envision that cut and fill may be required to
achieve the design finished grades for the project In general areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork
Vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities

prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require

Pavement Design
We envision that an access road and or parking lot may be constructed to facilitate
the proposed One Stop Center We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the access road
and parking lot may consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light trucks We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming that the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the clayey silt materials CBR value of 16 and an expansion value of about 0 2
percent encountered in our borings Based on the above assumptions we recommend
that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils under the pavement areas should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The
aggregate base course materials should consist of basalt aggregates and meet the
requirements of Section 703 06 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge
and Public Works Construction 1994 The aggregate base course should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR tests and or field observations
should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during construction to confirm that the
above design section is adequate The recommended pavement section assumes that
good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a
minimum of 2 inches below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for
migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system
is recommended to collect excess water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we believe that a subdrain system should be considered adjacent to
paved landscaped areas

Cut and Fill Slopes
Cut and fill slopes planned for the project should be constructed at an inclination of
2H 1V or flatter Fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point
and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the fill placement
recommendations presented above Excess water should be diverted away from the tops
of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as the cut and fill slopes are
completed to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Underground Utility Lines
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below new utility pipes planned at the
project site Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation
of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and placement could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils with a
maximum particle size of 6 inches The trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below building
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement surface should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
