PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on information provided the proposed project will consist of the construction
of two roads designated as Roads A and B respectively located within the Haina area
of Honokaa on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based
on the plans provided Road A will be approximately 1 200 feet in length while Road B
will be approximately 880 feet in length In general site grading will involve cuts up to about
10 feet deep and fills up to about 6 feet thick
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site we anticipate that the
project site is underlain by volcanic ash soils of varying thicknesses overlying weathered
basalt formation Based on our field reconnaissance and laboratory testing of the bulk soil
samples we have formulated recommendations for pavement design and site preparation
for the project

project site is located just east of Haina Access to the site is via Standard Oil Road or
Paauhau Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Portions of the two areas designated
for Roads A and B had been graded just prior to our site reconnaissance Two bulk

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field reconnaissance generally encountered brown sandy silt and silty sand
surface materials volcanic ash with varying amounts of gravel and cobbles Moderately to
severely weathered basalt rock outcrops were also noted along the proposed Road B The
thickness of the overlying soil mantle varies considerably across the project site In general
the soils appear to be thicker along the proposed Road A alignment than along the Road
B alignment The volcanic ash material generally has relatively low strength characteristics
and is highly susceptible to erosion in a dry and friable state Therefore special attention
should be given to the site grading and subgrade preparation for the subject project
Our laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests indicated that the near surface
ash soils within the project site generally have reasonable pavement support characteristics
Our experience with similar soils in the area suggests that the support characteristics of
these soils can vary widely It is difficult to visually distinguish volcanic ash soils with good
CBR values from those with poor pavement support characteristics Grading along the
proposed roadway alignments may expose in situ soils with lower CBR values
Based on our review of the grading plans cuts of up to about 10 feet deep are
anticipated along Road A from Station 7 00 to 10 50 The majority of the site grading for
both roadways are generally in a cut condition or near the existing ground Some fills of up
to about 6 feet thick will also be required for the roadway construction In general the
proposed cut and fill slopes along the proposed roadways may be constructed as shown on
the grading plans provided that the following recommendations are implemented during
construction Detailed geotechnical engineering recommendations are provided in the
following sections

We anticipate that saprolitic soils or basalt rock formation may be encountered in
certain areas especially deeper cut areas during the site grading operations Under such
conditions the 6 inch thick aggregate subbase may be eliminated depending on the exposed
soil rock conditions Therefore we recommend that the proposed roadway construction be
observed and tested by a representative from Geolabs to evaluate the actual exposed
subgrade soil conditions and to modify the above recommended pavement sections
It is generally difficult to achieve 95 percent relative compaction in the volcanic ash
soils Therefore we recommend that the subgrade soils under the pavement areas be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to at least 92 percent relative compaction CBR tests and or field
observations should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction
to confirm that the above design sections are adequate
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials required for the pavement
sections should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to Sections 703 06
and 703 17 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public
Works Construction 1994 HSS The aggregate base course and aggregate subbase
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential
contamination of the excavated materials Scattered loose boulders lying on the
existing ground surface at the finished subgrade elevation should be removed to
expose firm ground Soft loose and or yielding areas disclosed during clearing and
subgrade preparation operations should be backfilled with well compacted fill
Finished subgrades and areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement
for the finished subgrades below the aggregate base course and aggregate subbase
should be increased to a minimum of 92 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Fill Materials and Compaction Requirements
In general the fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill material up to the
bottom of the aggregate base course or aggregate subbase may consist of the
excavated on site materials if less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with the
exception of the very wet ash soils greater than 75 percent moisture content Fill
materials with rock fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension may also

be used as fill material provided that they are placed at least 3 feet below the finished
subgrade elevation
If imported general fill materials are required the imported materials should be
non expansive granular material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder
sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger
than 6 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR
value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less
when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill materials should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to
the project site for the intended use
General fill consisting of the excavated on site materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction The compaction requirement for finished subgrades below the
aggregate base course or aggregate subbase should be increased to at least
92 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction
should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers multiple wheel
pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Due to the sometimes dry and windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fill
materials will be a critical aspect during fill placement In some areas the in situ soils
appear to be dry relative to the optimum moisture content Therefore water trucks
should be available in sufficient number to provide adequate water for
moisture conditioning during compaction State and Federal governments have
enacted stringent regulations pertaining to the release of dust from construction sites
Therefore it is also advisable to institute a dust control program during grading
operations to reduce the amount of airborne dust due to the nature of the on site soils
Excavation
Based on our site reconnaissance we anticipate that the proposed roads are
underlain by slightly to severely fractured basalt formation mantled by volcanic ash
soils of varying thicknesses It is anticipated that the basalt may be excavated with
normal heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the
rock is near the existing ground surface However excavations into the deeper
formations may require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting The above
discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on our visual
observation of the existing rock formation at the site We recommend that contractors

Cut and Fill Slopes
We understand that cut slopes up to about 12 feet high are planned for the proposed
Roads A and B Based on our experience in the area we anticipate that the cut
slopes would generally expose volcanic ash soils and some basalt rock In general
we believe that the cut slopes planned may be designed with a slope inclination of
2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing
slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should
start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a
well compacted slope face
We recommend that the tops of cut and fill slopes be rounded In addition due to the
nature of the ash material mixtures anticipated at the project site water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes and where feasible slope planting should be
provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Construction Observation
Construction observation of the site grading work during construction is an integral
part of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of
the site preparation fill placement and compaction and pavement construction Other
aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications
or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those considered in this report The
recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
