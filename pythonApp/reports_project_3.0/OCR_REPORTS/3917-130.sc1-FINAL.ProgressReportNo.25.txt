Status of Earth Movements
The earth movements currently being monitored at Areas A and B have not
stabilized and continue to experience slow downhill creep movements Based on the
history of the earth movement monitoring sudden catastrophic earth movements are not
likely but cannot be completely ruled Earth movements detected after completion of the
subdivision were severe and occurred at a higher rate than the movements recorded
during our site monitoring Our records of the earth movements indicate that the
movements continue at a typically slow and relatively steady rate with some inclinometers
showing incremental patterns of accelerated and regressive movement Extreme rainfall
events and the increased subsurface groundwater seepage associated with such rain
events have the potential to accelerate the movement in the short term and cause
significant disruption to the existing pavements and fixed structures in the area
The continued earth movements will continue to adversely affect road surfaces and
buried utilities Continued maintenance of the infrastructure is important to reduce sources
of water infiltrating into the subsurface which can accelerate movements
Short Term and Long Term Repair Options
We understand that engineered mitigation efforts to slow the movements within the
public right of way will be performed as funds become available Retaining structures such
as drilled shaft walls and tie back retaining walls may be designed to restrain the hillside
areas adjacent to the public right of way
Short term solutions may include reinforced earth structures to stiffen the upper
portions of the slide to reduce differential movements at the surface Such a structure is
scheduled for construction in Kuahea Street at the upper portion of the
Waiomao Landslide

period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the

precipitation earth movements generally decrease slightly
