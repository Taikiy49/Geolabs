Project Considerations
The Waipahu Ash Landfill Closure project is located in Waipahu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii As part of the project closure existing gas probes and groundwater
monitoring wells at the 43 acre landfill site require abandonment and or modification In
addition new gas monitoring probes and groundwater monitoring wells were installed at
locations determined and designated by Our work on the project
was performed between and The work
performed by Geolabs Inc is presented below
A Gas Probe Abandonment
Six gas probes designated as GP 1 through GP 6 with depths varying from 18 to
30 feet were abandoned Abandonment of each probe consisted of removing the well
head pulling the pipe casing and screen drilling and cleaning the fill material for the
entire boring depth using hollow stem auger and backfilling the borehole from the bottom
to the top of the existing ground surface with bentonite slurry

B Monitoring Well Modification and Abandonment
Two monitoring wells were modified removed the existing
concrete pads and existing steel protective covers during the initial site grading operations
Geolabs Inc installed the temporary surface casing and PVC pipe extension to the
required finished grade as determined by
installed the geomembrane boot backfilled around the well to the required finished grade
and installed the concrete pad and well covers
One of the existing monitoring wells designated as C 2 that was to be
abandoned could not be located It is likely that the well casing corroded away since it
was located very close to the shoreline Please refer to attached Request for
Information Clarification response from AECOM
C New Gas Monitoring Probe Installation
Five new gas probe monitoring wells designated as GMP 1 through GMP 5
were installed to depths varying from 3 to 12 feet below ground surface bgs at
locations determined and designated by We observed
groundwater during installation at approximately 3 feet bgs for 5 and at
approximately 12 feet bgs for It should be noted that groundwater conditions
at the site could change due to fluctuations in tide levels variations in rainfall groundwater
withdrawal construction activities and or other factors not apparent at the time of our field
exploration
The new gas probes consist of 2 inch Schedule 40 PVC casing and screen with
0 040 inch slots installed above the groundwater table Well backfill consist of sand
filter bentonite seal and cement bentonite grout Surface completion for each well
consist of above ground well cover with 3 x 3 x 4 concrete pad and three 8 inch
diameter bollards
As built plans for the new gas monitoring probes are shown on Plates 1 through 5

determined and designated by The new monitoring wells were

well screens were installed below the encountered water table during our installation
Well No MW A was installed using 17 feet of screen 15 feet plus 2 feet above the
encountered groundwater table Well Nos MW B MW C were installed using 20 feet
