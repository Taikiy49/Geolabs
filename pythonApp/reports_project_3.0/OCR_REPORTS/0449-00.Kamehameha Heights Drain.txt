Submitted herewith are the results of a geology and soils investi
gation along the alignment of the present Kamehameha Heights storm
drain channel
Invert depths and other storm drain information were obtained from
plans for Nuhelewai Stream Flood Control Aupuni Street to Makanani
Drive Kapalama Honolulu Hawaii Plan and Profile Department of
Public Works and the City and County of Honolulu undated
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the soil and
geology conditions along the storm drain alignment in order to
assess the conditions that will affect the design and construction
of the project

The investigation was conducted between April 16 and 20 1973
and consisted of the drilling and logging of five 4 inch diameter
borings to a maximum depth of approximately 20 feet
STORM DRAIN LOCATION AND DESCRIPTION
The subject drain extends through the area of Makanani Drive and
Aupuni Street Kamehameha Heights Honolulu Hawaii The total
length of the proposed Kamehameha Heights drain is approximately
1400 feet according to information provided by your office The
drain generally consists of a partially excavated stream channel
Nuhelewai Stream with a box drain having a partly concreted
invert and stone side walls The walls vary from about 3 feet to
12 feet in height and are in varying degrees of repair
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The new channel is planned to be a lined U shaped structure with
vertical sides Present plans are to deepen the channel in some
places as much as 5 feet The existing dam will remain and the
channel in the silting basin area will be lined Present infor
mation also indicates that the channel may be widened in certain
areas
FIELD EXPLORATION
Five 4 inch diameter combination auger and diamond bit borings

GEOLOGY AND SOILS
GEOLOGY
The Kamehameha Storm Drain is located on the Honolulu flank of
the Volcanic basalt Koolau Mountain Range forming the north
eastern portion of Oahu After the formation of the Koolau
Range a period of extended weathering and erosion carved deep
canyons into the range Continued erosion has cut secondary
valleys into the flanks of ridges The Kamehameha Storm Drain
Page 4

May 15 1973
occupies one of the boulder filled secondary valleys Recently
owners of properties along the drain have built stone walls
along the drain and parts of the stream bed have been filled
with concrete

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the geologic mapping and selective subsurface ex
ploration performed the following conclusions and recommendations
are presented
A Foundation of Invert
1 In the area of the silting basin between Naio Street
and the debris dam the proposed invert of the new channel
based on latest information will vary from the present
silt level at the dam to a maximum depth below existing
ground of 8 feet near Station 2 00
Because the upper 5 feet maximum of alluvial deposited
materials adjacent to the dam are soft to very soft
removal and recompaction of these materials prior to
placing the new invert slab is necessary Diversion of
stream runoff will probably be necessary in order to

perform this recompaction As alternates the invert
near the dam could be lowered necessitating at least
partial removal and reconstruction of the existing dam
or the drain can be founded on short piers anchored
in the weathered basalt
In the vicinity of Boring 5 the proposed invert at a
maximum depth of 8 feet will probably require heavy
ripping and possibly some blasting of the partly
weathered basalt bedrock encountered at a depth of
2 5 feet
In the area between Naio Street and Hillcrest Street
the proposed new invert varies from 0 to 5 feet below
existing invert Because Boring 3 encountered basalt
bedrock immediately below the existing concrete
blasting or jackhammering will probably be necessary
if the present plans are adhered to Generally it is
strongly recommended that blasting be avoided where
possible and done only with the utmost care where
necessary because of the close proximity to existing
dwellings

B Walls
1 We understand that it is presently planned to widen and or
reconstruct the channel sidewalls Generally new unsur
charged walls along the channel may be designed for an
equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot
and an allowable bearing value of 10 ksf on hard basalt
bedrock and 5 ksf on weathered basalt bedrock AIl

retaining walls should be founded on basalt bedrock
The walls should be backfilled with adequate granular
material in accordance with the soils engineer s
recommendation and the walls and invert of the channel
should be provided with an adequate gravel and weep
hole system to prevent the buildup of hydrostatic pressure
It should be stressed that the above recommendations
are for structural design of the new channel walls
The construction problems associated with removal and
reconstruction of the existing channel sidewalls will
probably be great and specific recommendations are
beyond the scope of this report Due to the close
proximity of existing old cracked structures to the
channel walls and the probability of nonuniform dumped
fill originally placed to shape the old drain alignment
the stability of these adjacent structures cannot be
foretold It is recommended that when possible the
old walls be left in as a form for any new concrete
walls to avoid the anticipated construction problems and
possibly legal action Since several structures are
extremely close to the existing sidewalls underpinning
or removal may be necessary before reconstruction can be
