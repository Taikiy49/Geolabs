Based on the moderate to heavy column loads anticipated for the Napili Tower we
recommended a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal precast
pre stressed concrete piles bearing on the dense boulder layers and or hard basalt rock
formation be used for support of the building structure
The 16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles were designed based on an allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 110 tons per pile for the Napili Tower structure The
16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles would need to extend to average depths of about
150 feet below the existing ground surface in order to achieve the allowable compressive
load capacity Based on our field exploration some dense zones were encountered in the
shallow subsurface in some of the borings drilled across the project site Therefore we
recommend predrilling to depths on the order of about 40 feet below the ground surface be
implemented at each pile location
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
driven pile installation for the resort development only The general contractor was
Swinerton Builders and the concrete pile installation subcontractor was Healy Tibbitts
Builders Inc
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 18 concrete piles at various locations
across the site was conducted from September 5 through 11 2007 Two of the originally
planned test piles TP 13 and TP 15 corresponding to Pile Nos 130 and 156 were not
driven during the test pile program as they were located in an area subjected to frequent
construction traffic The locations of the driven test piles are shown on the Pile Location
Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The purpose of the test pile program was to establish the
driveability of the concrete piles given the subsurface conditions the selected driving
equipment and methods of concrete pile installation In addition the test pile program
also provided information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing strata
across the site which was used to estimate the length of the production concrete piles

Pile Driving Acceptance Criteria
Based on our field observations and the results of the test pile program we believe
that the driving equipment Junttan HHS9 used and methods of pile installation are
acceptable for installing the specified production piles in general accordance with the
project specifications In addition it is our professional opinion that the test piles have
been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of
220 kips 110 tons with a factor of safety of 2 0
Based on the results of the test pile program we recommended using the following
production pile driving criteria for the above project The production piles should be driven
with the same type of hammer Junttan HHSQ with a stroke height of 3 feet at the end of
the pile driving operations The pile driving operation may be terminated when the
aggregated blow counts are at least 100 blows per foot for the last foot or 10 blows per
inch for the last 6 inches In addition the pile driving also may be terminated as requested
by the Geolabs field representative
Production Pile Driving
Based on the test pile driving program and the static pile load test we
recommended production piles on the order of about 140 to 160 feet in length In addition
a predrilling depth of 40 feet was used to facilitate the pile driving operation through the
surface fill crust and dense zones in the shallow subsurface
