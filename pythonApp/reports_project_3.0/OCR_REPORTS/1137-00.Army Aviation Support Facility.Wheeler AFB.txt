INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of the soils engineering investiga
tion conducted for the above referenced site
Improvements will include extension of the existing hangar office
tow lane helicopter pad and hangar access apron at Wheeler Air Force
Base Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore the soil
conditions at the site to provide data for use in the design of the
proposed development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1137 00 Page 2
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance the
drilling of six new exploratory borings review of existing building
foundation design and laboratory test data
Results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were
used in the engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONS IDERAT IONS
The proposed army aviation support facilities renovation involves the
upgrading of the existing facilities by construction of extensions
to the present hangar and office building tow lane heli pads and
hangar access apron
Presently the aircraft that the existing facilities serve consist
mainly of skid type helicopters UH 1 AH 1 and OH 58 class One
fixed wing U 8 aircraft is also serviced there
The proposed development would consist of the following
1 An office and industrial shop extension about 50 by 66
feet in plan dimensions with concrete masonary CMU
bearing walls and steel roof joist
2 A steel framed hangar about 80 by 125 feet in plan
dimensions with columns approximately 20 feet on
center and metal sheath walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1137 00 Page 3
3 Adjacent to the eastern hangar entrance will be a new
rigid pavement access apron approximately 80 by 110
feet
4 The existing asphaltic concrete tow lane will be
extended about another 360 feet terminating at the new
access apron
5 A new heli pad extension about 410 by 490 feet in plan
dimension will have both rigid and flexible pavements
Anticipated structural loading will be about 2 6 kips per lineal foot
and 40 kips per column
Preliminary site grading plans are not available at the time of our
report preparation
The project site is located within the Wheeler Air Force Base complex
in Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii The proposed development is along the
southern side of Santos Dumont Avenue approximately 1000 feet west
of the main base entry gate at Kamehameha Highway see Plate 1
The site is bounded by Runway A to the south Santos Dumont Avenue
to the north existing office and hangar facilities to the east and
an open grass area to the west
Ground surface elevations range from 832 to 845 feet with most of the
site sloping gently to the southwest and a steeper bank in the north
west corner near the existing heli pad

FIELD INVESTIGATION
A total of six 6 exploratory borings were drilled to depths ranging
from 10 to 20 feet to investigate the subsurface conditions The
approximate boring locations are presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
An engineer from our firm performed the site reconnaissance and
logged all the borings drilled by MAS Drilling Co who was contracted

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the Schofield Plateau which is an area
between the Waianae and Koolau mountain ranges The plateau was
formed during the late Tertiary and early Quaternary primarily by
basaltic lava flows from the younger Koolau Volcano banking against
the eastern slopes of the older Waianae dome Since the cessation of
volcanic activity chemical weathering has formed a thick mantle of
residual soil derived from the parent basasltic rock
Soil Conditions
Relative uniform soil conditions were encountered during the course
of our site investigation

Stiff reddish brown clayey silt was encountered to depths of ten feet
overlying a stiff brown silty soil to the depth drilled
In the existing developed areas Borings 1 and 2 encountered about 6
to 9 inches of gravelly fill beneath the 6 inch concrete slab
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during the time
of our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by stiff
silty residual soils and is suitable for the proposed construction
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the stiff natural
soils can be utilized for the proposed hangar and office buildings
The field exploration and laboratory tests indicate tha the on site
soils generally have low swelling potential if maintained at or above
the optimum moisture content 30 and not allowed to dry out Ina
dry condition some of the soils exhibited moderate swelling poten
tial Care should be taken by the contractor to maintain the mois
ture of the exposed subgrade soils to 5 percent above the optimum
moisture content prior to slab and pavement placement

Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan was not available to us during the course
of the subsurface investigation Based on the existing elevations at
the site cuts up to 10 feet deep and fills up to 3 feet thick are
anticipated for the proposed development
It appears that the underground bunker located at the northwest
corner of the existing heli pad will be demolished during site
grading The topographic survey indicates that the floor of the
bunker is about 3 feet below the existing heli pad level and there
fore backfilling will be required
If the stability of the tunnel walls is maintained it is our opinion
that the tunnel will not adversely affect the stability of the new
heli pad However we recommend that the stability of the walls be
monitored and checked periodically

At the commencement of earthwork all deleterious materials such
as vegetation topsoil concrete slabs asphalt pavements and
unsuitable fill should be removed and disposed of properly

Building Foundations
Renovation of the Army Aviation Support Facility will consist of
extending both the existing hangar and office structures
The field exploration encountered very stiff silty clay soils at the
site Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the very
stiff in situ soil is recommended for the proposed structures
Footings should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below the finished
grade A review of the foundation plans for the existing hangar and
office indicate that the existing footings may be embedded 4 feet
below the finished grade Where the new footings are adjacent to the
existing footings we recommend that the new footing be deepended to
match the existing footing level This is to minimize the possibi
lity of settlement of the new footing due to loose footing trench
backfill above the existing footing level
For the abovementioned recommendations an allowable bearing capacity
of 5000 p s f may be used for foundations The bearing capacity
value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased by
one third for short duration wind and or seismic loading

Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of one half
inch total and one fourth inch differential
Minimum footing width of 36 inches for spread footings and 12 inches
for continuous footings should be maintained
