INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering investigation
for the observed ground floor slab settlement of Building No 35 at
Camp H M Smith Halawa Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
Site and its vicinity is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore the existing
soil conditions at the site to provide data in evaluating the possible
cause or Causes of the observed floor slab settlement and possible
remedial measures for Building No 35 at Camp H M Smith
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance the
drilling of six exploratory borings laboratory testing of selected
samples engineering analysis and report preparation

Results of our field investigation and laboratory tests which were
used in our engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
Building No 35 is located north of the parking lot at the end of
Lopez Road which is within the Camp H M Smith Military Installation
located in Halawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
On the northern side of the building the ground surface slopes upward
toward Building No 9 at an approximate grade of 2 1 H V Bordering
the site from the east to the south is Lopez Road and its adjoining
parking lot To the west of the site is an embankment which slopes
downward toward the south at an approximate grade of 2 1 H V
Building No 35 which was constructed around 1943 is currently being
used as individual offices It is a 5 story structure approximately
155 by 37 feet in plan dimension On the southern side of the
building the first floor level is situated about 5 feet above the
existing ground level The general layout of the offices are presented
on the Site Plan Plate 2
A concrete lined drainage swale along the entire northside of Building
No 35 is used to catch and divert surface runoff from the adjacent
slope and building roof

The concrete slab is relatively flat with elevations ranging from
576 8 to 577 0 feet
OBSERVED DAMAGE
The reported and observed damage is limited to the 5 offices situated
on the southwestern portion of the first floor level In these areas
the existing ground floor concrete slab shows quite an extensive
amount of settlement with some slab cracking in Offices 2 and 3
Along the interior portion of the structural wall settlements of 1 to
2 inches have been noted The partition walls which divide the indi
vidual offices have measurable separations varying from 1 16 inch near
the hallway corridor and up to 2 inches adjacent to the southern
structural walls as shown on the Cross Sections Plates 3 4
Cracking was also noted developing beneath the tile floor covering in
Office 2 In Office 3 a crack about 7 feet long and perpendicular to
the structural wall has been overlaid and reflection cracking has
developed
No distress was noted during our investigation in the structural wall
foundations and the upper second and third floors

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Pield Exploration
A total of six 6 exploratory borings were drilled through the
existing ground floor concrete slab to depths ranging from 4 0 to 6 5
feet to investigate the subsurface soil conditions The approximate
boring locations are presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and the log of borings
together with the laboratory testing results of selected samples are
presented in the appendices of this report
Soil Conditions
The six borings encountered a 5 to 6 inch thick concrete floor slab
over 4 inches of basalt base course Directly below the base course
layer is a brown silty clay with some basalt and coral gravels This
silty clay layer extended to the depth of our borings and appears to
be 111 material placed during the initial building construction
In and 4 the clayey silt encountered below the base
course layer varied from very soft to medium stiff grading to stiff
at about 2 5 feet The samples taken appeared to be very dry and
friable at depths up to 2 5 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The existing floor slab in Offices 1 to 5 of Building No 35 indicate
signs of settlement of up to 2 inches along the bearing walls as shown
on Site Plan Plate 2 and Cross Sections Plates 3 and 4 A review of
the plans of the building indicates that the northern three fourths of
the building is on cut while the southern portion required backfilling
behind the structure walls to maintain the first floor elevation
Our investigation disclosed several probable causes of the observed
floor slab settlement
1 The absence of a vapor barrier and water proofing beneath the
floor slab and the addition of air conditioning during the
late 1960 s may have contributed to the drying out of the
subgrade soils and a resulting shrinkage of the backfill
2 Improper moisture conditioning and or insufficient compactive
effort may have resulted in a loose backfill condition which
may have settled due to floor loading and overburden pressure
Generally the natural in place densities varied from 67 to 90
percent of the maximum dry density
The moisture content ranged from 13 to 35 percent generally
decreasing toward the wall footing and floor slab

Our field observations and laboratory testing indicate that
the floor slab settlement can be attributed to either one or a
combination of these conditions Therefore remedial work
should be attempted to correct or minimize these conditions
For remedial work we recommend the following
l
The existing floor slab in each office affected should be
saw out and removed to a point where no visible settlement or
gap is noticeable between the floor and partition walls
dividing the individual offices We estimate that this
distance will be about 11 feet from the southern bearing wall
The existing soft backfill beneath the slab should be excavated
to a depth where stiff or residual soil is encountered We
believe this depth should be about 5 feet at the bearing wall
and sloping up toward the hallway at about H V grade
3 Water proofing should be applied to the bearing wall face prior
to backfilling and recompacting to help protect the backfill
from future dehydration from external environmental sources

Our laboratory testing indicates that the on site soil is a
silty clay MH CH and could be moderately expansive if it is
compacted too dry or when it is well compacted In order to
prevent future slab movements due to shrinkage or swelling of
the backfill we recommend that non expansive granular
material such as select borrow should be used as backfill
The fill should be placed in maximum six 6 inches thick
compacted lifts and mechanically tamped to 95 percent of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 The
backfill should be keyed and benched into the stiff soil
during compaction
A new 5 inch thick concrete floor slab and 4 inch base course
layer to match the existing conditions can be placed over
the compacted backfill
Prior to pouring the slab a plastic vapor barrier should be
installed to further seal the subgrade from external
environmental sources
A second alternative which consists of drilling holes through the
slabs then pumping grout under pressure to relevel the slab was also
considered However this type of work requires a specialized con
tractor to be successful Also the very dry and loose soils would

remain under the slabs and could be susceptible to additional settle
ment due to the weight of the grout and or continued drying and
shrinking of the subsoils For the abovementioned reasons this
method is not recommended
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for the remedial construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommen
dations is included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the excavation and backfilling operations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ah he cama
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 6 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Hand carried portable drilling equipment was used to
drill the borings One bulk sample was recovered from the auger
cuttings of the existing subgrade at depths of 1 0 to 2 0 feet
below the existing ground surface
The material encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
and or geologist who continously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1
through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
log of borings at the appropriate sample depths
