SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Dole Plantation Expansion project in the Wahiawa area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for
the Dole Plantation Expansion project These recommendations are intended for the
design of foundations slabs on grade and parking expansion The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is bounded by the Helemano Plantation to the north and the east
an undeveloped open field to the south and Kamehameha Highway to the west in the
Wahiawa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that it is planned to
construct a store expansion and coffee mill at the northeastern side of the existing
pineapple pavilion This area is bounded by an open grass area with Shower Trees to
the north and the east a maintenance building to the south and an asphalt concrete
driveway and existing Pineapple Pavilion to the south and the west The parking
expansion area is located to the south of the existing asphalt concrete paved parking
area and it includes an existing employee parking lot and an open grass area This
area is bounded by an existing asphalt concrete parking area to the north Dole Maze to
the east open grass area to the south and Norfolk Pine trees along the Kamehameha
Highway to the west

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned
with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano during the late Pleistocene
Epoch The dips of the lava beds generally range from 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows
on the plateau have weathered to depths of 50 to 100 feet and are characterized by the
red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such as Waikakalaua Kipapa and
Kaukonahua are a result of this weathering The brown and reddish brown soils
generally consist of ancient alluvial deposits overlying the weathered rock

The project site is bounded by the Helemano Plantation to the north and the east

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that the new structures be supported on a shallow foundation system
consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the in situ stiff to hard silty
clays The footing should extend down a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade The bottom of footing elevations should be recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose materials that are
encountered at the bottom of footing elevations should be over excavated to the
underlying firm or dense materials The resulting over excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted fills In lieu of backfilling the over excavation with compacted fills
the footings may extend down to bear on the firm or dense materials
To provide more uniform support we recommend that the new store expansion
and coffee mill slab be supported on 12 inches of compacted select non expansive fill
material over the in situ stiff clayey soils The 12 inch select granular fill material
recommended should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or
more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent The select
non expansive fill material should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this report

3 2 Slabs On Grade
It is our understanding that the slab on grade for building interior will be 5 inches
thick and 4 inches for concrete walkway Our laboratory tests indicate that the silty clay
soils have a relatively low potential for shrinking and swelling due to moisture
fluctuation To reduce the potential for future distress to the slabs on grade resulting
from shrinking and swelling of the silty clay soils we recommend that the subgrade soils
underneath the slab areas be scarifiled to a depth of about 8 _ inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction To provide more uniform
support we recommend that the new store expansion and coffee mill slab be supported
on 12 inches of compacted select non expansive fill material over the in situ stiff to very
hard clayey soils
The select non expansive fill material should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the above discussions The aggregate subbase course and the underlying subgrade
soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of concrete Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the
soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill
We envision that some exterior concrete walkways may be required for the
proposed project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least
6 inches of non expansive select granular fill or aggregate subbase course The select

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the new structures should be sloped to shed surface
water runoff away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for
ponding It is also recommended that gutter systems be installed around the new
structures and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and slab areas
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided
Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or
slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and
slabs

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of
about 11 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket
penetrometer tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 inch Ring Swell test was performed on a relatively undisturbed sample of
the surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of the test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
