INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation for
the proposed Yum Yum Tree Restaurant at Pali Palms Plaza Kailua
accordance with the scope of services outlined in our proposal dated
The general vicinity of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed restaurant will be a one story structure occupying the
major portion of the site
W O 882 40 Page 2
The proposed layout of the structure is shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 The edge of the structure will be built very close to the 10 foot
wide sewer easement to the east and close to top of Kawainui Canal
bank It is our understanding that the existing sewer line is 54
inches in diameter and was placed some 22 feet below the ground This
deep excavation may have affected the subsurface soils adjacent to the
sewer easement
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this foundation investigation is to explore the sub
surface soil conditions at the site in order to provide soil data for
use in the design of the site grading and building foundation for the
proposed restaurant
Our field exploration consisted of drilling 4 borings ranging from
21 5 to 38 0 feet below the existing ground surface Previously 5
test pits were also excavated to examine the near surface conditions
The aproximate locations of the borings and test pits are shown on
Plate 2 Soil samples obtained from the borings were re examined in
our laboratory and selected samples were tested for expansion potential
and identification
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests are pre
sented in the appendices of this report Based on these findings
engineering analyses and evaluation were made to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
General Features
The project site is approximately 500 feet southeast of the inter
section of Mokapu Boulevard and North Kalaheo Avenue near Kailua Bay
in the Kalaheo area of Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
existing ground surface has been graded relatively flat with ground
surface elevations ranging from 9 to 10 feet above the Mean Sea Level
and sloping down gently toward the east The Kawainui Canal borders
the eastern project boundary and has sloped banks of about
horizontal to vertical
The ground cover consists primary of well maintained lawn grass
coconut palms hedges and some trees Having been previously
developed the site may contain buried tree stumps and foundations
Subsurface Conditions
The site is mantled by a clay and sand layer of about feet
thick It consists of stiff gray silty clay with pockets of tan
sand This material may have been dredged from the construction of
Kawainui Canal The stiff silty clay is somewhat expansive
Under the fill is a layer of coral sand about 6 to 8 feet thick It
is fine to medium grained medium dense to dense and appears to be a
beach deposit The sand grades somewhat looser toward its bottom
Under the sand is a layer of gray silty coral sand and gravel ina
loose state

This loose silty coral sand and gravel layer is about 6 feet thick and
underlain by a loose clayey sand layer that extends to about 20 feet
below the ground Below a depth of 20 feet the soil generally con
Sists of gray stiff silty clay
Groundwater was encountered at about 8 5 to 10 8 feet below the exist
ing surface in our borings The groundwater appear to fluctuate some
what with tide The depths to groundwater measured are listed in the
boring logs
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings we believe that the site is suitable for the
planned structure Any new deep utility trenches such as main sewer
connections should be installed ahead of footing excavation to avoid
footings being affected by deep trenching work
Because of the low to moderately expansive nature of surface clay fill
and potential disturbance of the previous sewer trenching foundation
and on grade slab will need special care in construction to minimize
potential expansion problem After the site has been filled and
subgrade compacted the entire building area should be thoroughly
pre moistened before the floor slab is placed

Site Grading
The discussions and recommendations in this portion applies mainly to
the building area covered by the roof
Prior to receiving any fill the ground surface should be stripped to
remove vegetation and debris All fill areas thus exposed should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimun
moisture content and compacted to a dry density of at least 90 of the
maximum density as determined in accordance with the ASIM D 1557 78
test procedure The existing fill surface should be so crowned to
drain water away from the middle of the building area
To minimize the potential expansion problem associated with the
on site clay fill we recommend that at least 12 inches of non
expansive material including the cushion fill be placed over the
on site clay fill It is our understanding that about 12 to 18 inches
of new fill will be required to raise the restaurant floor subgrade
Therefore it appears that the above recommended minimum thickness
requirement can be satisfied by importing select non expansive fill
material
The fill should consist of non expansive clean granular material
such as select borrow or crusher run material These material should
be permeable and stable under soaked condition to provide a stable
working pad while the existing fill is being presoaked to induce
expansion ahead of floor slab placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 882 40 Page 6
The new fill material should have a CBR value of at least 12 when
compacted to 90 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Any non expansive fill material whether imported or on site should
be inspected tested and approved by a soils eng ineer
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around the
footings Granular non expansive imported material should be used
for backfilling around footings
Fill and backfill within the limits of structure should be placed in
horizontal 8 inch lifts and compacted to a dry density of at least
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The compaction may be
reduced to 90 in the areas outside the structure
Foundation Support
All footings may be founded on stiff to very stiff gray silty clay or
dense compacted sand or new non expansive fill as appropriate
An allowable soil bearing pressure of 2000 p s f may be used for
footing design A one third increase may be applied for wind or
seismic loads
All footing should have a minimum dimension of 12 inches The bottom
of footing should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 882 40 Page 7
For the above recommendations it is estimated that the total and
differential settlements will not exceed one fourth of an inch
Because of the clayey nature of the soil footing excavations should
not be permitted to dry out excessively Moisture conditioning of
dried out soil may be required before foundation pour or backfilling
if materials are dried out Such requirements should be judged by a
soils engineer
Any loose or soft spots encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation should be removed and replaced with either concrete or
granular material compacted to a minimum of 953 maximun density
Excavations should be protected from surface run off which may enter
the excavations All loose soil or mud should be removed from the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
We recommend that all the footing excavations and filling operations
be inspected by the soils engineer to verify the foundation soil
conditions and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation

On Grade Slabs
Due to the somewhat expansive nature of the surface clay fill the
on grade floor design and construction will require a special con
sideration which should include placing non expansive granular
fill and cushion prewetting for several days damp proofing and
finally the placement of floor slab
The cushion i11 under the slab should be at least 4 inches thick and
should consist of crushed rock such as No 3A Fine The material
should be compacted by a hand operated plate vibratory compactor
making a minimum of 4 passes
After the compaction the subgrade should be wetted thoroughly for at
least 3 days This wetting is to pre induce the potential expansion
of subsurface material before the concrete slab is placed The mois
tening should be so controlled as not to flood the subgrade but to
keep the existing clayey fill moistened Therefore if the ground had
been kept moist through the grading and filling operation the moisten
period may be lessened This should be monitored by a soils engineer
Following moistening the vapor barrier membrane may be placed and
then concrete placed The membrane should be overlapped and taped at
joints Any rupture or tear of the membrane should be patched

It is recommended that the footing excavation site grading back
filling subgrade compaction and moistening operations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction This is to see that the

mendations given in this report are contingent upon the above inspec
tions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 5 to 38 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Truck mounted drilling equipment were used to drill
the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who contin
uously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the materials encountered is presented on the Logs
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
