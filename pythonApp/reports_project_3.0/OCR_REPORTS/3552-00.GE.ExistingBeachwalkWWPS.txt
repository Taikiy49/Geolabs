Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation
Existing Beachwalk Wastewater Pump Station
Waikiki Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have
conducted a geotechnical engineering evaluation on the foundation performance of the
existing Beachwalk Wastewater Pump Station WWPS in the Waikiki area on the island of
Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the field observations and findings and presents
our geotechnical engineering evaluation derived from our analyses of the subject project
Project Consi ion
The existing Beachwalk WWPS is located within the public parking lot at the
intersection of Kuhio Avenue and Kaiolu Street in the Waikiki area on the island of Oahu
Hawaii Based on the information provided the main facility that houses the Beachwalk
WWPS was constructed in 1966 The Beachwalk WWPS serves Waikiki and other
surrounding areas with 70 percent of its flow originating in Waikiki Discharge from the
Beachwalk WWPS is conveyed to the Ala Moana Park trunk sewer
The existing Beachwalk WWPS is a one story structure with roof framing consisting of
precast reinforced Y joists supported by cast in place concrete columns Precast concrete
wall panels are in filled between the columns The ground floor elevation of the pump station
building is at approximately 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with the below grade level
extending to a depth of approximately 30 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface
A generator building located to the southwest of the pump station building was
constructed in 1986 The generator building was built over what appears to be the old
WWPS We understand that the existing walls and foundation of the old WWPS was utilized

Evaluations a iscussions
Based on the soil boring information provided in the construction plans for the existing
Beachwalk WWPS facility we understand that the project site is generally underlain by a thin
surficial crust of less than 5 feet in thickness Below the thin surficial crust loose coralline
materials consisting of loose finger coral with sand and silt were encountered and extended to
depths of about 40 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface Coral formation was then
encountered below a depth of about 40 to 45 feet below the surface
We understand that the below grade level of the existing facility extends to depths of
about 30 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface The existing WWPS is supported by a
mat foundation floating on the underlying loose coralline deposits Because of the removal of
the soils as a result of the deep excavation for the below grade structure we anticipate that
the net bearing pressure from the WWPS building loads on the loose coralline materials
should be small Based on our review of the construction plans the makai section of the
pump station building is constructed on grade with the makai wall supported on shallow
footings bearing on the surficial fill crust consisting of dense sands and gravels
Based on our review of the video tape of the wet well inspection it appears that there
is no evidence of significant foundation distress observed around the wet well walls in both the
ewa and diamond head wet wells The wet well dividing wall appears to be in reasonably
good condition and no apparent signs of cracking and separation were observed from our
review of the video tape Our review of the construction plans indicated that the wet well
cover slab is a slab on grade bearing on fill materials and is not part of the wet well structure
Therefore separation of the wet well cover slab from the building walls does not appear to
reflect foundation problems and performance of the existing WWPS structure
Based on the construction plans provided we understand that the makai wall of the
pump station building is supported on shallow footings bearing on the thin surficial crust
Because of the thin surficial crust and the underlying loose coralline deposits we believe that
the shallow footings along the makai wall have settled and resulted in the wall being out of
plumb Since the makai section of the pump station building is supported on shallow footings
and the mauka section of the pump station building is supported by mat foundation floating on
the underlying loose coralline deposits we believe that differential settlement of the differing

We understand that recent development in the vicinity of the Beachwalk WWPS facility
have involved dewatering operations due to basement construction Because of the relatively
shallow groundwater table and permeable coral formation in the area large quantities of water
had to be removed or displaced from the excavation during construction Since the existing
WWPS is essentially floating on the underlying loose subsoils any dewatering operations in
its vicinity would likely cause subsoil consolidation resulting in potential foundation settlement
of the existing WPS We believe that these dewatering operations and or construction
activities in the past have contributed to some of the distress observed at the Beachwalk
WWPS facility In addition the potential for further foundation settlement at the existing
Beachwalk WWPS exists as long as dewatering operations and or construction activities in its
vicinity continues in the future
Based on the existing site and subsurface conditions we believe that the existing
Beachwalk WWPS would be able to function as it has in the past from a geotechnical
engineering point of view However any changes to the current conditions such as
dewatering operations construction activities flooding etc in the vicinity would result in
potential settlements and distress to the existing WWPS structure
