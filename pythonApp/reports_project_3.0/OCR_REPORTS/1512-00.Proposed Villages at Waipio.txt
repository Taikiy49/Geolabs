INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a scils investigation performed
for the proposed Villages at Waipio Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
l
PROJECT CONSIDERATTONS
The proposed project will include development of twenty six 2 and
3 story multi family wooden structures with related parking areas and
access roadways Cuts and fills up to about 3 to 6 feet are contemplated
to grade the site for the development A general layout of the project
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Building foundation loadings are anticipated to be moderate to
light and typical for 2 and 3 steory wooden structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to provide recommendations for
site grading building foundation and pavement designs based on the soil
information obtained from borings drilled for this investigation The
borings were generally drilled and sampled to a depth of 11 5 feet below
the existing ground at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

Soil sampling and borings were performed under the observation of
one of our geologists The results of our field exploration and labora
tory tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
findings and reccmmendations are presented in the appendices of this
report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located at the northeast corner of the
intersection of Waipio Uka Street and Ka Uka Boulevard in Waipio Oahu
The site is roughly rectangular in plan and encompasses an area of
approximately 19 acres It is bordered on the east by Ka Uka Boulevard
on the south by Waipio Uka Street extension and existing residential lots
and on the north and west by vacant lots
The site is located on a gently sloping terrace in the Gentry
Business Park just above and to the west of Waipio Gentry Subdivision
The ground surface elevations at the site range from 450 feet in the
north to 420 in the south At the time of our exploration the interior
of the site was overgrown with hale koa and California grass The
western portion is cleared and relatively flat A dirt road crosses the
northern boundary

From a topographic map of the area a drainage ditch about 3 to 7
feet deep runs from the northwest corner of the site down in a southerly
Girection then along the southerly boundary of the site The westerly
portion of the drainage ditch appears to have been recently filled
A leese soil berm approximately 4 feet high extends along the
southern boundary adjacent to the drainage ditch
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower gentle
slope of the Koolau Volcano The weathering has altered the basalt rocks
to an iron rich mass of reddish brown colored soils with spheroidally
weathered boulders The site soils consist of stiff silts with varying
amounts of clays
Subsurface Exploration
For this investigation six borings were drilled at the site to
depths of 11 5 feet The purpose of the borings was to explore the
subsurface scil conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
Pased on the information obtained from the borings the site is
underlain by stiff to very hard reddish brown clayey silts and silty
clays throughout the exposed depths There was no noticeable trend to
the arrangement of the soil in comparing all of the borings
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 5
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during the
course of our exploration
More detailed descriptions of the scils encountered in the borings
are presented in the Appendix
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATTONS
The field explorations encountered competent bearing soils at the
project site Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have
low to moderate swelling potential and therefore slab on grade design
may be utilized
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundations are reccommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on hard in situ soil For foundations bearing on
compacted structural fill an allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may
be utilized These bearing values are for total dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
We recommend the bottoms of footings should be placed a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
Building construction near the top of fill slopes should have a
minimum set back distance of 8 feet from the outer edge of the footing
foundation measured horizontally out to the slope surface
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the
backfill Also the drainage ditch that runs along the westerly and
southerly portions of the site should be identified in the field and
loose silts nud deposits be cleaned out during site grading operations
and the ditch should then be backfilled in lifts well compacted to obtain
S0 relative density
Slab on grade shovld be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted
base course or 3B Fine gravel cushion layer The thickened edge around
the slab on grade should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent
finish ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier may be considered
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed to
ary out prior to concrete placement

foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be negligible

Final grading plan was not available at the time of this investi

partially filled drainage ditch and at the southeast corner of the site

Fill Material
Excavated on site material generally less than 6 inches in size
may be used as a source of fill material
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive
soils with laboratory CBR swell test results of less than 1 and
submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory testing and
approval pricr to its intended delivery to the project site
Monitoring of the surface preparation by a scils engineer is
particularly important especially to identify soft areas or
unsuitable materials
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are
recommended along with proper slope plantings
Inspection for Expansive Soils
We recommend that the project site be inspected by the soils
engineer during mass grading to see if expansive soils exist within upper
3 feet of the finished rough grade within the building areas Such
inspection should also include sampling and performing laboratory tests

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture contents unit weight and two sets of Atterberg Limits
tests were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A through A 6 at the
appropriate sample depths The results of the Atterberg Limits tests are
summarized on Plate B l
Two laboratory CBR California Bearing Ratio tests were
performed on bulk surface samples to evaluate the subgrade support
characteristics of the compacted soil The test results are summarized
on Plate B 1l
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
the natural condition The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural soil The test
results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing preparation
of fill areas placement and compaction of fill removal and backfilling
of drainage ditches These specifications are of a general nature and
may be superseded by specific requirements or recommendations within the
soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of trash
Gebris organic matter and all other deleterious materials
These materials shall be removed and disposed off site
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Ioose surface scils shall be removed to expose competent
natural soil Localized soft pockets shall be undercut and
backfilled with compacted select materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill

Standard Grading Specifications C 2
TIT
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of
six 6 inches brought to a moisture content within 3 percen
tage points of the optimum moisture content and compacted as
specified herein
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved
inported material Import material shall be tested and approved
a mininum of seven 7 working days prior to transportation to
the site Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent
organic matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of six inches 6 in diameter
In addition the imported borrow fill material shall be of low
expansion potential less than 1 swell per ASTM D 1883 Test
Method and have CBR values of 8 or greater when compacted to
95 of the maximum dry density according to the ASTM D 1557 78
or as revised test procedure
GEOLABS HAWAI
Standard Grading Specifications C 3
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers not to exceed
ten inches 10 in compacted thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer
B Rock fragments less than six inches 6 in diameter may be
utilized and occasional boulders up to 2 feet in size may also
be recycled in the fill provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
C Rocks greater than two feet 2 in size shall be removed
from the fill unless specifically approved for placement in the
fill by the soils engineer
D All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Designation
B 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the soils report
GEOLABS HAWAII
Standard Grading Specifications C4
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the scil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not
in accordance with these specifications shall not be approved
and shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to
the fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing
GEOLABS HAWAII
Standard Grading Specifications C 5
Remove and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns septic
tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be demolished and removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifications
Other Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the
previous fill surface shall be approved before resumption of
earthmnoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the suface of the fill so that
it will be free draining and will not pond surface water
