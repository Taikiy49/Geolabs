PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the
building foundation for the subject house The recommendations are based
on the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this
exploration The study does not include recommendations for a swimming
pool design since the pool contractor will usually provide the design and
construction of their work
Our work consisted of drilling three 3 borings The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The results of
our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in the
engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are presented in
the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXTSTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Kuliouou District of Honolulu
This approximately rectangular shaped 10 000 square foot lot is located at
the end of a private road off of Kuliouou Road
The existing lot is generally flat with elevations ranging from
approximately 10 5 to 11 5 feet above Mean Sea Level The lot is bounded
by an 8 inch wide hollow tile wall along the northern and eastern property
lines

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling three 3 borings B 1
through B 3 to depths of 11 5 and 12 0 feet The borings were drilled
with a truck mounted drilling rig The locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

to the depths of the borings
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at approximately 9 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the time of the drilling exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown clay The laboratory test results and previous experience indicate
that this material has moderate to low expansion potential Therefore
the foundation will require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed structure
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches
below the finished grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2344 00 Page 4
such as crusher waste select borrow or coral The replaced soils should
be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted
non expansive soil for details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate
3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimm of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic
Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 1 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The moderately expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a
minimi depth of 1 foot below the outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining should
be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist condition

A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier
may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill or in situ soil The thickened edges should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for

Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 1 foot
capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be followed The
subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A minimum 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete
Lot Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed residence
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral All imported fill materials should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2344 00 Page 6
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specification or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings ranging between 11 5 to 12 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger was
used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
