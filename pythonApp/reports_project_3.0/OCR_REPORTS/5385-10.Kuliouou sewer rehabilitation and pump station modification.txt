PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves rehabilitating sewer lines and modifying a pump station for the
Kuliouou Waste Water Pump Station WWPS in Kuliouou Valley on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The pump station receives wastewater generated from within Kuliouou Valley and
a section south of Kalanianaole Highway for a total service area of approximately
263 acres
The Kuliouou WWPS is along May Way in a residential neighborhood The facility
is a reinforced concrete structure approximately 30 years old and is in relatively good
condition Replacing the existing emergency generator and installing an underground fuel
tank and new flow meter vault are among the recommended modifications
A National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES permit approved by
the Hawaii State Department of Environmental Services will be necessary for the
discharge of trench water into the storm sewer and sanitary sewer system if ground water
is encountered during the improvement work One borehole was drilled to a depth of
approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with hollow stem augers After drilling a monitoring well consisting of 2 inch

SITE CONDITIONS
The Kuliouou WWPS is at 6180 May Way in a residential neighborhood just north
of Kalanianaole Highway The pump station site is bounded by chain link fence along the
perimeter with an asphalt concrete access driveway leading from May Way to the front
gate The pump station building is a single story reinforced concrete structure with a
finished floor elevation of 6 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the motor room The
underground wet well and pump room have a finished floor elevation of about 18 25 feet
MSL An above ground propane gas tank was observed on the west side of the property
The remaining areas outside the pump station building generally consisted of asphalt
pavements landscaped lawn and a few scattered trees Based on the topographic survey
map provided the pump station site is relatively level with existing ground surface
elevations on the order of about 5 to 6 feet MSL
MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION
We conducted the drilling well installation and well development tasks on
The ground water monitoring well consisted of 2 inch diameter
Schedule 40 PVC pipe installed to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground
surface The well consisted of about 15 feet of screen with 0 020 inch slot opening and
5 feet of solid casing The surface completion consisted of a 6 inch well cover that was
installed flush with the existing ground surface
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck mounted drill rig
advancing 8 inch outside diameter OD hollow stem augers The hollow stem augers
and associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned prior to drilling of the
borehole
The boring and monitoring well has been identified as MW No 1 and the
approximate location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A detail of the monitoring well
installation is provided as Plate 3

depth explored of approximately 22 feet below the existing ground surface

GROUND WATER CONDITIONS
A relatively shallow ground water table was encountered at the project site Due to
the relative proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean it is likely that ground water
level measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level
In addition the ground water level at the project site may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation and storm surge conditions

BEST MANAGEMENT PRACTICES BMP
For this project BMP would generally involve developing a non discharge protocol
The most practical protocol for eliminating discharge of dewatering effluent is ground
disposal by infiltration If space on the site permits a temporary dewatering discharge pit
may be excavated on the site and the effluent discharged to the pit The pit should be
sized to accommodate a full day s discharge with allowances for freeboard decreased
infiltration rates with clogging and excessive dewatering rates
If space for a temporary discharge pit is not available consideration should be
given to disposal of the effluent by the back trench method in which the effluent is
discharged to the granular bedding of an existing trench or previously constructed trench

the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
hollow stem augers

examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plate A 1

general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
