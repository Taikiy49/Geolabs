EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project site is generally located at the Phases 1A & 1B areas within the 
Mauna`olu Estates development in the Makaha area in the District of Waianae on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1.  Based on the information provided, we understand that site 
grading work consisting of both cuts and fills will be performed to create building pads 
for the construction of about 20 new homes.  The proposed grading work is shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Goodfellow Bros. performed the site grading operations during the building pad 
construction for the project. The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, 
placement and compaction of fills and backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench 
excavation and backfill. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the over-excavated subgrades and 
areas designated to receive fills were scarified to a depth of about 12 inches, 
moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content, and 
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction, as appropriate.  Prior to 
placing the Waffleboxes at the building pads, the subgrade soils were generally 
moisture-conditioned to about 4 percent above the optimum moisture content, compacted 
to at least 90 percent relative compaction, and covered with a vapor barrier to maintain the 
soil moisture. 
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level 
lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent 
relative compaction.  The compaction requirement was increased to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction for areas subjected to vehicular traffic.  Relative 
compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the 
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by weight) corresponding to the 
maximum dry density. 
New imported fills required for the project generally consisted of 3-inch minus 
non-expansive select granular material imported from Grace Pacific Quarry.  During 
placement of the non-expansive select granular fill material, the fill materials were 
generally placed in 8-inch near-level loose lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). The compaction 
requirement for the fill materials below the pavements was increased to a minimum of 95 
percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor). 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory

Field density tests were performed during the fill operations on an as-need basis to

the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.33 of this report. 
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills at

on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only 
to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were 
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained by retesting and/or 
probing with a steel rod.

were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications, and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced reports, dated August 9, 2017 and August 27, 2018, addendum dated 
June 12, 2020, and subsequent recommendations.
