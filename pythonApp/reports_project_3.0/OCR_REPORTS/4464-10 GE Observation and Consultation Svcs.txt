Dear Mr Moore
In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
services during the construction of the Kings Shops Expansion project in Waikoloa South
Kohala Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services performed during the construction of the project between May 2000
through November 2000 In general our services were coordinated and requested through
your Office
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Waikoloa Beach Resort in the District of South
Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The Kings Shops Expansion consisted of the construction
of two new commercial retail buildings of approximately 18 000 square feet in the area of the
existing parking lot In general the site grading for the project consisted of minor cuts and
fills on the order of about 4 feet or less The general contractor for the expansion project was
U S Pacific Builders and the sitework subcontractor for the construction of the Kings Shops
Expansion project was
Site Preparation
In general the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing subgrade preparation
placement and compaction of fills and retaining wall construction After clearing the fill
areas and finished subgrades in cut areas were generally proof rolled with a large vibratory
drum roller During the construction of the project samples of imported materials utilized as
fill and backfili materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2

The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the new parking lot and
roadway areas the fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the grading and utility trench backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 9 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation
Fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus structural fills were also placed in the fill
areas of the project Due to the nature of the larger sized fill materials the compaction of
the 6 inch minus materials were evaluated based on field observations during the fill
placement During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the earthwork operations for the Kings Shops Expansion project observed by Geolabs
in the period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent
of the plans and specifications
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site In addition because our services were on an
intermittent basis some of the earthwork was performed without our observation Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans
and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
