Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
Proposed UV Disinfection Facility
Kailua Regional Wastewater Treatment Plant
Kailua Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have
completed the field exploration work pertaining to the proposed UV Disinfection Facility
project located within the existing Kailua Regional Wastewater Treatment Plant This
letter summarizes our work performed thus far on the project and presents our preliminary
findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the subject project
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Kailua Regional Wastewater
Treatment Plant WWTP in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided it is proposed to expand the existing Kailua WWTP with the
construction of an UV Disinfection Facility The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The proposed UV Disinfection Facility will be a U shaped concrete channel
measuring approximately 45 feet long and 8 feet wide in plan dimension The top of the
concrete channel will be set at an elevation of about 7 82 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The channel will be deepened at both ends with a slab top elevation of about 5 86 feet
MSL The remaining channel area will be higher with a slab top elevation of about
0 43 feet MSL The new UV module weighing approximately 14 000 pounds will be
located at the center of the concrete channel In addition we understand that new piping
and junction boxes will be installed to connect the new UV Disinfection Facility to the
existing Solids Contactor and 54 inch reinforced concrete pipe

Based on the information provided we understand that the new facility has a

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 to depths of about
31 5 and 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Our field exploration disclosed that the proposed UV Disinfection Facility is
underlain by highly variable subsoil conditions along its alignment Based on our past
project experience in the vicinity of the project site we believe that the project site has
been previously excavated to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface during the construction of the adjacent clarifiers and solids contactors The site
was subsequently backfilled to the present elevations using various types of fill materials
placed to varying densities and consistencies
Our field exploration indicated that highly compressible soft silty clays and loose
clayey sands were encountered in the drilled borings at a depth of about 10 feet below
the existing ground surface which corresponds to an elevation of about 2 feet MSL or
at about the invert elevation of the channel slab Previous borings drilled in the vicinity of
the project site suggest that highly compressible soils exist and extend to depths of over
60 feet below the existing ground surface Below the upper fill soils our borings generally
encountered alluvial deposits consisting of stiff gravelly clays extending to the maximum
depth explored of approximately 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 6 5 to 7 3 feet below the existing
ground surface at the time of our field exploration These depths generally correspond to
elevations of approximately 0 4 to 1 5 feet MSL Due to the proximity of the project site
to the shoreline and the Pacific Ocean it is likely that the water levels measured in the
borings during our field exploration were affected by tidal influences In addition the
water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm
surge conditions

Pile Foundations
Based on the highly compressible soil deposits encountered near the invert
elevation of the concrete channel and the stringent settlement tolerance allowed by the
new facility we believe that driven pile foundations would be required for support of the

proposed new UV Disinfection Facility planned at the subject site Based on our field
exploration and engineering analyses we recommend that 12 inch square pre cast
prestressed concrete piles be used to support the proposed concrete channel connecting
piping and junction boxes The piles would derive support primarily from end bearing on
the basalt rock formation underlying the site as encountered in our previous borings
drilled in the vicinity of the project site An allowable compressive load capacity of about
70 tons may be used for the recommended pile foundation design The allowable
compressive load capacity for the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces In general piles should
be spaced a minimum of 3 feet center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity due
to group action
For preliminary design and budgeting purposes it is our opinion that the piles
should be driven to a depth of about 100 to 110 feet below the existing ground surface to
develop the recommended pile capacity The estimated pile lengths are based on the
subsurface conditions encountered in our previous borings drilled in the vicinity of the
project site and the actual driven pile lengths for the adjacent Clarifier No 3 It is
anticipated that pile splicing using simple non mechanical type splice cans will be needed
for driving the long piles Therefore we recommend that the upper pile section be a
minimum of 50 feet long since the channel excavation and pile cut off will remove the
upper 10 to 20 feet of soil and pile
Predrilling
9 a op a
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that the piles may be
installed without the need for predrilling However we understand that impacts on
the operations of the existing structures due to vibrations generated from the pile
driving operations would be of great concern Therefore predrilling at the
proposed pile locations should be performed prior to pile installation to reduce the
potential impacts on the operations of the adjacent structures during the pile
driving operations
Our field exploration indicated that soft and or loose soil deposits were
encountered below the surficial fill crust at a depth of about 10 feet below the
existing ground surface Therefore we recommend that the proposed pile
locations be predrilled a minimum of 10 feet below the existing ground surface to
reduce the vibration impacts on the existing structures The required predrilling
depth should be verified and or modified by Geolabs Hawaii during the pile driving
program depending on the conditions encountered and pile driving equipment
used

Uplift Load Resistance
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead load of the driven pile
and by shear along the pile surface and the adjacent soils Based on our estimate
the remaining 30 to 40 foot pile length of the upper pile sections may provide an
uplift capacity of up to about 12 kips per pile to help resist the buoyant uplift force
on the concrete channel The uplift load capacity includes the dead weight of the
pile
Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of pile foundations will result primarily from the elastic compression of
the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total settlement of the
pile supported foundation to be less than inch with differential settlements
between piles not exceeding about one half that amount We believe that these
settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
Pavement Design
We understand that new pavements will be required as a result of the proposed
construction For preliminary design purposes we have assumed that the new pavement
will be subjected to passenger vehicles light pick up trucks with occasional heavy
maintenance trucks
Our preliminary pavement design assumes that the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the near surface clayey fill soils encountered in the borings with a high
shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Based on the above
assumptions we recommend that the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Ultra Violet UV Disinfection Facility located within the
existing Kailua Regional Wastewater Treatment Plant in the Kailua area on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations are intended for the design of foundations site grading
shoring and dewatering and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kailua Regional Wastewater
Treatment Plant WWTP in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided it is proposed to expand the existing Kailua WWTP with
construction of the UV Disinfection Facility The approximate location of the new facility is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The proposed UV Disinfection Facility will be a U shaped concrete channel
measuring approximately 47 feet long and 25 feet wide in plan dimensions The top of
the concrete channel will be set at an elevation of about 4 62 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL with a slab elevation of about 1 28 feet MSL In addition we understand that new
60 inch diameter reinforced concrete pipes and two junction boxes will be installed to
connect the new UV Disinfection Facility to the existing 54 inch reinforced concrete pipes
Based on the information provided we understand that the new UV module has a
differential settlement tolerance of not more than 0 25 inches along its alignment Due to
the strict requirement for differential settlement special attention was given to the
consolidation characteristics of the soft loose lagoonal deposits present at shallow depths
and their effects on the foundation design of the new below grade structure

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lava in central Oahu
The project site is located at the base of the northeastern slope of Puu Papaa
which is essentially an erosional remnant of caldera filling lava flows of the Koolau
Volcano After the Koolau Volcano ceased its activity it was followed by a period of
erosion and subsequent submergence during which time most of the eastern flank of the
volcano was removed Later glacio eustatic sea level changes during the Pleistocene
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3918 00 Rev Page 5
Epoch resulted in the accumulation of several hundred feet of marine and non marine
sediments
Previous field explorations at the wastewater treatment plant indicate that the
southwestern upper portion of the site is underlain by highly weathered rock and
saprolite soils derived from the Koolau Volcano flows while the central and northeastern
lower portions of the site are underlain by interbedded sediments of both detrital marine
and alluvial origins During recent years development of the treatment plant has shaped
the land to the present form by grading and fill placement

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of
about 31 5 and 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Pian Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the proposed UV Disinfection Facility site is
generally underlain by highly variable subsoil conditions along its alignment Based on
our past experience in the vicinity of the project site we believe that the project site has
been previously excavated to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface during construction of the adjacent clarifiers and solids contactors The site was
subsequently backfilled to the present elevations using various types of fill materials
placed to varying densities and consistencies We understand that the previous
excavations were shored and dewatered for the backfilling operations

clayey sands were encountered in the drilled borings at a depth of about 10 feet below

60 feet below the existing ground surface Below the upper fill soils our borings generally

ground surface at the time of our field exploration These depths generally correspond to
elevations of approximately 0 4 to 1 5 feet MSL Due to the proximity of the project site

borings during our field exploration were affected by tidal influences In addition the
water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm
surge conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site appears to be underlain primarily
with fill material extending to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface Soft silty clays and loose clayey sands were generally encountered near the
design invert elevations of the UV module concrete channel the new 60 inch concrete
pipes and the junction boxes Our previous exploration in the vicinity of the project site
suggest that highly compressible soils exist and extend to depths of over 60 feet below
the existing ground surface
Based on the highly compressible soil conditions anticipated at the invert elevation
and the stringent settlement tolerance allowed by the new UV module equipment we
believe that driven pile foundations would be required for support of the channel slab for
the new UV module equipment The new building enclosure over the UV module
equipment should also be supported on driven pile foundations For preliminary
foundation design and cost estimation purposes we recommend that 12 inch square
precast prestressed concrete piles driven to a depth of about 100 to 110 feet below the
existing ground surface be used for the proposed facility An estimated compressive load
capacity of up to 70 tons per pile may be developed by end bearing on the basalt rock
formation underlying the site at greater depths
Due to the potential for presence of soft loose soils near the invert elevations of the
new 60 inch concrete pipes and junction boxes we believe that driven pile foundations
should also be used for support of the new pipeline and junction boxes In order to
facilitate the construction of the pile supported concrete cradle over the soft loose soil
deposits a 12 inch thick layer of No 2 Rock ASTM C 33 No 4 gradation over a layer of
non woven filter fabric should be placed on top of the soft loose soil deposits for more
uniform support Details of our geotechnical engineering recommendations are further
discussed in the following sections of this report

Pile Foundations
Based on the compressible soil deposits encountered near the invert elevation of
the concrete channel and the stringent settlement tolerance required by the new
UV module we believe that driven pile foundations would be required for support of the
proposed new UV Disinfection Facility planned at the subject site Based on our field
exploration and engineering analyses we recommend that 12 inch square precast
prestressed concrete piles be used to support the proposed concrete channel and the
building enclosure The piles would derive support primarily from end bearing on the
basalt rock formation underlying the site at greater depths as encountered in our
previous borings drilled in the vicinity of the project site
An allowable compressive load capacity of up to 70 tons per pile may be used for
the recommended pile foundation design The allowable compressive load capacity for
the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as wind or seismic forces In general piles should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet
center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity due to group action
For preliminary design and cost estimation purposes we believe that the piles
should be driven to depths of about 100 to 110 feet below the existing ground surface to
develop the recommended pile capacity The estimated pile lengths are based on the
subsurface conditions encountered in our previous boring drilled in the vicinity of the
project site see Appendix C and the actual pile embedment depths for the adjacent
Clarifier No 3 It is anticipated that pile splicing using simple non mechanical type splice
cans will be needed for driving the long piles Therefore we recommend that the upper
pile section be a minimum of 50 feet long since the channel excavation and pile cut off
will remove the upper 10 to 20 feet of soil and pile assuming that the piles wiil be driven
from the surface

Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of pile foundations will result primarily from elastic compression of the
pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total settlement of the
pile supported foundation to be less than 0 5 inches with differential settlements
between piles not exceeding about one half that amount We believe that these
settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of about 30 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control such that driving within the loose soft soils may be controlled
at a lower hammer energy to reduce tensile stress development within the piles
which could damage or cause cracking to the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be
driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy
to the top of the pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs Hawaii
should review the capability of the pile driving equipment proposed for installing
the piles to the recommended capacity The contractor should forward pile and
driving equipment data forms to Geolabs Hawaii prior to construction for
evaluation Geolabs Hawaii should review the submittal and assess the
driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer Due to possible variation
in the length of the piles unit prices should be obtained during bidding for add ons
shorter piles etc
Pile Driving Criteria
For preliminary design purposes pile driving may be terminated when aggregate
blow counts are at least 60 blows per foot for the last foot or 5 blows per inch for
the last 6 inches This preliminary driving criteria should be confirmed after the
selection of the pile driving equipment by performance of a wave equation
analysis
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3918 00 Rev Page 12
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be
rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is
interrupted for more than four hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required
depth During soft driving such as when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the
hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant
tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
It should be noted that the pile design was developed from our pile analysis using
the available field exploration and pile driving data Because variations in soil
strength characteristics are likely to exist in the bearing soils the supporting
capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field by a representative from our
office during driving Due to the relatively small number of piles anticipated for the
proposed facility we believe that a test pile program and pile load test would not
be practical for the proposed construction Therefore we recommend that the
production piles be casted a minimum of 10 feet longer than the recommended
pile length to account for the potential variation in subsoil conditions at the subject
site
We recommend that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii be present during all
pile driving operations to observe the actual driving behavior and to further
evaluate the field performance It should be noted that the pile design and
driving criteria recommended were developed from our pile analysis using the
field exploration data Therefore observation of the pile driving operations by
Geolabs Hawaii is necessary to confirm our design assumptions

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipes
Loads on buried pipes are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between
the backfill material and the trench walls To calculate the vertical loads on the
buried concrete pipes an average unit weight of 120 pcf for backfill material and
a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are recommended For the rigid reinforced
concrete pipes earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench Therefore the width of the trench should be kept to a minimum Traffic
loads on the buried pipes should also be considered for the portion of the pipes
located in roadway areas

Excavation Support
In general we believe that interlocking steel sheet piling with horizontal bracing
may be used for excavation support and to reduce the potential for adjacent
ground movement It should be noted that during and after installation of the sheet
piles minor settlements may occur Therefore it is recommended that a qualified
geotechnical engineer be retained by the Contractor to design and evaluate the
shoring system used
It is important that adequate sheet piling be installed prior to excavation and
maintained tight against the excavation walls with proper bracing during
excavation The properly braced sheet piling is essential to reduce lateral
movements of the adjacent ground into the excavation which may result in
potential settlements or distress to adjacent structures or other improvements
such as roads or utilities
The steel sheet piling should be driven with a suitable hammer to a depth sufficient
to provide stability of the excavation bottom and to reduce the possibility of bottom
heaving or quick conditions which may be caused by upward seepage pressure
during dewatering Based on our estimate the sheet piling should be driven to a
minimum penetration depth of 12 feet below the trench bottom along the proposed
alignment The recommended sheet pile penetration depth is based on the
assumption that sump pumping inside the excavations is used to dewater the
excavations The use of a deep well system outside the excavation to draw down
the natural groundwater level to reduce the required sheet pile embedment should
not be allowed considering the soft soil conditions at the site
For estimating earth loads in the struts of the trench bracing the apparent

to surcharge equipment and traffic adjacent to the excavations should be added

The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the Contractor His Her representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequence or techniques etc which may affect shoring
stability
However it must be noted that some minor movements of the shoring system and
the adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation Due to the complexity of the stress changes it is difficult to accurately
estimate the magnitude of movement The magnitude also depends greatly upon
workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports
are installed the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the rate of
excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed
properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent ground
should be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
movements with instruments

Dewatering of excavations will be necessary where the existing groundwater level
is above the bottom of the proposed excavation Therefore we recommend that
dewatering provisions be included in the contract documents for construction Since the
excavation will involve discharge of groundwater a National Pollutant Discharge
Elimination System NPDES permit may be necessary The Contractor should consult
their independent consultant for the latest regulations and information pertaining to the
permit application

Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the Contractor The selection
of equipment and methods of dewatering should be left up to the Contractor and he she

any combination of the above and or other possible methods
Dewatering Considerations
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selection of a
suitable method of dewatering
a The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance or
damage to existing structures roads and environment
b The dewatering method should maintain stability of and also provide
safe and dry working conditions in the excavation
C The dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow
modifications to accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering Precaution and Monitoring
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained
by the Contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used

The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the Contractor His Her qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during
construction and to implement as soon as possible necessary precautionary or
remedial measures including but not limited to stopping or slowing down the
dewatering operations
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with shoring installation such that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in
plowing or heaving of the excavation bottom or sides
Groundwater drawdown outside the excavation will cause additional settlement
resulting from consolidation of the loose soft compressible soils Therefore the
use of a deep well system outside the excavations to draw down the groundwater
level should not be allowed
Special caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches
connected to excavations If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in
the utility trenches could act as subdrains and cause significant areal groundwater
drawdown resulting in settlements and potential damage to utility lines and or
other existing adjacent structures

Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis test ASTM C 136 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on Plate B 2
Two consolidation tests ASTM D 2435 were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the on site soils The test results are
presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
