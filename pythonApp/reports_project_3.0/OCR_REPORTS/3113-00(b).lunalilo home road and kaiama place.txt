SUMMARY REPORT
SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
LUNALILO HOME ROAD AND KAIAMA PLACE
HAWAII
In accordance with your request Geolabs Hawaii has conducted a soil resistivity
survey for a portion of the Lunalilo Home Road and Kaiama Place in Hawaii Kai on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated May 26 1993 This report presents the results of our soil resistivity survey
performed on November 11th and summarizes our work on the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the northern end of Kamiloiki Valley in Hawaii Kai on
the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed
to install approximately 1 650 lineal feet of new water mains and appurtenances beneath
all of Kaiama Place and a portion of Lunalilo Home Road as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 We understand that ductile iron and or polyvinyl chloride PVC pipes may be
used for the water main installation

In general the average depth of the proposed waterlines is anticipated to be
between 4 and 5 feet below the existing ground surface It was desired to perform soil
resistivity tests on the subsurface soils to evaluate the corrosion rating and anticipated
corrosion activity Because the ground surface at the site was paved it was not possible
to perform in situ field resistivity tests Instead borings were drilled along the proposed
waterline alignment to obtain soil samples for the soil resistivity testing
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion
Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and subsequent amendments and additions
The scope of work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of five shallow borings at the project site to depths of
approximately 4 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Geologist from our office
4 Laboratory resistivity testing of the samples obtained during the field
exploration utilizing the Soil Box method in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation G 57 78

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration was performed on under fair and dry
weather conditions Soil samples were obtained from five drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of soils by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California Sampler with a 140 pound
hammer falling 30 inches The borings were drilled in the roadways at approximately 300
foot intervals along the proposed new waterline alignment as shown on Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 Description and Identification of Soils
Visual manual Procedure by a Geologist from our office The logs of the borings are

encountered near refusal conditions at depths of about 31 feet below the ground surface
which would not allow us to obtain satisfactory samples for resistivity testing beneath this
layer Therefore our boring program was amended and soil samples were obtained in

Five Soil Box Resistivity Tests were performed in our laboratory on soil samples
retrieved at depths ranging from 2 to 3 feet below the ground surface Sample testing
was completed utilizing a Nilson model 400 Soil Resistivity Meter and a Horiba D 12 pH
Meter Results of the resistivity and pH testing as well as the corresponding corrosion
rating are presented in Plate 3 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the potential corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the
greater the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher
the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The soil resistivity data presented in this report are based upon laboratory test
results obtained from soil sampling by the means of drilled borings Variations of soil
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such
variations may not become evident until a later point in time during trench excavations
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided
in this report
The locations of the field borings and elevations to waterline inverts are
approximate having been estimated from the Plan and Profile for Lunalilo Home Road
and Kaiama Place prepared by R M Towill Corporation dated and
the Plan and Profile for Lunalilo Home Road Extension and Kaiama Place prepared by
Gray Rhee Associates Inc dated The locations of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
