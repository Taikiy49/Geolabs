INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this report is to evaluate the feasibility of
the currently planned subdivision development utilizing addi
tional soil data obtained from test pits dug at the site
The following report which was prepared by
was used as a reference for this project
Soils and Foundation Investigation
Kamiloiki Valley Unit 5 dated
August 2 1972
This report presents the results of our additional explorations
and recommendations for the site grading of the proposed develop
ment
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the extreme upper end of Kamilo
iki Valley The site covers the lower
slopes at the head of the valley
Access to the site is by Lunalilo Home Road which terminates
at the southern site boundary adjacent to Mariner s Village
Unit 3 to the west and a public park to the east
An existing drainage channel extends from the southern boundary
approximately 300 feet into the site The area surrounding
the drainage channel is partially graded and cleared The
remainder of the site is covered with dense weeds grass and
Keawe trees
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P
The ground surface is very rocky with some large boulders
exceeding 3 to 4 feet in diameter moderately abundant over
the entire site
Rock outcrops were noted in the upper areas of the valley
side slopes
The site is drained by a single small intermittent stream
running along the center of the valley floor and numerous
small steep gullies running down the valley walls The
stream was dry at the time of the field exploration
Generally the stream and valley floor slopes toward the
ocean at a grade of 12 1 H V The valley side slope
averages 4 1 H V
The elevation at the site ranged from about 70 to 230 feet
above sea level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to develop a total of 166 residential
lots by cutting into the valley side slopes and filling the
lower central portion of the site
The preliminary grading plan indicates cuts up to about 25
feet and fills up to about 15 feet are planned
A concrete drainage channel is planned through the central
portion of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 4
Relatively light 1 and 2 story single family residential
structures are anticipated for the project
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Twenty two 22 test pits were dug with a backhoe to depths
of 1 0 to 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing The test pit information was supplemented
by a geological reconnaissance of the site
The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the
field exploration test pit logs and laboratory test results
is given in the Appendix
Geology
Kamiloiki Valley is typical of the steep sided stream cut
valleys on the Leeward side of the Koolau Range Erosion
of the windward slope of the Koolau Range has removed most
of the headwater source of the rains and streams that ori
ginally carved this valley The initial period of erosion
was followed by several periods of relative rise and fall
of sea level which permitted the valley to fill with alluvium
followed by subsequent periods of erosion colluvium deposi
tion and formation of a clay soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 5
During a later stage of valley development submarine and
subaerial explosions of Koko Head Volcano deposited a layer
of volcanic ash and breccia Much of the ash and breccia
was eroded from the valley walls and mixed with the existing
boulder and clay deposits The Recent alluvial and colluvial
deposits have again developed a thin clay soil layer
soil Conditions
Based on the test pits and field observations the site is
generally blanketed by a thin layer of dark gray highly
expansive adobe clay Underlying this layer is a dense
boulder deposit with a variable soil matrix boulder collu
vium of variable thickness underlain by basalt bedrock
The surface layer of expansive adobe clay appears to be of
uniform thickness over the entire site averaging 2 to 3 feet
thick This layer also contained root material and a few small
boulders and cobbles
The boulder colluvium consisted mainly of large boulders with
a gray brown to brown clay silty clay or silty sand matrix
between the boulders Much of the boulder material was frac
tured and weathered although large unweathered boulders were
frequently encountered The thickness of this layer varies
considerably throughout the site but generally decreases in
thickness with increasing elevation along both sides of the
valley becoming less than 2 feet thick near the upper extent
of the proposed cut area

KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 6
The density and percentage of boulders in this layer increases
with depth The approximate relationship of the grading cuts
and bedrock locations are shown on the valley cross section
Plates 2 3
Groundwater was not observed in any of the test pits
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration indicates that the current site grading
development plan is feasible for the site
The expansive adobe clay layer appears to be relatively thin
at the site The adobe clay can be removed and utilized to
construct the lower portion of the fills The underlying boulder
colluvium and lava rock can be used to cap off the adobe clay
Fill
Site Grading
Upper Slope Cut Area
From the field exploration and preliminary grading plans
it appears that except for the northern and southern por
tions of the site most of the proposed cut slopes will
probably be in the lava rock formation In the northern
and southern portions of the site the cut slopes will
probably be in the boulder colluvium or partially in the
boulder colluvium and partially in the lava rock formation
GEOCLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P
The following cut slope ratios are recommended
Hard unweathered or slightly
weathered lava rock 35 1 H V
Weathered lava rock 1 17 H V
Dense boulder H V
Thin layers of adobe clay encountered at the
top of the cuts should be cut back to 3 horizontal
to 1 vertical ratio
The approximate location where the lava rock formation is
anticipated within a few feet of the ground surface is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The location is very
approximate and is only to be used as a guide in the
design of the project site grading
Field adjustments will be necessary if other materials or
buried gullies are encountered during the site grading
All cut slopes in rock and boulder colluvium should be
inspected by a soils engineer to evaluate the competence
of the material
To minimize erosion surface runoff should be diverted by
berms or ditches away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting should be considered

KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 8
Lower Cut Areas
ce
The lower cut areas will be partially in lava rock and
mostly in the dense boulder colluvium Where the cuts
are less than about 2 feet expansive adobe clay may
be encountered The adobe should be removed a minimum
of 3 feet below pavements and building construction areas
Construction of utility lines in the boulder colluvium
will be difficult The boulder colluvium should be
removed 3 feet below the finished grades and replaced
with non expansive material
Fill Areas
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
The matrix of the boulder colluvium consisted mostly of
non expansive or low expansive material However in some
areas moderately expansive material were also encountered
The matrix material generated from the excavated boulder
colluvium should be stockpiled for use in the capping layer
The low or non expansive material could be used under pave
ments and structures while the moderately expansive material
should be used outside of building and pavement areas
The adobe clay should be used in the lower fill areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P
Adobe clay should be kept a minimum of 3 feet below
pavement and building finished grades
In the stream area loose boulders should be removed
prior to filling operations To fill the voids between
the boulders a layer of granular material should be
thoroughly watered and tamped over the existing boulders
prior to general filling
To reduce underground erosion and piping action on the
new fill a subdrainage system consisting of perforated
pipe and granular fill material around the pipe should
be provided at the streambed location
It is anticipated that a large quantity of boulders will
be generated from the cutting of the lava rock formation
and boulder colluvium Sufficient fines may not be
generated to produce a satisfactory fill material or
capping layer The feasibility of crushing the site
boulders for use as fill material should be studied
Additional general guidelines for the filling operations
are as follows
1 Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal
lifts compacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 10
2 Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be
keyed and benched into the existing firm
ground
3 Imported borrow material should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer
Foundations
For the sloping areas spread footing foundations are recommended
The footings should be placed on rock or extended below the ground
surface such that the outer edge of the footing has a minimum hori
zontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face
On flat areas spread footing foundations or slab on grade may be
used Slabs should be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum
of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should have a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet
from the slope face Post footings away from the tops of
Slopes should be placed 12 inches below the finish grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 3000 p s f can be used for
footings resting on the stiff natural soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 11
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be used on level areas where the slab subgrade
soil is non expansive structural fill material at least 3 feet
thick It should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be constructed
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on
rock and partly on fill the rock should be excavated 1 foot
and backfilled with compacted select on site material
Drainage Channel
A concrete drainage channel about 10 feet wide and up to 15
feet deep is planned through the central portion of the site
The channel foundations should not rest on the adobe clay
soil If encountered at the footing level the adobe should
be removed 2 feet below the footing and replaced with compacted
select on site material An allowable bearing value of 3000
p S f can be used for footings resting on the dense boulders
or compacted fill
A 6 inch layer of granular cushion material should be provided
below the structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 12
Well graded granular material should be used as backfill
material behind the channel walls Weepholes should be
provided in the channel walls to reduce the buildup of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
The channel walls should be designed to resist lateral
loads using an equivalent fluid pressure of 40 p c f
Additional load due to sloping surcharge and traffic
should be included where applicable
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the development
Based on the results of the laboratory CBR tests and field
observation the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow Subbase
12 inch Borrow
26 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The total pavement thickness can be substantially reduced if
select material is used in the upper 2 feet below the roadway
pavement areas
CBR tests should be performed on the proposed fill material
during construction and the design section can be revised
based on these tests

KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 13
Rockfalls
There is a possibility of individual boulders rolling down
from the hillsides Some means to reduce this hazard is
recommended The natural trees along the upper portions
of the site should be retained to act as a natural barrier
against rolling boulders
A chain link fence and boulder catchment area about 10 to
15 feet wide could be utilized near the property boundary
to reduce the boulder hazard The planned interceptor ditch
along the upper boundary of the site could be used in this
capacity
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi
cations
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the adobe removal and
placement slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
KAMILOIKI ESTATES P 14
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
