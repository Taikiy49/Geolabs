As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of three 3 borings
drilled within the proposed building site laboratory soil tests and
a review of the grading and compaction test record on the building
site This report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure approximately 50 feet wide by 54 to 65 feet long in plan
dimension Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the house
will be set back about 20 feet from Kahena Street

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 5 is located on Kahena Street in the southeastern portion of the
subdivision development about 350 feet north of Ainapo Street It is
bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest subdivision lots on the
east and west and a concrete drainage channel on the north The lot
slopes downward from Kahena Street toward the north at about 4 percent
gradient The elevation of Lot 5 generally varies from 139 to 147
feet The existing rectangular concrete drainage channel is about 10
feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending across the
rear portion of the lot

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown clay fill The laboratory test results on this material
generally indicated that they have high expansion potential There
fore for Lot 5 the slab on grade and foundation construction would
require special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the finished grade and backfilled with on site
soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density The
footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled and
compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area It appears
that the garage area would require fill from about one to 2 feet in
order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage The on site
clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the expansive
clays should be overexcavated down to a depth of 3 feet below the slab
subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a mimimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over
excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select non
expansive material is required The above capping requirements should
be followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow
for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 4
percent gradient
Minor site grading is planned for the lot development
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
clay soil should not be used for fill material
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
Monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
