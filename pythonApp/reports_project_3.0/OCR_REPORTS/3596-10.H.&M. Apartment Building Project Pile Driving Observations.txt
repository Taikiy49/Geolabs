A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed for the subject project and the
results are presented in the above referenced report The purpose of our observations is to
monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation installation for
conformance with the recommendations presented in our report The project Architect is
James K Tsugawa Associates and the project Structural Engineer is Sato Associates
Inc The pile driving contractor is Lahaina Pier Pile Company Inc
Project Description
The project site is located on the Diamond Head side of the Waikiki area on the island
of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The subject parcel is
bounded approximately by Liliuokalani Avenue on the Diamond Head side a high rise
apartment building Waikiki Lanais on the ewa side Mountain View Drive on the mauka side
and Tusitala Street on the makai side It is proposed to construct a high rise apartment
building with 4 levels of above ground parking base structure and 22 floors of residential living
units for a total of 26 stories above the ground surface

Based on our geotechnical engineering exploration and the design concept it appears
that the loose and compressible lagoonal deposits underlying the site at shallow depths
cannot provide adequate support for shallow foundations such as mat or spread footings
without inducing excessive settlements under the high structural loads of the proposed
high rise structure Therefore deep pile foundations end bearing on the hard basalt formation
was recommended for foundation support of the project A deep foundation system consisting
of 16 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed concrete piles with a design vertical compressive
load capacity of 150 tons per pile was recommended for support of the structure
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 21 piles at various locations across the
site was conducted from September 23 through 26 and on The purpose of
the test pile program was to establish the driveability of the piles given the subsurface
conditions at the site driving equipment and methods of installation In addition the program
also provides information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing stratum
across the project site which was used to estimate the production pile tip elevations Results
of the test pile program are presented in our Test Pile Driving Report dated
Production Pile Driving Observations
Based on the test pile driving observations and results the production pile locations
were generally predrilled to a depth of about 65 feet below the existing ground surface prior to
pile installation Due to the significant number of broken and hung up piles encountered
during the production pile driving operation the predrilling depth for the majority of the
replacement and additional piles were extended to a depth of about 80 feet below the existing
ground surface to facilitate the pile driving operations through the hard coral and dense
sandstone layers
A Delmag single acting diesel hammer Model D30 32 was used to drive the 16 inch
octagonal pre cast prestressed concrete piles Our field observations indicated that
Setting No 4 was used for the pile hammer during the production pile driving operation with a
maximum rated energy of approximately 69 900 foot pounds
A total of 187 production piles were driven between and March
24 1998 for the subject project The pile driving operations were conducted under the
near continuous observation of a field representative of our firm During the pile driving
operation logs were maintained for each pile which included information pertaining to pile
number date driven predrilled depth blow counts observations size and length of the
piles The locations of the production piles driven are shown on the attached Pile Location
Plan Plate 2 This included twelve replacement piles Pile 115B 140A
170A and 170B to replace the original piles which
were either lost or broken during the pile driving operations Two piles Pile Nos 195 and
196 were driven for the tower crane foundation
During the course of pile driving operations a significant number of the driven piles
were hung up at depths between about 65 and 80 feet below the existing ground surface

Therefore four additional probe holes ranging in depths from about 86 to 114 feet below
the existing ground surface were drilled in the vicinity of these hung up piles in an attempt
to better delineate the underlying subsoil conditions in these areas Based on the probing
results six existing piles Pile Nos 122 through 124 and 156 through 158 were restriked to
deeper depths and four additional piles Pile Nos 191 through 194 were added to provide
additional load bearing capacity for the foundation system The Pile Driving Summary is
presented chronologically in Appendix A and by pile number in Appendix B Individual pile
driving records have been transmitted to Okada Trucking Company Ltd
Sato Associates Inc and Lahaina Pier Pile Company Inc during the course of the pile
driving operations
It is our opinion that the piles for this project have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices Based on our observations of hammer performance and pile
driving behavior it is our professional opinion that the piles have been driven to sufficient
depths and or resistance to provide the design vertical compressive load capacity of 150 tons
per pile
