PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Kakaina Street from Waikupanaha Street to
Mahailua Street in the Waimanalo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to construct new
8 inch water mains and appurtenances along Kakaina Street from Waikupanaha Street to
Mahailua Street for water system improvement The two streets are shown on the Site
Pian Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known
at this time but they are expected to be about three feet below ground surface In order
to evaluate the need for corrosion control protection a field resistivity testing was
conducted along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions
and results of our corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter
report

the project site Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 95 and the requirements provided in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey Our field work was performed under sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Sixty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot
intervals at 17 locations designated as R 1 through R 16 along the left shoulder of
Kakaina Street and the right shoulder of Mahailua Street using the in Pin
Method and a resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 manufactured by Nilsson Electrical
Laboratory Inc Due to significant downhill slope of the right shoulder at test location
R 14 additional tests were performed on the left shoulder designated as test location
R 14A The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test
depth was assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity survey are presented on Plates 3 1
through 3 4

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
right shoulders of the existing roads Kakaina Street and Mahailua Street of the proposed
8 inch water mains The average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was
evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacing of
generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface The depth of penetration of a
resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing Since the depth of the water
main invert was not available at the time of our field exploration a spacing of 3 to 9 feet
was used for the electrode spacing for each soil resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report Field Resistivity
Testing Summaries are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 It should be noted that the soil
resistivity might be influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground
facilities and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test locations
indicate the subsurface soils along the first proposed water main within the project site
have corrosion ratings of 2 to 3 This indicates the subsurface soils to have very
corrosive to corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion
ratings of 1 and 4 were noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
