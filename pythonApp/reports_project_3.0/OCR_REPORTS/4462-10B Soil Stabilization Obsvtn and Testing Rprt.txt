Project Considerations
The Halekuai Center project is located at 565 Farrington Highway at the corner of
Kapouka Place in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves the
construction of a two story retail office building encompassing approximately
20 000 square feet in plan dimension Based on the existing topography at the project site
minor cuts and fills will be required to achieve the design finished grades at the site In
addition retaining structures will be constructed to provide for grade separation at the
perimeter of the site
Based on the laboratory swell test results the stiff clayey fill materials at the site
may be classified as moderately to highly expansive when subjected to fluctuations in
the moisture content As an alternate to the conventional removal and replacement
alternative we understand that EcSS 3000 Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer was used to
stabilize the expansive soils at the footprint of the two story retail office building
Halekuai Center It should be noted that soil stabilization was not performed for the
Chevron Site and was specifically excluded from our scope of work for this project

EcSS 3000 Electrochemical Soil Stabilization
The main cause of expansion in clayey soils is the tendency of individual soil
particles to attract and hold water Clay particles have a net negative electrical charge and
attract positively charged ions present in the soil water matrix In the attempt to attract
positive ions many layers of water molecules are held by the clay particle These layers of
adsorbed water force individual clay particles away from each other resulting in soil
expansion

An electrochemical stabilization system EcSS 3000 has been selected to reduce
the swelling potential of the on site expansive soils at the Halekuai Center site
EcSS 3000 formerly known as ISS 2500 is a mixture of naphthalene oil sulfur trioxide
sulfuric acid and other additives A proprietary resin catalyst is also added to enhance the
reaction times The ion exchange solution is mixed with water and introduced into the soils
under pressure through injection points drilled at a grid pattern of about 3 feet on center
EcSS 3000 creates a cation rich environment in the injected zone and the individual
clay particle s negative charge is effectively neutralized The structure of the clay particles
is changed physically and chemically from an expanding crystalline lattice to a
non crystalline amorphous structure to greatly reduce the ability of the clay particle to
attract and hold water This results in a great reduction in the shrink swell potential of the
clay soils
The soil stabilization operation was performed by Structural Systems Inc the
authorized local distributor of the EcSS 3000 Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer between the
period from October 5 to 19 2000 for a total of 11 working days Prior to the injection
operation a grid pattern of 3 feet on centers in a staggered pattern was laid out in the field
extending a minimum of 5 feet beyond the building lines Hand held injection rods were
manually forced downward with a hammer in approximately 12 inch vertical intervals up to
a depth of about 6 feet below the existing ground surface or upon refusal at the injection
points
We understand that a diluted EcSS solution at a ratio of about 3 percent 1 gallon of
chemical to 300 gallons of water was injected at each one foot interval into the underlying
expansive soils at a pressure of about 200 to 250 pounds per square inch psi The
injection points were offset by about 6 to 12 inches if obstruction was encountered at
shallow depths during the injection operation The respective treatment areas during each
day of the treatment period are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Post Treatment Evaluation Program
A post treatment evaluation program was conducted by our office upon completion
of the EcSS injection operations by Structural Systems Inc Six boreholes designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 6 were drilled on October 20 extending to depths ranging between
about 2 8 and 10 3 feet below the existing ground surface The boreholes were selectively
located across the project site to obtain a wide range of treated soil samples to evaluate
the effectiveness of the stabilization operations under different curing times The
approximate locations of our borings are also shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were retrieved by using thin walled seamless
tube samplers and were obtained by near continuous sampling from the existing ground
surface down to the maximum depth drilled at each boring location A total of 19 selected
soil samples obtained from our field exploration were then subjected to one dimensional
swell tests conducted in general accordance with ASTM D 4546 MethodB as

Based on the laboratory test results it appears that the soil samples tested meet the
specification requirements with an average swell of not more than 1 percent and no
individual swell except for Test No 11 of more than 2 percent We believe that the
slightly higher swell test result obtained for Test No 11 may be attributed to the relatively
short curing time Therefore it is our opinion that the EcSS 3000 Electrochemical
Stabilization has effectively reduced the swelling potential of the on site expansive soils
and construction work for the Halekuai Center may proceed accordingly
