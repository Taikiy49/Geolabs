INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Circle K Store in Waimea on the
island of Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated March 4 1988
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at the
site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 65 foot store
and a pump island with gasoline dispenser units To supply the gas
island an underground gasoline storage area consisting of three
underground storage tanks is planned for an area adjacent to the pump
island The convenience facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic
concrete parking lot A drainage pit is planned for surface runoff
disposal of the project area
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil
data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the design of site grading foundation and subsurface runoff
disposal at the project site
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Circle
K Food Store c o Kauahikaua Chun Architects dated March 4 1988

the project site and its vicinity

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northeastern corner of the
junction of Mamalahoa Highway and Kinohou Street in Waimea Kamuela
Hawaii
The site measures approximately 170 by 130 feet in plan dimensions
and is generally flat with ground elevations varying between 2738 and 2743
feet above Mean Sea Level
Presently the site is vacant with vegetation consisting of grass
shrubs and small trees Debris piles composed of tree cuttings tree
stumps and other vegetation were encountered in the southwestern and
northeastern portions of the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
At the time of report preparation a final grading plan was not
available However minimal site grading is anticipated All imported
fill material should be non expansive free of organic and other
deleterious matter and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted to
the required density Generally the soil particles should be well graded
in size with individual particles less than 6 inches in maximum
dimension Additionally the imported fill material should have a minimum
CBR value of 15 The fill should be placed in maximum 12 inch loose level
lifts and compacted to 90 of maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed including any required
demolition Any unsuitable material such as vegetation rubbish and
other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
The existing surface soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of
6 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of their maximum ary density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft yielding or pumping areas noted during the
compacting process should be excavated to firm material and backfilled
with suitable material placed in 6 inch compacted lifts and compacted as
recommended above

Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Foundations
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered medium stiff to
stiff clayey silt volcanic ash which can provide adequate support for
the proposed lightly loaded Circle kK Store building and pump island
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings may be used to support the
proposed structures
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable
bearing value up to 2500 p s f may be used for foundations bearing on the
compacted in situ soil or fill The bearing capacity is for total dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind and
seismic loads
Resistance to lateral loads on the building can be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 3 may be used to calculate the friction between the
bottom of the footings and the soil In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive earth resistance against the buried vertical faces of the
footings The soil around the perimeters of the footings should be
properly compacted in order to provide the recommended passive resistance
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine rock to provide uniform
Support A moisture barrier membrane may be used under the slabs if

protection for the floor covering is desired The thickened edges of the
on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade The backfill against the concrete slab should be
adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three 3 8 foot diameter storage tanks in an
area adjacent to the pump island
Based on our field exploration the tanks will probably bear on the
underlying ash clinker zone which can provide adequate support Any
boulder size material encountered in the excavation should be disposed of
properly off site
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field exploration the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course Basalt Aggregate
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section may be modified if
necessary
Subsurface Runoff Disposal
It is our understanding that a drainage pit is required for surface
runoff disposal at the project area The most appropriate drainage layer
is the ash cinder and spatter layer generally encountered at about 13 feet
deep in our exploration

Based on our field percolation test performed in Boring B 3 the
measured coefficient of permeability is on the order of 6 5 x 1073
gal day sq ft The actual size of the subsurface
disposal system will depend on the design quantity of the future surface
runoff The details of the field percolation test are presented in
Appendix C
It should be pointed out that for the subsurface disposal system
the loss of infiltrative capacity and soil clogging can occur in the
future by combined physical chemical and biological factors Other
factors such as rainfall perched groundwater level etc may also
affect the permeability to some extent Therefore the disposal system
may probably require periodic maintenance
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the preliminary Site Plan undated provided by
Kauahikaua Chun Architects on Elevations of the data
points were determined by interpolation from elevation points on the
provided plan The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the site grading and design of
foundations and subsurface disposal system of the project in accordance
with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and
practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
