As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three 3
borings within the lot performing laboratory soil tests and
reviewing the grading plan This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
Based on the preliminary house plans provided to us the proposed
single family residence will be a two story wooden structure
approximately 28 by 50 feet in plan dimensions The house will be set
back about 5 feet from the end of Kulani Street
W Page 2
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire lower level of the
house garage and driveway A preliminary grading or house elevation
plan was not available at the time of our study However it appears
that some amount of small cuts and fills generally less than 2 feet
thick would be required to level the building site
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 66 is located at the end of Kulani Street on the southwestern
portion of the Hale Kai View Estates subdivision development in
The subject lot is situated near the base of the eastern Hahaione
Valley side slope The lower eastern half of the lot slopes upward
toward the west at gradients of about 10 and increases to 20 in the
upper western half of the lot The elevation of the lot generally
varies from about 175 to 207 feet An existing concrete drainage
ditch is located on the northeastern and northwestern sides of the lot
During the subsurface investigation two large stockpiles of
unsuitable soil and decomposing organic matter were observed along the
southeast and western boundaries respectively In addition some
amount of loose fill and concrete rubble were noted along the eastern
boundary The general locations of these stockpiles are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown silty clay to clay and boulders The laboratory test results on
these clayey materials generally indicate that they have moderate to
high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 66 slab on grade
foundation would require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that slab on grade is planned for the
garage and the lower level of the house The on site clay soils
are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade soills In order
to reduce the expansive effect of the soils the expansive clays
encountered below and for a distance of 3 feet around the slab
should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below the outside
finished grade and replaced with compacted non expansive off site
borrow material such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
as shown on the Schematic Section Plate 2 The underlying
remaining clay soil should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content and recompacted moderately 85
compaction prior to backfilling with the 3 foot capping of
select fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
The excavated clay soil is expansive and therefore is not
suitable for re use as fill below structures pavement or as
backfill behind retaining walls
The slab on grade thickened edge should extend a minimum of 8
inches below the outside finished grade
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The finished grade outside the slab and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding conditions near the slab area Also the owner should be
made aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
perimeter footings and walkways
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Post And Beam
For this lot post and beam construction is desirable due to the
sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions The post and beam
construction would generally result in lower foundation costs and
the individual footings can be readily adjusted should such a
need occur
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
865 82 Page 6
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow
or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished ground surface
The footings should be poured in place and backfilled to form an
impervious layer over the foundations refer to the Schematic
Section Plate 3 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff undisturbed soil Design of
perimeter thickened edge footings for slab on grade are discussed
under the SLAB ON GRADE section
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations This is
especially important in the more level portion of the house to
reduce ponding of surface run off water that may affect expansive
soils around the foundation

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils more than 3 feet thick were encountered in the
eastern portion of the lot driveway area In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the clay it should be kept a minimum of 2 feet
below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is
planned select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap
over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the relatively long driveway planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
Cuts and fills for the lot development are anticipated to be generally
less than 3 feet
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
Fill and backfill materials should be select non expansive borrow
material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The
on site clay soil should not be used for fill material below
structures and behind retaining walls
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structure
Retaining Walls
Low retaining walls may be required The following design procedures
are recommended
1 To reduce expansive effects of the existing clay soils on the
wall we recommend that the expansive clays if encountered be
over excavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The over excavation and replacement should extend a minimum
distance of 12 inches beyond the toe of the footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
3
The retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Therefore the total depth of the above over excavation and
replacement would be 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finished
grade where adobe clay is encountered refer to Schematic
Section Plate 4 for details
To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay
should be over excavated for a distance behind the wall equal
to the height of the wall
Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
such as select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill
would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required
for the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure on the
walls Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be
used for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of
surface run off behind the walls
A subsurface drainage system such as subdrains or weepholes
should be utilized behind the wall and be designed to drain
freely under gravity to lower open areas away from foundations
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on compacted fill

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 82 Page 11
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
