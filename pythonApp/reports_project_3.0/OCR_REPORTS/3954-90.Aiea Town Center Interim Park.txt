AIEA TOWN CENTER INTERIM PARK
Dear Mr Arakaki
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the Aiea Town Center Interim Park within the existing Aiea Industrial
Subdivision The industrial subdivision is located within the former Aiea Sugar Mill site
located northeast of Ulune Street northwest of Kulawea Street and southeast of
Hakina Street in the District of Aiea on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our review of previous field exploration laboratory
testing and engineering analyses The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Aiea Town Center site is located within the Aiea Industrial Subdivision which
is the site of the former Aiea Sugar Mill The project encompasses Lot Nos 7 through 14
of the industrial subdivision Each of the lots was previously mass graded to create
relatively level pads for industrial use The eight lots will be re graded to create a uniform
slope across the site
Boring data from our previous explorations at the site were used as the basis for
our evaluation of the new site grading
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The site is bounded by Halewiliko Street to the west Pohai Place to the north
Halewiliko Place to the south and a vacant lot Lot No 6 to the east The eight lots were
graded to relatively level pads that stepped down 2 to 6 feet from east to west The site
was Clear but covered with low brush

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were previously explored by drilling
and sampling borings to depths in excess of 15 feet below the existing ground surface
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by heterogeneous fill
material ranging from brown clay to silty sand and gravel over basalt formation at 3 to
8 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in all of the borings except at Lot No 10 at the
time of our preliminary field exploration Perched water was encountered at a depth of
about 5 feet at Lot No 10 It should be noted that groundwater level at the project site
may fluctuate depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the preliminary grading plan indicates that the eastern portion of the
site will be cut up to about 3 feet and the western portion will be filled up to about 3 feet to
create a gently sloping surface from east to west

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork vegetation debris deleterious or other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with properly compacted fill material
The excavated soft soils should be disposed of properly off site
Prior to filling and at finished subgrades the ground should be scarified to a depth
of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction

Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or the
subgrade should be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and
poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
properly compacted fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage of dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Fills and Backfills
Fill material should consist of well graded on site materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension and free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Import fill and select granular fill materials if required should consist of
non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or
cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell value of
1 percent or less Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the fills due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with properly
compacted fill material
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Inc during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining
the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where
compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be applied
with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction

Slopes
Slopes should not exceed a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the
design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Utilities
The methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation
should comply with all applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench
shoring design and installation should be made the responsibility of the contractor
Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety
requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular
material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils
as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finish subgrade should consist of the on site soils or select granular fill material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of trench backfill below pavement subgrade
should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical
compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials Water tamping jetting
or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed grading should be forwarded to
Geolabs Inc for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bid The review

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Inc be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed project The items of
construction monitoring that are critical which require Special Inspection include
observation of the fill and backfill placement Other aspects of earthwork construction
should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs Inc This is to observe
compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this letter report was
prepared The recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent upon such
observations If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this letter report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this
letter report
