This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Ferry Dock Relocation project which is part of the USS Arizona
Memorial Visitor Center Replacement project at Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration and engineering analyses These
recommendations are only intended for the design of the pile foundations supporting the
relieving platform The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report Our work was performed in general
accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated May 27 2009
Project Considerations
The project site is on the northern bank at the downstream end of
Halawa Stream in Pearl Harbor We understand that the ferry dock location will be
further downstream
The relocation project consists of abandoning the existing east abutment bridge
constructing a new abutment bridge west of the existing west abutment bridge and
repairing and relocating the floating docks to the new ferry dock location The new
abutment will be supported on eight driven piles consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal
precast prestressed concrete piles
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling one boring
Boring No 1 to a depth of about 218 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We used a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools

Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the boring by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate 3 Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
provided on the Logs of Boring Plates 4 1 through 4 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to
drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer and torvane shear tests were performed on selected
cohesive soil samples retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an
indication of the unconfined compressive strength of the sample The torvane shear test
provides a quick estimate of the undrained shear strength of the soil Pocket
penetrometer and torvane shear test results are summarized on the Logs of Boring at
the appropriate sample depths
Subsurface Conditions
In general the boring encountered medium dense clayey gravel fill with varying
amounts of cobbles and boulders that extended to a depth of about 16 feet below the
existing ground surface Very soft to soft harbor deposits silty clay were encountered
below the fill and extended to a depth of about 127 feet below the existing ground
surface The harbor deposits were underlain by alluvial deposits which consisted of
very stiff to hard silts and clays The alluvial deposits extended to the maximum depth
explored of about 218 5 feet below the existing ground surface
We encountered groundwater in the boring at a depth of about 2 feet below the
existing ground surface during our field exploration Groundwater levels are expected
to vary with tidal fluctuation seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and other
factors

Pile Lengths
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the anticipated structural
loads we recommend supporting the relieving platform on 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles The 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles
may be designed with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to 35 kips per pile
The 16 5 inch octagonal pile would derive support mainly from skin friction from
the surrounding alluvial deposits The ongoing consolidation of the very soft to soft
harbor deposits would result in negative skin friction forces downdrag forces acting on
the pile shaft Based on our foundation analyses we estimate that the downdrag loads
imposed on the vertical piles due to consolidation of the soft to very soft soils may be on
the order of about 300 kips per pile To account for downdrag loads the pile would
have to extend to a depth of about 175 feet below the existing ground surface to
achieve an allowable compressive load capacity of 35 kips
The allowable compressive load capacity provided is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by
wind or seismic forces A factor of safety of 2 0 was used in arriving at the allowable
compressive load capacity of 35 kips Due to the relatively long pile length we
recommend using the following pile sections for the relieving platform dock

Pile Driving Criteria
We understand that Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc is proposing to use a
diesel hammer to install the relieving platform piles The Delmag
D30 23 has a ram weight of 6 6 kips and a maximum stroke of 10 feet Pump Setting 4
The maximum rated energy of the diesel hammer is about 66 1 kip feet when set at
Pump Setting 4
The hammer cushion would consist of conbest and aluminum with a total
thickness of 5 inches Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions
anticipated a pile cushion consisting of 6 inches of plywood cushion blocks is proposed
for the pile driving operations We understand that the hammer and pile cushions have
cross sectional areas of approximately 625 and 342 square inches respectively

In general Pump Settings 3 or 4 should be used in stiff soil conditions with the
diesel hammer for the pile installation Based on our engineering
analyses conducted for the project the following pile driving criteria may be used during
the pile driving operations
1 Drive to the full depth of 175 feet below the existing ground surface
2 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the pile driving
resistance meets the following blow counts at a minimum depth of about
150 feet below the existing ground surface
Pump Setting 3
e Last foot of penetration of at least 70 blows per foot or
e Last 6 inch of penetration of at least 7 blows per inch
Pump Setting 4
e Last foot of penetration of at least 55 blows per foot or
e Last 6 inch of penetration of at least 6 blows per inch
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected
or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more
than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth and or penetration
resistance During soft driving such as when the pile tip is within the soft soils the
hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile
stresses do not develop within the pile In general a Pump Setting of 1 should be used
when the pile tip is within the soft soils
