PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the project site in order to provide soil data for
the design and construction of the site grading and foundations of the
proposed single family residential development The general location
of the project site and its vicinity are show on Plate 1
The information obtained from our field exploration and laboratory
tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appendices of
this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1318 00 Page 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development site is situated on the eastern side of
Salt Lake Boulevard between the existing Foster Village Subdivision and
the Stadium Mall Shopping Center It is bordered by Naulu Place on the
north Ala 01i Drive on the south Wila Street on the east and Salt
Lake Boulevard on the west
Most of the site consists of two drainageway areas which extend
from Foster Village Subdivision towards the south They are generally
about 20 to 30 feet deep with side slopes varying from about 4 1 H V
to near vertical The drainageways join at the center of the site then
extend towards the west to Salt Lake Boulevard Water was not noted in
the drainageway areas at the time of our field exploration
The site is presently vacant and heavily overgrown The ground
elevations vary from 24 to 76 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the 10 3 acre site for a single
family residential development with 44 foot wide roadways to provide
access into the subdivision
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1318 00 Page 3
The preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation indicated that most of the site grading will consist of
filling the lower drainageway areas and cutting along the upper
perimeters of the site Fills up to 22 feet in thickness and cuts up
to 11 feet deep are anticipated
A retaining wall up to 8 feet high is planned along the driveway
for Lot 26
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the northwestern flank of the
Aliamanu Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series AS
the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with the
groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions
which deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to
form tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu eruptive event deposited its material over alluvium
originating from Halawa Stream and in doing so dammed the course of the
stream causing deposition of alluvium on the northern flank of the
Aliamanu cone
Subsequent to the deposition of this alluvium the Salt Lake
eruptive event deposited its tuff over this mantle of alluvium
overlying the Aliamanu tuff
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1318 00 Page 4
Stream erosion and diversion of the course of Halawa Stream has
shaped the area to its present topography
The drainageways appear to have cut completely through the tuffs
exposing the older alluvium throughout the central portions of the site
Subsurface Exploration
Eight borings were drilled at the proposed development site to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The borings were drilled to depths of 15 4 to 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with logs of borings and
laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
In general the geologic reconnaissance disclosed the presence of
medium hard tuff over alluvium in the upper elevations along the
perimeter of the site The borings in the lower drainageway areas
encountered alluvium consisting primarily of hard grayish brown clayey
silt with occasional sand gravel and cobbles Spot elevations of the
bottom of the tuff layer are indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1318 00 Page 5
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the time of
our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively competent subsurface
conditions at the site From a soils engineering point of view the
site is feasible for the proposed development site grading provided our
recommendations are followed
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation indicated that the major portion of the grading will be
fills up to 22 feet thick in the lower drainageway areas and cuts to
approximately 11 feet in the upper areas Import fill will be required
for the proposed grading
Site Preparation

Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill
should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
density as established by ASTM D 1557 test method

Fill Placement
Oded ee
Fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to obtain 90 of the above specified maximum density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into firm existing ground
Fill Material
Excavated on site material including tuff generally less than 8
inches in size may be used as a source of fill material
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive
soils and submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory
testing and approval prior to its intended delivery to the project
Site
Settlement
Very stiff alluvial soils were encountered in the lower portion of
the site Therefore major ground settlement is not anticipated
for fills placed over this area

Excavation
me ane ie ae
Based on our previous experience we believe the tuff material can
generally be rippable by a D 8 dozer or its equivalent However
some isolated pockets of hard ripping may be encountered Care
should be exercised during grading to avoid over ripping and
undercutting of the proposed finished grades Any areas which are
over excavated need to be backfilled to obtain 90 of its maximum
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Utility Trenches
Tuff formation was encountered in the upper portions of the site
If utility trenches are required to extend through these materials
moderate to hard excavation may be anticipated Some special
excavating techniques such as hoerams may be needed to excavate the
utility trenches
Normal excavation is anticipated in the very stiff to hard
alluvial materials encountered in the lower portions of the site
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize
subsequent ground surface settlement along the utility alignments All
backfill should be compacted to 90 of its maximum density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII

Steeper cut slope ratios up to 1 1 H V may be used in the
upper competent tuff mudrock formation For cut into the underlying
undisturbed alluvial materials the cut slope ratio should be reduced
to 2 1 H V
Slope ratios of 2 1 H V can be used for the proposed embankment
construction that consists of the above specified imported fill
material
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical height
8 foot drainage benches should be placed at the mid height of the slope
Surface runoff water should be diverted away from the top of the
Slope Slope planting should be incorporated as soon as practicable to
reduce erosion on fill slope as well as alluvial soil exposed on the
cut slope surface
Foundations
Both the on site soil and the compacted fill should be able to
provide competent foundation support for the proposed structures
For the proposed light residential structures spread footing
foundations or slab on grade may be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1318 00 Page 9
Slabs should be set back from the tops of the slopes a minimum
distance of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing
out to the slope face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
2500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on tuff alluvium or
compacted fill This value may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Retaining Wall
A retaining wall is planned along the driveway for Lot 26 Medium
dense tuff and alluvium are anticipated at the above area The
following guidelines for retaining wall foundation design and
construction are recommended
l The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the outside finished grade
2 Granular backfill such as select borrow crusher waste or
mudrock less than 8 inches in size should be used for the wall
backfill

only for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of surface
runoff behind the wall

utilized behind the wall
5 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the stiff natural soil
6 A friction factor of 0 4 for soil can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall In addition a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized

Slab On Grade
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at
this site slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface

To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
ployethylene moisture barrier should be considered
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut
in tuff and partly on fill or alluvial the cut portions should be
over excavated one foot and then recompacted to match the adjacent soil
density
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
Assuming on site excavated tuff or select off site borrow soils CBR
greater than 12 are used in the upper portion of the road fill the
following preliminary pavement design may be used for cost estimate
purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on

Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil during construction and the above design section can be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and
that good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
4 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drilling rig was used to drill the borings

and textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1l
to A 8
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split
hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
for 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
