Project Considerations
The project site is within the existing Keahole Power Plant Unit ST 7 at 73 4249
Queen Kaahumanu Highway in the Kailua Kona area on the Island of Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
site is near the southeast corner of the intersection of Queen Kaahumanu Highway and
Keahole Airport Road as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided we understand it is desired to conduct
seismic refraction survey at two selected lines across the project site using a
multi channel recording system The seismic refraction method is based on the
measurement of the travel time of seismic waves refracted at the interfaces between
subsurface layers of different velocities
Seismic energy is provided by a source shot located on the surface Energy
radiates out from the shot point either traveling directly through the upper layer direct
arrivals or traveling down to and then laterally along higher velocity layers refracted
arrivals before returning to the surface This energy is detected on the surface using a
linear array of geophones Observation of the travel times of the refracted signals
provides information on the depth profile of the refractor Detailed discussions and
findings are presented in the Field Exploration section of this letter report

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by the performance of two seismic
refraction survey lines on September 25 through 27 2007 The approximate locations of
the seismic refraction surveys are shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
Refraction seismography is based on the transmission of elastic energy
compression waves through geologic materials The seismic refraction surveys were
conducted using a 12 channel signal enhancement seismograph with a 12 pound sledge
hammer as a seismic energy source Generally the denser or more consolidated a
material is the faster the transmission of the compression waves P waves These waves
are refracted at interfaces between materials with contrasting densities The refracted
waves are received by geophones on the surface and recorded by the operator
The recorded data is input into a desktop computer for processing which involves
the interpretive selection of the arrival times of the refracted waves After the interpretive
selection of arrival times the technique permits mathematical calculations that yield
approximate depths or thickness of materials below the survey lines and provide their
approximate compression wave P wave velocities The velocities are averaged along the
