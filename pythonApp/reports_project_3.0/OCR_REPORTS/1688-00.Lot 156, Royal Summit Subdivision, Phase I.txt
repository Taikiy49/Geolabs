In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of the
swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house plans This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story wood frame structure
approximately 48 by 71 feet in plan dimensions A basement level with
CMU walls up to 8 feet high will be included
Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the garage driveway and
basement level of the house

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
otential of the soil
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
to moderate swelling potential depending on the moisture content of the
soil Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected
from drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete
placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural
soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is
anticipated such as the garage driveway and the basement areas the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum
thickness of non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site

Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil

Slab On Grade
Tt should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations if any

2 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
3 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff
on site soil
4 A passive soil resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be

should be ignored for walls on slopes
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the

7 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition lso it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
small slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operation be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
