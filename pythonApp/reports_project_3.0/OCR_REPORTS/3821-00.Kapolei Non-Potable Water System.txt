This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the earthwork operation pertaining to the non potable water system construction within
the Villages of Kapolei development from August 13 1997 through in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated May 29 1997
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located within the Villages of Kapolei development in the District
of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded to the north and south
by open land to the east by the existing Kapolei Lower Channel and to the west by the
future Makai Villages 7 and 8 The project involved the construction of two 1 0 Million
Gallon MG non potable reservoirs a baseyard with control building and wet well an
access and perimeter roadway and underground utilities
The earthwork operation for the non potable water system construction was
performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd The earthwork operation consisted of
clearing of the existing ground excavation to the design finished grades of the reservoirs
proof rolling and compaction of the excavated subgrades dressing of the finished slope
faces backfilling of utility trench excavations and placement of fill and base course below
pavement areas The finished slope faces of the reservoirs were subsequently
shotcreted to provide a relatively smooth surface for the installation of the reservoir lining
system

During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Following the clearing operations the reservoir site was generally over excavated
to approximately 15 feet below the existing ground surface The over excavated
subgrades were generally moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor During placement of fill and backfill materials the
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about 2 percent above the
optinum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 295 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2 1
through 2 13 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by taping and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction
plans These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operation during the Kapolei
Non Potable Water System construction at the above referenced site observed and
tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated May 3 1996 and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have occurred without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appear to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
