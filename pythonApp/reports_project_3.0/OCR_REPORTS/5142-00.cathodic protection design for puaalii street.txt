PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to obtain an overview of the corrosion characteristics
of the subsurface soils along the 4 inch and 8 inch water lines planned at the project site
Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95
the requirements provided in the Board of Water Supply Water System Standards
dated 2002 and the Water System External Corrosion Standards Volume 3 dated
1991 and all subsequent amendments and additions of the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house geologic and soils information
for the project vicinity
2 Coordination of the field exploration and performance of 42 in situ soil
resistivity surveys at 14 test locations along the grass area of the existing
roadways near the water main alignment corresponding to the 300 foot
maximum spacing required in the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991 The resistivity surveys were performed using
the Wenner 4 Pin Method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 95
3 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertaining to the
proposed construction
4 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project area
and presenting our findings and recommendations
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project engineer from
our firm
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project
site on for the field survey Our field work was performed under partly
cloudy conditions with generally damp to moist surface conditions
Forty two soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals
and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory
Inc The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivity
values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth was
assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on the Site
Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Results of the resistivity surveys are presented on Plates 3 1
through 3 7
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
grass area of the existing roadways of the proposed 4 inch and 8 inch water lines The
average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey
location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally 2 to 7 feet The depth
of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The pin
spacings closest to the pipe invert depths are noted with an on Plates 3 4 through 3 7
The resistivity tests were conducted within the planting strips adjacent to and off the
existing roadways
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 provides a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms is obtained from the voltmeter dial reading The soil resistivity
in ohm cm which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the
corresponding corrosion ratings based on the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991 are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 7 of this report The
computed resistivity values with pin spacings closest to the pipe invert depths at the test
locations are connected with red lines in the graphs on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 It should
be noted that the soil resistivity may be influenced by temperature weather conditions
existing underground facilities and other factors

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test results for the
subsurface soils along the proposed water lines within the project site have corrosion
ratings of 2to4 at the invert depths This indicates the subsurface soils have very
corrosive to moderately corrosive potential One isolated resistivity measurement with
corrosion rating of 1 was encountered at Puaalii Street Station No 12 60 This indicates
the subsurface soils have extremely corrosive to very corrosive potential
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this report
The field resistivity test locations indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site and Profile Plan provided by
Oceanit Laboratories Inc on Elevations of the field resistivity test
locations were estimated from the profile plan and elevation points shown on the same
plan The physical test locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
