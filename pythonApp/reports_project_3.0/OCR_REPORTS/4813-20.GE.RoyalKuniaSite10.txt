PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the eastern portion of the Royal Kunia

Site 12 Recreational Center The project site includes a total of 90 residential lots and

pertaining to the house grading and utility installation within the house lot areas for the
project site
HOUSE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The house grading and utility installation work for the Royal Kunia Site 10 project
were observed by Geolabs Inc from August 19 2002 to The house
grading work consisted of footing excavation and footing and slab on grade subgrade
preparation for the project The utility installation work consisted of trench backfill of
plumbing lines within the house and garage pad areas and the backfill of the sewer
clean out areas The house grading and utility backfilling work were performed by Reef
Development of Hawaii Inc Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
within 3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to
slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils

Hawaii California

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
