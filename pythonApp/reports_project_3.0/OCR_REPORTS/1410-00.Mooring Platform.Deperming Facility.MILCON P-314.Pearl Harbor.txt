This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the subject project The approximate location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Our work was pertormed in general accordance with our proposal of
March 20 1984
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil conditions at the site below the water surface level and to
provide soil data for use in the foundation design of the proposed
construction

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed site is located within the offshore channel waters
adjacent to Beckoning Point It is proposed to construct two additional
mooring platforms at the northern and southern ends of the existing
deperming facility The platform superstructures each approximately 40
by 55 feet will be supported by concrete piles reinforced with
non magnetic rebars These new mooring platforms will be designed to
resist mooring forces from large CVN vessels These mooring forces will
be essentially wind loadings and transient in nature No specific
loading conditions were known at the time of our investigation
Construction and installation of dolphins and buoys together with
appurtenant electrical and lighting facilities and additional dredging
to a 53 2 foot project depth for large CVN vessels are also planned
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the eastern side of Waipio
Peninsula in Pearl Harbor on the south central coastal plain of Oahu
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 3
A complex series of erosional and depositional events resulting
from glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic
activities during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of
alluvial deposits in a lagoonal deltaic environment during higher stands
of the sea
The southern half of Waipio Peninsula is a remnant of the hard
reef rock that once covered a larger portion of the embayment and
subsequently was carved away in many places by streams during lower
stands of the sea
During the Recent epoch the sea has adjusted to its present level
and additional sediments have continued to accumulate along the lochs
and channels of the harbor Land developments in the past 40 to 60
years have added man made fills along the shoreline

were drilled over water to a depth of 83 feet for Boring 1 and 157 feet
for Boring 2 below MLLW

Soil Conditions
The borings drilled disclosed significantly different soil
conditions at the two proposed platform sites
At the new southern platform site Boring 1 encountered very stiff
older alluvium soils consisting of very stiff to hard clayey silt from a
depth of 35 feet mudline to 60 feet below MLLW underlain by
moderately hard poorly to moderately cemented coral debris with some
solution cavities to a depth of at least 83 feet the depth of that
boring
At the new northern mooring platform site Boring 2 encountered
very thick recent harbor sediments consisting of soft clayey silt and
loose sand from depths of 41 feet mudline to 106 5 feet underlain by
a thick deposit of consolidated older lagoonal sediments consisting of
very stiff silt to 132 5 feet and older alluvium consisting of stiff
tannish gray to grayish brown clayey silt to 159 feet the maximum depth
drilled
No artesian water condition was detected in the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed southern mooring
platform is underlain by very stiff older alluvium at the harbor bottom
while the northern mooring platform site is underlain by thick soft
harbor sediments to about 105 feet below MLLW
In the southern mooring platform area we believe the stiff older
alluvium and the underlying coral could provide a moderate amount of
vertical pile bearing capacity and lateral support In addition a
relatively steep dredge slope could be maintained
In the northern platform area we believe the soft sediments could
provide only limited amount of resistance against lateral mooring and
wind or seismic loadings Therefore relatively expensive large
diameter piles may be required to provide sufficient resistance to the
design lateral loads Due to the highly compressible nature of the soft
sediments we believe the long term loads should be carried by the
underlying stiff older alluvium In addition in this thick soft
sediment area relatively flat side slopes are required for underwater
dredging in order to maintain adequate factor of safety against slope
failure below the water level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1410 00 Page 6
Since the soil conditions on the southern side appear to be more
favorable an alternative to extend the deperming facility further
toward the south is being considered However such a possibility
should first be explored further by drilling additional borings
It is our understanding that such an alternative scheme would
consist of a single long mooring pier in lieu of multiple platforms
The long mooring pier would require somewhat higher vertical bearing
capacities while the lateral pile capacity requirement would be less
than the original mooring platform scheme
Southern Mooring Platform Site
Boring 1 disclosed that the southern mooring platform is underlain
by stiff older alluvium from the bottom of the harbor to a depth of 60
feet underlain further by coral rock Thus both short and long terin
loads can be sustained The following recommendations will also be
applicable to the mooring pier alternative that is being considered
Vertical Pile Capacities
Based on the materials encountered in Boring 1 we believe
moderate vertical pile support can be obtained by driving the pile
through the older alluvium to bear into the underlying coral
located at a depth of 60 feet below MLLW
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1410 00 Page 7
We recommend that 16 to 20 inch sized concrete piles primarily
deriving their capacities by end bearing in coral be utilized
Due to variations in cementation and the amount of cavities in the
coral we believe that the vertical pile capacity should be
limited to 50 tons each For estimating purpose pile tips may be
assumed to reach a depth of 75 feet below MLLW
These piles should be driven with a hammer delivering at least
15 000 t lbs of energy per blow As a guide the following
pile driving criteria may be considered
Based on a pile hammer with lbs energy the pile
driving may be stopped when encountering a penetration resistance
of 100 blows per foot after penetrating below a depth of 60 feet
MLLW The penetration resistance criteria should be modified
according to the actual hammer used

For long term vertical loading we recommend that the frictional
capacity in the soft sediments be ignored and the vertical loading be
supported by the underlying stiff silts and clays encountered below a
depth of 106 feet MLLW in Boring 2
For large diameter piles such as 54 inch cylinderical concrete
pipe we recommend that they be driven at least 15 feet into the stiff
older alluvium for a vertical pile capacity of up to 50 tons each The
smaller 16 to 20 inch sized piles should be driven at least 30 feet into
the stift older alluvium to develop a pile capacity of 50 tons each
Based on Boring 2 drilled in this site the pile cap would be at a depth
of about 135 to 140 feet below MLLW

More specific recommendations on the pile capacities and
corresponding pile penetrations could be developed when more specific
design requirements are established
If smaller 16 to 20 inch sized piles are considered in the
northern mooring platform site these piles will be very long and
probably require splicing In general conventional splicing in the
very soft clay harbor sediment would require special attention because
of the following reasons
1 The lower pile section may sink off during splicing which
will make splicing of batter piles particularly difficult
2 The lower pile section may accidentally slip off if heavy
hammer blows are applied during driving subsequent to splicing
3 A conventional sleeved splice joint will not transfer bending
or tension stresses
We believe that if splicing sleeves of non magnetic metal are
used bolts should be included in the splice sleeves to prevent the
lower pile section to slide out of the splice during driving

Test Pile Program
A test pile driving program is recommended to determine the final
production pile cast lengths We recommend that the test pile
installation be observed by the soils engineer who should assess the
actual driving conditions and make modifications as deemed necessary
Dolphin Piles and Fender Piles
For the new dolphin fender piles in which no long term loads will
be applied we believe that wooden piles will still be suitable The
length of the dolphin piles should be such that the pile embedments
would be at least 10 feet below the design dredge bottom in the firm
soil area as indicated by Boring 1 and at least 50 feet below the
design dredge bottom in the soft mud area as indicated by Boring 2
All wooden piles should be driven with a light hammer having a
rated energy not exceeding lbs per blow
The soft clay sediments in the northern platform area will tend to
slough at steep slopes and therefore a relatively flat dredge slope of
4 1 H V should be considered for dredge slope of up to 25 feet high
We believe that the stiff silt in the southern platform area will be
more stable and may be dredged to a slope of 1 1 H V for dredge slope
depths of up to 55 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII

Some sloughing from dredging activity and continued lagoonal
sediment should be anticipated
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed deperming facility
modification should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that test pile program if any and pile
installation operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction This is to see that adequate penetration has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation If conditions encountered during
construction are different from those presented in this report
modifications to the design may be needed

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this investigation
Although the subsurface conditions interpreted from the borings
are generally correct at the boring locations some variations may be
encountered during construction Unanticipated soil conditions are
commonly encountered and cannot be fully determined by reconnaissance
and soil borings These unforeseen conditions such as pockets of deep
soft soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs

Field Exploration

two 2 offshore borings to depths 83 and 157 feet below MLLW at the

monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All

Classification System Graphic presentations of the soils encountered
in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1 through
2 3

free falling a distance of 30 inches In addition a 3 0 inch 0 D

inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
