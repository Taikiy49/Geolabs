INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the Phase I design of the the project site
grading and structure foundations of the proposed Hilo Wastewater
Treatment Plant near the town of Hilo in the District of South Hilo on
the island of Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 00 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed development
The work was done generally in accordance with our revised fee
proposal of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of
nineteen 19 borings to depths ranging between 25 0 and 30 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Also included were engineering
evaluation of the field data the development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in
the appendix of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated about 6 500 feet to the east of the
existing Hilo International Airport General Lyman Field in the South
Hilo District of the island of Hawaii The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located in raw undeveloped land which is vegetated by
a subtropical forest with a dense understory of ferns and shrubs The
topography of the site is gently rolling with a slight slope towards
the north northwest Access to the site is by an access trail blazed
through the forest from the vicinity of the existing Hilo Sanitary
Landfill Extensive clearing and some grading was required to make the
access trail and project site accessible to truck mounted drilling
equipment

Most of the site is gently rolling to relatively level with some
stockpiles of grubbed material around the site The existing ground
surface elevations of the site range from approximately 26 to 48 feet
above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of
a municipal wastewater treatment plant facility The site improvements
will involve the construction of a bio filter wastewater treatment
plant with a capacity of about 5 0 million gallons per day sludge
handling facilities parking areas and driveways Asphaltic concrete
pavements are anticipated
The design of the treatment facilities have been broken down into
two phases Phase I includes the following structures
1 Influent Headworks
2 Primary Sedimentation Tanks

5 Solids Contactor
The remaining structures will be part of the Phase II design
effort
It is anticipated that the site grading will require minimal cuts
and fills on the order of six to eight feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITTONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the eastern flank of Mauna Loa a
large basaltic shield volcano which makes up a large portion of the
mass of the island of Hawaii The site and its vicinity has been
mapped by Stearns and Macdonald 1946 as rocks of the Kau Volcanic
Series of Mauna Loa which are Recent in age These rocks are generally
thinly bedded primitive tholeiitic basalts The Kau Volcanics are
frequently underlain at relatively shallow depths by lava flows of the
Kahuku Volcanic Series The Kahuku rocks are also primitive basalts
which may be capped with a thin layer of Pahala Ash
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of
ash cinder and Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and
when the moisture content is high enough it becomes thixotropic i e
it loses strength when remolded
Observations at the project site and in its immediate vicinity
indicate that rock is usually exposed at the ground surface below the
forest understory and that there is little or no ash soil over the rock
formation The forest has deposited a thin layer of organic matter on
its floor

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
arilling and sampling nineteen 19 borings to depths ranging between
25 0 and 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered relatively competent basaltic
rock formation to the maximum depths explored The lava flow formation
encountered at the project site appears to have been in the beginning
of the transitional phase between the pahoehoe and a a forms and also
appears to have been ponded or very slow moving at the time of
deposition The formation grades with depth from a fractured vesicular
rock at the surface to a dense massive rock at depths of about ten to
fifteen feet below the existing ground surface
Subsurface cavities lava tubes were encountered in Boring Nos
13 and 16 Voids which were open to the ground surface were
also noted along the access trail to the site and on the project site
near Boring No 3
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of
this report
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings
during our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered
at shallow depths below the surface of the project site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed wastewater treatment plant and
related site work However due to the potential existence of cavities
within the volcanic rock we recommend that a program of probing and
grouting at foundation locations be conducted during construction
This is discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations within
the portions of the grading limits designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing unengineered rockfill
be removed to natural rock formation within the project grading
limits If the stripped rockfill material does not contain organics
rubbish ash soil or other deleterious materials and if it is generally
less than 12 inches in maximm size it may be reused as fill
material Otherwise it should be wasted off site
The on site volcanic material if reduced to generally less than
12 inches in size should be a source of adequate fill material

Excavation
Due to the nature of the rock formation encountered at the site
it is anticipated that some hard excavation e g hoerams chipping
and or blasting will be required for cuts foundation structure
excavations or trenches that encounter dense rock formation
Generally the in situ basalt formation should be rippable by a
large bulldozer to a depth of about ten 10 feet below the existing
ground surface Excavations to greater depths will most likely require
hard excavation and or blasting resulting in higher excavation costs
Therefore the depth of excavation should be limited to about 10 feet
below the existing ground surface wherever possible
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing
should be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the
work However excavations in dense rock formation nommally can stand
unsupported for temporary construction purposes
Foundation Support
We recommend that the structure foundations bear on in situ basalt
or fill placed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein For the design of foundations bearing on in situ basalt an
allowable bearing capacity of 6 000 p s f may be utilized For
foundations bearing on fill composed of the on site excavated rock
less than 12 inches in maximum size compacted to 90 maximm dry
density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 an allowable
bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered

If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the
soft and or weak materials down to in situ basalt and backfilling of
the resultant void with fill material compacted in accordance with the
above recommendations

It is anticipated that backfilling will be required for
below grade structures and for trenches Backfills should be placed in

placement except that the maximm particle size should not exceed

clean granular material such as ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel be utilized
to reduce the amount of compactive effort required to attain 90 percent
relative dry density

Cavity Probi
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at
shallow depths below the project site Cavities or voids were
encountered in Boring 11 13 and 16 during our exploration
Also cavities open to the ground surface were observed near Boring 3
and along the access trail to the site Previous experience indicates
that such cavities lava tubes are commonly found in volcanic
formations Several substantial tubes have been found in the South
Hilo District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void situated
below a foundation causing settlement and or foundation failure we
recommend that the foundation subgrades be probed The probes should
consist of a 3 inch minimm diameter hole drilled to a depth of 10 feet
below the bottom of the footing Probes should be drilled at each
major footing location and on 10 foot centers along continuous or strip
foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout 1
sand to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage
fashion i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment it is
feasible to perform the probing and grouting operations prior to
structure and footing excavation
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of some of the proposed
structures will be interior concrete slabs on grade The slabs and
their cushions may be supported by dense basalt clinker or compacted
fill A 4 inch layer of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel should be provided
below the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept
moist prior to concrete placement

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may

evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations given in this report

by taping and estimation from reference points shown on the plan prepared

considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around then
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 00 Page 8
Appendix B Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and
number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
