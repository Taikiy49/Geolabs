Dear Ms Todani
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed
during installation and testing of the micropiles for the Royal Hawaiian Shopping Center
Building A project in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the tests
performed on the micropiles for the project from September 27 through
Charles Pankow Builders Inc was the general contractor for the project and Schnabel
Foundation Company was the specialty subcontractor responsible for installation of the
micropiles
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at Building A of the Royal Hawaiian Shopping Center in the
Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The renovation work will consist of new trellis elevators and escalators Based on
the information provided by the project structural engineer we understand the column
loads dead plus live loads for the new structures range between about 21 and 163 kips
per column
Based on the anticipated column loads and the subsurface conditions encountered
at the project site we believe appreciable foundation settlements would occur if the new
structures were supported on shallow foundations In addition appreciable differential
settlements will occur between the existing Building A supported on driven piles and the
new structures supported on shallow foundations Therefore we recommended a deep
foundation system consisting of micropile foundations to support the new structures

MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Personnel from Schnabel Foundation Company installed and tested the
28 micropiles for the project The approximate locations of the 28 micropiles are shown on
the Micropile Identification Plan Plate 2 Installation and testing of the micropiles for the

Micropile Installation
A Geolabs representative was present to observe the drilling reinforcing bar
installation and the grouting operations during construction of the micropiles for the
project The micropiles were drilled using a track mounted drill rig
and a track mounted drill rig
In general the micropiles were drilled to depths of about 72 5 to 77 4 feet below the
top of the existing concrete slab which corresponded to about 64 5 to 69 4 feet MSL
The volumes of grout placed within the micropiles ranged between approximately 26 and
102 cubic feet ft A summary of the micropile tip elevations and the amount of grout
placed into the drilled holes is presented on Plate 3
It should be noted that the micropile subcontractor was not able to remove portions
of the steel casing for MP 23 to provide the 27 foot long bond length Because the
subcontractor was not able to provide the minimum bond length shown on the project
plans installation of MP 23 was not acceptable and was rejected Therefore a
replacement micropile designated as MP 23A was installed adjacent to MP 23
During the course of construction we did not observe installation of MP 10 because
the subcontractor did not notify us that installation work would occur simultaneously with
load testing of the sacrificial load test micropile However we understand the same
process and procedures as the other micropiles were utilized to install MP 10

Micropile Testing
In accordance with the project requirements one pullout test was performed on a
sacrificial micropile The sacrificial micropile was installed within the area bounded by
Gridlines H and J and Gridlines 36 and 37 on The sacrificial
micropile was installed to a depth of about 73 feet below the top of the existing slab which
corresponded to about 65 feet MSL The bottom 27 feet of the reinforcing bar No 11 bar
was not sheathed while the remaining upper portion of the reinforcing bar was sheathed
to provide an unbonded length for the micropile A total grout volume of about 104 ft was
placed in the sacrificial micropile
In general the sacrificial micropile was loaded to the maximum test load of
200 percent of the design load 200 kips where the micropile was able to sustain the load
without appreciable deformation The associated movements for the micropile at each load
increment 25 percent of the design load up to the maximum test load were measured
and recorded The total recorded movement of the rebar at the maximum load was
2 639 inches Based on our analyses the theoretical rebar elongation at a load of 200 kips
is about 2 530 inches Therefore the total movement of the bonded length of the sacrificial
micropile was about 0 109 inches The total creep movement at a load of 100 kips
between 1 and 10 minutes is about 0 016 inches The total creep movement at a load of
200 kips between 6 and 60 minutes is about 0 030 inches In general the load test
performed on the sacrificial micropile met the acceptance criteria specified in the project
requirements
In addition to a pullout load test on a sacrificial micropile three proof tests pullout
tests were performed on three production micropiles The production micropiles
consisting of a No 14 reinforcing bar was placed into the micropiles designated for proof
testing
In general the production micropiles proof tested met the acceptance criteria
specified in the project requirements and our technical memorandum dated
Based on our observations of the micropile tests performed by the subcontractor it is our
professional opinion that the micropiles have been installed to provide the design load with
tolerable movements The test results the Load versus Displacement curves and the
Load versus Time curves are presented on the Micropile Test Data Sheets on Plates 4 1
through 7 4 of this letter report for reference
