This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is within the existing Queen’s Medical Center West facility in the 
Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. We understand that a new eight-level parking 
structure providing approximately 590 parking stalls is planned adjacent to the existing 
hospital. The proposed parking structure has a footprint of approximately 120 by 190 feet 
in plan dimensions at the ground surface. The layout of the new parking structure project is 
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Site grading work generally consisted of excavations of up to about 7 feet for the 
basement parking level construction. The project also included the installation of new utility 
connections, backfilling around footings, pavement construction, among others. The scope 
of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing during 
the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the new parking structure only.

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company is the general contractor for the project. 
CJ Peterson Services, Inc. performed the site grading operations, footing subgrade 
preparation, pavement construction, and utility line installation. The site grading work 
consisted of subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, 
pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and backfill. 
Following the demolition and clearing operations, the over-excavated subgrades 
and areas designated to receive fills were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and recompacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction, as appropriate.  Fill and backfill materials 
required for the project generally were placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the 
optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.  The 
compaction requirement was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction 
for areas subjected to vehicular traffic.  Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry 
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil 
established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content 
(percentage by weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are 
summarized on Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 195 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.14 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading, 
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with 
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations 
presented in the above-referenced geotechnical engineering report (October 12, 2018) 
and subsequent recommendations.
