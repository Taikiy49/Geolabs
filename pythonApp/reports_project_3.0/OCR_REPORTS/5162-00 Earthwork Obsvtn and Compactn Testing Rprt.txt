PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the southeast portion of the Royal Kunia Development
Area in the District of Kunia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The name of Road D has
been changed to Anonui Street The project includes the repair of two pavement areas
and several sidewalk areas
Based on construction plans the existing Anonui Street near the Royal Kunia Site 8
project is a 60 foot wide roadway We anticipate that the design pavement section for
Anonui Street will be similar to Anoiki Street that consisted of 2 inches of asphalt concrete
8 inches of aggregate base course and up to 18 inches of select borrow material
depending on the soil test data
The pavement repair will consist of removing the existing asphalt concrete and
base course material to expose the underlying subgrade material The condition of the
subgrade will be evaluated by hand probing and visual observations Soft subgrade
materials that are encountered will be removed and replaced with compacted base course
material In addition several sidewalk slabs will be removed and re constructed
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading performed during roadway and sidewalk reconstruction for
the project site

ROADWAY AND SIDEWALK RECONSTRUCTION
The pavement repair work during roadway and sidewalk reconstruction for the
Royal Kunia Road D Phase Il project was observed by Geolabs Inc from
to The site grading work consisted of removal of
the existing asphalt concrete and base course material and reconstruction of the existing
base course layer within the roadway areas and re compaction of the existing subgrade
and placement and compaction of base course within sidewalk areas to achieve the
design finished grades for the project The roadway and sidewalk reconstruction work
were performed by Albert C Kobayashi Inc The sidewalk repair work near the
intersection of Road A and Anonui Street was performed by Goodfellow Brothers The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content of the base course material from
Makakilo Quarry was obtained from Grace Pacific Corporation and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the
base course material was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from existing structures These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
The pavement repair area between Streetlight Nos 84 and 86 was re done
because the patch area was paved lower than the existing adjacent pavement In
addition an area approximately 11 feet by 34 feet in plan dimension between Streetlight
Nos 78 and 80 was repaired Soft subgrade soils about 1 foot in thickness was removed
and replaced with compacted aggregate base course material in the repair area
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading work for the Royal Kunia Road D
Phase II Pavement Repair project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered
by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as
amended
