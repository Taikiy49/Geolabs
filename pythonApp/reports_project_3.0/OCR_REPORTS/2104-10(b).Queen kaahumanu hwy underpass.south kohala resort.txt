INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration for the subject portion of the South Kohala Resort
project This report presents our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soils and rock conditions at the proposed highway underpass
site in order to provide data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the underpass
structure and foundations

The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1
Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig with operators to and
from the project site
Drilling and sampling of four 4 borings at the proposed
underpass site to depths ranging between 25 0 and 33 0 feet below
the existing ground surface
Coordination of the field work and logging of the borings by a
Field Geologist from our firn
Engineering analyses of the exploration data for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the

Preparation of this report presenting our findings and

clerical support
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The South Kohala Resort project site is located in the Ouli section
of the District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii and encompasses
approximately 485 acres The site is divided into two areas by the Queen
Kaahumanu Highway The mauka area is bordered to the north by Waiulaula
Gulch to the south and east by raw undeveloped land and to west by Queen
Kaahumanu Highway The makai area is bordered to north by the golf course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 3
of the existing Mauna Kea Beach Hotel to the south by Hapuna State Beach
Park to the west by the northern part of Hapuna Bay and to the east by
Queen Kaahumanu Highway
The terrain is generally rolling with some steep slopes locally
approaching a ratio of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V The
terrain typically reflects the original surface of thick basaltic lava
flows in the Ata form The ground surface elevations range from about sea
level to approximately 600 feet above sea level with drainage trending
towards the west
The site is vegetated with a thin cover of dry grasses with kiawe
algaroba trees occurring in the lower areas near Hapuna Bay and in
drainageways
Most of the ground surface is covered with basaltic cobbles and
gravel mantling an ashy soil There are localized exposures of basalt
clinker and rock formation
The proposed underpass site is where Queen Kaahumanu Highway crosses
a major intermittent drainageway The crossing was constructed as an
embankment fill with pipe culverts The existing slopes of the embankment
are at a ratio of about two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V and are
lightly vegetated with a thin cover of dry grasses and scrub kiawe
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the South Kohala Resort project site into a
planned resort residential and recreational development The resort
portion of the development is currently planned to be a hotel facility in
the makai portion of the site on a low bluff overlooking Hapuna Bay The
remainder of the makai area is planned as golf course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 4
It is proposed to develop the mauka area primarily as residential and
golf course with other recreational facilities and maintenance support
within the area
In order to provide access between the mauka and makai areas for
vehicular and golf cart traffic it is proposed to construct an underpass
below the existing Queen Kaahumanu Highway The preliminary plans for the
underpass show a multi span bridge structure with wing wall abutments
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua
Volcanic Series of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene
Stearns and Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which
are mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil at the project site differs from
Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the
in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists ina dry
friable state
The soils at the site have been subjected to wind erosion which has
resulted in the formation of a desert pavement on the surface of the
site This desert pavement is a surficial layer of cobbles and gravel
which were left behind as the finer ash was blown away
At and in the area of the project site the lava flows are in the A a
form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which
frequently has a thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the
massive core The clinker surface of the lavas at the site have been
mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 5
In parts of the main drainageways of the site the ash soil and
clinker have been stripped by erosion exposing the underlying dense rock
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the proposed underpass site were
explored by the drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths
ranging between 25 0 and 33 0 feet below the existing ground surface our
exploration encountered surface layers consisting of dense gray basalt
cobbles and gravel with some silt which appear to be either rockfill from
the road embankment construction or in situ clinker with some intermixed
ash At an average depth of about six 6 feet below the ground surface
our borings encountered in situ basalt rock formation which was generally
hard and massive The basalt formation was encountered to the maximum
depths explored in the drilled borings
Visual examination of the embankment fill indicates that the
embankment was constructed of a dense rockfill consisting of cobble and
gravel sized basalt fragments mixed with some ash soil
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our
exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration along with the boring
logs are presented in Appendix A of this report
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analyses it is our opinion that the
proposed highway underpass is feasible from a geotechnical engineering
point of view The existing dense rockfill dense clinker and competent
basalt formation should provide adequate bearing for the proposed bridge
structure With the exception of the ash soil which should be stockpiled
for use as topsoil excavated volcanic materials should provide a suitable
source of fill material

Site Grading
It is anticipated that the majority of the earthwork for the proposed
highway underpass will be cut to remove the existing embankment fill The
following recommendations may be used as guidelines for earthwork
In general it is our opinion that conventional grading methods may
be used It should be understood that customary grading practices on the
western side of the island of Hawaii are usually modified somewhat due to
the rocky nature of the material
Prior to commencement of grading surface vegetation and organic
matter should be cleared and grubbed The resulting spoils should be
disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
Ash soils generally have relatively low strength and if the in situ
moisture contents are high enough they are thixotropic i e they lose
strength when remolded Therefore it is our recommendation that the ash
soil be removed from fill roadway and structural areas The ash soil may
be either stockpiled for use as topsoil or thoroughly blended into
rockfill material as discussed below
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture
conditioning of fill material will be necessary It is also advisable to
institute a dust control program during grading operations
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below
any areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm
natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with well
compacted fill
In general the excavated soil and rock materials if reduced to less
than 12 inches in size will be suitable as fill material if needed The
excavated materials if reduced to less than six 6 inches in size will
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 7
be suitable as backfill material Imported material should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
The excavated rock material may not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material Therefore the surficial ash soil could
be mixed into the rocky excavated material to increase the percentage of
fines to create a better graded fill material
Generally any fills required should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry density The
upper two 2 feet of fills or backfills below road subgrade level
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills
composed of excavated volcanic material Instead a testing program to
determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction should be conducted at the start of the
grading phase of the project On the basis of this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate
compaction
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1
horizontal to vertical should be keyed and benched into the underlying
consolidated clinker or basalt formation to lessen potential instability
of the new fill see Plate 3 The keys and benches should not be based
in the upper ash soil or in loose clinker
Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in
its vicinity indicate that the site is underlain by dense clinker with
massive dense basalt ledges at relatively shallow depths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 8
It is anticipated that most of the rockfill ash and clinker
materials may be conventionally excavated Some hard excavation and or
ripping may be encountered in localized well cemented clinker areas or
when large lava balls boulders or ledges are encountered
The underlying basalt formation will probably require some hard
excavation such as heavy ripping hoerams or possibly blasting
Generally the excavation method should be left to the contractor
Blasting should be considered if it will substantially expedite the
excavation process However care should be exercised to avoid
overshooting which could disrupt the clinker or rock formation resulting
in a loss of strength
Slopes
Provided that granular materials 12 inch minus are utilized design
fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are recommended
Any slopes greater than 20 feet in height should have a bench at the
midpoint of the slope
Foundation Support
It is our opinion that relatively shallow spread or strip footing
foundations may be used to support the proposed underpass bridge
structure It is anticipated that foundations will bear on either in situ
basalt rock formation or on rockfill clinker
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 9 000 p s f may be used for
the design of foundations bearing on in situ basalt rock formation or
dense rockfill clinker in level areas A reduced allowable bearing
capacity of up to 4 500 p s f may be used for the design of foundations
located close to sloping areas These values may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2104 10 B Page 9
In order to provide uniform support for the foundations and to
minimize the potential of foundation distress resulting from voids which
may existing in the footing subgrade we recommend that the footing
excavations be over excavated two 2 feet below subgrade level The
over excavated area should be backfilled with base course placed in 8 inch
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximm
ary density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of four 4 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Foundations located on sloping areas
should have a minimum set back of eight 8 feet from the outside edge of
the footing to the adjacent slope face This may require additional
embedment to achieve the proper set back distance

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from the Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by
triangulation and measurement from reference points shown on a preliminary
sketch provided by Belt Collins and Associates Inc Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
contours shown on the same sketch The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
