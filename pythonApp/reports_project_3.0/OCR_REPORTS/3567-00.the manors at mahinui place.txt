INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Manors at Mahinui Place in the Keapuka area in Kaneohe on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene Age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southern flank of the Koolau shield and
composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic
Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular
basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of the Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3567 00 Page 5
lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering process has
formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In general
saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering
of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by stiff to
very stiff residual soils over stiff to hard saprolite extending to the maximum depths
drilled of about 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our field
exploration and laboratory testing we believe that the proposed house and retaining
wall structures may be supported by a shallow foundation system bearing on the stiff
to very stiff near surface soils and or compacted fills
Our laboratory test results indicate that the near surface soils have a slight
potential for shrink swell when subjected to changes in the moisture content
Therefore special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements
presented in the Slabs On Grade section of this report to reduce the potential
shrink swell effects on floor slabs

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3567 00 Page 8
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials
Following clearing and grubbing subgrades to receive fill should be scarified to
a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of fill material Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing and grubbing
operations should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the over
excavated subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches
properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over
excavation and fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation
quantities

moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content

House Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may
be used to support the proposed house structures
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the stiff to very stiff near surface soils and or compacted fills
This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third s for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade We estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated
design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of
approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the residual soils and or compacted fills These values assume
that the concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below
finished grade should be neglected
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3567 00 Page 11
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities
the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii

bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed house
structures The near surface soils encountered at the proposed site consist of silty
soils which exhibit a slight expansion potential Therefore we recommend that the
subgrade be properly prepared and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and
slab concrete The subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3567 00 Page 12
For more uniform support of slabs we recommend that the concrete slab on
grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer should
consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation or basalt termite barrier BTB which will also serve as a capillary moisture
break The slab cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on
grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable
membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture
vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the slab
concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a
2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab
concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garages
or driveways we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base
course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch
cushion fill layer and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural
slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be
used for the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any
unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or the approved on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch loose level lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings to depths of about 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 thru A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Seven 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are summarized on
Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits Graphic presentation of the test results is
provided on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on a bulk sample
of the near surface soils to evaluate the maximum dry density and optimum moisture
content relationship The test results are presented on Plate B 3
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM Test Method D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the soil strength for
preliminary pavement design Results of the CBR tests are presented on Plates B 4
and B 5
