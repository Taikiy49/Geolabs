INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Waimanalo Wastewater Treatment Plant WWTP
Improvements project at the existing Waimanalo WWTP in Waimanalo on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located within the existing Waimanalo WWTP in
Waimanalo on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Based on the information provided the proposed new construction will consist of
modifications to the existing chlorination building and construction of a new effluent filter
structure Based on the preliminary site plan provided the proposed new effluent filter
structure will have two finished floor elevations one at about 5 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL and a lower finished floor for the base at approximately 2 feet MSL The
proposed effluent filter structure will be constructed of reinforced concrete
Based on the information provided we anticipate that the site grading for the
proposed project will consist of an excavation on the order of approximately 12 to 14 feet
below the existing ground surface in order to construct the effluent filter structure The
bottom of the proposed excavation will be approximately 4 to 6 feet below the
groundwater table Therefore temporary shoring and dewatering of the excavation will
be required for the proposed construction Further details pertaining to the proposed
construction were not available at the time this report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the ocean basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
The processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows
to be highly weathered and mantled by residual soils During the Kaena stand of the sea
level at approximately 90 feet MSL of the Pleistocene Epoch a delta of silt sand and
gravel built out into the embayment near the project site Agricultural land development
and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present
form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed effluent filter structure site is generally
underlain by stiff clayey silts over medium dense sands Based on the generally dense
subsoil conditions encountered in our boring we believe that a shallow mat foundation
bearing on the medium dense sands may be used for support of the proposed effluent
filter structure The base of the proposed effluent filter structure will extend to a depth of
about 12 to 14 feet below the ground surface or about 4 to 6 feet below the groundwater
level encountered during our field exploration Therefore the effluent filter structure
should be designed to resist hydrostatic uplift forces due to the groundwater conditions
In addition we believe that shoring and dewatering of the excavation will be needed
during construction in order to construct the structure in a dry condition Detailed
discussions and recommendations for design of foundations site grading excavation
and shoring and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the
following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the preliminary plans provided we anticipate that the foundation of the
effluent filter structure will be at about 4 feet MSL and at about 3 feet MSL Based on
the generally dense subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that a mat
foundation may be used for support of the proposed effluent filter structure at the project
site An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
the concrete mat bearing on the medium dense sands This bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used to evaluate sliding resistance developed

between the mat and the underlying medium dense sands Resistance due to passive
pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square
groundwater table These values assume that the concrete for the mat is placed directly
against foundation excavations consisting of medium dense sands
For evaluating uplift forces due to hydrostatic pressures a high groundwater level
elevation of not lower than 3 feet MSL should be used for design to take into
consideration tidal fluctuations Uplift forces may be resisted by the dead weight of the
structure weight of the backfill material placed on and above the structure and also by
side shear between the sides of the effluent filter structure and the adjacent backfill For
evaluating the weight of the backfill a unit weight of 110 and 46 p c f may be used for
compacted backfill above and below the groundwater table respectively For side shear
between the structure and the backfill material a nominal value of 100 p s f may be used
in design
Based on the generally dense subsoils encountered foundation settlements are
estimated to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements of about inch

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted

Areas designated to receive fill and subgrades for improvements should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Dewatering
Dewatering of excavation for the effluent filter structure will be necessary since the
existing groundwater level is above the bottom of the proposed excavation Therefore
we recommend that dewatering provisions be included in the contract documents for
construction Since the excavation will involve discharge of groundwater a National
Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES permit may be necessary The
contractor should consult their independent consultant or the State of Hawaii
Department of Health for the latest regulations and information pertaining to the permit
application
Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the contractor The selection of
equipment and methods of dewatering should be left up to the contractor and he should
be aware that modifications to the dewatering system may be required during
construction depending on conditions encountered The dewatering method selected
should have minimal impact on the groundwater level surrounding the proposed
excavation In addition dewatering operations should be performed such that only clear
water is pumped Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a blowing
or heaving of the excavation bottom or sides
It should be noted that the subsoils in the zone where dewatering will take place
consist of sandy materials which may be considered to be relatively permeable It is our
opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents should be written to
include all works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table and or to
exclude the water from the excavations to allow construction of the proposed structures
under safe and dry conditions These works or systems may include but are not limited

Dewatering Considerations
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selection of a
suitable method of dewatering
a The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance or damage to
existing buildings roads and environment
b The dewatering method should maintain stability of and also provide safe
and dry working conditions in the excavation
Cc The dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications
to accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering Precaution and Monitoring
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained
by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His her qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during
construction and to implement as soon as possible necessary precautionary or
remedial measures including but not limited to stopping or slowing down the
dewatering operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3838 00 Page 15
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with shoring installation such that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected
Any groundwater drawdown outside the excavation will cause additional
settlement resulting from consolidation of the loose soft compressible soils
Therefore the use of a deep well system outside the excavations to draw down
the groundwater level should not be allowed Special caution should also be taken
to avoid dewatering utility trenches connected to excavations If this occurs the
granular bedding and or backfill in the utility trenches could act as subdrains and
cause significant areal groundwater drawdown resulting in settlements and
potential damage to utility lines and or other existing adjacent structures
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project Some
of the utility trenches may extend below the groundwater table In general a granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
or about 12 inches above the groundwater level to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes Itis critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support around the sides of the pipes

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling one
boring to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring was drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight and hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths

Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the test are summarized on the Log of Boring and are presented on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis test including a hydrometer test was performed to evaluate the
gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The test was performed in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 422 Graphic presentation of the test results
is provided on Plate B 2
