INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed U S Postal Service Sand Island Carrier Annex located near
the intersection of Sand Island Access Road and Auiki Street in Honolulu on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the subject project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
P Cc ID
The new carrier annex structure will consist of a one story steel framed structure
approximately 109 feet by 147 feet in plan dimensions with surrounding parking areas
The finished floor elevation of the building will be at about 11 5 feet A loading dock
area is planned along the northern side of the building with a finished pavement level
about 4 feet lower than the building floor level
A preliminary geotechnical engineering report for the project was issued by
another firm presenting general subsurface conditions from limited boring and
laboratory testing data Subsequently the building site was filled approximately 3 feet

monitored for a period of one to two months and were found to stabilize rapidly
Based on this previous work the building foundations were designed with
relatively low bearing values

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along Sand Island Access Road about 50 feet
southwest of the existing USPS vehicle maintenance facility near the junction of Sand
Island Access Road and Auiki Street
The site was generally clear and level except for about 4 to 5 feet of coral fill
which was placed to raise the proposed building site The project site plans indicate
that the original ground surface elevations vary from about 7 to 8 feet above Mean Sea
Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The previous borings encountered approximately 3 to 4 feet of medium dense
sandy fill over lagoonal sediments to depths of about 14 to 24 feet The lagoonal
sediments consisted primarily of soft clayey silt near the water level grading to very
loose sands and gravels
Two additional borings were drilled to provide information on the compressibility
of the lagoonal sediments near the center of the structure Both additional borings did
not encounter the soft clayey silt layer but encountered loose silty sand and gravel to
depths of about 24 to 26 feet below the existing filled surface
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging from about 9 5 to
10 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration These
depths generally correspond to elevations of approximately 1 5 to 2 0 feet MSL Due
to the proximity of Keehi Lagoon to the project site it is likely that the groundwater
levels measured in the borings may be affected by tidal fluctuation seasonal
precipitation and storm surge conditions

Shallow spread and continuous footings bearing on the recompacted fill soils
may be used to support the proposed building provided that some settlements are
tolerable Based on the subsoil conditions encountered footing settlements on the
order of 1 inch total may be anticipated for column loads of up to 35 kips Differential
settlements are expected to be about one half the total amount
An allowable bearing pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted fill soils This
bearing is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as possible to take
advantage of the fill crust thereby reducing the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits To utilize the surface fill crust we recommend that the footings
be embedded a maximum of about 3 feet below the finished grade
The loading dock area along the northern side of the building will be
approximately 4 feet below the building finished floor level Therefore the bottom of the
loading dock wall footing will be situated at about elevation 4 5 feet A lower allowable
bearing pressure of 500 p s f is recommended for these footings to maintain the
anticipated settlement to less than 1 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3578 00 Page 7
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of
0 4 may be used for footing bearing on the recompacted coral fill
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the recompacted coral
fill soil If soft or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with granular materials compacted to a minimum of 95 relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
competency of the foundation bearing materials and the required embedment depths
ment Monitorin
It should be recognized that it is very difficult to accurately predict the exact rate
and magnitude of the footing settlement because of variable localized soil conditions
Therefore the actual settlement rate and magnitude should be monitored during
construction to verify the accuracy of the assumed soil profile
To monitor the settlement reference surveys points should be placed on the
footings or columns and surveyed by optical instruments and the readings should be
transmitted to Geolabs Hawaii for review The readings should be taken on a weekly
basis for the duration of the project construction and or until the settlement readings
indicate that the settlement has stabilized

Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed U S Postal Service Sand
Island Carrier Annex project should be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and
written comments prior to construction This review is necessary to evaluate
conformance of the plans and specifications with the intent of the foundation
recommendations provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot be
responsible for misinterpretation of our recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed new carrier annex facility The
items of construction monitoring that are critical which require Special Inspection include
observation of footing excavation fill placement and settlement monitoring This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared
The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of about 26 to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 thru 2 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
