PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed scheme is to construct an interceptor sewer
ranging in size from 8 to 18 inches diameter extending from
the Halawa Jail Sewage Treatment Plant to the North Halawa

Valley Sewer Main
The project may be divided into two
sections the western half and the eastern half with the
division being an imaginary north south line extended from
the edge of the asphalt pavement of the eastern end of the
suspended H 3 Freeway project see Boring Location Plan
Plate 1
At the time of our explorations two possible routes were
being considered for the western half of the proposed sewer
line
Alternate A would follow the central access road of
the State of Hawaii Animal Quarantine Station
Alternate
B would be at the top of the slope along the southern pro
perty line of the Animal Quarantine Station
Subsequent to
the completion of the field exploration alternate B was
recently selected for the proposed interceptor sewer
In the boundary area between the eastern and western halves
of the project the sewer line will run parallel to the edge
of the asphaltic concrete pavement of the H 3 Freeway pro
ject to the north bank of South Halawa Stream
At this
point the sewer trench will follow the stream to the north
fork of South Halawa Stream
At this point the line will
turn and run parallel to the west bank of this north fork
for approximately 400 feet before turning to the east and
crossing the stream bed at the Halawa Jail Sewage Treatment
Plant

feet of trench excavation pipe installation and backfill
Along most of the alignment the proposed invert depths vary
between 5 and 9 feet
The invert depths increase to about a
15 to 20 foot depth near both ends of the alignment
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located to the north of Moanalua Road in
the lower portion of Halawa Valley
Portions of the pro
posed sewer line will run through the State of Hawaii Animal
Quarantine Station
The ground surface along these portions
is relatively clear and gently sloping although access by
construction equipment may somewhat be restricted by exist
ing buildings and utilities
The remainder of the sewer line will be constructed through
vacant land along the banks and within the North and South
Halawa Streams
Some relatively steep slopes will be en
countered along these portions of the alignment
Some
boulders rubbish piles and dense vegetation may restrict
access
There will be a crossing of an existing 42 inch water main
along the proposed sewer alignment

Regional Geology
The site is located in the lower portion of Halawa Valley
which has been brought to its present form by a complex
series of geologic events
The original Halawa Valley was a
youthful valley cut into the Koolau Dome during a lower
stand of the sea
Several subsequent stages of alluviation
have filled the valley floor with fluvial deposits
At an intermediate stage between two series of alluvial
deposition pyroclastic material such as cinder was laid
across the mouth of the valley by members of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
These deposits of volcanic material most
likely originating from Aliamanu and Salt Lake Craters
caused a diversion of Halawa Stream to the west
During the last century man has been an important force in
shaping Halawa Valley to its present form with the placement
of extensive fills over much of the older alluvial deposits

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
trench the size of the pipe the unit weight of the back
fill material and the friction resistance between the back
fill material and the trench walls
To compute the vertical
loads on the pipes a unit weight of 120 p c f and a coef
ficient of internal friction of 0 30 is recommended
Earth
forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench
Therefore the trench width Bd should be kept to
a minimum
The enclosed Plate 2 indicates three alternate
trench types
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The very stiff to hard alluvial and fill soils encountered
through the alignment have good bearing capacity
Also the
net increase in load on the subsoils caused by the sewer
construction will be small
Therefore minimal settlements
are anticipated

Loose miscellaneous fills may be encountered at subgrade
level between Boring Nos 4 and 12
Prior to placement of
bedding layer the bottom of the trench in this section
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 test designation
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe
therefore the pipe bedding is an important design conside
ration
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding should
be placed below the pipe
The granular bedding should
extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth
the outside pipe diameter and should extend halfway up the
pipe sides
The bedding material should consist of clean
granular material such as f3B Fine gravel
The sewer line crossing at North Halawa Stream will be
partially above the bottom of the stream
A concrete jac
ket extending 2 feet below the stream bottom is planned to
protect the sewer line
Stiff alluvial deposits consisting
of clayey silt cobbles and boulders are anticipated near

Sewer Construction
Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should
be the choice of the contractor within practical
limits
The anticipated depth of excavation along
most of the alignment is 5 to 9 feet with depths
of 15 to 20 feet near both ends of the alignment
Normal excavating techniques may be used throughout
the alignment although some harder excavation may
be anticipated in the portions where many boulders
and cobbles were encountered
Trench Wall Supports
Where the alignment extends next to the existing
animal quarantine station structures or any other
existing structures shoring of the trench walls
are recommended to minimize damage of the existing
structures
GEOLABS HA WAIi
w o 1083 00
Page 11
The f ill and alluvial deposits contained many boulder
layers
In general shoring of the sewer trench exca
vation is recommended to minimize caving in of the
trench walls and individual boulders
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing
in accordance with the applicable safety requirements
The design and safety of the trench supports should be
the responsibility of the contractor
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away
from the tops of the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the
excavation should be continuously monitored for
cracks or dips and other indications of movement
and possible sloughing of the trench walls
Such
monitoring is particularly critical in the areas
where existing structures are adjacent to the
trench excavation
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in
accordance with the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction May 1975

The excavated on site soils may generally be used
as the upper portion of the trench backfill from
above one foot over the pipe although some screening
may be necessary to remove boulders or cobbles larger
than 6 inches in diameter
Since there is a good possibility that the area along
portions of the proposed alignment may be developed in
the future it is important that the sewer line trench
be properly backfilled and compacted
Backfill should
be placed in lifts and mechnically compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557
Below road way areas the backfill
within 2 feet of the finished grade should consist of
select borrow and be compacted to 95 maximum density
as determined by ASTM D 1557

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
14 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the borings
were terminated at 12 0 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Bor
ing Location Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in
the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling opera
tions
The classifications were verified by visual inspection and test
ing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 14
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content Atterberg Limits and unit weight determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 14
The Atterberg Limit determinations are also summarized on Plates
B 1 through B 3
Two ASTM D 1557 curves modified proctor were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soil to evaluate its compaction character
istics The test results are summarized on Plate B 3
One 1 inch ring swell test was performed on an undisturbed sample
of the near surface soil to evaluate its swelling potential The test
result is summarized on Plate B 2
Five direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the in situ soil The test
results are presented on Plates B 4 through B 8
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed sample to
evaluate the settlement characteristics of the underlying alluvial soils
The consolidation curve is presented on Plate B 9
