INTRODUCTION
This investigation was undertaken to define subsurface condi
tions and to provide recommendations for the design and con
struction of the foundations to support the proposed multi
purpose dining room
The new dining room will be a one story concrete and masonry
building constructed essentially at the existing grade It is
W 1975
anticipated that the column loads will be approximately 80 kips
the bearing walls will have line loads of 2 to 4 kips per lineal
foot The columns will be located in the dining room area with
bearing walls along the perimeter and platform areas
Site Description
The proposed site is in the southern portion of the existing
Blanche Pope Elementary School Campus in Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
The planned construction area is immediately north of the exist
ing kitchen in the grass covered area between the kitchen and
the covered walkway The essentially level site is bound on all
four sides by paved walkways
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The proposed building site is underlain by alluvium water
deposited soil overlying older coral algal reefs During the
Pliocene and Pleistocene Epochs shallow water coral algal reefs
covered the Waimanalo area Subsequent lowering of the sea
level produced low lying coastal swamps which were later filled
by clay size material from the continued erosion of the Koolau
Volcanic Range
Minor fills have been deposited over these clays in the proposed
building area to a maximum depth of approximately three feet
W 1975
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing
three test borings drilled at the locations shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the
field exploration methods and the accompanying laboratory test
ing is given in the Appendix along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by 2 to 3
feet of sandy to clayey silt fill with rock fragments The fill
is underlain by alluvium a dark gray to dark brown clayey
sandy silt or silty clay Near the surface this alluvium has
a higher clay content and contains traces of organic material
In Borings 1 and 3 the upper 4 feet of alluvium immediately
below the fill was classified as adobe clay This is a
highly plastic soil which is moderately to highly expansive
In Borings 1 and 2 the clayey silt and silty clay are underlain
by a medium to dense coral sand at a depth of approximately 18
feet In Boring 3 the alluvium extended to the limit of the
explorations at 20 feet
The fill was very stiff in consistency where cohesive and
medium dense where granular The upper portion of the alluvium
W 1975
varied from stiff to extremely stiff in consistency The lower
portion varied from extremely stiff to hard
Groundwater
The groundwater levels observed in the borings varied from 1
foot at Boring 1 to 12 feet below the ground surface at Borings
2 and 3 It appears that the groundwater encountered in Boring
1 was due to an in flow of near surface water since some pond
ing was noted in the general area It is our opinion that the
true water level is approximately 12 feet below the ground sur
face as indicated by Borings 2 and 3
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the site is underlain by stiff to very stiff soils which
will provide adequate support for the anticipated loadings The
soils are expansive and will require special site preparations
to prevent distresses to floor slabs and other lightly loaded
sections
Site Preparation
To prevent distress due to swelling soils the following is an
outline of the recommended site preparation
W 1975
1 The proposed building area plus a 2 foot perimeter strip
should be undercut a minimum of 2 feet below the proposed
floor subgrade
2 The exposed undercut surface should be proof rolled to deli
neate localized soft spots which should be removed and
replaced with compacted granular fill
3 The excavation should be backfilled with select granular
material compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum
density established by American Society for Testing and
Materials ASTM Test D 1557 72T
Care should be taken during the excavation operations to minimize
the exposure of the expansive soils to drying The filling
Operations should be initiated properly after the completion of
the undercutting
The select granular material should be tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to transportation to the site The material
should have less than 10 per cent passing a number 200 sieve and
should have a maximum size of 3 inches This fill can either be
coral crusher waste sand or cinders The on site soils should
not be utilized for backfill with the building area
The finish soil surface should be sloped and maintained to pre
vent ponding of water in the vicinity of the structures
W 1975
All areas to receive paved walks or other areas sensitive to
vertical movements should be undercut a minimum of two feet and
backfilled with select granular fill
Foundations
1 The structures can be supported upon spread or line founda
tions bearing a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent
finish grade
2 The foundations may be designed for maximum contact bearing
pressures of 3 000 pounds per square foot
3 The bearing capacities can be increased to when
considering short term wind and seismic loads
4 The estimated settlements for the above design pressures
should not exceed inch total and inch differential
Floor Slabs
The laboratory testing of the on site near surface soils show
that they are moderately expansive Slab on grade construction
can therefore only be utilized with special precautions
1 Areas to receive slabs on grade should be undercut a minimum
of two feet below the proposed subgrade as per Site Preparation
2 The excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular
soils as specified above
W 1975
3 Slabs with moisture sensitive floor coverings should be pro
tected by plastic vapor barrier to eliminate capilliary
moisture
4 The on site soils should not be utilized for backfill
5 Floor slabs should be divorced from low bearing members with
a construction joint to prevent cracking due to slight differ
ential movements
on
Construction Monitoring and Plan Review
It is recommended that the proposed foundation design be reviewed
by our office prior to finalization to verify that the design is
in accordance with our recommendations
Earthwork utilized for structural support should be inspected
and tested to assure that the recommended degree of compaction
is consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such inspections

FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three borings utilizing truck mounted equipment All borings
extended 20 feet below the existing ground surface The loca
tions of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate
1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the filed by our engineer who continuously supervised the drill
ing operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a D ora
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
Sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture and density determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification of the
soils The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample location
A consolidation test was performed on a sample of the upper
alluvium from Boring 3 to measure the settlement characteristics
of the soil when subjected to vertical loadings The test
results are graphically presented on Plate A 4
Two swell tests were performed on selected samples from Borings
2 and 3 to measure the expansive potential of the soils under
a 60 psf surcharge The test results are also shown on the
boring logs

On we visited the above construction site at
the request of to examine the wet areas
found at the footing and slab locations This letter summarizes
our observations and comments
At the time of our site visit 2 feet of over excavation under
the slab and along the perimeter footing has been completed
The footings and floor slabs can be supported on two feet of
compacted granular soil above the undercut as recommended in
our soil and foundation investigation report
In the exposed undercut wet spots were found about 2 x 4
at the north east corner of the proposed building and along
the existing kitchen along the south side of the new building
Two small ponds of water at the wet spots area were about 6
in diameter and 4 inch in depth It appeared that the water
encountered was due to the in flow of near surface water seepage
through the more pervious granular layer The on site adobe
soil is a very tight soils and surface drainage downward is
rather difficult
The contractor was informed to remove the soft material around
the wet spots and replace with granular material The granular
material should be placed from dry area toward wet area and com
pacted in layers
j
The electric cable utility box at the existing convered walk
way next to the northwest corner of proposed building was also
inspected Water was found in the utility box at 3 5 feet below
the top of walkway The water in the box was estimated about
1 5 feet deep
Vertical Drain
It is our opinion that the water encountered was collected
due to the impervious surface adobe soil layers which is
about 16 feet thick To drain the water in the utility box
we suggest drilling of vertical drain through the adobe clay
layer and down to the more pervious coral sand layer below
3 inch diameter pvc plastic pipe should than be installed with
the bottom 5 feet of perforated section inbedded in the coral
sand layer
