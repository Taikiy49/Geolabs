In accordance with our fee proposal dated March 1, 2018 and the Professional 
Service Agreement entered into on July 12, 2018, we have provided earthwork 
observation and testing services during the site grading operations for the Kailua Road 
Wastewater Pump Station Shoring project from June 13 through July 11, 2018. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project generally involves the installation of a new cast-in-place concrete flow 
tube vault that connects to the existing 18-inch diameter ductile iron pipe force main at the 
Kailua Wastewater Pump Station (WWPS) in the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  
Due to the relatively shallow groundwater level and relatively loose subsurface 
materials encountered at the project site, we understand a jet grouted shoring system was 
required to provide temporary excavation support and groundwater control during the 
construction work.

Kiewit Infrastructure West Co. performed the site grading operations during the 
force main improvement project. The site grading work generally consisted of clearing and 
grubbing, subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fill materials, and pavement 
construction/reconstruction. 
Following the installation of the new concrete flow tube box and connection to the 
existing 18-inch diameter ductile iron pipe force main, the excavation was backfilled with 
controlled low strength material (CLSM) up to approximately 6 inches below the finished 
grade. One lift of 3-inch minus select granular fill material was used to bring the excavation 
up to the finished grade. The select granular fill material was moisture-conditioned to 
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). 
In addition, the existing pavement around the Kailua Road WWPS and the bypass 
trench in front of Paolani Apartments were restored. The asphaltic concrete pavement 
sections were reconstructed using base course material, moisture-conditioned to above 
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 90 percent of the maximum 
dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor).  The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents of the imported materials utilized 
as fill and backfill materials are summarized on Plate 1 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 9 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plate 2 of this report. These tests were performed on the 
constructed fills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading for 
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report 
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
