This addendum to our report titled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Waikiki Shell Improvements, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated March 8, 2019, provides

sound and lighting arch structures and the project adopting the International Building

are subject to the limitations noted in our above-referenced report. 
Seismic Design Considerations 
Based on the IBC 2012, the project site may be subject to seismic activity, and 
seismic design considerations will need to be addressed. Based on the subsurface 
materials encountered at the project site and the geologic setting of the area, we believe 
the project site may be classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as being a “Soft 
Soil Profile” site corresponding to a Site Class E soil profile type based on the ASCE 
Standard ASCE/SEI 7-10 (Table No. 20.3-1), referenced by the IBC 2012. Based on a 
Site Class E soil profile, the following seismic design parameters were estimated and 
may be used for the seismic analysis of the structures planned at the site.

The allowable compressive load capacities for the micropiles are for supporting 
dead-plus-live loads and may be increased by one third (⅓) for transient loads, such as 
wind or seismic forces.  
Uplift loads imposed on the micropiles may be resisted by shear along the grout 
bulb (bonded zone) and the adjacent soils. In general, an uplift load capacity of up to 
29 kips per pile may be achieved with a bonded zone extending a minimum of 10 feet 
below the bottom of the casing. The uplift load capacity provided is intended for transient 
load applications only. Therefore, the uplift load capacity should not be further increased 
for transient load applications. For sustained uplift loads, we recommend reducing the 
uplift load capacity provided above by 50 percent to resist sustained uplift loads. The 
capacity of the pile member in tension should be checked by the project structural 
engineer. 
Per the requirements of the IBC 2012, we recommend providing permanent casing 
for the upper 23 feet of the micropiles. To achieve the allowable compressive load capacity 
with a factor of safety of 2, each micropile would need to achieve the minimum bonded 
zone lengths below the tip of the permanent casing presented in the tables above. The 
permanent casing should have an outside diameter (OD) of at least 5.5 inches (same as 
the grout bulb size).  
To facilitate the micropile drilling and grouting, we recommend advancing the steel 
casing to the bottom of the micropile during the drilling operation. The steel casing may be 
withdrawn during the grouting operation while maintaining a minimum 5-foot grout head 
above the bottom of the casing at all times. The casing should be withdrawn above the 
design casing depth (minimum 3 feet) and plunged back to the design casing depth. 
In general, the micropiles should have a minimum center-to-center spacing of about 
30 inches to avoid the reduction in vertical load capacity due to group action and to 
facilitate drilling of the micropiles. Other recommendations regarding the design, 
construction, and testing of the micropile foundation system are included in our original 
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration report dated March 8, 2019.
