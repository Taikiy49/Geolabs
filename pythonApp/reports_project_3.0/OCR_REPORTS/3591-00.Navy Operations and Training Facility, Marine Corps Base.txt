INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Navy Operations and Training Facility project located within
the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH at Kaneohe Bay on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located approximately at the corner of 6th and C Streets within
the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH at Kaneohe Bay on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to construct an
operations and training facility in support of the Navy aviation units relocating to MCBH
due to the closure of the Naval Air Station at Barbers Point

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion cut
valleys deep into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
The site of the MCBH at Kaneohe Bay is located on Mokapu Peninsula which was
built by three volcanic vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series These vents are believed to
be among the oldest of the series The pyroclastic deposits and lava flows from vents
formed salients permitting the growth of a coral reef that merged with the main body of
the island of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age the sea level fell below the present sea
level in response to continental glaciation Subsequently sands from emerged coral
reefs were blown inland by the prevailing winds to form sand dunes along the northern
portion of the airfield area An extensive deposit of unconsolidated calcareous marine
sediments primarily consisting of beach sand made up of water worn coral shells and
organisms covers the western tip of Mokapu Peninsula Man made fills consisting of
marine sediments dredged from the ocean floor were used to build up the area around
the southern and western shorelines of Mokapu Peninsula

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located approximately at the corner of 6th and C Streets within
the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH at Kaneohe Bay on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The subject site exists as a vacant field consisting of a maintained lawn The topography
of the site is generally level with a gently inclined drainage swale trending approximately
southeast to northwest to the north of the existing parking lot The ground surface
elevations at the project site range from about 116 to 120 feet At the time of our field
exploration the ground surface was generally dry with no ponding of water or yielding
surfaces
A rectangular shaped concrete slab elevated above the ground surface exists to
the east of the existing parking lot The ground surface appears to consist of a thin layer
of reddish brown clayey silt topsoil overlying a layer of sandy coral gravel fill in places
This thin coralline layer does not appear to exist in other locations The areas where the
proposed parking lot expansions will be located exist were generally level and maintained
as lawns
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven borings to
depths ranging from about 6 5 to 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings encountered a surficial fill crust consisting of medium dense
sandy gravels and gravelly sands of approximately 2 to 5 feet thick at the proposed
building location Below the surficial fill crust stiff to very stiff clayey silts were
encountered to a depth of approximately 6 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface

sands extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 51 5 feet below the
existing ground surface It should be noted that strong petroleum odor was encountered
in the samples and soil cuttings obtained from Boring Nos 6 and 7 during our field
exploration
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths ranging from about

elevations between about 101 and 102 5 feet Due to the proximity of the project site
to Kaneohe Bay it is likely that the groundwater level would be affected by tidal
fluctuations In addition groundwater levels are also subject to change due to seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered medium dense silty sands and stiff
clayey silts throughout the depths of the borings drilled at the subject site Based on the
existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation new fills on the order of about
1 to 3 feet will be required to attain the design finish grades for the proposed construction
Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings and the
anticipated structural loads it is our opinion that shallow foundations bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils or new engineered fills may be used for support of the proposed
building Our laboratory swell test results indicate that the near surface soils exhibit a

Building Foundations
Based on the current design concept and the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the proposed Navy Operations and Training Facility
building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f
may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or
new engineered fills needed to achieve the final design grades This bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near top of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3591 00 Page 9
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new engineered fills as
recommended herein to be less than one inch total with differential settlements on the
order of half inch
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations
located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used
for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new engineered fills
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
granular soils overlying near surface clayey silt soils with slight to moderate swelling
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the slightly to moderately expansive subgrade soils
we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least
6 inches of non expansive select granular fill material placed in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report
For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that
a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab
To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top
of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above the
plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered
since they can better mask any minor slab cracking
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation a
6 inch layer of compacted base course is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion
layer The 2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted for these slabs The
base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of about 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base
course A minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes for slabs subjected to vehicular traffic Provisions should be made for proper
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3591 00 Page 11
load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened
edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase be provided below the concrete walkway slab Construction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right
angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation

Based on the existing topography and the finish floor elevation of the proposed

minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 3 feet relative to the existing ground surface

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft
and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered
fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over
excavation and engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over
excavation quantities
The existing concrete slab under the future building footprint should be
demolished removed and disposed of off site Any over excavation resulting from
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3591 00 Page 15
the demolition should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing
utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should
be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities that are
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressures
After clearing grubbing and demolition the areas within the proposed building
limits should be over excavated where necessary to provide a minimum 6 inch
thick capping layer of non expansive select granular fill material below the slab
subgrade level i e bottom of slab cushion The select granular fill should extend
a minimum of 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed building structure
The over excavated subgrades or areas designated to receive fills should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade
we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted engineered fill
Fills and Backfills
Based on our field exploration the excavated on site granular soils excluding
clays and organic materials may be reused as a source of fill material Some of
the on site clayey soils are slightly to moderately expansive particularly if

Imported fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finish subgrades should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve

maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to
the project site for its intended use
The on site soils to be used as fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in loose lifts not exceeding

Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum

Drainage
The finished grades outside the new facility should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling seven
borings to depths ranging from about 6 5 to 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled

The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologistfengineer who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed
visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation
of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through
A 7 2

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
