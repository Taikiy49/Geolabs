Our field exploration disclosed that the proposed drainline alignment is generally
underlain by an alluvial deposit consisting of medium stiff to stiff reddish brown Clayey silt
materials with decomposed gravel at shallow depths Soft sediment deposit was
encountered at the upper 18 inches of subsoils between Station and 0 60

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our opinion that the proposed drainline installation is feasible from a
geotechnical point of view provided the recommendations presented in this letter report
are properly followed It appears that the stiff clayey silt material encountered at the
excavated test pits should provide adequate bearing support for the proposed drainline
alignment at the project site
Site Preparation
We recommend that site filling to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finish grades be performed prior to trench excavation and installation of the drain pipes
for the subject project

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
Materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the subgrade soils should be proof rolled to about 85
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Since the project site is located in
a high rainfall area where the in situ clayey soils have moisture contents much above the
soil optimum moisture content it is our opinion that the compaction requirement for the
in situ clayey silt material may be modified from the normal 90 percent relative compaction
to a lower compaction density of 85 percent
Select granular fill material needed to raise the existing ground surface to
approximately one foot from the proposed finish grades should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Select granular fill material should consist of imported granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
and should contain a maximum of 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
The on site clayey silt material resulting from over excavation work may be reused
as a source of capping fill material for the top one foot of fill from the proposed finish

pressure acting against the wall footings and by frictional resistance developed between
the bottom of the walls and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive earth pressure
may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per

The wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest
adjacent finish grade It is recommended that the bottom of all footing excavations be
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If soft and or loose boulder
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated down to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
either be backfilled with compacted select granular fill material or the footing bottom be
extended down to the firm in situ soils
We recommend that footing excavations and subsequent recompaction be
observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior to construction of the headwall
structures to confirm foundation bearing conditions and the embedment depths
Backfill Behind Headwall Structures
Headwall structures are anticipated at the inlets and outlets of the proposed
drainline alignment
Well graded granular materials such as base course rock No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or sand should be used as backfill material directly behind
the walls This is to reduce the build up of excessive lateral pressures on the walls
The top one foot of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material
to reduce infiltration or surface water behind the wail

granular fill material Tne backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts
and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for future ground subsidence The top one foot of fill may consist of the on
site clayey silt material generated during the earthwork operations
