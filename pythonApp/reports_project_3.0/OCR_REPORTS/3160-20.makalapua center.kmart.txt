INTRODUCTION
In accordance with our revised fee proposal of April 15th and the Underground
Injection Control U 1 C permit application No UH 1865 we have completed our visual
observations during construction of 7 of the 25 new drywells planned at the
Makalapua Shopping Center K Mart site In addition we have performed seven
injection tests for the new drywells This report has been prepared in support of
Underground Injection Control permit application No UH 1865 The approximate
location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 This report summarizes findings resulting from our field observations and
testing of the new drywells at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Makalapua Center K Mart project site is located approximately mile
northwest of Palani Road in the town of Kailua Kona in the District of North Kona on
the island of Hawaii The project consists of a new K Mart retail store within the new
Makalapua Shopping Center Associated with the commercial development are
roadway improvements for Makala Boulevard previously Makalapua Boulevard and
Kamakaeha Avenue previously Liliuokalani Avenue as well as highway widening along
Queen Kaahumanu Highway and Palani Road The County of Hawaii requires that new
development create no net increase in discharge of surface or storm water runoff The
general practice on the island of Hawaii is to utilize drywells as a method of disposal

A total of 25 new drywells have been constructed at the Makalapua Center and
vicinity for disposal of storm water runoff under UIC permit No UH 1865 Under the
conditions set forth in the approval to construct the drywells the Department of Health
has required that seven of the new drywell excavations be logged for geologic lithology
The same seven drywells are also required to undergo short term injection testing to
evaluate their operational status after construction

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in Keahuolu District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii
The climate of the area is mild with an average annual temperature of about 74 F and
annual rainfall ranges from about 20 to about 40 inches per year Rainfall at the site
typically occurs as daily light afternoon showers except during periods of stormy
weather where heavier rainfall can be expected
The site is situated on the lower western flank of Mount Hualalai The
topography is relatively gentle to moderate sloping down toward the ocean westward
from the central portion of the island Elevations across the site range from about 70
to about 200 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Formerly the site had been undeveloped rocky land covered with brush and

Regional Geology
The island of Hawaii is composed of five basalt shield volcanoes built by the
extrusion of lavas during the Pleistocene and the Holocene Epochs The site is located
on the western flank of Mount Hualalai a now dormant volcano which last erupted
between 1801 to 1803 This mountain is thought to be of an intermediate age between
Mauna Kea and Mauna Loa its larger neighboring mountains Hualalai appears to
have entered the late stage of the eruptive cycle Rocks of Hualalai Volcano cover a
portion of the western side of the island of Hawaii Most of its lavas are alkalic olivine
basalt and physically are composed of both Pahoehoe and A a flow types
Earthquake Conditions
The island of Hawaii is designated in the 1991 Uniform Building Code as
Seismic Zone 3 Seismic Zone 3 is considered to be an active zone of earthquake
occurrence Most of the earthquakes that occur on the island of Hawaii are related to
subsurface magma movement and are therefore termed volcanic earthquakes
The drywells were constructed in a relatively competent layered rock formation
with some rock fill The drywells generally consist of simple excavated pits lined with
perforated precast concrete rings and catch basin inlets at the ground surface A
typical cross section of a drywell is shown on Plate 3 Due to the below grade and
interlocking construction of the precast drywell rings seismic conditions are not
considered significant
Flood and Tsunami
Site grading to provide positive drainage of the site has been constructed in
addition the site is located inland of and at a higher elevation than the expected

Land Use
The site is situated below the Underground Injection Control Line for this portion
of the island of Hawaii and is in a suburban area zoned for mixed commercial and
residential development The site is constructed as a shopping center with access
roadways and parking lot areas Construction and use of drywells at the site is
considered to be consistent with this use
SYSTEM OPERATION
It is proposed to use the new drywells for the disposal of surface and storm
water runoff flowing from the paved roadways graded areas and the shopping center
site Use of the drywells will be intermittent and will be dependent upon weather
conditions and storm water runoff
The influent will not be treated prior to disposal and the volume and rate of
injection will vary according to the intensity of rainfall The design maximum injection
rate is 10 cubic feet per second per drywell Injection will be under gravity pressure
i e the head pressure that builds up within the drywell Since the new drywells are
intended for intermittent disposal of storm water runoff only there is no additional
standby system nor are menitoring wells planned
The complete drywell system will consist of 25 drywell sumps along the shopping
center access roadways and parking areas All 25 drywells are located down gradient
of the Underground Injection Control Line

GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The site is located at Latitude North and Longitude West
The ground surface elevations at the site range from approximately 70 to about 200
feet MSL Subsurface conditions are basalt rock formation generally consisting of
interbedded clinker and massive to fractured rock Groundwater was not observed in
the drywells selected for observation during our field work
The regional characteristics of groundwater in the area is fresh to brackish water
in a basal volcanic aquifer of relatively high permeability Published data indicates that
the groundwater table slopes towards the ocean at a gradient of about one foot per
mile Regional discharge is generally along the coastline
The lithologies of the logged drywell excavations are presented in Appendix A
of this report All depths reported in the logs were measured from the existing ground
surface to the bottom of the drywell during construction
At the time of our field work it was discovered that Drywell No 12 was to be
constructed in a later phase of the shopping center construction therefore the
Department of Health approved Drywell No 17 as a substitute drywell to undergo
lithologic logging and injection testing Subsequently it was found that Drywell No 17
had been completed and the precast concrete rings placed Because of this we were
unable to log the exposed portion of the excavation therefore a lithologic log could not
be provided for Drywell No 17 Drywell No 17 was inje tion tested

WELL CONSTRUCTION
Generally the drywells encountered interbedded layers of Pahoehoe and A a
basalts of the Hualalai Volcanic Series In general the drywells were constructed by
blasting and excavating the fractured rock materials A sketch showing the typical
drywell construction is provided on Plate 3
Based on observations of the selected drywells the entire depth of the wells
were lined with precast perforated concrete rings each with dimensions of 6 feet in
diameter and 4 feet in height Crushed rock and cobbles were used as backfill behind
the concrete rings and the sidewall The completed drywells ranged in depth from
about 15 to 25 feet below the finished ground surface
INJECTION TESTING
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the injection
of clear fresh water from a water tank truck with a capacity of approximately 4 000
gallons Injection rates were measured by use of an in line paddle wheel flow sensor
During testing clean water was injected at rates of about 150 to 180 gallons per minute
for a period of about 20 minutes depending on the flow rate for each test During the
injection of water the variations of the water level in the drywells were recorded with
respect to time and rate of injection
These tests indicated that the basalt formation has an approximate coefficient
of permeability k with a range on the order of approximately 480 to 1 200 feet per day
Groundwater was not observed in any of the excavated drywells prior to the beginning
of the test The results of the injection tests are presented in Appendix B

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from data points such as excavated drywells and injection
tests Variations of conditions between the drywells may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until the drywells have been put into
operation If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
findings presented in this report
The locations of the drywells were determined by others We can not assume
responsibility for the accuracy of the location and or elevation of the drywells as used
in this report
Water level data from the field data points if indicated was collected at the times
shown on the graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this
report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in
rainfall tides temperature and other factors
