Geology
The project site is located on the north shore of the island of Maui
on the northwest flank of Haleakala Volcano
The bulk of the Haleakala shield was built during the late Pliocene
and early Pleistocene by thin bedded basaltic lava flows of the Hono
manu Volcanic Series During the Pleistocene the characteristics of
the lavas changed to thickly bedded flows of andesitic composition
These lavas have been grouped as the Kula Volcanic Series and are
generally much less vesicular hence more dense and harder than the
more vesicular thinly bedded basalt of the Honomanu Volcanic series
The Kula lava flows buried most of the Honomanu rocks throughout East
Maui and in some areas those flows buried alluvial deposits derived
from both Honomanu and early Kula rocks At the project site most of
the area is underlain by what appears to be a single thick flow of
Kula lava that overlies the older alluvial soils From the findings
of this project and the Airside Improvement work it is believed
that the flow of Kula lava discussed above did not cover the area
along Kalialinui Gulch It is felt that Kalialinui Stream would not
have had sufficient erosive power to cut through the hard dense Kula
rock

Engineered Fill
The term engineered fill used in this text covers any fill
of acceptable quality which is placed and compacted in
accordance with our recommendations for general filling
Special materials such as base course subbase capillary
break material cushion drainrock filter rock pipe bedding
are specified elsewhere for example in References 10 11 and
12 and are not included as engineered fill consideration
Engineered fill material should be hon expansive soil free
of organic matter and form a dense stable soil mass when
compacted to the required density Generally the particle
size should be less than 8 inches in size The pavement
designs presented in this report were based on a subgrade CBR
value of 15 therefore the material Placed within the top 3
feet of the fill under the pavements should have a soaked CBR
of at least 15 per ASTM D 1883 73 when compacted to 95 of
the maximum densities determined according to ASTM D 1557 78
The total expansion of sample during the soaking for CBR test
should not exceed 1 otherwise the pavement design should
be revised

Settlement
The proposed fill thickness in the western one third of the project
area will vary from 2 to 14 feet averaging perhaps 5 5 feet The
fill will cause some settlement due to the consolidation of the
underlying soft alluvial organic silt and loose dume sand deposits
encountered in Borings 8 and 1 The estimated settlements are
relatively small on the order of 2 to 4 inches under 5 to 10 feet of
fill These settlements should occur rapidly probably within 3
months after the application of the total fill weight Remaining long
term settlements after construction should be less than 1 2 inch
Therefore the mass grading should be done as soon as possible and at
least 3 months prior to the construction of structures that may be
sensitive to settlements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 20
It is probable that the distribution and thickness of soft organic
silt and loose sand may vary along the Kalialinui stream channel and
differential settlement may be Significant Therefore it is
recommended that any existing utility lines such as sewer water
fuel or drain lines under thick fill in this area should be removed
and rerouted as deemed necessary to avoid rupture due to the
settlement from new fill
Because of the abovementioned potential settlements routing of water
main or sewer main along the new Hemaloa Street or construction of
new lines in the adjacent areas should be delayed as late as possible
and at least 3 months after the mass grading is completed
The actual ground settlement should be verified by monitoring the
settlement observation markers The survey reading of the marker
elevation should be done at least once every two weeks Flexible
joints should be provided using shorter sections for sewer or water
main along the filled street and between Hemaloa Street and the area
underlain by basalt rock
Ideally the mass grading involving large fill over compressible soil
area should be scheduled as early as possible to minimize delay of the
pavement and utility construction due to the anticipated settlements

Subdrainage
If on site residual soil of reddish brown silt is used as engineered
fill proper subsurface drainage will be particularly important in the
pavement area It is recommended that pavement subgrade be so shaped
to drain away from paved area For example the pavement thickness
for a heavy traffic road area may be significantly thicker than the
shoulders Thus the subgrade elevation may be lower under the road
than under sidewalk or shoulder areas Such a situation is conducive
to trapping water under the pavement Suitable subdrain or blankets
should be provided to bleed off accumulation of water in such a case
or a thicker shoulder section may be used Small 1 to 2 inch diameter
weepholes should be provided through the walls of catch basins or
manhole to drain seepage water at the subgrade level provided that
water in the catch basin or manhole will not rise above the weephole
level

the utility pipes soil resistivity and pH tests were conducted on
selected soil samples The test results are summarized on Plate B 9
in Appendix B The tests were made in accordance to the California
test method 643 B Reference 16
Generally the soils tested have pH values about 7 to 8 and resis
tivity values from 400 to 14 000 ohms sq cm therefore the test
data indicates that the soils may be moderately corrosive to corrosive

edlieeieiontiasidtet
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifica
tion and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths The Atterberg Limits are also summarized on
Plates B 1 through B 3 and plotted on Plate B 4
Four laboratory compaction tests were conducted on bulk samples
obtained from cuttings of borings The same type of soil
cuttings from different borings were combined in some cases
The results of these tests are shown on Plates B 1 through B 3
Two specific gravity tests were performed on typical samples of
the underlying soil The test results are presented on Plates
B 1 through B 3
Five laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples
obtained from the auger cuttings near the proposed finished
pavement elevation to evaluate the subgrade Support capacity of
the compacted soil The test results are summarized on Plates
B 1 through B 3
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils in the natural and compacted condition The test results
are summarized on Plate B 5
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural soil The
test results are presented on Plates B 6 and B 7
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed sample
to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the natural
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 8
Six pH and resistivity tests were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the corrosion potential of the soils along the
utility alignments The test results are summarized on Plate
B 9
