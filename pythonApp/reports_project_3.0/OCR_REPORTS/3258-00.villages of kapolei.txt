PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop an idealized subsurface data set for
the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design
of road pavement site grading and utility construction for the project The work was
performed in general accordance with our proposal dated March 28 1994 The scope
of our work for this exploration included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
2 Drilling and sampling of four borings along the proposed road alignments
to a depth of about 16 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Engineer from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the
materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
geotechnical engineering recommendations for the proposed project

6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in Appendix A of this
report Results of the laboratory tests are presented in Appendix B
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is proposed to construct a road of approximately 800 lineal feet between Villages 5
and 6 and a road of approximately 600 lineal feet between Villages 7 and 8 The
proposed Villages 5 and 6 Bisector Road and Villages 7 and 8 Bisector Road are
located to the north and south of the existing Ewa Parkway Road respectively as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on the current plans the Bisector Roads will
intersect the existing Ewa Parkway Road at approximately Station Parkway
Road alignment
Based on the current plans the proposed Bisector Roads will have approximate
ly 50 feet of right of way Various utility lines are planned along both bisector roads
In general water lines will be located about 3 to 4 feet below the finish grade of the
roadway and drain lines will be located at greater depths
It is our understanding that minor cuts are anticipated at the Villages 5 and 6
Bisector Road and fills on the order of 1 foot are planned at the Villages 7 and 8
Bisector Road for the proposed roadway construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in an open and relatively flat area between the
proposed Kapolei Villages 5 and 6 and Villages 7 and 8 housing developments The
Villages 5 and 6 Bisector Road lies to the north of the existing Ewa Parkway and has
an existing elevation of about 62 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The site is currently
vegetated with grasses The Villages 7 and 8 Bisector Road is located to the south of
the existing Ewa Parkway with an existing ground elevation of about 57 feet MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS

of about 16 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the project site is
underlain by stiff to hard silty clay soils which have moderate to high swelling potential
Therefore design of the proposed improvements will need to take into consideration
the moderately to highly expansive soils at the ground surface Our geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of road pavements site grading
and utility construction for the proposed Bisector Roads are presented in the following
sections
Pavement Design
It is our understanding that asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the
Bisector Roads In general the anticipated subgrade soils of the proposed Bisector
Roads consist of the near surface silty clays encountered in our test borings Based
on our field exploration and laboratory tests the anticipated subgrade soil appears to
Considering the medium traffic anticipated for the proposed Bisector Roads we
recommend the following preliminary pavement designs for budgeting purposes
2 5 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Basaltic Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
18 0 Inch Select Borrow 95 percent relative compaction
26 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
A minimum of 6 inches of select borrow should be incorporated below concrete
walkways wheelchair ramps and passages to reduce the potential of heaving The
_ select borrow should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3258 00 Page 6
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
To reduce the swelling potential of the road subgrade the upper 6 inches of the
subgrade should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density If shrinkage cracks appear on the
subgrade prior to placement of select borrow the subgrade soils will need to be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction All subgrades should be thoroughly moistened and kept moist until
covered with select borrow and or base course
We recommend that CBR and density tests be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections
The recommended sections also assume that adequate drainage will be provided for
the paved areas Weep holes should be provided in drainage inlet structures to relieve
water which may accumulate at the subgrade and cause pumping failure of the
pavement The weep holes should be installed at the bottom of the base course and
subbase course levels
Site Grading
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the site should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All existing surface vegetation and deleterious materials within the
limits of the proposed Bisector Roads should be removed and disposed of off
site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3258 00 Page 7
2 Any zones of wet saturated or yielding areas encountered during the clearing
process should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill This
should be evaluated by the project geotechnical engineer Contract documents
should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation
quantities
3 Areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction unless
specified otherwise Saturation and subsequent yielding of the subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft
areas and replacement with select granular material
4 All fill and backfill material should be evaluated by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to use Because the site grading consists only of minor cuts we
anticipate that borrow fill material would be needed for this project Borrow
material should meet the material requirements for Borrow as specified in
Section 31 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated
September 1986
5 Generally all fill and backfill material should be placed in 8 inch loose level lifts
moisture conditioned to 2 percent above optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction unless specified
otherwise

In roadway areas the upper 2 feet of fill select borrow and base course
materials below the finish grade should be compacted to not less than 95
percent relative compaction Due to the expansive nature of the subgrade soils
over compaction of the subgrade soil is not recommended Therefore the
clayey on site soils below the pavements should be moisture conditioned to 2
percent above optimum moisture content compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction
The project site is relatively flat however there may be localized areas of
relatively steep slopes To properly bond new fill to the existing ground the new
fill material should be keyed and benched into existing slope where the existing
slope is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
6 In general cut and fill slopes may be designed with an inclination of 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical or flatter Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the
design fill slope and then cutting back Surface water run off should be diverted
away from the top of slopes and slope planting should be utilized to reduce the
potential for erosion
7 Observations and soil density tests should be performed during grading to assist
the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper
moisture content on each fill lift Where compaction is less than required
additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture
content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction

Utility Trench
It is anticipated that the utilities for the roadway construction will consist primarily
of water drainage and sewer We anticipate that the drainage line will be at a depth
of about 10 feet below the road finish grade and the sewer and water lines will be at
depths of about 3 to 5 feet below the road finish grade
In general good construction practices should be utilized for the installation and
backfilling of the trench for the proposed utilities The method and equipment to be
used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits
and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all applicable local
state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and installation should
be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing should conform to
the appropriate health and safety requirements
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that in general
conventional excavation methods may be utilized for utility line trenching at the site
The subsurface clayey soil materials encountered at the site are generally medium stiff
to very hard However deeper excavation may encounter dense coral formation as
described in the logs of borings
Utilities should be placed on a 6 inch cushion of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation Free draining granular backfill such as No 3B Fine gravel should
be used around the pipe and up to 12 inches above the top of the pipe It is critical
that a free draining granular material be used to reduce the potential of formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes The use of on site clayey soils as backfill around utility pipes is not recommend
ed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3258 00 Page 10
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inch loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In roadway
and structural areas the upper 2 feet of backfill below finish grade should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials at
the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding may not be used to compact the
on site clayey soils
Manhole and Catch Basin Structures
In general we believe that the bottom of manhole and catch basin structures will
be bearing on the very stiff silty clay or medium dense silty sand to sandy coral gravel
encountered within 10 feet of the ground surface in our borings To provide more
uniform support for the manhole structures we recommend that the manhole structures
be supported on a 6 inch layer of cushion fill consisting of compacted select borrow or
No 3B Fine gravel For these foundation conditions an allowable bearing pressure of
up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design Settlements on the
order of less than 1 inch are anticipated for these structures
The lateral earth pressures acting on the proposed underground manhole
structures will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of backfill and the
compactive effort on the backfill material around the structures Therefore granular
material with less than 10 percent passing the No 200 sieve is recommended for use
as backfill around the structures to reduce the compactive effort required which may
cause additional lateral earth pressures We recommend that the new manhole
structures be designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 50 pounds per cubic foot
p c f The above values assume that the backfill around the manhole structures is
properly placed and compacted

Construction Observation
It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe the site
grading utility backfill and road construction of the project This is to observe
compliance with the intent of the design concept specifications and or recommenda
tions and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated during design
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made by a qualified geotechnical engineer

conditions between and beyond the test borings may occur and the nature and extent
of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented
in this report
