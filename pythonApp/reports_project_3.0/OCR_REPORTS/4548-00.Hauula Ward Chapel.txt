REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island After the main shield building phase of Koolau the volcanic dome
became deeply eroded On the windward side of the island combined wind rain and wave
erosion cut the coastline back while forming steep cliffs
The project site is located on the windward northeastern flank of Koolau and its
subsurface conditions are directly related to this deep erosion and littoral processes at the
coast The subsurface conditions within the project area are dominated by beach and
lagoonal deposits which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the parking lot addition project consisted of drilling
and sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of
about 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition two bulk samples designated
as Bulk 1 and Bulk 2 were obtained from the near surface materials to evaluate its

In general our field exploration encountered loose to medium dense silty sands
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 6 5 feet below the existing
ground surface It should be noted that Boring No 3 which was drilled within the existing

encountered below the asphalt concrete paving in the boring drilled within the existing
pavements Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field
exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 6 of this report

Surface Water Run Off Disposal System
Three in situ falling head permeability tests were conducted to evaluate the
infiltration characteristics of the subsoils to provide data for design of the facilities to be
used for disposal of storm water run off The permeability tests were conducted in
Boring Nos 1 through 3 at depths of about 1 5 and 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
During the falling head testing the boreholes were carefully filled with water to the top
of the boreholes The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data
upon which to base calculation of the permeability values of the subsoils Based on the
permeability tests conducted the calculated permeability of the tested subsoils range from
about 1 8 x 10 to 5 9 x 10 feet per minute The results of our permeability tests are
presented on Plates 11 through 13 Due to the potential for variability of the subsoil
conditions the absorption capacity of the proposed disposal system should be confirmed by
additional percolation tests during construction
The planned surface water run off disposal facilities should be located away from the
building foundations since water leaching into the ground from the disposal facilities could
cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of bearing strength of the foundation
soils resulting in foundation settlement and building distress
