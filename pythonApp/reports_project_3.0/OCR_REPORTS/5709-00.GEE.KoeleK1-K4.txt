SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Subdivision project in Lanai City on the Island of Lanai
Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the design of foundations
slabs on grade retaining structures site grading and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The proposed project involves consolidating and re subdividing Land Court
Lots 45 A and 45 B in the Koele Project District in Lanai City on the Island of Lanai
Hawaii Based on the original conceptual design we understand that 80 new residential
lots a new park site and one remainder bulk lot will be created The conceptual design
was subsequently revised to provide for a total of 145 lots and a new park site
encompassing approximately 52 acres as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general we envision that the proposed residences will consist of lightly loaded
construction Perimeter wall loads for the proposed buildings are estimated to be less
than 1 000 pounds per lineal foot
New roadways consisting of flexible pavements will be constructed to provide
access to the new residential lots In addition installation of new underground utility
lines such as electrical cable telephone water sewer and drainage will be required to
service the new residential lots Details for the proposed lot grading and site
improvements construction of roadways with underground utilities were not available
at the time this report was prepared Based on the sloping terrain across the project

2 1 Site Description
The new subdivision site is in the Koele Project District area in Lanai City on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii The project site is a parcel of land that is approximately
shoe shaped and encompasses approximately 52 acres It is approximately bounded by
Kauna oa Drive and an existing golf course to the north undeveloped land to the east
Queens Street and residential dwellings to the south and Ninth Street to the west as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations the project site is heavily vegetated with wild
grasses shrubs and trees especially towards the west side The east and central parts
of the project site are traversed by some dirt trails which have been overgrown with
vegetation and are no longer accessible to vehicles
Based on the topographic survey map provided the project site slopes down
slightly toward the south for drainage In general the existing ground surface ranges
from approximately Elevation 1 760 feet Mean Sea Level MSL along the north to
approximately Elevation 1 620 feet MSL along the southern boundary
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling nineteen borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 19 each extending to a depth of about 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface In addition we collected five bulk samples of the
near surface soils at selected locations to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics The approximate boring and bulk sample locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our borings indicated that the project site is generally underlain by stiff to very
stiff sandy and clayey silts with some basaltic gravel extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 21 5 feet below the ground surface Laboratory tests conducted on
the near surface soils indicate that the materials exhibit a slight to moderate expansion
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations

Our field exploration encountered stiff to very stiff sandy and clayey silts
extending to the maximum depth explored Based on the subsurface conditions
encountered and the geology of the area we believe that the project site may be
classified as a Stiff Soil Profile Therefore we believe that the seismic design of the
structures planned may be designed based on a Soil Profile Type Sp in accordance with
Table 16 J of the Uniform Building Code 1997

3 1 Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we recommend using shallow
spread and or continuous footings to support the new residential structures planned at
the project site As an alternative the foundations for support of the structural loads of
the residential structures may consist of thickened edge slab footings An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the
design of footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills
placed to achieve the design finish grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by
wind or seismic forces
Due to the expansive nature of the on site soils footings should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade Footings constructed
near the tops of slopes or on slopes should be embedded deep enough such that the
horizontal distance measured from the outside edge at the base of the footing to the
face of the slope is no less than 6 feet
Foundations next to other foundations utility trenches or easements should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench or the footings should extend to as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
In general the subgrade soils at the bottom of footing excavations should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to placing reinforcing steel or concrete Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557

Hawaii California

Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
should be over excavated until dense and or stiff materials are exposed The
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular fill materials
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a

Differential settlements between adjacent footings supported on similar materials may
be on the order of about 0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by friction developed

pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff on site soils or new
compacted fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an

placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations

3 5 Pavement Design
We understand that new asphalt concrete paved access roads will be
constructed In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for these proposed
roadways would primarily consist of passenger vehicles with some light trucks
Therefore we have assumed generally light to medium traffic loading conditions for
pavement design purposes
We have assumed that the pavement subgrade soils will be similar to the sandy
and clayey silt soils encountered during our field exploration with a CBR value of about
30 and a swell of no more than 1 percent On this basis we recommend using the

depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted

3 6 Underground Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines i e water sewer and drain lines will be
required It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will be excavated in the
stiff to very stiff sandy and clayey silt soils
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended for support below the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of the pipes and to provide adequate support for
the sides of the pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper
placement of the backfill could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular materials generally
less than 6inches in maximum particle size The backfill material should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction The upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction

3 7 Drainage
The finished grades outside the houses should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the buildings and to divert discharge away from
the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations within 3 feet should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for excessive
water infiltration into the subsurface
To reduce the potential for excessive water infiltration into the subsurface the
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges
immediately after setting of the concrete In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff
away from the foundations and slabs

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling nineteen borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 19 extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight auger
tools The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 19
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in

compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Nineteen one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are
summarized on Plates B 1 through B 3
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 4
Five laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture content Test
results are presented on Plates B 5 through B 9
Five Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships Test results are presented on Plates B 10 through B 14
