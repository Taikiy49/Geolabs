conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was utilized to develop
a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the
design of the concrete fender piles for the project To accomplish this we completed the
following tasks and work efforts

activities adjacent to open waters Pearl Harbor
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig with an
over the water platform and two operators to the project site and back

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at Bravo Piers B7 through B9 within the Pearl Harbor
Naval Station on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located near Avenue D
and Seventh Street Bravo Piers B7 and B8 are both connected and tun in a
northeast southwest direction Bravo Pier B7 is further away from land Bravo Pier B9 is
connected to Bravo Pier B8 and is perpendicular to Bravo Piers B7 and B8 The project
site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Bravo Piers B7 through B9 consists of a concrete pier deck supported on square
concrete piles Based on the information provided the top of deck elevation is at
approximately 111 feet USND The total length of Bravo Piers B7 and B8 is about
680 linear feet Bravo Pier B9 is about 290 feet long
The existing timber fender piles to be demolished and removed are located along
the south side of Bravo Piers B7 and B8 and along the east side of Bravo Pier B9 Based
on our field observations the existing timber fender pile system appears to be in relatively
poor condition The timber fender piles are spaced at about 3 feet center to center The
water level during our field exploration ranged from about 10 5 to 12 feet below the
existing pier deck
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three test borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 103 B 101 through B 103 In
general the borings drilled extended to depths of about 84 5 to 110 feet below the existing
pier deck surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
rotary wash coring tools In addition two probes were performed designated as Probe
below the existing pier deck surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled and
the probes advanced are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a

Our field exploration and the field exploration performed by Pacific Geotechnical
Engineers Inc information contained in the Contract Documents indicated that the
mudline at the project site ranges between about 72 and 69 feet USND Below the
mudline the subsurface conditions generally consisted of very soft clays and silts harbor
deposits The harbor deposits extended down to about Elevation 66 to 56 feet USND

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The pile tip elevations of 45 feet USND at Bravo Pier B7 from Bents 1 to 17 and
40 feet USND at Bravo Pier B7 from Bents 17 to the end Bravo Pier B8 and Bravo Pier
Bg in the Contract Documents were based on a mudline elevation of 60 feet USND and
the borings performed by Pacific Geotechnical Engineers Inc
The additional borings drilled at the project site by Geolabs generally indicated that
the subsurface conditions appear similar to the subsurface conditions encountered by
Pacific Geotechnical Engineers Inc However our boring drilled near Bent 1 of Bravo
Pier B7 indicated that soft and or loose soils were encountered to about Elevation 20 feet
USND Then the soils graded to medium dense extending to about Elevation 2 feet
USND In addition the top of the volcanic tuff was encountered as deep as 55 feet
USND between Bents 4 and 15 of Bravo Pier B7 Because the subsurface conditions at
the far end of Bravo Pier B7 appears to differ from the subsurface conditions anticipated
and contemplated in the Contract Documents we recommend that the fender pile tip
elevations between Bents 1 and 13 extend deeper beyond the tip elevations indicated in
the Contract Documents
Based on the information provided the Contractor s pile handling and driving
equipment is not capable of handling piles with lengths greater than 82 feet Contractor
determined pile driving equipment based on the original lengths provided in the Contract
Documents therefore we have limited the concrete fender pile tip elevation to about
30 feet USND between Bents 1 and 13 of Bravo Pier B7
As indicated previously soft and or loose soils were encountered near the far end
of Bravo Pier B7 extending down to about Elevation 20 feet USND Therefore the
originally indicated embedment length of the concrete fender piles between Bents 1 and 5
of Bravo Pier B7 may not have sufficient resistance to support the dead weight of the
concrete fender pile with a tip elevation of 30 feet USND Therefore we anticipate that
some pile settlement may occur after the installation of the concrete fender piles between
Bents 1 through 5 with a tip elevation of 30 feet USND Based on our experience with
similar soil conditions we estimate that the concrete pile may settle on the order of about
1 to 6 inches after pile installation when the pile tip is terminated in the soft and or loose
soils If the piles between Bents 1 and 5 of Bravo Pier B7 are driven to the recommended
tip elevation of 30 feet USND without encountering a resistant subsurface layer for
bearing purposes it is possible that the pile may settle below the planned top of pile
elevation resulting in some additional loading on the fender pile collars

To address the above mentioned conditions associated with appreciable concrete
fender pile settlement at the far end of Pier Bravo B7 we recommend that the concrete
fender piles between Bents 1 and 5 of Bravo Pier B7 be driven to about 24 inches above
the estimated tip elevation of 30 feet USND After driving the piles to about 24 inches
above the estimated pile tip elevation the top of pile elevation should be monitored for
vertical movement or settlement We recommend that the top of pile elevation for each
concrete fender pile be taken immediately after pile installation to establish a baseline
elevation Subsequent readings of the top of pile elevation should be taken on a daily
basis The top of pile elevation readings should be transmitted to Geolabs for review in a
timely manner If the readings of the pile top elevations do not show any significant
movement then the Contractor may either cut the top of the pile to grade or push the pile
to the planned top of pile elevation

As mentioned previously we understand that it is proposed to replace the existing
timber pile fender system with a 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete pile fender
system In general the concrete fender pile system will absorb the energy generated by
the vessel berthing velocities Therefore the lateral load capacity of the 24 inch square
precast prestressed concrete fender piles is the main design consideration for the fender
pile system
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer the lateral
load imposed on a single pile is approximately 35 kips The lateral load likely will act at
depths between about 7 and 11 feet below the top of the pile A distributed load of about
10 kips per lineal foot applied between about 9 3 and 12 8 feet below the top of pile for a
total force of 35 kips was used in our engineering analyses of the fender piles
The lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for
Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program solves for
deflection and bending moment along a pile under lateral loads as a function of depth

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of the subsurface conditions
between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the Existing Conditions Topside Repair and
Demolition Plan prepared by Consulting Structural Hawaii Inc contained in the Contract
Documents Elevations of the borings shown on the Logs of Borings were determined
from the Pacific Geotechnical Engineers Inc Boring Logs provided in the Contract
Documents The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall temperature and other
factors
