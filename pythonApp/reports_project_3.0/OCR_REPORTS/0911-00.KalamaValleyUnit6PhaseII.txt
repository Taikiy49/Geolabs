INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of an additional soil in
vestigation for the above referenced subdivision development
The work was performed in accordance with our proposal dated
The grading plans for the project by Gray Rhee and Asso
Ciates Inc dated were used as a reference
for our work
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations for
the proposed development
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A previous soil investigation and subsequent report was pre
pared by our office on May 17 1976 for the entire development
area Phase I and II This previous exploration disclosed
the presence of relatively deep adobe Clay deposits in the
northern head of the valley area It appears that this area
contains former gullies which were filled with adobe clay
colluvium This type of adobe clay soil has tremendous
Slope creep and expansion potential and it is susceptible to
landslides These soils exhibit a tremendous loss of strength
low friction angle and cohesion when saturated
GEOLABS HAWAII
Kalama Valley Unit 6 Phase II P 3
The general grading concept for the development consisted of
removing the relatively thin upper adobe layer down to the
underlying rocky or boulderly deposits then utilizing the
adobe soil below the deeper fill areas Due to the thick
deposits of adobe clay in the northern portion of the valley
this general grading concept was not feasible in this area
since the high cuts would expose the thick adobe and create
an unstable condition Therefore it was recommended that
these northern drainageway areas be left undeveloped
A review of the above referenced grading plan for the Phase II
portion of the development indicated that grading involving
cutting into the deep adobe is planned in portions of these
drainageway areas which in our Opinion may be susceptible to
later slope instability
The purpose of this additional test pit exploration was to
delineate more accurately the extent of the adobe clay and
boulder colluvium deposits and to evaluate the feasibility of
developing this area
Due to the quite variable soil rock conditions encountered in
the slopes excavated so far Phase I and the very limited
flexibility of flattening the cut Slopes once the interceptor
ditch is constructed prior to cutting a series of test pits

better delineate the soil rock conditions Based on these
additional findings the recommended design cut slope ratios

SOIL CONDITIONS
The entire development area is blanketed by a layer of highly
expansive adobe clay
Along the eastern and western valley sides the adobe clay
is relatively thin one to two feet near the upper lot
boundaries and increases in thickness at lower elevations
Rock formation was noted in some areas near the upper lot
boundaries Broad filled drainageways extending down the hill
side were noted on each side of the valley The deposits in
these drainageways consisted of layers of boulder colluvium
and tuffaceous clayey silt and boulders with varying amounts
of cementation and densities
At the head of the valley area two large drainageways were
noted separated by a narrow mountain ridge The smaller
western drainageway contained up to about 12 feet of adobe
clay and boulders over dense boulder deposit or rock formation

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Along the valley side slopes the field exploration disclosed
a surface adobe clay layer generally less than 3 feet over
boulder colluvium and tuffaceous clayey silt and boulders
or adobe clay over rock formation
The general grading concept of cutting the upper area removing
the adobe clay down to rock or dense boulder colluvium and
filling the lower central area can be used here The recommended
Slope cuts will vary with the subsoils encountered in the cut
slopes
However in the head of the valley area relatively deep depo
sits of adobe clay were encountered Where the adobe clay
is fairly thick about 10 feet or more and extends up slope
beyond the property development utilizing cuts into this
adobe natural ground should be avoided due to the difficulty

of stabilizing the large mass of adobe soil above the cut
Slope Where the adobe clay is less than 10 feet thick the
area can be developed only if special buttress fill design
are used to stabilize the downhill creep of these soils More
detailed dsicussions and recommendations for each of these two

Head of the Valley Area
Lots 90 to 97 137 to 139 and 195 to 200
The field exploration disclosed relatively deep deposits of
adobe clay soils in the two major drainageways located in
this area
Slickensided zones were noted in some of the test pits indicating
shear creep movement of the deposit
These deep sloping adobe clay deposits have high shrink
swell potential tremendous downhill creep tendency as well as
landslide potential particularly when steepened by large cuts
The general grading concept of removing the upper adobe clay
deposit down to rock or dense competent boulder colluvium
cannot be utilized in this area due to the thickness and large
extent of the adobe deposit Extensive grading up the hill
side would be required to remove the adobe deposits beyond
the project limits If the deep upper adobe deposits are
not removed further steepening of these natural slopes by
GEOLABS HAWAIL
Kalama Valley Unit 6 Phase II P 8
cutting would create an unstable condition and could trigger
eventual downhill movement of the adobe soils
The adobe clay soils are very weak soils which are signi
ficantly affected by increase in moisture content and are sub
ject to slope creep or landslide movement during extended wet
periods
The high clay content and slickensided zones in the adobe
deposit create weak planes which substantially reduce the shear
Strength of the soil mass Therefore residual shear strength
values which are Significantly lower than the normally used
peak shear values must be utilized to evaluate the long term
Stability of these clay soils The slickensided surfaces also
reduce the effective cohesion which can be developed by the
soil particles Laboratory direct shear tests indicate a
residual effective friction angle of about 14 degrees and a
low cohesion value of less than 100 p s f for these on site
soils
Utilizing residual strength values slope ratios flatter than
4 1 H V depending on the slope height are recommended for
long term slope stability requirement Since the existing
ground surface in these areas already slopes down at gradients
GEOLABS HAWAII
Kalama Valley Unit 6 Phase TI P 9
to cut further into the adobe clay soil and still maintain
a stable slope ratio
The recommended grading concepts for this type of terrain and
soil condition are
1 avoid large cuts into the hillside and instead
fill up the lower areas to create a flat slope
and provide a buttress effect against the toe of
the existing adobe clay slope
2 the adobe soil below the uphill lots on the
natural slope should be removed and replaced with
select material to provide resistance against
the downhill slope movements of the adobe de
posit if the uphill lots are developed
If these recommended general grading concepts cannot be imple
mented and the upper adobe clay cannot be stabilized we
recommend that this area should be left undeveloped
