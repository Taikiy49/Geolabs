INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed in support of the planning and preliminary engineering for the
proposed North South Road project located in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The location and vicinity of the proposed site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations based on our site reconnaissance and literature research performed for
the proposed project These geotechnical findings and recommendations are intended for
general earthwork guidelines slope stability of cuts and fills and pavement design for the
preliminary design purposes only The findings and preliminary recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing East Kapolei Development in the
District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we
understand that the proposed North South Road alignment will stretch from Papipi Road to
the Interstate Route H 1 Highway as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
proposed North South Road will be one of the major arterial roadways that will service the
on going developments in the Kapolei and Ewa areas
Based on the available information the proposed roadway will cover approximately
8 000 lineal meters We understand that a preliminary alignment of the proposed roadway
is set within a corridor of approximately 100 meters in width We also understand that a
study will be conducted for a possible interchange at the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
intersection The proposed alignment will generally traverse through old cane fields along
the existing power lines at the northern portion of the site At the southern portion of the
site the proposed roadway will traverse close to several existing residential developments
in Ewa and through vacant lands toward the existing Papipi Road

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was formed by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two main
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau beginning in the mid Pliocene Epoch The older
volcano Waianae was the first to develop closely followed by the development of Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 5
volcano For a period of time Oahu was actually comprised of two islands formed by each
of the volcanic centers The volcanoes continued to erupt and bank against the slopes of
each other with Waianae terminating its eruptive activity prior to the cessation of Koolau
Following extrusion of the lavas in the early Pleistocene Epoch the island
underwent a long cycle of erosion and weathering forming the prominent ridgelines and
summits as we know today During the erosion period Oahu began to slowly subside
more than 1 200 feet in elevation resulting in the drowning and sedimentation of the
valleys and the formation of the steep Koolau Pali Coral reefs continued to grow in the
surrounding shallow waters of the island
From the mid to late Pleistocene Epoch the sea level repeatedly rose and fell in
response to global glaciation and the availability of surface waters to sustain the oceans
The various sea level elevations and their representative deposits are known as stands
and include from oldest to youngest the Kahuku Kahipa Kaena Laie Waialae Waipio
and Waimanalo stands Geologic deposits associated with the various sea level stands
including marine sediments and coral reefs were deposited and subsequently altered or
removed by later sea level fluctuation Therefore depositional records reflecting the
changes in sea level and the occurrence of emerged coral reef deposits are often
incomplete
The project site is situated on the Ewa Plain located on the southeast flank of the
Waianae mountains The Ewa Plain is a gently sloping alluvial plain formed by the
deposition of alluvial clays and silts derived from the weathering of basalt rock further
up slope The alluvial deposits form a wedge shaped profile which pinches out and
becomes coral reef formation as the southern shoreline is approached Beneath the
surficial clayey alluvium coral reef deposits intercalated with marine sediments coral
detritus and alluvium exist The thickness of the coral and marine deposits increases
toward the present day southern shoreline Basalt rock formation resides below the
marine deposits at a substantial depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 6
The majority of the project site northern portion is situated over the alluvial clay
soils The coral and marine deposits are believed to underlie the site at various depths
beneath the alluvium The southern most portion of the project site is underlain by
coralline deposits at the existing ground surface as the alluvial wedge thins towards the
southern shoreline
SITE DESCRIPTION
A site reconnaissance was conducted to record field observations pertaining to the
existing site conditions soil types and potential geotechnical hazards at the site The
reconnaissance was performed to confirm preliminary analyses derived from our literature
research and aerial photograph review
The proposed North South Road corridor will stretch from approximately Papipi
Road to the Interstate Route H 1 Highway for a total of about 8 000 lineal meters The
proposed roadway is located in the District of Ewa of the island of Oahu and traverses
through old cane fields several recent developments and vacant open land in the Ewa
area
The project site is part of the Ewa Plain downslope of the Makakilo area The
northern portion of the site once sustained a large acreage of sugar cane cultivation
Today sugar cane cultivation has ceased and the area is being developed incrementally
for residential and commercial use The northern portion of the site exists as
undeveloped vacant land vegetated by grass and some abandoned sugar cane The
ground surface is generally obscured by the growth of vegetation The average height of
the vegetation is about 1 to 1 5 meters above ground level with most of the sugar cane
growth having been removed from the site Trees are generally absent from the
landscape except where they line the banks of the existing drainage gulch features
A high voltage power transmission system and curving easement traverses from
north to south through the northern portion of the site The system consists of steel power
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 7
poles linked by overhead power lines We understand that the proposed North South
Road alignment will generally follow along the sides of these power lines
Many of the pre existing cane haul roads visible on area maps and aerial
photographs at the northern portion of the proposed roadway alignment have been
overgrown and are no longer readily visible or traveled The main arterial gravel and
asphalt haul roads near the site remain with gates at the perimeter of the cane fields
Several of the gates were locked at the time of our site reconnaissance The gates at
some of the haul roads were observed to be open and accommodating light truck traffic
possibly associated with agricultural operations
The Kaloi gulch is a prominent drainage feature which traverses the proposed
alignment at the northern half of the site Where the existing haul roads intersect the
drainage gulch bridge and culvert crossings were observed The Kaloi gulch appears to
incise alluvial clay soils to depths ranging from 1 5 to 5 meters The gulches are generally
U shaped in profile and have steep banks that allow good exposure of the shallow
subsurface soil profile In some portions of the gulch the banks were observed to have
been built up to form man made berms which are higher than the surrounding
topography These berms may have been constructed to reduce the flood potential from
the gulch
The soils exposed in the gulch sidewalls appear to consist of reddish brown to dark
brown silty clays and are very stiff in consistency Some thin lenses of alluvial gravel and
cobble deposits were observed in portions of the gulch Several prominent boulders of up
to about 1 5 meters in diameter were also observed to be wedged in the sidewalls of the
gulch At the time of our site visit the weather and gulches were dry
Scattered throughout the site abandoned irrigation equipment such as pipes
valves pumps and troughs were observed partially obscured by the growth of vegetation
An abandoned concrete flume was observed to trend to the east and west extending from
this abandoned facility The flume was dry at the time of our observations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 8
Several buried pipelines were observed crossing the gulches at culvert locations
The iron pipelines were estimated to be about 750 millimeters in diameter and were
observed to trend both north to south and east to west at separate locations Other buried
pipelines may exist throughout the site that were not observed at the ground surface
Many recent developments are currently on going near the southern half of the
project site These developments include infrastructure residential and commercial
developments The areas with no present developments are generally open vacant
lands Vegetation in these areas is limited to a few native grasses of up to about
0 5 meters in height and small shrubs
Ground surface elevations at the site range from about 80 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL at the northern portion of the site adjacent to the Interstate Route H 1 Freeway to
about 5 feet MSL at the southern boundary near Papipi Road In general the majority of
the site is gently sloping downward to the south and southeast The ground surface at the
northernmost portion of the site near Interstate Route H 1 Highway is slightly steeper
sloping gently to moderately toward the southeast
SURFACE SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on a review of available in house subsurface information in the vicinity and
observations at the site we anticipate that the northern portion of the site may be
underlain by alluvial soils consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clays and clayey silts As
previously mentioned seams of gravel and cobble deposits as well as boulders up to
about 1 5 meters in diameter were observed along the sidewalls of the Kaloi gulch
Based on the available information and our experience in the vicinity of the roadway
alignment the alluvial soils at the site may exhibit moderate to high shrink swell potential
upon moisture fluctuations of the soil It will be necessary to conduct geotechnical field
explorations to aid in defining the extent and degree of the expansive soil condition for
support of structural improvements such as pavement structural sections sidewalks and
other on grade structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 9
The majority of the proposed alignment corridor passes through old sugar cane
fields and or open undeveloped lands Vegetation consisting of native grasses and sugar
canes along these areas vary from light to moderate Therefore the upper 200 to
400 millimeters of soil may be contaminated with organic matter which is common in
sugar cane fields These organic laden soil may not be an appropriate source of fill
material for construction of embankments
Because the northern portion of the site was utilized for large scale sugar cane
cultivation abandoned agricultural equipment and structures such as pipes troughs
earthen reservoirs and ditches pumps and other structures may be encountered at the
site In addition soft soil conditions related to the historic use of earthen reservoirs and
irrigation ditches may be encountered at the site These features may be observed at the
ground surface or may be buried as undocumented fills
Based on available information and our site reconnaissance the alluvium layer
thins out and calcareous deposits underlie the southern portion of the project alignment
These calcareous deposits are typically coral detritus consisting of gravels and sands
and or medium hard to hard coral formations It should be noted that cavities and or voids
are commonly encountered in coral formations A program of proof rolling and or probing
and grouting may be necessary to help detect near surface cavities and or voids at
selected locations for constructing structures on these subsurface conditions
Based on our observation of the existing site drainage and surface soil conditions
large volumes of storm water runoff are believed to pass through the site during rainfall
events via the Kaloi and Hunehune gulches The flow originates from the mountainous
area on the mauka side of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway The flow is then transported
via the gulches through the project site to discharge in off site retention basins or at the
shoreline The runoff is believed to increase rapidly and in substantial volume as
evidenced by the large boulders deposited in the upper reaches of the gulches and the
scour observed at the existing bridge and gulch banks In between rainfall events the
gulches are believed to remain dry
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 10
The site had been used for the extensive cultivation of large acreage of sugar cane
As a result of this land use site drainage may have been artificially altered by the
furrowing or diversion of naturally occurring runoff thus inhibiting the natural development
of drainage ways at the site In addition the runoff entering the site may have been
diverted to storage reservoirs on site thus reducing the total flow which may have
otherwise traversed the site Furthermore the clay soils of the site have generally low
permeability characteristics with infiltration rates For these reasons we believe that site
drainage should be planned carefully to allow for possible increase in runoff derived both
from on site and off site localities Ve understand that the realignment of the Kaloi gulch
and a drainage study will be conducted by others
DISCUSSION AND PRELIMINARY RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our preliminary site evaluation we do not believe that there are
significant geologic and or geotechnical constraints present along the alignment which
would preclude development of the proposed North South Road in the area of study
However there are several geotechnical engineering considerations with regard to the
proposed roadway construction that should be addressed in the design and construction of
this project The following is a brief discussion of the geotechnical engineering
considerations for this project
Based on our literature research site reconnaissance and past experience in the
vicinity of the project site we believe that the majority of the project site is underlain by
alluvial soils over coral and marine deposits at greater depths with the exception of the
southern most corner of the project site where coralline deposits appear to exist at or near
the existing ground surface The alluvial soils observed near the surface appear to consist
of reddish brown to dark brown silty clays The alluvial soils at the site may
exhibit moderate to high shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore special provisions may be required to reduce the effects of shrink swell
potential of the near surface alluvial soils on future on grade improvements such as the
pavement structural sections sidewalks etc
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 11
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the site we believe that cut
slopes exposing the on site clayey alluvium and fill slopes constructed of the on site
materials may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter It should be noted that the clayey alluvial soils at the site are
susceptible to erosion Therefore provisions for slope planting and or other means of
erosion control should be implemented during construction of the roadway project
This preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation was performed for preliminary
assessment of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site only A design level
geotechnical engineering exploration should be conducted to provide recommendations
for design and construction of the roadway planned at the site Detailed discussion of
these items and our preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation are presented in the
following sections of this report
Earthwork
In general the project site is sloping gently down toward the south and southeast
Aside from the Kaloi gulch crossings major topographic features such as hills and
troughs along the proposed alignment do not appear to be evident However earthwork
operations will be a significant part of this proposed roadway Therefore we are providing
the following general site grading guidelines to be used for preliminary design purposes
As previously indicated a large portion of the proposed alignment traverses
through moderately vegetated fields The upper 200 to 400 millimeters of the soils in
these areas may be highly contaminated with organic materials considering the numerous
years of sugar cane cultivation at the site Therefore this upper organically laden layer of
soil may need to be removed at the on set of earthwork operations Soils containing
excessive amounts of organic matter are not suitable for use as fill materials and should
be disposed of off site or used in landscape areas where appropriate
Coral deposits and formations appear to underlie the southern portion of the site
near Papipi Road Therefore in areas where the subgrade exposes coral deposits and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3860 10 Page 12
formations we recommend that a program of proof rolling using a 15 ton vibratory roller or
similar heavy equipment be used to help detect and collapse near surface cavities Loose
areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled
with compacted structural fill materials In addition there may be a need for probing and
grouting the foundations for proposed structures such as box culverts bridges etc
located above these coralline materials
In general the on site materials except soils laden with organic materials may be
re used as a source of general fill and backfill materials provided that they contain no
lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension and are
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction Moisture conditioning the soils to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content is an integral part of our recommendations
to reduce the effects of shrink swell of the materials on future on grade improvements
In general fill and backfill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
200 millimeters in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement for fills and backfills within 3 feet below the pavement finish grade
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The soil subgrades
should be kept moist until covered by the subsequent fill layer or the aggregate subbase
course Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact the on site clayey
soils
For preliminary design purposes all permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed
with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter All fills to be placed on slopes with inclinations
steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point

Excavation
In order to construct the proposed box and pipe culverts we anticipate that some
excavations may be needed Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated we
anticipate that conventional excavation equipment may be used for the proposed
excavation The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined
by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
It should be noted that deposits of cobbles and boulders are present at the project
site based on our site reconnaissance Although the boulders of up to about 1 5 meters in
diameter appear to be scattered and generally sparse difficult excavations may be
encountered when the boulders are encountered in the excavations In addition the
southern portions of the proposed alignment may encounter coral formations at or near the
existing ground surface Some of the coral formations may be relatively hard especially in
localized areas Therefore excavations in some localized areas may require the use of
hoe ramming or chipping
