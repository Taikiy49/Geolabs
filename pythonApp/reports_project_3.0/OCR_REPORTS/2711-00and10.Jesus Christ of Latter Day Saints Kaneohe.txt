INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed reorganized church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in
Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration which was performed
in two 2 phases and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from analyses of the data obtained These recommendations are intended for design
input and are not intended to form the basis of preparation for construction cost estimates
or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new church building for the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter Day Saints at the project site
The new church will consist of a structural complex comprising three 3 adjoining
components on three 3 different levels The components include a one story sanctuary
and fellowship hall at the front a two story office and classroom area at the lower central
level and a gymnasium at the rear portion of the site on the lowest level It is our
understanding that the gymnasium structure may generate loading on the order of 30 to
40 kips per column
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 3
The proposed church structure will be built generally on the existing ground The
finished grades of the complex will vary from 91 feet above MSL at the sanctuary and
fellowship hall to 80 5 feet above MSL at the central office and classroom area and 62 5
feet at the gymnasium
Up to 15 feet of cut and 7 feet of fill are anticipated Retaining walls will be
required to create the grade separation between the three 3 building components and
outside the church structure where several grassed play areas and a parking lot will be
provided
The proposed development as well as existing site conditions are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is in an abandoned lot located on the east side of Kamehameha
Highway at approximately 200 feet southerly from the St Mark s Lutheran School on
Hoene Place in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 5
The trapezoidal lot measures approximately 150 feet by 440 feet in plan covering
an area of approximately 65 865 square feet The existing ground slopes gently from
approximately 94 feet above MSL at the front of the lot down to approximately 70 feet
above MSL on top of a small slope which creates a grade separation of about 6 feet
approximately 100 feet from the back of the lot The lower portion of the lot behind the
slope is approximately 61 to 65 feet above MSL
The front and middle portions of the lot are mostly overgrown with dense
vegetation An existing Asphalt Concrete A C driveway along the southern boundary
of the lot provides access to the lower rear area where an abandoned house is located
amid broken pavements and slabs
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
The subsoil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling a total of
nine 9 borings and one 1 probe to depths of about 11 5 to 41 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings and probe are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
At the front and central portions of the site on higher ground elevations the field
exploration encountered stiff to hard clays underlain by clayey and silty saprolites also stiff
to hard in consistency to the maximum boring depths of 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
In the borings located at the back of the site where the ground elevations are
lower however the clays were found to be underlain by moderately to highly
compressible subsoils The compressible subsoils include soft to medium stiff clayey silts
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
and loose to medium dense sandy saprolite at approximately 9 to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface
More competent subsoils of stiff to hard silty saprolites were encountered above
a weathered basalt formation below the compressible subsoils at the lower rear portion
of the site
Groundwater Conditions
Groundwater levels were measured at various depths in the borings ranging from
approximately 8 6 to 23 5 feet below the existing ground surface or at approximately 53
to 56 feet above MSL at the rear lot area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the subsurface conditions we believe that shallow spread and
continuous footing foundations may be used to support the entire proposed church
complex However a lower bearing capacity should be used for the footing design of the
gymnasium located on the lower level of the lot where compressible subsoils were
encountered at relatively shallow depths in order to reduce the potential for excessive
footing settlements
Our laboratory swell tests indicate that the near surface on site soils are moderately
expansive if allowed to dry and subsequently re wetted Therefore clayey subgrades
below new pavements and slabs should be over excavated a minimum of twelve 12
inches and backfilled with select non expansive granular material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 7
Footing Foundation
Our analyses indicate that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be
used for design of the footing foundations bearing on the competent subsoils below the
sanctuary and fellowship hall and the office and classroom portion of the church complex
For the gymnasium structure underlain by compressible subsoils it is
recommended that a lower bearing capacity of 2000 p s f be used for the footing design
to support the anticipated 30 to 40 kip column loads
The recommended bearing values may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for
accommodation of wind loads seismic loads and other short term loadings
The footing foundations should be adequately embedded to provide lateral
resistance However we recommend that the footing foundations at the gymnasium be
embedded not more than 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade to reduce
the imposition of footing bearing stresses onto the underlying compressibie subsoils
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
95 of the maximum dry density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft
or loose material encountered at the excavated footing subgrade level should be removed
to firm ground and the resulting depression replaced with properly compacted select non
expansive granular material
Up to one 1 inch of total settlement and one half 1 2 inch of differential
settlement are anticipated for the footing foundations under the recommended design
bearing pressures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 8
Resistance to lateral loading on the church structure may be obtained by passive
earth pressure and frictional resistance developed against the footing foundations We
recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth except for the
top one 1 foot below an unpaved area be used to determine the passive earth pressure
developed against the embedded footings A frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be used
to compute the frictional resistance developed between the footings and the recompacted
footing subgrade
Site Grading
Based on the available grading information it is anticipated that up to 15 feet of cut
and 7 feet of fill may be required for the proposed construction
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract grading limits should
be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation rubble slabs paving material
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site prior to initiation of grading
After clearing areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be scarified to a
depth of at least six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of
90 of the maximum dry density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Areas
to receive embankment which have slopes in excess of five horizontal to one vertical
5H 1V should be keyed and benched as illustrated on Plate 3
Due to the moderate expansion potential of the near surface on site soils clayey
subgrades below new pavement or slabs should be specially prepared as will be
discussed in the subsequent sections entitled Pavements and Slabs On Grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 9
Any soft or loose material encountered at finished subgrade levels should be
removed and replaced with imported select granular material compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Due to their low to moderate expansion potential the on site clay soils may be
re used only for general site fill For the backfilling of soft or loose spots and filling and
backfilling behind retaining walls imported select granular materials should be used
Imported materials may also be required as another source of general site fill
Imported fill or backfill materials should consist of non expansive select granular materials
less than three 38 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Fill materials imported or on site should be placed in maximum 8 inch thick
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of their maximum dry density
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 When used for general embankment
construction the on site clay soils should be moisture conditioned to the wet side of the
optimum moisture content prior to compaction
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
Retaining Wallis
The drawings provided to us indicate that retaining walls up to 15 feet high will be
required to create grade separation within the structure and to support fill areas outside
the structure Due to the expansive nature of the on site clay soils we recommend that
imported select granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel be used as backfill behind
the walls to reduce the compactive effort required and the lateral earth loads on the walls
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O Page 10
Guidelines for use in design of the retaining walls are presented in the following
1 imported select granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 should be used behind the retaining walls to a minimum horizontal
distance equal to the heights of the walls The No 3B Fine gravel material
used should be placed in maximum eight 8 inch thick horizontal lifts and
tamped with a vibrating pad for a minimum of three 8 passes
2 Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the
project site
3 A lateral earth load of 60 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used for the
design of walls restrained at the top and with level granular backfill A lower
lateral earth load of 40 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used for walls
unstrained at the top and with level granular backfill
4 Any surcharge pressure should be added to the above recommended
lateral earth loads for the wall design as appropriate
5 An allowable footing bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used The
footing bearing value may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for design
to account for a triangular stress distribution below the wall footings
6 A passive earth pressure of 300 p c f equivalent fluid pressure and a
frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be used to determine the lateral resistance
of the retaining walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 11
7 An adequate subdrain system and weepholes should be provided to
prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Pavements
Laboratory swell test results indicate that the on site near surface soils are
moderately expansive if allowed to dry and subsequently re wetted Therefore we
recommend that natural clayey subgrades below the new pavements in the parking lot
and driveways be over excavated a minimum of twelve 12 inches and replaced with a
capping backfill of non expansive select granular material such as select borrow or base
course
The over excavated pavement subgrade level should be moisture conditioned and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 The select granular backfill should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Provided the pavement subgrades are prepared as recommended above the

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed church construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to see that critical items
of work such as footing excavations pavement and slab subgrade over excavation and
backfill are properly constructed This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

information obtained from field borings and probe Variations of subsoil conditions
between the borings and probe may occur and the nature and extent of these variations

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the

transition
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuations may occur due to variation in rainfall storm surges temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing were performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed for appropriate tests to aid in
determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
were used as an aid in soil classification The dry weights and or the moisture contents
expressed as a percentage of the dry weights of the tested samples are shown at the
appropriate sample depths of the Boring Logs
One 1 Sieve Analysis Test ASTM Test Method D 422 was used to determine the
gradation of a selected sample The gradation of the sample is used to classify the soil
under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of the test are shown on Plate
B 1
Physical Property Tests
These tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical
parameters of a soil The physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses
pertaining to the performance or suitability of a soil
One 1 California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM Test Method D 1883 was
performed on a surface soil sample to evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of
the soil The test results are summarized on Plate 1
Eight 8 one inch ring swell tests were performed to evaluate the swelling potential
of the soils under structural loads The tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as fill
and pavement foundation support These tests results are summarized on Plates B 2
and B 3
Two 2 Consolidation tests ASTM Test Method D 4186 were used to determine
the compression characteristics of selected samples The test results are presented as
two 2 consolidation curves on Plates B 4 and B 5 respectively
