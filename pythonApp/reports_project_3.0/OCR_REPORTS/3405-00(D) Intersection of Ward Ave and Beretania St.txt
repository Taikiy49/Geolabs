Preliminary Geotechnical Recommendations
Proposed UNOCAL Gas Station Mini Mart
Intersection of Ward Avenue and Beretania Street
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
We have recently completed our field exploration for the proposed UNOCAL Gas
Station Mini Mart at the Intersection of Ward Avenue and Beretania Street in Honolulu on
the island of Oahu This letter serves to present an overview of the subsurface conditions
encountered at the site and to present our preliminary geotechnical recommendations for
design of foundations and site grading for the proposed Gas Station Mini Mart
At the time this letter was prepared the finish floor elevations for the proposed
structures such as the mini mart and pump islands were not available to us yet Based
on our field observations the site is relatively level therefore we envision that the
proposed structures will be constructed at very near the existing grades In addition
excavation and backfill on the order of 15 feet may be expected at the new gas tank
locations Based on this information we are providing the following preliminary
geotechnical engineering recommendations for your use
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the three borings drilled for the field exploration at the site medium dense
to dense sands and silty sands were generally encountered below the existing asphalt
pavement to about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface Below the sands
hard clay was encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 2 to the maximum depth explored of
about 16 feet below the existing ground In addition a clayey silt fill layer was
encountered near the ground surface in Boring No 2 at the location of a pump island
Groundwater was not encountered in the three borings at the time of our exploration

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our borings we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on the near surface medium dense
sands may be used for support of the proposed structures We understand that minimal
grading would be required to consiruct the proposed improvements such as mini mart and
pump islands Based on this we recommend that an allowable bearing pressure of up to
3 000 pounds per square foot p s f be used for design of footings bearing on the medium
dense sandy soils and or fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces An embedment depth of at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish
grade is recommended
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A
coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing on the medium dense sandy
soils
Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed structures
such as the mini mart and pump islands We recommend that the subgrade be properly
prepared and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete We envision
that the slabs on grade will bear on the medium dense sandy soils near the ground
surface The subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8
inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisiure is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs mini mart we recommend that concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer
should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67
gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill layer
should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and compaction of the existing
subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive
surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill
to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to

Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pump

The subgrade should also be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at

percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base course
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
As previously indicated minimal grading and an excavation and backfill of up to 15
feet may be expected for the proposed development In general we believe that the on
site soils may be reused as general fill material provided that the soils are properly
moisture conditioned and compacted in accordance with our recommendations
All subgrades should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of improvements subjected to
vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95
percent relative compaction Where used as backfill material the on site soils should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to 90 percent
relative compaction
Import fill material if needed should consist of well graded granular material less
than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Imported fills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Pavements
New flexible pavements are anticipated at the proposed project site We anticipate
the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles light trucks
and occasional heavy truck The preliminary pavement design is based on a CBR value
