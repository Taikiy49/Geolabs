In accordance with your request a test probing program was conducted at each
proposed drilled shaft location for the Synagro Digester project located at the Sand Island
Wastewater Treatment Plant in the Sand Island area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In
general the test probing operations for the project has been conducted in general
conformance with the intent of the project specifications This letter report presents the
results of our test probing at the above project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Synagro Digester project site is located at the Sand Island Wastewater
Treatment Plant in the Sand Island area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand
that it is planned to construct a sludge storage tank at the project site among other
structures
Based on the information provided the sludge storage tank will have a diameter of
approximately 30 feet and a height of approximately 108 55 feet The storage tank will be
supported on a deep foundation system consisting of both drilled shafts and driven piles
The drilled shafts will consist of 24 inch diameter cast in place concrete and ihe driven
piles will consist of 16 5 inch octagonal precast pre stressed concrete Twelve drilled
shafts are planned around the perimeter of the storage tank and thirty two driven piles are
planned within the interior portion of the storage tank We understand that the drilled shaft
will require a 9 5 foot embedment into the coral formation anticipated at the project site
As indicated in the project specifications the contractor is required to take
continuous core samples at each drilled shaft location down to 10 feet below the proposed
depth of embedment

TEST PROBING PROGRAM
The test probing program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring at each
drilled shaft location for a total of twelve test probes The test probes were drilled at the
center of each drilled shaft location as shown on the Pile Cap Layout Plan prepared by
CB l dated The test probes are designated as Boring Nos 1
through 12 and follow the same numbering scheme as the drilled shafts
Pile Cap Layout Plan dated The test probes extended to depths of
about 61 5 to 69 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the information
provided by the contractor the existing ground surface elevations for each test probe is
approximately 8 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers and rotary
coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate 1 Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 2 through 13
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barre Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of coral materials encountered at the project site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the coral The coral material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of coral and or rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material
recovered from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if
3 7 feet of material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be
74 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

In the project specifications we understand that the drilled shafts will need to be
embedded into competent coral It should be noted that both coralline detritus and coral
formation are considered coral reef deposits The difference between the two geologic
designations is the origin of the deposition Coral formation generally consists of
undisturbed coralline and algal skeletons that have been cemented with secreted
calcium compounds during the growth and expansion of the marine organisms In
comparison coralline detritus generally is composed of cemented calcareous fragments
such as coralline skeletons shells and sand that have been transported from the
original depositional environment and re deposited In some instances coralline
detritus may consist of pockets or zones of loose sands and gravel size coral fragments
