As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study con
sisted of five 5 hand auger probes within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION

with the remainder of the house above Concrete masonry walls
are planned around the garage level and the rear portion of the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the upper portion of the
lot is underlain by a surface layer of grayish brown highly
expansive clay about 1 foot thick over dense weathered rock
Foundations in this area should extend through the surface clay
layer and bear on the dense rock formation Concrete slab
should bear on a minimum of 3 feet of select non expansive soil
or on weathered rock formation The driveway should bear on a
minimum of 2 feet of select non expansive soil or on weathered
rock formation
Foundation
Individual spread and wall footings for the house should extend
through the surface clay and bear a minimum of 6 inches into
the dense weathered rock formation or 3 feet below the ground
surface where rock is not encountered An allowable bearing
value of 6000 p s f can be used on the dense weathered rock
and 3000 p s f on the natural soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
It is anticipated that individual spread footings and rear wall
footing may encounter rock formation at shallow depth while the
garage wall footing and stairway footings may require deeper
embedment
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the house
to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible
saturation and settlement or heaving of the foundation soils
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage The finished
floor elevation of the garage will generally be near the
existing grade and therefore may encounter the expansive
clay The clay soils are highly expansive and unsuitable as
slab subgrade soils Where encountered the expansive clays
should be overexcavated down to dense weathered rock or for a
maximum depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and then
replaced with non expansive borrow soils compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed
driveway In order to reduce the expansive effects of the
clay it should be kept a minimum of 2 feet below the driveway
subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over
the adobe clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
overexcavation and replacement of the adobe clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the
driveway
Since relatively long driveway is planned joints are
recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow
for some relative movement of the concrete

replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce

Site Grading
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the
house
A few feet of 111 is planned along the southern side of the
house
Fill and backfill material below structures and behind
retaining walls should be select non expansive borrow
material If available some of the weathered rock excavated
material smaller than 8 inches could be used in the fill if
it is not contaminated with the clay The clay soil should
only be used for fill material away from retaining walls and
where structures are not planned
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557

Retaining Walls
CRM retaining walls are planned along the southern lot boundary
1 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 24
inches below the outside finished grade or into the
weathered rock formation
2 Granular backfill such as select borrow or crusher waste
should be used for a distance behind the wall equal to the
height of the wall This would improve drainage reduce
the compactive effort required for the wall backfill and
reduce lateral pressure behind the walls
3 Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be used
only for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of
surface run off behind the walls
4 A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls
5 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff natural
soil

investigation
