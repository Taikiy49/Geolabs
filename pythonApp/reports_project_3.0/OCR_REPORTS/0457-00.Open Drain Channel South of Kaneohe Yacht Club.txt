I INTRODUCTION
A General
This report presents the results of our preliminary soils
engineering and geologic investigation for the proposed
Open Drain Improvement adjacent to the Kaneohe Yacht Club
Kaneohe Hawaii
The study consisted of subsurface borings laboratory testing
and engineering analyses for developing the relevant recom
mendations for the proposed project
B Project Description
The proposed alignment of the open channel drain extends
approximately 500 feet from Kaneohe Bay Drive to Kaneohe Bay
and is located south of the Kaneohe Yacht Club The right of
way is 30 feet wide of which 6 foot width is for the sewer
easement leaving 24 foot width for the open drain See Plate 1
II FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of 3 borings ranging in depth from
15 to 20 feet The drilling was accomplished using a truck mounted
rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight solid stem auger
The borings were continuously inspected by an engineer from our
firm The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and
Page 2
W O 457 00
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 4 inch
I D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not
exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of
driving a 2 inch O D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12
inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N
and is an approximate measure of the relative density or
consistency of a soil The number of blows to drive ring
sampler for 12 was converted to equivalent standard pene
tration resistance by using a conversion factor These resis
tance values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained
were visually classified in the field and representative
material was placed in air tight containers and returned
to our laboratory for inspection and testing
III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A Geology
1 General
The sewer alignment is located on the northeast side of
Page 3

the elongated Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the
southeast portion of Oahu Hawaii
After the formation of the Koolau Volcanic Range and
the associated Kailua Volcanic Series in the Kaneohe
Kailua area the constituent volcanoes were deeply
dissected by stream erosion Clastic debris eroded
from the mountains was redeposited along the moun
tain flanks and in Kaneohe Bay Changes of relative
sea level gave rise to varying configurations of
Kaneohe Bay and effected sediment deposition on the
project site
Subsequent uplift and or a eustatic lowering of sea
level and erosion has exposed these deposits
Deposits along the open channel drain route include
recent alluvium and bay muds and coral in Kaneohe Bay
Soil Conditions
Bay muds consisting of clay clayey sands and loose sands
with coral and shells were encountered in all borings
For detailed description of materials refer to boring logs
They are at least 20 feet thick

Type of Channel
It is our understanding that it is contemplated to construct
the channel with unlined bottom and two retaining walls
on the sides For the following reasons we recommend
to use a U shaped rigid frame concrete channel for this
project
Page 7
W O 457 00
1 The subsoils are relatively soft and the U shaped
channel will spread the load over a larger area thus
reducing the soil pressure
2 The concrete invert of the U shaped channel would
prevent the erosion of the channel bed
3 The concrete channel would prevent vegetation growth
which may obstruct free flow of water
4 Debris and silt build up can be easily cleaned in a
concrete channel

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
we ee terminations
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid himit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
e
Parr ARE as Arar Ads paren
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
