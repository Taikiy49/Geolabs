SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Resurface Parking and Install covered Parking project at the
Department of Accounting and General Services DAGS Central Services Division in
Moanalua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of pavement resurfacing
pavement reconstruction and foundations for the proposed covered parking structures
only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The proposed project consists of the resurfacing of all areas paved with asphaltic
concrete at the DAGS Central Services Division in the Moanalua area of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii Areas of distressed or damaged pavement will be reconstructed
as part of the project In addition four 4 covered parking areas ranging from 5 to
22 stalls in length will be constructed
Details of the vehicular traffic loading were not available for the preparation of this
report However it is anticipated that the majority of vehicular traffic at the site consists of
passenger vehicles and light trucks with some minor traffic by medium to heavy trucks
It is anticipated that covered parking areas will generally consist of metal framed
roofs supported by posts or cantilevered framing Structural loads for these covered
parking areas are anticipated to be light Vertical uplift due to wind loads will be a critical
consideration for design of the parking structures

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
still active
The DAGS Central Services Division is located on the southern flank of Koolau
and its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene and the genesis of Pearl Harbor The
base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world The higher sea
level stands caused the accumulation of sediments in the heads of the old bays
accumulation of reef deposits at corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine
sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused
streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Renewed erosion
deposited alluvial soils in the lagoons and bays
The geologic history of the area has been further complicated by the deposition
of recent pyroclastic materials and lava flows from eruptions of the vents of members of
the Honolulu Volcanic Series including the Aliamanu Salt Lake Makalapa vents These
events were contemporaneous with the Pleistocene sea level fluctuations The deposits
of pyroclastics have consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally referred to as mudrock
These eruptions caused a diversion of Moanalua Stream from its original course to its
current location
The site is situated at the head of Keehi Lagoon which is a drowned stream
channel at the mouth of Kalihi and Moanalua Streams The area now known as the
Mapunapuna Industrial Area was formerly a low marshy area Original surface soils

2 2 Existing Site Description and Conditions
The project site is located on the eastern side of the Mapunapuna Industrial Area
in the Moanalua portion of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The DAGS Central Services Division is a multi agency
baseyard providing offices storage and workshop space for several units of the Hawaii
state government
Several existing buildings mainly consisting of single story metal framed
warehouse type structures occupy the site The majority of the site area not occupied by
structures is paved with asphaltic concrete to provide access driveways and open parking
areas Much of the pavement surface exhibit signs of moderate wear with some
deterioration Portions of the existing pavement have been seal coated Some areas of
the pavement have become damaged due to traffic loading
2 3 Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program for the project consisted of drilling and sampling
seven 7 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 7 inclusive The borings
extended to depths between about 6 5 and 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface layer of flexible pavement
consisting of 1 25 to 4 inches of asphaltic concrete overlying 6 inches of base course
In some borings it was noted that the base course has deteriorated Under the
pavement section the borings generally encountered man made fill to depths between
about 4 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface The fills generally consisted of
dense sands and gravels with some appearing to be quarry products such as select

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project site we
believe that an overlay of asphaltic concrete will be appropriate for repaving most of the
areas at the site Damaged pavement areas should be reconstructed Spread footings
may be used to support the proposed covered parking roofs planned at the site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Pavements
Design traffic loadings for the paved areas at the site were not available for
preparation of this report However as previously discussed it is anticipated that the
majority of vehicular traffic at the site consists of passenger vehicles and light trucks with
some minor traffic by medium to heavy trucks In general it appears that most of the
paved areas at the site with the exception of the parking area at Boring No 7 were
previously resurfaced with an overlay of asphaltic concrete Based on the anticipated
subsurface conditions at the site and the current condition of the pavement surfaces
most of the existing paved areas may be rehabilitated by resurfacing with an overlay of
asphaltic concrete AC It is recommended that the AC overlay be on the order of
about 1 5 inches in thickness In the area of Boring No 7 the field exploration
encountered a thin 1 25 inches surface course of AC For this area it is
recommended that the overlay thickness be increased to be on the order of about
2 inches if feasible
For areas where the placement of an 1 5 inch overlay of AC would have an
adverse impact on drainage or curbline grades consideration should be given to the
use of cold planing to remove approximately one 1 inch of the existing AC prior to
installation of the new AC overlay If cold planing is used to remove the existing
AC material the contractor should vacuum sweep the areas immediately after planning
to remove all gravel sized fragments and dust not removed by the cold planing

density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 AASHTO T 180 Optimum
moisture content is the water content percentage by dry weight needed to achieve the
maximum dry density
3 2 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary mechanisms causing pavement distress is pumping of the
pavement section as a result of saturation of the aggregate base course and or
subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surfaces should be sloped to drain to
appropriate drainage structures or discharge areas Surface water ponding should not
be allowed on the site during or after construction The recommended pavement
sections assume that good drainage will be provided for the paved areas
3 3 Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe
that spread footing foundations may be used for support of the proposed covered parking

In general the bottom of spread footings should be embedded a minimum of

Lateral loads acting on the proposed roof structures may be resisted by frictional

pressure of 250 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Vertical uplift loads on the proposed roof structures may be resisted by a
combination of the dead weight of the foundations and by frictional resistance developed
against the sides of the foundations

It should be noted that Boring Nos 1 and 7 encountered what may be ash from any
old municipal incinerator Incinerator ash materials frequently contain elevated
concentrations of heavy metals and other environmental contaminants The material
encountered in Boring No 1 also had a hydrocarbon like odor If contaminated material is
encountered by foundation excavation there may be environmental issues related to the
handling and disposal of the material It is recommended that the contractor retain a

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling seven borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 7 extending to depths of
about 6 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools
The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
