In general the Nordstrom Ala Moana project site is bounded by Kapiolani
Boulevard to the north Keeaumoku Street to the west Kona Street to the south and the
new Ala Moana Center Expansion Parking Structure currently under construction to the
east The project site measures approximately 300 feet long by 250 feet wide in plan

Based on the referenced report for the project we recommended that the new
building structure be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable compressive load capacity
of up to 150 kips 75 tons per pile We envision that most of the driven piles would extend
through the soft and or loose lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support
primarily from end bearing on the upper coral ledges encountered at depths of about 20 to
30 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the variable quality and thickness of the
upper coral ledges we anticipated that some of the driven piles intended for end bearing
purposes may punch through the upper coral ledges during the pile driving operations
These piles would then need to be spliced and driven deeper to derive support from skin
friction by embedment into the underlying coralline detritus materials
TEST PILE PROGRAM FOR PRECAST DRIVEN PILES
As mentioned previously we recommended that the new building structure be
supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to 150 kips
75 tons per pile The driven pile installation contractor for the Nordstrom Ala Moana
project is Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company Inc HDCC
A test pile program which consisted of installing 26 precast prestressed concrete
test piles was conducted for the project from April 7 through 12 2006 The locations of
the test piles are shown on the Driven Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the
test pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles given the
subsurface conditions at the project site the selected driving equipment and methods of
concrete pile installation In addition the test pile program also provided information on
the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing strata across the project site to
estimate the length of the production concrete piles
Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled to a depth of
about 5 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate the pile driving operations
through the surface fill crust A hydraulic hammer was used to drive the
16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete test piles with pile segments of 45 feet
long Our field observations indicated that a stroke height of about 4 feet was used with
the hammer delivering a rated energy of approximately 40 000 foot pounds per blow
during the pile driving operations The test piles were driven to depths ranging from about
22 5 to 113 feet below the ground surface A summary of the test pile driving conditions
and observations is provided on Plate 3 of this report for reference Based on the driven
test pile program results it appears that the upper coral ledges traversing the building site

Static Pile Load Test
A compression pile load test was conducted on a selected test pile designated as
Pile No 53 on The purpose of the static pile load test is to evaluate the
amount of embedment length required for the driven pile friction pile in order to achieve
the design compressive load capacity of 75 tons per pile
The selected test pile was driven to a depth of about 92 feet below the ground
surface The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the pile was planned at
300 kips However the pile started to experience continued downward movement at a
maximum test load of about 169 kips Detailed discussions and results of the static pile
load test are provided in the report entitled Driven Pile Load Test Report Nordstrom
Ala Moana Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated August 18 2006 transmitted under separate
cover
Based on the static pile load test results it appears that an embedment length on
the order of about 130 to 135 feet will be required for the driven piles to achieve the design
compressive load capacity of 75 tons based on friction if end bearing on the coral ledges is
not encountered Upon further evaluation with General Growth Properties L P based on
cost comparisons and construction considerations it was decided that auger cast in place
ACIP piles would be used in lieu of driven piles for foundation support of the new
Nordstrom Building
AUGER CAST IN PLACE PILE INSTALLATION
The scope of work discussed in the following sections of this letter report
includes observation of the ACIP pile foundation installation adopted for the Nordstrom
Building The ACIP pile installation subcontractor is Morris Shea Bridge Company Inc
Static Pile Load Test
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design length of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on
May 15 and 16 2006 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile
with a length of approximately 65 feet long relative to the existing ground surface To
better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile
was instrumented with six vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various
depths in the load test pile The load test results indicated that the load test pile generally
behaved like a friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load
approximately 750 kips being resisted by adhesion between the ACIP pile and the
surrounding soils

Production ACIP Pile Installation
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 207 auger
cast in place piles 18 inch diameter was conducted from May 19 through
under the near continuous full time observations by a field representative from our office
Morris Shea Bridge Company Inc used the same drill rig equipped with automatic
monitoring equipment to provide grout flow rate batch number pressure etc on a
near continuous basis During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for
each ACIP pile which included information pertaining to pile number date installed depth
of pile grout volume used grout return depth etc The locations of the production
ACIP piles installed are shown on the attached ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 4 The
production ACIP pile installation summary is presented by pile number on
Plates 5 1 through 5 7 of this letter report
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been
installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load Capacity of
300 kips 150 tons per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
