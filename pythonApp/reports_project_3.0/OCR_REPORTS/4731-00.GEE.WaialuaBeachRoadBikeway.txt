2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was formed by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae Volcano became
extinct while Koolau Volcano was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried
below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
During the later Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation As the continental glaciers advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell to below the present stand When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans
of terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposition of lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused desiccation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits
and induration of the calcareous reef materials

provided above do not include hydrostatic pressure that may be caused by
groundwater trapped behind the MSE retaining wall structure

Overall Slope Stability
We have evaluated the overall slope stability of the MSE wall structures planned for
the project Slope stability analyses of two dimensional slope cross sections using
Bishop s simplified method of limit equilibrium analysis were performed on the
segmental retaining wall structure at several selected locations along the wall
alignment Based on our analyses the factor of safety for short term and long term
stability of the MSE retaining wall is at least 1 5 which is the minimum factor of
safety normally recommended
Based on our slope stability and external stability analyses a minimum base width
to wall height ratio of at least 0 70 is recommended for the MSE retaining wall
structures planned at the project site

As mentioned previously alluvial and lagoonal deposits consisting of soft silty and

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered along the retaining wall
alignments ground settlement along the retaining walls due to the amount of

occur The anticipated ground settlements are primarily the result of consolidation
of the in situ soils induced by the MSE wall loads imposed on the soft and or loose
alluvial lagoonal deposits at the site Based on the available plans retaining walls
with heights on the order of about 3 to 8 feet are planned at the project site

walls to be on the order of about 1 to 1 5 inches

3 5 Settlement Monitoring Program
Due to the soft and or loose soil deposits encountered along the MSE retaining wall
alignments appreciable ground settlements are anticipated to occur along the MSE walls
To reduce the potential for significant distress to the proposed improvements planned at
the MSE wall locations we recommend that a settlement waiting period be implemented
prior to final construction of improvements planned on the MSE walls Based on the sandy
nature of the compressible soil deposits encountered at the project site we believe that a
settlement waiting period of about 8 weeks should be incorporated into the construction
schedule for the project The settlement waiting period should commence after completion
of the reinforced fill and MSE wall backfill placement
It should be recognized that it is difficult to accurately predict the exact time
required for the filled ground to settle since the settlement rates will be affected by
variations of the subsoil structure and the history of the subsoil deposition For the soft
and or loose subsoils anticipated at the project site we believe that the estimated
settlement period could vary by as much as 50 to 100 percent from the actual settlement
period Therefore the actual settlement rates should be monitored and a settlement
monitoring program should be established to evaluate the magnitude and rate of the
estimated settlements during the settlement waiting period prior to construction of the
improvements such as the pavements concrete caps and metal railings In addition
provisions should be made for potential delays in the construction schedule if a longer
settlement waiting period is required

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
these tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Five sieve analysis tests ASTM Test Designation C 117 and C 136 were
performed on selected samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics and to
aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 2
One consolidation test ASTM D 2435 with time rates of consolidation was
performed on a sample of the soils to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the
materials encountered The results of the consolidation test are presented on Plate B 3
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils encountered to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the material tested
Results of the direct shear test are presented on Plate B 4
