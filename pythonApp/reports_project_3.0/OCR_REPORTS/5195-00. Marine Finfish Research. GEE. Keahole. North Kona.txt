SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Marine Finfish Research and Technology Demonstration
Facility and Temporary Public Education and Outreach Facility at the Oceanic Institute
NELHA Site in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of the saltwater effluent
disposal system for the proposed three shrimp tanks only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The site for the proposed Marine Finfish Research and Technology
Demonstration Facility and Temporary Public Education and Outreach Facility is a
4 acre portion of the larger Kona Research Facility NELHA at Keahole North Kona
Hawaii It is located to the west of Queen Kaahumanu Highway in vacant lava land
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of a small
office feed storage building a public education and outreach facility and an aquaculture
tank farm This exploration is limited to the design of the saltwater effluent disposal
system for tail water from three shrimp tanks that will initially be constructed at the site
Based on the information provided the proposed disposal system will consist of an
infiltration trench to accommodate saltwater effluent at an estimated rate of up to
350 gallons per minute gpm
Based on our experience with other projects in the Keahole area and the
NELHA site we envision that the project site would be underlain by relatively

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
five shield volcanoes Kohala Mauna Kea and Kilauea The older
volcanoes Kohala and Mauna Kea are estimated to be very late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age and the other volcanoes and Kilauea have
been active within historic time The project site is located on the western flank of
Hualalai near the site of the most recent activity which was in 1801 1803
The project site is underlain by Hualalai Basalts which consist of interbedded
pahoehoe and a a lava flows originating from the northwestern rift zone At the project
site the basalts encountered were relatively fresh and unweathered and are estimated
to be of an age on the order of a few hundreds of years or less The lavas exposed at
and near the project site were generally of the pahoehoe form with some clinker
areas
2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The proposed Marine Finfish Research and Technology Demonstration Facility
and Temporary Public Education and Outreach Facility is a roughly triangular shaped
parcel of vacant lava land to the north of the existing NELHA HOST Park access road in
Keahole District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The proposal saltwater effluent
disposal system is situated in the northwestern portion of the facility as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations the project site is presently raw lava land with a
sparse vegetative growth of scattered wild grasses The terrain is relatively rugged and
a bulldozer was required to cut access trails for this exploration
Based on our field observations the project site slopes down generally towards
the west Ground surface elevations at the proposal disposal trench site are on the
order of 50 to 55 feet above Mean Sea Level

as Boring Nos 1 through 5 at the site ranging in depths from about 26 0 to 27 0 feet

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by relatively dense
volcanic rock formation with moderate fracturing and isolated voids to the maximum
depth explored of about 27 0 feet below the existing ground surface An isolated clinker
horizon was encountered in the deeper portion of Boring No 3 Groundwater was not
encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration
Based on our field permeability tests the volcanic rock formation underlying the
site has a net permeability on the order of about 30 feet per day It should be noted that
these field permeability tests were conducted in small diameter borings that did not
encounter significant voids or clinker horizons such as are found in an areal
examination of volcanic rock formations in the vicinity of the site Such gross
permeability features would enhance the performance of the disposal system if
encountered during construction
3 1 Trench Design
Based on our exploration field testing and analyses it is our opinion that a
trapezoidal section trench with a base width of 10 feet excavated to a depth of 10 feet
at the site would provide a disposal capacity on the order of about 5 gallons per minute
per foot of trench length Therefore a total trench length of about 70 lineal feet would
be required for the design flow of about 350 gallons per minute This does not account
for clogging air entrainment or surge flows and does not account for additional
infiltration into the surrounding formation during periods of heavy precipitation It is
strongly recommended that the final design of the trench consider additional length to
provide a factor of safety for these reductions in capacity
Due to budget considerations it may be desirable to construct the trench
incrementally We recommend that a full scale testing program be developed and
implemented during the construction of the trench to evaluate the actual disposal
capacity This program should consist of step testing the absorption rate of the trench
during excavation i e introducing water into the trench at different rates and observing
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
the build up of the water level in the trench with respect to time This testing program
will provide the actual capacity of the trench as constructed and provide data for the
design of future expansion of the disposal system
If multiple trenches are to be constructed on the site we recommend that they
should be separated center to center by a minimum distance of five times the nominal
trench width to reduce the interference between the flow regimes induced by the
individual trenches
It is also suggested that the disposal system include a 100 backup capacity
This capacity should be a redundant system in the form of a completely separate
disposal component e g additional trenches injection wells etc The use of separate
components will allow for periodic maintenance and will also provide lead time in the
event of sudden failure of one of the systems
3 2 Construction Considerations
The drilling data as well as previous experience in NELHA area indicate that hard
excavation utilizing hoe rams and probably blasting will be needed for efficient
excavation Blasting will also fracture the underlying dense basalt and enhance
infiltration into the underlying rock formation
The rock formation at the site is generally very competent material and may be
cut at a slope ratio of about one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
3 3 Trench Operation
It is should be noted that in Hawaii there has been a relatively poor performance
record for effluent disposal systems for aqua cultural operations Although most of the
failures have been associated with injection well systems the mechanisms that
contribute to the failures are common to trench systems Generally the failures have
been attributed to clogging resulting from solids carry over gas blocking in the formation
and similar Therefore it is recommended that the project design incorporate systems
to reduce the suspended solids loading in the effluent
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We also recommend that a sand filter be incorporated into the bottom of the
trench This filter should be constructed by over excavating the bottom of the trench by
a minimum of 2 feet and backfilling with sand Installation of this filter sand would aid in
preventing infiltration of suspended solids into the underlying volcanic rock formation If
the trench shows signs of reduced capacity the effluent could be diverted to the backup
disposal system and the sand filter cleaned out
Past experience with effluent disposal systems also indicates that operational
problems such as clogging are reduced when the system is allowed to rest
periodically This is generally accomplished by cycling between the primary and backup
disposal systems The resting period appears to let solids break down due to biological
processes and may allow formation gases to dissipate

Field Exploration

five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 to depths of about 26 0 to 27 0 feet

in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil

addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
