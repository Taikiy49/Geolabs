FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
March 31st and our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally dry
surface conditions
Seventeen soil resistivity tests were conducted at four locations at the above
referenced road using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts and 24 watts output into the ground
This method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned
invert elevations of the water line were assessed by using this method at the test locations
listed on Plate 3 The approximate test locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of generally 5 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface for the
road between and at equal spacings of 2 to 4 feet for the road between

spacings are flagged with an asterisk on Plate 3
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plate 3 Results of laboratory soil resistivity tests of soil samples
obtained during our geotechnical engineering exploration are presented on Plate 4 for
reference
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
