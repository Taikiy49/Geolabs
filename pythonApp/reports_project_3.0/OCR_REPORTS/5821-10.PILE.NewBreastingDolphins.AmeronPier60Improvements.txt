In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have observed the
pile driving operations for the above project This letter report summarizes our
geotechnical observations during the installation of the piling foundations for the new

We performed a geotechnical engineering exploration and presented the results in
the above referenced report The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor
the geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation installation for conformance
with the recommendations presented in our report and subsequent modifications In
addition we provided Special Inspection services by observing the driven pile foundation
for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Description
The project is located at the existing Pier 60 Ameron Plant Facility off
Pahounui Drive in the Keehi Lagoon area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
project will involve the construction of two new breasting dolphins at selected locations
along the existing pier structure

Based on the information provided we understand that the breasting dolphins will
be used to secure vessels boarding alongside Pier 60 According to the project structural
engineer each breasting dolphin may be subjected to a lateral impact force of up to about
1 100 kips acting at about the mid height of the new pile cap We understand that each of
the new breasting dolphins will be supported by a group of 24 inch outside diameter OD
steel pipe piles with concrete in fill in a 3 by 2 configuration The five pile group will include
three vertical piles along the seaward side and two batter piles on the landward side
battered at two horizontal to twelve vertical 2H 12V with a minimum pile tip elevation of
about 175 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
driven pile foundation installation for the new breasting dolphins Hawaiian Dredging and
Construction Company Inc HDCC is the general and piling contractor for the project
Pile Driving Observations
The pile driving program consisting of a total of ten 10 24 inch OD steel pipe
piles designated as Pile Nos 1 through 10 was conducted from July 20 through
August 2 2011 under the near continuous observations by our field representative HDCC
used a Delmag Model D30 32 diesel hammer with a fuel setting of either No 3 or No 4
delivering a rated energy of approximately 62 960 and lb respectively during
the final stage of the pile driving operations Prior to the pile driving operations the pile
locations were predrilled up to a depth of about 40 feet below the existing ground surface
to facilitate the pile driving operations through the surface fill crust and other obstructions
During the pile driving operation water was added inside the pipe piles to counteract the
buoyancy effect Pile driving logs were maintained for each pile which included
information pertaining to pile number date driven predrilled depth blow counts
observations size and length of the piles The approximate locations of the driven piles
are shown on the Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The pile driving summary is presented by
pile number on Plate 3 Individual pile driving records are presented in Appendix A
Based on our observation of the hammer performance and pile driving behavior it
is our professional opinion that the piles have been driven to a minimum pile tip elevation
of 175 feet MSL to provide sufficient vertical and lateral resistance to support the new
breasting dolphins planned for the project
