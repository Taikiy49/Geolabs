This report presents the results of our additional foundation
investigation for the proposed lanai and stairway improvements to the
existing Building 21 E of Mayor Wright Homes in the Iwilei district of
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general area of the housing site is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that three levels of lanai and related
stairs are to be added to the existing Building 21 E The general layout
of this building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 It is further
understood that this buiding is supported on piles

It is anticipated that the proposed lanai and stairway additions
will impose foundation loading of 37 5 kips per interior colum and 5 1

PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this additional investigation is to examine the
subsurface conditions at the proposed building addition areas and to
provide recommendations for the design of spread footing foundations for
the proposed stairways Two 2 test borings were drilled at the
approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
A series of laboratory tests was conducted on selected soil
samples The consolidation and strength characteristics of subsoils were
examined for the foundation support considerations Detailed description
of our field exploration and boring logs together with the results of our
laboratory tests are presented in the appendices This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
Mayor Wright Homes is a public low income housing project located
near the northwest corner at the intersection of North King Street and

Liliha Street in the Iwilei district of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The

The site is relatively flat but slightly depressed from the

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our findings we believe that the subsurface conditions
consist of 2 feet of fill underlain by gray alluvial soils The surface
fill consisted mainly of medium stiff to stiff mottled brown clayey silt
with gravel We believe that the quality of fill probably varies fran
location to location
The alluvial soils under the upper fill consist of gray silty clay
and brown to gray clayey silt with weathered gravel All our borings
encountered hard boulders at depth of 13 0 feet that prevented further
auger drilling

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings we believe that spread footings constructed
according to the proposed construction plan by the Hawaii Housing
Authority Drawing Nos S 2 and S 3 may be adequate in bearing
capacity However total primary settlement on the order of one inch
should be anticipated for the footings Specific discussions and
recommendations are presented in the following sections
Bearing Capacity
Due to the presence of the soft alluvial soils the subgrade under
the footings should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted
select granular material to reduce the loading on the soft soils
We recommend that the over excavation under footing be extended
down at least 24 inches below the bottom of the footing and extended
outward another 12 inches horizontally beyond the edges of the footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1220 10 Page 5
It is important that the subgrade under the footing be kept moist
throughout the excavation and backfilling operations to prevent drying of
the underlying clayey materials
The footing over excavation should be backfilled as soon as
possible and not be permitted to dry out excessively Where water is not
encountered in the excavation the footing backfill material should
consist of the select borrow granular material meeting the requirements
under Section 30 of the Standard Specification of Public Works
Construction City County of Honolulu 1975 This select backfill
under footing should be placed in 6 inch campacted lifts with minimim 95
of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density Where water is encountered in the
excavation the backfill material should consist of 3B Fine gravel
placed in one lift and tamped fram the surface
For the footing bearing on the above specified materials an
allowable soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used with a
one third increase allowed for wind or seismic loads The bottom of the
footing should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
Any loose or soft spots which may be encountered at the bottom of
the footing over excavation should be removed and replaced with select
borrow granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
density

Excavations should be protected fram surface run off that may carry
loose mud or other debris into the excavations All loose soil or md
should be removed from the bottom of all footing excavations prior to
backfilling or concrete placement
We recommend that all the footing excavations and backfilling
operations be inspected by the soils engineer to verify the foundation
soil conditions and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Settlement
Primary settlement on the order of one inch under the footings may
occur due to compression of the soft alluvial soils
In case this amount of settlement is not tolerable we recommend
that either the footing sizes be enlarged or the over excavation be
extended deeper and wider to further spread the loading on the soils
Based on our findings we believe that either of the following measures
might reduce the settlement to about 1 2 to 3 4 inch
1 the allowable soil bearing pressure could be reduced to 1500
p s f

Plans and specifications for the new additions should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construc

Construction Monitoring
It is recomended that the footing excavation backfilling
subgrade compaction and moistening operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
_ The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
Grilling 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the borings are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving either a
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a height of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determination tests were
performed on selected samples to aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the logs
of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Torvane tests were performed on the clay materials to evaluate
their strength characteristics Their average results are presented on
the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two consolidation tests were performed on the clay materials
to evaluate their settlement characteristics The results are depicted
as consolidation curves and shown on Plates B l and B 2
