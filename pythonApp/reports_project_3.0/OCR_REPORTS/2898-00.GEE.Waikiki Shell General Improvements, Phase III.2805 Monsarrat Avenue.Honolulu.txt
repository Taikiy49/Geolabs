INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed improvements at 2805 Monsarrat Avenue in the west Waikiki
district of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct one story storage building and a new dumpster
enclosure at the project site It is our understanding that the ground floor level will consist
of CMU walls and concrete slab on grade for these two structures Light loading is
anticipated

set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to
foundation site grading and pavement design of the project

following tasks and work efforts

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2805 Monsarrat Avenue in the west Waikiki district of
Oahu Hawaii The new one story storage building will be situated at the northeast side
of the existing Waikiki Shell stage Presently the site is occupied by an old one story

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site it is our
opinion that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the two
proposed light weight structures if the footing size is restricted to 30 inches or less and
that they bear on the upper 3 5 foot crust with minimum footing embedment
Shallow Footing Design
We believe that an allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 000 p s f may be used
for design of continuous wall footing foundations bearing on the recompacted
surface soil
To provide more uniform bearing support we recommend that the bottoms of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The above allowable bearing value is for total dead and live loads only and may
be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short term loads such as those caused
by seismic activity and or wind
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2898 00 Page 6
We recommend that all footing excavations and recompactions of the footing
bottom be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior to placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete
Footing Settlements
Total footing settlement of less than one inch and a differential footing settlement
of less than inch should be anticipated
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed buildings may be obtained by passive
earth pressure and frictional resistance developed against the embedded footing
foundations We recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot
of depth be used to determine the passive earth pressure developed against the
embedded footings A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance developed between the footing bottoms and the recompacted
footing subgrade
Footing Embedment
It is recommended that the footings be embedded at relatively shallow depth up
to twelve 12 inches below the finish grade in order to reduce the imposition of
footing bearing stress down to the underlying looser deposits below the generally
competent surface soil crust

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction on this generally
flat lot However due to soft compressible soil conditions minimal site filling is
recommended
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable material should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After site clearing and any cut that may be required the resulting surface should
be scarified to a depth of at least six 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 95
percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the procedures
described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to receive the proposed pavement for the
ground floor at both proposed structures
Soft loose spots encountered at the prepared grade level should be removed and
replaced with material compacted to 95 percent maximum dry density as indicated above
If required fill materials should be placed as soon as possible and definitely prior
to the placement of structural footings and on grade slab
The new fill should be compacted in loose level lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches
in loose thickness to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to receive
the pavement The excavated on site soils may be used for backfill and the fill material

Slab on Grade
It is our understanding that on grade concrete slabs are planned for both proposed
structures at the ground floor level which will be subject to some vehicular traffic The
following slab section may be used for preliminary design purposes
6 Inch Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 percent Compaction
12 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The slab thickened edges adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subject to vehicular traffic
The above recommended pavement section is based on the assumptions that the
traffic will be light and that adequate drainage will be provided Laboratory CBR and
density tests should be performed on the exposed subgrade during construction to verify
the subgrade strength and the above pavement section may be revised if necessary
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the
pipes
Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No
67 gravel should be used for the trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes by compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2898 00 Page 9
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from the level one foot above
the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular
material and placed in maximum ten inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or slab immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundations This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts and recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time

upon such observations
if actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been determined by taping from features shown on the topographic map provided by
Design Partners Inc on May 13 1992 Elevations of the field borings were estimated by
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2898 00 Page 11
interpolation from the contours shown on the same map The physical locations and
elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the time given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve
as the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid
on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the
interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific
exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of two
2 borings to depths of 31 5 and 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted equipment
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Graphic presentations of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 2
