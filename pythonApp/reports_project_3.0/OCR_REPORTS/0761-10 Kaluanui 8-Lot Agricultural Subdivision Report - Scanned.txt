This report presents the results of our soil investigation at
the proposed roadway site The soil investigation consisted
of drilling four borings along the proposed roadway alignment
visual observations at the site and laboratory testing of
selected subsurface soil sample
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located along Kamehameha Highway about
1100 feet south of the intersection of Kaluanui Stream and
Kamehameha Highway
W P 2
The proposed roadway alignment will extend approximately
perpendicular to Kamehameha Highway The ocean is located
on the opposite side of the highway
The lower or eastern portion of the site is fairly level
with gradients generally less than 5 percent down toward
the east Some low wet areas were noted particularly near
the eastern portion of the area The wet areas appear to
drain through a culvert under Kamehameha Highway located
next to the proposed alignment Most of the lower area is
covered with tall grass A thick growth of Hau trees is
located along Kamehameha Highway The elevation in this
area generally varies from about minus 1 foot to 10 feet
The upper or western portion of the site is located on a
terrace about 10 feet higher than the eastern portion This
area is generally flat with gradients less than 5 percent
down toward the east A small ditch with a few inches of
running water was noted flowing down toward the lower area
An earth reservoir about 100 by 250 feet in plan was noted
south of the proposed alignment Most of this area was cleared
at the time of our field exploration The elevation in this
area generally varies from about 20 to 28 feet

Soil Conditions
Lower Area
The borings in this area indicated a variable surface
layer up to 6 feet thick of silty sand fill or beach
deposit or soft clay over a loose to medium deposit
of silty and clayey sand with coral alluvium to 12 to
21 feet the depths drilled The water level was en
countered at 6 inch depths in the borings About 2 feet
of water was noted in some of the low areas
Upper Area
The borings indicated a surface layer about 3 feet thick
of stiff brown silty clay with sand and gravel fill or
alluvium over stiff mottled brown clayey silt residual
soil Groundwater was not encountered in the borings
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed plan is to construct a 44 feet wide roadway
through the Kaluanui Agricultural Subdivision The proposed
alignment will start from Kam Highway and extend mauka for
a total length of about 1700 feet
The lower eastern portion of the alignment will be filled
up to a maximum of about 4 feet The finished elevation
of the new road in this area will be at about 5 to 7 feet
The upper western portion will be cut up to a maximum of
about 4 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration indicated that the site is suitable
for the proposed road construction
In the eastern portion of the site where the fill is planned
some low soft and wet areas were noted The drainage of
this area will be an important part of the site grading
If most of the area can be successfully drained by diversion
ditches temporary culverts and other means conventional
site grading procedures can be utilized If drainage of the
wet areas cannot be accomplished a granular stabilizing layer
will be required over the wet areas prior to filling
Site Grading
Prior to filling surface vegetation and organic material
should be removed and the exposed ground surface rolled with
a compactor
Water flowing into the site should be diverted away from the
grading area
Granular material should be used to construct fills below
and up to 1 foot above the water level To develop a working
platform over soft and wet areas encountered a 2 foot layer
of granular material is recommended over the soft and wet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
areas prior to filling Material such as 3B Fine may be
used for the above specified granular material
Select on site material can be used for general fill The
natural moisture content of the in situ soils is generally
about 10 to 15 percent above the optimum moisture content
Air drying of the excavated material may be required before
using it as fill material
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of
10 inches and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are
recommended
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and slope planting should be utilized to reduce
slope erosion
Slope protection may be required where drainageways cross
or run next to the proposed road alignment

Culvert Structure
A culvert structure is planned below the proposed road at
about where an existing drainageway crosses
the proposed roadway alignment
The bottom of the existing drainageway is at about elevation
minus 1 foot Loose or soft material is anticipated under
lying this area Total settlements of about 2 inches are
anticipated due to the fill in this area However due to
the sandy nature of the subsoils the settlements are
expected to occur during the construction operations
Due to the high water level loose subsoils and anticipated
settlements a pipe culvert structure is recommended
To provide uniform support a 2 foot thick layer of granular
material should be provided under the culvert structure
An allowable bearing value of 1000 p s f can be used on
the granular fill material
Granular material should be used to backfill around and
above the culvert structure

material at the site the preliminary pavement design is

The plans for site grading and culvert foundations should

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
To a degree the performance of any structure is dependent
upon construction procedures and quality Hence observations
of site clearing and fill placement should be carried out by a
soils engineer to permit correlation between the preliminary
soil data obtained during this investigation and the actual
soil conditions encountered during construction
