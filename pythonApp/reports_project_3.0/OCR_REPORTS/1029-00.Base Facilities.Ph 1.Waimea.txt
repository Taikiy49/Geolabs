As requested we have performed a soil investigation at
the above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed base facility buildings
Our investigation consisted of four borings drilled in
the proposed building locations visual observation of
the site and laboratory soil testing
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate
our conclusion and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed location of the Canada France Hawaii Tele
scope office complex is in Waimea Hawaii bordered by
Mamalahoa Highway to the west a dirt access road to the
south and a privately owned property similar to the site
on the east and north For site location see Plate l
The site is relatively flat with tall grass numerous
Eucalyptus and Iron Wood trees There is also an exis
ting corral with horses just off Mamalahoa Highway
The existing building foundations asphaltic and concrete
were noted in the northern and southern portions of the
site
Because of tall grasses access to the site is limited
to four wheel drive and vehicles with high ground clear
ance

SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered stiff to very stiff brown silt
down to 10 to 15 foot depth Below this dense basalt
gravel and clayey silt was encountered to the depth of
the boring
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very stiff to hard soils
at the project site which could provide foundation support
for the proposed structural fill and structures Labora
tory soil tests indicated that the on site subsoils have
low swell potential Therefore if these site soils are
used to construct the structural fill conventional slab
on grade design can be utilized

W P 4
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f is re
commended for footings bearing on the very stiff in situ
soil This value of bearing capacity is for total of
dead plus live loads and may be increased by 33 when
temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
An allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f is recommended
on properly compacted structural fill
A minimum footing width of 12 inches should be utilized
The bottom of all footings should be placed a minimum of
24 inches below the finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively
small on the order of less than half an inch
Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fills are anticipated for the
site
The site should be cleared of vegetation old foundations
and debris prior to filling The existing ground surface

Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings to a depth of 20 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted auger drilling equipment
was used to drill all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard pene
tration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a dis
tance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of boring at the
appropriate sample depths
Bulk surface soil samples were obtained in the proposed
parking lot area using pick and shovel The approximate loca
tions are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
