In accordance with the project design requirements a static pile load test was
conducted at a selected pile location designated as Pile No 99 for the proposed Lanikea
at Waikiki project on January 16 and 17 2004 This letter report presents the results of
our field observations and the test data obtained from the static pile load test The general
contractor for the project is Swinerton Pacific Joint Venture and the pile installation
subcontractor is Healy Tibbits Builders Inc
The pile load test was conducted on a 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete pile The load test pile was 120 feet long To better understand the load transfer
mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile was instrumented with twelve 12
vibrating wire embedment strain gages Model No VCE 4200 manufactured by Geokon
The embedment strain gages were installed in the load test pile at the following depths
below the ground surface

Pile No 99 is located within the pile cluster near and was driven on
The test pile was driven using a Junttan HHS9 hydraulic impact hammer
delivering a maximum energy of about 79 200 foot pounds During the test pile driving a
record of the blow counts and field observations of the driving behavior was maintained by
an engineer from our office The test pile driving record is provided on Plate 2 of this letter
report
Pile No 99 was driven to a tip elevation of about 113 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The embedment strain gages were pre fabricated and installed at selected locations
during the pile casting The strain gages were carefully monitored during pile casting
prestressing concrete curing after delivery to the project site and during pile driving using
a Vibrating Wire Readout Model GK 403 manufactured by Geokon for quality control
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Quick Load Test Method of
ASTM Test Designation D 1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface
conditions at the project site The design load of the pile was 250 kips with a design factor
of safety of 2 0 The maximum test load on the pile was planned at 600 kips In general
the test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of 600 kips in 25 kip load increments
Each load increment was held for at least 4 minutes or until the rate of the pile head
movement was generally stable
In addition the static load test was held for approximately 8 hours at the 250 kip
design load test load increment to evaluate the effects of creep After loading the test
pile to the maximum test load of 600 kips the test load was unloaded in approximately four
equal decrements and held for at least 15 minutes at each decrement The results of the
compressive pile load test are summarized as follows

The readings and the load displacement curve of the static load test are presented
on Plates 3 and 4 respectively Based on the shape of the curve of the compressive load
test it appears that the load test pile is capable of sustaining the design load of 250 kips
and the rate of the pile head movement was generally stable without signs of appreciable
creep The load test pile did not fail at the maximum test load of 600 kips and achieved a
factor of safety of at least 2 4
The twelve embedment strain gages in the load test pile were connected using a
multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 data acquisition system to record the strains in the
twelve embedment strain gages during the compressive pile load test Analyses were
conducted on reducing the data from the embedment strain gages for evaluating the load
transfer between the concrete pile and the surrounding soils The strain measurements at
each embedment strain gage during concrete casting pre stressing curing before and
after pile driving and load testing are summarized on Plate 5 The interpreted load
transfer curves in the load test pile are presented on Plate 6 of this letter report
Based on the load transfer curves presented on Plate 6 the load test pile generally
behaved like a friction pile in the static load test with the majority of the maximum test load
approximately 95 percent being resisted by adhesion between the pile shaft and the
surrounding soils Based on the load transfer curves it appears that approximately
30 kips about 5 percent of the maximum test load 600 kips was transferred to the tip of
the load test pile
General information pertaining to the embedment strain gages and calibration
sheets for the strain gages are included in the Appendix of this letter report for reference
