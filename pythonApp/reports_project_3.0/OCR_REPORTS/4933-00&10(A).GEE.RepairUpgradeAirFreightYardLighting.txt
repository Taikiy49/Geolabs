PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the air freight yard within Hickam Air Force Base on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The air freight yard is located near the intersection of Moffet
Street and McChord Street
Based on the information provided we understand that it is planned to provide

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the air freight yard within Hickam Air Force Base on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The air freight yard is located near the intersection of
Moffet Street and McChord Street It is planned to remove some of the existing light poles
and fixtures along the perimeter of the air freight yard and install new light poles and
fixtures The construction work will be located mainly at the northern northwestern
eastern and southern perimeters of the air freight yard The location of the project site is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our observations and the topographic survey map provided the project
site is relatively level with existing ground surface elevations ranging between 7 to
11 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A chain link fence surrounds the air freight yard area
with barbed wires at the top of the chain link fence The majority of the pavements within
the air freight yard area consist of asphaltic concrete Building No 4069 where the freight
is stored is located to the southwest corner of the air freight yard
The existing light poles are located along the northern perimeter fence along the
south and southwest perimeter fence and within the central portion of the air freight yard
At the time of our exploration equipment and freight was observed along the perimeter
fencing The vehicular traffic was relatively light with vehicles consisting of passenger
vehicles and heavy freight hauling vehicles The main entrance into the air freight yard is
located at the eastern end of the yard
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four test borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 In general the borings extended
to depths of about 16 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the attached Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with

Our field exploration generally encountered fill material extending to depths of
about 2 to 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface Majority of the fill material consisted
of dense gravel and sand Below the fill material to depths extending to about 14 to

Medium hard to hard coral formation was encountered in Boring No 1 to the maximum

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Drilled Shaft Foundations
Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsoil conditions encountered at
the project site we recommend that cast in place drilled shafts with a minimum diameter
of 42 inches be used for the support of the new light poles planned for the project The
cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support principally from skin
friction between the shafts and the surrounding soils Based on the anticipated structural
loads and the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we recommend that drilled
shaft foundations with an embedment length of not less than 14 feet below the design
finished grades be used to support the 40 foot light pole structures located on level ground
conditions An allowable compressive load capacity on the order of about 9 kips per shaft
may be used for dead plus live loads and may be increased by 50 percent for transient
loads such as wind or seismic forces A summary of the drilled shaft foundations for
support of the light pole structures are presented in the following table

Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures In our opinion the
following conditions would have a substantial effect on the effectiveness and cost of the
drilled shaft foundations
Soft and Loose Soils
Based on our field exploration soft clay and silt and loose silty gravel and sand
between the depths of about 2 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface were
encountered at the project site To reduce the potential for caving in of the drilled
holes temporary casing may be required during the foundation construction work
Therefore we recommend that casing of drilled holes be considered as a
contingent item with additive and deductive unit prices included in the contract
documents
Workmanship
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in
drilling the shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods
utilizing drilling fluids is not recommended
Based on our field exploration groundwater is expected in the drilled holes during
the shaft installation Since groundwater will likely be encountered in the drilled
holes placement of concrete by tremie methods is recommended During
placement of concrete a minimum of 5 feet of concrete head should be maintained

Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or
S4C is recommended under the duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site granular soils or
imported select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 6 inch
level loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content corresponding to the maximum dry density
