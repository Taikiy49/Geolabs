CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING
In general the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest Crosshole Sonic Logging
tests were performed between all the possible access tube combinations in the drilled
shaft four tubes resulting in six profiles The northernmost access tube was designated
as Tube 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 9 4 feet above
the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations We understand that potable
water was used to fill the access tubes after concrete placement and the water level was
maintained in the access tubes until CSL testing Based on our observation in the field the
water levels within the access tubes were above the top of shaft concrete in all access
tubes at the time of testing
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria CCRC Deviations from the
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating The
CCRC is summarized in the table below

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each
tube combination in a drilled shaft It should be noted that the average apparent sonic
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known
accurately
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A summary of the CSL test results including the tested concrete length computed
apparent sonic wave velocity and comments on the concrete condition are presented on
Plate 2 It should be noted that the test length represents the portion of the shaft above the
load cell as CSL access tubes extend only to the top of the load cell at about 73 feet below
the top of the concrete The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized
on Plate 3 The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination profile and a
plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2
Based on the CSL test results the increased first arrival times FATs were
generally less than 10 percent corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of
less than 10 percent and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for the
trial shaft except for the areas within the profile as described below It should be noted that
changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of
the reduction and the associated increase in FAT
feet below the top of concrete
Profile 3 4 FAT increases up to 17 2 percent from approximately 2 3 to
4 7 feet below the top of concrete
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in placed concrete for the trial
shaft ranged from approximately 11 622 to 13 840 feet per second Although FAT
increases of up to 13 9 and 17 2 percent were measured in two profiles with the estimated
thicknesses of 0 8 and 2 4 feet these Questionable areas were isolated and relatively
thin Therefore we believe no significant defect anomaly is indicated by the CSL test and
we recommend acceptance of the Load Test Shaft within the testing depth provided the
acceptable results of the load testing
