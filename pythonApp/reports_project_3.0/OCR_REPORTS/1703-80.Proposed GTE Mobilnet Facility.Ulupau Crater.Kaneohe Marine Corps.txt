PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The GIE Mobilnet network of the island of Oahu includes several cell
sites which together will create a mobile communication network
The facility at Ulupau Crater includes a new 12 by 20 by 10 feet high
prefabricated building placed on a permanent foundation and a 35 foot
high self supporting 3 legged tower It is our understanding that the
tower will be designed for the following base reactions
Total Weight 5 7 Kips
Moment 394 8 Kip Ft
Maximum Shear 15 9 Kips Total
Maximum Compression 77 9 Kips Per Leg
Maximum Uplift 74 1 Kips Per Leg
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on top of the Ulupau Crater in the U S
Marine Corps Air Station at Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general site is an open grassed area presently unoccupied
Topographically the site is on high ground elevation of about 664
feet above Mean Sea Level The tower and building locations are situated
on the top of the crater and are in general flat The distance from the
proposed construction to the nearest steep slope which has a slope ratio
steeper than 1H 1V varies from about 6 to 13 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A total of two 2 borings were drilled at the site at the
approximate locations indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring
Grilled at the proposed building locations extended to about 6 5 feet
below the existing ground surface while the boring at the proposed tower
location was drilled to a depth of 21 0 feet
In general the borings encountered a thin surface layer
approximately 0 5 foot thick of gray cinder sand over a medium hard
cemented cinder layer which is further underlain by medium hard tuff
formation
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent subsoil conditions for
support of the proposed structures using spread footing and mat
foundation
Spread Footing and Mat Foundation
Shallow spread footing may be used for the proposed one story
building and mat foundation may be used for the proposed 35 foot tower
structure An allowable bearing capacity of 5000 p s f can be used for
footing or mat foundation bearing on the medium hard cemented cinder
The footing for the proposed building should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent grade while the mat foundation for the
proposed tower should be embedded a minimum of three 3 feet
Settlement of the proposed structures are estimated to be minimal

Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structures can be
obtained by passive pressure developed by the buried part of the footing
or mat foundation and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the footing or mat foundation and the medium hard cemented
cinder
Design lateral earth pressures unit weights and coefficient of
friction are as follows
a Active soil pressure 30 p c f
b Passive soil pressure 300 p c f
c Soil moist unit weight 120 p c f
d Coefficient of friction 0 4
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the building and tower foundation The bottom of the excavation should be
thoroughly cleared of any debris or loose soils prior to placement of the
building and tower foundation
Excavated on site granular sand material free of any organic and
other expansive component may be used as a source of backfill material
All imported fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty
sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All backfill materials should be placed in
maximum of 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 95 of the
maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation support of the mobilnet
facility should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the excavations for foundation be inspected by the
soils engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to
check that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface exploration
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 6 5 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A portable
rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The rock encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered is presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Rock core samples were obtained by coring with a NX core barrel
The percentage of core recovery REC and the rock quality designation
RQD for each run are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
