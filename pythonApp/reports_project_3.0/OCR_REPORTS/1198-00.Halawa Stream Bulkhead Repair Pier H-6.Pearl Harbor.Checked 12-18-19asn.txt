INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary soils
investigation for the repair of the entire 510 feet of bulkhead H 6 at
Halawa Stream Pearl Harbor Naval Station Pearl Harbor Oahu
Hawaii The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
It is our understanding that the existing sheetpile bulkhead along H 6
is to be repaired by installing a new sheetpile bulkhead The
existing sheetpile had been driven to Elevation 50 feet U S N Datum
U S N Datum from the existing grades which are at
about Elevation 108 feet A row of sheetpile located about 40 feet
from the bulkhead serves as tieback anchor The dredged bottom in
front of the bulkhead varies from Elevations 85 to 100 feet

The new replacement sheetpile will be installed a few feet in front of
the existing concrete capped sheetpile The space between the new and
existing sheetpiles will be backfilled Several different tieback
schemes are being considered However more current consideration is
to abandon the existing sheetpile anchor system and replace it with a
new anchor system
SCOPE OF SERVICES
A General layout of the site is shown on Site Plan Plate 2 Because
of the existing buildings and other shelter structures along the
bulkhead as shown on Plate 2 the scope of our investigation was
limited to drilling one boring to explore the subsurface conditions
and develop preliminary design data for new sheetpile The location
of the boring is shown on Plate 2
The boring was drilled to a depth of 80 feet under the direction of
one of our engineers after the boring location was toned and cleared
by Navy Public Works Center Disturbed and undisturbed samples were
obtained for our laboratory examination and testing The tests
included triaxial tests with pore pressure measurement direct shear
consolidation and Atterberg Limits tests and moisture and density
determinations The test results and the findings from the field
exploration are evaluated and our preliminary design recommendations
developed for the sheetpile design

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface Conditions
The site is relatively flat and is occupied by various one story
structures This area was formerly used as staging area for tours to
the Arizona Memorial These structures are located very close to the
bulkhead and extend almost the full length of the bulkhead The
remaining areas are surfaced with asphalt concrete
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions disclosed in our boring consist of about 8
feet of fill overlying thick soft to medium stiff compressible
lagoonal deposits to at least 80 feet the maximun depth explored in
this investigation
The fill materials consist of generally dense to very dense brown to
tan silty sand and gravel that grades less dense near the bottom of
the fill at about 5 feet a depth near the ground water table
The lagoonal deposits below the fill consist mainly of dark blue gray
soft somewhat clayey silt with loose sand lenses and some organic
matter The upper portion of the lagoonal deposits contain several
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1198 00 Page 4
layers of loose gray silty sand and coral gravel to a depth of about
23 feet The material then become predominantly soft clayey silt
containing frequent decomposed wood pieces shell fragments and other
organic matter to about 60 feet
Below about 60 feet the lagoonal deposits become medium stiff to
stiff and less sandy and relatively free of decomposed wood pieces
The materials also become somewhat sensitive that is becomes weaker
when disturbed
It is probable that the soil profile along the bulkhead will be
essentially similar to that disclosed in Boring 1 with some variation
in composition of the lagoonal sediments and their total thickness
The groundwater level in the boring was measured at different times
The measurements are shown on the log of the boring The groundwater
ranged from 5 feet 3 inches to 6 feet 5 inches below the ground
surt ace
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The existing sheetpiles will tend to redistribute the earth pressures
on the new wall However it is reported that the tie backs may not
be functioning Thus these existing sheetpiles may be functioning as
cantilevered walls The effect of the existing sheetpile on new one
will be to reduce the earth pressures somewhat on the back of the new
one However the total effect will probably be small
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
The lateral earth pressures on the new bulkhead are estimated on the
basis of a few sample tests and general understanding of the site
conditions The backfill between the new and existing sheetpiles
should be granular fill such as crushed rock fragments This backfill
material will tend to dictate the active earth pressure behind the new
sheetpiles The recommended active and passive pressure distributions
are shown on Plate 3 An allowance for surcharge load and hydrostatic
pressure should be added The active earth pressures due to a uniform
surcharge should be one third of the surcharge load acting on the
full depth of the sheetpile
We believe that design high water behind the sheetpile should be
assumed to be not lower than Elevation 103 feet We understand that
the water level in front is to be designed at Elevation 98 feet
The new sheetpiling will be anchored near the top and embedded in
lagoonal deposit For a dredge depth of upto 23 feet below the top of
existing bulkhead the new sheetpile may need only to penetrate up to
about 60 feet or Elevation 55 feet Thus they will probably bottom
out in soft lagoonal deposits We believe that free earth support
method should be used for tie rod tension calculation
The backfill between the new and existing sheetpiles should consist of
clean gravel size rock fragments Water level should be lowered if
necessary during backfilling so that excessive hydrostatic pressure
may not occur

Tie Back Anchors
The passive earth pressure wedge of the tie back anchor should be
sufficiently outside the active pressure wedge to develop full
capacity This distance probably would be on the order of 70 feet
from the new bulkhead line for sheetpile penetrating to Elevation 50
feet The distance can be shorter where the dredge bottom is
shallower and active wedge is smaller
The soil conditions along the potential anchor location are not fully
disclosed It is anticipated that they would consist of dense to
medium dense granular fills or backfills of utility lines under the
asphalt paved staging area It is recommended that these conditions
be explored by additional test borings
Estimated tentative net total earth pressure distributions for the
anchor design is also shown on Plate 3 As mentioned earlier these
values are derived from an assumed soil conditions and should be
verified with additional exploration
The earth pressures presented on Plate 3 are assumed ultimate values
and appropriate factor of safety and or load factor should be used for
Sizing of the anchor

SUPPLEMENTARY INVESTIGATION
The recommendations presented herein are based on one boring While
we believe that the data presented are probably representative
additional exploration is recommended The subsurface conditions
along the wharf and anchorage area should be further explored when
access to these locations are provided after demolition of the shelter
structures The recommendations on the pressure distribution may need
to be modified as appropriate according to the additional findings
DESIGN CONSTRUCTION REVIEW
Plans and specifications for the new sheetpile system should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the sheetpile installation anchor block
installation and backfilling operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation and that adequate compactions have been obtained The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling a single boring to 80 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to
drill the boring
The soils encountered were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our Field Engineer who
continuously monitored the drilling Operations The classi
fications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general con
formance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a thin walled Shelby tube sampler a standard penetra
tion sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling at a height of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Log at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
re
Moisture content and dry unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples to aid in classifying and
evaluating soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the boring log at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed to further aid in
classifying the soils The test results are presented on Plate
Bel
Three consolidated undrained triaxial compression tests with
porewater pressure measurements were performed to evaluate the
soild strength characteristic of the subsoil The test results
are summarized on Plates B 2 and B 3 as plots of stress paths
Five unconfined compression tests and one direct shear test
were performed on undisturbed samples to further evaluate the
soil strengths The unconfined compression test results are
summarized on Plate B 4 The direct shear test results are
presented on Plate B 5
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed sample
to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the natural
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 6
