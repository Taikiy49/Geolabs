INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design and construction of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed Truck Fueling Facility in Barbers Point
Naval Air Station
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to obtain soils data for the formulation
of engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site
grading fueling rack and access road pavements and foundations of the
proposed pumphouse building
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of April
1 1988
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of six 6
borings to depths ranging between 4 5 and 10 9 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected
samples obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of
the field and laboratory data the development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
_ SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the existing fuel farm in Barbers
Point Naval Air Station in the district of The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

The site is situated on the south and southwestern sides of the two
2 existing above ground storage tanks in an area covered with a sparse
growth of low grasses and traversed by an asphaltic concrete access road
The site is nearly level with a slight slope towards the south or the
taxiway area
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a truck fueling rack for the loading of
aircraft fuel into tanker trucks and a fuel pumphouse on the site The
proposed fueling rack will be a Portland cement concrete apron
functioning as a rigid pavement The proposed pumphouse will be a
single story structure with a slab on grade floor It is anticipated
that floor and column loads will be light It is also proposed to
construct an asphaltic concrete access road between the existing road and
the taxiway
The site grading scheme was not available at the time of our
exploration however it is anticipated that grading will be minimal
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Ewa coastal plain which consists
of a thick horizontal series of interbedded marine and nommarine
sediments capping Waianae and Koolau Basalts The near surface structure
at the site consists of elevated Pleistocene coral reef deposits of
variable consistency and composition

At the project site these reef deposits are overlain by a very thin
layer of man made fill from grading during the development of the area
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging between 4 5 and
10 9 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered surface layers of fill composed
of stiff tan sandy silts with gravel varying between 0 5 and 1 5 feet in
thickness Below the fill the borings encountered medium hard
coral algal limestone formation coral to the maximum depths drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time
of our exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed We recommend that spread or strip
footings bear on the medium hard coral or on engineered fill compacted in
accordance with the recommendations given below
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that site
grading will be minimal

The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The
resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches or to coral
formation whichever is less The scarified area should then be
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content
proofrolled and recompacted to 95 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any rubble or
boulders encountered during the scarification which exceed six
6 inches in maximum dimension should be removed and
discarded Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during
the proofrolling and recompaction operations should be
overexcavated and backfilled with select material placed in
eight inch thick lifts compacted to 95 maximum dry density as
described above
Imported fill material if required should be non expansive
granular soils generally less than 3 inches in size
All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding nine 9 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density as established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Foundation Support
In order to provide uniform support for the proposed pumphouse
structure we recommend that the spread or strip footings bear either
entirely on in situ coral formation or entirely on compacted engineered
fill For the design of foundations bearing on the in situ coral an
allowable bearing capacity of 6000 p s f may be utilized For the
design of foundations bearing on fill compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein an allowable bearing capacity of 3000
p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimm of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
Settlements
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on engineered fill
constructed in accordance with the above recommendations are believed to
be less than one inch total and one half of an inch differential For
foundations bearing on the underlying dense coral formation minimal
settlements are anticipated
We recommend that provisions be made under the bases of the pumping
equipment to accommodate these and additional footing settlements which
might occur if levelness of the equipment is a critical consideration
Cavity Probing
Due to the variable nature of coral formations and the common
occurrence of voids and or cavities in such formations we recommend that
a foundation subgrade probing program be implemented Each foundation
column location and on 5 foot centers in strip footings and in the fuel
rack area should be probed to a minimum depth of ten 10 feet with

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work In addition the
geotechnical engineer should be retained to provide continuous
observation and consultation services during the proofrolling and cavity
probing phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points shown on the Topographic Survey and
Location of Fueling System Improvements at Barbers Point Naval Air
Station Oahu prepared by Towill Shigeoka and Associates undated
Elevations of the data points were approximately determined by estimation
from the Topographic Survey and Location of Fueling System Improvements
at Barbers Point Naval Air Station Oahu prepared by Towill Shigeoka
and Associates undated The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines show on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data if encountered and noted in the field data points
was collected at the times given on the graphic representations and or in
the text of this report This data has been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six
6 borings to depths of 4 5 to 10 9 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash NWD4 core
barrel The core recovery and rock quality designation RQD are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depths
