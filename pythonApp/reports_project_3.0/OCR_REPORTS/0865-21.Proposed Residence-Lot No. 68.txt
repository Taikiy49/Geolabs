As requested we have made a study of the near surface
soil conditions below the above referenced lot The
purpose of the study was to evaluate the suitability of
near surface soils as foundation support for the proposed
house The study consisted of 4 hand auger probes within
the proposed building site laboratory soil tests and a
review of the grading and compaction test record on the
building site This letter summarizes our observations
and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The preliminary house plans for the proposed single family
residence were not available during our soil investigation
It is anticipated that a 2 to 3 story wood framed structure
utilizing concrete slab on grade for the garage level and
post and beam construction for the remaining portions would
be constructed
Due to the sloping terrain at the lot it is anticipated
that cuts would be required in the rear portion of the
house to create sufficient level backyard area for the
residence
Existing Site Conditions
Lot No 68 is located on the southwestern portion of the
subdivision development near the end of Kulani Street
The lot is bounded by Kulani Street on the northeast
subdivision lots on the southeast and northwest and
the natural valley side slope on the west An existing
concrete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern
upper portion of the lot The lot generally slopes
upward from Kulani Street toward the west at about
24 1 H V ratios The elevation of the lot generally
varies from about 176 to 222 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain
by a surface layer of gray highly to critically expansive
Adobe clay about 1 5 to 2 0 feet thick over dense
weathered rock Foundations should extend through the
thin surface Adobe clay layer and bear on the dense
rock formation Concrete slab should bear on a minimum
of 3 feet of select non expansive soil or on weathered
rock formation While the driveway should bear on a
minimum of 2 feet of select non expansive soil or on
weathered rock formation
Foundations
Individual spread footings for the house should extend
through the surface Adobe clay and bear on the dense
weathered rock formation The footings should extend
a minimum of 6 inches into the weathered rock formation
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used on
the dense weathered rock
If a footing is located next to a utility line the
footing should extend to the bottom of the utility
trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement
of the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 21 P 5
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the
house to reduce ponding of surface run off water and
possible saturation and settlement or heaving of the
foundation soils
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade would probably be used for the garage
It appears that the rear portion of the garage area would
be cut in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage
Therefore it appears that dense weathered rock would be
encountered in the rear cut half of the garage excavation
The front half of the garage may encounter the Adobe clay
depending on the amount of cut The Adobe clay soils are
highly expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
Where encountered the Adobe clays should be overexcavated
down to dense weathered rock or for a maximum depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and replaced with non expansive borrow
soils compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM D 1557 This overexcavation and replacement should
be extended for a distance of 3 feet beyong the perimeter of
the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the
slab and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a polyethy
lene moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The membrane should be protected by a thin layer of sand

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A thin surface layer about 1 foot thick of expansive
Adobe clay soils were encountered in the front portion
of the lot driveway area In order to reduce the expan
sive effects of the Adobe clay it should be kept a
minimum of 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non
expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
Adobe clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
overexcavation and replacement of the Adobe clay with
select non expansive material is required The above
requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Field observation indicates that very dense weathered rock
would be encountered in some portions of the driveway exca
vation
If a relatively long driveway is planned joints are
recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to
allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the Adobe clay or over
excavation and replacement similar to the driveway is re
commended to reduce heaving caused by the expansive Adobe
clay soil

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down
at about 4 1 H V ratio or flatter A cut slope for
the backyard development may be required
Based on the field development we anticipate that cuts
up to about 2 feet would be in Adobe clay While deeper
cuts would encounter weathered rock The thin Adobe clay
layer should be stripped off or flattened as much as possible
to reduce later sloughing or downhill slope creep Cuts in
dense weathered rock could be made at slope ratios of 1 1
H V
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material If available some of the weathered rock
excavated material smaller than 8 inches could be used
in the fill if it is not contaminated with the Adobe
clay The Adobe clay soil should not be used for fill
material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557

Design Review
The plans for site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included in the project plans and specifica
tions
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
