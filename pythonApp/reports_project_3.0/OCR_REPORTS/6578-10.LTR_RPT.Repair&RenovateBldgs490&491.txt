PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is to the northeast of the intersection of North Road and
Neches Street within Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that it is
planned to repair and renovate the existing Buildings 490 491 The existing warehouse
buildings have asphaltic concrete and concrete slab on grade interior pavements The
existing interior pavements are sloping uneven and deteriorated with possible voids
below The existing pavements will be removed and replaced with reinforced concrete
slabs moisture barrier soil termite treatment and fill material
The interior building work will include demolition of existing offices and installation
of new offices and support spaces New office structures within the buildings will require
continuous wall and spread footings A new reinforced concrete slab with thickened edge

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Prior to our involvement in the construction phase of the project Raass
Brothers Inc general contractor demolished and removed the existing concrete
slabs on grade in Warehouse Buildings 490 and 491 We understand the underlying
subgrade materials were then scarified with a Caterpillar D3 dozer moisture conditioned
to near optimum moisture content and compacted using a 24 smooth drum
vibratory roller
Field density tests were performed by CEL during the grading operations using the
nuclear gauge method A total of 34 field density tests were performed by CEL during the
period of May 29 2012 through including retests of failed areas Results of
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 of this report
In addition a sample of the subgrade soils were obtained by CEL and the laboratory
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content of the subgrade soils were
determined in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Our field work for the construction phase of the project began on August 22 2012
At the time of our first site visit the subgrade soils had been covered with plastic sheeting
moisture barrier and steel rebar was tied and in place The purpose of our site visit was
to confirm that the subgrade soils beneath the future slabs on grade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction by providing supplementary field density
testing Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density determined by ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Our field density tests on the first and second site visits indicated that some areas
failed to meet the minimum compaction requirement per the project specifications The
contractor proceeded to remove the steel rebar and plastic sheeting in those failing areas
and during our observation and testing period areas assessed as unsatisfactory were
reworked and retested until the specified minimum compaction was obtained Field density
tests were performed by our field personnel using the nuclear gauge method in
accordance with ASTM D 6938 A total of 30 field density tests were performed by our firm
in this observation and testing period including retests of failed areas Results of the field
density tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 of this report Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the project
foundation plans These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used

Samples of the subgrade soils were obtained and transported to our office for
laboratory testing The laboratory maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
of the subgrade soils were determined in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 In
addition one laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk

subgrade soils The test results are summarized on Plate 3 and the individual laboratory

Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with
the project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
