INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil exploration performed for
the subject project
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil and
groundwater conditions along selected portions of the proposed water pond
retention fill embankment and cut areas to provide general subsurface
soil data to evaluate the stability of the planned retention ponds cuts
and embankments

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Each of the proposed pond areas are approximately 500 feet long and
about 150 to 240 feet wide in plan dimension at the top
Detailed design of the ponds are unavailable at the present time
however it is our understanding that the proposed ponds will be
approximately 16 feet deep with interior side wall slopes of 3 1 H V
A 2 1 H V slope ratio is planned for the exterior embankment wall where
required The proposed water pond embankments will be constructed
partially by excavation of up to about 20 feet into the existing ground
and partially by the placement of up to about 20 feet of fill above the
existing ground
We also understand that the interior walls of the proposed water
ponds will be lined with an impermeable membrane over a gravel drainage
blanket

SITE DESCRIPTION
The two proposed sites are located near the northern end of the
Mauka Golf Course about 450 feet south of the planned Mauka Clubhouse
area and about 100 feet east and west of Makawao Stream
The proposed pond located about 80 to 120 feet east of Makawao
Stream is approximately 500 feet long and 150 feet wide The existing
ground elevations of the site vary from about 171 to 191 feet The
existing ground surface generally slopes gently to the north and also to
the east This pond site is presently a banana plantation area
The second proposed pond site is located about 70 to 110 feet west
of Makawao Stream and is approximately 500 feet long and 120 to 240 feet
wide The existing ground elevations of the site vary from about 170 to
185 feet The site appears to consist of two terraces one at about 182
feet elevation which covers approximately the southern half of the pond
area and the second terrace is at about 74 feet elevation mostly
occupying the northern half of the proposed pond area This pond site is
covered by thick grass and trees

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration generally revealed that the explored
proposed site areas are underlain primarily by gravelly to clayey silts
which are medium stiff to very stiff alluvium colluvium Locally some
soft zones were encountered within the deposits Boring A 4
Based on our field exploration the most important aspect of this
geotechnical engineering evaluation is the stability of the proposed cuts
and fill embankments which are needed to retain the two 2 proposed
water ponds
Because of the close proximity of the two proposed sites to the
existing Makawao Stream a high groundwater level of about 6 3 to 8 0
feet below the existing ground surface was recorded in some areas of the
proposed sites during the field exploration Therefore dewatering may
be required for some portions of the pond to allow placement of the
lining and cushion
Slope Stability
It is our understanding that the water pond retention embankments at
the two proposed sites will be formed partially by cutting into the
existing ground and partially by the construction of fill embankments
Based on the laboratory determined shear strengths which were
measured after saturation and slope stability analysis performed we
believe that the present design slope ratios of 2 1 H V for the
exterior side walls and 3 1 H V for the interior side walls could
GEOLABS HAWAH

Dk No 30
provide adequate stability for the proposed water retention embankments
The analysis assumes that seepage water pressure will not be experienced
in the slope
We understand that an impervious liner and a gravel drainage blanket
will be placed on the walls and bottom of the ponds to reduce the
possibility of seepage To reduce the potential for punctures in the
lining a cushion layer of sand is recommended along the bottoms and
sides of the lagoons prior to placement of the liner The cushion layer
should be a minimm of 6 inches thick
Aithough the water ponds will be lined with an impervious liner
there is a possibility of some seepage due to punctures or breaks in the
seams Therefore we recommend that the exterior wall or downstream
slope of the fill embankments be constructed with a granular toe section
to allow drainage of seepage and reduce the possibility of piping through
the embankment
Water Ponds
During the field exploration the existing groundwater level was
encountered at about 6 3 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface in
areas of the site that are close to the existing Makawao Stream Borings
A 3 A 4 and A 5
High groundwater levels may create a muddy subgrade condition
which may pose special construction problems and increase the
construction costs
Additionally the water pond lining may be subjected to hydraulic
buoyant forces and may become unstable when the lagoons are emptied for
routine maintenance or some other operational purposes
Therefore we recommend that the base of the water ponds be designed
and constructed above the groundwater table and deep excavation of the
existing ground should be avoided in order to reduce construction
difficulties and potential embankment instability

Site Grading
In general the site grading for the two proposed water ponds will
consist of cutting and filling along the existing hillsides and terraces
adjacent to Makawao Stream at locations shown on Plate 2
Cuts and fills up to about 20 feet are planned
Ground Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
should be removed and disposed of properly off site to prevent
contamination of the excavated materials
In the areas to receive fill the existing ground should be
scarified to a depth of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of 85
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Any localized soft or weak areas should be over excavated to firm
ground and backfilled in 9 inch loose horizontal lifts compacted to
the above specified requirement
Fill Material
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site soils could be suitable for use as fill if they can be
dried out sufficiently to meet the compaction requirements stated
below The natural soil moisture may need to be reduced by about 20
percent to meet the compaction requirement This may require one to
two weeks of continuously dry weather to achieve It should be
realized that the project site is situated in a very high rainfall
area and therefore delays due to wet weather and insufficient
drying of the soil are very common and should be expected

Large boulders in excess of 2 foot in diameter may be used to
construct riprap or buttresses or should be reduced to smaller
sizes
If additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to
the site The material should generally be of low expansion
potential and should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or deleterious material
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have in place moisture contents about 40 to 60
well above its optimum moisture content Therefore drying of the
excavated material is required before using it as fill material
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in
other drier areas should be modified to 85 for this project
Laboratory shear strength tests on compacted samples indicate that
the 85 compaction can provide adequate soil strength for the
proposed embankment fill The fills should be placed in 10 inch
maximum compacted lifts
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted
fill
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm
ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope for details see
Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of the fills
particularly to reduce the potential of sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers

Slopes
Three horizontal to one vertical 3 1 slope ratio or flatter is
planned for the interior side walls of the proposed embankment A
steeper slope ratio up to two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 or
flatter may be used for constructing the exterior embankment wall
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design
fill slope and then cutting back
For f1i11 embankments to be constructed at or near existing
drainageways the toes of the fill slope may be susceptible to
erosion by flood water during high river stages In these areas
adequate rock fill riprap should be used to protect the fill toes
_ from scouring by flood water
Settlements
st te sh ese
Small settlements approximately 1 inch or less are anticipated for
most of the fill areas where medium stiff to stiff subsoils are
anticipated
However should lowlying soft areas be encountered during
construction we recommend that the placement of the required
embankment fill should be carried out in several increments of about
5 feet each with at least one week rest period in between This
will allow the underlying soft soils to gain strength incrementally
prior to additional loading in order to reduce the possibility of
bearing or slope failure
Design Review
The plans for site grading and water pond retention embankment
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly fill placement and slope cuts and
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is particularly important that the cut slope and fill embankment
construction be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that the subsurface conditions and ground behavior are consistent with
the findings of our exploration Additional borings may be required
during site grading operations depending on the materials actually
encountered during excavations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
