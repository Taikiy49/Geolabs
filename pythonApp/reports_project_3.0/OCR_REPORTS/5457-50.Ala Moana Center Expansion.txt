Dear Ms Thom
As part of the Ala Moana Center Expansion project development we understand
that a new sewer line will be installed below the Coral Level of the Ala Moana Center
The new sewer line will run in a north south direction extending from the southeastern
corner of the development near Kona Street traversing below the existing Ala Moana
Center and terminating at the existing 69 inch sewer line at Ala Moana Park across
Ala Moana Boulevard Based on the existing conditions site constraints and other
considerations we envision that trenchless technology such as microtunneling in lieu
of open trench methods will be required for installation of the new sewer line
Based on the information provided we understand that the new sewer line will be
located within the alignment corridor as shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1
A specific geotechnical engineering exploration was not conducted for this new sewer
line project due to time constraints This letter was prepared to present the pertinent
soils boring data obtained from our literature research of previous field explorations
conducted in the vicinity of the new sewer line alignment corridor The approximate
locations of the previously drilled borings by various geotechnical consultants and at
various times are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 References for the
pertinent borings drilled are presented on Plate 2 and the corresponding Logs of
Borings are provided in Appendix A for reference
It should be noted that the information presented in this letter is for information
purposes only and is not intended for detailed design and construction The contractor
should retain a qualified geotechnical engineer to review and interpret the anticipated
subsurface conditions for construction purposes Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the exploration points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until additional field investigation and or
construction is underway Therefore this letter may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use to form the basis for preparation of construction cost
estimates or contract bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a
