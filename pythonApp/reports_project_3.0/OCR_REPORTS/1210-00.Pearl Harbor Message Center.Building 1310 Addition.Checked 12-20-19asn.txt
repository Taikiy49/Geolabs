INTRODUCTION
This letter presents our recommendations regarding foundation support
for the proposed Addition to Message Center COMSUBPAC Headquarters
Building 1310 in Pearl Harbor Submarine Base Oahu Hawaii Navy
shown on the Project Location Plan Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The scope of our services is to review available soils data and develop
recommendations for the foundation design for the above project The
available information include boring logs on NAVFAC Drawing No 1160853
and other soils data in our file pertaining to subsurface conditions
within the Pearl Harbor area No boring was drilled specifically for
this building addition due to limited budget
W O 1210 00 Page 2
The proposed building addition will be a one story structure with
concrete masonry unit walls The maximum column loads are anticipated
to be about 25 kips per column The proposed location of the addition
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE CONDITIONS
One of our geologist visited the site and noted that the slope to the
east of the addition was a cut into massive coral with a thin cover of
reddish brown silt fill The site is covered with asphalt concrete
It is probable that several utility trenches as well as other buried
structure exist under the site
Although no boring was drilled for this proposed addition the sub
surface conditions were evaluated based on the available boring logs
and our understanding of soils and geologic conditions of the vicinity
The locations of the borings are shown on Plate 2
The boring logs shown on NAVFAC Drawing No 1160853 are reproduced on
Plate 3 According to the logs the subsurface conditions below the
asphalt concrete consist of about 2 feet of fill consisting of coral
and tuff mudrock fragments 2 to 4 feet of coral underlain by
volcanic tuff The tuff deposit in this vicinity is generally over 20
feet thick underlain by stiff to very stiff soils This subsurface
condition correlates well with information developed for other
investigation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation Support
Based on the above information we recommend that spread footing be
used for foundation support The footing should be placed at least 2
feet below the lowest adjacent grade or in the undisturbed coral
whichever is deeper For the proposed light building loads an
allowable soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used with 1 3
increase allowed for wind or seismic loads
In general the natural undisturbed coral is expected at or below
Elevation 109 feet MLLW Elevation 100 00 Therefore the footing
depth may need to be extended to or below this elevation If dis
turbed material such as old trench or structure backfills or soft
loose materials are encountered in the footing excavation they should
be removed to undisturbed coral The overexcavation should either be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill or lean concrete The
footing may also be supported directly on the above recompacted back
fill layer
The select granular backfill should consist of clean coral sand and
gravel crushed rock base course or any other select on site granular
material All backfill should be tested by a soils engineer for
approval prior to being used The backfill material should be placed
in 6 inch compacted lifts and compacted to a dry density of at least
95 of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with the ASTM
D 1557 78 test procedure

On Grade Slab
It is our understanding that the proposed addition will be used as an
office area with the finished floor at Elevation 111 58 feet The
existing A C surface is about 4 inches below the finished grade
adjacent to Building 619 and Slopes away from both Buildings 619 and
1310
Because the existing A C pavement is in a good condition the new
on grade slab may be constructed over the A C without removing the
asphaltic concrete However it is likely that the construction for
new utility foundation and catch basin May remove a significant
portion of the asphaltic concrete When A C thus become segmented
it should be removed to provide more uniform subgrade support
Where A C has been removed or is sufficiently below the slab a
4 inch thick crushed rock cushion consisting of S4C type material is
recommended under the floor slab This S4C material may also be used
as veneer of 111 below the new slab and over the existing A C as
levelling course
A continuous sheet of impervious membrane is recommended to serve as
vapor barrier This membrane should be of sufficient thickness to
Support workers on it without rupture Any rupture should be patched

The subgrade below the cushion rock layer including the existing A C
surface should be recompacted after all footing and underground
utility works are completed The rock cushion should also be com
pacted All compaction in soil and rock cushion should be to a dry
density of at least 95 of the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 densities
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the new building addition should be for
warded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavation and backfilling opera
tions be observed by the soils engineer during construction This is
to see that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the sub
surface conditions assumed and that adequate compactions have been
obtained The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
