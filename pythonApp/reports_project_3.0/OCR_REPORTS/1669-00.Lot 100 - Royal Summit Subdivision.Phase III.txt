Soil Engineering Report
Lot 100 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase III
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing of the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing of the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden structure
approximately 44 by 75 feet in plan dimension with a three car garage
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and the
lower house level

Subsurface Soil Conditions
Three borings were drilled to depths of 6 5 to 11 5 feet below the
existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
1
In general the borings encountered hard reddish brown clayey silt to
the full depth drilled
A review of the mass grading plan prepared by Community Planning
Inc dated August 23 1984 for Royal Summit Phase III Subdivision
indicated that this lot was cut and filled generally less than 4 feet
No groundwater was encountered during our investigation
Discussion and Recommendations
General
The hard reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some
care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling potential
of the soil
Laboratory tests performned on the hard clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1669 00 Page 4
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
I located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as crusher
waste select borrow or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the lower house areas the on site soil
should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of
non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils may be
expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site should be
over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom
thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported soils
as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place soil below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete placement
heavily soaked with water for the first two days and sprinkled with
water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3 fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the on site
soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some small slab
cracking is tolerable

The subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition as

Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally less than 5
feet high may be required at the middle of the proposed residence to
provide grade separation The guidelines for the retaining wall designs
are listed below
1 The following can be used for lateral earth pressures
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
Additional load due to surcharge should be included where
applicable
2 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade

3 An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported non expansive or on site
soil
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
6 Backfills behind the retaining wall should consist of granular
non expansive material such as select borrow crusher waste or
3B Fine gravel and extend a minimum horizontal distance of 2
feet behind the wall
7 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1669 00 Page 9
Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and slab
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the finds of our subsurface investigation
The report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
preparation of site grading and foundation plans for this project
