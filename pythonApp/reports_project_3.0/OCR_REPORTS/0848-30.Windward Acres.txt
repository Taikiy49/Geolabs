In accordance with your request we have made a study of
the near surface soil conditions at the above referenced
site situated next to the Windward Estates project
presently under construction
The existing surcharge material at Windward Estates is
scheduled for removal shortly In order to reduce hauling
away costs for the surcharge soil the possibility of using
the soil to fill the lower portion of the adjacent Windward
Acres project is being evaluated

The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the sui
tability of the proposed fill placement in the lower area
Our study consisted of six hand auger probes within the
fill area and laboratory soil tests on the soil samples
obtained from the probe holes This letter summaries our
observations and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed fill area is situated in a lowlying area
which borders the southern portion of Windward Estates
and is situated between Haiku Road and Kahuhipa Street
The site is situated about 600 feet east of Kahekili
Highway
The proposed fill area is relatively flat with ground
surface elevations of 118 to 120 The area slopes up
toward the south at gradients of about 14 1 to 3 1 H V
up to elevation 150
A small stream runs through the central portion of the
drainageway about 100 feet north of the fill site
An existing 8 inch sewer line is situated in this area
The site is presently covered with a thick growth of
California grass

SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the probings the site is underlain by 2 to 12
feet of medium to stiff alluvium consisting of brown to
gray brown clayey silt over dense sandy silt down to the
probe depths
A 2 foot soft layer was encountered at 10 to 12 foot depths
in Probe No 4
Groundwater level was encountered at 3 to 7 2 feet below
the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration the site appears suitable
for the proposed filling
Due to the low elevation and heavy vegetation at the site
the near surface soils are moist and moderately soft There
fore lighter equipment should be utilized in the initial
stages of the clearing and filling operations We antici
pate that after site clearing most of the surface soils
would be able to dry sufficiently to permit filling without
special soft ground stabilization procedures
New fill over the existing sewer line should be avoided to
minimize settlements of these structures

Site Preparation
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and
other deleterious material In areas to receive fill
the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of
6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 density
The existing surface soils are moist and moderately soft
therefore lighter equipment should be used for the initial
site clearing and ground preparation
Fill Placement
After site preparation the fill should be placed in
maximum 10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM test desig
nation D 1557 70
The underlying soils have high moisture content and are
moderately soft therefore fills should be placed at slow
rates to avoid squeezing or failure in the subsoils An
initial estimated rate of 2 feet per day is recommended
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to pro
perly bond the new fill to the slope The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
compacted layers in accordance with the above structural
fill requirements For construction details refer to
Plate 2

Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 fill slope is recom
mended for the site
To reduce the amount of run off over the slopes a drainage
bench should be provided for slopes having a vertical height
greater than 15 feet
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable
Settlements
Ground settlements are anticipated due to the weight of
the proposed new fill The amount of settlements experi
enced would depend on the amount and location of the new
fill
Normally these settlements occur over a relatively long
period of time However it is our understanding that
development of this area is not anticipated in the near
future
Should development plans change and a reduced settlement
waiting time be desired a surcharge fill program should
be considered We would be glad to discuss with you on
such program

In any event two to three settlement gauges are recommended
in this new fill area to monitor the settlement rates
Periodic readings of these gauges are recommended
