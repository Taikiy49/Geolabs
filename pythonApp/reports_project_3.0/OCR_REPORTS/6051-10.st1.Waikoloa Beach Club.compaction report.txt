This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the construction for the Waikoloa Beach Club project within Waikoloa Beach Resort on the
Island of Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is within the existing Waikoloa Beach Resort in the District of South
Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The project consisted of constructing a Beach Clubhouse
four Villas and other minor structures The new buildings were generally single story
structures In addition associated infrastructure improvements such as the construction of
driveways parking areas and utility lines were required for the project development
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The general contractor for the project was Clever Construction Inc The earthwork
for the project was performed by Kahanakoa Construction Inc Our observation and
testing services during the construction of the project occurred between July 2011 and
March 2012

In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a
Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or
voids contained in the porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been
ripped the ground generally was leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer
working the surface for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After
leveling of the rugged surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were
proof rolled with a Caterpillar 683 vibratory drum roller for about eight passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids

Fill materials used for the project consisted of structural fills and base course
materials Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally smaller
than 6 inches in dimension Structural fills required to raise the grades were generally
placed in level lifts of about 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Base course materials were
generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the imported materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 30 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Compaction test results are
summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction
of the Waikoloa Beach Club project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated May 18 2009 and subsequent
recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
