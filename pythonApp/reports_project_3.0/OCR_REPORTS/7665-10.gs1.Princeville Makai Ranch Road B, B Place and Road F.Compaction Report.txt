This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading, and utility and roadway construction for the 
Princeville Makai Ranch Road “B”, “B” Place and Road “F” project from July 13, 2018 to 
January 10, 2019 in accordance with our revised fee proposal dated January 19, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves the construction of approximately 3,325 linear feet of 
roadway planned to provide access to 19 future home sites at the North Shore Preserve 
development in the Princeville area of the Island of Kauai, Hawaii.  A Project Location 
Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
There are four roadways planned in the northwestern portion of the development, 
identified as Road “B”, Road “B” Driveway, “B” Place and Road “F”.  The roadways will be 
20 to 24 feet wide.  In addition, the project includes new utility installation along the 
roadways.  The roadway grading work involved cuts up to about 15 feet and fills up to 
about 5 feet thick. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading and utility and roadway construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY & ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading and utility and roadway construction work for the 
Princeville Makai Ranch, Road “B”, “B” Place and Road “F” project from July 13, 2018 to 
January 10, 2019. The site grading work consisted of placement and compaction of fills on 
the order of up to about 5 feet thick to achieve the finished grades for the project. 
Earthworks Pacific, Inc. performed the site grading and utility and roadway construction 
work. During the site grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 85 to 90 percent of the maximum dry 
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the 
aggregate base course fill materials were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
density.  In addition, Mirafi 180N filter fabric and Tensar TriAx Grid TX7 were place on the 
roadway finished subgrade. 
During the site grading and utility and roadway construction operations, periodic 
field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D2922 
(Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.8 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations 
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and utility and roadway construction 
work for the above-referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the 
recommendations in the following references.
