INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the
construction of a drainage culvert through the Matson Container Yard at Sand Island from
the Honolulu Harbor shoreline southward to the Sand Island Parkway Road and two 2
branches along the Sand Island Parkway Road The general location of the project site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and engineering recommendations resulting
from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analysis These
GEOLABS HAWAII

recommendations are intended for foundation design input and are not intended to form
the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop an industrial park at the existing Matson Container
Storage Yard at Sand Island on the island of Oahu Hawaii Concrete box drainage
culverts are to be installed as part of the development Starting at the downstream end
the structures begin at the shoreline of the Honolulu Harbor extending southward
approximately 1 500 linear feet to the Sand Island Parkway Road At this point the culvert
continues east and west on the parkway road with a total length of approximately 600
feet including bends
with a total opening of 99 0 square feet The two 2 branches will be single barrelled
culverts constructed of precast units with openings of 52 5 square feet on the easterly
400 foot length and 36 0 square feet on the westerly 200 feet

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on Sand Island and traverses the easterly side of the
existing Matson Container Yard in a fifty 50 foot wide easement between the Honolulu
Harbor and the proposed Sand Island Parkway Road A total length of six hundred 600
feet along the road is also included
The site is generally level except at the harbor shorelines with existing ground
surface elevations ranging between six 6 to ten 10 feet above Mean Lower Low Water
MLLW
At the harbor shore the ground drops abruptly to an elevation of at least thirty 30
feet below MLLW
Most of the ground surface of the site was covered by flexible asphaltic and
concrete pavements A chain link fence marks the east property line of the container yard
adjacent to the Coast Guard Facility along side which the fifty 50 foot wide easement
for the box culvert lies At the time of our exploration large portions of the proposed
drainage easement were being used to temporarily store large shipping containers The

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the seaward fringe of the Honolulu Coastal Plain
The coastal plain is composed of interbedded alluvial marine and post erosional volcanic
deposits Extensive development of the plain and reclamation of lowlying areas has given
the land its present shape
There is evidence that a basaltic flow of the Honolulu Volcanic Series possibly
originating from the Makuku vent in Nuuanu or from Punchbowl reached the eastern
portion of what is now Sand Island During the Waipio stand of the sea at elevation minus
sixty 60 feet this layer of basalt would have provided a resistant surface which could
have diverted the flows of Nuuanu and Kapalama streams to the west The diversion of
these two streams would have resulted in deposition of extensive subaerial sediments
consisting of alluvial clays and silts These exposed alluvial deposits would have become
consolidated due to seasonal moisture changes
Subsequently as the sea level rose to the Waimanalo stand at elevation twenty five
25 feet and then fell to its present level the stream laid alluvium was buried beneath
backreef deposits of sands as a fringing coral reef grew on the seaward margin of the
island
Land reclamation projects during this century have built Sand Island to its current
topography
Subsoil Conditions
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling eleven 11 borings to
depths of between 15 1 feet to 22 9 feet below the existing ground surface and the

collection of subsurface samples The drilling program was accomplished on the 27th
28th and 29th of August 1991 The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by previously placed fill
and alluvial deposits overlying in situ coral formations
The upper layer of the previously placed fill material and the alluvial deposits
generally comprised mixtures of silts clays and coralline sands and gravels with densities
ranging from soft and loose to dense Within the container yard the in situ coral
formations were encountered at approximately fifteen 15 feet below the existing surface
The borings performed along the drainage easement along the Sand Island Access Road
encountered soft and loose saturated sands silts and gravels at a depth of fifteen 15
feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of between 3 7 and
8 5 feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration Based on the
estimated elevations at the boring locations the measured groundwater level appears to
be within one 1 to three 3 feet above MLLW Due to the proximity of the site to the
ocean it is anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate in response to tides and
storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The preliminary plans provided to us indicate that the site grading improvements
will entail cuts and fills of less than three 3 feet in depth to achieve the finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2761 00 Page 7
elevation Excavations of up to about eighteen 18 feet in depth are anticipated to be
required for the placement of the box culverts
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the following recommendations are followed
Site Preparation
The area to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials Existing pavements concrete slabs debris and other unsuitable materials
should be removed from and disposed of properly off site
New pavement subgrades should be scarified and recompacted to a minimum of
95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Loose
or soft areas encountered at the pavement subgrade elevation or in areas to receive fill
should be removed to competent underlying soils or to a depth of two 2 feet below the
subgrade elevation and replaced with select granular material compacted to a minimum
of 95 of the maximum dry density
Excavation
Based upon the information provided to us and upon findings from our field
exploration it is anticipated that excavations of up to a minimum of eighteen 18 feet in
depth below the existing ground surface will be required for the project construction
Excavations for the proposed drainlines are anticipated to be in materials ranging
from localized soft to loose coralline and clay materials to some localized dense coral
formations Due to the possibility that portions of the culverts will be founded on soft or
loose materials it is recommended that a geotechnical engineer observe the excavations
at the invert elevation to determine if the materials encountered at the proposed bearing
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2761 00 Page 8
Surfaces are suitable to support the proposed structures in addition the exposed
bearing surfaces for the culverts should be probed by the excavation equipment backhoe
bucket in the presence of the geotechnical engineer to determine the competence of the
materials encountered
If the materials encountered at the elevation of the bottom of the bedding material
are found to be inadequate to support the proposed structures the area should be over
excavated to competent in situ material or to two 2 feet below the planned bottom of
the bedding whichever is less The resulting over excavation should then be backfilled
with select self compacting granular materials such as 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No
67 gravel
The excavated material may require some processing e g removal or breaking
of fragments greater than three 3 inches in maximum size prior to its use as fill or
backfill
Method of Excavation
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation should be selected by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations Excavation of
the in situ materials utilizing conventional mechanical earthmoving equipment
should not prove to be difficult However localized areas of hard coral formations
and or boulders may be encountered which may require some harder excavation
and the need for ripping and or a hoeram
In order to reduce the potential of ground movement or collapse of the excavated
trench we recommend that the excavated soils be stockpiled at least fifteen 15
feet away from the excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2761 00 Page 9
Excavation Support
Due to the anticipated soft loose soil conditions below the groundwater elevation
it is imperative that adequate shoring be provided in the excavations in order to
reduce the potential of adjacent ground movement We recommend that
continuously interlocking steel sheetpile walls properly braced with horizontal
swales and or struts be used The shoring support should not be removed until
the completion of the placement of the new structures in the excavations
The continuously interlocking steel sheet piling should be driven properly with a
suitable sheetpile hammer to a sufficient depth in order to maintain the stability of
the trench bottom and reduce the possibility of bottom heaving or piping quick
conditions which may be created in the soft loose subsoils by upward flow of
groundwater during dewatering
The excavation shoring system used must comply with all applicable safety
requirements It is recommended that a qualified structural engineer be retained
by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used
The adequacy and safety of the shoring design and installation should be made
the sole responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be
required to be continuously present on the site will have the best opportunity to
promptly observe any changing conditions during construction such as
unforeseen subsoil conditions unexpectedly high groundwater levels unsuitable
construction sequence or techniques etc which may affect the shoring stability

Construction Dewatering
Dewatering of the excavation will be necessary throughout the length of the project
Therefore we recommend that provisions for dewatering be included in the contract
documents for the construction of the proposed culverts
Method of Dewatering
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation dewatering should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
It is Our Opinion that the definition of dewatering in the contract documents should
be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural
groundwater table and to exclude the water from the excavations to permit
construction of the proposed underground structures under safe and dry
conditions These works or systems should therefore include but not be limited
to pumping only
It is required that the method selected for the dewatering process should maintain
the stability of and also provide safe and dry working conditions in the
excavations
It appears that sump pumping inside of the excavations may be used if sheetpile
shoring is driven sufficiently deep to prevent piping and or heaving of the
excavation bottoms
The potential difficulty and impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing
the natural groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2761 00 Page 11
to dewatering It is also recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
Where encountered at the excavation bottoms permeable granular subsoils may
be susceptible to piping and quick conditions Therefore the project dewatering
operations should be performed without pumping out the soil fines bumping clear
water only and should be coordinated with shoring installation so that the
excavation stability is not jeopardized The dewatering operations should be
carried out without creating a quick condition or softening of the excavation
bottom
Fill and Backfill
Fill material should consist of select on site coralline sand and gravel or off site
select granular material with a maximum particle size of three 3 inches and have less
than twenty 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Material from the excavation not
meeting these requirements should be appropriately processed or disposed of properly
off site Fill material within two 2 feet of finished subgrade level in pavement areas
should consist of material meeting the requirements of select borrow
Fill and backfill materials above the water level should be placed in maximum ten
10 inch loose lift thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Self compacting materials such as 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
should be used for fill and backfill below the water level
The structures should be placed and backfilled in conformance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction for the City County of Honolulu

Culvert Structure
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for the structures resting
on the properly prepared subgrade To provide uniform support a minimum of twelve
12 inches of granular bedding material should be placed under the structures
It is anticipated that wing walls will be constructed at the outlet end of the box
culverts The allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is also appropriate for these features
as is the requirement for a twelve 12 inch thick bedding of granular material
For lateral earth pressure design the following equivalent fluid pressures are
recommended
a Unrestrained condition level backfill 35 p c f
b Unrestrained condition 2 1 sloping backfill 55 p cif
on Restrained condition 55 p c f
Drainage
The finished grade outside the pavements should be shaped to shed water away
from the roadway and to avoid ponding Excessive landscape watering near pavement
areas should also be avoided
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading culvert and roadway construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2761 00 Page 13
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations

The project comprises installation of a drainage culvert from the north shore of

Backiill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least twelve 12
inches above the top of the pipe The first zone should consist of selected free draining
well graded granular material placed by hand or by suitable equipment so that the backfill
material is in contact with the entire periphery of the upper half of the pipe and in such
a manner as not to disturb the pipe The granular material is recommended to provide
high density backfill with minimum compactive effort Compaction of this portion of the
backfill by ponding and jetting may be permitted provided free draining conditions can be
attained The material should be well graded less than three 3 inches in size and with
no more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve
Most of the excavated on site soils can be re used as the upper zone of the trench
backfill from above twelve 12 inches over the pipe to within two 2 feet of the finished
grade except in areas to be paved Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 Below areas to be paved the backfill within two 2 feet of the
finished grade should consist of select borrow compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density as determined in accordance ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
