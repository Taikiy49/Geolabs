SUMMARY REPORT
SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
KAHANAHOU CIRCLE AND WAILELE ROAD 8 INCH WATER MAIN
In accordance with your request a soil resistivity survey was conducted for the
proposed new 8 inch water main along Kahanahou Circle and Wailele Road on the
windward side of Oahu Hawaii The sites are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
May 26 1993 This report presents the results of our soil resistivity survey and
summarizes our work on this project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located on the north side of Kaneohe within a
developed housing subdivision adjacent to Kaneohe Bay on the windward side of Oahu
as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 It is proposed to install approximately 3 100
lineal feet of new water mains and appurtenances beneath all of Kahanahou Circle and
under a portion of Wailele Road It is our understanding that ductile iron and or polyviny
chloride PVC pipes will be utilized for the planned water lines at the subject area of the

In general the average depth of the proposed water lines is anticipated to be

Wenner 4 Pin Method may be used to evaluate the corrosion rating of the near surface
soils within the levels of the proposed water lines Therefore the average resistivity of the
soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevation of
the water lines was assessed by this method at the road stations listed on Plates 4 1
through 4 4
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was in general conformance
with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and subsequent
amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the following work
efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site
2 Performance of 72 in situ soil resistivity surveys at 24 locations along
Kahanahou Circle and Wailele Road between William Henry and Waikapoki
Roads These stations generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing
maximum required in the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991
3 Reduction and analyses of the data pertaining to the corrosion
characteristics of soils at the site

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil resistance
in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity Ohm cm which is
directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated
soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion rating are
presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until the subsurface conditions are exposed If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in the report
Elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate having been scaled
from the construction plans prepared by R M Towill Corporation dated January 18th All
elevations were rounded to the nearest 4 foot to emphasize that they may not be
