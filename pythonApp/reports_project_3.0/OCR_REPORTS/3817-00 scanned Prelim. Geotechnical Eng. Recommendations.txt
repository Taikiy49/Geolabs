Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Queen Kaahumanu Highway Widening
North Kona Hawaii
This letter report presents the preliminary results and recommendations derived
from our geotechnical engineering exploration performed for the Queen Kaahumanu
Highway Widening project located in the District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii
Our findings are based on research of available information in the vicinity of the project
site our field exploration and engineering analyses A formal report summarizing our
work on the project will be submitted at a later date upon finalization of the proposed
alignment and completion of our office engineering work
Project Considerations
Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to widen the
existing Queen Kaahumanu Highway between Kailua and Keahole Airport on the island
of Hawaii The project will begin in the vicinity of the intersection of Queen Kaahumanu
Highway with Henry Street and terminate in the vicinity of the Keahole Airport Access
Road intersection covering approximately 9 miles of roadway
The project will involve widening the existing two lane roadway to a four lane
highway with the potential for future widening to a six lane highway We understand that
drainage facilities and utility installations are also planned for the subject project Based
on the existing site conditions we envision that site grading will generally consist of cuts
and fills up to about 20 feet in height In addition we understand that a retaining wall of

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling 20 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 20 extending to depths
ranging from about 20 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface A tabulation of the
borings with their corresponding station numbers offset from centerline and approximate
surface elevations are summarized on Table 1 Approximate Boring Locations
Based on our field exploration most of the project site is generally covered by
surficial clinkers loose rock fragments down to about 4 feet below the existing ground
surface The clinkers are generally underlain by slightly to moderately weathered
medium hard to hard basalt formation extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 25 feet below the existing ground surface
Many of the drilled borings indicated that the basalt formation was generally
interbedded with thin clinker layers In addition some voids of up to about 3 feet in
vertical height were also encountered in the borings We also observed signs of several
near surface lava tube collapses in the vicinity of the project alignment It should be
noted that lava tubes are quite common in the basalt formations in the Kona area
Therefore the potential for presence of lava tubes should be expected along portions of
this project
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings at the time of our exploration It
should be noted that water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
Earthwork
Based on the existing terrain at the subject site and the grading plans provided we
anticipate that site grading work for the proposed roadway construction will generally
consist of cuts and fills on the order of about 20 feet or less in vertical height
In general all areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork In areas to receive fill or finished
subgrades in cuts the subgrade should be proof rolled with heavy equipment such as

If the proof rolling operations detect a near surface cavity or loose zone the area
should be ripped open cleaned of debris and then backfilled with compacted fill
material In general the cavity should be backfilled with compacted general fill material
up to within 3 feet of the finished subgrade elevation Therefore we recommend that
contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill
quantities
General fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials with a
maximum dimension of 12 inches The on site excavated material may be re used as a
source of general fill materials provided that the materials are processed to less than
42 inches in maximum dimension The upper 3 feet of all fill areas below the pavement
subgrade elevation bottom of the pavement structural section should be capped with
structural fill materials Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular
materials with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or more
General fill materials up to 3 feet below the finish subgrade elevation should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
42 inches loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Fill
materials within 3 feet of the finish grade including structural fills should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
42 inches loose thickness and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Materials for the pavement structural section such as the aggregate subbase and
aggregate base materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches loose thickness
We understand that substantial amounts of import materials will be required for this
project Therefore import materials should meet the requirements for general and
structural fills recommended above

Retaining Wall Foundations
As previously indicated substantial retaining wall is planned along the mauka side
of Queen Kaahumanu Highway fronting the existing Wal Mart store A conventional
concrete retaining wall up to about 28 feet high is currently planned for this retaining
structure Based on our field exploration we recommend that the retaining wall
foundations bear on the basalt formation encountered at this location We understand
that this project will be designed in general accordance with the Load and Resistance
Factor Design LRFD methods
For foundations bearing on the basalt formation an ultimate bearing pressure of
40 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used to evaluate the foundations subjected
to an extreme event limit state The extreme event limit state of the foundations is
generally due to unique occurrences such as seismic loading conditions To evaluate
the strength limit state of the foundations a bearing pressure of up to 24 000 p s f may
be used based on a resistance factor of 0 6 For the service limit state a bearing
pressure of 12 000 p s f may be used We recommend that the foundations be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Lateral loads acting on the retaining structure may be resisted by frictional
resistance between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive
earth pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
foundations For foundations bearing directly on basalt rock formation a coefficient of
friction of 0 6 and 0 75 may be used for the strength limit state and extreme event limit
state respectively Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 450 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
foundations embedded in the basalt rock or compacted fill materials Unless covered

Foundation Probing
As previously indicated voids and or cavities are common in the basalt formation
at the site based on our field exploration and observations In order to reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of a void below the
retaining wall foundation we recommend that a program of cavity probing be required
during construction Probes should be drilled at 8 foot intervals center to center along
the length of the retaining wall The probes are generally 3 inches in diameter and should
be drilled to a depth of at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation elevation
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation
additional probes should be drilled at closer spacing to help delineate the vertical and
lateral extent of the cavity and or void
The probes are generally backfilled to aid in the detection of cavities Probes and
any cavities discovered should be filled with sand cement grout injected at low to
moderate pump pressures i e the pressure available from a grout pump A typical
sand cement grout should have a slump of about 6 to 9 inches
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would serve to monitor the presence of voids
and to allow additional recommendations to be made if changed conditions occur and or if
large voids are encountered
