This report presents the results of our boring exploration and
percolation tests performed at the Auto Center for the Atlas Pacific
construction site at 735 Dillingham Boulevard in order to provide
subsurface data for use in the design of a leaching field for surface
run off The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A previous investigation report entitled Geotechnical
Investigation Honolulu Nissan Honolulu
Boulevard Honolulu Oahu Hawaii for the foundation and site grading
design aspects of the project had been submitted on August 26 1986
Subsequently it was decided to utilize two leakage leaching field
systems along Drainlines C and E to permit disposal of surface
run off into the underlying permeable zones in lieu of the originally
planned drainline system
The purpose of the proposed percolation tests is to provide
subsurface soil data and to evaluate the permeability of the underlying
sandy soils at the two drainage manhole inlet locations The test
locations were selected after our meeting with the Civil Engineer Mr
David Imata on
It is our understanding that each leakage leaching field system
would primarily consist of a length of 15 inch diameter perforated pipe
connected to several drainage manholes and buried at a depth varying from
5 to 12 feet below finished ground surface At the deepest end of the
system a leaching area beneath the pipe would be overexcavated into the
permeable zone and then backfilled with gravel to facilitate disposal of
the run off
For the design and feasibility of the leaching field construction
the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the project site were
studied by drilling borings at three locations at the site The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
At the present time the project site is under construction
Concrete placement for footing foundations in the Service Building and a
majority of the footing foundations in the Chrysler Show Area had been
completed Footing excavations in the Nissan Show Area and Service
Entrance Building were being performed
The topography at the site is generally flat and percolation test
locations are at elevations close to the finished subgrade level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Continuous sampling technique was used in both Borings A and B from
3 feet to the bottom of borings i e 18 and 19 feet below the existing
ground surface respectively Boring A encountered loose to very loose
silty sand with gravels from approximately 3 to 5 feet Very soft gray
silty clay to clayey silt was encountered from about 5 to 8 feet Very
loose silty fine sand with traces of wood and gravel was encountered from
8 to 18 feet
Boring B encountered medium dense to loose tan coral sand and
gravels from 3 to 6 feet Very soft gray clayey silt to very loose silty
sand was encountered from 6 to 12 5 feet A thin layer of white coral
gravel with silty sand was encountered from 8 5 to 9 feet Very loose to
medium dense clayey gravel and silty fine sand were encountered from 12 5
to 19 feet

PERCOLATION TESTS
Percolation tests were performed in Borings A and B in order to
evaluate the in situ soil permeability After completion of drilling
the soils beneath the groundwater level caved in Therefore the borings
were cased with 4 inch I D casing using wash boring The holes were
cleaned by flushing with water before 2 5 inch I D PVC pipes were
inserted in the borings for the percolation tests The constant head
percolation test procedure was used for the tests and the data are
presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
From the test data the calculated average soil permeabilities in
Borings A and B are 1 2 x 10 and 1 3 x 1072 cn s respectively
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field percolation test results it appears that the
percolation rate of the soil at the manhole site for Drainline E
Boring A is poor to fair while the manhole site for Drainline c
Boring B is fair to good This is further confirmed by visual
examination of the soils encountered in the borings
