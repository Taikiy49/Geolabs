1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the FY05 PN57406 project to construct a road from Schofield
Barracks to the Helemano Military Reservation in the Wahiawa and Waialua areas on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses for the project These preliminary recommendations are intended
for planning and the preliminary design of bridge foundations retaining structures site
grading and pavements only The findings and preliminary recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned
with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene
Epoch The dips of the lava beds generally range from 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows
on the plateau have weathered to depths of 15 to 30 meters and are characterized by
the red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such as Waikakalaua Kipapa and
Kaukonahua are a result of this weathering The brown and reddish brown soils
generally consist of ancient alluvial deposits overlying the weathered rock

3 1 Bridge Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at Crossing No 2 we
recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous
footings may be used to support the bridge structure Based on the available plans the
footing on the south side of the bridge will bear on embankment fills and the footing on
the north side of the bridge will bear on stiff saprolitic soils
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 150 kPa may be used for design of
footings This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind and or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 600 mm below the lowest
adjacent finished grade For sloping ground conditions the footing should extend
deeper to obtain a minimum set back distance measured horizontally from
the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope
The soils encountered at the bottom of footing elevation should be compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction If soft loose and or yielding areas are
encountered at the bottom of footing elevation the areas should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm or dense soils and backfilled with compacted select granular
fill material
Foundations that are located next to utility trenches and or easements should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench Alternatively the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging

3 7 Underground Utilities
We envision that new underground utilities will be required for this project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that granular bedding consisting
of 150 mm of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The initial backfill up to about 300 mm above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfill material such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for
damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining
granular material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 300 mm above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils having
particles less than 75 mm in largest dimension or select granular material The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in about
200 mm loose lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Where trenches are located in paved areas the upper 1 meter of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX B

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Ten Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of the test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Two Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the undrained shear strength characteristics Results of
the test are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
Two Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear tests
are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
Fourteen laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils Results of the tests are presented on Plates B 6 through B 19
Four Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the
near surface soils to determine the dry density and moisture content relationship Results
of the tests are presented on Plates B 20 through B 23
