As requested, we performed Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing on Shafts 1.1
and 1.3 installed for the above project. Based on our field observations and the CSL test
results, the following recommendations are for your review and consideration.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project specification, Section 511 — Drilled Shafts, requires performing CSL
testing on Shafts 1.1 and 1.3 installed for the project. We performed the CSL testing on
April 13, 2020 for Shafts 1.1 and 1.3 installed for the above project.
Shaft 1.1 was drilled from March 2 to March 3, 2020, followed by concrete
placement on March 3, 2020. Shaft 1.3 was drilled from March 4 to March 5, 2020,
followed by concrete placement on March 5, 2020. The approximate locations of the shafts
are presented on the Site Plan, Plate 1. Four 2-inch diameter steel CSL access tubes
were installed along with the reinforcing cage. Details of the drilled shaft as-built conditions
are summarized in the following table:

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest. CSL tests were performed
between all the possible access tube combinations in the drilled shaft (4 tubes resulting in
6 profiles). The northernmost access tube was designated as Tube 1 and subsequent
tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction.
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 2.7 and (4.1
feet) above the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for Shafts 1.1
and 1.3, respectively. We understand that potable water was used to fill the access tubes
after concrete placement. Based on our observation in the field, the water levels within the
access tubes were above the top of shaft concrete in all access tubes at the time of
testing.
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. The
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known
accurately.
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition are presented on
Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on
Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile)
and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.4.
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were
generally less than 10%, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less
than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for Shafts 1.1 and
1.3 with the exception of the areas within the profile as described below. It should be noted
that changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the
degree of the reduction and the associated increase in FAT.
) Shaft 1.1 (Profile 1-2): FAT increases up to 11.3 and 249.3 percent from
approximately 37.1 to 37.9 feet and 0.1 to 2.1 feet below the top of concrete,
respectively.
° Shaft 1.1 (Profile 1-3): FAT increases up to 149.8 percent from
approximately 0.1 to 1.5 feet below the top of concrete.
° Shaft 1.1 (Profile 2-3): FAT increases up to 152.1 percent from
approximately 0.2 to 1.4 feet below the top of concrete, respectively.
° Shaft 1.1 (Profile 2-4): FAT increases up to 13.0 and 11.8 percent from
approximately 6.1 to 7.0 feet and 2.7 to 3.2 feet below the top of concrete,
respectively.
e Shaft 1.3 (All Profiles): FAT increases up to 298.8 percent from
approximately 0.0-2.4 feet below the top of concrete.
° Shaft 1.3 (Profile 2-3): FAT increases up to 12.3 percent from approximately
4.5 to 5.6 feet below the top of concrete.
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for
Shafts 1.1 and 1.3 ranged from approximately 8,073 to 16,602 and 7,460 to 14,994 feet
per second, respectively. Based on our review of the CSL testing data, we recommend
