The new Radiation Therapy Unit will be an addition at the northern side of the
existing Kona Community Hospital The new structure will be a single story structure
approximately 58 feet by 92 feet in dimensions The probing and grouting work was
performed by others Our services are for probing and grouting observations only in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated Geolabs is not
responsible for reviewing or assessing the adequacy of the current foundation design
for the new building
Probing and Grouting Operations
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation is to reduce the potential for
loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose
zones below the main building
The probing and grouting were performed on along the wall
column footing locations designated in the project structural plans The locations and
depths of the probeholes were marked and determined by Mr Mike Mearman of Quality
Builders Inc the building contractor In general the probeholes at the new building

site were drilled to depths ranging from about 4 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface The site was roughly excavated and graded prior to the probing work During
the probing work the existing ground surface elevation was close to the bottom of
footing elevation The required probing depth is 5 feet below the planned bottom of
footing The approximate locations of the probeholes are shown on the Probehole
Location Plan Plate 1
The probeholes approximately 8 inches in diameter were drilled by DeLima
Drilling Inc using a truck mounted tandem drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers A total of 31 probeholes were drilled for the project Materials encountered
consisted of surficial base course fill materials overlying basalt rock formation in varying
degrees of weathering massive to slightly jointed with laminated clayey silt with gravel
layers In general no significant voids and or cavities were observed during the probing
operation
Following the probing operation the probeholes were backfilled with controlled
low slump mix concrete injected at low pressures The grout materials were supplied by
West Hawaii Concrete The grouting chute was aligned into the open hole and grout
mix was applied from the bottom until the probehole was filled to the top The grouting
operation and the approximate grout intake of each probehole were observed and
recorded by our field representative Approximately 3 cubic yards of flowable fill was
utilized during the grouting operation to fill up the 31 probeholes No significant cavities
or voids were observed during the grouting operation The probing and grouting
summary is provided on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Limitations
The elevations and depths of the probeholes were provided by Quality
Builders Inc The elevations of the probeholes should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
