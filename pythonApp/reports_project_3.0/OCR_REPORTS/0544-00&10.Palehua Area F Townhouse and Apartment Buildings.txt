SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is suitable for the proposed development The
foundations may be designed to bear upon the natural soil
or upon compacted fill for maximum bearing pressures
of 3 000 pounds per square foot Foundations for the
apartment building should extend to the rock surface
where they may be designed for maximum contact bearing
pressures of 8 000 pounds per square foot
Expansive soils were not encountered and Slab on grade
construction can be utilized
Properly compacted on site soils will provide a CBR
value of ten when utilized for subgrade support
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface investigation
at the subject parcel and provides recommendations for the design
and construction of the foundations to Support the proposed structures
and for the ancilliary earthwork
W O
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to devélop the 9 3 acre site for townhouse
and apartment units The preliminary plans indicate 14 townhouses
and a three story apartment in the lower southwestern corner of the
property
It is anticipated that the units will be constructed at or near grade
will maximum cuts and fills of less than ten feet With the excep
tion of the three story apartment structure the remaining structures
will generally be lightly loaded slab on grade or post and beam
construction
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site is on the southern tip of the Waianae Volcanic Range and
the ground surface slopes towards the Hononliuli Plain The project
area is bound by Awanui Gulch on the west and Makakilo Gulch on the
east
The proposed site is on a south facing slope The grass and bush
covered ground surface drops at approximately a 6 slope from north
to south The site relief varies from approximately elevation 905
at the northeastern corner to elevation 830 along the southern pro
perty line At the southern portion of the western property line
the ground surface drops steeply into the ravine just west of the
site
W O
The ground surface elevations utilized in this report are based upon
the ground surface contours shown on an untitled site plan provided
to facilitate our investigation
Geology
As previously mentioned the site is situated upon the southern tip
of the Waianae Volcanic Range which is a relatively old formation
Greater erosion has occured in this area producing more gentle and
rounded slopes
This type of formation is generally composed of residual soil result
ing from the in place weathering of the underlying basalt The ba
salt deposited as lava flows is seldom uniform This erratic de
position can result in differential weathering and a variable rock
surface
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing 30 test
borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan and Boring Loca
tions Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration
methods and the accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appen
dix along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
The explorations disclosed that the site is generally blanketed by
a layer of residual soil composed of silt and Clayey silt underlain
by decomposed basalt Some areas of hard unweathered basalt or ba
salt boulders were encountered
W O
With the exception of Boring 21 the upper materials were predominately
very stiff to hard in consistency In Boring 21 a layer of medium to
stiff soil was encountered at a depth of 6 to 9 feet below the exist
ing ground surface The findings of the subsurface explorations are
summarized on Pages 5 and 6 along with the proposed finish floor
elevations
DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
With the exception of Building 11 the three story apartment structure
the remaining townhouse units are of frame construction and are sup
ported upon line or spread foundations bearing near the existing
ground surface The foundation bearing pressures are low which re
sults in small settlements
In some areas fills up to eight feet in thickness will be required
to obtain the proposed finish floor levels Due to the light loads
the foundations of the proposed structures can be supported upon pro
perly compacted fills
The foundations should bear a minimum of 12 inches below the proposed
finish grade upon the natural soils or upon compacted fill These
materials will provide an allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds
per square foot with estimated settlements of 1 inch total and inch
differential

For the apartment structure where slab on grade foundations will be
utilized with block walls the building is more sensitive to differ
ential movements The foundations should bear on similar materials
and it is recommendated that all foundations be extended to bear upon
the rock encounterd at approximately elevation 845 Foundation bear
ing upon the rock can be designed for maximum contact bearing pressures
of 8 000 pounds per square foot with estimated settlements of less than
4 inch
The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to the placement
of the concrete to assure that the bearing conditions are consistent
with the findings of the subsurface explorations and to inspect for
possible isolated pockets of expansive soils
Retaining Walls
It appears that low retaining walls will be utilized throughout this
site as part of the uphill foundations These walls should be designed
for an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pcf if the on site soils are
utilized as backfill The backfill should be compacted to between
90 to 95 of the maximum dry density established by American Society
for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T A fric
tional factor of 0 55 should be utilized to determine the sliding re
sistance of the retaining wall foundations
Since the walls will be utilized as an interior surface for the struc
tures it is recommended that subsurface drains be incorporated to pre
vent the buildup of hydrostatic pressures during periods of high perci
pitation
W 1974
Expansive soils were not encountered during the subsurface explorations
As shown in the appendix five samples of the near surface soils were
tested for expansive potential The resulting swells varied from 0 0
to a maximum of 1 1
Slab on grade construction can be utilized without special provisions
for expansive soils It is recommended that the subgrade be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as previously specified
This will provide relatively uniform slab support
Slab on grade to be provided with floor coverings should be protected
by a plastic vapor barrier The plastic should be covered by a thin
layer of sand for protection and to avoid punctures Groundwater was
not encountered within ten feet of the surface and it is not neces
sary to install a granular base beneath the slab to eliminate capilliary
moisture
Earthwork Grading
The proposed site grading entails shallow cuts and fills with the
structures bearing at or near the existing ground surface It appears
that the excavations will be within the upper soil mantle except for
Building 11 In this area the foundation excavations to obtain the
proposed finish floor level will penetrate the upper portion of the
basalt
W O
Isolated boulders and areas of unweathered basalt can be expected
throughout the site and may be difficult to excavate where they are
encountered within confined excavations such as foundation or utility
trenches Blasting may be required to remove these materials Bor
13 and 25 encountered rock which would require blasting
In Borings 13 and 25 the rock was below the proposed excavation
level
Portions of the proposed fill will be utilized to support the founda
tions and floor slabs of the dwellings This fill should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 72T Should on site soils be utilized the maxi
mum density is approximately 100 pounds per cubic foot at an optimum
moisture content of 23 The samples obtained from the test borings
had natural moisture contents generally exceeding 23 If the on
site soils are utilized as fill they will require some drying to
bring them to near optimum moisture content
Where fill will be placed upon existing slopes exceeding five hori
zontal to one vertical 5 to 1 the ground surface should be keyed
and benched to properly bond the newly placed fill to the existing
soil
Prior to the placement of any fill all organic and deleterious ma
terials should be removed and the upper six inches of the existing
ground surface scarified The scarified surface should then be
W O
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to the recom
mended density Additional fill should be placed in horizontal lifts
and compacted to the specified density
Where fill will be utilized for structural support or behind retain
ing walls the compaction operation should be inspected on full time
basis to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is consis
tently achieved
Cuts within the natural soil should be made no steeper than one hori
zontal to one vertical 1 to 1 The fill slopes should not exceed
two horizontal to one vertical 2 to 1 The residual soils can be
cut at near vertical slopes for short term construction conditions
Paving
During the subsurface explorations samples of the surface soils
were obtained and a CBR test performed The resulting CBR value of
10 represents a medium subgrade Based upon the City County of
Honolulu Design Standard for flexible pavements the required pave
ment section for a residential subdivision would be as follows
2 Asphalt Concrete
6 Base Course
_6 Select Borrow
14 Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section 31 2 Standard Specifi
cations for Public Works Construction or equivalent The selected
W O
borrow subbase should be crusher run wastes mudrock coral sand or
cinders conforming to Section 30 of the above specifications
Construction Monitoring
Foundations excavations should be inspected prior to concrete place
ment to assure that subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
The earthwork should be inspected on a full time basis if the fill
will be utilized for structural support to assure that the recommended
degree of compaction is consistently achieved

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 30
borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the bor
ings varied from 5 5 to 14 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan and
Boring Locations Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in the
field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accord
ance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plate A 1 through A 27
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D or a
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture density determinations and Atterberg Limits were made on
selected samples to aid in the classification of the soils The
results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample location
To provide data to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
encountered soils four direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples The test results are shown on Plates A 28 through A 31
An additional direct shear test was performed on a compacted fill
sample to measure the strength of the proposed fill The results
from this test is given on Plate A 32 All direct shear tests were
on saturated samples
A consolidation test was also performed on a selected sample from
Boring 21 and on a compacted sample of the surface soil to measure
the soil response under various vertical loadings The consolida
tion test results are graphically presented on Plates A 33 and A 34
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test and a modified procter com
paction test were performed on a surface sample of the on site soil
to evaluate the subitability of the soil as fill and pavement support
The results are given on Plate A 35

Gentlemen
This letter summarizes our recent consultations and supplementary
recommendations to our report of for the subject
project
In our telephone conversation with
of Muroda Associates Inc we reviewed the recommended pavement
section and it was concluded that the pavement section recommended
in our report 2 inches asphaltic concrete 6 inches base course
and 6 inches select borrow should be utilized for the roadways
A reduced pavement section consisting of 2 inches of asphaltic con
crete underlain by 4 inches of base course could be utilized for
the parking sections which would not be subjected to heavy traffic
It was also recommended that positive drainage be incorporated into
the base course to prevent the accumulation of water and saturation
of the subgrade
In a telephone conversation of October 9 we discussed with Mr Felix
R Sarupu of Richard R Bradshaw Consulting Engineers of Hawaii Inc
our foundation recommendations for the apartment structure For this
building it is recommended that all foundations be extended to bear
upon rock which was encountered at approximately elevation 845
Foundations bearing upon rock can be designed for maximum contact
bearing pressures of 8 000 pounds per square foot The estimated
settlements are 1 2 inch total and 1 4 inch differential
W O 544 00 Page 1974
The estimated settlements of 1 inch total and 1 2 differential is
the most severe foundation condition with one end of a townhouse
unit supported at or near the rock surface and the other end sup
ported upon more than four feet of fill The differential settle
ments can be minimized by stringent construction procedures or by
lowering all foundations to bear upon similar materials It appears
that the proposed structures will be sufficiently flexible to with
stand this 1 2 inch differential settlement which would occur over
a length of approximately 150 feet
It was also recommended that the allowable foundation loadings for
both the apartment structure and the townhouse units be increased
by 1 3 when considering short term wind and seizmic loadings
Where the fill exceeds four feet in height or where it will be uti
lized for structural support the compaction operations should be in
spected on a full time basis to assure that adequate compaction is
consistently achieved

please do not hesitate to contact us

made at the subject building pads parking and roadway areas This report
summarizes our inspections and testings As of to date all the building

tion test results for the in placed fills are enclosed in the Compaction
Test Summary
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were
made in accordance with the American Society for Testing and Materials
ASTM Test D 1556 Sand Cone Method During the course of fill construc
tion various types of on site soils were utilized These materials were
tested prior to accepting them as fill materials In each of the parking
subgrade additional samples were brought back to our laboratory for CBR
7 Swell testings The subgrade soil generally indicated higher CBR value
