PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is at 641 Keeaumoku Street in the Ala Moana area of Honolulu, on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site consists of two parcels (TMK No.: [1] 2-3-021: 037 
and 039) bounded approximately by Keeaumoku Street to the west, Makaloa Street to the 
south, an existing golf academy facility to the east, and the Samsung Plaza to the north. The 
general contractor for the project was Albert C. Kobayashi, Inc. and the ACIP pile installation 
subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company (HDCC). 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, we 
recommended supporting the new high-rise condominium and parking structure on a deep 
foundation system consisting of 18- and 24-inch ACIP piles with an allowable compressive 
load capacity of up to 1,200 kips per pile. Most of the ACIP piles would extend through the 
soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits (near the surface) and derive support primarily from skin 
friction and end-bearing within coral ledges and/or coralline detritus materials encountered 
below the lagoonal deposit. The ACIP pile length of up to about 105 feet was utilized for the 
1,200-kip capacity piles. The following table summarizes the ACIP pile foundation lengths 
utilized for the project.

BI-DIRECTIONAL METHOD LOAD TEST 
As part of the project specifications, two sacrificial load test piles, designated as LT-1 
and LT-2, were installed at the approximate locations indicated on the ACIP Pile 
Identification Plan, Plate 1.  LT-1 and LT-2 consisted of 18- and 24-inch diameter ACIP piles, 
respectively. The load test piles were installed by HDCC between April 15 and 
April 17, 2019. The load test piles were installed with a bi-directional load cell and 
embedment strain gage instrumentation. 
A drill rig with a continuous hollow stem auger was utilized to install the ACIP test 
piles. A hydraulic concrete pump was used to pump the grout into the pile system. The 18- 
and 24-inch diameter load test piles were extended to depths of about 100 and 120 feet 
below the existing ground surface, respectively.

After the load test piles were installed and cured for about 7 days, Applied Foundation 
Testing performed the load tests on April 23 and 24, 2019. The load test piles were loaded 
in general accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load Test Method to a maximum sustained 
bi-directional load of approximately 1,948 and 4,212 kips for LT-1 and LT-2, respectively. 
Complete details of the load test program are presented in the Bi-Directional Static Load 
Test Report, dated April 30, 2019, submitted by Applied Foundation Testing. 
Based on the results of the load tests conducted, we recommended utilizing the ACIP 
pile lengths shown in the above table for the production ACIP piles to support the allowable 
compressive loads (Design Capacities). 
PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of installing 274 ACIP piles 
(including six tower crane piles), was conducted from June 5, 2019 through July 25, 2019 
under the near-continuous (full-time) observations by field engineers from our office. 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company utilized a drill rig with continuous hollow stem 
augers to install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the 
drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, grout 
pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, which 
included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout volume 
used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed are shown 
on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, Plate 1. The production ACIP pile installation summary 
is presented on Plates 2.1 through 2.15 of this letter report. 
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction.  The associated problems and the corresponding resolutions are presented in 
the “Remarks” column of the ACIP Pile Installation Summary. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been installed 
to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the allowable design load capacities.
