INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of a preliminary subsurface
investigation at the subject site and provides recommendations
for the design and construction of the proposed grading construc
tion This report is not for building foundation construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the south west corner of the inter
section of Ahuimanu Road and Kahekili Highway in Kahaluu Oahu
Hawaii
AHUIMANU NEIGHBORHOOD CENTER 2
The 4 3 acre site ts trtangular tn shape and bounded by Ahuimanu
Road Ahutmanu Stream and Kahekil Highway
An existing knoll occupies about one third of the site at the
northern portion between the elevation from 36 up to about 52
Recent man made embankment fill butts against the higher area above
and occuptes the central portion of the site The top of the em
bankment has been graded to a smooth level pad at about elevation
38 The present lowlying area is located between elevation 28 and
34 feet at the western and southern sections
A ditch is formed by the fill banks between the embankments from the
site fill and the Kahekili Highway along the east boundary A dirt
road extends from Ahuimanu Road and runs along the west bank of the
knoll land to the recent man made embankment At the time of this
investigation truck loads of off site fill were stockpiled on top
of the man made embankment
Dense California grass few mango and guava trees scattered over
the lowlying and upper knoll areas No vegetation is on the man
made embankment at the central portion
At the time of the field exploration no surface water was noted
at the lower area
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The antictpated construction will consist of cutting down the upper
knoll to elevation 35 feet and filling up the lowlying area with
about 6 to 7 feet of fill

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed development site is located adjacent to the Ahuimanu
Stream The lowlying portion of the site below elevation 36 is
underlain with thick alluvial stream deposit Based on a review
of the topographic map of the area it seems that the area below
elevation 36 ts within the flood plain area which is underlain
with soft compressible material
The proposed fill within the flood plain area will initiate con
solidation of the underlying deposits Based on the limited boring
data at this time the compressible layer exceeds 40 feet thick
Therefore future land utilization should be coordinated and planned
with the anticipated settlement in the lowlying area
Because of the tremendous thickness of the compressible layer
removal of this soft material and replaced with selected fill is
unfeasible Our recommendation is to start filling up the low
lying area as soon as possible and to place a 5 foot surcharge
over the fill This will help to shorten the consolidation
waiting period
We anticipate that the site will settle a minimum of about a foot
or more depending on the actual thickness of the underlying soft
stream deposits To more accurately survey the ground settlement
AHUIMANU NEIGHBORHOOD CENTER 5
settlement gauges should be installed on the original ground
prior to the site grading and surcharge operations Based on
the results of the settlement gauge reading we will be able to
monttor when settlement rate has come to an acceptable level
prior to utility and surface improvements
Site Preparation
At the lowlying area the site should be cleared of the surface
vegetatton and the highly organic surface materials It is esti
mated that the undercutting of the organic materials will average
approximately six inches over fill portions of the site
The existing upper crust at the lowlying area ts relatively thin
generally 3 to 4 feet and this layer does not have sufficient
strength to support heavy construction equipment For this reason
it is recommended that light equipment be utilized for the initial
site clearing
It appears more expedient to delay the clearing and grubbing of
the area beneath the existing embankment at the central portion
until the completion of surface material removal in the lower area
The embankment material can then be transferred on to the lower
area to permit the grass and other vegetation to be removed
If posstble the construction operations should be conducted during
the dryer summer months when the soil crust has its greater strength
Extended periods of percipitation that occurs in this vicinity during
the rainy season can soften the soil crust until it will no longer
support even lightly loaded equipment
AHUIMANU NEIGHBORHOOD CENTER 6
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It ts recommended that three settlement gauges be installed at
this site to monitor the ground settlements The surveying of
the settlement gauges can be performed by the project surveyor
in cooperation with the civil engineer
The fill operations can be started upon completion of the settle
ment gauge installation It appears that the existing embankment
material should be first utilized to fill up the existing lowlying
area This material should be placed in maximum 6 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by the American Society for Testing and Material ASTM Test Desig
nation D1557
It is estimated that the fill placement in the lowlying area should
proceed at a rate of no more than 2 feet per week The actual rate
will be established during the construction operation by evaluating
the settlement gauges readings along with the visual inspection of
the ftll embankment stability
After the stockpile and on site cuts are utilized additional off
site borrow will be required The material should be non expansive
and should not contain large percentage of boulders organic or
deletertous materials
Settlements
Based upon our laboratory testing of the soil samples obtained from
Borings 2 and 3 it is estimated that the primary consolidation at
the site under the proposed fill loadings will be approximately
AHUIMANU NEIGHBORHOOD CENTER 7
15 inches or more Additional 3 or more inches of settlement
would come from long term secondary consolidation
It ts estimated that for 6 foot fill approximately 8 to 12
months or more will be required to obtain a 90 consolidation
level The remaining consolidatton would occur over an extended
period of several years
Settlement will be negligible at the upper cut area
Surcharging
To minimize future long term and differential settlements addi
ttonal surcharge fill ts recommended over the proposed fill under
lain with soft stream alluvial deposit This surcharge area will
generally occupy the lowlying area located below elevation 34 and
as outlined on the Site Plan
If a total of 5 feet of surcharge fill were placed sufficient
consolidation would occur in a shorter period of time
Settlement gauges should be placed to monitor the rate and amount
of consolidation
The information obtained from the settlement gauge monitoring pro
gram will establish when the surcharge fill can be removed after
sufficient pre consolidation has taken place The additional fill
may then be removed and construction of the site roadway and utility
improvements can then begin
AHUIMANU NEIGHBORHOOD CENTER 8
Slopes
The stability of the perimeter slopes ts a direct function of
the magnitude of pore water pressures within the underlying
sotis It is estimated from our laboratory testing that the
construction rate of approximately two feet per week will not
develop excessive pore water pressures and will maintain a factor
of safety of 1 5 against slope failure This factor of safety
will increase with the disstpatton of the pore water pressures
Utilities
Utilities construction tn the soft area should be delayed until
the surcharge fill ts removed Flexible joints in the pipes
placed within the fill after this time should be capable of to
lerating one inch differential settlement
The recommendations for the pipe support headwalls and other
spectal requirements will be covered in a separate report fol
lowing a review of the proposed construction plan
Construction Inspections
The construction monitoring and inspections are recommended to
assure that adequate degrees of compaction are consistently
achieved and more importantly to control the rate of construc
tion so that excess pore water pressures do not develop
This construction inspection and engineering along with the
instrumentatton will permit the fill placement to proceed at
the maximum rate and will establish the earliest date for initia
tion of the surface construction
