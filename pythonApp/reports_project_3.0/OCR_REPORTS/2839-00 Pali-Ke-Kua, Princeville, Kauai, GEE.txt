Subsurface Exploration
In general our borings encountered a surface layer of previously placed fills
ranging in depth from approximately six 6 feet below the surface in Boring No 1 toa
depth of approximately eight 8 feet below the existing surface at Boring No 2 The
surface fills were underlain by in situ residual and saprolitic soils to the maximum depth
drilled
The previously placed fills encountered at Boring No 2 were found to be soft and
consisting of rubbish and decaying organic matter mixed with silt and are unsuitable as
foundation bearing materials The previously placed fills at Boring No 1 and the
underlying in situ soils at both boring locations appeared to be dense however the
previously placed fills should be removed and replaced with controlled structural fills prior
to building construction Proper keying and benching into the undisturbed native soils of
the existing slopes is of paramount importance to the performance of the site
improvements
RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our recommendation that the unsuitable materials and previously placed fills
encountered at the project site be removed in their entirety and replaced with engineered
fill The excavation of the unsuitable material and previously placed fills and replacement
with engineered fill should be observed by a geotechnical engineer to check that
unsuitable previously placed materials are satisfactorily removed and that the engineered
fills are properly placed The footing excavations should be observed to check that the
exposed bearing soils are competent

excavation and mass grading

Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm natural material and the
resulting depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
The unsuitable materials and previously placed fills should be completely removed
from below and to within five 5 feet outside the building perimeter The unsuitable
materials should be removed from the site and disposed of properly off site The over
excavation should be performed unti firm and competent soils are encountered The
over excavation subgrade should be prabed to check that sufficiently dense soils have
been exposed
Rainfall and surface water run off should be diverted from the over excavation to
prevent saturation and subsequent softening of the exposed competent soils
Fill material should be non expansive with no particles greater than six 6 inches
in size It is recommended that it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to
determine if it is suitable for its intended use prior to its delivery to the job site It should
be noted that the existing soils in the Princeville area are wet well above optimum
moisture content and must be dried prior to use as structural fill As an alternative
imported granular fill my be used for structural fills
Fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 85 of their maximum dry density as

Foundation Support
It is our opinion that relatively shallow spread or strip footing foundations may be
used to support the structure
For the structure bearing on the soils or fill compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented in this report an allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f
may be utilized The bearing value is for dead and live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considerea
Footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the
nearest adjacent subgrade

The bottom of footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
their maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If loose or soft pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to dense in situ soils or placed engineered fills and
replaced with granular material compacted to 95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce the potential for footing settlement due to settlement of trench
backfill
Provided that the above recommendations are followed estimated footing
settlements should be less than of an inch for footings bearing on compacted structural
fills and should be negligible for footings bearing on the hard in situ soils
