This report presents the results of our soil investigation
for the proposed cluster development on Nuuanu Pali Drive
Honolulu Hawaii
The purpose of our study was to investigate the general
subsurface soil condition at the site and to provide founda
tion and site grading recommendations for subject development
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface at the site two borings were drilled
by utilizing portable rotary wash equipment and four test pits
were dug by hand The locations of the borings and test pits
are shown on the site plan Plate 1 Our field engineer was
present throughout the field investigation to log the borings
and to observe site conditions The boring logs and test pit
logs are presented on Plate 2 3 and 4

LABORATORY TESTING
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples in order
to evaluate the properties of the site soils and to assist in
its classification
In place moisture determinations and Atterberg Limits were made
on selected samples and their results are presented on the boring
and test pit logs at the appropriate sample location
SITE CONDITION
The proposed cluster development is located near the Honolulu
watershed forest reserve area on Nuuanu Pali Drive Honolulu
The center of the proposed site is relatively flat The east
west and south sides are approximately 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
slopes down to the stream Large blocks of blue rock can be seen
along the slope In general the slope is covered by dense trees
and vegetation The slope did not exhibit any signs of instability
at the time of our investigation The level portions of the site
appears to have been graded by man Vegetation in this area is
generally less dense than that nearby
A one story dwelling and a two story dwelling are located at the
northern end of the level area next to the Nuuanu Pali Drive No
depression was noted at the dwelling locations

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Except for the soft clayey silt layer encountered at Boring
No 1 location the site appeared to be firm ground No
evidence of deep fill was noted along the existing slope
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
post and beam structures
Foundation
The proposed post foundations should bear into the stiff clayey
Silt soils and bottomed a minimum of two feet below the
existing ground surface A bearing value of 1500 pound per square
foot may be used for dead plus live loads Pressures may be in
creased by one third when considering wind or reismic loads
Individual post footings on existing slopes should extend below
the slope surface and into firm soils such that the out edge of
footing should have a minimum horizontal set back distance of 4
feet out to the slope surface For erosion protection the
footing excavation on slopes should be backfilled with concrete
to match the upper slope surface Footing inspection or probing

Slab On Grade
Expansive soils were not encountered at the project site therefore
Slab on grade for the garage area can be utilized without special
provisions for expansive soil It is recommended that a 4 inch
cushion layer of S4C be placed above the subgrade and compacted
to 95 of its maximum dry density For erosion protection slab
on grade near the top slope should extend its edges down enough

Earthwork and Compaction
All area to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and topsoil fills The ground surface
should then be keyed and benched to properly bond the newly
placed fill to the existing soil Prior to placenene of any
new fill the existing ground surface should be compacted to
a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established by
ASIMe Test Do ss7 7214 Additional fills should be placed in
horizontal lifts and compacted to the specified density
Any imported fill material should be non expansive and must
be tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to placing
