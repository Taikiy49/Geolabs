INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate subsurface
conditions to provide recommendations for the design and con
struction of the proposed 2 story office building at Nihiwai
Place and Kilani Avenue Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located at the southwestern corner of the
intersection of Nihiwai Place Kilani Avenue in Wahiawa
Oahu Hawati
The lot is rectangular in shape with dimensions of about
95 and 200 feet The total area of the lot is about 19 000
square feet
The site is bordered by Kilani Avenue on the east houses
on the south and west and Nihiwai Place on the north
At the time of our soil investigation there were no existing
structures on the site
The site is generally level and covered with grass and weeds
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to construct a 2 story steel framed
structure with masonry walls on the eastern portion of the
site The structure will be approximately 50 by 60 feet in
plan
An A C parking area is planned for the western portion of
the site
Minor grading is anticipated for the site development

The borings indicate that the site is underlain by very

depths drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
subsurface exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicates that generally competent
foundation bearing soils are located beneath the proposed
structure
The stiff surface soils should provide adequate support with
out excessive settlement The in situ surface soils appear
to have low expansion potential Slab on grade may be designed
without special provisions for expansive soil conditions
Foundation
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing foun
dations are recommended Allowable bearing value of 4 000
pounds per square foot may be used for footings resting on
the stiff surface soils
The bottom of footings should be placed about 2 feet below the
finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 5
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trench or be designed to bridge over the trench
For the above recommendations it ts estimated that settle
ments will not exceed one half of an inch total and k inch
differential
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
subgrade should be kept moist and recompacted prior to
placing the base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
One to 2 feet of fill may be placed at the site
Vegetation and topsoil should be removed prior to filling
Select non expansive fill material should be used The
fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches
and compacted to 90 of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557

Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the driveway
and parking areas
For light traffic and drained subgrade conditions the
pavement design in these areas may be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course over a
prepared subgrade
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to assure that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigations
The fill under slabs and pavements should be tested to assure
that the proper degree of compaction has been achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX _A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 borings to depths of 11 to 16 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
During the subsurface exploration one bulk bag of sample
of near surface soil was taken in the proposed A C parking
area
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were verified by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates
A 1 to A 3
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either
a standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the boring logs at the appropriate sample depth
