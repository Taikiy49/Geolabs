In accordance with our proposal dated we have recently performed
a geotechnical engineering exploration program for the cited project This letter
presenting our findings and recommendations is provided in order to expedite the
foundation design for the project A formal report summarizing our work on the project
will be submitted at a later date
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths ranging between 22 0 to 27 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
proposed new treatment facilities Advantage was also taken of the limited information
gained by review of logs of borings previously drilled at the site
In general our exploration encountered a surficial layer of residual soil
approximately three 3 to four 4 feet thick consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clay This
was underlain by a deposit of saprolite consisting of very stiff clayey silt with highly
decomposed basalt gravel extending to a depth of six 6 to fourteen 14 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings then encountered a deposit of medium dense silty
sand and sandy gravel extending down to approximately nineteen 19 feet below the
existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation
Based on the information provided to us the proposed aeration tanks will be
embedded approximately fifteen 15 feet below the existing ground surface and the tank
bottoms will be at approximately the same elevations as the groundwater level was
encountered at the time of our field exploration
It is also anticipated that the proposed settling tanks will be embedded
approximately five 5 to eight 8 feet below the existing ground surface except along the
sludge collection troughs adjacent to the aeration tanks where the embedment will be
about nineteen 19 feet below the ground surface
Based on these design bottom elevations it appears that most of the tank bottom
area will be approximately six 6 feet above the existing groundwater table except along
the sludge collection troughs adjacent to the aeration tanks where the tank bottoms will
be about five 5 feet below the groundwater table encountered during our field
exploration
Based on the subsoil and groundwater conditions encountered during our field
exploration and the above anticipated design bottom elevations of the tank structures we
believe that the proposed structures may be supported by mat foundations

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the
proposed expansion of the existing wastewater treatment plant at Princeville on the island
of Kauai Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the field and
laboratory data obtained and the project data provided to us The recommendations
presented herein are intended for design input and may not be appropriate for preparation
of construction cost estimation or for construction bidding purposes
This report supplements our design memorandum dated May 14 1992
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in Princeville on the northern side of the island of Kauai
Hawaii The existing wastewater treatment plant was constructed in the early 1970 s and
consists of open concrete tanks partially embedded below grade
The site was previously explored by the drilling of borings for the existing facilities
Abbreviated logs of the borings were provided for our review based on a reduced size
General Plan prepared by Sun Low Tom and Hara Inc dated January 1971
It is proposed to expand the plant capacity by constructing new aeration and settling
tanks Based on a discussion with the structural engineer for the new facilities and data
transmitted to us the expansion would be of similar design to the existing tanks The new
tanks are to be constructed approximately ten 10 feet west of the existing tanks The

overall plan dimensions of the tanks are approximately 105 feet by 43 feet The
approximate embedment of the tanks are given in the recommendation section of this
report

Excavation and Backfill
The anticipated excavation for the proposed partially embedded tanks will be
extended down to approximate depths of seven 7 to twenty one 21 feet below the
existing ground surface into the stiff and or medium dense soils at approximately
elevations 38 0 to 26 5 feet above MSL Groundwater may be encountered during
construction up to elevation 40 0 feet above MSL based on the previous boring data by
others
The excavation method and excavation support system should be the choice of
the contractor and must comply with all applicable safety requirements Special
precautions should be exercised to avoid undermining the existing adjacent structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2892 00 Page 8
The adequacy and safety of the excavation support should be made the sole responsibility
of the contractor
The contractor s representative who should be continuously present on site will
have the best opportunity to observe any changing conditions during excavation and
construction such as unforeseen subsoil and groundwater conditions unsuitable
construction sequences or techniques etc which may affect the excavation stability
Therefore the adequacy and safety of the excavation support system required should be
made the sole responsibility of the contractor
Backfill behind the embedded tank walls should consist of imported select granular
materials such as base course rock No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
This is to prevent build up of excessive lateral pressures against the tank walls The top
two 2 feet of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the walls
Dewatering
Dewatering of the excavation for the new tanks may be required particularly if the
groundwater rises to the level indicated in the borings drilled in the 1970 s
We recommend that dewatering provisions be included in the contract documents
for the construction of the proposed structures The method and equipment to be used
for the dewatering operations should be the choice of the contractor The adequacy and
safety of the dewatering system should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and foundation construction phases
of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
