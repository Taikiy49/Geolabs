This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the Home Depot Dole from
October 1998 through July 1999 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
May 2 1997
The Home Depot Dole project is located in the Iwilei area in the vicinity of the
intersection of Nimitz Highway and Alakawa Street on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
retail center facility will consist of a retail building a garden center a parking structure
and other infrastructures Previously the retail and garden center building pad area had
been cleared and grubbed and fill for the pad was placed On top of this a 5 foot high
surcharge fill was placed to aid in the acceleration of ground settlements A compaction
report for this grading work was issued by our office on A general
layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1
Site Preparation
The contractor started the earthwork by clearing and grubbing the parking area
and around the perimeter of the retail and garden center building pad within the contract
grading limits in October 1998 Old footings old slabs pavement vegetation and other

In areas that received fill the original ground was scarified to a depth of about

over excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled with compacted non expansive

Fills and Backfills
Fill materials were placed in near level lifts of approximately 10 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned as needed and compacted to the project requirement of
at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Compaction was
generally accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers and vibratory drum rollers
The imported fill material used generally consisted of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve and well graded processed on site granular materials less
than 6 inches in maximum dimension structural fill During the site grading operations
samples of the on site materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the site grading operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted The field density tests were performed to determine the relative compaction
of the fills placed for conformance with the specified minimum compaction A total of 353
field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 One field density test was performed using the
sand cone method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation 1556 The results
of the compaction tests performed during mass grading operations for the project are
summarized on Plates 3 1 to 3 12 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
where available and sighting from plans These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used

Utilities
Installation of the new utility lines generally included over excavation of material
followed by backfilling the trench excavation with a one inch minus bedding material
After placement of the utility line generally free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel were used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes Trenches were then backfilled with 6 inch minus granular material and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches were located in
paved areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the road subgrade was
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Field density tests were
performed as appropriate and are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 12
Pavement Areas
Typically the subgrade materials under the pavement areas were scarified to
about 6 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to at least 95 percent relative compaction The degree of compaction was based on
visual observations and partially on the number of passes of a compactor over a lift of fill
material since the over sized nature of some fill material precluded field density testing of
the fill materials Field density tests were performed on the finished subgrade and base
course with results presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 12
Where low test results were noted the areas were reworked until the specificied
minimum compaction was obtained
EVALUATIONS
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Inc during construction of the project in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the plans and specifications and
recommendations stated in the above referenced report by C W Associates Inc dba
