INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions in the proposed construction area and to provide
recommendations for the design and construction of the foun
dations to support the proposed structures and for the
swimming pool areas
W 1975
Site Description
The proposed site in the Kalihi Section of Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii is adjacent to Kalihi Kai Elementary School immedi _
ately north of the intersection of McNeil and Kaumualti
Streets The site is presently utilized as a recreational
area which includes a gymnasium two recreation buildings a
court yard with surrounding play areas and a sumo ring The
ground surface is essentially level varying between elev 20
and elev 25
Proposed Construction
It is our understanding that the proposed development of the
Kalakaua Recreational Center will consist of a new multi
purpose building approximately 180 feet square in plan dimen
sions a 60 foot by 90 foot swimming pool facility a wading
pool and a pool administration and shower building It is
anticipated that the structures will be constructed essentially
at the existing grade Excavations reaching a maximum depth of
approximately 15 feet will be required for the swimming pool
and underground utilities

Foundations
Lightly loaded spread or line foundations can bear within the
upper clayey silt where they may be designed for maximum allow
able contact bearing pressures of 2 000 pounds per square foot
These foundations should bear a minimum of twelve inches below
the final adjacent grade
Due to the heterogeneous nature of the boulder deposits they
will not provide uniform support Foundations in the vicinity
of Boring 3 or in any area where boulders are encountered
should by pass the boulder layer and bear upon the underlying
cemented coral sand and gravel
For heavily load foundations the uppermost competent bearing
material is the cemented coral sand encountered between eleva
feet and 18 5 feet This layer will provide allow
able contact bearing pressures of 6 000 pounds per square foot
without detrimental settlements
Based upon these loadings it is estimated that the maximum
settlements will not exceed inch total and inch differ
ential All foundations within a structural unit should bear
upon similiar materials
Foundations bearing upon fill compacted to 90 per cent of the
maximum dry density can be designed for maximum allowable con
tact bearing pressures of 2 000 pounds per square foot
W 1975
The allowable bearing pressures can be increased by 1 3 when
considering seismic and wind loadings All foundations should
bear a minimum of 12 inches below the final adjacent grade
Foundation excavations should be inspected to assure that ade
quate bearing has been obtained and to verify that subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
explorations
Floor Slabs
Laboratory testing of the near surface soils show that they are
non expansive Slab on grade construction can therefore be
utilized without special precautions It is recommended that
all subgrades to receive slabs on grade be scarified to a depth
of six inches the scarified surface brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of
the maximum dry density established by American Society for
Testing and Materials Test Designation D 1557 72T Any boulders
or hard spots encountered during the scarifing operations should
be removed and backfilled with compacted granular material
It is recommended that all floor slabs with moisture sensitive
floor coverings be protected by a minimum of four inches of non
capillary fill and a plastic vapor barrier
Retaining Walls
Where retaining walls will be utilized for changes of grade or
W 1975
an the swimming pool area it is recommended that they be
designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 30 pounds per
cubic foot and a frictional factor of 0 55 providing that select
granular material is utilized for the backfill The backfill
should be compacted to between 90 and 95 per cent of the maxi
mum dry density
If the on site clayey silt is used for backfill the equivalent
fluid pressure would increase to 45 pounds per cubic foot
Earthwork and Grading
It is not anticipated that the proposed development will include
major cuts and fills with the exception of the pool excava
tion Where fills will be placed it is recommended that all
organic and deleterious material should be removed and the
upper six inches of the existing ground surface scarified The
scarified surface should then be brought to near optimum mois
ture content and compacted to the recommended dry density The
additional fi111 should then be placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to 90 per cent of the maximum dry density Where fills
will be utilized for structural support or behind retaining walls
the compaction operations should be inspected on a full time
basis to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is
consistently achieved
Cuts within the upper soil should be made no steeper than 1
horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1 The lower cemented coral sand
W 1975
can be cut at nearly vertical slopes for short term construc
tion conditions Due to the presents of relatively hard
layers it is anticipated that the swimming pool excavations
may require special methods such as jack hammers or rock exca
vating equipment
All existing concrete foundations or floor slabs should be
removed from the proposed construction area and from within
two feet beneath the foundations The vacated area can be
backfilled with compacted soil and the foundations designed to
bear upon the fill

It is recommended that the proposed foundation design be
reviewed by our office prior to finalization to verify that the
