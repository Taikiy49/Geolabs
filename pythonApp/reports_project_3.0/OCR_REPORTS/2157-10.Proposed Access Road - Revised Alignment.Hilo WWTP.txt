INTRODUCTION
Previously we performed an exploration for the originally proposed
alignment of the access road Subsequent to the issuance of our report
for that exploration the alignment of the proposed road was revised
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the grading and utility line
installation for the revised alignment of the proposed access road portion
of the proposed Hilo Wastewater Treatment Plant project near the town of
Hilo in the District of South Hilo on the island of Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 10 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions along the revised alignment of the proposed access
road in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the formulation
of engineering design recommendations pertinent to the proposed road and
infrastructure development
The work was done generally in accordance with our revised fee
proposal of March 13 1989
The scope of our work included the excavation and logging of eight
8 test pits to depths ranging between 1 0 and 8 0 feet below the
existing ground surface and the performance of six 6 seismic refraction
geophysical surveys along the revised road alignment Also included were
reduction and interpretation of the geophysical survey data engineering
evaluation of the field and geophysical data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the east of the existing Hilo
International Airport General Lyman Field in the South Hilo District of
the island of Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located in undeveloped land which is vegetated by a
subtropical forest with a dense understory of ferns and shrubs The
topography of the site is gently rolling with a slight slope towards the
north northwest Access along the proposed road alignment is by an access
trail blazed through the forest from the vicinity of the existing
airport Extensive clearing and some grading were required to make the
access trail and project site accessible to four wheel drive vehicles and
exploration equipment
W O 2157 10 Page 3
The existing ground surface elevations of the site were not available
during our exploration but are believed to range from approximately 30 to
40 feet above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the alignment for the construction
of an access road approximately 6 500 feet in length to serve a proposed
municipal wastewater treatment plant facility The site improvements will
involve the construction of a two lane paved roadway in an approximately
sixty foot wide right of way Asphaltic concrete pavements are
anticipated
It is anticipated that the site grading will require cuts and fills
on the order of six 6 to eight 8 feet
Included in the access road project will be the installation of
underground water communications and electrical lines
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the eastern flank of Mauna Loa a
large basaltic shield volcano which makes up a large portion of the mass
of the island of Hawaii The site and its vicinity has been mapped by
Stearns and Macdonald 1946 as rocks of the Kau Volcanic Series of Mauna
Loa which are Recent in age These rocks are generally thinly bedded
primitive tholeitic basalts The Kau Volcanics are frequently underlain
at relatively shallow depths by lava flow of the Kahuku Volcanic Series
The Kahuku rocks are also primitive basalts which may be capped with a
thin layer of Pahala Ash
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of
ash cinder and Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and when
the moisture content is high enough it becomes thixotropic i e it
loses strength when remolded
W O 2157 10 Page 4
Observations at the project site and in its immediate vicinity
indicate that rock is usually exposed at the ground surface below the
forest understory and that there is little or no ash soil over the rock
formation The forest has deposited a thin layer of organic matter on its
floor
During the site clearing which was performed to provide access for
the project surveyor s efforts and for our exploration some rough grading
work was done by the clearing contractor Part of this grading involved
the placement of some unengineered rockfill
Subsurface Exploration
In general our exploration encountered two 2 sets of subsurface
conditions on the project site In the western part of the site the
surface and subsurface conditions were dominated by a a lava flows
consisting of loose to medium dense clinker overlying hard dense basalt
In the eastern part of the site conditions were dominated by pahoehoe
lava flows consisting of fractured vesicular basalt The general
demarcation between these areas is in the vicinity of Station 44150 as
indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
The subsurface conditions along the revised alignment were explored
by the excavation of eight 8 test pits to depths ranging between 1 0 and
8 0 feet below the existing ground surface in areas where excavation was
feasible and by the performance of six 6 geophysical surveys seismic
refraction to obtain data pertaining to the characteristics of the rock
materials in areas where excavation was not possible The approximate
locations of the test pits and seismic surveys are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The logs of the test pits and the results of the seismic refraction
surveys are presented in Appendix A of this report along with detailed
descriptions of the field exploration The data from our previous
exploration are also presented in Appendix A

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered at
shallow depths below the surface of the project site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed access road utilities and related site
work However due to the potential existence of cavities within the
volcanic rock we recommend a program of cavity detection be conducted
during construction This is discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
roadway and right of way grading limits should be cleared of all
vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in
landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits designated
to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing unengineered rockfill
which was created due to recent site clearing and access be removed to
natural rock formation within the project grading limits If the stripped
rockfill material does not contain organics rubbish ash soil or other
deleterious materials and if it is generally less than 12 inches in
maximm size it may be re used as fill material Otherwise it should be
wasted off site or processed
W O 2157 10 Page 7
The on site volcanic material if reduced to generally less than
twelve 12 inches in size should be a source of adequate fill
material Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
dated September 1986
The on site volcanic material if reduced to less than 3 inches in
size should be a source of adequate trench and structural backfill
material Imported material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
dated September 1986
In general the anticipated rock fills could be placed in level
loose lifts up to about eighteen 18 inches in thickness Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
twelve 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
upper two 2 feet of fills and backfills below road subgrade level
should be compacted to 95 maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in rock
fills consisting of excavated volcanic material Therefore we
recommend a testing program under the observation of the geotechnical
engineer to determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to
achieve the desired level of compaction This program should be
conducted at the start of grading to establish the compaction criteria
such as the maximum loose lift thickness and mumber of passes required
for the remainder of the grading work

Slopes in clinker may be cut to a ratio of 2H 1V Care should be
exercised to avoid over cutting of the slopes

Excavation
It is our opinion that the clinker formation encountered in the
westerly portion of the proposed road alignment may be excavated by
conventional methods Due to the nature of the basalt rock formation
encountered in the easterly portion of the site it is anticipated that
some hard excavation e g hoerams chipping and or blasting will be
required for cuts foundation structure excavations or trenches in dense
rock formation
We anticipate that the clinker material will have a volume decrease
on the order of 10 to 15 per cent and that the on site basalt rock
formation will have a volume increase on the order of 20 to 30 percent
when excavated and reused as fill material The excavated material may
require some processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater
than twelve 12 inches in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill
material The material will require further processing to removal or
reduce the fragments to less than three 3 inches in size prior to its
being used as backfill
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing should
be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work However
excavations in dense rock formation will most likely stand unsupported for
temporary construction purposes Excavation in the upper clinker
formation may require sloping back to develop a stable trench and work
area
Cavities
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at shallow
depths below the project site Although our exploration did not encounter
any major voids or cavities during our exploration previous experience
W O 2157 10 Page 9
indicates that such cavities lava tubes are commonly found in volcanic
formations Several substantial tubes have been found in the South Hilo
District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential large void situated
below the road subgrade causing settlement of the pavement we recommend
that the subgrade be proof rolled first a minimum of three 3 to four 4
passes with a heavy bulldozer or heavy vibratory compactor prior to the
placement of fill in fill areas or base course in cut areas to detect and
collapse potential voids which could exist in the subgrade Surface voids
which are exposed should be backfilled with on site granular fill material
compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented under site
grading
Alternately geophysical surveys such as ground penetrating radar
could be used when the road is brought to subgrade level to detect
anomalies which could be potential voids The anomalies could then be
further probed with track drilling and if they are confirmed to be actual
voids and depending on the depths below the pavement surface should
either be backfilled with portland cement and sand grout if the volume is
small or the void be ripped open and backfilled with compacted granular
fill material The sand and cement grout should be injected at low to
moderate pressures in an upstage fashion i e from the bottom of the
probe hole to the top

The base course should be compacted to 95 maximm dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and the above
section be revised if necessary
The recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that
adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas The design section
does not consider construction phase traffic It is recommended that if
the proposed access road is to be used for construction traffic the
placement of the base course and surface course be delayed until after
construction of the wastewater treatment plant
The subgrade should be shaped to drain and daylight at the exterior
edges of paved areas Weepholes should be provided at the base course
level in drainage inlet structures in order to relieve water which may
accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the intent of the earthwork recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
W O 2157 10 Page 11
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as visual
observations test pits and geophysical surveys Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping and estimation from reference points located in the field by
and shown on the proposed alignment plan prepared by Inaba Engineering
Incorporated Elevations of the data points were not available at the
time of our exploration The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

II Special Considerations
A
Cut slopes shall be excavated to the lines and grades indicated
on the contract drawings and where necessary adjusted in
accordance with the recommendations of the Geotechnical Engineer
during construction
All trench backfill placed above 12 inches of the top of the
pipes shall be compacted in maximum lift thickness of 8 inches
unless specifically authorized by the Geotechnical Engineer
The Geotechnical Engineer may require thinner lifts should
compaction operations consistently fail to produce the specified
compaction requirements In any event the required levels of
compaction shall be obtained throughout the full depth and width
of each layer of backfill
Jetting and ponding of the backfill or fill materials shall
be permitted on this project if the backfill materials are
granular soils approved by the Geotechnical Engineer in writing
Upon completion of grading or temporary interruption of grading
and the subsequent termination of observation by the
Geotechnical Engineer no further filling or excavating shall be
performed without notifying the Geotechnical Engineer in
advance and ascertaining his requirements

III Site Preparation
A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be
disposed of in accordance with Section 10 CLEARING AND
GRUBBING of the Standard Public Works Specifications
All vegetation and deleterious materials encountered within the
Grading Limits shall be cleared and disposed of off site This
removal shall be completed prior to excavating and filling
Organically contaminated topsoil shall be stripped prior to
actual grading Materials determined as being unsuitable for
placement in compacted fill shall be removed and wasted
off site Any material incorporated as a part of the compacted
fill must be approved by the Geotechnical Engineer
Any abandoned underground structures such as cesspools
cisterns tunnels septic tanks wells pipelines or others not
located prior to grading are to be removed and the resulting
depression backfilled and compacted in accordance with these
specifications
Loose surface soils encountered in areas to receive fill shall
be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be observed and approved by the Geotechnical
Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

Representative samples of the materials to be used as compacted fill
shall be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the
proposed materials shall be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least
seven working days prior to its intended jobsite delivery

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which before
compaction will not exceed eighteen 18 inches in loose lift
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed
to attain uniformity of material and water content within each
layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve 12 inches in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve 12 inches in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2157 10 Page 8
Appendix B Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than three 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than one foot in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to verify the maximum lift
thickness and number of passes of a compactor required to
achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start
of the project The number of passes can then be used as the
criteria for compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill

L
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and

Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall

The Contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it

A
