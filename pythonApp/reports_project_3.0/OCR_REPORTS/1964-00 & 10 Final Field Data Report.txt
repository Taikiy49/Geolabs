Piezometer
Single or double level piezometers were installed in selected rorings
to monitor groundwater seepage and or utility leakage at various depths in
the study area
Readings were taken periodically for a period of
approximately 1 year and 2 months
A surnmar r of the collected piezometer
readings to date is presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
The approximate
locations surface elevations and depths of the piezometers are shown on
the Site Plan and Slill1t1laJ Y Of Surface Elevations and Depths of Borings and
Instruments Plates 1 and 2
The single level piezometer was installed by using a 3 4 or 2 inch
PVC riser pipe either perforated along the entire length or slotted for
arout 1 to 2 feet at a pre determined depth in the selected roring
Clean
granular materials such as sand and gravel were used to backfill the
annular space between the roring shaft and the perforated slotted pipe
section
A bentonite seal was then placed arove the granular backfill to
isolate the seepage interception zone
Similarly
the double level
piezometer was installed by using two
3 4 inch PVC riser pipes each
slotted for to 2 feet at a pre determined depth in the selected
roring backfilled with granular materials and isolated with bentonite
seals and below the seepage interception zones at the required
depths

APPENDIX
C
Inclinometer
Inclinometer systems were installed in selected borings to monitor
the subsurface lateral movements in the study area
Readings were taken
periodically for a period of about 1 year and 2 months
Ihe readings are
presented on Plates C 1 through C 12
Ihe inclinometer casings were left
in place and readings were taken by means of a separate deflection
measurement system
Ihe approximate locations surface elevations and
depths of the inclinometers are shown on the Site Plan and Summary of
Surface Elevations and Depths of Borings and Instruments Plates 1 and 2
After completion of the selected boring a 3 34 inch outer diameter
ABS inclinometer casing manufactured by Slo E Indicator Co of Seattle
Washington with four internal longitudinal yrooves was installed in the
borehole
Ihe annular space between the casing and the borehole shaft was
then grouted with a cementjbentoni te mixture
Ihe casing grooves were
generally orientated to provide displacement readings in the down slope
direction
Ihe top of the casing was generally cut level with the road
surface or at a height of about 1 5 feet above the road pavement and
covered with a plastic cap
Ihe deflection measurement system consists of a servo accelerometer
probe Model 50325 connected by special electrical cable to a digital
readout unit
Ihe probe has spring loaded guide wheels which fit into the
casing grooves
Ihe probe is lowered into the casing and readings of the
casing deflection are made at 2 foot intervals
After readings are made
in one direction the probe is rotated 180 degrees for a complementary set
of readings
The inclinometer readings provide a measurement of lateral
displacement of the casing relative to the bottom of the casing
