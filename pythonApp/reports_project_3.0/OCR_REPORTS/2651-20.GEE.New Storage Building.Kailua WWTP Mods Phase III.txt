PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one story storage building approximately 26 by 146
feet in plan dimension at the project site The finished floor of the proposed building may
be raised to an elevation of approximately 8 0 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL from the
elevation of 7 5 feet above MSL indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is our understanding that concrete columns with CMU walls and concrete floor
and ceiling construction will be utilized for the proposed building

on March 18 1992 loading on the order of 60 kips per column and 2 500 pounds per
foot of bearing wall is anticipated

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal dated March 23 1992 to
GMP Associates Inc The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drilling rig and operators
to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging from 23 0 to 23 5
feet below the existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings by a
geologist of our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that construction of the proposed storage building is feasible from
a geotechnical engineering point of view provided the recommendations presented in this
report are properly followed
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by a relatively thin
surficial fill layer of silty clay which is highly expansive as indicated by the laboratory test
results Therefore special construction procedures should be followed in order to reduce
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2651 20 Page 6
the potential deleterious effects which may result from swelling of the subsoil caused by
subsequent soil wetting after being allowed to dry
Site Grading
Due to the high swelling potential of the near surface soils at the project site
special precautions and procedures should be observed for the site grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
All areas to receive slabs and pavements should after clearing and grubbing be
over excavated to a minimum of two 2 feet below the subgrade elevation and backfilled
with off site select non expansive granular borrow fill material such as quarry waste or
select borrow Any loose or soft spots encountered at the over excavated subgrade level
should be removed and replaced with the above recommended compacted non
expansive select granular material The clayey material resulting from over excavation
should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The over excavated surface should be moisture conditioned and the select granular
material placed in compacted level lifts not exceeding nine 9 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of non
expansive select borrow granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with
less than 20 percent passing the 200 sieve Any imported fill materiai snouid be tested
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2651 20 Page 7
and approved for its intended use by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery
to the project site
Special care must be exercised during grading in the area near the east corner of
the building to minimize disturbance of the side slopes of the drainage ditch at this
location
Foundation Design
According to the information provided to us by the project structural engineer
loadings on the order of 60 kips per column and 2 500 pounds per foot of bearing wall
are anticipated
In consideration of the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the
site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on the coral
formation may be used to support the proposed building
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 500 p s f may be utilized for the design
of footing foundations bearing on the generally competent coral formation encountered
at shallow depths This bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for loads of short duration such as those caused by
wind or seismic loads
If soft loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they
should be over excavated to competent in situ material and repiaced with select granular
material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2651 20 Page 8
The footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade Deeper footing embedment should be used where
necessary to assure that the footings bear directly on the underlying competent coral
formation
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to allow verification of the bearing material
and the embedment depths

Slab On Grade
As indicated in the section entitled Site Grading it is recommended that the top
two 2 feet of the highly expansive clays at the site be removed below the slab subgrade
level and be replaced with select non expansive granular material Provided that this
recommendation is followed the following slab on grade section may be used for
preliminary design purposes
6 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
12 Inch Total Thickness on two foot of
Moist Compacted Select Granular Fill
The above recommended section considers medium load handling vehicular traffic
and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subject to vehicular traffic
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
the floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier such as a plastic membrane on top
of the base course layer is recommended One inch of moist fine sand may be placed
above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
The slab thickened edges adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
The base course should consist of crushed rock compacted to a minimum of 95
percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures
described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Based on the site plan provided to us it is our understanding that the pavement
for the future Maintenance Building will be extended up to the proposed Storage Building

Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should be used for the trench
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to reduce the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from the level one foot above
the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular
material and placed in maximum ten 10 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive jandscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2651 20 Page 11
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or slab footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

misinterpretation of the recommendations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of
three 3 borings to depths ranging between 23 0 to 23 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory Al soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a holiow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
