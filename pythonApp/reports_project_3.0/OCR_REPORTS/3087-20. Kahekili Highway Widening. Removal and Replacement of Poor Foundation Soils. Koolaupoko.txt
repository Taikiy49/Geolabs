Removal and Replacement of Poor Foundation Soils
From Station 53 80 to Station 55 60
Kahekili Highway Widening
In accordance with your facsimile request dated June 8th we have reviewed the
request by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company regarding the limits of removal
and replacement of poor foundation soils from about Station 53 80 to about Station
55 60 for the above referenced project
Based on our field exploration soft soils were encountered from near the ground
surface extending to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the surface in Boring Nos 33
53 and 54 Relatively stiff soils embankment fill materials were encountered in Boring
No 36 to a depth of about 17 feet below the surface underlain by residual soils and
basalt rock formation It should be noted that Boring No 36 was drilled from the side
slope of the highway embankment
The extent of poor foundation soils was estimated based on extrapolation of our
boring information and field observations at the project site Based on the estimated
extent of poor foundation soils our engineering analyses and other considerations
regarding the stability of the MSERW we recommended that the foundation soils within
a 40 foot zone in the east west direction and from about Station 53 80 to about
Station 55 60 in the north south direction be removed and replaced with granular fill
material for the support of the approximately 40 foot high MSERW

Although relatively stiff soils were encountered in Boring No 36 drilled atabout
Station 55 20 some of the soft soils may have been removed during the initial con
struction of the highway embankment or the soft soils may have consolidated under the
weight of the embankment since the boring was drilled from the side slopes of the
embankment Based on our evaluation of the existing topography and boring informa
tion we recommended that the limits of excavation in the north south direction be
extended to Station 55 60 due to the potential presence of poor foundation soils to the
west of the state Right of Way The extended limits of removal and replacement also
reduce the potential for significant differential settlements of the MSERW due to
differing foundation soils and for the higher bearing capacity needed Therefore the
limits of the soft soil removal and replacement should remain between Station 53 80
and Station 55 60 unless additional information indicates otherwise
As indicated in our report we recommend that a representative from Geolabs
Hawaii observe the excavation of the soft foundation soils to evaluate and confirm the
subsoil conditions assumed in our stability analyses If actual exposed foundation soil
conditions differ from those assumed or considered in our report then appropriate
modifications to the design may be made
