CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Existing Freezer
Based on the soils encountered in the borings water level
readings and laboratory soil tests the floor slab heaving
appears to be a result of frost action
Frost action occurs when there is a combination of freezing
temperature high water level and subgrade soils which
have high capillary rise such as very fine sands and silts
When the surface of the soil is below freezing temperature
upward moisture migration from the high water level occurs
due to capillary action and condensation This water
collects and freezes to form lenses of ice in the upper
part of the ground which displaces the soil and causes
heaving The formation of the ice lenses can continue
indefinitely as long as suitable conditions exist
W O 930 00
P 5
Frost action is most prevalent in very fine sands and
silts due to its high capillary rise potential and
moderate permeability Laboratory soil tests disclosed
that the soils under the area have sufficient fines to
cause capillary rise and are susceptible to frost action
The existing freezer floor slab should be removed and
replaced In order to reduce the frost action under the
freezer a capillary break layer consisting of clean
gravel such as 3B Fine gravel is required under the
freezer floor slab to prevent capillary rise The
thickness of the gravel layer required depends on the
depth of frost penetration and water level Due to the
high water level 3 depth encountered at the site
only about a 3 foot layer of gravel could be utilized
below the freezer unless the floor level is raised
Because of the limited depth of gravel backfill and
high water level insulation of the freezer to prevent
freezing temperatures from extending below about 2 feet
is critical If freezing temperatures penetrate to the
natural water level ice lenses could again form and
cause heaving

Loose sandy deposits were encountered below the freezer
area however detrimental settlements are not anticipated

New Freezer Location
New rreezer Location
The test boring in this area disclosed soil and water
conditions similar to those encountered below the existing
freezer The fine sandy soil and high water level encoun
tered indicate that this area is susceptible to frost
action
In order to reduce the possibility of frost action below
the new freezer the floor of the freezer should be raised
above the water level a sufficient distance such that the
freezing temperatures will not reach the water level A
minimum floor elevation of 5 feet above the high water
level is recommended A capillary break layer would be
required to prevent upward moisture migration from the
water level to below the freezer A four 4 foot layer
of clean gravel such as 3B Fine gravel is recommended
below the freezer slab to act as a capillary break against
upward moisture migration and as a drain for accumulated
condensation from the freezer

P 7
Loose sandy deposits were encountered to about 19 feet
in the boring Some settlements should be anticipated
due to the weight of any proposed fill and structures
Due to the sandy nature of the subsoils these settle
ments should occur relatively rapidly The anticipated
settlements should be analyzed when more definite data
on the location and type of structure which will house
this new freezer is available
Preliminary data from the one boring drilled indicate
that this area could support only very light one story
warehouse type structures with shallow foundations if
some settlements are tolerable If some settlements are
not tolerable or if heavier two and three story concrete
structures are planned pile foundations to the dense
coral layer at about 20 foot depths would probably be
required
Additional borings and analysis would be required when
more definite data on the type of building planned is
available
