SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in Kawela Molokai Access to
the site is from Kamehameha V Highway The proposed reser
voirs are part of the on site improvements for the Kawela
Plantation Unit II development The proposed reservoirs
are located along the eastern ridge of the project develop
ment

The upper 550 reservoir site is located near the center of
the eastern ridge which is sloping at about V
southwards The area is covered with some brush and a few
Kiawe trees The elevation generally varies from about 530
to 560 feet The reservoir site had been cut nearly to
finish grade at the time of our field explorations A dirt
road which was constructed by the project contractor pro
vides access between Kamehameha V Highway and the 550
reservoir The new access roadway alignment will generally
follow the existing dirt road
The 250 reservoir is located on the western side of the
existing dirt road The site is on a side hill that gene
rally slopes away from the existing dirt road southwest at
up to about a 3 1 H V slope Vegetation in the area con
sists of grass brush and a few kiawe trees The elevation
at the site generally varies from about 230 to 260 feet

SOTL CONDITIONS
550 Reservoir
The reservoir pad and perimeter road site grading were
nearly completed at the time of our field exploration Four
4 test pits were dug along the perimeter of the reservoir
pad to delineate the elevation of the competent underlying
competent rock layer

Field observations along with Test Pit No 4 disclosed that
competent weathered rock or clinker was encountered in the
upslope half of the tank site Test pits excavated
around the down slope makai half of the tank site indi
cated that the foundation bearing layer weathered rock or
clinker was about 2 below existing subgrade level overlain
by silty residual soil Therefore in order to bear the
entire tank footing on similar weathered rock formation we
recommend that the southern makai half of the perimeter
footing be over excavated and extend down to the weathered
rock layer The over excavation can then be backfilled with
lean concrete up to the tentative footing bottom elevations
as shown on the plans
250 Reservoir

the ground surface and extended down to elevation
feet In Borehole No 2 loose soil material was encoun
tered from about elevation 231 to 229 2 feet This loose
soil material was from the upper cut bank From about ele
vation 229 to 220 the boring encountered the underlying
dense gray basalt formation

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the reservoir sites are
underlain by a competent weathered rock or clinker formation
which can provide adequate support for the proposed tank
structures
Foundtions
Spread and continuous footings can be used for the reservoir
structures It appears that weathered rock or volcanic
clinker material will be encountered near the reservoir
level An allowable bearing value up to 4 000 p s f may be
used for footings resting on the dense weathered rock or
clinker material and up to 10 000 p s f for footing bearing
on dense basalt rock
Based on boring data and estimated reservoir loads small
settlements less than one fourth of an inch are antici
pated for footing loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
The footings should bear a minimum 12 inches below the adja
cent finish grade The ground surface around the footings
should not be excavated or allowed to erode
Slab on grade should bear on the very stiff in situ residual
soil or weathered rock
Surface water during and after constructton of the struc
tures should be directed away from the reservoir foundations
Lateral Resistance
A coefficient of friction between concrete and soil of 0 5
can be utilized to determine the lateral resistance of the
structure and foundation Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f may be used
Site Grading
At the time of our engineer s visit to the site on March 9
1981 the 550 reservoir site had been cut down approxi
mately to grade and cutting of the 250 reservoir was in
for both sites A thin layer of residual soil over wea
thered rock formation was exposed in the cut slopes It

At the time of our engineer s visit fill slopes were not
being constructed
Select on site material may be utilized for fill The fill
material should be placed on slopes free of all vegetation
and loose soil in horizontal loose lifts not exceeding 10
inches and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than V
must be keyed in natural ground and benched into the exist
ing hillside slope
Sliver or thin fills against hillsides should be avoided
Surface runoff of water should be diverted away from the
tops of slopes and slope planting should be utilized to
reduce slope erosion
Service Roadway Pavement
Based on field observations of the near surface soils at the
site and light traffic conditions the following pavement
design is recommended for the service roadways
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the site filling and
subgrade preparation for the reservoir and roadway
It is also recommended that the footing excavation be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
