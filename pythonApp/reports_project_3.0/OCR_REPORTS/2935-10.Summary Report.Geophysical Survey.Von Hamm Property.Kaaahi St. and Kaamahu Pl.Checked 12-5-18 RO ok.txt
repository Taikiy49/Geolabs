SUMMARY
In accordance with the request of Mr Michael
W Polkinghorn of PSI Hall Kimbrell we have
conducted a geophysical survey of portions of
the interior of the ground floor of the building
located at the subject property in Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The work was
performed in general accordance with our fee
quotation dated This report
summarizes our work on the project and
presents our findings
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is a textile factory and warehouse situated in the Iwilei area of
Honolulu Hawaii Prior to the geophysical field work attempts were made to drill and
install groundwater monitoring wells in the building as part of a due diligence proceeding
During these attempts concrete and steel materials were encountered These materials
prevented penetration of the drilling
Based on information subsequently provided to us the site was formerly part of
the oid Oahu Railroad and Land Company railyard It is believed that the concrete and
steel were remnants from the old railroad operations

In order to attempt to locate clear areas for the drilling and installation of monitoring
wells geophysical surveys were conducted at the site using the ground penetrating radar
method
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this portion of the project was to provide geophysical
survey services to attempt to locate areas where groundwater monitoring wells could be
drilled and installed with a minimal potential of encountering buried obstructions
The scope of our work on the project consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a Geophysical Survey Systems Inc
Model SIR 3 ground penetrating radar and a Senior Project Geologist to and
from the project site
2 Marking of a reference grid pattern on the warehouse floor in the areas
where the installation of groundwater monitoring wells was desired
3 Performance of geophysical surveys along the reference grid to attempt to
find areas with a minimal amount of anomalous data
When areas were identified as having a minimal amount of anomalies
traverse surveys were conducted across the grid pattern to further delineate
areas with minimal anomalies

4 Following completion of the geophysical field work the data acquired was
reviewed and interpreted Potential anomalies were plotted on the building

5 Upon completion of the data review and interpretation this report was
prepared summarizing our work on the project and presenting our findings
FIELD EXPLORATION
The geophysical surveys were conducted using a ground penetrating radar system
manufactured by Geophysical Survey Systems Incorporated The system consisted of
a SIR System 3 control unit with a 500 Megahertz transceiver antenna set
Ground penetrating radar utilizes impulse radar technology to obtain real time
profiles of the subsurface materials A radar frequency pulse is transmitted into the
subsurface and when it encounters an interface between two materials with differing
electrical properties it is reflected back to a receiver on the surface In a relatively
homogeneous medium this reflection is noted as an anomaly
The reflected pulse is intercepted by the receiver and then electronically processed
and printed on a graphic recorder producing a record similar to a marine depth recorder
chart As the transmitter and receiver antenna array is moved across the ground surface
a real time graphic record of the subsurface is generated Markers can be inserted into
the data print to reference distances as the array is moved providing a means to
physically locate anomalies
Ground penetrating radar is generally considered most applicable to the location
of subsurface voids and buried structures Depth of penetration and data resolution are

The use of ground penetrating radar is limited by the presence of conductive
materials within the profile being surveyed This is particularly true if the conductive
materials are in the upper or surface portions of the subsurface profile Conductive
materials such as clays or brackish to saline groundwater attenuate the electromagnetic
signal and interfere with penetration of the radar pulses Generally ground penetrating
radar will not function with conductivities higher than about meters Mho
meters
Based on interpretation of the data penetration of the radar signal was limited to
a depth of about ten 10 feet due to the presence of brackish groundwater below the
Site
FINDINGS
Our geophysical surveys of portions of the warehouse floor indicate that there are
numerous ground penetrating radar anomalies at shallow depths within the areas
traversed
Based on discussions with Mr Polkinghorn regarding the conditions encountered
during the attempts to drill groundwater monitoring wells and the information provided
regarding the historical use of the site it is likely that many of these anomalies are
indicative of buried metal or metal objects However it is not possible to conclusively
identify those anomalies as rails or old foundations

The areas with the least number of possible anomalies as noted during the field
work were marked on the warehouse floor at the conclusion of the field work
It is our opinion that based on interpretation of the ground penetrating radar data
the marked areas represent locations with the least possibility of encountering
obstructions for drilling and installation of the proposed groundwater monitoring wells
It should be realized that this interpretation is based on a limited amount of available
information pertaining to subsurface conditions at the site and the large number of
anomalies encountered in the geophysical surveys
It should also be realized that the presence of brackish groundwater below the site
limited the penetration of the signal and therefore objects at depths greater than about
ten 10 feet below the warehouse floor may not have been observed by the geophysical
surveys
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as geophysical surveys and visual
observations Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until a later point in time
lf variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings given in
this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined by reference
and measurement from building column lines as shown on the foundation plan provided
to us by PSI Hall Kimbrell The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
