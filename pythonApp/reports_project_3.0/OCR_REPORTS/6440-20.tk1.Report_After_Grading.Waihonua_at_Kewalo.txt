PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the makai side of Waimanu Street between the existing
Ko olani and Hawaiki Tower high rise condominium development in Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is an L shaped parcel designated as
006 017 encompassing approximately 75 500 square feet
A high rise condominium is planned for the site development In general the project
consists of a six level base parking structure with 37 stories of residential units above a
portion of the base parking structure The footprint of the high rise portion is about 70 feet
by 175 feet in plan dimensions The design finish floor elevation is set at approximately
8 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In addition a portion of the garage will be about half a
level below grade with the lowest elevation at around 3 5 feet MSL

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC is the general contractor
for the project Koga Engineering and Construction Inc performed the site grading
operations pavement construction and utility line installation The site grading work
consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 and
95 percent relative compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively
of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing
and Material ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
During the earthwork operations a layer of deleterious materials was encountered
below the slab on grade of the parking structure Based on our field observations and
evaluation we recommended removing the deleterious materials down to a maximum
depth of 2 feet below the slab subgrade i e bottom of the slab cushion The over
excavation was backfilled with approved on site soils and imported fill material The
bottom of the over excavation was recompacted and lined with a layer of woven
geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X prior to the backfilling operation

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 339 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 18 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations
presented in the above referenced geotechnical engineering report March 12 2012 and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork might
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
