EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located along Ko aki Street in the Milltown Center area of
Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site consists of two adjacent lots
Lot Nos 2 and 3 Lot No 2 is roughly trapezoidal in shape and Lot No 3 is rectangular
in shape approximately 100 feet wide by 200 feet deep
The site consists of flat level sparsely grassed areas separated by a 5 foot to
8 foot bank at the lot line There is a small bank emerging from the sidewalk and another
bank leading to a dirt access road at the back of the property Based on our field
observations the project site presently consists of vacant land with light vegetative growth
of wild grasses The majority of the ground surface is exposed reddish brown clayey soils
with some coralline gravel in a relatively dry condition The adjacent properties to the west
have not been developed and the property to the east consists of a warehouse facility
Based on our field observations and experience in the area we believe that the site
has been previously graded by others Therefore some areas may have been excavated
and some fills have been placed to achieve the current ground elevations at the site
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 20 7 to 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 8
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density plasticity and strength characteristics
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the
field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under
different surcharge loads The results of these tests are summarized on Plate 9
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate 10
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM D 2166 was performed on a selected
soil sample to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the soil encountered
Results of the unconfined compression test are presented on Plate 11
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of in situ soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
The test results are presented on Plate 12
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered a layer of fill material
of varying thickness below the existing ground surface Very stiff to hard residual soils
were encountered below the fill materials extending to the maximum depth drilled of about
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors

Shallow Foundations
We understand that it is planned to construct a distribution center at the project site
Based on the relatively competent subsurface soil condition encountered at the project
site we recommend that the proposed structure be supported on shallow foundations
consisting of spread and or continuous footings An allowable bearing pressure of
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for design of footings bearing on
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
and seismic forces The bottom of the footing should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm or dense materials The resulting
excavations should be backfilled with granular fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
the soils as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Foundations located adjacent to slopes or on sloping ground should be embedded
a sufficient depth to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 6 feet measured
from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope If foundations are located
next to utility trenches and easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal
to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench Alternatively the footing should be embedded to a depth as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
appreciable foundation settlements

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey soils with low to moderate expansion potential To reduce the
potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the subgrade soils we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with non expansive
select granular fill material
In general we recommend a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select
granular fill be provided below the building slabs The non expansive select granular fill
should be moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In addition we recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified to a depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of the
select granular fill Optimum dry density refers to the moisture content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The select granular fill should be placed immediately after the moisture conditioning
and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils The prepared select granular fill and the
underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this letter report Compaction requirements for
the subgrade soils and select granular fill are also discussed in the same section
It should be emphasized that the areas adjacent to the slabs should be tightly
backfilled against the slab edges with low expansive relatively impervious soils such as
the on site soils These areas should also be graded to divert water away from the slabs
and to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs and foundations
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB be placed below the slabs to serve as
a capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce the potential
for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel or BTB cushion layer Flexible floor
coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask
minor slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate
some flexibility to accommodate a small amount of possible ground movements
For slabs subjected to vehicular traffic the vapor barrier may be omitted and the
4 inch gravel BTB cushion layer should be replaced by 4 inches of base course For
design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pci may be used for the

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork vegetation debris deleterious material or other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site If
abandoned utility lines are encountered they should be removed or grouted with
cement If abandoned structures are exposed during the site preparation they
should be completely demolished removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with properly compacted fill material
The excavated soft soils should be disposed of properly off site
Prior to filling and at finished subgrades the ground should be scarified to a depth
of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For
pavement subgrades the ground should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or the
subgrade should be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and
poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
properly compacted fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage of dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Fills and Backfills
Fill material should consist of well graded on site materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension and free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Import fills and select granular fill materials should consist of non expansive select
granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing
the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the fills due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with properly compacted
fill material
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Inc during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining
the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where
compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be applied
with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction
Slopes
Slopes should not exceed a slope inclination of 2H 1V Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a
well compacted slope face
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations

In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finish subgrade should consist of the on site soils or select granular fill material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of trench backfill below pavement subgrade
should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical
compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials Water tamping jetting
or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material
Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the structures and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundations excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from walls and
foundations

part upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions
