The subject project consisted of the construction of four 2 to 3 story buildings
and other associated structures The earthwork performed included the placement and
compaction of up to approximately 18 inches of structural fill in the building areas to
attain the design finish subgrades over excavation of unsuitable soft loose subsoils and
replacement with engineered fill at some footing locations and backfilling of utility
trenches The earthwork was performed by RHS Lee Inc and Ron s Construction Inc
A general layout of the project site is presented on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general it is our opinion that the earthwork was performed in general
accordance with the intent of the recommendations presented in the referenced report
dated Detailed observations and discussions are presented in the
following sections of this letter report
Field Observation and Compaction Testing
During the earthwork operations representative samples of the on site and
imported soils utilized as fill materials were obtained for laboratory testing Sieve
analyses and CBR tests were performed on the structural flll material and test results
are presented on Plate 2 The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content
were determined in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Results of the maximum dry density and optimum moisture content are
summarized on Plate 3
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by
representatives from our office to observe that the earthwork was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report In
place density tests of the fills were performed periodically in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Method The test results are presented on
the attached Summary of Field Density Test Results Plates 4 1 through 4 41
The following subsections provide a brief description of our observations of the
earthwork performed at the site

Building 1
Two mat footings and 67 spread footings were planned for Building 1 Based
on our field exploration the building area is generally underlain by approximately 1 to
4 feet of dense granular fills over approximately 2 to 6 feet of soft clayey soils
Because of the presence of soft clayey soils and the relatively thin bearing layer all the
footings for Building 1 were over excavated and replaced with engineered fill The
depth of over excavation varied from about 3 5 to 7 feet below the bottom of footing
elevations The over excavation was backfilled with approximately 18 to 24 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel wrapped around by a layer of geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X Select
borrow materials were placed in controlled lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction up to the footing subgrade elevations Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures
Building 2
Two mat footings and 60 spread footings were planned for Building 2 Footings
with bottom of footing elevations extending below the existing ground surface were
over excavated by about 3 to 4 feet with the over excavation backfilled with engineered
fill as described for Building 1 above For footings bearing directly on the existing
ground surface a probing program was implemented during construction to confirm the
subsurface soil conditions at these locations Based on the results of the probing
program 19 additional footings were over excavated by about 3 to 4 feet and backfilled
with engineered fill
An abandoned oil trench was encountered along gridline 11 in Building 2 during
construction The existing grouted oil pipes were severed disposed of in the concrete
trench and the trench was backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel
Building 3
All footings for Building 3 were planned to bear on the previously existing ground
surface about 6 feet MSL with the exception of the footing at elevator No 3 and the
were over excavated by about 3 feet and backfilled with engineered fill The
abandoned oil trench traversing Building 3 area was either completely removed or
disposed of similar to the procedures described for Building 2 above

Building 4
Nine spread footings located on the Ewa side of Building 4 were designed with
footing subgrade elevations below the previously existing ground surface These
footing locations were over excavated by about 2 to 3 feet and backfilled with
engineered fill
Lean concrete was used to backfill portions of the oil trench crossing the building
and H 2 portions of the concrete wall of the trench were removed to the trench
bottom level to facilitate placement of engineered fill
Center Court
Sixteen spread footings were planned in the center court area Based on the
probing results conducted during the construction monitoring 12 footing locations were
over excavated to depths of about 4 to 5 5 feet below the ground surface to elevations
of about 2 0 to 0 5 feet MSL The over excavation was backfilled with engineered
fill as described above
Utility Lines
Underground utilities including storm drains sewers and water lines were
installed at the project site Free draining materials consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
was used as the bedding layer and as backfill up to about 12 inches above the top of
the pipe for the sewer and drain lines Sand bedding was provided under the water
lines as required by the Board of Water Supply of City and County of Honolulu The
excavated on site granular soils were generally used as subsequent backfills above the
initial backfill zone
Miscellaneous Earthworks
Footing locations for Pavilions 4 and 5 were over excavated by about 3 feet and
backfilled with engineered fill consisting of select borrow materials
New wall footings at the Aloha Tower for the tower renovation were over
excavated by about 3 to 4 5 feet and replaced with engineered fill consisting of select
borrow materials Where wet and or soft soils were encountered at bottom of over
excavation about 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped around by a layer of
geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X was placed followed by compacted engineered fill up to
the footing subgrade level

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe ail activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of earthwork observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
the earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance
with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
