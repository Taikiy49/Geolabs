Project Considerations
We understand that the project consists of the excavation and dewatering of a large
excavation for the South Kihei Road Widening Phase 3 project on the Island of Maui
Hawaii It was desired to conduct a field exploration program by drilling and sampling four
borings at the location of the large excavation in South Kihei Road The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that it is desired to evaluate and design a dewatering system for the
planned excavation Therefore two monitoring wells were installed to monitor the
groundwater level during dewatering operations We understand that the bottom of the
excavation varies from about 10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface We envision
that the sheet pile shoring for the excavation will extend to depths of about 25 to 30 feet
below the ground surface Two borings were drilled within the area of the excavation and
the other two borings were drilled adjacent to the existing building near the excavation
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our work on this project was to provide drilling and sampling services
in support of the dewatering operations for the large excavation In order to accomplish this
we conducted a program generally consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 31 5 to
52 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary wash drilling methods The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with an
emphasis on evaluating the moisture density and grain size distribution The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Thirteen sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 D 1140 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
Classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on
Plates 8 thru 10
Surface and Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously the project site is located in the Kihei area on the Island of
Maui Hawaii The large excavation is located along South Kihei Road just north of West
Kapu Place The existing Marco s South Grill Restaurant is located on the mauka side of the
road near West Kapu Place In general the surface conditions at the site are generally level
with some sandy areas adjacent to the road
Based on our borings the project site is generally underlain by near surface fills
consisting of mixtures of silty sands and gravel extending to depths of about 1 to 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Loose to medium dense coralline sands were encountered
below the near surface fills extending to depths ranging from about 10 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface Our borings generally encountered lagoonal deposits consisting of
soit silt and loose to medium dense silty sands and gravel below the coralline sands The
lagoonal deposits extended to depths of about 32 to 46 feet below the existing ground
surface The lagoonal deposits are generally underlain by very dense coral formation
overlying basalt rock formation in Boring No 3 extending to the maximum depth explored
of about 47 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposit in Boring No 1
extended to the maximum depth drilled of 52 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was generally encountered at about 1 to 3 feet below the existing ground
surface at the time of our field exploration t should be noted that groundwater levels are
expected to vary with tidal fluctuations seasonal rainfall time of the year and other factors
