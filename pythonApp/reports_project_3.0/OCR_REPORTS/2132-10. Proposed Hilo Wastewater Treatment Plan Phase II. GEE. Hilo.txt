INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the Phase II design of the project site grading
and structure foundations of the proposed Hilo Wastewater Treatment Plant
near the town of Hilo in the District of South Hilo on the island of
Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed development
The work was done generally in accordance with our revised fee
proposal of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of seventeen
17 borings to depths ranging between 5 5 and 25 0 feet below the
existing ground surface Also included were engineering evaluation of the
field data the development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration are presented in the
appendix of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated about 6 500 feet to the east of the
existing Hilo International Airport General Lyman Field in the South
Hilo District of the island of Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located in raw undeveloped land which is vegetated by a
subtropical forest with a dense understory of ferns and shrubs The
topography of the site is gently rolling with a slight slope towards
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 3
the north northwest Access to the site is by an access trail blazed
through the forest from the vicinity of the existing Hilo Sanitary
Landfill Extensive clearing and some grading was required to make the
access trail and project site accessible to truck mounted drilling
equipment
Most of the site is gently rolling to relatively level with some
stockpiles of grubbed material around the site The existing ground
surface elevations of the site range from approximately 26 to 48 feet
above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of a
municipal wastewater treatment plant facility The site improvements will
involve the construction of a bio filter wastewater treatment plant with a
capacity of about 5 0 million gallons per day sludge handling facilities
parking areas and driveways Asphaltic concrete pavements are
anticipated
The design Phase II of the treatment facilities includes the
following structures
1 Anaerobic Digesters and Control Building
2 Solids Handling Facilities
3 Secondary Treatment Facilities
4 Effluent Chlorination and Distribution Facilities
5 Administration Building
It is anticipated that the site grading will require cuts and fills
on the order of six to eight feet

The proposed treatment facility structures will be of concrete
construction and have lightly to moderate structural loads
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated on the eastern flank of Mauna Loa a
large basaltic shield volcano which makes up a large portion of the mass
of the island of Hawaii The site and its vicinity has been mapped by
Stearns and Macdonald 1946 as rocks of the Kau Volcanic Series of Mauna
Loa which are Recent in age These rocks are generally thinly bedded
primitive tholeitic basalts The Kau Volcanics are frequently underlain
at relatively shallow depths by lava flows of the Kahuku Volcanic Series
The Kahuku rocks are also primitive basalts which may be capped with a
thin layer of Pahala Ash
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of
ash cinder and Pele s Hair It generally has low shear strength and when
the moisture content is high enough it becomes thixotropic i e it
loses strength when remolded
Observations at the project site and in its immediate vicinity
indicate that rock is usually exposed at the ground surface below the
forest understory and that there is little or no ash soil over the rock
formation The forest has deposited a thin layer of organic matter on its
floor
During the site clearing which was performed to provide access for
the project surveyor s efforts and for our exploration some rough grading
work was done by the clearing contractor Part of this grading involved
the placement of as much as six 6 to eight 8 feet of unengineered
rockfill

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling seventeen 17 borings to depths ranging between 5 5
and 25 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered relatively competent basaltic
rock formation to the maximum depths explored The lava flow formation
encountered at the project site appears to have been in the transitional
phase between the pahoehoe and a a forms and also appears to have been
ponded or very slow moving at the time of deposition The formation
grades with depth from a fractured vesicular rock at the surface toa
dense massive rock at depths of about ten to fifteen feet below the
existing ground surface
Subsurface cavities lava tubes were encountered in Boring Nos
106 113 and 116 Voids which were open to the ground surface were also
noted along the access trail to the site and on the project site during
our exploration for Phase I
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of this
report
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings during
our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered at
shallow depths below the surface of the project site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed wastewater treatment plant and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 6
related site work However due to the potential existence of cavities
within the volcanic rock we recommend that a program of probing and
grouting at foundation locations be conducted during construction This
is discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak
or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations within the
portions of the grading limits designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing unengineered rockfill be
removed to natural rock formation within the project grading limits If
the stripped rockfill material does not contain organics rubbish ash
soil or other deleterious materials and if it is generally less than 12
inches in maximum size it may be reused as fill material Otherwise it
should be wasted off site
The on site volcanic material if reduced to generally less than 12
inches in size should be a source of adequate fill material
Imported fill material if required should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works
Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
Any fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 7
Structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills
consisting of excavated volcanic material Therefore we recommend a
testing program under the observation of the geotechnical engineer to
determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction This program should be conducted at the
start of grading to establish the compaction criterion for the remainder
of the grading work
Slopes in dense rock formation may be cut to a ratio of 0 5H to lv
Slopes in highly fractured basalt or clinker may be cut to a ratio of 2H
to 1V Care should be exercised to avoid overcutting of the slopes
Fill slopes should be placed at a ratio of 2H to lV Fill slopes
should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope
Excavation
Due to the nature of the rock formation encountered at the site it
is anticipated that some hard excavation e g hoerams chipping and or
blasting will be required for cuts foundation structure excavations or
trenches that encounter dense rock formation
Generally the in situ basalt formation should be rippable by a large
bulldozer to a depth of about five 5 feet below the rock formation
surface Excavations to greater depths will most likely require hard
excavation and or blasting resulting in higher excavation costs
Therefore the depth of excavation should be limited to about 10 feet
below the existing ground surface wherever possible
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 8
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing should
be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work However
excavations in dense rock formation normally can stand unsupported for
temporary construction purposes
Foundation Support
We recommend that the structure foundations bear on in situ basalt or
fill placed in accordance with the recommendations presented herein For
the design of foundations bearing on in situ basalt an allowable bearing
capacity of 6 000 p s f may be utilized For foundations bearing on fill
composed of the on site excavated rock less than 12 inches in maximum
size compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft
and or weak materials down to in situ basalt and backfilling of the
resultant void with fill material compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations

Backfill Placement
It is anticipated that backfilling will be required for below grade
structures and for trenches Backfills should be placed in general
accordance with the recommendations presented above for fill placement
except that the maximum particle size should not exceed three 3 inches
For below grade structures it is recommended that a clean granular
material such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel be utilized to reduce the
amount of compactive effort required to attain 90 percent relative dry
density
Settlements
Minimal settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the
basalt formation or on compacted fill prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein
Cavity Probing
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at shallow
depths below the project site Cavities or voids were encountered in
Boring and 116 during our exploration Previous
experience indicates that such cavities ava tubes are commonly found in
volcanic formations Several substantial tubes have been found in the
South Hilo District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void situated below
a foundation causing settlement and or foundation failure we recommend
that the foundation subgrades be probed The probes should consist of a
3 inch minimum diameter hole drilled to a depth of 10 feet below the
bottom of the footing Probes should be drilled at each major footing
location and on 10 foot centers along continuous or strip foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout 1 sand
to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage fashion
i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of some of the proposed structures
will be interior concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions
may be supported by dense basalt clinker or compacted fill A 4 inch
layer of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel should be provided below the slabs as
cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane is recommended
below the slabs

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the intent of the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

Special Considerations
A
Cut slopes shall be excavated to the lines and grades indicated
on the contract drawings and where necessary adjusted in
accordance with the recommendations of the Geotechnical Engineer
during construction
All trench backfill placed above 12 inches of the top of the
pipes shall be compacted in maximum lift thickness of 8 inches
unless specifically authorized by the Geotechnical Engineer
The Geotechnical Engineer may require thinner lifts should
compaction operations consistently fail to produce the specified
compaction requirements In any event the required levels of
compaction shall be obtained throughout the full depth and width
of each layer of backfill
Jetting and ponding of the backfill or fill materials shall
not be permitted on this project unless specifically authorized
by the Geotechnical Engineer in writing
Upon completion of grading or temporary interruption of grading
and the subsequent termination of observation by the
Geotechnical Engineer no further filling or excavating shall be
performed without notifying the Geotechnical Engineer in advance
and ascertaining his requirements

III Site Preparation
A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be
disposed of in accordance with Section 10 CLEARING AND
GRUBBING of the Standard Public Works Specifications
All vegetation and deleterious materials encountered within the
Grading Limits shall be cleared and disposed of off site This
removal shall be completed prior to excavating and filling
Organically contaminated topsoil shall be stripped prior to
actual grading Materials determined as being unsuitable for
placement in compacted fill shall be removed and wasted
off site Any material incorporated as a part of the compacted
fill must be approved by the Geotechnical Engineer
Any abandoned underground structures such as cesspools
cisterns tunnels septic tanks wells pipelines or others not
located prior to grading are to be removed and the resulting
depression backfilled and compacted in accordance with these
specifications
Loose surface soils encountered in areas to receive fill shall
be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be observed and approved by the Geotechnical
Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks or
lumps in excess of twelve inches 12 in diameter
Representative samples of the materials to be used as compacted fill
shall be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the
proposed materials shall be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least
seven working days prior to its intended jobsite delivery

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
E
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around then
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2132 10 Page 8
Appendix B Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and
number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill

The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted access
to the fill to perform the compaction tests
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous
fill surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
The Contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it
will be free draining and will not pond surface water
