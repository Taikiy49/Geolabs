INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of the Mokuleia Sands Condominium Association
of Apartment Owners we have drilled installed and tested two 2 new injection wells at
the subject site
The approximate location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
to the Mokuleia Sands Condominium Association of Apartment Owners dated
March 4 1993

This report summarizes our findings from the drilling installation and testing of the
new injection wells at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Mokuleia Sands is a residential condominium project located on the northern shore
of the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is in an unsewered area and utilizes on site
treatment and disposal to handle domestic sanitary wastes generated in the residential
complex The existing treatment plant is a package aeration unit which has been in
operation for approximately 17 years We understand that the existing wells have lost a
significant portion of their disposal capacity of treated effluent from the treatment plant
An application was submitted to the State of Hawaii Department of Health for
approval to construct four 4 new injection wells We understand that two 2 wells will
be installed and tested The remaining two 2 wells would be drilled and installed only
if additional capacity is needed

GENERAL INFORMATION AND SITE DESCRIPTION
Mokuleia Sands Condominium is located on the northern side of the town of
Waialua on the island of Oahu Hawaii The wells are situated on the parcel identified by
condominium site
The injection wells are located at the back yard of the Mokuleia Sands
Condominium It is bounded on the southwest by a masonry wall on the northwest side
by a wooden fence and on the northeast side by an existing building The general
locations of the two new wells on the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is nearly level with existing ground surface elevation of about

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in the Mokuleia area in the Waialua District on the northern
shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii The climate of the Mokuleia area is mild with an
average annual temperature of about 78 F and an average annual rainfall of about 40
inches per year
The site is situated on a coastal plain adjacent to the northwestern end of the
Koolau Range The topography of this coastal plain is a gentle slope down towards the
east from the mountains to the ocean
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and
Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end of its activity before Koolau
As volcanic activity on the island of Oahu drew to a close the island began to
subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing coral algal reefs began to grow on
the seaward margins of the island
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell in response to
the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the global sea level fell below the
existing level and as the glaciers melted and retreated the sea level rose to heights above
the current stand of the sea

Subsoil and Foundation Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to a depth of
about 61 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a layer of brown sandy silt with coral
gravel to a depth of about five feet 5 below the existing ground surface Underlying this
layer our borings encountered medium dense coral sand with gravel to a depth of
approximately 25 feet Below this layer a layer of sandy coral gravel was encountered
to the maximum depth explored of approximately 61 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater was encountered in both borings at a depth of about nine feet 9
below the existing ground surface
Since the injection wells are situated in the back yard of the existing building and
the foundation aspects of the building were addressed in separate geotechnical
engineering efforts foundation considerations are not considered to be of significance for
the injection wells
Earthquake Conditions
The City and County of Honolulu formally adopted the 1988 UBC with amendments
in which assigned Oahu to seismic zone 2A Since no new structures will be directly with
the wells seismic conditions are not considered to be significant
Land Use
The site is an area which is designated for residential use The site is currently
used for these purposes and the wells are for replacement of the failed component of an
existing treatment facility Therefore construction of injection wells at the site is consistent
with this use

SYSTEM OPERATION
It is proposed to use the new wells for the disposal of treated domestic sewage
Prior to disposal the sewage will be treated at the existing wastewater treatment plant
The plant is a package plant which is generally of extended aeration design The plant
is supposed to provide secondary level treatment
The plant capacity is about 20 000 gallons per day with an average flow on the
order of 11 gallons per minute and a design peak flow of 120 gallons per minute
Since the system of wells appears to provide more than adequate disposal
capacity there is no additional standby system nor are monitoring wells planned
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The site is located at Latitude N and Longitude W The
ground surface elevation at the site at the time of well drilling and testing was
approximately 10 feet Mean Sea Level
The logs of the drilled injection wells are presented in Appendix A Generally the
wells encountered clayey silt overlying coralline sands and gravels
The shallow groundwater in the area is brackish water in a sedimentary caprock
aquifer Instrument readings taken in the new wells indicate that the conductivity of the
groundwater ranges from about 1 000 to about per centimeter
PERMEABILITY TESTING
When the new wells had been drilled and cased to their intended depths the wells
were developed by air lifting and tested by the injection of relatively clear fresh water
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3057 00 Page 8
These tests indicated that the injection formation has a coefficient of permeability k in
the range of about 100 to 200 feet per day
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the injection of
Clear fresh water from a nearby fire hydrant at variable rates for a period of approximately
12 hours per well
During the injection of water the changes in water level in the wells were recorded
with respect to time and rate of injection The results of these tests are presented in
Appendix B
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled wells and pump tests
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until operation of the injection wells
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report This data has
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors
