Additional Foundation Recommendations
MSERW No 5
Kahekili Highway Widening
Likelike Highway to Vicinity of Haiku Road
Based on a site visit on September 25th and subsequent meetings with
representatives from the State Department of Transportation SDOT on September
26th and October 4th we are providing the following additional geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the foundation of MSERW No 5 in Valley No 1 of
the subject project
Site Visit
On September 25th the Resident Engineer of the subject project requested that
a representative of Geolabs Hawaii observe the exposed subgrade conditions at the
bottom of MSERW No 5 in the vicinity of to The exposed
subgrade was reported to be at an elevation of approximately 128 feet MSL Based
on the exposed subgrade soil conditions the subgrade soils consisted of a dark gray
silty clay of soft to medium stiff consistency Because of the disturbed surface of the
exposed subgrade a test pit excavation of about 4 to 5 feet deep was made to observe
the consistency of the silty clay subgrade soils
Based on the test pit excavation and some field tests performed on the sides of
the exposed subgrade soils we estimated the undrained shear strengths of the
subgrade soils to vary from 600 to over 1 000 p s f In our original slope stability

Discussions at Meetings
Following the site visit on September 25th a meeting was held on September
26th to further discuss the foundation soil conditions at MSERW No 5 At this meeting
concerns were raised with regard to the stability of MSERW No 5 considering the
medium stiff subgrade soils exposed between and In addition
concerns were also raised with regard to the bearing capacity of the softer soils given
the high bearing pressures of the MSERW No 5 of about 7 500 p s f The high bearing
pressure is a result of the heavier backfill material 145 p c f used for the select
granular backfill material required for the MSERW structure
Geolabs Hawaii recommended an allowable bearing pressure of up to
6 000 p s f at the location of MSERW No 5 based on a composite foundation material
consisting of the improved soil removal and replacement with rock fill zone and the
silty clay subgrade soils In addition our slope stability analyses at this location
indicated safety factors of about 1 5 with respect to global stability based on an
undrained shear strength value of 850 p s f for the subgrade soils We also conducted
a parametric study to evaluate the effects of a lower undrained shear strength of 400
500 and 700 p s f for the subgrade soils The geometric configuration for this study
was based on the configurations shown on the plans Our parametric study indicated
safety factors of at least 1 3 for overall slope stability

Recommendations
Based on our observations from the site visit and discussions in meetings with
representatives from SDOT we recommend some changes to the original design as
shown on the plans be implemented at MSERW No 5 to improve the bearing capacity
and the overall stability of the MSERW No 5 structure These additional recom
mendations are the result of the exposed subgrade soil conditions observed softer with
a large amount of deleterious organic matter and the significantly higher bearing
pressure of the MSERW structure due to the much heavier backfill material Our
additional recommendations are summarized as follows
1 Move the limits of the removal and replacement zone to extend between
