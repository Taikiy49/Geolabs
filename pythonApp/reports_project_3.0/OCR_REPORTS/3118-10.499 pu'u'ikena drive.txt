INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Wha Lin residence at 499 Pu u lkena Drive
within the Hawaii Loa Ridge Development on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single family residence at 499 Pu u ikena Drive within
the Hawaii Loa Ridge residential development on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on the preliminary information provided to us the subject lot is
approximately square in shape with an area of approximately 8 875 square feet The
proposed residence is anticipated to be a two story structure Detailed design information
pertaining to the architectural and structural aspects of the proposed residence were not
available to us at the time of preparation of this report However construction of high
retaining walls for grade separation are planned
The existing ground elevations estimated from the Topographic Survey Worksheet
by Engineers Surveyors of Hawaii dated range from about 493 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL at the southern corner of the site to 595 feet MSL at the northern
corner of the site Site grading will essentially consist of some minor cuts along with fills
up to 18 feet behind the proposed retaining walls

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 499 Pu u lkena Drive within the Hawaii Loa Ridge
residential development on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is approximately square in shape covering an area of about 8 875
square feet The subject property is presently vacant except for the presence of newly
constructed retaining walls at the rear and sides of the lot Vegetation consists of a
sparse cover of low shrubs and grasses growing in the surficial soils
At the time of our field exploration on November 22nd a large quantity of
overcast material was evident on the steep slope adjacent to the street This material
consisted of a reddish brown silty clay with erosional gullies draining downslope In
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 5
addition a stockpile of concrete blocks and other bouldery rubble was noted near the
base of the steep slope
The overall terrain at the project site slopes in a southerly direction The steeper
portion of the slope adjacent to the street approaches H V whereas the eastern
portion of the property is typically a 4 1 H V slope The existing ground elevations range
from 493 feet MSL at the southern corner to 535 feet MSL at the northern corner of
the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling five test pits
to depths ranging between 2 and 6 feet below the existing ground surface
In general our exploration encountered fill approximately 1 to 4 feet thick
consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clay material with some gravel The southern corner of
the property appeared to contain the greatest thickness of fill material These fill soils are
underlain by highly decomposed basalt material saprolite consisting of very stiff clayey
silts and locally relatively unweathered basalt rock formation extending to the maximum
depths explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration Groundwater levels may be expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time
of year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration indicates that portions of the site are underlain by a surficial
layer of stiff to very stiff fill soils of approximately 1 to 4 feet thick consisting of silty clays
with some gravel and cobbles The placement of this fill material was not observed nor
documented by Geolabs Hawaii
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered below the upper
surficial fill at the project site it is our opinion that conventional spread and or continuous
footing foundations may be used for support of the proposed single family residence if
they are founded on this underlying in situ reddish brown residual soil and weathered
rock material or on the compacted select granular backfill behind the retaining walls
Laboratory tests performed on the on site silty clay soils indicate that the material
generally has a high shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a layer of
capping fill consisting of non expansive select borrow to reduce the potential effects of
the highly expansive near surface soils Detailed recommendations are presented in the
following subsections
Retaining Walls
High retaining walls are planned to create a building pad and yard area at the
project site The proposed retaining walls may extend to heights of approximately 23 feet
to support backfill for the finish floor grade General guidelines for the design of the
retaining wall structures are as follows
1 The high retaining walls should be founded on either in situ saprolite soils
or in situ basalt rock formation An allowable bearing pressure of up to

5 Directly behind each retaining wall a subdrain should be constructed to
protect against build up of hydrostatic pressure A typical drainage system
would consist of a 1 foot to 2 foot wide zone of permeable material No 3B
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately adjacent to the
wall with a 4 inch diameter perforated PVC pipe perforations down at the
base of the wall discharging to a discharge facility A filter fabric such as
Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be placed between the drainage material
and the compacted backfill material The upper 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce surface water
infiltration behind the walls
6 Retaining wall backfill should consist of imported granular material such as

select backfill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding about
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
level and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 78 Over
compaction of the retaining wall backfill should be avoided

Foundations
Detailed information pertaining to the structural loadings are not available to us at
the time of preparation of this report Spread and or continuous footings may be used
for support of the proposed new residence provided they are founded on competent in
situ residual and basalt materials or select granular engineered fill
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of footing foundations bearing on stiff in situ soils or the select granular
engineered backfill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
On level ground the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade For sloping ground the footing should extend deeper
to obtain a minimum 8 foot setback distance away from the face of the slope We
estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing
on stiff in situ soils or the select granular backfill to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
If foundations are located next to utility trenches easements or retaining structures
they should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging retaining structures with additional loads
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 10
footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on either the undisturbed
stiff clayey soils or engineered select granular backfill If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should either be backfilled with granular
material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom
be extended down to the undisturbed firm in situ soil Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing
conditions and the embedment depths
Slab On Grade
The project site is underlain by a deposit of silty clay soils which are considered
to be moderately to highly expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless
floor slabs supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and
swelling of these soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential
for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately expansive soils
we recommend that the concrete slabs be supported on 18 inches of non expansive
select fill material The non expansive select fill should extend at least 2 feet beyond the
perimeter of the proposed structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 11
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The prepared capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report The select fill would serve as a
capping fill layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils
and this capping fill would also reduce the potential loss of moisture from the prepared
moist subgrade soils The reduction in future subgrade saturation should reduce the
potential swelling of the underlying moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction
requirements for the subgrade soils and select fill are discussed in the Site Grading
section of this report
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be installed below the slab to provide uniform
support for the slab
To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recom
mended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be placed
above the plastic membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid in curing
of the slab concrete
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading for the proposed project will consist of minor cuts
with fills up to about 18 feet to achieve the design finish floor elevations for the project
construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 12
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
After demolition clearing and grubbing all subgrade areas beneath future slabs
and pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 18 inches below the
future slab or pavement subgrade level such as the bottom of No 3B Fine gravel
cushion layer or the bottom of base course layer If zones of soft loose or saturated
soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may
be required to expose firm material The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
select granular fill in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to obtain 90 percent minimum
relative Compaction density The clayey material resulting from the over excavation
should be removed and properly disposed of off site
Subgrade areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to at
least 85 percent relative compaction Select granular fill material should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction
Select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such as
crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 1 inches in largest dimension The material
should have a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the
No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended
use by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site

Pavement Design
Concrete pavement for the access driveway is anticipated for the project
construction We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement
designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will consist of at least 18 inches of
imported select granular fill material The imported select granular fill material should be
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils below the
select granular fill material should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement sections be used for preliminary
design purposes
6 0 Inches Concrete
6 0 Inches Base Course 95 percent compaction
12 0 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on 18 Inches of
Compacted Select Granular Fill
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density tests should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy
of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping
in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade soil below the base course aggregate to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 14
performance we recommend a_ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about 1 foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material The
backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch loose level lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3118 10 Page 15
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

used
