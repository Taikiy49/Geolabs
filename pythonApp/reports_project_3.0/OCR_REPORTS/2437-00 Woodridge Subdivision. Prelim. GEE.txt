INTRODUCTION
This report represents the preliminary findings of our recent
geotechnical engineering exploration performed to update the design of
development at
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed residential development

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of twenty one lots on either side of
Alawiki Street and one lot on the mauka side of Hui Io Street in the
Ahuimanu area of
The site has been previously graded with the western portion of the
site cut to provide fill material for the downslope lots along the eastern
side of the street Prior to filling the site was cleared and grubbed
The fill was then placed in level lifts and compacted to a minimm of 85
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Grading work
was suspended prior to the completion of final grades Our exploration
has indicated that some of the surface soils on the top and face of the
fill slopes on the eastern portion of the site are not compacted
The existing previously graded ground surface elevations of the site
range from approximately 280 to 110 feet above Mean Sea Level

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is currently proposed to construct two story wooden framed
residences on the previously graded lots at the project site Post and
beam construction is anticipated for the houses and slab on grade for the
attached garages
It is anticipated that the existing elevations at the project site
will be altered by less than two feet during final grading prior to
building construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated at the base of the Koolau Pali a high
cliff which extends along most of the windward side of the Koolau Range
The climate along the Pali is typified by a high to very high average
annual rainfall
Following the main shield building stage of Koolau volcano in the
late Pliocene early Pleistocene stream erosion became the dominant
agent affecting the morphology of the area
The project site is located in a major amphitheater headed valley
along the two ridges of a drainageway Along these ridges in situ
chemical weathering of the parent Koolau Basalt has formed deep residual
soils and saprolites On the side slopes of the ridges and in the floor
of the drainageway mass transfer materials such as alluvium colluvium
and landslide debris have accumulated Some of these transported
materials are sufficiently old enough to have been subjected to in situ

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored recently
by drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 16 5
and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface with a truck mounted drill
rig Two additional hand probes and surface sampling of soils were also
performed Data obtained from previous subsurface exploration in the area
was also reviewed to augment our current information
In general the borings encountered stiff clayey silts and silty
clays to the maximum depths drilled Colors of the soils ranged from
browns to reds with frequent mottled areas resulting from the deep
chemical weathering which has occurred at the project site
No groundwater was encountered in any of the borings drilled during
our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided that
our recommendations are followed However the wet soil conditions and
high to very high average annual rainfall at the project site will require
special remedial earthwork design and special construction methodology
Continued Site Grading
For the remaining minor grading to be performed the on site soils
if available appear suitable for use as engineered fill However the
project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site materials
also have very high in situ moisture contents of about 15 to 35 percent
above optimum Therefore some drying of excavated materials will be
required prior to their use as fill and or backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2437 00 Page 5
Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the on site soils and
the high amounts of rainfall occurring in the area it is our opinion that
the compaction requirement for this residential project may be reduced
from the standard of 90 to a minimum of 85 maximm dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Even with this reduced 85 minimm
compaction requirement we believe substantial moisture reduction would
still be required along with the use of light compaction construction
equipment
If additional or drier imported materials are required the material
should generally be non expansive able to be compacted in a dense stable
mass and have a minimum California Bearing Ratio of 15 when tested in the
laboratory in accordance with ASTM D 1883
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness Fill placement should commence at the lowest
point and continue upward in level compacted layers
Where the buildings extend into the cut slopes in the rear of the
lots Parcels 26 and 36 a retaining wall should be constructed to
support the vertical cut of the slope These retaining structures should
be designed to allow for proper drainage and disposal of surface and
subsurface water from behind the wall Ponding and seepage in the rear
lot area should be diverted away with either proper swales or drainage
pipe outlet
It appears that the proposed slab on grade garage for Parcel No 41
extends out over the slope along the north edge of the lot It appears a
retaining wall is needed The required perimeter retaining wall footings
in this area should be extended downward below the slope surface to a
depth such that the bottom of the footings are set back a minimum of eight
8 feet from the face of the slope

House Foundations
It is our understanding that post footings will be used for the
proposed residences Slab on grade with thickened edges are proposed for
the garages An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas and away from the slope bank
should be embedded a minimm of twelve 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be deepened a
minimum of four 4 feet or down an additional amount to obtain a 8 foot
minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the outer edge of the
footing out to the slope face This additional embedment depth is
required to help resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop
the recommended bearing capacity of the soil Footing excavations at the
top face and bottom of the fill slopes on the east side of Alawiki Street
should be observed by a geotechnical engineer during the excavations to
verify that proper embedment and bearing soils have been obtained
Slab On Grade
To reduce the potential for post construction shrinkage of the
underlying soils we recommend that a minimm 12 inch thick perimeter
thickened edge footing be utilized along the on grade slab All backfill
around the thickened edge footing should be properly compacted to obtain a
85 compaction density
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The surface and subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four 4 borings to depths ranging from 16 5 to 26 5 feet below
the existing ground surface with a truck mounted drill rig In addition
two 2 hand probes and selective sampling of surface soils at near final
subgrade was performed The locations of the borings and hand probes are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Plate A 5 describes the materials encountered in the hand probes
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of
thirty 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sample the last twelve
12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
