INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Electric Power Plant Expansion
Phase I FY89 MCON Project P 950 at PWC Subic Bay Republic of the
Philippines
The general location of the project site is shown on the Area Map
Plate 1 and Project Location Map Plate 2 The general layout of the
proposed power plant structures and the 69 KV overhead line is shown on
the Site Plans Plates 3 and 4 respectively
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
The purpose of our exploration was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the project site to provide pertinent soil data for the site
grading and foundation design of the proposed power plant structures the
69 KV overhead line and related site improvements
The scope of the exploration was performed in general accordance with
our revised proposal dated for the power plant structures
and our proposal dated for the overhead line The
evaluation included a review of available aerial photographs geological
literature and previous soil reports of the general area field
reconnaissance drilling of seventeen 17 borings and excavation of three
3 test pits for the power plant structures and drilling of eight 8
borings for the overhead line laboratory soil testing engineering
analysis of the field and laboratory data and report preparation
The boring and test pit logs as well as the results of the field and
laboratory test data are presented in the appendices of this report

METHODOLOGY OF EXPLORATION
General Methodology
Available topographic maps aerial photographs geological and soil
literature and relevant existing technical reports were used in a
preliminary office study to attempt to identify pertinent geological
features and to assist in the planning of the subsequent field exploration
at the project site and its vicinity
The field exploration included drilling and sampling of borings
excavation of test pits in situ field vane shear testing and groundwater
level observation
Laboratory tests were carried out on selected soil and rock samples
obtained from the field exploration to classify and estimate the
engineering characteristics and properties of the various soil and rock
strata These data were assessed quantitatively and applied to subsequent
geotechnical analyses to provide site grading and foundation design
recommendations for the proposed construction
Aerial Photograph Interpretation
From the study of the available aerial photographs and topographic
maps of the project possible existing localized fills major
topographical depressions or natural drainageways and areas of surface
erosion were identified These findings were subsequently checked on site
by field observations of engineering geological features
Engineering Geological Mapping
Preliminary field mapping was carried out in conjunction with the
field exploration to determine the general geology and soil conditions
Based on this information some of the boring locations were revised to
optimize the amount of data that could be obtained by drilling and
sampling

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located within the Western Volcanic Intrusive
Province of Tuzon and comprises Recent to Pliocene Quaternary Volcanic
deposits The apparently E W Trending San Antonio Fracture Zone is
probably the closest identified major fault zone about 8 to 10 miles
northerly of the site
At the power plant project site the volcanic deposits are of
pyroclastic origin and primarily consist of volcanic breccia agglomerate
believed to have been generated by explosive eruptions from Mount Natib
Mount Santa Rita and Mount Balakibok three of the major volcanic centers
which comprise the Zambales Bataan Volcanic Highlands
The volcanic breccia generally consists of angular to subangular
gravel to boulder size fragments of basalt andesite and tuff in a weakly
to well cemented silty sandy ash matrix
Numerous glacial eustatic sea level variations caused the emergence
and submergence of the land mass that produced soil deposits at various
levels both above and below the present sea level
Examination of the geomorphology of the area leads to the conjecture
that the project site is located on an erosional volcanic remnant within a
marine deltaic flat The lowlying area was cut during low stands of the
sea by streams originating in the mountainous areas to the east of the
site
The field exploration revealed that the surface materials in the
lowlying portions of the study area along the 69 KV overhead line are
mainly derived from a low energy depositional estuarine or lagoonal
environment which would be encountered in a sheltered bay area such as
Subic Bay

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Structure Siting
Originally the proposed power plant structures were planned in lower
portion of the area south of the existing Muse Power units However at
the start of our field exploration program a check of PWC utility plans
revealed an existing 10 inch Air Force fuel line passes below the
originally proposed building and Tank No 2 sites
From the pre design meetings at Subic with the user the Base PWC and
the design team it was decided that the fuel line should be left in place
as is and the new Power Plant building would be relocated either on the
high ground where the Muse units are located or at another location away
from the 10 inch fuel line in the lower area presently used as a
baseyard Since the final locations of the structures were not known
borings were drilled in both the upper and lower areas in order to
delineate the general subsurface conditions to aid in the final site
selection

If the new structures were sited on the lower areas fills of at
least 10 feet high would be required to raise the area above potential
floods The associated cost of fill embankment would increase the total
site development cost due to the need to import fill material and the
effort required to properly compact the fill to finished grade
If the structures were placed on the higher areas near the existing
power plant most of the fill requirement could be eliminated and a higher
bearing value on rock versus bearing on fill could be used in the design
However the costs of relocating the existing Muse units would have to be
considered for this area
Subsequent to completion of the field exploration it was decided to
site the new power house new substation and the related parking and
pavement on the upper area where the Muse units are located to optimize
the cost and design of the structures
Building Foundation
The borings disclosed that the northern portion of the building site
toward existing power plant is underlain by rock formation near the
surface However at the southern portion of the building site the rock
formation is covered by up to 8 feet of fill Although the borings
generally indicated that the fill is dense there is some concern that
portions of the existing fill may be unsuitable for foundation support
Therefore based on the anticipated design loading and soil conditions we
recommend that spread or continuous wall footing bearing directly on the
hard rock formation be used to support the proposed Power House and
Substation
For footings bearing directly on hard rock formation an allowable
bearing value of 5000 p s f may be used The bearing capacity values are
for total dead plus live load only and may be increased by one third for
short duration wind and seismic loading Estimated footing settlements
under the design loading are negligible

Generator Foundation
The power generator units located within the New Power House will
need special foundation design consideration
Generally speaking a mat foundation bearing directly on the hard
rock formation may be used to support the generators with a static
allowable bearing value of 5000 p s f

existing ground and directly into the underlying hard rock formation The
embedment of the foundation below the upper fill and into rigid bedrock
should help to reduce vertical vibration motions of the generators Care

layer of fi11 above the rock formation as this may cause magnification of

Overhead Line Foundation
Based on the generally loose or soft subsoils encountered along the
proposed alignment we recommend that large diameter caissons be used to
support the 35 power poles of the overhead line to withstand the high
over turning moment requirement The vertical compressive load on the
pole is anticipated to be relatively light and may be supported by the
frictional resistance between the shaft of the caisson and the loose to
soft subsoils
Since the caissons would be subjected to repeated wind loads a
critical consideration would be to limit the permanent lateral deflections
of the caissons This may be achieved by sufficient embedment of the
caissons
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 16
Dk No 39
The lateral resistance of the caissons can be analyzed by using the
appropriate soil modulus values of the subsoils at various depths below
the existing ground surface at the proposed power pole locations A
lateral pier analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear P Y curves to represent the soil modulus values
Since the soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the
pier deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection
is then used to determine the modulus from the non linear P Y curves The
calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The
procedure is repeated until the new assumed and calculated moduli are
essentially the same
Based on the analysis we estimate that one 36 inch diameter caisson
should be used for each pole and the caisson should penetrate a minimm of
15 feet into the existing ground to generate sufficient lateral and
vertical resistance to fulfill the above mentioned design requirements
with a suitable factor of safety
The method and equipment used for caisson construction should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
consideration However due to the loose or soft subsoils and high
groundwater level it is anticipated that construction difficulties may
arise in keeping a drilled caisson shaft open without using a steel casing
prior to concrete placement If a temporary steel casing is used extreme
care must be exercised during removal of the casing while placing concrete
in order to avoid contamination or pinching off of the concrete caused
by inflow of the surrounding soil into the concrete Therefore an
alternative is to install the caisson with a permanent steel casing left
in the ground The steel casing could be driven into the ground first and
the soils within the casing completely removed by drilling and or
jetting The reinforcing cage may then be placed and the pole may then be
placed in the cleared caisson shaft and positioned center and vertical
prior to concrete placement

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seventeen 17 borings to depths of 20 0 to 65 0 feet and excavating three
3 test pits to depths of 3 0 to 6 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on Site Plan 1 Plate 3 One
additional shallow boring 3A was added to obtain samples at critical
depths Eight 8 borings were drilled along the proposed 69 KV overhead
line to depths of 21 0 to 27 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on Site Plan 2 Plate 4 The borings were
drilled with track mounted and trailer mounted equipment by a local
arilling contractor from Manila
The soils encountered in the borings and test pits were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologists who
continuously monitored the drilling and excavation operations The
classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Boring and Test Pit Logs Plates
A 1 1 through 28 1
Rock core samples of the underlying rock formation were obtained by
using a rotary wash NX core barrel
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 0 inch 0 D standard
penetration split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
In the soft sediments a 3 0 inch diameter stationary piston sampler
was used The average hydraulic pressures in P S I required to advance
the tube into the soft sediments are shown at the appropriate sample
depths on the Boring Logs
The field vane shear tests were performed by using a vane with
2 5 inch diameter and 5 inch length The vane was first pushed into the
soil and then rotated The change of the measured torque with angular
rotation was monitored Maximum torques of both the undisturbed and
remolded soil conditions were recorded The test results are summarized
and presented on Plate A 29

The moisture content and unit weight determination tests were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of the moisture content and
unit weight tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Three 3 unconfined compression tests were performed on selected
rock core samples The purpose of the tests was to evaluate the
unconfined compressive strength of the rock The test results are
summarized and presented on Plates B 1 through B 4
Four 4 unconsolidated undrained triaxial shear tests were performed
on selected soil samples The purpose of the tests was to evaluate the
shear strength parameters of the soils The test results are presented on
Plates B 5 and B 8
