Dear Mr Kunioka
This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed in
support of the design of the leaching field system at Pali Golf Course in Kaneohe on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii
Project Considerations
The project involves drainage and other improvements for the Pali Golf Course
which is a municipal golf course facility With the current treatment system wastewater
discharges to an old cesspool system located between Fairway Nos 15 and 16 Planned
improvement will involve the construction of a new leaching field approximately 20 feet
by 100 feet in dimension to replace the current wastewater treatment system The new
leaching field will be located be at the southern side of Fairway Nos 16 and 3
Percolation Test
One borehole was drilled using a Minuteman drill rig equipped with 4 inch
diameter continuous flight augers to a depth of approximately 5 15 feet below the
existing ground surface Soils encountered consisted of reddish brown silty clay with
traces of sand Groundwater was not encountered in the borehole at the time of our
exploration Field percolation test was performed in general accordance with the State
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the
permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The test was
performed in a 4 inch diameter borehole with approximately 1 inch of gravel placed at
the bottom of the borehole to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and
sediments During testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to a depth of
about 6 inches above the gravel and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water
drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation
test was based
