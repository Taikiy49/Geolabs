GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The South Kohala Resort project site is located in the Ouli section of
the District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii and encompasses
approximately 485 acres The site is divided into two areas by the Queen
Kaahumanu Highway The mauka area is bordered to the north by Waiulaula
Gulch to the south and east by raw undeveloped land and to west by Queen
Kaahumanu Highway The makai area is bordered to north by the golf course
of the existing Mauna Kea Beach Hotel to the south by Hapuna State Beach
Park to the west by the northern part of Hapuna Bay and to the east by
Queen Kaahumanu Highway
The subject sewer line crossing is situated in the westernmost portion
of the makai area fronting Hapuna Beach between Sewer Manholes A 6 and
A 7 The ground surface elevations range from about sea level to

approximately 20 feet above sea level with drainage trending towards the

The sewer alignment is vegetated with kiawe algaroba trees Most of
the ground surface is covered with a fine to medium grained beach sand
There are exposures of basalt clinker and rock formation along the eastern
side of the sewer alignment
The primary area of concern is where the proposed sewer line crosses
the course of a major intermittent drainageway This drainageway
terminates to the east of the sewer alignment at the top of a massive
basalt ledge forming an intermittent waterfall The action of this
intermittent waterfall has scoured and eroded the sand creating a small
pond
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site into a planned resort residential
and recreational development The proposed Sewer Line A will be a
gravity collector sewer leading to a pump station The existing scoured
and eroded condition of the drainageway crossing has raised concern about
the stability of the sewer line in this area
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua
Volcanic Series of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene
Stearns and Macdonald 1946
Wave erosion has truncated the seaward ends of these lava flows
forming low near vertical rock slopes along the beachfront Littoral
deposition in the relatively quiet environment of Hapuna Bay has resulted
in the deposition of a fine to medium beach sand at the the base of these
rock slopes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our exploration indicates that the sewer line crossing of the
drainageway area will require some specialized design and construction
The subsoils and rock formation encountered at the site are generally
competent and should provide adequate bearing for the proposed sewer
line However the existing pond will need to be filled to support the
sewer line and the sewer line will need to be protected from future scour
and erosion

Pond Filling
Prior to the start of sewer line construction at the drainageway
crossing the existing pond should be filled between the rock
ledge waterfall to a point at least 20 feet beyond the centerline of the
sewer We recommend that the fill material consist of a self compacting
granular material such as 3B Fine The granular fill should be placed to
a minimum of twelve 12 inches above the high water level in the pond and
trackrolled with a large bulldozer or similar type equipment until the
fill has stabilized
Sewer Line Construction
After the pond area has been filled construction of the sewer line
across the pond area should proceed in the normal manner Based on our
exploration it is anticipated that most of the excavation will be in the
fine to medium grained beach sand or in granular backfill placed as
recommended above Therefore sheeting or bracing of the excavation will
be required The method should be left to the contractor
Our exploration also indicates that the invert of the proposed line
will be below the existing groundwater table It is anticipated that
dewatering of the sewer trench will be difficult It may be necessary to
allow the contractor to construct the line in the wet
Sewer Line Protection
To protect the new sewer line from potential damage by scour
originating from the intermittent drainageway and waterfall in the future
we recommend that the drainage course be lined with a GRP apron with a
minimum thickness of two 2 feet This apron should be keyed into the
existing rock ledge and should extend a minimum of twenty 20 feet beyond
the centerline of the new sewer
The apron should be constructed to channelize the drainage towards its
center to reduce the potential of erosion of the sand along the sides of
the apron by intermittent drainage flows The sides and seaward end of

APPENDIX A
Laboratory Testing
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths ranging between 21 0 and 30 0 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
In addition the subsurface conditions were explored by the
excavation of three 3 test pits to depths ranging between 4 5 and 8 0
feet below the existing water or ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling and excavation operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring and Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
