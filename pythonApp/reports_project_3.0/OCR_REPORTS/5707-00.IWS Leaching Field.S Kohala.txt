In accordance with our proposal dated we performed field
percolation testing within the South Kohala Baseyard in South Kohala on the Island of
Hawaii This report summarizes our field exploration and percolation testing at the
proposed leach field location and presents our findings and discussions for the
proposed project These findings and discussions are intended for the leach field design
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that a new leach field will be constructed to replace an existing
cesspool within the South Kohala Baseyard The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
To evaluate the permeability of the underlying ground surface a percolation test
was conducted at the new location The result of the percolation test will be used to
assist in the new leach field design The new leach field location is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Further details of the proposed leach field were not available at the time this
report was prepared Based on our knowledge and experience in the project vicinity we
anticipate that project site may be underlain by volcanic ash soils consisting of clays
and silts overlying weathered basalt rock formation at deeper depths

The purpose of our work is to obtain information pertaining to the permeability of
the underlying ground surface to formulate a summary of the soil rock conditions for the
leach field design In order to accomplish these objectives we conducted an exploration
program generally consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring
designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 10 feet below the existing
ground surface Boring No 1 was drilled at the proposed leach field location using a
truck mounted drill rig The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The material encountered in the boring was classified by visual and textural
examination in the by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Log of Boring Plate 4

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
A field percolation test was performed in the boring in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the
subsoil profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the
percolation test was performed at 5 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately 1 to 2 inches of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the borehole
to protect the bottom of the hole from scouring and sediments During the test the hole
was carefully filled with water to a height of 6 inches above the gravel pack The time
intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the
percolation rates was based Results of the percolation test are presented on Plate 5
LABORATORY TESTING
One Grain Size Distribution test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a
selected sample of the subsurface soils to evaluate the partical size distribution and to aid
in soil classification Results of this test are presented graphically on Plate 6
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration at the new leach field location encountered
volcanic ash soils consisting of very dense sandy gravel extending to a depth of about
7 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below the volcanic ash soils basalt rock
formation was encountered extending to the maximum depth explored of about 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The basalt rock was generally medium hard
We did not encounter groundwater in the boring at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are presented on the
Log of Boring Plate 4

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that it is planned to construct a leach field within the baseyard
property Therefore we have conducted a field percolation test to obtain data pertaining to
the preliminary leach field design One percolation test was performed at the proposed
leach field location as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the test result we believe that the leach field location at a depth of about
5 feet below the existing ground surface is suitable for siting the proposed leach field The
percolation test was performed in volcanic ash soils consisting of sandy gravel Based on
the result we recommend using a percolation rate of about 13 minutes per inch for the
leach field design Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity
of the disposal system should be confirmed by conducting additional percolation tests
during construction
In addition based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department
of the leach field should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater level
As previously mentioned we did not encounter groundwater in our boring at the time of
our field exploration
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field boring and percolation test Variations of subsoil
conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided herein
The field boring location indicated in this report is approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by the County of
Hawaii Department of Public Works on The physical boring location
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
