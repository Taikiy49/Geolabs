PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located along Kaumuali`i Highway and Hanepepe Road in 
Hanapepe-Ele`ele area on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. The project involved reorganizing the existing water system, including the 
following. The project limits are illustrated on the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
• Kaumuali`i Highway 16-inch main and emergency booster pump connection 
• Hanapepe Road 6-inch main replacement 
The new 16-inch water main is PVC pipe approximately 3,000 linear feet long with 
inverts ranging from about -1 to +70 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The new 6-inch water main 
replacement consists of the following components with inverts varying between -3 and +10 
feet MSL. 
12-inch Ductile Iron Pipe Class 52 about 1,500 linear feet long 
12-inch TR Flex ductile iron pipe about 350 linear feet long 
12-inch C-900 DR14 PVC pipe about 750 linear feet long 
Dewatering operation was conducted for sections of the new waterlines where the 
design inverts were below the groundwater level to provide construction access. Goodfellow 
Bros. Inc. was the general contractor and performed the earthwork for the project.

EARTHWORK OPERATION 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the project 
site and some imported materials. In general, fill materials consisted of materials less than 
3 inches in maximum particle size.  Fills and backfills required to raise the grades were 
generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned and 
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. The compaction requirement 
for the areas under areas subjected to vehicular traffic was increased to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the roadway areas, the subgrade 
was moisture-conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum 
dry density. 
During the earthwork construction, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results 
are summarized on the attached Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and 
soil conditions were noted. A total of 472 field density tests were performed using the nuclear 
probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Compaction test results are summarized 
on Plates 4.1 through 4.36. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces 
and on constructed fills and backfills. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes and topographic features. These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the earthwork operation for the period covering October 2, 2018 through 
August 12, 2021. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it is 
our opinion that the earthwork construction for the Hanapepe-Ele`ele Water System 
Improvements project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, 
was performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications 
and the recommendations in the following references.
