INTRODUCTION
This report contains results of our reconnaissance of the subject project site and
recommendations for the stabilization of the wetlands prior to filling
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the Kuilima Resort in the Kahuku and Kawela areas
on the North Shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to use these wetlands
for resort development Site preparation for the development will result in about nine 9
acres being completely or partially filled
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kuilima Resort development areas in the
Kahuku Kawela areas on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is generally bound by
Kamehameha Highway on the south the Pacific Ocean to the north Kuilima Estates East
to the west and Marconi Road to the east
The terrain is flat and generally at elevations less than five 5 feet above Mean Sea
Level MSL California grass and bushes are the predominant vegetation with ironwood
and other trees growing in some areas Standing water is present in the lowest
elevations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our reconnaissance was to obtain an overview of the shallow soil
conditions at the site to develop a soils data set for the formulation of geotechnical
recommendations pertinent to the proposed fill areas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our reconnaissance encountered thin surface layers of soft
compressible soils underlain by more competent material The soft surface soils generally
ranged from about six 6 inches to three 3 feet in thickness
It is our understanding that all of the wetland areas will be filled to a minimum
elevation of eight 8 feet MSL It is our further understanding that there will be at least
one 1 year between the placement of the fills and construction of roads structures or
infrastructure In view of this it is our opinion that the majority of the new wetland fill
settlement will have occurred during this minimum one year period The remaining long
term residual settlement should be relatively small after construction of roads
infrastructure or structures To further verify this wetland fill settlements we recommend
that a number of settlement gauges be installed to further monitor the actual fill ground
settlements
Stabilized Gravel Blanket
To stabilize the existing soft surface soils we recommend that a stabilized gravel
blanket be installed at the base of the fill prior to placement of fill The stabilized gravel
blanket should also consist of a layer of Mirafi 140 N covered with a minimum of 12
inches of 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 Areas to receive fill should be mowed
or lightly grubbed without disturbing the underlying soils prior to placement of the blanket
In areas where there is standing water or where water is encountered during
grubbing the thickness of the 3B Fine gravel should be increased to provide a minimum
of 12 inches of gravel above the highest water level which could be assumed to exist
about 2 7 feet MSL or to about an elevation of 3 7 feet MSL
The stabilized gravel blanket will aid in drainage of the subsoils and will aid in
establishing a capillary break to minimize the potential of saturation of the subsequent fill
soils

Fill Settlement Gauges
To monitor settlements of the new fills we recommend that settlement gauges be
installed on top of the gravel blanket layer prior to the placement of the new fills The
gauges installed at the base of the fills as shown on Plate 1 must be protected from
damage during earthwork operations The project specifications should stipulate that the
contractor is responsible for maintaining the gauges The fill settlement gauges should
be monitored via optical survey equipment and the gauge should be monitored on a bi
weekly basis for the first two months followed by a monthly survey on the referenced
points marks placed on the installed gauges We recommend that the optical survey be
made for a minimum period of about one year after fill completion
Future Field Exploration
There is a possibility that some of the fill areas may be developed in the future for
structural uses such as resort or residential construction We must caution that the
presence of soft compressible subsoils even when partially consolidated could still
produce sufficient future fill ground settlements which could damage the surface ultility
structures sensitive to these long term settlements Therefore we recommend that site
specific exploration be conducted for specified structural areas when details of the future
use are available This exploration would allow the development of additional
recommendations for the design of the future site grading and building foundations to
minimize any adverse effects from the possible presence of soft subsoils
It is anticipated that variations in conditions will be encountered during the
earthwork Therefore we recommend that the grubbing installation of the stabilized
gravel blanket and fill placement operations be conducted under the observation of the
project geotechnical engineer This will allow verification of the assumptions made to
formulate our recommendations and permit adjustment in the design if necessary

LIMITATIONS
The results of this field exploration are limited by these factors
1 Any heavy rains or absence of rain will affect the areas of standing water
2 The results are limited by the actual traverse of the explorations All the
grounds were not traversed
3 Areas of standing water are assumed to be underlain by soft clays silts
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from visual observations and field data points such as hand auger
probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction commences
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report This data has
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors
