REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
The project site is located on the southern flank of Koolau and its geomorphology
and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea
level during the Pleistocene Epoch and the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The
subsurface conditions within the project area are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age
coral algal reef deposits and related deposits laid during high stands of the sea which are
intercalated with terrigenous sediments The base of the stratigraphic section in this area
is Koolau Basalt
During the later Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes
as a result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world These
glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower
relative to the present sea level on Oahu About 15 000 years ago a relatively rapid rise
in sea level occurred During that rise the valleys in the project area were drowned In
the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand Terrigenous
and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine or lagoonal
environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments along ihe coast in the
areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways

With the urbanization of the area man made fills were placed to reclaim marshy

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Our subsurface exploration program consisted of field reconnaissance floor level
survey and drilling and sampling of six test borings
Our field level survey was conducted in a computer storage room adjacent to a
computer room located on the west corner of the building as shown on Plate 2 Building
8816 Floor Plan Based on information provided by personnel at Honolulu Community
College the floor level of the computer room was raised about 12 inches many years ago
due to severe differential settlement Five 5 feet of an interval grid system was used for
our level survey for Building 8816 A reference point was set on the east corner of the
computer storage room Based on our survey results a maximum of 11 25 inches
differential settlement was detected between the east and west corners of the room
Detailed survey results are presented on Plate 3 Building 8816 Floor Survey Grids Plan
Our floor level survey was also conducted in the Men Women s restrooms located
on the west of Building 8805 Random survey points were selected and results are
presented on Plate 4 Based on our level survey a maximum of 0 75 inch differential
settlement was measured in the mid portion of the Men Women s restrooms
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings for each building extending to depths of approximately 26 5 to 41 5 feet
below the existing ground surface For Building 8805 Boring No 1 was located outside
ihe building area on the west of the structure near the restrooms Boring No 2 was
located inside the Women s resiroom and Boring No 3 was situated in the courtyard of
the building near the restrooms For Building 8816 Boring No 4 was located outside of
the building adjacent to the cracked CMU wall on the west side of the building Boring
Nos 5 and 6 were positioned within the Computer Storage Room The borings were
drilled using truck mounted and portable drill rigs equipped with 6 inch hollow stem augers
or a combination of 4 inch solid auger and 4 inch HQ casing
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil

Subsurface Soil Conditions
Our borings for Building 8805 indicate that the site is generally underlain by
medium dense or stiff surface fills extending to depths of about 3 5 to 6 feet below the
existing ground surface The surface fill layer is generally underlain by thick soft or loose
lagoona deposits extending to 23 to 34 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
lagoonal deposit is generally underlain by medium stiff to stiff alluvium and some coralline
detritus extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 41 5 feet below the
ground level Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 8 to 9 feet
below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that a
1 inch void was observed below the concrete floor slab in Boring No 2 It should also be
noted that about 3 feet of marsh type materials consisting of silty clays and organic clays
were encountered in Boring No 2 at about 7 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface
The marsh type materials are usually highly compressible
The Building 8816 site is generally underlain by very stiff to hard surface fills
extending to about 4 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The surface fill layer is
generally underlain by a thin layer of marsh type materials consisting of soft to medium
stiff silty clays about 2 to 3 feet thick Below these marsh type materials a thick lagoonal
deposit was encouniered extending to depths of about 20 to 36 feet below the existing
ground surface Alluvium and coralline detritus were encountered below the lagoonal
deposit and extended to the maximum depth drilled of approximately 41 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about
9 to 11 feet below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be
noted that very soft to soft lagoonal deposit consisting of silty clay was encountered in
Boring No 5 4 feet thick at a depth of about 13 feet below the existing ground surface

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on ihe results of our field exploration and laboratory tests soft clayey soil
materials were present at the depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface We believe that consolidation of the soft clayey soil materials is likely near
completion However small on going and or continuing settlement should still be
anticipated We recommend that the existing slab in the area of the restrooms of
Building 8805 and the computer storage room of Building 8816 be removed and replaced
with reinforced concrete slab overlying new lightweight fill In addition measurement of
continuing settlement may be performed immediately as precaution especially for
Building 8816 The following sections presents details of our recommendations

Building 8805
As mentioned previously Building 8805 was constructed in 1962 and is supported
on pile foundations Based on our field exploration a thin layer of marsh deposit underlies
the Building 8805 site Organic clay material was encountered in Boring No 2 where
localized ground settlement was observed at a depth of about 8 5 feet below the ground
surface However the organic clay soil was not encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 3 Our
laboratory tests indicated that the marsh type material is slightly over consolidated while
the organic clay material is slightly under consolidated In addition a 1 inch void under the
concrete slab was encountered in Boring No 2
lt appears that consolidation of the organic material has occurred resulting in
ground settlement since completion of the building construction evidenced by the 1 inch
void under the concrete slab in Boring No 2 There is no evidence that the organic
material extends to the locations of Boring Nos 1 and 3 Therefore we believe that
settlement in this area appears to be localized within the restrooms area Our
consolidation tests indicated that the soil sample is normally consolidated or slightly
under consolidated Therefore we believe that the consolidation of the organic material is
likely near completion
On this basis we believe that further settlement of the area would be small in
magnitude Therefore we recommend that the existing slab in the area of the restrooms
be removed and replaced with a new reinforced concrete slab If new fills are to be placed
below the slab we recommend thai the fills consist of lightweight fills such as lightweight
foam material In addition consideration may be given to the use of lightweight concrete
in lieu of conventional concrete Efforts should be made to keep the weight of the
replacement slab and underlying cushion maierial lighter than the materials removed to
reduce the potential for inducing additional settlement at the site
Building 8816
We understand that there are no as built plans available for Building 8816 Based
on conversations with personnel at Honolulu Community College we assumed that the
building was constructed in the early 1930s Based on the subsoil conditions indicated by
our drilling exploration and our field observation we believe that the building is likely
supported on pile foundations As mentioned previously marsh type materials were
encountered in our borings About 5 feet of soft lagoonal deposits consisting of silty clays
with some coralline sand were also encountered in Boring No 5 at a depth of 13 feet
below the ground surface In addition the mezzanine structure may have been
constructed in the settlement areas approximately 20 to 30 years ago We believe that the
mezzanine structure was supported directly on the slab on grade
Our laboratory consolidation test indicated that consolidation of the soft soils is near
completion However we understand that there have been some reports that new cracks
still appear on the CMU end wall located on the west side of Building 8816 and or slabs

We believe that relatively new surcharge loading from the mezzanine structure applied on
the soft marsh material may accelerate the ground settlement and cause continuing cracks
on the CMU end wall
Based on ihe available subsurface information we believe that the on going and or
continuing settlement of the ground should be small Therefore we recommend that the
existing concrete slab be removed and backfilled with lightweight fill and lightweight
concrete slab The lightweight fill and concrete slab will keep new overburden pressure on
the soft soils less than or equal to the original overburden pressure so that it would reduce
the potential for appreciable additional settlement Because of small on going and or
continuing settlement anticipated we believe that the CMU end wall may be repaired
Alternatively if the wall needs to be removed new wall should be supported on a deep
foundation system such as the mini pile system
As a precautionary measure we also recommend that the existing slabs and walls
be monitored for movements immediately to confirm our assessment In this manner we
believe that the reported cracks and our findings may be further confirmed for additional
implementation
