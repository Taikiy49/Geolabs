INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
design and construction of the foundations for the proposed
structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the north side of Lilipuna
Road approximately 150 feet east of the intersection with
Wailele Road The site slopes up toward the north at gra
near the northern half of the site
Some erosional gullies and surface sloughing of the northern
steeply sloping area were noted at the time of our field
exploration
Along the western boundary of the site the ground surface
drops off steeply near vertical in places up to a maximum
height of about 15 feet
Near the eastern site boundary the ground surface slopes down
at gradients of about 2 1 H V into the adjacent properties
The site is surrounded by residential developments

Most of the site ground cover consists of various trees and
grasses The project area is sloping with ground elevation
ranging from 100 feet near Lilipuna Road up to an elevation
of 284 feet at Puu Pahu a concrete surveying monument at
the north east corner of the project
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed development consists of eleven 11 separate
townhouse structures as shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate
1 The townhouses will be two and three story structures of
wood frame construction Two access roads off from Lilipuna
Road are planned along with the necessary parking areas for
each building site
Site grading will involve fills up to 14 feet high Antici
pated cuts will be up to about 10 feet high for the upper
northern area Site grading for the lower buildings was not
available at the time of our investigation

Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of about 16
feet below the existing ground surface to explore the subsur
face conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate l

logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed essentially uniform shallow
SOil conditions at the site The test borings encountered
a stiff to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt at the surface
down to 16 5 feet the maximum boring depth drilled
In Boring Nos 1 and 5 the clayey silt grades to a mottled
reddish brown color
In Boring No 2 at the upper northeastern corner of the Site
a very dense gray brown color and partially decomposed rock
was encountered one foot below the reddish brown clayey silt
material
Groundwater was not encountered in our test borings at the
time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent foundation bearing
soils at the proposed project site From a soil engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed cluster
development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 990 00 P 5
In general shallow foundations can be used to Support the
proposed structures except where they are situated on or
near steeply sloping areas On these sloping areas parti
cularly near the northern portion of the Site deeper pier
type footings are recommended
Laboratory swell tests on the on site Soils indicate low to
medium expansion potential therefore Slab on grade should
be designed for these low to moderate expansive soil conditions
Under the present grading scheme fills are planned over most
of the upper northern portions of the site Retaining walls
up to 14 feet high are planned at the garage locations
and around the lower perimeter of the parking area in order
to support these fills Due to the sloping ground condition
at these wall locations deeper footing embedment about 3
feet deep is required for these walls to help resist downhill
Slope creep and to develop the required bearing capacity
of the soils In order to reduce the amount of retaining
walls required an alternate grading scheme utilizing cut
in this upper area instead of filling in order to create a
level site could be considered
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the structures on level or gently sloping areas An allow
able bearing value up to 3500 p s f is recommended for footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 990 00 P 6
bearing on the stiff residual soil and 2500 p s f for footings
bearing on compacted fill The above values are for total
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary loads are considered
On level areas the bottom of the above designed footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside
finished grade to resist erosion develop the bearing capacity
of the subsoils and to reduce the effects of the moderately
expansive soils
Footings located on or near the tops of steep slopes will be
subject to erosion and slope creep of the surface soils
Therefore these footing should be embedded an additional
amount to help resist downhill movement Footings on the
sloping areas of the site should be embedded a sufficient
depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
which is measured from the outer edge of the footing out to
the slope face
Along the western boundary of the site the ground surface
drops off abruptly for a vertical height of about 5 to 10
feet Townhouse structures should be set back away from
this near vertical slope a minimum horizontal distance equal
_ to one and one half 1 5 times the vertical height of the
drop off should be provided in order to minimize the possibility
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 990 00 P 7
of loss of ground support for the structure due to future
soil erosion and surface sloughing of the vertical over
steepened bank
The footings located near the vertical drop off bank should
have sufficient footing embedment per above recommendations
Settlement
Anticipated settlements for foundations bearing on the natural
ground or properly compacted fill are estimated to be on
the order to 4 inch total and inch differential
Lateral Design
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination of
passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be taken
as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 350 p c
Friction may be taken as normal dead load times a coefficient
of friction equal to 0 4
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Site Preparation and Earthwork
Surface water should be diverted away from the building sites
Cuts and fills generally less than about 14 feet are antici
pated for the general project site grading Based on the
boring data relatively easy site excavation can be antici
pated except in the northeastern portion of the site near

Boring No 2 where weathered rock may be encountered
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to
properly bond the new fill to the Slope The filling opera
tion should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level compacted layers in accordance with the above struc
tural fill requirements For construction details refer
to Plate 2
The reddish brown on site soils have low to moderate expansion
potential and are acceptable fill and footing backfill material
if they are compacted on the wet side of optimum moisture con
tent Any additional off site borrow required should be
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to transpor
tation to the site The borrow material should be non expan
sive and should not contain boulders organic or deleterious
material
Slopes
The on site soils are generally silty and erosive as evidenced
GEOLABS HAWAII

by the existing eroded banks around the site
It is also difficult to mulch or plant these Slopes if they
are cut too steep and this will also increase erosion poten
tial For these reasons 2 1 H V fill Slopes and 14 1
H V cut slopes are recommended for the site
All fill slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and
cut back to obtain a well compacted Slope All fill slopes
should be properly keyed and benched as outlined under Site
Grading
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Slab on Grade
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have moderate swell potential therefore slab on grade should
have deeper perimeter footings extending down a minimum of
12 inches below the finish grade to reduce ground movement
due to drying and wetting around the slab All slab subgrade
should be kept adequately moist prior to concrete placement
to minimize future heaving
It is recommended that slab on grade should be placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum
dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 990 00 P 10
The subgrade should be protected from drying and kept moist
during construction It should be compacted toaminimum of
90 of maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls will
help to prevent seepage water
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls up to 14 feet high are planned around the
lower perimeter of the upper pavement area in order to retain
the proposed fill If practicable the site grading should
include the filling of all backfill behind the high retaining
walls and that a steeper 1 1 temporary fill slope ratio may
be used along the retaining wall area Well graded granular
material such as base course rock should be used as back
fill material behind the walls and the cut fill bank
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet of
the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows

Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equivalent

Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge

A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 350 p c f can be utilized However
the passive resistance should be reduced by one third when
combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Driveway and Parking Area Pavement
Asphaltic concrete driveway and parking areas are planned
adjacent to the proposed units on fairly level areas
Portions of the access road will have steep grades and
will require concrete pavement
The laboratory CBR tests indicate that the near surface
on site soils have moderate expansion potential especially
when it is used as a compacted fill This swelling charac
teristic would require thicker pavement sections on the sub
grade of fill area

The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by a soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate bearing and com
paction have been obtained and that the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
