PRELIMINARY RECOMMENDATIONS
Fill Embankment
It is Our opinion that both the upstream and downstream slope of the proposed fill
embankment can be constructed at a slope ratio of two and a half horizontal to one
vertical
It is our understanding that the downstream fill slope will be constructed with the
slope toe at elevation 540 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL The crest of the fill
embankment will be at elevation 600 feet above MSL Therefore the maximum slope
height of the downstream slope will be sixty 60 feet We recommend that a minimum
eight 8 foot wide slope bench be provided near mid height of the downstream slope
The slope bench should be graded to drain surface runoff away from the bench
The toe of the upstream slope will be at an elevation of about 580 feet above MSL
with a maximum slope height of about twenty 20 feet Therefore it is our opinion that
the upstream slope can be constructed without a slope bench
A minimum twenty 20 foot wide crest is recommended for the fill embankment
Surface drainage of the crest preferably by sloping the crest to drain towards the
upstream slope should be provided The surface of the crest should consist of concrete

Fill Materials
It is Our opinion that select excavated on site materials generated by the golf
course mass grading should be adequate as a source of embankment fill materials The
stockpiled quarry waste from the existing adjacent quarry should also be suitable as fill
materials In general the fill materials should consist of select well graded granular
materials with a maximum size less than six 6 inches and less than twenty 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
It is recommended that fill materials should be tested by the geotechnical engineer
to determine if it is suitable for its intended use prior to placement
The excavated basalt materials may not have sufficient fines to produce a well
graded fill material Therefore some mixing and handling of the excavated materials may
be required
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose
thickness The placed fills should then be compacted to a minimum of 95 of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Due to the dry and windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fill materials will
be necessary
Fills placed on existing slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm and dense in situ residual soil or
weathered volcanic formations to lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 for
details The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and subsequent cutting back to the
final grades in order to provide a well compacted fill slope face

impervious Liner
It is our understanding that impervious synthetic liners will be provided for the
proposed Lake D It is our opinion that the material type performance installation and

Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the impervious liner should also

saturation and buildup of seepage pressures in the embankment
We recommend that a drainage layer be provided underneath the impervious liners

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Proposed Irrigation Lake Embankment which forms a part of the
Makakilo Golf Course development The project site is located on the eastern slope of
Puu Makakilo in the Ewa District of leeward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and are not
intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a fill embankment in an existing gully to impound water
for irrigation purposes as part of the Makakilo Golf Course development
The lake to be impounded behind the proposed fill embankment is known as Lake
18 in the golf course development planning It is our understanding that the current
design of the fill embankment is based on a premise of flawless performance of a
permanent manufactured impervious liner to be placed at the bottom and on the sides
of the impounded lake The purpose of this liner is to assure watertight integrity of the
lake sides and bottom thus precluding infiltration and seepage of water through the fill
embankment Therefore there is no provision in the current design for an impervious
seepage cut off zone within the fill embankment as is normally incorporated in more
conventional earth fill dam designs This greatly simplifies construction of the
embankment fill We do however recommend that provision be made for a drainage

The pond behind the proposed fill embankment will have a roughly triangular

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs
Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of the Waianae volcano along the
southern rift zone
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events which produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into weak to strong rock
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally Puu Makakilo
is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles most of the site This residual soil grades rapidly to
the underlying volcanic materials

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and geotechnical engineering analyses it is our
opinion that construction of the proposed fill embankment is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view The weathered basalt formations encountered at shallow
depths within the project site should provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed fill embankment

The excavated granular materials generated from the on going mass grading of the
golf course development if reduced to less than 6 inches in maximum dimension and
having less than twenty 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve should provide suitable
fill material Fills on the existing relatively steeply sloping terrain should be properly keyed
and benched to bond the new fills to the existing slopes
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Embankment Geometry
It is Our recommendation that both the upstream and downstream slopes of the
proposed fill embankment be constructed at slope ratios of two and one half horizontal
to one vertical as indicated on the grading plans provided to us
It is our understanding that under the current design the downstream fill slope will
be constructed with the toe of the slope at an elevation of approximately four hundred
ninety 490 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL The crest of the fill embankment will be
at an elevation of approximately six hundred 600 feet above MSL Therefore the
maximum vertical height of the downstream slope will be one hundred and ten 110 feet
We recommend that a bench with a minimum width of eight 8 feet be provided near
mid height of the downstream slope The slope bench should be graded to drain surface

Fill Materials
It is our opinion that selected materials generated by excavation for the golf course
mass grading should provide an adequate source of embankment fill material The
stockpiled quarry waste from the existing adjacent quarry should also be suitable as fill
material In general the fill materials should consist of select well graded granular
materials with a maximum size of less than six 6 inches with less than twenty 20
percent passing the No 200 sieve These requirements may necessitate processing
crushing and or screening of the quarry waste material if it is selected for use
Samples of the existing stockpiled quarry waste and the materials excavated from
the driving range of the golf course were obtained for laboratory tests The test results
are presented in Appendix B of this report
It is recommended that prior to placement the selected and or processed
materials should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to determine if they are suitable
for the intended use Routine sieve analysis tests should be performed for the proposed
fill materials to evaluate their grain size distribution characteristics A minimum test
frequency of one 1 gradation test per 10 000 cubic yards of material is suggested
The excavated basalt materials may not have sufficient fines to produce an
acceptably well graded fill material If this is found to be the case and it is proposed to
use these materials in the embankment fill it may be necessary to add finer material with
thorough mixing to produce an acceptable mixture of uniformly well graded material This
process requires strict attention to construction quality control
Fill Placement
The embankment fill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12 inches
in loose thickness The material should then be compacted to a minimum of 95 of the

Due to the dry and windy site conditions it is important that proper moisture

on the wet side of the optimum moisture content rather than drier than optimum to facilitate
the required compaction
Fills placed on existing slopes having slope ratios steeper than five horizontal to

Impervious Liner
It is our understanding that a permanent impervious synthetic liner will be provided
for the proposed Lake 18 It is our opinion that the material type performance
installation and protection details of the impervious liners should be designed by the liner
manufacturer It is critically important that the liner maintain its integrity to prevent
saturation and buildup of seepage pressures within the fill embankment
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicates that there is
sometimes a tendency for the liners to become detached at the top of the water
impoundment It appears that this may be the result of inadequate anchoring of the top
of the liner and insufficient flexibility of the liner material It is our recommendation that
the liner manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction detailing and
installation of the liner with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 30 Page 13
of liner failure Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the impervious
liner near the top anchoring should also be evaluated
Although the synthetic liners are considered to be impervious there is a possibility
of seepage due to punctures in the liner or breaks in the seams Leakage through the
liner could potentially cause piping losses or weakening of the subgrade soils and
subsequently lead to liner failure and or embankment slope instability Therefore we
recommend that a drainage layer be provided underneath the impervious liner to collect
and drain possible leakage through and or seepage underneath the liner Due to the
sloping ground condition a synthetic drainage material such as Tensar DC1200 or
equivalent is recommended as an economical alternative to conventional gravel layers
Technical representatives of the manufacturers of the impervious liner and of the
drainage layer should be required to be on site at all times during their installation to
assure that proper construction procedures and precautions are followed A sales
representative of the supplier s may not have the technical expertise to adequately
perform this function
Subdrains in the existing natural drainage area are also recommended to relieve
potential seepage through the drainage layer and subgrade soils The subdrains should
be constructed in a herringbone pattern and be daylighted to drain outside the fill
embankment The approximate locations and details of the recommended subdrains are
shown on Plates 4 and 5 respectively

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4 borings to
depths of fifty 50 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations

The material encountered in the borings and test pits were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored
the drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the
materials encountered in the test pits is presented on the Test Pit Log Plate A 5 1
Samples were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques Core drilling is a
rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock
material left in the annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
Classification and testing The recovered cores were logged by a geologist or engineer
from our firm Descriptions of the recovered rock cores are presented on the Boring
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative rock
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX C
Stability Analyses
Stability of the fill embankment was analyzed by means of both sliding and
rotational stability analyses The critical sliding surface derived from the results of the
sliding stability analyses is presented on Plate C 1
Rotational stability analyses of the embankment slopes were performed by using
a computer program STABR developed by the University of California at Berkeley The
program calculates the factors of safety of circular slip surfaces by using both the
Ordinary Method of Slices and the Bishop s Modified Method The results of the
rotational stability analyses are presented on Plates C 2 and C 3 respectively
