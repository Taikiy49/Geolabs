INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed multi purpose dining room structure at
Mililani Waena Elementary School Waipio Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions at the proposed site and to determine the site grading and
foundation design requirements for the proposed construction

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is presently planned to construct a 60 foot span rigid steel
frame one story building wood sheathing and structural steel purlins
Site grading involves mainly 1 5 to 2 5 feet of fill required to
bring the finished slab up to an elevation of 724 50 feet
Concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the floors
SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing Mililani Waena Elementary School is located in
Waipio in central Oahu Hawaii The proposed building will be
located adjacent to the existing library building to the east and the
kitchen facility to the south The general location of the project
site is shown on Plate 1
GEOLOGY
The site is situated in the Schofield Plateau formed by the
basaltic flows of the Koolau Volcanic Series during the Pliocene Age
The flows reach a thickness of 3100 feet and are composed of Pahoehoe
and A a lavas ranging from 10 to 80 feet in thickness The reddish
brown clayey silt soils from the site belong to the Wahiawa Series
which is derived from in situ weathering of basic igneous rock

SOIL CONDITIONS
Surface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4
borings at the approximate locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan
Plate 2
A detailed description of the field exploration and laboratory
testing data along with the boring logs are attached to the appendices
of this report
The borings revealed that the site is underlain by a 3 to 7 foot
recent fill layer consisting probably of the on site reddish brown
clayey silt soils It is underlain by residual Wahiawa soils which
are very stiff to hard mottled reddish brown to brown Clayey silts
Groundwater was not encountered in the test borings
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed building site
is underlain by fill materials varying in thickness from 3 to 7 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on the proposed finished
floor elevation of 724 5 feet it appears that the western portion
of the building area will require 2 to 4 feet of additional fill to
raise the site to the required grade Shallow spread footing
foundations bearing on compacted fill may be used to support the
proposed building provided that the recommendations presented
herein are closely followed

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Sie tee ee
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Following
the clearing the ground surface should be scarified to a depth
of 6 inches moisture conditioned to optimum moisture and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with
select on site material or non expansive import fill material
compacted to the above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement
It is anticipated that imported fill material will be required to
raise the site to the proposed finish grades Imported fill
material should be non expansive free of organics and other
deleterious materials and generally less than 6 inches in size
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1250 00 Page 5
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as specified above
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Foundations
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
Footings for the multi purpose dining room structure should be
placed a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
In order to provide uniform support for the foundations we
recommend that the fill materials below the footings be overexcavated
a minimum of 18 inches below the bottom of the footing The resulting
excavation should be backfilled with select on site materials placed
in 6 inch lifts and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density See
Plate 3 for details
For foundations bearing on recompacted fill as specified above
an allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f may be used The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1250 00 Page 6
If a footing is situated adjacent to a utility trench the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used between the foundation and recompacted fill Additionally a
passive soil pressure of 300 p c f may be used to calculate lateral
resistance
Estimated total and differential settlements for footings bearing
on fill recompacted as specified above are anticipated to be less than
one half of an inch
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
Slabs on Grade
Slabs on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to act as
a capillary break and to provide uniform support
Should an impervious membrane be incorporated below the slab to
act as a vapor barrier one inch of moist sand should be placed on top
of the membrane to aid in concrete curing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1250 00 Page 7
The subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
Design Review
Plans for foundation and site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and back
filling operations be observed by the soils engineer during construc
tion This is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural
soil The test results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded
and undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils in natural and compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plate B 3
One consolidation test was performed on a selected
undisturbed sample to evaluate the settlement characteristics of
the on site soils The test result is presented on Plate B 4
