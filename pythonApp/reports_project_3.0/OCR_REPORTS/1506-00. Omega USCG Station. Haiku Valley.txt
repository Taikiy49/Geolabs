This report presents the results of our probing exploration and
percolation tests performed at the Omega USCG Station in Haiku Valley on
the island of Oahu Hawaii It is our understanding that a replacement
septic tank and leaching field are being considered as one of the
alternatives to alleviate operational difficulty encountered with the
existing sewage system The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
Purpose and Scope
For the design and feasibility of leaching field construction the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the project site were
investigated by hand augered probings at six locations at the site
W O 1506 00 Page 2
A series of percolation tests was then performed in the augered
probings to evaluate the permeability characteristics of the upper
3 5 foot soil layer The approximate locations of the probings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
According to the Engineering Study of the Sanitary Sewer System at
the U S Coast Guard Navigation Station a new septic
tank with leaching field is being considered as one of the acceptable
alternatives to upgrade the existing sewage disposable system at the
project site For this purpose an area located about 300 feet downslope
from the existing septic tank is being considered for the leaching
field This area is generally shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
According to the above referenced submittal it is our understanding
that a new leaching field with a dimension of about 20 by 100 feet in
plan 2 000 square feet is being proposed next to Haiku Road It
further stipulated that a new leaching field should be designed to
dispose of 2 000 gallons of sewage effluent per day Thus such a design
capacity has been assumed
Site Description
The proposed leaching field is situated on an area of about 3700
square feet between Haiku Road and a drainage gully in the upper portion
of Haiku Valley about 1400 feet easterly and downhill from the Coast
Guard transmitter Helix Building
The ground surface slopes down towards the east at about 7 to 9
percent gradient The area is overgrown with brush and small trees

Subsurface Soil Conditions
To examine the area for a leaching field six hand augered probings
were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 The probings generally encountered a surface fill layer about 1 to
2 5 feet of brown silty clay with gravel underlain by fill material
consisting of tan white silty sand and coral fragments to about 2 to
3 foot depths Underlying these fill layers was brown silty clay with
gravel alluvium to about 4 foot depth the maximum augered depth in
Probing No 2
Groundwater was not encountered in the probings during our
exploration
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the probings
together with the results of maisture tests are shown on the Probing
Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Several laboratory mechanical sieve analyses were performed on the
soil samples recovered in the probings to examine their grain size
distribution The test results are presented on Plate B 1

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our field percolation test results it appears that the
percolation rates of the soil at the site are marginal to fair This is
further confirmed by visual examination of the soils encountered in the
probings and the laboratory grain size analysis results Although it
appears that the underlying silty sand fill layer could provide fairly
good percolation rates for sewage effluent disposal this layer is very
thin 1 to 2 feet and therefore has limited total capacity The
capacity of the leaching field would be governed by the clayey alluvial
soils underlying the sandy deposits which have percolation rates that
appear to be marginal for disposal Based on the percolation test data
obtained in the drilled holes a drainage leaching field covering a
minimum area of about 5 000 square feet may be required for a leaching
field with a design capacity of design capacity
GEOLARS HAWAH
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors The latter is the most important factor
since an organic mat of several millimeters thick may form at the
soil liquid interface Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some
extent Therefore the leaching field will probably require periodic
maintenance
Investigation Limitations

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are not
intended to be used for final design purposes since they are based on
brief and limited preliminary geologic site reconnaissance of the site
and surrounding area a literature review soil information from a few
random auger probings and limited amount of laboratory tests performed on
shallow surface soil samples obtained from this exploration
Unanticipated soil and rock conditions are commonly encountered and
camiot be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen
conditions such as well cemented soils or clays and silts of very low
permeability may occur in localized areas and may require additional
probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional field
investigation as outlined hereunder are needed before design and
construction of the leaching field The following explorations are
recommended to further determine the feasibility of this project
