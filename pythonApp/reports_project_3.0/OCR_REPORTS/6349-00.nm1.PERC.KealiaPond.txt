Dear Mr West
This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed for the
On Site Wastewater System at the Kealia Pond Building project
Project Considerations
The project site is within the Kealia Pond Wildlife Refuge in the District of Puunene
on the Island of Maui Hawaii As part of the project we understand an individual
wastewater system will be required for the new building The State of Hawaii
Department of Health requires performance of a percolation test to evaluate the
suitability of the site location for use of an individual wastewater system Therefore field
percolation tests were performed at the proposed leaching field location
The project specification Section 3 4 of Division requires performance of
three percolation tests during the construction phase to verify the suitability of the
leaching field subsurface soil material
Percolation Test
Three field percolation tests designated as 2 and PT 3 were
performed at the proposed absorption bed area for the building A site plan showing the
percolation test locations is provided as Plate 1 The field percolation tests were
performed in general accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s
Administrative Rules Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the
Ten State s Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal
of wastewater effluent

The percolation tests were performed in an excavated pit of about 3 3 to 4 5 feet in
depth from the existing surface grade Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at
the bottom of the excavated pit to protect the bottom from scouring and sediments Due
to the silty and weathered rock nature of the on site materials the excavated pit was
pre soaked by placing at least 12 inches of water in the pit and left overnight
During testing the test pits were filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches
above the gravel layer and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water drops were
then recorded to provide data upon which the calculations of the percolation test was
based
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rate at PT 1 is
40 minutes per inch PT 2 is about 12 minutes per inch and PT 3 is about 60 minutes
per inch at the test elevations In accordance with the Manual of Septic Tank Practice
prepared by the U S Department of Health Education and Welfare the selected leach
field location with the percolation rate obtained at the site meets or exceeds the project
design requirements of 60 minutes per inch and therefore would be suitable for the siting
of the absorption system at this location
