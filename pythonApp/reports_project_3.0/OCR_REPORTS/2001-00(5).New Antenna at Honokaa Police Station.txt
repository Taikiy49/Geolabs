In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering evaluation at the above referenced site Our work was done in
general accordance with our proposal of

at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation design of the
proposed antenna
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered medium stiff clayey silt volcanic
ash with high moisture content over basalt at about 7 5 feet below ground
level For the proposed 80 foot high monopole antenna we recommend that
a drilled pier embedded into the underlying basalt be used to support the
structure
Monopole Foundation
Based on the preliminary design drawings supplied to us a drilled
pier of 42 inches in diameter embedded 12 feet below the ground level
has been selected for foundation support
Since the pier would be subjected to repeated wind loads a critical
consideration would be to limit permanent lateral deflections of the
pier This may be achieved by sufficient embedment of the pier
The vertical compressive load on the pole is relatively light and may
be supported by the frictional and end bearing resistance between the pier
and the subsoils
A laterally loaded pier analysis was performed for the pier
configuration used in the preliminary design Detailed descriptions of
the analysis and the results which include various loading conditions are
presented in Appendix c
Based on the analysis we feel that a 42 inch diameter drilled pier
embedded 12 feet into the existing ground and at least 4 5 feet into the
underlying basalt is adequate for the anticipated loading

Drilled Pier Construction
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the
geotechnical engineer in order to see that adequate foundation support is
achieved Since a portion of the loading capacities come from the
frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ soils extra care
should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole selection of concrete
mix design and the placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is
completed is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The concrete
mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to maintain high
frictional contact between the in situ soils and pier concrete The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the
aggregates
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made

misinterpretation of the recommendations

to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions away from the field data points may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by tape
measurement on the site with reference to the preliminary site plan
provided The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data point depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition

APPENDIX
Lateral Load Analysis of Pier
The lateral load analysis was performed using Program PILEDG which is
a microcomputer adaption of a finite difference laterally loaded
pile pier program originally developed at the University of Texas at
Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a
pile pier under lateral loads as a function of depth
The analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear P Y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection
was then computed using appropriate soil moduli at various depths
Since the soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates pier
deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated defection is then
used to determine the modulus from the non linear P Y curves The
calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The
procedure is repeated until the newly assumed and calculated moduli
converge
The results of the analysis are summarized in Plates C 1 and C 2 for
a 42 inch diameter pier embedded 12 feet down under various loading
conditions
