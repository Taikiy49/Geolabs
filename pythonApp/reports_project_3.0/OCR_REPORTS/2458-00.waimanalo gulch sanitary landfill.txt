INTRODUCTION
Geolabs Hawaii under contract to Waste Management International
Inc WMIT has performed quality assurance quality control observation
and testing for the construction of Phase I ash monofill base liner at the
Waimanalo Gulch Sanitary Landfill with the technical assistance of Golder
Associates Inc The general location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Figure 1
The ash monofill base liner section consists of bottom to top
1 An approximately three 3 to four 4 foot thick layer of
compacted soil liner with design permeability values equal to or
lower than
2 A layer of 60 mil high density polyethylene HDPE geomembrane
3 A geonet drainage layer
4 A layer of 16 ounce per square yard non woven geotextile
This report provides a summary of 1 the observation and soil
testing associated with the construction of the soil liner 2 the results
of the geosynthetic material conformance testing and 3 the observation
and testing of the geomembrane liner installation

EARTHWORK
Construction of the soil liner for the Phase I ash monofill base
began approximately on December 1989
It is our understanding that the design of the soil liner calls for a
minimum 3 foot thick layer of compacted clayey subgrade with permeability
values equal to or less than Laboratory test results of
the borrow soil samples provided by another soil testing laboratory
indicated that permeability in the order of may be obtained
if the samples are compacted to a minimm of 90 of the maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557
Compaction of the soil liner was performed with a _ vibratory
smooth drum roller or a sheepsfoot front end roller Geolabs Hawaii
performed compaction tests density and moisture content tests on the
compacted soil liner on an as needed basis requested by Waste Management
The moisture density test results are given in Appendix E
As built drawing of the soil liner is also attached in Appendix E
GEOMEMBRANE INSTALLATION
Approximately 93 663 square feet 10 rolls of 60 mil high density
polyethylene HDPE geomembrane manufactured by National Seal Company of
Galesburg Illinois was delivered to the site Approximately 71 508
square feet of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was used for the lining system
Geomembrane Deployment
Deployment of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane began on
and was completed on Deployment and welding progressed
continuously throughout this period
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 00 Page 6
The deployment of the geomembrane was observed by Geolabs
personnel Observation duties included measurement of the panel
thickness confirmation of panel overlap and a visual observation of the
overall sheet quality A summary of the deployment observations is
included in Appendix F 1
Deployment of the liner began at the southeast corner of the pad and
proceeded to the west and north The slope panels extended a minimm of
fifteen feet beyond the crest of the slopes into the anchor trench The
floor panels extended a minimum of five feet beyond the centerline of the
swale A minimum of five inches of overlap was maintained between
adjoining panels The geomembrane panel layout for the ash monofill base
liner is shown on Figure 2
Each panel of deployed 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was given a unique
panel number which identified that panel within the installation the
panels were numbered sequentially as they were deployed
Each geomembrane panel was observed for overall sheet quality and
measured for thickness as they were being deployed The thickness
measurements were made using a micrometer Five measurements were taken
on each of the leading edge one side and the trailing edge of each
panel The reported thicknesses in Appendix F 1 represent the average of
the five readings All panels had an average thickness equal to or
greater than 60 mils and all individual measurements were greater than 54
mils
Weld Observation
The majority of the seaming of the HDPE panels was accomplished using
fusion welding apparatus Extrusion welding was used to make repairs and
to weld the seams at the T junctions of three panels overlapping each
other The welding was observed by Geolabs personnel on a procedural
basis The entire length of all seams patches or other repairs was
observed either during welding or shortly after completion of the welding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 00 Page 7
Each seam was numbered according to the two panels being welded
together For example seam P1 2 is the seam between panels Pl and P2
Each seam was visually inspected by Geolabs personnel to determine that
the panels were properly cleaned prior to welding and that the weld was
continuous and free of defects A summary of the welding observations is
presented in Appendix F 2
Trial test seams were required of all welders and seaming apparatuses
on a regular basis as specified in the QA manual The welding personnel
each produced a test seam at the start of each seaming period normally
twice a day Four 4 one inch wide test strips were cut from each test
sample for testing Two 2 strips each were tested in the peel and shear
test mode using a tensiometer All test seams were performed in the
presence of Geolabs personnel All welders were required to produce a
trial test seam that did not fail in the weld during testing before they
were allowed to start welding on the liner A summary of the test seams
is presented in Appendix F 3
Destructive Testing
In accordance with the QA Manual and Specification Guidelines
destructive test samples of the welded seams were obtained at a frequency
of one destructive test sample per an average of 500 linear feet of weld
The test locations were selected by Geolabs QA inspectors based either on
the completion of approximately 500 feet of welding or on seaming
observations Once a destructive test location had been located a
12 inch by 48 inch sample centered on the weld was cut The sample was
cut into three equal pieces One section was destructively tested on site
using a calibrated tensiometer provided by National Seal one section
taken for National Seal s archives and the other section was taken for
the owner s archives After the removal of each destructive test sample
the sampled area was patched and the patch seam was non destructively
tested
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 00 Page 8
A total of 10 destructive samples were tested on site using a Wegener
Ultratest 500 tensiometer in accordance with ASTM D 638 The calibration
certificate of the tensiometer is included in Appendix F 5 Failure modes
for test specimens were reported based on the pass fail codes shown on
Figures 3 and 4 The destructive test locations are shown in Figure 2
From each destructive test sample 10 specimens were removed for
testing Five 5 specimens were tested for adhesion peel for both the
inner weld track and the outer weld track with the specified acceptance
criterion being that four out of five test specimens per weld track will
not fail in the weld Five specimens were also tested for bonded strength
shear with the specified acceptance criteria being that four out of five
specimens must have yield strengths greater than 132 pounds per inch and
that failure cannot occur in the weld A summary of the destructive test
results is included in Appendix F 5
Non Destructive Continuity Testi
Non destructive continuity testing of the welded seams were performed
using vacuum box and air pressure testing apparatuses For the vacuum
test a soapy solution was mopped on the seam and the vacuum box was
placed over the seam A vacuum of approximately 5 psi was applied in the
box for a duration of 10 seconds Detected leaks were repaired and
retested using the same procedure The seams that were not vacuum tested
were non destructively tested using air pressure testing For this test
both ends of the seam being tested were sealed and a needle with an
attached pressure gauge and air valve was inserted into the air channel
between the two welds A pressure of approximately 30 psi was then
applied to the air channel and monitored for five minutes If the
pressure drop over the five minute period was less than 2 psi the seam
was considered continuous and defect free If the loss in pressure
exceeded 2 psi the seam was then pressure tested in sections until the
defect was found or the seam was tested using a vacuum box Leaks
detected using the air pressure method were repaired and retested using a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 00 Page 9
vacuum box A summary of non destructive tests is included on the weld
inspection summary and repair summary of Appendix F 2 and Appendix F 4
respectively
Repairs
Each repair to the HDPE liner was given a unique number For each

GEONET INSTALLATION
Approximately 99 000 square feet of geonet manufactured by National
Seal Company was delivered to the project site Approximately 65 250
square feet of geonet was used for the lining system
One layer of geonet was placed over the geomembrane except along the
centerline of the leachate collection swale where an additional layer of
one roll width was placed The geonet panels were bond together using
white plastic ties spaced approximately every five feet End to end
panels were joined by double rows of plastic ties spaced approximately
every six inches The geomembrane was washed prior to placement of the
geonet
GEOTEXTILE INSTALLATION
The geotextile delivered to the site is a 16 ounce non woven
needle punched continuous filament polypropylene fabric manufactured by
Trivera Inc Approximately 99 000 square feet of geotextile was
delivered to the site A total of 72 000 square feet of geotextile was
used in this project
GEOLABS HAWAII

One layer of geotextile was placed over the geonet The geotextile
was continuously sewn on the slopes and heat seamed on the pad floor At
the completion of each day all the placed geonet was covered by geotextile
in order to keep the geonet clean The placement of the geotextile was
completed on
CONCLUSIONS
The activities described above associated with the construction and
the installation of the geosynthetic liner of the Phase I ash monofill
base were observed by Geolabs personnel with technical assistance from
the Golder Associates Inc Based upon our observations and the results of
the testing reported herein it is our opinion that the above mentioned
liner system has been installed in substantial compliance with the design
drawings and specifications and WMI s Quality Assurance Manual and
Specification Guidelines except as noted otherwise
