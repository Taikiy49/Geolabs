2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui Tertiary
Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene Epoch
The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these two
volcanoes
The rocks of the West Maui Volcano have been divided into volcanic series The
Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and is comprised of the basaltic lava flows and
associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major shield volcano The Wailuku
Volcanic Series was covered by thin discontinuous frosting of andesitic and trachytic
flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as the Honolua Volcanic Series After a
long period of inactivity where erosion incised deep valleys into the volcanic flows
volcanic activity returned with the eruptions that produced the flows and cones of the
Lahaina Volcanic Series
The East Maui Volcano better known as the Haleakala Mountain makes up the
eastern portion of the island Haleakala Mountain is considered to be dormant with the
last eruption taking place on the southerly side of the mountain around 1790 Haleakala
Mountain consists largely of the Honomanu Volcanic Series that built the mountain from
three rift zones probably during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs
The project site is situated on the western flank of the West Maui Volcano The
processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows to be highly
weathered and mantled by residual soils and saprolitic soils The coastal portions of the
West Maui Volcano are flanked by fringing reefs and alluvial deposits

3 2 Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the proposed new
locker shower room building floors As mentioned previously the project site is generally
underlain by a layer of surface fill materials of approximately 3 5 to 8 feet thick Based on
our laboratory test results the fill materials exhibit a moderate expansion potential when
subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future structural distress
resulting from the swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that the slab subgrade be
properly prepared and capped with a layer of select granular fill
In general we recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material be provided below the building slab areas The non expansive select
granular fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the building a minimum of
24 inches or the select granular fill should extend to the edge of the concrete sidewalk or
concrete apron surrounding the building if applicable The non expansive select granular
fill should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We also recommend that the building slabs on grades be reinforced with
reinforcing bars spaced at least 18 inches in each direction in lieu of the standard welded
wire fabric This recommendation is provided to reduce the potential for structural distress
due to differential movements of the slabs due to the nature of the on site soils
In addition we recommend that the subgrade soils below the select granular fill
layer be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction before the placement of the select granular fill Relative compaction refers to

It should be noted that minor differential slab movements between the building slab

Permanent cut and fill slopes at the site exposing the materials encountered in our
field exploration may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and

recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the locker shower room facilities should be sloped to
shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations
should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for
appreciable water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsurface
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to
drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings extending to depths of approximately 10 to 22 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
hollow stem augers Please note that the borings drilled were based on a preliminary site
plan The buildings in the finalized site plan were shifted slightly therefore some of the
borings drilled were not located directly within the footprint of the new buildings
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples to aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under a surcharge pressure The
results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 1
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected
sample of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 2
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation
of the test results is provided on Plate B 3
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the
direct shear test are presented on Plate B 4
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed ona
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 5
