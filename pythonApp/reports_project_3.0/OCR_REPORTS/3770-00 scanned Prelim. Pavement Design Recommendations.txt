Preliminary Pavement Design Recommendations
Proposed Paheehee Ridge Subdivision
Lualualei Waianae Oahu Hawaii
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing performed for the above
referenced project the northeastern portion of the existing Paheehee Road is generally
underlain by dense sandy soils with a California Bearing Ratio CBR of 29 and a swell of
0 35 percent However our field exploration encountered highly expansive clayey soils
with poor pavement support characteristics toward the southwestern portion of the
existing Paheehee Road Laboratory testing performed on the clayey subsoils indicated
a relatively low CBR value of 2 6 and a swell of up to 8 4 percent Considering the light to
medium traffic anticipated for the roadway it is our opinion that the following new
pavement sections may be used for the subject project
From Northeastern Limit Near Puhawai Road to Hakalina Road
2 0 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Basaltic B rse 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
From Hakalina Road to Southwestern Limit Near Puuhulu Road
2 0 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Basaltic Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inches Select Borrow 95 Percent Relative Com ion
26 0 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade

The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
It appears that the portion of the newly paved road is functioning with a 2 inch
asphalt concrete on 6 to 12 inches of coral fill material subbase If funds are available
we recommend that this portion of the existing pavement be reconstructed with the new
pavement section recommended above
