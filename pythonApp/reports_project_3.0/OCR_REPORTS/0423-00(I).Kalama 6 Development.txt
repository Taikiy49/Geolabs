INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional study per
formed on the proposed residential development located in
Kalama Valley
The purpose of this study is to evaluate the feasibility of
the currently planned subdivision concept utilizing additional
soil data obtained from test pits dug on the upper slope areas

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
It is presently planned to develop a total of 236 residential
lots by cutting the upper slope areas and filling the lower
central portion
The proposed grading and layout are shown on Plate 1 The
anticipated residential structures will be 1 and 2 story wood
framed single family structures The building loads are ex
pected to be relatively light
W 1
Page 3
Major cuts will be required for roadways and building lots on
both upper east and west slopes and the underlying cut materials
will be used to fill the lower central portion The center road
way and open channel will be constructed mainly on fill in the
lower central valley area The maximum fill and cut height will
be less than 25 feet
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed development area under study is located on the lower
slopes of the head of Kalama Valley Slope ratio averages from
4 horizontal to 1 vertical on the side of the valley and increased
to about 3 horizontal to 1 vertical at the head of the valley
Alluvial fans and boulder trains control the site topography
Site drainage is by numerous steep gullies in the talus These
gullies coalesce down slope into a small intermittent stream
FIELD EXPLORATION
The recent site investigation consisted of excavating 9 new test
pits The locations of these new test pits along with previous
test pits and boring are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The
new test pit logs and logs of previous investigation are pre
sented in the Appendix
The field investigation was conducted by an engineer from our firm
GEOLOGY SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
Kalama Valley was formed by stream erosion of Tertiary Age Koolau
W 1
Page 4
Range basalt flows The initial period of erosion and valley
carving was followed by several periods of relative rise and
fall of sea level which permitted valley filling with alluvium
followed by subsequent periods of erosion
During a late stage in the valley s development submarine and
subaerial explosions of Koko Head volcano blanketed the valley
with volcanic ash breccia and coral fragments
Much of this erupted debris was subsequently washed into the
valley and mixed with the clay and boulders being eroded from
the valley walls
Finally after the ash deposits had been washed from the valley
walls a one to at least nine foot thick layer of black expansive
boulder clay accumulated on the lower valley slopes Due to the
asymmetrical shape of the valley in cross section this adobe
layer is more thickly developed on the east side of the valley
A large boulder train cohesionless talus was formed in the
last stages of the valley s development at the head of the
valley
Soil Conditions
At the upper slope portion of the valley dark gray adobe clay
was observed as interstitial Fill between boulders This layer
had a thickness of about 1 to 5 feet and is thinning out at higher
elevation
W 1
Page 5
Underlying the adobe clay layer single to mottled colors
of gray orange brown and black tuffaceous silty sandy boul
der clay were noted to more than 19 feet below the existing
ground surface at the west slope and to 16 feet deep at the
east slope in the alluvial fan areas
Highly to moderately weathered basalt formation was encountered
at shallow depth in test pit E F and Fl These test pits are
located in vicinities of visable basalt outcropping along the
property line This outcrop is not evident in the alluvial fan
areas
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
residential development as currently planned provided the pro
ject design and site construction incorporate the recommendations
presented in this report
In general the site is blanketed with highly expansive adobe
clay soil The present scheme is to remove all the expansive
soils at the upper slope areas and utilizing these on site soils
to build up the lower central portion of the valley To stabilize
the lower expansive soil a low to non expansive fill layer is used
to cap off the underlying adobe boulder fills
The proposed upper slope cut will improve the overall stability
of the lower site against creep The complete removal of the
up slope adobe talus toward the rock outcrop will reduce the
W 1
Page 6
down slope driving force on the remaining slope areas Also
the new fill at the lower central area becames a stabilization
surcharge fill against creep at the toe of the slope
The remaining undisturbed middle slope areas between upper cut
and lower fill areas have an average slope ratio of 4 horizontal
to 1 vertical Based on our new calculations this area will
have new factor of safety greater than 1 5 against creeping
It is our opinion that this factor is an acceptable one for
the proposed development

Foundations
Upper Slope Cut Area Standard spread and line footing foun
dation can be used for the proposed residential structure
The footing will bear on dense sandy and tuffaceous boulder
clay matrix or on basalt rock formation Allowable bearing
pressure of 4 000 pounds per square foot may be use Generally
the bottom of all footings should be a minimum of 18 inches
below the finish grade
Lower Central Fill Area In this area most of the foundation
footings will be located on the 3 foot capping of select non
expansive fill soil Slab on grade can be utilized with thicker
perimeter footing which should be found a minimum of 12 inches
below final adjacent grade
Continuous footing should be reinforced with a minimum of two
No 4 reinforcing bars placed at the top and bottom of footings
A bearing value of 2 500 pounds per square foot may be used for
continuous and isolated footings bottomed in fill Continuous
footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
W 1
Page 11
Middle Slope Area The lower slope area between cut slope
area and lower central fill area will remain as it is Be
cause of the surface adobe soil post and beam construction
is recommended for this area
For the light loaded post footing foundation allowable bearing
pressure up to 1 500 pounds per square foot may be used on the
on site adobe clay soil
The bottom of all post footings should embed a minimum of 3 feet
below the finish grade
Due to the expansive nature of the underlying adobe clay soil
footing excavation should not be allowed to dry and crack prior
to placing concrete Visible cracks developed in the bottom of
footing excavations should be closed by soaking Some provision
should be incorporated in footing design to provide positive drain
age away from all building area
Slab on grade should be avoided in this middle slope area
Driveways and walkways constructed upon adobe soil should have
6 inch quarry waste base course above saturated subgrade soil
A C pavement is highly recommended in expansive soil condition
Concrete walkways if they are used should be constructed in
sections and should be individually reinforced with 6 x 6 8 x 8
wire mesh

Utility Line
Subsequent down slope creep movement in the adobe area may deform
utility line Therefore long utility lines should be constructed
in the direction perpendicular to the existing contours
W 1
Page 13
Utility lines placed in the direction parallel to the existing
contour should be located at the upper roadway section where
the fill soil is non expansive or they should be buried below
the upper creep zone which is estimated to be about 3 to 5 feet
Granular material should be used to backfill around the pipe in
utility trench excavation Positive drainage outlets should be
provided in the utility trenches to bleed out potential excessive
amount of water
Utility lines should have flexible joints particularly at points
where the lines are connected to structures
Drainage
Runoff from roofs and paved areas should be collected and drained
to a suitable discharge point Usually drainage is connected to
the storm drainage system Water should not be allowed to pond
adjacent to foundations and to the top of the slopes The con
struction site should continuously be graded to provide positive
drainage away from cut and fill slopes
Rock Disposal
If necessary and if approved by the soil engineer during construc
tion boulders and rock larger than 8 inches in diameter can be
disposed of in site deep fill areas in accordance with the fol
lowing guide lines
Rock and boulders shall be placed in windrows which should not
be over 5 feet in width and not closer than 10 feet between win
drows The height of the windrows should not exceed 4 feet The
W 1
Page 14
maximum size rock acceptable within the windrows should not
exceed 4 feet in the greatest dimension Rock over this size
should be broken down or disposed of in areas other than the
fill
The resultant rock configurations in the windrows will offer
considerable void space between the rocks Granular material
should be used for fill along and over the rock windrows tho
roughly watered and consolidated to insure that all void spaces
between the rocks are filled with granular material Successive
lifts may be made if the fill is sufficiently deep However
the windrows should be staggered horizontally and have a vertical
distance of at least 2 feet between the top of the lower windrow
and the bottom of the next windrow Rock should not be placed
within 10 feet of finished pad grade or above the lowest utility
lines in roadway All boulder disposal should be done under the
constant observation of the soil engineer to assure that these
recommendations are followed Plate C l illustrates the proper
rock disposal procedure

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
GEOLABS HAWATI
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
Specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
III
IV

D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
an diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
