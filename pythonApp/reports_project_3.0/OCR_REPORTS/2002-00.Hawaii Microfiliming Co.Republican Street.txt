INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design and construction of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed Hawaii Microfilming Company building in
the Kalihi Kai area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to obtain soils data for the formulation
of engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed building
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of three 3
borings to depths ranging between 9 8 and 19 8 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of the field
and laboratory data the development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report

The project site is located at 1713 Republican Street in the Kalihi
Kai area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated on the southern or makai side of Republican
Street and is currently occupied by a residential type wooden frame
structure a large boat and various types of rubbish and debris e g

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a microfilm processing plant on the
site The proposed plant will be a single story warehouse structure with
slab on grade floors It is anticipated that floor and column loads will
be light It is proposed to construct a parking area on the Republican
Street side of the site The parking area will be paved with asphaltic
concrete
The current site grading scheme is to raise the grade of the rear
portion of the lot by the placement of approximately two 2 to three 3
feet of fill
SUBSURFACE CON DITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain which
consists of a thick horizontal series of interbedded marine and nonmarine
sediments capping Koolau Basalts The near surface structure at the site
consists of elevated Pleistocene coral reef deposits of variable
consistency and composition
At the project site these reef deposits are overlain by a thin layer
of man made fill from grading during the urbanization of the area
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging between 9 8 and
19 8 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided that
our recommendations are followed However the variable nature of the
upper fill encountered on the site makes its capability to provide spread
footing foundation support questionable We recommend that spread or
strip footings bear on the medium hard coral or on engineered fill
compacted in accordance with the recommendations given below
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared However it is anticipated that
approximately 2 5 to 3 feet of fill will be required in the rear portion
of the lot to raise the grade to match the front portion of the lot

Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The
resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content proofrolled and
recompacted to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 Any rubble or boulders encountered during
the scarification which exceed six 6 inches in maximum
dimension should be removed and discarded Any soft weak or
yielding areas encountered during the proofrolling and
recompaction operations should be overexcavated and backfilled
with select material placed in eight inch thick lifts compacted
to 90 maximum dry density as described above
Imported fill material should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course
dated September 1986
All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding eight 8 to nine 9 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 903 maximum dry density
as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Foundation Support
In order to provide uniform support for the proposed structure we
recommend that the spread or strip footings bear either entirely on
in situ coral formation or entirely on compacted engineered fill For the
design of foundations bearing on the in situ coral an allowable bearing
capacity of 6000 p s f may be utilized For the design of foundations
bearing on fill compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Perimeter foundations should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent outside grade Interior footings should be
embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the slab finish grade
Settlements
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on engineered fill
constructed in accordance with the above recommendations are believed to
be less than one inch total and one half of an inch differential For
foundations bearing on the underlying dense coral formation minimal
settlements are anticipated
We recommend that provisions be made under the bases of process plant
equipment to accommodate these and additional footing settlements which
might occur if levelness of the equipment is a critical consideration
Cavity Probing
Due to the variable nature of coral formations and the common
occurrence of voids and or cavities in such formations we recommend that
a foundation subgrade probing program be implemented Each foundation
column location and on 10 foot centers in strip footings should be

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor s of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions may be supported by
fill placed in accordance with the recommendations presented under Site
Grading above A 6 inch layer of compacted Base Course should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be
kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings or process equipment an impervious moisture
membrane is recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist
fine sand is provided above the membrane to help concrete curing
Pavement Section
It is anticipated that the front portion of the lot will be paved for
use as a parking area and that asphaltic concrete will be used for the
surface course of the pavement Based on our field exploration and
laboratory testing we recommend the following pavement section
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The above recommended section assumes medium traffic adequate
drainage and a subgrade of select borrow or equivalent If any of these
assumptions are invalid the pavement section should be revised as
applicable

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work In addition the
geotechnical engineer should be retained to provide continuous observation
and consultation services during the proofrolling and cavity probing
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
