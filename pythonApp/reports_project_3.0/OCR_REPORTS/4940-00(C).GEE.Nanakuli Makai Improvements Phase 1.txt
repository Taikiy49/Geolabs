EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Nanakuli area in the District of Waianae on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii Phase 1 portion of the project generally extends from Nanakuli
Avenue towards Laumania Avenue for approximately 1 000 lineal feet as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The proposed roadway extension is generally bordered by Nanakuli
Beach Park along the makai side and by the existing OR L Rail and Farrington

existing ground elevations within the new roadway extension alignment for the Phase 1
portion generally range between about 9 and 17 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
LABORATORY TESTING
A bulk sample of the near surface soils designated as Bulk 1 was collected at a
selected location along the new roadway alignment to evaluate its engineering
properties The approximate location of the bulk sample collected is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on the bulk sample to evaluate its pavement support characteristics and the test results
are presented on Plate 3 of this report A Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D
1557 was also performed on the bulk sample to evaluate its dry density and moisture
content relationship The test results are presented on Plate 4 of this report
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site may be underlain by
coral sands and coral formation with pockets of expansive clays in isolated areas Based
on a review of the geology and our experience in the area we anticipate that the subsoils
underlying the proposed roadway alignment could range from Mokuleia clay consisting of
recent alluvium of high plasticity to coral deposits and consolidated terrestrial sediments
in a very dense state with cementation Laboratory CBR tests conducted on the Mokuleia
clays indicate the soils exhibit high swelling potential with relatively low CBR values when
subjected to moisture fluctuations

Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing performed for the project
we believe that the majority of the proposed roadway alignment for the Phase 1 portion
of the Nanakuli Makai Improvements project is underlain by coral sands and coral
formation with pockets of expansive clays Laboratory testing performed on the clayey
soils generally indicated a relatively low CBR value of less than 5 and a swell of over
10 percent Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is our opinion
that the following new pavement sections may be used for preliminary design and cost
estimating purposes
Pavement Design Non Expansive Granular Subgrade Soils
2 5 Inches Asphalt Concrete
8 0 Inches Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
10 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Pavement Design Expansive Clayey Subgrade Soils
2 5 Inches Asphalt Concrete
8 0 Inches Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inches Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
28 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Where expansive clayey soils are encountered at or near the bottom of the
base course layer the pavement subgrade should be over excavated to the coral layer or
to provide the 18 inch aggregate subbase layer as recommended above The
over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If coralline deposits are exposed at or
near the bottom of the base course layer the subgrade should be proof rolled by a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of eight passes to help detect
near surface cavities and or loose zones prior to the pavement construction The
aggregate base course and aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field
density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections The recommended sections
assume that good drainage will be provided for the paved areas

After clearing grubbing and demolition future pavement areas should be

about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density If coralline deposits are exposed at the pavement subgrade
level the subgrade should be proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a
minimum of eight passes to help detect near surface cavities and or loose zones prior
to pavement construction

subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
25 square feet of subgrade area

recommend that the soils be scarified and prepared again as recommended above

Imported fill materials below the subbase and base course layers if required
should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral or
basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger
than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the material should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or
less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should meet the
requirements of Sections 30 and 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications
The materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding
should not be allowed on the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs
are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend
that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soils below the base
and subbase course layers to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement
section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive
water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a
subdrain system be considered along the sides of the roadway
