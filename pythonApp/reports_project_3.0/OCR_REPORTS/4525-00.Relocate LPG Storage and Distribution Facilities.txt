INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the Relocate LPG Storage and Distribution Facilities project located in the Barbers Point
area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of foundations slabs on grade site
preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located off Malakole Street to the west side of the existing parking
lot at Kalaeloa Barbers Point Harbor in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to relocate the existing
facilities of The Gas Company at Pier 38 Sand Island to the Barbers Point area The new
facility at Barbers Point would consist of the following construction and improvements
e Relocation of six existing 30 000 gallon gas storage tanks These tanks are
above ground structures cylindrical in shape measuring approximately 12 feet in
diameter and 48 feet long Based on the information provided each tank will
weigh about 275 kips under fully loaded condition The exact location and layout
of these tanks at the project site was not determined at the time this report was
prepared However we envision that the six tanks will be placed horizontally
parallel to one another and supported by a saddle on both ends We anticipate
that the saddles will be supported on a large strip footing foundation at each end

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
GEOLABS INC
W O 4525 00 Page 5
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials
caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects within the last
century have brought the project site to its present form

of the existing parking lot at Kalaeloa Barbers Point Harbor in the District of Ewa on the

open concrete pavements serving Marisco Ltd to the east and undeveloped land along the
rest of the site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The majority of the project site is presently vacant except along the western
boundary which is occupied by an above ground 30 000 gallon cylindrical storage tank

survey map provided the project site is relatively level with existing ground elevations ranging
from about 6 to 8 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support
of the proposed gas storage tanks and pre engineered building planned at the project site

An allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for the foundation design of
the proposed relocated gas storage tank structures and pre engineered building The
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
However due to the potential presence of soft and or loose subsoils at shallow depths
as encountered in our field exploration a 2 foot thick layer of compacted fill material should
be provided below the bottom of footings for more uniform support The over excavation for
the compacted fill should extend beyond the edges of the footing a minimum of 12 inches
Over excavation for the 2 foot thick compacted fill below the footing bottom may be
terminated earlier if dense coral formation is encountered during the footing excavation work
Prior to placement of the compacted fill the bottom of the footing over excavations should
be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction If soft
and or loose soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade levels deeper
over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material Alternatively the
bottom of the over excavation may be lined with a layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi 600X
or equivalent prior to placement of the compacted fill
The compacted fill may consist of the excavated on site granular soils provided that
the largest rock fragments in the materials are less than 3 inches in maximum dimension and
that the materials are free of vegetation and deleterious materials Compacted fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Foundation Probing
Our previous experience indicate that cavities and or voids are commonly
encountered within the coral formations in Hawaii In addition the coral formation typically
encountered in the Barbers Point area is generally susceptible to the presence of cavities
and or voids Due to the critical nature of the gas storage tanks we recommend that a
program of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation locations be implemented during
construction to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
the cavities below foundations The probing and grouting program would not be required
at the proposed pre engineered building foundations
For estimating purposes we recommend that probeholes be drilled at each spread
footing location one probehole for every 50 square feet of footing area and at 10 feet on
center along wall foundations The probeholes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and
should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation elevation If cavities
and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional
probeholes should be drilled at closer spacings to aid in delineating the vertical and lateral
extent of the cavity and or void The probeholes and cavities discovered should be backfilled
with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures We recommend that a low
strength sand cement grout with a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting
operations In lieu of the sand cement grout fluid lean concrete such as controlled low
strength material flowable fill may also be used for the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting operation
is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a longer lag time between
probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be required in the construction
schedule

Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration results we anticipate that new concrete slabs on grade
for this project will likely bear on coralline materials and therefore we believe that
conventional slab on grade design may be utilized However if clayey or loose soils are
encountered at the subgrade level these soils should be over excavated and replaced with
select granular fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
We envision that concrete slab on grade for the pre engineered building may be
subjected to forklift traffic and or vibrations due to machinery Therefore we recommend that
the building slab for this structure be supported on at least 6 inches of aggregate subbase
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural
slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection
pci may be used for the compacted subbase material Provisions should be made for
proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The
thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Areas adjacent to concrete slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around
the foundation Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report
Site Preparation
As previously mentioned our field exploration disclosed that the project site may be
underlain by loose subsoils and cavities near the existing ground surface Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the project development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris rubbish
old pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
GEOLABS INC
W O 4525 00 Page 12
materiais with the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with
compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and
the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean
concrete under low pressure
The excavated subgrades underneath future slabs and pavements should be
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment a
minimum of 6 passes to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities or loose
zones The vibratory drum roller should be operated at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet
per minute If the proof rolling operations detect a near surface cavity or loose zone the
area should be ripped open cleaned of debris and then backfilled with compacted fill
material or lean concrete
In general the excavated on site granular soils may be re used as a source of fill
material provided that the largest rock fragment is less than 3 inches in maximum dimension
and that the materials are free of vegetation and deleterious materials Select granular fill
should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral and basalt The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should
have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The on site coralline materials should be suitable for use as select
granular fill materiai provided that they are processed to meet the above requirements

Aggregate subbase material should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should

Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes
Where soft and or loose soil deposits are encountered near the pipe invert elevations we
recommend that the utility pipe be supported on an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a non woven filter fabric Mirafi 180N or
equivalent Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of select fill material The backfill should
be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level
loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to
reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Drainage swales should be provided as soon

subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled

infiltration

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths ranging from about 6 5 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface A bulk sample designated as Bulk 1 was
obtained from the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics The
approximate locations of the test borings and bulk sample are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck mounted drill equipment equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on the
near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 1
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the typical
near surface soils encountered at the site The test results are presented on Plate B 2
