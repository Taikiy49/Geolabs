Project Considerations
The project site is within the existing Honolulu Zoo in the Waikiki area of Honolulu
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We
understand that the project will involve the construction of the Asian Tropical Forest
Elephant Exhibit at the south central portion of the zoo premises
Based on the information provided we understand that a cable barrier fence
system is planned between the new habitats and alongside the walkway to the north of
the new exhibit The new cable barrier fence approximately 8 feet high will consist of
four tiers of electrified cables running through steel posts spaced regularly along the
cable barrier fence alignment Based on the information provided the new fencing may
be subjected to loading from an elephant weighing up to and traveling as
fast as
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings and the structural
loads provided we recommend supporting the new cable barrier fence with a deep
foundation system consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts The drilled shaft
foundations should have a minimum diameter of 36 inches and extend to a minimum
depth of 10 feet below the design finish grades
The scope of our work discussed herein includes observation of the drilled shaft
foundation construction for the Cable Barrier Controls at the Asian Tropical Forest
Elephant Exhibit project only The general contractor for the project is Watts
Constructors LLC The drilled shaft foundations were installed by Healy Tibbitts
Builders Inc
Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc installed 63 drilled shafts from May 16 through
The approximate locations of the drilled shafts are shown on the
Foundation Plan Plate 2 The as built diameters and the lengths of the drilled shafts
installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 Representatives from
Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of reinforcing cage and
concrete operations during the installation of the drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Watson 3110
Shaft Nos 1 through 47 in Phase I and a Watson 2500 Shaft Nos 48 through 63 in
Phase II crawler type drill rig equipped with 36 and 42 inch diameter auger and clean out
bucket Temporary steel casings with an outside diameter of 38 inches were installed to
depths ranging from about 10 3 to 11 5 feet below the ground surface in the drilled shafts
After excavation of the drilled shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the use of a
crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was placed
through a 4 inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom During concrete placement we

observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the
tremie pipe
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method Concrete with a compressive strength
of 3 000 pounds per square inch psi and a slump ranging from 7 to 9 inches was used for
the drilled shafts Construction Engineering Labs was retained to perform slump tests and
compression tests on the concrete
During removal of the temporary casing for Shaft No 60 it was observed that the
reinforcing cage has stuck with the temporary casing and was lifted out of the shaft hole
together with the casing A new reinforcing cage was then pushed into the shaft concrete
to the design tip elevation
During drilling for Shaft No 66 an existing waterline was damaged and the shaft
hole was flooded with water Due to the presence of the existing waterline we understand
that Shaft Nos 64 through 68 will be cancelled and replaced with spread footing
foundations
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the drilled shafts supporting the Cable Barrier Controls at the Asian Tropical Forest
Elephant Exhibit project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in our
geotechnical engineering report for the project dated and subsequent
recommendations In addition it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have
been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design load capacities
Limitations
