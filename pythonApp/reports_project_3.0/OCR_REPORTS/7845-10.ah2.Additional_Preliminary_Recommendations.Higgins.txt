The following contains our additional preliminary geotechnical recommendations 
to support the design of the on-site storm water runoff management of the project. A 
detailed summary of our findings and recommendations will be contained in our 
geotechnical engineering report.  The final report should be consulted when it becomes 
available. 
INFILTRATION TESTS 
We envision that the project may require the on-site storm water runoff management, 
including detention basins, dry wells or bioswales. 
In order to obtain subsurface infiltration information in support of the planning of such 
system, we conducted falling head infiltration tests at three selected locations at the site to 
evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered. The 
infiltration tests were performed in locations designated as I-1 to I-3. These tests were 
performed in general accordance with the procedures in Appendix C of the State of 
Maryland, Department of the Environment “Stormwater Design Manual, Volumes I and II” 
(rev. 2009). These procedures are consistent with other state’s procedures and may 
generally be considered an industry standard. 
The field infiltration tests were performed by augering a borehole to a selected test 
depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface. Upon reaching the test depth, a 
4-inch diameter PVC solid casing was set to the bottom of the drilled borehole to allow 
infiltration only through the soil exposed on the bottom of the borehole. Falling head 
infiltration tests were performed to determine the average infiltration rates of the underlying 
subsurface materials. Each test consisted of four trials of filling the casing with 24 inches of 
water and taking periodic readings over a one-hour trial period. The infiltration rates are then 
calculated based on the result of the fourth and last trial for each test location. The calculated 
infiltration rates at each test location are summarized in the table below. Details of our field 
infiltration testing results are attached for reference. 
It should be noted that the infiltration values presented above are the rates of 
infiltration through the soil exposed at the bottom of the tested boreholes, which may not 
represent the actual infiltration condition within a typical infiltration chamber footprint or 
an open basin.
