In accordance with the project specifications two micropile proof tests were
conducted on selected micropiles designated as MP12 6 and MP12 16 at Pier 12 for the
Piers 12 and 15 Improvements project on September 25 and 28 2015 This letter report
presents the results of our field observations and the test data obtained during the
micropile proof tests
was observed by others and was advanced to a depth of about
67 feet below the existing ground surface which corresponds to a pile tip elevation of
about 62 feet Mean Sea Level 16 was installed to a depth of
about 63 feet below the existing ground surface which corresponds to a pile tip elevation
of about 58 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The approximate micropile locations are shown
on the Micropile Test Location Plan Plate 1
In general the micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of about 136 kips
200 percent of the design load of 68 kips in approximately 17 kip load increments The
micropile was held at each load increment for about 5 minutes and the maximum test load
of 136 kips for 10 minutes to evaluate the creep effect The test results are presented on
Plates 2 1 through 3 3 including proof test data sheets loading curves and creep
movement curves
6 and MP12 16 were loaded gradually to the maximum test
load of about 136 kips with about 0 315 and 0 382 inches of vertical displacement
respectively Subsequently the micropiles were unloaded to zero load in five decrements
At the maximum test load of 136 kips the creep movement between the 1 and 10 minute
holding period was less than 0 04 inches and the total movement exceeded 80 percent of
the theoretical elongation of the unbounded length of the micropile as required by the
