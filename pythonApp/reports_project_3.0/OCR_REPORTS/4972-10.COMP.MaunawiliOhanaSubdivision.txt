PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Maunawili Ohana Subdivision project site Tax Map Key 18 is located
in the Maunawili area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided the project site encompasses approximately 8 5 acres of land The property
was generally mass graded and subdivided into four lots of about 2 acres each as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the mass grading operations and utility line installation for the Maunawili
Ohana project

It should be noted that finish grades and finish subgrades referenced in our
compaction test summary may not reflect the actual finish grades and finish subgrades

the owner In addition highly organic materials and mulch were placed on the surfaces
of the lots and slopes Based on periodic field observations the thickness of the mulch
placed appears to vary from about 1 to 3 feet In general the thickness of the mulch on the
majority of the lots was about 1 foot or less Up to about 3 feet of mulch was placed along
slopes and edges of the lots Therefore the finish grades and finish subgrades
referenced in our compaction test summary may not reflect the grades shown on the
original and or as built grading plans
It should be noted that organic materials are generally highly compressible and
degrades over time Therefore it is generally not suitable to be used as fill materials
except in landscape areas We recommend that the individual lot owners retain services of
a qualified geotechnical engineer to evaluate the impact of organic materials placed for the
type of structures and improvements planned at their individual lots

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation should be obtained in the planning design and construction of
any future improvements
