Field Exploration
The field work consisted of three additional exploratory
and 41 feet deep and a visual reconnaissance
of the proposed cut area The drilling was accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
solid stem auger The borings were continuously inspected
by an engineering geologist from our firm The boring logs

inch I D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals
of approximately 5 feet Both the standard penetration test
spoon and the ring sampler were driven 18 inches with a 140
pound hammer falling a distance of 30 inches The number of
blows required to drive the samplers the last 12 inches is
shown on the boring logs

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings and field observations the slopes are
underlain by a strata of clayey silt and highly weathered
volcanics residual soil The surface soil deposits con
sist of from 3 to 19 feet of clayey silt with little gravel
The upper soil stratum is generally stiff to very stiff The
underlying formation generally consists of very stiff to hard
but highly weathered volcanics the term volcanics is used
herein to describe undifferentiated complex rocks consisting
of dikes and lavas of the Koolau volcanic series see Plate 2
Detailed description of materials encountered are presented
on the boring logs attached
Very slight perched water seepage and groundwater was encoun
tered in Boring 3 35 feet deep Presence of carbonate deposits
in this and in other borings generally confirms previous exten
sive perched water levels

Discussion and Recommendations
Stability analyses were performed for the two major cut
slopes Based upon the proposed slope ratio of 1 1 H V
and measured strengths after saturation shown on Plates
A 5 through A 7 the calculated factors of safety range
from 1 3 to 1 5 depending upon details of the method of
analysis The analyses consider a 1 horizontal to 1 ver
tical slope ratio with benches or terraces every 20 to 25
feet of vertical slope height
Because of the relatively low calculated values forthe
factor of safety the presence of perched water and water
seepage and the highly weathered condition of the bedrock
it is very important to provide intermediate terraces at
intervals of every 20 to 25 feet on the slope faces This
will help to regulate and control erosion debris sediment
surface water and groundwater and help the overall stability
of the slope
Based on our present data and computation it is our opinion
that the proposed 14 H 1 V cut slopes appear to have adequate
overall factors of safety and should perform satisfactorily if
constructed according to the recommendations of this supple
mentary letter report Due to the presence of the underlying
dike formation and resultant perched water levels some shallow
localized sloughing could occur if sufficient water pressure
