SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff clayey
silt to very dense silty gravel highly weathered
rock beneath the site Laboratory testing and
previous experience have demonstrated that the
near surface clayey silt material has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the
moisture content of the soil
To reduce the effects due to soil swelling under
the slab on grade it is recommended that the site
be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of
non expansive select fill The subgrade soils
should be protected from drying
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed Kawano residence at Drive
Hawaii Loa Ridge on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plan provided to us the proposed house
will consist of a two story residence
A concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage The given
foundation loadings for the proposed two story structures are 25 kips
column load and 3 6 kips per linear foot wall load

Site grading involving primarily cuts is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the
subject house foundation The recommendations are based on the soil
information obtained from borings drilled for this exploration
The study consisted of drilling 3 borings laboratory testing
engineering analyses and report preparation The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
were utilized in the engineering analysis to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Hawaii Loa Ridge District of
Honolulu This approximately rectangular shaped 10 000 square foot lot
is fronted by Puu Ikena Drive to the northeastern property line
At the time of the investigation the site was undeveloped and
partly covered with grasses and weeds
The topography of the site slopes downward from north to south with
a gradient of approximately four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V or
less The current elevations range from about 632 to 608 feet above
Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling three 3 borings to
depths of 5 5 to 11 5 feet The borings were drilled with a
truck mounted drill rig The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by
stiff clayey silt to very dense silty gravel derived from the weathering
of basalt rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of the
drilling exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by clayey
silt and silty gravel Results of the laboratory tests on the near
surface clayey silt soil and previous experience indicate that this
material has low to moderate expansion potential Therefore to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils a non expansive capping layer is
recommended below the slab on grade
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support
the proposed structure
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils
around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 12 inch thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The moderately expansive soil below the slab should be removed for
a minimum depth of one 1 foot below the outside finished grade and
replaced with non expansive off site borrow materials such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying soil remaining
should be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic Section
on Plate 3

Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the one
1 foot capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be
followed However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of
swelling the non expansive capping thickness could be eliminated to
reduce costs if some cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should
be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A minimm 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete
Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that retaining walls may be used for grade
separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2347 00 Page 6
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 8 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
from the toe of the wall to the existing sloping ground surface
The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a H V plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the house areas the on site clayey silt
soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm thickness of
non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade slabs as
recommended in the Slab On Grade Section
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2347 00 Page 7
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specification for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 test borings to depths of 5 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger was
used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineer who continuously monitored
the drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic presentation
of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in the Boring
logs Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in accordance
with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were cbtained by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Iogs at the
appropriate sample depths
