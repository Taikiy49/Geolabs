Dear Mr Perez
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and infrastructure installation work for the Mary Savio Medical Plaza at
Newtown project from through March 24 2014
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Newtown Square is an office complex on the east side of
Kaahumanu Street about 350 feet north of the intersection with Kamehameha Highway in
the Aiea area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site currently consists of a
asphalt concrete paved parking is around the existing building with the majority of the
parking stalls at the front and rear of the building
In general the overall project involves the construction of a new four story medical
office building along the southern side and a four story parking garage with seven levels of
parking at the northeastern side of the property adjacent to Waimalu Stream The new
Phase 1 parking structure is supported on a deep foundation system consisting of

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC is the general contractor
for the project Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility line installation The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 and
95 percent relative compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively
of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing
and Material ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 216 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 12 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
