As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot to evaluate the suitability of
the near surface soils as foundation support for the proposed residence
The study consisted of drilling three 3 borings within the lot
performing laboratory soil tests and reviewing the preliminary house
layout plans This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary house plans provided to us the proposed
residence will consist of a one story structure with garage and patio

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the eastern side of Puna Lane and
along the southern side of Pauoa Road The lot is bounded by existing
house lots on the southern and eastern property lines The lot slopes
gently down towards the front At present the lot is heavily vegetated
with grasses and plants
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within the building area to depths
ranging from 5 0 to 11 5 feet below the existing grade at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered stiff to medium stiff brown clay
to clayey silt weathered tuff to the full depths of the borings
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the borings are
presented on the attached logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
soil can be classified as Clay CH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests indicate
that the soil has high shrink swell potential The results of the
laboratory one inch ring swell tests are summarized on Plate B 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a
surface layer of brown clay over weathered tuff cinder deposit The
laboratory test results on this material generally indicate that it has
high expansion potential Therefore the slab on grade and footing
foundations would require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material be placed
below the slab on grade foundations
The expansive clay below and for a horizontal distance of 3 feet
around the slabs should be removed for a minimm depth of 3 feet below
the outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive off site
borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78 The underlying clayey soil should be scarified covered with
hydrated lime moistened to above optimum moisture content and
recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to backfilling with the
3 foot select fill capping layer
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade refer to Schematic Section
Plate 2 for details

For footing construction we recommend that the expansive clays be
over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent
finished grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then
be placed on the compacted non expansive soil Alternatively the
footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent
grade refer to Schematic Section Plate 3 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for slab
thickened edge and footings bearing on the compacted fill or natural
ground
It is recommended that the footing bottom be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade If a footing is located
next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the utility
trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations to reduce
ponding conditions that may saturate the expansive soils below the
foundation
Concrete Driveway and Patio
For the concrete driveway and patio recommendations provided for
the 3 foot capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be
followed However since the driveway and patios could tolerate some
amount of swelling the non expansive capping thickness could be reduced
to 2 feet to reduce costs The subgrade soils should be kept moist and
covered with lime prior to concrete placement

Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should be
selected non expansive material generally less than 3 inches in size
placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximm

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations
and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
