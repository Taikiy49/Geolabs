INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our sediment sampling and geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed dredging of Kawainui and Kaelepulu
streams in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from the analysis of the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or bidding
purposes
W O 2693 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to dredge Kawainui and Kaelepulu streams to improve their flow
characteristics
The scope of this report is limited to consideration of the design of the dredging
project and disposal of dredged material
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the sediment
conditions at various locations of the two 2 subject streams This was accomplished
by the probing and sampling of the sediments in the stream bottoms at six 6 locations
designated by the client Three 3 of the sampling points were located in Kaelepulu
Stream near storm drain outlets and the confluences of other tributary streams The
remaining three 3 sampling points were located in Kawainui Stream at selected sites
near drain outlets
The information obtained was intended to develop a generalized soil data set to
aid in determining the approximate thickness of the sediments the general engineering
properties and whether the sediment may be disposed of in a sanitary landfill
The work was done in accordance with our fee proposal dated
to Park Engineering

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject streams are located in the Kailua and Enchanted Lake areas on the
windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii The streams containing generally brackish
water flow through both residential and commercial districts The majority of the daily
flow and circulation through the streams is slow and generally in response to tidal
fluctuations
Except for developed areas along the stream banks vegetation along the streams
is quite thick In places mangrove trees threaten to constrict the cross sectional area of
Kawainui stream It was noted that water clarity is good near Kailua Beach Park but the
water becomes turbid in inland areas A large amount of trash including household
furniture and even a submerged automobile was noted in Kawainui Stream in the area
of Kailua town
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions within the two 2 streams were explored by the drilling
and sampling of six 6 probes to depths ranging between 7 0 and 14 3 feet below the
water surface The probes were drilled from a floating platform using a 3 inch O D
Modified California split barrel sampler driven by a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches
The mudline was determined by lowering a perforated plate approximately eight
8 inches in diameter through the water column until it came to rest This depth was
measured and was considered the top of the mud line The sampler was then lowered
to this depth and driven to a maximum depth of about seven 7 feet below the mudline
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2693 00 Page 5
Water depths at the probe locations varied from 2 6 to 9 3 feet deep The upper five to
seven feet of sediment below the mudline was sampled
Samples recovered from the split barrel sampler were examined and classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
Identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs
of the probings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 6
The samples were split for geotechnical laboratory testing and for analytical
laboratory testing The analytical samples were chilled and transported to an U S
Environmental Protecting Agency certified laboratory for testing The results of these tests
are presented in Appendix C of this report
Soil Conditions
In general our probes encountered sediments consisting of soft silts and clays with
traces of sand and gravel The silts and clays are primarily organic and have moisture
contents on the order of 100 percent of the dry weight of soil The sand and gravel
fractions of the sediments are primarily clasts of coral materials believed to have been
transported by tidal and storm surge action
The very soft clays and silty sands encountered in the upper five 5 feet of the
stream sediments is underlain by generally medium stiff loose alluvial clays and gravelly
coral sand The sediment was found to have very strong organic odors throughout the
area Sediments obtained from the Kawainui Stream area had the strongest organic
odors
The sandiest material was encountered in Kaelepulu Stream near its outlet to Kailua
Bay where a greater degree of mixing occurs when the stream is open to the ocean

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general the soft sediments encountered by our probes should be easily
dredged by conventional methods i e hard excavation will not be required Based on
the results of the analytical testing it appears that it will be possible to dispose of the
material in a municipal sanitary landfill The high organic contents and high moisture
contents of the sediments limit their use as construction fill material
Dredging
Based on the data obtained from the probes drilled for our exploration it appears
that hydraulic dredges will be appropriate for the project
Generally the method of excavation should be left to the discretion of the
contractor within the applicable safety regulations and in accordance with good
workmanship
To reduce the potential of siltation in the offshore waters fronting the site we
recommend that any channels be excavated in the last stages of the dredging To further
reduce the potential of siltation the dredging operations could be temporarily suspended
prior to the excavation of the channels and the silts allowed to settle The basin could
then be cleaned with hydraulic or suction dredges
GEOLABS HAWAII

Use of Dredged Materials
Our geotechnical engineering laboratory testing indicates that the stream sediments
have high organic contents and high natural moisture contents These characteristics
limit their use as fill materials It is our opinion that it may be acceptable from a
geotechnical engineering view point to use the dredged materials for nonstructural fill
However the high organic content would probably result in the formation of objectionable
odors Therefore appears that it may be preferable that the material be disposed of in
a sanitary landfill
A review of the results of the analytical testing conducted by Clayton Environmental
Consultant s laboratory indicates that there are no contaminant levels in the soils sampled
by our probes which exceed the regulatory levels given in 24 Therefore it
is most likely acceptable to place the materials in a municipal landfill
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled probes Variations of
conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until dredging of the streams If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings presented in this report

APPENDIX B
Geotechnical Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to perform appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties and environmentally hazardous
characteristics of the soil sediment materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the probing logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the probing logs
Sieve Analysis tests and Hydrometer tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are
performed to determine the grain size distributions of soils The grain size distribution is
used for classification in the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on
other engineering properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1
through B 8
Organic Content tests ASTM Test Method D 2974 87 were performed on selected
samples to determine the relative amount of organic matter in the soils The results of
these tests are presented on Plate B 9
