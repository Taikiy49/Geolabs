INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Arakaki Apartment project located at 2017 Fern Street in
the Moiliili area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 2017 Fern Street in the Moiliili area of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to develop the subject site with
a three story apartment building with ground floor level parking consisting of asphalt
concrete pavement A short concrete driveway is also planned at the front to provide
access from the street level to the project site

understand that the proposed building will be of a combination of wood concrete and
steel construction with maximum column loads estimated to be on the order of about

finish grades at the site will range from about 5 5 to 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

for the project construction

REGIONAL _ GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located on the southern
flank of the Koolau shield Its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly
related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and
the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Moiliili
area are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related
deposits which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of
the sea The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments
along the coast in the areas which were formerly valleys and drainageways Land
development and reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the
project area to its present form

Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings to
depths of about 20 3 and 21 1 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by approximately
15 feet of compressible deposits consisting of soft peat and loose silty sands and
gravels below a 3 foot crust of stiff silty clays
Based on our exploration and the anticipated maximum column loads of about
85 kips we believe that the thin upper fill crust and the underlying compressible
deposits would not provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building
without appreciable settlements under the new loads In order to reduce the
settlements we recommend that the soft loose compressible deposits at the footing
locations be over excavated and replaced with structural fill Generally the depth of
over excavation would be on the order of about 5 feet below the footing bottom
elevations In addition we recommend that efforts should be made to reduce the
building loads and the footing foundations be structurally connected together with tie
beams to reduce the potential for total and differential settlements We estimate the
footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of about
1 inch total Alternately pile foundations bearing on a suitable bearing layer should be
considered for foundation support if the anticipated settlement is not acceptable
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
material generally has low shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore it is our opinion that future pavements may be constructed on
grade provided that the subgrade is moisture conditioned and prepared in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this report
Because of the relatively shallow groundwater table and loose consistency of the
near surface soils special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for this

Shallow Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed apartment building provided that the soft loose soils at footing
locations are over excavated and replaced with structural fill An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of
footings bearing on structural fill constructed as described below This bearing value
is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such
as those caused by wind or seismic forces We recommend that the footing
foundations be structurally connected together with tie beams to improve the rigidity of
the foundation thus reducing the anticipated differential settlement
In order to reduce the potential for appreciable settlements due to the
compressible subgrade soils we recommend that the soft loose soils at the proposed
footing locations be removed and replaced with structural fill In general the depth of
the over excavations would be approximately 5 feet below the footing bottom
elevations Additional over excavation may be required at localized areas to completely
remove the highly compressible soft peat soils as encountered in our field exploration
The bottom of the over excavation should extend laterally a minimum of 18 inches
beyond the edge of the footings A schematic section showing the approximate extent
of the over excavation is presented on Plate 3 The soft loose soils resulting from the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3537 00 Page 9
over excavation should not be reused as backfill material and should be disposed of
properly off site
We anticipate that over excavation to the depths indicated will encounter the
groundwater level which was encountered at about 4 feet below the existing ground
surface To facilitate placement of structural fill below the groundwater level we
recommend that the over excavation be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric Mirafi
140N or equivalent and backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation to about 12 inches above the groundwater level The geotextile fabric
should be wrapped around the gravel backfill to reduce the potential for migration of
fines into the gravels Care should be exercised by the contractor to avoid damaging
the filter fabric during placement Structural fill consisting of select granular material
should then be placed on top of the gravel backfill in level horizontal lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
To increase the rigidity of the gravel backfill for more bearing support we
recommend that a layer of geogrid reinforcement such as Tensar Biaxial Geogrid
BX1200 or Miragrid 7T be placed at selected depths as shown on Plate 3 The bottom
layer should be placed on top of the filter fabric at the bottom of the over excavation
The top layer should be placed on the top of the filter fabric prior to the placement of
select granular structural fill material A third layer should be placed at about mid depth
between the top and bottom layer of geogrid reinforcement

Individual and continuous wall footings bearing on the structural fill prepared as
described above should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on
the order of 1 inch total with differential settlements of inch provided that the
structural fill is placed as recommended above If the anticipated settlement is not
tolerable pile foundations bearing on a suitable bearing layer should be considered for
support of the proposed apartment building
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for foundations bearing on the structural fill material
We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm
adequate foundation bearing conditions depth of over excavation and the required
footing embedment depths
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades minimal site
grading work is anticipated for the project construction The weight of new fills will
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3537 00 Page 11
initiate long term ground settlement due to compression of the soft subsoils Therefore
any site filling that may be required should be initiated as soon as possible and should
be limited to a maximum height of about 1 to 2 feet above the existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site Slabs and foundations of the
existing structures that are to be demolished should be completely removed Any over
excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with compacted granular
fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material placed in 6 to
8 inch loose lifts and compacted Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future pavements
should be over excavated to the bottom of the base course layer The subgrade should
then be moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Due to the relatively shallow groundwater table anticipated at the project site a
layer of reinforcing fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent may need to be
incorporated between the base course layer and the pavement subgrades for
stabilization purposes in the event that pumping subgrade conditions are encountered
in the field Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3537 00 Page 12
over excavation and engineered fill placement and reinforcing fabric to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
Structural fill material required under the footing locations should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction It should consist of imported granular material such as select borrow
crushed coral or basalt gravel The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have
a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D
1883 It should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use

near surface clayey soils Based on the above assumptions we recommend the

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade soil below the base course aggregate to reduce migration
of excessive landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain
system could be constructed to collect excessive seepage water from landscaping
irrigation For improved long term performance a subdrain system constructed
adjacent to paved landscape areas should be considered

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes For pipes with inverts near the underlying soft clays
or loose sand and gravel deposits an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
wrapped in filter fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be provided below the
bedding layer to provide more uniform support of the pipe Self compacting granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be
used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes A typical section of the trench detail is
presented on Plate 4
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site granular or select
material The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab and footing foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of about 20 3 and 21 1 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using truck mounted drilling equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
In soft cohesive soils samples were obtained using a thin walled tube sampler
Shelby Tube The sampler was hydraulically pushed The gauge pressure required
to advance the sampler is shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate depths
