SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is an approximately triangular parcel with an area of
17 260 square feet located on the western end of Kinalau Place and is
bounded by the Lunalilo Freeway and Kinau Street off ramp on the northerly
and westerly sides respectively
The lot slopes gently in the southerly direction with slope ratios
generally flatter than 10 1 H V The existing ground surface consists
of granular fill and some deteriorated asphalt concrete The lot is
presently used for parking buses
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled to depths of 14 and 15 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered a 3 foot thick layer of dense
fill consisting of silty sand with gravel overlying medium dense black
Cinder sand Brown weathered mudrock tuff was encountered at about 12
feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The dense sandy fill crust overlying the medium dense black cinder
sand encountered in our field exploration can generally provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed building
Some fill is planned for the proposed building with anticipated
finished floor elevation at 65 feet Select non expansive import fill
material should be used to provide adequate foundation support of the new
building
Our recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the
following sections
Foundations
Continuous wall or spread footings may be used for the proposed
building foundation
For continuous spread footing an allowable bearing value of 3500
p s f may be used for anticipated light foundation loads on the dense
natural sandy soil and 2500 p s f on the compacted fill The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for short duration wind and seismic loading
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement

If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used
between the foundation and the compacted fill
Minimal footing settlement is anticipated due to the light building
loads
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire structure
A 4 inch thick cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided
below the concrete on grade slab In order to protect against future
moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slab The on grade slabs should be structually separated from
wall foundations
Site Grading
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris Any loose soft soil encountered should be
over excavated and backfilled with select fill
The fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral All imported fill materials should
be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being used in the
site
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are

Pavements
The project includes new A C pavements for driveway and parking
facilities
Based on a subgrade CBR value of 40 the following pavement section
may be used for preliminary design purpose
Flexible Pavement Driveway and Parking Area
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade soil should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned and compacted to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 prior to the
placement of the above pavement sections
Additional CBR tests may have to be performed on the actual exposed
subgrade soil samples during construction and the above design section can
be revised if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic
and that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the building and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided

Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper functioning
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recomendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
