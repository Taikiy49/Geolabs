PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Sea Country Area 5 subdivision was constructed in 2005 The site is located
west of Pakeke Street and north of Kaipoi Street A slope about 10 feet high is between
the two southernmost rows of houses The slope was originally mass graded at a nominal
slope of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
Following a large storm in 2009 the lower portion of this slope behind Lots 68 to
71 failed Remedial work consisted of removal of the clay soils in the lower portion of
the slope for a distance of about 6 feet from the toe of slope Structural fill was then
used to replace the clay soil removed Horizontal drains consisting of 4 inch diameter
slotted pipe were installed at about 8 feet on center to improve the slope drainage
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations
SITE GRADING
The grading consisted of over excavating the clay soils in the bottom portion of the
slope and replacing with compacted structural fill

At the onset of grading the area was cleared and grubbed prior to filling Delta
Construction Corporation performed the site grading and backfilling work During the site
grading operations samples of the structural fill materials utilized as backfill were obtained
and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 These tests were performed on the constructed
backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion the site grading work observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered
by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references
