FOUNDATION AND PAVEMENT SECTION RECOMMENDATIONS
LOTS 1 AND 3 THROUGH 8
NUUANU PALMS 50 BATES STREET
At your request we have explored the near surface soil conditions at
the above referenced building sites The purpose of this exploration was
to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed two story residential buildings The exploration
consisted of the excavation of 8 shallow test pits within or near the
proposed building sites laboratory soil tests and a review of the logs of
borings drilled near the building sites during a previous soil
investigation by others
The test pits disclosed the following general soil conditions
Lots 5 and 6
A thin surface layer of stiff yellowish brown clayey silt about 1 5
to 3 5 feet thick was encountered over stiff dark brown clay In
Test Pit 7 Lot 1 numerous roots were encountered to the full depth
3 5 feet excavated

A layer of stiff dark brown clay more than 3 5 feet thick was
encountered over dense gray basalt rock formation
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the test pits are
presented on the attached Test pit Togs Plates A 1 through A 3 The
approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the attached Test
Pit Location Plan Plate 1
RECOMMENDAT TONS
In general the field exploration disclosed that Lots 5 and
6 are underlain by a thin surface layer of yellowish brown clayey silt
overlying dark brown clay lots 7 and 8 are underlain by a surface layer
of dark brown adobe clay overlying basalt rock formation The
laboratory test results on this upper yellowish brown clayey silt and dark
brown adobe clay materials generally exhibited moderate to critically
high expansion potential Therefore the slab on grade and footing
foundations will require special design considerations and construction
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Slab On Grade Foundations
Lots 5 and 6
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that each of these five lots should be filled within at least the
limits of the house and garage slab areas to provide a minimm
20 inch of 3B Fine capping layer of non expansive borrow material
over the specially prepared existing subgrade soil

Tots 7 and 8 should receive a minimm of a 24 inch 3B Fine gravel
capping layer
The portion of capping layer which extends a minimum of 12 inches
away from the edge of the house slab garage and driveway slab area
should consist of moist compacted low expansive impervious soil The
yellow brown on site clayey silt could be stockpiled for this use
refer to Schematic Section Plate 2 for details
Alternatively if the placement of 20 to 24 inches of capping fill
above the existing grade is not feasible to reduce the expansive effects
of the brown clayey silt and the underlying adobe clay soils the
expansive soils below the on grade slab area and driveway should be
removed for a minimum depth of 2 feet below the outside finished grade and
replaced with non expansive borrow material such as 3B Fine refer to
Schematic Section Plate 3 for details
The exterior thickened edge slab footings should be 12 inches wide
and embedded a minimum of 36 inches below the lowest adjacent grade to
form a cut off wall A No 4 bar should be placed at the top and bottom
of this cut off wall refer to Schematic Section Plate 4 for details
Interior footings should be a minimum of 12 inches in width and
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Subgrade Preparation
Prior to fill placement the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris The underlying clayey soil should be scarified covered with
hydrated lime at a rate of about 1 pound per square foot moistened to
above optimum moisture content and moderately recompacted 85
compaction prior to fill or backfill with the 20 to 24 inches 3Fine
gravel capping layer The 3B Fine gravel material could be placed in one
lift and compacted with light plate tamping
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1910 00 Page 4
Dk No 39
The subgrade soils should be saturated and kept moist by water
sprinkling through the 3B Fine gravel 2 to 3 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swell potential of the subsoil
Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the deep perimeter
footing excavation and slab subgrade soil prior to water saturating of the
subgrade soil About 2 lbs of lime per lineal foot or per square foot
of excavation should be applied
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill base course should be
provided below the slab In order to protect against moisture
infiltration a vapor barrier may be considered below the slab
The soils engineer should inspect the subgrade preparation and check
the moisture contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab
and perimeter footing
The concrete of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a way
that the time period between the initial excavation and final concrete
placement at each lot should be kept to a minimum
An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f can be used for slab
thickened edge and footings bearing on the compacted fill or natural
ground
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations to reduce
ponding conditions that May saturate the expansive soils below the
foundation
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 20 to 24
inches capping layer of the concrete slab on grade should also be
followed The existing subgrade soils should be scarified hydrated lime
added prior to moisture conditioning and recompacted to 85 relative
density The stabilized subgrade should be kept moist by sprinkling with
water for about two days prior to concrete placement

To reduce cost a minimm 8 inch thick perimeter thickened edge
extending down 12 inches below the outside finish grade is recommended for
the driveways since the driveway could tolerate some amount of heaving
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to
allow for some movements of the concrete
Roadway Pavements
A concrete pavement is planned for the project roadway
Based on the laboratory CBR test results on the on site soils the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
6 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
12 Inch Base Course
18 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The underlying subgrade soil Should be scarified covered with
hydrated lime at the rate of about 1 pound per square foot moistened to
above optimum moisture content and moderately recompacted 85 compaction
prior to fill
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
during construction and the above design sections may be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
The finished lot grade outside the slabs pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding
conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
each house and that gutter water be diverted away from foundations and
walkway areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided

Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water fron entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent infiltration of surface water
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to divert all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly preparation of subgrade soil with lime saturation
recompaction of the lime stabilized subgrade soil and subsequent fill
placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our study
Any other imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from test pits and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil study and boring data of a
nearby site by others
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting
the engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent
geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and
in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid
estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as soft
deposits trash fill seepage hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
