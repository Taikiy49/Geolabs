Project Considerations
The project site is along the southern boundary of the University of Hawaii Lower
Campus complex on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The project involves stabilizing the near vertical rock slope by installing a
Keystone retaining wall and constructing new embankments The approximate project
limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site grading works involved fills up to 10 to 15 feet relative to the original existing
ground surface to achieve the design grades for the embankments using primarily
imported soil material The constructed slopes generally have inclinations of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter with localized inclination of for
transition zone
Site Grading
Prometheus Construction Company performed the earthwork operations for the
project At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from
the clearing operations were disposed of off site Finish grades were compacted using a
sheepsfoot vibratory roller
In general imported soil and or on site excess soil materials generated from the
excavation were used in the fill slopes The fill materials generally consisted of silty and

Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained
and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D
1557 Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
In general 12 inch minus imported general fill was placed and compacted for the
new embankments Field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method where appropriate During the fill and backfill
operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction based
on visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were
performed during the fill and backfill operations
A total of 104 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The results of the
compaction tests performed during construction of the above project are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
In addition we also observed the subgrade preparation for the leveling pad of the
Keystone retaining wall In general the subgrade was exposed scarified and
recompacted to firm unyielding condition
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the Lower
Campus Quarry Wall Stabilization Phase 2 project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of
the plans and specifications and our recommendations
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
