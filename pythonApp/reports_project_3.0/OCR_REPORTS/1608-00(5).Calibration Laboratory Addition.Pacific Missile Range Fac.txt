Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C81 85
Calibration Laboratory Addition
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Calibration Laboratory Addition at the Pacific
Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The general location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Calibration Laboratory Addition includes an air conditioned CMU
building with concrete floor approximately 40x40 feet in plan

the construction of the building
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the near surface soils underlying the
proposed structure
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located east of the existing Receiver Facility
Building Building 515 at the Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking
Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The site is in an open area measured about 40 by 40 feet It is
currently used partly as a lawn and partly as a parking lot Ground
level at the site is generally flat However the ground surface
elevation within the site is not available at this stage
Ground cover consists of some grass and fine sand with silt at the
lawn area while the parking lot portion is paved with asphaltic
concrete
Subsoil Condition
Two borings were drilled to depth of 20 feet below the ground
surface Both borings encountered a thin top soil of medium dense to
dense dark brown to brown fine beach sand with silt underlain by medium
dense to dense brown beach sand with medium grain size at the depth of
about 2 feet and below which is dense brown to tan color medium to
coarse grain beach sand at the depth of about 13 feet
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at 9 5 to 10 feet
below ground surface at the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Based on the anticipated sandy soil conditions and the light wall
load we believe that continuous wall footing bearing on the in situ
medium dense to dense sand may be used
For footings bearing on sand we recommend they should be placed a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished exterior grade
Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading condition may further
increase the embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used to determine the passive resistance

Site Grading
Due to the relatively flat ground minimal cut and or fill is
anticipated for the site development The finished grade level is not
available at this stage but it is anticipated to match the finished
grade of the adjacent Receiver Facility Building
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be kept
moist and tamped prior to placement of the footing if they are loosened
during the excavation The areas which are to receive pavement should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation
moisture conditioned and reconpacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density Any loose spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level
should be either recompacted if it is sandy or removed if clayey or
organic and replaced with compacted select on site sandy soil
If fill is required prior to receiving fill the area should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade after clearing and
grubbing then moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum

density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots
encountered should be removed and replaced with select on site sandy soil
or with suitable imported material compacted to the above mentioned
standard
The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density If
the on site fine sandy soil is used the compaction requirement can be
modified to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density for the top 6 inches
below the subgrade elevation in the areas which are to receive pavement

The proposed concrete slab on grade floor may be designed according
to the recommendations for rigid pavement mentioned in the above pavement
section Alternatively the on site sandy soil may be used in lieu of
gravel type of base course material provided it is well confined by
18 inch deep thickened edges around the slab perimeter
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation

engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
