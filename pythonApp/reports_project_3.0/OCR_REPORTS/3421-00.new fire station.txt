INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed New Fire Station within the Naval Computer and
Telecommunication Area Master Station NCTAMS in the Wahiawa area on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses of the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed new fire station is located at the Naval Computer and
Telecommunication Area Master Station NCTAMS in the Wahiawa area on the island
of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a one story fire station with a floor area
of approximately 538 square meters The fire station will include areas for dormitory
kitchen classroom exercise room workroom storage offices fire fighting equipment
and standby generator The structure will be of concrete slab on grade construction
with concrete masonry exterior walls and metal roof Access driveways parking lot
and utility service connections are also planned for the subject project
Based on a finish floor elevation of 368 66 meters Mean Sea Level MSL
minor cuts and fills on the order of up to about 3 meters will be required to attain the
design finish grades for the proposed construction

underlain by residual soils consisting of stiff clayey silts and silty clays Therefore
special attention was given to the pavement design and slab on grade construction for
the project

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 5
the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island
The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Epoch Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was
partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked
or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau
The project site is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades with depth to basaltic rock
formation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered stiff residual soils and hard saprolites
throughout the depths of the borings drilled at the subject site Based on the current
grading concept new fills on the order of up to about 3 meters will be required to attain
the design finish grades for the proposed construction Based on the relatively
competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings and the anticipated light
structural loads it is our opinion that shallow foundations bearing on the undisturbed
in situ stiff soils or new engineered fills may be used for support of the proposed new
fire station Because the proposed fire station building will likely straddle across the
stiff in situ soils and new engineered fills we recommend that the building slab be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 7
supported on at least 300 millimeters of select granular fill material for more uniform
support Our laboratory testing indicated that the on site soils exhibit relatively low CBR
values with a low to slight expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
As a result we also recommend that a minimum 300 mm thick layer of select borrow
subbase material be provided below the pavement areas Detailed discussion of these
items and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in the
following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the current grading concept and the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the proposed new fire station An allowable
bearing pressure of up to may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff silty clay soils or new engineered fills
needed to achieve the final design grades This bearing value is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by
wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 500 mm below the
lowest adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near top of slopes or on sloping
ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 2 5 meters measured from the outside edge of the
footings to the slope face We understand that the new fire station building will be
situated on new fill embankment Therefore we recommend that the building be set
back a minimum of 1 5 meters away from the edge of the new fill embankment
For footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 8
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material For footings bearing on the new engineered fills the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils or engineered fills as
recommended herein to be less than 25 millimeters total with differential settlements
on the order of 13 millimeters
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of per square meter per meter of depth KN cu m This
assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should
be reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs

Slab On Grade
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation of the
fire station building the future slab on grade for the fire station building will likely
straddle across stiff in situ soils and engineered fill materials at the northern side of the
project site In order to provide more uniform support for the concrete slabs on grade
we recommend that concrete slabs on grade for the fire station be supported on at
least 300 millimeters of compacted select granular fill materials placed in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report
For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend
that a minimum 100 mm thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide more uniform
support of the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Fifty millimeters of moist fine sand
should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane
and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or
sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask any minor slab cracking
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 10
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation
such as the apparatus room driveway slab and transformer pad a 150 mm layer of
base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction is recom
mended in lieu of the 100 mm thick cushion layer The 50 mm moist fine sand and
vapor barrier may be omitted for these slabs The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about
per square meter per meter of deflection kKN cu m may be used
for the compacted base course A minimum slab thickness of 200 millimeters may be
used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should be made for proper load
transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened
edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved areas should be embedded at least 300 milli
meters below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 100 mm thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel on top
of the 300 mm granular capping fill be provided below the concrete walkway slab
Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways
with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the finish floor elevation of the proposed
facility we anticipate that site grading work for the proposed construction will consist
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 11
of minor cuts and up to approximately 3 meters of fill placement over the existing
ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing the areas within the proposed building limits should
be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 300 mm thick capping
layer of non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level i e
bottom of slab cushion The select granular fill should extend a minimum of 600 milli
meters beyond the perimeter of the proposed building structure
The over excavated subgrades or areas designated to receive fills should then
be scarified to a depth of about 200 millimeters moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities

Imported fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the
proposed finish subgrades should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well
graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 75 millimeters in largest
dimension and should contain a maximum of 20 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be
tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use
Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 200 millimeter in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
All permanent cut and fill slopes constructed with the select granular fill material
should be designed with an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter All fills to be placed on
slopes with inclinations steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well
compacted slope face Water should be diverted away from the top of slopes and
slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce potential erosion of
the finished slopes A typical sketch of keying and benching is shown on Plate 3

Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the parking
lot We anticipate that portions of these pavements will be subjected to vehicular traffic
from the fire trucks Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing the on site
clayey soils appear to have a relatively low CBR value of 9 with an expansion value of
about 0 5 percent Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following
flexible pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
75 mm Asphaltic Concrete
150 mm Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
300 mm Select Borrow Subbase 95 percent relative compaction
525 mm Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted
Moist Subgrade Soil
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 200 millimeters
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density tests should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy
of the above section

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs be
extended a minimum of 50 millimeters into the subgrade soil below the subbase course
layer to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 150 millimeters of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 300 millimeters above the pipes to provide adequate support around
the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to
reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes which could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 300 millimeters above the
pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils or
select fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 200 millimeter level loose
lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to
reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3421 00 Page 15
pavement areas the upper 600 millimeters of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the fire station building should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Itis also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the siab and footing foundations
