PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Waimanalo Gulch Sanitary Landfill in the Ewa District
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 7
There is an existing temporary construction cut slope located along the eastern border
of the East Access Road at the northeastern portion of the landfill facility The temporary cut
slope is currently under construction Based on the available aerial survey topographic plan
dated and information provided by yourself the lateral length of the cut
slope along the East Access Road is estimated to be about 1 700 feet The general location
of the subject cut slope is shown on the attached Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
We understand it is desired to flatten the existing temporary construction cut slope to
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V to complete the existing site development plan Based
on the information provided it is desired to keep the toe of the slope located at the East
Access Road and cut back the slope such that the new top of the slope would approach within
no less than 100 feet horizontally from the eastern landfill property boundary We anticipate
that the proposed 2H 1V hillside cut would be steepened to 1H 1V as the 100 foot set back
boundary is approached

FINDINGS DISCUSSION
A site visit was made on February 9 to observe the existing condition of the temporary
construction cut slope and the undisturbed hillside portion located above the cut slope under
construction The existing hillslope consists of layered basalt rock that dips gently toward the
ocean in the southerly direction
Based on our observations the existing temporary cut slope ranges in inclination from
nearly vertical to about 1H 1V depending upon location In general the undisturbed hillside
located above the construction cut slope appears to stand at about 1H 1V
Based on the available topographic plan the vertical height of the existing slope ranges
from about 80 feet at the southern portion of the slope to greater than about 220 feet at the
northern portion of the slope
In general the exposed basalt rock appears to represent layered lava flows consisting
of medium hard clinker layers interbedded with dense basalt rock Based on our observation
of the slope exposures the rock materials appear to range from moderately to highly
weathered with a moderaiely fractured character Some thin layers generally less than about
5 feet in thickness of highly weathered silty and sandy volcanic ash materials were observed
interbedded within the basalt rock layers
Based on our observation of the exposed basalt rock comprising the temporary
construction cut slope and the undisturbed hillslope located above we believe that there are
no obvious geotechnical constraints that would preciude overall flattening of the slope to 2H 1V
inclination The rock formation composing the subject hillside appears to be generally
competent and problems associated with the stability of the rock slope are not anticipated
Therefore we believe the slope may be cut back as planned
