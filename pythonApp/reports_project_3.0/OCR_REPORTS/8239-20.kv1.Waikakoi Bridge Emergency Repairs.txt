This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation performed 
during the soil nail installation and testing for the Waikakoi Bridge Emergency Repairs 
project, located in Hana on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project location and general 
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The general contractor for the 
project is Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company (HDCC), and the specialty drilling 
contractor for the project is Jensen Drilling Company, Inc. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the soil nail 
installation and testing performed for the project between July 18 and August 27, 2022. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Waikakoi Bridge is located at approximately Milepost 45.4 of Hana Highway (Route 
360). Hana Highway is a winding, narrow, two-lane undivided road with very steep cut 
slopes on one side and steep natural slopes on the downhill side. The area is heavily 
vegetated due to the regional high rainfall environment. The roadway is heavily used by 
residents and tourists. The emergency repairs project is generally limited to the northeast 
abutment of the Waikakoi Bridge (Hana side), as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Beneath the Waikakoi Bridge, the northeast bridge abutment rests on a near-vertical 
slope with bearing materials exposed to further erosion from the existing stream. Due to 
heavy rainfall in the area, soil nail anchors and a shotcrete wall were designed to temporarily 
mitigate the on-going erosion of the bearing materials below the bridge abutment.  
We provided geotechnical observation and consultation services during the soil nail 
shotcrete retaining wall construction. The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services

SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
As a part of the project requirements, an anchored shotcrete wall system consisting 
of individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled holes to provide lateral support to the 
bearing materials underneath the abutment and to reduce the risk of the bearing materials 
from further erosion was designed to stabilize the existing slope. A total of 22 soil nails 
(2 sacrificial and 20 productions) were installed at the project site. The approximate locations 
of the installed soil nails are illustrated on the Soil Nail Layout Plan, Plate 3. 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during the 
installation of the soil nails (anchors). The soil nails were drilled using a Brandon Trax mini 
crane equipped with a TEI Rock Drill and a 6-inch diameter down-the-hole hammer bit. 
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified lengths, about 15 feet long, soil 
cuttings were removed from the boreholes using pressurized air. A threaded epoxy-coated 
No. 8 reinforcing bar encased in HDPE corrugated tubing and equipped with centralizers 
was then inserted into the borehole. In general, the centralizers were placed at about 2 feet 
from the top and 2 feet from the bottom of the reinforcing bar. 
The grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal high-shear and high-speed grout 
mixer equipped with a grout pump. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to the top 
of the drilled hole through a tremie tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. 
Results of the soil nail installation records are summarized in the Summary of the Soil Nail 
Installation and Testing, Plate 4. 
Prior to the production soil nail installation, two verification tests were performed on 
the sacrificial/pre-production soil nails, designated as LT-1 and LT-2, in accordance with the 
project documents. Proof tests were selected and performed on three production soil nails, 
designated as Soil Nails A4, B3 and C3, installed for the anchored wall. In general, the 
verification testing anchors, and the proof testing anchors were tested to about 200 percent 
and 150 percent of the design pull-out load capacity, respectively (or maximum test loads of 
about 60 and 45 kips, respectively). The soil nail head movement at each load increment up 
to the maximum testing load was measured and recorded. Details of verification and proof 
testing conducted on the selected soil nail anchors are presented in Appendices A and B, 
respectively. 
Based on our observation of the soil nail installation and testing, it is our opinion that 
the specified soil nails described above were installed and tested, meeting the design intent 
and the minimum load capacity.
