GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
MILILANI MAUKA COMMERCIAL A RIGHT TURN ENTRY LANE
Dear Mr Gaston
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Mililani Mauka Commercial A Right Turn Entry
Lane project This letter report summarizes our work on the project and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of the pavement section
and site preparation for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located within the Mililani Mauka area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The proposed project will consist of
the construction of a right turn entry lane from Meheula Parkway into the new Commercial A
area The proposed entry lane is about 12 feet wide next to Meheula Parkway and widens to
about 19 feet wide at the right turn and into the new Commercial A area Cuts and fills on
the order of 1 to 3 feet are anticipated for the new roadway construction
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located along Meheula Parkway adjacent to Mililani Mauka
Recreation Center No 5 and between the recreation center and the Mililani Mauka Sales
Office as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The new roadway area is covered by an existing sidewalk along Meheula Parkway The
new roadway then right turns into an open undeveloped area between the recreation center
and the sales office Based on the topographic survey transmitted by Engineering Concepts
Inc on May 25 2001 the existing ground surface generally slopes towards the north with
elevations ranging from 762 to 766 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should maintained to carry

performance we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscaped areas
Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate cuts and fills on the order of about
1 to 3 feet In general site grading operations should be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading
operation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
and scarification process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
anticipated
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the
excavated materials Scattered loose boulders laying on the existing ground surface at the
finished subgrade elevation should be removed to expose firm ground Soft loose and or
yielding areas disclosed during clearing and subgrade preparation operations should be
backfilled with well compacted fill
Finished subgrades and areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill material up to the
bottom of the aggregate base course or aggregate subbase may consist of the excavated
on site materials provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock
fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension
If imported general fill materials are required the imported materials should consist
of materials with a low to moderate expansion potential and with particle sizes of 3 inches
or less in maximum dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR value of 22 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when

All fills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Construction Observation
Construction observation of the site grading work during construction is an integral
part of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of
the site preparation fill placement and compaction and pavement construction Other
aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may
be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those considered in this report
The recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based upon
visual observations made during our field exploration and laboratory testing of the soil
samples recovered Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the sampling
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc should be
contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
