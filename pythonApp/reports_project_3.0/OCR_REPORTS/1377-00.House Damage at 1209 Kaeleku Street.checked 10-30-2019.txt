resulted from the original site grading operation

Conclusions
Judging from the cracking patterns observed on the house we
believe that the main damages may be attributed to the heaving of
subgrade soils in the rear central area of the house According to the
owner cracks have been developing since he acquired the house about 3
years ago Some settlements have also developed on the southeastern
corner of the house The areas judged to be thus affected are shown on
the attached plan
The settlement along the back of the retaining wall in the den and
the dining room area appeared to be relatively minor These settlements
were probably caused by settlement of retaining wall backfill along
these areas
The distortion of the sliding door frame to the lanai at the den
area was probably due to the settlement of backfill behind the retaining
walls The crack pattern on the garage floor also indicated a moderate
amount of heaving of the slab

Remedial Measures
We believe that the slab heaving detected at the house is caused by
expansive subsoils swelling upon absorbing additional moisture which may
have come from rain run off or watering around the house It is
important that every effort be exercised to prevent further changes in
the moisture content of the subsoil under the floor slabs For such a
purpose we recommend that the following steps be taken It should be
understood that as long as the underlying expansive soil is not
removed a potential for slab heaving still exists although the
following recommended measures would minimize external source of
moisture change It is possible that source of moisture may have been
an underground seepage of unknown source Such a source is usually
difficult to detect and control
1 A buried cut off curtain wall of about 3 feet deep should be
provided along both the eastern and southern sides of the house
to minimize additional entry and escape of surface water
infiltration to the slab subgrade along the perimeter of the
house This cut off curtain and its joints should be
water tight
2 All joints between the exterior wall and the curtain wall and
concrete sidewalks should be sealed water tight
3 All cracks on exterior stucco wall should be sealed water tight

All buried utility lines both water and sewer under the slabs
should be checked for leakage If any leakage is found it
should be repaired as soon as possible
A swale should be provided at the back of the house to improve
the drainage in the area The ground should be sloped away
from the house to prevent any ponding of water around the house
It is important that the existing subdrain lines behind the
retaining walls be located to assure that they are functioning
properly The outlets of the subdrain should be located and
daylighted to provide drainage relief
The subdrain system behind the retaining wall should be
independent and any rock backfill over subdrain should be
covered by at least 12 inches of impervious soil backfill
It is important that the downspouts for rain run off should not
be tied into the subdrain system The run off should be
discharged away from the house
Structural and cosmetic repairs may be made concurrently with or
after the above measures are implemented
