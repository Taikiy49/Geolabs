Review of Temporary Soil Nail Shoring
Kahekili Highway Widening
Likelike Highway to Vicinity of Haiku Road
In accordance with your transmittal request of June 30th we have reviewed the
proposed temporary soil nail shoring submittal for Valley Nos 1 and 3 at the subject
project The calculations dated June 26th and plans dated June 27th for the
temporary soil nail shoring reviewed were prepared by Schnabel Foundation Company
Based on our review of the geotechnical engineering aspects of the submittal we have
the following comments
1 The calculations for the temporary soil nail shoring system submitted
utilized an 8 inch grouted soil nail for stabilizing the temporary cut slope
The plans did not indicate a minimum diameter for the soil nails except in
the typical detail which indicates a soil nail diameter between 3 to
8 inches Therefore the plans should indicate that an 8 inch minimum
diameter hole should be drilled for the soil nails to be consistent with the
calculations submitted In addition the proof test load should be
estimated based on an 8 inch diameter soil nail
2 The spacing of the first row of soil nails from the top of the shoring should
be shown on the plans The calculations were based on 2 feet from the
top of the shoring
3 Groundwater was shown to be within the depths of the planned excava
tions in Valley No 3 Boring No 48 The analysis for Zone E
did not take into consideration the groundwater table The

4 Proof testing locations were not indicated on the plans We recognize
that proof testing of the soil nails at the beginning is desirable to confirm
the bond stress used in the design therefore more proof testing may be
conducted initially However proof testing of the soil nails should be
distributed among the five rows of soil nails Since a portion of the soil
nails may encounter groundwater during installation and the presence of
groundwater may have a significant effect on the bond stress adequate
proof testing of the bottom rows of the soil nails should be conducted
5 At the time of this submittal review we have not reviewed the submittal
for the design of the Mechanically Stabilized Earth Retaining Wall
MSERW Since the location of the temporary soil nail shoring is located
within 1 foot of the back of the MSERW we wish to emphasize that the
excavation work for the temporary shoring should not commence until the
design of the MSERW has been reviewed and accepted by the Engineer
The limits of the MSERW shown on the plans are preliminary and the
minimum requirements are based on the overall stability considerations
only Final design of the MSERW may increase the length of the
reinforcements which will affect the location of the temporary shoring
