This pavement analysis report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation and analyses performed for the proposed Palau Compact Road project on the
island of Babeldaob in the Republic of Palau This report summarizes our findings from
our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our pavement analyses for the proposed project These
geotechnical recommendations are intended for pavement design purposes only
DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve the existing roadway system on the island of Babeldaob
in the Republic of Palau with the construction of approximately 85 kilometers of new
roadway and associated structures known as the Palau Compact Road project
The proposed Palau Compact Road project will involve the design and construction of a
two lane two way rural collector road in general accordance with U S standards with
optimum operation and maintenance characteristics including pavements roadside
drainage cross drainage bridges shoulders guardrails pavement markings signage
drainage markers kilometer markers and intersections Roadway lighting and signal
lights sidewalks and curbs are not required elements for the proposed project
In general the proposed roadway system is separated into the West Route East
Route East West Route and North Route as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 1
In addition the project has been separated into four design packages designated as
Packages A through D The approximate limits of each of the four design packages are
also shown on the General Site Plan Plate 1 This pavement analysis report should be
applicable for design of pavements for the four design packages

GENERAL SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general the proposed Palau Compact Road alignment was observed to
traverse terrain underlain by six main soil and rock materials of varying character and
engineering properties The soil and rock materials were identified during our
geotechnical ground surface reconnaissance of the alignment and during our subsurface
exploration at selected locations along the alignment
The geotechnical reconnaissance included visual observation of both naturally
occurring exposures of the ground surface and exposures created by the bulldozer during
the trail clearing operations related to the drilling exploration In addition manual probing
of the surficial soils was performed at selected locations along the alignment to evaluate
the consistency of the near surface soils As a result of our site reconnaissance and
subsurface exploration the soil and rock materials observed were condensed and
grouped into six major soil or rock types The six main soil or rock types are listed below
in the general order of the frequency of occurrence throughout the project site The list
begins with the most common materials encountered at the site and ends with the least
common type of material encountered
Saprolite Soil extremely weathered rock materials

Design Subgrade Conditions
Our field exploration generally encountered relatively consistent subsoil conditions
consisting of stiff saprolite along the majority of the proposed Palau Compact Road
alignment Therefore we have assumed that the pavement subgrade soils will generally
consist of the recompacted saprolite soils as encountered in our site reconnaissance and
field exploration for the subject project
To evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the saprolite soils a total of
62 laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed on the near surface
soils at selected locations along the proposed roadway alignment The CBR tests were
performed at or near the natural moisture content with a compacted density
corresponding to about 85 percent relative compaction to simulate the fill placement
conditions during future earthwork operations The approximate locations of the
near surface bulk samples and results of the laboratory CBR tests are summarized in
Appendix A of this report The recompacted saprolite soil samples generally have
CBR values ranging from 1 to 6 with an average CBR value of about 2 5 Based on an
average CBR value of about 2 5 we have used a Resistance R value of 5 in the
pavement design

Connecting Roads
We understand that unpaved roads will be provided in some areas as connector
access roads As a minimum these unpaved roads should consist of at least 200 mm of
subbase course for connecting roads with a roadway gradient of 8 percent or less The
200 mm subbase course should also be provided below the shoulder areas of the
connecting roads It should be noted that there is a potential for washout of the subbase
course depending on the amount of surface water runoff along these access roads
Therefore some maintenance of these access roads should be expected
For connecting roads with roadway gradients in excess of 8 percent we
recommend that a pavement section consisting of 50 mm asphalt concrete over 150 mm
of aggregate base course on 150 mm of subbase course be provided for a more durable
surface The shoulder areas for these steeper portions of the connecting roads should be
treated to reduce the potential for erosion and raveling of the untreated shoulder areas
due to the steeper connecting road gradients Similar to the paved roadway areas we
recommend that the shoulder areas of these connecting roads be treated with 130 mm of
cement treated subbase

Pavement Drainage System
One of the primary distress mechanisms observed in pavement structures is
pumping due to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Based on our
experience with projects having similar soil conditions there is a strong tendency for
water to accumulate at the interface between the granular subbase material and the
underlying clayey pavement subgrade soils In addition free water voids in the
pavement section repeated heavy wheel loads and an erodible base can also cause
GEOLABS HAWAII
Pavement Analysis Report Page 15
W O 3595 00
pumping conditions Unless the pavement subgrade soils are properly prepared and
adequate drainage is provided the accumulated water will likely soften the silty and
clayey subgrade soils and cause premature failure of the pavement Therefore paved
areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off the
roadway Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the roadway during or after
construction
In general the primary source of free water in the pavement section is surface
water infiltration through cracks and joints in the pavement A major source of infiltrated
moisture is the longitudinal pavement shoulder interface Water also enters the
pavement section from shallow ditches To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration
into the pavement structure and underlying subgrade soils pavement cracks should be
sealed Considering the high rainfall in this area longitudinal edge drains should be
considered along alignments with relatively flat gradients and transverse drains should
be provided at low spots along the roadway alignment and ford crossings
As mentioned earlier pavement damage is generally caused by poor surface and
subsurface drainage conditions along the roadway alignment Therefore we recommend
that longitudinal edge drains along both sides of the roadway alignment be considered
where the roadway gradient is 2 percent or less Where needed a longitudinal edge
drain collector system with outlet pipes should be provided for positive drainage
The outlets must discharge into appropriate drainage outlet structures such as drainage
ditches culverts or catch basins Longitudinal edge drains will not be required for the
causeway pavement section provided that the asphalt concrete aggregate base course
and subbase course extend to the outside edge of the shoulder
The typical edge drain should consist of a conventional edge subdrain
The conventional edge subdrain would consist of a perforated pipe
with perforations facing down surrounded by drain rock The invert of the edge drain
perforated pipe should be at least 200 mm below the bottom of the subbase course as
shown on Plate 3 A non woven geotextile such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent should be
placed around the permeable backfill material drain rock to reduce the potential for

base subbase and or construction of the surface course to avoid damaging the edge
