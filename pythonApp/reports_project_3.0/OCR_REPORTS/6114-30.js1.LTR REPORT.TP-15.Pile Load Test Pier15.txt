In accordance with the project specifications one compressive pile load test was
conducted on a specified test pile designated as TP 15 for the Piers 12 and 15
Improvements project at Pier 15 on This letter report presents the results
of our field observations and the test data obtained during the pile load test
The compressive pile load test was conducted on a 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete pile Based on the project shop drawings Test Pile TP 15 was cast
with a length of 113 0 feet The test pile was driven to a depth of about 102 1 feet below
the existing ground surface which corresponds to a pile tip elevation of about 97 7 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL The approximate load test pile location is shown on the Test Pile
Location Plan Plate 1
The test pile was driven using a Junttan HHS9A hydraulic hammer capable of
delivering a maximum rated energy of 79 200 foot pounds Test Pile TP 15 was initially
driven to an elevation 97 6 feet MSL on using a 1 foot hammer stroke
The test pile was restruck on advancing the pile 1 inch to elevation
97 7 feet MSL using a 2 foot hammer stroke During the test pile driving a Geotechnical
Specialty Inspector maintained a record of the blow counts and field observations of the
driving behavior The test pile driving record is presented as Plate 2
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with
Procedure A Quick Test Test Pile TP 15 was gradually loaded to a maximum test load
of 320 kips in increment of 8 kips Each load increment was held at least 5 minutes To
evaluate the creep effects on the pile the maximum test load was maintained for 2 hours
at which point the rate of movement was less than 0 01 inch per hour
