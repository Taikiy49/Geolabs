1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Kobayashi Residence located at the corner of Lanihuli Drive and
McKinley Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our field
exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundations slabs on grade and site grading only The findings
and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at the Ewa side of the intersection of Lanihuli Drive and
McKinley Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot has an existing
one story house with an attached garage It is proposed to demolish the existing one story
structure and construct a new two story residence on the subject lot We anticipate that
the new residence will be a two story slab on grade structure Based on the demolition
and grading plan we envision site grading for the proposed construction that will involve
cuts and fills of generally less than about 2 feet

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in
age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep
valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed
by cinder and tuff cone eruptions These later eruptions are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
The project site is located near the foot of Roundtop near the valley floor The
project site is underlain by thin deposits of mixed alluvial and colluvial materials

2 2 Site Description
The project site is located at the Ewa side of the intersection of Lanihuli Drive and
McKinley Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is a
rectangular shaped lot bounded by existing residences to the west and north by Lanihulli
Drive to the south and by McKinley Street to the east as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
There is an existing one story house and an attached garage on the project site
Scattered trees were observed throughout the site Based on our field observations and a
review of the demolition and grading plan prepared by Hida Okamoto and Associates
dated May 15 2001 the site is generally level with existing ground surface elevations of
approximately 203 to 211 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

cinder sand and gravel underlain by basalt formation was encountered to the maximum
depths drilled of approximately 10 to 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that the groundwater level is subject to fluctuation due to rainfall events
seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and other factors occurring off site which
were not apparent at the time of our field exploration

Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by stiff to very stiff fill
consisting of clayey silt to silty clay Below this fill layer a layer of cinder sand and gravel
underlain by basalt formation was encountered extending to depths of about 10 to
10 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our field exploration and laboratory
testing we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or

will be minimal with the demolition of the existing one story house and the construction of
a new two story residence We anticipate that minor cuts and fills may be required to
accomplish the design grade Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of

3 1 Site Grading
Based on the demolition and grading plan provided we understand that site
grading will involve minor cuts and fills with the demolition of the existing one story
structure and the construction of the new two story structure Site preparation should be
observed by a representative from Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from
our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to
those encountered in our exploration The following recommendations are intended to
provide guidelines for the design of site preparation work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials generated from demolition should be removed and disposed of off site After
clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as garage slab and driveway the

3 2 Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the site we recommend that a
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils and or compacted engineered fills be used for support of the
proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the compacted
subgrade This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The
bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent ground level
If loose materials are encountered at the bottoms of footing excavations they
should be over excavated and backfilled with select granular material compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for foundation settlement
If the foundation is designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads are estimated to be on
the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around the footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for

3 3 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new
residence Based on our field exploration the project site was underlain by clayey soil
with moderately high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuation To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slab on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils below the structure be properly prepared and capped
with at least 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill material capping fill below the
slab cushion
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final placement of
concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage

3 4 Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be needed for the project In general
a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site sandy soils or imported
granular material The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to
not less than 95 percent relative compaction
3 5 Drainage
The finished grades outside the residence should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of approximately 10 to 10 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples to aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively remolded samples
to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests
were run on air dried samples Results of these swell tests are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on selected soil sample to evaluate the
liquid limit LL and plastic limit PL as an aid in classifying the soils The Atterberg
Limits test was performed in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 4318 The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths A
graphic presentation of the test result is provided on Plate B 2
