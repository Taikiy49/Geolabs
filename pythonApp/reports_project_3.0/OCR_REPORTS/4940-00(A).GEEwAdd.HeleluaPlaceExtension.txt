EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Nanakuli area in the District of Waianae on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed roadway extension traverses over vacant land
extending from the western end of Helelua Place to Lualualei Naval Road as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 The project area is generally vacant lands slightly vegetated with
small trees and short grasses Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the
existing ground elevations within the new roadway extension alignment generally range
between about 11 and 16 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 4 9 and 5 6 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers A bulk sample designated as
Bulk 1 was obtained of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics The approximate locations of the borings drilled and bulk sample
collected at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the
drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration
Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration
sampler using the same hamme and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 4 9 and 5 6 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site may be underlain by
surface fills consisting of medium dense gravelly sands overlying hard coral reef formation
at shallow depths Based on a review of the geology and our experience in the area we
anticipate that the subsoils underlying the proposed roadway alignment could range from
Mokuleia clay consisting of recent alluvium of high plasticity to coral deposits and
consolidated terrestrial sediments in a very dense state with cementation Laboratory
CBR tests conducted on the Mokuleia clays indicate the soils exhibit high swelling
potential with relatively low CBR values when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors Descriptions and graphic representation of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 5 of this
report

pockets of expansive clays Laboratory testing performed on the clayey soils generaily
indicated a relatively low CBR value of less than 5 and a swell of over 10 percent

aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above sections The recommended sections assume that good drainage will be provided
for the paved areas

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water
ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction Where
concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we
recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soils below
the base and subbase course layers to reduce migration of landscape water into the
pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect
excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend that a subdrain system be considered along the sides of the roadway
