INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil conditions
at the proposed subdivision site in order to obtain pertinent soil
parameters for use in the design of the mass grading for the proposed
development
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal of July 25
1988 with the exception that exploration was limited to accessible areas
outside of the sugar cane fields More detailed exploration of the
remainder of the site will be performed after the fields are harvested
The scope of work included
1 review and compilation of the available soil and geologic data
pertinent to the general project area and its vicinity

2 drilling and sampling of five 5 borings to depths between 16 5
and 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface
3 logging of the borings and reconnaissance of the project site and
its vicinity by a Field Engineer from our firm
4 laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained
5 engineering evaluation of the laboratory and field data and
6 development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is currently proposed to grade the 280 acre Ewa by Gentry West site
into single family residence lots It is anticipated that the proposed
residences will be light one to two story structures Infrastructure for
the development will consist of roadways utilities and sewerage In
addition a drainage sump measuring approximately 800 by 70 feet in plan
dimensions is planned for the northeastern portion of the site and an 34
drain line is planned for the eastern portion In this study our services
have been limited to the mass grading aspects of the project
Based on the proposed site grading plan cuts and fills generally less
than 10 feet are anticipated A drainage channel about 10 to 20 feet
deep is planned through the central portion of the development A
drainage sump approximately 25 feet deep is planned at the western end of
the channel to pump the water into the golf course drainage system

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated along the western side of Fort Weaver
Road in The existing Hookum Subdivision is located
immediately northwest of the site The first increment of the development
is situated at the northern boundary of the site Existing cane fields and
a dirt road form the southern and western boundaries
Topographically the site is relatively flat sloping down gently to
the east Ground elevations range from approximately 40 feet above Mean
Sea Level MSL in the northwestern portion of the site to approximately
20 feet in the eastern portion near Fort Weaver Road
Most of the site is covered with sugar cane with the exception of the
northwestern corner which has been recently harvested The site is
traversed by several cane haul roads unmaintained dirt roads and drainage
ditches

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to
be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in
Waianae ended lava flows from Koolau banked against its eroded eastern
slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau or
Central Oahu
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the
project site and further south these alluvial sediments accumulated along
with and interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef formations
to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from zero feet in the area
of H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa
Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock over the
artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of these sediments
has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the preliminary field exploration the soil types at the
project site may be generalized into three distinct groups as follows
Fill
A surface layer of fill was encountered at various locations
throughout the site and primarily consists of medium stiff to very
stiff brown to reddish brown clayey silt This fill was probably
derived from nearby alluvial deposits and was placed during the early
development of the site for the purpose of sugar cane cultivation

Alluviun
Alluvial deposits were encountered in the extreme northern portion of
the site as well as interbedded with and underlying the fill The
alluvium sampled primarily consists of stiff to hard dark brown to
brown silty clay to clay and clayey silt with some sand and gravel
interbeds
In general most of the stiff to hard clays may be classified as
having low to moderately high swelling expansion potential when
wetted However clays with high shrink swell potential have been
encountered at nearby locations and are anticipated to exist at the
site
Coral Algal Formation
Loose to dense coralline sand and gravel was encountered in the
central portion of the site at elevations ranging between 18 5 to 30 0
feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Medium hard coralline reefrock was
encountered in borings immediately south of the site at approximately
25 0 feet above MSL Therefore it appears that coralline deposits
may occur at relatively shallow depths less than 5 feet in the
southern and eastern lowlying portions of the site
Groundwater was encountered in Boring No 5 at a depth of 32 0 feet
below the existing ground surface at about elevation 2 0 feet above MSL
However seepage zones were encountered in Boring No 5 at 23 0 feet above
MSL and in Boring No 1 at 26 0 feet above MSL It appears that the
observed seepage zones may reflect localized seepage conditions possibly
originating from nearby irrigation ditches reservoirs and other sources
Rises in groundwater levels are anticipated during and after periods of
heavy rainfall

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general it is our opinion that the subject residential development
project is feasible form a geotechnical engineering point of view provided
that our recommendations are followed
The preliminary subsurface exploration generally disclosed that the
explored areas are underlain primarily by medium stiff to hard clayey
silts silty clays and loose to dense hard coralline deposits Iocally
some loose wet zones were encountered within the deposits Boring No 1
As previously discussed the clay soils encountered at the site may have
low to moderately high swelling expansion potential Based on our limited
preliminary exploration it is difficult to delineate the low expansion
potential soils and the moderately high expansion potential soils
Therefore it is our opinion that additional exploration be performed to
delineate areas of highly expansive clays and to provide recommendations
for single family foundation design and utility excavations in the proposed
housing areas
Site Grading
Based on the proposed site grading plan from 0 to 10 feet of fill is
planned for most of the site However the proposed cuts and excavations
are confined primarily to the vicinity of the proposed drainage sump and
are not sufficient to generate the required fill It is our understanding
that fill material will be obtained from an off site source located to the
west of the project site at the proposed golf course location
Based on the report by Soils International Inc entitled Soils
Investigation Proposed Borrow Site No 2 North of Geiger Road Ewa Oahu
Hawaii dated August 7 1987 it appears that the proposed borrow material
would consist primarily of coralline deposits and are anticipated to be
suitable for use as fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2153 00 Page 7
The proposed borrow material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The upper clayey silt and silty clay soils generally exhibit moderate
to high swelling potential when excavated and recompacted as fill
Therefore should this material be required for use as fill it should be
used in the deeper portions of the fill a minimm of 3 feet below the
finished grade
The excavated coralline deposits less than 8 inches in size may be
used as general fill material To facilitate construction of house
foundations and utilities the upper 2 feet of fill should be limited to
material smaller than 6 inches
The following recommendations present general grading guidelines for
the site
1 Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared of vegetation
boulders debris and other deleterious materials Topsoil should
be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill material
2 In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimm moisture content
and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 If soft areas are encountered they should be
over excavated and stabilized by backfilling with select material
placed in 10 inch maximm loose lifts compacted to 90 maximum dry
density
3 Fill material should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximm dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78

The additional off site borrow should be examined by the
geotechnical engineer for its suitability as fill
Imported select material for any non expansive capping layer
required should be low expansion granular material such has
crushed coral mudrock or cinder The material should be less
than 3 inch in size with a laboratory CBR swell value not
exceeding 1 and CBR value of 20 or more It should not contain
large percentages of boulders organics or other deleterious
material should contain sufficient fines so that there is a
minimm of 20 passing the No 200 sieve and should be capable
of forming a competent stable mass when compacted
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical keying and benching are required to properly bond the
new fill to the slope The filling operations should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers as
recommended above For construction details refer to Plate 3
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are recommended
for the site Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling
past the design slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Generally slope drainage benches should be provided for slopes
having a vertical height greater than 20 feet

for the proposed drainage sump excavation However due to the

should be anticipated and may require special excavating techniques

sump It appears that these seepage zones may reflect localized
seepage conditions possibly originating from nearby irrigation
ditches reservoirs and other sources
Two horizontal to one vertical slope ratios are recommended for the
upper 10 feet of the proposed drainage sump excavation One and
used if required in the lower portion depending on the degree of
cementation of the coralline sediments and if hard coral reefrock is
encountered
Slope benches are recommended at maximm 20 foot vertical height
intervals

Excavation
In general the depth of excavation will vary from about 9 to 12 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on our field exploration a
surface layer of clayey silt underlain by dense to medium dense
coralline sands may be encountered Relatively easy digging is
anticipated although local harder coral reefrock should be anticipated
and may require special excavating techniques such as hoe ram
excavation
Due to the depth of the excavation shoring of the trench walls are
recommended to minimize caving in of the trench walls The excavation
should be provided with proper bracing in accordance with the
applicable safety requirements The design and safety of the trench
supports should be the responsibility of the contractors
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movement and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such monitoring
is particularly critical in the areas where existing structures are
located adjacent to the trench excavation
Pipe Cushion
We recommend that a 6 granular cushion consisting of clean granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel or sand should be provided below
the drainage pipe

Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with Section
11 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
September 1986 City and County of Honolulu
Granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or sand should be used for
the backfill around the bottom half of the pipe to reduce the
compactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe amd to
provide bearing support for the pipe
Except for the boulders and expansive clay most of the excavated
on site soil can be re used in the trench backfill up to within 2 feet
of the finished grade provide adequate compaction is achieved
Backfill should be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78 Where the drain line crosses
the existing roadway the backfill within 2 feet of finished grade
should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of its maximm
dry density
The trenches should be kept free of water during backfilling

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction
are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used

Special Considerations
A
Cut slopes shall be excavated to the lines and grades indicated
on the contract drawings and where necessary adjusted in
accordance with the recommendations of the Geotechnical Engineer
during construction
All trench backfill placed above 12 inches of the top of the
pipes and around the reinforced concrete pipe drainlines shall
be compacted in maximum lift thickness of 8 inches unless
specifically authorized by the Geotechnical Engineer The
Geotechnical Engineer may require thinner lifts should
compaction operations consistently fail to produce the specified
compaction requirements In any event the required levels of
compaction shall be obtained throughout the full depth and width
of each layer of backfill
Jetting and ponding of the backfill or fill materials shall
not be permitted on this project unless specifically authorized
by the Geotechnical Engineer in writing
Upon completion of grading or temporary interruption of grading
and the subsequent termination of observation by the
Geotechnical Engineer no further filling or excavating shall be
performed without notifying the Geotechnical Engineer in
advance and ascertaining his requirements

III Site Preparation
A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be
disposed of in accordance with Section 10 CLEARING AND
GRUBBING of the Standard Public Works Specifications
All vegetation and deleterious materials encountered within the
Grading Limits shall be cleared and disposed of off site This
removal shall be completed prior to excavating and filling
Organically contaminated topsoil shall be stripped prior to
actual grading Materials determined as being unsuitable for
placement in compacted fill shall be removed and wasted
off site Any material incorporated as a part of the compacted
fill must be approved by the Geotechnical Engineer
Any abandoned underground structures such as cesspools
cisterns tunnels septic tanks wells pipelines or others not
located prior to grading are to be removed and the resulting
depression backfilled and compacted in accordance with these
specifications
Loose surface soils encountered in areas to receive fill shall
be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials
All surfaces shall be observed and approved by the Geotechnical
Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks or
lumps in excess of eight inches 8 in diameter
Representative samples of the materials to be used as compacted fill
shall be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the
proposed materials shall be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least
seven working days prior to its intended jobsite delivery

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches 8 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fil11 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill

The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted access
to the fill to perform the compaction tests
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous
fill surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
The Contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it
will be free draining and will not pond surface water
