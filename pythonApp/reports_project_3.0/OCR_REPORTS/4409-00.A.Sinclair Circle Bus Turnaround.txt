GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
SINCLAIR CIRLCE BUS TURNAROUND
Dear Mr Toyota
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed
a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Sinclair Circle Bus Turnaround project in the
Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings
from our field exploration and presents recommendations for the proposed project The
findings and recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Sinclair Circle bus turnaround within the University of
Hawaii at Manoa campus on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that improvements are planned at the bus turnaround The existing
asphaltic concrete pavements are planned to be demolished and replaced with Portland
cement concrete pavements The bus turnaround will have a width of about 30 feet New
curb and gutters and sidewalks are also planned on the east side of the turnaround as part
of the improvement project In addition bus shelters are planned at the northeast area of
the project
Based on the existing ground surface elevations we anticipate only minor site
grading work will be required to achieve the design finished grade
PURPOSE AND SCOPE

subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was used
to evaluate the subsurface characteristics for the formulation of recommendations
pertaining to the design of pavements slabs on grade and site preparation for the
project The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the Sinclair Circle bus turnaround within the University
of Hawaii at Manoa campus in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located on the east side of University Avenue as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The existing bus turnaround consists of asphaltic concrete pavements with curb
and gutters on both sides of the turnaround The turnaround is about 20 feet in width
Based on our observations the existing asphaltic concrete pavement is in a fair condition
Five concrete benches were observed along the east side of the bus turnaround
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 1 75 to 4 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight augers In addition a bulk soil sample was taken of the
near surface soils for CBR analyses to determine the pavement support characteristics of

The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the attached Logs of

Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer and
tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered hard silty and sandy
clays extending to depths of about 2 5 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface The
silty and sandy clays were underlain by very dense basalt boulders cobbles in a clayey
silty matrix extending to the maximum depths explored The basalt boulders cobbles were
generally highly to moderately weathered Our boring drilled at the existing pavement
encountered 2 5 inches of asphaltic concrete underlain by 4 5 inches of medium dense fill
material consisting of sandy gravel with some silt
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our exploration
However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall
and other factors Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field
exploration are presented on the attached Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 6

LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained
from the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered and to evaluate
the engineering properties
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the surficial soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of this test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths and presented graphically on Plate 7
One California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on the
near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils The test
results are presented on Plate 8
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
consisted of hard silty and sandy clays underlain by very dense basalt boulders cobbles in
a clayey silt matrix The existing bus turnaround pavement section consisted of 2 5 inches
of asphaltic concrete underlain by 4 5 inches of fill materials The fill material under the
pavement section is characterized as medium dense sandy gravel with some silt
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend
that the new pavement section consist of 6 5 inches of Portland cement concrete underlain
by 12 inches of aggregate subbase course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design are presented in the following sections of this letter report
Pavements
As previously mentioned the existing asphaltic concrete turnaround will be
demolished and replaced with rigid pavements consisting of Portland cement concrete
Based on the available information a total of 74 bus trips are planned per weekday at the
Sinclair Circle bus turnaround
We have made our preliminary pavement designs assuming the pavement
subgrade soil will consist of the clayey near surface soils encountered during our field
exploration with a CBR value of 5 0 and an expansion value of 2 5 percent If site grading
exposes soil other than those assumed we should perform additional tests to confirm or

Rigid Pavements
6 5 Inch Portland Cement Concrete

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density The aggregate subbase should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the recommended design sections
The recommended pavement sections assume that good drainage will be provided for
areas adjacent to the pavements
To reduce the potential infiltration of water into the pavement structural section the
pavement surface and the subgrade should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained
to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures This is critical
to the long term performance of the pavement structure considering the nature of the
on site soils Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Ponding of water adjacent to the edge of the pavement structural section will
allow infiltration and accumulation of subsurface water which will likely soften the clayey
subgrade soils and cause premature failure of the pavement structure Extensive
landscaping should be avoided adjacent to the pavement areas otherwise we recommend
that a subdrain system be constructed to collect the excess water from landscaping
irrigation and to reduce the potential for migration of landscape water into the pavement
section The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage will be
provided for the paved areas
Slabs On Grade
We understand that bus shelters consisting of concrete slabs on grade are planned
for the project As mentioned previously clayey soils were encountered in our borings
drilled at the project site To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in

the moisture content we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and
capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material or aggregate
subbase course compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test
procedures
The slab subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The non expansive select
granular fill material or aggregate subbase course should be placed as soon as possible
after the subgrade soils are prepared The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of
non expansive select granular fill or aggregate subbase The select granular fill or
aggregate subbase should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with
expansion joints at right angle intersections
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs
Site Preparation
Based on the information provided we understand that minimal site grading work
will be required to achieve the design finished grades for the project In general areas
within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set
of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities

Pavements concrete slabs and exterior walkways that are to be demolished
should be completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with well compacted fills Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch
loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to

After clearing and grubbing the area within the future pavement and slab on grade
limits should be over excavated to the bottom of the aggregate subbase or non expansive
select granular fill layer The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction
requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in areas
subjected to vehicular traffic Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
backfill materials provided that they are free of deleterious materials vegetation and are
screened of particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The non expansive
select granular fill layer required under the slabs on grade and or walkway slabs and
imported fills where required should consist of select granular material such as crushed
coral basaltic gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D1883
Aggregate subbase materials should meet the material requirements in Section 30 as
specified in the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986 Imported
fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Select
granular fills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a

Underground Utility Lines
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below new utility pipes planned at the project
site Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and placement could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils with a
maximum particle size of 6 inches The trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement surface should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
