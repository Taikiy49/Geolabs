INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Multi Purpose Facility for the First Presbyterian Church in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings from our previous field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of
foundations slabs on grade and site preparation only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing First Presbyterian Church is located at the northwest corner of the
intersection of Keeaumoku Street and Nehoa Street in the Makiki area of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves the construction of a two level concrete
structure adjacent to the church building over an existing parking lot The street level will
serve as the parking area and the second level will be used as a multi purpose facility with
several offices Maximum column loads for the new structure are anticipated to be about
388 kips
A geotechnical exploration was performed by Geolabs Hawaii at the site in 1990 for
a previous concept involving a two level parking with semi basement type structure a
one story parish hall addition and a one story chapel addition Data obtained from the eight
boreholes drilled at the site will be used to formulate geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the proposed new structure
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information was used to
develop a generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations slabs on grade and site
preparation for the new structure In order to accomplish these objectives we conducted an
exploration generally consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The existing First Presbyterian Church of Honolulu is located at the northwest corner
of the intersection of Keeaumoku Street and Nehoa Street in the Makiki area of Honolulu
The new structure will be constructed over the eastern portion of the existing parking area
Adjacent properties to the church site are generally located away from the common property
lines with the exception of a small wooden building north of the church site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 4
The open parking area is fairly level near the existing church structure approximately
at an elevation of 116 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL The existing ground surface at the
southern half of the parking area then slopes down at an approximate gradient of 8 to
12 percent in a southwesterly direction to an approximate elevation of 106 feet above MSL
at the southern corner of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were previously explored by drilling and
sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 Two additional shallow borings
designated as Boring Nos 2A and 6A were drilled next to Boring Nos 2 and 6 respectively
to evaluate the variability of the near surface soils Four of the previous borings were located
near the proposed new structure The borings were drilled to depths ranging from about
13 5 to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings encountered cinder sand to the maximum drilled depths of 13 5 to
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The upper cinder was highly weathered and
consisted of brown to dark brown silty sand which generally increased in density with depth
from loose to medium dense in the upper 5 to 13 feet to medium dense to dense below
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology are presented in
Appendix A of this report Descriptions and graphic representations of the materials
encountered and water levels observed in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings
in Appendix A Laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples and the results
are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by loose to medium dense
silty cinder sands The consistency of the cinder sand was variable but generally loose near
the ground surface and increased in density with depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 5
The columns for the proposed Multi Purpose Facility may be supported on spread
footing foundations However due to possible presence of loose materials near the ground
surface the soils 24 inches below the column footing subgrades should be over excavated
and recompacted In addition new footings located adjacent to the existing footings should
be lowered to the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper to reduce
the potential of surcharging the existing foundations In general the new footings should not
extend deeper than the existing footings by more than about 6 inches to avoid undermining
the existing footings
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Building Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow
spread footings may be used for support of the proposed structure However our borings
encountered pockets of loose materials near the existing ground surface Therefore the
soils 24 inches below the column footing subgrades should be over excavated and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we recommend that an allowable
bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade New footings located adjacent to the existing footings should be lowered
to the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper to reduce the
potential of surcharging the existing foundations with additional structural loads In
addition the new footings should not extend deeper than the existing footings by more
than about 6 inches to avoid undermining the existing footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 6
The bottom of footings should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete A representative of
Geolabs Hawaii should be present on site to evaluate the bearing materials and depth of
embedment of the footings If unsuitable materials are encountered in the footing
excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils and replaced
with well compacted fill
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately
0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the building may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils or compacted fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf for footings embedded in the
surficial fill materials This assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected
Site Preparation
We anticipate that only minor site grading consisting of less than 2 feet of cuts and
fills would be needed to achieve the design finished grades for the proposed project Site
grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is
important that a representative from our office be present on site to observe the grading
operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in
our exploration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 7
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other deleterious and organic
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Existing pavements to be
demolished should be completely removed The over excavation resulting from demolition
should be backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be
abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled
with select granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed
structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Based on our field exploration the excavated on site soils are generally silty or
granular in nature and may be re used as a source of general fill and backfill material Fill
and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement for the finished subgrades of pavement areas should be increased
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Where needed imported materials should consist of non expansive select granular
fill materials such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 8
of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Fill materials should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to hauling to the
project site for the intended use
Pavements
We envision that asphalt concrete pavements may be constructed at the project site
We anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project and the new pavement areas will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks
We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the medium dense silty sand soils encountered in the borings Based on the
above assumptions we recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Laboratory
CBR and field density tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design section The
recommended pavement section is based on the assumption that good drainage will be
provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4304 00 Page 9
into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration of
landscape water into the pavement section
Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines such as water sewer and electric duct lines may
be required for the proposed project In general we recommend that granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of sand or S4C be used under the electrical duct lines for uniform
support The initial backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes should also consist of
free draining materials such as sand or S4C to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill The sand and or S4C should be densified using a vibratory
plate or similar light compaction equipment Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the improvements above the trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site granular soils or
select granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future
ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Services During Construction
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed structure Items of construction
monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of foundation
excavations and over excavations and fill placement and compaction Other aspects of
foundation and earthwork construction should also be monitored by a representative from
Geolabs Hawaii This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was
prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such
observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and beyond
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from features shown on the plans provided by Richard Matsunaga
Associates Inc on August 2 1999 Elevations noted on the boring logs were estimated
from the elevation points shown on the previous development plan The physical locations
and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the methods used

The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic

interpretations made in the formulation of this report Groundwater was not encountered in
the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration However it must be noted that
fluctuation is expected to occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations ASTM D 2937 were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Eight sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples of the
soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plates B 1 and B 2
Five direct shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the shear strength of the material tested Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plates B 3 through B 7
