This technical memorandum presents our geotechnical recommendations for the 
Waialae Nui Stream Channel Emergency Remedial Measures project in Honolulu on the 
Island of Oahu. Hawaii.  The geotechnical recommendations presented herein are based 
on our site visit and desktop study of available subsurface information near the project site.  
We understand that the drilling of field borings will be not performed for the project.  
Project Considerations 
The project site is located behind the residence at 5331 Kilauea Avenue in 
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  An existing U-shaped open channel structure is 
located at the back of the residence. Numerous cracks and tilting of the channel wall on 
the mauka side of the channel were observed. The backslope behind the mauka channel 
wall is about one horizontal to one vertical (1H:1V) inclination. 
We understand that temporary remedial measures to stabilize the mauka channel 
wall is desired to be implemented as soon as possible. In addition, we understand that 
permanent remedial measures are desired subsequent to temporary stabilizing the 
existing distress channel wall.  
Anticipated Subsurface Conditions 
Based on our desktop study, we anticipate that the project site is underlain by 
alluvial and colluvial deposits with basalt rock formation at deeper depths. We anticipate 
that the alluvial and colluvial deposits would consist of clayey soils intermixed with cobbles 
and boulders. These clayey soils generally exhibit high shrink/swell potential and would 
likely be classified as “CH” according to the Unified Classification System. These 
expansive clay soils generally exhibit low friction angle. 
Temporary Remedial Measures 
We understand that the use of steel plates and bracing consisting of steel pipes 
extending from one side to the other side of the existing channel walls are being 
considered for the temporary remedial measures.  In addition, the installation of vertical 
steel bars through the channel bottom slab is being considered to restrain the steel plates

Lateral Earth Pressure 
The backslope behind the mauka channel wall is estimated at about 1H:1V 
inclination. In addition, we anticipate the presence of clayey alluvium and colluvium behind 
the wall. The recommended lateral earth pressure for design of bracing system, expressed 
in equivalent fluid pressures of pounds per square foot per foot of depth (pcf), is 111 pcf. 
Existing weep holes at the base of the mauka channel wall were observed.  Therefore, the 
recommended lateral earth pressure does not include hydrostatic pressure and it is 
assume that the channel wall is properly drained. 
Pre-Construction Survey and Monitoring of Wall Movements 
A pre-construction survey of the existing residential and wall structures adjacent 
to the existing channel wall should be conducted, including photographs and detailed 
descriptions of pre-existing distresses, to document the existing conditions prior to the 
commencement of construction. In addition, the pre-construction survey should include 
the existing channel structure. 
The existing structures should be monitored for movement during and after the 
bracing is installed. Displacement markers should be placed on top of each side of the 
channel walls and also on the existing CRM wall in the residence property. The 
displacement markers should be surveyed. Since the remedial repair length is about 
100 feet, we recommend that the displacement markers be installed every 20 feet on 
the top of each channel wall and on the CRM wall for a total of about 18 displacement 
markers. 
Before the start of construction, the monitoring points should be surveyed to 
establish baseline readings for the monitoring points. Benchmarks should be established 
for the survey work. We recommend that the displacement markers be surveyed on a 
daily basis during construction and on a weekly basis subsequent to completion of the 
installation of the temporary remedial measures.
