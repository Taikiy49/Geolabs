INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Lawi Residence located at 859 Hao Street in Aina Haina
valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2744 00 Page 2
This report summaries our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommenaations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for foundation design
input and may not be appropriate to form the basis for final construction cost
estimates or for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 859 Hao Street in Aina Haina valley and was
occupied by an existing abandoned residence at the time of our field exploration It is
proposed to demolish the existing structure and to construct a new single family
residence at the project site
It is anticipated that the proposed residence will have a partial second floor and
will be fronted by a garage A new concrete driveway will also be provided for
vehicular access to the proposed garage
The site is essentially level with a slight gradient down toward the stream
direction to the east Cut and fill operations for site grading to provide anticipated
finish grades will be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of foundation design recommendations for the proposed residence
The work was done in accordance with our revised fee proposal to Mr
Tadjoedin Lawi dated The scope of work included the following tasks
and work efforts

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 859 Hao Street in the Wailupe area of Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is in a hilly terrain located within Aina Haina
valley
The project site is fronted by Hao Street along the western property boundary
An existing house of wooden post and beam construction is located in the front third
of the lot Wailupe Stream is flowing behind the lot and adjacent to its eastern
property limit The existing stream bank abutting the lot is approximately fourteen 14
to fifteen 15 feet high and Wailupe Stream is approximately eight 8 to ten 10 feet
wide The stream bank is overgrown with vegetation
Existing CRM walls bound the subject lot along both the northern and southern
property limits It is observed that the existing rubble wall along the southern
boundary of the lot has tilted and is leaning towards the makai southern direction
The topography of the project site is essentially level with current ground
elevations ranging from about two 2 to six 6 feet based on an assumed elevation of
zero at a City and County survey monument in front of 853 Hao Street

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by stiff brown clay The
laboratory test results and previous experience indicate that the surface clay material
has moderate to high swell shrink potential Therefore the foundation will require
Special design consideration and construction procedures in order to reduce
potentially damaging effects that may be caused by the swell shrink characteristics of
the subsoils
Slope Stability
The project site is located near the base of the western slope of Wailupe Valley
which has a history of instability The property s eastern boundary abuts the Wailupe
Stream with an existing stream bank of about 14 to 15 feet high and approximate
slope ratio of one horizontal to one vertical The site is fronted by Hao Street which is
sloping upwards in the northerly direction In general the project site and the adjacent
lots are relatively level
With assumed parameters of 100 p s f in cohesion and 28 degrees in friction
angle for the existing stream bank our analyses indicate that slip circles passing
through the toe of the stream bank to the ground surface beyond a horizontal set
back distance of 15 feet from the slope crest will possess factors of safety larger than
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 2744 00 Page 6
one and one half 1 5 It is our understanding that the proposed residence will be
located in the front half of the lot about sixty 60 feet away from the top of the slope
The proposed residence is also located beyond an imaginary inclined plane of about
four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V extending upwards from the toe of the stream
bank Therefore it is our opinion that the proposed residential construction is located
within an area which should not affect nor be affected by the stability of the existing
stream bank
It is Our understanding that the project site and the neighboring lots in the
subdivision have been developed for several decades Visual observation in the
vicinity of the project site lot did not reveal any indications of major distress due to
slope instability Our field exploration did not encounter thick deposits of adobe clay
which has been identified as one of the main causes of reported hill side landslides in
the Wailupe Valley Therefore it is our opinion that the proposed construction should
not pose potential threat to the stability of the proposed residential structure located
on the subject lot nor to the adjacent lots
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed structure
In order to reduce the detrimental effects of the moderately to highly expansive
soil we recommend that the expansive clays under the spread continuous footings be
over excavated to a minimum depth of three 3 feet below the finished grade and then
replaced with non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The non expansive materials should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D
1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive material
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2744 00 Page 7
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of three 8 feet below
the lowest adjacent grade
Both of these design concepts are shown schematically on Plate 3 Schematic
Sections Footings on Expansive Soil
An allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f can be used for footings bearing
on compacted non expansive material or on the in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of
the utility trench to reduce the potential for footing settlement due to the possibility of
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface runoff that may detrimentally affect the expansive soils around the
foundations All footing backfill should be properly compacted in 6 inch lifts to avoid
future surface water infiltration On site clayey soil if it is properly moisture
conditioned to about optimum moisture content could be utilized to provide a
relatively impermeable backfill
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the garage floor or even the ground floor of the proposed
residence will consist of a concrete slab on grade
in order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a
minimum three 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive import select borrow
material be placed below the slab on grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2744 00 Page 8
The moderately to highly expansive clay below the slab should be removed to a
minimum depth of three 3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow material such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
The underlying clay soil should be moistened by water sprinkling and maintained in a
moist condition prior to backfilling with the non expansive material The non expansive
material should then be placed in layers not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A minimum four 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided directly below the slab In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the
slab
The thickened edges of the slab should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12
inches below the lowest adjacent outside finish grade The three 3 foot over
excavation and replacement should extend a minimum distance of three 3 feet
horizontally outside the edges of the slab Ideally the replacement material in this
zone outside the slab should be impervious as well as non expansive If granular
material is used to replace the underlying expansive clay we recommend that a
minimum 12 inch capping of non expansive and impervious material be utilized above
the granular backfill The reddish brown clayey silt from Mililani area would be a good
source of capping soil

Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations regarding the three 3 foot thick
capping layer of non expansive material underneath the concrete slab on grade should
be followed However since the driveway slab can tolerate some amount of relative
movement the non expansive capping layer could be reduced to two 2 feet in
thickness to reduce construction costs if some slab cracking is tolerable The
subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to placement of the non expansive capping
materials
A thickness of six 6 inches is recommended for the vehicular concrete
pavement for the driveway
The thickened edges of the driveway slabs should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the adjacent grade Recommendations for the ground floor
slab on grade construction should be followed
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 feet intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the concrete slabs
Lot Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed residence
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble old footings and organic
matter encountered during scarifying and grubbing should be removed and properly
disposed of off site The lot grading operations should be performed with care so as
not to disturb the adjacent perimeter walls along the northern and southern property
boundaries
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2744 00 Page 10
The fills and backfills within the top three 3 feet below the on grade slabs
should consist of non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
Where embankment of slab construction is anticipated the on site clay soil should be
over excavated to provide a three 3 foot minimum thickness of non expansive select
capping fill underneath the on grade slabs
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches prior to
placement of fills The scarified subgrade should then be thoroughly moisture
conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Imported fill and backfill materials that are required should be tested and
approved by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Drainage
The finished lot grades outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter
runoff water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and
slabs immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two 2 borings to
depths of 18 0 and 18 1 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill
the borings

examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 5 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples were obtained by an HQ wireline core barrel The core recovery
REC in percents are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
