GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
HAWAIIAN MEMORIAL PARK EXPANSION
Dear Mr Leong
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing services
performed during the site grading operations for the Hawaiian Memorial Park Expansion
project Our services were conducted from through March 6 2000 in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The project site is located on the northeast perimeter of the existing Hawaiian
Memorial Park Cemetery in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
includes several burial gardens and a paved access road at the site An embankment up
to 40 feet high was required across Kawa Stream for the new access road and a box culvert
was provided through the embankment to carry the stream flow
A geotechnical investigation report for the subject site entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Exploration Proposed Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery Expansion Kaneohe
Oahu Hawaii dated was prepared by Geolabs Hawaii
The site grading operations were performed by Royal Contracting Co Ltd In
general the site grading operations consisted of clearing and grubbing preparation of the
subgrade soils and placement and compaction of on site and imported fills Much of the fills
needed at the site to raise the original grades to the design finished grades consisted of the
on site materials consisting of clayey silt with gravel and sand
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials that were utilized
as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum
dry density and optimum moisture content were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are

After clearing and grubbing of the site the existing and finished subgrades were
moisture conditioned and compacted with a vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment
In general fill materials were placed to bring the site to the design finished grades Periodic
field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density tests
Fills placed on existing slopes having slope ratios steeper than 5H 1V were keyed and
benched For fill slopes greater than 10 feet a key was provided at the base of the fill slope
Subdrains were provided where new us were placed against existing slopes having slope
ratios steeper than 5H 1V
Subdrains consisted of 4 inch diameter perforated pipes with perforations facing
down Subdrains were surrounded and underlain by at least 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
with a non woven filter fabric wrapped around the drainage material Subdrain trenches were
approximately 30 inches wide and at least 2 feet deep
A reinforced concrete box culvert measuring approximately 180 feet long 10 feet
wide and 8 feet high was placed at Kawa Stream Before constructing the box culvert a
temporary diversion of Kawa Stream was achieved by placing a single 78 inch reinforced
concrete pipe RCP Since it was not able to support more than 20 feet of overburden a
75 foot segment in the center portion was plugged with sand bags Holes were punched in
the top of the RCP and lean concrete was poured filling the RCP to the top A 12 inch thick
gravel cushion layer was provided between the new box culvert structure and the underlying
alluvium The drainage system for the retaining walls consisted of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone
of permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the base
of the wall discharging to an outlet The wing wall on the left side of the box culvert was
modified to achieve a 2H 1V fill slope
Installation of the new utility lines generally included over excavation of material
followed by backfilling the trench excavation with a one inch minus bedding material After
placement of the utility line generally free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel were used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes Trenches
were then backfilled with 6 inch minus granular material and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction Where trenches were located in paved areas the upper
2 feet of the trench backfill below the road subgrade was compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction

Typically the subgrade materials under the pavement areas were scarified to about
6 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction Six inches of select borrow subbase and 6 inches of base
course were compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction over the subgrade
material
A total of 371 field density tests were performed using the nuclear density gauge
method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The
results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the subject project are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared subgrade on constructed fills and backfills at the site and on the base course
materials Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on
site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the Hawaiian
Memorial Park Expansion project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and
specifications and our recommendations In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory
were re worked to achieve the specified minimum compaction
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork have occurred
without our observation because our observation services on this project were on a part time
basis However the level of earthwork observation and testing services provided appear to
be reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that
there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used
to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of

construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction
of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering
to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
