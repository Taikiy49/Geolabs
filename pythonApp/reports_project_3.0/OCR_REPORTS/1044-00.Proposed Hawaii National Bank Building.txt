PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This report presents the results of our preliminary subsur
face investigation performed at the site of the proposed
Hawaii National Bank Building
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsur
face soil and groundwater conditions at the project site in
order to provide soil data for use in the design of the foun
dations for the proposed structure
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal of
May 21 1981
Our study consisted of 4 borings within the accessible por
tions of the proposed building site laboratory testing and
field observations at the Site The results of our field
exploration and laboratory tests which were used in the
engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions and recon
mendations are presented in the appendix of this report
Additional borings will be required after the existing build
ings are removed and the site is accessible for drilling
equipment to verify the findings of the present borings
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 1 and the Boring Logs are presented

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in downtown Honolulu just north
of Pier 14 in Honolulu Harbor The site is bounded by King
Street on the northeast Smith Street on the northwest Marin
Street on the southwest and adjacent existing buildings along
the southern boundary
The ground elevations slope gently down toward the southwest
and vary from about 18 feet at King Street to 12 feet at
Marin Street
There is an asphaltic concrete driveway extending along the
southwestern boundary and continuing through the middle of
the site Except for a few small paved parking areas the
remainder of the site is occupied by various low rise buiid
ings
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed building will be a seven story reinforced con

half of the site The basement level is planned at elevation

Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain
which consists of a thick series of interbedded marine and
nonmarine sediments capping the Koolau Basalt The near sub
surface structure at the site consists of a broad elevated
coral reef at least 35 feet thick partly covered by non
marine alluvium and fill These conditions are considered
to be the result of a complex series of Pleistocene sea level
changes The present reef platform which stands approximately
5 to 10 feet above sea level is apparently a wave cut terrace
GEOLABS HAWAII

P 5
formed by wave erosion of anolderreef which originally stood
over 25 feet above sea level
The widespread reef structure is known locally as the Waima
nalo Reef or the plus 25 foot reef
Although commonly referred to as coral reefs Hawaii reef
structures are predominantly calcareous algae skeletons which
Support a small percentage of coral growth The reef envi
ronment is varied and complex but consists basically of
zones of growing coral and algae deposits of course coral
debris on the forereef and deposits of sand silts and clays
on the backreef or lagoon With changes of sea level these
basically calcareous deposits usually become cemented or
even recrystallized by the action of percolating fresh water
Most of the coral reef as typically seen along Hawaii shores
consists of beachrock cemented sand or beds of cemented
sand and coral algal fragments known as reéfrock All reefs
also have channels of deeper water leading through them
Normally these channels are opposite the mouths of rivers
and are typically filled with nonmarine sediments

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a thick coral reef formation at depths of about 5 to 7
feet below the existing ground surface
Spread footing foundations bearing on the dense coral forma
tion could be used for the proposed structure An allowable
bearing value up to 8000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the dense coral
The bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be
increased one third when temporary lateral loads wind and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1044 00
seismic are considered
The footings should be placed a minimum of 3 feet below the
adjacent floor level
Estimated settlements for the footings are on the order of
one half inch total Differential settlements are antici
pated to be about one half of the total
Although large cavities were not encountered in the borings
solution cavities may be present in coral formations There
fore in order to reduce the possibility of footing settle
ments due to possible cavities in the dense coral formation
a minimum of one probe hole per column location should be
drilled below the building foundations The probe holes
should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and should extend
below the bottom of footings a minimum of 10 feet The probe
hole should be properly grouted from the bottom of the drilled
hole using low pressures
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly

Excavation
Excavations up to about 13 feet will be required for the pro
posed basement level
Some relatively hard ripping may be required in the dense
coral formation The upper soil layers should be easy to
excavate
Due to the proximity of the surrounding existing structures
sheeting and bracing is recommended for the excavation in
the upper soil layer in order to reduce settlement and dam
sage to the adjacent structures
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the excavations
Except for the elevator shaft construction dewatering is
not anticipated for the proposed basement excavation which
is planned at about elevation 5 85 feet
Site Grading
Mimimum fill is anticipated for the proposed project The
site should be cleared of all debris old foundations and
asphaltic concrete pavements prior to any filling

Basement Walls
Basement walls up to 13 feet high are planned for the pro
posed structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
on site coral sand should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill A subusrface drainage system should
be utilized to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four 4 borings ranging in depth from 15 to 40 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in the
field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were verified by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
In the borings samples were obtained by driving either a standard
penetration split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches
Core samples of the underlying coral were obtained by using NW double
tube core barrel with a diamond bit
The blow counts to drive the sampler for the last 12 inches along
with percent core recovery and RQD are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depths RQD represents the Rock Quality
Designation
