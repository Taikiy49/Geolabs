Dear Mr Miller
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed Kewalo Basin Harbor Improvements project in the district of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
This letter report summarizes the findings and geotechnical recommendations
derived from our engineering analyses The geotechnical recommendations presented
herein are intended for the design of foundations only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided the project involves extension of the existing
Pier C at Kewalo Basin Harbor as part of the Ward Village Development We understand
that the existing Pier C is a catwalk pier without fingers about 400 feet long supported on
concrete piles The improvements consist of constructing a 450 foot long catwalk
extension and adding finger piers on each side of the catwalk We understand that the
new catwalk extension and new finger piers will be supported on pile foundation
We understand that the Kewalo harbor is currently managed by the Ward Village
Development as authorized by the Hawaii Community Development Authority HCDA
Four borings were drilled previously in the vicinity of the Pier C for the Kewalo Basin
Modernization project under other development We understand that the authorization
was given by the HCDA for utilizing the boring information for this project Therefore new
boring was not drilled for this new development project

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late
Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and

forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The project site is located on the
coastal plain of the southern flank of the Koolau Range which forms the eastern two thirds
of the island of Oahu Geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the
glacio eustatic fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the
genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions at the project site
generally consist of lagoonal deposits associated with Kewalo Basin deposits of
calcareous sediments and coral algal reef formations These formations are intercalated
with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is composed of Koolau Basalt
Over the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenious and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments along the
coast and in areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form

Based on the two cases described above along with the idealized soil profile
described in the Subsurface Conditions section herein we conducted lateral load
analyses to determine the induced pile reactions of the 20 inch square and 20 inch
octagonal concrete piles for the catwalk extension and new finger piers
The concrete pile analyses were accomplished by using the computer program
LPILE to estimate the lateral load pile deflection and bending moment relationships The
program solves for deflection and bending moment along a pile under lateral loads as a
function of depth Analyses were carried out with the use of non linear p y curves to
represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil
moduli at various depths

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site and the
structural loading provided we recommend installing the new 20 inch octagonal concrete
piles to a tip elevation of 50 feet to provide the necessary lateral load capacity and
bearing resistance
We recommend pre drilling the pile locations to a minimum elevation of about
35 feet to facilitate the pile driving operation and to clear the pile driving path from
obstructions
Based on our experience we recommend using a hammer capable of delivering
energies in the range of about 50 000 to 70 000 foot pounds The hammer should be
equipped with an energy control level such that driving within the soft and or loose soils

could be controlled at a lower hammer energy setting to reduce the potential for tensile
stress development within the piles which could damage or cause cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be driven
This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy to the top of the
pile and also reducing potential damage to the piles Geolabs should review the proposed
pile driving equipment to evaluate whether the equipment is capable of installing the piles
to the recommended pile tip elevation
Wave equation analysis should be performed after selection of the pile driving
equipment to demonstrate that the proposed pile driving hammer and systems are
capable of driving the piles to the design tip elevations without overstressing or damaging
the piles

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of the subsurface conditions
between and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until additional probing or construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
provided herein
The field boring locations indicated herein are approximate having been taped from
features shown on the site plan referenced in Google Map 2008 Elevations of the borings
shown on the Logs of Borings were determined from interpolating between the spot
elevations and contour lines shown in the same plan The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil and or rock types and as such may denote
a gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text herein These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall storm surge condition
temperature and other factors
