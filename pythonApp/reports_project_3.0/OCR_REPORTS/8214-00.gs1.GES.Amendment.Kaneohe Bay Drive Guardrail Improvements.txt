Dear Mr. Miyahara: 
This amendment provides a change to supporting the new guardrails on the 
existing drilled shaft retaining wall cap as provided in our report entitled "Geotechnical 
Engineering Study, Kaneohe Bay Drive Guardrail Improvements, Kaneohe, Oahu, 
Hawaii," dated July 19, 2021. We understand that relocation of the existing guardrails 
further from the roadway will not be performed. In addition, a new foundation will be 
required for attenuators at the north end of the project site. 
We understand that the attenuator foundation will be situated between the roadway 
and an existing low Concrete Rubble Masonry (CRM) wall structure. An allowable bearing 
pressure of up to 2,500 pounds per square foot (psf) may be used for the design of the 
attenuator foundation bearing on the recompacted stiff clay fill. This bearing value is for 
dead-plus-live loads and may be increased by one-third (⅓) for transient loads, such as 
those caused by wind, impact, or seismic forces. 
The bottom of the footing excavations should be scarified to a depth of at least 
8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture, and recompacted to at 
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing 
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete. Relative compaction 
refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry 
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum 
moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum 
dry density.

In general, the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades. The bottom of the footing should be
embedded down to the existing grade in front of the existing CRM wall or deeper. In
addition, the footing should be embedded below a 45-degree imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the existing CRM wall. This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging the existing CRM wall.
Lateral loads acting on the footing may be resisted by friction developed between
the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil, and by passive earth pressure acting
against the near-vertical faces of the foundation system. A coefficient of friction of 0.40
may be used for footings bearing on the stiff to very stiff clay fill. Resistance due to
passive earth pressure should be neglected due to the sloping ground conditions.
