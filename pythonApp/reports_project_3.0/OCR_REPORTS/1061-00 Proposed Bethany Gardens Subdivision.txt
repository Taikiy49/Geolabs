PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil conditions at the site in order
to provide data for use in the design of the site grading
for the proposed subdivision development
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 2 and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A Detailed information regarding the field explo
ration and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SLTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the lower slopes of the Koo
lau Range in the southwestern portion of Kaneohe Oahu The
area is situated on the western side of Likelike Highway
southwest of the intersection with Kahekili Highway see
Plate l Project Lecation Map
The terrain at the site is rolling and the ground surface
generally slopes down toward the east at slope ratios of
less than 10 to as much as 40 in the upper portions of the
Site
There is a relatively large drainageway extending in an
east west direction located in the southern portion of the
Site This area is covered with a heavy growth of grass and
trees Water was noted at the ground surface in the lower
central portion of this drainageway The approximate extent
of this wet ground area is outlined on the Boring Location
Plan
Wet marshy conditions were noted along the northern boun
dary of the project site Field reconnaissance in this area
indicated that water level was at or close to the existing
ground surface Several small gullys with water running
through this marshy area were noted

Regional Geology
The project site is located in the west central portion of
the Koolau volcanic caldera complex Stream action has
deeply eroded the original caldera filling basalts and depo
Sited a thick mantle of alluvial and colluvial materials
This mantle of alluvium consists generally of mottled brown
to reddish brown poorly sorted conglomerates which have been
weathered to silty soils
Near the heads of valleys and bases of cliffs this alluvium
grades into coarse talus deposits and frequently carry con
siderable amounts of water through permeable zones
Stream action has shaped the area into a series of rela
tively steep sided drainageways Springs and swampy seepage
areas are commonly encountered in the lower portion of these
drainageways

Fill Placement Compaction Requirements
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site subsoils have in place moisture contents of
about 10 to 30 percent above its optimum moisture content
Therefore some amount of drying of the excavated material
will be required before using them as fill material in the
embankment
Because of the high in situ moisture contents of the on site
borrow material and the site being located in a high rain
fall location it is our opinion that the normal 90 compac
tion standard used in other drier areas should be modified
to 85 for this project Laboratory shear strength tests on
compacted samples indicate that 85 compaction will be ade
quate for the roadway embankment fill and light building
loads anticipated

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determina
tions were made on selected samples as an aid in the classifi
cation and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Three direct shears and torvane shear tests were performed on
undisturbed ring samples to evaluate the strength characteris
ticesot the natural sorls The results of the direct shear
tests are presented on Plates B 1 through B 3 The torvane
shear values are presented on the boring logs at the appro
priate sample depths
Five consolidation tests were performed on samples of the
underlying soft soils to evaluate their settlement character
istics The test results are presented on Plates B 4 through
B 8
One ASTM D 1557 moisture density test modified proctor was
performed on a bulk sample of the on site surface soil to eva
luate its compaction characteristics The test result is pre
sented on Plate B 9
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the near sur
face soils in the cut area The results are summarized on
Plate B 10
Two one inch ring swell tests were prformed on samples of the
natural soil to evaluate its swelling potential The test
results are summarized on Plate B ll
