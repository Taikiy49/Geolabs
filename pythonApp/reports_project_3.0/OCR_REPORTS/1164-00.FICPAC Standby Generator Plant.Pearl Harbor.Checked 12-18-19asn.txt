INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils engineering
investigation conducted for the proposed construction of a
new stand by generator plant at FICPAC Pearl Harbor Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore
the soil conditions at the site to provide data for use in
the design of the foundations of the proposed structure
The scope of our investigation included field reconnais
sance the drilling of two exploratory borings and labora
tory testing of selected soil samples
Results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
were used in the engineering analysis that formulated our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed stand by generator plant will be a new one
story structure about 20 by 30 feet in plan dimension
The proposed generator plant will have precast concrete wall
panels with concrete slab on grade floor and concrete roof
construction
Anticipated structural loading are 2 5 kips per lineal foot
for wall foundations which include dead plus live loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1164 00 Page 3
A concrete sidewalk about 5 feet wide will surround the
generator building
Preliminary site grading plans were not available during our
investigation However discussions with the project civil
engineer indicated relatively small fills of about one foot
are planned
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the Fleet Intelligence
Command Pacific FICPAC parking area off Halawa Drive as
indicated on Plates 1 and 2 The site which is roughly
triangular in shape has a plan dimension of 110 by 180 by
210 feet and is bordered to the southeast by an existing
electrical utility building to the northeast by the
existing asphalt paved parking lot and to the southwest by a
H V slope which runs parallel to Kamehameha Highway
Ground surface elevations range from 109 to 112 feet U S
Navy Datum with most of the site sloping gently toward the
northwest
GEOLABS HAWAII
Buried utilities in close proximity to the proposed struc
ture include sprinkler irrigation and a 36 inch RCP drain
line located at the toe and parallel to the existing slope
along Kamehameha Highway
The area is presently covered with grass

The borings encountered a surface layer approximately 10
feet thick of fill and alluvium consisting of stiff gray
brown clayey silt over soft marine deposits consisting of
organic clays and silts to the depth of the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
it is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
construction from a soils engineering point of view
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Preliminary discussion indicates that shallow fills up
to one foot are planned
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as
vegetation and debris should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
Prior to filling the existing ground below the proposed
structure should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content
and then compacted according to the requirements for
structural fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
Structural Fill Material and Placement
na a me te a
It appears that off site borrow material may be required
for fill
The imported fill material should be free of organic
material non expansive and generally less than 6
inches in size
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 70
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Building Foundations
Light structural loading is anticipated Therefore spread
or continuous footing foundations can be used to support the
proposed building and generator
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade and should bear on the stiff clayey
silt layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1164 00 Page 7
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
foundations on the stiff clayey silt The bearing capacity
value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for short duration wind and or seismic loading
A minimum footing width of 18 inches is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 70 and inspected by the soils engineer prior to
concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should
be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench back
fill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one half inch total and one quarter inch differ
ential
Slab On Grade
Generally the on site soils have a low swell potential
therefore slab on grade construction may be utilized
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
For the anticipated static floor loads a 4 inch slab over 4
inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended
A plastic vapor barrier over 4 inches of 3 fine gravel may
be substituted for the base course should a capillary break
be deemed necessary to protect the floor covering A thin
layer of sand should be placed over the plastic barrier to
aid in the concrete curing and minimize shrinkage cracks
The concrete slab should be separated from the generator
footing
Fuel Tank
An underground fuel tank is also planned near the new
generator building
Excavations deeper than about 8 feet may encounter soft
soils and groundwater level which would require a thick
cushion layer under the tank It is anticipated that
excavations up to about 8 feet should encounter stiff soils
Since the weight of the tank and fuel would weigh less than
the excavated soil minimal settlements are anticipated
The backfill around the tank should be well compacted to
minimize loss of support or settlement of the nearby
building footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
We recommend that granular material such as sand or select
borrow be used for backfill to facilitate compaction
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading and
foundation construction be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and
backfilling operations be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compac
tion has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion The recommendations given in this report are contin
gent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were perf ormed
on selected samples to aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths
A laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface material obtained from the proposed subgrade
elevation to evaluate its swelling potential and subgrade
Support capacity The test result is summarized on Plate B 1l
Two direct shear tests were performed on both undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of
the natural soil The test results are presented on Plates B 2
and B 3
One one inch ring swell test was performed on an undisturbed
sample to evaluate the swelling potential of the existing
surface soil The test result is summarized on Plate B 4
One consolidation test was performed on a sample of the upper
soil layer to evaluate its settlement characteristics The
consolidation curve is presented on Plate B 5
