PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located generally north of the former Waipahu Sugar Mill in the
District of Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site includes a total of
33 industrial lots and three roadways The site grading work generally consisted of cuts up
to about 13 feet deep along the northern portion of the site and fills of less than 7 feet
within the lots and roadways The grading plan for the site is shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 1 through 2 3
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for the project was observed by
Geolabs Inc from May 2001 to December 2002 on an intermittent basis The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills up
to about 7 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project The site
grading and utility backfilling work was performed by Royal Contracting Company Ltd
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized
as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the
test results are summarized on the Summary of Optimum Moisture Maximum Dry Density
Relationships Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 47 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
During the construction of the road pavement and installation of the utilities pipes
and concrete structures from the previous sugar mill facility were encountered in isolated
areas When encountered the pipes structures were removed and replaced with
compacted fill There may be other underground pipes structures that were not detected
during the mass grading and infrastructure installation
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the grading operations

LOT OLD FILL
Pre existing fill was encountered below the finished grade of Lot Nos 21 and 22
Test pits were excavated below these lots to determine the composition and vertical and
horizontal limits of the pre existing fill At Lot 21 the subsurface conditions consisted of
about 10 feet of stiff gray brown clayey silt fill over about 5 feet of miscellaneous fill
consisting of stiff to medium stiff gray clayey silt with glass bottles and metal pieces The
fill above the miscellaneous fill appeared to be stiff and competent The miscellaneous fill
appeared to consist almost entirely of inert material with minimal degradable material and
with medium stiff consistency Therefore it appeared that the pre existing fills could
support light structures and were left in place A site specific geotechnical engineering
exploration should be conducted by the owner of this lot to verify the adequacy of the
subsoils to support the proposed building
A test pit was also excavated near the rear slope of Lot 22 for a distance of about
20 feet toward the front of the lot The test pit was excavated to a depth of about 15 feet
and encountered stiff clayey silt fill without the miscellaneous fill
BUILDING CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the building structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the structures and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near
foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils The
existing drainage swales should be kept intact and maintained

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from walls and foundations
A geotechnical engineering exploration should be performed for each lot with
consideration for the specific building and grading planned and subsurface conditions
