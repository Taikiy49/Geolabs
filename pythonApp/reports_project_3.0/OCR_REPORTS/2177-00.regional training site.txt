INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Regional Training Site at Pearl
City on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

W O 2177 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a regional maintenance training complex
in an existing National Guard facility at Pearl City Oahu Based on the
information provided to us the proposed project consists of demolishing
the existing Building 63 and replacing it with a one story masonry
structure Concrete slab on grade will be utilized for the building
floor Construction of new utility lines and flexible asphalt pavements
for parking areas are anticipated
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Okahara
Associates Inc dated The scope of work consisted of
the following
1 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface and obtaining surface samples
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2177 00 Page 3
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading pavement and foundation
design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Hawaii National Guard s
existing Mobilization and Training Equipment Site MATES at Pearl City
on the island of Oahu The site is bounded by Waihona Street to the east
and existing Building 27 to the north Waiawa Stream is located to the
west of the site
Currently the site is occupied by the existing Building 63 for
vehicle storage The topography of the site is generally level with
elevations ranging from 54 feet southern portion to 62 feet northern
portion above Mean Sea Level Along the northeastern portion of the
site the terrain slopes upward toward the existing Building 27 at a ratio
of about 5 1 H V

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the
surface It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed structure and pavement
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and
slabs and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should
be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2177 00 Page 5
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site soils may be used as fill material If required imported
fill material should consist of non expansive select granular materials
less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill The bearing value
is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements
are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of
an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2177 00 Page 6
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
vemoved prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes
that the soil around the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor of
the structure A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
Pavement Section
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the passenger car
parking area The following preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade

W O 2177 00 Page 7
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction and the above section may be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic
and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines are planned for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in
maximum 10 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of
903 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect
against potential future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should also be
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2177 00 Page 8
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from objects indicated on the plan provided by Okahara
Associates Inc Elevations of the data points were approximately
estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same plan The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Okahara
Associates Inc for specific application to the site grading pavement
and foundation design of the project in accordance with generally
accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other
warranty expressed or implied is made
This geotechnical engineering exploration report has been prepared
solely for the purpose of assisting the architect engineer in the
preliminary foundation design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper
information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor wishing to

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface and
obtaining surface samples at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the
borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soils samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
