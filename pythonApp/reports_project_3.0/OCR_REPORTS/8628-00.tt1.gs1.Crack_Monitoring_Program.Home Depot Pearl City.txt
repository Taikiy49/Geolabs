Crack Monitoring Results
Home Depot Pearl City CMU Wall Crack Work
E-MAIL: WunShenC@gloverltd.com
COPY TO: File / Original
This memorandum provides the results of the crack monitoring program performed
for the above project. A total of four crack gauges were installed subsequent to the repair of
the wall distresses at Home Depot in Pearl City. The gauges were installed along the
Concrete Rubble Masonry (CMU) wall at Aisle 39, Bay 17, at the store. The gauges were
designated as Left Joint Bottom Gauge (LB), Left Joint Top Gauge (LT), Right Joint Bottom
Gauge (RB), and Right Joint Top Gauge, (RT). Each gauge has a 20mm range of horizontal
movement and a 10 mm range of vertical movement.
At each wall location, two crack monitoring devices were installed. One of the gauges
was positioned just above the floor level and the second gauge was situated approximately
4 to 5 feet above the lower gauge. The crack monitoring gauge utilized for the project was
Model 4020A CRACKMON. Readings from these gauges were observed and recorded
monthly over the course of one year. The approximate locations of the crack gauges are
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1.
The one-year crack monitoring program commenced on March 27, 2023, and
concluded on March 27, 2024. The horizontal and vertical movements observed were
recorded, and their relative movement values were calculated. A summary of the monthly
movements recorded at each gauge location is presented on Plate 2. The direction of
movement at each gauge location is presented on plots of the cracks monitoring gauges
(LB, LT, RB, and RT) on Plates 3-1 through 6-3. Based on our field observations and the
readings obtained from the crack gauges, it appears that there is some minor continual
movement of the CMU wall. However, the maximum movement of the CMU wall on the left
and right joints is less than or about 0.04 and 0.07 inches, respectively.
During our monthly observations, the bottom gauge at the left joint (LB) showed no
horizontal and vertical movements relative to the initial reading. Gauge LT recorded a
general downward movement of about 0.03 inches relative to the initial reading. It should be
noted that we believe accidental contact between the gauge and Home Depot boxes and
equipment may be the cause of 0.04 inch upward movement of Gauge LT on April 2023.
Based on our observations, the bottom gauge at the right joint (RB) experienced a maximum
upward movement of about 0.07 inches relative to the initial reading, while RT recorded
smaller movements on the order of about 0.02 inches. We believe that accidental contact
between the products’ boxes and the RB gauge may have contributed to the relatively higher
vertical movement of the gauge.
