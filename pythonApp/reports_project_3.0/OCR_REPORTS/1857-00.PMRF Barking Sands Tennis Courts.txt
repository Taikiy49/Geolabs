PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the near surface
soil conditions at the site to provide soil parameters for use in the
site grading and pavement design of the proposed site improvements
The scope of our investigation included a geologic reconnaissance
of the site and the drilling of three 3 shallow hand auger probes to
obtain samples for laboratory testing

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed playing court site is located on Pacific Missile Range
Facility Barking Sands Kauai to the northeast of South Sidewinder Road
and to the south of an existing baseball field see Project Location
Map Plate 1 The site encompasses approximately 1 2 acres
The existing ground surface condition consists of a low hummocky
topography evidencing low sand dunes The site is somewhat overgrown
with lantana grasses and other dryland
vegetation Additionally there are numerous logs on the site which
appear to have been brought in from off site and dumped The ground
surface elevation of the site averages approximately 16 feet above mean
sea level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the island of Kauai which is the
eroded remnant of a single shield volcano At an earlier stage in its
history the island stood much higher with respect to sea level than it
does at present During this time wave action along the northern and
western shores cut a large sea cliff extending roughly from Waimea to
Haena Subsequently erosion of the uplands resulted in alluviation
which deposited sediments in the Waimea Kekaha Mana area forming the
Mana Coastal Plain This coastal plain consists of terrigeneous
alluvium on its landward side lagoonal and lacustrine deposits in its
middle reaches and calcareous beach and dune sands in its seaward
margin The project site is located in the northern end of the seaward
margin of the Mana Coastal Plain and is underlain by dune sands
Soil Conditions
Based upon the geologic reconnaissance and the hand auger probes
drilled at the site the near surface soils to a depth of at least 2 5
feet the maximum depth penetrated by the hand auger probes consist of
poorly graded fine sands with traces of silt These sands are generally
tan in color dry friable and loose to medium dense in consistency
There are some zones of weak cementation which can generally be broken
down under hand pressure However these weakly cemented zones may
yield some hard gravel to cobble sized particles when the on site sands
are excavated Due to the shallow penetration of the hand auger probes
groundwater was not encountered during our investigation

Grading
The details of the finished site grading were not known at the time
of our investigation However it is anticipated that grading will be
minimal Prior to the commencement of grading work the site should he
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any soil contaminated with organic or
vegetative matter should be disposed of properly off site Roots
exceeding two 2 inches below finish subgrade and the resulting
excavation backfilled with on site sand or its equivalent compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximm dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Holes or depressions resulting from tree removal should also be
backfilled and compacted to the same standard
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the subgrade soils
should be thoroughly moistened and then proof rolled for a minimum of four
4 passes with a vibratory compactor Any soft yielding or pumping
areas should be over excavated and backfilled with select on site sand or
its equivalent compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78 After the completion of the proof rolling the subgrade
soils should then be compacted to 95 as stated above
Any fill material required should be placed in level lifts having
maximum loose thickness of eight 8 inches and compacted to 95 maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Pavement
It is proposed to use asphaltic concrete pavement for the court
surfaces It is recommended that a pavement section of four 4 inches of
base course overlain by two inches of asphaltic concrete be utilized The
base course should be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
established by ASIM D 1557 78 It is recommended that the pavement be
extended beyond the limits of the courts to prevent unravelling of the
subgrade due to erosion and subsequent damage to the court surfaces

Pavillion Slab On Grade
Thickened edge slab foundations may be utilized to support the
proposed pavillion The thickened edges should extend a minimm of
twelve 12 inches below the nearest adjacent outside grade
A bearing capacity of 2000 p s f may be utilized for footings
bearing upon on site sand compacted to 95 maximm dry density as per
ASTM D 1557 78 This bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered
For the anticipated static floor loads a 4 inch slab over four 4
inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended
If the pavillion is to be enclosed and it is deemed necessary to
protect the floor covering a plastic vapor barrier over four 4 inches
of 3 Fine gravel 1 2 to 3 4 inch size rock aggregate may be
substituted for the base course A thin layer of sand should be placed
over the plastic barrier to aid in concrete curing and to minimize
shrinkage cracks

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance shallow hand auger probes boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of weak organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or

needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs

APPENDIX A
Field Reconnaissance
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 probes to depths of 1 5 to 2 5 feet deep utilizing hand augers In
addition to these probes detailed cbservations were made of the existing
surface soil conditions by our geologist The approximate locations of
the borings and test pits are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in the
field by our geologist who drilled the hand auger probes The
Classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in our
laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered in
the borings is presented on the Hand Auger Probe Logs Plate A 1
