Foundation Probing and Grouting Report
Kapolei Reservoir No 2
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Phillips
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Kapolei Reservoir
No 2 project conducted from March 14 2018 to Our work was performed in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
The project site is located to the west of the existing Kapolei 215 Reservoir in the
Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Project location Map Plate 1
In order to identify potential subsurface cavities within the footprint of the new
reservoir foundations a probing and grouting program was specified for the project The
probe hole locations were surveyed and marked in the field by the general contractor
Goodfellow Bros prior to the probing operations The probe holes were generally located
at the center of each of the 32 column footings which are spaced about 22 feet on centers
on a square grid pattern and every 10 feet interval along the perimeter wall foundation
Probing and grouting were conducted at the existing grade of the reservoir pad generally
at an elevation of about 183 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
In addition to the 80 probe holes planned an additional 37 probe holes were also
drilled and grouted The additional probe holes were performed in the vicinity of the probe
holes with excessive grout intake The approximate locations of the probe holes are shown
on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2

The probing and grouting work was performed by Mort Drilling Systems The probe
holes were drilled using a Komatsu backhoe track rig platform fitted with a TEI hydraulic
percussion drill equipped with a 3 inch diameter drill bit with grout injection portals
connected to a hollow core threaded bar The probe holes drilled were approximately 4
inches in diameter and generally extended from 11 to 12 5 feet below the existing ground
surface A summary of our foundation probing and grouting observations is provided on
Plates 3 1 through 3 4
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of a thin surface layer of gravel fill and on site residual soils underlain by moderately
weathered to fresh vesicular basalt rock formation
After the probe holes were drilled to their designated depths a neat water cement
grout mix with a water to cement ratio of about 0 70 was pumped from an Oberman
VS63 grout plant into the probe holes through the hollow stem drill rod Probe holes that
did not fill up with grout after consuming up to about six times approximately their
theoretical volume were regrouted with either sand cement or neat cement grout after
allowing at least an hour of curing from the previous grout applied Where cavities were
encountered additional probe holes were drilled
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project requirements
Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the voids encountered at the
foundation locations have been grouted and that the treated subgrade should provide the
recommended support for the proposed Kapolei Reservoir No 2 foundations
