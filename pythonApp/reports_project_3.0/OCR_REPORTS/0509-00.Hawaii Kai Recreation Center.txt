INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation in
vestigation for the proposed preschool building and additional
parking at the Hawaii Kai Recreation Center
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the site s
subsurface condition determine foundation requirements for the
proposed building evaluate suitability of the site soils for
fill evaluate the stability of the cuts and provide recommen
dations for construction of an additional parking area over the
existing pond
Page 2
W O 509 00
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a one story building of
block construction with concrete labs on grade An additional
parking area will be constructed on the southwesterly portion
of the site over an existing pond The pond will have to be
filled to allow construction of the parking area Maximum
vertical column loads on the proposed preschool structure will
be on the order of 60 kips Wall loads will be about 1 kip
per lineal foot of wall Maximum fills on the order of 6 feet
in the parking 2 feet for the building and cuts on the order
of 12 feet at the playground area will be required to prepare
the site for development
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site is located immediately north of the existing Hawaii Kai
Recreation Center on Hawaii Kai Drive The easterly portion of
the existing parking lot will be developed for the preschool
building with playground area in the rear The playground area
is presently covered with a heavy growth of weeds and trees on
slopes 20 and steeper The future parking area is presently
covered by landscaped areas and a pond The proposed develop
ment and existing conditions are shown on the Site Plan Plate l

Geology
Hahaione Valley is located on the southeastern portion of the
Koolau Volcanic Range After the formation of the range the
constituent volcanoes were deeply dissected by erosion
Hahaione Valley was probably formed during this period Several
periods of emergence and submergence of the area followed
These changes in relative sea level gave rise to varying con
figurations of the ancient Kuapa Pond Coral algal reefs and
bay muds were then deposited along with land derived older clay
colluvium from the valley walls It is anticipated that coral
is located about 20 to 30 feet below the existing grade During
the later stages of the area s geologic history volcanic tuff
was erupted from Koko Head and the Koko crater volcano and
covered all previous deposits Much of this material has been
subsequently eroded Continued uplift and bay filling by land
derived boulder clays have covered all previous deposits in the
upper end of Hahaione Valley
Subsurface Conditions
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site 5 borings
and 6 probes were drilled at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 A more detailed description of the field explor
ation and laboratory testing is contained in Appendix A which
also contains the log of each boring a summary of the probes
and the results of our laboratory tests

Our borings reveal that the lower portion of the site is imme
diately underlain by about 6 feet of a very stiff silty clay
This material was found predominantly in Hole No 1 On the
existing slopes of the preschool this material is inter
spersed with carbonate seams found in the upper 2 to 5 feet
This clay material was not encountered in Boring 2 which may
have been removed during construction of the existing parking
area to conform with the parking lot grading plan Underlying
this material throughout the site are dark gray fine to
medium sands which are decomposed tuff
Boring No 5 located in the future parking area encountered
about 14 feet of fill overlying about 1 feet of a very soft
organic clay Six probes in the pond area showed that the
soft organic clay has a maximum thickness of about 3 feet
Underlying the organic clay material is a dense dark gray
decomposed tuff sand
Groundwater was encountered at approximately elevation 1 and
it is anticipated that this water level will be subjected to
tidal influences No indication of subsurface water flows
above elevation 1 which was noted during our investigation
but this should not preclude that they will not be encountered
during grading operations The lagoon may be fed by subsurface
flow from the higher elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
preschool building constructed with conventional shallow
foundations supported on fill or the underlying very stiff
silty clay or decomposed tuff The pond area may be filled
with boulders and granular material to above the water level
and then normal compaction equipment may be used to place the
remainder of the fill
Foundations
The proposed preschool may be supported on conventional spread
or continuous footings bearing in fill very stiff silty clay
or decomposed tuff A soil bearing pressure of 2500 pounds
per square foot for dead plus real live loads may be used for
footings bottomed a minimum of 18 inches below final adjacent
grade Continuous footings should have a minimum width of
12 inches Pressures may be increased by one third when con
sidering wind or seismic loads
Maximum total settlement on footings bearing in the silty clay
will be on the order on one half inch with differential settle
ments on the order of one fourth of an inch
Page 6
W O 509 00
It is recommended that a soils engineer inspect the footing
excavations to confirm the conditions on which our recommen
dations are based
Slabs On Grade
The silty clay found in Boring 1 beneath the westerly portion
of the preschool building is slightly expansive It is there
fore recommended that slabs on grade be placed on a minimum
of four inches of granular material In areas where dampness
in the floor slab would be undesirable or where moisture
susceptible floor coverings are to be used a plastic membrane
should be structurally separated from column and wall footings
Six by six 10 gauge welded wire mesh should be used in slabs
on grade
Cut Slopes
It is anticipated that the preschool slopes will be on the
order of 12 vertical feet The upper 6 feet of cut will be
in clayey silts and sandy silts The lower portion will be
in dense sands It is therefore recommended that cut slopes
be designed for a maximum steepness of 1 horizontal to
1 vertical Due to the erosive nature of the fine sands it
is recommended that proper erosion control be implemented
after excavation Provision for interceptor ditches on the

top of slope should be made to reduce the run off over the
slope face
Grading
The subsurface conditions encountered during our investigation
indicate the development of the property by means of cuts and
fills are feasible from a geological and soils engineering
standpoint provided the recommendations presented below are
followed Our general specifications for fill placements
compactions and supervision are contained in the grading speci
fications included in this report as Appendix B
Clearing and Grubbing
All deleterious material should be removed from the area and
wasted from the site In order to construct the proposed
parking area the lagoon should be filled Two alternatives
for preparing this site can be considered One would be to
remove the soft material which extends to a maximum of 3 feet
below the bottom of the lagoon The second alternate would
be to displace the soft mud by placing boulders or granular
fill to provide a working platform for the placement of
additional fill The choice of alternates will depend upon
construction equipment available and economic feasibility
It does not appear that the lagoon can be drained feasibly

Alternate No 1
Due to the relatively firm material located about 3 feet
below the bottom of the lagoon removal by normal exca
vating equipment may prove to be feasible and should be
considered After removal of the soft material boulders
or granular fill may be end dumped until the fill is
brought to above the water level Fill should then be
placed in accordance to the requirements for structural
fill
Alternate No 2
This method will require pushing into the lagoon fill that
would displace the soft bottom sediments Sufficient
amounts of sand should be mixed with the boulders to
completely fill all voids between boulders Boulders
should not exceed 3 feet in largest dimension The dark
gray sand encountered at depths of approximately 5 feet
below the proposed play area will be suitable for fill
between the boulders If additional granular materials
are required granular material from the stockpile on
the west side of Keahole Street shall be used The mixture
of boulders and sand should be dumped at the edge of the
lagoon then pushed into the lagoon Subsequent lagoon
fill should be pushed into the lagoon in such a manner
Page 9
W O 509 00
that all voids between the boulders are filled with the
granular material and the soft material 3 t at the
bottom of the lagoon is displaced This procedure shall
be used until the fill is brought to approximately 12
inches above the water level or to where a firm working
surface is encountered Fill shall then be placed in
accordance to the requirements for structural fill and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum density
It should be determined after the lagoon fill has been
placed if well graded rip rap rock may be required on
the fill slope adjacent to the unfilled lagoon area if
infiltration and loss of the granular fill material
beneath the water surface and erosion above the water
surface is excessive
The grading operations should be monitored by the Soils
Engineer to ensure that these recommendations are followed

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 6 test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary auger
equipment The depths of the borings ranged from 3 to 13
feet below the existing ground surface Five probes were
made in the pond area The locations of the borings and
probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The soils were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field
by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
with the Unified Soil Classification System
Graphical representations of the soils encountered are pre
sented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1A through A 1F The
probes are outlined on Plate A 1G Soil samples were obtained
by driving a 2 4 inch diameter sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler were recorded on the Log of Borings at the appro
priate sample depth

B All clearing site preparation or earthwork performed on
the project should be conducted by the Contractor under the
supervision of a qualified Soils Engineer

in accordance with the specifications of the Soils Engineer
D It is also the Contractor s responsibility to have suitable
and sufficient compaction equipment on the job site to handle
the amount of fill being placed If necessary excavation
equipment will be shut down to permit completion of compaction
Sufficient watering apparatus will also be provided by the
Contractor with due consideration for the fill material rate
of placement and time of year

SITE PREPARATION
A All vegetation and deleterious material such as rubbish
shall be disposed of offsite This removal should be concluded
prior te placing fill
B All houses sheds sewage disposal systems large trees or
structures should be spotted in the field or on the grading plan
to the best of the Soil Engineer s knowledge prior to preparing
the ground surface
C Soil alluvium or rock materials determined by the Soils
Engineer as being unsuitable for placement in compacted fills
shall be removed and wasted from the site Any material incor
porated as a part of a compacted fill should be approved by the
Soils Engineer
GHOLABS INC
IIit
D After the ground surface to receive fill has been cleared
it should be scarified disced or bladed by the Contractor until
it is uniform and free from ruts hollows hummocks or other
uneven features which may prevent uniform compaction
E The scarified ground surface should be brought to optimum
moisture mixed as required and compacted as specified If the
scarified zone is greater than twelve inches in depth the excess
should be removed and placed in lifts restricted to six inches
F Prior to placing fill the ground surface should be approved
by the Soils Engineer Water courses and gullies should be
cleaned under the supervision of the Soils Engineer and or
Engineering Geologist
G Any underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
mining shafts tunnels septic tanks wells pipe lines or
others not located prior to grading are to be removed or treated
in a manner prescribed by the Soils Engineer
COMPACTED FILLS
A Any material imported or excavated on the property may be
utilized in the fill provided each material has been determined
to be suitable by the Soils Engineer Roots tree branches and
other matter missed during clearing shall be removed from the
fill as directed by the Soils Engineer
GHOoOLABS INC
B Rock fragments less than six inches in diameter may be uti
lized in the fill provided
1 They are not placed in concentrated pockets
2 There is a sufficient percentage of fine grained
material imported to surround the rocks
3 The distribution of the rocks is supervised by
the Soils Engineer
C Rocks greater than six inches in diameter shall be taken off
the site or else be placed in accordance with the recommendations
of the Soils Engineer in areas designated as suitable for rock
disposal Details for rock disposal such as location moisture
control percentage of rock placed etc will be referred to
in the Conclusions and Recommendations section of the soils
report
D No material that is spongy subject to decay or otherwise
considered unsuitable should be used in the compacted fill
E Representative samples of materials to be utilized as com
pacted fill should be analyzed in the laboratory by the Soils
Engineer to determine its physical properties If any material
other than that previously tested is encountered during grading
the appropriate analysis of this material should be conducted by
the Soils Engineer
GHOLABS INC
F Material used in the compacting process should be evenly
spread watered processed and compacted in six inch lifts to
obtain a uniformly dense layer The fill should be placed and
compacted on a horizontal plane unless otherwise approved by
the Soils Engineer
G If the moisture content or relative density varies from that
required by the Soils Engineer the Contractor will rework the
fill until it is approved by the Soils Engineer If the material
is too wet as determined by the Soils Engineer it should either
be dried by the Contractor or hauled away from the fill area
H Each layer should be compacted to 90 percent of the maximum
density in compliance with the testing method specified by the
controlling Governmental Authority In general ASTM D 1557 64T
will be used with a 5 layer curve If compaction to a lesser per
centage is authorized by the controlling Governmental Authorities
because of a specific land use or expansive soil conditions the
area to receive fill compacted to less than 90 percent should
either be delineated on the grading plan or appropriate reference
made to the area in the soil report
I All fills should be keyed and benched into bedrock or firm
material where the slope receiving fill exceeds a ratio of six
horizontal to one vertical in accordance with the recommendations
of the Soils Engineer
J The key for side hill fills should be a minimum of 15 feet
within bedrock or firm materials unless otherwise specified in
the soils report
GHOLABS INC
IV
K Drainage terraces and subdrainage devices should be con
structed in compliance with the ordinances of controlling
Governmental Authorities or with the recommendations of the
Soils Engineer and Engineering Geologist
L To insure the proper compaction on the face of fill slopes
the slope face should either be backrolled with sheepsfoot
rollers at every three foot vertical lift and then grid rolled
upon completion or the fill slope should be overbuilt and cut
back to the compacted core Slope tests will be taken to insure
compaction
M All fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion
by methods specified in the soils report
N Fill over cut slopes should be properly keyed into rock or
firm material and the transition should be stripped of all soil
prior to placing fill
CUT SLOPES
A The Engineering Geologist shall inspect all cut slopes
excavated in rock lithified or formation material at vertical
intervals not exceeding ten feet
B If any conditions not anticipated in the preliminary report
such as perched water seepage lenticular or confined strata
of a potentially adverse nature unfavorably inclined bedding

joints or fault planes are encountered during grading these
conditions should be analyzed by the Engineering Geologist and
Soils Engineer and recommendations should be made to treat

C Cut slopes that face in the same direction as the prevailing
drainage should be protected from slope wash by a non erosive
interceptor swale placed at the top of slope
D Unless otherwise specified in the soils and geological report
no cut slopes should be excavated higher or steeper than that
allowed by the ordinances of controlling Governmental Authorities
E Drainage terraces should be constructed in compliance with
the ordinances of controlling Governmental Authorities or with

Geologist

to receive fill if required by the Soils Engineer
