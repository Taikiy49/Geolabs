INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this tnvestigation is to evaluate the subsurface
conditions and to provide soil data for the design and construc

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located at the end of Pono Street on the
eastern side of Waimalu Valley The upper portion of the site
is located on the side slopes of the valley and generally slopes
down to the east at about 1 H V to 3 1 H V gradients The
upper area is covered with trees Rock outcrops were noted in
the higher portions of this area An old access road about
25 feet wide is located along the hillside above the proposed
lots Many boulders were noted along the slopes below the old
access road The ground elevations in this area generally vary
from about 40 to 120 feet
The lower portion of the site is situated on an alluvial terrace
next to Waimalu Stream The ground surface slopes down toward
Waimalu Stream on the west at gradients of about 2 to 5 percent
This area is covered with trees and grass Boulders were noted
in portions of the site The ground elevations in this area
generally vary from about 30 to 40 feet
Waimalu Stream is located generally on the western side of the
site The stream is about 5 to 10 feet deep

Most of the stream is unlined Many boulders were noted in
the stream bed A few inches of water was noted in portions

An old cane haul road extends through the southern portion

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development which will consist of 58 lots
with a 44 foot wide access roadway through the site
To develop the site cuts up to 18 feet are planned in the

Most of the upper portions of the site along the mountainside
will not be graded
A portion of the Waimalu Stream near the central portion of
the site will be realigned
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
During the development of Pearl Harbor the surrounding valleys
which emptied into the harbor area were continuously being sub
merged and re emerged as the sea level fluctuated This caused
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED WAIMALU SUBDIVISION P 4
deposits of mud silt sand and marine clays to accumulate in
the drowned valley areas and also coral reefs to develop at a
time when the sea level was at a higher elevation then its pre
sent state When the sea level dropped erosion of these depo
sits took place until another change of sea level altered the
situation The numerous changes of sea level accompanied by the
continuous deposition and erosion of material gives Pearl Harbor
and the surrounding coastal and valley mouth areas a very complex
geologic history
Waimalu Valley where the project site is located is one such
valley that was affected in its lower reaches by the events that
developed Pearl Harbor Currently it appears that the area about
the project site is being eroded or downcut by the ephemeral flows
when raining Waimalu Stream The evidence that suggests this is
the presence of a partially consolidated silty sand which comprises
the stream bed beneath a surficial layer of stream cobbles and boul
ders This silty sand was also exposed in the stream banks and in
an area where the bank was about 6 feet vertical Since the stream
appears to be cutting into this partially consolidated silty sand
deposit tt suggests downcutting rather than deposition within the
area investigated
Another observation about the project area is the localized surface
deposits of adobe dark brown clay CH near the base of the
valley walls veneering the lower portion of the talus slope but not
extending itself down near the stream It appears that the adobe
was deposited after the silty sand and both of these covered the
valley floor from wall to wall Then with the subsequent down
cutting by the stream the adobe along the valley floor was
removed exposing the silty sand below leaving adobe remaining
near the valley walls

of the site appears to be underlain by alluvium generally consis

In the northern portion of the site the alluvium is covered with

The upper portions of the site generally consist of talus material
over the hillside rock formation The depth of talus material
generally varied from 0 foot at the upper portions of the lots
to more than 7 feet at the base of the hillside slope

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally indicated relatively complex
soil conditions at the site The lower areas appear to be
underlain by competent alluvial soil
Portions of the upper slope areas are blanketed with expansive
adobe clay soil The clay soil should be removed to reduce
potential shrink swell and slope creep effects The excavated
Clay soil can be used in the lower portions of the proposed fill
Due to potential sloughing of the nearly vertical decomposed
rock slope and rock falls it is recommended that the slope be
flattened
Many boulders were noted at the site The boulders may be used
for rip rap slope protection and in the fill if special grading
procedures are followed
Earthwork and Grading
The present grading plan indicates that cuts up to about 18 feet
are planned in the southern portion of the site near the existing
Pono Street Fills up to about 12 feet are planned for the lower
area
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED WAIMALU SUBDIVISION P 7
Expansive adobe clay about 4 feet deep was noted on the
upper sloping area at about lots 39 to 43 The extent of the
adobe clay should be verified in the field then removed from
the sloping area and used in the lower portions of the fill
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation and
debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts compacted to
the above specified density In roadway areas the top 1 foot
should be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper then 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing
firm ground
Adobe clay should be kept a minimum of 3 feet below the finish
grade in the roadway and building areas and away from the slopes
The adobe should be capped with non expansive select material
A portion of the existing stream will be realigned and the aban
doned section will be filled To reduce underground erosion and
piping a subdrainage system consisting of perforated pipe and
granular filter material around the pipe should be provided at
the abandoned stream bed location
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests no major
settlement problems are anticipated for the proposed fill How
ever due to the possibility of localized soft areas settlement
gauges are recommended near Test Pits 6 and 8 locations
to monitor the fill

Slopes
The field exploration indicated that the proposed cuts in the
southeastern portion of the stte may be in rocky material
Design slope ratios of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used
here
Along the upper sloping area cut slope ratios of 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical can be used fn the non adobe talus material
Generally cut in the adobe soil should be avoided
For the nearly vertical decomposed rock slope noted near lots
33 to 38 slope ratios of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical can be
used The slope ratios and location may be revised after
additional surveying ts done fn this area
In general fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical should be
used If a lining or rip rap is utilized slope ratios of 1
horizontal to 1 vertical can be used The slope protection
should extend about 2 feet below the finished grade to reduce
the possibility of scour by the stream
Boulders
Numerous boulders were noted along the hillside particularly
in the northern half of the site Boulders were also noted
in some of the lower portions of the site
A boulder sweep should be considered to remove the loose boulders
along the hillside
The boulders may be used for rip rap construction and if neces
sary they can be disposed of in the deep fill areas in accor
dance with the following guidelines

Rock and boulders should be placed in windrows
not over 5 feet in width and not closer than
10 feet between windrows The height of wind
rows should not exceed 4 feet The maximum
size rock acceptable within the windrows should
not exceed 4 feet in the greatest dimension
Boulders over this size should be broken down
or disposed of
The resultant rock configurations in the windrows
will have considerable void space between the
rocks Granular material should be used for fill
along and over the rock windrows The granular
material should be thoroughly watered and consoli
dated to insure that all void spaces between the
boulders are filled Successive lifts may be made
if the fill is sufficiently deep The windrows
should be staggered horizontally and have a vertical
distance of at least 2 feet between windrows
Boulders should not be placed within 3 feet of
finished grade or above the lowest utility lines
All boulder disposal should be done under the
observation of the soil engineer to see that these
recommendations are followed Plate C 1 illustrates
the proposed rock disposal procedure

Foundations
For the sloping areas spread footing foundations are recommended
The footings should be placed on rock or extended below the ground
surface such that the outer edge of the footing has a minimum hori
zontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face
On flat areas spread footing foundations or slab on grade may be
used Slabs should be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum
of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should have a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from
the slope face Post footings away from the tops of slopes should
be placed 12 inches below the finish grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 2000 p s f can be used for
footings resting on the stiff natural soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be used on level areas where the slab subgrade
soil is non expansive structural fill material It should be
placed over a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the access road

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is

between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer See Rock Disposal Details in Soil Report
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
