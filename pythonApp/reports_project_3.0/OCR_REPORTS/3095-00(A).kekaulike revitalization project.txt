INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our vibration monitoring services performed
during the production pile driving operation at the Kekaulike Revitalization Ewa Block
Project in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field monitoring work and
presents our discussions derived from our engineering analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a four story commercial and
residential building complex on the site as a public housing development
The project site is currently bounded by low rise buildings on three sides and by
Hotel Street on the north The adjacent buildings were constructed of brick masonry
rock masonry as well as concrete masonry units Existing structural distresses such as
crack openings were observed at various locations of the building faces Due to the
relatively poor condition and proximity of the existing buildings to the project site it is
required to monitor the vibration waves generated during the pile driving operation and
evaluate their effects on the neighboring buildings under the subject project specifications

PRODUCTION PILE PROGRAM
The production pile driving operation was performed from September 28th to
October 21st A diesel hammer with a rated maximum driving energy of
approximately 69 898 foot pounds was used to drive the 16 inch octagonal precast and
prestressed concrete piles
The production pile locations were predrilled to depths ranging approximately
between 15 and 40 feet below the existing ground surface prior to the pile driving
operation A total of 276 production piles were driven to depths of about 39 to 87 feet
below the existing ground surface at the site Detailed observations of the production pile
driving operation are presented under a separate report prepared for Mitsunaga
Associates Inc The locations of the driven production piles are shown on the attached
Pile Location Plan Plate 2
METHODOLOGY
An was used to monitor the vibration generated during the
production pile driving operation A sensor geophone was placed in the proximity of the
existing structures during the course of the driving operation in order to obtain vibration
data representative of that experienced by the existing structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3095 00 A Page 4
The consists of a 3 component geophone and a signal
conditioner recorder unit The geophone senses ground motion along three mutually
perpendicular orthogonal axes thus providing detection of ground motion in the vertical
transverse and longitudinal directions The signal conditioner recorder unit receives and
processes these signals to provide a graphic record of vibrations The Blastmate Il
permits scanning of vibration levels over extended periods when operated in the Monitor
Mode Resultant peak particle velocities RPPV in units of inches per second ips are
automatically computed by vector summation of the motions along the orthogonal axes
The Blastmate Il was set at a scanning interval of every one minute during the
vibration monitoring process During the one minute scanning interval the Blastmate II
samples the vibration data at a rate of 1 024 data points per second and stores the
resultant peak level of vibration which occurs during that one minute interval At the end
of every scanning interval a stripchart record is produced on the recording paper by the
built in plotter The height of each bar on the stripchart record represents the highest
vibration level observed during each scanning interval The magnitude of the highest
vibration level observed and its corresponding time were shown on the stripchart graph
and data printout generated by the built in plotter at the completion of the driving
operation of each production pile A one page summary of each production pile driving
event can be produced by connecting the Blastmate II to a computer
During the pile driving operation the times corresponding with every foot of driven
pile length were recorded in our field monitoring log This was used to evaluate the depth
that produced the maximum vibrations during the driving of each production pile when
used in conjunction with the stripchart graph and data printout The horizontal surface
distance between the geophone and the production pile was also measured and entered
into the field log

Since vibration amplitudes for a given energy source attenuate with increasing
distance in exponential form graphic representation of vibration amplitude versus distance
data will appear linear when plotted in log log graph form
The threshold for persons to perceive vibrations occurs at a relatively low level of
motion Depending on character duration and repetitiveness of vibrations perception
may occur at levels between 0 01 and These levels are well below those
required to cause damage to typical structures
Vibration damage thresholds for pile driving induced motions have not been
specifically established A number of researchers have presented a variety of proposed
criteria The criteria that we normally use are related to the type of construction age and
general condition of structures and are adapted from a variety of studies of actual
damage thresholds observed for blasting operations and other vibration inducing sources
Based on Chae 1978 the following recommended threshold of damage criteria should
provide reasonable indicators for reducing the potential for direct vibration damage
Class 4 inches per second
for structures of substantial construction
Class Il 2 inches per second
for relatively new residential structures in sound condition
Class Ill 7 inch per second
for relatively old residential structures in poor condition
Class inch per second
for old residential structures in very poor condition
Reference Chae Y S 1978 Design of Excavation Blasts to
Prevent Damage Civil Engineering Vol 48 No 79

The structures adjacent to the Kekaulike Revitalization project site may be classified
as Class Ill to Class IV structures Because of the repetitive nature of pile driving
vibrations it has been recommended that the above categories be lowered by one class
Thus a vibration damage threshold criteria of 0 5 inch per second was used for the
subject project
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Vibration monitoring work for the production pile driving operation was conducted
between September 29th and October 21st A numerically ordered summary of the
resultant peak vibration levels observed during the production pile driving operations is
presented on Plates 3 through 12 Attenuation plots with different hammer settings for
the resultant peak vibration data obtained for the production pile driving operation are
presented on Plates 13 through 15 A regression analyses power curve was performed
on the vibration data to determine representative rates of vibration attenuation with
increasing distance from the source and these lines are also shown on the same plots
The Class III Reduced threshold value of was also drawn on these plots The
intersection of the regression line with the Class III Reduced criteria line suggests that
damages may occur to structures that are located within this distance of the vibration
source
Based on our vibration monitoring results obtained during the test pile driving
operation damages may occur to structures that are located within 30 feet of the vibration
source Due to the proximity of some of the production piles to the existing structures
efforts were made to reduce the potential adverse effects on the buildings from vibration
generated during the pile driving operation These efforts included increasing the
predrilling depths and using a lower hammer setting during the pile driving operations

Results obtained from the vibration monitoring performed during the production pile
driving operation appeared to indicate that the distance of concern has been decreased
from approximately 20 feet to 9 feet when the hammer setting is lowered from No 3 to
No 2 or 1 In addition the vibration levels obtained using setting No 1 is substantially
lower than that of setting No 2 Although some of the vibration levels recorded did
exceed the recommended threshold limit for the existing buildings we understand that
no significant structural damages on the existing structures have been reported at the
time of preparation of this report Based on our discussion with the contractor it
appeared that some minor cracking did occur in the existing buildings which could be a
result of the pile driving operations performed at the subject site
