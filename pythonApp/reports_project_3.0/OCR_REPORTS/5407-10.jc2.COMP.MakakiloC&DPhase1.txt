Project Considerations
The project site is on the eastern side of Makakilo Drive in the Makakilo area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
involves site grading for 180 single family units The site development also includes the
construction of a public roadway system within the residential zoned portion of the
project site and installation of sewer water supply and lighting facilities The
approximate project limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site grading involved cuts and fills up to 20 feet relative to the original existing
ground surface to achieve the design grades for the development The constructed
slopes generally have inclinations of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter with
8 foot wide benches at maximum 20 foot vertical height intervals
Site Grading
Royal Contracting Company performed the earthwork operations for the project At
the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally cleared and
grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from the clearing
operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing operations the
areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were compacted using a sheepsfoot
and or smooth drum vibratory roller

In general on site excess soil materials generated from the excavation were
primarily used in the trench backfill and some fill slopes The on site fill materials generally
consisted of silty and clayey soils with some gravel In addition on site rock materials
generated from the excavation were crushed to 3 inch minus meeting selected granular fill
requirements
The expansion of the on site fill material ranged from low to high Samples of the
fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained and transported to our
office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report
In general fill materials consist of low expansive 3 inch minus materials placed for
the fills and utility trench backfills while 12 inch minus general fills were placed below
5 feet from finished subgrade Field density tests were performed in general accordance
with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method where appropriate During the fill and
backfill operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near
the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based
on visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were
performed during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
Over excavation of about 2 to 3 feet was conducted at the lots where expansive
soil was encountered at the subgrade of the house foundation On site or imported low
expansive soils were used to backfill the over excavation
Some of the lots were partially cut and partially filled The shallow fill and cut
portions of the lots were over excavated to 2 feet below finished grade and re compacted
In general the fills were compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction where the
thickness of the fills was less than 15 feet When the thickness of the fills were greater
than 15 feet the upper 15 feet of the fills were compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction and the remaining fills more than 15 feet below the finished grade were
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Keying and benching consisting of 12 feet wide and 2 feet deep were constructed
at the toe of the fill slope Boulder fill was used in the deep embankment fill areas under
Road A from about Station 2 00 to Station 8 00 In general boulders 12 to 36 inches in
diameter generated during site grading were placed in a lift of about 3 to 5 feet in
thickness at the bottom of the roadway embankment where there were more than 20 feet
of fill The boulders were spread out with finer material placed and compacted to fill the
spaces between boulders

in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The results of the

Additional Recommendations for Homeowner
Homeowners should immediately verify whether their swales have been
compromised or altered from the original condition of initial move in If these swales have
been altered consult your plot plan and reinstall the swales Swales should also be
grassed as leaving them bare will result in soil erosion during heavy rains
If the swales can not be completely reinstalled because of constructed wall
obstructions installation of drain pipe along the rear and sides of the property can help
drain excess water away from the sidewalks and building slabs
If a homeowner constructs improvements or expansions beyond the original floor
area for example expanding the lanai adding another 1st floor bedroom or family room
etc the services of a qualified geotechnical engineer should be retained to evaluate the
soil conditions at their respective lot for site specific building recommendations
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the
surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements Lot owners should also be advised not to water
excessively adjacent to the house structure
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
