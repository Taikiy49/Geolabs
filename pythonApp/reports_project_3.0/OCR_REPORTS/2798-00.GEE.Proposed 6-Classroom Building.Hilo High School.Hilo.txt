SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain at
shallow depths by an in situ basaltic rock formation which may
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed three story
classroom building
It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used to support the structure
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 6 000 p s f may be used
for the design of footing foundations bearing on in situ rock
formation
Foundations bearing on in situ rock should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
except in the vicinity of Boring No 2 where the foundations
should extend through the upper highly weathered gravel layer
to bear on competent rock formation
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed and
special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed 6 classroom building at the Hilo High School in Hilo on the island of Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new 6 classroom building for the Hilo High School in
Hilo on the island of Hawaii
Based on the site plan dated provided to us on
a three story classroom building is proposed for the project The new building will measure
approximately eighty five 85 feet by fifty five 55 feet in plan and will accommodate
six 6 classrooms with two 2 classrooms on each floor
Based on the information provided to us the estimated column and wall loads will
be approximately 185 kips and 9 kips ft respectively It is anticipated that prestressed
concrete floors with reinforced concrete walls will be utilized for the proposed 6 classroom
building
A new 5 stall parking area and two 2 new concrete walkways are also planned
for the project
The finish grade of the proposed classroom building is estimated to be at approximately
160 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Therefore minimal site grading is anticipated to
be required for the proposed construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the Hilo High School in Hilo on the island of Hawaii
The building site is located on vacant grassed land bounded by existing Buildings
A B and F to the southeast southwest and northwest respectively It is accessible from
Waianuenue Avenue via a driveway between the existing buildings and a track field located
to the east of the site The site and its development are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing ground surface at the site slopes gently downward to the east towards
the driveway from the toe of a small slope rising to Building B located adjacent to the western
side of the site From the topographic map provided the existing ground elevations range
from approximately 158 feet to 168 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
A metal drainage pipe was observed daylighting within the small slope A large boulder
partially embedded was also observed at the site during our initial visit

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
of five 5 borings to depths ranging between twenty four 24 and thirty 30 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a thin layer of fill material composed of clayey
silt approximately three 3 inches thick overlying in situ basaltic rock formation In Boring
Nos 1 and 2 a deposit of volcanic ash was encountered from twenty 20 to twenty two
and one half 22 5 feet below the ground surface down to the maximum depths drilled
Ash was also encountered in Boring No 4 at a depth of sixteen and one half 16 5 to
approximately eighteen and one half 18 5 feet below the ground surface
Our laboratory testing indicates that the volcanic ash soil has very high in situ moisture
contents in excess of two hundred 200 percent This is typical for ash deposits which
are locally referred to as Pahala Ash Generally Pahala Ash soils may lose strength when
remolded by construction activity or when disturbed by outside influences such as seismic
vibrations
It is our opinion however that the ash deposit are at sufficient depth within the overlying
basalt and have been sufficiently consolidated by the overburden as to pose no threat to
the proposed structure
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface conditions are presented on the Boring Logs
in Appendix A

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation
Based on the information provided to us the finish grade of the proposed classroom
building will be at approximately 160 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Therefore minimal
site grading is anticipated and the finished building pad level will be on or close to the basaltic
rock formation encountered It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous footings
and slab on grade construction may be used to support the proposed building
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 6 000 p s f may be utilized for the design
of footing foundations bearing on the in situ rock formation Foundations bearing on in situ
rock should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
exterior grade except in the vicinity of Boring No 2 where the foundations should extend
through the upper highly weathered gravel layer to bear on competent rock formation at
about two 2 feet below the existing ground surface
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased by
up to one third 1 3 for loads of short duration such as those caused by wind and seismic
loads
If soft loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they
should be over excavated to competent in situ material and replaced with granular select
borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 78
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less

Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be provided by
passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommended that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive soil pressure developed
against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundations and a frictional coefficient of
0 4 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the footing bottoms
and the rock formation
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should
be removed and disposed of properly off site
The thin surface clayey silt soil layer should be removed below the proposed building
area
It is Our opinion that excavation in the basaltic rock formation encountered at the
site may require hard ripping or hoerams Care must be exercised to avoid over ripping
which would disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting in a potential loss of bearing
strength
If imported fill material is required the material should be a select granular material
less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 15 passing the No 200 sieve
Fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose
thickness and then compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the building
foundations
Utility Trenches
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should be used for the trench backfill
up to about one foot above the pipes to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from a level one foot above the
pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
and placed in maximum ten 10 inch horizontallifts mechanically compacted to aminimum
of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence

Slab on Grade
We recommended a minimum four 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine or ASTM C 33
No 67 cushion be installed below the slabs to provide uniform support for the slabs
The thickened slab footings can bear on the in situ rock with a minimum footing
embedment of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
A moisture barrier membrane covered with a one 1 inch layer of sand is also
recommended between the cushion layer and the slabs
Concrete Walkway
Two 2 new concrete walkways are anticipated for the proposed 6 classroom building
We recommend that a minimum six 6 inch thick base course cushion be provided below
the walkway slabs
Joints should be provided at close intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow
for some relative movements of the concrete slabs

engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the site grading and construction of the foundations This is
to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
lf actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate
the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
determined by taping from features shown on the topographic map prepared by Hilo Engineering
Inc dated Elevations of the field borings were estimated by interpolation
from the elevations on the same map The physical locations and elevations of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
for specific application to the proposed 6 classroom building project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty
expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the architect engineer
in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this report may
not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation
of construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain
a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in
the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly constructed
project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to accommodate these
possible extra costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
mE
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of five 5
borings to depths ranging between 24 0 and 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
by a truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary core drilling
tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
standard spilt spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core
of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is recovered
from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on the Boring
Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality based on the Practical Handbook
of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 5 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100
