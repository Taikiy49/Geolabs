I INTRODUCTION
A GENERAL
This report presents the results of a soils investigation
for the proposed project The study consisted of subsurface
borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses as outlined
in our proposal dated and our letter concern
ing additional requirements dated March 23 1972 Based on the
results of our engineering studies conclusions and recommenda
tiohs pertinent to site development are presented
B PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located at Waipahu Field near
the corner of Paiwa Street and Kahuamoku Street It is approxi
mately 40 feet from the existing Gym Annex and 50 feet from the
existing Swimming Pools
The proposed multi purpose building is presently planned
as a 2 story concrete structure Column spacing will be approxi
mately 25 feet with a 40 foot bay length The maximum column
loads are expected to be on the order of 120 kips per column

II FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of 1 boring which was
nee
subsequently deepened to 76 5 feet The drilling was accomplished
with truck mounted rotary drilling equipment and continuous
flight hollow stem auger The boring was continuously inspected
by an engineer or geologist from our firm
The approximate boring location is shown on Plate 1 and the
boring log is presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5
inch 0 D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals
not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists
of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 20 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of
a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field and
representative material was placed in air tight containers and
returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing

III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A GEOLOGY
The proposed Arts and Crafts building to be constructed at
Waipahu Field is situated in a nearly level portion of a
valley plain north of Farrington Highway Geologically this
area was part of an old embayment where the upper Middle Loch
probably merged with the West Loch and extended Pearl Harbor
further inland than at the present time This embayment was
filled with clays and silts washed in from the nearby hills
and later became a marsh where considerable organic material
developed
B SOILS
The boring logs indicate 4 feet of medium red Clayey SILT
overlying approximately 6 feet of very soft organic silt and
30 feet of very soft gray Clayey SILT Below 40 feet the
soft embayment muds overlie an older alluvium consisting of
very soft to very stiff brown Clayey SILT with several water
bearing strata The older alluvium appears to overlie
weathered basalt at 75 foot depth
IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions and
recommendations are presented
A GENERAL
soil conditions at the proposed building site In its present
condition the site is unsuitable for supporting even light struc
tural loads on spread or continuous footings because of the
excessive settlements anticipated
The alternates available for development of the site are
1 a pile foundation system to transfer the anticipated struc
tural loads to an adequate bearing stratum or 2 change the
proposed building design to a lighter structure that can tolerate
differential settlement and use a temporary preload fill and
spread or continuous footings for foundation support
Based on discussion with the architect and structural engineer
a pile foundation system would not be economically feasible for
this project We have therefore concentrated our studies on the
preload fill alternate
4
B PRELOAD FILL
In order to decrease total settlement and minimize differ
ential settlements due to column and floor loading we recommend
that the building area be preloaded with a minimum surcharge
ef 7 feet of dense fill
Fill material beneath finish floor elevation should be a
compacted structural fill The aditional 7 feet of surcharge
fill above finished floor elevation need only be moderately
compacted
The top of the preload fill should extend at least 20 feet
outside the perimeter of the building The temporary fill slopes
should not be steeper than 3H 1V
The required duration of the preload surcharge fill for the
conditions encountered should be approximately 6 months however
the actual time required for satisfactory results must be deter
mined by settlement observations made during and after construc
tion of the fill
C FOUNDATIONS
Spread or continuous footings may be utilized to support
relatively light structural loads in the area treated by the
preload surcharge fill An allowable net bearing capacity of
500 psf may by used for footing design The footings should
have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be at least 12 inches
below the adjacent ground surface

D SETTLEMENTS
Anticipated settlements due to primary consolidation could
be greater than 5 inches for the untreated site Settlements
due to secondary compression could equal primary settlements
It is therefore evident that the site should not be developed
without remedial work or pile foundations
Settlements that occur due to the preload surcharge fill
Should range between 4 and 8 inches Removal of the surcharge
fill will be based on the field time settlement curves Set
tlements on the order of inch should be anticipated as far as
100 feet from the preload area
E STRUCTURAL FILL
Structural fill should be imported material consisting of
a minimum of 2 feet of free draining granular material compacted
to 90 of the maximum densities based on ASTM D 1557 70 The
fill should be placed in lifts not exceeding 12 inches thick
and should have no rocks larger than 4 inches thick
The site must be cleared and grubbéd prior to placing struc _
tural fill to remove all deleterious matter
F FLOOR SLABS
Floor slabs should be supported on a minimum of 12 inches of
compacted granular fill provided preload fill surcharging has
been accomplished The slabs should be free floating i e not
connected to the footings
6
G CONSTRUCTION CONTROL
Careful instrumentation and control of the preload fill is
necessary in order to evaluate the performance of the project
At least three settlement plates should be installed across
the site to record the settlements due to the preload surcharge
fill The plates should be placed on a 6 inch thick mat of
compacted granular fill of existing ground elevation For
details of settlement plate construction see Plate 2 These
settlement observation devices must be protected against damage
from construction activity
The installation of the settlement plates and evaluation
of the data should be performed under the supervision of the
soils engineer

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
LABORATORY TESTING
1 Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from the
borings were brought to our laboratory where they were visually
reclassified to confirm to modify the field classification
prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
2 Water Content Determinations In addition to visual classi
fication the natural moisture content was determined for all
samples to aid in soil classification and evaluating soil properties
The water content values based upon the dry weight of the soil
are shown on the Boring Logs
3 Unconfined Compression Tests Selected split spoon soil
samples were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid scatter
of test results and to facilitate evaluation of shear strength
values for design purposes Strength values designated Qy are
shown on the Boring Logs
4 Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed on
selected samples of the compressible soils to provide basic
data for making settlement calculations In this test undisturbed
samples were carefully trimmed and fitted into a rigid ring
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were then applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior to
adding the next increment Measurements of the time compression
were obtained during each load increment and rebound was measured
during the unloading portion Consolidation test results plotted
in terms of percent consolidation versus applied loads are
presented on Plates B 1l 2
5 Shear Tests Shear tests were performed in the Direct Shear
Machine which is of the strain control type The rate of deforma
tion is approximately 0 025 inches per minute Each sample is
sheared under varying confining loads in order to determine the
Coulomb shear strength parameters cohesion and angle of internal
friction Samples are tested at the field moisture and in an
artificially saturated condition depending upon the sample
location and future site conditions The results are plotted
on the Shear Test Diagram
