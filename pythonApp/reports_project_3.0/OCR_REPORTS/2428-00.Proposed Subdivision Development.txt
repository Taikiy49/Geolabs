INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kalama Valley Subdivision in the
Maunalua District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Kalama Valley in Maunalua
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Mokuhano
Street on the south existing residential lots on the west Kalama Valley
School playground on the north and a drainage ditch on the east
The site grades from the north to the south with a slight knoll at
the southeastern portion A very dense basalt rock outcrop was observed
in the northern portion of the site
The area is presently vacant and overgrown with grasses and shrubs

Geology
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The
range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe and aa basalt
lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing mountain
trends During the Late Tertiary and Pleistocene Ice Age deep valleys
including Kalama Valley were eroded by streams
Later during the Pleistocene Period volcanics of the Koko fissure
named the Honolulu Volcanic Series erupted and deposited ash and cinders
from several vents trending in a southwest to northeast direction The
site lies to the south of a cinder cone that spread a lava flow down
towards the ocean During higher stands of sea level in the Pleistocene
the valleys were infilled with sediments alluviated grading to high sea
level stands Underlying these sediments are the older volcanic tuff and
cinder deposits Later man developed the area and has removed the cinder
cone and reworked portions of the site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The findings disclosed that the on site upper clayey silt soils are
moderately to highly expansive
In general we believe that if the on site expansive soils are capped
with at least three 3 feet of imported non expansive borrow fill
material the potential for future swelling can be reduced significantly
To minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs
driveways and sidewalks we recommend that the on site soils be capped
with at least 36 inches of select import non expansive fill Also that
the subgrade soils underneath the above select fill should be moistened
and protected from drying from the project construction until the select
fill capping is completed

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered
at fill areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and
then replaced with compacted non expansive select material
Relatively hard excavation i e hoeram or chipping may be
anticipated for cuts in the northern rock outcrop area
The fills and backfills within the top 36 inches minimm below the
finished grade should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is
anticipated the on site soil should be over excavated down to the
underlying tuff basalt or to provide a 36 inch minimm thickness of
non expansive select capping fill below the finished grade
The fill areas should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material
The on site cut silty soils may be used for fill if they are placed
below 36 inches from the finished grade and away from retaining walls
and compacted at least 5 above optimum moisture content

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the building and that gutter water be diverted far away from the structure
foundation and walkways Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water would cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3 Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earth work and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
