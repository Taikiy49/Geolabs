INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Gas LPG Storage and Distribution Facility Relocation project at Pier
38 in Honolulu Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are
intended for design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for
construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Gas Company site at Pier 38 in
Honolulu Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided to
us the relocation work at the subject facility would include the following
1 Relocation of four existing 30 000 gallon gas storage tanks to the area
adjacent to the pier gate at the northern corner of the Gas Company site
These tanks are above ground structures cylindrical in shape and weigh
about 188 kips each under fully loaded condition The four tanks will be
placed horizontally parallel to one another and supported by a saddle on
both ends It is our understanding that the saddles will be supported on
a large strip footing foundation at each end
2 Construction of a new restroom facility with a floor area of approximately
100 square feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3297 00 Page 3
3 Construction of new asphalt concrete parking areas for personne
vehicles bobtail tanker and tractor trailer tanker adjacent to the relocated
storage tanks
Our previous experience in the area suggested that the project site may be
underlain by a surficial man made fill layer over soft and compressible lagoonal
deposits Special attention was given to the thickness of the surficial crust and the
feasibility of utilizing shallow footing foundations for support of the relocated storage
tanks

The project site is located within the existing Gas Company site at Pier 38 in
Honolulu Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The area under the present scope of

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings The
maximum depth for borings B 1 and B 2 was approximately 13 and 81 feet below the
existing ground surface respectively The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration disclosed that the area where the storage tanks will be
relocated is generally underlain by a surface layer of fill materials over soft and
compressible lagoonal deposits at deeper depths In Boring No 1 the surface fill layer
consisted mainly of medium dense sand and gravel materials extending to a depth of
approximately 11 feet below the existing ground surface This was underlain by soft
silty clays with the boring terminated at a depth of approximately 13 feet below the
existing ground surface
In Boring No 2 which is located approximately 10 feet away from the edge of
the rock sea wall boulder fills with sandy gravel materials were encountered to a depth
of approximately 12 feet below the existing ground surface This was underlain by
approximately 11 feet of lagoonal deposit consisting of soft and compressible silty
clays The boring then encountered loose to medium dense sand and gravel deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a
relatively thick layer of fill material over soft and compressible lagoonal deposits
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site and the anticipated
loadings from the storage tanks it is our opinion that shallow foundations consisting of
spread and or continuous footings bearing on the surficial fill layer may be used to
support the relocated storage tanks We recommend that the footing foundations be
structurally reinforced such that it behaves as a rigid slab and that flexible joints and or
connections be used for piping wherever possible to reduce the potential for piping
distress due to differential settlements In addition we recommend that the edge of the
footing foundations be set back a minimum distance of 10 feet away from the top of the
GEOLABS HAWAII

Foundations
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a
relatively thick layer of fill materials over a soft and compressible layer of lagoonal
deposits Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the anticipated tank loads
it is our opinion that shallow footing foundations such as spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the relocated storage tanks provided that the
recommendations presented herein are properly followed
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 1 250 pounds per square foot p s f may
be used for the design of shallow footing foundations bearing on the near surface fill
materials This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The footing should have a minimum width of 5 feet and be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade It is feasible to have all the tanks
share the same foundation However we recommend that the footing foundations be
structurally reinforced such that it behaves as a rigid slab to reduce the potential impact
on the strip footing due to differential settlements Due to the limited information
available pertaining to the quality and integrity of the existing rock sea wall we
recommend that the edge of the footing foundations be set back a minimum distance
of 10 feet away from the top of the rock wall slope to reduce the potential for
surcharging the existing rock wall We recommend that a detailed inspection be
conducted for the rock sea wall to evaluate its existing conditions and that appropriate
actions and measures be implemented if needed to improve its quality and integrity
for long term stability and performance

We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated tank loads for the strip
footings bearing on the recompacted fill soils to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility tench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around the footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient
of friction of 0 3 may be used for foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ fill
soils
We understand that a restroom facility of slab on grade construction is planned
for the subject project The perimeter thickened edge footing foundations should be

recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be provided below the concrete slab as a cushion layer
Depending on the quality control and workmanship the existing surface fill
encountered at the site may not be uniformly placed nor adequately compacted
Therefore we recommend that the subgrades below all footing bottom be scarified to

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated finish grades minimal site
grading work consisting of minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 foot is
anticipated for the subject project
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular select fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with 6
to 8 inch thick horizontal lifts of compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future footings slabs
and pavement areas should be over excavated to the proposed finish subgrades The
over excavated surface should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to about optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Loose and or soft spots
encountered during the over excavation and scarification process should be removed
and replaced with compacted select granular fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3297 00 Page 12
Select granular fill material needed for backfilling over excavation should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Select granular fill material should consist
of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand
The materia should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than
3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent
when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain Jess
than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested
and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use
Select borrow required for the pavement areas should conform to the
requirements presented in Section 30 of the latest edition of the Standard Specifications
for Public Works Construction City and County of Honolulu
We recommend that the project geotechnical engineer Geolabs Hawaii be
retained to provide field observation and monitoring services during the site preparation
and footing excavations at the subject project in order to observe compliance with our
recommendations and if necessary to provide additional consultation
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about one foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
GEOLABS HAWAII

to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 10 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction

Laboratory Testing

these tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 inch ring swell test was performed on selected sample to evaluate the
swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One California Bearing Ratio test was performed on a bulk sample to evaluate
the pavement support characteristics The test results are presented on Plate B 2
