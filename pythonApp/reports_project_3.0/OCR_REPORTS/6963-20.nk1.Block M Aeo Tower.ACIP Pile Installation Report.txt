PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is at the southwest corner of Queen and Kamakee Streets in the 
Kakaako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site was previously 
occupied by the Office Depot and Nordstrom Rack stores and an on-grade parking lot.  
The general contractor for the project was Layton Construction Co., Inc. and the ACIP pile 
installation subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. (HDCC). 
Based on the geotechnical technical memorandum referenced above for the 
project, we recommended supporting the new high-rise condominium and parking 
structure on a deep foundation system consisting of 24-inch ACIP piles with an allowable 
compressive load capacity of up to 1,200 kips per pile. Most of the ACIP piles would 
extend through the soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits (near the surface) and derive 
support primarily from skin friction and end-bearing within coral ledges and/or coralline 
detritus materials encountered below the lagoonal deposit. The ACIP pile length of up to 
about 90 feet was utilized for the 1,200-kip capacity piles. The following table summarizes 
the ACIP pile foundation lengths utilized for the project.

OSTERBERG METHOD LOAD TEST 
As part of the project specifications, one sacrificial 24-inch diameter load test pile 
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, 
Plates 2.1 through 2.4. The load test pile was installed by HDCC on March 25, 2016.  The 
load test pile was installed with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage 
instrumentation.  
A Manitowoc 4100W series crane equipped with automatic monitoring equipment, a 
crane-mounted Bauer HD 460 Rotary Drill System with a 120 feet long continuous hollow 
stem auger, and Berminghammer lead system were utilized to install the ACIP test pile. A 
hydraulic concrete pump with an average stroke volume of about 0.092 cubic yard per 
stroke was used to pump the grout into the pile system. The 24-inch diameter load test pile 
was extended to a depth of about 95 feet below the existing ground surface.  
After the load test pile was installed and cured for about 7 days, Loadtest USA 
performed the load test on April 1, 2016. The 24-inch diameter load test pile was loaded in

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of installing 326 ACIP 
piles, was conducted from April 12, 2016 through May 19, 2016 under the 
near-continuous (full-time) observations by a field engineer from our office. HDCC utilized 
a Manitowoc 4100W series crane equipped with a crane-mounted Bauer HD 460 Rotary 
Drill System with a 120-foot long continuous hollow stem auger and Berminghammer lead 
system to install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on 
the drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, 
grout pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed 
are shown on the ACIP Pile Identification Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.4. The production 
ACIP pile installation summary is presented by pile number on Plates 3.1 through 3.14 of 
this letter report. 
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction.  The associated problems and the corresponding resolutions are presented 
in the “Remarks” column of the ACIP Pile Installation Summary. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been 
installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the allowable design load 
capacities.
