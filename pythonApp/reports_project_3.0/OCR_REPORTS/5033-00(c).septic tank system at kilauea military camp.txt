In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have recently
conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Septic Tank System at Kilauea
Military Camp project This data report provides the boring log information and field
percolation and laboratory test results for the above project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves work necessary to prepare Design Build
Request For Proposal RFP solicitation documents for the construction of leaching fields
and septic tanks at the Kilauea Military Camp on the Island of Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the Kilauea site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided there are six new leaching fields and 23 new septic
tanks proposed at the Kilauea site Field percolation testing was performed at the leaching
field sites Additional borings were drilled in the vicinity of the new septic tanks
FIELD EXPLORATION
Field Borings
A total of ten borings designated as Boring Nos K 1 through K 10 were drilled at
the Kilauea site The borings were drilled to depths of about 9 5 to 10 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger and coring
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
Outside Diameter OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6
inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Boring at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the basalt rock formation encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Field Percolation Testing
A total of six field percolation tests were performed to depths ranging from 3 5 to
5 feet below the existing ground surface The percolation tests were performed near
Boring Nos K 1 through K 6 The field percolation testing was performed in general
accordance with the provisions of Chapter Systems State of Hawaii
Department of Health The percolation test results are presented on the Site

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate C 1
Five Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on rock core
samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the underlying basalt rock
formation The test results are summarized on Plate C 2
LIMITATIONS
The preliminary geotechnical field and laboratory data submitted in this report are
based upon information obtained from limited field borings Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until additional exploration is underway
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the site plan transmitted by Parsons Brinckerhoff on
The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented in the Logs of Borings of this report were
based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic
representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variation in rainfall temperature and other factors
