INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed drain outlet at Kewalo Basin for the proposed Ward Avenue
extension located south of the Ward Avenue and Ala Moana Boulevard intersection in
the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation fill
placement and excavation only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 6 foot by 5 foot reinforced concrete box culvert drain
outlet near one of the boat slips in Kewalo Basin The outlet is planned near the edge of
an existing concrete and wood wharf deck with an invert level at approximately minus 6 to
minus 8 feet mean sea level MSL A new concrete outlet structure and replacement of
a portion of the existing deck and riprap are included in this portion of work The drain
system is part of the proposed Ward Avenue Extension project that will extend from the
southern end of Ward Avenue to Ilalo Street
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was used to develop
an idealized soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations fill placement and excavation
for the proposed project Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau shield reached the
end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago The project site is located
on the southwestern flank of Koolau
As the main shield building on the island of Oahu drew to a close the island began
to subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing coral algal reefs began to grow
on the seaward margins of the island
The project site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain a thick sedimentary
wedge composed of intercalated coral algal reef deposits terrigenous and marine
sediments and volcanics of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed drain outlet at Kewalo Basin is located south of the Ward Avenue
and Ala Moana Boulevard intersection in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The project site is located on the western side of Kewalo Basin near a boat slip
and a docking pier The outlet is located near the edge of the pier and near an existing
chain link fence and gate The project site is bounded by concrete masonry unit CMU
buildings to the north and west The proposed drain outlet alignment crosses through a
concrete bulkhead with a CMU wall above and a rock faced riprap slope below and
beneath a concrete topped wharf deck supported by wooden beams and steel piers The
concrete bulkhead is about 4 feet high and is inclined at about 4H 1V The rock faced
riprap is inclined at about The proposed drain outlet alignment may cross
through a sheetpile bulkhead though its existence cannot be confirmed at this time A

Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the proposed 6 foot by
5 foot reinforced concrete box culvert drain outlet structure may be supported by a
shallow foundation system bearing on 30 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation wrapped with geotextile fabric AMOCO 4545 or similar

New Replacement Piers Deep Foundations
Our field exploration did not encounter a distinct coral layer with sufficient strength
and thickness to develop refusal for high capacity end bearing piles The coral formation
encountered at a depth of 39 feet from the top of the wharf deck was found to have
variable hardness and highly fractured with thick sand seams throughout the coral
formation Therefore we recommend that piles utilizing a combination of end bearing
and frictional resistance in the coralline materials be used to support the new or
replacement piers It is our opinion that a 12 inch square prestressed precast concrete
pile driven into the loose sands and gravels to a depth of about 40 feet from the existing
top of wharf deck elevation would be capable of supporting an allowable design load of
20 tons
To avoid disturbance to existing piles a clearance space of 1 diameters of the
larger pile should be maintained It is our understanding that the existing piles will not be
depended upon to carry loads
Based on the plans six replacement piles are anticipated The row of 3 piles
closest to the pier will likely encounter the rock faced riprap slope This row of 3 piles
should be pre drilled to a depth beyond the bottom of the riprap about 5 to 10 feet thick
Due to the uncertainty of the adequacy of the current riprap buikhead structure these
three piles are designed to carry the deck structural load currently imposed on the
riprap bulkhead structure

Dewatering
Dewatering of the seawater will be necessary for the construction of the outlet
structure Therefore we recommend that dewatering provisions such as a National
Pollutant Discharge Elimination Plan NPDES permit be included in the contract
documents for construction The Contractor should consult his her independent
consultant or the State of Hawaii Department of Health for the latest regulations and
information pertaining to the permit application
Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the Contractor The selection
of equipment and methods of dewatering should be decided by the Contractor who
should be aware that modifications to the dewatering system may be required during
construction depending on conditions encountered The dewatering method selected
should have minimal impact on the water level surrounding the proposed excavation In
addition dewatering operations should be performed such that only clear water is
pumped Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a blowing or
heaving of the excavation bottom or sides
It should be noted that the subsoils in the zone where dewatering will take place
consist of sandy materials which may be considered to be relatively permeable It is our
opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents should be written to
include all works or systems required to lower the natural water table and or to exclude
the water from the excavations to allow for construction of the proposed structures under
safe and dry conditions
Dewatering Considerations
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selection of a
suitable method of dewatering

Dewatering Precaution and Monitoring
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
water level must be carefully evaluated by the Contractor prior to dewatering It is
recommended that a qualified Geotechnical Engineer be retained by the
Contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the Contractor His her qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during
construction and to implement as soon as possible necessary precautionary or
remedial measures including but not limited to stopping or slowing down the
dewatering operations
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with shoring installation such that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected

All fills and backfills from 2 feet above the water table up to the finish grade should

lowest point and continue upward in level compacted lifts
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Structural fill and or backfill material should consist of well graded granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or more and should have a

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling one
boring designated as Boring No 1 to a depth of about 78 feet below the top of the
existing wharf deck elevation at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with rotary wash and rock
coring equipment
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch
drive are shown on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths
Nine sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Results of these tests are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
