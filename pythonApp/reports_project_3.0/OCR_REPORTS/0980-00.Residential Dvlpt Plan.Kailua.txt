This report presents the results of our soil investigation
and percolation testing for the proposed silting basins at
the proposed residential development in Kailua Koolaupoko
Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions in the general area of the pro
posed silting basin locations and to evaluate the in situ
seepage characteristics of the existing soil along the edge
of the proposed development for the silting basin design
and construction
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The general area for the proposed silting basins will be

Manu Mele Street in Kailua
The topography along the northwestern boundary of the pro
posed residential development where the silting basins are
planned is relatively flat with ground surface elevations
ranging from about 10 0 on the north to 15 0 feet on the
south
The eastern end of the planned silting basin area has a 25 to
35 feet steep ridge bank which slopes down towards the
eastern lower portion Kawainui Swamp side of the site
FIELD EXPLORATION
Three 3 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled to a
depth of 5 0 feet each below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The drilling operation was monitored by our engineer who also
logged the borings and obtained the representative soil sam
ples A description of materials encountered and laboratory
test results are presented on the boring logs Plate A 1
through A 3

PERCOLATION TESTS
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the three
drill holes
After completion of the bore hole drilling and sampling each
hole was filled with water and allowed to saturate Each
hole was refilled with water to various depths and the water
allowed to percolate through the subsoils The changes of
water level drop with time was monitored and recorded
The test data is presented on Plate 2 and the test results
are summarized on Plate 3

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the percolation test results obtained from the three
drilled bore holes the in situ clayey silt materials under
lying the general area of the proposed Silting basins indi
cated low percolation rates The underlying soil is moist
and can generally be considered impervious Therefore it
is our opinion that the proposed silting basin design which
was based on construction of berm and then utilizing the
existing ground surface behind the berm to provide seepage
may be unfeasible due to the low percolation rates of the
underlying materials
We recommend that the proposed silting basin berm can still
be designed and constructed above the existing ground surface
It will serve to collect silts from upper surface run off at
the lower portion of the silting basin After the silt
from the run off has settled the de silted water could
then be discharged out to the swamp through pipe outlets
buried at the upper section of the silting bern However
some maintenance might be required to remove the accumulated
Silt on a peridioc basis
