Dear Mr. Castell: 
In general, the Park Lane Ala Moana project involves expansion of the makai 
(southern) portion of the shopping center by demolishing the existing parking deck in this 
area and constructing a three-level parking structure below multi-level residential 
condominium structures. The site is generally bounded by Neiman Marcus to the east, Ala 
Moana Boulevard to the south, Piikoi Street to the west, and the existing mall and mall 
expansion (currently under construction) to the north.  
In accordance with the project design requirements, static compressive pile load 
tests were conducted on two dedicated augered cast-in-place (ACIP) piles, designated as 
Test Pile Nos. 1 and 2A, on November 25 and 26, 2014 and December 19, 2014, 
respectively. This letter report presents the results of our engineering analysis, field 
observations, and test data obtained from the static compressive pile load tests. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Previously, we provided geotechnical engineering recommendations for the ACIP 
Pile foundation design, and our recommendations were presented in our report entitled, 
“Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, Park Lane Ala Moana, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii”, 
dated June 25, 2014.  
The pile load tests were conducted on two 18-inch diameter ACIP piles, each with a 
length of approximately 75 feet relative to the existing ground surface. The load test piles 
were installed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company (HDCC) using a Bauer 
BG-28 drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the drilling depth, 
drilling rate, auger torque, stroke count, etc., on a near-continuous basis. HDCC used 
continuous flight augers to drill each pile. As the augers were removed, the hole was 
grouted from the bottom upwards. After sound quality grout (based on visual observation) 
appeared at the top of the pile, a reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole. The 
approximate location of each load test pile are shown on the Load Test Pile Location Plan,

To provide support for the load tests, two 18-inch ACIP reaction piles, 
approximately 76 feet long, were installed for each test pile in similar fashion as the load 
test piles with 3-inch diameter center bars in lieu of a rebar cage. The reaction piles were 
completed on November 11 and December 10, 2014, and their approximate locations are 
shown on the Load Test Pile Location Plan, Plate 2. 
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, each 
load test pile was instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain gages (Model 
No. 4200A-2) manufactured by Geokon. The strain gages were connected using a 
multiplexer to a Geokon Micro-10 data acquisition system to record the strains during each 
compressive load test. The embedment strain gages were installed in both load test piles 
at the following depths below the existing ground surface.

HDCC provided the equipment for load testing including a reaction beam, reference 
beams, and other accessories. Geolabs Inc. provided the load cell, hydraulic jacks, and 
hydraulic pump. Based on the load cell calibration data, an equation relating the applied 
load (kips) to the load cell reading (voltage) was generated and used during the load tests.  
As requested, Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a 
multiplexer, data-logger, and the data acquisition program to collect the strain gage 
readings during the load tests. The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses 
and evaluation of the static pile load tests. In addition, displacements at the top of the load 
test piles were measured and recorded by Geolabs. 
The load test piles were loaded in general accordance with the Quick Load Test 
Method (ASTM D1143) and modified by our office to suit the subsurface conditions at the 
project site. In general, Test Pile No. 1 was loaded to about 1,000 kips and Test Pile No. 
2A was loaded to about 1,035 kips in approximately 62.5-kip load increments. Each load

It should be noted that an additional load test was performed on 
November 24, 2014 on a pile designated as Test Pile No. 2. This load test was 
subsequently terminated due to excessive spalling and cracking of the grout near the top 
of the pile during loading. In addition, Test Pile No. 2A was loaded to approximately 
751 kips on December 18, 2014 before the load test apparatus suddenly rotated. It was 
determined that the load test apparatus was slightly out of plumb creating an eccentric 
loading condition. The test apparatus was realigned the following day and the test 
resumed on December 19, 2014. 
After loading, the load test pile was unloaded in approximately five equal 
decrements and held for at least 5 minutes at each decrement. The exception was Test 
Pile No. 1 in which excessive displacement and pile top rotation was observed 3.9 hours 
into the 4-hour hold at the 1,000 kip load increment. Due to the observed pile top rotation, 
the load test apparatus was no longer plumb and rebound loading could not be performed. 
It is unclear whether the excessive displacement observed was a result of soil failure or a 
structural failure similar to that observed during loading of Test Pile No. 2. The results of 
the compressive pile load tests are summarized below.
