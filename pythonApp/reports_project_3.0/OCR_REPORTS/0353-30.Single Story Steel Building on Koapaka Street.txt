SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS

building area The site was covered with approximately 1 inch
of asphalt over 18 inches of tan CORAL FILL Beneath the coral
fill 12 to 18 inches of red brown CINDER FILL was observed

silty CLAY FILL Hard tan weathered CORAL was observed
beneath the fill

Based on the results of the field investigation and engineering
analyses the following conclusions and recommendations are
presented
A Foundations
The site is capable of supporting the relatively light
structural loads of the proposed temporary building
Spread or continuous footings founded in the upper layer
of CORAL FILL may be designed for a maximum allowable soil
bearing pressure of 1000 psf The footing trenches should
be compacted and inspected by a soils engineer prior to
placing forms reinforcing steel or concrete to ensure that
there are no soft spots which might cause excessive differ
ential settlements
Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should
be founded at least 12 inches below the ground surface
