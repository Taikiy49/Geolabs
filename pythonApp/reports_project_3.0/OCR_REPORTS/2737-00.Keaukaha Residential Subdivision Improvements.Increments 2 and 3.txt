PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
it is proposed to make roadway and utility improvements for the residential

also proposed for the site

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located to the north of General Lyman Field in the Keaukaha
district of Hilo on the island of Hawaii At the time of our exploration the site was a
residential neighborhood with paved and unpaved secondary roadways The
proposed Kamehameha and Pakele Avenues are alleyways with unpaved gravel
subgrades except for Pakele Avenue which is paved between Pua and Baker Avenues
Pua and Baker Avenues are paved secondary roadways
The general location of the project site is shown on the attached Project
Location Map Plate 1
The topography of the general area is basically level when considering vertical
differences in elevation over areal extent of the project site Locally however over
short horizontal distances the ground surface is very irregular with many dips and
ridges The differences between these local high and low elevations are as much as
ten 10 feet but generally range from three 3 to five 5 feet in vertical height
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five 5
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2737 00 Page 5
shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has
had activity during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Ka u Volcanic Series of
the Mauna Loa shield volcano which were deposited during the Pleistocene epoch
The rock throughout the project site and the surrounding area are the result
primarily of solidification of a pahoehoe lava flow Contrary to the relatively smooth
surface of a typical pahoehoe flow the rock at the project site has an irregular surface
with many localized dips and ridges These irregularities in the surface of the flow
were probably caused by the wrinkling of the flow surface crust as the crust was
affected by the more fluid oncoming lava In portions of the general area sections of
the smooth lava surfaces are in a disarrayed uplifted attitude tumuli again suggesting
a heaved crust which was once level
Cavities and or lava tubes are a common feature of pahoehoe lava flows and
some relatively small cavities were noticed throughout the project area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling eleven 11 borings
to depths of between eight and one half 8 5 and twenty 20 feet below the existing
ground surface and the collection of subsurface samples The borings were drilled
during the period of August 6 1991 through August 9 1991 The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plates 2 3 and 4

In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by basaltic lava
formations to the maximum depths explored with some previously placed relatively
thin one 1 to three and one half 3 5 foot surface fills consisting of silty basalt
gravel
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of six 6 to
seventeen 17 feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration
Based on the estimated elevations at the boring locations the measured groundwater
level appears to be at or close to Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the proximity of the
site to the ocean it is anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate in response
to tides and storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering viewpoint Some difficult excavation for the placement of the utilities may
be encountered in portions of the project site
Site Preparation
The area to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials and the materials removed from and disposed of properly off site In areas
to receive fill the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of twelve 12
inches and recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft yielding or pumping areas
should be overexcavated to firm underlying soils and stabilized by backfilling with
select materials placed in twelve 12 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2737 00 Page 7
Generally the on site excavated materials may be utilized as fill provided that
the materials are reduced to less than six 6 inches in maximum particle dimension for
general embankment and to less than three 3 inches in maximum particle dimension
for trench backfill
Any additional fills if needed should consist of low expansion granular
materials such as crushed coral gravel or cinder with a maximum particle dimension
of three 3 inches and should be approved in advance by the geotechnical engineer
The materials utilized for general embankment should be placed in maximum loose lift
heights of twelve 12 inches and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The materials utilized
for trench backfill should be placed in maximum loose lift heights of six 6 inches and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of their maximum dry density
Excavation
It is Our Opinion that excavation of trenches for installation of utility lines in the
in situ basalt formation encountered at the site may require hard excavation such as
ripping and or hoerams The contractor should review the boring logs and make his
own interpretation concerning the difficulty presented by the existing subsurface
conditions in regards to excavation of trenches for utilities
We anticipate that the material will have a volume increase on the order of five
5 to ten 10 per cent when excavated and reused as fill material The excavated
material may require some processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater
than six 6 inches in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill material The material
will require further processing to remove or reduce the larger fragments to less than
three 3 inches in size prior to its being used as backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2737 00 Page 8
It is anticipated that the excavations in the basalt formations will probably stand
unsupported temporarily for construction purposes It is important however that all
applicable safety requirements be complied with in this respect Determination of the
requirement for shoring and or bracing should be made the responsibility of the
contractor as should be its design and installation if required
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding six 6 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Mechanical compaction equipment is highly suited to the materials encountered
at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used as a means
to attempt to compact the on site soils
Utilities
Excavations for the proposed water and sewer lines and dry wells are
anticipated to be in materials ranging from localized loose clinker materials to hard
basalt formations Due to the possibility that portions of the utilities may be founded
on loose materials or upon cavities or voids within the rock formation it is
recommended that the excavations at the invert elevations be checked in areas of
loose clinker or suspected voids to determine if the materials encountered at the
proposed bearing surfaces are suitable to support the proposed structures The
check of the excavation floors for the utilities should be performed by the excavation
equipment backhoe bucket in the presence of the geotechnical engineer to
determine the competency of the materials encountered The bearing surfaces should
be struck with force by the backhoe bucket in suspect areas to collapse and or
expose underlying cavities
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2737 00 Page 9
If the materials encountered at the invert elevations for the utilities are found to
be inadequate to support the proposed structures the area should be overexcavated
to competent in situ material The resulting depression should then be backfilled with
select granular materials compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The utilities should be placed and backfilled in conformance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction for the City and County of Honolulu
Dry Wells
It is understood that dry wells will be installed for disposal of storm water run off
at the project site At the time of our exploration the locations of the proposed dry
wells had not been determined
The borings that were performed for the exploration of the subsurface
conditions indicate that the rock formations encountered vary from massive to severely
jointed basalt rock and clinker The clinker considered to be sufficiently permeable to
accept storm water run off was encountered in Boring 5 and 7 at depths of
one 1 to five 5 feet below the ground surface In Boring No 4 the clinker was
underlain by jointed basalt below a depth of eight 8 feet These borings are located
generally in the central portion of the project site
It is recommended that when the site grading plan is sufficiently developed to
allow tentative locations of the dry wells additional field exploration and testing be
performed to more definitively determine the location and permeability of the clinker
formations to provide the basis upon which to design the wells

Pavement Sections
The anticipated traffic volume for the roadway is anticipated to be relatively
moderate due to the residential nature of the proposed development
Based on the analysis of the materials encountered at the site during our
exploration and assumed moderate residential traffic the following pavement section is
recommended
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The above recommended pavement design is based on the assumption that
the roadway sections will be adequately drained
Drainage
The finished grade outside the pavements should be shaped to shed water
away from the roadway and to avoid ponding
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and roadway construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork recommendations given herein If this review is not made
the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
it is recommended that the utility excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
