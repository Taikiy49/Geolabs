In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of March 10 1986
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for site
grading and design of the foundation for the proposed structures
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling five 5
to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project includes the construction of a one story classroom office
building which consists of two separated portions about 30 by 50 feet and
25 by 110 feet in plan Two walkways to the target areas each about 140
feet long are planned in the pistol firing range in front of the proposed
building Fill up to about 1 foot is anticipated for the construction of
the proposed building
A 4 to 8 feet high earth berm is planned for extending the existing
earth berm by an additional length of about 80 feet A 95 foot long CMU
wall approximately 20 feet above the top of the berm is planned along
the center line of the southwestern half of the extended berm
A truck delivery area is planned in the small gully area north of the
proposed building where up to about 6 feet of fill is anticipated
Further north at a distance of about 150 feet the existing parking will
be relocated and partial new asphalt concrete paving will be required
Drywells are planned to serve these areas
The proposed layout of the facility is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located in the existing pistol firing
range in the Koko Head Crater

Currently the site is a partially paved open area fronting an
existing firing range Several narrow concrete walkways connect the site
to the target areas of the firing range
A 4 to 6 feet high earth berm about 115 feet long is located at the
Makai side of the existing firing range and extends in a northeastern to

The ground at the building area and the existing firing range slopes

To the north of the gully are existing parking and grassed areas

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 5
borings to depths of about 10 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general all borings encountered a crust of medium dense yellowish
brown silty sand with gravel overlying a deposit of medium stiff to stiff
yellowish brown sandy silt at depths of about 11 to 12 feet
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings during our
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our borings disclosed the presence of generally competent subsoils at
the site The soils appear suitable for supporting the proposed building
and CMU wall on footing foundations
W O 1692 00 Page 4
The on site sandy soils with gravel are generally suitable for use as
a source of fill and backfill material
Competent subgrade materials were encountered for both the proposed
walkways in the firing range and the proposed relocated parking area
Building Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light design loading
spread or continuous footing foundation is recommended for the proposed
building An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used on the
dense in situ crust soil
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading Estimated
settlements under the design loading for the proposed building are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structures can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
in situ soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used to determine the passive soil resistance
CMU Wall
Loading from the non structural CMU wall is anticipated to be low and
the wall may be supported by a continuous wall footing bearing directly on
the dense in situ soil

Estimated settlements under the wall loading alone are negligible

Site Grading
Site filling of about 1 to 6 feet are planned for the proposed
building site and the truck delivery area respectively
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish broken up pavements and vegetation
should be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
cleared grade or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and recompacted
to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test
method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the cleared grade footing
or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site soil or with suitable imported material compacted to the
abovementioned standard

The on site sandy soil with gravel may be used as fill and backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill placed under the proposed building and the truck

Earth Berm Extension
Site filling of about 4 to 7 feet is planned for extending the
existing earth berm from about 115 to 195 feet
The on site soil material can be used to form the berm The fill
should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 10 inches in compacted
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 85 its maximum dry density
Estimated settlements under the fill load are on the order of about
one to one and half inches
Side slopes of the extended berm should be graded to a maximum
gradient of 2H 1V to ensure slope stability and minimize future
maintenance
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for both the truck delivery
area and part of the relocated parking
Based on our laboratory CBR tests we recommend the use of the
following pavement section for preliminary design purpose
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
during construction to verify the adequacy of the above section
Drywells
It is our understanding that drywells may be used to serve the parking
areas
Based on the field and laboratory testing data it appears that the
subsoils have relatively low permeability Our laboratory sieve analysis
results indicate the presence of at least 25 fine minus 200 in the
soil Based on this finding we believe that the estimated permeability
of the soil is on the order of about 1073 to sec and that the
drywells would probably have low percolation rates The borings indicated
that the permeability of the subsoils decreases with depth due to
increasing the amount of fines Therefore it appears that relatively
shallow dry wells would be more effective at the site
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation pavement and
drywell design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation drywell
and footing excavation be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been odtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use

Field Exploration

5 borings to depths of 10 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A

monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
