PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at 91-401 Malakole Street in the Campbell Industrial Park 
area of Kapolei on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The property is approximately bounded by 
Malakole Street to the north, Hauna Street to the east, and an oil refinery facility to the south 
and west. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. The parcel is approximately rectangular in shape and encompasses about 20 acres 
in land area.  The project grading plan is shown on the Grading Plan, Plate 2. 
Based on the information provided, it is desired to develop the project site into an 
auto auction company. It is our understanding that the majority of the premises will be 
developed into coarse aggregate surface for parking of the auction vehicles.  In addition, a 
single-story Modular Office Building with a floor area of approximately 2,160 square feet is 
planned for the project construction.  Two canopy structures, each of about 36 feet by 75 feet 
in plan dimension, will also be constructed in the future for the proposed project.  
The mass grading consisted of fills of up to about 3 feet thick and cuts of up to 
about 3 feet deep to achieve the design finished grades within an area of about 20 acres. 
Lindemann Construction, Inc. performed the earthwork operations for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Deleterious materials 
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. The on-site coralline 
materials were crushed and screened through a grizzly to produce 3-inch minus general 
fill materials.  The cleared subgrade with exposed voids and cavities were proof-rolled 
with a 15-ton smooth drum vibratory roller.  The voids and cavities remained open after 
four to six passes of proof-rolling were filled with the 3-inch minus general fill materials 
and proof-rolled again.  Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were 
generally over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was 
backfilled with well-compacted on-site 3-inch minus general fill materials. 
The prepared subgrade was filled with on-site 3-inch minus general fill materials in 
designated fill areas and capped with 12 inches of 1.5-inch minus select borrow materials, 
which were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted 
to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance 
with ASTM D1557. In areas that were previously cleared and used for industrial purposes, 
the subgrade was capped with 6 inches of 1.5-inch minus select borrow materials 
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  In asphaltic concrete 
pavement areas, the subgrade was capped by 6 inches of base course materials 
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills during the mass grading operation.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.5. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering April 20 through 
September 17, 2021.
