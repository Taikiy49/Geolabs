It is proposed to develop the project site into a one story commercial building with
on grade parking for Hawaii Teamsters Local 996 This letter report presents the findings
of our preliminary geotechnical engineering study for the proposed project Our opinions
and recommendations are based on a brief site visit and subsurface information obtained
by another consultant for a previous development at the site
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project site consists of two properties located on the makai side of
Hart Street between Kalihi and Mokauea Streets The project site measuring
approximately 100 feet by 139 feet is rectangular in shape There are existing
developments located adjacent to the three sides of the project site
PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site and a soils report by
Soils International dated the subsurface conditions at the project site
generally consist of a thin surface fill layer overlying coral formation It is our opinion that
the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view Shallow
footing foundations bearing on the compacted fill layer or coral formation can be utilized
to support the proposed one story building

Building Foundations
An allowable bearing value of up to 3 500 p s f can be used for spread footings
bearing on the in situ coral formation with a minimum footing embedment of 2 feet
Where clayey silt soil were encountered at the bottom of footing excavation it should
be over excavated to a maximum depth of another 18 inches and replaced with
granular select borrow material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum
dry density as per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Alternatively the spread footings
should be embedded deep enough to bear on the in situ coral formation
The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Cavity Probing
Coral formation often contain voids or cavities Therefore to reduce the
possibility of footing settlement due to possible occurrence of cavities or
loose lenses near the footing bottom elevation drilled probes are
recommended below each column footing and at 15 foot centers for
continuous wall footings The probe holes should be a minimum of
3 inches in diameter and extend below the bottom of the footing to a
depth equal to two times the footing width or a minimum of 10 feet The
probe holes should be grouted with a sand water cement mixture under
low pressures If a cavity or loose lenses is suspected below a footing
additional probing and grouting should be performed to delineate the
horizontal extent and thickness of the cavity or loose lenses
Pavement Design
Both the concrete and asphaltic concrete pavements may be used for the
proposed project For preliminary design the following pavement sections are
recommended
6 Inch Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
10 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The final pavement sections will depend on the type of vehicle traffic anticipated
and the actual subgrade soil CBR Tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils during construction and the above design sections may be revised if necessary
