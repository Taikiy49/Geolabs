PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the soil and foundation engineering
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and
groundwater conditions at the site to provide data for
use in the design of the foundations for the proposed
high rise condominium and parking structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory
tests which were used in the engineering analysis
that formulated our conclusion and recommendations
are presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 and the boring logs are presented
in Appendix A Information regarding the field explora
tion and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of three 39 story
high rise condominium buildings and a large 4 level
parking and commercial building complex
The mauka and makai high rise towers are about 135 feet
long with staggered widths of 55 to 100 feet The
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 987 00 P
central tower will be approximately 96 by 112 feet in
plan The parking and commercial buildings will be
situated on the eastern Piikoi Street side of all
three towers Finished floor elevation is planned at
elevation 7 feet for the towers The lower level of
parking structure will be at about elevation 5 feet
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is situated along Piikoi Street
between Ala Moana Boulevard and Waimanu Street
See Plate 1
The site is located next to the existing 1350 Ala Moana
condominium which is located at the corner of Piikoi
Street and Ala Moana Boulevard
The site is presently occupied by various warehouse
structures and asphaltic concrete parking areas
The site is generally level with ground elevations of
4 to 6 feet
GEOLOGY
General
Physiographically the site is located on the Honolulu
Coastal Plain adjacent to the Koolau Volcanic Range
The Honolulu Coastal Plain was built up of interlayered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 4
deposits of coral coralline debris land derived sediments
from the Koolau Range and basalt and ash from the Honolulu
Volcanic Series Eruptions
The site is located on fill overlying a channel eroded
through the 15 foot coral algal reef ledge which
underlies much of the surrounding area
Geologic History
After formation of the Koolau Volcanic Range about two
million years ago a series of Pleistocene Ice Age sea
level fluctuations allowed deposition of several hundred
feet of reef rock coralline debris and volcanic sedimentary
deposits in the area of the site Later a fall in sea
level permitted the cutting of a deep 160 foot B 5 to
165 B 13 deep channel into the previously formed
deposits The age and geometry of this ancient channel
are not well defined
About 120 000 years ago sea level advanced to about 25
feet MSL resulting in deposition of sand and coralline
debris Subsequently sea level fell and paused at 15
feet MSL during which extensive coral algal reefs developed
over the site Continued regression of the sea resulted in
exposure of the reef to rain and weathering resulting in
a hard solution cavity pockmarked ledge at 15 feet MSL
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 5
Sea level ultimately fell to about 300 feet MSL
Apparently a branch of either the ancient Kaheka or
HIC Stream channels Ferrall C 1976 M S Thesis
U H cut through and removed the 15 foot MSL ledge
over most of the proposed construction site Erosion in
this channel apparently was not as deep as in the main
Kaheka or HIC channels since most of the 30 foot MSL
cemented coral sand and gravel layer is present over the
site The top of this layer ranges from about 28 feet
B 3 to 35 feet B 15 below the existing ground surface
This eroded layer ranges from about 4 5 feet B 4 to
30 feet B 9 thick The hard coral limestone 24 5 feet
below existing ground level in Boring 12 may be part of
the eroded 15 foot MSL coral limestone ledge
About 18 000 years ago sea level began to rise towards its
present level When it reached about the 80 foot MSL
level Tantalus Roundtop eruptions deposited ash over the
Site Infilling of the site continued as sea level
continued to rise to about 30 feet MSL Some reworked
Tantalus Roundtop ash is present at this level in
Borings 7 and 8
Sea level continued to rise with an offshore reef keeping
pace The result was an offshore barrier reef with a
GEOLABS HAWAII

lagoon behind it clayey coral gravel in upper 10 to 25
feet of borings
By the time the sea reached its present level about 4 000
years ago the lagoon began to fill with sediment leaving
a swamp upper few feet of silt and clay
Recently about 3 to 7 feet of silty clay cobbles sand
and other land fill were dumped on the site to create land
for development
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were investigated
by drilling fifteen borings at the locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The depths of the borings ranged from
78 to 200 feet below the existing ground surface
Detailed information on the field exploration is covered
in the appendix along with the boring logs
Based on the information revealed from the borings the
site is underlain by a surface layer of fill 4 to 9 feet
thick over loose lagoonal deposits consisting of silty
and clayey sands to about 28 to 35 foot depth Coralline
sandy deposits ranging from loose to very dense were
encountered below the upper lagoonal deposits down to
the depth of the borings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 7
A generally hard coral layer of variable thickness was
encountered at about 28 to 35 foot depth The thickness
of this layer varied from about 5 to 30 feet thick
Additional dense layers of variable thicknesses were
encountered at depths of 100 to 170 foot depths
Groundwater level was encountered at depths of 3 to 6 foot
depths in the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by about 4 to 9 feet of fill over loose clayey to silty
sand to about 28 to 35 foot depth Sandy coralline
deposits including dense coral ledges were encountered
below the loose upper deposits
A hard coral formation was generally encountered at about
28 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface minus
30 to 40 feet elevation The thickness of the coral
formation was variable and found to be quite thin in
some areas but generally could provide support for the
lower capacity 75 ton piles to be utilized for the
parking structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 8
For the high rise tower structures foundation Support on
this upper coral formation is not recommended Since future
foundation construction in the areas west of the towers
could penetrate and shear this upper layer thereby
decreasing its pile supporting capacity Previous pile
driving records from the adjacent 1350 Ala Moana condominium
project indicated it would be very difficult to drive the
pile through the upper coral crust Therefore we recommend
pre drilling through this upper coral formation prior to
continue driving the long 200 ton high capacity piles to
the underlying dense coral layers located at 150 to 200 foot
depths
High Capacity Piles High Rise Towers
It is estimated that a 16 inch diameter octagonal concrete
pile driven at least to elevation 145 would develop an
allowable bearing capacity of 200 tons per pile This pile
capacity would be developed mainly from end bearing within
the lower coral formation Therefore the 200 ton per
pile capacity can also be used for the pile groups
It is anticipated that the piles will require pre drilling
down to about 60 foot depths in order to penetrate the upper
minus 30 to 40 feet coral deposits The actual driving
characteristics should be evaluated by a series of pile
driving resistance tests We recommend that a minimum of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 9
fifteen 15 test piles be first driven per each high rise
Structure About two test piles from each tower would be
selected for load test to twice the 200 ton pile capacity
For estimating purposes it is anticipated that most of
the 200 ton piles would stop at about tip elevation of
170 feet The actual pile tip elevations may vary from
150 to 210 feet We recommend that the high capacity
piles be driven at least to elevation 145 The piles
should be driven to a final driving resistance of about
250 blows per foot for the last 2 feet or 20 blows per
inch for the last 3 inches with a hammer delivering a
rated energy of 32 500 foot pounds Field production
pile driving criteria may be established upon request for
different pile hammers
Splicing of the pile sections would be required to develop
the very long pile lengths recommended for the high rise
structure Where practicable the splices should be
placed so that the joint is embedded in firm soil Strong
splicing sleeves are recommended for splicing the piles
because of the long heavy piles to be used The
splicing sleeve should be at least 1 2 inch thick which
is thicker than normal to provide additional stiffness
and increased resistance against corrosion The total
length of the splice cans should be at least six feet
long
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 10
During the pile driving some heaving may occur in the
other piles already installed This possibility should
be monitored carefully in the field Should heaving occur
it would require redriving of all the affected piles to
assure their proper performance
Low Capacity Piles Parking Structure
The upper coral layer is not sufficiently thick to provide
adequate support for high capacity piles It should
provide adequate support for the 75 ton piles planned for
the proposed parking structures
It is estimated that a 12 inch square concrete pile driven
to about tip elevation of 40 feet will develop an
allowable bearing capacity of 75 tons per pile This pile
should be driven to a final driving resistance of 12 blows
per inch for 2 inches with a hammer delivering a rated
energy of 25 000 foot pounds
Because the upper coral layers are quite thin the pile
driving operations for the low capacity piles should be
closely monitored to assure that the piles are adequately
supported in the upper coral layer
We recommend that at least twenty 20 test piles be driven
at various locations to verify the locations of the coral
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P il
layer and two of the test piles selected for load tests to
verify the pile capacity
The driving of the high capacity pile in the pre drilled
hole may affect the upper coral layer structurally
Therefore the high capacity piles should be completely
installed before driving the low capacity piles
Test Piles
Due to the complex subsurface coral conditions below the
relative large project site a generally more extensive
test pile program is required for this project Specific
ally the purpose of the proposed test pile program would
be as follows
1 The thickness of the upper coral layer 40
foot elevation is generally variable Test
piles are required to verify the pile driving
characteristics and pile design bearing
capacity in the areas where the coral layer
is thin
2 The borings indicate a relatively hard layer
at about 100 foot depth below portions of the
site If the piles are unable to penetrate
through this layer load tests would be required
to verify the pile supporting capacity of this
upper layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 12
3 Pre drilling through the upper coral 40
is generally required for the high capacity
piles The test pile program would help to
determine the optimum depth of pre drilling
required
4 A better estimate of the casting lengths can
be obtained for the remaining production piles
required
5 Selected test piles would be load tested to
twice their design capacities to verify the
75 ton pile capacity of the upper coral and
the 200 ton pile capacity of the deep piles
Uplift Resistance
The uplift resistance of the above recommended piles will be
relatively low since pre drilling will be utilized for the
long high capacity piles and the shorter lower capacity
piles will be driven through very loose lagoonal deposits
For the high capacity piles an allowable uplift capacity
of 8 kips per pile could be utilized if the pile is driven
a minimum of 10 feet below the bottom of the pre drilled
hole and splices are not utilized above this length
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 13
The lower capacity piles will depend on the loose silty
and clayey sands for uplift resistance therefore a low
allowable uplift capacity of 3 kips per pile is recommended
Lateral Resistance
The capacity of the piles to resist lateral loads would be
very small because of the very loose nature of the upper
lagoonal deposit and because the high capacity piles will
be spliced and driven in pre drilled holes Therefore
any lateral forces on the high rise structures should be
resisted by passive soil resistance of the pile cap embedded
below the surface fill layer and batter piles if additional
lateral capacity is required
Based on the preliminary design data it appears that the
pile cap will be situated in the existing fill material
near the existing ground surface
This fill generally consists of loose Silty and clayey sand
therefore a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure is recommended The backfill around the pile
caps should be well compacted to aid in the passive soil
resistance of the pile caps
Batter piles should be installed with batter not exceeding
3 inches horizontal on 12 inches vertical for resistance
to lateral loads The same driving criteria and pile capa
city as recommended in the previous sections may be used

Groundwater
Because of the high groundwater levels basements or ele
vator shafts extending below approximately elevation 2
should be designed to resist hydrostatic uplift pressures
Also excavations below the above mentioned level would
require dewatering Although the method of dewatering
should be left open to the contractor yet adequate
Stability of the excavation should be maintained The
dewatering operations should also be done without pumping
out the soil fines pumping clear water only and without
creating a quick condition or softening the bottom of
the excavation
Careful dewatering is particularly essential near the
existing buildings to reduce settlements of the Slabs
pavements and other structures supported on grade
Grading
New fill under the high rise structure should be kept to
a minimum In order not to reduce the pile capacity due
to the downdrag force caused by consolidation of the loose
lagoonal deposit the fill should be placed prior to any
other construction work Fill used for structural support
should be compacted to at least 95 percent of its maximum
dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 987 00 P 15
The on site sandy fill soils are acceptable for backfill
provided that the organic and other deleterious materials
are removed Additional imported fill should be generally
less than 3 inch maximum size and non expansive
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course generally less than 3 4 inch and greater than 1 4
inch in size The subgrade should be kept moist prior to
pouring the concrete
Additional Exploration
During our field exploration the tenants and the existing
warehouse structures prevented the drilling of borings within
the proposed mauka tower location Borings were instead
drilled in the accessible surrounding area close to the
proposed tower location in order to delineate the general
soil conditions below the tower area Boring Nos 10 and
13 drilled on the opposite east and west sides of the
mauka tower encountered Significantly different coral
conditions below the site In order to obtain more detailed
information on the soil conditions and anticipated pile
driving conditions below the actual tower location we
recommend that two 2 additional deep borings to 200 feet
deep each should be drilled at the mauka tower location
when this area is cleared and accessible to the drilling
rig This additional exploration could be initiated after
site demolition

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations
is included in the project plans and specifications
Construction Inspection
It is recommended that the pile driving operations be monitored
on a full time basis by a qualified technician under the direction
of a soils engineer to verify that the piles have been driven in
accordance with the recommendations The pile capacities given
in this report are contingent upon such inspections
Pile load tests to verify the capacity of the 200 ton and 75 ton
piles should be performed in accordance with the City and County s
requirements or ASTM Test Designation D 1143 69 The test setup
and loading of the pile should be made the responsibility of the
contractor The readings and test reports should be prepared by
a registered soils engineer

drilling 15 borings ranging in depth from 78 0 to 200 0 feet

were obtained by driving either a standard penetration split
spoon sampler or 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches NX core
samples of the hard coral were obtained by using a 3 inch core
barrel The blow counts to drive the sampler for the last 12
inches along with percent core recovery are shown on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depth
