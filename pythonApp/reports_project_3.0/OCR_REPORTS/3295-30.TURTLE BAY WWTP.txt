INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings of our injection well performance evaluation for
the Turtle Bay Wastewater Treatment Plant in Kahuku on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The findings and recommendations presented herein are based on site
observations made during a site visit the performance of short term injection testing on
the wells and personal communication with operators and managers of the treatment
plant facility The approximate locations of the treatment plant facility and the injection
well field are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
and the State of Hawaii Department of Health Underground Injection
Control UIC Injection Well Status Report Guidelines Version 1 dated June 1992
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Turtle Bay Wastewater Treatment Plant injection wells are utilized for the
disposal of secondary treated effluent generated within the Turtle Bay Resort
development The majority of the effluent treated at the plant is used for irrigation of the
resort golf course within the Turtle Bay complex Therefore the wells are used for the
injection of treated effluent only on a stand by basis during periods of time when the
effluent cannot be utilized for irrigation purposes generally due to wet weather conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 2
As a condition of the Underground Injection Control UIC permit UIC Permit
No UO 1532 issued for the facility it is required that periodic monitoring of the wells be
made by a professional engineer or geologist familiar with injection well operations
Geolabs Hawaii has prepared this injection well evaluation report to provide the Owner
the operator and the regulatory agency with information regarding the performance of the
injection wells to aid in the prevention of adverse public health effects from an injection
well failure This evaluation is intended to assist in ascertaining that the wells remain
operational

DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
The wells are located on the easterly side of the tee boxes for the 10th Hole of
Golf Course G 2 The wells are situated in an open grass area between the tees and the
moat around Punahoolapa Marsh The topography of the well field area is generally level
with some topographic mounds and depressions associated with the adjacent golf course
terrain Based on a topographic map of the well field and vicinity ground surface
elevations range from about 5 to 6 feet Mean Sea Level At the time of our site visit on
June 28th the weather conditions were generally dry under sunny skies No evidence of
surface ponding of water was visible in the vicinity of the well field
The well heads are enclosed in below ground sump vaults of approximately 4 by
4 feet in plan dimension and about 5 feet in depth The vaults are covered with hinged
steel plate doors and are approximately flush with the adjacent grass covered ground
surface
Inside the vaults the well heads consist of steel tees with plates bolted to their
tops for security and to allow the build up of additional head if needed These plates
were removed by plant representatives for the inspection and performance of the injection
tests In 1994 two inch diameter inspection ports were installed in the well covers to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 6
eliminate the need for removal of the entire top plate during future inspection testing
However to allow complete access to the well interior for the performance of the short
term injection tests the top plate of each well was removed
Well Field Usage
In general only two of the three wells are placed in use for effluent disposal at any
time The inactive well is taken out of operation on a rotating basis so that at any given
time only two wells will be in operation Flow to an individual well is controlled by a valve
located adjacent to each well head A main control valve diverts the effluent flow from the
treatment plant to either the well field or the lagoon which serves as storage of water for
use as golf course irrigation
Based on available records for the past 12 months flow to the well field was
relatively low from July to September 1995 corresponding to the lower rainfall summer
months of the year The flow to the well field increased and was relatively high from
about October 1995 to March 1996 corresponding to the wetter winter months In
April 1996 the flow to the well field was reduced and became very low through the
present time During this period virtually all effluent flow was diverted from the well field
to provide for the irrigation needs of the golf course

Injection System
The injection system is regulated by valves which can be used to isolate the wells
so that any one or combination of the wells may be used for injection At the time of our
site visit the normal system operation was set up such that Wells 1 and 2 were currently
on stand by for use as disposal wells for the injection of effluent Well 3 was isolated from
the system as an emergency back up to the system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 11
The system is equipped with a flow totalizing meter This meter measures all flow
to the well field rather than to individual wells Flows to individual wells may be estimated
by records of which valves are open during injection periods
At the time of our site visit the totalizing meter read a cumulative reading of about
inspection in July 1995
Daily Flow to Well Field
Based on a review of the operational log of the injection well system for the
preceding 12 months daily flows ranged from zero to a maximum single day flow of
about 523 000 gallons per day Average daily flows appear to be on the order of about
300 000 gallons per day during heavy usage of the wells The months of January and
February were the most active periods of injection well usage due to the higher incidence
of rainfall and reduced irrigation needs for the golf course
In general from July to October 1995 flow to the well field followed a pattern of
several days of injection followed by several days of zero flow to the well field From
October 1995 to April 1996 flow to the well field occurred on a daily basis keeping the
wells continually active We believe that this pattern of injection is beneficial for
maintaining good well capacity as it provides the well with a chance to recover and keep
biological growth in balance
However from mid April through the month of June 1996 the well field received
very little to zero flow Considering that the summer months are historically the least
active for the wells the usage of the well field was still below the normal usage for the
summer period The demand for golf course irrigation water was above normal due to a
malfunctioned pump which mixes potable water with the treated effluent for production of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 12
irrigation water Therefore any water that would normally go to the well field was diverted
for irrigation needs and the wells remained inactive for an extended period of time As of
mid July we were informed that the pump was fixed and normal quantities of effluent may
be available for injection well disposal
Short Term Injection Tests
Attempts were made to perform short term injection testing of the wells by diverting
treated effluent from the treatment plant into each well separately Each well was isolated
from the others for the duration of each test by adjusting the valves controlling the effluent
flow to individual wells
During injection testing the head level response was measured and recorded
Readings from the totalizing meter were recorded prior to and after each short term
injection test to obtain the total quantity of injected effluent and to estimate the average
flow rate into each well
Attempts were made to conduct short term injection testing on the three injection
wells However Wells 1 and 2 repeatedly overflowed as soon as effluent was diverted to
the well necessitating the termination of the testing Therefore no test data is available
for Wells 1 and 2 In addition three attempts were made to conduct injection testing on
Well 3 The third attempt was successful in that the test duration continued for 9 minutes
before the well also overflowed The results of the injection testing are presented in
Appendix B
Based on our observations of the higher than normal static water level in the wells
and our attempts to conduct short term injection testing in Wells 1 and 2 we conclude
that the current operation of Wells 1 and 2 has deteriorated in relation to past well
performance evaluations The wells may be suffering from a temporary loss of capacity
due to the extended inactivity of the well field
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 13
Based on the results of the injection test for Well 3 we believe that Well 3 has also
suffered some lost capacity though not as severe as Wells 1 and 2 The average
injection rate for Well 3 was estimated to be about 328 gallons per minute gpm During
injection at this rate for about 9 minutes the head level in the well was observed to rise
from a depth of about 1 7 inches below the well cover to the top of the well cover causing
an overflow
Recent Flow to Well Field
Following our site visit on June 28th we advised the Owner to place the well field
into operation as often as practical and to closely monitor the system for indications of
changes in the disposal capacity The intent was to determine if the loss of capacity was
temporary due to the recent inactivity of the wells prior to our site visit From June 28th to
August 7th Wells 1 and 2 were placed into continuous operations with daily flows
averaging about 200 000 gallons Observations made with the well covers removed on
July 18th and August 6th reflect that the wells were operational with no overflow or
backing up of the system observed
In light of these most recent observations by plant personnel we believe the wells
have regained some of the lost capacity noted during our site visit Presently the wells
appear to be operating satisfactorily although the head levels continue to remain
elevated from prior well inspection observations
Recommendations
Based on our observations injection test results and recent well usage data we
believe the injection wells have sustained some loss of disposal capacity since our last
inspection in July 1995 The loss of capacity may be due in part to the extended period of
time that the wells were recently inactive allowing a build up of biological and chemical
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 14
matter to take place thereby reducing the porosity of the formation and or well screen
Sounding of the wells indicate they are open to their normal depths suggesting that
collapse or structural failure of the well has not occurred
It is a known fact that injection wells do sustain losses in capacity over time due to
natural biological and chemical processes or other introduced factors It should be noted
that some loss in capacity may be permanent Extended periods of well inactivity may
cause acceleration of the normal losses in capacity
Based on our field observations and a review of the available operational data we
recommend that the Operator continue to operate the injection wells on a routine rotating
basis in an attempt to maintain the disposal capacity of the system In addition we
recommend that the Operator closely monitor and document the operation of each of the
wells to assure that overflows or back ups related to a well failure are detected prior to
their occurrence In the event an overflow of a well or other failure occurs and the
situation cannot be remedied the Operator should promptly notify the Department of
Health
We believe that by increasing the use of the wells on a rotating basis especially
during the drier months of low effluent availability the current operational status of the
wells could be maintained Keeping the wells operational is not normally a problem
during the wetter winter months when effluent is more readily available for injection
However we believe it is crucial to maintain rotating operation of all the wells during the
summer months despite the lack of available effluent
Well redevelopment should be considered depending on the future performance of
the wells We recommend that the Operator record additional field observations such as
the following 1 notes regarding the overall system operation 2 observation of the head
levels within the wells prior to and during injection events 3 daily flow amounts and 4 a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3295 30 Page 15
log of individual well usage This additional information will be used to evaluate the future
performance of these wells In addition the results of our annual short term injection
testing of the wells may be used to evaluate the operation of the wells with respect to
prior well inspection data Generally redevelopment should be considered if head levels
continue to increase over time or when well failures such as overflow or system back ups
are experienced
Well redevelopment should be performed by a professional experienced with
coastal injection well construction and rehabilitation The redevelopment method selected
should be carefully evaluated based on the existing well design proper containment and
disposal of wastewater generated by the activity and other factors specific to the site and
operation of the wells
