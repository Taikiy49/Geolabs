INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary soil
investigation for the proposed subdivision development
The site plan for the project by Walter P Thompson Inc
dated was used as a reference for our
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recom
mendations for the proposed development

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The objective of our investigation was to evaluate the
general site conditions which could potentially affect
development Considerations included but were not res
tricted to topography geology soil engineering charac
teristics of the site and shallow groundwater conditions
Our investigation consisted of reconnaissance geology
mapping excavating and logging test pits soil sampling
and testing and engineering analysis
A preliminary report Geologic Constraints for the deve
lopment was prepared by our office in February 1981
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis and formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to subdivide the 8 acre site into
45 residential lots with the necessary access roads The
lot sizes are anticipated to be between 5 000 and 12 000
square feet
The site grading plan was not available at the time of
our investigation It is anticipated that site grading
would generally be limited to excavation and embankment
for the roadways

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located off of Palolo Avenue on the
west side of Palolo Valley Oahu Hawaii
The site is bordered on the southeast by existing residen
tial homes at lower ground elevation and the remainder is
bordered by undeveloped land see Location Map Plate 1
Ground elevations range from 305 feet in the southeast near
Palolo Avenue to 480 feet in the north corner of the site
Overall ground surface slopes range from about 3 1 to 4 1
H V throughout the site with locally steeper areas along
small drainageways
During the investigation many subangular and subrounded
surface boulders ranging from about 1 foot to 10 feet in
diameter were encountered particularly along drainageways
Two relatively small but distinct drainageways were present
within the site coinciding with proposed roadway A and
the lower half of proposed roadway B These two drain
ageways appear to provide drainage for the southwest and
portions of the northeast sides of the site Run off from
these drainageways empties into a concrete lined drain
beneath Palolo Avenue where the proposed entry roadway is
to be located
A heavy growth of Christmas berries Haole Koa and other
shrubs were present throughout the site during the investi
gation

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
General
The site is located on the southern flanks of the
elongated Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the south
eastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The range was formed
during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe and aa basalt
lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the exis
ting mountain trends During the Late Tertiary and Pleis
tocene Ice Age deep valleys including Palolo Valley
were eroded by streams During high stands of sea level
in the Pleistocene the valleys and slope breaks at the
base of the ridges were infilled with sediment alluviated
grading to the high sea level stands Continued weathering
and erosion of these ridges deposited boulders talus and
soil slope wash at their base to form alluvial colluvial
fans which form the site
The four basic geologic soil areas mapped at the site are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The site is currently adjusting its shape and geology to
the existing climatic and sea level regime
Bedrock
The ridge up slope from the site is composed mainly of
Koolau Range pahoehoe and aa basalt Pahoehoe is
a smooth hummocky highly vesiculated rock containing
lava tubes

Aa basalt consists of dense blocky core rock
surrounded by a layer of clinker The alternating
flows and clinker zones have weathered and eroded
to form a steep stairstep like topography in expo
sure above and probably beneath the site
The ridge is deeply incised by two steep asymme
trical gullys which feed eroded basalt boulders
into alluvial colluvial fans comprising much of
the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the sub
division development However certain safety measures
must be taken to insure the overall Stability of the
steep site against slope creep and Sliding Due to the
presence of the critically expansive Adobe boulder clays
special grading designs including buttress fill would be
required to stabilize these soils
Slope Stability
The field exploration disclosed deposits of Adobe clay
soils over the site
The Adobe clay was thickest 14 feet or more in the
central portion of the site The test pits in this area
encountered very moist soil conditions and numerous slic
kensides indicating possible shear creep movement of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
deposit
The rate of the natural creep is unknown It is to
some extent a function of long term precipitation
drainage slope angle and vegetation cover It is our
opinion that residential development could alter the
equilibrium of the Adobe clay slope by the following
1 Landscape watering will increase the
amount of moisture entering the creep
mass and reduce its shear strength and
overall stability
2 Concentration of stresses due to construc
tion of fills cuts walls and foundations
may cause settlements or lateral movements
that will cause cracking and allow water
to enter the creep mass
3 Subsequent movements in the mass may break
utility lines which will supply substantial
amount of water into the creeping mass and
thus accelerate its downward motion
The Adobe clay soils are very weak soils which are
Significantly affected by increase in moisture content
and are subject to slope creep or landslide movement
during extended wet periods
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1006 00 P 9
The high clay content and slickensided zones in the Adobe
deposit create weak planes which substantially reduce the
Shear strength of the soil mass Therefore residual
Shear strength values which are Significantly lower than
the normally used peak shear values must be utilized to
evaluate the long term Stability of these clay soils
The slickensided surfaces also reduce the effective cohe
sion which can be developed by the soil particles Labora
tory direct shear tests indicate a residual effective
friction angle of about 14 degrees and a low cohesion
value of less than 100 p s f for these on Site soils
When utilizing the above residual Strength values slope
ratios flatter than 4 1 H V depending on the slope
height are generally recommended for long term slope
Stability requirement Since the existing ground surface
in these areas already Slopes down at gradients of about
preventative measures be taken to reduce or eliminate the
potential for excessive downslope movements
There are two basic alternatives available to prevent
downhill creep movements
1 complete removal of the material within
the creep zone and replacement with a
compacted structural fill
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1006 00 P 10
2 partial removal and construction of
Stabilization fills to prevent further
movement of the creep mass
The first alternative will be economically unfeasible
Therefore the second alternative was selected as the
method to stabilize the site
Stabilization Fills
Additional cutting into the natural ground would create
an unstable slope Therefore if the grading for the
proposed roads consist of cutting buttress fill would
then be required to stabilize the exposed slope The
location and extent of the buttress fill would depend
on the future house development plans for the lots since
additional cutting for garage house construction would
remove part of the buttress fill and jeopardize the sta
bility of the buttress fill
Due to the relatively steep terrain at the site cutting
into the hillside would produce a relatively large slope
cut necessitating a substantial buttress fill Therefore
this type of grading concept is not generally recommended
for the project
Instead the recommended roadway grading concepts for this
type of terrain and Adobe clay soil condition are

instead utilize roadway embankment fill

Foundations
The location and type of houses proposed were unknown to
us at the time of our investigation It is our under
Standing that lots only will be sold to individual buyers
who will design and construct their individual units
Due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions
post and beam house construction is strongly recommended
and slab on grade should be avoided
Even after the roadways have been stabilized by buttress
fills individual house construction over Adobe clay
would be difficult and it must be done with extreme care
Cuts and fills on each lot should be avoided unless done
under the guidance of a qualified soils engineer We anti
cipate removal and replacement of the Adobe clay beneath
the proposed house location will probably be required
particularly for the up slope lots

The required limits of the Adobe removal would depend
on the actual ground condition thickness of Adobe
clay and ground slope ratio and the proposed individual
house design
Large retaining walls parallel to the contours and
supporting Adobe clay backfill are very difficult
to design due to large lateral forces and generally
should be avoided
We believe that the above soil conditions and recommenda
tions should be made available to the individual lot pur
chasers prior to their transactions It is important
that they should obtain proper guidance for their house
designs by a qualified soils engineer

Concrete Interceptor Ditch
A concrete interceptor ditch is planned along the upper
boundary of the site If placed directly on the existing
Adobe soils the concrete ditch would be subject to
large shrink swell movement and slope creep which could
damage and cause leaking of the structure Therefore
we recommend that the concrete interceptor ditch should
be over excavated and replaced with select non expansive
soil for a minimum of 3 feet around and below the drainage
ditch structure

Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling
down the hillside
The falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring
in all ridge slopes in Oahu Various methods of boulder
barriers or catchments can be used to reduce the hazard
of falling boulders The degree of safety of preventing
falling boulder hazards is a function of economics For
this site various methods of boulder prevention can be
utilized such as
1 A boulder catchment area about 10 to 15
feet wide with a fence or wall could
be utilized near the property boundary to
catch rolling boulders The concrete inter
ceptor ditch along the upper boundaries of
the site could be used in this capacity
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries
of the site could be performed to remove
the loose boulders that appear likely to
roll down
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1006 00 P 17
3 The natural trees and vegetation along
the upper portions of the site should be
retained to act as a natural barrier
against the boulders and to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the houses should be
set back away from the toe of the hillside
Slope to create an additional boulder catch
ment area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods are adopted
an inherent risk of falling boulders could still exist
for these hillside lots
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not
advanced to a point that a solution is available for this
area It is our opinion that no matter what one does
there is always the risk of boulders rolling down the
hill if not from areas near the house it will come
from areas high up on the slope Whatever measure is
chosen it only reduces the risk We would also like to
point out that no guarantee against future falling boulders
can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions with
time weathering erosion earthquake and construction vi
bration and improvements and construction activities at
and around the site

The owners of these hillside lots should be made aware of
the aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future
improvements in the upper areas of the lots which may dis
turb the stability of the existing boulders
Utilities
In order to reduce accumulation of water around the utility
lines from seepage leakage etc which could saturate
and weaken the Adobe soils we recommend that clean
gravel be used as cushion material below the pipes
The trench and gravel cushion layer should be shaped to
drain water into catch basins or manholes
