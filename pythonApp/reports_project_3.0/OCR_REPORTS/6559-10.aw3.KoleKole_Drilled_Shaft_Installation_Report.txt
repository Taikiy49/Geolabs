PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Kolekole Pass Road starts from the lower end of Lualualei Naval Magazine and
winds its way up the Waianae Range through Kolekole Pass and down into the Schofield
Army Reservation on the Wahiawa side of the mountain range The road is a two lane
asphalt paved road with unpaved shoulders A slope failure occurred during a storm in
December 2008 and was further aggravated by a storm in January 2011 The rainfall
events produced large amounts of water that flowed over the edge of the shoulder
embankment causing slope failure and damage collapse of the road pavement
Currently barriers are situated along the top of the slope failure and the road is closed to
traffic The project location and vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The Design Build project is to install a temporary steel panel bridge across the
failed area to allow traffic to bypass the failed area until permanent road repairs can be

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural
loading a deep foundation system consisting of 30 inch diameter cast in place concrete
drilled shafts were utilized for foundation support of the temporary bridge structure Each
abutment structure will be supported on two drilled shafts The drilled shafts were
designed to derive vertical support primarily from skin friction The end bearing component
of the drilled shafts was discounted in the design due to difficulties associated with
obtaining a clean bottom during construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The borings at the temporary bridge site generally encountered a surface fill layer
ranging from about 0 8 to 0 9 feet thick underlain by colluvium to depths of 21 to 36 5 feet
The colluvial deposit was underlain by brecciated basalt rock formation extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 101 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The surface fill consisted of asphaltic concrete and dense cobbly basalt gravel with
sand The colluvial deposit generally consisted of medium dense to very dense sand with
clay gravel cobbles and boulders and very dense silty gravel sand with cobbles and
boulders The brecciated basalt rock formation was hard to very hard
We did not encounter groundwater in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels may also vary with
seasonal rainfall time of the year and other factors

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company installed one trial shaft one load test
shaft and four production shafts for the temporary bridge project The approximate drilled
shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2
Trial and Load Test Shafts
A 30 inch diameter trial shaft designated as Trial Shaft was installed between
October 10 and 11 2012 down to a tip elevation of 119 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL on
the north side of the bridge The Trial Shaft was constructed using an IMT AF10
track mounted drill The purpose of the Trial Shaft was to examine the adequacy of the
methods and equipment proposed by the contractor to install the drilled shafts into the
existing subsurface soil rock deposits
Using the acceptable construction methods demonstrated in the trial shaft a
30 inch diameter instrumented load test shaft designated as Load Test Shaft was
installed from October 11 to 13 2012 at the project site The load test shaft was installed
using the same equipment that was used to install the trial shaft The purpose of the load
test shaft was to confirm the design assumptions and to confirm or modify the estimated
design tip elevations of the production drilled shafts
After the concrete for the load test shaft was allowed to cure for 9 days
LoadTest Inc performed the load test on The 30 inch diameter load
test shaft was loaded to approximately 1 293 kips The load test results are presented in
the report prepared by LoadTest Inc entitled Report on Drilled Shaft Load Testing

Production Drilled Shafts
A total of four 30 inch diameter production drilled shafts designated as
Shaft Nos 1 through 4 were installed for the temporary bridge structure between
November 27 and 29 2012 The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the
Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2 A Geolabs Inc representative observed the shaft
excavation placement of the steel reinforcements and concrete placement operations
during the installation of the production drilled shafts
The production shafts were installed using an IMT AF160 track mounted drill rig
equipped with 30 and 35 inch diameter rock augers and core barrels and a 22 inch
diameter clean out bucket A 35 inch outside diameter temporary steel casing was used to
case the upper portions of the drilled shafts during shaft construction The production
shafts were excavated down to elevations ranging from about 128 7 to 138 3 feet MSL
A summary of the production shafts installed at the project site is presented in the
Drilled Shaft Installation Summary Plate 3 In addition the drilled shaft records for the
production drilled shafts are attached
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the steel
reinforcement rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were
used to provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches
between the sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage To maintain the
vertical and horizontal alignment of the rebar cage during the concrete pour the rebar
cage was supported on the surface and aligned from the temporary casing until the cage
was supported by the placed concrete
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a 4 inch ID
steel pipe by tremmie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the tremmie
pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete

Based on our field observations and the results of the CSL testing we believe that
the production drilled shafts for the temporary bridge abutment for the Repairs to Kolekole
Pass Road Landslide Area project have been installed in general conformance with the
project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction
practices The location of the top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey
equipment to verify the shaft position is within the tolerance in the project specifications
Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that
the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load
capacity
