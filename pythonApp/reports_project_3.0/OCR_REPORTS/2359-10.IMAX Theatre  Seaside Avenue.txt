Pile Driving Report
imax Theatre
Seaside Avenue
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with E Kent Halvorson s special inspection services agreement
dated and our proposal letter dated we have observed the
production pile predrilling and driving at the project site from March 1 to March 7 1991
Sixteen and one half inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles were
utilized for the theatre foundations A single acting diesel hammer with
a rated driving energy of approximately 55 000 foot pounds was used to drive the piles
A total of forty eight 48 piles were driven to approximately 40 5 to 160 0 feet below the
existing ground surface Individual pile driving records have been transmitted to E Kent
Halvorson Inc during the course of pile driving and they are also enclosed here in the pile
driving report
Due to soft zones within the upper coral formation fifteen 15 of the piles were
spliced and driven deeper to the underlying competent bearing layers as recommended
in our letter for pile splicing issued during the course of pile driving on March 4 1991

A pile location plan and a summary of the driven piles are also presented
Based on our observations we believe that the production piles were driven to
sufficient resistance and or depths to provide the design capacity of 100 tons per pile
Due to the high blowcounts encountered during final driving of the piles we also believe
that a static pile load test on the driven piles may not be needed
