INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction of the proposed Waikoloa Greens lo
cated in Waikoloa Hawaii
Our preliminary recommendations were discussed with the pro
ject architect and structural engineer during a meeting on
August 14 1980
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
a total of eight 8 borings ranging in depth from 4 5 feet
to 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approxi
mate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate l

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of six two story
high townhouse structures probably of wood frame construc
tion A swimming pool and comfort station will be located
near the southern boundary adjacent to Melia Street Slab
on grade construction will be utilized for each townhouse
About 65 parking stalls with the necessary roadways are also
planned for the proposed development
During the site grading fill would be placed to generate
the required townhouse building areas over the existing north
and northwestern slopes Fill as much as 19 0 feet thick is
planned to achieve the desired grades Large retaining walls
are planned on these slopes to retain the planned fill and
structures

Geology
The site at Waikoloa is situated on the western slope of
the Mauna Kea volcano approximately near the contact be
tween the older upper member of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
towards the northeast and the younger lower member of the
Laupahoehoe Volcanic Series towards the southwest These
two series are comprised of basaltic lavas which vary in
chemical and mineral composition dependent upon the time or
age of its occurrence
At the site the rock presents itself as an Olivine basalt
laid probably by both pahoehoe and aa lava flows to
greater depths The relief of these flows like the surroun
ding areas of the site are usually rough hills and depres
Sions created as the lava migrated down Slope Lava tubes
and clinker layers are known to exist in the general area of
South Kohala
When compared to other nearby areas within even 5 miles
the Waikoloa site is comprised of relatively older flows
showing signs of surface weathering clinker layers and
the development of soil horizons Although weathering is
present erosion has not occured to any visible extent
probably due to the lack of surface run off in the area

Surface Conditions
The proposed project site is located directly across the
roadway from the Waikoloa Golf Course Club House in Waikoloa
South Kohala Hawaii The site s area is approximately
2 1 acres and is bordered on the north and east by the golf
course on the west by the Waikoloa Village Condominiums
and on the south by the existing roadway off from Lua Kula
Street The relief of the site is basically level but gently
sloping in a westward direction at a 5 to 8 percent grade
Along the north and northwest boundaries the property
slopes down towards the golf course with slopes ranging from
40 to 45 percent There were no existing structures present
on the site during the time of the investigation The exis
ting vegetation consisted of a slight to moderate growth of
grass and shrubs from 1 to 4 feet in height
Of particular interest are the slopes bordering the north
and northwest sides of the site Surficially these slopes
were observed to be comprised of moderately dense gravel to
cobble sized angular aggregates in a brown sandy and silty
matrix of very low moisture This layer of material was
noted to be at least 3 feet thick at the bottom of the slope
adjacent to the golf course A moderate growth of grass and
GEOLABS HAWAII

Page 5
shrubs up to 5 feet in height and a few small trees were
growing on these slopes There were no notable signs of
slope erosion or instability during our exploration
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
a total of eight 8 borings ranging from 4 5 feet to 15 0
feet Boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate l
Based on the information obtained from the borings the site
is generally underlain by the following materials
1 Sandy silt with gravel The surface material consis
ting of sandy silt with gravel highly weathered basalt
was encountered ranging in depths from 1 0 feet in
Boring No 7 to 5 0 feet in Boring No 8 About 8
inches of the upper material is in loose condition and
contains vegetation roots Some clayey silt pockets
were noted to exist in this formation due to the ex
cessive in place weathering of the rock near the sur
face
2 Silty sand and gravel The materials underlying the
above sandy silt consisted of mostly Silty sand and
gravel weathered basalt extending down to as much
as 7 feet in Boring No 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 950 00
Page 6
3 Olivine basalt Massive olivine basalt formation was
encountered underlying the site below the surface
materials and extended to the depths of the deeper
borings Boring 4 and 6
Thin weathered zones and clinker layers were noted to
be present throughout the basalt formation
No groundwater was encountered in our drilled borings during
our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on an evaluation of the field exploration data
laboratory testing and the results of our engineering ana
lysis the site is suitable for the proposed development
from a soils engineering point of view provided our recom
mendations are incorporated in the project design and con
struction
For the project site grading up to 19 feet of fill is
planned near the western corner of Building No B 2 loca
tion High retaining walls will be utilized along the
Slopes bordering the north and northwestern side of the
property to confine the fill material These high retaining
W O 950 00
Page 7
wall footings should bear on the underlying basalt rock
Large amount of lateral loads due to the high fill and
building structures will have to be resisted by the re
taining walls Wall tie backs could be used to provide
additional lateral support
Spread and line footings from the wood framed townhouse
structures can be supported on the upper undisturbed sandy
silt soil or controlled structural fill
Buildings close to the high retaining walls along the
existing slopes would bear on varying thicknesses of back
fill Some differential settlement should be anticipated
between the footings resting on varying thicknesses of fill
or different materials
Based on the above understanding additional recommendations
for the design and construction of the retaining walls
spread footing foundation and site preparation are presented
in the subsequent sections of this report

Settlement
Differential settlements would occur between footings when
the footings are placed on different thickness of fill or
materials However it is anticipated that most of the
settlement should occur during site and building construc
tion
Our preliminary estimates indicate that differential settle
ment less than inch should be anticipated for the above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
Page 14
conditions However the future differential settlement
could be minimized by founding most of the footings and
floor slabs of structure either on undisturbed in place
soil or on uniform thickness of compacted fill In the
event the floor slab and adjacent footings rest on varying
thickness of fill or partly on natural soils and fill
such as the case under Building B 2 we recommend
that the concrete slab on grade should be separated
from foundation with expansion joints provided at regular
intervals The differential settlement could also be re
duced by increasing the stiffness of the structural members
spanning the varying fill thickness or fill and natural
soil
Differential settlement of footings bearing on similar ma
terial would be minimal
Site Grading
Prior to receiving any fill the slopes and ground surface
should be stripped to remove vegetation and debris It is
estimated that at least 8 inches of the upper sand silt
crust consisting of organic material would have to be
wasted or stockpiled for possible future landscaping use
All the fill areas exposed should then be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 950 00
Page 15
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 prior to any fill
placement
Up to 19 0 feet of fill would be placed behind the north
and northwestern retaining walls during site grading
Based on our observations during this investigation the
near surface material up to 2 feet on the Slopes is very
dry and relatively loose Prior to placing any fill mater
ial on the slopes the dry and loose material should be
recompacted or removed and replaced with the new fill
material to avoid future settlement problems
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to pro
perly bond the new fill to the slope The filling opera
tion should start at the lowest point and continue to in
level compacted layers in accordance with the above struc
tural fill requirements
The on site excavated soils if available can be used for
minor fill purposes

Floor Slabs
The slab on grade floor can be supported on natural undis
turbed soil or engineered fill or on a combination of
both In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would
be undesirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings
are to be used a minimum of four inches of granular type
material should be placed to act as a capillary break In
addition a moisture proof membrane should be placed over
the granular material to act as a vapor barrier The
membrane should be covered by on inch of sand to protect
it during construction and to aid in curing the concrete
In order to minimize differential slab settlement supported
on a combination of fill and natural undisturbed soil or
GEOLABS HAWAII
