This letter report summarizes our probing operations at the above site between
January 17 and 20 2011
Probing Operations
The probing operations were performed in general conformance with the project
specifications The purpose of the probing operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the proposed facility
MJ Construction Inc marked the probe hole locations at the column locations and
around the facility perimeter prior to the probing operation
In general the probe holes were located on a 11 foot nominal spacing along interior
footings and 14 foot spacing along the exterior footings The approximate probe hole
locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the probe holes
were drilled to a depth of about 11 feet below the existing ground surface 10 feet below
bottom of the foundation
The probe holes about 4½ inches in diameter were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers After drilling the probe holes
temporary pipes 2 inch PVC were inserted into the holes to keep the holes open prior to
the grouting operations A total of 107 probe holes were drilled with an aggregate length
of about 1 177 lineal feet below the existing ground surface
Suspected voids were encountered while drilling several of the probes In
noticeable cavities ranging from 0 5 to 4 feet in diameter and 5 to 10 feet below the
