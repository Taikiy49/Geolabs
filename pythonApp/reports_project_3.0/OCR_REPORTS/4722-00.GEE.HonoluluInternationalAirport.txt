DRILLING AND SAMPLING
A total of 65 coring and boring locations selected by the owner client were
drilled at several sections of the existing pavement on the runways taxiways and aprons
at Honolulu International Airport
Boring Locations
Twenty locations were drilled down to the original subgrade material to depths
varying from 5 to 8 feet below the existing pavement Thickness of existing asphaltic and
Portland cement concrete pavement including thickness of structural fill were explored
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches and ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate a sample
depths Bulk soil samples were also collected from each borehole for CBR laboratory
testing The locations of the boreholes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and listed on
the Summary of Coring Boring Locations Plate A 1 The description and classifications of
the soils encountered in each borehole are shown on the Logs of Borings Plates A 3
through A 22

For each boring location selected samples were tested for moisture content unit

Coring Locations
Forty five coring locations were drilled through the existing pavement The cored

Laboratory Testing
Selected soils samples from the boring locations were tested as an aid in the soil
classification and evaluation of soil properties Tests performed included moisture content
unit weight particle size analyses and California Bearing Ratio CBR The results of the
laboratory tests are shown on Plates B 1 through B 37
Ten asphalt concrete core samples selected by Applied Pavement Technology Inc
were submitted to Advance Transportation Research and Engineering Laboratory in
The core samples were tested in accordance with ASTM D 1423
Test Resilient Modulus of Asphalt Concrete Test results were submitted directly to
Applied Technology Inc
Five of the Portland Cement concrete core samples were selected by Applied
Pavement Technology Inc and tested by Geolabs Inc in according to ASTM C 496
Split Tensile Strength The results of the laboratory tests are shown on Plate C 1

Limitations
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain a general
overview of the conditions of the pavement in several locations for maintenance and
rehabilitation purposes Boring coring were performed only at locations designated by
the owner client within the existing pavements along runways taxiways hardstands
and terminal aprons
It should be noted that variations of subsoil conditions between and beyond the
field borings corings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway
The locations of the field borings corings indicated in this report are approximate
having been designated by Fujita and Associates Inc The stratification lines shown on
graphic representations of the borings corings depict the approximate boundaries
between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition Water level data
presented in the text of this report were based on measurements recorded in the
