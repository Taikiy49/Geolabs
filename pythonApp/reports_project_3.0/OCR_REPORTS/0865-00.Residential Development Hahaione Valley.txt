Hahaione Valley The site covers
the lower western slope at the head of the valley
Access to the site is by Kulani and Kahena Streets at the
south makai end of the site and Pukoo Street at the north
east mauka corner of the site

Geology
Hahaione Valley is typical of the steep sided stream cut
valleys along the southern leeward side of the Koolau Range
Erosion of the windward slope of the Koolau Range has removed
most of the headwater source of the stream runoff that ori
ginally carved this valley The initial periods of erosion
were followed by several periods of relative rise and fall
of sea level which permitted the valley to fill with alluvium
followed by subsequent alternating periods of erosion alluvium
and colluvium deposition and formation of clay soils During
later stages of valley development submarine and subaerial
explosions of Koko Head Volcano deposited layers of volcanic
ash and breccia Much of the ash and breccia was eroded from
the valley walls and mixed with the existing boulder and clay
deposits The result is a subsurface profile of heterogeneous
deposits of clay boulder clay and boulder colluvium with
both ashy and clayey matrixes

Natural Soils
Excluding the stockpiled material the site is generally
covered by one to several feet of dark gray expansive adobe
clay Underlying this layer is a boulder deposit with a
variable cemented soil matrix underlain at varying depths
by basalt bedrock
On the western slope the adobe clay is about 1 to 4 feet
thick and in general overlies a boulder colluvium Dense
gray basalt was noted to outcrop within the property boun
daries as shown on the Site Plan Plate l

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based upon the results of our test pit excavations site
reconnaissance laboratory testing and engineering analyses
the site is suitable for the proposed residential development
provided our recommendations are implemented during the design
and construction phase Residential structures on post and
beam type of construction is well suited for the site
The upper stratum of existing fill soils within the relatively
level area makai of the stockpile at about elevation of 138
feet and below consists of moderately firm fill According
to recommendations of the previous soil engineer the fill
materials may be left in place However in order to provide
satisfactory footing support foundations should either be
supported on a 3 foot layer of compacted granular fill under
the footing or extended to the natural underlying soils
Shallow foundations consisting of a rigid mat or concrete
raft would also provide suitable support if desired we
would provide recommendations for this alternate foundation

this level area foundations may be established in the en

beneath the footings
Heterogeneous nests of boulders with large open spaces or
voids instead of soil matrix are located at random within
the existing stockpiles there is a potential for differential
footing settlements and migration of fines into the voids
could result in random loss of ground because of piping
Therefore these stockpile materials should not be left in
place
Preliminary grading plan for the site indicate that the
proposed final grade will range from elevation 142 feet
near the south end of the makai stockpile to about elevation
282 feet towards the north end of the site This grading
will require that majority of the stockpiles will be cut
away during the grading operations We recommend hauling
off site large boulder materials greater than 12 inches in
dimension The remaining material can then be reused as
engineered fill material with selective grading techniques
Even though the moist adobe has been preloaded beneath the
stockpile substantial downslope creep could occur because of

sloping natural soil

Subdrains
A subdrain or perforated pipe should be installed in the
former stream valley after excavating the existing fill
The pipe should be centered in a blanket of gravel drain
rock The recommended location of subdrains are roughly
indicated on Plate 1 Site Plan and may be modified
during construction In areas where no cut and fill is
planned such as the area southeast of Kahena Street
extension and below elevation 138 the subdrains should be
at least 3 feet deep and connected to a convenient discharge
outlet such as the existing drainage channel
Slopes
Maximum fill and cut slopes of up to 15 feet are planned
within the development Permanent cut slope in boulder
colluvium formation can be designed for 2 1 H V slope
ratio Flatter slope ratio of about 3 1 H V should be
maintained for cut greater than 5 feet high in adobe
clay talus formation
Fill slopes of 3 horizontal to 1 vertical are planned
and should be maintained for the on site clayey boulder
fill If this slope gradient is maintained no drainage
terrace in mid slope is required and no slope stability
problem are anticipated

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be avoided on this project Where
slab on grade is required in garage areas a four inch
layer of base rock should be provided between bottom of
concrete and subgrade In general subgrade soil should
consist of a two foot capping of non expansive select
borrow In order to protect against moisture infiltration
a polyethylene moisture barrier may be incorporated within
the base the membrance should be protected by a layer of
sand If the selective grading procedure of using low to
non expansive upper fill soil is implemented during con
struction the two foot capping may be eliminated

APPENDIX C
Standard Grading Specifications
STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
HAHAIONE VALLEY SUBDIVISION
UNIT 2B 3
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
II
Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of trash debris organic matter and all other dele
terious materials These materials shall be removed
and disposed off site including any boulders greater
than 12 inch in size
Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose
competent natural soils Localized soft pockets
shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted
select materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by
the soils engineer prior to the placement of any
fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to
a depth of six inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted as herein specified
IV
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope
of 7 horizontal to 1 vertical 7 1 the
ground surface shall be keyed and benched
into competent soil to bond the fill to
the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains will be required as
directed by the soils engineer where
springs or zones of seepage are encountered
For approximate recommended subdrain locations
refer to Plate 1 and body of the report
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils
or approved import material Import materials
shall be tested and approved prior to transpor
tation to the site Fill material shall contain
less than 5 percent organic matter and other
deleterious material and shall not contain
rocks or lumps in excess of twelve inches 12
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers
up to 12 inch lifts which when compacted
will not exceed nine inches 9 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade
water content within each layer
mr em ee
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve
inches 12 in diameter may be utilized
provided they are not concentrated in any
one area and that there is sufficient fine
grained material to fill the voids between
the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in
diameter shall be removed from the fill and
hauled off the site unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the
soils engineer in writing
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM Test Designation D 1557
unless otherwise specified in the soils
report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot
vibratory or pneumatic tire rollers suited
to the soil type and capable of producing the
specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working
condition fully ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a
systematic and uniform manner over the entire
fill area to produce a uniformly compacted fill
VI
4
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
