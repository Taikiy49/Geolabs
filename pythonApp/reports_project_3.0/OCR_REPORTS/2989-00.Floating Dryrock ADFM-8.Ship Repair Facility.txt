Project Considerations
The project is located in Apra Harbor at Naval Station Orote Point on the island of
Guam in the Mariana Islands There is an existing floating at the site
The existing moorings for the drydock have been damaged by recurrent typhoons and
tropical storms It is proposed to set new moorings for the existing drydock
In order to obtain soil data for the design of the anchors a total of eight 8 borings
were drilled and sampled around the drydock Four 4 of the borings were drilled
outboard of the drydock in water ranging from about 39 to about 62 feet in depth The
remaining four 4 borings were drilled on land or at the edge of the existing sheetpile wall
along the shore
It is our understanding that your firm will do the ocean engineering and anchor
design Our scope of work has been limited to drilling sampling and logging of borings
along with laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the exploration
This report summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings

General Site Description
The project site is situated at the Ship Repair Facility in the Apra Harbor Naval
Reservation on the island of Guam Mariana Islands The site is northerly of the steep
bluffs descending from Sumay to Apra Harbor and easterly of Sumay Cove The
topography on the land side of the site is generally level and appears to be reclaimed
land built by filling of shallow reefs on the southerly side of Outer Apra Harbor
The existing drydock moored at the site is at the end of a causeway leading from
the Ship Repair Facility area The end of the causeway is bulkneaded by sheetpile walls
The harbor bottom at the site drops sharply below the drydock to a depth of about 65
feet below Mean Sea Level and then rises on the outboard side of the drydock to depths
of less than 10 feet on the southern portion of the Jade Shoals
Summary of Soil Survey
Regional Geology
Guam is the southernmost of the Mariana Island archipelago which is an island arc
parallelling the trend of the subduction zone forming the Mariana Trench
The base of the island was formed during the Eocene Epoch by the eruption of
basaltic rhyolite lavas forming a volcano which is now the southern portion of the island
On the northern flank of this volcanic massif during the Oligocene and Miocene Epochs
a complex of coral and algal reefs built an elongated platform of limestones and related
deposits Tectonic activity has lifted these coral algal limestones above sea level to form
the northern Guam Plateau and Orote Peninsula
Weathering and solution working of the limestones has partially recrystallized the
calcium carbonate of the limestones forming massive limestones in some areas and
poorly to well cemented detrital limestones in others Typically the detrital limestones are
highly variable in strength characteristics and are frequently karstic or cavernous in
nature
The project site is situated at the base of the limestone plateau forming Orote
Peninsula on the southerly side of Apra Harbor There is evidence that Apra Harbor may
be the result of faulting along the northerly side of Orote Peninsula Military Geology of
Guam Mariana Islands Tracey et al 1959
Luminao Reef and Cabras Island on the northerly side of the harbor form a barrier
reef providing shelter to the interior This sheltered condition creates a lagoonal
environment in which patch reefs grow forming shoals such as the Jade Shoals In this
lagoonal environment the softer corals such as finger coral tend to thrive Therefore the

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by the drilling and sampling of eight 8
borings Four 4 of these borings were drilled in the water outboard of the existing
drydock Two 2 of the borings were drilled on land on the easterly side of the causeway
and two 2 were drilled on the water side of the sheetpile wall on the westerly side of the
Causeway
The locations of the borings were established in the field by surveyors from Black
Construction Company The elevations of the land borings were also established during
this survey
Off shore Exploration
Off shore drilling and sampling for this project was conducted using a skid
mounted Diedrich Drill Model D 25 on a makeshift raft platform The platform was
constructed of timber and lumber The raft was about 20 by 20 feet in dimension
and utilized 55 gallon drums for flotation It was necessary to import the lumber
materials from Honolulu since lumber of the proper dimensions was not available
on the local economy
The raft was moved to the boring locations by a small work boat Once the raft
was at the location it was secured using a four point mooring system of Danforth
and Hawaiian rock anchors with soft line The mooring system was adjusted to
compensate for tidal fluctuations and currents
The depth to the mudline was established by lowering a plate on a line The depth
at which the plate comes to rest was considered to be the mudline Once the
depth of the mudline was established the drilling and sampling of borings
commenced using the rotary wash boring method
Land Exploration
Exploration on land was conducted using a Central Mine Equipment Model CME 45B
truck mounted drilling rig Borings were drilled and sampled using a combination
of auger and rotary techniques

Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the annulus of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four inches 4 in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown
on the Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Sieve Analysis tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are performed to determine the
grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for classification in
the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other engineering
properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 4
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Specific Gravity may be used to corelate the blow counts from the Standard
Penetration Tests to the relative density to evaluate overburden loadings on anchors
Tests were performed in accordance with ASTM Test Method C 127 on the portions of
samples coarser than the No 4 Sieve and in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 854
on the portions of samples finer than the No 4 Sieve The values determined by C 127
are apparent specific gravity values These test results are summarized on Plate B 5
Limitations
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from field data points such as borings and visual observations Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaiuate the recommendations given in this report
