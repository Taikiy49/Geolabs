PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The three building sites are located near the center of the
Waimea School Complex
The proposed building sites are immediately southwest of the exist
ing classroom buildings The three proposed buildings will be
1l story buildings of hollow block columns and walls with steel
roof trusses Maximum column loads are expected to be on the
order of 50 kips
The site has been previously graded and approximately 2 4 feet
of fill has been placed over natural ground in this area

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
The Waimea School property is located on alluvial outwash and
residual silty clay soil developed as a thin veneer 2 5 feet
over slightly to highly weathered vesicular Koloa volcanic series
olivine basalt
Koloa volcanic series basalt flows were laid down over the actively
developing topography that separated the Koloa from the Waimea
volcanic series The contact between the two series is highly
irregular
Page 4
W O 345 50
The Koloa basalt flows have been weathered to produce an approxi
mately 6 layer of gray clay overlying its surface Many of the
fractures in the basalt flow are filled with this gray clay
Subsequently streams deposited red SILTS CLAYS and BASALT
GRAVEL and COBBLES over the surface to depths of a few feet
Very little sand size material is present
A representative section of the school ground site as seen exposed
in a trench consisted of approximately 2 feet of FILL overlying
about 3 to 4 feet of red SILTY CLAY which overlaid 6 inches of
gray clay on top of weathered BASALT Boring No 6 nearby
indicated about 4 feet of weathered BASALT underlain by generally
unweathered vesicular BASALT
The impermeable nature of the clay and basalt and wash boring
precluded accurate determination of natural subsurface water levels
at the school grounds

FOUNDATIONS
Conventional spread or continuous footings can be utilized
to support the proposed buildings
1 Footings on Firm Natural Ground
Footings founded on undisturbed firm natural ground
basalt and basalt boulders may be designed for 3000 psf
maximum allowable bearing capacity Any soft or loose
pockets in footing excavations must be removed and
replaced with lean concrete or compacted structural fill
2e Footings on Structural Fill
Our field investigation indicated that the entire site
was covered by 2 to 4 feet of fill An additional 1 to
2 feet of fill is also planned for the building pads
The maximum allowable bearing capacity for footings
founded on structural fill shall not exceed 1500 psf
Footing should have a minimum width of 12 inches and
should be at least 18 inches below the adjacent ground
surface

SLAB ON GRADE
Red silty CLAY and red clayey SILT encountered in all
borings are considered to be moderately expansive soils
It is recommended that the soil under slabs be kept moist
A vapor barrier is also recommended Slabs placed on
structural fill should be at least 4 inches thick and have
a 4 inch cushion of free draining material to provide a
capillary break
STRUCTURAL FILL
Topsoil disturbed natural ground and any uncompacted fill
must be removed prior to placing structural fill Structural

Backfill of footing and utility trenches should be compacted
to 90 relative compaction
LATERAL EARTH PRESSURE
Retaining walls should be designed for an equivalent fluid
pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot provided only moderate
compaction of backfill is required If a high degree of
backfill compaction is required or the walls cannot yield
even slightly an equivalent fluid pressure of 60 pounds
per cubic foot should be used Surcharges due to adjacent
footings hydrostatic pressure construction equipment etc
must be added to the above values
