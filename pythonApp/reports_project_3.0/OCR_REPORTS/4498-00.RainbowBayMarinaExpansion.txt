PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located within the existing Rainbow Bay Marina at
57 Arizona Memorial Drive in the Pearl Harbor area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Based on the information provided the existing docking facility consists of a long
floating dock and a short fixed wooden pier We understand that the proposed marina
expansion project will involve the demolition of the fixed wooden pier and steel H piles
addition of two floating concrete boat docks to accommodate 128 boat slips and associated
improvements
The new floating concrete boat docks designated as Bravo Dock and Charlie Dock
will be located to the southwest of the existing Delta Dock as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided the new Bravo and Charlie Docks will have a nominal
length of about 320 and 359 feet respectively We understand that the proposed new
floating concrete boat docks will be guided by approximately twenty 20 inch octagonal
precast prestressed concrete piles The layout of the piling foundations for the two new
floating docks is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The top of the piling foundations is set at
Elevation 6 5 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW Based on the information provided

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The available subsurface information at the project area was compiled and
evaluated to establish a generalized subsurface soil profile for our engineering analyses
The available subsurface information used in our analyses included but was not limited
to the following
previous borings conducted by Dames Moore in the project area
recent borings conducted by Dames Moore for this particular project
spudding results conducted by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc for this project and
geologic data and our experience in the project area
Based on the available information the mudline is generally encountered at depths

floating dock alignments Below the mudline the project site is generally underlain by soft
to very soft lagoonal deposits overlying stiff to very stiff alluvial soils extending to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 103 feet below the water surface Estuarine

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site and the spudding
data provided design pile lengths of 55 65 and 75 feet were selected for the piling
foundations of the proposed new floating docks for the subject project These design pile
lengths would generally result in an embedment length of about 33 to 45 feet into the
lagoonal and alluvial soil deposits encountered below the mudline The recommended pile
lengths at each of the proposed pile locations along the proposed new floating docks is
shown on the Pile Length Plan Plate 4 In addition the number of piles required for each
of the design pile lengths recommended for the project is summarized in the table presented
in the Lateral Load Resistance section of this report
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral load resistance for driven piles is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding
soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of the pile and the induced
moment in the pile Based on the available subsurface information and the lateral load
demand provided foundation analyses using the computer program LPILE plus for
Windows was performed to evaluate the maximum top of pile deflections of the various
design pile lengths selected for the project
The program LPILE plus for Windows is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite
difference laterally loaded deep foundation program originally developed at the University
of Texas at Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a deep
foundation under lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis was carried out with the
use of non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli at various depths The analyses were
performed for a laterally loaded 20 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete pile with a
free head boundary condition at the top of the pile Based on the lateral load demand of
6 kips per pile provided the results of our lateral load analyses are presented in the following

Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a rated energy of
about 30 000 foot pounds of energy per blow The hammer should be equipped with an
energy control level such that driving within the loose soft soils may be controlled at a lower
energy to reduce tensile stress development within the piles which could damage or cause
cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be driven
This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy to the top of the
pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs should review the pile driving
equipment proposed to evaluate if the equipment is capable of installing the piles to the
recommended pile tip elevations The Contractor should forward pile and driving equipment
data forms for review prior to construction for evaluation Geolabs should review the
submittal and assess the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven to the estimated pile tip elevations as shown in the above
table Pile driving may also be terminated when the aggregate blow counts are at least
10 blows per inch for the last 3 inches or 120 blows for the last foot The preliminary driving
criteria should be confirmed and or modified after selection of the pile driving equipment
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected
or its lateral load capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more
than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth During soft driving such
