PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves a study to select several potential sites for the location of new
2 to 6 Million Gallon MG reservoirs to supplement the existing Honolulu 180 System
The purpose of our work on the project is to provide preliminary geotechnical engineering
information for the sites to aid in evaluating their suitability for the intended use
This report addresses our review of available information including published
geologic maps soil surveys and aerial photography for the project site and a visit to the
site
HALAWA SITE H 9
The Halawa Site H 9 is on the southern wall of Halawa Valley adjacent to the H 201
freeway Ground surface elevations range from about 170 to 182 feet above Mean Sea
Level MSL
Based on a geologic map published by the US Geological Survey the site is
situated in a location which appears to be a contact between Koolau Basalt QTkl Older
Alluvium QTao and Honolulu Volcanic Series tuff QoT On this basis it is anticipated
that the site is underlain by shallow residual soils from both the basalt and tuff rock
formations underlain by saprolite and weathered basaltic rock formation derived from the
in place weathering of Koolau Basalt lavas Near the valley floor alluvial soils are
anticipated consisting of very stiff to hard clayey silts with gravel and cobbles

Exposed soils observed on the surface of the site were generally reddish brown
clayey silts These appear to have been weathered from volcanic ash which is
consistent with the soils in the area originating from the Salt Lake volcanic vent The
elevation of the site is relatively consistent with the cut slopes on the opposite side of
the freeway however the actual site is abnormally level as if it has been previously
graded
When viewing the site from the opposite side of Halawa Valley it was noted that
the site forms a distinct bench extending out from the valley wall with slopes of about
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V dropping down to the valley floor This is not
consistent with normal geomorphology and tends to further indicate that site has been
graded
Review of the topography shown on the Preliminary Grading Plan further
indicates that there has been previous grading work at the site with the western portion
of the site being fill built out from the valley wall It is believed that the cut fill daylight
line would be along an imaginary line created by extending the 165 foot contour across
the site in a north south direction
Based on our review of the site geology and geomorphology it appears that the site
is developable as a reservoir site and that adequate bearing would be available to
support a new reservoir However special design considerations might be required if the
structure does straddle between residual and fill soils Hard excavation may also be
required for grading work in volcanic rock formation if encountered
