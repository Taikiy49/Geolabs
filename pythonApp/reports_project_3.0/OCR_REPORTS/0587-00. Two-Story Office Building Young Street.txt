INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions at the proposed site and to provide recommenda
tions for the design and construction of the pavement and
the foundations for the proposed structure
W 1975
Site Description
The proposed site at 1276 Young Street Honolulu Hawati
is presently occupied by a single wall wood frame residence
A large portion of the essentially level lot is covered with
plants shrubs and fruit trees The back section is thickly
overgrown with haole koa 8 to 10 feet in height
Project Description
The proposed development will consist of a two story office
building with a parking lot It is anticipated that the
structure will be supported by spread and line foundations
and that slab on grade construction will be utilized for the
first floor
The office building will be constructed essentially at the
existing grade with minimal cuts and fills
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing two
test borings drilled at the location shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 Boring 1 extended to a depth of 12 9
feet and Boring 2 extended to 12 0 feet

Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
a layer of weathered volcanic cinders The moderate weath
ering has produced a brown silty sand with limited variation
in the sand to silt ratio The cinders extended to the limit
of the borings
No groundwater was encountered in either boring

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration and the laboratory testing dis
close that the site is suitable for the proposed development
Spread and line foundations with slabs on grade can be uti
lized without special provisions for expansive soils The
existing on site surface materials will also provide a good
subgrade for the proposed paved areas
Foundations
The existing on site silty sand is generally medium dense to
very dense and should provide adequate support for spread or

cc
Lie
W 1975
line foundations bearing a minimum of 18 inches below the
final adjacent grade The foundations should be designed
for maximum uniform contact bearing pressures of 3 000 pounds
per square foot and maximum toe pressures of 4 000 pounds per
square foot
The allowable uniform bearing pressure can be increased by
1 3 when considering seismic and wind loadings It is esti
mated that maximum settlements should not exceed inch total
and inch differential
Prior to the foundation construction the site should be
proof rolled and the surface compacted to 90 percent of the
maximum dry density to assure uniform foundation support
Any soft zones uncovered during the proof rolling operations
should be removed and the area backfilled with soil compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The
maximum density should be established in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation 1557 72T
Due to possible buried foundations or other subsurface irreg
ularities from the previous structures the foundations
excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to verify
that adequate bearing has been obtained Existing foundations
W 1975
uncovered in the building or pavement areas should be under
cut a minimum of 12 inches below the proposed subgrade and
backfilled with compacted soil
At the proposed bearing depth of 18 inches below grade the
foundations will not be protected against deep excavations on
adjoining properties In accordance with the Building Code
foundations less than twelve feet below grade must be under
pinned by the owner if deep excavations on adjoining proper
ties undercut the foundations Extending the foundation to
a twelve foot depth is not recommended unless there is a
strong possibility of deep excavations on adjoining proper
ties
Cesspools or other subsurface cavities encountered during
construction operations should be cleaned of uncompacted
debris and sludge and backfilled with compacted granular fill
Additional corrective measures or foundation modifications
may be required if the cesspool is within the proposed build
ing area
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade construction
will be used for the first floor Our laboratory tests show
W 1975
that the soil has expansive potential of less than one per
cent and is therefore considered non expansive A minimum
of four inches of crushed rock should be sufficient as
cushion material over prepared subgrade
A plastic vapor shield should be provided if moisture sensi
tive floor coverings are to be utilized
Asphalt Pavement
Upon examination of the on site material it is estimated
that a CBR value of 8 to 12 can be expected When compacted
properly the existing soils should provide adequate support
for the proposed pavement
The following pavement section should be considered for the
preliminary design of the paved areas
2 In Asphaltic Concrete
4 In Base Course
6 In Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a CBR value greater than 85 The final pave
ment section should be based upon the actual subgrade CBR
Measured during construction

Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
The earthwork placement and compaction should be inspected to
assure that the recommended degree of compaction is consis
tently achieved All fill should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
The on site soil is generally acceptable as fill but should
be tested to determine the maximum dry density and optimum
moisture content
