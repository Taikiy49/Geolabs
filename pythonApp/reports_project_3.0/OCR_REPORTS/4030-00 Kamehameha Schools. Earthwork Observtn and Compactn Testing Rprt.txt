This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the Kamehameha Schools
Snake Road from June 1998 through October 1998 in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated
Project Description
Snake Road is located on the campus of the Kamehameha Schools at Kapalama
Heights on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed project includes reconstruction of
approximately 600 feet of existing roadway from Princess Drive down to the dormitory
area A general layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1
Site Gradin
The grading operation at the project site consisted of clearing and grubbing
excavations to the design grades and or subgrades over excavation of unsuitable
materials expansive soils below the roadway subgrade and placement and compaction
of fills to near the finish subgrade elevation The contractor Royal Contracting Inc and
sub contractor Paul s Electric installed the underground utility lines and backfilled the

Site Preparation
The contractor started the earthwork by clearing and grubbing areas within the
contract grading limits in June 1998 Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials were removed and disposed of Following the clearing and grubbing
operations the exposed subgrades were generally moisture conditioned and compacted
to 90 percent of its maximum dry density
Where expansive soils were encountered near the finish subgrade elevations the
expansive soils were over excavated to a depth of 10 to 24 inches below the finish
subgrade level and backfilled with on site borrow material moisture conditioned and
compacted to 90 percent relative compaction in general accordance with ASTM Test
Method D1557 Modified Proctor
In areas that received fill the subgrade was scarified to a depth of about
12 inches where possible and compacted to a firm condition Loose areas disclosed
during clearing operations were over excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled
with compacted fill
Fills and Backfills
In areas to receive fill fill materials consisting of on site borrow were placed in
near level lifts of approximately 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned as
needed and compacted to the project requirement of at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Compaction was generally accomplished by
sheepsfoot rollers vibratory plates multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other
types of similar compaction equipment
The fill and backfill materials used generally consisted of well graded processed
on site granular materials less than 6 inches in maximum dimension structural fill
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report

In general the fills and backfills were placed in level lifts of approximately
12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction Fills and backfills within the upper 2 feet below road
subgrade level were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Periodic field density tests were performed to determine the relative compaction of
the fills placed for conformance with the specified minimum compaction Field density
tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method
performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the
compaction tests taken during mass grading operations for the Kamehameha Schools
Snake Road project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report These tests
were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at
the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes where available and sighting from plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked to achieve the
specified minimum compaction or until adequate compaction was achieved based on
visual observations
Utilities
Installation of the new utility lines generally included over excavation in
expansive soils with cobbles and boulders followed by backfilling the trench excavation
with one inch minus bedding material After placement of the utility line generally
free draining granular materials such as S4C were used for the trench backfill up to
about 12 inches above the pipes Trenches were then backfilled with 6 inch minus
on site material and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Where
trenches were located in paved areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the
road subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Field
density tests were performed as appropriate and are reported on Plates 3 1 through
3 4 However some of the unsatisfactory areas of utility trench backfill in the roadway
areas were reworked to achieve the specified minimum compaction during the
preparation of the roadway subgrade These tests are recorded in the remarks column
on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
Roadway
The expansive materials under the pavement areas were over excavated 10 to
24 inches then scarified to about 12 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and the top 24 inches compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction The degree of compaction was based on field density tests and visual
observations Field density tests were performed on the finished subgrade select
borrow and base course with results presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4

Retaining Walls
The retaining wall footings generally included over excavation in expansive soils
as per our Retaining Wall Subgrade Conditions letter dated This was
followed by backfilling the footing excavation with select borrow material The footing
subgrade was then compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The degree of
compaction was based on field density tests and visual observations Field density tests
were performed as appropriate and are reported on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
Evaluations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii during construction of the Kamehameha Schools Snake Road in the
period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
plans and specifications and recommendations stated in the above referenced report by
Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc dated
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
