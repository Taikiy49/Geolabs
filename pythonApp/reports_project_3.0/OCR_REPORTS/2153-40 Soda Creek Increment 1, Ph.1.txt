SITE GRADING
The subject site is located in the Ewa by Gentry development in the District of Ewa
on the island of Oahu Hawaii A general layout of the project site is provided on the Site
Plan Plate 1 The grading operations were performed by Delta Construction Company
The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of
engineered fills for house pads and roadways
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were
scarified brought to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of

90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American
Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill operation the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor Road subgrade materials were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill and
backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of 42 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests
taken during the house pad and roadway grading operations are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the house pad
grading and roadway construction observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii covered by
this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the
above referenced letter report dated May 4 1995
