INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 6 classroom building at the Kahakai Elementary School in
Kailua Kona on the island of Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2807 00 Page 3
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new 6 classroom building for the Kahakai Elementary
School in Kailua Kona on the island of Hawaii
Based on the general site plan dated provided to us a two 2 story
classroom building is proposed for the project The new building will measure
approximately one hundred 100 feet by sixty 60 feet in overall plan dimensions and will
accommodate six 6 classrooms three 3 on each floor a teachers workroom and
toilet facilities
Based on the information provided to us the estimated column and wall loads will
be approximately one hundred forty 140 kips and five and one half 5 5 kips per foot
respectively It is anticipated that concrete floors with CMU walls will be utilized for the
proposed building
Concrete corridors an exterior stairwell and a five 5 stall parking area are also
planned for the project
It is also our understanding that a septic tank and leaching field is being
considered for disposal of the sanitary waste generated by the facility

Although the site is relatively level cuts of approximately two or more feet
extending into the basaltic rock and embankments of up to approximately two 2 to
three 3 feet high may be anticipated for the classroom building

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project is located at the Kahakai Elementary School in Kailua Kona on the
western coast of the island of Hawaii
The building site is open grassy land bounded by an existing Building D to the
northeast a driveway to the southeast a parking lot to the southwest and a walkway to
the northwest Existing portable classroom buildings are located on the northern side of
the proposed building site while playground facilities such as swings jungle gyms tether
balls and monkey bars are located over the area The site and its development are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The existing ground surface at the site slopes gently downward to the south at a
gradient of about three 3 percent The topographic map provided indicates that the
existing ground elevations range from approximately sixty 60 feet to sixty seven 67 feet
above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven 7 borings to depths ranging between five 5 and twenty six and one half 26 5
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a very thin one 1 to five 5 inch thick
surface layer of sandy silt overlying an in situ basaltic rock formation down to the
maximum depths drilled
In Boring No 1 a gravelly sand layer was encountered below the very thin layer
of sandy silt down to a depth of approximately four 4 feet below the existing ground
surface

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The finish floor elevation s of the proposed building were not established at the
time of preparation of this report Review of the topographic map of the site provided to
us indicates that the finish floor elevations s will be governed by the existing pavements
and drainage structures and will probably be at about sixty four 64 and or sixty five 65
feet above Mean Sea Level
It may be anticipated that construction of the footings and slabs and the
recommended cushion layer below the slabs will entail excavation into the basaltic rock
formation over the northerly half of the building
Spread and wall foundations are recommended for the building
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2807 00 Page 9
The corridors will be of bi level construction the lower or ground level may be of
simple slab on grade walkway construction
The various building components should be structurally isolated each from the
other s
Footings Foundations
All spread and wall footings should be designed to bear on the in situ rock
formation including those in the vicinity of Boring No 1 where the surficial
gravelly sand must be penetrated and in the portion of the structure s
elevated above the existing ground surface All footings should be
embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade
An allowable bearing capacity of 5 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footing foundations bearing on the in situ rock formation The bearing value
indicated is for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up
to one third for loads of short duration such as those caused by wind and
seismic forces
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2807 00 Page 10
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be free of all rock debris and
loose surfaces All such loosened material should be scaled off and
removed and the concrete placed against firm and sound rock surfaces
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive pressure and frictional resistance We recommended
that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to
calculate the passive soil pressure developed against the buried vertical
faces of the footing foundations and a frictional coefficient of 0 4 be used
to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the footing bottoms
and the rock formation
Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the ground floors of the 6 classroom
building Since the slab may span over soil and rock conditions we
recommend that a one 1 foot thick cushion layer be provided below the
slabs to provide uniform bearing
The cushion layer should consist of non expansive granular materials
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of their maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2807 00 Page 11
The ground floor slabs for the corridors and stairwell may be designed as
walkways with four 4 inch thick slabs resting on a minimum six 6 inch
thick layer of compacted non expansive granular material Construction
joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways
with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Settlement
Settlements under the design loadings are anticipated to be minimal on the
differential It is considered important to isolate the various structural elements
of the 6 classroom building each from the other to reduce the potential for
Structural and cosmetic cracking which might otherwise be induced by
differential settlements
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The thin surficial layer of sandy silt in the area to be occupied by the proposed
construction should be completely removed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2807 00 Page 12
It is our opinion that excavation in the basaltic rock formation encountered at the
site may require hard ripping or hoerams Care must be exercised to avoid over ripping
which would disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting in a potential loss of
bearing strength
If imported fill material is required the material should be a select granular material
less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than twenty 20 percent passing the
No 200 sieve
Fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with the procedure described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the building
foundations
Sewage Disposal System
It is our understanding that a septic tank and leaching field system is being
considered for disposal of the sanitary waste generated by the facility

Based on our exploration and percolation test results it appears that the rate of
percolation in the vicinity of Boring Nos 5 and 6 is moderate to high However
percolation test results for Boring No 7 indicates a very slow absorption rate due to the
massive structure of the basalt formation encountered
It is Our opinion that a shallow leachfield design is feasible for the areas in the
vicinity of Boring Nos 5 and 6 at the site However due to the variability of the
percolation characteristics of the subsurface materials the absorption capacity of the
disposal system should be verified by additional field percolation tests performed during
construction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of
seven 7 borings to depths ranging between 5 0 and 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled by a truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary core
drilling tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard spilt spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches or for fraction of a foot where a full
foot of penetration was not practicable are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core
of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 5 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100

APPENDIX B
Field Percolation Testing
The in situ permeability of the on site soil was estimated by performing falling head
percolation tests in Boring and 7 The test results are presented in Plates B 1
through B 3
The percolation tests were performed by drilling four 4 inch diameter boreholes
down to approximately five 5 feet below the ground surface Six 6 inches to one 1
foot of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to protect them from
scouring and sediment The holes were then carefully filled with water to a minimum
depth of twelve 12 inches over the gravel pack The time required for the water level
inside the borehole to drop each inch was then recorded to provide data upon which to
base calculation of the percolation rates
