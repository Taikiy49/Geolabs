In accordance with our fee proposal dated June 21st and the subsequent
revisions and negotiations we have completed the cavity and probe hole grouting and
observation services at the above referenced site
The project involves the construction of a one story building for the proposed
Child Development Center within the Barbers Point Naval Air Station on the island of
Oahu Hawaii A subsurface probing program was conducted between May 26th and
June 2nd in an attempt to locate subsurface cavities beneath the foundation of the
proposed building A total of 87 probe holes were drilled to depths ranging from about
11 to 14 feet below the existing ground surface Results of the cavity probing program
conducted are presented in the above referenced report
Our cavity and probe hole grouting services were conducted on August 21st
under the near continuous observations by our field representative A total of 36 cubic
yards of grout mix consisting of 3 1 sand slurry supplied by Ameron was used in filling
up the probe holes and the existing large void in the slab area During our grouting
operations we were unable to locate probe hole nos 63 through 65 and 68 through

As discussed in our letter dated July 24th prepared for the subject project the
grouting operation was commenced at a depth of approximately 8 feet below the
existing ground surface for those probe holes where cavities and or voids were not
encountered For those probe holes where the probing logs indicated that cavities
and or voids may exist at greater depths the grouting operation was commenced at the
bottom of the cavities and or voids to reduce the potential for loss of foundation
support
Normally the grouting operations are followed closely after the probing
operations Therefore the longer the lag time between the probing and grouting
operations the higher the potential for a caved in probe hole It should be noted that
more than 6 weeks have elapsed since the initial probing operations for the subject
project Although the probe holes have been fitted with PVC pipes to maintain the
integrity of the open hole a slight amount of caving in was still observed in some of the
probe holes Based on our field observations and evaluation we believe that the
amount of caving in observed at the site did not significantly affect integrity of the probe
hole for our grouting operations
The grouting operation was generally proceeded in the following manner
Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe was
removed and a 2 inch diameter grouting probe was inserted into the open hole
and lowered to approximately 2 feet from the desired depth Grout injection was
then commenced at the direction of our field representative who recorded the
approximate grout take and provided direction as to when the injection was
completed or whether periodic injection was required
Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upwards by low to
moderate pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit which was retracted
from the probe hole as the grout filled If the grout take was noted to exceed
about 1 cubic yard in one probe hole the injection was suspended and the
apparatus moved to an adjacent hole for injection The hole was marked for
later reapplication of grout The purpose for grout interruption and reintroduction
was to allow the grout to set slightly prior to successive injection in an attempt
to control excess migration of the grout off site or away from the area of concern
