Shaft No. 1.7 installed for the above project. Based on our field observations and the CSL
test results, the following recommendations are presented for your review and
consideration.

CSL testing on Shaft No. 1.7 installed for the project. We performed CSL testing on
January 12, 2024 for Shaft No. 1.7 installed for the above project.

on December 28, 2023. The approximate locations of the shafts are presented on the

along with the reinforcing cage. Details of the drilled shaft as-built conditions are

Crosshole sonic logging tests were performed between all the possible access tube 
combinations in the drilled shaft (4 tubes resulting in 6 profiles). The northernmost access 
tube was designated as Access Tube No. 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered in a 
clockwise direction. 
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 0.1 feet above 
the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for Shaft No. 1.7. We 
understand that potable water was used to fill the access tubes after concrete placement. 
Based on our observation in the field, the water levels within the access tubes were above 
the top of the shaft concrete in the access tubes at the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the 
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. The 
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, are presented on 
Plate 2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on Plate 3. The 
individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile) and a plan showing the 
tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.6. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10%, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less 
than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all profiles for Shaft No. 1.7. It 
should be noted that changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects 
depending on the degree of the reduction and the associated increase in FAT. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for Shaft 1.7 
ranged from approximately 12,056 to 16,237 feet per second. We understand that during 
the concrete operations, the drilled shaft consisted of an overpour extending approximately 
to the road surface and concrete chipping will need to be performed down to the shaft 
cutoff elevation of approximately -0.52 feet MSL.  
Based on the results of the CSL test and FAT increases of less than 10%, we 
believe that the concrete integrity of Shaft No. 1.7 within the testing depth meets a 
Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the above CCRC. Based on our 
review of the CSL testing data and no significant defect/anomaly indicated by the CSL test, 
we recommend acceptance of Shaft 1.7 within the testing depth.
