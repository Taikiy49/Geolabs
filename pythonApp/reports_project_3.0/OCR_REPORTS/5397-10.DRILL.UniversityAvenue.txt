DRILLED SHAFT OBSERVATION REPORT
UNIVERSITY AVENUE MAILE WAY RETAINING WALL REPAIR
Dear Mr Higuchi
As requested Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering services during the
drilled shaft foundation installation for the University Avenue Maile Way Retaining Wall
Repair project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations performed
during the drilled shaft installation for the above retaining wall structure In general the
purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled shaft
foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
Geolabs Inc performed a geotechnical engineering exploration and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Maile Way and University Avenue Repair Project Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is near the intersection of University Avenue and Maile Way The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consists of the
removal and replacement of approximately 50 linear feet of the existing Concrete Rubble
Masonry CRM retaining wall adjacent to the Mid Pacific School baseball field
The existing CRM wall has a retaining height of about 9 feet with a concrete
sidewalk at the top of the wall area Based on visual observations of the existing wall face
it appears that the existing wall has moved out horizontally In addition it appears that the
existing concrete sidewalk and curb at the top of the wall has settled
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loading a deep
foundation system consisting of 24 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled shafts was
utilized for foundation support of the retaining wall structure Based on the drilled shaft
analysis results a minimum drilled shaft length of 15 feet was recommended for the
retaining wall foundation shafts The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the

The critical structural loading condition is due to lateral loading In addition the
drilled shafts were designed to derive vertical support primarily from skin friction The
end bearing component of the drilled shafts was discounted in the design
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The boring drilled at the retaining wall site generally encountered a surface fill
layer about 9 feet thick The surface fill layer generally consisted of stiff to very stiff clay
and loose to medium dense sands and gravels The surface fill layer was underlain by
medium stiff to very stiff clay to about 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
CMZ of Hawaii Inc installed the trial and production shafts A 24 inch diameter trial
shaft designated as TS was installed on Temporary steel casing was
not used in the construction of the trial shaft Some seepage water was encountered
during trial shaft construction The trial shaft was backfilled with Controlled Low Strength
Material CLSM
Based on the trial shaft installation the drilled shaft subcontractor generally
demonstrated that the proposed excavation methods and equipment were acceptable for
installing the drilled shaft foundations in general accordance with the project specifications
Nine 24 inch diameter production shafts were installed at the University
Avenue Maile Way Retaining Wall Repair project site from October 24 to
The shafts were designated as Shaft Nos 1 through 9 The
approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan
Plate 2 A Geolabs representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel
reinforcements and concrete placement operations during the installation of the
production drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a Casagrande B130E track mounted
drill rig equipped with 24 inch rock and soil augers and 22 inch cleanout bucket
Temporary casing was not used during the shaft construction The production shafts were
excavated to elevations ranging from about 118 7 to 119 4 feet MSL
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches between the
sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage

The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a
4 0 inch ID steel pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the
tremie pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete A pump truck was used during the
concrete placement The shaft concrete was supplied by Hawaiian Cement
Since seepage water was encountered during the construction of the trial shaft the
contractor ordered shaft concrete with slump from 7 to 9 inches for the production shafts
Seepage water was not encountered during the production shaft construction Although
the project specifications specifies shaft concrete with slump from 6 to 8 inches be used
for dry holes we believe the use of the higher slump concrete is satisfactory
The exposed subgrade at the bottom of the footing level was saturated and soft
from heavy rainfall We recommend removing the soft subgrade material to firm natural
ground and replacing with compacted fill CLSM or lean concrete subsequent to
completion of the drilled shaft installation
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the University
Avenue Maile Way Retaining Wall Repair project have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices The locations of the top of the shafts should be measured
using optical survey equipment to verify that the shaft position is within the tolerance in the
project specifications Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our
professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to
provide the design load capacity
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
