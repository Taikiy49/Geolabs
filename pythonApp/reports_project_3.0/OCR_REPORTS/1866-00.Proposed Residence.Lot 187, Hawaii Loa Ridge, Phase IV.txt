SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff clay
soils beneath the site Laboratory testing and
previous experience have demonstrated that this
material has high swelling potential
Special foundation design is recommended to
reduce the effects of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation the
recommended site improvement technique is to
place a minimm 3 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive material below the foundation
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation the recommended site improvement
technique is to place the footing on 3 feet of
non expansive material or to embed the footing
a minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade
The text of this report should be referred to
for detailed and special design
recommendations

performed for the proposed residence at Lot Ridge Phase
IV on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the
building foundation for the subject house The recommendations are
based on the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this
exploration
The study consisted of drilling 2 borings The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The results
of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in
the engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are presented
in the appendices of this report

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
reddish brown expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous
experience indicate that this material is highly expansive Therefore
the foundation will require special design and construction procedures
in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support
the proposed structure
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the bottom of the individual footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The replaced soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive soil for details refer to the
Schematic Section on Plate 3

Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic
Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimum
depth of three 3 feet below outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining should
be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
Slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 3 foot
capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be followed
However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet to reduce
costs if some cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be kept
moist and covered with lime prior to concrete placement
A minimm 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete

A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 8 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
from the toe of the wall to the existing sloping ground surface
The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a H V plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 36 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the house areas the on site soil should
be over excavated to provide a 36 inch minimum thickness of non expansive
select fill capping underneath the on grade slabs
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 test borings 11 and 13 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger was used to drill
the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
the Iogs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification Systen
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
