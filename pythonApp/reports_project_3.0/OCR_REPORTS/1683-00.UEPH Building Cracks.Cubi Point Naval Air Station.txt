located near the top of Tarlac Road in the upper ridge area at Cubi
Point The general project location is shOv Tn on Plate 1
Ihe three buildings 8358 8360 and 8361 are essentially identical

Description of the cracks and results of the periodic survey
readings at the building sites from January to December of 1986 are
documented in the four quarterly reports by DCCD
Ihe pertinent results of the DCCD observations and survey readings
are summarized below
1
Ihe majority of the cracks for the three buildings seem to occur
on the longitudinal walls reinforced concrete
In general
the cracks on these walls tend to decrease in number and in
measured crack widths as one goes from the ground to the fourth
floor and from the ends to the center of the buildings
Also
the majority of the cracks are diagonal in nature rising at an
angle greater than 45 degrees from the horizontal as it
progresses towards the ends of the buildings

Possible causes of Distress
Page 6
Based on the information to date several possible causes of the
observed cracking have been evaluated and are discussed below
FCX ting Settlement
Ihe possibility of fCX ting settlement due to structural and or fill
loads on the subsoils at the site was evaluated
results of our analysis are as follows
Ihe pertinent
1
Ihe boring and laboratory test data from the Dames and MCX re
reports were used to estimate the settlements due to the fill
and fCX ting loads at the site
Ihe calculated settlements were
about 3 4 to inches total in general agreement with the
estimated settlements by Dames and Moore
Generally this
amount of settlement would not be expected to cause the observed
building cracking
2
Our analysis indicates that large settlements could occur if the
subsoils below the footing excavations were saturated and
softened
However it appears that the foundations were
constructed primarily during the dry season and therefore it
does not appear likely that the soils were saturated
Additionally the survey readings indicate that the ground flCX r
slab is presently within one inch of the design finished flCX r
elevation indicating probable settlements of less than one
inch
3
Ihe cracking pattern obse rved is indicative of a building in
which the ends have settled relative to the center
However
for the subject buildirigs t 1 e
settle Uent at the ends of the
building would be expected to be much less than the center due
to higher structural loads in the center of the building

4
Comparison of the ground floor level survey with the design
finished floor elevation does not indicate a consistent
settlement trend for the three buildings al though the observed

5
The site grading cut and fills at the building sites do not

Seismic Forces
It is our understanding that during construction of the buildings a
small to moderate earthquake may have occurred in 1985 We were not
able
to obtain specific infonnation on this earthquake however
discussion with the base personnel appeared to confirm this
occurrence
Although it appears that the magnitude of earthquake experienced was
probably too small to result in the obse rved building distress
there is a possibility that the seismic forces could have
contributed to the propagation of some cracking of the buildings
prior to the earthquake occurrence
Based on OCCD Engineering Corporation s December 1987 Report there
was a reported earthquake with an estimated rnagni tude of 5 on the
Richter scale on August 29 1986

Concrete Shrinkage
Page 8
he obse rved cracking pattern and the predominance of the cracks on
the reinforced concrete walls instead of the masonry walls could
indicate that the cracks may be a result of concrete shrinkage
The longitudinal walls are relatively long 90 and could
experience significant shrinkage if they were poured in one long
section
he concrete mix utilized would also have a significant effect on
the shrinkage experienced
A review of the construction records indicated that construction
joints were provided for the slabs and footings however
verification of construction joints for the concrete walls were not
found Iherefore concrete shrinkage could have been a factor
Improper Construction
Improper construction such as substandard concrete bnproper
curing defficiencies in rebar size or position etc could result
in some building distress
An attempt was made to locate and review the constructing testing
records hOW ever they were unavailable during the time of our site

Ihe trends and magnitude of the move TTients are not sufficient to
determine the cause of the building cracking

It is possible that a combination of concrete shrinkage and

3
Very small movements have been recorded by the sw vey program
conducted by OCCD Engineering Corporation

Although some new cracks have been noticed this does not

walls caused by the existing cracks
5
The present building cracks do not appear to jeopardize the
