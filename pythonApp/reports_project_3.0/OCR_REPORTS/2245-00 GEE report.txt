SITE DESCRIPTION
The referenced lots are located in the North Kohala district on the
northwestern part of the Island of Hawaii approximately one fourth to
one and one half miles above the Akoni Pule Coastal Highway in Project 1
The Heathers of the Kohala Ranch The most prominent feature of the
area is the Kohala mountain which is one of the five volcanoes that formed
the island The topography is characterized by fairly smooth rolling
hills with poorly defined gullies and drainageways The sites are
arid type pasture land moderately covered with dry grasses and kiawe
trees The ground surface is covered with basaltic gravel and cobbles
The surface ash soils are highly desiccated friable and susceptible to
wind and water erosion

as tennis courts and swimming pools It is anticipated that the

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the The Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the
activity of five 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long
extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
still active

The Kohala mountains which make up the northern part of the island
consist largely of the Pololu Volcanic Series that are mostly olivine
basalt with intercalated beds of ash After this series erupted the
mountains were deeply eroded on its windward side and a deep soil layer
covered its other slopes Later andesite and trachyte lava flows known
as the Hawi Volcanic Series were erupted
The late activity of the younger Pleistocene age Hawi Volcanics
the rock formation at the site consists of fractured vesicular andesite
with thick beds of clinker that did not travel far from their source The
eruptions were moderately explosive and formed large cinder cones that dot
the surface of the volcano The flows developed from Aa type lavas are
mostly mugearite with some thin beds of ash or lenticular deposits of
gravel laid down by streams Andesites in Hawaii are not the same as the
common andesites of continental areas which are richer in silica and
sodium Macdonald and Abbott 1970
A thin dry desiccated and erodible layer of volcanic ash locally
referred to as Pahala Ash covers the surface of the lava flows
Soil Conditions
The test pit explorations disclosed that the sites are covered with a
thin 2 to 3 foot thick ash surface soil layer which grades into an
unconsolidated clinker deposit consisting of cobble and gravel sized
andesistic fragments mixed with silty ash soils Occasionally boulders
are scattered in the clinker layers The clinker material generally
appears to be weakly cemented
No groundwater was encountered in the test pits during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
From a geotechnical engineering point of view the sites are suitable
for the proposed development

The field explorations disclosed that the sites are underlain at shallow
depths by clinker and lava rock formations
The volcanic materials below the surface ash layer should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed houses retaining structures pavements
and related recreational facilities such as tennis courts and swimming
pools
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to lot grading surface vegetation and organic material should
be removed and wasted off site
In fairly level areas prior to receiving fill the existing ground
should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moistened and compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material and Compaction Requirements
In general the excavated rock material less than eight 8 inches
in size may be used as general fill material If excavated rock
material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded
material surficial ash soil may be mixed into the excavated rocky
material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better
graded material for compaction and increase the stability of the
fills
Because of the scarcity of soils in the area the existing surface
silts may be stockpiled and saved for later use as topsoil for the
development of the lots

Slopes
In general two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 fill slopes are
recommended
It appears that most of the cut slopes will be in varying materials
such as ash clinker and possibly dense rock An average design cut
slope of 2H 1V is recommended for cuts above the structures
If required steeper cut slopes may be made in areas of competent
hard rock However they should be evaluated by the geotechnical
engineer on an individual basis
Any cuts or fills greater than 20 feet in height should have a bench
at the mid point of the slope
Soil slopes should be protected as soon as possible after
construction to minimize the erosion potential
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
proposed structures
The bottom of all footing excavations should be compacted to 95 of
its maximm dry density prior to the placement of reinforcing steel
except when the footing will bear directly on in situ rock
An allowable bearing value up to 5 000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on recompacted or dense in situ clinker and volcanic rock
formation For footings bearing on compacted structural fill a bearing
value of 2 500 p s f may be used to design the footings The above
values are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered

A minimm footing width of 18 inches should be utilized and the
bottom of all footings should be placed a minimm of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade or six 6 inches into the dense basalt rock
formation In addition the outside edge of the footing should be
set back a minimum of ten 10 feet away from the nearest outside slope
face This set back may require deepening of the footing embedment to
maintain the above specified minimum ten foot set back measured
horizontally out to the finish slope face
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small on the
order of less than one quarter of an inch for structures bearing on rock
or on compacted structural rock fill
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 5 between the
foundations and underlying rock material is recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 400 p c f may be used
against in situ clinker volcanic rock surfaces if the footings are poured
neat onto the footing excavation All other footing over excavation
should be properly backfilled with fines and adequately compacted to
provide lateral resistance
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than three fourths of an inch and larger than one fourth of
an inch in size
Slab on grade should have perimeter footings extending down a minimm
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade or to rock formation

The subgrade soil should be compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1157 78 The slab subgrade
should be kept moist and protected from drying prior to concrete placement
in order to minimize slab cracking due to dehydration
Utilities
It is anticipated that most of the trenching will be done in the
partially consolidated clinker material however some boulders or areas
of massive basalt may be encountered especially in the deeper trenches
Trench backfill should be granular material generally less than six
6 inches in size and compacted by water and mechanical tamping to 90
of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls up to a vertical height of ten 10 feet are planned
for Lot Nos 307 and 205 Based on visual observations consolidated
clinker material or basalt rock is anticipated at retaining wall
locations For the foundations of the retaining walls the footing
excavation should penetrate through the upper ashy loose clinker and bear
on the dense consolidated clinker material or one foot into dense basalt
An allowable bearing capacity up to 5 000 p s f may be utilized for
the underlying dense clinker material and 8 000 p s f may be utilized
for dense basalt These values are for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
Clean granular materials such as the on site gravel and sand sized
clinker materials should be used as backfill directly behind the walls
This would reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill and the

Design Revi
Plans and specifications for site grading roadway retaining walls
and utility constructions should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading earthwork
and construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
