In accordance with our proposal of we have
performed a soil and foundation investigation at the above
referenced site
The purpose of the soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface conditions at the site to provide data
for use in the design of the site grading foundation and pave
ments for the proposed child care center
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the U S Naval Submarine Base at Pearl
Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is bounded to the
northeast by North Road the southeast by Edge Street and the
northwest by Bole Street To the southwest there is an exist
ing parking area and 3 storage sheds and
1242
The site is currently being used as a material storage yard by
the Naval Supply Center and is occupied by an open sided steel
framed storage shed Bldg 1246 The ground surface is
generally level and covered by asphaltic concrete pavement
The ground surface elevation is approximately 14 feet above sea
level or 114 feet based on U S Navy Datum
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building will consist of a one story child care
center approximately 40 by 60 feet in plan dimension The
proposed structure will have CMU block walls and a concrete
slab on grade
Building loads are anticipated to be very light

Soil Conditions
In general after penetrating the existing surface pavement
which consisted of 1 5 inches of asphaltic concrete over 5
inches of Portland Cement concrete the borings encountered a
layer of fi11 varying in thickness from 2 to 4 feet This fill
material is somewhat variable but generally consists of medium
dense sandy materials

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
relatively competent materials
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed building
Foundation
Shallow spread footing or continuous wall foundations may be
used for the proposed one story structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1121 00 Page 5
We recommend that all foundations should either bear entirely
on the existing upper fill layer or entirely on the underlying
volcanic tuff to minimize differential settlements In gen
eral the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade If a footing is located next
to a utility trench it should be extended to the bottom of the
utility trench or be designed to span over the trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
For foundations bearing on the existing sandy fill a bearing
value of 2500 p s f is recommended provided that the material
at the base of the footing excavation is compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 A
higher bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for foundations
bearing on dense undisturbed volcanic tuff
Estimated settlements due to the foundation loads on the upper
fill layer are anticipated to be less than one half inch total
and one quarter inch differential Footing on the dense under
lying volcanic tuff will have minimum settlements
The site should be graded to drain water away from the struc
ture and foundations

Slab On Grade
The slab on grade design will be governed by the final finished
floor elevation If the present grade level is retained the
new slab on grade may be placed directly on the existing pave
ment A polyethylene moisture barrier should be placed between
the new slab and the existing pavement
If the site is lowered or raised the slab on grade should be
placed over a 4 inch layer of capillary break material such as
3B Fine aggregate to prevent infiltration of moisture into
the floor slab by capillary action
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed
to dry out prior to concrete placement
Site Grading
Relatively minor site grading is anticipated for the proposed
child care center
If the grade is to be lowered we recommend that after the
Site has been cut to finish subgrade all new building and
pavement areas be compacted to obtain at least 90 of the
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 prior to any foundation
excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1121 00 Page 7
If the future site grade is to be raised we recommend that the
existing pavements be broken up The small fragments less
than 6 inches in size can be reused in the fill All building
and pavement areas should be compacted to 90 maximum dry den
Sity prior to the placement of any new fill All new fills
should be compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557
If additional off site borrow is required it is recommended
that it be tested and approved by a soils engineer prior to
transportation to the site to assure that it is suitable for
the proposed construction The borrow or imported material
should generally be non expansive and not contain large per
centages of boulders organics or other deleterious matter
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for the new parking area
If the site is to remain at its existing grade resurfacing of
the existing asphaltic concrete pavement should be sufficient
If the grade is to be lowered based on a laboratory CBR test
performed on the existing fill material the preliminary pave
ment design for the parking area is as follow

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings to depths of 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was
used to drilled all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer or
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
