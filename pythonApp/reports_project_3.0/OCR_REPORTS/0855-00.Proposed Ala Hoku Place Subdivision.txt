This report presents the results of soil engineering investi
gation at the above referenced development The work was
performed according to proposal number 8670 79 dated April
19 1979
The site was originally vacant and undeveloped Stockpiles
of fill and boulders were recently observed on site during
construction of utilities at Lots 1 2 and 3

PURPOSE SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to determine general
subsurface conditions We were also to provide recommen

grading and general site development

GEOLOGY
The ancestral Moanalua Valley was formed by erosion of
the flank of the Koolau Mountains which are composed of
numerous basalt flows generally dipping makai Weathered
basalt forms steep outcrops in the cliff west of the lots
After prolonged submergence the Koolau volcano resumed
eruptions through smaller cones and vents which deposited
pyroclastics cinders ash and tuff along the low lying
Moanalua Valley Renewed uplift and erosion have removed
parts of these pyroclastic deposits from the portion of
Moanalua Valley beneath Tripler Hospital the upper tuff
beneath the golf course adjacent to the site however has
been weathered and oxidized in place

CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
The most feasible development scheme from a geotechnical
standpoint would be to place one story residences on the
golf course side of Lots 1 and 2 This may also be the
preferred locations based upon architectural considerations
A shallow concrete mat or raft type foundation should be
used on the existing grade to provide uniform support and
to minimize differential settlements The floor slab will
be top of the concrete mat
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 855 00 P 6
Due to the large boulders contained in the existing fill
development costs for heavier 1 and 2 story residences
are expected to be greater Due to expected settlements
large 1 and 2 story structures cannot be supported on a
shallow mat Continuous footings excavated through the
fill to natural ground will be very difficult note the
refusal of Test Pit 2 The lower level will necessarily
consist of a basement extending through fill and supported
on underlying weathered tuff Excavations in fill will be
slow and difficult the on site fill materials cannot be
reused due to the large boulders High active pressures
will occur on walls below grade If you desire further
consideration on 1 and 2 story residences in spite of
these potential difficulties we would be pleased to
provide additional design recommendations
Due to heterogeneous or non uniform condition of the fill
and the sloping basalt as it is it does not appear that
there is a buildable residential site on Lot 3 Develop
ment would probably be confined to an uneven and relatively
narrow strip roughly from 30 to 40 feet wide of hillside
Either the loose fill materials should be entirely removed
and transported from the site or extensive basalt excava
tion into the hillside would be required in order to provide
a suitable site for a residence on Lot 3

Foundations
Due to the weathered basalt in the ledge beneath the
cliff see Test Pit 4 the mat or raft foundations on
Lots 1 and 2 should be set back at least 25 to 30 feet
from the toe of the cliff to minimize large differential
settlement between rock and loose fill The residences
should also be set back at least ten feet from the top
of existing or future fill slope It is estimated that
a post tensioned slab or an 1 5 feet thick reinforced
concrete mat will be suitable A gross allowable bearing
value of 600 pounds per square foot may be used for design
of raft type foundations underlain by existing in situ
fill or by compacted fill Since the existing fill soils
contain some large boulders and pieces of concrete it is
our opinion that some settlements can be anticipated
roughly from 0 5 to 1 5 inches Estimated differential
settlements are approximately 0 5 inch
Grading
The existing ground surface should be scarified and compacted
any additional fill should be compacted in horizontal layers
benched into the existing fill slope A key or butress should
be excavated at least two feet into red brown natural materials
if additional fill materials are placed adjacent to the golf
course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 855 00 P 8
After compacting the ground surface by at least eight
passes of a roller any required additional fill soils
should be placed in maximum nine inch thick lifts and
compacted to 90 percent relative compaction using ASTM
Designation D 1557 70 Modified Proctor as the standard
Due to many cobbles and boulders the on site materials
are not recommended for use in compacted fill
All new lifts must be placed horizontally
Shallow excavations less than 3 feet deep for benching
during filling and for any underground utilities may be
excavated vertically Permanent fill slopes more than
five feet high should be sloped at 2 horizontal to 1 ver
tical These recommendations are illustrated on the
enclosed Plate 2
Slope Stability
According to our reconnaissance of the basalt cliff the
hillside although weathered is nonetheless relatively
stable The average slope angle above the cliff is only
24 degree or 45 percent The conclusion of stable slopes
is further enhanced by the presence of weathered tuff
encountered in Test Pit 1 at the base of the hillside
adjacent to the golf course
