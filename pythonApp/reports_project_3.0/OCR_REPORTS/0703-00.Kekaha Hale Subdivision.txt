PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed Kekaha Hale Subdivision will consist of 49 single
family lots over the 7 2 acre site with associated access road
ways and driveways
The structures will probably be one story wood framed single
family residences with slab on grade floors The building
loads will be light and typical of this type of structure
Grading plans are not yet available at the writing of this
report Based on the existing topography removing the
existing topographic highs and filling the low areas to
or above the street level are anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site of Kekaha Hale is located on the Leeward
side of the island of Kauai about 3 miles from Waimea
Kauai The area is near the coastline off Highway 50
The project site is bounded on the north mauka by Iwipo
lena Road south makai by Ulili Street east by Akialoa
Street and west by Kawailoa Street
The total area of the site is approximately 7 2 acres con
sisting of undeveloped land with hummocky features at various
locations Access into the site area is primarily through
three existing dirt roads from Akialoa Street on the east
side Two of these roads run through the central portion
of the site on relatively level firm sandy ground The
third road loosely compacted with black cinders runs
through the makai higher portion of the site
As shown and presented on Plate 1 site plan the noticeable
land features are several topographic highs throughout the
site One topographic high is on the of the site
about 9 to 14 feet above the surrounding street grades and
consist mainly of debris broken bottles sugar mill wash
and silty sandy material There are two other topographic
highs or mounds which were not as high one located on the
western half of the central portion of the site about 2 to
3 feet above street elevation and one on the mauka portion
of the site mainly consisting of mill wash and lava rock

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Kekaha Hale site is underlain by a calcareous sand
formation consisting of loose to partly cemented fine
sands originating from coral and seashells This type
of calcareous formation usually occurs as strips along
the coastal plains adjacent to the ocean and is associated
with old beaches and windblown sand dune deposits probably
developed during the Waipio stand of the sea minus 40
feet
The general areas where such formations are found on Kauai
are in places on the Mana plain and along the southeast
coast between Kipu Kai and Makahuena Point
The U S Soil Conservation Service s Soil Survey of Islands
of Molokai and Lanai State of Hawaii
August 1972 describes the project site soil as follows
JfB Jaucas Loamy fine sand 0 to 8 percent slopes
Unified Soil Classification SP shvink swell potential

PERCOLATION TESTS
Several percolation tests had been performed previously
adjacent to and in the immediate vicinity of the site
High percolation rates had been obtained in the natural
sand soils
To further verify the percolation rate of the on site
soils two additional percolation tests were performed
to supplement the previously obtained data
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 8
Percolation tests for this investigation were performed
in Test Pits 6 and 10 as shown on Plate 1 site plan
Previous test pits and borings locations performed within
the site are shown on Plate 2
The percolation tests were performed by excavating the
test pits down to a depth which indicated the natural
sandy soil would be represented For Test Pit 10 the
depth was about 4 feet and for Test Pit 6 the depth
was about 7 feet The pits were then cleaned from the
loose sand and shaped to a rectangular configuration
Water was then placed in the test pits from a water
truck and measurements of dissipation taken
The results of the percolation tests indicate high per
colation rates of 0 76 gallon per square foot per minute
for Test Pit 6 and 0 25 gallon per square foot per minute
for Test Pit 10
Previous percolation test results indicate a percolation
rate of 0 78 gallon per square foot per minute
For design purposes a conservative percolation rate of
0 25 gallon per square foot per minute may be used for
cesspool design

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The proposed housing development is feasible from a soils and
foundation engineering standpoint Conventional spread footings
established either in the properly compacted engineered fill or
natural sandy soil may be used
Due to the large amount of debris and trash within the topogra
phic high areas or mounds special process in removing the
debris will be required before they can be used for engineered
fill material This process if done by hand may result in
higher cost than removing all debris fill material off site
Final grading of the site should be based on economic consi
derations of debris fill material
Site Grading
The on site silty sand and sandy silt materials are non expansive
and therefore they can be used as ftll material for the lowlying
areas provided they are free of debris organics and vegetation
It is anticipated the entire site will be brought up to or
slightly above the existing road grades Fill ranging from
two to three feet will be required in the lowlying areas and
cuts of about 5 feet will be required in some high areas
However the high areas within the site are composed of mill
wash with large amount of debris and will not be adequate for
fill material If the debris such as glass bottle and other
deletrious material are removed they may be used in the fill
areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 10
The cost of removal of the debris within the dump fill
will be largely influenced by the speed and method of
removal We estimated that about 50 of the material
will be useable after debris removal
Prior to the start of construction it is necessary to
clear and grub the area such that all vegetation Kiawe
trees and dump debris are removed from the site Consi
derable amount of soil may be lost in the project area
due to the removal of the heavy tree growth
After clearing and grubbing and removal of any debris the
upper 12 inches of the exposed natural ground surface should
be scarified watered to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of laboratory maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D1557 prior to placing any
fill
Fill shall then be placed in horizontal 9 inch maximum loose
lifts moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density
Fill to be placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched to pro
vide an adequate bond between the existing ground and new
material

Slopes
Cut and fill slopes can be designed for slope ratios up to
2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 slope To prevent future
wind and drainage erosion slopes should be planted immedi
ately after construction
Foundation
The light single family residences may be supported on con
ventional spread footing foundations Allowable bearing
pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot may be used for
the footing bearing on the on site sand or compacted fill
Perimeter footings should be established at a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent final grade
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be placed on the compacted non expansive
site sandy soils A plastic membrane should be placed be
neath the slab area to prevent moisture transmission if
dampness is undesirable The slab perimeter footing should
extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade for erosion protection purpose
Roadway Pavement

were brought into our laboratory and appropriate test con
ducted to formulate pavement section design

Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of trash debris organic matter and all other dele
terious materials These materials shall be removed
and disposed offsite
The on site topographic highs or mounds consist of
useable soil if debris can be effectively and econo
mically removed The soil after debris removal
should be inspected and approved by the soils engineer
prior to its use in compacted fill areas
Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose com
petent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall
be undercut and backfilled with compacted approved
materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
_ sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
