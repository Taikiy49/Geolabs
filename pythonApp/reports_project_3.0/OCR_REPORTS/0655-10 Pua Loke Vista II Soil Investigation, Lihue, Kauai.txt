INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site and to provide recommendations
for the site grading and foundations for the proposed sub
division
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located outside of Lihue at the end
of Puanani Aheahe and Aukoi Streets in the Pua Loke District
of Kauai
Except for the southeastern corner of the site the ground
surface generally slopes down toward the east at gradients
of about 5 to 30 percent A shallow stream flows through
this area generally running from northwest to east Generally
less than 6 inches of water was noted in the stream at the
time of our field exploration The width of the stream varies
from about 3 to 20 feet The ground elevations generally
varied from about 180 to 120 feet Dense vegetation was
noted near the stream area
The southeastern portion of the site is located at the end of
a knoll and is higher than the rest of the site The upper
ground surface slopes down toward the north and east at gra
dients of about 25 to 30 percent The ground elevations
generally vary from 175 to 105 feet Dense vegetation was
noted at the lower portion of this sloping area while exposed
decomposed rock was noted in the upper areas
Some previous cut slopes were noted along the southern portion
of the site A near vertical 30 to 40 feet high cut bank was
noted along the southeastern corner of the site

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed development plan calls for the creation of 86
residential lots and a 56 feet wide roadway through the
area
In general the grading for the site development will
consist of cutting the higher northern and southeastern
portions of the site and filling the lowlying central por
tion Cuts up to 26 feet in the southeastern portion of
the area and fills up to 26 feet over the existing stream
are planned
The existing stream will be filled and the water diverted
into a new drain line through the project site
A retaining wall about 150 feet long and up to 10 feet high
is planned below a high cut slope in the southeastern section
Relatively light one and two story wood framed residential
houses are anticipated

Soil Conditions
The field exploration indicates that the higher portions of
the site is underlain by stiff residual soil with weathered
boulders
Some of the lowlying areas near the stream appear to be
underlain by a surface layer of alluvium or fill about 5
feet thick over soft silty clay The soft layer encountered
was about 5 feet thick and is underlain by stiff clayey silt
with decomposed rock
Hand probings in the streambed indicated about 5 feet of
very soft material
Water was encountered in the borings at 6 to 10 foot depths
in the low stream area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan indicates that the existing
stream and surrounding low area will be filled The stream
water will be diverted into a new drain line through the site
PUA LOKE VISTA II Pa 5
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Soft material will be encountered in the streambed area
Where the proposed roads cross over the existing stream
the soft material should be removed down to stiff soil
In the other areas the soft material in the streambed
should be removed down to a maximum depth of 5 feet
After filling settlement gages should be placed along
the stream alignment to monitor the fills
To reduce underground erosion and piping a subdrainage
System consisting of perforated pipe and granular filter
material around the pipe should be provided at the stream
bed location prior to filling The subdrainage system
should be daylighted
Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to the above specified density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing
firm ground
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for
cut and fill slopes Surface water should be diverted away
from slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
PUA LOKE VISTA II P 6
The existing vertical cut bank located at the southern
boundary if left unprotected will present a potential
slide hazard to the upper and lower residential construc
tion Therefore we recommend that immediate correction
measures be made to minimize this unstable condition
The remedial measures include reducing the height of the
cut bank by a stabilization fill against the existing
toe of slope or raising the site grading in front of the
bank area A shorter retaining wall could then be con
structed for the balance of the exposed cut bank
Based on our previous experience in this area cut slope
ratio of about 1 horizontal to 1 vertical may be used for
preliminary design purpose Fill slope should be constructed
at a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical slope
Foundations
Laboratory tests indicate that the on site soil may become
moderately expansive when remolded and compacted Also
some soft material is anticipated in the stream area For
these reasons it is recommended that spread or post footing
foundations be used in the fill areas
Post footings should be placed 18 inches below the finished
grade in fill areas and 12 inches below the finished grade
in cut areas
PUA LOKE VISTA II Pad
Post footings located on sloping ground or close to the
tops of slopes should have a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 5 feet from the slope face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing
pressure of 3000 p s f can be used for footings resting
on the stiff natural soil or compacted fill
House construction for the lots located over the filled
existing stream should be delayed until the settlement
gages indicate negligible or Golenable settlement rates
It is estimated that about 90 of the settlements will
occur within 6 months after filling however the settle
ment gage readings should be used to determine when house
construction may proceed
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench Surface water
should be diverted away from building foundations
Slab on grade may be used in the cut areas The slabs should
be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum distance of 5
feet
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
PUA LOKE VISTA II P 8
Retaining Wall
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the wall
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used for
wall footings resting on the stiff natural soil
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls with horizontal backslope 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Based on the results of the laboratory CBR test the preliminary
pavement design is as follows

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified

Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of Seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
ae
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
