INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site and to provide recommenda
tions for the design and construction of the foundations of
the proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed building is located mauka
north of Citron Street east of Paani Street It is
bordered by Citron Street on the south and residential
structures on the other sides Access to the site is by
Citron Street
The site is presently occupied by 1 and 2 story wood
framed residences and a 2 story CMU apartment building The
site is generally flat and about 1 to 2 feet lower than Citron
Street
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed highrise building will be a 24 story rein
forced concrete structure with a basement The tower struc
ture will be approximately 65 by 65 feet in plan and will be
located near the central portion of the site A 2 story park
ing structure will be located adjacent to the tower on the
northern and western portions of the site The lower parking
level will be below grade and extended up to or near the
northern and western property lines A topo map and the
proposed finish basement level elevations were not available
at the time of our investigation
Maximum column loads up to about 2000 kips are antici
pated for the tower and 60 kips for the parking structure

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five test borings were drilled at the site to depths of
20 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface to explore
the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for labora
tory testing Continuous penetration tests utilizing a
2 inch diameter blunt point were performed at the bottom
of Boring The test borings were concentrated
around the tower structure location due to the much larger
stresses anticipated there and the inaccessibility of the
perimeter areas of the site The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration methods and
laboratory testing is covered in the appendices along with
the boring logs and laboratory test results
Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain
which consists of a thick series of interbedded marine
and nonmarine sediments capping the Koolau Basalt The
near subsurface structure at the site consists of a broad
elevated coral reef about 30 feet thick partly covered
by nonmarine alluvium and directly overlying another
extensive nonmarine alluvium These conditions are con
sidered to be the result of a complex series of Pleistocene
GEOLABS HAWAII
sea level changes The present reef platform which stands
approximately 5 feet above sea level is apparently a wave
cut terrace formed by wave erosion of an older reef which
originally stood over 25 feet above sea level
The widespread reef structure is known locally as the
Waimanalo Reef or the plus 25 foot reef The Waimanalo
Reef directly overlies a basically nonmarine deposits of
sediment which formed during a period of time when the
sea level was 40 feet lower than present
Although commonly referred to as coral reefs Hawaii reef
structure are predominately calcareous algae skeletons
which support a small percentage of coral growth The
reef environment is varied and complex but consists
basically of zones of growing coral and algae deposits
of coarse coral debris on the forereef and deposits of
sand silts and clays on the backreef or lagoon With
changes of sea level these basically calcareous deposits
usually become cemented or even recrystallized by the
action of percolating fresh water Most of the coral
reef as typically seen along Hawaii shores consists of
beachrock cemented sand or beds of cemented sand and
coral algal gravel known as reefrock All reefs also
have channels of deeper water leading through them
Normally these channels are opposite the mouths of rivers
and are typically filled with nonmarine sediments

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a surface layer about 1 5 to 5 feet thick of brown clayey
silt with boulders alluvium or fill over a coral reef
formation to about 36 feet The coral reef formation con
sisted mostly of very dense coral algal reefrock near the
ground surface grading to dense to medium dense cemented
sand at greater depths
Hard soil was encountered at 36 5 feet in the probing
below Boring No 3 Previous experience in the area
indicated that this is probably an overconsolidated
alluvial deposit
Water was encountered in the borings at 4 to 5 foot depths
during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a coral reef formation at shallow depths The coral
formation is very dense near the surface with pockets of
medium dense to loose sand at greater depths
Shallow foundations bearing on the dense coral are recom
mended for the structure Due to the natural variability
of coral reef formations we recommend a mat foundation
for the tower structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
P 6
The mat will help to reduce the effects of medium or loose
pockets and other natural irregularities in the coral for
mation Spread footing foundations bearing on the dense
coral are recommended for the parking structure
Additional shallow borings are recommended around the peri
meter of the parking area to verify the presence of the
dense coral formation and to locate possible cavities
The borings should be grouted from the bottom of the drill
holes Much of the area is inaccessible due to existing
residences therefore the additional borings should be
made after the site is cleared
The foundations should be placed a minimum of 2 feet into
the dense coral formation An allowable bearing value of
5000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on the
dense coral reef formation
The bearing value may be increased by one third when con
sidering temporary loads such as wind or seismic loads
Estimated settlements for the foundation are on the order
of about one half inch total and one fourth inch differ
ential Due to the large difference in loads between the
tower and parking structures some provision for differ
ential movement should be incorporated in the design

P 7
The footing excavations should be performed in the dry to
reduce the disturbance and subsequent softening of the
foundation subgrade
The footing excavations should be observed by the soil
engineer to see that adequate bearing material is encoun
tered
Excavation
Excavations up to about 5 feet will be required for the
proposed basement level and up to about 10 feet for the
elevator pit
Some relatively hard ripping may be required in the dense
coral formation The upper soil layers should be easy
to excavate
Due to the proximity of the surrounding existing structures
sheeting and bracing is recommended for the excavation in
the upper soil layer to reduce settlement and damage to
the adjacent structures
Where there is sufficient distance 1 horizontal to 1 ver
tical side slopes can be utilized for the excavation slopes
in lieu of sheeting and bracing Heavy equipment and stock
piles should be kept away from the tops of the excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at about 4 to
5 foot depths
Some dewatering will be required for the foundation exca
vations and elevator pit The quantity of water pumped
through coral formations varies considerably and depends
on the characteristics of the coral structure
For shallow excavations perimeter drains can be used to
lower the water level For deeper excavations wellpoint
or deep wells are recommended
The dewatering operations should be performed without
pumping out the soil fines creating a quick condition
or softening the bottom of excavation
Site Grading
A few feet of fill is planned to raise the site
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris
topsoil and old foundations prior to filling
Select granular material such as the on site coral should
be used for fill and footing backfill material The fill
and backfill should be placed in 8 inch maximum horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course generally less than 3 4 inch and greater than 1 4
inch in size The subgrade should be kept moist prior to
pouring the concrete
Basement and Retaining Walls
Basement and retaining walls up to 8 feet high are planned
for the proposed structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
or on site sand should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended
for the wall design
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 5 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
The basément walls and slabs should be waterproofed

Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located on the site Where exist
ing cesspools are located below or adjacent to footings and
slabs the sludge should be removed and the cesspool filled
with compacted granular material or concrete The cesspool
walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should be removed
Design Review
The pians for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site filling footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by a soil engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site

FIELD EXPLORATION

by drilling 5 borings to depths of 20 to 25 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Continuous penetration

engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
