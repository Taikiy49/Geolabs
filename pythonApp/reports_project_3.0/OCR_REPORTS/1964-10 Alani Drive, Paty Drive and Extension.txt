Progress Report No 4
Earth Movements in the Vicinity of
Alani Drive Paty Drive and Paty Extension
Manoa Valley Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In general accordance with our scope of work dated we
have performed additional field exploration and installed additional
instrumentation in the vicinity of the intersection of Alani Drive Paty
Drive and Paty Extension in Manoa Valley to obtain additional data for
evaluation of the various remedial work recommendations previously
presented in our Progress Report No 2 dated March 8 1988 Presented
below is a summary of our latest work our current findings and
recommendations for remedial work pertaining to the subject earth
movements
Exploration Area
The exploration areas considered in this progress report include the
right of way at the subject intersection and four 4 house lots from 3093
to 3114 Paty Drive It is believed that the earth movements in these
areas are interrelated and should be corrected or mitigated with the same
remedial scheme s
The approximate locations of the subject study areas are shown on the
attached Site Plan Plate 1

Field Exploration and Instrumentation
The additional field exploration and instrumentation program included
the drilling and sampling of five 5 borings and the installation of
three 3 piezometers in selected borings
Four 4 borings were drilled in the three upper house lots along Paty
Drive in order to obtain additional data for delineation of the subsoil
profiles across the lots The fifth boring was drilled in Paty Drive in
order to further delineate the portion of the road area which has
experienced significant distress Subsurface data from previous borings
in the area were also used
The piezometers were installed in three 3 of the borings in the
upper house lots to attempt to detect hillside water seepage s
The approximate locations of the drilled borings and the installed
piezometers are shown on the attached Site Plan Also included are the
detailed Boring Logs for the five 5 recent borings Plates A 1 1 through
A 5 1 and a summary of the piezometer readings Plate 2
Subsoil Conditions
The borings indicate that the three upper house lots along Paty Drive
generally have somewhat more favorable subsoil conditions than in the road
area
Generally the soft and wet upper layer of adobe clay encountered in
the borings was found to be thinner in the house lots than in the
roadway At 3093 Paty Drive dense colluvium was encountered at shallow
depths about 5 feet
These subsoil conditions were correlated with the previous boring data
along Alani and Paty Drives and Paty Extensions and our interpretations
of the data are presented on the attached Generalized Subsurface Cross
Sections A A B B and C C Plates 3 through 5

As noted in our previous report the upper 10 feet of soils
encountered in the distressed areas on the roads appeared to be
significantly wetter and softer than the soils in the stable areas In
particular there seems to be an existing thin zone of soft soils over a
stiff to hard layer of alluvium below Paty and Alani Drives as shown on
cross sections B B and C C Based on observed earth movements toes and
scarps in the lot this soft zone also appears to extend below the house
lot at 3114 Paty Drive
Piezometer Readings
It was suspected that groundwater seepage and or utility leakage have
played an important role in the pavement distresses or earth movements in
these areas Therefore the groundwater levels in the borings were
monitored by piezometers installed in Boring and 204 in the
upper house lots along Paty Drive for comparison or correlation with the
observed groundwater levels in other piezometers installed previously in
the lower areas on Alani and Paty Drives
The observed groundwater levels in the three piezometers in the upper
house lots were generally stable and the depths ranged from about 16 to 24
feet below the existing ground surface which correspond to elevations 334
to 341 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
The observed groundwater level in the piezometer in Paty Drive Boring
No 106 fluctuated from 7 to 14 6 feet below the pavement level which
correspond to about elevations 339 to 332 MSL
On the other hand the observed groundwater level in the lower
piezometer in Alani Drive in Boring No 109A where no pavement distress
or ground movement was observed was consistently lower at an elevation of
about 306 feet MSL

One possible explanation for the measured groundwater levels may be
that groundwater accumulates below this distressed area and flows downhill
in a generally western direction following the trend of the heavily
damaged areas
Mechanism of Earth Movements
As discussed in our previous report we believe that the earth
movements in Paty and Alani Drives may have been initiated by gradual
downhill creep of the adobe clay soils due to hillside groundwater
seepages such as those intercepted in the recently installed
piezometers in the upper house lots along Paty Drive This initially
small creep movement eventually triggered utility line leakage along
the roads as evidenced by the unusually high groundwater levels
recorded in some of the lower piezometers and observations by the
residents
As water found under gravity random passages downslope probably
through the more permeable granular or bouldery soils often present in
the adobe matrix the alluvial clays in the vicinity of the water
paths became further saturated and weakened as shown on the
generalized cross sections B B and C C
In some localized areas the saturated and weakened subsoils could
have been eroded due to the passage of water and or washed into broken
utility lines
The general groundwater levels in this area were found to be about 10
to 20 feet below the ground surface However the groundwater levels
could rise substantially during heavy storms and possible additional
contributions from utility line breaks These added hydraulic forces
could have initiated the dramatic downslope movements along Paty Drive
and across 3114 Paty Drive and down to Alani Drive The subsoil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1964 10 Page 5
movements below the roads were manifested on the surface in the form
of pavement distresses such as the cracks and settlements observed
previously The subsoil movements below the lot at 3114 Paty Drive
were manifested on the ground surface as small toes and scarps of
landslips also as shown on cross sections B B and C C
It appears that due to these downslope movements in the lower road
and house areas the slopes and structures in the upper house lots
above Paty Drive which are generally underlain by stiffer soils
became undermined and then slipped
It is believed that earth movements directly below the houses at 3093
and 3103 Paty Drive were mainly caused by surface erosion of hillside
runoff such as during the 1987 1988 New Year s storm Based on our
site reconnaissance the area of surface erosion between and directly
below the two houses appears to be in the course of a runoff channel
In the distressed pavement areas on the road embankment along Paty
Extension a loose and wet upper fill layer was encountered over very
stiff alluvium in a previous boring Boring No 120 However in the
recent boring Boring No 205 drilled near the damaged garage at 3093
Paty Drive the surface fill layer was found to be thinner and medium
stiff Stiffer subsoil conditions were also encountered in another
boring Boring No 204 recently drilled further upslope in the lot
The groundwater level initially measured in Boring No 204 appears to
be the residue from foam drilling operations during field exploration
instead of natural groundwater
These updated data on the subsoil and groundwater conditions appear to
verify that the movements of the road embankments along Paty Extension
were confined in the upper portions of the subsurface depths and that

the movements may have been caused by water seepage and or utility
leakage from Paty and Alani Drives but not from the adjacent
hillside As discussed in our previous report it was reported that

In turn the road movements at Paty Extension may have undermined the
embankment at 3093 Paty Drive causing the severe ground settlement and
damages to the garage The earth movement below the corner of the
house appears to have been caused by surface erosion from hillside
runoff as discussed above and may be unrelated to the garage movement
Remedial Work
Alani and Paty Drives
In our previous Progress Report No 2 an option to completely remove
the alluvial adobe clay soils within the damaged portions of the
subject roads in conjunction with a basic remedial scheme of
installing trench drains on both sides of the roads was proposed
This option was recognized to be effective but also considered an
expensive way to stabilize the road areas In addition construction
difficulties such as the maintenance of through traffic and the risk
of further undermining the adjacent damaged house lots were concerns
However based on the updated subsoil and groundwater information we
now believe that this scheme may be effective and economically
feasible for stabilizing the road areas by reducing the removal and
backfill depth of the clay subsoils Although both the previous and
the recent boring data indicate that clay soil thickness varies from
10 to 30 feet or more only the upper 10 to 15 feet appears to be soft
and wet Therefore it appears that clay removal and replacement may
be limited to about 10 to 12 feet in most areas and up to about 15
feet at the junction of Alani and Paty Drives
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1964 10 Page 7
The excavation should be backfilled with compacted structural fill
materials such as select borrow aggregate which will effectively
form a stable buttress along Paty and Alani Drives to replace the
distressed road areas facilitate new utility installation and retain
the damaged upslope house lots The granular nature of the backfill
will allow future hillside seepage and utility leakage if any to be
collected by a subdrain system installed along the bottom of the
excavation Therefore the originally proposed upslope trench drain
may be eliminated The general scheme is schematically illustrated on
Plate 6
To maintain through traffic and reduce the risk of undermining
adjacent house lots we recommend that the removal and backfill
operations be performed in two stages across the road section as shown
on Plate 7 The excavations may be retained by using either
cantilever or braced sheetpile walls Along the upslope side of the
braced excavation the braced sheetpile wall should be additionally
supported with heavy section steel H piles to minimize adjacent ground
movement in the already damaged upper house lots Based on the
available subsoil data we recommend that the lateral earth pressure
diagrams presented on Plate 8 be used for sizing the cantilever or
braced sheetpile wall system
New retaining structures are anticipated along the upper lot boundary
at 3114 Paty Drive and along the property boundaries of the downslope
house lots along Alani Drive including 3112 and 3120 Alani Drive to
replace existing retaining walls prior to the proposed removal and
backfill operations
Paty Extension
The updated boring and groundwater data appear to verify that the
primary cause of the movements was the saturation of the upper 8 feet
of the road embankment As a result the previously proposed basic
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1964 10 Page 8
remedial scheme in Progress Report No 2 of removing and replacing
the upper 10 to 15 feet of fill and adobe clay in the area may still
be considered a feasible solution This basic scheme is illustrated
schematically on Plate 9 Step 1
Since the boring data indicate that the distressed garage and
embankment in 3093 Paty Drive are probably underlain by only thin
layers of potentially unstable materials over more competent subsoil
conglomerate we believe that the originally proposed removal and
backfill scheme supplemented with a crib wall may be used to repair
the road embankment Plate 9 Step 2
Optionally since the existing narrow road extension is the only
access to Hale Manoa it is anticipated that maintaining through
traffic flow on the road and also the safety of the already undermined
garage at 3093 Paty Drive will impose critical construction
difficulties during the installation of a buttress fill within the
road right of way
To mitigate these difficulties it had previously been proposed to
acquire an easement through 3102 Paty Drive for construction of a
buttress outside of the present right of way This would allow
vehicular access during construction and reduce the construction
hazard to 3093 Paty Drive This optional scheme is reiterated and
also illustrated schematically on Plate 10
Additional Studies
The piezometers should be monitored on a long term basis to evaluate
the effectiveness of the remedial work and to aid in the planning of
additional remedial work should it be necessary
Additional borings may be required in selected locations to provide
data for final design of the initial remedial work

LIMITATIONS
The general site is underlain by adobe clay soils which are subject
to long term creep movements The recommendations presented in this
report should help to reduce drastic movements however creep movements
may continue
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based in
part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings and
piezometers Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by measuring
from the attached Site Plan Elevations of the data points were
determined by referring to the Topographic Survey Map prepared by
Controlpoint Surveying and Engineering Inc dated August 4 1988 The
physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field
data depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or
cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data points and
may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations the summary of piezometer
readings and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuations may occur due to variations in rainfall
tides temperature utility leakage s and other factors
