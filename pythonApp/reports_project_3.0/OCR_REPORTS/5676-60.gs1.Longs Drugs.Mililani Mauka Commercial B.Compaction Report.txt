PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the Mililani Mauka Commercial B development area in
the Mililani District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by
Meheula Parkway Ainamakua Drive and Ukuwai Street The project location is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves the construction of a single story
22 140 square foot concrete building with a loading dock The layout of the project is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the foundation preparation and utility construction at the project site
FOUNDATION PREPARATION AND UTILITY CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork construction at the Longs Drugs Mililani Mauka Commercial B
project consisted of foundation preparation and utility construction The footing
construction and utility backfilling work was performed by Jayar Construction and
Paradigm Construction LLC During the site grading operations samples of the on site
and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported
to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were

The compaction test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the parking areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density In addition the select borrow and base course materials
below the pavement areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the footing preparation and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 9 of
this report The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes The locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
