Project Description
The proposed project is a design build project which consists of the construction
of a new bike and pedestrian path along the shoreline from Lihi Park to Ahihi Point A
10 to 12 foot wide by 6 inch thick concrete path is specified for the new path In addition
an equestrian path is also planned along the bike and pedestrian path from the north end
of Kealia Beach Park to Ahihi Point The project will also include bridge improvements at
Waikaea and Moikeha Stream Bridges and Kapaa Stream Bridge a new bridge structure
at Kumukumu Stream crossing new comfort stations new pavements at Kealia Lookout
Parking Area and site grading The overall project location is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
A new bridge superstructure will be constructed over the existing bridge structure
for the bridge improvements at Waikaea Stream Bridge Portions of the existing bridge
and one pier structure were demolished to provide a wider opening for boat passage The
existing bridge structure will be re constructed as a three span bridge superstructure about
108 feet in length with spans of 28 40 and 40 feet The new bridge superstructure will be
of concrete construction and includes the extension of two existing pier bents the
re construction of an existing abutment structure and construction of a new abutment
structure Based on the findings and recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Waikaea Stream
Bridge Kapaa Kealia Bike Pedestrian Path dated

The existing bridge structure at Moikeha Stream will be re constructed to

Test Pile Program
The test pile program included a Pile Driving Analyzer PDA during driving of
selected production piles Pile Nos W 5 M 3 and K 3 for the three new bridge
structures In addition a sacrificial pile was installed and statically load tested at the
Waikaea Bridge site A PDA was used during the installation of the sacrificial pile The
test pile locations are shown on the Pile and Drilled Shaft Identification Plans
Plates 2 through 4 The purpose of the test pile program was to establish the driveability
of the concrete piles given the subsurface conditions at the project site the selected
driving equipment and methods of concrete pile installation In addition the test pile
program also provided information on the driving resistance and contours of the
load bearing strata across the project site
precast pre stressed concrete piles The Delmag D 30 32 pile hammer has a maximum
rated energy of approximately 69 900 foot pounds per blow of the hammer
A total of three test piles with pile lengths from 115 to 160 3 feet were driven to tip
elevations ranging from about 95 to 147 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A summary of
the test pile driving is provided on Plate 5 of this report for reference
performed the dynamic pile monitoring during driving on the test pile The PDA results are
presented on Plates 6 1 through 6 4
For Pile No W 5 pile capacity based on the PDA at the design tip elevation was
below the required pile capacity Therefore the pile was driven about 7 feet beyond the
design tip elevation and was re struck to evaluate the strength gain after 4 days The
GRLWEAP program and the PDA results were used to verify the pile capacity The
GRLWEAP results are presented on Plate 7

Static Pile Load Test
A compressive pile load test was conducted on a sacrificial test pile designated as
TP 1 on The test pile was loaded to a test load of 270 kips Unfactored
Single Pile Capacity in 16 9 kip load increments in general accordance with the Quick
Loading Procedure of ASTM Test Designation D 1143 Detailed discussions and results
of the static pile load test are provided in the report entitled Pile Load Testing Report

Based on our field observations and the results of the test pile program the driving
equipment and methods of installation were accepted for installing the specified production
piles It is our professional opinion that the test piles were driven to sufficient depths
and or resistance to provide the design load capacities
Based on the results of the test pile program the following production pile driving
criteria was used for the project The production piles for the project were driven with the
same hammer used for the test piles The pile driving operation was terminated when the
following driving criteria was satisfied
1 Pile Capacity of 80 to 120 kips When the aggregate blow counts are at
least 20 blows per foot for the last foot or 4 blows per inch for the last
3 inches
2 Pile Capacity of 210 to 225 kips When the aggregate blow counts are at
least 50 blows per foot for the last foot or 6 blows per inch for the last
3 inches
Production Pile Driving
The production pile driving program consisting of a total of 19 precast pre stressed
concrete piles 16 5 inch octagonal was conducted from January 26 through
March 13 2006 under the near continuous observations by our field representative
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company used the same hammer Delmag D 30 32
during pile driving operations Prior to the pile driving operations some of the pile
locations were predrilled up to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface
to facilitate the pile driving operations through the surface fill crust During the pile driving
operation logs were maintained for each pile which included information pertaining to pile
number date driven predrilled depth blow counts observations size and length of the
piles The locations of the production piles driven are shown on the attached Pile and
Drilled Shaft Identification Plans Plates 2 through 4 The production pile driving summary
is presented on Plate 5

Production Drilled Shaft Installation
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company performed the installation of the
production shafts at Kapaa Stream Bridge between March 23 and 28 2006 Two 36 inch
diameter drilled shafts were installed The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown
on the Pile and Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 4 In general a Geolabs
representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel reinforcements and
concrete placement operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a track mounted drill rig equipped with
36 inch diameter auger drilling bucket core barrel and cleanout bucket Temporary
casing with a diameter of 38 inches was utilized to case the upper portions of the drilled
shafts The production shafts were excavated to elevations from about 36 5 and 37 5
feet MSL The drilled shaft installation summary is presented on Plate 8
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with the minimum concrete cover between the sidewalls
of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a 4 inch ID
steel pipe by pump methods During concrete placement the bottom of the pump line was
immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the specifications In the shaft
excavation where temporary casing was installed a minimum of 5 feet of concrete was
maintained above the bottom of the casing during casing removal and concrete
placement
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the Kapaa
Stream Bridge have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
location of the top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to
verify that the shaft position is within the tolerance specified in the project specifications
Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that
the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load
capacity
