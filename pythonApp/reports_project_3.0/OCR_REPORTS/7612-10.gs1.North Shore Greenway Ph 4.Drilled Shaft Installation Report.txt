Dear Mr. Tanaka: 
In accordance with your request, Geolabs, Inc. observed the installation of the drilled 
shafts for the above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations 
during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the Kailua Stream bridge-culvert 
foundations as part of the North Shore Greenway Phase IV, located in Makawao on the 
Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1.  
Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the project. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering 
aspects of the drilled shaft foundation installation for conformance with the plans and 
specifications and to provide subsequent recommendations, as appropriate. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The proposed project involves a bikeway crossing over the Kailua Stream channel 
as part of the overall project, consisting of an asphalt paved bike/walkway approximately 
4,300 linear feet in length, from Baldwin Beach Park to Ulupua Place. A layout of the drilled 
shafts locations is shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2. 
The new bridge-culvert foundations are designed to be supported on deep 
foundations consisting of 18-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. The 
drilled shafts were designed to derive vertical support primarily from skin friction between 
the sides of the drill shaft and the underlying alluvial soils. The performance of a static 
load test was not required due to the limited amount of production shafts; however, 
integrity coring was required to be performed on the trial shaft and a production shaft to 
evaluate the condition of the placed concrete within the drilled shafts.

The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the North Shore Greenway Phase IV project only. 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company was the general contractor for the project and 
CMZ International, the subcontractor, drilled the shafts and were the installers of the rebar 
and concrete. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
As part of the project requirements, one trial shaft and four production shafts were 
installed for the North Shore Greenway Phase IV project.  The following subsections provide 
descriptions of the trial shaft construction and discussions on the production drilled shaft 
installation. 
Trial Shaft 
A minimum 18-inch diameter trial shaft was installed between October 28, 2019 and 
November 1, 2019 down to a tip elevation of -20.2 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The trial 
shaft was excavated using a Bauer BG 12H track-mounted drill rig. The purpose of the 
trial shaft was to examine the adequacy of the methods and equipment proposed by the 
contractor to install the drilled shafts into the existing subsurface soil/rock deposits. 
Excavation tools used for the 18-inch diameter shafts included 18-inch and 20-inch 
diameter augers and an 18-inch cleanout bucket. Also, a 20-inch casing was used for 
support of the sidewalls of the shaft due to soft to medium stiff soil conditions encountered 
below the groundwater. The groundwater was encountered about 10 feet below the 
existing ground surface.  The logs for the trial shaft are presented on Plates 3-1.1 through 
3-1.4. 
Integrity coring of the trial shaft was performed from November 12 to 15, 2019. Based 
on our observation of the recovered concrete core samples, we recommended acceptance 
of the trial shaft.  The log for the concrete coring is presented on Plate 5. Photographs of the 
recovered concrete core samples from the trial shaft are provided on Plates 7.1 through 7.3. 
Production Drilled Shafts 
The drilled shaft subcontractor installed a total of four production shafts from 
November 25, 2019 to December 4, 2019 at the locations shown on the Drilled Shaft 
Location Plan, Plate 2. A representative from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft 
excavation, placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete operations during the installation 
of the production drilled shafts.  
The drill rig used for the excavation of the trial shaft was also used for the 
production shafts. In addition, the excavation tools and temporary casing used for the trial 
shaft were also used for the production shafts. 
During excavation, the generalized soil conditions encountered consisted of fill and 
alluvial deposits consisting of sandy silt, silty clay, silty sand, cobbles and boulders.

After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with 
the use of the Bauer BG 12H Track Drill. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the 
drilled hole, we observed that concrete was placed within an acceptable time frame from 
substantial completion of the excavation. Concrete was placed through a 4-inch diameter 
tremie hose starting from about 0.5 feet from the bottom of the shaft. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremie pipe. Concrete with slump ranging from about 8 to 8.5 inches was 
used for the drilled shafts. Drilled shaft installation records of the production shafts are 
presented on Plates 4-1.1 through 4-4.4. 
Concrete Integrity Coring – Production Shaft 
Integrity coring was performed on Shaft No. B-1 on December 5, 2019 using a 
CME-45 truck-mounted drill rig equipped with HQ-sized wireline coring tools. A 4-inch solid 
stem auger was used to make a starter hole in the top 2 inches of the shaft. The HQ-sized 
coring tool cuts a hole measuring 3.8 inches (outside diameter) and extracts a 2.4-inch 
diameter core sample. It should be noted that the elevation of the top of concrete at the time 
of integrity coring was at approximately +11.3 feet MSL.  
The integrity coring for Shaft No. B-1 was advanced to a depth of about 30.7 feet 
below the top of concrete corresponding to an approximate elevation of -19.4 feet MSL. In 
general, sound concrete cores were recovered throughout the length of the cored hole. It 
should be noted that steel reinforcing, including cross-bracing and vertical rebars, were 
encountered within the core hole. The concrete coring log is presented on Plate 6.  
Photographs of the recovered core samples are presented on Plates 7.2 and 7.3. 
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the production drilled 
shafts supporting the bridge culvert for the Northshore Greenway Phase IV project were 
installed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications. In 
addition, it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed at 
sufficient depths to provide the allowable design compressive load capacities. 
LIMITATIONS
