This report presents the results of our geotechnical exploration
performed for the proposed Hazardous Material Waste Facility DPDO Hawaii
at its up dated revised location in Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard Oahu
Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction cost
estimation or bidding purposes
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
existing subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation and
pavement design of the proposed storage facility

material waste storage along with a loading and unloading area with
containment sumps and an attached PWC facility building The building
will be a one story prefabricated rigid frame steel structure and concrete
slab with CMU perimeter walls The proposed layout of the building is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu Ewa Coastal Plain This
plain is a sedimentary wedge composed of intercalated marine and lagoonal
sediments terrigeneous sediments and pyroclastic deposits from the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
In the immediate vicinity of the project site from the Pleistocene to
historic times the depositional environment was that of a lagoon or
estuary characterized by the low energy accumulation of silts sands and
gravels with sparse growths of coral algal reef masses At some point
during the Pleistocene volcanic activity along the Salt
Lake Aliamanu Makalapa Rift of the Honolulu Volcanic Series laid down ash
deposits which consolidated to form volcanic tuff
During historic times as the facilities at Pearl Harbor were
developed and improved the area was graded and low areas were filled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the floor slabs of the proposed
structures will be designed to be liquid tight for hazardous
material waste storage while allowing the operation of 4000 1b capacity
forklift trucks
The new concrete slabs will be located over existing structures such
as concrete slabs column and wall footings etc and in places a soil
layer To reduce the potential of developing cracks in these new slabs
that may span over hard and soft areas we recommend that a layer of
granular base course be provided This may be achieved by raising the
finished floor grades so that at least 6 inches of base course may be
placed prior to placement of the new slabs
To provide drainage of free water which may accumulate below the new
slabs and could weaken the slab subgrade support the existing concrete
slabs should be perforated by drilling 4 inch minimum diameter holes at
8 foot intervals The drilled holes should extend down below the water
level to about elevation 100 feet U S Navy Datum and should be
backfilled with 3B Fine crushed rock All backfill aggregate should be
adequately tamped in about 1 foot lifts to reduce future backfill
settlements
Another alternative would be to completely remove the involved
portions of the existing concrete slabs for the proposed warehouse
construction

footings or small concrete blocks then these concrete structures should

soils are adequate for lightly loaded spread footing foundation support
Any obstacles posed by the existing structures should be removed prior to

Site Preparation
All debris and other deleterious materials should be cleared grubbed
and removed from the site The existing concrete slabs below the new
structures should be perforated where necessary if they are to be left
in place below new fill The existing structures such as concrete walls
buildings sump pits etc should be removed to provide a minimum of
twelve 12 inches of vertical separation between the existing structure
and the bottom of the new slabs
The existing slabs in Building T 29 should be removed where
necessary
Any soft or weak spots disclosed during site preparation should be
repaired by excavating the soft or weak soils to firm ground and
backfilling with compacted on site granular coral material
After demolition clearing and grubbing the surface of the subgrade
soils to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about ten 10
inches moisture conditioned and then recompacted to at least 95 of the
ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
In the new loading and unloading area where heavy truck trailer and
forklift truck operations are planned the top portion of the existing
coral sand and gravel material within 2 feet below the finished grade

Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that the minimum 8 foot thick crust
of generally medium dense to dense subsoils and or soft to medium hard
weathered tuff will be adequate to support spread footing type foundation
provided the footing size is limited to a maximum of 3 5 by 3 5 feet to
reduce the bearing stress on the underlying very loose detritus deposits
encountered at the northeastern portion of the site
The footing should be embedded at least twenty four 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade and bear on dense to medium dense

or other loading conditions may further increase the embedment of the

An allowable soil bearing capacity of up to 3500 p s f may be used

addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to develop the passive soil resistance against the buried vertical
faces of the footings of the proposed structure

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
