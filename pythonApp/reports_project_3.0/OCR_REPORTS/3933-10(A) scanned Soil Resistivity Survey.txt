PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
generally consists of new roadways infrastructure and related improvements to the new llalo
Street extension from South Street to Ahui Street in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu The
proposed new roadway intersects the existing Ahui Street beginning at and
continues northwest to the existing South Street at As part of the llalo Street
Improvements it is proposed to install new 12 inch diameter water lines along the new
roadway
The total length of the proposed 12 inch diameter water line is approximately
2 150 lineal feet The invert of the 12 inch water line ranges from approximately 4 to 6 feet
below the existing ground surface along the proposed new roadway Soil resistivity surveys
utilizing the in Pin Method were performed to evaluate the corrosion rating
of the subsurface soils within the depths of the proposed water lines

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
properties of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements
provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of resistivity equipment and field engineers to and
from the project site
2 Performance of 27 in situ soil resistivity surveys at nine locations in the
shoulder areas along the proposed water line alignment corresponding
approximately to the 300 foot maximum spacing required in the Water System
External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The resistivity
surveys were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
G 57 78
3 Coordination of the work by a field engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion characteristics
of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
and for the field survey Our field work was performed under
sunny conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Twenty seven soil resistivity surveys were conducted at nine locations of the project
site using the in Pin Method and a resistance meter Nilsson Model 400
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity of the soil mass extending from the existing
ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the water lines was assessed by using this

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed shoulder areas along the existing Ilalo Street The average soil resistance along
the shoulders of llalo Street was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of generally 4 to 6 feet apart at the existing ground surface The
depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The
electrode spacing for each soil resistivity survey location was generally determined by the
depth of the water line invert from the existing ground surface It should be noted that some
underground utility lines were observed within the shoulder areas near some survey
locations
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plate 3 of this letter report However it should be noted that the soil resistivity
may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the survey locations temperature weather
conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the lesser the potential for corrosion
Based on the resistivity values and the corrosion ratings of the subsurface materials
at the site the subsurface materials at the site may be classified as highly corrosive to buried
metallic structures In general it appears that the proposed water line may need to be
designed based on Corrosion Ratings of 1 and 2
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the ratings provided in this report
