INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering exploration
performed for the overlay design of the existing Taxiways K KA KB
up 6TH and WHA and also the design of a new 50 foot wide temporary
taxiway planned adjacent to Taxiway P at the Naval Air Station Barbers
Point Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 2
Dk No 31
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to evaluate the subgrade soil and
the condition of the various pavement components to provide pertinent soil
parameters for the design of pavement overlay The subgrade soil at the
proposed temporary taxiway area was also required to be evaluated for the
design of the new pavement section
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling ten 10
borings approximately 2 0 to 5 0 feet below the existing pavement or
ground surface Field CBR tests were performed at various levels to
evaluate the in place bearing support capacity of the pavement
subgrade components Also included were laboratory testing of selected
samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of repair and design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing asphalt concrete pavements of the subject taxiways have
experienced some low severity cracking and wearing damages in various
areas The proposed project includes the design of a new asphalt concrete
overlay to repair these existing taxiway pavements which have a total
length of about 20 000 feet
In addition a 50 foot wide temporary taxiway approximately 1700
feet long is planned adjacent to the existing Taxiway P Both ends of
the new temporary taxiway will be connected to Taxiway Pp

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the airfield situated at the southern
end of the Barbers Point Naval Air Station All the taxiways included in
the exploration were paved with bituminous material The existing surface
conditions ranged from some low severity longitudinal cracks to some low
severity raveling and weathering of the asphalt concrete surface courses
Very low severity alligator cracks and block cracks were also observed in
some areas
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 10
borings to depths of 2 0 to 5 0 feet below the existing pavement ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed similar pavement sections and subgrade
conditions at each of the taxiways except with varying thicknesses of
inches The base course material ranged from mostly silty coral sand with
gravel to locally silty basalt gravel with sand The subbase and or
subgrade consisted primarily of silty coral sand with gravel The base
course subbase and subgrade soils were found to be in a dense condition
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Visual observation indicated that the bituminous surface courses of
the subject taxiways were generally in fair to good condition with
localized low severity cracking and wearing damages
GEOLABS HAWAII

Our laboratory testing indicates that most of the base course
materials have percentages of fines which exceed the Navy gradation
requirements for base course however this is not unusual since some
crushing of the sand particles usually occur during compaction and under
traffic loads The existing subbase courses appear to satisfy the Navy
grading requirement for subbase material with maximum design CBR of 30 to
40 The field CBR tests at various levels indicate that these pavement
components are generally competent and satisfy the Navy CBR requirements
for base and subbase materials
Therefore the subgrade soils and pavement components including base
and subbase appear to be suitable for support of the aircraft traffic
Based on these findings and the anticipated air traffic we believe
that the pavements of the subject taxiways may be repaired by removing the
upper 1 2 inch of the existing bituminous surface courses sealing the
existing cracks and overlaying with a 2 inch thickness of
asphalt concrete
For the design of the proposed 50 foot wide temporary taxiway which
is anticipated to serve the similar air traffic as the other taxiways we
recommend the use of a similar pavement section as the existing adjacent
Taxiway P

Causes of Pavement Cracking
A a a i RE ye
The relatively high field CBR values indicate that pavement
components are competent and are not a primary cause of the pavement
cracking
The cracking and wearing damages may be due to poorly constructed
paving lane joints shrinkage of the asphalt concrete surface due to
low temperatures or hardening of the asphalt and or local fatigue
failure of the asphalt concrete surface under repeated traffic
loading
Generally the pavement distress is minor and localized

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring laboratory and
field test data obtained from this soil engineering exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may occur in
localized ares and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to evaluate the pavement and
subgrade conditions below the existing taxiway pavements for pavement
overlay design of this remedial project and to evaluate the subsurface
conditions for the design of the proposed temporary taxiway
