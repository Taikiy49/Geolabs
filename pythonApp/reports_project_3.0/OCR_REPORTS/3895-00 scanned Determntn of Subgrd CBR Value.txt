In accordance with the review comment provided by the Federal Aviation
Administration we have prepared this letter to provide additional information pertaining
to the determination of the subgrade CBR value for the subject project
Based on Paragraph 206a 4 of the Advisory Circular 6D Airport
Pavement Design and Evaluation we understand that the common paving engineering
practice is to select a CBR subgrade value that is one standard deviation below the
mean Based on the limited number of CBR tests performed the mathematical mean
and standard deviation for the CBR values obtained i e 11 and 36 are 14 6
and 14 5 respectively This will result in a CBR subgrade value of 0 1 one standard
deviation below the mean which does not appear to be sound nor practical for
engineering analyses
Since a majority of the pavement area over 75 percent appears to be similar in
material to the samples with CBR values of 11 or lower we believe that the CBR test
with a value of 36 may not be representative of the general soil conditions encountered
at the site Therefore this test was neglected in our evaluation of the subgrade CBR
value determination Based on our engineering judgement we believe that a subgrade
CBR value of 8 obtained by calculating the average of the remaining three tests i e
and 11 should provide a fairly representative value of the soil conditions
encountered at the site
