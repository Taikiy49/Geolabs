INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the future Mililani Intermediate School site in the Mililani area
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses of the proposed project These recommendations are
intended for planning purposes only and they are not intended for final design of the
project The discussions and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed
a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is
located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our preliminary field exploration the proposed Mililani Intermediate
School site is generally underlain by stiff to very stiff clayey residual soils and stiff
clayey fills As previously indicated substantial fills of up to about 40 feet thick were
needed in the gully areas to raise the original ground to the existing grades Based on
the test borings drilled in these substantial fill areas the consistency of the clayey fill
materials were generally stiff Based on the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration the fills appear to have been placed with good compactive
effort However it should be noted that these preliminary findings are based on three
borings drilled in the filled gully area Additional test borings or other information may
reveal localized zones of poor quality fill material
As a result in order to confirm our preliminary findings that the fills appear to
have been placed with good compactive effort we suggest that surface settlement
markers be placed on the existing ground surface at buildings over substantial fills to
evaluate if the ground has stabilized under the weight of the fill We also understand
that the proposed project will be developed at a rapid rate Therefore to reduce the
potential for delays in the schedule resulting from encountering poor quality fill we
recommend that settlement markers be established on the existing ground surface at
buildings above substantial fill areas to evaluate potential fill settlements This is
especially critical where the new buildings and infrastructure will be constructed over
substantial fill thicknesses or across cut fill transition areas
Loose overcast materials were not observed along the top bank of the gully to
the south from our field observations and test pit excavations In general we believe
that future improvements may be constructed adjacent to the gully provided that the
applicable minimum set back distances are provided Deeper footing embedments may
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 00 Page 10
be required for some of these improvements to provide additional stability against
sliding and overturning
Based on the existing topography and current grading concept we anticipate that
some of the proposed new buildings will straddle across cut and fill areas with the fills
on the order of about 5 feet thick To reduce the effects of potential differential
movements between the cut and fill subgrade we recommend that concrete slabs on
grade be supported on at least 12 inches of select granular fill for more uniform
support Because the in situ clayey soils exhibit a moderate shrink swell potential when
subjected to moisture fluctuations the 12 inch select granular fill under the building
slabs will also serve to reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slab
on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the moderately expansive soils We
also recommend that a minimum 12 inch thick layer of select borrow subbase course
be provided below new pavement structures
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions native soils and fills encountered
in our borings we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or
continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed new facilities For
buildings that straddle across the in filled gully and in situ ground we recommend that
the foundations be structurally connected together with tie beams to reduce the
potential for differential movements

Settlement Monitoring
Based on the limited test borings drilled to evaluate the quality of the fill in the
gully area of the site it appears that the fill was placed with good compactive effort
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 00 Page 11
Based on the results of our preliminary exploration it is our opinion that the proposed
buildings and structures located above the deep fill areas may be constructed on a
shallow foundation system provided that the preliminary recommendations provided in
this report are implemented during detail design and construction However we
understand that the time schedule for development of this project is progressing at a
rapid pace Because of this constrained time schedule we believe that it is prudent to
establish some surface settlement markers on the existing ground surface at the
buildings located above the deep fill areas to confirm that the ground has stabilized
under the weight of the fill Readings from the settlement markers would also serve to
confirm our preliminary findings prior to construction of the project
To monitor the ground settlement rates we recommend that a number of
settlement markers be installed on the existing ground surface at the building areas
above deep fills and near and across the edges of the in filled gully The settlement
markers should be read by a professional land surveyor The readings should be taken
to establish the base elevation of the settlement marker and the surface elevation of
the ground adjacent to the settlement marker The readings should be taken
approximately once a month for a period of about 9 months to establish a trend
Additional readings may be taken beyond this period depending on the results of the
settlement marker readings Settlement readings should be referenced to at least 2
benchmarks located beyond the influence of the fill area greater than 300 feet away
The readings should be transmitted to Geolabs Hawaii for review during the settlement
period and or until the settlement readings indicate that the settlement rates have
stabilized
A proper contingency plan should be devised to accommodate the potential
delay in the construction schedule and the possible extra costs that may be involved

Foundations
Based on the current grading concept and the generally stiff subsoil conditions
encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may
be used for support of the proposed new building construction An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the
undisturbed in situ stiff clayey soils or new engineered fills needed to achieve the final
design grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
As previously indicated we recommend that the foundations for the buildings
straddling across the in filled gully and in situ ground be structurally connected together
with tie beams to reduce the potential for differential movements
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades for one story buildings Footing embedment should be
increased to a minimum of 24 inches for two story buildings For footings constructed
near top of slopes or on sloping ground the footings should be embedded deep enough
to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outside
edge of the footings to the slope face Footings located adjacent to the planned
retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining
wall foundations or the retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional
structural loads
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 00 Page 13
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
For footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material For footings bearing on new engineered fills the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils or engineered fills as
recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the
order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced
for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction
of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils or engineered fills

Slabs On Grade
Based on the existing topography and the current grading concept the future
slabs on grade for the proposed facilities will likely straddle across stiff in situ soils and
engineered fill materials In order to provide more uniform support for the concrete
12 inches of compacted non expansive select granular fill materials placed in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this
report In addition because the in situ clayey soils exhibit a moderate shrink swell
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations the 12 inch non expansive select
granular fill under the building slabs will also serve to reduce the potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the
moderately expansive soils
In accordance with the requirements of D A G S Technical Memorandum No 61
dated a basaltic termite barrier BTB will need to be incorporated into
the slab on grade design of new facilities Therefore for the interior building slabs not
subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of
cushion fill consisting of BTB be placed below the slabs to serve as a termite barrier
and to provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce future moisture infiltration
and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such
as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the BTB cushion layer Two inches
of moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 00 Page 15
of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings
such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask any
minor slab cracking
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation
such as driveways and parking aprons a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick
BTB cushion layer The 2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted for
these slabs The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a
modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection p c i may be used for the compacted base course A minimum slab
thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should
be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular
traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill or select borrow subbase Construction joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the slabs and foundations

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be required for grade separation at the project site The
following general guidelines may be used for preliminary design of retaining structures
Wall Foundations
In general we believe that retaining wall foundations may be designed in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Foundations section
of this report Wall footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches Wall
foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade For sloping ground
conditions the footing should extend deeper to obtain a minimum 8 foot set back
distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face
of the slope Wall footings oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should
be constructed in stepped footings

to reduce potential erosion of the finished slopes
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to
evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration

The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted

crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes
and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes It is critical to use a free draining material for the initial backfill to
reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes If the haunches of the pipes are not
properly backfilled it could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be

avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab and footing foundations
