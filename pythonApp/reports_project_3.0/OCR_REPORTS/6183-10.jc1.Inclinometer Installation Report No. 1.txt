INCLINOMETER INSTALLATION
LOWER KUAHEA STREET INTERIM REPAIRS
Dear Mr Ding
This letter report presents our inclinometer installation services for the Lower
Kuahea Street Interim Repairs project in the Palolo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
It was proposed to construct reinforced fill under concrete pavement as part of
interim repairs Intent of the interim repairs is to prolong underground utility and road
services within the predicted landslide area The construction will involve removing existing
concrete pavement excavating about 5 feet below the pavement surface backfilling with
geogrid reinforced soil mat and installing new concrete pavement
Majority of the existing inclinometers which was installed within the roadway will
be removed during the construction To provide access and continuous monitoring for the
ground lateral movement during construction it is desired to install new inclinometers
outside the excavation limits
We installed three inclinometers designated as I 35 I 36 and I 37 at the planned
locations outside the excavation limits as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 These three
inclinometers will provide lateral ground movement monitoring data before during and
after the construction for the construction documentations In addition three other
inclinometers will be installed within the excavation limits after the construction to replace
the inclinometers demolished by the excavation This report only covers the three new
inclinometers installed outside the excavation limits The installation logs for the other new
inclinometers and monitoring data will be contained in separated report after completion of
the construction
In general three inclinometer boreholes were advanced to depths of approximately
70 to 73 feet below existing ground surface The bottom of the boreholes extended into
either dense colluvium deposit or basalt formation

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos I 35 through I 37 extending to depths of about
70 to 73 feet below the ground surface The borings were drilled with a track mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils where encountered were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log
Legend Plate A 0 1 Symbols and abbreviations used in the boring logs are also
described on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1 Deviations made to the soil classification
in accordance with ASTM D2487 are described in the Soil Classification Log Key
Plate A 0 2 Graphic representations of the materials encountered are presented on the
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition we obtained
some samples from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to
drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core
of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description Rock cores were
described in general accordance with the Rock Description System as shown on the
Rock Log Legend Plate A 0 3
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run in rock that is
sound material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting
any drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
