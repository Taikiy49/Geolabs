PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site encompasses a new roundabout at the intersection of Piilani 
Highway and Kulanihakoi Street in the Kihei area on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The new 
roadways extend 650, 200, 1,000, and 200 feet in the north, east, south, and west 
directions, respectively, from the new intersection, as shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1, and Site Plan, Plate 2. The project included the construction of the new 
roundabout and road improvements described below. 
Site grading in preparation of subgrade for the roadway, sidewalks, medians 
and curbs. 
Construction of a 72-inch CMP culvert extension. 
Excavation and backfilling for utility installation.

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The site grading and utility installation work for the Kihei Roundabout project was 
observed by Geolabs, Inc. from March 29, 2022 to February 7, 2023.  The site grading and 
utility installation consisted of clearing and grubbing, placement, and compaction of fills 
and backfills to achieve the design finished grades for the project. Goodfellow Bros., Inc. 
performed the site grading and utility installation work. During the site grading operations, 
we obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in accordance with 
ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate A. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content and compacted in accordance with project specifications to a minimum of 
90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. 
Where fills were located in paved areas, the upper 3 feet of the finished subgrade was 
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of 
the compaction tests performed on the subgrade are summarized in Appendix A (Plates 
A-1 through A-46).  These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.  These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas 
assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was 
obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and 
hand probing. 
In addition, a sample of the asphalt concrete base was obtained and transported to 
our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry theoretical density was established in our 
laboratory in accordance with AASHTO T209, and the test results are summarized on 
Plate B. Asphalt density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938. 
Results of the asphalt density tests are summarized in Appendix B (Plate B-1).

in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
