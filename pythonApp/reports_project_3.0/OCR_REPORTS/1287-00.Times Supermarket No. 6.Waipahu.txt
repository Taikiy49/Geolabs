This report presents the results of a foundation investigation
for the proposed remodeling of Times Super Market 6 in Waipahu Ewa
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The investigation was performed in accordance with our proposal
dated and consisted of drilling two borings adjacent to
the location of the proposed structure making field observations
performing laboratory tests and engineering analysis
This report summarizes our findings and presents our
recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located approximately 500 feet east of
Waipahu Depot Road and south of and immediately adjacent to Hikimoe
Street The area is presently occupied by a wooden structure part of
which has an existing concrete slab floor
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is currently proposed to replace the existing wooden structure
with a storage area of concrete masonry unit CMU construction
attached to the existing building It is our understanding that no
cold storage area is planned Preliminary estimate indicated the CMU
walls would impose a load of about 2 4 kips per foot
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Two borings were drilled adjacent to the location of the proposed
structure to explore the subsurface conditions Boring 1 was drilled
with a truck mounted rotary drilling rig while Boring 2 was drilled
by a portable drill inside the existing nursery Access to the site
for drilling equipment was restricted by the existing structure
The borings were advanced to depths of 10 5 and 15 0 feet below
the existing ground surface

Soil Conditions
After penetrating the surface pavement section our borings
encountered a layer of fill material approximately 8 5 feet thick
This fill consisted predominantly of clayey silts and varied in
consistency between medium stiff and very stiff
Below the fill layer our borings encountered soft dark
brown black orgaic silt deposits to the maximum depths drilled This
organic silt is highly compressive

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain at
relatively shallow depths by soft orgaic silts which are highly
compressible and will produce settlements when stressed by loads such
as those induced by site filling and or building foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1287 00 Page 4
Due to the small scale of the proposed project and the
uncertainty of the thickness of the soft silts it appears that
elaborated foundation schemes such as piles caissons or stabilized
mats are not economically justifiable To limit the related movement
between the existing and new structures we believe that shallow
spread foundations can be designed to support the light bearing wall
loading provided that they are designed to take some settlements
If practicable steel or wood framed walls should be considered
since they impose less stress to the underlying soft deposit Because
of their flexibility we believe that they are more able to withstand
more differential settlement MU walls will impose a higher loading
more susceptible to differential settlements and therefore most
likely will develop cracks
It is recommended that little or no new fill be placed within the
new construction area to minimize general ground subsidence which
could provide a downdrag effect to the existing adjacent structure
Shallow Spread Footing Foundations
The shallow spread footings should be constructed as high as
possible above the soft organic silt while still maintaining a minimum
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade To provide a
uniform bearing surface the bottom of all footing excavations should
be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and recompacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1287 00 Page 5
Footings prepared in this manner may be designed for a net
allowable bearing value up to 1500 p s f This value may be increased
by one third for temporary wind or seismic loading However the
minimum footing dimension should be kept at 2 feet
In addition extra reinforcement should be placed in the footing
and be designed to span a distance of 15 feet If CMU walls are used
the lower four courses of the masonry wall should be solidly grouted
and reinforced to allow the lower portion of the wall to perform as a
beam Alternatively concrete stem walls 30 inches high may be used
instead of the grouted masonry
The bottom of all footing excavations should be cleaned of loose
materials and bear on firm soil The excavations should be observed
by a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been reached
Settlements
If the above recommendations are closely followed we anticipate
settlements of 1 to 1 5 inches total and three fourths of an inch
differential and over 30 feet for CMU walls If steel or wood framed
walls are used the estimated settlements will be smaller perhaps
about one half of the magnitudes estimated for the CMU walls

On Grade Slab
Our laboratory tests indicate that subgrade soils fill
materials generally exhibited low to moderately high expansion
potential Therefore special care should be exercised in the
on grade slab construction It is recommended that a 12 inch
perimeter footing embedment be utilized below the outside finished
grade To further minimize the swelling potential of the soil the
following procedures recommended for on grade slab construction should
be followed
l
The slab subgrade soils should be water saturated with a
perforated hose constant sprinkling for at least 2 days
prior to concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential
of the underlying subsoil It is important that the soils
engineer be notified to inspect and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab
and perimeter footing
The finished subgrade and the bottom of the thickened edge
footing excavations should be neat and free of loose soils
Just prior to concrete placement any soft silt deposited at
the footing after saturation should be removed

5 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in

Design Review
Plans and specification for the proposed building addition should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations backfilling
operation and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
