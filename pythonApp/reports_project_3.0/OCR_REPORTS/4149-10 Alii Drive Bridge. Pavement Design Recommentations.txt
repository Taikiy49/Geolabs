Methodology of Pavement Design
The pavement structural section presented in this addendum letter generally was
determined based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of the Pavement
Design Manual dated March 2002 The Pavement Design Manual was prepared
by the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division
Materials Testing and Research Branch The pavement design methodology is
based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the California
Department of Transportation Caltrans

Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on the borings drilled during our field exploration program we envision the
new pavements will likely be underlain by medium dense to dense silty and
gravelly sands anticipated in the shallow subsurface Based on our experience
with similar materials we have adopted a design Resistance R value of 40 for the
sandy subgrade materials in our pavement design analyses If site grading
exposes soils other than those assumed additional tests should be performed to

Design Pavement Section
As mentioned above the new pavement is likely underlain by sandy soils that may
have little or no cohesion As a result the subgrade may not be able to compact
into a dense mass to receive placement of the asphalt concrete base ACB layer
Based on our evaluation and this construction consideration we recommend
providing a layer of aggregate subbase between the upper ACB layer and the
underlying subgrade soils The aggregate subbase will serve to provide
confinement of the sandy soils as well as a compacted surface for the placement
of the ACB
Based on the assumptions and the construction consideration described above
we have determined the following flexible pavement design section for the new
pavements at the Alii Drive Bridge project Detailed calculations and analyses of
the pavement design are presented on Plate 2
New Pavements for Alii Drive Bridge
2 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Asphalt Concrete Base 92 Percent Relative Specific Gravity
6 0 Inches Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be excavated where necessary to the
pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate subbase layer The pavement
subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with AASHTO T 180 or ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
