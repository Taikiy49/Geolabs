Dear Mr Tatch
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated this letter
report provides the results of the field permeability testing performed for the subsurface
infiltration system for storm water runoff disposal at the Kapolei Business Park Phase 2
development in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the field permeability testing conducted at the project site
The findings are intended to assist in the design of the subsurface storm water infiltration
system planned for the project only The findings presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Kalaeloa area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The Kapolei Business Park Phase 2 development encompasses approximately
54 4 acres and is approximately bounded in a clockwise direction from the north by
the Kapolei Business Park Phase 1 development Hawaii Raceway Park and
Kalaeloa Boulevard
We understand that a subsurface infiltration system for storm water runoff disposal
is planned along the east side of Road B south of Road A at the approximate location
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The subsurface infiltration system will consist of
100 lineal feet of perforated corrugated metal pipe CMP with a diameter of 14 feet The
CMP will be surrounded by No 2 course gravel with 6 feet of general fill cover below the
finish grade Based on an invert elevation of 12 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the
proposed infiltration system we anticipate that the bottom of the percolation test hole

proposed subsurface storm water infiltration system site to obtain percolation

subsurface storm water infiltration system Our work was performed in general accordance
with the scope of services outlined in our revised fee proposal dated The

presenting our findings and recommendations

FIELD PERMEABILITY TESTING
Two field permeability tests were conducted at the proposed subsurface storm
water infiltration system site to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface
materials encountered at the boring locations The permeability tests were performed in
Boring Nos 1 and 2 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Falling head permeability tests were performed to determine the average hydraulic
conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field exploration the
subsurface materials encountered generally consist of medium dense to dense coralline
sands with gavel The permeability test results are presented in Appendix B
per minute at the test locations and depth based on the falling head field permeability test
results Based on the k value obtained and the information provided for the proposed
subsurface infiltration system the infiltration rates are estimated to be on the order of
about 11 8 and 11 5 inch hour at the two tested locations respectively
CONSTRUCTION OBSERVATION
The underground storm water infiltration system installation and testing program
should be conducted under the observation of a Geolabs representative This would allow
our firm to monitor the subsurface conditions to evaluate the storm water disposal
capacity and to allow for additional recommendations to be made In addition this is to

recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report
was prepared The findings presented herein are contingent upon such observations and
additional tests The owner client should be aware that substantial variations in subsurface
conditions may occur If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made Therefore a contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate the potential extra costs associated with these variations

LIMITATIONS
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings and
recommendations provided in this letter report
The boring locations indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the General Site Plan dated November
2013 prepared by Belt Collins Hawaii LLC The physical locations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or indicated in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors
