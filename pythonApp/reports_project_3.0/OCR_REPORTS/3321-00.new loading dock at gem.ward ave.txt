INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the New Loading Dock at GEM Ward Avenue in the district of Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII

This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The recommendations presented herein are intended for foundation design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis in the preparation of construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the district of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new loading dock at the south side of the existing
GEM Ward Avenue store as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the new loading dock will occupy an area of approximately
32 by 50 feet with a suspended concrete slab supported on steel posts and beams
It is our understanding that the posts and beams will be approximately 4 feet high and
the concrete slab will be subjected to forklift loads on the order of about 20 kips Since
the concrete slab will be structurally supported slabs on grade will not be necessary
We anticipate the foundations for the posts will be near the existing ground surface and
site grading for the project will be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an
overview of the subsurface conditions at the approximate location of the new loading
dock The subsurface information obtained was used to develop an idealized soil data
set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of the new loading dock
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to Group 70
International dated August 10 1994 The scope of work included the following tasks
and work efforts

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to a
depth of approximately 21 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the two borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
On the basis of our exploration the subsurface conditions for the new loading
dock consist of about 3 to 3 inches of asphaltic concrete pavement overlying about
6 inches of sandy gravel fill Underlying the fill material medium dense silty sands and
gravelly sands overlying sandy gravels were encountered to the maximum depth drilled
of 21 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at a depth of approximately
4 feet below the existing ground surface The groundwater levels were observed during
our exploration on an intermittent basis and the groundwater levels measured are
shown on the logs of borings
Due to the proximity of the site to the shoreline and the Pacific Ocean it is likely
that the groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration may
be affected by the tidal fluctuations In addition the groundwater levels at the project
site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered and groundwater
levels observed are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 of
Appendix A Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained from our
field exploration Results of laboratory tests are presented on Plate B 1 of Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the near surface
medium dense silty sands encountered in our borings would be capable of supporting
a shallow foundation system for the new loading dock Based on our laboratory test
results the near surface silty sands generally encountered at shallow depths have a
relatively high in situ moisture content The high moisture content of the near surface
soils may pose difficulties for compaction during construction Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation of footings
Recommendations addressing the above geotechnical considerations and other
geotechnical aspects of the new loading dock are discussed in the following sections
of this report
Loading Dock Foundation
As previously discussed the concrete slab for the loading dock will be supported
by steel posts and beams For the design of the footings of the loading dock we
recommend that an allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 p s f be used The allowable
bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by up to one third
when considering transient loads such as wind and or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade Where the new footings will be located less than 3 feet from
the existing GEM Store building foundation the new footings should extend to the same
elevation as the bottom of the footings of the existing building This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging the footings of the existing building with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement The excavations
for the new footings adjacent to the existing building should not extend below the
existing perimeter footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
Based on the laboratory test results the in situ moisture contents of the near
surface soils are relatively high Therefore prior to placement of steel reinforcing we
recommend that the footing subgrade be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the subgrade level they should be
over excavated to expose competent in situ material and replaced with select granular
material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Subgrade soils should be kept moist and should not be allowed to dry prior to
placement of steel reinforcing If shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations
they should be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks prior to placement of concrete
Therefore foundation subgrade should be thoroughly wetted to allow it to remain ina
moist condition prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressures
exerted against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the base of
footing and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure equal to an equivalent
fluid weighing 350 pounds p c f and a base friction factor of 0 40 may be used for
design Unless covered by slabs or pavements passive soil resistance in the upper
12 inches should be neglected
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the
order of inch
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site
during grading or after construction

Site Gradin
Only minor cuts and fills are anticipated during construction The following
recommendations are presented to provide general guidelines for the site grading work
1 The area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed All vegetation excavated pavement material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
2 In areas to receive fill the subgrade soils should be proof rolled to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction
3 Generally the on site sandy soils may be reused as fill material Ail fill
materials should be placed in level loose lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
4 If needed imported fill material should consist of non expansive well
graded select granular material less than 3 inches in maximum size with
less than 15 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material
should be tested and approved for its intended use by Geolabs Hawaii
prior to delivery to the project site
5 Measures should be incorporated into the grading of the project site to
reduce the potential for erosion and to prevent concentration of water
around structures The ground surface around structures should be
sloped away from structures

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to a depth of about 21 feet below the existing ground surface at the proposed
site of the new loading dock The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
advancing continuous hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
nia sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depths
