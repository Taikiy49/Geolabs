INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the house located at Street Lot 385 of the Kumu Iki
Phase 2 Subdivision in the District of Kapolei on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the remedial work design of the
house only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to perform remedial work to the house located at
Street Lot 385 of the Kumu Iki Phase 2 Subdivision Based on the information provided
settlement and crack propagation within the walls ceilings and floors were noticed by the
homeowner soon after moving into the house
Subsequent to the initial observation of settlement and crack propagation a
geotechnical engineering exploration was performed and report submitted by Hawaii
Geotechnical Group Inc dated The exploration generally consisted
of three interior borings drilled to depths of about 5 to 5 7 feet below the top of the
concrete slab four exterior borings drilled to depths of about 4 7 to 5 4 feet below the
ground surface and several test pits excavated to below the house foundation elevation
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 4061 00 Page 3
The exploration performed by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc generally
encountered moist stiff reddish brown clay below the gravel cushion at the interior and
below the ground surface at the exterior However the two borings outside the rear of
the house revealed slightly dry conditions within the upper 18 inches The test pits at the
rear of the house also revealed a one fourth to one half inch gap between the
footing bottoms and the underlying soil
According to the report by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc the cause of the
house distress is mainly due to soil shrinkage with a lesser degree due to soil swelling
from the accumulation of capillary moisture within the further interior sections of the
house To remedy the situation it was recommended that the perimeter thickened edge
slab footings be underpinned with concrete to 2 feet below the ground surface
Also since the observation of settlement and crack propagation floor level surveys
were conducted within the interior of the house by M E Pacific Inc These floor level
surveys were conducted on and These floor
level surveys generally revealed settlement occurring at the rear corners of the living
room

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject house is located on the south side of Kumulipo Street west of
Kamaaha Loop east of Barbers Point Access Road and south of a proposed industrial
subdivision The house lot is bounded by other houses to the south east and west
The house is located on a relatively level pad that is at a higher elevation than the
road The yard around the house consisted of stiff soil with sparse grass except at the
front of the house where the western side of the kitchen and the northern side of the
garage were abutting concrete slabs At the western side of the garage a gravel strip of
about 1 to 2 feet wide was encountered between the garage and the concrete sidewalk
The soil appeared to be more moist at the eastern side of the house as compared to the
other sides of the house and very moist near the air conditioner unit of the master
bedroom
The house generally consisted of a single story two bedroom two bathroom
slab on grade wood framed house with drywall interior walls The house contained a
vaulted ceiling within the living room an attic and lowered ceilings throughout the
remainder of the house The main footings for the house appear to be located along the
perimeters and about 2 to 3 feet off center in a longitudinal direction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Possible Causes of the Distress
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the house structure
distress may be due to footing settlement caused by soil shrinkage around the perimeter
of the house and overstress of the interior footings This assessment is based on the
data obtained from our recent exploration and on the data obtained from the previous
exploration and floor level surveys
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 4061 00 Page 11
The moisture content of the soil beneath the footings and slab were generally
found to be about 23 in the Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc exploration and about 12
to 17 in our exploration Based on these moistures the estimated shrinkage would be
about 2
The shrinkage would be concentrated along the perimeter of the house where the
supporting soils are more susceptible to drying out Settlements of the perimeter footings
could cause additional eccentric loading on the interior wall footing resulting in
settlements due to this overstress The presence of the existing diagonal crack at the
western side of the interior bearing wall and the non existence of a crack on the eastern
side supports the eccentric loading hypothesis The diagonal crack also correlates well
with the settlement pattern as the area of influence beyond the footing increases with
increasing magnitudes of footing settlement
The settlement of the western corner at the rear of the house may be due to soil
shrinkage and some additional load transfer from the interior eccentrically loaded bearing
wall Note that signs of heaving were not present during our site visit Also our
exploration indicates that the moisture content decreased not increased
Remedial Work Recommendations
Based on the house structure distress observed and the results of our exploration
we recommend that a minimum of five Atlas piers be installed at the locations shown on
Plate 8 We also recommend that a 3 foot wide thickened edge sidewalk be installed
around the entire house to reduce soil moisture migration which could cause soil swelling
or additional soil shrinkage A schematic sidewalk section is shown on Plate 9
Installation of the sidewalk will require removal of the existing tree trunk and the existing
lanai concrete slab In addition we recommend that the interior concrete slab up to 1 foot
beyond the existing large crack be saw cut and replaced with a slab doweled into the
adjacent concrete slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4061 00 Page 12
The Atlas piers should be installed to provide support to the foundations only The
Atlas piers should not be used to re level the house as further damage to the house
structure may result Also since the windows and doors function satisfactorily and the
floor settlements are less than 1 inch we believe that re leveling is not necessary
Installation of the Atlas piers will likely require enlarging the footing to allow proper
_ support and resistance during installation It will also require over excavation of about
2 feet laterally beyond the Atlas pier location to allow installation of the bracket and pipe
pile jacking assembly The excavation will also extend laterally about 1 foot beneath the
footing The Atlas piers should be jacked downward until a bearing capacity of
15 000 pounds based on a 3 5 inch outside diameter steel pipe is obtained
Prior to placement of the concrete sidewalks the subgrade should be
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and proof rolled or lightly
compacted to a relatively unyielding surface Over compaction of the subgrade should be
avoided The subgrade should also be moistened to maintain the near optimum moisture
content prior to placement of the concrete
The sidewalks should be provided with contraction joints at 3 foot intervals and
right angle intersections The excavated clay material may be used to re grade the lot to
slope away from the house structure
Cosmetic work and patch work to the cracks in the walls and ceilings should
proceed after the above recommendations are completed

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field exploration and information from the previous
exploration and floor level surveys Variations of conditions between and beyond the field
borings and test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring and test pit locations were determined by taping from structures
indicated on the Topographic Map provided by Mé E Pacific Inc dated
The locations of the borings and test pits should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks described in the boring and test pit logs depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings and test pits were measured on the dates
described in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may
occur due to variation in seasonal rainfall and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to a depth of about 2 3 feet below the
existing top of concrete slab at the approximate locations shown on the Borings and Test
Pits Location Map Plate 2 The borings were drilled with a concrete coring machine and
a hand auger
The subsurface conditions at the site were also explored by excavating and
sampling three test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 3 to depths of about 1 3
to 1 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Borings and Test Pits Location Map Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings and test pits were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our Project Engineer who monitored the drilling
and excavation operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System Descriptions of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Interior Borings and Logs of Exterior Test Pits
Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of
an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Interior Borings and the Logs of
the Exterior Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the soils under surcharge loads These tests were run on air dried
and non air dried samples Results of these swell tests are presented on Plate B 1
Six one inch ring shrinkage tests were performed on remolded samples to
evaluate the shrinkage potential of the soils when air dried from near optimum moisture
content Results of these shrinkage tests are presented on Plate B 2
