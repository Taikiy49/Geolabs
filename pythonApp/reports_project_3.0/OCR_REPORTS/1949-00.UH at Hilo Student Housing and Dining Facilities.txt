INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Student Housing and Dining
Facilities at the Hilo Campus of the University of Hawaii

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil rock
conditions at the project site in order to provide pertinent soil
parameters for the site grading and foundation design of the proposed
development
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance
coordination and supervision of the drilling and sampling of 18 borings at
the project site engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
and the appropriate logs are presented in Appendix A Detailed
information regarding the field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northwestern portion of the
University of Hawaii Hilo Campus on the Island of Hawaii The site is
bordered by Lanikaula Street on the north and student dormitories on the
west Hale Kanilehua east Hale Kaunoe and south Hale Ikena
The southern portion of the site where the proposed Student Housing
structures are to be located consists of a relatively flat well trimmed
grass lawn area sloping down gently to the east and west A 1 1 H V
slope trending northeast lies along the western boundary and existing
A C basketball and volleyball courts occupy the central portion of the
site

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of one 1 one story Dining
Facility Building approximately 160 by 120 feet in plan dimensions and
four 4 three story student dormitory structures each about 180 by 25
feet in plan A parking lot measuring 210 by 60 feet is also planned
A grading plan was not available at the time of our investigation
however we believe that site grading will be relatively small except for
the existing drainageway which will be filled up to about 10 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the eastern flank of the Mauna Loa
Shield Volcano which has been built up by basaltic lava flows These
flows are of Pleistocene and Recent Age and belong to the Ka u volcanic
series The lava flows were subsequently covered by the volcanic ash
deposits
Basaltic lava flows generally occur in two forms pahoehoe and aa
Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth ropy or billowy surface and
frequently contains voids of lava tubes Aa is characterized by a
clinkery surface and has a dense inner core

The lava flows at the project site are a transitional phase between
the two forms and have characteristics of each

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed relatively competent subsurface
conditions at the site From a geotechnical engineering point of view
the site is suitable for the proposed construction provided our
recommendations are followed
The structures may be supported by spread or wall footing foundations
bearing on the clinker fill or hard rock formation
Special design consideration is required for the Dining Facility to
reduce differential settlement due to the variable soil conditions
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan was not available however it appears
that the major site grading work would consist of embankment fills up to
10 feet thick over the lowlying drainageway area Thus imported fill may
be required for the proposed grading Some cuts and fills of generally
less than 2 feet would be required in the remaining areas
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill or
foundations should then be scarified to a depth of 8 inches and
recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method Any soft or loose areas disclosed during site
preparation should be repaired by removing the soft or loose
GEOLABS HAWAII

Dk No 49
materials and backfilling with compacted suitable on site or import
material In particular the high moisture content ash where
encountered under the new fills or structures should be removed and
replaced with compacted on site granular fill
Existing Fill
Based on our boring data and a review of the grading plan for the
existing Student Housing Facilities Phase II the approximate limits
of the previously placed fill is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The fill thickness generally varies from about 2 to 6 feet at the
proposed Student Housing area to about 11 feet near the southeastern
corner of the proposed Dining Facility
Generally our borings indicate that the existing fill is moderately
to well compacted However the existing fill areas should be
carefully observed by the project soils engineer during the site
preparation to locate loose areas
Fill Material
Generally the on site clinker material would be suitable for fill if
it is free of vegetation and over sized particles Clinkers or rock
fragments in excess of 8 inches in size should be crushed down to
smaller sizes or removed from the fill material The on site ash
should not be re used in the embankment fill It may be stockpiled
for use as topsoil for planting
Imported fill material required for the embankment fill should be
well graded granular material with a maximum rock size less than 8
inches and less than 25 passing the No 200 sieve The fill
materials should be tested and approved by the project soils engineer
prior to being transported to the project site

Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 12
inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the above specified
maximum density Conventional compaction testing may not be
practicable in rock fill material Instead a testing program should
be conducted by the soils engineer at the start of the project to
determine the minimum number of passes by the contractor s compactor
required to achieve the desired compaction The number of passes
could then be used as the criteria for subsequent compaction under
the observation of the project soils engineer
Fills placed against slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched to properly bond the new fill into firm
existing ground see Plate 4
To provide adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back to
the design grade The amount of slope overfilling required will
depend on the type of compactor used but generally should be a
minimum of 2 feet
ee ms ms
Cut or fill slopes should not be steeper than 2 1 H v
Intermediate benches may not be required for fill slopes up to 20
feet in vertical height For fill slopes greater than 20 feet but
less than 30 feet an intermediate bench about 8 feet wide should be
placed at the mid height of the slope
Surface runoff water should be diverted away from the top of the
slope Slope planting should be utilized as soon as practicable to
reduce erosion of fill slope

Student Housing Building Foundations
Spread and wall footing foundations may be used for the four 4
proposed structures
The bottom of the footing excavations on clinker or fill should be
recompacted to a minimum of 95 of ASTM D 1557 prior to concrete
placement
Exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of twenty four
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum
embedment for interior footings may be reduced to eighteen 18 inches
below the finished grade
For the footings bearing on the clinker or compacted fill an
allowable net soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used This
value may be increase by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads
are considered
A higher bearing value of 6000 p s f may be used on the underlying
fractured rock formation However the depth to hard rock is usually very
variable and may require deepening of some of the footings during
construction in order to bear on the rock formation
Spread footing foundations located close to the tops of the fill
slopes should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 10 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing
to the slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away
from building foundations and slabs

Cavity Probing
lava flows often contain tubes or cavities Several of the borings
encountered small cavities therefore to reduce the possibility of
footing settlement due to possible occurrence of cavities or loose
lenses near the footing bottom elevation drilled probes are
recommended below each column footing and at 15 foot centers for
continuous wall footings The probe holes should be a minimum of
3 inch in diameter and extend below the bottom of the footing toa
depth equal to two times the footing width or a minimum of 10 feet
The probe holes should be grouted with a sand water cement mixture
under low pressures
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens Then the cavity may be filled
by grouting through the probe holes Alternatively if a large
cavity is encountered near the ground surface it may be more
economical to expose the cavity and backfill with select fill The
probing and grouting operations should be monitored by the soils
engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are carried
out properly and to evaluate other field solutions which may be
required to properly backfill potential voids and densify the loose
soil which may occur near the footing elevations
Dining Facility Foundations
Most of the proposed structure is situated over an area underlain by
hard rock formation at shallow depths However the eastern portion of
the structure is situated over a drainageway and will require up to 10
feet of new fill Additionally this drainageway area appears to have

been filled previously with up to about 11 feet of fill refer to the
Generalized Subsurface Cross Section A A Plate 3 Therefore at the
proposed dining facility location there is a concern for potential
differential settlement between the portion of the building bearing on
rock formation and the portion on up to 20 feet of new and existing fill
One alternative to limit the differential settlement is to relocate
the building such that it will bear entirely on rock formation or
entirely on fill This alternative was discussed with the project
architect and found to be unfeasible due to the limited property
available
Since relocation appears unfeasible the next alternative would be to
attempt to limit the amount of structural settlement within the fill
area The existing 10 feet of old fill encountered in Boring No 17 was
found to be only moderately compacted Therefore we recommend that the
existing fill be overexcavated down to the rock formation and recompacted
as specified in the Site Grading portion of this report The
overexcavation should extend under the building area and for a minimum
distance of 10 feet beyond the building perimeter Following the
recompaction of the existing fill and the subsequent placement of the new
engineered fill we recommend that settlement gauges be installed near the
finished grade see Plate 5 to monitor the ground settlements if any
The settlement gauges should be read by the surveyor at least once a
week Building foundation construction for the dining facility should be
delayed until the settlement gauges indicate negligible fill settlement
We estimate that this waiting period would be a minimum of about 1 to 2
months
Following the above site preparation the foundation recommendations
presented for the Student Housing structures may be used

On Grade Slab and Exterior Walkways
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at this
site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
The thickened edge around the on grade slab should extend a minimum of 8
inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
To reduce differential settlement of the slab the condition where
the slab bears partly on rock formation and partly on fill should be
avoided If this condition occurs the rock formation should be
overexcavated to provide a minimum of 2 feet of fill under the slab
subgrade
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the automobile parking
area In general the on site sand and clinker materials can provide an
excellent subgrade support when evenly mixed and compacted Assuming that
either the on site soils or imported select granular fills with CBR
greater than 12 are used in the pavement subgrade we recommend that the
following preliminary pavement design be used for cost estimate purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at
least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density to a depth of 6 inches
below the bottom of the base course if rock formation is not encountered

during construction and the above design section can be revised to

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction to monitor
the site grading footing excavation cavity probe drilling and grouting
and foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling
from the topographic map Elevations of the data points were determined
from the topographic map The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
