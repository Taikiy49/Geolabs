This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Honoapiilani Highway
Realignment Phase 1A project between and
The highway realignment project is east of the existing Honoapiilani Highway in
Lahaina on the Island of Maui Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project includes the construction of a grade separation structure on Lahainaluna Road
and Kahoma Stream Bridge structure

Because of potential subsurface cavities and or voids within the underlying basalt
formation a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath
foundations was specified for the project The probe hole locations were surveyed and
marked in the field by the general contractor Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company
prior to the probing operations The probe holes were spaced at about 10 foot on center
along the structure foundations The total number of scheduled probe holes is provided
below The approximate locations and identification of the scheduled probe holes for the
below structures are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plans Plate 2 1 and 2 2
e Lahainaluna Road Grade Separation Structure 15 probe holes
Kahoma Stream Bridge Structure 61 probe holes
The probing and grouting work for the project was performed after the excavation
for the structure foundations for the Kahoma Stream Bridge Structure For the
Lahainaluna Road Grade Separation Structure the probing and grouting work was
performed from about 9 06 and 5 69 feet above the bottom of footing elevation for
Abutment Nos 1 and 2 respectively The probing work was performed by Blasting Tech
The probe holes were drilled with a Ramtec air track drill rig equipped with drill rods anda
cutting bit The probe holes drilled were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to about
15 to 26 feet below the existing ground surface at least 15 feet below the bottom of footing
elevations A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is
provided on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of saprolite underlain by basalt rock formation After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled with 2 500 pounds per square inch psi grout mix
The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Ameron Hawaii and placement of
the grout was performed by Maui Maco using a trailer pump Since the scheduled probe
holes had grout intakes of less than 4 times the theoretical probe hole volumes additional
probe holes were not drilled
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications
Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the relatively small voids
and or cavities encountered at the structure foundation locations have been grouted and
that the subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the
structure foundation
