DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The initial field explorations were made at the site in 1966 At that
time a large portion of the site was covered with a layer of dark
brown organic clay and akulekule grass at about the high tide elevation
In the spring of 1969 the site was cleared and silting basins were
constructed over the site for the storage of hydraulically dredged
material from Kuapa Pond The site was cleared and grubbed drainage
paths installed and berms constructed with mostly coral along the
waterway perimeter
Dredged material was pumped into the basins from June 1969 thru April
1970 Approximately 10 to 14 ft of material were pumped into the basins
then left to drain At present about 7 to 10 ft of material is stored
in the basins A surface crust of about 1 to 2 ft has formed over the
soft ponded material
Some local slumping occurred during the construction of the berm over
the deeper soft pockets at the north end of the site around Boring Nos
5 5A and 6 The berms were beconctrueted in the slump area and the
silting basins were subsequently filled without further slumping of
the berms
The present plan is to remove the stored material and grade the site for
residential development The fills would generally be constructed with
the ponded material after aerating and possible mixing if necessary with
imported soils to a workable condition for compaction
The storage or ponding of the dredged material in the silting basins
for the past several years has acted as a surcharge load over the
soft material that was originally underlying the site Future
settlements because of the consolidation of the underlying materials
will be much less than if the site were loaded for the first time
In general fills along the waterway perimeter should be kept less than
about 6 to 8 ft elevation Around Boring and 6 where slumping
has occurred during the filling of the basin additional slumping may
occur when more dredging is done to remove the mud and silts in the
adjoining waterway The final planning for the use of this section of
land should be délayed until dredging is done and the limite of the
settlement area can be better established
Some settlements are likely to occur particularly at the north end
of the site A settlement observation program should be initiated and
maintained to use as a guide in scheduling of surface construction
