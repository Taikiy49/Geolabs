As part of our special inspection services for the above project we have observed
the pile load testing on one micropile including recording the load test data at a selected
location along the sewer line alignment at the project site The project location is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This letter report presents the results of our field
observations during the pile load test program and our findings and recommendations for
the proposed micropile foundation support system for the sewer replacement project
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
The new sewer line will be supported on a concrete beam and micropile foundation
system Installation of the micropile foundation will be conducted by the micropile
installation contractor Schnabel Foundation Company to provide a micropile capable of
supporting an allowable compressive load of 48 kips with a factor of safety of 2 0
Based on the project drawings 7 inch diameter micropiles with an embedment
depth of approximately 64 feet below the design micropile cut off elevation will be used to
achieve the allowable compressive load capacity of 48 kips The micropile system
generally consists of a threaded reinforcing bar as a load carrying steel member steel
casing installed within upper soft soil layers to prevent surrounding soils from caving in
together with a grout body of neat Portland cement mix which allows transfer of tension
and compression forces mainly through friction of the threaded bar via the grout body into
the soil
Prior to drilling operation a trench about 4 foot deep was dug at the designated
load test micropile location The load test micropile was then installed using a Hutte 605
double head drill rig employing drag bit drilling techniques The Hutte 605 double head
drill rig is fitted with two drilling motors that simultaneously turn separate inner and outer
drill strings in opposite directions The inner drill string or rod has a drag bit attached to it
that delivers the majority of the cutting energy to advance the hole The outer casing is
fitted with J teeth welded to the leading piece of casing and rotated in with down pressure
from the drill rig

During the micropile installation the entire length of the drilled hole was cased
using steel casings which were installed simultaneously while the hole was drilled A
threaded reinforcing bar with PVC centralizers at about 10 foot spacing were inserted into
the drilled hole Neat cement grout was placed through a tremie pipe starting from the
bottom of the hole During grouting the tremie pipe was slowly withdrawn as the grout
level in the hole rose Grouting was stopped and the tremie pipe was withdrawn from the
hole when grout started overflowing and clean grout appeared at the top of the casing
Steel casing was then extracted from the hole until the required casing tip elevation was
reached Additional grout was placed from the top of the hole when the grout level in the
hole dropped during the extraction The load test micropile was installed with an
embedment depth of approximately 73 5 feet below the bottom of trench
During the micropile installation the drilling time required to advance each section
of the casing total strokes of grout pumping and number of sacks of cement used were
recorded for evaluation and documentation Special attention was given to the presence
of drilling obstacles and groundwater during the installation process which may indicate
change of subsurface conditions

The load test was performed using a load cell and a donut jack operated by a
hydraulic pump equipped with two pressure gauges Calibration of the load cell and
donut jack and hydraulic pump was provided by the micropile installation contractor
Schnabel Foundation Company The reaction for the hydraulic jack was provided by a
steel reaction beam The ends of the reaction beam was supported by two short steel
beams that were connected to two reaction micropiles at each end According to the
contractor Schnabel Foundation Company the reaction piles were the same diameter
and embedment depth as the load test pile However all steel casings were retracted

The load test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1143 Standard Load Test Method The maximum test load on the load test micropile
was planned at about 96 kips which is 2 0 times the allowable compressive load of
48 kips In general the load test was loaded to the maximum test load of 96 kips in
approximately 12 kip load increments Each load increment was held until pile
movement stabilized except for the maximum load of 96 kips which was held for
12 hours After loading the test pile to the maximum test load the test load was
unloaded in approximately four equal decrements Each load increment during
unloading was held for 60 minutes The results of the load test are presented on

provided on Plate 5 of this report Based on the Micropile Design and Construction
Guidelines published by the U S Department of Transportation Federal Highway
Administration in failure is defined as continued pile top

Based on the shape of the load test curves it appears that the load test micropile
was capable of sustaining the test load of about 96 kips for 12 hours and the rate of the
pile head movement was generally stable at the maximum test load without signs of
appreciable creep In addition the creep displacements measured at the 96 kip load
increment for are plotted on Plate 6 The maximum creep rate per log
cycle of time in minutes shown on Plate 6 is 0 0015 inches The measured creep rate
for the load test micropile is less than the allowable creep displacement of 0 08 inches
per log cycle of time in minutes specified in the Micropile Design and Construction
Guidelines Therefore we believe that the proposed micropile foundation system is
capable of supporting a design load of up to 48 kips with a factor of safety of 2 0
