CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING
In general the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest Crosshole Sonic Logging
tests were performed between all the possible access tube combinations in the drilled
shaft four tubes resulting in six profiles The northernmost access tube was designated
as Tube 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered clockwise
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 1 6 feet above
the top of the drilled shaft concrete We understand potable water was used to fill the
access tubes after concrete placement Based on our observation in the field the water
levels within the access tubes were above the top of the shaft concrete in all the access
tubes at the time of testing
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria CCRC Deviations from the
following values are generally used to determine the concrete condition rating The CCRC
is summarized in the table below

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test
results by dividing the average tube distance by the average first arrival time for each tube
combination in a drilled shaft It should be noted that the average apparent sonic wave
velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not
accurately known
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A summary of the CSL test results including the tested concrete length computed
apparent sonic wave velocity and comments on the concrete condition are presented on
Plate 2 The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on Plate 3 The
individual CSL test plot for each tested tube combination profile and a plan showing the
tube locations are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2
Based on the CSL test results the increased first arrival times FATs were
generally less than 10 percent corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of
less than 10 percent and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for the
trial shaft except for the areas within the profile as described below It should be noted that
changes in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of
the reduction and the associated increase in FAT
Profile 2 3 FAT increases up to 10 6 percent from approximately 17 6 to
18 1 feet below the top of concrete
Profile 3 4 FAT increases up to 17 1 percent from approximately 2 1 to
3 1 feet below the top of concrete
Profile 3 4 FAT increases up to 14 9 percent from approximately 71 0 to
71 9 feet below the top of concrete
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in placed concrete for
Shaft No 6 ranged from approximately 10 199 to 12 934 feet per second Based on the
results of the CSL test and FAT increase between 10 and 20 percent we believe that the
concrete integrity of Shaft No 6 within the testing depth would be classified as
Questionable based on the above CCRC
The highest FAT increases are within the overpour zone The other questionable
FAT increases are in relatively thin isolated zones Therefore we believe that no
significant defect anomaly is indicated by CSL testing and we recommend the acceptance
of Shaft No 6 within the testing depth
