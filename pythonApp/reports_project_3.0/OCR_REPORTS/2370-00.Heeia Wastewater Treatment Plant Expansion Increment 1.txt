INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of foundations site preparation and
earthwork for the proposed expansion of the Heeia Wastewater Treatment
Plant in Keauhou North Kona on the island of Hawaii
PURPOSE _ AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions within the proposed plant expansion area in order to
develop a generalized soils rock data set for the formation of
engineering design recommendations pertinent to the proposed wastewater
treatment plant expansion

STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the eastern side of the existing
Heeia Wastewater Treatment Plant facilities in Keauhou North Kona on the
island of Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Most of the proposed expansion site is relatively level partially
graded and covered with light grass Existing CRM walls are located along
most of the boundary lines of the project site
The existing ground surface elevations of the site range from about
28 to 37 feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling ten 10 borings to depths ranging between 21 0 and
25 5 feet below the existing ground surface using auger and core drilling
techniques The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The logs of the borings are presented in Appendix A
of this report along with detailed descriptions of the field exploration
In general the borings encountered a 0 to 5 feet thick surface layer
of dense clinker graded clinker fill overlying hard basalt formation to
the maximum depths drilled with exceptions in Boring Nos 6 through 8
Tan to light gray sandstone beach rock were encountered in Boring Nos 6
through 8 at depths ranging between 22 5 to 24 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
Occasional clinker zones were encountered in Boring Nos 8 and 10 at
depths of 7 to 9 feet and 23 to 25 feet respectively below the existing
ground surface
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings during
the time of our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view The underlying clinker and basalt
formation encountered at shallow depths below the existing ground surface
of the project site should provide adequate bearing for the proposed
wastewater treatment plant expansion facilities
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
project grading limits should be cleared of any vegetation rubbish or
deleterious material The resulting grub and spoil material should be
disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed
during the clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered
fill
The on site clinker and basalt material if reduced to generally less
than twelve 12 inches in size should be a source of adequate fill
material Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated
September 1986
Fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximm dry
density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum
dry density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two
2 feet of fills and backfills below road subgrade level should he
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density

Cut and fill slopes should be designed at a ratio of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V Fill slopes should be constructed by
over filling and cutting back to the design slope Care should be
exercised to avoid over cutting of the slopes since repair of over cut
areas would create undesirable sliver fills
Excavation
Generally the in situ clinker and basalt formation should be
rippable by a bulldozer or other heavy equipment Due to the nature of
the basalt formation encountered at the site it is anticipated that some
hard excavation e g hoerams chipping and or blasting may be required
Therefore allowance should be made for the additional expense and time to
perform the hard excavation
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The excavation method and design of the
shoring bracing should be left to the discretion of the contractor
performing the work However excavations in competent basalt formation
normally can stand unsupported at relatively steep angles for temporary
construction purposes
Foundation Support
We recommend that spread footing foundations on the underlying
in situ dense basalt formation or on compacted in situ clinker material be
used for the proposed structures For the design of foundations bearing
on in situ dense basalt formation an allowable bearing capacity of up to
clinker or compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density compaction effort
should be monitored by the project geotechnical engineer an allowable
bearing capacity of 5 000 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered

Some of the clinker zones may require a choking layer consisting of
well graded sandy gravel over the surface of the exposed clinker to fill
void spaces to provide uniform footing support
Foundations should be embedded a minimm of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft
and or weak material down to in situ dense clinker or basalt formation and
backfilling of the resultant void with fill material compacted in
accordance with the above compaction recommendations

Backfill Placement
It is anticipated that backfilling will be required for
_below grade structures and for trenches Backfills should be placed in
general accordance with the recommendations presented above for fill
placement except that the maximm particle size should not exceed
three 3 inches For below grade structures it is recommended that a
clean granular material such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel be utilized
to reduce the amount of compactive effort required to attain 90 percent
relative dry density
Settlements
Minimal settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the
compacted in situ clinker material or basalt formation prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented herein

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of some of the proposed
structures will have interior concrete slabs on grade The slabs may
be supported by dense basalt compacted clinker or backfill material
A 4 inch layer of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel should be provided below the
slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade underneath the cushion fill
should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane should be
considered below the slabs
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for access roads
within the expansion project We recommend that the A C pavement
section consist of the following
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
or In Situ Basalt Formation
The base course should be compacted to 95 maximum dry density as
established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction
and the above section may be revised if necessary The recommended
section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage
will be provided in the paved areas Weepholes should be provided at
the base course level in drainage inlet structures to relieve water
which may accumulate in the subgrade

Design Revi
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the intent of the earthwork recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as visual
observations and drilled borings Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations
given in this report
