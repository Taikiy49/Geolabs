SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed two distinct
soil conditions at the site The upper
areas are underlain by relatively competent
soil over dense cemented sand Conventional
grading design can generally be utilized
here The upper soils are moderately ex
pansive and may require selective grading
below proposed structure areas
The lowlying area is underlain by miscel
laneous fill over loose sandy deposits
High groundwater level was also encountered
in this area Fills over this lowlying
area would be subject to settlement Special
ground stabilization may be required in
isolated areas prior to site filling
Additional exploration would be required
when more detailed grading and development
plans are available
The text of this report should be referred
to for detailed and special design recommen

INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the general
subsurface soil conditions at the site to evaluate the suita
bility of the proposed development from a soil engineering
point of view Of particular concern would be the stability
and anticipated settlement behavior of the proposed new fill
over the lowlying soft subsoils
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dix of this report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is presently planned to develop the lowlying central por
tion of the site by filling over the existing dump fill
material and wet areas for residential development using
borrowed material from the higher portions of the site The
extent and amount of site grading has not been determined at
this time
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located along the Cackle Farm
Road approximately 500 feet west of Kamehameha Highway in
GEOLABS HAWAII

Laie Oahu Hawaii The site area is approximately 37 acres
and is generally surrounded by similar undeveloped land
on the north east and western sides while Kahawainui Stream
borders the site on the south
The relief is basically level in the center one third of
the site with ground elevations of approximately 2 to 5 feet
The eastern and western portions of the site with elevations
of approximately 23 feet in the east and up to 60 feet in
the west gradually slope down towards the central portion
Within the center one third an existing pond was present
adjacent to the south boundary with a surrounding wet surface
condition This wet condition was noticed throughout the
central lowlying areas either at surface or near surface
Also noticed within the central area were suspected sand
mining deposits and miscellaneous dump fill materials
There were no noticeable structures present within the pro
ject site with the exception of the dirt road and wire fences
sectioning off areas of the site
The vegetation consisted of a dense growth of Haole Koa on
the eastern portion of the site while the central and western

portions used for grazing cattle and horses were covered
by a moderate growth of grass and shrubs
GEOLOGY
Located on the north northeast coastal plains of the Koolau
mountain range in Laie the site lies near the separation
of two geologic formations of different composition and
Origin Towards the southwest the general areas has been
surficially mapped to consist of a younger alluvium made up
of clayey deposits usually associated with stream and shallow
water depositions throughout its development Towards the
north the general area in closer proximity to the site
has been mapped to consist of consolidated calcareous sand
dunes and also marine limestones formed during periods of
different sea levels A typical feature about the physical
characteristics of lithified dunes and marine limestone
occurring below a water table is the possible presence of
voids or solution cavities and channels created by the flow
of underground water
Within the investigated area of the site the borings gener
ally encountered cemented sand with traces of coral fragments
of varying densities generally beneath the looser silty and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 951 00
Page 5
sandy surface layers which become harder with increased
depths Basically the borings appear to verify the pre
viously mapped formation of the area
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Seven borings were drilled at the Site ranging in depths
from 15 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface to ex
plore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration and labora
tory testing is covered in the appendices along with the
boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the site can be generalized into the
following areas
Eastern Area
Based on Borings 4 and 5 the eastern portion of the site
is covered with a stiff reddish brown clayey soil to a
depth ranging from 2 5 feet to 4 5 feet below the surface
Beneath this soil cover granular material of varying
densities was encountered through to the bottom of both
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 951 00
Page 6
borings The granular material generally consisted of a
silty cemented sand with traces of coral fragments Boring
4 revealed what appeared to be a cavity or very loose layer
approximately from 13 feet to 17 feet below the surface
Western Area
In the western portion of the site Borings 6 and 7 revealed
a similar profile to that of the east A stiff reddish brown
clayey soil covers the area to a depth ranging from 7 5 feet
to 8 feet below the surface Beneath this soil a very dense
cemented sand was encountered to the bottom of both borings
Within the central lowlying portion of the site the borings
revealed a miscellaneous clayey and silty dump fill extending
to a depth of 2 to 9 feet below the surface Beneath the
upper fill a loose dark brown to gray silty sandy material
was encountered This material generally graded into a sand
with cemented sand fragments A dense cemented sand layer
with traces of coral fragments was encountered at 16 to 26
feet through to the bottom of the borings in Borings 1
3 The dense cemented sand layer was not encountered in
Boring 2 to a depth of 45 feet the depth drilled

Groundwater Level
The groundwater level encountered in the borings at the
time of field exploration was approximately 1 to 2 feet
in elevation based on the provided existing surface ele
vations for the various boring locations Fluctuations of
this water level are directly influenced by the quantity
of water flowing in the nearby stream During wet periods
the water level in the lowlying central area could rise
above the present ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The general site grading concept for the proposed develop
ment appears to consist of cutting the higher eastern and
western portions of the site and filling the lower central
area
In the lowlying area the borings encountered up to 9 feet
in Boring No 3 of soil and rubbish fill over loose sand
to depths of 15 to 40 feet High water table was encountered
in this area Based on the borings the rubbish fill appears
to consist mainly of medium density glass wire metal and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 951 00
Page 8
soil without large void spaces Additional field explora
tion utilizing test pits should be made to further evaluate
the composition of the upper rubbish fill Of particular
concern would be large buried structures such as aban
doned cars with substantial void spaces which could cause
large future ground settlements
New fills to be placed over this lowlying area would be sub
ject to settlements due to the loose sandy soils and rubbish
fill The amount of settlement will vary depending on the
fill height Preliminary estimates of the anticipated
settlements due to 15 feet of fill are on the order of 6
to 10 inches However due to the sandy nature of the
loose underlying subsoils we estimate that 90 of the settle
ments would occur within about 4 months Settlement gauges
are required to monitor the settlement rates and to deter
mine when utility and housing construction can commence
Due to the high water level in this low area special ground
stabilization may be required in isolated low pockets prior
to site filling
In the upper areas the borings encountered 2 5 to 8 feet
of brown silty clay and clay over dense cemented sand In

general the on site upper soils are relatively competent
and suitable as a source of borrow fill However labora
tory ring swell tests indicated that some of the silty clay
soil has moderate swelling potential
Therefore where slab on grade and light structures are
planned these soils should be capped with a 1 to 2 foot
layer of the other on site non expansive coral and sandy
soils to reduce the moderately expansive potential of the
soils
Site Preparation
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and other
deleterious material In areas to receive fill the ground
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought
to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a mini
mum of 90 density
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and other
deleterious material
Depending on weather conditions isolated low areas may have very

a working platform prior to filling The working platform
may consist of a 2 to 3 foot layer of gravel 3B Fine or
a combination of a synthetic filter fabric such as Mirafi
140 and a 12 inch gravel 3B Fine layer
Due to the high groundwater level in this lower area ligh
ter equipment should be used during the site preparation
and early fill operations
Fill Placement
After site preparation the fill should be placed in maximum
10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM test designation D 1557 70
Fills placed over the loose subsoils in the low area should
be placed at slow rates to avoid squeezing or failure in
the loose subsoils An initial estimated rate of 2 feet
of

in the field based on the settlement gauge readings
The on site cut material from the upper areas is suitable
for use in compacted fill and backfill

required it should be tested and approved by the soils

Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes
are recommended for the site
To reduce the amount of runoff over the slopes a drainage
bench should be provided at mid height for slopes having a
vertical height greater than 20 feet
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable

Settlements
Due to the loose subsoils in the lower central area
ground settlements are anticipated due to the weight of the
new fill The total settlements experienced would depend
on the height of fill placed For the anticipated 15 feet
of fill estimated settlements are on the order of 6 to
10 inches
The time required for the estimated settlement to occur is
very difficult to predict due to variations in the nature
of the subsoils Due to the sandy nature of the subsoils
we estimate that 90 of the anticipated settlements would
occur within about 4 months The actual settlement rates
should be monitored by settlement gauges to determine when
construction of structural improvements can begin If it
is desired to reduce the waiting period for the settlement
a temporary surcharge fill could be placed over the fill
areas We would be glad to discuss various surcharge fill
schemes with you if they are desired
Settlement gauges should be installed prior to filling to
monitor the settlement rate and determine when building
and utility construction can proceed
Based on the borings drilled we have assumed that large
GEOLABS HAWAII

void spaces or thick organic deposits are not present in
the soil and rubbish fill and therefore additional large
settlements are not anticipated due to this layer Addi
tional test pits would be required to verify this assumption
Foundations
We anticipate that the proposed light residential struc
tures could be supported by conventional spread or conti
nuous footing foundation bearing on the stiff natural ground
or compacted fill
Detailed foundation design parameters could be provided af
ter more detailed site grading and structure designs are
available
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete roadways are anticipated for the develop
ment
Laboratory CBR tests on the upper on site soil indicate
moderate expansion and therefore the preliminary pavement
design over this type of soil is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
6 inch Borrow
20 inch Total Pavement Thickness

In fill areas the pavement thickness could be reduced by
utilizing the other better material on site such as
coral sand within the top 2 feet of the roadway areas
If this selective site grading and stockpiling procedure
is specified then the above select borrow and borrow re
quirements could be eliminated
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual sub
grade soil samples at the time of the roadway construction
and the above design section may be revised accordingly if
other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located throughout the site If
encountered the cesspool locations should be flagged in
the field Where existing cesspools are located below or
adjacent to footings and slabs the Sludge should be removed
and the cesspool filled with compacted granular backfill
The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should
be removed

Additional field investigation and analysis would be required
when more detailed site grading and structure designs are
available

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our sursurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
