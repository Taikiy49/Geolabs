In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted
a geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Septic Tank and Leaching Field at

project These findings and discussions are intended for design input only and are subject

Please note the soil samples recovered during our field exploration remaining after
testing will be stored for a period of two months from the date of this report The samples
will be discarded after that date unless arrangements are made for a longer sample
storage period Please contact our office for alternative sample storage requirements if

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves renovation and replacement of some cottages in the University
of Hawaii at Manoa UHM Lyon Arboretum in Manoa Valley on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project site is located in the rear of the valley above Paradise Park as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Sanitary wastes generated are currently treated and disposed of through a
cesspool system As part of the improvements related to the cottage renovation and
replacement it is proposed to install a new wastewater system for treatment and disposal
of the sanitary wastes This new system would utilize a septic tank for the treatment
component and a leaching field for disposal of the wastewater effluent Details of the
system design and capacity were not available for preparation of this report
The existing operational cesspool is located near the Children s Learning Center in
the arboretum It is proposed to locate the new septic system in the same area as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work was to obtain an overview of the subsurface conditions at
the proposed absorption bed site and to perform field percolation tests to evaluate the
subsoil infiltration characteristics to formulate preliminary recommendations for the design
of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal system The work was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated Our
scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of hand operated drilling equipment and
two operators to the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of two boreholes each to a depth of about 5 3 feet
below the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profile
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
field engineer geologist
4 Performance of field percolation tests in the drilled borings to evaluate the
subsoil infiltration characteristics for the design of the disposal components
of the on site sewage disposal system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our project geologist
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

DISCUSSION SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Borings
A total of two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled at the project
site for percolation testing The borings were drilled to a depth of about 5 3 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using hand auger equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Subsurface Conditions
The borings encountered soft to medium stiff moist brown to dark brown clayey silts
to the maximum depth explored of about 5 3 feet below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the borings
drilled Groundwater levels may vary significantly depending on tidal fluctuation seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation tests were performed in the borings in general accordance with
the provisions of the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards
to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal
The percolation test results are presented on the Site Evaluation Percolation Test forms
Plates B 1 and B 2
The percolation tests were performed in the silty subsoils by drilling 3 5 inch
diameter boreholes to depths of about 5 3 feet below the existing ground surface During
testing the holes were carefully filled with clean water to a minimum depth of 12 inches
above the bottom of the hole The time intervals and water level drops were recorded to
provide field data upon which calculation of the percolation tests were based
Based on the percolation test results obtained from our field testing it appears that
the percolation rate of the shallow subsoils is about 3 minutes per inch which is relatively
good Therefore it is our opinion that the infiltration characteristic of the subsurface soils is
suitable for the use of a shallow depth absorption bed and seepage pit system Based on

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussion submitted in this report are based upon information
obtained from limited field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussion provided in
this report
The boring locations indicated in this report are approximate having been taped
from features shown on the Site Plan provided by Wilson Okamoto dated
The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented on the logs of borings were based on
measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic representations
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall
temperature and other factors
