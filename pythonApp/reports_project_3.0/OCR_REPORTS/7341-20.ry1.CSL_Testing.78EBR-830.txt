CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using 
the CHAMP-Q CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Pile Dynamics, Inc. (PDI). 
Crosshole sonic logging tests were performed between all the possible access tube 
combinations in the drilled shafts (5 tubes resulting in 10 profiles). The northernmost 
access tube was designated as Access Tube No. 1, and subsequent tubes were 
numbered in a clockwise direction. 
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 4.9 and 
5.0 feet above the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for 
Shaft Nos. 3 and 4, respectively. We understand that potable water was used to fill the 
access tubes after concrete placement. Based on our observations in the field, the water 
levels within the access tubes were above the top of the shaft concrete in the access tubes 
at the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the 
following values are generally used to determine the Concrete Condition Rating. The 
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, are presented on 
Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 and 4.2. The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile) 
and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 5.1 through 5.20. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10 percent, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of 
less than 10 percent, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all profiles for Shaft 
Nos. 3 and 4. It should be noted that changes in the relative energy can sometimes 
indicate defects depending on the degree of the reduction and the associated increase in 
FAT. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for 
Shaft Nos. 3 and 4 ranged from 11,598 to 12,866 and 11,753 to 13,073 feet per second. 
Based on the results of the CSL test and FAT increases of less than 10 percent, we 
believe that the concrete integrity of Shaft Nos. 3 and 4 within the testing depth meet a 
Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the above CCRC. Based on our 
review of the CSL testing data and no significant defect/anomaly indicated by the CSL test, 
we recommend acceptance of Shaft Nos. 3 and 4 within the testing depth.
