PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that at the present time no decision has
been reached regarding the location size and type of structure that will
be included in the complex development However because of various
limitations imposed on the project it has been felt that a single floor
basement structure connected to and in the vicinity of the existing State
Library may be a more likely option but other alternatives involving
two story structures are also being considered
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the Phase I investigation was to carry out a review
study of the existing library building foundation plans and available
soil boring information in the vicinity of the project in order to
1 Provide general soil data for use in siting and determining the
number of basement levels of the proposed library basement
extension and expansion
2 Plan the subsequent second phase of the geotechnical
investigation of the site
3 To identify potential design and construction problems relating
to the basement excavation and connection of the new basement to
the existing basement

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the Ewa side of Punchbowl Street
between King Street and the former Hotel Street makai of the State
Capital Building Plate 2
Presently the existing library structure is a two story building
with a single story basement located beneath part of the building Makai
of the building is 32 000 square feet of scenic open grassed area with
trees On the mauka side of the building is about 14 000 square feet of
generally grass and tree covered area
The existing ground gently slopes down towards the southwest King
Street The ground surface elevations vary from about 14 27 feet at the
Junction of Hotel and Punchbowl Streets to about 13 51 feet at the
junction of King and Punchbowl Streets
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
After formation of the Koolau Volcano which built up the eastern
side of Oahu ceased about 2 million years ago Macdonald et al 1983
the coastal plain along the southern shores of Oahu was formed by a
complex series of erosional and depositional processes
Numerous sea level variations caused the emergence and submergence
of the land mass that produced coral reef deposits at various levels that
can be identified at both above and below the present day sea level

The lowering of the sea level during periods of Ice Age stages
permitted the mountain streams to cut channels through the predominantly
coral reef deposits
The next rise in the sea level resulted in the redeposition of
alluvial deposits in these channels At least two such buried channels
were identified in the general vicinity of the project area The extent
of these buried channels was difficult to identify because of the limited
data that was available
A major buried channel is believed to run in an approximately
northwest to southeast direction from the Punchbowl and Beretania Streets
junction beneath part of the new State Office Building and along the
vicinity of Kapiolani Boulevard Plates 2 and 3
A boring B 12 drilled at the southwestern portion of the new State
Office Building site indicated that a second smaller buried channel
probably exists in the vicinity but the extent of this channel is not
known Plates 2 and 3
A third shallower 14 feet or more deep buried channel may exist
immediately north of the project site as indicated by borings E F and
12 drilled along the southern edge of the State Capital site Plate 3
The extent of this channel is not known but is likely to extend into the
project area
During or subsequent to the erosion of these alluvial channels the
Tantalus Punchbowl and Roundtop eruptions Honolulu Volcanic Series
deposited cinder and ash deposits over the vicinity of the project site
These deposits were probably contemporaneous with the Pleistocene sea
level changes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
At the present time no decision has been reached regarding the
location size and type of structure that will be included in the complex
development Because of various limitations imposed on the proejct it
has been felt that a single level basement connected to and in the
vicinity of the existing State Library may be a likely option However
it is also our understanding that alternative options involving a
two story structure at the back of the existing building or in the
existing court yard area are also being considered The two story
structure may include a single level or two level basement
The pertinent soil data and subsurface conditions required for the
design of building foundation and basement excavation are not available at
this stage of the investigation Exploratory borings and soil sampling
are required to obtain more detailed information on the subsurface profile
and pertinent engineering properties of the anticipated ground conditions
which may be encountered during construction
Foundation and Settlement Considerations
Based on existing available boring data coral reef material may be
present 8 to 10 feet beneath the existing ground surface The underlying
unweathered coral reef is expected to have good bearing capacity although
pockets of loose sand may exist in local areas If the coral reef ledge
is laterally continuous and sufficiently thick throughout the site
minimal settlements are anticipated for structures supported on this
layer

The presence of buried stream channel s may result in a significant

CONSIDERATIONS FOR SINGLE LEVEL BASEMENT
If a single level basement structure option is chosen the depth of
excavation for basement construction is anticipated to be about 10 feet
below ground surface Several methods of excavation and ground support
may be used
1 Qpen cut Excavation If properly designed open cut is probably
the most enconomical method of excavation however this would
require the use of a cut slope which would reduce the effective
building space inside the excavation Depending on the soil
shear strength and the position of the ground water table the
temporary cut slope may vary from 45 to 20 degrees If the
proposed complex development is constructed at the back of the
existing building the existing basement wall can be exposed and
temporary cut slopes would only be needed on the remaining three
sides of the excavation
2 Sheet Pile Supported Excavation In order to excavate close to
the project site boundary an excavation supported by sheet
piles can be carried out Because a coral reef ledge is
expected to exist 8 to 10 feet below ground surface the

CONSTDERATION FOR TWO LEVEL BASEMENT
If a two level basement is to be constructed in the project site
the depth of excavation for basement construction is anticipated to he
about 20 feet below ground surface The construction_costs will be
substantially higher because of the following
a Proper underpinning and shoring to protect the existing building
foundations will be required
b Basement excavation using the open cut method will not be
practicable
c Sheet piles will not be able to adequately penetrate into the
coral reef ledge and expensive alternative support for the
basement excavation may involve drilling and placing
cast in place soldier piles with lagging placed between the
piles during excavation
d The bottom of the basement excavation will be about 8 to 10 feet
below the groundwater table and construction dewatering in
porous coral reef material can be very expensive and difficult
The adverse effects of dewatering on the settlement of adjacent
structures and utilities will need to be evaluated and 2
monitored
The geotechnical investigation costs for a proposed two story
basement will also be substantially higher than that for a single story
basement Additional numbers of deeper borings and field pump out tests
may be required to determine excavation support requirements and the
feasibility of construction dewatering
