2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be
late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age It is believed that Waianae became extinct
while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below the Koolau
lavas in central Oahu
The Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene
Epoch The dips of the lava beds range from 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows on the
plateau have weathered to depths of 50 to 100 feet and are characterized by the red
and brown colors of the soil The project site is near the center of the Schofield Plateau

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets fill material requirements
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement
subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent Where
shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
thoroughly moistening the soil to close cracks or preparing again as recommended
above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas
and replacement with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may
be used as fill provided that it meets fill material requirements Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Fills and Backfills
The fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials The fill material below 5 feet
from finished subgrade may consist of larger sized material 12 inches in largest
dimension provided that the material is well graded In general the on site silty
clay clayey silt materials may be used as general fill materials

Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to the City and County of Honolulu Department of

Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by using sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment

critical elements of earthwork Geolabs should conduct observations and perform

3 4 Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface soils with low swelling potential Unless floor slabs are properly designed
and constructed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in
the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the
shrinking and swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend keeping the subgrade soils
within about 2 percent above the optimum moisture content prior to final concrete
placement Conventional slab on grade design may be utilized above the properly
prepared subgrade soils
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help to stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling and softening of the subgrade
soils
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundation
We recommend placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill below the
slab The cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB should provide more uniform support of the slabs and
should serve as a capillary break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of
the gravel or BTB cushion layer Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl
should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking It is also

The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems be installed around the house structures and to divert
the discharge away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Gravel strips adjacent
to slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils To reduce the
potential for water infiltration the foundation excavations should be backfilled properly
against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete Drainage swales should
be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away
from walls and foundations
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help to stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils The sidewalk design should follow the recommendations for exterior
concrete walkways and street sidewalks in the Slabs On Grade section of this report

Three one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were run on

these tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth

to evaluate the shear strength parameters The test results are presented on Plates B 3
and B 4
Four Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected
in situ cohesive soil samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the
soils The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth and graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plates B 5 through B 8

characteristics of the soils The sample was remolded to near the optimum moisture
content of the soils Compaction of the sample was performed by dropping a 10 pound
