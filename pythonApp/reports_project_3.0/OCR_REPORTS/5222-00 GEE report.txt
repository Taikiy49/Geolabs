PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves a new comfort station waterline and sewerline at
Pier 1 within Kahului Harbor on the Island of Maui Hawaii The planned improvements
involve the demolition of Pier 1 mauka comfort station and the replacement with a
facility that is up to Americans with Disability Act ADA standards with sufficient
capacity to accommodate existing cruise passenger and stevedore needs
The wastewater disposal cesspools at Pier 1 also need to be phased out
including at least one disposal system along Ala Luina Street which will be replaced
with a new sewer system The new wastewater disposal system will be connected to
the municipal wastewater system at a manhole on Ala Luina Street near the driveway
to the maintenance loop A lift station and force main are likely required somewhere
along the alignment due to the length of run and the flat topography
The Pier 1 waterline is also aged and too small to satisfactorily service up to
two cruise vessels that occasionally berth simultaneously The waterline needs to be
replaced with a larger waterline and connected to the 12 inch waterline along

designated as Boring Nos 1 thru 7 extending to depths of approximately 7 5 to 26 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by medium
dense to dense silty gravel and sands throughout the depths explored Based on the
generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we envision that
slabs on grade with thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new
comfort station anticipated for the above project Based on the sandy soil conditions
encountered in the borings special attention should be given to the site preparation
recommendations to compact the subgrade prior to the construction of new improvements
Detailed discussion and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report
Slab On Grade Foundations
We envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will likely
be used to support the proposed new comfort station anticipated for the above project
Based on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils or select granular fill material This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces In general thickened edge footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The thickened
edges of concrete walkway slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
6 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
We recommend that the bottom of footings bear on the firm and unyielding
near surface soils The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and unyielding bearing
surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing
should be extended down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Designation D 1557

If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement If foundations are designed
and constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total settlements
to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or select
granular fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless
covered by pavemenis or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected
In general we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a
minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill The cushion fill should consist of open graded
gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation for more uniform
support of the slab and to serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for
moisture infiltration through the slab concrete an impervious moisture barrier
membrane should be provided below the slab
The subgrade soils supporting the slabs on grade should be scarified to a depth
of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should be
firm and unyielding prior to the placement of the cushion fill Subgrade preparation and
footing excavations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs prior to
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
Site Preparation
Based on our field observation we anticipate that minimal site grading work on the
order of less than 1 foot will be required for construction of the new comfort station at the
project site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared at the on set of earthwork Vegetation old foundations debris deleterious
materials and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if
appropriate

After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to
a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by
slab cushion fill or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
compacted granular fill material
In general the on site sandy soils generated from the excavations may be used as
a source of fill materials At this time we do not anticipate that imported fill materials for
grading purposes will be required for this project However if imported fill materials will be
necessary the imported fill materials should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in maximum dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use Imported fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
Utility Trench
New utility lines will be required for the proposed project In general granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of
the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils or
select granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of
the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new comfort station should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should also
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the
foundations and slabs

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not

necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations on the logs of borings
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
