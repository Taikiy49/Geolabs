2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano
ceased lava flows from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope
forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is
located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project
site and to the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness
of the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1
Highway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves
as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern
Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the
present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the Village 5 Parcel 3 project site is generally
underlain by about 2 to 11 feet of stiff to very hard silty clay with sand and gravel
overlying medium dense to dense coralline deposits extending to the maximum depth
explored of 22 feet below the existing ground surface Our laboratory tests indicate that
the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate to high shrink swell characteristics when
subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents Therefore special attention
should be given to the site preparation recommendations for the site grading and
foundation design to account for the moderately to highly expansive soil conditions at
the site
Based on the generally stiff subsurface soil conditions encountered we believe
that a shallow foundation system slabs on grade with thickened edge footings and
spread footings may be used to support the proposed building structures Due to the
moderate to highly expansive soil conditions at the site we recommend that the
expansive clayey soils be capped with a minimum of 18 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material below the building slab areas We also recommend that 18 inches
of non expansive select granular fill material be provided below the driveways walkway
and lanai slabs In addition special attention should be given to the recommended
procedures for subgrade soil preparation below slabs and pavements to reduce the
potential shrink swell effects of the near surface clayey soils
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

3 2 Slabs On Grade
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that slabs on grade with thickened edge footings may be used to support the one to
two story residential and building structures planned for the project Our exploration
indicates that the site is generally underlain by moderate to highly expansive clayey
soils at or near the existing ground surface Unless slab on grade foundations
supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to the residential and building structures resulting from wetting and subsequent
swelling of the expansive clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select granular fill material
capping fill be provided for a minimum depth of 18 inches below the building slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the building slab a
minimum of 2 feet The perimeter thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The embedment of the
interior thickened edge footings may be reduced to a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the adjacent finished grade The base of the thickened edge should have a
minimum width of 12 inches
The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom
longitudinal steel reinforcement In addition the concrete slab should be reinforced with
reinforcing steel bars The project structural engineer should be consulted for

barrier and as a perimeter bearing wall footing
Prior to placement of the non expansive select granular fill material the soil
subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density

The slab subgrades including the select granular fill should be kept moist until
the placement of cushion fill and concrete The select granular fill material should
provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive
subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In
our opinion the reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should
reduce the impact of shrink swell effects on the slab due to potential heaving of the
underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be incorporated
below the concrete slab The cushion fill should consist of free draining materials such
as No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a
capillary moisture break The slab subgrade should be thoroughly wetted prior to
concrete placement to allow the select granular fill layer to remain in a moist condition
To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered on the slab
since they can better mask minor slab cracking It is also recommended that interior
walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amounts of
possible ground movements

3 6 Utilities
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular
material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site
soils as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to
the finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty clay soils or select
granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of
the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to

3 7 Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the residential and building structures should be
sloped to shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding
It is also advised that gutter systems be installed around the residential and building
structures and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement
areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be
avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations should be avoided Planters
next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce
water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from walls and
foundations
Consideration should be given to construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk adjacent
to and surrounding the entire residential structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help to stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the residential
and building structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and
softening of the subgrade soils The sidewalk design should follow the
recommendations for exterior concrete walkways and street sidewalks in the
Slabs On Grade section of this report
