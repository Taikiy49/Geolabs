Soil Engineering Report
Lot 18 Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing of the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden framed structure
approximately 44 x 84 feet in plan dimension with a two car garage
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the driveway garage the
entire rear lower level and portions of the main floor level of the
house

Site Conditions
The subject lot is at the corner of Leialii and Lanikuaka a Streets
in the Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II located in Aiea on the island
of Oahu Hawaii
There is a 20 foot wide level pad at the front portion of the lot
the ground then slopes downward towards the south at about 5 1 H V
slope ratio to a second level pad The ground then slopes downward at
about 2 1 H V slope ratio to the rear property line which is adjacent
to the Public Park
Subsurface Soil Conditions
Three borings were drilled to depths of 6 5 to 11 5 feet below the
existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate l
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth drilled
A review of the mass grading plan prepared by Community Planning
Inc dated for Royal Summit Subdivision Phase II
indicated that this lot received up to 29 feet of compacted fills
No groundwater was encountered during our investigation

Discussion and Recommendations
General
The very stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the very stiff clayey silt soil
indicate that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive off site select borrow fill and that the
subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the top of the footing out to
the slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help
resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended
bearing capacity of the soil

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral The on site soil under the garage
driveway and lanai areas should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch
minimum thickness of the above specified non expansive select fill
capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris Benching into the stiff on site soil
is required prior to keying the new fill lifts on ground steeper than
5 1 H V
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
expansive heave if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil should
be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom
thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported
select borrow fills as previously discussed The thickened edge of the
slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place soil below
the upper capping select fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally up to 10
feet high may be required to provide rear yard fill retainage for the
proposed residence The guidelines for the retaining wall designs are
listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
granular backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade If located on a 2 1
H V steep sloping area the foundation should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a minimum 6 feet horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the top of
footing horizontal cut to the slope face

3 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff
on site soil
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the

7 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be _ properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water diverted far away
from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing
immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding
condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of
all other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions
such as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas
and may require additional probing or corrections in the field
Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during construction
to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency fund is
thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
