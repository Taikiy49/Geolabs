INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new development at 922 Wiliwili Street in the Moiliili area on
the island of Oahu The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2713 00 Page 2
our proposal to Cheng dated May 17 1991 This report summarizes the
findings from the exploration and our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis
for preparation of construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing four 4 buildings at the site and construct
a new three 3 story building with asphaltic concrete pavement parking at the ground
level Based on the plans provided to us it appears that post and beam type of
construction will be used Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal to Cheng
dated May 17 1991 The scope of work comprised the following items of work
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surface soil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2713 00 Page 3
3 Drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 20 0 to 21 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing foundation and pavement design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in
the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 922 Wiliwili Street at the junction of Wiliwili and
Algaroba Street in the Moiliili area of Honolulu Currently the site is occupied by four 4
buildings The existing parking lots front Algaroba Street No topographic map of the site
was available at the time of our field exploration the site is generally level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths of 20 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2713 00 Page 4
on June 12 and 14 1991 The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The exploration results indicate that the subsoils are comprised of about one 1
to four 4 feet of stiff fill material consisting of clayey silt with sand overlying medium
dense to dense coral sand and gravel to the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered at between 3 5 to 5 7 feet below the ground surface
in the borings at the time of our field exploration It is anticipated that the groundwater
level will vary with tidal fluctuation rainfall and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff fill material overlying medium dense
to dense coral sand and gravel Some amount of fill may be needed to raise the existing
surface elevations Spread footing type of foundations may be used for the proposed
new structure
Site Grading
A grading plan of the project site was not available at the time of our field
exploration or preparation of this report Based on a topographic map provided and
observation of site conditions made during our reconnaissance of the project site it is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2713 00 Page 5
anticipated that imported fill will be required to raise the site up to about two 2 feet for
drainage considerations
_ Itis recommended that a preliminary grading plan of the project site be forwarded
when available to the project geotechnical engineer for review with respect to the
considerations on which the design recommendations presented in this report are based
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and pavements and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to firm natural ground and the resulting depression backfilled with
fill material properly compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended
above
Imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular materials less
than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the No 200 sieve All
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior
to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78

The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footings may be used for the proposed new structure It is
anticipated that the footings will bear on compacted fill or recompacted in situ material
A minimum footing embedment of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade is recommended An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
foundation design The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
If foundations are located next to existing or proposed sewer or drainage lines
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trenches or as deep as the inverts
of the utility lines The anticipated footing bearing surfaces at the bottom of footing
excavations should be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The properly prepared footing bottoms should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been obtained prior to
placement of reinforcement and concrete For footings properly constructed and observed
as recommended above the estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
1 2 inch total and one fourth 1 4 inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2713 00 Page 7
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used to calculate frictional resistance
between the bottom of the foundations and the bearing soils Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soils should be
neglected
Pavement Sections
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the ground level parking Based on
the anticipated subsoil conditions and need for some amount of fill the following
preliminary pavement sections may be used for design
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course at 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction to verify the adequacy of the above sections The above pavement sections
assumes medium traffic and that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved area

Utilities
New underground utility lines may be anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is
recommended under the pipes and around the lower one half 1 2 of the pipe
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence
It is considered to be the contractor s responsibility to protect all existing utility lines
during construction
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for
bid This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated during preparation of this report The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from the project boundaries and existing structures indicated on the topographic
map dated by Cummins and Cummins Land Surveying and Mapping
Consultants Inc The physical locations of the field borings should be considered
GEOLABS HAWAII

accurate only to the degree implied by the method used The surface elevation of the
field borings were estimated from the same map
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Ground water level data was collected during our field exploration at the times
given on the graphic representations of the borings and or in the text of this report This
data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Cheng and
his consultants for specific application to the proposed new three 8 story structure at
922 Wiliwili Street in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the

Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4 borings to
depths of 20 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill these borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler for the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the corresponding sample depth
