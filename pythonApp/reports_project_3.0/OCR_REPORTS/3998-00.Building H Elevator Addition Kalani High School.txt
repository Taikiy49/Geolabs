INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Proposed Building H Elevator Addition located within the Kalani High
School campus north of Kalanianaole Highway in the Kahala Waialae area in the District
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3998 00 Page 2
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations slabs and
fill placement only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new elevator elevator room and mechanical room for
Building H The elevator will have a carrying capacity of 2 000 pounds and will serve the
first and second floors of Building H The elevator room will be about 7 feet by 8 7 feet in
plan dimensions and the mechanical room will be about 6 3 feet by 8 7 feet in plan
dimensions Based on the information provided the finish floor elevation for the
elevator room will be 4 feet below the existing ground elevation to accommodate the
hydraulic equipment and the mechanical room will be near the existing ground
elevation Both rooms will be connected to each other and structurally tied into the
adjacent Building H structure
Building loads are not known at this time however it is estimated that the building
loads and the down thrust load from the elevator hydraulic equipment are light

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age Ice Age and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3998 00 Page 4
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau shield reached the
end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Koolau Range and is
composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava from the Koolau Volcanic Series
These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt
interbedded with thin clinker layers The lava flows have weathered in place and are
characterized by the red and orange colors of the soil The brown and gray soils are
generally ancient alluvium deposited over the surface

The proposed building addition is located on a nearly level area at the top of a fill
slope approximately 6 feet high The ground surface slopes downward from Building H
to the sidewalk and driveway at a ratio of about four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V
The site is currently covered with a strip of plants along the existing building and
with lawn grass further away from the building The site is bounded by an extension of
the lanai and a stairway to the west Building H to the north and the top of the slope to
the south The proposed building addition will be located between two columns of
Building H

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the proposed building
addition may be supported on the on site soils However to reduce the potential for
distress resulting from swelling of the clayey materials we recommend that the footings
be extended to bear on the underlying stiff orange gray clay with boulders at 5 feet below
the existing top of slope elevation
Our laboratory testing indicated that the brownish gray adobe clay fill has high
expansion potential while the orange gray clay has moderate expansion potential To
reduce the potential for distress to slabs on grade resulting from swelling of the clayey
materials we recommend that non expansive structural fill material be used below the
building addition slabs on grade to 5 feet below the existing grade

Foundations
Spread and wall footing foundations bearing on the stiff on site material at 5 feet
below the existing grade may be utilized for the building addition
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the underlying stiff orange gray
clay with boulders Footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches Footings at
different elevations should be constructed as stepped footings Footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet
measured from the outside edge of footings to the slope face
The above bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on the underlying stiff
orange gray clay with boulders as recommended herein to be less than inch total
Differential settlement is estimated to be less than inch

should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
should be backfilled with concrete or structural fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the
underlying competent material
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear evident in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement

Retaining Walls
Based on the finish floor elevation the building walls will act as retaining walls
under restrained conditions For design of retaining structures an equivalent fluid earth
pressure of 65 pcf may be used
Surcharge and hydrostatic loads are not accounted for in the above values
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges live loads and point loads should be
considered in the design
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3998 00 Page 8
For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a
rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 60 percent of the vertical
surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall may be used for design
Additional analyses during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of
point loads and line loads
Backfill behind retaining walls should consist of structural fill material compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Over compaction of the retaining wall
backfill should be avoided
Unless covered by concrete the upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of low
to moderately expansive fine grained soil free of organics compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Moderately expansive fine grained soils are defined as a
soil with more than 50 percent passing the No 200 sieve and with an air dried ring swell
of less than 8 percent The on site orange brown clayey silt topsoil may be used for this
purpose The on site brownish gray clay is not suitable for this purpose
Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the building site is generally underlain by 5 feet
of highly expansive brownish gray adobe clay fill followed by moderately expansive
orange gray clay There is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in the
moisture content
To reduce the potential for distress to slabs on grade resulting from swelling of
the clayey materials we recommend that non expansive structural fill material be used
below the building addition slabs on grade to 5 feet below the existing grade
For the exterior walkways we recommend 24 inches of non expansive structural
fill material be used below the slabs on grade Control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3998 00 Page 9
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundation
The structural fill should be placed immediately after moisture conditioning and
compaction of the clayey subgrade soils The prepared structural fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Grading section of this report Compaction requirements for the subgrade
soils and structural fill are also discussed in the same section of this report
It is our understanding that the elevator room and mechanical room will not have
floor coverings however if floor coverings are utilized we offer the following
recommendations
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be placed below
the slab The cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB should provide more uniform support
of the slabs and should serve as a capillary break To reduce future moisture
infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture
vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel or
BTB cushion layer Consideration may be given to the placement of 2 inches of
moist sand or BTB placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such
as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor
slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to
incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amounts of possible ground
movements

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with structural fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and grubbing the orange brown clayey silt should be excavated and
stockpiled for future use as backfill for the upper 12 inches behind retaining walls
The soil material within the proposed building limits should be excavated to 5 feet
below the existing grade The soil material below the proposed sidewalk should
be excavated to 2 feet below the bottom of slab elevation
After excavation the subgrade should be proof rolled or compacted to 85 to
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3998 00 Page 11
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks or prepared again as
recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
engineered fill
Fills and Backfills
Structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
and should be free of organics The material should have a laboratory CBR value
of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Structural fill materials should be moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction

critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be

Drainage
The finished grades outside the structure should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAI

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided
as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface water run off away
from slabs and foundations
