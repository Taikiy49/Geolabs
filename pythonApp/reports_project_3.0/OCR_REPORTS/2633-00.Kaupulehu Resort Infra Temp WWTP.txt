INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the site grading and foundation design of the proposed Temporary Sewage
Treatment Plant for the Kaupulehu Resort Infrastructure
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soils and rock conditions at the project site in order to obtain data for the
formulation of preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of the site grading and the foundations for the proposed structures
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tions of the surface conditions and to select locations for the borings
3 Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and water wagon with operators to
and from the project site
4 Drilling and sampling a total of six 6 borings at the proposed project site
to depths ranging between twenty 20 and thirty 30 feet below the existing
ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2633 00 Page 3
5 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Geologist from our firm
6 Engineering analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the proposed
temporary sewage treatment plant
7 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendation
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated December
17 1990
A detailed description of our field exploration is presented in the appendix of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the construction of a temporary wastewater
treatment plant which will include the following structures
1 An aeration equalization basin and associated pump station and grinder vault The
basin will have a bottom elevation of 49 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL and a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2633 00 Page 4
top elevation of 61 feet above MSL The aeration basin will require cuts of up to
seventeen 17 feet in depth to reach finish floor elevation
2 A control building and belt press with finish floor at 55 feet above MSL Cuts of
between nine 9 and seventeen 17 feet will be required to reach the finish floor
elevation of these structures
3 A final clarifier with an associated chlorinator pump with a bottom elevation of 53
feet above MSL Excavations of up to seven 7 feet in depth will be required to
reach the finish floor elevation
4 A perimeter berm and an unsurfaced access road The perimeter berm will require
fills of up to twelve 12 feet in vertical height along its northwest corner
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the west coast of the island of Hawaii in the district
of Kaupulehu The site is presently bounded on the north and east by an improved
access road but is otherwise surrounded by barren undeveloped land with virtually no
vegetation The project area is characterized by basaltic lava flows which are predomi
nantly pahoehoe with the exception of the southwest corner which is covered by a more
recent a a flow Elevations at the project site range from approximately 74 feet above
MSL in the southwest corner to 50 feet above MSL in the northeast corner The general
location of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Vicinity Map Plate 1 The
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five 5 shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea both still active
The project site is located on the northern flank of the Hualalai volcano which
makes up the west central portion of the island and consists partly of the prehistoric
basaltic lava flows of the Hualalai Volcanic Series which were most likely deposited during
the late Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and MacDonald 1946
Hawaii volcanic activity is typified by an abundance of flank eruptions along rift
zones or zones of weakness in the earth s crust The project site is situated in the
northwest rift zone of Hualalai
The lava flows at the site are pahoehoe basalt types with dense zones of hard rock
and loose zones of clinker The southwest corner of the project area is covered with a
more recent a a flow overlying and intermixed with older pahoehoe flows The clinker
phase is highly permeable Lava tubes are generally not encountered in a a flows but
are not uncommon in the pahoehoe flows

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering viewpoint The volcanic formation encountered at the site should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed wastewater treatment plant and related site work
However due to the potential existence of cavities within the volcanic rock encountered
at the site we recommend that a program of probing and grouting at foundation locations
be conducted during construction
Cavities
Our reconnaissance and exploration encountered a number of surface blisters and
two 2 subsurface cavities of one 1 to two 2 feet in vertical height However the
potential for larger and more frequent occurrence of cavities is not uncommon in the type
of rock formation composing the project site It is our opinion that proofrolling with a
minimum of ten 10 passes with a heavy bulldozer such as D 9 would be sufficient to
detect and collapse most of the near surface voids below deep fill areas i e fills greater
that five 5 feet thick Collapsed voids should be backfilled with rock fill or imported

To detect and define the extent of voids or cavities below foundations we
recommend that a program of probing and grouting be implemented Probes should be
drilled to a minimum depth of ten 10 feet below the bottom of the foundations at each
column footing locations and on ten 10 foot intervals below strip footings If a void is
encountered and it is suspected to be too large to grout additional probes may be
performed to delineate the horizontal area which may be affected
The probe holes and any voids discovered should be backfilled with a mortar like
are discovered which are too large to be conveniently backfilled with grout they may be
collapsed all loose material removed and backfilled with properly compacted material
less than twelve 12 inches in maximum size
The probing grouting and backfilling should be performed under the observation
of the project geotechnical engineer to monitor proper filling of the probe holes and voids
and to provide program modifications without undue delay if it is found to be necessary

Site Preparation
Prior to placement of embankment the area to receive fill should be cleared of all
deleterious materials which should be removed from the project site In areas to
receive fill the subgrade should be proofrolled with a D 9 bulldozer or similar
heavy equipment to detect and collapse near surface voids Loose areas or voids
disclosed during site preparation should be repaired by collapsing the void
removing the loose material and backfilling with suitable compacted on site
materials
The proofrolling and backfilling should be monitored by the project geotechnical
engineer to verify discovery and proper backfilling of voids and cavities that may
be present
Embankment
Construction of embankments for this project may be conveniently considered with
regard to the various materials to be used and the zones in which they are intended to
be placed This is not to be constructed as a suggestion for classification of embankment
for bidding or payment purposes

construction envelope and interior backfill behind retaining structures and

Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts with a maximum height
of twenty four 24 inches in general fill areas and eighteen 18 inches thick within
two 2 feet below structural foundations general fills should be compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Fills and backfills within two 2 feet of structural foundations should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in embankments
constructed of volcanic rock material due to the large particle sizes Instead a
testing program consisting of visual observations and evaluation of test strips to
determine the lift thickness and number of passes of a compactor required to
achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes may then be used as the criteria for compaction

Foundations
We believe the in situ rock formations resulting from the above site grading will
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed structures
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structures
In the event that foundations are to bear on cut areas exhibiting varying rock
conditions bearing on both hard basalt rock and loose clinker we recommend that the
portion under the proposed structure found on the hard basalt rock be ripped or scarified
to a depth of twelve 12 inches below the footing subgrade backfilled with well graded
material less than twelve 12 inches in size and then compacted as indicated above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2633 00 Page 14
Foundations bearing on in site clinker should be re compacted by conventional
mechanical means to density potential loose areas
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum embedment for interior
footings may be reduced to twelve 12 inched below the finished grade Footings
located next to utility trenches should extend down to a grade equal to or deeper than
that of the bottom of the trench
For foundations bearing on compacted backfill an allowable net soil bearing
pressure of up to 3000 p s f may be used This allowable bearing value may be
increased to 6000 p s f on hard rock formation These values my be increased by one
third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
To resist lateral forces imposed on the foundations an equivalent fluid pressure
of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 4 may be utilized to
determine the sliding resistance of the structure
Slab thickened edges and spread footing foundations located close to the tops of
slope should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimum horizontal setback distance
of ten 10 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away from building
foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance with the above
recommendations should experience negligible settlements
