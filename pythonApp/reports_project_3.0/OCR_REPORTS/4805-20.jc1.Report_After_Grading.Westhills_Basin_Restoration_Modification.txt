This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and compaction work for the Westhills Basin Restoration and
Modification project from July 14 through August 15 2014
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located east of the Westhills Phase III Subdivision in the
Makakilo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is also known as Kahiwelo Park The
project involved installing grouted rip rap inflow section repairing existing eroded slope
adjacent to the water quality basin and installing PVC perforated pipe and chain link
fences at existing water quality detention basin The layout of the basin restoration and
modification site is shown on the Grading Erosion Control Plan Plan Sheet 2 as
attached for reference
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of less than
about 3 feet thick The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation
and compaction testing during the earthwork for the Westhills Basin Restoration and
Modification only

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company Ltd is the general contractor for the project and
performed the site grading operations The site grading work consisted of clearing and
grubbing subgrade preparation and placement and compaction of fills and backfills
Following clearing and grubbing of the over grown areas of the previously mass
graded site the excavated subgrades were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted with a
sheepsfoot roller to obtain a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction of the maximum
dry density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to the minimum of 90 percent
of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified
Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 1
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 12 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plate 2 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the earthwork
construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered
by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials

construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
