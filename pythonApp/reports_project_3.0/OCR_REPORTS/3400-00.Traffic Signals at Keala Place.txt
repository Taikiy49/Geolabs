INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed South Kihei Road Improvements project at Keala Place in
the Kihei area on the island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of
pavement structures site grading and box culvert structure only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Maui the second largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago was formed by the
activity of two volcanoes East Maui or Haleakala and West Maui The broad gently
sloping plain connecting the two volcanoes known as the Maui Isthmus was formed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 5
when lavas of the Haleakala Volcano banked against the already existing West Maui
Volcano
The volcanic rocks of the West Maui Volcano have been divided into three
volcanic series The Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and comprises of the
basaltic lava flows and associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major
shield volcano The Wailuku Volcanic Series was covered by a thin discontinuous
frosting of andesitic and trachytic flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as
the Honolua Volcanic Series After a long period of inactivity where erosion incised
deep valleys into the volcanic flows volcanic activity returned with the eruptions that
produced the flows and cones of the Lahaina Volcanic Series
The East Maui Volcano or better known as the Haleakala Mountain makes up
the eastern portion of the island Haleakala Mountain is considered to be dormant with
the last eruption taking place on the southerly side of the mountain around 1790
Haleakala Mountain consists largely of the Honomanu Volcanic Series that built the
mountain from three rift Zones probably during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epochs
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Haleakala Mountain
The coastal portions of the Haleakala Mountain are flanked by fringing reefs and alluvial
deposits Land development and reclamation projects within the last century have
brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the project site is
underlain by medium dense sands over silty gravels In Boring Nos 1 and 3 a layer
of clayey silts of about 2 feet thick was encountered near the ground surface
Generally the soils encountered atthe site are competent and should provide adequate
support for the proposed intersection improvements and box culvert structure planned
for this project
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered we believe that the
planned box culvert structure may be supported on a minimum of 6 inches of free
draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
equivalent To install the new box culvert structure excavation of the existing ditch and
subsequent backfilling of about 5 to 7 feet relative to the existing ground surface around
the box culvert structure are anticipated

pavements site grading and box culvert construction for the proposed project are
presented in the following sections

Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete is planned for the new pavements for the
proposed project Based on the information provided to our office we understand that
the design traffic for the proposed roadway will have an average daily traffic ADT of
about 15 800 vehicles per day for two directions with 2 percent truck traffic However
the truck type information needed for pavement design was not available at the time
this report was prepared Therefore in order to perform pavement design assumptions
of truck type distribution were made based on the information from similar projects and
our experience in the area
Based on our field exploration clayey silt soils were encountered near the
ground surface in Boring Nos 1 and 3 Therefore we have made our preliminary
pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soils will consist of stiff silty soils
with a minimum CBR value of 12 and a maximum expansion value of 1 percent or less

pavement sections may be used for preliminary design purposes

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The subbase and base
course materials should also be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with AASHTO T D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
If silty soils are encountered at the subgrade level they should be properly
prepared and kept moist prior to placement of subbase and base course material If
shrinkage cracks appear on the subgrade prior to placement of subbase course the
subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction
We understand that sidewalks are planned for the proposed roadway
improvements To provide more uniform support of the concrete we recommend that
a minimum of 4 inches of select borrow be incorporated below the sidewalks The
subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches moisture

than 90 percent relative compaction The select borrow should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Construction joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections

Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading will generally consist of minimal cuts and fills on
the order of 1 to 2 feet relative to the existing ground surface for the proposed roadway
construction In general all grading work should conform to Section 200 of the
Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction Department
of Transportation State of Hawaii 1994 and the site specific recommendations
contained in this report
Site grading operations should be observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer
It is important that the geotechnical engineer observe the site grading operations to
evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris from the demolition rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials Any soft weak or yielding
areas disclosed during the clearing and grubbing operations should be over excavated
to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
engineered fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 12
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with AASHTO
T D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the subgrade to receive fills
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of fill material The subgrade in fill areas within 3 feet
below the finish pavement grade and in cut areas should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
Generally the granular on site materials may be reused as a source of general
fill and backfill materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension Soft and wet soils generated from the excavation of
the new box culvert structure should not be used as a source of fill and backfill
material Structural fill material aggregate subbase course base course and asphalt
concrete base course material should meet the material requirements as specified in
Section 700 of the Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction Department of Transportation State of Hawaii 1994
lf imported fill materials are required as general fill and backfill materials the
imported fill and or backfill material should consist of well graded granular materials of
less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material should have a laboratory
California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell
of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
In addition it should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 13
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
All permanent fill slopes should have slope inclinations of 2H 1V or flatter All
fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched Benching
should be of a sufficient width to permit operations of placing and compacting
equipment
Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optinum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The requirement for moisture conditioning to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content may be waived for granular materials The fill and backfill
material within the upper 3 feet below the pavement finish grade should be compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The silty subgrade should be kept
moist until covered by the aggregate subbase course
Observations and field density tests should be performed during grading to assist
the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture
content on each fill lift Where compaction is less than required additional compactive
effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the
specified compaction
Box Culvert Structure
Based on the information provided we understand that the existing box culvert
will connect to a new box culvert with dimensions of 7 by 4 feet and traversing the
proposed new Kalama Park Access Road We envision that a precast concrete box
culvert will be used for the construction Based on the subsurface soil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 14
encountered near the new box culvert location we anticipate that the proposed box
culvert structure would be underlain by medium dense to dense sandy soils To
provide more uniform support for the box culvert structure we recommend that the box
culvert structure be supported on a minimum of 6 inches of free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or equivalent
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for the design of the box culvert structure bearing on a minimum of 6 inches of
free draining granular bedding
The walls of the culvert structure should be designed for lateral earth pressure
for a restrained wall or an unyielding condition The lateral earth pressures acting on
the box culvert structure will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of backfill
and the compactive effort on the backfill material around the structures We
recommend that the new box culvert structure be designed for an equivalent fluid
below the groundwater level The above values assume that the backfill around the
box culvert structures is properly placed and compacted
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges traffic loads line loads and point
loads within a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the structure should be
considered in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the structure
a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 50 percent of the vertical
surcharge pressure acting over the entire depth of the structure may be used in design
Additional analyses during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of
point loads and line loads
Due to the proximity of the site to the Pacific Ocean and the relatively permeable
subsoils encountered we believe that the new box culvert structure will likely be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 15
installed in a wet condition If the box culvert installation is to be performed in a wet
condition we recommend that open graded granular materials be used as backfill
material around the box culvert structure up to about 1 foot above the water table The
backfill from 1 foot above the water table to the finish subgrade may consist of the
on site sandy soils or select granular material with 10 to 30 percent passing the No
200 sieve If box culvert is installed in a dry condition the on site sandy soils or select
granular material may be used as backfill up to the finish subgrade level In general
the on site sandy or select granular material should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
backfill material within the upper 3 feet below the finish pavement grade should be
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Utility Trench
Installation of utility lines may be needed for the proposed intersection improve
ments project It should be noted that groundwater was encountered within 3 feet of
the existing ground surface Therefore any trench excavation deeper than 3 feet will
likely encounter groundwater In general for support of the utility lines we recommend
that a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be used under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the
pipes or 1 foot above the groundwater level should consist of free draining backfills
such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes or
1 foot above the groundwater level to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should
consist of select granular material or the on site granular soils The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3400 00 Page 16
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement finish
grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was formulated to aid in evajuating pertinent
engineering properties of the soil and materials sampled
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
test results are summarized on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis test ASTM D 422 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is shown on Plate B 2
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR ASTM D 1883 tests were performed on
samples of the near surface soil to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement
subgrade support Results of the CBR tests are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
