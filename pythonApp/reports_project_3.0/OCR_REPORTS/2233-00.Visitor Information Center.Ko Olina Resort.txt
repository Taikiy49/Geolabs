INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Visitor Information Center for the
Ko Olina Resort in The general location of the site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed
in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction bidding
estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the area and construct a Visitor Information
Center for the Ko Olina Resort The facility will consist of two 2
one story concrete buildings with slab on grade and metal roof An
asphaltic concrete parking lot is also planned

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the foundation and pavement design of the
proposed Visitor Information Center

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject site is located near the central portion of the Ko Olina
Resort Development
The northern two thirds of the site has been filled with about ten
10 to fifteen 15 feet of coralline material The remainder of the
site is currently being filled to finished grade When completed the
site will be generally level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Four 4 borings were drilled and sampled at the site to depths of
about 6 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface The subsurface
conditions disclosed by our field exploration consisted of dense coralline
silty sand and gravel
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the borings are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation Support
We recommend that the structure foundations bear on the compacted
fill placed in accordance with the recommendations presented herein For
the design of foundations bearing on fill an allowable bearing capacity
of 2500 p s f may be utilized
The bearing value may be increased by one third when temporary wind
or seismic loads are considered
W O 2233 00 Page 4
Foundations should be embedded a minimm of twelve 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft
and or weak materials down to firm ground and backfilling of the resultant
void with fill material compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations
Site Grading
Fill placement is in progress at the site
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to a
maximm of ten 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Surface run off water from the slope should be diverted away from the
buildings and facilities The slope should be planted or otherwise
protected as soon as possible to reduce damage and unravelling by
erosion
Pavement Section
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete will be used for the
surface course of the parking areas The following preliminary pavement
section may be used
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The above pavement section assumes light traffic conditions and a
compacted subgrade shaped to drain

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may

The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by measuring from field marks provided by the Project Surveyor
Elevations of the data points were determined by interpolation from
elevations points on the Site Plan by Mitchell The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of about 6 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
