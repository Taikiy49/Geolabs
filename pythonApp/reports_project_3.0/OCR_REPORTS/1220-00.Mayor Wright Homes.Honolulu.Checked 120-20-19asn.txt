This report presents the results of our foundation investiga
tion for the proposed lanai and stairway improvements to the existing
Buildings 15 A 17 A and 18 C of Mayor Wright Homes in the Iwalei
district of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general area of the housing
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that three levels of lanai and related
Stairs are to be added to existing Building Nos 15 A 17 A and 18 C
The general layout of these buildings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 It is further understood that these buildings are supported
on piles

It is anticipated that the proposed lanai and stairway
additions will impose foundation loading of 37 5 kips per interior
column and 5 1 to 7 5 kips per linear foot of wall footings
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this investigation is to examine the subsurface
conditions at the proposed building addition areas and to provide
recommendations for the design of foundations We drilled 3 test
borings at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring 5A indicates the first attempted location of the third boring
where a storm drain was encountered at 5 foot depth and abandoned
A series of laboratory tests was conducted on selected soil
samples The swell consolidation and strength characteristics of
subsoils were examined for the foundation support considerations
Detailed description of our field exploration and boring logs
together with the results of our laboratory tests are presented in
the appendices The scope of our services is performed in general
accordance with our revised proposal dated This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
Mayor Wright Homes is a public low income housing project
located near the northwest corner at the intersection of North King
Street and Liliha Street in the Iwilei district of Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii The proposed improvements are located at the back of existing
Buildings 15 A 17 A and 18 C which front North King and Liliha
Streets
Ground surface elevations were not available to us at the
time of the investigation However the site is relatively flat but
slightly depressed from the surrounding areas The ground generally
slopes toward the southeast
Ground cover consisted mainly of lawn grass low bushes and
concrete sidewalks with some taller trees at the back of Building 18 C
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the findings we believe that the subsurface conditions
consist of 8 to 10 feet of Fills underlain by gray alluvial soils
The surface fills consisted of medium stiff to stiff mottled brown and
dark brown silty clay clayey silt and dense clayey gravel with
concrete rubble We believe that the quality of fill probably varies
from location to location Small voids were noted in clayey silt fill
at a depth of about 6 feet in Boring 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
The alluvial soils under the upper fill consist of dense clayey
sand stiff silty clay or clayey silt predominantly gray in color
All our borings encountered hard basalt boulders that prevented
further auger drilling
Groundwater encountered in the boring were measured and are
listed at the end of each boring on the log of borings in Appendix A
Generally water table is located 9 to 12 feet below the existing
ground level
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings and the recognition that pile foundation
support for the new addition would be costly due to the site and
building accessibility we believe that spread footing may be used for
the foundation support of the proposed lanai stairway additions Some
differential settlements should be anticipated between the existing
pile supported structures and the new additions Specific
recommendations for foundation and slab on grade supports are
presented in the following sections
Spread Footings
Due to moderately expansive nature of the underlying clay and
silt fill and apparently poor fill compaction partial over excavation
of existing soil below the footing level is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1220 00 Page 5
We recommend that over excavation under footings be extended
down at least 12 inches below the bottom of the footing and extended
outward another 12 inches horizontally beyond the edge of the
footings The bottom of the over excavation should first be moisture
conditioned to a moisture content of 3 to 5 percentage point wet of
the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a density of at least
90 of the maximum density as determined in accordance with the ASTM
D 1557 78 test procedure
It is important that the subgrade under the slab and footing be
kept moist throughout the excavation and backfilling operations to
prevent drying of the underlying clayey fill materials
The footing over excavation should be backfilled as soon as
possible and not be permitted to dry out excessively The footing
backfill material should consist of the select borrow granular
material meeting the requirements under Section 30 of the Standard
Specification of Public Works Construction City County of Honolulu
1975 This select backfill under footing should be placed in two
6 inch compacted lifts with minimum 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density
For the footings bearing on the above specified select borrow
an allowable soil bearing pressure of 2000 p s f may be used with an
one third increase allowed for wind or seismic loads The bottom of
footing should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
Any loose or soft spots which may be encountered at the bottom
of the footing over excavation should be removed and replaced with
select borrow granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum density
Excavations should be protected from surface run off that may
carry loose mud or other debris into the excavations All loose soil
or mud should be removed from the bottom of all footing excavations
prior to backfilling or concrete placement
For footings designed and constructed according to the above
recommendations it is estimated that the total and differential
settlements will not exceed one third of an inch over a distance of 15
feet Since the amount of settlements may vary at different
locations provisions should be incorporated in the design to
accomodate some movement of the new addition relative to the existing
pile supported structure
We recommend that all the footing excavations and backfilling
operations be inspected by the soils engineer to verify the foundation
soil conditions and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation

On Grade Slabs
To minimize the potental expansion problem associated with
the on site clay fill we recommend that at least 16 inches of
non expansive material including the cushion fill be placed over
the on site clay fill under the new on grade slab areas
Prior to placing the non expansive fill over the slab area the
subgrade soil should also be moisture conditioned as previously
recommended in the Spread Footing section
The fill should consist of non expansive clean granular
material such as select borrow or crusher run material The new fill
materials should be placed in 6 inch compacted lifts with minimum 903
of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
The cushion fill under the slab should be at least 4 inches
thick and consist of crushed rock such as No 3A Fine The material
should be compacted by a hand operated plate vibratory compactor
making at least 4 passes
If the subgrade soils are allowed to dry out there is a
possibility that the soils could shrink and then subsequently swell
and cause distresses to the structure Therefore the edges or sides
of the existing slabs and the underlying subgrade soils exposed during
construction should be carefully covered with plastic sheeting to
prevent loss of moisture from the soil Other methods such as
periodic wetting could also be used to minimize moisture loss

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the new additions should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavation backfilling
subgrade compaction and moistening operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon the above inspections
