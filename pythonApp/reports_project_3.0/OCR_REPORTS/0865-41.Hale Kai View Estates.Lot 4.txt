As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study
consisted of three 3 borings drilled within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story
wood structure approximately 53 feet long with widths of 27 to
46 feet and a 20 by 22 foot garage situated in front of the
house

Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage will
be set back about 15 feet from Kahena Street
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and
house
CMU retaining walls about 4 feet high are planned around the
house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 4 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of the
subdivision development about 300 feet northeast of Ainapo
Street It is bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest sub
division lots on the southeast and northwest and a concrete
drainage channel on the north The lot slopes downward from
Kahena Street toward the east at about 2 1 to 3 2 percent
gradient The elevation of Lot 4 generally varies from 136 to
142 feet The existing rectangular concrete drainage channel
is about 5 feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending
across the rear portion of the lot

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation the south
western corner of Lot 4 was covered with a large stockpile of
soil and boulders left over from previous grading operations in
the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down
to a depth of 3 to 6 feet below the design finished grade and
replaced with compacted available on site soils The available
on site soil consisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt
soils which had been mixed with some adobe clay pockets from
the previous stockpiles Minimal grading was required over the
remaining portion of the lot
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicate that Lot 4 is underlain by grayish
brown expansive clay to the full depth of the drilled borings
which was 4 8 to 11 5 feet
Detailed description of the soils encountered in borings are
presented on the attached Log of Borings Plates A 1 through
A 3 The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
attached Site Plan Plate l

Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate
that the soil can be classified as clay CH according to the
Unified Soil Classification System Laboratory one inch ring
swell tests indicate that the soil has a moderate to high
shrink swell potential The laboratory test results are sun

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is under
lain by grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on
this material indicated that they have high expansion potential
Therefore for Lot 4 the slab on grade and foundation con
struction would require special design procedures in order to
reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
The laboratory tests indicate that the on site soils have high
expansion potential To reduce the expansive effects of the
clay soils we recommend that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of
non expansive material be placed below the house garage and
lanai concrete slabs
In the garage areas where minimum site fill is required for
lot grading the expansive clay below and for a distance of 3
feet around the slabs should be removed for a minimum depth of
3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced with con
pacted non expansive off site borrow material such as quarry
waste or select borrow The underlying remaining clay soil
should be scarified moistened to above optimum moisture
content and recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to
backfilling with the 3 foot capping select fill
Normal slab on grade construction with a 12 inch deep thickened
edge can then be utilized for the garage
For the proposed residence 1 to 4 feet of fill is planned In
the portion of the residence where less than 3 feet of fill is
required the existing ground should be overexcavated for a
depth of 3 feet below the finished floor elevation and replaced
with select non expansive material such as quarry waste The
underlying expansive clay subgrade should be scarified mois
tened to above optimum moisture content then recompacted to
85 density prior to backfilling Where more than 3 feet of
fill is planned additional overexcavation below the existing
ground is not required The clay subgrade soils must be kept
saturated prior to fill or footing concrete placement If the
clay overexcavation and replacement are carried out for a dis
tance of 3 feet beyond the slab perimeter a 12 inch deep peri
meter footing can be used

Alternatively if the clay overexcavation and replacement are
carried out only under the slab the perimeter house retaining
wall footing should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the outside finished grade to provide a cut off wall to reduce
future infiltration of outside moisture under the slab area
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine
gravel base course
In order to reduce the above specified overexcavation and
replacement costs for slab on grade foundations we recommend
that post and beam type of house construction be considered
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select
borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then
be placed on the compacted non expansive soil Alternatively
the individual post footing can be extended a minimum depth of
36 inches below the finished ground surface The footings
should be poured in place and backfilled to form an impervious
layer over the foundations

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff existing ground or compacted
select material The bottom of the footing excavation should
be neat and free of loose soils or debris
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding condition near the slab area Also the owner should
be made aware that gutter water should be diverted far away
from the perimeter footings and walkways
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
The footings along the rear portion of the house are situated
next to the sewer easement The construction plans indicate
that the sewer line in this area is about 3 feet below the
existing ground In order to avoid footing load on the sewer
and to minimize the effect of possible later sewer line excava
tion on the footing a minimum footing embedment of 3 feet is
recommended for this area

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
For concrete driveway and sidewalk recommendations provided
for concrete slab on grade should be followed However since
the driveway and sidewalk could tolerate some amount of swel
ling the non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2
feet All subgrade should be kept moist prior to concreting
Site Grading
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow
material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should not be used for fill
material below structures and behind retaining walls
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water
away from the structures
Retaining Walls
CRM retaining walls may be used around the house to retain the
proposed fill of up to about 4 feet

Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
