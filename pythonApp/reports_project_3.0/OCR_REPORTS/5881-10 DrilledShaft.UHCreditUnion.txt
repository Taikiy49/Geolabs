In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated Geolabs has
provided geotechnical engineering services during the drilled shaft foundation installation
for the University of Hawaii Federal Credit Union project This letter report summarizes our
geotechnical observations performed during the drilled shaft installation In general the
purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled shaft
foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
Geolabs Inc performed a geotechnical engineering exploration for the building
foundation design and our recommendations were presented in the report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration University of Hawaii Federal Credit Union

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the intersection of McCully and King Streets in the Moiliili area
of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by existing
commercial buildings to the north south and east sides and McCully Street to the west
The existing University of Hawaii Federal Credit Union building adjoins the site on the east
side The site is rectangular shaped with plan dimensions of about 60 feet by 95 feet
Single story wooden buildings formerly on the eastern portion of the site have been
demolished and removed
The project will involve site grading and infrastructure installation including parking
areas sidewalks and utilities Based on the design Level B of the new building will be
constructed below the grade with Level 1 at the same elevation as the parking level of the
existing building

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Foundation International Inc performed the drilled shaft installation A total of
40 drilled shafts with a diameter of 36 inches were installed at the site from May 13 to
May 29 2009 The shafts were designated as Shaft Nos 1 through 40 The approximate
drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Numbering Plan Plate 2 A Geolabs
representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel reinforcements and
concrete placement operations during the installation of the drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a track mounted drill rig equipped with
36 inch drilling buckets 36 inch diameter core barrels and 36 inch cleanout buckets The
production shafts were excavated to depths ranging from about 19 to 26 3 feet below the
ground surface which corresponds to tip elevations ranging from about 11 5 to 18 7 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover between the sidewalls of
the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage The shaft concrete was placed after
installation of the rebar cage using tremie methods
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the University
of Hawaii Federal Credit Union project have been installed in general conformance with
the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction
practices The top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to
verify that the shaft position is within the tolerance of the project specifications Based on
our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
