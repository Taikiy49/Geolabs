This report presents the results of additional soil and foundation
investigation performed by this firm for the reference project
The purpose of this investigation was to obtain additional sub
surface information regarding the feasibility of a spread footing
type of foundation system for revised building heights and loads

SITE AND SOIL CONDITIONS
At the time of our field exploration site clearing was in
progress Part of the site on the Waikiki end was used to
stock pile materials brought in from the new Federal Building
site
A concrete box drain approximately 4 feet wide by 3 feet deep
crosses the site beneath the existing ground surface
The subsurface soils encountered were essentially similar to
those in the soil report dated March 7 1973 Generally below
the surface fill material at about 2 to 4 feet deep soft clayey
materials were noted above the hard coral crust
Water level was noted in our drill holes at about 3 5 feet to
4 5 feet below the existing ground elevation The water level

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The following recommendations are based upon presently available
information only If any conditions are encountered which are
not covered in this report or which are contrary to the contents
of this report the Soil Engineer should be immediately contacted
to enable him to review and if necessary revise his recommen
dations in light of changed conditions
GENERAL
The revised development plan is to construct a total of five 5
timber structures one and two story in height with about 18
inches of fill placed on the existing site
We have obtained a copy of the Soil Exploration Report by Walter
Lum Associates Inc dated regarding the future
office complex development at the corner lot Ewa of the present
project site
Because of the future semi basement which will cut through the
coral crust possible loss of fines due to poor dewatering
methods and pile driving for the office complex at a later date
pile foundations are recommended for the proposed two story
structures

For the two one story structures located at the Waikiki end of
the site spread footing found on recompacted fill may be used
SITE GRADING
The proposed fill should be constructed as soon as possible to
allow the ground to consolidate and to minimize differential
settlements at a later date
The recommendations for site grading are essentially the same
to those in the soil report dated March 7 1973
Generally the onsite and stockpile materials are granular and
non expansive and they are suitable as fill material However
since the materials contain some fine sands and silts dusting
might be a problem
FOUNDATIONS
1 Two Story Structures Pile Foundations
The exploration program disclosed a coral and dense silty
sand formation at about 18 to 30 feet from the existing
ground surface A pile foundation system composed of 12
x 12 concrete piles driven to the coral layer should be
capable of supporting an allowable design load of 50 tons

Based on the subsurface information the pile length
should vary between 25 and 38 feet
Pre drilling on spudding may be necessary through the
surface crust to about 6 foot depths
Splicing of piles should be avoided if practicable
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a
minimum energy of about 15 000 foot pounds
The piles should be driven to a depth of about 18 to
30 feet from the existing ground elevation and into
the bearing stratum for about 2 to 5 feet of penetra
tion or to about 100 blows per foot for the last foot
but not over driven to more than 15 blows per inch or
the last fraction of an inch
The pile caps should be well tied together with deep
grade beams particularly around the perimeter of the
structures
The piles may be considered to be fixed at 5 foot
depths from present grade

One Story Structures Spread Footings
For the proposed one story structures and stairs no s
2 and 6 spread or continuous footing foundations well
tied together are recommended
An over excavation of about 2 5 feet deep below the footing
and 2 5 feet wider than the footing widths should be made
and the footings shall be placed on select granular back
fill material
The following guidelines may be used for the spread footing
foundation design
a Footings should be placed as high as practicable

All soft pockets and loose material encountered at the
bottom of footing excavations should be removed down
to the coral crust near the water level and backfilled
with compacted granular material such as onsite sandy
soils or other select material
The bottom of all footing excavations should be tamped
prior to pouring of concrete
Bearing values of about 2000 psf may be used Minimum
footing width of about 3 feet should be used for spread
footings and about 18 inches for continuous footings
Footings should be well tied together with well rein
forced deep grade beams particularly around the
perimeter of the buildings
Block walls should be supported on deep concrete grade
beam type foundations to minimize differential settle
ments The walls should be well tied together with
bond beams particularly at the tops of walls
Recommendations pertaining to expansive soils lateral loads
concrete floor slab on grade review and inspection are the
same as those in the soil report dated March 7 1973
