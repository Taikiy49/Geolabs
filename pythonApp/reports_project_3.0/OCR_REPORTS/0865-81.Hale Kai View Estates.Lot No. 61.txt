As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three 3
borings within the lot performing laboratory soil tests and
reviewing the mass grading plan for the subdivision This report
summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
Based on the preliminary house plans provided to us the proposed
single family residence will be a two story wooden structure
approximately 44 by 44 feet in plan dimensions The house will be set
back about 25 feet from Kulani Street

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire house garage and
driveway
CMU retaining walls up to 4 5 feet high are anticipated for grade
separation between the garage and the lower level of the house
Fills up to 2 5 feet thick are planned under the garage slab with
cuts up to 2 feet below the existing grade to accommodate the lower
level of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 61 is located on Kulani Street in the southeastern portion of the
Hale Kai View Estates subdivision development about 200 feet east of
Ainapo Street in
The lot is bounded by Kulani Street on the south and subdivision lots
on the north east and west An existing concrete drainage ditch is
located along the lower northeastern boundary of the lot
The lot generally slopes downward from Kulani Street toward the
northeast at about 3 8 to 4 5 gradient The elevation of the lot
generally varies from about 140 to 149 5 feet
During the subsurface investigation numerous haole koa tree stumps
were noted protruding above the existing ground surface

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation the northeastern
and northwestern portions of Lot 61 were covered with a large
stockpile of soil and boulders left over from previous grading
operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 6 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil
consisted of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been mixed
with expansive adobe clay from the previous stockpile

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by more than
5 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory test results on
this material generally indicate that it has high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 61 the slab on grade foundation would
require special design and construction procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
It is our understanding that slab on grade is planned for the garage
and the lower level of the house The on site clay soils are
expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade soils In order to reduce
the expansive effect of the soils the expansive clays within a depth
of 3 feet below the slab subgrade level should be over excavated and
replaced with non expansive borrow materials such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The over excavation should extend for
a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the slabs as shown on the
Schematic Section Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene vapor barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the compacted
fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Alternative Foundation System
We recommend that the proposed house foundation system be re evaluated
in an effort to reduce the foundation construction costs
For this lot post and beam construction is desirable due to the
sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions The post and beam
construction would generally result in lower foundation costs and the
individual footings can be readily adjusted should such a need occur
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 81 Page 6
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the finished grade and backfilled with
on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density The footings should be poured in place and the excavation
backfilled with the on site clay soils and compacted to provide an
impervious layer over the footing see Plate 3 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due
to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations This is
especially important in the more level portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water that may affect expansive soils
around the foundation

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
over excavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the relatively long driveway planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 4 5
grade or flatter
Cuts and fills generally less than 2 5 feet thick are anticipated for
most of the area below the house and the garage
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 81 Page 8
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation including
protruding haole koa stumps and debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill materials should be select non expansive borrow
material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASIM D 1557 78 The
on site clay soil should not be used for fill material below
structures and behind retaining walls
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structure

over excavation and replacement should extend a minimum

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 11
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
