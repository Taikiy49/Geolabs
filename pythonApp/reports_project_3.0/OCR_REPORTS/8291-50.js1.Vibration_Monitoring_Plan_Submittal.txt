The following contains our proposed vibration monitoring work plan, procedures, 
methods, and equipment to be used for the Farrington Highway Makaha Bridges 
project. The project site is located near the intersection of Farrington Highway and 
Kili Drive in the Makaha area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  
The project includes the installation of a new waterline.  As part of the waterline 
installation, sheet pile shoring installation is required. The installation of sheet piles may 
involve pre-drilling and using vibratory action to drive sheet piles to the required 
elevations. Based on the information provided, we understand that it is desired to 
monitor vibrations during pre-drilling and the sheet pile installation in the vicinity of a 
residential structure located at TMK No.: 8-4-008: 020, south of Makaha Stream. 
In order to monitor vibrations to the existing residential structure, we propose a 
monitoring plan consisting of vibration monitoring of the existing structure. Details of the 
vibration monitoring are provided in the subsequent sections. 
VIBRATION MONITORING 
Vibration monitoring will be conducted using an Instantel Micromate equipped 
with a 3-component geophone and a signal conditioner/recorder unit. The geophone 
senses ground motion along three mutually perpendicular (orthogonal) axes; therefore, 
it provides detection of ground motion in the longitudinal, vertical, and transverse 
directions. The signal conditioner/recorder unit receives and processes these signals to 
provide a graphic record of the vibrations. Resultant peak particle velocities - peak 
vector sum (PPV PVS) in units of inches per second are automatically computed by 
vector summation of the motions along the orthogonal axes. The data is stored to the 
conditioner/recorder unit’s onboard memory.

The Micromate unit is set at a scanning interval of 15 minutes and continuously 
monitors vibrations while activated. During the 15-minute scanning interval, the 
Micromate samples the vibration data at a rate of 1,024 data points per second and 
stores the resultant peak level of vibration, which occurs during that 15-minute interval. 
At the end of every scanning interval, a record is produced representing the highest 
vibration level observed. The magnitude of the highest vibration level observed and its 
corresponding time are plotted, and a histogram is generated.  
We propose to utilize two vibration monitors near the vicinity of the residential 
structure located at TMK No.: 8-4-008: 020, shown on the attached Site Plan, Plate 1. 
The specification of the Instantel Micromate vibration monitoring unit is shown on the 
attached Micromate Specifications, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
Vibration thresholds will be based on the type of construction, age and general 
condition of structures, adopted from a variety of studies of actual damage thresholds 
observed for blasting operations and other vibration-inducing sources. Based on Chae 
(1978), the following recommended threshold of damage criteria should provide 
reasonable indicators for reducing the potential for direct vibration damage.

Based on the information provided, we recommend adopting Class III of the 
Threshold of Damage Criteria for existing building structures surrounding the project 
site. Based on these considerations, we recommend a vibration threshold be set as 
peak particle velocities of 1.0 inches per second. 
A pre-alarm vibration monitoring value of 0.5 inches per second will be set to 
trigger an alert notification. In the event the recorded vibrations reach or exceed the 
pre-alarm value of 0.5 inches per second, our field personnel will immediately notify the 
General Contractor. Upon receiving a vibration monitoring notification, the General 
Contractor can investigate the source of the vibration, inspect the residential structure 
for any potential distress and, if necessary, discuss methods to reduce the vibrations. A 
sample vibration alert monitoring event histogram plot print-out is included on Plate 3.
