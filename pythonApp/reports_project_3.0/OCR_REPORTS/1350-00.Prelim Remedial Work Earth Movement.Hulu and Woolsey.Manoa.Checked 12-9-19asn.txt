Submitted herewith are some preliminary findings for the above
referenced project
1 Our previous investigation indicated the thickness of the upper
adobe clay layer to be only about 9 to 14 feet However our
new Borings 6A and 7 encountered what appears to be a buried
drainageway in which the thickness of the upper adobe clay
soil was found to be 25 and 15 feet respectively This thicker
deposit of adobe clay will have a significant effect on the
design and construction of the caisson wall by increasing the
driving forces and required embeddment of the caisson

5 The appropriate spacing between caissons will depend on the
actual size of the caisson used and the behavior of the soil
behind the wall A study of the suitable spacing for the
caisson wall is being studied at the moment and the result of
the study will be presented in our final report However a
preliminary value of 1 5 times the diameter of the caisson or
4 5 feet centre to centre whichever is smaller can be used
We hope that the above findings can furnish you with sufficient
information for the preliminary structural design of the caisson wall
Meanwhile we will try to make every effort to expedite the issuance of
the final report If you have further questions about this letter or our
investigation in general please feel free to contact us
