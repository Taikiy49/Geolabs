FOUNDATION EXPLORATION
NAVAL STATION PEARL HARBOR OAHU HAWAII
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
exploration performed for the proposed Submarine Damage Control Training
Facility at the Naval Submarine Training Center in the Naval Station at
Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATTON
The Submarine Damage Control Training Facility consists of a
one story building addition approximately 30 by 30 feet in plan
Structural loading of the building is anticipated to be light and
minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the foundation
design of the proposed building addition

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located adjacent to the existing Buildings 39
and 257 on Ford Island in Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The building site is an open and unoccupied area measured at about
30 by 30 feet The southeastern side of the site is currently occupied
by an existing small building addition to Building 39
The surface elevation within the site was not available at this
time However the ground level at the site is generally flat Based on
an available site plan of the southeastern small building addition by
others the existing grade of the site appears to be approximately 111 5
feet U S Navy Datum Mean Sea Level elevation 100 feet
Ground cover consists of a 10 inch concrete slab at the southeastern
quarter portion and a 2 inch asphaltic concrete pavement at the remainder
of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled to depths of 20 feet each below the
existing ground surface Both borings encountered dense stiff highly
weathered volcanic tuff silty sand and clayey silt over medium hard
coral formation A 2 foot thick layer of stiff moderately expansive clay
was encountered above the highly weathered tuff in Boring 2
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at about 11 3 to
11 9 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Footing Foundation
Based on the soil conditions and the anticipated light building
loads we believe that shallow spread or continuous footing foundation
bearing on the in situ stiff clay clayey silt or base course may be
used
Footings should be placed a minimim of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade or embedded to the same depth as the adjacent
footing of the existing buildings Uplift wind blast or other lateral
loading conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing
foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead
plus live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration
wind and seismic loading Footing settlements under the design loading
are anticipated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch total
and one fourth of an inch differential

Resistance to lateral loads on the building addition can be obtained
by frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 35 may be used to compute the frictional resistance
between the bottom of the footing and the underlying recompacted in situ
soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot
of depth may be used to determine the passive earth resistance against
the buried vertical faces of the footing
The near surface soils may have low to moderate swelling potential
and therefore should be maintained in a moist condition at all times
Site Grading
The finished grade elevation of the proposed building addition was
not available at this stage but is anticipated to match with the
finished grade level of the existing Building 39 Due to the relatively
flat ground minimal cut is anticipated for the building construction
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
pavements slab debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated materials
The excavated footing foundation and pavement areas should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the footing subgrade
elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
Ioose or soft spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level
should be removed and replaced with compacted on site soil

The on site clayey soil should not be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular material less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed building
addition Based on the soil conditions the following section may be
used for the preliminary design of the building slab on grade which is
not anticipated to support any vehicular traffic
4 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Thickness
The subgrade soils have low to moderate swelling potential and
therefore should be maintained in a moist condition at all times
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and
slab on grade should be forwarded to the soil engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavation be observed by the soil engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling to the site for use

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
two 2 borings to depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The Classifications
were further reviewed by visual observation and testing in the
laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with
the Unified Soil Classification System A summary of the materials
encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths
