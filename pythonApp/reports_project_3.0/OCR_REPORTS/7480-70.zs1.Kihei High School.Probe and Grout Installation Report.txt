In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated March 1, 2023, Geolabs 
observed the probing and grouting operations for the Kihei High School project located in 
Kihei on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical 
observations during the probing and grouting operations for the project. 
The new Kihei High School structures will be supported on shallow foundations 
consisting of spread and continuous strip footings. To reduce the potential for loss of 
foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones 
below the building structure, a probing and grouting program consisting of a total of 173 
4-inch diameter probe holes was implemented. Probing and grouting was performed for 
spread and continuous strip footings for Buildings I and J, and the footings for the 
walkway between Buildings H and I. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the Kihei High School 
project, and the findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced report. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the probing and grouting operations for conformance with the intent 
of the recommendations presented in our report. In addition, we provided “Special 
Inspection” services (as stipulated in Section 1704 of the 2012 International Building Code) 
by observing the probing and grouting operation and existing site soil conditions for 
general conformance with the project plans and specifications.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project consists of the development of a new public high school in the town of 
Kihei on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project site encompasses approximately 70 acres 
of undeveloped land east of Piilani Highway, between Kulanihakoi and Waipuilani 
Gulches, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  
As indicated in the referenced report, cavities and/or voids are commonly present in 
the basaltic lava flows and were encountered in several of the borings drilled at the project 
site.  To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of 
cavities below foundations, Geolabs recommended implementing a program of cavity 
probing and grouting of the new structure foundations. In accordance with this 
recommendation, probing and grouting was performed for spread and continuous strip 
footings for Buildings I and J, and the footings for the walkway between Buildings H 
and I. 
PROBING AND GROUTING OPERATIONS 
The probing and grouting operation was conducted over a time span from May 9 
through May 23, 2023 under the near-continuous (full-time) observation by our field 
engineer. In general, the probe holes were located along the continuous strip footings (one 
probe hole at 10 feet on-center) and at each isolated spread footing location (one probe 
hole per 50 square feet). The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probing 
Location Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.3.  In general, the probe holes at the new structure 
foundations were drilled to depths between about 10 to 12 feet below the existing ground 
surface, corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design bottom of footing 
elevations. 
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished by Kelikai, Inc., utilizing a 
Casagrande C6 rock drill equipped with a 4-inch diameter Downhole hammer. Pressurized 
air continuously flowed through the hollow rods while advancing to the target probe depth. 
Water was also used to aid in the drilling process. It should be noted that the drilling 
method utilizing pressurized air and water produced a hole larger than the proposed 4-inch 
diameter hole. Based on our field measurements, the average size of the holes drilled was 
approximately 5.2 inches in diameter.  
Evidence of cavities/voids was observed by advancement rate and drilling intensity 
during the probing operation.  After completion of the probe drilling operation, the probe 
holes were tremie grouted through a tremie pipe.  The grout mix used for the project 
consisted of a grout mix provided by Kelikai, Inc. utilizing a 130-gallon dual mixing grout 
plant. 
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe time, drilling intensity, and approximate grout intake.  A total of 173 probe holes 
with an aggregate length of about 1,848 lineal feet below the existing ground surface
