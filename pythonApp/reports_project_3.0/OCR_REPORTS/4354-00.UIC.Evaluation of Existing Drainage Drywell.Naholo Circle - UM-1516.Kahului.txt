FINDINGS DISCUSSION
The subject drywell facility is on the eastern side of Naholo Circle located
between Holua Drive and Luka Place intersections in the town of Kahului on the Island
of Maui Hawaii The drywell evaluation was performed on and
under generally fair weather conditions The steel grate covering the
concrete catch basin was removed and the steel elbow pipe drywell inlet was unbolted
and removed for access to the vertically oriented well casing
Physical Charateristics
Our observation of the visible portion of the concrete catch basin and well casing
indicated that the system was in generally good condition with no overt signs of leakage
or deterioration No static water level was encountered in the well prior to testing
The depth of the well was measured by sounding with a weighted tape to locate
the bottom of the well The measured well depth was confirmed during the down hole
video recording effort The depth and diameter measurements including a sketch of the
drywell are presented on the attached Summary of Drywell Dimensions and Lithologic
Log Plate 1
Down Hole Video
The down hole video recording was conducted to evaluate the conditions within
the cased and open hole segments of the well Wailani Drilling Co of Maui performed
the video work under the direction of our field engineer The equipment used was a
GeoVision Micro Down Hole color video camera The video obtained from the well was
recorded on a VHS tape for later review and analyses
The camera recorded a continuous video of the well condition along with
corresponding depth data Based on our review of the video recording the interior of
the well appeared to be in generally fair condition However upon observation of the
bottom of the well with the video camera it appeared that a rock might be bridged in the
well creating a false bottom Some open space was visible around the margin of the
potentially bridged segment
Due to the solid steel casing lining the upper portion of the well the lithology of
the cased portion of the well was inferred based on available geologic data and our
experience in the vicinity of the project site We believe that the well casing was
installed to protect against caving in of the potentially loose sands alluvial soils and
weathered rock comprising the upper portion of the drywell The lithologic log for the
well is presented on the attached Summary of Drywell Dimensions and Lithologic Log
Plate 1

Injection Testing
A short term injection test was conducted by our field engineer to evaluate the
disposal capacity of the drywell A 4 000 gallon capacity tanker equipped with a pump was
used to introduce clear fresh water into the drywell The usable volume of the tanker
source water to perform the test was estimated to be about 3 900 gallons A pressure
transducer and electronic data logger were utilized to collect and record the head level
response within the drywell No groundwater was encountered in the drywell prior to the
start of the testing
In general approximately 2 000 gallons of fresh water was pumped into the well
during the injection test The average flow rate was estimated at about 220 gallons per
minute gpm The injection test was terminated when the drywell overflowed from the top
of the casing at approximately 9 minutes after the start of the test Upon overflow of the
well a falling head test was performed for an additional 32 minutes to evaluate the
percolation rate
Based on the injection test results the drywell appeared to be operating with
generally poor capacity at an average injection rate of about 220 gpm The head level rose
to overflow at the top of the well casing within about 9 minutes from the start of introduction
of the influent at an average injection rate of about 220 gpm Based on the short term
injection test results we estimate that the disposal capacity of the drywell is substantially
less than about 220 gpm and may be on the order of about 15 gom assuming that the
drywell is filled with water to the top of the casing The estimated disposal capacity of
15 gpm is estimated based on the falling head test results The results of the short term
injection test are presented on the attached Injection Test Results Plates 2 and 3
LIMITATIONS
The evaluation presented in this report is based upon visual observations
down hole video recording and short term testing of the injection well Variation from
our findings may occur and the nature of such variations may not become evident until
at a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will become necessary to
reevaluate the findings in this report
