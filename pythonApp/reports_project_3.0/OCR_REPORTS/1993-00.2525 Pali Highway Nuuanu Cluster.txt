INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading and house
foundations of the proposed Nuuanu Cluster residential development in the
Nuuanu Valley area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soils
data set for the formulation of engineering recommendations pertinent to
the design of the site grading and the design of the foundations of the
proposed residential buildings

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2525 Pali Highway in the Nuuanu Valley
area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated on the eastern or Diamond Head side of Pali
Highway between the highway and Nuuanu Strean The site is a flag lot
encompassing approximately 3 acres bounded by Nuuanu Stream to the east
and existing residences to the other sides
Ground surface elevations range from about 255 feet above Mean Sea
Level MSI at the Pali Highway entrance to the site to about 210 feet MSL
at the southern end of the site at Nuuanu Strean In general the ground
slopes in an easterly direction towards Nuuanu Strean Portions of the
site have been terraced by using CRM and cMU retaining walls A
semi abandoned auwai traverses the site in a roughly east west direction
There is a single family residential structure on the site and at
one time the site had been extensively landscaped including greenhouses
and fishponds At present the greenhouses and fishponds are in a state
of disrepair

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to subdivide the subject lot into a cluster
development of seven 7 single family residences The development will
require the grading and construction of two 2 access roads and site
grading to create level building pads The currently proposed site
grading involves cuts of up to about 15 feet in depth and fills of up to
about 12 feet in thickness
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located in Nuuanu Valley an amphitheater headed
valley carved by erosion into Koolau Basalts Filling the base of this
valley is a thick sequence of alluvium interbedded with deposits of lavas
and pyroclastics of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
At the project site the drilled borings encountered a surface layer
of alluvium overlying basaltic lavas believed to be of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 17 0 and
20 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered surface layers of stiff brown
silty clays and clays varying between 5 5 and 12 0 feet in thickness
Alluvium Below these surficial soils the borings encountered either
brown orange mottled medium dense silty sands alluvium and or medium
hard gray basalt Honolulu Volcanic Series to the maximum depths drilled

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The presence of shallow groundwater
conditions in the proposed fill areas may require the use of special
grading techniques as discussed below We recommend that spread or strip
footings for the proposed residences bear on the stiff in situ alluvium or
on engineered fill compacted in accordance with the recommendations given
below
Site Grading
It is anticipated that approximately 8 to 12 feet of fill will be
required in portions of the eastern part of the development to raise the
grade to provide level building pads

grading

off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill

Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading

Imported select fill material should be non expansive have a
minimum CBR value of 15 when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Method D 1883 and have less than 20 fines passing the 200
sieve
All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

fill areas we recommend that light equipment be used to place the first

subgrade The compaction of the first two 2 feet of fill should he

Foundation Support
It is anticipated that both post and beam foundations and
slabs on grade will be used for the residential structures
For the design of foundations bearing on the in situ stiff alluvial
soils or on fill compacted in accordance with the recommendations
presented herein an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be
utilized This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Post footing foundations on level areas should be embedded a minimum
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade On sloping
areas the footings should be embedded an additional depth to provide a
6 foot minimum set back distance measured from the outer edge of footing
to the slope face The thickened edges of slabs on grade should be
embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
exterior grade
A cushion of four 4 inches of 43 Fine crushed rock is recommended
below slabs A moisture barrier membrane is recommended between he slab
and the cushion to reduce the possibility of moisture damage to the floor
coverings
Settlements
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on the stiff in situ
alluvial soils or on engineered fill constructed in accordance with the
above recommendations are believed to be less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location and elevations of the field data points were determined
approximately by taping from reference points shown on the Preliminary
Grading and Utility Plan by Community Planning Incorporated undated
The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in stream flow rainfall tides temperature
and other factors

import material Fill material shall contain less than 5

shall not contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches
8 in diameter

Vv Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within each
layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill
layer which has not been properly compacted Subsequent
removal of any unacceptable layer shall be done at the
Contractor s expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches 8 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated
in any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained
material to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for
placement in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical
Engineer s Report

The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable
of producing the specified results in an expedient and
efficient manner The equipment shall be in good working
condition fully ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a
uniformly compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above
specified levels of compaction out to the finish slope of
fill slopes This may be achieved by either overbuilding
the slope and cutting back to the compacted core or by
direct compaction of the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes
shall be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope
material into firm material and the transition stripped of
loose material prior to fill placement Fill shall be
placed and compacted from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not
in accordance with these specifications shall not be
approved and shall be removed or reworked until the required
density is obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests

Unforeseen Subsurface Conditions
A
If any conditions not described in the contract documents
such as perched water seepage lenticular or confined
strata of a potentially adverse nature are encountered
during grading these conditions shall be immediately
brought to the attention of the Project Engineers so that
supplemental recommendations may be made to treat these
problems
Should excavations encounter loose or unstable conditions
the Contractor shall notify the Geotechnical Engineer and
Project Civil Engineer immediately so that supplemental
recommendations can be given
