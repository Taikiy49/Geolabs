PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

Kailua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to install a new
lighting system for the two parking lots located on the Kainalu Drive side of the park as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The new light poles will be of aluminum construction approximately 25 feet in
height 160 to 210 pounds in weight and 5 inches square at the base The following
information pertinent to the foundation demand of the new light poles was provided for

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the parking lot lighting system consisted of drilling
and sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to a depth
of about 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain
by an asphalt concrete pavement overlying medium dense silty sands extending to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration
are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 of this report
Groundwater was not encountered in the shallow drilled borings at the time of our
field exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to Kailua Bay it should be noted
that groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration would likely
be affected by tidal fluctuations The groundwater levels measured may also be
influenced by other factors such as seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and or
injection and storm surge conditions

underlain by medium dense silty sands throughout the majority of depths explored As
mentioned previously we understand that a standard light pole foundation design
consisting of 22 inch square round drilled shaft sections extending to a depth of 5 feet
below the design finished grade will be utilized for the parking lot lighting system

lateral load analyses indicate that the estimated deflection at the top of the light pole

the proposed standard light pole foundation design

Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures In our Opinion the
following conditions would have a substantial effect on the effectiveness and cost of the
drilled shaft foundations
Loose Sandy Soils
Based on our field exploration the proposed new light pole locations are generally
underlain by medium dense sands extending to a depth of about 11 5 feet below
the existing ground surface However localized zones of loose sandy subsoils
may be encountered during the drilled shaft installation To reduce the potential
for caving in of the drilled holes temporary casing may be required during the
foundation construction work Therefore we recommend that casing of drilled
holes be considered as a contingent item with additive and deductive unit prices
included in the contract documents
Workmanship
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in
drilling the shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods
utilizing drilling fluids is not recommended
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils
Concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix If precast concrete pole
bases are used the gap between the pole base and the surrounding soils should
be backfilled with concrete or lean concrete A representative of Geolabs Hawaii
should be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts during construction to
confirm the subsoil conditions and embedment depths
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or
S4C is recommended under the duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the

critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids
below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or imported
select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 6 inch level loose
lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content corresponding to the maximum dry density

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical

expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident Geolabs Hawaii should be contacted to re evaluate the
recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Electrical Site Plan transmitted
by Bennett Engineers Inc on March 16 1999 The physical locations of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Bennett Engineers Inc
their client City County of Honolulu Department of Design and Construction and
other project subconsultants for specific application to the proposed Parking Lot Lighting
System project at the Kailua District Park in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve
as the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes
Acontractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the performance of
additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
