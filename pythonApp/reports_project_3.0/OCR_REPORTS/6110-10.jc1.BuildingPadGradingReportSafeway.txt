Subsurface Conditions
In general the original topography of the project site prior to site grading activities
slopes down gently from the high point near the intersection of East Makaala Street and
Railroad Avenue to the low point along the northern boundary
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site
generally is underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that cavities and or voids are commonly encountered
in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous relatively smaller cavity
features were observed at the project site during the mass grading operations
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations
ranged from about 70 to 74 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the Safeway Store site The
Safeway Store site generally was graded to a finished pad elevation of about 75 25 feet
MSL as shown on the referenced As Built Topographic Survey by Island Survey Inc
dated Mass grading at the Safeway Store site generally consisted of placing
up to about 5 feet of new fills with minimal excavation to achieve the design grades
At the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade
preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential
for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the site The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities
and or voids within the near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of
practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a Caterpillar D 10
bulldozer to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained
in the porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 65EX bulldozer working the surface for
about four to six passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged
surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a vibratory
drum roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
In general fill materials consisting of structural and general fills were placed in
the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular

Limitations

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
