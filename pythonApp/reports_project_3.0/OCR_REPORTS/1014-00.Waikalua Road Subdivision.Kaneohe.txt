PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development consisting of 59 lots and
access roads through the site
To develop the site most of the planned lots will be fill
lots with minor cuts along the west east and south portions
of the site Fills from 2 to 14 feet and cuts up to 10 feet
are anticipated to create a relatively level subdivision lot
area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The proposed project is located within an old meander loop
of Kaneohe Stream The high 40 foot bank at the curved
boundaries of the site is the outside cut bank of the old
meander loop It is deeply incised in older alluvium con
sisting of dark brown Clayey silts and Silty clays contain
ing highly weathered gravels and boulders

were deposited on the inside

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings test pits and field observations the
site appears to be underlain by alluvial deposits The higher
portions of the site appear to be underlain by Older Alluviun
consisting of stiff clayey silt with some weathered gravel to
the depths drilled These soils are generally competent and
have low to moderate swelling potential
The low areas appear to be underlain by more recent alluvial
deposits consisting of a surface crust generally 4 to 6 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed complex soil conditions at
the site The upper areas are underlain by relatively comn
petent stiff alluvial soils The lower areas are underlain
by a layer of loose sands and silts The loose layer will
be subject to settlements due to the weight of the new fill

junk on the site Prior to filling the site should be
cleared of vegetation surface debris and other deleterious
material In areas to recieve fill the ground should be
first scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to
near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 density per ASTM D 1557 70 Test Method
After the above site preparation the fill should be moistened

Settlements
Fills up to about 14 feet high are anticipated over the
lower areas of the site Ground settlements are antici
pated due to consolidation of the soft and loose alluvial
deposits caused by the weight of the new fill The amount
of total settlements experienced will vary due to the nat
ural variability of alluvial deposits consistency thick
ness and pre loading and the amount of fill The labora
tory consolidation tests indicate that the loose soils may
have been pre load Based on the laboratory tests and
assuming a 10 foot fill height anticipated primary settle
ments are on the order of 2 to 4 inches Some additional
long term secondary settlements should also be anticipated
The time required for the estimated primary settlements to
occur are very difficult to predict because they depend on
many variables including the composition and structure of
the subsoil The laboratory tests indicate that most of
the loose soils consist of silts and fine sands therefore
relatively rapid settlements are anticipated Based on the
laboratory consolidation tests it is estimated that 90 of
the settlements would occur within 1 to 4 months Settle
ment gauges should be installed prior to filling to monitor
the actual settlement rate in order to determine when con
struction of the utilities roads and houses can proceed

If it is desired to reduce waiting period we would be glad
to consult with you on a surcharge program to accelerate

Cut and fill slopes generally less than 10 feet in vertical
height are anticipated
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended
Pill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cut
ting back to the design slope to obtain a well compacted
slope face
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of slopes
and slope planting utilized as soon as possible to reduce
erosion
House Foundations
Some amount of secondary settlement is anticipated for the
fill areas Therefore post and beam foundations are recom
mended for areas with more than 5 feet of fill since they
are better able to tolerate some long term settlements
For cut areas and areas with less than 5 feet of fill slab
on grade or post and beam foundations can be utilized
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1014 00
P 9
To minimize the effects of slope creep or erosion slabs
Should be set back a minimum distance of 5 feet away from
the tops of slopes Also post footings located close to
the tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth
to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet
from the slope face For post footings located 5 feet or
more away from the tops of the slopes the footing embed
ment should be a minimum of 12 inches below finished grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pres
sure of 2 500 p s f can be used for footings resting on
very stiff in situ soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
Should extend to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of com
pacted base course The thickened edge around the slab on
grade should extend a minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent
ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

The slab subgrade should be kept moist and not allowed to
dry out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
Pavement

vided adjacent to paved areas
In fill areas select off site borrow material with a CBR

tion
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
Il Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified

2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 1
H V the ground surface shall be keyed and benched
into competent soil to bond the fill to the exist
ing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by
the soils engineer where springs or zones of seep
age are encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
submitted to the soils engineer for testing and approval
a minimum of 5 working days prior to its proposed deliv
ery to the site Fill material shall contain less than
5 organic matter and other deleterious material and
shall not contain rocks or lumps in excess of 8 inches
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not exceeding 10 inches in thickness Each layer
shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within
each layer

Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
