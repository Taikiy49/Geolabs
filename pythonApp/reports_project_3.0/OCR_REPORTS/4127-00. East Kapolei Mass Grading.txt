SITE GRADING
The project site is located in the East Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The site is bounded by the future Kapolei Parkway and the
existing Kapolei Lower Drainage Channel We understand that it is proposed to construct
a baseball sports complex consisting of a 4 000 seat baseball stadium and multiple full size
practice fields This portion of the project is only to mass grade the site to the design rough
grades shown on the contract documents
The site grading operations were performed by Royal Contracting Company Ltd In
general the site grading operations consisted of clearing and grubbing preparation of the
subgrade soils and placement and compaction of on site and imported fills The depths of
the excavations and thickness of embankment fills at the site were generally on the order of
about 2 to 5 feet Much of the fills needed at the site to raise the pre existing grades to the
design mass grading grades consisted of on site excavated materials and various sources
of imported materials
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The suitability
of the imported materials for use as general fill was evaluated based on performing

D 1883 The results of the CBR tests on the imported fill materials are presented on

moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this
report
The project site was generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation debris rubbish and
other unsuitable materials After clearing and grubbing of the site the existing soil subgrades
were then scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted fo at least 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor During the placement of fill materials
the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill operations to confirm our field
observations as variations in the construction procedures and soil conditions were noted A
total of 136 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The
results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this
report
The field density tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on the
compacted fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from the on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were re worked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
above referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the
mass grading of the above referenced project site observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii
in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent
of the project plans and specifications
It should be noted that due to the nature of the soil materials and the severe dry
weather conditions in the Ewa Kapolei area the finished surfaces of the mass grading
operations would be susceptible to shrinkage cracking Therefore if the site is left
unattended without irrigation for a period of time the ground surfaces will develop shrinkage
cracks Future development above the mass graded areas will need to address the impact
of shrinkage cracks on the future development We wish to point out that shrinkage cracks
in the ground are inherent and common in the Ewa Kapolei area
