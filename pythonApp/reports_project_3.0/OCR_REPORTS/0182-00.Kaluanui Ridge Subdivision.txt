DISCUSSION
Geology
_ The rocks which outcrop on the Kaluanui Ridge are composed
Ino
predominately of interlayered lavas which are a part of the
material
t
Pliocene Koolau volcanic series
The basaltic lavas are separated into flows of varying thick
nesses The predominate rock type that is exposed is classified
as a massive micro crystalline basalt with layers of Aa and
clinker beds In general the lava flows are very massive with
talus accumulations occurring near the bottom of the various
slopes which make up the Main ridge area
a Where the lava flows are distinctly layered the flows generally
dip ina southerly direction at an angle of less than 15 degrees
Differential erosion of the varying lava types has caused vertical
cliffs which are composed of the harder more resistant basalt
Some soil material occurs between the various lava flows which
represents a previously existing erosion surface The soil
material that does occur between the various lava flows is very
thin and will not be a source of any quantity for embankment
fills
The geomorphology of the Kaluanui Ridge is typical of that
formed in volcanic areas where the land forms are composed
Page 7
W O 182
basically of successive interlayered lava flows As is also
typical in this area the upper parts of the ridges consist
of decomposed lava material which is classified as a Silty
CLAY soil In this area the residual soil material generally
occurs at an elevation of 500 feet above sea level or higher
In the lower areas from sea level to approximately 500 feet
little or no soil cover exists The residual soil material
which occurs at the higher elevations will serve as a source
of borrow material to be incorporated into embankment fills
