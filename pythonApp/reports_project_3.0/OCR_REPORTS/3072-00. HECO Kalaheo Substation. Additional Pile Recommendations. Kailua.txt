Additional Pile Recommendations
HECO Kalaheo Substation
Kailua WWTP Modifications Phase Ill
Kailua Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we are providing additional recommendations
pertinent to the pile driving operation at the above referenced project
1 On the basis of the boring data the recommended minimum pile length
should be cast based on the anticipated pile tip elevation of 105 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL
It is our understanding that a total of 12 piles are to be installed for the new
foundation system of the substation Therefore a test pile program may not
be feasible and we recommend that an additional 5 feet of pile length be
added to the piles to account for potential variability in subsoil conditions
Additional pile lengths should also be added by the contractor to the
minimum pile lengths estimated above to account for construction related
considerations
2 All piles should be located at least 3 feet away from the 42 inch force main
Based on the cross sections of Drawing No C066 the invert of the force
main is approximately at 2 feet MSL
We have evaluated the options of constructing a deep trench along the
sides of the force main to reduce the vibration induced during pile driving

It is anticipated that the trench may extend 12 feet below the pile cap
elevation at about 9 feet MSL Therefore we have the following
concerns
a Groundwater level may be encountered during the trench excavation
which may cause difficulty in future compaction and backfill of the
trench Any inadequate compaction would affect the lateral capacity
of the driven pile
b A 12 foot deep near vertical trench may not be stable especially with
the vibration induced by the pile driving operation In addition
backfilling of the trench by the contractor will likely require shoring of
the trench sides or sloping back
As a result we recommend that all piles located within 8 feet of the force
main be predrilled to a depth of about 3 feet MSL as indicated in our soils
report dated May 17 1993
