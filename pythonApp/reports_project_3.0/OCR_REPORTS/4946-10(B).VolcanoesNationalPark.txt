SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers
an area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of
five shield volcanoes which include the following Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea
activity during recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa
and Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated near the Kilauea summit which has formed the
southeast portion of the island The project site appears to be underlain by basaltic lava
flows of the Puna Volcanic Series of Kilauea which were deposited during the
Pleistocene and Holocene Recent Epochs Stearns and Macdonald 1946
The lava formation at the site appears to be of both a a and pahoehoe basalt
type flows A a lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow
surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and
boulders Typically a more dense and layered lava rock material is contained within the
lava flow core Pahoehoe lava is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface
and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered
in pahoehoe lavas Cavities are formed when the lavas are still in a molten state and
represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow
cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving
a hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along
the flow Blisters and pockets inter flow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal
extent
Our field exploration at some locations encountered ash and cinder deposits in
the drilled shallow borings These deposits are pyroclastic materials that were most
likely placed as a result of lava fountain eruptions in the summit area

2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located in the summit area of Hawaii Volcanoes National Park
The existing cesspools serve a number of facilities within the park as shown on the
General Site Plan Plate 2 The existing ground surface elevations at the project site
vary from about 3 928 to 3 973 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
2 3 Subsurface Conditions
The interiors of unlined cesspools generally consist of interbedded layers of
pahoehoe and a a basalt
Some voids that were observed in the exposed rock formation appeared to be
small lava tubes Lava tubes and cavities are commonly encountered in basalt
formations
The shallow borings encountered ash and cinder deposits These deposits are
pyroclastic materials that were most likely placed as a result of lava fountain eruptions
in the summit area The cinder is generally sand and generally gravel sized and the ash
particles are generally silt sized
Permeability of the basalt rock formations is generally high with fluids moving
along the coarse gravel material in aa clinker through lava tubes and cavities and
along joints and fractures in the rock formation The cinder deposits also have relatively
high rates of infiltration due to the coarse granular nature of the material The
permeability of ash deposits is generally relatively low because of the silty
characteristics of the ash
Groundwater in the site region is anticipated to occur near sea level However
higher level dike impounded groundwater may be present at portions of the site
underlain by Kilauea s rift zones

3 1 Inspection and Injection Testing
The cesspools that were located and accessible for testing were first visually
inspected in an attempt to obtain a geological log of the formations penetrated by the
individual cesspool These cesspools were then sounded to determine the depth to
bottom and the depth to fluid in the cesspool Following this inspection testing was
conducted by using a water truck to place approximately 3 000 gallons of clean fresh
water into the cesspools and by recording the time required for the water to dissipate
i e a falling head infiltration test Since the actual geometry of the wells were not
available and the actual characteristics of the receiving aquifer were not known the
performance test data was not subjected to rigorous analyses to estimate hydraulic
conductivities or maximum capacities The test data was reviewed on a subjective basis
to estimate the performance of the cesspools
To provide information for the installation of new leachfields at the facilities where
the cesspools were failing or could not be located relatively shallow borings were drilled
and percolation tests were conducted in general accordance with the Hawaii
Department of Health requirements
