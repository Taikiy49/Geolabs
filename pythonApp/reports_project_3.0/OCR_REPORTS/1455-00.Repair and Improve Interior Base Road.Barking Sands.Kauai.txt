PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the interior base road is presently
constructed on earthen dikes over an existing irrigation ditch which
previously emptied into the adjacent ocean It is further understood
that during extremely heavy rains such as those occuring in October 1982
severe flooding occurred to the adjacent sugar cane fields due to the
impounded flood waters
It is proposed to construct a box culvert beneath the roadway at the
existing irrigation ditch to allow flood water to flow out toward the
ocean It is also anticipated that flood control gates will be needed
seaward of the planned culvert structure to prevent the ocean surf from
entering the drainage system and destroying the sugar cane fields that
border the activity
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on South Kalanamahiki Place about 5000
feet south of the main gate within the Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands on the Island of Kauai
An existing earthen dike had been constructed over the present
irrigation ditch to allow the crossing of South Kalanamahiki Place over
the ditch and a second dike about 100 feet seaward of the first provides
a secondary crossing over the ditch
During the field investigation observations indicate the irrigation
ditch side walls and earthen dike slope vary from 1 1 down to 2 1 H V
It appears that the irrigation ditch contains perennial flows and
the existing earthen dikes would obstruct even small flows impounding
the water flow upstream of the first dike
Ground cover consist mainly of grass and weeds with small stockpiles
of coral sand adjacent to the first dike

Soil Conditions
In general the borings encountered loose to dense silty coral sand
fill from 7 5 to 9 5 feet thick below the existing ground surface
Underlying the fill layer the borings encountered bluish gray
medium dense to dense silty sand which appears to be lagoonal deposits
laid down during low velocity stream flows and or during changes in sea
level
Dense highly cemented coral sand sandstone was encountered below
the lagoonal deposit to the full depth of our borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings encountered competent subsoil condition for the
construction of the proposed culvert and control gate The sandy
lagoonal deposit at the proposed site with thickness varying from 9 to
13 feet was found to be medium dense to dense and hence appears to
exhibit a low to moderate scouring potential Therefore shallow spread
footings may be used as foundation for both the culvert and the control
gate
Shallow Foundation
New Culvert
It is recommended that shallow spread footings can be used for the
culvert
To help resist against loss of ground the culvert should be
over excavated and backfilled with base coarse material to a depth
of at least 6 inches below the bottom of the structure The
over excavation should extend at least 2 feet beyond both the
upstream and downstream sides of the culvert The base coarse
backfill is designed to offer some resistance to scouring below the
bottom of the box culvert Also it serves as a better bedding
layer than the in situ dense sand which may be loosened during the
excavation and dewatering for the construction

Control Gate
an lm A a i a emit
It is our understanding that a reinforced concrete retaining wall
with removable planks will be used as a flood control gate Dense
silty sand encountered at the bottom elevation of the wall suggests
that shallow spread footings may be used
Embedment of the footing of the wall should not be less than 2 feet
as measured from the bottom of the channel to the upper face of the
footing Six inches of over excavation at the bottom of the footing
with replacement of base coarse material is recommended
The over excavation should extend at least 2 feet beyond both the
upstream and downstream ends of the wall The base coarse backfill
is designed to offer some resistance to scouring below the bottom of
the wall Also it serves as a better bedding layer than the
in situ dense sand which may be loosened during the excavation and
dewatering for the construction
Wingwalls should also be constructed to retain and protect backfill
materials on the two sides of the control gate
Riprap Erosion Protection
eS i ele SR ti i
It is further recommended that riprap stones be placed for at least
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1455 00 Page 7
5 feet on both the upstream and downstream sides of the culvert and
control gate to provide resistance to scouring A 6 inch layer of
base course is recommended below the riprap to provide a filter
layer between the larger riprap stones and underlying sand
The riprap should also incorporate cutoff walls along the upstream
and downstream ends to minimize seepage along the contact and
subsequent scouring The cutoff walls should extend at least 3 feet
below the invert of the box
If the riprap is grouted we recommend that drainholes be installed
so that excess water pressure under the grouted riprap may be
released
Backfill Behind Walls
On site sandy material can be utilized as backfill behind the box
culvert walls wingwalls and the two sides of the control gate The
backfill should be compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established
by ASTM D 1557 test method
Design Bearing and Lateral Pressures
The box culvert wingwails and the control gate may be designed
using an allowable soil bearing pressure up to 3000 p s f on the base
coarse backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1455 00 Page 8
The lateral earth pressures on both structures should be considered
as acting on a restrained wall The design earth pressures should
consist of that developed by an equivalent fluid weighing 45 p c f In
addition surcharge load may be converted to an equivalent fill height of
1 foot for every 100 p s f of uniform surcharge load
Lateral loads acting in the direction of the stream on the control
gate may be resisted by passive earth pressure developed against the
buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by friction resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 145 p c f The frictional resistance may be
calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety
Settlement of Proposed Structures
Minimal settlement less than half of an inch is anticipated for
both the new culvert and the control gate
Dewatering
It is anticipated that the site will be dewatered for the construc
tion of both the culvert and the control gate The method of dewatering
should be left open to the contractor provided adequate stability of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1455 00 Page 9
excavation is maintained The deposits encountered in the borings
consisted mostly of coarse to medium fine sand which are relatively
permeable and susceptible to piping and quick conditions
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines pumping clear water only and should be coordinated with
the excavation support so that the stability of the excavation is not
jeopardized The operations should be carried out without creating a
quick condition or softening the bottom of the excavation Improper
and excessive sump pumping can cause large ground settlement to the
nearby areas
Pile Foundation
We believe that the spread footing foundation system as described
above can provide moderate resistance to scouring However should an
extremely high resistance to scouring be desired a pile foundation
system could be utilized Based on our findings good bearing layer
which consists of highly cemented sandstone can be located within 10 feet
below the bottom of the new culvert and the control gate
For bearing pile analysis a bearing capacity of 10 tons per square
foot can be used for the cemented sandstone
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1455 00 Page 10
Predrilling is probably necessary for the installation of piles
since very hard pile driving is anticipated above the sandstone A
minimum penetration of 5 feet into the sandstone is recommended
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the construction of the new culvert and
control gate should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the dewatering excavation and backfilling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction This
is to see that an adequate bearing has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
