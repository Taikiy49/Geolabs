In accordance with your request we have prepared this letter to document the
earthwork and grading operations performed for the house pads at Area 5 Phase E of
the Ewa by Gentry Development Our observation and testing of the earthwork and
grading operations took place from November 1993 through March 1996 Partial grading
at building locations was performed at the same period with mass grading operations of
Phases A through D and the final grading was completed during regrading operations of
Phase 5E The mass grading and regrading operations were performed by Oahu
Construction Company
During the earthwork and grading operations engineering technicians from
Geolabs Hawaii observed the lot grading operations at the site for the house pads
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the grading process consisted of
scarifying the pad subgrade to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned the
subgrade soils to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction Following the subgrade preparation approximately 2 feet of
non expansive coral capping fill was placed and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction To the best of our knowledge and based on the on site survey
stakes we believe that the 2 foot of non expansive capping fills were provided at the
building pad locations

This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the house pads roadway regrading and utility construction at Phase E of the above
referenced project from December 1995 through March 1996 in general accordance with

SITE GRADIN
The subject site is located in the Ewa by Gentry development in the District of Ewa
on the island of Oahu Hawaii A general layout of the project site is provided on the Site
Plan Plate 1 The house pads roadway regrading operations and utility construction
were performed by Oahu Construction Company The site grading consisted of clearing
placement and compaction of engineered fills and utility trench excavation and backfill

Following the clearing operations the exposed subgrades were scarified brought
to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill and backfill operations the fill and backfill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The upper 2 feet of fills below the finish
grade and road subgrade generally consist of non expansive capping fill materials The
fill and backfill materials at the finish subgrade levels were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill and
backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of 142 field density tests including retesis of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The
results of the compaction tests taken during the Area 5 E house pad roadway regrading
and utility construction are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 of this report These
tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered fills
and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the house pads
and roadway regrading and utility construction observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii in
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the above referenced report dated and the addendum
to the soils report dated

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the
