Dear Mr Bumgardner
This letter report presents the results of the percolation tests performed for the
new wastewater disposal system at the Konawaena High School Our work was
performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated May 8 2007
Project Considerations
Konawaena High School is in the Kealakekua area in the District of South Kona on
the Island of Hawaii We understand injection wells and or leach fields are planned for the
restroom facilities at the school We understand the State of Hawaii Department of
Health requires performance of percolation tests at the specific site to evaluate the
suitability of the site location for use of an individual wastewater system Therefore it
was desired to conduct field percolation testing at the leach field locations
Percolation Tests
Five field percolation tests were performed at the proposed injection well and or
leach field areas for Konawaena High School The field percolation tests were
performed in general accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s
Administrative Rules Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the
Ten State s Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface materials for
disposal of wastewater effluent
The percolation tests were performed in approximately 4 inch diameter holes of
about 5 to 16 5 feet deep Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom
of the boreholes to protect the bottom of the boreholes from scouring and sediments
During testing the test holes were carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches
above the gravel layer and refilled as necessary It should be noted that 6 inches of
head could not be established in Percolation Test No 3 due to the very porous nature of
