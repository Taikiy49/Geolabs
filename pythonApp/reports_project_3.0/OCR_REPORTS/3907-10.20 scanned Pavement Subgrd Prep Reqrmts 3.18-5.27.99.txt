Kauai Intermediate School
Increments Il
Dear Ms Warren
In accordance with your request we have been providing field observations and
compaction testing services during the site grading for the above referenced project This
letter is presented to provide clarification of the pavement subgrade preparation
requirements for the subject project
As stated in our geotechnical engineering report prepared for the subject project
the pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 92 percent relative compaction The compaction requirements for the
pavement subgrades have been reduced from the normal 95 percent relative compaction
due to the high rainfall in this area

Response to Request for Information No 5
Proctor Tests for On Site Material
Kauai Intermediate School
Increment One
Dear Mr Lee
In response to the Request for Information RFI No 5 dated February 4 by
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company pertaining to the Proctor Tests for On Site
Material we are providing the following response and comment for your review and
consideration
Based on our review of the documents transmitted in RFI No 5 we understand
that it was requested that the AASHTO T 99 proctor test method be adopted for the
on site soils in lieu of the proctor test method as stated in the project
specifications The AASHTO T 99 test method will require the use of a hammer
with a 12 inch drop as opposed to a 10 lb hammer with an 18 inch drop per the
test method
It is our opinion that the proctor value produced by the AASHTO T 99 test method
will be significantly lower than that by the test method due to the
substantial difference in energy level applied during testing i e lower hammer weight and
height of drop Therefore we believe that the degree of compaction of the on site soil
obtained using the AASHTO T 99 test method would be significantly lower than that using
the test method Therefore it is our opinion that the use of AASHTO T 99
proctor test method is not acceptable for the subject project

Footing Subgrade Preparation Requirements
Kauai Intermediate School
Increment One
Dear Ms Warren
In accordance with your request we have been providing field observations and
compaction testing services during the site grading and foundation excavations for the
above referenced project This letter is presented to provide clarification on the footing
subgrade preparation requirements for the subject project
As stated in the notes of the construction plans prepared for the subject project
where footing subgrades are located in cut areas footings bearing on the undisturbed
in situ soils the bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete For footing subgrades in these conditions a visual observation and probing of
the footing subgrade should be performed by our field personnel to confirm the subgrade
conditions of the footings The footing subgrades should be firm and unyielding A
compaction test of the footing subgrade is not required for these conditions since the
subgrade exist in a relatively high moisture condition
For footings bearing on new engineered fills where the footing subgrades are
located in fill areas the bottom of footing excavations should be recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete In the fill conditions compaction tests should be performed in the footing
excavations to confirm that the compaction requirements of the subgrades are met
It should be emphasized that if the footing subgrades are saturated or softened
after the visual observations probing or compaction testing due to inclement weather
the footing subgrades may need to be over excavated to expose firm ground and
backfilled with compacted fill As an alternative to backfilling with compacted fill the
bottom of footing may be extended deeper to bear on the firm over excavated subgrade
