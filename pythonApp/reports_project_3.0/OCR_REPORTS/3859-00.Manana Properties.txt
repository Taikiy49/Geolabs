In accordance with our proposal dated September 12th we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration at Building No 16 of the Manana Properties in the
Pearl City area on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings
from our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of the proposed project These
recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Building No 16 within the existing Manana Properties
in the Pearl City area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on
the information provided we understand that it is proposed to demolish part of the
existing asphalt concrete AC paving inside the warehouse building and replace it with
moderate storage loads and forklift traffic
Based on our field observations and the information provided the existing
warehouse is a single story building with wood trusses and metal construction The
building measures approximately 504 feet long and 96 feet wide in plan dimension The

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the slab on grade design and
site preparation of the subject project The work was performed in general accordance
with our fee proposal dated Our scope of work included the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of six borings to a depth of about 11 feet below the
existing ground surface for a total of 68 lineal feet of exploration
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
geologist from our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed improvements at
the subject site
7 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 to a depth of about 11 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a surficial AC pavement consisting of
3 inches of asphalt concrete over 3 inches of basalt gravel This was generally underlain
by residual soils consisting of very stiff to hard clayey silts extending to the maximum
depth explored of 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
seasonal precipitation and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 8 Laboratory testing was performed
on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented on Plates 9 and 10
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the subject site generally encountered a surficial AC
pavement over very stiff to hard residual soils extending to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface Our laboratory swell test
results indicate that the near surface residual soils exhibit slight to low swelling and
shrinkage potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore it is our opinion
that concrete slabs may be constructed on grade provided that the subgrade is prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of
this report A 6 inch layer of compacted base course is recommended below the
concrete slab for more uniform support Detailed discussion and recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this letter report
Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade are planned to replace part of the
existing AC pavement at the subject warehouse building The new concrete
slabs on grade may be subjected to light to moderate storage loads and vehicular traffic
such as forklifts and pick up trucks In order to reduce the potential for distress due to
differential movements between the heavily loaded slabs on grade and the existing
foundations we recommend that free floating slabs on grade with no structural
connections to the wall and column foundations be used Joint filler and sealant may be
used to fill the opening between the edge of slab and other structural elements

Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the vehicular traffic anticipated
for the warehouse building it is our opinion that the new slabs on grade may be
supported on 6 inches of compacted base course over the in situ very stiff to hard
residual soils The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should be prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this
report
We recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural engineer for the
intended loads and should be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some
amount of differential settlement For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base
course A minimum slab thickness of 5 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic
Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project
construction In general the following guidelines should be used for site grading At the
on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed All asphalt concrete paving vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below any future slab areas
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
After clearing grubbing and demolition the future slab areas should be
excavated where necessary to the slab subgrade level i e bottom of the base course
layer Our laboratory tests indicate that the surficial residual soils exhibit slight to low
potential for swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
structural distress of the slabs on grade resulting from the swelling of the near surface
clayey soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent
above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil
be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas
and replacement with well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Base course materials required for the new slabs on grade should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Imported fill
materials if required should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less
than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those encountered in our field exploration

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the building lines and bay numbers shown on the Existing
Roof Framing Plan transmitted to our office on September 8th The physical locations of
the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tidal fluctuation temperature and other
factors
