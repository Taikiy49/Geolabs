Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the fifteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in the borings drilled at the project site A list of the piezometer
installations for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of
Instrument Installations The fifteen operational piezometers are installed in the borings
identified as Boring Nos P 1 through P 8 as shown on Plate 2
Three piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8 consist of dedicated vibrating
wire piezometer installations that provide a near continuous record of the water level
encountered in the well casing The remaining 12 piezometers consist of simple standpipe
well casings which are monitored manually on about a bi monthly basis
It should be noted that the standpipe Piezometer P 2B has experienced a blockage
in the pipe at a depth of about 22 feet which has rendered the instrument as
non functional for further seepage monitoring

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
A reconnaissance of the Kuahea Street Area Movement project site was conducted
on to record current observations pertaining to the existing site
conditions In general no new ground surface deformations or surface water seepage
conditions were observed in comparison with our previous site reconnaissance
Waiomao Road Central Waiomao Landslide Area
Based on our visual observations no significant changes in ground surface
deformation were observed Ground surface deformation appears to be generally
static Specifically the open landscaped areas on both sides of Waiomao Road
show minimal visible evidence of earth movement In addition the Waiomao Road
surfaces exhibit no new pavement deformation other than pre existing broad
undulating pavement conditions and arc shaped cracks previously observed
across the central portion of the landslide

Kuahea Street Waiomao Landslide Head Area
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide indicates that the roadway pavement and northern road
shoulder continue to experience settlement of the ground surfaces The ground
settlements have produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that
traverse the roadway Some open cracks and localized holes about 2 to 4 inches
wide with settlement depressions of about 1 foot in diameter have formed along
the existing pavement repair patches in the center of the road Void space
underlying the larger open cracks in the roadway was observed Settlement within
the southern half of the paved roadway has produced regions conducive to the
ponding of surface rainwater It appears that some uplift may be occurring along the
southern roadside along the interface between the asphalt pavement and concrete
gutter The uplift may be contributing to the ponding of water along the southern
half of the roadway Based on our observation of the slowly increasing roadway
deformation re leveling of depressed sections of roadway and patching of the open
cracks is recommended to reduce the ponding and infiltration of runoff into the
pavement subgrade
Finally the open landscaped slopes on the northern side of Kuahea Street show
minimal visible evidence of earth movement or ground surface deformation
Kipona Place Waiomao Landslide Toe Area
From our observation of the toe of slope and street area along Kipona Place we
conclude that no overt visible sign of ground surface deformation related to the
Waiomao Landslide earth movement is occurring
Kuahea Street Area A
The primary area of ground surface deformation within Kuahea Street Area A was
observed to extend between about 2326 Kuahea Street and 2221 Kuahea Place
Based on our observations the temporary pavement repairs on Kuahea Street are
in generally fair condition with some thin cracks visible in the elongated narrow
concrete patches By visual observation the undulatory condition of the existing
concrete pavement on Kuahea Street appears to be generally static or only slowly
increasing in magnitude and no significant pavement surface rupture was
observed The pavement settlement and roadway deformation on Kuahea Place
continues to slowly increase with clearly defined lateral movement boundaries
adjacent to the area of settlement We observed some new retaining wall
construction occurring along the property boundary between 2293 and
2326 Kuahea Street The new retaining wall is along the existing storm drainage
easement at the western side of 2326 Kuahea Street It should be noted that the
underground drainage pipe within the easement has experienced deformation
