In accordance with our fee proposal dated March 15 2005 we performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Kapolei Parkway West project in the
Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings
from our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents our findings and
recommendations for the design of the project The findings and recommendations are
intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided it is desired to extend the western side of the
existing Kapolei Parkway in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new
roadway extension will traverse approximately 1 950 feet to the west extending from the
intersection of Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kapolei Parkway towards the existing power line
area on the Ko Olina side The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The new roadway extension for the Kapolei Parkway West will be constructed in
two phases Phase 1 of the roadway extension project will extend from the intersection
with Kalaeloa Bouevard to the west end of the Honolulu Advertiser production and
distribution facility The Phase 2 will extend from the west end of the newspaper facility to
the existing power line area on the Ko Olina side as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The new roadway extension will consist of 32 to 52 foot wide travelways with an
8 foot wide sidewalk on both sides separated by a 20 to 40 foot wide median Based on
our field observations and the information provided we anticipate that the majority of the
site grading work will consist of cuts and fills on the order of about 3 feet deep and 1 foot
thick respectively to achieve the design finish grades for the project construction
However cuts of up to about 6 feet deep will be required for the last 200 feet of roadway

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soil data set to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of pavements and site
preparation only To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of
the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of four borings extending to depths of about
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of approximately
86 lineal feet of field exploration
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
field engineer
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and
evaluating their engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data to formulate geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of pavements and
site preparation
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our senior engineer
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and the Logs of Borings are
presented on Plates 3 through 7 of this report The results of the laboratory tests
performed on selected soil samples are presented on Plates 8 through 12

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
As mentioned previously the proposed new Kapolei Parkway West extension is
located in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate limits of the
new roadway extension are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations the majority of the proposed roadway extension
generally consists of vacant lands moderately vegetated with small trees and tall
grasses Dry and loose soils were exposed at the existing ground surface with potholes
and trenches caused by soil shrinkage in the surface clayey soil A long narrow strip of
asphalt concrete paved driveway was installed along the north side of the Honolulu
Advertiser site to serve the newspaper production and distribution facility Based on the
plan and profile drawings provided the existing ground elevations within the new
roadway alignment generally range from about 65 feet Mean Sea Level MSL near the
intersection with Kalaeloa Boulevard to about 70 feet MSL at the existing power line
area
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers Two bulk samples designated as
Bulk 1 and Bulk 2 were obtained from the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement
support characteristics The approximate locations of the borings drilled and bulk
samples collected at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density swelling potential and strength
characteristics The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples
obtained from the field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Two sets of Corrosion tests including Minimum Resistivity
Chloride Content and Sulfate Content were
performed by Oceanic Analytical Laboratory Inc and Del Mar Analytical Irvine on
selected soil samples obtained from our field exploration to evaluate the corrosivity of
the in situ soils to buried metallic and concrete substructures The results of these tests
are summarized on Plate 8
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the clayey soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the
soils The test results are presented on Plates 9 and 10 of this report
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557A were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates 11 and 12
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration disclosed that the majority of the roadway extension
alignment is underlain by very stiff to hard clayey silts extending to a depth of about 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Below the upper clayey silt soils our drilled borings
generally encountered medium dense to dense silty and sandy coralline gravel
interbedded with occasional layers of hard silty clays extending to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal
precipitation surface water runoff and other factors Descriptions and graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings the principal
geotechnical considerations for the project design include proper site preparation
requirements which take into consideration the expansive nature of the near surface soils
and the relatively dry condition of the soils and the site Detailed discussion of our
geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of pavements and site preparation
are presented in the following sections of this report
Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration the proposed new roadway alignment is generally
underlain by very stiff to hard clayey silts at the ground surface Laboratory testing
performed on the surface clayey soils indicated CBR values of about 3 1 and 3 7 and
swells of 4 2 and 4 5 percent Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
we recommend that the following new pavement section be used for preliminary design
and cost estimating purposes
Pavement Design
4 0 Inches Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inches Asphalt Concrete Base 91 Percent Relative Specific Gravity
18 0 Inches Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
28 0 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to greater than 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity
determined in accordance with ASTM D 2041 The aggregate subbase should consist of
crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Our experience in the Kapolei area suggests that the swelling potential of the in situ
soils could be highly variable in nature Therefore CBR and field density tests should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the
adequacy of the above section The above recommended pavement section assumes that
good drainage will be provided for the paved areas
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to
carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water
ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after construction

Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the
subgrade soils below the base and subbase course layers to reduce the potential for
migration of excessive landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excess water from the landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system be
considered along the sides of the roadway
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish grades for the
proposed roadway project we anticipate that site grading work consisting of cuts and
fills on the order of about 3 feet deep and 1 foot thick respectively will be required for
the project construction In general the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris
deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firm and or stiff material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed
off site
Existing structures and pavements to be demolished should be completely
removed The over excavations resulting from demolition should be backfilled with
compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities to
be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
Our field observations indicate that shrinkage cracks and ground depressions are
present at the existing ground surface along the roadway extension alignment In
general shrinkage cracks will develop in the ground due to the lack of maintenance of
the soil moisture content as the surface soils start to dry out under the sun and dry
weather conditions The development of shrinkage cracks in the ground was apparently
intensified by the prolonged dry climatic conditions characteristic of the Kapolei area
resulting in large fluctuations of the soil moisture content
If the existing ground cracks are left unattended for an extended period of time
and are allowed to penetrate deeper below the surface the ground cracks could
adversely affect the performance of the future roadway The ground cracks could serve
as conduits for water to percolate into the pavement subgrade soils and cause loss of
strength of the subgrade soils resulting in potential settlement of the pavement
structure Therefore remedial work to correct or mitigate the observed ground cracks

After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades should be proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and
collapsing near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks and
ground depressions The exposed subgrades should be _ proof rolled without
moisture conditioning of the subgrade soils at the pre existing moisture contents
Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation should be backfilled with
general fill material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The
proof rolling operations should be conducted under the near continuous observation of
a Geolabs representative
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be scarified and re prepared as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to greater than 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity
determined in accordance with ASTM D 2041 The aggregate subbase material should
meet the requirements of Section 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications
September 1986 The material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Imported fill materials below the subbase course layer should consist of

of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 The materials should be moisture conditioned to above

Utility Trenches
We anticipate that the utilities for the new roadway project will consist primarily of
water drain and sewer lines In general good construction practices should be utilized for
the installation and backfilling of the trenches for the new utilities at the site The method
and equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations should
comply with the applicable federal state and local safety requirements
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that conventional
excavation methods may generally be utilized for utility line excavation at the site The
upper clayey soils encountered at the site are generally very stiff to hard However some
of the utility trench excavations will likely encounter the dense coralline materials as
described in the boring logs which may pose some excavation difficulties during the
trench excavation
In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils with a maximum
particle size of 6 inches or select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement finished grade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction

Corrosion Protection for Water Lines
Two sets of pH Minimum Resistivity Chloride and Sulfate content tests were
performed to evaluate the corrosivity of the on site soils generally encountered at the
site The results of these tests are presented on Plate 8 of this report The minimum
resistivity values of the near surface soil samples tested are on the order of about
5 420 and 7 210 ohm cm which correspond to a Corrosion Rating of 4 moderately
corrosive based on the guidelines provided by the City County of Honolulu Board of
Water Supply Therefore we recommend all metallic substructures such as ductile iron
water lines in contact with the soils be properly designed for protection against potential
corrosion

construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
