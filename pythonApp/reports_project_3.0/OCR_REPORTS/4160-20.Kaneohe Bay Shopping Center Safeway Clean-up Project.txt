performed for the existing Safeway store building within the Kaneohe Bay Shopping

at the end of this letter report

of removing the PCB contaminated soils The majority of the contaminated soils have
already been excavated and currently this excavation is about 700 square feet in plan
view and about 5 to 10 feet deep It is our understanding that the excavation will be
deepened and extended laterally The removal may also extend below the existing
Safeway store by a lateral distance of about 3 feet and thereby undermine the existing
foundations and slab

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the service drive area behind the Safeway store
and behind a vacant store within the building between Safeway and Longs Drug Store
The area is fairly flat with an elevation of about 89 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In
general the site is covered with deteriorated asphalt pavement
The current excavation is bounded by the vacant store to the southwest
two concrete pads to the northwest and the Safeway store to the northwest The
southeast boundary of the excavation is about 30 feet beyond the Safeway store The
northeast boundary of the excavation is about 35 to 50 feet beyond the vacant store
At the northern concrete pad the excavation slope is nearly vertical At the
northeastern side of the western concrete pad and at the Safeway store the excavation
slope is at an inclination of about one half horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V At the
southeastern side of the western concrete pad the excavation slope is at an inclination of
about 1H 1V At the other sides the excavation slope is at an inclination of about 2H 1V
to 1H 1V
The Safeway building is estimated to be about 30 years old It is a one story
structure composed of CMU concrete masonry units walls lightly reinforced concrete
footings perimeter wall and spread footings and slabs on grade

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Underpinning System
Based on the results of our exploration we recommend the installation of Atlas
piers below the exterior perimeter footing to support the building foundation We
understand that the existing perimeter wall footing load is estimated at about 3 kips per
lineal foot We also understand that the existing exterior spread footing load is estimated
at about 18 kips
The structural support may consist of Atlas piers extending to below the soft clayey
material and bearing on the underlying basalt formation at depths of about 20 feet below
the existing ground surface The Atlas piers should be sleeved to allow for greater rigidity
for the 12 foot unsupported height The Atlas piers should consist of galvanized steel
We recommend a total of 6 Atlas piers at the following locations
e one Atlas pier at the exterior spread footing located about 20 feet southwest
from the corner of the Safeway building
one Atlas pier at the corner of the Safeway building
e two Atlas piers between the above mentioned locations
one Atlas pier at the corner of the northern concrete pad and the Safeway
building and
one Atlas pier at the midpoint of the northern concrete pad which supports
an electrical transformer
Installation of Atlas piers normally involves lifting the footing about one fourth
to one half 4 inch to increase the amount of positive load transfer CMU structures are
GEOLABS INC
Safeway Inc Page 4

generally sensitive to settlement and differential movements Since the Safeway building
is a CMU structure and a relatively old structure built to old building standards we
anticipate that some small cracking may occur during the above procedure and during the
removal of contaminated soil beneath the building We believe that the cracking will be
more of a cosmetic problem than a structural problem We therefore recommend that
cosmetic work and patch work to the cracks in the walls floors and ceilings proceed after
the filling of the excavation is complete
Installation of Atlas piers will likely involve over excavation of about 3 feet laterally
beyond the Atlas pier location to allow installation of the bracket and pipe pile jacking
assembly The excavation will also extend laterally about 1 feet beneath the footing
Based on the foundation plans the footings appear to be large enough that enlargement
of the footing for proper support and resistance during installation will not be needed The
Atlas piers should be jacked downward until a bearing capacity of 20 000 pounds based
on a 3 5 inch outside diameter steel pipe is obtained and to a minimum depth of 20 feet
below the ground surface The maximum Atlas pier spacing should be at intervals of
6 feet on center
lf the undermining excavation below the building is anticipated to exceed a lateral
distance of 3 feet additional piers below the slab may be considered However due to
the minimal existing slab thickness the span distance between piers should be limited to
about 3 feet The actual span distance should be determined by the structural engineer
An alternative to Atlas piers would be to excavate and fill under the building in
small 3 to 5 foot increments with temporary building supports However we believe that
this method may not be cost effective due to the incremental construction and is more
likely to result in damage to the existing building Therefore we believe that the Atlas
pier system should be utilized
Filling of Excavation
Earthwork operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Inc It is
important that a representative from our office observe the earthwork operations to
evaluate whether undesirable materials in terms of engineering properties not
environmental properties are encountered during the site preparation or filling process
and to confirm that the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to that encountered in our
field exploration
It is our understanding that imported clay material will be utilized in the lower
section of the excavation and select borrow material will be utilized in the upper 5 foot
section of the excavation A 10 mil liner material is proposed around the edges of the
excavation Either the liner material will be wrapped around the clay material or a

non woven geotextile fabric AMOCO 4546 or equivalent will be utilized between the clay
material and the select borrow material to prevent penetration of the granular material into
the clay material
Prior to filling and placement of the 10 mil liner material vegetation debris
rubbish or other unsuitable materials should be removed from the excavation and
disposed of properly off site
The clay fill material should consist of material with more than 50 percent passing
the 200 sieve less than 3 inches in largest dimension and free of vegetation and
deleterious materials The select borrow material should conform to City and County of
Honolulu standards for select borrow subbase material These standards are stated in
Section 31 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Department of
Public Works City and County of Honolulu September 1986 Imported fill materials
should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use
Below the existing structures CLSM controlled low strength concrete material or
flowable fill should be utilized CLSM may also be used in lieu of compacted fill
material outside of the building footprint if desired Note that if CLSM is utilized it should
be utilized above and below the utility pipes to allow encasement and reduce surcharge
on the pipe
CLSM should have a 28 day compressive strength of 50 to 250 psi and a
maximum aggregate size of sand 4 Sieve Size if it is desired to allow possible future
excavation with normal construction equipment CLSM should have a maximum setting
time of 24 hours where the setting time is defined as the amount of time required to
achieve a compressive strength of 12 psi CLSM should have no corrosive material and
all materials within the mix should meet ACI standards
Clay fills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction Select borrow subbase materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The upper 2 feet of select borrow subbase material should be compacted to
95 percent relative compaction Saturation and subsequent yielding of the fills due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with properly compacted fill material
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with

ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
of dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by
Geolabs Inc during earthwork to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of
compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required
additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as
necessary to obtain the specified compaction
Utilities
We understand that utilities removed during the excavation process will be
replaced with appropriate reconnections The methods and equipment to be used for
excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety
considerations The excavation should comply with all applicable local state and federal
safety requirements Trench shoring design and installation should be made the
responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the
appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of clay soil or select borrow
subbase material as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from the field boring Variations of subsoil conditions
beyond the field boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this letter report
The boring location in the field was determined by taping from structures indicated
on the Topographic Plan by ControlPoint Surveying Inc dated The
elevation of the boring was also estimated from this plan The location and elevation of
the field boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring was measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and interpretations made in the formulation of this letter report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions perched water lenses and other factors

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
one boring designated as Boring No 1 to a depth of about 24 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring
was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger equipment
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 and D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch O D standard penetration sampler and a 3 inch
O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch
drive are shown on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
