This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering consultation
performed in support of the design and construction of the micropile underpinning system
planned at the existing Beachwalk Wastewater Pump Station WWPS in connection with
the on going Beachwalk Emergency Contingency Sewer Bypass Force Main project in the
Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our literature review and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design installation and testing of the micropile
underpinning system planned at the existing Beachwalk WWPS only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
Project Considerations
According to the geotechnical report entitled Beachwalk Wastewater Pump
Station Condition Assessment Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated January 2008 prepared by
Yogi Kwong Engineers LLC YKE the new Beachwalk WWPS replacement dual force
mains and the existing 42 inch force main are supported by jet grouted columns
extending to stiff soils If the pump station is not underpinned to reduce potential future
settlements such as those induced by construction activities the potential differential
movements between the new force mains supported by jet grout and unlikely to settle
and the existing force main between the new force main and the pump station and the
pump station itself may cause future deflection or damage to the unsupported section of
the existing force main just outside the pump station Therefore it is recommended that
the existing pump station and associated surface structure be underpinned to reduce

lacustrine deposits lagoonal deposits and older alluvium including probable tuffaceous

The fill materials encountered in the exploratory borings by YKE generally
consisted of medium dense to dense silty sands and gravel with cobbles and boulders
extending to depths of approximately 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the upper fill materials lacustrine deposits consisting of loose silty sands and

and fat clays and very loose to medium dense liquefiable silty to clayey coralline sands
and gravel were encountered below the lacustrine deposits and extended to depths of
about 35 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the lagoonal deposits older alluvial deposits were encountered and
extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 47 feet below the existing
ground surface The older alluvial deposits generally consisted of medium stiff to very stiff
silts and elastic silts and loose to medium dense silty cinder sands

Micropile Foundations
To reduce potential movements of the structure due to possible construction
activities in the adjoining areas in the future we understand that cased micropile
foundations will be used to underpin the existing wastewater pump station structure In
general the cased micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually less
than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile foundation
typically is constructed by drilling a hole with or without casing placing reinforcing steel in
the hole and grouting the hole Micropiles are desirable because they can be installed
readily in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and ground
conditions In addition the micropile installation generally causes minimal disturbance to
adjacent structures the adjacent soils and the environment
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
a cased micropile foundation system with a grout bulb diameter of 5 5 or 7 0 inches may
be used to underpin the existing wastewater pump station structure We understand that
the micropiles will need to be designed based on a minimum allowable compressive load
capacity of 13 kips with a factor of safety of at least 2

compressible lacustrine and lagoonal deposits will occur at the project site When the soft

movement will result in negative skin friction forces generally known as downdrag forces
acting on the micropile shaft The magnitude of the downdrag force depends on the

Based on our engineering analyses we estimate that the downdrag loads imposed

deposits may be on the order of about 15 and 19 kips for the 5 5 and 7 0 inch diameter
micropiles respectively We believe that the downdrag loads imposed on the micropile
foundations as a result of soil liquefaction may be considered a transient load
Ultimate Compressive Load Capacities
Based on the anticipated downdrag loads discussed above and an allowable
compressive load capacity of 13 kips with a factor of safety of 2 the proposed micropiles
would need to be designed based on an ultimate compressive load capacity of 41 and 45
kips for the 5 5 and 7 0 inch diameter micropiles respectively
Recommended Micropile Lengths
In order to develop these compressive load capacities the micropiles would need
to extend through the potentially compressible liquefiable lacustrine and lagoonal deposits
and embed into the older alluvial deposits anticipated at depths of about 40 feet below the
existing ground surface
Based on our engineering analyses we believe the 5 5 and 7 0 inch diameter
micropiles would need to be embedded a minimum of 14 and 12 feet into the older alluvial
deposits in order to provide an allowable compressive load capacity of 13 kips with a
minimum factor of safety of 1 1 during seismic events These depths generally correspond
to a total length of approximately 54 and 52 feet below the existing ground surface for the
5 5 and 7 0 inch diameter micropiles respectively The micropiles would derive its vertical
support primarily from skin friction between the grout and the surrounding medium stiff to
very stiff silts and elastic silts and loose to medium dense silty cinder sands Detailed
calculations are presented on Plates 1 and 2 with this report
Based on the subsurface conditions at the project site we recommend casing the
micropiles permanent casing at the top The permanent casing should have an outside
diameter OD of about 5 5 or 7 0 inches same as the grout bulb size and the permanent
casing should extend about 40 feet below the existing ground surface

strongly recommend conducting a micropile load test program consisting of at least one
static load test to further evaluate and validate our assumptions in providing the above
micropile design recommendations for the underpinning of the existing wastewater pump

station at the project site In order to determine whether the contractor s methods of
micropile installation are adequate and to determine the production micropile lengths we
recommend performing at least one pre production compressive load test In general the
purpose of the pre production load test on a micropile is to fulfill the following objectives
To examine the adequacy of the methods and equipment proposed by the
contractor to install the micropiles to the depths required
To assess the contractor s method of drilling and grout injection
To confirm the production micropile length and to evaluate the degree of
long term settlements of the micropile embedded in the older alluvial
deposits
In general the pre production load test should be performed in accordance with
ASTM D 1143 Standard Loading Procedure Based on our experience we believe that
the load test should be conducted no earlier than 7 days after completion of the micropile
installation to allow the grout adequate time to cure Two additional micropiles may be
used for reaction during the compressive load testing of the pre production load test
micropile The reaction micropiles may be installed to depths as deep as the load test
micropile to provide adequate reaction in uplift
The load test micropile should be loaded gradually to the ultimate compressive load
capacity of 41 and 45 kips for the 5 5 and 7 0 inch diameter micropiles respectively We
recommend holding the test load at 200 percent of the design load i e 26 kips for a
minimum of 4 to 8 hours depending on the recorded movements of the load test micropile
The pre production load test is an integral part of the design of the micropile foundation
system Therefore we recommend a Geolabs representative observe the pre production
load test
