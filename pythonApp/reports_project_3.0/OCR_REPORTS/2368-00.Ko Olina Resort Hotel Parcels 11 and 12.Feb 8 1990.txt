INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Ko Olina Resort Hotel project in Ko
Olina Resort in the Leeward district of Ewa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated The present exploration
program does not include the condominium portion of the project
This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended for
construction bidding estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Ko Olina resort hotel is situated near the Lagoon No 1
within the Ko Olina development area in the Leeward district of Ewa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The current development plan is for the
construction of a 16 story hotel and a 7 story parking structure with
roof top tennis courts Based on the information provided us the maximum
anticipated column loads will be on the order of 1200 kips for the hotel
tower and 700 kips for the parking structure
It is our understanding that two 2 finish elevations are planned at
the hotel tower i e elevation 6 00 feet for the service area and
21 00 feet for the function area Therefore a 15 foot grade difference
is anticipated within the structure At the parking structure site the
finish elevations will drop gradually from elevation 17 34 feet to 8 08
feet Also it is planned that the parking structure will be constructed
in two 2 phases
Site grading involving excavations up to 15 feet at the hotel tower
site and 10 feet at the parking structure site is also anticipated

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading basement
excavation retaining walls and foundation design of the hotel tower and
parking structure of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to PPH
Incorporated dated The scope of work is limited to the
following
1 Drilling and sampling sixteen 16 borings to depths ranging
between 19 2 and 141 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading basement excavation
retaining wall and foundation design recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the junction of Roads A and
Cc near recently completed Lagoon No 1 area in the Ko Olina Resort
development The subject site is generally level with elevations ranging
from 16 to 22 feet MSL At the time of exploration the site was planted
with grass PVC pipelines were used for irrigation purposes at the site
The project site was originally used as pasture and for sugar cane
cultivation The site was cleared grubbed and rough graded during the
mass grading efforts of the Ko Olina development project During the mass
grading fills up to 12 to 14 feet had been placed on the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Exploration Program
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling sixteen
16 borings to depths ranging from 19 2 to 141 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Generally deeper borings were drilled at the hotel tower site for
pile foundation design Relatively shallower borings and probes were
performed at the parking structure site to provide information on the
consistency of the upper crust material and its suitability to support
shallow foundations
Subsoil Conditions
Based on the exploration results the subsoils at the two sites
consist of recent fill materials placed during mass grading of the
general Ko Olina Resort area overlying cemented coral sand and medium
dense to loose coralline reef detritus deposits to depths exceeding 80
feet Below the reef detritus deposits the borings then encountered very
stiff alluvial silty clay

Some thin coral ledges and cemented sand layers of varying thickness
were encountered generally between 15 and 30 foot depths The upper
recent fill materials consist mainly of coralline sand and gravel At the
hotel tower site some clayey materials were encountered in the fill
At the parking structure site the borings encountered clinker and
weathered basalt at depths of about 100 feet At the hotel tower site the
very stiff silty clay extended to more than 140 feet and no rock was
encountered in the borings
Groundwater Level
The groundwater level in the borings was noted at different times

groundwater level may vary from elevation 0 00 to about 3 00 feet MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the heavy structural loads 1200 kips per column provided
us we believe excessive total and differential settlements would occur
for the hotel tower if shallow footing foundations are used Therefore
deep friction pile foundations are recommended to support the hotel tower
structure since the borings did not encounter an adequate end bearing
layer to support end bearing piles
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2368 00 Page 6
The vertical pile capacity will develop mainly from friction between
the pile shafts and the reef detritus deposits and the underlying very
stiff alluvial silty clay Predrilling through the upper dense fill and
thin coral ledges is recommended to facilitate pile driving
For the parking structure it is our opinion that the upper compacted
coral fill may provide adequate support for the anticipated structural
loads up to 700 kips per column and shallow spread footing foundations may
be utilized
Site Grading
In general the site grading would consist of excavations up to 15
feet at the hotel tower site for the service area and up to about 10 feet
at the parking structure site
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation rubbish and other
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should
be disposed of properly off site
It is anticipated that the site will be rough graded down to the
finish elevations prior to pile driving for the hotel tower
Excavation and Slopes
Cuts up to about 15 feet into the compacted coral sand and gravel
fill and some in situ cemented coral sand are anticipated
Moderately hard excavations may be anticipated at localized areas
For temporary construction purposes a slope ratio of up to 1 5
horizontal to 1 vertical for cut slopes in the upper fill
materials may be used

Fill Material
Based on the exploration results it appears that the on site upper
fill materials consist mainly of coral sand and gravel At the hotel
tower site however the borings encountered some clayey materials in
the fill
It is our opinion that the on site excavated coral sand and gravel
may be reused as fill material The clayey materials are not
suitable for structural fill but may be used for top soil or
landscape purposes All excavated material greater than 12 inches in
maximum diameter should be processed e g removed or broken down
prior to its use as fill material
The material will require further processing to remove or reduce the
fragments to 6 inches minus size prior to be used as trench and
structural backfill
If required all imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as select
borrow crusher waste cinder or coral The fill material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
transportation to the site

No major fill placement is anticipated for the project The
fill backfill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to

Pile Capacities
fourteen inch 14 inch square prestressed concrete piles with an
allowable vertical pile capacity of 80 tons per pile may be used
The piles may develop the capacity primarily from adhesion between
the the pile shafts and the surrounding reef detritus deposits and
the very stiff alluvial silty clay
A minimm pile spacing of four 4 times the pile diameter on centers
is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII

An allowable lateral pile capacity of up to 12 kips per pile may be
used for design provided that the predrilled holes are properly
backfilled as recommended in the Predrilling Backfilling section
An allowable uplift capacity of 20 tons per pile may be used
Pile Length
Based on the exploration results estimated pile length is 120 feet
below the finish elevations for the deep friction pile foundation
It is anticipated that two 2 sections of pile and a splice can
would be needed to obtain the 120 foot depth Due to uplift capacity
requirement the top pile section should be of minimum 60 feet long
The above pile length is for initial cost estimation purposes The
actual production pile lengths should be determined after a test pile
program
Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy
between 39 000 and 55 000 foot pounds The hammer should be
equipped with an energy level control so that driving within the
loose sandy deposits may be controlled at a reduced energy to
minimize the tensile stresses development within the piles which
could cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2368 00 Page 10
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose
sandy soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled
reduced energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not
induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the whole pile length is
driven down to the finished elevations or the blow counts
steadily increase with depth and reach 5 blows per inch for the
last three inches or when aggregated blow counts are 60 blows
for the last foot
The above criteria are based on our preliminary wave equation
analyses performed for a Delmag D 30 diesel hammer delivering a
maximum rated energy of 54 300 foot pounds to drive a 14 inch square
concrete pile to develop an 80 ton allowable pile capacity The
results of the wave equation analyses for pile driving are presented
The purpose of the wave equation analyses was to provide contractor
the data to select pile driving hammers for production pile driving
Since the results will vary depending on the equipment the contractor
uses a final pile driving wave equation analysis should be performed
when the actual hammer data are available
Predrilling Backfilling
Predrilling through the upper compacted fill layer and thin coral
ledges is recommended for each pile The predrilling would facilitate
pile driving and assure pile plumbness
The predrill depths are 30 feet below the function area of elevation
21 00 feet and 15 feet below the service area of elevation 6 00 feet
The predrill depths should be verified and revised if necessary after a
test pile program

After installation of piles in the predrilled holes each pile
location should be backfilled with calcareous sand prior to pile driving
The calcareous sand should have a minimum sand equivalent value of 70 per
ASTM Test Method D 2419 74 A lateral pile capacity of 12 kips per pile
may be used for design assuming the predrilled holes are properly
backfilled prior to driving
Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles with the use of one or more
selected hammers
2 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the predrill depths
and driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program
should consist of a minimum of 40 test piles The test piles may be
incorporated into the actual foundation system later
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical
engineer should be consulted for selection of the test pile locations
The test piles should be casted 10 feet longer than the estimated pile
length Also the test pile program should provide the flexibility of
addition or relocation of the test piles as needed in order to suit the
ground conditions
Splice cans are anticipated to be used for the deep friction piles
Splice cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least 5 feet long

The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be
used for production pile driving The test pile program should he

During the test pile driving period the geotechnical engineer may

The geotechnical engineer should evaluate the final pile load

test pile driving and the pile load test

APPENDIX Cc
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using
Program WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP
originally written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway
Administration to solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides ultimate resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate pile capacity of the pile
The analysis was performed for driving 14 inch square prestressed
concrete piles with a DEIMAG D 30 hammer The relationships of ultimate
capacity and hammer blowcounts commonly known as the bearing graph are
presented on Plate C 1 together with input information The maximum
compressive stresses are presented on Plate C 2
