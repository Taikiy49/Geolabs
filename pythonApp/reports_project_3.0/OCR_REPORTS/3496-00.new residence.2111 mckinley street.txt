INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new residence at 2111 McKinley Street in the Manoa area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 2111 McKinley Street in the Manoa area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The majority of the subject lot is presently
occupied by two single story structures of wood construction
It is proposed to demolish the existing structures and construct a 2 story
residential dwelling at the subject site Based on the information provided the finish
floor elevations of the garage and the house slab will be at approximately 218 5 and
222 8 feet Mean Sea Level MSL respectively Concrete access driveway and utility
installation are also anticipated for the project construction Based on the information
provided by the project structural engineer the maximum wall load for the proposed
residential dwelling is on the order of about 2 kips per lineal foot Minimal site grading
work is anticipated for the proposed construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epoch by the activities of two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Koolau
is the younger of the two and reached the end of its main shield building phase about
2 million years ago The project site is located on the southern flank of Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
Koolau was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from northwest to southeast
trending rift zones These lava flows are interbedded pahoehoe and a a flows with
pahoehoe being the dominant form After the cessation of the main shield building
phase of Koolau there was a long period of erosion The project site is situated in
Manoa Valley which is an amphitheater headed valley which was deeply eroded into
the Koolau shield
During the Pleistocene Epoch there was a renewal of volcanic activity with the
eruptions of vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series Along the crest of the ridge along
the westerly side of Manoa Valley there are the Tantalus Roundtop and Sugarloaf
vents When these vents were active one or more lava flows from these vents spilled
down the westerly wall of the valley and moved across the valley floor These lavas
ponded and filled the lower portion of the valley The ponding allowed the lava to
densify as it cooled forming dense massive rock
Other eruptive events of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled these lava flows
with pyroclastic material particularly sand sized cinder Subsequent fluvial action has
transported minor amounts of alluvial soils onto the site During the development of the
project site man made fills were placed to grade the area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is underlain by a surficial layer
of fill materials over dense cinder sands extending to the maximum depth explored
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site and the anticipated light
structural loadings it is our opinion that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the recompacted surficial fill soils or underlying dense
cinder sands may be used for support of the proposed new residence
Based on our laboratory testing the surficial fill soils generally exhibit a slight to
low potential of shrinking swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore
it is our opinion that concrete slabs on grade may be constructed on grade provided
that the subgrade is moisture conditioned and prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this report Detailed
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds
per square foot p s f may be used for the design of footings bearing on the
recompacted near surface fill soils or underlying dense cinder sands This bearing
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades If the footings are located adjacent to a slope the footings
should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum 6 foot set back distance
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3496 00 Page 8
measured from the outside edge of the footings horizontally to the slope face If
foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines We
estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing
on the recompacted in situ soils or underlying dense cinder sands as recommended
herein to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted surficial fill soils
or underlying dense cinder sands
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the near surface fill
soils or underlying cinder sands compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom be extended to the underlying
firm materials

Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
foundation bearing materials and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
residence Our laboratory tests indicate that the surficial fill soils consisting of silty
sands and sandy silts exhibit low to slight potential of swelling when subjected to
moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural distress of the slabs on
grade resulting from the swelling of the near surface fill soils we recommend that the
subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the
soils be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3496 00 Page 10
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer cushion fill consisting of free
draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be
placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the
potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on
top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above the
plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate small amount of possible ground movements
Where the slab will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab and
driveway area a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer The
2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted for the garage and driveway
slabs
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
or select borrow be provided below the concrete walkway slab Where used the select
borrow should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
subgrade soils underneath the sidewalk slabs should be prepared in accordance with
the recommendations presented above Construction joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundations

plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loadings
such as wind or seismic forces

loaded side of the wall a rectangular distribution over the height of the

wall with a pressure equal to 30 and 45 percent of the surcharge load is
normally used for active and at rest conditions respectively
3 For resistance to lateral loads a passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used for wall footings on level areas The passive
pressure should be reduced for walls located on slopes A base friction
factor of 0 35 may also be used to evaluate the resistance against sliding

4 Wall footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches Wall
foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth

potential build up of hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system

Site Preparation
We understand that minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project
construction At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3496 00 Page 14
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density If
zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the subgrade level deeper
over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting
over excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material
Granular material for fills and backfills under the slab and driveway areas should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Granular fill materials should consist of
imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than
3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent
when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain
between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material
should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site
for its intended use

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the project A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or the on site granular soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3496 00 Page 17
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
