SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is
underlain by variable alluvial soils consisting of
silty clay near the surface to silty sand
Laboratory testing and previous experience have
demonstrated that the silty clay soil has moderate
swelling potential when saturated from a dry
condition
To reduce the effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs it is recommended that the site be
capped with a minimum of 18 inches of
non expansive select fill The subgrade soils
should also be protected from drying
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at LIot 3 J Pauoa
Subdivision Pauoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes

subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a

design of the project

visual observations of the surficial soil conditions

the project site and its vicinity

exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located at the northern portion of the Pauoa
Subdivision which is at the eastern end of Pauoa Road The lot is
rectangular shaped with a frontage of 50 feet and a depth of 105 feet
The topography of the site slopes downward from Booth Road with an
average gradient of approximately 12 percent
The current ground elevations range from about 274 feet to 286 feet
along Booth Road
There is an existing drainage swale along the lower boundary of the
lot

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site soil encountered can generally provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care is
exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the soil
as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the near
surface stiff on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has
low to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture
content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce
the effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that
the on site soil be capped with at least 18 inches of non expansive select
fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
the slab concrete placement
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 18 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where minimal fill or cut is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide an 18 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade
Slabs

All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the compacted fill soil or stiff natural ground
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry then saturated Therefore the
on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 18 inches below
the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
GEOLABS HAWAII

adjacent finished grade refer to Plate 3 for details This
over excavation and replacement will not be necessary where more than 18
inches of fill will be placed such as the garage area
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 18 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be reduced to six 6
inches if some amount of small slab cracking are tolerable The subgrade
soils should be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying
out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far
away from the foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering
near foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plans provided Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the plans The physical location and elevation of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote gradual transition the strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
