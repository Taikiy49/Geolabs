SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The geologic character of the existing hillside at MP 19 of Hana Highway may be
classified as undulating basalt rock containing discontinuous joints with adverse orientation
as well as major differential erosion features and moderate difference in erosion rates
The basalt rock hillside is eroding and producing falling rocks ranging from small stones to
large boulders Due to a lack of open shoulder area along the roadway the falling rocks
have been reported to periodically encroach upon the travelway resulting in falling rock
hazards for personal injuries and traffic accidents
Based on the results of our field exploration engineering evaluation and input from
community and various agencies we believe that the rockfall mitigation program for the
hillside at about MP 19 should entail the following measures 1 installation of a rockfall
impact barrier above the toe area of the existing slope and 2 widening of the existing
roadway on the makai side with a cantilevered deck to provide space for a rockfall
catchment area The combination of the rockfall impact barrier and rockfall catchment
area would serve to control falling rocks from encroaching into the travelway
The rockfall impact barrier proposed for construction within the project site consists
of a specialized barrier fence structure which is designed to absorb the energy from
rockfall impact and retain the fallen material Based on our engineering analysis for the
project site the proposed rockfall impact barrier should consist of a 10 foot high barrier
rated at moderate impact energy absorption capacity with a rated capacity encompassing
about 74 foot tons The maximum spacing between the fence posts should be limited to
about 25 feet
Based on our evaluation of the site conditions with respect to the proposed
construction we recommend that a deep foundation system consisting of cast in place
concrete drilled shafts be used to support the new cantilevered deck and guardrail system
The drilled shaft foundations should consist of 60 inch diameter concrete drilled shafts
extending a minimum of 50 feet below the bottom of the drilled shaft cap or footing to
support the structural loading provided by the structural engineer
The text of this report should be referred to for more detailed discussions and
specific design recommendations

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene
Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these
two volcanoes The Isthmus of Maui was created by lava flows from Haleakala ponding
on West Maui and is comprised of alluvium washed from the slopes of West Maui and
East Maui Haleakala The erosional processes are dominated by the detachment of
soil and rock masses from the mountain walls and the soil materials are transported
downslope toward the isthmus primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials
reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant
and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that large
volumes of water are transmitted for very short durations Because of this the transport
of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a
poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the
erosional base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes
begin to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the
materials _ Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the
coarse grained portion of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or
conglomerate Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors
and upstream in headwaters This continued erosion generates material which is
transported downslope covering the older alluvial soil deposits Depending on the local
base level and rate of transport these newer sediments are generally transient in terms
of geologic time In addition their consistency and density are generally less than those
of the older partially consolidated deposits

2 3 Existing Rockfall Conditions
In general the intent for the installation of the rockfall protection system at the
site is to reduce the hazard of falling rocks from the existing steep rocky slopes located
adjacent to the highway Because the project addresses the potentially hazardous
condition of rockfall at the site a definition of rockfall is needed Where cliffs and steep
slopes composed of rock material occur rockfall is a common naturally occurring
process of erosion and mass wasting Rockfall is the detachment and transportation
downslope of individual rock fragments showers of rock fragments or large boulders of
rock material as a result of geologic weathering or disturbance to the slope The fall
may involve more than one rock but does not include large volumes of rock or
landslides
Because of the proximity of the highway to the steep rocky mountain slopes
the rockfall source falling rock that bounces rolls or falls directly onto the highway is
a real problem with a significant potential to cause personal injury and or traffic
accidents Accidents involving personal injury or even death occur as the result of a
direct impact from falling rock and collisions with other vehicles and objects and of
vehicles swerving to avoid fallen material in the roadway

have been documented for the portion of Hana Highway between to 19 52

Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused solely by shifts
in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before
or during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the
underground movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not
erupt The Island of Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but
most of the earthquakes are so small that they may only be detected by sensitive
instruments However some of the earthquakes are strong enough to be felt and
a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on the
Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on
the Island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii
over 90 percent of earthquakes are related to volcanic activity the risk of seismic
activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes with increased distance from the
Island of Hawaii The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the
Island of Hawaii
To a lesser degree the Island of Maui has experienced numerous earthquakes
greater than M5 Therefore moderate to strong earthquakes also have occurred
in the County of Maui For example two substantial earthquakes have been
recorded in the County of Maui including the Maui Earthquake of and
the Lanai Earthquake of 1871 M7

3 1 Rockfall Mechanisms
Rockfall involves the detachment and fall of rock material from the slope face
Rockslide is another term that may be used to describe the detachment from a slope
and fall of a mass of rock material Rockfall is a more general term used to describe the
process of falling rock from a hillslope Rockfall may encompass the fall of a single
boulder or rock fragment or a shower of boulders or rock fragments Some of the
applicable mechanisms generating rockfall events at the site are described below

Loosening of rocks from the slope may occur due to a multitude of causes
including water pressure behind the rock differential weathering or erosion along
geologic contacts faults or shear zones weakening of rock due to temperature and
moisture cyclic fluctuations loosening by animals trees and wind ground vibration due
to earthquake seismic forces construction activity and vibration from loud sound
waves
A frequent cause of rockfalls is by invasive tree roots prying on the existing
cracks located in the rock slope surface Tree roots frequently develop and grow larger
in the discontinuities of rock materials The added force of wind action on tree branches
may create leveraged forces that are transferred to the roots lodged within the
discontinuity Over time the repetitive leveraging of the discontinuity can loosen rock
and cause it to fall
Inter layered rock such as lava flows with variable strength and rates of
weathering commonly exhibit accelerated erosion and rockfall The weaker layers
clinker tend to erode and undercut the more weathering resistant rock located above
This may lead to failure of undercut blocks of rock as support from the underlying
weaker layers is removed It is an especially important failure mechanism where thick
layers of altered clinker are bounded by very dense fine grained basalt rock The
contrast in rock strength is great and the dense rock layers act to retard groundwater
percolation thus forcing groundwater to build up and seep from the more permeable
clinker horizons
The construction of cut slopes create external disturbance to the slopes by
opening and widening joints in the rock and loosening the weaker clinker layers In
addition poor quality rock blasting techniques may create a network of secondary
cracks and stress induced crack patterns in addition to the natural joint patterns Over
the passage of time open face exposure of rock cut slopes allows the development of
cracks as a result of the relief of stress from the rock body

The daily cyclic temperature change from alternating exposure to sunlight and
shade as well as air temperature changes can accelerate erosion and weathering to
produce rockfall and landslide activity Although the lower elevations of Maui do not
experience extreme daily or seasonal air temperature fluctuation the effect of rock
surface exposure to sunlight does cause a significant fluctuation of temperature on the
rock surface More importantly in Maui is the increase in erosion due to cyclic
fluctuation between saturated ground surface conditions by rainfall or groundwater
discharge and dry ground surface conditions
Large trees that are dead or weakened by disease can come loose and fall from
the slope When the entire tree falls from a steep slope a potentially large amount of
entrained soil or rock material may accompany the root system The tree fall and
entrained earth material produces substantial debris that may reach the road In
addition to the fall of the tree the movement of the tree downslope may trigger
additional slope instability and cause additional fall of loose rock and debris
Furthermore the portion of the steep slope that the tree once occupied may be left ina
state of instability The slope instability may consist of a pocket or void space and the
loss of the pre existing root network may upset the equilibrium of stability that existed on
the hillside
Groundwater seepage from a slope and high pore water pressure in a slope
reduces the shear strength of soil and rock materials leading to potential slope failure
In addition water pressure acting upon tension cracks in rock formation may lead to
rockfall Groundwater seepage and pore water pressure build up are related to the
regional and localized groundwater conditions Groundwater seepage and pore water
pressure build up are also related to precipitation therefore there may be seasonal
variation in groundwater levels The seasonal groundwater variation may compound
the effects of normal cyclic ground moisture fluctuations from precipitation events

3 3 Rockfall Impact Barrier
The rockfall impact barrier proposed for construction within the project site consists
of a specialized fence structure which is designed to absorb the energy from rockfall
impact and retain the fallen material The rockfall impact barrier consists of cable wire
rope ring nets spanning between support posts that are held in place by anchor plates
and lateral restraint cables The barrier is designed to absorb the energy of a rock impact
and dissipate the energy through the use of flexible nets which act as a braking element
The rocks are thereby caught in the barrier as opposed to bouncing or striking with enough
force to topple the retention system The barrier may be designed for varying load impacts
that are estimated based on the existing site conditions such as boulder size slope
inclination and composition computed rolling velocities and possible boulder bounce
heights
The barriers are commonly used along highways and other areas where structures
located along lengthy slope segments must be protected from rockfall The barriers are
suitable for sites where rockfall sources are widely scattered and where it may be
generally impractical to locate and stabilize the individual hazardous blocks of rock
because of the quantity of hazards difficult slope access and other restrictions The
barriers would require some periodic maintenance to check the performance of the system
and remove accumulated rock material behind the barrier

Hawaii California

The primary advantage of the rockfall impact barrier is that it is a comprehensive
rockfall protection system which should provide a high level of protection against most of
the potential rockfall hazards on the slopes In addition man induced disturbance to the
slope and natural vegetation would be limited to the immediate area surrounding the
rockfall impact barrier installation
The main components of the rockfall impact barrier include the support posts wire
ring nets anchorage ground plates wire retaining ropes braking rings and the spiral rope
anchors The steel components are typically hot dipped galvanized and the wire ropes are
DIN 2078 coated A special protective zinc aluminum coating may be applied where
environmental corrosion is of concern Under normal field conditions the lifespan of the
steel material components is estimated to range on the order of about 50 years Because
of the higher corrosiveness of the environment in Hawaii primarily due to the oceanic
environment warm air temperature and higher humidity the lifespan of the steel
materials may be estimated on the order of about 20 years It is recommended that some
periodic inspection occurring on the order of about 5 to 10 year intervals be implemented
to check the condition of the rockfall impact barrier system for possible signs of corrosion
It should be noted that the ring nets which span between the posts may be
temporarily disconnected in order to remove boulders that may be caught by the rockfall
impact barrier Larger boulder impacts may require that some net panels be replaced to
maintain the integrity of the system It is difficult if not impossible to predict the frequency
of possible boulder impacts on the barrier It may be roughly estimated that a large
boulder impact under normal conditions may occur on the order of about once per 5 to
15 years

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling four borings designated
as Boring Nos 6 through 9 extending to depths of about 37 to 42 feet below the
existing pavement surface at about Mile Post 19 of Hana Highway The approximate
locations of the borings drilled at the project site are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1
and 2 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered in the test borings are presented on the
Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled boring in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the coral rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of soil rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material
recovered from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if
3 7 feet of material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be
74 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of coral rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is
sound material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
