FIELD EXPLORATION
We explored the subsurface by drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 and 2 each to a depth of about 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Boring No 1 was
drilled approximately 8 5 feet away from the existing electric pole No 3 19 and Boring
No 2 was drilled approximately 14 feet away from the existing electric pole No 2 19 The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the attached Logs of Borings Plates 4
and 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a
hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the hollow core of the bit is
mechanically recovered for examination and description
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test results are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned above two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled
and sampled for this project extending to depths of approximately 41 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
Boring No 1 was drilled near existing electric pole No 3 19 Our field exploration
disclosed that this location is underlain by fill materials below 3 5 inches of concrete The
fill materials generally consist of loose sandy gravel with some clay and extend to
approximately 5 feet below the ground surface The fill layer is underlain by colluvium
which consists of some cobbles and boulders in clay matrix More cobbles and boulders
were encountered from 17 5 feet below the ground surface during our field exploration
The colluvium layer extends to the maximum depth explored
Boring No 2 was drilled near existing electric pole No 2 19 Our field exploration
generally encountered 7 feet of fill below 4 inches of concrete The fill materials consist of
loose silty sand with gravel and grade to sandy clay from 4 to 7 feet below the ground

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the information provided a new electric pole will be installed to replace
the existing leaning pole No 3 19 at the project site We understand the current design
concept involves installing a steel pole up to 55 feet long Based on the loading conditions
provided by the project structural engineer and subsurface soil conditions encountered
during our field exploration we recommend supporting the new electric pole on a single
cast in place drilled shaft foundation Detailed discussions and recommendations for
foundation designs are presented in the following sections herein
Drilled Shaft Foundations
Based on the structural loads and subsurface conditions a 3 foot diameter drilled
shaft extending to a minimum depth of 25 feet below the ground surface should be used to
support the new electric pole The drilled shaft may be designed with an allowable
compressive load capacity of 80 kips The allowable compressive load capacity for the
drilled shafts is for dead plus live loads The compressive load capacity may be increased

Lateral Load Resistance
The lateral load resistance of drilled shaft is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the drilled shaft allowable deflection at the top of
the drilled shaft and the induced moment in the drilled shaft The lateral load
analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for Windows which is a
microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded deep foundation
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program
solves for deflection and bending moment along a deep foundation under lateral
loads as a function of depth The analysis was carried out with the use of non linear
o y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then computed
using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths

Foundation Settlement
Settlement of the drilled shaft foundation will result from elastic compression of the
shaft and subgrade response of the foundation embedded in the soils encountered
at the site We anticipate that the total settlements of the drilled shafts are
estimated to be less than 0 25 inch We believe that a significant portion of the
settlement will be elastic and should occur as the loads are applied

and the surrounding soil Therefore the contractor should exercise care in drilling

variations a Geolabs representative should be present to observe installation of the

1
To reduce the potential for significant caving in of the drilled hole
temporary casing may be required during drilled shaft installation Care
should be exercised during removal of the temporary casing to reduce
the potential for necking of the drilled shaft concrete
Boulders and cobbles were encountered in the borings during our field
exploration Difficult drilling conditions will likely be encountered at the
project site and should be expected The drilled shaft contractor will need
to have the appropriate equipment and tools to drill through these types
of natural obstructions in the subsurface Appropriate measures also will
be needed to avoid dislodging boulders into the drilled shaft hole during
the drilling and shaft installation process
We did not encounter groundwater at the time of our field exploration
However due to the relatively wet weather condition in the Manoa area
some water is expected in the drilled hole during the shaft installation If
the drilled hole is not dry the shaft concrete should be placed by tremmie
methods During concrete placement a minimum of 5 feet of concrete
head should be maintained above the bottom of the tremmie pipe A
low shrink high slump 7 to 9 inch slump range concrete mix should be
used to provide close contact between the drilled shaft and the
surrounding soils The concrete should be placed promptly after drilling
to reduce the potential for softening and or caving in of the sides of the
drilled hole In addition although the drilled shaft is primarily designed
based on skin friction the bottom of the drilled hole should be relatively
free of loose materials prior to concrete placement
Due to the proximity of the existing house and cobbles and boulders anticipated
the residence will be subjected to some vibrations during drilling Therefore we
recommend that monitoring points be set up around the house and a damage survey be
conducted before and after construction
It is imperative that a Geolabs representative is present at the project site to
observe the drilling and installation of drilled shaft during construction Geolabs
observation of the drilled shaft installation operations is necessary to confirm the assumed
subsurface conditions and make prompt adjustment if needed

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided
The field boring locations indicated herein are approximate having been taped from
features shown on the Location Plan provided by HECO on The physical
locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text herein These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of HECO and other project
consultants for specific application to the Paty Drive Electric Pole Relocation project in
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No
warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in
the project design Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper
information to serve as the basis for construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to
bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the
interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration
for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil rock conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft
deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional
probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a
properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
This geotechnical engineering exploration conducted at the project site was not
intended to investigate the potential presence of hazardous materials existing at the site It
should be noted that the equipment techniques and personnel used to conduct a
geo environmental exploration differ substantially from those applied in geotechnical
engineering
