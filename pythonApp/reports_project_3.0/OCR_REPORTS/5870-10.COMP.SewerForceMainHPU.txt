This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during construction
for the Sewer Force Main, Hawaii Pacific University project from July 29 to August 28, 2009
in accordance with our fee proposal dated March 17, 2009.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is near Kamehameha Highway, the Pali Golf Course, and Hawaii
Pacific University — Hawaii Loa Campus in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The
sewer force main will connect Hawaii Pacific University (HPU) — Hawaii Loa Campus and
Halekou Sewage Pump Station. In general, the alignment for the sewer force main will begin
at the Halekou Sewage Pump Station and extend in a southeasterly direction to a grassy
median about 260 feet from the pump station. This portion of the sewer force main will be
installed using conventional cut and cover methods.
From the grassy median, the sewer force main will extend about 1,900 feet in a
southerly to southeasterly direction to near a comfort station on the Pali Golf Course. The
sewer force main will cross below the H-3 Freeway. This portion of the sewer force main will
be installed using trenchless methods consisting of horizontal directional drilling (HDD).
The sewer force main will cross Kamehameha Highway onto the Hawaii Pacific
University — Hawaii Loa Campus. The portion of the sewer force main crossing Kamehameha
Highway will be installed using trenchless methods consisting of HDD methods. The
remaining portion of the sewer force main will be installed by conventional cut and cover
methods.

SMH A-1 (Station 0+00) to near the connection between the open trench installed and HDD

The CLSM was provided by Ameron Hawaii.
The trench backfill at the connection between the open trench installed and HDD
installed sewer line and the sewer line between Station 28+31 and the new pump station
located on the HPU campus consisted of the excavated on-site materials. We obtained
samples of the on-site materials utilized as backfill materials and transported them to our
laboratory for testing. The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor),
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2.
The backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum moisture
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557.
During the utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction
tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.2 of this report. These tests were
performed on the backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.
The compaction of the sewer line backfill located in undeveloped areas was approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained.
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor's backfill
placement during the water line installation. Based on the observations of our field
representatives, it is our opinion that the backfilling for the above project observed by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general
intent of the project documents.
