In accordance with your request Geolabs Inc has provided geotechnical engineering

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on Maalo Road near the intersection with Kuhio Highway in
the Lihue area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to construct a new 16 inch
water line pipe bridge crossing Hanamaulu Stream on the upstream side of the existing Kapaia
Bridge
Based on the available information the new water line will span approximately 62 feet
across Hanamaulu Stream The water line will be supported on a concrete beam with
abutments on each side of the stream We understand that the pipeline will have both
horizontal and vertical bends near the stream crossing Based on the information provided
the dead load reaction at each abutment will be about 65 kips The internal pipe pressure of
150 pounds per square inch psi will generate vertical and horizontal thrust forces of about
30 kips at the bends

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural loads
a deep foundation system consisting of a single 24 inch diameter cast in place drilled shaft
was recommended at each abutment location to support the proposed water line crossing
The drilled shafts would be extended to a minimum depth of 30 feet below the bottom of the
cap to derive the necessary support for the design loads The approximate locations of the
drilled shafts are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The drilled shafts were designed to derive vertical support primarily from skin friction
The end bearing component of the drilled shafts was discounted in the design due to
difficulties associated with obtaining a clean bottom below the groundwater level during
construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The ground surface at the stream crossing location is at about Elevation 93 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL Based on the field exploration performed in 1998 the project site is
underlain by medium stiff to stiff clayey silts and silty clays extending from the ground surface
to about Elevations 53 to 57 feet MSL The clayey silts and silty clays were underlain by
highly weathered to unweathered medium hard to hard basalt formation extending to the
maximum depths explored of approximately 20 to 26 feet MSL Groundwater was
encountered in the borings at about Elevations 70 to 73 feet MSL at the time of the field
exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to the Hanamaulu Stream seasonal
precipitation runoff and other factors may influence the groundwater levels
PRODUCTION DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
The installation of the production shafts was performed by Foundation International Inc
A total of two drilled shafts designated as Shaft 1 and Shaft 2 were installed between
January 18 and 20 2000 The approximate locations of the two drilled shafts are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A representative from Geolabs observed the excavation placement of
the steel reinforcements and concrete operations during the installation of the production
shafts Due to the limited number of drilled shafts for this project a test shaft and or a load test
was not specified in the contract documents
The production shafts were excavated using a Watson 3000 track mounted drill rig
equipped with a 24 inch diameter core barrel The production shafts were excavated to about
excavation for Shaft 1 it was noted that the sidewalls of the drilled shaft hole held up relatively
well without the need for temporary casing Therefore the excavation proceeded without the
use of a temporary casing for drilled hole stabilization However difficult drilling conditions
were encountered during the drilling of Shaft 2 therefore a 24 inch inside diameter ID
temporary steel casing of approximately 22 feet in length was installed to stabilize the drilled
hole Logs of the production shafts and the materials encountered during the shaft excavation
are provided on Plates 3 1 through 4 2

After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing bar
rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to provide
the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches between the sidewalls
of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a 4 inch ID steel
pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the tremie pipe was
immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the specifications In the shaft
excavation where temporary casing was installed a minimum of 5 feet of concrete was
maintained above the bottom of the casing during casing removal and concrete placement
Since the elevation of the top of concrete cut off elevation was above the elevation of the
existing ground surface the shafts were extended above the ground surface to the design cut
off elevation by means of a 24 inch ID steel casing It was noted that concrete placement
continued until sound concrete was observed at the top of the shaft
Based on our field observations we believe that the two drilled shafts for the water line
pipe bridge across Hanamaulu Stream have been installed in general conformance with the
project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction
practices Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our opinion that the
drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
