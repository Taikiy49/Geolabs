Proposed New Armory Addition
Kaunakakai Army National Guard Armory
We have prepared this letter memorandum in order to expedite the project foundation
design of the proposed armory addition at the above referenced site The recommendations
presented herein are for preliminary design purposes only and may need to be revised if
necessary
Foundations
We believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on the in situ soils or
compacted fill may be used to support the proposed facility
Depending on the swelling potential of the near surface soils a capping layer may be
required under the building and pavement slabs
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of spread
footings bearing on the in situ soils or compacted granular fill This bearing value is for total
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
if foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom
edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less than
one inch 1 total and 1 2 inch differential
