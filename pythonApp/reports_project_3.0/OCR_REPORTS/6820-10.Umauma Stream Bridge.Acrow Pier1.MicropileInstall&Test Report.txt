GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING OBSERVATIONS 
MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING FOR 
TEMPORARY ACROW BRIDGE PIER NO. 1 
REHABILITATION OF UMAUMA STREAM BRIDGE 
NORTH HILO, ISLAND OF HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Fujiwara: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the micropile installation and testing for the temporary Acrow Bridge Pier 
No. 1 for the Rehabilitation of Umauma Stream Bridge project located on the Island of 
Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and the results of testing 
performed for the micropiles from April 7 through May 9, 2014.   
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is along Hawaii Belt Road (Route 19) between Kauniho and 
Leopolino Roads in North Hilo on the Island of Hawaii. A temporary detour road is planned 
on the mauka side of the existing bridge for the rehabilitation of the bridge.  The detour 
road will be supported by an Acrow bridge structure across Umauma Stream.  The Acrow 
bridge structure will be a three-span structure consisting of two pier structures supported 
on micropiles and two abutment structures supported on spread footings.  This report is for 
the Pier No. 1 micropiles. 
Based on the structural loading provided, a micropile length of 28 feet or a 
minimum rock embedment of 8 feet was recommended.  An 8-foot long 6-inch diameter 
steel casing was installed in the upper portion of the micropile.  A total of 21, 8-inch 
diameter micropiles were specified for the project. The micropile was composed of No. 10, 
Grade 75 threaded bar and 4,000 pounds per square inch (psi) cement grout.  In addition, 
one of the micropiles was installed with No. 10, Grade 150 threaded bar and tested to 
verify the micropile capacity.

The micropiles are not considered permanent. 
MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Micropile Installation 
The micropile installation was performed by AIS Construction Company. A total of 
21 micropiles were installed for Acrow Pier No. 1 footing.  The micropiles were installed 
using a Spider Track Drill.  The Spider Drill rig was equipped with a downhole hammer and 
an 8-inch diameter drill bit. The approximate locations of the micropiles are shown on the 
Micropile Plan for Acrow Pier #1, Plate 1. 
The installation of the micropiles was performed between April 7, 2014 and May 9, 
2014.  The micropiles were drilled to depths of about 25 to 31 feet below the bottom of the 
footing.  A 1.25-inch diameter Grade 75 bar and a 1.25-inch diameter Grade 150 bar were 
installed in the drilled hole for the micropiles and load test micropile, respectively.  
Permanent steel casing (65/8-inch OD) eight feet long was also installed in the upper 
portion of the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along the micropile at 5-foot intervals. 
A ½-inch diameter high density plastic grouting tube was installed along the 
reinforcing bar for grouting purposes. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top 
through the grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting 
tube was removed after the completion of the grouting operation. A summary of the 
micropile installation is provided in the Summary of Micropile Installation and Testing, 
Plate 2. 
Micropile Testing 
A compression load test was performed on Micropile No. 1-11 to a maximum test 
load of 97 kips.  The micropile testing was performed by AIS Construction Company. 
The micropile head movements at each load increment up to the maximum test 
load were measured and recorded.  The results of the micropile testing are provided in the 
Summary of Micropile Installation and Testing, Plate 2. 
CLOSURE 
Based on our field observations, we believe that the observed micropile installation 
for the temporary Acrow Bridge Pier No. 1 footing for the Rehabilitation of Umauma 
Stream Bridge project have been installed in general conformance with the geotechnical 
engineer of record recommendations and within the limits of generally accepted 
construction practices. Based on our observations of the micropile installation, it is our 
professional opinion that the micropiles have been installed to sufficient depths to provide 
the design load capacity.
