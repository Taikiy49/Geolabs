INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation for the foundation design of the proposed new floodlights at
the Ala Wai Golf Course The general location of the site is show on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project involves the installation of
four 4 floodlights at the proposed driving range in the golf course
The floodlights will be mounted on poles about 39 feet high The design
lateral loads to which the poles will be subjected have been determined by
the project structural engineer to be 30 7 foot kips
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the foundation design of
the proposed floodlight poles
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to depths of 20 feet below the existing ground surface A shallow
probe at the nearby Proposed new lake was performed to determine the depth
to water level Surface samples were collected for CBR testing to aid in
the preliminary pavement design for new parking areas The results of our
field exploration laboratory testing data and engineering analyses are
presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is within the Ala Wai Golf Course The golf course
is located in the Kaimuki district of Honolulu Hawaii and is bounded by
Date Street to the north Kapahulu Avenue to the east Manoa Palolo
Drainage Channel to the west and Ala Wai Canal to the south The proposed
floodlights are in the southeastern portion of the golf course as shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site is generally level
with a ground elevation of about 4 to 5 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three 3 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 10 and 20
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a fill layer about 3 feet thick
consisting of medium dense silty sand with gravel and clay underlain by
medium dense to loose silty sand lagoonal deposits Coral reef formation
was reached at depths of 12 feet in Boring B 1 and 19 feet in Boring B 2
Groundwater was encountered at depths of 4 feet at the time of our
Qrilling The shallow probe at the proposed new lake indicated water
level at 8 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the close
proximity of the site to the Ala Wai Canal it is anticipated that
groundwater levels will fluctuate in response to tidal influences and
flood conditions in the canal
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates aA 1 through A 3 Laboratory testing were
performed on selected samples obtained The results were utilized in our
engineering analyses and are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered poor soil conditions
consisting primarily of loose silty sand lagoonal deposits
Both drilled pier and mat foundations have been evaluated for the new
floodlights
Pier Foundation
Based on the design lateral loads and soil data from the borings
drilled pier foundations may be used as foundation support for the new
floodlights Various combinations of pier diameters and embedment lengths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1915 00 Page 4
Dk No 42
may be used however based on available equipment and previous
experience it is our opinion that a 36 inch diameter pier embedded 10
feet below the ground surface may be used The relationship between the
estimated lateral ground deflection and pier length is presented in
Appendix C
The vertical compressive load on the pole is anticipated to he
relatively light and may be supported by the frictional resistance between
the shaft of the pier and the loose subsoils
Since the pier would be subjected to repeated wind loads a critical
consideration would be to limit the permanent lateral deflections of the
pier This may be achieved by sufficient embedment of the pier
The lateral resistance of the pier can be analyzed by using the
appropriate soil modulus values of the subsoils at various depths below
the existing ground surface at the proposed pole locations A lateral
pier analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear P Y curves to represent the soil modulus values
Since the soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the
pier deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection
is then used to determine the modulus from the non linear P Y curves The
calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The
procedure is repeated until the new assumed and calculated moduli are
essentially the same
Based on the analysis we estimate that one 36 inch diameter pier
should be used for each pole and the pier should penetrate a minimum of 10
feet into the existing ground to generate sufficient lateral and vertical
resistance to fulfill the above mentioned design requirements with a
suitable factor of safety

The method and equipment used for caisson construction should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
consideration However due to the loose or soft subsoils and high
groundwater level it is anticipated that construction difficulties may
arise in keeping a drilled pier shaft open without using a steel casing
prior to concrete placement If a temporary steel casing is used extreme
care must be exercised during removal of the casing while placing concrete
in order to avoid contamination or pinching off of the concrete caused
by inflow of the surrounding soil into the concrete Therefore an
alternative is to install the caisson with a permanent steel casing left
in the ground The steel casing could be driven into the ground first and
the soils within the casing completely removed by drilling and or
jetting The reinforcing cage may then be placed and the pole may then be
placed in the cleared shaft and positioned center and vertical prior to
concrete placement
The concrete mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to
maintain high frictional contact between the steel casing or soil and pier
concrete The concrete should also be placed in a suitable mamner to
avoid segregation of the aggregates

A mat foundation with sufficient dead weight may be used to resist
the lateral load and to provide vertical bearing support to each
floodlight Our analyses indicate that a mat foundation with dimensions
of 7 by 7 feet square and 2 feet thick appears to be adequate in resisting
the given design moment
It is anticipated that the bottom of the mat foundation will be close
to the groundwater level To develop a firm stabilized bearing surface
we recommend a 12 inch over excavation and replacement with a 3B Fine
gravel cushion below the mat

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained fron field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and aS such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
