Dear Mr Nagashima
At your request Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the solar shade
structures at the above project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering
observations during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the solar shade
structures at Salt Lake Manor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the
construction of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special Inspection
services by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation for general conformance with
the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of two carport solar shade structures and a
small concrete equipment pad in the parking lot at Salt Lake Manor in the Salt Lake area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The two carport solar shade structures measure
approximately between 8 and 11 feet in height and will cantilever out about 19 feet over
the existing parking lot The project location and vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
Based on the project plans provided by the project structural engineer the new
solar shade structures were designed to be supported on a deep foundation system
consisting of 24 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled shafts The project plans
required that the drilled shafts extend down at least 10 feet below the existing ground
surface when constrained by concrete at the ground surface and at least 12 feet below

Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract
documents for this project The drilled shaft contractor installed six production shafts on
November 5 and 6 2012 Detailed installation logs for the production shafts are attached
to this letter report A representative from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation
and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a truck mounted
Watson 200 drill rig equipped with a 24 inch diameter core barrel and clean out bucket
The drilled shafts were generally extended to depths of 10 and 12 feet below the existing
ground surface as required on the contract plans After excavation of the production
shafts to their designated tip elevations the solar shade steel support columns were
lowered into the drilled holes to their planned elevations using a forklift After the columns
were set into the drilled hole concrete was discharged directly from the concrete truck
chute into the drilled hole Once the concrete filled the excavation the contractor vibrated
the concrete around the embedded steel column
The concrete for the production shaft was supplied by Ameron It should be noted
that Pacific Testing monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the
performance of slump tests and casted cylinders for compression tests Logs of the
individual drilled shafts installed and the materials encountered in each of the drilled shafts
during shaft excavation are presented on the attached Drilled Shaft Records
Based on the observations of our field representative it is our professional opinion
that the production drilled shafts for the solar shade structures at Salt Lake Manor were
installed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
