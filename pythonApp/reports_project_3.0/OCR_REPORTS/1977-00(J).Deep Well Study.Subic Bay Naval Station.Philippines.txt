INTRODUCTION
Currently the U S Naval facilities at Subic Bay Naval Station and
Cubi Point Naval Air Station are dependent upon surface water sources for
fresh water and potable water These surface sources although generally
reliable decrease during dry seasons from low stream flows may be
interrupted by blockage and may be contaminated from various sources
upstream Therefore it is desired to explore groundwater within the
Naval complex boundaries for potential sources of fresh water and potable
water
A previous study was made by the U S Geological Survey to assess the
potential for development of groundwater within the boundaries and to site
three 3 locations for the drilling of test wells These test wells
would be sampled and test pumped to obtain aquifer characteristics and
other data for the design of supply wells at Subic Bay Naval Station
Based on information provided to us by personnel of the U S Naval
Facilities Engineering Command Pacific Division PACDIV these wells
will be drilled and tested by forces of the Naval Mobile Construction
Battalions Seabees The Seabees have requested detailed specifications
for the drilling and testing of the wells In addition they have
requested a list of materials for the wells and a cost estimate for
purchasing the materials

Our firm was retained by PACDIV to provide geotechnical
engineering geological consulting services in the form of preparing the
specifications materials list and cost estimate This report summarizes
our findings and activities pursuant to developing the specifications
materials list and materials cost estimate

Regional Geology
Subic Bay is located within the Western Volcanic Intrusive Province
of Iuzon and comprises Recent to Pliocene Quaternary Volcanic deposits
The east west trending San Antonio Fracture zone is probably the closest
identified major fault zone approximately 4 to 10 miles northerly of the
Naval Base
The volcanic deposits in the Subic Bay Area are primarily of
pyroclastic origin and consist of lithic tuffs volcanic breccia
agglomerate and volcanic mudflows lahar believed to have been
generated by explosive eruptions from Mount Natib Mount Santa Rita and
Mount Balakibok three of the major volcanic centers which comprise the
Zambales Bataan Volcanic Highlands Subsequent chemical weathering of
these deposits has resulted in a mantle of chemically decomposed rock
residual soil saprolite overlying the relatively less weathered
tuffaceous rock
Numerous glacial eustatic sea level variations caused the emergence
and submergence of the land mass that produced soil deposits at various
levels both above and below the present sea level
Examination of the geomorphology for the area leads to the conjecture
that the highland portions of the area consist primarily of eroded
volcanic deposits which have been deeply incised by five north to
northwest trending streams the Boton and
Triboa rivers north to south respectively Alluvial and deltaic
deposits are primarily the product of stream deposition and accumulate
along the river mouths associated drainageways and valley entrances
Slopewash and colluvium are chiefly gravity driven deposits and form a
thin veneer over most of the slopes in the area and accumulate to
significant thicknesses at the base of steep slopes and cliffs The
materials encountered in the lowlying portions of the area are mainly of a
low energy deltaic eustarine or lagoonal depositional environment which
would be encountered in a sheltered bay area such as Subic Bay

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Background
Although previous drilling efforts in the Subic Bay Naval Station
area failed to locate exploitable sources of groundwater the study
conducted by the U S Geological Survey indicated that there is merit to
further exploration Due to the heterogeneous nature of the geologic
formations at the site there is a possibility that a test well could
intercept an aquifer On the basis of field reconnaissance performed by
the U S Geological Survey three 3 sites were chosen by the U S
Geological Survey for the drilling of test wells
Prior to our own field reconnaissance and preparation of the
specifications material list and material cost estimate we requested
information on the drilling rig and equipment which would be assigned to
the project Based upon the information provided by Seabee personnel it
appears that the drill rig is a custom built machine which combines
features of several production model rigs The equipment package is
capable of drilling a 12 inch diameter hole to a depth of about 1 500 feet
below the ground surface by either mud rotary or air rotary methods It
is our understanding that an additional air compressor must be used to
reach the full depth with air rotary We recommend that this compressor
be mobilized with the other equipment
In addition to drill pipe bits tools etc we understand that the
package includes a electric logger which may be used to
obtain subsurface geophysical data concerning the nature of the formations
encountered in a drilled well Use of this logger as part of the data
collection program could yield valuable information concerning the welis

Sawmill Area TH SAW

Well Drilling
Based upon the information provided to us we recommend that the test
wells be drilled to a nominal diameter of ten 10 inches and that 8 inch
diameter flush threaded PVC casing and screen be temporarily installed in
the wells for testing purposes The recommended well bore diameter would
W O 1977 007 Page 8
facilitate the installation and removal of the temporary casing and
screen The recommended casing diameter would allow the use of 6 inch
diameter submersible turbine pump for testing of the wells while
minimizing the potential of the pump binding in the casing during
installation or removal
The use of casing and screen to the bottom of the drilled hole would
reduce the potential of hole collapse during pump testing entrapping the
pump The casing and screen could be salvaged and the hole should be
properly backfilled and abandoned The screen should have nominal 1 8
slots to reduce the flow restriction to the pump
We also recommend that the test wells be drilled by the air rotary
method unless it is absolutely necessary to use mud to stabilize the
hole This is recommended because drilling mud has the tendency to invade
and clog permeable formations thereby reducing the yield and capacity of
a well drilled with mud rotary since complete removal of the md is
difficult Air rotary drilling generally provides a cleaner well and
tends to develop the well during drilling

Pump Testing
Meaningful pump tests conducted during an exploratory well drilling
and testing project must yield data not only about an individual well
being tested but also about the aquifer which the well is tapping To
this end the specifications call for both short term and long term pump
tests to be conducted at variable rates For this reason we recommend
that a vertical turbine pump be utilized for the pump tests Since a
vertical turbine pump is usually driven by an engine with a transmission
for testing the use of this system will facilitate varying the pumping
rates However due to comments provided to us regarding the costs of a
line shaft turbine pump we revised the Technical Specifications to
reflect the use of a less expensive submersible turbine pump
To provide a range of pump rates and capacities we are recommending
the use of two submersible pumps one 30 hp and one 15 hp
Pump testing procedures are detailed in the Technical
Specifications
Field Reconnaissance
During our field trip to Subic Bay Naval Station a geologic
reconnaissance was made of the accessible areas of Subic Bay Naval Station
and Cubi Point Naval Air Station Observations were also made of geologic
conditions along the National Highway outside of the base boundaries
It was noted that although the structural geology of the area is
highly complex the hydrogeologic aspects of the exposed formations are
similar in that all appear to have low hydraulic conductivities
nerally the exposed tuffs and mudflows showed moderately to widely
spaced joint patterns Based on textural examination of the rock
materials it appears that these formations would transmit groundwater
primarily along joints and bedding planes rather than through porosity of
the rock mass
W O 1977 007 Page 10
The exposed andesitic lava flows were generally massive with no
exposed clinker or fragmental strata Again these rocks would generally
transmit water along joints and interflow surfaces Joints generally
tighten with depth due to increase in overburden pressure and the
permeability decreases as the joints tighten
Only the exposure of andesitic lava in the vicinity of the Cubi Power
Plant and fuel truck yard showed a significant degree of jointing No
seepage of groundwater was noted at these outcrops even though on base
personnel indicated that there had been heavy rains for about three weeks
prior to our field work It is estimated that the jointing in this
andesitic lava flow is discontinuous and most likely would not store or
yield an appreciable amount of water

Consolidated formation means any geologic formation in which the

formations
Contamination is an impairment of natural ground water quality by
biological chemical physical or radiological materials which lower the
water quality to a degree which creates a potential hazard to the

10 ABANDONING OF WELLS
Upon completion of all testing operations at the Contracting
Officer s direction the well shall be abandoned and permanently sealed
The casing shall be removed and recovered for salvage The drilled bore
shall then be backfilled to a depth of no less than 100 feet below the
existing ground surface with clean granular material Following the
installation of this backfill the remainder of the drilled bore shall be
filled with cement or cement bentonite grout
Cement grout consisting of not more than 6 gallons of potable water
per 94 lb bag of cement and up to 6 percent bentonite by weight of
cement shall be tremied into the well through a pipe of not less than
diameter The grout shall be uniform and free of lumps The grout
pipe shall be withdrawn gradually in stages as the well fills with grout
