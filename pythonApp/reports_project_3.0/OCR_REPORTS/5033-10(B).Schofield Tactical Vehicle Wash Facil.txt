Project Considerations
The project site is along Higgins Road within Schofield Barracks in Wahiawa on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
generally involves constructing six wash stations one sedimentation basin an
equalization basin two above ground water storage tanks and a structure to house the
mechanical secondary treatment units The sedimentation basin is approximately 6 7
meters wide by 24 4 meters long by 3 0 meters deep
We made a site visit on March 14 2006 to observe the distress to the
sedimentation basin structure following a period of heavy rains during construction
Transverse cracks were observed along the top of the concrete curb on the northern
and southern sides of the basin and along the top of the center basin wall Based on
optical survey readings taken by the contractor the top of the basin had moved
upwards as much as about 3 inches Based on our observations it appeared that the
probable cause of the cracks in the basin was water entering into the base course layer
below the basin slab primarily from the exposed base course layer on the western side
of the basin and possibly from the open excavation on the eastern side of the basin It
appears that the hydrostatic pressure from the water in the base course layer lifted the
center portion of the basin slab Based on our observations it was recommended to
perform a probing and grouting program to observe and fill possible voids below the
basin slab

Probing and Grouting
The probing and grouting program consisted of Phases and Il A total of 12 and
58 probe holes were designated for Phases and II respectively The purpose of the
Phase probe holes was to evaluate whether significant voids were present below the
basin slab The Phase II probe holes would be performed if significant voids are
encountered in the Phase probe holes The basin slab probe hole locations were
surveyed and staked out by the general contractor Niking Corporation
Phase
A total of 12 probe hole locations were marked for the probing and grouting
operations A pachometer was used to locate existing rebar locations near the probe
holes The approximate locations of the Phase probe holes are shown on the attached
Sedimentation Basin Probe Location Plan Plate 2
The probe holes were drilled on using concrete coring equipment
from Concrete Coring Company of Hawaii The probe holes were about 4 inches in
diameter and extended to the bottom of the concrete basin slab Voids up to about
2 inches high were observed below the basin slab
After completion of the probe drilling operations the probe holes were filled with
cement grout mixed on site on by Structural System Inc A 5 gallon
bucket was used to pour the grout into the probe holes A total of about 1 06 cubic feet
of cement grout was used to fill the 12 probe holes A maximum grout intake of
approximately 0 16 cubic feet of grout or about 2 5 times the theoretical grout volume
was encountered at Probe Hole No 1 5 A summary of probing and grouting
observations is provided on the attached Plate 3 1
It should be noted that a non shrink additive was not used in the cement grout
mix as specified in the project specification The upper portions of the probe holes were
subsequently sealed with an epoxy grout by the contractor
Based on our field observations and grout intake quantities it was recommended
to perform the Phase II probing and grouting work In addition a revised grout mix to
improve flowability was recommended

holes on each side of the basin center wall The approximate location of the Phase

The probe holes on the south side of the basin slab were drilled on
October 2 and 16 2006 using concrete coring equipment from Concrete Coring
Company of Hawaii The probe holes were grouted on by Structural
Systems Inc The probe holes on north side of the basin were drilled on October 16
2006 and grouted on October 19 and 20 2006 Voids up to about 2 inches high were
observed in the probe holes Admixtures consisting of HC and
Intraplast N were added to the cement water slurry to improve the grout flowability
A total of about 97 24 cubic feet of grout was used to fill the 58 probe holes The
maximum grout intake was encountered at Probe Hole No 2 23 where approximately
14 30 cubic feet of grout was placed into the probe hole A summary of the Phase II
probing and grouting observations is provided on the attached Plates 3 2 and 3 3
In summary the basin slab probing and grouting operations have been
conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications for this
project Based on our field observations our professional opinion is that the voids
encountered at the slab locations probed have been grouted and that the base course
layer should provide the recommended support for the basin slab
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all the cavities
and or voids present beneath the slabs for the existing sedimentation basin Therefore
some of the cavities and or voids may not have been detected even with the probing
and grouting program undertaken for the project However the probing and grouting
program appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the risks to the project
