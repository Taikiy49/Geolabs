PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Lokahi Greens Subdivision project site is located in the District of Ewa on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on our field observations and the information provided
the significant distresses observed at the subject subdivision generally include the following
Separation of the drywalls at the joints
Cracking of drywalls interior floor slabs and exterior sidewalks
Misalignment of exterior siding and trim
Popping up of the vinyl cover inside the bathroom
Doors not properly opening or closing
Separation of the kitchen counter from the wall
Some settlement and or heaving of perimeter sidewalks

soils The water infiltration or excess moisture once trapped in the subsoils underneath the
sidewalk and the perimeter of the house structure are not able to evaporate as a result of the
protective cover provided by the concrete slab Due to the clayey nature of the subsoils in the

The main cause of expansion in clayey soils is the tendency of individual soil
particles to attract and hold water Clay particles have a net negative electrical charge and
attract positively charged ions present in the soil water matrix In the attempt to attract
positive ions many layers of water molecules are held by the clay particle These layers
of adsorbed water force individual clay particles away from each other resulting in soil
expansion
Because the distresses observed at the subject subdivision may be attributed to the
swelling behavior of the expansive clayey soils it is important that the remedial measures are
directed at stabilizing the moisture content of the expansive clayey soils Stabilizing the
moisture content of the expansive clayey soils should reduce the potential for further swelling
and shrinkage of the underlying clayey soils
In order to reduce the potential for moisture fluctuations underneath the sidewalks and
house structures we recommend that the following remedial measures be considered for
implementation These remedial measures include the following 1 chemical stabilization
of the in situ expansive clayey soils 2 installation of a moisture cut off barrier along the
perimeter sidewalk and 3 construction of a new sidewalk with a concrete cut off wall
Available information indicate that chemical stabilization using the EcSS 3000
electrochemical soil stabilizer could be an effective way of coping with the expansive clayey
soils at the site Therefore we have conducted a field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering evaluation program to further evaluate the effectiveness of this treatment option
for the subdivision site Of particular importance is the effectiveness of the EcSS 3000
chemical stabilization system in reducing the swelling and shrinkage potential of the expansive
soils on a long term basis
EcSS 3000 ELECTROCHEMICAL SOIL STABILIZATION
In general the EcSS 3000 chemical stabilization involves the injection of a diluted
chemical diluted in water into the ground to chemically stabilize the expansive nature of the
soils Information from the product supplier indicates that the chemical injection reacts with
the clay particles in the soil to form a soil mass that is less susceptible to shrink swell effects
when subjected to moisture fluctuations EcSS 3000 formerly known as ISS 2500 is a
mixture of naphthalene oil sulfur trioxide sulfuric acid and other additives A proprietary resin
catalyst is also added to enhance the reaction times The ion exchange solution is mixed with
water and introduced into the subsoils under pressure through injection points drilled at a
closely spaced grid pattern
The EcSS 3000 electrochemical soil stabilizer creates a cation rich environment in the
injected zone and the individual clay particle s negative charge is effectively neutralized The

Prior to the injection operation at the backyard of Lot 84 a grid pattern of 3 feet on
centers in a staggered pattern was laid out in one half of the backyard The other half of
the backyard in Lot 84 was intentionally left untreated as a control area for this study The
injection points along the perimeter sidewalk of Lot 81 were generally spaced at about
18 inches and setback from the building walls at about 6 inches Hand held injection rods
were manually forced downward with a hammer in approximately 12 inch vertical intervals
up to a depth of about 6 feet below the existing ground slab surface or upon refusal at the
injection points
We understand that a diluted EcSS 3000 solution at a ratio of about 3 percent
1 gallon of chemical to 300 gallons of water was injected at each 1 foot interval into the
underlying expansive soils at a pressure of about 200 to 250 pounds per square inch psi
The injection points were offset by about 6 to 12 inches if obstruction was encountered at
shallow depths during the injection operation After injection of the chemical stabilizer a
curing time of about one month was allowed prior to post treatment sampling and testing
for evaluation of the treated product

LABORATORY TESTING PROGRAM
In general three types of laboratory tests were performed on the selected soil
samples obtained from our field exploration program to evaluate the swelling and shrinkage
potential of the on site clayey soils A brief description of these laboratory swell shrink tests
is presented below
ASTM D 4546 Methods A and B seating and overburden loads
Soil samples tested were either relatively undisturbed or remolded in 2 4 inch
diameter by 1 inch high rings Some samples were air dried overnight prior to being
subjected to one dimensional swell tests conducted in general accordance with Method A
seating load and Method B overburden load of ASTM D 4546 Standard Test Methods
for One Dimensional Swell or Settlement Potential of Cohesive Soils It should be noted
that the ASTM D 4546 Method B test is the recommended swell test by the manufacturer
of the EcSS 3000 Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer
1 Inch Ring Swell Test 55 and 255 psf surcharge pressure
Soil samples tested were either relatively undisturbed or remolded in 2 4 inch
diameter by 1 inch high rings Some samples were air dried overnight and then saturated for
24 hours under a surcharge pressure of 55 or 255 pounds per square foot psf
Shrinkage Test
Soil samples tested were relatively undisturbed in 2 43 inch diameter by 6 inch
high thin walled seamless tube samplers The test generally consists of removing the soil
sample from the tube sampler and placing the soil sample on the laboratory table and then
taking volume and weight measurements of the sample with the passage of time

Effect of Sample Disturbances
Based on the swell test results obtained for the remolded soil samples Tables 5 and
6 it appears that there is a slight decrease in the swelling potential of the soil samples after
the EcSS 3000 treatment However the swell test results obtained for the relatively
undisturbed soil samples Tables 1 through 4 taken from Lots 81 and 84 are generally
inconclusive in determining the effectiveness of the EcSS 3000 electrochemical soil
stabilization system
In general we envision that soil samples existing in a loose state would have a lower
density and would result in a lower degree of swelling when compared to those soil samples
existing in a dense state higher density Similarly samples that are disturbed during the
sampling operation would have a lower density and would result in a lower degree of swelling
when compared to those soil samples that are relatively undisturbed higher density
We speculate that some of the high swell test results for the relatively undisturbed soil
samples obtained from Lot 84 could be attributed to the situation presented above In order
to evaluate whether the swell test results are affected by the in situ condition of the soil
samples and the subsequent sampling disturbance a plot of swelling potential versus dry
density was prepared for the relatively undisturbed soil samples as shown on Plates 5 and 6
Based on the scattering of the data points shown in these two plots it appears that the high
swell test results are not directly related to the in situ condition of the soil samples and the
subsequent sampling disturbances

Based on our study and assessment we could not arrive at a definitive conclusion
that the EcSS 3000 electrochemical soil stabilizer is truly effective in reducing the shrinkage
and or swelling potential of the on site expansive soils However it is our opinion that the
chemical is able to serve and function as a minimum in lubricating the subsoils and
maintaining a more constant soil moisture content especially under the protective covers _
such as the sidewalk and house slabs Therefore we believe that chemical stabilization
using the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer ECSS 3000 could be a useful remedial measure at
the subject subdivision site and it deserves serious consideration and cost comparison If
the chemical stabilization alternative is to be implemented Geolabs should be consulted
to provide supplemental recommendations for implementation during design and
construction
Based on the previous and current swell test results it appears that the on site
clayey soils may be considered slightly to moderately expansive Therefore it is our
opinion that our recommendation of the 3 foot capping fill below the building slab should
be more than adequate considering the slight to moderate swelling potential of the on site
clayey soils We believe that the distresses observed at the subject subdivision is a direct
result of water infiltration underneath the sidewalk slabs due to excessive landscape
irrigation negative drainage due to re grading of the yard area and alteration blockage of
the drainage swales
The importance of these drainage requirements have been emphasized in our
geotechnical engineering report prepared for the subject subdivision and relayed to the
Developer through other channels Apparently the Developer has overlooked this matter
and did not convey the importance of this information drainage requirements to the
individual lot owners To make the situation worse the Developer decided to dowel
structurally connect the perimeter sidewalk slab to the house foundation system The
rigid connection of the sidewalk slab to the house foundation resulted in a larger surface
area for the expansive soils to react against The larger surface area resulted in a higher
uplift load acting on the perimeter of the house and has caused the sidewalk slab and the
perimeter house footings to lift up along its outside edge upon swelling of the subgrade
soils
