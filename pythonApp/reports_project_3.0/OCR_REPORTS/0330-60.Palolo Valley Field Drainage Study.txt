INTRODUCTION
Submitted herein is our final report on a drainage study of
Palolo Valley Field Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The objective
of this report was to identify the drainage problems of the
field and to make recommendations for alleviating the prob
lems

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
1 Geology and Soil
Much of Palolo Valley Field is underlain by at least 20
feet of very moist stiff CLAY overlying dense silty
GRAVEL and clayey GRAVEL with boulders at varying depths
The CLAY is a gray Adobe type with potential high swell
and shrinkage characteristics Gypsum crystals are abun
dant in the Shrinkage cracks The clay is generally stiff
below 2 feet and quite moist

External Water Sources
Some of the outside rain and irrigation water apparently enters
into Palolo Field through the porous fill on the upslope Palolo
Avenue side of the field A small quantity probably infiltrates
through the clay itself and a large amount simply runs down the

Internal Water Sources
Rain and irrigation appear to be the main water sources within
Palolo Field The possibility of leaks from broken water pipes
and sewer mains cannot be discounted although no evidence of
such breaks was seen

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
MN ALUN
Based on our investigation research and observations the
following conclusions and recommendations are presented
a
It is necessary to improve the existing drainage system to
collect and carry runoff and irrigation water to existing
drain inlets in order to prevent ponding and to prevent
water from entering the fill beneath the existing buildings
We recommend that a collection system of french drains and
closed conduits be constructed in general accordance with
the details shown on Plate 1
It is important that all roof downdrains be tied into the
proposed closed drainage system to keep water from entering
