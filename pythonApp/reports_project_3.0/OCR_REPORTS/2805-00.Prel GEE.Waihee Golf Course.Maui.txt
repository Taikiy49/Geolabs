SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our preliminary exploration was performed in an area within
the southern portion of the project site where the proposed
clubhouse parking area drainageways and Hole No 4 are
planned The area will be designated hereinafter as the study
area
The field exploration generally encountered stiff to hard alluvial
deposits consisting of clayey silts silty clays and silty sands
at the site
The alluvial soils encountered are fairly susceptible to erosion
and therefore concrete lining of drainageways may be
required The use of a lining will also depend on the
anticipated water velocity and drainageway slope
Based on our laboratory testing it is anticipated that the soils
encountered near the proposed subgrade levels will be
moderately expansive Special site grading procedures are
recommended to reduce the potential of building distress due
to possible shrinkage and heaving of these subsoils
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed structures An allowable bearing value of 3 000
p s f may be used for the footings bearing on properly
compacted select fill material

specific design recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the town of Waiehu near the existing Waiehu Golf
Course on the island of Maui Hawaii
The overall project involves the construction of a new eighteen 18 hole
championship golf course at the site Included in the development will be a new club
house parking area maintenance and practice areas and water features The proposed
clubhouse parking area and Hole No 4 are planned in the study area A new drainage
system is also planned in this portion of the overall project site The Project Location
Map Plate 1 shows our geotechnical engineering exploration study area in relation to the
extent of the overall project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located roughly six 6 miles northwest of Kahului Airport on the
island of Maui The northern project limit is near Waihee Point The Waiehu Golf Course
is adjacent to the southeastern boundary of the project The Pacific Ocean is to the east
and Kahekili highway is to the west The town of Waihee is located near the
southwestern corner of the overall project area
The southern portion of the study area generally slopes gently in the northeasterly
direction with ground elevations ranging from eighteen 18 to ninety 90 feet above
Mean Sea Level
Presently the study area is covered with an abandoned sugar cane field tall grass
macadamia nut and eucalyptus trees
An existing asphalt paved road from Kahekili Highway serving as the entrance road
to the Waiehu Golf Course crosses the study area in a generally west to east direction

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff to hard alluvial deposits consisting
of clayey silts silty clays and silty sands underlying the study area Since the subsurface
soils encountered are generally not highly erosion resistant it appears that lining of
drainageways should be considered
It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide adequate bearing support
for the proposed structures and pavements However our laboratory testing also
indicates that these underlying clay soils have moderately high expansion potential
Therefore special grading design and construction procedures will be required to reduce
potential distress to the proposed structures caused by shrinkage and heaving of the
clayey subsoils
Drainageway
Drainageway improvements are planned in the study area which is located in the
southern portion of the proposed golf course site
One of the purposes of our subsurface soil exploration was to explore the study
area for the presence of an underlying highly erosion resistant deposit such as coral or
rock to eliminate the use of concrete lining for the proposed drainageways
In our exploration alluvial deposits consisting of stiff to hard clayey silts silty clays
and silty sands were generally encountered at the site Since a highly erosion resistant
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2805 00 Page 7
rocky deposit was not encountered during our exploration it appears that lining of the
drainageways for protection against erosion may be required The selection and design
of a lining will also depend on the anticipated water velocity and the drainageway slope
ratio
Site Grading
In general based on the results of our preliminary exploration it appears that
conventional grading methods may be used for the mass grading in the study area
Due to the moderately high expansion potential of the clayey subsoils encountered
near the surface we recommend that the on site clayey subgrade soils at the bottom of
all on grade slabs be over excavated to a minimum depth of two 2 feet and backfilled
with compacted select non expansive material The expansive subgrade soil removal
should also extend about two 2 feet beyond the perimeters of the on grade slabs This
extended area should be capped with about one 1 foot of non expansive impervious
soils with a ring swell of not greater than 2 percent for details see Plate 4
The following additional recommendations are general guidelines for the design of
the mass grading and for the development of general grading specifications
1 Prior to commencement of grading the area within the contract
grading limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
2 Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
below any structure areas and areas designated to receive embankment
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2805 00 Page 8
should be over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting
depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
3 In general the on site sandy soils if they can be separated or stockpiled
should be suitable sources as select fill material The silty clay soils can
only be used as general fill material and should not be placed closer than
two 2 feet below the future building and parking subgrade elevations Any
boulders and cobbles larger than twelve 12 inches in maximum dimension
should be removed from the excavated material and either be disposed of
off site or be placed properly in deeper zones of the embankments per our
recommendations as discussed below
4 Imported select fill material if required should generally be non expansive
with no particles greater than three 3 inches in size and no more than
fifteen 15 percent passing the 200 sieve It is recommended that if off
site borrow is required it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to
determine its suitability for the intended use prior to its delivery to the job
site
5 Large boulders or rock fragments could be buried in the deepest
portions of the embankment golf course fills In general the boulders
should not be nested and an adequate amount of compacted finer grained
soil should be placed in the space between the boulders to reduce the
potential for loss of fines by migration of fines from the upper portion of the
fine grained fill and top soils into the voids between boulders which would
otherwise occur Care must be exercised to avoid placement of any
boulders in locations which would conflict with future utility lines In general

boulder and rock fragments larger than twelve 12 inches in size should be
placed at depths greater than four 4 feet below the finish subgrade level
6 Generally fine grained soils fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
ten 10 to twelve 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
7 Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1

to lessen potential instability of the new fill A sketch showing Typical
Keying and Benching details is shown on Plate 3 The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement
Slopes
In general design cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter are recommended for the on site materials To minimize loose fill material present
at the finished embankment slopes the fill slope should be constructed by overfilling and
Cutting back to the design slope Care should be exercised to avoid over cutting of the
future slope profiles If over cutting does occur the resultant slope should be repaired
by keying and benching into the cut slope face in order to preclude placement of sliver
fills After over cutting has been overfilled again it should be cut back to the Original
design slope ratio
Sandy and silty soils such as those encountered at the project site are susceptible
to erosion when exposed and subjected to flowing water particularly in sloping areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2805 00 Page 10
We recommend that drainage run off water should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and proper type of slope planting and slope improvements should be utilized to
reduce potential erosion and or destabilization of the finished slopes that could be caused
by excessive drainage run off For slopes steeper than three horizontal to one vertical
3H 1V we recommend that the use of an erosion control mat such as Miramat or similar
be considered to reduce the potential of slope erosion until such time as the appropriate
slope protection vegetative cover is well established
In general midpoint slope benches should be provided for slopes having heights
greater than twenty 20 feet
Installation of Utilities
It is anticipated that underground utilities such as water sewer drainage and
irrigation lines will be installed as part of the development work Based on the results of
our field exploration it appears that conventional excavation methods and equipment may
be utilized for utility line trenching However bouldery horizons may be encountered in
the deeper trench excavations
In general the subsurface two 2 to three 3 feet of stiff brown clayey silt with fine
sand materials encountered at the site are relatively competent and for shallow depth up
to four 4 feet probably will require minimal trench shoring However it is also
anticipated that areas of loose sand or gravel may be encountered We recommend that
trench shoring design and installation should be left to the contractor and they should
conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
It is critical that trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 To reduce
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2805 00 Page 11
compaction effort in deep and narrow trenches select granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel or clean sand should be used in the lower portion of the trench backfill to
allow compaction by water tamping method Clayey soils are not appropriate for water
tamping use and mechanical tamping method should be used
Foundation Support
Our field exploration and laboratory testing for this preliminary exploration indicates
that the on site soils are sufficiently competent to support the relatively light structures
anticipated Our laboratory test results also indicate however that the near surface soils
on which the structures will probably bear on exhibit moderately high expansion potential
when saturated from a dry and compacted condition Therefore special foundation
design is recommended for lightly loaded slabs on grade
Spread or continuous wall footings can bear directly on the in situ soil provided
they are embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent ground An
allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of footings bearing on
the compacted select fill material The bearing value is for total dead load plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
Individual footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade
Special provisions are recommended for slabs on grade Since the on site surface
soils have a moderately high expansion potential the existing surface soils should either
be filled over or be over excavated below the slab bottom to a depth sufficient to provide
a minimum twenty four 24 inch capping layer of non expansive soil between the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2805 00 Page 12
and the natural ground In addition the twenty four 24 inch layer of capping material
should extend out two 2 feet beyond the slab perimeter
The thickened edges of slabs on grade should be embedded a minimum of twelve
12 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The slab should be a minimum of
four 4 inches thick and should be supported by a four 4 inch thick cushion of 3B
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 on top of the capping material A moisture barrier
membrane should be incorporated between the slab and cushion if necessary to protect
the interior floor covering from moisture infiltration
Additional site specific exploration should be conducted to provide data for
foundation design recommendations for all golf course structures when their final
locations and configurations have been established

Additional Exploration
This exploration was performed for a preliminary assessment of the underlying soils
and was limited to the study area comprising only a part of the overall project site Our
field exploration was performed to provide preliminary design information for drainage site
grading foundation and pavement design within the study area As such the locations
of the drilled borings may not coincide with the final location of the structural elements of
the proposed project and the analyses used to formulate the findings and
recommendations presented herein may not be sufficient or proper for the final project
design It must also be recognized that subsurface conditions in areas within the overall
project site and outside the study area may differ from those indicated by the field
exploration upon which this report is based
Therefore it is recommended that additional exploration and geotechnical
engineering analyses be performed when the design details for the subject project are
available

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling fifteen 15 borings
to depths of 15 0 to 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by a truck mounted
rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary and core drilling tools as
appropriate
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved from in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and unit weight determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain engineering
properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the
tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Physical property tests are performed to develop a correlative indices of certain
physical parameters of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering
analyses pertaining to the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment construction These test results are summarized on Plate B 1
