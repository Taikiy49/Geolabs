INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions and to provide soil data for the
design and construction of the proposed Hardstand 4 and
related structures at Honolulu International Airport
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED HARDSTAND 4 P22
Based on the boring data and laboratory test results
engineering analysis was performed to develop recommenda
tions for pavement and structural design drainage grading
and site development
7 SCOPE
The scope of this study includes field reconnaissance
geologic research review of available soil and geologic
literature and boring data from previous soil investigation
reports drilling 29 new exploratory borings laboratory
and field CBR testing analysis of field and laboratory
data engineering analysis and report preparation
This report is for the Diamond Head Hardstand No 4 area only
and the scope of this study is performed generally in accor
dance with our proposals dated 1977
This study is based on a set of preliminary plans prepared
by Park Engineering Inc Honolulu Hawait The Site Plan
and Conceptual Grading Plan for Hardstand No 4 dated February
28 1978 indicated the project site layout and tentative finish
grade elevations
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the eastern portion of the
airport terminal adjacent to Hardstand 5 of the Diamond Head
Gull Wing
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED HARDSTAND 4 P 3
The site is bounded by Taxiway 0 on the north Taxiway K
on the east Taxiway 2 on the south and the existing Gate 5
on the west
The site generally slopes down from the existing Gate 5 pave
ments toward the northeast at gradients of less than 5 percent
A concrete slab approximately 100 feet by 100 feet was noted in
the northern portion of the site Asphaltic pavements were
noted in the western and southern portions of the area
Underground fuel and electric lines were also noted at this
site
The elevations at the site generally vary from 7 to 12 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to construct a new hardstand and approach
apron areas adjacent to the existing Hardstand 5
The construction will consist of both flexible and rigid
pavements designed to support large aircraft such as the
Boeing 747
Construction of an aircraft flood light structure and six
fuel spill collectors will also be required

Soil Conditions
In general the site appears to be underlain by a
surface layer about 3 to 10 feet thick of dense
coral silty sand dredged coral fill over the
natural cemented sand and coral formation
Layers or pockets of silty clay were noted within
the upper fill layer at various locations The
underlying coral materials generally were dense
but occassional loose to medium pockets were also
encountered
Water level was encountered in the borings generally
between elevations 0 and 1 foot

FUEL SPILL COLLECTORS
The proposed fuel spill collectors will be about 4 by 4 feet
or 4 by 8 feet concrete box structures extending down to
approximately elevation minus 5 feet
The borings indicated that at this level medium to dense
sand and coral may be encountered
Dewatering for the proposed fuel collector structure should
be done with care The dewatering operation should not
loosen the trench bottom material which will support the
foundation of the structure and the dewatering pump should
discharge only clear water without fines
A 1 foot layer of granular bedding material should be placed
below the footing to provide uniform support
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on medium to dense sand and coral
For lateral earth pressures the following may be used
Above water 45 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Below water 25 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
plus water pressure
Additional loads due to aircraft loads should be
included

FLOOD LIGHT TOWER
Two aircraft flood light towers about 60 feet high are
planned near the hardstand area
The tower footings will rest on compacted granular fill
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used on
compacted granular fill The bearing values may be increased
by 1 3 for temporary loading conditions
A passive soil resistance of 300 p c f and coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used to resist lateral forces
CONSTRUCTION CONTROL AND INSPECTION
We recommend that the subgrade preparations and the soft
clay removal and backfill operations be monitored by the
soils engineer Field density tests should be taken
during the site filling operations to evaluate the com
paction density
Laboratory gradation and CBR tests should be performed on
the select fill subgrade subbase and base course materials
to verify their adequacy per design criteria
The structural footing excavations should be inspected by
the soils engineer to assure that competent bearing soils
have been obtained prior to steel and concrete placement

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
29 test borings to depths of from 10 to 26 feet at the locations
shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 and 2 The borings were drilled
with 4 inch augers using Model Versa Drill rigs Drilling
operations were performed under the observation of our project soil
engineer or geologist who assisted in obtaining representative un
disturbed samples and bulk samples of the underlying materials
Our engineers maintained field boring logs and the soils were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field
The classifications were verified by additional visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Federal Aviation Agency FAA System and
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation
of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through
A 32 In the borings samples were obtained by driving either a
standard penetration or 2 4 inch 1 D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three 3 field California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were carried
out to obtain data which would aid to evaluate the characteristics
of the on site soils for pavement design The results of the field
CBR tests are summarized on Plate A 0
