In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
services during the roadway and retaining walls construction for the above project This
report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and compaction testing
services performed during the construction for the period between June and October
2002 In general our services were coordinated and requested through Kaikor
Construction Associates Inc and your office
SITE GRADING
The project site is located in the Hanapepe area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii In
general the project involves two independent components The first component involves
replacing the pipeline at Hanapepe Stream crossing and the second component involves
slope stabilization at Hanapepe Well No 3 site New concrete paved roadways a total of
approximately 300 linear feet and 10 feet wide included in the first component were
constructed along the pipeline near the Hanapepe River Crossing New retaining walls
included in the second component were constructed at Hanapepe Well No 3 and used to
stabilize the slope failure
In general the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing and grubbing placement
and compaction of fills trench excavation and backfill and subgrade preparation and
compaction for the pavements and retaining walls construction Following the clearing and
grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were generally scarified brought to near the
optimum moisture content and compacted to the specified degree of compaction based
on visual observations and or field density tests In general the materials used as fills and
backfills consisted of 6 inch minus granular materials

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and tested The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of
this report
In general fill materials consisting of some boulders cobbles and 6 inch minus
structural fills were placed for the roadway fills and utility trench backfills Due to the
nature of the fill materials boulders cobbles and 6 inch minus fill materials the fills and
backfills were evaluated based on field observations during the fill placement Where
applicable such as the base course materials field density tests were performed in place
by the sand cone method ASTM Test Designation D 1556 During the fill and backfill
operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on
visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed
during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of seven field density tests were performed using the sand cone method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1556 The results of the compaction
tests performed during construction of the subject project are summarized on Plate 2 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared subgrade and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site where possible Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the
Replacement of Pipeline at Hanapepe River Crossing and Control Slope Failure at
Hanapepe Well No 3 observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
were performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and specifications
and our recommendations In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re
worked to achieve the specified minimum compaction
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site In addition since our services were on an
intermittent basis some of the earthwork may have been performed without our
observation It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor earthwork construction
and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted
standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied
