Dear Mr Nojima
Following the issuance of our geotechnical engineering report we conducted a
lot by lot evaluation of the mass grading plan and the anticipated soil conditions based on
our field exploration Based on this recent review it appears that the number of lots that
may be affected are significantly higher than originally anticipated The reason for this is
that portions of some of the lots may have less than the recommended 3 feet of fill over
the natural clay soil while in other lots the anticipated cuts may not remove all of the
expansive clay soils Due to the variability of the clay thickness and accuracy of the
topographic survey it should be noted that in many cases the expansive clay removal may
not involve all of the finished lot grade i e only a portion of the lot may require clay
removal and replacement A revised list of 81 house lots that may encounter clayey soils
near the finished subgrade are listed below
Phase 2A Lot Nos 10 small portion 33 50 and 51
