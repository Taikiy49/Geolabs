In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing of the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing of the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown silty clay encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some
care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling potential
of the soil
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low swelling
potential if it is kept moist However previous experience indicates
that its swelling potential may increase to moderate range if allowed to
dry out Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils

underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the swelling
potential of the natural soil

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is antici
pated such as the garage driveway and the lower house areas the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum
thickness of non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infil
tration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the
slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1500 00 Page 9
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or less of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
