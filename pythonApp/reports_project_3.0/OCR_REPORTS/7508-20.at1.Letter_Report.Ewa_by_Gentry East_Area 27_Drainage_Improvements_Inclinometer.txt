Geolabs, Inc. is pleased to submit our letter report entitled "Inclinometer Installation 
and Monitoring, Ewa by Gentry-East, Area 27 Drainage Improvements, Ewa, Oahu, 
Hawaii," prepared for the project. The project location and general vicinity are shown on 
the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report is intended to provide the results of our inclinometer installation 
and monitoring services for the drainage improvement project.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is along the eastern border of Area 27 of the Ewa by Gentry-East 
development in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that this project includes 
the removal of the 48, 54, and 78-inch diameter aluminum and metal drain lines that connect 
to an existing retention basin to the north and replacing them with a new drainage system 
consisting of 8-foot by 6-foot concrete box culverts. Due to the close proximity of the drain 
line excavation to existing structures, the installation of an inclinometer to monitor the lateral 
movements with depth was desired. During box culvert excavation and installation, daily 
monitoring of the inclinometer was recommended. Subsequently, the inclinometer should 
be monitored on a weekly basis until one month after project completion.  
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS  
In general, our boring performed for the inclinometer casing installation encountered 
a 4-foot-thick surface layer of fill consisting of loose to medium dense silty sand that was 
underlain by alluvium to a depth of about 7 feet below the existing ground surface. The 
alluvium layer consisted of silty clay and was underlain by medium hard to hard coral 
formation to the maximum borehole depth of about 22 feet below the existing ground 
surface.

INCLINOMETER INSTALLATION 
The inclinometer casing was installed in the drilled borehole on March 12, 2022 to 
monitor horizontal movement with depth during construction. The slope inclinometer used 
on this project was manufactured by Slope Indicator Company. The inclinometer casing 
was made of acrylonitrile butadiene styrene (ABS) semi-rigid plastic with an outside 
diameter of 2.75 inches and was installed in the drilled borehole to a depth of 22 feet 
below the existing ground surface. The purpose of the inclinometer casing is to allow the 
lowering of the slope inclinometer device into the borehole to measure the lateral 
deflection of the soil at various depths in order to determine where the earth movements 
are taking place as well as how much movements have occurred.  The inclinometer was 
read daily during the construction and at approximately one-week intervals for one month 
after the completion of the project. The approximate location of the inclinometer is shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
INCLINOMETER MONITORING RESULTS 
Our monitoring program included daily inclinometer readings during the box culvert 
installation from March 17, 2022 to August 31, 2022, and weekly readings from 
September 1, 2022 to September 30, 2022. The results of our inclinometer monitoring 
program are shown as plots of profile change vs. depth. The change in profile measures the 
lateral movement of the ground between the base line reading taken on March 17, 2022 and 
each subsequent inclinometer reading. 
In general, the inclinometer recorded lateral earth movements perpendicular to the 
box culvert alignment (A-Axis) and parallel to the alignment (B-Axis). As shown on 
Plates 4 through 10, the inclinometer results indicated no significant lateral ground 
movements were measured during the monitoring period.
