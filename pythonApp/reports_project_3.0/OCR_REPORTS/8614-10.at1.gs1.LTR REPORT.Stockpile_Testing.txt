Dear Mr. Phillips: 
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services performed 
for the Kapolei Harborside Stockpile Materials project in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project site is generally located north of Malakole Street and west of Kalaeloa 
Boulevard in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical recommendations resulting 
from our sampling and laboratory testing for the project. These findings and geotechnical 
recommendations are intended to evaluate the suitability of the soils for use as general fill 
and/or non-expansive select granular fill material only. The findings and recommendations 
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the information provided, there are two main soil stockpile areas at the 
project site. The larger of the two stockpiles, designated as Stockpile A, is located west 
of Hanua Access Road on the south end of the project site. The second stockpile is 
designated as Stockpile B and is located east of Hanua Access Road. Stockpile A is 
roughly 1,330 feet by 360 feet and roughly 30 feet in height. Stockpile B is roughly 260 
feet by 80 feet and roughly 20 feet in height. We understand it is desired to evaluate the 
suitability of the existing stockpiled materials for use as general fill and/or 
non-expansive select granular material.  
This report presents our findings and recommendations derived from our field 
exploration conducted specifically for the material evaluation of Stockpiles A and B for 
the Kapolei Harborside project. The general location of each stockpile is shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2.

SITE DESCRIPTION 
The two stockpiles of the materials excavated from the drainage channel are 
located west and east of the Hanua Access Road. Based on our field observations at the 
time of our exploration, Stockpiles A and B encompass areas of about 53,200 and 
2,310 square yards, respectively. Additionally, the average heights of Stockpiles A and B 
were measured at about 30 and 20 feet, respectively. It should be noted that grading 
operations, including excavation of the drainage channel adjacent to Stockpile A, are still 
in progress at the time of this report. 
STOCKPILE EXPLORATION 
We explored the existing stockpiles at the project site by taking a total of 15 bulk 
samples from the near-surface materials. The bulk samples are designated as Bulk A-1 
through Bulk A-10 and Bulk B-1 through Bulk B-5. The approximate bulk sample locations 
are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
The stockpile materials generally consisted of tannish brown silty/gravelly sand, 
silty/clayey gravel, and sandy silt. It should be noted that the stockpile materials contained 
cobbles and boulders. 
Based on the laboratory test results, CBR values ranged from 9.8 to greater than 
100, with CBR swells ranging from 0.00 to 2.79 percent for the samples tested. The 
majority of the samples tested (13 out of 15 samples) had CBR values of 17 or greater 
with CBR swell of less than 1 percent.  We believe that these materials may be used as 
non-expansive granular fill material provided the material is screened to a 3-inch minus 
material. 
Two of the 15 samples tested had CBR values of 14.2 and 9.8, with CBR swells of 
1.31 and 2.79 percent.  We believe that these samples do not qualify as non-expansive 
granular fill material and may be used as general fill material provided the material is 
screened to a 3-inch minus material. 
Additional testing should be performed on the 3-inch minus material to verify its 
suitability.  It should be noted that the material in the stockpiles may vary as more of the 
stockpile is exposed, and unsuitable fill material may be encountered. 
The findings submitted herein are based in part upon information obtained from the 
soil samples collected at selected locations across the stockpile limits. Variations of 
subsurface conditions between and beyond the sampling locations may occur, and the 
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until excavation and hauling 
of the stockpile materials are underway. If variations then appear evident, Geolabs should 
be contacted to re-evaluate the findings presented in this report. 
LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from the bulk samples. Variations of subsoil conditions beyond the 
field data point may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become 
evident until excavation and hauling of the stockpile material are underway. If variations 
then appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided 
herein. 
The sampling locations indicated in this report are approximate, having been 
located using a handheld GPS device. The physical location and elevation of the test pits 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.
