PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the Makai side of Ala Moana Center in Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided Ala Moana Center was
originally built in the late 1950s and was expanded recently The current pavement within
the center consists of both flexible and rigid pavements A pavement management
program was proposed by Zimmer Consultants Ltd to ensure the quality of the
pavements within the center Currently the program involves a 10 year pavement
rehabilitation starting at Year 2010
This project is considered as Phase 1 of the 10 year program In general
three areas were selected for this initial phase Area 1 is near the west south corner of
Ala Moana Center near Ala Moana Boulevard and Piikoi Street Areas 2 and 3 are at the
main entrance along the Ala Moana Boulevard and extend into the middle of Ala Moana
Center A Site Plan showing the project areas is presented as Plate 2

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from the
Waianae and Koolau Shield Volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which
time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a
series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred
to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is on the southern flank of the Koolau Volcano and on the coastal
plain of Southern Oahu Therefore the geomorphology and subsurface conditions in the
area are directly related to the glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the
Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The coastal
plain was built on the eroded flanks of the Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern
two thirds of the Island of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive accumulation of
alluvium derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and associated
deposits
The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana area are dominated by fossil
Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are intercalated with
terrigenous sediments laid during the higher stands of the sea Therefore the project site
is generally underlain by deposits consisting of calcareous sediments and lagoonal
deposits A surface layer of man made fill was placed over these deposits for the
development of the Ala Moana area within the last century

PAVEMENT DESIGN
The design procedures used in determining the new flexible pavement section is
based on the Guide for Design of Pavement Structures 1993 developed by the
American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials AASHTO In
general we recommend the pavement rehabilitation for this project consist of removing
the existing asphaltic concrete layer by milling or cold planning and replacing it with new
asphaltic concrete of about 3 inches in thickness
Traffic Determinations
While the project site is located within a shopping center we expect the majority of
the traffic that will travel through the project scope likely will involve passenger vehicles
and some small trucks Based the AASHTO design guide we utilized a minimum
50 000 equivalent single axial loads ESALs in our design for a 20 year design period
Pavement Structural Sections
Our field observations indicate that the pavement area under the current project
scope generally exhibit medium to high severity distress The current design concept
involves removing the existing the asphaltic concrete and replacing with new asphaltic
concrete surface
Based on our field exploration and our experience at the center we anticipate loose
fill materials at relatively shallow depths as evidenced by the subsurface conditions
encountered in Area 2 B 1 and B 2 Due to the presence of the loose materials very near
the surface conventional pavement reconstruction would involve removing a minimum of
2 feet of the near surface materials and replacing it with gravel backfill wrapped with
geotextile fabrics The new flexible pavement section asphaltic concrete and base course
would then be constructed over the engineered backfill This typical approach likely will
involve a fair amount of excavation and potential over excavation
To reduce the potential for encountering the near surface loose to very loose fill
materials below the existing pavement structure we recommend the following approach to
rehabilitate the pavement at the project locations In general we recommend removing the
existing asphaltic concrete layer to expose the underlying base and or subbase materials
We then recommend proof rolling the exposed base materials to obtain a dense and
unyielding surface A 3 inch thick layer of new asphaltic concrete layer may be placed on
the proof rolled existing base In this way we would not excavate below the existing
pavement surface any deeper than necessary to reduce the potential for encountering the
loose to very loose fill materials encountered in the borings
After removal of the existing asphaltic concrete layer to accommodate the new
pavement structural section the exposed subgrades should be proof rolled with a
minimum 5 ton static weight drum roller for a minimum of eight passes to compact the
exposed subgrade The drum roller should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per

Based on our reconnaissance of the project area we noted that some ponding of
surface water on the pavement surface in Areas 2 and 3 The low spots where ponding
water occurred also coincided with the distress area We believe the ponding water on the
pavement surface contributed to the distress observed at these locations Therefore we
recommend the paved areas be sloped adequately and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavements In addition surface water ponding
should not be allowed on site during or after construction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
In general we recommended Geolabs be retained to provide follow up
geotechnical services during construction The critical items of construction monitoring that
require Special Inspection include observation of the base material proof rolling This is
to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared
The recommendations provided herein are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
