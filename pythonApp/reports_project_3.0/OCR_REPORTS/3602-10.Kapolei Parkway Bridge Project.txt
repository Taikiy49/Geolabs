TRIAL SHAFT AND LOAD TEST PROGRAM
KAPOLEI PARKWAY BRIDGE PROJECT
Dear Mr Thomas
In accordance with the project specifications a trial shaft program including an
instrumented load test was conducted at a selected location of the west abutment for the
Kapolei Parkway Bridge project between August 13 and August 20 1998 This report
presents the results of our field observations during the trial shaft program and the results
of the instrumented load test conducted
Based on the results of the instrumented load test we recommend that the new
estimated drilled shaft tip elevations presented herein be used for the production drilled
shafts for the project The new production drilled shaft tip elevations are shorter than
those shown on the project plans and reflect the more favorable skin friction values
obtained from instrumented load test results
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kapolei Parkway Bridge project is located within the Villages of Kapolei in the
District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The new bridge structure will be an
extension of the existing Kapolei Parkway across the existing Kapolei Lower Channel
Based on the project plans the bridge will be approximately 120 feet long from abutment
to abutment about 125 feet wide and about 18 feet above the bottom of the Kapolei
Lower Channel floor The bridge will be a two span bridge supported at each end by
abutments and by a center pier located within the open drainage channel The west
abutment of the bridge structure will straddle an existing twin box culvert of about 36 feet
wide discharging into Kapolei Lower Channel
Based on discussions with the project structural engineer the structural loading of
the bridge at the east and west abutments is approximately 25 kips per lineal foot of
abutment and the structural loading at the center pier is about 36 kips per lineal foot of

TRIAL SHAFT PROGRAM
As part of the project specifications a trial shaft consisting of an 18 inch diameter
drilled shaft was excavated at a selected location at the west abutment area on
August 13 1998 by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company the contractor The trial
shaft was placed at a location approximately 23 feet west of the centerline of the west
abutment The intent of the trial shaft installation is to demonstrate the adequacy of the
drilling method and equipment proposed by the contractor for the soil formation at the
project site
The trial shaft was excavated using a Watson 2500 track mounted drill rig and
the bottom of the trial shaft was excavated to about 71 feet below the existing ground
surface or to elevation 11 feet Mean Sea Level MSL During the drilled shaft
excavation it was noted that the sidewalls of the drilled shaft hole held up relatively well
without the need for temporary casing of the drilled shaft hole Therefore the trial shaft
excavation proceeded without using a temporary casing for drilled hole stabilization
During the trial shaft excavation a log of materials encountered was maintained by an
engineer from our office A log of the trial shaft construction and materials encountered is
provided on Plates 1 1 and 1 2
As part of the trial shaft program the trial shaft was converted to the load test shaft
by constructing the trial shaft with a reinforcing steel cage Following the drilled shaft
excavation the contractor placed a reinforcing steel cage equipped with seven
embedment strain gages manufactured by Geokon into the drilled shaft hole The project
specifications required only six embedment strain gages to be installed Concrete was
then placed in the drilled hole using a PVC tremie pipe After approximately 1 cubic yard
of concrete was placed in the trial shaft hole one of the PVC tremie pipe fittings was
damaged Since the hole appeared to be relatively dry by observation concrete
placement proceeded by the dry construction method where the concrete was pumped
through a rubber concrete drop hose and allowed to free fall to the bottom of the hole

The load test shaft was extended above the existing ground surface by means of a

tip of the load test shaft The seven embedment strain gages were connected through a

The load test shaft was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the shaft
was planned at about 600 kips In general the load test was loaded to the maximum
test load of 600 kips in 50 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at
least 15 minutes or until the rate of settlement was generally stable The 200 kip and
400 kip load increments were held for 60 minutes After loading the load test shaft to
the maximum test load of 614 kips the maximum test load was held for 4 hours and
12 hours as required Subsequently the test load was unloaded in approximately four
equal decrements and held for at least 15 minutes at each decrement The results of
the load test and the strain gage readings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 10 and
are summarized as follows

INTERPRETATION OF LOAD TEST RESULTS
Based on the readings obtained from the embedment strain gages it appears that
two of the seven embedment strain gages experienced some damage during the load
test shaft installation The two embedment strain gages with some peculiar readings
two of the instruments malfunction in the load test shaft due to installation of the shaft
Even with the five remaining embedment strain gages we were able to interpret the load
test results to provide reasonable load transfer curves for reducing the length of the
production drilled shafts
In order to interpret the load transfer in the load test shaft the modulus of elasticity
of the concrete will be required On the days of the load test August 19th and 20th two
concrete compression tests were performed on the load test shaft concrete The results
of the two unconfined compressive strength tests were 5 554 and 5 660 pounds per
square inch psi with an average of about 5 600 psi Based on an unconfined
compressive strength of 5 600 psi we estimate the modulus of elasticity of the concrete
to be on the order of about psi
Using a modulus of elasticity of psi and the remaining five embedment
strain gages the interpreted load transfer curves in the load test shaft are shown on
Plates 9 and 10 Based on these load transfer curves we estimate that the skin friction
values between the drilled shaft concrete in the soil materials to be between 1 5 and
2 0 kips per square foot ksf It should be noted that the portion of the load test shaft
between depths of about 45 and 70 feet did not come close to mobilizing their ultimate
skin friction values in the load test
RECOMMENDED PRODUCTION SHAFT TIP ELEVATIONS
Based on our observations of the trial shaft program we believe that the
demonstrated excavation method and equipment are acceptable for installing the
production drilled shafts in general accordance with the project specifications It should
be noted that the trial shaft was installed without the use of a temporary casing
Therefore the need for temporary casing of the production drilled shafts will need to be
evaluated on a shaft by shaft basis by an engineer from our office during the production
shaft installation In the event that one or more of the production drilled shafts require the
