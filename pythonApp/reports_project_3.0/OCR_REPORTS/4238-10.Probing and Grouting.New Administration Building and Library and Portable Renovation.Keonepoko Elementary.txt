This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing and grouting operations for the New Administration Building and Library project at
Keonepoko Elementary School in the Pahoa area from June 12 through 19 2001
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Keonepoko Elementary School in the
Pahoa area on the Island of Hawaii The project will involve the construction of a new
administration building and library as part of the development plan of the Keonepoko
Elementary School The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided the new administration building will be located east
of the existing cafetorium and north of the existing parking lot The new building will be a
one story concrete structure covering approximately 4 600 square feet in area The new
library will be located between the existing 2 story classroom buildings E and H to the
south and north respectively The new library will be a one story structure covering
approximately 6 300 square feet in building area
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished floor elevations of 368 5
and 362 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the administration building and library
respectively minor cuts of about 1 foot and fills of up to about 5 feet thick are anticipated for

Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with the
recommendations presented in our report for the project dated March 26 1999 The purpose
of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation
support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the new
buildings
The probe hole locations were marked by Stan s Contracting Inc prior to the probing
operation In general the probe holes were drilled at proposed column footing locations
and or spaced at about 10 feet on centers along continuous footings The probe holes
about 3 inches in diameter were drilled by Goodfellow Brothers Inc using a TAMRAC a
pressurized air percussion hammer on a truck mounted drill rig The probe holes within the
building footprints were drilled to depths of about 12 feet below the footing subgrade
elevation Following the probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into
each of the probe holes to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential
for cave in prior to the grouting operation Based on our observation of the probing
operations it appeared that cavities and or voids were encountered at Probe Hole Nos C 1
the Library Building Additional probe holes were drilled at closer spacing at these locations
to delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavities A total of 121 probe holes with an
aggregate length of about 1 452 lineal feet were drilled The approximate locations of the
probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans Plate 2 1 and 2 2
The probe holes were backfilled with sand cement grout supplied by Concrete
Aggregate Hawaii injected at low to moderate pressures The grouting work was performed
by Goodfellow Brothers Inc and observed by a field representative from Geolabs The
grouting operation generally proceeded in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe was
removed
e A 2 inch diameter grouting probe was inserted into the open hole and lowered
to approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole

Approximately 49 cubic yards of sand cement grout was utilized for the grouting
