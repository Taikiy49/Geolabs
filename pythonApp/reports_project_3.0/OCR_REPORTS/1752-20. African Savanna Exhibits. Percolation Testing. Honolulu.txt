SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the eastern portion of the Honolulu Zoo and
is situated on the Diamond Head side of Kapahulu Avenue between Paki
Avenue and Monsarrat Avenue Currently the site is hilly with ground
elevations ranging from 3 to 15 feet These hills were constructed as
part of the initial grading for the African Savanna Exhibits project

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration the most appropriate drainage layer
for the surface run off disposal is the silty fine sand generally
encountered below the lagoonal clay deposit at an elevation of 0 feet
The percolation rates of this layer were measured to be moderate to low in
our field tests The measured coefficients of permeability ranged from
3 9 x 10 to 7 0 x and are typical for silty fine
sand The drainage capacity of the leaching field is estimated to be
about 0 3 inch per hour per square foot of contact area between the gravel
backfill material and the in situ silty sand The depth of the trench
for the leaching field thus depends on the quantity of design surface
run off
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some extent
Therefore the leaching field will probably require periodic maintenance
