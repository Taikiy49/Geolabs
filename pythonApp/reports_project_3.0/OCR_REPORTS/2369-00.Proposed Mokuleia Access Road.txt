RECONNAISSANCE REPORT
PROPOSED MORULETA ACCESS ROAD
In accordance with your request we have performed a preliminary
geologic reconnaissance of the near surface soil conditions at the
proposed access road
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the reconnaissance was to explore the near surface
soil conditions at the project site in order to provide preliminary
recommendations for pavement design and road grading
The scope of our work on the project consisted of reviewing available
soils geologic maps and literature concerning the area geologic field
reconnaissance excavation and sampling of shallow test pits along the
alignment laboratory testing engineering analysis and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in what was formerly known as the
Dillingham Ranch in Mokuleia on the north shore of the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to construct an all weather access road
approximately 4 000 L F in length between the area of the Dillingham
House and Peacock Flats Access Road to replace the portion of the existing
access road which will be included in a future golf course development
The area from Dillingham House to Farrington Highway a distance of
about 3 000 L F is not included in our scope of work
SITE DESCRIPPION
The project site is located on a gently sloping hillside in the
Mokuleia area on the north shore of the island of Oahu
The ground elevations vary from approximately 60 to 360 feet above
Mean Sea Level MSL
In general the topography slopes downward from south to north at
about 3 1 to 5 1 H V The lower area east of the Dillingham House is
flatter at about 10 1 to 20 1 H V The area is vegetated with small
trees and tall grasses and used as pasture land A wet area spring was
noted east of Test Pit No 5
Lava rock outcrops were noted along the southwest portions of the
proposed road above elevation 280 to 370 near Test Pit and 3

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the north flank of the Waianae Shield
Volcano After the cessation of volcanic activity in the later Pliocene
early Pleistocene erosional dissection of the original dome has created a
thick alluvial apron along the seaward margins of the Waianae Range This
alluvial plain is composed of interbedded sediments of both terrigenous
and marine origin The roadway alignment crosses through the Waianae
Volcanic Series older alluvium and younger alluvial deposits
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating
eleven 11 Test Pits with a hoptoe to observe and sample the near surface
soil conditions for testing
Based on the field exploration the soil types within the project
site may be generalized into three distinct groups as follows
Recent Alluvium
Recent alluvial deposits were encountered in the lower area of the
site The recent alluvium encountered consisted primarily of medium
stiff to soft brown to gray clay The area is generally situated
where run off from sugarcane irrigation and upslope water drains
through the flatter ground
Older Alluviun
Based on the field exploration the soils encountered in the higher
elevations upper area may be characterized as Older Alluviun
The soils encountered in the exploration generally ranged from hard
brown clays to hard brown silty clays

In general most of the Older Alluvium soils may be classified as
having moderately high to high swelling potential However there
was also considerable variation in the swelling potential of the
soils encountered with the results of our laboratory testing ranging
from 6 to over 20 Visually the clayey soils appear to be similar
and the difference in swelling potential when wetted is delineated
mainly by laboratory swell testing
Lava Rock Formation
Outcrops of lava rock were observed near the areas from Test Pit No
1 to Test Pit No 3
GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING CONSIDERATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS
The test pits and laboratory testing disclosed that the project site
area is in general covered by a 3 to 5 foot or more thick highly
expansive clay layer which contains many cobbles and boulders Basalt
rock was encountered at shallow depth only at the upper end of the road
alignment
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris previous fill and soft material
Fill Placement
The soils and rock formation materials excavated during earthwork
should provide adequate fill material for low embankments Expansive
soils if encountered should be placed a minimm of 3 feet below
finished grade and away from slope areas if possible
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
2369 00 Page 5
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in fills provided
that they are not allowed to nest together and the voids between
boulders are filled
If additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to
the site The material should generally be of low expansion
potential and should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or deleterious materials
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
All fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding
10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 95 of its
maximum dry density established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into
the existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope
for details see Plate 3 This detail is essential for the
stability of the fills particularly to reduce the potential of
sliding along the fill in situ ground contact The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers
Proper drainage is essential for satisfactory performance of the cuts
and embankment fills as excessive water will weaken the clayey soils
Slopes
For the proposed roadway the hillsides may be cut for a vertical
height of about 15 to 20 feet

Based on the Test Pit information the surficial clay layer is about
3 to 5 feet thick or more at the site Shallow cuts will therefore
be made mostly in clay soils
In general cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter may be considered for slope heights of about 20
feet or less in silty and sandy soils decomposed rock and in select
fill materials Low slopes less than 6 feet in clay CH soils
should be designed at three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V or
flatter slopes or reconstructed with select soils after removal of
the clay soils
Some surficial sloughing and slumping in the clay slope may occur
and therefore the routine site maintenance program should include
periodic clearing of debris and slope repair
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Surface water run off should be diverted away from the tops and toes
of cut and fill slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Soft materials seepage zones and other adverse geologic conditions
may be encountered during site grading and may significantly affect
the stability of the proposed cut and fill slopes We recommend that
provisions should be made in the construction budget for the
construction of local rock fill buttress which may be required to
support over excavation and to increase the stability of the cut and
fill slopes
Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the upper southwest portions
of the site may be underlain at shallow depths by basalt formation
GEOLABS HAWAII

Relatively hard ripping is anticipated in the basalt formation The
basalt rock in this area may require special excavating procedures
such as hoeram equipment or blasting to make the proposed road cuts
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the Test Pit data
Groundwater seepage was noted near Test Pit No 5 Subdrains are
recommended across the road in these areas to prevent the build up of
seepage pressures which could destabilize the road pavement and
embankments A detail of the subdrain is presented on Plate 4
Access Road Pavements
For the light automobile traffic anticipated for the access roadway
the following preliminary pavement design thickness may be used for
estimating purposes depending on the results of laboratory CBR tests on
the on site soils during construction
1 5 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 0 Inch Select Borrow for about 75 of the roadway
19 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The 12 inch select borrow section may be eliminated if the top 2 feet
below the subgrade is low expansive bouldery material or weathered lava
rock
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas

Additional Exploration
The recommendations presented in this reconnaissance report are
presented as a guide for planning purposes only Subsurface explorations
including borings or additional test pits may be required for specific
project areas when the road alignment is determined and the grading plan
delineates the cut and fill areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and pavement design recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
grading phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Detailed construction observations are of paramount importance in
verifying the slope ratios and pavement design assumptions during the site
grading
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from test pits and laboratory
test data obtained from this exploration Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined from the
surveyed location of the test pits on the topographic map provided The
physical location of the field data points should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Belt Collins
Associates for specific application to this project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No
other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating
purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
