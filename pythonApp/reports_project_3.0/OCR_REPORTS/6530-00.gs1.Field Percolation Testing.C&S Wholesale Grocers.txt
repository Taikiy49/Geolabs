In accordance with our fee proposal dated this letter report provides
the results of the field percolation testing performed for the C S Wholesale Grocers
project located in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the field percolation testing and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended to assist in the design of the sewage waste disposal for
the existing building only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is near the intersection of Malakole Street and Hanua Street in the
Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that a septic system
consisting of leaching field and septic tanks will be installed for wastewater disposal from
the existing building for the project The location of the septic system is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site and to perform field percolation testing at the leaching field site to
obtain percolation characteristic of the subsoil The percolation information will be utilized
in the septic system design for the existing building Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated The scope of our work included
the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 each to a depth of about 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface fill layer comprised of asphaltic
concrete and dense silty gravel to about 1 foot depth The surface fill layer was underlain
by coralline detritus deposit consisting of dense silty gravel with sand to the maximum
depth explored of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface We did not encounter
groundwater in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 in Appendix A
Field percolation tests were performed in the borings in general accordance with
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the

subsoil profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the
percolation tests were performed by drilling 4 0 inch diameter boreholes extending to a

During the percolation testing the holes were carefully filled with water to a
minimum height of 6 inches above the bottom of the holes The time intervals and water
drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the percolation rates
were based Based on the test results percolation rates of 0 98 and 1 8 minutes per inch
were measured within the silty gravel with sand in Boring Nos 1 and 2 respectively The
results of the individual percolation tests are presented on Plates A 3 and A 4 of this
report
Based on the results of the field percolation tests we believe that the silty gravel
with sand encountered at the proposed leaching field site is suitable for shallow leaching
field system

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation testing Variations of conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from reference points
indicated on the Site Plan received from Engineering Concepts Inc on
The locations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tidal fluctuation seasonal rainfall and
other factors
