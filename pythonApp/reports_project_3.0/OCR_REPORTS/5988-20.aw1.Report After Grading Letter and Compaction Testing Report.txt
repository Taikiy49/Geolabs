This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing
services performed during the earthwork construction of the Kapa a Homesteads 325

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located mainly on Kaapuni Road in the vicinity of Kapa a on the
Island of Kauai Hawaii The project generally consisted of installing 3 600 lineal feet of
new overflow drain line a lined drainage ditch and an overflow drain line along the
right of way of Kaapuni Road A Project Location Map is shown on Plate 1
Construction of the new drain line consisted of installing new 18 inch and 12 inch
HDPE drain pipes and underground concrete drainage structures The overflow
drain line from the well site at the intersection of Kaapuni Road and Kawaihau Road
empties into a newly excavated and lined drainage ditch An overflow drain line from the
ditch empties into an existing waterway The location of the project is shown on the
Overall Site Plan Plate 2 1 and Site Plans Plates 2 2 and 2 3

during the site grading backfill and compaction work for the period between

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 15 feet
deep for installation of new drainage structures drain lines and the drainage ditch
Additionally placement and compaction of fills and backfills for the drain lines and
drainage structures and pavement construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the limits of grading was cleared and
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials During the site grading operations samples of
the on site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
In general the utility trench backfills consisting of on site soils placed in near
level lifts and were moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Due to the high in situ
moisture content of the on site soils the specified minimum relative compaction
requirements for on site soils were reduced to 85 percent relative compaction per our
geotechnical report
Aggregate Base Coarse materials imported from Kauai Aggregates were utilized
in lieu of the aggregate subbase specified in the project requirements The first lift of
base coarse materials above the subgrade was moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 relative compaction Due to the
reduced subgrade compaction requirement and the high in situ moisture content of the
subgrade soils the first lift of Aggregate Base Coarse materials was reduced to
90 percent relative compaction per our geotechnical report
The final lift of Aggregate Base Coarse materials below the roadways was
moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as needed basis to confirm subgrade soil conditions exposed during the earthwork
operations The field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 17 of this report These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas that did not meet the required moisture and density
