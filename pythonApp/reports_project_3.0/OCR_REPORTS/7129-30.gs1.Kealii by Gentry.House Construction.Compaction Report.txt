Dear Mr. Kayser: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during house construction for the Keali‘i by Gentry project from 
October 21, 2019 to September 30, 2021 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
October 22, 2019. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located between Kualakai Parkway and Kapolei Golf Course in 
the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The planned development consists of 
66 single-family residences, landscaped areas, a park, and roadways. The site 
development during house construction consisted of house pads, lanais, garages, 
driveways, and retaining walls.  In addition, electrical and plumbing lines were installed. 
A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the house grading and utility installation at the project site. 
HOUSE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION 
We observed the house grading and utility installation work for the Keali’i by Gentry 
project from October 21, 2019 to September 30, 2021. The house grading work consisted 
of footing excavation and footing and slabs-on-grade subgrade preparation for the project. 
The utility installation work consisted of trench backfill of plumbing and sewer lines within 
the house lot. Dura Construction performed the house grading and utility installation work. 
During the house grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for

testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the house grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density 
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.25 of 
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained 
and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the house grading and utility installation work for the 
above-referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.

Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Kapolei Gentry, Kapolei, Oahu, Hawaii", dated March 2, 2015.
