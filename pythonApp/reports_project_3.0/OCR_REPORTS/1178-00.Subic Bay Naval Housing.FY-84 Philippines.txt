This letter presents our observations and comments on Geo
technical Investigation Report Appendix C by Technosphere
Consultants Group Inc TCG for the above project Also
reviewed is a landslide investigation report by The Earth
Technology Corporation Ertec dated August 12 1981 for
projects in the vicinity of the site and in other areas inside
Subic Bay
There are substantial amounts of soil test data in the above
mentioned reports related to the site To date we have made a
cursory review of the report without detailed study of these
data mainly because samples are not available to us for visual
examiantion and evaluation testing

In addition we have not made any field inspection or recon
naissance of the site to enable us to closely relate past
experiences with the data presented
Our comments are directed mainly to the soils engineering
investigation approach of the report rather than detail of
testing
Slope Stability
It is our understanding that one of the major concerns is slope
stability at the project site either in a natural cut fill or
a combination slope We believe that this site investigation
could have focussed more on the geologic origin of the on site
materials and how that relates to the stability of future site
grading required for the proposed development
The Ertec report indicates that translational slides and debris
flow had occurred often related to heavy rains particularly
the debris flow type landslides That report further states
that translational slides are more prevailant in the Kalayaan
Housing predominantly along northerly facing slopes bordering
Kalaklan River Valley It is also noted in the TCG report
that in Site 1 the borings indicated some soil zones having
low N values ranging from 4 to 10 blows per foot See Boring

It is conceivable that these soil zones may be critical in the
stability analysis
In light of the above we believe that the aspect of slide
potential at these sites should be investigated more vigorously
in the TCG investigation report Addition strength testing
particularly triaxial tests such as CU and CD tests may be
required
Debris flows are reported to occur most frequently along
saprolite zone that overlies less permeable underlying unit
The TCG soils report seems to imply that based on the test
results there is no problematic soils saprolite at either
Site However we believe that a more detailed discussions
with substantiating test results as well as a geologist s con
firmation would be justified
While the cause or causes and the mechanics of debris flow are
not fully understood from a soil mechanic s point of view it
is our belief that they seem to relate to geologic origin type
of soil and slope configuration The importance of identifying
the geologic units in the field exploration their relationships
and any potential planes of weakness and discontinuity should
be also a primary concern of the TCG soils report Geologic
units encountered in the borings and test pits should be iden
tified relating the soils encountered to their origins and
structures

As for stability of fi11 slopes the TCG soils report seems to
indicate that the use of on site materials would be acceptable
provided that a good construction procedure is followed Fre
quently the outer portions of the fill slope receive little
compaction during construction and may eventually slough down
Thus details for i11 slope construction should be provided
For example fill slopes could be constructed oversize and
trimmed back to the design grades
It is our understanding that an amended recommendation on
embankment slopes is 2 horizontal to 1 vertical A specific
slope analysis may be required if any design slope height
becomes excessive A recommendation on an acceptable factor of
safety for various slopes under seismic condition should be
made When a slope is benched according to the recommendation
in the report the factor of safety also changes A comment on
such a situation would also be appropriate
Drainage
The slope stability is also related to drainage conditions
Frequently cross slope ditches are used to minimize sheet Flow
Situation and to control slope erosion In general the drain
age should be away from the top of Slope and limit ponding in
the upper lot of the slope In other words the upper lots
could be graded to shed water including gutter water toward
the front of the lot and onto the paved roadway area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1178 00
Page 5
As much as possible drainage ditches and buried service lines
should not be placed near the top of slope to minimize poten
tial water infiltration into the underlying soils due to
possible utility leakage or breakage Otherwise special
design details such as lining the trench using impervious
backfill or other considerations to minimize seepage into the
soil may be required These details should be provided by a
soils engineer after consultation with project planners and
civil designers before proceeding with the final design
Erosion Control
Debris flow could also be associated with highly erodable
nature of the soils Continued erosion of superficially
disturbed slope could cause a Slough to develop progressively
into a major problem Although well developed slope pianting
and grassing tend to minimize erosion however if the site
soils are heavily leached and lack nutrients effectiveness of
initial slope planting and cover may be limited Cost impact
of maintainence requirements such as matting fertilizing and
watering on grassed slope should also be considered Other
methods to minimize the on set of slope erosion such as
guniting chemical spraying terraced slopes riprap or use of
rockwalls and cribbed walls with proper filter protection can
be evaluated

We believe that it is important that during the construction
particularly in cut slopes and in keying and benching effort
should be made to delineate geologic units and their configura

Footing Design
The recommendations on allowable bearing pressures in the TCG
report will probably require further interpretation and input
by a soils engineer Simplification of allowable bearing
capacity recommendation is suggested because the range of the
values is not significantly different
Tie beams are recommended in the soils report for foundations
in fill It is felt that with a controlled engineered fill
and sufficient footing setback from the top edge of the fill
slope tie beams may not be required
There is no indication whether the allowable bearing pressures
when calculated with various conditions in the report will
include allowance for the wind or seismic loads This point
should be clarified

The anticipated settlements should be addressed in more detail
Perhaps a range of expected settlements under a typical fill
and or building loading would better aid the design engineer
Pavement Design
The pavement design calls for 4 inch minimum select subgrade
It is not clear whether the select subgrade is a base course
subbase or actually a better on site material but not quite as
good a quality to be called as subbase And if sO what type
of on site material would be acceptable
It is felt that there should be a typical pavement section
with recommended minimum CBR values for subbase and base
course and reference to other pertinent qualities It is
presumed that there are locally available quarry materials that
meet typical American standards in particular the U S Navy
standards

clarification on this matter should be made

Permeability
Much of concern on stability is related to flow of water
through soil media Thus permeability tests of undisturbed
and remolded compacted samples may provide additional data in
assessing mud flow potential Furthermore field percolation
through boreholes or test pits may be conducted to examine the
overall in situ soil permeability characteristics The degree
of compactive effort on the fill materials should also be
related to how that may affect the water infiltration potential
of the compacted fill
Groundwater Monitoring
The Ertec report suggests that perched ground water may
develop during seasonal storms Groundwater if appears in
any existing boring should be monitored by casing the hole
with a perforated pipe to permit periodic observation of the
water table fluctuation throughout the storm seasons Such
additional long term observations would be very important to
the site stability evaluation and drainage design
Statistical Evaluation of Failed Slopes
A statistical evaluation of slope stability problem could be
made of all known slides as to its type geologic unit slope
height steepness rainfall data time of occurrence etc
within the vicinities of the two project sites in Subic Bay

Site Visits
It is felt that a site visit by one of our principal engineer
and a reconnaissance by our senior geologist is highly desired
In this way the site conditions can be related intimately

obtained from the previous investigation could be used to
