Recommendations for Pan Handle Portion of Subdivision
Royal Kunia Site 12 Subdivision
Dear Mr Gaston
We understand that only Lot Nos 1 through 8 have been fine graded thus far
within the pan handle section of Site 12 and compaction tests by Hawaii
Geotechnical Group are on record for these lots The other lots in the pan handle
area were only mass graded thus far
It is our understanding that the house layout for this pan handle area will be
changed to accommodate larger lots We anticipate that some fine grading will likely be
necessary We recommend that scarification moisture conditioning and re compaction
be performed for the existing grade since the site is overgrown with tall grasses and
weeds and the soil appears to be dry loose and disturbed Site grading
recommendations for this project are presented below
Site Grading
Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include 1 Site
Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills 3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements and
4 Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on our experience with the silty clay material in this area we observed
some of the materials to have a high collapse potential from water infiltration when the
soil was compacted to a low density at a moisture content below optimum Therefore
proper moisture conditioning and compaction are very important
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils
may be used as fill provided that they meet the requirements for fill material
If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the subgrade level
deeper excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The
excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that they meet the requirements
for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches
moisture conditioned to a minimum of 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement
subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks or prepared again as
recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that they
meet the requirements for fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Fills and Backfills
The fill material should consist of well graded low expansive materials less than
3 inches in largest dimension with a CBR swell less than 2 percent The fill
material should also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials In general
the on site silty clay material should be suitable for fill material

Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt

Imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such as

for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for
its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to a minimum of 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Hawaii during site grading to assist the contractor in
obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content
Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be
applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the fills and

Cut and Fill Slopes
Cut and fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In the
event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements
should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with
sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion
of the finished slopes
Utilities
It is anticipated that some utilities may be constructed for the proposed project
The methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should
comply with all applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring
design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and
bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty sand or gravel fill soils or
select granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to a
minimum of 2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not

Recommendations for Removal of Sewer Utilities and Manholes
Pan Handle Portion of Subdivision
Royal Kunia Site 12 Subdivision
Dear Mr Gaston
We understand that due to the new layout of house lots and roads at the
pan handle portion of the Royal Kunia Site 12 Subdivision the 8 inch diameter sewer
utilities and manholes previously installed will need to be removed and or properly filled
We understand that the sewer utilities and manholes are as deep as about 15 feet
below existing grade
We offer the following alternatives for removal and or filling of the existing sewer
utilities and manholes
1 TOTAL REMOVAL OF UTILITIES AND MANHOLES
After removal of the utilities and manholes the excavation should be
cleaned of the pipe cushion material The excavation should then be filled
with fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose level lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The pipe cushion material
may be used as fill if mixed with acceptable fill material to achieve a soil
with sufficient fines to fill the interstices
In lieu of or in combination with compacted fill material low strength
concrete CLSM also known as flowable fill may be used The concrete
should have a maximum 28 day compressive strength of 80 psi to
facilitate future excavation
2 REMOVAL OF MANHOLES ONLY
The same recommendations for filling noted in the first alternative applies
for the manholes in this alternative In addition the utilities may be
abandoned in place by pumping lean concrete under low pressure The
concrete should consist of a sand cement slurry mix with a high slump
