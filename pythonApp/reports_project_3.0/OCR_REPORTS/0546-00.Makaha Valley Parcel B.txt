PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to develop the approximately 25 acre site
for residential units The proposed units will be lightly loaded
residential structures constructed at or near the existing grades
Cuts approaching five feet will be made for the entrance at the
northeastern corner of the project
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed site is located makai of the Makaha East Golf Course
between Makaha Valley Road and Jade Street in approximately the
center of the lower portion of Makaha Valley It is connected to
Makaha Valley Road by a narrow entrance way A second narrow strip
of land which will not be utilized for the development connects
the property to Noholio Road on the south
The ground surface is grass and weed covered with areas of brush
and slopes from north to south at approximately a 5 grade The
total vertical interval varies from approximately 120 feet at
Makaha Valley Road at the extreme northeast corner of the site to
approximately Elevation 46 where the narrow strip land intersects
Noholio Road

Geology
Makaha Valley is bound by two major ridges of the Waianae Volcanic
Range Keaau Ridge on the northwest reaches an elevation of
approximately 2 400 feet On the southeast Kamaileunu Ridge has
a summit elevation of approximately 2 800 feet The soils within
the valley originate from the erosion of soil and rock from these
ridgelines
Near the side walls of the valley the soil is basically a colluvium
resulting from the collection of fallen materials at the base of
the slopes Throughout the center portion of the valley the soil
are stream sediments deposited during periods of high flow Vol
canic boulders and some cinders can also be expected throughout
this area
The deposition of the sediments was cyclic They therefore occur
in thin layers interbedded with materials deposited during low flow
periods or coral deposited during periods of high sea levels
The typical subsurface condition to be expected at Parcel B in the
center portion of the valley is an interbedded sequence of sand

silt and clay with layers of volcanic boulder sand and gravel In the
lower portion of the valley coral or other marine deposits may be
encountered
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing five test
borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan and Boring
Location Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration
methods and the accompanying laboratory testing is given in the
appendix along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
by 3 to 5 feet of clayey silt with gravel This material is under
lain by a layer of volcanic boulders or thin lava flows The
boulder layer is intermixed with clayey silt which extended to the
limits of the explorations
The upper soil varies from very stiff to hard in consistency The
boulder layer varies from medium dense to dense
Groundwater was not encountered during the subsurface explorations
PERCOLATION TESTS
The locations of the three percolation tests are also shown on the
enclosed Site Plan These holes were advanced with augers to a
Page 5
W O 546 00
depth of 10 feet and 8 5 feet of steel casing was installed The
holes were then filled to the top and left over night for the
initial saturation of the soils
The casings were again filled with water on the following day and
periodic measurements were made of the rate of change in the water
level within each casing The tests were conducted for a minimum
of four hours each Percolation Tests 1 and 2 terminated within
the boulder layer and had calculated permeabilities of approxi
mately 20 x 1074 centimeters per second This is equivalent to
the percolation of a well graded silty sand and gravel
Percolation Test 3 was terminated within a massive boulder or thin
lava flow The resulting permeability of 1 x 107 is equivalent
to a sandy clay The percolation test results indicate that the
permeability of the subsurface soils are more than adequate for
on site sewage disposal
The boring logs of the soils encountered during the auger drilling
of the percolation test holes are given in the appendix on Plates
A 6 through A 8

Portions of the proposed fill will be utilized for support of the
foundations and floor slabs of the dwellings It is therefore
recommended that the fill be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density established by American Society for Testing
and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T
Should on site soils be utilized the maximum density would be
approximately 94 pcf at an optimum moisture content of 26 The
samples obtained in our testing program had natural moisture con
tents of 20 or less It therefore appears that if the on site
soils are utilized for fill they will require the addition of
water to raise them to near optimum moisture content
Prior to the placement of any fills all organic and other dele
terious materials should be removed and the upper six inches of
the existing ground surface scarified The scarified surface
should then be brought to near optimum moisture content and com
pacted to the recommended density Additional fill should be
placed in six inch maximum lifts and compacted to the specified
density

Foundations
The soils encountered at the site will provide adequate support for
lightly loaded structures The allowable bearing capacity is in
excess of which far exceeds the anticipated maximum
loading of approximately The estimated maximum settle
ments are less than one half inch total and one quarter inch
differential
The foundations should bear a minimum of 12 inch below the finished
grade and may bear upon the natural soils or upon compacted fill

The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to placement
of the concrete to determine that the bearing conditions are con
sistent with the findings of our subsurface explorations and to
inspect for possible isolated pockets of expansive soils
Retaining Walls
It appears that low retaining walls will be utilized throughout the
Site for minor grade changes These walls should be designed for
an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pcf if the on site soils are
utilized as backfill The backfill should be compacted to between
90 and 95 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
D 1557 72T A frictional factor of 0 55 should be utilized to
determine the sliding resistance of the retaining walls foundations
Due to the free draining characteristics of the upper soils it is
not anticipated that subsurface drains will be required to prevent
the buildup of hydrostatic pressures behind the retaining walls
Weep holes should be provided to handle short term water accumu
lations during periods of high percipitation
Floor Slabs
No expansive soils were encountered during the subsurface explora
tions Slab on grade construction can therefore be utilized with
out special provisions for expansive soils It is recommended that

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
borings and three percolation test holes utilizing truck mounted
equipment The depths of the borings varied from 9 0 to 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The locations of the borings
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan and Boring Locations Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in the
field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accord
ance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 8
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ora
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depth
