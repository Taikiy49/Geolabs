PERCOLATION TESTING
KUKUILONO GOLF COURSE CLUBHOUSE
Dear Mr Billingsley
In accordance with our fee proposal dated August 23 2001 we have conducted
percolation testing for design of a septic tank disposal system at the Kukuiolono Golf
Course Clubhouse in Koloa on the Island of Kauai Hawaii This letter report summarizes
our work performed on the project and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration field percolation testing and
engineering analyses The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The clubhouse for the Kukuiolono Golf Course is situated in an area that is not
served by a sanitary sewer system Therefore it is necessary to utilize on site treatment
and disposal for the management of wastewater generated in the clubhouse facility
Details of the proposed wastewater system were not available at the time this exploration
and testing was conducted however it has been assumed that the proposed system will
consist of a septic tank and shallow disposal component such as a leachfield
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings to depths ranging between approximately 5 and 10 feet below the existing
ground surface and conducting falling head percolation tests in the drilled borings The
original scope of our proposal called for two borings however due to the poor percolation
conditions encountered in the initial borings additional borings and percolation tests were
conducted to verify these conditions The approximate location of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface layer of very hard reddish orange to
reddish brown silty clay to the maximum depths explored Groundwater was not
encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally encountered very hard silty clay to the maximum
depths explored Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of
our field exploration
Falling head percolation tests in general accordance with the requirements of
Chapter Administrative Rules Wastewater Systems were conducted in
the drilled borings to provide data for design of the proposed leachfield planned at the
site The percolation tests were performed at depths of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface to evaluate the permeability characteristics of the subsoils at depths
typically associated with leachfield installation
These percolation tests indicated that the very hard silty clay soils underlying the
site have very poor infiltration characteristics and that use of a shallow subsurface
wastewater disposal system such as a leachfield is not practical at this site Other
methods for wastewater management such as retention tanks could be considered
It is our opinion based on previous experience in the Koloa area that it may be
possible to utilize a deeper disposal component such as a seepage pit or injection well
However additional exploration is required to evaluate this possibility It should also be
noted that since the facility will most likely generate more than 1 000 gallons per day of
wastewater a deeper disposal component such as described would be considered an
injection well under Chapter Administrative Rules Underground Injection
Control Therefore a permit from the Hawaii Department of Health Safe Drinking
Water Branch would be required to construct and operate the injection well s We
would be pleased to assist in evaluating the possibility of using a deep disposal
component and if feasible assist in preparing applications for the permits to construct
and operate the injection wells
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
