PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves improvements to the existing cesspool system
located at the DOT Harbor at Kalaeloa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
planned improvements generally involve the installation of new septic system components
consisting of a shallow depth absorption bed 2 000 gallon pre cast septic tank and
associated pipelines In addition we understand that the existing Cesspool will be re used
as a seepage pit
The project site is located at the existing Harbor Agent s office at Kalaeloa Harbor
The project site encompasses generally level ground located in a grassed area
Surrounding the existing facility An existing operational cesspool is located in the near
vicinity of the proposed septic system improvements The location of the proposed septic
system improvements and the existing cesspool structure is shown on the Site Plan

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed absorption bed site and to perform field percolation tests to
evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics for the formulation of preliminary
recommendations pertaining to the design of the disposal components of the on site
sewage disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated Our Scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment and
two operators to the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of three boreholes to depths ranging between about
2 and 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface
soil profile
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
field engineer geologist from our firm
4 Performance of three field percolation tests at selected locations to evaluate
the subsoil infiltration characteristics pertinent to the design of the disposal
components of the on site sewage disposal system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating
their engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project geologist from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm

CONDITIONS
Field Borings
A total of three borings designated as Boring Nos B 1 through B 3 were drilled at
the Kalaeloa Harbor project site The borings were drilled to depths of about 2 to 8 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths
Subsurface Conditions
In general the borings encountered a thin clayey topsoil layer overlying fill materials
composed of dense coralline silty sand with gravel extending to a depth of about 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Very stiff medium dense clayey and sandy alluvial soils
were encountered below about 5 feet and extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the borings
drilled at the project site however it is anticipated that the groundwater level may be
encountered at depths of about 9 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface It should

Field Percolation Testing
Three field percolation tests were performed at depths of about 2 3 and 8 5 feet
below the existing ground surface in Boring Nos B 1 B 2 and B 3 respectively The field
percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the provisions of the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the infiltration
characteristics of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation test
results are presented on the Site Evaluation Percolation Test Forms Plates B 1 through
B 3
The percolation tests were performed in the sandy subsoils by drilling 4 inch
diameter boreholes to depths ranging between 2 and 8 5 feet below the existing ground
surface During testing the holes were carefully filled with clean water to a minimum depth
of 12 inches above the bottom of the hole The time intervals and water level drops were
recorded to provide field data upon which calculation of the percolation tests were based
Based on the percolation test results obtained from depths of about 2 to 3 feet
below the existing ground surface it appears that the percolation rate of the near surface
compacted granular fill materials is relatively fair to good with a percolation rate of about
30 minutes per inch The test result obtained from the deeper percolation test performed
at about 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface indicates that the deeper percolation is
significantly greater at about 0 10 minute per inch Therefore it is our opinion that the
infiltration characteristic of the subsurface soils is suitable for the use of a shallow depth
absorption bed and seepage pit system Due to possible variability of the subsoil
conditions at the site the ultimate absorption capacity of the disposal system should be
confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the Classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of the moisture content and unit weight tests are presented on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths In addition three Sieve Analysis
tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples to evaluate the gradation
characteristics and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size
distribution is provided on Plate C 1

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussion submitted in this report are based upon information
obtained from limited field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussion provided in
this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the Site Plan provided by M E Pacific Inc on
The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented in the logs of borings of this report were
based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic
representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variation in tides rainfall temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of M E Pacific Inc and their
client the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Harbors Division for specific
application to the proposed septic system improvement project located at the Kalaeloa
Harbor Agents office in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This data report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer
in the planning and preliminary design of the Proposed project Therefore this report may
not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use in Preparation of final design
drawings construction cost estimates or other uses A contractor wishing to bid on this
project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation
of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration for detailed
design and bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen subsurface conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
