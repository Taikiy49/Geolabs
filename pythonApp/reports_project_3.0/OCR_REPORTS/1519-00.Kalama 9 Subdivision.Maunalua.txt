Soil Engineering Investigation
Proposed Kalama 9 Subdivision
Maunalua Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the site in order to provide preliminary data
for use in the design of the site grading work for the proposed lots and
access roadways The location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
The scope of our investigation included the excavation of eighteen
18 test pits visual observations of the existing site conditions
laboratory testing and engineering analysis
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of 36 single family residential
lots with the necessary access roads The site may have been partially
graded several years ago but apparently without engineering inspection
and compaction testing
The project site is 4 0 acres in size and bounded by Kealahou Street
on the west Mokuhana Street on the south Kalama Valley School and park
site on the north and a vacant lot on the east
Preliminary grading plans prepared by Imata Associates Inc of
the site indicate that the proposed site grading consists mainly of fills
varying from one foot up to 3 feet high
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located at the northeast corner of the intersection of
Kealahou and Mokuhana Streets in Kalama Valley
The ground surface at the site is generally level with a slight
gradient of about one per cent down towards the east
The area is presently vacant and mostly overgrown with grasses and
some bushes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 00 Page 3
An existing boulders soil stockpile is located along the central
portion of the site Another small soil stockpile is located near the
eastern boundary of the site
A very dense basalt outcrop is located at the extreme northeast
corner
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The
range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe and aa basalt
lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing mountain
trends During the Late Tertiary and Pleistocene Ice Age deep valleys
including Kalama Valley were eroded by streams
ater during the Pleistocene Period volcanics of the Koko fissure
named the Honolulu Volcanic Series erupted and deposited ash and cinders
from several vents trending in a southwest to northeast direction The
site lies a few hundred feet southwest of a cinder cone that spread a
lava flow down towards the ocean During higher stands of sea level in
the Pleistocene the valleys were infilled with sediments alluviated
grading to the high sea level stands Underlying these sediments are the
older volcanic tuff and cinder deposits Later man developed the area
and has removed the cinder cone and reworked portions of the site

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating 18
test pits to depths of 3 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Soil
samples obtained from the test pits were selectively tested in our
laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering characteris
tics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration the test pit logs
the laboratory testing and results are presented in the appendices
Surface samples were also obtained from the test pits for CBR pave
ment tests Laboratory one inch ring swell tests were performed on
selected samples
Soil Conditions
Based on the results of test pits and field observations the near
surface soils at the site can be grouped into three 3 major
catagories
1 Adobe clay stiff to moderately compacted
2 Sandy silty soils moderately compacted fills
3 Clayey silt fairly loose fills
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 00 Page 5
The Adobe clay soils are generally located in the northwestern
portion of the site Underlying the surface fills the dominant natural
soil consisted of dense silty sand and gravel cinders or a very hard
weathered tuff that resembles a hardpan layer
Approximately 3 to 4 feet of loose fill materials were encountered in
Test Pit 9 consisting of clayey silt with pieces of wood Moderately
compacted fills were encountered in the remaining test pits
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that about one half of the on site near
surface adobe clay soils are generally highly expansive while the sandy
and silty soils are low to moderately expansive For approximate
locations refer to Site Plan Plate 2
In general we believe that if the on site expansive soils are capped
with at least three 3 feet of imported low expansion potential borrow
fill material the potential for future swelling can be reduced
significantly
To minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs
driveways and sidewalks we recommend that the on site adobe soil be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 00 Page 6
capped with at least 36 inches of select import fill which will exhibit
low swelling potential when wetted and that the subgrade soils
underneath the above select fill also be moistened and protected from
excessive drying or over compaction due to heavy traffic from the
project construction until the select fill capping is completed Because
the lot sizes are small in relation to the structure we believe it may
be practical that the adobe excavation and backfilling should extend
over the entire lot areas
Site Grading
From the preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation it is contemplated that the grading will consist of minor
cutting generally along portions of Road A The rest of the site will
be filled to depths of about 1 to 3 feet with imported borrow materials
Test Pit 9 indicated a loose or soft layer about 3 to 4 feet deep
The soft material around this area should be removed and replaced with
compacted select materials
While the rest of the test pits indicated that the other areas are
generally competent they may have been placed without sufficient
engineering control to assure uniform density It is therefore
recommended that the proposed lots and roadway areas be proof rolled with
a heavy roller to delineate areas of uncompacted or unsuitable support
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1519 00 Page 7
The proof rolling should be monitored by a soils engineer who would
delineate the areas of unsuitable subgrade support These areas if
encountered should be undercut to remove the unsuitable materials to a
maximum depth of three feet and replaced with compacted select on site
or borrow fill as previously specified
Periodic in place density determinations should be made to assure
that the subgrade has been uniformly compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum density
In addition the existing piles of rock and soil fills should be
removed and the underlying ground stripped of organic or loose soil down
to firm soil
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill
should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 or as revised test method
Any other localized soft or loose fill area encountered during
clearing and grubbing operations should be excavated and backfilled
with compacted select material

Fill Material
The site will require imported fill to raise the site grade backfill
of trenches and over excavation of adobe clay soil within the upper
3 foot select fill capping
Imported fill especially when used as capping fill should in
general be of low expansion potential with maximm particle size not
exceeding 3 inches The CBR expansion value should be less than 1
percent and the CBR value should be 10 or greater We recommend
imported fill materials be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to being hauled to the site

The fill materials within the top 2 feet of the finished grade should
be placed in horizontal lifts of about 10 inches in compacted
thickness The moisture content of the on site fill should be kept
within 3 percentage points wet of the optimum during compaction
For the light residential construction we believe each fill layer
should be compacted to between 85 to 90 relative compaction
according to ASTM D 1557 78 or as revised maximum density The top 6
inches of the roadway subgrade should be increased to 95 relative
compaction

Inspection for Expansive Soils
We recommend that lots in the adobe areas with less than 3 foot of
fill be identified in the field and inspected by the soils engineer
during mass grading to see if highly expansive soil exist within
three feet of the finished rough grade Such inspection may also
include near surface soil sampling and performing laboratory swell
tests If soils are found to be expansive then they would have to
be over excavated Generally for preliminary cost estimate purpose
about 3 feet of capping or over excavation and backfill with low
expansion potential soil is recommended
Also lots within the area presently identified in the soil report
as low to moderately expansive soils should also be inspected after
site clearing We believe that eighteen 18 inches of new fill
capping or over excavation and backfill may be required at this time
Utility Trenches
A dense weathered tuffaceous or cinder formation was encountered at
about 2 to 4 feet in some of the test pits at the site If utility
trenches are required to extend through these materials moderate
excavation may be encountered
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize
subsequent ground surface settlement along the utility alignments

It is anticipated that the houses will be of slab on grade
construction
If select fill is used to cap the on site soils the perimeter
thickened edge wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 10
inches below the outside adjacent finished grade

2000 p s f can be used for spread or line footings bearing on

We recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine basalt gravel fill be

The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface water away from

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
each house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape and its watering near the
house foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition

to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility

recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should have low expansion potential
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the
site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
