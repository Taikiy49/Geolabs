This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during mass grading for the Hardware Hawaii project from

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the northwest corner of Kalaeloa Boulevard and Lauwiliwili
Street in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by Kalaeloa
Boulevard to the northwest Lauwiliwili Street to the southwest and U u Place to the
southeast A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
The project consists of the construction of a new building structure an asphalt
concrete parking area and Portland cement concrete and asphalt concrete storage areas
The mass grading work involved fills up to about 5 feet thick
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
mass grading operations
MASS GRADING
We observed the mass grading work from to The
mass grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of
fills up to about 5 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades C J Peterson Services
performed the mass grading work During the mass grading operations we obtained
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and
transported them to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum

moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the mass grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 7 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Clay soils were encountered at finished subgrade at three areas at the project site
One of the areas is near the entrance driveway from U u Place The second area is near
the southern portion of the asphalt concrete pavement area and the southeastern portion
of the Portland cement concrete pavement area The third area is at the eastern corner of
the future asphalt concrete pavement area The clay subgrade soils were over excavated
to about 12 inches below finished subgrade and replaced with compacted coralline fill
material
Additional grading was performed at the eastern end of the project In addition the
slope between the parking area and U u Place was regraded Geolabs was not provided
with the opportunity to observe the grading work in these areas Therefore this report
does not include the additional area at the eastern end of the project that was graded and
the regraded slope between the parking area and U u Place
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass grading work for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references

grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
