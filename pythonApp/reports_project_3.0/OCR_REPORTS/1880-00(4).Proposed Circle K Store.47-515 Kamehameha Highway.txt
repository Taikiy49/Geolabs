INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the design of site grading slah on grade and foundation
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at
this site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 76 foot
store a pump island with three gasoline dispenser units To supply the
gas island an underground gasoline storage area consisting of three
10 000 gallon tanks is planned for a 28 by 32 foot area adjacent to the
Circle K store The convenience facility will be surrounded by an
asphaltic concrete parking lot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of Kamehameha
Highway across from the Hygienic Store in the town of Kahaluu Oahu
Hawaii
Currently the site is in use as a Texaco Service Station and is
covered with A C pavement a small 1 story building and two 2 concrete
gas pump islands
The site measures approximately 270 by 130 feet in plan dimension
and is generally flat with ground elevations varying between 5 7 and 7 8
feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Two 2 borings were drilled at the project site to depths of 16 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface fill layer consisting
of dense brown silty sand with gravel underlain by stiff brown clayey
silt to mottled reddish brown silt with sand and gravel approximately
3 5 to 8 0 feet thick
Below this the drilling encountered a stiff brown to dark gray
black organic clayey silt to a depth of 7 5 to 13 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
Underlying the organic clayey silt was a stiff tannish brown sandy
silt to a depth of 16 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater levels at the time of our exploration were encountered
from 4 7 to 5 1 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the existing A C pavement areas within
the building and new grading parking limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed Any unsuitable materials such as vegetation and rubbish
should be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavation material

It is our opinion that the present poor condition of the existing
asphaltic concrete pavement requires that it be broken and disposed of
properly off site or if feasible after broken up to 2 inch minus
sizes reused by mixing with the sandy soil to become part of the new
fill materials
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive slab on grade
footing and new pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches below the bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the
footing or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
select on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
The site should be graded to provide drainage for surface run off
away from all foundations
Backfill
It is anticipated that it may be necessary to remove the existing
underground storage tanks and other buried utilities The resulting
excavations should be backfilled up to two feet below finish subgrade
with non expansive select borrow material placed in 6 inch thick
compacted level lifts to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 From two feet below finish subgrade to finish subgrade the
excavation should be backfilled with non expansive select borrow placed
as recommended above and compacted to 95 maximm dry density
If groundwater is encountered in the excavations the excavation
should be backfilled with 3 Fine rock to a level at least 2 feet above
the groundwater surface This layer of 3 Fine rock should then be
capped with an 8 inch lift of base course compacted to 90 maximum dry
density Backfilling operations with select on site soils may then
proceed as recommended above

Store Building and Pump Island
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered a competent upper
fill crust which will provide adequate support for the proposed lightly
loaded Circle K store building and pump island Shallow spread or
continuous wall footings bearing directly on the recompacted existing
silty sand and gravel fill may be used
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimm
embedment of 18 inches An allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f may
be used The bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be considered under
the slabs for moisture sensitive flooring material
The bottom of all thickened edge excavations should be kept moist
until the concrete is poured The backfill against the poured concrete
slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from
the slab perimeter
It is planned to place three approximately 10 000 gallon tanks
underground in a 28 by 32 foot area adjacent to the Circle K Store
There is presently a 3 5 to 8 foot fill crust and the tank
diameters as indicated on the Site Plan are 8 feet The tanks will
therefore bear on the underlying stiff organic clayey silt at least
several feet below the groundwater level Because of the high ground

Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the preliminary pavement
design is as follow
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course Basalt Aggregate
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section should be verified in
the field
Utilities
Existing utilities and previously buried cesspools should be located
and verified in the field It may be necessary to move an existing line
or adjust structural details to accommodate the utility Should an
unanticipated condition or buried structure be encountered during
construction each condition should be evaluated by the soils engineer
and the design modified if necessary
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations and pavement design should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that all subgrade preparation footing excavations and
excavation backfill be observed by the soils engineer to check that
adequate bearing and compaction have been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of weak organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of the foundation design of this project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
se SH NE En
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
