INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed road development project from the proposed Kukuiula
Wastewater Treatment Plant to Koloa Road west of the town of Koloa on the island of
Kauai Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Koloa District on the island of Kauai Hawaii It
is proposed to construct a bypass road from the proposed Kukuiula Wastewater
_ Treatment Plant to Koloa Road

The proposed bypass alignment generally runs in a south to north direction
beginning from the proposed Kukuiula Wastewater Treatment Plant Road D Station
38 00 and terminating at Koloa Road approximately 200 feet east of the intersection of
Omao Road and Koloa Road Road D Station 84 13
The proposed bypass road will consist of approximately 4600 feet of road with a
paved travelled way of two 2 lanes totaling 24 feet in width with ten 10 foot unpaved
shoulders on each side
It is our understanding that an Asphalt Treated Base ATB pavement section will
be initially utilized and will be overlaid in the future with a two and a half 2 5 inch
asphaltic concrete surface layer
Based on the grading plans provided to us cuts up to 15 feet and fills up to 28
feet in vertical dimension are anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Kauai was built up by a single large shield volcano During the
Tertiary period basaltic lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series built a roughly
circular island to a height over 17 000 feet above the surrounding sea floor Near the end
of the Tertiary Period this main shield building voicanic phase came to a halt
Following a long period of erosion and subsidence vulcanism was renewed during
the Pleistocene epoch with the Koloa Volcanic Series This post erosional volcanic activity
laid a veneer of fresh lava over the older Waimea Canyon Series across most of the
western half of the island
After the cessation of this period of renewed vulcanism the geologic forces of
weathering erosion and deposition during the late Pleistocene and the Recent epochs
have shaped the island to its present form

Site Preparation
Prior to initiation of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of six
6 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over
excavated down to the firm subsoils and replaced with compacted non expansive
select material
Fill Placement
The on site clayey silt and silty clay soils are suitable for use in the compacted fill

basalt formation The rock material if reduced to iess than eight 8 inches in

Large boulders or rock fragments greater than eight 8 inches in maximum size
may be used in the deeper fills provided that they are not nested and are placed
at depths greater than four 4 feet below finish subgrade level Care must be
exercised to avoid placement of any boulders in locations which would conflict with

an eight 8 feet bench should be provided at the middle of slope

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampied
Ciassification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of the soils The Plasticity Index of the material is
used to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these
tests are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs and are
summarized on Plate B 1
Physicai Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of the soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining
to the performance or suitability of a soil
Proctor ASTM D 1557 78 tests and California Bearing Ratio ASTM D 1883 tests
were performed on selected samples as an aid to evaluating their embankment fill and
pavement support characteristics The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 1
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B 2
