This report presents our findings of a geologic reconnaissance
and our recommendations for the construction of the proposed
residence located at 1214 Ala Awapuhi Place just off Likini Street
in the Salt Lake Subdivision
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed residential constructton will be supported at two
ground levels of elevation 127 and 150 feet Site grading for
this building will involve terracing into the existing mudrock
ridge slope for a vertical cut height of about 23 feet at the
lower pad and another 20 feet for the upper building area Some
minor site grading will also occur bycutting a maximum of 4 feet
of top surface material from the top of the ridge

SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located on the slope face of a mudrock ridge in
Salt Lake which extended upward from the cul de sac for a height
of 52 feet The site slope ratio is about 1 1 one horizontal
to one vertical for the eastern two thirds of the parcel tapering
off to a 2 1 ratio along its southern boundary
The west property line its located on the top of the ridge where
the ground ts relatively level Beyond this upper boundary the
ridge begins to slope toward west at 1 1 slope ratio
The ridge is sparsely vegetated Recently test pit cut into this
slope exposed laminated layers of mudrock or tuff formation Gene
rally the surface has more weathering see attached photograph A
On the west side of this same ridge vertical cut slope into the

DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the site geologic reconnaissance it is our opinion that
the site is suitable for the proposed grading construction Ver
tical slope cut less than 25 feet can be constructed in the mudrock
formation provided the cut slope surface is designed to minimize
erosion and weathering Slab on grade and spread footing founda
tion located on the cut bank should have a minimum 8 foot set back
away from the edge of the vertical cut slope No imposed load
should be allowed within this set back area
Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundation can bear on the rock formation
with a bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per square foot
Within the 8 foot wide slope cut set back area located between
the lower and upper construction pads the building should be
designed structurally to bridge over this set back area This
means that the support of the upper floor s front area should
be on posts or bearing wall of the rear portion of the lower
structure
Slab On Grade
Expansive soil condition is not expected from the tuff formation
Therefore no special construction is required
To provide a uniform slab support on cut a 4 inch layer of base
course gravel material ts recommended A moisture barrier is also
recommended to be placed beneath the concrete slab
Earthwork
Cut slope into the site mudrock formation can be constructed ver
tically up to a maximum height of 25 feet To minimize erosion
and weathering problems over the exposed slope retaining wall
or concrete gunite should be constructed immediately after the
excavation

Retaining Wall

The backfi11 immedtately against the wall to the slope cut sur

Adequate drainage system should be provided to prevent the build
