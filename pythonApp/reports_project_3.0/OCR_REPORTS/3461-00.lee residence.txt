Discussions and Recommendations
Based on our field observations all of the footings are not embedded into the
underlying firm soil and or rock formation In general the majority of the footings are
embedded approximately 6 to 12 inches below the existing sloping ground with
loose soft soils in front of the post and beam footings As a result it is our opinion that
the existing foundation system may have a potential for lateral movement due to the
lack of adequate footing embedment and lack of passive resistance in front of the
footings This condition should be evaluated by the structural engineer Due to
budgetary constraints we understand that it may not be feasible to extend all of the
existing footings deeper to achieve the required embedment depth for lateral restraint

structure will need to be evaluated by the contractor
