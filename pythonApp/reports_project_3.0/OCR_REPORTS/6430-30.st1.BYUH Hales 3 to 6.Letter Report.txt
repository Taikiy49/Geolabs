This letter report summarizes our findings based on observations from a site visit
and presents our recommendations for the Student Housing Phase 1b and 1c Hales 3
thru 6 project within the Hawaii Campus of Brigham Young University The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
Project Considerations
The project site is within the existing Hawaii Campus of Brigham Young University
in the Laie area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Renovations and additions are planned
for four existing two story dormitory buildings designated as Hales 3 through 6 We
understand that the current plan is to remove the existing concrete roof and construct a
third floor for Hales 3 through 5 New roof consisting of lighter materials will be utilized to
maintain the overall weight of the building Due to flood elevation issues we understand
that Hale 6 will need to be demolished and reconstructed at a slightly higher ground floor
elevation
Borings were previously drilled near Hales 3 through 6 for a separate housing
building project It was desired to utilize the existing data to confirm that the existing
footings may be reused for the renovated buildings
Existing Conditions
A site visit was made on March 12 2014 to evaluate the condition of the existing
Hales 3 through 6 Hales 3 through 6 are two story concrete buildings constructed in the
1950s Each building has an open interior courtyard space The buildings were evaluated
around the outsides of the structures and within the courtyard areas

In general the buildings appear to be in a good condition Signs of ground
settlement were not observed at the time of our site visit Some spalling and cracking of
the concrete were observed in isolated locations However we do not believe that these
cracks are a result of ground settlement
Several existing footings were partially excavated at the time of our site visit The
exposed footings generally appeared to be intact and in a good condition The soil
surrounding the footings generally consisted of stiff to very stiff silty clays with some
gravel Pocket penetrometer tests indicated that the subgrade soils have unconfined
compressive strength of about 1 to 2 5 tons per square foot
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the input from the structural engineer the existing buildings generally
have structural loads resulting in bearing pressures of up to about 2 750 pounds per
square foot psf Removal of the concrete roof and addition of the third floor would
generally increase the bearing pressures to up to about
Based on the generally good condition of the existing buildings we believe that the
removal of the concrete roof and addition of the third floor would not result in a significant
ground settlement of the existing buildings We estimate the total settlements of the
foundations to be on the order of less than 0 5 inches Differential settlements between
adjacent footings may be on the order of about 0 25 inches or less We believe a
significant portion of the settlement would be elastic and should occur as the loads are
applied
As previously mentioned some cracking and spalling of the concrete was observed
at the existing buildings The structural engineer should be consulted to formulate
remediation measures in these areas
Limitations
The recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in part upon
information obtained from our site visit literature research and past experience in the
vicinity of the project site only Variations of the subsurface conditions may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations presented in this letter report
