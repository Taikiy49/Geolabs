Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed in support of the Repairs to O Club Pool Building 537 Marine Corps Base
D 0006 project within the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH at
Kaneohe Bay on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the design and repair or
replacement of foundations and slabs only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2
Project Considerations
Based on the information provided the proposed project involves the
replacement of a portion of the existing pool deck at the O Club Pool Building 537
within the MCBH at Kaneohe Bay on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
presently a military recreational facility situated on the northeast slope of Puu Hawaiiloa
and is at the top of a bluff overlooking the Kaneohe Klipper Golf Course
At present the existing pool deck has cracked and a low retaining fence wall
along the westerly boundary of the pool deck area has tilted outward at about 4 degrees
from vertical It is envisioned that the portion of the pool deck slab closest to the pool
will be replaced and that the foundations of the wall may be underpinned and restored
to vertical
1 3

accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau Shield Volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae Volcano
became extinct while Koolau Volcano was still active and its eastern flank was partially
buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank The Koolau Volcano
reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The project site is on the northeast flank of Koolau which has eroded away
After the main shield building and a long period of erosion volcanic activity was
renewed on the Koolau side with the extrusion of lavas of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
These rocks are generally characterized by tuff and cinder cones in the southeastern
part of the Koolau dome The site is situated on the northeast slope of Puu Hawaiiloa
which is a cinder cone of the Honolulu Series At the surface in the vicinity of the site
the cinder and ash forming the cone has chemically weathered into a clayey silt residual
soil with occasional exposures of boulders and native rock

Site Description
The project site is generally in the north central portion of the Marine Corps Base
Hawaii at Kaneohe Bay on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is approximately bounded by
the Bachelor Officers Quarters to the northwest the upper slopes of Puu Hawaiiloa to
the south and southwest the Kaneohe Klipper Golf Course and tennis courts to the
east and the lower slope of Puu Hawaiiloa to the northeast
The pool deck is generally rectangular in plan view and is to the east of the main
Officers Club building The pool deck is nearly level Based on the topographic plan
provided the existing ground surface elevation is about 181 4 feet with respect to a
nearby bench mark assumed to reference the US Navy datum of MLLW minus 100 feet
The existing pool deck consists of a concrete slab with a skim coat to give the surface

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the borings drilled for this exploration under the existing pool deck we
encountered about 4 to 8 feet of silty clay fill overlying about 11 feet of rock fill material
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our exploration
Our laboratory tests indicate the near surface soils at the project site exhibit a
moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture
content Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and our laboratory testing
we recommend after demolition of the existing slab over excavating the existing clayey

compacted select granular fill material
As previously discussed the existing fence wall and columns on the eastern side
of the pool area have experienced some distress i e they have tilted out away from the
pool deck at about 4 degrees from vertical It is believed that this distress is the result
of settlement due to a differential in the moisture content between the moderately
expansive soils under the pool deck and those outside the wall The soil under the slab
is protected from drying by the slab and the moisture will increase over time with
infiltration through the slab resulting in some degree of expansion The soil outside the
wall loses moisture due to evaporation and is subject to shrinking We recommend
installing support piers to underpin and re level the fence columns and walls

Pool Deck Replacement
Our laboratory tests indicate the near surface soils at the project site exhibit a
moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture content
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and our laboratory testing we
recommend after demolition of the existing slab over excavating the existing clayey silt
soils a minimum of 12 inches below finish subgrade level and replacing with compacted
select granular fill material Select granular fill material should consist of crushed coralline
or basaltic materials The materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles
no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The materials should have a laboratory

Site Preparation
Based on the existing surface conditions at the project site the existing pool deck
within the contract grading limits should be demolished and removed at the on set
of earthwork
After demolition of the existing slab existing subgrade soils should be excavated to
a depth of 12 inches below finished subgrade The bottom of the excavation
should be scarified to a depth of about 6 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where yielding areas or soft and or loose areas are disclosed during the
scarification process the soft and or loose materials should be over excavated to
expose firm and or dense ground conditions and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted fill materials Therefore contract documents
should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Fills and Backfills
In general the excavated on site soils should be disposed of properly at an off site
location Imported fill materials should consist of select granular fill materials such
as crushed coralline or basaltic materials The materials should be well graded from
coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
materials should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Proposed imported fill materials should be tested for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
The select fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Select granular
fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density Compaction of fill materials should be accomplished using vibratory rollers
or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site generally is underlain by clayey soils
below the existing pool deck slab It is anticipated that the near surface materials
may be excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as excavating
with a backhoe excavator However it should be noted that rock fill material was
encountered below the near surface clayey soil at relatively shallow depths in the
borings drilled at the project site Therefore it should be noted that the contractors
may encounter some localized hard excavation conditions requiring the use of
hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the excavation of the materials are based on our
visual observation of the existing site conditions and field data from the borings
drilled at the project site Contractors proposing to bid on this project should be
encouraged to examine the site conditions and the boring data to make their own
interpretation

the moderately expansive soils under the pool deck and those outside the wall The soil
under the slab is protected from drying by the slab and the moisture will increase over

excavating a working pit and then attaching a bracket to the existing foundation A

mechanically pinned in place to support the wall or foundation After jacking of the
foundation we recommend backfilling the working pit with Controlled Low Strength
Material CLSM to ensure that the foundation is properly backfilled The CLSM will
also help to stabilize the moisture content adjacent to the wall and columns

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of
approximately 3 6 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
both a track mounted drill rig and portable drilling equipment equipped with continuous
flight augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed
visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D 2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths The penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the
number of blows required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need
to be factored to obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Pocket penetrometer test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths and
are presented graphically on Plate B 1
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on
Plate B 2
Eight one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are
summarized on Plate B 3
