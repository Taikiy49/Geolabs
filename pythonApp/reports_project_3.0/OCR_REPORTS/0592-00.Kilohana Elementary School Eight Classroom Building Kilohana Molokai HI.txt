This report presents the results of a soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed project The study consisted
of subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses as outlined in our proposal dated
Based on the results of our study conclusions and recommen
dations pertinent to site development are presented

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration for the proposed classroom buildings
consisted of eight 8 borings ranging in depth from 20 0 to
40 0 feet
The drilling was accomplished using truck mounted rotary dril
ling equipment with continuous flight solid stem auger The
borings were continuously inspected by a geologist from our firm
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Continuous core sampling was performed for the full depth of
and 7 In boring 1 and 8 Standard
Penetration Test was taken at selected depth intervals The
Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a 2 inch 0O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of
the relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material
was placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory
for inspection and testing

Geology
Molokai is the eroded remnant of 2 volcanoes the older one of
which joins the western part of the island and the younger the
eastern part East Molokai where the site is located is the
a coco

remnant of a large volcano that initially produced basaltic
lava from a caldera and associated rift zones The initial
phase was followed by outpourings of andesitic lavas Erosion
and sedimatation accompanied and followed the periods of volcanic
activity The site area is affected by this old alluvium which
is the product of variations in ocean level changes and was
deposited early in the Pleistocene The alluvium may be several
hundred feet thick and it extends well below sea level at the
valley mouths The onsite soil is generally known as Colluviol
Soil which is formed from material that has been moved downhill
by gravity soil creep or local wash It accumulates on the
lower slopes and at the base of the slopes
Soils
Ciderly Matrix Cobbles and Boulders
Very hard dark gray ciderly matrix cobbles and boulders was
encountered in all borings The material were found from ground
surface to the full depth of all the borings
Groundwater
No standing water was noted in the holes while drilling
ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented

General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory
program were relatively good from a soils and foundation
engineering viewpoint
It is our professional Opinion that the onsite soils in
general are capable of supporting the proposed classroom
building structures and future additions at the school site
Grading
Site grading should be minimal however as much as 4 feet
of cut are necessary on the north of the building site
1 Clearing and Grubbing
All unsuitable material such as vegetation top
soil pipes trash fill etc should be removed
and wasted from the site
2 Preparation of Fill Areas
Areas close to finish grade or to receive fill
should be scarified watered if necessary and
recompacted according to the requirements for
structural fills
Local soft spots should be removed and replaced
with compacted structural fill

Fill Material
The onsite cinderly matrix cobbles and boulders
appear to be suitable for compacted fill However
the fill material shall not contain any particle
size larger than four 4 inches
Any imported fill material should be non expansive
and must be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to placing
Structural Fill Placement
Approved fill material should be placed in 8 inch
lifts and compacted to 95 relative compaction using
ASTM D 1557 70 as a standard
Finish Grade
To provide slab support in cut and natural grade
portions the upper one foot of soils beneath the
finish subgrade over the entire building pad up
to 2 feet from outer edges of exterior footings
should be removed and recompacted to a minimum of
90 of the maximum dry density
Drainage
Provisions for site drainage both during and
after completion of the construction must be
considered Water should not be allowed to pond
in the vicinity of the structures or in footing
excavations

Foundations
Spread and or continuous footings may be utilized for the
proposed structure Footings for individual buildings should
be all on stiff in situ soil or all on compacted structural

adjacent ground surface and should have a minimum width
of 12 inches
Total settlements for the structure should be less than
1 2 inch with differential settlement less than 1 4 inch
Concrete Floor Slab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should be
scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density A moisture barrier consisting of plastic
membrane should be provided under the slab areas which will
receive covering such as Carpeting or tiles The concrete

LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site However due to the boulderly material a
limited test program was performed The tests where applicable
to the subject project are included in this Appendix
Classification Tests
a

Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field
classification prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
Water Content Determination In addition to visual
classification typical sample was tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content value
is based upon the dry weight of the soil
