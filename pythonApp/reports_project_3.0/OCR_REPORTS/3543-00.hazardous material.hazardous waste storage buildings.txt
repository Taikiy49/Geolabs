Preliminary Geotechnical Recommendations
Proposed Hazardous Material Hazardous Waste Storage Buildings
Marine Corps Base Hawaii
Kaneohe Bay Oahu Hawaii
This letter serves to present our preliminary geotechnical recommendations for
the design of foundations and site preparation in order to facilitate the on going FACD
process for the proposed Hazardous Material Hazardous Waste Storage Buildings
project to be located within the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH in Kaneohe Bay on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The preliminary recommendations presented herein are
based on our knowledge and past experience in the vicinity of the project site and are
subject to modifications upon completion of our proposed geotechnical engineering
exploration program
General Geologic Conditions
Based on previous subsurface information and other reconnaissance performed
in the vicinity of the project site we anticipate the subsurface conditions at the site will
likely consist of residual soils of clayey silts and silty clays with sands and gravels of
very stifffmedium dense consistency These deposits may grade to hard saprolitic
materials of silty sandy soils or coral formation at greater depths The site lies near a
transition zone between the alluvial residual soils of the flank of Puu Hawaiiloa and
coral formation commonly found in the lowland areas and airfield location on the base
Substantial existing man made fills are not anticipated at the site
The near surface soils in the area appear to be dry and grades to moist with
increasing depth Based on our previous laboratory tests performed for the near
surface soils in the area the silty clay and clayey silt soils have moderate to high

Shallow Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we believe
that a shallow foundation system may be used to support the proposed one story
structures Allowable bearing pressures on the order of 2 500 to 3 000 pounds per
square foot p s f may be used for preliminary design of isolated and continuous
footings bearing on the stiff residual soils and or on compacted engineered fill needed
to raise the grades The allowable bearing pressures are for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third for transient loads such as wind and or seismic
forces
Wall footings should have a minimum width of at least 18 inches Footings
should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For
footings constructed on sloping ground the footings should be embedded deep enough
to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from the edge
of the footing to the slope face Where footings will be located adjacent to below grade
structures or utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary
45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or
utility line trench This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below
grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
foundation settlement
For footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations they should be
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying
competent material For footings bearing on the new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff clayey soils and or compacted fills as
recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements of about
inch
In general lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by a
combination of frictional resistance between the base of the footing and the bearing
materials and by passive earth pressure developed against the near vertical faces of

Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade for the proposed project may consist of concrete floor slabs and
exterior flatwork To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling
of the on site expansive soils we recommend that a minimum of 18 inches of non
expansive capping fill material be provided below slabs on grade The non expansive
capping fill should extend at least 2 feet beyond the limits of the proposed structures
For exterior flatwork such as sidewalks the non expansive capping fill may be reduced
to a minimum of 12 inches
The non expansive fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential
drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the distress on the slab due to
potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
In general the non expansive capping fill should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
The non expansive fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and compaction of the underlying subgrade soils prepared in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this letter Soil
subgrades should be kept moist prior to the placement of non expansive capping fill
and or concrete If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrades the soil should be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to capping fill or
concrete placement Alternatively the subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
For more uniform support slabs on grade should be supported on a cushion
layer consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The
gravel cushion layer should be placed above the non expansive capping fill and would

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential
contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
engineered fill moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement
of the fill material the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction we recommend the soil be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over
excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
In general the on site clayey materials may be used as fill materials provided
that they are placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Due to the expansive potential of the
clayey materials the clayey materials should not be used as backfill for retaining

Pavements
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavements may be required for the
project development We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles with occasional delivery trucks Our preliminary
pavement design assumes that the pavement subgrade soil will consist of the silty clay
residual soils anticipated at the project site Our previous experience with the silty clay
soils suggests that the materials have relatively low CBR values with high
swelling shrinkage potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Based on the
above assumptions the following pavement section may be used for preliminary design
purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
18 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 percent relative compaction
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The clayey subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to 90 percent relative compaction The base course and select borrow should consist
of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended preliminary
design sections The recommended pavement sections assume that good drainage will
be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to
carry all surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed
anywhere on the site during or after construction It is important that provisions be
included in the design to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement
section For long term performance a subdrain system should be constructed adjacent
to paved landscape areas

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations constructed on expansive soils since ponded water could cause subsurface
soil saturation and subsequent loss of strength or heaving of the near surface soils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
