PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand it is planned to demolish and remove the existing
timber fender pile system and replace it with a concrete fender pile system

prestressed concrete piles and chocks subjected to lateral berthing loads All elevations
referenced in this report are based on the U S Navy Datum where Elevation 100 feet is

driving The estimated pile tip elevations are presented in the following table

Pile Details
The specified fender piles will consist of 24 inch square precast prestressed
concrete piles The estimated length of Test Pile No 1 is about 108 75 feet including the
additional 15 foot length
We understand the piles will be cast from concrete with a minimum 28 day
unconfined compressive strength of 6 000 pounds per square inch psi for the 24 inch
square concrete fender piles The design prestress of the 24 inch square pile is
approximately 1 145 psi as shown on Construction Drawing Sheet No S 512 Therefore
the maximum allowable stress compression in the 24 inch square precast prestressed
concrete pile during pile driving is about 2 455 psi while the maximum allowable stresses
tension is approximately 1 145 psi

Driving Stresses
A graph showing the computed maximum compression and tensile stresses during
driving is provided on Plate 2 for Test Pile No 1 Please note that the graph was prepared
based on kips per square inch ksi which is equivalent to 1 000 psi
Based on our analyses the compression stresses developed during pile driving for
Test Pile No 1 did not exceed the allowable compressive stresses of 2 455 psi for the full
range of driving resistance at Pump Settings 1 and 2 However the compressive stresses
exceed the allowable compressive stress at Pump Settings 3 and 4 while the driving
resistances are greater than 175 and 100 blows per foot respectively
The tensile stresses developed during the pile driving of Test Pile No 1 are less
than the allowable tensile stress of 1 145 psi for the 24 inch square concrete piles for the
full range of driving resistance at Pump Settings 1 through 4
Based on the above results and the anticipated subsurface conditions it is our
professional opinion that the proposed pile driving equipment is generally able to install the
fender piles to the specified tip elevations without over stressing the concrete fender piles
We understand that the following pile driving refusal criteria were specified in the contract
documents in the event that a hard or thin cemented layer is encountered
12 blows per inch or
10 blows per inch for 6 consecutive inches or
8 blows per inch for 12 consecutive inches
To reduce the risk of over stressing during hard pile driving conditions we
recommend using the above refusal criteria only at Pump Settings 1 through 3 When
Pump Setting 4 is engaged we recommend revising the pile driving refusal criteria to 8
blows per inch or 6 blows per inch for 6 consecutive inches

ABOUT THE WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS RESULTS
The GRLWEAP program simulates the behavior of a preformed pile driven by
either an impact hammer or a vibratory hammer The program is based on
mathematical models which describe motion and forces of hammer driving
system pile and soil under the hammer action Under certain conditions
the models only crudely approximate often complex dynamic situations
A wave equation analysis generally relies on input data which represents
normal situations In particular the hammer data file supplied with the
program assumes that the hammer is in good working order All of the input
data selected by the user may be the best available information at the time
when the analysis is performed However input data and therefore results
may significantly differ from actual field conditions
Therefore the program authors recommend prudent use of the GRLWEAP results
Soil response and hammer performance should be verified by static and or
dynamic testing and measurements Estimates of bending or other local
non axial stresses and prestress effects must also be accounted for by the
user
The calculated capacity blow count relationship i e the bearing graph
should be used in conjunction with observed blow counts for the capacity
assessment of a driven pile Soil setup occurring after pile installation
may produce bearing capacity values that differ substantially from those
expected from a wave equation analysis due to soil setup or relaxation This
is particularly true for pile driven with vibratory hammers The GRLWEAP user
must estimate such effects and should also use proper care when applying blow
counts from restrike because of the variability of hammer energy soil
resistance and blow count during early restriking
Finally the GRLWEAP capacities are ultimate values They MUST be reduced by
means of an appropriate factor of safety to yield a design or working load
The selection of a factor of safety should consider the quality of the
construction control the variability of the site conditions uncertainties
in the loads the importance of building and other factors
