PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located west of the USDA Pacific Basin Agricultural Research
Center site near Komohana Street in the vicinity of the University of Hawaii at Hilo
campus on the Island of Hawaii Based on the information provided the proposed
facility will consist of a main building electrical building mechanical building and other
improvements We understand that the buildings will be one story structures consisting
of aluminum wood and concrete masonry units CMU The planned finished floor
elevations range between 358 5 and 361 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
DRILLING OF PROBEHOLES
Probing of the underlying ground surface was performed by Isemoto
Contracting Inc using an air track drill at the center of columns and grade beams on
August 29 30 and 31 2005 The probeholes were drilled to depths of approximately
10 to 16 feet below the bottom of footing elevation The location of the probehoiles are
presented on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 1
A representative from Geolabs Inc was present during the probing operation to
observe the rate of penetration of the drill rod to monitor the consistency of the underlying
ground and observe the possible presence of cavities

No 1 Main Building
A total of 115 probeholes were drilled at the center of the column footings and
grade beams Willocks Corporation an earthwork contractor performed the site grading
work under subcontract with Tai Sei Construction Corporation the main contractor At
the time of the probing work the site was graded approximately to the proposed bottom
of footings The probeholes were drilled to depths of approximately 10 feet below the
existing ground surface The column footing locations were staked on site by Tai Sei
Construction Corporation Immediately after probing 2 inch diameter PVC pipe casings
were inserted into the probehole to protect the annulus of the probehole wail prior to
grouting work Materials encountered were hard basalt rock with occasional pockets of
clay silt seams and clinkers No major cavities were observed or encountered during the
probing work
No 2 Mechanical Building
A total of 18 probeholes were drilled at the center of the exterior column footings
and grade beams Willocks Corporation performed the site grading work under
subcontract with Tai Sei Construction Corporation At the time of the probing work the
site was graded to an elevation approximately 4 feet higher than the proposed bottom of
footings The probeholes were drilled to depths of approximately 14 feet below the
existing ground surface immediately after probing 2 inch diameter PVC pipe casings
were inserted into the probehole to protect the annulus of the probehole wall prior to
grouting work Materials encountered were hard basalt rock with occasional pockets of
clay silt seams No major cavities were observed or encountered during the probing work
No 3 Electrical Building
A total of 17 probeholes were drilled at the center of the exterior column footings
and at the middle of a proposed grade beam Willocks Corporation performed the site
grading work under subcontract with Tai Sei Construction Corporation At the time of
the probing work the site was graded to an elevation approximately 4 feet higher than
the proposed bottom of the footings The eastern side the site was cut to an elevation
6 feet higher than the proposed bottom of footings The probeholes were drilled to
depths of approximately 14 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface The column
footing locations were staked on site by Tai Sei Construction Corporation Immediately
after probing 2 inch diameter PVC pipe casings were inserted into the probehole to
protect the annulus of the probehole wall prior to grouting work Materials encountered
were hard basalt rock with occasional pockets of clay silt seams and clinkers No major
cavities were observed or encountered during the probing work

GROUTING
The probeholes were filled with sand cement grout on by
Isemoto Contracting using a Mayco Pump Sand cement grout from Concrete Aggregate
of Hawaii was used to fill up the probeholes Grout quantities were estimated based on
the number of strokes to fill each probehole The volume of grout per stroke was
compared to the number of strokes to fill up a 5 gallon bucket A total of 11 5 cubic yards
of flowable sand cement grout was used to backfill the 150 probeholes No significant
voids or cavities were observed during the grouting work except at a few probeholes
At the Main Building grout intake at 80 and 96 were 1 018
0 2583 and 0 187 cubic yards respectively The grout intake for these probeholes were
significantly greater than the theoretical volume of 0 0355 cubic yards to fill each
probehole At the Mechanical Building grout intake at Probehole Nos M 1 and M 12 were
0 477 and 0 541 cubic yards respectively The grout intake for these probeholes were
greater than 0 0496 cubic yards the theoretical volume of the probeholes in this area At
the Electrical Building grout intake at Probehole No E 9 was 0 234 cubic yards which
was greater than 0 0496 cubic yards the theoretical volume of the probehole
At locations where grout intakes exceeded the theoretical volume no additional
probing was performed since the air track drill was demobilized prior to grouting work
Excavation within the immediate vicinity using a backhoe or equivalent or proof rolling with
a 20 ton roller should be performed prior to subgrade preparation to observe the extent of
potential presence of voids or cavities
A representative from Geolabs Inc was present during the grouting operation to
