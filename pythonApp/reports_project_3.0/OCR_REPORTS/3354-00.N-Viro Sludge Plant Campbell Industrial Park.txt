INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed N VIRO Sludge Processing Plant project within the
Campbell Industrial Park in the Ewa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located adjacent to the southern
flank of the older volcano Waianae After cessation of volcanic activity the island
began to subside The flanks of the subsiding domes formed a salient for the growth
of fringing reefs built by corals and calcareous algae
During the Pleistocene Epoch or Ice Age global sea levels fluctuated in
response to the advance and retreat of the continental glaciers As the glaciers grew
and advanced less water was available to fill the ocean basins so the sea level fell to
stands below the current level As the glaciers melted and retreated the sea rose to
stands often exceeding the current level
During the high stands of the sea sediments and marine deposits were laid
down at elevations above the current sea level The coral algal reef limestones
encountered at the project site were believed to have been deposited during the Kaena

during the Waimanalo stand 25 feet MSL

Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the design finish floor elevation of the
proposed facility we anticipate that the proposed site grading work will consist of
approximately 1 to 2 feet of fill placement over the existing ground surface Site
grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is
important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to evaluate
whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our exploration
Due to the significant number of near surface cavities observed during our field
exploration we recommend that a detailed site reconnaissance be performed at the on
set of earthwork in order to locate these potentially collapsible cavities These cavities
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3354 00 Page 9
should be ripped open and cleaned of all organic and deleterious matters and loose
sediments and replaced with compacted engineered fill
All areas within the contract grading limits should then be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the existing ground should be scarified to a depth
of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to about optimum moisture content and proof
rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory smooth drum roller with a minimum of six
passes prior to the placement of fill materials to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities Any loose areas or cavities disclosed during the above scarification and proof
rolling operations should be backfilled with compacted engineered fill
Due to the uncertainty in the number and dimensions of cavities present at the
site we recommend that contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices
for the fill construction to account for variations in the backfill quantities
We believe that the procedures recommended above will help to reduce the
inherent risk associated with the potential collapse of a cavity It should be noted that
the recommendations provided are to reduce the potential for collapse of a cavity
Undetected cavities may still be present at the site and could collapse in the future
under heavy and or repetitive loadings
The on site coral sands and gravels may be used as a source of fill or backfill
materials provided they are processed and reduced to a relatively well graded select
granular material with an average maximum size of about 3 inches Imported fill

materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed finish grade
should consist of select granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder
sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater
than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California
Bearing Ratio CBR vaiue of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1
percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should also

density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use

that the on site dense coral materials or imported engineered fill would provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed sludge processing facility

dense coral materials encountered at shallow depths or on the required imported

one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces

Foundation Probing
Based on our previous experience in the area numerous voids and or cavities
may be present in the coralline deposits encountered at the project site To reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below a
footing we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting be required
during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probes be drilled at each spread
footing location and 10 foot on centers for continuous footings The probe holes should
be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned
bottom of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the
probing operation additional probes should be drilled at closer spacings to help
delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and
any cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3354 00 Page 13
moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of 1 part sand 1 part
cement and 0 7 part water
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site
we recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a
longer lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction may
likely be required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the
presence of cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess
grout take and or changed conditions are observed

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building structure should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building structure and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations slabs and pavements should also be
avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the wails or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
