This letter summarizes our observations during test pile driving of
the above referenced project The locations of the piles are shown on the
Test Pile Location Plan Plate 1 The test pile program started on
and was completed on
Test Pile Installation
A total of one hundred and two 102 test piles were driven The
piles consisted of 20 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete
piles The pile driving hammer used was a Delmag D 30 32 delivering a
maximum rated energy of about 69 8 foot kips Predrilling was adopted to
facilitate pile driving through hard coral _ ledges at shallow Geptns and on
sloping ground
The original test pile locations were revised during the test pile
program to accommodate the variable ground conditions In the plan
attached we have followed the pile numbers originally prepared by the
contractor for consistency For example TP 118 and TP 1371 indicate test
piles driven at locations 118 and 1371 respectively

The test piles not meeting the resistance probably indicate areas
where a number of production piles also will not achieve the design
capacity with the same pile lengths Additional piles or piles with
longer lengths should be used in these areas
For piles not meeting the design capacity it is recommended that
additional piles be driven to support the design loads Per the
structural engineer one pile should be driven down deeper to obtain the
design capacity when four 4 consecutive piles not meeting the resistance
are present due to the load distribution concerns of the structural deck
Also if splice cans are utilized the cans must be driven to below the
predrilled depths
Pile Load Tests
Four 4 load tests are currently being conducted by the Material
Testing Research Branch Highway Division Department of
Transportation Based on the available load test results and test pile
driving records we believe that the majority of the test piles may be
incorporated into the actual pier foundations It is our opinion that
piles driven to 40 blows for the last foot using the Delmag D 30 32
hammer with a minimm stroke of about 7 6 feet may support the design
load of 120 tons per pile
Production Pile Lengths z
At the time of report preparation the pile load tests were still
under way We will furnish the casting lengths for production pile
driving in increments under Separate memorandums when the load test
results are available

driving records are attached for your reference
