This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing services
performed during site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the Air
Pollution Control System Improvement project from January 9, 2009 to February 9, 2011 in
accordance with our fee proposal dated November 13, 2008. We understand that the site
grading for the roadway and sidewalk areas will be performed at a later time.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the H-Power Plant facility (91-174 Hanua Street) at the
Campbell Industrial Park in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project
includes the construction of several new structures at the south end of the existing facility.
The new structures include two baghouses, fan and duct structures. In addition, the
installation of new utility lines is included in the project. The location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1 and the new building structures are shown on the
Site Plan, Plate 2.
A probing and grouting program was implemented for the new structure foundations
because of the potential presence of underground cavities. The results of the probing and
grouting program were provided in our reports entitled “Foundation Probing and Grouting
Report, Air Pollution Control System Improvement Project, Phase 1, Campbell Industrial Park,
Ewa, Oahu, Hawaii” dated November 30, 2009 and “Foundation Probing and Grouting Report,
Air Pollution Control System Improvement Project, Phase 2, Campbell Industrial Park, Ewa,
Oahu, Hawaii’ dated March 8, 2011.
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading and backfill operations for the project site.

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs, Inc. observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Air Pollution
Control System Improvement project from January 29, 2009 to February 9, 2011. The site
grading and utility installation consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and
compaction of fills and backfills to achieve the design finished grades for the project. Parsons
Construction Group — Pacific Division performed the site grading and utility installation work.
During the site grading and backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory
for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3.
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557. In the structure foundation and pavement
areas, the fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.15 of this report. These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at
the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing.
Underground cavities were encountered near Duct Foundation Nos. 2, 3, and 6. The
cavities were exposed and backfilled with 2,000 pounds per square inch Controlled Low
Strength Material (CLSM) and compacted coralline material.
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill and backfill
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading
operations.
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and backfill installation work for the Air Pollution
Control System Improvement project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered
by this report, was performed in conformance with the general intent of the following
references.
