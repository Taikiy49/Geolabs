SOILS INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED COLD STORAGE BUILDING NO 4
AIRPORT INDUSTRIAL PARK
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
performed for the proposed Cold Storage Building No 4 at the Airport
Industrial Park Moanalua Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to build a warehouse type structure about 140 feet
wide and 320 feet long abutting the existing Cold Storage Building Nos
2 and 3 Included in the new construction will be a second floor office
over the new 4 foot high loading dock and an A C paved truck yard
Geology Soils and Foundation Engineering
W O 1353 00 Page 2
In addition the existing automobile parking areas will be extended
along the northern and southern sides of the proposed Building No 4
The new building will be of tilt up construction The proposed layout
of the new building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is planned that a portion of the new roof load will be carried
by the existing wall footings along the eastern side of Building Nos 2
and 3 These existing wall foundations are believed to consist of
3 foot wide continous wall footings bearing a depth of about 5 2 feet
below the finished floor grade The new building will have the same
finished floor level as the existing floor elevation of 21 875 feet
The new foundation loadings are anticipated from up to 50 kips per
column and up to about 4 kips per linear foot for the wall footings It
is our understanding that when the new roof loading is added the
calculated total bearing pressure of the existing wall footing will be
about 2100 p s f maximum
The proposed new building will be subjected to the similar type of
floor loading as the existing Building Nos 2 and 3 The planned floor
section will be similar to the existing Building No 3 floor section
and will consist of 6 inches of concrete slab over several inches of
rigid insulation The portion of the floor within 6 feet of the
exterior wall will be underlain by 2 inches of sand cushion on a vapor
barrier
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 3
The vapor barrier and or the insulation will be placed over either a
6 inch base course layer on 12 inches of select borrow or directly over
18 inches of select borrow The ground under the cold storage areas
will be maintained at temperatures above freezing by a series of heating
pipes circulating warm air
The southern half of the building area will require fills up to 3
feet thick to the bottom of the select borrow level while the northern
half will require only a few inches of fill
Some cut will be required in the truck yard area
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF OUR SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions and to provide geotechnical design recommendations for the
proposed project
For this investigation 10 borings were drilled to depths ranging
from 5 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In addition 3
probe holes were hand excavated to examine the existing footing depth
and soils at the bottom of the footings Subsoil samples obtained were
examined in our laboratory and selected for a series of testings Based
on the field and laboratory findings engineering analyses were made
and our recommendations and conclusions developed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 4
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the borings
along with the laboratory test data are presented in the appendices of
this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SOIL CONDITIONS
The project site is a 200 by 436 foot parcel adjacent to the
existing U S Cold Storage facility situated between Ualena and Koapaka
Streets in the Airport Industrial Park near Honolulu International
Airport
The project site is generally level with the ground gently sloping
down about 4 feet toward Ualena Street in the southern quarter of the
site At the time of our field exploration a small metal open shed and
an asphalt concrete paved area occupied most of the northern half of the
site The remaining portion of the site was vacant and covered with
patches of sparse vegetation and a thin cover of crushed rock or coral
gravel surfacing
Subsurface Conditions
Our borings disclosed generally consistent subsurface soil
conditions throughout the site Underlying the A C pavement or a thin
layer of crushed rocks were 1 5 to 2 0 feet of gray brown color fill
consisting of clay and sandy silt with occasional gravel The lower
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 5
portion of the fill exhibited moderate expansion in our laboratory
tests Under the fill was a 1 to 2 5 foot thick layer of very stiff to
hard light brown silty clay which exhibited moderate to high expansion
The expansive silty clay was underlain by weathered dense brown gray
volcanic tuff The volcanic tuff layer was about 10 to 11 5 feet thick
with a poorly cemented zone of 1 to 1 5 feet at the bottom Under the
tuff was a layer of very stiff to hard dark brown clayey silt to 16 5
Feet the maximum depth of our borings
It was observed that the fill materials immediately above the light
brown silty clay layer were damp to wet particularly in Borings 7 and
8 where the site is paved with asphalt concrete The dampness of the
soil was also noted in the backfill of the existing eastern wall
footings of Building Nos 2 and 3 as examined in our probes
Groundwater level was not encountered in our borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a thin layer of
gray sandy silt and clay fill a layer of expansive stiff light brown
silty clay overlying a competent weathered volcanic tuff Generally
the weathered volcanic tuff will provide excellent foundation support
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Q 1353 00 Page 6
Although the stiff silty clay can provide limited foundation support at
a Suitable moisture content it would be difficult to achieve and
maintain the proper moisture content during construction to control
post construction swelling Therefore placing foundation in this
expansive clay will be less desirable from the foundation performance
point of view
select fill should be used at the subgrade In addition the existing
on site soils should be kept moist to minimize potential swelling of the
underlying silt and or clay under the structures
The ground under the cold storage area will be in contact with the
slabs that will be maintained at sub freezing temperatures To control
deep penetration of sub freezing temperatures below the floor slab a
sub floor heating system consisting of circulating warm air through a
pipe system will be used Detailed discussion and our recommendations
are presented in the following sections and should be followed When
developing our recommendations it is assumed that the sub floor heating
system will effectively maintain the subgrade soil temperature above
freezing
GEOLABS HAWAI

Site Preparation
After clearing the site the existing ground area to receive fill
should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches The existing A C pavement
may be allowed to remain below the building area without scarification
If the soil is too wet or too dry it should be dried or moistened so
that the moisture content at the time of compaction will be within 5
percentage points on the wet side of the optimm moisture content The
scarified soil should then be compacted to 90 of the maximum density as
determined per the ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure
Fill Construction
After the fill area is prepared in accordance to the above
recommendations selected on site materials may be re used in the lower
portion of the ill within the building area The on site fill material
should be free of vegetation or debris Concrete or A C fragments in
excess of 4 inches should not be included For estimating purpose only
the surface 9 inches of soil including AC and base course may be
considered to be suitable as fill The expansive silty clay should not
be used as fill
Tt is important that the suitable portion of the on site material
is carefully segregated during excavation to avoid being contaminated by
the expansive clays Therefore field monitoring by a qualified soils
engineer will be required during the site grading work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 8
It is anticipated that imported fill materials may be required for
the balance of the fill up to subgrade The imported fill materials
should be a non expansive soil generally less than 3 inches in size
free of organic matter and should form a dense stable mass when
compacted to the densities required
All on site and imported fill materials should be inspected tested
and approved by a qualified soils engineer prior to being used as fill
under the slabs
The i11 materials should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in compacted thickness All fill and backfill should
be compacted to dry densities not less than 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density
The subgrade surface and the bottom of the heating pipe trench
should be graded to drain
Subgrade Moistening During Construction
The existing subsoils are expansive and may swell if allowed to dry
and then saturated after slabs on grade or the tilt up wall panels are
placed To minimize the potential soil heaving the soils should be
maintained at a near saturated moisture condition up to the time
concrete is placed Therefore the base course or select borrow surface
should be wetted frequently during construction to prevent drying out of
the underlying soils
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 1353 00 Page 9
The amount of watering and its duration will depend on weather and
site condition A relatively heavy wetting may be made at the end of
each day during two or three week period before concrete or vapor
barrier placement Excessive wettings that tend to muddy up the site
should be avoided
The moistening work may be performed concurrently with other
construction activities and should be monitored by a soils engineer

In view of the expansive nature of the light brown stiff silty
clay we recommend that all new foundations be placed on the
volcanic tuff at about 2 5 to 4 5 feet below the existing grade
The footing bottom should be at least 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Where a deep excavation is required to
reach the underlying tuff the lower portion of the excavation may
be backfill with a lean mix concrete to appropriate foundation
depth for tilt up wall construction
An allowable soil bearing value of up to 8 000 p s may be used
for footings bearing on the volcanic tuff A one third increase
inay be allowed for soil bearing under wind or seismic loads

The total and differential settlements of footings founded on the
volcanic tuff would be negligible
Footing on Soil
Alternatively footings may be placed on the upper soils provided
that the stiff expansive silty clay within 10 inches below the
bottom of the footing is removed The width of the excavation
should extend at least 6 inches beyond the edges of the footing
The over excavation should be backfilled immediately with lean mix
concrete or non expansive soil such as coral or select borrow
compacted to 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
The foundation soils should be maintained in a moist condition
throughout the construction period to minimize their potentials for
post construction heaving The moistening work for the foundation
soils should be similar to that discussed and recommended under the
SUBGRADE MOISTENING DURING CONSTRUCTION section
The bottom of the wall footing in soil should be at least 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade An allowable net soil
bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used with one third
increase permitted under wind or seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 11
The interior column footing may also be founded on soil as
discussed above However the minimum embedment of the footing
bottom may be reduced to 18 inches and the allowable net soil
bearing pressure reduced to 2500 p s f maximum
I the footings are constructed according to the above recommended
criteria we estimate that the total and differential settlements
will range up to three fourths of an inch and one half of an inch
respectively over a distance of 20 feet For continuous wall
footings there should be a structural separation at the soil rock
transition The location of the soil rock transition should be
determined in the field
The footing excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to
verify that suitable bearing material has been encountered
Footings adjacent to any utility trench should be deepened to the
level of the adjacent trench bottom
Non load bearing walls may be placed on the new compacted base
course These wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade and designed for an allowable soil
bearing pressure of 2500 p s f

Existing Footings
The soil conditions directly under the eastern wall footing of the
existing Building No 2 were examined by hand excavated probes It
appears that the footings are resting directly on natural soil of stiff
light brown silty clay in a moist to near saturated condition
Although the light brown silty clay is found to be expansive in
other areas we believe that the soil swelling under the existing wall
footings has stabilized and that the soil is capable to carry a final
total loading including the new roof load of up to 2500 p s f It is
important that the existing moisture of the soils be maintained
Excavating and drying the existing foundation soil near the existing
footing should be avoided
Retaining Walls
The walls facing the truck yard or A C paved automobile parking
areas will act as low retaining walls For the design of the retaining
wall we recommend that the following criteria be used
1 In general the dock height walls should be considered as
restrained top retaining walls Thus the earth pressures for
retaining walls should be 55 p s f per foot of depth
The high tilt up walls on the northern and southern sides of the
building may be considered as unrestrained walls the active earth
pressure may be reduced to 35 p s f per foot of depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1353 00 Page 13
The surcharge load if applied should be converted to equivalent
fill height at a rate of 1 foot of fill height for every 120 p s f
of surcharge load
2 Passive earth pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth may be used
to resist the lateral load In addition a base friction factor of
0 4 may be used
3 The wall footings should be designed according to the criteria
presented under the SPREAD FOOTINGS section of this report
4 All backfill material behind the retaining wall should be
free draining granular material such as No 3 Fine compacted to a
dry density of at least 90 of the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 density
The backfill within 2 feet below the finished floor should be
compacted to 95 of the maximum dry density
5 Subddrains or weepholes should be provided to prevent build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the wall
It is anticipated that most of the subgrade soil under the new
pavements will be a select on site material or imported select borrow
fill Therefore a relatively good subgrade support can be expected
For the new pavements in the truck yard and automobile parking areas
the following sections are recommended

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
08 OP em cay A ae rs A ate
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
To further identify soil characteristics 3 sets of Atterberg Limits
tests were performed The results are presented on Plate B 1
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a bag sample of the surface
materials to evaluate its subgrade supporting characteristics The test
results are summarized on Plate B 1
One moisture density relationship test modified Proctor was
performed on a bag sample of the surface materials to determine the
compaction characteristics The test results are summarized on Plate
B l
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils The
test results are summarized on Plate B 2
One unconfined compression test was performed on an undisturbed
sample to further evaluate soil strength characteristics The test
results are presented on Plate B 3
