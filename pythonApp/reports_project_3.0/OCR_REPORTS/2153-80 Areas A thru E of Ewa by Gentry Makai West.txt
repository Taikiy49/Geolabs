Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Areas A through E of Ewa by Gentry Makai West
Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading operations for Areas A through E of Ewa by Gentry Makai West from
May 1998 through September 1998
The subject site is located east of the Barbers Point Golf Course west of the Coral
Creek Golf Course and south of Geiger Road in Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site
is also bounded by undeveloped pasture land to the south
The mass grading work for Areas A through E was performed by RHS Lee Inc The
mass grading work consisted of fills on the order of about 3 to 5 feet in height Cuts were only
made at high areas to allow placement of 24 inches of coralline material from the finished
grade The fill materials were generally generated from Area G3 and the western end of Area
G2 of the adjacent Coral Creek Golf Course The available fill materials generally consisted
of tannish white silty coral sand
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed the exposed subgrades
were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Method D 1557 The fill material was then placed in level lifts and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and
the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results
of the compaction tests are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 10 of this report Note that
the test numbers are not consecutive as the adjacent golf course grading tests were also
included in the numbering

reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred without our
observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should be
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and
methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our services were
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering
