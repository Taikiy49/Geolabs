PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kaupuni Village residential development is located at a vacant parcel
encompassing approximately 3 35 acres off Plantation Road in the District of Waianae on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the construction drawings and information provided we understand that
a reinforced concrete jacket is planned for a 7 foot long section of the existing 21 inch
sewer on Plantation Road as part of the off site utility improvements Due to the presence
of soft loose soils encountered during the new sewer manhole excavation in the proximity
of the proposed jacket the contractor has expressed concerns that the anticipated
soft loose soils may not be able to support the weight of the reinforced jacket and the pipe
may crack if the weight of the jacket causes differential settlement as stated in the
Request for Information RFI No 3 dated
Based on the subsequent meeting and discussions it was decided that
one boring would be drilled to evaluate the softness and thickness of the anticipated
soft loose soils underlying the planned sewer concrete jacket The information obtained
will be used to determine what course of action should be taken to reduce the potential
for distress to the existing sewer line resulting from the weight of the concrete jacket
which may cause potential settlement of the sewer line into the underlying soft loose
soils

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the additional field exploration program is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions in the proximity of the planned reinforced concrete jacket for the
existing 21 inch sewer on Plantation Road In order to accomplish this we conducted an
exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of one boring extending to a depth of about 39 feet
below the existing pavement surface for one day of field exploration
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the boring by our
geologist
4 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our senior engineer
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions in the proximity of the planned reinforced
concrete jacket on Plantation Road by drilling and sampling one boring designated as
Boring No 101 extending to a depth of about 39 feet below the existing pavement
surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our additional boring drilled in the proximity of the planned reinforced concrete
jacket on Plantation Road generally encountered medium dense to dense silty gravel and
silty sands between depths of about 8 to 15 feet below the existing pavement surface
Lagoonal deposits consisting of loose silty sands gravel and soft sandy silts were then
encountered and extended to a depth of about 31 feet below the existing pavement
surface The lagoonal deposits were generally underlain by medium dense silty sands
and medium stiff clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
39 feet below the existing pavement surface

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our review of the construction drawings it appears that the bottom of the
proposed reinforced concrete jacket will be located at a depth of about 9 5 feet below the
existing pavement surface Our drilled boring generally encountered medium dense to
dense silty gravel and silty sands between depths of about 8 to 15 feet below the existing
pavement surface Based on our evaluation it is our opinion that the proposed reinforced
concrete jacket may bear directly on the medium dense to dense silty gravel and silty
sands anticipated below the bottom of the concrete jacket Additional over excavation
below the concrete jacket or deep foundation support system would not be required for the
support of the planned concrete jacket Geolabs will monitor the actual exposed subsoil
conditions during the excavation work to confirm and or modify the recommendations
presented herein

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions in the proximity of the planned reinforced
concrete jacket at Plantation Road by drilling and sampling one boring designated as
Boring No 101 The boring extended to a depth of about 39 feet below the existing
pavement surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the boring by visual and
textural examination in the field and observed the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and testing in the
laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The
penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the number of blows
required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need to be factored to
obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
