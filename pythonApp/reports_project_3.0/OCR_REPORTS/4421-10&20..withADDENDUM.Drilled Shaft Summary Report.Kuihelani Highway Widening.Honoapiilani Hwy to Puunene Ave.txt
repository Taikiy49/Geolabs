PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The highway widening project is located along Kuihelani Highway Route 380
between Honoapiilani Highway and Puunene Avenue in the Wailuku District on the Island
of Maui Hawaii The project begins on Kuihelani Highway at Station No 1 35 in the
vicinity of Honoapiilani Highway and proceeds towards Kahului ending at the intersection
of Puunene Avenue and Dairy Road at Station No 276 82 The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The roadway
will be widened from a two lane highway into a four lane divided highway The proposed
improvement will also include widening of seventeen existing box pipe culverts and three
stream bridge crossings
At two of the stream bridge crossings Paleaahu and Waikapu Streams drilled shaft
foundations consisting of 5 foot diameter shafts were used for the abutment and wingwall
structures Each drilled shaft for the bridge foundation was designed based on unfactored
single shaft compression load capacities of about 860 and 1 310 kips for the Paleaahu and
Waikapu Stream Bridge structures respectively The identification numbers and
approximate locations of the drilled shafts are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration the Paleaahu Bridge site is generally
underlain by older alluvial deposit consisting of stiff to hard clayey silts and silts with sand
gravel and cobbles and dense silty sand with gravel to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 76 5 feet below the existing ground surface
At the Waikapu Bridge site the borings drilled near the bridge site generally
indicated that the older alluvial deposits were overlain by consolidated dune sand deposits
The consolidated dune sand deposits generally consisted of medium dense fine
calcareous sand
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
Since the bridge structures are located adjacent to existing streams and drainageways
the groundwater levels will likely vary in response to the water level in the streams and
drainageways Water levels at the project site may also be influenced by seasonal
precipitation and other factors
TRIAL SHAFT PROGRAM
The construction of one trial shaft at each bridge site was specified at the Paleaahu
and Waikapu Stream Bridges The trial shafts were 5 feet in diameter and were located
near the northern abutment structure at each bridge The trial shafts were constructed at
Paleaahu Stream Bridge on March 6 and 7 2002 and at the Waikapu Stream Bridge on
March 5 2002 by Malcolm Drilling Company Inc The intent of the trial shaft installation is
for the contractor to demonstrate the adequacy of the drilling method and equipment
proposed for the subsurface conditions at the project site
The trial shafts were excavated using a Soilmec R 622 track mounted drill rig and
the bottom of the trial shafts were excavated to about 66 9 feet below the existing ground
surface or to Elevations 104 9 and 121 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the Paleaahu
and Waikapu Stream Bridge structures respectively During the trial shaft excavation a
log of materials encountered was maintained by an engineer from our office Logs of the
trial shaft construction and materials encountered are provided in Appendix A on
Plates A 1 through A 4
As part of the trial shaft program the trial shaft was converted into the load test
shaft by constructing the trial shaft with a reinforcing steel cage Therefore the contractor
placed a reinforcing steel cage into the trial shaft hole after the trial shaft excavation The
reinforcing steel cage was equipped with embedment strain gages Concrete was then
placed in the drilled hole using a 4 inch diameter steel tremie pipe to complete the
construction of the load test shaft
