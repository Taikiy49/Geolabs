INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation performed
for the proposed Hapapa Gardens Cluster Subdivision The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate l

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The cluster subdivision project site is about 6 acres in size and
bounded by Hapapa Street to the north general directions Mahoe Street
to the east Waipahu Street to the south and a 4 acre vacant lot to the
west Substantial amount of cuts and fills are required to grade the
site for the subdivision lot and related access roadway constructions A
general layout of the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the preliminary grading plan the estimated earthwork
quantities required would be about 10 000 cubic yards of excavation and
16 800 cubic yards of embankment Cuts up to about 6 feet and fills up
to 13 feet are planned
House foundation loadings are anticipated to be light and typical
for single family structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to provide recommendations for
site grading house foundation and pavement design based on the soil
information obtained from Borings 1 through 5 drilled for this
investigation The borings were generally drilled and sampled to about
15 feet below the existing ground at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 3
Soil sampling and standard penetration tests were performed under
the observation of our engineer The results of our field and laboratory
tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
findings and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this
report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
At the time of our exploration the ground surface at the site was
generally at Elevation 72 to 74 feet along Hapapa Street and sloped down
at various grades to about Elevation 48 feet along Waipahu Street to the
south
The site was occupied by old plantation homes with grasses shrubs
large trees driveways and occasional piles of loose fills Piles of
trash and organic matter were noted in several localized areas throughout
the site There were several up rooted large trees showing large root
penetrations of up to 5 feet below the ground
A wet area which was probably due to a nearby broken water line
was noted close to Boring 1 location near the edge of Hapapa Street on
the eastern quarter of the site A broken water line was also noted on
the ground surface nearby

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a detrital alluvial formation of Quaternary
period identified as a valley flat that abuts the lower Slope of the
Koolau Volcano This older alluvium has generally weathered to an
iron rich mass of various colored soils with spheroidally weathered
cobbles and boulders The site soils consist of stif silts with vary ing
amount of clays and sands These valley flats are frequently cut by
numerous streams and gullies of various sizes Man made cuts and fills
were since made for various activities
Soil Conditions
Underlying the heavy vegetation and localized surface fill the
natural soils consisted mainly of stiff to very stiff clayey silts with
pockets of clay The clay soils are moderately to highly expansive and
change in volume with changes in moisture content The silt soils
generally exhibited expansion of 1 to 4 percent while the clay soil
showed expansion of up to 13 in our laboratory testing Weathered and
less weathered basalt gravel cobbles and boulders could be expected
occasionally within the top 10 feet but might be more frequent below that
depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W B Page 5
Water was encountered in Boring 1 at a depth of about 2 5 feet
This water level appeared to be localized only as groundwater was not
encountered in the other borings which were drilled to depths of about 15
feet below the ground surface As discussed earlier the abnormally high
water encountered in Boring 1 was probably caused by leaking of old water
lines in the vicinity
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclose that the on site soils are generally low to
moderately expansive with occasional highly expansive pockets or layers
of soils occurring at various locations and depths In general we
believe that if the on site expansive soils are capped with at least 2
feet of imported non expansive fill material the potential for future
swelling can be reduced significantly
In order to minimize the swelling potential of the on site soils
the general grading concept should be to use the on site cut material in
the deeper portion of the fill then capping the fill lots with imported
non expansive soil
For the cut lots it is anticipated that the expansive soil
condition will occur only in some of the lots and can be handled on an
individual lot basis at the time of site grading The cut or thinly
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 6
filled lots should be inspected individually by the soils engineer to see
if expansive soils exist within shallow depths and if over excavation
and capping with imported fill will be required For budgeting purposes
it is estimated that about one third of the cut and thinly filled lots
would require special handling
After the on site materials are properly capped we believe that
on grade slab construction as well as spread foundation support may be
used for the house construction with the understanding that small amount
of swelling might occur on the floors It is important that subgrade
soils are thoroughly moistened for at least 2 days before placing of the
upper capping fill or slabs and that the perimeters of the slab are
deepened at least 12 inches in order to better control future subgrade
soil moisture fluctuations Detailed recommendations are presented in
the following sections
Site Grading
Clearing Grubbing and Stripping
Prior to clearing and stripping all water lines and drain lines
should be plugged or removed as appropriate If the source of
groundwater is eliminated we believe that the high water level in
the Boring 1 area would return to normal level at a much lower
elevation and improve site grading conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 7
The existing building loose piles of fills existing footings all
trash and debris should be removed
Existing vegetation should also be stripped Tree roots should be
grubbed and any roots in excess of 1 5 inches in diameter should be
removed
Cesspool may be encountered near the old plantation homes They
should be identified prior to grading operation if practicable
Sludge and other organic matter should be removed and the cesspool
backfilled with compacted select material Any other localized soft
or loose fill area encountered during clearing and grubbing
operations should be excavated and backfilled with compacted select
material
After clearing and grubbing the area should be stripped of topsoil
that contains vegetation and organic matter The stripping may
average 12 inches The stripped material may be reused as topsoil
in the planting areas but should not be used as fill Entrapping
deleterious materials such as mats of grasses or piles of wood
under the fill should be avoided
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials

Surface Preparation
a se em se ee a ee ee a ae an
After stripping the area to receive fill should be scarified at
least 6 inches below the stripped grade moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of the maximum density as determined by the ASTM
D 1557 78 test Modified Proctor Any soft or weak areas disclosed
during this recompaction should be over excavated removed and then
backfilled with engineered fill
Monitoring of the surface preparation by a soils technician is
particularly important especially to identify soft areas or
unsuitable materials
Slope Construction
Fill or cut slope should not be steeper than 2 1 H V The on site
soil is primarily silt with some sand or clay and easily erodable
Therefore the slope should be protected from run off water to
reduce soil erosion Interceptor ditches or swales should be
provided at the top to direct water away from the top of the slope
To assure adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back
to the design grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 9
The construction of fill on slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched properly Keying and benching
should consist of excavating a horizontal bench into the slope A
typical section showing keying and benching into an existing slope
for this project is shown on Plate 3
Inspection for Expansive Soils
tiated aetna ate et tk
We recommend that lots in cut and with less than 2 feet of fill also
be identified in the field and inspected by the soils engineer
during mass grading to see if highly expansive soil exist within the
2 feet of the finished rough grade Such inspection should also
include sampling and performing laboratory tests If soils are
found to be expansive then they would have to be over excavated
Generally for preliminary cost estimate purpose about 2 feet of
capping or over excavation and backfill with non expansive soil is
recommended at this time
Fill Material Placement
We believe that most of the on site natural soils will be suitable
for re use as fill provided that they are not contaminated with
unsuitable materials or over sized particles
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 10
Some of the on site soils contain some highly expansive clays and
it is recommended that these on site soils should be placed at the
deeper part of the new fill followed by capping with at least 2 feet
of select imported fill
Cobbles and boulders were encountered in some borings and also
noted in the loose piles of fill at the site Therefore boulders
would probably be found interspersed over the site Some of the
boulders less than 2 feet in size may be recycled in the fill as
described below The larger boulders should be hauled off site
Imported fill especially when used as a capping fill should in
general be of low expansion potential with maximum particle size not
exceeding 6 inches The expansion value should be less than 1
percent and the CBR value should be 8 or greater We recommend
imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being hauled to the site
The fill material within the top 3 feet of the finished grade should
be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness For the engineered fill below 3 feet of the finished
grade the thickness of the lift may be increased to 12 inches
Boulders or rock fragments in excess of 6 inches should not be
placed within the top 3 feet of the rough finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 11
Occasional over sized rocks may be placed in the lower portion of
the 111 provided they are not more than 2 feet in maximum diameter
and can be embedded in compacted soil mass
If the material is too wet or too dry it should be dried or
moistened so that the moisture content at the time of compaction
will be within 3 percentage points of the optimum moisture content
Each layer should be compacted to 90 relative compaction according
to ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The top 6 inches of the roadway
subgrade should be compacted to 95 relative compaction
Foundations
Spread Footings
Ce
We believe if the site grading procedures are performed in
accordance with the recommendations presented herein spread footing
foundations bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill may be
used to support the proposed structures
All footings should be placed at a minimum depth of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade The spread footing may be designed using
a net allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f for total of dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for wind or
seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 12
Anticipated settlements are small and probably less than one half of
an inch
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the proposed structures If
the highly expansive soils which might be encountered occasionally are
capped with at least 2 feet of non expansive soils we believe that
on grade slabs may be constructed provided they are constructed according
to the procedures recommended hereunder
It is recommended that a 12 inch thickened edge embedment be
utilized to provide a protective barrier against future water intrusion
under the house slab area
To further minimize the swelling potential of the site soil at this
project the following general procedures are recommended
1 The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
2 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 13
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer
of aggregate cushion fill such as 3B Fine rock and separated from
wall foundations
Pavements
Three short roads are planned within the subdivision Some cut and
fill are planned
Based on the results of our preliminary laboratory CBR tests on the
site materials the preliminary pavement design for cost budget purpose
is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow Subbase
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Subgrade Soil Compacted to 95
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section can be revised
accordingly
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1361 00 B Page 14
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that excavation and placement of fill be observed
by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of existing structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by
specific requirements or recommendations within the soils
report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of trash debris organic matter and all other dele
terious materials These materials shall be removed and
disposed off site All existing water or sewer lines to
be abandoned shall be plugged
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose com
petent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select materials
Page 2
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches brought to a moisture content
within 3 percentage points of the optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a Slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface shall
be keyed and benched into competent soil to bond the
fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
III Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved a minimum of seven 7 working days
prior to transportation to the site Fill material
shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks
or lumps in excess of six inches 6 in diameter

In addition the imported fill material shall be of low
expansion potential less than 1 swell per ASTM D 1883
Test Method and have CBR values of 8 or greater when
compacted to 95 of the maximum dry density according to
the ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill within the top 3 feet of finished grade
shall be placed in horizontal loose layers not to
exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Below 3 feet the
thickness of the lift may be increased to 12 inches
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within
each layer
B In general rock fragments or boulders less than six
inches 6 in diameter may be utilized and occasional
boulders up to 2 feet in size may also be recycled in
the fill provided they are not concentrated in any one
area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
C Rocks greater than two feet 2 in diameter shall
be removed from the fill unless specifically approved
for placement in the fill by the soils engineer
GEOLABS HAWAII
Page 4
D All 111 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 per
cent of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the
soils report
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tired rollers suited to the soil type
and capable of producing the specified results in an
expedient and efficient manner The equipment shall be
in good working condition fully ballasted and self
cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce
a uniformly compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications shall
not be approved and shall be removed or reworked until
the required density is obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the necessary inspections
and field density testing

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
The existing underground structures such as cesspools
cisterns septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be
demolished and all unsuitable materials removed as
directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with the above
specifications under Placement and Compaction of Fill
Other Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to heavy
rain the previous fill surface shall be approved before
resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the
fill so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
