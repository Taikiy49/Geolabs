INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 1 0 M G reservoir tank at The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the southern coast of the island of Kauai to the west
of Koloa The proposed 1 0 M G reservoir is located approximately 150 feet southwest
of an existing steel water tank situated between the new Kukuiula Wastewater Treatment
Plant and the existing Koloa Road
It is proposed to construct a 1 0 million gallon capacity reinforced concrete tank
structure on the site The tank will be approximately 96 feet in diameter and 23 feet in
height
Based on the preliminary grading plans cuts up to 12 feet will be required to
create a level pad for the new tank with a proposed finished floor elevation of 225 feet
above Mean Sea Level
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading and
foundations of the proposed reservoir
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of September
4 1991 The scope of work was limited to
1 Mobilization and demobilization ot a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of five 5 borings at the project site to depths from
11 5 to 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface

Geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and pertinent engineering properties of the
materials encountered
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed reservoir
6 Preparation of six 6 copies of a formal report summarizing our work on the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed water reservoir site is located above the Kukuiula Planned
Community Phase project currently under construction on the island of Kauai at an
elevation of approximately 230 feet MSL
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2809 00 Page 4
At the time of our exploration the project site was being used as a cattle ranch
The site was covered with moderately tall grasses scattered shrubs and Hale Koa trees
An existing cane haul road crosses the site
The site generally slopes downwards towards the southeast direction with existing
ground elevations ranging from 220 to 240 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling five 5 borings
to depths of 11 5 to 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a hard residual soil layer at the surface
approximately 11 0 to 17 5 feet in thickness consisting of reddish brown silty clays and
clayey silts underlain by a hard to very stiff saprolite deposit consisting of mottled reddish
brown and gray clayey silt to 46 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the time of our
exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test results are
presented in the appendices of this report
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicated that the proposed reservoir site is underlain by
competent subsoils consisting of very stiff to hard silty clays and clayey silts

Reservoir Foundation
It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may be
used to support the proposed reservoir structure
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be utilized for the design
of the footing foundations bearing on the stiff on site soils This bearing value is for total
dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short
duration wind and seismic load considerations
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent exterior grade
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed reservoir structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend that an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive
soil pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundations and
a frictional coefficient of 0 4 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed
between the footing bottoms and the stiff subgrade soils
Site Grading
Based on the current grading plan the earthwork for the project involves up to 12
feet of excavation to create a level pad for the reservoir foundation
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2809 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The site should be graded to drain water away from the reservoir foundation
Cut slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used in the on site
soils
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that the exterior walls of underground structures such as
the altitude valve vault will function as retaining walls The guidelines for design of
retaining walls are as follows
1 For lateral earth pressures the following may be used
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Walls restrained at top 55 p c f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
An allowable bearing value of up to 3 000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2809 00 Page 7
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
the wall footing on level areas
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the resistance
of the wall against sliding
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade On sloping
areas the wall footing should be embedded an additional amount to provide
a minimum 10 foot horizontal set back distance measured from the outer
edge of the wall footing to the slope face
5 Select granular backfill material such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel No 3B
Fine is recommended behind the retaining wall to minimize excessive
compaction required and to aid in the drainage of the area behind the wall
The granular backfill should have a minimum horizontal thickness of 18
inches from the back face of the wall
The granular backfill should extend to a height of two 2 feet below finish
grade The remaining backfill should consist of an impervious capping of
non expansive soils such as on site clayey silt soils
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be utilized
behind the walls Subdrains should be properly constructed to drain water
away from the wall

Pavement Section
An access road into the site and a driveway around the proposed reservoir is
planned The following pavement section may be utilized for the preliminary design of the
access road and driveway
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The recommended section considers light traffic and is based upon the assumption
that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
The base course should be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
We recommend that additional CBR and density tests and or field observations be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to verify the
adequacy of the above section
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations

It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of the

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of two of the field borings were surveyed by R M Towill Corporation
The locations of the other field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from structures indicated on the topographic survey map Elevations of the
borings are also approximate having been estimated by interpolation from elevation points
on the same plan The physical locations and elevations of the field borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2809 00 Page 10
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulations of this report However it must be noted
that fluctuations may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer in

contain sufficient data or the proper information for use in contract bid estimation A
contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical

constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs

APENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 90 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These results are summarized on Plate B 1
