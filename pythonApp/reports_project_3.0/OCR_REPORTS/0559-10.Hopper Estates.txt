INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface in
vestigation at the subject Phase I parcel and provides re
commendations for the design and construction of the founda
tions to support the proposed structures and for the ancilliary
earthwork
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to develop the 22 6 acre Hopper Estate
site for townhouse development Phase I consists of a total of
six 2 story townhouse structures located at the north east corner
of the property A 260 feet long section of the proposed 44 feet
W 1975
wide roadway will be provided in this first increment development
It is anticipated that most of the structures for Phase I will
be constructed at or near grade Maximum cuts and fills will
be less than two feet Bearing walls with light loading of
20 feet on centers will be utilized to support upper floor
and roof loads
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The Phase I development site is located south of the intersection
of California Ave and Hill Drive at the upper Wahiawa area The
Phase I site is presently bordered by houses at its north and east
boundaries the Iliahi Elementary School is located to its west
and a deep gully with dense Encolyptus trees are to the south
The 250 feet by 650 feet site is presently covered with dense
California grass Guava and fruit trees suggested that the
site was once occupied
The surface soil is dry and some area show signs of mud cracks
During the wetter winter season ponds of standing water was
observed on the site
The site is essentially level with very slight sloping to the
southwest The steep slope to the southeast is well protected
by forest and have no signs of erosion or landslide The slope
here is well over 60
W 1975
The ground surface elevations utilized in this report are based
upon the ground surface contours shown on the plan titled Site
Plan Grading Plan Sheet by Village Architect Asso
ciates Corp
Geology
The Wahiawa area is situated between the two known volcanic
shields of Koolau and Waianae The central plain region as
it is known in Oahu is possibly the results of alluviation
due to the weathering of both shields Regional ocean level
changes caused deep gullies which cut into the alluvium plain
exposing some very well solidified and consolidated alluvium
and in some cases into the bed rock The soil in these area
is rich in aluminium and iron thus give it the famous reddish
color
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing seven
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration methods
and the accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
The explorations disclosed that the site in general is underlain
by a uniform layer of residual soil composed of silty clay and
clayey silt These materials were found from ground surface to
depths of 10 to 15 feet in the borings
W 1975
Generally the upper soils are predominately very stiff to hard
in consistency In Boring 5 a layer of medium stiff soil was
encountered at a depth of 7 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface The same medium stiff soil layer was also found in
boring 7 from 13 to 16 feet
DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is suitable for the Phase I development The proposed
2 story structure can be supported on spread or line footings
bearing on the stiff on site soils No expansive soils were
encountered at this increment and slab on grade can be utilized
Foundations
The townhouse units are of frame construction and supported upon
line or spread footings bearing at or near the existing ground
surface The 2 story structural loadings are low which will
result in minimum settlements
The foundations should bear a minimum of 18 inches below the
proposed finish grade and upon the natural soils These materials
will provide an allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per
square foot with estimated settlements of less than inch total
and inch differential
W 1975
For the foundation located close to the gully at southeast of
the site a minimum of 10 feet set back shall be allowed from
the outer edge of foundation out to the slope surface
The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to the
placement of the concrete to assure that the bearing conditions
are consistent with the findings of the subsurface explorations
and to inspect for possible isolated pockets of expansive soils
Floor Slabs
Expansive soils were not encountered at this increment during
the subsurface explorations Surface soil samples at their
natural moisture contents were tested for expansive potential
The resulting swells were less than half of one percent
Slab on grade construction can be utilized without special
provisions for expansive soils It is recommended that the
subgrade be compacted wet of optimum to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density as previously specified This will
provide relatively uniform slab support
Slab on grade to be provided with floor coverings should be
protected by a plastic vapor barrier The plastic should be
covered by a thin layer of sand for protection and to avoid
punctures
W 1975
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings and it is not
necessary to install a granular base beneath the slab to eliminate
capilliary moisture
Earthwork Grading
The proposed site grading entails shallow cuts and fills with the
structures bearing at or near the existing ground surface
The proposed fill will be utilized to support the floor slabs of
the dwellings parking and walkways This structural fill should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T
Should on site soils be utilized the maximum density is appro
Xximately 95 pounds per cubic foot at an optimum moisture content
of 30 The samples obtained from the test borings had natural
moisture contents generally exceeding 30 If the on site soils
are utilized as fill they will require some drying to bring them
to near optimum moisture content
Prior to the placement of structural fill organic and deleterious
materials should be removed and the upper six inches of the existing
ground surface scarified The scarified surface should then be
brought to wet of optimum moisture content and compacted to the
recommended density Additional fill should be placed in 8 inch
horizontal lifts and compacted to the above specified density
W 1975
Where fill will be utilized for structural support the com
paction operation should be inspected on a continuous basis
to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is consis
tently achieved
Cuts within the natural soil should be made no steeper than
one and a half horizontal to one vertical 1 to 1 The
fill slopes should not exceed two horizontal to one vertical
2 to 1 The stiff residual soils can be cut at near vertical
slopes for short term construction conditions
Parking and Roadway Pavements
During the subsurface explorations samples of the surface soils
were obtained and a CBR test performed The resulting CBR value
of 15 represents a medium subgrade Based upon a CBR value of 15
and an expansion less than 0 5 the following may be used
Residential Parking 44 Ft Roadway

145 Asphalt Concrete 6 Base Course

The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section 31 2 Standard Specifi
cations for Public Works Construction or equivalent The subgrade
soils should be scarified down to a depth of 12 inches wetted and
compacted wet of optimum moisture to 95 compaction
W 1975
The above design thickness for the proposed 44 feet wide
roadway is based on present soil informations only and it shall
be used for cost estimate purpose The final design shall be
adjusted in the field based on the subgrade condition at grading
Additional modification will be required if expansive soil should
be encountered at isolated roadway section in this Phase I con
struction or at the other increment developments
Construction Monitoring
Foundations excavations should be inspected prior to concrete
placement to assure that subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation
The earthwork should be inspected on a continuous full time basis
if the fill will be utilized for structural support to assure
that the recommended degree of compaction is consistently achieved
Should you have any questions concerning our findings or conclusions
please do not hesitate to contact us

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 7
borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the bor
ings varied from 10 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan and
Boring Locations Platel
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in the
field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accord
ance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plate A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
samplers or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture density determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification of the
soils The results of these tests are presented on the Log
of Borings at the appropriate sample location
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test and a modified proctor
compaction test were performed on a surface sample of the on
site soil to evaluate the suitability of the soil as fill and
pavement support The results are given on Plate 2
Three swell tests were conducted on near surface samples to
measure the swell potential The swell test results are
summarized on Plate 3
