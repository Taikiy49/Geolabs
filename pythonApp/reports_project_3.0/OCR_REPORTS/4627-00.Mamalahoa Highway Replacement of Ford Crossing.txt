REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
GEOLABS INC
W O 4627 00 Page 6
volcanoes including the following Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea some activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and is considered to be dormant and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both are still active The project site is situated on the southeastern
flank of the Mauna Loa Shield Volcano where more recent lava flows from Kilauea Volcano
have subsequently covered the landscape
The volume of the Mauna Loa Shield Volcano extending from the ocean floor is
estimated to be on the order of about 10 000 cubic miles making it possibly the largest
volcanic mountain on earth Furthermore it is estimated from current lava production that
Mauna Loa may have been forming for the past 1 to 2 million years Mauna Loa is actually
composed of two separate shield volcanoes Mauna Loa and Ninole The latter having been
covered by Mauna Loa lavas left only a few localized surface expressions visible at present
The Mauna Loa shield has been built by volcanic eruptions along two principal rift zones that
extend southwestward and east northeastward from the summit caldera
The Kilauea Volcano caldera is located approximately 14 miles toward the northeast
of the project site The eruptive rift zones radiating from the caldera are located toward the
east southwest rift zone and northeast east rift zone of the project site The present
eruption of Kilauea Volcano is located along the east rift zone in the vicinity of the
Pu u O o vent
The majority of the surface rock exposures at Mauna Loa consist of lavas and volcanic
deposits belonging to the Kau Volcanic Series Pleistocene and Holocene age and the older
Kahuku Volcanic Series Pleistocene age In addition a regional volcanic ash deposit
identified as Pahala Ash Pleistocene age forms a soil mantle overlying and interbedded
within the rocks of the Kau Volcanic Series The volcanic ash deposits typically occur as
kipukas isolated deposits with some significant accumulations ranging up to about 30 feet
in thickness
Pahala Ash is a low plasticity silt derived from the weathering of ash cinder and Pele s
Hair Pahala Ash generally has low shear strength and when the moisture content is high
GEOLABS INC
W O 4627 00 Page 7
enough it loses significant strength when it is remolded becomes thixotropic Therefore the
typical Pahala Ash may be potentially liquefiable during seismic events
The Ford Crossing Makakupu Bridge project site is located on land that has been
mapped by others as covered with Puna Volcanic Series basalt rock Holocene age at the
ground surface The map symbol p40 shown on the Geologic Map Plate 3 identifies the
general location of the Puna Volcanic Series lava flows The Puna Volcanic Series unit p4o
essentially consists of extensive regional coverage by pahoehoe lava flows composed of
tholeiitic and alkalic basalt that were deposited approximately 450 to 750 years ago The older
Kau Volcanic Series unit k3 of Mauna Loa consisting of tholeiitic basalt mixed with some
volcanic ash may be buried beneath the more recent age Puna Volcanic Series deposits at
the project site
Based on our reconnaissance of the project site some thin vesicular pahoehoe lava
flows representative of the Puna Volcanic Series unit p40 of Kilauea Volcano cap a layer of
reddish brown colored volcanic ash The surface pahoehoe lava flow appears to range on the
order of between about 2 and 6 feet in thickness depending on location The underlying
volcanic ash layer is estimated to be about 5 feet in thickness Based on observations of
some near surface exposures about 500 feet upstream of the existing ford crossing the
volcanic ash layer is underlain by older pahoehoe basalt rock that is believed to be
representative of the Kau Volcanic Series unit k3 of Mauna Loa Volcano
The Ford Crossing Makakupu Bridge project site is located on gently sloping terrain
that appears to have formed by the banking of younger Kilauea Volcano lavas against the
older lavas comprising the flank of Mauna Loa Volcano The project site and vicinity supports
a relatively thicker growth of vegetation with basalt rock and alluvial soils exposed at the
ground surface The existing stream channel located at the project site appears to be of lower
gradient with banks on the order of less than about 5 to 10 feet in height The stream appears
to have eroded the near surface volcanic ash and the capping lava flow to expose the older
pahoehoe lava flows at the stream channel bottom The basalt rock exposed at the stream
GEOLABS INC
W O 4627 00 Page 8
channel bottom appears to consist of thinly bedded strongly vesicular pahoehoe lava flows
that are closely to moderately fractured and slightly weathered
Pahoehoe basalt rock is generally the result of thinner more fluid lava flows The
feeding river lobes of a pahoehoe flow crust over to develop a continuous roof leaving the
interior lava stream to flow through a tunnel of its own making These remnant flow tunnels
or voids are known as lava tubes The lava tubes feed the advancing lava flow front and
margins As a result the lava tube may branch or converge generally in a downhill direction
to maintain the lava flow In the waning stages of an eruption the lava may drain from the
main tube system leaving open tunnels ranging from several feet to over 40 feet in diameter
The open tubes resemble tunnels with arched roofs and nearly flat floors The main lava tubes
may extend downslope for several miles occasionally reaching all the way to the coastline
Large lava tubes were not encountered by the borings drilled at the project site
Another type of void space that is typically encountered in pahoehoe rock is a pocket
blister Rock blisters are typically isolated closed void structures formed by the cooling of the
lava flow and trapped volcanic gas within the flow interior Some blisters may extend a short
distance downhill before pinching out and terminating
The surface of pahoehoe type lava flows are generally smoother than their rough and
irregular counterpart the a a lava flow A a lava flows form from generally more viscous
slower moving lavas and are expressed by a solid rock core sandwiched between an upper
and lower less dense clinker layer The pahoehoe flow surface may be expressed by broad
dome shaped hillocks created by the heaving and buckling of the flow crust as it approaches
obstructions
FAULTING AND SEISMICITY
Based on our review of available geologic information the Ford Crossing Makakupu
Bridge project site is not located in the immediate proximity of mapped geologic fault
structures The terminus of the nearest mapped faults is located approximately 0 75 miles
toward the northwest and southwest of the project site as shown on the Geologic Map Plate 3
GEOLABS INC
W O 4627 00 Page 9
Others have mapped these faults previously as concealed normal faults Concealed faults are
faults where the surface expression of the fault structure has not been identified
Furthermore the project site is located easterly of multiple northeast to southwesi
oriented normal faults associated with the regional Kaoiki Honuapo Fault System which is
located toward the west and southwest of the site In addition some possible ground
instability earthquake potential may be associated with the east rift zone of Kilauea Volcano
which is located approximately 2 miles toward the east of the site Therefore the project site
should be considered to be located in a region of high seismic activity The general location
of these fault and rift features is shown on the Geologic Map Plate 3
Due to the close proximity of the project site to active volcanoes and mapped fault
zones the project site is considered to be located in a seismic zone of potential recurring
activity Earthquake activity associated with fault movement and underground magma
movement should be anticipated However the potential for fault rupture at the ground
surface of the project site is not known at this time

Scour Potential
Scour is the result of the erosive action of flowing water excavating and carrying away
material from the bed and banks of the stream Total scour over a period of time generally
consists of three components 1 Aggregation and Degradation 2 Contraction Scour and
3 Local Scour The rates of scour depend on a number of factors such as the shape and
dimensions of a pier or abutment depth of flow velocity of approach flow size and gradation
of streambed material and bed configuration
One of the factors affecting the scour depth is the grain size characteristic of the stream
bed material The median diameter of the stream bed material Ds in conjunction with the

Foundation Probing
Based on our experience in the area and observation of the core samples retrieved
voids and or cavities commonly exist in the basalt rock formation at the project site In
addition small voids were encountered in the basalt rock formation encountered in one of the
borings drilled To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the
collapse of cavities below a footing we recommend that a program of cavity probing and
grouting at the foundation locations abutments and piers be implemented during
construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that a probe hole be drilled for every
100 square feet of foundation area or at 10 feet on center along the abutment and pier
foundations The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to
a depth of at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of footing elevation If cavities and or
voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional probe holes
should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating the vertical and lateral extent of the
cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with
sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures We recommend that a low strength
sand cement grout mixture with a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting
operations As an alternative to sand cement grout Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM
may also be used for the grouting operations
