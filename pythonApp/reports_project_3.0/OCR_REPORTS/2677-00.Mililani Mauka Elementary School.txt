INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Mililani Mauka Elementary School in Mililani on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2677 00 Page 2
obtained during the exploration and by laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of final
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located approximately 2000 feet northeast of the Meheula
Parkway and the H 2 Interchange in the new Mililani Mauka development
The proposed new school will include construction of four 4 one to two story
buildings each approximately two hundred 200 feet by ninety 90 feet in plan dimension
located in the westerly third of the project area A basement is planned for one of the
buildings Portland cement concrete bus fire lanes are proposed to provide access to
the buildings
Also proposed is a asphaltic concrete paved parking lot approximately one
hundred 100 feet by three hundred 300 feet and a paved play court approximately
seventy 70 feet by one hundred 100 feet in plan Finally a grassed playing field
approximately two hundred fifty 250 feet by three hundred eighty 380 feet is also
planned These facilities are all to be sited in the easterly portion of the area

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
foundation and pavement design for the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located about 2000 feet northeast of Meheula Parkway
and the H 2 Interchange near Mililani Town on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Currently the site is undeveloped with an abandoned pineapple field covering most
of the area The eastern half of the area slopes down from the northeast to the
southwest at an approximate slope of four 4 percent The western half of the site slopes
in the same direction at approximately two and one half 2 5 percent
New roads and underground utilities were under construction in the areas
surrounding the site at the time of our field exploration Two of the new roads when
completed will form the northerly and westerly boundaries of the new school These
roads are to be named Makakai Street north side and Ainakua Drive west side
A boulder stockpile was noted near the eastern boundary of the site and scattered
boulders were noted throughout the site Several access roads were also noted crossing
the site
At the time of our exploration an area at the western portion of the site was being
used for stockpiling spreading and moisture conditioning soil
A contractor s storage yard and a soil stockpile about 12 feet high were noted at
the eastern portion of the site
These construction activities are ail part of the on going overall development of the
area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seventeen 17 borings
to depths of about six and one half 6 5 to thirty 30 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration revealed that the site is underlain by very stiff to hard silty clay
and clayey silt residual soil and saprolite to at least the full depth of the borings drilled
for this exploration These soils are comprised of residual materials which were derived
from weathering of basic igneous rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 17 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered very stiff to hard silty clay and clayey
silt extending to at least a depth of thirty 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface the
maximum depth drilled It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide adequate
bearing support for the proposed structures and pavements
Our laboratory testing indicates that the in situ materials have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition Therefore it is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2677 00 Page 6
recommended that if slab on grade construction is adopted the slab subgrades should
be kept moist and prevented from drying prior to placement of the overlying cushion fill
and concrete
Site Grading
Based on the project grading plan provided to us by Hida Okamoto Associates
Inc on site grading work involving up to approximately eight 8 feet
of cut and five 5 feet of fill will be required for development of the project
The on site clayey silt and silty clay soils are acceptable for use as fill or backfill
materials If additional imported material is required the material should consist of non
expansive select materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
In the soil stockpile area at the eastern portion of the site and in the area at the
western portion of the site that was being used for stockpiling spreading and moisture
conditioning soil at the time of our exploration the loose soils should be removed down
to stiff natural ground
Area designated to receive fill and subgrades of slabs and pavements should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 wet of optimum
moisture content and recompacted while in the moistened condition to a minimum of

90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any
loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to the firm subsoil and
the resulting depression backfilled with properly compacted imported non expansive
granular material
The fill and or backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Cut and fill slopes should not be steeper than two horizontal to one vertical

The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations and surfaced

Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed structures If
loose or soft areas are encountered in the footing excavations they should be over
excavated down to firm subsoil and the resulting depression backfilled with select material
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on stiff
natural ground or compacted fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2677 00 Page 8
Individual column footings and or continuous wall footings should be embedded
a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The
minimum dimension of individual footing foundations or the width of continuous footings
should be not less than sixteen 16 inches
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of less than one half inch
total and one fourth inch differential
If foundations are located next to utility trenches they should be embedded below
an imaginary plane with a slope of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the planned invert
elevations of the utility lines to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
insufficient compaction of the utility trench backfill
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to placement of the concrete
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is properly compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the footings
and the properly prepared subgrade may be used Unless covered by pavements or
Slabs the passive resistance in the upper eighteen 18 inches of soil should be
neglected

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the buildings will be of concrete slab on grade
construction
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt and silty clay soils may be moderately
expansive if compacted to dense conditions allowed to dry and subsequently rewetted
Therefore the subgrade soils especially the upper 12 inches below the concrete slab and
gravel cushion should not be over compacted The subgrade soil and gravel cushion
should be thoroughly moistened and prevented from drying out prior to the placement
of the concrete slab The thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion fill should
be provided below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Pavements
Asphaltic concrete flexible and portland cement concrete rigid pavements are
anticipated for the parking area and for the bus and fire lanes Based on our laboratory
test results and the anticipated type and volume of traffic we recommend that the
pavement sections consist of the following
Flexible Pavements
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2677 00 Page 12
Rigid Pavements
6 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
12 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The above pavement sections are based on a subgrade of on site materials having
a relative compaction of at least 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density within
12 inches below the subgrade
Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of their
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
We recommend that additional CBR and density tests and or field observations be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and that the
above sections be revised if necessary The recommended sections assume that
adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas Weepholes in drainage inlet
structures or subdrainage of seepage water away from the subgrade areas should be
provided in order to relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated to be required for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials such as the above 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
should also be used for the trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2677 00 Page 13
reduce the compaction effort required thus minimizing the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from a level one foot above the
pipes to the top of subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch
horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to minimize future ground subsidence
Below pavement areas within one 1 foot of the finished subgrade the subgrade level
compaction criteria should be increased to 95
Drainage
The finished grade outside the buildings should be shaped to shed water away
from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is suggested that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundations and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving and loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent development of ponding
GEOLABS HAWAITI
W O 2677 00 Page 14
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bidding
This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork foundation and
pavement recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior to the start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material that may be required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered at the time of preparation of this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
and also on Plate B 1
Four 4 one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected near surface soil
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under both natural and compacted
conditions The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 laboratory CBR test were performed on selected surface samples to
evaluate their subgrade support characteristics The test results are summarized on Plate
B 1
One 1 Direct shear test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate its
shear strength parameters The test results are presented on Plate B 3
One 1 consolidation test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the
consolidation characteristics of the soil The test results are presented on Plate B 4
