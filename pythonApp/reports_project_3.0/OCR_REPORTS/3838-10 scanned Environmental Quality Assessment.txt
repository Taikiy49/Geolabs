This report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services in support
of the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES construction dewatering
permit application for the subject project Our work was performed in general accordance
with our fee proposal dated The analytical laboratory testing was
performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located within the existing Waimanalo WWTP in
Waimanalo on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Based on the information provided the proposed new construction will consist of
modifications to the existing chlorination building and construction of a new effluent filter
structure Based on the preliminary site plan provided the proposed new effluent filter
structure will have two finished floor elevations one at about 5 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL and a lower finished floor for the base at approximately 2 feet MSL The
proposed effluent filter structure will be constructed of reinforced concrete
Based on the information provided we anticipate that site grading for the proposed
project will consist of an excavation on the order of approximately 12 to 14 feet below the
existing ground surface in order to construct the effluent filter structure The bottom of the
proposed excavation will be approximately 4 to 6 feet below the groundwater table
Therefore temporary shoring and dewatering of the excavation will be required for the
proposed construction

Since the excavation dewatering may involve discharge of groundwater from the
dewatering operations into the adjacent drainage system an NPDES permit for
construction dewatering will be necessary In order to obtain general baseline
groundwater data for the preparation of the permit application it is desired to sample and
test the groundwater underlying the subject site
The groundwater samples were collected on August 28 1997 from a monitoring
well which was installed on August 18 1997 by Geolabs Hawaii during the geotechnical
engineering exploration for this project
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the
groundwater sampling and testing services necessary to obtain baseline groundwater
quality data in support of the preparation of an NPDES permit application for construction
dewatering activities Our scope of work generally consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Collection of groundwater samples from the monitoring well at the site by an
Engineer from our firm The chain of custody documentation handling and
transportation of the water samples to the laboratory were also performed
by our Engineer
2 Analytical laboratory testing of the collected samples in general accordance
with the requirements of the Department of Health NOI Form A
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the northern portion of the existing Waimanalo WWTP
in Waimanalo on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed effluent filter structure site is
approximately triangular in shape and bounded by the WWTP access road to the south
Aloiloi Road to the northwest and a chain link fence to the northeast as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 At the time of our field exploration and water sampling the project site
was an open level field covered with well manicured grass and a few trees The
approximate elevation of the field is about 9 5 feet MSL
Monitoring Well Installation
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted in
August 1997 Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished using a truck mounted drill
rig advancing 8 inch outside diameter O D hollow stem augers The environmental
monitoring well consists of a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe installed to a depth of about
GEOLABS HAWAII

15 feet below ground level The well consists of about 10 feet of perforated casing below
about 5 feet of solid casing extending just below the ground surface The surface
completion consists of a flush mount cover with a tamper resistant locking cap
A Monitoring Well Detail which depicts the installation of the monitoring well for the
project is provided on Plate 3 The monitoring well sampled is identified as MW 1 The
approximate location of the monitoring well is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Subsurface Conditions
During our field exploration subsurface materials were examined and classified by
an Engineer from our firm Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
falling 30 inches
In general the borings encountered a surficial fill layer consisting of very stiff
clayey silt extending to a depth of approximately 3 feet below the existing ground surface
The near surface fills were generally underlain by sandy materials consisting of medium
dense beach deposits followed by a layer of loose lagoonal deposits consisting of clayey
sand at a depth of about 15 feet In Boring No 1 at approximately 21 feet below the
existing ground surface stiff alluvium consisting of sandy and silty clays were
encountered extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 26 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
The groundwater level in MW 1 was measured at a depth of about 7 2 feet below
the ground surface prior to groundwater sampling on August 28 1997
Detailed descriptions of the soil materials encountered in the drilled boring are
presented on the Log of Boring Plate 4
Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were encountered during the drilling and
monitoring well installation Due to the close proximity of the project site to the shoreline
it is likely that the groundwater levels measured in the boring during our field exploration
will be affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at
the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
The analytical laboratory results indicate that no significant adverse water quality
conditions were detected in the water samples that were tested The water sample test
results are provided in Appendix A and include the completed Department of Health
NPDES NOI Form A and the official laboratory report

The test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents in
general appear to be within normal values for typical fresh to brackish groundwater
However the levels of Total Nitrogen and Nitrate Nitrite Nitrogen appear to be high
This could be due to the plant operations prior to improvements to the existing injection
wells In addition the test results for the NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents
including Benzene Toluene Ethylbenzene Total Xylenes and metals including Lead and
Cadmium all revealed levels less than the reporting limit
In general the test results indicate the reporting limit which refers to the detection
limit of the laboratory equipment The reporting limit is dependent on the testing
equipment and the statistical control applied by the laboratory to the test results
Therefore laboratories adopting the same standard test method may have different
reporting limits
Abandonment of Monitoring Well
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the installed monitoring well be
included in the construction documents In general the monitoring well should be
properly abandoned and sealed with cement grout
LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from the field monitoring well Variations of conditions beyond the monitoring
well may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the variations
The location and elevation of the field monitoring well is approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Topographic Survey Map by Towill
Shigeoka Associates Inc dated The elevation of the monitoring
well was based on interpolation between contour lines The physical locations and
elevations of the monitoring well should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the boring log depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring was collected at the time shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in tides rainfall temperature and other factors
