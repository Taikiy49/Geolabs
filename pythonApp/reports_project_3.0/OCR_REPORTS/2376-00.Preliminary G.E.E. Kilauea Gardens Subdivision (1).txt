INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kilauea Gardens Subdivision in the town of
Kilauea on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our field testing and exploration and
presents our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations
are intended for preliminary design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the project site as a 43 lot residential subdivision No site
grading plans are currently available however we anticipate that no major grading will
be required for the proposed site improvements
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized data set for the formulation
of preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site
grading residential foundations and pavement design of the proposed residential
subdivision project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated September 6
1989 The scope of work was limited to
1 Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig with operators to and from the
project site
2 Drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to depths between 30 25 and 46 5
feet below the existing ground surface
3 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tions of the surficial soil conditions

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the northern coast of the island of Kauai Hawaii
The project site is located on the northwestern corner of the town of Kilauea on the
western side of Kilauea Lighthouse Road The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Formerly the site was used for agricultural purposes Currently the site is an open
field with a thick surface cover of tall grasses
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 4
Based on our field reconnaissance the site is nearly level with a very slight slope
down towards the north Neither a topographic plan nor the existing ground elevations
were available at the time of our exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a gently sloping plateau along the north shore of the
island of Kauai This plateau was built during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the
extrusion of basaltic lavas and pyroclastics from the Kilauea and related vents of the
Koloa Volcanic Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived from the in situ
weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual soils grade with depth to saprolite
a form of residual soil which retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent
rock
At the project site the saprolite soils appear to be the weathering products of
pyroclastic cinders and still retain some of the open structure of the original cinder
deposit
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling seven 7
borings to depths ranging between 30 25 and 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project site appear to
be relatively uniform Most of our drilled borings encountered a surface layer extending
to about fifteen 15 feet below the existing ground surface of medium stiff to stiff reddish
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 5
brown to brown clayey silt These are residual soils that derived from the in situ
weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally structureless
Below this surface layer the borings generally encountered medium stiff to stiff
reddish brown to brown sandy silts to the maximum depths explored in the drilled
borings
These sandy silts are saprolite soils having predominantly reddish brown to brown
color mottled with black spots or streaks Occasional soft pockets and or seams were
encountered in the saprolite layers
During our field exploration groundwater was measured in the drilled borings
generally at depths of about 16 to 18 feet below the existing ground surface This
groundwater appears to be in a perched condition and is possibly the result of low
permeability ash beds or lenses within the Koloa Volcanic Series Therefore groundwater
may be encountered in deep excavations within the project site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils within the project
site However modified compaction standard and special grading techniques are
required for the earthwork portion of the proposed site improvements due to the relatively
high in situ moisture content of the subsoils which will be the source of fill material from
on site area and the relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that slab on grade and spread or continuous footing foundations
be used to support the proposed residential structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 6
Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and grading plans are not
available However minimal cuts and fills are anticipated for the project site grading The
following grading recommendations are presented as guidelines for the design of the
project site grading
Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area most of the subsoils within
the top 15 feet had in situ moisture contents of about 35 to 55 percent at the time
of our exploration These moisture contents are considerably higher than the
anticipated optimum moisture contents which range from about 32 to 36 percent
for similar soils in northern Kauai Therefore considerable drying of the excavated
materials would normally be required prior to their use as fill materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally occurs in the area
throughout the year drying and placing of large quantities of high moisture borrow
fill soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading operations We believe
some amount of drying or aeration of the moist soil would be required prior to
compaction of each fill lift Even if the required drying of the fill soils is carried out
within several hours of each grading day we believe the normal 90 ASTM D
1557 91 compaction standard for general site fills as used in drier areas may also
have to be reduced to 85 for this project Our previous experience in other
similar areas indicates that 85 compaction is generally adequate for the normal
site fills and light building loads It is recommended that site filling should be
initiated first and the fill settlements be observed by settlement gauges to
determine when building construction can commence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 7
Fills within six 6 inches below roadway or footing subgrade should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 91 maximum dry density
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Topsoil should be stripped and stockpiled for
later use in landscaping Any unsuitable materials such as debris rubbish
vegetation and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches below
the cleared grade level air dried if necessary and recompacted to the above
recommended minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 91
The ground surface below construction haul roads pavements or footings should
be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches below subgrade level air dried
if necessary and recompacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
Any loose soft or yielding areas spots encountered at the pavement or footing
subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm ground and replaced
with air dried select on site soil or imported backfill compacted to a minimum of
85 of its ASTM D 1557 91 maximum dry density Backfill within 12 inches below
roadway or footing subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
ASTM D 1557 91 maximum dry density

Fill Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is our opinion that the
on site soils have low expansion potential and may be used as a source of fill and
backfill materials lf additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site to
see that it is suitable for the proposed construction The material should generally
be non expansive and should not contain large percentages of boulders organic
or deleterious matters
On site borrow or off site fills should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding
ten inches 10 in loose thickness and compacted to the level recommended
above in Fill Compaction Backfills should be placed in level horizontal lifts not
exceeding eight inches 8 in loose thickness and compacted as recommended
above
Fills to be placed on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 6H 1V
should be keyed and benched to properly bond the new fill to the existing ground
See Plate 3 for details
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes should be designed with slope ratios of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V or flatter Slope planting should be provided as soon as
practicable to minimize erosion

Post Footing Foundations
For preliminary foundation design an allowable bearing value of up to 2 000 p s f
with a minimum footing embedment of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade may
be used The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only and may be increased by
one third for short duration wind or seismic loading For the interior post footings located
at the middle of the post and beam type of residential structures our previous experience
has indicated that unless the footing is buried very deep and extend down to a zone 4
to 5 feet below the ground surface there is a high probability that the post footings will
settle due to the very moist near ground surface soil experiencing shrinkage or ground
settlement The ground shrinkage settlement is due to the eventual soil moisture loss
under a protective shelter environment such as those under the post and beam type of
residential structure
Therefore we believe that the post footing should extend down to a minimum
depth of about two 2 feet to reduce potential excessive footing settlements that could
occur in the future due to ground surface soil shrinkage Since embedment of post
footings below the 2 foot minimum depth would be desirable but could be considered
cost prohibitive therefore it might be necessary to establish some provision to
accommodate and to re level for these footing movements in the initial months after
house construction and particularly those in the middle portion of the post and beam
structure It should be pointed out in the future sale document to the prospective
homeowner about the need to make the necessary adjustment under the structural floor
level If the above provisions are not acceptable then we recommend that slab on grade
with deep perimeter thickened edge be considered in lieu of a post and beam type of
construction
For buildings to be constructed on or adjacent to slopes the building should be
setback or the depth of the footing embedment extended so that there is a minimum of
ten 10 feet between the toe of the footing and the outside slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 10
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the footing should be
extended down to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span over the trench
to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Based on the boring data and not including soil shrinkage settlements estimated
total footing settlements under the residential design loads are anticipated to be on the
order of less than one half 1 2 of an inch differential Shrinkage of near surface soil
layer would provide some additional initial post footing settlement depending on the depth
of footing embedment and location
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be provided by passive
soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to
compute the frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
recompacted in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot
of depth may be used against the buried vertical faces of the footings to estimate the
passive soil pressure
Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade construction with minimum 4 inch concrete slabs placed over a 4
inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel may be used for the proposed residential
structures We recommend that the thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend
a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest outside adjacent ground surface
For slabs located adjacent to slopes the building should be setback or the depth of
perimeter footing embedment be extended down so that there is a minimum of ten 10
feet between the toe of the thickened edge and the outside slope face
To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material a moisture
barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2376 00 Page 11
Our laboratory test results indicate that the moist on site soils in general have low
swelling potential at their natural moisture contents Due to the high natural moisture
content these soils exhibit a tendency to shrink when allowed to dry Therefore the slab
thickened edge should be properly embedded below the ground surface along the
building perimeter and the subgrade soils below the slab should not be allowed to dry out
after concrete placement Landscaping and plantings should be away from footing and
slab perimeters to minimize the potential of moisture loss or root growth in this building
area
Utilities
Underground utilities are anticipated for this development It is anticipated that
most trenching will be in medium stiff residual or saprolite soils
Our exploration encountered groundwater at depths of about 15 feet below the
existing ground surface Caution should be exercised in deep excavations and
particularly extending to depths of 15 feet or more where the underlying soils are sandy
and saturated Interlocking sheetpiles extending to sufficient depths below the future
excavation level would be required to provide the required shoring system
On site soils are generally suitable for trench backfill and should be compacted as
recommended in the FILL PLACEMENT section Mechanical compaction is required as
the on site fine grained soils are not conducive to water tamping or jetting
Septic system leach lines or seepage pits if required should be located away from
building foundations and pavements to avoid saturating or softening of soils in these
areas

Pavements
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavement will be used for the project roads
and driveways
Based on the anticipated subgrade conditions the following preliminary pavement
sections may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
12 Inch Select Borrow
20 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above pavement sections are assumed to be placed over the subgrade of on
site materials having a relative compaction of at least 90 of its ASTM D 1557 91
maximum dry density within 12 inches below the subgrade
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction and the above design sections should be verified and if necessary may be
revised by the soils engineer The recommended section considers medium traffic and
that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas Due to the high rainfall in
the area it is essential that the road subgrades and shoulders be shaped to drain water
away from the pavement sections Weepholes should be provided at drainage inlet
structures in order to relieve the seepage water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading utility backfill excavation and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such field observations
Any imported material required should generally be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as borings Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by measurement from
reference points staked in the field by the client owner Elevations of the data points
were not available at the time of our exploration The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used

depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a

Water level data from the field data points were collected at the times given on the

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
