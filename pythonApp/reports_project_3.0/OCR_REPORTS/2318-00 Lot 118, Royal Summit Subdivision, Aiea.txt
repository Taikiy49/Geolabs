SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 118 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE III
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three 3 shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a two 2 story structure approximately 40 by 88 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab is planned for the garage driveway and the main floor
level of the house The house will be of wood framed construction
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls generally less than
eight 8 feet high are planned along two 2 sides of the garage to
provide grade separation

STTE CONDITT
The subject lot is located on the northern side of Kaahele Street in
the Royal Summit Phase III Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu
Most of this triangular shaped lot is relatively level At the rear
or northeastern portion the ground surface slopes upward at about two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V for a horizontal distance of 25 feet to
other level pads Hollow tile and CRM walls are present along the
northwestern property line as grade separation to other lots
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Three 3 borings were drilled within Lot 118 to depths of 11 5 feet
below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown silty
clay to clayey silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the
mass grading plan for Royal Summit Subdivision Phase III dated August
16 1984 prepared by Community Planning Inc indicated that this lot
was cut by approximately 12 to 34 feet to provide the present level pad
No groundwater was encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The very stiff reddish brown silty clay soil encountered generally
can provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the silty clay soil obtained from the
borings indicate that this material generally has moderate swelling
potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
2318 00 Page 3
Therefore to reduce effects due to soil swelling under on grade
Slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 24
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the swelling
potential of the natural soil
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
stiff natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the adjacent finished
grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 10 foot minimm horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional amount of embedment is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately to highly expansive if allowed to dry Therefore new fills
within 24 inches of the finished lot grade should consist of non expansive

fill On site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 24 inches
below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete placement
heavily soaked with water for the first two days and sprinkled with water
for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveways
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 24 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be reduced to 12 inches
if some small slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete

Lot Grading
Minimal cut or fill is anticipated for the lot grading The
available on site material at the Royal Summit Subdivision area generally
has moderate to high swelling potential Therefore if this material is

used it should be kept below 24 inches of the finished lot grade and the
soil should be placed and compacted on the wet side of optimm moisture
content The remaining fill should consist of non expansive soil such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used at the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of

strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface runoff away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Revi
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Grading Plans by Community Planning Inc
dated August 16 1984 Elevations of the borings were approximately
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the same plan The
physical location and elevation of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Architects
Hawaii for specific application to the foundation design of the new
residence in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is
made
